तद वां नराः शंस्यं राध्यं चाभिष्टिमन् नासत्याः वरूथम् |यद विद्वांसः निधिमिवापगूढम् उद्दर्शतादूपथुर वन्दनाय ||
tad vāṁ narā śaṁsyaṁ rādhyaṁ cābhiṣṭiman nāsatyā varūtham | yad vidvāṁsā nidhim ivāpagūḻham ud darśatād ūpathur vandanāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vāṁ: speech
    • narā: men or people
    • śaṁsyaṁ: to be praised or glorified
    • rādhyaṁ: worthy of respect or honor
    • cābhiṣṭiman: one who is desired or sought after
    • nāsatyā: the truthful or honest
    • varūtham: the divine or celestial
    • yad: which
    • vidvāṁsā: the wise or knowledgeable
    • nidhim: treasure or wealth
    • ivāpagūḻham: like a hidden or concealed
    • ud: up or out
    • darśatād: having shown or revealed
    • ūpathur: they have approached or come near
    • vandanāya: for worship or reverence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of speech and communication among people.
    • It emphasizes the value of being praised and respected, particularly in the context of truthfulness and divinity.
    • The wise individuals are likened to treasures that are often hidden, suggesting that true knowledge and wisdom may not always be immediately visible.
    • When wisdom is revealed, it draws people closer, encouraging reverence and respect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, effective communication can lead to a culture of respect and collaboration, where employees feel valued and praised for their contributions.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful and open fosters trust, making individuals more likely to approach each other for support and guidance.
    • In education, teachers who reveal the hidden treasures of knowledge inspire students to seek wisdom and show respect for learning.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of communication, truthfulness, and the pursuit of wisdom in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that respect and reverence are earned through honesty and the sharing of knowledge.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more harmonious and respectful existence.
