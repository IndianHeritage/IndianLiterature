तव विश्वे सजोषसो देवासः पीतिम आशत |मदेषु सर्वधा असि ||
tava viśve sajoṣaso devāsaḥ pītim āśata | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tava: Your
    • Viśve: All
    • Sajoṣaso: With joy
    • Devāsaḥ: The gods
    • Pītim: Nourishment or sustenance
    • Āśata: May they enjoy
    • Madeṣu: In the pleasures
    • Sarvadhā: In all ways
    • Asi: You are
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer to the divine, acknowledging the presence of all gods and their joy.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that the divine sustenance is essential for well-being.
    • The phrase "may they enjoy" indicates a desire for the gods to partake in the pleasures of life, which reflects a reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude. For instance, when we share a meal with family or friends, we can invoke the spirit of this mantra by appreciating the nourishment and joy that comes from togetherness.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be applied in community gatherings or festivals where people come together to celebrate and share joy, recognizing the divine presence in their collective happiness.
    • In personal well-being, one might meditate on this mantra to cultivate a sense of joy and gratitude for the sustenance received from nature, food, and relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the nourishment they receive in various forms, fostering a sense of gratitude and joy.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a guiding principle for living harmoniously, celebrating life, and acknowledging the divine in everyday experiences.
