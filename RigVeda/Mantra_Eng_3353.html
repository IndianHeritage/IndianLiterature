गर्भे नु सन्न अन्व एषां अवेदम अहं देवानां जनिमानि विश्वा |शतम् मा पुर आयसीर अरक्षन्न अध श्येनो जवासां निर अदीयम् ||
garbhe nu sann anv eṣām avedam ahaṁ devānāṁ janimāni viśvā | śatam mā pura āyasīr arakṣann adha śyeno javasā nir adīyam ||
  • Garbhe nu sann anv eṣām: This phrase can be interpreted as "In the womb, may we be born." It signifies the importance of the womb as a sacred space for the development of life. In real life, this can be related to the care and nurturing that a mother provides during pregnancy, emphasizing the need for a healthy environment for the unborn child.
  • Avedam ahaṁ devānāṁ janimāni viśvā: This translates to "I know the births of all the gods." It suggests a connection to divine origins and the understanding of life cycles. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the way cultures celebrate birth and the arrival of new life, often viewing it as a divine blessing.
  • Śatam mā pura āyasīr: This means "May I have a hundred (years) of life." It reflects the desire for longevity and vitality. In modern life, this can be related to the pursuit of health and wellness, where individuals strive to live long, fulfilling lives through proper diet, exercise, and mental well-being.
  • Arakṣann adha śyeno javasā nir adīyam: This can be interpreted as "May I be protected like the swift eagle." The eagle symbolizes strength, freedom, and protection. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek protection and guidance, whether through community support, spiritual beliefs, or personal resilience in facing challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the sanctity of life, the importance of nurturing, the desire for longevity, and the need for protection. Its significance lies in the holistic view of existence, where every stage of life is revered and supported. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cherish life, promote health, and seek strength in adversity, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
