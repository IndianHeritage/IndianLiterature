ओमानम् आपो मानवशीर अमृतं धाता तोकाय तनयाय शं योः |यूयम् हि ष्ठा भिषजो मातृतमा विश्वस्य स्थातुर जगतो जनित्रिः ||
omānam āpo mānuṣīr amṛktaṁ dhāta tokāya tanayāya śaṁ yoḥ | yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā bhiṣajo mātṛtamā viśvasya sthātur jagato janitrīḥ ||
  • Omānam: This term can be understood as a call or invocation, often used to seek blessings or guidance.
  • Āpo: Refers to the waters, symbolizing purity, nourishment, and life.
  • Mānuṣīr: This word relates to humanity or human beings, emphasizing the connection between the divine and human existence.
  • Amṛktaṁ: Meaning 'immortal' or 'eternal', it signifies the essence of life that transcends physical existence.
  • Dhāta: This term means 'to support' or 'to uphold', indicating the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • Tokāya: Refers to offspring or progeny, highlighting the importance of continuity and legacy in human life.
  • Tanayāya: Similar to Tokāya, it also pertains to children or descendants, reinforcing the theme of family and lineage.
  • Śaṁ: This word means 'peace' or 'well-being', suggesting a state of harmony and balance.
  • Yoḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'of those', indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Yūyaṁ: Meaning 'you all', it addresses a group, emphasizing unity and togetherness.
  • Hi: A particle that adds emphasis, often translated as 'indeed' or 'certainly'.
  • Ṣṭhā: This word means 'to stand' or 'to be stable', symbolizing strength and resilience.
  • Bhīṣajo: Refers to healers or physicians, indicating the importance of health and wellness in life.
  • Mātṛtamā: This term relates to motherhood or maternal qualities, emphasizing nurturing and care.
  • Viśvasya: Meaning 'of the universe', it signifies the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Sthātur: Refers to stability or permanence, highlighting the enduring nature of existence.
  • Jagato: This word means 'of the world', indicating the broader context of life and existence.
  • Janitrīḥ: Refers to 'mothers' or 'creators', emphasizing the role of nurturing and creation in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of humanity, nature, and the divine. It highlights the importance of nurturing, health, and stability in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as family gatherings, rituals for health and well-being, and celebrations of motherhood. It serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards each other and the environment, encouraging us to foster peace, stability, and harmony in our communities. The invocation of this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives and contribute positively to the world around them.
