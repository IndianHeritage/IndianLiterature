प्र मात्राभी रिरिचे रोचमानः प्र देवैभिर विश्वतो अप्रतीतः |प्र मजनना दिव इन्द्रः पृथिव्याः प्रोरोर महो अन्तरिक्षाद ऋजीषी ||
pra mātrābhī ririce rocamānaḥ pra devebhir viśvato apratītaḥ | pra majmanā diva indraḥ pṛthivyāḥ proror maho antarikṣād ṛjīṣī ||
  • pra mātrābhī ririce rocamānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who shines brightly, nourished by the mothers." Here, "mātrā" refers to the nurturing aspects of nature or the divine feminine, symbolizing the sources of sustenance and support in life. In real life, this can be likened to the role of mothers or caregivers who provide love, guidance, and nourishment to individuals.
  • pra devebhir viśvato apratītaḥ: This translates to "the one who is revered by the gods, omnipresent and unrecognized." It suggests a divine presence that is everywhere but often goes unnoticed. In our daily lives, this can be seen in the way we may overlook the small miracles and blessings that surround us, such as the beauty of nature or the kindness of strangers.
  • pra majmanā diva indraḥ: This part means "the great Indra, the king of the heavens." Indra is often associated with strength, power, and leadership. In a contemporary context, this can represent leaders or influential figures in society who guide and protect their communities, much like Indra protects the heavens.
  • pṛthivyāḥ proror maho antarikṣād ṛjīṣī: This translates to "from the earth, the great one rises, and from the sky, he shines." This signifies the connection between the earth and the heavens, emphasizing the importance of balance and harmony in life. In real life, this can be reflected in sustainable practices that honor both the earth and the cosmos, such as environmental conservation and respect for nature.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing, divine presence, leadership, and the interconnectedness of earth and sky. Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the support we receive from our caregivers, recognize the divine in our everyday lives, value strong leadership, and strive for harmony with nature. In practical terms, this mantra encourages us to cultivate gratitude, mindfulness, and a sense of responsibility towards our environment and community.
