अस्मै ते प्रतिहर्यते जातवेदो विचर्षणे |अग्नि जनामि सुष्टुतिम् ||
asmai te pratiharyate jātavedo vicarṣaṇe | agne janāmi suṣṭutim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmai: to him
    • te: your
    • pratiharyate: is invoked or called upon
    • jātavedo: O Jātavedas (a name for Agni, the fire god)
    • vicarṣaṇe: in the act of moving or wandering
    • agne: O Agni
    • janāmi: I know or I acknowledge
    • suṣṭutim: the excellent praise or commendation
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It expresses the speaker's intention to invoke Agni, acknowledging his role in the universe and the importance of fire in rituals and daily life.
    • The phrase "I know the excellent praise" indicates a recognition of Agni's qualities and the significance of honoring him through words and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings), where fire serves as a witness and a means of communication with the divine.
    • In everyday life, the act of lighting a lamp or a fire can symbolize the dispelling of ignorance and the welcoming of knowledge and purity, reflecting the essence of Agni.
    • In a metaphorical sense, invoking Agni can relate to igniting passion and motivation in one's personal or professional life, emphasizing the importance of dedication and hard work.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring the forces of nature, particularly fire, which is essential for sustenance, warmth, and transformation.
    • It serves as a reminder to acknowledge the divine in our daily lives and to express gratitude for the blessings we receive.
    • In a broader context, the mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of respect for the elements and to understand their interconnectedness with the universe.
