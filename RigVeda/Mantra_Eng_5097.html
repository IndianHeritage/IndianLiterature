उदु ष्य देवः सविता हिरण्यया बाहू अयंस्त सवनाय सुक्रतुः |घृतेन पाणि अभि प्रुष्णुते मखो युवा सुदक्षो रजसो विधर्मणि ||
ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā hiraṇyayā bāhū ayaṁsta savanāya sukratuḥ | ghṛtena pāṇī abhi pruṣṇute makho yuvā sudakṣo rajaso vidharmaṇi ||
  • ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā: This phrase refers to the divine being Savitā, who is often associated with the sun and is considered a source of life and energy. In real life, this can be likened to the sun's role in providing light and warmth, essential for the growth of plants and sustaining life on Earth.
  • hiraṇyayā bāhū: This translates to "with golden arms." The imagery of golden arms symbolizes strength, prosperity, and the ability to bestow blessings. In a practical sense, this can be seen in individuals or communities that possess the resources and capabilities to uplift others, much like a successful entrepreneur who creates jobs and opportunities.
  • ayaṁsta savanāya sukratuḥ: This part means "may this offering be directed towards the one with good intentions." It emphasizes the importance of intention behind actions. In everyday life, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and charity, where the motivation is to help others without expecting anything in return.
  • ghṛtena pāṇī abhi pruṣṇute: This translates to "may the hands be anointed with ghee." Ghee, or clarified butter, is often used in rituals and symbolizes purity and nourishment. In a modern context, this can represent the idea of preparing oneself (mentally and physically) to undertake important tasks or responsibilities, much like a chef preparing ingredients for a meal.
  • makho yuvā sudakṣo: This means "the young and skilled one." It highlights the value of youth and skill in achieving success. In real life, this can be seen in the rise of young innovators and entrepreneurs who bring fresh ideas and energy to various fields, such as technology and social entrepreneurship.
  • rajaso vidharmaṇi: This phrase refers to "upholding righteousness." It underscores the importance of ethical conduct and moral values in all endeavors. In practice, this can be observed in leaders who prioritize integrity and fairness in their decision-making processes, fostering trust and respect within their communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine blessings, strength, good intentions, purity, youth, skill, and righteousness. Its significance lies in the reminder to align our actions with positive intentions and ethical values, fostering a harmonious and prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to contribute positively to society, whether through acts of kindness, ethical leadership, or innovative endeavors that uplift others. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and thriving world.
