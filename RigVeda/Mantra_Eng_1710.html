अनु त्वा रथो अनु मर्यो अर्वन्न अनु गावोऽनु भागः कनीनाम् |अनु व्रातासस तव सख्यम् ईयुर अनु देवाः ममिरे वीर्यं ते ||
anu tvā ratho anu maryo arvann anu gāvo 'nu bhagaḥ kanīnām | anu vrātāsas tava sakhyam īyur anu devā mamire vīryaṁ te ||
  • anu tvā ratho: This phrase can be translated as "May we follow your chariot." Here, "anu" means "following" and "tvā" refers to "you," while "ratho" means "chariot." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the pursuit of a leader or a guiding principle in life, akin to following a mentor or a role model.
  • anu maryo: This translates to "May we follow the hero." "Maryo" signifies a hero or a brave person. In everyday life, this can represent the aspiration to emulate courageous individuals, such as activists or leaders who stand for justice and integrity.
  • arvann: This word means "to the vastness" or "to the expanse." It can signify the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, or spiritual growth. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek broader horizons, whether through education, travel, or personal development.
  • anu gāvo: This means "May we follow the cows." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and sustenance. In modern life, this can be interpreted as valuing resources that provide sustenance, such as food security, environmental sustainability, and ethical consumption.
  • anu bhagaḥ kanīnām: This translates to "May we follow the blessings of the wise." "Bhagaḥ" refers to blessings or prosperity, and "kanīnām" means "of the wise." This encourages individuals to seek wisdom and guidance from those who are knowledgeable, such as teachers, elders, or mentors.
  • anu vrātāsas: This means "May we follow the company of the righteous." "Vrātāsas" refers to those who are virtuous or righteous. In real life, this emphasizes the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences and like-minded individuals who inspire moral and ethical behavior.
  • tava sakhyam īyur: This translates to "May they attain your friendship." This phrase highlights the value of friendship and camaraderie. In practical terms, it signifies the importance of building strong relationships and networks that support personal and communal growth.
  • anu devā mamire vīryaṁ te: This means "May the gods bestow upon us your strength." "Devā" refers to gods, "mamire" means "bestow," and "vīryaṁ" signifies strength or valor. This can be interpreted as a prayer for divine support in achieving one's goals and overcoming challenges in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking guidance, wisdom, and strength from various sources in life. It emphasizes the importance of following virtuous paths, emulating heroes, and building supportive relationships. In today's context, this mantra can be applied in personal development, leadership, and community building, reminding us to pursue noble ideals and seek the blessings of those who inspire us. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
