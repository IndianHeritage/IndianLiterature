मन्तर: नासद आसीन नो सद आसीत तदानीं नासीद रजो नो व्योमा परो यत् |किम आवरीवः कुहा कस्य शर्मन् अम्भः किम आसीद गहनं गभीरम् ||
nāsad āsīn no sad āsīt tadānīṁ nāsīd rajo no vyomā paro yat | kim āvarīvaḥ kuha kasya śarmann ambhaḥ kim āsīd gahanaṁ gabhīram ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to: "In the beginning, there was neither existence nor non-existence. There was neither the realm of space nor the sky beyond. What was it that covered all? Where was it? In whose protection was it? What was the deep, unfathomable abyss?"
  • Meaning of Words:
    • nāsad: Neither existence
    • āsīn: Was
    • no: Not
    • sad: Existence
    • tadānīṁ: At that time
    • rajo: The realm of space
    • vyomā: Sky
    • paro: Beyond
    • kim: What
    • āvarīvaḥ: Covered
    • kuha: Where
    • kasya: Whose
    • śarmann: Protection
    • ambhaḥ: Water
    • gahanaṁ: Deep
    • gabhīram: Unfathomable
  • Philosophical Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the profound philosophical inquiry into the nature of existence and the cosmos.
    • It poses fundamental questions about the origin of the universe, existence, and the nature of reality.
    • It suggests a state of primordial chaos or non-being before creation, which can be likened to the scientific concept of the universe before the Big Bang.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern science, the exploration of the universe's origins often leads to discussions about what existed before the Big Bang, echoing the questions posed in this mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals often face moments of uncertainty and chaos, akin to the 'gahanaṁ gabhīram' (deep unfathomable abyss), where they must seek clarity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of questioning and exploring the unknown, which is essential in both philosophical and scientific pursuits.
    • It encourages individuals to seek understanding and meaning in their lives, especially during times of confusion or uncertainty.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the mysteries of existence and the continuous quest for knowledge and truth.
