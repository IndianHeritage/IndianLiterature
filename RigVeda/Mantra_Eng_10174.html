यः प्राणतो निमिषतो महित्वैक इद् राजा जगतो बभूव |य ईशे अस्य द्विपदश चतुष्पदः कसमै देवाय हविषा विद्हेम ||
yaḥ prāṇato nimiṣato mahitvaika id rājā jagato babhūva | ya īśe asya dvipadaś catuṣpadaḥ kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • yaḥ prāṇato: This phrase translates to "who is the life-giver." It signifies the source of life and vitality, akin to how the sun provides energy to all living beings.
  • nimiṣato: This means "in the blink of an eye." It emphasizes the swift and immediate nature of existence and creation, similar to how opportunities can arise and vanish in an instant.
  • mahitvaika: This translates to "the one greatness." It refers to the singularity of supreme power or authority, much like a leader who embodies the essence of their nation.
  • id rājā: This means "this king." It symbolizes leadership and governance, representing the idea that a ruler must be wise and just, akin to a good manager in a corporate setting.
  • jagato: This translates to "of the world." It signifies the universal aspect of existence, reminding us that our actions have global implications, similar to how climate change affects the entire planet.
  • babhūva: This means "became" or "came into being." It indicates the process of creation and transformation, much like how a seed grows into a tree over time.
  • ya īśe: This translates to "who rules." It refers to the authority and control over the universe, akin to how laws govern society.
  • asya: This means "of this." It signifies possession or belonging, reminding us of our responsibilities towards our community and environment.
  • dvipadaś catuṣpadaḥ: This translates to "two-footed and four-footed." It encompasses all living beings, highlighting the interconnectedness of life, similar to how humans and animals coexist in ecosystems.
  • kasmai devāya: This means "to which deity." It signifies the search for divine guidance or support, much like how people turn to mentors or spiritual leaders for wisdom.
  • haviṣā vidhema: This translates to "we offer our oblation." It represents the act of giving and seeking blessings, akin to how individuals contribute to their communities through service and charity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of life, leadership, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder that our actions have far-reaching consequences and that we should seek guidance from higher powers while contributing positively to our world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to take responsibility for their actions, promote harmony among all living beings, and strive for greatness in their endeavors, whether in personal life, community service, or professional leadership.
