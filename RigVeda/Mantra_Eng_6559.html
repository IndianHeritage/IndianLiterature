इन्द्र यथा ह्य अस्ति ते 'परीतं नृतो शवः |अमृत्का रातिः पुरुहूत दाशुषे ||
indra yathā hy asti te 'parītaṁ nṛto śavaḥ | amṛktā rātiḥ puruhūta dāśuṣe ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Yathā: Means "as" or "like," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • Hy: A particle that emphasizes the statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
  • Astī: Means "is" or "exists," affirming the presence of something.
  • Te: Translates to "your," indicating possession or relation.
  • Parītaṁ: Means "surrounded" or "enclosed," suggesting protection or support.
  • Nṛto: Refers to "man" or "human," emphasizing the human condition.
  • Śavaḥ: Means "dead" or "corpse," indicating mortality and the transient nature of life.
  • Amṛktā: Translates to "immortal" or "eternal," contrasting with the concept of death.
  • Rātiḥ: Refers to "joy" or "delight," suggesting a state of happiness or fulfillment.
  • Puruhūta: Means "many offerings" or "abundant," indicating generosity and abundance.
  • Dāśuṣe: Refers to "to the giver" or "to the one who bestows," emphasizing the act of giving and sharing.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for his blessings and protection. It highlights the duality of existence—while humans face mortality (symbolized by "nṛto śavaḥ"), there is also the potential for immortality and joy (represented by "amṛktā rātiḥ"). The mantra suggests that just as Indra is surrounded by strength and power, so too can humans find support and joy in their lives through generosity and the act of giving.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of strength, individuals in leadership positions can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with courage and protect their communities.
  • Community Support: The idea of being "surrounded" can be reflected in the importance of community support during difficult times, reminding us to be there for one another.
  • Generosity: The emphasis on giving and sharing can inspire individuals to contribute to their communities, fostering a sense of abundance and joy.
  • Acceptance of Mortality: Acknowledging the transient nature of life encourages individuals to cherish their experiences and relationships, leading to a more fulfilling life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between mortality and immortality, the importance of community, and the power of generosity. It encourages individuals to seek strength in leadership, support one another, and embrace the joy that comes from giving, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
