हये देवाः यूयमिदापयः स्थ ते मृळत नाधमानाय मह्यम् |मा वो रथो मध्यमवाच् ऋते भूः मा युष्मावत्स्व आपिषु श्रमिष्म ||
haye devā yūyam id āpayaḥ stha te mṛḻata nādhamānāya mahyam | mā vo ratho madhyamavāḻ ṛte bhūn mā yuṣmāvatsv āpiṣu śramiṣma ||
  • Transliteration: "haye devā yūyam id āpayaḥ stha te mṛḻata nādhamānāya mahyam | mā vo ratho madhyamavāḻ ṛte bhūn mā yuṣmāvatsv āpiṣu śramiṣma ||"
  • Meaning:
    • Haye devā: O divine beings (gods),
    • yūyam id āpayaḥ: you who are here,
    • stha te mṛḻata: may you grant me strength,
    • nādhamānāya mahyam: and not let me fall into weakness.
    • Mā vo ratho: May your chariot (or vehicle) not be obstructed,
    • madhyamavāḻ ṛte bhūn: may it be in the middle (balanced),
    • mā yuṣmāvatsv: and may you not tire of me,
    • āpiṣu śramiṣma: as I strive for success.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine beings for strength and support in one's endeavors.
    • The invocation of divine beings signifies the importance of seeking guidance and assistance from higher powers in our lives.
    • The request for strength and the avoidance of weakness reflects a common human experience where individuals often face challenges and obstacles.
    • The metaphor of a chariot represents the journey of life, where balance and direction are crucial for success.
    • The plea for the divine not to tire of the devotee emphasizes the need for perseverance and continuous effort in the pursuit of goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may recite this mantra before a big presentation, seeking confidence and clarity to perform well.
    • A student preparing for exams might use this mantra to invoke focus and strength, hoping to overcome anxiety and perform to the best of their abilities.
    • During challenging times, such as illness or personal loss, individuals may turn to this mantra for emotional support and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the need for balance and strength in our pursuits, encouraging individuals to remain steadfast in the face of challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where one seeks guidance, strength, and perseverance, making it a valuable tool for personal growth and resilience.
