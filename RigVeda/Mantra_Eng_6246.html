यद अप्सु यद वनस्पतौ यद ओषधिषु पुरुदंससा कृतम् |तेन माविष्टम अश्विनाः ||
yad apsu yad vanaspatau yad oṣadhīṣu purudaṁsasā kṛtam | tena māviṣṭam aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "yad apsu yad vanaspatau yad oṣadhīṣu purudaṁsasā kṛtam | tena māviṣṭam aśvinā ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever is created in the waters, in the plants, and in the herbs by the great powers, may that be infused in us by the Ashvins."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yad: Whatever
    • apsu: In the waters
    • vanaspatau: In the plants
    • oṣadhīṣu: In the herbs
    • purudaṁsasā: By the great powers or energies
    • kṛtam: Created or made
    • tena: By that
    • māviṣṭam: May it be infused or filled
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are known for their healing abilities and are often associated with the dawn and the rejuvenation of life.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of water, plants, and herbs in the creation of life and health.
    • The mantra suggests that the essence or benefits derived from nature (water, plants, herbs) should be infused into our lives, promoting well-being and vitality.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Herbal Medicine: The use of herbal remedies in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda and Traditional Chinese Medicine reflects the essence of this mantra. For instance, turmeric (a herb) is known for its anti-inflammatory properties and is widely used in various health practices.
    • Environmental Awareness: The mantra highlights the importance of preserving natural resources. For example, clean water sources and biodiversity in plants are crucial for sustainable living and health.
    • Holistic Health Practices: Modern wellness practices often incorporate elements from nature, such as aromatherapy using essential oils derived from plants, which aligns with the idea of being infused with the benefits of nature.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that nature plays in our health and well-being.
    • It encourages us to seek harmony with the natural world and to utilize its resources wisely for our physical and mental health.
    • In a world increasingly disconnected from nature, this mantra calls for a reconnection and appreciation of the healing powers found in the environment around us.
