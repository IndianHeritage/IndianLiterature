असत् सुमे जरीतः साभिवेगो यत् सुन्वते यजमानाय शिक्षम् |अनाशीरदाम् अहम् अस्मि प्रहन्ता सत्यधृतं वृजिनायन्तम् आबुम् ||
asat su me jaritaḥ sābhivego yat sunvate yajamānāya śikṣam | anāśīrdām aham asmi prahantā satyadhvṛtaṁ vṛjināyantam ābhum ||
  • asat su me jaritaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the falsehoods that are within me be dispelled." Here, "asat" refers to falsehood or untruth, while "su" means good or well. The mantra begins with a plea for the removal of negativity or deceit from one's life.
  • sābhivego yat sunvate: This translates to "With the force of truth, may those who speak well of me." "Sābhivego" implies a powerful or forceful presence, while "sunvate" refers to those who speak positively or truthfully. This part emphasizes the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences and truthful individuals.
  • yajamānāya śikṣam: This means "I offer my devotion to the one who is worthy of worship." "Yajamānāya" refers to the one who is being worshipped or honored, and "śikṣam" indicates the act of offering or devotion. This highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring those who embody truth and righteousness.
  • anāśīrdām aham asmi: This translates to "I am free from all that binds me." "Anāśīrdām" signifies liberation or freedom, while "aham asmi" means "I am." This part of the mantra expresses a sense of personal liberation from negativity and falsehood.
  • prahantā satyadhvṛtaṁ: This means "I am the one who overcomes the path of truth." "Prahanatā" refers to overcoming or conquering, while "satyadhvṛtaṁ" indicates the path of truth. This emphasizes the importance of striving for truth and integrity in one's life.
  • vṛjināyantam ābhum: This translates to "May I be surrounded by those who protect me from harm." "Vṛjināyantam" refers to protection from harm or negativity, while "ābhum" means to be surrounded. This part of the mantra calls for the presence of protective and positive influences in one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of truth, integrity, and positive influences in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek liberation from falsehoods and negativity while surrounding ourselves with those who uplift and support us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of truth and seeking positive connections, individuals can foster a more harmonious and fulfilling life. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire self-reflection and encourage a commitment to living authentically and truthfully.
