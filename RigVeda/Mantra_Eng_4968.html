सोमं अन्य उपासदत पातवे चम्वोः सुतम् |करम्भं अन्य इच्छति ||
somam anya upāsadat pātave camvoḥ sutam | karambham anya icchati ||
  • Mantra: "somam anya upāsadat pātave camvoḥ sutam | karambham anya icchati ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "One who worships the Soma, the son of the powerful, desires the actions of others."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Soma: In Vedic literature, Soma refers to a sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
    • Upāsadat: This term means to worship or to approach with reverence. It indicates a sense of devotion and seeking connection with the divine.
    • Pātave: This word translates to "powerful" or "strong," often referring to deities or forces that have significant influence.
    • Camvoḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of the strong" or "of the powerful," emphasizing the qualities of strength and capability.
    • Sutam: This means "son" or "offspring," indicating a lineage or a result of a powerful source.
    • Karambham: This word refers to "actions" or "deeds," highlighting the importance of what one does in life.
    • Icchati: This means "desires" or "wishes," reflecting the aspirations and intentions of individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of strength and inspiration (like the Soma) can motivate their team to achieve collective goals. Their actions (karambham) influence the desires (icchati) of their team members, leading to a productive work environment.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who show reverence and appreciation for their loved ones (upāsadat) can foster deeper connections. Their strong character (pātave) can inspire others to act positively, creating a supportive community.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of worship, strength, and the influence of actions on desires. It teaches us that by revering the divine (Soma) and embodying strength, we can inspire and uplift those around us. In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, relationships, and community building, where our actions and intentions can significantly impact others' aspirations and behaviors.
