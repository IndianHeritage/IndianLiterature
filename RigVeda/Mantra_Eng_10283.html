पुराणाम् अनुवेणन्तं चरन्तं पापयामुयाः |असूयन् अभ्यचाकशं तस्माद् अस्पृहयम् पुनः ||
purāṇām̐ anuvenantaṁ carantam pāpayāmuyā | asūyann abhy acākaśaṁ tasmā aspṛhayam punaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • purāṇām̐: of the ancient or old
    • anuvenantaṁ: following or adhering to
    • carantam: moving or wandering
    • pāpayāmuyā: with the intention of sin or wrongdoing
    • asūyann: without envy or malice
    • abhy acākaśaṁ: did not perceive or notice
    • tasmā: therefore
    • aspṛhayam: without desire or attachment
    • punaḥ: again or once more
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of following ancient wisdom and traditions.
    • It emphasizes the need to move through life without malice or envy towards others.
    • The phrase "did not perceive" suggests a state of mindfulness, where one is aware but not judgmental.
    • Finally, it highlights the significance of being free from desires and attachments, which can lead to a more peaceful existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, adhering to the principles of teamwork and collaboration can lead to a more harmonious environment. For instance, a manager who encourages open communication and values each team member's input embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, practicing empathy and understanding without envy can strengthen bonds. For example, celebrating a friend's success without feeling jealous reflects the teachings of this mantra.
    • In spiritual practices, many individuals strive to let go of material desires and focus on inner peace, which aligns with the idea of being "without desire or attachment."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to embrace ancient wisdom and live a life free from envy and attachment.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a mindful and compassionate approach to life, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and kindness.
    • In today's fast-paced world, applying these principles can lead to greater personal fulfillment and harmonious relationships, making it highly relevant in various aspects of life.
