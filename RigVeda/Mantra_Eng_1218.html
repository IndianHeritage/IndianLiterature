याभिः शचिभिर वृषणा परावृजं प्राण्डं श्रोणं चक्षसा एतवे कृतः |याभिर वर्तिकाṁ ग्रसितां अमुञ्चतं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनां गतम् ||
yābhiḥ śacībhir vṛṣaṇā parāvṛjam prāndhaṁ śroṇaṁ cakṣasa etave kṛthaḥ | yābhir vartikāṁ grasitām amuñcataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ - This word can be translated as "by whom" or "with which." It indicates a connection or a means through which something is achieved.
  • śacībhir - Refers to "the powerful" or "the capable." In a broader sense, it can denote those who possess strength or energy.
  • vṛṣaṇā - This term means "the strong" or "the mighty." It often refers to forces or entities that are robust and influential.
  • parāvṛjam - Translates to "overcoming" or "surpassing." It suggests the act of transcending challenges or obstacles.
  • prāndhaṁ - Means "the blind" or "the ignorant." It symbolizes a state of unawareness or lack of insight.
  • śroṇaṁ - Refers to "the ear" or "hearing." It signifies the ability to listen and comprehend.
  • cakṣasa - Translates to "the eye" or "sight." It represents perception and understanding through vision.
  • etave - Means "for this" or "for that purpose." It indicates intention or reason behind an action.
  • kṛthaḥ - This word means "done" or "achieved." It signifies the completion of an action or task.
  • vartikāṁ - Refers to "the one who is engaged" or "the one who is active." It denotes involvement in a particular activity.
  • grasitām - Means "consumed" or "devoured." It suggests being overwhelmed or taken over by something.
  • amuñcataṁ - Translates to "release" or "let go." It indicates the act of freeing oneself from a burden or constraint.
  • tābhir - Means "by them" or "through them." It signifies the means or agents through which something is accomplished.
  • ū - This word can be interpreted as "the sound" or "the voice." It represents communication or expression.
  • ṣu - Refers to "good" or "well." It indicates a positive outcome or beneficial aspect.
  • ūtibhir - Means "by the powers" or "by the energies." It signifies the influence of various forces.
  • aśvinā - Refers to "the Ashvins," who are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being in Vedic literature.
  • gatam - Translates to "gone" or "departed." It indicates something that has moved away or has been completed.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming ignorance and blindness through the power of perception and understanding. It suggests that by engaging with the right energies and forces, one can transcend challenges and achieve clarity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, education, and healing. For instance, when faced with difficulties, seeking knowledge and understanding can help one navigate through life's challenges effectively. The mantra serves as a reminder of the potential within each individual to rise above obstacles and attain wisdom through active engagement and awareness.
