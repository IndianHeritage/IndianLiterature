वातोपधूत इषितो वशां अनु तृषु यदन्ना वेविषद वितिष्ठसे |आ ते यतन्ते रथ्यो यथा पृथक् छर्धांस्य अग्ने अजरााणि धक्षतः ||
vātopadhūta iṣito vaśām̐ anu tṛṣu yad annā veviṣad vitiṣṭhase | ā te yatante rathyo3 yathā pṛthak chardhāṁsy agne ajarāṇi dhakṣataḥ ||
  • vātopadhūta: This term refers to something that is influenced or moved by the wind. In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the forces of nature that affect our lives.
  • iṣito: This word means 'desired' or 'sought after'. It signifies the aspirations and goals that individuals pursue in life.
  • vaśām̐: This translates to 'under control' or 'influence'. It indicates the ability to manage or direct one's circumstances or desires.
  • anu tṛṣu: This phrase means 'after the thirst'. It symbolizes the pursuit of satisfaction or fulfillment after a period of longing or desire.
  • yad annā: This means 'that which is food'. Food here can be interpreted not just as physical nourishment but also as the sustenance of knowledge, relationships, and experiences.
  • veviṣad: This term means 'has entered' or 'has pervaded'. It suggests the idea of something becoming an integral part of our lives.
  • vitiṣṭhase: This translates to 'you stand' or 'you exist'. It emphasizes the importance of presence and existence in the moment.
  • ā te yatante: This phrase means 'you strive' or 'you exert effort'. It highlights the importance of effort and determination in achieving one's goals.
  • rathyo3: This refers to 'the chariots' or 'vehicles'. In life, this can symbolize the means or methods we use to achieve our aspirations.
  • yathā pṛthak: This means 'as separate' or 'distinct'. It suggests the individuality of paths and choices in life.
  • chardhāṁsy: This term refers to 'the divisions' or 'the parts'. It indicates the various aspects of life that we must navigate.
  • agne: This is a reference to 'Agni', the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • ajarāṇi: This means 'immortal' or 'everlasting'. It signifies the eternal nature of certain truths or values.
  • dhakṣataḥ: This translates to 'burns' or 'consumes'. It represents the transformative power of fire, which can destroy but also purify and renew.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of striving for one's desires while being influenced by the forces of nature. It teaches us that fulfillment comes from both physical and spiritual nourishment, and that our efforts are essential in navigating the distinct paths we choose. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as pursuing education, career goals, or personal growth. It reminds us to harness our inner strength and the transformative power of our experiences, much like fire that can both consume and purify, leading us to a more profound understanding of existence and purpose.
