आ न इन्दो शतग्विनं रयिं गोमन्तम अश्विनम् |भरā सोम सहस्रिणम् ||
ā na indo śatagvinaṁ rayiṁ gomantam aśvinam | bharā soma sahasriṇam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix that can mean "towards" or "to".
    • na: Means "not" or "no".
    • indo: Refers to "Indra\ the king of gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain and thunder.
    • śatagvinaṁ: Translates to "hundred-fold" or "abundant".
    • rayiṁ: Means "wealth" or "prosperity".
    • gomantam: Refers to "possessing cows\ symbolizing wealth and nourishment.
    • aśvinam: Refers to the "Aśvins\ twin gods associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • bharā: Means "bring" or "carry".
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • sahasriṇam: Means "thousand-fold" or "abundant".
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and the Aśvins for abundant wealth and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nourishment and health, symbolized by cows and the sacred drink Soma.
    • The repetition of "hundred-fold" and "thousand-fold" signifies a desire for immense abundance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may chant this mantra during rituals to invoke rain and ensure a bountiful harvest, reflecting the desire for prosperity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs might use this mantra as a form of motivation, seeking to attract wealth and success in their ventures.
    • Health practitioners may recite this mantra to promote well-being and healing, aligning with the Aśvins' association with health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the universal quest for prosperity, health, and abundance.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal well-being to professional success.
    • By invoking divine blessings, individuals can align their intentions with the energies of abundance and nourishment, fostering a holistic approach to life.
