इन्द्रं तं शुम्भ पुरुहन्मन्न अवसे यस्य द्वितā विधर्तरि |हस्ताय वज्रः प्रति धायि दर्शतो महो दिवे न सूर्यः ||
indraṁ taṁ śumbha puruhanmann avase yasya dvitā vidhartari | hastāya vajraḥ prati dhāyi darśato maho dive na sūryaḥ ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • taṁ: This word means "that," indicating a focus on a specific entity or concept, in this case, Indra.
  • śumbha: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is auspicious" or "the one who brings prosperity."
  • puruhanmann: This word signifies "the one who is of many forms" or "the one who embodies various aspects of existence."
  • avase: Meaning "who resides" or "who dwells," it indicates a sense of presence or being.
  • yasya: Translates to "whose," referring to possession or association.
  • dvitā: This means "two," which can symbolize duality or balance in nature.
  • vidhartari: Refers to "the sustainer" or "the one who maintains," emphasizing the role of Indra in preserving order.
  • hastāya: Meaning "to the hand," it signifies action or the ability to perform tasks.
  • vajraḥ: Translates to "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing strength, resilience, and the power to overcome obstacles.
  • prati dhāyi: This phrase means "to the one who holds," indicating a protective or supportive role.
  • darśato: Meaning "to the one who sees," it refers to perception and awareness.
  • maho: This word means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of the subject.
  • dive: Refers to "in the sky" or "in the heavens," indicating a celestial or divine context.
  • na: Means "not," negating the following term.
  • sūryaḥ: Refers to the "sun," symbolizing light, energy, and life.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the attributes of Indra as a powerful and sustaining force in the universe. It emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and awareness in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to leadership roles where one must embody resilience and the ability to maintain balance and order. Just as Indra is depicted as a protector and sustainer, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom, ensuring the well-being of those they serve. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine qualities that can be cultivated within ourselves to navigate the challenges of life effectively.
