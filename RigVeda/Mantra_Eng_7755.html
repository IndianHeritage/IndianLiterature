पवमानो अभि स्पृधो विश्वो राजेव सिदति |यद ईं ऋण्वन्ति वेधसः ||
pavamāno abhi spṛdho viśo rājeva sīdati | yad īm ṛṇvanti vedhasaḥ ||
  • pavamāno: This term can be translated as "the purifying one" or "the one that is being purified." It signifies the process of cleansing, both physically and spiritually.
  • abhi: This word means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or intention towards a goal.
  • spṛdho: This can be interpreted as "with desire" or "with intention." It reflects the idea of aspiration or longing for something greater.
  • viśo: This term refers to "the community" or "the people." It emphasizes the collective aspect of human experience.
  • rājeva: This word means "like a king" or "royally." It suggests a sense of authority, dignity, and respect.
  • sīdati: This translates to "sits" or "resides." It indicates a state of being or presence.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which." It serves as a connector in the mantra.
  • īm: This word can be interpreted as "we" or "us," indicating inclusivity.
  • ṛṇvanti: This term means "they sing" or "they chant." It reflects the act of expressing through sound, often in a spiritual or communal context.
  • vedhasaḥ: This translates to "of the wise" or "of the learned." It refers to those who possess knowledge and understanding.

The mantra can be understood as a call for purification and collective aspiration towards a higher state of being. It emphasizes the importance of community and the role of wisdom in guiding that community. The imagery of sitting like a king suggests a dignified presence that comes from purity and intention.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own purification processes, whether through meditation, self-discipline, or ethical living, aiming to elevate their personal state of being.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on community encourages collective efforts towards common goals, such as social justice, environmental sustainability, or cultural preservation.
  • Leadership: The idea of sitting like a king can inspire leaders to act with dignity and wisdom, guiding their communities with integrity and respect.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purification, community, and wisdom in our lives. It encourages us to aspire towards higher ideals and to work together for the betterment of society. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened world.
