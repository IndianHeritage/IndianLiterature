पवस्व गोहिजद अश्वजिद विश्वजित सोम रण्यजित |प्रजावद रत्नम् आ भर ||
pavasva gojid aśvajid viśvajit soma raṇyajit | prajāvad ratnam ā bhara ||
  • pavasva: This word can be interpreted as "purify" or "make pure." In a real-life context, this can relate to the importance of mental and physical cleanliness, such as practicing mindfulness or maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
  • gojid: This term refers to "the one who is associated with cows" or "nourishing." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in many cultures. For example, in agrarian societies, the health of livestock directly impacts the community's prosperity.
  • aśvajid: This word means "the one who is associated with horses." Horses symbolize strength, speed, and nobility. In modern life, this can be likened to the importance of having strong and reliable resources, whether they be human or material, to achieve success.
  • viśvajit: This term translates to "the one who conquers all" or "universal victor." It signifies the idea of overcoming challenges and achieving greatness. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who strive to overcome personal or professional obstacles, embodying resilience and determination.
  • soma: Soma is often associated with a sacred drink that grants immortality and divine inspiration. In contemporary terms, this can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to seeking education or spiritual growth.
  • raṇyajit: This word means "the one who is victorious in battle." It symbolizes triumph over adversity. In everyday life, this can be related to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or achieving career goals despite challenges.
  • prajāvad: This term translates to "the one who speaks of progeny" or "the one who promotes creation." It emphasizes the importance of family and community. In modern society, this can be reflected in the value placed on nurturing relationships and fostering future generations.
  • ratnam: This word means "jewel" or "precious thing." It signifies the value of wisdom, love, and relationships. In real life, this can be seen in the appreciation of meaningful connections and the treasures of life that are not material but emotional and spiritual.
  • ā bhara: This phrase means "may you bring" or "may you bestow." It is a call for blessings and abundance. In practical terms, this can relate to the act of giving and sharing, whether it be knowledge, resources, or love.

Conclusion: The mantra "pavasva gojid aśvajid viśvajit soma raṇyajit prajāvad ratnam ā bhara" encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing purity, nourishment, strength, victory, enlightenment, and the importance of relationships. Its significance lies in the encouragement to strive for personal and communal growth, to overcome challenges, and to cherish the invaluable aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with integrity, foster strong relationships, and contribute positively to their communities, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
