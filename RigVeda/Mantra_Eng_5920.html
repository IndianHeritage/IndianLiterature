उरुं यज्ञाय चक्रथुरु लोकं जनयन्ता सूर्यं उषासम् अग्निम् | दासस्य चिद वृषशिप्रस्य मायां जग्नथुर नराः पृथनाज्येषु ||
uruṁ yajñāya cakrathur u lokaṁ janayantā sūryam uṣāsam agnim | dāsasya cid vṛṣaśiprasya māyā jaghnathur narā pṛtanājyeṣu ||
  • uruṁ: This word translates to "great" or "vast." It signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the importance of broad-mindedness in our actions.
  • yajñāya: This means "for the sacrifice" or "for the worship." It emphasizes the significance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • cakrathur: This term can be interpreted as "they have created" or "they have established." It indicates the act of creation and the role of humans in shaping their destiny.
  • u lokaṁ: This translates to "the world" or "the realm." It refers to the physical and spiritual worlds we inhabit.
  • janayantā: This means "giving birth" or "producing." It highlights the generative aspect of existence and the continuous cycle of life.
  • sūryam: This word means "the sun." It symbolizes light, knowledge, and the source of life.
  • uṣāsam: This translates to "the dawn." It represents new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • agnim: This means "fire." In Vedic tradition, fire is a sacred element, representing purification and transformation.
  • dāsasya: This translates to "of the servant" or "of the devotee." It emphasizes humility and the importance of service in spiritual practice.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness." It signifies the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions.
  • vṛṣaśiprasya: This can be interpreted as "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It highlights the strength and resilience required in life.
  • māyā: This translates to "illusion" or "deception." It refers to the transient nature of the material world and the importance of seeking truth.
  • jaghnathur: This means "they have killed" or "they have overcome." It signifies the triumph over ignorance and illusion.
  • narā: This translates to "men" or "humans." It emphasizes the human experience and the collective journey of humanity.
  • pṛtanājyeṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the sacrifices" or "in the offerings." It highlights the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, sacrifice, and the interplay between the material and spiritual realms. It emphasizes the importance of rituals, awareness, and the pursuit of truth in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in selfless service, seek knowledge, and embrace new beginnings. It serves as a reminder that through our actions, we can create a positive impact on the world and transcend the illusions of the material existence. By understanding and applying these principles, we can lead a more meaningful and fulfilling life.
