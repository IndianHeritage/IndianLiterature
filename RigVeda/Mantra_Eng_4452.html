त्वं होतामन्द्रतमो नो अध्रुगन्तर देवो विदथा मर्त्येषु |पावकया जुह्वा वह्निरासाग्ने यजस्व तन् वं तव स्वाम् ||
tvaṁ hotā mandratamo no adhrug antar devo vidathā martyeṣu | pāvakayā juhvā3 vahnir āsāgne yajasva tanva1ṁ tava svām ||
  • tvaṁ hotā: This phrase translates to "You are the one who offers." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the divine being or deity who is the recipient of offerings in a ritual. This can be likened to how people today may offer gratitude or seek blessings through various forms of worship or meditation.
  • mandratamo: This means "the most excellent" or "the best among the wise." It signifies the supreme wisdom and intelligence of the divine. In real life, this can be compared to seeking guidance from wise individuals or mentors who help us navigate complex situations.
  • no adhrug: This translates to "may we not be deprived." It expresses a desire for protection and abundance. In everyday life, this can relate to the human need for security and the pursuit of a fulfilling life, free from fear and deprivation.
  • antar devo vidathā martyeṣu: This phrase means "the divine is within the mortal." It emphasizes the belief that the divine presence exists within all human beings. This can be seen in practices like mindfulness and self-reflection, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and recognize their inherent worth.
  • pāvakayā juhvā: This translates to "with the pure fire, we offer." Fire is often seen as a symbol of purity and transformation. In modern life, this can be related to the idea of personal growth and the transformative power of challenges we face, akin to how fire refines gold.
  • vahnir āsāgne: This means "O fire, you are the divine." Fire is revered in many cultures as a sacred element. In practical terms, this can be related to the importance of passion and enthusiasm in our endeavors, igniting our pursuits with energy and commitment.
  • yajasva tanvaṁ tava svām: This translates to "may you accept our offerings." It is a call for the divine to accept the devotion and efforts of the worshippers. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek validation and acceptance for their hard work and contributions in various fields, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, wisdom, and the divine presence within humanity. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of the divine and the mortal, urging individuals to seek wisdom, offer gratitude, and embrace transformation. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves, seek guidance from wise mentors, and approach life with a sense of purpose and passion. It serves as a reminder that our offerings—whether they be in the form of actions, thoughts, or prayers—are valuable and worthy of acceptance by the greater universe.
