प्रजानन्न अग्ने तव योनिम ऋत्वियम इळायास पदे घृतवन्तम् आसदः |आ ते चिकीत्र उषसाम इवेत्तयो 'रेपसः सूर्यस्येव रश्मयः ||
prajānann agne tava yonim ṛtviyam iḻāyās pade ghṛtavantam āsadaḥ | ā te cikitra uṣasām ivetayo 'repasaḥ sūryasyeva raśmayaḥ ||
  • prajānann: This word can be translated as "O Agni, the one who is born of the people" or "O Agni, the one who nourishes the people." It signifies the connection between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the role of Agni as a sustainer.
  • agne: Refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Agni is not just a physical fire but symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • tava yonim: This phrase means "your source" or "your womb." It indicates the origin of Agni, suggesting that all forms of energy and transformation emanate from this divine source.
  • ṛtviyam: This term refers to the "sacrificial" or "ritualistic" aspect. It highlights the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the natural order.
  • iḻāyās: This word can be interpreted as "in the abode" or "in the place of." It signifies the sacred space where rituals are performed, emphasizing the importance of location in spiritual practices.
  • pade: Meaning "in the step" or "in the path," this word suggests the journey or the process of spiritual growth and understanding.
  • ghṛtavantam: This term translates to "the one who possesses ghee" or "the one who is rich in nourishment." Ghee is a symbol of purity and sustenance in Vedic rituals, representing the essence of life.
  • āsadaḥ: This word means "may we sit" or "may we dwell." It indicates a desire for stability and connection with the divine through the act of sitting in reverence.
  • ā te: This phrase means "to you" or "for you," indicating devotion and offering to the divine.
  • cikitra: This word translates to "wonderful" or "extraordinary," suggesting the awe-inspiring nature of the divine and the universe.
  • uṣasām: Referring to "of the dawn," this term symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • ivetayo: This means "like these" or "in this manner," indicating a comparison or a way of understanding the divine presence.
  • 'repasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of the rays" or "of the light," emphasizing the illuminating aspect of the divine.
  • sūryasyeva: This phrase means "like the sun," drawing a parallel between Agni and the sun, both of which are sources of light and life.
  • raśmayaḥ: This word translates to "rays" or "beams," further emphasizing the theme of illumination and energy.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of Agni as a divine force that nourishes and sustains life. It emphasizes the role of rituals in connecting with this divine energy and the significance of sacred spaces in spiritual practices.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, during family gatherings or community events, lighting a fire or a candle can symbolize the presence of Agni, fostering a sense of unity and connection. Additionally, the idea of rituals can be seen in practices such as meditation, yoga, or any form of spiritual gathering that aims to connect individuals with a higher purpose.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment, both physically and spiritually, and to recognize the importance of rituals in their lives. By embracing the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and the divine energy that surrounds them.
