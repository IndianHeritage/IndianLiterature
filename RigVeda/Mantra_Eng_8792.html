यत्रानुकामं चरणं त्रिनाके त्रिदिवे दिवः |लोकाः यत्र ज्योतिर्मन्तस तत्र मां अमृतं कृधīन्द्रायेंदो परि श्रव ||
yatrānukāmaṁ caraṇaṁ trināke tridive divaḥ | lokā yatra jyotiṣmantas tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava ||
  • yatrānukāmaṁ: This word can be broken down into "yatra" meaning "where" and "anukāma" meaning "desire" or "wish." Together, it refers to a place where desires are fulfilled.
  • caraṇaṁ: This translates to "foot" or "path." It signifies the journey or the means to reach a destination.
  • trināke: This term means "three" and "nāk" refers to "nails" or "points." It can symbolize the three realms or dimensions of existence.
  • tridive: This means "in the three worlds" or "in the three heavens," indicating a divine or elevated state of being.
  • divaḥ: This translates to "of the heavens" or "celestial," emphasizing the divine aspect of the mantra.
  • lokā: This means "worlds" or "realms," referring to the various planes of existence.
  • yatra: Reiterating "where," it emphasizes the location or state being described.
  • jyotiṣmantas: This word means "radiant" or "illuminated," suggesting a place filled with light and knowledge.
  • tatra: This means "there," indicating the location being referred to in the mantra.
  • mām: This translates to "me," referring to the speaker or the seeker of the mantra's blessings.
  • amṛtaṁ: This means "immortal" or "nectar," symbolizing eternal life or divine essence.
  • kṛdhī: This translates to "make" or "create," indicating an action of manifestation.
  • ndrāyendo: This term combines "Indra," the king of gods, and "Ayendo," meaning "to lead" or "to guide." It signifies divine guidance.
  • pari: This means "around" or "surrounding," indicating a protective or encompassing presence.
  • srava: This translates to "flow" or "stream," suggesting a continuous movement or abundance.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks of a divine space where desires are fulfilled, illuminated by knowledge and surrounded by the essence of immortality. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from higher realms, represented by Indra, to navigate through life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of decision-making, where one seeks clarity and direction. It encourages individuals to pursue their desires with the understanding that they are supported by a higher power, leading to a fulfilling and enlightened existence.
