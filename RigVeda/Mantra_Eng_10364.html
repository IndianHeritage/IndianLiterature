सुष्वाणासा इन्द्र स्तुमसि त्वा ससवांसश्च तुविनृम्ण वाजम् |आ नो भर सुवितं यस्य चाकन तमनā तनā सनुयाम त्वोताः ||
suṣvāṇāsa indra stumasi tvā sasavāṁsaś ca tuvinṛmṇa vājam | ā no bhara suvitaṁ yasya cākan tmanā tanā sanuyāma tvotāḥ ||
  • suṣvāṇāsa: This term can be broken down into "su" meaning good or auspicious, and "vāṇāsa" which relates to speech or sound. Together, it suggests a call for auspicious speech or communication.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • stumasi: This word means to praise or to extol. It indicates the act of offering reverence or acknowledgment.
  • tvā: This simply means "you," referring directly to Indra in this context.
  • sasavāṁsaś: This term can be interpreted as "having great strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the might of Indra.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas together.
  • tuvinṛmṇa: This word can be understood as "to grant" or "to bestow," indicating a request for blessings or gifts.
  • vājam: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," often referring to resources or abundance.
  • ā no bhara: This phrase means "may you bring to us," suggesting a plea for abundance or blessings.
  • suvitaṁ: This means "good fortune" or "auspiciousness," indicating a desire for positive outcomes.
  • yasya: This means "of whom," referring to the blessings or fortunes being requested.
  • cākan: This can be interpreted as "to be fulfilled" or "to be realized," indicating the manifestation of desires.
  • tmanā: This means "with the mind," suggesting that the request is made with intention and focus.
  • tanā: This translates to "body" or "form," indicating a holistic approach to well-being.
  • sanuyāma: This term means "to be guided" or "to be led," suggesting a desire for direction and support.
  • tvotāḥ: This is a form of "you," reinforcing the connection to Indra and the request for his blessings.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer for blessings, prosperity, and guidance from Indra. It emphasizes the importance of auspicious communication and the need for divine support in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where one seeks strength and guidance, such as during important decisions, starting new ventures, or facing challenges. For example, a student preparing for exams might recite this mantra to seek clarity and focus, while a businessperson might use it to attract success and prosperity in their endeavors.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for success and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of mind, body, and spirit in the pursuit of prosperity. By invoking the blessings of Indra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of positivity and resilience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
