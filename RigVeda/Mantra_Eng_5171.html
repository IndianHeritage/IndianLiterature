प्रोथद अश्वो न यवसे 'विष्यान यदा महः संवराणाद व्य अस्तात् |आद अस्य वातो अनु वाति शोचिर अध स्म ते व्रजनं कृष्णम् अस्ति ||
prothad aśvo na yavase 'viṣyan yadā mahaḥ saṁvaraṇād vy asthāt | ād asya vāto anu vāti śocir adha sma te vrajanaṁ kṛṣṇam asti ||
  • Prothad aśvo na yavase 'viṣyan: This phrase can be translated to "The horse, which is not restrained, moves freely." Here, the horse symbolizes strength, energy, and the potential for progress. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or organizations that are unencumbered by limitations, allowing them to pursue their goals with vigor.
  • Yadā mahaḥ saṁvaraṇād vy asthāt: This translates to "When the great one (or the powerful) is not held back." This can refer to the idea that when great potential or talent is allowed to flourish without restrictions, it can lead to significant achievements. For example, in a workplace, when employees are given the freedom to innovate, they often produce remarkable results.
  • Ād asya vāto anu vāti: This means "The wind blows after it." The wind here can symbolize change or the influence of external factors. In life, this can be seen in how trends or societal changes can impact individual choices and actions. For instance, the rise of technology has changed how we communicate and work.
  • Śocir adha sma te vrajanaṁ kṛṣṇam asti: This translates to "The shining one is below; your gathering is dark." This can imply that while there may be challenges or darkness in a situation, there is always a source of light or hope. In real life, this can be reflected in how communities come together in times of crisis, finding strength and support in each other.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of freedom, potential, and the interplay of light and darkness in our lives. It signifies that when individuals or groups are allowed to operate without constraints, they can achieve greatness. The wind represents the inevitable changes that come with time, while the mention of light amidst darkness serves as a reminder that hope and support can be found even in challenging circumstances. This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and community resilience, encouraging us to embrace our potential and support one another through difficulties.
