परि ष्य सुवानो अव्ययं रथे न वर्माव्यत |इन्दुर अभि द्रुणा हितो हियानो धाराभिर अक्षाः ||
pari ṣya suvāno avyayaṁ rathe na varmāvyata | indur abhi druṇā hito hiyāno dhārābhir akṣāḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pariṣya: Surrounding or encompassing.
    • suvāno: Good or auspicious.
    • avyayaṁ: Imperishable or eternal.
    • rathe: In the chariot or vehicle.
    • na: Not.
    • varmāvyata: Shield or protection.
    • indur: Moon or light.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • druṇā: Firm or steadfast.
    • hito: Beneficial or good.
    • hiyāno: To be carried or borne.
    • dhārābhir: By streams or flows.
    • akṣāḥ: Eyes or vision.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the encompassing nature of auspiciousness and the eternal qualities of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of protection and the steadfastness of beneficial forces in our lives.
    • The imagery of a chariot suggests a journey, where the moon symbolizes guidance and illumination.
    • The phrase "by streams" indicates the flow of life and experiences that shape our vision and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal journey, one may feel surrounded by supportive friends and family, providing a protective shield against life's challenges.
    • In a professional context, a strong team can be seen as a chariot, guiding each member towards a common goal, illuminated by shared values and vision.
    • In nature, rivers and streams can symbolize the flow of time and experiences that shape our perspectives and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, protection, and guidance in our lives.
    • It teaches us to recognize the auspicious elements surrounding us and to appreciate the eternal qualities that help us navigate our journeys.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek support, remain steadfast in our pursuits, and embrace the flow of experiences that contribute to our growth and understanding.
