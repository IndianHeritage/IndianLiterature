तं इन्द्र मदं आ गहि बर्हिष्ठां ग्रावभिः सुतम् |कुविन nv अस्य तृप्णवः ||
tam indra madam ā gahi barhiḥṣṭhāṁ grāvabhiḥ sutam | kuvin nv asya tṛpṇavaḥ ||
  • tam indra madam ā gahi: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, grasp the intoxication." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with strength and power. The term "madam" signifies intoxication or a state of heightened awareness and joy. In real life, this can be likened to moments of inspiration or creativity when one feels empowered and motivated.
  • barhiḥṣṭhāṁ grāvabhiḥ sutam: This part translates to "the one who is born from the highest place, the son of the birds." "Barhiḥṣṭhāṁ" refers to something that is elevated or of high status, while "grāvabhiḥ" relates to birds, symbolizing freedom and transcendence. "Sutam" means son or offspring. This can be interpreted as the emergence of new ideas or innovations that come from a place of elevated thought or inspiration, similar to how a bird soars high in the sky.
  • kuvin nv asya tṛpṇavaḥ: This translates to "the wise one who is satisfied." "Kuvin" refers to a wise person, while "tṛpṇavaḥ" means one who is content or fulfilled. This can be related to individuals in our lives who, through their wisdom and understanding, find satisfaction in their pursuits, whether in personal relationships, careers, or spiritual journeys.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking inspiration and wisdom in our lives. It encourages individuals to reach for higher ideals and to find joy in the pursuit of knowledge and creativity.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in the workplace where innovation is key. For instance, a team brainstorming session can be seen as a moment where individuals grasp the "intoxication" of creativity, leading to elevated ideas that can transform projects. Similarly, in personal development, seeking wisdom through reading, mentorship, or self-reflection can lead to a sense of fulfillment and satisfaction.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder to embrace the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, to seek inspiration from elevated thoughts, and to find contentment in the wisdom we gain. It highlights the interconnectedness of inspiration, wisdom, and fulfillment in our lives, encouraging us to strive for higher ideals and to appreciate the journey of growth.
