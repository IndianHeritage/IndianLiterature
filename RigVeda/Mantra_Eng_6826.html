अस्मा ऊ षु प्रभूतये वरुणाय मरुद्भ्यो 'र्चा विदुष्टरेभ्यः |यो धीतā मानवाणां पश्वो गा इव रक्षति नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
asmā ū ṣu prabhūtaye varuṇāya marudbhyo 'rcā viduṣṭarebhyaḥ | yo dhītā mānuṣāṇām paśvo gā iva rakṣati nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā: for us
    • ū: and
    • ṣu: in the best way
    • prabhūtaye: for abundance
    • varuṇāya: to Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • marudbhyo: to the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • 'rcā: hymns
    • viduṣṭarebhyaḥ: to those who are wise
    • yo: who
    • dhītā: has understood
    • mānuṣāṇām: of humans
    • paśvo: of animals
    • gā: like cows
    • iva: as
    • rakṣati: protects
    • nabhantām: from the heavens
    • anyake: from others
    • same: in the same way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces of Varuna and the Maruts, seeking their blessings for abundance and protection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in human life, suggesting that those who are wise can protect themselves and others.
    • The comparison to cows highlights the nurturing aspect of protection, as cows are often seen as symbols of sustenance and care in many cultures.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when people come together to support each other during difficult times, they embody the essence of this mantra by creating a protective and nurturing environment.
    • In a workplace, leaders who act wisely and protect their team members foster a culture of trust and collaboration, similar to the protective nature described in the mantra.
    • Parents who provide guidance and protection to their children, ensuring they grow up in a safe and nurturing environment, reflect the teachings of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and abundance through wisdom and understanding.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community support, workplace dynamics, and family relationships, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and protective actions.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that by seeking wisdom and acting with care, we can create a harmonious and protective environment for ourselves and others.
