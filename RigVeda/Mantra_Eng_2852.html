इन्द्रं सोमस्य पीतये स्तोमैर इह हवामहे |उक्तेभिः कुविद आगमत ||
indraṁ somasya pītaye stomair iha havāmahe | ukthebhiḥ kuvid āgamat ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • Somasya: This word relates to 'Soma,' a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • Pītaye: Meaning 'for drinking' or 'to be drunk,' this term emphasizes the act of consuming the divine essence, which is vital for spiritual and physical nourishment.
  • Stomair: Refers to hymns or praises. In this context, it indicates the act of chanting or singing praises to the deities, which is a common practice in Vedic rituals.
  • Iha: Translates to 'here' or 'in this place,' suggesting the immediacy and presence of the divine in our current reality.
  • Havāmahe: This means 'we invoke' or 'we call upon,' indicating a collective effort to reach out to the divine through prayer and offerings.
  • Ukthebhiḥ: This term means 'by the utterances' or 'by the words spoken,' highlighting the power of spoken words in invoking divine presence.
  • Kuvid: Refers to 'the wise' or 'the knowledgeable,' suggesting that wisdom is essential in understanding and connecting with the divine.
  • Āgamat: This means 'to come' or 'to arrive,' indicating the arrival of divine blessings or presence in response to the invocation.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies through hymns and praises, particularly focusing on Indra and the essence of Soma. In real life, this can be seen in various spiritual practices where individuals or communities come together to chant, pray, or perform rituals to seek blessings, guidance, and strength. The significance lies in the belief that through collective intention and reverence, one can invite positive energies and wisdom into their lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and the human experience, encouraging individuals to seek spiritual nourishment and strength in their daily lives.
