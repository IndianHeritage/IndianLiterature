महदाद्य महताम् ā वृणीमहे 'वो देवानाम् बृहताम् अनर्वणाम् |यथा वसु वीरजातं नशामहै तद् देवानाम् अवो अद्या वृणीमहे ||
mahad adya mahatām ā vṛṇīmahe 'vo devānām bṛhatām anarvaṇām | yathā vasu vīrajātaṁ naśāmahai tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • mahad adya mahatām ā vṛṇīmahe: This phrase translates to "We invoke the great ones today." Here, "mahad" means great, "adya" means today, and "mahatām" refers to the great beings or deities. This invocation signifies the importance of seeking the blessings of powerful and benevolent forces in our lives.
  • 'vo devānām bṛhatām anarvaṇām: This part means "to you, the great and boundless deities." "Devānām" refers to the deities, "bṛhatām" means vast or great, and "anarvaṇām" implies those who are limitless. This highlights the infinite nature of divine energy and its ability to influence our existence.
  • yathā vasu vīrajātaṁ naśāmahai: This translates to "as we become strong and prosperous." "Yathā" means as, "vasu" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "vīrajātaṁ" means to become strong. This line emphasizes the aspiration for strength and prosperity in life, which is a common goal for many individuals.
  • tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe: This means "therefore, we invoke those deities today." "Tad" means therefore, "avo" is a call to the deities, and "adyā" means today. This reinforces the idea of actively seeking divine support in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with higher powers to achieve prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in various situations such as starting a new venture, facing challenges, or seeking clarity in decision-making. By invoking the blessings of the great deities, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
