अधि यां बृहतो दिवोऽभि यूथेव पश्यतः |ऋतावानां सम्राजा नमसे हिता ||
adhi yā bṛhato divo3 'bhi yūtheva paśyataḥ | ṛtāvānā samrājā namase hitā ||
  • adhi yā bṛhato divo: This phrase can be translated as "those who are above the vastness of the sky." Here, "adhi" means "above" or "beyond," "bṛhato" refers to "vast" or "great," and "divo" means "of the sky" or "heaven." This can be interpreted as a call to look beyond the immediate and the mundane, to seek higher truths and greater understanding.
  • ‘bhi yūtheva paśyataḥ: This part translates to "like those who see in unity." The word "yūtheva" suggests a sense of togetherness or unity, while "paśyataḥ" means "those who see." This implies that there is strength and clarity in collective vision and understanding, emphasizing the importance of community and shared perspectives.
  • ṛtāvānā samrājā: This can be interpreted as "the ruler who is established in truth." "ṛtāvānā" refers to one who is grounded in truth or righteousness, and "samrājā" means "the great ruler" or "sovereign." This highlights the significance of truth and righteousness in leadership, suggesting that a true leader must be aligned with ethical principles.
  • namase hitā: This phrase translates to "we bow down to those who are beneficial." "Namase" means "to bow" or "to honor," and "hitā" refers to "those who are beneficial" or "good." This indicates a reverence for those who contribute positively to society and the importance of acknowledging and respecting their contributions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher truths, the strength found in unity, the necessity of truth in leadership, and the value of honoring those who contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, ethical leadership, and fostering a culture of respect and gratitude. For instance, in a workplace, leaders who embody these principles can create a more harmonious and productive environment, while communities that value collective vision can work together more effectively to address challenges. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of truth, community, and respect in creating a better society.
