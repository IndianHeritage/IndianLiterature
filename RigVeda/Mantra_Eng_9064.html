दशानाम एकं कपिलं समानं तं हिन्वन्ति क्रतवे पार्याय |गर्भं माता सुधितं वक्षाणास्व अवेनन्तं तुषयन्ती बिभर्ति ||
daśānām ekaṁ kapilaṁ samānaṁ taṁ hinvanti kratave pāryāya | garbham mātā sudhitaṁ vakṣaṇāsv avenantaṁ tuṣayantī bibharti ||
  • daśānām: This word translates to "of the ten" or "of the many." It signifies a collective or a group, which can be interpreted as the various aspects of life or the universe.
  • ekaṁ: Meaning "one," this word emphasizes unity amidst diversity. It suggests that despite the multitude of forms and phenomena, there is an underlying oneness.
  • kapilaṁ: This term can refer to "the brown" or "the tawny," often associated with the earth or material existence. It symbolizes the physical realm and its manifestations.
  • samānaṁ: Meaning "similar" or "equal," this word indicates that all beings and elements share a common essence, reinforcing the idea of interconnectedness.
  • taṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "that," referring to the unity or the essence being discussed.
  • hinvanti: This verb means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It suggests an active engagement with the divine or the universal principles.
  • kratave: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "effort," indicating the importance of intention and action in achieving one's goals.
  • pāryāya: Meaning "in various ways" or "through different means," this word highlights the diversity of paths one can take to reach a common goal.
  • garbham: This translates to "the womb" or "the source," symbolizing creation, potential, and the nurturing aspect of existence.
  • mātā: Meaning "mother," this word emphasizes the nurturing and protective qualities associated with creation and life.
  • sudhitaṁ: This term means "well-formed" or "well-prepared," indicating the importance of readiness and maturity in the process of creation.
  • vakṣaṇāsv: This word refers to "in the bosom" or "in the heart," symbolizing the intimate connection between the creator and creation.
  • avenantaṁ: Meaning "infinite" or "endless," this term signifies the boundless nature of existence and the universe.
  • tuṣayantī: This verb means "to satisfy" or "to nourish," indicating the nurturing aspect of the divine or the universe.
  • bibharti: This term means "to bear" or "to carry," emphasizing the role of the mother or the nurturing force in sustaining life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity in diversity, the nurturing aspect of creation, and the importance of intention and action in the process of life. It signifies that while there are many paths and forms in existence, they all stem from a singular source. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork, where diverse skills and perspectives come together to achieve a common goal, or in parenting, where the nurturing aspect of a mother is crucial for the development of a child. The mantra encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of our actions in shaping our reality.
