अमी ये पञ्चोक्षणो मध्य तस्थुर महो दिवः |देवत्रा नु प्रवाच्यं सदृचीनां न वावृतुर वित्तं मे अस्य रोदसी ||
amī ye pañcokṣaṇo madhye tasthur maho divaḥ | devatrā nu pravācyaṁ sadhrīcīnā ni vāvṛtur vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • amī ye pañcokṣaṇo: This phrase translates to "those who are fivefold." In the context of the Rigveda, it often refers to the five senses or the five elements that constitute existence. In real life, this can be related to how we perceive the world around us through our senses: sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell.
  • madhye tasthur maho divaḥ: This means "in the midst of the great sky." It signifies the vastness of the universe and our place within it. For example, when we look up at the night sky filled with stars, we are reminded of our smallness in comparison to the cosmos, yet we are part of this grand design.
  • devatrā nu pravācyaṁ: This translates to "the divine beings speak." It suggests that there is a communication from the divine or higher powers. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as the guidance we receive through intuition, inspiration, or even through the teachings of spiritual leaders.
  • sadhrīcīnā ni vāvṛtur: This means "may they not be obstructed." It implies a desire for clarity and unobstructed paths in life. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of removing obstacles in our personal and professional lives, whether they be fears, doubts, or external challenges.
  • vittam me asya rodasī: This translates to "wealth is mine in this world." It emphasizes the idea of abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of financial stability, emotional wealth through relationships, or spiritual richness through knowledge and experience.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of existence, the divine, and the pursuit of prosperity. It teaches us to be aware of our senses and the world around us, to seek guidance from higher powers, and to strive for clarity and abundance in our lives. In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering mindfulness, seeking spiritual growth, and working towards removing obstacles that hinder our progress. It serves as a reminder that we are part of a larger universe, and by aligning ourselves with divine guidance, we can achieve a fulfilling and prosperous life.
