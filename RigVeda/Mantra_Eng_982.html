विनः पथः सुविताय चियन्त्व इन्द्रो मरुतः |पुषा भगो वन्द्यासः ||
vi naḥ pathaḥ suvitāya ciyantv indro marutaḥ | pūṣā bhago vandyāsaḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the paths be well-prepared for us, may Indra and the Maruts gather for us. May Pūṣa and Bhaga be worthy of worship."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi naḥ: "for us" - indicating a collective need or desire.
    • pathaḥ: "paths" - symbolizing the journey or direction in life.
    • suvitāya: "well-prepared" - suggesting that the paths should be favorable and conducive to success.
    • ciyantv: "may gather" - a call for divine forces to come together.
    • Indra: the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • Marutaḥ: the storm gods, symbolizing energy and dynamism.
    • Pūṣa: the god of nourishment and prosperity.
    • Bhaga: the god of wealth and fortune.
    • vandyāsaḥ: "worthy of worship" - indicating reverence and respect towards these deities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when embarking on a new project, teams often seek guidance and support from experienced mentors (akin to Indra and the Maruts) to ensure that their paths are well-prepared for success.
    • In personal life, individuals may pray or meditate for clarity and direction when facing significant life decisions, hoping for divine intervention to guide them along a favorable path.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals or rituals that honor deities like Pūṣa and Bhaga, seeking blessings for prosperity and abundance in their lives.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors. It highlights the need for well-prepared paths in life, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts. By invoking the blessings of powerful deities, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve success, prosperity, and fulfillment. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our journeys and the divine forces that can aid us along the way.
