वृषाणं वृषभिर यतं सुन्वन्ति सोमं अद्रिभिः |दुहन्ति शक्मना पयः ||
vṛṣāṇaṁ vṛṣabhir yataṁ sunvanti somam adribhiḥ | duhanti śakmanā payaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vṛṣāṇaṁ: This term refers to the "bull" or "strength," symbolizing power and vitality.
    • vṛṣabhir: This is the plural form of "bull," indicating multiple sources of strength or power.
    • yataṁ: This means "from which" or "from where," indicating a source or origin.
    • sunvanti: This translates to "they pour" or "they offer," suggesting an act of giving or sharing.
    • somam: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine energy.
    • adribhiḥ: This means "from the mountains," indicating a natural source or origin.
    • duhanti: This translates to "they extract" or "they draw out," suggesting a process of obtaining or deriving something valuable.
    • śakmanā: This means "with strength" or "with power," emphasizing the force behind the action.
    • payaḥ: This refers to "milk," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks of the strength and vitality represented by bulls, which are often seen as symbols of power in Vedic literature.
    • It highlights the act of offering or pouring out the sacred soma, which is derived from the mountains, suggesting a connection between nature and divine energy.
    • The extraction of milk with strength signifies the nurturing aspect of life, where strength is used to derive nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, bulls are often used for plowing fields, symbolizing the strength needed to cultivate the land and produce food.
    • The act of sharing resources, like the sacred soma, can be likened to community gatherings where people come together to share food and drink, fostering unity and connection.
    • In personal development, the idea of drawing out nourishment with strength can be seen in the pursuit of education and skills, where individuals harness their inner strength to gain knowledge and improve their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, nourishment, and the connection between nature and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder of the vital resources we derive from our environment and the strength we must cultivate to nurture ourselves and our communities.
    • In real life, it encourages us to recognize the power of collaboration, the significance of sharing resources, and the importance of drawing strength from our surroundings to foster growth and sustenance.
