प्रति वां रथं नृपती जरध्यै हविष्मता मनसा यज्ञियेन |यो वां दूतो न धिष्ण्याव अजीगर acchā सूनुर न पितरा विवक्मि ||
prati vāṁ rathaṁ nṛpatī jaradhyai haviṣmatā manasā yajñiyena | yo vāṁ dūto na dhiṣṇyāv ajīgar acchā sūnur na pitarā vivakmi ||
  • Mantra: prati vāṁ rathaṁ nṛpatī jaradhyai haviṣmatā manasā yajñiyena | yo vāṁ dūto na dhiṣṇyāv ajīgar acchā sūnur na pitarā vivakmi ||
  • Translation: "With the mind focused on the sacred offerings, I invoke the noble charioteer (the king) to guide us. I speak of the messenger who does not falter, who is like a serpent, and I declare the son who is not like the father."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prati: towards, in accordance with
    • vāṁ: you (plural)
    • rathaṁ: chariot, symbolizing guidance and support
    • nṛpatī: noble charioteer or king, representing leadership
    • jaradhyai: with strength or power
    • haviṣmatā: with offerings, indicating devotion
    • manasā: with the mind, emphasizing intention
    • yajñiyena: through sacrifice or worship, highlighting the importance of rituals
    • yo: who
    • dūto: messenger, symbolizing communication
    • na: not
    • dhiṣṇyāv: faltering, indicating reliability
    • ajīgar: like a serpent, representing wisdom and stealth
    • acchā: good or virtuous
    • sūnur: son, indicating lineage or heritage
    • na: not
    • pitarā: father, representing tradition
    • vivakmi: I declare or speak, indicating proclamation
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of focused intention and devotion in seeking guidance from leaders or noble figures in our lives.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where the king or noble charioteer represents those who guide us through challenges.
    • The mention of the messenger highlights the need for clear communication and reliability in relationships, whether personal or professional.
    • The contrast between the son and the father suggests the idea of individuality and the potential to carve one's own path, even when coming from a lineage of tradition.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In leadership roles, this mantra can be a reminder for leaders to guide their teams with wisdom and strength, ensuring that their intentions are clear and focused.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages open communication and reliability, fostering trust and understanding.
    • For individuals seeking to establish their identity, it serves as a reminder that while heritage is important, one can create their own unique path in life.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of guidance, communication, and individuality. It serves as a powerful reminder of the roles we play in our own lives and the lives of others, urging us to be mindful of our intentions and the paths we choose to follow. By embodying these principles, we can navigate our journeys with purpose and clarity.
