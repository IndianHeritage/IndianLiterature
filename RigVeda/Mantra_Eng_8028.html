कृतानीद अस्य कर्त्वा चेतन्ते दस्युतर्हणा |ऋणा च धृष्णुश चयते ||
kṛtānīd asya kartvā cetante dasyutarhaṇā | ṛṇā ca dhṛṣṇuś cayate ||
  • kṛtānīd asya kartvā: This phrase can be translated as "the actions performed by one who is capable." It emphasizes the importance of taking responsibility for one's actions and the consequences that follow.
  • cetante dasyutarhaṇā: This part means "those who are aware of the consequences of their actions." It highlights the need for mindfulness and awareness in our decisions.
  • ṛṇā ca dhṛṣṇuś cayate: This translates to "the debts and obligations accumulate." It signifies that every action has a reaction, and with every decision, we may incur responsibilities that we must eventually address.

The mantra as a whole conveys a profound message about the significance of being conscious of our actions and their repercussions. It teaches us that we are accountable for what we do, and that awareness of our choices is crucial in navigating life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Responsibility: In our daily lives, whether in our careers or personal relationships, we must recognize that our actions have consequences. For example, a manager who makes a decision that affects their team must consider how it impacts morale and productivity.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mantra can also relate to our environmental responsibilities. When we choose to use plastic or drive cars that pollute, we must be aware of the long-term effects on the planet and future generations.
  • Financial Decisions: In financial matters, taking loans or making investments requires an understanding of the obligations that come with them. This mantra reminds us to be cautious and informed about our financial choices.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the importance of mindfulness in our actions. It encourages us to be aware of the consequences of our decisions, fostering a sense of responsibility that is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to global issues. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, we can lead more thoughtful and accountable lives.
