यस्य त्यान महित्वं वाताप्यं अयं जनः |विप्र आ वंसद धीतिभिः चिकेट सुष्टुतीनाम् ||
yasya tyan mahitvaṁ vātāpyam ayaṁ janaḥ | vipra ā vaṁsad dhītibhiś ciketa suṣṭutīnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tyan: that greatness
    • mahitvaṁ: majesty or glory
    • vātāpyam: the wind or air
    • ayaṁ: this
    • janaḥ: people or humanity
    • vipra: the wise or learned
    • ā: towards
    • vaṁsad: the lineage or family
    • dhītibhiś: with wisdom or understanding
    • ciketa: may perceive or understand
    • suṣṭutīnām: of those who are well praised or honored
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness and majesty of a divine force or principle that influences humanity.
    • It emphasizes the role of wise individuals (vipra) who, through their understanding and wisdom, can perceive the essence of this greatness.
    • The reference to "vātāpyam" suggests a connection to nature, particularly the wind, which can symbolize change, movement, and the unseen forces that shape our lives.
    • The phrase "suṣṭutīnām" indicates that those who are well-praised or honored are able to grasp this greatness, suggesting a link between recognition and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody wisdom and understanding can inspire their teams to recognize and harness the greatness of their collective efforts, much like the "vipra" in the mantra.
    • In environmental movements, individuals who understand the importance of nature (like the wind) can lead initiatives that promote sustainability, reflecting the connection to "vātāpyam."
    • In education, teachers who are well-respected and praised can help students perceive the deeper meanings of knowledge, akin to the "suṣṭutīnām."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and understanding in recognizing the greatness that surrounds us.
    • It teaches us that through knowledge and respect for nature and humanity, we can achieve a deeper understanding of our place in the world.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as leadership, education, and environmental stewardship, where wisdom leads to greater awareness and appreciation of the forces that shape our existence.
