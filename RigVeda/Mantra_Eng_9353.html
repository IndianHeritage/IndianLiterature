आ रोडसी अपृणाद ओत मध्यं पाँच देवां ऋतुषः सप्त-सप्त | चतुस्त्रिंशता पुरुधा विच चष्टे सरूपेण ज्योतीषा विवृतेन ||
ā rodasī apṛṇād ota madhyam pañca devām̐ ṛtuśaḥ sapta-sapta | catustriṁśatā purudhā vi caṣṭe sarūpeṇa jyotiṣā vivratena ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rodasī: From the two worlds (heavens and earth)
    • apṛṇād: He has not withheld
    • ota: The sustenance or support
    • madhyam: The middle or central
    • pañca devām̐: The five deities
    • ṛtuśaḥ: Of the seasons
    • sapta-sapta: Seven times seven
    • catustriṁśatā: Thirty-four
    • purudhā: In various forms
    • vi caṣṭe: He moves or manifests
    • sarūpeṇa: In his own form
    • jyotiṣā: By light or illumination
    • vivratena: With open or revealed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence that is omnipresent and sustains all forms of life.
    • The reference to "two worlds" signifies the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The "five deities" can be interpreted as the fundamental elements or forces of nature that govern existence.
    • The mention of "seasons" indicates the cyclical nature of life and the importance of time in our existence.
    • "Thirty-four" could symbolize the various aspects of life and the universe that are interconnected.
    • The idea of moving in "various forms" suggests the adaptability and omnipresence of the divine in all aspects of life.
    • "By light" signifies knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment that guide us through life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see the interplay of the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that sustain life.
    • The changing seasons remind us of the cycles of life, such as birth, growth, decay, and renewal.
    • In our daily lives, we often seek knowledge and enlightenment, akin to the "light" mentioned in the mantra, to navigate challenges.
    • The adaptability of the divine can be seen in how people find strength and resilience in difficult times, reflecting the mantra's essence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence that sustains it.
    • It serves as a reminder to recognize the divine in our surroundings and to seek knowledge and enlightenment in our journey.
    • In real life, it encourages us to appreciate the cycles of nature and the support we receive from the universe.
    • Ultimately, it inspires us to live in harmony with the world and to acknowledge the divine light that guides us.
