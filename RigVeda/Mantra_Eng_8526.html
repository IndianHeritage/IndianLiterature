परि यत कविः काव्याऽभरते शूरो न रथो भुवनानि विश्वा |देवेषु यशो मर्ताय भूषण दक्षाय रायः पुरुभूषु नव्यः ||
pari yat kaviḥ kāvyā bharate śūro na ratho bhuvanāni viśvā | deveṣu yaśo martāya bhūṣan dakṣāya rāyaḥ purubhūṣu navyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pari yat kaviḥ kāvyā bharate śūro na ratho bhuvanāni viśvā |
  • Translation: "Wherever the poet (kavi) creates (kāvyā), the brave (śūro) does not ride (na ratho) on the worldly (bhuvanāni) chariots (viśvā)."
  • Explanation: This line emphasizes the power of creativity and intellect over mere physical strength or worldly possessions. It suggests that true valor lies in the ability to create and inspire rather than in material achievements.
  • Real-life Example: Consider a renowned author or artist whose works inspire millions. Their creativity and ability to convey profound messages can have a more lasting impact than the wealth or power of a ruler. For instance, the works of Shakespeare or the paintings of Van Gogh continue to influence and inspire people long after their time.
  • Continuation: "deveṣu yaśo martāya bhūṣan dakṣāya rāyaḥ purubhūṣu navyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "To the gods (deveṣu) glory (yaśo) is offered for the mortal (martāya) adornment (bhūṣan), to the skilled (dakṣāya) and prosperous (rāyaḥ) who are abundant (purubhūṣu) and new (navyaḥ)."
  • Explanation: This part of the mantra highlights the importance of glory and recognition in the context of divine favor. It suggests that those who are skilled and prosperous should be honored, as their achievements contribute to the greater good of society.
  • Real-life Example: In modern society, we often celebrate innovators and leaders who contribute positively to the community. For instance, entrepreneurs who create jobs and improve living standards are often recognized and honored for their contributions, reflecting the essence of this mantra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of creativity, valor, and the recognition of those who contribute positively to society. It teaches us that true strength is not just in physical prowess or material wealth, but in the ability to inspire and uplift others through our actions and creations. In real life, this mantra can be applied by valuing and honoring creativity, supporting artists and innovators, and recognizing the contributions of skilled individuals in our communities.
