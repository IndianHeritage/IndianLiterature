उचथ्ये वपुषि यः स्वराल् उता वायो घृतस्नाः |अश्वेषितं रजेषितं शुनेषितं प्राज्म तद इदं नु तत् ||
ucathye3 vapuṣi yaḥ svarāḻ uta vāyo ghṛtasnāḥ | aśveṣitaṁ rajeṣitaṁ śuneṣitam prājma tad idaṁ nu tat ||
  • ucathye: This word can be interpreted as "to be uplifted" or "to be elevated." It signifies the importance of rising above challenges and seeking higher knowledge or understanding.
  • vapuṣi: This refers to "form" or "body." It emphasizes the physical aspect of existence and how it relates to the spiritual or mental state.
  • yaḥ: This is a pronoun meaning "who." It indicates a subject, often referring to a person or entity that embodies certain qualities.
  • svarāḻ: This term can be translated as "sound" or "voice." It highlights the significance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • uta: This means "or" and serves as a conjunction, presenting alternatives or choices.
  • vāyo: This word means "of the wind" or "air." It symbolizes movement, change, and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
  • ghṛtasnāḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are nourished by ghee" or "who partake in the essence." Ghee is often associated with purity and nourishment in Vedic traditions.
  • aśveṣitaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is driven by horses." It symbolizes strength, speed, and the power of action.
  • rajeṣitaṁ: This means "the one who is adorned with glory." It signifies success, honor, and recognition in one's endeavors.
  • śuneṣitam: This translates to "the one who is well-spoken" or "who has good speech." It emphasizes the importance of wise and thoughtful communication.
  • prājma: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It highlights the value of wisdom in decision-making and life choices.
  • tad idaṁ nu tat: This phrase can be interpreted as "this is indeed that." It suggests a connection between different aspects of existence, emphasizing unity and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of rising above challenges, the importance of communication, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek higher knowledge, communicate effectively, and act with strength and honor. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and interpersonal relationships, where the qualities of wisdom, strength, and effective communication are essential for success and fulfillment.
