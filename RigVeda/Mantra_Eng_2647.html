अग्नि अपां सम इध्यसे दुरोण नित्यः सूनो सहसो जातवेदः |सदस्थानि महयमान ऊती ||
agne apāṁ sam idhyase duroṇe nityaḥ sūno sahaso jātavedaḥ | sadhasthāni mahayamāna ūtī ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Apāṁ: This translates to "of the waters." In Vedic texts, water is often associated with life, nourishment, and the flow of energy.
  • Sam idhyase: This means "you are present" or "you manifest." It indicates the omnipresence of the divine in various forms.
  • Duroṇe: This word can be interpreted as "in the distant" or "in the far-off." It suggests the vastness of the universe and the reach of divine energy.
  • Nityaḥ: This means "eternal" or "everlasting." It emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine and its constant presence in our lives.
  • Sūno: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring." It signifies the generative aspect of the divine, relating to creation and progeny.
  • Sahaso: This means "with strength" or "powerfully." It highlights the potency of the divine energy in the universe.
  • Jātavedaḥ: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It signifies the omniscience of the divine, encompassing all forms of knowledge and existence.
  • Sadhasthāni: This means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It indicates the importance of community and collective consciousness in spiritual practices.
  • Mahāyamāna: This translates to "greatly revered" or "greatly honored." It signifies the respect and veneration given to the divine.
  • Ūtī: This means "offering" or "sacrifice." It represents the act of giving and the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his presence in the waters and the universe. It emphasizes the eternal nature of the divine, the strength of creation, and the importance of community in spiritual endeavors. The invocation of Agni signifies the transformative power of fire, which can purify and energize both the physical and spiritual realms.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke divine blessings, especially in fire ceremonies (Homa) where Agni is a central figure.
  • Personal Transformation: Individuals can reflect on the qualities of Agni—purity, strength, and knowledge—to inspire personal growth and transformation in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: The emphasis on community in the mantra can be a reminder of the importance of collective efforts in spiritual practices, fostering unity and shared purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine's omnipresence, the significance of community, and the transformative power of spiritual practices. It encourages individuals to seek connection with the divine and to honor the sacredness of life through offerings and rituals.
