सद्यः ह जATO वृषभः कनीनः प्रभार्तुम आवद अन्धसः सुतस्य |साधोः पिब प्रतिकामं यथा ते रसाशिरः प्रथमं सोम्यस्य ||
sadyo ha jāto vṛṣabhaḥ kanīnaḥ prabhartum āvad andhasaḥ sutasya | sādhoḥ piba pratikāmaṁ yathā te rasāśiraḥ prathamaṁ somyasya ||
  • Mantra: "sadyo ha jāto vṛṣabhaḥ kanīnaḥ prabhartum āvad andhasaḥ sutasya | sādhoḥ piba pratikāmaṁ yathā te rasāśiraḥ prathamaṁ somyasya ||"
  • Translation: "Immediately born is the bull, the young one, to lead forth the blind one’s offspring; drink, O wise one, as you desire, the first of the sweet juices of the gentle one."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Sadyo: Immediately or at once.
    • Ha: Indeed or certainly.
    • Jāto: Born or created.
    • Vṛṣabhaḥ: Bull, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Kanīnaḥ: Young one or offspring.
    • Prabhartum: To lead forth or to guide.
    • Āvad: The blind one, often representing ignorance or lack of awareness.
    • Andhasaḥ: Of the blind or ignorant.
    • Sutasya: Offspring or children.
    • Sādhoḥ: O wise one or learned person.
    • Piba: Drink.
    • Pratikāmaṁ: As you desire or according to your wish.
    • Yathā: Just as or in the manner that.
    • Te: Your.
    • Rasāśiraḥ: Sweet juices or essence.
    • Prathamaṁ: First or foremost.
    • Somyasya: Of the gentle one, often referring to the moon or a calm entity.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance and wisdom in overcoming ignorance.
    • The "bull" symbolizes strength and leadership, suggesting that strong leaders are essential for guiding those who are lost or unaware.
    • The act of drinking the "sweet juices" represents the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, which should be sought after with desire and intention.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In educational settings, teachers (the "bull") guide students (the "blind ones") towards knowledge and understanding.
    • In personal development, mentors help individuals navigate through life's challenges, leading them from ignorance to awareness.
    • In leadership roles, effective leaders inspire and motivate their teams to achieve their goals, ensuring that everyone is aligned and informed.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role of wisdom and guidance in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge actively and to be open to the teachings of those who are more experienced.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of strength, guidance, and the pursuit of enlightenment in both personal and communal contexts.
