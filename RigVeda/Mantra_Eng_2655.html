त्रिभिः पवित्रैर अपुपोद ध्यर्कं हृदां मतीं ज्योतिर अनु प्रजानन |वरिष्ठं रत्नम् अकृत स्वधाभिर आदिद्यावापृथिवी पर्य अपश्यत ||
tribhiḥ pavitrair apupod dhy a1rkaṁ hṛdā matiṁ jyotir anu prajānan | varṣiṣṭhaṁ ratnam akṛta svadhābhir ād id dyāvāpṛthivī pary apaśyat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tribhiḥ: by three
    • pavitrair: pure
    • apupod: he has established
    • dhy a1rkaṁ: the sun
    • hṛdā: with the heart
    • matiṁ: intellect or understanding
    • jyotir: light
    • anu: following
    • prajānan: knowing or understanding
    • varṣiṣṭhaṁ: the best or most excellent
    • ratnam: jewel or precious thing
    • akṛta: not made or created
    • svadhābhir: with offerings or sacrifices
    • ād: then
    • id: this
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • pary: all around
    • apaśyat: has seen or perceived
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the establishment of purity and understanding through the heart and intellect.
    • The reference to "three" can symbolize various triads in life, such as body, mind, and spirit, or past, present, and future.
    • The "sun" represents enlightenment, knowledge, and the source of life, which we should strive to understand with our hearts.
    • The "best jewel" signifies the highest truths or values that are not created but discovered through wisdom and experience.
    • The mention of heaven and earth indicates the interconnectedness of all existence and the importance of perceiving this unity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, one might seek to cultivate purity of thought and intention (pavitrair) to achieve clarity in decision-making.
    • In relationships, understanding and empathy (hṛdā matiṁ) can lead to deeper connections, akin to the light (jyotir) that illuminates our interactions.
    • In environmental awareness, recognizing the value of nature (varṣiṣṭhaṁ ratnam) encourages sustainable practices that honor both heaven and earth (dyāvāpṛthivī).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, understanding, and the pursuit of knowledge as essential elements in life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and wisdom, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
    • In practical terms, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more harmonious existence with nature.
