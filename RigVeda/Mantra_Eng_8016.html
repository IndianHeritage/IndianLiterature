स नो अर्षाभि दूत्यं त्वं इन्द्राय तोषसे |देवान सकिभ्य आ वरम् ||
sa no arṣābhi dūtya1ṁ tvam indrāya tośase | devān sakhibhya ā varam ||
  • sa no arṣābhi dūtyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May you, O Indra, come to us with your messengers." Here, "sa" means "may," "no" means "us," "arṣābhi" refers to "with the messengers," and "dūtyaṁ" means "to come." This invocation suggests a desire for divine presence and guidance.
  • tvam indrāya tośase: This translates to "You, Indra, who is pleased." "Tvam" means "you," "indra" refers to the deity Indra, and "tośase" means "who is pleased." Indra is often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens, and this line emphasizes the importance of pleasing the divine for blessings.
  • devān sakhibhya ā varam: This means "O gods, grant us the best." "Devān" refers to "gods," "sakhibhya" means "to friends" or "to companions," and "ā varam" translates to "grant us the best." This line expresses a plea for the best blessings from the divine, highlighting the importance of community and friendship in seeking divine favor.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine connection and the role of community in seeking blessings. It reflects a deep understanding of the relationship between humans and the divine, where individuals seek guidance and support from higher powers.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during communal gatherings, rituals, or ceremonies where individuals seek blessings for collective well-being. For instance, during a wedding ceremony, families may invoke divine blessings for a prosperous and harmonious union, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, as well as the importance of community in seeking blessings. It encourages individuals to foster relationships with both the divine and their peers, promoting a sense of unity and shared purpose in life.
