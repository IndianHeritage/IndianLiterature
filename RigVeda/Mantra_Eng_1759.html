त्रयः केशिन ऋतुथा वि चक्षते संवत्सरे वपत एक एषाम् |विश्वम् एको अभि चष्टे शचीभिर ध्राजिर एकस्य ददृशे न रूपम् ||
trayaḥ keśina ṛtuthā vi cakṣate saṁvatsare vapata eka eṣām | viśvam eko abhi caṣṭe śacībhir dhrājir ekasya dadṛśe na rūpam ||
  • Mantra: "trayaḥ keśina ṛtuthā vi cakṣate saṁvatsare vapata eka eṣām | viśvam eko abhi caṣṭe śacībhir dhrājir ekasya dadṛśe na rūpam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trayaḥ: three
    • keśina: of the hair or the one with hair (often referring to the divine or cosmic being)
    • ṛtuthā: in the seasons
    • vi: indeed
    • cakṣate: sees or perceives
    • saṁvatsare: in the year
    • vapata: the one who creates or manifests
    • eka: one
    • eṣām: of these
    • viśvam: the universe or all
    • eko: one
    • abhi: towards
    • caṣṭe: moves or acts
    • śacībhir: with power or energy
    • dhrājir: the one who holds or supports
    • ekasya: of the one
    • dadṛśe: has been seen
    • na: not
    • rūpam: form or shape
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity that perceives the world through the three seasons, indicating a connection to the cyclical nature of time and existence.
    • The phrase "trayaḥ keśina" suggests a divine being with three aspects or forms, which can be interpreted as the past, present, and future, or the three main deities in Hinduism: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva.
    • The mention of "saṁvatsare" emphasizes the importance of time and the yearly cycles that govern life, akin to how we experience seasons in our own lives.
    • "Viśvam eko abhi caṣṭe" indicates that despite the apparent diversity in the universe, there is a singular essence that unites all, much like how different cultures and societies share fundamental human experiences.
    • The line "na rūpam" suggests that this essence cannot be confined to a single form, reflecting the idea that the divine or the ultimate reality transcends physical manifestations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we observe the changing seasons, which can be seen as a metaphor for the cycles of life, growth, decay, and rebirth. Just as the seasons change, so do our experiences and perspectives.
    • In our daily lives, we often encounter situations that require us to see beyond the surface. For instance, in conflict resolution, understanding the underlying motivations of all parties involved can lead to a more harmonious outcome.
    • The concept of unity in diversity can be observed in multicultural societies where different traditions coexist, yet all contribute to a shared human experience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all existence and the cyclical nature of time. It encourages us to recognize the unity underlying diversity and to appreciate the transient nature of life.
    • In real life, this understanding can foster compassion, patience, and a broader perspective, helping us navigate the complexities of our experiences and relationships.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek the deeper truths that bind us all, transcending the superficial differences that often divide us.
