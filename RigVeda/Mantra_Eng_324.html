निष्वापयामिथूदृशा सस्तामबुध्यमाने |आ तु न इन्द्र शंसय गोष्व अश्वेषु शुभ्रिषु सहस्रेषु तुवीमघ ||
ni ṣvāpayā mithūdṛśā sastām abudhyamāne | ā tū na indra śaṁsaya goṣv aśveṣu śubhriṣu sahasreṣu tuvīmagha ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ni ṣvāpayā: This can be interpreted as "by the power of the divine" or "through the auspices of the divine." It suggests a connection to a higher power or divine influence.
    • mithūdṛśā: This term can mean "with a unified vision" or "with a shared perspective." It emphasizes the importance of collective understanding and harmony.
    • sastām: This word translates to "may we be protected" or "may we be safeguarded." It indicates a plea for safety and well-being.
    • abudhyamāne: This means "when we are awakened" or "when we become aware." It signifies the importance of consciousness and awareness in our actions.
    • ā tū na indra: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Indra, do not abandon us." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and protection.
    • śaṁsaya: This means "doubt" or "uncertainty." It highlights the human experience of doubt and the need for clarity.
    • goṣv: This translates to "in the herds" or "among the cattle." It symbolizes prosperity and abundance, as cattle were a sign of wealth in ancient times.
    • aśveṣu: This means "among the horses." Horses represent strength, speed, and nobility, further emphasizing the theme of abundance.
    • śubhriṣu: This term means "in auspicious things" or "in good fortune." It reflects the desire for positive outcomes and blessings.
    • sahasreṣu: This translates to "in thousands" or "in multitudes," indicating a vastness of blessings and abundance.
    • tuvīmagha: This can be interpreted as "may we be free from sin" or "may we be purified." It signifies the aspiration for moral and spiritual purity.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It calls for unity and collective awareness, suggesting that we should work together with a shared vision.
    • The plea to Indra reflects a desire for strength and support in overcoming doubts and uncertainties.
    • The references to cattle and horses symbolize prosperity and abundance, indicating that we should strive for a life filled with blessings.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to seek moral and spiritual purity while being aware of our surroundings and the collective journey.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek divine guidance during challenging times, fostering a sense of connection to a higher power.
    • In community settings, it encourages collaboration and unity, promoting collective efforts towards common goals.
    • In professional environments, it serves as a reminder to overcome doubts and uncertainties, fostering a culture of support and strength among colleagues.
    • In spiritual practices, it can be used as a chant for purification and seeking blessings for abundance and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection, unity, and abundance in life.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, reminding us of the importance of collective awareness and moral integrity.
    • By applying its teachings in our daily lives, we can cultivate a sense of purpose, strength, and harmony in our personal and communal endeavors.
