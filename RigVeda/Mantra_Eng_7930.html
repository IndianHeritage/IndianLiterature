मन्त्र: सुनोताम मधुमत्तमं सोमं इन्द्राय वज्रीणि | चाऽरुं शर्धाय मत्सरम् ||
sunotā madhumattamaṁ somam indrāya vajriṇe | cāruṁ śardhāya matsaram ||
  • sunotā: This word can be translated as "may we hear" or "let us listen." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • madhumattamaṁ: This term means "the sweetest" or "the most delightful." It refers to something that brings joy and satisfaction, often associated with the divine or spiritual experiences.
  • somam: "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and the connection to the divine.
  • indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is associated with thunder, rain, and war. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • vajriṇe: This term means "the wielder of the thunderbolt," referring to Indra's strength and ability to overcome obstacles. It signifies protection and the ability to conquer challenges.
  • cāruṁ: This word translates to "beautiful" or "charming." It denotes something that is pleasing to the senses and evokes admiration.
  • śardhāya: This term means "to the one who is worthy of praise" or "to the one who is revered." It highlights the importance of respect and devotion towards the divine.
  • matsaram: This word can be interpreted as "jealousy" or "envy." It suggests the need to overcome negative emotions and cultivate positive qualities.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for the sweetest and most delightful experiences in life, symbolized by the sacred Soma. It emphasizes the importance of listening to divine wisdom and seeking beauty and reverence in our lives while overcoming negative emotions like jealousy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to be open to learning and to seek joy in their experiences.
  • In relationships, it promotes the idea of appreciating beauty and harmony while working to eliminate feelings of jealousy or envy.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to connect with the divine and seek inspiration from higher powers.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of receptivity to divine wisdom, the pursuit of joy and beauty, and the need to overcome negative emotions. It is applicable in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to cultivate a positive mindset and a deeper connection with the divine.
