न जामये तान्वो ऋक्तम् आरैक चकार गर्भं सन्तुर निधानम् |यदी मातरो जनयन्त वह्निम अन्यः कर्ता सुखृतोर अन्य ऋन्धन ||
na jāmaye tānvo riktham āraik cakāra garbhaṁ sanitur nidhānam | yadī mātaro janayanta vahnim anyaḥ kartā sukṛtor anya ṛndhan ||
  • na jāmaye tānvo riktham: This phrase can be translated to "I do not desire the wealth of others." It emphasizes the importance of self-sufficiency and contentment with what one has.
  • āraik cakāra garbhaṁ sanitur nidhānam: This part means "the womb that has produced wealth." It signifies the source of prosperity and abundance, which can be interpreted as one's own efforts and actions leading to success.
  • yadī mātaro janayanta vahnim: This translates to "if the mothers give birth to the fire." Here, 'fire' symbolizes energy, passion, and transformation. It suggests that nurturing and supportive environments can lead to the birth of great ideas and innovations.
  • anyaḥ kartā sukṛtor anya ṛndhan: This means "another creator of good deeds is not to be found." It highlights the uniqueness of individual contributions and the importance of personal responsibility in creating one's destiny.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the values of self-reliance, the significance of personal effort in achieving prosperity, and the unique contributions of individuals to society. In real life, it encourages us to focus on our own paths rather than coveting what others have. It also reminds us of the importance of nurturing environments that foster creativity and innovation. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, entrepreneurship, and community building, where individual efforts and a supportive atmosphere can lead to collective success.
