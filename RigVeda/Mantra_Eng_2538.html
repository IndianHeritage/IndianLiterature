शृङ्गाणीवेच छृङ्गिणां सं ददृश्रे चषालवंतः स्वरवः पृथिव्याम् |वाघद्भिर वा विहवे श्रोषमाणां अस्मांं अवन्तु पृथनाज्येषु ||
śṛṅgāṇīvec chṛṅgiṇāṁ saṁ dadṛśre caṣālavantaḥ svaravaḥ pṛthivyām | vāghadbhir vā vihave śroṣamāṇā asmām̐ avantu pṛtanājyeṣu ||
  • śṛṅgāṇī: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with the power of perception" or "those who are alert and aware." In real life, this can refer to individuals who are observant and attentive to their surroundings, such as a teacher who notices the needs of their students.
  • vec: This word means "to speak" or "to express." It highlights the importance of communication in human interactions. For example, effective communication in a workplace can lead to better teamwork and productivity.
  • chṛṅgiṇāṁ: This can be understood as "those who are wise" or "those who possess knowledge." In a practical sense, this refers to mentors or leaders who guide others with their wisdom, such as a community leader who helps resolve conflicts.
  • saṁ dadṛśre: This phrase translates to "they have seen together" or "they have come together." It emphasizes unity and collective vision. For instance, a group of friends coming together to support a cause demonstrates the power of collaboration.
  • caṣālavantaḥ: This term can mean "those who are strong" or "those who have the ability to endure." In real life, this could refer to athletes who train hard to overcome challenges and achieve their goals.
  • svaravaḥ: This word signifies "the sound" or "the voice." It can represent the importance of expressing oneself and being heard. For example, activists using their voices to advocate for social change illustrate the power of sound in effecting change.
  • pṛthivyām: This translates to "on the earth" or "in the world." It signifies the physical realm and our existence within it. This can relate to environmental awareness and the need to protect our planet.
  • vāghadbhir: This term means "with words" or "through speech." It emphasizes the role of language in shaping our reality. For instance, a motivational speaker using words to inspire others showcases the impact of language.
  • vā vihave: This can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be safe." It reflects a desire for safety and security in our lives. For example, communities coming together to support each other during crises exemplify this protective instinct.
  • śroṣamāṇā: This word means "those who listen" or "those who are receptive." It highlights the importance of listening in communication. For instance, a good therapist listens attentively to their clients, fostering healing and understanding.
  • asmām̐: This translates to "us" or "among us," emphasizing community and togetherness. It signifies the importance of belonging and support within a group.
  • avantu: This term means "may they protect" or "may they guide." It reflects a wish for guidance and support from others. For example, parents guiding their children through life's challenges illustrate this protective role.
  • pṛtanājyeṣu: This can be understood as "in the paths of life" or "in our journeys." It signifies the various paths we take in life and the experiences we encounter along the way.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awareness, communication, wisdom, unity, strength, and protection in our lives. It serves as a reminder that we are interconnected and that our words and actions can have a profound impact on ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, community building, environmental advocacy, and personal relationships. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, we can foster a more harmonious and supportive world.
