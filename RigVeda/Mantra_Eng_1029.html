अग्निषोमाव इमं सु मे श्रिणुतं वृषणा हवम् |प्रति सूक्तानि हर्यतम भवतं दाशुषे मयः ||
agnīṣomāv imaṁ su me śṛṇutaṁ vṛṣaṇā havam | prati sūktāni haryatam bhavataṁ dāśuṣe mayaḥ ||
  • Agnīṣomāv: This term refers to the duality of Agni (fire) and Soma (the sacred drink). Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification, while Soma represents nourishment, immortality, and the divine essence.
  • imaṁ: This word means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current offering.
  • su me: Translated as "good for me," it expresses a desire for beneficial outcomes from the offerings made.
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This means "listen" or "hear," calling upon the deities to pay attention to the invocation.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This term can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful," often referring to the might of the deities or the strength of the offerings.
  • havam: This refers to the "offering" or "sacrifice," a central theme in Vedic rituals where offerings are made to the gods.
  • prati: Meaning "towards" or "in response to," indicating a reciprocal relationship between the devotee and the divine.
  • sūktāni: This term refers to "hymns" or "sacred verses," emphasizing the importance of recitation and prayer in Vedic traditions.
  • haryatam: This can be interpreted as "the most auspicious" or "the best," highlighting the quality of the offerings and the intentions behind them.
  • bhavataṁ: Meaning "to you" or "for you," it signifies the direction of the offerings towards the deities.
  • dāśuṣe: This term means "to the worshipper" or "to the devotee," indicating that the offerings are made for the benefit of the one who performs the ritual.
  • mayaḥ: This means "by me," emphasizing the personal involvement and agency of the devotee in the ritual.

Conclusion: The mantra "agnīṣomāv imaṁ su me śṛṇutaṁ vṛṣaṇā havam | prati sūktāni haryatam bhavataṁ dāśuṣe mayaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, where the devotee seeks the attention and blessings of the divine through offerings. It highlights the relationship between the devotee and the deities, emphasizing the importance of intention, strength, and reciprocity in spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, rituals, or even in moments of seeking guidance and support in challenging situations. By invoking the energies of Agni and Soma, individuals can aspire for transformation and nourishment in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the universe.
