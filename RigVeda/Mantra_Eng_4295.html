मन्त्र: अश्विनाः हरिणाव इव गौराव इवाणु यवसम् | हंसाव इव पततम् आ सुताम् उप ||
aśvinā hariṇāv iva gaurāv ivānu yavasam | haṁsāv iva patatam ā sutām̐ upa ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā hariṇāv iva gaurāv ivānu yavasam | haṁsāv iva patatam ā sutām̐ upa ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Like the two Ashvins, like the two deer, like the two cows, like the two swans, may we attain the offspring." The Ashvins are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health and vitality.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aśvinā: Refers to the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with medicine and health.
    • hariṇāv: Means "like deer," symbolizing grace and agility.
    • gaurāv: Refers to "cows," which are often seen as symbols of nourishment and abundance.
    • haṁsāv: Means "like swans," representing purity and beauty.
    • sutām̐: Refers to "offspring" or "children," indicating the desire for progeny.
    • upa: Means "may we attain" or "may we reach," expressing a wish or prayer.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Ashvins can be likened to modern-day doctors or healthcare professionals who strive to bring health and wellness to individuals.
    • The grace of deer can be seen in athletes or dancers who embody agility and elegance in their movements.
    • Cows symbolize the importance of nurturing and providing sustenance, much like how parents care for their children.
    • Swans are often associated with love and fidelity, reflecting the beauty of relationships and family bonds.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of health, grace, nourishment, and beauty in the pursuit of progeny.
    • It signifies the importance of seeking blessings from divine forces for a healthy and prosperous lineage.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as family planning, health and wellness practices, and fostering loving relationships.
    • By invoking the qualities of the Ashvins, deer, cows, and swans, individuals can aspire to create a harmonious and fulfilling life for themselves and their future generations.
