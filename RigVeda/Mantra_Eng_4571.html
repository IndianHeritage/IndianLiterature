अनु dyāvāpṛthivī तत्त त ओजो 'मार्त्याः जिहत इन्द्र देवाः |कृष्वा कृत्नो अकृतं यत ते अस्ति उक्तं नवीयो जनयस्व यज्ञैः ||
anu dyāvāpṛthivī tat ta ojo 'martyā jihata indra devāḥ | kṛṣvā kṛtno akṛtaṁ yat te asty ukthaṁ navīyo janayasva yajñaiḥ ||
  • anu dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "the heavens and the earth." It signifies the duality of existence, representing both the spiritual and the material realms. In real life, this can be seen in the balance we seek between our spiritual pursuits and our earthly responsibilities.
  • tat ta ojo 'martyā: This means "that strength, O mortal." It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality in human life. For example, athletes train rigorously to build physical strength, which is essential for their performance and overall health.
  • jihata indra devāḥ: This translates to "the divine beings, Indra, strive." Indra is often associated with power and leadership. In a real-life context, this can be related to leaders who inspire and motivate others to achieve greatness, much like a coach guiding a sports team to victory.
  • kṛṣvā kṛtno akṛtaṁ: This phrase means "by cultivating what is uncreated." It suggests the idea of nurturing potential and creativity. In everyday life, this can be seen in artists and innovators who bring forth new ideas and creations from their imagination, transforming the uncreated into reality.
  • yat te asty ukthaṁ: This translates to "whatever is your utterance." It highlights the power of words and intentions. In real life, this can be observed in the impact of positive affirmations and motivational speeches that encourage individuals to pursue their dreams and aspirations.
  • navīyo janayasva yajñaiḥ: This means "generate newness through sacrifices." It emphasizes the importance of making sacrifices for growth and progress. In practical terms, this can be seen in the dedication of students who sacrifice leisure time to study hard for exams, ultimately leading to their success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for strength, balance, and creativity in life. It teaches us the significance of nurturing our potential, the power of our words, and the necessity of sacrifices for achieving new heights. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to seek harmony between their spiritual and material lives while fostering growth and innovation through dedication and effort.
