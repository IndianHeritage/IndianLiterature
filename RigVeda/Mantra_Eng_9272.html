प्र होताऽ जातो महान् नभोविन नृषद्वा सीदद अपाम् उपस्थे | दधिर यो धायि स ते वयांसी यन्ता वसूनि विधते तनूपाः ||
pra hotā jāto mahān nabhovin nṛṣadvā sīdad apām upasthe | dadhir yo dhāyi sa te vayāṁsi yantā vasūni vidhate tanūpāḥ ||
  • pra hotā: This term refers to the "one who invokes" or "the priest who offers." In a real-life context, this can be likened to a leader or a person who takes the initiative to bring people together for a common cause, such as a community leader organizing a festival or a charity event.
  • jāto mahān: This phrase means "born great" or "of great origin." It signifies the importance of heritage and the potential that comes from one's background. For example, a person from a family of educators may feel a sense of responsibility to continue that legacy by becoming a teacher themselves.
  • nabhovin: This word can be interpreted as "of the sky" or "heavenly." It suggests a connection to higher ideals or aspirations. In modern life, this could relate to individuals who strive for lofty goals, such as scientists working towards breakthroughs in technology or medicine.
  • nṛṣadvā: This term refers to "the one who knows the truth" or "the wise." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and knowledge. In everyday life, this can be seen in educators, mentors, or anyone who shares their knowledge to help others grow and learn.
  • sīdad apām upasthe: This phrase translates to "who sits in the lap of waters." It symbolizes nourishment and support. In a practical sense, this can be related to the importance of community support systems, such as family and friends who provide emotional and physical support during challenging times.
  • dadhir yo dhāyi: This means "he who holds" or "he who sustains." It reflects the idea of responsibility and care. For instance, a parent who works hard to provide for their family embodies this principle by ensuring their loved ones have what they need to thrive.
  • sa te vayāṁsi: This translates to "he is your strength." It highlights the importance of support and encouragement from others. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals rely on each other's strengths to achieve a common goal, such as in sports or collaborative projects.
  • yantā vasūni vidhate tanūpāḥ: This phrase means "who arranges the wealth of the body." It signifies the importance of health and well-being. In contemporary life, this can relate to the significance of maintaining physical and mental health through exercise, nutrition, and self-care practices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of leadership, heritage, wisdom, support, responsibility, and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the roles we play in our communities and the importance of nurturing relationships and values that contribute to a harmonious society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to take initiative, seek knowledge, support one another, and prioritize health, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and balanced life.
