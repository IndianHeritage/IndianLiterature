यā गोमतीर उषसः सर्ववीराः व्युःछन्ति दाशुषे मार्त्याय | वायोर इव सूनृतानाम उदर्के ता अश्वदाः अश्नवत सोमसुत्वा ||
yā gomatīr uṣasaḥ sarvavīrā vyucchanti dāśuṣe martyāya | vāyor iva sūnṛtānām udarke tā aśvadā aśnavat somasutvā ||
  • yā gomatīr: This phrase can be translated as "that which is rich in cows" or "the one who possesses wealth." In ancient Vedic society, cows were considered a symbol of wealth and prosperity. For example, a farmer with a large herd of cows would be seen as successful and respected in the community.
  • uṣasaḥ: This word means "of the dawn" or "morning." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In real life, every morning offers a fresh start, allowing individuals to set new goals and intentions for the day ahead.
  • sarvavīrā: This translates to "all the brave" or "all the heroes." It signifies the collective strength and courage of individuals. In modern contexts, this can relate to teamwork and collaboration, where the combined efforts of many can lead to success, such as in sports teams or community projects.
  • vyucchanti: This means "they disperse" or "they separate." It can refer to the idea of overcoming obstacles or challenges. For instance, when a group of friends faces a problem together, they may find solutions that allow them to move forward, just as the dawn dispels the darkness of night.
  • dāśuṣe: This word means "for the mortal" or "for humanity." It emphasizes the importance of human experience and the need for support and guidance in life. In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and community service, where individuals come together to help those in need.
  • martyāya: This translates to "to the mortal" or "to the human being." It highlights the transient nature of human life and the importance of making the most of our time on Earth. For example, people often strive to leave a legacy or make a positive impact on future generations.
  • vāyor iva: This phrase means "like the wind." It suggests the idea of freedom and movement. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of dreams and aspirations, where individuals are encouraged to be adaptable and resilient, much like the wind that flows freely.
  • sūnṛtānām: This translates to "of the truthful" or "of the righteous." It emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity. In everyday life, being truthful can build trust in relationships, whether personal or professional, leading to stronger connections and collaborations.
  • udarke: This means "in the awakening" or "in the rise." It signifies the importance of awareness and enlightenment. In modern life, this can relate to personal growth and self-improvement, where individuals seek knowledge and understanding to better themselves and their circumstances.
  • tā: This word means "those" or "that." It refers to the qualities or attributes being discussed in the mantra.
  • aśvadā: This translates to "the swift" or "the fast." It can symbolize agility and the ability to adapt quickly to changing situations. In real life, this can be seen in businesses that pivot rapidly in response to market demands or in athletes who train to enhance their speed and performance.
  • aśnavat: This means "like a horse." Horses are often associated with strength, power, and nobility. In life, this can represent the drive and determination needed to achieve one's goals, much like a racehorse striving to win a race.
  • somasutvā: This translates to "the essence of the moon" or "the nectar of the moon." It symbolizes nourishment and the finer aspects of life. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of beauty, art, and culture, which enrich our lives and provide joy and inspiration.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, courage, human experience, and the pursuit of truth and enlightenment. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, integrity, and adaptability in our lives. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to embrace new beginnings, support one another, and strive for excellence. By embodying the qualities expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
