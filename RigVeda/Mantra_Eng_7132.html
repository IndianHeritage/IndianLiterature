अयुजो असमो नृभिर एकः कृष्तीः अयास्यः |पूर्वीर अति प्र वावृधे विश्वा जातान्य ओजसा भद्राः इन्द्रस्य रातयः ||
ayujo asamo nṛbhir ekaḥ kṛṣṭīr ayāsyaḥ | pūrvīr ati pra vāvṛdhe viśvā jātāny ojasā bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • ayujo asamo nṛbhir ekaḥ kṛṣṭīr ayāsyaḥ: This phrase can be translated to mean "May we be united, strong, and without strife, like a single entity." Here, "ayujo" refers to being united or together, "asamo" means without strife or conflict, "nṛbhir" refers to people or humans, "ekaḥ" means one or singular, and "kṛṣṭīr" signifies strength or power. This part emphasizes the importance of unity and strength among people, suggesting that when individuals come together as one, they can achieve greater things.
  • pūrvīr ati pra vāvṛdhe: This translates to "The ancient ones have greatly increased." "Pūrvīr" refers to the ancestors or those who came before us, "ati" means greatly, and "pra vāvṛdhe" signifies growth or increase. This line highlights the wisdom and achievements of our predecessors, suggesting that we should learn from their experiences and strive to build upon their legacy.
  • viśvā jātāny ojasā bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ: This can be interpreted as "All beings are endowed with strength and auspiciousness, like the divine blessings of Indra." "Viśvā" means all or universal, "jātāny" refers to beings or creatures, "ojasā" signifies strength or vitality, "bhadrā" means auspicious or beneficial, and "indrasya rātayaḥ" refers to the blessings or grace of Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition. This part emphasizes the inherent strength and potential in all beings, suggesting that with divine blessings, we can achieve auspicious outcomes.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, strength, and the importance of learning from our ancestors. It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together, they can overcome challenges and achieve greatness. The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support in our endeavors.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Community Building: Encouraging collaboration and unity among community members to address social issues.
    • Teamwork in Organizations: Promoting a culture of teamwork in workplaces where individuals work together towards common goals.
    • Learning from History: Emphasizing the importance of understanding and learning from the experiences of past generations to make informed decisions in the present.
    • Spiritual Practices: Using the mantra in meditation or prayer to seek strength and auspiciousness in personal and collective endeavors.
