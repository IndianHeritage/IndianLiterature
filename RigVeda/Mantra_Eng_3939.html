यन् मन्यसे वरेण्यं इन्द्र द्युक्षं तद आ भर |विद्याम तस्य ते वयम् अकूपारस्य दावने ||
yan manyase vareṇyam indra dyukṣaṁ tad ā bhara | vidyāma tasya te vayam akūpārasya dāvane ||
  • yan manyase - "What you think" or "What you consider": This phrase emphasizes the power of thought and intention. In real life, our thoughts shape our actions and ultimately our reality. For example, a person who believes in their ability to succeed is more likely to take the necessary steps to achieve their goals.
  • vareṇyam - "Worthy of worship" or "Adorable": This term refers to something that is highly esteemed or revered. In a practical sense, it can relate to values or ideals that we hold dear, such as honesty, compassion, or wisdom. For instance, a community that values education may invest in schools and libraries, reflecting their reverence for knowledge.
  • indra - "Indra": In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and leadership. In modern contexts, this can symbolize the qualities of a good leader or a person who inspires others. For example, a manager who leads by example and motivates their team embodies the spirit of Indra.
  • dyukṣaṁ - "The one who is powerful" or "The one who is capable": This word highlights the importance of capability and strength in achieving one's goals. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome challenges through perseverance and skill, such as athletes training for a competition.
  • tad ā bhara - "Bring that to us": This phrase is a call for the manifestation of the qualities or blessings being sought. In practical terms, it can be interpreted as a request for support or guidance in our endeavors. For example, a student may seek the help of a mentor to achieve their academic goals.
  • vidyāma - "We know" or "We have knowledge": This emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding. In real life, knowledge is power; for instance, a well-informed citizen is better equipped to make decisions that affect their community.
  • tasya te vayam - "We are yours" or "We belong to you": This phrase signifies a sense of belonging and connection. In a community context, it reflects the idea of unity and collective responsibility, such as neighbors coming together to support each other during difficult times.
  • akūpārasya - "Of the one who is unyielding" or "Of the one who is steadfast": This term refers to resilience and determination. In real life, individuals who face adversity with strength and resolve, such as activists fighting for social justice, exemplify this quality.
  • dāvane - "In the fire" or "In the heat": This can symbolize challenges or trials that test our strength and character. For example, a person going through a difficult period in their life, such as a job loss, may emerge stronger and more resilient after overcoming the challenges they face.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, knowledge, and support in our lives. It highlights the importance of intention, reverence for values, and the power of community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts, seek knowledge, and support one another in overcoming challenges. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and empowered existence, both individually and collectively.
