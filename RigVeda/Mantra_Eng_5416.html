यद इन्द्र यावतस् त्वम् एतावद अहम् ईशीय |स्तोतारम् इद दिधिषेयं रदावसो न पापत्वाय रासीय ||
yad indra yāvatas tvam etāvad aham īśīya | stotāram id didhiṣeya radāvaso na pāpatvāya rāsīya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • yāvatas: as long as
    • tvam: you
    • etāvad: this much
    • aham: I
    • īśīya: may I be empowered
    • stotāram: a worshipper
    • id: this
    • didhiṣeya: may I attain
    • radāvaso: with the strength
    • na: not
    • pāpatvāya: to sin
    • rāsīya: to accumulate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking empowerment and strength.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to be a devoted worshipper and to attain the qualities that lead to righteousness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and avoiding sinful actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may invoke this mantra when facing ethical dilemmas, seeking guidance to make the right choices.
    • Students may recite this mantra before exams, asking for the strength to focus and perform well, while also maintaining integrity.
    • In personal relationships, one might reflect on this mantra to cultivate patience and understanding, avoiding actions that could lead to conflict.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us to strive for righteousness and to be mindful of our actions, ensuring that we do not accumulate negative karma.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to align our intentions with ethical values, fostering a sense of responsibility and integrity in our daily lives.
