महī वाम ऊतिर अश्विनā मयोभूर उता श्रामं धिष्ण्यā सं रिणीथाḥ |अथा युवाम इद अह्वयत पुरंधर आगच्छतं सīm वृषणाव अवोभिः ||
mahī vām ūtir aśvinā mayobhūr uta srāmaṁ dhiṣṇyā saṁ riṇīthaḥ | athā yuvām id ahvayat puraṁdhir āgacchataṁ sīṁ vṛṣaṇāv avobhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahī: great, vast, or expansive
    • vām: your
    • ūtir: support or assistance
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities of medicine and health)
    • mayobhūr: may I be endowed with strength or power
    • uta: and
    • srāmaṁ: effort or labor
    • dhiṣṇyā: with wisdom or intellect
    • saṁ: together
    • riṇīthaḥ: may you grant
    • athā: then
    • yuvām: you both
    • id: now
    • ahvayat: I invoke or call upon
    • puraṁdhir: O master of the house or protector
    • āgacchataṁ: may you come
    • sīṁ: with strength
    • vṛṣaṇāv: with vigor or energy
    • avobhiḥ: with your blessings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, the divine twin brothers known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity.
    • The speaker seeks their support and assistance in achieving strength and wisdom in their endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine help in times of need, reflecting a belief in the interconnectedness of human effort and divine grace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for an important exam, students often pray for guidance and strength, similar to invoking the Ashvins for support.
    • In healthcare, practitioners may call upon divine assistance for healing, paralleling the Ashvins' role as deities of medicine.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often seek blessings from higher powers, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance in our lives, particularly in times of effort and struggle.
    • It teaches us that while we must put in our own efforts, invoking higher powers can provide the necessary support and strength to overcome challenges.
    • In modern life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from personal challenges to collective endeavors, reminding us of the balance between human effort and divine grace.
