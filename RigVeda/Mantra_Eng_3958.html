उप वा एषे वन्द्येभिः शूषैः प्र यह्वी दिवश् चितयद्भिर् अर्कैः |उषासानक्तā विदुषीव विष्वम् आ हā वहतो मर्त्याय यज्ञम् ||
upa va eṣe vandyebhiḥ śūṣaiḥ pra yahvī divaś citayadbhir arkaiḥ | uṣāsānaktā viduṣīva viśvam ā hā vahato martyāya yajñam ||
  • upa va eṣe vandyebhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "Come, let us praise with reverence." It signifies the importance of approaching the divine or the sacred with respect and devotion. In real life, this can be seen in various religious practices where individuals gather to offer prayers or perform rituals, such as in temples or during festivals.
  • śūṣaiḥ pra yahvī divaś citayadbhir arkaiḥ: This part means "with offerings, let us invoke the divine light." Here, "arkaiḥ" refers to offerings or light, symbolizing the illumination of knowledge and wisdom. In practical terms, this can be related to how people seek enlightenment through education, meditation, or community service, aiming to bring light into their lives and the lives of others.
  • uṣāsānaktā viduṣīva viśvam: This translates to "the dawn, like a wise one, spreads across the universe." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In everyday life, this can be reflected in how each day presents a chance for renewal, growth, and the pursuit of goals, much like how a new dawn brings light after darkness.
  • ā hā vahato martyāya yajñam: This means "may the offerings be carried forth for the mortal beings." It emphasizes the significance of selfless actions and contributions to society. In real life, this can be seen in charitable acts, community service, and the importance of giving back to the community, which fosters a sense of unity and support among individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence, the pursuit of knowledge, the promise of new beginnings, and the importance of selfless contributions to society. Its significance lies in reminding individuals to approach life with respect and gratitude, seek enlightenment, embrace new opportunities, and actively participate in the welfare of others. In a world where individualism often prevails, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for fostering community, compassion, and collective growth.
