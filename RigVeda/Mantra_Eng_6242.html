आ नूनम अश्विनां युवं वत्सस्य गन्तम अवसे |प्रास्मै यच्छतम अवृकम पृथु छर्दिर युयुतं या अरातयः ||
ā nūnam aśvinā yuvaṁ vatsasya gantam avase | prāsmai yacchatam avṛkam pṛthu cchardir yuyutaṁ yā arātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā nūnam: Indeed, certainly
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • vatsasya: of the calf (symbolizing nourishment and growth)
    • gantam: going, moving
    • avase: to the place, to the goal
    • prāsmai: to the one who is deserving
    • yacchatam: may you give
    • avṛkam: to the wolf (symbolizing danger or challenges)
    • pṛthu: vast, great
    • cchardir: strength, vigor
    • yuyutaṁ: may you engage, may you strive
    • yā: who
    • arātayaḥ: the enemies, obstacles
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the divine twins known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity.
    • The reference to "vatsasya" (the calf) symbolizes nurturing and the potential for growth, suggesting that the mantra is about fostering development and well-being.
    • The phrase "to the one who is deserving" indicates a call for blessings to be bestowed upon those who are worthy, emphasizing the importance of merit in receiving divine grace.
    • The mention of "avṛkam" (the wolf) represents challenges or adversities that one may face, while "pṛthu cchardir" (great strength) signifies the need for resilience and vigor in overcoming these obstacles.
    • Finally, the call to "yuyutaṁ" (may you engage) encourages active participation in the pursuit of goals, rather than passive waiting for blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when embarking on a new project. One must seek the blessings of mentors (akin to the Ashvins) and work diligently to overcome challenges (the wolves) while striving for success.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their growth (the calf) and actively engage in self-improvement, seeking knowledge and skills that will help them navigate life's adversities.
    • In health and wellness, the invocation of the Ashvins can be seen as a reminder to prioritize physical and mental well-being, ensuring that one is deserving of the blessings of health and vitality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance while actively engaging in one's own growth and overcoming challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the balance between receiving blessings and taking personal responsibility for one's actions and outcomes.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate resilience, seek support from others, and strive towards one's goals with determination and merit.
