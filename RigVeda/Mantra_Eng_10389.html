ईङ्खयन्तीर अपस्युव इन्द्रं जातम उपासते |भेजानासः सुवीर्यम् ||
īṅkhayantīr apasyuva indraṁ jātam upāsate | bhejānāsaḥ suvīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īṅkhayantīr: Those who are engaged in the act of invoking or calling upon.
    • apasyuva: Those who are unseen or hidden.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
    • jātam: Born or manifested.
    • upāsate: Worship or meditate upon.
    • bhejānāsaḥ: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • suvīryam: Good strength or valor.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of invoking the unseen forces, particularly the divine figure of Indra, who represents strength and power.
    • The phrase "īṅkhayantīr apasyuva" suggests a connection to the spiritual realm, where practitioners seek to engage with energies that are not immediately visible or tangible.
    • By calling upon Indra, the mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that govern strength and vitality in our lives.
    • The term "suvīryam" highlights the aspiration for good strength, which can be interpreted as both physical and moral strength in our daily lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for a competitive exam or a sports event, individuals often invoke their inner strength and resilience, akin to calling upon Indra for support.
    • In team settings, leaders may inspire their teams by invoking the spirit of collaboration and strength, much like the invocation of divine forces in this mantra.
    • During rituals or personal meditative practices, individuals may recite this mantra to seek guidance and strength from higher powers, fostering a sense of connection to the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the unseen forces that influence our lives and the importance of invoking strength and valor in our endeavors.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of both external and internal sources of power, encouraging individuals to seek support from the divine while also cultivating their own strength.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal challenges or collective efforts, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals towards resilience, unity, and the pursuit of excellence.
