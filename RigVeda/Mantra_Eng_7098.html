शोचाऽ शोचिष्ट दिदिहि विशे मयो रास्व स्तोत्रे महांऽऽसि |देवानां शर्मन् मम सन्तु सूरयः शत्रूषाहः स्वग्नयः ||
śocā śociṣṭha dīdihi viśe mayo rāsva stotre mahām̐ asi | devānāṁ śarman mama santu sūrayaḥ śatrūṣāhaḥ svagnayaḥ ||
  • śocā: This word can be interpreted as "purity" or "brightness." It signifies the importance of maintaining a pure and clear mind, akin to how one might strive for clarity in their thoughts and actions in daily life.
  • śociṣṭha: This term refers to "the most pure" or "the best of purity." It emphasizes the highest standards of purity, which can be related to ethical living and moral integrity in our interactions with others.
  • dīdihi: This word means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It suggests the idea of enlightenment and knowledge, similar to how education and wisdom can illuminate our paths in life.
  • viśe: This translates to "in the special" or "in the unique." It highlights the significance of recognizing and valuing the unique aspects of life, such as individual talents and experiences.
  • mayo: This word means "of me" or "my." It personalizes the mantra, indicating that the qualities of purity and illumination are sought for oneself.
  • rāsva: This term can mean "short" or "brief." It may refer to the fleeting nature of life and experiences, reminding us to cherish each moment.
  • stotre: This means "in praise" or "in worship." It signifies the importance of gratitude and reverence in our lives, akin to how we might express appreciation for the people and experiences that enrich our lives.
  • mahām̐: This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It emphasizes the power of the divine and the greatness of the forces that govern our existence.
  • asi: This means "you are" or "you exist." It affirms existence and presence, reminding us of our own being and the interconnectedness of all life.
  • devānāṁ: This translates to "of the gods." It invokes the divine, suggesting that the qualities being sought are aligned with higher spiritual principles.
  • śarman: This word means "happiness" or "bliss." It indicates the pursuit of joy and contentment in life, akin to how we seek fulfillment in our personal and professional endeavors.
  • mama: This means "my." It personalizes the pursuit of happiness, indicating that it is a personal journey.
  • santu: This translates to "may they be." It expresses a wish or prayer for the fulfillment of desires.
  • sūrayaḥ: This word means "the wise" or "the enlightened." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in achieving happiness and success.
  • śatrūṣāhaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "overcoming enemies" or "defeating adversaries." It signifies the need to confront challenges and obstacles in life, much like how individuals face personal or professional adversities.
  • svagnayaḥ: This means "with fire" or "fiery." It suggests passion and intensity in our pursuits, akin to how one might approach their goals with enthusiasm and vigor.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of purity, wisdom, and happiness while acknowledging the challenges we face. It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth and enlightenment, recognizing the fleeting nature of life and the importance of gratitude. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and spiritual practices. By embodying the qualities of purity, wisdom, and resilience, individuals can navigate life's challenges and cultivate a fulfilling existence.
