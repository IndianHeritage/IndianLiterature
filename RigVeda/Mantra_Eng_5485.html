आ यत साकं यशसो वावशानाः सरस्वती सप्तती सिंधुमाता |याḥ सुष्वयन्त सुदुघाḥ सुधारा अभि स्वेन पयसा पीप्यानाः ||
ā yat sākaṁ yaśaso vāvaśānāḥ sarasvatī saptathī sindhumātā | yāḥ suṣvayanta sudughāḥ sudhārā abhi svena payasā pīpyānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yat: That which is
    • sākaṁ: Together
    • yaśaso: Of fame or glory
    • vāvaśānāḥ: Those who are endowed
    • sarasvatī: Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge and wisdom
    • saptathī: The seventh
    • sindhumātā: Mother of the river (often referring to the sacred rivers)
    • yāḥ: Who
    • suṣvayanta: Who are well-nourished
    • sudughāḥ: Good milkers
    • sudhārā: Good nourishment
    • abhi: With
    • svena: Their own
    • payasā: Milk
    • pīpyānāḥ: Who are to be drunk
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities associated with Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge, and the nurturing aspects of nature.
    • The phrase "ā yat sākaṁ yaśaso vāvaśānāḥ" suggests a collective pursuit of glory and fame, emphasizing the importance of unity in achieving success.
    • The reference to "sindhumātā" highlights the reverence for rivers, symbolizing the flow of knowledge and sustenance in life.
    • The qualities of being "suṣvayanta" and "sudughāḥ" indicate the importance of nourishment, both physical and intellectual, in fostering growth and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational institutions, the collective effort of students and teachers can lead to a nurturing environment where knowledge flourishes, akin to the blessings of Saraswati.
    • Communities that come together to support local rivers and ecosystems reflect the reverence for "sindhumātā," recognizing the importance of nature in sustaining life.
    • In families, the nurturing aspect of parents providing "sudhārā" (good nourishment) to their children is essential for their overall development and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, knowledge, and nourishment in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor the sources of knowledge and sustenance in our lives, whether they be educational, natural, or familial.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous existence, fostering growth in both individual and collective endeavors.
