अच्छा मा इन्द्रं मतयः स्वर्विदः सदृचीर विश्वा उषतीर अनूषत | परि स्वजन्ते जनयो यथा पतिम मर्यं न शुन्द्युम मगवानम् ऊतये ||
acchā ma indram matayaḥ svarvidaḥ sadhrīcīr viśvā uśatīr anūṣata | pari ṣvajante janayo yathā patim maryaṁ na śundhyum maghavānam ūtaye ||
  • acchā ma indram matayaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May we have the wisdom of Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call for guidance and intelligence in decision-making, akin to seeking the advice of a wise leader or mentor.
  • svarvidaḥ sadhrīcīr: This means "those who know the truth." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our lives. For example, in a workplace, having knowledgeable team members can lead to better outcomes and innovations.
  • viśvā uśatīr anūṣata: This translates to "all beings are united in harmony." It highlights the significance of community and cooperation. In real life, this can be seen in collaborative projects where diverse individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as community service initiatives.
  • pari ṣvajante janayo: This means "the people come together." It signifies unity and collective effort. An example of this is seen during festivals or national celebrations where people unite to celebrate their culture and heritage.
  • yathā patim maryaṁ: This translates to "as a husband to his wife." It reflects the idea of mutual respect and partnership in relationships. In real life, this can be applied to any partnership, whether in marriage or business, where both parties support and uplift each other.
  • na śundhyum maghavānam ūtaye: This means "may we not be deprived of the blessings of the mighty." It is a plea for abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking opportunities for growth and success in various aspects of life, such as career advancement or personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, unity, and mutual respect in our lives. It serves as a reminder that by seeking knowledge and working together harmoniously, we can achieve greater success and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, nurturing relationships, and fostering community spirit. Ultimately, it encourages us to strive for collective well-being and prosperity, ensuring that we do not lose sight of the blessings that come from collaboration and understanding.
