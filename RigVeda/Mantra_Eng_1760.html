मन्त्र: चत्वारि वाक् परिमिता पदानि तानि विदुर ब्राह्मणा ये मनिषिणः |गुहा त्रिणि निहिता नेङ्गयन्ति तुरीयं वाचो मनुष्याः वदन्ति ||
catvāri vāk parimitā padāni tāni vidur brāhmaṇā ye manīṣiṇaḥ | guhā trīṇi nihitā neṅgayanti turīyaṁ vāco manuṣyā vadanti ||
  • catvāri vāk parimitā padāni: This phrase translates to "the four words that are limited in number." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to the four essential aspects of speech or communication that are fundamental to human interaction.
  • tāni vidur brāhmaṇā ye manīṣiṇaḥ: This means "those who know them are the wise ones, the Brāhmaṇas." Here, the Brāhmaṇas symbolize the learned and knowledgeable individuals who understand the deeper meanings of these words and their implications in life.
  • guhā trīṇi nihitā neṅgayanti: This translates to "three hidden caves do not move." The "three hidden caves" can be interpreted as the three layers of consciousness or understanding that are often obscured from ordinary perception.
  • turīyaṁ vāco manuṣyā vadanti: This means "the fourth (state) of speech is spoken by humans." The term "turīya" refers to a higher state of consciousness or awareness that transcends ordinary speech and thought.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper aspects of communication and consciousness. It suggests that while we may engage in everyday speech, there are profound truths and insights that lie beneath the surface.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, effective communication is crucial. This mantra encourages us to look beyond mere words and to seek the underlying meanings in our interactions. For example, in conflict resolution, understanding the emotions and intentions behind words can lead to more meaningful dialogue and resolution.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder that true wisdom lies in the ability to perceive and articulate the deeper truths of existence. By striving to understand and communicate these truths, we can enhance our relationships and foster a greater sense of connection with others.
