अभ्यर्ष सहस्रिणं रयिं गोमन्तम अश्विनम् |अभि वाजम् उता श्रवः ||
abhy arṣa sahasriṇaṁ rayiṁ gomantam aśvinam | abhi vājam uta śravaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhy arṣa sahasriṇaṁ rayiṁ gomantam aśvinam | abhi vājam uta śravaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhy: towards, for
    • arṣa: the seer, sage
    • sahasriṇaṁ: of a thousand, numerous
    • rayiṁ: wealth, prosperity
    • gomantam: possessing cows, symbolizing abundance
    • aśvinam: the Aśvins, divine twin horsemen, representing health and vitality
    • abhi: towards, for
    • vājam: strength, power
    • uta: and, also
    • śravaḥ: fame, glory
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Aśvins, who are considered the divine physicians in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "sahasriṇaṁ rayiṁ" emphasizes the desire for abundant wealth and prosperity, suggesting that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses health and well-being.
    • "Gomantam" signifies the importance of cattle in Vedic society, representing sustenance and economic stability.
    • The mantra calls for strength and fame, indicating that one should strive for not just material success but also for recognition and respect in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of holistic success, where individuals seek not only financial stability but also health and social recognition.
    • For instance, a successful entrepreneur may embody the principles of this mantra by building a thriving business (rayiṁ) while also contributing to community health initiatives (aśvinam).
    • In personal life, one might apply this mantra by focusing on a balanced lifestyle that includes physical fitness (strength) and community involvement (fame).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, health, and social standing.
    • It encourages individuals to seek prosperity not just for themselves but for the greater good, aligning personal success with community welfare.
    • In essence, it highlights the importance of a well-rounded life, where material gains are complemented by health and social contributions.
