त्वं नो अग्न आयुषु त्वं देवेषु पूर्व्य वस्व एक इराज्यसि |त्वाम आपः parisrutaḥ परि यन्ति स्वसेतवो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
tvaṁ no agna āyuṣu tvaṁ deveṣu pūrvya vasva eka irajyasi | tvām āpaḥ parisrutaḥ pari yanti svasetavo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • tvaṁ no agna āyuṣu: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni, are our life." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, symbolizing energy, vitality, and the essence of life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of maintaining our physical and mental health, as they are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • tvaṁ deveṣu pūrvya vasva: This means "You are the foremost among the gods." Agni is revered as a primary deity in Vedic traditions, signifying the importance of fire in rituals and daily life. In modern terms, this can be likened to the respect we give to foundational elements in our lives, such as family, education, and culture, which guide our actions and decisions.
  • eka irajyasi: This translates to "You alone are the one who is invoked." It emphasizes the singular importance of Agni in rituals and prayers. In everyday life, this can be seen in how individuals often turn to a single source of inspiration or guidance, whether it be a mentor, a spiritual figure, or a personal belief system.
  • tvām āpaḥ parisrutaḥ: This means "The waters flow towards you." Water symbolizes nourishment and sustenance. In a practical sense, this can be related to how we seek resources and support in our lives, whether it be emotional, financial, or spiritual, and how these resources are essential for our growth and well-being.
  • pari yanti svasetavo: This translates to "They come to you from all directions." This signifies the universal appeal and acceptance of Agni. In real life, this can be compared to how people from various backgrounds and cultures come together for a common cause or purpose, highlighting the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • nabhantām anyake same: This means "May you protect us from all that is harmful." It is a plea for safety and protection. In contemporary life, this can be related to the need for security in various forms—physical, emotional, and social. It underscores the importance of creating safe environments for ourselves and others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the reverence for Agni as a source of life, guidance, and protection. Its significance lies in the recognition of foundational elements in our lives—health, unity, and safety. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives, emphasizing the importance of nurturing relationships, seeking guidance, and creating safe spaces for growth and development. By invoking these principles, we can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
