एतावतस्त इमहा इन्द्र सुम्नस्य गोमतः |यथा प्रावो मघवन मेध्यातिथिं यथा निपातिथिं धनें ||
etāvatas ta īmaha indra sumnasya gomataḥ | yathā prāvo maghavan medhyātithiṁ yathā nīpātithiṁ dhane ||
  • etāvatas: This word can be translated as "of this much" or "to this extent." It signifies a measure or a limit.
  • ta: This is a pronoun meaning "that" or "those," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • īmaha: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request directed towards a deity.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • sumnasya: This translates to "of auspiciousness" or "of good fortune," suggesting a positive outcome or blessing.
  • gomataḥ: This word means "of the cow," which is a symbol of wealth, nourishment, and sustenance in Vedic culture.
  • yathā: This means "as" or "like," used to draw a comparison or analogy.
  • prāvo: This can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," emphasizing the act of summoning divine presence.
  • maghavan: This refers to "the bounteous one," often used to describe Indra as a giver of wealth and prosperity.
  • medhyātithiṁ: This translates to "the guest who is worthy," indicating respect and honor towards guests, a significant aspect of Vedic hospitality.
  • yathā: Again, this word is used for comparison.
  • nīpātithiṁ: This means "the guest who is to be honored," further emphasizing the importance of treating guests with reverence.
  • dhane: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the material and spiritual abundance sought in life.

The mantra essentially invokes Indra, asking for blessings of auspiciousness and prosperity, particularly in the context of hospitality and the treatment of guests. It emphasizes the importance of honoring those who come to our homes, reflecting a deep-rooted cultural value in Vedic society.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During family gatherings or celebrations, where guests are treated with respect and offered the best of what one has.
  • In business, where honoring clients and partners can lead to fruitful relationships and prosperity.
  • In community events, where the spirit of hospitality fosters unity and goodwill among people.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings in the realm of hospitality and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the values of respect, generosity, and the importance of nurturing relationships, which are essential for a harmonious and prosperous life.
