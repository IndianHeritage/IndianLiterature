भास्वती नेत्री सूनृतानाम् अचेति चित्रा वि दुरो न आवः |प्रार्प्या जगद व्य उ नो रायो अख्यद उषा अजिगर भुवनानि विश्वा ||
bhāsvatī netrī sūnṛtānām aceti citrā vi duro na āvaḥ | prārpyā jagad vy u no rāyo akhyad uṣā ajīgar bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • bhāsvatī: This word translates to "radiant" or "illuminating." It signifies the light of knowledge and wisdom that dispels ignorance.
  • netrī: Meaning "eye" or "vision," it symbolizes perception and insight, suggesting the importance of clarity in understanding.
  • sūnṛtānām: This term refers to "those who speak the truth" or "truthful ones," emphasizing the value of honesty and integrity in communication.
  • aceti: This can be interpreted as "not forgetting" or "not losing sight of," indicating the need to remain aware and mindful.
  • citrā: Meaning "varied" or "colorful," it represents the diversity of life and experiences that enrich our understanding.
  • vi duro: This phrase can be understood as "not far away" or "accessible," suggesting that wisdom and truth are within reach if we seek them earnestly.
  • na āvaḥ: This means "we do not call" or "we do not invoke," indicating a sense of humility in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • prārpyā: This translates to "having attained" or "having reached," signifying the achievement of goals through effort and dedication.
  • jagad: Meaning "the world" or "universe," it encompasses all of existence and the interconnectedness of life.
  • vy u no: This can be interpreted as "may we be blessed" or "may we receive," reflecting a desire for abundance and prosperity.
  • rāyo: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • akhyad: Meaning "revealed" or "disclosed," it suggests that knowledge and truth are unveiled through sincere inquiry.
  • uṣā: This translates to "dawn" or "new beginnings," symbolizing hope and the potential for renewal.
  • ajīgar: This term can be interpreted as "the great serpent," often representing transformation and the cyclical nature of life.
  • bhuvanāni: Meaning "worlds" or "realms," it signifies the various dimensions of existence and consciousness.
  • viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal," emphasizing inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, truth, and prosperity while remaining mindful of the diverse experiences of life. It encourages individuals to pursue wisdom with integrity and humility, recognizing that enlightenment is accessible to all who earnestly seek it. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, ethical communication, and fostering a sense of community. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
