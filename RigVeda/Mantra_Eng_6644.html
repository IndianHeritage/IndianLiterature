प्र वः शंसाम्य अद्रुहः संस्थ उपस्तुतीनाम् | न तं धूर्तिर वरुण मित्र मर्त्यं यो वो धामभ्यः 'विदत् ||
pra vaḥ śaṁsāmy adruhaḥ saṁstha upastutīnām | na taṁ dhūrtir varuṇa mitra martyaṁ yo vo dhāmabhyo 'vidhat ||
  • pra vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "let us speak" or "let us express." It signifies the importance of communication and sharing thoughts.
  • śaṁsāmy: This means "I praise" or "I extol." It reflects the act of acknowledging and honoring the divine or the virtues of others.
  • adruhaḥ: This term translates to "without malice" or "non-harmful." It emphasizes the value of kindness and the absence of ill will in our interactions.
  • saṁstha: This can be understood as "established" or "firm." It suggests stability and the importance of a solid foundation in relationships and communities.
  • upastutīnām: This refers to "those who are being praised" or "the worthy." It highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.
  • na taṁ: This means "not him" or "not that." It indicates a rejection of negativity or harmful actions.
  • dhūrtir: This translates to "deceit" or "dishonesty." It warns against the dangers of treachery and the importance of integrity.
  • varuṇa: Varuṇa is a deity associated with cosmic order and truth. This invokes the idea of divine oversight and moral authority.
  • mitra: Meaning "friend," it emphasizes the value of friendship and supportive relationships in life.
  • martyaṁ: This refers to "mortal" or "human." It reminds us of our human condition and the need for compassion towards one another.
  • yo vo: This means "who you" or "who belongs to you," indicating a connection or relationship with the divine or the community.
  • dhāmabhyo: This translates to "from the realms" or "from the abodes," suggesting a connection to higher principles or spiritual realms.
  • 'vidhat: This means "to create" or "to establish." It signifies the act of forming or shaping our reality through our actions and intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of communication, praise, and integrity in our interactions with others. It advocates for a life lived without malice, promoting kindness and stability in relationships. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering a positive work environment, nurturing friendships, and building communities based on trust and respect. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society, reflecting the values of truth and friendship as embodied by the deities Varuṇa and Mitra. This mantra serves as a reminder to uphold moral values and to recognize the worthiness of those around us, ultimately leading to a more compassionate and just world.
