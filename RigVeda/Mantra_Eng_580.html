ये चिद्धि त्वां ऋषयः पूर्व ऊतये जुहूरेऽवसे महि |सां नः स्तोमां अभि गृणीहि राधसोषः शुक्रेण शोचिषा ||
ye cid dhi tvām ṛṣayaḥ pūrva ūtaye juhūre 'vase mahi | sā naḥ stomām̐ abhi gṛṇīhi rādhasoṣaḥ śukreṇa śociṣā ||
  • Translation: "Those sages of old, who have known you, O divine one, have offered their prayers to you. May you, O radiant one, accept our hymns and grant us prosperity and brilliance."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: have known
    • tvām: you
    • ṛṣayaḥ: sages or seers
    • pūrva: of old
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of offering
    • juhūre: have offered
    • 'vase: to you
    • mahi: great or mighty
    • sā: that
    • naḥ: our
    • stomām̐: hymns or praises
    • abhi: towards
    • gṛṇīhi: accept or sing
    • rādhasoṣaḥ: of prosperity or abundance
    • śukreṇa: with brilliance or purity
    • śociṣā: shining or illuminating
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the wisdom of ancient sages who have sought the divine.
    • It reflects a deep respect for tradition and the knowledge passed down through generations.
    • The invocation of the divine to accept our hymns signifies a desire for connection and blessings.
    • The mention of prosperity and brilliance highlights the aspiration for a fulfilling and enlightened life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude, where individuals acknowledge the contributions of their ancestors and mentors.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may invoke the wisdom of past leaders to guide their strategies and decisions, seeking to achieve success and innovation.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on the teachings of great thinkers and apply those lessons to enhance their own lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, tradition, and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom from the past while striving for prosperity and enlightenment in the present.
    • By invoking the divine and acknowledging the sages, we open ourselves to receiving guidance and blessings in our endeavors.
