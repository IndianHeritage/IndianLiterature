स्वयं सो अस्मद ā निदो वधैर अजेता दुरमतिम् |अव श्रवेद घशंसो 'वतारम् अव क्षुद्रम इव श्रवेत्त ||
svayaṁ so asmad ā nido vadhair ajeta durmatim | ava sraved aghaśaṁso 'vataram ava kṣudram iva sravet ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svayaṁ: oneself
    • so: he/that
    • asmad: us
    • ā: towards
    • nido: without any harm
    • vadhair: by killing
    • ajeta: may not conquer
    • durmatim: the one with bad intentions
    • ava: down
    • sraved: may flow
    • aghaśaṁso: the one who is free from sin
    • 'vataram: may descend
    • ava: down
    • kṣudram: the small or insignificant
    • iva: like/as
    • sravet: may flow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of self-empowerment and the importance of maintaining a positive mindset.
    • It emphasizes that one should not be conquered by negative thoughts or harmful intentions.
    • The phrase "may flow down" suggests a release of negativity, allowing positive energy to take its place.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, if an employee faces criticism or negativity from colleagues, this mantra encourages them to focus on their strengths and not let negative opinions affect their performance.
    • In personal relationships, when faced with misunderstandings or conflicts, one can use this mantra to remind themselves to rise above the negativity and foster understanding and compassion.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire resilience and positivity in the face of adversity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as overcoming personal challenges, dealing with negativity in social interactions, and maintaining a constructive mindset in professional environments.
    • By internalizing the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling life, free from the burdens of negativity.
