एवेद एषा पुरुतमा दृष्टे कं नाजामिं न परि वृणक्ति जामिम् |अरेपसां तन्वा शाशदानां नार्भाद ईषते न महो विभाति ||
eved eṣā purutamā dṛśe kaṁ nājāmiṁ na pari vṛṇakti jāmim | arepasā tanvā3 śāśadānā nārbhād īṣate na maho vibhātī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eved: indeed, this
    • eṣā: this, she
    • purutamā: most excellent, supreme
    • dṛśe: to the sight, vision
    • kaṁ: whom
    • nājāmiṁ: I do not know
    • na: not
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • vṛṇakti: chooses, selects
    • jāmim: offspring, progeny
    • arepasā: with a body, physical form
    • tanvā: with a body, in form
    • śāśadānā: bestowing immortality
    • nārbhād: not to be feared
    • īṣate: shines, radiates
    • na: not
    • maho: of the great, vast
    • vibhātī: shines, manifests
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme essence that is beyond comprehension and selection.
    • It reflects on the nature of existence and the divine, suggesting that the ultimate reality is not something that can be easily known or chosen.
    • The phrase "bestowing immortality" indicates a connection to the eternal nature of the soul and the divine.
    • It emphasizes that true understanding and connection to the divine cannot be grasped through mere physical or superficial means.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, individuals often seek deeper understanding and connection beyond material success. This mantra encourages looking beyond the surface.
    • In relationships, the essence of love and connection cannot be fully understood or chosen; it is something that unfolds naturally and profoundly.
    • In spiritual practices, the pursuit of enlightenment often leads individuals to realize that the divine cannot be confined to rituals or dogmas, but is an experience that transcends understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the limitations of human understanding and the vastness of the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek deeper truths and connections in life, rather than being satisfied with superficial knowledge.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, relationships, and spiritual journeys, urging us to embrace the mystery of existence and the divine.
