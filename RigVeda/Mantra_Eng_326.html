सम इन्द्र गदर्भं मृण नुवन्तं पापयामुयाः |आ तु न इन्द्र शंसय गोष्व अश्वेषु शुभ्रिषु सहस्रेषु तुवीमघ ||
sam indra gardabham mṛṇa nuvantam pāpayāmuyā | ā tū na indra śaṁsaya goṣv aśveṣu śubhriṣu sahasreṣu tuvīmagha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Indra, who drives away the evil, grant us strength and prosperity. May we not be hindered by doubts, and may we be blessed with abundance in our herds and horses."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Indra: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • Gardabham: This term refers to something that is burdensome or difficult, often associated with challenges or adversities.
    • Mṛṇa: This word means to drive away or to dispel, indicating a desire to remove negativity or obstacles.
    • Pāpayāmuyā: This term signifies the removal of sins or negative influences, suggesting a plea for purification and protection.
    • Śaṁsaya: This means doubt or uncertainty, highlighting the importance of faith and confidence in one's endeavors.
    • Goṣv: Refers to cattle or herds, which are symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • Aśveṣu: This refers to horses, which are also symbols of strength and status.
    • Śubhriṣu: This term means auspicious or favorable, indicating a desire for good fortune.
    • Tuvīmagha: This signifies abundance or plenty, emphasizing the wish for prosperity in all aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions and abundant harvests. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges such as drought or pests.
    • In business, entrepreneurs may face uncertainties and doubts about their ventures. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and clarity, encouraging them to push through obstacles and strive for success.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter difficulties in relationships or health. The invocation of Indra in this mantra can inspire one to seek support and guidance, fostering resilience and positivity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention to overcome challenges and doubts. It emphasizes the importance of strength, prosperity, and abundance in various aspects of life. By invoking Indra, individuals can find the courage to face adversities, seek blessings for their endeavors, and cultivate a mindset of positivity and abundance. This mantra is applicable in daily life, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or communal efforts, reminding us of the power of faith and the pursuit of prosperity.
