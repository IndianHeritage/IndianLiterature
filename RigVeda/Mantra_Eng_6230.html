पुरुमन्द्रा पुरूवासू मनोतरा रयीणाम् |स्तोमं मे अश्विनाव इम अभि वह्नी अनूषाताम् ||
purumandrā purūvasū manotarā rayīṇām | stomam me aśvināv imam abhi vahnī anūṣātām ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • purumandrā: Refers to the "greatness" or "vastness" of the universe.
    • purūvasū: Indicates "abundance" or "riches," often associated with prosperity.
    • manotarā: Suggests "thoughts" or "intentions," emphasizing the power of the mind.
    • rayīṇām: Means "wealth" or "prosperity," often linked to material and spiritual abundance.
    • stomam: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating a call for recognition and reverence.
    • me: Translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or request.
    • aśvināv: Refers to the "Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.
    • imam: Means "this," pointing to the specific hymn or prayer being offered.
    • abhi: Translates to "towards" or "for," indicating direction or purpose.
    • vahnī: Refers to "fire," symbolizing transformation, energy, and purification.
    • anūṣātām: Suggests "eternal" or "everlasting," indicating a timeless quality.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the vastness and richness of the universe, recognizing the abundance that surrounds us.
    • It emphasizes the importance of our thoughts and intentions in manifesting prosperity and well-being.
    • The mention of the Ashvins highlights the connection to health and healing, suggesting that divine assistance is sought for physical and mental wellness.
    • The reference to fire symbolizes the transformative power of our desires and intentions, urging us to channel our energy towards positive outcomes.
    • Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our thoughts, actions, and the universe's abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can inspire entrepreneurs to focus on their intentions and thoughts, fostering a mindset of abundance that attracts success.
    • In personal health, individuals may use this mantra as a meditation to align their thoughts with their goals for well-being, invoking the Ashvins for support in their healing journey.
    • During challenging times, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to maintain a positive outlook and trust in the universe's abundance, encouraging resilience and hope.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the power of thoughts, the importance of health, and the abundance of the universe.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to business success, reminding us to align our intentions with the greater forces at play.
    • By invoking the Ashvins and the transformative power of fire, we are encouraged to seek divine guidance and harness our inner strength for positive change.
