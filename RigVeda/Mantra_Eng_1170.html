बृहस्पते सदा मि नः सुगं कृधि शं योर यत ते मनुर्हितं तद ईमहे | रथं न दुर्गाद वसवः सुदानवो विश्वस्मान नो अंहसो निष पिपर्तन ||
bṛhaspate sadam in naḥ sugaṁ kṛdhi śaṁ yor yat te manurhitaṁ tad īmahe | rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate sadam in naḥ sugaṁ kṛdhi śaṁ yor yat te manurhitaṁ tad īmahe | rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana ||"
  • Translation: "O Bṛhaspati, may you create for us a good abode; may we attain the auspiciousness that is your will. May the Vasus, who are the bringers of wealth, protect us from all difficulties and grant us strength and prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity representing wisdom, knowledge, and eloquence.
    • Sadam: A good or auspicious place or abode.
    • Naḥ: For us.
    • Sugaṁ: Goodness or auspiciousness.
    • Kṛdhi: Create or make.
    • Śaṁ: Auspiciousness or peace.
    • Yor: Of you (referring to Bṛhaspati).
    • Manurhitaṁ: That which is in accordance with the will of the divine.
    • Tad: That.
    • Īmahe: We aspire or we seek.
    • Rathaṁ: Chariot, symbolizing movement and progress.
    • Na: Not.
    • Durgād: From difficulties or obstacles.
    • Vasavaḥ: The Vasus, deities associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • Sudānavo: Those who bring good fortune.
    • Viśvasmān: All-encompassing or universal.
    • No: Our.
    • Aṁhaso: From darkness or ignorance.
    • Niṣ pipartana: May they protect or guard us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek wisdom and guidance to create a positive work environment. This mantra can be invoked to inspire leaders to make decisions that benefit all employees.
    • During challenging times, such as economic downturns, individuals and families may recite this mantra to seek protection and prosperity, hoping for divine intervention to overcome difficulties.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to foster unity and collective strength, encouraging members to work together towards common goals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine wisdom and protection. It emphasizes the importance of seeking auspiciousness and strength in the face of challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, community building, and organizational leadership. By reciting this mantra, individuals and groups can align themselves with positive energies, fostering an environment conducive to success and well-being.
