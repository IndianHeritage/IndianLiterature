जने न शेव आहूर्यः सन् मध्ये निषत्तो रण्वो दुरोणे ||
jane na śeva āhūryaḥ san madhye niṣatto raṇvo duroṇe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • jane: Refers to people or individuals.
    • na: Means 'not' or 'no'.
    • śeva: Can be interpreted as 'to serve' or 'to worship'.
    • āhūryaḥ: Refers to 'those who are called' or 'invoked'.
    • san: Means 'being' or 'existing'.
    • madhye: Means 'in the midst' or 'among'.
    • niṣatto: Refers to 'those who are steadfast' or 'firm'.
    • raṇvo: Can be interpreted as 'in battle' or 'in conflict'.
    • duroṇe: Refers to 'in the difficult situation' or 'in the hard times'.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of individuals who do not serve or worship in the midst of conflict or difficult situations.
    • It highlights the idea that those who are steadfast and firm in their beliefs or actions are crucial, especially when faced with challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, during a crisis, employees who remain committed and do not waver in their responsibilities are essential for overcoming challenges.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who stand firm and do not abandon their loved ones during tough times exemplify the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on steadfastness and commitment in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength is demonstrated not just in moments of ease but in times of struggle.
    • This principle can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community support, encouraging individuals to remain resolute and supportive during challenging times.
