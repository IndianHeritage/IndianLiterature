उर्व्यचसां महिनी असश्चता पिता माता च भुवनानि रक्षतः |सुधृष्टमे वपुष्ये न रोदसी पिता यत सीम अभि रूपैर आवासयत ||
uruvyacasā mahinī asaścatā pitā mātā ca bhuvanāni rakṣataḥ | sudhṛṣṭame vapuṣye3 na rodasī pitā yat sīm abhi rūpair avāsayat ||
  • Uruvyacasā: This term can be broken down into "uru" meaning vast or expansive, and "vyacasā" which refers to the act of speaking or proclaiming. Together, it suggests a vast proclamation or a call to the universe.
  • Mahinī: This word signifies greatness or vastness, often associated with the divine or cosmic forces that govern the universe.
  • Asaścatā: This term implies the idea of protection or safeguarding. It indicates a nurturing aspect, akin to a guardian watching over something precious.
  • Pitā mātā: These words mean father and mother, respectively. They symbolize parental figures, representing care, love, and guidance in life.
  • Bhuvanāni: This term refers to the worlds or realms, indicating the various aspects of existence and the universe.
  • Rakṣataḥ: This is derived from the root "rakṣ," meaning to protect or preserve. It emphasizes the act of safeguarding the worlds or realms.
  • Sudhṛṣṭame: This word can be interpreted as the most auspicious or favorable, suggesting a state of well-being and positivity.
  • Vapuṣye: This term refers to form or body, often associated with the divine or the essence of life.
  • Rodasī: This word means the sky or the heavens, representing the vastness above and the connection to the divine.
  • Sīm: This term refers to boundaries or limits, indicating the idea of containment or protection within defined spaces.
  • Rūpair: This word means forms or shapes, suggesting the various manifestations of existence.
  • Avāsayat: This term implies the act of dwelling or residing, indicating a sense of belonging or stability.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the vastness of the universe and the protective roles of parental figures in safeguarding the realms of existence. It highlights the importance of nurturing and protection in our lives, akin to how parents care for their children. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to the concept of community and family, where individuals look out for one another, creating a safe and supportive environment. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine protection that surrounds us, encouraging us to foster love, care, and responsibility in our relationships and communities.
