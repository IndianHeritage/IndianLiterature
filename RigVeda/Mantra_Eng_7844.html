तं उ त्वा वाजिनं नरो धीभिर विप्राः अवस्यवः |मृजन्ति देवतातये ||
tam u tvā vājinaṁ naro dhībhir viprā avasyavaḥ | mṛjanti devatātaye ||
  • tam u tvā vājinaṁ naro dhībhir viprā avasyavaḥ
    • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
    • u - This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
    • tvā - This means "you," indicating a direct address to the divine or a revered figure.
    • vājinaṁ - This term can be interpreted as "the one who grants strength" or "the powerful one," often associated with divine energy or blessings.
    • naro - This translates to "man" or "human," emphasizing the connection between the divine and humanity.
    • dhībhir - This word means "with wisdom" or "with intellect," highlighting the importance of knowledge and understanding.
    • viprā - This refers to "the wise" or "the learned," often denoting those who possess spiritual or intellectual insight.
    • avasyavaḥ - This means "we must" or "we are bound to," indicating a sense of duty or obligation.
  • mṛjanti devatātaye
    • mṛjanti - This translates to "they cleanse" or "they purify," suggesting a process of spiritual or moral purification.
    • devatātaye - This means "for the sake of the divine" or "in devotion to the gods," indicating that the act of cleansing is directed towards a higher purpose.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the relationship between humanity and the divine, highlighting the importance of wisdom and the pursuit of strength through knowledge. It suggests that individuals, especially the wise, have a duty to seek divine blessings and to purify themselves in the process. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to gain knowledge and wisdom, which can lead to personal empowerment and a stronger character.
  • In community settings, leaders and educators can embody the principles of this mantra by guiding others towards enlightenment and moral integrity.
  • In spiritual practices, rituals of cleansing and devotion can be performed to seek divine guidance and blessings, reinforcing the connection between the individual and the divine.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, the pursuit of strength, and the need for purification in our spiritual journeys.
