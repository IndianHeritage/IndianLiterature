मन्त्र: किम अङ्ग राध्रचोदनः सुन्वानस्यावितेद असि | कुवित स्व इन्द्र णः शकः ||
kim aṅga radhracodanaḥ sunvānasyāvited asi | kuvit sv indra ṇaḥ śakaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim aṅga: What indeed
    • radhracodanaḥ: is the cause of agitation or disturbance
    • sunvānasyāvited asi: You are the one who is well-versed in the truth
    • kuvit: What is the wrong or evil
    • sv: Your own
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • ṇaḥ: of us
    • śakaḥ: You are capable or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the source of disturbance or agitation in life.
    • It acknowledges the divine power of Indra, who is seen as a protector and a source of strength.
    • The mantra suggests that understanding the truth is essential to overcoming challenges.
    • It also implies that one should seek to identify and rectify wrongs in their life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, if there is a conflict among team members, asking "What is causing this disturbance?" can lead to a resolution.
    • In personal relationships, recognizing the truth behind misunderstandings can help in mending bonds.
    • In times of stress, turning to a mentor or a wise figure (akin to Indra) for guidance can provide clarity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking truth and understanding the root causes of disturbances in life.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and the impact of negativity, urging them to strive for clarity and resolution.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the divine support available to those who seek to overcome challenges with wisdom and strength.
