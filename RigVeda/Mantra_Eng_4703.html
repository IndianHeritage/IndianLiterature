अस्मा एतद् दिव्य आर्चेव मासा मिमिक्ष इन्द्रे न्य आयामि सोमः |जनं न धन्वन्न अभि सं यद आपः सत्रा वावृधुर हवनानि यज्ञैः ||
asmā etad divy a1rceva māsā mimikṣa indre ny ayāmi somaḥ | janaṁ na dhanvann abhi saṁ yad āpaḥ satrā vāvṛdhur havanāni yajñaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā: from us
    • etad: this
    • divy: divine
    • a1rceva: like the light
    • māsā: months
    • mimikṣa: I seek
    • indre: O Indra (the deity)
    • ny ayāmi: I go
    • somaḥ: the Soma (sacred drink)
    • janaṁ: people
    • na: not
    • dhanvann: with weapons
    • abhi: towards
    • saṁ: together
    • yad: that
    • āpaḥ: waters
    • satrā: sacrifices
    • vāvṛdhur: have increased
    • havanāni: offerings
    • yajñaiḥ: through sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for divine guidance and blessings from Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • The speaker seeks the divine light and wisdom, akin to the illumination that comes with the changing months, symbolizing the passage of time and the cycles of life.
    • The reference to Soma indicates a desire for spiritual nourishment and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective efforts in achieving prosperity and well-being, rather than relying solely on weapons or force.
    • The mention of waters and sacrifices highlights the significance of nature and the environment in sustaining life and fostering growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of seeking wisdom and guidance in decision-making, whether in personal or professional contexts.
    • Communities coming together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice, reflect the collective spirit emphasized in the mantra.
    • The practice of rituals and traditions in various cultures can be seen as a way to connect with the divine and seek blessings for prosperity and harmony.
    • In times of conflict, the mantra serves as a reminder that collaboration and understanding are more effective than aggression.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, the importance of community, and the role of rituals in fostering spiritual and material well-being.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless message that resonates in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for harmony, wisdom, and collective growth.
    • In a world often marked by division and conflict, the teachings of this mantra remind us of the power of unity and the pursuit of higher ideals.
