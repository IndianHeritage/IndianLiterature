विदानासो जनमनो वाजरत्ना उता ऋतुभिर ऋभवो मदयध्वम् |सं वो मदा अग्मता सम पुरंढिः सुवीराम अस्मे रयिम एरयध्वम् ||
vidānāso janmano vājaratnā uta ṛtubhir ṛbhavo mādayadhvam | saṁ vo madā agmata sam puraṁdhiḥ suvīrām asme rayim erayadhvam ||
  • vidānāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who know" or "the wise ones." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding one's actions and decisions.
  • janmano: This word means "of birth" or "of life." It signifies the beginning of existence and the journey that follows.
  • vājaratnā: Translated as "the diamond-like jewel," it symbolizes something precious and unbreakable, representing strength and resilience.
  • uta: This word means "and" or "also," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • ṛtubhir: This term refers to "seasons" or "periods," indicating the cyclical nature of life and the importance of timing in various endeavors.
  • ṛbhavo: Meaning "those who are strong" or "powerful," it highlights the qualities of strength and capability.
  • mādayadhvam: This can be interpreted as "let us rejoice" or "may we be joyful," suggesting a call to celebrate life and its experiences.
  • saṁ: This word means "together" or "with," emphasizing unity and collective action.
  • vo: This translates to "you" or "your," addressing the audience directly.
  • madā: Meaning "intoxication" or "ecstasy," it refers to a state of bliss or heightened awareness.
  • agmata: This term means "to come" or "to arrive," indicating a movement towards a goal or destination.
  • sam: This word means "together" or "completely," reinforcing the idea of unity.
  • puraṁdhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is wise" or "the intelligent one," referring to a person of great insight.
  • suvīrām: Meaning "the brave" or "the valiant," it highlights the qualities of courage and heroism.
  • asme: This word means "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal connection to the message.
  • rayim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," symbolizing abundance in various forms.
  • erayadhvam: This can be interpreted as "may you bestow" or "may you grant," suggesting a request for blessings or gifts.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, unity, and joy in life. It encourages individuals to come together, celebrate their existence, and seek prosperity while recognizing the cyclical nature of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, celebrations of achievements, or even in personal reflections on growth and resilience. By embodying the principles of knowledge, strength, and unity, individuals can navigate life's challenges and foster a sense of collective well-being.
