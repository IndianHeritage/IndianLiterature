प्रेद्धो अग्नि दीदिहि पुरो नोऽजस्रया सूर्यम्या यविष्ट |त्वां शश्वन्त उप यन्ति वाजाः ||
preddho agne dīdihi puro no 'jasrayā sūrmyā yaviṣṭha | tvāṁ śaśvanta upa yanti vājāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • preddho: O ignited (or kindled)
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dīdihi: shine or illuminate
    • puro: in front of us
    • no: our
    • 'jasrayā: with your brilliance
    • sūrmyā: with your strength
    • yaviṣṭha: the best or most excellent
    • tvāṁ: you
    • śaśvanta: eternal or everlasting
    • upa: towards
    • yanti: they approach
    • vājāḥ: the powerful or the strong
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking him to shine brightly in front of us with his brilliance and strength. It acknowledges Agni as the eternal source of power and invites the strong and powerful to approach him.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Hindu wedding, the sacred fire (Agni) is invoked as a witness to the vows taken by the couple. This reflects the importance of Agni as a source of light and strength in their new journey together.
    • In daily life, when facing challenges, individuals often seek inspiration and strength from their inner 'Agni'—the fire of determination and resilience that helps them overcome obstacles.
  • Significance and Application: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and strength from divine sources. In real life, it can be applied in various situations such as:
    • During difficult times, invoking the spirit of Agni can symbolize the inner strength needed to persevere.
    • In leadership, the qualities of Agni—brilliance, strength, and the ability to inspire—are essential for motivating others and leading effectively.
