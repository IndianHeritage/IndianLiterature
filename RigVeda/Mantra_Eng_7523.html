इन्द्रः स दामने कृत ओजिष्टः स मदे हितः |द्युन्मी श्लोकी स सोम्यः ||
indraḥ sa dāmane kṛta ojiṣṭhaḥ sa made hitaḥ | dyumnī ślokī sa somyaḥ ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sa dāmane kṛta: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is skilled in restraint." It emphasizes the importance of self-control and discipline in achieving success.
  • Ojiṣṭhaḥ: Meaning "the most powerful" or "the strongest," this word highlights the idea of supreme strength and capability.
  • Sa made hitaḥ: Translates to "he who is beneficial in his actions." This suggests that true strength is not just about power but also about using that power for the good of others.
  • Dyumnī: This word signifies "glory" or "brilliance," indicating that the actions of such a powerful being lead to honor and respect.
  • Ślokī: Refers to "verses" or "poetry," suggesting that the deeds of this powerful figure are worthy of being celebrated and remembered.
  • Sa somyaḥ: Meaning "he who is gentle" or "pleasant," this phrase indicates that true strength is complemented by kindness and gentleness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of true leadership and strength. It teaches that power should be coupled with restraint, benevolence, and gentleness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles where a leader must balance authority with compassion. For instance, a manager in a corporate setting can be powerful in decision-making but should also practice empathy towards their team members. This mantra serves as a reminder that the most effective leaders are those who wield their power wisely and for the benefit of others, ultimately leading to a legacy of respect and admiration.
