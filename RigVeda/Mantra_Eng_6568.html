तं वो वाजानाम् पतिम अहूमहि श्रवस्यवः | अप्रायुभिर यज्ञेभिर वावृधेन्यम् ||
taṁ vo vājānām patim ahūmahi śravasyavaḥ | aprāyubhir yajñebhir vāvṛdhenyam ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • vo - This means "to you," indicating a connection or offering to the divine.
  • vājānām - This term means "of the offerings" or "of the rewards," suggesting the importance of offerings in rituals.
  • patim - This translates to "lord" or "master," referring to the divine being who is the recipient of the offerings.
  • ahūmahi - This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a prayer or invocation to the divine.
  • śravasyavaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "of great fame" or "renowned," highlighting the glory and significance of the divine being.
  • aprāyubhir - This means "with long life" or "without shortness of life," emphasizing the desire for longevity and prosperity.
  • yajñebhir - This translates to "by the sacrifices" or "through the rituals," indicating the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • vāvṛdhenyam - This means "may it be increased" or "may it grow," expressing a wish for abundance and growth in life.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer invoking the divine lord of offerings, asking for blessings of longevity and abundance through rituals. It emphasizes the significance of connecting with the divine through offerings and sacrifices, which are seen as a means to attain prosperity and well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Offerings: Individuals can perform rituals and make offerings to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and success in their endeavors.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, this mantra can be recited during festivals or gatherings to invoke collective blessings and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Personal Reflection: One can use this mantra as a meditative tool, reflecting on the importance of gratitude and the desire for growth in personal and professional life.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking the divine for blessings and prosperity through rituals and offerings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the divine, and the significance of gratitude and intention in our daily lives.
