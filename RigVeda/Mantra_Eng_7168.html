यद वाऽप्रस्रवणे दिवो मादयासे स्वर्णरे |यद वाऽसमुद्रे अन्धसः ||
yad vā prasravaṇe divo mādayāse svarṇare | yad vā samudre andhasaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad vā prasravaṇe divo mādayāse svarṇare | yad vā samudre andhasaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Whatever flows from the heavens, bringing joy and golden light; whatever emerges from the depths of the ocean, hidden in darkness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad vā: "Whatever" - signifies openness to all possibilities.
    • prasravaṇe: "flowing" - refers to the movement of energy or blessings.
    • divo: "from the heavens" - symbolizes divine or spiritual sources.
    • mādayāse: "bringing joy" - indicates the positive impact of these blessings.
    • svarṇare: "golden light" - represents purity, wealth, and enlightenment.
    • samudre: "from the ocean" - signifies depth, mystery, and the subconscious.
    • andhasaḥ: "hidden in darkness" - refers to the unknown or unmanifested potential.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "flowing from the heavens" can be likened to moments of inspiration or creativity that seem to come from nowhere, such as a writer suddenly finding the perfect words to express their thoughts.
    • The "golden light" can be seen in achievements that bring joy, like receiving a promotion at work or completing a challenging project that brings satisfaction and recognition.
    • The "ocean" represents the vastness of human experience, where deep emotions and thoughts reside, similar to how people often find clarity and understanding through introspection or meditation.
    • "Hidden in darkness" can relate to the potential within each individual that may not be immediately visible, such as a talent or skill that is discovered later in life, like someone taking up painting in their retirement and finding joy in it.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine blessings and human experiences, highlighting the importance of being open to both the known and the unknown.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek joy and enlightenment from both external sources (like nature and divine inspiration) and internal depths (like personal reflection and hidden talents).
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during moments of decision-making, creative pursuits, or personal growth, reminding individuals to embrace all aspects of their journey.
