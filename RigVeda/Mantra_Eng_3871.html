आ प्र द्रव हरिवो मा वि वेनः पीशङ्गराते अभि नः सच्चस्व |नहि त्वद इन्द्र वस्यो अन्यद अस्त्य अमेनांश चिज जनिवतश चकर्थ ||
ā pra drava harivo mā vi venaḥ piśaṅgarāte abhi naḥ sacasva | nahi tvad indra vasyo anyad asty amenām̐ś cij janivataś cakartha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • pra: forward, towards
    • drava: flowing, liquid
    • harivo: of the horses, often symbolizing strength and power
    • mā: not
    • vi: apart, separate
    • venaḥ: the one who is powerful, often referring to a deity
    • piśaṅgarāte: with the yellowish hue, possibly referring to a specific aspect of a deity
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • naḥ: our
    • sacasva: may you be favorable, may you grant us strength
    • nahi: indeed, not
    • tvad: your
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and authority
    • vasyo: under control, subdued
    • anyad: other, different
    • asty: there is
    • amenām̐ś: a part of the non-physical, possibly referring to the spiritual aspect
    • cij: anything, whatever
    • janivataś: born, created
    • cakartha: you have done, you have created
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for divine favor and strength.
    • The speaker seeks to draw forth the power and blessings of the divine, represented by Indra, while emphasizing the importance of unity and collective strength.
    • The mention of "harivo" (horses) symbolizes the energy and vigor needed to overcome challenges.
    • The phrase "may you be favorable" indicates a desire for positive outcomes in endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, this mantra can be likened to a group of individuals coming together to achieve a common goal, invoking the spirit of collaboration and support.
    • In personal challenges, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance from a higher power, especially during difficult times.
    • In sports, athletes often invoke a sense of unity and strength among teammates, similar to the collective energy sought in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine support and the importance of unity in overcoming obstacles.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength can be drawn from both the divine and from collective efforts.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, teamwork, or community efforts, invoking such sentiments can lead to greater resilience and success.
