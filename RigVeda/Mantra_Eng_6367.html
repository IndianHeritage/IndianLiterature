तव द्यौर इन्द्र पौंश्यं पृथिवी वर्धति श्रवः |त्वाम आपः पर्वतासश च हिन्विरे ||
tava dyaur indra pauṁsyam pṛthivī vardhati śravaḥ | tvām āpaḥ parvatāsaś ca hinvire ||
  • tava dyaur - "Your sky": This phrase refers to the vastness of the sky, symbolizing the infinite possibilities and the divine presence that surrounds us.
  • indra - "Indra": In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • pauṁsyam - "of man": This term signifies humanity, emphasizing the connection between the divine and human existence.
  • pṛthivī - "earth": The earth represents stability, nourishment, and the physical realm where human life unfolds.
  • vardhati - "grows" or "flourishes": This word indicates growth, development, and the positive evolution of life.
  • śravaḥ - "fame" or "glory": This term refers to the reputation and honor that come from virtuous actions and achievements.
  • tvām - "you": This is a direct address to the divine, acknowledging the presence and influence of a higher power.
  • āpaḥ - "waters": Waters symbolize purity, life, and the essential element for survival and growth.
  • parvatāsaś - "mountains": Mountains represent strength, stability, and the challenges that one must overcome in life.
  • ca - "and": This conjunction connects the various elements of the mantra, indicating their interrelatedness.
  • hinvire - "may they bestow": This term expresses a wish or prayer for blessings and support from the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the relationship between the divine and the earthly realm, highlighting the importance of growth, strength, and the pursuit of glory through virtuous actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance in our endeavors, to appreciate the beauty of nature, and to strive for personal and communal growth. It encourages individuals to recognize their potential and the support they receive from the universe, urging them to overcome challenges and flourish in their pursuits. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community development projects, or even in moments of reflection and gratitude for the resources and support available in life.
