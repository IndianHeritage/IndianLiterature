तव स्वादिष्टाग्ने संदृष्टिर इदाचिद अह्न इदाचिद अक्तोः |श्रिये रुक्मो न रोचत उपाके ||
tava svādiṣṭhāgne saṁdṛṣṭir idā cid ahna idā cid aktoḥ | śriye rukmo na rocata upāke ||
  • tava svādiṣṭhāgne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, your delightful presence." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the warmth and light of a home, representing comfort and safety.
  • saṁdṛṣṭir idā cid ahna: This means "may your vision be present here and now." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our actions. For example, in a business meeting, being fully present can lead to better decision-making and collaboration.
  • idā cid aktoḥ: This translates to "may it be here and now." It reinforces the idea of immediacy and the significance of the present moment. In daily life, this can be seen in practices like meditation, where focusing on the present can reduce stress and enhance well-being.
  • śriye rukmo na rocata: This means "the radiant one does not shine for the sake of wealth." It suggests that true value lies beyond material wealth. In real life, this can be illustrated by individuals who prioritize community service and personal fulfillment over financial gain.
  • upāke: This term can be interpreted as "in the offering." It signifies the act of giving and sharing. In practical terms, this can relate to charitable acts or community support, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of presence, awareness, and the true essence of value beyond material wealth. In real life, it encourages individuals to focus on meaningful connections, mindfulness, and the importance of giving back to the community. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and purposeful life, fostering a sense of harmony and balance in both personal and communal spheres.
