इमे मां पीता यशसा उरुष्यवो रथं न गावाḥ सम अनाह पर्वसु |ते मां रक्षन्तु विस्रसश चरित्राद उता मां श्रामाद यवयन्त्व इन्दवः ||
ime mā pītā yaśasa uruṣyavo rathaṁ na gāvaḥ sam anāha parvasu | te mā rakṣantu visrasaś caritrād uta mā srāmād yavayantv indavaḥ ||
  • ime mā pītā yaśasa uruṣyavo rathaṁ na gāvaḥ sam anāha parvasu
    • ime - This word translates to "these" or "this." It indicates a focus on the present or immediate context.
    • mā - This means "not" or "do not," suggesting a negation or a request for protection.
    • pītā - Refers to "fathers" or "ancestors," symbolizing lineage and heritage.
    • yaśasa - This translates to "fame" or "glory," indicating a desire for recognition and honor.
    • uruṣyavo - This can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful," referring to individuals of great strength or capability.
    • rathaṁ - Means "chariot," which symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • na gāvaḥ - Translates to "not the cows," where "cows" often symbolize wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature.
    • sam anāha parvasu - This phrase can be understood as "together in the seasons," indicating harmony and unity in various phases of life.
  • te mā rakṣantu visrasaś caritrād uta mā srāmād yavayantv indavaḥ
    • te - This means "you" or "those," referring to divine entities or forces.
    • mā rakṣantu - Translates to "may they protect," indicating a plea for safeguarding and support.
    • visrasaś - This can mean "from the untruth" or "from falsehood," emphasizing the importance of truthfulness.
    • caritrād - Refers to "from actions" or "from conduct," highlighting the significance of one's deeds.
    • uta - This means "or," suggesting an alternative or additional request.
    • mā srāmād - Translates to "from fatigue" or "from exhaustion," indicating a desire for relief from burdens.
    • yavayantv - This can be interpreted as "may they grant" or "may they bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
    • indavaḥ - Refers to "the moon" or "the divine," symbolizing illumination, guidance, and tranquility.

Conclusion:

The mantra expresses a deep desire for protection and support from divine forces, emphasizing the importance of truthfulness and righteous conduct. It reflects a plea for safeguarding one's lineage and achievements while seeking relief from life's burdens. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • During challenging times, individuals may recite this mantra to seek divine protection and guidance.
  • In family gatherings or ceremonies, it can be invoked to honor ancestors and seek their blessings for prosperity and unity.
  • In moments of decision-making, it serves as a reminder to act with integrity and truthfulness.

Overall, the mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support while navigating the complexities of life, urging individuals to remain grounded in their values and heritage.
