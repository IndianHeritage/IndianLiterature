उत द्यावापृथिवी क्षत्रम् उरु बृहद् rodasī शरणं सुषुम्ने |महस् करथो वरिवो यथा नोऽस्मे क्षयाय धिषणे अनिहः ||
uta dyāvāpṛthivī kṣatram uru bṛhad rodasī śaraṇaṁ suṣumne | mahas karatho varivo yathā no 'sme kṣayāya dhiṣaṇe anehaḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī - Refers to "heaven and earth," symbolizing the duality of existence and the balance between the spiritual and material worlds.
  • kṣatram - This term means "power" or "authority," often associated with governance and protection.
  • uru - Translates to "vast" or "great," suggesting something expansive and significant.
  • bṛhad - Means "broad" or "large," reinforcing the idea of greatness and magnitude.
  • rodasī - Refers to "the two worlds" or "the sky," often interpreted as the boundaries of existence.
  • śaraṇaṁ - Means "refuge" or "shelter," indicating a place of safety and security.
  • suṣumne - This word can be understood as "in the right way" or "properly," suggesting the importance of following the correct path.
  • mahas - Translates to "greatness" or "majesty," often associated with divine qualities.
  • karatho - Means "chariots" or "vehicles," symbolizing movement and progress.
  • varivo - Refers to "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating a pursuit of the highest ideals.
  • yathā - Means "as" or "like," used to draw comparisons.
  • no - Translates to "us," indicating a collective or community aspect.
  • 'sme - This word can be interpreted as "we are," suggesting a state of being.
  • kṣayāya - Means "for destruction" or "for downfall," indicating a warning or caution against negative outcomes.
  • dhiṣaṇe - Refers to "the place of decision" or "the seat of authority," emphasizing the importance of wise governance.
  • anehaḥ - Translates to "without fear" or "fearless," suggesting a state of confidence and courage.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of the universe, emphasizing the balance between the spiritual and material realms. It highlights the importance of power and authority in governance, urging individuals to seek refuge in the right path while striving for greatness. The imagery of chariots symbolizes progress and movement towards higher ideals, while the caution against destruction serves as a reminder to make wise decisions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In leadership roles, where the balance of power and responsibility is crucial for effective governance.
  • In personal development, where individuals seek to find their path and purpose in life, ensuring they make decisions that lead to growth rather than downfall.
  • In community building, where collective efforts are made to create a safe and supportive environment for all members.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a profound reminder of the significance of balance, wise decision-making, and the pursuit of greatness in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to be fearless in their endeavors while remaining grounded in their values and responsibilities.
