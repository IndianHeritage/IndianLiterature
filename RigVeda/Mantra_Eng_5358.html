शतं ते शिप्रिन्न ऊतयः सुदासे सहस्रं शंसा उता रातिर अस्तु |जहि वधार वनुषो मार्त्यस्यास्मे द्युम्नम अधि रत्नं च धेहि ||
śataṁ te śiprinn ūtayaḥ sudāse sahasraṁ śaṁsā uta rātir astu | jahi vadhar vanuṣo martyasyāsme dyumnam adhi ratnaṁ ca dhehi ||
  • śataṁ te - "A hundred to you": This phrase signifies abundance and prosperity. In real life, it can be related to the idea of wishing someone a hundredfold success in their endeavors, such as a business or personal goals.
  • śiprinn ūtayaḥ - "swift and strong": This refers to the qualities of speed and strength. In practical terms, this can be seen in athletes who train to enhance their physical capabilities, or in businesses that aim to be agile and robust in their operations.
  • sudāse - "to the well-doers": This emphasizes the importance of righteousness and good deeds. In everyday life, this can be reflected in community service or charitable actions that benefit society.
  • sahasraṁ - "a thousand": This term denotes a large number, symbolizing vastness and abundance. For instance, it can relate to the idea of having numerous opportunities or resources available to one.
  • śaṁsā - "praise": This word highlights the significance of recognition and appreciation. In a workplace, for example, acknowledging employees' hard work can lead to increased motivation and productivity.
  • uta rātir astu - "may there be joy and happiness": This phrase expresses a wish for well-being and contentment. In real life, it can be seen in celebrations, such as weddings or festivals, where joy is shared among people.
  • jahi vadhar - "overcome the obstacles": This encourages individuals to face and conquer challenges. In a practical scenario, this can relate to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or pursuing education against all odds.
  • vanuṣo - "of the mortal beings": This term reminds us of our human condition and the shared experiences of life. It can be related to empathy and understanding among people, recognizing that everyone faces difficulties.
  • martyasyāsme - "we are of the mortals": This reinforces the idea of our mortality and the transient nature of life. It can inspire individuals to make the most of their time and cherish relationships.
  • dyumnam - "glory": This signifies the pursuit of honor and respect. In real life, this can be seen in the achievements of individuals who strive for excellence in their fields, such as scientists or artists.
  • adhi ratnaṁ ca dhehi - "bestow upon us the jewel": This phrase symbolizes the desire for wisdom and valuable insights. In practical terms, it can relate to seeking knowledge through education or mentorship.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound wish for abundance, strength, righteousness, joy, and the overcoming of obstacles. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to strive for personal and communal growth while recognizing our shared human experiences. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as setting personal goals, engaging in community service, or pursuing education, ultimately guiding individuals towards a fulfilling and meaningful life.
