न यं शुक्रो न दुराशीर न तृप्रा उर्व्यचसम् |अपस्पृण्वते सुहार्दम् ||
na yaṁ śukro na durāśīr na tṛprā uruvyacasam | apaspṛṇvate suhārdam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na - not
    • yaṁ - who
    • śukro - pure, bright, or auspicious
    • na - not
    • durāśīr - one who is difficult to understand or comprehend
    • na - not
    • tṛprā - one who is satisfied or content
    • uruvyacasam - one who has vast or extensive speech or expression
    • apaspṛṇvate - those who do not touch or affect
    • suhārdam - friendly or benevolent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of individuals who are not influenced by negativity or harmful traits.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity, understanding, and contentment in one's character.
    • The phrase "apaspṛṇvate suhārdam" suggests that those who embody these qualities do not harm others and are friendly in nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who is pure in intentions, easy to understand, and content with their achievements fosters a positive environment.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who are friendly and do not harbor negative feelings contribute to healthier interactions.
    • In community settings, those who promote understanding and goodwill, rather than conflict, help build a supportive society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate positive qualities in ourselves and to strive for purity, understanding, and contentment.
    • Its significance lies in promoting harmonious relationships and a peaceful environment, applicable in various aspects of life, including personal, professional, and social spheres.
    • By embodying these traits, we can contribute to a more benevolent and supportive world.
