यवं वृकेणाश्विनā वपन्तेषं दूहन्ता मनुषाया दश्रā |अभि दस्युम बकुरेणā धमन्तोरु ज्योतिश चक्रथुर आर्याय ||
yavaṁ vṛkeṇāśvinā vapanteṣaṁ duhantā manuṣāya dasrā | abhi dasyum bakureṇā dhamantoru jyotiś cakrathur āryāya ||
  • yavaṁ: This word refers to "barley" or "grain," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • vṛkeṇāśvinā: "Vṛkeṇa" means "with the wolf," and "Aśvinā" refers to the twin deities associated with health and medicine in Vedic literature. This phrase can imply the protection and guidance provided by divine forces.
  • vapanteṣaṁ: This translates to "they who are engaged in the act of giving," indicating a sense of generosity and sharing.
  • duhantā: Meaning "milking" or "extracting," this word suggests the act of drawing out benefits or resources.
  • manuṣāya: This means "for humanity," emphasizing the mantra's relevance to human beings and their needs.
  • dasrā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," possibly referring to the rapidity of life and the need for timely actions.
  • abhi: This word means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a movement or intention.
  • dasyum: This translates to "the enemy" or "obstacle," representing challenges that one may face in life.
  • bakureṇā: This can be understood as "with the help of the divine," suggesting reliance on higher powers for overcoming difficulties.
  • dhamantoru: This means "to drive away" or "to dispel," indicating the action of removing negativity or obstacles.
  • jyotiś: This translates to "light" or "illumination," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and clarity.
  • cakrathur: This word means "they who revolve" or "they who move in cycles," possibly referring to the cyclical nature of life and the universe.
  • āryāya: This term means "for the noble" or "for the righteous," indicating that the mantra is directed towards those who uphold virtue and righteousness.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and the role of divine forces in guiding humanity through challenges. It highlights the need for generosity, the swift action required to overcome obstacles, and the pursuit of knowledge and righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in contexts such as community service, where individuals come together to support one another, or in personal development, where one seeks to dispel negativity and cultivate wisdom. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine guidance, and the pursuit of a virtuous path in the face of challenges.
