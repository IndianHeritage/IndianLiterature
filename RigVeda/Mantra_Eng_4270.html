अस्ति हि वाम इह स्तोताऽस्मसि वां संदृशि श्रीये |नू श्रुतं मा आगतम अवोभिर वाजिनीवासू ||
asti hi vām iha stotā smasi vāṁ saṁdṛśi śriye | nū śrutam ma ā gatam avobhir vājinīvasū ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Indeed, you are the one who praises here; may you be seen in glory. Let not the sound of your praise be lost; may you come to us with abundance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asti hi: "Indeed, there is" - affirming the presence of something significant.
    • vām: "you" - addressing a divine entity or a revered figure.
    • iha: "here" - indicating the present moment or the current situation.
    • stotā: "the one who praises" - referring to the act of worship or commendation.
    • smasi: "may you be" - a wish or a prayer for the presence of the divine.
    • vāṁ: "you" - again addressing the divine.
    • saṁdṛśi: "in glory" - a state of honor or recognition.
    • śriye: "prosperity" - referring to wealth, success, or abundance.
    • nū: "let not" - a plea to prevent something from happening.
    • śrutam: "the sound" - referring to the praises or hymns sung.
    • ma: "not" - a negation.
    • ā gatam: "be lost" - a desire for continuity and remembrance.
    • avobhir: "with abundance" - a request for plentiful blessings.
    • vājinīvasū: "may you come to us" - a call for the divine presence in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be recited during moments of gratitude, such as after achieving a goal or receiving help from others. It serves as a reminder to acknowledge and praise the support received.
    • In community gatherings or religious ceremonies, this mantra can be used to invoke blessings and prosperity for the group, emphasizing the importance of collective praise and recognition of divine presence.
    • In professional settings, it can be a motivational mantra, encouraging teams to celebrate their achievements and maintain a positive outlook, ensuring that their efforts are recognized and appreciated.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on recognition, praise, and the desire for abundance. It teaches us the importance of acknowledging the divine and the support we receive in our lives. By incorporating this mantra into our daily practices, we can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and abundance, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal achievements to community celebrations, reminding us to honor the contributions of others and seek prosperity in all aspects of life.
