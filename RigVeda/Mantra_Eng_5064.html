अश्वा न यां वाजिना पूतबन्धू ऋता यद गर्भं अदितिः भरध्यै |प्र यां महि महान्ता जायमानā घोरा मर्ताय रिपवे न दीधाḥ ||
aśvā na yā vājinā pūtabandhū ṛtā yad garbham aditir bharadhyai | pra yā mahi mahāntā jāyamānā ghorā martāya ripave ni dīdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvā: horses
    • na: not
    • yā: who
    • vājinā: swift or powerful
    • pūtabandhū: pure or sacred
    • ṛtā: truth or righteousness
    • yad: that which
    • garbham: womb or creation
    • aditir: Aditi, the mother of gods
    • bharadhyai: may bear or support
    • pra: towards
    • yā: who
    • mahi: great
    • mahāntā: the mighty
    • jāyamānā: being born
    • ghorā: fierce or terrible
    • martāya: for the mortal
    • ripave: for the enemy
    • ni: not
    • dīdhaḥ: to be seen or perceived
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine support and strength that Aditi, the mother of gods, provides.
    • The mention of "horses" symbolizes speed, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "may bear or support" indicates the nurturing aspect of Aditi, suggesting that she provides the necessary strength for creation and sustenance.
    • The reference to "fierce" and "mortal" highlights the challenges and adversities faced in life, emphasizing the need for divine protection against enemies or negative forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power, much like invoking Aditi for support.
    • In competitive environments, such as sports or business, the mantra can be seen as a call for the inner strength and resilience needed to face challenges.
    • Parents may relate to the nurturing aspect of Aditi, striving to provide a supportive environment for their children to grow and thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges and adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder of the strength that comes from nurturing and protection, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as personal struggles, competitive situations, and parenting.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can seek the courage and resilience needed to navigate through difficulties, reinforcing the belief in a higher power that aids in their journey.
