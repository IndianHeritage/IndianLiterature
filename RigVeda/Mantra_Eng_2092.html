उत्तानायाम जनयन सुशूतं भुवद अग्निः पुरुपेशासु गर्भः |शिरिणायां चिद् आक्तुनां महोभिर अपरिवृतो वसति प्रचेताः ||
uttānāyām ajanayan suṣūtam bhuvad agniḥ purupeśāsu garbhaḥ | śiriṇāyāṁ cid aktunā mahobhir aparīvṛto vasati pracetāḥ ||
  • uttānāyām: This term can be translated as "in the upward direction" or "in the act of rising." It signifies growth, elevation, and the pursuit of higher knowledge or consciousness.
  • ajanayan: This means "to give birth" or "to create." It reflects the idea of bringing forth new life or new ideas, emphasizing creativity and the generative aspect of existence.
  • suṣūtam: Translated as "well-nurtured" or "well-born," this word highlights the importance of care and attention in the process of growth and development.
  • bhuvad: This term means "of the earth" or "earthly." It connects the spiritual or higher aspirations with the material world, indicating that our pursuits should be grounded in reality.
  • agniḥ: Referring to "fire," this word symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a divine element that can both destroy and create.
  • purupeśāsu: This term can be interpreted as "in the many forms" or "in various manifestations." It suggests the diversity of existence and the multiple ways in which life can be experienced.
  • garbhaḥ: Meaning "womb" or "embryo," this word signifies potential and the nurturing aspect of creation, representing the space where new life begins.
  • śiriṇāyāṁ: This translates to "in the act of nourishing" or "in the process of sustaining." It emphasizes the importance of support and sustenance in growth.
  • cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," this term highlights the role of awareness in the process of creation and existence.
  • aktunā: This can be interpreted as "with energy" or "with power," indicating that the process of creation is dynamic and requires vitality.
  • mahobhir: Translated as "with great strength" or "with might," this word emphasizes the power behind creation and the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
  • aparīvṛto: This means "unobstructed" or "without hindrance," suggesting that the process of creation and growth should be free from obstacles.
  • vasati: Meaning "to dwell" or "to reside," this term indicates the presence of consciousness or energy in a particular space or form.
  • pracetāḥ: This translates to "the wise" or "the enlightened," referring to those who possess knowledge and understanding, guiding the process of creation and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, growth, and the interplay between the spiritual and material realms. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing, awareness, and strength in the process of bringing forth new life and ideas. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as personal development, parenting, and creative endeavors. It serves as a reminder that growth requires care, consciousness, and the ability to overcome obstacles, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enriched existence.
