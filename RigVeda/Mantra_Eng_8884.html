सदासी रण्वो यवसेव पूष्यते होत्राभिर अग्ने मनुष्यः स्वध्वरः |विप्रस्य वा यच छशमान उक्त्यं वाजं ससवाम् उपयासि भूर्बिभिः ||
sadāsi raṇvo yavaseva puṣyate hotrābhir agne manuṣaḥ svadhvaraḥ | viprasya vā yac chaśamāna ukthya1ṁ vājaṁ sasavām̐ upayāsi bhūribhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadāsi: always, eternally
    • raṇvo: in the battle, in the struggle
    • yavaseva: for the one who is devoted, for the one who serves
    • puṣyate: is nourished, is made prosperous
    • hotrābhir: by the priests, by the ritual speakers
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • manuṣaḥ: of humans, of mankind
    • svadhvaraḥ: self-possessed, self-ruled
    • viprasya: of the wise, of the learned
    • vā: or
    • yac: whatever, that which
    • shaśamāna: is being praised, is being honored
    • ukthyaṁ: the hymn, the invocation
    • vājaṁ: wealth, prosperity
    • sasavām̐: I have obtained, I have achieved
    • upayāsi: you approach, you come near
    • bhūribhiḥ: with abundance, with plenty
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of devotion and service, suggesting that those who are dedicated and serve selflessly will always find nourishment and prosperity.
    • The mention of priests indicates the significance of rituals and the power of collective prayer in achieving one's desires.
    • The phrase "self-possessed" highlights the importance of self-control and inner strength in the pursuit of goals.
    • It also suggests that wisdom and learning are essential for achieving prosperity and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, employees who are dedicated to their work and serve their team often find themselves rewarded with promotions and recognition.
    • In community service, individuals who selflessly contribute to their communities often inspire others and create a ripple effect of positive change.
    • In personal relationships, those who practice self-control and understanding tend to build stronger, more meaningful connections with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of dedication, service, and wisdom in achieving prosperity and success in life.
    • It teaches us that through selfless actions and inner strength, we can attract abundance and positive outcomes.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and professional success.
