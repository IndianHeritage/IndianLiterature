त्वं अग्नि बृहद् वयो दधासि देव दाशुषे |कविः गृहपतिः युवा ||
tvam agne bṛhad vayo dadhāsi deva dāśuṣe | kavir gṛhapatir yuvā ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • agne - Refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • bṛhad - Means "great" or "vast," signifying the immense power and significance of Agni.
  • vayo - Translates to "age" or "life," suggesting the timeless nature of Agni.
  • dadhāsi - Means "you bestow" or "you provide," indicating Agni's role as a giver of blessings and sustenance.
  • deva - Means "god," reinforcing the divine status of Agni.
  • dāśuṣe - Refers to "for the worshippers" or "for the devotees," highlighting Agni's role in connecting the divine with humanity.
  • kavir - Means "the wise one" or "the poet," suggesting Agni's role as a source of inspiration and knowledge.
  • gṛhapatir - Translates to "lord of the house," indicating Agni's presence in domestic life and rituals.
  • yuvā - Means "young" or "youthful," symbolizing vitality and the continuous renewal of life.

This mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, acknowledging his greatness, timelessness, and role as a provider for his devotees. It emphasizes the importance of Agni in both the cosmic and domestic realms, portraying him as a source of wisdom and inspiration.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices), symbolizing the presence of the divine and the purification of intentions.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of Agni—transformation, energy, and wisdom—can inspire individuals to seek personal development and renewal in their lives.
  • Community and Family: As the "lord of the house," Agni represents the importance of family and community bonds, encouraging individuals to foster harmony and connection within their households.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, urging us to recognize the transformative power of Agni. It highlights the significance of rituals, personal growth, and community, making it relevant in various aspects of daily life.
