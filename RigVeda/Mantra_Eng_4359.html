मन्त्र: एवन्द्राग्निभ्यां अहावि हव्यम् शूष्यम् घृतं न पूतम् अद्रिभिः |तासूषु श्रवो बृहद् रयिं गृणत्सु दिधृतम् इषं गृणत्सु दिधृतम् ||
evendrāgnibhyām ahāvi havyaṁ śūṣyaṁ ghṛtaṁ na pūtam adribhiḥ | tā sūriṣu śravo bṛhad rayiṁ gṛṇatsu didhṛtam iṣaṁ gṛṇatsu didhṛtam ||
  • evendrāgnibhyām: This term refers to the divine forces of Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
  • ahāvi: This word means "I invoke" or "I call upon." It signifies the act of seeking blessings or assistance from the divine.
  • havyaṁ: This translates to "offering" or "sacrifice." It represents the act of giving something valuable to the gods in hopes of receiving their favor.
  • śūṣyaṁ: This means "pure" or "uncontaminated." It emphasizes the importance of purity in offerings made to the divine.
  • ghṛtaṁ: This refers to "ghee" or clarified butter, which is a sacred offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and prosperity.
  • na pūtam: This phrase means "not spoiled" or "not rotten," indicating the necessity of freshness and quality in offerings.
  • adribhiḥ: This translates to "by the mountains," which can symbolize stability and strength, as mountains are often seen as eternal and unyielding.
  • tā sūriṣu: This means "those who are radiant" or "the shining ones," referring to the gods or enlightened beings who are worthy of worship.
  • śravo: This word means "glory" or "fame," indicating the importance of seeking recognition and honor through one's actions.
  • bṛhad: This translates to "great" or "vast," suggesting the expansive nature of the blessings being sought.
  • rayiṁ: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the desire for abundance in life.
  • gṛṇatsu: This term means "in the act of singing" or "in praise," indicating the importance of devotion and reverence in worship.
  • didhṛtam: This translates to "held" or "maintained," suggesting the need to sustain the blessings received.
  • iṣaṁ: This means "desire" or "wish," indicating the aspirations one has in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine forces, particularly Indra and Agni, to seek blessings of purity, prosperity, and glory. It highlights the importance of offering pure and fresh items, such as ghee, to the gods, symbolizing the nourishment of both body and spirit. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, or personal reflections where one seeks to connect with higher powers for guidance and support. It serves as a reminder of the values of purity, devotion, and the pursuit of greatness in one's endeavors, encouraging individuals to maintain their aspirations and sustain the blessings they receive through righteous actions.
