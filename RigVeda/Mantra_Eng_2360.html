तं अस्मेराऽ युवतयो युवानं मर्मृज्यमानाः परि यन्त्य आपः |स शुक्रेभिः शिक्वभिः रेवद अस्मे दीदायानिध्मो घृतनिर्णिग अप्सु ||
tam asmerā yuvatayo yuvānam marmṛjyamānāḥ pari yanty āpaḥ | sa śukrebhiḥ śikvabhī revad asme dīdāyānidhmo ghṛtanirṇig apsu ||
  • tam asmerā yuvatayo yuvānam marmṛjyamānāḥ pari yanty āpaḥ: This line can be translated to mean "The waters, which are being stirred by the young ones, are moving around." Here, "asmerā" refers to the young or youthful energy, while "yuvatayo" signifies the youth. The term "marmṛjyamānāḥ" indicates the act of being stirred or agitated, suggesting a dynamic and lively interaction with the waters.
  • sa śukrebhiḥ śikvabhī revad asme dīdāyānidhmo ghṛtanirṇig apsu: This part translates to "May that which is pure and bright, which is being poured out, grant us the wealth of ghee in the waters." The word "śukrebhiḥ" refers to purity and brightness, while "ghṛtanirṇig" relates to ghee, a symbol of nourishment and prosperity. "Apsu" refers to waters, indicating a connection to abundance and sustenance.

  • Real-Life Example: The imagery of youth stirring the waters can be likened to the energy and creativity of young people in society. For instance, in a community project, the enthusiasm of young volunteers can rejuvenate a stagnant initiative, much like how the waters are stirred. The second part emphasizes the importance of purity and nourishment, which can be seen in sustainable practices, such as using clean water for agriculture to ensure healthy crops.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of youthful energy and the importance of purity in our actions. It teaches us that the vigor of youth can bring about positive change, while also reminding us to seek nourishment and abundance in a pure form. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community development, environmental sustainability, and personal growth, where the combination of youthful enthusiasm and a commitment to purity can lead to prosperity and well-being.
