तं पृच्छता स जगाम स वेद स चिकीत्वाम् ईयते सा nv ईयते |तस्मिन्सन्ति प्राशिषस तस्मिन्न इष्टयः स वाजस्य शवसः शुष्मिणस पतिः ||
tam pṛcchatā sa jagāmā sa veda sa cikitvām̐ īyate sā nv īyate | tasmin santi praśiṣas tasminn iṣṭayaḥ sa vājasya śavasaḥ śuṣmiṇas patiḥ ||
  • tam pṛcchatā sa jagāmā: This phrase can be translated as "He who is asked, he who has gone." It signifies the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from those who possess it. In real life, this can be seen in the context of education, where students seek guidance from teachers or mentors.
  • sa veda sa cikitvām̐: This means "He knows, he understands." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and comprehension. For example, in a workplace, an employee who understands the intricacies of a project is more likely to succeed and contribute effectively to the team.
  • īyate sā nv īyate: This translates to "He is led, he is guided." It reflects the idea of being guided by wisdom and knowledge. In everyday life, this can be related to how individuals often rely on mentors or leaders to navigate challenges and make informed decisions.
  • tasmin santi praśiṣas: This means "In him, there are teachings." It highlights the presence of valuable lessons within knowledgeable individuals. For instance, a wise elder in a community often shares life lessons that can guide younger generations.
  • tasminn iṣṭayaḥ: This translates to "In him, there are desires." It suggests that knowledge and wisdom can help fulfill one's aspirations. In a practical sense, a person who is well-informed about their goals is more likely to achieve them.
  • sa vājasya śavasaḥ: This means "He is the lord of strength." It signifies that knowledge and wisdom empower individuals. For example, a leader who possesses deep understanding and insight can inspire and strengthen their team.
  • šuṣmiṇas patiḥ: This translates to "The lord of the wise." It emphasizes the reverence for those who are knowledgeable. In society, we often look up to experts in various fields, such as scientists, philosophers, and educators, for guidance and inspiration.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda underscores the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from those who possess it. It highlights the importance of guidance, understanding, and the empowerment that comes from knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra is applicable in educational settings, workplaces, and community interactions, where individuals seek to learn from others, fulfill their aspirations, and lead with strength and wisdom. By valuing knowledge and the wise, we can navigate life's challenges more effectively and contribute positively to society.
