माता च यत्र दुहिताऽ च धेनू सबरदुग्धे धापयते समीचि |ऋतस्य ते सदसिल् अन्तर महद् देवानाम् असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
mātā ca yatra duhitā ca dhenū sabardughe dhāpayete samīcī | ṛtasya te sadasīḻe antar mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • mātā ca - This translates to "and the mother." In many cultures, the mother is revered as the primary caregiver and nurturer. For example, in a family setting, a mother often sacrifices her own needs for the well-being of her children.
  • yatra duhitā ca - This means "where the daughter is." The daughter represents the future and continuity of the family lineage. In modern society, daughters are increasingly taking on roles of leadership and empowerment, showcasing their importance in both familial and societal contexts.
  • dhenū - This word refers to "cows," which are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in agrarian societies. For instance, in rural India, cows are not only a source of milk but also play a crucial role in the agricultural economy.
  • sabardughe - This term can be interpreted as "with abundant resources." It signifies prosperity and the blessings of nature. In real life, communities that practice sustainable farming often experience a bountiful harvest, reflecting the abundance mentioned in this mantra.
  • dhāpayete samīcī - This phrase means "they are well-nourished." It emphasizes the importance of proper care and nourishment for growth and development. For example, children who receive balanced nutrition are more likely to thrive academically and physically.
  • ṛtasya te sadasīḻe - This translates to "in the abode of truth." It highlights the significance of living a life aligned with truth and righteousness. In a corporate setting, companies that adhere to ethical practices often gain trust and loyalty from their customers.
  • antar mahad devānām - This means "within the great beings of the gods." It suggests a connection to the divine and the importance of spirituality in life. Many people find solace and guidance through spiritual practices, which can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • asuratvam ekam - This translates to "the unity of divine qualities." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of harmony. In real life, fostering unity and collaboration in diverse teams can lead to innovative solutions and a more inclusive environment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of family, nourishment, truth, and unity. It highlights the roles of mothers and daughters in society, the importance of resources, and the connection to the divine. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as nurturing relationships, promoting ethical practices, and fostering community spirit. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can work towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
