त्वं सोम पाणिभ्य आ वसु गव्यानी धारयः |ततं तन्तुम अचिक्रदः ||
tvaṁ soma paṇibhya ā vasu gavyāni dhārayaḥ | tataṁ tantum acikradaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • soma - Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • paṇibhya - This term means "from the hands," suggesting the act of offering or presenting something valuable.
  • ā - A preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating movement or intention.
  • vasu - Vasu means "wealth" or "prosperity," often referring to material and spiritual abundance.
  • gavyāni - This word relates to "cows" or "cattle," which are symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture.
  • dhārayaḥ - This means "to hold" or "to sustain," indicating the act of maintaining or nurturing something valuable.
  • tataṁ - This word can be interpreted as "therefore" or "thus," linking the previous ideas to a conclusion or result.
  • tantum - This refers to "the thread" or "the connection," symbolizing continuity and the interwoven nature of existence.
  • acikradaḥ - This term means "to be established" or "to be firm," suggesting stability and permanence in the context of the mantra.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to a divine force, asking for the sustenance of wealth and prosperity through the sacred essence of Soma. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and maintaining abundance in both material and spiritual realms.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra calls for the nurturing of wealth, individuals can focus on personal development and the cultivation of skills that lead to prosperity.
  • Community Support: The reference to cattle symbolizes community and support systems. In modern life, this can translate to fostering relationships and networks that provide mutual assistance.
  • Environmental Awareness: The emphasis on sustenance can be linked to sustainable practices in agriculture and resource management, ensuring that wealth is maintained without depleting natural resources.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine blessings, material wealth, and the importance of nurturing both. It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives, ensuring that we not only pursue prosperity but also sustain it through responsible actions and community support.
