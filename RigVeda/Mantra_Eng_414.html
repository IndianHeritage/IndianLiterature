अभīvṛtaṁ कृत्स्नैर विश्वरूपं हिरण्यशम्यं यजतो बृहन्तम् |आस्थाद रथं सविता चित्रभानुः कृष्णा राजांसी तविषीं दधानाḥ ||
abhīvṛtaṁ kṛśanair viśvarūpaṁ hiraṇyaśamyaṁ yajato bṛhantam | āsthād rathaṁ savitā citrabhānuḥ kṛṣṇā rajāṁsi taviṣīṁ dadhānaḥ ||
  • abhīvṛtaṁ: This term can be understood as "enveloped" or "surrounded." It signifies the idea of being protected or covered by a higher power.
  • kṛśanair: This word means "by the weak" or "by the feeble." It suggests that even those who may seem weak or vulnerable can find strength and support.
  • viśvarūpaṁ: This translates to "of universal form." It indicates the omnipresence and all-encompassing nature of the divine.
  • hiraṇyaśamyaṁ: This term means "golden radiance" or "golden brilliance." It symbolizes prosperity, wealth, and the divine light that guides individuals.
  • yajato: This word refers to "those who perform sacrifices" or "worshippers." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and ritual in connecting with the divine.
  • bṛhantam: This means "the great one" or "the vast." It signifies the greatness of the divine and the expansive nature of existence.
  • āsthād: This translates to "having taken refuge" or "having relied upon." It indicates a sense of trust and surrender to a higher power.
  • rathaṁ: This word means "chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the journey of life and the means by which one navigates through challenges.
  • savitā: This term refers to "the sun" or "the solar deity." It symbolizes illumination, energy, and the source of life.
  • citrabhānuḥ: This translates to "the colorful sun" or "the radiant sun." It emphasizes the beauty and diversity of life.
  • kṛṣṇā: This word means "black" or "dark." It can symbolize the unknown or the challenges that one faces in life.
  • rajāṁsi: This term refers to "royal powers" or "kingly attributes." It signifies authority, strength, and the ability to rule over one's life.
  • taviṣīṁ: This means "the powerful" or "the strong." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • dadhānaḥ: This translates to "holding" or "possessing." It indicates the act of maintaining control and balance in one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and guidance in the journey of life. It highlights the importance of devotion, strength, and the universal nature of the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when individuals face challenges or uncertainties, reminding them to trust in a higher power and to find strength within themselves. It encourages people to perform their duties with dedication and to seek the light of knowledge and wisdom, much like a charioteer navigating through the complexities of life with the guidance of the sun. This mantra serves as a reminder that even in moments of weakness, one can find refuge and strength through faith and devotion.
