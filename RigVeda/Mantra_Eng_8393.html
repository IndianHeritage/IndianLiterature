यं त्वा वाजिन्नघ्न्याभ्यअनूषतायोहतं योनिमारोहसि द्युमान् |मघोनामायुः प्रतिरन् महि श्रव इन्द्राय सोम पवसे वृषा मदः ||
yaṁ tvā vājinn aghnyā abhy anūṣatāyohataṁ yonim ā rohasi dyumān | maghonām āyuḥ pratiran mahi śrava indrāya soma pavase vṛṣā madaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • vājinn: with strength
    • aghnyā: not to be harmed
    • abhy: towards
    • anūṣatāyohataṁ: the one who is the source of nourishment
    • yonim: womb or origin
    • ā: from
    • rohasi: you rise
    • dyumān: shining or radiant
    • maghonām: of the wealthy or prosperous
    • āyuḥ: life
    • pratiran: in abundance
    • mahi: great
    • śrava: fame or glory
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • soma: the sacred drink
    • pavase: you who purify
    • vṛṣā: strong or powerful
    • madaḥ: intoxication or bliss
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energy represented by Indra, emphasizing strength and protection.
    • The phrase "whom you with strength" suggests a relationship of devotion and reliance on divine power.
    • The term "not to be harmed" indicates a plea for safety and security in life.
    • It speaks of nourishment and the source of life, symbolizing the importance of sustenance in both physical and spiritual forms.
    • The reference to "shining" and "radiant" highlights the pursuit of enlightenment and wisdom.
    • By mentioning "wealthy" and "prosperous," it connects spiritual richness with material abundance.
    • The invocation of "Indra" and "soma" signifies the quest for divine blessings and the joy that comes from spiritual fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and protection through prayer or meditation, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • People may turn to community support or spiritual practices to find nourishment and security during challenging times.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can be seen in educational endeavors, where individuals strive to rise and shine in their respective fields.
    • Celebrations and rituals that honor deities, like Indra, reflect the cultural significance of seeking divine blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine strength, protection, and nourishment in life.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, where spiritual and material aspects are intertwined.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek balance, strength, and divine guidance in their pursuits.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, spirituality, and the quest for prosperity and well-being.
