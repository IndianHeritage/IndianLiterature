स्तोमं जुषेथां युवशेवा कन्यनां विश्वेह देवौ सवनावा गच्छतम् |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चेषं नो वोळ्हम् अश्विनाः ||
stomaṁ juṣethāṁ yuvaśeva kanyanāṁ viśveha devau savanāva gacchatam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ceṣaṁ no voḻham aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "stomaṁ juṣethāṁ yuvaśeva kanyanāṁ viśveha devau savanāva gacchatam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ceṣaṁ no voḻham aśvinā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • stomaṁ: praise or hymn
    • juṣethāṁ: may you enjoy
    • yuvaśeva: like a youthful maiden
    • kanyanāṁ: of the maidens
    • viśveha: all
    • devau: gods
    • savanāva: may they be invoked
    • gacchatam: may they go
    • sajoṣasā: with joy
    • uṣasā: of the dawn
    • sūryeṇa: with the sun
    • ceṣaṁ: may you be active
    • no: not
    • voḻham: may you be hindered
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin gods)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the enjoyment of praise and blessings from the divine.
    • The invocation of the gods, particularly the Ashvins, symbolizes a request for health, vitality, and joy.
    • The reference to "yuvaśeva kanyanāṁ" suggests a youthful and vibrant energy, akin to the freshness of dawn.
    • The phrase "sajoṣasā uṣasā" emphasizes the joy that comes with the new day, representing hope and new beginnings.
    • In real life, this can be related to the start of a new project, a new phase in life, or even the dawn of a new day where one seeks positivity and energy.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of invoking divine blessings for success and joy in our endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder to embrace the energy of youth and vitality in our daily lives.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied during significant life events such as weddings, new job beginnings, or any new venture where one seeks divine support.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of positivity and openness to the blessings of the universe.
