पवमान मह्य अर्णो वि धावसि सूरो न चित्रो अव्ययानी पव्ययाः |गभस्तिपूतो नृभिर अद्रिभिः सुतो महे वाजाय धन्याय धन्वसि ||
pavamāna mahy arṇo vi dhāvasi sūro na citro avyayāni pavyayā | gabhastipūto nṛbhir adribhiḥ suto mahe vājāya dhanyāya dhanvasi ||
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "sublime" aspect, often associated with the wind or breath. In real life, this can be likened to the cleansing power of nature, such as fresh air that revitalizes and rejuvenates us.
  • mahy: This means "to me" or "for me," indicating a personal connection or appeal. It emphasizes the importance of individual experience and perception in the journey of life.
  • arṇo: This word translates to "ocean" or "vastness," symbolizing the depth and breadth of existence. Just as the ocean is vast and full of mysteries, our lives are filled with experiences that shape us.
  • vi dhāvasi: This phrase means "you run" or "you flow," suggesting movement and dynamism. In real life, this can be compared to the flow of time and how we must adapt to the changes it brings.
  • sūro: This translates to "hero" or "brave one," representing strength and courage. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for their beliefs or help others in need.
  • na citro: Meaning "not strange" or "not unusual," this phrase suggests familiarity and comfort. It reminds us that many experiences are universal, and we are not alone in our struggles.
  • avyayāni: This term means "imperishable" or "eternal," indicating the lasting nature of certain truths or values. In life, this can refer to love, wisdom, and kindness that endure beyond time.
  • pavyayā: This word can be interpreted as "flowing" or "spreading," symbolizing the dissemination of knowledge and goodness. It reflects how positive actions can ripple through society.
  • gabhastipūto: This translates to "pure light," representing clarity and enlightenment. In real life, this can be likened to moments of insight or understanding that illuminate our paths.
  • nṛbhir: Meaning "by men" or "among people," this emphasizes the social aspect of human existence. It highlights the importance of community and relationships in our lives.
  • adribhiḥ: This term means "by the mountains," symbolizing strength and stability. Mountains can represent challenges we face, and overcoming them leads to personal growth.
  • suto: This translates to "born" or "produced," indicating creation and new beginnings. In life, this can refer to the birth of ideas, projects, or even new life itself.
  • mahe: Meaning "great," this word signifies the importance of greatness in our endeavors. It encourages us to strive for excellence in all that we do.
  • vājāya: This term means "for wealth" or "for prosperity," indicating the pursuit of abundance in life. It reflects our desire for success and fulfillment.
  • dhanyāya: This translates to "for the blessed" or "for the fortunate," suggesting gratitude and appreciation for the good things in life. It reminds us to recognize our blessings.
  • dhanvasi: This word means "in wealth" or "in abundance," reinforcing the theme of prosperity and the importance of nurturing what we have.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of purification, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the enduring values that guide us. In real life, we can apply these teachings by striving for personal growth, embracing change, and recognizing the blessings in our lives. By embodying the qualities of courage, clarity, and gratitude, we can navigate the complexities of existence with grace and purpose.
