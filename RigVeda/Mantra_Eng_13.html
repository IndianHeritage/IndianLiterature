मन्त्र: इन्द्रवायू इमे सुताः उप प्रयोभिर आगतम् | इन्दवो वाम उषन्ति हि ||
indravāyū ime sutā upa prayobhir ā gatam | indavo vām uśanti hi ||
  • Indravāyū: This term refers to the combined forces of Indra, the king of gods, and Vayu, the god of wind. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the powerful and dynamic forces of nature.
  • Ime sutā: This translates to "these sons" or "these offerings." It indicates the offerings made to the deities, which are essential for invoking their blessings.
  • Upa prayobhir ā gatam: This phrase means "having come near with offerings." It signifies the act of approaching the divine with sincerity and devotion, bringing forth one's offerings or prayers.
  • Indavo: This refers to the deities or the divine forces, particularly Indra and Vayu, who are being invoked in this mantra.
  • Vām uśanti hi: This means "they are pleased with you." It expresses the idea that the deities are satisfied with the offerings and prayers made to them.

  • Meaning: The mantra emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with sincere offerings and prayers. It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine forces of nature, suggesting that when we make offerings, we can invoke their blessings and support.
  • Real-life Example: In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to seek blessings for prosperity, health, and protection. For instance, during harvest festivals, farmers often make offerings to deities to ensure a good yield. This practice reflects the essence of the mantra, where the act of giving is seen as a way to connect with the divine and receive blessings in return.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine. It teaches us that by approaching the divine with respect and sincerity, we can cultivate a harmonious relationship with the forces of nature. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of gratitude and reverence, whether through rituals, community service, or simply by appreciating the natural world around them. By doing so, we not only honor the divine but also foster a sense of connection and responsibility towards our environment and each other.
