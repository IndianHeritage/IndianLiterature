तव द्रप्सो नीलवान वाश ऋत्विया इन्धानः सिष्णव आ ददे |त्वं महीनाम उषसाम असि प्रियः क्षपो वस्तुषु राजसि ||
tava drapso nīlavān vāśa ṛtviya indhānaḥ siṣṇav ā dade | tvam mahīnām uṣasām asi priyaḥ kṣapo vastuṣu rājasi ||
  • tava drapso: This phrase can be interpreted as "your essence" or "your nature." It signifies the intrinsic qualities of a divine entity or a higher power.
  • nīlavān: This word means "blue" or "dark," often associated with the vastness of the sky or the depth of the ocean. It symbolizes the infinite and the unknown.
  • vāśa: This term refers to "control" or "dominion." It indicates the power to govern or influence, often in a spiritual or cosmic sense.
  • ṛtviya: This word relates to "truth" or "righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with cosmic laws and moral principles.
  • indhānaḥ: This translates to "the one who ignites" or "the source of light." It represents the divine spark that brings life and energy to the universe.
  • siṣṇav: This term can mean "to create" or "to manifest." It highlights the act of bringing forth existence from the formless.
  • ā dade: This phrase means "to give" or "to bestow." It signifies the act of sharing blessings or resources.
  • tvam: This word means "you," addressing the divine or the higher power directly.
  • mahīnām: This translates to "of the great" or "of the mighty." It refers to the powerful forces of nature or the universe.
  • uṣasām: This word means "of the dawn" or "of the new beginnings." It symbolizes hope, renewal, and the promise of a new day.
  • asi: This translates to "you are," affirming the presence and essence of the divine.
  • priyaḥ: This term means "beloved" or "dear." It indicates a close relationship or affection towards the divine.
  • kṣapo: This word refers to "the twilight" or "the transition." It symbolizes the balance between day and night, light and darkness.
  • vastuṣu: This translates to "in the things" or "in the objects." It signifies the material world and the divine presence within it.
  • rājasi: This term means "to rule" or "to govern." It emphasizes the authority and power of the divine in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine presence and its relationship with the universe. It highlights the importance of truth, righteousness, and the power of creation. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance in difficult times, understanding the interconnectedness of all beings, and recognizing the divine spark within ourselves and others. It serves as a reminder to live in harmony with the cosmic order and to appreciate the beauty of new beginnings, much like the dawn that follows the darkness of night.
