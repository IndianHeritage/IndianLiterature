ईशानाय प्रहुतिं यस् त आनाṭ छुचिं सोमं शुचिपास तुभ्यं वायो |कृतोषि तं मर्त्येषु प्रशस्तं जातो-जातो जायते वाज्य अस्य ||
īśānāya prahutiṁ yas ta ānaṭ chuciṁ somaṁ śucipās tubhyaṁ vāyo | kṛṇoṣi tam martyeṣu praśastaṁ jāto-jāto jāyate vājy asya ||
  • īśānāya: This term refers to the divine or supreme authority, often associated with the concept of a higher power or God.
  • prahutiṁ: This means offering or sacrifice, indicating a gesture of devotion or submission to the divine.
  • yas: Translates to "who" or "that," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • ta: This means "that" or "those," emphasizing the connection to the divine.
  • ānaṭ: This term can be interpreted as "eternal" or "everlasting," suggesting the timeless nature of the divine.
  • chuciṁ: This means "pure" or "sacred," highlighting the importance of purity in offerings and intentions.
  • somaṁ: Refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine blessings.
  • śucipās: This term means "pure" or "clean," reinforcing the idea of purity in both the offering and the recipient.
  • tubhyaṁ: This translates to "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine.
  • vāyo: Refers to the wind or air, often symbolizing the life force or spirit.
  • kṛṇoṣi: This means "you create" or "you make," suggesting the active role of the divine in the world.
  • tam: This means "that," again referring to the divine presence.
  • martyeṣu: This translates to "among mortals," indicating the connection between the divine and human beings.
  • praśastaṁ: This means "praiseworthy" or "glorious," emphasizing the qualities of the divine.
  • jāto-jāto: This phrase can be interpreted as "born again," suggesting the cyclical nature of life and rebirth.
  • jāyate: This means "is born" or "comes into being," indicating the manifestation of the divine in the world.
  • vājy: This term can be understood as "worthy" or "deserving," highlighting the importance of merit in spiritual pursuits.
  • asya: This means "of this," referring to the divine presence and its significance in the lives of mortals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine and humanity, highlighting the importance of purity, devotion, and the cyclical nature of existence. In real life, it serves as a reminder to approach life with reverence and to recognize the divine in our actions and offerings. It encourages individuals to cultivate purity in their thoughts and deeds, fostering a deeper connection with the spiritual realm. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or moments of gratitude, reinforcing the significance of acknowledging the divine presence in everyday life.
