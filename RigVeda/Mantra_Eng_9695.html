परा देहि शामुल्यम् ब्रह्मभ्यो वि भजावसु |कृत्यैषा पद्वती भूत्व्याः जाया वि विशते पतिम् ||
parā dehi śāmulyam brahmabhyo vi bhajā vasu | kṛtyaiṣā padvatī bhūtvy ā jāyā viśate patim ||
  • parā dehi: This phrase translates to "give me the supreme" or "bestow upon me the ultimate." It signifies a request for the highest form of knowledge or enlightenment.
  • śāmulyam: This word can be interpreted as "wholeness" or "completeness." It suggests a desire for unity and integration in one's life.
  • brahmabhyo: This term refers to "the Brahman" or "the ultimate reality." It indicates a connection to the divine or the universal consciousness.
  • vi bhajā vasu: This can be understood as "may the wealth be distributed." It implies a sharing of resources and blessings among all beings.
  • kṛtyaiṣā: This word means "this action" or "this deed." It emphasizes the importance of actions in achieving one's goals.
  • padvatī: This term can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way." It signifies the journey one undertakes in life.
  • bhūtvy: This word refers to "existence" or "being." It highlights the importance of being present and aware in the moment.
  • ā jāyā: This phrase means "may it be born" or "may it arise." It signifies the emergence of new possibilities and opportunities.
  • viśate patim: This translates to "may it enter the lord" or "may it reach the master." It indicates a desire for guidance and support from a higher power.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual request for enlightenment, unity, and the sharing of resources. It emphasizes the significance of actions and the journey of life, urging individuals to be present and open to new possibilities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community building, and spiritual practices. By seeking the ultimate knowledge and sharing blessings, individuals can foster a sense of interconnectedness and purpose in their lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
