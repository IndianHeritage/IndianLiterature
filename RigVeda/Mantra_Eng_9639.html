य इमा विश्वा भुवनानि जुह्वद ऋषिर होतā न्य असीदत पिता नः |स आशीषा द्रविणम् इच्छमानः प्रथमच्छद अवराम् आविवेश ||
ya imā viśvā bhuvanāni juhvad ṛṣir hotā ny asīdat pitā naḥ | sa āśiṣā draviṇam icchamānaḥ prathamacchad avarām̐ ā viveśa ||
  • ya imā viśvā bhuvanāni juhvad: This phrase can be translated as "He who offers to all the worlds." Here, the term "viśvā" refers to the entirety of the universe or all realms of existence. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who contribute to the welfare of society, such as philanthropists or community leaders who work for the betterment of all.
  • ṛṣir hotā ny asīdat pitā naḥ: This translates to "The sage who is the priest, our father." The word "ṛṣir" refers to a sage or seer, someone who possesses deep wisdom. In contemporary terms, this could represent mentors or educators who guide us in our lives, akin to a father figure who imparts knowledge and values.
  • sa āśiṣā draviṇam icchamānaḥ: This means "He, desiring wealth with blessings." The term "draviṇam" refers to wealth or resources. In today's context, this can be seen in entrepreneurs or innovators who seek to create wealth not just for themselves but for the community, emphasizing the importance of ethical wealth creation.
  • prathamacchad avarām̐ ā viveśa: This translates to "He entered the first covering." The "first covering" can symbolize the initial stages of creation or existence. In real life, this can be interpreted as the beginning of any venture or project, where foundational work is crucial for success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals within the universe and the importance of wisdom, guidance, and ethical wealth creation. It highlights the role of sages and mentors in our lives and encourages us to contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to act selflessly, seek knowledge, and create wealth that benefits not just themselves but the larger community. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of responsibility and interconnectedness in our actions and aspirations.
