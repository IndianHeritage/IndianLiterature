एता विश्वा विदुषे तुभ्यं वेधो नीतानि अग्ने निन्यā वचांसि |निवचनā कवये काव्यान्य अशंषिषम् मतिभिर विप्र उक्थैः ||
etā viśvā viduṣe tubhyaṁ vedho nīthāny agne niṇyā vacāṁsi | nivacanā kavaye kāvyāny aśaṁsiṣam matibhir vipra ukthaiḥ ||
  • etā viśvā viduṣe tubhyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "All these (mantras) are directed towards you, O wise one." Here, "etā" means "these," "viśvā" means "all," "viduṣe" refers to "the wise one," and "tubhyaṁ" means "to you." This indicates a reverence towards a higher intelligence or divine being.
  • vedho nīthāny agne: This translates to "the knowledge of the Vedas, O Agni." "Vedho" refers to "of the Vedas," "nīthāny" means "knowledge," and "Agne" is a reference to Agni, the fire god. This highlights the importance of Vedic knowledge and its connection to the divine.
  • niṇyā vacāṁsi: This means "bring forth the words." "Niṇyā" translates to "bring forth" or "lead," and "vacāṁsi" means "words" or "speech." This suggests a call for divine inspiration in communication and expression.
  • nivacanā kavaye: This translates to "for the poet's utterance." "Nivacanā" means "utterance" or "expression," and "kavaye" refers to "the poet." This emphasizes the role of poets and sages in conveying wisdom and knowledge through their words.
  • kāvyāny aśaṁsiṣam: This means "the poetic compositions." "Kāvyāny" refers to "poetic works," and "aśaṁsiṣam" means "to be composed." This highlights the significance of poetry in expressing profound truths and emotions.
  • matibhir vipra ukthaiḥ: This translates to "with the thoughts of the wise." "Matibhir" means "with thoughts," "vipra" refers to "the wise" or "the learned," and "ukthaiḥ" means "with hymns." This indicates that the wisdom of the learned is essential in the creation and recitation of hymns.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, knowledge, and poetic expression in connecting with the divine. It signifies that all forms of communication and artistic expression should be directed towards a higher understanding and should be inspired by divine wisdom. In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as literature, education, and spirituality, where the pursuit of knowledge and the expression of ideas are essential. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to use their words thoughtfully, whether in writing, speaking, or creating art, thereby fostering a deeper connection with the world around them and the divine.
