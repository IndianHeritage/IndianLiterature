जम्भयताम अभितो रायतः शुनो हतम मृधो विदथुस तान्य अश्विनाः |वाचं-वाचं जरितू रत्निनीं कृतम उभा शंसं नासत्यावतं मम ||
jambhayatam abhito rāyataḥ śuno hatam mṛdho vidathus tāny aśvinā | vācaṁ-vācaṁ jaritū ratninīṁ kṛtam ubhā śaṁsaṁ nāsatyāvatam mama ||
  • Mantra: "jambhayatam abhito rāyataḥ śuno hatam mṛdho vidathus tāny aśvinā | vācaṁ-vācaṁ jaritū ratninīṁ kṛtam ubhā śaṁsaṁ nāsatyāvatam mama ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jambhayatam: To be awakened or to be roused.
    • abhito: From all sides or around.
    • rāyataḥ: The rays or the light.
    • śuno: Of the sound or the voice.
    • hatam: Struck down or defeated.
    • mṛdho: From the earth or the ground.
    • vidathus: Of the assembly or the gathering.
    • tāny: Those.
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen.
    • vācaṁ-vācaṁ: Speech or words.
    • jaritū: To be made or created.
    • ratninīṁ: Precious or valuable.
    • kṛtam: Done or made.
    • ubhā: Both.
    • śaṁsaṁ: Praise or commendation.
    • nāsatyāvatam: Of the Nāsatyas, another name for the Ashvins.
    • mama: My or mine.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • The phrase "jambhayatam abhito rāyataḥ" suggests a call for awakening and illumination from all directions, symbolizing the need for guidance and clarity in life.
    • "śuno hatam mṛdho" indicates the defeat of ignorance or obstacles that may arise from the earthly realm, emphasizing the importance of overcoming challenges.
    • The repetition of "vācaṁ-vācaṁ" highlights the significance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality.
    • "ratninīṁ kṛtam ubhā" refers to the creation of something precious, suggesting that our words and actions can lead to valuable outcomes.
    • The concluding part "nāsatyāvatam mama" personalizes the invocation, indicating a desire for the blessings of the Ashvins in one's own life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity and guidance in decision-making processes, especially during challenging times.
    • In personal relationships, the emphasis on communication ("vācaṁ-vācaṁ") can serve as a reminder to express oneself clearly and listen actively to others.
    • During times of adversity, invoking the qualities of the Ashvins can inspire individuals to overcome obstacles and strive for valuable achievements.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking guidance, overcoming challenges, and the transformative power of words.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to awaken their potential, communicate effectively, and create valuable outcomes in their lives.
    • By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins, one can cultivate a mindset of positivity and resilience, applicable in various aspects of life.
