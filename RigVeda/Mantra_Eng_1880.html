ओ सुष्टुत इन्द्र याह्य अर्वाङ् उप ब्रह्माणि मान्यस्य कारोः |विद्याम वस्तोर अवसाऽ गृणन्तो विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
o suṣṭuta indra yāhy arvāṅ upa brahmāṇi mānyasya kāroḥ | vidyāma vastor avasā gṛṇanto vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • Mantra: "o suṣṭuta indra yāhy arvāṅ upa brahmāṇi mānyasya kāroḥ | vidyāma vastor avasā gṛṇanto vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, who is well praised, come here to us; we honor the great power of the divine. We seek knowledge of the essence of things, and we sing of that knowledge which brings us strength."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Indra: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of thunder and rain. He symbolizes strength and power.
    • Suṣṭuta: This term means "well praised" or "well spoken," indicating the reverence and respect for Indra.
    • Brahmāṇi: Refers to the divine or the ultimate reality, often associated with knowledge and wisdom.
    • Vidyā: This means "knowledge" or "wisdom," which is essential for understanding the world and oneself.
    • Vṛjana: This term can be interpreted as "strength" or "power," indicating the force that knowledge can provide.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of Indra by seeking wisdom and strength to guide their teams through challenges. They may hold meetings to discuss strategies, emphasizing the importance of collective knowledge.
    • In educational institutions, students and teachers come together to honor the pursuit of knowledge, much like the invocation of Indra. They celebrate achievements and encourage each other to seek deeper understanding.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on this mantra to invoke inner strength and clarity, seeking to understand their true essence and purpose in life.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of understanding the essence of things and harnessing that knowledge for strength and empowerment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and personal growth, reminding us that wisdom and strength are interconnected and essential for navigating life's challenges.
