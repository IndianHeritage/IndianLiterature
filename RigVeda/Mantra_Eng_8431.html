अत्यो न हियानो अभि वाजम् अर्ष स्वरवित कोशं दिवो अद्रिमातरम् |वृषा पवित्रे अधि सानो अव्यये सोमः पुनान इन्द्रियाय धायसे ||
atyo na hiyāno abhi vājam arṣa svarvit kośaṁ divo adrimātaram | vṛṣā pavitre adhi sāno avyaye somaḥ punāna indriyāya dhāyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atyo: beyond, surpassing
    • na: not
    • hiyāno: to be restrained, held back
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • vājam: strength, vigor
    • arṣa: sacred, divine
    • svarvit: that which is made manifest, revealed
    • kośaṁ: treasure, wealth
    • divo: of the heavens
    • adrimātaram: the one who is the source of mountains, the earth
    • vṛṣā: the bull, symbolizing strength
    • pavitre: pure, sacred
    • adhi: upon, over
    • sāno: the one who is the essence, the core
    • avyaye: imperishable, eternal
    • somaḥ: the divine nectar, often associated with immortality
    • punāna: purifying, cleansing
    • indriyāya: for the senses, the faculties of perception
    • dhāyase: you bestow, you grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of transcending limitations and not being held back from achieving strength and divine qualities.
    • The reference to "somaḥ" indicates a connection to the divine nectar that purifies and grants immortality, symbolizing the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment.
    • The mention of "indriyāya" emphasizes the importance of the senses in experiencing the world and attaining knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often face challenges that can restrain their potential. By embracing the essence of this mantra, one can strive to surpass these limitations and achieve greater success.
    • In personal development, the idea of purifying one's thoughts and actions can lead to a more fulfilling life. Engaging in practices such as meditation can help in achieving this state of purity.
    • In relationships, understanding and enhancing our sensory perceptions can lead to deeper connections with others, allowing for more meaningful interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to transcend limitations and embrace purity and strength in all aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional success, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment and connection.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life that is rich in purpose, clarity, and strength, ultimately leading to a more profound understanding of oneself and the universe.
