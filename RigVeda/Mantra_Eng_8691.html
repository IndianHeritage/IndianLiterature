इन्द्राय वृषणं मदं पवस्व विश्वदर्शतः |सहस्रयामां पथिकृत् विचक्षणः ||
indrāya vṛṣaṇam madam pavasva viśvadarśataḥ | sahasrayāmā pathikṛd vicakṣaṇaḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and leadership.
  • Vṛṣaṇam: This term means "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful." It signifies the qualities of vigor and vitality.
  • Mada: This word translates to "intoxication" or "joy." It represents a state of bliss or ecstasy, often associated with divine experiences.
  • Pavasva: This means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It indicates a desire for spiritual and physical purification.
  • Viśvadarśataḥ: This term means "of universal vision" or "seeing all." It suggests a perspective that encompasses the entirety of existence.
  • Sahasrayāmā: This translates to "thousands" or "many." It implies abundance and a multitude of blessings.
  • Pathikṛd: This word means "the one who creates paths" or "the guide." It signifies guidance and direction in life.
  • Vicakṣaṇaḥ: This term means "wise" or "perceptive." It refers to someone who has insight and understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, asking for strength, joy, and purification while seeking a universal perspective and guidance in life. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the ability to navigate through life's challenges with clarity and insight.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom.
  • Personal Growth: The call for purification and joy can motivate individuals to seek personal development, letting go of negativity and embracing positivity.
  • Guidance: In times of uncertainty, invoking the qualities of a path creator can help individuals find direction and make informed decisions.
  • Community and Collaboration: The idea of universal vision encourages collaboration and understanding among diverse groups, fostering harmony and cooperation.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities of strength, joy, and wisdom that are essential for personal and communal growth. It encourages individuals to seek purification and clarity in their lives while embracing a broader perspective that connects them to the universe and others around them.
