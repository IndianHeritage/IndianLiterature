मन्त्र: नहि नु याद अधीमसीन्द्रं को वीर्यापरः |तस्मिन् नृम्णमुत क्रतुं देवाः ओजांस्य सं दधुर् अर्चन्न् अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
nahi nu yād adhīmasīndraṁ ko vīryā paraḥ | tasmin nṛmṇam uta kratuṁ devā ojāṁsi saṁ dadhur arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, we do not know of any other power greater than Indra. In him, the gods have placed their strength and wisdom, and they have offered their worship to him, seeking his sovereignty."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi nu: Indeed, we do not.
    • yād: any other.
    • adhīmasīndraṁ: greater than Indra.
    • ko: who.
    • vīryā: power or strength.
    • paraḥ: greater.
    • tasmin: in him.
    • nṛmṇam: human beings.
    • uta: or.
    • kratuṁ: wisdom or intellect.
    • devā: gods.
    • ojāṁsi: strength or energy.
    • saṁ dadhur: have placed.
    • arcann: worshiping.
    • anu: seeking.
    • svarājyam: sovereignty or rule.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the supremacy of Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • In real life, this can be related to the idea of recognizing and respecting authority and leadership in various aspects of life, such as in governance, community leadership, or even in personal relationships.
    • For example, in a workplace, acknowledging the leadership of a manager or a team leader can foster a collaborative environment where everyone works towards a common goal.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength and wisdom in leadership.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance from those in positions of authority while also recognizing the collective strength that comes from unity and worship.
    • In a broader sense, it can be applied to societal structures where respect for leadership and the pursuit of collective goals are essential for progress.
