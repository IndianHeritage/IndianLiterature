अपाम अतिष्ठद धरणह्वरं तमोऽन्तर वृत्रस्य जटहरेषु पर्वतः |अभीम इन्द्रो नद्यो ववृिणा हिताऽविश्वानुश्ठाः प्रवणेषु जिघ्नते ||
apām atiṣṭhad dharuṇahvaraṁ tamo 'ntar vṛtrasya jaṭhareṣu parvataḥ | abhīm indro nadyo vavriṇā hitā viśvā anuṣṭhāḥ pravaṇeṣu jighnate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apām: of the waters
    • atiṣṭhad: has risen or has ascended
    • dharuṇahvaraṁ: the strong or powerful
    • tamo: darkness
    • 'ntar: within
    • vṛtrasya: of Vritra (the demon of drought)
    • jaṭhareṣu: in the bellies
    • parvataḥ: mountains
    • abhīm: fearless
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • nadyo: rivers
    • vavriṇā: has conquered
    • hitā: beneficial
    • viśvā: all
    • anuṣṭhāḥ: actions or deeds
    • pravaṇeṣu: in the flowing
    • jighnate: slays or destroys
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful forces of nature, particularly water and its ability to overcome darkness and obstacles.
    • It highlights the role of Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is depicted as a fearless warrior conquering the demon Vritra, symbolizing drought and obstruction.
    • The imagery of mountains and rivers suggests the natural landscape where these forces interact, emphasizing the importance of water in sustaining life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of monsoon rains is crucial for crop growth. Just as Indra conquers Vritra, farmers rely on the rains to overcome the challenges of drought.
    • In environmental conservation, efforts to restore rivers and wetlands can be seen as a modern-day battle against the 'Vritras' of pollution and climate change, ensuring that water remains a life-giving force.
    • In personal life, overcoming fears and obstacles can be likened to Indra's fearless nature, inspiring individuals to confront their challenges with courage.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the triumph of natural forces over darkness and adversity, symbolizing hope and renewal.
    • It teaches us the importance of resilience and the need to respect and protect our natural resources.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to embody the qualities of courage and determination in their own lives, facing challenges head-on and striving for a better future.
