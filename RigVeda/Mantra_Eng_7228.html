उप मां षड् द्वाद्व नरः सोमस्य हर्ष्या |तिष्ठन्ति स्वादुरातयः ||
upa mā ṣaḍ dvā-dvā naraḥ somasya harṣyā | tiṣṭhanti svādurātayaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • upa: near, towards
    • mā: not, do not
    • ṣaḍ: six
    • dvā-dvā: two by two
    • naraḥ: men, humans
    • somasya: of the moon, related to the soma (a sacred drink)
    • harṣyā: joyful, filled with delight
    • tiṣṭhanti: they stand, they remain
    • svādurātayaḥ: sweet, pleasant, or delightful
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a group of six men who stand joyfully, near the delightful essence of the moon (soma).
    • The phrase "upa mā" suggests a call to come closer, while "ṣaḍ dvā-dvā" indicates a gathering of six individuals in pairs.
    • The reference to "somasya harṣyā" implies a connection to the divine or spiritual joy that comes from the sacred drink, soma, which is often associated with immortality and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to a group of friends or colleagues coming together to celebrate a significant achievement, such as a wedding or a promotion, where joy and camaraderie are shared.
    • In spiritual gatherings, such as yoga retreats or meditation sessions, participants often come together in pairs or small groups to share experiences, fostering a sense of community and collective joy.
    • In cultural festivals, people often gather in groups to partake in rituals that celebrate life, nature, and the divine, similar to the joyous gathering described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, joy, and the spiritual connection that can be found in shared experiences.
    • It serves as a reminder that coming together in celebration and joy can enhance our lives and deepen our connections with one another.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from personal relationships to community events, highlighting the significance of unity and shared happiness in our daily lives.
