अहं तद आसु धारयम् यद आसु न देवश चन त्वष्टाधारयद रुशत् |स्पार्हं गवाम ऊधःसु वक्षाणास्वा मधोर मधु श्वात्र्यं सोमम् आशिरम् ||
ahaṁ tad āsu dhārayaṁ yad āsu na devaś cana tvaṣṭādhārayad ruśat | spārhaṁ gavām ūdhaḥsu vakṣaṇāsv ā madhor madhu śvātryaṁ somam āśiram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahaṁ: I
    • tad: that
    • āsu: in the essence
    • dhārayaṁ: I hold
    • yad: which
    • āsu: in the essence
    • na: not
    • devaś: the divine
    • cana: or
    • tvaṣṭā: the creator
    • adhārayat: has held
    • ruśat: in anger
    • spārhaṁ: the desire
    • gavām: of the cows
    • ūdhaḥsu: in the heights
    • vakṣaṇāsu: in the bosoms
    • ā: in
    • madhor: of sweetness
    • madhu: honey
    • śvātryaṁ: the essence
    • somam: of the moon
    • āśiram: the blessing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the speaker's connection to the divine essence and the creative force that sustains life.
    • The phrase "I hold that which is in the essence" suggests a deep understanding of one's own existence and the interconnectedness with the universe.
    • The mention of "the divine" and "the creator" indicates a recognition of higher powers that govern the cosmos.
    • The reference to "the desire of the cows" symbolizes abundance and nourishment, which can be interpreted as the sustenance provided by nature.
    • The imagery of "sweetness" and "the essence of the moon" evokes feelings of tranquility and harmony, suggesting that one should seek balance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the practice of mindfulness, where individuals hold onto their essence and remain aware of their thoughts and actions.
    • In agriculture, the reference to cows can be seen in sustainable farming practices that honor the earth and promote abundance.
    • In relationships, the idea of sweetness and harmony can guide individuals to foster loving and supportive connections with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing one's own essence and the divine forces at play in the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to seek balance, abundance, and harmony in their lives, reminding them of their interconnectedness with nature and the cosmos.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the world, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
