मित्राय पञ्च येमिरे जनाऽभिष्टिष्वसे |स देवान् विश्वान् बिभर्ति ||
mitrāya pañca yemire janā abhiṣṭiśavase | sa devān viśvān bibharti ||
  • Mantra: "mitrāya pañca yemire janā abhiṣṭiśavase | sa devān viśvān bibharti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mitrāya: To Mitra, the deity representing friendship and harmony.
    • pañca: Five, which can symbolize completeness or the five elements.
    • yemire: Those who have come or those who are present.
    • janā: People or beings.
    • abhiṣṭiśavase: To the one who grants desires or blessings.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • devān: The gods or divine beings.
    • viśvān: All or the universe.
    • bibharti: Sustains or supports.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Mitra, emphasizing the importance of friendship and unity among people.
    • The mention of "five" can represent the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that are essential for life.
    • It highlights the idea that those who come together in harmony are blessed and supported by divine forces.
    • The mantra suggests that the divine sustains all beings and the universe, reinforcing the interconnectedness of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when people work together harmoniously, they can achieve greater goals, much like a team in a workplace where collaboration leads to success.
    • In personal relationships, fostering friendship and understanding can lead to a supportive environment where individuals thrive.
    • Environmental initiatives that bring together diverse groups to protect nature exemplify the unity and support that this mantra speaks of.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, friendship, and divine support in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community efforts and environmental sustainability.
    • By embracing the principles of harmony and collaboration, we can create a more supportive and prosperous world.
