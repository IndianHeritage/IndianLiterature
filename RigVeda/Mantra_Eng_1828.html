किम् न इन्द्र जिघांससि भ्रातरः मरुतः तव |तेभिः कल्पस्व साधुया मा नः समरणे वधिः ||
kiṁ na indra jighāṁsasi bhrātaro marutas tava | tebhiḥ kalpasva sādhuyā mā naḥ samaraṇe vadhīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ - What
    • na - not
    • indra - O Indra (the king of gods)
    • jighāṁsasi - do you wish to harm
    • bhrātaro - brothers
    • marutas - O Maruts (storm deities)
    • tava - your
    • tebhiḥ - with them
    • kalpasva - grant us
    • sādhuyā - with goodness
    • mā - may not
    • naḥ - us
    • samaraṇe - in battle
    • vadhīḥ - destruction
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, questioning why he would wish to harm his brothers, the Maruts.
    • It seeks protection and blessings from Indra and the Maruts, asking them to grant goodness and prevent destruction in battles.
    • The invocation reflects a plea for unity and harmony among divine forces, emphasizing the importance of brotherhood and cooperation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, this mantra can be likened to a team working together towards a common goal, where members must support each other rather than compete destructively.
    • In family dynamics, it highlights the importance of resolving conflicts amicably and maintaining harmony among siblings or family members.
    • In community settings, it serves as a reminder to work collaboratively for the greater good, avoiding actions that may harm others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, cooperation, and the avoidance of conflict among those who are closely related, whether in divine, familial, or social contexts.
    • Its application in real life encourages individuals to seek harmony and support one another, fostering a sense of community and collective strength.
    • By invoking the blessings of higher powers, it serves as a reminder to prioritize goodness and constructive actions over destructive tendencies.
