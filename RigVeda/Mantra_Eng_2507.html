प्र कारवो मननाऽवच्यमानā देवद्रीचīm नयत देवयन्तः |दक्षिणावाड् वाजिनी प्राच्य एति हविः भरन्त्य अग्नये घृताचī ||
pra kāravo mananā vacyamānā devadrīcīṁ nayata devayantaḥ | dakṣiṇāvāḍ vājinī prācy eti havir bharanty agnaye ghṛtācī ||
  • pra kāravo mananā vacyamānā: This phrase can be translated as "those who are engaged in thoughtful contemplation." It emphasizes the importance of reflection and meditation in our lives. For example, in modern life, individuals often engage in mindfulness practices to enhance their mental well-being.
  • devadrīcīṁ nayata devayantaḥ: This part means "let us be guided by the divine light." It suggests seeking guidance from higher powers or wisdom. In real life, this can be seen in how people often turn to spiritual leaders or texts for direction during challenging times.
  • dakṣiṇāvāḍ vājinī prācy eti: This translates to "the one who moves towards the right path." It signifies the importance of making wise choices and following a righteous path in life. For instance, individuals who choose ethical practices in their careers contribute positively to society.
  • havir bharanty agnaye ghṛtācī: This phrase means "they offer their sacrifices to the divine fire." It symbolizes the act of giving and devotion. In contemporary life, this can be reflected in charitable actions, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of others.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of contemplation, seeking divine guidance, making ethical choices, and the importance of devotion through acts of giving. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, ethical decision-making in professional life, and community service. By embodying these values, individuals can lead a more fulfilling and purposeful life, contributing positively to themselves and society at large.
