विद्मा सखित्वम् उता शूर भोज्यम् आ ते ता वज्रिन्नीमहे |उतो समस्मिन्न् आ शिशीहि नो वसो वाजे सुशिप्र गोमती ||
vidmā sakhitvam uta śūra bhojya1m ā te tā vajrinn īmahe | uto samasminn ā śiśīhi no vaso vāje suśipra gomati ||
  • vidmā: This word translates to "we know" or "we are aware." It signifies knowledge and understanding, which is fundamental in any aspect of life.
  • sakhitvam: This term refers to "friendship" or "companionship." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and support systems in our lives.
  • uta: This word means "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to the previous thought.
  • śūra: This translates to "brave" or "valiant." It highlights the qualities of courage and strength that are essential in overcoming challenges.
  • bhojya1m: This term can be interpreted as "worthy of being enjoyed" or "to be consumed." It suggests the importance of sharing and enjoying the fruits of our labor.
  • ā te: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction of intention or offering.
  • tā: This word translates to "that" or "those," referring to the qualities or attributes being discussed.
  • vajrinn: This term means "like a thunderbolt" or "powerful." It symbolizes strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
  • īmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a reflective and thoughtful approach to life.
  • uto: Again, this means "and" or "also," reinforcing the connection between ideas.
  • samāsminn: This term means "in this together" or "in this unity," emphasizing the importance of collective effort and harmony.
  • āśiśīhi: This translates to "may you protect" or "may you bless," indicating a plea for guidance and support.
  • no: This means "our" or "of us," indicating a sense of belonging and community.
  • vaso: This term can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • vāje: This translates to "in strength" or "in power," emphasizing the need for strength in our endeavors.
  • suśipra: This term means "well-being" or "good fortune," indicating a desire for positive outcomes.
  • gomati: This translates to "with wisdom" or "intelligence," highlighting the importance of wisdom in decision-making.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of knowledge, friendship, bravery, and the pursuit of well-being. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength in achieving prosperity and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in professional settings, building strong relationships in personal life, and encouraging a supportive environment in communities. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges with courage and wisdom, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
