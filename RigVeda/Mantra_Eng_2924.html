जायेद अस्तं मघवन् सेद उ योनिस तद इत त्वा युक्ता हरयो वहन्तु |यदा कदा च सुनवाम सोमं अग्निष ट्वा दूतो धन्वात्य अच्छ ||
jāyed astam maghavan sed u yonis tad it tvā yuktā harayo vahantu | yadā kadā ca sunavāma somam agniṣ ṭvā dūto dhanvāty accha ||
  • Translation: "May the powerful ones (the Maghavan) bring forth prosperity and strength to you, O Agni. Whenever we invoke you with our offerings, may the Harayas (the divine beings) carry our prayers to you. Whenever we call upon you, O Agni, may you, as our messenger, grant us wealth and abundance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • jāyed: May it come to be; a wish for prosperity.
    • astam: Powerful or strong; referring to the divine forces.
    • maghavan: A term for the divine, often associated with wealth and abundance.
    • sed: To bring forth or to produce.
    • u: And; a conjunction linking thoughts.
    • yonis: Source or origin; referring to the divine source of blessings.
    • tad: That; referring to the divine presence.
    • it: Here; indicating the immediate presence of the divine.
    • tvā: You; addressing Agni directly.
    • yuktā: Connected or united; referring to the divine beings working together.
    • harayo: The divine beings or forces; often associated with the sun and light.
    • vahantu: May they carry; a request for the divine to take our prayers.
    • yadā: Whenever; indicating the frequency of invocation.
    • kadā: At any time; emphasizing the timeless nature of the invocation.
    • ca: Also; adding to the previous thought.
    • sunavāma: We call upon; a direct invocation to the divine.
    • somam: The sacred drink; often associated with immortality and divine blessings.
    • agniṣ: O Agni; addressing the fire god, a symbol of purity and transformation.
    • dūto: Messenger; referring to Agni as a carrier of prayers.
    • dhanvāty: Granting wealth; a request for abundance.
    • accha: To bestow or to give; a plea for divine generosity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to the practice of setting intentions during meditation or prayer. Just as the mantra invokes divine blessings, individuals today often seek guidance and support from a higher power in their personal and professional lives.
    • In business, leaders may invoke the spirit of collaboration and prosperity, similar to how the mantra calls upon divine beings to carry prayers. This can be seen in team-building exercises where the focus is on unity and shared goals.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often perform rituals to invite blessings, akin to the invocation of Agni in the mantra, seeking abundance and success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the interconnectedness of human efforts with the divine. It emphasizes the importance of intention and the belief that our prayers and actions can attract prosperity and abundance. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance, remain connected to our goals, and trust in the support of the universe as we navigate our journeys. Whether in personal aspirations or collective endeavors, invoking such blessings can inspire confidence and a sense of purpose.
