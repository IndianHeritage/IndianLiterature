यच चिद्धि ते विश्व यथा प्र देव वरुण व्रतम् |मिनिमासि द्यवि-द्यवि ||
yac cid dhi te viśo yathā pra deva varuṇa vratam | minīmasi dyavi-dyavi ||
  • Mantra: "yac cid dhi te viśo yathā pra deva varuṇa vratam | minīmasi dyavi-dyavi ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever is your will, O Varuna, as the divine law, we invoke you; may we be protected in the heavens."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yac: whatever
    • cid: is, exists
    • dhi: will, intention
    • te: your
    • viśo: community, people
    • yathā: as, in accordance with
    • pra: before, in front of
    • deva: divine, god
    • varuṇa: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • vratam: law, vow, promise
    • minīmasi: we invoke, we call upon
    • dyavi-dyavi: in the heavens, celestial realms

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with cosmic order and moral law.
    • The phrase "whatever is your will" signifies a deep respect for the divine will and an acknowledgment of the higher order that governs the universe.
    • By invoking Varuna, the speaker seeks protection and guidance, emphasizing the importance of divine support in human affairs.
    • The reference to "heavens" suggests a connection to higher consciousness and spiritual realms, indicating that the divine law is not just a set of rules but a guiding principle for living harmoniously.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during gatherings to invoke unity and collective intention, reminding everyone of the importance of working together in accordance with a higher purpose.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on this mantra during times of decision-making, seeking clarity and alignment with their own values and the greater good.
    • In environmental contexts, invoking Varuna can symbolize respect for nature and the laws that govern ecological balance, encouraging sustainable practices.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of aligning personal and communal actions with divine will and cosmic order.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking guidance from higher principles in our daily lives.
    • By incorporating this mantra into rituals or personal reflections, individuals and communities can foster a sense of purpose, responsibility, and harmony with the universe.
