दां नो अग्नि धियाऽ रयिं सुवीरं स्वपत्यम् सहस्य प्रशस्तम् |न यं यावात् तरति यातुमावान् ||
dā no agne dhiyā rayiṁ suvīraṁ svapatyaṁ sahasya praśastam | na yaṁ yāvā tarati yātumāvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dā: Give
    • no: to us
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dhiyā: with intellect
    • rayiṁ: wealth
    • suvīraṁ: excellent strength
    • svapatyaṁ: prosperity
    • sahasya: courage
    • praśastam: commendable
    • na: not
    • yaṁ: who
    • yāvā: as long as
    • tarati: crosses
    • yātumāvān: the one who is capable of going
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for the bestowal of wealth, strength, prosperity, and courage.
    • It emphasizes the importance of intellect and wisdom in acquiring these qualities.
    • The phrase "na yaṁ yāvā tarati yātumāvān" suggests that as long as one possesses these qualities, they can overcome challenges and obstacles in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may seek the guidance of a mentor (Agni) to gain wisdom (dhiyā) and achieve success (rayiṁ) in their career.
    • A student may invoke this mantra while preparing for exams, focusing on acquiring knowledge (intellect) and the courage to face challenges.
    • In personal relationships, one may strive for commendable qualities (praśastam) such as empathy and understanding, which can lead to stronger bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and the role of intellect and courage in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, including personal growth, professional development, and interpersonal relationships.
    • By embodying the qualities of wisdom, strength, and courage, individuals can navigate life's challenges effectively and achieve their goals.
