स्वश्वा यशसा यातम अर्वाग दसराः निधिम मधुमन्तं पिबाथः |वि वां रथो वध्वा यादमानो अन्तान् दिवो बाधते वर्तनिभ्यां ||
svaśvā yaśasā yātam arvāg dasrā nidhim madhumantam pibāthaḥ | vi vāṁ ratho vadhvā3 yādamāno 'ntān divo bādhate vartanibhyām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svaśvā: Your own horse or strength
    • yaśasā: With fame or glory
    • yātam: May you go or proceed
    • arvāg: To the front or forward
    • dasrā: With a clear vision or sight
    • nidhim: Treasure or wealth
    • madhumantam: Sweet or pleasant
    • pibāthaḥ: Drink or consume
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'with' or 'together'
    • vāṁ: Your
    • ratho: Chariot or vehicle
    • vadhvā: With strength or power
    • yādamāno: Moving or advancing
    • 'ntān: Towards the end or goal
    • divo: Of the heavens or divine
    • bādhate: Is obstructed or hindered
    • vartanibhyām: By the actions or deeds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of personal strength and glory in achieving one's goals.
    • It suggests that one should move forward with clarity and vision, seeking the treasures of life that are sweet and fulfilling.
    • The reference to the chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where one must harness their inner strength to navigate challenges.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea that one's actions can either facilitate or obstruct their progress towards their goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A student preparing for exams can relate to this mantra by focusing on their studies (svaśvā) to achieve academic success (yaśasā).
    • An entrepreneur may embody this mantra by leveraging their unique skills (svaśvā) to build a successful business (nidhim) while navigating market challenges (vadhvā).
    • In personal relationships, one can apply this mantra by fostering clear communication (dasrā) to strengthen bonds and resolve conflicts (bādhate).
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to harness one's inner strength and clarity to pursue goals.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions shape our journey and outcomes in life.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in education, business, or personal relationships, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to success and fulfillment.
