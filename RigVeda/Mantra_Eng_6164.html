आद इत प्रात्नस्य रेतसो ज्योतिष् पश्यन्ति वासरम् |परो यद इध्यते दिवा ||
ād it pratnasya retaso jyotiṣ paśyanti vāsaram | paro yad idhyate divā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: Beginning or origin
    • it: This or that
    • pratnasya: Of the seeker or the one who is searching
    • retaso: Of the essence or the vital energy
    • jyotiṣ: Light or illumination
    • paśyanti: They see or perceive
    • vāsaram: The day or the time
    • paro: Beyond or higher
    • yad: That which
    • idhyate: Is manifested or comes into being
    • divā: In the daytime or during the day
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the beginning of perception and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking the essence of life and the illumination that comes from it.
    • The phrase "they see the light of the essence" suggests that those who seek knowledge and truth will find clarity and understanding.
    • The reference to "the day" symbolizes enlightenment and awareness, contrasting with ignorance, which is often associated with darkness.
    • The idea of something "beyond" indicates that there is a higher truth or reality that can be realized through this pursuit.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who actively seek knowledge and understanding often achieve greater clarity in their subjects, akin to "seeing the light."
    • In personal development, individuals who engage in self-reflection and mindfulness practices can experience profound insights, representing the "illumination" of their inner essence.
    • In scientific exploration, researchers who delve deeply into their fields often uncover truths that were previously hidden, paralleling the idea of seeking beyond the ordinary.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding as a means to achieve enlightenment.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond the surface and strive for deeper truths in their lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in education, personal growth, or scientific inquiry, the pursuit of knowledge leads to greater awareness and clarity, embodying the essence of this mantra.
