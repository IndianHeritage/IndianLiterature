स त्वं नो अर्वन् निदायā विश्वेभिर अग्ने अग्निभिर इधानः | वेषि रायो वि यासि दुष्चुनā मदेम शतहिमाः सुवीराः ||
sa tvaṁ no arvan nidāyā viśvebhir agne agnibhir idhānaḥ | veṣi rāyo vi yāsi ducchunā madema śatahimāḥ suvīrāḥ ||
  • sa tvaṁ no arvan nidāyā viśvebhir agne agnibhir idhānaḥ
    • sa tvaṁ: "You" - This refers to the divine presence or energy, often interpreted as Agni, the fire god.
    • no arvan: "our protector" - This indicates a plea for protection and guidance.
    • nidāyā: "in our endeavors" - This suggests the importance of divine support in our actions.
    • viśvebhir: "with all" - This emphasizes inclusivity and the collective nature of existence.
    • agne: "O Agni" - Directly addressing the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • agnibhir: "with the fires" - Referring to the various forms of fire, representing different aspects of life and energy.
    • idhānaḥ: "may you come" - A call for the divine presence to manifest in our lives.
  • veṣi rāyo vi yāsi ducchunā madema śatahimāḥ suvīrāḥ
    • veṣi: "may you bestow" - A request for blessings and abundance.
    • rāyo: "wealth" - This refers to prosperity, both material and spiritual.
    • vi yāsi: "you may go" - Suggesting movement or progress towards a goal.
    • ducchunā: "with ease" - A desire for smoothness in our endeavors.
    • madema: "let us be joyful" - A call for happiness and fulfillment in life.
    • śatahimāḥ: "with a hundredfold" - A wish for abundance and prosperity in all aspects.
    • suvīrāḥ: "with great strength" - A plea for courage and resilience in facing challenges.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound invocation for divine guidance, protection, and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of seeking support from the divine in our endeavors, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal challenges, individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance, fostering a sense of resilience.
  • In communal gatherings or rituals, it can be used to invoke collective blessings for prosperity and harmony.
  • In professional settings, it serves as a reminder to seek divine support in achieving goals and overcoming obstacles.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to strive for abundance, joy, and strength, while recognizing the divine presence in their lives.
