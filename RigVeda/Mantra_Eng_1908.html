मन्त्र: असर्जि वां स्थविरा वेधसा गीर बाळ्हे अश्विनाऽ त्रेधā क्षरन्ती |उपस्तुताव अवतं नाधमानं यामन्न अयामञ्च श्रिणुतं हवम् मे ||
asarji vāṁ sthavirā vedhasā gīr bāḻhe aśvinā tredhā kṣarantī | upastutāv avataṁ nādhamānaṁ yāmann ayāmañ chṛṇutaṁ havam me ||
  • asarji vāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be strong" or "may you be powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in life.
  • sthavirā vedhasā: This translates to "with the wisdom of the ancients." It suggests that one should draw upon the knowledge and experiences of those who came before us, highlighting the value of tradition and learning from history.
  • gīr bāḻhe: This means "may the words be elevated." It signifies the importance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality and influencing others.
  • aśvinā tredhā kṣarantī: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine, this phrase can be understood as "the Aśvins who flow in three ways." It symbolizes the multifaceted nature of healing and support that can come from divine sources.
  • upastutāv avataṁ: This means "having been praised." It indicates the significance of recognition and appreciation in our lives, whether it be for our efforts or the efforts of others.
  • nādhamānaṁ: This translates to "not diminishing." It emphasizes the idea of growth and the importance of maintaining one's strength and integrity in the face of challenges.
  • yāmann ayāmañ: This can be interpreted as "to whom I call." It reflects the act of seeking help or guidance from higher powers or the universe.
  • chṛṇutaṁ havam me: This means "listen to my invocation." It is a plea for attention and support, underscoring the importance of being heard and understood in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, communication, and divine support. In real life, it serves as a reminder to draw upon the knowledge of our ancestors, to communicate effectively, and to seek help when needed. It encourages us to remain resilient and to appreciate the efforts of ourselves and others. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one needs to invoke strength and support, or in moments of decision-making where wisdom and guidance are essential. Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of our actions, words, and the divine, urging us to live with intention and purpose.
