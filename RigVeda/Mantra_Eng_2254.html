मन्त्र: ब्रह्मणस पतेर अभवद यथावशं सत्यो मन्युर महि कर्मा करिष्यतः | यो गा उदाजत स दिवे विचाभजन महीव रीतिः शवसासरत पृथक् ||
brahmaṇas pater abhavad yathāvaśaṁ satyo manyur mahi karmā kariṣyataḥ | yo gā udājat sa dive vi cābhajan mahīva rītiḥ śavasāsarat pṛthak ||
  • Brahmaṇas pater abhavad: This phrase translates to "the father of the Brahmanas (the learned or wise) has come into being." Here, "Brahmanas" refers to those who possess knowledge and wisdom, often associated with the priestly class in Vedic society.
  • yathāvaśaṁ satyo manyur: This means "as it is desired, the true anger." It suggests that true emotions, such as anger, should be expressed appropriately and in accordance with one's intentions.
  • mahi karmā kariṣyataḥ: This translates to "the great deeds are to be performed." It emphasizes the importance of undertaking significant actions that contribute to the greater good.
  • yo gā udājat: This means "whoever has risen up." It signifies the idea of awakening or rising to a higher state of consciousness or awareness.
  • sa dive vi cābhajan: This translates to "he who has worshipped in the day." It implies the importance of devotion and worship in one's daily life.
  • mahīva rītiḥ: This means "like the great way." It suggests following a path that is noble and virtuous.
  • śavasāsarat pṛthak: This translates to "separate from the dead." It indicates the need to distance oneself from negativity or lifelessness, promoting a life filled with purpose and vitality.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of wisdom, appropriate emotional expression, and the performance of great deeds. It encourages individuals to rise to higher states of consciousness through devotion and to follow a noble path while distancing themselves from negativity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in leadership roles where wise decisions are crucial, in personal development through self-awareness and emotional intelligence, and in community service where significant actions can lead to positive change. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of living a life filled with purpose, wisdom, and devotion to the greater good.
