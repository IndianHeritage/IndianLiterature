तां वां विश्वस्य गोपा देवā देवेषु यज्ञियā |ऋतावानां यजसे पूतदक्षसā ||
tā vāṁ viśvasya gopā devā deveṣu yajñiyā | ṛtāvānā yajase pūtadakṣasā ||
  • tā vāṁ viśvasya gopā: This phrase can be translated as "those who are the protectors of the universe." Here, "tā" refers to "those," "vāṁ" means "you," "viśvasya" translates to "of the universe," and "gopā" means "protectors" or "guardians." In real life, this can be likened to individuals or entities that safeguard the environment, culture, or community, such as environmental activists or community leaders.
  • devā deveṣu yajñiyā: This part translates to "the divine beings who are worthy of worship." "Devā" means "gods" or "divine beings," "deveṣu" refers to "among the gods," and "yajñiyā" means "worthy of offerings or sacrifices." This can be related to the respect and reverence we show towards those who contribute positively to society, such as teachers, mentors, or spiritual leaders.
  • ṛtāvānā yajase: This phrase means "you who perform the righteous acts." "ṛtāvānā" translates to "those who are established in truth or righteousness," and "yajase" means "you perform." In a real-life context, this can refer to individuals who engage in ethical practices, such as honest businesspeople or social workers who strive to make a difference.
  • pūtadakṣasā: This can be interpreted as "those who are pure and skilled." "Pūta" means "pure," and "dakṣasā" translates to "skilled" or "capable." This highlights the importance of purity in intentions and actions, akin to professionals who uphold integrity in their work, such as healthcare workers or scientists dedicated to their fields.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of guardianship, reverence for the divine, righteousness, and purity in actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embody these qualities in our daily lives, whether through environmental stewardship, respect for mentors, ethical practices in our professions, or maintaining purity in our intentions. By doing so, we contribute to a harmonious and balanced society, reflecting the essence of the mantra in our actions and interactions.
