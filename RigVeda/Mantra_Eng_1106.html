दस्यूँ चिम्यूँश च पुरुहूत एवैर हत्वा पृथिव्यां शर्वा नि बरहीत् |सनत क्षेत्रं सकिभिः श्वित्न्येभिः सनत सूर्यं सनद अपः सुवज्रः ||
dasyūñ chimyūm̐ś ca puruhūta evair hatvā pṛthivyāṁ śarvā ni barhīt | sanat kṣetraṁ sakhibhiḥ śvitnyebhiḥ sanat sūryaṁ sanad apaḥ suvajraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dasyūñ chimyūm̐ś ca puruhūta evair hatvā pṛthivyāṁ śarvā ni barhīt | sanat kṣetraṁ sakhibhiḥ śvitnyebhiḥ sanat sūryaṁ sanad apaḥ suvajraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dasyūñ: enemies or those who oppose
    • chimyūm̐ś: those who are deceitful or treacherous
    • ca: and
    • puruhūta: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • evair: by these
    • hatvā: having defeated
    • pṛthivyāṁ: on the earth
    • śarvā: all beings or creatures
    • ni: in
    • barhīt: the sacred grass or the place of worship
    • sanat: eternal or everlasting
    • kṣetraṁ: the field or domain
    • sakhibhiḥ: with friends or companions
    • śvitnyebhiḥ: with those who are pure or bright
    • sanat: eternal
    • sūryaṁ: the sun
    • sanad: eternal or everlasting
    • apaḥ: waters
    • suvajraḥ: well-formed or powerful
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about overcoming adversaries and deceitful individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support (puruhūta) to defeat negativity (dasyūñ and chimyūm̐ś).
    • The phrase "hatvā pṛthivyāṁ" suggests that this victory is not just spiritual but also has a tangible impact on the earthly realm.
    • It highlights the significance of community and friendship (sakhibhiḥ) in achieving success and overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of "sūryaṁ" symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness, which can be interpreted as ignorance or evil.
    • The reference to "apaḥ suvajraḥ" indicates the importance of pure and powerful resources, such as water, in sustaining life and supporting growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when facing competition or unethical practices. By invoking teamwork and integrity, one can overcome challenges.
    • In personal life, it can serve as a reminder to surround oneself with supportive friends and to seek wisdom and clarity in difficult situations.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to water and nature can inspire actions towards sustainability and the protection of natural resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity, integrity, and the pursuit of knowledge in overcoming adversities.
    • It teaches that with the right support and mindset, one can triumph over negativity and deceit.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to foster strong relationships, seek enlightenment, and protect the environment, leading to a harmonious existence.
