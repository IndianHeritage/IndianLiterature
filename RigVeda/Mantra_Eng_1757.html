तस्याः समुद्राः अधि वि क्षरन्ति तेन जीवन्ति प्रदिशश चतस्रः |ततः क्षरत्य अक्षरं तद विश्वं उप जीवति ||
tasyāḥ samudrā adhi vi kṣaranti tena jīvanti pradiśaś catasraḥ | tataḥ kṣaraty akṣaraṁ tad viśvam upa jīvati ||
  • tasyāḥ samudrā adhi vi kṣaranti: This phrase can be translated to "the oceans flow above." Here, "tasyāḥ" refers to "of that," "samudrā" means "oceans," "adhi" means "above," and "vi kṣaranti" translates to "they flow." This can be understood as the vastness and depth of the oceans, which are essential for life on Earth.
  • tena jīvanti pradiśaś catasraḥ: This translates to "by that, the four directions live." "Tena" means "by that," "jīvanti" means "they live," "pradiśaś" refers to "the directions," and "catasraḥ" means "four." This indicates that the life force or energy that sustains existence comes from the oceans, which influence the climate and ecosystems in all four directions.
  • tataḥ kṣaraty akṣaraṁ: This means "from that, the imperishable flows." "Tataḥ" translates to "from that," "kṣaraty" means "flows," and "akṣaraṁ" refers to "the imperishable." This suggests that from the life-giving properties of the oceans, something eternal and unchanging emerges, symbolizing the cycle of life and death.
  • tad viśvam upa jīvati: This translates to "that universe lives." "Tad" means "that," "viśvam" refers to "the universe," and "upa jīvati" means "lives." This emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms and the universe, highlighting that everything is sustained by the same source.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the profound relationship between the oceans and life on Earth. It signifies the importance of water as a life-giving force and its role in sustaining the environment. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in the way ecosystems depend on oceans for climate regulation, food sources, and biodiversity. The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibility to protect water bodies, as they are vital for the survival of all living beings. It encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of life and the importance of preserving our natural resources for future generations.
