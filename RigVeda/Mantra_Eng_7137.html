विष्वे त इन्द्र वीर्यं देवाऽनु क्रतुं ददुः |भुवो विश्वस्य गोपतिः पुरुष्टुत भद्राऽ इन्द्रस्य रातयः ||
viśve ta indra vīryaṁ devā anu kratuṁ daduḥ | bhuvo viśvasya gopatiḥ puruṣṭuta bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • Translation: "All the gods, O Indra, have given you strength and power; you are the protector of the universe, and may the blessings of Indra bring us prosperity."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśve: all, universal
    • ta: you (addressing Indra)
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • vīryaṁ: strength, power, valor
    • devā: gods
    • anu: according to, following
    • kratuṁ: will, intention, wisdom
    • daduḥ: have given
    • bhuvo: of the earth, worldly
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • gopatiḥ: protector, lord
    • puruṣṭuta: praised by men
    • bhadrā: auspicious, good
    • indrasya: of Indra
    • rātayaḥ: blessings, prosperity

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine strength and guidance in achieving success and prosperity in life.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes leadership, power, and protection. The invocation of Indra reflects a desire for strength and support in overcoming challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
      • Leadership: Leaders can invoke the qualities of Indra to inspire strength and unity among their teams.
      • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing difficulties can recite this mantra to seek divine support and courage.
      • Community Prosperity: Communities can come together to chant this mantra during festivals or gatherings to invoke blessings for collective well-being.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine strength and human effort. By acknowledging the support of higher powers, individuals and communities can cultivate resilience and strive for prosperity in their endeavors. It highlights the significance of seeking guidance and strength from the divine in both personal and collective journeys.
