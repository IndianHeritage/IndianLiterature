कुहा स्विद दोषा कुहा वस्तोर अश्विनā कुहाभिपित्वं करतः कुहोषतुः |को वां शयुत्रा विधवेव देवराम मर्यं न योषा कृतुते सदस्थ ā ||
kuha svid doṣā kuha vastor aśvinā kuhābhipitvaṁ karataḥ kuhoṣatuḥ | ko vāṁ śayutrā vidhaveva devaram maryaṁ na yoṣā kṛṇute sadhastha ā ||
  • Mantra: "kuha svid doṣā kuha vastor aśvinā kuhābhipitvaṁ karataḥ kuhoṣatuḥ | ko vāṁ śayutrā vidhaveva devaram maryaṁ na yoṣā kṛṇute sadhastha ā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kuha: Where
    • svid: Perhaps
    • doṣā: Faults or defects
    • kuha: Where
    • vastor: Of the object
    • aśvinā: By the Aśvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • kuhābhipitvaṁ: The power of the Aśvins
    • karataḥ: Of the doers
    • kuhoṣatuḥ: Where is the remedy
    • ko: Who
    • vāṁ: You
    • śayutrā: In the bed
    • vidhaveva: Like a widow
    • devaram: Divine being
    • maryaṁ: Mortal
    • na: Not
    • yoṣā: Woman
    • kṛṇute: Who makes
    • sadhastha: In the assembly
    • ā: Here
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses questions about the presence of faults and the search for remedies.
    • It invokes the Aśvins, who are known for their healing abilities, suggesting a plea for help in overcoming difficulties.
    • The mention of a "widow" symbolizes loss or absence, indicating a state of vulnerability.
    • The phrase "who makes" implies the role of individuals in creating or resolving situations, emphasizing personal responsibility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a medical context, when someone is ill, they may seek the help of doctors (akin to the Aśvins) to find remedies for their ailments.
    • In relationships, when conflicts arise, individuals must identify the faults and work together to find solutions, much like the search for remedies in the mantra.
    • In a community setting, when facing challenges, collective efforts are needed to address issues, reflecting the assembly aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing faults and seeking remedies, both individually and collectively.
    • It serves as a reminder that healing and resolution often require the intervention of others, as well as personal accountability.
    • In real life, it encourages proactive engagement in problem-solving, whether in health, relationships, or community issues.
