प्र यद भण्डिष्ठ एषाम प्रास्माकाशश च सूरयः |अप नः शोशुचद अगम् ||
pra yad bhandiṣṭha eṣām prāsmākāsaś ca sūrayaḥ | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • Mantra: "pra yad bhandiṣṭha eṣām prāsmākāsaś ca sūrayaḥ | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||"
  • Translation: "As the strong ones among us, may we be free from all sins and may the divine protect us from all evils."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix indicating 'before' or 'in front of', often used to denote a sense of priority or importance.
    • yad: Meaning 'that' or 'which', referring to a specific quality or characteristic.
    • bhandiṣṭha: Translates to 'the strong ones' or 'the powerful', indicating those who possess strength or authority.
    • eṣām: Meaning 'of these', referring to a group or collective.
    • prāsmākāsaś: This term can be interpreted as 'may we be protected' or 'may we be free from', indicating a desire for safety and security.
    • sūrayaḥ: Refers to 'the divine' or 'the gods', emphasizing the role of higher powers in providing protection.
    • apa: Meaning 'from' or 'away from', indicating a separation from negativity or harm.
    • naḥ: Meaning 'us' or 'our', indicating a collective identity.
    • śośucad: Translates to 'be free from' or 'be cleansed of', indicating a desire for purity.
    • agham: Refers to 'sin' or 'evil', highlighting the negative aspects that one wishes to be free from.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be invoked during gatherings or festivals to seek blessings for collective strength and protection from negativity.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of distress or moral dilemmas, seeking guidance and strength to overcome challenges.
    • In a corporate environment, teams can use this mantra to foster a sense of unity and purpose, aiming to achieve their goals while remaining ethical and free from wrongdoing.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and protection from evils, both on a personal and collective level. It emphasizes the importance of moral integrity and the desire to be free from sin. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, personal challenges, and professional environments, serving as a reminder to seek strength and purity in our actions and intentions. By invoking this mantra, individuals and groups can cultivate a sense of unity, purpose, and resilience against negativity.
