श्रियें मर्यासो अञ्जीमृ अकृत्न्वत सुमारुतं न पूर्वीर् अति क्षपः |दिवस पुत्रास एता न येतिर आदित्यासस ते अक्रा न वावृधुः ||
śriye maryāso añjīm̐r akṛṇvata sumārutaṁ na pūrvīr ati kṣapaḥ | divas putrāsa etā na yetira ādityāsas te akrā na vāvṛdhuḥ ||
  • śriye: This word translates to "for prosperity" or "for wealth." It signifies the pursuit of abundance and well-being in life.
  • maryāso: Refers to "the noble" or "the honorable." It emphasizes the importance of virtue and integrity in one's actions.
  • añjīm̐r: This term can be understood as "to be adorned" or "to be decorated." It suggests the idea of beautifying one's life through good deeds and positive actions.
  • akṛṇvata: Meaning "they who create" or "they who make," this word highlights the role of individuals in shaping their own destinies.
  • sumārutaṁ: Translates to "the good wind" or "the favorable breeze." It symbolizes the positive forces in life that aid in progress and success.
  • na pūrvīr: This means "not like the ancients" or "not like those before." It suggests a departure from past ways, encouraging innovation and new approaches.
  • ati kṣapaḥ: Refers to "beyond the limits" or "exceeding boundaries." It encourages individuals to push beyond their limitations and strive for greater achievements.
  • divas: This word means "day" or "heaven." It signifies the importance of time and the divine in our lives.
  • putrāsa: Translates to "sons" or "children." It emphasizes the role of future generations and the legacy we leave behind.
  • etā: Meaning "these" or "this," it refers to the present moment and the actions we take now.
  • na yetira: This means "do not stray" or "do not deviate." It encourages focus and determination in pursuing one's goals.
  • ādityāsas: Refers to "the sons of Aditi," symbolizing divine beings or celestial forces that guide and protect us.
  • te akrā: Meaning "they do not harm," it emphasizes the importance of non-violence and peace in our actions.
  • na vāvṛdhuḥ: This translates to "they do not increase," suggesting a caution against excess and the importance of moderation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity through virtuous actions while being mindful of the past and the future. It encourages individuals to create their own paths, embrace positive influences, and focus on their goals without straying. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, career growth, and community service. By embodying the principles of integrity, innovation, and moderation, individuals can contribute to a better society and leave a lasting legacy for future generations.
