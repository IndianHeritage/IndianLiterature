पुरुत्रा हि सदृङ्ग् असि विश्व विश्वा अनु प्रभुः |समत्सु त्वा हवामहे ||
purutrā hi sadṛṅṅ asi viśo viśvā anu prabhuḥ | samatsu tvā havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Purutrā: Everywhere, in all directions.
    • Hi: Indeed, certainly.
    • Sadṛṅṅ: Like, similar to.
    • Asi: You are.
    • Viśo: Of the universe, of all beings.
    • Viśvā: All, everything.
    • Anu: Following, in accordance with.
    • Prabhuḥ: Lord, master, or supreme being.
    • Samatsu: In harmony, in unity.
    • Tvā: You.
    • Havāmahe: We invoke, we call upon.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for a universal presence that is omnipresent and embodies the essence of all beings.
    • It acknowledges the divine as a guiding force that is similar to the universe itself, suggesting that the divine is not separate but rather intertwined with all existence.
    • The invocation of the divine in harmony signifies a call for unity and collective strength among all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in the way people come together to support one another, recognizing that each individual contributes to the greater whole.
    • In environmental movements, the idea of being in harmony with nature aligns with the mantra's call for unity and respect for all forms of life.
    • In personal relationships, invoking this mantra can remind individuals to see the divine in each other, fostering compassion and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence that permeates the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to live in harmony with one another and with nature, promoting a sense of community and collective responsibility.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through acts of kindness, environmental stewardship, and fostering unity in diversity.
