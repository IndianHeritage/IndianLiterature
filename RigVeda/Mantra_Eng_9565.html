ऋचां त्वः पोषम आस्ते पूपुष्वान गायतं तव गायति शक्वरीषु |ब्रह्मा त्वो वदति जातविद्याṁ यज्ञस्य मात्राṁ वि मिमीत उ त्वः ||
ṛcāṁ tvaḥ poṣam āste pupuṣvān gāyatraṁ tvo gāyati śakvarīṣu | brahmā tvo vadati jātavidyāṁ yajñasya mātrāṁ vi mimīta u tvaḥ ||
  • ṛcāṁ tvaḥ poṣam āste: This phrase can be translated as "You are the nourisher of the hymns." Here, "ṛcāṁ" refers to hymns or verses, and "poṣam" means nourishment or sustenance. In real life, this can be likened to how poetry and music nourish the soul, providing inspiration and emotional support.
  • pupuṣvān gāyatraṁ tvo gāyati śakvarīṣu: This part means "You who flourish, sing the Gāyatra." The Gāyatra is a sacred meter in Vedic literature. The idea of flourishing can be related to personal growth and success in life, where one thrives in their endeavors, much like a plant that grows and blossoms under the right conditions.
  • brahmā tvo vadati jātavidyāṁ: This translates to "Brahma speaks of your knowledge." Brahma, the creator in Hindu mythology, represents ultimate knowledge and wisdom. This can be seen in real life as the pursuit of knowledge and education, where individuals seek to understand the world around them and contribute positively to society.
  • yajñasya mātrāṁ vi mimīta u tvaḥ: This means "You are the measure of the sacrifice." In Vedic traditions, yajna (sacrifice) is a significant ritual that symbolizes selflessness and the importance of giving back to the community. In modern life, this can be reflected in acts of charity, volunteering, and contributing to the welfare of others.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment through knowledge, the flourishing of life through creativity, and the significance of selfless acts. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, express themselves creatively, and engage in altruistic behaviors. This mantra serves as a reminder that our actions and contributions can lead to personal growth and the betterment of society as a whole.
