आ चर्षणिप्रावृषभो जनानां राजा कृष्तीनाम् पुरुहूत इन्द्रः |स्तुतः श्रवस्यन्न अवसोप मद्रिग युक्त्वा हरिवृषणा याह्य अर्वाङ् ||
ā carṣaṇiprā vṛṣabho janānāṁ rājā kṛṣṭīnām puruhūta indraḥ | stutaḥ śravasyann avasopa madrig yuktvā harī vṛṣaṇā yāhy arvāṅ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā carṣaṇiprā: "O, the one who is the protector of the people."
    • vṛṣabho: "the bull" or "the strong one."
    • janānāṁ: "of the people."
    • rājā: "king" or "ruler."
    • kṛṣṭīnām: "of the cultivated fields" or "of the harvest."
    • puruhūta: "the one who is invoked often" or "the one who is praised."
    • indraḥ: "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • stutaḥ: "praised" or "worshipped."
    • śravasyann: "who is renowned" or "who has fame."
    • avasopa: "may he grant" or "may he bestow."
    • madrig: "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one."
    • yuktvā: "with wisdom" or "with understanding."
    • harī: "the one who is swift" or "the one who is quick."
    • vṛṣaṇā: "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one."
    • yāhy: "may he lead" or "may he guide."
    • arvāṅ: "to the right path" or "to the correct way."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, as a protector and guide for the people.
    • It emphasizes the importance of leadership and strength in guiding communities towards prosperity.
    • The reference to "cultivated fields" signifies the connection between divine blessings and agricultural success.
    • Indra is praised for his wisdom and strength, suggesting that effective leadership requires both qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders who are wise and strong can effectively guide their communities through challenges, much like Indra.
    • Farmers often pray for divine blessings for a good harvest, reflecting the connection between spirituality and agriculture.
    • Community leaders who are praised and respected can inspire others to work together for common goals, similar to the invocation of Indra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance in leadership and community welfare.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength and wisdom are essential qualities for those in positions of power.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and leaders to seek divine support in their endeavors, fostering a sense of responsibility towards their communities.
