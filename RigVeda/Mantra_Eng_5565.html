याः प्रवतो निवत उद्वत उदन्वतीर अनुदकाश च याः |ताः अस्मभ्यम् पयसा पिन्वमानाः शिवा देवीर अशिपदाः भवन्तु सर्वा नद्यो अशिमिदा भवन्तु ||
yāḥ pravato nivata udvata udanvatīr anudakāś ca yāḥ | tā asmabhyam payasā pinvamānāḥ śivā devīr aśipadā bhavantu sarvā nadyo aśimidā bhavantu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāḥ: Those who
    • pravato: Flowing down
    • nivata: Flowing in a calm manner
    • udvata: Flowing upwards
    • udanvatīr: Those that flow towards the sky
    • anudakāś: Without water
    • ca: And
    • tā: They
    • asmabhyam: To us
    • payasā: With nourishment
    • pinvamānāḥ: Abundantly flowing
    • śivā: Auspicious
    • devīr: Goddesses
    • aśipadā: With good paths
    • bhavantu: May they become
    • sarvā: All
    • nadyo: Rivers
    • aśimidā: May they be free from obstacles
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the rivers, personified as goddesses, to flow abundantly and auspiciously.
    • The rivers are seen as sources of nourishment and life, essential for agriculture and sustenance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of water in our lives, symbolizing purity, prosperity, and fertility.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, urging us to respect and cherish our natural resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, rivers provide the necessary water for crops, highlighting the significance of this mantra in ensuring a good harvest.
    • Communities often gather near rivers for rituals and celebrations, recognizing their importance in cultural and spiritual practices.
    • Environmental movements advocate for the protection of rivers, aligning with the mantra's call for rivers to flow freely and without obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that water plays in our lives and the need to honor and protect our rivers.
    • It encourages us to seek harmony with nature, ensuring that we live sustainably and responsibly.
    • In modern contexts, it can inspire initiatives for water conservation and environmental protection, emphasizing the sacredness of natural resources.
