वेषीद वस्य दूत्यं यस्य जुजोषो अध्वरम् |हव्यं मर्तस्य वोळभवे ||
veṣīd v asya dūtya1ṁ yasya jujoṣo adhvaram | havyam martasya voḻhave ||
  • Mantra: "veṣīd v asya dūtyaṁ yasya jujoṣo adhvaram | havyam martasya voḻhave ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • veṣīd: to be adorned or decorated
    • v: and
    • asya: of this
    • dūtyaṁ: messenger or envoy
    • yasya: whose
    • jujoṣo: who is engaged or involved
    • adhvaram: the sacrifice or ritual
    • havyam: offerings or oblations
    • martasya: of the mortal
    • voḻhave: to be accepted or received
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the adornment and the role of a messenger in the context of a ritual or sacrifice.
    • The phrase "whose messenger is engaged in the sacrifice" suggests the importance of communication and connection in spiritual practices.
    • The term "havyam martasya voḻhave" indicates that the offerings made by mortals are to be accepted, highlighting the significance of human efforts in spiritual endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the role of a messenger can be likened to a project manager who communicates between teams to ensure that the project (sacrifice) is successful.
    • In personal relationships, the importance of communication is crucial; just as the mantra emphasizes the role of the messenger, effective communication can strengthen bonds and resolve conflicts.
    • During festivals or religious ceremonies, the offerings made by individuals symbolize their devotion and the hope that their efforts will be acknowledged and accepted by the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of communication and the role of individuals in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that our efforts, whether in rituals or daily life, are valuable and worthy of recognition.
    • In real life, it encourages us to be effective communicators and to appreciate the contributions of others in our personal and professional lives.
