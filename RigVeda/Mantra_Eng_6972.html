यो अश्वेभिर वहते वस्त उष्रास त्रिः सप्त सप्ततीनाम् | एभिः सोमैः सोमसुद्भिः सोमपाद दानाय शुक्रपूतपाः ||
yo aśvebhir vahate vasta usrās triḥ sapta saptatīnām | ebhiḥ somebhiḥ somasudbhiḥ somapā dānāya śukrapūtapāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yo aśvebhir vahate vasta usrās triḥ sapta saptatīnām | ebhiḥ somebhiḥ somasudbhiḥ somapā dānāya śukrapūtapāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the one who carries the wealth (vasta) with the help of horses (aśvebhir), who is associated with the seven (saptatīnām) and the three (triḥ) aspects of prosperity, and who is offered the pure and sacred soma (somebhiḥ somasudbhiḥ) for the purpose of giving (dānāya).
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • aśvebhir: with horses
    • vahate: carries or brings
    • vasta: wealth or abundance
    • usrās: associated with the three
    • triḥ: three
    • saptatīnām: of the seven
    • ebhiḥ: with these
    • somebhiḥ: with soma
    • somasudbhiḥ: pure soma
    • somapā: those who partake of soma
    • dānāya: for giving
    • śukrapūtapāḥ: those who are pure and sacred
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the concept of teamwork in a corporate environment, where individuals (akin to horses) work together to carry the wealth of the organization.
    • In a community setting, the idea of sharing resources (soma) for the benefit of all reflects the importance of charity and giving back to society.
    • The emphasis on purity and sacredness can be seen in practices such as environmental conservation, where individuals strive to maintain the purity of nature for future generations.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collaboration, sharing, and purity in our endeavors. It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also involves the collective effort of individuals working together for a common good. In real life, this mantra can inspire us to engage in teamwork, community service, and environmental stewardship, reminding us that our actions should be guided by a sense of responsibility and purity.
