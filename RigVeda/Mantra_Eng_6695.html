वयं घा ते अपि ष्मसि स्तोतार इन्द्र गिरवणः |त्वं नो जिन्व सोमपाः ||
vayaṁ ghā te api ṣmasi stotāra indra girvaṇaḥ | tvaṁ no jinva somapāḥ ||
  • Mantra: vayaṁ ghā te api ṣmasi stotāra indra girvaṇaḥ | tvaṁ no jinva somapāḥ ||
  • Translation: "We are your singers, O Indra, who are here to praise you; may you grant us strength, O Soma-drinker."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • ghā: Singers or those who praise
    • te: Your
    • api: Also
    • ṣmasi: Here or present
    • stotāra: Praisers or worshippers
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the songs or hymns
    • tvaṁ: You
    • no: Us
    • jinva: Grant or bestow
    • somapāḥ: O Soma-drinker, referring to Indra's association with the sacred drink Soma

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, acknowledging his power and seeking his blessings. Indra is often invoked in Vedic rituals for strength, prosperity, and protection.
    • The phrase "We are your singers" emphasizes the role of the community in worship, highlighting the importance of collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "Soma-drinker" signifies the connection between divine blessings and the sacred drink, which is believed to bestow immortality and divine insight.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in modern life as a reminder of the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals. Just as the singers come together to praise Indra, individuals can collaborate to support one another in various endeavors.
    • In times of difficulty, invoking strength and support from a higher power or the community can provide comfort and motivation, similar to how the mantra seeks blessings from Indra.
    • The concept of seeking divine blessings through rituals can be seen in various cultures today, where people come together to pray for strength, health, and prosperity.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, community, and the pursuit of strength through divine connection. It serves as a reminder that in both spiritual and worldly matters, seeking support and working together can lead to greater achievements.
    • By understanding and applying the principles behind this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of unity and purpose in their lives, drawing strength from both their community and their beliefs.
