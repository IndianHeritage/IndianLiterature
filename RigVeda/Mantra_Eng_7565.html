श्रुधी हवं तिरश्च्या इन्द्र यस् त्वा सपार्यति |सुवीर्यस्य गोमतो रायस पूर्धि महाम् असी ||
śrudhī havaṁ tiraścyā indra yas tvā saparyati | suvīryasya gomato rāyas pūrdhi mahām̐ asi ||
  • śrudhī: This word can be translated as "to hear" or "to listen." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to divine guidance and wisdom.
  • havaṁ: This term means "offering" or "sacrifice." It signifies the act of giving or dedicating something valuable to the divine, which is a common practice in many cultures, such as making offerings in temples or during rituals.
  • tiraścyā: This word refers to "the one who is beyond" or "the transcendent." It suggests a connection to higher realms or divine beings, indicating that the mantra is invoking a higher power.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. He represents power and protection, akin to how leaders in society are expected to safeguard their people.
  • yas: This means "who" or "that." It serves as a connector in the mantra, linking the divine to the offerings made.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," referring directly to Indra, indicating a personal appeal or invocation.
  • saparyati: This term means "worships" or "serves." It highlights the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine, similar to how individuals may engage in prayer or rituals in various religions.
  • suvīryasya: This word means "of the strong" or "of the valiant." It emphasizes strength and valor, qualities that are admired in both historical and contemporary contexts, such as in leaders or heroes.
  • gomato: This translates to "of the cow" or "wealthy." In Vedic culture, cows were considered symbols of wealth and prosperity, much like how certain animals or resources are valued in different cultures today.
  • rāyas: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and success, which are universal aspirations in life.
  • pūrdhi: This term refers to "fullness" or "completeness." It suggests a state of abundance and fulfillment, akin to achieving one's goals or desires in life.
  • mahām̐: This means "great" or "mighty." It emphasizes the significance of the qualities being invoked, suggesting that they are of utmost importance.
  • asi: This translates to "you are" or "you exist." It affirms the presence and power of the divine being invoked.

Conclusion: The mantra "śrudhī havaṁ tiraścyā indra yas tvā saparyati | suvīryasya gomato rāyas pūrdhi mahām̐ asi ||" is a powerful invocation that emphasizes the importance of listening to divine wisdom, making offerings, and recognizing the strength and prosperity that come from a connection with the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal prayers, community rituals, or even in moments of reflection when seeking guidance and strength. It serves as a reminder of the values of devotion, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity, encouraging individuals to cultivate these qualities in their lives. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to connect with higher powers, seek abundance, and embody strength in their daily lives.
