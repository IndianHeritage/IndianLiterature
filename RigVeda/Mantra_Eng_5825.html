किमाग आस वरुण ज्येष्ठं यत्स्तोतारं जिघांससि सखायम् |प्र तन् मे वचो दूलभ स्वधावोऽव त्वानेन नमसातुर इयाम् ||
kim āga āsa varuṇa jyeṣṭhaṁ yat stotāraṁ jighāṁsasi sakhāyam | pra tan me voco dūḻabha svadhāvo 'va tvānenā namasā tura iyām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • āga: has come
    • āsa: to be
    • varuṇa: Varuna (the god of water and celestial ocean)
    • jyeṣṭhaṁ: the elder or the greatest
    • yat: that which
    • stotāraṁ: the one who praises
    • jighāṁsasi: you wish to harm
    • sakhāyam: friend
    • pra: for
    • tan: that
    • me: my
    • voco: speech or words
    • dūḻabha: difficult or rare
    • svadhāvo: self-formed or self-existent
    • 'va: or
    • tvā: you
    • anenā: with this
    • namasā: with reverence
    • tura: swift
    • iyām: to this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and water.
    • The speaker questions what has come to be with Varuna, who is considered the elder among the gods.
    • It expresses concern about a friend who may be in danger or under threat.
    • The speaker seeks to convey their thoughts and words to Varuna, asking for guidance or protection.
    • The term "dūḻabha" suggests that the situation is rare or difficult, indicating a sense of urgency.
    • The invocation of reverence ("namasā") highlights the importance of humility and respect in seeking divine intervention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking guidance from a higher power, similar to how the speaker seeks Varuna's help.
    • When facing challenges in relationships, one might reflect on their actions and seek forgiveness or understanding, akin to addressing a friend in distress.
    • In environmental issues, invoking the spirit of water and nature can remind us of our responsibility towards preserving the earth, resonating with Varuna's domain.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance in times of trouble, particularly when relationships are at stake.
    • It teaches the importance of humility and respect when approaching higher powers, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to reflect on our actions and their impact on others, fostering a sense of community and responsibility.
