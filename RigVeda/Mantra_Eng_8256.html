त्वं सोम सूर एषस तोकस्य साता तनूनाम् |वृणीमहे सख्याय वृणीमहे युज्याय ||
tvaṁ soma sūra eṣas tokasya sātā tanūnām | vṛṇīmahe sakhyāya vṛṇīmahe yujyāya ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "You," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • soma - Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • sūra - This means "hero" or "brave one," often used to describe deities or powerful beings.
  • eṣas - This translates to "this," referring to the present moment or the immediate context.
  • tokasya - This means "of the offspring" or "of the progeny," highlighting the importance of lineage and continuity.
  • sātā - This word means "of the body" or "of the physical form," emphasizing the material aspect of existence.
  • tanūnām - This translates to "of the bodies," referring to the various forms of life and existence.
  • vṛṇīmahe - This means "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating a collective desire or intention.
  • sakhyāya - This translates to "for friendship" or "for companionship," emphasizing the importance of relationships.
  • yujyāya - This means "for union" or "for connection," highlighting the desire for harmony and togetherness.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine entity, specifically Soma, to bless the community with strength, unity, and the continuity of life. It emphasizes the importance of relationships and the collective well-being of individuals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra calls for unity and friendship, it can inspire individuals to foster strong relationships within their communities, promoting collaboration and support.
  • Family Values: The reference to offspring and lineage highlights the significance of family bonds. It encourages individuals to nurture their relationships with family members, ensuring a supportive environment for future generations.
  • Teamwork: In professional settings, the mantra's emphasis on companionship and union can be a guiding principle for teamwork, encouraging collaboration and mutual respect among colleagues.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing relationships. It encourages individuals to seek strength and unity, both in personal and communal contexts. By invoking these values, one can create a harmonious environment that fosters growth, support, and collective well-being.
