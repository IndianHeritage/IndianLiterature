त्वोतासो मगवन्न इन्द्र विप्रā वयं ते स्याम सूरयो गृणन्तः |भेजानासो बृहद्दिवस्य राया आकाश्यस्य दावने पुरुक्षोः ||
tvotāso maghavann indra viprā vayaṁ te syāma sūrayo gṛṇantaḥ | bhejānāso bṛhaddivasya rāya ākāyyasya dāvane purukṣoḥ ||
  • tvotāso: This word can be interpreted as "of your praise" or "of your worship." It signifies the importance of acknowledging and honoring the divine.
  • maghavann: This term refers to "the mighty one" or "the powerful." In the context of the mantra, it is often associated with Indra, the king of the gods, who represents strength and authority.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, symbolizing power, rain, and thunder. He is often invoked for protection and strength.
  • viprā: This word means "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the role of knowledge and wisdom in understanding the divine and the world around us.
  • vayaṁ te syāma: This phrase translates to "may we be with you." It expresses a desire for unity and connection with the divine.
  • sūrayo: This term means "the brave" or "the heroic." It highlights the qualities of courage and valor that are essential in facing life's challenges.
  • gṛṇantaḥ: This word means "singing" or "chanting." It signifies the act of devotion and the importance of expressing reverence through vocalization.
  • bhejānāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are devoted" or "those who worship." It emphasizes the community of believers who come together in faith.
  • bṛhaddivasya: This phrase means "of the great sky" or "of the vast heavens." It signifies the expansive nature of the divine and the universe.
  • rāya: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It reflects the desire for abundance and success in life.
  • ākāyyasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the body" or "of the physical form." It emphasizes the connection between the spiritual and the material world.
  • dāvane: This word means "in the fire" or "in the heat." It symbolizes transformation and purification through challenges.
  • purukṣoḥ: This term refers to "the many" or "the multitude." It signifies the collective strength and unity of the community.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, strength, and community. It emphasizes the importance of praising the divine, seeking wisdom, and fostering unity among individuals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, rituals, and personal reflections. It serves as a reminder of the power of collective faith and the significance of courage and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for personal growth and contribute positively to their communities.
