मन्त्र: विश्वा रूपाण्य आविशन् पुनानो याति हर्यतः | यत्रामृतासा आसते ||
viśvā rūpāṇy āviśan punāno yāti haryataḥ | yatrāmṛtāsa āsate ||
  • Mantra: "viśvā rūpāṇy āviśan punāno yāti haryataḥ | yatrāmṛtāsa āsate ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • rūpāṇy: forms, shapes
    • āviśan: have entered, pervaded
    • punāno: renewing, revitalizing
    • yāti: goes, moves
    • haryataḥ: from the horse, symbolizing speed and power
    • yatra: where
    • amṛtāsa: the immortal ones, the eternal beings
    • āsate: dwell, reside
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the universal forms that have entered and pervaded the world.
    • It suggests a process of renewal and revitalization, indicating that life is constantly moving and evolving.
    • The reference to "haryataḥ" (from the horse) symbolizes the swiftness and power of this movement.
    • Finally, it points to a place where the immortal beings reside, suggesting a connection to the eternal and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see the cycle of life and death, where forms are constantly changing and renewing, much like the seasons.
    • In personal growth, individuals often go through phases of transformation, shedding old habits and adopting new ones, akin to the renewal mentioned in the mantra.
    • The concept of "haryataḥ" can be likened to the rapid advancements in technology, where innovation drives society forward at an unprecedented pace.
    • In spiritual practices, many seek to connect with the eternal or the divine, aiming to dwell in a state of higher consciousness, similar to the "amṛtāsa" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all forms and the continuous cycle of renewal in life.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us of the transient nature of existence and the importance of embracing change.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek growth, adapt to new circumstances, and connect with the eternal aspects of our being.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a philosophical guide, urging us to recognize the beauty in transformation and the presence of the divine in our lives.
