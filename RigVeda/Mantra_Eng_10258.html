पुत्रम् इव पितराव अश्विनोभेन्द्रावथुः काव्यैर दंशनाभिः |यत सुरामं व्यपिभः शचीभिः सरस्वती त्वा मगवन्न अभिष्णक ||
putram iva pitarāv aśvinobhendrāvathuḥ kāvyair daṁsanābhiḥ | yat surāmaṁ vy apibaḥ śacībhiḥ sarasvatī tvā maghavann abhiṣṇak ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • putram - son
    • iva - like
    • pitarāv - parents
    • aśvinobhendrāvathuḥ - O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen
    • kāvyair - with poetic verses
    • daṁsanābhiḥ - with sharp teeth (or incisive words)
    • yat - that which
    • surāmaṁ - the divine nectar
    • vy apibaḥ - you have drunk
    • śacībhiḥ - with the goddesses
    • sarasvatī - O Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge
    • tvā - you
    • maghavann - O Maghavan (Indra)
    • abhiṣṇak - may you be invoked
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine Ashvins, who are often associated with health, wealth, and the well-being of children.
    • The phrase "putram iva pitarāv" suggests a relationship akin to that of a son and his parents, emphasizing care and protection.
    • The mention of "kāvyair daṁsanābhiḥ" indicates the power of words and poetry, which can be both nurturing and incisive, much like a parent’s guidance.
    • The reference to "surāmaṁ vy apibaḥ" highlights the importance of divine knowledge and wisdom, akin to how we seek knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
    • Invoking "sarasvatī" signifies the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, essential for personal and societal growth.
    • Finally, "maghavann abhiṣṇak" calls upon Indra, the king of gods, to bless and protect, symbolizing the need for divine intervention in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, the mantra reflects the nurturing role of parents, who guide their children with wisdom and love, much like the Ashvins.
    • In education, invoking Sarasvati can be seen in the way students seek knowledge and strive for excellence, drawing inspiration from the goddess of learning.
    • In creative fields, artists and writers often channel the essence of "kāvyair daṁsanābhiḥ" to express profound truths and emotions through their work.
    • In times of difficulty, individuals may seek blessings from higher powers, akin to invoking Indra for strength and protection in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of familial bonds, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of divine blessings in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us of the interconnectedness of wisdom, creativity, and protection, which are vital for personal and communal well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek knowledge, nurture relationships, and invoke divine support in our journeys.
