ये ते वृषणो वृषभास इन्द्र ब्रह्मयुजो वृषरथासो अत्याः |तां आ तिष्ठ तेभिर आ याह्य अर्वाङ् हवामहे त्वा सुत इन्द्र सोमे ||
ye te vṛṣaṇo vṛṣabhāsa indra brahmayujo vṛṣarathāso atyāḥ | tām̐ ā tiṣṭha tebhir ā yāhy arvāṅ havāmahe tvā suta indra some ||
  • ye te vṛṣaṇo vṛṣabhāsa indra brahmayujo vṛṣarathāso atyāḥ: This line addresses Indra, the king of the gods, who is often associated with strength and power. The term "vṛṣaṇo" refers to the powerful, while "vṛṣabhāsa" signifies the one who shines like a bull, symbolizing strength and virility. "Brahmayujo" indicates the connection to the divine or spiritual wisdom, and "vṛṣarathāso" refers to those who ride the chariot of strength. The word "atyāḥ" suggests the supreme or the ultimate, emphasizing Indra's unparalleled might.
  • tām̐ ā tiṣṭha tebhir ā yāhy arvāṅ: This line is a call to Indra to come forth and be present. "Tām̐" means 'that' or 'him', referring to Indra. "Ā tiṣṭha" translates to 'stand' or 'be present', indicating a plea for Indra's attention and support. "Tebhir" means 'with these', suggesting the presence of offerings or prayers. "Ā yāhy" means 'let us go', and "arvāṅ" refers to the offerings or the essence of the prayers being made.
  • havāmahe tvā suta indra some: This line expresses the act of invocation. "Havāmahe" means 'we invoke' or 'we call upon', indicating a collective prayer or offering. "Tvā" means 'you', directly addressing Indra. "Suta" translates to 'son', often referring to the divine or the essence of the offerings. "Indra some" refers to the soma, a sacred drink associated with immortality and divine inspiration, which is often offered to the gods during rituals.

  • Significance: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings and presence in rituals. It emphasizes the importance of strength, divine wisdom, and the collective effort of the community in seeking divine favor.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking strength and guidance in challenging situations. For example, during times of personal or communal crisis, individuals may come together to invoke support from a higher power, whether through prayer, meditation, or community gatherings. The essence of this mantra encourages unity, strength, and the pursuit of wisdom in overcoming obstacles.
