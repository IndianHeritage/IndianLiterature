निवो यामाय मानुषो दध्र उग्राय मन्यवे |जिहीत पर्वतो गिरिः ||
ni vo yāmāya mānuṣo dadhra ugrāya manyave | jihīta parvato giriḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • vo: your
    • yāmāya: to the one who is going to be sacrificed
    • mānuṣo: human
    • dadhra: hold or support
    • ugrāya: fierce or powerful
    • manyave: with thought or intention
    • jihīta: may he be favorable
    • parvato: mountain
    • giriḥ: hill or peak
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the need for support and favor from higher powers.
    • The phrase "ni vo yāmāya" suggests a plea for protection and guidance, particularly in challenging situations.
    • The term "ugrāya" indicates the fierce nature of the challenges one may face, while "manyave" implies that these challenges require thoughtful consideration and intention.
    • The reference to "parvato giriḥ" symbolizes stability and strength, akin to mountains that stand firm against the elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or support from their community, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In leadership, a manager may face fierce competition (ugrāya) and must thoughtfully consider their strategies (manyave) to navigate the challenges while seeking support from their team (dadhra).
    • During natural disasters, communities come together to support each other, invoking the strength of their collective spirit, much like the steadfastness of mountains (parvato giriḥ).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of seeking divine support and guidance in the face of life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may encounter fierce obstacles, thoughtful action and community support can help us navigate through them.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, from personal struggles to collective challenges, emphasizing resilience and the need for a supportive network.
