युवं शक्रा मायाविना समीची निर अमन्थतम् |विमदेन यद ईळिता नासत्याः निरमन्थतम् ||
yuvaṁ śakrā māyāvinā samīcī nir amanthatam | vimadena yad īḻitā nāsatyā niramanthatam ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and change. In real life, this can be seen in the enthusiasm and innovative ideas that young people bring to various fields, such as technology and social movements.
  • śakrā: Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic literature, this term embodies strength, power, and leadership. In contemporary terms, it can represent influential leaders or figures who inspire and guide others, such as activists or entrepreneurs who drive change.
  • māyāvinā: This word means "one who is skilled in illusion" or "deceptive." It highlights the idea that appearances can be misleading. In everyday life, this can relate to situations where things are not as they seem, such as in marketing or social media, where curated images may not reflect reality.
  • samīcī: This term means "properly" or "correctly." It emphasizes the importance of doing things in the right way. In real life, this can be applied to ethical practices in business or personal conduct, where integrity and honesty are paramount.
  • nir amanthatam: This phrase translates to "without disturbance" or "unwavering." It suggests a state of calmness and stability. In practical terms, this can be seen in mindfulness practices or meditation, where individuals seek to maintain inner peace amidst external chaos.
  • vimadena: This word means "with joy" or "with delight." It signifies the importance of positivity and happiness in our actions. In real life, this can be reflected in how a positive attitude can influence workplace culture or personal relationships, fostering collaboration and support.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the previous ideas. It serves as a bridge in the mantra, linking the concepts together.
  • īḻitā: This term means "to be worshipped" or "to be revered." It highlights the importance of respect and admiration for those who embody the qualities mentioned earlier. In real life, this can be seen in how communities honor leaders, mentors, or even cultural icons who inspire them.
  • nāsatyā: This word refers to "truth" or "reality." It emphasizes the value of honesty and authenticity. In contemporary society, this can relate to the importance of transparency in governance, media, and personal relationships.
  • niramanthatam: Reiterating the idea of being "without disturbance," this phrase reinforces the notion of maintaining a steady and calm demeanor in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of youthful energy, strength, and the importance of integrity and authenticity in our actions. It serves as a reminder to approach life with joy and respect for truth, while also recognizing the potential for illusion in our perceptions. In real life, these teachings can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal development, and community engagement, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and truthful existence amidst the complexities of modern life.
