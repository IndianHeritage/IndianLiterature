इन्द्रो वसुभिः परि पातु नो गयम् आदित्यैः नो अदितिः शर्म यच्छतु |रुद्रो रुद्रैभिर देवो मृळयाति नस् त्वष्टा नो ज्ञाभिः सुविताय जिवातु ||
indro vasubhiḥ pari pātu no gayam ādityair no aditiḥ śarma yacchatu | rudro rudrebhir devo mṛḻayāti nas tvaṣṭā no gnābhiḥ suvitāya jinvatu ||
  • Indro vasubhiḥ pari pātu no: This phrase invokes Indra, the king of the gods, asking him to protect us with the Vasus, who are deities associated with the elements and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to seeking protection and support from influential figures or forces in our lives, such as mentors or community leaders.
  • Gayam ādityair no aditiḥ: Here, the mantra calls upon the Adityas, the solar deities, to provide us with peace and prosperity. The term "Aditi" refers to the infinite or boundless, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of the universe. This can be related to the importance of seeking guidance from nature and the universe in our daily lives, such as through meditation or mindfulness practices.
  • Śarma yacchatu: This means "may he grant us protection." In a practical sense, this can be understood as a prayer for safety and security in our endeavors, whether it be in personal relationships, professional pursuits, or health. For example, individuals often seek blessings before starting a new venture or journey.
  • Rudro rudrebhir devo mṛḻayāti: This line refers to Rudra, a fierce aspect of Shiva, asking for his blessings to calm our fears and anxieties. In real life, this can be seen as a call to confront and overcome challenges, much like how people seek therapy or counseling to address their mental health issues.
  • Nas tvaṣṭā no gnābhiḥ: Tvaṣṭā is the divine craftsman, and this line seeks his creative blessings. This can be related to the importance of creativity and innovation in our lives, such as in art, technology, or problem-solving. For instance, entrepreneurs often rely on creative thinking to develop new products or services.
  • Su-vitāya jinvatu: This phrase means "may we be blessed with good fortune." In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a desire for success and well-being in all aspects of life, including health, relationships, and career. People often express this sentiment during celebrations or significant life events, such as weddings or graduations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for protection, guidance, creativity, and prosperity. Its significance lies in the recognition of divine forces that influence our lives and the importance of seeking their blessings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or when embarking on new journeys, reminding us to remain connected to the spiritual and natural world while pursuing our goals.
