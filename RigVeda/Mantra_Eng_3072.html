विष्वेषाम् अदितिः यज्ञियानां विष्वेषाम् अतिथिः मानवाणाम् | अग्निः देवानाम् अव आवृणानः सुमृळीको भवतु जातवेदाः ||
viśveṣām aditir yajñiyānāṁ viśveṣām atithir mānuṣāṇām | agnir devānām ava āvṛṇānaḥ sumṛḻīko bhavatu jātavedāḥ ||
  • viśveṣām: This word translates to "of all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the idea that the mantra addresses all beings and entities.
  • aditir: Aditi is often referred to as the mother of the gods in Vedic literature. Here, it implies a nurturing and protective force.
  • yajñiyānāṁ: This term relates to "sacrificial offerings" or "those who perform sacrifices." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.
  • atithir: This means "guest" or "host." It highlights the significance of hospitality and the respect given to guests in society.
  • mānuṣāṇām: This translates to "of humans." It indicates the focus on human beings and their interactions.
  • agnir: Referring to "fire," Agni is a central figure in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine messenger.
  • devānām: This means "of the gods," indicating a connection to the divine and the spiritual realm.
  • ava āvṛṇānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may he protect" or "may he cover." It suggests a plea for protection and guidance.
  • sumṛḻīko: This term means "well-remembered" or "auspicious." It implies the importance of good deeds and positive actions.
  • bhavatu: This translates to "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or intention.
  • jātavedāḥ: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It signifies wisdom and the understanding of the cycles of life.

In essence, this mantra invokes the universal forces of protection, hospitality, and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and the role of fire as a purifying element in these practices. The mantra calls upon Aditi, the mother of the gods, to bless both the offerings made in rituals and the interactions among humans, particularly in the context of hospitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Hospitality: When welcoming guests into our homes, we can embody the spirit of atithir mānuṣāṇām by treating them with respect and kindness, ensuring they feel valued and comfortable.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In cultural or religious ceremonies, the significance of yajñiyānāṁ reminds us of the importance of gratitude and the act of giving back to the universe.
  • Seeking Protection: In times of uncertainty or distress, invoking the protective qualities of agnir devānām can serve as a reminder to seek guidance and support from higher powers.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings, the importance of hospitality, and the need for divine guidance in our lives. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and respectful society.
