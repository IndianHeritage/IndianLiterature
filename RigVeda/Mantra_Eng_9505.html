दैव्याः होतारः प्रथमā पुरोहित ऋतस्य पन्थाम् अन्वेमि साधुयā |क्षेत्रस्य पतिम् प्रतिवेशम् ईमहे विश्वान् देवाम् अमृताम् अप्रयुच्छतः ||
daivyā hotārā prathamā purohita ṛtasya panthām anv emi sādhuyā | kṣetrasya patim prativeśam īmahe viśvān devām̐ amṛtām̐ aprayucchataḥ ||
  • daivyā hotārā: This phrase refers to the divine priests or the celestial beings who perform rituals. In real life, this can be likened to spiritual leaders or guides who help individuals connect with the divine or higher consciousness.
  • prathamā purohita: This means the first or foremost priest. It signifies the importance of having a knowledgeable and wise guide in any spiritual or life journey. For example, a mentor in a professional setting can be seen as a purohita who leads the way.
  • ṛtasya panthām: This translates to the path of truth or righteousness. In everyday life, this can be understood as the moral and ethical choices we make. For instance, choosing honesty in business dealings reflects following the path of ṛta.
  • anv emi sādhuyā: This means "I follow the path of the good." It emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with virtuous actions and thoughts. In practice, this could be seen in community service or acts of kindness towards others.
  • kṣetrasya patim: This refers to the lord or master of the field or domain. In a modern context, this could represent leaders or influential figures in various fields, such as a CEO in a company or a community leader.
  • prativeśam īmahe: This means "we invoke the presence." It signifies the act of calling upon divine energy or support in our endeavors. For example, people often pray or meditate before starting a significant task or project.
  • viśvān devām̐: This translates to "all the gods." It reflects the idea of universal divinity and the interconnectedness of all spiritual beings. In real life, this can be seen in the practice of inclusivity and respect for all beliefs and traditions.
  • amṛtām̐ aprayucchataḥ: This means "who do not abandon immortality." It signifies the pursuit of eternal truths and values that transcend the material world. In practical terms, this can relate to the quest for knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual growth that lasts beyond physical existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, the pursuit of truth, and the invocation of universal energies in our lives. It teaches us to seek wisdom from knowledgeable leaders, to follow a righteous path, and to connect with the divine in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, community leadership, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the significance of virtue, guidance, and interconnectedness in our journey through life.
