आरे ते गोग्नम् उता पूरुषघ्नं क्षयद्वीर सुम्नम अस्मे ते अस्तु |मृळा च नो अधि च ब्रूहि देवाधा च नः शर्मा यच्छ द्विभराः ||
āre te goghnam uta pūruṣaghnaṁ kṣayadvīra sumnam asme te astu | mṛḻā ca no adhi ca brūhi devādhā ca naḥ śarma yaccha dvibarhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "āre te goghnam uta pūruṣaghnaṁ kṣayadvīra sumnam asme te astu | mṛḻā ca no adhi ca brūhi devādhā ca naḥ śarma yaccha dvibarhāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āre: O, calling upon
    • te: you
    • goghnam: the one who destroys cattle (symbolizing wealth)
    • uta: or
    • pūruṣaghnaṁ: the one who destroys men (symbolizing strength)
    • kṣayadvīra: the one who causes the loss of heroes
    • sumnam: auspiciousness
    • asme: to us
    • te: your
    • astu: may it be
    • mṛḻā: gentle
    • ca: and
    • no: to us
    • adhi: above
    • ca: and
    • brūhi: speak
    • devādhā: O divine beings
    • ca: and
    • naḥ: our
    • śarma: protection or happiness
    • yaccha: grant
    • dvibarhāḥ: two-fold (often referring to dual aspects of life)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking divine beings for protection and auspiciousness.
    • The first part addresses the forces that can bring destruction, both to wealth (cattle) and to human life (men), indicating a recognition of the challenges and adversities in life.
    • It seeks to transform these destructive forces into auspiciousness for the supplicant, emphasizing the importance of resilience and positivity in the face of adversity.
    • The second part of the mantra calls upon divine beings to provide gentle guidance and protection, highlighting the need for support from higher powers in navigating life's challenges.
    • The mention of "two-fold" aspects suggests a balance between different elements of life, such as material and spiritual, or strength and gentleness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when facing competition or market challenges. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and maintain a positive outlook while navigating difficulties.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of stress or uncertainty, invoking a sense of protection and support from the universe.
    • In community settings, it can be used during gatherings or rituals to foster a sense of unity and collective strength against adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing challenges while seeking divine support for protection and auspiciousness.
    • Its significance lies in the balance it promotes between strength and gentleness, encouraging individuals to face adversities with resilience and positivity.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a powerful reminder to seek guidance and maintain hope, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to communal challenges.
