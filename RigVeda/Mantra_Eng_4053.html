देवं वो अद्य सवितारं एषे भागं च रत्नं विभजन्तम आयोः |आ वां नराः पुरुभुजां ववृत्यां दिवे-दिवे चिद अश्विनां सखीयन ||
devaṁ vo adya savitāram eṣe bhagaṁ ca ratnaṁ vibhajantam āyoḥ | ā vāṁ narā purubhujā vavṛtyāṁ dive-dive cid aśvinā sakhīyan ||
  • Devaṁ vo adya savitāram: This phrase translates to "O Deity, today we invoke the Sun God." Here, "Devaṁ" refers to the divine or godly aspect, while "Savitāram" specifically denotes the Sun, a symbol of light, energy, and life.
  • Eṣe bhagaṁ ca ratnaṁ vibhajantam āyoḥ: This means "who distributes wealth and prosperity." "Bhaga" refers to wealth or fortune, and "ratnaṁ" means jewels or precious things. This part of the mantra emphasizes the Sun's role in bestowing material and spiritual wealth upon humanity.
  • Ā vāṁ narā purubhujā vavṛtyāṁ: This translates to "May we, the people, be nourished." "Narā" means people or humans, and "purubhujā" refers to those who partake in abundance. This line expresses a desire for nourishment and sustenance from the divine.
  • Dive-dive cid aśvinā sakhīyan: This means "day by day, may we be friends with the Aśvins." The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being. "Sakhīyan" implies friendship or companionship, indicating a wish for a continuous relationship with these divine beings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound invocation for divine blessings, prosperity, and health. It highlights the importance of the Sun as a source of life and abundance, while also emphasizing the need for community and friendship with divine entities like the Aśvins. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as:


  • Daily Rituals: Individuals may recite this mantra during morning prayers to seek blessings for health and prosperity.
  • Community Gatherings: It can be used in communal ceremonies to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants.
  • Health and Wellness Practices: The invocation of the Aśvins can be particularly relevant in health-related contexts, such as during medical treatments or wellness programs.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine blessings, and the pursuit of well-being in both individual and communal aspects of existence.
