मनुṣ्वद इन्द्र सवनाṁ जुषाणः पिबा सोमं शाश्वते वीर्याय |स आ ववृत्स्व हर्यश्व यज्ञैः सरण्युभिर अपो अर्णा सिसर्षि ||
manuṣvad indra savanaṁ juṣāṇaḥ pibā somaṁ śaśvate vīryāya | sa ā vavṛtsva haryaśva yajñaiḥ saraṇyubhir apo arṇā sisarṣi ||
  • manuṣvad: This term can be interpreted as "human" or "mankind." It emphasizes the connection between divine forces and human beings, suggesting that humans are integral to the cosmic order.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • savanaṁ: This word refers to the act of offering or sacrifice, particularly in the context of rituals. It signifies the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • juṣāṇaḥ: This term means "enjoying" or "delighting in." It indicates the joy and fulfillment that comes from participating in sacred rituals and receiving blessings.
  • pibā: Translated as "drink," this word refers to the act of consuming, particularly in the context of the soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a significant element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine nectar or elixir. It represents spiritual nourishment and the quest for enlightenment.
  • śaśvate: This term means "eternal" or "everlasting," suggesting the timeless nature of the divine and the enduring impact of spiritual practices.
  • vīryāya: This word translates to "for strength" or "for valor." It highlights the importance of strength, both physical and spiritual, in overcoming life's challenges.
  • sa ā vavṛtsva: This phrase can be understood as "may he bestow" or "may he grant." It is a call for divine blessings and support in one's endeavors.
  • haryaśva: This term refers to "the swift horse," symbolizing speed, agility, and the ability to navigate through life's challenges effectively.
  • yajñaiḥ: This word means "by sacrifices" or "through offerings." It emphasizes the role of selfless actions and rituals in achieving spiritual goals.
  • saraṇyubhir: This term refers to "with the waters" or "with the streams," symbolizing the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • apo: Meaning "water," it signifies purity, sustenance, and the essential nature of water in life and rituals.
  • arṇā: This word can be interpreted as "the source" or "the origin," indicating the importance of understanding one's roots and origins in the spiritual journey.
  • sisarṣi: This term means "to flow" or "to spread," suggesting the dissemination of blessings and the impact of divine grace in the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of human existence in relation to the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals, the pursuit of strength and enlightenment, and the interconnectedness of all beings through the elements of nature. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges where one seeks strength and guidance, or in communal rituals that foster unity and spiritual growth. By engaging in selfless actions and honoring the divine through rituals, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and enhance their spiritual journey.
