यज्ञेन यज्ञं अयजन्त देवास तानि धर्माणि प्रथमाण्य आसन |ते ह नाकं महिमानः साचन्त यत्र पूर्वे साध्याः सन्ति देवाः ||
yajñena yajñam ayajanta devās tāni dharmāṇi prathamāny āsan | te ha nākam mahimānaḥ sacanta yatra pūrve sādhyāḥ santi devāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yajñena yajñam ayajanta devās tāni dharmāṇi prathamāny āsan | te ha nākam mahimānaḥ sacanta yatra pūrve sādhyāḥ santi devāḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The gods, having performed the sacrifice (yajña), established the laws (dharmas) that were first. They attained the great glory (mahimā) of heaven (nākam), where the ancient accomplished gods reside."

  • Key Terms Explained:
    • Yajña: Refers to a ritual of offerings accompanied by chanting of Vedic mantras. In real life, this can be seen in various religious ceremonies where offerings are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and seeking blessings.
    • Devās: The gods or divine beings. In contemporary terms, this can represent the forces of nature or higher ideals that guide human actions.
    • Dharmāṇi: The laws or principles of righteousness. In everyday life, this can be related to ethical conduct, social responsibilities, and moral values that govern human behavior.
    • Nākam: Refers to heaven or a state of bliss. This can be interpreted as the ultimate goal of human life, which is to achieve peace, happiness, and fulfillment.
    • Mahimānaḥ: Great glory or magnificence. This can be seen in the achievements of individuals or communities that strive for excellence and contribute positively to society.

  • Significance of the Mantra:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals (yajñas) in establishing moral and ethical laws (dharmas) that govern society.
    • It highlights the connection between divine beings and human actions, suggesting that through righteous actions, one can attain a higher state of existence (nākam).
    • The mantra serves as a reminder that the pursuit of dharma leads to glory and fulfillment, both in this life and beyond.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern society, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community service and charitable acts, reflecting the essence of yajña as selfless giving.
    • It encourages adherence to ethical principles in personal and professional life, promoting a harmonious and just society.
    • The pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual growth can be seen as a contemporary form of yajña, leading to personal and collective enlightenment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the idea that through selfless actions and adherence to dharma, individuals can connect with the divine and achieve a state of bliss and fulfillment. It serves as a guiding principle for ethical living and community engagement, reminding us of our responsibilities towards ourselves and others in the pursuit of a meaningful life.
