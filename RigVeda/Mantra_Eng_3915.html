यद इन्द्र ते चतस्रो यच छूर santi तिस्रः |यद वा पञ्च क्षितीनाम अवस तत सु न आ भर ||
yad indra te catasro yac chūra santi tisraḥ | yad vā pañca kṣitīnām avas tat su na ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad - whatever
    • indra - O Indra (the king of gods)
    • te - your
    • catasro - four
    • yac - whatever
    • chūra - three
    • santi - exist
    • tisraḥ - three
    • yad - whatever
    • vā - or
    • pañca - five
    • kṣitīnām - of the earths
    • avas - may it be
    • tat - that
    • su - good
    • na - not
    • ā - to
    • bhara - bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for his blessings.
    • The phrase "yad indra te catasro" refers to the four aspects or realms that Indra governs.
    • "yac chūra santi tisraḥ" suggests the three qualities or virtues that exist within these realms.
    • The latter part, "yad vā pañca kṣitīnām," speaks of the five earths or dimensions, emphasizing the vastness of existence.
    • Finally, "avas tat su na ā bhara" is a plea for good things to not be withheld from us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of drought, communities often pray for rain, similar to how this mantra seeks the blessings of Indra for sustenance.
    • The four realms can be likened to the four cardinal directions, representing stability and balance in our lives.
    • The three virtues can be seen in personal development, where individuals strive for wisdom, courage, and compassion.
    • The five earths can symbolize the different aspects of life—physical, emotional, mental, spiritual, and social—reminding us to nurture all dimensions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of seeking divine blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance around us and to strive for balance in our pursuits.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to come together in prayer and action for the greater good, fostering unity and harmony.
