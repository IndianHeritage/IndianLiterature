स हि विश्वानि पार्थिवाम् एको वसूनि पत्यते |गिर्वणस्तमो अध्रिगुः ||
sa hi viśvāni pārthivām̐ eko vasūni patyate | girvaṇastamo adhriguḥ ||
  • sa hi viśvāni pārthivām̐: This phrase translates to "Indeed, he (or it) is the one who governs all earthly beings." Here, "sa" refers to a singular entity, often interpreted as a divine force or universal consciousness. "viśvāni" means all or universal, and "pārthivām̐" refers to earthly or material beings.
  • eko vasūni patyate: This means "One who rules over all the treasures." "eko" signifies one or singular, "vasūni" refers to treasures or wealth, and "patyate" means to govern or rule. This suggests that there is a singular force that oversees all forms of wealth and resources in the world.
  • girvaṇastamo adhriguḥ: This can be interpreted as "The one who is the source of all sounds and knowledge." "girvaṇa" refers to sound or voice, and "tamo" means darkness or ignorance, while "adhriguḥ" implies the one who dispels darkness. This indicates that the divine force not only governs but also enlightens and provides knowledge.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra emphasizes the idea of a singular divine force that governs all aspects of life, including material wealth and knowledge.
  • In real life, this can be seen in the way individuals or leaders who embody wisdom and integrity can influence and guide communities towards prosperity and enlightenment.
  • It encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of a unified approach to life, where we seek knowledge and share resources for the greater good.
  • This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community building, and personal development, where the focus is on collective well-being and enlightenment.
