पीपिवांसं सरस्वतः स्तनं यो विश्वदर्शतः |भक्षीमहि प्रजाम इषम् ||
pīpivāṁsaṁ sarasvataḥ stanaṁ yo viśvadarśataḥ | bhakṣīmahi prajām iṣam ||
  • pīpivāṁsaṁ: This word can be broken down into "pīpivāṁsa," which refers to something that is nourishing or life-giving. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the essence of sustenance and abundance.
  • sarasvataḥ: This term is often associated with Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. It signifies the flow of knowledge and creativity.
  • stanaṁ: This word means "breast" or "nourishment." It represents the source of sustenance, akin to a mother providing nourishment to her child.
  • yo: This is a pronoun meaning "who" or "that." It connects the subject to the action or quality being described.
  • viśvadarśataḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of universal vision" or "seeing all." It implies a perspective that encompasses the entirety of existence.
  • bhakṣīmahi: This word means "we consume" or "we partake." It indicates an act of receiving or absorbing something valuable.
  • prajām: This translates to "creatures" or "beings." It refers to all forms of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of existence.
  • iṣam: This word means "desire" or "wish." It signifies the aspirations and intentions that drive actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for nourishment and wisdom. It expresses a desire to partake in the abundance of knowledge and sustenance that the universe offers. The connection to Saraswati highlights the importance of learning and creativity in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education: Just as the mantra invokes the goddess of knowledge, individuals can seek wisdom and understanding in their educational pursuits, striving to absorb knowledge that nourishes their intellect.
  • Creativity: Artists and creators can draw inspiration from this mantra, recognizing the importance of tapping into the universal flow of creativity to produce meaningful work.
  • Community and Relationships: The idea of nourishment can extend to relationships, where individuals support and uplift one another, creating a nurturing environment for growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for holistic nourishment—intellectual, emotional, and spiritual. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge, foster creativity, and build supportive communities. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can lead a more fulfilling and interconnected life, recognizing the abundance that surrounds us and the importance of sharing it with others.
