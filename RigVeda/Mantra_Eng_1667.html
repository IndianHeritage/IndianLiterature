किमु श्रेष्ठः किम्यविष्टो न आजगन् किमीयते दूत्यम् कद् यदूचिम |न निन्दिम चामसं यो महाकुलोऽग्ने भ्रातर द्रुण इद्भूतिम ऊदिम ||
kim u śreṣṭhaḥ kiṁ yaviṣṭho na ājagan kim īyate dūtya1ṁ kad yad ūcima | na nindima camasaṁ yo mahākulo 'gne bhrātar druṇa id bhūtim ūdima ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • u: or
    • śreṣṭhaḥ: the best
    • kiṁ: what
    • yaviṣṭho: is to be desired
    • na: not
    • ājagan: is born
    • kim: what
    • īyate: is to be done
    • dūtyaṁ: messenger
    • kad: when
    • yad: that
    • ūcima: is spoken
    • na: not
    • nindima: to criticize
    • camasaṁ: the vessel
    • yo: who
    • mahākulo: of great lineage
    • 'gne: O Agni (Fire)
    • bhrātar: brother
    • druṇa: strong
    • id: this
    • bhūtim: prosperity
    • ūdima: may it be attained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about what is truly the best and what should be desired in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding the value of actions and words, particularly in the context of communication and relationships.
    • The reference to Agni (Fire) symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine connection, suggesting that one should seek clarity and truth in their pursuits.
    • The mantra warns against criticism and negativity, especially towards those of noble lineage or character.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to encourage positive communication among colleagues. Instead of criticizing a team member's ideas, one should focus on constructive feedback that fosters growth.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the strengths of others rather than pointing out their flaws, promoting a more harmonious environment.
    • In community interactions, it encourages individuals to seek the best in others and to uplift those who contribute positively to society.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and the pursuit of higher values in life.
    • It teaches us to prioritize understanding and positivity over criticism and negativity, which is essential for personal and communal growth.
    • In today's fast-paced world, where negativity can often overshadow positivity, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for fostering better relationships and a more supportive environment.
