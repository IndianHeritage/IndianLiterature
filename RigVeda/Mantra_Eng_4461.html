अध स्मास्य पनयन्ति भासो वृथा यत तक्षद अनुयाति पृथ्वीम् |सद्यः यः स्यन्द्रो विषितो धवीयान ऋणो न तायुर अति धन्वा राट् ||
adha smāsya panayanti bhāso vṛthā yat takṣad anuyāti pṛthvīm | sadyo yaḥ syandro viṣito dhavīyān ṛṇo na tāyur ati dhanvā rāṭ ||
  • Adha smāsya panayanti bhāso vṛthā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the light that shines from below is not in vain." It suggests that there is a source of illumination or knowledge that comes from humble beginnings or lower realms. In real life, this can be likened to the idea that wisdom often comes from unexpected places, such as learning from those who may not hold traditional authority or status.
  • yat takṣad anuyāti pṛthvīm: This translates to "what shapes and follows the earth." It implies a connection between creation and the earth itself, emphasizing the importance of our actions and their impact on the world around us. For example, sustainable practices in agriculture and environmental conservation reflect this principle, as they shape the earth positively.
  • sadyo yaḥ syandro viṣito dhavīyān: This can be understood as "the one who is immediately established in strength." It speaks to the idea of immediate power or influence. In a real-world context, this could refer to leaders or individuals who take decisive action in times of crisis, demonstrating strength and resilience.
  • ṛṇo na tāyur ati dhanvā rāṭ: This phrase means "the debt does not exceed the bow of the king." It suggests that there are limits to obligations and responsibilities, particularly for those in power. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of ethical leadership, where leaders must balance their duties without overextending themselves or their resources.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of humility, the impact of our actions on the earth, the importance of decisive leadership, and the balance of responsibilities. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and leaders alike in making wise decisions that benefit both themselves and the larger community. By recognizing the value of knowledge from all sources, acting responsibly towards the environment, demonstrating strength in leadership, and maintaining ethical obligations, we can create a more harmonious and sustainable world.
