राजा समुद्रं नद्योऽविगाहते पाम ऊर्मिं साचते सिन्धुषु श्रितः |अध्यास्थात सानु पवमानो अव्ययं नाभा पृथिव्यां धरुणो महो दिवः ||
rājā samudraṁ nadyo3 vi gāhate 'pām ūrmiṁ sacate sindhuṣu śritaḥ | adhy asthāt sānu pavamāno avyayaṁ nābhā pṛthivyā dharuṇo maho divaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of a king (rājā) who traverses the vastness of the ocean (samudraṁ) and the rivers (nadyo) that flow into it. It describes how he is supported by the waves (ūrmiṁ) and is established (sacate) in the waters (sindhuṣu). The mantra further mentions the king's connection to the earth (pṛthivyā) and the heavens (divaḥ), emphasizing his stability and strength (avyayaṁ).
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rājā: King or ruler
    • samudraṁ: Ocean or vast body of water
    • nadyo: Rivers
    • vi gāhate: He traverses or moves through
    • ūrmiṁ: Waves
    • sacate: He is established or supported
    • sindhuṣu: In the waters or oceans
    • adhy: Above or over
    • asthāt: He stands or is situated
    • sānu: With strength or stability
    • pavamāno: Moving or flowing
    • avyayaṁ: Imperishable or eternal
    • nābhā: Center or core
    • pṛthivyā: Of the earth
    • dharuṇo: Strong or firm
    • maho: Great or vast
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The metaphor of a king navigating the ocean can be likened to a leader in today's world who must navigate the complexities of governance and societal challenges, much like a captain steering a ship through turbulent waters.
    • The concept of being supported by waves can represent the support systems in our lives, such as family, friends, and community, which help us stay afloat during difficult times.
    • The connection to the earth and heavens signifies the balance one must maintain in life, drawing strength from both material and spiritual realms, similar to how individuals seek grounding in their values while aspiring for higher goals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its portrayal of leadership, stability, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that true strength comes from being grounded while also being adaptable to the changing tides of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire leaders to be resilient and supportive, reminding individuals to seek balance and support in their personal and professional journeys.
