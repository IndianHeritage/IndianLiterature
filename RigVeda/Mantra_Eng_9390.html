पुनर् नो असुम् पृथिवी ददातु पुनर् द्यौर् देवī पुनर् अन्तरिक्षम् |पुनर् नः सोमस् तन्वं ददातु पुनः पूषा पथ्यां या स्वस्तिः ||
punar no asum pṛthivī dadātu punar dyaur devī punar antarikṣam | punar naḥ somas tanvaṁ dadātu punaḥ pūṣā pathyā3ṁ yā svastiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • punar: again, anew
    • no: to us
    • asum: life, vitality
    • pṛthivī: the earth
    • dadātu: may grant
    • dyaur: the sky
    • devī: goddess
    • antarikṣam: the space between
    • somas: the moon, nectar
    • tanvaṁ: body
    • pūṣā: the nourisher
    • pathyāṁ: the path
    • yā: which
    • svastiḥ: well-being, prosperity
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for renewal and rejuvenation.
    • It seeks blessings from the earth, sky, and divine forces for vitality and well-being.
    • The repetition of "punar" emphasizes the desire for a fresh start and continuous nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, this mantra can be invoked during times of stress or fatigue, reminding individuals to seek renewal and balance.
    • In environmental contexts, it can symbolize the need for sustainable practices that rejuvenate the earth.
    • In health and wellness, it can be a reminder to nourish the body and mind, promoting holistic well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for renewal and prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to environmental sustainability.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of rejuvenation, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
