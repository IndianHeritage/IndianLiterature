ह्रदं न हि त्वां न्यृषन्त्य ऊर्मयो ब्रह्माणीन्द्र तव यानि वर्धना | त्वष्टा चित्ते युज्यं वावृधे शवस् तातक्ष वज्रम अभिभूत्योजसम् ||
hradaṁ na hi tvā nyṛṣanty ūrmayo brahmāṇīndra tava yāni vardhanā | tvaṣṭā cit te yujyaṁ vāvṛdhe śavas tatakṣa vajram abhibhūtyojasam ||
  • hradaṁ - This word refers to a "lake" or "body of water," symbolizing abundance and the source of life.
  • na hi - This phrase means "not indeed," indicating a negation or a strong assertion.
  • tvā - This translates to "you," referring to a divine entity or a powerful force.
  • nyṛṣanty - This means "to be disturbed" or "to be shaken," suggesting a sense of stability or calmness that is not easily disrupted.
  • ūrmayo - This word means "waves," representing the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life.
  • brahmāṇīndra - This term combines "Brahman" (the ultimate reality) and "Indra" (the king of gods), symbolizing supreme power and authority.
  • tava - This means "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine.
  • yāni - This translates to "which," referring to the qualities or attributes of the divine.
  • vardhanā - This means "growth" or "increase," symbolizing prosperity and development.
  • tvaṣṭā - This refers to "the creator" or "the one who shapes," indicating a force that brings things into existence.
  • cit - This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," representing the essence of being.
  • te - This translates to "your," again indicating a connection to the divine.
  • yujyaṁ - This means "to be united" or "to be connected," suggesting harmony and balance.
  • vāvṛdhe - This translates to "to grow" or "to expand," indicating the potential for development.
  • śavas - This word means "life force" or "energy," representing vitality and strength.
  • tatakṣa - This means "to strike" or "to hit," suggesting action and impact.
  • vajram - This translates to "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing strength and indestructibility.
  • abhibhūtyojasam - This phrase means "overcoming obstacles" or "the power to conquer," indicating resilience and determination.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between the divine and the natural world, emphasizing stability amidst the chaos of life. It highlights the importance of growth, unity, and the power of creation. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals often face challenges (waves) but can find stability (lake) through self-awareness and connection to their inner strength (consciousness).
  • In community building, the collective growth (vardhanā) of individuals can lead to a stronger, united front (yujyaṁ) that can overcome obstacles (vajram).
  • In environmental conservation, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life (tava) can inspire actions that promote sustainability and harmony with nature.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, encouraging us to seek stability, embrace growth, and harness our inner strength to overcome challenges. Its significance lies in its applicability to personal, communal, and environmental contexts, guiding us towards a more harmonious existence.
