प्र वो वाजाऽभिद्यवो हविष्मन्तो घृताच्याः |देवाञ्ज जिगाति सुम्नयुः ||
pra vo vājā abhidyavo haviṣmanto ghṛtācyā | devāñ jigāti sumnayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • vo: your
    • vājā: the best, excellent, or powerful
    • abhidyavo: those who are worthy of worship or offerings
    • haviṣmanto: those who are endowed with offerings
    • ghṛtācyā: with clarified butter or ghee
    • devāñ: the gods
    • jigāti: who are invoked or called upon
    • sumnayuḥ: who bring auspiciousness or good fortune
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, inviting the gods to partake in the offerings made with ghee, and to bless the devotees with prosperity and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the importance of sincere offerings and the connection between the devotee and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, ghee is often used in offerings to the deities, symbolizing purity and the essence of life. For instance, during Diwali, people light lamps filled with ghee to invite prosperity and dispel darkness.
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team coming together to present their best ideas (vājā) to their leaders (devāñ) in hopes of receiving guidance and support (sumnayuḥ) for successful projects.
  • Significance and Application: The mantra highlights the importance of devotion, sincerity, and the act of giving. It teaches that when we offer our best (whether it be time, resources, or efforts) with a pure heart, we invite blessings and positive outcomes into our lives. This principle can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal relationships, professional endeavors, and spiritual practices.
