उदु श्रीया उषसो रोचमानाऽस्थुर अपां नर्मयो रुशन्तः |कृतोति विश्वा सुपथा सुगाण्य अभूदु वस्वी दक्षिणा मगहोनी ||
ud u śriya uṣaso rocamānā asthur apāṁ normayo ruśantaḥ | kṛṇoti viśvā supathā sugāny abhūd u vasvī dakṣiṇā maghonī ||
  • ud u śriya: This phrase can be translated as "upward and auspicious." It signifies the pursuit of higher ideals and prosperity in life.
  • uṣaso rocamānā: This means "the dawn is shining." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
  • asthur apāṁ normayo ruśantaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the waters are flowing with strength." It represents the life-giving properties of water and the importance of natural resources in sustaining life.
  • kṛṇoti viśvā supathā: This translates to "he who creates all good paths." It emphasizes the idea of guidance and the importance of making wise choices in life.
  • sugāny abhūd: This means "good things have come." It reflects the abundance and positivity that can arise from following the right path.
  • u vasvī dakṣiṇā maghonī: This phrase can be understood as "may the generous and prosperous blessings come." It highlights the significance of generosity and the blessings that come from it.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity, guidance, and new beginnings. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new venture, making important life decisions, or seeking spiritual growth. The emphasis on water signifies the importance of nurturing our environment and resources, while the call for good paths encourages individuals to make wise choices. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for positivity, abundance, and the pursuit of higher ideals in our daily lives.
