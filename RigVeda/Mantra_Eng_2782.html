मन्त्र: ब्रह्मणा ते ब्रह्मयुजा युनज्मि हरि सखायाऽसधमाद āशू |स्थिरं रथं सुखं इन्द्राधितिष्ठन् प्रजानन् विद्वां उप याहि सोमम् ||
brahmaṇā te brahmayujā yunajmi harī sakhāyā sadhamāda āśū | sthiraṁ rathaṁ sukham indrādhitiṣṭhan prajānan vidvām̐ upa yāhi somam ||
  • Brahmaṇā te: This phrase translates to "by the divine" or "through the supreme consciousness." It signifies the connection to a higher power or universal consciousness that guides and supports us.
  • Brahmayujā yunajmi: This means "I unite with the divine." It emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with the higher self or the ultimate reality, suggesting a journey towards spiritual awakening.
  • Harī sakhāyā: This translates to "with the friend, the divine." It indicates the presence of a supportive force or companion in our spiritual journey, akin to having a mentor or guide in life.
  • Sadhamāda āśū: This phrase means "may I attain happiness swiftly." It reflects the desire for quick fulfillment of joy and well-being, highlighting the importance of seeking happiness in our lives.
  • Sthiraṁ rathaṁ sukham: This translates to "a stable chariot of happiness." The chariot symbolizes our life journey, and stability in this context refers to a balanced and secure life that leads to happiness.
  • Indrādhitiṣṭhan: This means "standing under the protection of Indra." Indra is often seen as a deity of strength and protection, suggesting that we seek divine support in our endeavors.
  • Prajānan vidvām̐: This translates to "knowing the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life, encouraging us to learn from those who are knowledgeable.
  • Upa yāhi somam: This means "come near to the nectar." The nectar symbolizes bliss or ultimate happiness, suggesting that we should strive to attain this state of joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, aligning oneself with higher consciousness, and striving for happiness and stability in life. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the support of divine forces in our journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks guidance, in moments of decision-making where wisdom is required, or in the pursuit of personal happiness and fulfillment. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with their spiritual selves and navigate life's challenges with a sense of purpose and joy.
