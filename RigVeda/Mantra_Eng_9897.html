तां वज्रीणं मन्दिनं स्तोम्यं मद इन्द्रं रथे वहतो हर्यतां हरि |पुरूण्य अस्मै सवनानि हर्यत इन्द्राय सोमाः हरयो दधन्विरे ||
tā vajriṇam mandinaṁ stomyam mada indraṁ rathe vahato haryatā harī | purūṇy asmai savanāni haryata indrāya somā harayo dadhanvire ||
  • tā vajriṇam mandinaṁ: This phrase refers to the powerful and mighty Indra, who is often depicted as the wielder of the thunderbolt (Vajra). In real life, this can symbolize strength and resilience in overcoming challenges.
  • stomyam mada indraṁ: "Stomyam" means to praise or extol, while "mada" refers to intoxication or joy. This suggests that Indra is celebrated for his joyous nature and achievements. In our lives, we often celebrate individuals who inspire joy and positivity, much like how we honor leaders or heroes.
  • rathe vahato haryatā harī: This line speaks of Indra riding in his chariot, symbolizing movement and progress. The chariot can represent the journey of life, where we strive to achieve our goals. In real life, this can be likened to pursuing our dreams and ambitions with determination.
  • purūṇy asmai savanāni: "Purūṇy" means many or abundant, and "savanāni" refers to offerings or sacrifices. This indicates the abundance of offerings made to Indra, representing gratitude and respect. In our daily lives, we often express gratitude for the abundance we receive, whether through rituals, celebrations, or simple acts of kindness.
  • haryata indrāya somā: "Haryata" means to bring forth or present, and "somā" refers to the sacred drink offered to deities. This line emphasizes the importance of offerings in seeking blessings. In modern life, this can be seen in various forms of giving back to the community or supporting causes that resonate with us.
  • harayo dadhanvire: This phrase suggests the strength and valor of Indra, who is depicted as a warrior. It highlights the importance of courage and bravery in facing adversities. In real life, we often need to summon our inner strength to overcome obstacles, much like how warriors face challenges with valor.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the themes of strength, joy, gratitude, and the importance of offerings. It serves as a reminder to celebrate the positive influences in our lives, pursue our goals with determination, and express gratitude for the abundance we receive. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and communal endeavors, encouraging us to embody the qualities of resilience, joy, and generosity in our daily interactions and pursuits.
