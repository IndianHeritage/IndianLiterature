तं न इन्द्रो वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः आपः ओषधीर वनिनो जुषन्ता |शर्मन् स्याम मारुताम उपस्थे युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
tan na indro varuṇo mitro agnir āpa oṣadhīr vanino juṣanta | śarman syāma marutām upasthe yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • tan - This word translates to "that" or "those," indicating a focus on the subjects that follow.
  • na - Meaning "not," it negates the following terms, emphasizing the importance of the subjects.
  • indro - Refers to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
  • varuṇo - Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing moral authority and the natural order.
  • mitro - Refers to Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony, emphasizing the importance of relationships.
  • agnir - Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • āpa - Meaning "water," representing life, sustenance, and the flow of energy.
  • oṣadhīr - Refers to plants or herbs, symbolizing healing and nourishment.
  • vanino - Meaning "of the forest," indicating a connection to nature and its resources.
  • juṣanta - Meaning "enjoying" or "delighting in," suggesting a state of well-being and abundance.
  • śarman - Refers to peace or happiness, indicating a desire for tranquility.
  • syāma - Meaning "may we be," indicating a wish or aspiration.
  • marutām - Refers to the Maruts, the storm gods, symbolizing strength and dynamic energy.
  • upasthe - Meaning "in our presence," indicating a call for divine presence and support.
  • yūyam - Meaning "you all," addressing the divine entities collectively.
  • pāta - Meaning "protect," indicating a plea for safety and guidance.
  • svastibhiḥ - Refers to auspiciousness or well-being, emphasizing health and prosperity.
  • sadā - Meaning "always," indicating a continuous desire for the blessings mentioned.
  • naḥ - Meaning "us," indicating a collective plea for the community.

This mantra is a prayer invoking various deities for protection, well-being, and harmony. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human existence. The invocation of Indra, Varuna, Mitra, and Agni highlights the importance of strength, moral order, friendship, and transformation in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for festivals or communal activities, invoking these deities can foster a sense of unity and harmony.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to nature and plants encourages respect for the environment, promoting sustainable practices.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of strength, moral integrity, and transformation can inspire individuals to strive for personal development and resilience in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for divine support in achieving peace, prosperity, and harmony. It serves as a reminder of our connection to the natural world and the divine, encouraging us to seek balance and well-being in our lives.
