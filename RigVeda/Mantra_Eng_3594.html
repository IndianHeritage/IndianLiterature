दिवो धार्ता भुवनस्य प्रजापतिः पीशङ्गं द्रापिम प्रति मुंचते कविः |विचक्षणः प्रथयन्न आपृणन्न उर्व अजीजनत सविता सुम्नम उक्त्यम् ||
divo dhartā bhuvanasya prajāpatiḥ piśaṅgaṁ drāpim prati muñcate kaviḥ | vicakṣaṇaḥ prathayann āpṛṇann urv ajījanat savitā sumnam ukthyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Dhartā: The sustainer or supporter
    • Bhuvanasya: Of the world or universe
    • Prajāpatiḥ: The lord of creatures or progenitor
    • Piśaṅgaṁ: Yellow or golden
    • Drāpim: The one who is restrained or held
    • Prati: Towards
    • Muñcate: Releases or liberates
    • Kaviḥ: The wise or seer
    • Vicakṣaṇaḥ: The one who is perceptive or insightful
    • Prathayann: Spreading or manifesting
    • Āpṛṇann: Filling or providing
    • Urva: The vast or expansive
    • Ajījanat: Created or brought forth
    • Savitā: The sun or the divine light
    • Sumnam: Auspicious or favorable
    • Ukthyam: The hymn or praise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine force that sustains the universe and all living beings.
    • The term "Divo dhartā" refers to the heavenly sustainer, indicating a higher power that supports life.
    • "Prajāpatiḥ" signifies the creator or lord of creatures, emphasizing the importance of creation and procreation.
    • The phrase "piśaṅgaṁ drāpim prati muñcate" suggests the release of constraints, symbolizing liberation from ignorance or limitations.
    • "Kaviḥ" and "vicakṣaṇaḥ" highlight the role of wisdom and insight in understanding the universe.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for auspiciousness and favorable outcomes, invoking the divine light of the sun.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek understanding and enlightenment.
    • In a community setting, it can inspire collective efforts towards sustainability and support for one another, akin to the sustainer of the universe.
    • In personal growth, the idea of releasing constraints can be applied to overcoming fears and limitations, allowing individuals to reach their full potential.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support that sustains life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek wisdom, embrace creation, and strive for auspiciousness in our actions.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to work towards enlightenment, sustainability, and mutual support, fostering a harmonious existence.
