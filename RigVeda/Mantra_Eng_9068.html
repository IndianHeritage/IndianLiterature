एतौ मे गावौ प्रमरस्य युक्तौ मोषु प्र सेधीर्मुहुर इन ममन्धि |आपश्चिदस्य विनाशन्त्यर्थं सूरश्च मार्क उपरो बभूवान ||
etau me gāvau pramarasya yuktau mo ṣu pra sedhīr muhur in mamandhi | āpaś cid asya vi naśanty arthaṁ sūraś ca marka uparo babhūvān ||
  • etau me gāvau: This phrase translates to "these two cows of mine." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity. They are often associated with the sustenance of life and the agricultural economy.
  • pramarasya yuktau: This means "in the context of the wise." It suggests that the speaker is referring to the cows in a manner that is thoughtful and wise, indicating a deeper understanding of their significance.
  • mo ṣu pra sedhīr: This can be interpreted as "may they not be obstructed." It reflects a desire for the cows (or the blessings they represent) to be free from hindrances, allowing for prosperity and abundance.
  • muhur in mamandhi: This phrase can be understood as "may they come to me repeatedly." It expresses a longing for continuous blessings and the return of prosperity in cycles.
  • āpaś cid asya vi naśanty arthaṁ: This translates to "the waters of this (blessing) do not diminish." It signifies the idea that true wealth and blessings are everlasting and should not be depleted.
  • sūraś ca marka uparo babhūvān: This means "the brave and the wise have become prosperous." It emphasizes that those who are courageous and knowledgeable will achieve success and abundance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity, wisdom, and the continuous flow of blessings in life. It highlights the importance of nurturing one's resources (symbolized by the cows) and the need for wisdom in managing them. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as business, personal growth, and community welfare. For instance, in a business context, it encourages entrepreneurs to seek knowledge and act wisely to ensure the growth and sustainability of their ventures. In personal life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate and nurture the resources we have, ensuring they are not wasted but rather cultivated for future prosperity. Overall, the mantra signifies the interconnectedness of wisdom, courage, and abundance in achieving a fulfilling life.
