नपाताऽशवसो महः सूनु दाक्षस्य सुक्रतू |सृप्रदानू इषो वास्त्व अधि क्षितः ||
napātā śavaso mahaḥ sūnū dakṣasya sukratū | sṛpradānū iṣo vāstv adhi kṣitaḥ ||
  • napātā: This word translates to "descendants" or "offspring." It signifies the importance of lineage and the continuation of family or community values.
  • śavaso: This term means "of the dead" or "of the ancestors." It highlights the connection to those who have passed away, emphasizing respect for ancestors and the wisdom they impart.
  • mahaḥ: This word means "great" or "mighty." It denotes strength, power, and significance, often referring to the greatness of the divine or the universe.
  • sūnū: This translates to "son" or "child." It represents the next generation and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • dakṣasya: This word means "of Dakṣa," referring to a deity associated with creation and prosperity. It symbolizes skill, competence, and the ability to achieve goals.
  • sukratū: This term means "one who performs good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and ethical behavior in life.
  • sṛpradānū: This translates to "those who give generously." It signifies the value of generosity and sharing with others.
  • iṣo: This word means "of the desires" or "of the wishes." It reflects the aspirations and goals that individuals strive for in life.
  • vāstv: This term means "may it be" or "let it be." It expresses a wish or a prayer for fulfillment.
  • adhi: This translates to "above" or "over." It indicates a higher state or a greater purpose.
  • kṣitaḥ: This word means "earth" or "ground." It represents the physical world and our connection to it.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that seeks blessings for the descendants, acknowledging the greatness of the ancestors and the importance of good deeds and generosity. It emphasizes the need for ethical living and the pursuit of one's desires in a righteous manner.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In family gatherings, it can serve as a reminder to honor and respect the contributions of ancestors while guiding the younger generation.
  • In community service, it encourages individuals to perform good deeds and share their resources with those in need.
  • In personal development, it inspires individuals to pursue their goals ethically and with a sense of responsibility towards others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of generations, the importance of ethical behavior, and the value of generosity. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals to lead a life that honors the past while striving for a better future.
