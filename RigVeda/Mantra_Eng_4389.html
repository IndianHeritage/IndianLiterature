मन्त्र: क्रत्वा हि द्रोणे अज्यसे 'ग्ने वाजी न कृतव्यः |परिज्मेव स्वधा गयो 'त्यो न ह्वार्यः शिशुः ||
kratvā hi droṇe ajyase 'gne vājī na kṛtvyaḥ | parijmeva svadhā gayo 'tyo na hvāryaḥ śiśuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kratvā hi droṇe ajyase 'gne vājī na kṛtvyaḥ | parijmeva svadhā gayo 'tyo na hvāryaḥ śiśuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kratvā: by performing or through action
    • hi: indeed
    • droṇe: in the vessel or container
    • ajyase: you are being offered or poured
    • 'gne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vājī: the one who brings strength or energy
    • na: not
    • kṛtvyaḥ: to be done or performed
    • parijmeva: as if surrounding or encompassing
    • svadhā: self-offering or self-sacrifice
    • gayo: the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • 'tyo: beyond or surpassing
    • na: not
    • hvāryaḥ: to be invoked or called upon
    • śiśuḥ: the child or offspring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of actions and offerings in the context of rituals.
    • It suggests that one should not merely perform rituals for the sake of performance but should engage in them with intention and purpose.
    • The reference to Agni highlights the significance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • The cow, representing wealth and sustenance, indicates that true offerings should lead to nourishment and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the concept of mindful living. For instance, when engaging in any task, whether at work or home, one should do so with full awareness and intention rather than as a mere obligation.
    • In the context of community service, the mantra encourages individuals to contribute selflessly, ensuring that their actions lead to the betterment of others, much like the self-offering of the cow in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to invest genuine effort and care, rather than superficial gestures, to foster deeper connections.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intentionality in our actions and offerings.
    • It teaches that true value lies not in the act itself but in the spirit and purpose behind it.
    • By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can cultivate a more meaningful existence, fostering growth and connection in both personal and communal spheres.
