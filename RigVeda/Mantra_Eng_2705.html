दिशः सूर्यो न मिनाति प्रदिष्टा दिवे-दिवे हर्यश्वप्रसूताḥ |सं यद आनाḻ अध्वना आद इद अश्वैर विमोचनं कृतुते तात् त्व अस्य ||
diśaḥ sūryo na mināti pradiṣṭā dive-dive haryaśvaprasūtāḥ | saṁ yad ānaḻ adhvana ād id aśvair vimocanaṁ kṛṇute tat tv asya ||
  • diśaḥ - Directions or realms
  • sūryo - The Sun
  • na mināti - Does not diminish
  • pradiṣṭā - Established or fixed
  • dive-dive - Day by day
  • haryaśvaprasūtāḥ - Born of the swift horses
  • saṁ - Together
  • yad - That which
  • ānaḻ - Fire or energy
  • adhvana - Path or way
  • ād - From the beginning
  • id - This
  • aśvair - With the horses
  • vimocanaṁ - Liberation or release
  • kṛṇute - Creates or brings about
  • tat - That
  • tv - You
  • asya - Of this

The mantra can be interpreted as follows:

  • The Sun, representing light and knowledge, does not diminish as it travels through the directions.
  • Each day, it is established and brings forth the energy that is born from the swift horses, symbolizing speed and power.
  • As we journey through life, the energy that we harness from the Sun and the paths we take can lead to liberation and fulfillment.

In real life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the constancy of the Sun and the energy it provides. Just as the Sun rises each day without fail, we too can find strength and resilience in our daily lives. The swift horses can symbolize the rapid pace of modern life, where we must harness our energy wisely to navigate our paths effectively.


Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of consistency, energy, and the paths we choose in life. It teaches us that, like the Sun, we should strive to remain steadfast and vibrant, using our energy to create positive outcomes. In practical terms, it can inspire us to maintain a routine that nurtures our physical and mental well-being, ensuring that we are always moving towards liberation and fulfillment in our lives.
