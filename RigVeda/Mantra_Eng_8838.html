तं उष्राम इन्द्रं न रेजमानं अग्निं गीरभिर नमोभिर आ कृणुध्वम् |आ यं विप्रासो मतीभिर गृणन्ति जातवेदसं जुह्वं सहानाम् ||
tam usrām indraṁ na rejamānam agniṁ gīrbhir namobhir ā kṛṇudhvam | ā yaṁ viprāso matibhir gṛṇanti jātavedasaṁ juhvaṁ sahānām ||
  • tam usrām indraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "that Indra who is awake." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can symbolize the awakening of potential or the realization of one's inner strength.
  • na rejamānam: This means "not being disturbed." It suggests a state of calmness and stability. In everyday life, this can relate to maintaining composure in challenging situations, such as during a stressful work environment or personal crisis.
  • agniṁ: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, who represents energy, transformation, and purification. In practical terms, this can be likened to the fire of passion or creativity that drives individuals to pursue their goals, such as an artist creating a masterpiece or a scientist conducting groundbreaking research.
  • gīrbhir namobhir ā kṛṇudhvam: This translates to "with hymns and salutations, we invoke." It emphasizes the importance of respect and reverence in our actions. In real life, this can be seen in rituals, ceremonies, or even in daily interactions where gratitude and acknowledgment of others play a crucial role, such as thanking a mentor or appreciating a colleague's efforts.
  • ā yaṁ viprāso matibhir gṛṇanti: This means "that which the wise ones sing with their intellect." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In practical scenarios, this can be related to the pursuit of education and the sharing of knowledge, such as teachers imparting wisdom to students or researchers publishing their findings for the benefit of society.
  • jātavedasaṁ juhvaṁ sahānām: This translates to "the one who is the knower of all beings, we offer our oblations." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of community. In real life, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and support within communities, such as volunteering for local charities or helping neighbors in need.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of awakening, stability, reverence, wisdom, and interconnectedness. Its significance lies in the reminder to harness our inner strength, maintain composure, and act with respect and gratitude towards others. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in personal growth, community engagement, and the pursuit of knowledge, ultimately fostering a harmonious and supportive environment for all.
