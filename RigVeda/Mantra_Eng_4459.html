तेजिष्ठा यस्यारतिर्वनेराट् तदोऽध्वन् न वृधसानो अद्यौत् |अद्रोघो न द्रविता चेतति तन्मन् अमर्त्योऽवर्त्रो ओषधिषु ||
tejiṣṭhā yasyāratir vanerāṭ todo adhvan na vṛdhasāno adyaut | adrogho na dravitā cetati tmann amartyo 'vartra oṣadhīṣu ||
  • tejiṣṭhā: This word can be translated as "the most radiant" or "the most powerful." It signifies the essence of energy and vitality that is present in all forms of life.
  • yasyāratir: This means "whose desire" or "whose inclination." It refers to the aspirations and motivations that drive individuals.
  • vanerāṭ: This can be interpreted as "in the forest" or "in nature." It emphasizes the connection between human desires and the natural world.
  • todo: This word means "to break" or "to overcome." It suggests the idea of overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • adhvan: This translates to "the path" or "the way." It represents the journey one takes in life, filled with various experiences and lessons.
  • na vṛdhasāno: This phrase means "not in vain" or "not without purpose." It highlights the importance of intention and meaningful actions.
  • adyaut: This word means "has shone" or "has appeared." It signifies the arrival of enlightenment or understanding.
  • adrogho: This translates to "without deceit" or "truthful." It emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity in one's actions.
  • na dravitā: This means "not to be disturbed" or "not to be shaken." It suggests a sense of stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • cetati: This word means "to think" or "to reflect." It encourages introspection and mindfulness in one's thoughts and actions.
  • tmann: This refers to "the self" or "the soul." It emphasizes the importance of self-awareness and understanding one's true nature.
  • amartyo: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the idea of the soul's everlasting nature beyond physical existence.
  • 'vartra: This word means "to protect" or "to guard." It suggests the need for safeguarding one's values and principles.
  • oṣadhīṣu: This translates to "in the herbs" or "in the plants." It highlights the connection between nature and the healing properties found within it.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interplay between human desires, the natural world, and the journey of life. It encourages individuals to pursue their aspirations with integrity and purpose, while remaining connected to nature and self-aware. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, environmental conservation, and ethical decision-making. By embodying the principles of honesty, resilience, and mindfulness, individuals can navigate their paths with clarity and intention, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
