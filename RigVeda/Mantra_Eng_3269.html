मन्त्र: उता माता महिषं अन्व अवेनद अमी त्वा जहति पुत्र देवाः |अथाब्रवीद वृत्रम इन्द्रो हनिष्यन् सखे विष्णो वितरं वि क्रमस्व ||
uta mātā mahiṣam anv avenad amī tvā jahati putra devāḥ | athābravīd vṛtram indro haniṣyan sakhe viṣṇo vitaraṁ vi kramasva ||
  • uta mātā mahiṣam anv avenad amī tvā jahati putra devāḥ
  • Translation: "O Indra, the gods have sent you to slay the demon Vritra, who is like a mother to the cattle. They abandon you, O son, if you do not act."
  • Explanation:
    • Indra: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of thunder and rain. He represents strength and power.
    • Mahiṣam: Refers to the demon Vritra, who is often depicted as a serpent or dragon that obstructs the waters and is a symbol of chaos and drought.
    • Putra devāḥ: This phrase emphasizes the relationship between Indra and the other gods, highlighting the expectation that he must fulfill his duty to protect and provide for them.
  • athābravīd vṛtram indro haniṣyan sakhe viṣṇo vitaraṁ vi kramasva
  • Translation: "Then Indra spoke, 'I will slay Vritra, O friend Vishnu, grant me strength and let me proceed.'"
  • Explanation:
    • Vritra: Represents obstacles and challenges in life, often seen as a metaphor for ignorance or negativity that needs to be overcome.
    • Vishnu: Another principal deity in Hinduism, representing preservation and protection. Indra seeks assistance from Vishnu, symbolizing the importance of collaboration and support in overcoming challenges.
    • Haniṣyan: Indicates the act of slaying or overcoming, which can be interpreted as the need to confront and defeat our personal demons or challenges.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the themes of duty, strength, and the importance of collaboration in overcoming obstacles. It serves as a reminder that even the most powerful beings, like Indra, require support and guidance from others, such as Vishnu, to achieve their goals.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal life, this mantra can inspire individuals to confront their fears and challenges, much like Indra confronting Vritra. It encourages seeking help from friends or mentors when facing difficulties.
    • In a professional context, it highlights the importance of teamwork and collaboration. Just as Indra relies on Vishnu, individuals in a workplace should recognize the value of working together to achieve common goals.
    • In a broader societal context, it can be seen as a call to action for communities to unite against common challenges, such as environmental issues or social injustices, emphasizing collective strength.
