एव rāजेव क्रतुमां अमेन विश्वा घनिघ्नद दुरितापवस्व |इन्दो सूक्ताय वचसे वयो धा युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
evā rājeva kratumām̐ amena viśvā ghanighnad duritā pavasva | indo sūktāya vacase vayo dhā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • evā rājeva kratumām̐: This phrase can be interpreted as "May the thoughts and intentions of the ruler (or leader) be aligned with righteousness." In real life, this emphasizes the importance of ethical leadership. For example, a CEO who prioritizes the well-being of employees and the environment over mere profit maximization embodies this principle.
  • amena viśvā ghanighnad: This translates to "May all that is heavy and burdensome be removed." It suggests the idea of alleviating difficulties and challenges. In practical terms, this could relate to a community coming together to support individuals facing hardships, such as organizing food drives for those in need.
  • duritā pavasva: This means "May the obstacles be washed away." This can be likened to overcoming personal challenges, such as a student who struggles with academic performance but seeks help and ultimately succeeds, illustrating resilience and determination.
  • indo sūktāya vacase: This phrase can be understood as "With the words of wisdom, may we be guided." It highlights the significance of knowledge and guidance in our lives. For instance, a mentor providing advice to a young professional can be seen as embodying this wisdom, helping them navigate their career path.
  • vayo dhā yūyam pāta: This translates to "May you protect our age and vitality." This emphasizes the importance of health and well-being. In real life, this could relate to community health initiatives that promote physical fitness and mental well-being, such as yoga classes or wellness workshops.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "Always may you bring us well-being." It signifies a prayer for continuous prosperity and health. For example, a family that practices gratitude and mindfulness can create a positive environment that fosters well-being and happiness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing ethical leadership, community support, the removal of obstacles, the importance of wisdom, health, and well-being. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to community development. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can strive for a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
