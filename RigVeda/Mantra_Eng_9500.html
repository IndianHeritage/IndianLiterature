धृतव्रताः क्षत्रियाः यज्ञनिष्कृतो बृहद्विवा अध्वराणाम् अभिश्रियः | अग्निहोत्रा ऋतसापो अद्रुहोऽपो असृजनन् अनु वृततूर्ये ||
dhṛtavratāḥ kṣatriyā yajñaniṣkṛto bṛhaddivā adhvarāṇām abhiśriyaḥ | agnihotāra ṛtasāpo adruho 'po asṛjann anu vṛtratūrye ||
  • dhṛtavratāḥ: This term refers to those who are steadfast in their vows or commitments. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who remain dedicated to their goals, such as athletes training for a competition or students pursuing academic excellence despite challenges.
  • kṣatriyāḥ: This word denotes the warrior class in ancient Indian society, symbolizing strength, courage, and the duty to protect. In modern contexts, this can be seen in individuals who take on leadership roles, such as military personnel or community leaders who stand up for justice and safety.
  • yajñaniṣkṛto: This term refers to those who are engaged in sacrificial rituals or offerings. In contemporary life, this can be compared to acts of charity and service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, akin to volunteers working in non-profit organizations.
  • bṛhaddivā: This phrase means "great light" or "vastness of the day." It symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and wisdom, where individuals seek to illuminate their minds and the minds of others through learning and sharing knowledge.
  • adhvarāṇām: This refers to the rituals or sacrifices performed. In a modern context, it can be seen as the various ceremonies and traditions that bring communities together, such as weddings, festivals, or memorial services that honor shared values and history.
  • abhiśriyaḥ: This term signifies those who are prosperous or blessed. In real life, this can be associated with individuals or communities that thrive through hard work and ethical practices, leading to success and well-being.
  • agnihotāra: This refers to the fire sacrifice, a significant ritual in Vedic traditions. In today's world, it can symbolize the importance of maintaining a connection with nature and the environment, such as sustainable practices that honor the earth.
  • ṛtasāpo: This term means "truthful" or "righteous." In real life, it can be related to individuals who uphold integrity and honesty in their actions, such as whistleblowers who expose wrongdoing for the greater good.
  • adruho: This word means "non-hostile" or "peaceful." In modern contexts, it can be associated with conflict resolution and diplomacy, where individuals or nations strive to maintain peace and harmony.
  • 'po: This term refers to "water" or "life." In real life, it can symbolize the essential resources we need for survival and the importance of conserving water and protecting our natural resources.
  • asṛjann: This means "to create" or "to produce." In contemporary life, it can be related to innovation and creativity, where individuals or companies develop new ideas, products, or solutions to improve society.
  • anu vṛtratūrye: This phrase can be interpreted as "following the path of overcoming obstacles." In real life, this can be seen in the journeys of individuals who face challenges and adversities but persist in their efforts to achieve their dreams, such as entrepreneurs who navigate the complexities of starting a business.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of commitment, courage, righteousness, and the pursuit of knowledge. It highlights the importance of community, ethical practices, and the interconnectedness of life. In today's world, these principles are applicable in various scenarios, from personal development to social responsibility. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their lives and the lives of others.
