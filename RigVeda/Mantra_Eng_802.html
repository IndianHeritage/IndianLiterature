वर्धान यं पूर्वीः क्षपो विरूपाः स्थातुश्च रथम ऋतप्रवीतम् ||
vardhān yam pūrvīḥ kṣapo virūpāḥ sthātuś ca ratham ṛtapravītam ||
  • Vardhān: This word translates to "growth" or "prosperity." It signifies the idea of increasing wealth, knowledge, or well-being.
  • Yam: This means "whom" or "that which." It indicates a connection or relationship with the subject being discussed.
  • Pūrvīḥ: This term refers to "the past" or "ancient." It suggests a link to traditions, heritage, or the wisdom of ancestors.
  • Kṣapo: This word can mean "of the night" or "darkness." It symbolizes challenges, ignorance, or the unknown aspects of life.
  • Virūpāḥ: This translates to "having different forms" or "varied." It indicates diversity and the multifaceted nature of existence.
  • Sthātuś: This means "to stand" or "to be stable." It represents stability, strength, and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas together.
  • Ratham: This word means "chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the journey of life, progress, and movement towards goals.
  • Ṛtapravītam: This term translates to "guided by truth" or "following the path of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of truth and ethical conduct in one's journey.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for growth and prosperity, rooted in the wisdom of the past, while acknowledging the challenges of life. It emphasizes the importance of stability and resilience as one navigates through the diverse experiences of life, all while being guided by truth and righteousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their past experiences to foster personal development, learning from both successes and failures.
  • Community Development: Communities can strive for prosperity by embracing diversity and working together towards common goals, ensuring stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Ethical Leadership: Leaders can embody the principles of truth and righteousness, guiding their teams or organizations towards ethical practices and sustainable growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of growth, stability, and truth. It encourages individuals and communities to learn from the past, embrace diversity, and remain steadfast in their pursuit of righteousness, ultimately leading to a prosperous and meaningful life.
