यदी मातुर उप स्वसा घृतम् भरन्त्य स्थिता |तासाम अध्वर्युर आगतौ यवो वृष्टिवा मोदते ||
yadī mātur upa svasā ghṛtam bharanty asthita | tāsām adhvaryur āgatau yavo vṛṣṭīva modate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadī: if
    • mātur: of the mother
    • upa: near
    • svasā: of the sister
    • ghṛtam: ghee (clarified butter)
    • bharanty: they carry
    • asthita: steadfast
    • tāsām: of them
    • adhvaryur: the priest (performer of the sacrifice)
    • āgatau: in the arrival
    • yavo: of the young ones
    • vṛṣṭīva: like the rain
    • modate: rejoices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nurturing and supportive roles of women, particularly mothers and sisters, who provide sustenance (symbolized by ghee) and stability in life.
    • The act of carrying ghee signifies the importance of nourishment and care that women provide in a family and society.
    • The reference to the priest indicates the spiritual and ritualistic aspects of life, suggesting that the presence and contributions of women are essential for the success of rituals and community well-being.
    • The comparison to rain highlights the joy and abundance that comes from their contributions, much like how rain brings life and prosperity to the earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, mothers are seen as the backbone of the family, providing emotional and physical support. For instance, a mother’s encouragement can motivate her children to pursue their dreams, much like the ghee that nourishes the body.
    • Sisters often play a crucial role in supporting each other through life's challenges, akin to the steadfastness mentioned in the mantra. Their bond can be a source of strength and joy, similar to the rain that nourishes the earth.
    • In community settings, women often take on roles that ensure the well-being of the group, such as organizing events or providing care for the elderly, reflecting the nurturing aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of women in nurturing and sustaining life, both in familial and societal contexts.
    • It serves as a reminder of the essential roles that mothers and sisters play, encouraging respect and appreciation for their contributions.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire us to recognize and support the vital roles of women in our communities, fostering an environment of gratitude and collaboration.
