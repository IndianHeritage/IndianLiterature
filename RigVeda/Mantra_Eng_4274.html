अश्विनाऽयद्ध कर्हि चिच्छुश्रूयातम इमं हवम् |वस्वीरूषु वाम्भुजः पृञ्चन्ति सुवां पृचः ||
aśvinā yad dha karhi cic chuśrūyātam imaṁ havam | vasvīr ū ṣu vām bhujaḥ pṛñcanti su vām pṛcaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā yad dha karhi cic chuśrūyātam imaṁ havam | vasvīr ū ṣu vām bhujaḥ pṛñcanti su vām pṛcaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Ashvins, whenever you hear this invocation, may you come swiftly to us; may the strong ones, who are your companions, bring you to us."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are twin deities in Hindu mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Havam: This term refers to the offerings or invocations made to the deities during rituals. It signifies the connection between the devotee and the divine.
    • Vasvīr: This word denotes strength and power, often referring to the companions or forces that assist the Ashvins in their divine tasks.
    • Pṛñcanti: This term means to bring or to carry, indicating the action of the deities responding to the invocation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, one can relate to the Ashvins as symbols of hope and healing. For instance, when someone is ill, they may invoke the Ashvins for health and recovery, reflecting the belief in divine intervention.
    • The mantra can be used in rituals or prayers for guidance and support during challenging times, such as during a medical procedure or a significant life change, emphasizing the need for strength and assistance.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of invoking divine help and the belief in the presence of higher powers in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there are forces that can aid us if we seek their assistance.
    • The Ashvins represent the balance of dualities, encouraging individuals to embrace both the light and dark aspects of life, fostering resilience and adaptability.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, particularly in times of need, uncertainty, or transition.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and connection to the divine, reinforcing the belief in support and healing.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages a proactive approach to seeking help, reminding us of the strength found in community, both earthly and divine.
