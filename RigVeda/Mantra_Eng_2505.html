उद अस्तम्भीत समिधा नाकं ऋष्वोऽग्निर्भवन् उत्तमो रोचनानाम् |यदी भृगुभ्यः परि मातरिश्वा गुहा सन्तं हव्यवाहं समीदहे ||
ud astambhīt samidhā nākam ṛṣvo3 'gnir bhavann uttamo rocanānām | yadī bhṛgubhyaḥ pari mātariśvā guhā santaṁ havyavāhaṁ samīdhe ||
  • ud astambhīt: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the fire rise up." It signifies the importance of fire in rituals and its role as a purifying agent.
  • samidhā nākam: This translates to "with the offerings, may we reach the heavens." It emphasizes the connection between earthly actions and divine realms, suggesting that our offerings can elevate us spiritually.
  • ṛṣvo 'gnir bhavann: Here, "the fire of the sages" is invoked, indicating the wisdom and knowledge that fire represents. It symbolizes enlightenment and the pursuit of truth.
  • uttamo rocanānām: This means "the best among the shining ones." It refers to the highest form of light or knowledge, suggesting that the ultimate goal is to attain the highest wisdom.
  • yadī bhṛgubhyaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "if we are blessed by the seers." It highlights the importance of guidance from wise individuals in our spiritual journey.
  • pari mātariśvā: This translates to "surrounding the vast expanse." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • guhā santaṁ: This means "the hidden one." It refers to the deeper truths that lie beneath the surface of our understanding, suggesting that true knowledge requires introspection.
  • havyavāhaṁ samīdhe: This translates to "I offer to the one who carries the offerings." It emphasizes the act of giving and the importance of selfless service in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of spiritual elevation through the act of offering and the pursuit of wisdom. It teaches us that by engaging in selfless acts and seeking guidance from enlightened beings, we can rise above our mundane existence and connect with the divine. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, community service, and seeking knowledge from mentors. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that our actions have the power to transcend the physical realm and lead us towards enlightenment and a deeper understanding of our existence.
