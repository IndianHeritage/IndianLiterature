भरेषु हव्यो नमसोपसद्य गन्ता वाजेषु सानिता धनं-धनम् |विश्वा इद आर्यो अभिदिप्स्वो मृधो बृहस्पतिर्वि ववर्हा रथाम् इव ||
bhareṣu havyo namasopasadyo gantā vājeṣu sanitā dhanaṁ-dhanam | viśvā id aryo abhidipsvo3 mṛdho bṛhaspatir vi vavarhā rathām̐ iva ||
  • Mantra: "bhareṣu havyo namasopasadyo gantā vājeṣu sanitā dhanaṁ-dhanam | viśvā id aryo abhidipsvo3 mṛdho bṛhaspatir vi vavarhā rathām̐ iva ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhareṣu: In the context of abundance or fullness.
    • havyo: Worthy of offerings or sacrifices.
    • namasopasadyo: Salutations or reverence to those who are worthy.
    • gantā: The one who moves or travels.
    • vājeṣu: In the context of strength or power.
    • sanitā: To be established or made firm.
    • dhanam-dhanam: Wealth or riches, often repeated for emphasis.
    • viśvā: All or universal.
    • id: This or here.
    • aryo: Noble or honorable.
    • abhidipsvo: Desiring or aspiring.
    • mṛdho: From the earth or grounded.
    • bṛhaspatir: Referring to the deity Brihaspati, the lord of speech and wisdom.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'with' or 'together.'
    • vavarhā: To have or to possess.
    • rathām̐: Chariot, symbolizing movement and progress.
    • iva: Like or as.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of abundance and the reverence for those who are worthy of offerings.
    • The phrase "gantā vājeṣu sanitā" suggests the idea of moving forward with strength and establishing oneself firmly in the pursuit of wealth.
    • It highlights the universal aspiration for prosperity and the noble qualities associated with it.
    • The invocation of Brihaspati signifies the importance of wisdom and guidance in achieving one's goals.
    • The imagery of a chariot represents the journey of life, where one must navigate through challenges to attain success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of success and wealth through ethical practices and respect for all stakeholders.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, much like how students aspire to learn and grow in their academic and professional journeys.
    • In community settings, it encourages collective efforts towards prosperity, where everyone contributes to the common good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the values of abundance, respect, and wisdom in our pursuits.
    • It encourages individuals to aspire for wealth not just for personal gain but for the betterment of society.
    • By invoking the qualities of Brihaspati, it emphasizes the need for guidance and ethical considerations in all endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom, respect, and the collective progress of humanity.
