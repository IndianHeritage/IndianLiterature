आदित्यासो अदितिः मादयन्तां मित्रो आर्यमाः वरुणो रजिष्ठाः |अस्माकं सन्तु भुवनस्य गोपाः पिबन्तु सोमं अवसे नो अद्य ||
ādityāso aditir mādayantām mitro aryamā varuṇo rajiṣṭhāḥ | asmākaṁ santu bhuvanasya gopāḥ pibantu somam avase no adya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityāso: Referring to the Adityas, the solar deities.
    • aditir: A term that can mean 'the mother of the gods' or 'the infinite'.
    • mādayantām: May they bring joy or happiness.
    • mitro: Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony.
    • aryamā: A deity associated with the sun and the welfare of humanity.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • rajiṣṭhāḥ: Those who are established in their realms or positions.
    • asmākaṁ: Our or belonging to us.
    • santu: May they be or may they exist.
    • bhuvanasya: Of the world or the universe.
    • gopāḥ: Protectors or guardians.
    • pibantu: May they drink.
    • somam: Soma, a sacred drink associated with immortality.
    • avase: Today or in this moment.
    • no: For us.
    • adya: Today.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the solar deities, asking them to bring joy and happiness into our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of harmony and friendship, as represented by Mitra.
    • Varuna's presence signifies the need for cosmic order and the protection of the universe.
    • The mantra calls for the guardians of the world to partake in the sacred drink, Soma, which symbolizes spiritual nourishment and immortality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek joy and harmony in our relationships, much like how we celebrate festivals with friends and family.
    • It can inspire individuals to act as guardians of their communities, promoting peace and order, similar to community service initiatives.
    • The concept of 'Soma' can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek spiritual and intellectual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of joy, harmony, and cosmic order in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate positive relationships and to act as protectors of our environment and society.
    • In a world often filled with chaos, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a balanced and fulfilling life.
