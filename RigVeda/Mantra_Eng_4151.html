गॊमद अश्वावद रथवत सुवीरं चन्द्रवत राधो मरुतो ददात नः |प्रशस्तिं नः कृतु Rudriyāso भक्षीय वॊ 'वासो दैव्यस्य ||
gomad aśvāvad rathavat suvīraṁ candravad rādho maruto dadā naḥ | praśastiṁ naḥ kṛṇuta rudriyāso bhakṣīya vo 'vaso daivyasya ||
  • gomad: This word can be interpreted as "possessing cows" or "wealthy." In ancient Vedic society, cows were a symbol of wealth and prosperity. For example, a farmer with a large herd of cows would be considered prosperous and respected in the community.
  • aśvāvad: This term means "like horses." Horses were essential for transportation and warfare in Vedic times. A person who had horses was seen as powerful and influential. In modern life, this can be likened to having a good vehicle or means of transport that signifies status and capability.
  • rathavat: This translates to "like a chariot." Chariots were symbols of speed and strength in battle. In contemporary terms, this could represent having the means to achieve one's goals swiftly and effectively, akin to having a strong support system or resources.
  • suvīraṁ: Meaning "good hero" or "brave one," this word emphasizes the importance of courage and valor. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or lead their communities through difficult times, much like activists or leaders.
  • candravad: This means "like the moon," symbolizing beauty, calmness, and serenity. The moon is often associated with peace and tranquility. In everyday life, this can refer to someone who brings calmness to chaotic situations, like a mediator in conflicts.
  • rādho: This term can be interpreted as "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies abundance and success. In modern contexts, this could relate to achieving financial stability or personal fulfillment in one's career.
  • maruto: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities, this word symbolizes strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to the forces of nature or the challenges we face that require resilience and strength to overcome.
  • dadā naḥ: This phrase means "give us." It is a plea for blessings and support from the divine. In practical terms, this can be seen in how people seek guidance and help through prayer or meditation in times of need.
  • praśastiṁ: This means "praise" or "glory." It signifies the importance of recognition and appreciation in life. In real life, this can be related to how acknowledgment of one's efforts can motivate individuals to strive for excellence.
  • kṛṇuta: This translates to "make" or "create." It emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence. In a modern context, this can refer to the creative process in art, business, or personal development.
  • rudriyāso: This term refers to the "Rudras," who are associated with transformation and change. It signifies the inevitability of change in life. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals adapt to new circumstances or challenges.
  • bhakṣīya: Meaning "to be consumed" or "to be enjoyed," this word highlights the importance of sharing and enjoying the fruits of one's labor. In contemporary life, this can relate to community sharing, such as potlucks or charitable acts.
  • vo 'vaso: This phrase means "your abode" or "your home." It signifies the importance of community and belonging. In real life, this can be seen in how people create supportive environments for each other, like families or close-knit communities.
  • daivyasya: This means "of the divine" or "heavenly." It emphasizes the connection to the spiritual or higher powers. In modern life, this can relate to how people seek spiritual guidance or inspiration in their daily lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for blessings, strength, and prosperity from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of community, courage, and the recognition of one's efforts. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking support during challenging times, striving for personal and communal growth, and fostering a sense of belonging and appreciation among individuals. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of divine guidance in our pursuits.
