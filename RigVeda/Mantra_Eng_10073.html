सद्योजातो व्यामिमीत यज्ञं अग्निर देवानाम् अभवत् पुरोगाः |अस्य होतुः प्रदिश्य ऋतस्य वाचि स्वाहाकृतं हविः अदन्तु देवाः ||
sadyo jāto vy amimīta yajñam agnir devānām abhavat purogāḥ | asya hotuḥ pradiśy ṛtasya vāci svāhākṛtaṁ havir adantu devāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadyo: immediately, at once
    • jāto: born, created
    • vy: indeed, certainly
    • amimīta: unmeasured, limitless
    • yajñam: sacrifice, offering
    • agnir: fire, the divine fire
    • devānām: of the gods
    • abhavat: became, came into being
    • purogāḥ: the foremost, the leader
    • asya: of this, of it
    • hotuḥ: of the priest, the one who offers
    • pradiśy: directed, guided
    • ṛtasya: of truth, of order
    • vāci: in speech, in words
    • svāhākṛtaṁ: made offering, dedicated
    • havir: the offering, the oblation
    • adantu: may they give, may they bestow
    • devāḥ: the gods
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation of the divine fire (Agni) which is central to Vedic rituals.
    • It emphasizes the immediacy of creation, suggesting that the divine fire was born at once and is limitless in its essence.
    • Agni is portrayed as the leader among the gods, highlighting its importance in Vedic rituals where fire is a medium to connect with the divine.
    • The mantra also calls upon the gods to accept the offerings made through the priest, reinforcing the idea of reciprocity in rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked at the beginning of ceremonies, symbolizing purity and the presence of the divine.
    • In modern contexts, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in acts of charity and service, where individuals offer their time and resources for the greater good.
    • The idea of truth and order (ṛta) can be applied in everyday life, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and honesty in their dealings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the gods through offerings and rituals.
    • It teaches us about the significance of immediate action and the limitless potential of our intentions when we engage in acts of service and sacrifice.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to uphold truth and order in our lives, fostering a harmonious relationship with the world around us.
