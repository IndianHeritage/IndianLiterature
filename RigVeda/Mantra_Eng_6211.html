सुषोमे शर्यणावत्य आरजीके पस्त्यावति |ययुर निचक्रय नरः ||
suṣome śaryaṇāvaty ārjīke pastyāvati | yayur nicakrayā naraḥ ||
  • suṣome: This word can be broken down into "su" meaning good or auspicious, and "śome" which relates to the moon or a pleasant sound. Together, it signifies something that is pleasing or harmonious.
  • śaryaṇāvaty: This term refers to the act of flowing or moving smoothly, often associated with water or a river. It symbolizes the idea of continuity and sustenance.
  • ārjīke: This word can be interpreted as "in the right manner" or "with righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of ethical conduct and integrity in actions.
  • pastyāvati: This term suggests abundance or prosperity. It indicates a state of having plenty, whether in terms of resources, wealth, or well-being.
  • yayur: This word means "who" or "that which." It introduces the subject of the mantra, referring to individuals or beings.
  • nicakrayā: This term can be understood as "without deceit" or "honestly." It highlights the value of truthfulness and transparency in one's dealings.
  • naraḥ: This word means "man" or "human." It signifies the focus on humanity and the qualities that define a good person.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra speaks about a person who embodies goodness, flows smoothly like a river, acts righteously, and is abundant in resources. This person is characterized by honesty and integrity, representing the ideal human qualities.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A community leader who promotes harmony and cooperation among residents, ensuring that everyone feels included and valued.
    • A businessperson who operates with transparency, building trust with clients and employees, leading to a prosperous and sustainable enterprise.
    • An individual who practices ethical behavior in their daily life, making decisions that benefit not only themselves but also their family and society.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to embody virtues such as goodness, righteousness, abundance, and honesty. In real-life scenarios, these qualities are essential for building strong relationships, fostering community well-being, and achieving personal success. By striving to be like the person described in the mantra, one can contribute positively to society and create a harmonious environment for all.
