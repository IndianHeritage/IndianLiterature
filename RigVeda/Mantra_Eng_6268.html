यद अन्तरिक्षे पततः पुरुभुजा यद वेमे रोधसी अनु |यद वा स्वधाभिर अधितिष्ठथो रथम अत आ यातम अश्विनाः ||
yad antarikṣe patathaḥ purubhujā yad veme rodasī anu | yad vā svadhābhir adhitiṣṭhatho ratham ata ā yātam aśvinā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • antarikṣe: in the sky or space
    • patathaḥ: you who travel or move
    • purubhujā: with many enjoyments or pleasures
    • yad: whatever
    • veme: in this world
    • rodasī: in the two realms (heaven and earth)
    • anu: following or along with
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • svadhābhir: with offerings or sacrifices
    • adhitiṣṭhatho: you who are established
    • ratham: the chariot
    • ata: thus
    • ā: towards
    • yātam: may you go
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • It speaks of the journey through the sky and the pleasures of life, suggesting a connection between the divine and the earthly experiences.
    • The mention of offerings indicates the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine forces in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the journey of individuals pursuing their dreams and aspirations, akin to the Ashvins guiding them.
    • For instance, a student preparing for exams may seek blessings for success, paralleling the invocation of divine support in the mantra.
    • Similarly, in healthcare, the Ashvins symbolize the quest for healing and well-being, reminding us to appreciate medical advancements and practitioners.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance in our journeys, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
    • It encourages us to acknowledge the forces that support us and to express gratitude through our actions and offerings.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our endeavors, and that divine assistance is always available to those who seek it.
