अस्मभ्यं सु त्वं इन्द्र तां शिक्ष या दोहते प्रतिवरं जारित्रे |अच्छिद्रोध्नी पीपयद यथा नः सहस्रधारा पयसा मही गौः ||
asmabhyaṁ su tvam indra tāṁ śikṣa yā dohate prati varaṁ jaritre | acchidrodhnī pīpayad yathā naḥ sahasradhārā payasā mahī gauḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: For us
    • su: Good or auspicious
    • tvam: You
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tāṁ: That
    • śikṣa: Teach or instruct
    • yā: Which
    • dohate: Milks or extracts
    • prati: Towards
    • varaṁ: The best or excellent
    • jaritre: In the context of the earth or the world
    • acchidrodhnī: Unbroken or uninterrupted
    • pīpayad: May nourish or provide
    • yathā: Just as
    • naḥ: For us
    • sahasradhārā: With a thousand streams
    • payasā: With milk or nourishment
    • mahī: Great or vast
    • gauḥ: Cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for his blessings in providing nourishment and prosperity.
    • The phrase "asmabhyaṁ su tvam indra" indicates a plea for good fortune and auspiciousness for the devotees.
    • By asking Indra to "teach" or "instruct," the mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance in achieving success and abundance.
    • The imagery of "sahasradhārā payasā" refers to the idea of receiving nourishment in abundance, akin to a cow that provides milk, which is a vital source of sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather conditions, similar to how this mantra seeks Indra's blessings for nourishment.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance from mentors or elders, paralleling the request for instruction from Indra.
    • Communities may come together in rituals to invoke blessings for prosperity, reflecting the collective desire for abundance expressed in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and the need for nourishment in various aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals or communities seek prosperity, guidance, and sustenance.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human effort in achieving a fulfilling life.
