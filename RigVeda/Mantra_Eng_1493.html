तवायम् भाग आयुषु सोमो देवेषु हूयते |वह वायो नियुतो याह्य अस्मयुर जुषाणो याह्य अस्मयुः ||
tavāyam bhāga āyuṣu somo deveṣu hūyate | vaha vāyo niyuto yāhy asmayur juṣāṇo yāhy asmayuḥ ||
  • tavāyam bhāga āyuṣu somo deveṣu hūyate - This line can be translated to "This portion of life, O Soma, is offered to the gods." Here, 'tavāyam' refers to 'this', 'bhāga' means 'portion' or 'share', 'āyuṣu' relates to 'life' or 'longevity', and 'somo' refers to the divine drink or nectar associated with immortality and the gods.
  • vaha vāyo niyuto - This translates to "Carry, O Wind, the offerings." 'Vaha' means 'carry', 'vāyo' refers to 'wind', and 'niyuto' implies 'guided' or 'directed'. This line invokes the wind to carry the offerings to the divine.
  • yāhy asmayur juṣāṇo yāhy asmayuḥ - This can be interpreted as "May we be nourished by the offerings." 'Yāhy' means 'may we', 'asmayur' refers to 'our offerings', 'juṣāṇo' means 'enjoying' or 'receiving', and 'asmayuḥ' relates to 'our life' or 'our existence'. This line expresses a desire for the divine to accept the offerings and bless the devotees with sustenance and longevity.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the connection between humans and the divine. It highlights the belief that life is a shared experience, where offerings made to the gods can lead to blessings in terms of longevity and prosperity.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and giving back. Just as the ancient Vedic practitioners offered their best to the gods, individuals today can practice generosity and kindness in their daily lives. This could be through charitable acts, community service, or simply being supportive to those around them.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of honoring the divine through our actions. By recognizing the blessings we receive and sharing them with others, we can cultivate a life of abundance and fulfillment. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, community involvement, and spiritual practices.
