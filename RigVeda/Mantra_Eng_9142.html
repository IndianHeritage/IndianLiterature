सभाम् एति किटवः पृच्छमानो जेष्यामिति तन्ब्वा शूशुजानः |अक्षासो अस्य विं तिरन्ति कामम् प्रतिदीव्ने दधत आ कृतानि ||
sabhām eti kitavaḥ pṛcchamāno jeṣyāmīti tanvā3 śūśujānaḥ | akṣāso asya vi tiranti kāmam pratidīvne dadhata ā kṛtāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sabhām: assembly or gathering
    • eti: goes or reaches
    • kitavaḥ: the clever or cunning ones
    • pṛcchamāno: inquiring or asking
    • jeṣyāmīti: will conquer or prevail
    • tanvā: with body or form
    • śūśujānaḥ: those who are wise or knowledgeable
    • akṣāso: the unyielding or steadfast
    • asya: of this
    • vi tiranti: they cross or overcome
    • kāmam: desires or wishes
    • pratidīvne: in every day or daily
    • dadhata: they hold or maintain
    • ā kṛtāni: the actions or deeds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the dynamics of a gathering or assembly where the clever and wise individuals inquire and seek knowledge.
    • It emphasizes the importance of asking questions and being inquisitive in order to conquer challenges and achieve success.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are knowledgeable and steadfast can overcome desires and obstacles in their daily lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, employees who ask questions and seek clarity during meetings often lead their teams to success. Their inquisitiveness helps in identifying potential issues before they escalate.
    • In educational environments, students who actively participate in discussions and seek knowledge from their teachers tend to perform better academically, as they are more engaged and informed.
    • In personal relationships, open communication and the willingness to ask questions can lead to deeper understanding and stronger bonds between individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement of inquiry and the pursuit of knowledge as a means to overcome challenges.
    • It highlights the value of wisdom and steadfastness in achieving one's goals and desires.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in workplaces, educational institutions, and personal relationships, where asking questions and seeking knowledge can lead to growth and success.
