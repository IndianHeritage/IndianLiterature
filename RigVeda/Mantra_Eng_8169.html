वृषणं धीभिर अप्तुरं सोमं ऋतस्य धारया |मती विप्राः सम अस्वरन् ||
vṛṣaṇaṁ dhībhir apturaṁ somam ṛtasya dhārayā | matī viprāḥ sam asvaran ||
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." In a real-life context, it can refer to individuals or entities that exhibit strength, resilience, or leadership qualities.
  • dhībhir: This term means "with intellect" or "with wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of using one's intellect and wisdom in decision-making and actions.
  • apturaṁ: This word translates to "the one who is worthy" or "the one who is deserving." It signifies the importance of recognizing and valuing those who are deserving of respect and honor in society.
  • somam: This refers to "the moon" or "soma," which is often associated with nourishment, vitality, and the essence of life. In practical terms, it can symbolize the importance of nurturing relationships and the well-being of oneself and others.
  • ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of righteousness." It highlights the significance of living a life aligned with truth and moral values.
  • dhārayā: This translates to "holding" or "sustaining." It suggests the idea of maintaining and supporting the principles of truth and righteousness in our lives.
  • matī: This term means "intellect" or "thought." It emphasizes the role of thoughtful consideration in our actions and decisions.
  • viprāḥ: This word refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding society.
  • sam asvaran: This phrase can be interpreted as "they have spoken together" or "they have united in thought." It emphasizes the power of collective wisdom and collaboration.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and righteousness. It encourages individuals to use their intellect and recognize the deserving, while also emphasizing the importance of truth and moral values. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership roles, community building, and personal development. By fostering a culture of wisdom and collaboration, we can create a more harmonious and just society. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity in thought and action, guiding us towards a life of purpose and integrity.
