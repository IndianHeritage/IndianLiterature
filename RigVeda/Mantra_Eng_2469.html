स जिव्नते जठरेषु प्रजज्ञिवान वृषा चित्रेषु नानदन न सिंहः |वैश्वानरः पृथुपाजा अमर्त्यो वसु रत्ना दयमानो विदाशुषे ||
sa jinvate jaṭhareṣu prajajñivān vṛṣā citreṣu nānadan na siṁhaḥ | vaiśvānaraḥ pṛthupājā amartyo vasu ratnā dayamāno vi dāśuṣe ||
  • sa jinvate: This phrase translates to "he who lives" or "he who is alive." It signifies the essence of life and vitality.
  • jaṭhareṣu: This means "in the bellies" or "in the wombs." It refers to the nurturing aspect of life, where beings are sustained and developed.
  • prajajñivān: This term means "having been born" or "having come into existence." It emphasizes the process of birth and creation.
  • vṛṣā: This word can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful." It denotes strength and vigor, often associated with masculinity and vitality.
  • citreṣu: This translates to "in the colorful" or "in the diverse." It highlights the variety and richness of life experiences.
  • nānadan: This means "not a lion" or "not a powerful being." It suggests humility and the idea that true strength does not always come from dominance.
  • na siṁhaḥ: This phrase reinforces the previous one, emphasizing that one should not be like a lion, which symbolizes aggression and power.
  • vaiśvānaraḥ: This term refers to the universal fire or the divine energy that sustains life. It represents the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • pṛthupājā: This means "the one who is worshipped widely." It signifies the importance of reverence and respect in life.
  • amartyo: This translates to "immortal" or "beyond death." It suggests the eternal nature of the soul and the continuity of existence.
  • vasu ratnā: This phrase means "the treasures of wealth." It symbolizes the abundance and richness of life experiences and material possessions.
  • dayamāno: This means "being compassionate" or "showing kindness." It emphasizes the importance of empathy and care for others.
  • vi dāśuṣe: This translates to "to the one who gives." It highlights the value of generosity and sharing in human relationships.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of vitality, compassion, and interconnectedness. It teaches us that true strength lies not in dominance but in humility and kindness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering relationships based on empathy, respecting the diversity of experiences, and recognizing the value of generosity. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both individually and collectively.
