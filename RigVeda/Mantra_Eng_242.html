आ पूषाण् चित्रबरहिषम् आगृह्णे धरुणं दिवः |आजा नष्टं यथा पशुम् ||
ā pūṣañ citrabarhiṣam āghṛṇe dharuṇaṁ divaḥ | ājā naṣṭaṁ yathā paśum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā pūṣañ: O Pusha, the nourisher
    • citrabarhiṣam: with beautiful and varied offerings
    • āghṛṇe: I invoke or call upon
    • dharuṇaṁ: the strong or powerful
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • ājā: the offspring or progeny
    • naṣṭaṁ: lost or destroyed
    • yathā: as
    • paśum: the animal or creature
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Pusha, the divine nourisher, invoking his strength and power to restore what has been lost, particularly in the context of livestock or animals. It emphasizes the importance of nourishment and protection in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Agriculture: Farmers often pray for the health and safety of their livestock, recognizing that their well-being is crucial for sustenance and livelihood. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine assistance in ensuring the prosperity of their herds.
    • Parenting: Parents may relate to the idea of nourishing and protecting their children, invoking a sense of responsibility to restore any lost opportunities or guidance in their upbringing.
    • Community Support: In times of crisis, communities often come together to support one another, invoking the spirit of nourishment and restoration, much like the invocation of Pusha in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for nourishment, protection, and restoration. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the divine support available to us in times of need. In real-life scenarios, whether in agriculture, parenting, or community support, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to seek strength and assistance in nurturing and safeguarding what is valuable to them.
