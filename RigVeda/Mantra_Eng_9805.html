तं यज्ञं बरहिषि प्रौक्षन् पुरुषं जातम् अग्रतः |तेन देवाः अयजन्त साध्याः ऋषयश्च ये ||
taṁ yajñam barhiṣi praukṣan puruṣaṁ jātam agrataḥ | tena devā ayajanta sādhyā ṛṣayaś ca ye ||
  • taṁ yajñam: This phrase refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering." In the context of the Rigveda, yajña is a central theme, symbolizing the act of giving and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • barhiṣi: This word signifies the "sacred grass" used in Vedic rituals. It represents purity and is often associated with the offerings made to the deities.
  • prauṣan: This term means "to pour forth" or "to offer." It indicates the act of presenting offerings to the divine, emphasizing the importance of intention and devotion in rituals.
  • puruṣaṁ: This word translates to "the cosmic being" or "the universal man." In Vedic philosophy, Puruṣa represents the essence of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • jātam agrataḥ: This phrase means "born first" or "originating at the forefront." It signifies the primordial nature of the Puruṣa, highlighting the idea that all creation stems from this cosmic being.
  • tena: This word means "by him" or "through him," indicating the agency of the Puruṣa in the act of creation and the performance of yajña.
  • devā: This term refers to "the gods." In the Vedic context, the gods are the forces of nature and the divine beings that govern the universe.
  • ayajanta: This word means "performed the sacrifice." It emphasizes the active participation of the gods in the ritual, showcasing the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
  • sādhyā: This term refers to "the accomplished" or "the achieved." It signifies the results of the yajña, which are blessings and prosperity bestowed upon the practitioners.
  • ṛṣayaś ca ye: This phrase means "and the sages." It acknowledges the role of the wise and enlightened beings who guide humanity in spiritual practices and rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the significance of sacrifice, the interconnectedness of all beings, and the active participation of both humans and divine forces in the process of creation and sustenance. In real life, this mantra can be applied to the concept of selfless service and the importance of community rituals that foster unity and harmony. It reminds us that our actions, when performed with intention and devotion, can lead to collective well-being and spiritual growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to engage in acts of giving and to recognize their role in the larger cosmic order, promoting a sense of responsibility towards oneself, others, and the environment.
