न मृत्युर्वासीत अमृतं न तर्हि न रात्रीआ अह्ना आसीत प्रकेतः |आनीद अवातं स्वधया तद एकं तस्माद धान्यन न परः किं चनास ||
na mṛtyur āsīd amṛtaṁ na tarhi na rātryā ahna āsīt praketaḥ | ānīd avātaṁ svadhayā tad ekaṁ tasmād dhānyan na paraḥ kiṁ canāsa ||
  • na mṛtyur āsīd - This phrase translates to "there was no death." It suggests a state of existence where mortality did not exist, indicating a primordial condition of being.
  • amṛtaṁ - Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," this word emphasizes the concept of everlasting life, contrasting with the idea of death.
  • na tarhi - This means "not then," indicating a time when certain conditions or states were not present.
  • na rātryā ahna āsīt - Translated as "there was neither night nor day," this phrase points to a time before the duality of time was established, suggesting a state of timelessness.
  • praketaḥ - This word means "the one who is the creator" or "the source." It refers to the ultimate reality or the divine principle that governs existence.
  • ānīd avātaṁ - This translates to "the one who breathed," indicating the act of creation or the initiation of life through breath.
  • svadhayā - Meaning "by its own will," this term suggests that the creation and existence are self-driven and not dependent on external forces.
  • tad ekaṁ - This means "that one," referring to the singularity of existence or the ultimate reality that is undivided.
  • tasmād dhānyan - Translated as "from that, the grain," this phrase signifies the emergence of sustenance and life from the ultimate reality.
  • na paraḥ kiṁ canāsa - This means "there is nothing else," emphasizing the idea that all that exists comes from this singular source and that nothing exists outside of it.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about the nature of existence, creation, and the relationship between life and death. It signifies a time before dualities such as night and day, life and death, highlighting the unity of existence. In real life, this can be applied to understanding the interconnectedness of all beings and the cyclical nature of life. It encourages individuals to reflect on the essence of life beyond material existence and to recognize the divine source from which all sustenance arises. This mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of the soul and the importance of seeking deeper truths in our lives, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in a world often divided by superficial differences.
