आ दस्युघ्ना मनसा याह्य अस्तम भुवत ते कुत्सः सख्ये निकामः |स्वे योनौ नि षदतं सरूपा विबां चिकीषद ऋतचिद ध नारी ||
ā dasyughnā manasā yāhy astam bhuvat te kutsaḥ sakhye nikāmaḥ | sve yonau ni ṣadataṁ sarūpā vi vāṁ cikitsad ṛtacid dha nārī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • dasyughnā: the one who is a slayer of enemies
    • manasā: with the mind
    • yāhy: who goes
    • astam: to the west
    • bhuvat: of existence
    • te: your
    • kutsaḥ: the one who is criticized
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • nikāmaḥ: without desire
    • sve: in one's own
    • yonau: womb
    • ni: in
    • ṣadataṁ: may sit
    • sarūpā: with a good form
    • vi: indeed
    • vāṁ: your
    • cikitsad: may heal
    • ṛtacid: with truth
    • dha: indeed
    • nārī: woman
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of the mind and the importance of friendship.
    • It emphasizes the idea of overcoming adversities (dasyughnā) through mental strength (manasā).
    • The reference to "going to the west" (astam) can symbolize a journey towards enlightenment or understanding.
    • The term "kutsaḥ" suggests that criticism can be faced with resilience, especially in friendships (sakhye).
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of one's own identity (sve yonau) and the nurturing aspect of healing (cikitsad).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when facing criticism, one can use the strength of their mind to remain focused and resilient, fostering a supportive environment among colleagues.
    • In personal relationships, overcoming challenges together can strengthen bonds, as friends support each other without selfish desires.
    • In health and wellness, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek healing through self-awareness and truth, promoting mental and emotional well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of mental strength, resilience in the face of criticism, and the nurturing aspect of friendships.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and health journeys.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a supportive and healing atmosphere in their lives.
