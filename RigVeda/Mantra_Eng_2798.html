आ तु भर माकिर एतत् परिष्ठाद् विद्मा हि त्वा वसुपतिं वसूनाम् |इन्द्र यत् ते माहिनं दात्रम् अस्त्य अस्मभ्यं तद् धर्यश्व प्र यन्धि ||
ā tū bhara mākir etat pari ṣṭhād vidmā hi tvā vasupatiṁ vasūnām | indra yat te māhinaṁ datram asty asmabhyaṁ tad dharyaśva pra yandhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā tū bhara: "O you, bring forth" - a call to a divine entity to provide or bestow something.
    • mākir: "this" - referring to the subject or object of the invocation.
    • etat: "this" - emphasizing the importance of the request.
    • pari ṣṭhād: "from all sides" - indicating a comprehensive or holistic approach.
    • vidmā: "we know" - expressing awareness or understanding.
    • hi: "indeed" - reinforcing the statement.
    • tvā: "you" - addressing the divine or the entity being invoked.
    • vasupatiṁ: "lord of wealth" - referring to a deity associated with prosperity.
    • vasūnām: "of all riches" - encompassing all forms of wealth and abundance.
    • indra: "Indra" - the king of gods, often associated with strength and power.
    • yat: "that which" - introducing a clause that specifies the subject.
    • te: "your" - indicating possession or association with the divine.
    • māhinaṁ: "greatness" - referring to the vastness or significance of the divine.
    • datram: "gift" - something bestowed or given.
    • asty: "exists" - affirming the presence of something.
    • asmabhyaṁ: "to us" - indicating the recipients of the divine gift.
    • tad: "that" - referring back to the gift or blessing.
    • dharyaśva: "hold firmly" - a call to maintain or uphold the blessing.
    • pra yandhi: "may you lead" - a request for guidance or direction.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer invoking a divine presence, asking for the bestowal of wealth and prosperity from all sides. It acknowledges the greatness of the divine and requests guidance in receiving and holding onto these blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited by entrepreneurs seeking success and abundance in their ventures, asking for divine guidance in their endeavors.
    • During a wedding ceremony, couples may invoke this mantra to bless their union with prosperity and happiness, ensuring a supportive and abundant life together.
    • In personal finance, individuals may use this mantra as a form of meditation or affirmation, focusing on attracting wealth and stability into their lives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to invoking divine blessings for wealth and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the source of abundance and the need for guidance in maintaining it. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as business, personal finance, and relationships, serving as a reminder to seek divine support and to be grateful for the blessings received.
