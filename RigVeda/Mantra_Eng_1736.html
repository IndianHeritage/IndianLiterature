यत्रा सुपर्णा अमृतस्य भागम् अनिमेषं विदथाभिस्वरन्ति |इनो विश्वस्य भुवनस्य गोपाः स मा धीः पाकम् अत्रा विवेश ||
yatrā suparṇā amṛtasya bhāgam animeṣaṁ vidathābhisvaranti | ino viśvasya bhuvanasya gopāḥ sa mā dhīraḥ pākam atrā viveśa ||
  • yatrā suparṇā: This phrase translates to "where the beautiful birds (or divine beings)". The term "suparṇā" often refers to the mythical birds, which can symbolize higher knowledge or spiritual enlightenment.
  • amṛtasya bhāgam: This means "the portion of immortality". In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of eternal truths or wisdom that transcends the physical existence.
  • animeṣaṁ vidathābhisvaranti: This translates to "that which speaks without blinking". This can be understood as the unchanging, eternal truths that are always present, regardless of the chaos of life.
  • ino viśvasya bhuvanasya gopāḥ: This means "the protectors of the universe". In everyday life, this can refer to those who safeguard knowledge, culture, and the environment, akin to guardians of wisdom and nature.
  • sa mā dhīraḥ pākam atrā viveśa: This translates to "may the wise enter into this purity". This suggests that wisdom leads one to a state of purity and understanding, which is essential for personal growth and enlightenment.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher knowledge and understanding the eternal truths of life. It encourages individuals to be like the "gopāḥ" or guardians, who protect and nurture wisdom and purity in their lives.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as education, environmental conservation, and personal development. For instance, educators can embody the role of "gopāḥ" by nurturing students' curiosity and guiding them towards wisdom. Similarly, individuals can strive for purity in their thoughts and actions, leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened life.
