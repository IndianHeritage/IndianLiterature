मन्त्र: न हि मन्युः पुरुषेया ईशे हि वः प्रियजात |त्वमिदसि क्षपावान ||
nahi manyuḥ pauruṣeya īśe hi vaḥ priyajāta | tvam id asi kṣapāvān ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, anger is not a human trait; it is you who are the one who brings forth the light."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Nahi: Indeed, not
    • Manyuḥ: Anger, wrath
    • Pauruṣeya: Human, pertaining to man
    • Īśe: Lord, master
    • Hi: Indeed, certainly
    • Vaḥ: You, your
    • Priyajāta: One who brings forth love, light
    • Tvam: You
    • Id: This, it
    • Asi: Are
    • Kṣapāvān: One who brings forth light, illuminator
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea that anger is not a natural or desirable quality for humans. Instead, it suggests that the divine or higher consciousness (represented by "you") is the source of light and positivity.
    • In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals are faced with conflict or anger. For example, in a workplace setting, rather than reacting with anger during a disagreement, one can choose to respond with understanding and clarity, thereby bringing light to the situation.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to transcend their base emotions and to embody qualities of compassion and wisdom, which are often associated with enlightenment.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate inner peace and to avoid letting anger dictate our actions. It highlights the importance of self-awareness and emotional regulation.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as conflict resolution, personal relationships, and even in leadership roles where maintaining composure is crucial for effective decision-making.
    • By embodying the qualities of light and understanding, individuals can foster a more harmonious environment, whether at home, in the workplace, or in the community.
