ā yas tatantha rodasī vi bhāsā śravobhiś ca śravasya1s tarutraḥ | bṛhadbhir vājaiḥ sthavirebhir asme revadbhir agne vitaraṁ vi bhāhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • yas: who
    • tatantha: has spread out
    • rodasī: the heavens
    • vi: with
    • bhāsā: light
    • śravobhiś: with fame
    • ca: and
    • śravasya: of glory
    • tarutraḥ: the trees
    • bṛhadbhir: with great
    • vājaiḥ: with strength
    • sthavirebhir: with the aged
    • asme: for us
    • revadbhir: with wealth
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vitaraṁ: distribute
    • vi: with
    • bhāhi: may you shine
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "who has spread out the heavens with light and fame" suggests the vastness and brilliance of the universe, which can be likened to the way the sun illuminates the sky, providing life and energy.
    • The mention of "trees" symbolizes growth and stability, representing the importance of nature in our lives.
    • The call for Agni to "distribute wealth" reflects a desire for prosperity and abundance, akin to how a farmer prays for a bountiful harvest.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the importance of environmental conservation. Just as the trees are vital for sustaining life, we must protect our natural resources.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek guidance and blessings for success in their endeavors, much like how this mantra seeks the blessings of Agni for wealth and prosperity.
    • During festivals, people light lamps and candles, symbolizing the invocation of Agni, representing hope, purity, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human aspirations.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine presence in everyday life and the importance of seeking blessings for prosperity and growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to honor nature, seek guidance in our pursuits, and appreciate the light and wealth that life offers.
