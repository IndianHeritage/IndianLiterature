एवाग्निर्मर्तैः सह सूरिभिरवसुः ष्टवे सहसः सूनरो नृभिः |मित्रासो न ये सुदिताः ऋतायवो द्यावो न द्युम्नैरभि सन्ति मानवशान ||
evāgnir martaiḥ saha sūribhir vasuḥ ṣṭave sahasaḥ sūnaro nṛbhiḥ | mitrāso na ye sudhitā ṛtāyavo dyāvo na dyumnair abhi santi mānuṣān ||
  • evāgnir: This term refers to the divine fire or Agni, which is considered a vital force in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • martaiḥ: This word means "with mortals" or "among humans." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • saha: This translates to "together" or "with," indicating unity and collaboration.
  • sūribhir: This term means "with the brave" or "with heroes," highlighting the importance of courage and valor in human endeavors.
  • vasuḥ: This word signifies "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting that the mantra invokes abundance and well-being.
  • ṣṭave: This means "to praise" or "to invoke," indicating a call for reverence and acknowledgment of the divine forces.
  • sahasaḥ: This translates to "with strength" or "with power," emphasizing the need for resilience and determination.
  • sūnaro: This term means "noble" or "virtuous," suggesting that the qualities of nobility and righteousness are essential in life.
  • nṛbhiḥ: This means "by men" or "by humans," reinforcing the idea that human actions and intentions are crucial in the pursuit of prosperity.
  • mitrāso: This word refers to "friends" or "allies," highlighting the importance of relationships and community support.
  • na ye: This translates to "those who do not," indicating a contrast or a warning against certain behaviors.
  • sudhitā: This means "wise" or "intelligent," suggesting that wisdom is a key attribute for achieving success.
  • ṛtāyavo: This term refers to "those who follow the truth" or "righteous," emphasizing the significance of truthfulness in life.
  • dyāvo: This means "heavens" or "sky," representing the divine and the higher realms of existence.
  • na dyumnair: This translates to "not by glory" or "not by fame," indicating that true success is not solely based on external recognition.
  • abhi santi: This means "they are present" or "they exist," suggesting the omnipresence of divine forces.
  • mānuṣān: This word means "of humans," reinforcing the connection between the divine and human experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, courage, wisdom, and righteousness in human life. It calls for collaboration among individuals, highlighting that true prosperity and success come from collective efforts and virtuous actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal relationships. By embodying the qualities of bravery, wisdom, and truthfulness, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous environment, both personally and collectively. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message that encourages individuals to strive for excellence while fostering connections with others, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
