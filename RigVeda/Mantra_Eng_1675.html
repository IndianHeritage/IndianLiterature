आपो भूयिष्ठा इत्य एको अब्रवीद अग्निर भूयिष्ठ इत्य अन्यो अब्रवीत् |वधार्यन्तीं बहुभ्यः प्रैको अब्रवीद ऋता वदन्तश् चमसाम् अपिंशत ||
āpo bhūyiṣṭhā ity eko abravīd agnir bhūyiṣṭha ity anyo abravīt | vadharyantīm bahubhyaḥ praiko abravīd ṛtā vadantaś camasām̐ apiṁśata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: waters
    • bhūyiṣṭhā: the best or most excellent
    • ity: thus
    • eko: one
    • abravīd: said
    • agnir: fire
    • bhūyiṣṭha: the best or most excellent
    • ity: thus
    • anyo: another
    • abravīt: said
    • vadharyantīm: sustaining or supporting
    • bahubhyaḥ: from many
    • praiko: one
    • abravīd: said
    • ṛtā: truth or order
    • vadantaś: speaking
    • camasām̐: of the vessels or containers
    • apiṁśata: of the essence or substance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of water and fire, highlighting their importance in sustaining life.
    • The phrase "āpo bhūyiṣṭhā" emphasizes that water is the most excellent element, which can be related to its vital role in agriculture, drinking, and sanitation.
    • Similarly, "agnir bhūyiṣṭha" indicates that fire is also of utmost importance, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • The mantra suggests a dialogue between these two elements, where water and fire are seen as essential forces that support life and maintain balance in nature.
    • The mention of "ṛtā" signifies the underlying order and truth in the universe, which can be observed in the natural cycles of life, such as the water cycle and the energy flow in ecosystems.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the relationship between water and fire can be seen in practices like controlled burning, which helps in managing crop residues and enhancing soil fertility.
    • In daily life, the importance of water is evident in cooking, cleaning, and hydration, while fire is crucial for cooking, heating, and energy production.
    • In spiritual practices, both elements are often used in rituals, where water symbolizes purity and fire represents transformation and offering.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water and fire as fundamental elements that sustain life and maintain balance in the universe.
    • It teaches us to respect and understand the natural elements, recognizing their roles in our lives and the environment.
    • In a broader context, it encourages a harmonious relationship with nature, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all elements in sustaining life.
