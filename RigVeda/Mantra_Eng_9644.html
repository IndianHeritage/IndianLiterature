विष्कर्मण हविषा वावृद्धानः स्वयम् यजस्व पृथिवीं उता द्यां |मुह्यन्त्व अन्ये अभितो जनासा इहास्माकं मगवाऽ सूरीर अस्तु ||
viśvakarman haviṣā vāvṛdhānaḥ svayaṁ yajasva pṛthivīm uta dyām | muhyantv anye abhito janāsa ihāsmākam maghavā sūrir astu ||
  • viśvakarman: This term refers to the universal creator or the architect of the universe. It signifies the divine force that shapes and constructs all that exists.
  • haviṣā: This word means offerings or sacrifices. In the context of the mantra, it implies the act of giving or dedicating something valuable to the divine.
  • vāvṛdhānaḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'growing' or 'expanding'. It suggests the idea of development and prosperity that comes from divine blessings.
  • svayaṁ: This means 'by oneself' or 'on one's own'. It emphasizes self-reliance and personal effort in achieving goals.
  • yajasva: This is a call to worship or perform rituals. It encourages individuals to engage in spiritual practices that connect them with the divine.
  • pṛthivīm: This word refers to the Earth, symbolizing the physical realm and our material existence.
  • uta dyām: This phrase translates to 'and the sky', representing the spiritual or celestial realm. It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • muhyantv: This term means 'may they be confused' or 'may they be bewildered'. It suggests a desire for those who oppose or challenge the divine order to be misled.
  • anye: This means 'others', referring to those who may not align with the divine will or purpose.
  • abhito: This word means 'around' or 'in the vicinity', indicating the presence of others who may be in conflict with the divine intentions.
  • janāsa: This refers to 'people' or 'beings', emphasizing the collective nature of humanity.
  • ihāsmākam: This means 'here among us', indicating a sense of community and shared existence.
  • maghavā: This term refers to the powerful or mighty one, often associated with divine strength and blessings.
  • sūrir: This means 'wise' or 'intelligent', suggesting the importance of wisdom in navigating life.
  • astu: This word means 'let it be' or 'may it be', expressing a wish or intention for the outcome.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, self-reliance, and the relationship between the earthly and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of personal effort in spiritual practices while invoking the blessings of the universal creator. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, personal development, and community gatherings, where individuals seek to connect with the divine and foster a sense of unity. The call for wisdom and strength serves as a reminder to navigate challenges with intelligence and resilience, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
