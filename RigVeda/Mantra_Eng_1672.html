इन्द्रो हरिः युयुजे अश्विनां रथम् बृहस्पतिः विश्वरूपाम् उपाजात |ऋभुर् विभ्वा वाजो देवाम् अगच्छत स्वपसो यज्ञियम् भागम् ऐतन ||
indro harī yuyuje aśvinā ratham bṛhaspatir viśvarūpām upājata | ṛbhur vibhvā vājo devām̐ agacchata svapaso yajñiyam bhāgam aitana ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Harī: Often associated with the divine, representing the aspect of the divine that brings prosperity and well-being.
  • Yuyuje: Means to unite or join together, indicating collaboration and harmony.
  • Aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, twin gods associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, symbolizing duality and balance.
  • Ratham: Means chariot, which symbolizes the journey of life and the means to achieve goals.
  • Bṛhaspatir: Refers to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods, representing wisdom and guidance.
  • Viśvarūpām: Means universal form, indicating the all-encompassing nature of the divine.
  • Upājata: Means to approach or attain, suggesting the pursuit of higher knowledge or goals.
  • Ṛbhur: Refers to the wise or learned, emphasizing the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Vibhvā: Means abundant or prosperous, indicating the blessings of wealth and success.
  • Vājo: Refers to strength or energy, symbolizing the vigor needed to achieve one's goals.
  • Devām̐: Means divine or godly, emphasizing the connection to the spiritual realm.
  • Agacchata: Means to go or move towards, indicating action and progress.
  • Svapaso: Refers to the dream or vision, symbolizing aspirations and goals.
  • Yajñiyam: Means sacrificial or offering, indicating the importance of giving and sharing.
  • Bhāgam: Refers to a share or portion, emphasizing the distribution of blessings.
  • Aitana: Means to attain or achieve, suggesting the fulfillment of desires and goals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and divine guidance in achieving one's goals. It highlights the significance of collaboration (as seen in teamwork in workplaces), the pursuit of knowledge (as in education), and the need for spiritual connection (as in meditation or prayer). In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as group projects, community service, or personal development, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, guided by wisdom and a sense of purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder that success is not just an individual endeavor but a collective journey enriched by divine blessings and shared efforts.
