विषां राजानम अद्भुतम अध्यक्षं धर्मणाम इमम् |अग्निम ईळे स उ श्रवत ||
viśāṁ rājānam adbhutam adhyakṣaṁ dharmaṇām imam | agnim īḻe sa u śravat ||
  • viśāṁ: This word translates to "of the people" or "of the community." It signifies the collective aspect of society, emphasizing the importance of unity and cooperation among individuals.
  • rājānam: This means "king" or "ruler." In a broader sense, it can refer to any leader or authority figure who guides and protects the community.
  • adbhutam: This translates to "wonderful" or "extraordinary." It highlights the qualities of the ruler that inspire awe and respect among the people.
  • adhyakṣaṁ: This means "overseer" or "supervisor." It indicates the role of the ruler in ensuring justice and order within the community.
  • dharmaṇām: This word refers to "righteousness" or "moral law." It underscores the importance of ethical governance and the ruler's duty to uphold dharma.
  • imam: This translates to "this" or "here." It points to the specific context or situation being addressed in the mantra.
  • agnim: This means "fire," which is often symbolic of purity, transformation, and divine presence in Vedic literature.
  • īḻe: This translates to "I invoke" or "I praise." It indicates an act of reverence and calling upon the divine.
  • sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to the divine presence or the fire being invoked.
  • u: This translates to "and" or "also," connecting the ideas in the mantra.
  • śravat: This means "to flow" or "to pour forth," often used in the context of blessings or divine grace.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to a wonderful ruler who oversees the community with righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of ethical leadership and the role of the ruler in maintaining order and justice. The invocation of fire symbolizes the divine presence and the transformative power of righteousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Leadership: It serves as a reminder for leaders to embody qualities of wisdom, justice, and compassion, inspiring their followers to work together for the common good.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on unity and cooperation among people highlights the importance of working together to achieve shared goals and values.
  • Ethical Governance: It underscores the need for leaders to uphold moral principles and act in the best interest of their communities, fostering trust and respect.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the vital role of ethical leadership in society. It calls upon leaders to be extraordinary in their governance, ensuring justice and righteousness while fostering unity among the people. By invoking the divine presence, it reminds us of the transformative power of ethical actions and the importance of working together for a harmonious community.
