इन्द्रावरुण नू नु वां सिषासन्तीषु धिष्वा |अस्मभ्यं शर्म यच्छतम् ||
indrāvaruṇa nū nu vāṁ siṣāsantīṣu dhīṣv ā | asmabhyaṁ śarma yacchatam ||
  • Indrāvaruṇa: This term refers to the combined deities Indra and Varuna. Indra is the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power, while Varuna is associated with the cosmic order and the waters. Together, they represent the balance of nature and the forces that govern the universe.
  • Nū nu: This phrase can be interpreted as an invocation or a call to attention. It emphasizes the urgency and importance of the following request or prayer.
  • Vāṁ siṣāsantīṣu: This part of the mantra can be translated to mean "may you grant us your protection." It is a plea for safety and security, highlighting the need for divine intervention in our lives.
  • Dhīṣv ā: This term refers to wisdom or intelligence. It suggests that the protection sought is not just physical but also encompasses mental and spiritual guidance.
  • Asmabhyaṁ: This word means "to us" or "for us," indicating that the prayer is personal and directed towards the deities on behalf of the worshippers.
  • Śarma yacchatam: This phrase translates to "may you grant us peace or happiness." It is a request for well-being and tranquility in life, emphasizing the importance of inner peace and contentment.

  • Real-Life Example: In times of uncertainty, such as during a natural disaster or personal crisis, individuals often seek protection and guidance. This mantra can be recited to invoke a sense of safety and to remind oneself of the balance of nature and the support of higher powers.
  • Significance: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and wisdom. It serves as a reminder that in our daily lives, we often face challenges that require not just physical strength but also mental clarity and spiritual support.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during meditation, prayer, or even in moments of reflection. It encourages individuals to seek harmony within themselves and with the world around them, fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "indrāvaruṇa nū nu vāṁ siṣāsantīṣu dhīṣv ā | asmabhyaṁ śarma yacchatam ||" is a profound invocation for protection, wisdom, and peace. Its significance lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine forces of nature, reminding us of the importance of balance, safety, and inner tranquility in our lives.
