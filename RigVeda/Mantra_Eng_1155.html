अमी ये सप्त रश्मयस् तत्रा मे नाभिर आतताः |तृतस् तद वेदाप्त्यः स जामित्वाय रेब्हति वित्तम् मे अस्य रोधसी ||
amī ye sapta raśmayas tatrā me nābhir ātatā | tritas tad vedāptyaḥ sa jāmitvāya rebhati vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • amī ye sapta raśmayas: This phrase translates to "these seven rays." In the context of the Rigveda, the "seven rays" can symbolize various aspects of life, such as the seven colors of the rainbow or the seven days of the week. In real life, this can be related to the diversity and richness of experiences we encounter daily.
  • tatrā me nābhir ātatā: This means "there my navel is extended." The navel is often seen as a source of life and nourishment, connecting us to our origins. In a practical sense, this can be related to our roots, family, and the foundational aspects of our identity that shape who we are.
  • tritas tad vedāptyaḥ: This translates to "the third one, who knows the Vedas." The Vedas are ancient scriptures that hold immense knowledge and wisdom. This part emphasizes the importance of knowledge and learning in our lives. For example, in modern society, education and continuous learning are crucial for personal and professional growth.
  • sa jāmitvāya rebhati: This means "he who has attained it flourishes." This highlights the idea that achieving knowledge and understanding leads to prosperity and success. In real life, individuals who invest in their education and skills often find better opportunities and fulfillment in their careers.
  • vittam me asya rodasī: This translates to "wealth is mine in this world." This phrase signifies the abundance and prosperity that can be attained through wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, it can be seen in how individuals who apply their knowledge effectively can create wealth and improve their quality of life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, roots, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that by understanding our origins and investing in our education, we can flourish and attain prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, career development, and personal growth, reminding us that wisdom is a key to unlocking the wealth of opportunities in our lives.
