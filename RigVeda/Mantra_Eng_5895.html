उभे यत ते महिना शुभ्रे अन्धसी अधिक्षियन्ति पूर्वः | सा नो बोध्य अवित्री मरुत्सखा चोद राधो मगोनाम ||
ubhe yat te mahinā śubhre andhasī adhikṣiyanti pūravaḥ | sā no bodhy avitrī marutsakhā coda rādho maghonām ||
  • Translation: "Both the bright and the dark forces that govern the universe, may they inspire us and guide us towards prosperity and wisdom."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ubhe: Both
    • yat: That which
    • te: Your
    • mahinā: Greatness or power
    • śubhre: Bright or auspicious
    • andhasī: Dark or hidden
    • adhikṣiyanti: Govern or control
    • pūravaḥ: The ancient or primordial
    • sā: That
    • no: Us
    • bodhy: Inspire or enlighten
    • avitrī: Without fear or obstacles
    • marutsakhā: Friends of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • coda: Guide or direct
    • rādho: Towards prosperity
    • maghonām: Of the wealthy or prosperous
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra acknowledges the duality of existence, represented by the bright and dark forces. In life, we often encounter both positive and negative experiences.
    • For example, in a career, one may face challenges (dark forces) but also opportunities for growth (bright forces). Recognizing both can lead to better decision-making.
    • The invocation of guidance from these forces suggests a need for balance and wisdom in navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be a reminder to embrace both successes and failures as part of the journey.
    • In relationships, understanding that conflicts (dark forces) can lead to deeper connections (bright forces) can foster resilience and empathy.
    • In community building, recognizing the diverse perspectives (both positive and negative) can lead to more inclusive and effective solutions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and harmonizing the dual aspects of life. By seeking guidance from both the bright and dark forces, individuals can cultivate wisdom and prosperity.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that challenges can lead to growth, and that wisdom often comes from understanding the interplay of different forces in our lives.
    • In essence, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where one learns from every experience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and prosperous existence.
