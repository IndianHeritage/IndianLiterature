ते हि यज्ञेषु यज्ञियास ऊमाः सदस्थं विश्वे अभि सन्ति देवाः | तां अध्वर उषतो यक्ष्य अग्ने श्रुष्टी भागं नासत्याः पुरंधिम् ||
te hi yajñeṣu yajñiyāsa ūmāḥ sadhasthaṁ viśve abhi santi devāḥ | tām̐ adhvara uśato yakṣy agne śruṣṭī bhagaṁ nāsatyā puraṁdhim ||
  • te hi yajñeṣu yajñiyāsa ūmāḥ: This phrase can be translated to "They (the deities) are indeed present in the sacrifices." Here, "yajñeṣu" refers to the rituals or sacrifices performed, which are central to Vedic traditions. In real life, this can be likened to community gatherings or events where people come together for a common purpose, such as weddings or festivals, emphasizing the importance of collective participation.
  • sadhasthaṁ viśve abhi santi devāḥ: This means "All the gods are present in the assembly." The term "sadhasthaṁ" refers to being present in a gathering or assembly. This can be compared to modern-day meetings or conferences where diverse perspectives come together to create a harmonious outcome, highlighting the significance of unity and collaboration.
  • tām̐ adhvara uśato yakṣy agne: This translates to "That which is the sacred act, may Agni (the fire god) protect us." Agni symbolizes transformation and purification, akin to how fire is used in various cultures for rituals and ceremonies. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance and protection through their actions, whether in personal endeavors or community service.
  • śruṣṭī bhagaṁ nāsatyā puraṁdhim: This phrase can be interpreted as "The creation and prosperity, may the Nāsatyas (the twin deities) grant us wisdom." The Nāsatyas represent duality and balance, much like the balance we seek in our lives between work and leisure, or between personal and communal responsibilities. This reflects the importance of seeking wisdom and balance in our daily decisions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of community, the presence of divine forces in our collective actions, and the pursuit of wisdom and balance in life. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder of the importance of unity in diversity, the sacredness of our actions, and the need for guidance in our endeavors. By recognizing the divine in our communal activities and seeking balance, we can foster a more harmonious and prosperous society.
