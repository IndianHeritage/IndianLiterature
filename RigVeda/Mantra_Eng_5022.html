विजयुषा रथ्या यातम अद्रिं श्रुतं हवं वृषणाऽवध्रिमत्याः |दशस्यन्ता शयवे पिप्यथुर गाम इति च्यवानाऽसुमतिं भुरण्यु ||
vi jayuṣā rathyā yātam adriṁ śrutaṁ havaṁ vṛṣaṇā vadhrimatyāḥ | daśasyantā śayave pipyathur gām iti cyavānā sumatim bhuraṇyū ||
  • vi jayuṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of energy and vitality in our actions.
  • rathyā: This word means "on the chariot" or "in the vehicle." It symbolizes the journey of life and the means by which we navigate through it.
  • yātam: This translates to "we go" or "we move." It signifies progress and the act of moving forward in life.
  • adriṁ: Meaning "the mountain," it represents stability, strength, and the challenges we face in life.
  • śrutaṁ: This word means "heard" or "listened to." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom gained through experience.
  • havaṁ: This translates to "offering" or "sacrifice." It signifies the importance of giving and contributing to the greater good.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word means "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It emphasizes the qualities of strength and resilience.
  • vadhrimatyāḥ: This can be interpreted as "with great strength" or "with power." It reinforces the idea of harnessing strength in our endeavors.
  • daśasyantā: This translates to "the ten" or "the multitude." It signifies community and the collective effort of many.
  • śayave: Meaning "to the one who lies down," it can symbolize rest or contemplation, suggesting the need for balance in our lives.
  • pipyathur: This word means "thirsting" or "desiring." It represents the human condition of longing for knowledge, fulfillment, and connection.
  • gām: This translates to "the cow" or "the herd." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize nourishment and abundance.
  • iti: This word means "thus" or "so." It serves as a connector in the mantra, summarizing the preceding thoughts.
  • cyavānā: This translates to "having strayed" or "having deviated." It signifies the challenges and distractions we face in our journey.
  • sumatim: Meaning "good thought" or "wisdom," it emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and clarity of mind.
  • bhuraṇyū: This can be interpreted as "may we be blessed" or "may we be enriched." It signifies the aspiration for abundance and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, community, and the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of moving forward with vigor, seeking knowledge, and contributing to the greater good. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, teamwork, and the pursuit of wisdom. It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength, work collaboratively, and maintain a positive mindset, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and enriched life.
