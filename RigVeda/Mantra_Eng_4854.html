यम आपो अद्रयो वना गर्भं ऋतस्य पिप्रति |सहसा यो मथितो जायते नृभिः पृथिव्या अधि सानवी ||
yam āpo adrayo vanā garbham ṛtasya piprati | sahasā yo mathito jāyate nṛbhiḥ pṛthivyā adhi sānavi ||
  • yam - This word translates to "whom" or "that which." It indicates a subject or entity that is being referred to.
  • āpo - Meaning "waters," this term signifies the essential element of water, which is vital for life and sustenance.
  • adrayo - This translates to "mountains" or "rocks," symbolizing stability and strength in nature.
  • vanā - Meaning "forests," it represents the natural world and its abundance, highlighting the importance of biodiversity.
  • garbham - This word means "womb" or "embryo," indicating the process of creation and nurturing life.
  • ṛtasya - Translated as "of truth" or "of order," it signifies the cosmic order and the principles that govern the universe.
  • piprati - This means "nourishes" or "supports," emphasizing the role of nature in providing sustenance.
  • sahasā - Meaning "suddenly" or "swiftly," it indicates the rapidity of certain processes in nature.
  • yo - This translates to "who" or "that," referring to the subject of the action.
  • mathito - Meaning "churned," it symbolizes the process of transformation and the extraction of essence from something.
  • jāyate - This means "is born" or "comes into being," indicating the emergence of new life or ideas.
  • nṛbhiḥ - Translated as "by men" or "by humans," it signifies human involvement in the processes of creation and sustenance.
  • pṛthivyā - Meaning "of the earth," it emphasizes the connection between life and the planet.
  • adhi - This translates to "upon" or "over," indicating a position or state of being.
  • sānavi - This word can mean "with support" or "with strength," suggesting the importance of community and collaboration.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the interconnectedness of nature, humanity, and the cosmic order. It speaks to the nurturing aspect of the earth and the role of water, mountains, and forests in sustaining life. The process of creation is highlighted, showing how life emerges from the elements and is supported by the natural world.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: It emphasizes the importance of preserving natural resources like water and forests, which are essential for life.
  • Community Collaboration: The idea of working together ("sānavi") can be seen in community efforts to address environmental issues or social challenges.
  • Personal Growth: The process of "churning" can be likened to personal development, where individuals undergo transformation through experiences and challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the vital relationship between humanity and nature. It encourages respect for the environment and highlights the importance of truth and order in our lives. By recognizing our interconnectedness with the earth and each other, we can foster a more sustainable and harmonious existence.
