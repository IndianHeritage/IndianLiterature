तां सम्राजागृहात्सुती आदित्य दानुनस पतिः |सचेत एवानवह्वरम् ||
tā samrājā ghṛtāsutī ādityā dānunas patī | sacete anavahvaram ||
  • tā samrājā: This phrase can be translated as "that ruler" or "that king." In a broader sense, it refers to a powerful leader or authority figure who governs with wisdom and strength.
  • ghṛtāsutī: This term means "the one born of ghee" or "the one who is nourished by ghee." Ghee, a clarified butter, is often associated with purity and nourishment in Vedic literature. It symbolizes the essence of life and prosperity.
  • ādityā: This word refers to the sun or solar deities. In the Vedic context, the sun is a source of light, energy, and life, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • dānunas patī: This phrase translates to "the lord of the demons" or "the ruler of the dark forces." It signifies the challenges and adversities one may face in life, often represented by negative energies or obstacles.
  • sacete: This term means "may he be pleased" or "may he be satisfied." It reflects a desire for harmony and goodwill from the divine or higher powers.
  • anavahvaram: This word can be interpreted as "without obstacles" or "without hindrances." It conveys the idea of a smooth path or journey, free from difficulties.

Conclusion: The mantra "tā samrājā ghṛtāsutī ādityā dānunas patī | sacete anavahvaram ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance and support from a higher power or authority in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of leadership, nourishment, and enlightenment while acknowledging the presence of obstacles. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of personal struggle, when seeking clarity in decision-making, or when striving for success in endeavors. By invoking the qualities of a wise ruler and the nurturing aspects of life, individuals can find strength and resilience to navigate through difficulties, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
