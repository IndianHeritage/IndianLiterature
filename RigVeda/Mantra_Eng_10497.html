प्र वो ग्रावाणः सविता देवः सुवतु धर्मणा |धूर्षु युज्यध्वं सुनुत ||
pra vo grāvāṇaḥ savitā devaḥ suvatu dharmaṇā | dhūrṣu yujyadhvaṁ sunuta ||
  • pra vo grāvāṇaḥ savitā devaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine Savita, the sun god, who is the source of all energy and life, bless you." Here, "Savitā" represents the sun, which is often seen as a symbol of enlightenment and vitality in various cultures.
  • suvatu dharmaṇā: This means "may you be blessed with righteousness." The term "dharma" refers to the moral and ethical duties that guide an individual's life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of living a life of integrity and adhering to one's principles.
  • dhūrṣu yujyadhvaṁ sunuta: This translates to "may you be united in your efforts and may your actions be fruitful." It emphasizes the significance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals. In a workplace setting, for example, this can be seen in how team members work together to complete a project successfully.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for divine blessings, righteousness, and unity in efforts. Its significance lies in the recognition of the importance of moral integrity and collective action in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in family gatherings, team projects, or community initiatives, where the focus is on working together harmoniously while upholding ethical values. By invoking the blessings of Savita, individuals are reminded to strive for a life filled with purpose, collaboration, and righteousness.
