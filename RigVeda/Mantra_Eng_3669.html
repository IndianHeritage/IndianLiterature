तव श्रियाऽसुदृशो देव देवाः पुरू दधाना अमृतं सपन्त |होतारं अग्निम मनुषो न षेदुर दशस्यन्त उषिजः शंसम आयोः ||
tava śriyā sudṛśo deva devāḥ purū dadhānā amṛtaṁ sapanta | hotāram agnim manuṣo ni ṣedur daśasyanta uśijaḥ śaṁsam āyoḥ ||
  • tava śriyā: This phrase translates to "by your glory" or "through your splendor." It signifies the divine radiance and power of the deity being addressed.
  • Sudṛśo: Meaning "of good vision" or "beautiful," this term emphasizes the aesthetic and spiritual beauty associated with the divine.
  • deva devāḥ: This translates to "gods of the gods," indicating the supreme status of the deity among all divine beings.
  • purū dadhānā: This means "who holds many" or "who possesses abundance," suggesting the deity's ability to provide and sustain various forms of life and prosperity.
  • amṛtaṁ sapanta: This phrase means "the immortal essence" or "the nectar of immortality," which symbolizes the ultimate truth and eternal life.
  • hotāram agnim: This translates to "the fire that is invoked," referring to Agni, the fire god, who is a vital element in Vedic rituals and represents transformation and purification.
  • manuṣo ni ṣedur: Meaning "humans have settled," this indicates the connection between the divine and human realms, emphasizing the importance of rituals and offerings.
  • daśasyanta: This term can be interpreted as "the ten directions," symbolizing the all-encompassing nature of the divine presence in every aspect of life.
  • uśijaḥ: This means "the wise" or "the learned," referring to those who understand and appreciate the divine wisdom.
  • śaṁsam āyoḥ: This translates to "the auspiciousness of the two," suggesting a duality that brings harmony and balance, often interpreted as the balance between material and spiritual pursuits.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine glory, abundance, and the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms. It highlights the importance of rituals, the pursuit of wisdom, and the quest for immortality through spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during religious ceremonies, personal meditations, or when seeking guidance in making decisions that align with both material success and spiritual fulfillment. It serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of maintaining a balance between our worldly responsibilities and spiritual aspirations.
