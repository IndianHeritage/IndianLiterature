इन्द्रं वो नरः सख्याय सेपुर महो यन्तः सुमतये चकानाः |महो हि दाता वज्रहस्तो अस्ति महाम् उ रण्वम् अवसे यजध्वम् ||
indraṁ vo naraḥ sakhyāya sepur maho yantaḥ sumataye cakānāḥ | maho hi dātā vajrahasto asti mahām u raṇvam avase yajadhvam ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • vo: Means "to you," indicating a direct address to the audience or the worshippers.
  • naraḥ: Translates to "men" or "humans," emphasizing the collective strength of humanity.
  • sakhyāya: Means "for friendship" or "for companionship," highlighting the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • sepur: Means "to have poured" or "to have offered," suggesting the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • maho: Translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance of the offerings made.
  • yantaḥ: Means "those who are moving" or "those who are striving," referring to individuals who are actively pursuing their goals.
  • sumataye: Translates to "for good thoughts" or "for wisdom," emphasizing the importance of positive intentions and intelligence.
  • cakānāḥ: Means "those who are enlightened" or "those who have knowledge," referring to wise individuals.
  • maho hi: "For indeed, great," reinforcing the importance of the preceding concepts.
  • dātā: Means "giver" or "donor," indicating the act of generosity and sharing.
  • vajrahasto: Refers to "one with a thunderbolt in hand," symbolizing Indra's power and ability to overcome obstacles.
  • asti: Means "there is" or "exists," affirming the presence of the qualities being discussed.
  • mahām: Translates to "great" or "mighty," further emphasizing the strength of the giver.
  • u: A conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas together.
  • raṇvam: Means "battle" or "struggle," indicating the challenges faced in life.
  • avase: Means "to be prepared" or "to be ready," suggesting the need for readiness in facing challenges.
  • yajadhvam: Means "perform your sacrifice" or "offer your devotion," encouraging active participation in rituals or acts of devotion.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom among individuals. It calls upon the collective power of humanity to strive for greatness and to offer their best intentions and efforts. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, or any collaborative effort where individuals come together for a common goal. The significance lies in recognizing that through friendship, wisdom, and shared strength, we can overcome challenges and achieve greatness, much like how a team works together to win a game or a community unites to support a cause. The mantra serves as a reminder to be generous, to support one another, and to be prepared for the struggles that life presents, all while maintaining a spirit of cooperation and goodwill.
