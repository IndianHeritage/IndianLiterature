तद् वो जातिम्त्वं मरुतः परे युगे पुरू यच्छंसम् अमृतास आवत |अयां धियā मनवे श्रुष्टिम आव्यां साकं नरो दंसनैर आ चिकीत्रिरे ||
tad vo jāmitvam marutaḥ pare yuge purū yac chaṁsam amṛtāsa āvata | ayā dhiyā manave śruṣṭim āvyā sākaṁ naro daṁsanair ā cikitrire ||
  • tad vo jāmitvam marutaḥ pare yuge: This phrase can be translated to "That which you (the Maruts) have created in the distant age." Here, "Maruts" refers to the storm deities or winds, symbolizing the forces of nature and their creative power.
  • purū yac chaṁsam amṛtāsa āvata: This translates to "Many are the blessings that you bestow, O immortal ones." The term "amṛtāsa" signifies immortality, indicating the divine nature of the blessings provided by the Maruts.
  • ayā dhiyā manave śruṣṭim āvyā: This means "With this intellect, O Man, may we understand the creation." It emphasizes the importance of human intellect and understanding in relation to the divine creation.
  • sākaṁ naro daṁsanair ā cikitrire: This translates to "Together, men have created with their efforts." It highlights the collaborative nature of human endeavor in shaping the world around them.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the relationship between divine forces and human beings. It acknowledges the blessings of the divine (Maruts) and the importance of human intellect and collaboration in understanding and shaping creation.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, where collaboration leads to innovation and success. It also highlights the need for respect towards nature and the divine forces that govern it, reminding us to work harmoniously with the environment.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of utilizing our intellect and collaborative spirit to navigate the challenges of life. It encourages us to seek blessings from the divine while actively participating in the creation and betterment of our world.
