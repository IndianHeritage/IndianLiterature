इमा जुषेथां सवनाः येभिर हव्याणि ऊहथुः | इन्द्राग्नि आ गतं नराः ||
imā juṣethāṁ savanā yebhir havyāny ūhathuḥ | indrāgnī ā gataṁ narā ||
  • Translation: "May these offerings be pleasing to you, with which the mortals invoke the divine Indra and Agni."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • imā: these
    • juṣethāṁ: may be pleasing
    • savanā: offerings
    • yebhir: with which
    • havyāny: offerings (specifically to the gods)
    • ūhathuḥ: invoke or call upon
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (two significant deities)
    • ā gataṁ: have come
    • narā: mortals or humans

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with divine forces.
    • Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength, while Agni represents fire and transformation.
    • The invocation of these deities signifies a plea for their blessings and support in human endeavors.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra during the planting season is crucial for ensuring rain and a good harvest.
    • Agni is often invoked during rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings, where fire symbolizes purity and transformation.
    • Modern practices may include meditation or prayer that seeks to align oneself with these divine energies for personal growth and success.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between humans and the divine.
    • It highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in seeking guidance and blessings from higher powers.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages individuals to recognize the importance of intention and respect in their pursuits, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual realms.
