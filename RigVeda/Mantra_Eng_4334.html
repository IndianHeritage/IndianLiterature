विवृक्षान्हन्त्युत हन्ति रक्षसो विश्वं बिभाय भुवनं महावधात् |उतानागा ईषते वृष्ण्यावतो यत् पार्जन्यः स्तनयन हन्ति दुष्कृतः ||
vi vṛkṣān hanty uta hanti rakṣaso viśvam bibhāya bhuvanam mahāvadhāt | utānāgā īṣate vṛṣṇyāvato yat parjanyaḥ stanayan hanti duṣkṛtaḥ ||
  • Translation: "He who strikes down the trees, he who destroys the demons, he who fears the universe, the great one who strikes down all. The one who is invoked by the Vṛṣṇis, the one who is like the thunder, he who strikes down the wicked."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi vṛkṣān: "strikes down the trees" - This can symbolize the removal of obstacles or negativity in life.
    • hanty: "destroys" - Represents the act of overcoming challenges.
    • uta: "and" - A conjunction that connects ideas.
    • hanti rakṣaso: "destroys the demons" - This can refer to overcoming fears or negative influences.
    • viśvam bibhāya: "fears the universe" - Suggests a sense of awe and respect for the vastness of existence.
    • bhuvanam: "the world" - Represents the physical and metaphysical realms we inhabit.
    • mahāvadhāt: "the great one who strikes down all" - Refers to a powerful force or deity that eliminates evil.
    • utānāgā: "invoked by the Vṛṣṇis" - Indicates a connection to a lineage or group that seeks divine assistance.
    • īṣate: "is like" - A simile that draws comparisons.
    • vṛṣṇyāvato: "the one who is like the thunder" - Thunder symbolizes power and authority.
    • yat: "he who" - Refers to the subject of the mantra.
    • parjanyaḥ: "the rain" - Represents nourishment and abundance.
    • stanayan: "strikes down" - Again emphasizes the action of overcoming.
    • hanti duṣkṛtaḥ: "the wicked" - Refers to those who engage in negative actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to confront their fears and challenges, much like a person overcoming anxiety to pursue their dreams.
    • In a community context, it can symbolize collective action against social evils, such as a group coming together to fight against corruption or injustice.
    • In environmental activism, the reference to "striking down trees" can be interpreted as a call to protect nature from destruction, advocating for sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its powerful imagery of overcoming obstacles, confronting fears, and striving for righteousness. It serves as a reminder that, like the forces of nature, we too have the capacity to confront and eliminate negativity in our lives and communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, social justice movements, and environmental conservation efforts, encouraging individuals and groups to take action against adversity and promote positive change.
