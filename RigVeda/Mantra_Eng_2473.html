मन्द्रं होतारं शुचिम अद्वयाविनं दामूनसम् उक्थ्यं विश्वचर्षणिम् | रथं न चित्रम् वपुषाय दर्शतम् मनुर्हितं सदम् इद् राया ईमहे ||
mandraṁ hotāraṁ śucim advayāvinaṁ damūnasam ukthyaṁ viśvacarṣaṇim | rathaṁ na citraṁ vapuṣāya darśatam manurhitaṁ sadam id rāya īmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandraṁ: a hymn or mantra
    • hotāraṁ: the one who offers or invokes
    • śucim: pure or sacred
    • advayāvinaṁ: without a second, unique
    • damūnasam: one who is restrained or controlled
    • ukthyaṁ: the one who is praised or invoked
    • viśvacarṣaṇim: the one who encompasses all, universal
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • na: not
    • citraṁ: colorful or diverse
    • vapuṣāya: in form or appearance
    • darśatam: to be seen or manifested
    • manurhitaṁ: the one who is guided by Manu (the lawgiver)
    • sadam: the abode or dwelling
    • id: this
    • rāya: wealth or prosperity
    • īmahe: we invoke or seek
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of purity and uniqueness, emphasizing the importance of a singular divine presence that is both universal and all-encompassing.
    • The reference to a chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where the divine guidance is sought to navigate through the complexities of existence.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of restraint and control, suggesting that true power lies in the ability to govern oneself.
    • By invoking wealth and prosperity, it reflects the human desire for abundance, not just materially but also spiritually.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek guidance and clarity (akin to invoking the divine) to make decisions that affect the entire organization, much like the invocation of the mantra for universal guidance.
    • In personal life, individuals may practice meditation or mindfulness to achieve self-control and purity of thought, resonating with the mantra's emphasis on restraint and purity.
    • During festivals or significant life events, people often seek blessings for prosperity and abundance, paralleling the invocation of wealth in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance in our lives, emphasizing purity, control, and the pursuit of prosperity.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional endeavors, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and guided existence.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that by invoking higher principles and values, we can navigate the complexities of life with clarity and purpose.
