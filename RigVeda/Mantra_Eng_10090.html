विः हि त्वां इन्द्र पुरुधा जनासो हितप्रयसो वृषभ ह्वयन्ते |अस्माकं ते मधुमत्तमानīmā भुवन सवनā तेषु हर्य ||
vi hi tvām indra purudhā janāso hitaprayaso vṛṣabha hvayante | asmākaṁ te madhumattamānīmā bhuvan savanā teṣu harya ||
  • Translation: "O Indra, the people call upon you in many ways, seeking your favor and strength. We offer you our sweetest prayers, and may you grant us abundance in all realms."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi hi tvām: "Indeed, we call upon you" - This emphasizes the earnestness of the invocation.
    • indra: "O Indra" - Indra is the king of the gods, representing strength, power, and the force of nature.
    • purudhā: "in many ways" - This indicates the diverse approaches and methods people use to seek divine assistance.
    • janāso: "of the people" - Refers to the collective community that seeks the blessings of Indra.
    • hitaprayaso: "seeking welfare" - This highlights the intention behind the prayers, focusing on the well-being of individuals and society.
    • vṛṣabha: "the strong one" - A title for Indra, symbolizing his might and capability to protect and provide.
    • hvayante: "they invoke" - This shows the active role of the people in calling upon the divine.
    • asmākaṁ: "our" - Indicates possession, emphasizing the personal connection to the divine.
    • te: "to you" - Directly addressing Indra, reinforcing the relationship between the worshippers and the deity.
    • madhumattamānīmā: "the sweetest offerings" - Refers to the best and most cherished prayers or offerings made to Indra.
    • bhuvan: "worlds" - This signifies the various realms of existence that Indra oversees.
    • savanā: "abundance" - Represents the blessings and prosperity that are sought through the prayers.
    • teṣu: "in these" - Refers to the realms or aspects of life where blessings are desired.
    • harya: "may you grant" - A hopeful plea for Indra's benevolence and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, farmers often pray to Indra for rain and favorable weather, reflecting the invocation of divine support for their livelihoods.
    • During festivals, people gather to chant mantras and offer prayers, seeking blessings for health, prosperity, and harmony within their families and communities.
    • In personal struggles, individuals may turn to spiritual practices, invoking divine assistance to overcome challenges, much like the collective calling upon Indra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for the welfare of individuals and society. It highlights the importance of community in spiritual practices and the belief in a higher power that can provide strength and abundance. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of people and their shared aspirations for prosperity, health, and harmony. It encourages individuals to actively seek support from the divine in their endeavors, fostering a sense of hope and unity in the face of challenges.
