यां ते धामानि परमाणि यावमा या मध्यमा विश्वकर्मन्न उतेमा |शिक्षा सखिभ्यो हविषि स्वधावः स्वयं यजस्व तंवं वृद्धानः ||
yā te dhāmāni paramāṇi yāvamā yā madhyamā viśvakarmann utemā | śikṣā sakhibhyo haviṣi svadhāvaḥ svayaṁ yajasva tanvaṁ vṛdhānaḥ ||
  • yā te dhāmāni paramāṇi: This phrase can be translated as "those who are your supreme abode." Here, "dhāmāni" refers to the realms or places of existence, and "paramāṇi" signifies the ultimate or supreme. In real life, this can be understood as the search for a higher purpose or ultimate truth in our lives, akin to individuals seeking fulfillment through spirituality or personal growth.
  • yāvamā yā madhyamā: This translates to "as long as you are in the middle." The term "madhyamā" suggests balance or moderation. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of maintaining balance in our lives, such as work-life balance, emotional stability, or even dietary habits. For example, a person who practices mindfulness may strive to find a middle ground between stress and relaxation.
  • viśvakarmann utemā: This means "in all actions, may you be present." "Viśvakarman" refers to the universal creator or the essence of all actions. This can be applied in real life by emphasizing the importance of being fully engaged in our tasks, whether at work or in personal relationships. For instance, a student who practices active learning techniques is more likely to retain information than one who passively reads.
  • śikṣā sakhibhyo haviṣi svadhāvaḥ: This translates to "knowledge is offered to friends and those who are worthy." "Śikṣā" means knowledge or learning, and "haviṣi" refers to offerings. This highlights the value of sharing knowledge with others, which can be seen in mentorship programs or collaborative learning environments. For example, a teacher who encourages students to share their insights fosters a richer learning experience.
  • svayaṁ yajasva tanvaṁ vṛdhānaḥ: This means "may you worship with your own body and grow." "Tanvaṁ" refers to the body, and "vṛdhānaḥ" signifies growth or development. This can be interpreted as the importance of self-care and personal development. In real life, this could manifest as individuals engaging in physical fitness, meditation, or other practices that promote holistic well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking higher truths, maintaining balance, being present in our actions, sharing knowledge, and nurturing personal growth. In today's fast-paced world, these principles can guide individuals towards a more fulfilling and meaningful life. By applying these teachings, one can cultivate a sense of purpose, improve relationships, and enhance overall well-being, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of modern life.
