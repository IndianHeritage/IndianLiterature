शुष्मिन्तमो हि ते मदो द्युम्निन्तमा उता क्रतुः |अध स्मा ते परि चरन्त्य अजरा श्रुष्टिवानो नाजर ||
śuṣmintamo hi te mado dyumnintama uta kratuḥ | adha smā te pari caranty ajara śruṣṭīvāno nājara ||
  • śuṣmintamo hi te mado dyumnintama uta kratuḥ
    • śuṣmintamo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is full of joy" or "the one who is blissful." In real life, this can refer to individuals who radiate positivity and happiness, often uplifting those around them.
    • hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding word, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • te: This means "your" or "of you," indicating possession or relation.
    • mado: This word signifies "intoxication" or "ecstasy," which can be understood as a state of heightened joy or spiritual bliss.
    • dyumnintama: This translates to "the most radiant" or "the most glorious," suggesting a state of brilliance or excellence.
    • uta: This means "or" and is used to present an alternative or additional idea.
    • kratuḥ: This refers to "intellect" or "willpower," indicating the mental strength and determination to achieve one's goals.
  • adha smā te pari caranty ajara śruṣṭīvāno nājara
    • adha: This means "below" or "under," suggesting a foundation or base.
    • smā: This can be interpreted as "let us" or "may we," indicating a collective intention or desire.
    • te: Again, this means "your" or "of you."
    • pari caranty: This translates to "they move around" or "they circulate," indicating a sense of activity or dynamism.
    • ajara: This means "immortal" or "eternal," suggesting a state of being that transcends time.
    • śruṣṭīvāno: This can be interpreted as "the one who creates" or "the creator," referring to a divine or higher power responsible for creation.
    • nājara: This means "not old" or "not decaying," reinforcing the idea of immortality and the eternal nature of existence.

Conclusion:

The mantra speaks to the essence of joy, brilliance, and the power of intellect, while also emphasizing the eternal nature of existence and creation. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, cultivating a joyful and positive mindset can lead to greater success and fulfillment.
  • In leadership, embodying brilliance and strong willpower can inspire others and create a thriving environment.
  • In spiritual practices, recognizing the eternal nature of life can help individuals find peace and purpose beyond material concerns.

Overall, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace joy, strive for excellence, and acknowledge the eternal aspects of existence, guiding them towards a more meaningful and fulfilling life.
