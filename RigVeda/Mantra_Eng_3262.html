किं स ऋधक कृत्नवद यं सहस्रं मासो जभारा शरदश्च पूर्वीः |नहि nv अस्य प्रतिमानम अस्त्य अन्तर जातेषूत ये जनित्वाḥ ||
kiṁ sa ṛdhak kṛṇavad yaṁ sahasram māso jabhāra śaradaś ca pūrvīḥ | nahī nv asya pratimānam asty antar jāteṣūta ye janitvāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ: What
    • sa: he/that
    • ṛdhak: is prosperous
    • kṛṇavad: who acts
    • yaṁ: whom
    • sahasram: a thousand
    • māso: months
    • jabhāra: has borne
    • śaradaś: autumns
    • ca: and
    • pūrvīḥ: the previous
    • nahī: indeed not
    • nv: certainly
    • asya: of this
    • pratimānam: equivalent
    • asty: exists
    • antar: within
    • jāteṣūta: among the born
    • ye: those who
    • janitvāḥ: have given birth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a profound question about prosperity and existence.
    • It reflects on the nature of actions and their consequences over time.
    • The phrase "a thousand months" symbolizes a long duration, suggesting that true prosperity is not fleeting but enduring.
    • The mention of "autumns" indicates a cycle of life, where each season brings its own lessons and experiences.
    • Ultimately, it asserts that there is no equivalent to the essence of life and existence among those who have been born.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be related to the idea that sustainable success requires consistent effort over time, much like nurturing a plant to grow through different seasons.
    • In personal development, it emphasizes the importance of patience and perseverance, as true growth and understanding come from enduring experiences rather than quick fixes.
    • In relationships, it highlights that deep connections are built over time, through shared experiences and mutual support, rather than superficial interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the value of time, effort, and the unique journey of each individual.
    • Its significance lies in understanding that true prosperity and fulfillment come from a deep engagement with life, rather than mere existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and the long-term impact they have on their lives and the lives of others.
