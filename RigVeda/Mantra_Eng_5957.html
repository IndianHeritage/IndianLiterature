यो मां पाकेन मनसा चरन्तम अभिचष्टे अनृतैभिर वचोभिः |आप इव काशिना संगृभिता असन्न अस्त्व आसत इन्द्र वक्ता ||
yo mā pākena manasā carantam abhicaṣṭe anṛtebhir vacobhiḥ | āpa iva kāśinā saṁgṛbhītā asann astv āsata indra vaktā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • mā: not
    • pākena: by sin
    • manasā: by the mind
    • carantam: wandering
    • abhicaṣṭe: perceives
    • anṛtebhir: by falsehoods
    • vacobhiḥ: by words
    • āpa: like water
    • iva: as
    • kāśinā: by the one who is pure
    • saṁgṛbhītā: gathered
    • asann: not existing
    • astv: may it be
    • āsata: may it dwell
    • indra: O Indra (the deity)
    • vaktā: speaker
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the purity of thought and speech.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not allowing sinful or false thoughts to influence one's mind.
    • The reference to "like water" suggests that purity and clarity are essential, just as water is essential for life.
    • Indra, as a deity, represents strength and power, and invoking him signifies a plea for guidance and protection against falsehood.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, maintaining integrity in communication is crucial. This mantra reminds individuals to speak truthfully and avoid gossip or deceit.
    • In personal relationships, being honest and transparent fosters trust. The mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their thoughts and words before expressing them.
    • In education, students are taught the value of academic honesty. This mantra can serve as a reminder to avoid plagiarism and to value original thought.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mental and verbal purity.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal integrity, professional ethics, and social interactions.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life of honesty, clarity, and strength, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
