स हि सत्यो यं पूर्वे चिद्देवासश्चिद्यं ईधिरे |होतारं मन्द्रजिह्वं इति सुदीतिभिर्विभावसुम् ||
sa hi satyo yam pūrve cid devāsaś cid yam īdhire | hotāram mandrajihvam it sudītibhir vibhāvasum ||
  • sa hi satyo: This phrase translates to "Indeed, he is the truth." It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and integrity in one's life. In real life, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who uphold honesty in their personal and professional dealings, fostering trust and respect.
  • yam pūrve cid devāsaś: This means "whom the ancient gods have desired." It suggests that there is a divine selection or approval of certain qualities or individuals. For example, leaders who are chosen for their wisdom and ethical conduct can be seen as embodying this divine approval, guiding their communities towards prosperity.
  • cid yam īdhire: This translates to "whom they have invoked." It indicates the act of calling upon or seeking the presence of the divine. In practical terms, this can relate to how people seek guidance through prayer or meditation, invoking higher powers for support in challenging times.
  • hotāram: This word refers to "the one who offers the oblation" or "the priest." It symbolizes the role of a mediator between the divine and the earthly realm. In modern contexts, this can be likened to leaders or mentors who facilitate connections and provide guidance to others, helping them navigate their paths.
  • mandrajihvam: This term means "one with a gentle tongue." It highlights the importance of communication and the power of words. In everyday life, individuals who communicate with kindness and respect can foster better relationships and create harmonious environments, whether at home, work, or in the community.
  • it sudītibhir: This translates to "with the good deeds." It emphasizes the significance of actions that are beneficial and virtuous. In real life, this can be seen in community service or charitable acts, where individuals contribute positively to society, reflecting the essence of good deeds.
  • vibhāvasum: This means "the one who shines or illuminates." It signifies the presence of light and knowledge. In practical terms, this can refer to individuals who inspire others through their wisdom and actions, serving as beacons of hope and guidance in their communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of truth, divine approval, and the importance of virtuous actions and communication. It serves as a reminder of the qualities that lead to personal and communal growth. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, mentorship, and community service, encouraging individuals to embody truthfulness, kindness, and the pursuit of good deeds. By doing so, they not only enhance their own lives but also contribute positively to the world around them.
