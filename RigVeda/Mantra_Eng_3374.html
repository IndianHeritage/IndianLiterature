मन्त्र: किमाद उतासि वृत्रहन् मगवन मण्युमत्तमः | अत्राह दानुम आतिरः ||
kim ād utāsi vṛtrahan maghavan manyumattamaḥ | atrāha dānum ātiraḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kim: What
    • ād: You have
    • utāsi: You have risen
    • vṛtrahan: Slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • maghavan: O Maghavan (another name for Indra, the king of gods)
    • manyumattamaḥ: The one with the greatest wrath
    • atrāha: Here, now
    • dānum: Gift or boon
    • ātiraḥ: You have given
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the powerful deity known for his strength and ability to conquer evil.
    • It questions what Indra has achieved or what he has risen to do, emphasizing his role as a protector and slayer of demons.
    • The term "manyumattamaḥ" highlights Indra's fierce nature, suggesting that he is not only powerful but also capable of great wrath when provoked.
    • The latter part of the mantra speaks of the gifts or boons that Indra has provided, indicating a relationship of gratitude and reverence from the devotee.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be likened to seeking strength and protection in times of adversity, much like how individuals turn to leaders or mentors for guidance during challenging situations.
    • For instance, a student facing academic challenges may invoke the spirit of Indra by seeking help from a teacher, symbolizing the quest for knowledge and strength to overcome obstacles.
    • In a broader sense, communities often look to leaders who embody the qualities of Indra—strength, wisdom, and the ability to protect and uplift others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, protection, and the acknowledgment of divine support in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our struggles, we can seek help from those who embody strength and wisdom, whether they be spiritual figures, mentors, or community leaders.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to recognize the power of collaboration and the importance of seeking and giving support in the face of adversity.
