हुवे वः सुद्योत्मानं सुवृक्तिं विश्वाम अग्निम अतिथिं सुप्रयसम् |मित्र इव यो दिधिषाय्यो भूद देव आदेव जन जातवेदाः ||
huve vaḥ sudyotmānaṁ suvṛktiṁ viśām agnim atithiṁ suprayasam | mitra iva yo didhiṣāyyo bhūd deva ādeve jane jātavedāḥ ||
  • huve vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine presence or assistance.
  • sudyotmānaṁ: This word means "radiant" or "brilliant." It refers to the illuminating qualities of the divine or the energy that brings clarity and enlightenment.
  • suvṛktiṁ: This translates to "good conduct" or "virtuous action." It emphasizes the importance of ethical behavior and righteousness in one's life.
  • viśām: This means "of the people" or "of the community." It highlights the collective aspect of human existence and the interconnectedness of individuals within society.
  • agnim: This word refers to "fire," which in Vedic literature symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence. Fire is often seen as a medium through which offerings are made to the gods.
  • atithiṁ: This means "guest" or "stranger." It signifies the importance of hospitality and the respect given to those who visit, reflecting the values of kindness and generosity.
  • suprayasam: This translates to "great effort" or "excellent endeavor." It underscores the significance of hard work and dedication in achieving one's goals.
  • mitra iva: This phrase means "like a friend." It suggests a relationship built on trust, support, and mutual respect.
  • yo didhiṣāyyo: This can be interpreted as "who is to be worshipped" or "who is worthy of reverence." It points to the divine qualities that inspire devotion and admiration.
  • bhūd: This means "may it be" or "let it be." It expresses a desire for positive outcomes and blessings.
  • deva: This word means "god" or "divine being." It represents the higher powers that govern the universe and influence human affairs.
  • ādeve: This translates to "in the divine" or "in the sacred." It emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with spiritual values and principles.
  • jane: This means "people" or "human beings." It reflects the focus on humanity and the shared experiences of individuals.
  • jātavedāḥ: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It signifies the omniscience of the divine and the understanding of the cycles of life and existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine guidance and support for virtuous living within a community. It emphasizes the importance of ethical conduct, hospitality, and hard work while recognizing the divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, rituals, or personal reflections, where individuals seek to align their actions with higher principles and foster a sense of unity and respect among people. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and enlightened society.
