यस्य गावाव अरुषा सूयवस्यू अन्तर ऊषु चरतो रेरिहाणां |स सृञ्जयाय तुर्वशं परादाद वृचिवतो दैववाताय शिक्षन् ||
yasya gāvāv aruṣā sūyavasyū antar ū ṣu carato rerihāṇā | sa sṛñjayāya turvaśam parādād vṛcīvato daivavātāya śikṣan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • gāvāv: the cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • aruṣā: red (often associated with vitality and energy)
    • sūyavasyū: those who are well-nourished or prosperous
    • antar: within
    • ū: the essence or core
    • ṣu: good or auspicious
    • carato: moving or wandering
    • rerihāṇā: who is revered or respected
    • sa: he or that one
    • sṛñjayāya: for the one who is wise or knowledgeable
    • turvaśam: the one who is powerful or strong
    • parādād: from the enemies or adversaries
    • vṛcīvato: the one who is elevated or exalted
    • daivavātāya: to the divine or godly
    • śikṣan: to teach or instruct
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine figure who is associated with prosperity and reverence.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth and nourishment, which are essential for a thriving community.
    • The "red" aspect signifies vitality, suggesting that this figure embodies energy and life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in overcoming adversities.
    • It highlights the role of divine guidance in teaching and instructing individuals towards a prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the reverence for cows can be seen in many cultures where they are considered sacred and are integral to farming practices.
    • In business, the mantra can be applied to the importance of wisdom and strength in leadership, guiding teams through challenges.
    • In personal development, the idea of divine guidance can be reflected in mentorship, where experienced individuals teach and uplift others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, strength, and divine guidance in achieving prosperity and overcoming challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to personal development, emphasizing the need for respect towards sources of nourishment and the value of mentorship.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and strength while remaining connected to their roots and the divine.
