त्वं हि स्मा च्यावयन्न अच्युतान्य एको वृत्राः चरसि जिघ्नमानः |तव dyāvāpṛthivī पर्वतासोऽनु व्रताय निमितेव तस्थुः ||
tvaṁ hi ṣmā cyāvayann acyutāny eko vṛtrā carasi jighnamānaḥ | tava dyāvāpṛthivī parvatāso 'nu vratāya nimiteva tasthuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hi: indeed
    • ṣmā: the one who is the source of all
    • cyāvayann: driving away
    • acyutāny: the imperishable
    • eko: alone
    • vṛtrā: the obstacle or enemy
    • carasi: you move
    • jighnamānaḥ: seeking to destroy
    • tava: your
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • parvatāso: mountains
    • 'anu: following
    • vratāya: for the purpose of the vow
    • nimiteva: as if for a cause
    • tasthuḥ: they stand
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence that drives away obstacles and challenges.
    • The phrase "You indeed are the source of all" emphasizes the singularity and importance of the divine in overcoming difficulties.
    • The reference to "heaven and earth" signifies the vastness of the divine influence, encompassing all aspects of existence.
    • The mountains symbolize stability and strength, suggesting that the divine presence supports and upholds the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to spiritual practices or prayers, seeking strength and guidance from a higher power.
    • Communities facing natural disasters may come together in faith, invoking divine support to overcome the challenges posed by nature.
    • In professional settings, teams may rely on shared values and a common purpose, akin to the divine vow mentioned in the mantra, to achieve their goals despite obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, regardless of the obstacles we face, there is a higher power that aids us in our journey.
    • In real life, this can be applied through faith, community support, and personal resilience, encouraging individuals to seek strength from within and from the divine.
