यो वः सुनोति अभिपित्वे अह्नां तीव्रं वाजासः सवनं मदाय |तस्मै रयिम ऋभवः सर्ववीरम् आ तक्षत वृषणो मन्दसानाः ||
yo vaḥ sunoty abhipitve ahnāṁ tīvraṁ vājāsaḥ savanam madāya | tasmai rayim ṛbhavaḥ sarvavīram ā takṣata vṛṣaṇo mandasānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vaḥ: your
    • sunoty: brings forth
    • abhipitve: in the act of worship
    • ahnāṁ: of the day
    • tīvraṁ: intense
    • vājāsaḥ: of the horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • savanam: offering
    • madāya: for the sake of joy
    • tasmai: to that (deity)
    • rayim: wealth
    • ṛbhavaḥ: may we attain
    • sarvavīram: all heroes
    • ā: from
    • takṣata: may he grant
    • vṛṣaṇo: of the strong
    • mandasānāḥ: who are gentle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a deity who brings forth blessings and prosperity.
    • The reference to "intense" and "horses" symbolizes the strength and vigor needed in life.
    • The act of offering is emphasized, suggesting that joy and gratitude are essential in worship.
    • It seeks wealth and success for all, particularly for those who are strong yet gentle.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of success through hard work and ethical practices.
    • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the importance of strength combined with kindness, fostering a supportive environment.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of combining strength with gentleness in our pursuits.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth comes from a balance of power and compassion.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to strive for success while maintaining ethical values and supporting one another.
