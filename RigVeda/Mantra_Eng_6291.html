यं विप्राः उक्तवाहसोऽभिप्रमन्दुर आयवः |घृतं न पिप्य आसन्य ऋतस्य यत् ||
yaṁ viprā ukthavāhaso 'bhipramandur āyavaḥ | ghṛtaṁ na pipya āsany ṛtasya yat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • viprā: the learned or wise ones
    • ukthavāhaso: who have spoken or chanted
    • 'bhipramandur: with great intent or purpose
    • āyavaḥ: those who seek or strive
    • ghṛtaṁ: clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment and purity
    • na: not
    • pipya: to drink or consume
    • āsany: to be without or lacking
    • ṛtasya: of truth or righteousness
    • yat: that which
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of wisdom and intention in the pursuit of truth and righteousness.
    • The learned individuals (viprā) who chant or speak with purpose (ukthavāhaso) are encouraged to seek knowledge and understanding.
    • The reference to clarified butter (ghṛtaṁ) symbolizes the essence of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, which should not be consumed without the presence of truth (ṛtasya).
    • It emphasizes that one should not lack the essence of truth in their pursuits, as it is essential for genuine fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who approach their studies with intention and a quest for understanding (like the viprā) are more likely to succeed and gain true knowledge.
    • In professional settings, leaders who communicate with clarity and purpose inspire their teams to strive for excellence, ensuring that their actions are aligned with ethical practices (ṛtasya).
    • In personal relationships, individuals who engage in honest and open communication foster deeper connections, ensuring that their interactions are rooted in truth and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, intention, and truth in all aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that genuine fulfillment and success are achieved when one aligns their actions with righteousness and seeks knowledge with purpose.
    • In a world where distractions and misinformation abound, this mantra encourages individuals to remain steadfast in their pursuit of truth and to nourish their minds and spirits with wisdom.
