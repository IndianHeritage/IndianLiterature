उभे द्यावापृथिवी विश्वमिन्वे आर्यमा देवो अदितिर्विधाता |भगो नृशंस उर्व अन्तरिक्षं विश्वे देवाः पवमानं जुषन्त ||
ubhe dyāvāpṛthivī viśvaminve aryamā devo aditir vidhātā | bhago nṛśaṁsa urv a1ntarikṣaṁ viśve devāḥ pavamānaṁ juṣanta ||
  • Mantra: "ubhe dyāvāpṛthivī viśvaminve aryamā devo aditir vidhātā | bhago nṛśaṁsa urv a1ntarikṣaṁ viśve devāḥ pavamānaṁ juṣanta ||"
  • Translation: "Both the heavens and the earth, may they unite in harmony. Aryama, the divine, the unbounded, is the creator. May the divine blessings of the universe, the benevolent, be enjoyed by all beings."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ubhe: both
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heavens and earth
    • viśvaminve: may they unite in harmony
    • aryamā: a divine being, often associated with the sun
    • devo: the divine
    • aditir: the unbounded or limitless
    • vidhātā: the creator or the one who establishes
    • bhago: blessings or prosperity
    • nṛśaṁsa: benevolent or kind
    • urv: vast or expansive
    • antarikṣaṁ: the space or atmosphere
    • viśve: all or universal
    • devāḥ: the divine beings or gods
    • pavamānaṁ: the purifying or sacred
    • juṣanta: may they enjoy or partake

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of harmony between nature (heavens and earth) and humanity. For instance, in environmental conservation efforts, communities come together to protect natural resources, reflecting the unity of dyāvāpṛthivī.
    • The concept of Aryama as a divine creator can be seen in various cultures where the sun is revered as a life-giving force. For example, many agricultural societies celebrate the sun during harvest festivals, acknowledging its role in sustaining life.
    • The idea of blessings and benevolence can be related to acts of kindness in society, such as community service or charitable work, where individuals and groups strive to uplift others, embodying the spirit of bhago nṛśaṁsa.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, urging us to seek harmony and balance in our lives.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as environmental sustainability, community building, and fostering kindness and compassion among individuals.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, we can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous world, reflecting the divine blessings that the mantra invokes.
