आ जागृविर विप्र ऋता मतीनां सोमः पुनानो असदच चमूषु |सपन्ति यं मिथुनासो निकामा अध्वर्यवो रथिरासः सुहस्ताः ||
ā jāgṛvir vipra ṛtā matīnāṁ somaḥ punāno asadac camūṣu | sapanti yam mithunāso nikāmā adhvaryavo rathirāsaḥ suhastāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā jāgṛvir: "Awakened" or "one who is alert."
    • vipra: "Wise" or "intelligent person."
    • ṛtā: "Truth" or "righteousness."
    • matīnāṁ: "Of the minds" or "of the intellects."
    • somaḥ: "Soma," a sacred drink symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • punāno: "Purifying" or "refreshing."
    • asadac: "In the midst of" or "among the untruths."
    • camūṣu: "In the armies" or "among the groups."
    • sapanti: "They who are united" or "they who are in harmony."
    • yam: "Whom" or "that which."
    • mithunāso: "In pairs" or "couples."
    • nikāmā: "Without desire" or "free from longing."
    • adhvaryavo: "Those who perform rituals" or "sacrificers."
    • rathirāsaḥ: "The charioteer" or "the one who drives the chariot."
    • suhastāḥ: "With good hands" or "skillful."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of being alert and wise in the pursuit of truth and righteousness.
    • The reference to "Soma" signifies the need for spiritual nourishment and inspiration, which can be likened to how individuals seek motivation in their lives through various means, such as education, mentorship, or spiritual practices.
    • The idea of unity and harmony among individuals ("sapanti") suggests that collaboration and teamwork are essential for achieving common goals, much like how successful projects in workplaces require collective effort.
    • The mention of "mithunāso" highlights the significance of partnerships, whether in personal relationships or professional collaborations, where mutual respect and understanding are key.
    • The "adhvaryavo" refers to those who perform rituals, which can be seen as a metaphor for individuals who take responsibility and lead initiatives in society, guiding others towards a common purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the values of wisdom, truth, and unity in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, work collaboratively, and maintain integrity in their actions.
    • Its significance can be observed in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments, where the principles of cooperation, understanding, and righteousness lead to success and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, it inspires us to be vigilant and proactive in our pursuit of truth and harmony, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
