भीताय नाधमानाय ऋषये सप्तवध्रये | मायाभिर अश्विनां युवं वृक्षं सं च वि चाचथः ||
bhītāya nādhamānāya ṛṣaye saptavadhraye | māyābhir aśvinā yuvaṁ vṛkṣaṁ saṁ ca vi cācathaḥ ||
  • bhītāya: This word translates to "to the fearful" or "to those who are afraid." It signifies a state of vulnerability or concern.
  • nādhamānāya: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is in distress" or "to the one who is suffering." It reflects the human condition of facing challenges and hardships.
  • ṛṣaye: This refers to "the sages" or "the wise ones." In the context of the mantra, it acknowledges the guidance and wisdom of those who have attained higher knowledge.
  • saptavadhraye: This term can be understood as "the sevenfold path" or "the seven streams." It symbolizes the various paths or approaches one can take in life.
  • māyābhir: This means "by illusions" or "through the power of illusion." It suggests that life is often influenced by deceptive appearances and distractions.
  • aśvinā: This refers to the twin deities, the Ashvins, who are associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize rejuvenation and healing.
  • yuvaṁ: This translates to "young" or "youthful," indicating vitality, energy, and potential.
  • vṛkṣaṁ: This means "tree," which is often a symbol of life, growth, and stability in various cultures.
  • saṁ ca vi cācathaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "and you have come together" or "and you have united." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration.

The mantra speaks to the human experience of fear and suffering, while also highlighting the wisdom of sages and the various paths available to navigate life's challenges. It acknowledges the illusions that can distract us and calls upon the divine for rejuvenation and healing. The imagery of the tree symbolizes growth and stability, suggesting that despite the trials we face, there is potential for renewal and strength through unity and support from others.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In times of personal crisis, individuals can seek wisdom from mentors or sages who can guide them through their fears and challenges.
  • Communities can come together to support those who are suffering, creating a network of strength and resilience.
  • In the face of illusions or distractions in modern life, individuals can focus on their personal growth and the importance of nurturing their relationships, much like a tree that provides shelter and sustenance.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking wisdom, and the power of community in overcoming fear and adversity. It encourages individuals to embrace their youthful energy and potential while remaining grounded in the wisdom of the past.
