मन्त्र: उता त्यद वां जुरते अश्विनाऽ भूच्यवाना प्रतीयं हविर्दे | अधि यद वर्प इतौति धत्तः ||
uta tyad vāṁ jurate aśvinā bhūc cyavānāya pratītyaṁ havirde | adhi yad varpa itaūti dhatthaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • tyad: that, which
    • vāṁ: you, your
    • jurate: may you be nourished
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • bhūc: may it be, may it happen
    • cyavānāya: for the one who is falling, for the one who is in distress
    • pratītyaṁ: having understood, having realized
    • havirde: the offerings, the invocations
    • adhi: upon, over
    • yad: that which
    • varpa: the sacrifice, the offering
    • itaūti: may it be accepted, may it be fulfilled
    • dhatthaḥ: you have given, you have provided
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing powers and ability to restore health.
    • The phrase "may you be nourished" suggests a plea for sustenance and support during difficult times.
    • The reference to "the one who is falling" indicates a recognition of vulnerability and the need for assistance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and invocations, suggesting that gratitude and acknowledgment of divine support are crucial.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of illness or distress, individuals often seek help from healthcare professionals, akin to invoking the Ashvins for healing.
    • During challenging life situations, people may turn to spiritual practices or rituals, offering prayers and gratitude, similar to the concept of making offerings in this mantra.
    • Communities often come together to support those in need, reflecting the idea of nourishing and uplifting those who are struggling.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine assistance during times of need and the importance of community support.
    • It serves as a reminder to acknowledge and express gratitude for the help we receive, whether from the divine or from those around us.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to be more compassionate and supportive towards others who are facing difficulties, fostering a sense of unity and care.
