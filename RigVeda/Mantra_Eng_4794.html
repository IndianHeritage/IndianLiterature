न घा वसुर न यमते दानं वाजस्य गोमताḥ |यत सिम उप श्रवद गिरः ||
na ghā vasur ni yamate dānaṁ vājasya gomataḥ | yat sīm upa śravad giraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • ghā: to harm
    • vasur: wealth or riches
    • ni: in the sense of 'to' or 'towards'
    • yamate: to control or restrain
    • dānaṁ: giving or donation
    • vājasya: of the one who is strong or powerful
    • gomataḥ: of the cow, which symbolizes nourishment and wealth
    • yat: that which
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • upa: near or towards
    • śravad: flowing or streaming
    • giraḥ: words or speech
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of not harming wealth or riches.
    • It suggests that true strength lies in the ability to control one's desires and to give generously.
    • The cow, a symbol of nourishment, represents the wealth that should be cherished and protected.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of words and communication, indicating that what we say can have a profound impact on our lives and the lives of others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to ethical practices. For instance, a company that prioritizes fair trade and sustainable practices is not harming its wealth but rather nurturing it.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of giving and not harming can be seen in acts of kindness, where individuals support each other without expecting anything in return.
    • In community service, donating time or resources to help those in need reflects the essence of this mantra, as it promotes the idea of sharing wealth and resources for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the ethical responsibilities that come with wealth and power.
    • It encourages individuals to practice generosity and to communicate thoughtfully, recognizing the impact of their words.
    • In a world where materialism often overshadows compassion, this mantra is a call to action to cultivate a mindset of giving and to respect the wealth that sustains us.
