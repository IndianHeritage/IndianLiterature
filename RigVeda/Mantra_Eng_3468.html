श्रेष्टं वः पेसो अधि धायि दर्शतं स्तोमो वाजाः ऋभवस्तं जुजुष्टन |धीरा सो हि ष्ठा कवयो विपश्चितस्तान व एनां ब्रह्मणा वेदयामसि ||
śreṣṭhaṁ vaḥ peśo adhi dhāyi darśataṁ stomo vājā ṛbhavas taṁ jujuṣṭana | dhīrāso hi ṣṭhā kavayo vipaścitas tān va enā brahmaṇā vedayāmasi ||
  • śreṣṭhaṁ: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the ultimate goal.
  • vaḥ: This means "of you" or "for you," indicating a personal connection or offering.
  • peśo: Refers to "the one who is skilled" or "the one who has expertise." It can relate to a person who possesses knowledge or talent.
  • adhi: This means "above" or "over," suggesting a position of superiority or elevation.
  • dhāyi: Translates to "the one who supports" or "the one who holds." It implies a nurturing or protective role.
  • darśataṁ: This means "to be seen" or "to be visible," indicating the importance of perception and understanding.
  • stomo: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," highlighting the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • vājā: This translates to "strength" or "power," often associated with the idea of victory or success.
  • ṛbhavas: Means "the wise" or "the enlightened," referring to those who possess deep understanding and insight.
  • taṁ: This means "that" or "him," referring to a specific entity or concept being addressed.
  • jujuṣṭana: This translates to "we desire" or "we seek," indicating a longing or aspiration towards something greater.
  • dhīrāso: Refers to "the wise" or "the thoughtful," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in decision-making.
  • hi: This is a particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • ṣṭhā: This means "to stand" or "to be established," indicating stability and permanence.
  • kavayo: Refers to "the poets" or "the seers," highlighting the role of creativity and insight in understanding life.
  • vipaścitas: This means "the knowledgeable" or "the wise," referring to those who have deep understanding and perception.
  • tān: This means "them" or "those," referring to the wise individuals mentioned earlier.
  • va: This means "and" or "also," connecting ideas or concepts.
  • enā: This translates to "by this" or "through this," indicating a means or method.
  • brahmaṇā: Refers to "the ultimate reality" or "the divine," emphasizing the connection to a higher power.
  • vedayāmasi: This means "we know" or "we understand," indicating a quest for knowledge and enlightenment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the pursuit of excellence, wisdom, and understanding. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and to recognize the importance of skilled and wise individuals in society. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and personal development. By striving for excellence and valuing wisdom, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and foster a culture of learning and growth. The mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of knowledge and the pursuit of higher ideals in our lives.
