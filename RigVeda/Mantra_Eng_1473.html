नेदिष्टे अस्मिन्न अहन्य अधि वोचाऽ नु सुन्वते |अस्मिन यज्ञे विचयेमाऽ भरे कृतं वाजयन्तो भरे कृतम् ||
nediṣṭhe asminn ahany adhi vocā nu sunvate | asmin yajñe vi cayemā bhare kṛtaṁ vājayanto bhare kṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nediṣṭhe: In this context, it refers to the act of offering or presenting.
    • asminn: This means "in this" or "in this place."
    • ahany: This translates to "daily" or "every day."
    • adhi: This means "above" or "over."
    • vocā: This can be interpreted as "to speak" or "to declare."
    • nu: This is often used as an interrogative particle, meaning "indeed" or "truly."
    • sunvate: This means "to hear" or "to listen."
    • asmin: Again, this means "in this" or "in this place."
    • yajñe: This refers to a "sacrifice" or "ritual."
    • vi: This can mean "in" or "within."
    • cayemā: This translates to "we may attain" or "we may achieve."
    • bhare: This means "to bring" or "to carry."
    • kṛtaṁ: This translates to "that which is done" or "the deed."
    • vājayanto: This means "those who bring strength" or "those who provide vigor."
    • bhare: Repeated, meaning "to bring" or "to carry."
    • kṛtam: Repeated, meaning "that which is done" or "the deed."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of daily offerings and rituals.
    • It suggests that through these acts, one can achieve strength and fulfillment.
    • The act of speaking and listening in the context of rituals signifies the importance of communication with the divine.
    • It highlights the collective effort in rituals, where the community comes together to achieve a common goal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to daily practices such as meditation or prayer, where individuals seek strength and guidance.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, reflect the collective effort to achieve a common purpose, similar to the yajña mentioned in the mantra.
    • In workplaces, team meetings can be seen as a form of yajña, where members come together to share ideas and strengthen their collective goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of daily rituals and community efforts in achieving personal and collective strength.
    • It teaches us that through consistent practice and communication, we can attain our goals and fulfill our duties.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to engage in activities that foster unity and strength, whether in personal life, community, or professional settings.
