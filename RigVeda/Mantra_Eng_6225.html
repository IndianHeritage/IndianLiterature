दिवश्चिद्रोचनादध्यानो गन्तं स्वरविदा। धीभिर्वत्सप्रचेतसा स्तोमेभिरहवनश्रुता॥
divaś cid rocanād adhy ā no gantaṁ svarvidā | dhībhir vatsapracetasā stomebhir havanaśrutā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divaś: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • Cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • Rocanād: From the brilliance or radiance.
    • Adhy: Upon or towards.
    • Āno: To us or towards us.
    • Gantaṁ: The goal or the destination.
    • Svarvidā: Knowledge of the celestial or divine.
    • Dhībhir: With intellect or understanding.
    • Vatsapracetasā: With the wisdom of a calf or young one.
    • Stomebhir: With hymns or praises.
    • Havanaśrutā: Known through offerings or invocations.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the celestial consciousness and brilliance, asking for guidance towards a higher understanding.
    • The phrase "knowledge of the celestial" suggests a pursuit of wisdom that transcends the mundane, akin to seeking enlightenment in various spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "the wisdom of a calf" symbolizes innocence and purity, indicating that true understanding often comes from a place of simplicity and openness.
    • Using hymns and praises signifies the importance of devotion and gratitude in the journey towards knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the pursuit of knowledge can be likened to this mantra, where students are encouraged to seek understanding with an open mind, much like the innocence of a young calf.
    • In spiritual practices, meditation and prayer can be seen as a way to connect with the divine consciousness, similar to the invocation of celestial knowledge in the mantra.
    • In personal development, the idea of approaching life with gratitude and humility can lead to greater insights and wisdom, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking higher knowledge and understanding through purity of heart and mind.
    • It teaches us that the journey towards enlightenment is not just about intellectual pursuit but also about cultivating a sense of devotion and gratitude.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, deeper connections with others, and a more profound understanding of the universe.
