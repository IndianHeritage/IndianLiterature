उरुष्याणो अभिशस्तेः सोम निपाह्य अम्हसः |सखा सुशेवा एधि नः ||
uruṣyā ṇo abhiśasteḥ soma ni pāhy aṁhasaḥ | sakhā suśeva edhi naḥ ||
  • uruṣyā: This word can be interpreted as "of the man" or "of the human." It signifies the essence of humanity and the connection between individuals.
  • ṇo: This is a form of the pronoun "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • abhiśasteḥ: This term refers to "protection" or "safeguarding." It emphasizes the need for security and support in life.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes nourishment for the soul and spirit.
  • ni: This is a preposition that can mean "down" or "towards," indicating direction or focus.
  • pāhy: This verb means "to protect" or "to guard." It reinforces the theme of seeking safety and care.
  • aṁhasaḥ: This word can be translated as "from the darkness" or "from ignorance." It suggests a desire to move towards enlightenment and understanding.
  • sakhā: This means "friend" or "companion," highlighting the importance of relationships and support systems in life.
  • suśeva: This term translates to "good fortune" or "auspiciousness," indicating a wish for prosperity and well-being.
  • edhi: This verb means "to grow" or "to thrive," suggesting a desire for development and progress.
  • naḥ: This means "us" or "our," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra "uruṣyā ṇo abhiśasteḥ soma ni pāhy aṁhasaḥ | sakhā suśeva edhi naḥ" encapsulates a profound message about the importance of community, protection, and spiritual nourishment. It emphasizes the need for safeguarding one another, seeking enlightenment, and fostering relationships that lead to prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, friendships, and community support systems. It serves as a reminder that we thrive best when we care for each other and seek collective growth and well-being. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious environment that nurtures both personal and communal development.
