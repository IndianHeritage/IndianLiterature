द्रप्सः समुद्रम अभि यज जिगाति पश्यन् गृध्रस्य चक्षसा विधर्मन् |भानुः शुक्रेण शोचिषा चकानस् तृतीये चक्रे रजसि प्रियाणि ||
drapsaḥ samudram abhi yaj jigāti paśyan gṛdhrasya cakṣasā vidharman | bhānuḥ śukreṇa śociṣā cakānas tṛtīye cakre rajasi priyāṇi ||
  • Mantra: "drapsaḥ samudram abhi yaj jigāti paśyan gṛdhrasya cakṣasā vidharman | bhānuḥ śukreṇa śociṣā cakānas tṛtīye cakre rajasi priyāṇi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • drapsaḥ: The one who is seeking or desiring.
    • samudram: The ocean or vastness.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • yaj: To sacrifice or to worship.
    • jigāti: To move or to progress.
    • paśyan: Seeing or perceiving.
    • gṛdhrasya: Of the vulture, symbolizing a higher perspective.
    • cakṣasā: With the eye or vision.
    • vidharman: The righteous path or duty.
    • bhānuḥ: The sun or light.
    • śukreṇa: With purity or brightness.
    • śociṣā: Illuminating or enlightening.
    • cakānas: To shine or to radiate.
    • tṛtīye: In the third (contextually referring to a cycle or phase).
    • cakre: In the wheel or cycle.
    • rajasi: In the realm of action or activity.
    • priyāṇi: The beloved or cherished things.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of a seeker who desires to understand the vastness of existence (samudram) and is willing to engage in worship (yaj) to progress (jigāti) towards enlightenment.
    • The reference to the vulture (gṛdhrasya) suggests a need for a higher perspective, as vultures soar high and have a broad view of their surroundings, symbolizing the importance of seeing beyond the immediate.
    • The mantra emphasizes the significance of following a righteous path (vidharman) while being guided by the light of the sun (bhānuḥ), which represents knowledge and purity (śukreṇa).
    • The mention of the third cycle (tṛtīye cakre) indicates that this journey is part of a larger process, where actions (rajasi) lead to the realization of cherished values (priyāṇi).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may seek to advance their career (drapsaḥ) by engaging in continuous learning (yaj) and networking (jigāti), while maintaining ethical standards (vidharman).
    • In personal relationships, one may strive to understand their loved ones better (paśyan) by adopting a broader perspective (gṛdhrasya) and communicating openly (bhānuḥ).
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often meditate (yaj) to gain insights (jigāti) and align themselves with their higher purpose (vidharman), guided by inner wisdom (bhānuḥ).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our lives while remaining grounded in righteousness and ethical conduct.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of our actions and the broader cycles of life, encouraging us to pursue our desires with a sense of purpose and clarity.
    • In essence, it teaches us that true progress comes from a combination of desire, ethical living, and the pursuit of higher knowledge, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional endeavors.
