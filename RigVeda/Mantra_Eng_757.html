तक्वा न भूर्णिर्वना सिषक्ति पयो न धेनुः शुचिर विभावा ||
takvā na bhūrṇir vanā siṣakti payo na dhenuḥ śucir vibhāvā ||
  • takvā: This word can be interpreted as "to give up" or "to renounce." It signifies the act of letting go of attachments or desires that may hinder spiritual growth.
  • na: A negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the importance of what follows in the mantra.
  • bhūrṇir: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It suggests that true wealth is not merely material but also spiritual and ethical.
  • vanā: Meaning "forest" or "nature," it symbolizes the natural world and the importance of living in harmony with it.
  • siṣakti: This word translates to "power" or "energy." It indicates the inherent strength and potential within all beings.
  • payo: Meaning "milk," it represents nourishment, purity, and sustenance. Milk is often seen as a symbol of nurturing and care.
  • na: Again, a negation, reinforcing the importance of the subsequent terms.
  • dhenuḥ: This term means "cow," which is a sacred symbol in many cultures, particularly in Hinduism, representing abundance and motherhood.
  • śucir: Translated as "pure," it signifies cleanliness, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
  • vibhāvā: This word can be understood as "manifestation" or "expression," indicating the realization of one's potential and virtues.

The mantra "takvā na bhūrṇir vanā siṣakti payo na dhenuḥ śucir vibhāvā" emphasizes the importance of renouncing material wealth and instead focusing on spiritual and ethical values. It suggests that true power and nourishment come from purity and living in harmony with nature.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, individuals may choose to let go of materialistic pursuits and focus on inner growth, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • In environmental conservation, recognizing the importance of nature and living sustainably can lead to a healthier planet.
  • In relationships, prioritizing purity of intention and nurturing connections can foster deeper bonds and understanding.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek true wealth in spiritual and ethical dimensions rather than in material possessions. It encourages individuals to cultivate purity, recognize their inherent power, and live harmoniously with nature, ultimately leading to a more meaningful and enriched life.
