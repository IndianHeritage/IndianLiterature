अधि द्वयोः अदधाऽ उक्त्यं वचो यतस्रुचाऽ मिथुनाऽ या सपर्ण्यतः |असंयत्तो व्रते ते क्षेति पुष्यति भद्रा शक्तिर यजमानाय सुन्वते ||
adhi dvayor adadhā ukthya1ṁ vaco yatasrucā mithunā yā saparyataḥ | asaṁyatto vrate te kṣeti puṣyati bhadrā śaktir yajamānāya sunvate ||
  • Adhi dvayor adadhā ukthyaṁ vaco: This phrase can be interpreted as "In the presence of two, the utterance is made." It signifies the importance of collective intention and speech. For example, in a team meeting, when two or more individuals come together to express their thoughts, the outcome is often more fruitful than when one person speaks alone.
  • Yatasrucā mithunā yā saparyataḥ: This translates to "With the flow of the sacred, they are united in worship." It emphasizes the idea of unity in devotion and purpose. In real life, this can be seen in community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, where people come together to celebrate and worship, creating a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
  • Asaṁyatto vrate te kṣeti: This means "Unrestrained in their vows, they flourish." It highlights the significance of commitment and dedication. For instance, a person who is dedicated to their fitness goals, consistently working out and eating healthily, will see positive results over time, demonstrating the power of unwavering commitment.
  • Puṣyati bhadrā śaktir yajamānāya sunvate: This can be interpreted as "The auspicious power nourishes those who perform the sacrifice." It suggests that those who engage in selfless acts or sacrifices for the greater good will be rewarded. An example of this is volunteers who dedicate their time to help others; their efforts often lead to personal fulfillment and community improvement.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, commitment, and the power of collective action. It teaches us that when individuals come together with a shared purpose and dedication, they can create positive change in their lives and communities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal relationships. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that our collective efforts, when driven by commitment and a shared vision, can lead to prosperity and well-being for all involved.
