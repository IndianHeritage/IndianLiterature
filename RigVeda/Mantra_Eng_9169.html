मन्त्र: इन्द्रो बर्हिः सिदतु पिन्वताम् इळा बृहस्पतिः सामभिर ऋक्वो अर्चतु | सुप्रकेतं जीवसे मनमा धीमहि तद् देवानाम् अवो अद्य वृणीमहे ||
endro barhiḥ sīdatu pinvatām iḻā bṛhaspatiḥ sāmabhir ṛkvo arcatu | supraketaṁ jīvase manma dhīmahi tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Endro barhiḥ sīdatu: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the blessings of Indra, the king of the gods, to be present. "Endro" refers to Indra, while "barhiḥ" signifies the sacred grass used in rituals. The invocation suggests a desire for divine presence and support.
  • Pinvatām iḻā: "Pinvatām" means to be filled or imbued, and "iḻā" refers to the essence or the divine energy. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of being filled with divine energy and blessings, which can be related to seeking inspiration and strength in our daily lives.
  • Bṛhaspatiḥ sāmabhir ṛkvo arcatu: Here, "Bṛhaspati" is the guru of the gods and represents wisdom and knowledge. "Sāmabhir ṛkvo" refers to hymns or praises. This line calls for the wisdom of Bṛhaspati to be invoked through hymns, highlighting the significance of knowledge and guidance in our lives.
  • Supraketaṁ jīvase: "Supraketaṁ" means well-formed or well-structured, and "jīvase" refers to life. This suggests a desire for a well-structured and meaningful life, which can be related to the importance of planning and purpose in our personal and professional endeavors.
  • Manma dhīmahi: This phrase translates to "let us meditate upon" or "let us contemplate." It encourages reflection and mindfulness, which are essential practices in today's fast-paced world. Taking time to meditate can lead to better decision-making and emotional well-being.
  • Tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe: This final part translates to "we choose that which is for the gods today." It signifies the importance of making choices that align with higher values and principles, encouraging individuals to act in ways that are beneficial not only to themselves but also to the community and the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual and practical message. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, wisdom, and mindfulness in leading a fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by seeking guidance from mentors (like Bṛhaspati), engaging in reflective practices (like meditation), and making choices that resonate with our higher selves and the greater good. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose, ultimately contributing to a harmonious existence.
