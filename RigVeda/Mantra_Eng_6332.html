यदि मे सख्यम् आवर इमस्य पाहि अन्धसः |येन विश्वा अतिद्विषो अतारिम ||
yadi me sakhyam āvara imasya pāhy andhasaḥ | yena viśvā ati dviṣo atārima ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadi: if
    • me: my
    • sakhyam: friendship or companionship
    • āvara: cover or protect
    • imasya: of this
    • pāhy: may you protect
    • andhasaḥ: from darkness or ignorance
    • yena: by which
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • ati: beyond
    • dviṣo: enemies or those who oppose
    • atārima: may I cross or overcome
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection and companionship.
    • The speaker seeks the safeguarding of their friendships and relationships from negativity and ignorance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and support in overcoming adversities and challenges posed by opposition or enmity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when team members support each other to overcome challenges posed by competition or conflict.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the need for friends to stand by each other during tough times, shielding one another from negative influences.
    • In community settings, it can be seen when groups come together to protect their interests against external threats, fostering a sense of solidarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of friendship and mutual support in navigating life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that together, individuals can overcome darkness and opposition, fostering a sense of community and resilience.
    • In real life, applying the essence of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, better teamwork, and a more supportive environment, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
