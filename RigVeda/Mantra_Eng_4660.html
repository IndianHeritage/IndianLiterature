मन्त्र: न हि नु ते महिमनाḥ समस्या न मगहन मगहत्त्वस्य विद्मा | न राधसो-राधसो नूतनस्येन्द्र नकिर्ददृश इन्द्रियं ते ||
nahi nu te mahimanaḥ samasya na maghavan maghavattvasya vidma | na rādhaso-rādhaso nūtanasyendra nakir dadṛśa indriyaṁ te ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, we do not know your greatness, O Maghavan (Indra), nor do we know the extent of your power. We do not see your divine form, O Indra, nor do we perceive your senses."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi nu: Indeed, we do not
    • te: your
    • mahimanaḥ: greatness or glory
    • samasyā: extent or measure
    • maghavan: O Maghavan (a name for Indra, the king of gods)
    • maghavat: powerful or mighty
    • tvasya: of your
    • vidma: we know
    • rādhaso: of the divine form
    • nūtanasyendra: O Indra, the new or fresh
    • nakir: we do not see
    • dadṛśa: perceive or behold
    • indriyaṁ: senses or faculties
    • te: your
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra reflects a deep sense of humility and reverence towards the divine, particularly towards Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • It acknowledges the limitations of human understanding when it comes to the divine and the vastness of the universe.
    • In real life, this can be seen in moments of awe when we encounter nature's beauty or power, such as during a thunderstorm or witnessing a breathtaking landscape.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • When standing at the edge of the Grand Canyon, one might feel a sense of insignificance in the face of such immense natural beauty, echoing the sentiment of not fully grasping the greatness of the divine.
    • In scientific exploration, as we uncover the mysteries of the universe, we often realize that there is still so much we do not understand, similar to the acknowledgment in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the limits of human perception and the need for humility in the face of the divine and the mysteries of existence.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge while recognizing that some aspects of life and the universe may remain beyond our comprehension.
    • In daily life, this can inspire a sense of wonder and curiosity, prompting us to explore and appreciate the world around us while maintaining respect for its complexities.
