कुविन मां गोपां करसे जनस्य कुविद राजानं मगवन्न ऋजीषिन् |कुविन मा ऋषिम पपिवांसं सुतस्य कुविन मे वस्वो अमृतस्य शिक्षाः ||
kuvin mā gopāṁ karase janasya kuvid rājānam maghavann ṛjīṣin | kuvin ma ṛṣim papivāṁsaṁ sutasya kuvin me vasvo amṛtasya śikṣāḥ ||
  • kuvin mā gopāṁ karase janasya: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine to protect and guide the people. The term "kuvin" suggests a seeker of knowledge or wisdom, while "gopāṁ" refers to the guardians or protectors, often associated with cattle herders in Vedic culture. In real life, this can be likened to leaders or mentors who guide communities.
  • kuvid rājānam maghavann ṛjīṣin: Here, "kuvid" refers to the one who is wise or knowledgeable, and "rājānam" means king or ruler. "Maghavann" implies a powerful or prosperous being, and "ṛjīṣin" suggests one who is righteous. This can be related to the importance of having wise and just leaders in society, akin to modern-day leaders who prioritize the welfare of their citizens.
  • kuvin ma ṛṣim papivāṁsaṁ sutasya: This line emphasizes the importance of learning from sages or wise individuals ("ṛṣim"). "Papivāṁsaṁ" indicates the act of drinking or absorbing knowledge. In contemporary terms, this can be compared to the value of education and mentorship, where individuals seek wisdom from experienced figures in their fields.
  • kuvin me vasvo amṛtasya śikṣāḥ: The final part speaks of the teachings ("śikṣāḥ") of immortality or eternal truth ("amṛtasya"). "Vasvo" can be interpreted as the essence or spirit. This highlights the pursuit of eternal knowledge and truth, which is relevant in today's world where individuals seek deeper understanding and purpose in life.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of wisdom, guidance, and the pursuit of knowledge. It underscores the importance of having wise leaders and mentors in society who can protect and guide the community. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational institutions where teachers play a crucial role in shaping the minds of students, or in governance where leaders are expected to act justly and wisely for the benefit of their people. The mantra serves as a reminder of the timeless value of seeking knowledge and the importance of righteous leadership in fostering a harmonious society.
