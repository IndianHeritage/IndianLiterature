मन्त्र: प्रेताः जयतां नर इन्द्रो वः शर्म यच्छतु |उग्राः वः सन्तु बाहवोऽनाधृष्याः यथासथ ||
pretā jayatā nara indro vaḥ śarma yacchatu | ugrā vaḥ santu bāhavo 'nādhṛṣyā yathāsatha ||
  • Translation: "May the spirits (pretā) conquer; may Indra, the king of men, grant us protection (śarma). May your strong arms (bāhavo) be invincible (ugrā) and may you not be disheartened (nādhṛṣyā) as you wish."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pretā: Refers to spirits or the departed souls, symbolizing the connection between the living and the dead.
    • Jayatā: Means to conquer or to be victorious, indicating a desire for triumph over challenges.
    • Nara: Translates to 'man' or 'human', emphasizing the human experience and struggles.
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and protection.
    • Śarma: Signifies protection, comfort, or shelter, highlighting the need for safety in life.
    • Ugrā: Means strong or fierce, suggesting the importance of resilience and strength.
    • Bāhavo: Refers to arms or strength, indicating the physical and mental capabilities one possesses.
    • Nādhṛṣyā: Means not to be disheartened or discouraged, emphasizing the importance of perseverance.
    • Yathāsatha: Translates to 'as you wish', indicating a sense of agency and personal desire.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal loss, invoking the spirits (pretā) can be a way to seek guidance and strength from those who have passed, helping individuals to cope with grief.
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace or sports, the mantra can inspire individuals to conquer their challenges and strive for victory, much like athletes who train hard to overcome their opponents.
    • During difficult times, such as economic hardships or personal struggles, seeking protection (śarma) from a higher power or community can provide comfort and a sense of security.
    • In moments of self-doubt, reminding oneself of their inner strength (ugrā) and capabilities (bāhavo) can empower individuals to face their fears and challenges head-on.
    • Encouraging resilience (nādhṛṣyā) in the face of adversity is crucial, as seen in stories of individuals who have overcome significant obstacles to achieve their dreams.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, protection, and resilience in the face of life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that, despite the presence of spirits and the weight of the past, individuals have the power to conquer their struggles and pursue their desires. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, from personal growth to community support, emphasizing the importance of inner strength and the collective power of humanity in overcoming obstacles.
