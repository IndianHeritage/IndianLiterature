सुतासो मधुमत्तमाḥ सोमā इन्द्राय मन्दिनः |पवित्रवन्तो अक्षरान देवān गच्छन्तु वो मदाः ||
sutāso madhumattamāḥ somā indrāya mandinaḥ | pavitravanto akṣaran devān gacchantu vo madāḥ ||
  • Translation: "The sweet, most excellent ones, the Somas, are for Indra, the one who is full of joy. May the pure, imperishable ones, the gods, come to you with their delight."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sutāso: Refers to the sweet or delightful ones, often associated with the intoxicating drink Soma.
    • Madhumattamāḥ: Means the most excellent or the best among the sweet ones.
    • Somā: Refers to the Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Indrāya: Pertains to Indra, the king of gods, representing strength, power, and the force of nature.
    • Mandinaḥ: Means full of joy or happiness, indicating the bliss associated with divine presence.
    • Pavitravanto: Refers to the pure or sacred ones, emphasizing the sanctity of the gods.
    • Akṣaran: Means imperishable or eternal, highlighting the everlasting nature of the divine.
    • Devān: Refers to the gods, the divine beings that are invoked in the mantra.
    • Gacchantu: Means may they come or arrive, indicating a request for divine presence.
    • Vo: Means to you, addressing the audience or the worshippers.
    • Madāḥ: Refers to delight or joy, suggesting the blissful state that the divine brings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a celebratory gathering, such as a wedding or festival, people often invoke blessings for joy and happiness, similar to how the mantra calls for divine delight.
    • During moments of achievement or success, individuals may reflect on their journey and express gratitude, akin to the invocation of the gods for their presence and joy.
    • In meditation or spiritual practices, practitioners seek purity and connection with the divine, resonating with the essence of the mantra's call for the pure and imperishable gods.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine presence and joy in our lives. It signifies the connection between the material and spiritual realms, reminding us to seek purity and delight in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, celebrations, and personal reflections, encouraging individuals to invite positivity and divine blessings into their lives. By recognizing the significance of joy and purity, we can cultivate a more fulfilling and spiritually enriched existence.
