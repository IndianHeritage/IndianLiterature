तेभ्यो द्युम्नं बृहद् यश उषो मघोन्य आवह |ये नो राधांस्य अश्व्या गव्याः भजन्त सूरयः सुजाते अश्वसूनृत ||
tebhyo dyumnam bṛhad yaśa uṣo maghony ā vaha | ye no rādhāṁsy aśvyā gavyā bhajanta sūrayaḥ sujāte aśvasūnṛte ||
  • tebhyo: This word translates to "from them" or "to them," indicating a connection or relationship with a group or entity.
  • dyumnam: This means "glory" or "brightness," referring to the radiance or fame that one can achieve.
  • bṛhad: This translates to "great" or "vast," suggesting something of significant magnitude or importance.
  • yaśa: This word means "fame" or "renown," highlighting the importance of reputation and recognition in society.
  • uṣo: This refers to "dawn" or "morning," symbolizing new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light.
  • maghony: This translates to "abundance" or "wealth," indicating prosperity and the blessings of material and spiritual wealth.
  • ā vaha: This means "bring forth" or "carry," suggesting a call to action or a request for something to be delivered or manifested.
  • ye: This word means "those who," referring to a specific group of people or entities.
  • no: This translates to "our," indicating possession or belonging.
  • rādhāṁsy: This means "prosperity" or "wealth," emphasizing the importance of abundance in life.
  • aśvyā: This refers to "horses," which in Vedic literature symbolize strength, speed, and power.
  • gavyā: This translates to "cows," representing nourishment, wealth, and sustenance in ancient Indian culture.
  • bhajanta: This means "worship" or "serve," indicating devotion and reverence towards the divine or higher powers.
  • sūrayaḥ: This translates to "the wise" or "the learned," referring to those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • sujāte: This means "well-born" or "noble," indicating a sense of honor and virtue.
  • aśvasūnṛte: This can be interpreted as "in the truth of horses," suggesting a connection to strength and honesty in one's actions.

The mantra essentially calls upon the divine forces to bestow glory, fame, and abundance upon those who serve and worship with devotion. It emphasizes the importance of new beginnings, prosperity, and the strength that comes from both material and spiritual wealth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their goals and aspirations, seeking to achieve greatness and recognition in their respective fields.
  • In business, the mantra can inspire entrepreneurs to pursue abundance and prosperity through ethical practices and dedication to their work.
  • In community service, it can motivate individuals to serve others, fostering a sense of unity and shared prosperity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings on those who strive for greatness while serving others. It serves as a reminder that true wealth comes from a combination of personal achievement and communal well-being, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and purpose in their lives.
