स्पार्हा यस्य श्रीयो दृष्टे रयिर वीरवतः यथा |अग्रे यज्ञस्य शोचतः ||
spārhā yasya śriyo dṛśe rayir vīravato yathā | agre yajñasya śocataḥ ||
  • spārhā: This word translates to "desire" or "aspiration." It signifies the longing for something valuable or significant in life.
  • yasya: Meaning "of whom," this word indicates possession or belonging.
  • śriyo: This term refers to "prosperity" or "wealth." It embodies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • dṛśe: Translated as "to the sight" or "to the vision," it emphasizes perception and understanding.
  • rayir: This word means "wealth" or "abundance," often associated with resources and prosperity.
  • vīravato: Meaning "of the brave" or "of the valiant," it highlights courage and strength in the pursuit of goals.
  • yathā: This translates to "as" or "like," indicating a comparison or manner of action.
  • agre: Meaning "in front" or "before," it suggests precedence or priority.
  • yajñasya: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," often associated with offerings made to deities for blessings.
  • śocataḥ: Translated as "of the one who is lamenting" or "of the one who is grieving," it indicates a state of sorrow or yearning.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to aspire for prosperity and abundance through courage and vision, especially in the context of sacrifices or efforts made for a greater good. It emphasizes the importance of having a clear vision and the bravery to pursue one's desires while acknowledging the sacrifices that may be necessary along the way.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Career Aspirations: Individuals seeking success in their careers can draw inspiration from this mantra. By having a clear vision of their goals and the courage to pursue them, they can achieve prosperity.
  • Entrepreneurship: Entrepreneurs often face challenges and sacrifices. This mantra encourages them to remain brave and focused on their vision, leading to eventual success and abundance.
  • Personal Growth: In the journey of self-improvement, one must aspire for emotional and spiritual wealth. This mantra serves as a reminder to pursue personal goals with courage and clarity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to aspire for wealth and prosperity through courage and vision. It teaches that while the path may involve sacrifices, the pursuit of one's desires with a brave heart can lead to abundant rewards. This mantra is applicable in various aspects of life, including career, personal growth, and community service, reminding us to remain focused on our goals while being mindful of the efforts required to achieve them.
