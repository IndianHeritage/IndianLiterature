तं वृधन्तं मारुतं भ्राजदृष्टिं रुद्रस्य सूनुं हवसा विवासे |दिवः शर्धाय शुचयो मनिषा गिरयो नाप उग्राऽस्पृध्रन ||
taṁ vṛdhantam mārutam bhrājadṛṣṭiṁ rudrasya sūnuṁ havasā vivāse | divaḥ śardhāya śucayo manīṣā girayo nāpa ugrā aspṛdhran ||
  • taṁ vṛdhantam: This phrase can be translated as "that which is growing" or "that which is expanding." In real life, this can refer to personal growth, such as the development of skills or knowledge over time.
  • mārutam: This term refers to the wind or the forces of nature. It symbolizes the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life. For example, just as the wind can change direction, our lives can take unexpected turns.
  • bhrājadṛṣṭiṁ: This means "brilliant vision" or "illuminated sight." It signifies clarity of thought and perception. In practical terms, this can relate to having a clear vision for one's future or goals, akin to a business leader who sees opportunities where others do not.
  • rudrasya sūnuṁ: This translates to "the son of Rudra," referring to a divine figure associated with storms and transformation. In life, this can represent the challenges we face that lead to personal transformation, much like how a storm can clear the air and bring new growth.
  • havasā vivāse: This phrase means "may we be united in our offerings." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where collective efforts lead to greater achievements, such as in a successful project at work.
  • divaḥ śardhāya: This translates to "for the sake of the heavens." It suggests a higher purpose or aspiration. In everyday life, this can relate to pursuing goals that contribute to the greater good, such as environmental sustainability efforts.
  • śucayo manīṣā: This means "pure thoughts" or "noble intentions." It highlights the importance of integrity and ethical behavior. In practice, this can be seen in individuals who strive to act with honesty and fairness in their personal and professional lives.
  • girayo nāpa ugrā aspṛdhran: This phrase can be interpreted as "the mountains do not touch the fierce." It symbolizes resilience and strength in the face of adversity. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who remain steadfast during challenging times, such as a community coming together after a natural disaster.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of growth, transformation, community, and resilience. Its significance lies in its call for unity and noble intentions while navigating the challenges of life. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to pursue personal and collective growth, maintain clarity of vision, and act with integrity, ultimately contributing to a harmonious and progressive society.
