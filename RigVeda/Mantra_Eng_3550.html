इन्द्रश्च वायवेषां सोमानां पीतिमर्हतः |युवां हि यन्तीन्दवो निम्नमापो न सध्र्यक ||
indraś ca vāyav eṣāṁ somānām pītim arhathaḥ | yuvāṁ hi yantīndavo nimnam āpo na sadhryak ||
  • Indraś ca vāyav eṣāṁ somānām pītim arhathaḥ
    • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
    • vāyav: This term refers to the wind or air, symbolizing movement and the unseen forces of nature.
    • eṣāṁ: This means "of these," indicating a connection to the subjects being discussed.
    • somānām: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • pītim: This translates to "nourishment" or "satisfaction," suggesting the fulfillment derived from divine blessings.
    • arhathaḥ: This means "you are worthy" or "you deserve," indicating a call for recognition of the divine forces at play.
  • yuvāṁ hi yantīndavo nimnam āpo na sadhryak
    • yuvāṁ: This means "you two," referring to the duality of forces, possibly Indra and Vayu (the wind).
    • hi: This is an emphatic particle, reinforcing the statement that follows.
    • yantīndavo: This can be interpreted as "the two who lead," referring to the guiding forces of Indra and Vayu.
    • nimnam: This means "downward" or "lower," indicating a connection to the earth or the material world.
    • āpo: This refers to "waters," symbolizing life, sustenance, and the flow of energy.
    • na sadhryak: This translates to "not in abundance," suggesting a caution against excess or imbalance.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and human existence. It calls for the recognition of the nourishment provided by these forces, particularly through the sacred Soma. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of balance in our lives—between power and humility, between the material and the spiritual. Just as Indra and Vayu work together to sustain life through rain and wind, we too must find harmony in our relationships and environments. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the blessings we receive and to maintain a respectful relationship with nature and the divine, ensuring that we do not take more than we need, thus promoting sustainability and gratitude in our daily lives.
