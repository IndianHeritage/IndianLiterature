दश रात्रीर अशिवेना नव द्यून अवनद्धं शनथितं अप्स्व अन्तः | विपृतं रेबहम उदनि प्रवृक्तं उन्निन्यथुः सोमं इव स्रुवेण ||
daśa rātrīr aśivenā nava dyūn avanaddhaṁ śnathitam apsv a1ntaḥ | viprutaṁ rebham udani pravṛktam un ninyathuḥ somam iva sruveṇa ||
  • Mantra: "daśa rātrīr aśivenā nava dyūn avanaddhaṁ śnathitam apsv a1ntaḥ | viprutaṁ rebham udani pravṛktam un ninyathuḥ somam iva sruveṇa ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the ten nights (daśa rātrīr) that are blessed (aśivenā) and the nine heavens (nava dyūn) that are interconnected (avanaddhaṁ). It describes the waters (apsv) that are stirred (śnathitam) and the essence (soma) that flows (sruveṇa) like a river (rebham) that has been released (viprutaṁ) and is now in motion (pravṛktam).
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • Ten Nights (daśa rātrīr): The concept of ten nights can symbolize a period of reflection or transformation. In real life, this can be likened to a ten-day retreat or a period of meditation where one seeks inner peace and clarity.
    • Blessed (aśivenā): The term 'aśivenā' implies a state of being blessed or auspicious. This can be related to moments in life when one feels fortunate or supported, such as during a wedding or the birth of a child.
    • Nine Heavens (nava dyūn): The nine heavens can represent various dimensions of existence or states of consciousness. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge across different fields, where each 'heaven' represents a different area of expertise.
    • Stirred Waters (śnathitam apsv): The imagery of stirred waters can symbolize the emotions and thoughts that are often turbulent. In life, this can be compared to times of crisis or change, where one must navigate through emotional upheaval to find clarity.
    • Essence of Soma (soma): Soma is often associated with nourishment and vitality. In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle, where the essence of life is preserved through good nutrition and self-care.
    • Flowing Like a River (sruveṇa): The flowing river represents the continuous flow of life and experiences. This can be seen in how we adapt to changes and challenges, much like a river that carves its path through the landscape.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of transformation, interconnectedness, and the flow of life. It teaches us to embrace periods of reflection, recognize the blessings in our lives, and navigate through emotional turbulence with grace. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of personal growth, such as during significant life changes, spiritual retreats, or moments of introspection. By understanding and embodying the principles within this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and connection in their lives.
