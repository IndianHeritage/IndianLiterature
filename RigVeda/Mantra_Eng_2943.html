न सायकस्य चिकीते जनासो लोढं नयन्ति पशु मन्यमानाः |नावाजिनं वाजिना हासयन्ति न गर्दभं पुरो अश्वान नयन्ति ||
na sāyakasya cikite janāso lodhaṁ nayanti paśu manyamānāḥ | nāvājinaṁ vājinā hāsayanti na gardabham puro aśvān nayanti ||
  • na sāyakasya cikite: This phrase translates to "the arrows do not strike." It suggests a sense of protection or invulnerability. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of being shielded from harm, much like how a strong community or family can protect its members from external threats.
  • janāso lodhaṁ nayanti: This means "the people do not lead to destruction." It emphasizes the importance of guidance and leadership in society. For example, a good leader in a community can steer people away from negative influences and towards constructive actions, much like a teacher guiding students away from distractions.
  • paśu manyamānāḥ: This translates to "the animals, being considered." It reflects the idea of valuing all forms of life. In modern contexts, this can relate to animal rights movements where the welfare of animals is prioritized, showing respect for all living beings.
  • nāvājinaṁ vājinā hāsayanti: This means "the swift do not mock the slow." It highlights the importance of empathy and understanding in society. In real life, this can be seen in sports or teamwork, where faster or more skilled individuals support and uplift those who may struggle, fostering a sense of community and cooperation.
  • na gardabham puro aśvān nayanti: This translates to "the donkey does not lead the horses." It signifies the idea that not everyone is suited for leadership roles. In practical terms, this can be observed in workplaces where individuals are assigned roles based on their strengths and expertise, ensuring that the right person leads the right task.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of protection, responsible leadership, empathy, respect for all living beings, and the importance of appropriate roles in society. Its significance lies in promoting harmony and understanding within communities. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community building, workplace dynamics, and social justice movements, fostering a more compassionate and effective society.
