प्र दैवोदासो अग्निर देवां अच्‍छा न मजनना |अनु मातरं पृथिवीं वि वावृते तस्थौ नाकस्य सानवी ||
pra daivodāso agnir devām̐ acchā na majmanā | anu mātaram pṛthivīṁ vi vāvṛte tasthau nākasya sānavi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • daivodāso: divine servants or those who serve the divine
    • agnir: fire, often symbolizing energy or transformation
    • devām̐: the divine, gods
    • acchā: good, auspicious
    • na: not
    • majmanā: by the offerings or sacrifices
    • anu: following, after
    • mātaram: mother
    • pṛthivīṁ: earth
    • vi: in all directions
    • vāvṛte: has spread, has expanded
    • tasthau: stood, remained
    • nākasya: of the sky
    • sānavi: with support, with strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine servants (daivodāso) and the fire (agnir) that is auspicious and not to be neglected by offerings.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the earth (pṛthivī) and the sky (nākasya), suggesting a harmonious relationship between the two realms.
    • The phrase "anu mātaram pṛthivīṁ" indicates that the earth is like a mother, nurturing and providing for all beings.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that govern our existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in our respect for nature and the environment, recognizing the earth as a nurturing mother.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, the importance of fire as a purifying element can be observed, where offerings are made to honor the divine.
    • In community gatherings, the idea of serving the divine can manifest through acts of kindness and service to others, fostering a sense of unity and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that sustain life.
    • It teaches us to honor and respect both the earth and the divine, recognizing their roles in our existence.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and respectful relationship with nature and each other, promoting sustainability and community well-being.
