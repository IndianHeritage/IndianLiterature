इदं अकर्म नमो अभ्रियाय यः पूर्वीर अन्व आनोनवीति |बृहस्पतिः स हि गोभिः सो अश्वैः स वीरैभिः स नृभिर नो वयो धात ||
idam akarma namo abhriyāya yaḥ pūrvīr anv ānonavīti | bṛhaspatiḥ sa hi gobhiḥ so aśvaiḥ sa vīrebhiḥ sa nṛbhir no vayo dhāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idam: this
    • akarma: non-action or inaction
    • namo: homage or salutation
    • abhriyāya: to the one who is worthy of praise
    • yaḥ: who
    • pūrvīr: by the ancients or predecessors
    • anv: following
    • ānonavīti: does not go away or does not diminish
    • bṛhaspatiḥ: the deity of wisdom and knowledge
    • sa: he
    • hi: indeed
    • gobhiḥ: with the cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • so: he
    • aśvaiḥ: with horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • sa: he
    • vīrebhiḥ: with heroes or warriors
    • sa: he
    • nṛbhir: with men or people
    • no: our
    • vayo: age or lifespan
    • dhāt: may he support or sustain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a salutation to the divine, acknowledging the importance of inaction or non-action in certain contexts.
    • It emphasizes the wisdom of the ancients, suggesting that there is value in following the paths laid out by those who came before us.
    • The mention of Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom, indicates a call for guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • The reference to cows, horses, and heroes symbolizes the various forms of wealth, strength, and valor that are essential for a fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, the mantra seeks the divine's support for our age and existence, highlighting the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the importance of strategic planning and the wisdom of experienced leaders. Just as the ancients laid down paths for future generations, modern leaders can learn from past successes and failures.
    • In personal life, the mantra encourages individuals to seek guidance from mentors or elders, recognizing that their experiences can provide valuable insights.
    • In community settings, the emphasis on strength and valor can inspire collective action, where individuals come together to support one another, much like a team working towards a common goal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the interconnectedness of various aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to honor the teachings of the past while seeking divine support for their present and future endeavors.
    • In essence, it highlights that true strength and sustenance come from a harmonious blend of knowledge, action, and community support.
