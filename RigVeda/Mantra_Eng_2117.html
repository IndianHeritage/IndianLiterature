यो जात एव प्रथमो मनस्वान देवो देवान क्रतुनā पर्यभूषत् |यस्य शुष्माद् rodasī अभ्यसेतां नृम्णस्य मह्ना स जनास इन्द्रः ||
yo jāta eva prathamo manasvān devo devān kratunā paryabhūṣat | yasya śuṣmād rodasī abhyasetāṁ nṛmṇasya mahnā sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • jāta: born or created
    • eva: indeed
    • prathamo: first
    • manasvān: powerful or wise
    • devo: god
    • devān: of the gods
    • kratunā: by strength or power
    • paryabhūṣat: adorned or surrounded
    • yasya: whose
    • śuṣmād: from the radiant
    • rodasī: heavens or sky
    • abhyasetāṁ: may be invoked or called upon
    • nṛmṇasya: of mankind
    • mahnā: by greatness
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: of the people
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of Indra, the king of gods, who is the first among the powerful and wise.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and wisdom in leadership, suggesting that true power comes from a divine source.
    • The invocation of the radiant heavens signifies a connection to the higher realms, where divine guidance and inspiration can be sought.
    • It reflects the idea that the greatness of mankind is intertwined with the blessings of the divine, particularly through the figure of Indra.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, whether in business or community, individuals who embody wisdom and strength often inspire those around them, much like Indra.
    • In times of crisis, people often look to higher powers or principles for guidance, paralleling the invocation of divine support in this mantra.
    • In various cultures, leaders are often seen as representatives of divine will, and their actions are believed to reflect a higher moral authority.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine wisdom and strength in human affairs, particularly in leadership.
    • It serves as a reminder that true greatness is achieved through a connection to the divine and the embodiment of noble qualities.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and strength in their endeavors, fostering a sense of responsibility and purpose.
