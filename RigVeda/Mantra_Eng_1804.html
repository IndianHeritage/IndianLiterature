प्र तं विवक्मि वाक्म्यो य एषां मरुतां महिमा सत्यो अस्ति |सचā यद īं वृषमणā अहंयुः स्थिरा चिज जनीर वहते शुभागाः ||
pra taṁ vivakmi vakmyo ya eṣām marutām mahimā satyo asti | sacā yad īṁ vṛṣamaṇā ahaṁyuḥ sthirā cij janīr vahate subhāgāḥ ||
  • pra taṁ vivakmi: This phrase translates to "I speak of that." It indicates the speaker's intention to express or reveal something significant.
  • vakmyo: This word means "what is to be spoken" or "what is to be declared." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the act of sharing knowledge.
  • ya eṣām marutām mahimā: This translates to "the glory of those who are Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, representing the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds. Their glory signifies the power and majesty of natural phenomena.
  • satyo asti: This means "is true" or "is real." It affirms the authenticity and reliability of the subject being discussed.
  • sacā yad īṁ vṛṣamaṇā: This phrase can be interpreted as "and when they are invoked." It suggests the act of calling upon these forces or deities, highlighting the importance of intention and reverence in spiritual practices.
  • ahaṁyuḥ sthirā cij janīr: This translates to "the steadfast ones who bear life." It refers to those who are stable and nurturing, possibly indicating the role of nature and its elements in sustaining life.
  • vahate subhāgāḥ: This means "they carry good fortune." It implies that the forces being invoked bring prosperity and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of communication and the reverence for natural forces, particularly the Maruts, in Vedic tradition. It highlights the importance of acknowledging the power of nature and the truth that comes from understanding and respecting these forces. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as recognizing the impact of environmental changes on our lives, fostering a sense of gratitude for the natural world, and understanding the interconnectedness of all beings. By invoking these forces with intention, we can cultivate a sense of stability and prosperity in our lives, reminding us of the importance of harmony with nature and the blessings it provides.
