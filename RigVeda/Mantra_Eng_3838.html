समिद्धो अग्न आहुत देवान यक्षि स्वध्वर |त्वं हि हव्यवाह् असि ||
samiddho agna āhuta devān yakṣi svadhvara | tvaṁ hi havyavāḻ asi ||
  • Mantra: "samiddho agna āhuta devān yakṣi svadhvara | tvaṁ hi havyavāḻ asi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddho: kindled or ignited
    • agna: fire
    • āhuta: invoked or called upon
    • devān: the gods or divine beings
    • yakṣi: to bestow or grant
    • svadhvara: your own offering or sacrifice
    • tvaṁ: you
    • hi: indeed
    • havyavāḻ: the one who is worthy of offerings
    • asi: you are
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "samiddho agna" signifies that the fire is kindled, representing the readiness to offer sacrifices.
    • "āhuta devān" indicates that the gods are being invoked, highlighting the importance of calling upon divine forces.
    • "yakṣi svadhvara" suggests that the offerings made are personal and sincere, emphasizing the connection between the devotee and the divine.
    • The concluding part "tvaṁ hi havyavāḻ asi" reaffirms Agni's role as the recipient of offerings, underscoring his significance in rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices), where offerings are made to the fire as a symbol of purity and transformation.
    • In a modern context, the act of lighting a candle or a bonfire during celebrations can be seen as a way to invoke positive energies and honor the divine.
    • People often make personal offerings in the form of prayers or intentions during significant life events, reflecting the "svadhvara" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of fire as a sacred element that connects humans with the divine.
    • It teaches us the value of sincere offerings and the act of invoking higher powers in our lives.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various rituals, personal reflections, and moments of gratitude, reminding us of the sacredness of our intentions and actions.
