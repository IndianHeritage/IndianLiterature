तवाहम अग्न ऊतिभिर नेदिष्टाभिः सचेय जोषम आ वसो |सदा देवस्य मर्त्यः ||
tavāham agna ūtibhir nediṣṭhābhiḥ saceya joṣam ā vaso | sadā devasya martyaḥ ||
  • tavāham: This word translates to "I am yours" or "I belong to you." It signifies a sense of devotion and surrender to a higher power or divine entity.
  • agna: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. Fire is often seen as a purifying force in many cultures.
  • ūtibhir: This term means "with the powers" or "with the energies." It indicates the various forces or energies that are at play in the universe.
  • nediṣṭhābhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "by the offerings" or "through the rituals." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • saceya: Meaning "if you wish" or "if it pleases you," this word reflects the humility and respect one should have when seeking blessings or guidance from the divine.
  • joṣam: This translates to "joy" or "delight." It signifies the happiness and fulfillment that comes from divine blessings and connection.
  • ā vaso: This phrase means "come to us" or "be present with us." It is an invitation for the divine presence to be felt in one’s life.
  • sadā: Meaning "always" or "forever," this word indicates a desire for continuous connection and support from the divine.
  • devasya: This translates to "of the divine" or "of the god." It emphasizes the focus on the divine aspect of existence.
  • martyaḥ: Referring to "mortals" or "human beings," it highlights the human condition and the need for divine guidance in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra "tavāham agna ūtibhir nediṣṭhābhiḥ saceya joṣam ā vaso | sadā devasya martyaḥ ||" encapsulates a deep sense of devotion, humility, and the desire for divine connection. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, rituals, or moments of reflection. It serves as a reminder to seek the divine presence in our lives, to offer our intentions sincerely, and to remain open to the joy and blessings that come from this connection. By acknowledging our dependence on the divine, we cultivate a sense of peace and purpose, allowing us to navigate the complexities of life with grace and gratitude.
