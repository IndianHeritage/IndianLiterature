त्वं विश्वस्य मेधिर दिवश्च गमश्च राजसि |स यामनि प्रति श्रुधि ||
tvaṁ viśvasya medhira divaś ca gmaś ca rājasi | sa yāmani prati śrudhi ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • viśvasya - Meaning "of the universe" or "of all," it signifies the all-encompassing nature of the divine.
  • medhira - This term refers to "intelligence" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of knowledge and understanding.
  • divaś - Translated as "of the heavens," it connects the divine to the celestial realm.
  • ca - This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the various elements of the mantra.
  • gmaś - This word can be interpreted as "of the earth," grounding the divine in the physical world.
  • ca - Again, this conjunction connects the earthly and heavenly aspects.
  • rājasi - Meaning "in the royal" or "in the ruling," it suggests authority and governance.
  • sa - This word means "that" or "he," referring to the divine presence being addressed.
  • yāmani - This term can be understood as "who is present" or "who exists," emphasizing the omnipresence of the divine.
  • prati - Meaning "towards" or "in relation to," it indicates a direction or focus.
  • śrudhi - This word translates to "listen" or "hear," calling for attention and receptivity to divine wisdom.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine entity that embodies universal intelligence, connecting both the heavens and the earth, and recognizing the authority that governs all. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education: The call for wisdom and intelligence can inspire students and educators to seek knowledge and understanding in their pursuits.
  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on the significance of wise governance, ensuring that their decisions are informed by a broader understanding of the universe and its complexities.
  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals may use this mantra in meditation or prayer to connect with a higher power, seeking guidance and clarity in their lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and understanding, bridging the divine and earthly realms. It serves as a reminder of the importance of knowledge in leadership, education, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to listen and be receptive to the divine guidance that surrounds them.
