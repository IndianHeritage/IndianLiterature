मन्त्र: मनुष्वद अग्नि अङ्गिरस्वद अङ्गिरो ययातिवत सदने पूर्ववच छुचे |अच्छ याह्य आ वहा दैव्यम जनम आ साधय बरहिषि यक्षि च प्रियं ||
manuṣvad agne aṅgirasvad aṅgiro yayātivat sadane pūrvavac chuce | accha yāhy ā vahā daivyaṁ janam ā sādaya barhiṣi yakṣi ca priyam ||
  • manuṣvad agne: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, the one who is associated with humanity." Agni, the fire god, represents the divine energy that connects humans with the spiritual realm. In real life, this can be seen in rituals where fire is used as a medium to connect with the divine, such as during weddings or religious ceremonies.
  • aṅgirasvad aṅgiro: This refers to "the energy of the Angiras," which signifies the wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations. The Angiras are considered sages in Vedic literature. In modern life, this can be likened to the importance of education and the transmission of knowledge from elders to the younger generation.
  • yayātivat sadane pūrvavac chuce: This can be interpreted as "like Yayati, who was blessed with a prosperous home." Yayati is a character in Hindu mythology who was granted the ability to enjoy life to the fullest. This reflects the idea of striving for a fulfilling life, which is relevant today as people seek happiness and success in their personal and professional lives.
  • accha yāhy ā vahā: This means "may you bring forth good things." It emphasizes the importance of positive intentions and actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of kindness and generosity, which can lead to a more harmonious society.
  • daivyaṁ janam ā sādaya: This translates to "may you achieve the divine essence of humanity." It suggests striving for a higher purpose and understanding our connection to the divine. In real life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of spiritual growth and self-improvement.
  • barhiṣi yakṣi ca priyam: This phrase means "may you protect and cherish what is dear." It highlights the importance of safeguarding relationships and values. In contemporary society, this can be applied to nurturing family bonds, friendships, and community ties.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the connection between the divine and humanity, the importance of knowledge, the pursuit of happiness, and the protection of cherished values. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a fulfilling life, fostering positive relationships, and striving for spiritual growth. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in family dynamics, educational pursuits, and community involvement, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and meaningful existence.
