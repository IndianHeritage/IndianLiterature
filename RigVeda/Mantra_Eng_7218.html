विष्वानरस्य वस पतिम अनानतस्य शवसः |एवैश च चर्षणीनाम ऊती हुवे रथानाम ||
viśvānarasya vas patim anānatasya śavasaḥ | evaiś ca carṣaṇīnām ūtī huve rathānām ||
  • viśvānarasya: This term can be broken down into "viśvā" meaning "all" or "universal" and "anara" which refers to "nourishment" or "sustenance." Thus, "viśvānarasya" signifies the universal sustainer or the one who provides nourishment to all.
  • vas patim: "Vas" translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," and "patim" means "lord" or "master." Together, "vas patim" refers to the lord of wealth or the master of prosperity, indicating a divine figure who governs abundance.
  • anānatasya: This word means "without end" or "infinite." It suggests a quality of eternity or boundlessness, emphasizing the limitless nature of the divine or the sustainer.
  • śavasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of the dead" or "of the lifeless." It may refer to the aspect of the divine that transcends physical existence, highlighting the connection between life and death.
  • evaiś: This word means "thus" or "in this way," indicating a continuation or affirmation of what has been stated.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • carṣaṇīnām: "Carṣaṇī" refers to "sacred spaces" or "places of worship." The suffix "nām" indicates possession, thus "carṣaṇīnām" means "of the sacred spaces," suggesting a connection to divine worship or rituals.
  • ūtī: This term translates to "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating a gesture of devotion or reverence towards the divine.
  • huve: This means "I invoke" or "I call upon," expressing a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • rathānām: "Ratha" means "chariot," and "nām" again indicates possession. "Rathānām" refers to "of the chariots," symbolizing movement, progress, or the journey of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking the universal sustainer, the lord of wealth, and the infinite nature of the divine. It emphasizes the importance of sacred spaces and offerings in connecting with the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection where one seeks abundance, nourishment, and guidance. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, death, and the divine, encouraging individuals to recognize the sacredness in their surroundings and the importance of gratitude and reverence in their daily lives.
