कर्णगृह्या मगवाऽशौरदेव्यो वत्सं नस् त्रिभ्य आनीयत |अजां सूरीर न धातवे ||
karṇagṛhyā maghavā śauradevyo vatsaṁ nas tribhya ānayat | ajāṁ sūrir na dhātave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • karṇagṛhyā: "He who is attentive" or "listening carefully."
    • maghavā: "the powerful" or "the great one," often referring to the deity Indra.
    • śauradevyo: "the divine one of valor" or "the heroic god."
    • vatsaṁ: "the calf" or "the young one," symbolizing innocence or new beginnings.
    • nas: "to us" or "for us."
    • tribhya: "from three" or "from the three realms," indicating a connection to the physical, mental, and spiritual worlds.
    • ānayat: "bring" or "lead," suggesting a call for guidance or support.
    • ajāṁ: "the unborn" or "the eternal," referring to something that transcends life and death.
    • sūrir: "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," indicating a source of wisdom.
    • na: "not."
    • dhātave: "to be restrained" or "to be held back."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the attention of powerful deities, particularly Indra, asking them to bring forth blessings and guidance.
    • The reference to "the calf" symbolizes new beginnings, suggesting that the speaker seeks nurturing and support in their endeavors.
    • The mention of "three realms" indicates a holistic approach, encompassing physical, mental, and spiritual aspects of life.
    • The plea for the "unborn" and "wise" to not be restrained suggests a desire for freedom and the pursuit of knowledge without limitations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking guidance from mentors or leaders who embody wisdom and strength, encouraging individuals to pursue their goals with confidence.
    • In personal development, it can serve as a reminder to remain open to new opportunities and experiences, much like a young calf exploring its surroundings.
    • In spiritual practices, it emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom while remaining free from the constraints of fear or doubt.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace new beginnings and remain open to learning, while also emphasizing the importance of being attentive and responsive to the world around them.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a powerful reminder to seek support from wise figures and to pursue one's goals with courage and clarity.
