अधारयतं पृथिवीं उता द्यां मित्रराजानां वरुणा महोभिः |वर्धयतं ओषधिः पिन्वतं गा अव वृष्टिं सृजतं जीरदानु ||
adhārayatam pṛthivīm uta dyām mitrarājānā varuṇā mahobhiḥ | vardhayatam oṣadhīḥ pinvataṁ gā ava vṛṣṭiṁ sṛjataṁ jīradānū ||
  • adhārayatam pṛthivīm uta dyām: This phrase can be translated as "Let the Earth and the Sky be supported." Here, the Earth (pṛthivī) represents the physical realm, while the Sky (dyām) symbolizes the spiritual or celestial realm. This duality emphasizes the importance of balance between the material and spiritual aspects of life.
  • mitrarājānā varuṇā mahobhiḥ: This part refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who are often associated with friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. The term "mahobhiḥ" suggests the greatness or vastness of their powers. In real life, this can be seen as a call for unity and cooperation among people, akin to how communities thrive when they work together harmoniously.
  • vardhayatam oṣadhīḥ: This translates to "Let the herbs and plants be nourished." It highlights the significance of nature and agriculture in sustaining life. In modern contexts, this can relate to sustainable farming practices and the importance of preserving biodiversity to ensure food security.
  • pinvataṁ gā ava vṛṣṭiṁ: This phrase means "Let the cows be nourished and let the rain be produced." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in many cultures, particularly in agrarian societies. The mention of rain signifies the essential role of water in agriculture. This can be connected to contemporary issues like climate change and the need for effective water management in farming.
  • sṛjataṁ jīradānū: This can be interpreted as "Let the life-giving essence be created." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and sustenance in life. In a broader sense, this can relate to the need for innovation and creativity in various fields, from science to the arts, to foster growth and development.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of the Earth, Sky, and all living beings. It calls for harmony, cooperation, and the nurturing of nature, which are essential for sustaining life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, promote unity, and foster a deeper respect for the environment. By recognizing the significance of balance between material and spiritual realms, we can work towards a more harmonious existence.
