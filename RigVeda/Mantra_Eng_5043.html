उत ते वयश्चिद्वसतेरपाप्तन् नरश्च ये पितुभाजो व्युष्टौ |अमा सते वहसि भूर्वामम् उषो देवी दाशुषे मार्त्याय ||
ut te vayaś cid vasater apaptan naraś ca ye pitubhājo vyuṣṭau | amā sate vahasi bhūri vāmam uṣo devi dāśuṣe martyāya ||
  • Mantra: "ut te vayaś cid vasater apaptan naraś ca ye pitubhājo vyuṣṭau | amā sate vahasi bhūri vāmam uṣo devi dāśuṣe martyāya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for the blessings of the divine, invoking the energy of the dawn (Uṣas) to bestow prosperity and well-being upon those who are deserving.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ut: a particle often used to denote a wish or desire.
    • te: your, referring to the divine or the goddess.
    • vayaś: youth or vitality, symbolizing energy and life force.
    • cid: consciousness or awareness, indicating the presence of the divine in all things.
    • vasater: to dwell or reside, suggesting a connection to the divine presence.
    • apaptan: those who are deserving or worthy.
    • naraś: human beings, emphasizing the connection between the divine and humanity.
    • pitubhājo: those who partake in the blessings of the ancestors.
    • vyuṣṭau: in the context of dawn, representing new beginnings and opportunities.
    • amā: a call for abundance or plenty.
    • sate: truth or reality, indicating the importance of sincerity.
    • vahasi: you carry or bestow, referring to the divine's role in granting blessings.
    • bhūri: great or abundant, emphasizing the magnitude of blessings.
    • vāmam: auspicious or favorable, indicating the positive nature of the blessings.
    • uṣo: the goddess of dawn, representing hope and renewal.
    • devi: goddess, a term of reverence for the divine feminine.
    • dāśuṣe: for the mortals, indicating the connection to human existence.
    • martyāya: to the mortal beings, emphasizing the human experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during the early morning hours, symbolizing the start of a new day filled with potential and opportunities.
    • During significant life events such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may invoke this mantra to seek blessings for prosperity and success.
    • In moments of uncertainty or challenge, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder of the divine support available to those who are deserving and sincere.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine blessings for vitality, prosperity, and well-being. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, encouraging individuals to seek truth and sincerity in their actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of inspiration and hope, guiding individuals towards positive outcomes and new beginnings. By recognizing the divine presence in our lives, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
