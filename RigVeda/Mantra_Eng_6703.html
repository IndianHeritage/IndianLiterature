मन्त्र: नकीर अस्य शचीनां नियन्ता सूनृतानाम् |नकीर वक्ता न दाद इति ||
nakir asya śacīnāṁ niyantā sūnṛtānām | nakir vaktā na dād iti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakir: Not (or no one)
    • asya: Of this (or of him)
    • śacīnāṁ: Of the powerful (or the mighty)
    • niyantā: The controller (or the ruler)
    • sūnṛtānām: Of the truthful (or the righteous)
    • vaktā: Speaker (or one who speaks)
    • dād: Give (or grant)
    • iti: Thus (or so)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea that no one can control or dictate the actions of the powerful and truthful individuals.
    • It suggests that the true essence of power lies in righteousness and truthfulness, and that such individuals are not easily swayed or manipulated.
    • The phrase "not a speaker" implies that those who are truly powerful do not need to boast or speak of their power; their actions speak for themselves.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a leader who leads by example rather than through forceful speeches. Their integrity and commitment to truth inspire others to follow, demonstrating the essence of this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who consistently delivers results and maintains ethical standards may not need to assert their position vocally; their work and character establish their authority.
    • In social movements, individuals who advocate for justice and truth often do so without seeking personal recognition, embodying the principle that true power comes from righteousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of integrity and truthfulness in positions of power.
    • It encourages individuals to focus on their actions rather than their words, highlighting that true influence comes from living a life of honesty and righteousness.
    • In today's world, where misinformation and manipulation are prevalent, this mantra is particularly relevant, urging us to seek truth and act with integrity in all aspects of life.
