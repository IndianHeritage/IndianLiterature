मित्रं न यं शिम्याः गोषु गव्यवः स्वाध्यो विदथे अप्सु जीवजनन |अरेजेतां रोधसी पाजसा गिरा प्रति प्रियं यजतां जनुषां अवः ||
mitraṁ na yaṁ śimyā goṣu gavyavaḥ svādhyo vidathe apsu jījanan | arejetāṁ rodasī pājasā girā prati priyaṁ yajataṁ januṣām avaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mitraṁ: friend or ally
    • na: not
    • yaṁ: who
    • śimyā: in the waters
    • goṣu: in the cows
    • gavyavaḥ: those who are worthy of praise
    • svādhyo: self-sufficient
    • vidathe: in the assembly or gathering
    • apsu: in the waters
    • jījanan: who gives life
    • arejetāṁ: may they be praised
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • pājasā: with glory
    • girā: with speech or words
    • prati: towards
    • priyaṁ: dear or beloved
    • yajataṁ: those who perform sacrifices
    • januṣām: of the people
    • avaḥ: may they come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of friendship and community.
    • It emphasizes the value of self-sufficiency and the role of individuals in a collective.
    • The reference to "waters" and "cows" symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
    • The call for praise and glory suggests a recognition of contributions made by individuals in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, collaboration among colleagues can be seen as a manifestation of the principles in this mantra, where each member contributes to the success of the team.
    • In community service, individuals coming together to support a cause reflect the essence of friendship and collective effort.
    • In family settings, the nurturing of relationships and the importance of self-sufficiency can be observed in how family members support each other.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, cooperation, and the recognition of individual contributions within a community.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community engagements.
    • By embodying the principles of friendship and collective effort, individuals can foster a more harmonious and productive society.
