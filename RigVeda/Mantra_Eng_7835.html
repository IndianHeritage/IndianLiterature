पुनानो रूपे अव्यये विश्वा अर्षन्न अभि श्रीयः |शूरो न गोषु तिष्ठति ||
punāno rūpe avyaye viśvā arṣann abhi śriyaḥ | śūro na goṣu tiṣṭhati ||
  • punāno: This word can be interpreted as "renewed" or "rejuvenated." It signifies the idea of regeneration and the cyclical nature of life.
  • rūpe: Meaning "form" or "shape," this word emphasizes the physical manifestation of ideas or entities in the world.
  • avyaye: Translated as "imperishable" or "eternal," it refers to that which does not decay or diminish over time.
  • viśvā: This term means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • arṣann: This word can be understood as "shining" or "radiating," suggesting a sense of brilliance or enlightenment.
  • abhi: Meaning "towards" or "in the direction of," it indicates movement or progression towards a goal or state.
  • śriyaḥ: This term translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," often associated with abundance and success in various forms.
  • śūro: Meaning "brave" or "heroic," it refers to qualities of courage and valor.
  • na: This is a negation, meaning "not," which emphasizes the absence of something.
  • goṣu: This word means "in cows" or "in the realm of cattle," which can symbolize wealth and sustenance in the Vedic context.
  • tiṣṭhati: Translated as "stands" or "remains," it indicates stability and presence.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "The renewed form, imperishable and universal, radiates towards prosperity; the brave do not remain in the realm of cattle." This suggests a profound message about the nature of existence and the pursuit of higher ideals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: The idea of renewal and imperishability can inspire individuals to seek personal development and transformation, emphasizing that true growth is eternal and not limited to physical forms.
  • Community and Interconnectedness: The notion of universality encourages people to recognize their interconnectedness with others, fostering a sense of community and collective responsibility.
  • Bravery and Courage: The emphasis on bravery suggests that individuals should strive to rise above mundane concerns (symbolized by "cattle") and pursue noble goals, such as social justice, environmental sustainability, or personal integrity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of striving for higher ideals, embracing personal transformation, and recognizing our interconnectedness with the universe. It encourages individuals to cultivate bravery and pursue prosperity that transcends material wealth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
