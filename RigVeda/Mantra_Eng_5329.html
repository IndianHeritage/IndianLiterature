न यातव इन्द्र जूजुवुर नो न वन्दना शविष्ठ वेद्याभिः |स शर्धद आर्यो विषुणस्य जन्तोर मा शिश्नदेवाऽपि गुरु ऋतं नः ||
na yātava indra jūjuvur no na vandanā śaviṣṭha vedyābhiḥ | sa śardhad aryo viṣuṇasya jantor mā śiśnadevā api gur ṛtaṁ naḥ ||
  • na yātava indra jūjuvur: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the king of gods, to come forth and assist. The term "yātava" suggests a plea for action or intervention.
  • no na vandanā śaviṣṭha vedyābhiḥ: Here, the speaker expresses a desire for the best offerings and praises to be directed towards Indra. "Vandanā" refers to worship or reverence, while "śaviṣṭha" indicates the highest or best.
  • sa śardhad aryo viṣuṇasya jantor: This line speaks of the noble and powerful nature of Indra, who is associated with strength and protection. "Viṣuṇasya" refers to the one who is all-pervading, highlighting Indra's omnipresence.
  • mā śiśnadevā api gur ṛtaṁ naḥ: The final part of the mantra is a request for the divine to grant truth and righteousness ("ṛtaṁ") to the speaker. "Mā" suggests a plea for protection or guidance, emphasizing the importance of divine support in maintaining moral order.

  • Real-Life Example: In times of crisis, individuals often seek divine intervention or guidance, much like the plea to Indra in this mantra. For instance, during natural disasters, communities may come together to pray for safety and support, reflecting the universal human desire for protection and assistance from higher powers.
  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine help and the importance of righteousness in life. It serves as a reminder that in our struggles, we can turn to the divine for strength and guidance, reinforcing the belief in a moral order that governs the universe.
  • Application: The principles expressed in this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal challenges, community issues, or even global crises. By invoking higher powers and striving for truth and righteousness, individuals and communities can foster resilience and hope.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine support in our lives and the pursuit of truth and righteousness. It encourages individuals to seek help from higher powers while also reminding them of their responsibility to uphold moral values. In a world filled with challenges, this mantra serves as a source of inspiration and guidance, urging us to remain steadfast in our beliefs and actions.
