त्री षड्हस्थाऽसिन्धवस्त्रिः कवीनामुत त्रिमाता विदथेऽस्मराट् |ऋतावरिः योषणास तिस्रोऽप्यास त्रिरादिवो विदथे पत्यमानाः ||
trī ṣadhasthā sindhavas triḥ kavīnām uta trimātā vidatheṣu samrāṭ | ṛtāvarīr yoṣaṇās tisro apyās trir ā divo vidathe patyamānāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "trī ṣadhasthā sindhavas triḥ kavīnām uta trimātā vidatheṣu samrāṭ | ṛtāvarīr yoṣaṇās tisro apyās trir ā divo vidathe patyamānāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trī: three
    • ṣadhasthā: standing in six places
    • sindhavas: rivers
    • triḥ: thrice
    • kavīnām: of the poets
    • uta: and
    • trimātā: three mothers
    • vidatheṣu: in the assemblies
    • samrāṭ: the ruler
    • ṛtāvarīr: the one who upholds truth
    • yoṣaṇās: of the women
    • tisro: three
    • apyās: even
    • trir: thrice
    • ā: from
    • dīvo: the heavens
    • vidathe: in the assembly
    • patyamānāḥ: being ruled
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of various elements in life, represented by the three rivers and the three mothers. The rivers symbolize the flow of life and knowledge, while the mothers represent nurturing and support.
    • The phrase "standing in six places" can be interpreted as the various aspects of life that one must navigate, such as family, work, and community.
    • The mention of "poets" signifies the importance of creativity and expression in understanding and interpreting life’s experiences.
    • The term "samrāṭ" refers to a ruler, indicating the need for leadership and guidance in these various aspects of life.
    • The reference to "the one who upholds truth" emphasizes the importance of integrity and honesty in all endeavors.
    • The inclusion of "women" highlights the role of femininity and the feminine perspective in the nurturing and sustaining of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the three mothers can represent the different roles women play—mother, daughter, and grandmother—each contributing to the family’s strength and unity.
    • In a workplace, the "poets" can be seen as creative thinkers who bring innovation and new ideas, essential for progress and success.
    • In community leadership, the "samrāṭ" can be a metaphor for a leader who guides with wisdom and integrity, ensuring that all voices are heard and valued.
    • The rivers can symbolize the flow of resources and knowledge that must be managed wisely to sustain growth and harmony in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance and harmony in life, emphasizing the importance of nurturing relationships, creativity, leadership, and truth.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, reminding us to honor our connections with others and the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek balance in our roles, to lead with integrity, and to appreciate the diverse contributions of those around us.
