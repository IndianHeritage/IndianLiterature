तुभ्यं हिन्वानो वसिष्ठ गा अपो 'धुक्षन् सीम अविभिर अद्रिभिर नरः |पिबेन्द्र स्वाहा प्रहुतं वषट्कृतं होत्राद आ सोमं प्रथमो य ईशिषे ||
tubhyaṁ hinvāno vasiṣṭa gā apo 'dhukṣan sīm avibhir adribhir naraḥ | pibendra svāhā prahutaṁ vaṣaṭkṛtaṁ hotrād ā somam prathamo ya īśiṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • hinvāno: offering
    • vasiṣṭa: the best or most excellent
    • gā: song or hymn
    • apo: waters
    • 'dhukṣan: that which is churned or stirred
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • avibhir: without division
    • adribhir: by the mountains
    • naraḥ: man or human
    • pib: drink
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • svāhā: an expression of offering
    • pra-hutaṁ: that which is offered
    • vaṣaṭkṛtaṁ: invoked with the sacred syllable 'vaṣaṭ'
    • hotrād: from the priest or the one who offers
    • ā: from
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink
    • prathamo: first
    • ya: who
    • īśiṣe: rules or governs
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine, specifically addressing Indra, the king of gods, asking for blessings and abundance.
    • The mention of "waters" and "mountains" symbolizes the natural elements that sustain life, indicating a connection to nature and the environment.
    • The act of offering and invoking through the sacred syllable 'vaṣaṭ' signifies the importance of rituals and the role of priests in connecting the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The reference to "soma" highlights the significance of this sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing immortality and divine consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and respect towards nature. For instance, when we engage in sustainable practices, we honor the "waters" and "mountains" that provide us with resources.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, such as weddings or festivals, the invocation of blessings from higher powers can be likened to the offerings made in this mantra, emphasizing the continuity of tradition.
    • In a corporate setting, the idea of invoking blessings for success and abundance can be paralleled with team-building activities that foster unity and collaboration, akin to the "without division" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity and harmony in life.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of all beings and the natural world, urging us to live in balance and respect.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a guiding principle for sustainable living, community bonding, and the importance of rituals in maintaining cultural heritage.
