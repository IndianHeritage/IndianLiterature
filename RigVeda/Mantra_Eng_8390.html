दिवि ते नाभा परमो य आडदे पृथिव्याः ते रुरुहुः सानविः क्षिपः |अद्रयस् त्वा बप्सति गोर अधि त्वच्य अप्सु त्वा हस्तैर दुदुहुर मनीषिणः ||
divi te nābhā paramo ya ādade pṛthivyās te ruruhuḥ sānavi kṣipaḥ | adrayas tvā bapsati gor adhi tvacy a1psu tvā hastair duduhur manīṣiṇaḥ ||
  • Divi te nābhā paramo ya ādade: This phrase can be translated as "In the heavens, your navel is supreme." Here, "divi" refers to the heavens or the celestial realm, while "nābhā" signifies the navel, which is often seen as a source of nourishment and life. The term "paramo" indicates something that is the highest or supreme. This can be likened to the idea of a central source of energy or life, much like the sun in our solar system, which is essential for life on Earth.
  • Pṛthivyās te ruruhuḥ sānavi kṣipaḥ: This translates to "From the earth, your energies are released swiftly." "Pṛthivyā" refers to the Earth, and "ruruhuḥ" suggests the emergence or manifestation of energies. "Sānavi kṣipaḥ" implies a swift action. This can be compared to how natural resources like water and minerals are extracted from the Earth, providing energy and sustenance for life. For instance, the rapid growth of plants after rain showcases how the Earth releases its energies to support life.
  • Adrayas tvā bapsati: This means "The waters are drawn to you." "Adrayas" refers to waters or fluids, and "bapsati" indicates the act of drawing or attracting. This can be related to the way rivers and lakes attract various forms of life, including animals and plants, which depend on water for survival. For example, the migration of birds to water bodies during dry seasons illustrates this attraction.
  • Gor adhi tvacy a1psu tvā hastair duduhur manīṣiṇaḥ: This translates to "The wise ones draw you forth with their hands." "Gor" refers to cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature. "Adhi tvacy" suggests being above or on the surface, while "a1psu" means in the waters. "Hastair duduhur" indicates the action of drawing forth, and "manīṣiṇaḥ" refers to the wise or learned individuals. This can be likened to how farmers and caretakers nurture their livestock and crops, drawing forth the benefits of the land through their wisdom and effort.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of the heavens, Earth, and the life forms that inhabit them. It highlights the importance of natural resources and the role of wise individuals in harnessing these resources for the benefit of society. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to sustainable practices in agriculture, environmental conservation, and the responsible use of natural resources. By recognizing the supreme source of life and energy, we can work towards a harmonious existence with nature, ensuring that future generations also benefit from the Earth's bounties.
