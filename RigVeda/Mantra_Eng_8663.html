त्रीणि त्रितस्य धारया पृष्ठेष्व एरया रयिम् |मिमीते अस्य योजनā वि सुक्रतुः ||
trīṇi tritasya dhārayā pṛṣṭheṣv erayā rayim | mimīte asya yojanā vi sukratuḥ ||
  • Trīṇi: This word translates to "three." In various contexts, the number three symbolizes completeness or a triad of elements, such as the mind, body, and spirit in holistic practices.
  • Tritasya: This refers to "of the three." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the three elements mentioned earlier.
  • Dhārayā: Meaning "by holding" or "by supporting," this word suggests the act of sustaining or maintaining something.
  • Pṛṣṭheṣv: This translates to "on the backs" or "on the support." It indicates a foundation or a base that carries weight or responsibility.
  • Erayā: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the resources that contribute to well-being.
  • Rayim: This is another term for "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the idea of abundance.
  • Mimīte: This translates to "he/she/it measures" or "he/she/it evaluates." It implies a process of assessment or understanding.
  • Asya: Meaning "of this," it refers to the subject being discussed, which in this context relates to the wealth or prosperity being measured.
  • Yojanā: This word means "planning" or "arrangement." It suggests the importance of strategy and foresight in achieving goals.
  • Vi: This is a prefix that can mean "well" or "good," indicating a positive aspect.
  • Sukratuḥ: This translates to "of good intention" or "of good thought." It emphasizes the importance of having a positive mindset and ethical considerations in actions.

Conclusion: The mantra "trīṇi tritasya dhārayā pṛṣṭheṣv erayā rayim | mimīte asya yojanā vi sukratuḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of balance, support, and the pursuit of prosperity through thoughtful planning and good intentions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, business strategies, and community building. For instance, when starting a new project, one must consider the three pillars of success: planning, execution, and evaluation. By ensuring that these elements are well-supported and aligned with positive intentions, individuals and organizations can achieve sustainable growth and prosperity. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the importance of maintaining a holistic approach to life and work.
