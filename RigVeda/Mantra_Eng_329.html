आ व इन्द्रं कृविं यथा वाजयन्तः शतक्रतुम् |मंहिष्ठं सिञ्च इन्दुभिः ||
ā va indraṁ kriviṁ yathā vājayantaḥ śatakratum | maṁhiṣṭhaṁ siñca indubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O
    • va: indeed
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • kriviṁ: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • yathā: as
    • vājayantaḥ: those who bestow strength or power
    • śatakratum: the one with a hundred powers or the one who has performed a hundred sacrifices
    • maṁhiṣṭhaṁ: the best or the most excellent
    • siñca: pour or sprinkle
    • indubhiḥ: with the moon or moonlight
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, to bestow his strength and blessings upon the devotee. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine powers to achieve excellence and prosperity, akin to how the moonlight nourishes and enhances growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable conditions, similar to invoking Indra for blessings. Just as the moonlight is essential for the growth of crops, divine blessings are sought for a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal endeavors, individuals may seek guidance and strength from higher powers when embarking on significant life changes, such as starting a new business or pursuing education, paralleling the invocation of Indra for support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of seeking divine assistance in our pursuits. It teaches us that just as the moonlight nurtures life, the blessings of higher powers can guide us towards excellence and success in our endeavors. This mantra can be applicable in various life scenarios, from personal growth to communal prosperity, emphasizing the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace.
