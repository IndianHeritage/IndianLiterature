अयम् इह प्रथमो धायि धातृभिर् होतā यजिष्ठो अध्वरेषु ईड्यः |यम् अप्नवानो भृगवो विरुरुचुर् वनेषु चित्रं विभ्वं विशे-विशे ||
ayam iha prathamo dhāyi dhātṛbhir hotā yajiṣṭho adhvareṣv īḍyaḥ | yam apnavāno bhṛgavo virurucur vaneṣu citraṁ vibhvaṁ viśe-viśe ||
  • ayam iha prathamo dhāyi: This phrase translates to "this one is the first among the givers." It emphasizes the importance of being a provider or a source of support in various aspects of life.
  • dhātṛbhir hotā: This means "the one who is invoked by the creators." It signifies the role of those who create and nurture, such as parents, teachers, and leaders, in our lives.
  • yajiṣṭho adhvareṣv īḍyaḥ: This translates to "the most worshipful among the sacrificers." It highlights the value of dedication and commitment in our actions, especially in spiritual or communal activities.
  • yam apnavāno bhṛgavo: This means "whom the Bhṛgus (a clan of sages) have sought." It suggests the importance of wisdom and guidance from those who have deep knowledge and experience.
  • virurucur vaneṣu: This translates to "who shines brightly in the forests." It symbolizes the idea of finding clarity and brilliance in nature, representing the connection between the natural world and human existence.
  • citraṁ vibhvaṁ viśe-viśe: This means "the diverse and splendid one in various forms." It reflects the richness of life and the multitude of experiences that shape our understanding of the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of support, creation, dedication, wisdom, and the beauty of diversity in life. Its significance lies in recognizing the roles we play as providers and nurturers in our communities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as family dynamics, educational settings, and community service. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of responsibility and interconnectedness, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and supportive society.
