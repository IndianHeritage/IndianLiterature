असश्चन्ती भूर्धारे पयस्वती घृतं दूहाते सुखृत्ये शुचिव्रते |राजन्ती अस्य भुवनस्य रोडसी अस्मे रेतः सिञ्चतं यन् मनुर्हितम् ||
asaścantī bhūridhāre payasvatī ghṛtaṁ duhāte sukṛte śucivrate | rājantī asya bhuvanasya rodasī asme retaḥ siñcataṁ yan manurhitam ||
  • asaścantī: This term can be interpreted as "one who is peaceful" or "calm." In real life, this can relate to individuals who maintain tranquility in stressful situations, such as a mediator in a conflict resolution scenario.
  • bhūridhāre: This means "one who bears great wealth" or "abundant." It can refer to people or entities that possess significant resources, like a philanthropist who supports various causes.
  • payasvatī: Translated as "nourishing" or "rich in nourishment." This can be likened to a mother providing sustenance and care to her children, ensuring their growth and well-being.
  • ghṛtaṁ: This word means "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in rituals and cooking. In a broader sense, it symbolizes purity and richness, akin to high-quality ingredients in gourmet cooking.
  • duhāte: This translates to "to produce" or "to yield." It can be seen in the context of hard work leading to fruitful results, such as a farmer reaping a bountiful harvest after diligent cultivation.
  • sukṛte: Meaning "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." This can be exemplified by community service, where individuals contribute positively to society.
  • śucivrate: This term refers to "one who is pure in conduct." It can relate to individuals who adhere to ethical principles, like a teacher who inspires students through integrity and honesty.
  • rājantī: This means "shining" or "radiant." It can be associated with leaders who inspire others through their vision and charisma, much like a successful entrepreneur who motivates their team.
  • asya bhuvanasya: Translated as "of this world" or "of this universe." This phrase emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of harmony in our environment.
  • rodasī: This refers to "the heavens" or "the sky." It can symbolize aspirations and dreams, akin to someone striving for their goals, reaching for the stars.
  • asme: Meaning "we" or "us," it signifies unity and collective effort, much like a team working together towards a common goal.
  • retaḥ: This translates to "semen" or "seed," symbolizing potential and creativity. It can be related to innovation in technology, where new ideas are the seeds for future advancements.
  • siñcataṁ: Meaning "to sprinkle" or "to bestow." This can be likened to sharing knowledge or resources, such as a mentor guiding a young professional.
  • yan: This means "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the preceding concepts.
  • manurhitam: Translated as "that which is beneficial for humanity." This emphasizes actions and thoughts that contribute positively to society, like sustainable practices that protect the environment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of peace, abundance, nourishment, and virtuous actions. It highlights the importance of ethical conduct and collective effort in creating a harmonious world. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, leadership, and personal growth. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more prosperous and peaceful society, ultimately benefiting humanity as a whole.
