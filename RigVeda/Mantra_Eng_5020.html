तां वल्गू दश्रां पुरुषाकतमां प्रात्ना नव्यसा वचसा विवासे |या शंसते स्तुवते शम्भविष्टा बभूवतुर्गृणते चित्रराति ||
tā valgū dasrā puruśākatamā pratnā navyasā vacasā vivāse | yā śaṁsate stuvate śambhaviṣṭhā babhūvatur gṛṇate citrarātī ||
  • tā valgū dasrā puruśākatamā pratnā: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine or the higher consciousness. The term "tā" refers to "that," indicating a connection to a higher power. "Valgū" suggests something that is swift or agile, while "dasrā" can mean "ten" or "many," indicating a multitude of aspects or qualities. "Puruśākatamā" refers to the supreme being or the ultimate person, emphasizing the importance of recognizing the divine in all forms.
  • navyasā vacasā vivāse: "Navyasā" means "new" or "fresh," suggesting the idea of renewal or rebirth. "Vacasā" refers to "speech" or "words," indicating the power of communication. "Vivāse" can be interpreted as "to dwell" or "to reside," implying that the essence of the divine resides in our words and expressions. This highlights the significance of using our speech wisely and positively in our daily lives.
  • yā śaṁsate stuvate śambhaviṣṭhā: "Yā" means "who" or "that which," referring to the divine presence. "Śaṁsate" means "praises" or "glorifies," indicating the act of worship or reverence. "Stuvate" also means "to praise," reinforcing the idea of devotion. "Śambhaviṣṭhā" refers to the auspicious or blessed state, suggesting that those who engage in praise and worship attain a state of grace and fulfillment.
  • babhūvatur gṛṇate citrarātī: "Babhūvatur" means "may it become" or "may it manifest," indicating a desire for the divine qualities to manifest in our lives. "Gṛṇate" means "to sing" or "to celebrate," emphasizing the importance of expressing joy and gratitude. "Citarātī" refers to "varied delights" or "beautiful experiences," suggesting that through devotion and praise, one can experience the richness of life.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the power of our words and actions. It encourages us to engage in praise and worship, leading to a state of grace and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by practicing gratitude, using positive language, and celebrating the beauty of life. By doing so, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and experience the varied delights that life has to offer.
