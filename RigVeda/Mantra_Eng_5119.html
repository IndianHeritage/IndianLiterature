बह्वीनाम् पिता बहुर अस्य पुत्रश्चिच्छा कृत्नोति समानावगत्य। इषुधिः सङ्काः पृतनाश्च सर्वाः पृष्ठे निनद्धो जयति प्रसूतः॥
bahvīnām pitā bahur asya putraś ciścā kṛṇoti samanāvagatya | iṣudhiḥ saṅkāḥ pṛtanāś ca sarvāḥ pṛṣṭhe ninaddho jayati prasūtaḥ ||
  • bahvīnām pitā: This phrase translates to "the father of many." It signifies a universal father figure or a source of creation that nurtures and supports numerous beings. In real life, this can be likened to a community leader or a mentor who guides and nurtures many individuals, fostering growth and development.
  • bahur asya putraś: This means "many are his sons." It emphasizes the idea of abundance and the interconnectedness of life. For example, in a family business, the father figure may have multiple children who contribute to the family legacy, each bringing their unique strengths and talents.
  • ciścā kṛṇoti samanāvagatya: This translates to "he creates with understanding." It highlights the importance of wisdom and awareness in the act of creation. In a modern context, this can be seen in how leaders in various fields, such as technology or education, create innovative solutions by understanding the needs of their communities.
  • iṣudhiḥ saṅkāḥ: This phrase means "the arrows of doubt." It represents the challenges and uncertainties that one may face in life. For instance, when starting a new venture, individuals often encounter doubts about their capabilities or the viability of their ideas.
  • pṛtanāś ca sarvāḥ: This translates to "all the struggles." It acknowledges the various hardships and obstacles that one must overcome. In real life, this can be related to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or dealing with loss, which require resilience and determination.
  • pṛṣṭhe ninaddho: This means "bound to the back." It suggests that these struggles and doubts are part of the journey and must be carried along. For example, a student may face academic challenges but learns to carry those experiences as lessons for future growth.
  • jayati prasūtaḥ: This translates to "victorious is the one who is born." It signifies triumph and success that comes from overcoming challenges. In a real-life scenario, this can be seen in individuals who rise above their circumstances, such as an athlete who trains hard to achieve their dreams despite facing numerous obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, struggle, and triumph. It emphasizes the role of a nurturing figure in fostering growth, the importance of wisdom in creation, and the inevitability of challenges in life. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal development to community leadership. It serves as a reminder that while doubts and struggles are part of our journey, victory and success are attainable through perseverance and understanding. In today's world, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their roles as nurturers and leaders, guiding others while navigating their own challenges.
