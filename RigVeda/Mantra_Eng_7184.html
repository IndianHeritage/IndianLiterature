सचā सोमेषु पुरुहूत वज्रिवो मदाया द्युक्ष सोमपाः |त्वमिद धि ब्रह्मकृत काम्यं वसु देष्टः सुन्वते भुवः ||
sacā someṣu puruhūta vajrivo madāya dyukṣa somapāḥ | tvam id dhi brahmakṛte kāmyaṁ vasu deṣṭhaḥ sunvate bhuvaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Puruhūta, who is the giver of strength, we invoke you with our offerings of Soma. You are the one who fulfills the desires of those who perform rituals for the sake of Brahman, the ultimate reality, and you are the one who bestows wealth and prosperity upon the earth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Sacā: This word signifies the act of invoking or calling upon.
    • Someṣu: Refers to the sacred drink Soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Puruhūta: A name for the divine, often interpreted as the one who is invoked in rituals.
    • Vajrivo: This term denotes strength or power, often associated with the thunderbolt (Vajra) in Vedic literature.
    • Madāya: Means intoxication or bliss, often linked to the effects of Soma.
    • Dyukṣa: Refers to the act of bestowing or granting.
    • Somapāḥ: Those who partake of Soma, indicating the priests or worshippers.
    • Brahmakṛte: Pertaining to the ultimate reality or Brahman, the source of all existence.
    • Kāmyaṁ: Desirable or fulfilling desires.
    • Vasu: Wealth or prosperity.
    • Deṣṭhaḥ: One who is worthy or deserving.
    • Sunvate: To be praised or honored.
    • Bhuvaḥ: Refers to the earth or the material world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, the invocation of divine forces for strength and prosperity can be seen in various cultural practices, such as during weddings or housewarming ceremonies where blessings are sought for a prosperous life.
    • The concept of Soma can be likened to the modern idea of seeking inspiration or motivation through various means, such as meditation, yoga, or even consuming nourishing foods that uplift the spirit.
    • In business, the mantra's essence can be applied when entrepreneurs seek divine guidance and strength to fulfill their ambitions and desires, often invoking blessings during significant ventures.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking higher powers to fulfill desires and achieve success in various aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, personal aspirations, and even in professional endeavors where individuals seek guidance and blessings for their pursuits. By recognizing the interconnectedness of spiritual and material wealth, individuals can cultivate a balanced approach to life, ensuring that their desires align with higher principles and values.
