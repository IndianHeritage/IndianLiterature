तानीद अहानि बहुलान्य आसन या प्राचीनम् उदिता सूर्यस्य |यतः परि जार इवाचरन्त्य उषो ददृक्षे न पुनर यतीव ||
tānīd ahāni bahulāny āsan yā prācīnam uditā sūryasya | yataḥ pari jāra ivācaranty uṣo dadṛkṣe na punar yatīva ||
  • Mantra: "tānīd ahāni bahulāny āsan yā prācīnam uditā sūryasya | yataḥ pari jāra ivācaranty uṣo dadṛkṣe na punar yatīva ||"
  • Translation: "They who have come forth in the many mornings, those who have arisen from the ancient sun; from where the dawns move about like the rays of the sun, they do not return again."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • tānīd: They who have come forth
    • ahāni: in the mornings
    • bahulāny: many
    • āsan: have arisen
    • yā: those who
    • prācīnam: ancient
    • uditā: arisen
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • yataḥ: from where
    • pari: around
    • jāra: rays
    • ivācaranty: move about
    • uṣo: dawns
    • dadṛkṣe: they do not return
    • na: not
    • punar: again
    • yatīva: like the sun

  • Significance:
    • This mantra reflects the cyclical nature of time and existence, emphasizing the idea that each dawn brings new opportunities and experiences.
    • It suggests that just as the sun rises each day, bringing light and warmth, so too do we have the chance to embrace new beginnings.
    • The phrase "they do not return again" can be interpreted as a reminder to live in the present and make the most of each moment, as time is fleeting.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to approach each day with a fresh perspective, letting go of past regrets and focusing on the possibilities that lie ahead.
    • For example, someone starting a new job can take this mantra to heart, viewing their first day as a new dawn filled with potential rather than being weighed down by previous experiences.
    • In a broader sense, communities can use this mantra to foster resilience and hope, encouraging members to embrace change and look forward to new opportunities for growth and development.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of renewal and the continuous flow of time.
    • By recognizing the significance of each new day, we can cultivate a mindset that values growth, learning, and the beauty of the present moment.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to live fully and consciously, making the most of the time we have.
