आ कृष्णेन रजसा वर्तमानो निवेशयन्न अमृतं मृत्युं च |हिरण्येन सविता रथेन देवो याति भुवनानि पश्यन् ||
ā kṛṣṇena rajasā vartamāno niveśayann amṛtam martyaṁ ca | hiraṇyayena savitā rathenā devo yāti bhuvanāni paśyan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • kṛṣṇena: by the black (or dark) one, often referring to Lord Krishna
    • rajasā: with activity or passion
    • vartamāno: being engaged or existing
    • niveśayann: placing or establishing
    • amṛtam: the immortal or eternal
    • martyaṁ: the mortal or transient
    • ca: and
    • hiraṇyayena: with gold or wealth
    • savitā: the sun or the creator
    • rathenā: by the chariot
    • devo: the divine or god
    • yāti: goes or travels
    • bhuvanāni: the worlds or realms
    • paśyan: seeing or observing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine interplay between the eternal and the mortal.
    • It suggests that through the influence of the divine (Krishna), one can navigate the complexities of life (rajasā) while being aware of both the immortal (amṛtam) and the mortal (martyaṁ).
    • The reference to the sun (savitā) indicates the source of life and energy, guiding the journey through various realms (bhuvanāni).
    • The chariot (rathenā) symbolizes the vehicle of life, which carries us through our experiences and challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might face challenges (mortal experiences) that require passion and activity (rajasā) to overcome. The guidance of a mentor (the divine) can help navigate these challenges effectively.
    • In personal relationships, understanding the transient nature of life (martyaṁ) can lead to deeper connections, as one learns to appreciate the moments shared with loved ones, recognizing the eternal bond (amṛtam) that exists beyond physical presence.
    • In spiritual practices, meditation can be seen as a chariot (rathenā) that helps individuals connect with their inner selves (the divine) and observe the world (bhuvanāni) with clarity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the balance between the eternal and the transient in our lives.
    • It teaches us to engage actively with the world while being guided by higher principles and values.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence, as we learn to navigate our experiences with wisdom and grace.
