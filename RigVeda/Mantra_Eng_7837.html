त्वं सोम विपश्चितं तनापुनान आयुषु |व्यो वारणं विधावसि ||
tvaṁ soma vipaścitaṁ tanā punāna āyuṣu | avyo vāraṁ vi dhāvasi ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, in this case, Soma, who is often associated with the moon and is considered a deity of inspiration and vitality.
  • soma - Soma is not only a deity but also refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals that symbolizes immortality and divine ecstasy. It represents the essence of life and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • vipaścitaṁ - This term means "wise" or "intelligent." It highlights the quality of discernment and understanding, suggesting that the divine presence of Soma is imbued with profound wisdom.
  • tanā - This word means "body" or "form." It signifies the physical aspect of existence, emphasizing the importance of the material world in conjunction with the spiritual.
  • punāna - This translates to "purifying" or "cleansing." It indicates the transformative power of Soma, which can purify both the body and the mind, leading to spiritual elevation.
  • āyuṣu - This word means "in life" or "in longevity." It refers to the duration and quality of life, suggesting that the blessings of Soma contribute to a long and fulfilling existence.
  • avyō - This term can be interpreted as "unharmed" or "safe." It conveys a sense of protection and security, indicating that the divine presence ensures safety from harm.
  • vāraṁ - This means "protection" or "shelter." It emphasizes the safeguarding aspect of the divine, suggesting that the blessings of Soma provide a protective shield in life.
  • vi dhāvasi - This phrase translates to "you run" or "you move." It signifies the dynamic nature of the divine, suggesting that the blessings of Soma are active and ever-present in the world.

In summary, this mantra invokes the divine essence of Soma, recognizing its wisdom, purifying qualities, and protective nature. It emphasizes the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, highlighting the importance of divine blessings in achieving a long, safe, and fulfilling life.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as during rituals for health and well-being, meditation practices aimed at achieving clarity and wisdom, or in moments of seeking protection and guidance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing both the body and spirit, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives. By invoking the qualities of Soma, one can aspire to lead a life filled with wisdom, purity, and longevity, ultimately contributing to personal growth and a deeper connection with the divine.
