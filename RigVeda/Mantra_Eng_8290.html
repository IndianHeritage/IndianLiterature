पवमानः सो अद्य नः पवित्रेण विचर्षणिः |यः पोताऽस पुनातु नः ||
pavamānaḥ so adya naḥ pavitreṇa vicarṣaṇiḥ | yaḥ potā sa punātu naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pavamānaḥ so adya naḥ pavitreṇa vicarṣaṇiḥ | yaḥ potā sa punātu naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the purifying one, who is today our purifier, cleanse us with his purity. May he, the one who is the potter, purify us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pavamānaḥ: The purifying one; often associated with the wind or breath, symbolizing the cleansing force.
    • so: He; referring to the divine or the purifying force.
    • adya: Today; indicating the present moment, emphasizing the immediacy of the request.
    • naḥ: Our; a possessive pronoun indicating a collective need.
    • pavitreṇa: With purity; referring to the essence of cleanliness and sanctity.
    • vicarṣaṇiḥ: The one who moves or travels; can symbolize the journey of life or the movement of the divine force.
    • yaḥ: Who; referring to the divine entity being addressed.
    • potā: Potter; symbolizing the creator or the one who shapes and molds.
    • sa: He; again referring to the divine force.
    • punātu: May he purify; a request for cleansing and sanctification.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek purification in various forms, such as through meditation, yoga, or rituals that help cleanse our mind and spirit.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be invoked during ceremonies to seek collective purity and harmony among members.
    • In personal relationships, the essence of this mantra can be applied by seeking forgiveness and understanding, thus purifying the bonds between individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, both physically and spiritually, in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we can seek cleansing and renewal at any moment, particularly in times of distress or confusion.
    • By invoking the purifying force, we align ourselves with a higher power that aids in our journey towards clarity and peace.
    • In essence, this mantra is applicable in various life scenarios, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for purity in thoughts, actions, and relationships.
