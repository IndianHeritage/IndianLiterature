यस्यानाप्तः सूर्यस्येव यामो भरे-भरे वृत्रहा शुष्मो अस्ति |वृषन्तमः सखिभिः स्वेभिर एवैर मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
yasyānāptaḥ sūryasyeva yāmo bhare-bhare vṛtrahā śuṣmo asti | vṛṣantamaḥ sakhibhiḥ svebhir evair marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • Mantra: "yasyānāptaḥ sūryasyeva yāmo bhare-bhare vṛtrahā śuṣmo asti | vṛṣantamaḥ sakhibhiḥ svebhir evair marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for strength and support. It speaks of the sun's power and the importance of friendship and unity in overcoming obstacles.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yasyānāptaḥ: "whose attainment" - refers to the achievements or successes that one seeks.
    • sūryasyeva: "like the sun" - symbolizes brightness, energy, and life-giving force.
    • yāmo: "may we be protected" - a plea for safety and security.
    • bhare-bhare: "with strength" - emphasizes the need for power and resilience.
    • vṛtrahā: "slayer of Vritra" - Indra is known for defeating the serpent Vritra, representing the removal of obstacles.
    • śuṣmo: "the one who is strong" - refers to the strength that one seeks in life.
    • vṛṣantamaḥ: "the best of the strong" - highlights the importance of being the strongest among peers.
    • sakhibhiḥ: "with friends" - signifies the value of companionship and support.
    • svebhir: "with our own" - indicates the importance of relying on one's own resources and allies.
    • evair: "thus" - suggests a conclusion or a result of the previous statements.
    • marutvān: "may we be like the Maruts" - the Maruts are storm deities, symbolizing strength and power.
    • no bhavatv: "may it be ours" - a wish for the blessings to be granted.
    • indra ūtī: "O Indra, grant us your support" - a direct appeal to Indra for assistance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a team seeks to overcome challenges in a project. The emphasis on unity and strength can inspire team members to work together effectively.
    • In personal life, individuals facing difficulties may recite this mantra to invoke a sense of resilience and support from friends and family, reminding them of the power of collective strength.
    • During community events, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of collaboration and friendship in achieving common goals.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, unity, and the support of divine forces in overcoming life's challenges. It teaches us that, like the sun, we must shine brightly and work together with our friends to achieve our goals. In real life, it serves as a powerful reminder to seek strength in community and to rely on the support of others while striving for personal and collective success.
