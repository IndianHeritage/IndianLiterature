इयं या नीच्य अर्किणी रूपा रोहिण्याः कृताः |चित्रेव प्रत्य अदर्श्य आयत्य अन्तर्दशसु बाहुषु ||
iyaṁ yā nīcy arkiṇī rūpā rohiṇyā kṛtā | citreva praty adarśy āyaty a1ntar daśasu bāhuṣu ||
  • iyaṁ yā nīcy arkiṇī rūpā rohiṇyā kṛtā: This phrase can be translated to "This form, created by the lowly light, is like that of Rohini." Here, "nīcy" refers to something that is humble or lowly, while "arkiṇī" relates to light or radiance. "Rohini" is a reference to a star, often associated with beauty and fertility in Vedic literature.
  • citreva praty adarśy āyaty a1ntar daśasu bāhuṣu: This part translates to "Like a picture, it is not seen, it extends within the ten arms." The term "citreva" suggests something that is colorful or vivid, while "adarśy" means unseen or invisible. The "ten arms" can symbolize various aspects of existence or the universe, indicating a vastness that is not immediately perceivable.

  • Interpretation: The mantra speaks to the duality of existence—how something that may appear humble or lowly can possess profound beauty and significance. It suggests that there is more to reality than what meets the eye, encouraging us to look beyond the surface.
  • Real-life examples:
    • In nature, a simple flower may seem ordinary, but upon closer inspection, it reveals intricate patterns and colors, symbolizing the hidden beauty in everyday life.
    • In human relationships, individuals who may not stand out initially can possess deep wisdom and kindness, reminding us to value inner qualities over outward appearances.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to seek deeper understanding and appreciation of the world around us. It teaches us to recognize the beauty in simplicity and the unseen aspects of life. In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering empathy in relationships, appreciating art and nature, and encouraging mindfulness in our daily experiences. By embracing this perspective, we can cultivate a richer, more meaningful existence.
