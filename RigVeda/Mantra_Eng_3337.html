नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणान इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance or blessings from a higher power or authority in times of need.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This means "we sing your praises." Singing praises can be seen as expressing gratitude and recognition of someone's efforts or achievements, similar to how we celebrate the accomplishments of leaders or mentors in our lives.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This translates to "the rivers that flow, do not dry up." Rivers symbolize sustenance and life. In a real-life context, this can represent the importance of maintaining resources and ensuring that our environment remains healthy and sustainable for future generations.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "may we perform your deeds." This can be interpreted as a call to action, encouraging individuals to engage in good deeds and contribute positively to society, much like community service or volunteer work.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new and fresh knowledge." In today's world, the pursuit of knowledge and innovation is crucial. This can be related to continuous learning and adapting to new technologies or ideas in our personal and professional lives.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This means "may we always be endowed with wisdom." Wisdom is essential for making informed decisions. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of education and critical thinking in navigating complex situations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance, expressing gratitude, and engaging in positive actions. It highlights the importance of sustainability, continuous learning, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various aspects, such as environmental conservation, community service, and lifelong education, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
