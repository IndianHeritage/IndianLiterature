प्रदक्षिणिद अभि गृणन्ति कारवो वयो वदन्त ऋतुथा शकुन्तयः |उभे वाचौ वदति सामगाऽइव गायतृं च त्रैष्टुभं चानु राजति ||
pradakṣiṇid abhi gṛṇanti kāravo vayo vadanta ṛtuthā śakuntayaḥ | ubhe vācau vadati sāmagā iva gāyatraṁ ca traiṣṭubhaṁ cānu rājati ||
  • Mantra: "pradakṣiṇid abhi gṛṇanti kāravo vayo vadanta ṛtuthā śakuntayaḥ | ubhe vācau vadati sāmagā iva gāyatraṁ ca traiṣṭubhaṁ cānu rājati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pradakṣiṇid: In a circular motion or circumambulation.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • gṛṇanti: They sing or chant.
    • kāravo: The makers or creators.
    • vayo: The age or time.
    • vadanta: They speak or declare.
    • ṛtuthā: In the season or during the time.
    • śakuntayaḥ: The birds or creatures.
    • ubhe: Both.
    • vācau: In speech or words.
    • vadati: They say or express.
    • sāmagā: The harmonious or melodious.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • gāyatraṁ: The Gāyatrī meter or hymn.
    • ca: And.
    • traiṣṭubhaṁ: The Trishtubh meter.
    • cānu: And also.
    • rājati: It shines or rules.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of circumambulation, which is a common practice in many cultures, symbolizing respect and devotion. For example, in Hindu rituals, devotees often walk around sacred objects or deities.
    • The phrase "they sing" refers to the collective expression of joy and reverence, similar to how communities come together to celebrate festivals through song and dance.
    • The mention of "birds" can be seen as a metaphor for freedom and the natural world, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all living beings. For instance, observing birds in nature can evoke a sense of peace and reflection.
    • The reference to "both" in speech indicates the duality of expression, where both the spoken word and the unspoken feelings hold significance, much like how communication in relationships involves both verbal and non-verbal cues.
    • The Gāyatrī and Trishtubh meters represent different forms of poetic expression, highlighting the beauty of language and its ability to convey deep emotions and thoughts, akin to how poetry and literature enrich our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, expression, and the natural world. It teaches us to appreciate the beauty of our surroundings and the connections we share with others.
    • In real life, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by engaging in communal activities, celebrating nature, and valuing both spoken and unspoken communication in our relationships.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to honor the rhythms of life and the interconnectedness of all beings, encouraging us to live harmoniously with ourselves and the world around us.
