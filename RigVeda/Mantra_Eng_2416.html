न यत् परो नान्तर आदधर्षद वृषण्वसू |दुःशंसो मर्त्यो रिपुः ||
na yat paro nāntara ādadharṣad vṛṣaṇvasū | duḥśaṁso martyo ripuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na - not
    • yat - that which
    • paro - another or external
    • nāntara - not within
    • ādadharṣad - has been supported or upheld
    • vṛṣaṇvasū - the one who is strong or powerful
    • duḥśaṁso - of bad reputation or ill-famed
    • martyo - mortal or human
    • ripuḥ - enemy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea that one should not rely on external forces or support that is not inherently strong or trustworthy.
    • The term "vṛṣaṇvasū" suggests a focus on inner strength and resilience rather than depending on external validation or support.
    • The phrase "duḥśaṁso martyo ripuḥ" implies that a person of ill repute or a mortal enemy should not be the source of one's strength or support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, relying on a colleague who has a history of dishonesty (duḥśaṁso) can lead to failure. Instead, one should build relationships based on trust and integrity.
    • In personal relationships, depending on friends who do not have your best interests at heart can lead to betrayal. It is essential to surround oneself with supportive and trustworthy individuals.
    • In sports, a team that relies on a player known for poor sportsmanship (duḥśaṁso) may struggle to achieve success. Fostering a culture of teamwork and respect is crucial.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the need to be cautious about whom we choose to rely on for support.
    • It teaches us to seek out trustworthy relationships and to cultivate our own resilience rather than depending on those who may not have our best interests at heart.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and secure life.
