इन्द्र मारुत्व इह पाहि सोमं यथा शार्याते अपिभः सुतस्य |तव प्रणीती तव शूर शर्मन्ना विवासन्ति कवयः सुयज्ञाः ||
indra marutva iha pāhi somaṁ yathā śāryāte apibaḥ sutasya | tava praṇītī tava śūra śarmann ā vivāsanti kavayaḥ suyajñāḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Marutva: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities who are the companions of Indra. They symbolize the forces of nature and the power of the wind.
  • Iha: Means "here" or "in this place," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current situation.
  • Pāhi: Translates to "protect" or "guard," suggesting a plea for safety and security.
  • Somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Yathā: Means "as" or "like," used to draw a comparison or illustrate a point.
  • Śāryāte: Refers to the act of being nourished or sustained, often in a spiritual or divine context.
  • Apibaḥ: Means "to drink," indicating the consumption of the divine essence or blessings.
  • Sutasya: Refers to the "son" or "offspring," often symbolizing the results of one's actions or the fruits of labor.
  • Tava: Means "your," indicating possession or belonging to the divine.
  • Praṇītī: Refers to "guidance" or "leadership," suggesting the importance of divine direction.
  • Tava Śūra: "Your bravery" or "your strength," emphasizing the valor of the divine.
  • Śarmann: Means "shelter" or "protection," indicating a desire for safety under divine care.
  • Ā Vivāsanti: Translates to "they who are wise" or "the learned," referring to those who seek knowledge and understanding.
  • Kavayaḥ: Means "poets" or "sages," indicating those who express divine truths through their words.
  • Suyajñāḥ: Refers to "well-performed sacrifices," emphasizing the importance of righteous actions and offerings to the divine.

This mantra is a prayer to Indra and the Maruts, seeking their protection and blessings. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the nourishment that comes from spiritual practices. The invocation of Soma highlights the desire for divine inspiration and the sustenance that comes from a connection with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking Protection: Individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, asking for divine protection and strength to overcome obstacles.
  • Spiritual Nourishment: It can be used in meditation or prayer to seek spiritual nourishment and guidance in one's life journey.
  • Rituals and Offerings: The mantra can be incorporated into rituals where individuals perform acts of devotion, symbolizing their commitment to righteousness and seeking blessings for their actions.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection, guidance, and nourishment. It serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine through prayer, rituals, and righteous actions. In a world filled with challenges, invoking the strength of Indra and the Maruts can inspire individuals to seek courage and wisdom in their daily lives.
