यो विश्वाभि विपश्यति भुवना सं च पश्यति | स नः पूषाविता भुवत ||
yo viśvābhi vipaśyati bhuvanā saṁ ca paśyati | sa naḥ pūṣāvitā bhuvat ||
  • Mantra: "yo viśvābhi vipaśyati bhuvanā saṁ ca paśyati | sa naḥ pūṣāvitā bhuvat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • viśvābhi: with all beings
    • vipaśyati: sees or perceives
    • bhuvanā: the worlds or realms
    • saṁ: together
    • ca: and
    • paśyati: sees or perceives
    • naḥ: our
    • pūṣā: the nourisher or sustainer
    • avitā: protector or guardian
    • bhuvat: may he be or exist
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity who perceives all beings and the various realms of existence.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of a protector who nourishes and sustains us.
    • In real life, this can be related to the role of leaders, mentors, or guardians who look after the well-being of their communities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, parents act as the "pūṣā" by nurturing their children and ensuring their safety and growth.
    • In a workplace, a good manager or leader who supports their team and fosters a positive environment embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In a broader sense, community leaders or social workers who strive to uplift and protect vulnerable populations reflect the qualities described in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and appreciation of the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It reminds us of the importance of having protectors and nurturers in our lives, whether they are family members, leaders, or community figures.
    • In daily life, we can apply this mantra by being mindful of our roles as nurturers and protectors in our own circles, fostering a sense of community and support.
