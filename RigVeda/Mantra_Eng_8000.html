दुहानः प्रात्नम् इति पयः पवित्रे परि शिच्यते |क्रन्दन देवां अजीजनत ||
duhānaḥ pratnam it payaḥ pavitre pari ṣicyate | krandan devām̐ ajījanat ||
  • duhānaḥ: This term refers to the act of milking or drawing out. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the extraction of nourishment or essence from a source.
  • pratnam: This word means 'the first' or 'the foremost'. It signifies the importance of prioritizing what is essential or foundational.
  • it: This is a pronoun that can mean 'this' or 'that', indicating a specific focus or direction in the context of the mantra.
  • payaḥ: This translates to 'milk', which is often seen as a symbol of purity, nourishment, and sustenance in many cultures.
  • pavitre: Meaning 'pure' or 'sacred', this word emphasizes the importance of purity in both thought and action.
  • pari: This means 'around' or 'about', suggesting a sense of encompassing or surrounding something.
  • ṣicyate: This term means 'is poured' or 'is sprinkled', indicating an act of offering or sharing.
  • krandan: This word translates to 'crying' or 'lamentation', which can symbolize the expression of deep emotions or the call for divine intervention.
  • devām̐: This refers to 'the gods', indicating a connection to the divine or higher powers.
  • ajījanat: This means 'gave birth to' or 'produced', suggesting creation or manifestation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to draw out the pure essence of nourishment (both physical and spiritual) and to offer it to the divine. It emphasizes the importance of purity in our actions and thoughts, and the need to connect with higher powers through our expressions of emotion and devotion.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to extract the best qualities from themselves and share them with others, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to maintain purity in intentions and actions, ensuring that one's offerings to the divine are sincere and heartfelt.
  • In relationships, it highlights the importance of expressing emotions openly, allowing for deeper connections and understanding between individuals.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, nourishment, and connection with the divine. It teaches us to prioritize what is essential, to express our emotions, and to share our best selves with the world. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life, both for ourselves and for those around us.
