अङ्गिरस्वन्ता उता विष्णुवन्ता मरुत्वन्ता जरतुर गच्छथो हवम् |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चादित्यैर यातम अश्विनाः ||
aṅgirasvantā uta viṣṇuvantā marutvantā jaritur gacchatho havam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cādityair yātam aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "aṅgirasvantā uta viṣṇuvantā marutvantā jaritur gacchatho havam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cādityair yātam aśvinā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṅgirasvantā: Referring to the Angiras, a group of sages or divine beings associated with knowledge and wisdom.
    • uta: And, or also.
    • viṣṇuvantā: Pertaining to Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology.
    • marutvantā: Related to the Maruts, the storm deities or winds, symbolizing power and energy.
    • jaritur: The one who is born or arises.
    • gacchatho: May you go or proceed.
    • havam: To the offering or invocation.
    • sajoṣasā: With joy or happiness.
    • uṣasā: Referring to the dawn or the goddess of dawn.
    • sūryeṇa: With the sun.
    • cādityair: With the Adityas, the solar deities.
    • yātam: May you go or proceed.
    • aśvinā: Referring to the Ashvins, the twin gods associated with health and medicine.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes various divine entities, including the Angiras, Vishnu, and the Maruts, suggesting a call for their blessings and presence.
    • The mention of "gacchatho havam" indicates a desire for these divine beings to come forth and accept the offerings made by the devotee.
    • By invoking the dawn ("uṣasā") and the sun ("sūryeṇa"), the mantra emphasizes the importance of light, knowledge, and new beginnings in life.
    • The reference to the Ashvins highlights the significance of health, vitality, and well-being, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just spiritual but also physical.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek blessings for new ventures, such as starting a business or a new job. This mantra can be recited to invoke divine support for success and prosperity.
    • During health-related challenges, people may chant this mantra to seek the blessings of the Ashvins for healing and recovery.
    • At the beginning of a new day, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to embrace new opportunities and face challenges with positivity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of various divine forces and their roles in our lives, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance and blessings.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from health and well-being to new beginnings and spiritual growth.
    • By invoking these divine entities, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope, strength, and positivity in their daily lives.
