शासस तं इन्द्र मार्त्यम् अयज्युं शवसस पते |महिम् अमुष्णाः पृथिवीम् इमा अपो मंदसाना इमा अपः ||
śāsas tam indra martyam ayajyuṁ śavasas pate | mahīm amuṣṇāḥ pṛthivīm imā apo mandasāna imā apaḥ ||
  • śāsas tam indra martyam ayajyuṁ śavasas pate
    • śāsas: This word can be interpreted as "to command" or "to govern." It signifies authority and control.
    • tam: This means "that" or "him," referring to a specific entity, in this case, Indra.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • martyam: This translates to "mortal" or "human," indicating the connection between the divine and human realms.
    • ayajyuṁ: This word means "to be worshipped" or "to be honored," emphasizing the importance of reverence towards the divine.
    • śavasas pate: This phrase can be understood as "lord of the dead" or "master of the departed souls," indicating Indra's role in the cycle of life and death.
  • mahīm amuṣṇāḥ pṛthivīm imā apo mandasāna imā apaḥ
    • mahīm: This means "greatness" or "vastness," often referring to the earth or the universe.
    • amuṣṇāḥ: This translates to "not heated" or "cool," suggesting a state of calmness or stability.
    • pṛthivīm: This refers to "the earth," symbolizing the physical realm and our connection to it.
    • imā: This means "these," indicating the elements or aspects being referred to.
    • apo: This translates to "waters," representing the life-giving and purifying aspects of water.
    • mandasāna: This means "gentle" or "calm," suggesting a peaceful state of being.
    • imā apaḥ: This phrase reiterates the focus on water, emphasizing its significance in life and sustenance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the relationship between the divine (Indra) and the mortal realm, highlighting the importance of reverence and worship. It signifies the need for stability and calmness in our lives, much like the earth and waters that sustain us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to our daily practices of gratitude and respect towards nature and the divine forces that govern our existence. By acknowledging the greatness of the earth and the calming presence of water, we can foster a sense of peace and harmony in our lives, promoting a balanced relationship with the world around us.
