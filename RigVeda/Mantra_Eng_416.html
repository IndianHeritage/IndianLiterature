तिस्रो द्यावः सवितुर द्वा उपस्थां एका यमस्य भुवने विराट् |आणिं न रथ्यम अमृताधि तस्थुर इह ब्रवीतु य उ तच चिकेटत ||
tisro dyāvaḥ savitur dvā upasthām̐ ekā yamasya bhuvane virāṣāṭ | āṇiṁ na rathyam amṛtādhi tasthur iha bravītu ya u tac ciketat ||
  • tisro dyāvaḥ: This phrase translates to "the three heavens." In Vedic cosmology, the three heavens represent different realms of existence. They can be seen as the physical world, the mental world, and the spiritual world. For example, in real life, one might experience these realms through daily activities, thoughts, and spiritual practices.
  • savitur dvā: This means "the two doors of the sun." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. The two doors can symbolize the duality of existence, such as day and night or life and death. In practical terms, this can relate to the choices we make in life, where each decision can lead us down different paths.
  • upasthām̐: This word means "to approach" or "to come near." It signifies the act of seeking knowledge or enlightenment. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of education or personal growth, where one actively seeks to improve oneself.
  • ekā yamasya bhuvane virāṣāṭ: This translates to "one who is the ruler of the world." Yama is often associated with the god of death and moral order. This line emphasizes the importance of understanding the moral laws that govern our lives. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we adhere to ethical principles and the consequences of our actions.
  • āṇiṁ na rathyam: This means "do not stray from the path." It serves as a reminder to stay true to one's values and beliefs. In real life, this can be applied to situations where individuals face temptations or distractions that may lead them away from their goals or moral compass.
  • amṛtādhi tasthur: This phrase translates to "the immortal truth." It signifies the eternal nature of truth and wisdom. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge that transcends time, such as philosophical teachings or scientific discoveries that remain relevant across generations.
  • iha bravītu ya u tac ciketat: This means "let it be spoken here, whoever understands it." It emphasizes the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be seen in educational settings, discussions, and the sharing of ideas that contribute to collective understanding and growth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts that are applicable in various aspects of life. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge, adhere to moral principles, and share wisdom with others. In a world filled with distractions and ethical dilemmas, this mantra serves as a guiding light, reminding us of the importance of staying true to our values and the pursuit of truth. Whether in personal development, ethical decision-making, or community engagement, the teachings of this mantra can inspire individuals to lead a more meaningful and purposeful life.
