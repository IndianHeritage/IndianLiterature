अद्वेषो नो मरुतो गातुम एतान श्रोताः हवं जारितुर एवयामरुत् |विष्णोर महः समन्यवो युयोतन स्मद रथ्योऽ न दंसनाप द्वेषांसी सनुतः ||
adveṣo no maruto gātum etana śrotā havaṁ jaritur evayāmarut | viṣṇor mahaḥ samanyavo yuyotana smad rathyo3 na daṁsanāpa dveṣāṁsi sanutaḥ ||
  • Adveṣo no maruto gātum etana: This phrase translates to "May there be no hostility among us, O Maruts." The Maruts are the storm deities in Vedic literature, representing the forces of nature. This line emphasizes the importance of harmony and peace among individuals.
  • Śrotā havaṁ jaritur evayāmarut: This means "Let us hear the call of the Maruts." It signifies the need to be receptive to the divine messages and guidance that come from nature and the universe. In real life, this can be interpreted as being open to learning and understanding the world around us.
  • Viṣṇor mahaḥ samanyavo yuyotana: This translates to "The great power of Vishnu, may it unite us." Vishnu is known as the preserver in Hindu mythology, and this line suggests that the divine power can bring people together, fostering unity and cooperation.
  • Smad rathyo3 na daṁsanāpa dveṣāṁsi sanutaḥ: This means "May we not be divided by enmity, but rather be united in praise." It highlights the significance of overcoming differences and celebrating commonalities. In a real-life context, this can be seen in community efforts where individuals from diverse backgrounds come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of unity, peace, and receptiveness to divine guidance. In today's world, where divisions and conflicts are prevalent, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of fostering harmony among individuals and communities. It encourages us to listen to each other, appreciate our shared humanity, and work together towards common goals. By embodying these principles, we can create a more peaceful and cooperative society.
