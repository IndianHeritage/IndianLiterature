एव त इन्दो सुभवं सुपेशसं रसं तुञ्जन्ति प्रथमाः अभिश्रियः |निदं-निदं पवमान नि तारीषा आविष ते शुष्मो भवतु प्रियः मदः ||
evā ta indo subhvaṁ supeśasaṁ rasaṁ tuñjanti prathamā abhiśriyaḥ | nidaṁ-nidam pavamāna ni tāriṣa āvis te śuṣmo bhavatu priyo madaḥ ||
  • evā ta indo: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Indra," addressing the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens in Vedic literature.
  • subhvaṁ: This word means "auspicious" or "good," indicating a positive quality or state.
  • supeśasaṁ: This term refers to "well-formed" or "beautiful," often used to describe something that is aesthetically pleasing or well-structured.
  • rasaṁ: This word translates to "essence" or "flavor," symbolizing the core or the most enjoyable part of something.
  • tuñjanti: This means "they enjoy" or "they take delight in," suggesting an action of experiencing joy or pleasure.
  • prathamā: This translates to "first" or "foremost," indicating something of primary importance.
  • abhiśriyaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are glorious" or "those who are exalted," referring to individuals or entities that possess great honor or prestige.
  • nidaṁ-nidam: This phrase can be interpreted as "again and again," suggesting repetition or continuity.
  • pavamāna: This word means "the purifying one" or "the wind," often symbolizing a force that cleanses or refreshes.
  • ni tāriṣa: This can be understood as "to the one who is exalted" or "to the one who is elevated," referring to a higher state of being.
  • āvis: This term means "may you come" or "may you arrive," indicating a call for presence or manifestation.
  • te: This simply means "you," addressing the deity or the force being invoked.
  • śuṣmo: This word translates to "pleasant" or "agreeable," indicating a state of harmony or satisfaction.
  • bhavatu: This means "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or desire for something to occur.
  • priyo: This translates to "beloved" or "dear," indicating something that is cherished or valued.
  • madaḥ: This word means "intoxication" or "joy," often referring to a state of bliss or ecstasy.

Conclusion: This mantra invokes the deity Indra, celebrating the auspicious and beautiful aspects of life. It emphasizes the joy and essence found in the first and foremost experiences, calling for the presence of purity and exaltation. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, celebrations, or moments of reflection, where one seeks to invite positivity, joy, and a sense of divine presence. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the beauty and essence of life, fostering a connection with the divine and the joyous experiences that come with it.
