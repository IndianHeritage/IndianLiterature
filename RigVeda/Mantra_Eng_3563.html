सोमं इन्द्राबृहस्पति पिबतं दाशुषो गृहे |मादयेतां तदोकसा ||
somam indrābṛhaspatī pibataṁ dāśuṣo gṛhe | mādayethāṁ tadokasā ||
  • Mantra: "somam indrābṛhaspatī pibataṁ dāśuṣo gṛhe | mādayethāṁ tadokasā ||"
  • Translation: "May Indra and Brihaspati drink the soma in the house of the worshipper; may they be pleased with the offerings."
  • Key Terms:
    • Somam: Refers to the soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Indra: The king of the gods in Vedic mythology, associated with thunder, rain, and war.
    • Brihaspati: The guru of the gods, representing wisdom, knowledge, and eloquence.
    • Pibataṁ: Means "to drink," indicating the act of consuming the soma.
    • Dāśuṣo: Refers to the worshipper or the one who offers the soma.
    • Gṛhe: Means "in the house," signifying the domestic space where rituals are performed.
    • Mādayethāṁ: Means "may they be pleased," expressing a desire for divine favor.
    • Tadokasā: Refers to the "power" or "strength" of the deities, indicating the blessings sought from them.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Brihaspati, two powerful deities in Vedic tradition.
    • The act of offering soma is symbolic of seeking divine inspiration and strength in one's life.
    • In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance and support from mentors or leaders in various fields, such as business or education.
    • For example, a student may seek the blessings of their teachers (akin to Brihaspati) before an important exam, hoping for wisdom and clarity.
    • Similarly, a leader may invoke the support of their team (representing Indra) to achieve collective goals, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and strength.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and fulfillment in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and strength from higher powers, whether they be spiritual, intellectual, or communal.
    • In modern contexts, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as during important life events (weddings, new ventures) where blessings and support are sought.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace, encouraging individuals to cultivate relationships with mentors and seek guidance in their endeavors.
