त्रिर नो अश्विनाऽ दिव्यानि भेषजाः त्रिः पार्थिवानि त्रिरु दत्तम अद्भ्यः |ॐआनं शंयोः ममकाय सूनवे त्रिधातु शर्म वहतं शुभस पती ||
trir no aśvinā divyāni bheṣajā triḥ pārthivāni trir u dattam adbhyaḥ | omānaṁ śaṁyor mamakāya sūnave tridhātu śarma vahataṁ śubhas patī ||
  • Mantra: "trir no aśvinā divyāni bheṣajā triḥ pārthivāni trir u dattam adbhyaḥ | omānaṁ śaṁyor mamakāya sūnave tridhātu śarma vahataṁ śubhas patī ||"
  • Translation: "Three times may the divine Ashvins bring us healing remedies; three times may they bring us earthly benefits; three times may they grant us what is excellent and auspicious. O Ashvins, may you bestow upon us the protection of the three realms and bring us good fortune."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are twin deities in Hindu mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between physical and spiritual well-being.
    • Bheṣajā: This term refers to healing or medicinal remedies. It emphasizes the importance of health and wellness in life.
    • Pārthivāni: This word relates to earthly or material benefits, highlighting the need for prosperity and abundance in our lives.
    • Omānaṁ: This signifies the desire for protection and support, indicating a plea for divine assistance in navigating life's challenges.
    • Tridhātu: Referring to the three realms—physical, mental, and spiritual—this term underscores the holistic approach to well-being.
    • Śubhas patī: This phrase translates to "lord of auspiciousness," indicating a request for blessings and positive outcomes in life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the importance of holistic health is recognized, where physical, mental, and emotional well-being are interconnected. This mantra reflects that understanding, as it calls for remedies that address all aspects of health.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek balance in their lives, striving for success in their careers (earthly benefits), maintaining good health (healing remedies), and nurturing their spiritual growth (protection and auspiciousness).
    • During challenging times, such as illness or financial hardship, people may recite this mantra as a form of prayer or meditation, seeking divine intervention and support from the Ashvins.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking balance in life through health, prosperity, and spiritual well-being. It serves as a reminder that we can call upon divine forces for support in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during health challenges, when pursuing personal goals, or in moments of uncertainty. By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and resilience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
