प्र णु त्यं विप्रम अध्वरेषु साधुम अग्निं होतारं ईळते नमोभिः |आ यस ततान rodasī ऋतेन नित्यम मृजन्ति वाजिनं घृतेन ||
pra ṇu tyaṁ vipram adhvareṣu sādhum agniṁ hotāram īḻate namobhiḥ | ā yas tatāna rodasī ṛtena nityam mṛjanti vājinaṁ ghṛtena ||
  • pra ṇu tyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we invoke" or "let us praise." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies.
  • vipram adhvareṣu: "vipram" refers to the learned or wise, while "adhvareṣu" relates to rituals or sacrifices. Together, this suggests the importance of wisdom in performing sacred rituals.
  • sādhum agniṁ: "sādhum" means "good" or "auspicious," and "agniṁ" refers to "fire." In Vedic tradition, fire is a symbol of purity and is often associated with the divine. This line emphasizes the need to honor the sacred fire.
  • hotāram īḻate namobhiḥ: "hotāram" means "the one who offers" or "the priest," and "īḻate" implies "is invoked." "namobhiḥ" translates to "with reverence." This part highlights the role of the priest in rituals and the importance of approaching the divine with respect.
  • ā yas tatāna rodasī: "ā yas" means "that which is," and "tatāna" refers to "spreads" or "expands." "rodasī" signifies "the heavens" or "the sky." This line suggests the expansive nature of the divine and its connection to the cosmos.
  • ṛtena nityam: "ṛtena" means "with truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order," and "nityam" means "always." This emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in our actions.
  • mṛjanti vājinaṁ ghṛtena: "mṛjanti" means "they purify," "vājinaṁ" refers to "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful," and "ghṛtena" means "with ghee" (clarified butter). This line indicates that offerings made with purity and devotion can lead to strength and prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energies through rituals performed with wisdom and respect. It highlights the significance of truth, purity, and the role of the priest in connecting the material world with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during religious ceremonies, personal prayers, or even in moments of reflection where one seeks guidance and strength. By embodying the principles of respect, truth, and purity in our actions, we can foster a deeper connection with the divine and enhance our spiritual journey.
