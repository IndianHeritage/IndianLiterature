शास इत्त्था महाम् अस्य अमित्रखादो अद्भुतः |न यस्य हन्यते सखा न जीवते कदाचन ||
śāsa itthā mahām̐ asy amitrakhādo adbhutaḥ | na yasya hanyate sakhā na jīyate kadā cana ||
  • śāsa itthā: This phrase can be interpreted as "thus is the command" or "this is the decree." It signifies the authority or the will that governs actions and outcomes.
  • mahām̐: This term translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating something of significant power or importance.
  • asy: Meaning "of this" or "of this one," it refers to the subject or entity being discussed.
  • amitrakhādo: This word can be broken down into "amitra" (enemy) and "khādo" (to consume or devour). It suggests the idea of an enemy being consumed or defeated.
  • adbhutaḥ: This translates to "wonderful" or "extraordinary," indicating something that is remarkable or awe-inspiring.
  • na yasya hanyate sakhā: This phrase means "whose friend is not killed." It implies a protective aspect, where a friend or ally is safeguarded from harm.
  • na jīyate kadā cana: This translates to "does not live at any time," suggesting a state of permanence or eternal existence, possibly referring to the enduring nature of the command or decree mentioned earlier.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the power of a great command that protects friends and defeats enemies. It highlights the extraordinary nature of such a decree, suggesting that it is both protective and enduring. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various contexts, such as leadership, where a strong and just leader commands respect and loyalty, ensuring the safety of their allies while overcoming adversaries. It can also relate to personal relationships, where a strong bond of friendship provides protection and support against external challenges.


In conclusion, the significance of this mantra lies in its portrayal of strength, protection, and the enduring nature of true friendship and authority. It serves as a reminder of the importance of having strong, protective relationships in our lives and the power of a commanding presence that can overcome challenges. This mantra can be applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, leadership roles, and even in the context of community support systems.
