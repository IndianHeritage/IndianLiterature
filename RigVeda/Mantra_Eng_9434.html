या उदाजन पितरो गोमयं वस्व ऋतेनाभिन्दन परिवत्सरे वलम् |दीर्घायुत्वम अंगिरसो वो अस्तु प्रति गृभ्णीत मानवं सुमेधसः ||
ya udājan pitaro gomayaṁ vasv ṛtenābhindan parivatsare valam | dīrghāyutvam aṅgiraso vo astu prati gṛbhṇīta mānavaṁ sumedhasaḥ ||
  • ya udājan pitaro: This phrase can be translated as "those who have departed, the ancestors." It refers to the forefathers or ancestors who have passed away. In real life, this can relate to the respect and remembrance we give to our ancestors during rituals and festivals, such as Pitru Paksha in Hindu culture, where offerings are made to honor them.
  • gomayaṁ: This word means "the essence of the cow" or "cow's product." In many cultures, especially in India, the cow is considered sacred and symbolizes wealth, strength, and abundance. The cow's milk, ghee, and other products are integral to various rituals and daily life, representing nourishment and prosperity.
  • vasv ṛtenābhindan: This can be interpreted as "through the universal truth." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and truth in life. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the principles of honesty and integrity that guide personal and professional relationships.
  • parivatsare valam: This phrase translates to "may you have strength throughout the year." It signifies the wish for enduring strength and vitality. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of maintaining physical and mental health through regular exercise, a balanced diet, and mindfulness practices.
  • dīrghāyutvam: This means "long life." It is a blessing for longevity and well-being. In real life, this can be associated with the practices of healthy living, such as regular medical check-ups, a nutritious diet, and a positive lifestyle that contribute to a longer, healthier life.
  • aṅgiraso vo astu: This translates to "may the blessings of the Angiras (a group of sages) be upon you." The Angiras are often associated with wisdom and knowledge. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding in their personal and professional lives.
  • prati gṛbhṇīta mānavaṁ sumedhasaḥ: This phrase means "may the wise and intelligent person receive this." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intelligence in making decisions. In real life, this can be applied to the value of critical thinking and informed decision-making in various aspects of life, from personal choices to business strategies.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about honoring our ancestors, embracing truth, seeking strength, and valuing wisdom. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life it advocates—encouraging respect for the past, a commitment to righteousness, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal growth, relationships, and contributions to society, fostering a balanced and meaningful existence.
