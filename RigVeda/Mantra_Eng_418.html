अष्टौ व्यख्यात ककुभः पृथिव्याः त्रि धन्व योजनाः सप्त सिन्धून् |हिरण्याक्षः सविता देव आगाद दधद रत्ना दाशुषे वार्याणि ||
aṣṭau vy akhyat kakubhaḥ pṛthivyās trī dhanva yojanā sapta sindhūn | hiraṇyākṣaḥ savitā deva āgād dadhad ratnā dāśuṣe vāryāṇi ||
  • Mantra: "aṣṭau vy akhyat kakubhaḥ pṛthivyās trī dhanva yojanā sapta sindhūn | hiraṇyākṣaḥ savitā deva āgād dadhad ratnā dāśuṣe vāryāṇi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṣṭau: eight
    • vy akhyat: have been declared
    • kakubhaḥ: the directions or quarters
    • pṛthivyās: of the earth
    • trī: three
    • dhanva: bows or weapons
    • yojanā: measurement or distance
    • sapta: seven
    • sindhūn: rivers
    • hiraṇyākṣaḥ: golden-eyed (often referring to the sun)
    • savitā: the sun or solar deity
    • deva: god or divine being
    • āgād: has come
    • dadhad: bestowing or giving
    • ratnā: jewels or treasures
    • dāśuṣe: for the worshipper or devotee
    • vāryāṇi: powers or strengths
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eight directions of the earth and the three bows, symbolizing the completeness and protection offered by the universe.
    • The mention of seven rivers signifies abundance and the flow of life, akin to how rivers nourish the land and sustain life.
    • The reference to "hiraṇyākṣaḥ savitā deva" highlights the sun as a giver of life and energy, much like how sunlight is essential for photosynthesis in plants, which in turn sustains the food chain.
    • The phrase "dadhad ratnā dāśuṣe vāryāṇi" suggests that the divine bestows treasures and strengths upon those who worship, similar to how hard work and dedication can lead to success and rewards in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, the eight directions can be seen in the way we organize our lives, ensuring balance and harmony in our surroundings, such as in architecture and urban planning.
    • The concept of rivers can be related to the importance of water conservation and management in our daily lives, emphasizing the need to respect and preserve natural resources.
    • The sun's role can be reflected in our daily routines, where we seek sunlight for health benefits, such as Vitamin D synthesis, which is crucial for our well-being.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the treasures bestowed by the divine can be interpreted as the opportunities and blessings we receive through our efforts and faith, encouraging us to remain dedicated and grateful.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, abundance, and divine blessings in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us of the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of worship and gratitude, and the pursuit of harmony in our daily existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to appreciate the resources around us, strive for balance in our endeavors, and recognize the divine presence in our achievements.
