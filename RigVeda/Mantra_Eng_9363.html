सहोभिर विश्वं परि चक्रमूरु राजाḥ पूर्वा धामान्य अमितā मिमानाः |तनूषु विश्वा भुवना न येमिरे प्रासारयन्त पुरुध प्रजा अनु ||
sahobhir viśvam pari cakramū rajaḥ pūrvā dhāmāny amitā mimānāḥ | tanūṣu viśvā bhuvanā ni yemire prāsārayanta purudha prajā anu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahobhir: Together, in unity
    • viśvam: The universe, all beings
    • pari: Around, encompassing
    • cakramū: The wheel, cycle
    • rajaḥ: The essence, the principle
    • pūrvā: The past, the earlier
    • dhāmāny: The realms, the abodes
    • amitā: Boundless, infinite
    • mimānāḥ: Those who contemplate, reflect
    • tanūṣu: In bodies, in forms
    • viśvā: All, every
    • bhuvanā: Worlds, realms
    • ni yemire: Who have attained, who have reached
    • prāsārayanta: Expanding, spreading
    • purudha: Abundantly, richly
    • prajā: Beings, creatures
    • anu: Following, in succession
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and interconnectedness of all beings in the universe.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the cyclical nature of life, where every action has a reaction, and everything is part of a larger cycle.
    • The reference to "amitā" suggests that the essence of existence is boundless and infinite, encouraging us to reflect on the vastness of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of contemplation and reflection ("mimānāḥ") in understanding our place in the universe.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of expansion and growth ("prāsārayanta"), indicating that as we grow and evolve, we contribute to the richness of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when individuals come together ("sahobhir") to support each other, they create a harmonious environment that benefits everyone.
    • The concept of sustainability in environmental practices reflects the cyclical nature of life, where taking care of the earth ensures its resources for future generations.
    • In personal development, reflecting on one's actions and their impact on others can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
    • In education, fostering a collaborative learning environment allows students to expand their knowledge and contribute to the collective understanding of their peers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity and collaboration.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on the world around them.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, sustainable practices, and a deeper understanding of our shared existence.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a collective effort to create a better world, where every being contributes to the richness of life.
