न द्यावा इन्द्रं ओजसा नान्तरिक्षाणि वज्रीणम् |न विव्यचन्त भूमयः ||
na dyāva indram ojasā nāntarikṣāṇi vajriṇam | na vivyacanta bhūmayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • dyāva: heaven
    • indram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ojasā: with strength
    • nāntarikṣāṇi: nor the atmosphere
    • vajriṇam: the one who wields the thunderbolt (another name for Indra)
    • na: not
    • vivyacanta: they separate
    • bhūmayaḥ: the earthly beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and inseparability of the divine and earthly realms.
    • It emphasizes that neither the heavens nor the atmosphere can separate the strength of Indra from the earthly beings.
    • Indra, as a symbol of power and strength, represents the divine force that is always present and accessible to humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of struggle, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power, much like how the mantra invokes Indra's strength.
    • In a community facing challenges, the collective strength and unity can be seen as a manifestation of the divine presence that binds them together.
    • In personal development, individuals may draw upon their inner strength, akin to the strength of Indra, to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and the earthly, reminding us that we are never alone in our struggles.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength from both within and from the divine, fostering a sense of unity and resilience.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of adversity, encouraging individuals and communities to draw upon their collective strength and the support of the divine.
