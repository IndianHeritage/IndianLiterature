विद्युनमहसो नरो अश्मदिद्यवो वातत्विषो मरुतः पर्वतच्युतः |अब्दयाऽचिन मुहुर आ ह्रादुनिवृतः स्तनयदमा रभसा उदोजसः ||
vidyunmahaso naro aśmadidyavo vātatviṣo marutaḥ parvatacyutaḥ | abdayā cin muhur ā hrādunīvṛtaḥ stanayadamā rabhasā udojasaḥ ||
  • Vidyunmahaso: This term refers to the "greatness of lightning" or "the brilliance of the sky." It symbolizes illumination and knowledge, akin to how a flash of insight can enlighten one's understanding.
  • Naro: This word means "man" or "human." It emphasizes the human experience and the quest for understanding and connection with the divine.
  • Aśmadidyavo: This can be interpreted as "the firmament of the heavens." It represents the vastness of the universe and the stability of the cosmic order.
  • Vātatviṣo: This term refers to "the wind" or "air," which is essential for life. It symbolizes movement, change, and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
  • Marutaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities associated with the winds and storms. They represent the power of nature and the dynamic forces that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • Parvatacyutaḥ: This means "the mountain that has fallen." It can symbolize obstacles or challenges that one faces in life, akin to a mountain that has crumbled, representing the impermanence of all things.
  • Abdayā: This term means "eternal" or "timeless." It signifies the everlasting nature of the soul and the divine, reminding us of the continuity of existence beyond physical forms.
  • Cin muhur: This phrase translates to "again and again." It emphasizes the cyclical nature of life and experiences, where lessons are learned repeatedly until fully understood.
  • Ā hrādunīvṛtaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is free from obstacles." It signifies the importance of overcoming challenges and finding clarity in one's path.
  • Stanayadamā: This term refers to "the one who gives strength." It symbolizes the support and resilience that one can draw upon in times of difficulty.
  • Rabhasā: This means "with great force" or "intensity." It represents the passionate energy that drives actions and decisions in life.
  • Udojasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who rises." It signifies growth, ascension, and the pursuit of higher goals and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interplay between human existence, the forces of nature, and the pursuit of knowledge and strength. It serves as a reminder of the challenges we face (like the fallen mountain) and the importance of resilience and enlightenment (the brilliance of lightning). In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as overcoming personal obstacles, seeking knowledge, and harnessing the dynamic forces of change in our lives. It encourages individuals to embrace the cyclical nature of experiences, learn from them, and rise with renewed strength and purpose.
