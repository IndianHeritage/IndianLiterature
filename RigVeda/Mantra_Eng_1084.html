स नः सिंधुम इव नावयाति पर्वषा स्वस्तये |अप नः शोशुचद अगम् ||
sa naḥ sindhum iva nāvayāti parṣā svastaye | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • sa naḥ: This phrase translates to "may he" or "may that" referring to a divine entity or force.
  • sindhum: This word means "ocean" or "river," symbolizing vastness, abundance, and the flow of life.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • nāvayāti: This translates to "may guide" or "may lead," suggesting a protective or guiding force.
  • parṣā: This word means "the group" or "the community," indicating a collective or society.
  • svastaye: This translates to "for well-being" or "for prosperity," emphasizing health and success.
  • apa: This means "away" or "from," indicating a removal or distancing from negativity.
  • naḥ: This means "us" or "our," referring to the collective group mentioned earlier.
  • śośucad: This translates to "purify" or "cleanse," indicating a desire for spiritual or moral cleansing.
  • agham: This word means "sin" or "wrongdoing," referring to negative actions or thoughts.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for guidance and protection from negativity, invoking a divine force to lead the community towards well-being and prosperity, while also seeking purification from sins or wrongdoings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Support: In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or social unrest, communities can come together, invoking this mantra to seek guidance and support from a higher power for collective well-being.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or reflection, focusing on personal purification and the removal of negative thoughts or actions, leading to a more positive and prosperous life.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "sindhum" can also symbolize the importance of nature. Communities can use this mantra to promote environmental conservation, seeking guidance to protect natural resources for future generations.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of collective well-being, guidance, and purification. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of seeking higher guidance for a prosperous and harmonious life. By applying its teachings in daily life, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity, purpose, and moral integrity.
