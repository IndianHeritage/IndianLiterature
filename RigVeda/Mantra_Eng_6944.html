भिन्दि विश्वा अप द्विषः परि बाधो जही मृतः | वसु स्पार्थं तद आ भर ||
bhindhi viśvā apa dviṣaḥ pari bādho jahī mṛdhaḥ | vasu spārhaṁ tad ā bhara ||
  • bhindhi: This word translates to "break" or "shatter." It signifies the act of overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • viśvā: Meaning "all" or "universal," it refers to the entirety of existence, encompassing all beings and elements.
  • apa: This means "away" or "from," indicating a separation or removal of negativity or harmful influences.
  • dviṣaḥ: Translated as "enemies" or "hatred," it represents the adversities or negative forces that one may encounter.
  • pari: This word means "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a comprehensive approach to dealing with challenges.
  • bādho: Meaning "obstruction" or "hindrance," it refers to the barriers that prevent progress or peace.
  • jahī: This translates to "let go" or "release," emphasizing the importance of freeing oneself from negativity.
  • mṛdhaḥ: Meaning "harm" or "injury," it signifies the pain or suffering caused by adversities.
  • vasu: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," symbolizing abundance and positive outcomes.
  • spārhaṁ: Translated as "desire" or "aspiration," it refers to the positive goals and ambitions one wishes to achieve.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it points to the aspirations and goals mentioned earlier.
  • ā: This word means "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • bhara: Translated as "bring" or "fill," it signifies the act of manifesting desires and aspirations into reality.

Conclusion: The mantra "bhindhi viśvā apa dviṣaḥ pari bādho jahī mṛdhaḥ | vasu spārhaṁ tad ā bhara ||" serves as a powerful invocation for overcoming obstacles and negativity in life. It encourages individuals to break free from the constraints of hatred and harm, allowing for the manifestation of wealth and positive aspirations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as when facing personal conflicts, professional setbacks, or emotional turmoil. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset focused on resilience, positivity, and the pursuit of goals, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
