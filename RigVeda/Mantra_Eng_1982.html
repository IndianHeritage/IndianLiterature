अवोचाम निवचनान्य अस्मिन मानस्य सूनुः सहसाने अग्नौ |वयं सहस्रं ऋषिभिः सनेम विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम ||
avocāma nivacanāny asmin mānasya sūnuḥ sahasāne agnau | vayaṁ sahasram ṛṣibhiḥ sanema vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • avocāma: This word translates to "we have spoken" or "we have declared." It signifies the act of communication or expression of thoughts.
  • nivacanāny: This means "the words" or "the statements." It emphasizes the importance of the spoken word in conveying ideas and intentions.
  • asmin: This translates to "in this" or "in this context," indicating a specific situation or environment.
  • mānasya: This word means "of the mind" or "mental." It relates to thoughts, consciousness, and the inner workings of the human psyche.
  • sūnuḥ: This translates to "the son" or "descendant," which can symbolize lineage, heritage, or the continuation of knowledge and values through generations.
  • sahasāne: This means "with great effort" or "with intensity," suggesting that the actions or thoughts being discussed require significant energy or focus.
  • agnau: This translates to "in the fire," which can symbolize transformation, purification, or the divine presence in rituals.
  • vayaṁ: This means "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • sahasram: This translates to "a thousand," symbolizing abundance, multitude, or a vast array of possibilities.
  • ṛṣibhiḥ: This means "by the sages" or "by the seers," referring to wise individuals who possess deep knowledge and insight.
  • sanema: This translates to "we may attain" or "we may achieve," indicating a goal or aspiration.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: This means "this knowledge" or "this wisdom," emphasizing the importance of learning and understanding.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This translates to "the group" or "the community," highlighting the significance of collective effort and unity.
  • jīradānum: This means "the essence of life" or "the vital force," which can refer to the fundamental energy that sustains existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the power of spoken words and collective thought in shaping our reality. It highlights the importance of knowledge, wisdom, and the efforts of the community in achieving goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, where effective communication and shared knowledge lead to success. It also underscores the significance of mentorship and learning from wise individuals, as well as the transformative power of collective action in addressing societal challenges. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of thoughts, words, and actions in the pursuit of a meaningful life.
