न घा त्वद्रिग अप वेति मे मनस त्वे इति कामम् पुरुहूत शिश्रय |राजेव दसम नि षदोऽधि बरहिष्य अस्मिन सु सोमेऽवपानम् अस्तु ते ||
na ghā tvadrig apa veti me manas tve it kāmam puruhūta śiśraya | rājeva dasma ni ṣado 'dhi barhiṣy asmin su some 'vapānam astu te ||
  • na ghā tvadrig apa veti me manas tve it kāmam puruhūta śiśraya
    • na ghā: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not let my mind stray." It emphasizes the importance of focus and concentration in one's thoughts and actions.
    • tvadrig: Refers to the divine or higher consciousness. It suggests seeking guidance from a higher power.
    • apa veti: Means "to not be led astray." This reinforces the idea of staying true to one's path and purpose.
    • me manas: Translates to "my mind." It indicates a personal plea for clarity and direction.
    • tve it kāmam: This can be understood as "to you, I offer my desires." It signifies surrendering one's wishes to the divine will.
    • puruhūta: Refers to the one who is invoked or called upon, often associated with deities or divine beings.
    • śiśraya: Means "to support" or "to uphold." It indicates a request for divine support in fulfilling one's desires.
  • rājeva dasma ni ṣado 'dhi barhiṣy asmin su some 'vapānam astu te
    • rājeva: This term can be interpreted as "like a king." It suggests a sense of authority and power.
    • dasma: Means "from this place" or "from here." It indicates a specific origin or source.
    • ni ṣado: Translates to "do not be disturbed." It emphasizes the need for stability and peace.
    • 'dhi: Refers to "in this." It connects the previous phrases to the current context.
    • barhiṣy: This term is often associated with offerings or sacrifices, indicating a ritualistic aspect.
    • asmin: Means "in this." It reinforces the idea of being present in the moment.
    • su some: Translates to "good moon." The moon often symbolizes calmness and serenity.
    • 'vapānam: Refers to "the offering." It signifies the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine.
    • astu te: Means "may it be for you." It is a concluding phrase that expresses a wish or blessing.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of focus, divine guidance, and the act of surrendering one's desires to a higher power. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In moments of decision-making, one can recite this mantra to seek clarity and avoid distractions.
  • During challenging times, it serves as a reminder to remain grounded and seek support from a higher source.
  • In rituals or personal practices, it can be used to dedicate one's efforts and desires to the divine, fostering a sense of connection and purpose.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to guide individuals towards a focused and purposeful life, encouraging them to seek divine support in their endeavors.
