युवं विप्रस्य जरणाम उपेयुषः पुनः कालेर अकृतुतं युवद वयः |युवं वन्दनम् ऋष्यदाद उद ūपथुर युवं सद्यो विश्पलाम एतवे कृतः ||
yuvaṁ viprasya jaraṇām upeyuṣaḥ punaḥ kaler akṛṇutaṁ yuvad vayaḥ | yuvaṁ vandanam ṛśyadād ud ūpathur yuvaṁ sadyo viśpalām etave kṛthaḥ ||
  • yuvaṁ viprasya: This phrase refers to the youth or the young person who is wise or learned. In real life, this can be seen in young individuals who are educated and possess knowledge beyond their years, such as prodigies or young leaders.
  • jaraṇām upeyuṣaḥ: This means the one who approaches or seeks the wisdom of the elders. It highlights the importance of learning from those who have more experience. For example, a young entrepreneur might seek mentorship from seasoned business leaders to gain insights and avoid common pitfalls.
  • punaḥ kaler akṛṇutaṁ yuvad vayaḥ: This translates to the rejuvenation of youth and vitality. It signifies the idea of renewal and the cyclical nature of life. In a real-life context, this can be observed in how new generations bring fresh perspectives and innovations to various fields, such as technology or social movements.
  • yuvaṁ vandanam ṛśyadād: This part emphasizes the respect and reverence for the youth who are wise and knowledgeable. In modern society, this can be seen in initiatives that promote youth leadership and encourage young people to take active roles in community service or governance.
  • ud ūpathur yuvaṁ: This suggests that the youth are the ones who pave the way for progress and change. For instance, young activists often lead movements for social justice, climate change, and equality, demonstrating their role as catalysts for transformation.
  • sadyo viśpalām etave kṛthaḥ: This phrase indicates the immediate and impactful actions taken by the youth. It reflects how young individuals can make significant contributions to society, such as through volunteering, advocacy, or creative endeavors that inspire others.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of youth in society, highlighting their potential to learn from the wisdom of elders while also bringing about renewal and change. It encourages respect for young individuals who are knowledgeable and active in their communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various contexts, such as education, mentorship, and social activism, where the collaboration between generations leads to a more vibrant and progressive society.
