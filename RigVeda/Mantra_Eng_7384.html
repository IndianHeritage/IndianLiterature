त्वं सोम तनूकृत्भ्यो द्वेषोभ्योऽन्यकृतिभ्यः |उरु यन्तासि वरूथम ||
tvaṁ soma tanūkṛdbhyo dveṣobhyo 'nyakṛtebhyaḥ | uru yantāsi varūtham ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • soma: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • tanūkṛdbhyo: From those who are thin or weak.
    • dveṣobhyo: From those who harbor enmity or hatred.
    • anyakṛtebhyaḥ: From those who act differently or are not aligned with righteousness.
    • uru: Vast or abundant.
    • yantāsi: You move or you lead.
    • varūtham: To the protection or shelter.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses the divine essence of Soma, invoking its protective qualities.
    • It emphasizes the need for protection from those who are weak, hostile, or unrighteous.
    • The phrase "uru yantāsi varūtham" suggests that the divine leads us to a vast shelter or protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, one may seek protection from negative influences or toxic colleagues. This mantra can be a reminder to focus on positive relationships and seek guidance from supportive mentors.
    • In personal life, individuals may face challenges from those who do not wish them well. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek the company of those who uplift and protect us.
    • In times of conflict, invoking this mantra can help one find inner strength and guidance to navigate through adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and guidance against negativity and hostility.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and during challenging times.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of safety and support, encouraging them to move forward with confidence and resilience.
