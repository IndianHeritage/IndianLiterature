तृषु यदन्ना तृषुणा ववक्ष तृषुं दूतं कृतुते यह्वो अग्निः | वातस्य मेळिं सचते निजूर्वन्न आशुं न वाजयते हिन्वे अर्वा ||
tṛṣu yad annā tṛṣuṇā vavakṣa tṛṣuṁ dūtaṁ kṛṇute yahvo agniḥ | vātasya meḻiṁ sacate nijūrvann āśuṁ na vājayate hinve arvā ||
  • tṛṣu yad annā: This phrase translates to "that which is food for the three." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to the sustenance that nourishes the body, mind, and spirit. In real life, this can be related to the importance of a balanced diet that supports physical health, mental clarity, and emotional well-being.
  • tṛṣuṇā vavakṣa: This means "the one who is nourished by the three." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of various aspects of life. For example, a person who engages in physical exercise, mental stimulation, and spiritual practices is likely to experience holistic growth and fulfillment.
  • tṛṣuṁ dūtaṁ kṛṇute: This translates to "the messenger who brings the three." It signifies the role of communication and connection in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how friends, family, and mentors act as messengers of support and guidance, helping us navigate challenges.
  • yahvo agniḥ: This phrase means "the fire that is present." Fire symbolizes transformation and energy. In everyday life, this can be likened to the passion and drive that individuals have towards their goals, igniting their journey towards success.
  • vātasya meḻiṁ sacate: This translates to "the wind that carries the essence." It represents the unseen forces that influence our lives. For instance, the impact of societal norms and cultural values can be seen as the 'wind' that shapes our thoughts and actions.
  • nijūrvann āśuṁ: This means "the swift movement of the essence." It highlights the importance of being adaptable and responsive to change. In real life, this can be observed in how businesses must pivot quickly in response to market demands or how individuals adapt to new life circumstances.
  • na vājayate: This translates to "does not falter." It signifies resilience and strength in the face of adversity. For example, individuals who face challenges in their careers but continue to persevere exemplify this quality.
  • hinve arvā: This means "to overcome obstacles." It emphasizes the importance of determination and courage. In real life, this can be seen in athletes who train rigorously to overcome physical and mental barriers to achieve their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nourishment, communication, transformation, and resilience. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, emphasizing the balance between physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek nourishment in various forms, communicate effectively, embrace change, and remain resilient in the face of challenges. By applying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and balanced life.
