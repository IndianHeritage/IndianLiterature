अस्मे इन्द्र सचा सुते नि षदā पीतये मधु |कृधī जारित्रे मगवन्न अवो महद अस्मे इन्द्र सचा सुते ||
asme indra sacā sute ni ṣadā pītaye madhu | kṛdhī jaritre maghavann avo mahad asme indra sacā sute ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asme: for us
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sacā: with power or strength
    • sute: in the drink or nectar
    • ni: always
    • ṣadā: ever
    • pītaye: for drinking
    • madhu: honey or sweetness
    • kṛdhī: may you create or bestow
    • jaritre: in the act of creation
    • maghavann: O powerful one
    • avo: come forth
    • mahad: great or vast
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking for his strength and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the desire for the divine nectar (madhu) that brings joy and vitality.
    • The repetition of "asme" indicates a collective plea for support and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often seek blessings for prosperity and unity, similar to how this mantra seeks Indra's blessings for collective well-being.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities, symbolizing the request for divine nectar that brings happiness and fulfillment.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and support from a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the value of community and collective strength in achieving goals.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek support from others and the divine, especially in times of need.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to embrace positivity and abundance in our lives.
