प्राविविपद वाच उर्मिं न सिन्धुर गिरः सोमः पवमानो मनिषाः |अन्तः पश्यन् वृजनेमावराण्य आ तिष्ठति वृषभो गोषु जानन ||
prāvīvipad vāca ūrmiṁ na sindhur giraḥ somaḥ pavamāno manīṣāḥ | antaḥ paśyan vṛjanemāvarāṇy ā tiṣṭhati vṛṣabho goṣu jānan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāvīvipad: to be endowed with wisdom or knowledge
    • vāca: speech or words
    • ūrmiṁ: wave or movement
    • na: not
    • sindhur: ocean or vastness
    • giraḥ: sound or voice
    • somaḥ: the moon or a divine drink
    • pavamāno: purifying or cleansing
    • manīṣāḥ: intellect or thought
    • antaḥ: within
    • paśyan: seeing or perceiving
    • vṛjanemā: to remove or eliminate
    • avarāṇy: obstacles or hindrances
    • ā: towards
    • tiṣṭhati: stands or remains
    • vṛṣabho: the bull, symbolizing strength
    • goṣu: among the cows, representing nourishment
    • jānan: knowing or understanding
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of knowledge and wisdom that transcends the vastness of the ocean.
    • It emphasizes the importance of pure thought and intellect, which can cleanse and remove obstacles from our path.
    • The imagery of the bull and cows symbolizes strength and nourishment, suggesting that true understanding leads to a fruitful life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies wisdom and clear communication can guide their team through challenges, much like the purifying aspect of the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and empathy can help remove misunderstandings and strengthen bonds, akin to the removal of obstacles mentioned.
    • In education, a student who seeks knowledge with a pure heart can overcome difficulties and achieve success, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound impact of wisdom and intellect in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by cultivating knowledge and understanding, we can navigate through life's challenges and emerge stronger.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or spiritual—applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
