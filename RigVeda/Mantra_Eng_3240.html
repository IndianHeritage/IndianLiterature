भिनद गिरीं शवसां वज्रम इष्णन्न आविष्कृत्वानः सहसाना ओजः | वधीद वृत्रं वज्रेण मंदसानाḥ सरन् आपो जवासां हतवृष्णीः ||
bhinad giriṁ śavasā vajram iṣṇann āviṣkṛṇvānaḥ sahasāna ojaḥ | vadhīd vṛtraṁ vajreṇa mandasānaḥ sarann āpo javasā hatavṛṣṇīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhinad: breaking or splitting
    • giriṁ: the mountain or the great obstacle
    • śavasā: with strength or power
    • vajram: the thunderbolt or weapon of Indra
    • iṣṇann: having struck or hit
    • āviṣkṛṇvānaḥ: revealing or manifesting
    • sahasāna: suddenly or with force
    • ojaḥ: energy or vigor
    • vadhīd: to kill or destroy
    • vṛtraṁ: the demon or obstacle
    • vajreṇa: with the thunderbolt
    • mandasānaḥ: being slow or lazy
    • sarann: flowing or moving
    • āpo: waters or streams
    • javasā: with speed or swiftness
    • hatavṛṣṇīḥ: having been defeated or vanquished
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful act of breaking through obstacles, represented by the mountains, with the strength of a thunderbolt.
    • It emphasizes the sudden manifestation of energy and the ability to overcome challenges, particularly those represented by the demon Vritra, who symbolizes ignorance or obstacles in life.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of swiftness and determination in overcoming these challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face significant challenges, such as a difficult project or a competitive work environment. By embodying the spirit of this mantra, they can approach these challenges with vigor and determination, breaking through the metaphorical mountains that stand in their way.
    • In personal life, someone may struggle with self-doubt or fear of failure. By invoking the energy of this mantra, they can find the strength to confront their fears and overcome the obstacles that hinder their growth.
    • In a broader societal context, communities facing natural disasters or economic hardships can draw inspiration from this mantra to unite and work together to overcome their challenges, much like the waters that flow swiftly to bring life and sustenance.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength and energy within us to overcome obstacles and challenges in life.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their inner power and act decisively in the face of adversity, much like the heroism depicted in ancient texts.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or collective societal issues, the essence of this mantra can inspire action, resilience, and the pursuit of victory over difficulties.
