न्य अग्निं जातवेदसं होत्रवाहं यविष्ठ्यम् |दधात देवम् ऋत्विजम् ||
ny a1gniṁ jātavedasaṁ hotravāhaṁ yaviṣṭhyam | dadhātā devam ṛtvijam ||
  • Mantra: "ny a1gniṁ jātavedasaṁ hotravāhaṁ yaviṣṭhyam | dadhātā devam ṛtvijam ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, the one who is born from the knowledge of the Vedas, the one who is invoked by the priests, may you bestow upon us the divine blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Agni: The fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger.
    • Jātavedasa: The one who knows all that is born; refers to Agni as the embodiment of knowledge and wisdom.
    • Hotravāha: The one who is invoked by the priests; signifies the role of Agni in rituals and sacrifices.
    • Yaviṣṭhyam: The act of bestowing or granting; indicates the blessings that Agni can provide.
    • Devam: The divine; refers to the higher spiritual powers.
    • Ṛtvijam: The priest; signifies the importance of rituals and the role of priests in connecting the material and spiritual worlds.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices) to seek blessings for prosperity and harmony.
    • In a corporate setting, the concept of invoking Agni can be likened to seeking guidance and wisdom from experienced mentors or leaders to achieve success in projects.
    • In personal development, individuals may invoke the energy of Agni by engaging in meditation or yoga, seeking transformation and clarity in their lives.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge, rituals, and divine blessings in achieving one's goals.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual realms, reminding us that our actions and intentions can invoke higher powers for support.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and guidance in their endeavors, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual pursuits.
