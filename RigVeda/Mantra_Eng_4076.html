स्वस्ति मित्रावरुणा स्वस्ति पथ्ये रेवति |स्वस्ति न इन्द्रश्चाग्निश्च स्वस्ति नो अदिते कृधि ||
svasti mitrāvaruṇā svasti pathye revati | svasti na indraś cāgniś ca svasti no adite kṛdhi ||
  • svasti: This word translates to "well-being" or "prosperity." It signifies a state of health, happiness, and success.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship and cosmic order, respectively. They symbolize the importance of harmony and balance in life.
  • pathye: This means "on the path" or "in the right direction." It emphasizes the significance of following a righteous path in life.
  • revati: Revati is often associated with prosperity and abundance. It can also refer to the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • indraś: Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and protection. His mention signifies the need for divine support in overcoming challenges.
  • cāgniś: Agni is the fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification. This highlights the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • no adite: Aditi is the mother of the gods, representing boundless space and nurturing. This phrase calls for her blessings for a prosperous life.
  • kṛdhi: This means "may you create" or "may you bring forth." It is a plea for the divine to manifest blessings in life.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a prayer for well-being and prosperity, invoking the blessings of various deities to ensure a harmonious and successful life. It emphasizes the importance of friendship, cosmic order, and the right path, while also calling for divine support in times of need.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During significant life events like weddings or new ventures, individuals can recite this mantra to seek blessings for a prosperous journey.
  • In times of personal or professional challenges, invoking the deities mentioned can serve as a reminder to stay resilient and seek support from friends and the community.
  • In daily life, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and ensure they are aligned with a righteous path, promoting ethical behavior and harmony in relationships.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to well-being, emphasizing the interconnectedness of personal, social, and cosmic harmony. By invoking the blessings of various deities, it serves as a reminder to seek balance, support, and resilience in our lives, making it applicable in both personal and communal contexts.
