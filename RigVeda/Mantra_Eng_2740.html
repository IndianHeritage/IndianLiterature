ये ते शुष्मं ये तविषीम अवर्धन्न आर्चन्त इन्द्र मरुतस् त ओजः |माध्यंदिनि सवने वज्रहस्त पिबा रुद्रैभिः सगणः सुशिप्र ||
ye te śuṣmaṁ ye taviṣīm avardhann arcanta indra marutas ta ojaḥ | mādhyaṁdine savane vajrahasta pibā rudrebhiḥ sagaṇaḥ suśipra ||
  • ye te śuṣmaṁ - "Those who are swift" or "those who are agile". This phrase refers to the forces or entities that are quick and effective in their actions.
  • ye taviṣīm - "Those who are powerful" or "those who possess strength". This highlights the importance of strength and power in achieving goals.
  • avardhann - "who increase" or "who enhance". This suggests a sense of growth and improvement, indicating that these forces contribute positively to the world.
  • arcanta - "who are worshipped" or "who are revered". This emphasizes the significance of respect and devotion towards these powerful entities.
  • indra marutas - "Indra and the Maruts". Indra is the king of the gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms, while the Maruts are storm deities. Together, they symbolize the forces of nature that bring life and nourishment.
  • ta ojaḥ - "that energy" or "that strength". This refers to the vital energy that these deities embody, which is essential for life and prosperity.
  • mādhyaṁdine - "in the midday" or "at noon". This time of day is often associated with peak energy and activity, symbolizing the height of power and potential.
  • savane - "in the sacrifice" or "in the offering". This indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine forces.
  • vajrahasta - "with a thunderbolt in hand". This symbolizes strength and the ability to overcome obstacles, as a thunderbolt is a powerful weapon.
  • pibā - "drink" or "consume". This suggests the act of receiving blessings or energy from the divine.
  • rudrebhiḥ - "with the Rudras". The Rudras are fierce deities associated with storms and destruction, representing the duality of creation and destruction in nature.
  • sagaṇaḥ - "together with the group". This emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength in achieving goals.
  • suśipra - "well-prepared" or "well-structured". This indicates the need for careful planning and execution in any endeavor.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of agility, strength, and reverence towards the divine forces that govern nature. It highlights the importance of community and collective effort in harnessing these energies for growth and prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, or in personal development, where one seeks to enhance their strengths and capabilities. The mantra serves as a reminder to respect and honor the forces that contribute to our well-being and to approach our endeavors with careful planning and collective effort.
