सुखं रथं युजुजे सिन्धुर अश्विनं तेन वाजं सनीषद अस्मिन्न आजौ |महान ह्य अस्य महिमा पनस्यते 'दब्धस्य स्वयशसो विरप्शिनः ||
sukhaṁ rathaṁ yuyuje sindhur aśvinaṁ tena vājaṁ saniṣad asminn ājau | mahān hy asya mahimā panasyate 'dabdhasya svayaśaso virapśinaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sukhaṁ: happiness, joy
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • yuyuje: to unite, to join
    • sindhur: ocean, river
    • aśvinaṁ: the twin gods, Ashvins
    • tena: by that
    • vājaṁ: strength, energy
    • saniṣad: to sit, to dwell
    • asminn: in this
    • ājau: in the great, in the powerful
    • mahān: great
    • hy: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • mahimā: glory, greatness
    • panasyate: is revealed, is known
    • 'dabdhasya: of the one who is firm, steadfast
    • svayaśaso: of one's own glory
    • virapśinaḥ: of the one who is powerful, strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the joy and strength that comes from unity and collaboration.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where happiness is achieved through the combined efforts of individuals.
    • The reference to the Ashvins, the twin gods, signifies the importance of partnership and teamwork in achieving goals.
    • The ocean or river represents the vastness of opportunities and experiences that life offers.
    • The mantra emphasizes that true greatness and glory are revealed through steadfastness and personal integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works together harmoniously can achieve greater success than individuals working in isolation.
    • In sports, teamwork is crucial; a well-coordinated team can outperform a group of talented individuals who do not collaborate.
    • In personal relationships, mutual support and understanding lead to a more fulfilling and joyful life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, collaboration, and steadfastness in achieving happiness and success.
    • It serves as a reminder that our individual strengths can be amplified when we work together towards a common goal.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful relationships, successful teamwork, and a deeper sense of fulfillment.
