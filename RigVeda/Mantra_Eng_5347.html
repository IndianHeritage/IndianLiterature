आपश् चित् पिप्युः स्तर्यो न गावो नक्षन्न ऋतं जरितारस् त इन्द्र |याहि वायुर न नियुतो नो अच्छा त्वं हि धीभिर दयसे वि वाजान् ||
āpaś cit pipyuḥ staryo3 na gāvo nakṣann ṛtaṁ jaritāras ta indra | yāhi vāyur na niyuto no acchā tvaṁ hi dhībhir dayase vi vājān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpaś: waters
    • cit: gathered
    • pipyuḥ: drinkers
    • staryo3: of the stars
    • na: not
    • gāvo: cows
    • nakṣann: falling
    • ṛtaṁ: truth
    • jaritāras: those who are born
    • ta: that
    • indra: O Indra (the deity)
    • yāhi: come
    • vāyur: wind
    • na: not
    • niyuto: restrained
    • no: our
    • acchā: good
    • tvaṁ: you
    • hi: indeed
    • dhībhir: with wisdom
    • dayase: with compassion
    • vi: with
    • vājān: the strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the waters and the deity Indra, asking for their blessings and strength.
    • The phrase "āpaś cit pipyuḥ" suggests the gathering of waters, which can symbolize the collection of knowledge or resources.
    • The mention of "gāvo" (cows) can represent nourishment and prosperity, as cows are often seen as a source of sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • "ṛtaṁ jaritāras" refers to the truth that is born, indicating the importance of truth in our lives.
    • The call to "yāhi vāyur" signifies a request for the wind (or energy) to come freely, representing the need for unrestrained movement and progress.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and compassion ("dhībhir dayase") in achieving strength and success ("vājān").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, gathering resources (like the waters) can be likened to pooling knowledge and skills from a team to achieve a common goal.
    • The importance of truth can be seen in personal relationships, where honesty fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
    • In environmental discussions, the call for unrestrained wind can symbolize the need for freedom in nature, advocating for sustainable practices that allow ecosystems to thrive.
    • Compassion in leadership can lead to a more motivated and productive team, as leaders who show empathy often inspire loyalty and hard work.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, truth, and human values.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek wisdom and compassion in our actions, ensuring that we progress without losing sight of our ethical responsibilities.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, both personally and collectively, as we strive for truth and support one another in our endeavors.
