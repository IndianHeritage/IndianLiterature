इयम् एषाम अमृतानां गीः सर्वताता ये कृपणन्त रत्नम् |धियं च यज्ञं च साधन्तस् ते नो धान्तु वसव्यं असामि ||
iyam eṣām amṛtānāṁ gīḥ sarvatātā ye kṛpaṇanta ratnam | dhiyaṁ ca yajñaṁ ca sādhantas te no dhāntu vasavya1m asāmi ||
  • iyam eṣām amṛtānāṁ gīḥ: This phrase translates to "this is the song of the immortal ones." Here, "amṛtānāṁ" refers to those who are immortal or eternal, symbolizing wisdom and enlightenment. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and truth that transcends the temporal nature of existence.
  • sarvatātā ye kṛpaṇanta ratnam: This means "the jewel that is sought by all." The term "kṛpaṇanta" suggests a sense of longing or desire, while "ratnam" refers to a precious gem. In everyday life, this can represent the universal quest for happiness, fulfillment, and purpose, akin to how people strive for their dreams and aspirations.
  • dhiyaṁ ca yajñaṁ ca sādhantas: This translates to "those who cultivate wisdom and sacrifice." "Dhiyaṁ" refers to intellect or understanding, while "yajñaṁ" signifies sacrifice or offering. This can be related to the idea that personal growth and success often require sacrifices, such as time, effort, and resources, much like students who dedicate themselves to their studies for a better future.
  • te no dhāntu vasavya1m asāmi: This means "may they not abandon us, O divine ones." The term "vasavya" refers to the divine or celestial beings. This line emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and support in our endeavors. In real life, it can be seen as a reminder to seek help from mentors, teachers, or even spiritual beliefs when facing challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, fulfillment, and divine support in our lives. It highlights the importance of knowledge, sacrifice, and the pursuit of higher ideals. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can serve as a guiding principle for individuals striving for personal and spiritual growth, encouraging them to seek wisdom, make sacrifices for their goals, and remain open to divine guidance in their journey.
