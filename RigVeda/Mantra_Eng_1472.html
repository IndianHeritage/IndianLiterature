जहि यो नो अघायति श्रिणुष्व सुश्रवस्तमः |ऋष्टं न याम्न्न अप भूतु दुरमतिर विश्वाप भूतु दुरमतीः ||
jahi yo no aghāyati śṛṇuṣva suśravastamaḥ | riṣṭaṁ na yāmann apa bhūtu durmatir viśvāpa bhūtu durmatiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "jahi yo no aghāyati śṛṇuṣva suśravastamaḥ | riṣṭaṁ na yāmann apa bhūtu durmatir viśvāpa bhūtu durmatiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Overcome the one who does not harm us; listen to the one who is of good reputation. May we not be afflicted by the evil-minded; may the evil-minded be removed from our path."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • jahi: Overcome or conquer.
    • yo: Who.
    • no: Us.
    • aghāyati: Does not harm or injure.
    • śṛṇuṣva: Listen or heed.
    • suśravastamaḥ: The one who is of good reputation or well-known for good deeds.
    • riṣṭaṁ: Afflicted or troubled.
    • na: Not.
    • yāmann: By whom.
    • apa: Remove or take away.
    • bhūtu: Become or manifest.
    • durmatir: Evil-minded or ill-intentioned.
    • viśvāpa: May it be removed or dispelled.
    • bhūtu: Become or manifest.
    • durmatiḥ: Evil intention or bad thought.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when dealing with colleagues who may have ill intentions or are harmful to the work environment. By focusing on overcoming negativity and listening to those who contribute positively, a healthier workplace culture can be fostered.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra encourages individuals to distance themselves from toxic people and to surround themselves with those who uplift and support them. For instance, choosing friends who inspire and motivate rather than those who bring negativity.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be a guiding principle for promoting harmony and cooperation. By addressing harmful behaviors and encouraging positive contributions, communities can thrive and grow stronger.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for overcoming negativity and embracing positivity. It serves as a reminder to focus on good intentions and to actively remove harmful influences from our lives. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or communities, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and supportive environment. By prioritizing positive interactions and addressing negativity, individuals can cultivate a life filled with peace and prosperity.
