अध्वर्यवो हविष्मन्तो हि भूताच्छाप इतोषतीर उशन्तः |अव याश चष्टे अरुणः सुपर्णस तं आस्यध्वम् ऊर्मिम अद्या सुहस्ताः ||
adhvaryavo haviṣmanto hi bhūtācchāpa itośatīr uśantaḥ | ava yāś caṣṭe aruṇaḥ suparṇas tam āsyadhvam ūrmim adyā suhastāḥ ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals and sacrifices in the Vedic tradition. They are responsible for the proper execution of the rites.
  • haviṣmanto: This word signifies those who are engaged in offering oblations or sacrifices. It emphasizes the importance of offerings in Vedic rituals.
  • hi: A conjunction meaning "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the significance of the preceding terms.
  • bhūtācchāpa: This can be interpreted as the essence or spirit of the beings, indicating a connection to the natural world and its elements.
  • itośatīr: This term suggests a sense of movement or flow, possibly referring to the flow of energy or consciousness.
  • uśantaḥ: This word means "those who are awake" or "those who are aware," highlighting the importance of awareness in spiritual practices.
  • ava: A term meaning "down" or "towards," indicating a direction or focus in the context of the mantra.
  • yāś: This word signifies "success" or "victory," emphasizing the desired outcomes of the rituals.
  • caṣṭe: This term can be interpreted as "to strive" or "to exert effort," indicating the need for active participation in the spiritual journey.
  • aruṇaḥ: This word refers to the reddish hue of dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • suparṇas: This term means "having good wings," often associated with the divine or celestial beings, suggesting a connection to higher realms.
  • tam: A pronoun meaning "that," referring to the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • āsyadhvam: This word means "let us speak" or "let us express," indicating the importance of communication in spiritual practices.
  • ūrmim: This term means "wave" or "flow," possibly referring to the flow of life or consciousness.
  • adyā: This word means "today," emphasizing the immediacy and relevance of the mantra in the present moment.
  • suhastāḥ: This term means "with good hands," suggesting the importance of skillful action and intention in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of ritualistic practice, emphasizing the importance of awareness, intention, and connection to the divine. It serves as a reminder that spiritual endeavors require active participation and a deep understanding of the natural world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life, such as personal growth, community service, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of awareness, effort, and connection, individuals can navigate their spiritual journeys and contribute positively to the world around them.
