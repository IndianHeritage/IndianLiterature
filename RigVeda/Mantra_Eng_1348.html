प्र याग्घोषे भृगवाणे न शोभे यया वाचा यजति पज्रियो वाम् | प्रैषयुर न विद्वान् ||
pra yā ghoṣe bhṛgavāṇe na śobhe yayā vācā yajati pajriyo vām | praiṣayur na vidvān ||
  • Mantra: "pra yā ghoṣe bhṛgavāṇe na śobhe yayā vācā yajati pajriyo vām | praiṣayur na vidvān ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, emphasizing the importance of speech and communication in rituals and offerings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or action.
    • yā: Refers to "that which" or "which," pointing to a specific quality or aspect.
    • ghoṣe: Means "sound" or "noise," often associated with the sounds of rituals or chants.
    • bhṛgavāṇe: Refers to the sage Bhrigu, known for his wisdom and knowledge.
    • na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • śobhe: Means "to shine" or "to be beautiful," often used in the context of something that is pleasing or admirable.
    • yayā: Means "by which," indicating a means or method.
    • vācā: Refers to "speech" or "word," highlighting the power of communication.
    • yajati: Means "to perform a sacrifice" or "to worship," indicating the act of offering.
    • pajriyo: Refers to "the one who is worthy" or "the one who is deserving," often used in the context of offerings.
    • vām: Means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection.
    • praīṣayur: Means "they send forth" or "they dispatch," indicating action or movement.
    • na vidvān: Means "not the wise one," indicating a lack of knowledge or understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, effective communication is crucial for teamwork and project success. Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of speech in rituals, clear communication can lead to better collaboration and outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, the way we express our thoughts and feelings can significantly impact our connections with others. The mantra reminds us that our words hold power and can either build or hinder relationships.
    • In spiritual practices, chanting and mantras are often used to focus the mind and connect with the divine. This mantra highlights the significance of intention and the quality of our speech in spiritual offerings.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of speech and communication in both spiritual and everyday contexts. It serves as a reminder that our words have the power to influence our surroundings and relationships. By being mindful of how we communicate, we can enhance our rituals, strengthen our connections, and foster a deeper understanding of ourselves and others. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal interactions to professional environments, highlighting the universal significance of effective communication.
