मन्त्र: न हि वो अस्त्य अर्बकः देवासो न कुमारकः | विश्वे सतोमहान्त इति ||
nahi vo asty arbhako devāso na kumārakaḥ | viśve satomahānta it ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "There are no gods who are children; all are great and true." This emphasizes the idea that divinity is not limited to a youthful or immature state but encompasses a vast and profound essence.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • vo: your
    • asty: exists
    • arbha: child or infant
    • devāso: gods
    • na: not
    • kumārakaḥ: young ones or children
    • viśve: all
    • sat: true
    • omahānta: great or vast
  • Philosophical Interpretation: This mantra suggests that divinity is not characterized by youth or inexperience. Instead, it points to the eternal and omnipotent nature of the divine, which transcends human limitations. In a world where we often associate greatness with age and wisdom, this mantra serves as a reminder that true divinity is beyond such categorizations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, we often see young leaders emerging who challenge traditional norms. For instance, figures like Malala Yousafzai and Greta Thunberg have shown that age does not determine the impact one can have on society.
    • In the realm of science, young prodigies like Blaise Pascal and Marie Curie made significant contributions at a young age, demonstrating that wisdom and greatness can manifest early in life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize the potential for greatness in all, regardless of age or experience. It encourages a broader understanding of divinity and excellence, applicable in various aspects of life, including education, leadership, and personal growth. By embracing this perspective, we can foster an environment that values contributions from all ages, promoting inclusivity and innovation.
