त्वां अस्य व्युषि देव पूर्वे दूतं कृत्वाना अयजन्त हव्यैः |संस्थे यद अग्न ईयसे रयीणां देवो मर्तैर वसुभिर इध्यमानः ||
tvām asyā vyuṣi deva pūrve dūtaṁ kṛṇvānā ayajanta havyaiḥ | saṁsthe yad agna īyase rayīṇāṁ devo martair vasubhir idhyamānaḥ ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to the divine or a deity, often Agni, the fire god.
  • asyā - Meaning "of this," it indicates a connection to the speaker's offerings or intentions.
  • vyuṣi - This means "in the morning," symbolizing new beginnings and the dawn of a new day.
  • deva - Translates to "god" or "deity," emphasizing the divine nature of the subject being addressed.
  • pūrve - Meaning "ancient" or "previous," it connects the present with the wisdom of the past.
  • dūtaṁ - This means "messenger," indicating a role of communication between the divine and humans.
  • kṛṇvānā - Translates to "creating" or "making," suggesting an active role in the process of offering.
  • ayajanta - Meaning "they worshipped," referring to the act of devotion and ritualistic offerings.
  • havyaiḥ - This translates to "with offerings," indicating the material and spiritual gifts presented to the deity.
  • saṁsthe - Meaning "in the assembly," it refers to the gathering of people for worship or ritual.
  • yad - This means "that," pointing to the actions or qualities of the deity being praised.
  • agna - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes transformation.
  • īyase - Meaning "you move" or "you go," indicating the dynamic nature of the divine presence.
  • rayīṇāṁ - This translates to "of wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the blessings sought from the deity.
  • devo - Meaning "the god," reinforcing the divine aspect of Agni.
  • martair - This means "by mortals," indicating the human aspect of the worshippers.
  • vasubhir - Translates to "with the wealth," referring to the abundance and blessings from the divine.
  • idhyamānaḥ - Meaning "being invoked," it emphasizes the active engagement of the deity in the ritual.

This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, particularly through the worship of Agni. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine, invoking blessings of prosperity and abundance. The act of worship is not just a ritual but a means of communication and relationship-building with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Morning Rituals: Just as the mantra speaks of worship in the morning, individuals can start their day with gratitude and intention, setting a positive tone for the day ahead.
  • Community Gatherings: The reference to assembly highlights the importance of community in spiritual practices. Engaging in collective worship or community service can foster a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
  • Offering and Gratitude: The act of making offerings can be translated into modern practices of giving back to the community, whether through charity, volunteering, or simply expressing gratitude for the blessings in life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of rituals, community, and the divine in our lives. It encourages individuals to actively engage in their spiritual practices, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and a sense of purpose in their daily lives.
