को अद्य नार्यो देवकाम उषन्न इन्द्रस्य सख्यं जुजोषा |को वा महे 'वसे पार्याय समिद्धे अग्नौ सुतसोमा ईट्टे ||
ko adya naryo devakāma uśann indrasya sakhyaṁ jujoṣa | ko vā mahe 'vase pāryāya samiddhe agnau sutasoma īṭṭe ||
  • Mantra: "ko adya naryo devakāma uśann indrasya sakhyaṁ jujoṣa | ko vā mahe 'vase pāryāya samiddhe agnau sutasoma īṭṭe ||"
  • Translation: "Who today, desiring the divine, will join in friendship with Indra? Who, in the great assembly, will offer the Soma to the kindled Agni?"
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ko: Who
    • adya: Today
    • naryo: Women (or people)
    • devakāma: Desiring the divine
    • uśann: Will join
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • sakhyaṁ: Friendship
    • jujoṣa: Will join or participate
    • mahe: Great
    • vā: Or
    • avase: In the assembly
    • pāryāya: Offering
    • samiddhe: Kindled
    • agnau: In the fire
    • sutasoma: Soma (a sacred drink)
    • īṭṭe: Will be offered
  • Context and Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects a call to participation in a sacred ritual, emphasizing the importance of community and divine connection.
    • It highlights the role of women and individuals in seeking divine blessings and engaging in spiritual practices.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and power, while Agni symbolizes the fire that is central to Vedic rituals.
    • The mention of Soma indicates the significance of offerings in these rituals, which were believed to bring about divine favor and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, where individuals come together to seek blessings.
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can symbolize teamwork and collaboration, where individuals unite for a common goal, akin to offering their efforts to a greater cause.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and support through prayer or meditation, fostering a sense of connection with the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, divine connection, and the act of offering in both spiritual and everyday life.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking divine blessings and participating in collective rituals can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to engage actively in their spiritual journeys and to foster relationships that are supportive and uplifting.
