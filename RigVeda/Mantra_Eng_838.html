यः स्निहितीषु पूर्व्यः संजग्मानासु कृष्टिषु |अरक्षद दाशुषे गयम् ||
yaḥ snīhitīṣu pūrvyaḥ saṁjagmānāsu kṛṣṭiṣu | arakṣad dāśuṣe gayam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • snīhitīṣu: in the warm or affectionate
    • pūrvyaḥ: of the past or ancient
    • saṁjagmānāsu: in the gatherings or assemblies
    • kṛṣṭiṣu: in the cultivated or productive
    • arakṣad: protected or safeguarded
    • dāśuṣe: for the servant or devotee
    • gayam: the sacred or revered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a person who is warm and affectionate, drawing from the wisdom of the past.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and gatherings, suggesting that collective efforts in cultivation lead to protection and reverence.
    • The term "arakṣad" indicates a protective nature, highlighting the role of guardianship in relationships, especially towards those who serve or are devoted.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a parent who nurtures their children with love and warmth creates a safe environment for them to grow, much like the protective nature described in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, a leader who fosters a sense of community and collaboration among team members can cultivate a productive atmosphere, ensuring that everyone feels valued and protected.
    • In social movements, individuals who gather to support a cause demonstrate the power of collective action, where the warmth of community can protect and uplift the marginalized.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of warmth, community, and protection in our interactions.
    • It teaches us that by drawing from the wisdom of the past and fostering affectionate relationships, we can create a safe and productive environment for ourselves and others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as family dynamics, workplace culture, and social activism, reminding us of our responsibility to protect and uplift those around us.
