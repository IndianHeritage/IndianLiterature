सत्रे ह जाताव इषिता नमोभिः कुम्भे रेतः सिषिचतुः समानम् |ततो ह मान उद इयाय मध्यात ततो जातम् ऋषिम आहुर वसिष्ठम् ||
satre ha jātāv iṣitā namobhiḥ kumbhe retaḥ siṣicatuḥ samānam | tato ha māna ud iyāya madhyāt tato jātam ṛṣim āhur vasiṣṭham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satre: In the context of a gathering or assembly.
    • ha: Indeed or certainly.
    • jātāv: Born or originated.
    • iṣitā: The one who is desired or sought after.
    • namobhiḥ: With reverence or salutations.
    • kumbhe: In the pot or vessel.
    • retaḥ: Semen or vital essence.
    • siṣicatuḥ: May be poured or sprinkled.
    • samānam: Equal or similar.
    • tato: Therefore or from that.
    • ha: Indeed.
    • māna: Respect or honor.
    • ud: Up or above.
    • iyāya: Went or moved.
    • madhyāt: From the middle or center.
    • tato: Therefore.
    • jātam: Born or created.
    • ṛṣim: Sage or seer.
    • āhur: They say or declare.
    • vasiṣṭham: The best or most excellent.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a gathering where something significant is born or created.
    • The act of pouring the vital essence into a vessel symbolizes the nurturing of ideas or knowledge.
    • The reference to respect and honor suggests that the outcomes of this gathering are to be revered.
    • The mention of a sage indicates that wisdom is a key component of this process.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a brainstorming session can be seen as a 'satre' where ideas are generated and nurtured.
    • In educational institutions, collaborative projects allow students to pour their knowledge into a common goal, akin to the 'kumbhe retaḥ'.
    • Community gatherings for social causes can also reflect this mantra, where collective efforts lead to significant outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collaboration, respect, and wisdom in creating something valuable.
    • It serves as a reminder that great ideas often emerge from collective efforts and should be honored and nurtured.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and groups to come together, share their insights, and work towards a common purpose, leading to growth and innovation.
