प्रोष्ठेशयाऽवहेशयाऽनारीर्यास्तल्पशिवरीः |स्त्रियः याः पुण्यगन्धास ताः सर्वाः स्वापयामसि ||
proṣṭheśayā vahyeśayā nārīr yās talpaśīvarīḥ | striyo yāḥ puṇyagandhās tāḥ sarvāḥ svāpayāmasi ||
  • Proṣṭheśayā: This term can be interpreted as "with the support of the back" or "from behind." It signifies strength and support, often associated with the foundational aspects of life.
  • Vahyeśayā: This translates to "with the external or outward support." It emphasizes the importance of external factors and influences in our lives.
  • Nārīr: This word means "women." It highlights the role of women in society and their significance in various aspects of life.
  • Yās: This means "who" or "those who." It refers to the women being discussed in the mantra.
  • Talpaśīvarīḥ: This can be understood as "those who are like the soft bed" or "comforting." It suggests nurturing and care, qualities often associated with women.
  • Striyo: This is another term for "women," reinforcing their importance in the context of the mantra.
  • Yāḥ: This means "who" or "those who," again referring to the women mentioned.
  • Puṇyagandhās: This translates to "those who have a sacred fragrance." It symbolizes purity, virtue, and positive qualities that women embody.
  • Tāḥ: This means "they" or "those," referring to the women with sacred qualities.
  • Svāpayāmasi: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies a request or prayer for the qualities and blessings of these women.

The mantra essentially celebrates the strength, nurturing qualities, and sacredness of women. It acknowledges their role as foundational support in society and invokes their virtues for blessings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Empowerment of Women: In contemporary society, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of empowering women and recognizing their contributions in all fields, from family to leadership roles.
  • Celebrating Motherhood: The nurturing aspect highlighted in the mantra can be reflected in the way we honor and celebrate mothers and caregivers, acknowledging their sacrifices and love.
  • Promoting Gender Equality: The mantra can inspire movements aimed at achieving gender equality, emphasizing that women’s qualities are essential for a balanced and harmonious society.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the reverence for women and their vital role in society. It encourages us to recognize and celebrate their contributions, fostering an environment where their strengths are acknowledged and valued. By invoking the qualities of women, we can aspire to create a more compassionate and equitable world.
