उत सूर्यो बृहद् आर्चींष्य अश्रेत पुरु विश्वा जनिम मानवशाणाम् |समो दिवा ददृशे रोचमानः क्रत्वा कृतः सुखृतः कर्तृभिर भूत् ||
ut sūryo bṛhad arcīṁṣy aśret puru viśvā janima mānuṣāṇām | samo divā dadṛśe rocamānaḥ kratvā kṛtaḥ sukṛtaḥ kartṛbhir bhūt ||
  • ut sūryo: This phrase translates to "as the sun rises." The sun is often seen as a symbol of enlightenment, knowledge, and vitality in many cultures. In real life, the rising sun signifies the start of a new day, bringing hope and opportunities.
  • bṛhad arcīṁṣy: This means "with great rays." The rays of the sun can be seen as a metaphor for the spread of knowledge and wisdom. Just as sunlight nourishes plants, knowledge nourishes the mind and spirit.
  • aśret puru: This can be interpreted as "the one who has created." It refers to the divine or the creator. In a real-life context, this can relate to the idea of creation in various forms, whether it be art, technology, or relationships.
  • viśvā janima mānuṣāṇām: This translates to "the universal birth of humanity." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all human beings. In today's world, this can be seen in the global community where people from different backgrounds come together to address common challenges.
  • samo divā: This means "equal by day." It suggests fairness and equality. In real life, this can relate to the importance of equality in society, where everyone deserves the same rights and opportunities regardless of their background.
  • dadṛśe rocamānaḥ: This translates to "shining brightly." It signifies the brilliance of truth and righteousness. In practical terms, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice and integrity, illuminating the path for others.
  • kratvā kṛtaḥ sukṛtaḥ: This means "by actions performed, good deeds are done." It emphasizes the importance of actions in creating positive outcomes. In real life, this can be exemplified by community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others through their actions.
  • kartṛbhir bhūt: This translates to "by the doers, beings are created." It highlights the role of individuals in shaping the world around them. In a real-life scenario, this can be seen in entrepreneurs and innovators who create new opportunities and solutions for society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, knowledge, and the interconnectedness of humanity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of good deeds and the role of individuals in shaping a better world. In real life, it encourages us to embrace knowledge, act with integrity, and work towards equality and justice for all. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community building, social justice movements, and personal development, guiding us to live a life of purpose and contribution.
