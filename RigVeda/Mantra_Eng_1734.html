ये अरवाँचस तां उ पराच आहुर ये पराँचस तां उ अरवाच आहुः | इन्द्रश्च या चक्रथुः सोम तानि धुरा न युक्ता रजसो वहन्ति ||
ye arvāñcas tām̐ u parāca āhur ye parāñcas tām̐ u arvāca āhuḥ | indraś ca yā cakrathuḥ soma tāni dhurā na yuktā rajaso vahanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • arvāñcas: who are wise or knowledgeable
    • tām̐: that
    • u: and
    • parāca: who are beyond or superior
    • āhur: they say

    • parāñcas: who are far or distant
    • tām̐: that

    • arvāca: who are wise or knowledgeable

    • indraś: Indra (the king of gods)

    • soma: the divine nectar or elixir

    • rajaso: of the passions or desires

  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of wisdom and knowledge.
    • It contrasts two types of wise individuals: those who are knowledgeable and those who are beyond knowledge.
    • Indra, the king of gods, is mentioned as a creator of the divine nectar (soma), which symbolizes enlightenment and spiritual awakening.
    • The mantra suggests that the burdens of desires and passions are not suitable for those who seek true wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who is knowledgeable about their field (arvāñcas) may be effective, but a leader who is visionary and understands the broader implications of their decisions (parāca) can drive the organization towards greater success.
    • In personal development, individuals who pursue knowledge (arvāñcas) may find fulfillment, but those who transcend mere knowledge to seek wisdom (parāñcas) often experience deeper satisfaction and purpose.
    • In spiritual practices, the pursuit of enlightenment (soma) often requires letting go of worldly desires (rajaso), allowing individuals to focus on higher truths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking true wisdom beyond mere knowledge.
    • It highlights the need to transcend desires and passions to achieve spiritual enlightenment.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal growth, and spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to strive for a higher understanding and purpose.
