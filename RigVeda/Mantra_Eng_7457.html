तं वो दश्मं ऋतीषहं वसोर मन्दानम् अन्धसः |अभि वत्सं न स्वसरेषु धेनव इन्द्रं गीरभिर नवामहे ||
taṁ vo dasmam ṛtīṣahaṁ vasor mandānam andhasaḥ | abhi vatsaṁ na svasareṣu dhenava indraṁ gīrbhir navāmahe ||
  • taṁ vo dasmam ṛtīṣahaṁ vasor mandānam andhasaḥ: This line can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically addressing Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition. The phrase "taṁ vo" translates to "that we offer to you," indicating a sense of devotion and reverence. "Dasmam" refers to the tenth, which can symbolize completeness or perfection. "Ṛtīṣahaṁ" suggests the embodiment of truth and order, while "vasor mandānam andhasaḥ" can be understood as the essence of the universe, representing the cosmic order and the hidden aspects of existence.
  • abhi vatsaṁ na svasareṣu dhenava indraṁ gīrbhir navāmahe: This line expresses a desire to invoke Indra with praises. "Abhi vatsaṁ" means "we approach you," and "na svasareṣu" implies a connection to the nurturing aspects of life, akin to a mother cow providing milk. "Dhenava" refers to the cows, which are symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. "Indraṁ gīrbhir navāmahe" translates to "we sing praises to Indra," highlighting the importance of worship and the power of words in invoking divine blessings.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of seeking guidance and support from higher powers or the universe. For instance, individuals often turn to meditation or prayer during challenging times, invoking a sense of peace and clarity. Just as the mantra calls upon Indra for blessings, people today may seek strength and wisdom from their spiritual beliefs.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, order, and divine connection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for harmony in life. By invoking Indra, the mantra encourages individuals to seek balance and support from the universe, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or community gatherings. It can be recited to invoke blessings for prosperity, health, and harmony in relationships. Additionally, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the nurturing aspects of life, much like the cow symbolizes sustenance and care.
