वृषा जजाना वृषणं रणाय तं उ चिन् नारी नर्यं ससूव |प्र यः सेनानीर अध नृभ्यो अस्तीनः सत्वा गवेषणः स धृष्णुः ||
vṛṣā jajāna vṛṣaṇaṁ raṇāya tam u cin nārī naryaṁ sasūva | pra yaḥ senānīr adha nṛbhyo astīnaḥ satvā gaveṣaṇaḥ sa dhṛṣṇuḥ ||
  • vṛṣā jajāna: This phrase can be translated as "the strong one has been born." In a real-life context, this can refer to the emergence of a leader or a strong individual in a community who takes charge during challenging times.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ raṇāya: This means "for the purpose of battle." It signifies the readiness to face challenges or conflicts. For example, in a corporate setting, this could relate to a team preparing to tackle a significant project or competition.
  • tam u cin: This translates to "that one should be recognized." It emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and honoring those who take on leadership roles or exhibit strength. In everyday life, this could be seen in how communities celebrate their heroes or leaders.
  • nārī naryaṁ sasūva: This phrase means "the woman has given birth to a hero." It highlights the role of women in nurturing and bringing forth strength in society. In modern contexts, this can be related to mothers raising strong, capable children who contribute positively to society.
  • pra yaḥ senānīr: This translates to "the leader of the army." It signifies the importance of having a strong leader in any endeavor, whether in military, business, or community efforts. For instance, a project manager leading a team towards a successful project completion embodies this concept.
  • adha nṛbhyo astīnaḥ: This means "to the men who are strong." It acknowledges the strength and valor of men in society. In real life, this can be seen in various professions where physical or mental strength is required, such as in sports or emergency services.
  • satvā gaveṣaṇaḥ: This translates to "the search for strength." It emphasizes the pursuit of inner strength and resilience. In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate their strengths through education, training, and self-reflection.
  • sa dhṛṣṇuḥ: This means "he who is courageous." It highlights the value of courage in facing life's challenges. In real life, acts of bravery, whether in standing up for justice or taking risks in business, reflect this quality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, leadership, and the vital roles played by both men and women in society. It signifies the importance of recognizing and nurturing strength in individuals, whether they are leaders, nurturers, or warriors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in family dynamics, community leadership, and personal development. It serves as a reminder that strength comes in many forms and is essential for overcoming challenges and achieving success.
