विभु प्रभु प्रथमं मेहनावतो बृहस्पतेः सुविदात्राणि राध्या |इमा सातानि वेन्यस्य वाजिनो येन जनाः उभये भुञ्जते विषः ||
vibhu prabhu prathamam mehanāvato bṛhaspateḥ suvidatrāṇi rādhyā | imā sātāni venyasya vājino yena janā ubhaye bhuñjate viśaḥ ||
  • Vibhu Prabhu: This phrase refers to the "all-pervading Lord" or "the great master." It signifies the omnipresence and omnipotence of the divine.
  • Prathamam: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word emphasizes the importance of the divine in all aspects of life.
  • Mehanāvato: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth rain" or "the rain-giver," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • Bṛhaspateḥ: Referring to the deity Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence, this word highlights the importance of knowledge and guidance in our lives.
  • Suvidatrāṇi: This means "well-being" or "good fortune," indicating the blessings that come from the divine.
  • Rādhyā: This term signifies "prosperity" or "success," suggesting that divine blessings lead to fruitful outcomes.
  • Imā sātāni: Translated as "these seven," it may refer to the seven aspects of life or the seven elements that contribute to a fulfilling existence.
  • Venyasya: This word can be understood as "of the one who is worthy," indicating the importance of deserving blessings through righteous living.
  • Vājino: Meaning "of the strong" or "the powerful," this term emphasizes strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • Yena: This means "by whom," indicating the source of blessings and support in our lives.
  • Janā: Referring to "people" or "humanity," this word emphasizes the collective experience of life.
  • Ubhaye: Meaning "both," it signifies the dual aspects of existence, such as material and spiritual, or individual and collective.
  • Bhuñjate: This term means "to enjoy" or "to partake," indicating the act of receiving and utilizing blessings.
  • Viśaḥ: Referring to "the universe" or "the community," this word encompasses all beings and the interconnectedness of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, nourishment, and prosperity in life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and blessings from the divine, which can lead to well-being and success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for prosperity, seeking guidance in difficult times, or fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness. By acknowledging the divine presence and striving for righteousness, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and prosperous life, benefiting both themselves and the larger community.
