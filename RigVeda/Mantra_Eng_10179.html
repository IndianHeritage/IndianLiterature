यश्चिदापो महिना पर्यपश्यद् दक्षं दधानाऽ जनयन्तीर्यज्ञम् |यो देवेष्वधि देव एक आसीत कसमै देवाय हविषा विद्हेम ||
yaś cid āpo mahinā paryapaśyad dakṣaṁ dadhānā janayantīr yajñam | yo deveṣv adhi deva eka āsīt kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaś: who
    • cid: indeed
    • āpo: waters
    • mahinā: with might
    • paryapaśyad: observed
    • dakṣaṁ: skilled
    • dadhānā: holding
    • janayantīr: creating
    • yajñam: sacrifice
    • yo: who
    • deveṣv: among the gods
    • adhi: above
    • deva: god
    • eka: one
    • āsīt: existed
    • kasmai: to whom
    • devāya: to the god
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • vidhema: we offer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters that are powerful and creative, suggesting that they are a source of life and nourishment.
    • The skilled creator, who is capable of performing sacrifices, is highlighted, indicating the importance of skill and intention in rituals.
    • The mantra also refers to a singular divine presence among the gods, emphasizing the unity of the divine and the importance of devotion to that one god.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the significance of water is paramount. Just as the mantra highlights the creative power of water, farmers rely on it for crop growth, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • In modern life, the concept of skilled craftsmanship can be seen in various professions, from artists to engineers, where dedication and skill lead to the creation of valuable offerings to society.
    • The idea of devotion to a singular divine presence can be reflected in various spiritual practices where individuals focus their prayers and offerings to a specific deity, fostering a sense of connection and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, skill, and devotion, reminding us of the interconnectedness of nature, human effort, and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to recognize the power of water as a life-giving force, the importance of skill in our endeavors, and the value of focused devotion in our spiritual practices.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by respecting natural resources, honing our skills, and cultivating a sense of devotion in our daily lives, leading to a more harmonious existence.
