नयसीद वति द्विषः कृतोष्य उक्तशंसिनः | नृभिः सुवीर उच्यसे ||
nayasīd v ati dviṣaḥ kṛṇoṣy ukthaśaṁsinaḥ | nṛbhiḥ suvīra ucyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nayasīd: May you lead or guide.
    • v: And.
    • ati: Beyond or exceedingly.
    • dviṣaḥ: Enemies or those who harbor hatred.
    • kṛṇoṣy: You create or you make.
    • ukthaśaṁsinaḥ: Of those who sing praises or hymns.
    • nṛbhiḥ: By men or among people.
    • suvīra: Good heroes or valiant ones.
    • ucyase: You are spoken of or you are declared.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance and leadership in overcoming enmity and negativity.
    • The phrase "May you lead" suggests a call for divine or spiritual guidance in navigating life's challenges.
    • It acknowledges the presence of adversaries ("dviṣaḥ") and the need to rise above them through positive actions and intentions.
    • The reference to "those who sing praises" indicates the value of community and collective effort in achieving greatness.
    • "Good heroes" signifies the ideal qualities of courage, strength, and virtue that are celebrated in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a leader who guides their team through challenges can help mitigate conflicts and foster a positive environment.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who seek to understand and resolve differences rather than harboring resentment embody the essence of this mantra.
    • Community leaders who inspire collective action against social issues demonstrate the principles of guidance and heroism highlighted in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of positive leadership and the importance of rising above negativity.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance and to act as "good heroes" in their communities, promoting harmony and collaboration.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, effective teamwork, and a more supportive society.
