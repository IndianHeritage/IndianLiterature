वनस्पतिम पवमान मध्वा सम अङ्ग्धि धारणया | सहस्रवल्शं हरितं भ्राजमानं हिरण्यं ||
vanaspatim pavamāna madhvā sam aṅgdhi dhārayā | sahasravalśaṁ haritam bhrājamānaṁ hiraṇyayam ||
  • Vanaspatim: This term refers to the "lord of the forest" or "plant life." It symbolizes nature, vegetation, and the interconnectedness of all living beings. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of forests and plants in maintaining ecological balance.
  • Pavamāna: This word means "purifying" or "that which is pure." It signifies the cleansing aspect of nature and the importance of purity in our lives. For example, clean air and water are essential for health and well-being.
  • Madhvā: This translates to "sweetness" or "honey." It represents the sweetness of life and the joy that comes from nature. In everyday life, this can be related to the enjoyment of natural foods, like fruits and honey, which provide nourishment and pleasure.
  • Sam aṅgdhi dhārayā: This phrase means "may it be well established." It emphasizes the idea of stability and support in life. In practical terms, this can refer to the importance of strong relationships and community support in achieving personal and collective goals.
  • Sahasravalśaṁ: This term means "thousand-fold" or "abundant." It signifies the richness and diversity of nature. In real life, this can be observed in the variety of species in an ecosystem and the abundance of resources that nature provides.
  • Haritam: This translates to "green" or "prosperous." It symbolizes growth, fertility, and the vibrancy of life. For instance, a thriving garden or a lush forest represents the health of the environment and the importance of sustainability.
  • Bhrājamānaṁ: This means "shining" or "radiant." It reflects the beauty and brilliance of nature. In real life, this can be seen in the way sunlight illuminates the world, bringing warmth and life to all living beings.
  • Hiraṇyayam: This term means "golden" or "wealthy." It symbolizes prosperity and abundance. In practical terms, this can relate to the wealth of resources that nature provides, as well as the spiritual wealth that comes from living in harmony with the environment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nature and its purifying, nurturing, and abundant qualities. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the environment. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, appreciate the beauty of nature, and recognize the vital role that plants and ecosystems play in our well-being. By honoring the "lord of the forest," we acknowledge our responsibility to protect and preserve the natural world for future generations.
