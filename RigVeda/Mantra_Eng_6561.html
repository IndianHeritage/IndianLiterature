नū अन्यत्रा चिद अद्रिवस् त्वन् नो जग्मुर आशसः | मगवञ्च चाग्धि तव तन् न ऊतिभिः ||
nū anyatrā cid adrivas tvan no jagmur āśasaḥ | maghavañ chagdhi tava tan na ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nū: indeed
    • anyatrā: elsewhere
    • cid: at all
    • adrivas: without a path
    • tvan: your
    • no: us
    • jagmur: have gone
    • āśasaḥ: to the desires
    • maghavañ: O Mighty One
    • chagdhi: grant
    • tava: your
    • tan: that
    • na: not
    • ūtibhiḥ: by the powers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for guidance and support from a higher power, often interpreted as a divine entity or force.
    • The phrase "nū anyatrā cid adrivas" suggests that without a clear path or direction, one cannot find fulfillment or reach their goals.
    • The invocation of "maghavañ" refers to a powerful deity, emphasizing the need for divine assistance in navigating life's challenges.
    • The request for "chagdhi" indicates a desire for blessings and support, highlighting the importance of seeking help from the divine in times of uncertainty.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, individuals often seek mentorship or guidance when faced with career decisions, similar to seeking divine guidance in the mantra.
    • During times of personal crisis, people may turn to spiritual practices, such as prayer or meditation, to find clarity and direction, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community settings, collective prayers or rituals are performed to seek blessings for success and harmony, paralleling the invocation for divine support in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking guidance and support from a higher power when faced with uncertainty and challenges in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may strive for our goals, the journey often requires assistance beyond our own capabilities.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek help, whether through spiritual practices, mentorship, or community support, reinforcing the interconnectedness of human experience and divine influence.
