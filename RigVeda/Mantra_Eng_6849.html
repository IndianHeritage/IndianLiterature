जिव्हाभिर अह नन्नमद आर्चिषा जन्जणाभवन् |अग्निर वनेषु रोचते ||
jihvābhir aha nannamad arciṣā jañjaṇābhavan | agnir vaneṣu rocate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jihvābhir: With tongues (or speech)
    • aha: I (or my)
    • nannamad: Not to be denied (or not to be neglected)
    • arciṣā: By the light (or by the radiance)
    • jañjaṇābhavan: Having become (or having manifested)
    • agnir: Fire (or the divine fire)
    • vaneṣu: In the forests (or in the wilderness)
    • rocate: Shines (or radiates)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) that shines in the wilderness, emphasizing the importance of speech and expression.
    • The phrase "jihvābhir aha nannamad" suggests that one's speech should not be neglected or denied, highlighting the power of words in shaping reality.
    • The reference to "arciṣā" indicates that this expression or speech is illuminated by a higher truth or light, suggesting that our words should be guided by wisdom.
    • Agni, as a symbol of transformation and purification, signifies the energy that fuels our intentions and actions, especially in the natural world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when individuals express their thoughts and feelings openly, it fosters understanding and connection, much like the divine fire that illuminates the forest.
    • In personal relationships, using words wisely and with intention can lead to healing and growth, akin to how Agni purifies and transforms.
    • In environmental discussions, recognizing the importance of nature (the forests) and advocating for its protection can be seen as a modern application of this mantra, where our voices (jihvā) play a crucial role.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of speech and expression, urging individuals to use their words wisely and with intention.
    • It highlights the connection between our inner light (wisdom) and the outer world (nature), suggesting that our words can create positive change.
    • In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to communicate authentically, respect the power of language, and recognize our role in the larger ecosystem.
