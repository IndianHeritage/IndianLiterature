पूषण तव व्रते वयं न ऋष्यम कदाचन |स्तोतारस्त इह स्मसि ||
pūṣan tava vrate vayaṁ na riṣyema kadā cana | stotāras ta iha smasi ||
  • pūṣan: This term refers to the deity Pūṣan, who is associated with nourishment, growth, and the sun. In a broader sense, Pūṣan symbolizes the life-giving aspects of nature.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or connection to the deity Pūṣan.
  • vrate: This word translates to "vow" or "sacred promise." It signifies a commitment to a particular path or principle.
  • vayaṁ: This means "we," referring to the collective group or community that is invoking Pūṣan.
  • na: This translates to "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • riṣyema: This means "we may suffer" or "we may be harmed." It expresses a concern for well-being and protection.
  • kadā cana: This phrase means "at any time" or "ever," emphasizing the timeless nature of the plea.
  • stotāras: This refers to "those who praise" or "worshippers," indicating the act of devotion and reverence towards Pūṣan.
  • ta iha: This means "here" or "in this place," grounding the invocation in the present moment.
  • smasi: This translates to "you will be," indicating a future promise or assurance from the deity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a heartfelt invocation to Pūṣan, expressing a collective vow to remain steadfast and protected under his guidance. The worshippers seek assurance that they will not suffer or face harm at any time, emphasizing the importance of divine protection in their lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to stay committed to their personal goals and values, seeking nourishment and support from their environment.
  • Community Support: Groups or communities can invoke this mantra during gatherings or rituals to foster a sense of unity and collective strength, ensuring that they stand together against challenges.
  • Spiritual Practice: Practitioners can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to cultivate a sense of safety and divine protection in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of divine support and the importance of commitment to one's values and community. It serves as a reminder that through devotion and collective strength, individuals can navigate life's challenges with confidence and assurance of protection.
