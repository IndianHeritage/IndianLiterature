यस्त्वाम् अग्ने हविष्पतिर् दूतं देव सपर्णति |तस्य स्म प्राविताभव ||
yas tvām agne haviṣpatir dūtaṁ deva saparyati | tasya sma prāvitā bhava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvām: you
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • haviṣpatir: the lord of offerings
    • dūtaṁ: messenger
    • deva: divine or god
    • saparyati: worships or honors
    • tasya: of him
    • sma: indeed
    • prāvitā: be filled or be nourished
    • bhava: become or may you become
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, who is considered the messenger between humans and the divine. It expresses a prayer for Agni to be honored and to ensure that the offerings made to him are accepted, leading to nourishment and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings). The fire acts as a witness and a medium for the offerings to reach the divine.
    • In a modern context, the concept of 'Agni' can be seen in the way people seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders who act as messengers of wisdom and knowledge.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of honoring the divine and recognizing the role of intermediaries in our spiritual practices.
    • In daily life, it serves as a reminder to respect and acknowledge those who guide us, whether they are spiritual leaders, teachers, or even friends who provide support.
    • It encourages individuals to make offerings of gratitude, whether through prayers, acts of kindness, or contributions to the community, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and with each other.
