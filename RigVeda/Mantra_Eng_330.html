शतं वा यः शुचीनां सहस्रं वा समाशिराम् |एदु निम्नं न रीयते ||
śataṁ vā yaḥ śucīnāṁ sahasraṁ vā samāśirām | ed u nimnaṁ na rīyate ||
  • śataṁ vā yaḥ: This phrase translates to "one who is a hundred" or "one who possesses a hundred." It signifies abundance and the idea of having a multitude of qualities or virtues.
  • śucīnāṁ: This word means "of the pure" or "of the virtuous." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • sahasraṁ vā samāśirām: This translates to "or one who is a thousand" or "one who possesses a thousand." It suggests an even greater abundance, indicating that the virtues of the pure are limitless.
  • ed u nimnaṁ na rīyate: This phrase means "this does not diminish." It conveys the idea that the qualities of the virtuous do not decrease or fade away, regardless of circumstances.

The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity and abundance in one's character. It suggests that whether one possesses a hundred or a thousand virtues, the essence of these qualities remains intact and does not diminish over time. This can be related to real-life scenarios where individuals who embody virtues such as honesty, compassion, and integrity continue to shine, regardless of challenges they face.


For example, consider a teacher who is dedicated to their students. Their commitment to education and the well-being of their students can be seen as a hundred virtues. Even when faced with difficulties, such as lack of resources or support, their dedication does not wane. Similarly, a community leader who works tirelessly for the betterment of their community embodies a thousand virtues, and their efforts continue to inspire others, demonstrating that true virtue is resilient.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of cultivating purity and abundance in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for virtuous qualities, knowing that these attributes will not diminish but will instead contribute to a more meaningful and impactful existence. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community service, where the focus on virtue leads to lasting positive change.
