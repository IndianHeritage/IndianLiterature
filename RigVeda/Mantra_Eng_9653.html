यस्ते मन्योऽविदद् वज्र सायक सह ओजः पुष्यति विश्वम आन्नुशक |साह्यामा दासम् आर्यं त्वया युजा सहस्कृतेन सहसा सहस्वता ||
yas te manyo 'vidhad vajra sāyaka saha ojaḥ puṣyati viśvam ānuṣak | sāhyāma dāsam āryaṁ tvayā yujā sahaskṛtena sahasā sahasvatā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • manyo: thought or mind
    • vidhad: has created or established
    • vajra: thunderbolt or strong
    • sāyaka: weapon or tool
    • saha: together with
    • ojaḥ: energy or strength
    • puṣyati: nourishes or supports
    • viśvam: the universe or all
    • ānuṣak: in accordance with
    • sāhyāma: may we be united
    • dāsam: servant or devotee
    • āryaṁ: noble or honorable
    • tvayā: by you
    • yujā: may we be joined
    • sahaskṛtena: with strength
    • sahasā: with great effort
    • sahasvatā: with abundance or prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine strength and energy that supports and nourishes the universe.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration, suggesting that when individuals come together with a noble purpose, they can achieve great things.
    • The reference to "vajra sāyaka" symbolizes the power and effectiveness of one's actions when aligned with a higher purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members collaborate effectively, they can produce outstanding results, much like the "vajra" that signifies strength and effectiveness.
    • In community service, when individuals unite for a common cause, such as environmental conservation, their collective efforts can lead to significant positive changes.
    • In personal relationships, when partners support each other with strength and unity, they can overcome challenges and thrive together.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, strength, and collaboration in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that when we align our thoughts and actions with a noble purpose and work together, we can nourish not only ourselves but also the world around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
