त्वं चित्ती तव दाक्षैर दिव आ पृथिव्याऽऋजीषिन् |यावीर अगस्य चिद् द्वेषः ||
tvaṁ cittī tava dakṣair diva ā pṛthivyā ṛjīṣin | yāvīr aghasya cid dveṣaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ cittī: This phrase translates to "You are the consciousness" or "You are the mind." It emphasizes the idea that the essence of a person is their consciousness, which is the source of thoughts, emotions, and awareness.
  • tava dakṣair: This means "by your skill" or "through your abilities." It highlights the importance of one's skills and capabilities in achieving goals and navigating life.
  • dva ā pṛthivyā ṛjīṣin: This can be interpreted as "in the heavens and on the earth." It signifies the connection between the spiritual and the material realms, suggesting that consciousness and skills are applicable in both dimensions.
  • yāvīr aghasya: This phrase translates to "the strength of the brave." It refers to the courage and valor that individuals possess, which are essential for overcoming challenges.
  • cid dveṣaḥ: This means "the hatred of consciousness." It points to the negative emotions and thoughts that can arise within the mind, which can hinder personal growth and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human experience, emphasizing the significance of consciousness, skills, courage, and the need to overcome negative emotions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, professional growth, and interpersonal relationships. For instance, in a workplace setting, recognizing one's skills (tava dakṣair) and maintaining a positive mindset (overcoming cid dveṣaḥ) can lead to better teamwork and productivity. Similarly, in personal life, cultivating consciousness (tvaṁ cittī) and bravery (yāvīr aghasya) can help individuals face challenges with resilience and grace. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of mind, skill, and spirit in navigating life's journey.
