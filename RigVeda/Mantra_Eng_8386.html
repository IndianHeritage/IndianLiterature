एतानि सोम पवमानो अस्मयुः सत्यानि कृत्वान द्रविणानि अर्षसि |जहि शत्रुम अन्तिके दूरके च य उर्वीं गव्युतिम अभयं च नस् कृधि ||
etāni soma pavamāno asmayuḥ satyāni kṛṇvan draviṇāny arṣasi | jahi śatrum antike dūrake ca ya urvīṁ gavyūtim abhayaṁ ca nas kṛdhi ||
  • etāni: This word translates to "these" or "these things." It indicates a focus on specific elements or offerings.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and is considered a source of strength and vitality.
  • pavamāno: This means "purifying" or "flowing." It signifies the cleansing aspect of the Soma, which is essential for spiritual and physical well-being.
  • asmayuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the essence of stone" or "the strength of stone." It symbolizes stability, endurance, and resilience.
  • satyāni: This translates to "truths" or "truthful." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • kṛṇvan: This means "creating" or "making." It suggests an active role in manifesting one's desires and intentions.
  • draviṇāny: This refers to "wealth" or "resources." It highlights the significance of material abundance and prosperity in life.
  • arṣasi: This can be interpreted as "to attain" or "to achieve." It indicates the pursuit of goals and aspirations.
  • jahi: This means "to conquer" or "to overcome." It signifies the need to face and defeat obstacles or adversaries.
  • śatrum: This translates to "enemy" or "foe." It represents challenges or negative forces that one must confront.
  • antike: This means "in front" or "nearby." It indicates the immediacy of the challenges faced.
  • dūrake: This translates to "far away." It suggests that some challenges may be distant but still relevant.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting thoughts and ideas.
  • ya: This translates to "who" or "that." It introduces a clause about the qualities or actions of the subject.
  • urvīṁ: This means "earth" or "land." It symbolizes the physical realm and the foundation of life.
  • gavyūtim: This refers to "abundance" or "prosperity," often associated with cattle and wealth in Vedic culture.
  • abhayaṁ: This means "fearlessness" or "safety." It emphasizes the importance of security and confidence in one's endeavors.
  • ca: Again, this means "and," reinforcing the connection between ideas.
  • nas: This translates to "us" or "our." It indicates a collective aspect, emphasizing community and shared experiences.
  • kṛdhi: This means "to do" or "to make." It calls for action and the realization of intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of purity, truth, resilience, and the pursuit of prosperity while overcoming challenges. In real life, it serves as a reminder to maintain integrity and courage in the face of adversity. By invoking the qualities of Soma, individuals can seek spiritual and material abundance, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, business endeavors, and community building, encouraging individuals to act with intention and fearlessness in their pursuits.
