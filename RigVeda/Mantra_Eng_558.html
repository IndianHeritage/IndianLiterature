त्रिवन्धुरेण त्रिवृतā सुपेशसā रथेनā यातम अश्विनā |कण्वासो वाम ब्रह्म कृत्वन्त्य अध्वरे तेषां सु श्रिणुतं हवम् ||
trivandhureṇa trivṛtā supeśasā rathenā yātam aśvinā | kaṇvāso vām brahma kṛṇvanty adhvare teṣāṁ su śṛṇutaṁ havam ||
  • Mantra: "trivandhureṇa trivṛtā supeśasā rathenā yātam aśvinā | kaṇvāso vām brahma kṛṇvanty adhvare teṣāṁ su śṛṇutaṁ havam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trivandhureṇa: With three bindings or connections.
    • trivṛtā: Threefold or triple.
    • supeśasā: With good or auspicious attire.
    • rathenā: By the chariot.
    • yātam: May you go or proceed.
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities of health and medicine).
    • kaṇvāso: O descendants of Kanva (a sage).
    • vām: Your.
    • brahma: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit.
    • kṛṇvanty: They create or make.
    • adhvare: In the sacrifice or ritual.
    • teṣāṁ: Of them.
    • su: Well or good.
    • śṛṇutaṁ: Listen or hear.
    • havam: The offering or invocation.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and well-being.
    • The phrase "trivandhureṇa trivṛtā" suggests a connection to the three realms: physical, mental, and spiritual, emphasizing a holistic approach to life.
    • "Supeśasā" indicates the importance of being well-prepared and adorned, which can be related to how we present ourselves in society, such as dressing appropriately for an interview or a significant event.
    • The mention of "rathenā" symbolizes the journey of life, where the chariot represents our path and the choices we make.
    • The invocation to the "descendants of Kanva" highlights the importance of lineage and tradition, reminding us of our roots and the wisdom passed down through generations.
    • "Brahma kṛṇvanty" signifies the creation of a sacred space or intention during rituals, which can be applied in modern practices like meditation or mindfulness, where we create a conducive environment for inner peace.
    • The call to "listen" to the offerings emphasizes the importance of being receptive to guidance and wisdom, whether from nature, elders, or our own inner voice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.
    • It encourages us to approach life with intention, preparation, and respect for our heritage.
    • In real-life scenarios, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by being mindful of our health, making informed choices, and honoring our traditions while being open to new experiences.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of listening to the wisdom around us and within us, guiding us on our journey through life.
