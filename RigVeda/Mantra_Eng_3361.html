विष्वस्मात् सीम अधमाम् इन्द्र दस्यून् विषो दासीर अकृत्नोर अप्रशस्ताः |अबाद्धेताम् अमृततं नि शत्रून् अविन्देथाम् अपचितिं वधत्रैः ||
viśvasmāt sīm adhamām̐ indra dasyūn viśo dāsīr akṛṇor apraśastāḥ | abādhethām amṛṇataṁ ni śatrūn avindethām apacitiṁ vadhatraiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvasmāt: From all directions or from the universe.
    • sīm: Protection or boundary.
    • adhamām̐: The lowest or the most inferior.
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods.
    • dasyūn: The enemies or those who oppose.
    • viśo: Of the people or community.
    • dāsīr: The servants or followers.
    • akṛṇor: Who do not act or do not engage.
    • apraśastāḥ: Those who are not praiseworthy or commendable.
    • abādhethām: May they not harm or injure.
    • amṛṇataṁ: Immortality or eternal life.
    • ni: Not.
    • śatrūn: The enemies.
    • avindethām: May they not find or discover.
    • apacitiṁ: The punishment or retribution.
    • vadhatraiḥ: By the means of killing or destruction.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a plea for protection from all forms of negativity and harm.
    • It invokes Indra, the god of strength and protection, to safeguard the community from enemies and those who do not act in a commendable manner.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective strength in facing adversities.
    • It highlights the desire for peace and the avoidance of conflict, urging that those who wish harm should not be able to inflict injury.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community facing external threats, such as violence or discrimination, this mantra can serve as a reminder to come together for mutual protection.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra during times of stress or conflict, seeking inner strength and resilience against negativity.
    • Organizations can use the essence of this mantra to foster a culture of support and protection among members, ensuring that harmful behaviors are addressed collectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, protection, and the pursuit of peace.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community issues, reminding us of the power of collective strength against adversity.
    • By invoking the divine protection of Indra, individuals and communities can find solace and courage in the face of challenges, fostering a spirit of resilience and harmony.
