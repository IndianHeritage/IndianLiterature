अनु श्रुताम् अमतिं वर्धद उर्वीम् बर्हिर इव यजुषा रक्षमााणा |नमस्वन्ता धृतदक्षाधि गर्ते मित्रासाथे वरुणेऽलास्व अन्तः ||
anu śrutām amatiṁ vardhad urvīm barhir iva yajuṣā rakṣamāṇā | namasvantā dhṛtadakṣādhi garte mitrāsāthe varuṇeḻāsv antaḥ ||
  • anu śrutām: This phrase translates to "having heard" or "after listening." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom gained through listening and learning.
  • amatiṁ: This word means "intellect" or "understanding." It signifies the mental capacity to comprehend and discern.
  • vardhad: This means "to increase" or "to grow." It suggests the idea of enhancing one's understanding and wisdom.
  • urvīm: This translates to "the earth" or "the vastness." It symbolizes the foundation of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • barhir iva: This phrase means "like a bird" or "like a feather." It can represent lightness, freedom, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • yajuṣā: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It highlights the importance of giving and contributing to the greater good.
  • rakṣamāṇā: This means "protecting" or "guarding." It signifies the need for safeguarding knowledge, wisdom, and the environment.
  • namasvantā: This translates to "those who bow" or "the respectful." It emphasizes humility and reverence towards knowledge and the divine.
  • dhṛtadakṣādhi: This phrase means "with steadfastness and skill." It signifies the importance of being resolute and capable in one's actions.
  • garte: This means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It highlights the significance of community and collective wisdom.
  • mitrāsāthe: This translates to "in the company of friends." It emphasizes the value of companionship and support in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • varuṇeḻāsv: This refers to "in the presence of Varuna," the deity associated with cosmic order and truth. It signifies the importance of aligning oneself with universal principles.
  • antaḥ: This means "within" or "inside." It suggests introspection and the inner journey of understanding.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of listening, understanding, and growing in wisdom. It highlights the importance of community, humility, and the protection of knowledge and the environment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, where listening and learning from others is crucial for personal growth. It also underscores the importance of collaboration and support among friends and community members in achieving common goals. By aligning ourselves with universal truths and principles, we can navigate life's challenges with greater understanding and purpose.
