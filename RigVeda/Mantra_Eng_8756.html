बिभर्ति चर्व इन्द्रस्य नाम येन विश्वानि वृत्रा जग्हान ||
bibharti cārv indrasya nāma yena viśvāni vṛtrā jaghāna ||
  • Mantra: "bibharti cārv indrasya nāma yena viśvāni vṛtrā jaghāna"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bibharti: carries, supports
    • cārv: the essence, the core
    • indrasya: of Indra, the king of gods
    • nāma: name, identity
    • yena: by which
    • viśvāni: all, the universe
    • vṛtrā: the obstacles, the demons
    • jaghāna: has slain, has overcome
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and strength of Indra, who is often regarded as the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing the force of nature and the protector of humanity.
    • The phrase "bibharti cārv indrasya" suggests that the essence or core strength of Indra supports and carries the universe. This can be likened to how leadership and guidance in our lives can provide strength and direction.
    • "Yena viśvāni vṛtrā jaghāna" indicates that through Indra's name or essence, all obstacles (vṛtrā) are overcome. In real life, this can be interpreted as the power of belief and faith in overcoming challenges. For instance, when individuals face personal or professional hurdles, drawing strength from a higher purpose or belief can help them persevere.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to overcome challenges, much like Indra overcoming the demons. This can be seen in successful companies where leaders motivate their employees to push through difficult projects.
    • In personal life, individuals often turn to spiritual practices or community support to find strength in times of adversity, reflecting the essence of Indra's support in overcoming life's vṛtrā.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the strength that comes from belief and the support of a higher power or community in overcoming obstacles.
    • It teaches us that by embodying the qualities of strength, leadership, and resilience, we can face and conquer the challenges in our lives.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, and even in collective efforts to address societal issues, emphasizing the importance of unity and strength in overcoming adversity.
