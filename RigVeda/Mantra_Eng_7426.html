प्र भ्रातृत्वं सुदानवोऽध द्वितासमाण्या |मातुर गर्भे भरामहे ||
pra bhrātṛtvaṁ sudānavo 'dha dvitā samānyā | mātur garbhe bharāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forth, forward
    • bhrātṛtvaṁ: brotherhood, the state of being a brother
    • sudānavo: well-nourished, prosperous
    • 'dha: below, beneath
    • dvitā: two, duality
    • samānyā: common, shared
    • mātur: of the mother
    • garbhe: in the womb
    • bharāmahe: we bear, we carry
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of brotherhood and shared prosperity.
    • The phrase "pra bhrātṛtvaṁ" emphasizes the importance of brotherhood and unity among individuals.
    • "Sudānavo 'dha" suggests that this unity is rooted in a well-nourished and prosperous foundation.
    • The mention of "mātur garbhe" indicates that this brotherhood and shared prosperity originate from the nurturing aspect of motherhood, symbolizing care and support.
    • Finally, "bharāmahe" signifies the collective responsibility to uphold and carry forward this unity and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, siblings often support each other through challenges, embodying the concept of "bhrātṛtvaṁ." For instance, when one sibling faces a financial crisis, the others may come together to provide assistance.
    • In communities, initiatives like food banks or mutual aid groups reflect "sudānavo," where members work together to ensure that everyone has access to basic needs, fostering a sense of shared responsibility.
    • In workplaces, teamwork and collaboration can be seen as a manifestation of this mantra, where colleagues support each other to achieve common goals, thus creating a prosperous work environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, support, and shared prosperity among individuals.
    • It highlights the importance of nurturing relationships, whether in families, communities, or workplaces, to create a harmonious and prosperous environment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger bonds, increased cooperation, and a collective effort towards achieving common goals, ultimately benefiting society as a whole.
