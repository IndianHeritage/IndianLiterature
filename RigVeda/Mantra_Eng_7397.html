इन्द्र दृष्यस्व पूर असि भद्रा त एति निष्कृतम् |इयं धीः ऋत्वियावतिः ||
indra dṛhyasva pūr asi bhadrā ta eti niṣkṛtam | iyaṁ dhīr ṛtviyāvatī ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Hindu mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Dṛhyasva: This term means "be steadfast" or "be strong." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination.
  • Pūr: Translates to "full" or "complete," indicating abundance and fulfillment.
  • Asi: Means "you are," affirming the presence and identity of the subject.
  • Bhadrā: Signifies "auspicious" or "good," suggesting positivity and favorable outcomes.
  • Ta: Means "that," referring to the qualities or attributes being discussed.
  • Eti: Translates to "goes" or "approaches," indicating movement towards a goal or destination.
  • Niṣkṛtam: Means "freedom" or "liberation," often in the context of overcoming obstacles or achieving a state of purity.
  • Iyaṁ: Means "this," pointing to the current situation or context.
  • Dhīr: Refers to "wisdom" or "intellect," highlighting the importance of thoughtful decision-making.
  • Ṛtviyāvatī: This term relates to "ritual" or "sacred duty," emphasizing the significance of performing one's responsibilities with dedication.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, urging him to be steadfast and embody auspicious qualities, while also seeking liberation from obstacles through wisdom and dedication to one's duties. It reflects the importance of strength, positivity, and the pursuit of knowledge in overcoming challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom.
  • Personal Growth: The call for resilience and the pursuit of knowledge can motivate individuals to overcome personal challenges and strive for self-improvement.
  • Community Service: The emphasis on performing one's duties can inspire individuals to engage in community service, fulfilling their responsibilities towards society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities necessary for success and fulfillment in life. By embodying strength, wisdom, and a commitment to one's duties, individuals can navigate challenges and contribute positively to their communities.
