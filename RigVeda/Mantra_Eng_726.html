त्वं ह त्यद इन्द्रारिषण्यन् दृढस्य चिन् मर्तानाम् अजुष्टौ |व्य स्मद आ काष्ठा अर्वते वर घनेव वज्रीञ्छ्नथिह्य अमित्रान् ||
tvaṁ ha tyad indrāriṣaṇyan dṛḻhasya cin martānām ajuṣṭau | vy a1smad ā kāṣṭhā arvate var ghaneva vajriñ chnathihy amitrān ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: Indeed
    • tyad: That
    • indrāriṣaṇyan: O Indra, the enemy of the enemies
    • dṛḻhasya: Of the strong
    • cin: Who knows
    • martānām: Of mortals
    • ajuṣṭau: Not to be neglected
    • vy: We
    • a1smad: Our
    • ā: To
    • kāṣṭhā: Wood or timber
    • arvate: To be invoked
    • var: Best
    • ghaneva: Like a cloud
    • vajriñ: Thunderbolt
    • chnathihy: To destroy
    • amitrān: Enemies
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of gods, who is often depicted as a powerful warrior and protector.
    • It emphasizes the strength and knowledge of Indra, who is invoked for protection against adversaries.
    • The phrase "not to be neglected" suggests the importance of invoking divine support in times of need.
    • The imagery of "wood" and "cloud" symbolizes stability and abundance, indicating that divine blessings can lead to prosperity.
    • The mention of "thunderbolt" signifies the power to overcome obstacles and enemies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal or professional challenges, individuals often seek guidance and strength from higher powers, similar to how this mantra invokes Indra for support.
    • In a business context, leaders may draw upon the strength of their teams (akin to invoking Indra) to overcome competition and achieve success.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, people often come together to seek blessings for prosperity and protection, reflecting the communal aspect of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention in overcoming challenges and adversaries.
    • It serves as a reminder of the strength that comes from unity and the importance of seeking support from higher powers in both personal and communal endeavors.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and the importance of collaboration in achieving their goals.
