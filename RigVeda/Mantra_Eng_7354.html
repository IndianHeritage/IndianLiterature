इन्द्रं प्रात्नेना मनमना मरुत्वन्तं हवामहे |अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
indram pratnena manmanā marutvantaṁ havāmahe | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Pratnena: This word can be interpreted as "with devotion" or "with effort," indicating the importance of sincere intention in worship or action.
  • Manmanā: This term means "with a focused mind" or "with contemplation," emphasizing the need for mental clarity and concentration in one's endeavors.
  • Marutvantaṁ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms, representing dynamic energy and the forces of nature.
  • Havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for divine assistance or blessings.
  • Asya: Meaning "of this," it connects the invocation to the specific offering or context being referred to.
  • Somasya: Refers to Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment, vitality, and the essence of life.
  • Pītaye: This means "for drinking" or "to be consumed," indicating the act of offering or sharing the essence of life with the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a call to Indra and the Maruts, invoking their strength and dynamic energy with focused intention and devotion. It highlights the importance of mental clarity and sincere effort in seeking divine blessings, particularly in relation to the sacred Soma, which symbolizes nourishment and vitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with clarity and purpose.
  • Teamwork: The invocation of the Maruts signifies the importance of collaboration and dynamic energy in achieving common goals, reminding us that collective effort can lead to success.
  • Mindfulness: The emphasis on a focused mind encourages practices like meditation and mindfulness, which can enhance personal well-being and decision-making.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In spiritual practices, this mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke divine blessings, particularly when offering food or drink, symbolizing the sharing of life's essence.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of intention, focus, and collaboration in our lives. By invoking the divine energies of Indra and the Maruts, we can seek strength and vitality in our endeavors, fostering a deeper connection with the sacred aspects of life.
