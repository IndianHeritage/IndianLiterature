मां वो रसानितभा कुभा कृमुर मां वः सिन्धुर नीरिरमत् |मां वः परिष्टात् सरयूः पुरीषिण्य अस्मे इति सुम्नम अस्तु वः ||
mā vo rasānitabhā kubhā krumur mā vaḥ sindhur ni rīramat | mā vaḥ pari ṣṭhāt sarayuḥ purīṣiṇy asme it sumnam astu vaḥ ||
  • mā vo rasānitabhā: This phrase can be interpreted as a plea for the removal of obstacles and negativity. "Mā" means "not" or "do not," while "rasānitabhā" refers to the essence or the quality of something that is obstructive or harmful.
  • kubhā krumur: "Kubhā" translates to "bad" or "evil," and "krumur" means "worm" or "creature." This part of the mantra suggests a desire to eliminate harmful influences or negative entities from one's life.
  • mā vaḥ sindhur ni rīramat: Here, "sindhur" means "river," and "ni rīramat" can be understood as "do not disturb" or "do not agitate." This line emphasizes the importance of maintaining peace and tranquility, akin to a calm river that flows without disturbance.
  • mā vaḥ pari ṣṭhāt: "Pari ṣṭhāt" means "to surround" or "to encircle." This phrase conveys a wish for protection from all sides, ensuring that one is shielded from negativity and harm.
  • sarayuḥ purīṣiṇy: "Sarayuḥ" refers to a sacred river, often symbolizing purity and spiritual cleansing. "Purīṣiṇy" can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth" or "the one who nourishes." This part of the mantra invokes the idea of being nourished by purity and spiritual energy.
  • asme it sumnam astu vaḥ: "Asme" means "to us," "it" refers to "this," "sumnam" translates to "auspiciousness" or "good fortune," and "astu vaḥ" means "may it be for you." This concluding line expresses a wish for blessings and auspiciousness to be bestowed upon the individuals reciting the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation for protection, purification, and the removal of negativity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied during challenging times when one feels overwhelmed by negative influences or when seeking peace and tranquility in a chaotic environment. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to create a protective space around themselves, fostering a sense of calm and inviting positive energies into their lives. Its significance lies in the universal desire for safety, purity, and auspiciousness, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal well-being to communal harmony.
