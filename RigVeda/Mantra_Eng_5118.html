ते आचरन्ती समानेव योषा मातेव पुत्रम् बिभृताम् उपस्थे |अप शत्रून् विध्यतां संविदाने आर्त्नी इमे विष्फुरन्ती अमित्रान् ||
te ācarantī samaneva yoṣā māteva putram bibhṛtām upasthe | apa śatrūn vidhyatāṁ saṁvidāne ārtnī ime viṣphurantī amitrān ||
  • te ācarantī samaneva yoṣā: This phrase can be translated as "They (the women) act in harmony." It emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation among individuals, particularly women, in society. In real life, this can be seen in community initiatives where women come together to support each other, such as women's cooperatives or support groups.
  • māteva putram bibhṛtām upasthe: This translates to "Like a mother nurturing her child." It highlights the nurturing aspect of femininity and the role of women as caregivers. In everyday life, this can be observed in the way mothers care for their children, providing emotional and physical support, which is crucial for the child's development.
  • apa śatrūn vidhyatāṁ: This means "May they (the women) destroy the enemies." This can be interpreted as a call for empowerment and strength, encouraging women to overcome obstacles and adversities. In modern contexts, this can relate to women fighting against societal norms, discrimination, or any form of oppression, such as movements for gender equality.
  • saṁvidāne ārtnī ime viṣphurantī amitrān: This translates to "In the assembly, may they shine forth against the foes." It signifies the importance of women’s voices in decision-making processes and their ability to stand strong against adversaries. This can be seen in political arenas where women leaders advocate for change, or in corporate settings where women push for equal representation and rights.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the strength, unity, and nurturing qualities of women, advocating for their empowerment and active participation in society. Its significance lies in the recognition of women's roles not just as caregivers but as powerful agents of change. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire women to come together, support one another, and take a stand against injustices, thereby fostering a more equitable society. The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from community building to leadership roles, highlighting the essential contributions of women in all spheres of life.
