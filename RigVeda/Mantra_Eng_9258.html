गोभिष् टरेमामतिं दुरेवां यवेना क्षुधं पुरुहूत विश्वाम् |वयं राजभिः प्रथमां धनान्य अस्माकेन वृजनेन जयेम ||
gobhiṣ ṭaremāmatiṁ durevāṁ yavena kṣudham puruhūta viśvām | vayaṁ rājabhiḥ prathamā dhanāny asmākena vṛjanenā jayema ||
  • gobhiṣ: This word can be interpreted as "with the cows" or "with the herds." In ancient Vedic society, cows were considered sacred and a symbol of wealth and prosperity.
  • ṭaremāmatiṁ: This translates to "may we attain wisdom" or "may we achieve understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and insight in life.
  • durevāṁ: This means "from afar" or "from a distance." It suggests the idea of reaching out to something that may seem unattainable or distant.
  • yavena: This word refers to "barley," which is a staple grain. It symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • kṣudham: This translates to "hunger" or "thirst." It represents the basic human needs and desires that drive individuals to seek fulfillment.
  • puruhūta: This term can be understood as "the one who is invoked often" or "the one who is called upon." It signifies the importance of prayer and invocation in seeking blessings.
  • viśvām: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates a sense of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vayaṁ: This translates to "we" or "us," emphasizing community and collective effort.
  • rājabhiḥ: This word means "with kings" or "with rulers." It highlights the significance of leadership and governance in society.
  • prathamā: This translates to "first" or "foremost," indicating priority or importance.
  • dhanāny: This means "wealth" or "riches," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
  • asmākena: This translates to "by us" or "through us," indicating agency and responsibility in achieving goals.
  • vṛjanenā: This word means "by the means" or "through the efforts." It emphasizes the importance of action and effort in attaining success.
  • jayema: This translates to "may we conquer" or "may we achieve victory." It signifies the aspiration for success and triumph in endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of wisdom, community, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort in overcoming challenges and achieving success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, leadership, and personal growth. For instance, in a business setting, the mantra encourages collaboration among team members to achieve common goals, invoking the spirit of unity and shared purpose. It serves as a reminder that through knowledge, effort, and community, individuals can overcome obstacles and attain their aspirations, leading to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
