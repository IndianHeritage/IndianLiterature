आयं विश्वानि तिष्ठति पुनानो भुवनोपरि |सोमो देवो न सूर्यः ||
ayaṁ viśvāni tiṣṭhati punāno bhuvanopari | somo devo na sūryaḥ ||
  • ayaṁ viśvāni tiṣṭhati: This phrase translates to "This (universe) stands in its entirety." It signifies the interconnectedness of all things in the universe, suggesting that everything exists in harmony and balance.
  • punāno bhuvanopari: This means "renewing above the worlds." It implies a process of rejuvenation and transformation, indicating that the universe is in a constant state of renewal and evolution.
  • somo devo na sūryaḥ: This translates to "The moon is a deity, not the sun." Here, the mantra highlights the significance of the moon (Soma) as a source of nourishment and tranquility, contrasting it with the sun, which represents energy and activity.

  • Real-Life Example of Interconnectedness: In nature, ecosystems demonstrate how various organisms depend on each other for survival. For instance, bees pollinate flowers, which in turn produce fruits that feed other animals, illustrating the principle of "this universe stands in its entirety."
  • Renewal and Transformation: The changing seasons are a perfect example of "renewing above the worlds." Each season brings its own beauty and challenges, reminding us that change is a natural part of life. For example, spring symbolizes rebirth and new beginnings, while autumn signifies letting go and preparation for rest.
  • Significance of the Moon: The moon's phases affect tides and have been linked to human emotions and behaviors. For instance, many cultures celebrate the full moon as a time for reflection and community gatherings, recognizing the moon's role as a calming influence in contrast to the sun's intensity.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates profound truths about the universe's interconnectedness, the cyclical nature of life, and the duality of celestial influences. Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the harmony of existence, embrace change, and find balance between activity and tranquility in our lives. In practical terms, it encourages us to cultivate awareness of our environment, nurture relationships, and seek renewal in our personal journeys.
