आत्मा देवानाम् भुवनस्य गर्भो यथावशं चरति देव एषः |घोषा इद अस्य श्रिण्विरे न रूपं तस्मै वाताय हविषा विदेम ||
ātmā devānām bhuvanasya garbho yathāvaśaṁ carati deva eṣaḥ | ghoṣā id asya śṛṇvire na rūpaṁ tasmai vātāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • ātmā devānām: This phrase translates to "the soul of the gods." It signifies the divine essence that exists within all beings, suggesting a connection between the divine and the earthly.
  • bhuvanasya garbho: This means "the womb of the universe." It represents the source of creation and the nurturing aspect of the universe, akin to how a mother nurtures her child.
  • yathāvaśaṁ carati deva eṣaḥ: This translates to "as the deity moves at will." It implies that the divine operates freely and with intention, guiding the universe according to its will.
  • ghoṣā id asya śṛṇvire: This means "the sounds of this are heard." It suggests that the divine presence and its actions are perceptible in the world around us, much like how we hear the wind or the rustling of leaves.
  • na rūpaṁ: This translates to "without form." It indicates the formless nature of the divine, emphasizing that the essence of the divine transcends physical appearances.
  • tasmai vātāya haviṣā vidhema: This means "to that wind, we offer our oblation." It signifies a reverence towards the divine forces of nature, recognizing their importance in our lives and the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine and its relationship with the universe and humanity. It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence that guides and nurtures creation. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as recognizing the importance of nature and the environment, understanding our place in the universe, and fostering a sense of unity with all living beings. By acknowledging the divine within ourselves and others, we can cultivate compassion, respect, and harmony in our interactions, ultimately leading to a more peaceful and balanced existence.
