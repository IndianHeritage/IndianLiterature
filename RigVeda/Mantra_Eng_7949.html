प्र सुवानो धारणया तनेन्दुर हिन्वानो अर्षति |रुजद दृढा व्य ओजसा ||
pra suvāno dhārayā tanendur hinvāno arṣati | rujad dṛḻhā vy ojasā ||
  • pra suvāno: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth good things" or "the one who is auspicious." In real life, this can relate to individuals or leaders who inspire positivity and progress in their communities.
  • dhārayā: This word means "to support" or "to uphold." It signifies the importance of support systems in our lives, such as family, friends, and mentors who help us navigate challenges.
  • tanendur: This term refers to "the body" or "the physical form." It emphasizes the significance of physical well-being and health in achieving our goals and aspirations.
  • hinvāno: This can be understood as "removing" or "dispelling." It suggests the act of overcoming obstacles or negativity in our lives, akin to how we might work to eliminate toxic influences or harmful habits.
  • arṣati: This word means "to shine" or "to radiate." It reflects the idea of personal growth and the ability to shine in one's endeavors, much like how a successful person inspires others through their achievements.
  • rujad: This term can be interpreted as "pain" or "suffering." It acknowledges the challenges and hardships we face in life, reminding us that they are a part of the human experience.
  • dṛḻhā: This word means "firm" or "strong." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination in overcoming difficulties, similar to how athletes train rigorously to build strength and endurance.
  • vy ojasā: This phrase translates to "with energy" or "with vigor." It highlights the necessity of maintaining enthusiasm and vitality in our pursuits, akin to how passionate individuals often inspire those around them.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of support, health, resilience, and positivity. It serves as a reminder that while we may face pain and obstacles, we have the power to dispel negativity and shine brightly in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, leadership, and community building, encouraging individuals to uplift themselves and others while navigating the complexities of life.
