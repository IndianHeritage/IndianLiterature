मन्त्र: एते वदन्ति शतवत् सहस्रवत् अभि क्रन्दन्ति हरितेभिर् आसभिः |विष्ट्वी ग्रावाणः सुक्रितः सुक्रित्यया होतुश् चित् पूर्वे हविराद्यम् आशत ||
ete vadanti śatavat sahasravad abhi krandanti haritebhir āsabhiḥ | viṣṭvī grāvāṇaḥ sukṛtaḥ sukṛtyayā hotuś cit pūrve haviradyam āśata ||
  • Translation: "These (sages) speak with a hundred voices, with a thousand voices they cry out, with the green (plants) they are nourished. The one who is endowed with good deeds, may he, through his good actions, be the one who offers the oblation to the ancient ones."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete vadanti: "these (sages) speak" - Refers to the wise individuals or sages who possess knowledge and wisdom.
    • śatavat sahasravad: "with a hundred voices, with a thousand voices" - Symbolizes the multitude of perspectives and the vastness of knowledge that can be shared.
    • abhi krandanti: "they cry out" - Indicates a call to action or a plea for attention to important matters.
    • haritebhir āsabhiḥ: "with the green (plants) they are nourished" - Suggests a connection to nature and the sustenance derived from it.
    • viṣṭvī grāvāṇaḥ: "the one who is endowed with good deeds" - Refers to an individual who acts righteously and performs good actions.
    • sukṛtaḥ sukṛtyayā: "through his good actions" - Emphasizes the importance of performing virtuous deeds.
    • hotuś cit: "may he be the one who offers" - A prayer or wish for the individual to be a benefactor or a giver.
    • pūrve haviradyam āśata: "to the ancient ones" - Refers to the ancestors or the wise sages of the past, acknowledging their contributions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of speaking with "a hundred voices" can be seen in modern-day discussions where diverse opinions are valued, such as in community meetings or forums where everyone is encouraged to share their thoughts.
    • The nourishment from "green plants" can be related to sustainable living and the importance of agriculture, where communities rely on nature for their sustenance, promoting environmental awareness.
    • The emphasis on "good deeds" resonates with various charitable organizations that encourage individuals to contribute positively to society, such as volunteering or participating in community service.
  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the significance of collective wisdom, the importance of nature, and the value of good deeds. In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in meaningful dialogue, respect the environment, and act with integrity. By embodying these principles, one can contribute to a harmonious society and honor the legacy of those who came before us. This mantra serves as a reminder that our actions and words can have a profound impact on the world around us, urging us to strive for a better future through unity and virtue.
