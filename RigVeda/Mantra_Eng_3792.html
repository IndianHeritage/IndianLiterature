मन्त्र: होतारं त्वा वृणीमहे 'ग्ने दाक्षस्य साधनम् |यज्ञेषु पूर्व्यं गिरा प्रयस्वन्तो हवामहे ||
hotāraṁ tvā vṛṇīmahe 'gne dakṣasya sādhanam | yajñeṣu pūrvyaṁ girā prayasvanto havāmahe ||
  • hotāraṁ: This term refers to the hotar, the priest or the one who invokes the deities during a ritual. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the role of a guide or facilitator in any spiritual or communal gathering.
  • tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to the divine or the energy being invoked.
  • vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose" or "we invoke," suggesting a collective intention or desire to connect with the divine.
  • agne: This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals. Fire represents transformation, purification, and the divine presence in the material world.
  • dakṣasya: This word means "of the capable" or "skilled," referring to the qualities of Agni as a deity who is efficient and effective in his role.
  • sādhanam: This translates to "means" or "instrument," indicating that Agni is the medium through which offerings are made and blessings are received.
  • yajñeṣu: This means "in sacrifices," highlighting the context of rituals and offerings that are performed to honor the deities.
  • pūrvyaṁ: This translates to "the ancient" or "the prior," suggesting the timeless nature of these rituals and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • girā: This means "with words" or "with speech," emphasizing the importance of verbal invocation and the power of sound in spiritual practices.
  • prayasvanto: This translates to "those who strive" or "those who exert effort," indicating the active participation of the community in the rituals.
  • havāmahe: This means "we offer" or "we invoke," reinforcing the collective action of the community in making offerings to the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra "hotāraṁ tvā vṛṇīmahe 'gne dakṣasya sādhanam | yajñeṣu pūrvyaṁ girā prayasvanto havāmahe ||" encapsulates the essence of communal worship and the invocation of divine energy through the medium of Agni. It signifies the importance of collective intention, skillful action, and the power of words in spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, rituals, and ceremonies where individuals come together to seek blessings, guidance, and transformation. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals in their spiritual journeys and the significance of invoking higher energies for personal and communal growth.
