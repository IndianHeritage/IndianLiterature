ये चाऽर्हन्ति मरुतः सुदानवः स्मन् मीळ्हुषश्चरन्ति ये |अतश्चिदान उप वस्यसाऽहृदाऽयुवानाऽववृद्धम् ||
ye cārhanti marutaḥ sudānavaḥ sman mīḻhuṣaś caranti ye | ataś cid ā na upa vasyasā hṛdā yuvāna ā vavṛdhvam ||
  • Translation: "Those who are nourished by the Maruts, the strong and mighty, who move with joy, may they, with their hearts, be ever youthful and grow in strength."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • cārhanti: are nourished or supported
    • marutaḥ: by the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • sudānavaḥ: the strong and mighty
    • sman: with joy
    • mīḻhuṣaś: who move or act
    • caranti: they may
    • ataś: therefore
    • cid: indeed
    • ā: may
    • na: not
    • upa: near or close
    • vasyasā: with their hearts
    • hṛdā: in the heart
    • yuvāna: youthful
    • ā: may
    • vavṛdhvam: grow or increase

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The Maruts are often associated with storms and winds, symbolizing strength and vitality. In real life, this can be likened to the forces of nature that provide nourishment and growth, such as rain that helps crops flourish.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of joy and vitality in life. For instance, individuals who engage in activities that bring them happiness, such as pursuing hobbies or spending time with loved ones, often experience a sense of rejuvenation and strength.
    • The idea of being "ever youthful" can be related to maintaining a positive outlook and a zest for life, which is crucial for mental and physical well-being. For example, people who practice mindfulness or engage in regular physical activity often report feeling more energetic and youthful.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing oneself through positive influences and experiences, much like how nature nourishes life. It encourages individuals to seek joy and strength in their lives, fostering a sense of youthfulness and vitality.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by focusing on personal growth, surrounding oneself with supportive and uplifting people, and engaging in activities that promote happiness and well-being.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that by embracing joy and strength, one can cultivate a fulfilling and vibrant life.
