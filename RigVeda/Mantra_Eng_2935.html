ससरपरीर अमतिम बादहमाना बृहन् मिमाय जामदग्निदत्तā |आ सूर्यस्य दुहितā ततान श्रवो देवेष्व अमृतम् अजुर्म् ||
sasarparīr amatim bādhamānā bṛhan mimāya jamadagnidattā | ā sūryasya duhitā tatāna śravo deveṣv amṛtam ajuryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sasarparīr: Refers to the serpent or snake, symbolizing the cycle of life and death.
    • amatim: Implies immortality or eternal wisdom.
    • bādhamānā: Indicates being bound or constrained.
    • bṛhan: Means great or vast, often referring to the universe or the divine.
    • mimāya: Suggests the act of creating or manifesting.
    • jamadagnidattā: Refers to the gift of knowledge or enlightenment, often associated with the sage Jamadagni.
    • ā: A preposition meaning 'from' or 'out of.'
    • sūryasya: Pertaining to the sun, symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • duhitā: Means daughter, often used to signify a divine or celestial being.
    • tatāna: Refers to the act of spreading or extending.
    • śravo: Implies fame or glory.
    • deveṣv: Refers to the gods or divine beings.
    • amṛtam: Means immortality or the nectar of immortality.
    • ajuryam: Suggests something that is eternal or unchanging.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal struggle between the mortal and the immortal, represented by the serpent and the divine.
    • The mention of the sun signifies the source of life and enlightenment, suggesting that wisdom and knowledge are derived from a higher power.
    • The reference to Jamadagni indicates the importance of sages and their teachings in achieving enlightenment and understanding the universe.
    • The mantra emphasizes the idea that true wisdom leads to immortality, both in a spiritual sense and in the legacy one leaves behind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the serpent is a symbol of transformation and healing, as seen in the medical symbol of the caduceus.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is a common theme in education, where individuals strive to gain insights that can lead to personal and societal growth.
    • Many spiritual practices emphasize the importance of connecting with a higher power, akin to the sun in this mantra, to gain clarity and purpose in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of the human experience—our quest for knowledge, the struggle against mortality, and the pursuit of a legacy that transcends our physical existence.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us that wisdom and enlightenment are pathways to a more profound understanding of life and our place in the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge, embrace transformation, and connect with the divine to achieve a sense of purpose and immortality through their actions and contributions.
