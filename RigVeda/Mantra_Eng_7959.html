तं गीरभिर वाचमीङ्खयम् पुनानं वासयामसि |सोमं जनस्य गोपतिम् ||
taṁ gīrbhir vācamīṅkhayam punānaṁ vāsayāmasi | somaṁ janasya gopatim ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • gīrbhir - This means "with hymns" or "with words," indicating the importance of speech and expression in connecting with the divine.
  • vācamīṅkhayam - This can be interpreted as "we invoke" or "we call upon," emphasizing the act of invocation and the power of words in spiritual practices.
  • punānaṁ - This word means "purifying" or "cleansing," suggesting a desire for spiritual renewal and purification.
  • vāsayāmasi - This translates to "we are dwelling" or "we reside," indicating a sense of belonging or connection to the divine.
  • somaṁ - Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • janasya - This means "of the people" or "of the community," highlighting the collective aspect of spirituality.
  • gopatim - This translates to "the lord" or "the protector," referring to a divine figure who safeguards and nurtures the community.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to a divine entity, asking for purification and connection through the power of words and hymns. It emphasizes the importance of community and the role of a divine protector in guiding and nurturing the people.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek purification and a deeper connection with the divine.
  • Community Gatherings: In group settings, such as festivals or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke a sense of unity and collective spiritual growth.
  • Personal Reflection: The themes of purification and connection can inspire individuals to reflect on their own lives, seeking to cleanse negative influences and foster a deeper relationship with their spiritual beliefs.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and community in spiritual practices. It highlights the significance of seeking purification and connection with the divine, which is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to communal harmony.
