वयं ते त इन्द्र ये च नरः शर्धो जजñानां याताश च रथाः |आस्मान् जगम्याद अहिषुष्म सत्वा भगो न हव्यः प्रभृतेषु चाऽरुः ||
vayaṁ te ta indra ye ca naraḥ śardho jajñānā yātāś ca rathāḥ | āsmāñ jagamyād ahiśuṣma satvā bhago na havyaḥ prabhṛtheṣu cāruḥ ||
  • vayaṁ te ta indra: This phrase translates to "We are yours, O Indra." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership. In real life, this can be seen as an expression of devotion or allegiance to a higher authority or a leader, such as a mentor or a guiding figure in one's life.
  • ye ca naraḥ: This means "and those who are human." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of community and the importance of human relationships, where individuals come together for a common purpose.
  • śardho jajñānā: This translates to "who have been born in strength." It signifies the idea of being born with potential and the strength to achieve one's goals. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who are born into circumstances that provide them with opportunities, such as supportive families or communities.
  • yātāś ca rathāḥ: This means "and those who have gone forth in chariots." Chariots symbolize progress and movement. In modern terms, this can represent the journey of life, where individuals take steps towards their aspirations, much like pursuing education or career goals.
  • āsmāñ jagamyād: This translates to "we have come here." It indicates a sense of arrival or achievement. In everyday life, this can be seen when individuals reach milestones, such as graduating from school or completing a significant project.
  • ahiśuṣma satvā: This phrase means "the powerful and strong." It highlights the qualities of resilience and strength. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who overcome challenges, such as athletes who train rigorously to achieve their goals.
  • bhago na havyaḥ: This translates to "wealth is not to be sacrificed." It suggests the importance of valuing resources and not taking them for granted. In practical terms, this can relate to financial literacy and the responsible management of one's resources.
  • prabhṛtheṣu cāruḥ: This means "in the beautiful things." It signifies appreciation for beauty and goodness in life. In real life, this can be seen in the way people find joy in art, nature, and relationships, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and mindfulness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, strength, community, progress, and appreciation for life's beauty. Its significance lies in the reminder to recognize our connections with the divine and each other, to strive for strength and resilience, and to appreciate the resources and beauty around us. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to build strong relationships, pursue their goals with determination, and cultivate gratitude for their experiences.
