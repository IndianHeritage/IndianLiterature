त्रिंशच्छतं वर्मिण इन्द्र साकं यव्यावत्यम् पुरुहूत श्रवस्याः | वृचिवन्तः शरवे पत्यमानाः पात्रा भिन्दानां न्यर्थान्य आयन ||
triṁśacchataṁ varmiṇa indra sākaṁ yavyāvatyām puruhūta śravasyā | vṛcīvantaḥ śarave patyamānāḥ pātrā bhindānā nyarthāny āyan ||
  • triṁśacchataṁ: This term refers to "thirty" or "thirty times," indicating a significant quantity or measure.
  • varmiṇa: This word can be interpreted as "armor" or "protection," suggesting a sense of security or defense.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with strength, power, and rain. He symbolizes leadership and authority.
  • sākaṁ: This term means "together" or "with," indicating unity or collaboration.
  • yavyāvatyām: This word can be translated as "in the context of the world" or "in the earthly realm," suggesting a connection to the material world.
  • puruhūta: This term refers to "many offerings" or "abundant sacrifices," indicating generosity and the importance of giving.
  • śravasyā: This word means "of fame" or "renown," highlighting the significance of reputation and honor.
  • vṛcīvantaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful," emphasizing strength and resilience.
  • śarave: This word means "in the battle" or "in conflict," indicating challenges or struggles that one may face.
  • patyamānāḥ: This term refers to "those who are being protected" or "sheltered," suggesting the importance of safety and security.
  • pātrā: This word means "vessels" or "containers," symbolizing the capacity to receive or hold something valuable.
  • bhindānā: This term can be interpreted as "breaking" or "shattering," indicating the overcoming of obstacles or challenges.
  • nyarthāny: This word means "the true essence" or "the real meaning," suggesting a deeper understanding of life and existence.
  • āyan: This term refers to "arrival" or "coming," indicating the manifestation of goals or desires.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and unity in overcoming challenges in life. It highlights the significance of collaboration and generosity in achieving success and fame. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during difficult times, or personal growth through resilience and determination. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with courage and purpose, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
