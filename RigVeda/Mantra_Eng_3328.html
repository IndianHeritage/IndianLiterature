स वृत्रहत्ये हव्यः स ईड्यः स सुश्रुत इन्द्रः सत्यराधाः |स यामन्ना मगवाऽमार्त्याय ब्रह्मण्यते सुश्रवये वरिवो धात ||
sa vṛtrahatye havyaḥ sa īḍyaḥ sa suṣṭuta indraḥ satyarādhāḥ | sa yāmann ā maghavā martyāya brahmaṇyate suṣvaye varivo dhāt ||
  • sa vṛtrahatye havyaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "He who is to be invoked for the slaying of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology that represents chaos and obstruction. In real life, this can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges that hinder progress.
  • sa īḍyaḥ: This means "He who is worthy of worship." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and respect towards divine forces or principles that guide us. In everyday life, this can relate to the respect we give to mentors, leaders, or even moral values that guide our actions.
  • sa suṣṭuta indraḥ: Translated as "He who is well-praised, Indra." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. This can be seen in real life as the recognition of leaders or individuals who have made significant contributions to society, deserving of praise and acknowledgment.
  • satyarādhāḥ: This means "who is established in truth." It highlights the value of truthfulness and integrity. In practical terms, this can be applied in personal relationships, business dealings, and ethical decision-making, where honesty is paramount.
  • sa yāmann ā maghavā martyāya: This translates to "He who is the great one for mortals." It signifies the divine support available to humans. In real life, this can be interpreted as the guidance and help we receive from higher powers or the universe when facing difficulties.
  • brahmaṇyate suṣvaye varivo dhāt: This means "He who is the bestower of wealth and prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and well-being. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of prosperity in our lives, whether through career success, financial stability, or personal fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in overcoming challenges, recognizing the importance of truth and integrity, and striving for prosperity. In real life, it serves as a reminder to invoke higher powers when faced with obstacles, to honor those who contribute positively to our lives, and to maintain a commitment to truth and ethical living. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal struggles, in professional environments, or in community leadership, guiding individuals towards a path of righteousness and success.
