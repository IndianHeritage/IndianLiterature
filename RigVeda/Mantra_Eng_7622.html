अनर्षरातिं वसुदाम उप स्तुहि भद्रा इन्द्रस्य रातयः |सो अस्य कामं विधतो न रोषति मनो दानाय चोदयन् ||
anarśarātiṁ vasudām upa stuhi bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ | so asya kāmaṁ vidhato na roṣati mano dānāya codayan ||
  • anarśarātiṁ: This term can be broken down into "an" (not) and "arśarāti" (one who is angry or hostile). It signifies the absence of anger or hostility.
  • vasudām: This word refers to "the earth" or "the wealth of the earth," symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • upa stuhi: This phrase means "to praise" or "to invoke," indicating a call for reverence or acknowledgment.
  • bhadrā: This word translates to "auspicious" or "beneficial," suggesting a positive outcome or good fortune.
  • indrasya: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, representing power, strength, and leadership.
  • rātayaḥ: This term means "the paths" or "the ways," indicating the routes or methods to achieve something.
  • so asya: This translates to "this" or "of this," referring to the subject at hand.
  • kāmaṁ: Meaning "desire" or "wish," it signifies the aspirations or goals one wishes to achieve.
  • vidhato: This word means "to create" or "to establish," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • na roṣati: This phrase means "does not get angry," reinforcing the theme of calmness and composure.
  • mano: Referring to "mind," it emphasizes the importance of mental state and attitude.
  • dānāya: This term means "to give" or "to donate," highlighting the act of generosity.
  • codayan: This word translates to "to inspire" or "to encourage," suggesting a call to action or motivation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the absence of anger and hostility while invoking the blessings of prosperity and auspiciousness from Indra. It emphasizes the importance of a calm mind in the pursuit of desires and encourages generosity and inspiration in our actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal relationships, practicing the absence of anger can lead to healthier communication and stronger bonds.
  • In professional settings, fostering a calm and positive environment can enhance teamwork and productivity.
  • In community service, the act of giving and inspiring others can create a ripple effect of kindness and support.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life. It teaches us to cultivate a peaceful mind, seek prosperity, and act with generosity. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both for ourselves and those around us.
