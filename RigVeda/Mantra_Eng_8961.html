परम मृत्युो अनु परेहि पन्थां यस ते स्व इतरो देवयानात् |चक्षुष्मते श्रिण्वते ते ब्रवीमि मा नः प्रजां रीरीषो मोत वीरान् ||
param mṛtyo anu parehi panthāṁ yas te sva itaro devayānāt | cakṣuṣmate śṛṇvate te bravīmi mā naḥ prajāṁ rīriṣo mota vīrān ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Death, you who are the ultimate, lead us along the path that is beyond. I speak to those who have the divine vision; may we not be envious of the progeny of the brave."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • param mṛtyo: "O ultimate Death" - This addresses the concept of death as a powerful and inevitable force in life.
    • anu parehi: "lead us along" - A plea for guidance through the journey of life and beyond.
    • panthāṁ: "the path" - Refers to the spiritual or moral path one should follow.
    • yas te sva: "that is beyond" - Indicates a realm or state that transcends ordinary existence.
    • itaro devayānāt: "to those with divine vision" - Refers to enlightened beings or those who possess wisdom.
    • cakṣuṣmate: "to the seers" - Acknowledges those who have insight and understanding.
    • śṛṇvate: "who listen" - Emphasizes the importance of being receptive to wisdom.
    • te bravīmi: "I speak to you" - A direct address to the enlightened beings.
    • mā naḥ prajāṁ: "may we not be envious of progeny" - A wish for harmony and lack of jealousy among people.
    • rīriṣo: "of the brave" - Refers to those who are courageous and noble.
    • mota vīrān: "of the brave" - Reiterates the focus on valor and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In facing the inevitability of death, individuals often seek guidance through spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, to find peace and understanding.
    • The mantra encourages us to follow a righteous path, similar to how leaders and mentors guide others in their personal and professional lives.
    • In communities, fostering an environment free from envy can lead to stronger relationships and collaboration, much like successful teams in workplaces that thrive on mutual respect and support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its profound acknowledgment of death as a part of life and the importance of seeking guidance on a higher path. It encourages individuals to cultivate wisdom, listen to enlightened voices, and foster a spirit of camaraderie rather than envy. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, community building, and leadership, reminding us to strive for a life of purpose, understanding, and unity.
