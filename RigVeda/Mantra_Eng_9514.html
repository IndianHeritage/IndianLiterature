स ईं सत्येभिः सकिभिः शुचद्भिर गोधायसं वि धनसैर अदर्दः | ब्रह्मणस पतिर वृषभिर वराहैर घर्मस्वेदेभिर द्रविणं व्य आनट ||
sa īṁ satyebhiḥ sakhibhiḥ śucadbhir godhāyasaṁ vi dhanasair adardaḥ | brahmaṇas patir vṛṣabhir varāhair gharmasvedebhir draviṇaṁ vy ānaṭ ||
  • sa īṁ satyebhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "with truth." It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness in our actions and thoughts. In real life, this can be seen in situations where honesty is crucial, such as in personal relationships or professional integrity.
  • sakhibhiḥ: This word means "with friends" or "companions." It highlights the value of community and support from others. For example, in a workplace, teamwork and collaboration among colleagues can lead to greater success and innovation.
  • śucadbhir: This term translates to "with purity." It signifies the need for purity in intentions and actions. In everyday life, this can relate to ethical practices, such as sustainable living or charitable actions that are done selflessly.
  • godhāyasaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the wealth of cows," which symbolizes prosperity and abundance. In modern terms, this could refer to the importance of nurturing resources, whether they are financial, natural, or human resources, to ensure growth and sustainability.
  • vi dhanasair adardaḥ: This phrase means "may we not be harmed by wealth." It suggests that while wealth is important, it should not lead to negative consequences or moral degradation. In real life, this can be seen in discussions about wealth inequality and the ethical responsibilities of the wealthy towards society.
  • brahmaṇas patir: This translates to "the lord of the Brahmanas," referring to a higher spiritual authority or wisdom. It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and spiritual growth. In practical terms, this can be related to the importance of education and lifelong learning in achieving personal and professional goals.
  • vṛṣabhir varāhair: This phrase can be understood as "with the best of the strong." It emphasizes strength and excellence in endeavors. In real life, this can be applied to striving for excellence in various fields, such as sports, academics, or business.
  • gharmasvedebhir: This term refers to "with the warmth of sweat," symbolizing hard work and effort. It highlights the importance of diligence and perseverance in achieving success. For instance, athletes train rigorously to achieve their goals, demonstrating the value of hard work.
  • draviṇaṁ vy ānaṭ: This translates to "may we attain wealth." It encapsulates the aspiration for prosperity and success in life. In a broader context, this can relate to the pursuit of financial stability and the importance of setting and achieving personal and professional goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, community, purity, and hard work in the pursuit of prosperity and spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder that while wealth and success are important, they should be pursued with integrity and ethical considerations. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community engagements, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence while maintaining moral values.
