एतत् त्यत् त इन्द्र वृष्णं उक्तं वार्षागिरा अभि गृणन्ति राधः |ऋज्राश्वः प्रश्निभिर अम्बरीषः सहदेवो भयमानः सुराधाः ||
etat tyat ta indra vṛṣṇa ukthaṁ vārṣāgirā abhi gṛṇanti rādhaḥ | ṛjrāśvaḥ praṣṭibhir ambarīṣaḥ sahadevo bhayamānaḥ surādhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etat: this
    • tyat: that
    • ta: those
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • vṛṣṇa: the strong, powerful
    • ukthaṁ: the hymn or praise
    • vārṣāgirā: with the voice of the rain
    • abhi: towards
    • gṛṇanti: they sing or chant
    • rādhaḥ: the prosperous or wealthy
    • ṛjrāśvaḥ: the swift horse
    • praṣṭibhir: with the support or assistance
    • ambarīṣaḥ: the one who is beyond the sky
    • sahadevo: the divine companion
    • bhayamānaḥ: being fearful or in fear
    • surādhāḥ: the divine beings or gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing his strength and power.
    • The phrase "they sing or chant" suggests a communal aspect of worship, where people come together to praise the divine.
    • The mention of "the swift horse" symbolizes speed and agility, often associated with success and victory.
    • The term "being fearful" indicates the challenges and fears that individuals face, which can be overcome through divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people may chant this mantra during a festival to invoke blessings for prosperity and strength.
    • In sports, athletes may draw inspiration from the idea of swiftness and power, akin to the "swift horse," to overcome their fears and perform at their best.
    • During challenging times, individuals may recite this mantra to seek courage and divine assistance in overcoming their fears.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving success.
    • It serves as a reminder of the strength found in community and the power of collective praise.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help from the divine and to face their fears with courage and determination.
