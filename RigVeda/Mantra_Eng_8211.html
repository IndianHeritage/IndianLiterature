आ पवमान सुष्टुतिं वृष्टिं देवभ्यो दुवः |इषे पवस्व संयतम् ||
ā pavamāna suṣṭutiṁ vṛṣṭiṁ devebhyo duvaḥ | iṣe pavasva saṁyatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From, away from
    • pavamāna: Purifying, the one who purifies
    • suṣṭutiṁ: Well-formed praise, good praise
    • vṛṣṭiṁ: Rain, abundance
    • devebhyo: To the gods
    • duvaḥ: Offering, invocation
    • iṣe: To the one who is invoked
    • pavasva: May you purify
    • saṁyatam: With control, with restraint
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for purification and abundance, directed towards the divine forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of well-formed praise and the invocation of the gods for blessings, particularly in the form of rain, which is essential for life and prosperity.
    • The call for purification suggests a desire for clarity and spiritual cleanliness, which is necessary for receiving divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of their hopes for divine intervention in providing the necessary rainfall.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek purification through meditation or rituals, aiming to clear their minds and hearts to receive positive energy and blessings.
    • Communities may come together in festivals or rituals to invoke the gods for prosperity and abundance, echoing the sentiments expressed in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and purification, which are essential for both individual and communal well-being.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of spiritual practices and the natural world, reminding us that our actions and intentions can invoke positive outcomes.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance, clarity, and abundance through mindful practices and community engagement.
