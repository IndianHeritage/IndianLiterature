मन्त्र: अश्वायन्तो गव्यन्तो वाजयन्तो हवामहे त्वोपगन्तवा उ |आभूषन्तस ते सुमतौ नवायां वयम् इन्द्र त्वा शुनं हुवेम ||
aśvāyanto gavyanto vājayanto havāmahe tvopagantavā u | ābhūṣantas te sumatau navāyāṁ vayam indra tvā śunaṁ huvema ||
  • Mantra: "aśvāyanto gavyanto vājayanto havāmahe tvopagantavā u | ābhūṣantas te sumatau navāyāṁ vayam indra tvā śunaṁ huvema ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke you, Indra, who are swift as horses, who are abundant like cattle, who are powerful like chariots. We call upon you to come to us, adorned with good qualities, and we offer you our praises."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvāyanto: swift as horses
    • gavyanto: abundant like cattle
    • vājayanto: powerful like chariots
    • havāmahe: we invoke
    • tvopagantavā: come to us
    • ābhūṣantas: adorned with good qualities
    • te: you
    • sumatau: with good thoughts
    • navāyāṁ: in a new way
    • vayam: we
    • indra: O Indra
    • tvā: you
    • śunaṁ: our praises
    • huvema: we offer
  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and abundance.
    • It emphasizes the qualities of swiftness, abundance, and power, which are essential in various aspects of life.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in situations where one seeks strength and support, such as during challenges in personal or professional life.
    • For example, athletes may recite this mantra before competitions to invoke strength and speed, while business leaders might use it to inspire confidence and abundance in their ventures.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities we aspire to embody: swiftness in action, abundance in resources, and power in our endeavors.
    • By invoking these qualities, we align ourselves with the divine energies represented by Indra, fostering a mindset conducive to success and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to seek and cultivate these attributes in our daily lives, enhancing our personal growth and collective well-being.
