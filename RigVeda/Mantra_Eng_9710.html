अघोरचक्षुर अपातिघ्न्य एधि शिवा पशुभ्यः सुमनाः सुवर्चाः |वीरासूर देवकामाऽ स्योनाऽ शं नो भव द्विपादे शं चतुष्पादे ||
aghoracakṣur apatighny edhi śivā paśubhyaḥ sumanāḥ suvarcāḥ | vīrasūr devakāmā syonā śaṁ no bhava dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||
  • aghoracakṣur: This term can be broken down into two parts: "aghora" meaning "not terrible" or "auspicious," and "cakṣur" meaning "eye." Together, it refers to the divine vision that perceives the world without fear or negativity.
  • apatighny: This word means "one who removes obstacles." It signifies a force or deity that helps in overcoming challenges and difficulties in life.
  • edhi: This term translates to "rise" or "elevate." It is a call for upliftment, both spiritually and materially.
  • śivā: Referring to the auspiciousness and benevolence of the divine, "śivā" embodies the qualities of peace, harmony, and well-being.
  • paśubhyaḥ: This word means "to all living beings." It emphasizes the inclusivity of the divine blessings being sought, extending to all creatures.
  • sumanāḥ: This term means "good-minded" or "well-disposed." It refers to the state of having a positive and constructive mindset.
  • suvarcāḥ: This translates to "radiant" or "brilliant." It signifies the illumination and enlightenment that comes from divine grace.
  • vīrasūr: This term combines "vīra," meaning "hero" or "brave," and "sūr," meaning "divine." It refers to the divine qualities of courage and valor.
  • devakāmā: This word means "desiring the divine." It reflects the aspiration for spiritual connection and fulfillment.
  • syonā: This term means "may we be protected." It is a plea for safety and security in life.
  • śaṁ no bhava: This phrase translates to "may peace be with us." It is a wish for tranquility and harmony in our lives.
  • dvipade: This word means "two-legged," referring to humans.
  • śaṁ catuṣpade: This translates to "and four-legged," referring to animals. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living beings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for divine vision, the removal of obstacles, and the elevation of both human and animal life. It emphasizes the importance of a positive mindset, courage, and the desire for peace and protection. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one seeks clarity and guidance, or when striving for harmony in relationships with others, including both humans and animals. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors.
