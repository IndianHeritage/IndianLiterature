परेहि विग्रम अस्तृतं इन्द्रं पृच्छा विपश्चितम् |यस् ते सखिभ्य आ वरम् ||
parehi vigram astṛtam indram pṛcchā vipaścitam | yas te sakhibhya ā varam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Indra, the one who is beyond the reach of the senses, we seek your guidance and wisdom. Grant us the best of companions."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • parehi: This word can be understood as "to go beyond" or "to transcend." It signifies the pursuit of higher knowledge or understanding.
    • vigram: This term refers to "the senses" or "perception." It highlights the limitations of human understanding.
    • astṛtam: Meaning "beyond" or "transcendent," it emphasizes the divine nature of Indra, who is not confined by human limitations.
    • indram: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and wisdom.
    • pṛcchā: This means "to inquire" or "to ask," indicating a quest for knowledge.
    • vipaścitam: Translated as "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," it refers to those who possess deep understanding.
    • yas: This word means "who" or "that," linking the inquiry to the wise ones.
    • te: Meaning "your," it denotes possession or connection to Indra.
    • sakhibhya: This translates to "companions" or "friends," emphasizing the importance of relationships.
    • ā varam: Meaning "the best" or "the most excellent," it signifies the desire for the highest quality of companionship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, seeking guidance from a mentor can be likened to asking Indra for wisdom. Just as the mantra requests the best companions, having a mentor can lead to better decision-making and career growth.
    • In personal relationships, surrounding oneself with wise and supportive friends can enhance one's life experience. The mantra emphasizes the value of choosing companions who uplift and inspire.
    • In education, students often seek knowledge from teachers who are wise and knowledgeable. This aligns with the mantra's call for guidance from those who possess deep understanding.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for transcending ordinary perception and seeking wisdom from higher sources. It teaches us the importance of choosing our companions wisely, as they can greatly influence our journey in life. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or educational pursuits, the essence of this mantra encourages us to seek guidance and surround ourselves with those who inspire and elevate us. By doing so, we can navigate life's challenges with greater insight and strength, much like the divine qualities attributed to Indra.
