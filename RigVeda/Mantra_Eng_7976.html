एष स्य मानुषीष्वा श्येनो न विक्षु सीदति |गच्छञ जारो न योषितम् ||
eṣa sya mānuṣīṣv ā śyeno na vikṣu sīdati | gacchañ jāro na yoṣitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of him
    • mānuṣīṣv: among humans
    • ā: towards
    • śyena: the eagle
    • na: not
    • vikṣu: to be disturbed
    • sīdati: sits
    • gacchañ: going
    • jāro: the one who is wise
    • na: not
    • yoṣitam: towards a woman
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of wisdom and the behavior of a wise person among humans.
    • The eagle symbolizes a high perspective and clarity, suggesting that a wise person remains undisturbed by the chaos around them.
    • The phrase "gacchañ jāro na yoṣitam" implies that a wise person does not engage in frivolous pursuits or distractions, particularly in matters of attraction or desire.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, a wise leader remains focused on the goals of the organization, not getting distracted by office politics or gossip.
    • A student who prioritizes their studies over social distractions exemplifies the wisdom of staying focused on long-term goals.
    • In personal relationships, someone who maintains respect and boundaries, avoiding unnecessary drama, embodies the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and focus in life.
    • It teaches that true wisdom involves maintaining clarity and not being swayed by external distractions or temptations.
    • In real life, applying this principle can lead to better decision-making, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
