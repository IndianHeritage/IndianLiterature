स्वयं कविः विधर्तरि विप्राय रत्नम् इच्छति |यदी मर्मृज्यते धियः ||
svayaṁ kavir vidhartari viprāya ratnam icchati | yadī marmṛjyate dhiyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svayaṁ: oneself
    • kavir: the wise or the poet
    • vidhartari: the sustainer or the creator
    • viprāya: to the learned or the wise
    • ratnam: the jewel or the treasure
    • icchati: desires or wishes
    • yadī: if
    • marmṛjyate: is understood or grasped
    • dhiyaḥ: the intellect or the mind
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea that the wise individual, who is both a creator and sustainer, desires to share their treasures of knowledge and wisdom.
    • The phrase "if the intellect is grasped" suggests that true understanding and insight come from a deep engagement with knowledge.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intellect in achieving one's desires and goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a mentor (the wise) shares their knowledge (the treasure) with a mentee, helping them to grow and succeed in their career.
    • In education, a teacher imparts wisdom to students, encouraging them to think critically and grasp complex concepts, which can lead to their personal and academic growth.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who share their experiences and insights can help others navigate challenges, fostering a supportive community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and the sharing of knowledge as a means to uplift others.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as mentorship, education, and community support, where the exchange of ideas and insights leads to collective growth.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to be generous in sharing their knowledge, as this enriches both the giver and the receiver.
