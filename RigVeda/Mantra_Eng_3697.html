शिवस्त्वष्टार इह गहि विभुः पोष उता तमनā |यज्ञे-यज्ञे न उद अव ||
śivas tvaṣṭar ihā gahi vibhuḥ poṣa uta tmanā | yajñe-yajñe na ud ava ||
  • śivas: This word refers to the auspicious or benevolent aspect of divinity. In real life, it can be associated with positive energy and harmony in our surroundings.
  • tvaṣṭar: This term denotes the creator or the divine craftsman. It symbolizes the creative force in the universe, akin to an artist or a skilled artisan who shapes and molds materials into beautiful forms.
  • ihā: Meaning "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the importance of the present moment and our immediate environment. It reminds us to be mindful and aware of our surroundings.
  • gahi: This translates to "hold" or "grasp." It suggests the act of taking control or embracing something, which can be related to taking charge of our lives and responsibilities.
  • vibhuḥ: This word means "the vast" or "the all-pervading." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine, reminding us that the divine energy is everywhere, in all aspects of life.
  • poṣa: This term refers to nourishment or sustenance. It highlights the importance of nurturing ourselves and others, both physically and spiritually.
  • uta: Meaning "or," it presents an alternative or a choice, indicating the need for discernment in our decisions.
  • tmanā: This word means "with the self" or "by oneself." It emphasizes self-awareness and the importance of inner reflection in our actions.
  • yajñe-yajñe: This phrase refers to "in every sacrifice" or "in every offering." It signifies the importance of selfless actions and contributions to the greater good.
  • na ud ava: This translates to "do not rise up" or "do not go away." It suggests the need for stability and consistency in our efforts and commitments.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nourishment, and the divine presence in our lives. It encourages us to be mindful of our actions, embrace our responsibilities, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute positively to our surroundings and foster a sense of harmony and balance in our lives and the lives of others.
