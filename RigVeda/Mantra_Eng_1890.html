इमं नु सोमं अन्तितो हृत्सु पीतम उप ब्रुवे |यत सिम आगश चक्रमा तात सु मृळतु पुलुकामो हि mortalः ||
imaṁ nu somam antito hṛtsu pītam upa bruve | yat sīm āgaś cakṛmā tat su mṛḻatu pulukāmo hi martyaḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "I now speak of the Soma, which is drunk in the heart; that which I have made, may it be well for me, for the mortal is indeed desirous of it."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Soma: In Vedic literature, Soma refers to a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine insight. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.
    • Antito hṛtsu: This phrase suggests the internalization of the experience, indicating that the effects of Soma are felt deeply within the heart.
    • Pītam: This means 'drunk' or 'consumed', emphasizing the act of taking in something vital for one's existence.
    • Martyaḥ: Refers to a mortal being, highlighting the human condition and the desire for transcendence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Spiritual Practices: Many people today engage in meditation and mindfulness practices to achieve a state of inner peace and enlightenment, akin to the effects of Soma.
    • Health and Wellness: The pursuit of health through nutrition and wellness can be seen as a modern-day quest for 'Soma', where individuals seek to nourish their bodies and minds for a better quality of life.
    • Art and Creativity: Artists and creators often seek inspiration that can be likened to the divine intoxication of Soma, striving to produce works that resonate deeply with human experience.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner nourishment and the quest for spiritual fulfillment. It emphasizes the importance of seeking that which enriches our lives, whether through spiritual practices, health, or creative expression. In today's fast-paced world, this mantra serves as a reminder to connect with our inner selves and pursue what truly matters, fostering a sense of purpose and well-being in our mortal existence.
