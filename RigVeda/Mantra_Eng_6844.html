आरोका इव घेद अह तिग्मा अग्ने तव त्विषः |दद्भिर वनानि बाप्सति ||
ārokā iva ghed aha tigmā agne tava tviṣaḥ | dadbhir vanāni bapsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ārokā: free from disease, healthy
    • iva: like, as if
    • ghed: to shine, to illuminate
    • aha: day, brightness
    • tigmā: sharp, intense
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • tava: your
    • tviṣaḥ: radiance, brilliance
    • dadbhir: with the help of, by means of
    • vanāni: forests, woods
    • bapsati: to nourish, to sustain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of health and vitality.
    • The phrase "ārokā iva" suggests a state of being free from ailments, akin to the purity and brightness of a new day.
    • Agni is depicted as a source of illumination and intensity, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The mention of "vanāni" indicates the nurturing aspect of nature, where forests provide sustenance and support life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern health practices, the emphasis on maintaining a healthy lifestyle mirrors the idea of being "ārokā." This can be seen in the popularity of fitness regimes and balanced diets.
    • The concept of Agni can be related to the importance of fire in various cultures, symbolizing warmth, cooking, and community gatherings.
    • Forests play a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth, providing oxygen, food, and habitat, which aligns with the nurturing aspect mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of health, knowledge, and the nurturing power of nature.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in life, embracing both physical well-being and the wisdom that comes from nature.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate a healthy lifestyle, appreciate the environment, and seek enlightenment through knowledge and experience.
