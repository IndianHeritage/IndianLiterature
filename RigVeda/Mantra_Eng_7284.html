मन्त्र: अन्तर इच्छन्ति तं जनं रुद्रम् परो मनिषया | गृभ्णन्ति जिव्हया ससम् ||
antar icchanti taṁ jane rudram paro manīṣayā | gṛbhṇanti jihvayā sasam ||
  • antar icchanti: This phrase translates to "they desire within." It signifies the inner aspirations and intentions of individuals. In real life, this can be related to personal goals and dreams that people hold deep within themselves, such as pursuing a career, nurturing relationships, or seeking spiritual growth.
  • taṁ jane: This means "that person." It refers to an individual who embodies certain qualities or virtues. In a practical context, this could represent a mentor, leader, or anyone who inspires others through their actions and character.
  • rudram: Rudra is often associated with the storm, the fierce aspect of the divine, and transformation. In everyday life, this can symbolize the challenges and adversities one faces, which can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • paro manīṣayā: This translates to "with supreme wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of seeking higher knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education, philosophical inquiry, or spiritual enlightenment, where individuals strive to gain deeper insights into life and existence.
  • gṛbhṇanti: This means "they grasp" or "they receive." It indicates the act of accepting or embracing something. In practical terms, this could refer to how individuals accept opportunities, knowledge, or experiences that come their way.
  • jihvayā: This translates to "with the tongue." It highlights the power of speech and communication. In real life, this can be related to how words can influence thoughts, relationships, and actions, emphasizing the importance of mindful communication.
  • sasam: This term can be interpreted as "with joy" or "with happiness." It signifies the positive emotions associated with the experiences and knowledge one gains. In everyday life, this can be reflected in moments of joy that arise from achieving goals, forming connections, or experiencing personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of inner desire, the pursuit of wisdom, and the acceptance of experiences that lead to personal transformation. It emphasizes the significance of understanding one's inner aspirations and the role of communication in shaping our realities. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as setting personal goals, seeking mentorship, embracing challenges, and communicating effectively to foster positive relationships. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to pursue our inner truths with joy and wisdom, leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
