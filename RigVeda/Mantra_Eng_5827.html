न स स्वो दक्षो वरुण धृतिः सा सुरा मन्युर विभीदको अचित्तिः |अस्ति ज्यायान कनीयस उपारे स्वप्नश चनेद अनृतस्य प्रयोताः ||
na sa svo dakṣo varuṇa dhrutiḥ sā surā manyur vibhīdako acittiḥ | asti jyāyān kanīyasa upāre svapnaś caned anṛtasya prayotā ||
  • na sa svo dakṣo varuṇa dhrutiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "He who is not capable, O Varuna, is not steadfast." Here, Varuna is a deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. The term "dakṣo" refers to capability or skill, while "dhrutiḥ" signifies steadiness or resolve.
  • sā surā manyur vibhīdako acittiḥ: This translates to "That anger which is not to be feared is indeed a divine quality." "Surā" refers to divine or celestial beings, and "manyur" means anger. The phrase suggests that not all anger is negative; some can be righteous and just.
  • asti jyāyān kanīyasa upāre: This means "There exists something greater than the lesser." "Jyāyān" refers to the greater or superior, while "kanīyasa" means the lesser. This can be interpreted as a reminder of the hierarchy and the importance of recognizing greater truths or values in life.
  • svapnaś caned anṛtasya prayotā: This translates to "If one dreams, let it not be a falsehood." "Svapna" means dream, and "anṛtasya" refers to falsehood or untruth. This line emphasizes the importance of authenticity in our aspirations and dreams.

  • Significance and Real-Life Application: This mantra encapsulates the essence of integrity, capability, and the pursuit of truth. In real life, it serves as a guiding principle for individuals to strive for excellence and authenticity in their actions and aspirations.
  • For example, in a professional setting, one might face challenges that test their skills and resolve. The mantra encourages individuals to remain steadfast and capable, much like a leader who must navigate through crises with determination.
  • Furthermore, the acknowledgment of righteous anger can be applied in social justice movements, where individuals channel their emotions towards positive change rather than fear or chaos.
  • Lastly, the reminder to pursue genuine dreams can resonate with students and professionals alike, urging them to seek paths that align with their true values and aspirations, rather than succumbing to societal pressures or falsehoods.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reminder of the virtues of capability, integrity, and authenticity. It encourages individuals to recognize their strengths, channel their emotions constructively, and pursue genuine aspirations. In a world often filled with distractions and falsehoods, this mantra stands as a beacon of guidance, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional endeavors.
