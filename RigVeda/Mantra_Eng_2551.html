स घा यस् ते ददाशति समिधा जातवेदसे |सो अग्ने धत्ते सुवीर्यं स पुष्यति ||
sa ghā yas te dadāśati samidhā jātavedase | so agne dhatte suvīryaṁ sa puṣyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he/that
    • ghā: who/that which
    • yas: who/that
    • te: to you
    • dadāśati: gives
    • samidhā: with the offering
    • jātavedase: O Jātavedas (the one who knows all births)
    • so: he/that
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dhatte: holds/possesses
    • suvīryaṁ: great strength
    • sa: he/that
    • puṣyati: nourishes
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, and Jātavedas, who is considered the knower of all births.
    • It expresses a prayer for the divine to bestow strength and nourishment upon the devotee.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings made with devotion, which are believed to invoke divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Hindu ceremony, offerings made to Agni during a yajna (sacrificial fire ritual) symbolize the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • Individuals seeking strength in their personal or professional lives may recite this mantra to invoke divine support and guidance.
    • In times of hardship, people may turn to this mantra as a source of inspiration and hope, believing that divine nourishment will help them overcome challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine connection through offerings and prayers.
    • It serves as a reminder of the strength and nourishment that can be derived from spiritual practices.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek divine support in their endeavors, fostering resilience and hope in challenging times.
