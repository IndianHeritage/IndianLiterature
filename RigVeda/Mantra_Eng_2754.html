शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek help or guidance from a higher power or a source of strength when facing challenges.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking the support of powerful forces or leaders in our lives, akin to how we might look to mentors or influential figures for guidance.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This translates to "in this burden, we seek the best." "Asmin" means "in this," "bhare" refers to "burden," "nṛtamaṁ" means "the best," and "vājasātau" can be interpreted as "in the context of strength or power." This suggests that in times of difficulty, we should strive to find the best solutions or support systems, much like how individuals seek the best resources or advice during tough times.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: "śṛṇvantam" means "listening," "ugram" refers to "fierce" or "powerful," and "ūtaye" means "for the purpose of elevation." This part of the mantra highlights the importance of being receptive to powerful ideas or teachings that can elevate our lives. For example, listening to motivational speakers or engaging in educational pursuits can lead to personal growth.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: "samatsu" means "in the assembly," "ghnantaṁ" refers to "striking down," and "vṛtrāṇi" can be interpreted as "obstacles" or "enemies." This suggests that in a community or group setting, we can collectively overcome challenges. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where collaboration helps to tackle problems more effectively.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: "saṁjitaṁ" means "gathered" or "assembled," and "dhanānām" refers to "wealth" or "resources." This emphasizes the idea of pooling resources together for a common goal. In practical terms, this can be likened to community efforts, such as fundraising or cooperative businesses, where individuals come together to achieve greater success.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, guidance, and collaboration in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of invoking powerful forces, listening to wise teachings, and working together to achieve common goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and community building. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate difficulties more effectively and foster a sense of unity and purpose in their endeavors.
