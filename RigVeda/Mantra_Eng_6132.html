तां मे अश्विनां सनीनां विद्यात्म नवानाम् |यथा चिच्चैद्यः कशुः शतमुुष्ट्राणां ददात सहस्रादश गोनाम् ||
tā me aśvinā sanīnāṁ vidyātaṁ navānām | yathā cic caidyaḥ kaśuḥ śatam uṣṭrānāṁ dadat sahasrā daśa gonām ||
  • tā me aśvinā sanīnāṁ vidyātaṁ navānām: This phrase can be translated to "May the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, grant me knowledge of the new." The Ashvins are considered deities of health, medicine, and the dawn in Vedic literature. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • yathā cic caidyaḥ kaśuḥ: This part translates to "As the one who knows the essence of the universe." It emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper truths of existence, akin to gaining wisdom through experience and introspection.
  • śatam uṣṭrānāṁ dadat sahasrā daśa gonām: This translates to "Giving a hundred camels, a thousand, and tenfold." This line signifies abundance and generosity, suggesting that true wealth is not just material but also in the form of knowledge and wisdom shared with others.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern life, the quest for knowledge and understanding can be seen in the pursuit of education and personal growth. Just as the Ashvins are invoked for wisdom, individuals often seek mentors or educational resources to gain insights that can lead to a better life.
  • Generosity in Action: The idea of giving abundantly can be reflected in charitable acts. For instance, a successful entrepreneur might donate a portion of their wealth to support education for underprivileged children, embodying the spirit of sharing knowledge and resources.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding the essence of life while also highlighting the importance of generosity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through continuous learning, sharing wisdom, and contributing to the welfare of others. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
