त्वं ह्यङ्ग दैव्याऽपवमान जनिमानि द्युमत्तमः |अमृतत्वाय घोषय ||
tvaṁ hy a1ṅga daivyā pavamāna janimāni dyumattamaḥ | amṛtatvāya ghoṣayaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ hy a1ṅga: This phrase translates to "You are indeed the divine essence." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," and "a1ṅga" refers to the divine or celestial aspect. This highlights the belief in the presence of divinity in all beings.
  • daivyā pavamāna: "daivyā" means "divine" and "pavamāna" refers to the "purifying" or "sacred." This suggests that the divine essence has a purifying quality, which can cleanse and elevate the human spirit.
  • janimāni dyumattamaḥ: "janimāni" translates to "birth" or "creation," and "dyumattamaḥ" means "the most radiant" or "brilliant." This part of the mantra emphasizes the idea of divine creation and the brilliance of life that stems from this divine source.
  • amṛtatvāya ghoṣayaḥ: "amṛtatvāya" means "for immortality" and "ghoṣayaḥ" translates to "proclaim" or "call forth." This indicates a call to attain immortality or eternal life through the divine essence.

In summary, this mantra encapsulates the essence of recognizing the divine presence within ourselves and the world around us. It emphasizes the purifying nature of this divinity, the brilliance of creation, and the aspiration for immortality. In real life, this can be applied in various ways:


  • In personal development, individuals can strive to recognize and cultivate their inner divinity, leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
  • In community and social settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards purification and elevation of society, promoting values of compassion and unity.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder of the ultimate goal of achieving a state of immortality through spiritual growth and connection with the divine.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call to acknowledge the divine within and to pursue a path of purity and brilliance, ultimately leading to a state of eternal existence.
