द्युता dyutadyāmānam बृहतीं bṛhatīm ऋतेन ṛtena ऋतावरिम् ṛtāvarīm अरुणप्सुं aruṇapsuṁ विभातिम् vibhātīm |देवीं devīm उषसं uṣasaṁ स्वर् svar āवहन्तीम् āvahantīm प्रति prati विप्रासो viprāso मतिभिर् matibhir जारन्ते jarante ||
dyutadyāmānam bṛhatīm ṛtena ṛtāvarīm aruṇapsuṁ vibhātīm | devīm uṣasaṁ svar āvahantīm prati viprāso matibhir jarante ||
  • dyutadyāmānam: This term can be broken down into "dyuta" meaning brightness or light, and "yāmānam" which refers to the act of being adorned or decorated. Together, it signifies the radiant and luminous nature of the divine.
  • bṛhatīm: This word translates to "the vast" or "the great." It emphasizes the expansive and all-encompassing nature of the divine presence.
  • ṛtena: Meaning "by truth" or "through righteousness," this term highlights the importance of truth and moral order in the universe.
  • ṛtāvarīm: This can be understood as "the one who possesses truth." It signifies a divine force that embodies and upholds cosmic order.
  • aruṇapsuṁ: "Aruṇa" refers to the reddish hue of dawn, while "apsuṁ" relates to water. Together, they evoke the image of the dawn illuminating the waters, symbolizing new beginnings and the cleansing nature of water.
  • vibhātīm: This term means "shining" or "illuminating," reinforcing the theme of light and clarity associated with the divine.
  • devīm: This translates to "goddess," indicating a feminine divine presence, often associated with nurturing and creation.
  • uṣasaṁ: Referring to "dawn," this word signifies the arrival of light and the dispelling of darkness, symbolizing hope and renewal.
  • svar: Meaning "heaven" or "sky," this term connects the divine with the celestial realm, suggesting a higher power that governs the universe.
  • āvahantīm: This means "bringing forth" or "conveying," indicating the active role of the divine in manifesting light and life.
  • prati: This translates to "towards" or "in relation to," suggesting a connection or interaction between the divine and humanity.
  • viprāso: Referring to "the wise" or "the learned," this term emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in understanding the divine.
  • matibhir: This means "with thoughts" or "with intellect," indicating that the divine can be approached through contemplation and understanding.
  • jarante: This translates to "they sing" or "they celebrate," suggesting a communal expression of reverence and gratitude towards the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine light, truth, and the cyclical nature of life represented by dawn. It signifies the importance of righteousness and wisdom in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to moments of new beginnings, such as starting a new project, entering a new phase in life, or seeking clarity in difficult situations. By invoking the qualities of light, truth, and wisdom, individuals can find guidance and inspiration in their daily lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the universe around them.
