तवे असुर्यं वसवो न ऋण्वन् क्रतुं हि ते मित्रमहो जुषन्त। त्वं दस्यूम् ओकसो अग्न आज उरु ज्योतिर जनयन्न आर्याय।
tve asurya1ṁ vasavo ny ṛṇvan kratuṁ hi te mitramaho juṣanta | tvaṁ dasyūm̐r okaso agna āja uru jyotir janayann āryāya ||
  • tve asuryaṁ vasavo ny ṛṇvan: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Sun, are the source of all prosperity and wealth." Here, "Asurya" refers to the Sun, which is often seen as a divine source of energy and life. In real life, the Sun is essential for agriculture, providing the energy needed for plants to grow, which in turn sustains human life.
  • kratuṁ hi te mitramaho juṣanta: This translates to "Your power, O Sun, is enjoyed by all beings." The word "kratu" signifies strength or power, while "mitram" means friend. This suggests that the Sun is a friend to all, providing light and warmth that are crucial for survival. For example, in many cultures, the Sun is celebrated in festivals, symbolizing gratitude for its life-giving properties.
  • tvaṁ dasyūm̐r okaso agna: This part means "You, O Fire, are the one who drives away darkness." "Dasyūm" refers to darkness or ignorance, while "agna" signifies fire. Fire is often associated with knowledge and enlightenment. In a practical sense, fire is used in cooking, heating, and even in rituals, symbolizing the removal of ignorance and the illumination of knowledge.
  • āja uru jyotir janayann āryāya: This translates to "You generate vast light for the noble." "Jyotir" means light, and "āryāya" refers to the noble or the righteous. This indicates that the light provided by the Sun and Fire is not just physical but also metaphorical, representing wisdom and virtue. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge and ethical living, where individuals strive to be enlightened and virtuous.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of the Sun and Fire as sources of life, prosperity, and knowledge. In our daily lives, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by recognizing the importance of nature and its elements in sustaining life. It encourages us to be grateful for the resources we have and to seek knowledge and enlightenment. By fostering a connection with these natural forces, we can lead a more harmonious and fulfilling life, embodying the virtues of gratitude, wisdom, and ethical living.
