शेषे वनेषु मात्रोः सं त्वा मर्तास इन्दते |अतन्द्रो हव्यां वहसि हविष्कृत आद इद देवेषु राजसि ||
śeṣe vaneṣu mātroḥ saṁ tvā martāsa indhate | atandro havyā vahasi haviṣkṛta ād id deveṣu rājasi ||
  • śeṣe vaneṣu mātroḥ: This phrase can be translated as "In the end, in the forests, of the mother." Here, "śeṣe" refers to the end or conclusion, "vaneṣu" means in the forests, and "mātroḥ" signifies the concept of mother or nurturing. This can be interpreted as a call to return to nature and recognize the nurturing aspect of the earth, akin to how a mother nurtures her child.
  • saṁ tvā martāsa indhate: This translates to "the mortals invoke you." "Saṁ" means together or in unison, "tvā" refers to you, and "martāsa" means mortals or human beings. This line emphasizes the collective human desire to connect with the divine or the higher powers, showcasing the importance of community and shared beliefs in spirituality.
  • atandro havyā vahasi: This can be interpreted as "you carry the offerings without fatigue." "Atandro" means without effort or fatigue, "havyā" refers to offerings, and "vahasi" means to carry. This suggests that the divine or the higher powers are always ready to accept our offerings and prayers, highlighting the importance of devotion and sincerity in our actions.
  • haviṣkṛta ād id deveṣu rājasi: This translates to "you, who are the ruler among the gods, are made of offerings." "Haviṣkṛta" means made of offerings, "ād" signifies the beginning or source, "id" means this, "deveṣu" refers to the gods, and "rājasi" means ruler or king. This line underscores the idea that the divine is sustained by the devotion and offerings of mortals, reinforcing the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of the relationship between humanity and the divine. It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of nature, the collective invocation of higher powers, the importance of sincere offerings, and the reciprocal relationship between mortals and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in rituals, community gatherings, and personal spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder of our connection to nature and the divine, encouraging us to approach life with gratitude, respect, and a sense of community. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize their place in the larger tapestry of existence, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their spiritual journey.
