ते त्वा मदा अमदन् तानि वृष्ण्याः ते सोमासो वृत्रहत्येषु सत्पते |यत्कारवे दश वृत्राण्य अप्रति बर्हिष्मते न सहस्राणि बर्हयः ||
te tvā madā amadan tāni vṛṣṇyā te somāso vṛtrahatyeṣu satpate | yat kārave daśa vṛtrāṇy aprati barhiṣmate ni sahasrāṇi barhayaḥ ||
  • te tvā madā amadan tāni vṛṣṇyā te somāso vṛtrahatyeṣu satpate
    • te tvā - "to you" or "for you": This phrase indicates a direct address to a divine entity, suggesting a relationship of devotion and reverence.
    • madā - "intoxication" or "ecstasy": This word signifies a state of bliss or heightened awareness, often associated with spiritual experiences.
    • amadan - "not intoxicated": This juxtaposition highlights the balance between spiritual ecstasy and groundedness.
    • tāni vṛṣṇyā - "those (gifts) from the Vṛṣhṇi": Referring to the blessings or boons that come from the divine lineage of the Vṛṣhṇis, a prominent clan in Vedic texts.
    • te somāso - "your soma": Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine connection.
    • vṛtrahatyeṣu - "in the slaying of Vṛtra": Vṛtra is a dragon or demon representing obstacles; this phrase signifies overcoming challenges.
    • satpate - "O Lord of Truth": Acknowledging the divine as the ultimate source of truth and righteousness.
  • yat kārave daśa vṛtrāṇy aprati barhiṣmate
    • yat kārave - "what I do": This indicates the speaker's intention or actions directed towards the divine.
    • daśa vṛtrāṇy - "ten Vṛtras": Symbolizing various challenges or obstacles that one may face in life.
    • aprati - "not to be opposed": Suggesting that these challenges can be faced without resistance when aligned with divine will.
    • barhiṣmate - "to the one who is worthy of offerings": Referring to the divine as deserving of respect and offerings.
  • ni sahasrāṇi barhayaḥ
    • ni - "down" or "to the ground": This indicates a grounding aspect, bringing the spiritual into the material world.
    • sahasrāṇi - "thousands": Suggesting abundance and the vastness of blessings.
    • barhayaḥ - "of the sacred grass": Referring to the sacredness of offerings made in rituals, symbolizing purity and devotion.

Conclusion:

This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the pursuit of spiritual ecstasy, and the overcoming of life's challenges through divine connection. It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions with the divine will, recognizing the blessings that come from a higher power, and the significance of rituals in grounding spiritual experiences. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Facing personal challenges with a sense of faith and trust in a higher power.
  • Engaging in rituals or practices that foster a deeper connection with the divine.
  • Recognizing the abundance of blessings in life and expressing gratitude.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between the spiritual and material worlds, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives.
