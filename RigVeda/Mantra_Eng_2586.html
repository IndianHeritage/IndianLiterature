मित्रश्च तुभ्यं वरुणः सहस्वोऽग्ने विश्वे मरुतः सुम्नमर्चन |यच्छोचिषा सहसस् पुत्र तिष्ठा अभि क्षितीः प्रथयन सूर्यो नृन् ||
mitraś ca tubhyaṁ varuṇaḥ sahasvo 'gne viśve marutaḥ sumnam arcan | yac chociṣā sahasas putra tiṣṭhā abhi kṣitīḥ prathayan sūryo nṝn ||
  • Mitra: In Vedic literature, Mitra is often associated with friendship, harmony, and the natural order. He represents the aspect of the divine that fosters relationships and social bonds.
  • Varuṇa: Varuṇa is the god of cosmic order and water. He embodies the moral and ethical laws of the universe, ensuring that truth and justice prevail.
  • Sahasvo: This term translates to "powerful" or "mighty," indicating strength and capability. It suggests the importance of having strength in both physical and moral dimensions.
  • Agne: Referring to Agni, the fire god, this term symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings. Fire is also a purifying element in many cultures.
  • Viśve: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • Marutaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life. They signify change, movement, and the power of the elements.
  • Sumnam: This term means "auspicious" or "favorable," suggesting the importance of seeking positive outcomes and blessings in life.
  • Arcan: This refers to worship or offering, highlighting the significance of rituals and devotion in connecting with the divine.
  • Yac: Meaning "whatever," this word emphasizes the idea of openness to all possibilities and experiences.
  • Shociṣā: This term can be interpreted as "radiance" or "brilliance," symbolizing enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • Sahasas: Similar to "sahasvo," it denotes strength and vigor, reinforcing the theme of power.
  • Putra: Meaning "son" or "child," this term signifies lineage, family, and the continuation of values and traditions through generations.
  • Tiṣṭhā: This means "to stand" or "to remain," indicating stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Abhi: This term means "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting movement and progress.
  • Kṣitīḥ: Referring to the earth, this word symbolizes grounding, stability, and the physical realm.
  • Prathayan: This means "to spread" or "to expand," indicating growth and the dissemination of knowledge or blessings.
  • Sūryo: Referring to the sun, this term symbolizes light, life, and vitality. The sun is often seen as a source of energy and sustenance.
  • Nṝn: This word means "of men" or "human beings," emphasizing the human experience and the connection to the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony, strength, and divine connection in life. It emphasizes the importance of relationships (Mitra), moral order (Varuṇa), and the transformative power of energy (Agni). In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering friendships, promoting ethical behavior, and seeking auspicious outcomes in endeavors. It serves as a reminder to remain grounded (Kṣitīḥ) while striving for growth (Prathayan) and enlightenment (Shociṣā). By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose, ultimately contributing to a harmonious and prosperous existence.
