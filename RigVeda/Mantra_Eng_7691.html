स्वादिष्टया मदीष्टया पवस्व सोम धारया |इन्द्राय पातवे सुतः ||
svādiṣṭhayā madiṣṭhayā pavasva soma dhārayā | indrāya pātave sutaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svādiṣṭhayā: With the essence of sweetness or delight.
    • madiṣṭhayā: With the essence of intoxication or joy.
    • pavasva: Flow or move; often refers to the flow of the soma juice.
    • soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • dhārayā: Holding or sustaining.
    • indrāya: For Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • pātave: For the one who is powerful or strong.
    • sutaḥ: Offspring or son; often refers to the offerings made to the gods.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the essence of sweetness and joy, asking for the flow of the soma to be sustained and directed towards Indra, the powerful deity, as an offering. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the sustenance of joy and vitality in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In celebrations, such as weddings or festivals, people often seek blessings for happiness and prosperity, similar to invoking the essence of sweetness and joy in this mantra.
    • In personal growth, individuals may strive to cultivate joy and positivity in their lives, akin to the flow of soma, which represents vitality and inspiration.
    • In community gatherings, sharing food and drink can symbolize the sustenance of joy and togetherness, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings that sustain joy and vitality in our lives. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as celebrations, personal growth, and community bonding. By invoking the essence of sweetness and joy, we can foster a positive environment that encourages growth, happiness, and connection with the divine.
