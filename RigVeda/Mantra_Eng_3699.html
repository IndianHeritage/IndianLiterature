स्वाहाग्नये वरुणाय स्वाहेन्द्राय मरुद्भ्यः |स्वाहा देवभ्यो हविः ||
svāhāgnaye varuṇāya svāhendrāya marudbhyaḥ | svāhā devebhyo haviḥ ||
  • svāhā: This term is often used in Vedic rituals and can be understood as an invocation or offering. It signifies the act of surrendering or dedicating something to the divine.
  • agnaye: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. Fire is a symbol of purity and transformation.
  • varuṇāya: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical aspects of the universe, overseeing the laws of nature and human conduct.
  • svāhendrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods, associated with rain, storms, and warfare. Indra is often invoked for strength and protection.
  • marudbhyaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature. They are often associated with wind and are seen as powerful and dynamic forces.
  • devebhyo: This term means "to the gods," indicating that the offerings are being made to various divine entities.
  • haviḥ: This refers to the offerings or oblations made during rituals, symbolizing the sustenance provided to the gods in exchange for their blessings.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation where the speaker is offering their respects and offerings to various deities, including Agni, Varuna, Indra, and the Maruts. Each of these deities represents different aspects of life and nature, and the act of offering signifies a desire for their blessings and support.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invoke divine blessings for prosperity, health, and harmony.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra as a meditative tool, reflecting on the qualities of the deities and how they can embody those qualities in their own lives.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of nature deities like Varuna and the Maruts can remind individuals of the importance of respecting and preserving the natural world.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, recognizing the interconnectedness of various forces in the universe. By offering respect and seeking blessings from these deities, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude, responsibility, and harmony in their lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of balance between human actions and the natural world, encouraging a respectful and mindful approach to existence.
