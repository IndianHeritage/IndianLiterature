आ यद वज्रम बाह्वोः इन्द्र धातसे मदाच्युतम अहये हन्तवा उ |प्र पर्वता अनवन्त प्र गावः प्र ब्रह्माणो अभिनक्षन्त इन्द्रम् ||
ā yad vajram bāhvor indra dhatse madacyutam ahaye hantavā u | pra parvatā anavanta pra gāvaḥ pra brahmāṇo abhinakṣanta indram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: Whatever
    • vajram: Thunderbolt or weapon
    • bāhvor: Of the arms
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • dhatse: You wield
    • madacyutam: The intoxication of strength
    • ahaye: O Indra
    • hantavā: You who can destroy
    • u: And
    • pra parvatā: The great mountains
    • anavanta: Unyielding
    • pra gāvaḥ: The cows
    • pra brahmāṇo: The Brahmanas (wise ones)
    • abhinakṣanta: Who do not abandon
    • indram: Indra
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
    • The reference to "vajram" (thunderbolt) signifies the might and authority that Indra possesses.
    • The phrase "madacyutam" suggests the intoxication or overwhelming strength that comes from divine blessings.
    • By addressing Indra as "hantavā," the mantra acknowledges his ability to destroy obstacles and enemies.
    • The mention of "pra parvatā" (great mountains) and "pra gāvaḥ" (the cows) symbolizes stability and prosperity, indicating that Indra's blessings can lead to abundance.
    • The reference to "brahmāṇo" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in harnessing divine power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, invoking Indra is common during rituals for rain, as farmers seek his blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to seek strength and resilience in overcoming difficulties.
    • Communities often come together in festivals to honor Indra, celebrating the abundance and prosperity that his blessings bring.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine strength and wisdom in achieving success and overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, strength, and knowledge, encouraging individuals to seek balance in their lives.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be applied in personal development, community building, and environmental stewardship, emphasizing the need for strength, wisdom, and harmony with nature.
