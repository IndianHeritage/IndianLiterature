बलं धेहि तनूषु नो बलम इन्द्राणुत्सु नः |बलं तोकाय तनयाय जीवसे त्वं हि बलदाऽसि ||
balaṁ dhehi tanūṣu no balam indrānaḻutsu naḥ | balaṁ tokāya tanayāya jīvase tvaṁ hi baladā asi ||
  • Translation: "Grant us strength in our bodies, O Indra; may we not be weak. Strength for our offspring, for our children; for life, you are indeed the giver of strength."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • balaṁ: strength or power
    • dhehi: grant or bestow
    • tanūṣu: in our bodies
    • no: our
    • indrānaḻutsu: O Indra (the deity representing strength and power)
    • naḥ: us
    • tokāya: for our offspring
    • tanayāya: for our children
    • jīvase: for life
    • tvaṁ: you
    • hi: indeed
    • baladā: giver of strength
    • asi: you are

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for strength, not just for oneself but also for future generations. It emphasizes the importance of physical and spiritual strength in life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the context of parenting, where parents often pray for the health and strength of their children, hoping to provide them with the resilience needed to face life's challenges.
    • In a broader sense, communities and societies often seek strength through unity and support, reflecting the idea that strength is not only an individual trait but a collective one.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of strength in various aspects of life, including physical health, familial bonds, and community support.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking strength is a universal desire, applicable in personal, familial, and societal contexts.
    • By invoking the divine for strength, individuals and communities can foster resilience and well-being, ensuring a better future for themselves and their descendants.
