वयं अग्ने अरवतā वā सुवीर्यं ब्रह्मणा वā चितयेमā जनां अति |अस्माकं द्युम्नम् अधि पञ्च कृष्टिषूच्चा स्वर्ण न शुशुचिता दुष्टारम् ||
vayam agne arvatā vā suvīryam brahmaṇā vā citayemā janām̐ ati | asmākaṁ dyumnam adhi pañca kṛṣṭiṣūccā sva1r ṇa śuśucīta duṣṭaram ||
  • Vayam Agne: This phrase translates to "We, O Agni (the Fire God)." Agni is often seen as a divine force that purifies and transforms. In real life, this can be likened to the role of fire in cooking, which transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals.
  • Arvatā vā: This means "with strength." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in our endeavors. For example, in sports, athletes train rigorously to build physical and mental strength to achieve their goals.
  • Suvīryam: This translates to "good strength" or "heroic strength." It suggests that not only physical strength is important but also moral and ethical strength. In a workplace, a leader who demonstrates integrity and courage can inspire their team to perform better.
  • Brahmaṇā vā: This means "by the divine" or "through the sacred." It indicates that our actions should be aligned with higher principles or values. For instance, in community service, individuals often work towards the greater good, embodying the spirit of selflessness.
  • Citayemā janām̐ ati: This translates to "may we inspire the people." It highlights the importance of leadership and influence. In real life, teachers and mentors play a crucial role in inspiring the next generation to pursue knowledge and excellence.
  • Asmākaṁ dyumnam: This means "our glory." It signifies the collective achievements and recognition of a group. For example, a sports team celebrating a championship victory reflects the glory of teamwork and dedication.
  • Adhi pañca kṛṣṭiṣūccā: This translates to "above the five (elements)." It suggests transcending the material world and focusing on spiritual growth. In meditation practices, individuals seek to rise above their physical surroundings to connect with their inner selves.
  • Sva1r ṇa: This means "our own." It emphasizes personal ownership and responsibility in our actions. In environmental conservation, individuals taking responsibility for their local ecosystems exemplify this principle.
  • Śuśucīta duṣṭaram: This translates to "may we overcome difficulties." It conveys a message of perseverance and resilience in the face of challenges. For instance, entrepreneurs often face numerous obstacles but must remain determined to succeed.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, inspiration, and collective glory. It serves as a reminder that our actions should be guided by higher principles and that we have the power to inspire others. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, teamwork, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence while uplifting those around them. By embodying these values, we can create a positive impact in our communities and beyond.
