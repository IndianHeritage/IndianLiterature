तद् वो गाया सुत सचा पुरुहूताय सत्वने |शं यद् गवे न शाकिने ||
tad vo gāya sute sacā puruhūtāya satvane | śaṁ yad gave na śākine ||
  • tad vo gāya sute sacā puruhūtāya satvane
    • tad - that
    • vo - for you
    • gāya - to sing or praise
    • sute - in the context of offerings or sacrifices
    • sacā - with truth or sincerity
    • puruhūtāya - to the one who is invoked frequently
    • satvane - to the one who possesses strength or vitality
  • śaṁ yad gave na śākine
    • śaṁ - peace or well-being
    • yad - that which
    • gave - in the context of cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • na - not
    • śākine - to the one who is weak or lacking

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to praise and offer to a powerful and benevolent deity, seeking strength and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of sincerity in offerings and the desire for peace and prosperity, particularly in relation to the nurturing aspects of life, symbolized by cows.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals can recite this mantra during prayers or meditation to invoke strength and peace in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: It can be used in rituals or ceremonies that aim to bring the community together for a common purpose, fostering unity and well-being.
  • Personal Reflection: The mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their intentions and the sincerity of their actions, promoting a life of integrity and purpose.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for sincere offerings to a powerful entity, seeking strength and peace. It serves as a reminder of the importance of truthfulness in our actions and the nurturing aspects of life, encouraging individuals to cultivate well-being not just for themselves but for their communities as well. In a world often filled with challenges, this mantra can inspire a sense of hope and collective strength.
