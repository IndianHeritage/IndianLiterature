पर्षि दीने गभीर आम्ं उग्रपुत्रे जिघांसतः |माकिस तोकस्य नो ऋषत ||
parṣi dīne gabhīra ām̐ ugraputre jighāṁsataḥ | mākis tokasya no riṣat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parṣi: to be spread out or to be distributed
    • dīne: in the day or during the daytime
    • gabhīra: deep or profound
    • ām̐: a particle often used for emphasis
    • ugraputre: fierce or powerful offspring
    • jighāṁsataḥ: desiring to harm or to attack
    • mākis: may we not be harmed
    • tokasya: of the offspring or progeny
    • no: our
    • riṣat: to be angry or to be disturbed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of protection and the desire for safety from harm.
    • The phrase "parṣi dīne" suggests a sense of openness and distribution of light or knowledge during the day, symbolizing clarity and awareness.
    • "Gabhīra ām̐" emphasizes the depth of understanding or the profound nature of existence.
    • The term "ugraputre" refers to powerful forces or entities that may pose a threat, indicating the challenges one may face in life.
    • "Jighāṁsataḥ" reflects the intent to harm, which can be interpreted as the negative influences or adversities that one encounters.
    • The concluding plea "mākis tokasya no riṣat" is a request for protection, asking that we and our progeny remain unharmed by these fierce forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often face fierce competition ("ugraputre") that can lead to stress and anxiety ("jighāṁsataḥ"). This mantra serves as a reminder to seek clarity and maintain focus on one's goals ("parṣi dīne").
    • Parents may recite this mantra for the safety and well-being of their children, wishing to shield them from negative influences and harmful situations.
    • In times of societal unrest or personal crisis, this mantra can be a source of comfort, invoking a sense of protection and resilience against adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection and the pursuit of safety amidst challenges.
    • It highlights the importance of awareness and understanding in navigating life's complexities.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied as a meditative practice or a protective invocation, fostering a sense of security for oneself and loved ones.
