स यो व्यास्थाद अभि दक्षित उर्वीम् पशुर नैति स्वयुर अगोपाः |अग्निः शोचिष्मां अतसांन्य उष्णन कृष्णव्यथिर अस्वदयन न भूम ||
sa yo vy asthād abhi dakṣad urvīm paśur naiti svayur agopāḥ | agniḥ śociṣmām̐ atasāny uṣṇan kṛṣṇavyathir asvadayan na bhūma ||
  • sa yo vy asthād abhi dakṣad urvīm: This phrase can be translated to "He who has established himself in the vastness." It signifies the importance of grounding oneself in the universe and understanding one's place within it. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who find their purpose and work towards it, such as a scientist dedicated to research or an artist committed to their craft.
  • paśur naiti svayur agopāḥ: This translates to "The animal does not go astray; it is guided by its own nature." This highlights the idea that every being has an inherent nature that guides its actions. For example, a dog will instinctively protect its owner, while a bird will migrate according to seasonal changes. This reflects the importance of understanding and respecting one's instincts and natural inclinations.
  • agniḥ śociṣmām̐: This means "the fire shines brightly." Fire is often a symbol of knowledge, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, where individuals seek to illuminate their minds and the world around them, much like a teacher inspiring students or a mentor guiding a protégé.
  • atasāny uṣṇan kṛṣṇavyathir asvadayan: This can be interpreted as "the warmth of the sun brings forth the fruits of the earth." It emphasizes the connection between nature and sustenance. In practical terms, this can be seen in agriculture, where farmers rely on the sun's warmth to grow crops, illustrating the interdependence of life and the environment.
  • na bhūma: This phrase translates to "not of the earth." It suggests a transcendence beyond the physical realm. In a real-life context, this can refer to spiritual pursuits or aspirations that go beyond material possessions, such as the quest for inner peace or enlightenment through meditation and self-reflection.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about self-awareness, the guidance of nature, the pursuit of knowledge, the interdependence of life, and the transcendence of material existence. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to understand their place in the universe, respect their instincts, seek knowledge, appreciate the natural world, and aspire for spiritual growth. In today's fast-paced life, this mantra serves as a reminder to pause, reflect, and align oneself with these timeless principles for a more fulfilling existence.
