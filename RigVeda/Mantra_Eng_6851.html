उद अग्ने तव तद घृताद् आर्ची रोचत आहुतम् |निंसानं जुह्वो मुखे ||
ud agne tava tad ghṛtād arcī rocata āhutam | niṁsānaṁ juhvo3 mukhe ||
  • ud agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," where Agni represents the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings in rituals.
  • tava tad ghṛtād: This means "that which is from your ghee." Ghee (clarified butter) is considered sacred and is used in various rituals and offerings, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
  • arcī rocata: This translates to "the rays shine." It signifies the brilliance and divine light associated with Agni, representing knowledge, enlightenment, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • āhutam: This means "offered." It refers to the offerings made to Agni during rituals, emphasizing the importance of devotion and the act of giving.
  • niṁsānaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." It highlights the relationship between the devotee and the divine.
  • juhvo: This means "I offer" or "I invoke." It indicates the act of making a conscious offering to the divine, reinforcing the connection between the human and the divine.
  • mukhe: This translates to "in the mouth" or "in the presence." It signifies the importance of speech and communication in invoking the divine presence.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of fire (Agni) as a divine entity that purifies and connects the material and spiritual worlds. It highlights the act of offering and the significance of rituals in establishing a relationship with the divine.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Rituals and Ceremonies: Just as in ancient times, people today still perform rituals that involve offerings to deities, symbolizing gratitude and devotion.
    • Personal Growth: The concept of invoking divine light can be related to seeking knowledge and enlightenment in one's personal and professional life.
    • Community and Connection: The act of coming together for rituals fosters a sense of community and shared values, which is essential in today's fragmented society.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of devotion, the act of giving, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to recognize the significance of rituals in fostering community and personal growth.
