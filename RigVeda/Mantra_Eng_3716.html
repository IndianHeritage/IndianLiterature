स हि ष्मा धन्वाक्षितं दाता न दात्य ā पशुः | हिरीश्मश्रुः शुचिदन्न ऋभुर अनिभृत्स्ततविषिः ||
sa hi ṣmā dhanvākṣitaṁ dātā na dāty ā paśuḥ | hiriśmaśruḥ śucidann ṛbhur anibhṛṣṭataviṣiḥ ||
  • sa hi ṣmā dhanvākṣitaṁ dātā na dāty ā paśuḥ
    • sa hi - "Indeed, he" or "truly he". This phrase emphasizes certainty and truth.
    • ṣmā - "in this world" or "in this context". It refers to the earthly realm or the material world.
    • dhanvākṣitaṁ - "the one who is skilled in archery" or "the archer". This symbolizes someone who is adept and focused, often representing a warrior or protector.
    • dātā - "the giver" or "the one who bestows". This term signifies generosity and the act of giving.
    • na dāty - "does not give" or "does not bestow". This indicates a refusal or inability to give.
    • ā paśuḥ - "like an animal" or "like cattle". This phrase suggests a comparison to those who are unthinking or lack awareness.
  • hiriśmaśruḥ śucidann ṛbhur anibhṛṣṭataviṣiḥ
    • hiriśmaśruḥ - "the one who is full of compassion" or "the compassionate one". This highlights the importance of empathy and kindness.
    • śucidann - "pure food" or "nourishing sustenance". This refers to the idea of purity in what we consume, both physically and spiritually.
    • ṛbhur - "the righteous" or "the virtuous". This term emphasizes moral integrity and ethical behavior.
    • anibhṛṣṭataviṣiḥ - "the one who is unshaken" or "the steadfast". This signifies resilience and stability in the face of challenges.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the qualities of a true leader or protector. It highlights the importance of being skilled and focused (like an archer), generous in giving, and compassionate towards others. The comparison to animals serves as a reminder to rise above mere instinct and thoughtlessness. The mantra also underscores the significance of purity in our actions and sustenance, advocating for a life of righteousness and steadfastness.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership roles, community service, and personal development. For instance, a leader in a community should embody these qualities by being compassionate, generous, and morally upright, ensuring that their actions benefit others and promote a harmonious society. In personal life, individuals can strive to be more aware and thoughtful in their interactions, focusing on ethical choices and nurturing relationships that reflect these values.
