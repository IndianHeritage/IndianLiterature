प्र णो देवī सरस्वती वाजेभिर वाजिनीवती |धीनाम अवित्री अवतु ||
pra ṇo devī sarasvatī vājebhir vājinīvatī | dhīnām avitry avatu ||
  • Mantra: "pra ṇo devī sarasvatī vājebhir vājinīvatī | dhīnām avitry avatu ||"
  • Translation: "May the goddess Saraswati, who is endowed with the power of speech and knowledge, protect our understanding and wisdom."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "for" or "towards."
    • ṇa: Refers to "breath" or "life force," symbolizing vitality.
    • devī: Means "goddess," indicating a divine feminine presence.
    • sarasvatī: The name of the goddess associated with knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • vājebhir: Translates to "with strength" or "with power," often associated with the ability to articulate and express thoughts.
    • vājinīvatī: Means "like a charioteer," symbolizing guidance and direction in one's pursuits.
    • dhīnām: Refers to "understanding" or "intellect," emphasizing the importance of mental clarity.
    • avitry: Means "to protect" or "to guard," indicating a plea for safeguarding knowledge.
    • avatu: A verb meaning "may it protect," reinforcing the desire for divine protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, students often recite this mantra before exams or important presentations, seeking Saraswati's blessings for clarity of thought and expression.
    • Musicians and artists may invoke this mantra to inspire creativity and enhance their skills, as Saraswati is the goddess of arts and knowledge.
    • In moments of decision-making or when facing challenges, individuals may reflect on this mantra to seek guidance and wisdom in their choices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and the divine guidance that comes from the goddess Saraswati.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, particularly in education, creativity, and decision-making, where clarity and understanding are essential.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with their inner wisdom and seek protection for their intellectual pursuits.
