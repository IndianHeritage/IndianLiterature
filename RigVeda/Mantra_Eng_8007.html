मन्त्र: इन्दुर अत्यो न वाजसृत् कणिक्रान्ति पवित्रा आ |यद अक्षार अति देवयुः ||
indur atyo na vājasṛt kanikranti pavitra ā | yad akṣār ati devayuḥ ||
  • Indur: This word translates to "the moon." In Vedic literature, the moon is often associated with calmness, nourishment, and the mind's reflective qualities.
  • Atto: This term means "beyond" or "above." It signifies a state that transcends ordinary experiences.
  • Na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the absence of something.
  • Vājasṛt: This word can be interpreted as "the one who brings strength" or "the one who provides nourishment." It is often associated with the divine forces that empower and sustain life.
  • Kanikranti: This term refers to "purity" or "sacredness." It denotes a state of being that is free from impurities and negativity.
  • Pavitra: Similar to kanikranti, pavitra means "pure" or "sacred." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings.
  • Ā: This is a preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or intention.
  • Yad: This word means "that" or "which." It is often used to refer to something specific or significant.
  • Akṣār: This term translates to "imperishable" or "eternal." It signifies something that is beyond the transient nature of the material world.
  • Ati: This means "beyond" or "exceeding." It indicates a state that surpasses normal limits.
  • Devayuḥ: This word refers to "divine beings" or "gods." It signifies the higher powers that govern the universe.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the divine forces to bring forth purity and strength, transcending the ordinary and reaching towards the eternal. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions while seeking strength from the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Mental Clarity: Just as the moon reflects light and brings calmness, individuals can seek mental clarity and peace through meditation and mindfulness practices.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of transcending ordinary experiences can inspire individuals to strive for personal growth and self-improvement, pushing beyond their limits.
  • Purity in Intentions: Maintaining purity in thoughts and actions is crucial in personal and professional relationships. This mantra serves as a reminder to act with integrity and sincerity.
  • Connection with the Divine: Seeking strength from divine forces can be a source of comfort and guidance during challenging times, encouraging individuals to trust in a higher power.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant meaning in promoting purity, strength, and transcendence. It encourages individuals to seek clarity and divine support in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the universe. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with grace and resilience.
