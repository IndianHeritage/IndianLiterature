त्वां हि मन्द्रतमम अर्कशोकैर ववृमहे महि नः श्रोष्य अग्ने | इन्द्रं न त्वा शवसा देवता वायुम पृणन्ति राधसाः नृतमाः ||
tvāṁ hi mandratamam arkaśokair vavṛmahe mahi naḥ śroṣy agne | indraṁ na tvā śavasā devatā vāyum pṛṇanti rādhasā nṛtamāḥ ||
  • tvāṁ hi mandratamam: This phrase translates to "We invoke you, the most powerful." Here, "tvāṁ" refers to the divine entity being addressed, often interpreted as Agni, the fire god. The term "mandratamam" signifies the supreme power and energy that this deity embodies.
  • arkaśokair vavṛmahe: This means "We sing praises with the light of the sun." "Arka" refers to light or radiance, while "śokair" implies joy or happiness. This line emphasizes the importance of light and positivity in our lives, suggesting that we should celebrate and acknowledge the divine presence through our actions and words.
  • mahi naḥ śroṣy agne: This translates to "May you listen to us, O Agni." Here, "mahi" means great or vast, and "śroṣy" indicates listening or paying attention. This line signifies the importance of communication with the divine, urging the deity to heed our prayers and intentions.
  • indraṁ na tvā śavasā devatā: This means "Indra, the divine being, does not overshadow you." "Indra" is another significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. This line suggests that while Indra is powerful, the divine entity being addressed (Agni) holds a unique and irreplaceable position.
  • vāyum pṛṇanti rādhasā nṛtamāḥ: This translates to "The winds are filled with the essence of truth." "Vāyum" refers to the wind, "pṛṇanti" means to fill, and "nṛtamāḥ" signifies truth or righteousness. This line highlights the idea that truth permeates the universe, carried by the winds, and emphasizes the importance of living a life grounded in honesty and integrity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energy, celebrating light and positivity, and emphasizing the importance of communication with the divine. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance and support from higher powers while also encouraging us to live truthfully and joyfully. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to connect with the divine, acknowledge their own power, and strive for a life filled with integrity and positivity. It can be applied in daily rituals, meditation practices, or moments of reflection, reinforcing the connection between the individual and the divine, and promoting a harmonious existence.
