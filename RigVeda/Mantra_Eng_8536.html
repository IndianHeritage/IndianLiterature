स नो देव देवताते पवस्व महे सोम प्सरस इन्द्रपानः |कृत्वन्न अपो वर्षयन् dyām उतेमाम उरोर ā नो वरिवस्यā पुनानः ||
sa no deva devatāte pavasva mahe soma psarasa indrapānaḥ | kṛṇvann apo varṣayan dyām utemām uror ā no varivasyā punānaḥ ||
  • sa no deva devatāte: This phrase translates to "May the divine beings (devas) bless us." It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers or divine entities. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people seek blessings before starting new ventures, such as a business opening or a wedding ceremony.
  • pavasva mahe soma: Here, "pavasva" means "purify" and "mahe soma" refers to the "great soma," which is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and wisdom. This can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in our lives, where individuals strive to purify their minds and spirits through education and self-improvement.
  • psarasa indrapānaḥ: This part can be interpreted as "the drink of Indra," where Indra is the king of the gods and symbolizes strength and power. In a modern context, this could represent the pursuit of strength and resilience in facing life's challenges, akin to athletes or leaders who draw upon their inner strength to overcome obstacles.
  • kṛṇvann apo varṣayan dyām: "Kṛṇvann" means "creating" or "making," "apo" refers to "water," and "varṣayan dyām" translates to "the sky that brings rain." This signifies the importance of nurturing and creating a harmonious environment. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation efforts where individuals and communities work together to create a sustainable future.
  • utemām uror ā no varivasyā punānaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "may the waters purify us and bring us prosperity." It highlights the significance of water as a life-giving force. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of clean water access for health and prosperity in communities, as well as the spiritual cleansing rituals found in many cultures.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine blessings, the pursuit of knowledge, strength, environmental harmony, and the essential nature of water in our lives. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek purity and strength while fostering a sustainable and prosperous environment. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work towards personal growth, environmental stewardship, and collective well-being, reflecting the timeless wisdom of the RigVeda in contemporary society.
