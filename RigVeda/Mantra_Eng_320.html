तां नो अद्य वनस्पति ऋष्वाव ऋष्वेभिः सोतृभिः |इन्द्राय मधुमत सुतम् ||
tā no adya vanaspatī ṛṣvāv ṛṣvebhiḥ sotṛbhiḥ | indrāya madhumat sutam ||
  • tā no adya vanaspatī: This phrase translates to "May that tree (or plant) be ours today." Here, "vanaspatī" refers to a tree or plant, symbolizing growth, nourishment, and stability. In real life, trees are essential for the environment, providing oxygen, shade, and resources.
  • ṛṣvāv ṛṣvebhiḥ sotṛbhiḥ: This part can be interpreted as "with the strong and powerful." The term "ṛṣvāv" refers to strength and power, while "sotṛbhiḥ" suggests support or assistance. This emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving strength. For example, in a workplace, teamwork can lead to greater success than individual efforts.
  • indrāya madhumat sutam: This translates to "the sweet offspring for Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the heavens. "Madhumat" means sweet or pleasant, indicating the desire for prosperity and abundance. In practical terms, this can relate to the blessings of good harvests or successful ventures, akin to how a farmer prays for rain to nourish crops.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking growth, strength, and prosperity through nature and community. It highlights the interconnectedness of life, where the well-being of one contributes to the well-being of all.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as environmental conservation efforts, community building, and personal growth. For instance, when individuals come together to plant trees, they not only contribute to the environment but also foster a sense of community and shared purpose.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nature, collaboration, and the pursuit of prosperity. It encourages individuals to seek strength in unity and to appreciate the resources provided by the earth, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
