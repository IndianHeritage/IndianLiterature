मन्त्र: सरस्वतीं देवयन्तो हवन्ते सरस्वतीं अध्वरे तायमाने |सरस्वतीं सुक्रतो अह्वयन्त सरस्वती दाशुषे वार्यं दात ||
sarasvatīṁ devayanto havante sarasvatīm adhvare tāyamāne | sarasvatīṁ sukṛto ahvayanta sarasvatī dāśuṣe vāryaṁ dāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sarasvatīm: Refers to the goddess Saraswati, the embodiment of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • Devayanto: Those who are divine or godly.
    • Havante: Invoke or call upon.
    • Adhvare: In the context of rituals or sacrifices.
    • Tāyamāne: While being invoked or called upon.
    • Sukṛto: Those who perform good deeds or righteous actions.
    • Ahvayanta: They call upon or invite.
    • Dāśuṣe: For the one who is worthy or deserving.
    • Vāryaṁ: Strength or power.
    • Dāt: To give or bestow.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Goddess Saraswati, emphasizing the importance of invoking her presence during rituals and ceremonies.
    • The invocation is not just for personal gain but is also a call for collective wisdom and knowledge, highlighting the role of the divine in guiding righteous actions.
    • It suggests that those who are engaged in good deeds should seek the blessings of Saraswati to enhance their capabilities and wisdom.
    • In real life, this can be seen in educational institutions where students and teachers often seek blessings for knowledge and success in their endeavors.
    • For example, before examinations, students may recite this mantra to invoke clarity of thought and wisdom, believing that the goddess will guide them in their studies.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in the pursuit of knowledge and righteousness.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom and learning are not solely human endeavors but are also influenced by divine grace.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as educational settings, artistic pursuits, and any situation where knowledge and wisdom are sought.
    • By invoking Saraswati, individuals can align themselves with higher principles and seek to contribute positively to society through their knowledge and actions.
