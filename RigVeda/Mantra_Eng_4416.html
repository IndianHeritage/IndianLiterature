प्र नव्यसाः सहसः सूनुमच्छा यज्ञेन गातुम अव इच्छमानः |वृश्चद्वनं कृष्णयामं रुशन्तं वीती होतारं दिव्यं जिगाति ||
pra navyasā sahasaḥ sūnum acchā yajñena gātum ava icchamānaḥ | vṛścadvanaṁ kṛṣṇayāmaṁ ruśantaṁ vītī hotāraṁ divyaṁ jigāti ||
  • pra navyasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with newness" or "with freshness." It signifies the importance of renewal and innovation in life.
  • sahasaḥ: This word means "swift" or "quick." It emphasizes the need for prompt action and responsiveness in our endeavors.
  • sūnum: Translated as "son" or "offspring," it represents the continuation of lineage and the importance of nurturing future generations.
  • acchā: This term means "good" or "well." It highlights the pursuit of goodness and virtue in our actions.
  • yajñena: This word refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It signifies the importance of selflessness and contributing to the greater good.
  • gātum: Meaning "to go" or "to move," it suggests progress and the journey of life.
  • ava icchamānaḥ: This phrase translates to "desiring to attain." It reflects the human aspiration to achieve goals and fulfill desires.
  • vṛścadvanaṁ: This term refers to "the forest of the scorpion," which can symbolize danger or challenges in life.
  • kṛṣṇayāmaṁ: This means "the black or dark time," indicating periods of difficulty or adversity.
  • ruśantaṁ: Translated as "roaring" or "fierce," it represents the intensity of challenges we face.
  • vītī: This word means "the one who is free" or "liberated," suggesting the importance of overcoming obstacles.
  • hotāraṁ: Referring to "the priest" or "the one who offers," it signifies the role of guidance and support in our lives.
  • divyaṁ: This term means "divine" or "heavenly," indicating the higher purpose or spiritual aspect of our actions.
  • jigāti: Meaning "to move" or "to progress," it emphasizes the continuous journey of life and the pursuit of higher goals.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life’s journey, emphasizing the importance of renewal, swift action, and the nurturing of future generations. It teaches us to pursue goodness through selfless actions and to face challenges with courage and determination. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and leadership. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and navigate the complexities of life with resilience and purpose.
