यं रक्षन्ति प्रचेतसो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः |नू चित स दभ्यते जनः ||
yaṁ rakṣanti pracetaso varuṇo mitro aryamā | nū cit sa dabhyate janaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ rakṣanti pracetaso varuṇo mitro aryamā | nū cit sa dabhyate janaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are protected by the wise, Varuna, Mitra, and Aryama, are never troubled by any fear."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yaṁ: who/that
    • rakṣanti: protect
    • pracetaso: wise or intelligent
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water
    • mitro: Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony
    • aryamā: Aryama, associated with the path of righteousness
    • nū: never
    • cit: troubled
    • sa: he/that
    • dabhyate: is afflicted
    • janaḥ: people or individuals
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and guidance.
    • Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order, suggesting that those who align themselves with righteousness are safeguarded.
    • Mitra symbolizes friendship and cooperation, indicating that social bonds and harmony contribute to one's safety.
    • Aryama signifies the path of virtue, reinforcing the idea that living a righteous life leads to protection from harm.
    • In real life, this can be seen in communities where mutual support and ethical living create a safe environment for individuals.
    • For example, in a workplace where colleagues support each other and adhere to ethical practices, employees often feel secure and valued.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the protective power of wisdom, friendship, and righteousness.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate these qualities in their lives to ensure safety and peace.
    • In a broader context, it highlights the significance of community and ethical living in fostering a secure and harmonious society.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges with confidence, knowing they are supported by divine forces and their community.
