पितुर न पुत्रः सुभृतो दुरोण आ देवां एतु प्र णो हविः ||
pitur na putraḥ subhṛto duroṇa ā devām̐ etu pra ṇo haviḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pitur na putraḥ subhṛto duroṇa ā devām̐ etu pra ṇo haviḥ"
  • Translation: "May the son of the father, who is well-nourished, approach the gods with our offerings."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pitur: of the father
    • na: not
    • putraḥ: son
    • subhṛto: well-nourished or well-cared for
    • duroṇa: strong or powerful
    • ā: towards
    • devām̐: the gods
    • etu: may come
    • pra: with
    • ṇo: our
    • haviḥ: offerings or sacrifices

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of the relationship between a father and son, highlighting the role of nourishment and care in fostering strength and virtue.
    • The phrase "well-nourished" suggests that physical and emotional support is crucial for the development of a child, which can be seen in real-life examples such as the impact of a nurturing environment on a child's education and emotional well-being.
    • The invocation of the gods signifies a call for divine blessings and guidance, which can be related to how individuals seek spiritual support in times of need, such as during family gatherings or significant life events.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in family settings where the bond between parents and children is celebrated, such as during festivals or rituals that honor familial relationships.
    • In modern contexts, it can serve as a reminder for parents to provide a nurturing environment that fosters the growth of their children, both physically and emotionally.
    • It can also be relevant in community gatherings where collective offerings are made to seek blessings for the well-being of families and society as a whole.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the nurturing role of parents and the importance of seeking divine blessings for the well-being of the family.
    • It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with parenthood and the need for a supportive environment for children to thrive.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the interconnectedness of family, community, and spirituality, encouraging individuals to honor these relationships through offerings and gratitude.
