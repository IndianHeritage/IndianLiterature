मन्त्र: असूत पूर्वो वृषभो ज्यायान इमा अस्य शुरुधः सन्ति पूर्वीः |दिवो नपाताऽविदथस्य धीभिः क्षत्रं राजानां प्रदिवो दधाथे ||
asūta pūrvo vṛṣabho jyāyān imā asya śurudhaḥ santi pūrvīḥ | divo napātā vidathasya dhībhiḥ kṣatraṁ rājānā pradivo dadhāthe ||
  • asūta pūrvo vṛṣabho: This phrase can be translated as "the ancient bull (or strong one) that was born." In this context, "bull" symbolizes strength and power, often associated with leadership and authority.
  • jyāyān imā asya śurudhaḥ santi pūrvīḥ: This translates to "these (powers) are greater than those who are strong." It suggests that there are forces or powers that surpass even the mightiest, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and strategy over brute strength.
  • divo napātā vidathasya dhībhiḥ: This means "the offspring of the sky, through the wisdom of the assembly." Here, "offspring of the sky" can be interpreted as divine or celestial influences, while "wisdom of the assembly" refers to collective intelligence and decision-making.
  • kṣatraṁ rājānā pradivo dadhāthe: This translates to "the kings establish the realm of power." It highlights the role of leaders in shaping and maintaining authority and governance.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the interplay between strength, wisdom, and leadership. It suggests that true power comes not just from physical might but also from intelligence, strategy, and the ability to unite people for a common purpose.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to leadership in various fields, such as politics, business, and community organization. For instance, a successful business leader may rely on both their strength in decision-making and the wisdom of their team to navigate challenges and achieve goals.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder that effective leadership requires a balance of strength and wisdom. In a world where challenges are complex and multifaceted, the ability to harness collective intelligence and make informed decisions is crucial for success.
