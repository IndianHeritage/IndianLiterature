मन्त्र: होताध्वर्युः आवयाऽग्निमिन्दो ग्रावग्राभा उता शन्ता सुविप्रः |तेन यज्ञेन स्वरंक्र्तेन स्विष्टेन वक्षाणाऽआ पृणध्वम् ||
hotādhvaryur āvayā agnimindho grāvagrābha uta śaṁstā suvipraḥ | tena yajñena svaraṁkṛtena sviṣṭena vakṣaṇā ā pṛṇadhvam ||
  • hotādhvaryur: This term refers to the priest who performs the rituals and offerings. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the role of a facilitator or mediator in any sacred or important task.
  • āvayā: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It emphasizes the act of seeking divine presence or assistance.
  • agnimindho: This translates to "O Agni, the divine fire." Agni is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing the fire that purifies and transforms offerings into spiritual energy.
  • grāvagrābha: This term can be understood as "the one who holds the community together." It signifies unity and collective effort in rituals or endeavors.
  • uta: This means "and" or "also," linking the various elements of the mantra together.
  • śaṁstā: This refers to the "guardian" or "protector," indicating the importance of safeguarding the community and its values.
  • suvipraḥ: This means "the wise one" or "the learned." It highlights the value of knowledge and wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • tena yajñena: This translates to "through that sacrifice." It emphasizes the significance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • svaraṁkṛtena: This means "by the sound or voice created." It signifies the power of words and chants in invoking spiritual energies.
  • sviṣṭena: This translates to "with the best" or "with the most auspicious." It indicates the importance of quality and intention in offerings.
  • vakṣaṇā: This means "with the heart" or "with sincerity." It emphasizes the need for genuine intention in all actions.
  • ā pṛṇadhvam: This translates to "may you be filled" or "may you be nourished." It signifies the desire for abundance and fulfillment in life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine presence through rituals, emphasizing the roles of community, wisdom, and sincerity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, family rituals, or any collective effort where unity and intention are paramount. The significance lies in recognizing the importance of collaboration, knowledge, and heartfelt offerings in achieving collective goals and spiritual fulfillment.
