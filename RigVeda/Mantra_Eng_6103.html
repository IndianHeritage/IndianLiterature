येबिष तिस्रः परावतो दिवो विश्वानि रोचनाः |त्रीम् अक्तून परिदीयथः ||
yebhis tisraḥ parāvato divo viśvāni rocanā | trīm̐r aktūn paridīyathaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yebhis tisraḥ parāvato divo viśvāni rocanā | trīm̐r aktūn paridīyathaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "By whom the three luminous (or radiant) realms of the sky are illuminated, may they bestow upon us the threefold wealth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yebhis: By whom
    • tisraḥ: Three
    • parāvato: Luminous or radiant
    • divo: Of the sky or heavens
    • viśvāni: All or universal
    • rocanā: Illuminations or radiances
    • trīm̐r: Threefold
    • aktūn: Wealth or abundance
    • paridīyathaḥ: May they bestow or grant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces that illuminate the three realms of existence, which can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
    • The "three luminous realms" can be seen as a metaphor for the different aspects of life that require illumination—knowledge, wisdom, and understanding.
    • In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education, personal growth, and spiritual enlightenment. For example, a student seeking knowledge in a university setting is metaphorically seeking the illumination of the mind.
    • The request for "threefold wealth" signifies not just material wealth but also wealth in terms of health, knowledge, and spiritual fulfillment. This can be seen in the context of holistic well-being, where one strives for balance in all aspects of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking illumination in all areas of life, encouraging individuals to pursue knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual growth.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to wealth, suggesting that true richness comes from a balanced life that nurtures the mind, body, and spirit.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by setting goals for personal development, engaging in lifelong learning, and fostering a sense of community and support among peers.
