अरायि काणे विकटे गिरिं गच्छ सदान्वे |शिरिम्बिठस्य सत्वभिस तेभिष ट्वा चातयामसि ||
arāyi kāṇe vikaṭe giriṁ gaccha sadānve | śirimbiṭhasya satvabhis tebhiṣ ṭvā cātayāmasi ||
  • arāyi: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who is in need" or "to the one who is seeking." It signifies a call or an appeal to a higher power or divine entity.
  • kāṇe: This translates to "ear" or "hearing." It emphasizes the importance of listening and being receptive to guidance or wisdom.
  • vikaṭe: This term means "to the one who is fierce" or "powerful." It can refer to a deity or a force that embodies strength and might.
  • giriṁ: This means "mountain" or "hill." In many cultures, mountains symbolize stability, strength, and a connection to the divine.
  • gaccha: This translates to "go" or "move towards." It indicates a direction or a journey, both physically and spiritually.
  • sadānve: This can be interpreted as "always" or "eternally." It suggests a continuous or unending aspect of the journey or the appeal being made.
  • śirimbiṭhasya: This word refers to "the one who is seated in glory" or "the one who is exalted." It denotes a state of honor and respect.
  • satvabhis: This translates to "with strength" or "with vitality." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • tebhiṣ: This means "with you" or "with them." It indicates a connection or a relationship with the divine or the powerful entity being addressed.
  • ṭvā: This translates to "you." It is a direct address to the divine or the powerful force.
  • cātayāmasi: This means "we are invoking" or "we are calling upon." It signifies an act of devotion or prayer.

In summary, this mantra is a powerful invocation directed towards a divine entity that embodies strength and glory. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and support from a higher power, especially in times of need. The imagery of mountains suggests stability and a strong foundation, while the call to listen and respond highlights the need for receptivity in our spiritual journeys.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in moments of personal crisis or when facing challenges, reminding individuals to seek strength and guidance from a higher power.
  • In a practical sense, it encourages people to listen to their inner voice and the wisdom of those around them, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • It serves as a reminder to remain resilient and steadfast, much like a mountain, in the face of adversity.
  • In daily life, one can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, creating a sense of connection with the divine and reinforcing the importance of inner strength.
