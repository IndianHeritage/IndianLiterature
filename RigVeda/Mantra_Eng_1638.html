मन्त्र: द्वे इद अस्य क्रमणे स्वदृशोऽभिख्याय मर्त्यो भुरण्यति | तृतीयम् अस्य नकीर आ दधर्षति वयश्च न पतयन्तः पतत्रिणः ||
dve id asya kramaṇe svardṛśo 'bhikhyāya martyo bhuraṇyati | tṛtīyam asya nakir ā dadharṣati vayaś cana patayantaḥ patatriṇaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dve id asya kramaṇe svardṛśo 'bhikhyāya martyo bhuraṇyati | tṛtīyam asya nakir ā dadharṣati vayaś cana patayantaḥ patatriṇaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "In this journey, there are two paths; the mortal being, endowed with divine vision, seeks the higher realms. The third path is not to be obstructed by those who are like birds in flight."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dve: two
    • id: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • kramaṇe: in the journey
    • svardṛśo: of the divine vision
    • 'abhikhyāya: seeking
    • martyo: the mortal
    • bhuraṇyati: strives or seeks
    • tṛtīyam: the third
    • asya: of this
    • nakir: not
    • ā: to
    • dadharṣati: obstruct
    • vayaś: those who are like
    • cana: and
    • patayantaḥ: flying
    • patatriṇaḥ: birds
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In life, individuals often face choices that can be likened to the two paths mentioned in the mantra. For instance, a student may choose between pursuing a stable job or following their passion for art. Each path represents a different journey with its own challenges and rewards.
    • The concept of 'divine vision' can be seen in leaders who possess foresight and wisdom, guiding their teams towards success. For example, a visionary entrepreneur who anticipates market trends and adapts accordingly embodies this quality.
    • The idea of the 'third path' suggests that there are alternative routes to success that should not be hindered. In a corporate setting, this could refer to innovative ideas that challenge the status quo, which should be encouraged rather than obstructed.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the choices we have in our journeys and the need for clarity of vision. It encourages individuals to seek higher understanding and not to let conventional barriers limit their potential.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as career decisions, personal growth, and creative pursuits. By embracing the two paths and remaining open to the third, individuals can navigate their lives with greater purpose and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that while the journey may present challenges, the pursuit of higher knowledge and the courage to explore uncharted territories can lead to profound growth and success.
