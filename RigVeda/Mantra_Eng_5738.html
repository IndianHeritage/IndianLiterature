उदु स्तोमासो अश्विनोर अबुध्राण जामि ब्रह्माण्य उषसश्च देविः |आविवासन रोडसी धिष्ण्ये मे अच्छा विप्रो नासत्याः विवक्ति ||
ud u stomāso aśvinor abudhrañ jāmi brahmāṇy uṣasaś ca devīḥ | āvivāsan rodasī dhiṣṇyeme acchā vipro nāsatyā vivakti ||
  • ud u stomāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "upward and outward praise." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence, which is essential in many cultures. For example, in a family gathering, when someone expresses appreciation for the food prepared, it creates a sense of unity and gratitude.
  • aśvinor: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life, such as joy and sorrow, or health and illness. In real life, we often encounter situations where we must balance these dualities, like managing work-life balance.
  • abudhrañ jāmi: This can be translated as "I know the unmanifest." It suggests an understanding of deeper truths beyond the physical realm. For instance, a scientist may understand the laws of physics but also appreciate the mysteries of the universe, reflecting a balance between knowledge and the unknown.
  • brahmāṇy uṣasaś ca devīḥ: This phrase refers to the divine energies of creation and dawn. It emphasizes the importance of new beginnings and the creative forces in life. In everyday life, this can be seen in the start of a new project or a new day, where hope and potential are abundant.
  • āvivāsan rodasī: This means "may the heavens reveal." It signifies the desire for enlightenment and understanding. In a practical sense, this can relate to moments of inspiration, such as when an artist suddenly finds clarity in their work or a student grasps a complex concept.
  • dhiṣṇyeme: This translates to "these abodes." It refers to the places or states of being where divine energies reside. In real life, this can be interpreted as the environments we create around us, such as a peaceful home or a supportive community that fosters growth and positivity.
  • acchā vipro: This phrase means "the good seer." It highlights the importance of wisdom and insight. In modern contexts, this can relate to leaders or mentors who guide others with their knowledge and experience, helping them navigate challenges.
  • nāsatyā vivakti: This can be understood as "the truth of the untruth." It suggests the importance of discerning reality from illusion. In everyday life, this is crucial in decision-making, where one must evaluate information critically to avoid deception.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of praise, duality, understanding, creativity, enlightenment, and discernment. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek balance in life, appreciate the divine forces at play, and strive for wisdom in our actions. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in personal growth, relationships, and professional endeavors, encouraging a holistic approach to living. By embodying these teachings, one can foster a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
