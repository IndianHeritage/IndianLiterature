अदितिर्द्यौः अदितिरन्तरिक्षम् अदितिर्माता स पिताऽस पुत्रः |विश्वे देवाः अदितिः पञ्च जनाः अदितिः जातम् अदितिः जनित्वम् ||
aditir dyaur aditir antarikṣam aditir mātā sa pitā sa putraḥ | viśve devā aditiḥ pañca janā aditir jātam aditir janitvam ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • Aditi: The term "Aditi" refers to the cosmic mother or the infinite, representing the source of all creation.
    • Dyaur: This means "the sky" or "heaven," symbolizing the vastness and the divine realm.
    • Antarikṣam: This refers to "the space" or "the atmosphere," indicating the intermediary realm between the earth and the heavens.
    • Mātā: This translates to "mother," emphasizing nurturing and protective qualities.
    • Pitā: Meaning "father," it represents authority, guidance, and support.
    • Putraḥ: This means "son," symbolizing the continuation of lineage and the future generation.
    • Viśve Devā: This phrase means "all the gods," indicating the collective divine forces at play.
    • Pañca Janā: This translates to "five beings," which can represent the five elements or the essential aspects of life.
    • Jātam: Meaning "born," it signifies the act of creation or manifestation.
    • Janitvam: This refers to "parenthood" or "the act of giving birth," emphasizing the nurturing aspect of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of Aditi can be seen in the role of mothers in families, who provide unconditional love and support, similar to how Aditi nurtures all beings.
    • The balance between Dyaur (heaven) and Antarikṣam (space) can be observed in the harmony between nature and human existence, where both realms must coexist for life to thrive.
    • In modern society, the roles of Mātā (mother) and Pitā (father) can be reflected in parenting styles that emphasize collaboration and shared responsibilities in raising children.
    • The idea of Viśve Devā (all gods) can be related to the diversity of cultures and religions, where each belief system contributes to the collective understanding of the divine.
    • Pañca Janā (five beings) can symbolize the importance of community and teamwork, where different individuals come together to create a harmonious society.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships within families and communities.
    • It highlights the roles of parents as both protectors and guides, fostering a sense of responsibility towards future generations.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate love, respect, and harmony in their relationships, recognizing the divine presence in everyday interactions.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the cosmic unity that binds all life, encouraging us to honor our roles within the larger tapestry of existence.
