अधā ह्य अग्न एषां सुवीर्यस्य मंहनā |तम इद यह्वं न रोदसी परि श्रवो बभूवतुः ||
adhā hy agna eṣāṁ suvīryasya maṁhanā | tam id yahvaṁ na rodasī pari śravo babhūvatuḥ ||
  • adhā - This word translates to "down" or "below." It signifies a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • hy - This is an emphatic particle, often used to emphasize the preceding word.
  • agna - Refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within.
  • eṣāṁ - This means "of these" or "of this," indicating a connection to the subject being discussed.
  • suvīryasya - Translates to "of the strong" or "of the valiant," referring to those who possess strength and valor.
  • maṁhanā - This word means "greatness" or "majesty," often associated with the qualities of strength and valor.
  • tam - This means "that," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • id - This translates to "this," indicating immediacy or presence.
  • yahvaṁ - This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," suggesting a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • na - This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • rodasī - Refers to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," symbolizing the duality of existence.
  • pari - This means "around" or "about," indicating a surrounding or encompassing quality.
  • śravo - This translates to "glory" or "fame," referring to the recognition of strength and valor.
  • babhūvatuḥ - This means "may it become" or "may it manifest," indicating a desire for realization or actualization.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, asking for the manifestation of strength and glory in the world. It emphasizes the importance of grounding oneself in the foundational aspects of life while invoking divine energy to achieve greatness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to cultivate their inner strength and valor, striving for greatness in their personal and professional lives.
  • Leadership: Leaders can invoke the qualities of Agni to inspire their teams, fostering an environment of strength, courage, and recognition of achievements.
  • Community Building: In community settings, this mantra can serve as a call to unite individuals in their shared strengths, promoting collective glory and success.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the invocation of divine energy to manifest strength and glory in various aspects of life. It serves as a reminder of the importance of grounding oneself while striving for greatness, making it applicable in personal growth, leadership, and community building.
