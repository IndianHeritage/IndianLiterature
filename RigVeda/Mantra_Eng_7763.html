मृजन्ति त्वा दश क्षिपो हिन्वन्ति सप्त धीतयः |अनु विप्राः अमादिषुः ||
mṛjanti tvā daśa kṣipo hinvanti sapta dhītayaḥ | anu viprā amādiṣuḥ ||
  • mṛjanti: This word translates to "they cleanse" or "they purify." It signifies the act of removing impurities or obstacles.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the divine or the higher consciousness that is being addressed in the mantra.
  • daśa: This translates to "ten," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle in various contexts.
  • kṣipo: This means "they throw" or "they cast away," indicating an action of discarding negativity or unwanted elements.
  • hinvanti: This translates to "they bring forth" or "they produce," suggesting the generation of positive outcomes or results.
  • sapta: This means "seven," often representing spiritual growth or the seven chakras in the human body.
  • dhītayaḥ: This translates to "intellects" or "understandings," referring to the wisdom or knowledge that is cultivated.
  • anu: This means "following" or "after," indicating a sequence or a process that unfolds.
  • viprā: This translates to "the wise" or "the learned," referring to those who possess knowledge and insight.
  • amādiṣuḥ: This means "in the beginning" or "from the start," suggesting a foundational aspect of wisdom and understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for purification and the generation of wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of discarding negativity and embracing knowledge, suggesting that through the process of cleansing and understanding, one can achieve a higher state of consciousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to cleanse their thoughts and emotions, letting go of negativity to foster personal development.
  • Education: In academic settings, it can symbolize the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom, encouraging students to pursue learning with dedication.
  • Meditation and Mindfulness: Practicing this mantra during meditation can help individuals focus on purifying their minds and cultivating a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on purification and the pursuit of wisdom. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals seeking to improve themselves and their understanding of life. By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can navigate challenges, foster personal growth, and contribute positively to their communities.
