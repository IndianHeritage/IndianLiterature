प्र सु विश्वान रक्षसो धक्ष्य अग्ने भव यज्ञानाम अभिशस्तिपावा |अथा वह सोमपतिं हरिभ्यां आतिथ्यम अस्मै चक्रमा सुदाव्ने ||
pra su viśvān rakṣaso dhakṣy agne bhavā yajñānām abhiśastipāvā | athā vaha somapatiṁ haribhyām ātithyam asmai cakṛmā sudāvne ||
  • pra su viśvān rakṣaso: This phrase can be translated as "May all beings be protected." Here, "pra" means "forth" or "outward," "su" means "good," "viśvān" refers to "all beings," and "rakṣaso" means "protection." In real life, this can be understood as a call for universal safety and well-being, akin to community efforts to ensure the safety of all individuals, such as neighborhood watch programs or global initiatives for peace.
  • dhakṣy agne bhavā: This translates to "O Agni, may you be favorable." "Agni" represents the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In modern terms, this can relate to the importance of harnessing energy responsibly, such as using renewable energy sources to foster a sustainable environment.
  • yajñānām abhiśastipāvā: This means "May you be the bestower of the fruits of sacrifices." "Yajñānām" refers to sacrifices or offerings, and "abhiśastipāvā" implies being the one who grants blessings. This can be likened to the idea of giving back to the community, where acts of charity and service lead to collective benefits, such as food drives or educational programs.
  • athā vaha somapatiṁ: This translates to "Now, we invoke the lord of the soma." "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and vitality. In contemporary life, this can be seen as a metaphor for seeking inspiration and creativity, much like how artists and innovators draw from their experiences to create impactful work.
  • haribhyām ātithyam asmai: This means "May we offer hospitality to him." "Haribhyām" refers to the divine, and "ātithyam" means hospitality. This reflects the importance of welcoming others and fostering connections, similar to how communities come together to support newcomers or during cultural festivals.
  • cakṛmā sudāvne: This translates to "We have made a delightful offering." "Cakṛmā" means "we have made," and "sudāvne" implies something that is pleasing or delightful. This can be related to the idea of sharing joy and positivity in our interactions, such as celebrating achievements or milestones with friends and family.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, favor, and hospitality in our lives. It calls for a collective effort to ensure the well-being of all beings while recognizing the importance of energy and creativity. In real-life scenarios, these themes can be applied through community service, environmental responsibility, and fostering connections among individuals. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a harmonious and supportive society, reflecting the essence of the mantra in our daily actions.
