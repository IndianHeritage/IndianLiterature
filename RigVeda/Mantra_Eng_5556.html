ते चिद्धि पूर्वीरभि सन्ति शाशा विश्वाम् आर्य उपरताति वान्वान् |इन्द्रो विभ्वाम् ऋभुक्षा वाजो आर्यः शत्रोर मिथत्याऽकृतवान् विनृम्णम् ||
te cid dhi pūrvīr abhi santi śāsā viśvām̐ arya uparatāti vanvan | indro vibhvām̐ ṛbhukṣā vājo aryaḥ śatror mithatyā kṛṇavan vi nṛmṇam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: intellect or understanding
    • pūrvīr: from the ancients or predecessors
    • abhi: towards
    • santi: peace or tranquility
    • śāsā: command or authority
    • viśvām̐: all or universal
    • arya: noble or honorable
    • uparatāti: to be restrained or controlled
    • vanvan: in the forest or wilderness
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • vibhvām̐: powerful or mighty
    • ṛbhukṣā: the one who provides or nourishes
    • vājo: strength or energy
    • aryaḥ: noble or honorable
    • śatror: of enemies
    • mithatyā: with falsehood or deceit
    • kṛṇavan: to create or establish
    • vi: in or among
    • nṛmṇam: mankind or human beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of wisdom and understanding derived from the teachings of our ancestors.
    • It emphasizes the need for peace and authority in our lives, suggesting that a noble character leads to universal harmony.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the power and strength that comes from righteous actions and the ability to overcome adversities.
    • It also warns against deceit and falsehood, indicating that true strength lies in honesty and integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of wisdom and integrity can foster a positive work environment, leading to greater productivity and employee satisfaction.
    • In personal relationships, practicing honesty and open communication can strengthen bonds and create a sense of trust and security.
    • In community service, individuals who act with noble intentions can inspire others to contribute positively, creating a ripple effect of goodwill.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the values of wisdom, integrity, and strength in overcoming challenges.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and just society.
