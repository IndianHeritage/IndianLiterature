मन्त्र: कद ध नूनं कधप्रियो यद इन्द्रं अजहातन |को वः सखित्व ओहते ||
kad dha nūnaṁ kadhapriyo yad indram ajahātana | ko vaḥ sakhitva ohate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • dha: indeed
    • nūnaṁ: certainly
    • kadhapriyo: who is dear
    • yad: that
    • indram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ajahātana: who has not abandoned
    • ko: who
    • vaḥ: to you
    • sakhitva: friendship
    • ohate: calls upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of rhetorical questions, reflecting on the nature of relationships and the essence of loyalty.
    • It questions when someone who is dear to us, like Indra, would abandon us, and who among our friends would truly call upon us in times of need.
    • The invocation of Indra symbolizes strength, power, and protection, suggesting that true friends are those who stand by us unwaveringly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can be reflected in the bonds of friendship where true friends support each other through thick and thin, much like how Indra is a protector.
    • In a professional setting, it can relate to colleagues who stand by each other during challenging projects, showcasing loyalty and teamwork.
    • In community settings, it emphasizes the importance of solidarity, where individuals come together to support one another in times of crisis, such as during natural disasters.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its exploration of loyalty and friendship, urging individuals to reflect on their relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish those who remain steadfast in our lives and to be that unwavering support for others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to cultivate meaningful connections and to recognize the value of true companionship, which is essential for personal and communal well-being.
