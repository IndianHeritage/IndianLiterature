युवां स मारुतो गणस त्वेषरथो अनेद्यः |शुभंयावाप्रतिष्कुतः ||
yuvā sa māruto gaṇas tveṣaratho anedyaḥ | śubhaṁyāvāpratiṣkutaḥ ||
  • yuvā: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and change. In real life, this can be seen in the enthusiasm of young people who drive innovation and progress in society.
  • sa: This means "he" or "that one." It refers to a specific entity or group, emphasizing the importance of unity and collective strength.
  • māruto: This term refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize power, strength, and the forces of nature. In a modern context, this can be likened to the forces of change in our lives, such as technological advancements or social movements.
  • gaṇas: This means "groups" or "hosts." It highlights the idea of community and collaboration. In today's world, this can be seen in teamwork and collective efforts in various fields, such as business, sports, and social activism.
  • tveṣaratho: This word can be broken down into "tve" (you) and "ṣaratha" (charioteer). It suggests guidance and direction. In real life, this can be related to mentors or leaders who guide individuals or groups towards their goals.
  • anedyaḥ: This translates to "unconquerable" or "invincible." It signifies resilience and strength in the face of challenges. In practical terms, this can be seen in individuals or communities that overcome adversity, such as those who recover from natural disasters or personal hardships.
  • śubhaṁ: This means "auspicious" or "good." It represents positive outcomes and blessings. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of happiness and well-being, as well as the importance of positive thinking and actions.
  • yāvā: This word means "as long as" or "until." It indicates a sense of continuity and perseverance. In real life, this can be seen in long-term goals and commitments, such as education, relationships, and career paths.
  • pratiṣkutaḥ: This translates to "established" or "set in place." It signifies stability and foundation. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of having a strong base in various aspects of life, such as financial security, education, and personal values.

Conclusion: The mantra "yuvā sa māruto gaṇas tveṣaratho anedyaḥ | śubhaṁyāvāpratiṣkutaḥ" encapsulates themes of youth, strength, community, guidance, resilience, positivity, continuity, and stability. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are part of a larger community, and through collaboration and guidance, we can overcome challenges and achieve auspicious outcomes. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their youthful energy, work together with others, seek guidance from mentors, and remain resilient in the face of adversity, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
