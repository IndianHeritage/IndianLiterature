अस्य यामासो बृहतो न वग्नून इन्धाना अग्नेः सख्युः शिवस्य |ईड्यस्य वृष्णो बृहतः स्वासो भामासो यामन्नक्तवश चिकीते ||
asya yāmāso bṛhato na vagnūn indhānā agneḥ sakhyuḥ śivasya | īḍyasya vṛṣṇo bṛhataḥ svāso bhāmāso yāmann aktavaś cikitre ||
  • Mantra: "asya yāmāso bṛhato na vagnūn indhānā agneḥ sakhyuḥ śivasya | īḍyasya vṛṣṇo bṛhataḥ svāso bhāmāso yāmann aktavaś cikitre ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni (the fire god) for blessings and prosperity. It speaks of the greatness of Agni and the importance of fire in rituals and daily life.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • yāmāso: of the seasons or time
    • bṛhato: of the great or vast
    • na: not
    • vagnūn: the fires
    • indhānā: kindled or ignited
    • agneḥ: of Agni
    • sakhyuḥ: of friends or companions
    • śivasya: of auspiciousness or well-being
    • īḍyasya: of the one who is worshipped
    • vṛṣṇo: of the strong or powerful
    • bṛhataḥ: of the great
    • svāso: of the breath or life
    • bhāmāso: of the shining or radiant
    • yāmann: who or which
    • aktavaś: who has been invoked
    • cikitre: has created or manifested
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni as a divine force that brings people together and fosters auspiciousness.
    • It highlights the role of fire in rituals, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where fire is central, such as in weddings, festivals, and other significant ceremonies.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire or a candle is a way to invoke blessings and create a sacred space, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During family gatherings or celebrations, the act of sharing food cooked over a fire can symbolize unity and the nurturing aspect of Agni.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony and auspiciousness in their lives, fostering connections with others and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in our lives, representing not just physical fire but also the warmth of relationships and the light of knowledge.
    • By invoking Agni, we seek to create a life filled with prosperity, friendship, and auspiciousness, applicable in both personal and communal settings.
