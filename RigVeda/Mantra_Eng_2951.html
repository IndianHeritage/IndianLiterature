समान्या वियुते दूरान्ते ध्रुवे पदे तस्थतुर जागरूके |उता स्वसारा युवती भवन्ती आदु ब्रुवाते मिथुनानि नाम ||
samānyā viyute dūreante dhruve pade tasthatur jāgarūke | uta svasārā yuvatī bhavantī ād u bruvāte mithunāni nāma ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of the unity and connection among beings, emphasizing the importance of relationships and the bonds that tie individuals together.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samānyā: commonality or unity
    • viyute: in separation or distance
    • dūreante: far away or distant
    • dhruve: stable or fixed
    • pade: in the place or position
    • tasthatur: they stand or exist
    • jāgarūke: awake or alert
    • uta: or
    • svasārā: sisters or female relatives
    • yuvatī: young women
    • bhavantī: they become or they are
    • ād: thus or in this way
    • u: and
    • bruvāte: they speak or express
    • mithunāni: pairs or couples
    • nāma: by name or called
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the bond between siblings (svasārā) is crucial for emotional support and shared experiences, reflecting the unity (samānyā) mentioned in the mantra.
    • In friendships, even when friends are physically distant (dūreante), their connection remains strong and stable (dhruve), showcasing the enduring nature of true relationships.
    • In a community, young women (yuvatī) often come together to support each other, forming a network of mutual aid and understanding, which aligns with the idea of expressing (bruvāte) shared experiences and challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of individuals, regardless of physical distance. It highlights the importance of relationships, whether familial, friendly, or communal. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to nurture their connections, remain supportive of one another, and recognize the value of unity in diversity. It serves as a reminder that even when apart, the bonds we share can keep us grounded and connected, fostering a sense of belonging and community.
