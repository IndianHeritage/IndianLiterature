यद एषां पृषती रथे प्रष्टर वहति रोहितः |यान्ति शुभ्रा रिणन्न अपः ||
yad eṣām pṛṣatī rathe praṣṭir vahati rohitaḥ | yānti śubhrā riṇann apaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • eṣām: of those
    • pṛṣatī: the one who is in the chariot
    • rathe: in the chariot
    • praṣṭir: the one who is the leader or the one who guides
    • vahati: carries or brings
    • rohitaḥ: the red one, often referring to the sun or a horse
    • yānti: they go or they move
    • śubhrā: auspicious or pure
    • riṇann: making a sound or resonating
    • apaḥ: waters or streams
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of life and the guiding forces that lead us.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the body or the vehicle of life, while the "leader" represents our intellect or consciousness guiding us.
    • The "red one" can be interpreted as the sun, symbolizing energy, vitality, and the source of life.
    • The "auspicious waters" signify the positive influences and opportunities that come our way as we navigate through life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a career context, the mantra can be seen as a reminder that our skills and knowledge (the leader) guide us through our professional journey (the chariot), while the opportunities (auspicious waters) we encounter can lead to success.
    • In personal relationships, the guiding force can be our values and principles, which help us navigate through challenges, while the positive experiences we share with others can be seen as the auspicious waters that enrich our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance and the positive forces in our lives.
    • It teaches us to recognize the role of our intellect and consciousness in steering our life’s journey.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek out and appreciate the auspicious opportunities that come our way, reminding us that we are not alone in our journey.
