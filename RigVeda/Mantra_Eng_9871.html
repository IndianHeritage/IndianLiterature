ध्रुवा एव वः पितरो युगे-युगे क्षेमकामासः सदसो न युञ्जते |अजुर्व्यासो हरिषाचो हरिद्रव आ द्यां रवेन पृथिवीम अशुश्रवुः ||
dhruvā eva vaḥ pitaro yuge-yuge kṣemakāmāsaḥ sadaso na yuñjate | ajuryāso hariṣāco haridrava ā dyāṁ raveṇa pṛthivīm aśuśravuḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, your ancestors (pitaro) are constant (dhruvā) across the ages (yuge-yuge), desiring well-being (kṣemakāmāsaḥ) in the assembly (sadaso) do not unite (na yuñjate). The immortal (ajuryāso) ones, shining (hariṣāco), golden (haridrava), have swiftly (aśuśravuḥ) traversed the earth (pṛthivīm) with the sun (raveṇa) in the sky (dyāṁ)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhruvā: Constant, unchanging, eternal.
    • pitaro: Ancestors, forefathers.
    • yuge-yuge: Across the ages, in every era.
    • kṣemakāmāsaḥ: Desiring well-being, seeking prosperity.
    • sadaso: In the assembly, in gatherings.
    • na yuñjate: Do not unite, do not come together.
    • ajuryāso: Immortal, eternal beings.
    • hariṣāco: Shining, radiant.
    • haridrava: Golden, precious.
    • aśuśravuḥ: Swiftly, quickly.
    • pṛthivīm: Earth, the physical realm.
    • raveṇa: With the sun, light.
    • dyāṁ: Sky, heavens.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of ancestors being constant can be seen in family traditions that are passed down through generations, such as festivals, rituals, and values that shape our identity.
    • The desire for well-being is reflected in modern society's focus on mental health and wellness, where individuals seek harmony and balance in their lives.
    • The idea of immortality and shining beings can be likened to the legacy of influential figures in history whose contributions continue to inspire future generations.
    • The imagery of the sun traversing the earth can be related to the daily cycle of life, where each day brings new opportunities and challenges, reminding us of the transient nature of existence.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the eternal connection we have with our ancestors and the importance of seeking well-being in our lives. It serves as a reminder that while we may not always unite in physical gatherings, the values and legacies of those who came before us continue to influence our paths. In real life, this mantra encourages us to honor our roots, strive for prosperity, and recognize the transient beauty of life, much like the sun that rises and sets each day, illuminating our journey on earth.
