ब्रह्म जिन्वतम उता जिन्वताṁ धियो हतं रक्षांसी सेधतम अमीवाः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च सोमं सुन्वतो अश्विनाः ||
brahma jinvatam uta jinvataṁ dhiyo hataṁ rakṣāṁsi sedhatam amīvāḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca somaṁ sunvato aśvinā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Brahma: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit.
    • Jinvatam: Those who are victorious or enlightened.
    • Uta: And also.
    • Jinvataṁ: Those who are living or thriving.
    • Dhiyo: Intellects or minds.
    • Hataṁ: Destroyed or removed.
    • Rakṣāṁsi: Protections or safeguards.
    • Sedhatam: May they be established or firm.
    • Amīvāḥ: Those who are not like others or unique.
    • Sajoṣasā: With joy or happiness.
    • Uṣasā: Dawn or new beginnings.
    • Sūryeṇa: With the sun.
    • Ca: And.
    • Somaṁ: The moon or nectar.
    • Sunvato: Those who are praised or honored.
    • Aśvinā: The divine twin horsemen, representing health and vitality.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It emphasizes the importance of intellect and the need to protect and establish our understanding and wisdom.
    • The mention of dawn and the sun symbolizes new beginnings and the illumination that knowledge brings.
    • The reference to the moon (Soma) suggests the nurturing aspect of knowledge, akin to how the moon nourishes the earth.
    • Aśvinā represents health and vitality, indicating that knowledge and enlightenment lead to a fulfilling life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a journey towards enlightenment, where students are encouraged to think critically and protect their understanding from misinformation.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to establish their own beliefs and values, akin to the establishment of "rakṣāṁsi" or protections for their intellect.
    • In community building, fostering a supportive environment for sharing knowledge can lead to collective growth and vitality, much like the blessings of Aśvinā.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge, protection of intellect, and the joy that comes with enlightenment.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from education to personal growth and community development.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom, protect their understanding, and embrace the new beginnings that knowledge brings.
