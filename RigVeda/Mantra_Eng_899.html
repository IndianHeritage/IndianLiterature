यो आर्यो मर्तभोजनं पराददाति दाशुषे |इन्द्रो अस्मभ्यं शिक्षतु वि भजा भूरी ते वसु भक्षीय तव राधसः ||
yo aryo martabhojanam parādadāti dāśuṣe | indro asmabhyaṁ śikṣatu vi bhajā bhūri te vasu bhakṣīya tava rādhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • aryo: noble or worthy
    • martabhojanam: the food of mortals
    • parādadāti: bestows or gives
    • dāśuṣe: to the deserving or the worthy
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • asmabhyaṁ: to us
    • śikṣatu: may he teach or instruct
    • vi: indeed
    • bhajā: may he distribute
    • bhūri: abundant or plentiful
    • te: your
    • vasu: wealth or good things
    • bhakṣīya: to be enjoyed or consumed
    • tava: your
    • rādhasaḥ: of the riches or treasures
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine, asking for the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, to bestow food and wealth upon the worthy.
    • The phrase "who noble bestows the food of mortals" emphasizes the importance of generosity and the sharing of resources with those who are deserving.
    • It highlights the role of divine guidance in ensuring that wealth and resources are distributed fairly and abundantly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in the practice of sharing food during festivals or communal gatherings, where everyone is invited to partake, ensuring that no one goes hungry.
    • In a corporate environment, leaders can embody this mantra by promoting a culture of inclusivity and support, where resources and opportunities are shared among employees, fostering a sense of community and collaboration.
    • In personal life, individuals can practice this mantra by engaging in charitable acts, such as donating food or resources to those in need, thereby embodying the spirit of generosity and support for the deserving.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance in the equitable distribution of resources and the importance of generosity.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just in material possessions but in the ability to share and uplift others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to act with compassion and responsibility, ensuring that everyone has access to the necessities of life.
