उप क्षत्रं पृञ्चीत हन्ति राजभिर भय चित सुख्सितिं दधे |नास्य वार्ता न तरुतां महाधने नार्भे अस्ति वज्रीणः ||
upa kṣatram pṛñcīta hanti rājabhir bhaye cit sukṣitiṁ dadhe | nāsya vartā na tarutā mahādhane nārbhe asti vajriṇaḥ ||
  • upa kṣatram: This phrase can be interpreted as "approaching the power" or "seeking strength." In real life, this can relate to individuals or communities seeking empowerment or support in times of need, such as during a crisis or when facing challenges.
  • pṛñcīta: This word means "to inquire" or "to ask." It emphasizes the importance of communication and seeking knowledge. For example, in a workplace, employees may need to ask questions to understand their roles better and improve productivity.
  • hanti rājabhir: This translates to "is harmed by kings." It suggests that authority figures or leaders can have a significant impact on the well-being of individuals. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how government policies affect citizens' lives, such as taxation or social welfare programs.
  • bhaye cit: This means "in fear of the mind." It highlights the psychological aspect of fear and its influence on decision-making. For instance, fear of failure can prevent individuals from pursuing their dreams or taking risks in their careers.
  • sukṣitiṁ dadhe: This phrase translates to "establishes prosperity." It signifies the importance of creating a foundation for success and well-being. In real life, this can be related to financial planning, education, and personal development, which are essential for achieving long-term goals.
  • nāsya vartā: This means "there is no existence." It suggests a state of absence or lack. In practical terms, this can refer to situations where resources or opportunities are not available, such as unemployment or lack of access to education.
  • na tarutā: This translates to "not by the tree." It can symbolize the idea that growth and support do not come from passive sources but require active effort. For example, individuals must take initiative in their careers rather than waiting for opportunities to come to them.
  • mahādhane: This means "in great wealth." It refers to abundance and prosperity. In a real-life context, this can relate to the importance of financial literacy and the responsible management of wealth to ensure stability and growth.
  • nārbhe asti vajriṇaḥ: This translates to "there is no strength of the thunderbolt." It implies a lack of power or resilience. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or communities feel powerless against larger forces, such as economic downturns or natural disasters.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength, communication, and the impact of leadership on individual well-being. It highlights the psychological aspects of fear and the necessity of establishing a foundation for prosperity. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, community empowerment, and the importance of proactive efforts in achieving success. Understanding this mantra can inspire individuals to take charge of their lives, seek knowledge, and work towards creating a prosperous future despite challenges.
