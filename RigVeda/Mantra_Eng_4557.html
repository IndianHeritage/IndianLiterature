तं उष्ठुहि यो अभिभूत्योजावन्वन्न अवातः पुरुहूत इन्द्रः |अशाल्हम् उग्रं सहमानम् आभिर गीरभिर वर्ध वृषभं चर्षणीनाम् ||
tam u ṣṭuhi yo abhibhūtyojā vanvann avātaḥ puruhūta indraḥ | aṣāḻham ugraṁ sahamānam ābhir gīrbhir vardha vṛṣabhaṁ carṣaṇīnām ||
  • tam u ṣṭuhi: This phrase can be translated as "to him, we offer our praise." It signifies the act of devotion and recognition of a higher power.
  • yo abhibhūtyojā: This means "who is the one that overcomes obstacles." It refers to a divine force that helps individuals triumph over challenges in life.
  • vanvann avātaḥ puruhūta indraḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who moves through the forests, the great Indra." Indra is often seen as a god of rain and thunder, symbolizing strength and power in nature.
  • aṣāḻham ugraṁ: This translates to "the one who is fierce and powerful." It emphasizes the might and intensity of the divine force being addressed.
  • sahamānam ābhir: This means "who is supported by many." It suggests that this divine entity is not alone but is backed by a multitude of forces or followers.
  • gīrbhir vardha: This translates to "may our songs increase." It indicates the importance of praise and worship in enhancing the connection with the divine.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ carṣaṇīnām: This means "the bull among the singers." The bull symbolizes strength and fertility, representing the power of the divine in the context of creation and sustenance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of devotion, strength, and overcoming obstacles through divine support. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as facing personal challenges, seeking guidance in difficult times, or striving for success in endeavors. The mantra serves as a reminder that through praise and connection with a higher power, individuals can find the strength to overcome adversity and achieve their goals. It highlights the significance of community and collective support in the journey of life, encouraging individuals to come together in worship and recognition of the divine forces that guide and protect them.
