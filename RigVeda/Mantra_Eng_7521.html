ये सोमासः परावती ये अर्वावती सुन्विरे |सर्वांँस तांँ इन्द्र गच्छसि ||
ye somāsaḥ parāvati ye arvāvati sunvire | sarvām̐s tām̐ indra gacchasi ||
  • Translation: "Those who are the drinkers of Soma, who are exalted, who are the ones who bring forth the sun, all of them, O Indra, you go to them."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye: Those who
    • somāsaḥ: drinkers of Soma (a sacred ritual drink)
    • parāvati: exalted or elevated
    • ye: who
    • arvāvati: who bring forth or produce
    • sunvire: the sun or light
    • sarvām̐s: all of them
    • tām̐: those
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • gacchasi: you go to

  • Contextual Significance:
    • The mantra invokes Indra, a prominent deity in Vedic literature, known for his strength and leadership.
    • Soma is not just a drink but symbolizes divine inspiration, ecstasy, and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • The exalted drinkers of Soma represent those who seek enlightenment and higher consciousness.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, the "drinkers of Soma" can be likened to individuals who pursue knowledge, creativity, and spiritual growth, such as artists, philosophers, and spiritual seekers.
    • Just as the sun is essential for life, the pursuit of enlightenment and wisdom is crucial for personal and societal development.
    • Communities that foster learning and creativity can be seen as modern-day equivalents of those who "bring forth the sun," illuminating the path for others.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher knowledge and enlightenment.
    • It encourages individuals to aspire towards greatness and to connect with the divine through their pursuits.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to engage in activities that elevate our consciousness and contribute positively to society.
