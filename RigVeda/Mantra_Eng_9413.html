स īṁ वृषा न फेनम् अस्यद आजौ स्मद आ परैद अप दभ्रचेताः |सरत पदा न दक्षिणा परावृङ न ता नु मे पृषण्यो जगृभ्रे ||
sa īṁ vṛṣā na phenam asyad ājau smad ā paraid apa dabhracetāḥ | sarat padā na dakṣiṇā parāvṛṅ na tā nu me pṛśanyo jagṛbhre ||
  • sa īṁ vṛṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is strong" or "the powerful one." In real life, this can refer to individuals who exhibit strength in various forms, such as physical strength, mental resilience, or moral integrity. For example, a leader who stands firm in the face of adversity embodies this strength.
  • na phenam asyad: This translates to "do not let the foam of this (life) arise." The foam can symbolize distractions or superficial aspects of life that can cloud our judgment. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of focusing on what truly matters, such as relationships and personal growth, rather than getting caught up in materialism or fleeting pleasures.
  • ājau smad ā paraid: This can be understood as "may we be protected from harm." This highlights the human desire for safety and security. In everyday life, this can be seen in the actions of parents who strive to create a safe environment for their children or communities that come together to support one another during crises.
  • apa dabhracetāḥ: This phrase suggests "those who are wise and discerning." Wisdom is a quality that is highly valued in society. For instance, a mentor who guides young professionals in their careers exemplifies this wisdom, helping them navigate challenges and make informed decisions.
  • sarat padā: This means "on the path of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of following a moral and ethical path in life. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who choose to act with integrity, such as whistleblowers who expose wrongdoing despite potential personal consequences.
  • na dakṣiṇā parāvṛṅ: This translates to "do not turn away from the right path." It serves as a reminder to stay committed to one's values and principles. For example, a student who resists peer pressure to cheat on an exam demonstrates this commitment to integrity.
  • na tā nu me pṛśanyo jagṛbhre: This can be interpreted as "may they not take away my strength." This reflects the human desire to maintain one's power and agency. In a real-life context, this can relate to individuals who advocate for their rights and stand up against oppression, ensuring that their voices are heard.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, wisdom, moral integrity, and the pursuit of righteousness. Its significance lies in the encouragement to focus on what truly matters in life, to seek protection from harm, and to remain steadfast in one's values. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, fostering resilience, ethical behavior, and a sense of community. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, one can navigate the complexities of life with purpose and clarity.
