भजन्त विश्वे देवत्वं नाम ऋतं सपन्तो अमृतम् एविः ||
bhajanta viśve devatvaṁ nāma ṛtaṁ sapanto amṛtam evaiḥ ||
  • bhajanta: This word translates to "worship" or "serve." It signifies the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • viśve: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity, suggesting that the worship is directed towards all deities or aspects of divinity.
  • devatvaṁ: This refers to "divinity" or "the nature of the gods." It emphasizes the sacredness and spiritual essence of the divine beings being worshipped.
  • nāma: This translates to "name." In many spiritual traditions, the name of a deity holds significant power and meaning, representing their qualities and attributes.
  • ṛtaṁ: This word means "truth" or "cosmic order." It signifies the underlying principle of harmony and righteousness in the universe.
  • sapanto: This can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "intelligent." It refers to individuals who understand and appreciate the deeper truths of existence.
  • amṛtam: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting nature of the divine and the ultimate truth.
  • evaiḥ: This word means "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statements made in the mantra.

The mantra "bhajanta viśve devatvaṁ nāma ṛtaṁ sapanto amṛtam evaiḥ" encapsulates a profound spiritual message. It speaks to the importance of worshipping all forms of divinity, recognizing the universal nature of the divine, and understanding the truth that underlies existence. The wise, who are aware of this truth, attain immortality through their devotion and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Inclusivity in Spirituality: It encourages individuals to embrace a broad perspective on spirituality, recognizing that different paths can lead to the same ultimate truth.
  • Devotion and Service: It highlights the importance of serving others and the divine, fostering a sense of community and compassion.
  • Seeking Truth: The mantra inspires individuals to seek deeper truths in their lives, promoting wisdom and understanding over superficial beliefs.
  • Connection with the Eternal: It reminds us of the eternal nature of the soul and the divine, encouraging a focus on spiritual growth and enlightenment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine in various forms. It encourages a life of devotion, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth, which can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
