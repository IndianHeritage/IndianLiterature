मां नो देवानां विशः प्रasnातीर इवोश्राः |कृषं न हासुर अग्न्याः ||
mā no devānāṁ viśaḥ prasnātīr ivosrāḥ | kṛśaṁ na hāsur aghnyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • no: our
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • viśaḥ: of the community or society
    • prasnātīr: questions or inquiries
    • iva: like
    • osrāḥ: streams or flows
    • kṛśaṁ: weak or feeble
    • na: not
    • hāsur: to laugh or mock
    • aghnyāḥ: those who are not to be harmed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection and understanding from the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the need for divine guidance in addressing questions and challenges.
    • The phrase "not to mock the weak" suggests a call for compassion and support for those who are vulnerable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when team members face challenges. Instead of mocking or belittling those who struggle, a supportive environment can be fostered.
    • In community service, this mantra encourages individuals to uplift the weak and vulnerable, ensuring that no one is left behind.
    • In educational institutions, it serves as a reminder for students to support their peers, especially those who may be struggling academically or socially.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion, understanding, and support within society.
    • It highlights the role of divine guidance in navigating life's challenges and encourages a collective approach to problem-solving.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to treat others with kindness and to seek help from the divine when faced with difficulties.
