स हि वेदाः वसुधितिम् महाम् āरोधनं दिवः |स देवाम् एष वक्षति ||
sa hi vedā vasudhitim mahām̐ ārodhanaṁ divaḥ | sa devām̐ eha vakṣati ||
  • sa hi vedā: This phrase translates to "indeed, the Vedas." The Vedas are the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism, containing hymns, philosophy, and guidance for rituals.
  • vasudhitim: This word means "the earth" or "the world." It signifies the physical realm we inhabit, as well as the interconnectedness of all beings within it.
  • mahām̐ ārodhanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "great elevation" or "high ascent." It suggests a spiritual or moral upliftment, indicating the importance of striving for higher ideals.
  • divaḥ: This word means "of the heavens" or "of the divine." It represents the spiritual realm, the ultimate truth, or the divine consciousness.
  • sa devām̐: This translates to "that deity" or "those divine beings." It refers to the gods or higher powers that guide and protect humanity.
  • eha vakṣati: This means "speaks here" or "proclaims here." It indicates the communication of divine wisdom or knowledge in the present moment.

The mantra can be understood as a call to recognize the wisdom of the Vedas, which guide us in our earthly existence and encourage us to strive for spiritual elevation. It emphasizes the connection between the material world and the divine, suggesting that through understanding and following the teachings of the Vedas, we can achieve a higher state of being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use the teachings of the Vedas to enhance their moral and ethical standards, leading to personal development and a more fulfilling life.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "vasudhitim" reminds us of our responsibility towards the earth. It encourages sustainable living and respect for nature.
  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in meditation, yoga, or rituals based on Vedic teachings can help individuals connect with the divine and elevate their consciousness.
  • Community and Society: The mantra promotes the idea of collective upliftment, urging communities to come together for the common good, fostering harmony and cooperation.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the profound wisdom contained in the Vedas and its relevance in our daily lives. It encourages us to seek higher truths, respect our environment, and strive for personal and collective growth, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
