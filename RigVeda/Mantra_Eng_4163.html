गवाम् इव श्रीयसे श्रिङ्गम् उत्तमं सूर्यो न चक्षू रजसो विसर्जने |अत्याऽ इव सुभवश्चारवः स्थान मर्याऽ इव श्रीयसे चेतथा नरः ||
gavām iva śriyase śṛṅgam uttamaṁ sūryo na cakṣū rajaso visarjane | atyā iva subhva1ś cāravaḥ sthana maryā iva śriyase cetathā naraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "gavām iva śriyase śṛṅgam uttamaṁ sūryo na cakṣū rajaso visarjane | atyā iva subhvaś cāravaḥ sthana maryā iva śriyase cetathā naraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • gavām: of cows; symbolizes wealth and prosperity.
    • iva: like; a comparative term indicating similarity.
    • śriyase: for glory or wealth; refers to the abundance and richness.
    • śṛṅgam: peak or summit; denotes the highest point or excellence.
    • uttamaṁ: the best; signifies superiority.
    • sūryo: the sun; represents light, knowledge, and life.
    • na: not; negation indicating absence.
    • cakṣū: eyes; refers to perception or vision.
    • rajaso: of passion; relates to desires and material attachments.
    • visarjane: in the act of letting go; signifies release or renunciation.
    • atyā: beyond; indicates transcendence.
    • subhvaś: auspicious; denotes something favorable or good.
    • cāravaḥ: the one who moves; refers to the dynamic aspect of life.
    • sthana: place or position; indicates stability or grounding.
    • maryā: boundary or limit; signifies discipline or restraint.
    • cetathā: thus; indicates a conclusion or result.
    • naraḥ: man; refers to human beings.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the ideal qualities of a person, comparing them to the best attributes of cows and the sun.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wealth and glory, akin to the peak of excellence.
    • The mantra suggests that one should not be blinded by desires and passions, but rather strive for a higher understanding and purpose.
    • It highlights the significance of being grounded and disciplined while also being dynamic and adaptable in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A successful entrepreneur who builds a thriving business (wealth) but remains humble and grounded (sthana) in their values.
    • A student who excels academically (uttamaṁ) while also engaging in community service, showing a balance between personal achievement and social responsibility.
    • A leader who inspires others through vision (sūryo) and clarity, avoiding the pitfalls of greed and selfishness (rajaso).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities that lead to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek excellence while remaining aware of their desires and the impact of their actions on others.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, better relationships, and a positive contribution to society.
