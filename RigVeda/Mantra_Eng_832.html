ऋतस्य हि धेनवो वावशानाः स्मदूद्नीः पीपयन्त द्युभक्ताः |परावतः सुमतिम भिक्षमाणā वि सिन्धवः समया सस्रुर अद्रिम ||
ṛtasya hi dhenavo vāvaśānāḥ smadūdhnīḥ pīpayanta dyubhaktāḥ | parāvataḥ sumatim bhikṣamāṇā vi sindhavaḥ samayā sasrur adrim ||
  • ṛtasya: This word refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." In real life, it can be related to the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions.
  • hi: This is an emphatic particle, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the truth of what follows.
  • dhenavo: This term means "cows" or "nourishing entities." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in many cultures, representing the importance of nurturing and providing for others.
  • vāvaśānāḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are endowed" or "those who possess." It signifies the blessings and resources one has in life.
  • smadūdhnīḥ: This word means "those who are engaged in the act of giving" or "generous." It highlights the virtue of generosity and sharing with others.
  • pīpayanta: This term translates to "nourishing" or "sustaining." It reflects the idea of providing support and care to others, akin to how a mother nourishes her child.
  • dyubhaktāḥ: This means "those who partake of the sky" or "those who are connected to the divine." It suggests a connection to higher ideals and spiritual aspirations.
  • parāvataḥ: This word means "from above" or "higher realms." It indicates the influence of higher powers or divine forces in our lives.
  • sumatim: This translates to "good understanding" or "wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in making decisions and navigating life.
  • bhikṣamāṇā: This means "seeking alms" or "requesting." It reflects humility and the recognition of our needs and the needs of others.
  • vi sindhavaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the rivers" or "the flowing waters." Rivers symbolize the flow of life, change, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • samayā: This means "in time" or "at the right moment." It signifies the importance of timing in our actions and decisions.
  • sasrur: This translates to "they have flowed" or "they have moved." It indicates the dynamic nature of life and the inevitability of change.
  • adrim: This word means "the mountain" or "the firm ground." It symbolizes stability and strength in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, generosity, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us to be nurturing and supportive, to seek understanding, and to recognize the divine influence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering community support, practicing honesty in relationships, and making wise decisions that benefit not only ourselves but also those around us. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a harmonious and balanced existence, much like the cosmic order that the RigVeda speaks of.
