बृहस्पतिर्नः परि पातु पश्चादुतोत्तरस्मादधरादघायोः | इन्द्रः पुरस्तादुत मध्यतो नः सखा सकिभ्यो वरिवः कृणोतु ||
bṛhaspatir naḥ pari pātu paścād utottarasmād adharād aghāyoḥ | indraḥ purastād uta madhyato naḥ sakhā sakhibhyo varivaḥ kṛṇotu ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspatir naḥ pari pātu paścād utottarasmād adharād aghāyoḥ | indraḥ purastād uta madhyato naḥ sakhā sakhibhyo varivaḥ kṛṇotu ||"
  • Translation: "May Brihaspati protect us from behind, from above, and from below, from all evils. May Indra be in front of us and in the middle, and may he grant us friendship and prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Brihaspati: The deity representing wisdom, knowledge, and eloquence.
    • naḥ: Our.
    • pari pātu: Protect us from all sides.
    • paścād: From behind.
    • utottarasmād: From above.
    • adharād: From below.
    • aghāyoḥ: From all evils or obstacles.
    • Indraḥ: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • purastād: In front of us.
    • uta: And.
    • madhyato: In the middle.
    • sakhā: Friend.
    • sakhibhyo: To friends.
    • varivaḥ: Grant or bestow.
    • kṛṇotu: May he make or create.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, invoking the protection of Brihaspati can symbolize seeking wisdom and guidance to navigate through difficulties.
    • When facing personal challenges, such as health issues or relationship problems, this mantra can be a reminder to seek support from friends and loved ones, just as Indra is asked to be present in all aspects of life.
    • In educational settings, students may recite this mantra before exams to invoke the blessings of knowledge and protection from failure.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to protection and support. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, friendship, and divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, and educational pursuits. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and encouragement, fostering resilience and positivity in their lives.
