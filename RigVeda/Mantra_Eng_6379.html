इन्द्रो ब्रह्मेन्द्र ऋषिः इन्द्रः पुरू पुरूहूतः |महान महीभिः शचीभिः ||
indro brahmendra ṛṣir indraḥ purū puruhūtaḥ | mahān mahībhiḥ śacībhiḥ ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Brahmendra: This term combines "Brahma," representing the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, with "Indra," suggesting a divine authority that governs the universe.
  • Ṛṣir: Means sage or seer, indicating a person with profound wisdom and insight, often associated with spiritual knowledge.
  • Indraḥ: A repetition of Indra, emphasizing his importance and multifaceted nature.
  • Purū: Translates to "many" or "abundant," suggesting a richness in qualities or attributes.
  • Puruhūtaḥ: Refers to one who is invoked or called upon, indicating the importance of prayer and supplication in seeking divine assistance.
  • Mahān: Means "great" or "mighty," highlighting the immense power and significance of the divine.
  • Mahībhiḥ: Refers to the earth or the material world, suggesting a connection between the divine and the physical realm.
  • Śacībhiḥ: Refers to the divine powers or energies, often associated with the goddess Śachi, Indra's consort, symbolizing strength and capability.

  • Meaning: This mantra invokes the powerful presence of Indra, the king of gods, and acknowledges the wisdom of sages. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, highlighting the importance of invoking divine powers for strength and guidance.
  • Real-Life Example: In times of difficulty or uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, invoking higher powers for strength and clarity. For instance, during a challenging project at work, a person might reflect on their goals and seek inspiration from their inner wisdom, akin to the invocation of Indra and the sages.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, the wise, and the material world. It teaches us to seek strength and guidance from higher powers while recognizing our own inner wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience and seek support during challenging times, reinforcing the belief that divine assistance is available to those who earnestly seek it.
