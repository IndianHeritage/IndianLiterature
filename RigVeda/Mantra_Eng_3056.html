त्वं नो अग्नि वरुणस्य विद्वान् देवस्य हेळोऽव यासिसीष्ठाः |यजिष्ठो वह्नितमः शोशुचानो विश्वा द्वेषांसी प्र मुमुग्ध्य अस्मत् ||
tvaṁ no agne varuṇasya vidvān devasya heḻo 'va yāsisīṣṭhāḥ | yajiṣṭho vahnitamaḥ śośucāno viśvā dveṣāṁsi pra mumugdhy asmat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • no: for us
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • vidvān: knowledgeable or wise
    • devasya: of the divine
    • heḻo: in the form of light
    • 'va: or
    • yāsisīṣṭhāḥ: those who are most excellent
    • yajiṣṭho: the most worshipful
    • vahnitamaḥ: the best of the fire
    • śośucāno: purifying
    • viśvā: all
    • dveṣāṁsi: enmities
    • pra: away
    • mumugdhy: may you remove
    • asmat: from us
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, and Varuna, the god of cosmic order, seeking their wisdom and purity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine knowledge and the removal of enmities from our lives.
    • The invocation of Agni and Varuna signifies the need for guidance and protection in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek guidance and wisdom to make informed decisions. This mantra can be likened to a call for ethical leadership and clarity in vision.
    • In personal relationships, the desire to remove enmities and foster understanding can be seen as a practical application of this mantra, promoting harmony and peace.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may invoke divine blessings for wisdom and purity, similar to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine wisdom and the purification of relationships.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional integrity, reminding us of the importance of seeking guidance and fostering harmony.
    • By invoking the qualities of Agni and Varuna, we align ourselves with higher principles, promoting a life of clarity, purpose, and peace.
