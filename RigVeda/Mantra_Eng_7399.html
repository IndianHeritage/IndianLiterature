तुरीयं नाम यज्ञियं यदा करस तद उष्मसि |आदित पतिर न ओहसे ||
turīyaṁ nāma yajñiyaṁ yadā karas tad uśmasi | ād it patir na ohase ||
  • Mantra: "turīyaṁ nāma yajñiyaṁ yadā karas tad uśmasi | ād it patir na ohase ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • turīyaṁ: Refers to the fourth state of consciousness, beyond waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.
    • nāma: Means 'name' or 'that which is called.'
    • yajñiyaṁ: Pertains to sacrifice or offerings, often in the context of rituals.
    • yadā: Means 'when' or 'at the time of.'
    • karas: Refers to actions or deeds.
    • tad: Means 'that.'
    • uśmasi: Refers to warmth or energy, often associated with the sun.
    • ād: Means 'from' or 'out of.'
    • it: Refers to 'that' or 'this.'
    • patir: Means 'lord' or 'master.'
    • na: Means 'not.'
    • ohase: Refers to 'to be praised' or 'to be honored.'
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the fourth state of consciousness, which is a state of pure awareness and transcendence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of actions performed in a sacrificial manner, suggesting that such actions lead to a higher understanding or realization.
    • The reference to warmth or energy indicates the transformative power of these actions, akin to the sun's ability to nurture and sustain life.
    • It also highlights the idea that the ultimate lord or master does not seek praise or recognition, suggesting humility in the pursuit of spiritual goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In meditation practices, individuals often seek to reach a state of pure awareness, akin to the 'turīyaṁ' state, where they transcend their thoughts and emotions.
    • In daily life, performing actions selflessly, such as volunteering or helping others without expecting anything in return, embodies the essence of 'yajñiyaṁ.'
    • Just as the sun provides warmth and energy, individuals can bring positivity and support to their communities through their actions.
    • Humility in leadership, where leaders serve their teams without seeking accolades, reflects the idea of 'patir na ohase.'
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of transcending ordinary consciousness through selfless actions and rituals.
    • It teaches that true fulfillment comes from serving others and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, community well-being, and a deeper understanding of one's purpose.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to act with intention and humility, fostering a more harmonious existence.
