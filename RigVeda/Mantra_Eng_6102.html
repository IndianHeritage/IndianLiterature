आ नः स्तोमम् उप द्रवत तुयं श्येनेभिर आशुभिः |यातम् अश्वेभिर अश्विना ||
ā naḥ stomam upa dravat tūyaṁ śyenebhir āśubhiḥ | yātam aśvebhir aśvinā ||
  • Transliteration: ā naḥ stomam upa dravat tūyaṁ śyenebhir āśubhiḥ | yātam aśvebhir aśvinā ||
  • Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "May our" - This phrase indicates a collective desire or prayer for the community or group.
    • stomam: "hymn" or "praise" - Refers to the act of offering praise or a hymn, often to deities or forces of nature.
    • upa dravat: "flow towards us" - A request for blessings or positive energies to come towards the supplicants.
    • tūyaṁ: "purity" or "sacredness" - This signifies the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and offerings.
    • śyenebhir: "with the swiftness of the hawks" - Hawks are often seen as symbols of speed and precision, suggesting a desire for swift and effective blessings.
    • āśubhiḥ: "from the inauspicious" - A plea to be protected from negative influences or misfortunes.
    • yātam: "may we go" - A call to action, indicating a journey or movement towards a goal.
    • aśvebhir: "with the horses" - Horses symbolize strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with success and progress.
    • aśvinā: "O Ashvins" - The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and protective qualities, often invoked for assistance and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for a successful event, seeking purity and protection from negativity.
    • During a sports event, athletes might recite this mantra to channel the swiftness and strength of horses and hawks, aiming for victory and success.
    • In personal rituals, individuals may use this mantra to seek guidance and support from the divine, especially when embarking on new ventures or facing challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, purity, and divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving goals.
    • Its significance lies in the collective aspiration for positive energies and protection from negativity, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios such as community events, personal challenges, and competitive situations.
    • By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins and embodying the qualities of swiftness and strength, individuals and communities can strive for success and well-being in their endeavors.
