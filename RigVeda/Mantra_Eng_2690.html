अजीजनन्न अमृतं मार्त्यासोऽस्रेमाणं तरणिं वीळुजम्भम् |दश स्वसारो अग्रुवः समीचिः पुमाांसं जातम अभि सं रभन्ते ||
ajījanann amṛtam martyāso 'sremāṇaṁ taraṇiṁ vīḻujambham | daśa svasāro agruvaḥ samīcīḥ pumāṁsaṁ jātam abhi saṁ rabhante ||
  • ajījanann: This term can be translated as "giving birth" or "producing." It signifies the act of creation or emergence.
  • amṛtam: This word means "immortal" or "eternal." It represents the concept of something that transcends death and is everlasting.
  • martyāso: This refers to "mortals" or "human beings." It highlights the transient nature of human life.
  • 'sremāṇaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is prosperous" or "the one who brings wealth." It denotes abundance and success.
  • taraṇiṁ: This word means "the one who helps to cross" or "the savior." It symbolizes guidance and support in overcoming challenges.
  • vīḻujambham: This term can be understood as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who brings strength." It represents resilience and fortitude.
  • daśa: This means "ten." It can symbolize completeness or a full cycle.
  • svasāro: This refers to "brothers" or "siblings." It emphasizes the importance of community and familial bonds.
  • agruvaḥ: This term means "those who are strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and capability.
  • samīcīḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are righteous" or "the virtuous." It highlights the value of morality and ethics.
  • pumāṁsaṁ: This refers to "the man" or "the human." It emphasizes the essence of humanity.
  • jātam: This means "born" or "created." It signifies the beginning of existence.
  • abhi: This term can be understood as "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or progression.
  • saṁ rabhante: This means "they engage" or "they take hold." It signifies action and involvement.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the cycle of life, the interplay between mortality and immortality, and the importance of community and strength. It emphasizes the idea that while humans are mortal, they are part of a larger, eternal existence. The mantra encourages individuals to seek prosperity, support one another, and act with righteousness.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: It encourages individuals to embrace their mortality while striving for a meaningful and prosperous life.
  • Community Support: It highlights the importance of familial and communal bonds, urging people to support one another in times of need.
  • Ethical Living: The emphasis on righteousness serves as a reminder to act with integrity and morality in daily life.
  • Resilience: The mantra inspires individuals to be strong and resilient in the face of challenges, reminding them of their inner power.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a balanced and fulfilling life, recognizing the transient nature of existence while striving for eternal values.
