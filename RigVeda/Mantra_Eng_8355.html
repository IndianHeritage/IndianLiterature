सहस्रधारे वितते पवित्रा आ वाचं पुनन्ति कवयो मनीषिणः |रुद्रास एषां इषिरासो अद्रुहः स्पशः स्वञ्चः सुदृशो नृचक्षसः ||
sahasradhāre vitate pavitra ā vācam punanti kavayo manīṣiṇaḥ | rudrāsa eṣām iṣirāso adruhaḥ spaśaḥ svañcaḥ sudṛśo nṛcakṣasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradhāre: One who has a thousand streams or channels.
    • vitate: Spread out or extended.
    • pavitra: Pure or sacred.
    • ā vācam: This speech or utterance.
    • punanti: Purifies or cleanses.
    • kavayo: Poets or wise individuals.
    • manīṣiṇaḥ: Those who are thoughtful or wise.
    • rudrāsa: Of Rudra (a form of Shiva).
    • eṣām: Of these.
    • iṣirāso: Arrows or intentions.
    • adruhaḥ: Without malice or harm.
    • spaśaḥ: Clear or evident.
    • svañcaḥ: Self-possessed or self-aware.
    • sudṛśo: Of good vision or insight.
    • nṛcakṣasaḥ: Of human eyes or perception.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purity and power of speech, emphasizing that wise individuals can purify their words and intentions.
    • The reference to "sahasradhāre" suggests an abundance of wisdom and knowledge that can flow from a single source, much like a river with many tributaries.
    • The mention of "kavayo manīṣiṇaḥ" highlights the role of poets and thinkers in society, who have the ability to inspire and elevate through their words.
    • Furthermore, the mantra invokes the essence of Rudra, symbolizing strength and protection, suggesting that pure intentions and clear communication can lead to positive outcomes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, effective communication can lead to successful teamwork. Just as the mantra emphasizes the power of pure speech, clear and honest dialogue among team members can foster collaboration and innovation.
    • In personal relationships, the ability to express oneself without malice (adruhaḥ) can strengthen bonds. For instance, resolving conflicts through open and sincere conversations can lead to deeper understanding and trust.
    • In education, teachers who embody the qualities of "kavayo manīṣiṇaḥ" can inspire students. By sharing knowledge and wisdom, they can purify the minds of their students, guiding them towards enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of pure speech and wise intentions in our lives. It encourages individuals to communicate thoughtfully and to seek clarity in their expressions.
    • In a world where misunderstandings are common, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in various scenarios—be it in personal relationships, professional environments, or educational settings.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights that through purity of thought and intention, we can create a more harmonious and understanding society.
