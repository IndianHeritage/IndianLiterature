यो अप्सु चन्द्रमा इव सोमश्चमूषु ददृशे |पिबेदस्य त्वम ईशिषे ||
yo apsu candramā iva somaś camūṣu dadṛśe | pibed asya tvam īśiṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • apsu: in the waters
    • candramā: like the moon
    • iva: as if
    • somaś: of the Soma (a sacred drink)
    • camūṣu: in the armies
    • dadṛśe: has been seen
    • pibed: may drink
    • asya: of this
    • tvam: you
    • īśiṣe: O Lord
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine presence, comparing it to the moon that shines in the waters, symbolizing purity and tranquility.
    • The reference to "Soma" indicates a connection to spiritual nourishment and the essence of life, often associated with rituals and offerings in Vedic traditions.
    • The phrase "in the armies" suggests strength and unity, indicating that the divine presence is recognized and revered in collective endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke blessings for unity and strength, similar to how this mantra calls upon divine support in collective efforts.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may draw inspiration from this mantra when motivating their teams, emphasizing the importance of working together harmoniously, akin to an army united for a common goal.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may reflect on the calming influence of the moon and the nourishing aspects of life, seeking peace and strength through meditation or prayer.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine support in both personal and collective endeavors, reminding us of the importance of unity, strength, and spiritual nourishment.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various contexts, from community celebrations to personal reflections, emphasizing the need for harmony and divine guidance in our actions.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek and acknowledge the higher powers that guide and support us in our journeys, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and with each other.
