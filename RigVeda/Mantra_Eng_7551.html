यस्यā देवā उपस्थे व्रतā विश्वे धारण्ते |सूर्यामासā दृष्टे कम ||
yasyā devā upasthe vratā viśve dhārayante | sūryāmāsā dṛśe kam ||
  • Mantra: "yasyā devā upasthe vratā viśve dhārayante | sūryāmāsā dṛśe kam ||"
  • Translation: "Whose divine beings (devas) uphold the vows (vrata) of all, who are present in the light of the sun (sūrya), what is that (kam)?"

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yasyā: "Whose" - indicating possession or relation.
    • devā: "Divine beings" - refers to the gods or celestial entities that embody various aspects of nature and existence.
    • upasthe: "Uphold" - signifies support or maintenance of something important.
    • vratā: "Vows" - represents commitments or sacred promises made by individuals or communities.
    • viśve: "All" - denotes inclusivity, referring to the entirety of existence.
    • dhārayante: "Sustain" - implies the act of holding or maintaining something over time.
    • sūryāmāsā: "In the light of the sun" - symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the life-giving force of the sun.
    • dṛśe: "In sight" - refers to perception or awareness.
    • kam: "What" - a question word that seeks to understand or inquire about something.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the relationship between divine beings and the commitments made by individuals.
    • It suggests that the divine supports and sustains the vows taken by all beings, highlighting the importance of these commitments in the cosmic order.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes the source of life and knowledge, indicating that true understanding and enlightenment come from a higher power.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as the importance of community vows in social settings, where collective commitments lead to harmony and support.
    • For example, in a family, the vows taken during marriage are upheld by the family members, creating a supportive environment for growth and love.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine support and human commitments.
    • It teaches us that our vows and promises are not just personal but are upheld by a greater cosmic order.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to honor their commitments, knowing that they are part of a larger framework that sustains life and relationships.
    • By recognizing the divine presence in our vows, we can cultivate a sense of responsibility and awareness in our actions, leading to a more harmonious existence.
