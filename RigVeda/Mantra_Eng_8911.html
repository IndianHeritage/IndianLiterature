अपेत वीत विच सरपतातोऽस्मा एतम पितरो लोकम् अकृन् |अहोभिर अद्भिर अक्तुभिर व्यक्तं यमो ददात्य अवसानम् अस्मै ||
apeta vīta vi ca sarpatāto 'smā etam pitaro lokam akran | ahobhir adbhir aktubhir vyaktaṁ yamo dadāty avasānam asmai ||
  • Mantra: "apeta vīta vi ca sarpatāto 'smā etam pitaro lokam akran | ahobhir adbhir aktubhir vyaktaṁ yamo dadāty avasānam asmai ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the journey of the soul after death, invoking the ancestors and the divine forces that guide the departed to their rightful place.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • apeta: departed or gone away
    • vīta: life or vitality
    • vi: in this context, it can mean 'in this manner' or 'thus'
    • sarpatāto: from the realm of serpents, symbolizing the underworld or lower realms
    • pitaro: ancestors or forefathers
    • lokam: world or realm
    • akran: to attain or reach
    • ahobhir: with great force or power
    • adbhir: with water or the divine essence
    • aktubhir: with the light or illumination
    • vyaktaṁ: manifest or clear
    • yamo: Yama, the god of death
    • dadāty: gives or bestows
    • avasānam: final resting place or conclusion
    • asmai: to him or to that soul
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the departed souls, asking for their safe passage to the afterlife.
    • It acknowledges the role of ancestors in guiding the souls and emphasizes the connection between the living and the dead.
    • The imagery of serpents can represent the challenges faced in the journey after death, while the invocation of Yama signifies the acceptance of death as a natural part of life.
    • Real-life example: In many cultures, rituals are performed for the deceased, such as the Day of the Dead in Mexico, where families honor their ancestors, believing that they return to visit.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of remembering and honoring our ancestors, recognizing their influence in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the transient nature of life and the inevitability of death, encouraging individuals to live meaningfully.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applicable during memorial services, funerals, or personal moments of reflection on the lives of those who have passed.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can find solace in the belief that their loved ones are being cared for in the afterlife.
