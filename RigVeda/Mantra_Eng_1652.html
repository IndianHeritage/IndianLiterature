को वां दाशत सुमतये चिद अस्यै वसू यद धेथे नमसां पदे गोः |जिगृतम अस्मे रेवतीः पुरंधीः कामप्रेणेव मनसा चरन्ता ||
ko vāṁ dāśat sumataye cid asyai vasū yad dhethe namasā pade goḥ | jigṛtam asme revatīḥ puraṁdhīḥ kāmapreṇeva manasā carantā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko vāṁ: Who are you?
    • dāśat: To give or to bestow.
    • sumataye: For good thoughts or wisdom.
    • cid asyai: This consciousness or awareness.
    • vasū: Wealth or prosperity.
    • yad dhethe: That which is established or created.
    • namasā: With reverence or respect.
    • pade goḥ: In the realm of the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment).
    • jigṛtam: To be attained or achieved.
    • asme: For us.
    • revatiḥ: Prosperity or abundance.
    • puraṁdhīḥ: The one who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • kāmapreṇeva: Like the desire or wish.
    • manasā: With the mind.
    • carantā: Moving or wandering.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a question, "Who are you?" which invites introspection and self-awareness.
    • The phrase "to give for good thoughts" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and positive thinking in our lives.
    • Wealth and prosperity are symbolized by "vasū," indicating that true wealth is not just material but also includes wisdom and consciousness.
    • The reference to "the realm of the cow" signifies nourishment and sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
    • By invoking "prosperity" and "knowledge," the mantra suggests that these qualities should be sought after and revered.
    • The concluding lines highlight the importance of desires and thoughts, suggesting that our mental state influences our actions and outcomes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often ask, "Who are you?" to encourage employees to reflect on their roles and contributions, fostering a culture of self-awareness.
    • Practicing mindfulness and positive thinking can lead to better decision-making and improved relationships, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on good thoughts.
    • In personal finance, understanding that true wealth includes knowledge and wisdom can lead to more informed financial decisions, rather than just chasing material possessions.
    • In agriculture, the cow is revered for its role in providing sustenance; similarly, nurturing our environment can lead to prosperity for future generations.
    • Setting intentions and visualizing goals can be seen as "moving with the mind," which aligns with the mantra's call to harness our desires for positive outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of self-awareness, wisdom, and prosperity.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and cultivate positive thoughts, which can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance personal growth, improve relationships, and foster a sense of community and abundance.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of mental and spiritual wealth as foundational to achieving true prosperity.
