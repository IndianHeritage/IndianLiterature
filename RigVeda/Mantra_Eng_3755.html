अग्निः जुषत नो गीरः होतā यो मानुषेष्व् ā | स यक्षद दैव्यम् जनम् ||
agnir juṣata no giro hotā yo mānuṣeṣv ā | sa yakṣad daivyaṁ janam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, often symbolizing energy, purity, and transformation.
    • juṣata: May you enjoy or may you be pleased.
    • no: Our.
    • giro: Words or speech.
    • hotā: The one who invokes or calls upon.
    • yo: Who.
    • mānuṣeṣv: Among humans.
    • ā: In or within.
    • sa: He or that.
    • yakṣad: May he bestow or grant.
    • daivyaṁ: Divine or heavenly.
    • janam: People or community.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings on our words and actions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and the role of divine energy in human interactions.
    • By seeking Agni's favor, we aim to enhance our ability to connect with others and foster a harmonious community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team meeting, invoking the spirit of Agni can symbolize the desire for constructive dialogue and collaboration.
    • During a family gathering, this mantra can remind us to communicate openly and supportively, enhancing familial bonds.
    • In public speaking or presentations, channeling the energy of Agni can help in delivering messages with clarity and passion.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in our communication and interactions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, where effective communication is key.
    • By invoking Agni, we not only seek blessings for ourselves but also for the community, fostering unity and understanding among people.
