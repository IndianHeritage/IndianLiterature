परी सद्मेव पशुमान्ति होताराजा न सत्यः समितीर इयानः |सोमः पुनानः कलशाम् आयासीत सीदन मृगो न महिषो वनेषु ||
pari sadmeva paśumānti hotā rājā na satyaḥ samitīr iyānaḥ | somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśām̐ ayāsīt sīdan mṛgo na mahiṣo vaneṣu ||
  • Mantra: "pari sadmeva paśumānti hotā rājā na satyaḥ samitīr iyānaḥ | somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśām̐ ayāsīt sīdan mṛgo na mahiṣo vaneṣu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • sadmeva: dwelling, abode
    • paśumānti: the animals, the living beings
    • hotā: the priest, the one who invokes
    • rājā: king, ruler
    • na: not
    • satyaḥ: truth, reality
    • samitīr: gathering, assembly
    • iyānaḥ: those who go, those who travel
    • somaḥ: the sacred drink, soma
    • punānaḥ: purifying, cleansing
    • kalaśām̐: the pot, vessel
    • ayāsīt: has been made, has been created
    • sīdan: sitting, residing
    • mṛgo: the deer, the animal
    • na: not
    • mahiṣo: the buffalo, the strong animal
    • vaneṣu: in the forests, in the woods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the harmony of nature and the role of the king or ruler in maintaining that harmony.
    • The phrase "pari sadmeva paśumānti" suggests that all living beings dwell together in a shared space, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • The "hotā rājā" indicates the importance of leadership in guiding and invoking the right energies for the welfare of all beings.
    • The mention of "somaḥ punānaḥ" highlights the significance of purification and the sacredness of certain rituals that cleanse and rejuvenate the environment.
    • Furthermore, the contrast between "mṛgo" and "mahiṣo" in the forests suggests a balance between gentleness and strength in nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to environmental conservation efforts where leaders (rulers) play a crucial role in protecting wildlife and natural habitats.
    • Community gatherings that focus on sustainability and ecological balance reflect the essence of "samitīr iyānaḥ," where people come together to discuss and implement solutions for a healthier planet.
    • Rituals and practices that promote mental and physical well-being, akin to the "somaḥ punānaḥ," can be seen in yoga and meditation sessions that aim to purify the mind and body.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of leadership, community, and the sacredness of nature.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to maintain harmony within our environment and among all living beings.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that promote ecological balance, community well-being, and the pursuit of truth and purity in our actions.
