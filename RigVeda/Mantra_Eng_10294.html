अप्सरासां गन्धर्वाणां मृगाणां चरणे चरण | केशी केतस्य विद्वान् सखा स्वादुर मदिन्तमः ||
apsarasāṁ gandharvāṇām mṛgāṇāṁ caraṇe caran | keśī ketasya vidvān sakhā svādur madintamaḥ ||
  • Apsarasāṁ: Refers to the celestial nymphs or divine beings in Hindu mythology, often associated with beauty and grace.
  • Gandharvāṇām: Refers to the Gandharvas, who are celestial musicians and are known for their artistic skills and knowledge of music.
  • Mṛgāṇāṁ: Means "of the animals" or "of the creatures," indicating a connection to the natural world.
  • Caraṇe caran: Translates to "the one who walks on the feet of others," suggesting a sense of humility and interconnectedness.
  • Keśī: Refers to a person with long hair, often symbolizing beauty and strength.
  • Ketasya: Means "of the mind" or "of the intellect," indicating wisdom and understanding.
  • Vidvān: Refers to a knowledgeable or wise person, someone who possesses deep understanding.
  • Sakhā: Means "friend," emphasizing the importance of companionship and support.
  • Svādur: Translates to "sweet" or "pleasant," often used to describe something enjoyable or delightful.
  • Madintamaḥ: Refers to the "most intoxicating" or "the highest bliss," indicating a state of ultimate joy or ecstasy.

The mantra speaks to the beauty of relationships and the interconnectedness of all beings, both divine and earthly. It highlights the importance of wisdom, friendship, and the joy that comes from these connections. The imagery of celestial beings and animals walking together suggests harmony in nature and the universe.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Friendship: It emphasizes the value of having wise and supportive friends in our lives, who can guide us through challenges.
  • Interconnectedness: It reminds us of our connection to nature and the importance of respecting all living beings.
  • Joy in Relationships: The mantra encourages us to seek joy and sweetness in our interactions with others, fostering a sense of community.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the beauty of relationships, the importance of wisdom, and the joy that comes from being connected to others and the world around us. It encourages us to cultivate friendships, appreciate the interconnectedness of life, and seek bliss in our daily experiences.
