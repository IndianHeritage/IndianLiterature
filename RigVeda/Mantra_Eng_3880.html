सूरश्चिद्रथमपरितकम्यायां पूर्वं करदुपरं जूजुवांसम् |भरचचक्रमेतशः सं रिणाति पुरो दधत सनीष्यति क्रतुं नः ||
sūraś cid ratham paritakmyāyām pūrvaṁ karad uparaṁ jūjuvāṁsam | bharac cakram etaśaḥ saṁ riṇāti puro dadhat saniṣyati kratuṁ naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūraś: strong, powerful, or valiant
    • cid: indeed, or certainly
    • ratham: chariot
    • paritakmyāyām: in the surrounding or encompassing area
    • pūrvam: previously or in the past
    • karad: hand or action
    • uparam: above or beyond
    • jūjuvāṁsam: the one who is strong or powerful
    • bharac: bearing or carrying
    • cakram: wheel or cycle
    • etaśaḥ: from this place or here
    • saṁ: together or in union
    • riṇāti: to sing or chant
    • puro: in front or ahead
    • dadhat: holding or possessing
    • saniṣyati: to establish or set firmly
    • kratuṁ: will or intention
    • naḥ: our or us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful chariot that is surrounded by strength and action.
    • The reference to "previously" suggests a connection to past actions or traditions that guide the present.
    • The imagery of a strong individual (jūjuvāṁsam) indicates the importance of inner strength and resilience.
    • The "wheel" (cakram) symbolizes the cyclical nature of life and the importance of movement and progress.
    • The act of singing or chanting (riṇāti) emphasizes the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality.
    • Establishing our will (kratuṁ) is crucial for achieving our goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader (sūraś) can guide their team (ratham) through challenges, drawing on past experiences (pūrvam) to make informed decisions.
    • In personal development, individuals often reflect on their past actions (karad) to build resilience (jūjuvāṁsam) and move forward with purpose (cakram).
    • In community building, collective efforts (saṁ) can lead to significant change, as people come together to chant (riṇāti) for a common cause.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, intention, and the cyclical nature of life.
    • It teaches us that by reflecting on our past and harnessing our inner strength, we can navigate challenges and achieve our goals.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal growth, and community efforts, reminding us of the power of unity and resilience.
