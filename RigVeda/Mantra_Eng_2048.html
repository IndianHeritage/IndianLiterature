इमं विधन्तो अपां सधस्थे द्वितादधुर भृगवो विक्ष्व आयोः |एष विश्वान्य अभ्य अस्तु भूमां देवानाम अग्निर अरतिर जीराश्वः ||
imaṁ vidhanto apāṁ sadhasthe dvitādadhur bhṛgavo vikṣv ā3yoḥ | eṣa viśvāny abhy astu bhūmā devānām agnir aratir jīrāśvaḥ ||
  • imaṁ vidhanto apāṁ sadhasthe dvitādadhur bhṛgavo vikṣv ā3yoḥ
    • imaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "these," indicating a focus on the present or immediate context.
    • vidhanto - Meaning "those who create" or "those who establish," it refers to the divine or powerful beings who shape reality.
    • apāṁ - This means "of waters," symbolizing the life-giving and purifying aspects of water.
    • sadhasthe - Translates to "in the place of abundance," suggesting a fertile or prosperous environment.
    • dvitādadhur - This can be interpreted as "twofold" or "dual," indicating a balance or duality in nature.
    • bhṛgavo - Refers to the "Bhrigus," a group of sages or seers known for their wisdom and knowledge.
    • vikṣv ā3yoḥ - This phrase can be understood as "in the vastness of the world," emphasizing the expansive nature of existence.
  • eṣa viśvāny abhy astu bhūmā devānām agnir aratir jīrāśvaḥ
    • eṣa - Meaning "this," it again emphasizes the present moment or the immediate context.
    • viśvāny - Translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • abhy - This means "towards" or "for," suggesting an intention or direction.
    • astu - Meaning "may it be" or "let it be," it expresses a wish or blessing.
    • bhūmā - Refers to "the earth" or "the ground," symbolizing stability and support.
    • devānām - Meaning "of the gods," indicating divine presence and influence.
    • agnir - Refers to "fire," symbolizing transformation, energy, and purification.
    • aratir - This can be interpreted as "freedom from obstacles" or "removal of hindrances."
    • jīrāśvaḥ - Meaning "the one who is strong and enduring," it signifies resilience and longevity.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe, highlighting the role of divine forces in creating and sustaining life. It calls for blessings from the divine to ensure abundance, stability, and the removal of obstacles in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives.
  • In environmental contexts, it reminds us of the importance of water and nature in sustaining life.
  • In community settings, it fosters a sense of unity and collective well-being, urging individuals to work together for a prosperous future.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us and the importance of nurturing our relationships with nature and each other for a fulfilling life.
