ऋजुरिच्छंसो वनवद्वनुष्यतो देवयन्निदादेवयन्तमभ्यसत् |सुप्रवीरिद्वनवत्पृत्सुदुष्टरं यज्वेदयज्योर्विभजाती भोजनेम् ||
ṛjur ic chaṁso vanavad vanuṣyato devayann id adevayantam abhy asat | suprāvīr id vanavat pṛtsu duṣṭaraṁ yajved ayajyor vi bhajāti bhojanam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛjur: Straight, honest, or upright.
    • ic: Desire or wish.
    • chaṁso: Of the essence or nature.
    • vanavad: Like a forest or wilderness.
    • vanuṣyato: Of the human beings or mankind.
    • devayann: Divine or godly.
    • id: This.
    • adevayantam: Not leading to the divine or spiritual.
    • abhy: Towards or in the direction of.
    • asat: Untruth or falsehood.
    • suprāvīr: Well-armed or strong.
    • id: This.
    • vanavat: Like a forest.
    • pṛtsu: In the fields or lands.
    • duṣṭaraṁ: Difficult or challenging.
    • yajved: One who performs sacrifices or rituals.
    • ayajyor: Those who do not perform rituals.
    • vi: Apart or separately.
    • bhajāti: Partakes or shares.
    • bhojanam: Food or nourishment.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of being upright and honest in one's desires and actions.
    • It suggests that while one may wish for material or worldly gains (like the abundance found in a forest), it is essential to align these desires with divine principles.
    • The mantra contrasts the qualities of those who are spiritually inclined (devayann) with those who are not (adevayantam), highlighting the significance of truthfulness and righteousness.
    • It also points out that engaging in rituals (yajved) is crucial for spiritual nourishment, while neglecting them (ayajyor) leads to separation from divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who is honest and transparent in their dealings (ṛjur) is more likely to gain the trust of colleagues and superiors, leading to a more harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful and sincere (asat) fosters deeper connections and mutual respect, akin to the nurturing environment of a forest (vanavad).
    • Individuals who engage in community service or charitable acts (yajved) often find a sense of fulfillment and connection with others, contrasting with those who focus solely on self-interest (ayajyor).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of integrity and spiritual alignment in our desires and actions.
    • It encourages individuals to seek nourishment not just from material gains but also from spiritual practices and community engagement.
    • In essence, it highlights that true fulfillment comes from a balance of worldly and spiritual pursuits, guiding us towards a more meaningful and connected life.
