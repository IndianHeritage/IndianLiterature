इन्द्राविष्णूमदपतिमदानामासोमंयातंद्रविणोद्धानाः। संवामञ्जन्त्वक्तुभिर्मतीनांसंस्तोमाशःशस्यमानासुक्थैः॥
indrāviṣṇū madapatī madānām ā somaṁ yātaṁ draviṇo dadhānā | saṁ vām añjantv aktubhir matīnāṁ saṁ stomāsaḥ śasyamānāsa ukthaiḥ ||
  • Indrāviṣṇū: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra and Vishnu, two of the most significant deities in Vedic literature. Indra is often associated with rain, storms, and war, while Vishnu represents preservation and protection.
  • Madapatī: This word translates to "the lord of intoxication" or "the one who brings joy." It can be interpreted as a reference to the divine essence that brings happiness and ecstasy.
  • Madānām: This term means "of the intoxications" or "of the joys." It signifies the various forms of bliss and ecstasy that life can offer.
  • Ā somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • Yātaṁ: This word means "having gone" or "having traveled." It indicates a journey or a movement towards a higher state of being.
  • Draviṇo dadhānā: This phrase translates to "holding wealth" or "possessing resources." It emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual wealth in life.
  • Saṁ vām añjantv: This means "may we be united" or "may we come together." It highlights the significance of unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • Aktubhir matīnāṁ: This phrase refers to "with the strength of our minds" or "with the power of our thoughts." It underscores the importance of mental strength and clarity in achieving goals.
  • Saṁ stomāsaḥ: This term means "with our praises" or "with our hymns." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine.
  • Śasyamānāsa ukthaiḥ: This translates to "may we be blessed with abundance" or "may we receive the fruits of our labor." It emphasizes the desire for prosperity and success in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the interconnectedness of divine forces, the importance of joy and unity, and the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering unity and collaboration among family and friends.
  • In professional settings, encouraging teamwork and collective efforts to achieve common goals.
  • In spiritual practices, invoking the blessings of divine forces for prosperity and abundance.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between material and spiritual pursuits, urging individuals to seek joy, unity, and abundance in their lives.
