इन्द्र तुभ्यं इन मगवन्न अभूम वयं दात्रे हरिवो मा वि वेनः |नकिर आपिर ददृशे मार्त्यत्रा किम अङ्ग रध्रचोदनं त्वाहुः ||
indra tubhyam in maghavann abhūma vayaṁ dātre harivo mā vi venaḥ | nakir āpir dadṛśe martyatrā kim aṅga radhracodanaṁ tvāhuḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Tubhyam: This word translates to "to you," indicating a direct address to Indra, showing reverence and devotion.
  • Maghavann: This term refers to Indra as the "great giver" or "generous one," emphasizing his role in bestowing blessings and prosperity.
  • Abhūma: Meaning "we have become" or "we have attained," this word signifies a sense of achievement or fulfillment.
  • Vayaṁ: This translates to "we," indicating a collective group, possibly the worshippers or the community seeking Indra's blessings.
  • Dātre: Meaning "to the giver," this word reinforces the idea of Indra as a benefactor who provides for his devotees.
  • Harivo: This term can be interpreted as "O Lord," a respectful address to Indra, acknowledging his divine status.
  • Mā vi venaḥ: This phrase translates to "may we not be harmed," expressing a plea for protection and safety from adversities.
  • Nakir: Meaning "not," it negates the following term, indicating a desire for the absence of negativity or harm.
  • Āpir: This word means "to see" or "to perceive," suggesting a longing for clarity and understanding in life.
  • Dadṛśe: This translates to "to be seen," indicating a desire for visibility or acknowledgment in the world.
  • Martyatrā: Meaning "in this mortal realm," it emphasizes the human experience and the challenges faced in life.
  • Kim aṅga: This phrase translates to "what indeed," posing a rhetorical question about the nature of existence or challenges.
  • Radhracodanaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the driving force" or "the impetus," referring to the motivation behind actions and decisions.
  • Tvāhuḥ: Meaning "they call you," this word indicates that others recognize and invoke Indra's name, acknowledging his importance.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for strength, protection, and prosperity. It reflects the human desire for safety and acknowledgment in a world filled with challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as during times of uncertainty, when seeking guidance in decision-making, or when facing adversities. By invoking Indra's name, individuals can find solace and strength, reminding them of the importance of community and divine support in overcoming life's obstacles.
