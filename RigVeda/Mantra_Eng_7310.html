मन्त्र: किम इदं वाम पुराणवज जरतोर इव शस्यते |अन्ति षड् भूतु वाम अवः ||
kim idaṁ vām purāṇavaj jarator iva śasyate | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • idaṁ: this
    • vām: to you
    • purāṇavaj: like the ancient
    • jarator: of the aged
    • iva: like
    • śasyate: is nourished
    • anti: there are
    • ṣad: six
    • bhūtu: elements
    • vām: to you
    • avaḥ: may we attain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the essence of existence and nourishment, asking what is it that nourishes you like the ancient ones.
    • The reference to "aged" suggests wisdom and experience, indicating that true nourishment comes from understanding and learning from the past.
    • The mention of "six elements" can be interpreted as the fundamental components of life, which may include earth, water, fire, air, space, and consciousness.
    • Overall, the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from ancient traditions to nourish our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, we often look back at historical texts and philosophies to guide our understanding of the world. For instance, studying ancient Greek philosophy can provide insights into ethics and governance.
    • In personal development, many people turn to the teachings of ancient sages, such as the Bhagavad Gita or the Tao Te Ching, to find guidance in their daily lives.
    • Environmental sustainability can also be linked to this mantra, as ancient practices often emphasized living in harmony with nature, which is crucial for our survival today.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of ancient wisdom in our contemporary lives. It encourages us to seek nourishment not just from material sources but also from the rich heritage of knowledge that has been passed down through generations.
    • By reflecting on the teachings of the past, we can better understand our present and shape a more sustainable and enlightened future.
    • In essence, the mantra invites us to connect with the deeper elements of existence and to appreciate the wisdom that can guide us in our journey through life.
