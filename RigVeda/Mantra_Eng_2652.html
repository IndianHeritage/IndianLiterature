अग्निश्रियो मरुतो विश्वकृष्टय आ त्वेषम् उग्रं अव इमहे वयम् |ते स्वानिनो रुद्रिया वर्षनिर्णिजः सिंहा न हेषक्रतवः सुदानवः ||
agniśriyo maruto viśvakṛṣṭaya ā tveṣam ugram ava īmahe vayam | te svānino rudriyā varṣanirṇijaḥ siṁhā na heṣakratavaḥ sudānavaḥ ||
  • Agniśriyo: This term refers to the "glorious Agni" or "fire," which symbolizes purity, energy, and transformation. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and even industrial processes, representing the transformative power of energy.
  • Maruto: This word signifies the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with wind and storms. They represent the forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal. For example, storms can clear the air and nourish the earth, leading to new growth.
  • Viśvakṛṣṭaya: This term means "all-encompassing" or "universal." It suggests a connection to the entire universe and the interconnectedness of all beings. In our lives, this can be seen in how our actions impact the environment and society as a whole.
  • Ā tveṣam ugram ava īmahe vayam: This phrase translates to "we invoke your fierce form." It indicates a call for strength and protection from the divine forces. In real life, this can be likened to seeking courage and resilience in challenging situations, such as facing personal or professional adversities.
  • Te svānino: This means "your own" or "belonging to you." It emphasizes the idea of ownership and connection to the divine. In practical terms, it can relate to the importance of community and belonging in our lives, where we find strength in our relationships.
  • Rudriyā: This refers to the "Rudras," who are associated with storms and destruction but also with healing and regeneration. This duality can be seen in life events that may seem negative at first but lead to personal growth and new opportunities.
  • Varṣanirṇijaḥ: This term means "those who bring rain." Rain is vital for agriculture and sustenance, symbolizing nourishment and abundance. In real life, it reminds us of the importance of nature's cycles and our dependence on them for survival.
  • Siṁhā na heṣakratavaḥ sudānavaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the lions who are powerful and strong." Lions symbolize strength, courage, and leadership. In our lives, we can draw inspiration from this to be leaders in our communities and to face challenges with bravery.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, transformation, and interconnectedness with the universe. It serves as a reminder of the divine forces that guide and protect us, encouraging us to seek courage in adversity and to recognize our place within the larger tapestry of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges, fostering community, and respecting the natural world, ultimately leading to personal growth and a deeper understanding of our interconnected existence.
