गोभिष् टरेमामतिं दुरेवां यवेना क्षुधं पुरुहूत विश्वाम् |वयं राजभिः प्रथमां धनान्य अस्माकेन वृजनेन जयेम ||
gobhiṣ ṭaremāmatiṁ durevāṁ yavena kṣudham puruhūta viśvām | vayaṁ rājabhiḥ prathamā dhanāny asmākena vṛjanenā jayema ||
  • gobhiṣ: This word can be interpreted as "with the cows" or "with the herds." In ancient Vedic society, cows were considered sacred and a symbol of wealth and prosperity.
  • ṭaremāmatiṁ: This translates to "may we attain wisdom" or "may we achieve understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and insight in life.
  • durevāṁ: This means "from afar" or "from a distance." It suggests the idea of reaching out to something that may seem unattainable or distant.
  • yavena: This word refers to "barley," which is a staple grain. It symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • kṣudham: This translates to "hunger" or "thirst." It represents the basic human needs and desires that drive individuals to seek fulfillment.
  • puruhūta: This term can be understood as "the one who is invoked often" or "the one who is called upon." It signifies the importance of prayer and invocation in seeking blessings.
  • viśvām: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates a sense of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vayaṁ: This translates to "we." It emphasizes community and collective effort.
  • rājabhiḥ: This means "with kings" or "with rulers." It signifies the importance of leadership and governance in society.
  • prathamā: This translates to "first" or "foremost." It indicates priority and the importance of being at the forefront of endeavors.
  • dhanāny: This means "wealth" or "riches." It represents material prosperity and abundance.
  • asmākena: This translates to "by us" or "through us." It emphasizes personal agency and responsibility in achieving goals.
  • vṛjanenā: This means "by the means" or "through the efforts." It signifies the importance of action and effort in attaining success.
  • jayema: This translates to "may we conquer" or "may we triumph." It expresses the aspiration for victory and success in endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of wisdom, community, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort, leadership, and the invocation of divine blessings to overcome challenges and achieve success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, or in personal endeavors where one seeks knowledge and sustenance. It serves as a reminder that through unity, effort, and the pursuit of wisdom, individuals and communities can overcome obstacles and attain prosperity.
