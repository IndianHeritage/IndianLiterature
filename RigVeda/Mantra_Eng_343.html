आ यद दुवाह शतक्रतव आ कामं जरीतृणाम् |ऋणोर अक्षं न शचीभिः ||
ā yad duvaḥ śatakratav ā kāmaṁ jaritṝṇām | ṛṇor akṣaṁ na śacībhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: whatever
    • duvaḥ: is desired
    • śatakratav: of the one who has performed a hundred sacrifices (referring to Indra)
    • ā kāmaṁ: for the fulfillment of desires
    • jaritṝṇām: of those who are wise or learned
    • ṛṇor: of debts
    • akṣaṁ: the unbroken or undiminished
    • na: not
    • śacībhiḥ: by the powerful or strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the desires and the fulfillment of those desires.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wise actions and the consequences of one's debts.
    • The reference to "śatakratav" (Indra) signifies the power of sacrifice and the strength that comes from it.
    • The phrase "ṛṇor akṣaṁ na śacībhiḥ" suggests that one should not let their debts be diminished by the powerful, indicating the importance of integrity and responsibility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the importance of ethical practices. A company that fulfills its obligations and maintains integrity will build trust and long-term success.
    • In personal relationships, being responsible for one's actions and debts fosters respect and strengthens bonds. For instance, if a friend borrows money, repaying it promptly shows integrity and builds trust.
    • In community service, individuals who contribute selflessly to society (akin to performing sacrifices) often find their own needs met through the goodwill they generate.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wise actions in fulfilling desires while maintaining integrity.
    • It teaches that true strength lies not just in power but in the ability to uphold one's responsibilities and debts.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
