यā इन्द्र प्रस्वस त्वासā गर्भम् अचक्रिरन् |परि धर्मेव सूर्यं ||
yā indra prasvas tvāsā garbham acakriran | pari dharmeva sūryam ||
  • yā - This word translates to "who" or "that which." It indicates a subject or a focus of attention.
  • indra - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • prasvas - This term can be interpreted as "to breathe" or "to inspire." It signifies the act of giving life or vitality.
  • tvāsā - This word means "with your strength" or "by your power." It emphasizes the divine strength that is being invoked.
  • garbham - This translates to "womb" or "embryo," symbolizing creation, potential, and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • acakriran - This term means "they have created" or "they have made." It indicates the act of bringing something into existence.
  • pari - This word means "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a protective or encompassing nature.
  • dharmeva - This translates to "in accordance with dharma," where dharma refers to the moral order, duty, and righteousness.
  • sūryam - This word means "the sun," which symbolizes light, knowledge, and life-giving energy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for his strength and creative power to surround and nurture life, much like the sun provides light and energy to the world. It emphasizes the importance of divine intervention in the process of creation and the moral order that governs it.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the sun nurtures life, individuals can seek inspiration and strength from their surroundings and the divine to foster personal development and creativity.
  • Community Building: The idea of surrounding and nurturing can be applied to community efforts, where collective strength and moral values help in creating a supportive environment.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the sun can remind us of the importance of nature and the need to protect our environment, as it is essential for life and sustenance.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine strength, creation, and moral order. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking inspiration and support from higher powers while also emphasizing our responsibility to nurture and protect life in all its forms. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can strive for a harmonious existence that aligns with the greater good.
