उषसां न केतवोऽध्वरश्रियः शुभंयवो नाञ्जिभिर व्य अश्वितन |सिन्धवो न ययियो भ्राजदृष्टयः परावतो न योजनानि ममिरे ||
uṣasāṁ na ketavo 'dhvaraśriyaḥ śubhaṁyavo nāñjibhir vy aśvitan | sindhavo na yayiyo bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ parāvato na yojanāni mamire ||
  • uṣasāṁ: This word refers to "dawn" or "the early morning light." In many cultures, dawn symbolizes new beginnings and hope.
  • na ketavo: "Ketavo" can be interpreted as "the stars" or "the celestial bodies." The phrase suggests a contrast between the dawn and the stars, indicating the transition from night to day.
  • 'dhvaraśriyaḥ: This term can be translated as "the glory of the sacrifice" or "the auspiciousness of rituals." It emphasizes the importance of rituals in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • śubhaṁyavo: This word means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It highlights the positive outcomes that arise from righteous actions and intentions.
  • nāñjibhir: This term can be understood as "without any hindrance" or "unobstructed." It suggests that the path to success should be clear and free from obstacles.
  • vy aśvitan: "Aśvitan" refers to "the horses" or "the swift ones." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize speed, strength, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • sindhavo: This word means "rivers." Rivers are often seen as sources of life and nourishment, representing the flow of resources and abundance.
  • na yayiyo: This phrase can be interpreted as "not to be obstructed" or "not to be hindered." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a free flow of energy and resources.
  • bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ: This term means "those who have clear vision" or "those who see clearly." It suggests the importance of wisdom and insight in navigating life's challenges.
  • parāvato: This word can be translated as "the winds" or "the forces of nature." It signifies the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life.
  • na yojanāni: This phrase means "not to be connected" or "not to be bound." It suggests the importance of freedom and independence in one's journey.
  • mamire: This term can be understood as "to achieve" or "to attain." It signifies the ultimate goal of the mantra, which is to reach success and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of new beginnings, the importance of rituals, and the need for clarity and wisdom in overcoming obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new project, engaging in community service, or pursuing personal growth. By embracing auspiciousness and maintaining a clear vision, individuals can navigate challenges and achieve their goals. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain open to new opportunities and to approach life with a positive and proactive mindset.
