स होतā विश्वं परिभूत्वध्वरं तं उ हव्यैर मनुष ऋञ्जते गिरा | हिरिषिप्रो वृद्धसाना सु जर्भुरद द्यौर न स्तृभिः चितयद रोडसी अनु ||
sa hotā viśvam pari bhūtv adhvaraṁ tam u havyair manuṣa ṛñjate girā | hiriśipro vṛdhasānāsu jarbhurad dyaur na stṛbhiś citayad rodasī anu ||
  • sa hotā: This phrase refers to the priest or the one who offers the sacrifice. In a real-life context, this can be likened to a leader or a facilitator who guides a group towards a common goal.
  • viśvam: This means 'the universe' or 'all beings'. It signifies the interconnectedness of all life forms, similar to how in a community, every individual plays a role in the larger ecosystem.
  • pari bhūtv adhvaraṁ: This can be interpreted as 'having become the sacrifice'. It emphasizes the importance of selflessness and contribution to a greater cause, akin to volunteering for community service or participating in charitable activities.
  • tam u havyair: This phrase translates to 'that which is offered'. It highlights the significance of offerings in rituals, which can be compared to the acts of kindness and generosity we extend to others in our daily lives.
  • manuṣa ṛñjate girā: This means 'humans invoke with their words'. It underscores the power of speech and intention, similar to how motivational speeches can inspire and uplift communities.
  • hiriśipro: This term refers to the divine or celestial beings. In a modern context, it can represent the ideals or values we aspire to, such as integrity and compassion.
  • vṛdhasānāsu: This translates to 'in the great ones'. It signifies the importance of wisdom and experience, much like seeking guidance from mentors or elders in our lives.
  • jarbhurad: This can be interpreted as 'the one who is invoked'. It reflects the idea of calling upon higher powers or principles for support, similar to how people pray or meditate for guidance in challenging times.
  • dyaur na stṛbhiś: This means 'the sky does not cover'. It suggests transparency and openness, akin to the importance of honesty in relationships and communication.
  • citayad rodasī anu: This translates to 'the earth follows'. It emphasizes the connection between the heavens and the earth, symbolizing how our actions on earth resonate with the universe, much like the ripple effect of our deeds in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, selflessness, and the power of intention. It teaches us that our actions, words, and offerings contribute to the greater good of the universe. In real life, this can be applied through community service, ethical leadership, and fostering relationships built on trust and transparency. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious environment that benefits all beings, reflecting the unity and diversity of life as celebrated in the RigVeda.
