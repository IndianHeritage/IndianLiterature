महो रुजामि बन्धुतā वचोभिस् तन् मापितुर् गोतमाद् अन्व इयाय |त्वं नो अस्य वचसश् चिकीद्धि होतर् यविष्ट सुक्रतो दमूनाः ||
maho rujāmi bandhutā vacobhis tan mā pitur gotamād anv iyāya | tvaṁ no asya vacasaś cikiddhi hotar yaviṣṭha sukrato damūnāḥ ||
  • maho rujāmi: This phrase can be translated as "I am greatly afflicted" or "I am suffering greatly." It expresses a deep sense of distress or pain.
  • bandhutā: This word refers to "kinship" or "brotherhood." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and connections among people.
  • vacobhis: This term means "by words" or "through speech." It highlights the power of communication and the impact of words in our lives.
  • tan mā pitur gotamād anv iyāya: This can be interpreted as "may I not be led astray by my father's words." It suggests a desire for guidance and wisdom, urging one to seek the right path.
  • tvaṁ no asya vacasaś: This translates to "you, O Hotar (priest), of this speech." It acknowledges the role of the priest or spiritual guide in providing direction and support.
  • cikiddhi: This word means "to understand" or "to comprehend." It emphasizes the importance of gaining insight and clarity in difficult situations.
  • hotar: Referring to the priest or the one who offers sacrifices, this term signifies the importance of rituals and spiritual practices in seeking help and guidance.
  • yaviṣṭha: This means "the best" or "the most excellent." It indicates a pursuit of the highest ideals and virtues in life.
  • sukrato: This translates to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." It emphasizes the significance of ethical behavior and moral integrity.
  • damūnāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with restraint" or "with control." It highlights the importance of self-discipline and moderation in one's actions.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects a deep yearning for relief from suffering and the importance of kinship and communication in overcoming challenges. It emphasizes the need for guidance from wise individuals, particularly in times of distress. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as seeking support from family and friends during tough times, valuing the advice of mentors, and striving for ethical conduct in our actions. It serves as a reminder to maintain self-control and to pursue excellence in our endeavors, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
