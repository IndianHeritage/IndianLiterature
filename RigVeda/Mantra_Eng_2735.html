मिहः पावकाः प्रतताऽभूवन स्वस्ति नः पिपृहि पारम् आसाम् |इन्द्र त्वं रथिरः पाहि नो ऋषो मक्षू-मक्षू कृणुहि गोजितो नः ||
mihaḥ pāvakāḥ pratatā abhūvan svasti naḥ pipṛhi pāram āsām | indra tvaṁ rathiraḥ pāhi no riṣo makṣū-makṣū kṛṇuhi gojito naḥ ||
  • mihaḥ: This word translates to "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the environment we inhabit.
  • pāvakāḥ: Meaning "purifiers" or "sacred," this term refers to the forces or elements that cleanse and elevate our existence.
  • pratatā: This word means "spread" or "extended," indicating the vastness of the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • abhūvan: Translated as "they have become," it suggests transformation and the evolution of existence.
  • svasti: This term means "well-being" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of health and harmony in life.
  • naḥ: Meaning "to us," it indicates a collective aspect, highlighting the community and shared experiences.
  • pipṛhi: This word translates to "nourish" or "sustain," pointing to the need for support and sustenance in our lives.
  • pāram: Meaning "beyond" or "transcendent," it refers to higher states of being or consciousness.
  • āsām: This word means "of us," reinforcing the idea of unity and shared purpose.
  • indra: A reference to the deity Indra, symbolizing strength, power, and protection.
  • tvaṁ: Meaning "you," it personalizes the invocation, addressing the divine directly.
  • rathiraḥ: This term translates to "charioteer," symbolizing guidance and direction in life.
  • pāhi: Meaning "protect," it expresses a plea for safety and security.
  • no: This word means "us," again emphasizing the collective aspect of the prayer.
  • riṣo: Translated as "anger" or "hostility," it acknowledges the challenges and adversities we face.
  • makṣū-makṣū: This term refers to "obstacles" or "hindrances," indicating the difficulties that can arise in our path.
  • kṛṇuhi: Meaning "make" or "create," it calls for action and intervention.
  • gojito: This word translates to "well-being" or "prosperity," reinforcing the desire for a flourishing life.
  • naḥ: Again meaning "to us," it concludes the mantra with a focus on the collective.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of purification, well-being, and divine guidance in our lives. It acknowledges the challenges we face and calls upon the divine for protection and sustenance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of uncertainty or difficulty, reminding us to seek support from our community and the divine. It encourages us to focus on the present, nurture our well-being, and strive for a harmonious existence. The significance lies in its universal message of interconnectedness, resilience, and the pursuit of a prosperous life, making it relevant in various aspects of our daily experiences.
