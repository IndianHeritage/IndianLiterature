यūयम् मर्तं विपण्यवः प्रणेता इति धियā |श्रौतारो यामहूतिषु ||
yūyam martaṁ vipanyavaḥ praṇetāra itthā dhiyā | śrotāro yāmahūtiṣu ||
  • yūyam: This word translates to "you" or "all of you," indicating a collective group, often referring to the audience or the community.
  • martaṁ: This means "the mortal" or "the one who is subject to death," highlighting the transient nature of human life.
  • vipanyavaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "those who have knowledge," suggesting a group that possesses understanding and insight.
  • praṇetāra: This translates to "the leaders" or "the guides," referring to those who lead or direct others, often in a spiritual or moral context.
  • itthā: This word means "thus" or "in this way," indicating a manner or method of action.
  • dhiyā: This translates to "with wisdom" or "with intellect," emphasizing the importance of thoughtful consideration and understanding in actions.
  • śrotāro: This means "the hearers" or "the listeners," referring to those who are receptive to knowledge and teachings.
  • yāmahūtiṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the offerings" or "in the invocations," suggesting a context of rituals or prayers where offerings are made to the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the community, urging them to recognize their mortal nature while also emphasizing the importance of wisdom and guidance in navigating life. It highlights the role of leaders and wise individuals in directing the community towards meaningful actions and decisions, especially in spiritual or ritual contexts.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Leadership: Leaders in any community, whether in a workplace, a religious organization, or a social group, can embody the qualities of wisdom and guidance as they help others navigate challenges.
  • Education: Teachers and mentors play a crucial role in imparting knowledge and wisdom to students, guiding them through their learning journey.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals and prayers, the collective participation of individuals, guided by wise leaders, can create a sense of unity and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, leadership, and community in the face of life's transience. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and guidance, fostering a supportive environment where everyone can thrive together. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, promoting a harmonious balance between mortality and the pursuit of wisdom.
