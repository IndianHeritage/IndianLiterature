स त्वं न इन्द्र धियसानो अर्कैर हरिणां वृषण योक्त्रम अश्रेः |या इथ्था मगवन्न अनु जोषं वक्षो अभि प्रार्यः साक्षि जनान ||
sa tvaṁ na indra dhiyasāno arkair harīṇāṁ vṛṣan yoktram aśreḥ | yā itthā maghavann anu joṣaṁ vakṣo abhi prāryaḥ sakṣi janān ||
  • sa tvaṁ: This phrase translates to "you" or "that you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • na indra: "Not Indra" suggests a distinction or a specific quality that is being highlighted, possibly referring to a different aspect of divinity or a different deity altogether.
  • dhiyasāno: This term can be understood as "one who is seated in wisdom" or "the wise one," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in divine actions.
  • arkair: Meaning "with hymns" or "with praises," this word signifies the act of worship and the power of sound and words in spiritual practices.
  • harīṇāṁ: This translates to "of the horses," which can symbolize strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with divine chariots or the sun.
  • vṛṣan: This word means "the strong" or "the powerful," reinforcing the idea of strength and might in the context of divinity.
  • yoktram: Referring to "the harness" or "the yoke," this term can symbolize the connection between the divine and the earthly, or the control of powerful forces.
  • aśreḥ: This means "of the shelter" or "of the refuge," indicating a place of safety and protection provided by the divine.
  • yā itthā: Translated as "who thus," this phrase introduces a description of the divine qualities or actions that follow.
  • maghavann: This term refers to "the mighty one," often used to describe Indra or a powerful deity, emphasizing strength and capability.
  • anu joṣaṁ: Meaning "following the delight" or "in pursuit of joy," this phrase suggests a journey or quest for happiness and fulfillment.
  • vakṣo: This translates to "the chest" or "the heart," symbolizing the core of emotions and intentions.
  • abhi prāryaḥ: Meaning "having been invoked," this phrase indicates the act of calling upon the divine for assistance or blessings.
  • sakṣi janān: This translates to "the witness of people," suggesting a divine presence that observes and interacts with humanity.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and divine connection in the pursuit of joy and fulfillment. It highlights the role of divine entities in guiding and protecting individuals on their life journeys.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking guidance in decision-making, invoking strength during challenging times, or finding joy in the pursuit of one's goals. For instance, when facing a difficult situation at work, one might recite this mantra to invoke wisdom and strength, reminding themselves of the divine support available to them.
