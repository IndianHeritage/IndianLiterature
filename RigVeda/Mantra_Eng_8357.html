ऋतस्य तन्तुर विततः पवित्र आ जिह्वायाः अग्रे वरुणस्य मायया | धीराश्चित तत समिनक्षन्त आषतात्रा कर्तम अव पदात्य अप्रभुः ||
ṛtasya tantur vitataḥ pavitra ā jihvāyā agre varuṇasya māyayā | dhīrāś cit tat saminakṣanta āśatātrā kartam ava padāty aprabhuḥ ||
  • ṛtasya: This word refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." In real life, it can be related to the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions.
  • tantur: This means "thread" or "connection." It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, much like how our actions can have far-reaching effects on others.
  • vitataḥ: This translates to "spread out" or "extended." It signifies the vastness of truth and order in the universe, reminding us that our understanding of reality is often limited.
  • pavitra: Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this word emphasizes the importance of purity in thought and action, akin to how we strive for cleanliness in our environment and relationships.
  • ā jihvāyā: This refers to "the tongue" or "speech." It highlights the power of words and communication, similar to how our words can uplift or harm others.
  • agre: Meaning "in front" or "before," it suggests the importance of foresight and planning in our endeavors, much like how we prepare for future challenges.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "Varuna," the deity associated with water and cosmic order. It symbolizes the natural laws that govern our existence, akin to how we respect nature and its resources.
  • māyayā: This means "by illusion" or "through the veil." It reminds us of the deceptive nature of appearances, similar to how we often misjudge situations based on superficial observations.
  • dhīrāś: This translates to "the wise" or "the prudent." It emphasizes the value of wisdom in navigating life's complexities, much like how experienced individuals guide us through difficult times.
  • cit: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it highlights the importance of being mindful and present in our actions, similar to how meditation helps us focus and reflect.
  • tat: This means "that" or "it," referring to the essence of truth and order that we seek to understand.
  • saminakṣanta: This translates to "they who see together" or "those who perceive." It signifies the collective understanding and shared vision among individuals, much like teamwork in achieving common goals.
  • āśatātrā: This means "in the realm of protection" or "in the shelter." It emphasizes the need for safety and security in our lives, akin to how we create safe spaces for ourselves and others.
  • kartam: This translates to "the doer" or "the creator." It signifies the importance of action and responsibility in shaping our destinies, similar to how our choices define our paths.
  • ava: Meaning "down" or "below," it suggests humility and grounding in our pursuits, much like how we should remain connected to our roots.
  • padāty: This means "footsteps" or "path." It symbolizes the journey we undertake in life, akin to how every step we take leads us toward our goals.
  • aprabhuḥ: This translates to "not powerful" or "without authority." It serves as a reminder of the limitations we face and the importance of humility in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, interconnectedness, and wisdom in our lives. It teaches us to communicate mindfully, act with integrity, and remain aware of the cosmic order that governs our existence. In real-life scenarios, we can apply these principles by fostering honest relationships, being responsible in our actions, and respecting the natural world around us. Ultimately, it encourages us to seek a deeper understanding of ourselves and our place in the universe, guiding us toward a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
