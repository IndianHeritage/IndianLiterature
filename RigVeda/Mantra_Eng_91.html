गायन्ति त्वा गायत्रिणो 'र्चन्त्य अर्कम् अर्किणः |ब्रह्माणस्त्वा शतक्रत उद वंशम् इव येमिरे ||
gāyanti tvā gāyatriṇo 'rcanty arkam arkiṇaḥ | brahmāṇas tvā śatakrata ud vaṁśam iva yemire ||
  • gāyanti tvā gāyatriṇo: This phrase translates to "the Gayatris sing to you." The Gayatri mantra is one of the most revered mantras in Hinduism, symbolizing the essence of the Vedas. It represents the divine light of knowledge and wisdom.
  • rcanty arkam arkiṇaḥ: This means "the singers (or priests) offer you the light." Here, "arkam" refers to light or energy, and "arkiṇaḥ" indicates those who are engaged in the act of offering. This can be seen as a metaphor for the illumination of knowledge and the pursuit of truth.
  • brahmāṇas tvā śatakrata: This translates to "the Brahmanas (the learned or wise) invoke you, O hundred-powered one." "Brahmanas" refers to the knowledgeable individuals who understand the deeper meanings of the Vedas, while "śatakrata" signifies someone with immense power or capability, often associated with Lord Indra.
  • ud vaṁśam iva yemire: This means "they (the wise) have approached you like a lineage." This suggests a connection to tradition and the passing down of wisdom through generations, emphasizing the importance of learning and spiritual heritage.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and the divine light that guides individuals on their spiritual journey. It emphasizes the role of learned individuals in invoking higher powers and the importance of tradition in understanding and passing down spiritual truths. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, where the pursuit of knowledge is paramount, or in spiritual practices where individuals seek guidance from learned mentors. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, tradition, and divine guidance in our lives.
