तुभ्यं ब्रह्माणि गिर इन्द्र तुभ्यं सत्रा दधिरे हरिवो जुषस्व |बोध्य आपीर अवसो नूतनस्य सखे वसो जरित्रिभ्यो वयो धाः ||
tubhyam brahmāṇi gira indra tubhyaṁ satrā dadhire harivo juṣasva | bodhy ā3pir avaso nūtanasya sakhe vaso jaritṛbhyo vayo dhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyam: to you
    • brahmāṇi: the divine or the ultimate reality
    • gira: speech or words
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • satrā: the sacred gathering or assembly
    • dadhire: they have offered
    • harivo: O divine beings
    • juṣasva: may you enjoy
    • bodhy: awaken or inspire
    • āpir: to us
    • avaso: dwelling or residing
    • nūtanasya: of the new or fresh
    • sakhe: O friend
    • vaso: dwelling
    • jaritṛbhyo: to the elders or ancestors
    • vayo: our age or time
    • dhāḥ: may you bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the king of gods, asking for divine blessings and inspiration.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective offerings during sacred gatherings.
    • The invocation of "harivo" signifies a call to divine beings, suggesting a connection to higher spiritual realms.
    • The plea for awakening and inspiration reflects a desire for knowledge and enlightenment, which is essential in both personal and communal growth.
    • The reference to "elders" highlights the respect for tradition and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be applied during community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, where collective prayers are offered for peace and prosperity.
    • In educational settings, invoking the spirit of inspiration and knowledge can be seen in the way teachers encourage students to seek wisdom and understanding.
    • In family gatherings, honoring elders and seeking their blessings can strengthen familial bonds and ensure the transmission of values and traditions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of seeking divine guidance.
    • It highlights the significance of respecting traditions and the wisdom of elders, which is crucial for personal and societal development.
    • In essence, the mantra encourages us to cultivate a spirit of unity, respect, and aspiration in our daily lives, making it relevant in various real-life scenarios.
