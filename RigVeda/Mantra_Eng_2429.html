आ वाम उपस्थं अद्रुहा देवाः सिदन्तु यज्ञियाः |इहाद्य सोमपीतये ||
ā vām upastham adruhā devāḥ sīdantu yajñiyāḥ | ihādya somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "ā vām upastham adruhā devāḥ sīdantu yajñiyāḥ | ihādya somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: "May the divine beings who are not hostile, sit here for the purpose of the sacrifice, for the enjoyment of the Soma today."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā vām: "to you" or "for you" - indicating a direction towards the divine beings.
    • upastham: "to sit" or "to be present" - a call for the divine to be present during the ritual.
    • adruhā: "not hostile" - emphasizing the peaceful and benevolent nature of the divine beings being invoked.
    • devāḥ: "gods" or "divine beings" - referring to the celestial entities that are being called upon.
    • sīdantu: "may they sit" - a request for the divine beings to take their place in the ritual.
    • yajñiyāḥ: "for the sacrifice" - indicating the purpose of the gathering, which is a sacred offering.
    • ihādya: "today" - specifying the time of the ritual, emphasizing its immediacy.
    • somapītaye: "for the enjoyment of the Soma" - referring to the sacred drink, Soma, which is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes divine bliss and immortality.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies to invite positive energies and divine presence, similar to how people might light candles or incense to create a sacred space.
    • In community gatherings, such as weddings or festivals, invoking a peaceful and benevolent atmosphere can be seen as a way to ensure harmony among participants, akin to the invocation of non-hostile divine beings.
    • In personal meditation or prayer, individuals may use this mantra to focus on attracting positive influences and dispelling negativity from their lives, reflecting the essence of seeking divine support.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence and support during sacred rituals. It emphasizes the importance of peace and benevolence in spiritual practices. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts where individuals seek to create a harmonious environment, whether in personal meditation, community gatherings, or during significant life events. By invoking the divine in a non-hostile manner, practitioners can foster a sense of unity and positive energy, enhancing the overall experience of the ritual or gathering.
