अभि प्रियाम् मरुतो याऽवो अश्व्या हव्याः मित्र प्रयाथनम् |आ बर्हिर इन्द्रो वरुणस्तुरान नरा आदित्याशः सदन्तु नः ||
abhi priyā maruto yā vo aśvyā havyā mitra prayāthana | ā barhir indro varuṇas turā nara ādityāsaḥ sadantu naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • priyā: beloved
    • maruto: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • yā: who
    • vo: your
    • aśvyā: swift
    • havyā: offerings
    • mitra: O Mitra (the god of friendship and contracts)
    • prayāthana: striving or effort
    • ā: may
    • barhir: on the ground
    • indro: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • varuṇas: O Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • turā: swift
    • nara: O man
    • ādityāsaḥ: sons of Aditi (the celestial beings)
    • sadantu: may they be favorable
    • naḥ: to us
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Maruts, Indra, Varuna, and the celestial beings, asking for their blessings and support.
    • It emphasizes the importance of striving towards beloved goals and the need for divine assistance in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek the support of mentors or colleagues (akin to the divine beings) to achieve their career goals.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to nurture friendships and seek harmony, much like the god Mitra represents.
    • During challenging times, people may turn to spiritual practices or community support, reflecting the call for divine intervention and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support.
    • It teaches us to strive for our goals while recognizing the importance of seeking help from others and the universe.
    • In real life, it encourages collaboration, friendship, and the pursuit of harmony in all aspects of life.
