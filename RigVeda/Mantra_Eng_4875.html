प्र वायुम् अच् छा बृहती मनिषा बृहद्रयं विश्ववारणं रथप्राम् |द्युतद्यामा नियुतः पत्यमानः कविः कविम् इयक्षसि प्रयज्यो ||
pra vāyum acchā bṛhatī manīṣā bṛhadrayiṁ viśvavāraṁ rathaprām | dyutadyāmā niyutaḥ patyamānaḥ kaviḥ kavim iyakṣasi prayajyo ||
  • pra vāyum acchā: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the vastness of the wind." In real life, this can symbolize the importance of freedom and the ability to move and adapt in various situations, much like the wind that flows freely across the earth.
  • bṛhatī manīṣā: This translates to "the great intellect" or "the expansive wisdom." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and understanding in our lives. For example, in a workplace, having a team with diverse knowledge can lead to innovative solutions and better decision-making.
  • bṛhadrayiṁ viśvavāraṁ: This can be understood as "the great support of the universe." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the support we receive from the universe. In real life, this can be seen in community support during challenging times, where people come together to help one another.
  • rathaprām: This term refers to "the chariot of progress." It symbolizes the journey of life and the importance of moving forward. In practical terms, this can relate to personal growth, where individuals strive to achieve their goals and aspirations, much like a chariot moving towards its destination.
  • dyutadyāmā niyutaḥ: This phrase means "the shining brilliance of the heavens." It represents inspiration and the pursuit of higher ideals. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of excellence in various fields, such as art, science, or sports, where individuals aim to shine and inspire others.
  • patyamānaḥ: This translates to "being guided." It emphasizes the importance of guidance in our lives, whether it be from mentors, teachers, or even our own experiences. For instance, students often rely on teachers for guidance in their academic journey.
  • kaviḥ kavim: This means "the poet of poets" or "the ultimate creator." It highlights the significance of creativity and expression in human life. In real life, this can be seen in how artists, writers, and musicians express their thoughts and emotions, influencing society and culture.
  • iyakṣasi prayajyo: This phrase can be interpreted as "you who are worthy of worship." It signifies reverence for the divine or the higher powers in our lives. In practical terms, this can relate to the respect and gratitude we show towards nature, our parents, or anyone who has positively impacted our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, guidance, creativity, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in reminding us to seek knowledge, support one another, and strive for progress while being guided by higher ideals. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community building, and the pursuit of excellence in various fields, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
