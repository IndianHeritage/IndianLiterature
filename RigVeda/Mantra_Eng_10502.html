प्र देवं देव्या धियā भरतā जातवेदसम् |हव्यā नो वक्षद āनुषक ||
pra devaṁ devyā dhiyā bharatā jātavedasam | havyā no vakṣad ānuṣak ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • devaṁ: the divine, god
    • devyā: by the goddess, divine feminine
    • dhiyā: with intellect, understanding
    • bharatā: the one who is worthy, the one who carries
    • jātavedasam: the one who knows all births, the fire god (Agni)
    • havyā: offerings, that which is to be offered
    • no: our
    • vakṣad: may it be spoken, may it be expressed
    • ānuṣak: may it be accepted, may it be received
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, asking for guidance and understanding through the intellect.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the divine feminine and the role of Agni, the fire god, in our lives.
    • The phrase "may it be spoken" suggests a desire for clear communication and expression of thoughts and intentions.
    • It also highlights the significance of offerings, both material and spiritual, in seeking blessings from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one might seek guidance from a mentor or a wise figure, akin to invoking the divine for clarity and understanding.
    • In moments of decision-making, reflecting on one's intentions and seeking divine guidance can lead to better outcomes.
    • Offering gratitude through small acts of kindness or rituals can be seen as a way to honor the divine and seek blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and understanding through intellect and offerings.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, such as decision-making, seeking clarity, and expressing gratitude.
    • By invoking the divine, individuals can align their actions with higher principles and foster a deeper connection with the spiritual realm.
