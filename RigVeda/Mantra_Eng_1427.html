परिज्मानम् इव द्यां होतारं चर्षणीनाम् |शोचिष्केशं वृषणं यम् इमा विशः प्रावन्तु जूतये विशः ||
parijmānam iva dyāṁ hotāraṁ carṣaṇīnām | śociṣkeśaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ yam imā viśaḥ prāvantu jūtaye viśaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "parijmānam iva dyāṁ hotāraṁ carṣaṇīnām |
  • Translation: "Like the encompassing sky, the hotar (priest) of the offerings, who is adorned with the radiance of the sun, may he bring forth the blessings of the universe."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Parijmānam: Encompassing or surrounding.
    • Dyāṁ: Sky or heavens.
    • Hotāraṁ: The priest or one who offers sacrifices.
    • Carṣaṇīnām: Of the offerings or sacrifices.
    • Śociṣkeśaṁ: Radiant or shining.
    • Vṛṣaṇaṁ: One who brings forth or produces.
    • Yam: Who.
    • Imā: These.
    • Viśaḥ: Beings or creatures.
    • Prāvantu: May they come forth.
    • Jūtaye: For the purpose of offering or sacrifice.

  • Real-Life Example:
    • In a community gathering, a leader or elder often takes on the role of a "hotar," guiding the group in rituals or ceremonies that foster unity and shared purpose, much like the priest in the mantra.
    • During a wedding ceremony, the officiant invokes blessings from the universe, symbolizing the encompassing nature of the sky and the radiance of the sun, which is a reflection of the divine presence in the event.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of spiritual leadership and the role of rituals in connecting individuals to the greater universe.
    • It signifies that through collective offerings and the guidance of a wise leader, communities can harness blessings and foster harmony.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various communal activities, such as festivals, weddings, and other significant events where collective intention and spiritual guidance are sought for prosperity and well-being.
