जोषा सवितर यस्य ते हरः शतं सवाम् आर्हति |पाहि नो दिद्युतः पतन्त्याः ||
joṣā savitar yasya te haraḥ śataṁ savām̐ arhati | pāhi no didyutaḥ patantyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • joṣā: The one who brings joy or happiness.
    • savitā: The Sun or the divine source of energy.
    • yasya: Whose.
    • te: Your.
    • haraḥ: The one who removes or takes away.
    • śataṁ: A hundred.
    • savam: Offerings or sacrifices.
    • arhati: Deserves or is worthy of.
    • pāhi: Protect or guard.
    • no: Us.
    • didyutaḥ: The one who shines or radiates.
    • patantyāḥ: Falling or descending.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Savitar, the Sun God, who is seen as a source of life and energy.
    • The speaker acknowledges the joy and vitality that Savitar brings into life.
    • It expresses a desire for protection and blessings, asking Savitar to guard and bestow his radiance upon us.
    • The mention of "a hundred offerings" signifies the abundance and gratitude one should have towards the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate to this mantra by recognizing the importance of the Sun in agriculture, as it provides the energy necessary for crops to grow, thus sustaining life.
    • People often seek the warmth and positivity of the Sun during winter months, which can be seen as a metaphor for seeking joy and protection in life.
    • In moments of difficulty, individuals may turn to nature, particularly the Sun, for solace and strength, reflecting the protective aspect of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine energy and the joy it brings into our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to be grateful for the blessings we receive and to seek protection and guidance from higher powers.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to connect with nature and recognize the interdependence of life, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence.
