याभी रेब्हं निवृतं सितम अद्भ्य उद वन्दनम् ऐरयतं स्वर दृष्टे |याभिः कण्वं प्र सिषासन्तम् आवतं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतम् ||
yābhī rebhaṁ nivṛtaṁ sitam adbhya ud vandanam airayataṁ svar dṛśe | yābhiḥ kaṇvam pra siṣāsantam āvataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhī: By whom
    • rebhaṁ: The shining one
    • nivṛtaṁ: Having been turned away
    • sitam: White or pure
    • adbhya: From the waters
    • ud: Up
    • vandanam: Salutation or worship
    • airayataṁ: To the one who is invoked
    • svar: Sound or voice
    • dṛśe: To the sight
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • kaṇvam: The small one or the minute
    • pra: Forth
    • siṣāsantam: Who is to be protected
    • āvataṁ: The one who is sheltered
    • tābhir: By those
    • ū: O
    • ṣu: Good
    • ūtibhir: With strength
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin gods)
    • gatam: Having gone
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.
    • The phrase "yābhī rebhaṁ nivṛtaṁ sitam" suggests a call to the divine for purity and clarity, perhaps in the context of seeking healing or enlightenment.
    • The mention of "adbhya ud vandanam" indicates a connection to the waters, which can symbolize life, nourishment, and the cleansing of impurities.
    • In a real-life scenario, this can be likened to seeking help from healthcare professionals when one is unwell, emphasizing the importance of purity in both body and mind.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins highlights the significance of divine intervention in human affairs, particularly in matters of health and vitality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms, particularly in the pursuit of health and well-being.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking help and guidance from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or the support of community and healthcare.
    • In modern life, this can be applied by recognizing the need for holistic approaches to health, integrating physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages individuals to remain open to divine assistance and to honor the purity and sanctity of life.
