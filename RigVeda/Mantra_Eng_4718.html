वरिष्ठो अस्य दक्षिणाम इयर्तीन्द्रो मगोनां तुविकूर्मितमः |यया वज्रिवः पर्यास्य अंहो मगहा च धृष्णो दयसे वि सूरीन् ||
variṣṭho asya dakṣiṇām iyartīndro maghonāṁ tuvikūrmitamaḥ | yayā vajrivaḥ pariyāsy aṁho maghā ca dhṛṣṇo dayase vi sūrīn ||
  • Variṣṭho: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the supreme aspect of something.
  • Asya: Meaning "of this" or "of him," it indicates possession or relation to the subject being discussed.
  • Dakṣiṇām: This refers to "the southern" direction, which in Vedic literature often symbolizes prosperity and auspiciousness.
  • Iyartīndro: This can be interpreted as "the one who moves or travels," often referring to Indra, the king of gods, who is associated with strength and power.
  • Maghonāṁ: Meaning "of the wealthy" or "of the rich," it emphasizes abundance and prosperity.
  • Tuvikūrmitamaḥ: This term suggests "the most well-formed" or "the most perfect," indicating a state of completeness or excellence.
  • Yayā: Translated as "by which," it connects the previous ideas to the action or result that follows.
  • Vajrivaḥ: This refers to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," a metaphor for strength and invincibility, often associated with Indra.
  • Pariyāsy: Meaning "to surround" or "to encompass," it indicates protection or support.
  • Aṁho: This word can mean "of the heavens" or "of the sky," suggesting a divine or celestial aspect.
  • Maghā: Referring to "wealth" or "prosperity," it emphasizes the importance of abundance in life.
  • Ca: Meaning "and," it connects ideas or elements within the mantra.
  • Dhṛṣṇo: This term signifies "of the brave" or "of the strong," highlighting courage and valor.
  • Dayase: Meaning "of compassion" or "of kindness," it emphasizes the importance of benevolence in one's actions.
  • Vi: This can mean "in" or "with," indicating the manner in which something is done.
  • Sūrīn: Referring to "the heroes" or "the strong ones," it highlights the qualities of bravery and strength.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of excellence, prosperity, strength, and compassion. It calls upon the divine qualities of Indra, urging individuals to embody these traits in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, where one must be strong and compassionate, or in personal development, where striving for excellence and abundance is essential. By invoking these qualities, individuals can create a positive impact in their communities and lead fulfilling lives.
