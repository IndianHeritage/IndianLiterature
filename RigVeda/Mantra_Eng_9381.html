यत् ते विश्वम् इदं जगन् मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत् त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जीवसे ||
yat te viśvam idaṁ jagan mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • yat te viśvam idaṁ jagan: This phrase translates to "Whatever is this universe, it belongs to you." It emphasizes the idea that the entire cosmos is interconnected and is a manifestation of a higher power or consciousness.
  • mano jagāma dūrakam: This means "the mind has gone far away." It suggests that human thoughts and perceptions can often stray from the essence of reality, leading to a disconnect from the universal truth.
  • tat ta ā vartayāmasīha: This translates to "That we shall bring back." It indicates a desire to return to the original state of unity and understanding with the universe.
  • kṣayāya jīvase: This means "for the purpose of life." It highlights that the ultimate goal of this return is to enhance and enrich our lives.

In essence, this mantra reflects the profound relationship between the individual and the universe. It calls for a recognition of our connection to the cosmos and encourages us to realign our thoughts and actions with this understanding.


For example, in real life, when individuals feel overwhelmed or disconnected, practices such as meditation or mindfulness can help them reconnect with their inner selves and the world around them. This mantra serves as a reminder that despite the distractions of daily life, we can always return to a state of awareness and unity.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and connection to the universe. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, mental health practices, and spiritual growth. By recognizing our place in the cosmos and striving to align our thoughts with this understanding, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
