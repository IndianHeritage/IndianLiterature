इन्द्र जामय उता ये 'जामयो 'र्वाचीनासो वनुषो युयुज्रे |त्वम् एषां विथुराशवांसी जहि वृष्ण्यानी कृणुही पराचः ||
indra jāmaya uta ye 'jāmayo 'rvācīnāso vanuṣo yuyujre | tvam eṣāṁ vithurā śavāṁsi jahi vṛṣṇyāni kṛṇuhī parācaḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Jāmaya: This term refers to the offspring or progeny. It signifies the importance of lineage and the continuation of life.
  • Uta: This word means "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to the previous thought.
  • Ye: This means "those" or "who," referring to a group of beings or entities.
  • 'Jāmayo: Similar to "jāmaya," it emphasizes the idea of progeny or descendants.
  • 'Rvācīnāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "those who have knowledge," highlighting the value of wisdom.
  • Vanuṣo: This refers to "the beings" or "the creatures," indicating a connection to all living entities.
  • Yuyujre: This means "they have united" or "they have come together," suggesting harmony and cooperation.
  • Tvam: This means "you," addressing a divine or powerful entity, likely Indra in this context.
  • Eṣāṁ: This means "of these" or "of those," referring to the beings mentioned earlier.
  • Vithurā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who provides" or "the giver," emphasizing the role of a benefactor.
  • Śavāṁsi: This refers to "the bodies" or "the forms," indicating the physical existence of beings.
  • Jahi: This means "to conquer" or "to overcome," suggesting a call to action against obstacles.
  • Vṛṣṇyāni: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," often associated with strength and valor.
  • Kṛṇuhī: This means "to create" or "to make," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • Parācaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the highest" or "the supreme," suggesting a connection to the ultimate truth or reality.

The mantra essentially calls upon Indra to support and empower the beings who are wise and united. It emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and cooperation among individuals. The invocation of Indra signifies a request for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving harmony.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra emphasizes unity, it can inspire communities to come together for a common cause, fostering cooperation and strength.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from the qualities of Indra, embodying strength and wisdom to guide their teams through challenges.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the importance of wisdom and strength in their own lives, striving to overcome personal obstacles and support others.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of unity, strength, and wisdom in overcoming challenges. It encourages individuals and communities to seek divine support while fostering cooperation and resilience in their endeavors.
