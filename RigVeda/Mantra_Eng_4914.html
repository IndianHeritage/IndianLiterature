अप त्यं वृजिनं रिपुं स्तेनम अग्ने दुराध्यम् | दविष्टम् अस्य सत्पते कृधī सुगम् ||
apa tyaṁ vṛjinaṁ ripuṁ stenam agne durādhyam | daviṣṭham asya satpate kṛdhī sugam ||
  • apa tyaṁ: This phrase translates to "remove this" or "take away this." It signifies a request for the removal of obstacles or negativity.
  • vṛjinaṁ: This word means "sin" or "fault." It represents the negative aspects or impurities that hinder progress.
  • ripuṁ: This translates to "enemy." In a broader sense, it can refer to any adversarial force or challenge that one faces in life.
  • stenam: This means "thief" or "deceiver." It symbolizes deceitful forces that can lead one astray or cause harm.
  • agne: This refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • durādhyam: This translates to "difficult to approach" or "hard to attain." It signifies challenges that are formidable and require effort to overcome.
  • daviṣṭham: This means "to be desired" or "worthy of worship." It indicates the qualities or goals that are noble and should be aspired to.
  • asya: This translates to "of this" or "of this situation." It refers to the context in which the mantra is being invoked.
  • satpate: This means "the lord of truth" or "the one who is righteous." It signifies the ultimate truth or the guiding principle in life.
  • kṛdhī: This translates to "may you create" or "may you make." It is a call to action, urging the divine to manifest positive outcomes.
  • sugam: This means "easy" or "pleasant." It signifies the desired state of ease and comfort in life.

In essence, this mantra is a plea for the removal of obstacles, deceit, and negativity, while invoking the divine energy of Agni to create a path towards truth and ease. It acknowledges the challenges one faces and seeks divine assistance in overcoming them.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal applicability to life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, individuals often encounter obstacles, whether they be personal struggles, professional challenges, or moral dilemmas. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset focused on overcoming adversity, seeking truth, and striving for a harmonious existence. It serves as a reminder to seek clarity and support from higher energies, encouraging individuals to confront their "enemies" and "thieves" of peace with courage and determination.
