ते हि dyāvāpṛthivī मातरā महī देवी देवाञ जनमनā यज्ञिये इतः |उभे बिभृत उभयम् भरīmabhiḥ पुरū रेतांसी पितृभिः च सिञ्चतः ||
te hi dyāvāpṛthivī mātarā mahī devī devāñ janmanā yajñiye itaḥ | ubhe bibhṛta ubhayam bharīmabhiḥ purū retāṁsi pitṛbhiś ca siñcataḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, the Earth and the Sky are our divine mothers, the goddesses who give birth to the gods through sacrifice. They both support us, and they nourish us with abundant resources, just as our ancestors shower us with blessings."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te hi: indeed, they
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: sky (dyau) and earth (pṛthivī)
    • mātarā: as mothers
    • mahī: great, vast
    • devī: goddess
    • devāñ: gods
    • janmanā: through birth
    • yajñiye: through sacrifice
    • itaḥ: from here
    • ubhe: both
    • bibhṛta: support, sustain
    • ubhayam: both of them
    • bharīmabhiḥ: with abundant resources
    • purū: many, plentiful
    • retāṁsi: seeds, progeny
    • pitṛbhiś: with ancestors
    • ca: and
    • siñcataḥ: nourish, shower
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of Earth and Sky as divine mothers can be seen in various cultures where nature is revered. For instance, many indigenous tribes perform rituals to honor the Earth and Sky, acknowledging their role in providing sustenance and life.
    • In modern agriculture, sustainable practices reflect the idea of nurturing the Earth, ensuring that it continues to provide resources for future generations, akin to the blessings of ancestors.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the interconnectedness of all life, similar to the mantra's message of support and nourishment from both the Earth and Sky.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and honoring the natural world as a source of life and sustenance. It teaches us to appreciate the resources provided by the Earth and Sky, urging us to engage in practices that respect and protect our environment. In real life, this can manifest in sustainable living, environmental conservation, and a deep respect for nature, reminding us that we are all interconnected and reliant on these divine forces for our existence and well-being.
