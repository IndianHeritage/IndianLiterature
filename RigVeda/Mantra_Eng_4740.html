प्रत्यस्मै पिपीषते विश्वानि विदुषे भर |अरङ्गमाय जग्मये 'पश्चाद्दघ्वने नरे ||
praty asmai pipīṣate viśvāni viduṣe bhara | araṁgamāya jagmaye 'paścāddaghvane nare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • praty: towards, in the direction of
    • asmai: to him, to that person
    • pipīṣate: may be filled, may be satisfied
    • viśvāni: all, everything
    • viduṣe: for the wise, for the knowledgeable
    • bhara: bring, provide
    • araṁgamāya: for the one who is moving forward
    • jagmaye: may go, may proceed
    • paścāddaghvane: after the sacrifice, after the offering
    • nare: to the man, to the human
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of providing abundance and fulfillment to those who are wise and knowledgeable.
    • It emphasizes the importance of moving forward in life, suggesting that progress and action are essential.
    • The phrase "after the sacrifice" indicates that rewards and benefits come after effort and dedication.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a person who works hard and demonstrates knowledge in their field often receives promotions and recognition. This aligns with the idea of being filled with abundance after putting in the effort.
    • In education, students who dedicate themselves to their studies and seek knowledge are often rewarded with good grades and opportunities for further advancement.
    • In personal relationships, those who invest time and effort into nurturing their connections often find deeper and more fulfilling relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to pursue knowledge and take action, assuring that fulfillment and abundance will follow.
    • It serves as a reminder that success is often the result of hard work and dedication, and that wise individuals are rewarded for their efforts.
    • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including career, education, and personal growth, reinforcing the idea that progress is achievable through commitment and wisdom.
