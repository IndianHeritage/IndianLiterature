यत्र द्वाव् इव जगहनाधिषवण्याः कृताः |उलूकहलसुतानां अवेद व इन्द्र जलगुलः ||
yatra dvāv iva jaghanādhiṣavaṇyā kṛtā | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yatra dvāv iva jaghanādhiṣavaṇyā kṛtā | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Where two are like the two who are bound together, may Indra, the strong, protect us from the evil of the owl's nest."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yatra: where
    • dvāv: two
    • iva: like
    • jaghanādhiṣavaṇyā: bound together
    • kṛtā: made or created
    • ulūkhalasutānām: of the owl's nest
    • aved: may protect
    • v: and
    • indra: Indra, the deity
    • jalgulaḥ: strong or powerful
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of unity and protection. The reference to "two" suggests a partnership or a bond that is essential for strength.
    • The "owl's nest" symbolizes danger or negativity, as owls are often associated with omens or misfortune in various cultures.
    • Indra, as a powerful deity, represents the force that can protect individuals from harm and negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In relationships, the mantra can be interpreted as a reminder of the strength found in partnership. For instance, a couple facing challenges together can draw strength from their bond, much like the two mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to teamwork. When individuals come together with a common goal, they can overcome obstacles that may seem daunting alone.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to seek protection from negative influences, akin to avoiding toxic environments or relationships that can hinder growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity and the protective power of strong bonds. It serves as a reminder that together, individuals can face challenges and ward off negativity.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or individual growth, the principles of partnership and protection are universally applicable.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to seek strength in unity and to rely on protective forces, both divine and communal, to navigate life's challenges.
