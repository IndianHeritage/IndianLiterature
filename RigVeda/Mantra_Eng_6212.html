कदागच्छाथ मरुत इत्त्था विप्रं हवमानम् |मार्दीकैभिर नाधमानम् ||
kadā gacchātha maruta itthā vipraṁ havamānam | mārḍīkebhir nādhamānam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā: When
    • gacchātha: Will you go
    • maruta: O Winds (or Storms)
    • itthā: Thus
    • vipraṁ: The learned or wise one
    • havamānam: Being invoked or called upon
    • mārḍīkebhir: With the sounds or voices
    • nādhamānam: Not being disturbed or agitated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the Maruts, the divine winds or storm deities in Vedic literature.
    • The speaker is inquiring when the Maruts will come to the wise one who is being invoked.
    • The phrase "with the sounds or voices" suggests a call for the Maruts to arrive with their powerful presence, yet in a manner that does not disturb the learned individual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to calling upon natural forces or energies in a respectful manner, such as invoking the power of nature during a meditation or a spiritual practice.
    • For instance, during a storm, people often reflect on the power of nature and may seek to understand or connect with it through rituals or prayers, similar to how the mantra seeks the presence of the Maruts.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the relationship between humans and the natural elements, highlighting the importance of respect and understanding in that relationship.
    • It can be applied in real life by encouraging individuals to seek harmony with nature, recognizing its power while also understanding the need for calmness and wisdom in the face of its might.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can serve as a reminder to invoke energies or forces in a way that is respectful and mindful, ensuring that one remains grounded and undisturbed amidst the chaos of life.
