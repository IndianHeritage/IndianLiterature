व्युषा आवो दिविजा ऋतेनाविष्कृत्वानां महिमानम् आगात् |अप द्रुहस्तम आवर अजुष्टम अङ्गिरस्तमाः पथ्या अजिगः ||
vy u1ṣā āvo divijā ṛtenāviṣkṛṇvānā mahimānam āgāt | apa druhas tama āvar ajuṣṭam aṅgirastamā pathyā ajīgaḥ ||
  • vy u1ṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Dawn" or "O Light". In many cultures, dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • āvo divijā: This translates to "we invoke the divine". It signifies a call to the celestial or divine forces, seeking their presence and blessings.
  • ṛtenāviṣkṛṇvānā: This means "by the truth, revealing". It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in our actions and thoughts.
  • mahimānam āgāt: This translates to "the greatness has come". It suggests the arrival of something significant, possibly enlightenment or divine grace.
  • apa druhas: This means "away from harm". It is a plea for protection from negativity and malevolence.
  • tama āvar: This translates to "from darkness". It signifies the desire to be liberated from ignorance and negativity.
  • ajuṣṭam: This means "unharmed" or "unobstructed". It reflects a state of being free from obstacles and challenges.
  • aṅgirastamā: This can be interpreted as "the best of the Angiras". Angiras refers to a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge.
  • pathyā ajīgaḥ: This translates to "on the right path". It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous and truthful path in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking light, truth, and divine guidance while striving to overcome darkness and negativity. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, spiritual practices, and ethical decision-making. For instance, when faced with challenges, one can recite this mantra to invoke positivity and clarity, reminding oneself to stay on the path of righteousness and truth. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of hope, protection, and the pursuit of enlightenment, making it relevant in both spiritual and everyday contexts.
