प्र वावृजे सुप्रयाः बर्हिर एषाम् आ विश्पतिव बीरिट इयाते |विशाम् अक्तोर उषसः पूर्वहूतौ वायुः पूषा स्वस्तये नियुत्वान् ||
pra vāvṛje suprayā barhir eṣām ā viśpatīva bīriṭa iyāte | viśām aktor uṣasaḥ pūrvahūtau vāyuḥ pūṣā svastaye niyutvān ||
  • pra vāvṛje: This phrase can be interpreted as "may it be well-guarded" or "may it be protected." It emphasizes the importance of safeguarding what is valuable in our lives.
  • suprayā: This term means "well-prepared" or "well-structured." It suggests that one should approach life with careful planning and readiness.
  • barhir: This word refers to "the covering" or "the layer." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the protective layers we build around ourselves, such as our values and beliefs.
  • eṣām: This means "of these" or "of those," indicating a connection to a specific group or community.
  • ā viśpatīva: This phrase can be translated as "like a lord" or "like a ruler," suggesting a sense of authority and responsibility.
  • bīriṭa: This term can mean "to be strong" or "to be powerful," highlighting the importance of strength in character and action.
  • iyāte: This means "to go" or "to move," indicating progress or movement towards a goal.
  • viśām: This word means "of the community" or "of the people," emphasizing the collective aspect of human existence.
  • aktor: This can be interpreted as "the one who acts" or "the doer," highlighting the importance of action in achieving results.
  • uṣasaḥ: This term refers to "the dawn" or "the new beginning," symbolizing hope and renewal.
  • pūrvahūtau: This means "having been invoked before," suggesting the importance of tradition and the wisdom of the past.
  • vāyuḥ: This word means "the wind," which can symbolize change and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
  • pūṣā: This term refers to "the nourisher" or "the sustainer," indicating the importance of support and nourishment in our lives.
  • svastaye: This means "for well-being" or "for prosperity," emphasizing the ultimate goal of health and happiness.
  • niyutvān: This term can be interpreted as "being directed" or "being guided," suggesting the importance of having a clear direction in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, preparation, strength, and community in our lives. It encourages individuals to act with authority and responsibility while being mindful of their roots and the support they receive from others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community service, and personal development. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a sense of well-being and prosperity not only for themselves but also for their communities. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, guided by strength and purpose, can lead to a harmonious and prosperous existence.
