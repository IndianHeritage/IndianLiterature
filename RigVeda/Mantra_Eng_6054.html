अयं सहस्रम ऋषिभिः सहस्कृतः समुद्र इव पप्रथे |सत्यः सो अस्य महिमा गृणे शवो यज्ञेषु विप्रराज्ये ||
ayaṁ sahasram ṛṣibhiḥ sahaskṛtaḥ samudra iva paprathe | satyaḥ so asya mahimā gṛṇe śavo yajñeṣu viprarājye ||
  • ayaṁ sahasram: This phrase translates to "this thousand." It signifies abundance and the vastness of resources or knowledge.
  • ṛṣibhiḥ sahaskṛtaḥ: This means "created by the sages." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the contributions of enlightened beings in shaping our understanding and practices.
  • samudra iva paprathe: Translated as "like the ocean, it spreads." This metaphor illustrates the vastness and depth of knowledge or truth, akin to the ocean's immeasurable expanse.
  • satyaḥ so asya mahimā: This means "the truth is its greatness." It highlights that the essence of greatness lies in truth and authenticity.
  • gṛṇe: This translates to "I sing" or "I praise." It indicates an act of reverence and acknowledgment of the divine or the profound truths.
  • śavo yajñeṣu viprarājye: This phrase means "in the sacrifices of the wise." It suggests that the true value and significance of rituals and sacrifices are realized through the wisdom of the learned.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of wisdom, truth, and the collective efforts of sages in creating a vast reservoir of knowledge. In real life, it signifies the importance of learning from wise individuals and recognizing the depth of knowledge that exists around us, much like the ocean. It encourages us to seek truth and authenticity in our actions and to honor the contributions of those who have come before us. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings where the wisdom of teachers is revered, or in community gatherings where collective knowledge is shared and celebrated. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true greatness is rooted in truth and the wisdom of the learned, guiding us in our personal and communal journeys.
