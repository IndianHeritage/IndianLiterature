ते दशग्वाः प्रथमā यज्ञं ऊहिरे ते नो हिन्वन्तूषसो व्युष्टिषु |उषा न रामीर अरुणैर अपोर्णुते महो ज्योतीषा शुचता गोर्णसā ||
te daśagvāḥ prathamā yajñam ūhire te no hinvantūṣaso vyuṣṭiṣu | uṣā na rāmīr aruṇair aporṇute maho jyotiṣā śucatā goarṇasā ||
  • te daśagvāḥ prathamā yajñam ūhire: This phrase can be translated to "The ten steeds (or horses) are the first to invoke the sacrifice." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. The number ten often represents completeness or wholeness. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where horses are revered for their power and grace, such as in equestrian sports or traditional ceremonies.
  • te no hinvantūṣaso vyuṣṭiṣu: This translates to "May they not hinder us in the dawns." Here, 'dawns' symbolizes new beginnings or opportunities. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of not letting obstacles prevent us from seizing new opportunities in life, such as pursuing a new job or starting a new project.
  • uṣā na rāmīr aruṇair aporṇute: This means "The dawn does not fill us with joy with its red rays." The dawn is often associated with hope and renewal. However, this line suggests a yearning for a deeper, more fulfilling experience of joy. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of genuine happiness beyond superficial pleasures, such as finding fulfillment in relationships or personal achievements.
  • maho jyotiṣā śucatā goarṇasā: This translates to "With great light, may we shine forth." Light symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and self-improvement, where individuals strive to illuminate their paths and those of others through sharing knowledge and experiences.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, opportunity, joy, and enlightenment in our lives. It encourages us to harness our inner power (symbolized by the ten steeds) to overcome obstacles and embrace new beginnings. The call for deeper joy reminds us to seek meaningful experiences rather than fleeting pleasures. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to pursue knowledge and wisdom, allowing us to shine brightly in our personal and communal lives. This can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and professional endeavors, where the focus is on growth, resilience, and enlightenment.
