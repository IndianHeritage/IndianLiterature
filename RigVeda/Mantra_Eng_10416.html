मन्त्र: चक्षुर् नो देवः सविता चक्षुर् न उता पर्वतः |चक्षुर् धाता दधातु नः ||
cakṣur no devaḥ savitā cakṣur na uta parvataḥ | cakṣur dhātā dadhātu naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakṣur: Eye or vision
    • no: Our
    • devaḥ: God or divine being
    • savitā: The Sun or the source of life
    • na: Not
    • uta: Or
    • parvataḥ: Mountain
    • dhātā: The creator or sustainer
    • dadhātu: May grant or bestow
    • naḥ: Us
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer for divine vision and insight. It invokes the Sun God Savitā, asking for clarity and understanding, not just from the divine but also from the natural world, represented by the mountains. The essence of the mantra is a plea for enlightenment and the ability to perceive truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Seeking Guidance: In moments of confusion or uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance from a higher power or nature. For instance, a student preparing for exams may recite this mantra to gain clarity and focus.
    • Decision Making: When faced with important life decisions, such as choosing a career path, one might reflect on this mantra to seek insight and wisdom, hoping to see the situation clearly.
    • Mindfulness and Awareness: In daily life, practicing mindfulness can be seen as a way to cultivate the vision referred to in this mantra. By being present and aware, individuals can better understand their surroundings and make informed choices.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine insight and clarity. It emphasizes the importance of perception, both in a spiritual and practical sense. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, decision-making, and personal growth. By invoking the divine for clarity, individuals can navigate life's challenges with greater wisdom and understanding.
