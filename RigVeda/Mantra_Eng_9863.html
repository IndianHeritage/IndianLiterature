बृहद्वदन्ति मदिरेण मन्दिनेद्रं क्रोशन्तोऽविदन्न अनामधु |संरभ्य धीराः स्वसृभिर अनर्तिषुर आगोषयन्तः पृथिवीं उपब्धिभिः ||
bṛhad vadanti madireṇa mandinendraṁ krośanto 'vidann anā madhu | saṁrabhyā dhīrāḥ svasṛbhir anartiṣur āghoṣayantaḥ pṛthivīm upabdibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • vadanti: they speak, they declare
    • madireṇa: with intoxication, with joy
    • mandinendraṁ: the lord of the gentle, the wise
    • krośanto: shouting, calling out
    • 'vidann: not knowing, unaware
    • anā: without
    • madhu: sweetness, nectar
    • saṁrabhyā: having become excited, stirred up
    • dhīrāḥ: the wise, the prudent
    • svasṛbhir: with their own sisters
    • anartiṣur: they do not harm, they do not injure
    • āghoṣayantaḥ: making a loud sound, proclaiming
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • upabdibhiḥ: with their own strength, with their own power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a collective expression of joy and wisdom.
    • The "great" or "vast" refers to the expansive nature of knowledge and understanding.
    • The "lord of the gentle" symbolizes a wise leader or figure who guides with compassion.
    • The act of "shouting" or "calling out" suggests a communal celebration or acknowledgment of truth.
    • The phrase "without sweetness" indicates a state of being unaware or lacking in joy, which can be a metaphor for ignorance.
    • The "wise" individuals, when stirred up, do not harm others but instead uplift and proclaim the truth.
    • The mention of "sisters" can symbolize unity and support among individuals in a community.
    • The proclamation that they "do not harm" emphasizes the importance of peaceful coexistence and the power of positive expression.
    • Finally, the reference to "the earth" signifies the grounding nature of these actions and their impact on the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they embody the spirit of this mantra by expressing joy and unity.
    • In educational settings, wise teachers guide students with compassion, encouraging them to seek knowledge without causing harm to others.
    • Activism for social justice can reflect the essence of this mantra, where individuals raise their voices for truth and equality, promoting a peaceful and supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of collective joy, wisdom, and peaceful expression in our lives.
    • It teaches us the importance of uplifting one another and creating a harmonious community.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in celebrations, education, or activism, the principles of this mantra can guide us towards a more compassionate and understanding world.
