पितुर्मातुरध्याय ये समस्वरन्न ऋचाः शोचन्तः संधन्तो अव्रतान् |इन्द्रद्विष्टाम् अप धामन्ति मायया त्वचम् असिक्नीम् भूमानो दिवस परि ||
pitur mātur adhy ā ye samasvarann ṛcā śocantaḥ saṁdahanto avratān | indradviṣṭām apa dhamanti māyayā tvacam asiknīm bhūmano divas pari ||
  • pitur mātur adhy ā ye - This phrase translates to "those who are devoted to their father and mother." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and respecting one's parents, which is a fundamental value in many cultures.
  • samasvarann ṛcā - This means "with harmonious voices." It suggests the idea of unity and collective effort, where individuals come together in agreement and harmony, much like a choir singing in unison.
  • śocantaḥ saṁdahanto avratān - This can be interpreted as "those who lament and bind the uncommitted." It reflects the idea of compassion towards those who may be lost or without direction, encouraging a sense of responsibility to guide and support them.
  • indradviṣṭām apa dhamanti - This translates to "they who drive away the enemies of Indra." Indra is a deity associated with strength and power, and this line suggests the importance of overcoming obstacles and adversities in life.
  • māyayā tvacam asiknīm - This means "by the illusion of the skin." It points to the transient nature of physical existence and the illusions that can distract us from our true purpose.
  • bhūmano divas pari - This translates to "the day of the earthly beings." It signifies the importance of living in the present and making the most of our earthly existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the values of respect for parents, unity, compassion, overcoming challenges, and the recognition of the transient nature of life. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in various ways:

  • In family dynamics, honoring parents can strengthen familial bonds and create a supportive environment.
  • In community settings, working together harmoniously can lead to collective success and a sense of belonging.
  • Offering guidance to those who are lost can foster a sense of responsibility and community support.
  • Facing challenges with strength and resilience can empower individuals to overcome personal and societal obstacles.
  • Recognizing the illusions of life can lead to a deeper understanding of one's purpose and priorities.
  • Living in the present encourages mindfulness and appreciation for the experiences of life.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the importance of living a life of purpose, compassion, and unity.
