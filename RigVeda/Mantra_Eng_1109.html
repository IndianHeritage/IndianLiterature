यो व्यंसं जाहरिषाणेन मन्युनā यः शम्बरं यो अहन पिप्रम अव्रतम् |इन्द्रो यः शुष्णम अशुषं न्य आवृणङ् मरुत्वन्तं सख्याय हवामहे ||
yo vyaṁsaṁ jāhṛṣāṇena manyunā yaḥ śambaraṁ yo ahan piprum avratam | indro yaḥ śuṣṇam aśuṣaṁ ny āvṛṇaṅ marutvantaṁ sakhyāya havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • vyaṁsaṁ - the one who is powerful
    • jāhṛṣāṇena - with great strength
    • manyunā - with anger
    • yaḥ - who
    • śambaraṁ - the one who is a foe or enemy
    • yo - who
    • ahan - has killed
    • piprum - the one who is a great threat
    • avratam - the one who is unyielding
    • indro - Indra, the king of gods
    • yaḥ - who
    • śuṣṇam - the one who is dry or barren
    • aśuṣaṁ - the one who is not dry
    • ny - not
    • āvṛṇaṅ - cover or conceal
    • marutvantaṁ - the one who is powerful like the winds
    • sakhyāya - for friendship
    • havāmahe - we invoke or call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful deity Indra, who is known for his strength and ability to overcome enemies.
    • It speaks of the duality of strength and anger, suggesting that true power comes from the ability to confront and defeat adversaries.
    • The mention of "dry" and "not dry" symbolizes the balance between desolation and abundance, indicating that one must navigate through challenges to achieve prosperity.
    • The reference to "marutvantaṁ" highlights the importance of being dynamic and adaptable, much like the winds, in the face of adversity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may face competition (the enemy) and must harness their strength and strategic thinking (Indra's qualities) to overcome challenges.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter conflicts or obstacles that require them to confront their fears and adversities with courage and resilience.
    • In environmental contexts, the balance between "dry" and "not dry" can relate to sustainable practices, where one must address issues like drought while promoting growth and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, resilience, and adaptability in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking inner strength and confronting adversities can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to embrace their power, seek friendship and support, and navigate through life's challenges with courage and wisdom.
