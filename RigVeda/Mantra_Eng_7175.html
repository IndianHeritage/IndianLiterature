विष्वाम् आर्यो विपश्चितोऽतिख्यास तुयम् आगहि |अस्मे धेहि श्रवो बृहत् ||
viśvām̐ aryo vipaścito 'ti khyas tūyam ā gahi | asme dhehi śravo bṛhat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvām̐: all, universal
    • aryo: noble, worthy
    • vipaścito: wise, knowledgeable
    • 'ti: thus, so
    • khyas: known, recognized
    • tūyam: your, belonging to you
    • ā gahi: may you grasp, may you hold
    • asme: for us
    • dhehi: grant, bestow
    • śravo: fame, glory
    • bṛhat: great, vast
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra calls upon the universal wisdom and nobility of the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and grasping the knowledge that is available to us.
    • The plea for the bestowal of great fame and glory signifies a desire for recognition and success in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking guidance and wisdom from mentors or leaders who embody noble qualities.
    • Students can invoke this mantra while preparing for exams, aiming to grasp knowledge and achieve recognition for their hard work.
    • In community service, individuals may reflect on this mantra to inspire themselves to act wisely and nobly for the benefit of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for universal wisdom and noble action.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and recognition while acting with integrity and purpose.
    • In real life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to strive for greatness in our personal and professional lives, fostering a sense of responsibility towards ourselves and society.
