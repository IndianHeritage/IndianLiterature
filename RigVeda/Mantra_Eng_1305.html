युवं श्यावाय रुशतीं अदत्तम महः क्षोणस्याश्विनाऽ कण्वाय |प्रवाच्यं तद वृषणा कृतं वां यन् नार्षदाय श्रवो अध्यधत्तम ||
yuvaṁ śyāvāya ruśatīm adattam mahaḥ kṣoṇasyāśvinā kaṇvāya | pravācyaṁ tad vṛṣaṇā kṛtaṁ vāṁ yan nārṣadāya śravo adhyadhattam ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ śyāvāya ruśatīm adattam mahaḥ kṣoṇasyāśvinā kaṇvāya | pravācyaṁ tad vṛṣaṇā kṛtaṁ vāṁ yan nārṣadāya śravo adhyadhattam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • śyāvāya: dark, black, or blue
    • ruśatīm: shining, radiant
    • adattam: ungrasped, unattached
    • mahaḥ: great, vast
    • kṣoṇasyā: of the earth
    • āśvinā: of the Ashvins, the twin gods of medicine and health
    • kaṇvāya: to the wise, to the learned
    • pravācyaṁ: that which is spoken, declared
    • tad: that
    • vṛṣaṇā: of the strong, powerful
    • kṛtaṁ: done, created
    • vāṁ: your
    • yan: which
    • nārṣadāya: for the one who is wise
    • śravo: fame, glory
    • adhyadhattam: has been placed, has been established
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the youthful and radiant qualities associated with the Ashvins, who are often depicted as divine physicians and bringers of health and vitality.
    • The phrase "yuvaṁ śyāvāya ruśatīm" suggests a young and vibrant energy that is both powerful and unattached, symbolizing the potential of youth.
    • The reference to "mahaḥ kṣoṇasyā" connects this energy to the earth, indicating a grounding force that is essential for growth and stability.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, the mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in achieving one's goals, as seen in the line "pravācyaṁ tad vṛṣaṇā kṛtaṁ vāṁ".
    • The concluding part, "yan nārṣadāya śravo adhyadhattam\ highlights the establishment of fame and glory for those who are wise and strong, suggesting that true success comes from a combination of knowledge and power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a young entrepreneur may embody the qualities of the Ashvins by being innovative and resilient, leading their team to success through wisdom and strength.
    • In health and wellness, individuals who seek knowledge about nutrition and fitness can harness the youthful energy described in the mantra to improve their well-being and vitality.
    • In education, students who embrace the spirit of the Ashvins by being curious and dedicated to learning can achieve great things, establishing their own "fame" in their respective fields.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of youthfulness, wisdom, and strength in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their potential and strive for greatness while remaining grounded and connected to the earth.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide us in our personal and professional journeys, emphasizing the balance between knowledge, power, and vitality.
