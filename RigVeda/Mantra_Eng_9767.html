मूर्धा भूवो भवति नक्तम् अग्निस ततः सूर्यो जायते प्रातर उद्यान् |मायामू तु यज्ञियानाम् एताम् अपो यत तूर्णिश चरति प्रजानन् ||
mūrdhā bhuvo bhavati naktam agnis tataḥ sūryo jāyate prātar udyan | māyām ū tu yajñiyānām etām apo yat tūrṇiś carati prajānan ||
  • mūrdhā bhuvo bhavati: This phrase translates to "the head of the world becomes." Here, "mūrdhā" refers to the head or the topmost part, while "bhuvo" means of the world or existence. This can be interpreted as the pinnacle of creation or consciousness.
  • naktam agnis tataḥ sūryo jāyate: This means "from the night, fire is born; from that, the sun arises in the morning." It signifies the cyclical nature of existence, where darkness (night) gives way to light (day). In real life, this can be likened to overcoming challenges (night) to achieve success (day).
  • prātar udyan: This translates to "in the morning, it rises." It emphasizes the idea of renewal and the start of a new day, symbolizing hope and new beginnings.
  • māyām ū tu yajñiyānām etām: This means "this illusion is for those who perform sacrifices." Here, "māyām" refers to illusion or the transient nature of the world, while "yajñiyānām" pertains to those who engage in selfless acts or sacrifices. It suggests that understanding the nature of reality requires effort and selflessness.
  • apo yat tūrṇiś carati prajānan: This translates to "the waters that swiftly flow, creating life." "Apo" refers to water, which is essential for life, and "tūrṇiś" means swiftly or quickly. This highlights the importance of water in sustaining life and the interconnectedness of all living beings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, transformation, and the cyclical nature of life. It teaches us that from darkness comes light, and through selfless actions, we can understand the deeper truths of existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, where overcoming difficulties leads to enlightenment, or in community service, where selfless acts contribute to the greater good. The mantra encourages us to recognize the transient nature of life and the importance of nurturing our environment, as water symbolizes the foundation of life. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of our actions in the grand tapestry of existence.
