विषु विश्वा अरातयो 'र्यो नशन्त नो धियः |अस्तासी शत्रवे वधं यो न इन्द्र जिघांसति या ते रातिर ददिर वसु नभन्ताम् अन्यकेषां ज्याकाद अधि धन्वसु ||
vi ṣu viśvā arātayo 'ryo naśanta no dhiyaḥ | astāsi śatrave vadhaṁ yo na indra jighāṁsati yā te rātir dadir vasu nabhantām anyakeṣāṁ jyākā adhi dhanvasu ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the wise and noble ones, who are free from enmity, protect us. May we be shielded from those who wish us harm, and may our thoughts be directed towards peace and prosperity. O Indra, may we be free from the intentions of those who seek to harm us, and may we attain the wealth of the heavens, surpassing all adversities."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi ṣu viśvā: Refers to the wise and noble beings, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and nobility in our lives.
    • arātayo: Those who are free from enmity or hostility, highlighting the value of peace.
    • naśanta: To protect or shield, indicating a desire for safety and security.
    • dhiyaḥ: Thoughts or intentions, suggesting that our mental state is crucial for our well-being.
    • astāsi: To be free or liberated, pointing towards the idea of overcoming obstacles.
    • śatrave vadhaṁ: The intention to harm or kill, representing the negative forces we wish to avoid.
    • yo na indra jighāṁsati: O Indra, may we be free from those who wish us harm, invoking the divine for protection.
    • yā te rātir: The wealth or prosperity that comes from the heavens, symbolizing abundance.
    • dadir vasu: To attain wealth or good fortune, emphasizing the importance of prosperity in life.
    • nabhantām: Surpassing or transcending, indicating the aspiration to rise above challenges.
    • anyakeṣāṁ: All adversities or obstacles, recognizing the various challenges we face.
    • jyākā adhi dhanvasu: To be directed towards peace and prosperity, reinforcing the mantra's focus on positive intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when dealing with competition or conflicts. By fostering a culture of collaboration and support, employees can work together harmoniously, avoiding hostility.
    • In personal relationships, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to resolve conflicts peacefully and to focus on nurturing positive connections, rather than harboring resentment.
    • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards peace and prosperity, such as organizing community service projects that benefit everyone and reduce enmity among different groups.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, peace, and protection from harm. It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions, both individually and collectively. In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, a more supportive community, and a greater sense of security. By invoking the divine for protection and striving for prosperity, we can navigate life's challenges with resilience and grace.
