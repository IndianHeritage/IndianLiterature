यो विश्वस्य जगतः प्राणतस् पतिर् यो ब्रह्मणे प्रथमो गा अविन्दत् |इन्द्रो यो दस्यूम् अधराम् अवातिरन् मरुत्वन्तं सख्याय हवामहे ||
yo viśvasya jagataḥ prāṇatas patir yo brahmaṇe prathamo gā avindat | indro yo dasyūm̐r adharām̐ avātiran marutvantaṁ sakhyāya havāmahe ||
  • Translation: "He who is the Lord of all beings, who first discovered the essence of the universe, we invoke Indra, who vanquished the enemies and is a friend to the Maruts."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: He who
    • viśvasya: of all beings
    • jagataḥ: of the universe
    • prāṇatas: the life force
    • patir: Lord
    • yo: who
    • brahmaṇe: to the essence
    • prathamo: first
    • gā: discovered
    • avindat: found
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • yo: who
    • dasyūm̐r: the enemies
    • adharām̐: the lowly
    • avātiran: vanquished
    • marutvantaṁ: the Maruts (storm deities)
    • sakhyāya: as friends
    • havāmahe: we invoke
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra highlights the omnipresence of a divine force that governs all beings and the universe.
    • Indra is invoked as a powerful deity who protects and supports the righteous while defeating adversaries.
    • The mention of the Maruts signifies the importance of collaboration and friendship in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to work together, vanquishing competition and achieving success.
    • In personal life, individuals can draw strength from their friendships (like the Maruts) to face adversities, emphasizing the importance of community support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine guidance available to us in our endeavors.
    • It encourages us to seek strength in unity and to recognize the power of a higher force in our lives.
    • By invoking such energies, we can navigate challenges with confidence and purpose, fostering a sense of community and resilience.
