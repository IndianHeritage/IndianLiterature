हतो वृत्राण्य आर्य हतो दासानि सत्पति |हतो विश्वा अप द्विषः ||
hato vṛtrāṇy āryā hato dāsāni satpatī | hato viśvā apa dviṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "hato vṛtrāṇy āryā hato dāsāni satpatī | hato viśvā apa dviṣaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The Aryans have defeated the Vritra; the servants have been defeated by the true lord. All enemies have been vanquished."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Hato: Defeated or vanquished.
    • Vṛtrāṇy: Refers to Vritra, a serpent or dragon symbolizing chaos and obstacles.
    • Āryā: The noble or the Aryans, representing a righteous and virtuous group.
    • Dāsāni: Servants or those who are subservient.
    • Satpatī: The true lord or the rightful ruler.
    • Viśvā: All or universal.
    • Apa: Away or against.
    • Dviṣaḥ: Enemies or those who harbor ill will.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the triumph of good over evil, where the noble (Aryans) overcome chaos (Vritra) and the rightful ruler (Satpatī) prevails over subservience (Dāsāni).
    • In a real-life scenario, this can be likened to a leader who stands against corruption and injustice, rallying the community to overcome societal challenges.
    • For example, a community leader who fights against systemic oppression can be seen as embodying the spirit of this mantra, defeating the metaphorical Vritra that represents societal ills.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness and the collective strength of a community in overcoming obstacles.
    • It serves as a reminder that unity and virtue can lead to the defeat of adversities and enemies, whether they are external forces or internal struggles.
    • In modern contexts, it can inspire movements for social justice, environmental protection, and any cause that seeks to vanquish negativity and promote the greater good.
