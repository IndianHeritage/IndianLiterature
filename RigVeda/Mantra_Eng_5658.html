द्यावाभूमी अदिते त्रासीतां नो ये वां जज्ञुः सुजनिमाना ऋष्वे |मा हेळे भूम वरुणस्य वायोः मा मित्रस्य प्रियतमस्य नृणाम् ||
dyāvābhūmī adite trāsīthāṁ no ye vāṁ jajñuḥ sujanimāna ṛṣve | mā heḻe bhūma varuṇasya vāyor mā mitrasya priyatamasya nṛṇām ||
  • dyāvābhūmī: This term refers to the duality of heaven (dyāva) and earth (bhūmi). It signifies the interconnectedness of the cosmos, where both realms are essential for life. In real life, this can be seen in the balance of nature, where the sky provides rain and sunlight, while the earth offers sustenance and habitat.
  • adite: Aditi is often considered the mother of the gods and represents infinity and the boundless. This can be related to the concept of nurturing and protection in our lives, where a mother figure or a guardian provides unconditional support and love.
  • trāsīthāṁ: This word means to protect or shield. In a modern context, this can be likened to the role of safety measures in our lives, such as laws, regulations, and personal safety practices that protect individuals and communities.
  • no ye vāṁ jajñuḥ: This phrase translates to "those who have been born to you." It emphasizes the importance of lineage and community. In real life, this can be seen in family ties and the support systems we create with friends and loved ones.
  • sujanimāna: This term means "well-born" or "noble." It reflects the idea of virtue and moral integrity. In contemporary society, this can be related to the importance of character and ethics in personal and professional relationships.
  • ṛṣve: Referring to the sages or wise individuals, this highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, we often seek guidance from mentors, teachers, or elders who provide insight and direction in our lives.
  • mā heḻe bhūma: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not let the earth be disturbed." It signifies the need for harmony and stability in our environment. In today's world, this can relate to environmental conservation efforts aimed at preserving our planet for future generations.
  • varuṇasya vāyor: Varuna is the god of water and the celestial ocean, representing the importance of water in sustaining life. This can be connected to the current global focus on water conservation and the need to protect our water resources.
  • mā mitrasya priyatamasya nṛṇām: This translates to "do not let the beloved of Mitra (the god of friendship) be harmed." It emphasizes the significance of friendship and community support. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of building strong relationships and networks that foster collaboration and mutual aid.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony between the heavens and the earth, the nurturing aspect of Aditi, and the importance of protection, wisdom, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder to maintain balance in our lives, respect our environment, and cherish our relationships. In a world that often feels chaotic, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life rooted in integrity, support, and respect for all beings.
