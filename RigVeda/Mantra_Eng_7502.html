यस्मिन विश्वा अधि श्रीयो रणन्ति सप्त संसदः |इन्द्रं सुतं हवामहे ||
yasmin viśvā adhi śriyo raṇanti sapta saṁsadaḥ | indraṁ sute havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin - in whom
    • viśvā - all, the universe
    • adhi - upon, in relation to
    • śriyo - prosperity, wealth, glory
    • raṇanti - they fight, they strive
    • sapta - seven
    • saṁsadaḥ - assemblies, gatherings
    • indraṁ - Indra, the king of gods
    • sute - with offerings, with praises
    • havāmahe - we invoke, we call upon
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • It highlights the unity of the universe and the collective effort of various assemblies striving for prosperity and glory.
    • The mention of "seven assemblies" can symbolize completeness and the importance of collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team working together towards a common goal, where each member contributes their strengths to achieve success.
    • In community projects, the idea of "seven assemblies" can represent different groups coming together to address a social issue, emphasizing the power of collective action.
    • In personal life, it can reflect the importance of family and friends supporting each other in times of need, striving for shared prosperity and happiness.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving prosperity and success.
    • It teaches us that invoking higher powers, like Indra, can be a way to seek guidance and strength in our endeavors.
    • In real life, it encourages us to work together, respect each other's contributions, and strive for common goals, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or professional environments.
