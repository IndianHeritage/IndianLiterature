यो वो देवाः घृतसुनना हव्येन प्रतिभूषति |तं विश्व उप गच्छत ||
yo vo devā ghṛtasnunā havyena pratibhūṣati | taṁ viśva upa gacchatha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vo: your
    • devā: gods
    • ghṛtasnunā: with clarified butter (ghee)
    • havyena: with offerings
    • pratibhūṣati: shines forth or is adorned
    • taṁ: that
    • viśva: all or universal
    • upa: towards
    • gacchatha: may you go or approach
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of the gods, emphasizing the importance of offerings made with ghee, which is considered sacred in Vedic rituals.
    • The phrase "taṁ viśva upa gacchatha" suggests a collective approach towards the divine, encouraging unity and harmony among all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, ghee is often used in offerings to deities, symbolizing purity and the essence of life. For instance, during Diwali, people light lamps filled with ghee to invite prosperity and dispel darkness.
    • The concept of collective worship can be seen in community gatherings, such as during festivals or religious ceremonies, where individuals come together to offer prayers and seek blessings.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder that offerings made with sincerity can attract divine blessings.
    • It also emphasizes the need for unity in spiritual pursuits, suggesting that collective efforts can lead to greater spiritual fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encourages individuals to approach the divine with offerings and a sense of community. In real life, this can translate to acts of kindness, charity, and collective worship, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that through sincere offerings and unity, one can attain spiritual growth and divine grace.
