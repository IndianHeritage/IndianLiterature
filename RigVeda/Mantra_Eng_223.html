स्योंा पृथिवी भवानृक्षरा निवेशनī |यच्छा नः शर्म सप्रथः ||
syonā pṛthivi bhavānṛkṣarā niveśanī | yacchā naḥ śarma saprathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • syonā: auspicious, favorable
    • pṛthivi: earth, the world
    • bhavānṛkṣarā: may you be the protector, the one who provides
    • niveśanī: the one who grants shelter or abode
    • yacchā: grant, provide
    • naḥ: to us
    • śarma: comfort, happiness, security
    • saprathaḥ: together, in unity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the earth as a nurturing and protective force.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking shelter and comfort from the earth, which can be interpreted as a call for harmony with nature.
    • The phrase "may you be the protector" suggests a relationship of trust and reliance on the earth for sustenance and safety.
    • The call for "comfort" and "happiness" indicates a desire for peace and well-being in our lives, which can be achieved through unity and cooperation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to environmental conservation efforts. By protecting the earth, we ensure our own comfort and security.
    • Community initiatives that promote unity and cooperation, such as neighborhood clean-up drives or community gardens, embody the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, fostering a sense of security and support among family and friends reflects the protective aspect of the earth as described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of our connection to the earth and the importance of nurturing that relationship.
    • It serves as a reminder that our well-being is intertwined with the health of our environment and the strength of our communities.
    • By embracing the values of protection, comfort, and unity, we can create a more harmonious and sustainable world for ourselves and future generations.
