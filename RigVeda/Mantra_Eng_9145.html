नीचां वर्तन्त उपरी स्फुरन्त्य अहस्तासो हस्तवंतं सहन्ते |दिव्यां अंगारा इरिणे न्युप्ताः शीताः संतो हृदयं निर दहन्ति ||
nīcā vartanta upari sphuranty ahastāso hastavantaṁ sahante | divyā aṅgārā iriṇe nyuptāḥ śītāḥ santo hṛdayaṁ nir dahanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nīcā: lowly or inferior
    • vartanta: they exist or they are present
    • upari: above or higher
    • sphuranty: they shine or they illuminate
    • ahastāso: without hands or powerless
    • hastavantaṁ: having hands or powerful
    • sahante: they endure or they tolerate
    • divyā: divine or celestial
    • aṅgārā: embers or fiery
    • iriṇe: with the sound or with the voice
    • nyuptāḥ: having been placed or established
    • śītāḥ: cool or calm
    • santo: peaceful or serene
    • hṛdayaṁ: heart or core
    • nir: without
    • dahanti: they burn or they consume
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the contrast between the lowly and the powerful, highlighting how those who are seemingly powerless can exist alongside those who are powerful.
    • The phrase "nīcā vartanta" suggests that even those who are considered lowly have a presence and significance in the world.
    • The imagery of "sphuranty" indicates that there is a light or illumination that comes from the powerful, represented by "hastavantaṁ," who can endure challenges.
    • The divine embers, "divyā aṅgārā," symbolize the potential for transformation and renewal, suggesting that even in adversity, there is a divine presence that can bring about change.
    • The coolness of "śītāḥ" represents peace and tranquility, which can be found in the heart, "hṛdayaṁ," when one is free from the burning desires or conflicts that often consume us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, individuals who may feel inferior or less powerful can still contribute significantly to the team's success, demonstrating that every role is important.
    • In social movements, those who are marginalized often shine a light on issues that the powerful may overlook, showing that their voices can lead to significant change.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining peace and understanding in the face of conflict can lead to stronger bonds, as seen in families that prioritize communication and empathy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the value of all individuals, regardless of their perceived power or status.
    • It teaches us that true strength lies in endurance and the ability to maintain peace within ourselves, even amidst challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire us to appreciate the contributions of everyone around us and to cultivate a sense of inner calm that allows us to navigate life's difficulties with grace.
