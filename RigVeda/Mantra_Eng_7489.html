त्यम् उ वः सत्रासाहं विश्वासु गीरिष्व आयतम् |आ च्यावयस्य ऊतये ||
tyam u vaḥ satrāsāhaṁ viśvāsu gīrṣv āyatam | ā cyāvayasy ūtaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyam: that
    • u: indeed
    • vaḥ: your
    • satrāsāhaṁ: I am the one who brings forth strength
    • viśvāsu: in all realms
    • gīrṣv: in songs or hymns
    • āyatam: to be extended or to be reached
    • ā: towards
    • cyāvayasy: of the one who is being invoked
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of prosperity or well-being
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between the speaker and the divine, emphasizing the strength and support that is available in all aspects of life.
    • The phrase "I am the one who brings forth strength" suggests that the divine presence is a source of empowerment, encouraging individuals to seek strength in their endeavors.
    • The reference to "in all realms" indicates that this strength is not limited to a specific area but is applicable universally, whether in personal, social, or spiritual contexts.
    • The invocation of songs or hymns signifies the importance of expression and communication in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing challenges in career or relationships, individuals can recite this mantra to remind themselves of the inner strength they possess and the support available to them.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during gatherings or rituals to invoke collective strength and unity, fostering a sense of togetherness and shared purpose.
    • In educational or professional environments, this mantra can serve as a motivational reminder for students and employees to harness their potential and strive for excellence.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire strength and resilience in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that divine support is always accessible, encouraging individuals to reach out for help and to express their needs through prayer or meditation.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life to cultivate a mindset of empowerment, fostering both personal growth and communal harmony.
