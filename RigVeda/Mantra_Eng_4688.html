त्वं शतान्य अव शम्बरस्य पुरो जगन्थाप्रतीनि दस्योः |अशिक्षो यत्र शच्याः शचिवो दिवोदासाय सुन्वते सुतक्रे भरद्वाजाय गृणते वसूनि ||
tvaṁ śatāny ava śambarasya puro jaghanthāpratīni dasyoḥ | aśikṣo yatra śacyā śacīvo divodāsāya sunvate sutakre bharadvājāya gṛṇate vasūni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • śatāny: Hundred
    • ava: Down
    • śambarasya: Of the Shambara (a mythical being)
    • puro: In the front
    • jaghanthā: The one who is to be worshipped
    • pratīni: The one who is present
    • dasyoḥ: Of the Dasyus (a term often referring to non-Aryan tribes)
    • aśikṣo: Not taught
    • yatra: Where
    • śacyā: Powerful
    • śacīvo: Of the powerful
    • divodāsāya: To the one who is worshipped in the heavens
    • sunvate: To those who are good
    • sutakre: To the one who is born
    • bharadvājāya: To Bharadvaja (a sage)
    • gṛṇate: Sing
    • vasūni: Wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, acknowledging the power and wealth that comes from the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worship and the role of sages like Bharadvaja in connecting the divine with the earthly realm.
    • The mention of Dasyus suggests a contrast between the divine and those who are not aligned with the divine order, highlighting the need for righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call to recognize the importance of spiritual guidance and the wisdom of elders in our communities.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and honor those who contribute positively to society, much like the sage Bharadvaja.
    • The idea of wealth here can be interpreted not just as material wealth but also as the wealth of knowledge, compassion, and community support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine connection and the role of wisdom in achieving prosperity and harmony in life.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, support, and a sense of community, reminding us to honor those who contribute positively to our lives.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that true wealth comes from spiritual alignment and the nurturing of relationships that uplift and empower.
