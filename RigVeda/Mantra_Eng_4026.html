मन्त्र: एतो nv आद्य सुध्यो भवाम प्र दुद्चुनā मिनवामā वरीयः |आरे द्वेषांसी सन्तर दधामायाम प्राञ्चो यजमानम् अच् छ ||
eto nv a1dya sudhyo3 bhavāma pra ducchunā minavāmā varīyaḥ | āre dveṣāṁsi sanutar dadhāmāyāma prāñco yajamānam accha ||
  • Mantra: "eto nv a1dya sudhyo3 bhavāma pra ducchunā minavāmā varīyaḥ | āre dveṣāṁsi sanutar dadhāmāyāma prāñco yajamānam accha ||"
  • Translation: "May we attain purity today, may we be free from all negativity; may we overcome enmity and embrace harmony, as we offer our prayers to the divine."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • eto nv: "today" or "now" - signifies the present moment, emphasizing the importance of the current time in spiritual practice.
    • sudhyo: "purity" - refers to both physical and mental cleanliness, essential for spiritual growth.
    • bhavāma: "may we become" - a call for transformation and aspiration towards higher states of being.
    • pra ducchunā: "free from negativity" - a plea to be liberated from harmful thoughts and actions.
    • minavāmā: "overcome" - indicates the action of rising above challenges and adversities.
    • varīyaḥ: "embrace harmony" - encourages fostering peace and unity among individuals.
    • āre: "as we" - a conjunction that connects our actions with our intentions.
    • dveṣāṁsi: "enmities" - refers to conflicts and negative feelings towards others.
    • sanutar: "with great effort" - signifies the need for diligence in overcoming obstacles.
    • dadhāmā: "we offer" - an act of devotion and surrender to the divine.
    • yajamānam: "the one who is worshipped" - refers to the divine or the higher power we seek to connect with.
    • accha: "may it be so" - a concluding affirmation of the prayer's intent.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire team members to work collaboratively, setting aside personal differences to achieve common goals.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to communicate openly and resolve conflicts, fostering a harmonious environment.
    • During community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to promote peace and understanding among diverse groups.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, harmony, and overcoming negativity in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal relationships, workplaces, and community interactions, where fostering a positive environment is essential.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and united society.
