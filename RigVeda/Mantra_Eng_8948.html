अपागूहनं अमृतां मर्त्येभ्यः कृत्वा सवराणां अददुर् विवस्वते |उताश्विनाव् अभरद् यत् तद् आसीत् अजहाद् उ द्वा मिथुनां सरण्युः ||
apāgūhann amṛtām martyebhyaḥ kṛtvī savarṇām adadur vivasvate | utāśvināv abharad yat tad āsīd ajahād u dvā mithunā saraṇyūḥ ||
  • Mantra: "apāgūhann amṛtām martyebhyaḥ kṛtvī savarṇām adadur vivasvate | utāśvināv abharad yat tad āsīd ajahād u dvā mithunā saraṇyūḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāgūhann: to remove or to take away
    • amṛtām: of immortality
    • martyebhyaḥ: from mortals
    • kṛtvī: having made or created
    • savarṇām: of the same kind or similar
    • adadur: he gave or bestowed
    • vivasvate: to the shining one (often referring to the Sun)
    • utāśvināv: to the two Ashvins (divine twin horsemen)
    • abharad: he brought or carried
    • yat: that which
    • tad: that
    • āsīd: was
    • ajahād: not born or eternal
    • u: and
    • dvā: two
    • mithunā: pairs or couples
    • saraṇyūḥ: of the protectors or guardians
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine act of creation and the relationship between immortality and mortality.
    • The phrase "apāgūhann amṛtām martyebhyaḥ" suggests the removal of the mortal condition, indicating a transition from mortality to immortality.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the context of spiritual practices that aim to transcend the physical body, such as meditation or yoga, which promote a sense of eternal existence beyond physical limitations.
    • The mention of "savarṇām" implies a unity or similarity among beings, suggesting that all life is interconnected.
    • When it states "adadur vivasvate," it refers to the Sun, a symbol of life and energy, bestowing vitality and light upon all beings.
    • The "utāśvināv abharad" part highlights the role of the Ashvins, who are often seen as healers and protectors, bringing health and well-being to humanity.
    • This can be related to modern healthcare practices where the focus is on holistic healing and the well-being of individuals.
    • The concluding lines about "dvā mithunā saraṇyūḥ" emphasize the importance of pairs or partnerships, which can be interpreted as the significance of relationships in life, whether they are romantic, familial, or friendships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, death, and the quest for immortality, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that while we may be mortal, there are paths to transcendence through spiritual practices and the nurturing of relationships.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek deeper connections with themselves and others, fostering a sense of community and shared existence.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a philosophical guide, urging us to recognize the divine in the mundane and to strive for a life that honors both our mortality and our quest for immortality.
