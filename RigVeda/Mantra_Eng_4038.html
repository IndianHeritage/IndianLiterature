उत त्ये नः पर्वतासः सुशस्तयः सुदीतयो नद्यः त्रामणे भुवन |भगो विभक्ताः शवसावसाः गमद उर्व्यचाः अदितिः श्रोतु मे हवम् ||
uta tye naḥ parvatāsaḥ suśastayaḥ sudītayo nadya1s trāmaṇe bhuvan | bhago vibhaktā śavasāvasā gamad uruvyacā aditiḥ śrotu me havam ||
  • uta tye naḥ parvatāsaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the mountains be favorable to us." In real life, mountains often symbolize strength and stability. For example, in many cultures, mountains are seen as sacred places that provide a sense of peace and protection.
  • suśastayaḥ sudītayo: This means "with excellent qualities and good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of virtue and morality in our actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who strive to do good in their communities, such as volunteers or activists who work for social justice.
  • nadya1s trāmaṇe bhuvan: This translates to "the rivers that protect the world." Rivers are vital for life, providing water for drinking, agriculture, and transportation. For instance, the Nile River has been crucial for the civilization of Egypt, showcasing how rivers sustain and protect communities.
  • bhago vibhaktā: This phrase means "may prosperity be divided." It suggests the idea of shared wealth and resources. In modern society, this can relate to economic policies that promote equitable distribution of resources, such as progressive taxation or community support programs.
  • śavasāvasā gamad: This can be interpreted as "may the energies of life flow." It highlights the importance of vitality and the life force. In real life, this can be seen in practices like yoga or meditation, which aim to enhance one's life energy and overall well-being.
  • uruvyacā aditiḥ: This translates to "the boundless and infinite." It signifies the limitless potential of the universe and human beings. In practical terms, this can be related to personal growth and the pursuit of knowledge, where individuals strive to expand their horizons and capabilities.
  • śrotu me havam: This means "listen to my invocation." It is a call for attention and recognition of one's prayers or desires. In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals or moments of reflection where individuals seek guidance or support from a higher power or the universe.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for nature, the importance of virtuous living, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the need for protection, prosperity, and the flow of life energy. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, community service, and personal development. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous world.
