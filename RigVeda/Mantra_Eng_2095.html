ज्ञेया भागं सहसानो वरेण त्वादूतासो मनुवद वदेम |अनूनम अग्निं जुह्वा वचस्याम मधुपृचं धनसां जोहवीमि ||
jñeyā bhāgaṁ sahasāno vareṇa tvādūtāso manuvad vadema | anūnam agniṁ juhvā vacasyā madhupṛcaṁ dhanasā johavīmi ||
  • jñeyā bhāgaṁ sahasāno vareṇa: This phrase can be translated to "With knowledge, I seek the portion that is most excellent." Here, "jñeyā" refers to knowledge or understanding, while "bhāgaṁ" means portion or share. The term "sahasāno" implies a sense of urgency or intensity in seeking, and "vareṇa" signifies the best or most excellent.
  • tvādūtāso manuvad vadema: This translates to "From you, O divine, I speak with intention." "Tvādūtāso" indicates a connection to the divine or higher power, while "manuvad" means with intention or thought. "Vadema" refers to the act of speaking or expressing.
  • anūnam agniṁ juhvā vacasyā: This can be interpreted as "I offer the unblemished fire with my words." "Anūnam" means unblemished or pure, "agniṁ" refers to fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals, and "juhvā" means to offer. "Vacasyā" relates to the power of speech or words.
  • madhupṛcaṁ dhanasā johavīmi: This translates to "I invoke the sweet nectar of wealth." "Madhupṛcaṁ" refers to something that is sweet or nectar-like, often associated with abundance or prosperity. "Dhanasā" means wealth or riches, and "johavīmi" indicates the act of invoking or calling forth.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge, intention, and purity in one's actions and offerings. It reflects a deep connection to the divine and the desire to seek the best in life, both materially and spiritually.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge and wisdom in their personal and professional endeavors. For example, a student may embody the essence of this mantra by striving for excellence in their studies, seeking knowledge with intention, and maintaining integrity in their actions. Similarly, in a business context, leaders can apply this mantra by making decisions that are not only profitable but also ethical and beneficial to society.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder to align one's actions with higher principles, to seek knowledge and understanding, and to offer one's best efforts in all endeavors. By doing so, individuals can cultivate a life of abundance, both materially and spiritually, and contribute positively to the world around them.
