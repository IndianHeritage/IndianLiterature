तां अतनत वयुनं वीरवक्षणं समाण्या वृत्तया विश्वम् आ रजः |अपो अपाचीर अपरā अपेजते प्र पूर्वाभिस तिरते देवयुर जनः ||
tā atnata vayunaṁ vīravakṣaṇaṁ samānyā vṛtayā viśvam ā rajaḥ | apo apācīr aparā apejate pra pūrvābhis tirate devayur janaḥ ||
  • tā atnata vayunaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are strong and powerful." In real life, this can refer to individuals or groups who possess great strength, whether physical, mental, or emotional. For example, athletes who train rigorously to achieve peak performance embody this strength.
  • vīravakṣaṇaṁ: This term translates to "the protector of heroes." In a contemporary context, this could refer to leaders or mentors who guide and support others in their endeavors, much like a coach who nurtures the talents of their team members.
  • samānyā vṛtayā: This means "by the common virtue." It emphasizes the importance of shared values and ethics in society. For instance, community service initiatives that bring people together to work for a common cause reflect this principle.
  • viśvam ā rajaḥ: This phrase translates to "the whole world is filled with radiance." It suggests a universal presence of light and positivity. In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and compassion that illuminate the lives of others, creating a ripple effect of goodwill.
  • apo apācīr aparā apejate: This can be interpreted as "the waters that flow and nourish." Water is essential for life, and this phrase highlights the importance of nurturing and sustaining life. In practical terms, this can relate to environmental conservation efforts aimed at preserving water sources for future generations.
  • pra pūrvābhis tirate: This means "those who move forward with determination." It speaks to the idea of progress and perseverance. In real life, this can be exemplified by entrepreneurs who face challenges head-on to achieve their goals, demonstrating resilience and tenacity.
  • devayur janaḥ: This translates to "the divine energy of the people." It signifies the collective strength and spirit of humanity. In a modern context, this can be seen in social movements where individuals unite for a common cause, harnessing their collective energy to bring about change.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, protection, shared values, positivity, nourishment, determination, and collective energy. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community building, leadership, environmental conservation, and social activism. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can work towards a harmonious and prosperous existence, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in the world.
