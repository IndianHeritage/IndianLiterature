इन्द्रं स्तवां नृतमं यस्य मह्ना विबभाधे रोचनां वि ज्मो अन्तान् |आ यः पप्रौ चर्षणीधृतद वरोभिः प्र सिंधुभ्यो रिरिचानो महित्वा ||
indraṁ stavā nṛtamaṁ yasya mahnā vibabādhe rocanā vi jmo antān | ā yaḥ paprau carṣaṇīdhṛd varobhiḥ pra sindhubhyo riricāno mahitvā ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • stavā: Means praise or hymn, indicating the act of worship or reverence towards Indra.
  • nṛtamaṁ: Translates to the best among men, highlighting the qualities of an ideal leader or hero.
  • yasya: Means whose, indicating possession or association.
  • mahnā: Refers to greatness or vastness, emphasizing the immense power of Indra.
  • vibabādhe: Means to shine or illuminate, suggesting the brilliance of Indra's presence.
  • rocanā: Refers to beauty or radiance, further emphasizing the divine qualities of Indra.
  • vi jmo: Translates to the one who knows, indicating wisdom and knowledge.
  • antān: Means the ends or boundaries, suggesting the far-reaching influence of Indra.
  • ā yaḥ: Means he who, referring to Indra's actions or attributes.
  • paprau: Means to hear or listen, indicating the importance of communication and understanding.
  • carṣaṇīdhṛd: Refers to the one who holds the earth, symbolizing stability and support.
  • varobhiḥ: Means with great strength, emphasizing the power of Indra.
  • pra: A prefix meaning 'before' or 'in front of', indicating precedence.
  • sindhubhyo: Refers to the oceans or waters, symbolizing abundance and life.
  • riricāno: Means to desire or long for, indicating aspiration and ambition.
  • mahitvā: Refers to greatness or magnificence, emphasizing the importance of noble qualities.

This mantra praises Indra, the embodiment of strength, wisdom, and leadership. It highlights the qualities of an ideal leader who not only possesses power but also radiates beauty and brilliance. The mantra suggests that true leadership involves understanding, communication, and the ability to support and stabilize those around you.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership in Organizations: Leaders who embody the qualities of Indra can inspire their teams, communicate effectively, and create a supportive environment.
  • Community Building: Individuals who take on leadership roles in their communities can promote unity, understanding, and collective growth.
  • Personal Development: Striving to embody the qualities of wisdom, strength, and beauty in one's character can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities that define a great leader and the importance of wisdom, strength, and support in our lives. By embodying these attributes, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and organizations, fostering an environment of growth and harmony.
