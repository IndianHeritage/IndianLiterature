न यजमान ऋष्यसि न सुन्वान न देवयो |देवानां य इन् मनो यजमान इयक्षत्य अभीद अयज्वनो भूवत ||
na yajamāna riṣyasi na sunvāna na devayo | devānāṁ ya in mano yajamāna iyakṣaty abhīd ayajvano bhuvat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • yajamāna: the one who performs the sacrifice
    • riṣyasi: you are distressed
    • na: not
    • sunvāna: the one who listens
    • na: not
    • devayo: to the gods
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • ya: who
    • in: in
    • mano: mind
    • yajamāna: the one who performs the sacrifice
    • iyakṣaty: is protected
    • abhīd: is not harmed
    • ayajvano: the one who does not perform the sacrifice
    • bhuvat: becomes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of performing rituals and sacrifices with a pure heart and intention.
    • It suggests that those who do not engage in these practices may face distress or lack protection.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the connection between human actions, divine favor, and the overall well-being of individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and protection. For instance, a family may perform a puja (worship) before starting a new venture to ensure success.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of 'sacrifice' can also be seen in the dedication of time and effort towards personal goals, such as studying hard for exams or working diligently in a job to achieve career advancement.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of spiritual practices and their impact on our lives.
    • It teaches that engaging in rituals with sincerity can lead to divine protection and blessings, while neglecting these practices may result in challenges.
    • In real life, this can be applied by recognizing the value of commitment, whether in spiritual, personal, or professional endeavors, and understanding that our actions have consequences that can affect our well-being.
