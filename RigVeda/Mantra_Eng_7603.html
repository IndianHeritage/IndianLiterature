नेमिं नमन्ति चक्षसा मेषं विप्राः अभिस्वराः |सुदीतयो वो अद्रुहोऽपि कर्णे तरस्विनः सम ऋक्वभिः ||
nemiṁ namanti cakṣasā meṣaṁ viprā abhisvarā | sudītayo vo adruho 'pi karṇe tarasvinaḥ sam ṛkvabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nemiṁ: (to) the wheel or cycle
    • namanti: (they) bow down or pay homage
    • cakṣasā: with the eye or vision
    • meṣaṁ: the ram or sheep
    • viprā: the wise or learned ones
    • abhisvarā: (who are) endowed with sound or voice
    • sudītayo: (with) good thoughts or intentions
    • vo: for you
    • adruho: (without) enmity or malice
    • 'pi: even
    • karṇe: in the ears
    • tarasvinaḥ: (of) the swift or energetic
    • sam: together
    • ṛkvabhiḥ: with hymns or verses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the reverence shown by the wise towards the cycle of life and the natural world.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the continuous cycle of existence, and the "ram" can represent strength and sacrifice.
    • The wise ones, endowed with sound, are those who communicate and share knowledge, promoting harmony.
    • The emphasis on "good thoughts" and "without enmity" highlights the importance of positive intentions in our interactions.
    • The mention of "ears" signifies the importance of listening and understanding in communication.
    • The swift nature of the energetic beings suggests the need for prompt action and responsiveness in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members respect each other's ideas and communicate openly, they create a harmonious environment that fosters creativity and productivity.
    • In personal relationships, practicing good intentions and active listening can lead to stronger bonds and mutual understanding.
    • In community settings, promoting non-violence and cooperation can help resolve conflicts and build a supportive network.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of respect, communication, and positive intentions in our lives.
    • It teaches us to honor the cycles of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • By applying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and understanding world, both personally and collectively.
