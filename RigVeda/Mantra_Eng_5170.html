अग्निं वो देवम् अग्निभिः सजोषा यजिष्टं दूतम् अध्वरे कृणुध्वम् |यो मर्त्येषु निध्रुविर ऋतावा तपुर्मूर्धा घृतान्नः पावकः ||
agniṁ vo devam agnibhiḥ sajoṣā yajiṣṭhaṁ dūtam adhvare kṛṇudhvam | yo martyeṣu nidhruvir ṛtāvā tapurmūrdhā ghṛtānnaḥ pāvakaḥ ||
  • Agniṁ vo devam: This phrase translates to "O Agni, the divine one." Agni represents the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Agnibhiḥ sajoṣā: This means "with the fires." It emphasizes the importance of fire in rituals and its purifying qualities.
  • Yajiṣṭhaṁ: This translates to "the most worthy of worship." It indicates that Agni is the foremost deity to be honored in rituals.
  • Dūtam adhvare kṛṇudhvam: This means "send forth the messenger in the sacrifice." Here, Agni is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, carrying offerings to the gods.
  • Yo martyeṣu nidhruvir ṛtāvā: This translates to "who among mortals is the one who upholds the truth." It highlights the importance of truth and righteousness in human life.
  • Tapurmūrdhā: This means "the one who is the source of heat." It signifies the transformative power of fire, which can be both destructive and purifying.
  • Ghṛtānnaḥ pāvakaḥ: This translates to "the fire that purifies our offerings." It emphasizes the role of Agni in sanctifying the offerings made during rituals.

  • Significance: This mantra underscores the importance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the spiritual realm. It highlights the role of fire in rituals, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the transmission of offerings to the gods.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, the act of cooking can be seen as a ritual where fire transforms raw ingredients into nourishing food, symbolizing the importance of sustenance and community. Additionally, the emphasis on truth and righteousness can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging ethical behavior and integrity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of fire and its role in connecting the material and spiritual worlds. It encourages individuals to uphold truth and righteousness in their lives, making it relevant in both spiritual practices and everyday ethical considerations.
