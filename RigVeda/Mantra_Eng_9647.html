विष्वकर्मा विमना आद विहाय धाता विधाता परमोत संदृक |तेषाम इष्टानि सम इषा मदन्ति यत्रा सप्तर्षीं पर एकम् आहुः ||
viśvakarmā vimanā ād vihāyā dhātā vidhātā paramota saṁdṛk | teṣām iṣṭāni sam iṣā madanti yatrā saptaṛṣīn para ekam āhuḥ ||
  • viśvakarmā: This term refers to the universal architect or creator, symbolizing the divine force that shapes the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the role of an architect or a designer who envisions and constructs buildings or systems that serve a purpose.
  • vimanā: This word translates to "the flying vehicle" or "aerial chariot." It can be associated with modern advancements in technology, such as airplanes or spacecraft, which allow humans to transcend earthly limitations and explore new horizons.
  • ād vihāyā: This phrase means "having abandoned" or "having left behind." It signifies the act of letting go of the past or previous limitations to embrace new possibilities. In life, this can be seen in personal growth, where individuals leave behind old habits or mindsets to pursue new opportunities.
  • dhātā: This term means "the sustainer" or "the one who supports." It represents the nurturing aspect of creation, akin to a parent or guardian who provides for and protects their children. In a broader sense, it can refer to the environment that sustains life.
  • vidhātā: This word means "the ordainer" or "the one who establishes." It reflects the idea of a higher power or force that governs the laws of nature and existence. In real life, this can be compared to the principles of justice and order in society.
  • paramota: This term translates to "the highest" or "the supreme." It signifies the ultimate reality or truth that transcends all forms and manifestations. In a practical sense, it can be related to the pursuit of enlightenment or self-realization.
  • saṁdṛk: This word means "to observe" or "to witness." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our actions and thoughts. In daily life, this can be applied through practices like meditation or reflective thinking.
  • teṣām iṣṭāni: This phrase means "their desires" or "their wishes." It highlights the aspirations and goals that individuals or communities strive for. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of dreams, whether personal or collective.
  • sam iṣā madanti: This translates to "they rejoice in the same command." It signifies unity and harmony in purpose among individuals or groups. In a practical context, this can be observed in teamwork and collaboration, where shared goals lead to collective success.
  • yatrā saptaṛṣīn para ekam āhuḥ: This phrase refers to the "seven sages" or "saptarishis," who are revered figures in Vedic literature. It suggests that there is a singular truth or path that these wise beings recognize. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of wisdom and guidance in navigating life's complexities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, unity, and the pursuit of higher truths. It emphasizes the importance of letting go of past limitations, embracing new possibilities, and working together towards common goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, teamwork, and the quest for knowledge and enlightenment. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces at play, individuals can find greater meaning and purpose in their lives.
