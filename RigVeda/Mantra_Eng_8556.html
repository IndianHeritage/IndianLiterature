अपघ्नन्न एषि पवमान शत्रून प्रियां न जारो अभिगीत इन्दुः |सीदन वनेषु शकुनो न पत्वा सोमः पुनानः कलशेषु सत्तā ||
apaghnann eṣi pavamāna śatrūn priyāṁ na jāro abhigīta induḥ | sīdan vaneṣu śakuno na patvā somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśeṣu sattā ||
  • Mantra: "apaghnann eṣi pavamāna śatrūn priyāṁ na jāro abhigīta induḥ | sīdan vaneṣu śakuno na patvā somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśeṣu sattā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apaghnann: One who removes or dispels.
    • eṣi: You (the divine or the essence).
    • pavamāna: The purifying or sacred (often referring to the wind or breath).
    • śatrūn: Enemies or obstacles.
    • priyāṁ: Beloved or dear.
    • na: Not.
    • jāro: One who is born or produced.
    • abhigīta: Invoked or called upon.
    • induḥ: Moon or nectar (often referring to the soma).
    • sīdan: Sitting or residing.
    • vaneṣu: In the forests or wilderness.
    • śakuno: Birds or messengers.
    • na: Not.
    • patvā: Having flown or taken flight.
    • somaḥ: The sacred drink or essence.
    • punānaḥ: Purifying or cleansing.
    • kalaśeṣu: In the vessels or containers.
    • sattā: Existence or being.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of purification and the removal of obstacles.
    • The invocation of the divine essence (pavamāna) suggests a call for spiritual cleansing and protection against adversities (śatrūn).
    • The reference to the moon (induḥ) symbolizes nourishment and the vital essence that sustains life.
    • The imagery of birds (śakuno) not taking flight in the wilderness indicates a state of stagnation or being held back, which can be interpreted as the challenges we face in life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of the sacred drink (soma) in purifying our existence (sattā) and restoring balance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of stress or conflict, individuals often seek meditation or spiritual practices to dispel negativity and restore peace, akin to the purification mentioned in the mantra.
    • Communities may come together in rituals or gatherings to invoke blessings and protection against adversities, reflecting the collective call for divine assistance.
    • In personal development, the idea of overcoming obstacles resonates with individuals striving to achieve their goals despite challenges, paralleling the removal of enemies (śatrūn).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of purification and the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the belief that through spiritual practices, one can cleanse their existence and foster a harmonious life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to engage in practices that promote peace, protection, and the removal of obstacles, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
