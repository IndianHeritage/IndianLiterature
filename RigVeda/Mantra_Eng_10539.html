यो अस्य पारि रजसः शुक्रो अग्निः अजायत | स नः पार्षद् अति द्विषः ||
yo asya pāre rajasaḥ śukro agnir ajāyata | sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • asya: of this
    • pāre: beyond
    • rajasaḥ: of the realm of passion or activity
    • śukro: pure or bright
    • agnir: fire
    • ajāyata: was born or came into being
    • sa: he
    • naḥ: our
    • parṣad: assembly or council
    • ati: exceedingly
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies or those who oppose
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity or force that transcends the material world, represented by the term "pāre rajasaḥ," which refers to the realm beyond passion and activity.
    • The mention of "śukro agnir" signifies the purity and brightness of fire, symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and transformation.
    • The phrase "sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ" invokes this divine force to protect and guide us against our enemies or adversaries, suggesting a plea for support in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing competition or challenges, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or higher powers to navigate through difficulties, similar to invoking the divine force in this mantra.
    • In personal life, during times of conflict or strife, people may turn to spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, to seek clarity and strength, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher wisdom and guidance in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of a force that transcends our immediate struggles, encouraging individuals to connect with their inner strength and seek support from the universe or divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of adversity, reminding us to look beyond our immediate circumstances and trust in a greater power for protection and guidance.
