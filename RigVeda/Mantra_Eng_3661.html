हृणीयमाणो अप हि मद ऐयेः प्र मे देवानां व्रतपा उवाच |इन्द्रो विद्वांऽ अनु हि त्वा चचक्ष तेनाहम अग्ने अनुशिष्ट आगाम ||
hṛṇīyamāno apa hi mad aiyeḥ pra me devānāṁ vratapā uvāca | indro vidvām̐ anu hi tvā cacakṣa tenāham agne anuśiṣṭa āgām ||
  • hṛṇīyamāno: This term can be understood as "being heard" or "listened to." It signifies the importance of communication and the need for divine attention in our prayers and actions.
  • apa hi mad aiyeḥ: This phrase translates to "come to me, O divine beings." It reflects a call for connection with the divine, emphasizing the human desire for guidance and support from higher powers.
  • pra me devānāṁ: This means "for me, O gods." It indicates a personal appeal to the deities, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine, where individuals seek blessings and assistance.
  • vratapā uvāca: This can be interpreted as "the one who observes vows speaks." It suggests the significance of commitment and discipline in spiritual practices, where adherence to vows is essential for spiritual growth.
  • indro vidvām̐: This phrase translates to "Indra, the wise one." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, representing strength and wisdom. This highlights the importance of wisdom in leadership and decision-making.
  • anu hi tvā cacakṣa: This means "I will follow you." It signifies loyalty and the willingness to learn from the divine, emphasizing the importance of guidance in our lives.
  • tenāham agne anuśiṣṭa āgām: This translates to "by that, I am instructed, O Agni." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification. This line underscores the role of divine instruction in personal development and the pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of communication with the divine, the commitment to spiritual practices, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of decision-making, where individuals seek clarity and direction. It serves as a reminder to remain open to divine guidance, to observe commitments in our spiritual journeys, and to value wisdom in our actions. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and enhance their personal growth and understanding.
