मन्त्र: द्वे नप्तुर देववतः शते गोर द्वा रथाः वधूमन्ता सुदासः |अर्हन्न अग्ने पैजवनेस्य दानं होतेव सद्म पर्य एमी रेब्हन ||
dve naptur devavataḥ śate gor dvā rathā vadhūmantā sudāsaḥ | arhann agne paijavanasya dānaṁ hoteva sadma pary emi rebhan ||
  • Mantra: "dve naptur devavataḥ śate gor dvā rathā vadhūmantā sudāsaḥ |
  • Translation: "Two grandsons of the divine, with a hundred cows, two chariots, and a bride, Sudasa."
  • Explanation:
    • Dve naptur: Refers to "two grandsons," symbolizing the continuation of lineage and the importance of family in Vedic culture.
    • Devavataḥ: Means "of the divine," indicating a connection to the divine or spiritual heritage.
    • Śate gor: "With a hundred cows," signifies wealth and prosperity, as cows were considered a measure of wealth in ancient India.
    • dvā rathā: "Two chariots," representing readiness for action and the ability to move forward in life.
    • vadhūmantā: "With a bride," emphasizes the importance of marriage and family in society.
    • sudāsaḥ: Refers to a specific individual, Sudasa, who is often associated with prosperity and success.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the values of family, prosperity, and divine connection.
    • It reflects the importance of lineage and the blessings of wealth and readiness in life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way families celebrate marriages, emphasizing the union of two families and the continuation of lineage.
    • The mention of cows and chariots can be related to modern symbols of wealth and mobility, such as owning a home or a vehicle.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that underpin a prosperous and fulfilling life.
    • It encourages individuals to honor their heritage, seek prosperity, and build strong family bonds.
    • In contemporary society, these principles can guide personal and communal growth, fostering a sense of responsibility towards family and community.
