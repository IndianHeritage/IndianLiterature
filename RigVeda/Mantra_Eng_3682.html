जुष्टो दामूनाऽतिथिर दुरोण इमं नो यज्ञं उप याहि विद्वान् |विश्वा अग्ने अभियुजो विहत्याऽशत्रूयताम् आ भराऽभोजनानि ||
juṣṭo damūnā atithir duroṇa imaṁ no yajñam upa yāhi vidvān | viśvā agne abhiyujo vihatyā śatrūyatām ā bharā bhojanāni ||
  • juṣṭo damūnā: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is pleased with the offerings." In a real-life context, this can refer to a person or deity who appreciates the efforts and contributions made by others, similar to how a teacher values the hard work of their students.
  • atithir duroṇa: This means "the guest who is difficult to please." In everyday life, this can be likened to a challenging customer or client who has high expectations and requires extra effort to satisfy.
  • imaṁ no yajñam upa yāhi vidvān: This translates to "O wise one, come to our sacrifice." This can be seen as an invitation to someone knowledgeable to partake in a communal effort or project, much like inviting an expert to a workshop or seminar to share their insights.
  • viśvā agne abhiyujo: This phrase means "all beings are united in the fire." It symbolizes the collective energy and spirit of a community working together towards a common goal, akin to a team collaborating on a project where each member contributes their unique skills.
  • vihatyā śatrūyatām ā bharā bhojanāni: This can be interpreted as "may we overcome our enemies and be filled with nourishment." In a practical sense, this reflects the desire for success and abundance in life, whether it be in personal endeavors or in business, where overcoming challenges leads to growth and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of community, collaboration, and the pursuit of knowledge. It highlights the significance of inviting wisdom into our endeavors and working together to achieve common goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in workplaces, community service projects, or even family gatherings where collective efforts lead to shared success and fulfillment. The mantra serves as a reminder that through unity and the sharing of knowledge, we can overcome challenges and thrive together.
