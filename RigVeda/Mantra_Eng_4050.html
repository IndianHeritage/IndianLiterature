आ ग्रावभिर अहन्येभिर अक्तुभिर वरिष्टं वज्रम आ जिघर्ति मायिनि |शतम् वा यस्य प्रचरण स्वे दमे संवार्तयन्तो वि च वर्तयन्न अहा ||
ā grāvabhir ahanyebhir aktubhir variṣṭhaṁ vajram ā jigharti māyini | śataṁ vā yasya pracaran sve dame saṁvartayanto vi ca vartayann ahā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • grāvabhir: with the support of the people
    • ahanyebhir: in the daily activities
    • aktubhir: with strength
    • variṣṭham: the best or the most powerful
    • vajram: thunderbolt or weapon
    • ā: towards
    • jigharti: strikes or attacks
    • māyini: with illusion or power
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • vā: or
    • yasya: whose
    • pracaran: moving or operating
    • sve: in his own
    • dame: home or place
    • saṁvartayanto: causing to revolve or turn
    • vi: indeed
    • ca: and
    • vartayann: maintaining or sustaining
    • ahā: this (or here)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and strength that comes from unity and collective action.
    • The reference to "vajram" symbolizes an indomitable force, akin to the strength found in a community working together.
    • The phrase "with the support of the people" emphasizes the importance of collaboration in achieving goals.
    • In real life, this can be seen in community projects, where individuals come together to create something greater than themselves, such as building a school or organizing a festival.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of collective strength and the impact of working together towards a common goal.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in individual capabilities but in the synergy created through collaboration.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied in team settings, social movements, or any scenario where group effort leads to significant change.
