यथा वः स्वाहाग्नये दाशेम परीळाभिर घृतवद्भिः च हव्यैः |तेभिर नो अग्ने अमितैर महोभिः शतम पूर्वभिर आयसीभिर न पाही ||
yathā vaḥ svāhāgnaye dāśema parīḻābhir ghṛtavadbhiś ca havyaiḥ | tebhir no agne amitair mahobhiḥ śatam pūrbhir āyasībhir ni pāhi ||
  • Mantra: "yathā vaḥ svāhāgnaye dāśema parīḻābhir ghṛtavadbhiś ca havyaiḥ | tebhir no agne amitair mahobhiḥ śatam pūrbhir āyasībhir ni pāhi ||"
  • Translation: "As we offer our oblations to you, O Agni, may we be blessed with abundance through our offerings of ghee and other sacred substances. Protect us, O Agni, with your immense power and strength, as you have done in the past."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yathā: As
    • vaḥ: You
    • svāhā: An expression used during offerings, signifying acceptance
    • agnaye: To Agni, the fire god
    • dāśema: May we be blessed
    • parīḻābhir: With abundance
    • ghṛtavadbhiś: With offerings of ghee
    • havyaiḥ: Sacred substances
    • tebhir: With these
    • no: Us
    • agne: O Agni
    • amitair: Immense
    • mahobhiḥ: With power
    • śatam: A hundred
    • pūrbhir: In the past
    • āyasībhir: With strength
    • ni pāhi: Protect us
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and rituals in seeking blessings from divine forces, particularly Agni, who represents transformation and purification.
    • It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, where offerings are made to invoke protection and abundance.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where people offer food, prayers, or other items to seek blessings for prosperity and safety.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during rituals symbolizes the presence of the divine and is a way to connect with spiritual energies.
    • People often perform rituals during significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, to invoke blessings for a prosperous future.
    • This mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their actions and the importance of gratitude and respect towards the forces that sustain them.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of rituals, and the need for respect towards nature and the divine.
    • By understanding and applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility in their daily lives.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a harmonious relationship with the world around us, fostering both personal and communal well-being.
