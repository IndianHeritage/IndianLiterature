मन्त्र: उता यासि सवितस्त्रीणि रोचनोत सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः सम उच्यसि | उता रात्रिम् उभयतः परीयस उता मित्रो भवसि देव धर्मभिः ||
uta yāsi savitas trīṇi rocanota sūryasya raśmibhiḥ sam ucyasi | uta rātrīm ubhayataḥ parīyasa uta mitro bhavasi deva dharmabhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "uta yāsi savitas trīṇi rocanota sūryasya raśmibhiḥ sam ucyasi | uta rātrīm ubhayataḥ parīyasa uta mitro bhavasi deva dharmabhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Savita, you who bring forth three radiant lights, you are invoked with the rays of the Sun. May you bring the night to us from both sides, and may you be our friend, O divine one, through righteousness."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • yāsi: you go, you lead
    • savitas: O Savita (a name for the Sun)
    • trīṇi: three
    • rocanota: you illuminate, you shine
    • sūryasya: of the Sun
    • raśmibhiḥ: with rays
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • ucyasi: you are spoken of, you are invoked
    • uta: and, also
    • rātrīm: night
    • ubhayataḥ: from both sides
    • parīyasa: may you surround, may you encompass
    • uta: and, also
    • mitro: friend
    • bhavasi: you become
    • deva: divine, god
    • dharmabhiḥ: through righteousness, through virtue

  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Savita, the Sun God, who is revered for his life-giving energy and illumination.
    • The mention of "three radiant lights" can symbolize the three phases of the day: dawn, noon, and dusk, representing the cycle of life and the importance of light in our existence.
    • The invocation of night from both sides signifies the balance between light and darkness, a reminder of the dualities in life.
    • By calling Savita a friend through righteousness, it emphasizes the importance of living a virtuous life and seeking divine guidance in our actions.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be recited during morning prayers to seek the blessings of the Sun for a fruitful day ahead, symbolizing the start of new opportunities.
    • In daily life, it serves as a reminder to maintain balance between work (light) and rest (darkness), promoting a healthy lifestyle.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate friendships based on mutual respect and righteousness, fostering a supportive community.
    • In times of uncertainty, invoking the divine presence can provide comfort and guidance, reinforcing the belief in a higher power that supports us through our journey.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine light and guidance in our lives while emphasizing the importance of balance and righteousness.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community building, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings under the divine light of Savita.
