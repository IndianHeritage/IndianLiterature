मन्त्र: सूर्यरश्मिः हरिकेशः पुरस्तात् सविता ज्योतिरुदयाम् अजयाम् |तस्य पूषा प्रसवे याति विद्वान् सम्पश्यन् विश्व भुवनानि गोपाः ||
sūryaraśmir harikeśaḥ purastāt savitā jyotir ud ayām̐ ajasram | tasya pūṣā prasave yāti vidvān sampaśyan viśvā bhuvanāni gopāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryaraśmir: The rays of the sun
    • harikeśaḥ: The one with golden hair (often referring to the sun)
    • purastāt: In front or before
    • savitā: The sun or the divine source of light
    • jyotir: Light or radiance
    • ud ayām̐: Rising or emerging
    • ajasram: Always or continuously
    • tasya: Of that (the sun)
    • pūṣā: Nourisher or sustainer
    • prasave: In creation or birth
    • yāti: Goes or moves
    • vidvān: The wise or knowledgeable one
    • sampaśyan: Observing or seeing
    • viśvā: All or universal
    • bhuvanāni: Worlds or realms
    • gopāḥ: Protectors or guardians
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the sun, symbolizing light, knowledge, and life. The sun's rays are seen as a source of nourishment and vitality.
    • The phrase "sūryaraśmir harikeśaḥ" emphasizes the beauty and brilliance of the sun, which is essential for life on Earth.
    • "Purastāt savitā jyotir" indicates that the sun is always present in front of us, guiding and illuminating our path.
    • The term "ajasram" suggests the continuous nature of the sun's light, which is a metaphor for the constant presence of knowledge and wisdom in our lives.
    • "Tasaya pūṣā prasave" highlights the nurturing aspect of the sun, akin to how sunlight helps plants grow, which can be related to personal growth and development in individuals.
    • "Vidvān sampaśyan viśvā bhuvanāni gopāḥ" speaks to the wise individuals who observe and protect the various realms of existence, akin to guardians of knowledge and wisdom in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the sun's rays are crucial for the growth of crops, symbolizing how essential light and knowledge are for nurturing life.
    • In education, teachers can be seen as the "gopāḥ" (guardians) of knowledge, guiding students through the realms of learning.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek wisdom and understanding can be likened to the "vidvān," striving to observe and learn from the world around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of light, knowledge, and nurturing in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and to be aware of the continuous presence of guidance in our journey.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace learning, protect knowledge, and appreciate the nurturing aspects of life, much like the sun nurtures all living beings.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to be vigilant observers of the world, fostering growth and understanding in ourselves and others.
