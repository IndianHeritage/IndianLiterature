अवसृजन् उप तमनā देवān यक्षि वनस्पते |अग्निः हव्यā सुषूदति देवो देवेषु मेधिरः ||
avasṛjann upa tmanā devān yakṣi vanaspate | agnir havyā suṣūdati devo deveṣu medhiraḥ ||
  • Avasṛjann: This word can be translated as "casting forth" or "releasing." It signifies the act of bringing forth or manifesting something into existence.
  • Upa tmanā: This phrase means "with the self" or "with one's own essence." It suggests a connection to one's inner self or consciousness.
  • Devān: This translates to "the gods." In the context of the mantra, it refers to divine beings or higher powers that govern various aspects of life.
  • Yakṣi: This word means "to bestow" or "to grant." It implies the act of giving or providing something valuable.
  • Vanaspate: This term refers to "the lord of the forest" or "the plant." It symbolizes nature, growth, and the sustenance provided by the earth.
  • Agnir: This means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire is often associated with transformation, energy, and purification.
  • Havyā: This word translates to "offering" or "sacrifice." It signifies the act of giving something valuable to the divine.
  • Suṣūdati: This means "he who is well-pleased" or "he who is satisfied." It indicates the fulfillment of desires or needs.
  • Devo: This translates to "the god" or "divine being." It emphasizes the presence of divinity in the act of offering.
  • Deveṣu: This means "among the gods." It suggests a communal aspect of worship or offering to multiple divine entities.
  • Medhiraḥ: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It refers to the quality of discernment and understanding in the context of spiritual practices.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to manifest divine energies and blessings through offerings made with a pure heart and intention. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with one's inner self while recognizing the divine presence in nature and the universe.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of casting forth and connecting with the self, individuals can use this principle to focus on personal development and self-awareness. For example, engaging in meditation or self-reflection can help one understand their true essence.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to vanaspate (nature) highlights the importance of respecting and nurturing the environment. People can take action by participating in conservation efforts or sustainable practices.
  • Community and Offerings: The act of giving (yakṣi) can be seen in community service or charitable acts. By contributing to the welfare of others, individuals can create a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra encourages offerings to the divine, which can be practiced through rituals, prayers, or acts of gratitude. This can foster a deeper connection with spirituality and enhance one's sense of fulfillment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of self, nature, and the divine. It encourages individuals to manifest their inner potential while recognizing the importance of giving and nurturing both themselves and the world around them. By applying these principles in daily life, one can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
