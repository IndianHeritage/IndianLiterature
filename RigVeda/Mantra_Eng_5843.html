ध्रुवासु त्वासु क्षितिषु क्षीयन्तो व्य अ1स्मत पाशं वरुणो मुमोचत् |अवो वन्बानाऽदितेरुपस्थाद् यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
dhruvāsu tvāsu kṣitiṣu kṣiyanto vy a1smat pāśaṁ varuṇo mumocat | avo vanvānā aditer upasthād yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, who resides in the firmament, may you release us from the bonds that bind us. As we approach you, may you protect us with your blessings, always."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhruvāsu: Refers to the eternal or unchanging aspects of existence.
    • tvāsu: Addressing Varuna, the deity of cosmic order and water.
    • kṣitiṣu: Pertaining to the earth or the physical realm.
    • kṣiyanto: Refers to the act of being released or liberated.
    • pāśaṁ: Means bonds or ties that bind.
    • varuṇo: Again, addressing Varuna, emphasizing his role as a liberator.
    • mumocat: To release or free from bondage.
    • avo: A call for protection or guidance.
    • vanvānā: Refers to the one who is benevolent or kind.
    • aditer: Referring to Aditi, the mother of gods, symbolizing infinity and the cosmos.
    • upasthād: To approach or come near.
    • yūyam: You (plural), addressing the divine forces.
    • pāta: To protect or safeguard.
    • svastibhiḥ: With blessings or auspiciousness.
    • sadā: Always or forever.
    • naḥ: Us or our.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of distress or uncertainty, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or protection, similar to how the mantra calls upon Varuna for liberation from bonds.
    • In a community setting, people may come together to pray for collective well-being, invoking the blessings of higher powers to safeguard their unity and harmony.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new beginnings, rituals are performed to seek divine blessings, reflecting the essence of this mantra's plea for protection and auspiciousness.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking liberation from the constraints of life and the need for divine protection. In a world filled with challenges, the invocation of Varuna serves as a reminder of the cosmic order and the benevolence of the universe. It encourages individuals to seek spiritual guidance and maintain a connection with the divine, fostering a sense of security and peace in their lives. This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or significant life transitions, reinforcing the belief in a higher power that watches over and protects us.
