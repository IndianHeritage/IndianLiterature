यो नः पिता जनिता यो विधाता धामानि वेद भुवनानि विश्वा |यो देवानां नामधा एक एव तं सम्प्रश्नं भुवना यन्त्य अन्याः ||
yo naḥ pitā janitā yo vidhātā dhāmāni veda bhuvanāni viśvā | yo devānāṁ nāmadhā eka eva taṁ sampraśnam bhuvanā yanty anyā ||
  • Translation: "He who is our father, the creator, who knows the worlds and all beings; He who is the sole name of the gods, to Him all beings turn for inquiry."

  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • yo naḥ pitā: Refers to the divine figure as a father, symbolizing care, guidance, and protection.
    • janitā: The creator or originator, indicating the source of life and existence.
    • vidhātā: The one who establishes or ordains, suggesting a role in maintaining order in the universe.
    • dhāmāni veda: Knows the realms or worlds, emphasizing omniscience and the understanding of all dimensions of existence.
    • bhuvanāni viśvā: All beings or creatures, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
    • devānāṁ nāmadhā: The sole name of the gods, indicating a singular divine essence that encompasses all deities.
    • sampraśnam: Inquiry or questioning, reflecting the human quest for knowledge and understanding.
    • bhuvanā yanty anyā: All beings turn towards Him, signifying the universal search for truth and guidance.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, people often seek guidance from a higher power or spiritual leader, much like turning to the divine father for wisdom.
    • Parents play a crucial role in shaping their children's lives, akin to the creator's role in the universe, providing support and direction.
    • In scientific inquiry, researchers seek to understand the laws of nature, paralleling the quest for knowledge mentioned in the mantra.
    • Communities often come together in prayer or meditation, seeking answers and guidance from a higher source, reflecting the collective inquiry of beings.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of a divine presence that acts as a father figure, creator, and source of knowledge. It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the universal quest for understanding and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in the ways individuals and communities seek support, wisdom, and answers to life's questions, whether through spirituality, family, or scientific exploration. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking knowledge and connection in our lives, fostering a sense of unity and purpose among all beings.
