एष द्रप्सो वृषभो विश्वरूप इन्द्राय वृष्णे सम अकारी सोमः |एतम पिब हरिवः स्थातर उग्र यस्येशिषे प्रदिवि यस ते अन्नम् ||
eṣa drapso vṛṣabho viśvarūpa indrāya vṛṣṇe sam akāri somaḥ | etam piba harivaḥ sthātar ugra yasyeśiṣe pradivi yas te annam ||
  • eṣa drapso vṛṣabho viśvarūpa indrāya vṛṣṇe sam akāri somaḥ
    • eṣa - This means "this" or "here." It indicates the presence of something significant.
    • drapso - Refers to "the drink" or "the essence," often associated with the divine nectar or soma.
    • vṛṣabho - Translates to "the bull," symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • viśvarūpa - Means "of many forms," indicating the omnipresence and multifaceted nature of the divine.
    • indrāya - Refers to Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • vṛṣṇe - Refers to the divine or the one who is worthy of worship, often associated with the Vishnu aspect.
    • sam akāri - Means "has made" or "has created," indicating the act of bringing forth something significant.
    • somaḥ - Refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • etam piba harivaḥ sthātar ugra
    • etam - Means "this," referring to the soma or divine essence being offered.
    • piba - Translates to "drink," indicating the act of consuming the divine essence.
    • harivaḥ - Refers to "the one who brings joy," often associated with the divine or auspicious beings.
    • sthātar - Means "the one who is stable" or "the one who supports," indicating a foundation or source of strength.
    • ugra - Translates to "fierce" or "powerful," indicating the strength and intensity of the divine presence.
  • yasyeśiṣe pradivi yas te annam
    • yasyeśiṣe - Means "whose command" or "whose will," indicating the authority of the divine.
    • pradivi - Refers to "in the sky" or "in the heavens," indicating the celestial realm.
    • yas - Means "who" or "which," referring back to the divine presence.
    • te - Means "your," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
    • annam - Refers to "food" or "nourishment," symbolizing sustenance and the essential needs of life.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of the divine essence (soma) and its role in providing strength, nourishment, and stability in life. It highlights the multifaceted nature of divinity and the connection between the divine and the earthly realm. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to seek strength and guidance in challenging situations, reminding us to draw upon the divine for support and sustenance. It encourages individuals to recognize the presence of the divine in their lives and to consume the 'soma' of experiences that bring joy and stability, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and empowered existence.
