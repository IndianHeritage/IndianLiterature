वृषभं चर्षणीनां विश्वरूपम अदाभ्यम् |बृहस्पतिं वरेण्यम् ||
vṛṣabhaṁ carṣaṇīnāṁ viśvarūpam adābhyam | bṛhaspatiṁ vareṇyam ||
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This word translates to "the bull" or "the strong one." In many cultures, the bull symbolizes strength, power, and fertility. For example, in agriculture, a bull is often seen as a vital asset for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity.
  • carṣaṇīnāṁ: This term refers to "the one who is associated with the earth" or "the one who is grounded." It signifies stability and connection to the physical world. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who are practical and down-to-earth, often serving as the backbone of their communities.
  • viśvarūpam: This means "of universal form" or "having many forms." It suggests adaptability and the ability to take on various roles. For instance, a leader may embody different qualities depending on the situation, showcasing versatility in their approach.
  • adābhyam: This word can be interpreted as "without fear" or "unhindered." It conveys a sense of courage and the ability to face challenges head-on. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by individuals who confront their fears, such as public speaking or pursuing a new career path.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ: This refers to "Bṛhaspati," the deity of wisdom and knowledge. Bṛhaspati is often associated with guidance and intellect. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking wisdom from mentors or educators who help shape our understanding of the world.
  • vareṇyam: This translates to "worthy of worship" or "to be revered." It emphasizes the importance of respect and admiration for those who possess wisdom and strength. In real life, this can be seen in how we honor leaders, teachers, or anyone who contributes positively to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and adaptability. It encourages individuals to embody these qualities in their lives, promoting a sense of courage and reverence for knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to approach challenges with confidence, seek wisdom from those we respect, and remain grounded in our values. By integrating these principles, we can foster personal growth and contribute positively to our communities.
