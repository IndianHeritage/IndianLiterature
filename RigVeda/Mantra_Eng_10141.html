अग्नि हंसि न्यात्रिणं दीद्यान मर्त्येष्वा |स्वे क्षये शुचिव्रत ||
agne haṁsi ny a1triṇaṁ dīdyan martyeṣv ā | sve kṣaye śucivrata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • Haṁsi: You are (or you are present)
    • Ny a1triṇaṁ: In the form of the one who is the protector
    • Dīdyan: Shining or illuminating
    • Martyeṣv: Among mortals
    • Ā: Indeed
    • Sve: In your own
    • Kṣaye: Destruction or end
    • Śucivrata: Pure in vow or intention
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his presence as a protector among humans.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity in intentions and actions, especially in the face of destruction or challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a personal crisis or a natural disaster, invoking the spirit of Agni can symbolize seeking guidance and protection.
    • Individuals who practice mindfulness and maintain purity in their thoughts and actions can relate to the concept of "śucivrata," striving to keep their intentions clear and focused.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, encouraging us to seek protection and guidance from higher powers.
    • It highlights the importance of maintaining purity in our intentions, which can lead to positive outcomes in our personal and professional lives.
    • In rituals and prayers, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and protection, especially during significant life events or transitions.
