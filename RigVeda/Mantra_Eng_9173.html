सनेमा तत् सुसनिता sanitvabhir वयं जीवाः जीवपुत्राः अनागसः |ब्रह्मद्विषो विश्वग एनो भरैरता तद् देवानाम् अवो अद्याऽ वृणीमहे ||
sanema tat susanitā sanitvabhir vayaṁ jīvā jīvaputrā anāgasaḥ | brahmadviṣo viṣvag eno bharerata tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Sanema tat susanitā sanitvabhir vayaṁ jīvā jīvaputrā anāgasaḥ:
    • Sanema: This word translates to "we seek" or "we desire." It indicates a collective intention or aspiration.
    • Tat: Meaning "that," it refers to a higher state or ideal that is being sought.
    • Susanitā: This can be interpreted as "well-being" or "good health," suggesting a desire for a harmonious state of existence.
    • Sanitvabhir: This word relates to "purity" or "wholesomeness," emphasizing the importance of maintaining a clean and virtuous life.
    • Vayaṁ: Meaning "we," it signifies a collective identity or community.
    • Jīvā: This translates to "life" or "living beings," highlighting the essence of existence.
    • Jīvaputrā: Meaning "children of life," it refers to all living beings as interconnected and part of a larger family.
    • Anāgasaḥ: This term means "innocent" or "free from sin," suggesting a state of purity and moral integrity.
  • Brahmadviṣo viṣvag eno bharerata:
    • Brahmadviṣo: This refers to "those who are hostile to the divine" or "enemies of the cosmic order," indicating a caution against negativity and harmful influences.
    • Viṣvag: Meaning "all" or "universal," it emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe.
    • Eno: This can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be guided," suggesting a plea for divine guidance.
    • Bharerata: This term means "to bear" or "to carry," indicating the responsibility of individuals to uphold and protect the cosmic order.
  • Tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe:
    • Tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the divine or higher power being invoked.
    • Devānām: This translates to "of the gods," indicating a connection to the divine and the spiritual realm.
    • Avo: Meaning "may we be granted" or "may we receive," it expresses a desire for blessings.
    • Adyā: This means "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the request.
    • Vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating an active participation in seeking divine favor.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for collective well-being, purity, and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of living a life free from negativity and moral corruption while recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, spiritual practices, or personal reflections, where individuals seek harmony, protection from negative influences, and a connection to the divine. By invoking this mantra, one can foster a sense of unity, responsibility, and a commitment to uphold the values of purity and innocence in their lives and communities.
