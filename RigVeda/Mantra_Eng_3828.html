अनस्वन्ता सत्पतिर मामहे मे गावा चेतिष्ठो असुरो मग्नोः |त्रैवृष्णो अग्ने दशभिः सहस्रैर वैश्वानर त्र्यरुणश चिकेट ||
anasvantā satpatir māmahe me gāvā cetiṣṭho asuro maghonaḥ | traivṛṣṇo agne daśabhiḥ sahasrair vaiśvānara tryaruṇaś ciketa ||
  • anasvantā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is not lacking" or "the one who is complete." In real life, this can refer to individuals who possess a sense of fulfillment and abundance, whether in material wealth, emotional stability, or spiritual enlightenment.
  • satpatir: This means "the one who follows the right path" or "the righteous one." In everyday life, this can be exemplified by individuals who adhere to ethical principles and strive to make morally sound decisions, such as community leaders or activists advocating for justice.
  • māmahe: This translates to "we meditate upon" or "we contemplate." This reflects the importance of mindfulness and reflection in our lives, akin to practices like meditation or journaling that help individuals gain clarity and focus.
  • me: This word means "my" or "mine," indicating a personal connection or ownership. It emphasizes the importance of personal responsibility and the impact of individual actions on the collective.
  • gāvā: This term refers to "cows," which in Vedic culture symbolize wealth, nourishment, and sustenance. In modern contexts, this can relate to the importance of agriculture and animal husbandry in sustaining communities and economies.
  • cetiṣṭho: This can be interpreted as "the one who is steadfast" or "the one who is firm." This quality is essential in various aspects of life, such as maintaining resilience in the face of challenges or staying committed to one's goals.
  • asuro: This term means "the divine" or "the celestial." It signifies the connection to higher powers or spiritual forces that guide and protect us in our endeavors.
  • maghonaḥ: This translates to "of great strength" or "of great power." In real life, this can refer to individuals or communities that exhibit strength in unity, such as social movements that bring about significant change.
  • traivṛṣṇo: This term can be understood as "the one who is threefold" or "the one who encompasses all." It reflects the idea of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings, similar to how diverse communities come together for a common cause.
  • agne: This means "O Agni," referring to the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of fire in various cultures for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • daśabhiḥ: This translates to "with ten" or "by ten." This can symbolize completeness or wholeness, as the number ten often represents a full cycle or a complete set in various cultures.
  • sahasrair: This means "with thousands" or "by thousands," indicating abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the context of large gatherings, festivals, or community events that celebrate unity and shared joy.
  • vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, representing the connection between the individual and the universe. It signifies the importance of recognizing our place in the larger scheme of existence.
  • tryaruṇaś: This can be interpreted as "the threefold youth" or "the youthful one." It symbolizes vitality, renewal, and the potential for growth and change, akin to the energy of youth in driving innovation and progress.
  • ciketa: This means "he has understood" or "he has perceived." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and understanding in navigating life's complexities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of completeness, righteousness, mindfulness, personal responsibility, strength, and interconnectedness. Its significance lies in the reminder to strive for a balanced and ethical life, recognizing the abundance around us and the divine forces that guide our actions. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community building, and fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our endeavors. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
