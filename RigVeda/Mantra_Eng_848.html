मन्त्र: कस ते जामिर जनानाम् अग्ने को दाश्वध्वरः | को ह कस्मिन्न असि श्रितः ||
kas te jāmir janānām agne ko dāśvadhvaraḥ | ko ha kasminn asi śritaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kas: Who
    • te: You
    • jāmir: Born
    • janānām: Of beings/people
    • agne: O Fire (Agni)
    • ko: Who
    • dāśvadhvaraḥ: The one who is the master of the ten (dāśa) directions
    • ko: Who
    • ha: Indeed
    • kasminn: In whom
    • asi: You are
    • śritaḥ: Dependent or sheltered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions directed towards Agni, the fire god, asking about his origin and his role among beings.
    • The first part, "kas te jāmir janānām agne," inquires about who Agni is in relation to the beings of the world, suggesting a search for understanding of his divine nature.
    • The second part, "ko dāśvadhvaraḥ," refers to Agni as the master of the ten directions, symbolizing his omnipresence and control over the universe.
    • The final inquiry, "ko ha kasminn asi śritaḥ," asks who is dependent on Agni, highlighting the importance of fire in sustaining life and its role as a source of energy and warmth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a sacred element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked as a witness to vows and offerings, emphasizing the importance of fire in spiritual practices.
    • In modern life, fire represents energy and transformation. For example, in cooking, fire transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, showcasing its essential role in daily life.
    • Fire safety and management are crucial in urban planning and disaster management, reflecting the need to respect and understand the power of fire in our environments.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a central figure in both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It serves as a reminder of our dependence on natural elements and the need to respect and harness them wisely.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding of the forces that govern their lives, fostering a sense of connection with the universe.
