मन्त्र: एते सोमाः अभि प्रियं इन्द्रस्य कामं अक्षरम् |वर्धन्तोऽस्य वीर्यम् ||
ete somā abhi priyam indrasya kāmam akṣaran | vardhanto asya vīryam ||
  • Translation: "These (soma) are dear to Indra, the desires that are imperishable; they enhance his strength."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • somā: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and immortality
    • abhi: Towards
    • priyam: Dear or beloved
    • indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • kāmam: Desires or wishes
    • akṣaran: Imperishable or eternal
    • vardhanto: Enhancing or increasing
    • asya: His
    • vīryam: Strength or power

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks to the relationship between the sacred drink Soma and the deity Indra. Soma is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration, while Indra represents strength and power.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be interpreted as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our desires and aspirations that are aligned with our higher selves. Just as Soma enhances Indra's strength, our positive desires can empower us in our personal and professional lives.
    • For example, when individuals pursue their passions—be it in art, science, or social work—they are essentially consuming their own 'Soma,' which fuels their inner strength and creativity.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning our desires with our true purpose. It suggests that when our aspirations are pure and noble, they contribute to our growth and strength.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various aspects of life, such as setting career goals, engaging in community service, or pursuing personal development. By focusing on what truly matters to us, we can enhance our capabilities and make a positive impact on the world around us.
