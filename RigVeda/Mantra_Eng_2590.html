विपाजसां पृथुनाशोशुचानो बादस्व द्विषो रक्षसो अमीवाः |सुशर्मणो बृहतः शर्मणि स्याम अग्नेर अहं सुहवस्य प्रणीतौ ||
vi pājasā pṛthunā śośucāno bādhasva dviṣo rakṣaso amīvāḥ | suśarmaṇo bṛhataḥ śarmaṇi syām agner ahaṁ suhavasya praṇītau ||
  • vi pājasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with power." It emphasizes the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
  • pṛthunā: This word means "great" or "vast." It signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the challenges that come with it.
  • śośucāno: This term can be understood as "purifying" or "cleansing." It suggests the need for purification in thoughts and actions.
  • bādhasva: This means "to remove" or "to dispel." It indicates the act of overcoming obstacles or negativity.
  • dviṣo: This word translates to "enemies" or "those who oppose." It highlights the presence of adversities in life.
  • rakṣaso: This refers to "demons" or "evil forces." It symbolizes the negative influences that can affect one's life.
  • amīvāḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are not friendly" or "hostile." It reinforces the idea of facing opposition.
  • suśarmaṇo: This means "good fortune" or "auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of seeking positive outcomes.
  • bṛhataḥ: This translates to "great" or "large." It signifies the vastness of blessings and opportunities available.
  • śarmaṇi: This word means "protection" or "shelter." It indicates the need for safety and security in life.
  • syām: This means "may I be" or "let me be." It expresses a desire for a certain state of being.
  • agner: This refers to "of fire." Fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and purification.
  • ahaṁ: This means "I" or "myself." It emphasizes personal responsibility and self-awareness.
  • suhavasya: This can be interpreted as "of good company" or "of good association." It highlights the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences.
  • praṇītau: This means "to lead" or "to guide." It signifies the importance of guidance in one's journey.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength and purification in the face of adversities. It emphasizes the importance of dispelling negativity and surrounding oneself with positive influences. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as overcoming personal challenges, fostering positive relationships, and seeking guidance in difficult times. The mantra serves as a reminder to focus on personal growth, resilience, and the pursuit of good fortune while navigating the complexities of life.
