आ त्व अद्य सबरदुघां हुवे गायत्रवेपसम् |इन्द्रं धेनुं सुदुघाम् अन्याम् इषम् उरुधाराम् अरंक्रितम् ||
ā tv a1dya sabardughāṁ huve gāyatravepasam | indraṁ dhenuṁ sudughām anyām iṣam urudhārām araṁkṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā tv: To you (Indra)
    • a1dya: Today
    • sabardughāṁ: I call upon the one who is the best among the strong
    • huve: I invoke
    • gāyatrave: For the Gāyatrī (a sacred meter)
    • pasam: For the purpose of protection
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • dhenuṁ: The cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • sudughām: The one who is well-nourished
    • anyām: Other
    • iṣam: Power or strength
    • urudhārām: The one who is capable of bearing great burdens
    • araṁkṛtam: The one who is adorned or decorated
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The invocation emphasizes the importance of nourishment and protection, symbolized by the cow, which is a vital source of sustenance in agrarian societies.
    • The reference to Gāyatrī suggests a connection to spiritual wisdom and enlightenment, indicating that the prayer is not just for material wealth but also for spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Indra for rain is a common practice, as rain is essential for crops. This mantra can be recited during rituals to seek blessings for a good harvest.
    • The cow is often revered in many cultures as a symbol of wealth and prosperity. This mantra can be relevant in contexts where livestock is essential for livelihood.
    • In modern life, the principles of seeking strength and nourishment can be applied to personal development, where individuals strive for both physical and spiritual well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of material and spiritual needs, emphasizing the importance of seeking divine support for both physical sustenance and inner strength.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine as a source of protection and nourishment, applicable in various aspects of life, from agriculture to personal growth.
    • In contemporary society, the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, nurturing both their physical and spiritual selves.
