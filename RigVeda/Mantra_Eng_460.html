यद यूयम् पृष्णिमातरः मर्तासः स्यातन |स्तोता वो अमृतः स्यात ||
yad yūyam pṛśnimātaro martāsaḥ syātana | stotā vo amṛtaḥ syāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • yūyam: you (plural)
    • pṛśnimātaro: those who are seekers of truth
    • martāsaḥ: mortals or human beings
    • syātana: may become
    • stotā: praise or worship
    • vo: for you
    • amṛtaḥ: immortal or eternal
    • syāt: may be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of human existence and the pursuit of truth.
    • It suggests that whatever you, as seekers of truth, may become, through your praise and worship, you can attain immortality.
    • The term "amṛtaḥ" signifies the eternal nature of the soul, implying that through spiritual practices, one can transcend the mortal experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the act of worship or praise is seen as a way to connect with the divine. For instance, in Christianity, prayer is a means to seek guidance and strength, which can lead to a sense of eternal peace.
    • In modern life, individuals often engage in mindfulness and meditation practices, which can be viewed as a form of seeking truth and understanding oneself, leading to personal growth and a sense of immortality through legacy.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement for individuals to seek truth and engage in practices that elevate their spiritual existence.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals strive for personal development, spiritual awakening, and a deeper understanding of their purpose.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that through our actions and intentions, we can achieve a state of being that transcends the physical realm, leading to a legacy that endures beyond our mortal lives.
