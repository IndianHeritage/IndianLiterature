यस्तित्याज साचिविदं सखायं न तस्य वाच्य अपि भागो अस्ति |यद ईं श्रिणोति अलकम् श्रिणोति न हि प्रवेद सुखृतस्य पन्थाम् ||
yas tityāja sacividaṁ sakhāyaṁ na tasya vācy api bhāgo asti | yad īṁ śṛṇoty alakaṁ śṛṇoti nahi praveda sukṛtasya panthām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tityāja: gives up
    • sacividaṁ: the counsel of friends
    • sakhāyaṁ: companions
    • na: not
    • tasya: his
    • vācy: spoken
    • api: even
    • bhāgo: part
    • asti: exists
    • yat: that
    • īṁ: indeed
    • śṛṇoty: hears
    • alakaṁ: the unadorned
    • śṛṇoti: listens
    • nahi: not
    • praveda: knows
    • sukṛtasya: of the righteous
    • panthām: path
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of companionship and the counsel of friends.
    • It suggests that one who gives up the advice of friends and companions does not possess any part of their wisdom.
    • Furthermore, it emphasizes that true understanding and knowledge of the righteous path cannot be attained without listening to the unembellished truths shared by others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, an employee who disregards the advice of experienced colleagues may miss out on valuable insights that could guide their career.
    • In personal relationships, ignoring the counsel of trusted friends can lead to poor decision-making, as friends often provide perspectives that one may overlook.
    • In education, students who engage with their peers and listen to their experiences often gain a deeper understanding of the subject matter.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the value of community and the wisdom that comes from shared experiences.
    • It teaches us that collaboration and open-mindedness are essential for personal growth and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this wisdom can lead to better decision-making, stronger relationships, and a more profound understanding of the world around us.
