स पवस्व मदाय कं नृचक्षां देववीतये |इन्दव इन्द्राय पीतये ||
sa pavasva madāya kaṁ nṛcakṣā devavītaye | indav indrāya pītaye ||
  • sa pavasva: This phrase can be translated as "may you blow" or "may you inspire." It signifies the invocation of a divine force or energy that brings vitality and inspiration into our lives.
  • madāya: This word means "for the purpose of intoxication" or "for the purpose of joy." It suggests a state of bliss or heightened awareness that can be achieved through divine inspiration.
  • kaṁ nṛcakṣā: This translates to "who has the eyes of a man" or "who sees like a human." It emphasizes the importance of human perception and understanding in the context of divine inspiration.
  • devavītaye: This means "for the sake of the divine" or "for the purpose of the gods." It indicates that the inspiration and joy sought are not just for personal gain but are aimed at a higher, divine purpose.
  • indav: This word refers to "the Indus" or "the divine." It symbolizes the source of inspiration and creativity that flows from the divine realm.
  • indrāya: This translates to "for Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. It signifies the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers for strength and guidance.
  • pītaye: This means "for the purpose of drinking" or "to imbibe." It suggests the act of taking in or absorbing the divine energy and inspiration that is being invoked.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa pavasva madāya kaṁ nṛcakṣā devavītaye | indav indrāya pītaye ||" serves as a powerful invocation for divine inspiration and joy. It emphasizes the importance of human perception in understanding and receiving divine blessings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as during creative endeavors, spiritual practices, or moments of seeking guidance. By invoking this mantra, individuals can open themselves to higher energies, fostering creativity, joy, and a deeper connection to the divine. It reminds us that our pursuits should not only be for personal gain but also for a greater purpose, aligning our actions with the divine will.
