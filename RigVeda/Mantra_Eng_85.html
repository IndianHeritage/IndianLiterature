सं चोदय चित्रम् अर्वाग् राध इन्द्र वरेण्यम् |असद इत ते विभु प्रभु ||
saṁ codaya citram arvāg rādha indra vareṇyam | asad it te vibhu prabhu ||
  • saṁ codaya: This phrase can be translated as "inspire" or "motivate." It signifies a call for divine inspiration or guidance.
  • citram: This word means "wonderful" or "extraordinary." It refers to something that is remarkable or worthy of attention.
  • arvāg: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is capable" or "the one who has the ability." It denotes strength and capability.
  • rādha: This word is often associated with "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies abundance and success.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often regarded as the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He symbolizes power and authority.
  • vareṇyam: This term means "worthy of worship" or "admirable." It indicates something or someone that deserves reverence and respect.
  • asad: This word translates to "non-existent" or "false." It represents the idea of illusion or that which is not real.
  • it: This is a pronoun meaning "that" or "this," referring to the subject being discussed.
  • te: This means "your" or "of you," indicating possession or relation.
  • vibhu: This word means "powerful" or "great." It signifies strength and vastness.
  • prabhu: This translates to "lord" or "master," indicating authority and control.

The mantra can be interpreted as a plea for divine inspiration and guidance from Indra, the powerful deity, to help manifest extraordinary capabilities and prosperity while dispelling illusions and falsehoods. It emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and wisdom from a higher power to achieve success in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking motivation to pursue their goals, whether in education, career, or personal growth.
  • Leadership: Leaders can invoke this mantra to inspire their teams, encouraging them to harness their capabilities and work towards a common vision.
  • Spiritual Practice: Practitioners can use this mantra in meditation to connect with divine energy, seeking clarity and strength in their spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the pursuit of extraordinary capabilities. It serves as a reminder that, in the face of challenges and illusions, one can seek strength and inspiration from a higher power to achieve prosperity and success in various aspects of life.
