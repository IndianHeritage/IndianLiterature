गावा उपावतावतं मही यज्ञस्य रप्सुदा |उभा कर्णा हिरण्यया ||
gāva upāvatāvatam mahī yajñasya rapsudā | ubhā karṇā hiraṇyayā ||
  • gāva: This word translates to "cows," which in the Vedic context symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and prosperity. Cows are often considered sacred in Hindu culture, representing abundance and the sustenance of life.
  • upāvatāvatam: This term can be broken down into "upāvatā," meaning "to be near" or "to approach," and "vatam," which implies "to be endowed with." Together, it suggests the idea of being close to or endowed with something valuable.
  • mahī: This word means "earth" or "the great one." It signifies the physical world and the nurturing aspect of nature that provides for all living beings.
  • yajñasya: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual." In Vedic tradition, yajna is a sacred act of offering to the divine, often involving fire and various offerings, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • rapsudā: This word can be interpreted as "richness" or "abundance." It emphasizes the idea of prosperity that comes from performing righteous actions and rituals.
  • ubhā: This means "both" or "together," indicating a duality or partnership in the context of the mantra.
  • karṇā: This translates to "ears," symbolizing the ability to listen and receive knowledge or wisdom.
  • hiraṇyayā: This term means "golden" or "wealthy," often associated with prosperity and the divine blessings that come from righteous living.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the blessings of wealth and prosperity, symbolized by cows, through the performance of sacred rituals on earth. It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to divine wisdom and the abundance that comes from living a life aligned with righteousness and sacrifice.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • In agricultural communities, the reverence for cows and the earth can lead to sustainable farming practices that honor nature and promote prosperity.
  • In personal life, the mantra encourages individuals to engage in selfless acts and rituals that foster community and connection, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • In business, the principles of ethical practices and giving back to the community can create a cycle of abundance and prosperity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony between humans, nature, and the divine. It teaches that true wealth comes from righteous actions and the nurturing of relationships, both with the earth and with each other. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can cultivate a life of abundance and fulfillment.
