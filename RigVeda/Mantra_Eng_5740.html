आ पश्चातान नासत्यā पुरस्ताद आश्विनā यातम अधरād उदक्तात् |आ विश्वतः पाञ्चजन्येन राया यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ā paścātān nāsatyā purastād āśvinā yātam adharād udaktāt | ā viśvataḥ pāñcajanyena rāyā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • paścātān: behind
    • nāsatyā: O Nāsatyas (the twin deities)
    • purastāt: in front
    • āśvinā: O Aśvins (the divine horsemen)
    • yātam: you go
    • adharād: from below
    • udaktāt: from above
    • ā: from
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
    • pāñcajanyena: with the conch (Pāñcajanya)
    • rāyā: with wealth
    • yūyam: you all
    • pāta: protect
    • svastibhiḥ: with auspiciousness
    • sadā: always
    • naḥ: us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine twin deities, Nāsatyas and Aśvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • The phrase "from behind and in front" suggests a comprehensive protection, indicating that the deities are being called upon to safeguard the devotee from all directions.
    • The mention of "wealth" and "auspiciousness" signifies a desire for prosperity and well-being in life.
    • In real life, this can be related to seeking guidance and protection in challenging situations, such as during a major life decision or when facing uncertainties.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and guidance in our lives.
    • It can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during rituals, prayers for safety, or when embarking on new ventures.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and confidence, knowing that they are supported by divine forces.
    • In a broader context, it encourages a mindset of seeking positivity and abundance, fostering a harmonious environment in personal and professional relationships.
