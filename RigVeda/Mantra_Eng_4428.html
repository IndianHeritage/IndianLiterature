वैश्वानरस्य विमितानि चक्षसा सानूनि दिवो अमृतस्य केतुनā |तस्येद उ विश्वा भुवनाधि मूर्धनि वयā इव रुरुहुः सप्त विस्रुहः ||
vaiśvānarasya vimitāni cakṣasā sānūni divo amṛtasya ketunā | tasyed u viśvā bhuvanādhi mūrdhani vayā iva ruruhuḥ sapta visruhaḥ ||
  • Vaiśvānarasya: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence that sustains life and connects all beings.
  • Vimitāni: This word means 'measured' or 'calculated,' indicating a sense of order and balance in the universe.
  • Cakṣasā: Translated as 'with the eye,' it suggests perception, awareness, and the ability to see beyond the physical realm.
  • Sānūni: This term can be understood as 'the many,' referring to the multitude of beings and elements in existence.
  • Divo: Meaning 'of the heavens,' it connects the mantra to the celestial realm and the divine.
  • Amṛtasya: This word means 'immortal' or 'nectar of immortality,' symbolizing the eternal essence of life.
  • Ketunā: Referring to a 'flag' or 'banner,' it signifies a guiding principle or a mark of identity.
  • Tasyed: This means 'of that,' pointing to the divine or cosmic fire mentioned earlier.
  • U: A conjunction meaning 'and,' linking ideas together.
  • Viśvā: Meaning 'all' or 'universal,' it emphasizes inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Bhuvanādhi: This term refers to 'the world' or 'the universe,' indicating the vastness of existence.
  • Mūrdhani: Meaning 'on the head' or 'top,' it suggests a position of prominence or importance.
  • Vayā: This word means 'we' or 'our,' indicating a collective identity.
  • Iv: A particle meaning 'like' or 'as,' used for comparison.
  • Ruruhuḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'to grow' or 'to flourish,' indicating vitality and growth.
  • Sapta: Meaning 'seven,' it often symbolizes completeness or a cycle.
  • Visruhaḥ: This word means 'to spread' or 'to expand,' indicating the growth and dissemination of life and knowledge.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings through the cosmic fire, emphasizing the importance of perception and awareness in understanding the universe. It highlights the idea that everything is measured and balanced, and that we are all part of a greater whole. The reference to immortality suggests that the essence of life transcends physical existence, and the imagery of a guiding flag indicates a path or purpose that unites us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Environmental Awareness: Understanding our connection to nature and the importance of preserving the environment for future generations.
  • Community Building: Recognizing the value of inclusivity and working together towards common goals, much like the interconnectedness described in the mantra.
  • Personal Growth: Emphasizing the importance of self-awareness and perception in our personal journeys, allowing us to flourish and grow.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of our place in the universe and the importance of recognizing our interconnectedness with all beings. It encourages us to seek balance, awareness, and purpose in our lives, guiding us towards a more harmonious existence.
