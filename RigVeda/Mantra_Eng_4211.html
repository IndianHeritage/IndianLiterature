धर्मणा मित्रावरुणा विपश्चिता व्रता रक्षेथे असुरस्य मायया |ऋतेन विश्वं भुवनं वि राजथः सूर्यं आ धत्तो दिवि चित्र्यं रथम ||
dharmaṇā mitrāvaruṇā vipaścitā vratā rakṣethe asurasya māyayā | ṛtena viśvam bhuvanaṁ vi rājathaḥ sūryam ā dhattho divi citryaṁ ratham ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the righteous (dharmaṇā) protect us from the illusions (māyayā) of the Asuras (demons) through the wisdom (vipaścitā) of Mitra and Varuna. With truth (ṛtena), may we rule the universe (bhuvanaṁ) and may the sun (sūryam) guide us in the celestial chariot (ratham)."
  • Key Terms Explained:
    • Dharmaṇā: Refers to righteousness or moral law. In real life, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who strive to do what is right, such as a judge who upholds justice.
    • Mitrāvaruṇā: Represents the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship and cosmic order. In everyday life, this can be likened to the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships and social order.
    • Vipaścitā: Means wisdom or insight. This can be related to individuals who make informed decisions based on knowledge, such as a leader who considers the welfare of their community.
    • Vratā: Refers to vows or commitments. This can be seen in people who dedicate themselves to a cause, like environmental activists who commit to protecting nature.
    • Asurasya māyayā: The illusions of the Asuras represent negative forces or distractions. In real life, this can be compared to the temptations and challenges that lead individuals away from their goals.
    • ṛtena: Signifies truth or cosmic order. This can be observed in the principles of honesty and integrity in personal and professional relationships.
    • Viśvam bhuvanaṁ: Refers to the universe or all realms of existence. This highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and can be seen in global movements for peace and unity.
    • Sūryam: The sun symbolizes light, knowledge, and guidance. In real life, this can be related to mentors or leaders who illuminate the path for others.
    • Citryaṁ ratham: The celestial chariot represents the journey of life. This can be likened to the various paths individuals take in pursuit of their dreams and aspirations.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness, wisdom, and truth in overcoming challenges and distractions in life. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher principles and to maintain harmony in our relationships. The invocation of celestial beings signifies the support we can receive from the universe when we align ourselves with these values.
  • Real-Life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their values and strive to live a life of integrity and purpose.
    • In community building, fostering relationships based on mutual respect and understanding can lead to a more harmonious society.
    • In leadership, making decisions that are informed by wisdom and truth can inspire others and create a positive impact.
