यः शग्मस तुविशग्म ते रायो दामा मतीनाम् |सोमः सुतः स इन्द्र तेऽस्ति स्वधापते मदः ||
yaḥ śagmas tuviśagma te rāyo dāmā matīnām | somaḥ sutaḥ sa indra te 'sti svadhāpate madaḥ ||
  • yaḥ - This word translates to "who" or "that one." It indicates a subject or a person of significance.
  • śagmas - Refers to "strength" or "power." It signifies the might or capability of an individual or entity.
  • tuviśagma - This can be interpreted as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is worthy of worship." It emphasizes the importance of recognition and reverence.
  • te - This means "your" or "of you," indicating possession or relation.
  • rāyo - Translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the blessings of material and spiritual wealth.
  • dāmā - This word means "bonds" or "ties." It can refer to relationships or connections that are significant in life.
  • matīnām - This translates to "of the wise" or "of those with understanding." It highlights the importance of wisdom and intellect.
  • somaḥ - Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing divine inspiration and ecstasy.
  • sutaḥ - This means "son" or "offspring," indicating lineage and the continuation of values and traditions.
  • sa - Translates to "he" or "that one," referring to a specific entity or deity.
  • indra - Refers to the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • te - Again means "your," reinforcing the personal connection.
  • ’sti - This means "exists" or "is," indicating presence or reality.
  • svadhāpate - This can be interpreted as "lord of one's own self," emphasizing self-mastery and control.
  • madaḥ - Refers to "intoxication" or "joy," symbolizing the bliss that comes from spiritual fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra speaks to the essence of strength, wisdom, and the divine connection that leads to prosperity and joy. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who embody these qualities, much like how we celebrate leaders and mentors in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in contexts such as leadership, where a wise leader inspires their followers, or in personal development, where one seeks to cultivate strength and wisdom within themselves. The significance lies in the pursuit of a balanced life, where material wealth and spiritual fulfillment coexist, leading to a harmonious existence.
