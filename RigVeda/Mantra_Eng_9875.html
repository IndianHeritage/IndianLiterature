किम एता वाचा कृतवां तवाहं प्राक्रमिषम् उषसां अग्रियेव |पुरूरवः पुनर अस्तं परेहि दुरापना वाता इवाहं अस्मि ||
kim etā vācā kṛṇavā tavāham prākramiṣam uṣasām agriyeva | purūravaḥ punar astam parehi durāpanā vāta ivāham asmi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim etā: What is this?
    • vācā: Speech or words
    • kṛṇavā: I create or I make
    • tavāham: I am yours
    • prākramiṣam: I will act or I will perform
    • uṣasām: Of the dawn
    • agriyeva: Like the foremost or the best
    • purūravaḥ: Pururavas (a mythological king)
    • punar: Again
    • astam: To the west or to the end
    • parehi: Go away or depart
    • durāpanā: Difficult to obtain
    • vāta: Wind
    • ivāham: I am like
    • asmi: I am
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep inquiry into the nature of speech and action.
    • The speaker is questioning the essence of their words and their purpose, indicating a desire to create meaningful communication.
    • By referencing the dawn, it symbolizes new beginnings and the potential for enlightenment.
    • The mention of Pururavas, a figure known for his romantic and adventurous exploits, suggests a connection to human desires and aspirations.
    • The phrase "go away" implies a release from burdens or obstacles, akin to letting go of negative influences in life.
    • The comparison to the wind signifies the transient nature of existence and the importance of adaptability.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to communicate effectively and purposefully, ensuring that their words lead to constructive actions.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages open dialogue, allowing individuals to express their feelings and intentions clearly, much like the dawn brings clarity after darkness.
    • In moments of challenge, the idea of letting go of burdens can be applied through mindfulness practices, helping individuals to release stress and embrace change.
    • The reference to the wind can remind us of the importance of being flexible and resilient in the face of life's uncertainties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of thoughtful communication and the power of intention behind our words and actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek clarity and purpose in our interactions, fostering deeper connections with others.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to embrace change and adaptability, much like the cycles of nature, leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
