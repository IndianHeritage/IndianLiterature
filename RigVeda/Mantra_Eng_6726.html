दानां मृगो न वारणः पुरुत्रा चरथं दधे |नकीष ट्वा न यमदाऽसुते गमो महांश्चरस्य ओजसा ||
dānā mṛgo na vāraṇaḥ purutrā carathaṁ dadhe | nakiṣ ṭvā ni yamad ā sute gamo mahām̐ś carasy ojasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dānā: gifts or offerings
    • mṛgo: deer or animals
    • na: not
    • vāraṇaḥ: elephant
    • purutrā: everywhere
    • carathaṁ: moving or wandering
    • dadhe: to place or establish
    • nakiṣ: not to be restrained
    • ṭvā: you
    • niyamad: control or limit
    • ā: towards
    • sute: offspring or progeny
    • gamo: to go or move
    • mahām̐ś: great or significant
    • carasy: of the moving or wandering
    • ojasā: with energy or strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of giving and the freedom of movement.
    • The mention of animals like deer and elephants symbolizes the diversity of life and the importance of coexistence.
    • The phrase "purutrā carathaṁ" suggests that one should move freely and explore the world without restrictions.
    • It emphasizes the idea that gifts and offerings should be made without expecting anything in return, promoting selflessness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when people come together to help those in need, such as during a natural disaster, they embody the spirit of "dānā."
    • Wildlife conservation efforts reflect the respect for all creatures, akin to the mention of deer and elephants, highlighting the need for harmony with nature.
    • Traveling and exploring different cultures can be seen as "purutrā carathaṁ," encouraging individuals to embrace diversity and learn from others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of generosity, freedom, and respect for all living beings.
    • It teaches us to give selflessly and to appreciate the interconnectedness of life.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more compassionate and harmonious society, where individuals support each other and coexist peacefully.
