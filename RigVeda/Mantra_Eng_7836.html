मन्त्र: दिवो न सानु पिप्युषी धाराऽसुतस्य वेधसः | वृथा पवित्रे अर्षति ||
divo na sānu pipyuṣī dhārā sutasya vedhasaḥ | vṛthā pavitre arṣati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or divine.
    • Na: Not.
    • Sānu: The essence or the core.
    • Pipyuṣī: The nectar or the essence.
    • Dhārā: Flow or stream.
    • Sutasya: Of the son or offspring.
    • Vedhasaḥ: Of the wise or learned.
    • Vṛthā: In vain or without purpose.
    • Pavitre: Pure or sacred.
    • Arṣati: To rise or to ascend.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence that flows from the heavens, emphasizing the importance of purity and wisdom.
    • The phrase "divo na sānu pipyuṣī dhārā" suggests that the divine nectar or essence is not just a physical substance but represents spiritual knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The mention of "sutasya vedhasaḥ" indicates that this essence is particularly significant for the wise or learned individuals, suggesting that knowledge and wisdom are essential for understanding the divine.
    • The concluding part, "vṛthā pavitre arṣati," warns against the futility of actions that are not pure or devoid of purpose, highlighting the importance of intention in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom often find that their efforts yield fruitful results, akin to the divine nectar flowing from the heavens.
    • In personal relationships, the essence of love and understanding can be seen as a divine nectar that nurtures connections, emphasizing the need for purity of intention.
    • In education, students who approach their studies with a pure heart and a quest for knowledge often excel, embodying the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity, wisdom, and intention in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding while ensuring that their actions are meaningful and pure.
    • In a world filled with distractions and superficial pursuits, this mantra calls for a return to the essence of what truly matters—wisdom, purity, and the divine flow of knowledge.
