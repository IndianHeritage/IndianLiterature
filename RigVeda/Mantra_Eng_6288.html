इयं त ऋत्वियावतī धीतिर एति नवीयसी |सपर्ण्यन्ती पुरुप्रियā मिमीत इति ||
iyaṁ ta ṛtviyāvatī dhītir eti navīyasī | saparyantī purupriyā mimīta it ||
  • iyaṁ ta ṛtviyāvatī dhītir eti navīyasī
    • iyaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
    • ta - Meaning "that," it connects the subject to a broader context or idea.
    • ṛtviyāvatī - Refers to "the one who is endowed with the qualities of ṛtvik," which means a priest or one who performs rituals. It signifies the importance of ritualistic practices in life.
    • dhītir - This means "intellect" or "understanding," emphasizing the role of wisdom and knowledge.
    • eti - Translates to "comes" or "arrives," indicating the action of bringing forth or manifesting something.
    • navīyasī - Meaning "new" or "fresh," it suggests innovation, renewal, and the emergence of new ideas or perspectives.
  • saparyantī purupriyā mimīta it
    • saparyantī - This word means "one who is worshipped" or "who is revered," indicating the importance of respect and devotion.
    • purupriyā - Translates to "beloved by many" or "dear to people," highlighting the value of community and relationships.
    • mimīta - This means "to be measured" or "to be evaluated," suggesting the importance of assessment and reflection in life.
    • it - A particle that can mean "thus" or "therefore," linking the previous ideas to a conclusion or outcome.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, ritual, and community in our lives. It suggests that through understanding and reverence, we can bring forth new ideas and innovations that are beloved by many. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In education, fostering a culture of respect and understanding can lead to innovative teaching methods that benefit students.
  • In community service, valuing relationships and collective efforts can result in impactful social initiatives.
  • In personal development, embracing new perspectives and continuous learning can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, respect, and community in creating a meaningful and progressive life.
