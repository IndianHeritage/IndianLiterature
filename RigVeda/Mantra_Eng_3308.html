विष्वा रोधांसी प्रवतश च पूर्वीर द्यौर ऋष्वाज जनिमान रेजत क्षाः |आ मातरां भरति शुष्म्य आ गोर नृवत परिज्मन नोनुवन्त वाताः ||
viśvā rodhāṁsi pravataś ca pūrvīr dyaur ṛṣvāj janiman rejata kṣāḥ | ā mātarā bharati śuṣmy ā gor nṛvat parijman nonuvanta vātāḥ ||
  • viśvā rodhāṁsi: This phrase can be interpreted as "all the obstacles" or "all the barriers." In real life, this can refer to the challenges we face in our personal and professional lives, such as financial difficulties, health issues, or emotional struggles.
  • pravataś ca pūrvīr: This translates to "and the winds that blow from the east." The east is often associated with new beginnings and opportunities. In a practical sense, this can symbolize the fresh starts we encounter, such as a new job, a new relationship, or a new phase in life.
  • dyaur ṛṣvāj: This means "the sky is vast." The vastness of the sky can represent limitless possibilities and aspirations. In our lives, this can remind us to dream big and aim high, whether in our careers, education, or personal goals.
  • janiman rejata kṣāḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the earth is filled with life." It signifies the abundance of life and resources available to us. In real life, this can be seen in the richness of nature, the diversity of cultures, and the opportunities for growth and learning that surround us.
  • ā mātarā bharati: This translates to "the mothers nourish." This can refer to the nurturing aspect of life, whether it be through family, community, or nature. In practical terms, it highlights the importance of support systems in our lives, such as friends, family, and mentors who help us grow and thrive.
  • śuṣmy ā gor: This can be interpreted as "the gentle and strong." It reflects the balance between softness and strength in our character. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who are both compassionate and assertive, or in individuals who show resilience in the face of adversity.
  • nṛvat parijman nonuvanta vātāḥ: This phrase means "the winds that do not cease to blow." It symbolizes the constant change and movement in life. In practical terms, it reminds us that change is inevitable, and we must adapt to it, whether it be in our careers, relationships, or personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of overcoming obstacles, embracing new beginnings, recognizing the vast possibilities around us, and valuing the nurturing aspects of our existence. It serves as a reminder that while challenges are a part of life, there is also an abundance of support and opportunities available. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to face their challenges with resilience, seek out new opportunities, and appreciate the nurturing relationships that help them grow. It encourages a balanced approach to life, where one can be both gentle and strong, adapting to the ever-changing winds of existence.
