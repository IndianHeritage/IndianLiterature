प्रवत् ते अग्नि जनिमा पितुयतः साचिव विश्वा भुवना न्य ऋञ्जसे |प्र सप्तयः प्र सनिṣन्त नो धियः पुरश्चरन्ति पशुपा इव त्मना ||
pravat te agne janimā pitūyataḥ sācīva viśvā bhuvanā ny ṛñjase | pra saptayaḥ pra saniṣanta no dhiyaḥ puraś caranti paśupā iva tmanā ||
  • Mantra: "pravat te agne janimā pitūyataḥ sācīva viśvā bhuvanā ny ṛñjase | pra saptayaḥ pra saniṣanta no dhiyaḥ puraś caranti paśupā iva tmanā ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, you who are born from the father, may you illuminate the world with your brilliance. The seven sages, who are wise, guide our thoughts as the cattle move towards their pasture."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Agni: The fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation.
    • Janimā: Born or generated, indicating the origin of Agni.
    • Pitūyataḥ: From the father, suggesting a divine lineage or source.
    • Sācīva: Illuminating or enlightening, referring to the role of Agni in providing knowledge and clarity.
    • Viśvā Bhuvanā: The entire universe or all worlds, emphasizing the vastness of Agni's influence.
    • Ṛñjase: To shine or to illuminate, highlighting the transformative power of Agni.
    • Pra Saptayaḥ: The seven sages, representing wisdom and guidance.
    • Pra Saniṣanta: Those who guide or direct, indicating the role of sages in leading thoughts.
    • No Dhiyaḥ: Our thoughts, emphasizing the importance of mental clarity.
    • Puraś: Forward or ahead, suggesting progress and direction.
    • Caranti: Move or act, indicating the dynamic nature of life.
    • Paśupā: Cattle, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • Tmanā: With the mind, indicating the connection between thought and action.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance from wise individuals, akin to the seven sages, when making important decisions.
    • The concept of Agni can be related to the fire within us that drives our passions and ambitions, illuminating our path in life.
    • Just as cattle move towards their pasture, we are drawn towards our goals and aspirations, guided by our thoughts and the wisdom we acquire.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance and the illumination of knowledge in our lives.
    • It teaches us to seek wisdom and clarity in our thoughts, enabling us to navigate the complexities of life effectively.
    • In a broader sense, it reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our inner fire to achieve our goals.
