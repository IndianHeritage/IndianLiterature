स संस्तिरो विष्टिरः सं गृभायति जानन्न एव जानतीर नित्य आ शये |पुनर वर्धन्ते अपि यन्ति देव्यम अन्यद वर्पः पितरोः कृत्वते सचा ||
sa saṁstiro viṣṭiraḥ saṁ gṛbhāyati jānann eva jānatīr nitya ā śaye | punar vardhante api yanti devyam anyad varpaḥ pitroḥ kṛṇvate sacā ||
  • sa saṁstiro viṣṭiraḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is established and expansive." It signifies stability and growth, suggesting that a person or entity that is grounded can also expand their influence and reach.
  • saṁ gṛbhāyati: This means "he/she/it gathers." It implies the act of collecting or bringing together, which can be related to the idea of community or collaboration in real life.
  • jānann eva jānatīr: Translated, this means "knowing, indeed, they know." This emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in achieving one's goals.
  • nitya ā śaye: This phrase translates to "eternally in the abode." It suggests a sense of permanence and stability in one's existence or state of being.
  • punar vardhante: This means "they grow again." It signifies renewal and the cyclical nature of life, where growth can occur repeatedly.
  • api yanti devyam: This translates to "they also go to the divine." It indicates a connection to the spiritual or higher realms, suggesting that one's actions can lead to divine favor or enlightenment.
  • anyad varpaḥ: This means "another offering." It implies the idea of giving or contributing, which is essential in various aspects of life, including relationships and community service.
  • pitroḥ kṛṇvate: This translates to "they make for the ancestors." It highlights the importance of honoring one's lineage and the contributions of previous generations.
  • sacā: This means "and indeed." It serves as a conjunction that emphasizes the preceding statements.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of stability, growth, knowledge, and the importance of community and ancestry. It reflects the interconnectedness of life, where individual actions contribute to collective well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and honoring family traditions. For instance, in a workplace, fostering a collaborative environment where knowledge is shared can lead to collective growth and success. Similarly, in family gatherings, honoring ancestors and sharing stories can strengthen bonds and instill values in younger generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of being grounded while also striving for growth. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge, contribute to their communities, and honor their roots. By embodying these principles, one can lead a fulfilling life that resonates with both personal and collective significance.
