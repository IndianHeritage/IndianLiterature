प्रातर्यजध्वम् अश्विनाः हिनोत न सायं अस्ति देवया अजुष्टम् |उतान्यो अस्मद यजते वि चावः पूर्वः-पूर्वो यजमानो वनीयान् ||
prātar yajadhvam aśvinā hinota na sāyam asti devayā ajuṣṭam | utānyo asmad yajate vi cāvaḥ pūrvaḥ-pūrvo yajamāno vanīyān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prātar: In the morning
    • yajadhvam: You should perform the sacrifice
    • aśvinā: With the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • hinota: May they bestow
    • na: Not
    • sāyam: In the evening
    • asti: There is
    • devayā: By the divine
    • ajuṣṭam: Not to be neglected
    • utānyo: And others
    • asmad: Our
    • yajate: Sacrifice
    • vi: Indeed
    • cāvaḥ: And you
    • pūrvaḥ-pūrvo: The earlier and the earlier
    • yajamāno: The one who is performing the sacrifice
    • vanīyān: May he be prosperous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of performing rituals and sacrifices in the morning, particularly invoking the blessings of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • The phrase "may they bestow" indicates a request for divine assistance and prosperity, highlighting the belief that the divine presence is essential for success in any endeavor.
    • The mention of "not in the evening" suggests that the morning is a more auspicious time for spiritual activities, aligning with the idea that starting the day with positive actions can lead to a fruitful outcome.
    • The reference to "others" implies that the collective effort of the community in performing sacrifices is vital, reinforcing the idea of unity and shared purpose.
    • The repetition of "pūrvaḥ-pūrvo" signifies the importance of tradition and continuity in rituals, suggesting that the practices of the past should be honored and carried forward.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, starting the day with a morning ritual, such as meditation or prayer, is believed to set a positive tone for the rest of the day. This aligns with the mantra's emphasis on morning sacrifices.
    • Community events, such as festivals or collective prayers, reflect the idea of "others" participating in rituals, fostering a sense of belonging and shared spirituality.
    • In modern workplaces, team-building activities in the morning can enhance collaboration and productivity, mirroring the mantra's call for collective effort and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a reminder of the significance of morning rituals and the invocation of divine blessings for success and prosperity.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal spiritual practices to community engagements, emphasizing the importance of starting the day with intention and collective effort.
    • By integrating these principles into daily life, individuals and communities can cultivate a sense of purpose, unity, and connection to the divine, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
