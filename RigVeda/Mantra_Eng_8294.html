त्रिभिष् त्वं देव सवितर वरिषिष्ठैः सोम धामभिः |अग्नि दाक्षैः पुनिहि नः ||
tribhiṣ ṭvaṁ deva savitar varṣiṣṭhaiḥ soma dhāmabhiḥ | agne dakṣaiḥ punīhi naḥ ||
  • tribhiṣ: This word can be broken down into "tri" meaning three and "bhiṣ" which relates to the concept of healing or protection. In a broader sense, it signifies the three realms of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual.
  • ṭvaṁ: This translates to "you," referring to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • deva: This means "god" or "deity," indicating the divine nature of the entity being invoked.
  • savitar: This term refers to the Sun God, Savitar, who is associated with light, energy, and life-giving properties.
  • varṣiṣṭhaiḥ: This word means "with the best" or "most excellent," suggesting a plea for the highest blessings or qualities.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine nourishment.
  • dhāmabhiḥ: This translates to "with the abodes" or "with the realms," indicating a connection to higher states of existence or consciousness.
  • agne: This means "O Agni," referring to the fire god, who is a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • dakṣaiḥ: This word means "with skill" or "with competence," emphasizing the importance of capability and wisdom.
  • punīhi: This translates to "purify" or "cleanse," indicating a desire for spiritual and physical purification.
  • naḥ: This means "us," referring to the collective group of individuals seeking blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra "tribhiṣ ṭvaṁ deva savitar varṣiṣṭhaiḥ soma dhāmabhiḥ | agne dakṣaiḥ punīhi naḥ ||" is a profound invocation for divine blessings and purification. It emphasizes the need for protection and nourishment from the divine, particularly from the Sun God and Agni, the fire deity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as during rituals, meditation, or personal reflection, where individuals seek to align themselves with higher energies and attain clarity, strength, and purity in their lives. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual realms, and the importance of divine guidance in achieving balance and harmony.
