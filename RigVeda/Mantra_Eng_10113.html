भूम्यां अन्तं पर्य एके चरन्ति रथस्य धूर्षु युक्तासो अस्तुः | श्रमस्य दायं वि भजन्त्य एभ्यो यदा यमो भवति हर्म्ये हितः ||
bhūmyā antam pary eke caranti rathasya dhūrṣu yuktāso asthuḥ | śramasya dāyaṁ vi bhajanty ebhyo yadā yamo bhavati harmye hitaḥ ||
  • Translation: "The ones who move on the earth, who are united with the chariot, are indeed the ones who share the fruits of labor. When Yama (the god of death) arrives, they are the ones who are in the abode of well-being."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhūmyā: of the earth
    • antam: end or limit
    • pary: around or about
    • eke: those who
    • caranti: move or travel
    • rathasya: of the chariot
    • dhūrṣu: in the midst or in the company
    • yuktāso: united or connected
    • asthuḥ: indeed or truly
    • śramasya: of labor or effort
    • dāyaṁ: the fruits or rewards
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • bhajanty: share or partake
    • ebhyo: from these
    • yadā: when
    • yamo: Yama, the god of death
    • bhavati: becomes or arrives
    • harmye: in the abode or home
    • hitaḥ: well-being or good
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration. Just as a chariot requires the coordinated effort of its wheels and horses to move forward, individuals must work together to achieve common goals.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, where each member contributes their skills to complete a project successfully.
    • The phrase "fruits of labor" signifies that hard work and effort lead to rewards. For example, students who study diligently are more likely to achieve good grades.
    • The mention of Yama, the god of death, serves as a reminder of mortality and the importance of living a life of purpose and integrity. It suggests that those who contribute positively to society will find peace and well-being in the afterlife.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us the value of cooperation, hard work, and the pursuit of a meaningful life. It encourages individuals to contribute to their communities and to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in family dynamics, workplace environments, and social responsibilities. By working together and sharing the fruits of our labor, we create a harmonious society.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions have consequences, and living a life of service and collaboration leads to a fulfilling existence both in this life and beyond.
