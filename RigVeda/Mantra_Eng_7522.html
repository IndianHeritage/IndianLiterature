तं इन्द्रं वाजयामसि महे वृत्राय हन्तवे |स वृषा वृषभो भवत ||
tam indraṁ vājayāmasi mahe vṛtrāya hantave | sa vṛṣā vṛṣabho bhuvat ||
  • tam indraṁ: This phrase refers to "that Indra," where Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • vājayāmasi: This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for assistance or blessings from Indra.
  • mahe: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this word emphasizes the immense power and significance of Indra.
  • vṛtrāya: This term refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon-like demon that represents obstacles, chaos, or ignorance that needs to be overcome.
  • hantave: This means "to be defeated" or "to be killed," indicating the intention to overcome the challenges posed by Vritra.
  • sa vṛṣā: This translates to "he who is strong" or "the powerful one," reinforcing the idea of Indra's strength.
  • vṛṣabho: Meaning "the bull," this symbolizes vigor, fertility, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • bhuvat: This means "exists" or "is present," indicating the manifestation of strength and power in the world.

The mantra can be understood as a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his strength and assistance in overcoming obstacles represented by Vritra. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in facing challenges and achieving success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios where individuals or communities face significant challenges, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as health issues or personal crises, individuals can invoke the strength of Indra to find resilience and overcome their struggles.
  • Community Struggles: In times of natural disasters or societal issues, communities can come together, invoking the spirit of Indra to unite and find strength in adversity.
  • Leadership and Decision Making: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to embody the qualities of strength and decisiveness, guiding their teams through challenges with confidence.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine support and the strength within oneself to overcome obstacles. It encourages individuals and communities to seek help from higher powers while also recognizing their own capabilities in facing life's challenges.
