उप त्वा जुह्वोऽम म घृताचीर यन्तु हर्यत |अग्नि हव्या जुषस्व नः ||
upa tvā juhvo3 mama ghṛtācīr yantu haryata | agne havyā juṣasva naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "upa tvā juhvo mama ghṛtācīr yantu haryata | agne havyā juṣasva naḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • tvā: you (Agni, the fire god)
    • juhvo: I offer, I invoke
    • mama: my
    • ghṛtācīr: with clarified butter (ghee)
    • yantu: may they go, may they arrive
    • haryata: with joy, with happiness
    • agne: O Agni
    • havyā: offerings, oblations
    • juṣasva: enjoy, accept
    • naḥ: our
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to offer clarified butter (ghee) to Agni, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
    • The invocation "upa tvā juhvo" indicates a personal connection, as the speaker is calling upon Agni to come closer and accept the offerings.
    • The phrase "yantu haryata" conveys a wish for joy and happiness to accompany the offerings, emphasizing the importance of positive energy in rituals.
    • Finally, "agne havyā juṣasva naḥ" is a request for Agni to accept the offerings and bless the devotees, highlighting the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, ghee is often used in fire ceremonies (yajnas) to invoke blessings from deities, similar to the offerings made in this mantra.
    • The act of offering food or gifts to others can be seen as a modern-day reflection of this mantra, where the intention is to bring joy and happiness to both the giver and the receiver.
    • In personal development, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek positivity and joy in their actions, fostering a sense of community and connection with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of offerings and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us to approach our actions with intention, seeking to bring joy and positivity into our lives and the lives of others.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of gratitude, connection, and the act of giving can enhance our relationships and foster a sense of community.
