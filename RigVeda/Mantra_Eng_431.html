यं त्वा देवासो मनवे दधुर इह यजिष्ठं हव्यवाहन |यं कण्वो मेध्यातिथिर धनस्पृताṁ यं वृषा यम् उपस्तुतः ||
yaṁ tvā devāso manave dadhur iha yajiṣṭhaṁ havyavāhana | yaṁ kaṇvo medhyātithir dhanaspṛtaṁ yaṁ vṛṣā yam upastutaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • tvā: You
    • devāso: The gods
    • manave: To Manu (the progenitor of mankind)
    • dadhur: Have placed
    • iha: Here
    • yajiṣṭham: The most worshipful
    • havyavāhana: The one who carries offerings
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • kaṇvo: The sage Kanva
    • medhyātithir: The pure guest
    • dhanaspṛtam: The one who is rich in wealth
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • vṛṣā: The bull (symbolizing strength)
    • yam: Whom
    • upastutaḥ: Is praised
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the gods and humanity, particularly through the figure of Manu, who represents the ideal human.
    • The phrase "yajiṣṭhaṁ havyavāhana" refers to the one who is most worthy of worship and who carries offerings, indicating the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "medhyātithir" highlights the significance of purity and hospitality, suggesting that welcoming guests with respect is a sacred duty.
    • The reference to "dhanaspṛtam" emphasizes the value of wealth, not just in material terms but also in spiritual richness and abundance.
    • The "vṛṣā" symbolizes strength and power, indicating that those who are praised and worshipped possess these qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the act of giving and sharing resources can be seen in charitable organizations that provide for the less fortunate, embodying the spirit of "havyavāhana."
    • Hospitality is a key aspect of many cultures, where welcoming guests with open arms reflects the principle of "medhyātithir." For instance, in Indian culture, offering food and comfort to guests is considered a sacred duty.
    • Wealth can be interpreted in various ways, such as investing in education or community development, which enriches society as a whole, aligning with the idea of "dhanaspṛtam."
    • Strength and resilience are often celebrated in leaders and role models who inspire others, akin to the "vṛṣā" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, hospitality, and the pursuit of wealth in a holistic sense, urging individuals to recognize their responsibilities towards others and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in promoting values that foster community, respect, and spiritual growth, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to societal interactions.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, reflecting the ideals of the RigVeda in contemporary life.
