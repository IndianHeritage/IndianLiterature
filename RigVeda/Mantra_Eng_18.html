कवी नो मित्रावरुणा तुविजाताऽ उरुक्षया |दक्षं दधाते अपसम् ||
kavī no mitrāvaruṇā tuvijātā urukṣayā | dakṣaṁ dadhāte apasam ||
  • kavī: This word translates to "the wise" or "the poet." It signifies those who possess knowledge and insight, often seen as seers or sages in Vedic literature.
  • no: This means "our," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This compound word refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna. Mitra represents friendship, harmony, and the social order, while Varuna embodies the cosmic order, truth, and moral authority.
  • tuvijātā: This means "you who are born of," referring to the divine qualities or attributes of the deities mentioned.
  • urukṣayā: This term can be interpreted as "with great strength" or "powerful," indicating the might and capability of the deities.
  • dakṣaṁ: This word means "skilled" or "capable," referring to the ability to perform tasks effectively and efficiently.
  • dadhāte: This translates to "they bestow" or "they grant," indicating the action of giving or providing something valuable.
  • apasam: This means "without hindrance" or "freely," suggesting that the blessings or skills provided are given without obstacles.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the wise deities Mitra and Varuna, asking them to bestow their skills and strengths upon us without any hindrance. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, friendship, and moral order in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on the qualities of wisdom and skill in their personal and professional lives, striving to embody these attributes in their actions.
  • Community Building: The invocation of Mitra and Varuna highlights the importance of harmony and moral order in society. This can inspire community leaders to foster relationships based on trust and cooperation.
  • Conflict Resolution: The qualities of friendship and moral authority can be applied in resolving disputes, encouraging individuals to seek understanding and common ground.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in cultivating wisdom, strength, and moral integrity. By invoking the qualities of Mitra and Varuna, individuals and communities can strive for a harmonious existence, fostering relationships built on trust and cooperation. This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that underpin a balanced and fulfilling life.
