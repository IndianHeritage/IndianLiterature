विन्द्र यासि दिव्यानि रोचनā वि पार्थिवानि रजसा पुरुष्टुत |ये त्वा वहन्ति मुहुर अध्वराम् उप ते सु वन्बन्तु वग्वनाम् आराधसः ||
vīndra yāsi divyāni rocanā vi pārthivāni rajasā puruṣṭuta | ye tvā vahanti muhur adhvarām̐ upa te su vanvantu vagvanām̐ arādhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vīndra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • yāsi: you go or you arrive
    • divyāni: divine or celestial
    • rocanā: brilliance or radiance
    • vi: indeed
    • pārthivāni: earthly or worldly
    • rajasā: with the dust or through the medium of
    • puruṣṭuta: praised or honored
    • ye: those who
    • tvā: you
    • vahanti: carry or bring
    • muhur: repeatedly or again
    • adhvarām̐: the sacrificial rites or offerings
    • upa: near or towards
    • te: your
    • su: good or auspicious
    • vanvantu: may they flourish or prosper
    • vagvanām̐: of those who speak or utter
    • arādhasaḥ: from the lower or inferior
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, invoking his divine presence and brilliance.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the celestial and earthly realms, suggesting that divine qualities can manifest in the material world.
    • The phrase "those who carry you" indicates the importance of community and collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of rituals and offerings, suggesting that they are a means to connect with the divine.
    • Finally, it expresses a desire for prosperity and flourishing, both in spiritual and material aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering or festival, people come together to celebrate and honor their traditions, much like the collective effort mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may engage in rituals or practices that help them connect with their spiritual beliefs, such as meditation or prayer, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In a workplace, teamwork and collaboration can be seen as a modern-day equivalent of "those who carry you," where collective efforts lead to success and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine connection and the interplay between the celestial and earthly realms.
    • It teaches us that through collective efforts and rituals, we can invite prosperity and divine blessings into our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in community, personal practices, or teamwork, the principles of honoring the divine and working together can lead to flourishing outcomes.
