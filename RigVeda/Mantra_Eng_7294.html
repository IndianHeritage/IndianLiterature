आ सुते सिञ्चत श्रीयं रोदस्योर अभिश्रियम् |रसाऽ दधीत वृषभम् ||
ā sute siñcata śriyaṁ rodasyor abhiśriyam | rasā dadhīta vṛṣabham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā sute: "from the source" or "from the essence"
    • siñcata: "pour" or "bestow"
    • śriyaṁ: "prosperity" or "wealth"
    • rodasyor: "of the two worlds" or "of the heavens and the earth"
    • abhiśriyam: "the auspiciousness" or "the divine wealth"
    • rasā: "essence" or "flavor"
    • dadhīta: "may you create" or "may you establish"
    • vṛṣabham: "the bull" or "the strong one"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces to bestow prosperity and auspiciousness from the essence of the universe.
    • The reference to "the two worlds" signifies the connection between the material and spiritual realms, emphasizing the importance of balance in life.
    • The term "bull" symbolizes strength, vitality, and fertility, representing the power needed to achieve success and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for divine blessings to ensure a bountiful harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra in seeking prosperity from the earth.
    • In business, entrepreneurs may invoke this mantra to attract success and wealth, recognizing the need for both spiritual and material support in their endeavors.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may recite this mantra to invite auspiciousness and strength into their new beginnings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings for prosperity and strength in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual worlds, encouraging individuals to cultivate both for a fulfilling life.
    • By invoking this mantra, one can aspire to achieve balance, strength, and abundance, making it applicable in personal, professional, and communal contexts.
