यो रेवान् यो अमीवहाः वसुवित् पुष्टिवर्धनः |स नः सिषक्तु यस् तुरः ||
yo revān yo amīvahā vasuvit puṣṭivardhanaḥ | sa naḥ siṣaktu yas turaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yo revān yo amīvahā vasuvit puṣṭivardhanaḥ | sa naḥ siṣaktu yas turaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • revān: is the one who brings prosperity
    • yo: who
    • amīvahā: is the one who carries the essence of life
    • vasuvit: the one who is the source of wealth
    • puṣṭivardhanaḥ: the one who enhances nourishment
    • sa: may he
    • naḥ: our
    • siṣaktu: bless
    • yas: who
    • turaḥ: is swift or quick
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine force that brings prosperity and nourishment into our lives.
    • The repetition of "yo" emphasizes the qualities of the divine being addressed, highlighting the importance of these attributes in our lives.
    • The terms "vasuvit" and "puṣṭivardhanaḥ" suggest a connection to material wealth and spiritual nourishment, indicating a holistic approach to prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited by entrepreneurs seeking success and growth in their ventures. For instance, a startup founder may chant this mantra before a pitch to attract investors and ensure the prosperity of their business.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of financial difficulty, seeking blessings for abundance and nourishment in their lives.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be used during festivals or gatherings to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of material and spiritual wealth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings not just for oneself but for the community as a whole.
    • In today's fast-paced world, where the pursuit of wealth can often overshadow spiritual growth, this mantra encourages a balanced approach to life, fostering both prosperity and nourishment.
    • By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and gratitude, leading to a more fulfilling life.
