मन्त्र: निर आविध्यद गिरिभ्य आ धारणयत पक्वम् ओदनम् | इन्द्रो बुण्डं स्वाततम् ||
nir āvidhyad giribhya ā dhārayat pakvam odanam | indro bundaṁ svātatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir: without, free from
    • āvidhyad: to be covered or hidden
    • giribhya: from the mountains or hills
    • ā: towards
    • dhārayat: to hold or support
    • pakvam: ripe or matured
    • odanam: rice or food
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • bundaṁ: a form of sustenance or nourishment
    • svātatam: the best or most excellent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine provision of nourishment and sustenance.
    • It suggests that true nourishment comes without any hindrance or obstruction, symbolized by the phrase "without being covered."
    • The reference to "mountains" can be interpreted as the natural world providing for us, as mountains are often seen as sources of water and fertile land.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate source of strength and abundance, ensuring that we receive the best of what is available.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be related to the importance of cultivating crops in a way that allows them to grow freely and without obstruction, leading to a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal life, it can symbolize the need for individuals to seek nourishment—both physical and emotional—without barriers, such as stress or negativity.
    • In community settings, it can reflect the idea of sharing resources and support, ensuring that everyone has access to what they need to thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine support in our quest for sustenance and well-being.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the natural resources around us and to seek nourishment that is pure and unobstructed.
    • In real life, it encourages us to foster environments—whether in our homes, communities, or workplaces—where everyone can access the resources they need to flourish.
