यम अग्ने पृत्सु मार्त्यम अवा वाजेषु यं जुनाः |स यन्ता शाश्वतीर इषः ||
yam agne pṛtsu martyam avā vājeṣu yaṁ junāḥ | sa yantā śaśvatīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: whom
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • pṛtsu: in the mortal realm
    • martyam: mortal
    • avā: protect
    • vājeṣu: in the offerings
    • yaṁ: whom
    • junāḥ: the wise or learned
    • sa: he
    • yantā: who leads
    • śaśvatīr: eternal
    • iṣaḥ: desires or wishes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking for his protection in the mortal world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and guidance from learned individuals.
    • The phrase "who leads" suggests a call for direction in life, aligning with eternal desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, one might seek guidance from a mentor or teacher, akin to invoking Agni for protection and wisdom.
    • During rituals or significant life events, individuals often seek blessings for clarity and direction, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern contexts, this can relate to seeking inspiration from leaders or role models who embody wisdom and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our mortal lives.
    • It highlights the value of wisdom and the need for direction in achieving our eternal desires.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to look for mentors and wise counsel, especially during times of uncertainty or decision-making.
