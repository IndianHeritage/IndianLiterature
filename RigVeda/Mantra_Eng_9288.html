वनीवाणो मम दूतासा इन्द्रं स्तोमाश्चरन्ति सुमतीर इयानाः |हृद्स्पृशो मनसा वच्यमानाः अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयं दाह ||
vanīvāno mama dūtāsa indraṁ stomāś caranti sumatīr iyānāḥ | hṛdispṛśo manasā vacyamānā asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • vanīvāno mama dūtāsa: This phrase can be translated as "my messengers, who are like the forest." Here, "vanīvāno" refers to the forest, symbolizing abundance and nature's bounty. In real life, forests are often seen as sources of life and resources, providing food, shelter, and medicine.
  • indraṁ stomāś caranti: This means "the hymns (stomas) move towards Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. The act of hymns moving towards Indra signifies the importance of devotion and the acknowledgment of divine forces in our lives. For example, in modern life, people often turn to prayer or meditation to seek guidance and strength during challenging times.
  • sumatīr iyānāḥ: This translates to "those who have good thoughts are coming." It emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and intentions. In real life, maintaining a positive mindset can lead to better decision-making and improved relationships, as seen in practices like mindfulness and cognitive behavioral therapy.
  • hṛdispṛśo manasā vacyamānā: This phrase means "those who touch the heart and are spoken of by the mind." It highlights the connection between emotions and thoughts. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we are influenced by our feelings and how they shape our thoughts and actions, such as in the context of empathy and compassion towards others.
  • asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ: This translates to "grant us the colorful wealth (rayi) that is powerful." Here, "citraṁ" refers to diversity and richness, while "vṛṣaṇaṁ" signifies strength. This can be related to the pursuit of a fulfilling life, where one seeks not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual richness. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of community, relationships, and personal growth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, fostering positive thoughts, and recognizing the interplay between emotions and intellect. Its significance lies in the reminder to connect with nature, acknowledge the divine, and cultivate a mindset that attracts abundance in various forms. In real life, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, community building, and personal development, encouraging individuals to seek a balanced and enriched life.
