परिमे गाम अनेषत पर्य अग्निम अहृषत |देवेष्व अकृत श्रवः का इमां आ दधर्षति ||
parīme gām aneṣata pary agnim ahṛṣata | deveṣv akrata śravaḥ ka imām̐ ā dadharṣati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parīme: to measure or to encompass
    • gām: the world or the earth
    • aneṣata: without any hindrance or obstruction
    • pary: around or in all directions
    • agnim: the fire or the divine energy
    • ahṛṣata: has been invoked or called upon
    • deveṣv: among the gods or divine beings
    • akrata: has not been created or established
    • śravaḥ: fame or glory
    • ka: who
    • imām̐: this
    • ā: to
    • dadharṣati: can bear or support
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the vastness of the universe and the divine energy that permeates it.
    • It emphasizes the idea that the world can be measured and understood, yet it remains boundless and free from obstruction.
    • The invocation of fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine presence that fuels life.
    • The mention of gods suggests a connection to the divine and the importance of seeking their blessings for success and glory.
    • The question posed at the end, "who can bear this?" reflects the humility required to understand and engage with such vast concepts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In scientific exploration, the quest to understand the universe mirrors the idea of measuring the world. For instance, astronomers use telescopes to explore the cosmos, yet they acknowledge the limits of human understanding.
    • In personal growth, individuals often seek transformation through education and self-improvement, akin to invoking the divine energy of fire to fuel their journey.
    • In community building, the collective effort to create a supportive environment reflects the idea of divine beings working together for a common goal, fostering fame and glory through collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the universe and the divine energy that sustains it.
    • It teaches us the importance of humility in the face of vast knowledge and the need for divine guidance in our endeavors.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek transformation, embrace collaboration, and recognize the divine presence in our journeys.
