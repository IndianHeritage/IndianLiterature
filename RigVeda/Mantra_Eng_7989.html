स नः पुनान आ भर रयिं स्तोत्रे सुवीर्यम् |जरितुर वर्धयाऽ गिरः ||
sa naḥ punāna ā bhara rayiṁ stotre suvīryam | jaritur vardhayā giraḥ ||
  • sa naḥ: This phrase translates to "may He" or "may that (divine force)". It signifies a call to a higher power or divine entity for assistance and blessings.
  • punāna: This word means "purify" or "cleanse". It indicates a desire for spiritual and physical purification, which is essential for personal growth and well-being.
  • ā bhara: This translates to "bring forth" or "bestow". It expresses a request for the divine to provide or grant something valuable.
  • rayiṁ: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity". It encompasses not just material wealth but also spiritual richness and abundance in life.
  • stotre: This means "in praise" or "in worship". It highlights the importance of devotion and reverence in seeking blessings.
  • suvīryam: This translates to "great strength" or "heroic power". It signifies the strength of character, courage, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • jaritur: This word means "aging" or "decay". It reflects the natural process of life and the inevitability of change.
  • vardhayā: This means "increase" or "enhance". It indicates a desire for growth, whether in knowledge, strength, or resources.
  • giraḥ: This translates to "words" or "speech". It emphasizes the power of communication and expression in shaping our reality.

In summary, this mantra is a heartfelt invocation for divine assistance in achieving purification, prosperity, and strength. It acknowledges the natural cycle of life while seeking growth and enhancement in various aspects of existence.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life as a reminder to seek spiritual and personal purification, encouraging individuals to engage in practices such as meditation, yoga, or self-reflection.
  • It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and praise, suggesting that expressing appreciation for what one has can attract more abundance and positivity.
  • The call for strength and courage can inspire individuals facing challenges, reminding them to harness their inner power and resilience.
  • In a broader context, this mantra can be used in community settings, such as group prayers or gatherings, to foster a sense of unity and collective growth.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual, material, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and fulfilling life.
