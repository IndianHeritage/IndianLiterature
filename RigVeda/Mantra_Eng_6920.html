इमा उ त्वा वि चक्षते सखाय इन्द्र सोमिनः |पुष्टावन्तो यथा पशुम् ||
ima u tvā vi cakṣate sakhāya indra sominaḥ | puṣṭāvanto yathā paśum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ima: these
    • u: and
    • tvā: you
    • vi: indeed
    • cakṣate: they see
    • sakhāya: friends
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sominaḥ: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • puṣṭāvanto: nourished
    • yathā: as
    • paśum: the cattle
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation to Indra, the king of gods, asking for his blessings and nourishment. It highlights the relationship between friends and the divine, emphasizing the importance of community and support in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Community Support: Just as friends come together to support one another, this mantra can be seen in real life when communities unite to help those in need, such as during natural disasters or personal crises.
    • Celebration of Togetherness: The concept of sharing a sacred drink, like soma, can be likened to modern-day gatherings where people come together to celebrate milestones, reinforcing bonds and nourishing relationships.
    • Seeking Divine Blessings: In many cultures, people invoke divine blessings for prosperity and health, similar to how this mantra seeks the favor of Indra for nourishment and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the divine. It teaches us that by supporting one another and seeking blessings from higher powers, we can achieve prosperity and harmony in our lives. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community efforts, reminding us of the importance of unity and divine grace in our journey through life.
