आ श्वैत्रेयस्य जन्तवो द्युमद वर्धन्त कृष्टयः |निष्कग्रीवो बृहदुक्त एना मध्वा न वाजयुः ||
ā śvaitreyasya jantavo dyumad vardhanta kṛṣṭayaḥ | niṣkagrīvo bṛhaduktha enā madhvā na vājayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • śvaitreyasya: of the white (or bright) beings
    • jantavo: creatures or beings
    • dyumad: radiant or shining
    • vardhanta: they grow or increase
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: through cultivation or effort
    • niṣkagrīvo: free from bondage or attachment
    • bṛhaduktha: great praise or invocation
    • enā: with this
    • madhvā: sweet or pleasant
    • na: not
    • vājayuḥ: to be hindered or obstructed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the growth and flourishing of beings that are bright and radiant.
    • It emphasizes the importance of effort and cultivation in achieving growth.
    • The phrase "free from bondage" suggests a state of liberation, where beings are not held back by attachments or limitations.
    • The invocation of "great praise" indicates a call for recognition and appreciation of the divine or the higher powers.
    • Finally, the mantra assures that with this sweet invocation, there will be no hindrance to progress or success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often strive to grow and improve themselves through education and self-reflection, akin to the "cultivation" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a workplace setting, a team that works collaboratively and praises each other's efforts can create a positive environment that fosters growth and innovation.
    • In spiritual practices, the idea of being "free from bondage" resonates with the pursuit of enlightenment, where one seeks to detach from material desires and attachments.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of effort, collaboration, and spiritual growth in achieving success and fulfillment in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that through positive actions and a supportive environment, individuals and communities can thrive and overcome obstacles.
    • In essence, it encourages us to cultivate our inner strengths and to recognize the divine presence in our endeavors, ensuring that we remain unimpeded in our journey towards growth and enlightenment.
