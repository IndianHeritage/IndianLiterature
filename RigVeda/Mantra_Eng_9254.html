पृथक् प्रायन् प्रथमā देवहूतयः 'कृत्वता श्रवस्यानि दुष्टरा |न ये शेकुर यज्ञियां नावम् आरुहम ईर्मैव ते न्य अविशन्त केपयः ||
pṛthak prāyan prathamā devahūtayo 'kṛṇvata śravasyāni duṣṭarā | na ye śekur yajñiyāṁ nāvam āruham īrmaiva te ny aviśanta kepayaḥ ||
  • pṛthak prāyan prathamā devahūtayo 'kṛṇvata śravasyāni duṣṭarā
    • pṛthak - separate or distinct
    • prāyan - journey or path
    • prathamā - first or foremost
    • devahūtayo - divine beings or gods
    • 'kṛṇvata - those who create or make
    • śravasyāni - fame or glory
    • duṣṭarā - difficult or challenging
  • na ye śekur yajñiyāṁ nāvam āruham īrmaiva te ny aviśanta kepayaḥ
    • na ye - those who do not
    • śekur - are able or capable
    • yajñiyāṁ - in the sacrificial or sacred
    • nāvam - boat or vessel
    • āruham - to embark or board
    • īrmaiva - indeed or certainly
    • te ny aviśanta - they do not enter
    • kepayaḥ - with effort or struggle

This mantra speaks about the journey of divine beings who create fame and glory, emphasizing the importance of embarking on a sacred path. It suggests that those who are unable to engage in this sacred journey will not find success or fulfillment.


In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of one's goals and aspirations. For instance, individuals who strive to achieve their dreams, whether in their careers, education, or personal lives, often face challenges. However, those who remain dedicated and committed to their path, much like the divine beings mentioned in the mantra, are more likely to achieve their desired outcomes.


Furthermore, the mantra highlights the significance of effort and capability in achieving success. It serves as a reminder that while the journey may be difficult, it is essential to remain focused and determined. For example, a student preparing for a competitive exam must put in the effort and dedication to succeed, just as the divine beings create their path to glory.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to embark on a sacred journey with dedication and effort. It teaches us that while challenges may arise, those who are committed to their goals and work hard will ultimately find success. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including education, career, and personal development, reminding us that the path to achievement requires both effort and perseverance.
