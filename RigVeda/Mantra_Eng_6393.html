इन्द्र प्रेहि पुरस्त्वं विश्वेशान ओजसा |वृत्राणि वृत्रहञ्ज जहि ||
indra prehi puras tvaṁ viśvasyeśāna ojasā | vṛtrāṇi vṛtrahañ jahi ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Prehi: This term can be translated as "to send forth" or "to direct." It implies a call to action or a request for assistance.
  • Puras: Meaning "in front" or "before," this word indicates a position of leadership or guidance.
  • Tvaṁ: This translates to "you," referring directly to Indra, emphasizing the personal connection in the invocation.
  • Viśvasyeśāna: This compound word can be broken down into "viśva" (universe or all) and "īśāna" (lord or master), suggesting Indra as the master of the universe.
  • Ojasā: This word means "with strength" or "with energy," highlighting the power that Indra possesses.
  • Vṛtrāṇi: Referring to "Vṛtra," a serpent or dragon-like demon that represents obstacles or challenges. "Vṛtrāṇi" indicates the various forms of these challenges.
  • Vṛtrahañ: This term means "to slay Vṛtra," signifying the act of overcoming obstacles and challenges.
  • Jahi: This is a command form meaning "to defeat" or "to conquer," reinforcing the call to action against adversities.

The mantra can be understood as a powerful invocation to Indra, asking him to lead and empower the devotee to overcome obstacles and challenges in life. It emphasizes the need for divine assistance in facing adversities, much like how a leader guides their followers through difficult times.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in personal life, such as health issues or emotional struggles, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance.
  • Professional Obstacles: In the workplace, when encountering challenges in projects or conflicts with colleagues, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek help and overcome hurdles with confidence.
  • Community Leadership: Leaders in communities can use this mantra to inspire themselves and others to tackle social issues, encouraging collective action to defeat challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that with strength and guidance, one can conquer obstacles, much like Indra, the powerful deity, who is called upon to lead and empower. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and determination in their personal and professional lives.
