अध क्रत्वा मगवन तुभ्यं देवाऽनु विश्वे अददुः सोमपेयं |यत सूर्यस्य हरितः पतन्तीः पुरः सतीर उपराऽएतशे कः ||
adha kratvā maghavan tubhyaṁ devā anu viśve adaduḥ somapeyam | yat sūryasya haritaḥ patantīḥ puraḥ satīr uparā etaśe kaḥ ||
  • Adha kratvā maghavan tubhyaṁ devā anu viśve adaduḥ somapeyam:
    • Adha: This word means "down" or "below," indicating a sense of humility or grounding.
    • Kratvā: Refers to "with effort" or "by means of action," suggesting the importance of active participation.
    • Maghavan: This is a name for the deity Indra, often associated with strength and abundance.
    • Tubhyaṁ: Means "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine.
    • Devā: Refers to the gods or divine beings.
    • anu viśve: Translates to "along with all," suggesting inclusivity and unity among all beings.
    • Adaduḥ: Means "they have given," indicating a sense of offering or sharing.
    • Somapeyam: Refers to "the drink of immortality," symbolizing nourishment and spiritual sustenance.
  • Yat sūryasya haritaḥ patantīḥ puraḥ satīr uparā etaśe kaḥ:
    • Yat: Means "that which," introducing a clause that describes a condition or quality.
    • Sūryasya: Refers to "of the sun," symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • Haritaḥ: Means "green" or "prosperous," indicating growth and vitality.
    • Patantīḥ: Refers to "falling" or "descending," suggesting a natural process or cycle.
    • Puraḥ: Means "in front" or "before," indicating precedence or importance.
    • Satīr: Refers to "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," suggesting discernment and understanding.
    • Uparā: Means "above" or "beyond," indicating a higher state or elevation.
    • Etaśe: Refers to "this," pointing to a specific context or situation.
    • Kaḥ: Means "who," posing a question about identity or agency.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of humility, effort, and the interconnectedness of all beings in the pursuit of spiritual nourishment.
    • It highlights the role of divine forces in providing sustenance and abundance, encouraging individuals to recognize and appreciate these gifts.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, where individuals come together to support one another, or in personal growth, where one acknowledges the contributions of others in their journey.
    • The mantra serves as a reminder to remain grounded while striving for higher understanding and connection with the divine and the universe.
