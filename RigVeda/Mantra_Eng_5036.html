सँ वाँ शतां नासत्यां सहस्राश्वानां पुरुपन्था गिरे दात् |भरद्वाजाय वीर नू गिरे दाद धता रक्षांसी पुरुदंससां स्युः ||
saṁ vāṁ śatā nāsatyā sahasrāśvānām purupanthā gire dāt | bharadvājāya vīra nū gire dād dhatā rakṣāṁsi purudaṁsasā syuḥ ||
  • saṁ vāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "together with you" or "with your voice." It emphasizes unity and collaboration.
  • śatā nāsatyā: "Nāsatyā" refers to the Asvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine. "Śatā" means "hundreds," indicating abundance or multitude.
  • sahasrāśvānām: This term translates to "of the thousand steeds," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • purupanthā: Meaning "the path of the wise" or "the way of the enlightened," it suggests a journey guided by knowledge and wisdom.
  • gire dāt: "Gire" means "speech" or "voice," and "dāt" means "to give." This can be understood as a call to share wisdom and knowledge.
  • bharadvājāya: Referring to the sage Bharadvaja, this invokes the idea of seeking guidance from wise individuals.
  • vīra nū: "Vīra" means "hero" or "brave one," and "nū" can be interpreted as "may you" or "let it be." This encourages bravery and valor.
  • gire dād: Reiterating the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom through speech.
  • dhathā rakṣāṁsi: "Dhathā" means "to hold" or "to maintain," and "rakṣāṁsi" refers to "protections" or "safeguards." This emphasizes the importance of maintaining safety and security.
  • purudaṁsasā syuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may they be endowed with great strength" or "may they possess the qualities of the strong." It highlights the aspiration for strength and resilience.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, bravery, and protection. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in organizations, where collaboration and shared knowledge lead to success. It also resonates in personal development, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom from mentors and to be brave in facing challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder that strength comes not only from individual efforts but also from collective wisdom and support, making it significant in both personal and communal growth.
