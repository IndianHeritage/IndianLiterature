पूर्वाम् अनु प्रयतिम् आ ददॆ वस त्रीन् युक्ताम् अष्टाव् अरिधायसो गाः |सुबन्धवो ये विषयाऽ इव व्राऽ अनस्वन्तः श्रव आइषन्त पज्राः ||
pūrvām anu prayatim ā dade vas trīn yuktām̐ aṣṭāv aridhāyaso gāḥ | subandhavo ye viśyā iva vrā anasvantaḥ śrava aiṣanta pajrāḥ ||
  • pūrvām anu prayatim: This phrase can be translated as "with prior effort" or "after careful consideration." It emphasizes the importance of preparation and intention in any endeavor.
  • ā dade vas trīn yuktām̐: This means "to give three well-formed (or well-prepared) things." It suggests that one should focus on quality and thoughtfulness in what they offer or create.
  • aṣṭāv aridhāyaso gāḥ: This translates to "the eight (aspects) of the difficult (or challenging) paths." It indicates that there are multiple dimensions or challenges in life that one must navigate.
  • subandhavo ye viśyā: This can be interpreted as "those who are well-connected or well-related." It highlights the significance of relationships and connections in achieving success.
  • iva vrā anasvantaḥ: This means "like the unyielding (or steadfast) ones." It suggests the importance of resilience and determination in the face of challenges.
  • śrava aiṣanta pajrāḥ: This translates to "they who have heard (or understood) the call." It emphasizes the value of awareness and responsiveness to the needs of the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of preparation, quality, resilience, and the importance of relationships in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, professional endeavors, and community building. For instance, when starting a new project, one should invest time in planning (pūrvām anu prayatim), ensure that the resources or ideas are well-prepared (ā dade vas trīn yuktām̐), and be aware of the challenges that may arise (aṣṭāv aridhāyaso gāḥ). Building strong connections with others (subandhavo ye viśyā) and remaining steadfast in pursuit of goals (iva vrā anasvantaḥ) can lead to greater success. Ultimately, being attuned to the needs of others (śrava aiṣanta pajrāḥ) fosters a collaborative environment that benefits all.
