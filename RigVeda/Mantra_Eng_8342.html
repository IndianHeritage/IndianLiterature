अरममाणो अति एति गा अभि सूर्यस्य प्रियं दुहितुस तिरो रवम् |अन्व अस्मै जोषम् अभरद विनङ्गृषः सं द्वयीभिः स्वसृभिः क्षेति जामिभिः ||
aramamāṇo aty eti gā abhi sūryasya priyaṁ duhitus tiro ravam | anv asmai joṣam abharad vinaṁgṛsaḥ saṁ dvayībhiḥ svasṛbhiḥ kṣeti jāmibhiḥ ||
  • aramamāṇo: This term can be interpreted as "one who is engaged in the pursuit of happiness" or "seeking joy." In real life, this can relate to individuals who strive for personal fulfillment and contentment through various means, such as relationships, career achievements, or spiritual practices.
  • aty eti: This phrase translates to "goes beyond" or "transcends." It signifies the idea of surpassing limitations or obstacles. For example, a student who studies diligently and overcomes academic challenges exemplifies this concept by achieving success beyond their initial capabilities.
  • gā abhi: This can be understood as "to the song" or "to the praise." It reflects the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation. In everyday life, this could be seen in how people celebrate achievements, such as a wedding or a promotion, often accompanied by songs and praises.
  • sūryasya priyaṁ: This means "beloved of the sun." The sun is often associated with vitality, energy, and life. In a practical sense, this could refer to individuals who embody positivity and warmth, much like the sun, and who are cherished by those around them for their uplifting presence.
  • duhitus: This word translates to "daughter." It can symbolize nurturing and the importance of familial bonds. In real life, this can be seen in the way families support and uplift their daughters, encouraging them to pursue their dreams and aspirations.
  • tiro ravam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the sound that comes from afar." It suggests the idea of distant communication or the impact of one's actions reaching beyond immediate surroundings. For instance, a leader's vision can inspire people far beyond their immediate community, creating a ripple effect of influence.
  • anv asmai: This means "to him" or "to that one." It indicates a direction of devotion or focus. In practical terms, this could relate to how individuals dedicate their efforts towards a cause or a person they admire, such as a mentor or a leader.
  • joṣam abharad: This translates to "brought joy" or "bestowed happiness." It emphasizes the act of sharing joy with others. In real life, this can be seen in community service or acts of kindness, where individuals contribute to the happiness of others, such as volunteering at a shelter.
  • vinaṁgṛsaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is wise" or "the knowledgeable." It highlights the value of wisdom and understanding. In everyday life, this could refer to a teacher or a mentor who imparts knowledge and guides others in their journey.
  • saṁ dvayībhiḥ: This means "with two" or "in pairs." It suggests the importance of companionship and collaboration. In real life, this can be seen in partnerships, whether in business or personal relationships, where two individuals work together towards a common goal.
  • svasṛbhiḥ: This translates to "with sisters" or "with kin." It emphasizes the significance of familial ties and support systems. In practical terms, this can be observed in how siblings or close friends support each other through life's challenges.
  • kṣeti jāmibhiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is established" or "the one who is grounded." It signifies stability and rootedness. In real life, this could refer to individuals who have a strong sense of identity and purpose, providing a foundation for themselves and others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of joy, transcendence, familial bonds, wisdom, and collaboration. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek happiness, support one another, and strive for personal growth while maintaining strong connections with loved ones. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering positive relationships, pursuing personal goals, and contributing to the well-being of the community. By embodying these values, individuals can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, much like the interconnectedness celebrated in this mantra.
