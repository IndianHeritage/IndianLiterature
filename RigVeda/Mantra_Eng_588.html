अप त्ये तायवो यथा नक्षत्रा यन्त्यक्तुभिः |सूराय विश्वचक्षसे ||
apa tye tāyavo yathā nakṣatrā yanty aktubhiḥ | sūrāya viśvacakṣase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apa: away, from
    • tye: those
    • tāyavo: who are strong, powerful
    • yathā: as, like
    • nakṣatrā: stars
    • yanty: move, go
    • aktubhiḥ: by the light
    • sūrāya: to the wise, the enlightened
    • viśvacakṣase: to the one with universal vision
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the movement of powerful beings (or forces) akin to the stars that navigate through the universe by their own light.
    • The phrase "apa tye tāyavo" suggests a departure from ignorance or darkness, emphasizing the strength and power of those who seek enlightenment.
    • The comparison to stars indicates guidance and the ability to shine brightly in the darkness, symbolizing knowledge and wisdom.
    • "Sūrāya viśvacakṣase" refers to the enlightened ones who possess a universal vision, suggesting that true wisdom allows one to see beyond the immediate and understand the broader context of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of strength and wisdom can guide their team through challenges, much like stars guiding travelers at night.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and understanding can illuminate their paths, helping them make informed decisions and navigate life's complexities.
    • In education, teachers who inspire students to think critically and see the bigger picture are akin to the "sūrāya viśvacakṣase," fostering a generation that can navigate the world with insight.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment, encouraging individuals to move away from ignorance and towards wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that, like stars, we can guide ourselves and others through the darkness of uncertainty by embracing knowledge and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership, education, and personal growth, highlighting the transformative power of wisdom and the strength it brings to navigate life's challenges.
