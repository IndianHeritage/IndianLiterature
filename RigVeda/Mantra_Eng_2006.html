कुषुम्भकस्तद अब्रवीद गिरेः प्रवर्तमानकः |वृश्चिकस्यारसं विषम अरसं वृश्चिक ते विषम् ||
kuṣumbhakas tad abravīd gireḥ pravartamānakaḥ | vṛścikasyārasaṁ viṣam arasaṁ vṛścika te viṣam ||
  • kuṣumbhakas: This term refers to a type of flower, often associated with beauty and vibrancy. In a broader sense, it can symbolize the essence of life and nature.
  • tad abravīd: This phrase translates to "that spoke" or "that declared," indicating a communication or revelation of some kind.
  • gireḥ: This word means "of the mountain" or "of the voice," suggesting a connection to nature or the divine, often representing strength and stability.
  • pravartamānakaḥ: This term means "the one who is in motion" or "the one who initiates," indicating an active force or energy that drives change.
  • vṛścikasyārasaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the poison of the scorpion," symbolizing danger or harmful elements in life.
  • viṣam: This word means "poison" or "toxic," reinforcing the idea of harmful influences that can affect one's life.
  • arasaṁ: This term translates to "non-poisonous" or "pure," suggesting the presence of both good and bad in life.
  • vṛścika te viṣam: This phrase can be understood as "the poison of the scorpion is yours," indicating a personal connection to the dangers or challenges one faces.

The mantra speaks to the duality of existence, highlighting the interplay between beauty and danger, purity and toxicity. It suggests that while life is filled with vibrant experiences (like the kuṣumbhakas flower), there are also inherent risks (symbolized by the scorpion's poison) that one must navigate.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the kuṣumbhakas blooms amidst challenges, individuals can find beauty and strength in overcoming obstacles.
  • Relationships: The balance of good and bad in relationships mirrors the duality presented in the mantra, reminding us to appreciate the positive while being aware of potential dangers.
  • Health and Well-being: The reference to poison can be seen as a metaphor for unhealthy habits or toxic environments, encouraging individuals to seek purity and wellness in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the complexities of life, urging us to embrace both the beauty and the challenges we encounter. It encourages mindfulness and awareness, helping us to navigate our paths with wisdom and resilience.
