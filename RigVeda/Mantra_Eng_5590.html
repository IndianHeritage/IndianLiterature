सां विट सुवीराः मरुद्भिर अस्तु सनात् सहन्ती पुष्यन्ती नृम्णम् ||
sā viṭ suvīrā marudbhir astu sanāt sahantī puṣyantī nṛmṇam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sā: This word translates to "that" or "she," often referring to a divine or auspicious entity.
    • viṭ: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance.
    • suvīrā: This translates to "good heroes" or "valiant ones," suggesting strength and valor.
    • marudbhir: This word refers to "the winds" or "the storm gods," symbolizing power and movement.
    • astu: This means "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or blessing.
    • sanāt: This translates to "eternal" or "everlasting," indicating something that transcends time.
    • sahantī: This means "to bear" or "to support," suggesting resilience and strength.
    • puṣyantī: This translates to "nourishing" or "prospering," indicating growth and sustenance.
    • nṛmṇam: This refers to "human beings" or "mankind," emphasizing the focus on humanity.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for prosperity, strength, and support from divine forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength, as it calls upon the "good heroes" and "winds" to bless humanity.
    • The use of "eternal" suggests that the blessings sought are not temporary but are meant to last through time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during gatherings or festivals to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and strength.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of challenge, seeking resilience and support from their community and the divine.
    • In business, leaders might invoke this mantra to inspire teamwork and collective effort towards achieving common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and prosperity for all of humanity.
    • It serves as a reminder that individual success is intertwined with the well-being of the community.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal challenges to community efforts, emphasizing the importance of collective strength and support.
