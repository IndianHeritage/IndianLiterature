अग्नि दिवः सूनुरसि प्रचेतस तना पृथिव्या उता विश्ववेदाः |ऋधग देवां इह यजाः चिकीत्वः ||
agne divaḥ sūnur asi pracetās tanā pṛthivyā uta viśvavedāḥ | ṛdhag devām̐ iha yajā cikitvaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a source of energy.
  • Divaḥ: Meaning "of the heavens" or "celestial," this word connects the divine with the earthly realm.
  • Sūnur: Translated as "son," it signifies a relationship, often implying a lineage or a connection to the divine.
  • Asi: This means "you are," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • Pracetās: Refers to "intelligent" or "wise," suggesting the importance of wisdom in understanding the divine.
  • Tanā: Meaning "body" or "form," it emphasizes the physical aspect of existence.
  • Pṛthivyā: This translates to "of the earth," grounding the mantra in the physical world.
  • Uta: Meaning "and" or "also," it connects different elements of the mantra.
  • Viśvavedāḥ: This term means "all-knowing" or "universal knowledge," indicating the vastness of understanding that can be achieved.
  • Ṛdhag: This word means "prosperous" or "successful," suggesting a state of abundance.
  • Devām̐: Referring to "gods," it highlights the divine aspect of the mantra.
  • Iha: Meaning "here" or "in this world," it emphasizes the relevance of the mantra in our current existence.
  • Yajā: This means "to worship" or "to offer," indicating the act of devotion.
  • Cikitvaḥ: Translated as "with understanding" or "with wisdom," it underscores the importance of knowledge in worship.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Agni, the divine fire, recognizing his celestial nature and the wisdom he embodies. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that through worship and understanding, one can achieve prosperity and knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to embrace change and seek wisdom in their personal journeys.
  • Environmental Awareness: The connection to the earth (Pṛthivyā) encourages respect for nature and the importance of sustainable living.
  • Community and Relationships: The emphasis on worship and understanding can be applied to fostering strong relationships within communities, promoting harmony and cooperation.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in our lives and the wisdom that comes from understanding our connection to both the celestial and earthly realms. It serves as a guide for personal development, environmental consciousness, and community building, making it highly relevant in today's world.
