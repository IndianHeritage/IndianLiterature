तया पवस्व धारया यया गावा इहागमन | जन्यास उप नो गृहम् ||
tayā pavasva dhārayā yayā gāva ihāgaman | janyāsa upa no gṛham ||
  • Mantra: "tayā pavasva dhārayā yayā gāva ihāgaman | janyāsa upa no gṛham ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tayā: by that
    • pavasva: may you purify
    • dhārayā: by the support
    • yayā: by which
    • gāva: the cows
    • ihāgaman: come here
    • janyāsa: of the progeny
    • upa: near
    • no: to us
    • gṛham: home
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for purification and support, invoking the divine to bless the household with prosperity and abundance.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and the sustenance of life, as cows were considered sacred and vital in Vedic culture.
    • The phrase "may you purify" indicates a desire for spiritual and physical cleanliness, essential for a harmonious life.
    • By asking for the progeny to come near, it reflects a longing for family, community, and the continuation of life and traditions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the health of livestock, particularly cows, directly impacts the livelihood of families. This mantra can be recited during rituals to ensure the well-being of animals, which in turn supports the family.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be adapted to invoke blessings for the home, such as during housewarming ceremonies or family gatherings, emphasizing the importance of unity and prosperity.
    • In personal development, the idea of purification can be related to mental and emotional clarity, encouraging individuals to seek inner peace and harmony in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, support, and the importance of family and community in achieving a prosperous life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for spiritual and physical well-being.
    • In contemporary life, the principles behind this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community building, highlighting the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom.
