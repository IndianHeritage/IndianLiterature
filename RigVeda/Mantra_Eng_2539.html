वनस्पते शतवल्शो वि रोह सहस्रवल्शा वि वयं रुहेमा |यं त्वाम अयं स्वधितिस तेजमानः प्राणिनाय महते सौभगाय ||
vanaspate śatavalśo vi roha sahasravalśā vi vayaṁ ruhema | yaṁ tvām ayaṁ svadhitis tejamānaḥ praṇināya mahate saubhagāya ||
  • vanaspate: This term refers to the "lord of the plants" or "vegetation." In a real-life context, this can be understood as the importance of nature and the environment in sustaining life.
  • śatavalśo: This word means "having a hundred branches." It symbolizes abundance and diversity in nature, akin to a tree that provides shelter and sustenance to various forms of life.
  • vi roha: This phrase translates to "may you grow." It signifies the aspiration for growth and prosperity, not just for individuals but for communities and ecosystems as well.
  • sahasravalśā: Meaning "having a thousand branches," this term emphasizes the vastness and richness of nature, suggesting that true wealth lies in biodiversity and ecological balance.
  • vi vayaṁ ruhema: This can be interpreted as "may we attain longevity." It reflects the desire for a long and healthy life, which is often linked to a harmonious relationship with nature.
  • yaṁ tvām: This phrase means "to you." It indicates a reverence towards the natural world, acknowledging its significance in our lives.
  • ayaṁ svadhitis: This translates to "this is the essence." It suggests that the essence of life is intertwined with nature and its elements.
  • tejamānaḥ: Meaning "radiant" or "brilliant," this term highlights the vitality and energy that nature provides to all living beings.
  • praṇināya: This word means "for all beings." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of nurturing our environment for the benefit of all.
  • mahate saubhagāya: This phrase translates to "for great fortune." It signifies that a healthy relationship with nature leads to prosperity and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nature and its vital role in sustaining life. It calls for respect and reverence towards the environment, highlighting the interconnectedness of all living beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as promoting environmental conservation, sustainable practices, and biodiversity. By nurturing our natural surroundings, we not only ensure our own well-being but also contribute to the prosperity of future generations. This mantra serves as a reminder that our health and fortune are deeply linked to the health of our planet.
