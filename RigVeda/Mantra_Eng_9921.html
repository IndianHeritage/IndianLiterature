अवपतन्तीर अवदन दिव ओषधयस् परि |यं जीवम अश्नवामहै न स रिष्याती पुरुषः ||
avapatantīr avadan diva oṣadhayas pari | yaṁ jīvam aśnavāmahai na sa riṣyāti pūruṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "avapatantīr avadan diva oṣadhayas pari | yaṁ jīvam aśnavāmahai na sa riṣyāti pūruṣaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avapatantīr: those that fall or descend
    • avadan: from the sky or heaven
    • diva: of the day or celestial
    • oṣadhayas: herbs or plants
    • pari: around or in abundance
    • yaṁ: which
    • jīvam: living or life
    • aśnavāmahai: we do not desire or seek
    • na: not
    • sa: he or that
    • riṣyāti: is troubled or afflicted
    • pūruṣaḥ: the person or human being
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the herbs and plants that descend from the heavens, symbolizing the divine gifts of nature.
    • It emphasizes the importance of life and the sustenance provided by nature, suggesting that we should not desire to harm or exploit these gifts.
    • The phrase "na sa riṣyāti pūruṣaḥ" indicates that a person who respects and cherishes these natural gifts will not face troubles or afflictions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, sustainable practices that respect the natural ecosystem can lead to healthier crops and a more stable environment.
    • Herbal medicine, which utilizes the healing properties of plants, reflects the reverence for nature's gifts as mentioned in the mantra.
    • Environmental conservation efforts, such as reforestation and protecting biodiversity, align with the idea of cherishing and preserving what nature provides.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of living in harmony with nature and respecting the resources it provides.
    • It serves as a reminder that by valuing and protecting our environment, we can lead healthier, more fulfilling lives.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges, the teachings of this mantra are increasingly relevant, encouraging us to adopt sustainable practices and foster a deeper connection with the natural world.
