मम ब्रह्मेन्द्र याह्य acchā विश्वा हरि धुरी धिष्वा रथस्य |पुरुत्रा हि विहव्यो बभूथास्मिञ्चूर सवने मादयस्व ||
mama brahmendra yāhy acchā viśvā harī dhuri dhiṣvā rathasya | purutrā hi vihavyo babhūthāsmiñ chūra savane mādayasva ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Brahman, may I be endowed with the strength of the universe; may I be victorious in the great battle of life. May I be like the swift horses of the chariot, and may I be filled with joy in the divine offering."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mama: my
    • brahmendra: the supreme consciousness or the ultimate reality
    • yāhy: may I be endowed
    • acchā: strength or power
    • viśvā: the universe or all-encompassing
    • harī: swift or powerful
    • dhuri: in the great battle
    • dhiṣvā: may I be victorious
    • rathasya: of the chariot
    • purutrā: in all directions
    • hi: indeed
    • vihavyo: may I be filled
    • babhūthāsmiñ: in this offering
    • chūra: joy or delight
    • savan: divine offering
    • mādayasva: may I be filled with joy
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a job interview, one can invoke the essence of this mantra to seek strength and confidence, aiming to present oneself as the best candidate.
    • During challenging times, like facing a personal crisis, one can reflect on this mantra to find inner strength and resilience, akin to the swift horses that drive a chariot forward.
    • In team sports, athletes often draw on collective strength and unity, similar to the idea of being victorious in a great battle, where teamwork leads to success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, victory, and joy in the face of life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of inner power and resilience, encouraging individuals to harness their potential and strive for success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional endeavors, or collective efforts, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength from within and to celebrate the victories, big or small, that life presents. It is applicable in various contexts, from personal development to teamwork, making it a timeless invocation for empowerment and joy.
