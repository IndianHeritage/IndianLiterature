आ नो भरा दक्षिणेनाभि सव्येन प्र मृश |इन्द्र मा नो वसोः निर्भाक् ||
ā no bhara dakṣiṇenābhi savyena pra mṛśa | indra mā no vasor nir bhāk ||
  • Mantra: "ā no bhara dakṣiṇenābhi savyena pra mṛśa | indra mā no vasor nir bhāk ||"
  • Translation: "May we be filled with the blessings of the right hand, and may we not be deprived of the wealth of Indra."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May
    • no: us
    • bhara: fill
    • dakṣiṇenābhi: with the right hand
    • savyena: with the left hand
    • pra: forth
    • mṛśa: touch
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • mā: may not
    • no: us
    • vasor: of wealth
    • nir: devoid
    • bhāk: share or portion
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of prosperity and abundance. The phrase "ā no bhara" signifies a request for fulfillment and abundance in life.
    • The mention of "dakṣiṇenābhi" (right hand) and "savyena" (left hand) symbolizes the balance of giving and receiving. In real life, this can be seen in the act of charity, where one gives with the right hand while being open to receiving blessings from the universe.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength, power, and wealth. By invoking Indra, the mantra seeks to ensure that one is not deprived of wealth and prosperity, which can be interpreted as a call for divine support in achieving success in various endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited by entrepreneurs seeking success and abundance in their ventures. It serves as a reminder to maintain a balance between hard work (giving) and openness to opportunities (receiving).
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of financial difficulty, seeking divine intervention to overcome challenges and attract prosperity.
    • During community gatherings or charitable events, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance in life—between giving and receiving, and between material wealth and spiritual fulfillment.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal aspirations to communal efforts, reminding individuals to seek divine blessings while actively participating in the flow of abundance.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages a mindset of gratitude and openness, fostering a harmonious relationship with wealth and prosperity.
