प्रातर्जितं भागं उग्रं हुवेम वयं पुत्रं अदितेर यो विधर्ता |आध्रश्चिद्यं मन्यमानस्तुरश्चिद्राजा चिद्यं भागं भक्षीत्याह ||
prātarjitam bhagam ugraṁ huvema vayam putram aditer yo vidhartā | ādhraś cid yam manyamānas turaś cid rājā cid yam bhagam bhakṣīty āha ||
  • Translation: "In the morning, we invoke the powerful fortune; we call upon the son of Aditi, who sustains all. We consider him to be the one who is the ruler, the one who is the sustainer, and the one who is the fortune that is to be consumed."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prātarjitam: Morning invocation
    • bhagam: Fortune or wealth
    • ugraṁ: Powerful or fierce
    • huvema: We invoke or call upon
    • vayam: We
    • putram: Son
    • aditer: Of Aditi (the mother of gods)
    • yo: Who
    • vidhartā: Sustainer or nourisher
    • ādhraś: Considered or thought of
    • cid: Indeed or certainly
    • yam: Him
    • manyamānas: Thinking or considering
    • turaś: Ruler or king
    • rājā: King
    • bhagam: Fortune
    • bhakṣīty: To be consumed
    • āha: He said
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings at the start of the day. Just as we prepare for our daily tasks, this invocation serves as a reminder to seek guidance and strength from higher powers.
    • The mention of Aditi, the mother of gods, signifies the nurturing aspect of the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the support we receive from our families and communities, which sustains us through challenges.
    • The idea of considering the divine as a ruler or sustainer reflects the belief that there is a higher order in the universe that governs our lives. This can be seen in how people often turn to spirituality or religion during difficult times, seeking comfort and direction.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of starting each day with a sense of purpose and connection to the divine. It encourages individuals to recognize the sources of strength and support in their lives.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied by incorporating morning rituals, such as meditation or prayer, into daily routines to foster a positive mindset and attract good fortune.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our endeavors; we are supported by both the divine and our community, which can empower us to face the challenges of life with confidence.
