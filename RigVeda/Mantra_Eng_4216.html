आ नो मित्र सुदीतिभिर वरुणश्च सदस्थ आ |स्वे क्षये मग्नानां सखीनां च वृद्धसे ||
ā no mitra sudītibhir varuṇaś ca sadhastha ā | sve kṣaye maghonāṁ sakhīnāṁ ca vṛdhase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May we (us)
    • no: our
    • mitra: O Friend (Mitra, the deity of friendship and harmony)
    • sudītibhir: with good thoughts or intentions
    • varuṇaś: and Varuna (the deity of cosmic order and water)
    • ca: and
    • sadhastha: who is present in the assembly
    • ā: may
    • sve: in our own
    • kṣaye: destruction or downfall
    • maghonāṁ: of the wealthy or prosperous
    • sakhīnāṁ: of friends or companions
    • ca: and
    • vṛdhase: may you grow or increase
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the blessings of Mitra and Varuna, seeking their guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship, good intentions, and the presence of divine forces in our lives.
    • The mantra also expresses a desire for prosperity and growth, both personally and within our community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business setting, this mantra can be invoked to foster teamwork and collaboration among colleagues, ensuring that everyone works with good intentions and mutual respect.
    • During community gatherings or family events, reciting this mantra can enhance the spirit of togetherness and prosperity, encouraging everyone to support one another.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to cultivate friendships based on trust and goodwill, which can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, prosperity, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, highlighting the importance of good intentions and divine support.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous life, fostering connections that lead to collective growth and well-being.
