उत स्वानासो दिवि शान्त्व अग्नेस तिग्मायुधा रक्षसे हन्तवा उ |मदे चिद अस्य प्र रुजन्ति भामाऽ न वरन्ते परिबाधो अदेविः ||
uta svānāso divi ṣantv agnes tigmāyudhā rakṣase hantavā u | made cid asya pra rujanti bhāmā na varante paribādho adevīḥ ||
  • uta svānāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the horses (or steeds) be swift." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the need for speed and efficiency in our endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal goals.
  • divi ṣantv: Translated as "may they be in the heavens," this suggests a connection to higher aspirations or divine guidance. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to aim for lofty goals and seek inspiration from higher ideals or mentors.
  • agnes tigmāyudhā: This refers to "O Agni, with your sharp weapons." Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and purification. In everyday life, this can be seen as a call to harness our inner strength and passion to overcome challenges and obstacles.
  • rakṣase hantavā u: This means "to vanquish the enemies." In a contemporary setting, this can relate to overcoming personal fears, doubts, or external challenges that hinder progress. It emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination in facing adversities.
  • made cid asya pra rujanti bhāmā: This translates to "may the beautiful ones (or the enlightened) be free from distress." This highlights the importance of mental and emotional well-being. In real life, it can be applied to the pursuit of happiness and the need for supportive relationships that foster a positive environment.
  • na varante paribādho adevīḥ: This means "may the divine beings not be obstructed." It signifies the importance of maintaining a clear path for spiritual or personal growth. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to remove distractions and obstacles that prevent them from achieving their true potential.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a powerful message of aspiration, strength, and the pursuit of higher ideals. Its significance lies in the encouragement to strive for excellence, overcome challenges, and seek divine guidance in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, professional growth, and the cultivation of positive relationships, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and purposeful life.
