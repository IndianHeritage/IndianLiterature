मन्त्र: अन्यव्रतम अमानुषम् अयज्वानम् अदेवयुम् |अव स्वः सखा दुधुवीत पर्वतः सुघ्नाय दस्युम् पर्वतः ||
anyavratam amānuṣam ayajvānam adevayum | ava svaḥ sakhā dudhuvīta parvataḥ sughnāya dasyum parvataḥ ||
  • anyavratam: This term can be translated as "other vows" or "different commitments." It signifies the various paths or duties that individuals may choose in life.
  • amānuṣam: Meaning "non-human" or "superhuman," this word refers to forces or entities beyond human comprehension, often associated with divine or cosmic powers.
  • ayajvānam: This translates to "those who do not perform sacrifices." In the context of the Vedic tradition, sacrifices (yajnas) are essential for maintaining harmony with the universe.
  • adevayum: This term means "without the divine" or "devoid of the sacred." It suggests a state of existence that lacks spiritual connection or divine guidance.
  • ava: This word means "down" or "away," indicating a sense of separation or distancing from something.
  • svaḥ: Translated as "heaven" or "the celestial realm," it represents a higher state of existence or consciousness.
  • sakhā: This means "friend" or "ally," emphasizing the importance of companionship and support in one's journey.
  • dudhuvīta: This term can be interpreted as "to draw out" or "to extract," suggesting the act of bringing forth something valuable or essential.
  • parvataḥ: Meaning "mountain," it symbolizes stability, strength, and the challenges one must face in life.
  • sughnāya: This translates to "for the good" or "for welfare," indicating actions taken for the benefit of oneself and others.
  • dasyum: This term refers to "the enemy" or "obstacle," representing the challenges and adversities one encounters.
  • parvataḥ: Repeated for emphasis, it reinforces the idea of mountains as both physical and metaphorical challenges in life.

Conclusion: This mantra reflects the complexities of human existence, emphasizing the importance of spiritual connection, the need for sacrifices, and the role of companionship in overcoming life's challenges. In real life, it serves as a reminder to stay aligned with one's higher purpose, seek divine guidance, and support one another in the face of adversities. The metaphor of mountains signifies that while challenges may be daunting, they can also lead to personal growth and enlightenment when approached with the right mindset and support.
