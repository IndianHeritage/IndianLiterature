सप्त युञ्जन्ति रथम एकचक्रम् एको अश्वो वहति सप्तनामा |त्रिनाभि चक्रम् अजरो अनर्वं यत्रेमा विश्वा भुवनाधि तस्थुः ||
sapta yuñjanti ratham ekacakram eko aśvo vahati saptanāmā | trinābhi cakram ajaram anarvaṁ yatremā viśvā bhuvanādhi tasthuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of the seven who unite to drive a single chariot, where a single horse carries the seven names. The three-spoked wheel is imperishable and unassailable, and wherever it is, all worlds stand firm.
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sapta: Seven
    • Yuñjanti: Unite or join
    • Ratham: Chariot
    • Ekacakram: One wheel
    • Eko: One
    • Aśvo: Horse
    • Vahati: Carries
    • Saptanāmā: Seven names
    • Trinābhi: Three spokes
    • Cakram: Wheel
    • Ajara: Imperishable
    • Anarvaṁ: Unassailable
    • Yatremā: Wherever
    • Viśvā: All
    • Bhuvanādhi: Worlds
    • Tasthuḥ: Stand firm
  • Interpretation:
    • The "seven" can symbolize various aspects of life, such as the seven chakras in the human body, which need to be harmonized for overall well-being.
    • The "chariot" represents the journey of life, where the individual must navigate through various experiences and challenges.
    • The "one wheel" signifies unity and the importance of a singular focus in achieving goals.
    • The "horse" symbolizes strength and energy, essential for propelling the chariot forward.
    • The "three-spoked wheel" can represent the three fundamental aspects of existence: creation, preservation, and destruction.
    • The idea of being "imperishable" and "unassailable" suggests resilience and the ability to withstand life's adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the "seven" can represent team members working together towards a common goal, where each member brings unique strengths to the table.
    • In personal development, focusing on the "one wheel" can mean prioritizing mental, physical, and emotional health to achieve a balanced life.
    • In business, the "three spokes" can symbolize the need for innovation, customer satisfaction, and operational efficiency to ensure long-term success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, resilience, and focus in both personal and collective endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may have different roles and identities (the seven names), we are all part of a larger journey (the chariot) that requires collaboration and strength.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective teamwork, and a balanced approach to challenges.
