त्वं नो अग्न एषां गयम् पुष्टिं च वर्धय |ये स्तोमेभिः प्र सूरयो नरो मग्हान्य आनाšuḥ ||
tvaṁ no agna eṣāṁ gayam puṣṭiṁ ca vardhaya | ye stomebhiḥ pra sūrayo naro maghāny ānaśuḥ ||
  • tvaṁ no agna: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger. In real life, Agni represents the inner fire or passion that drives individuals to pursue their goals.
  • eṣāṁ gayam: This means "this song of praise." It signifies the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people celebrate achievements or milestones, often through songs or speeches that honor contributions and efforts.
  • puṣṭiṁ ca vardhaya: This translates to "increase our nourishment." Here, nourishment can be understood both physically and spiritually. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking knowledge, health, and well-being, emphasizing the need for holistic growth in life.
  • ye stomebhiḥ: This means "those who with hymns." It refers to individuals who engage in rituals or prayers. In modern contexts, this can be likened to community gatherings or events where people come together to share their thoughts, prayers, or intentions, fostering a sense of unity.
  • pra sūrayo: This translates to "the brave ones." It highlights the importance of courage and valor in facing life's challenges. Real-life examples include individuals who stand up for justice or those who take risks to achieve their dreams, inspiring others in the process.
  • naro maghāny ānaśuḥ: This means "may men attain great wealth." It emphasizes the aspiration for prosperity and success. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of education, career advancement, and financial stability, which are common goals for many individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, expressing gratitude, and striving for personal and communal growth. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of individual aspirations and collective well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal reflections, community gatherings, or rituals aimed at fostering unity and prosperity. By invoking the spirit of Agni, individuals are reminded to nurture their inner fire, pursue their goals with courage, and contribute positively to their communities.
