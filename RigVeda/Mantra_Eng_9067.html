अपश्यं ग्रामं वहमानम आराद अचक्रया स्वधया वर्तमानम् |सिषक्त्य आर्यः प्र युगानां सद्यः शिष्णा प्रमिनानो नवीयान ||
apaśyaṁ grāmaṁ vahamānam ārād acakrayā svadhayā vartamānam | siṣakty aryaḥ pra yugā janānāṁ sadyaḥ śiśnā pramināno navīyān ||
  • apaśyaṁ grāmaṁ: This phrase translates to "I do not see the village." It signifies a state of unawareness or lack of perception regarding one's surroundings or community.
  • vahamānam: Meaning "carrying" or "bearing," this word suggests the act of taking on responsibilities or burdens, possibly referring to the collective duties of a community.
  • ārād acakrayā: This can be interpreted as "by the power of the divine" or "through the grace of the divine." It emphasizes the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
  • svadhayā vartamānam: This means "existing by one's own nature" or "acting according to one's own will." It highlights the significance of individual agency and self-determination.
  • siṣakty aryaḥ: This phrase translates to "the noble ones with strength." It refers to individuals who possess both moral integrity and physical strength, suggesting that true leadership combines virtue with capability.
  • pra yugā janānāṁ: Meaning "the people of the age," this indicates a focus on the current generation and their actions, emphasizing the relevance of contemporary society.
  • sadyaḥ: This word means "immediately" or "suddenly," suggesting a sense of urgency or the need for prompt action.
  • śiśnā pramināno: This can be interpreted as "the one who is wise and knowledgeable." It underscores the value of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • navīyān: Meaning "new" or "fresh," this word signifies innovation and the importance of embracing new ideas and perspectives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, individual agency, and the interplay between divine support and personal responsibility. In real life, it serves as a reminder to be aware of our surroundings and the collective burdens we carry. It encourages us to act with integrity and wisdom, especially in the context of contemporary challenges. By embracing new ideas and acting promptly, we can contribute positively to our communities and navigate the complexities of modern life. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership roles, community service, and personal development, where the balance of individual action and collective responsibility is crucial.
