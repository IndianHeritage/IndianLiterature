ब्रह्माणि मे मतयः शं सुतासः शुष्म इयर्ति प्रभृतो मे अद्रिः |आ शासते प्रति हर्यन्त्यukthemā हरī वहतस ता नो अच्छ ||
brahmāṇi me matayaḥ śaṁ sutāsaḥ śuṣma iyarti prabhṛto me adriḥ | ā śāsate prati haryanty ukthemā harī vahatas tā no accha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May my thoughts be directed towards the divine, may my intellect be sharp and clear, may the mountains of my aspirations be strong. Let the forces of nature guide me, and may the divine protect us from all obstacles."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Brahmāṇi: Refers to the divine or the ultimate reality.
    • Me: My, indicating a personal connection.
    • Matayaḥ: Thoughts or intellect, emphasizing the importance of mental clarity.
    • Śaṁ: Peace or auspiciousness, suggesting a desire for harmony.
    • Sutāsaḥ: Sharpness or clarity, indicating the need for a keen intellect.
    • Śuṣma: Subtle or refined, referring to the quality of thoughts.
    • Iyarati: To direct or guide, emphasizing the need for guidance.
    • Prabhṛto: Strong or powerful, indicating the strength of aspirations.
    • Me: Again, my, reinforcing personal ownership.
    • Adriḥ: Mountains, symbolizing strength and stability.
    • Ā: A call for action or invocation.
    • Śāsate: To command or govern, indicating authority.
    • Prati: Towards, suggesting direction.
    • Haryanty: To remove or eliminate, indicating the removal of obstacles.
    • Ukthemā: Those who are spoken of, referring to divine beings.
    • Harī: Divine or auspicious, often associated with the divine presence.
    • Vahatas: Carriers or bearers, indicating support.
    • Tā: That, referring to the divine forces.
    • No: Us, indicating a collective aspect.
    • Accha: To protect or safeguard, emphasizing the need for protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, one might recite this mantra to seek clarity and direction in their thoughts, ensuring that their decisions are guided by wisdom.
    • Students preparing for exams can use this mantra to focus their minds and aspirations, invoking the strength of their intellect to overcome academic challenges.
    • During times of personal crisis, individuals may find solace in this mantra, asking for divine guidance to navigate through obstacles and emerge stronger.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mental clarity, divine guidance, and strength in the face of challenges. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as academic pursuits, professional challenges, and personal crises. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can align their thoughts with a higher purpose, seek protection from obstacles, and cultivate a sense of peace and direction in their lives.
