स बन्धना उद इयर्ति प्रजावतीर विश्वायुर विश्वः सुभरा अहर्दिवि |ब्रह्म प्रजावद रयिम अश्वपस्त्यम पीत इन्दव इन्द्रं अस्मभ्यं याचतात् ||
sa bhandanā ud iyarti prajāvatīr viśvāyur viśvāḥ subharā ahardivi | brahma prajāvad rayim aśvapastyam pīta indav indram asmabhyaṁ yācatāt ||
  • sa bhandanā ud iyarti: This phrase can be translated as "He who frees from bondage." It signifies the idea of liberation and the removal of obstacles that bind us. In real life, this can be related to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or breaking free from toxic relationships.
  • prajāvatīr viśvāyur viśvāḥ: This translates to "the one who brings forth all beings and life." It emphasizes the importance of creation and nurturing life. For example, a parent nurturing their child or a teacher guiding students can be seen as embodying this principle.
  • subharā ahardivi: This means "the one who is auspicious and brings good fortune every day." It highlights the significance of positivity and good deeds in our daily lives. For instance, engaging in acts of kindness or community service can create a ripple effect of positivity.
  • brahma prajāvad rayim: This phrase can be interpreted as "the ultimate reality that brings prosperity." It suggests that understanding and aligning with the fundamental truths of existence can lead to abundance. In practical terms, this could relate to ethical business practices that lead to sustainable success.
  • aśvapastyam pīta indav indram: This translates to "the one who is like a powerful horse, the lord of the heavens." It symbolizes strength and leadership. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire and motivate others, much like a strong horse leading a charge.
  • asmabhyaṁ yācatāt: This means "may he grant us our desires." It reflects the human aspiration for fulfillment and the pursuit of goals. In everyday life, this can be related to setting personal or professional goals and seeking the support of others to achieve them.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of liberation, creation, positivity, prosperity, strength, and the pursuit of desires. Its significance lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek freedom from limitations, nurture life, spread positivity, align with truth for prosperity, embody strength in leadership, and pursue their aspirations. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal development, community engagement, and ethical practices, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
