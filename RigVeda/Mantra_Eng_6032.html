यो वेदिष्टो व्यथिष्व अश्वावन्तं जरतृभ्यः |वाजं स्तोत्रिभ्यो गोमन्तम् ||
yo vediṣṭho avyathiṣv aśvāvantaṁ jaritṛbhyaḥ | vājaṁ stotṛbhyo gomantam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vediṣṭho: is the best knower
    • avyathiṣv: is not disturbed
    • aśvāvantaṁ: possessing horses (symbolizing strength and wealth)
    • jaritṛbhyaḥ: to the singers or poets
    • vājaṁ: the prize or reward
    • stotṛbhyo: to those who praise
    • gomantam: possessing cows (symbolizing prosperity)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine figure who is the ultimate knower, unshaken by disturbances.
    • The mention of "aśvāvantaṁ" and "gomantam" signifies the importance of strength and wealth in ancient society, where horses and cows were symbols of prosperity.
    • The mantra emphasizes the value of knowledge and stability, suggesting that true wealth comes from understanding and wisdom.
    • It also highlights the role of poets and singers, indicating that those who praise and celebrate the divine are integral to society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, individuals who possess knowledge and remain calm in the face of challenges are often seen as leaders. For instance, a successful entrepreneur who navigates market fluctuations with a steady hand embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • The importance of community and cultural expression can be seen in events like poetry slams or music festivals, where artists celebrate life and wisdom, much like the "stotṛbhyo" in the mantra.
    • In agriculture, the possession of livestock and the ability to manage resources wisely reflect the values of prosperity and stability, akin to the "gomantam" and "aśvāvantaṁ" mentioned.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of knowledge, stability, and the celebration of life through art and culture.
    • It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also intellectual and emotional, urging us to seek wisdom and remain undisturbed by life's challenges.
    • In our daily lives, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by valuing knowledge, supporting our communities, and recognizing the importance of cultural expressions that enrich our lives.
