नवं नु स्तोमम् अग्नये दिवः श्येनाय जिजनम् |वस्वः कुविद वनाति नः ||
navaṁ nu stomam agnaye divaḥ śyenāya jījanam | vasvaḥ kuvid vanāti naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "navaṁ nu stomam agnaye divaḥ śyenāya jījanam | vasvaḥ kuvid vanāti naḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • navaṁ: new, fresh
    • nu: indeed, certainly
    • stomam: hymn, praise
    • agnaye: to Agni, the fire god
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • śyenāya: to the eagle, symbolizing strength and speed
    • jījanam: born, created
    • vasvaḥ: all, everything
    • kuvid: in the forest, wilderness
    • vanāti: brings, carries
    • naḥ: to us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of fire in both a literal and metaphorical sense.
    • The phrase "navaṁ nu stomam" suggests a new hymn or praise, indicating a fresh approach to worship or gratitude.
    • By addressing Agni, the mantra acknowledges the divine presence in the natural elements, particularly fire, which is essential for life, warmth, and purification.
    • The reference to "śyenāya" symbolizes the qualities of an eagle, representing strength, vision, and the ability to rise above challenges.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "vasvaḥ kuvid vanāti naḥ," can be interpreted as a request for all that is good and beneficial to come to us from the wilderness, suggesting a connection to nature and its resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the invocation of Agni can be seen in rituals where fire is used for purification, such as during weddings or religious ceremonies.
    • The qualities of the eagle can inspire individuals to strive for excellence and to overcome obstacles, much like how athletes train to achieve their goals.
    • The idea of receiving blessings from nature can be reflected in sustainable practices, where individuals seek to live in harmony with the environment, such as organic farming or conservation efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a purifying and transformative force in our lives.
    • It encourages a fresh perspective on worship and gratitude, reminding us to appreciate the natural world and its resources.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength and vision, much like the eagle, to navigate life's challenges and to seek harmony with nature.
