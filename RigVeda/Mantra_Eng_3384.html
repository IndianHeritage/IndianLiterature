उत त्या तुर्वशायदू अस्नातारा शचीपतिḥ |इन्द्रो विद्वांँ अपारयत ||
uta tyā turvaśāyadū asnātārā śacīpatiḥ | indro vidvām̐ apārayat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tyā: by that
    • turvaśāyadū: the one who is powerful
    • asnātārā: who has taken the form of a protector
    • śacīpatiḥ: the lord of strength and power
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • vidvām̐: the wise one
    • apārayat: has bestowed or granted
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful and protective nature of Indra, who is revered as the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • It emphasizes the idea that through wisdom and strength, one can receive blessings and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as facing a difficult exam or a job interview, individuals often seek the strength and wisdom to overcome their fears. This mantra can serve as a reminder to tap into one's inner power and knowledge.
    • In times of crisis, communities often come together, invoking the spirit of protection and strength, much like how Indra is called upon for support in the Vedic texts.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and wisdom in the face of challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal struggles, community challenges, and even in leadership roles where guidance and protection are essential.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find the courage to face adversities and the wisdom to make sound decisions.
