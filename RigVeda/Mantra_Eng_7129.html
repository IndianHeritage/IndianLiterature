आद्याद्याऽ श्वः-श्व इन्द्र त्रास्व परे च नः |विश्वा च नो जरीतृन् सत्पते अहा दिवा नक्तं च रक्षिषः ||
adyādyā śvaḥ-śva indra trāsva pare ca naḥ | viśvā ca no jaritṝn satpate ahā divā naktaṁ ca rakṣiṣaḥ ||
  • adyādyā: This word translates to "today" or "this day." It signifies the present moment, emphasizing the importance of the current time in our lives.
  • śvaḥ-śva: This means "tomorrow" or "the next day." It reflects the anticipation of the future and the hope that comes with it.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, power, and protection. He represents the force that can overcome obstacles and challenges.
  • trāsva: This word means "to protect" or "to save." It indicates a plea for safety and security from the divine.
  • pare ca naḥ: This phrase translates to "and from the enemies." It highlights the need for protection not just from physical dangers but also from adversities in life.
  • viśvā: This means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ca no jaritṝn: This translates to "and from those who harm us." It is a call for safeguarding against those who may wish to do harm.
  • satpate: This word means "the lord of truth" or "the one who is righteous." It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in our lives.
  • ahā: This means "day" or "light." It symbolizes clarity, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • divā naktaṁ: This translates to "by day and by night." It signifies the continuous nature of protection and guidance that one seeks.
  • ca rakṣiṣaḥ: This means "and protect us." It is a direct appeal for divine protection and guidance in all aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection and guidance from the divine, emphasizing the importance of being safeguarded in both the present and the future. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges, uncertainties, or adversities. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra during times of stress, such as before an important exam, a job interview, or any situation that requires courage and strength. It serves as a reminder that one is not alone in their struggles and that divine support is always available. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to instill hope, encourage resilience, and foster a sense of security in the face of life's uncertainties.
