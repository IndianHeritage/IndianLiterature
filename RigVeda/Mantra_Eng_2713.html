इमं कामं मन्दयाऽगोभिर अश्वैश चन्द्रवतā राधसā पप्रथश च |स्वर्यवो मतििभिस तुभ्यं विप्रā इन्द्राय वाहः कुशिकासो अक्रन् ||
imaṁ kāmam mandayā gobhir aśvaiś candravatā rādhasā paprathaś ca | svaryavo matibhis tubhyaṁ viprā indrāya vāhaḥ kuśikāso akran ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • kāmam: desire or wish
    • mandayā: with a gentle or pleasing manner
    • gobhir: with cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • aśvaiś: with horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • candravatā: like the moon (symbolizing beauty and calmness)
    • rādhasā: with joy or happiness
    • paprathaś: may it be fulfilled
    • ca: and
    • svaryavo: with heavenly or divine qualities
    • matibhis: with thoughts or intentions
    • tubhyaṁ: for you
    • viprā: the wise or learned ones
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • vāhaḥ: the vehicle or means
    • kuśikāso: the descendants of Kuśika (referring to a lineage)
    • akran: may they be fulfilled or achieved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for the fulfillment of desires in a gentle and pleasing manner.
    • The mention of cows and horses symbolizes the importance of wealth and strength in achieving one's goals.
    • By likening the desired outcomes to the moon, it emphasizes the beauty and tranquility that comes with fulfillment.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a request for divine support and guidance in the pursuit of these desires.
    • The reference to the wise or learned ones suggests that knowledge and wisdom are essential in achieving one's aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success in a new venture. One might approach the endeavor with a calm and strategic mindset, ensuring that the resources (wealth and strength) are utilized effectively.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can serve as a reminder to pursue desires with kindness and joy, fostering a nurturing environment for love and connection.
    • In educational pursuits, invoking the wisdom of learned individuals can guide students in their quest for knowledge, helping them achieve their academic goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of approaching desires with a balanced mindset, utilizing available resources wisely, and seeking divine guidance.
    • Its application in various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or spiritual—demonstrates the timeless relevance of these teachings.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to align their intentions with higher wisdom, ensuring that their pursuits lead to fulfillment and joy.
