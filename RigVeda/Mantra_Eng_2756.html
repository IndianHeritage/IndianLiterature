इन्द्रेषिते प्रसवम् भिक्षमाणे अच्छा समुद्रं रथ्येव याथः |समाराणे ऊर्मिभिः पिन्वमाणे अन्यां वाम अन्याम अप्य एति शुभ्रे ||
indreṣite prasavam bhikṣamāṇe acchā samudraṁ rathyeva yāthaḥ | samārāṇe ūrmibhiḥ pinvamāne anyā vām anyām apy eti śubhre ||
  • Indreṣite: This term refers to the divine or the supreme power, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. It signifies the guidance and influence of a higher force in our lives.
  • Prasavam: This word means 'birth' or 'creation.' It symbolizes the beginning of new ventures, ideas, or phases in life.
  • Bhikṣamāṇe: This translates to 'seeking alms' or 'requesting.' It reflects the act of seeking support or help from the universe or others around us.
  • Acchā: Meaning 'good' or 'auspicious,' this word emphasizes the positive outcomes that can arise from our actions and intentions.
  • Samudraṁ: This means 'ocean.' The ocean is often a metaphor for vastness, depth, and the unknown, representing the limitless possibilities in life.
  • Rathyeva: This term means 'like a chariot.' A chariot symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • Yāthaḥ: This word can be interpreted as 'as it goes' or 'in the manner of.' It suggests a sense of direction and purpose in our endeavors.
  • Samārāṇe: This means 'in the battle' or 'in the struggle.' It signifies the challenges we face in life and the need for resilience.
  • Ūrmibhiḥ: This translates to 'with waves.' Waves can represent the ups and downs of life, the ebb and flow of experiences.
  • Pinvamāne: This means 'being filled' or 'being enriched.' It indicates the process of gaining wisdom and experience through life's challenges.
  • Anyā vām anyām: This phrase means 'one another' or 'each other.' It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community.
  • Apī eti: This translates to 'also goes' or 'also reaches.' It signifies the idea that our actions and intentions have far-reaching effects.
  • Śubhre: Meaning 'auspicious' or 'pure,' this word emphasizes the positive outcomes that arise from virtuous actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of resilience in the face of challenges and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this mantra can be applied when embarking on new projects, facing difficulties, or seeking harmony within communities. By recognizing the divine influence and the potential for positive outcomes, individuals can navigate their journeys with purpose and optimism, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life.
