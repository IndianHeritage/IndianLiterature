पवते हर्यतो हरिर अति हवारांसी रंह्या |अभ्यर्षन् स्तोत्रिभ्यो वीरवत यशः ||
pavate haryato harir ati hvarāṁsi raṁhyā | abhyarṣan stotṛbhyo vīravad yaśaḥ ||
  • pavate: This word translates to "he who purifies" or "the one who cleanses." In a real-life context, this can refer to the role of nature in purifying the environment, such as how trees and plants filter air and water.
  • haryato: This means "of the horses" or "swift." Horses are often symbols of strength and speed. In modern life, this can be likened to the importance of agility and efficiency in our daily tasks, whether in business or personal endeavors.
  • harir: This term refers to "the Lord" or "the divine." It signifies a higher power or consciousness that guides and protects us. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance through spirituality or religion during challenging times.
  • ati: This means "exceedingly" or "very much." It emphasizes the intensity of the following qualities or actions. In our lives, this can relate to the passion and dedication we put into our work or relationships.
  • hvarāṁsi: This word can be interpreted as "the offerings" or "sacrifices." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in our communities. For example, charitable acts or community service can be seen as modern-day offerings to society.
  • raṁhyā: This translates to "delight" or "joy." It signifies the happiness that comes from fulfilling one's duties and responsibilities. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the joy we feel when helping others or achieving our goals.
  • abhyarṣan: This means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It suggests the act of seeking help or blessings from a higher power. In real life, this can be seen in prayer or meditation practices where individuals seek guidance and support.
  • stotṛbhyo: This translates to "to the worshippers" or "to those who praise." It acknowledges the importance of community and collective worship. In modern society, this can be reflected in group activities, such as team sports or community gatherings, where people come together to support one another.
  • vīravad: This means "like a hero" or "bravely." It emphasizes courage and valor in facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or take risks to achieve their dreams.
  • yaśaḥ: This translates to "fame" or "glory." It signifies the recognition and respect one earns through their actions. In contemporary life, this can be related to the pursuit of success and the impact one has on others through their achievements.

Conclusion: The mantra "pavate haryato harir ati hvarāṁsi raṁhyā | abhyarṣan stotṛbhyo vīravad yaśaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of purification, strength, divine guidance, joy, and the importance of community and courage. Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, whether they are offerings to society or personal endeavors, should be rooted in purity of intention and a desire to uplift others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek balance in their lives, to act with integrity, and to strive for excellence while remaining connected to their community and higher purpose. It serves as a guiding principle for living a meaningful and impactful life.
