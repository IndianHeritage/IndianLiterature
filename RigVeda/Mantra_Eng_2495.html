आ याह्य अग्ने समिधानो अर्वाङ इन्द्रेण देवैः सरथं तुरेबिः |बरहिर न आस्ताम अदितिः सुपुत्रा स्वाहा देवाः अमृताः मादयन्ताम् ||
ā yāhy agne samidhāno arvāṅ indreṇa devaiḥ sarathaṁ turebhiḥ | barhir na āstām aditiḥ suputrā svāhā devā amṛtā mādayantām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāhy: O Agni, may you come forth.
    • agne: O Fire (Agni), the divine energy.
    • samidhāno: who is kindled or ignited.
    • arvāṅ: with strength or vigor.
    • indreṇa: by Indra, the king of gods.
    • devaiḥ: with the divine beings.
    • sarathaṁ: with chariots or vehicles.
    • turebhiḥ: with swift ones.
    • barhir: the sacred grass or the altar.
    • na āstām: may it not be absent.
    • aditiḥ: Aditi, the infinite or boundless.
    • suputrā: with good sons or offspring.
    • svāhā: an invocation or offering.
    • devā: the gods.
    • amṛtā: immortal or eternal.
    • mādayantām: may they be pleased or delighted.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
    • The mention of Indra and the divine beings highlights the interconnectedness of various deities in Vedic traditions, emphasizing the need for divine support in human endeavors.
    • The phrase "may it not be absent" reflects a desire for the continuous presence of divine blessings and prosperity in life.
    • Aditi represents the concept of infinity and the nurturing aspect of the universe, suggesting that the blessings sought are boundless.
    • The reference to "good sons" indicates the importance of family and lineage in Vedic culture, where offspring are seen as a continuation of one's legacy.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, invoking the gods to be pleased, signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine forces in achieving success and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the offering of prayers to the divine.
    • In family gatherings, invoking blessings for "good sons" can be seen in the context of wishing for the well-being and success of children, reflecting the values of continuity and legacy.
    • In times of challenge, individuals may seek divine support through prayers, echoing the sentiment of relying on higher powers for strength and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, prosperity, and continuity in life.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support, reminding us to remain grateful and aware of the forces that shape our existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to invoke positivity, seek guidance, and appreciate the blessings we receive, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine.
