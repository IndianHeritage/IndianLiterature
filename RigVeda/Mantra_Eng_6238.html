याभिः कण्वं मेधातिथिं याभिर वशं दशव्रजम् |याभिर गोशर्यम आवतं ताभिर नो 'वतं नराः ||
yābhiḥ kaṇvam medhātithiṁ yābhir vaśaṁ daśavrajam | yābhir gośaryam āvataṁ tābhir no 'vataṁ narā ||
  • yābhiḥ: This word can be translated as "by whom" or "through which." It indicates a means or method.
  • kaṇvam: Refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It signifies those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • medhātithiṁ: This term can be broken down into "medha," meaning intelligence or wisdom, and "tithi," which can refer to a guest or a visitor. Together, it suggests the idea of welcoming wisdom or intelligence.
  • yābhir: Similar to the first instance, it means "by whom" or "through which," reinforcing the idea of means or methods.
  • vaśaṁ: This word means "control" or "influence." It implies the ability to guide or direct.
  • daśavrajam: This term refers to "the tenfold herd" or "the group of ten." It can symbolize a collective or community.
  • yābhir: Again, it emphasizes the means or methods by which something is achieved.
  • gośaryam: This word translates to "the protector of cattle" or "the guardian of wealth." Cattle in ancient times were a symbol of wealth and prosperity.
  • āvataṁ: This means "to protect" or "to shelter." It indicates the act of providing safety or security.
  • tābhir: This word means "by those" or "through them," referring to the means of protection or guidance.
  • no: This translates to "for us," indicating a personal or collective plea.
  • 'vataṁ: This means "to protect" or "to shelter," similar to "āvataṁ." It emphasizes the need for safety.
  • narā: This word means "men" or "humans," indicating the focus on human beings and their needs.

The mantra essentially speaks to the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding and protecting a community. It emphasizes the role of learned individuals in influencing and controlling the collective, ensuring the safety and prosperity of all.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as wise individuals are called upon to guide a community, effective leaders in modern society must possess knowledge and wisdom to make informed decisions that benefit the collective.
  • Education: The mantra highlights the importance of education and learning. In today's world, fostering an environment where knowledge is valued can lead to a more prosperous society.
  • Community Support: The idea of protecting and sheltering the community resonates with modern initiatives aimed at supporting vulnerable populations, ensuring that everyone has access to resources and safety.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding and protecting communities. It underscores the need for learned individuals to take on roles of influence and control, ensuring the safety and prosperity of all members of society. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can work towards creating a more harmonious and supportive environment for everyone.
