प्रति वां सूर उदिते मित्रं गृणीषे वरुणम् |आर्यमणं ऋशादसम ||
prati vāṁ sūra udite mitraṁ gṛṇīṣe varuṇam | aryamaṇaṁ riśādasam ||
  • Mantra: "prati vāṁ sūra udite mitraṁ gṛṇīṣe varuṇam | aryamaṇaṁ riśādasam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prati: towards or in relation to
    • vāṁ: you (addressing a divine entity)
    • sūra: brave or heroic
    • udite: arising or emerging
    • mitraṁ: friend or ally
    • gṛṇīṣe: I sing or I praise
    • varuṇam: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water
    • aryamaṇaṁ: Aryaman, a deity associated with social order and harmony
    • riśādasam: the one who brings forth or manifests
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer that acknowledges the emergence of bravery and heroism.
    • It expresses a desire to praise and seek the blessings of Varuna, who represents the cosmic order and the moral law.
    • It also invokes Aryaman, emphasizing the importance of social harmony and relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a team project at work, one might invoke the qualities of bravery and teamwork, akin to the essence of this mantra.
    • When facing moral dilemmas, individuals can reflect on the principles of cosmic order and justice represented by Varuna, guiding their decisions.
    • In community gatherings, invoking Aryaman can remind participants of the importance of social bonds and collective harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of courage, friendship, and moral integrity in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our relationships and to act bravely in the face of challenges.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more just and harmonious society, reflecting the teachings of the Rig Veda in contemporary life.
