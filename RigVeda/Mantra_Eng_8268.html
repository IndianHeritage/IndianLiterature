यस्य ते द्युम्नवत् पयः पवमानाभृतं दिवः |तेन नो मृळ जīvसे ||
yasya te dyumnavat payaḥ pavamānābhṛtaṁ divaḥ | tena no mṛḻa jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • dyumnavat: glorious, radiant
    • payaḥ: milk, nourishment
    • pavamānābhṛtaṁ: flowing, moving (like a river)
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • tena: by that
    • no: us
    • mṛḻa: grant, bestow
    • jīvase: life, vitality
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine source of nourishment and vitality.
    • The "glorious milk" symbolizes the essence of life and sustenance that comes from the heavens.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in our lives, suggesting that true nourishment comes from a higher power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the way we seek nourishment in our daily lives, not just physically but also spiritually.
    • For instance, when we engage in meditation or prayer, we are seeking that divine connection which nourishes our soul.
    • In a community context, acts of kindness and support can be seen as a way of sharing this "milk" of life, fostering a sense of unity and vitality among individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of seeking divine nourishment.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the sources of vitality in their lives, whether through nature, community, or spiritual practices.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to cultivate gratitude for the blessings we receive and to share our own resources with others, thereby enhancing the collective well-being.
