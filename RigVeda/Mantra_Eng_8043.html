प्रसवे त उद īरते तिस्रो वाचो मखस्युवः |यद अव्य एषि सानविः ||
prasave ta ud īrate tisro vāco makhasyuvaḥ | yad avya eṣi sānavi ||
  • Prasave: This word translates to "in the act of giving birth" or "to produce." It signifies the creative force and the beginning of life.
  • Ta: This is a pronoun meaning "that" or "those," referring to the divine or the forces at play.
  • Ud īrate: This phrase can be interpreted as "they rise up" or "they are elevated," indicating a sense of growth, elevation, or ascension.
  • Tisro: Meaning "three," this number often symbolizes completeness or a triad of forces in various cultures.
  • Vāco: This translates to "words" or "speech," emphasizing the power of communication and expression.
  • Makhasyuvaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are engaged in sacrifice" or "those who perform rituals," highlighting the importance of spiritual practices.
  • Yad: This means "that which" or "whatever," introducing a clause that elaborates on the preceding ideas.
  • Avya: This word translates to "unseen" or "invisible," suggesting the presence of forces or energies that are not immediately apparent.
  • Eṣi: Meaning "you go" or "you move," it implies action or progression.
  • Sānavi: This term can be interpreted as "with the essence" or "with the spirit," indicating a connection to the deeper aspects of existence.

The mantra can be understood as a celebration of the creative forces of life, the power of words, and the significance of spiritual practices. It emphasizes the importance of communication and the unseen energies that guide our actions and intentions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Creative Endeavors: When embarking on a new project, whether it be artistic, professional, or personal, invoking the essence of "prasave" can inspire creativity and innovation.
  • Communication: In situations where effective communication is crucial, such as negotiations or public speaking, the emphasis on "vāco" reminds us of the power of our words and the impact they can have.
  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in rituals or meditative practices can help connect with the "makhasyuvaḥ," allowing individuals to tap into the unseen energies that support their journey.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the power of expression, and the importance of spiritual engagement. It encourages individuals to recognize and harness the creative forces within themselves and the world around them, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
