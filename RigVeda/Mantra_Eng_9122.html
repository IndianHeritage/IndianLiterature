तद इति सदस्थम् अभि चारु दीधय गावो यच छासन वहतुः न धेनवः |माता यन् मन्तुर यूथस्य पूर्व्याभि वाणस्य सप्तधातुर इज जनः ||
tad it sadhastham abhi cāru dīdhaya gāvo yac chāsan vahatuṁ na dhenavaḥ | mātā yan mantur yūthasya pūrvyābhi vāṇasya saptadhātur ij janaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • it: it
    • sadhastham: in the assembly
    • abhi: towards
    • cāru: beautiful
    • dīdhaya: shining
    • gāvo: cows
    • yac: which
    • chāsan: may be offered
    • vahatuṁ: to be carried
    • na: not
    • dhenavaḥ: the milch cows
    • mātā: mother
    • yan: that
    • mantur: the mantra
    • yūthasya: of the group
    • pūrvyābhi: from the past
    • vāṇasya: of speech
    • saptadhātur: seven elements
    • ij: may be invoked
    • janaḥ: people
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the beauty and significance of unity and collective strength.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes wealth and nourishment, indicating that prosperity comes from harmonious living.
    • The term "mātā" (mother) emphasizes the nurturing aspect of community and the importance of caring for one another.
    • The "mantra" signifies the power of words and intentions in shaping reality, suggesting that collective prayers and thoughts can invoke positive outcomes.
    • The "saptadhātur" refers to the seven elements, which can be interpreted as the fundamental aspects of life that need to be balanced for harmony.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, teamwork and collaboration can lead to greater success, much like the collective strength mentioned in the mantra.
    • In families, nurturing relationships and supporting one another can create a harmonious environment, reflecting the essence of "mātā."
    • Community gatherings, where people come together to share resources and support each other, embody the spirit of unity and collective prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, collective strength, and the nurturing aspect of community.
    • It teaches us that by coming together, we can invoke positive energies and create a prosperous environment for all.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, successful teamwork, and a supportive community.
