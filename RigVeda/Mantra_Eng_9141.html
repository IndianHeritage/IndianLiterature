यद आदिध्ये न दविषाण्य एभिः परायद्भ्योऽव हीये सकिभ्यः |न्युप्ताश च बभ्रवो वाचम् अक्रतम् इमीद एषां निष्कृतं जारिणीव ||
yad ādīdhye na daviṣāṇy ebhiḥ parāyadbhyo 'va hīye sakhibhyaḥ | nyuptāś ca babhravo vācam akratam̐ emīd eṣāṁ niṣkṛtaṁ jāriṇīva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • ādīdhye: I meditate upon
    • na: not
    • daviṣāṇy: the divine beings
    • ebhiḥ: with these
    • parāyadbhyo: from the higher realms
    • 'va: or
    • hīye: I seek
    • sakhibhyaḥ: from friends
    • nyuptāś: those who are established
    • ca: and
    • babhravo: who have spoken
    • vācam: words
    • akratam̐: without deceit
    • emīd: may this
    • eṣāṁ: of these
    • niṣkṛtaṁ: be liberated
    • jāriṇīva: like a flame
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep yearning for truth and clarity.
    • The speaker meditates on the divine and seeks guidance from both higher realms and friends.
    • It emphasizes the importance of honest communication and the liberation that comes from truthful expression.
    • Just as a flame illuminates darkness, the mantra suggests that truth can dispel ignorance and confusion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, open and honest communication among team members can lead to better collaboration and innovation.
    • In personal relationships, expressing feelings truthfully can strengthen bonds and resolve conflicts.
    • In education, encouraging students to seek knowledge and ask questions fosters a deeper understanding of subjects.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking truth and clarity in all aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and educational settings.
    • By embracing honesty and seeking guidance, individuals can achieve liberation from confusion and ignorance, leading to a more fulfilling life.
