दातामे पृषतीनां राजा हिरण्यवीनाम् |मा देवाः मघवाः ऋषत ||
dātā me pṛṣatīnāṁ rājā hiraṇyavīnām | mā devā maghavā riṣat ||
  • Mantra: "dātā me pṛṣatīnāṁ rājā hiraṇyavīnām | mā devā maghavā riṣat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dātā: giver, donor
    • me: to me
    • pṛṣatīnāṁ: of the strong, powerful
    • rājā: king, ruler
    • hiraṇyavīnām: of those who possess gold, wealth
    • mā: not
    • devā: gods, divine beings
    • maghavā: the powerful, the mighty
    • riṣat: be angry, be displeased
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or invocation to the divine for the bestowal of wealth and strength.
    • The speaker acknowledges the power of the king or ruler who is generous and possesses wealth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not inciting anger in the divine beings, suggesting a desire for harmony and favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be likened to seeking blessings for prosperity and success from higher powers or mentors.
    • In personal life, it reflects the importance of being grateful for the support and resources provided by influential figures, such as parents or leaders.
    • In community settings, it can be seen as a call for unity and cooperation, ensuring that the powerful do not misuse their strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of generosity, strength, and the need for divine favor in achieving success.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and honor those who provide support and resources, while also maintaining a harmonious relationship with the divine.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek blessings for their endeavors while being mindful of their actions and the impact on others.
