त्वं हि वृत्रहन् एषां पाताऽसोमानाम् असि |उप नो हरिभिः सुतम् ||
tvaṁ hi vṛtrahann eṣām pātā somānām asi | upa no haribhiḥ sutam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hi: indeed
    • vṛtrahann: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • eṣām: of these
    • pātā: protector or guardian
    • somānām: of the soma (sacred drink)
    • asi: are
    • upa: near or close
    • no: to us
    • haribhiḥ: with the help of the divine beings
    • sutam: the drink or essence
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses a divine entity, acknowledging its role as the slayer of the demon Vritra, who represents obstacles and chaos.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of the divine in protecting and nurturing the essence of life, symbolized by soma, which is often associated with immortality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • It calls upon the divine to be close to us, suggesting a desire for guidance and support in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, we often face challenges that can feel overwhelming, much like the chaos represented by Vritra. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine support and guidance in difficult times.
    • For instance, during a personal crisis, one might recite this mantra to invoke a sense of protection and clarity, trusting that they are not alone in their struggles.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during rituals or gatherings to foster a sense of unity and collective strength against common challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine protection and support in overcoming life's obstacles.
    • It teaches us the importance of seeking help from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or community support.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of comfort and strength, reminding us that we are part of a larger cosmic order that aids us in our journey.
