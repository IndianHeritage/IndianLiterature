यजाऽनो मित्रावरुणा यजाऽ देवां ऋतम् बृहत् |अग्नि यक्षि स्वं दामम् ||
yajā no mitrāvaruṇā yajā devām̐ ṛtam bṛhat | agne yakṣi svaṁ damam ||
  • yajā no mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase can be translated as "May we invoke the divine Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. In real life, this can be seen as a call for unity and cooperation among people, emphasizing the importance of relationships and social bonds.
  • yajā devām̐: This means "May we invoke the gods." This invocation signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our endeavors. In everyday life, this can be related to seeking inspiration and support from higher ideals or values, whether through spirituality, community, or personal reflection.
  • ṛtam bṛhat: Translated as "the great truth" or "the vast order," this phrase emphasizes the significance of adhering to universal truths and moral principles. In practical terms, this can be applied to ethical decision-making in our lives, where we strive to act in accordance with honesty, integrity, and justice.
  • agne yakṣi svaṁ damam: This translates to "O Agni, may you bestow your own control." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification. This line can be interpreted as a request for the ability to control one's desires and actions. In real life, this can relate to self-discipline, personal growth, and the pursuit of goals with focus and determination.

Conclusion: The mantra "yajā no mitrāvaruṇā yajā devām̐ ṛtam bṛhat | agne yakṣi svaṁ damam ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support, fostering unity, adhering to truth, and seeking self-control. Its significance lies in its applicability to our daily lives, encouraging us to build harmonious relationships, seek higher guidance, uphold ethical values, and cultivate self-discipline. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with a sense of purpose and integrity.
