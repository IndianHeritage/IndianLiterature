त्वं एतानि पप्रिषे वि नामेशान इन्द्र दधिषे गभस्तौ |अनु त्वा देवाः शवसां मदन्त्य उपरिबुध्नान वनिनश चकर्थ ||
tvam etāni papriṣe vi nāmeśāna indra dadhiṣe gabhastau | anu tvā devāḥ śavasā madanty uparibudhnān vaninaś cakartha ||
  • tvam etāni papriṣe: This phrase translates to "You have asked these questions." It signifies the importance of inquiry and seeking knowledge. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and understanding, where asking questions is fundamental to learning.
  • vi nāmeśāna indra: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods, often associated with strength and power. "Nāmeśāna" can be interpreted as "the one who is not known by name." This suggests that true power and divinity may not always be recognized or understood. In modern contexts, this can relate to the idea that true leaders or influential figures may operate behind the scenes, shaping events without seeking recognition.
  • dadhṣe gabhastau: This means "you have established the light." Light symbolizes knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of truth and wisdom, where individuals strive to illuminate their paths and those of others through education and awareness.
  • anu tvā devāḥ: This translates to "the gods follow you." It implies that divine forces support those who seek knowledge and truth. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as the idea that when individuals act with integrity and purpose, they often find support from their community or even from unseen forces, leading to success in their endeavors.
  • śavasā madanty: This phrase can be understood as "with great strength." It emphasizes the power that comes from knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be related to the strength one gains from education, experience, and wisdom, enabling individuals to face challenges effectively.
  • uparibudhnān: This means "to awaken." It signifies the process of gaining awareness and enlightenment. In contemporary life, this can be linked to personal growth and self-discovery, where individuals strive to awaken their potential and understand their purpose.
  • vaninaś cakartha: This translates to "you have created the forest." The forest symbolizes a rich environment filled with life and diversity. In a real-world context, this can represent the creation of a community or a supportive environment where ideas and individuals can thrive.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of inquiry, knowledge, and the support of divine forces in the pursuit of truth and enlightenment. It teaches that through asking questions and seeking understanding, individuals can establish clarity and strength in their lives. This is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, leadership, and personal development, where the quest for knowledge leads to empowerment and the creation of supportive communities. Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embrace their journey of learning and growth, knowing that they are supported by greater forces in their endeavors.
