अत्रेर इव श्रिणुतं पूर्व्यस्तुतिं श्यावाश्वस्य सुन्वतो मदाच्युताः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चाश्विनां तिरोऽह्न्यम् ||
atrer iva śṛṇutam pūrvyastutiṁ śyāvāśvasya sunvato madacyutā | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cāśvinā tiroahnyam ||
  • atrer iva: This phrase can be translated as "like the one who is the source of light." It signifies the importance of enlightenment and knowledge in our lives.
  • śṛṇutam: This means "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the value of listening to wisdom and teachings from the past.
  • pūrvyastutiṁ: This translates to "the praise of the ancients." It highlights the significance of honoring and acknowledging the contributions of those who came before us.
  • śyāvāśvasya: This refers to "the dark horse," symbolizing strength and resilience. In real life, it can represent overcoming challenges and emerging victorious.
  • sunvato: This means "to those who are generous." It encourages us to be open-hearted and giving in our interactions with others.
  • madacyutā: This translates to "the one who is intoxicated with joy." It signifies the importance of joy and happiness in our lives.
  • sajoṣasā: This means "with harmony." It emphasizes the need for unity and cooperation among individuals and communities.
  • uṣasā: This refers to "the dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities. It reminds us that every day brings a chance for renewal.
  • sūryeṇa: This translates to "with the sun." The sun is often associated with vitality and energy, representing the life force that sustains us.
  • cāśvinā: This refers to "the twin deities," symbolizing balance and duality in life. It reminds us of the importance of maintaining equilibrium in our actions and thoughts.
  • tiroahnyam: This means "to dispel darkness." It signifies the need to overcome ignorance and negativity in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, honoring the past, embracing joy, and fostering harmony in our lives. It serves as a reminder to listen to wisdom, be generous, and strive for balance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, community building, and self-improvement. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more enlightened and harmonious society.
