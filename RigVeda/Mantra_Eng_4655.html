त्वं श्रद्धाभिर मन्दसानः सोमैर दभीतये चुमुरिम इन्द्र सिष्वप् |त्वं रजिम पीठीनसे दशस्यन षष्टिं सहस्रā शच्यā सचाहन ||
tvaṁ śraddhābhir mandasānaḥ somair dabhītaye cumurim indra siṣvap | tvaṁ rajim piṭhīnase daśasyan ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrā śacyā sacāhan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • śraddhābhir: With faith
    • mandasānaḥ: Sitting gently or calmly
    • somair: With the moon or soma (nectar)
    • dabhītaye: For the purpose of protection
    • cumurim: The enemy or adversary
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • siṣvap: You have awakened
    • rajim: The power or strength
    • piṭhīnase: To the one who is seated
    • daśasyan: Of the ten directions
    • ṣaṣṭiṁ: Sixty
    • sahasrā: Thousands
    • śacyā: With strength or power
    • sacāhan: And with support
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and protection.
    • The phrase "with faith" emphasizes the importance of belief and trust in divine powers for overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to "soma" symbolizes nourishment and vitality, suggesting that spiritual sustenance is essential for strength.
    • The mantra speaks of protection against adversaries, indicating a plea for divine intervention in times of conflict.
    • The mention of "sixty" and "thousands" signifies abundance and the vastness of divine support available to the devotee.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as facing a difficult decision or conflict, individuals often turn to their faith for guidance and strength, similar to the invocation of Indra for protection.
    • Communities facing natural disasters may perform rituals or prayers to seek divine intervention, reflecting the essence of this mantra in seeking safety and support.
    • In professional settings, when teams face challenges, fostering a culture of trust and collaboration can be seen as a modern interpretation of invoking strength and support from a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of faith, divine protection, and the strength derived from spiritual nourishment.
    • It serves as a reminder that in the face of adversity, turning to one's beliefs and seeking support can lead to overcoming challenges.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in personal, communal, and professional contexts, emphasizing the importance of faith, collaboration, and resilience.
