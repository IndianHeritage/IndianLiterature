अधाय्य अग्निर मानुषीषु विक्ष्व अपां गर्भो मित्र ऋतेन साधन |आ हर्यतो यजतः सान्व अस्ताध अभूद उ विप्रो हव्यो मतीनाम ||
adhāyy agnir mānuṣīṣu vikṣv a1pāṁ garbho mitra ṛtena sādhan | ā haryato yajataḥ sānv asthād abhūd u vipro havyo matīnām ||
  • adhāyy agnir mānuṣīṣu: This phrase translates to "the fire (Agni) is present among humans." Agni, in Vedic literature, symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in human life. In real life, this can be seen in the way we harness energy for various purposes, such as cooking, heating, and even in spiritual rituals.
  • vikṣv a1pāṁ garbho: This means "the seed of the waters." Here, "waters" can be interpreted as the source of life, and "seed" represents potential. In practical terms, this can relate to how water is essential for agriculture, sustaining life, and fostering growth in various ecosystems.
  • mitra ṛtena sādhan: This translates to "friendship through truth." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in relationships. In everyday life, this can be observed in the way trust is built in friendships and partnerships, highlighting that true connections are formed through sincerity.
  • ā haryato yajataḥ: This means "those who perform sacrifices." Sacrifices here can be understood as acts of giving or service. In modern contexts, this can be seen in charitable actions, community service, or any effort where individuals contribute for the greater good.
  • sānv asthād abhūd u vipro: This translates to "the wise one who is exalted." It suggests that wisdom and knowledge elevate an individual. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and personal growth, where individuals strive to gain knowledge and apply it for the benefit of society.
  • havyo matīnām: This means "the offerings of the wise." It signifies that the contributions of knowledgeable individuals are valuable. In practical terms, this can be seen in how experts in various fields provide insights and solutions that benefit communities and industries.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of energy, truth, wisdom, and service in human life. It highlights the significance of Agni (fire) as a transformative force, the essential role of water in sustaining life, and the importance of integrity in relationships. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various aspects, such as fostering genuine friendships, engaging in community service, and pursuing knowledge. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the values that contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
