विष्वे हि स्मा मनवे विश्ववेदसो bhuvan वृधे ऋशादसः |अरीष्टैभिः पायुभिर विश्ववेदसो यन्ता नो 'वृकम् छर्दिः ||
viśve hi ṣmā manave viśvavedaso bhuvan vṛdhe riśādasaḥ | ariṣṭebhiḥ pāyubhir viśvavedaso yantā no 'vṛkaṁ chardiḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, all beings are under the protection of the universal knowledge; may they be free from all obstacles and may they not be harmed by any adversities."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve: all beings or universal
    • hi: indeed
    • ṣmā: protection or shelter
    • manave: of the mind or intellect
    • viśvavedaso: of universal knowledge or wisdom
    • bhuvan: the world or universe
    • vṛdhe: growth or prosperity
    • riśādasaḥ: free from obstacles or hindrances
    • ariṣṭebhiḥ: from adversities or troubles
    • pāyubhir: by the protectors or guardians
    • yantā: may they lead or guide
    • no: us or our
    • 'vṛkaṁ: harm or danger
    • chardiḥ: obstacles or disturbances
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when people come together to support each other during difficult times, ensuring that everyone feels protected and valued.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra as a form of meditation or affirmation, fostering a sense of security and resilience against life's challenges.
    • In educational institutions, teachers can use this mantra to create a nurturing environment where students feel safe to express themselves and grow without fear of judgment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on universal protection and the importance of collective wisdom. It serves as a reminder that we are all interconnected and that by fostering a supportive environment, we can overcome adversities together. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in community building, personal development, and educational settings, promoting a culture of care, understanding, and resilience. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can thrive, ensuring that no one is left vulnerable to harm or obstacles.
