पुषा सुभन्धुर दिव आ पृथिव्या इळस पतिर्मघवा दसमवर्चाः |यं देवासो अददुः सूर्यायै कामेन कृतं तवसं स्वञ्चम् ||
pūṣā subandhur diva ā pṛthivyā iḻas patir maghavā dasmavarcāḥ | yaṁ devāso adaduḥ sūryāyai kāmena kṛtaṁ tavasaṁ svañcam ||
  • pūṣā: This term refers to the deity Pūṣan, who is associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of sustenance in our daily lives, whether it be through food, knowledge, or emotional support.
  • subandhur: This word means 'well-connected' or 'having good relationships'. In a modern context, it emphasizes the significance of networking and building strong relationships in both personal and professional spheres.
  • diva: This translates to 'heaven' or 'sky', symbolizing higher aspirations and goals. It reminds us to aim for lofty ideals in our lives, much like setting ambitious career goals or personal dreams.
  • ā pṛthivyā: Meaning 'from the earth', this phrase connects us to our roots and the material world. It signifies the importance of grounding ourselves in reality while pursuing our aspirations.
  • iḻas patir: This can be interpreted as 'the lord of wealth'. In contemporary terms, it reflects the pursuit of financial stability and the role of wealth in achieving a comfortable life.
  • maghavā: This term refers to the powerful or mighty one, often associated with strength and capability. It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength to overcome challenges.
  • dasmavarcāḥ: This means 'with tenfold brilliance'. It suggests the idea of multiplying one's efforts and talents to achieve greater success, akin to the concept of working smarter, not just harder.
  • yaṁ devāso adaduḥ: This phrase translates to 'whom the gods have given'. It signifies the blessings and opportunities that come our way, reminding us to recognize and appreciate them.
  • sūryāyai: Referring to the sun, this symbolizes energy, vitality, and enlightenment. In real life, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of staying energized and motivated.
  • kāmena: This means 'with desire' or 'with intention'. It highlights the importance of having clear goals and desires in life, which drive our actions and decisions.
  • kṛtaṁ: This translates to 'done' or 'achieved', emphasizing the importance of taking action to realize our desires and goals.
  • tavasaṁ: This means 'through your power'. It signifies the role of personal strength and determination in achieving success.
  • svañcam: This translates to 'self-control' or 'self-discipline', which is crucial for personal growth and achieving long-term goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity, building strong relationships, and harnessing one's inner strength to achieve lofty goals. It serves as a reminder that while we aim for higher aspirations (diva), we must remain grounded (ā pṛthivyā) and recognize the blessings (yaṁ devāso adaduḥ) that come our way. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in career development, personal relationships, and self-improvement, encouraging individuals to pursue their desires with intention and discipline while appreciating the support and opportunities provided by the universe.
