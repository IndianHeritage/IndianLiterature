वृत्रेण यद अहिना बिभ्रद आयुधा समस्थिता युधये शंसम आविदे |विश्वे ते अत्र मरुतः सह तमनावर्धन्न उग्र महिमानम् इन्द्रियम् ||
vṛtreṇa yad ahinā bibhrad āyudhā samasthithā yudhaye śaṁsam āvide | viśve te atra marutaḥ saha tmanāvardhann ugra mahimānam indriyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛtreṇa: by Vritra (a demon representing obstacles or challenges)
    • yad: that which
    • ahinā: by the serpent (symbolizing fear or ignorance)
    • bibhrad: holding or carrying
    • āyudhā: weapons
    • samasthithā: firmly established
    • yudhaye: for battle
    • śaṁsam: for peace or well-being
    • āvide: to overcome or to dispel
    • viśve: all
    • te: you
    • atra: here
    • marutaḥ: the winds or storm gods (representing strength and power)
    • saha: together
    • tmanā: with the self
    • vardhann: increasing or enhancing
    • ugra: fierce or powerful
    • mahimānam: greatness or glory
    • indriyam: senses or faculties
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about overcoming challenges and obstacles represented by Vritra, which can be seen as fears or ignorance that hold us back.
    • The mention of weapons signifies the tools or resources we possess to confront these challenges.
    • The call for peace and well-being indicates that the ultimate goal is harmony, both within ourselves and in the world around us.
    • The reference to the winds or storm gods suggests that we are not alone in our struggles; there are forces of nature and divinity that support us.
    • By enhancing our inner strength and faculties, we can achieve greatness and overcome the adversities we face.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face fears that prevent them from pursuing their goals. This mantra encourages them to confront these fears with the tools they have, such as education and self-awareness.
    • In a professional setting, teams may encounter obstacles in projects. The mantra serves as a reminder to unite and leverage each member's strengths to achieve success.
    • In social movements, communities often battle against systemic issues. The mantra inspires collective action and resilience in the face of adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of courage, unity, and inner strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are equipped with the necessary tools to face our fears and obstacles.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to work together, harness their strengths, and strive for peace and well-being, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
