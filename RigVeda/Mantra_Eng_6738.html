मन्त्र: इन्द्र याहि हरिभिर उप कण्वस्य सुश्रुतिम् |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
endra yāhi haribhir upa kaṇvasya suṣṭutim | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Mantra: "endra yāhi haribhir upa kaṇvasya suṣṭutim | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Indra, come here with the swift ones, to the praise of Kanva. May you, who rules the heavens, grant us the divine light that shines during the day."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Indra: The king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • yāhi: A call to come or approach, indicating a request for presence or assistance.
    • haribhir: Refers to the swift ones, often associated with the forces of nature or divine beings that assist in achieving goals.
    • upa: Means 'near' or 'towards', suggesting a closeness or direct approach.
    • kaṇvasya: Refers to Kanva, a sage or a lineage known for wisdom and spiritual insight.
    • suṣṭutim: Means 'praise' or 'commendation', indicating the act of honoring or recognizing the divine.
    • divo: Means 'of the heavens', referring to the celestial realm.
    • amuṣya: Means 'of this' or 'of that', indicating a specific aspect of the divine or the universe.
    • śāsato: Means 'ruling' or 'governing', referring to the authority of the divine over the cosmos.
    • divaṁ: Refers to 'heaven' or 'sky', the abode of the divine.
    • yaya: Means 'by which' or 'through which', indicating a means or method.
    • divāvaso: Refers to 'daylight' or 'day', symbolizing clarity, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek guidance and support from higher powers or mentors, similar to calling upon Indra for assistance.
    • The concept of 'praise' can be seen in various cultures where people express gratitude towards nature, deities, or influential figures who have positively impacted their lives.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of teamwork (the 'swift ones') to achieve collective goals, much like the divine forces working together in harmony.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and guidance in achieving clarity and strength in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the forces that aid us in our journey, whether they be spiritual, natural, or communal. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios such as seeking help during challenges, fostering teamwork, and promoting gratitude and recognition in our daily interactions. By acknowledging the divine and the supportive forces around us, we can cultivate a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
