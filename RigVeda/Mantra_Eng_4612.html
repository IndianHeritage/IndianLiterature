तं ईमहा इन्द्रं अस्य रायः पुरुवीरस्य नृवतः पुरुक्षोः |यो अस्कृधोयुर अजराḥ स्वर्वान तं आ भर हरिवो माधयध्यै ||
tam īmaha indram asya rāyaḥ puruvīrasya nṛvataḥ purukṣoḥ | yo askṛdhoyur ajaraḥ svarvān tam ā bhara harivo mādayadhyai ||
  • tam īmaha indram: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and support in challenging situations, much like how people pray for rain during droughts.
  • asya rāyaḥ puruvīrasya: This translates to "of this wealth of the many heroes." It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and resources. In modern contexts, this can relate to teamwork in organizations or communities where shared resources lead to greater achievements.
  • nṛvataḥ purukṣoḥ: This means "of the man who is strong." It highlights the value of individual strength and resilience. In everyday life, this can be seen in athletes or leaders who inspire others through their determination and hard work.
  • yo askṛdhoyur ajaraḥ: This phrase translates to "who is immortal and unaging." It signifies the pursuit of eternal values and wisdom. In real life, this can be related to the quest for knowledge and personal growth, which transcends physical aging.
  • svarvān tam ā bhara: This means "bring that which is heavenly." It suggests a desire for divine blessings and higher aspirations. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as striving for excellence in one’s endeavors, whether in career, education, or personal life.
  • harivo mādayadhyai: This translates to "may the horses of the gods bring joy." Horses symbolize power and speed, and this line can be seen as a call for the swift arrival of prosperity and happiness. In contemporary life, this can relate to the pursuit of success and the joy it brings to individuals and communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, collective wealth, individual resilience, eternal wisdom, divine blessings, and joy. Its significance lies in the reminder that we can draw upon both individual and collective strengths to overcome challenges and achieve our goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, personal development, and the pursuit of excellence, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while supporting one another in their journeys.
