त्वां वाजी हवते वाजिनeyo महो वाजस्य गध्यस्य सातौ |त्वां वृत्रेष्व इन्द्र सत्तपिं तरुत्रं त्वां चष्टे मुष्ठिहा गोषु युध्यन् ||
tvāṁ vājī havate vājineyo maho vājasya gadhyasya sātau | tvāṁ vṛtreṣv indra satpatiṁ tarutraṁ tvāṁ caṣṭe muṣṭihā goṣu yudhyan ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and refers to the deity being addressed, often Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • vājī - This means "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful one." It signifies the strength and might of the deity.
  • havate - This translates to "is invoked" or "is called upon," indicating a prayer or supplication to the deity.
  • vājineyo - This term means "the one who is worthy of offerings" or "the one who deserves praise," emphasizing the reverence for the deity.
  • maho - This means "great" or "mighty," highlighting the immense power of the deity.
  • vājasya - This translates to "of strength" or "of power," reinforcing the theme of might and valor.
  • gadhyasya - This word means "of the strong" or "of the powerful," again emphasizing the strength associated with the deity.
  • sātau - This means "in the battle" or "in the conflict," indicating the context of struggle or competition.
  • vṛtreṣv - This translates to "against the Vritras," referring to the demons or obstacles that one must overcome.
  • indra - This is the name of the deity, Indra, who is often invoked for protection and strength.
  • satpatiṁ - This means "the lord of the good" or "the protector," indicating Indra's role as a guardian.
  • tarutraṁ - This translates to "the one who helps in crossing over," symbolizing assistance in overcoming challenges.
  • tvāṁ - Reiterating the address to the deity, emphasizing the personal connection in the invocation.
  • caṣṭe - This means "to strive" or "to exert effort," indicating the need for action and determination.
  • muṣṭihā - This translates to "with a fist," symbolizing strength and readiness for battle.
  • goṣu - This means "in the cows" or "in the wealth," indicating the importance of prosperity and abundance.
  • yudhyan - This translates to "in the fight" or "in the struggle," reinforcing the theme of conflict and resolution.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a powerful invocation to Indra, calling upon his strength and might in times of struggle. It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in overcoming obstacles, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times in personal or professional life, where one seeks strength and guidance to overcome difficulties. The significance lies in the belief that invoking divine support can empower individuals to face their battles with courage and determination, ultimately leading to success and prosperity.
