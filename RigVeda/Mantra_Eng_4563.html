स मञ्जमानā जनिम माणुषाणाम अमर्त्येन नाम्नाति प्र सर्स्रे |स द्युम्नेन स शवसोता राया स वीर्येण नृतमः समोकाः ||
sa majmanā janima mānuṣāṇām amartyena nāmnāti pra sarsre | sa dyumnena sa śavasota rāyā sa vīryeṇa nṛtamaḥ samokāḥ ||
  • sa majmanā: This phrase can be translated as "from the womb" or "from birth." It signifies the beginning of life and the potential that comes with it.
  • janima mānuṣāṇām: This means "the birth of human beings." It emphasizes the importance of human life and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • amartyena: Translated as "immortal" or "eternal," this word suggests a connection to the divine or the eternal aspect of existence.
  • nāmnāti pra sarsre: This can be interpreted as "by name, he is called forth." It indicates the significance of identity and recognition in the human experience.
  • sa dyumnena: Meaning "with glory," this phrase highlights the importance of honor and reputation in one's life.
  • sa śavasota: This translates to "with strength," emphasizing the need for physical and mental resilience.
  • rāyā: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • sa vīryeṇa: Translated as "with valor," this phrase underscores the significance of courage and bravery in facing life's challenges.
  • nṛtamaḥ samokāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the best among men," suggesting the aspiration to be the highest version of oneself.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the journey from birth to the realization of one's potential. It highlights the importance of identity, strength, glory, and valor in achieving a fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence, embrace their identities, and cultivate resilience. It serves as a reminder that each person has the potential to contribute positively to society and achieve greatness through their actions and character.
