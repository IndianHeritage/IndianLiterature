गिरश्च यास ते गिरवाह उक्ताः च तुभ्यं तानि |सत्रा दधिरे शवांसि ||
giraś ca yās te girvāha ukthā ca tubhyaṁ tāni | satrā dadhire śavāṁsi ||
  • giraś ca yās te girvāha ukthā ca tubhyaṁ tāni
    • giraś - This word refers to "speech" or "words." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the power of communication and expression.
    • yās - This means "who" or "that which." It indicates the subject of the mantra, referring to the divine or the cosmic order.
    • te - This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine.
    • girvāha - This term can be understood as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is invoked." It emphasizes the importance of reverence in communication.
    • ukthā - This means "sacred utterance" or "mantra." It highlights the significance of spoken words in rituals and spiritual practices.
    • tubhyaṁ - This translates to "to you," reinforcing the connection between the speaker and the divine.
    • tāni - This means "those," referring to the offerings or praises directed towards the divine.
  • satrā dadhire śavāṁsi
    • satrā - This word refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals." It signifies the importance of performing acts of devotion and service.
    • dadhire - This means "they have established" or "they have created." It indicates the act of bringing something into existence through intention and action.
    • śavāṁsi - This translates to "bodies" or "forms." In this context, it can refer to the physical manifestations of the divine or the offerings made during rituals.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the power of words and communication in connecting with the divine. It highlights the significance of sacred utterances and rituals in establishing a relationship with the cosmic order. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, using positive affirmations and mindful communication can help individuals manifest their goals and connect with their inner selves.
  • In community settings, collective rituals and shared expressions of gratitude can strengthen bonds and foster a sense of belonging.
  • In spiritual practices, chanting mantras and engaging in rituals can enhance one's connection to the divine and promote inner peace.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the profound impact that words and rituals can have in our lives, encouraging us to use them wisely and with intention.
