जातो यद अग्ने भुवना व्य अख्यः पशून न गोपा इर्यः परिज्मा |वैश्वानर ब्रह्मणे विन्द गातुं यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
jāto yad agne bhuvanā vy akhyaḥ paśūn na gopā iryaḥ parijmā | vaiśvānara brahmaṇe vinda gātuṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • jāto yad agne bhuvanā vy akhyaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, born from the world, you are the one who illuminates." Here, Agni represents the divine fire or energy that sustains life and is often associated with knowledge and enlightenment.
  • paśūn na gopā iryaḥ parijmā: This part means "May you protect the cattle and the guardians." In ancient times, cattle were a symbol of wealth and prosperity. This line emphasizes the importance of safeguarding resources and nurturing life.
  • vaiśvānara brahmaṇe vinda: Translated, this means "Seek the universal spirit, the Brahman." Vaiśvānara refers to the cosmic aspect of Agni, representing the universal consciousness that connects all beings.
  • gātuṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This can be interpreted as "May you always protect us with auspiciousness." It is a prayer for divine protection and blessings in our lives, ensuring well-being and harmony.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine energy (Agni), and the need for protection and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature and the resources we depend on.
  • Real-life Application: In modern times, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, where we seek to protect our natural resources (akin to protecting cattle). It also resonates in personal development, where individuals strive for enlightenment and seek to connect with a higher consciousness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, urging us to seek knowledge, protect our resources, and strive for harmony. It is applicable in daily life as we navigate our responsibilities towards ourselves, society, and the environment, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
