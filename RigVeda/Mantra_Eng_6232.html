यन् नासत्याः परावती यद्वा स्थोऽध्य अम्बरे। अतः सहस्रनिर्णिजा रथेन यातम अश्विनाः॥
yan nāsatyā parāvati yad vā stho adhy ambare | ataḥ sahasranirṇijā rathenā yātam aśvinā ||
  • yan nāsatyā parāvati: This phrase can be translated as "what the Nāsatyas (the twin gods) have made manifest." The Nāsatyas, also known as the Aśvins, are deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of existence and the balance between opposites.
  • yad vā stho adhy ambare: This translates to "what is established in the sky." This part of the mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and the cosmos, suggesting that the actions of the Nāsatyas have a celestial significance.
  • ataḥ sahasranirṇijā: This means "therefore, with a thousand decisions." It indicates the multitude of choices and paths available to individuals, reflecting the complexity of life and the importance of making informed decisions.
  • rathenā yātam aśvinā: This translates to "let us travel with the Aśvins in their chariot." The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, and invoking the Aśvins suggests seeking their guidance and blessings as one navigates through life's challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the importance of divine guidance in our lives. It emphasizes the need to recognize the dualities in existence and the choices we face. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to decision-making processes, where one seeks wisdom and clarity before taking action. By invoking the blessings of the Aśvins, individuals can find strength and direction in their journeys, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual aspects of life. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain connected to the divine and to seek balance and guidance in all endeavors.
