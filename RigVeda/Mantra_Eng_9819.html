तव श्रीयो वर्ष्यस्येव विद्युतश् चित्राश् चकित् उषसां न केतवः |यद ओषधीर अभिसृष्टो वनानि च परि स्वयम् चिनुषे अन्नम् आस्ये ||
tava śriyo varṣyasyeva vidyutaś citrāś cikitra uṣasāṁ na ketavaḥ | yad oṣadhīr abhisṛṣṭo vanāni ca pari svayaṁ cinuṣe annam āsye ||
  • tava śriyo - "Your glory" or "Your radiance": This phrase refers to the divine brilliance and splendor associated with a higher power or deity. In real life, we can relate this to the admiration we have for individuals who excel in their fields, such as scientists, artists, or leaders who inspire others through their achievements.
  • varṣyasyeva - "Like the rain": This simile compares the divine glory to the nourishing and life-giving qualities of rain. Just as rain is essential for growth and sustenance, the blessings from the divine are crucial for our well-being. For example, in agriculture, timely rain is vital for crops, symbolizing how divine support is necessary for success in our endeavors.
  • vidyutaś citrāś - "Brilliant like lightning": This phrase emphasizes the striking and powerful nature of divine energy. Lightning is often seen as a force of nature that can illuminate the darkest skies. In our lives, moments of inspiration or sudden clarity can be likened to this brilliance, guiding us through challenges.
  • cikitra uṣasāṁ - "Of the colorful dawn": Here, the dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. Just as each dawn brings a fresh start, we too experience new chances in life. For instance, starting a new job or moving to a new city can be seen as a dawn in our personal journey.
  • na ketavaḥ - "Not the deceivers": This phrase suggests a distinction between genuine blessings and false promises. In real life, this can be related to the importance of discernment in our choices, such as recognizing trustworthy relationships versus those that may lead us astray.
  • yad oṣadhīr - "When the herbs (plants) are released": This refers to the natural world and its bounty. The release of herbs signifies the abundance of nature and its healing properties. In our daily lives, we often turn to nature for remedies, such as herbal teas or natural supplements, highlighting the importance of nature in our health.
  • abhisṛṣṭo - "Having been released": This indicates the act of letting go or allowing something to flourish. In personal growth, this can be seen in the context of letting go of past grievances to allow new relationships to develop.
  • vanāni ca - "And the forests": The forests represent the richness of biodiversity and the interconnectedness of life. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where protecting forests is crucial for maintaining ecological balance.
  • pari svayaṁ - "You yourself": This phrase emphasizes personal agency and responsibility. It reminds us that we have the power to shape our destinies through our actions and choices. For example, pursuing education or skill development is a way of taking charge of our future.
  • cinuṣe annam āsye - "You gather food in your mouth": This symbolizes the act of nourishment and sustenance. It highlights the importance of food in our lives, not just for survival but also for cultural and social gatherings, such as family meals or festivals that bring people together.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine blessings, the importance of nature, and personal responsibility. It signifies that just as rain nourishes the earth and lightning illuminates the sky, divine grace supports our endeavors. In real life, this mantra encourages us to appreciate the beauty of new beginnings, the richness of nature, and the significance of making wise choices. It serves as a reminder to seek genuine connections and to take responsibility for our growth, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and harmonious life.
