त्वं नो अग्नि सानये धनानां यशसां कारुं कृणुहि स्तवाणः |ऋध्याम कर्मापसां नवेन देवैर dyावापृथिवी प्रावतम् नः ||
tvaṁ no agne sanaye dhanānāṁ yaśasaṁ kāruṁ kṛṇuhi stavānaḥ | ṛdhyāma karmāpasā navena devair dyāvāpṛthivī prāvataṁ naḥ ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within us. In real life, this can be likened to the inner drive or passion that motivates individuals to pursue their goals.
  • sanaye dhanānāṁ: This means "bestow upon us wealth." Here, wealth is not just material riches but can also refer to knowledge, health, and prosperity in various forms. For example, a person who invests in education gains wealth in knowledge, which can lead to better opportunities in life.
  • yaśasaṁ kāruṁ: This translates to "create fame." Fame here can be understood as recognition for one's contributions or achievements. In a modern context, this could relate to someone who works hard in their profession and gains respect and acknowledgment from peers and society.
  • kṛṇuhi stavānaḥ: This means "perform our praises." It signifies the importance of acknowledging and celebrating achievements, whether personal or communal. For instance, in a workplace, recognizing team members' efforts can boost morale and productivity.
  • ṛdhyāma: This word means "may we prosper." It emphasizes the desire for growth and success in life. In practical terms, this could relate to a community working together to improve their living conditions, such as building better infrastructure or supporting local businesses.
  • karmāpasā: This translates to "through our actions." It highlights the significance of taking initiative and being proactive in achieving one's goals. For example, a student who diligently studies and participates in extracurricular activities is more likely to succeed academically and socially.
  • navena devair: This means "with new gods." It suggests the idea of innovation and embracing new ideas or technologies. In today's world, this could refer to adopting new methods in business or education to enhance efficiency and effectiveness.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This translates to "heaven and earth." It symbolizes the balance between the spiritual and material worlds. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining a balance between work and personal life, ensuring that one does not neglect either aspect.
  • prāvataṁ naḥ: This means "may it come to us." It expresses a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life. For instance, a community coming together to support a charitable cause hopes for the blessings of prosperity and well-being for all involved.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of inner strength, wealth in various forms, recognition, proactive actions, innovation, and balance. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for prosperity and harmony. By invoking these principles, one can foster a fulfilling and successful life, both personally and collectively.
