एव पित्रे विश्वदेवाय वृष्णे यज्ञैर विद्हेम नमसाऽ हविर्भिः |बृहस्पते सुप्रजा वीरवंतो वयं स्याम पतयो रयीणाम ||
evā pitre viśvadevāya vṛṣṇe yajñair vidhema namasā havirbhiḥ | bṛhaspate suprajā vīravanto vayaṁ syāma patayo rayīṇām ||
  • evā pitre: This phrase translates to "to the father" or "to the ancestral spirits." It signifies respect and reverence towards one's ancestors, acknowledging their role in our lives.
  • viśvadevāya: This means "to all the gods." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • vṛṣṇe: Referring to Lord Vishnu, this term highlights the aspect of preservation and protection in the cosmic order.
  • yajñair vidhema: This translates to "we perform sacrifices." It indicates the act of offering and devotion, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals.
  • namasā havirbhiḥ: This means "with our offerings and salutations." It reflects the importance of humility and gratitude in our approach to the divine.
  • bṛhaspate: Referring to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods, this term signifies wisdom and guidance.
  • suprajā: This translates to "with good progeny." It emphasizes the desire for prosperity and well-being in one's lineage.
  • vīravanto: Meaning "brave" or "valiant," this term highlights the qualities of courage and strength that are desirable in individuals.
  • vayaṁ syāma: This means "may we become." It expresses a wish or aspiration for the collective well-being of the community.
  • patayo rayīṇām: This translates to "the lords of wealth." It signifies the pursuit of abundance and prosperity in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, respect for ancestors, and the pursuit of prosperity and wisdom. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals with the divine and the importance of community. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as family gatherings, rituals, and ceremonies where gratitude and respect are expressed. It encourages individuals to strive for bravery and wisdom while fostering a sense of unity and abundance in their lives and communities.
