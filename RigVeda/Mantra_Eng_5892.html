इमा जुह्वाना युष्मद् आ नमोभिः प्रति स्तोमं सरस्वति जुषस्व |तव शर्मन् प्रियतमे दधानाऽ उप स्थेयाम शरणं न वृक्षम् ||
imā juhvānā yuṣmad ā namobhiḥ prati stomaṁ sarasvati juṣasva | tava śarman priyatame dadhānā upa stheyāma śaraṇaṁ na vṛkṣam ||
  • imā juhvānā: This phrase can be translated as "these offerings" or "these invocations." It signifies the act of offering or invoking divine presence through rituals.
  • yuṣmad ā: This means "to you" or "towards you," indicating a direction of devotion and respect towards the divine.
  • namobhiḥ: This translates to "with salutations" or "with reverence," emphasizing the importance of humility and respect in spiritual practices.
  • prati stomaṁ: This can be understood as "in response to the praise" or "in accordance with the hymns," suggesting that the offerings are made in alignment with the divine praises.
  • sarasvati juṣasva: "O Sarasvati, may you be pleased." Sarasvati is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. This line expresses a desire for her blessings and favor.
  • tava śarman priyatame dadhānā: This translates to "holding your protection, dear one." It signifies the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in life.
  • upa stheyāma: This means "may we be steadfast" or "may we remain firm," indicating a desire for stability and strength in one's spiritual journey.
  • śaraṇaṁ na vṛkṣam: This translates to "not to the tree for refuge," suggesting that true refuge and support should come from the divine rather than from material or transient sources.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion, respect, and seeking divine guidance, particularly from the goddess Sarasvati. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in moments of uncertainty or when one seeks knowledge and wisdom. For instance, students may recite this mantra before exams to invoke Sarasvati's blessings for clarity and understanding. Additionally, it serves as a reminder to seek spiritual refuge rather than relying solely on material possessions or external circumstances. The significance lies in fostering a deep connection with the divine, which can provide strength and stability in life's challenges.
