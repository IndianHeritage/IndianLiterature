आ देवानाम् अपि पन्थाम् अगन्म यच् चक्नवाम तद् अनु प्रवोढुम् |अग्निर् विद्वान् स यजात् सेद् उ होताऽ सो अध्वरान् स ऋतून् कल्पयाति ||
ā devānām api panthām aganma yac chaknavāma tad anu pravoḻhum | agnir vidvān sa yajāt sed u hotā so adhvarān sa ṛtūn kalpayāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • devānām: of the gods
    • api: also
    • panthām: the path
    • aganma: we have come
    • yac: whatever
    • chaknavāma: we are capable of
    • tad: that
    • anu: following
    • pravoḻhum: we shall speak
    • agnir: fire
    • vidvān: the wise
    • sa: he
    • yajāt: who sacrifices
    • sed: indeed
    • u: and
    • hotā: the priest
    • so: he
    • adhvarān: of the rituals
    • sa: he
    • ṛtūn: the seasons
    • kalpayāti: arranges
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey towards the divine path, acknowledging the capabilities we possess.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the role of fire (Agni) as a symbol of transformation and purification.
    • The wise one who performs sacrifices is seen as a mediator who arranges the seasons and the cycles of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of making informed decisions.
    • Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can harness their inner fire to overcome challenges and achieve personal growth.
    • The concept of rituals can be seen in various cultural practices where individuals seek blessings and guidance from higher powers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of following a righteous path and utilizing our capabilities to connect with the divine.
    • It teaches us that wisdom and sacrifice are essential in navigating life's complexities and that we have the power to shape our destinies.
    • In real life, this can be applied by engaging in self-reflection, seeking knowledge, and participating in meaningful rituals that foster community and personal growth.
