एव त्वां इन्द्र वज्रिन् अत्र विश्वे देवासः सुहवासा ऊमाः |महाम उभे रोधसी वृद्धं ऋष्वं निर एकं इद वृणते वृत्रहत्ये ||
evā tvām indra vajrinn atra viśve devāsaḥ suhavāsa ūmāḥ | mahām ubhe rodasī vṛddham ṛṣvaṁ nir ekam id vṛṇate vṛtrahatye ||
  • evā tvām indra vajrinn: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, we invoke you, Indra, the wielder of the thunderbolt." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, storms, and the power to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be likened to seeking strength and support in challenging situations, much like how athletes prepare for competitions by invoking their inner strength.
  • atra viśve devāsaḥ suhavāsa ūmāḥ: This translates to "Here, all the gods, who dwell in harmony, are present." This line emphasizes the unity and collective presence of divine forces. In everyday life, this can be seen in community gatherings or teamwork, where individuals come together for a common purpose, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
  • mahām ubhe rodasī: This means "In the great expanse of the two realms." The two realms can refer to the physical and spiritual worlds. This concept is applicable in our lives when we balance our material pursuits with spiritual growth, recognizing that both aspects are essential for holistic well-being.
  • vṛddham ṛṣvaṁ: This translates to "the one who is great and powerful." It signifies the importance of recognizing and respecting those who possess wisdom and strength. In real life, this can be related to mentorship, where individuals seek guidance from those who have more experience and knowledge.
  • nir ekam id vṛṇate: This means "He who chooses the one without a second." This line suggests the idea of singularity and focus. In practical terms, it can be applied to decision-making processes where one must choose a path or a goal amidst various options, emphasizing the importance of clarity and commitment.
  • vṛtrahatye: This translates to "in the slaying of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. This can be seen in our lives as the challenges we face, and the mantra serves as a reminder that with determination and support, we can overcome these obstacles, much like how individuals face personal struggles and emerge victorious.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, unity, wisdom, focus, and overcoming challenges. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; we have the support of the divine and the community around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength in adversity, work collaboratively with others, and make wise choices in their personal and professional lives. By invoking these principles, one can navigate the complexities of life with resilience and purpose.
