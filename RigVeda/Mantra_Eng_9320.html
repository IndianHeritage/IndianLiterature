को मां ददर्श कतमः स देवो यो मे तंवो बहुधा पर्यपश्यत् |क्वाह मित्रावरुणा क्षीयन्त्य अग्नेर विश्वाः समिधो देवयानिः ||
ko mā dadarśa katamaḥ sa devo yo me tanvo bahudhā paryapaśyat | kvāha mitrāvaruṇā kṣiyanty agner viśvāḥ samidho devayānīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • mā: me
    • dadarśa: has seen
    • katamaḥ: which
    • sa: that
    • devo: god
    • yo: who
    • me: my
    • tanvo: body
    • bahudhā: in many ways
    • paryapaśyat: has observed
    • kvāha: where
    • mitrāvaruṇā: by Mitra and Varuna
    • kṣiyanty: are diminishing
    • agner: of fire
    • viśvāḥ: all
    • samidho: offerings
    • devayānīḥ: divine paths
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the divine presence and observation. It asks who has seen the divine in various forms and where the deities Mitra and Varuna are when offerings are made to the fire.
    • The mention of "Mitra and Varuna" signifies the duality of nature, representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This reflects the interconnectedness of relationships in our lives.
    • The phrase "in many ways" suggests that divinity can manifest in various forms, much like how we perceive love, kindness, and support in different relationships.
    • The reference to offerings to fire symbolizes the importance of rituals and the acknowledgment of divine forces in our lives, akin to how we celebrate significant moments with family and friends.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the fire is often a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing the divine presence in their union.
    • During festivals, people make offerings to the fire, representing gratitude and respect towards the divine, similar to how we express appreciation to our loved ones.
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance from mentors or friends (akin to Mitra and Varuna) when faced with challenges, reflecting the divine support we receive from our relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in our everyday lives and relationships. It encourages us to be aware of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of rituals in acknowledging the divine presence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the support we receive from others and to honor the moments of connection and celebration, reinforcing the idea that divinity is present in our interactions and experiences.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to cultivate a sense of gratitude and mindfulness towards the divine forces that guide us, whether through nature, relationships, or rituals.
