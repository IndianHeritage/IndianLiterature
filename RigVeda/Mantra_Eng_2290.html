नमः पुरा ते वरुणोता नूनमुतापरं तुविजात ब्रवाम |त्वे हि कम पर्वते न श्रितान्य अप्रच्युतानि दूळभ व्रतानि ||
namaḥ purā te varuṇota nūnam utāparaṁ tuvijāta bravāma | tve hi kam parvate na śritāny apracyutāni dūḻabha vratāni ||
  • Translation: "Salutations to you, O Varuna, we indeed speak of your greatness. For you are the one who is not dependent on any mountain, and your vows are unyielding and difficult to attain."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • namaḥ: Salutations or reverence.
    • purā: Ancient or primordial.
    • te: To you.
    • varuṇota: O Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and water.
    • nūnam: Indeed or certainly.
    • utāparaṁ: Beyond or above.
    • tuvijāta: Your greatness or glory.
    • bravāma: We speak or declare.
    • tve: You.
    • hi: Indeed.
    • kam: Who.
    • parvate: Mountain.
    • na: Not.
    • śritāny: Dependent or resting upon.
    • apracyutāni: Unfailing or unyielding.
    • dūḻabha: Difficult or rare.
    • vratāni: Vows or commitments.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the reverence for Varuna, who represents the moral and cosmic order in the universe.
    • It highlights the idea that true greatness is not dependent on physical attributes or locations, such as mountains, but rather on the strength of one's character and commitments.
    • The mention of "difficult vows" suggests that the pursuit of righteousness and truth may not always be easy, but it is essential for spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to uphold their values and commitments, even when faced with challenges.
    • For example, a person may face ethical dilemmas at work; invoking the spirit of Varuna can encourage them to choose integrity over convenience.
    • Additionally, it serves as a reminder that true strength lies in one's character and moral choices rather than external circumstances.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of inner strength, moral integrity, and the pursuit of truth.
    • By acknowledging the divine qualities of Varuna, individuals can strive to embody these principles in their daily lives, fostering a sense of responsibility and commitment to the greater good.
