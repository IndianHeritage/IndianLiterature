तृष्टम एतत कटुकम एतद अपाष्ठवद विषवन नैतद अत्तवे | सूर्यां यो ब्रह्मा विद्याद स इद वाधूयम अर्हति ||
tṛṣṭam etat kaṭukam etad apāṣṭhavad viṣavan naitad attave | sūryāṁ yo brahmā vidyāt sa id vādhūyam arhati ||
  • tṛṣṭam etat: This phrase can be translated as "this is seen" or "this is perceived." It indicates the importance of observation and understanding in our lives.
  • kaṭukam etad: Translated as "this is bitter," it suggests that certain experiences or truths in life may be difficult to accept or understand, much like bitter medicine that is necessary for healing.
  • apāṣṭhavad viṣavan: This can be interpreted as "like poison that is not to be taken." It emphasizes the idea of avoiding harmful influences or toxic situations in life.
  • naitad atende: This phrase means "this is not to be taken lightly." It serves as a reminder to approach serious matters with caution and respect.
  • sūryāṁ yo brahmā vidyāt: This translates to "he who knows the sun, knows Brahman." Here, the sun symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the ultimate reality (Brahman). It suggests that understanding the universe and its truths leads to a deeper understanding of the divine.
  • sa id vādhūyam arhati: This means "he is worthy of worship." It implies that those who seek knowledge and truth are deserving of respect and reverence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in navigating life's challenges. It acknowledges that while some truths may be bitter or difficult to accept, they are essential for growth and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals must confront uncomfortable truths about themselves or their circumstances to achieve greater wisdom. It also highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding the world around us, as this pursuit leads to a deeper connection with the divine. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to approach life with discernment, respect for knowledge, and an awareness of the potential dangers of ignorance.
