अṁशुं दूहन्ति स्तनयन्तं अक्षितं कविं कवयोऽपसो मनिषिणः |सम ī गावो मतयो यन्ति संयत ऋतस्य योना सदने पुनर्भुवः ||
aṁśuṁ duhanti stanayantam akṣitaṁ kaviṁ kavayo 'paso manīṣiṇaḥ | sam ī gāvo matayo yanti saṁyata ṛtasya yonā sadane punarbhuvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṁśuṁ: a ray or a part
    • duhanti: they churn or extract
    • stanayantam: nourishing or providing sustenance
    • akṣitaṁ: unblemished or pure
    • kaviṁ: the poet or seer
    • kavayo: the wise or learned
    • 'paso: the waters or the flow
    • manīṣiṇaḥ: the thinkers or those who contemplate
    • sam: together or in harmony
    • ī gāvo: the cows or the sources of nourishment
    • matayo: thoughts or ideas
    • yanti: they go or move
    • saṁyata: controlled or disciplined
    • ṛtasya: of truth or order
    • yonā: womb or source
    • sadane: in the abode or dwelling
    • punarbhuvaḥ: being reborn or renewed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the process of creation and nourishment, where the wise and learned individuals extract knowledge and sustenance from the pure and unblemished sources.
    • The imagery of cows (ī gāvo) symbolizes nourishment and abundance, suggesting that wisdom and knowledge are essential for growth and renewal.
    • The phrase "sam ī gāvo matayo yanti" indicates that when thoughts and ideas are harmonized, they lead to a greater understanding of truth (ṛtasya).
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of contemplation and discipline (saṁyata) in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students (the seekers of knowledge) must engage in disciplined study (saṁyata) to extract wisdom from their teachers (the unblemished sources).
    • In a corporate setting, teamwork (sam) among employees can lead to innovative ideas (matayo) that drive the company forward, much like the harmonious flow of thoughts in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals who reflect deeply (manīṣiṇaḥ) on their experiences can find renewed purpose (punarbhuvaḥ) in their lives, akin to the rebirth mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, harmony, and discipline in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
    • It serves as a reminder that through contemplation and collaboration, individuals can nourish their minds and spirits, leading to personal and collective growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence, whether in education, work, or personal endeavors.
