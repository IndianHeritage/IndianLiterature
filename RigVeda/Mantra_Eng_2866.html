आयं ते अस्तु हर्यतः सोम आ हरिभिः सुतः |जुषाण इन्द्र हरिभिर न आ गह्य आ तिष्ठ हरितं रथम ||
ayaṁ te astu haryataḥ soma ā haribhiḥ sutaḥ | juṣāṇa indra haribhir na ā gahy ā tiṣṭha haritaṁ ratham ||
  • ayaṁ te astu haryataḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May this be for you, O Indra, who is vibrant and full of life." Here, "ayaṁ" means "this," "te" means "for you," and "haryataḥ" refers to the vibrant or lively aspect of Indra.
  • soma ā haribhiḥ sutaḥ: This part translates to "the Soma, produced by the vibrant ones." "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and strength. "Haribhiḥ" refers to the vibrant or lively beings, and "sutaḥ" means "produced" or "born from."
  • juṣāṇa indra haribhir: This translates to "O Indra, delighting in the vibrant ones." "Juṣāṇa" means "delighting" or "enjoying," indicating Indra's appreciation for the lively and vibrant aspects of life.
  • na ā gahy ā tiṣṭha: This means "do not be held back, stand firm." "Na" means "not," "ā gahy" refers to being held back or restrained, and "tiṣṭha" means "to stand" or "to remain." This is a call for strength and resilience.
  • haritaṁ ratham: This translates to "the green chariot." "Haritaṁ" means "green" or "prosperous," and "ratham" means "chariot." The green chariot symbolizes prosperity, growth, and the journey of life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of vibrancy, strength, and resilience in life. It invokes Indra, the king of gods, to bless the seeker with vitality and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where one seeks motivation and strength to pursue their goals, whether in personal endeavors, professional challenges, or spiritual journeys. It serves as a reminder to embrace the vibrant aspects of life and to remain steadfast in the face of adversity, ultimately leading to growth and prosperity.
