मां त्वा सोमस्य गल्दयā सदā याचन्न अहं गिरा |भूर्णिम मृगं न सवनेषु चुकृधं का ईशानं न याचिषत ||
mā tvā somasya galdayā sadā yācann ahaṁ girā | bhūrṇim mṛgaṁ na savaneṣu cukrudhaṁ ka īśānaṁ na yāciṣat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • tvā: you
    • somasya: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • galdayā: with the flow
    • sadā: always
    • yācann: asking
    • ahaṁ: I
    • girā: with words
    • bhūrṇim: the great one
    • mṛgaṁ: the deer
    • na: not
    • savaneṣu: in the offerings
    • cukrudhaṁ: having been disturbed
    • ka: who
    • īśānaṁ: the lord
    • na: not
    • yāciṣat: has asked
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea to the divine, specifically addressing the sacred drink of soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The speaker is asking not to be disturbed or hindered in their offerings, emphasizing the importance of maintaining purity and focus in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "the deer" can symbolize grace and the natural world, suggesting a connection between the divine and nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals seek to maintain a clear and undisturbed mind while engaging in spiritual or reflective practices.
    • In a professional setting, it can symbolize the need for focus and clarity in decision-making, avoiding distractions that may lead to confusion or misjudgment.
    • In relationships, it can represent the importance of clear communication and understanding, ensuring that one does not misinterpret the intentions of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of maintaining clarity and purity in one's spiritual and daily practices.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance while also being aware of the distractions that can arise in life.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more focused and harmonious existence, fostering deeper connections with themselves, others, and the divine.
