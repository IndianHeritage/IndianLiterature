अव स्वराति गर्गरो गोधा परि सनिṣ्वणत् |पिङ्गा परि चनिṣ्कदद इन्द्राय ब्रह्मोद्यतम् ||
ava svarāti gargaro godhā pari saniṣvaṇat | piṅgā pari caniṣkadad indrāya brahmodyatam ||
  • Ava svarāti: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the sound (or voice) be auspicious." In real life, this can relate to the importance of positive communication and the impact of our words on others. For example, when we speak kindly to someone, it can uplift their spirits and foster a positive environment.
  • Gargaro godhā: "Gargaro" refers to a sound or a call, while "godhā" can mean a cow or a source of nourishment. This can symbolize the call of nature or the nurturing aspects of life. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we respond to the needs of our community, much like how a farmer tends to their livestock, ensuring they are well-fed and cared for.
  • Pari saniṣvaṇat: This phrase suggests a sense of completeness or wholeness. It can be related to the idea of harmony in life. For instance, when we balance our work and personal life, we create a sense of fulfillment and well-being.
  • Piṅgā pari caniṣkadad: "Piṅgā" can refer to something that is yellow or golden, often associated with prosperity. "Caniṣkadad" implies a gathering or collection. This can be interpreted as the gathering of wealth or resources. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts to pool resources for a common goal, such as a cooperative business or a community garden.
  • Indrāya brahmodyatam: "Indrāya" refers to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power. "Brahmodyatam" can be understood as the ultimate reality or the cosmic order. This suggests a connection between divine strength and the pursuit of truth. In practical terms, this can be reflected in leadership that is both strong and just, guiding others towards a higher purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of positive communication, nurturing relationships, harmony in life, collective prosperity, and the pursuit of truth and strength. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering a supportive community, balancing personal and professional life, and leading with integrity. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
