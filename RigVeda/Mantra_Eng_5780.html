अभूद उषा इन्द्रतमां मघोनी अजीजनत सुविताया श्रवांसी |वि दिवो देवी दुहितā दधात्य अंगिरस्तमा सुखृतै वसूनि ||
abhūd uṣā indratamā maghony ajījanat suvitāya śravāṁsi | vi divo devī duhitā dadhāty aṅgirastamā sukṛte vasūni ||
  • abhūd uṣā: This phrase translates to "the dawn has arisen." In a real-life context, dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. Just as each day brings a fresh start, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace new possibilities.
  • indratamā: This term refers to "the most powerful" or "the best among the Indra." Indra is often seen as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength and leadership. In our lives, this can be interpreted as striving for excellence and leadership in our endeavors.
  • maghony ajījanat: This means "the one who has given birth to wealth." It signifies the importance of prosperity and abundance. In practical terms, it can relate to the pursuit of wealth not just in material terms but also in knowledge, relationships, and experiences.
  • suvitāya: This word translates to "well-born" or "of good lineage." It emphasizes the value of good character and virtues. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of nurturing values and ethics in ourselves and our communities.
  • śravāṁsi: This means "fame" or "renown." It highlights the significance of reputation and how our actions contribute to our legacy. In today's world, this can be related to how we are perceived in our professional and personal lives.
  • vi divo: This translates to "from the heavens." It suggests a divine or higher influence in our lives. In a practical sense, it can be interpreted as seeking guidance from higher principles or values in decision-making.
  • devī duhitā: This means "the daughter of the goddess." It symbolizes feminine power and nurturing. In real life, this can be seen in the role of women in society, emphasizing the importance of empowerment and respect for feminine qualities.
  • dadhāty: This translates to "she bestows." It signifies generosity and the act of giving. In our lives, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and support for others, fostering a sense of community.
  • aṅgirastamā: This means "the best of the Angiras." The Angiras are a group of sages known for their wisdom. This phrase encourages us to seek wisdom and knowledge in our pursuits.
  • sukṛte vasūni: This translates to "the good deeds bring wealth." It emphasizes the idea that virtuous actions lead to prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the concept of karma, where good actions lead to positive outcomes.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of new beginnings, strength, prosperity, virtue, and wisdom. Its significance lies in the encouragement to embrace opportunities, strive for excellence, and act with integrity. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, fostering a sense of purpose and community. By embodying these values, one can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
