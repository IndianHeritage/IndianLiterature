उतो हि वाम पूर्व्या आविविद्र ऋतावरि रोडसी सत्यवाचः | नरश्चिद वां समिथे शूरसातौ ववन्दिरे पृथिवी वेविदानाः ||
uto hi vām pūrvyā āvividra ṛtāvarī rodasī satyavācaḥ | naraś cid vāṁ samithe śūrasātau vavandire pṛthivi vevidānāḥ ||
  • uto hi vām: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, you are." It signifies a recognition of the divine presence or the essence of the subject being addressed.
  • pūrvyā āvividra: This means "from the east, the light has come." It symbolizes the arrival of knowledge, wisdom, or enlightenment, often associated with the dawn or new beginnings.
  • ṛtāvarī: This term refers to "the one who upholds truth." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and the moral order in the universe.
  • rodasī: This word means "the heavens" or "the sky." It represents the vastness of the universe and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • satyavācaḥ: This translates to "the one who speaks the truth." It highlights the value of honesty and integrity in communication.
  • naraś cid vāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "humans, indeed, you." It acknowledges the role of humanity in the cosmic order and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • samithe: This means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It signifies the importance of community and collective action.
  • śūrasātau: This term refers to "the brave ones" or "the heroes." It celebrates courage and valor in facing challenges.
  • vavandire: This translates to "they worshipped" or "they honored." It indicates the act of reverence and respect towards the divine or the forces of nature.
  • pṛthivi: This word means "the earth." It symbolizes the physical realm and our connection to it.
  • vevidānāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who know." It refers to the wise or enlightened individuals who possess knowledge and understanding.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of truth, community, and the divine connection between humanity and the universe. It emphasizes the importance of righteousness, courage, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, where honesty and integrity are crucial, or in community gatherings that promote collective wisdom and action. It serves as a reminder to honor the truth and to recognize our interconnectedness with the world around us, inspiring individuals to act with purpose and respect towards both the divine and the earthly realms.
