आ नो रुद्रस्य सूनवो नमन्ताम् अद्यā हूतासो वसवोऽधृष्टाः |यद ईम अर्बे महति वा हितासो बाधे मरुतो अह्वाम देवान् ||
ā no rudrasya sūnavo namantām adyā hūtāso vasavo 'dhṛṣṭāḥ | yad īm arbhe mahati vā hitāso bādhe maruto ahvāma devān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we be blessed
    • rudrasya: of Rudra (a form of Shiva, representing storm and wind)
    • sūnavo: sons or followers
    • namantām: may they bow down or pay homage
    • adyā: today
    • hūtāso: those who are invoked or called upon
    • vasavo: the Vasus (deities of abundance and prosperity)
    • 'dhṛṣṭāḥ: who are steadfast or strong
    • yad: whatever
    • īm: we desire
    • arbhe: in the great or vast
    • mahati: mighty
    • vā: or
    • hitāso: those who are beneficial
    • bādhe: may they protect or guard
    • maruto: the Maruts (storm deities)
    • ahvāma: we invoke or call upon
    • devān: the gods
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for blessings and protection from the divine forces represented by Rudra and the Vasus.
    • It emphasizes the importance of humility and reverence, as it calls upon the deities to bow down to the divine will.
    • The invocation of the Maruts signifies a request for strength and support in overcoming challenges.
    • In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance and help from mentors or leaders in times of difficulty.
    • For example, in a corporate setting, employees may seek the wisdom of experienced leaders to navigate through tough projects.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • The mantra highlights the interconnectedness of individuals with divine forces, suggesting that seeking blessings can lead to success and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to remain humble and respectful towards those who guide us, whether they are spiritual leaders, mentors, or even nature itself.
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during challenging times, such as during exams, job interviews, or important decisions, to invoke a sense of calm and clarity.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us the value of community and support, encouraging us to call upon the strengths of others while remaining grounded in our own humility.
