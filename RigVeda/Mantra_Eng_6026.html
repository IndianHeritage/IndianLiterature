इच्छन्ति देवाः सुन्वन्तं न स्वप्नाय स्पृहयन्ति |यन्ति प्रमादम् अतन्द्राः ||
icchanti devāḥ sunvantaṁ na svapnāya spṛhayanti | yanti pramādam atandrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • icchanti: desire, wish
    • devāḥ: the gods
    • sunvantaṁ: one who is virtuous or righteous
    • na: not
    • svapnāya: for dreams
    • spṛhayanti: they long for, they aspire
    • yanti: go, proceed
    • pramādam: to negligence, carelessness
    • atandrāḥ: those who are vigilant, alert
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra conveys that the gods desire those who are virtuous and righteous.
    • It emphasizes that the divine does not aspire for those who are lost in dreams or illusions.
    • Instead, it is the vigilant and alert individuals who are favored and guided by the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, an employee who consistently demonstrates integrity and hard work is often recognized and rewarded, akin to the "virtuous" individuals mentioned in the mantra.
    • Conversely, those who are distracted or lack focus on their responsibilities may miss opportunities for growth and success, reflecting the idea of being lost in dreams.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who are attentive and caring are more likely to build strong connections, while those who are negligent may find their relationships faltering.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to cultivate virtues such as diligence, integrity, and awareness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to remain alert and focused, as these qualities attract positive energies and divine blessings.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including career, relationships, and personal development, guiding us to strive for righteousness and attentiveness.
