उत स्वराजो अदितिर अदब्दस्य व्रतस्य ये |महो राजाना ईशते ||
uta svarājo aditir adabdhasya vratasya ye | maho rājāna īśate ||
  • uta: This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to what has been previously mentioned.
  • svarājo: This term refers to "the one who is sovereign" or "the ruler of the heavens." It signifies a divine authority or power that governs the universe.
  • aditir: Aditi is often understood as "the boundless" or "the infinite." In Vedic literature, Aditi is the mother of the gods and represents the cosmic order and the source of all creation.
  • adabdhasya: This word means "of the unbroken" or "of the undisturbed." It suggests a state of purity and completeness, free from any disturbances or imperfections.
  • vratasya: This term translates to "of the vow" or "of the commitment." It indicates a promise or a sacred duty that one undertakes, often in a spiritual or moral context.
  • ye: This word means "those who" or "whoever," referring to individuals or entities that embody certain qualities or actions.
  • maho: This term means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance and power of what follows.
  • rājāna: This word translates to "kings" or "rulers," indicating those who hold authority and governance.
  • īśate: This term means "rule" or "govern," referring to the act of exercising power or control.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "And the sovereign, the boundless Aditi, of the unbroken vow, those who are great rulers govern." This suggests a connection between divine authority, the purity of commitment, and the power of leadership.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: It emphasizes the qualities of great leaders who govern with integrity and commitment. For example, a successful business leader who maintains ethical practices and inspires their team embodies the essence of this mantra.
  • Spiritual Commitment: The idea of "vratasya" highlights the importance of vows and commitments in spiritual practices. Individuals who undertake spiritual disciplines, such as meditation or service, reflect the unbroken dedication to their path.
  • Community Governance: In a societal context, this mantra can inspire community leaders to govern with wisdom and compassion, ensuring that their actions are aligned with the greater good.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine authority, purity of commitment, and the qualities of great leadership. It serves as a reminder for individuals in positions of power to govern with integrity, uphold their commitments, and strive for the greater good in their communities and personal lives.
