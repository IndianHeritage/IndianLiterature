यद इन्द्र राधो अस्ति ते मागोनम् मगवत्तम |तेन नो बोधि सदहमाद्यो वृधे भगो दानाय वृत्रहन ||
yad indra rādho asti te māghonam maghavattama | tena no bodhi sadhamādyo vṛdhe bhago dānāya vṛtrahan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • rādho: is the source of strength or power
    • asti: exists
    • te: for you
    • māghonam: of great wealth or abundance
    • maghavattama: the most powerful or the best among the mighty
    • tena: by that
    • no: for us
    • bodhi: knowledge or enlightenment
    • sadhamādyo: the best of the best
    • vṛdhe: in growth or prosperity
    • bhago: wealth or fortune
    • dānāya: for giving or sharing
    • vṛtrahan: the slayer of Vritra, a demon
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, to bestow upon us the strength and wealth that he possesses. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and prosperity, asking for enlightenment that leads to growth and the ability to share wealth with others. The reference to Vritra signifies overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek the strength and wisdom to make decisions that lead to the growth of their organization. This mantra can be a reminder to seek knowledge and share success with their teams.
    • In personal life, individuals may face challenges that seem insurmountable. By invoking the spirit of this mantra, one can find the strength to overcome these challenges, much like Indra defeated Vritra.
    • In community service, the idea of sharing wealth and resources can be seen in charitable organizations that aim to uplift the less fortunate, embodying the essence of 'dānāya' (for giving).
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, knowledge, and the sharing of wealth. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community service, reminding us that true power comes from enlightenment and the ability to uplift others. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges and contribute positively to society.
