नूनमर्च विहायसे स्तोमेभिः स्थूरयूपवत् |ऋषे वैयश्व दाम्यायाग्नये ||
nūnam arca vihāyase stomebhiḥ sthūrayūpavat | ṛṣe vaiyaśva damyāyāgnaye ||
  • nūnam: This word can be translated as "indeed" or "certainly," indicating a strong affirmation or certainty about what follows.
  • arca: This refers to "worship" or "praise," highlighting the act of reverence towards a deity or divine force.
  • vihāyase: This means "you who are free" or "you who have transcended," suggesting a state of liberation or elevation.
  • stomebhiḥ: This translates to "with hymns" or "with praises," emphasizing the importance of vocal expressions of devotion.
  • sthūrayūpavat: This can be interpreted as "like a great pillar" or "like a strong support," symbolizing stability and strength.
  • ṛṣe: This word means "sage" or "seer," referring to a wise person who has deep spiritual insight.
  • vaiyaśva: This term is often associated with "the one who is worthy of respect" or "the revered one," indicating a high status or honor.
  • damyāyāgnaye: This translates to "to the fire of restraint" or "to the controlled fire," which symbolizes the transformative power of discipline and control.

The mantra can be understood as a call to worship and praise a divine force that embodies strength and stability, while also recognizing the wisdom of sages and the importance of restraint. It emphasizes the significance of vocal expressions of devotion and the transformative power of disciplined energy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of restraint and discipline, individuals can apply these principles in their daily lives to achieve personal goals, such as maintaining a healthy lifestyle or pursuing education.
  • Community Support: The idea of being a "great pillar" can be reflected in community service, where individuals support one another, creating a strong and stable society.
  • Spiritual Practices: The act of worship and praise can be integrated into daily rituals, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and promoting inner peace.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of worship, strength, and discipline in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom, practice restraint, and support one another, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
