मखस्य ते तविषस्य प्र जूतिम इयर्मि वाचं अमृताय भूषण |इन्द्र क्षितीनाम् असि मानवशीलां विश्वां दैवीनाम् उता पूर्वयावः ||
makhasya te taviṣasya pra jūtim iyarmi vācam amṛtāya bhūṣan | indra kṣitīnām asi mānuṣīṇāṁ viśāṁ daivīnām uta pūrvayāvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • makhasya: of the sacrifice or offering
    • te: your
    • taviṣasya: of strength or power
    • pra: towards
    • jūtim: brilliance or light
    • iyarmi: I invoke or I call
    • vācam: speech or word
    • amṛtāya: for immortality
    • bhūṣan: adornment or decoration
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • kṣitīnām: of the earth or the earthly beings
    • asi: you are
    • mānuṣīṇāṁ: of humans
    • viśāṁ: of the community or society
    • daivīnām: of the divine
    • uta: and
    • pūrvayāvā: of the ancient or previous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the divine strength and brilliance associated with the act of sacrifice.
    • It emphasizes the importance of words and speech in achieving immortality and divine connection.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate power and authority, and the mantra seeks his blessings for the community of humans.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of communication and expression in achieving personal and communal goals.
    • For instance, in a team setting, clear and powerful communication can lead to successful project outcomes, much like the invocation of divine strength in the mantra.
    • Additionally, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in acts of selflessness, such as volunteering or helping others, which can lead to a sense of fulfillment and connection to the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in the pursuit of strength, brilliance, and immortality through words and actions.
    • It teaches us that our speech and intentions can have a profound impact on our lives and the lives of those around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, effective teamwork, and a deeper connection to the community and the divine.
