मन्त्र: सूर्यो नो दिवस पातु वातो अन्तरिक्षात् | अग्निर्नः पार्थिवेभ्यः ||
sūryo no divas pātu vāto antarikṣāt | agnir naḥ pārthivebhyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sūryo no divas pātu vāto antarikṣāt | agnir naḥ pārthivebhyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the Sun protect us from the sky; may the Wind protect us from the atmosphere; may Fire protect us from the earth."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryo: Sun
    • no: our
    • divas: from the sky
    • pātu: protect
    • vāto: Wind
    • antarikṣāt: from the atmosphere
    • agnir: Fire
    • naḥ: us
    • pārthivebhyaḥ: from the earth
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the protective energies of three fundamental elements: the Sun, Wind, and Fire.
    • Sun (Sūrya): The Sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In real life, we rely on sunlight for warmth, growth of plants, and overall well-being. For example, during winter months, the absence of sunlight can lead to Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD), highlighting the importance of the Sun in our lives.
    • Wind (Vāta): Wind represents the air we breathe and the movement of energy. It can be seen in the way it carries seeds for plants to grow in new areas, symbolizing change and adaptability. In our daily lives, we experience the wind as a refreshing force, reminding us of the importance of fresh air and nature.
    • Fire (Agni): Fire symbolizes transformation and purification. It is essential for cooking food, providing warmth, and even in industrial processes. For instance, in many cultures, fire is used in rituals to signify purification and the removal of negativity, showing its significance in both practical and spiritual realms.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the elements that sustain life. By invoking the protection of the Sun, Wind, and Fire, we acknowledge their vital roles in our existence.
    • In real life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to respect and protect our environment, as these elements are crucial for our survival. It encourages us to seek balance and harmony with nature, promoting a sustainable lifestyle.
    • Overall, the mantra is a call for divine protection and guidance, urging us to remain aware of the natural forces that influence our lives and to cultivate gratitude for their presence.
