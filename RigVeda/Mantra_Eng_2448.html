अपां गर्भं दर्शतम् ओषधीनां वनाः जजाना सुभगा विरूपम् |देवासश्चिन मनसा सं हि जग्मुः पनिष्ठं जातं तवसं दुवास्यन् ||
apāṁ garbhaṁ darśatam oṣadhīnāṁ vanā jajāna subhagā virūpam | devāsaś cin manasā saṁ hi jagmuḥ paniṣṭhaṁ jātaṁ tavasaṁ duvasyan ||
  • Mantra: "apāṁ garbhaṁ darśatam oṣadhīnāṁ vanā jajāna subhagā virūpam | devāsaś cin manasā saṁ hi jagmuḥ paniṣṭhaṁ jātaṁ tavasaṁ duvasyan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāṁ: of water
    • garbhaṁ: the womb or essence
    • darśatam: manifest or visible
    • oṣadhīnāṁ: of the plants or herbs
    • vanā: from the forest
    • jajāna: born or produced
    • subhagā: auspicious or blessed
    • virūpam: having a beautiful form
    • devāsaś: of the gods
    • cin: with thought or mind
    • manasā: with the mind
    • saṁ hi: indeed
    • jagmuḥ: they went or moved
    • paniṣṭhaṁ: to the best or highest
    • jātaṁ: born or created
    • tavasaṁ: through your power
    • duvasyan: to be nourished or sustained
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of water and its connection to the plants and herbs that grow in the forest. It highlights the idea that water is the source of life, nurturing the flora that is essential for the ecosystem.
    • The phrase "apāṁ garbhaṁ darśatam" suggests that the essence of water is visible and manifest in the life it supports. For example, when we see a lush green forest, we can appreciate how water has given life to the plants.
    • The term "subhagā virūpam" indicates that the plants are not only alive but are also beautiful and auspicious, contributing to the overall harmony of nature. This can be seen in gardens or natural landscapes where diverse plant life coexists, creating a serene environment.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "devāsaś cin manasā saṁ hi jagmuḥ," implies that the gods, through their thoughtful intention, have created this beautiful and nourishing environment. This can be related to the idea of divine intervention in nature, where the balance of ecosystems is maintained.
    • Furthermore, "paniṣṭhaṁ jātaṁ tavasaṁ duvasyan" emphasizes the importance of sustaining life through the power of water and nature. In real life, this can be observed in agricultural practices where water management is crucial for crop production and food security.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of water, plants, and divine energy in sustaining life. It reminds us of the importance of respecting and preserving our natural resources.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in environmental conservation efforts, sustainable agriculture, and holistic approaches to health and wellness, where the balance of nature is crucial for human existence.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to appreciate the beauty and significance of nature, encouraging us to live in harmony with our environment.
