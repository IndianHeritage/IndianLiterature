ये स्था मनोऽर्जनीयास ते श्रिणोतन यद् वो देवाः इमहे तद् ददातन। जैत्रं क्रतुः रयिमद् वीरवत् यशस तद् देवानाम् अवो अद्या वृणीमहे॥
ye sthā manor yajñiyās te śṛṇotana yad vo devā īmahe tad dadātana | jaitraṁ kratuṁ rayimad vīravad yaśas tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Translation: "Those who are steadfast in their minds, may they hear our call; we invoke the deities for that which we desire. May they grant us victory, strength, wealth, and fame; we choose today from among the gods."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye sthā: "Those who are steadfast" - Refers to individuals who are firm in their resolve and commitment.
    • manor: "of the mind" - Indicates the importance of mental focus and clarity.
    • yajñiyās: "in sacrifices" - Relates to the act of offering and devotion, often in a ritualistic context.
    • te: "may they" - A call for the attention or blessings of the deities.
    • śṛṇotana: "hear" - An appeal for the divine to listen to the prayers and intentions of the devotees.
    • yad: "that which" - Refers to the desires or aspirations of the devotees.
    • vo: "for you" - Acknowledges the presence of the deities being invoked.
    • devā: "gods" - The divine beings that are being called upon for assistance.
    • īmahe: "we invoke" - A declaration of the act of calling upon the divine.
    • tad: "that" - Refers back to the desires or blessings being sought.
    • dadātana: "may grant" - A request for the deities to bestow their blessings.
    • jaitraṁ: "victory" - The aspiration for success in endeavors.
    • kratuṁ: "strength" - The desire for mental and physical fortitude.
    • rayimad: "wealth" - The wish for prosperity and abundance.
    • vīravad: "fame" - The longing for recognition and honor.
    • yaśas: "glory" - The pursuit of a life filled with achievements and respect.
    • tad devānām: "from among the gods" - Acknowledging the divine source of these blessings.
    • avo: "may we" - A humble request for the blessings to be granted.
    • adyā: "today" - Emphasizing the immediacy of the request.
    • vṛṇīmahe: "we choose" - A declaration of intention and selection from the divine offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may recite this mantra before a crucial presentation, focusing their minds and invoking the support of higher powers for success.
    • During personal challenges, individuals might meditate on this mantra to strengthen their resolve and seek divine assistance in overcoming obstacles.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to foster unity and collective strength, invoking blessings for the welfare of all members.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of mental focus, devotion, and the invocation of divine support in achieving one's goals. It serves as a reminder that success, strength, wealth, and fame are not solely the result of individual effort but also require the blessings of the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, professional endeavors, and community initiatives, reinforcing the belief in a higher power that aids in the journey of life.
