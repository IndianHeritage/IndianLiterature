यस्य तीव्रसुतं मदं मध्यं अन्तं च रक्षसे |अयं स सोम इन्द्र ते सुतः पिब ||
yasya tīvrasutam madam madhyam antaṁ ca rakṣase | ayaṁ sa soma indra te sutaḥ piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tīvrasutam: the intense or powerful offspring
    • madam: the intoxication or bliss
    • madhyam: the middle or central
    • antaṁ: the end or conclusion
    • ca: and
    • rakṣase: to the protector or guardian
    • ayaṁ: this
    • sa: he or that
    • soma: the divine nectar or drink
    • indra: the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • te: your
    • sutaḥ: offspring or son
    • piba: drink or consume
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful offspring of a divine source, suggesting a connection to strength and vitality.
    • The term "madam" refers to the bliss or intoxication that comes from consuming the divine nectar, symbolizing spiritual enlightenment or ecstasy.
    • The phrase "rakṣase" indicates a protective aspect, suggesting that this divine energy or strength is safeguarded.
    • In a real-life context, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, where the "soma" represents the experiences and insights gained through learning.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and wisdom from divine sources, akin to how individuals seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in their lives.
    • It highlights the balance between the intense pursuit of knowledge (tīvrasutam) and the blissful state of understanding (madam), suggesting that true wisdom comes from both effort and reflection.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various life scenarios, such as in education, where students are encouraged to absorb knowledge (soma) while being mindful of their well-being and balance (rakṣase).
