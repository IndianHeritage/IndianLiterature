अग्नि याहि सुशस्तिभिर हव्यā जुह्वान āनुṣक |यथा दूतो बभूथा हव्यवाहनः ||
agne yāhi suśastibhir havyā juhvāna ānuṣak | yathā dūto babhūtha havyavāhanaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agne: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • yāhi: come
    • suśastibhir: with good offerings
    • havyā: the offerings
    • juhvāna: while invoking
    • ānuṣak: with attention
    • yathā: just as
    • dūto: a messenger
    • babhūtha: may become
    • havyavāhanaḥ: the one who carries the offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Agni, the divine fire, to come forth and accept the offerings made with care and devotion.
    • It emphasizes the importance of intention and attention in rituals, suggesting that the quality of offerings matters.
    • The comparison to a messenger highlights the role of Agni as a facilitator between humans and the divine, carrying our prayers and offerings to the gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or religious ceremonies, the lighting of a fire symbolizes the presence of Agni, where participants offer their intentions and prayers.
    • In daily life, the mantra can be applied to any act of giving or sharing, where the intention behind the action is as important as the action itself. For instance, when donating to charity, the thoughtfulness and care put into the donation reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention and the quality of offerings in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that our actions, when performed with sincerity and attention, can bridge the gap between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • In real life, it encourages mindfulness in our interactions and contributions, reminding us that the spirit behind our actions is what truly matters.
