आ दिवस पृष्टम अश्वयुर गव्ययुः सोम रोहसि |वीरयुः शवसस पते ||
ā divas pṛṣṭham aśvayur gavyayuḥ soma rohasi | vīrayuḥ śavasas pate ||
  • ā divas pṛṣṭham: This phrase translates to "from the heights of heaven." It signifies the divine or celestial realm, suggesting a connection to higher powers or spiritual dimensions.
  • aśvayur: This term refers to "the horse," which in Vedic literature symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality. Horses were also essential in ancient Indian culture, representing wealth and power.
  • gavyayuḥ: This word means "the one who brings cows." Cows are considered sacred in Hinduism and symbolize nourishment, wealth, and abundance. The mention of cows emphasizes the importance of agriculture and pastoral life in Vedic society.
  • soma rohasi: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. "Rohasi" means "you grow" or "you thrive," indicating the flourishing of life and spirituality through divine blessings.
  • vīrayuḥ: This term translates to "the one who possesses valor." It highlights the qualities of bravery and strength, essential for overcoming challenges and achieving success.
  • śavasas pate: This phrase means "O Lord of the dead" or "O Lord of the spirits." It acknowledges the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, recognizing the importance of honoring ancestors and the cycle of life and death.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine connection, strength, nourishment, and the interplay between life and death. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Spiritual Growth: Individuals can seek inspiration and strength from higher powers to overcome life's challenges.
  • Community and Agriculture: The emphasis on cows and horses reflects the importance of community support and sustainable practices in agriculture.
  • Valor and Bravery: The mantra encourages individuals to embody courage in their endeavors, whether in personal challenges or societal issues.
  • Respect for Ancestors: Acknowledging the spiritual connection to ancestors can foster a sense of belonging and continuity in cultural practices.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of strength and valor, and the blessings that come from the divine, applicable in various facets of modern life.
