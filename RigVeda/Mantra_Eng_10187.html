इषं दूहन् सुदुगां विश्वधायसं यज्ञप्रिये यजमानाय सुक्रतो |अग्नि घृतस्नुस त्रिर ऋतानि दीद्यद वर्तिर यज्ञं पर्यन सुक्रतूयसे ||
iṣaṁ duhan sudughāṁ viśvadhāyasaṁ yajñapriye yajamānāya sukrato | agne ghṛtasnus trir ṛtāni dīdyad vartir yajñam pariyan sukratūyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iṣaṁ: that which is desired or sought after
    • duhan: to milk or extract
    • sudughāṁ: the one that is difficult to extract or obtain
    • viśvadhāyasaṁ: the one who supports or sustains everything
    • yajñapriye: dear to the sacrificial rites
    • yajamānāya: for the one who performs the sacrifice
    • sukrato: of good deeds or actions
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ghṛtasnus: one who is rich in ghee (clarified butter)
    • trir: three times
    • ṛtāni: the truths or principles
    • dīdyad: may shine or illuminate
    • vartir: the one who exists or resides
    • yajñam: the sacrifice or offering
    • pariyan: surrounding or encompassing
    • sukratūyase: for the one who performs good deeds
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "iṣaṁ duhan sudughāṁ" suggests the pursuit of that which is difficult to attain, symbolizing the challenges in life that require effort and perseverance.
    • "viśvadhāyasaṁ" emphasizes the importance of support and sustenance, indicating that we must seek help from the universe or higher powers in our endeavors.
    • The term "yajñapriye" highlights the significance of rituals and sacrifices in achieving our goals, suggesting that dedication and commitment are essential.
    • "sukrato" refers to the importance of good deeds, reinforcing the idea that our actions should be virtuous and beneficial.
    • The invocation of Agni as "ghṛtasnus" signifies the richness and abundance that can come from our efforts when aligned with divine principles.
    • The repetition of "trir ṛtāni" suggests the need for consistency and truth in our actions, illuminating the path towards success.
    • Finally, "yajñam pariyan sukratūyase" encapsulates the essence of the mantra, indicating that through our sacrifices and good deeds, we can achieve a fulfilling and prosperous life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of dedication, good deeds, and the pursuit of higher goals in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as personal growth, professional endeavors, and community service.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life of purpose, abundance, and fulfillment, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence with themselves and the universe.
