प्र धन्वा सोम जागृविर इन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव |द्यमन्तं शुष्मं आ भरा स्वर्विदम् ||
pra dhanvā soma jāgṛvir indrāyendo pari srava | dyumantaṁ śuṣmam ā bharā svarvidam ||
  • pra dhanvā: This phrase can be translated as "with the bow" or "with the weapon." In a broader sense, it symbolizes readiness and the power to overcome obstacles.
  • soma jāgṛvir: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "Jāgṛvir" means "awakened" or "alert." Together, this suggests a state of heightened awareness and spiritual readiness.
  • indrāyendo: This term refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership. "Indrāyendo" can be interpreted as "to Indra" or "for Indra," indicating a devotion or offering to this deity.
  • pari srava: This means "to flow around" or "to spread." It signifies the idea of abundance and the distribution of blessings or resources.
  • dyumantaṁ: Translated as "radiant" or "shining," this word evokes images of light, clarity, and enlightenment.
  • śuṣmam: This term means "dry" or "barren." In a spiritual context, it can represent a state of emptiness or lack of vitality that needs to be filled or rejuvenated.
  • ā bharā: This phrase translates to "bring forth" or "fill." It is a call to action, urging the listener to manifest abundance and vitality.
  • svarvidam: This can be interpreted as "the knowledge of the heavens" or "divine wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of understanding and aligning with higher truths.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra dhanvā soma jāgṛvir indrāyendo pari srava | dyumantaṁ śuṣmam ā bharā svarvidam ||" encapsulates themes of readiness, spiritual awakening, divine strength, and the flow of abundance. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one needs to summon inner strength and clarity. It encourages individuals to be alert and receptive to divine guidance, to seek enlightenment, and to actively manifest their desires and goals. By invoking the qualities of Indra and the essence of Soma, one can cultivate a mindset of abundance and resilience, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
