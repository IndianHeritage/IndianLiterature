मन्त्र: न त्वा दधे वर आ पृथिव्या इळायास पदे सुदिनत्वे अह्नाम् | दृषद्वत्यां मानवश अपयायां सरस्वत्यां रेवद अग्ने दिदीहि ||
ni tvā dadhe vara ā pṛthivyā iḻāyās pade sudinatve ahnām | dṛṣadvatyām mānuṣa āpayāyāṁ sarasvatyāṁ revad agne didīhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni tvā: to you
    • dadhe: I place or I offer
    • vara: the best or the most excellent
    • ā pṛthivyā: from the earth
    • iḻāyās: in the realm of prosperity
    • pade: in the place or position
    • sudinatve: in the state of being well-formed or auspicious
    • ahnām: of the day or of the sun
    • dṛṣadvatyām: in the shining or radiant
    • mānuṣa: human or mankind
    • āpayāyāṁ: in the flow or stream
    • sarasvatyāṁ: of Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge and wisdom
    • revad: of the wealth or abundance
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god
    • didīhi: grant or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for the best offerings from the earth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • The reference to Sarasvati highlights the connection between knowledge, wisdom, and material wealth.
    • It suggests that true wealth is not just material but also includes wisdom and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for ethical practices that lead to sustainable prosperity.
    • In education, it reflects the idea that knowledge (Sarasvati) is essential for achieving true success and wealth.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek balance between material pursuits and spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of material wealth, knowledge, and auspiciousness.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, education, and business ethics.
    • By invoking Agni and Sarasvati, we are reminded to seek a holistic approach to prosperity that encompasses both material and spiritual dimensions.
