एव महान बृहद्विवो अथर्वावोचत स्वां तन्वं इन्द्रं एव |स्वसारो मातरिभ्वरीर अरिप्राहिन्वन्ति च शवसां वर्धयन्ति च ||
evā mahān bṛhaddivo atharvāvocat svāṁ tanva1m indram eva | svasāro mātaribhvarīr ariprā hinvanti ca śavasā vardhayanti ca ||
  • evā mahān bṛhaddivo: This phrase can be translated as "Thus, the great and vast one of the heavens." Here, "mahān" signifies greatness, and "bṛhad" indicates vastness, referring to the expansive nature of the universe or the divine.
  • atharvāvocat: This means "the Atharva Veda speaks." The Atharva Veda is one of the four Vedas, which are ancient Indian scriptures that contain hymns, philosophy, and guidance for various aspects of life.
  • svāṁ tanva1m indram eva: This translates to "Indra, indeed, is the one who is our own body." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. This line suggests a personal connection to the divine, indicating that the power of Indra resides within us.
  • svasāro mātaribhvarīr: This phrase can be interpreted as "the self-born, the mother of all beings." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the divine, portraying a sense of motherhood and creation.
  • ariprā hinvanti ca: This means "they drive away the enemies." In a metaphorical sense, this can refer to overcoming obstacles or negative influences in life, suggesting that divine support helps us face challenges.
  • śavasā vardhayanti ca: This translates to "they also increase the vitality of the dead." This line can be understood as a reference to rejuvenation or the renewal of life, indicating that divine energy can bring forth new beginnings even from endings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine connection, strength, and renewal. It signifies that the divine presence, represented by Indra, is not only vast and powerful but also intimately connected to our existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal challenges, where invoking the strength of the divine can help overcome difficulties. It also highlights the importance of nurturing and supporting one another, akin to a mother's role in fostering growth and vitality. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the divine, and the potential for renewal and strength within us all.
