सहस्रधā पञ्चदशान्युक्ता यावद् dyāvāpṛthivī तावद् इति तत् |सहस्रधā महिमानः सहस्रं यावद् ब्रह्म विष्ठितं तावती वाक् ||
sahasradhā pañcadaśāny ukthā yāvad dyāvāpṛthivī tāvad it tat | sahasradhā mahimānaḥ sahasraṁ yāvad brahma viṣṭhitaṁ tāvatī vāk ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradhā: With a thousand (or countless)
    • pañcadaśāny: Fifteen
    • ukthā: Invocations or hymns
    • yāvad: As long as
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth
    • tāvad: Until
    • it: This
    • tat: That
    • sahasradhā: With a thousand (or countless)
    • mahimānaḥ: Of greatness or glory
    • sahasraṁ: A thousand
    • yāvad: As long as
    • brahma: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • viṣṭhitaṁ: Established or situated
    • tāvatī: So much
    • vāk: Speech or word
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the infinite nature of the universe and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the power of invocations and the significance of words in manifesting reality.
    • The phrase "sahasradhā" suggests that there are countless ways to connect with the divine, and "pañcadaśāny ukthā" indicates that there are specific hymns or invocations that hold great power.
    • The reference to "dyāvāpṛthivī" symbolizes the connection between the heavens and the earth, highlighting the unity of all existence.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that as long as the ultimate reality (Brahman) is established, the power of speech and invocation remains significant.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often use affirmations or mantras to manifest their desires, similar to the invocations mentioned in the mantra.
    • In various cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to connect with the divine, reflecting the idea of "dyāvāpṛthivī" as a bridge between the spiritual and the material world.
    • Public speaking and communication can be seen as a form of invoking change, where the power of words can influence thoughts, emotions, and actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound connection between the divine, the universe, and the power of words.
    • It teaches us that our invocations and expressions can shape our reality and that there is an infinite potential in our connection with the cosmos.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to recognize the importance of our words and thoughts, urging us to use them wisely to create a harmonious existence.
