यज्ञैर अथर्वा प्रथमः पथस तते ततः सूर्यो व्रतपा वेन आजनि |आ गा आजद उशनā काव्यः सचा यमस्य जातम अमृतं यजामहे ||
yajñair atharvā prathamaḥ pathas tate tataḥ sūryo vratapā vena ājani | ā gā ājad uśanā kāvyaḥ sacā yamasya jātam amṛtaṁ yajāmahe ||
  • yajñair: This word refers to sacrifices or offerings made to the deities. In real life, this can be likened to acts of charity or service to others, which are often seen as offerings to the greater good.
  • atharvā: This term relates to the Atharva Veda, which emphasizes knowledge and wisdom. In contemporary terms, it can represent the pursuit of education and understanding in our lives.
  • prathamaḥ: Meaning 'first' or 'foremost', this suggests the importance of prioritizing certain values or actions in life. For example, putting family and health first can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • pathas: This translates to 'path' or 'way', symbolizing the journey of life. It can be compared to the various choices we make that shape our destiny.
  • tate: Meaning 'there', it indicates a destination or goal. In life, this can refer to our aspirations and the places we aim to reach, both physically and metaphorically.
  • tataḥ: This means 'then' or 'after that', suggesting a sequence of events. It reflects the cause-and-effect nature of our actions and decisions.
  • sūryo: Referring to the sun, it symbolizes light, knowledge, and life. In real life, this can be seen as the pursuit of enlightenment and positivity.
  • vratapā: This term relates to vows or commitments. It emphasizes the importance of dedication in our endeavors, such as committing to personal goals or ethical standards.
  • vena: This can refer to a channel or flow, symbolizing the interconnectedness of life. It reminds us of the importance of relationships and community.
  • ājani: Meaning 'born' or 'created', it signifies the emergence of new ideas or beginnings. This can be related to innovation and creativity in our lives.
  • ā gā: This phrase means 'come forth', encouraging action and movement towards our goals.
  • ājad: This translates to 'to be born' or 'to arise', indicating the potential for growth and development in our lives.
  • uśanā: Referring to a divine figure, it symbolizes guidance and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to mentors or role models who inspire us.
  • kāvyaḥ: This term relates to poetry or art, emphasizing the beauty of expression. It reminds us of the importance of creativity in our lives.
  • sacā: Meaning 'and', it connects ideas, suggesting the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving our goals.
  • yamasya: Referring to Yama, the god of death, it signifies the inevitability of mortality. This can encourage us to live life fully and meaningfully.
  • jātam: Meaning 'born' or 'created', it emphasizes the idea of new beginnings and the potential for transformation.
  • amṛtaṁ: This translates to 'immortal' or 'eternal', symbolizing the quest for lasting impact and legacy in our lives.
  • yajāmahe: Meaning 'we worship' or 'we offer', it signifies the act of devotion and the importance of gratitude in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life’s journey, emphasizing the importance of knowledge, commitment, and the pursuit of higher ideals. It encourages us to prioritize our values, seek enlightenment, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be applied through acts of service, dedication to personal growth, and fostering relationships that uplift and inspire. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to live with purpose and intention, contributing positively to the world around us.
