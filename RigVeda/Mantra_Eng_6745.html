आ त्वा होतā मनुर्हितो देवत्रा वक्षद īḍ्यः |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
ā tvā hotā manurhito devatrā vakṣad īḍyaḥ | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from, out of
    • tvā: you
    • hotā: the one who offers, the priest
    • manurhito: inspired by Manu (the first man, a progenitor)
    • devatrā: by the gods
    • vakṣad: may you speak, may you express
    • īḍyaḥ: worthy of praise, deserving of worship
    • divo: of the sky, of heaven
    • amuṣya: of this, of that
    • śāsato: by the command, by the rule
    • divaṁ: heaven
    • yaya: by which
    • divāvaso: dwelling in the day, living in the light
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence and seeks blessings from the gods.
    • It emphasizes the role of the priest (hotā) who is inspired by the ancient wisdom of Manu, suggesting a connection to tradition and moral guidance.
    • The phrase "may you speak" indicates a desire for communication and understanding between the divine and the devotee.
    • The reference to "heaven" and "day" symbolizes enlightenment, clarity, and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the role of leaders and mentors who guide others with wisdom and integrity.
    • In educational settings, teachers embody the spirit of the hotā, inspiring students to seek knowledge and express themselves.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on the teachings of their ancestors (like Manu) to navigate moral dilemmas and make ethical choices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and the role of tradition in shaping our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom, communicate openly, and strive for enlightenment in their daily lives.
    • By invoking the blessings of the divine, one can aspire to live a life that is worthy of praise and aligned with higher principles.
