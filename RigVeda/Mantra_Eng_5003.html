इयं शुष्मेभिर बिसखाऽविरुजत सानु गिरिणां तविषेभिर ऊर्मिभिः |पारावतघ्नीम अवसे सुवृक्तिभिः सरस्वतीं आ विवासेम धीतिभिः ||
iyaṁ śuṣmebhir bisakhā ivārujat sānu girīṇāṁ taviṣebhir ūrmibhiḥ | pārāvataghnīm avase suvṛktibhiḥ sarasvatīm ā vivāsema dhītibhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "iyaṁ śuṣmebhir bisakhā ivārujat sānu girīṇāṁ taviṣebhir ūrmibhiḥ | pārāvataghnīm avase suvṛktibhiḥ sarasvatīm ā vivāsema dhītibhiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iyaṁ: this
    • śuṣmebhir: with the dry (or wise)
    • bisakhā: branches or offshoots
    • ivārujat: like a tree that flourishes
    • sānu: with strength
    • girīṇāṁ: of the mountains
    • taviṣebhir: with the power of
    • ūrmibhiḥ: waves
    • pārāvataghnīm: the one who destroys obstacles
    • avase: we invoke
    • suvṛktibhiḥ: with good speech or eloquence
    • sarasvatīm: Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge and wisdom
    • ā: may
    • vivāsema: we may dwell or reside
    • dhītibhiḥ: with understanding or intelligence
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of wisdom and knowledge, represented by the goddess Sarasvati.
    • The imagery of flourishing branches and mountains signifies strength and stability, suggesting that wisdom is rooted deeply like a tree.
    • The mention of waves indicates the dynamic nature of knowledge, which can ebb and flow but remains powerful.
    • By calling upon Sarasvati, the mantra emphasizes the importance of eloquence and good speech in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In academic settings, students often seek the blessings of Sarasvati before exams, symbolizing the desire for wisdom and clarity of thought.
    • Professionals in creative fields may recite this mantra to enhance their communication skills and inspire innovative ideas.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on this mantra to cultivate a deeper understanding of their own thoughts and actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek understanding and eloquence in their communication, fostering better relationships and personal growth.
    • By invoking Sarasvati, we acknowledge the importance of learning and the pursuit of truth, which is applicable in every aspect of life, from education to professional endeavors.
