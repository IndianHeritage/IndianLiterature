एष स्य पीतये सुतो हरिर अर्षति धर्णसिः |क्रन्दन योनिम् अभि प्रियम् ||
eṣa sya pītaye suto harir arṣati dharṇasiḥ | krandan yonim abhi priyam ||
  • eṣa sya: This phrase translates to "this is his" or "this belongs to him." It signifies possession or connection to a higher power or divine entity.
  • pītaye: This word means "for the one who is yellow" or "for the one who is golden." In the context of the RigVeda, it often refers to the sun or a divine figure associated with brightness and purity.
  • suto: This translates to "son" or "offspring." It indicates a lineage or a continuation of a legacy, often referring to divine progeny or spiritual descendants.
  • harir: This term is commonly associated with Lord Vishnu, the preserver in Hindu mythology. It signifies protection, sustenance, and the maintenance of cosmic order.
  • arṣati: This means "to shine" or "to radiate." It conveys the idea of brilliance, enlightenment, or the manifestation of divine qualities.
  • dharṇasiḥ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who holds" or "the supporter." It refers to the sustaining force of the universe, often linked to the earth or a divine protector.
  • krandan: This translates to "crying" or "lamenting." It reflects human emotions, struggles, or the call for help in times of distress.
  • yonim: This word means "womb" or "source." It symbolizes creation, nurturing, and the origin of life.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or intention towards something significant.
  • priyam: This translates to "beloved" or "dear." It signifies affection, connection, and the importance of relationships.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, emphasizing the importance of lineage, protection, and the nurturing aspects of life. It reflects the human experience of seeking support and love from higher powers during times of distress. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, protection, and emotional support, reminding them of the divine presence in their lives and the importance of nurturing relationships.


Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine's role in sustaining life and providing comfort, encouraging individuals to seek connection with the higher powers and to cherish their relationships with loved ones.
