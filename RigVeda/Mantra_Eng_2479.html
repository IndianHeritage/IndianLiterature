अग्निर देवैभिर मनुषश्च जन्तुभिः तन्मानो यज्ञं पुरुपेशसं धियाः | रथीर अन्तर ईयते साधदिष्टिभिर जीरो दमूनां अभिशस्तिचातनः ||
agnir devebhir manuṣaś ca jantubhis tanvāno yajñam purupeśasaṁ dhiyā | rathīr antar īyate sādhadiṣṭibhir jīro damūnā abhiśasticātanaḥ ||
  • Agnir: The term "Agnir" refers to fire, which is often seen as a symbol of purity and transformation. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and energy, representing the transformative power of nature.
  • Devebhir: This word means "by the gods." It signifies the divine influence and guidance in human endeavors. For example, in many cultures, people seek blessings from deities before starting important tasks, such as a new business or marriage.
  • Manuṣaś: This translates to "humans" or "mankind." It emphasizes the role of humanity in the cosmic order and the responsibilities that come with it. In real life, this can be seen in community service and humanitarian efforts aimed at improving society.
  • Jantubhis: Meaning "living beings," this term highlights the interconnectedness of all life forms. In practice, this can be observed in environmental conservation efforts, where the well-being of all creatures is considered essential for a balanced ecosystem.
  • Tanvāno: This word means "with bodies" or "in form." It suggests the physical aspect of existence and the importance of the material world. For instance, taking care of one's health and well-being is a practical application of this concept.
  • Yajñam: Referring to "sacrifice" or "offering," this term embodies the idea of giving back to the universe. In modern life, this can be seen in charitable donations and acts of kindness, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • Purupeśasaṁ: This means "with many forms" or "in various manifestations." It reflects the diversity of existence and the multitude of paths one can take. In real life, this can be related to the acceptance of different cultures and lifestyles, promoting inclusivity.
  • Dhiyā: This translates to "with wisdom" or "intellect." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in decision-making. In practice, this can be seen in education and lifelong learning, where individuals strive to gain wisdom for better living.
  • Rathīr: Meaning "charioteer," this term symbolizes guidance and control. In life, this can be likened to mentors or leaders who help navigate challenges and steer individuals toward their goals.
  • Antar: This means "within" or "inside." It suggests introspection and self-awareness. In real life, practices like meditation and self-reflection are ways to connect with one's inner self.
  • Īyate: This translates to "moves" or "travels." It signifies progress and the journey of life. In a practical sense, this can be seen in personal growth and the pursuit of dreams.
  • Sādhadiṣṭibhir: Meaning "by the established order," this term refers to the principles that govern life. In real life, this can be related to laws and ethics that guide societal behavior.
  • Jīro: This word means "vital" or "alive." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and energy in life. In practice, this can be seen in the pursuit of a healthy lifestyle and active living.
  • Damūnā: This translates to "with restraint" or "control." It highlights the significance of self-discipline. In real life, this can be observed in personal finance management and healthy habits.
  • Abhiśasticātanaḥ: This means "to attain the highest state." It signifies the ultimate goal of life, which is to reach one's fullest potential. In practice, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal and professional excellence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings, the importance of wisdom, and the pursuit of higher goals. Its significance lies in guiding individuals to live harmoniously with nature, contribute positively to society, and strive for personal growth. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, environmental conservation, and self-improvement, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
