याम् अथर्वा मनुष पितā दध्यङ् धियम् अत्नत || तस्मिन् ब्रह्माणि पूर्वथेन्द्र उक्ताऽ सम अग्मतार्चन्न् अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
yām atharvā manuṣ pitā dadhyaṅ dhiyam atnata | tasmin brahmāṇi pūrvathendra ukthā sam agmatārcann anu svarājyam ||
  • yām atharvā manuṣ pitā dadhyaṅ dhiyam atnata: This phrase can be translated to "That which the Atharva Veda, the father of humanity, has established in the mind." Here, "Atharvā" refers to the Atharva Veda, one of the four Vedas in Hinduism, which is often associated with knowledge and wisdom. "Manuṣ pitā" signifies the father of mankind, indicating a universal father figure or creator. "Dadhyaṅ dhiyam atnata" implies the establishment of wisdom or intellect in the mind.
  • tasmin brahmāṇi pūrvathendra ukthā sam agmatārcann anu svarājyam: This part translates to "In that, the Brahman (the ultimate reality) has been invoked, as was done by the ancient Indra, who attained sovereignty." "Brahmāṇi" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, while "pūrvathendra" refers to Indra, the king of gods in Hindu mythology, who is often associated with power and sovereignty. "Ukthā" means invocation or recitation, and "sam agmatārcann anu svarājyam" suggests the attainment of a higher state or sovereignty through this invocation.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge as foundational elements in human existence. It highlights the role of the Vedas in guiding humanity towards understanding the ultimate reality (Brahman) and achieving a higher state of being, akin to the sovereignty attained by Indra.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • Education: The pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is crucial in education. Just as the Atharva Veda provides guidance, modern education systems aim to cultivate critical thinking and understanding in students.
    • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from Indra's sovereignty, striving to lead with wisdom and integrity, invoking the collective knowledge of their communities to make informed decisions.
    • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the mantra to seek wisdom in their personal lives, fostering a mindset that values learning and understanding as pathways to personal sovereignty and fulfillment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, wisdom, and the pursuit of a higher state of existence. It encourages individuals and societies to seek understanding through the teachings of the Vedas, ultimately leading to personal and collective empowerment.
