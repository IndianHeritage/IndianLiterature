आयं निधिः सरमे अद्रिबुध्नो गोभिः अश्वेभिः वसुभिः न्यृष्टः |रक्षन्ति तं पाणयो ये सुगोपाः रेकु पदम् आलकम् आ जगन्त ||
ayaṁ nidhiḥ sarame adribudhno gobhir aśvebhir vasubhir nyṛṣṭaḥ | rakṣanti tam paṇayo ye sugopā reku padam alakam ā jagantha ||
  • ayaṁ nidhiḥ: This phrase translates to "this treasure" or "this wealth." It signifies the abundance and resources that one possesses or seeks in life.
  • sarame: Referring to the "dog," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes loyalty and protection. It can also represent vigilance and the ability to guard one's treasures.
  • adribudhno: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is knowledgeable about the mountains" or "the wise one." It suggests a connection to wisdom and understanding of the natural world.
  • gobhir: Meaning "by the cows," this word emphasizes the importance of cattle in Vedic society, symbolizing wealth, nourishment, and sustenance.
  • aśvebhir: This translates to "by the horses," which are often associated with strength, speed, and power. Horses were vital for transportation and warfare in ancient times.
  • vasubhir: Referring to "the wealth" or "the good things," this term highlights the abundance of resources and prosperity.
  • nyṛṣṭaḥ: This word means "protected" or "guarded," indicating the safeguarding of one's wealth and resources.
  • rakṣanti: Translated as "they protect," this term emphasizes the role of guardianship and the importance of protection in maintaining one's wealth.
  • tam paṇayo: This phrase means "that wealth" or "that treasure," reinforcing the focus on the importance of safeguarding one's resources.
  • ye sugopā: Meaning "those who are good protectors," this highlights the qualities of guardianship and the responsibility of protecting what is valuable.
  • reku padam: This can be interpreted as "the path of prosperity" or "the way to wealth," suggesting a journey towards achieving abundance.
  • alakam: This term refers to "the beautiful" or "the auspicious," indicating that the wealth and resources should be pleasing and beneficial.
  • ā jagantha: This phrase means "to the world" or "in the universe," suggesting that the wealth and resources are not just personal but have a broader significance in the world.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wealth, protection, and guardianship in life. It signifies that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom, loyalty, and the ability to protect what is valuable. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as financial management, the importance of community and family in safeguarding resources, and the need for vigilance in protecting one's assets. The mantra serves as a reminder that prosperity is a collective responsibility and that we must be wise and protective stewards of our resources, ensuring that they contribute positively to the world around us.
