त्वं अग्नि वृजिनवर्तनीं नरं सक्मन पिपर्षि विदथे विचर्षणे |यः शूरसाता परितकम्ये धनं दभ्रैभिः चित समृतā हंसि भूयसः ||
tvam agne vṛjinavartaniṁ naraṁ sakman piparṣi vidathe vicarṣaṇe | yaḥ śūrasātā paritakmye dhane dabhrebhiś cit samṛtā haṁsi bhūyasaḥ ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings.
  • vṛjinavartaniṁ: This means "the one who removes obstacles." It signifies the ability of Agni to clear the path for progress and success.
  • naraṁ: This translates to "the man" or "the human." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • sakman piparṣi: This means "you who nourish with strength." It highlights the nurturing aspect of Agni, providing strength and vitality to those who seek it.
  • vidathe vicarṣaṇe: This translates to "in the assembly of the wise." It refers to the gathering of knowledgeable individuals, where wisdom and guidance are shared.
  • yaḥ: This means "who." It introduces the qualities of the person being addressed.
  • śūrasātā: This translates to "the brave one." It signifies courage and valor, essential traits for overcoming challenges.
  • paritakmye dhane: This means "in the wealth of the surroundings." It refers to the abundance and resources available in one's environment.
  • dabhrebhiś: This translates to "with the help of the strong." It emphasizes the importance of support and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • cit samṛtā: This means "the one who is remembered." It signifies the lasting impact of one's actions and contributions.
  • haṁsi bhūyasaḥ: This translates to "you who are the greatest." It acknowledges the supreme nature of Agni and the divine presence in all aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of strength, guidance, and transformation in our lives. It highlights the importance of courage, collaboration, and wisdom in overcoming obstacles and achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. By invoking the qualities of Agni, individuals can cultivate resilience and foster a supportive environment that encourages growth and prosperity.
