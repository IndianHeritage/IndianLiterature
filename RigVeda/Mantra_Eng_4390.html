त्वं त्या चिद अच्युताग्ने पशुर न यवसे |धामाः ह यत ते अजरा वनाः वृश्चन्ति शिख्वसः ||
tvaṁ tyā cid acyutāgne paśur na yavase | dhāmā ha yat te ajara vanā vṛścanti śikvasaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • tyā - This means "by you" or "through you," suggesting a reliance on the divine for guidance or support.
  • cid - This term refers to "consciousness" or "awareness," emphasizing the importance of awareness in spiritual practices.
  • acyutāgne - This compound word can be broken down into "acyuta," meaning "infallible" or "imperishable," and "agne," meaning "fire." Together, it refers to the eternal and unchanging nature of the divine fire or energy.
  • paśur - This translates to "the animal" or "creature," which can symbolize the physical body or the material aspect of existence.
  • na yavase - This phrase means "not to be restrained" or "not to be limited," indicating the boundless nature of the divine.
  • dhāmā - This word means "abode" or "place," referring to the divine realm or the essence of the divine presence.
  • ha - This is an emphatic particle, often used to stress the importance of what follows.
  • yat - This means "that" or "which," pointing to something specific that is being referred to.
  • te - This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging to the divine.
  • ajara - This means "immortal" or "eternal," highlighting the everlasting nature of the divine.
  • vanā - This word means "forest" or "wilderness," symbolizing the natural world and its connection to the divine.
  • vṛścanti - This translates to "they who are strong" or "they who are powerful," referring to the forces of nature or divine beings.
  • śikvasaḥ - This term can mean "those who are radiant" or "those who shine," indicating the brilliance and purity of the divine energy.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between the divine and the material world, emphasizing the eternal and unchanging nature of the divine fire (Agni) and its connection to all living beings. It suggests that through the divine, one can transcend limitations and connect with the eternal essence of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to invoke a sense of connection with the divine and to seek guidance in overcoming life's challenges.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the forest and natural world encourages a respect for nature and the understanding that all living beings are interconnected through the divine energy.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of transcending limitations can inspire individuals to pursue their goals and aspirations, recognizing that they are supported by a greater force.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in all aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek a deeper connection with the eternal and to recognize their place within the larger tapestry of existence. It highlights the significance of awareness, strength, and the boundless nature of the divine, making it applicable in spiritual, environmental, and personal development contexts.
