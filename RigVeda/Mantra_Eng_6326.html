यच चक्रासि परावती यद अर्वावति वृत्रहन् |यद वा समुद्रे अन्धसोऽवितेदसि ||
yac chakrāsi parāvati yad arvāvati vṛtrahan | yad vā samudre andhaso 'vited asi ||
  • Mantra: "yac chakrāsi parāvati yad arvāvati vṛtrahan | yad vā samudre andhaso 'vited asi ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Whatever you have created, O Parāvati, whatever you have made to conquer Vṛtra, whatever you have spread in the depths of the ocean, that is what we seek."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yac: Whatever
    • chakrāsi: You have created
    • parāvati: O Parāvati (a reference to the divine feminine energy)
    • yad: Whatever
    • arvāvati: You have made to conquer
    • vṛtrahan: The slayer of Vṛtra (symbolizing obstacles or ignorance)
    • samudre: In the ocean
    • andhaso: Of the depths
    • vited: Spread or manifested
    • asi: You are
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine feminine energy, represented by Parāvati, who is often associated with strength, nurturing, and the power to overcome challenges.
    • The reference to "Vṛtra" symbolizes obstacles or ignorance that one must conquer in life. In real life, this can be likened to personal challenges such as fear, doubt, or external adversities.
    • The mention of the ocean signifies the vastness of existence and the depths of knowledge and experience. Just as the ocean holds countless mysteries, so does life present us with numerous challenges and opportunities for growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges in their career, akin to the Vṛtra. By invoking the strength of Parāvati, they can find the courage to overcome these obstacles, much like a leader who inspires their team to push through difficulties.
    • In personal relationships, one may encounter misunderstandings or conflicts. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek understanding and resolution, drawing on the nurturing aspect of Parāvati to foster harmony.
    • In the pursuit of knowledge, students often face difficulties in grasping complex subjects. The mantra encourages perseverance and the quest for wisdom, akin to diving into the depths of the ocean to uncover hidden treasures of knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like Parāvati, we have the power within us to create, conquer, and explore the depths of our potential.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where one seeks empowerment, resilience, and the ability to navigate through obstacles, ultimately leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
