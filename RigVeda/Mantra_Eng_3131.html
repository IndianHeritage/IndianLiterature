प्रवाच्यं वचसः किम मे अस्य गुहा हितम् उप निनिग वदन्ति |यद उष्रियाणाम् अप वारण इव व्रण पाति प्रियं रूपो अग्रं पदं वेः ||
pravācyaṁ vacasaḥ kim me asya guhā hitam upa niṇig vadanti | yad usriyāṇām apa vār iva vran pāti priyaṁ rupo agram padaṁ veḥ ||
  • pravācyaṁ vacasaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "what is the speech that is to be spoken?" It emphasizes the importance of communication and the words we choose to express ourselves.
  • kim me asya guhā hitam: This means "what is hidden in this cave for me?" The "cave" symbolizes the depths of knowledge or the subconscious mind, suggesting that there are hidden truths or insights waiting to be discovered.
  • upa niṇig vadanti: This translates to "they speak of it as if it is near." It indicates that the knowledge or truth we seek is accessible and can be understood if we are willing to listen and learn.
  • yad usriyāṇām: This phrase means "that which is for the seekers." It highlights the idea that knowledge and wisdom are available to those who actively seek them.
  • apa vār iva vran: This can be interpreted as "like the water that flows." It suggests that knowledge and wisdom are fluid and can be found in various forms, much like water that nourishes life.
  • pāti priyaṁ rupo: This means "it protects the beloved form." It implies that knowledge and understanding can safeguard what is dear to us, whether it be relationships, values, or our own well-being.
  • agram padaṁ veḥ: This translates to "the highest place of the path." It signifies the ultimate goal or destination in our pursuit of knowledge and truth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding through effective communication. It encourages individuals to explore the depths of their own minds and the world around them to uncover hidden truths. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, personal development, and relationships. By valuing the words we use and being open to learning, we can protect what is important to us and strive towards our highest potential. This mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom is not only found in the spoken word but also in the pursuit of understanding and connection with others.
