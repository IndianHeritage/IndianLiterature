सुपर्णं वस्ते मृगो अस्य दन्तो गोभिः संनद्धा पतति प्रसूता |यत्रा नरः सं च विच द्रवन्ति तत्रास्मभ्यम् इषवः शर्मा यंसन ||
suparṇaṁ vaste mṛgo asyā danto gobhiḥ saṁnaddhā patati prasūtā | yatrā naraḥ saṁ ca vi ca dravanti tatrāsmabhyam iṣavaḥ śarma yaṁsan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suparṇaṁ: well-winged or auspicious
    • vaste: in the abode or dwelling
    • mṛgo: the deer or creature
    • asyā: of this
    • danto: with tusks or teeth
    • gobhiḥ: with the cows or cattle
    • saṁnaddhā: bound or tied
    • patati: falls or descends
    • prasūtā: born or produced
    • yatrā: where
    • naraḥ: man or human
    • saṁ: together
    • ca: and
    • vi: apart or separately
    • dravanti: flow or move
    • tatrā: there
    • asmabhyam: for us
    • iṣavaḥ: arrows or weapons
    • śarma: protection or shelter
    • yaṁsan: may they bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a well-winged creature, possibly a metaphor for auspiciousness or divine presence, residing in a place where humans and nature coexist.
    • The mention of "mṛgo" (deer) and "gobhiḥ" (cattle) suggests a harmonious relationship between humans and animals, emphasizing the importance of coexistence in nature.
    • The phrase "yatrā naraḥ saṁ ca vi ca dravanti" indicates a space where humans move together and apart, symbolizing community and individuality.
    • The concluding part, "asmabhyam iṣavaḥ śarma yaṁsan," implies a plea for protection and safety, highlighting the need for security in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be reflected in conservation efforts where humans strive to protect wildlife and their habitats, ensuring a balance between development and nature.
    • Community initiatives that promote togetherness while respecting individual differences resonate with the idea of "naraḥ saṁ ca vi ca dravanti," fostering a sense of belonging and support.
    • In personal life, seeking protection and safety, whether through family, community, or spiritual practices, aligns with the essence of the mantra, reminding us of the importance of security in our daily lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of harmony between humans and nature, the importance of community, and the need for protection.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless message that encourages us to seek balance in our lives, respect for nature, and the importance of safety and security.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to foster relationships with our environment and community, ensuring a sustainable and secure future for all.
