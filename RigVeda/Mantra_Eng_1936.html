उर्वी सद्मनी बृहतī ऋतेन हुवे देवानाम् अवसā जनित्रī | दधाते ये अमृतं सुप्रतीके dyāvā रक्षतं पृथिवी नो अभ्वात् ||
urvī sadmanī bṛhatī ṛtena huve devānām avasā janitrī | dadhāte ye amṛtaṁ supratīke dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||
  • Mantra: "urvī sadmanī bṛhatī ṛtena huve devānām avasā janitrī | dadhāte ye amṛtaṁ supratīke dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • urvī: vast, expansive
    • sadmanī: in the abode, in the home
    • bṛhatī: great, vast
    • ṛtena: with truth, in accordance with the cosmic order
    • huve: I invoke, I call upon
    • devānām: of the gods
    • avasā: dwelling, residing
    • janitrī: mother, source
    • dadhāte: they who give, they who provide
    • ye: those who
    • amṛtaṁ: immortality, eternal life
    • supratīke: in the well-protected, in the safe
    • dyāvā: heaven
    • rakṣatam: may protect, may safeguard
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • no: for us
    • abhvāt: may not be absent, may not be lost
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the vastness of the universe and the divine presence within it.
    • The term "urvī" signifies the expansive nature of existence, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all life.
    • "Sadmanī" refers to the home or abode, suggesting that this vastness is also a nurturing space.
    • The invocation of "devānām" emphasizes the connection to the divine, calling upon the gods to bless and protect.
    • By stating "ṛtena huve," the mantra highlights the importance of truth and cosmic order in our lives.
    • The phrase "janitrī" signifies the nurturing aspect of the earth and the universe, akin to a mother providing for her children.
    • When it mentions "amṛtaṁ," it speaks to the pursuit of immortality, not just in a physical sense but in the legacy we leave behind.
    • "Supratīke" indicates a state of safety and protection, which is essential for growth and flourishing.
    • The invocation for the protection of "dyāvā" (heaven) and "pṛthivī" (earth) reflects our need for balance and harmony between the spiritual and material worlds.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in the context of environmental conservation, where we recognize the earth as a nurturing mother that needs protection.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of truth and integrity in our actions aligns with the cosmic order, leading to a fulfilling life.
    • In community building, invoking the divine presence can foster a sense of unity and collective responsibility towards each other and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vastness of existence and our place within it, urging us to live in harmony with the universe.
    • Its significance lies in the call for protection and nurturing, both for ourselves and the world around us.
    • By embodying the principles of truth, interconnectedness, and respect for nature, we can create a more sustainable and harmonious life.
