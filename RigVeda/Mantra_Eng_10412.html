हत्वा य देवाः असुरान् यद् आयन देवाः देवत्वम् अभिरक्षमानाः ||
hatvāya devā asurān yad āyan devā devatvam abhirakṣamāṇāḥ ||
  • Hatvāya: This term translates to "having killed" or "after killing." It signifies the act of overcoming or defeating something that is considered negative or harmful.
  • Devā: This means "gods" or "divine beings." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the positive forces or energies in the universe.
  • Asurān: This translates to "demons" or "negative forces." It represents the obstacles, challenges, or evil influences that one may encounter in life.
  • Yad: This word means "that" or "which," indicating a connection or relationship between the preceding and following elements.
  • Āyan: This term can be interpreted as "approaching" or "coming towards." It suggests a movement towards a goal or a state of being.
  • Devā: Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the presence of divine forces.
  • Devatvam: This means "divinity" or "the state of being divine." It signifies the qualities and attributes associated with the divine.
  • Abhirakṣamāṇāḥ: This translates to "protecting" or "guarding." It indicates the act of safeguarding or ensuring the well-being of something.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks to the victory of divine forces over negative influences. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges and obstacles (asurān) through the support and protection of divine energies (devā). The act of "killing" in this context symbolizes the removal or defeat of negativity, leading to a state of divinity and protection.

  • Real-Life Example: In a practical scenario, consider a person facing significant challenges in their career, such as workplace conflicts or personal doubts. By seeking guidance and support from mentors (representing divine forces), they can overcome these obstacles. The mantra serves as a reminder that with determination and the right support, one can defeat negativity and achieve success.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its message of empowerment and protection. It encourages individuals to confront and overcome their challenges with the help of positive forces. In real life, this can be applied in various situations, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or even societal issues. By invoking the essence of this mantra, one can cultivate resilience and a sense of divine support in their journey towards success and well-being.
