हृदिस्पृशस्त आसेते विश्वेषु सोम धामसु |अधā कामा इमे मम वि वो मदे वि तिष्ठन्ते वसूयवो विवक्षसे ||
hṛdispṛśas ta āsate viśveṣu soma dhāmasu | adhā kāmā ime mama vi vo made vi tiṣṭhante vasūyavo vivakṣase ||
  • hṛdispṛśas: This term can be broken down into two parts: "hṛd," meaning heart or mind, and "spṛśas," meaning to touch or to influence. Together, it signifies the influence or touch upon the heart or mind.
  • ta āsate: "ta" refers to "they" or "those," and "āsate" means to dwell or reside. This phrase suggests that those who are influenced in their hearts are present or dwelling.
  • viśveṣu: This word means "in all" or "in every." It indicates a universal presence or influence.
  • soma dhāmasu: "soma" refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "dhāmasu" means in the realms or abodes. Thus, this phrase can be interpreted as the divine influences present in all realms.
  • adhā: This means "below" or "under." It suggests a foundation or basis for what follows.
  • kāmā: This term translates to "desires" or "wishes." It indicates the aspirations or longings of individuals.
  • ime mama: "ime" means "these," and "mama" means "my." Together, they refer to the desires that belong to the speaker.
  • vi vo: "vi" means "in" or "among," and "vo" means "you" or "your." This phrase suggests a connection or relationship with others.
  • made: This term translates to "joy" or "delight." It signifies a state of happiness or bliss.
  • vi tiṣṭhante: "vi" again means "in" or "among," and "tiṣṭhante" means "to stand" or "to remain." This indicates a state of being or presence.
  • vasūyavo: This term can be interpreted as "those who are prosperous" or "wealthy." It refers to individuals who possess abundance.
  • vivakṣase: This means "you wish" or "you desire." It indicates an intention or aspiration.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the universal influence of divine inspiration and the desires that dwell within the hearts of individuals. It emphasizes the connection between personal aspirations and the broader cosmic or divine realms. In real life, this can be applied to the pursuit of one's goals and dreams, recognizing that our desires are not isolated but are part of a larger tapestry of existence. The mantra encourages individuals to align their personal aspirations with a sense of universal purpose, fostering a deeper connection with others and the divine. It serves as a reminder that our desires can lead to joy and prosperity when they are in harmony with the greater good.
