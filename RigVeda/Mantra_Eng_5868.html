शुचिं नु स्तोमं नवजातम अद्येन्द्राग्नि वृत्रहणा जुषेथाम् |उभा हि वां सुहवा जोहवीमि ता वाजं सद्य उषते धेष्ठा ||
śuciṁ nu stomaṁ navajātam adyendrāgnī vṛtrahaṇā juṣethām | ubhā hi vāṁ suhavā johavīmi tā vājaṁ sadya uśate dheṣṭhā ||
  • śuciṁ nu stomaṁ navajātam: This phrase can be translated as "pure and new praise." The term "śuciṁ" refers to purity, while "stomaṁ" signifies a hymn or praise. "Navajātam" means newly born or fresh. This suggests the importance of purity in our thoughts and actions, as well as the value of new beginnings.
  • adyendrāgnī vṛtrahaṇā juṣethām: This part translates to "may the Indra and Agni, the slayer of Vritra, be pleased." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification. Vritra represents obstacles or negativity. This line emphasizes the need for divine support in overcoming challenges.
  • ubhā hi vāṁ suhavā johavīmi: This translates to "I offer you both, the beautiful ones." The term "ubhā" means both, and "suhavā" refers to beauty or goodness. This indicates a desire to offer something valuable and beautiful to the divine, reflecting the importance of gratitude and respect in our lives.
  • tā vājaṁ sadya uśate dheṣṭhā: This can be interpreted as "may that strength come to us quickly." "Vājaṁ" refers to strength or power, and "sadya" means quickly or immediately. This line expresses a longing for immediate support and empowerment from the divine, highlighting the urgency of our needs in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, new beginnings, divine support, gratitude, and the urgency of seeking strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as starting a new project, seeking guidance during challenging times, or expressing gratitude for the blessings we receive. By invoking these principles, individuals can cultivate a positive mindset and foster resilience in the face of adversity. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek purity in our intentions, appreciate the beauty around us, and actively pursue strength and support from the divine and our community.
