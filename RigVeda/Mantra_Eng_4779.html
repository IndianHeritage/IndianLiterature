यस्य विश्वानि हस्तयोः उचुर वसूनि न द्विता |वीरस्य पृथनाशः ||
yasya viśvāni hastayor ūcur vasūni ni dvitā | vīrasya pṛtanāṣahaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • viśvāni: all (things)
    • hastayor: of the hands
    • ūcur: have spoken
    • vasūni: the wealth
    • ni: not
    • dvitā: second
    • vīrasya: of the hero
    • pṛtanāṣahaḥ: the one who is victorious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine figure whose hands are capable of manifesting all forms of wealth and prosperity.
    • The phrase "have spoken" suggests that this wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom and knowledge.
    • The term "not second" implies that this wealth is unique and unparalleled, emphasizing the singularity of the divine source.
    • The reference to "the hero" indicates that this wealth is accessible to those who are courageous and virtuous.
    • The term "victorious" suggests that success comes to those who align themselves with this divine source of wealth and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to entrepreneurs who seek to create wealth through innovation and ethical practices. Their "hands" symbolize their efforts and actions in the marketplace.
    • In personal development, individuals who strive for knowledge and wisdom can find that their efforts lead to a wealth of opportunities and experiences, reflecting the idea of "all things" being within reach.
    • In community service, those who act heroically to uplift others can experience a unique form of wealth—social capital and respect—demonstrating that true richness comes from selfless actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions with divine principles to access true wealth and success.
    • It serves as a reminder that courage, wisdom, and ethical behavior are essential in the pursuit of prosperity.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life, both materially and spiritually.
