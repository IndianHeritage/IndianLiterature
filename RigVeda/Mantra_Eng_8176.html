पुनानः सोम धारयन्दो विश्वा अप श्रिधः |जहि रक्षांसी सुक्रतो ||
punānaḥ soma dhārayendo viśvā apa sridhaḥ | jahi rakṣāṁsi sukrato ||
  • punānaḥ: This word can be translated as "purifying" or "refreshing." In a real-life context, this can refer to the act of cleansing oneself, both physically and spiritually. For example, taking a moment to meditate or engage in a ritual can serve as a purification process, helping individuals to clear their minds and focus on positive thoughts.
  • soma: Soma is often associated with a divine drink or elixir that grants immortality and enlightenment. In modern terms, this can be likened to experiences or substances that elevate our consciousness, such as art, music, or nature. For instance, spending time in nature can rejuvenate the spirit and provide a sense of connection to something greater.
  • dhārayendo: This term means "holding" or "sustaining." It signifies the importance of maintaining balance and stability in life. In practical terms, this could relate to the need for emotional resilience during challenging times, such as coping with loss or stress. For example, practicing mindfulness can help individuals sustain their mental well-being.
  • viśvā: This word translates to "all" or "universal." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another during crises, highlighting the importance of unity and collective strength.
  • apa: This term means "away" or "from." It can signify the act of distancing oneself from negativity or harmful influences. For example, setting boundaries in relationships or environments that are toxic can be a practical application of this concept, allowing for personal growth and well-being.
  • sridhaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "prosperous" or "wealthy." It reflects the idea of abundance in life, not just in material terms but also in love, health, and happiness. In real life, this can be seen in practices of gratitude, where individuals focus on appreciating what they have, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • jahi: This means "to conquer" or "to overcome." It suggests the importance of overcoming obstacles and challenges. In practical terms, this could relate to personal struggles, such as overcoming fears or addictions. For instance, someone who has battled with anxiety may find strength in therapy and support groups.
  • rakṣāṁsi: This term refers to "protections" or "guardians." It highlights the need for safeguarding oneself from harm, whether physical, emotional, or spiritual. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of self-care practices, such as seeking therapy or engaging in activities that promote mental health.
  • sukrato: This word means "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." It emphasizes the significance of living a life of integrity and kindness. In everyday life, this can manifest in acts of charity, volunteering, or simply being kind to others, which contributes to a more compassionate society.

Conclusion: The mantra "punānaḥ soma dhārayendo viśvā apa sridhaḥ | jahi rakṣāṁsi sukrato ||" encapsulates profound wisdom about purification, sustenance, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek purity in thoughts and actions, to sustain oneself through challenges, and to foster a sense of community and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in self-care, practice gratitude, and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
