आ नो ब्रह्माणि मरुतः समन्यवो नरां न शंसः सवनानि गन्तना | अश्वाम् इव पिप्यत धेनुम ऊधनि कर्ता धियं जारित्रे वाजपेशसम् ||
ā no brahmāṇi marutaḥ samanyavo narāṁ na śaṁsaḥ savanāni gantana | aśvām iva pipyata dhenum ūdhani kartā dhiyaṁ jaritre vājapeśasam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we be blessed
    • brahmāṇi: with the divine knowledge
    • marutaḥ: O Winds (or Storms)
    • samanyavo: who are united
    • narāṁ: with mankind
    • na: not
    • śaṁsaḥ: causing harm
    • savanāni: in the offerings
    • gantana: to be fulfilled
    • aśvām: like horses
    • iva: as if
    • pipyata: may they be nourished
    • dhenum: like a cow
    • ūdhani: in the waters
    • kartā: the doer
    • dhiyaṁ: of intellect
    • jaritre: in the world
    • vājapeśasam: the one who brings strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly the Maruts, who are the personifications of the storm winds and are associated with strength and vitality.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony among mankind, urging the divine forces to bless humanity with knowledge and protection.
    • The imagery of horses and cows suggests the nurturing aspect of nature, where the divine forces are seen as providers of sustenance and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during gatherings or festivals where people come together to celebrate unity and seek blessings for collective well-being.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra during times of uncertainty or conflict, seeking guidance and protection from negative influences.
    • In environmental contexts, it can serve as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and nature, encouraging sustainable practices that honor the divine forces of the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in fostering unity, strength, and protection among humanity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community gatherings, reminding us of our connection to the divine and to each other.
    • By invoking the Maruts, we seek not only personal growth but also the collective upliftment of society, emphasizing the need for harmony and cooperation in our daily lives.
