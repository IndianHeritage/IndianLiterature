वृषा वृष्णे दुदुहे दोहसा दिवः पयांसि यह्वो अदितेर अदाभ्यः |विश्वं स वेद वरुणो यथा धियाऽस यज्ञियो यजतु यज्ञियाम् ऋतून् ||
vṛṣā vṛṣṇe duduhe dohasā divaḥ payāṁsi yahvo aditer adābhyaḥ | viśvaṁ sa veda varuṇo yathā dhiyā sa yajñiyo yajatu yajñiyām̐ ṛtūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: The one who is strong or powerful.
    • vṛṣṇe: Pertaining to the Vrishnis, a clan of warriors.
    • duduhe: To extract or draw out.
    • dohasā: From the source of nourishment.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • payāṁsi: The nourishing substances or milk.
    • yahvo: Who is the source or origin.
    • aditer: Of Aditi, the mother of gods.
    • adābhyaḥ: From the unbounded or infinite.
    • viśvaṁ: The whole universe or all.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • veda: Knows or understands.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order.
    • yathā: As or in the manner.
    • dhiyā: With wisdom or intellect.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • yajñiyo: The one who is worthy of worship or sacrifice.
    • yajatu: May he perform or offer.
    • yajñiyām̐: Of the sacrifices or offerings.
    • ṛtūn: The seasons or cycles.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that nourishes and sustains life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in the performance of rituals and sacrifices.
    • The reference to Aditi signifies the infinite nature of the universe and the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • Varuna represents the cosmic order, suggesting that all actions should align with universal principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be seen as a call to respect the natural cycles and seasons, ensuring that farming practices are sustainable and in harmony with nature.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, drawing from the infinite sources of learning available to them.
    • In community rituals, it highlights the importance of collective offerings and the shared responsibility of maintaining harmony and balance in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of aligning our actions with the greater cosmic order.
    • It serves as a reminder to draw upon the wisdom of the universe and to perform our duties with intention and respect.
    • In real life, it encourages us to nurture our relationships, respect the environment, and seek knowledge, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
