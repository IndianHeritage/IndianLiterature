एभिर न इन्द्राहभिर दशस्य दुरमित्रासो हि क्षितयः पवन्ते |प्रत यच चष्टे अनृतम अनेनाऽऽव द्वितā वरुणो मायī नः सात् ||
ebhir na indrāhabhir daśasya durmitrāso hi kṣitayaḥ pavante | prati yac caṣṭe anṛtam anenā ava dvitā varuṇo māyī naḥ sāt ||
  • Translation: "With these (powers), O Indra, may we overcome the tenfold enemies; for they are indeed the evil ones on the earth. Whatever falsehood moves about, may Varuna, the magician, remove it from us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ebhir: with these (powers or forces)
    • na: not
    • indrāhabhir: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • daśasya: of the tenfold (enemies)
    • durmitrāso: evil ones or adversaries
    • hi: indeed
    • kṣitayaḥ: on the earth
    • pavante: may they be blown away or removed
    • prati: whatever
    • yac: that which
    • caṣṭe: moves about
    • anṛtam: falsehood or untruth
    • anenā: by this
    • ava: may it be removed
    • dvitā: the second or dual
    • varuṇo: O Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • māyī: magician or illusionist
    • naḥ: from us
    • sāt: may it be
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of Indra and Varuna to protect against adversaries and falsehoods.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a plea for strength and clarity in the face of challenges, whether they be personal, social, or professional.
    • For example, in a competitive work environment, one might feel surrounded by deceitful practices or unethical behavior. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek truth and integrity while relying on inner strength to overcome such challenges.
  • Significance:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and adversaries.
    • It highlights the need for truthfulness and the rejection of falsehood in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to cultivate resilience and moral integrity, which are essential for personal and communal harmony.
