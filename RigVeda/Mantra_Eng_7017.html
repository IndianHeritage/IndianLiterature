आ नः स्तोमम् उप द्रवद धियानो अश्वो न सोत्रिभिः |यं ते स्वधावन स्वदायन्ति धेनव इन्द्र कण्वेषु रातयः ||
ā naḥ stomam upa dravad dhiyāno aśvo na sotṛbhiḥ | yaṁ te svadhāvan svadayanti dhenava indra kaṇveṣu rātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards, for
    • naḥ: our
    • stomam: hymn, praise
    • upa: near, close
    • dravad: flowing, moving
    • dhi-yāno: with intelligence, with thought
    • aśvo: horse
    • na: not
    • soṭṛbhiḥ: with the singers, with the bards
    • yaṁ: whom
    • te: you
    • svadhāvan: who are self-sustaining
    • svadayanti: nourish, sustain
    • dhenava: cows, nurturing beings
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • kaṇveṣu: in the families of Kanva
    • rātayaḥ: the rulers, the leaders
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, asking for his blessings and support.
    • The phrase "ā naḥ stomam upa dravad" suggests a desire for our praises to flow towards Indra, indicating the importance of devotion and recognition of divine power.
    • The mention of "aśvo" (horse) symbolizes strength and vitality, often associated with success and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • By invoking "svadhāvan svadayanti," the mantra emphasizes the idea of self-sustenance and nourishment, which can be interpreted as a call for the divine to provide for the community and ensure their well-being.
    • The reference to "dhenava" (cows) highlights the significance of nurturing and sustenance, as cows were considered sacred and vital for agricultural societies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to celebrate and express gratitude for the resources and support they receive from nature and the divine.
    • In agricultural practices, invoking the blessings of deities like Indra can be seen during rituals for rain and fertility, reflecting the reliance on divine forces for sustenance.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, seeking guidance and support in their endeavors, much like how one might turn to mentors or leaders for assistance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and recognizing the interconnectedness of community, nature, and spirituality.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the need for nourishment—both physical and spiritual—and the importance of expressing gratitude for the blessings received.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to cultivate a sense of community, to honor the sources of our sustenance, and to remain open to the guidance of higher powers in our lives.
