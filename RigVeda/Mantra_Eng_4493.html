वयं उ त्वा गृहपते जनानाम् अग्ने अकर्म समिधा बृहन्तम् |अस्थूरी नो गार्हपत्यानि सन्तु तिग्मेन नस् तेजसा सं शिशाधि ||
vayam u tvā gṛhapate janānām agne akarma samidhā bṛhantam | asthūri no gārhapatyāni santu tigmena nas tejasā saṁ śiśādhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayam: We
    • u: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • gṛhapate: O Lord of the House
    • janānām: of the people
    • agne: O Agni (Fire)
    • akarma: without action
    • samidhā: kindled
    • bṛhantam: the great one
    • asthūri: may there be
    • no: for us
    • gārhapatyāni: of the household
    • santu: may they be
    • tigmena: with brilliance
    • nas: for us
    • tejasā: with energy
    • saṁ: together
    • śiśādhi: may you dwell
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of Agni in household rituals and the well-being of the community.
    • The phrase "O Lord of the House" signifies the central role of Agni in domestic life, symbolizing warmth, light, and sustenance.
    • The request for "kindled without action" suggests a desire for a fire that brings prosperity and abundance without the need for excessive effort.
    • The mantra also expresses a wish for brilliance and energy to dwell within the household, fostering a nurturing environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in a family gathering, lighting a candle or a fireplace can create a warm and inviting atmosphere, similar to the role of Agni in the mantra.
    • In modern households, the concept of "home" often revolves around creating a space filled with love, warmth, and energy, akin to the blessings sought in this mantra.
    • During festivals, lighting lamps or bonfires is a common practice, representing the invocation of divine energy and the dispelling of darkness, paralleling the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a vital force in our lives, representing not just physical fire but also the warmth of relationships and the energy that sustains us.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate a nurturing environment in our homes and communities, where love, prosperity, and positive energy can thrive.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to create spaces that foster connection and well-being, making it relevant in various aspects of modern life, from family dynamics to community gatherings.
