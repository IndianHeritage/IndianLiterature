द्युक्षं सुदानुं तविषीभिर आवृतं गिरिं न पुरुभोजसम् |क्षुमन्तं वाजं शतिनं सहस्रिणं मक्षू गोमन्तं इमहे ||
dyukṣaṁ sudānuṁ taviṣībhir āvṛtaṁ giriṁ na purubhojasam | kṣumantaṁ vājaṁ śatinaṁ sahasriṇam makṣū gomantam īmahe ||
  • dyukṣaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is bright" or "the one who shines." In real life, this can refer to individuals who possess knowledge, wisdom, or enlightenment, much like a teacher or a mentor who illuminates the path for others.
  • sudānuṁ: This term means "well-fed" or "nourished." It signifies abundance and prosperity. In a modern context, it can relate to individuals or communities that have access to resources, education, and opportunities that allow them to thrive.
  • taviṣībhir: This word translates to "with strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength in overcoming challenges. For example, athletes train rigorously to build physical strength, which can be a metaphor for mental and emotional resilience in everyday life.
  • āvṛtaṁ: This means "surrounded" or "enveloped." It can symbolize being in a protective environment. In real life, this could refer to a supportive family or community that nurtures and protects its members.
  • giriṁ: This word means "mountain." Mountains often symbolize stability and permanence. In life, they can represent the challenges we face, which require effort and determination to overcome, much like climbing a mountain.
  • na purubhojasam: This phrase can be interpreted as "not to be neglected" or "not to be overlooked." It highlights the importance of recognizing and valuing what is essential in life, such as relationships, health, and personal growth.
  • kṣumantaṁ: This term means "hungry" or "desiring." It reflects the human condition of longing for fulfillment, whether it be in terms of material needs, emotional connections, or spiritual growth.
  • vājaṁ: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the importance of abundance in various forms, such as financial stability, emotional richness, and fulfilling relationships.
  • śatinaṁ: This means "of the rich" or "of the prosperous." It emphasizes the value of wealth, not just in material terms but also in the richness of experiences and relationships.
  • sahasriṇam: This term means "thousandfold" or "multiplied." It signifies abundance and the idea that prosperity can grow exponentially when nurtured properly.
  • makṣū: This word translates to "the bee." Bees are often associated with hard work, community, and the production of honey, which symbolizes sweetness and nourishment in life.
  • gomantam: This term means "possessing cows" or "wealthy." In ancient times, cattle were a symbol of wealth and prosperity, representing sustenance and livelihood.
  • īmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we seek." It signifies a call to action, encouraging individuals to actively seek out abundance and prosperity in their lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking abundance, strength, and nourishment in life. It emphasizes the importance of being surrounded by supportive environments, recognizing the value of what we have, and striving for prosperity in all aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, community building, and the pursuit of goals. By invoking these qualities, individuals can create a fulfilling and prosperous life, much like a bee that works diligently to produce honey, symbolizing the sweetness of success and abundance.
