विजानीहि आर्याण ये च दस्यवो बरहिष्मते रन्धया शासद अव्रतान् |शाकी भव यजमानस्य चोदिता विश्वेत ता ते सदहमादेषु चाकन ||
vi jānīhy āryān ye ca dasyavo barhiṣmate randhayā śāsad avratān | śākī bhava yajamānasya coditā viśvet tā te sadhamādeṣu cākana ||
  • Translation: "Let those who are noble and those who are of the Dasyu (non-Aryan) class, who are engaged in sacrificial rites, be known. May they be guided by the one who is the master of the sacrifice, and may they be inspired by the universal spirit to achieve the highest good."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi jānīhy: Let us know or recognize.
    • āryān: Noble or honorable people.
    • ye ca: And those who.
    • dasyavo: Those who are considered non-Aryan or of a lower class.
    • barhiṣmate: Engaged in sacrificial rites.
    • randhayā: By the one who is the master or lord.
    • śāsad: To guide or direct.
    • avratān: Those who are committed or devoted.
    • śākī: Inspired or motivated.
    • bhava: Become or be.
    • yajamānasya: Of the one who performs the sacrifice.
    • coditā: Inspired or urged.
    • viśvet: Universal or all-encompassing.
    • tā: Those.
    • te: You or them.
    • sadhamādeṣu: In the highest good or welfare.
    • cākana: To achieve or attain.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and valuing both noble individuals and those from marginalized backgrounds. In modern society, this can be seen in movements advocating for social justice, where the voices of all individuals, regardless of their background, are acknowledged and uplifted.
    • The concept of guiding and inspiring individuals towards a common good can be applied in community service initiatives, where leaders motivate volunteers to work together for the welfare of society.
    • In the workplace, fostering an inclusive environment where all employees feel valued and inspired to contribute can lead to greater innovation and success, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and recognition of all individuals, regardless of their social standing. It encourages us to guide and inspire one another towards achieving the highest good for all. In real-life scenarios, this principle can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, workplace inclusivity, and social justice movements, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and equitable society.
