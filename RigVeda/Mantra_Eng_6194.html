यूयं हि ष्ठा सुदानवो रुद्रा ऋभुक्षणो दमे |उत प्रचेतसो मदे ||
yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā sudānavo rudrā ṛbhukṣaṇo dame | uta pracetaso made ||
  • yūyaṁ hi - "You all indeed": This phrase addresses a collective group, emphasizing unity and togetherness.
  • ṣṭhā - "stand" or "be established": This word signifies stability and presence, suggesting the importance of being grounded.
  • Sudānavo - "well-fed" or "nourished": This term implies abundance and prosperity, indicating that the group is in a state of well-being.
  • Rudrā - "O Rudras": Referring to the fierce deities associated with storms and the hunt, symbolizing power and transformation.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇo - "those who enjoy the offerings": This word highlights the act of receiving and enjoying the fruits of one's labor or devotion.
  • dame - "in the home" or "in the dwelling": This signifies the importance of the household and the sanctity of home life.
  • uta - "and" or "also": A conjunction that connects thoughts, indicating continuity and addition.
  • pracetaso - "of the wise" or "of the intelligent": This term refers to those who possess wisdom and insight, suggesting the value of knowledge.
  • made - "in joy" or "in ecstasy": This word conveys a sense of happiness and fulfillment, emphasizing the emotional aspect of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, stability, and prosperity. It highlights the importance of being grounded in one's home while also celebrating the joy that comes from wisdom and shared experiences. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as family gatherings, community events, or even in workplaces where collaboration and mutual support are essential. The mantra serves as a reminder that when individuals come together with a sense of purpose and joy, they can create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and happiness for all.
