यथा चित कण्वम आवतम प्रियमेधम उपस्तुतम् |अत्रिं शiñजाम अश्विनाः ||
yathā cit kaṇvam āvatam priyamedham upastutam | atriṁ śiñjāram aśvinā ||
  • yathā - This word translates to "as" or "in the way that." It sets the context for the following actions or qualities being described.
  • cit - Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it refers to the state of being aware or enlightened.
  • kaṇvam - This term can be interpreted as "small" or "minute," often referring to the subtle aspects of existence.
  • āvatam - This means "to protect" or "to shelter," indicating a sense of care or guardianship.
  • priyamedham - This can be broken down into "priya" (beloved) and "medham" (intellect or understanding), suggesting a cherished wisdom or knowledge.
  • upastutam - This means "to be praised" or "to be honored," indicating the act of reverence or acknowledgment.
  • atriṁ - Referring to "Atri," a sage or a revered figure in Vedic literature, symbolizing wisdom and guidance.
  • śiñjāram - This term can be interpreted as "to flow" or "to move," often associated with the flow of life or energy.
  • aśvinā - Referring to the "Aśvins," the twin deities of health and medicine, representing healing and vitality.

The mantra can be understood as a call for protection and guidance in the pursuit of knowledge and consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of cherishing wisdom and honoring those who guide us, like the sage Atri and the healing Aśvins.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: In a learning environment, students can invoke this mantra to seek protection and guidance from their teachers, cherishing the knowledge imparted to them.
  • Health and Well-being: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking healing or support during times of illness, invoking the Aśvins for vitality and recovery.
  • Personal Growth: It can serve as a reminder to protect one's consciousness and awareness, encouraging mindfulness and the pursuit of understanding in daily life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection, wisdom, and reverence for the guiding forces in our lives. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their journeys with greater awareness and appreciation for the knowledge and health that sustain them.
