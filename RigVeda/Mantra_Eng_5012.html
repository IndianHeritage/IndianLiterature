आपप्रुषी पार्थिवान्य उरु रजो अन्तरिक्षम् |सरस्वती निदस पातु ||
āpapruṣī pārthivāny uru rajo antarikṣam | sarasvatī nidas pātu ||
  • Mantra: "āpapruṣī pārthivāny uru rajo antarikṣam | sarasvatī nidas pātu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpapruṣī: One who is free from sin or fault.
    • pārthivāny: Earthly or related to the earth.
    • uru: Vast or expansive.
    • rajo: Dust or particles.
    • antarikṣam: The space or atmosphere.
    • sarasvatī: The goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and learning.
    • nidas: Protection or shelter.
    • pātu: May protect or safeguard.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess Sarasvatī, asking for her protection and guidance.
    • The phrase "āpapruṣī pārthivāny" suggests a connection to the earthly realm, emphasizing purity and the need for a faultless existence.
    • "Uru rajo antarikṣam" refers to the vastness of the universe and the particles that fill it, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all things.
    • By calling upon Sarasvatī, the mantra seeks wisdom and clarity in navigating the complexities of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In academic pursuits, students often recite this mantra before exams or important presentations, seeking Sarasvatī's blessings for knowledge and clarity.
    • Artists and musicians may invoke this mantra to inspire creativity and ensure their work is free from flaws.
    • In daily life, individuals may reflect on the mantra when facing challenges, reminding themselves to seek purity in their actions and thoughts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of seeking wisdom and purity in our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder of the vastness of knowledge and the need for guidance in our earthly journey.
    • By invoking Sarasvatī, we align ourselves with the pursuit of knowledge and the aspiration to live a life free from faults, applicable in education, creativity, and personal growth.
