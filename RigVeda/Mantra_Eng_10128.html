व्य आर्य इन्द्र तनुहि श्रवांस्य ओजः स्थिरेव धन्वनोऽभिमातिः |अस्मद्र्यग वावृद्धानः सहोभिर अनिभृष्टस्तन्वं वावृद्धस्व ||
vy a1rya indra tanuhi śravāṁsy ojaḥ sthireva dhanvano 'bhimātīḥ | asmadryag vāvṛdhānaḥ sahobhir anibhṛṣṭas tanvaṁ vāvṛdhasva ||
  • Mantra: "vy a1rya indra tanuhi śravāṁsy ojaḥ sthireva dhanvano 'bhimātīḥ | asmadryag vāvṛdhānaḥ sahobhir anibhṛṣṭas tanvaṁ vāvṛdhasva ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vy: O
    • a1rya: noble or worthy
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • tanuhi: in your body
    • śravāṁsy: of fame or glory
    • ojaḥ: strength or energy
    • sthireva: as firm or stable
    • dhanvano: of the wealthy or prosperous
    • with great intention or purpose
    • asmadryag: our strength
    • vāvṛdhānaḥ: may it increase
    • sahobhir: with companions
    • anibhṛṣṭas: unshaken or undisturbed
    • tanvaṁ: body or form
    • vāvṛdhasva: may you grow or expand
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The invocation of "noble Indra" emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from a higher power that embodies virtues such as nobility and strength.
    • The phrase "in your body" suggests a connection between the divine and the physical realm, indicating that divine qualities can manifest in our lives.
    • By asking for "strength" and "energy," the mantra highlights the need for inner power to face challenges, much like athletes who prepare mentally and physically for competitions.
    • The call for stability ("as firm or stable") reflects the desire for resilience in the face of adversity, akin to how a tree stands strong against the wind.
    • The mention of "wealthy" and "prosperous" can be interpreted as a request for abundance in various forms, whether material, emotional, or spiritual.
    • Finally, the plea for growth and companionship emphasizes the importance of community and support in achieving one's goals, similar to how teamwork is essential in sports or collaborative projects.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, community, and divine guidance in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek inner strength and stability while fostering relationships that support our growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork in professional settings, or even in spiritual practices where one seeks to align with higher virtues.
    • Ultimately, it inspires individuals to cultivate resilience and seek support from both the divine and their community to thrive in their endeavors.
