अभिक्रन्दन कलशं वाज्य अर्षति पतिर्विद्वः शतधारो विचक्षणः |हरिर मित्रस्य सदनेषु सिदति मार्मृजानो 'विभिः सिन्धुभिर वृषा ||
abhikrandan kalaśaṁ vājy arṣati patir divaḥ śatadhāro vicakṣaṇaḥ | harir mitrasya sadaneṣu sīdati marmṛjāno 'vibhiḥ sindhubhir vṛṣā ||
  • Mantra: "abhikrandan kalaśaṁ vājy arṣati patir divaḥ śatadhāro vicakṣaṇaḥ | harir mitrasya sadaneṣu sīdati marmṛjāno 'vibhiḥ sindhubhir vṛṣā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhikrandan: calling out or invoking
    • kalaśaṁ: the pot or vessel, often symbolizing abundance
    • vājy: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • arṣati: to rise or ascend
    • patir: lord or master
    • divaḥ: of the heavens or sky
    • śatadhāro: one who holds a hundred (often referring to abundance or wealth)
    • vicakṣaṇaḥ: wise or perceptive
    • harir: the one who brings joy or happiness
    • mitrasya: of the friend or ally
    • sadaneṣu: in the abodes or homes
    • sīdati: resides or dwells
    • marmṛjāno: one who removes obstacles or difficulties
    • 'vibhiḥ: without fear
    • sindhubhir: with the oceans or waters
    • vṛṣā: the one who is powerful or strong
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine presence, calling upon a powerful lord who is wise and perceptive.
    • The reference to "kalaśaṁ" symbolizes abundance and prosperity, suggesting that the divine is a source of wealth and nourishment.
    • The phrase "śatadhāro" indicates that this divine being holds immense power and resources, capable of providing for many.
    • In real life, this can be likened to a leader or a mentor who supports and guides their community, ensuring that everyone has access to resources and opportunities.
    • The mention of "harir mitrasya sadaneṣu" emphasizes the importance of friendship and community, suggesting that the divine presence dwells among those who are united and supportive of one another.
    • The idea of "marmṛjāno 'vibhiḥ" speaks to the removal of fears and obstacles, which can be seen in the role of a counselor or a supportive friend who helps others navigate challenges in life.
    • Finally, the reference to "sindhubhir vṛṣā" highlights the strength and power that comes from unity and collaboration, akin to how communities can achieve great things when they work together.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance, community support, and the removal of obstacles in our lives.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek strength in unity, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community initiatives.
    • By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of abundance, wisdom, and fearlessness, encouraging a harmonious and prosperous life.
