अभि ते मधुनā पयो 'थर्वाणो अशिश्रयुः |देवं देवाय देवयु ||
abhi te madhunā payo 'tharvāṇo aśiśrayuḥ | devaṁ devāya devayu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • te: you
    • madhunā: with sweetness, with honey
    • payo: milk
    • 'tharvāṇo: nourishing, sustaining
    • aśiśrayuḥ: providing support, shelter
    • devaṁ: the divine, God
    • devāya: to the divine, for the God
    • devayu: to the divine essence, the divine nature
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence, asking for nourishment and support.
    • The use of "madhunā payo" symbolizes the sweetness and richness of life, akin to how honey and milk are nourishing and vital for sustenance.
    • The phrase "devaṁ devāya devayu" emphasizes the connection to the divine, suggesting that the offerings or prayers are directed towards a higher power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the act of seeking guidance and support from a higher power during challenging times, much like how one might turn to a mentor or a trusted friend.
    • In rituals, offering milk and honey to deities symbolizes gratitude and the desire for blessings, reflecting the nurturing aspects of these elements.
    • In a broader sense, it can be seen in community gatherings where people come together to share resources and support each other, embodying the essence of nourishment and shelter.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support and nourishment in our lives.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and the material world, reminding us to appreciate the sweetness and sustenance that life offers.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and to seek help from both the divine and their community, fostering a supportive environment.
