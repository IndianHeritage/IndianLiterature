त्वं मानेब्य इन्द्र विश्वजन्या रदा मरुद्भिः शुरुधो गोAGRāḥ |स्तवानेभिः स्तवसे देव देवैर विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
tvam mānebhya indra viśvajanyā radā marudbhiḥ śurudho goagrāḥ | stavānebhiḥ stavase deva devair vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • tvam - You
  • mānebhya - To those who are worthy
  • indra - O Indra, the king of gods
  • viśvajanyā - Who is the source of all creation
  • radā - With strength
  • marudbhiḥ - With the Maruts (storm deities)
  • śurudho - Who is powerful
  • goagrāḥ - The best among the cattle (symbolizing wealth and prosperity)
  • stavānebhiḥ - With praises
  • stavase - We praise
  • deva - O divine one
  • devair - By the gods
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ - This knowledge
  • vṛjanaṁ - The protection
  • jīradānum - Bestow upon us

This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, asking for strength, protection, and prosperity. It acknowledges Indra's role as a source of creation and power, invoking the support of the Maruts, who are associated with storms and strength. The mention of "goagrāḥ" symbolizes the importance of wealth and abundance, often represented by cattle in ancient times.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving success. For instance, in a business context, one might recite this mantra to seek guidance and strength in making important decisions or during times of uncertainty. Similarly, students may use it to invoke focus and clarity while preparing for exams.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and strength. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking help from higher powers in our endeavors. In various aspects of life, whether personal, professional, or academic, this mantra encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and the support available to them, fostering a sense of confidence and resilience.
