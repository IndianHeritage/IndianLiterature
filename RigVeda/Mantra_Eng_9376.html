यत् ते समुद्रम अर्णवम् मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत् त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जीवसे ||
yat te samudram arṇavam mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • samudram: ocean
    • arṇavam: vastness or depth
    • mano: mind
    • jagāma: has gone
    • dūrakam: far away
    • tat: that
    • ta: you
    • ā: indeed
    • vartayāmasi: we are moving or we are existing
    • ha: indeed
    • kṣayāya: for destruction or for the end
    • jīvase: for life or for living
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the vastness of the ocean and the mind's journey into the depths of thought and existence.
    • The phrase "yat te samudram arṇavam" suggests that the ocean represents the vastness of knowledge and consciousness.
    • "mano jagāma dūrakam" indicates that the mind can traverse great distances, exploring ideas and concepts that may seem far removed from our immediate reality.
    • The latter part, "tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase," emphasizes the importance of this exploration for the purpose of life and existence, suggesting that understanding and knowledge are essential for living fully.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often dive deep into subjects that may seem distant from their everyday lives, much like the mind exploring the ocean of knowledge.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on their thoughts and emotions, navigating through complex feelings to gain clarity and understanding.
    • In scientific research, scientists explore the vast unknowns of the universe, pushing the boundaries of what is known, akin to the mind's journey into the depths of the ocean.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of exploration and understanding in our lives.
    • It teaches us that the journey of the mind is crucial for personal growth and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in education, personal reflection, or scientific inquiry, the essence of this mantra encourages us to delve deeper into our thoughts and the world around us, ultimately enriching our lives.
