मन्त्र: न हि त्वा शूर देवाः न मर्तासो दित्सन्तम् |भीमं न गां वारयन्ते ||
nahi tvā śūra devā na martāso ditsantam | bhīmaṁ na gāṁ vārayante ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, the gods do not protect you, nor do mortals who desire you; they do not restrain the mighty one."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: indeed, not
    • tvā: you
    • śūra: brave, mighty
    • devā: gods
    • na: not
    • martāso: of mortals
    • ditsantam: desiring, wishing for
    • bhīmaṁ: the mighty one
    • na: not
    • gāṁ: the earth, the world
    • vārayante: restrain, protect

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a profound understanding of the nature of strength and protection.
    • It suggests that true strength does not come from divine intervention or the support of others, but rather from within oneself.
    • The phrase "the gods do not protect you" implies that reliance on external forces for safety or success may be misguided.
    • Similarly, "mortals who desire you" indicates that even those who wish to help may not have the power to truly protect you from challenges.
    • The "mighty one" represents an individual or force that cannot be restrained, emphasizing the idea that true power is inherent and cannot be easily controlled.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek external validation or support, but the mantra reminds us that inner strength and self-reliance are crucial for overcoming obstacles.
    • In competitive sports, athletes may rely on their training and mental fortitude rather than solely on the support of coaches or fans to achieve success.
    • In leadership, effective leaders understand that while they may have followers, their true strength lies in their vision and ability to inspire others from within.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-reliance and inner strength.
    • It encourages individuals to look within themselves for power and resilience rather than depending solely on external forces.
    • In various aspects of life, such as personal growth, sports, and leadership, the essence of this mantra can guide individuals to harness their true potential and face challenges with confidence.
