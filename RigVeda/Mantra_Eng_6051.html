पिबा सुतस्य रसिनो मत्स्वा न इन्द्र गोमताḥ |आपिर नो बोधि सदहमाद्यो वृधेऽस्मां अवन्तु ते धियः ||
pibā sutasya rasino matsvā na indra gomataḥ | āpir no bodhi sadhamādyo vṛdhe3 'smām̐ avantu te dhiyaḥ ||
  • pibā sutasya rasino: This phrase translates to "drink the essence of the son." Here, "sutasya" refers to the son or offspring, and "rasino" means essence or juice. In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as consuming the knowledge or wisdom passed down through generations, much like how we learn from our parents and ancestors.
  • matsvā na indra gomataḥ: This part means "may we not be deprived of the strength of Indra, the lord of cattle." Indra is often associated with power and prosperity. In modern terms, this can signify the importance of seeking strength and support from our community or society, akin to how we rely on teamwork and collaboration in various aspects of life.
  • āpir no bodhi sadhamādyo: This translates to "may the wise and good thoughts come to us." The word "bodhi" refers to wisdom or enlightenment, while "sadhamādyo" implies good or virtuous thoughts. In everyday life, this can be seen as a call to cultivate positive thinking and wisdom, which can lead to better decision-making and a more fulfilling life.
  • vṛdhe3 'smām̐ avantu te dhiyaḥ: This means "may your thoughts increase our understanding." Here, "vṛdhe" refers to growth or increase, and "dhiyaḥ" means thoughts or intellect. This can be related to the idea of continuous learning and personal development, emphasizing the importance of expanding our knowledge and understanding through education and experience.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, community support, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek guidance from their heritage, collaborate with others for strength, and foster positive thoughts for personal growth. This mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in educational settings, workplaces, and personal relationships, where the collective wisdom and support of a community can lead to greater achievements and a more enriched life.
