तवे पितो महानां देवानाम् मनो हितम् |अकारि चारु केतुनā तवाऽहिम् अवसावधीत् ||
tve pito mahānāṁ devānām mano hitam | akāri cāru ketunā tavāhim avasāvadhīt ||
  • tve pito: This phrase translates to "You are the father" or "You are the source." It signifies a divine origin or a higher power that nurtures and guides.
  • mahānāṁ: This means "of the great ones" or "of the mighty." It refers to the powerful beings or deities that hold significant influence in the universe.
  • devānām: This translates to "of the gods." It emphasizes the divine aspect and the connection to higher spiritual entities.
  • mano hitam: This means "the mind is beneficial" or "the mind is well-disposed." It suggests that a positive and constructive mindset is essential for well-being.
  • akāri: This translates to "has done" or "has created." It indicates the action of creation or manifestation.
  • cāru ketunā: This means "with a beautiful banner" or "with a splendid flag." It symbolizes victory, honor, and the display of one's achievements.
  • tavāhim: This translates to "of yours" or "belonging to you." It signifies possession or connection to the divine.
  • avasāvadhīt: This means "may it be established" or "may it be fulfilled." It expresses a desire for stability and realization of intentions.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine source, acknowledging the greatness of the gods and the importance of a positive mindset in achieving one's goals. It emphasizes the role of divine guidance in the process of creation and the significance of displaying one's achievements with pride.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: By recognizing the divine influence in our lives, we can cultivate a positive mindset that encourages personal development and resilience.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of the "great ones" by inspiring others and creating a vision that is beneficial for the community.
  • Celebration of Achievements: Just as the "beautiful banner" symbolizes victory, individuals and organizations can celebrate their accomplishments, fostering a sense of pride and motivation.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, the importance of a constructive mindset, and the celebration of achievements. It encourages individuals to seek guidance from higher powers while actively participating in their own growth and success.
