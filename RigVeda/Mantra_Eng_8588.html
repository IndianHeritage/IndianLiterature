प्र ते धारामधुमतीर असृग्न वारण यत पूतो अत्येष्य अव्यां | पवमान पवसे धाम गोनां जज्ञानाḥ सूर्यं अपिन्वो अर्कैः ||
pra te dhārā madhumatīr asṛgran vārān yat pūto atyeṣy avyān | pavamāna pavase dhāma gonāṁ jajñānaḥ sūryam apinvo arkaiḥ ||
  • pra te dhārā madhumatīr: This phrase can be translated as "May your flow be sweet and nourishing." The term "dhārā" refers to a flow or stream, while "madhumatīr" signifies something that is sweet or pleasant. In real life, this can be likened to the flow of positive energy or resources in our lives, such as love, knowledge, or prosperity.
  • asṛgran vārān: This part means "that which is pure and untainted." The word "asṛgran" indicates purity, and "vārān" refers to something that is excellent or virtuous. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of purity in our thoughts, actions, and relationships, striving to maintain integrity and honesty in our daily lives.
  • yat pūto atyeṣy avyān: This translates to "that which is pure and transcends all." Here, "yat" means "that," "pūto" signifies "pure," and "atyēṣy" implies something that goes beyond. This can be understood as the quest for higher consciousness or spiritual enlightenment, which many individuals seek through meditation, self-reflection, or philosophical inquiry.
  • pavamāna pavase dhāma: This phrase means "the purifying force that brings us to our true abode." "Pavamāna" refers to the purifying aspect, while "dhāma" signifies a place or abode. In real life, this can be seen in practices that cleanse the mind and body, such as yoga, mindfulness, or even engaging in nature, which helps individuals reconnect with their true selves.
  • gonāṁ jajñānaḥ: This translates to "the birth of the divine qualities." "Gonāṁ" refers to qualities or attributes, and "jajñānaḥ" means birth or emergence. This can be related to the development of virtues such as compassion, kindness, and wisdom in our lives, which are essential for personal growth and harmonious living.
  • sūryam apinvo arkaiḥ: This means "the sun, which is invoked through hymns." "Sūryam" refers to the sun, and "arkaiḥ" signifies hymns or praises. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how people celebrate the sun's life-giving energy through rituals, festivals, or simply by appreciating nature, recognizing the importance of gratitude in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking purity, nourishment, and divine qualities in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a positive flow of energy, striving for higher consciousness, and recognizing the virtues that contribute to our personal and collective well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that promote mental and spiritual health, foster positive relationships, and cultivate a sense of gratitude for the natural world. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
