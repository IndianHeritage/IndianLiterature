न योर उपब्धिर अश्व्यः श्रिण्वे रथस्य कच चन |यद अग्ने यासि दूत्यम् ||
na yor upabdir aśvyaḥ śṛṇve rathasya kac cana | yad agne yāsi dūtyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • yor: of those
    • upabdir: who are approaching
    • aśvyaḥ: horses
    • śṛṇve: hear
    • rathasya: of the chariot
    • kac: any
    • cana: at all
    • yad: whatever
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • yāsi: you go
    • dūtyam: as a messenger
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, and expresses a desire for him to be a messenger.
    • The phrase "na yor upabdir aśvyaḥ" suggests that there should be no obstacles or hindrances for those who are approaching, symbolized by horses and chariots.
    • The invocation of Agni as a messenger indicates a request for divine guidance and support in communication and relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity and effective communication during negotiations or partnerships.
    • In personal relationships, invoking Agni as a messenger can symbolize the desire for open and honest dialogue, ensuring that misunderstandings are minimized.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to seek blessings for smooth interactions and harmonious relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of clear communication and the removal of obstacles in relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine assistance in our interactions, ensuring that we approach others with openness and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a powerful tool for fostering positive connections, whether in personal or professional settings.
