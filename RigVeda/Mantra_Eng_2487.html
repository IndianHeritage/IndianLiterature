प्र दीधितिर विश्ववारा जिगाति होतारम इळः प्रथमं यजध्यै |अच्छा नमोभिर वृषभं वन्दध्यै स देवान यक्षद इषितो यजीयान ||
pra dīdhitir viśvavārā jigāti hotāram iḻaḥ prathamaṁ yajadhyai | acchā namobhir vṛṣabhaṁ vandadhyai sa devān yakṣad iṣito yajīyān ||
  • pra dīdhitir: This phrase can be interpreted as "may there be illumination" or "may there be clarity." It signifies the desire for enlightenment and understanding in life.
  • viśvavārā: This term refers to "the all-encompassing" or "the universal protector." It suggests a divine presence that safeguards all beings and aspects of life.
  • jigāti: This word means "to move" or "to progress." It indicates the importance of forward movement and growth in one's life journey.
  • hotāram: This refers to "the one who invokes" or "the priest." It symbolizes the role of a mediator between the divine and the earthly realms, emphasizing the importance of rituals and prayers.
  • iḻaḥ: This term can be understood as "the divine essence" or "the sacred." It represents the spiritual aspect of existence that connects individuals to the higher powers.
  • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word highlights the significance of prioritizing spiritual practices and values in life.
  • yajadhyai: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It emphasizes the importance of selflessness and giving in various forms, whether it be time, resources, or love.
  • acchā: This means "good" or "auspicious." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from righteous actions and intentions.
  • namobhir: This term translates to "with reverence" or "with salutations." It indicates the importance of humility and respect towards the divine and others.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This word means "the bull," often symbolizing strength and stability. It represents the qualities of resilience and determination in facing life's challenges.
  • vandadhyai: This means "to worship" or "to honor." It emphasizes the significance of devotion and gratitude in one's life.
  • sa devān: This phrase translates to "with the gods." It signifies the connection and relationship between humans and the divine forces.
  • yakṣad: This term means "to bestow" or "to grant." It indicates the blessings and support that come from the divine when one engages in righteous actions.
  • iṣito: This means "desired" or "sought after." It highlights the importance of pursuing one's goals and aspirations with determination.
  • yajīyān: This refers to "the one who is worthy of worship." It signifies the qualities and virtues that make an individual deserving of respect and reverence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking enlightenment, protection, and growth through devotion and righteous actions. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual practices, humility, and the connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community service, or spiritual gatherings, where individuals seek clarity, strength, and blessings. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can cultivate a life of purpose, resilience, and harmony with the universe.
