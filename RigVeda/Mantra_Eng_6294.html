यत सोमं इन्द्र विष्णविः यद वा घ त्रित आप्त्ये |यद वा मरुत्सु मन्दसे सम इन्दुभिः ||
yat somam indra viṣṇavi yad vā gha trita āptye | yad vā marutsu mandase sam indubhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yat somam indra viṣṇavi yad vā gha trita āptye | yad vā marutsu mandase sam indubhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "That which is the soma for Indra and Vishnu, that which is the essence of the three realms, that which is the strength of the Maruts, may it come to us with the moon's light."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yat: That which
    • somam: Soma, a sacred drink that grants immortality and is associated with divine inspiration
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms
    • viṣṇavi: Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology
    • yad vā: That which or whatever
    • gha: Essence or strength
    • trita: The three realms or dimensions
    • āptye: Attainment or realization
    • marutsu: The Maruts, storm deities associated with wind and storms
    • mandase: Strength or power
    • sam: Together or in harmony
    • indubhiḥ: With the light of the moon
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of soma can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in modern life. Just as soma was believed to provide divine inspiration, individuals today seek education and experiences that elevate their understanding and creativity.
    • Indra and Vishnu symbolize the forces of nature and preservation. In real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts where people strive to protect and preserve the natural world, much like Vishnu's role as a protector.
    • The Maruts represent the power of storms and winds, which can be compared to the challenges and adversities we face. Just as storms can bring destruction but also renewal, challenges in life can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces and the natural world, highlighting the importance of seeking strength and inspiration from both.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine in everyday life and the pursuit of knowledge, strength, and harmony.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek enlightenment, embrace challenges, and work towards the preservation of nature, reflecting the values of cooperation and respect for the environment.
