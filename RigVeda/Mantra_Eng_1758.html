शकमयं धूमम आराद अपश्यं विषुवतां पर एनावरेण |उक्षाणं पृथ्निम अपचन्त वीरास तानि धर्माणि प्रथमाण्य आसन ||
śakamayaṁ dhūmam ārād apaśyaṁ viṣūvatā para enāvareṇa | ukṣāṇam pṛśnim apacanta vīrās tāni dharmāṇi prathamāny āsan ||
  • śakamayaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "capable" or "powerful." It signifies strength and the ability to achieve goals.
  • dhūmam: This word means "smoke." In a metaphorical sense, smoke can represent confusion or obscurity, but it can also signify transformation, as smoke arises from fire.
  • ārād: This translates to "from above" or "from the heavens." It indicates a divine or higher influence in the context of the mantra.
  • apaśyaṁ: Meaning "not seen" or "invisible," this term suggests the presence of unseen forces or energies that influence our lives.
  • viṣūvatā: This can be understood as "in all directions" or "universal." It emphasizes the omnipresence of certain principles or energies.
  • para: This means "beyond" or "transcendent," indicating something that surpasses ordinary understanding.
  • enāvareṇa: This term can be interpreted as "with protection" or "with guidance," suggesting the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
  • ukṣāṇam: This translates to "the one who is nourished" or "the one who is cultivated," indicating growth and development.
  • pṛśnim: This word means "the earth" or "the ground," symbolizing stability and foundation in our lives.
  • apacanta: This means "to cook" or "to prepare," suggesting the process of transformation and readiness.
  • vīrās: This translates to "heroes" or "warriors," representing strength, courage, and the pursuit of righteousness.
  • tāni: This means "those" or "these," referring to the principles or qualities being discussed.
  • dharmāṇi: This term signifies "righteousness" or "moral principles," emphasizing the importance of ethical conduct.
  • prathamāny: This translates to "first" or "primary," indicating the foundational nature of the principles being referred to.
  • āsan: This means "to sit" or "to establish," suggesting the act of grounding oneself in these principles.

The mantra speaks to the interplay of strength, divine guidance, and the cultivation of righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of both seen and unseen forces in our lives, and how these forces can guide us towards our goals. The imagery of smoke suggests that while the path may sometimes be unclear, there is a transformative process at work that can lead to growth and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Development: Recognizing the unseen challenges and strengths within oneself can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw upon the principles of righteousness and strength to guide their teams through uncertainty.
  • Community Building: The mantra encourages individuals to come together, grounded in shared values and principles, to create a supportive environment.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, divine guidance, and ethical conduct in our lives. It encourages us to recognize the unseen forces at play and to cultivate our inner strengths to navigate the complexities of life. By grounding ourselves in righteousness and supporting one another, we can achieve our goals and contribute positively to the world around us.
