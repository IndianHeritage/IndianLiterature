गर्भो यज्ञस्य देवायुः क्रतुम् पुनीत आनुषक् |स्तोमैर इन्द्रस्य वावृधे मिमीत इति ||
garbho yajñasya devayuḥ kratum punīta ānuṣak | stomair indrasya vāvṛdhe mimīta it ||
  • Garbho: This word translates to "the womb" or "the source." In a broader sense, it signifies the origin or the foundational aspect of creation.
  • Yajñasya: This refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual." In Vedic tradition, yajna is a sacred act performed to invoke divine blessings.
  • Devayuḥ: This means "of the gods" or "divine." It emphasizes the connection between the rituals performed and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • Kratu: This word signifies "intention" or "will." It reflects the mental and spiritual focus behind the actions performed in rituals.
  • Punīta: This translates to "purified" or "sacred." It indicates the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and the environment during rituals.
  • Ānuṣak: This means "following" or "in accordance with." It suggests that the actions taken should align with the cosmic order and divine will.
  • Stomair: This refers to "hymns" or "chants." It highlights the significance of verbal expressions in rituals, invoking divine energies through sound.
  • Indrasya: This means "of Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Vāvṛdhe: This translates to "may it grow" or "may it increase," indicating a desire for abundance and prosperity.
  • Mimīta: This means "to be established" or "to be made firm," suggesting the importance of stability and permanence in the outcomes of rituals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine forces, particularly Indra, to bless the rituals performed with purity and intention, ensuring that the outcomes are fruitful and aligned with cosmic order. It emphasizes the significance of the foundational aspects of creation (garbho) and the necessity of performing rituals (yajñasya) with a pure heart (punīta) and focused intention (kratu).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as rituals require intention and purity, individuals can approach personal development with a clear purpose and a commitment to ethical living.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, the principles of this mantra can guide the organization of events that seek to invoke blessings for the community, ensuring that they are conducted with respect and alignment to higher values.
  • Environmental Awareness: The emphasis on purity can extend to our relationship with nature, encouraging sustainable practices that honor the earth as a sacred source of life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of intention, action, and divine support. It highlights the importance of performing our duties with purity and purpose, ensuring that our efforts contribute positively to ourselves and the world around us.
