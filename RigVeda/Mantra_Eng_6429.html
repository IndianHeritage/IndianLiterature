प्रशंसमानो अतिथिर न मित्रियो अग्नीर रथो न वेद्यः |त्वे क्षेमासो अपि सन्ति साधवस त्वं राजा रयीणाम ||
praśaṁsamāno atithir na mitriyo 'gnī ratho na vedyaḥ | tve kṣemāso api santi sādhavas tvaṁ rājā rayīṇām ||
  • praśaṁsamāno: This word translates to "praising" or "extolling." It signifies the act of recognizing and appreciating the virtues of others.
  • atithir: This means "guest." In many cultures, including Indian traditions, a guest is treated with utmost respect and honor.
  • na mitriyo: This phrase translates to "not a friend." It suggests that the guest may not be a familiar or close friend, yet deserves respect.
  • 'gnī: This refers to "fire." Fire is often associated with purity, energy, and transformation in various cultures.
  • ratho: This means "chariot." The chariot symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • na vedyaḥ: This translates to "not to be known." It implies that the true nature or intentions of the guest may not be immediately apparent.
  • tve: This means "to you." It indicates a direct address, often to a deity or a revered figure.
  • kṣemāso: This translates to "those who are safe" or "those who are secure." It refers to the well-being and protection of individuals.
  • api santi: This means "there are also." It suggests the presence of additional qualities or entities.
  • sādhavas: This translates to "the virtuous" or "the righteous." It refers to individuals who embody moral and ethical values.
  • tvaṁ: This means "you." It emphasizes the importance of the addressed entity.
  • rājā: This translates to "king" or "ruler." It signifies authority, leadership, and governance.
  • rayīṇām: This means "of wealth" or "of prosperity." It refers to abundance and the resources that sustain life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of treating guests with respect, regardless of their familiarity. It highlights the virtues of safety, righteousness, and the role of leadership in ensuring prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as hospitality practices, community leadership, and ethical governance. By recognizing the value of every individual, we foster a culture of respect and inclusivity, which ultimately leads to a harmonious society. The mantra serves as a reminder that every person, even those who may seem unfamiliar, has a role in our collective journey towards well-being and prosperity.
