ते मार्मृजत ददृवांसो अद्रिं तद एषां अन्ये अभितो वि वचन |पश्वयन्त्रासो अभि कारम् अर्चन विदन्त ज्योतिष चक्रिपन्त धिभिः ||
te marmṛjata dadṛvāṁso adriṁ tad eṣām anye abhito vi vocan | paśvayantrāso abhi kāram arcan vidanta jyotiś cakṛpanta dhībhiḥ ||
  • te marmṛjata: This phrase can be interpreted as "they who are aware of the essence" or "those who understand the core." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in life.
  • dadṛvāṁso: This term refers to "those who see" or "those who perceive." It highlights the significance of perception and insight in understanding the world around us.
  • adriṁ: This word means "mountain" or "rock." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent stability, strength, and the challenges one faces in life.
  • tad eṣām: This translates to "that is theirs" or "that belongs to them," indicating ownership or connection to knowledge and understanding.
  • anye abhito vi vocan: This phrase means "others speak about them" or "others recognize them." It suggests that those who possess knowledge and insight are acknowledged by others.
  • paśvayantrāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in the pursuit of knowledge" or "those who are seekers." It emphasizes the importance of actively seeking wisdom.
  • abhi kāram: This means "to create" or "to make." It signifies the act of bringing ideas and knowledge into reality through action.
  • arcan vidanta: This phrase translates to "worship through knowledge" or "devotion through understanding." It highlights the idea that true worship is rooted in knowledge and comprehension.
  • jyotiś: This word means "light" or "illumination." It symbolizes enlightenment, clarity, and the pursuit of truth.
  • cakṛpanta dhībhiḥ: This translates to "those who are wise create" or "the wise bring forth." It emphasizes the role of wisdom in creation and innovation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, perception, and wisdom in our lives. It suggests that true understanding leads to recognition by others and that the pursuit of knowledge is a noble endeavor. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and personal development. For instance, a teacher who imparts knowledge with insight and understanding can inspire students to seek wisdom, while a leader who embodies these qualities can create a positive impact on their community. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that knowledge and wisdom are foundational to personal growth and societal progress.
