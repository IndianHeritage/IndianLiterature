सर्गां इव सृजतं सुष्टुतीर उप श्यावाश्वस्य सुन्वतो मदाच्युतā |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चाश्विनां तिरोहन्यं ||
sargām̐ iva sṛjataṁ suṣṭutīr upa śyāvāśvasya sunvato madacyutā | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cāśvinā tiroahnyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sargām̐: creation or manifestation
    • iva: like or as
    • sṛjataṁ: may create or produce
    • suṣṭutīr: well-formed or well-structured
    • upa: towards or near
    • śyāvāśvasya: of the dark or dusky one (often referring to the night or a deity)
    • sunvato: of the one who is pleasing or agreeable
    • madacyutā: not falling into intoxication or delusion
    • sajoṣasā: with joy or happiness
    • uṣasā: of the dawn or morning
    • sūryeṇa: with the sun
    • cāśvinā: with the Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • tiroahnyam: hidden or concealed in the night
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creative forces of the universe, likening them to a well-structured creation that emerges from the darkness of night.
    • The reference to the Ashvins suggests a connection to healing and rejuvenation, emphasizing the importance of health and vitality in the process of creation.
    • The phrase "not falling into intoxication" can be interpreted as a call for clarity and awareness in our actions and thoughts, promoting a balanced approach to life.
    • The imagery of dawn and the sun symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment, suggesting that every day brings the potential for renewal and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace new opportunities and create positive changes in their lives, much like the dawn brings light after darkness.
    • In a community setting, it can encourage collaboration and collective efforts to build a harmonious environment, reflecting the well-structured creation mentioned in the mantra.
    • In health and wellness, the reference to the Ashvins can remind us to prioritize our physical and mental well-being, seeking balance and clarity in our daily routines.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of creation, clarity, and health in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that every day is an opportunity for renewal and that we should approach life with joy and awareness.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can foster a more harmonious existence, both individually and collectively, leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
