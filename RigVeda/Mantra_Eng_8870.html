गर्भे नु नौ जनिता दम्पती कर देवस् त्वष्टा सविता विश्वरूपः |नकिर अस्य प्र मिनन्ति व्रतानि वेद नाव अस्य पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
garbhe nu nau janitā dampatī kar devas tvaṣṭā savitā viśvarūpaḥ | nakir asya pra minanti vratāni veda nāv asya pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Divine Creator, may the couple conceive in the womb; may the all-forming Savitar, the divine architect, bless them. The vows of the righteous do not falter; the earth and the sky are the vessel of this creation."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • garbhe: in the womb
    • nu: indeed
    • nau: us (the couple)
    • janitā: may conceive
    • dampatī: the couple
    • kar: do (or create)
    • devas: O divine
    • tvaṣṭā: the creator or architect
    • savitā: the sun or life-giver
    • viśvarūpaḥ: of all forms
    • nakir: not
    • asya: of this
    • pra: before
    • minanti: falter
    • vratāni: vows or commitments
    • veda: know or understand
    • nāv: vessel or boat
    • asya: of this
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • uta: and
    • dyauḥ: sky
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be recited by couples who are planning to conceive, invoking divine blessings for a healthy pregnancy.
    • In a broader sense, it can be applied to any creative endeavor, such as starting a new business or project, where the couple seeks divine guidance and support.
    • It emphasizes the importance of commitment and vows in relationships, reminding couples to uphold their promises to each other.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support for creation and commitment. It highlights the sacredness of conception and the responsibilities that come with it. In real life, it serves as a reminder for couples to seek divine guidance in their journey of parenthood and to honor their vows, fostering a strong foundation for their family. This mantra can be applicable in various life scenarios, including marriage, parenting, and any collaborative efforts where unity and commitment are essential.
