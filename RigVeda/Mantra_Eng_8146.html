प्र ते दिवो न वृष्टयो धाराः यन्त्य असश्चतः |अभि शुक्राम उपस्तिरम् ||
pra te divo na vṛṣṭayo dhārā yanty asaścataḥ | abhi śukrām upastiram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for you
    • te: your
    • divo: of the heavens
    • na: not
    • vṛṣṭayo: rains
    • dhārā: streams or flows
    • yanty: go or move
    • asaścataḥ: from the non-existent or the void
    • abhi: towards
    • śukrām: the pure or bright
    • upastiram: to be established or set
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine connection between the heavens and the earthly realm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the rains (vṛṣṭayo) that nourish life on Earth.
    • The phrase "not from the void" suggests that the blessings and resources we receive are not random but are a result of divine order.
    • The mention of "pure" (śukrām) indicates that these blessings are sacred and should be respected and cherished.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine intervention to bring forth the necessary rains.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek clarity and purity in their thoughts and actions, aligning with the idea of establishing a pure intention (upastiram) in their endeavors.
    • Communities often come together in rituals or prayers during droughts, invoking the heavens for rain, reflecting the collective hope for sustenance and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and the natural world, highlighting the importance of purity and intention in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions and prayers can influence the flow of blessings in our lives.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to respect and nurture our environment, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of the Earth.
