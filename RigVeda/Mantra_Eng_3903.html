उत त्ये मां मारुताश्वस्य शोणाः क्रत्वामघासो विदथस्य रातौ |सहस्रां मे च्यवतानो ददाना आनूकम आर्यो वपुषे नर्चत ||
uta tye mā mārutāśvasya śoṇāḥ kratvāmaghāso vidathasya rātau | sahasrā me cyavatāno dadāna ānūkam aryo vapuṣe nārcat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tye: to you
    • mā: not
    • mārutāśvasya: of the wind (Māruta) and the horses (Ashva)
    • śoṇāḥ: red, often symbolizing vitality or strength
    • kratvā: through the power of ritual or sacrifice
    • maghāso: of the generous or bountiful
    • vidathasya: of the assembly or gathering
    • rātau: in the night
    • sahasrā: a thousand
    • me: my
    • cyavatāno: those who are falling or descending
    • dadāna: giving
    • ānūkam: in a gentle or kind manner
    • aryo: noble or worthy
    • vapuṣe: in form or body
    • nārcat: do not worship or honor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powers of the wind and the horses, symbolizing strength and speed.
    • The phrase "not to you" suggests a plea or a warning against neglecting the divine forces that govern the universe.
    • The reference to "the night" indicates a time of introspection and reflection, where rituals can be performed to connect with the divine.
    • The mention of "a thousand" signifies abundance and the vastness of the universe, emphasizing the importance of community and collective effort.
    • The gentle giving of blessings highlights the importance of kindness and generosity in our interactions with others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of teamwork and collaboration, much like how a group of people come together to achieve a common goal.
    • During challenging times, such as natural disasters, communities often unite to support each other, reflecting the essence of "giving in a gentle manner."
    • The concept of honoring the divine forces can be seen in various cultural rituals where people express gratitude for nature's bounty, such as harvest festivals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and honoring the forces that shape our lives, both seen and unseen.
    • It teaches us the value of community, generosity, and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • In a world that often feels disconnected, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate kindness and to appreciate the abundance around us.
