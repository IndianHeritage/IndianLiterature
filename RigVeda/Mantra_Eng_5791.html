श्रवः सूरिभ्यो अमृतं वसुत्वनं वाजां अasmभ्यं गोमताḥ |कोदायित्री मग्होनः सूनृतावत्य उषा उच्छद अप श्रीधः ||
śravaḥ sūribhyo amṛtaṁ vasutvanaṁ vājām̐ asmabhyaṁ gomataḥ | codayitrī maghonaḥ sūnṛtāvaty uṣā ucchad apa sridhaḥ ||
  • śravaḥ: This word translates to "hearing" or "listening." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • sūribhyo: This means "to the wise" or "to the learned." It suggests that wisdom comes from those who have deep understanding and experience.
  • amṛtaṁ: This word means "immortal" or "nectar." In a spiritual context, it refers to the essence of life or eternal truth.
  • vasutvanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from wisdom and knowledge.
  • vājām̐: This translates to "strength" or "power." It indicates the force that comes from understanding and wisdom.
  • asmabhyaṁ: This means "to us." It indicates a personal connection and the desire for wisdom and strength to be bestowed upon the individual or community.
  • gomataḥ: This translates to "from the cow" or "nourishing." In Vedic culture, the cow is a symbol of abundance and sustenance.
  • codayitrī: This means "the inspirer" or "the one who motivates." It refers to a guiding force that encourages individuals to seek knowledge and truth.
  • maghonaḥ: This translates to "of the great" or "of the powerful." It signifies the strength that comes from collective wisdom.
  • sūnṛtāvaty: This means "truthful" or "righteous." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • uṣā: This translates to "dawn" or "new beginnings." It symbolizes the arrival of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • ucchad: This means "to reveal" or "to uncover." It indicates the process of discovering truth and wisdom.
  • apa: This translates to "from" or "away from." It suggests a separation from ignorance or falsehood.
  • sridhaḥ: This means "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies the rewards that come from seeking and embracing knowledge.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of listening to the wise and seeking knowledge that leads to immortality and prosperity. It highlights the connection between wisdom, strength, and nourishment, suggesting that true power comes from understanding and truth.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, leadership, and personal development. For instance, students who listen to their teachers (the wise) and seek knowledge will gain strength and prosperity in their careers. Similarly, leaders who inspire their teams to pursue truth and integrity will foster a culture of trust and success.
