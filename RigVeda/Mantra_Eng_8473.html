मन्त्र: अग्रेगो राजा अप्यस तविष्यते विमानो अह्नाम् भुवनेष्व् अर्पितः |हरिर घृतस्नुः सुदृशीकः अर्णवो ज्योतीरथः पवते राया ओक्यः ||
agrego rājāpyas taviṣyate vimāno ahnām bhuvaneṣv arpitaḥ | harir ghṛtasnuḥ sudṛśīko arṇavo jyotīrathaḥ pavate rāya okyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "agrego rājāpyas taviṣyate vimāno ahnām bhuvaneṣv arpitaḥ | harir ghṛtasnuḥ sudṛśīko arṇavo jyotīrathaḥ pavate rāya okyaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agrego: foremost, the best
    • rājā: king, ruler
    • apyas: even, also
    • taviṣyate: will be, is destined
    • vimāno: aircraft, vehicle
    • ahnām: of the day, daytime
    • bhuvaneṣv: in the worlds, in the realms
    • arpitaḥ: dedicated, offered
    • harir: the divine, Lord Vishnu
    • ghṛtasnuḥ: one who is rich in ghee, prosperous
    • sudṛśīko: having a beautiful form
    • arṇavo: ocean, vastness
    • jyotīrathaḥ: charioteer of light, guiding light
    • pavate: blows, moves
    • rāya: wealth, prosperity
    • okyaḥ: abode, home
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a supreme ruler or king who is destined to be the best among all.
    • The reference to "vimāno" suggests a vehicle that transcends earthly limitations, symbolizing progress and advancement.
    • The phrase "harir ghṛtasnuḥ" indicates a divine presence that is abundant and prosperous, akin to the richness of ghee in Indian culture, which is often associated with purity and nourishment.
    • "Sudṛśīko" refers to the beauty and grace of the divine, suggesting that true leadership is not just about power but also about wisdom and aesthetics.
    • The "arṇavo" signifies the vastness of the ocean, representing the depth of knowledge and experience that a true leader must possess.
    • "Jyotīrathaḥ" implies that the divine guides us like a charioteer, leading us towards enlightenment and clarity.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for prosperity and a safe abode, emphasizing the importance of a stable and nurturing environment for growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, whether in business or governance, the qualities of wisdom, beauty, and prosperity are essential for effective leadership.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of a "vimāno" can be likened to technological advancements that help us transcend limitations, such as the internet or space travel.
    • The emphasis on community and nurturing environments can be seen in initiatives that promote sustainable living and social welfare.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the qualities of an ideal leader and the importance of divine guidance in achieving prosperity and stability.
    • It serves as a reminder that true leadership is multifaceted, requiring not just authority but also compassion, wisdom, and a vision for the greater good.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to more effective leadership, better community engagement, and a more harmonious society.
