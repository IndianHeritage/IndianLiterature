मन्त्र: क्रत्वा वेधाऽइषूयते विश्वा जातानि पस्पशे | यतो घृतश्रीर अतिथिर अजायत वह्निर वेधा अजायत ||
kratvā vedhā iṣūyate viśvā jātāni paspaśe | yato ghṛtaśrīr atithir ajāyata vahnir vedhā ajāyata ||
  • Kratvā: This term refers to the act of performing or executing. In a real-life context, it can be likened to taking action or making efforts towards a goal, such as working hard to achieve success in one's career.
  • Vedhā: This word signifies the act of penetrating or understanding deeply. For example, in education, vedhā can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge where one seeks to understand complex subjects thoroughly.
  • Iṣūyate: This term means to be directed or aimed at. In practical terms, it can relate to setting goals or intentions in life, such as aiming for personal growth or professional advancement.
  • Viśvā: This word translates to 'all' or 'universal.' It emphasizes inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be observed in community service, where individuals come together to support and uplift everyone in society.
  • Jātāni: This refers to 'born' or 'created.' It can be related to the idea of new beginnings, such as the birth of a child or the start of a new project that brings hope and potential.
  • Paspaśe: This term means to flourish or thrive. In a real-life scenario, it can be seen in the growth of a business or the success of a community initiative that benefits many.
  • Yato: This word means 'from where' or 'because of.' It indicates a source or origin, which can be related to the idea of drawing inspiration from one's roots or heritage.
  • Ghṛtaśrīr: This term refers to the 'glory of ghee' or 'nourishment.' In practical terms, it can symbolize the importance of sustenance and well-being, such as the role of healthy food in maintaining physical and mental health.
  • Atithir: This word means 'guest' or 'stranger.' It highlights the significance of hospitality and kindness towards others, which can be seen in cultural practices of welcoming guests into one's home.
  • Ajāyata: This term means 'was born' or 'came into being.' It signifies the emergence of new ideas or innovations, such as the development of technology that transforms lives.
  • Vahnir: This word translates to 'fire.' Fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, it can represent the passion and drive that fuels one's ambitions.
  • Vedhā: Reiterated here, it emphasizes the importance of understanding and insight, which is crucial in decision-making processes in both personal and professional realms.
  • Ajāyata: Again, this term signifies the concept of birth or creation, reinforcing the idea of continuous growth and evolution in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of action, understanding, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the importance of taking initiative, aiming for universal well-being, and recognizing the significance of hospitality and nourishment in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination, foster community spirit, and embrace the continuous cycle of creation and growth. Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to life, where every action contributes to the greater good of society.
