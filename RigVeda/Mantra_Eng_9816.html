स दर्षतश्रीर अतिथिर गृहे-गृहे वने-वने शिश्रिये तक्ववीर इव |जनं-जनं जन्यो नाति मन्यते विश आ क्षेति विश्वोऽ विश्वं-विशम् ||
sa darśataśrīr atithir gṛhe-gṛhe vane-vane śiśriye takvavīr iva | janaṁ-janaṁ janyo nāti manyate viśa ā kṣeti viśyo3 viśaṁ-viśam ||
  • sa darśataśrīr: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is seen with glory." It signifies the presence of auspiciousness and prosperity.
  • atithir: This means "the guest." In many cultures, especially in Indian tradition, a guest is considered equivalent to God (Atithi Devo Bhava).
  • gṛhe-gṛhe: This translates to "in every home." It emphasizes the idea that hospitality and respect for guests should be a universal practice.
  • vane-vane: This means "in every forest." It suggests that the principles of respect and hospitality extend beyond just homes to nature and the environment.
  • śiśriye: This can be interpreted as "to dwell" or "to reside." It indicates a sense of belonging and community.
  • takvavīr iva: This phrase means "like the brave." It implies that one should approach life and challenges with courage and valor.
  • janaṁ-janaṁ: This translates to "from every person." It highlights the importance of recognizing the value in every individual.
  • janyo: This means "born of" or "produced by." It suggests that our actions and attitudes are shaped by our interactions with others.
  • nāti manyate: This translates to "does not think too highly." It emphasizes humility and the importance of not underestimating others.
  • viśa ā kṣeti: This means "the community protects." It signifies the role of collective responsibility in safeguarding the well-being of society.
  • viśyo3 viśaṁ-viśam: This can be interpreted as "the community of communities." It reflects the interconnectedness of various groups and the importance of unity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of hospitality, respect for individuals, and the interconnectedness of communities. It teaches us that every home and every part of nature should be a place of welcome and kindness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as ensuring that we treat guests with honor, fostering a sense of community, and recognizing the value of every person we encounter. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and respectful society, where everyone feels valued and protected.
