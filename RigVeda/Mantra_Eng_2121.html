यं स्मा पृच्छन्ति कुहा सेति घोरम् उतेम आहुर नैषो अस्तीत्य एनम् |सो आर्यः पुष्टीर विज इवामिनाति श्रद अस्मै धत्त स जनास इन्द्रः ||
yaṁ smā pṛcchanti kuha seti ghoram utem āhur naiṣo astīty enam | so aryaḥ puṣṭīr vija ivā mināti śrad asmai dhatta sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • smā: we
    • pṛcchanti: ask
    • kuha: where
    • seti: resides
    • ghoram: terrible
    • utem: they say
    • āhur: have said
    • naiṣo: not
    • astīty: is
    • enam: him
    • so: he
    • aryaḥ: noble
    • puṣṭīr: nourishment
    • vija: like
    • ivā: as
    • mināti: pierces
    • śrad: faith
    • asmai: to him
    • dhatta: grant
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: people
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a dialogue about the nature of existence and the divine.
    • The opening lines question the whereabouts of a powerful entity, suggesting a search for understanding in the face of fear and uncertainty.
    • The phrase "ghoram utem āhur" indicates that people often speak of this entity in fearful terms, highlighting the human tendency to fear the unknown.
    • However, the mantra shifts to a more positive note with "so aryaḥ puṣṭīr," indicating that this entity, despite being feared, is also a source of nourishment and strength.
    • The reference to Indra suggests that this entity is not just a distant power but is intimately connected to the lives of people, providing them with faith and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal loss, people often seek answers and comfort from higher powers, reflecting the search for understanding in fear.
    • In many cultures, the concept of a protective deity or force is prevalent, where individuals turn to their faith for strength and nourishment during difficult times.
    • For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, many turned to spirituality and community support, echoing the mantra's theme of seeking strength and nourishment from a higher source.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the duality of fear and faith, illustrating how the unknown can be both terrifying and a source of strength.
    • It encourages individuals to seek understanding and nourishment from their beliefs, especially in challenging times.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging us to embrace faith as a means of overcoming fear.
