सो चिन् नु भद्रā क्षुमती यशस्वती उषा उवासा मनवे स्वर्वती |यद ईम उषन्तम उषताम अनु क्रतुम अग्निं होतारं विदथाय जिजनन ||
so cin nu bhadrā kṣumatī yaśasvaty uṣā uvāsa manave svarvatī | yad īm uśantam uśatām anu kratum agniṁ hotāraṁ vidathāya jījanan ||
  • so cin nu bhadrā: This phrase can be translated as "may we see auspiciousness." The word "bhadrā" signifies something that is beneficial or favorable. In real life, this can relate to seeking positive outcomes in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and endeavors.
  • kṣumatī: This term refers to intelligence or wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of being wise in our actions and decisions. For example, in a business context, making informed decisions based on market research can lead to success.
  • yaśasvaty: This word means "fame" or "glory." It suggests that one should strive for recognition through good deeds and achievements. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who work hard and contribute positively to society, earning respect and admiration.
  • uṣā uvāsa: "Uṣā" refers to dawn or new beginnings, while "uvāsa" means "to dwell" or "to reside." This can symbolize the arrival of new opportunities and the importance of embracing change. For instance, starting a new job or moving to a new city can represent a fresh start.
  • manave svarvatī: "Manave" relates to the mind or intellect, and "svarvatī" can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth." This highlights the significance of using our intellect to create and manifest our desires. In practical terms, this can be seen in creative fields where innovative ideas lead to new products or art forms.
  • yad īm uśantam uśatām anu: This phrase can be understood as "whatever is beneficial and brings happiness." It emphasizes the pursuit of joy and well-being in life. For example, engaging in activities that promote mental health, such as meditation or spending time with loved ones, aligns with this idea.
  • kratum agniṁ hotāraṁ: "Kratu" refers to intention or purpose, "agniṁ" means fire, and "hotāraṁ" signifies the one who offers. This can be interpreted as the importance of having a clear purpose and passion (the fire) in our actions. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who are driven by their goals, such as activists fighting for a cause.
  • vidathāya jījanan: "Vidathāya" means "for the purpose of creation," and "jījanan" refers to birth or emergence. This suggests that our intentions and actions lead to new creations and outcomes. For instance, entrepreneurs create businesses that fulfill needs in society, contributing to economic growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking auspiciousness, wisdom, and glory while embracing new beginnings and using our intellect to manifest our desires. It highlights the significance of having clear intentions and the role of our actions in creating positive outcomes in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with purpose, engage in meaningful activities, and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to personal fulfillment and collective well-being.
