इयम् मनीषा इयम् अश्विनाः गीर इमां सुवृक्तिं वृषणा जुषेथाम् |इमा ब्रह्माणि युवयून्य अग्मन यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyam manīṣā iyam aśvinā gīr imāṁ suvṛktiṁ vṛṣaṇā juṣethām | imā brahmāṇi yuvayūny agman yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • iyam manīṣā: This phrase translates to "this is the thought" or "this is the intention." It signifies the importance of having a clear and focused mindset in our endeavors.
  • iyam aśvinā gīr: Referring to the "words of the Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health and medicine, this part emphasizes the power of words and communication in healing and well-being.
  • imāṁ suvṛktiṁ vṛṣaṇā juṣethām: This means "may we enjoy this good thought." It highlights the aspiration to embrace positive and constructive thoughts that lead to beneficial actions.
  • imā brahmāṇi yuvayūny agman: This translates to "these thoughts are the young Brahmanas." It suggests that these thoughts are fresh, vibrant, and full of potential, akin to the energy of youth.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you protect us always with auspiciousness." It is a prayer for divine protection and blessings in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of positive thinking, effective communication, and the pursuit of well-being. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, focusing on constructive thoughts can lead to better decision-making and emotional health.
  • In professional settings, clear communication, inspired by the words of the Ashvins, can foster teamwork and collaboration.
  • In community and social interactions, promoting positive thoughts and actions can enhance relationships and create a supportive environment.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention, the significance of positive communication, and the importance of seeking divine blessings for a prosperous and harmonious life.
