समिद्धो विश्वतस् पतिः पवमानो वि राजति |प्रीणन् वृषा कनिक्रदत ||
samiddho viśvatas patiḥ pavamāno vi rājati | prīṇan vṛṣā kanikradat ||
  • Mantra: "samiddho viśvatas patiḥ pavamāno vi rājati | prīṇan vṛṣā kanikradat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddho: kindled or ignited
    • viśvatas: from all sides or universally
    • patiḥ: lord or master
    • pavamāno: blowing or moving (often refers to the wind)
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • rājati: shines or rules
    • prīṇan: pleasing or satisfying
    • vṛṣā: the strong or powerful
    • kanikradat: making a sound or roaring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful force that is universally present and kindled, often interpreted as the divine or cosmic energy.
    • The term "pavamāno" suggests the movement of air or wind, symbolizing the life force or prana that sustains all living beings.
    • The phrase "vi rājati" indicates that this energy shines brightly and rules over all, emphasizing its omnipresence and authority.
    • "Prīṇan vṛṣā kanikradat" can be understood as this energy being pleasing and powerful, making its presence known through sound, akin to the roar of a lion or the rustle of leaves in the wind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the wind can be seen as a manifestation of this energy, moving through trees and landscapes, creating sounds that resonate with life.
    • In human life, the concept of 'life force' can be related to the energy we feel when we are inspired or motivated, often described as a 'spark' that ignites our passions.
    • In community settings, leaders who embody this energy can inspire and uplift others, much like the wind that carries seeds to new places, fostering growth and development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and harnessing the universal life force that exists around us.
    • It teaches us to be aware of the energies that influence our lives and the world, encouraging us to align ourselves with this powerful force for personal and communal growth.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in meditation practices, leadership, and community building, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the energy that flows through us.
