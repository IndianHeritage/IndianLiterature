मन्त्र: प्रेद व अग्निर वावृधे स्तोमेभिर गीरभिः स्तोत्राणां नमस्य उक्थैः |पूर्वीर ऋतस्य संदृशश चकानः सं दूतो अद्यौद उषसो विरोके ||
pred v agnir vāvṛdhe stomebhir gīrbhiḥ stotṝṇāṁ namasya ukthaiḥ | pūrvīr ṛtasya saṁdṛśaś cakānaḥ saṁ dūto adyaud uṣaso viroke ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pred: to praise or to invoke
    • v: and
    • agnir: Agni, the fire god
    • vāvṛdhe: has been made to grow or has been nourished
    • stomebhir: with hymns or praises
    • gīrbhiḥ: with words or songs
    • stotṝṇāṁ: of the worshippers or those who praise
    • namasya: with reverence or respect
    • ukthaiḥ: with chants or hymns
    • pūrvīr: the earlier or previous
    • ṛtasya: of truth or cosmic order
    • saṁdṛśaś: having been seen or manifested
    • cakānaḥ: has been made to shine or has appeared
    • saṁ: together or in union
    • dūto: the messenger
    • adyaud: today or in this moment
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • viroke: in the light or in the brightness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of fire in rituals and daily life.
    • It highlights the role of hymns and praises in connecting with the divine, suggesting that words have power and significance.
    • The reference to the earlier manifestations of truth indicates a continuity of wisdom and cosmic order that transcends time.
    • The mention of the dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the Agni is central to ceremonies, representing the divine presence.
    • In modern life, the act of expressing gratitude or reverence through words can be seen in practices like meditation, affirmations, or even in daily conversations where positivity is encouraged.
    • The concept of cosmic order can be related to the idea of karma, where actions have consequences, and maintaining harmony in life is essential.
    • The dawn symbolizes new opportunities; for example, starting a new job or embarking on a new project often comes with the hope of success and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of reverence in our actions, and the power of words.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the divine in everyday elements like fire and light, fostering a sense of gratitude and mindfulness.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in rituals, personal reflections, and in the pursuit of truth and harmony in our lives.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the importance of honoring the past while embracing the present and future with hope and positivity.
