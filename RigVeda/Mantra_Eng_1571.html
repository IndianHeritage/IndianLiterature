घृतप्रतीकं व ऋतस्य धूर्षदं अग्निम मित्रं न समिधाना ऋञ्जते | इन्धानो अक्रो विदथेṣu दीद्यच् शुक्रवर्णाम् उद उ नो यंसेते धियम् ||
ghṛtapratīkaṁ va ṛtasya dhūrṣadam agnim mitraṁ na samidhāna ṛñjate | indhāno akro vidatheṣu dīdyac chukravarṇām ud u no yaṁsate dhiyam ||
  • ghṛtapratīkaṁ: This term refers to the "butter" or "ghee" that is offered in rituals. Ghee is considered pure and is often used in Hindu rituals as a symbol of nourishment and prosperity.
  • va ṛtasya: "va" means "and," while "ṛtasya" refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." This suggests a connection between the offerings and the fundamental truths of the universe.
  • dhūrṣadam: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is to be invoked" or "the one who is powerful." It emphasizes the strength and significance of the deity being addressed.
  • agnim: This means "fire," which is a central element in Vedic rituals. Fire is seen as a purifying force and a medium through which offerings are made to the gods.
  • mitraṁ: This translates to "friend" or "ally," indicating a relationship of trust and support between humans and the divine.
  • na samidhāna: "na" means "not," and "samidhāna" refers to "kindling" or "igniting." This phrase suggests that the fire should not be ignited in a careless manner, emphasizing the importance of intention in rituals.
  • ṛñjate: This term means "to shine" or "to illuminate," indicating the positive outcomes of the ritual when performed correctly.
  • indhāno: This refers to "the one who is the source of light" or "the illuminator," often associated with the divine presence in rituals.
  • akro: This can mean "without anger" or "calm," suggesting that the approach to the divine should be peaceful and respectful.
  • vidatheṣu: This term refers to "in the gatherings" or "in the assemblies," indicating the communal aspect of rituals and the importance of collective worship.
  • dīdyac: This means "to shine" or "to radiate," reinforcing the idea of divine light and guidance.
  • shukravarṇām: This translates to "of the color of brilliance" or "radiant," symbolizing purity and divine qualities.
  • ud u no: This phrase can be interpreted as "upward" or "to the heights," suggesting an aspiration towards higher spiritual realms.
  • yaṁsate: This means "to attain" or "to achieve," indicating the goal of the ritual is to reach a higher state of being.
  • dhiyaṁ: This refers to "intellect" or "understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of intention, purity, and communal participation in rituals. It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, suggesting that rituals should be performed with respect and mindfulness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects of life, such as fostering harmonious relationships, engaging in community service, and approaching challenges with a calm and focused mindset. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, when aligned with truth and purity, can lead to illumination and understanding in our lives.
