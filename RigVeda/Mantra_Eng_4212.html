वरुणं वो ऋशादसम ऋचां मित्रं हवामहे |परि व्रजेव बाह्वोर जगन्वांसां स्वर्णरम् ||
varuṇaṁ vo riśādasam ṛcā mitraṁ havāmahe | pari vrajeva bāhvor jaganvāṁsā svarṇaram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Varuṇaṁ: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • Vo: Means "to you" or "for you."
    • Riśādasam: Can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector" or "the one who is vigilant."
    • Ṛcā: Means "with hymns" or "with praises."
    • Mitraṁ: Refers to Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts.
    • Havāmahe: Means "we invoke" or "we call upon."
    • Pari: Means "around" or "surrounding."
    • Vrajeva: Can be interpreted as "like a protector" or "like a guardian."
    • Bāhvor: Refers to "of the arms" or "of the strength."
    • Jaganvāṁsā: Means "of the universe" or "of the world."
    • Svarṇaram: Refers to "golden" or "radiant."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking Varuna and Mitra, seeking their protection and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of cosmic order and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The invocation of these deities signifies a call for harmony, friendship, and the safeguarding of moral and ethical values.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be relevant during gatherings where people seek to strengthen their bonds and ensure mutual respect and cooperation.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the essence of this mantra can remind individuals to uphold trust and friendship, fostering a supportive environment.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to water and cosmic order can inspire actions towards sustainability and respect for nature, recognizing the divine connection to the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the promotion of harmony among individuals and within the universe.
    • It serves as a reminder of the values of friendship, cooperation, and respect for the natural order, which are essential in today's world.
    • By incorporating the teachings of this mantra into daily life, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and harmonious society.
