ऊर्ध्वा हि ते दिवे-दिवे सहस्रā सूनृतā शतā | जरितृभ्यो विमंहते ||
ūrdhvā hi te dive-dive sahasrā sūnṛtā śatā | jaritṛbhyo vimaṁhate ||
  • ūrdhvā hi te dive-dive: This phrase translates to "You are elevated, day by day." The term "ūrdhvā" signifies elevation or upliftment, while "dive-dive" refers to the daily or continuous aspect of this elevation. In real life, this can be related to personal growth and development, where individuals strive to improve themselves consistently.
  • sahasrā sūnṛtā śatā: This part means "a thousand truths, a hundred." Here, "sahasrā" indicates a thousand, "sūnṛtā" refers to truths or righteousness, and "śatā" means a hundred. This can be interpreted as the abundance of truth and righteousness that one can achieve or experience. In practical terms, it emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions, suggesting that a life grounded in truth leads to greater fulfillment.
  • jaritṛbhyo vimaṁhate: This phrase translates to "it is freed from the constraints of the old." "Jaritṛbhyo" refers to the old or past, while "vimaṁhate" means to be liberated or freed. This can be seen in the context of letting go of past burdens or limitations to embrace new opportunities. In everyday life, this could relate to overcoming past failures or traumas to move forward positively.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of continuous growth, the pursuit of truth, and liberation from past constraints. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to strive for personal development, uphold integrity, and release themselves from the limitations of their past. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and self-improvement journeys. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
