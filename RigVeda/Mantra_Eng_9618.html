अग्निर्न ये भ्राजसā रुक्मवक्षसो वातासो न स्वयुजः सद्यौतयः | प्रज्ञातारो न ज्येष्ठाः सुनीतयः सुशर्माणो न सोमाऽऋतं यते ||
agnir na ye bhrājasā rukmavakṣaso vātāso na svayujaḥ sadyaūtayaḥ | prajñātāro na jyeṣṭhāḥ sunītayaḥ suśarmāṇo na somā ṛtaṁ yate ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • na ye: Translates to "those who do not," indicating a negation or absence of certain qualities.
  • bhrājasā: Means "shining" or "radiant," often associated with brilliance and clarity.
  • rukmavakṣaso: Refers to "golden-bodied," symbolizing wealth, prosperity, and auspiciousness.
  • vātāso: Means "those who are wind-born," suggesting a connection to the elements and the natural world.
  • na svayujaḥ: Translates to "not self-joined," indicating a lack of unity or connection.
  • sadyaūtayaḥ: Refers to "those who are swift," symbolizing speed and efficiency in action.
  • prajñātāro: Means "those who are wise," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • na jyeṣṭhāḥ: Translates to "not the eldest," indicating a reference to hierarchy or seniority.
  • sunītayaḥ: Means "well-guided," suggesting the importance of proper direction and guidance in life.
  • suśarmāṇo: Refers to "those who are well-protected," indicating safety and security.
  • na somā: Translates to "not the moon," which can symbolize calmness, tranquility, and emotional stability.
  • ṛtaṁ yate: Means "those who strive for truth," emphasizing the pursuit of righteousness and moral integrity.

The mantra essentially speaks about the qualities and attributes that one should aspire to embody. It contrasts certain negative traits with positive ones, urging individuals to seek wisdom, guidance, and truth while avoiding disconnection and lack of unity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their qualities and strive to be wise, well-guided, and protective of themselves and others.
  • Leadership: Leaders can take inspiration from this mantra to foster unity and wisdom within their teams, ensuring that they guide their followers effectively.
  • Community Building: In community settings, this mantra encourages collaboration and the pursuit of common goals, emphasizing the importance of truth and righteousness.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-improvement and the cultivation of positive attributes. By embodying the qualities of wisdom, guidance, and truth, individuals can lead more fulfilling lives and contribute positively to society. It serves as a reminder to reflect on our actions and strive for a harmonious existence with ourselves and others.
