यद अक्रन्दः प्रथमं जायमान उद्यान समुद्राद उता व पूरीषात |श्येनस्य पक्षाः हरिणस्य बाहू उपस्तुत्यम महि जातं ते अर्वन ||
yad akrandaḥ prathamaṁ jāyamāna udyan samudrād uta vā purīṣāt | śyenasya pakṣā hariṇasya bāhū upastutyam mahi jātaṁ te arvan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • akrandaḥ: cried out
    • prathamaṁ: first
    • jāyamāna: being born
    • udyān: from the garden or the field
    • samudrād: from the ocean
    • uta: or
    • vā: or
    • purīṣāt: from the excrement
    • śyenasya: of the falcon
    • pakṣā: wings
    • hariṇasya: of the deer
    • bāhū: arms
    • upastutyam: for praise
    • mahi: great
    • jātaṁ: born
    • te: your
    • arvan: O powerful one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the origins of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The phrase "yad akrandaḥ" suggests the first cry of life, symbolizing the moment of birth.
    • It highlights the idea that life can emerge from various sources, whether from the ocean, the earth, or even from waste, indicating the cyclical nature of existence.
    • The mention of "śyenasya pakṣā" and "hariṇasya bāhū" symbolizes the strength and grace found in nature, representing the balance between power and gentleness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see how life thrives in diverse environments, from the depths of the ocean to the heights of the mountains, illustrating the mantra's message of life's resilience.
    • In human life, the struggles and triumphs we face can be seen as a reflection of this mantra, where challenges (like the excrement) can lead to growth and new beginnings.
    • The relationship between strength (symbolized by the falcon) and gentleness (symbolized by the deer) can be observed in various aspects of life, such as leadership styles that balance assertiveness with empathy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of birth, life, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the origins of life and the diverse paths it can take, reminding us that every being has its place and purpose.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire us to embrace our own journeys, recognizing that challenges can lead to growth and that strength can coexist with gentleness.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic view of existence, fostering respect for all forms of life and their contributions to the greater whole.
