तां वर्तिर यातं जयुषा वि पर्वतम अपिन्वतं शयवे धेनुम अश्विनाः |वृक्षस्य चिद वर्तिकाम अन्तर आस्याद युवं शचीभिर ग्रसिताम अमुञ्चताम ||
tā vartir yātaṁ jayuṣā vi parvatam apinvataṁ śayave dhenum aśvinā | vṛkasya cid vartikām antar āsyād yuvaṁ śacībhir grasitām amuñcatam ||
  • tā vartir yātaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is moving or flowing." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the universe, emphasizing the importance of movement and change.
  • jayuṣā vi parvatam: Translated as "with strength, towards the mountain," this part highlights the idea of striving towards lofty goals or aspirations, much like climbing a mountain requires strength and determination.
  • apinvataṁ śayave dhenum aśvinā: This can be understood as "may the Ashvins grant us the wealth of the cow." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine, and the cow symbolizes abundance and nourishment. This line reflects the desire for prosperity and well-being.
  • vṛkasya cid vartikām: This phrase means "the essence of the wolf." The wolf often represents instincts, survival, and the primal aspects of nature. It suggests a connection to our inner instincts and the importance of being attuned to our environment.
  • antar āsyād yuvaṁ: Translated as "may the young one be within," this part emphasizes the importance of youth, vitality, and the potential for growth and renewal.
  • śacībhir grasitām amuñcatam: This can be interpreted as "may we be freed from the grasp of the powerful." It speaks to the desire for liberation from forces that may constrain or limit us, whether they be societal pressures, fears, or obstacles in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of movement, aspiration, abundance, instinct, youth, and liberation. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embrace change and strive towards our goals with strength and determination. It encourages us to seek prosperity and well-being while remaining connected to our instincts and the natural world. The call for liberation from constraints resonates in various contexts, such as personal growth, overcoming challenges, and pursuing one's dreams. This mantra can be applied in daily life as a source of inspiration and motivation, urging individuals to recognize their potential and strive for a fulfilling existence.
