त्वं न इन्द्र शूर शूरैः उता त्वोतासो बरहणा |पुरुत्रा ते वि पूर्तयो नवन्त क्षोणयो यथा ||
tvaṁ na indra śūra śūrair uta tvotāso barhaṇā | purutrā te vi pūrtayo navanta kṣoṇayo yathā ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • na - This means "not," indicating a negation or absence.
  • indra - Indra is the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
  • śūra - This translates to "brave" or "heroic," emphasizing valor and courage.
  • śūrair - This is the plural form of "śūra," indicating multiple heroes or brave individuals.
  • uta - This means "or," presenting an alternative or addition.
  • tvotāso - This can be interpreted as "your strength" or "your power," referring to the divine energy of the entity being addressed.
  • barhaṇā - This word signifies "with great glory" or "with splendor," indicating a state of high honor or respect.
  • purutrā - This means "from all sides" or "in all directions," suggesting omnipresence or universal reach.
  • te - This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging to the divine entity.
  • vi - This means "indeed" or "certainly," adding emphasis to the statement.
  • pūrtayo - This refers to "fulfillment" or "completion," often in the context of desires or needs being met.
  • navanta - This means "new" or "fresh," indicating renewal or rejuvenation.
  • kṣoṇayo - This translates to "earth" or "land," referring to the physical realm.
  • yathā - This means "as" or "like," introducing a comparison or analogy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine power, acknowledging its strength and glory while seeking fulfillment and renewal in all aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of bravery and heroism, both in the divine and human realms.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can draw inspiration from the qualities of bravery and strength, encouraging them to face challenges with courage.
  • Community Support: The idea of collective strength can be applied in community efforts, where individuals come together to support one another in times of need.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the earth (kṣoṇayo) can inspire actions towards environmental conservation and sustainability, recognizing the need for renewal and care for our planet.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine qualities of strength, bravery, and the importance of seeking fulfillment in life. It encourages individuals to embody these virtues in their daily lives, fostering a sense of community and responsibility towards the environment. By invoking these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
