तां इयानो महि वरूथम ऊतय उप गहेद एनां नमसां गृणीमसि |तृतो न यान पाँच होतृन अभिष्टय आववर्तद अवरां चक्रियावसे ||
tām̐ iyāno mahi varūtham ūtaya upa ghed enā namasā gṛṇīmasi | trito na yān pañca hotṝn abhiṣṭaya āvavartad avarāñ cakriyāvase ||
  • tām̐ iyāno mahi varūtham ūtaya: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke that great and powerful Varuna." Here, Varuna is a deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. In real life, this can be seen as a call for justice and righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • upa ghed enā namasā gṛṇīmasi: This translates to "We offer our respectful homage to Him." This emphasizes the importance of humility and respect in our interactions with others, acknowledging the greater forces at play in our lives.
  • trito na yān pañca hotṝn abhiṣṭaya: This means "Three times, we call upon the five priests." This can be interpreted as a reminder of the importance of community and collaboration. In modern life, this could relate to teamwork in professional settings or the importance of family and friends in personal matters.
  • āvavartad avarāñ cakriyāvase: This translates to "May they bring forth the best actions." This highlights the significance of positive actions and intentions in our lives. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to strive for excellence and to contribute positively to society.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking higher powers for guidance, showing respect, fostering community, and striving for positive actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek justice, act with humility, collaborate with others, and contribute positively to the world around us. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, in professional teamwork, or in community service, guiding individuals to align their actions with higher principles and values.
