सहस्रवाजम् अभिमातिषाहं सुतेरणम् मगवानं सुवृक्तिम् |उप भूषन्ति गिरो अप्रतीतम् इन्द्रं नमस्य जारितुः पनन्त ||
sahasravājam abhimātiṣāhaṁ suteraṇam maghavānaṁ suvṛktim | upa bhūṣanti giro apratītam indraṁ namasyā jarituḥ pananta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasravājam: One who has a thousand steeds; symbolizes abundance and strength.
    • abhimātiṣāhaṁ: I am the one who is powerful and capable; indicates self-assertion and confidence.
    • suteraṇam: The one who is well-versed or skilled; refers to wisdom and knowledge.
    • maghavānaṁ: The mighty one; often associated with Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • suvṛktim: Good speech or eloquence; highlights the importance of communication and expression.
    • upa bhūṣanti: They adorn or praise; signifies the act of honoring or recognizing someone.
    • giro: Words or speech; emphasizes the significance of language and dialogue.
    • apratītam: Unseen or unknown; refers to the mysteries of life and the universe.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms; symbolizes strength and leadership.
    • namasyā: Salutation or respect; indicates reverence and humility.
    • jarituḥ: Of the one who is born; relates to the cycle of life and existence.
    • pananta: Infinite or eternal; signifies the limitless nature of the universe.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and eloquence in our lives. Just as a horse symbolizes power and freedom, individuals are encouraged to harness their inner strength and capabilities.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the need for leadership and the ability to navigate challenges, much like a leader in a community or organization who must make decisions for the greater good.
    • The act of praising and honoring others through words is crucial in building relationships and fostering a sense of community. In real life, this can be seen in practices such as public speaking, mentorship, and teamwork.
    • The mention of the unseen and unknown encourages individuals to embrace uncertainty and explore the mysteries of life, akin to scientific inquiry or personal growth journeys.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to respect the interconnectedness of life, the importance of communication, and the pursuit of knowledge, which are essential in both personal and professional realms.
