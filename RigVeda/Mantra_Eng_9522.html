साध्वर्या अतिथिनीर इषिराः स्पार्हाः सुवर्णा अनवद्यरूपाः |बृहस्पतिः पर्वतेभ्यो वितूर्याः निर गा ऊपे यवम् इव स्थिविभ्यः ||
sādhvaryā atithinīr iṣirāḥ spārhāḥ suvarṇā anavadyarūpāḥ | bṛhaspatiḥ parvatebhyo vitūryā nir gā ūpe yavam iva sthivibhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sādhvaryā: With auspiciousness or goodness.
    • atithinīr: Of the guest or the visitor.
    • iṣirāḥ: The seers or sages.
    • spārhāḥ: Desiring or aspiring.
    • suvarṇā: Golden or precious.
    • anavadyarūpāḥ: Having an unblemished form.
    • bṛhaspatiḥ: The deity of wisdom and knowledge.
    • parvatebhyo: From the mountains.
    • vitūryā: With strength or power.
    • nir gā: Without any hindrance.
    • ūpe: Approaching or coming near.
    • yavam: Barley or grain.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • sthivibhyaḥ: From the firm or stable.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspiciousness associated with guests and the wisdom of sages.
    • The mention of "golden" and "unblemished form" suggests the purity and value of knowledge and wisdom.
    • Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom, is invoked to provide strength and guidance, akin to how mountains stand firm and provide stability.
    • The reference to barley symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, indicating that wisdom and knowledge are essential for growth and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In hospitality, treating guests with respect and kindness reflects the essence of "sādhvaryā atithinīr," promoting goodwill and positive relationships.
    • In educational settings, the role of teachers and mentors can be likened to the "iṣirāḥ," guiding students towards knowledge and wisdom.
    • In personal development, aspiring for "suvarṇā anavadyarūpāḥ" can inspire individuals to pursue excellence and integrity in their actions.
    • In community building, the strength of "bṛhaspatiḥ parvatebhyo" can be seen in leaders who provide stability and guidance, much like mountains that offer a solid foundation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, hospitality, and the pursuit of excellence in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to value knowledge and treat others with respect, fostering a harmonious and prosperous community.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and strength in their endeavors, ensuring that their actions are guided by integrity and purpose.
