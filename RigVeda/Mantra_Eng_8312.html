उपो मतिः पृच्यते सिच्यते मधु मन्द्राजनī चोदते अन्तर आसनि |पवमानः संतानिः प्रघ्नताम iva मधुमान द्रप्सः परि वारम् अर्षति ||
upo matiḥ pṛcyate sicyate madhu mandrājanī codate antar āsani | pavamānaḥ saṁtaniḥ praghnatām iva madhumān drapsaḥ pari vāram arṣati ||
  • Upo matiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the mind is uplifted" or "the intellect is elevated." It signifies the importance of mental clarity and focus in achieving one's goals.
  • Pṛcyate: This means "is invoked" or "is called upon." It suggests that the elevation of the mind is not a passive process; it requires active engagement and intention.
  • Sicyate: Translated as "is sprinkled" or "is nourished," this word indicates that the mind needs to be nurtured with positive thoughts and experiences, much like a plant needs water to grow.
  • Madhu mandrājanī: This phrase refers to "sweet nectar" or "honey-like essence." It symbolizes the rewards of wisdom and knowledge that come from a well-cultivated mind.
  • Codate: Meaning "stimulates" or "encourages," this word emphasizes the role of external influences in inspiring and motivating the mind.
  • Antar āsani: This translates to "inner seat" or "inner space," referring to the inner self or consciousness where true understanding resides.
  • Pavamānaḥ: This means "the purifying one" or "the one that brings clarity." It represents the cleansing of thoughts and emotions, leading to a more profound understanding of oneself.
  • Saṁtaniḥ: Translated as "the one who is steady" or "the one who is stable," this word highlights the importance of maintaining composure and balance in life.
  • Praghnatām iva: This phrase means "like the one who strikes" or "like the one who overcomes." It suggests the power of determination and resilience in facing challenges.
  • Madhumān drapsaḥ: This translates to "the one who is rich in sweetness" or "the one who possesses abundance." It symbolizes the rewards of a well-cultivated mind and the richness of life experiences.
  • Pari vāram arṣati: This means "surrounds with protection" or "envelops with care." It indicates the importance of a supportive environment in nurturing the mind and spirit.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of cultivating a focused and elevated mind through active engagement, nurturing, and positive influences. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, personal development, and mental health. For instance, students can benefit from creating a conducive learning environment that stimulates their intellect, while individuals seeking personal growth can engage in practices like meditation and mindfulness to purify their thoughts and emotions. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of the mind and the importance of surrounding oneself with positivity and support to achieve a fulfilling and enriched life.
