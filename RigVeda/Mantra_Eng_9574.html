सप्तभिः पुत्रैर अदितिर उप प्रैत पूर्व्यं युगम् |प्रजायै मृत्यवे त्वत पुनर मार्ताण्डम् आभारत ||
saptabhiḥ putrair aditir upa prait pūrvyaṁ yugam | prajāyai mṛtyave tvat punar mārtāṇḍam ābharat ||
  • Mantra: "saptabhiḥ putrair aditir upa prait pūrvyaṁ yugam | prajāyai mṛtyave tvat punar mārtāṇḍam ābharat ||"
  • Translation: "With seven sons, Aditi, may you approach the ancient age; for the sake of progeny, may you bring back the sun to the realm of death."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • saptabhiḥ: Seven - symbolizes completeness and wholeness.
    • putrair: Sons - represents progeny, lineage, and continuity of life.
    • aditir: Aditi - the mother of gods, symbolizing the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
    • upa: Approach - signifies the act of seeking or moving towards something.
    • pūrvyaṁ: Ancient - refers to the past, traditions, and the wisdom of ages.
    • yugam: Age - denotes a cycle of time, often associated with cosmic cycles.
    • prajāyai: For progeny - emphasizes the importance of offspring and continuation of life.
    • mṛtyave: To death - acknowledges the inevitability of death in the cycle of life.
    • tvat: Your - indicates possession or connection to the divine or cosmic order.
    • punar: Again - signifies repetition or return, often associated with rebirth.
    • mārtāṇḍam: Sun - symbolizes life, energy, and the source of illumination.
    • ābharat: Bring back - an action of restoration or revival.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of having "seven sons" can be seen in various cultures where large families are valued for their ability to support and sustain the family lineage.
    • Aditi, as a nurturing figure, can be compared to the role of mothers in modern society who provide unconditional love and support to their children.
    • The idea of approaching ancient wisdom can be reflected in practices like yoga and meditation, which draw from ancient texts to provide guidance in contemporary life.
    • The acknowledgment of death and the cycle of life is evident in various cultural rituals that celebrate life and honor those who have passed away.
    • The sun, as a source of life, can be related to renewable energy initiatives today, emphasizing the importance of sustainable practices for future generations.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of family, continuity, and the cyclical nature of life and death.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom from the past while nurturing future generations.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of legacy, and the need to honor both our ancestors and descendants.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a holistic approach to living, where one acknowledges the past, embraces the present, and prepares for the future.
