सुदेवोऽसि वरुण यस्य ते सप्त सिन्धवः |अनुक्षरन्ति काकुदं सूर्यम् सुशिराम् इव ||
sudevo asi varuṇa yasya te sapta sindhavaḥ | anukṣaranti kākudaṁ sūrmyaṁ suṣirām iva ||
  • Mantra: "sudevo asi varuṇa yasya te sapta sindhavaḥ | anukṣaranti kākudaṁ sūrmyaṁ suṣirām iva ||"
  • Translation: "O Varuna, you are the good deity, of whom the seven rivers flow, like the swift birds that fly in the sky."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sudevo: Good deity or divine being, representing purity and righteousness.
    • asi: You are, indicating existence or presence.
    • varuṇa: The god of water and celestial ocean, symbolizing cosmic order and moral authority.
    • yasya: Of whom, referring to Varuna.
    • te: Your, indicating possession.
    • sapta: Seven, often symbolizing completeness or totality.
    • sindhavaḥ: Rivers, representing the flow of life and sustenance.
    • anukṣaranti: They flow or move, indicating action and dynamism.
    • kākudaṁ: Like birds, symbolizing freedom and swiftness.
    • sūrmyaṁ: Swift or quick, emphasizing speed and agility.
    • suṣirām: Like a melody or sound, indicating harmony and beauty.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The rivers mentioned can be likened to the major rivers in our lives, such as the Ganges or the Nile, which are not only sources of water but also of culture, spirituality, and sustenance.
    • The concept of Varuna as a deity can be related to the importance of maintaining moral and ethical standards in our lives, akin to how leaders and role models influence society.
    • The imagery of birds flying freely can be compared to the aspirations and dreams of individuals, emphasizing the need for freedom and the pursuit of one's goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect the natural elements, such as water, which is essential for life, and to uphold moral values in our actions.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony in their lives, to act with integrity, and to appreciate the beauty and flow of life, much like the rivers and birds described.
