indrotibhir bahulābhir no adya yācchreṣṭhābhir maghavañ chūra jinva | yo no dveṣṭy adharaḥ sas padīṣṭa yam u dviṣmas tam u prāṇo jahātu ||

  • Chūra: This word means 'brave' or 'valiant,' highlighting the qualities of courage and strength.

  • Adharaḥ: This translates to 'the lower' or 'the inferior,' suggesting a contrast between higher and lower states of being.
  • Sas: This means 'with' or 'together with,' indicating a connection or relationship.
  • Padīṣṭa: This term means 'to be placed' or 'to be established,' indicating a desire for stability and grounding.
  • Yam: This means 'whom,' referring to the subject of the mantra's focus.
  • U: This is a conjunction meaning 'and' or 'also,' linking ideas together.
  • Dviṣmas: This means 'we hate' or 'we oppose,' indicating a collective stance against negativity.
  • Tam: This means 'that' or 'him,' referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • U: Again, this conjunction links ideas.
