वयं ते वय इन्द्र विद्धि षु णः प्र भरामहे वाजयुर न रथम् |विपण्यवो दीध्यतो मनिषा सुम्नम इयक्षन्तस तवावतो नृन् ||
vayaṁ te vaya indra viddhi ṣu ṇaḥ pra bharāmahe vājayur na ratham | vipanyavo dīdhyato manīṣā sumnam iyakṣantas tvāvato nṝn ||
  • Mantra: "vayaṁ te vaya indra viddhi ṣu ṇaḥ pra bharāmahe vājayur na ratham | vipanyavo dīdhyato manīṣā sumnam iyakṣantas tvāvato nṝn ||"
  • Translation: "We are your friends, O Indra, know us; we are the strong ones who bring forth the chariot. The wise ones, who are thoughtful, seek your favor, and those who are devoted to you, O Indra, are blessed."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • vayaṁ te: "We are yours" - signifies a sense of belonging and connection.
    • vaya: "friends" - indicates camaraderie and support.
    • indra: "O Indra" - refers to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and leadership.
    • viddhi: "know" - a call for recognition and understanding.
    • ṣu ṇaḥ: "the strong ones" - refers to those who are capable and powerful.
    • pra bharāmahe: "we bring forth" - implies action and initiative.
    • vājayur: "the chariot" - symbolizes progress and movement in life.
    • na ratham: "not the chariot" - suggests a distinction between mere existence and purposeful action.
    • vipanyavo: "the wise ones" - refers to those who are knowledgeable and insightful.
    • dīdhyato: "thoughtful" - emphasizes the importance of contemplation and wisdom.
    • manīṣā: "favor" - indicates a desire for blessings and support.
    • sumnam: "blessed" - refers to the state of being fortunate or favored.
    • iyakṣantas: "those who are devoted" - signifies dedication and loyalty.
    • tvāvato: "O Indra" - a reiteration of the appeal to the divine.
    • nṝn: "to men" - signifies humanity and the collective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the mantra reflects the importance of collaboration and mutual support, where each member recognizes their role and contributes to a common goal.
    • In leadership, it emphasizes the need for leaders to acknowledge and understand their followers, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the value of friendship and loyalty, where individuals support each other through challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in human relationships and endeavors.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, leadership, and personal connections, reminding us that collective effort and mutual respect lead to success.
    • By invoking the blessings of a higher power, it encourages individuals to seek guidance and support in their pursuits, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
