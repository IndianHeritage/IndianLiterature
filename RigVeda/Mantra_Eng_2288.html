अपो सु म्याक्ष वरुण भियसमत सम्राज ऋतावोऽनु मां गृभाय | दामेव वत्साद विमुमुग्ध्य अंहो न हि त्वदारे निमिषश्चनेशे ||
apo su myakṣa varuṇa bhiyasam mat samrāḻ ṛtāvo 'nu mā gṛbhāya | dāmeva vatsād vi mumugdhy aṁho nahi tvad āre nimiṣaś caneśe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apo: waters
    • su: good
    • myakṣa: may you grant
    • varuṇa: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • bhiyasam: from fear
    • mat: my
    • samrāḻ: ruler
    • ṛtāvo: of truth
    • 'nu: indeed
    • mā: do not
    • gṛbhāya: seize
    • dāmeva: like a gift
    • vatsād: from the calf
    • vi: indeed
    • mumugdhy: may you free
    • aṁho: from this
    • nahi: not
    • tvad: your
    • āre: enemies
    • nimiṣaś: in the blink of an eye
    • caneśe: O Lord of the heavens
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Varuna, the deity associated with water and cosmic order, asking for protection and freedom from fear.
    • The speaker seeks the blessings of Varuna to not let their fears seize them, emphasizing the importance of truth and righteousness in life.
    • The reference to "like a gift from the calf" symbolizes innocence and purity, suggesting that the speaker wishes to receive blessings in a pure and untainted manner.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, such as during a health crisis or personal loss, individuals often turn to spiritual practices or prayers for comfort and guidance, similar to the invocation of Varuna for protection.
    • In a professional setting, when facing challenges or competition, one might seek to uphold truth and integrity, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on righteousness.
    • Parents often teach their children the values of honesty and purity, akin to the symbolism of the calf, nurturing them to grow up free from fear and deceit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine protection and the importance of living a life rooted in truth and righteousness.
    • It serves as a reminder that in the face of fear and challenges, one can find solace in spiritual practices and the pursuit of moral values.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, encouraging individuals to maintain integrity and seek support from higher powers during difficult times.
