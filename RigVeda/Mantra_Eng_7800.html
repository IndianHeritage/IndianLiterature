यः सोमः कलशेष्व आमन्तः पवित्र आहितः |तम इन्दुः परि शस्वजे ||
yaḥ somaḥ kalaśeṣv ām̐ antaḥ pavitra āhitaḥ | tam induḥ pari ṣasvaje ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • somaḥ: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • kalaśeṣv: in the vessels
    • ām̐: indeed
    • antaḥ: within
    • pavitra: pure
    • āhitaḥ: placed or offered
    • tam: that
    • induḥ: the moon or nectar
    • pari: all around
    • ṣasvaje: may it be invoked or may it shine
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sacred soma, which is often associated with divine inspiration and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The phrase "in the vessels" suggests that the essence of soma is contained within, symbolizing the inner purity and potential of an individual.
    • The invocation of "that moon" or "nectar" signifies the desire for illumination and wisdom, which can be drawn from the divine source.
    • The term "may it shine" indicates a wish for the blessings of this divine essence to manifest in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, the "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment through education and self-discovery.
    • Just as the soma is offered in vessels, individuals can cultivate their inner purity through practices like meditation, yoga, and mindfulness.
    • The invocation of divine wisdom can be seen in the way people seek guidance from mentors, spiritual leaders, or through introspection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner purity and the quest for divine wisdom.
    • It serves as a reminder that true enlightenment comes from within and can be nurtured through conscious effort and spiritual practices.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge, maintain purity of thought and action, and invoke the divine for guidance in their personal and spiritual journeys.
