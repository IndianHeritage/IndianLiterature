हिमेनाग्निं घ्रम्सम् अवारयेताम् पितुमतीं ऊर्जम् अस्मा अधत्तम् |ऋबीसे अत्रिम अश्विनावनीतम् उन्निन्यतुः सर्वगणं स्वस्ति ||
himenāgniṁ ghraṁsam avārayethām pitumatīm ūrjam asmā adhattam | ṛbīse atrim aśvināvanītam un ninyathuḥ sarvagaṇaṁ svasti ||
  • Mantra: himenāgniṁ ghraṁsam avārayethām pitumatīm ūrjam asmā adhattam | ṛbīse atrim aśvināvanītam un ninyathuḥ sarvagaṇaṁ svasti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • himena: with the essence of warmth or energy
    • agniṁ: fire (often symbolizes knowledge, transformation, and purification)
    • ghraṁsam: to be grasped or held
    • avārayethām: may it be protected or preserved
    • pitumatīm: maternal or nurturing (often refers to the Earth or nature)
    • ūrjam: energy or strength
    • asmā: us or our
    • adhattam: may it be bestowed or granted
    • ṛbīse: to the seers or wise ones
    • atrim: to Atri (a sage or deity)
    • aśvinā: to the Ashvins (divine twin horsemen, symbolizing health and medicine)
    • avanītam: may it be brought down or made accessible
    • un: not or without
    • ninyathuḥ: may they carry or convey
    • sarvagaṇaṁ: all beings or all groups
    • svasti: well-being or auspiciousness
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of fire, representing knowledge and transformation, and asks for its protection and nurturing.
    • The reference to "pitumatīm" emphasizes the importance of nature and the Earth in providing strength and energy to us.
    • By calling upon the wise seers and the divine Ashvins, the mantra seeks blessings for health, well-being, and the ability to share these gifts with all beings.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call to respect and protect our environment, as well as to seek knowledge and wisdom from those who have come before us.
    • It also highlights the importance of community and the interconnectedness of all beings, encouraging us to work together for the common good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing knowledge and energy while respecting the Earth and its resources.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as environmental conservation, community health initiatives, and the pursuit of wisdom through education.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, fostering well-being for all.
