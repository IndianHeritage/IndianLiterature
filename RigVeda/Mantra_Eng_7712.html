एष देवो विपाकृतोऽतिः हवरांसी धावति। पवमानो अदाभ्यः॥
eṣa devo vipā kṛto 'ti hvarāṁsi dhāvati | pavamāno adābhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • devo: god or divine being
    • vipā: who is invoked or worshipped
    • kṛto: made or created
    • 'ti: indeed or truly
    • hvarāṁsi: offerings or sacrifices
    • dhāvati: runs or moves swiftly
    • pavamāno: the purifying one
    • adābhyaḥ: from the unworthy or impure
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is invoked through offerings and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "runs swiftly" suggests the immediacy and responsiveness of the divine to the offerings made by devotees.
    • The term "pavamāno" indicates a purifying aspect, emphasizing the importance of purity in both the offerings and the intentions behind them.
    • The reference to "adābhyaḥ" highlights the need to distance oneself from impurity or unworthiness, suggesting that the divine is approached with sincerity and purity of heart.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to deities to seek blessings, such as lighting candles in churches or offering flowers in temples.
    • The concept of purity can be seen in practices like fasting or cleansing before significant religious events, which aligns with the idea of approaching the divine with a pure heart.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be related to the idea of mindfulness and intention in our actions, where we strive to act with purity and sincerity in our daily lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of approaching the divine with purity and sincerity through offerings and sacrifices.
    • It serves as a reminder that our intentions and the purity of our actions matter when seeking a connection with the divine.
    • In real life, this can be applied by fostering a mindset of mindfulness, ensuring that our actions are aligned with our values, and striving for purity in our thoughts and deeds.
