प्र तत ते अद्यां करणं कृतम् भूत कुत्सं यद आयुम् अतिथिग्वम् अस्मै |पुरू सहस्राणि शिशा अभि क्षाम उत तूर्वयाणं धृतता निनेत ||
pra tat te adyā karaṇaṁ kṛtam bhūt kutsaṁ yad āyum atithigvam asmai | purū sahasrā ni śiśā abhi kṣām ut tūrvayāṇaṁ dhṛṣatā ninetha ||
  • Translation: "May this action of yours today, O Lord, be fruitful; may it bring forth the essence of life, the strength of the guest. May you grant us a thousand powers, and may we be able to overcome obstacles with courage."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for, towards
    • tat: that
    • te: your
    • adyā: today
    • karaṇaṁ: action, deed
    • kṛtam: done, performed
    • bhūt: essence, being
    • kutsaṁ: strength, power
    • yad: which
    • āyum: life, longevity
    • atithigvam: guest, visitor
    • asmai: to us
    • purū: many, numerous
    • sahasrā: thousands
    • ni: not
    • śiśā: to be overcome
    • abhi: towards
    • kṣām: strength, power
    • ut: and
    • tūrvayāṇaṁ: with courage
    • dhṛṣatā: with bravery
    • ninetha: lead, guide
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of actions taken with intention and purpose. It reflects the belief that our deeds can lead to fruitful outcomes when aligned with higher principles.
    • The reference to "the strength of the guest" highlights the significance of hospitality and the respect given to visitors, which is a common cultural value in many societies today.
    • The call for "a thousand powers" signifies the desire for abundance and the ability to face challenges, which resonates with individuals striving for success in various aspects of life, such as career, relationships, and personal growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new project. The team can invoke this sentiment to ensure their efforts are fruitful and that they are prepared to face any challenges that arise.
    • In personal life, when hosting guests, one can reflect on the importance of making them feel welcome and valued, thereby strengthening relationships and fostering goodwill.
    • In educational settings, students can use this mantra as a motivation to approach their studies with courage and determination, seeking knowledge and overcoming obstacles in their learning journey.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purposeful action, the value of hospitality, and the pursuit of strength in overcoming challenges. It serves as a reminder that our intentions and actions can lead to positive outcomes, both for ourselves and for those around us. In real life, it encourages individuals to act with courage and to embrace the power of community and support in their endeavors.
