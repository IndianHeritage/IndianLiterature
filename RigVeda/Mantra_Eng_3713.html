सा स्मा कृणोति केतुम् आ नक्तं चिद् दूर आ सते |पावकः यद् वनस्पतिं प्र स्मा मिनात्य् अजरोः ||
sa smā kṛṇoti ketum ā naktaṁ cid dūra ā sate | pāvako yad vanaspatīn pra smā mināty ajaraḥ ||
  • sa smā kṛṇoti ketum: This phrase can be translated as "He who creates the banner." In this context, "He" refers to a divine force or energy that brings forth order and structure in the universe. The "banner" symbolizes a guiding principle or a source of inspiration.
  • ā naktaṁ cid dūra ā sate: This part translates to "from the night, he brings forth light." Here, "night" represents ignorance or chaos, while "light" symbolizes knowledge and clarity. This line emphasizes the transformative power of divine energy in dispelling darkness and confusion.
  • pāvako yad vanaspatīn: This translates to "the fire that purifies the plants." "Pāvako" refers to fire, which is often seen as a purifying force in many cultures. "Vanaspatīn" refers to plants or nature. This line highlights the importance of purification and growth, suggesting that just as fire can cleanse, so too can knowledge and wisdom nurture growth.
  • pra smā mināty ajaraḥ: This can be interpreted as "he who transcends decay." "Ajaraḥ" means immortal or eternal, indicating that the divine force is beyond the limitations of time and decay. This line speaks to the idea of enduring truth and the eternal nature of the spirit.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of transformation, purification, and the pursuit of knowledge. It signifies the journey from ignorance to enlightenment, emphasizing the role of divine energy in guiding individuals through life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and clarity in their lives, moving away from confusion and ignorance.
  • In environmental contexts, it highlights the importance of purification and sustainability, reminding us of our responsibility to care for nature.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of the soul and the importance of seeking higher truths beyond the material world.

Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the transformative power of knowledge, the importance of purification, and the eternal nature of existence, guiding individuals towards a more enlightened and purposeful life.
