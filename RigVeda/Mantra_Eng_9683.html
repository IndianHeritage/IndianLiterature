मन्त्र: सूर्यायै देवभ्यो मित्राय वरुणाय च |ये भूतस्य प्रचेतस इदं तेभ्योऽकरं नमः ||
sūryāyai devebhyo mitrāya varuṇāya ca | ye bhūtasya pracetasa idaṁ tebhyo 'karaṁ namaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryāyai: To the Sun (Sūrya), the divine light and energy source.
    • devebhyo: To the gods (devas), the celestial beings.
    • mitrāya: To Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony.
    • varuṇāya: To Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • ye: Those who.
    • bhūtasya: Of the created beings or existence.
    • pracetasa: With consciousness or awareness.
    • idaṁ: This.
    • tebhyo: To them.
    • 'karaṁ: Offering or action.
    • namaḥ: Salutations or reverence.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer or invocation directed towards the Sun and the divine beings, specifically addressing Mitra and Varuna.
    • It acknowledges the consciousness present in all beings and offers reverence to the divine forces that govern existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate to the Sun as a source of energy and vitality. Just as the Sun nourishes life on Earth, individuals can seek to cultivate positive energy and harmony in their relationships, akin to the qualities of Mitra.
    • Varuna represents the moral and ethical order of the universe. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining integrity and balance in one’s actions, reflecting the cosmic order that Varuna embodies.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that influence our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate awareness and consciousness in our actions, promoting harmony and balance in our interactions with others.
    • In practical terms, reciting this mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their relationships and ethical responsibilities, fostering a sense of community and respect for the natural order.
