मन्त्र: वेद यस् त्रीणि विदथान्येषां देवानां जन्म सन्तर आ च विप्रः |ऋजु मार्तेषु वृजिनां च पश्यन्न अभि चष्टे सूरो आर्य एवन् ||
veda yas trīṇi vidathāny eṣāṁ devānāṁ janma sanutar ā ca vipraḥ | ṛju marteṣu vṛjinā ca paśyann abhi caṣṭe sūro arya evān ||
  • veda: This term refers to knowledge or wisdom, particularly the sacred knowledge contained in the Vedas, which are ancient Indian scriptures.
  • yas: This means "who" or "that," indicating a subject or a person.
  • trīṇi: This translates to "three," suggesting a triad or a set of three important elements.
  • vidathāny: This word can be interpreted as "the places of assembly" or "the gatherings," referring to the spaces where knowledge and wisdom are shared.
  • eṣāṁ: This means "of these," indicating a connection to the previously mentioned subjects.
  • devānāṁ: This translates to "of the gods," referring to divine beings or higher powers.
  • janma: This means "birth" or "origin," indicating the source or beginning of something.
  • sanutar: This can be interpreted as "well-being" or "prosperity," suggesting a positive outcome or state of existence.
  • ā ca: This means "and also," connecting ideas or elements together.
  • vipraḥ: This refers to a "sage" or "wise person," someone who possesses knowledge and insight.
  • ṛju: This translates to "straight" or "honest," indicating integrity and truthfulness.
  • marteṣu: This means "among mortals," referring to human beings and their experiences.
  • vṛjinā: This can be interpreted as "obstacles" or "afflictions," indicating challenges faced in life.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
  • paśyann: This translates to "seeing" or "perceiving," indicating awareness and understanding.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting movement or intention.
  • caṣṭe: This can be interpreted as "acts" or "performs," indicating action or behavior.
  • sūro: This translates to "hero" or "brave one," referring to someone who exhibits courage and strength.
  • arya: This means "noble" or "honorable," indicating a person of high moral character.
  • evān: This can be interpreted as "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the truth of the statement.

The mantra speaks to the importance of knowledge and wisdom, particularly in the context of divine guidance and human experience. It emphasizes the role of sages and wise individuals in navigating life's challenges with integrity and courage. The mention of "three" could symbolize the triad of thought, word, and action, which are essential for a balanced life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In education, where the pursuit of knowledge is paramount, and the guidance of wise teachers can lead to personal and communal growth.
  • In leadership, where integrity and courage are essential for making decisions that affect others, ensuring that actions are aligned with noble values.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive to overcome obstacles and challenges by seeking wisdom and acting with honesty.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the pursuit of knowledge, the importance of wise guidance, and the necessity of integrity in human actions. It serves as a reminder that by embracing these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges effectively and contribute positively to society.
