यदि स्तोमं मम श्रवद अस्माकं इन्द्रं इन्दवः |तिरः पवित्रं ससृवांस आाशवो मन्दन्तु तुग्र्यावृद्धः ||
yadi stomam mama śravad asmākam indram indavaḥ | tiraḥ pavitraṁ sasṛvāṁsa āśavo mandantu tugryāvṛdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadi: if
    • stomam: hymn or praise
    • mama: my
    • śravad: to be heard or listened to
    • asmākam: our
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • indavaḥ: the drinkers of nectar or those who partake in divine bliss
    • tiraḥ: beyond or across
    • pavitraṁ: pure
    • sasṛvāṁsa: those who flow or move
    • āśavo: swift or quick
    • mandantu: may they be favorable or auspicious
    • tugryāvṛdhaḥ: those who are strong and powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for the divine presence of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to listen to the hymns offered by the devotees.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and the flow of divine energy, suggesting that the blessings of the gods can be invoked through sincere prayers.
    • The mention of "drinkers of nectar" symbolizes those who are spiritually enlightened and connected to divine wisdom.
    • The plea for swiftness and strength indicates a yearning for rapid and powerful blessings in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of drought, communities often come together to perform rituals and prayers to invoke rain, similar to how this mantra seeks the attention of Indra for blessings.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to seek divine guidance and support, hoping for purity in their intentions and strength in their actions.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people may chant this mantra to invite auspiciousness and divine favor.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of devotion, purity, and the pursuit of divine blessings in everyday life.
    • It serves as a reminder that through sincere prayers and actions, individuals can connect with higher powers and seek guidance in their endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a harmonious relationship with the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared spiritual aspirations.
