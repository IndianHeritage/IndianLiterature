परि णेतā मतीनां विश्वदेवो अदाभ्यः |सोमः पुनानश चाम्वोर विशद धरीः ||
pari ṇetā matīnāṁ viśvadevo adābhyaḥ | somaḥ punānaś camvor viśad dhariḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari ṇetā: The one who leads or guides all around.
    • matīnāṁ: Of the minds or intellects.
    • viśvadevo: The universal deities or divine beings.
    • adābhyaḥ: From the unblemished or pure.
    • somaḥ: The divine nectar or essence, often associated with immortality.
    • punānaś: Purifying or cleansing.
    • camvor: Of the bodies or forms.
    • viśad: Clear or pure.
    • dhariḥ: The one who holds or sustains.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the guidance provided by divine forces to the intellects of individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and clarity in thought and action.
    • The reference to "soma" suggests a connection to spiritual nourishment and the quest for enlightenment.
    • It highlights the role of universal deities in guiding humanity towards a higher understanding and purity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek guidance from mentors or advisors (the "pari ṇetā") to make informed decisions that benefit the organization (the "matīnāṁ").
    • In personal development, individuals may turn to spiritual practices or meditation (the "somaḥ") to cleanse their minds and achieve clarity (the "viśad").
    • In community service, volunteers embody the essence of "viśvadevo" by working selflessly for the betterment of society, guided by pure intentions (the "adābhyaḥ").
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers and maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including leadership, personal growth, and community service.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for clarity, purity, and a deeper connection with the divine, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened life.
