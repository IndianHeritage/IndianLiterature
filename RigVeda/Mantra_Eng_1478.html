दूरि चत्ताय च्छन्तसद गहनं यद इनक्षत् |अस्माकं शत्रून् परि शूर विश्वतो धर्मा दर्शीष्ट विश्वतः ||
dūre cattāya cchantsad gahanaṁ yad inakṣat | asmākaṁ śatrūn pari śūra viśvato darmā darṣīṣṭa viśvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūre: far away
    • cattāya: to the enemies
    • cchantsad: who are hostile
    • gahanaṁ: dense or difficult
    • yad: that which
    • inakṣat: has been seen or perceived
    • asmākaṁ: our
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • pari: all around
    • śūra: brave or valiant
    • viśvato: from all sides
    • darmā: righteousness or duty
    • darṣīṣṭa: has been shown or revealed
    • viśvataḥ: from all directions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the distance from enemies and the challenges posed by them.
    • It emphasizes the importance of bravery and righteousness in facing adversities.
    • The phrase "dūre cattāya" suggests a desire to keep enemies at bay, highlighting the need for protection and security.
    • The mention of "gahanaṁ" indicates that the challenges may be complex and difficult to navigate.
    • It calls for a collective effort ("asmākaṁ") to confront these challenges with courage and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a company faces competition. The need to maintain a competitive edge while upholding ethical practices reflects the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter conflicts or challenges in relationships. Approaching these situations with bravery and a sense of duty can lead to resolution and harmony.
    • In a community context, this mantra can inspire collective action against social injustices, encouraging individuals to stand together for righteousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for courage and righteousness in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges, whether personal, professional, or societal, can be overcome through collective strength and ethical conduct.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and just society, where individuals support each other in overcoming obstacles.
