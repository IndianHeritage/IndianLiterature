परिं घृणा चरति तित्विषे शवोऽपो वृत्वी रजसो बुद्धं आशयत् |वृत्रस्य यत प्रवणे दुर्गृभिश्वनो निजघन्थ हन्वोर इन्द्र तान्यतुम् ||
parīṁ ghṛṇā carati titviṣe śavo 'po vṛtvī rajaso budhnam āśayat | vṛtrasya yat pravaṇe durgṛbhiśvano nijaghantha hanvor indra tanyatum ||
  • Mantra: "parīṁ ghṛṇā carati titviṣe śavo 'po vṛtvī rajaso budhnam āśayat | vṛtrasya yat pravaṇe durgṛbhiśvano nijaghantha hanvor indra tanyatum ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • parīṁ: around, encompassing
    • ghṛṇā: compassion, understanding
    • carati: moves, acts
    • titviṣe: for the powerful, the strong
    • śavo: of the dead, lifeless
    • 'po: water, life-giving force
    • vṛtvī: having strength, power
    • rajaso: of the mind, mental energy
    • budhnam: of wisdom, intelligence
    • āśayat: to dwell, to reside
    • vṛtrasya: of Vritra, the obstructive force
    • yat: that which
    • pravaṇe: in the act of moving, flowing
    • durgṛbhiś: with great difficulty, obstacles
    • vano: to be overcome, to be defeated
    • nijaghantha: to strike down, to defeat
    • hanvor: of the slayer, the destroyer
    • indra: the king of gods, the powerful
    • tanyatum: may it be extended, may it be spread
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of compassion and understanding that moves through the strong and powerful.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and mental energy in overcoming obstacles and challenges.
    • The reference to Vritra symbolizes the challenges and adversities in life that need to be confronted and defeated.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate power and strength that can help in overcoming these challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who shows compassion and understanding can inspire their team to overcome challenges and work together effectively.
    • In personal relationships, showing empathy can help resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
    • In education, a teacher who encourages critical thinking and wisdom can empower students to face academic challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of compassion, wisdom, and strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that by embodying these qualities, we can confront and defeat the obstacles we face, much like Indra defeating Vritra.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and success in various endeavors.
