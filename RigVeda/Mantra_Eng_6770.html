ऋभुमन्ता वृषणā वाजवन्तā मरुत्वन्तā जारितुर गच्छथो हवम् |सजोषसā उषसā सूर्येण चादित्यैर यातम् अश्विनā ||
ṛbhumantā vṛṣaṇā vājavantā marutvantā jaritur gacchatho havam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cādityair yātam aśvinā ||
  • ṛbhumantā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with righteousness" or "those who are virtuous." In real life, this can refer to individuals or leaders who act with integrity and uphold moral values in their decisions and actions.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word signifies "the strong" or "the powerful." It can be related to people who possess strength, whether physical, mental, or emotional. For example, a community leader who stands firm in the face of adversity embodies this quality.
  • vājavantā: This term translates to "those who are rich in resources" or "those who possess wealth." In a modern context, this could refer to individuals or organizations that have the means to support others, such as philanthropists or successful businesses that contribute to social causes.
  • marutvantā: This can be understood as "those who are powerful like the winds" or "the stormy ones." This might symbolize individuals who bring about change or transformation, akin to activists or innovators who challenge the status quo.
  • jaritur: This word means "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." In everyday life, this could refer to educators or mentors who nurture and develop the potential in others, helping them to grow and succeed.
  • gacchatho havam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you go towards the offerings" or "may you approach the sacrifices." It signifies the importance of making offerings or contributions, whether in a spiritual sense or through acts of kindness and generosity in society.
  • sajoṣasā: This term means "with joy" or "with enthusiasm." It emphasizes the importance of approaching life and duties with a positive attitude, which can lead to better outcomes and a more fulfilling life.
  • uṣasā: This word refers to "the dawn" or "the new beginning." It symbolizes hope and the potential for renewal. In real life, this can be seen in the context of new opportunities, such as starting a new job or embarking on a new project.
  • sūryeṇa: This translates to "with the sun" or "by the light of the sun." The sun is often associated with energy, vitality, and clarity. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of optimism and clarity in decision-making.
  • cādityair: This term refers to "with the Adityas," who are solar deities. It signifies the collective strength and support of a community or group working together towards a common goal.
  • yātam aśvinā: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be guided by the Ashvins," who are twin deities representing health and well-being. It suggests seeking guidance and support from those who embody wisdom and care.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness, strength, resourcefulness, and the power of community. It encourages individuals to approach life with joy and optimism, to contribute positively to society, and to seek guidance from wise figures. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, community service, and personal development, reminding us of the values that lead to a harmonious and prosperous life.
