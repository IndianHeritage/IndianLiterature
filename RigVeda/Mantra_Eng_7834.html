प्र त्वा नमोभिर इन्दव इन्द्र सोमाऽसृक्षत | महे भराय कारीणः ||
pra tvā namobhir indava indra somā asṛkṣata | mahe bharāya kāriṇaḥ ||
  • pra tvā: This phrase translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction of reverence and devotion towards a higher power.
  • namobhir: This means "with salutations" or "with reverence," emphasizing the act of bowing or showing respect.
  • indava: Refers to "the Indus" or "the divine," often associated with the celestial or spiritual essence.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the force of nature.
  • somā: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • asṛkṣata: This term means "to protect" or "to guard," indicating a sense of safeguarding or nurturing.
  • mahe: Translates to "great" or "mighty," often used to describe the vastness of the universe or the power of the divine.
  • bharāya: This means "to bear" or "to carry," suggesting the act of supporting or sustaining.
  • kāriṇaḥ: This term refers to "the doer" or "the one who acts," indicating an active force in the universe.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer that invokes the divine presence, specifically addressing Indra and the essence of soma.
    • It expresses a desire for protection and support from the divine forces, acknowledging their greatness and power.
    • The mantra can be seen as a call for strength and guidance in times of need, much like how individuals seek support from mentors or leaders in their lives.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In challenging situations, such as during a personal crisis or a significant life decision, individuals often turn to spiritual practices or prayers for guidance and strength.
    • In a community setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of unity and strength, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for support.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, participants may recite this mantra to seek blessings and protection for their endeavors, akin to how people seek the blessings of elders or spiritual leaders.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of human experience with the divine, reminding us to acknowledge and respect the forces that support us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of uncertainty, as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or community support.
