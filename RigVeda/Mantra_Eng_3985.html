प्र सुष्टुतिः स्तनयन्तं रुवन्तम इळस पतिं जरितर नूनम अश्याः |यो अब्दिमां उदनिमां इयर्ति प्र विद्युतां रोडसी उक्षमाणः ||
pra suṣṭutiḥ stanayantaṁ ruvantam iḻas patiṁ jaritar nūnam aśyāḥ | yo abdimām̐ udanimām̐ iyarti pra vidyutā rodasī ukṣamāṇaḥ ||
  • pra suṣṭutiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "well-being" or "prosperity." It signifies a state of harmony and abundance in life.
  • stanayantaṁ: This word refers to "nourishing" or "sustaining." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and supporting life.
  • ruvantam: This term means "crying" or "lamenting." It can represent the struggles and challenges that individuals face in life.
  • iḻas patiṁ: This phrase translates to "the lord of the earth" or "the ruler of the world." It signifies a higher power or authority that governs the universe.
  • jaritar: This word means "old" or "aged." It can symbolize wisdom gained through experience over time.
  • nūnam: This term can be interpreted as "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statement being made.
  • aśyāḥ: This word refers to "the living beings" or "creatures." It encompasses all forms of life that inhabit the earth.
  • yo abdimām̐: This phrase translates to "who moves the ocean" or "who stirs the waters." It signifies a powerful force that can influence vast and deep aspects of existence.
  • udanimām̐: This term means "the sky" or "the heavens." It represents the vastness and the higher realms of existence.
  • iyarti: This word means "to move" or "to stir." It indicates action and the dynamic nature of life.
  • pra vidyutā: This phrase translates to "with lightning" or "with brilliance." It symbolizes energy, illumination, and the power of knowledge.
  • rodasī: This term refers to "the two worlds" or "the earth and sky." It signifies the connection between different realms of existence.
  • ukṣamāṇaḥ: This word means "stirring" or "shaking." It indicates movement and change, reflecting the dynamic nature of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, highlighting the balance between prosperity and struggle, the nurturing of existence, and the influence of higher powers. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all living beings and the forces that govern our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community support, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to embrace both the challenges and the blessings of life, fostering a sense of harmony and purpose in their journey.
