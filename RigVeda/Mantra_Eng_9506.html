वसिष्ठासः पितृवत वाचम् अक्रत देवाम् ईळाना ऋषिवत स्वस्तये |प्रियता इव ज्ञातयः कामम् एत्यास्मे देवासोऽव धूनुतā वसु ||
vasiṣṭhāsaḥ pitṛvad vācam akrata devām̐ īḻānā ṛṣivat svastaye | prītā iva jñātayaḥ kāmam etyāsme devāso 'va dhūnutā vasu ||
  • vasiṣṭhāsaḥ: This term refers to the wise or the enlightened ones. In a real-life context, it can be associated with individuals who possess deep knowledge and understanding, such as teachers or mentors who guide others.
  • pitṛvad: Meaning "like a father," this phrase emphasizes the nurturing and protective qualities of a father figure. In everyday life, this can relate to parental guidance and the importance of familial bonds in providing support and wisdom.
  • vācam akrata: This translates to "speech that is not spoken." It suggests the power of unexpressed thoughts or intentions. In modern scenarios, this can be likened to non-verbal communication, where actions speak louder than words.
  • devām̐ īḻānā: This means "invoking the divine." It highlights the importance of spirituality and seeking blessings from higher powers. In real life, this can be seen in rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection where individuals seek guidance or strength.
  • ṛṣivat: This term refers to the qualities of a sage or seer. It signifies wisdom and insight. In practical terms, this can be related to individuals who provide valuable advice based on their experiences, such as counselors or spiritual leaders.
  • svastaye: Meaning "for well-being," this emphasizes the pursuit of health and prosperity. In contemporary life, this can be connected to wellness practices, such as yoga and meditation, aimed at achieving a balanced and healthy lifestyle.
  • prītā iva jñātayaḥ: This translates to "like joyful relatives." It signifies the importance of community and relationships. In real life, this can be observed in gatherings, celebrations, and the support systems we build with friends and family.
  • kāmam etyāsme: Meaning "may desires come to us," this reflects the human aspiration for fulfillment and happiness. In practical terms, this can relate to goal-setting and the pursuit of personal dreams and ambitions.
  • devāso 'va dhūnutā vasu: This phrase means "may the divine remove obstacles." It emphasizes the belief in divine intervention in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be seen in moments of prayer or meditation where individuals seek help in difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, guidance, and the pursuit of well-being through divine connection and community support. Its significance lies in the reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships, seeking knowledge, and striving for personal fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate meaningful connections, embrace spiritual practices, and pursue their goals with the belief that divine support is available in overcoming life's challenges.
