शं नः सत्यस्य पतयो भवन्तु शं नो अर्वन्तः शमु सन्तु गावाḥ |शं न ऋभवः सुक्रितः सुहस्ताः शं नो भवन्तु पितरो हवेषु ||
śaṁ naḥ satyasya patayo bhavantu śaṁ no arvantaḥ śam u santu gāvaḥ | śaṁ na ṛbhavaḥ sukṛtaḥ suhastāḥ śaṁ no bhavantu pitaro haveṣu ||
  • śaṁ naḥ satyasya patayo bhavantu: This phrase translates to "May the protectors of truth be auspicious to us." Here, "śaṁ" signifies peace or auspiciousness, "satyasya" refers to truth, and "patayo" means protectors or guardians. In real life, this can be related to the importance of having truthful leaders or mentors who guide us towards righteousness.
  • śaṁ no arvantaḥ: This means "May those who are worthy bring us peace." The term "arvantaḥ" refers to those who are deserving or virtuous. This can be seen in the context of community leaders or role models who embody integrity and inspire others to follow a path of virtue.
  • śam u santu gāvaḥ: This translates to "May the cows be peaceful." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and non-violence. This line emphasizes the importance of harmony in nature and agriculture, which is crucial for sustenance and prosperity in society.
  • śaṁ na ṛbhavaḥ sukṛtaḥ suhastāḥ: This means "May those who are righteous and have good hands bring us peace." "Ṛbhavaḥ" refers to the righteous, "sukṛtaḥ" means good deeds, and "suhastāḥ" indicates those with good actions. This can be related to individuals in society who contribute positively through their actions, such as volunteers or social workers, promoting peace and well-being.
  • śaṁ no bhavantu pitaro haveṣu: This translates to "May our ancestors bring us peace." "Pitaro" refers to ancestors or forefathers, and "haveṣu" indicates offerings or respect. This highlights the significance of honoring our heritage and the values passed down through generations, which can guide us in making ethical decisions in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, righteousness, and harmony in both personal and communal life. It calls for the blessings of virtuous leaders, the sanctity of nature, and the respect for our ancestors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as community building, ethical leadership, environmental conservation, and honoring family traditions. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can strive for a more peaceful and prosperous existence.
