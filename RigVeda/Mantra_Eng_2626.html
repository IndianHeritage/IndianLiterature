तुभ्यं श्चोतन्त्य अध्रिगो शचिवः स्तोकासो अग्ने मेदसो घृतस्य | कविशस्तो बृहता भानुनागा हव्याः जुषस्व मेधिर ||
tubhyaṁ ścotanty adhrigo śacīvaḥ stokāso agne medaso ghṛtasya | kaviśasto bṛhatā bhānunāgā havyā juṣasva medhira ||
  • tubhyaṁ - This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion or offering directed towards a divine entity.
  • ścotanty - Meaning "they who shine" or "they who illuminate," this word suggests the presence of light or knowledge, often associated with wisdom and enlightenment.
  • adhrigo - This can be interpreted as "the one who is steadfast" or "the unshakable," symbolizing stability and reliability.
  • śacīvaḥ - Translated as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who possesses strength," this word emphasizes the importance of strength in various forms, whether physical, mental, or spiritual.
  • stokāso - This means "small" or "little," which can refer to humble beginnings or the idea that even small contributions can lead to significant outcomes.
  • agne - Referring to "Agni," the fire god, this word symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • medaso - Meaning "of wisdom" or "of intellect," this word highlights the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.
  • ghṛtasya - This translates to "of ghee" or "of clarified butter," which is often used in Vedic rituals as an offering, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • kaviśasto - Meaning "the one who is wise" or "the poet," this word signifies the value of creativity and insight in human experience.
  • bṛhatā - This translates to "the great" or "the vast," indicating the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.
  • bhānunāgā - Meaning "the one who is radiant" or "the sun," this word symbolizes illumination, guidance, and the life-giving force of the sun.
  • havyā - This refers to "the offerings" or "the sacred," emphasizing the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • juṣasva - Meaning "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," this word expresses a desire for the divine to accept the offerings made.
  • medhira - This translates to "of intelligence" or "of wisdom," reinforcing the theme of seeking knowledge and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion, wisdom, and the transformative power of offerings. It highlights the relationship between the devotee and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and strength in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or moments of seeking guidance. It serves as a reminder that even small acts of devotion and the pursuit of wisdom can lead to significant spiritual growth and enlightenment.
