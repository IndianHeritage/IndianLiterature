ववक्ष इन्द्रो अमितं ऋजिष्युंभे आप प्रौ रोदसी महित्वा |अतश्चिदस्य महिमा वि रेच्य अभि यो विश्वा भुवना बभूव ||
vavakṣa indro amitam ṛjīṣy u1bhe ā paprau rodasī mahitvā | ataś cid asya mahimā vi recy abhi yo viśvā bhuvanā babhūva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vavakṣa: to be strong or powerful
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • amitam: immeasurable or infinite
    • ṛjīṣy: to be victorious or to conquer
    • u1bhe: both
    • ā paprau: to have attained or achieved
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • mahitvā: greatness or magnificence
    • ataś: therefore
    • cid: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • mahimā: glory or splendor
    • vi: in all
    • recy: to be known or recognized
    • abhi: towards
    • yo: who
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • bhuvanā: worlds or realms
    • babhūva: has become or has manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the immense power and glory of Indra, the king of gods, who is capable of conquering both realms of existence.
    • It emphasizes the idea that Indra's greatness is recognized universally, and his influence spans across all worlds.
    • The mantra can be seen as a celebration of strength, victory, and the acknowledgment of divine power in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a leadership context, this mantra can be related to a strong leader who inspires and unites people, achieving great victories for their community.
    • In personal life, it can symbolize overcoming challenges and recognizing one's own strength and potential to achieve greatness.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the idea of universal interconnectedness, where the actions of one can impact many, similar to how a leader's decisions affect an entire nation.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of divine power and the potential for greatness within all individuals.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength and victory are attainable, and that one's actions can resonate across various realms of existence.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to recognize their own capabilities and strive for excellence, while also acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings.
