इन्द्रावरुणयोः अहं सम्राजोर अव आ वृणे |तां नो मृळात इदृशे ||
indrāvaruṇayor ahaṁ samrājor ava ā vṛṇe | tā no mṛḻāta īdṛśe ||
  • Indrāvaruṇayor: This term refers to the deities Indra and Varuna. Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and war, while Varuna represents the cosmic order and the waters. Together, they symbolize the balance of nature and the forces that govern the universe.
  • Ahaṁ: This means "I" or "myself," indicating a personal invocation or connection to the divine forces being addressed.
  • Samrājor: This word translates to "of the ruler" or "of the king." It signifies authority, power, and leadership, suggesting a request for strength and guidance from the divine rulers.
  • Ava ā vṛṇe: This phrase means "I choose" or "I select." It indicates an active participation in the relationship with the divine, expressing a desire to receive blessings or guidance.
  • Tā no mṛḻāta: This translates to "may they bless us." It is a plea for the deities to bestow their grace and support upon the individual or community.
  • Īdṛśe: This word means "in this manner" or "in this way," suggesting a specific context or situation in which the blessings are sought.

In summary, this mantra is a heartfelt invocation to the deities Indra and Varuna, seeking their blessings and guidance in a personal and communal context. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in leadership and decision-making, reflecting the interconnectedness of human actions and the natural world.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra highlights the importance of seeking divine guidance in leadership roles, reminding leaders to be humble and receptive to higher wisdom.
  • In real life, individuals can apply this mantra by invoking it during important decisions, such as starting a new project, making significant life changes, or leading a team, to ensure they are aligned with the greater good.
  • It serves as a reminder of the balance between human efforts and the natural forces, encouraging respect for nature and the environment in our daily lives.
