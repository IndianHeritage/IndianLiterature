तन् नो रायः पर्वतास तन् न आपस तद रातिषाच ओषधीर उता द्युः |वनस्पतिभिः पृथिवी सजोषा उभे रोधसी परि पासतो नः ||
tan no rāyaḥ parvatās tan na āpas tad rātiṣāca oṣadhīr uta dyauḥ | vanaspatibhiḥ pṛthivī sajoṣā ubhe rodasī pari pāsato naḥ ||
  • tan no rāyaḥ parvatās: This phrase can be translated as "May the mountains grant us prosperity." Here, "rāyaḥ" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "parvatās" refers to the mountains, which are often seen as symbols of stability and strength.
  • tan na āpas: This translates to "May the waters be favorable to us." Water is essential for life, and in many cultures, it symbolizes purity and sustenance. This line emphasizes the importance of water in our lives.
  • tad rātiṣāca oṣadhīr uta dyauḥ: This means "May the herbs and the heavens be beneficial to us." "Oṣadhīr" refers to herbs or plants, which are vital for health and healing, while "dyauḥ" refers to the sky or heavens, indicating a connection to the divine and the cosmos.
  • vanaspatibhiḥ pṛthivī sajoṣā: This translates to "The earth, along with the trees, is full of joy." "Vanaspati" refers to trees or plants, and "pṛthivī" refers to the earth. This line highlights the interconnectedness of nature and the joy it brings to life.
  • ubhe rodasī pari pāsato naḥ: This means "May both realms (the earth and the sky) protect us." "Rodasī" refers to the two realms, emphasizing the balance between the earthly and the celestial, and the need for protection from both.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, prosperity, and the divine. It calls for blessings from the mountains, waters, herbs, and the heavens, highlighting the importance of these elements in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to environmental conservation efforts, where we seek to protect our natural resources for future generations. It also serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance of nature and the prosperity it can bring when respected and nurtured. By acknowledging the significance of these elements, we can foster a harmonious relationship with our environment, leading to a more sustainable and fulfilling life.
