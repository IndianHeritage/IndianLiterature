सुदेवाः स्थ काण्वायना वयो-वयो विचरन्तः |अश्वासो न चङ्क्रमत ||
sudevāḥ stha kāṇvāyanā vayo-vayo vicarantaḥ | aśvāso na caṅkramata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sudevāḥ: Good or auspicious deities
    • stha: Standing or established
    • kāṇvāyanā: Those who are wise or knowledgeable
    • vayo-vayo: Age or life span
    • vicarantaḥ: Wandering or moving about
    • aśvāso: Horses or swift beings
    • na: Not
    • caṅkramata: Stumbling or faltering
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspicious deities who are established and wise.
    • It emphasizes the importance of stability and wisdom in life.
    • The phrase "vayo-vayo" suggests a focus on the journey of life and the experiences that come with age.
    • The mention of "aśvāso" indicates the need for swiftness and agility in our actions.
    • Finally, the mantra reassures that those who are wise and established do not falter in their path.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a wise leader who remains stable and does not falter can guide their team effectively through challenges.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom can navigate the complexities of relationships and life decisions without stumbling.
    • In sports, athletes who train diligently and maintain their focus are less likely to falter during competitions, embodying the essence of "aśvāso."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, stability, and agility in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and remain steadfast in our pursuits.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to success and fulfillment, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or community leadership.
