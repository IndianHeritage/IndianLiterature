या उग्रः सन्न अनिष्टृतः स्थिरो रणाय संस्कृतः |यदि स्तोतुर मगवाः श्रिणवद धवं निन्द्रो योषत्य आगमत ||
ya ugraḥ sann aniṣṭṛtaḥ sthiro raṇāya saṁskṛtaḥ | yadi stotur maghavā śṛṇavad dhavaṁ nendro yoṣaty ā gamat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ugraḥ: fierce or powerful
    • sann: being or existing
    • aniṣṭṛtaḥ: unshaken or steadfast
    • sthira: stable or firm
    • raṇāya: for battle or conflict
    • saṁskṛtaḥ: well-prepared or refined
    • yadi: if
    • stotur: to praise or worship
    • maghavā: the powerful one (often referring to Indra)
    • śṛṇavad: listening or hearing
    • dhavaṁ: the one who is strong or mighty
    • nendro: not Indra (the king of gods)
    • yoṣaty: who is engaged or involved
    • ā gamat: has come or arrived
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful being who is steadfast and well-prepared for battle.
    • It suggests that if one praises the mighty (often symbolized by Indra), they will be heard and supported.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of being strong and stable in the face of challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who is firm and well-prepared can inspire confidence in their team, much like the "ugraḥ" who is ready for battle.
    • In personal life, individuals facing challenges (like health issues or financial difficulties) can draw strength from their inner stability and preparation, akin to the steadfastness described in the mantra.
    • In sports, athletes who train rigorously and maintain mental fortitude exemplify the qualities of being "sthira" and "saṁskṛtaḥ," ready to face their opponents.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, preparation, and the power of praise.
    • It teaches that by being steadfast and well-prepared, one can face life's battles effectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal challenges, professional endeavors, or competitive sports, embodying these qualities can lead to success and resilience.
