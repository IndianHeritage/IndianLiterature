परिषद्यं ह्यरणस्य रेक्णो नित्यस्य रायः पतयः स्याम | न शेषो अग्ने अन्यजातम अस्त्य चेतनस्य मा पथो विदुक्षः ||
pariṣadyaṁ hy araṇasya rekṇo nityasya rāyaḥ patayaḥ syāma | na śeṣo agne anyajātam asty acetānasya mā patho vi dukṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pariṣadyaṁ hy araṇasya rekṇo nityasya rāyaḥ patayaḥ syāma | na śeṣo agne anyajātam asty acetānasya mā patho vi dukṣaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pariṣadyaṁ: surrounded or encompassed
    • hy: indeed
    • araṇasya: of the shelter or refuge
    • rekṇo: of the eternal or everlasting
    • nityasya: of the eternal
    • rāyaḥ: wealth or prosperity
    • patayaḥ: may we become or may we attain
    • syāma: may we be
    • na: not
    • śeṣo: the remaining or leftover
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • anyajātam: other than that which is born
    • asty: there is
    • acetānasya: of the unconscious or inert
    • mā: do not
    • patho: of the path
    • vi: indeed
    • dukṣaḥ: difficult or obstructive
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of being surrounded by the eternal and prosperous aspects of life.
    • It emphasizes the need for a protective shelter (araṇasya) that is everlasting (rekṇo, nityasya) and leads to wealth (rāyaḥ).
    • The invocation to Agni suggests a call for guidance and illumination on our paths, urging us to avoid distractions or obstacles (dukṣaḥ) that may arise from the unconscious or inert aspects of existence (acetānasya).
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of surrounding ourselves with positive influences, whether they be people, environments, or ideologies that foster growth and prosperity.
    • For example, in a professional setting, being part of a supportive team can lead to greater success and fulfillment, while being surrounded by negativity can hinder progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for conscious living and the pursuit of prosperity through positive surroundings.
    • It teaches us to seek out and create environments that nurture our growth and well-being, while also being mindful of the distractions that may lead us astray.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal relationships, career choices, and even spiritual practices, reminding us to align ourselves with what is eternal and beneficial.
