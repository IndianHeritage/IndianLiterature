मां नो गव्येभिर अश्व्यैः सहस्रैभिर अति ख्यातम् |अन्ति षड् भूतु वाम अवः ||
mā no gavyebhir aśvyaiḥ sahasrebhir ati khyatam | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • mā no gavyebhir aśvyaiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May we not be harmed by the powerful ones." Here, "gavyebhir" refers to the strong or powerful beings, and "aśvyaiḥ" implies those who are swift or capable. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for protection from those who may have more power or influence over us, such as authority figures or adversaries.
  • sahasrebhir ati khyatam: This part means "with thousands of praises." It suggests that the speaker is invoking the power of numerous accolades or blessings. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea that when we are recognized and praised, we gain strength and support from our community, which can help us overcome challenges.
  • anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ: This translates to "May the six elements come to us." The "six elements" can refer to the fundamental aspects of existence, such as earth, water, fire, air, space, and consciousness. In a real-life context, this can symbolize a holistic approach to life, where one seeks balance and harmony among all aspects of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from powerful influences while also acknowledging the strength that comes from community support and recognition. It encourages a balanced approach to life by invoking the six elements, which can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and environmental awareness. By understanding and integrating these elements, individuals can strive for a harmonious existence, fostering resilience against challenges and promoting well-being in their lives and communities.
