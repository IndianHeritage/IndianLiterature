त्रिरादिवः सविता सोषवीति राजानां मित्रावरुणा सुपाणि |आपश्चिदस्य रोधसी चिदुर्वी रत्नं भिक्षन्त सवितुः सवाय ||
trir ā divaḥ savitā soṣavīti rājānā mitrāvaruṇā supāṇī | āpaś cid asya rodasī cid urvī ratnam bhikṣanta savituḥ savāya ||
  • Mantra: "trir ā divaḥ savitā soṣavīti rājānā mitrāvaruṇā supāṇī | āpaś cid asya rodasī cid urvī ratnam bhikṣanta savituḥ savāya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Three times, O Savitar, the Sun, you shine; you are the king of the kings, the friend of Mitra and Varuna, with auspicious hands. The waters, indeed, are the essence of this sky; the earth is the jewel that Savitar bestows."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • trir: Three times - signifies the cyclical nature of time and existence.
    • ā divaḥ: O divine one - refers to the Sun, a symbol of divinity and enlightenment.
    • savitā: The Sun - represents life, energy, and illumination.
    • soṣavīti: You shine - indicates the brilliance and radiance of the Sun.
    • rājānā: King of kings - denotes supreme authority and power.
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Friend of Mitra and Varuna - highlights the importance of friendship and harmony in the universe.
    • supāṇī: Auspicious hands - symbolizes benevolence and the ability to bestow blessings.
    • āpaś: Waters - represents the life-giving and purifying aspects of nature.
    • cid: Indeed - emphasizes certainty and truth.
    • asya: This - refers to the immediate environment or context.
    • rodasī: Sky - signifies the vastness and the overarching presence of the divine.
    • urvī: Earth - represents stability, grounding, and material existence.
    • ratnam: Jewel - symbolizes value, beauty, and preciousness.
    • bhikṣanta: Bestows - indicates the act of giving or sharing.
    • savituḥ: Of the Sun - reiterates the connection to the divine source of light and life.
    • savāya: Bestow upon us - a plea for blessings and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The cyclical nature of time can be observed in the changing seasons, which remind us of the importance of renewal and growth in our lives.
    • The Sun's role as a source of energy is akin to how we rely on the sun for agriculture, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • The concept of friendship among deities can be reflected in our relationships, highlighting the importance of collaboration and support in achieving common goals.
    • The idea of the Earth as a jewel can be seen in our appreciation for nature and the environment, urging us to protect and cherish our planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of the Sun as a source of life, energy, and divine authority.
    • It teaches us about the importance of harmony, friendship, and the interconnectedness of all elements of existence.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by fostering relationships, respecting nature, and recognizing the cyclical patterns in our lives.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek blessings and abundance from the divine, encouraging us to live in alignment with the natural world.
