महो महानी पनयन्त्य अस्येन्द्रस्य कर्म सुक्रता पुरूणि |वृजनेन वृजनान सम पिपेष मायााभिर दस्यूम् अभिभूत्योजाः ||
maho mahāni panayanty asyendrasya karma sukṛtā purūṇi | vṛjanena vṛjinān sam pipeṣa māyābhir dasyūm̐r abhibhūtyojāḥ ||
  • maho mahāni: This phrase translates to "great and mighty." It emphasizes the vastness and power of the subject being discussed, often referring to divine forces or cosmic principles.
  • panayanty: This means "they flow" or "they come forth." It suggests a sense of movement or emergence, indicating that these great forces are active and dynamic.
  • asyendrasya: This translates to "of this Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the king of gods. Here, it signifies the divine authority or power at play.
  • karma sukṛtā purūṇi: This means "the actions of the virtuous." It highlights the importance of righteous deeds and their outcomes, suggesting that good actions lead to positive results.
  • vṛjanena: This translates to "by the removal" or "by the dispelling." It indicates an action of clearing or eliminating obstacles.
  • vṛjinān: This means "obstacles" or "impurities." It refers to the challenges or negative influences that one may encounter in life.
  • sam pipeṣa: This translates to "he has crushed" or "he has overcome." It signifies triumph over difficulties or adversities.
  • māyābhir: This means "by illusions" or "by deceptive forces." It points to the distractions and falsehoods that can lead one astray.
  • dasyūm̐r: This translates to "the robbers" or "the enemies." It symbolizes the negative forces or adversaries that threaten one's progress.
  • abhibhūtyojāḥ: This means "having conquered" or "having gained victory." It signifies the ultimate success and mastery over challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming obstacles through virtuous actions and divine support. It teaches that by engaging in righteous deeds, one can navigate through life's challenges, dispelling illusions and adversities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction, facing financial difficulties, or dealing with interpersonal conflicts. By focusing on positive actions and seeking divine guidance, individuals can find strength to conquer their challenges and emerge victorious. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that greatness and success are achievable through virtue and resilience.
