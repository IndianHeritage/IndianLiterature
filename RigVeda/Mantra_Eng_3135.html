किम् नोऽस्य द्रविणं कद् ध रत्नं वि नो वोचो जातवेदश् चिकीत्वान् |गुहाध्वनः परमं यन् नोऽस्य रेकु पदं न निदानाऽगन्म ||
kiṁ no asya draviṇaṁ kad dha ratnaṁ vi no voco jātavedaś cikitvān | guhādhvanaḥ paramaṁ yan no asya reku padaṁ na nidānā aganma ||
  • kiṁ no asya draviṇaṁ - "What is this wealth for us?"
  • kad dha ratnaṁ - "When will the jewel (or treasure) come?"
  • vi no voco - "Speak to us."
  • jātavedaś cikitvān - "O Jātaveda, the one who knows all." (Jātaveda refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.)
  • guhādhvanaḥ - "Of the one who travels in the hidden paths."
  • paramaṁ yan no asya - "What is the supreme thing for us?"
  • rekupadaṁ - "The path of the rays (or light)." (This can symbolize enlightenment or knowledge.)
  • na nidānā aganma - "May we not be deprived of it."

This mantra from the Rigveda expresses a deep yearning for wealth, knowledge, and enlightenment. It is a prayer directed towards Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a source of wisdom and a guide through the complexities of life.


In real life, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for guidance in our pursuit of material and spiritual wealth. For instance, in a business context, one might seek the wisdom of experienced mentors (akin to Agni) to navigate challenges and achieve success. Similarly, in personal development, individuals often look for enlightenment through education, meditation, or spiritual practices.


The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth. It emphasizes that true wealth is not merely material but also encompasses knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual fulfillment. In today's fast-paced world, where materialism often overshadows spiritual growth, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance and to value the deeper aspects of life.


In conclusion, this Rigveda mantra encourages us to seek guidance and clarity in our pursuits, ensuring that we do not lose sight of what is truly valuable. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional endeavors, reminding us to ask for wisdom and to cherish both material and spiritual wealth.
