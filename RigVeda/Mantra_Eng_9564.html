सर्वे नन्दन्ति यशसागतेन सभासाहेन सख्याः सखायः |किल्बिषस्पृत पितुषणिर ह्य एषाम अरं हितो भवति वाजिनाय ||
sarve nandanti yaśasāgatena sabhāsāhena sakhyā sakhāyaḥ | kilbiṣaspṛt pituṣaṇir hy eṣām araṁ hito bhavati vājināya ||
  • Translation: "All rejoice with the arrival of fame, through the assembly of friends, and through companionship. The one who is free from sin, the one who is a protector, becomes beneficial to them, like a charioteer."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Sarve: All
    • Nandanti: Rejoice
    • Yaśasāgatena: With the arrival of fame
    • Sabhāsāhena: Through the assembly of friends
    • Sakhyā: Through companionship
    • Sakhāyaḥ: Friends
    • Kilbiṣaspṛt: Free from sin
    • Pituṣaṇir: Protector
    • Hy: Indeed
    • Eṣām: To them
    • Araṁ: Beneficial
    • Hito: Good
    • Bhavati: Becomes
    • Vājināya: Like a charioteer
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the joy that comes from fame and recognition, particularly when shared with friends and companions. It highlights the importance of community and support in achieving success.
    • The metaphor of a charioteer suggests guidance and leadership. Just as a charioteer skillfully navigates a chariot, a protector or guide helps individuals navigate through life's challenges.
    • Being free from sin implies a moral and ethical foundation, suggesting that true success and joy come from virtuous living and positive relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a team that celebrates each other's achievements fosters a positive environment. When one member succeeds, the entire team rejoices, creating a culture of support and collaboration.
    • In sports, a coach acts as a charioteer, guiding the team towards victory. The success of the team is celebrated collectively, reinforcing the bond among teammates.
    • In personal relationships, friends who uplift and support each other during challenging times exemplify the essence of this mantra. Their companionship brings joy and strength, making them resilient against life's adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, support, and ethical living in achieving success and happiness. It teaches us that our accomplishments are more meaningful when shared with others and that having a moral compass enhances our relationships and overall well-being.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger bonds, greater joy in achievements, and a more fulfilling life, as we navigate our journeys with the support of those around us.
