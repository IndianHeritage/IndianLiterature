मृजानो वारे पवमानो अव्यये वृषावा चक्रदो वने |देवानां सोम पवमान निष्कृतं गोभिर अञ्जानो अर्षसि ||
mṛjāno vāre pavamāno avyaye vṛṣāva cakrado vane | devānāṁ soma pavamāna niṣkṛtaṁ gobhir añjāno arṣasi ||
  • mṛjāno vāre: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who purifies" or "the one who cleanses." In real life, this can relate to the importance of purification in various cultures, such as the practice of cleansing rituals before significant events or ceremonies.
  • pavamāno: This term means "the one who is moving or flowing." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the universe. For example, rivers are often seen as symbols of life and movement, representing the continuous flow of time and experiences.
  • avyaye: This word translates to "imperishable" or "eternal." It emphasizes the idea of something that transcends time and space. In a real-life context, this can be related to the legacy we leave behind, such as the impact of great leaders or thinkers whose ideas continue to influence generations.
  • vṛṣāva: This can be understood as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It reflects the qualities of strength and resilience. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome challenges and adversities, showcasing human strength and determination.
  • cakrado vane: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the forest of the wheel." The wheel often symbolizes the cycle of life, death, and rebirth. In real life, this can relate to the natural cycles we observe, such as the changing seasons or the life cycles of plants and animals.
  • devānāṁ soma: This refers to "the soma of the gods," which is often associated with divine nourishment or enlightenment. In a practical sense, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom that nourishes the mind and spirit, similar to how education and learning enrich our lives.
  • pavamāna niṣkṛtaṁ: This can be translated as "the purified one." It signifies the importance of purification in spiritual and personal growth. In real life, this can be seen in practices such as meditation or mindfulness, which help individuals cleanse their thoughts and emotions.
  • gobhir añjāno arṣasi: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the cows, you are nourished." Cows are often seen as symbols of abundance and sustenance in many cultures. In a real-life context, this can relate to the importance of agriculture and the sustenance it provides to communities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes themes of purification, strength, and the eternal nature of life. It highlights the significance of divine nourishment and the cycles of existence. In real life, these concepts are applicable in various scenarios, such as personal growth through purification practices, the resilience shown in overcoming challenges, and the importance of knowledge and sustenance in our lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the divine presence that guides us through our journeys.
