तां उषतो वि बोधय यद अग्ने यासि दूत्यम् |देवैर आसत्सि बर्हिषि ||
tām̐ uśato vi bodhaya yad agne yāsi dūtyam | devair ā satsi barhiṣi ||
  • tām̐ uśato vi bodhaya: This phrase can be translated as "Awaken that which is in the state of sleep." Here, "tām̐" refers to "that," "uśato" means "of the sleeping," and "vi bodhaya" translates to "awaken." This can be understood as a call to bring awareness to something that is dormant or not fully realized.
  • yad agne yāsi dūtyam: This part means "when you, O Agni, go as a messenger." "Yad" means "when," "agne" refers to "O Agni" (the fire god), and "yāsi dūtyam" translates to "you go as a messenger." This signifies the role of Agni as a divine intermediary, conveying messages between humans and the gods.
  • devair ā satsi barhiṣi: This translates to "may you be present with the gods." "Devair" means "with the gods," "ā satsi" translates to "may you be present," and "barhiṣi" refers to "in the sacred grass" or "in the offerings." This indicates a desire for divine presence and blessings in rituals and offerings.

  • Real-Life Example: In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in various situations where awakening potential is necessary. For instance, in education, a teacher may use this concept to inspire students to realize their hidden talents and capabilities, encouraging them to awaken their intellectual curiosity.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awakening awareness and potential, the role of divine guidance, and the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It serves as a reminder that there is a need to recognize and harness the dormant energies within ourselves and our surroundings.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in personal development, spiritual practices, and community rituals. For example, during a meditation session, one might recite this mantra to invoke clarity and insight, awakening the mind to new possibilities. In community gatherings, it can be used to seek divine blessings for collective endeavors.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "tām̐ uśato vi bodhaya yad agne yāsi dūtyam | devair ā satsi barhiṣi ||" encapsulates the essence of awakening, divine communication, and the importance of rituals. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to seek awareness, embrace their potential, and connect with the divine for guidance and support in their endeavors.
