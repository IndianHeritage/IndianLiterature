उद वृहा रक्षः सहमूलम् इन्द्र वृश्चा मध्यं प्रत्य अग्रं श्रिणीहि |आ कीवतः सललूकं चकर्थ ब्रह्मद्विषे तपुषिं हेतिम अस्य ||
ud vṛha rakṣaḥ sahamūlam indra vṛścā madhyam praty agraṁ śṛṇīhi | ā kīvataḥ salalūkaṁ cakartha brahmadviṣe tapuṣiṁ hetim asya ||
  • ud vṛha rakṣaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, protect us from the evil forces." Here, "ud" means "up" or "out," and "vṛha" refers to "to grow" or "to increase." "Rakṣaḥ" means "protection" or "guardianship." In real life, this can be understood as a plea for safeguarding oneself from negative influences, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • sahamūlam indra: This translates to "with the root, O Indra." "Sahamūlam" implies a connection to the foundational aspects of life. In practical terms, this can refer to the importance of staying grounded and connected to one's roots, such as family, culture, and values, which provide strength and support in challenging times.
  • vṛścā madhyam praty agraṁ: This can be interpreted as "the middle path leads to the forefront." "Vṛścā" means "to rise," "madhyam" refers to "the middle," and "praty agraṁ" means "to the forefront." This suggests that a balanced approach in life, avoiding extremes, can lead to success. For example, in a career, maintaining a balance between work and personal life can lead to greater fulfillment and productivity.
  • śṛṇīhi: This word means "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to wisdom and guidance. In everyday life, this can be applied to the practice of active listening in conversations, which fosters better relationships and understanding.
  • ā kīvataḥ salalūkaṁ: This phrase translates to "from the depths of the ocean." "Ā kīvataḥ" means "from the depths," and "salalūkaṁ" refers to "the ocean." This can symbolize the vastness of knowledge and experiences that one can draw from. In real life, it encourages individuals to explore their inner depths and learn from their experiences, much like diving into the ocean to discover its treasures.
  • cakartha brahmadviṣe: This means "to act against the forces of chaos." "Cakartha" implies "to do" or "to act," and "brahmadviṣe" refers to "the adversaries of the cosmic order." This highlights the importance of taking action against negativity and chaos in life. For instance, standing up against injustice or working towards harmony in a community reflects this principle.
  • tapuṣiṁ hetim asya: This translates to "the cause of this effort." "Tapuṣiṁ" refers to "effort" or "austerity," and "hetim" means "cause." This emphasizes that every action has a purpose and that one should strive for meaningful endeavors. In practical terms, this can relate to setting goals in life that align with one's values and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking protection from negativity, staying grounded, embracing balance, and taking meaningful action against chaos. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to cultivate resilience, wisdom, and purpose. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with strength and clarity, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
