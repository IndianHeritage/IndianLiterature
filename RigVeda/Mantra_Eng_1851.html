यज्ञो हि स्मेन्द्रं कश्चिद ऋन्द्हां जुहुराणश्चिन मनसा पर्यन |तीर्थे नाच्छा तातृषाणम ओको दीर्घो न सिध्रम आ कृतोत्य अध्वा ||
yajño hi ṣmendraṁ kaś cid ṛndhañ juhurāṇaś cin manasā pariyan | tīrthe nācchā tātṛṣāṇam oko dīrgho na sidhram ā kṛṇoty adhvā ||
  • yajño hi ṣmendraṁ kaś cid ṛndhañ juhurāṇaś cin manasā pariyan
    • yajño: This term refers to a sacrificial ritual or offering, which is central to Vedic practices. It symbolizes the act of giving and sharing with the divine.
    • hi: This word means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the importance of the preceding term.
    • ṣmendraṁ: This can be interpreted as "the lord of the sacrifice," referring to a divine presence or energy that is invoked during the yajña.
    • kaś cid: This phrase translates to "anyone" or "whoever," indicating inclusivity in the act of sacrifice.
    • ṛndhañ: This word means "offering" or "gift," highlighting the essence of yajña as a selfless act.
    • juhurāṇaś: This term refers to the act of offering or invoking, suggesting a deep connection between the individual and the divine.
    • cin manasā pariyan: This phrase means "with a focused mind," indicating that the intention behind the offering is crucial for its effectiveness.
  • tīrthe nācchā tātṛṣāṇam oko dīrgho na sidhram ā kṛṇoty adhvā
    • tīrthe: This word refers to a sacred place or a crossing, symbolizing a transition or transformation.
    • nācchā: This means "not" or "without," indicating a negation of something.
    • tātṛṣāṇam: This term can be interpreted as "thirst" or "desire," suggesting a longing for spiritual fulfillment.
    • oko: This word means "home" or "shelter," representing a place of safety and comfort.
    • dīrgho: This means "long" or "enduring," indicating the lasting impact of the actions taken.
    • na sidhram: This phrase translates to "not obstructed," suggesting that the path is clear for those who engage in the ritual.
    • ā kṛṇoty adhvā: This means "he who follows the path," emphasizing the importance of adhering to the right course of action.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of selfless offerings and the importance of intention in spiritual practices. It teaches that anyone can connect with the divine through sincere acts of giving, and that such actions can lead to spiritual fulfillment and clarity in one's life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through charitable acts, community service, or any form of altruism, where the focus is on the intention behind the action rather than the action itself. By engaging in these practices, individuals can create a positive impact on their surroundings and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
