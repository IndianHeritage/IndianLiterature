यस्य मन्दानो अन्धसो मागोनं दधिषे शवः |अयं स सोम इन्द्र ते सुतः पिब ||
yasya mandāno andhaso māghonaṁ dadhiṣe śavaḥ | ayaṁ sa soma indra te sutaḥ piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • mandāno: the one who is slow or dull
    • andhaso: of the blind or ignorant
    • māghonaṁ: of the great or powerful
    • dadhiṣe: you have given
    • śavaḥ: the dead or lifeless
    • ayaṁ: this
    • sa: he
    • soma: the moon or soma (a divine nectar)
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • te: your
    • sutaḥ: son or offspring
    • piba: drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine connection between the powerful and the ignorant, suggesting that even those who may seem dull or lifeless can receive blessings.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates a source of nourishment and vitality, often associated with spiritual enlightenment and divine grace.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and authority, and the mantra implies that through divine intervention, one can overcome ignorance and gain wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may recognize the potential in an underperforming employee (the "mandāno andhaso") and provide them with mentorship and resources (the "soma") to help them grow and succeed.
    • In education, a teacher may identify a student who struggles with learning (the "śavaḥ") and offer additional support, enabling the student to thrive and achieve their potential.
    • In personal relationships, one may support a friend going through a tough time, helping them to regain their strength and confidence through encouragement and love.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of compassion and support for those who may be struggling or lacking awareness.
    • It teaches us that everyone has the potential for growth and enlightenment, and it is our duty to help others realize their capabilities.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as mentoring, teaching, and personal relationships, highlighting the significance of nurturing and uplifting those around us.
