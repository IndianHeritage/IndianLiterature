अस्मादहम् तविषाद् ईषमाण इन्द्राद् भियā मरुतो रेजमानः |युष्मभ्यं हव्यā निश्चितानि आसन् तानि आर्ये चक्रिमā मृळतā नः ||
asmād ahaṁ taviṣād īṣamāṇa indrād bhiyā maruto rejamānaḥ | yuṣmabhyaṁ havyā niśitāny āsan tāny āre cakṛmā mṛḻatā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmād: From this
    • ahaṁ: I
    • taviṣād: From the powerful
    • īṣamāṇa: Desiring
    • indrād: From Indra
    • bhiyā: With fear
    • maruto: The Maruts (storm deities)
    • rejamānaḥ: Being agitated
    • yuṣmabhyaṁ: For you
    • havyā: Offerings
    • niśitāny: Sharp
    • āsan: They are
    • tāny: Those
    • āre: We invoke
    • cakṛmā: We have made
    • mṛḻatā: To be pleased
    • naḥ: For us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for divine assistance and protection.
    • The speaker acknowledges the power of Indra, the king of gods, and the Maruts, who are associated with storms and strength.
    • It reflects a desire for the blessings of these deities, particularly in the context of offerings made to them.
    • The use of "fear" indicates a recognition of the might of these divine beings, suggesting that their favor is sought to overcome challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek divine intervention, similar to how the mantra seeks the favor of Indra and the Maruts.
    • In various cultures, rituals and offerings are made to deities for protection and guidance, akin to the offerings mentioned in the mantra.
    • During natural disasters, communities may come together to pray for safety and strength, reflecting the invocation of divine powers in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming fears and challenges.
    • It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the need for respect and acknowledgment of greater powers.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek help and guidance from higher forces during difficult times, fostering a sense of community and shared belief in the power of the divine.
