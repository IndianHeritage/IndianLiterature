आयम् एमी विचाकशद विचिन्वान दासम् आर्यम् |पिबामि पाकसुत्वनोऽभि धीराम अचाकशं विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
ayam emi vicākaśad vicinvan dāsam āryam | pibāmi pākasutvano 'bhi dhīram acākaśaṁ viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • ayam emi vicākaśad vicinvan dāsam āryam: This phrase can be translated to "I am the one who has come, discerning the noble servant." Here, the speaker identifies themselves as someone who has arrived with the ability to discern and understand the qualities of a noble person or servant. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or mentor who recognizes the potential in others and guides them towards their strengths.
  • pibāmi pākasutvano 'bhi dhīram acākaśaṁ: This translates to "I drink the essence of the pure, steady one." The act of drinking here symbolizes the absorption of wisdom, knowledge, or virtues from a pure and steadfast source. In practical terms, this could refer to learning from wise individuals, such as teachers or elders, who embody qualities we aspire to develop in ourselves.
  • viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ: This means "from all, I seek the supreme Indra." Indra is often regarded as a powerful deity representing strength and leadership. This line suggests a quest for the highest form of strength or guidance. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as striving for excellence and seeking inspiration from the best in our fields, whether in personal development, career, or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of discernment, learning from noble qualities, and striving for excellence. In real life, it encourages individuals to recognize and cultivate the virtues of wisdom and strength within themselves and in others. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and impactful life, contributing positively to society and inspiring those around them.
