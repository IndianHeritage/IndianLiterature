स हि ष्मा जरीत्रिभ्य ā वाजं गोमन्तम् इन्बति |पवमानः सहस्रिणम् ||
sa hi ṣmā jaritṛbhya ā vājaṁ gomantam invati | pavamānaḥ sahasriṇam ||
  • sa hi ṣmā: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, this is." It emphasizes the certainty and importance of what follows.
  • jaritṛbhya: This word refers to "those who are strong" or "the powerful." It signifies individuals or forces that possess strength and capability.
  • ā vājaṁ: This translates to "the excellent or powerful one." It denotes something that is of high quality or superior in nature.
  • gomantam: This term means "the one who possesses cows" or "wealth." In the Vedic context, cows symbolize prosperity and abundance.
  • invati: This means "invokes" or "calls upon." It indicates an action of seeking or requesting something.
  • pavamānaḥ: This word refers to "the purifying one" or "the one that brings purity." It signifies a force or entity that cleanses and elevates.
  • sahasriṇam: This translates to "the thousandfold" or "the one that is abundant." It suggests a sense of vastness and richness.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the powerful and prosperous forces to invoke abundance and purity. It highlights the importance of strength and wealth in achieving a higher state of being or consciousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can invoke their inner strength and resources to achieve their goals, much like how the mantra calls upon powerful forces.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can come together to seek abundance and prosperity, similar to the invocation of wealth represented by "gomantam."
  • Spiritual Cleansing: The concept of "pavamānaḥ" can be applied in practices of meditation and mindfulness, where individuals seek to purify their thoughts and actions.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, prosperity, and purity. It serves as a reminder that by invoking these qualities, individuals and communities can achieve greater heights in their personal and collective journeys. The mantra encourages us to recognize and harness our inner power and the abundance around us, leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life.
