न त्वद धोतā पूर्वो अग्ने यजीयान न काव्यैः परो अस्ति स्वधावः |विशश च यस्याऽ अतिथिर भवासि स यज्ञेन वनवत देव मार्तान ||
na tvad dhotā pūrvo agne yajīyān na kāvyaiḥ paro asti svadhāvaḥ | viśaś ca yasyā atithir bhavāsi sa yajñena vanavad deva martān ||
  • na tvad dhotā pūrvo agne yajīyān: This phrase translates to "There is no one who can surpass you, O Agni, in the performance of sacrifices." Here, Agni, the fire god, is revered as the supreme deity who presides over rituals and sacrifices.
  • na kāvyaiḥ paro asti svadhāvaḥ: This means "There is no one greater than you in the realm of offerings." It emphasizes Agni's unique position as the ultimate recipient of offerings, highlighting the importance of fire in Vedic rituals.
  • viśaś ca yasyā atithir bhavāsi: This translates to "You are the one who welcomes guests." Agni is seen as a host, welcoming all beings and ensuring their well-being, which reflects the cultural importance of hospitality in Vedic society.
  • sa yajñena vanavad deva martān: This means "Through the sacrifice, you become the divine protector of mortals." It signifies that through rituals and sacrifices, Agni provides protection and blessings to humanity.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the central role of Agni in Vedic rituals and the importance of fire as a purifying and transformative element. It underscores the belief that through proper offerings and sacrifices, one can attain divine favor and protection.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of rituals and traditions in maintaining a connection with the divine. It encourages individuals to engage in acts of hospitality and generosity, reflecting the values of community and support.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation of Agni, emphasizing the significance of fire in spiritual practices and the importance of honoring the divine through rituals. It teaches us that through our actions and offerings, we can foster a sense of connection with the divine and with each other, promoting harmony and well-being in our lives.
