यā शर्धाय मārुताय स्वभानवे श्रवो 'मृत्यु धुक्षत |यā मृḻीके मरुतां तुराणां यā सुम्नैर एवयावरī ||
yā śardhāya mārutāya svabhānave śravo 'mṛtyu dhukṣata | yā mṛḻīke marutāṁ turāṇāṁ yā sumnair evayāvarī ||
  • yā śardhāya: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is associated with faith or devotion." In real life, this can relate to the importance of having faith in one's actions and decisions, much like how individuals trust in their abilities to achieve their goals.
  • mārutāya: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, this signifies the power of nature and the forces that govern it. For example, the Maruts can be likened to the winds that can both destroy and rejuvenate, similar to how challenges in life can lead to growth and transformation.
  • svabhānave: This term means "to the one who shines by their own light." It emphasizes self-reliance and inner strength. In a real-life context, this can be seen in individuals who pursue their passions and shine in their respective fields, inspiring others through their dedication and hard work.
  • śravo 'mṛtyu dhukṣata: This phrase translates to "may the glory of immortality be attained." It reflects the human desire for legacy and remembrance. In practical terms, this can be related to how people strive to leave a lasting impact on society, whether through their work, art, or contributions to their communities.
  • yā mṛḻīke marutāṁ: This part can be understood as "that which is associated with the Maruts." It signifies the connection to the divine forces of nature. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people seek harmony with nature, understanding its rhythms and cycles, much like how farmers depend on seasonal changes for their crops.
  • turāṇāṁ: This term means "swift" or "quick." It can be related to the urgency of life and the need to act promptly in various situations. For instance, in business, timely decisions can lead to success, while delays can result in missed opportunities.
  • yā sumnair evayāvarī: This phrase translates to "that which brings auspiciousness." It highlights the importance of positivity and good fortune in our endeavors. In real life, this can be seen in how people often seek blessings or engage in rituals to attract good luck and success in their ventures.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of faith, the power of nature, self-reliance, and the pursuit of immortality through legacy. Its significance lies in the reminder to harness inner strength, act swiftly in the face of challenges, and seek harmony with the world around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate faith in their abilities, respect the forces of nature, and strive to leave a positive impact on society, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
