अध्वर्यवो यन् नरः कामयाध्वे श्रुष्टि वहन्तो नशथाऽत इन्द्रे |गभस्तिपूतम् भरत श्रुतायन्द्राय सोमं यज्यवो जुहोत ||
adhvaryavo yan naraḥ kāmayādhve śruṣṭī vahanto naśathā tad indre | gabhastipūtam bharata śrutāyendrāya somaṁ yajyavo juhota ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals and sacrifices in the Vedic tradition. They are responsible for the meticulous execution of the rites.
  • yan naraḥ: This translates to "what men" or "those who are human." It emphasizes the role of humanity in the rituals and their connection to the divine.
  • kāmayādhve: This means "desiring" or "with intention." It highlights the purpose behind the rituals, which is to fulfill specific desires or needs.
  • śruṣṭī vahanto: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who carry forth creation." It signifies the role of the priests and the rituals in the process of creation and sustaining the universe.
  • naśathā: This word means "not in vain" or "not without purpose." It underscores the importance of intention and the belief that rituals have a significant impact.
  • tad indre: This refers to "that Indra," invoking the king of the gods, who is associated with strength, rain, and thunder. Indra represents the power of nature and the divine forces at play.
  • gabhastipūtam: This translates to "purified light" or "radiant energy." It symbolizes the spiritual illumination that comes from performing the rituals correctly.
  • bharata: This term can refer to the people of Bharata, or India, and signifies a collective identity and cultural heritage.
  • śrutāyendrāya: This means "to Indra, who is heard" or "to the one who listens." It emphasizes the importance of communication with the divine through prayers and offerings.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • yajyavo: This term refers to those who perform sacrifices or offerings, highlighting the communal aspect of rituals.
  • juhota: This means "they offer" or "they invoke." It signifies the act of giving and the importance of offerings in the Vedic tradition.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals and the role of humanity in connecting with the divine. It highlights the importance of intention, community, and the belief that these rituals are not performed in vain. In real life, this can be applied to various aspects such as community gatherings, celebrations, and personal rituals where individuals seek to fulfill their desires and connect with a higher power. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, when performed with purpose and intention, can lead to positive outcomes and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
