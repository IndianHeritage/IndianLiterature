त्वं कविं चोदयोऽर्कसातौ त्वं कुत्साय शुष्णं दाशुषे वर्क |त्वं शिरो अमर्मणः पराहन्न अतिथिग्वाय शंस्यं करिष्यन् ||
tvaṁ kaviṁ codayo 'rkasātau tvaṁ kutsāya śuṣṇaṁ dāśuṣe vark | tvaṁ śiro amarmaṇaḥ parāhann atithigvāya śaṁsyaṁ kariṣyan ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "You," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • kaviṁ - Meaning "the poet" or "the seer," this term refers to someone who has profound insight and wisdom, often associated with divine knowledge.
  • codayo - This means "inspire" or "motivate," suggesting a call for guidance or enlightenment.
  • 'rkasātau - This can be interpreted as "in the realm of light" or "in the presence of the sun," symbolizing clarity, truth, and knowledge.
  • tvaṁ - Reiterating the address to the divine.
  • kutsāya - This word can mean "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who deserves respect," emphasizing reverence.
  • śuṣṇaṁ - This translates to "the one who is powerful" or "the one who brings strength," indicating a source of energy and vitality.
  • dāśuṣe - Meaning "to the servant" or "to the devotee," this term highlights the relationship between the divine and its followers.
  • vark - This can be interpreted as "blessings" or "prosperity," suggesting a desire for abundance and well-being.
  • tvaṁ - Again, addressing the divine.
  • śiro - Meaning "head" or "leader," this term signifies authority and guidance.
  • amarmaṇaḥ - This translates to "the one who protects" or "the one who safeguards," indicating a protective aspect of the divine.
  • parāhann - Meaning "to overcome" or "to conquer," suggesting triumph over challenges.
  • atithigvāya - This can be interpreted as "to the guest" or "to the visitor," emphasizing hospitality and the importance of welcoming others.
  • śaṁsyaṁ - Meaning "peace" or "well-being," indicating a desire for harmony and tranquility.
  • kariṣyan - This translates to "I will do" or "I will act," suggesting a commitment to take action towards achieving these goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking divine inspiration and guidance in our lives. It highlights the relationship between the divine and humanity, where the divine is seen as a source of wisdom, strength, and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks clarity and direction, or when welcoming guests, emphasizing the values of hospitality and peace. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and commitment to act in ways that promote well-being and harmony in their lives and communities.
