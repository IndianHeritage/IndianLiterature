पुनानः सोम धारयापो वसानो अर्षसि |आ रत्नधा योनिम ऋतस्य सिदास्य उतसो देव हिरण्ययः ||
punānaḥ soma dhārayāpo vasāno arṣasi | ā ratnadhā yonim ṛtasya sīdasy utso deva hiraṇyayaḥ ||
  • punānaḥ: This word can be translated as "purifying" or "refreshing." In a real-life context, this can refer to the act of cleansing oneself, both physically and spiritually, to achieve a state of purity and readiness for new experiences.
  • soma: Soma is often associated with a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In modern life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, which refreshes the mind and spirit.
  • dhārayāpo: This term means "holding" or "sustaining." It can be related to the idea of nurturing relationships or responsibilities in our lives, emphasizing the importance of support and stability.
  • vasāno: This word means "dwelling" or "residing." It can represent the idea of finding a place of comfort and safety, whether it be in our homes, communities, or within ourselves.
  • arṣasi: This term can be interpreted as "in the heavens" or "in the celestial realm." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, reminding us of our aspirations and the higher goals we strive for.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "to." It indicates direction and intention in our actions.
  • ratnadhā: This word translates to "possessing jewels" or "rich in treasures." In a practical sense, it can refer to the wealth of knowledge, experiences, and relationships we accumulate throughout our lives.
  • yonim: This term means "womb" or "source." It symbolizes creation and the nurturing aspect of life, akin to the potential that exists within us to create and innovate.
  • ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of order." It emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with the natural laws and principles that govern our existence.
  • sīdasy: This term can be interpreted as "sitting" or "resting." It suggests the need for contemplation and reflection in our lives, allowing us to pause and consider our actions and their consequences.
  • utso: This word means "uplifted" or "elevated." It signifies the aspiration to rise above challenges and to achieve greatness in our endeavors.
  • deva: This term translates to "divine" or "god." It represents the higher powers or forces that guide and inspire us in our journey through life.
  • hiraṇyayaḥ: This word means "golden" or "radiant." It symbolizes prosperity, success, and the rewards of our efforts, reminding us of the beauty and richness that life can offer.

Conclusion: The mantra "punānaḥ soma dhārayāpo vasāno arṣasi | ā ratnadhā yonim ṛtasya sīdasy utso deva hiraṇyayaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of purification, sustenance, and the pursuit of higher truths. It encourages us to seek purity in our thoughts and actions, to nurture our relationships, and to aspire towards our higher selves. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and spiritual growth. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, we can create a life that is rich in meaning, connection, and fulfillment.
