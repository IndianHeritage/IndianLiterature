अध्वर्यो अद्रिभिः सुतं सोमं पवित्र आ सृजा |पुनिहीन्द्राय पातवे ||
adhvaryo adribhiḥ sutaṁ somam pavitra ā sṛja | punīhīndrāya pātave ||
  • adhvaryo: This term refers to the priest or the officiant in a Vedic ritual, particularly in the context of sacrifices. The adhvaryu is responsible for the physical aspects of the ritual, ensuring that everything is performed correctly.
  • adribhiḥ: This word means "with stones" or "by means of stones." In Vedic rituals, stones are often used to create altars or as part of the sacrificial fire.
  • sutaṁ: This translates to "the drink" or "the offering." In this context, it refers to the soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • somam: Soma is both a deity and a plant in Vedic texts, associated with the moon and considered a source of vitality and strength. It is often linked to the idea of enlightenment and spiritual awakening.
  • pavitra: This means "pure" or "sacred." Purity is a significant concept in Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of cleanliness in both physical and spiritual practices.
  • ā sṛja: This phrase means "let us pour out" or "let us bring forth." It indicates an action of offering or presenting something to the divine.
  • punīhīndrāya: This term can be broken down into "punīhi," meaning "purify," and "Indra," referring to the king of the gods in Vedic mythology. It suggests a call to purify the offering for Indra.
  • pātave: This word means "for the powerful" or "for the strong." It emphasizes the strength and might of Indra, who is often invoked for protection and blessings.

The mantra can be understood as a ritual invocation where the priest, using stones, prepares to offer the pure soma drink to Indra, seeking his blessings and purification. The act of pouring out the soma symbolizes the sharing of divine energy and the connection between the earthly and the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for the importance of rituals and offerings in our daily lives. Just as the adhvaryu prepares the soma with care and intention, individuals can approach their daily tasks with mindfulness and dedication. For example, in a corporate setting, a team might come together to present a project (the soma) to their leaders (Indra), ensuring that their work is thorough and well-prepared (pavitra).


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of purity, intention, and the connection between the material and spiritual realms. It teaches us that our actions, when performed with dedication and mindfulness, can lead to blessings and success in our endeavors. Whether in personal rituals, professional tasks, or community service, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide us toward a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
