एहि ऊषु ब्रवाणि ते अग्न इत्तेतराः गिरः |एभिर वर्धास इन्दुभिः ||
ehy ū ṣu bravāṇi te 'gna itthetarā giraḥ | ebhir vardhāsa indubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ehy: Come here
    • ū: O
    • ṣu: You
    • bravāṇi: I speak
    • te: Your
    • 'gna: Knowledge
    • itthetarā: In this manner
    • giraḥ: Words
    • ebhir: With these
    • vardhāsa: May you grow
    • indubhiḥ: With the moon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, inviting the presence of knowledge and wisdom.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to communicate and share insights, emphasizing the importance of words and knowledge.
    • The reference to "the moon" symbolizes growth, nourishment, and the cyclical nature of life, suggesting that wisdom and knowledge can flourish over time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, teachers often invoke the spirit of knowledge when they encourage students to engage in discussions and share their thoughts.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek wisdom through meditation or reflection, inviting insights that help them grow in their understanding of life.
    • In community gatherings, leaders may use words to inspire and unite people, fostering an environment where knowledge is shared and valued.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of communication and the sharing of knowledge as essential components of personal and communal growth.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom can be cultivated through dialogue and reflection, much like the phases of the moon that nurture life on Earth.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to embrace the power of words and knowledge, fostering an environment of learning and growth.
