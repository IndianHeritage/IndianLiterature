उक्तं चन शस्यमानम् अगोर अरिर आ चिकेत |न गायतृं गीयमानम् ||
ukthaṁ cana śasyamānam agor arir ā ciketa | na gāyatraṁ gīyamānam ||
  • ukthaṁ: This word refers to a "sacred utterance" or "mantra" that is chanted or recited. It signifies the importance of vocalizing thoughts and intentions in a spiritual context.
  • cana: This term means "also" or "indeed," indicating an addition or emphasis on what follows.
  • śasyamānam: This word translates to "the one who is being praised" or "the one who is being celebrated." It highlights the act of recognizing and honoring someone or something.
  • agor: This term can be interpreted as "of the wise" or "of the learned," suggesting a connection to knowledge and wisdom.
  • arir: This word means "enemy" or "opponent," which can symbolize challenges or obstacles in life.
  • ā ciketa: This phrase translates to "has understood" or "has realized," indicating a moment of enlightenment or awareness.
  • na: This word means "not," serving as a negation in the context of the mantra.
  • gāyatraṁ: This term refers to "the one who sings" or "the one who chants," emphasizing the importance of expression through sound.
  • gīyamānam: This word means "being sung" or "being chanted," reinforcing the idea of vocal expression and its significance.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks to the power of vocalization and the importance of recognizing and celebrating wisdom.
    • It suggests that understanding and realization come from engaging with knowledge and confronting challenges.
    • The negation of "na gāyatraṁ" implies that there are moments when singing or expressing oneself may not be appropriate, highlighting the need for discernment in communication.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a team may face challenges (arir) that require wisdom (agor) to overcome. Recognizing and celebrating the contributions of team members (śasyamānam) can foster a positive environment.
    • In personal relationships, understanding (ā ciketa) each other's perspectives can lead to deeper connections, while knowing when to speak (gāyatraṁ) and when to listen (na gāyatraṁ) is crucial for effective communication.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of vocal expression, wisdom, and the recognition of challenges in our lives.
    • It teaches us the importance of discernment in communication, reminding us that there are times to speak and times to reflect.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to better relationships, effective teamwork, and personal growth.
