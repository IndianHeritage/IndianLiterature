अत्यायातम अश्विनā तिरो विश्वा अहं सनā |दस्रā हिरण्यवर्तनी सुषुम्नā सिन्धुवाहसā माध्वी मम श्रुतं हवम् ||
atyāyātam aśvinā tiro viśvā ahaṁ sanā | dasrā hiraṇyavartanī suṣumnā sindhuvāhasā mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||
  • atyāyātam: This term can be understood as "beyond" or "transcending." It suggests a state that goes beyond ordinary experiences or limitations.
  • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and life-giving powers. They symbolize duality and the balance of opposites.
  • tiro: This word means "to go away" or "to disappear." It indicates a movement away from something, possibly towards enlightenment or a higher state of being.
  • viśvā: Translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • ahaṁ: Meaning "I" or "myself," this word emphasizes the individual’s consciousness and self-awareness.
  • sanā: This term can be interpreted as "eternal" or "everlasting," suggesting a timeless quality to existence or consciousness.
  • dasrā: This word means "ten," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle, often associated with the ten directions or aspects of life.
  • hiraṇyavartanī: This term refers to "golden" or "radiant," often associated with prosperity, wealth, and auspiciousness.
  • suṣumnā: This word signifies "the most excellent" or "the best," indicating a state of supreme quality or virtue.
  • sindhuvāhasā: This term can be broken down into "sindhu," meaning "ocean" or "river," and "vāhasā," meaning "carrying" or "bearing." It symbolizes the flow of life and the abundance of resources.
  • mādhvī: This word means "sweet" or "pleasant," often associated with joy, happiness, and the sweetness of life.
  • mama: Meaning "my," this word personalizes the experience, indicating ownership or connection to the feelings expressed.
  • śrutaṁ: This term translates to "heard" or "listened," suggesting the importance of communication and understanding in relationships and experiences.
  • havam: Refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating a gesture of devotion or gratitude, often in a spiritual or ritualistic context.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of transcending ordinary experiences through the divine qualities represented by the Aśvins. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of self-awareness in achieving a higher state of consciousness. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, healing, and fostering relationships. By recognizing the universal aspects of existence and embracing the sweetness of life, individuals can cultivate a sense of harmony and balance, leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life experience.
