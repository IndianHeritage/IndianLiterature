इमा उ वः सुदानवो घृतं न पिप्युषीर इषः |वर्धान काणवस्य मन्मभिः ||
imā u vaḥ sudānavo ghṛtaṁ na pipyuṣīr iṣaḥ | vardhān kāṇvasya manmabhiḥ ||
  • imā u vaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "these (offerings) are for you." It signifies the act of offering and devotion, indicating a connection between the devotee and the divine.
  • sudānavo: This term refers to "good nourishment" or "excellent food." It emphasizes the importance of quality sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • ghṛtaṁ: This word means "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in Vedic rituals. Ghee symbolizes purity and is considered a sacred offering that enhances the spiritual atmosphere.
  • na pipyuṣīr: This phrase translates to "not to be taken away" or "not to be consumed." It suggests the idea of preservation and the importance of maintaining the sanctity of offerings.
  • iṣaḥ: This word means "power" or "strength." It indicates the divine energy that is invoked through the offerings made in rituals.
  • vardhān: This term means "growth" or "prosperity." It signifies the blessings that come from the divine, leading to abundance in life.
  • kāṇvasya: This refers to "of the Kāṇva family," which connects the mantra to a specific lineage or tradition, highlighting the importance of heritage and community in spiritual practices.
  • manmabhiḥ: This word translates to "with our minds" or "with our thoughts." It emphasizes the role of intention and focus in spiritual offerings and rituals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, nourishment, and the invocation of divine blessings. It highlights the significance of quality offerings and the intention behind them. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, family gatherings, or community events where food and offerings are shared. It serves as a reminder to approach such occasions with mindfulness, ensuring that the offerings are made with purity and respect. By doing so, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the divine and promote growth and prosperity in their lives and communities.
