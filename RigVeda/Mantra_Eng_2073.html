मां नो अरातिर ईशत देवस्य मार्त्यस्य च |पर्षि तस्याऽउत द्विषः ||
mā no arātir īśata devasya martyasya ca | parṣi tasyā uta dviṣaḥ ||
  • Translation: "May we not be harmed by the divine or by mortals; may we be free from enmity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mā: May not
    • no: us
    • arātir: enmity or harm
    • īśata: from the divine or the lord
    • devasya: of the divine
    • martyasya: of mortals or human beings
    • ca: and
    • parṣi: may we be free
    • tasyā: from that
    • uta: or
    • dviṣaḥ: of enmity or hatred
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection from both divine and human adversities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of harmony and the desire to live without conflict.
    • In a world filled with competition and strife, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek peace and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be invoked to foster a collaborative environment where colleagues support each other rather than compete.
    • In personal relationships, it can remind individuals to resolve conflicts amicably and avoid harboring grudges.
    • In community settings, it encourages people to work together for common goals, promoting unity over division.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for peace and protection from negativity.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions.
    • By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and supportive society.
