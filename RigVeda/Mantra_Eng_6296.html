यद्वासि सुन्वतो वृहद् यजमानस्य सत्पते | उक्ते वाऽयस्य रण्यसि सम इन्दुभिः ||
yad vāsi sunvato vṛdho yajamānasya satpate | ukthe vā yasya raṇyasi sam indubhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad vāsi sunvato vṛdho yajamānasya satpate | ukthe vā yasya raṇyasi sam indubhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Whatever you, O wise one, have spoken in the presence of the worshipper, may it be fulfilled, and may it shine forth like the moon."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yad: Whatever
    • vāsi: You have spoken
    • sunvato: O wise one
    • vṛdho: The one who is venerable or respected
    • yajamānasya: Of the worshipper
    • satpate: O Lord or Master
    • ukthe: In the spoken word
    • vā: Or
    • yasya: Whose
    • raṇyasi: You may fulfill
    • sam: Together or completely
    • indubhiḥ: Like the moon
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of words and intentions in rituals and worship.
    • It highlights the relationship between the worshipper and the divine, suggesting that the divine listens to and fulfills the desires expressed in prayers.
    • The reference to the moon symbolizes clarity, illumination, and the fulfillment of wishes, as the moon is often associated with calmness and serenity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal prayers, individuals often express their hopes and desires, believing that their words will be heard and answered by a higher power.
    • In community gatherings, such as weddings or festivals, the collective prayers and intentions of the participants are thought to invoke blessings and prosperity.
    • In modern contexts, affirmations and positive intentions are used in self-help practices, reflecting the belief that spoken words can manifest reality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of our words and intentions, both in spiritual practices and everyday life.
    • It encourages individuals to speak positively and with purpose, as their words hold the potential to create and transform their reality.
    • Ultimately, it underscores the interconnectedness of the worshipper and the divine, suggesting that sincere expressions of desire can lead to fulfillment and enlightenment.
