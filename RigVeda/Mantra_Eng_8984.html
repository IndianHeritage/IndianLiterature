अग्निम ईळे भुजां यविष्टं शाशा मित्रं दुर्धरीतुम् |यस्य धर्मन् स्वर्णेन सपर्यन्ति मातुर ऊधः ||
agnim īḻe bhujāṁ yaviṣṭhaṁ śāsā mitraṁ durdharītum | yasya dharman sva1r enīḥ saparyanti mātur ūdhaḥ ||
  • Agnim: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a symbol of energy and transformation.
  • īḻe: This means "I invoke" or "I praise." It indicates a call to the divine, expressing reverence and devotion.
  • bhujāṁ: This translates to "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It signifies strength and capability.
  • yaviṣṭhaṁ: This means "the most excellent" or "the best." It denotes the highest quality or the supreme aspect of something.
  • śāsā: This word can be interpreted as "command" or "rule." It suggests authority and governance.
  • mitraṁ: This translates to "friend" or "ally." In the Vedic context, it often refers to the divine as a protector and supporter.
  • durdharītum: This means "difficult to be held" or "hard to control." It implies the challenges and complexities of life.
  • yasya: This translates to "whose." It indicates possession or belonging.
  • dharman: This word means "duty" or "righteousness." It refers to the moral and ethical obligations one has.
  • sva1r: This can be interpreted as "own" or "personal." It emphasizes individuality and personal responsibility.
  • enīḥ: This means "the paths" or "the ways." It signifies the journey one takes in life.
  • saparyanti: This translates to "worship" or "serve." It indicates devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • mātur: This means "of the mother." It signifies nurturing, care, and the foundational aspects of life.
  • ūdhaḥ: This translates to "uplifted" or "raised." It suggests elevation and progress.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine fire (Agni) as a source of strength and guidance in our lives. It highlights the significance of righteousness (dharma) and the role of divine friendship (mitra) in navigating the challenges (durdharītum) we face. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking strength during difficult times, fostering a sense of community and support, and upholding moral values in our actions. By recognizing the divine presence in our lives and serving it with devotion, we can uplift ourselves and those around us, creating a harmonious and righteous existence.
