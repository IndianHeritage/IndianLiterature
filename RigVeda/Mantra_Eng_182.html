सदसस पतिम अद्भुतम् प्रियं इन्द्रस्य काम्यम् |सनीं मेधाम् अयासिषम् ||
sadasas patim adbhutam priyam indrasya kāmyam | sanim medhām ayāsiṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadasas: Of the assembly or gathering.
    • patim: Lord or master.
    • adbhutam: Wonderful or extraordinary.
    • priyam: Beloved or dear.
    • indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods.
    • kāmyam: Desired or wished for.
    • sanim: With or together.
    • medhām: Intelligence or wisdom.
    • ayāsiṣam: You have come or arrived.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the presence of a divine lord, specifically Indra, who is revered for his strength and wisdom.
    • The phrase "sadasas patim" suggests a gathering of people or a community coming together under the guidance of a wise leader.
    • "Adbhutam priyam" emphasizes the extraordinary qualities of this leader, making him beloved among the people.
    • The term "kāmyam" indicates that the qualities of this leader are not just admired but are also desired by the community.
    • Finally, "sanim medhām ayāsiṣam" expresses the arrival of wisdom and intelligence, suggesting that the leader brings with him the knowledge and insight needed for the community's growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a CEO who inspires and leads with wisdom can be seen as a modern-day embodiment of Indra, guiding the company towards success.
    • In a community, a local leader or activist who brings people together for a common cause, demonstrating extraordinary qualities, can be likened to the figure described in this mantra.
    • In education, a teacher who not only imparts knowledge but also inspires students to think critically and creatively embodies the essence of "medhām" or intelligence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wise leadership and the collective strength of a community.
    • It serves as a reminder that extraordinary qualities in leaders are essential for the growth and well-being of society.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek out and support leaders who embody wisdom, compassion, and the ability to unite people for a common purpose.
