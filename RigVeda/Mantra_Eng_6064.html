मन्त्र: कदु स्तुवन्त ऋतयन्त देवता ऋषिः को विप्र ओहते | कदाहवम् मगवन्न इन्द्र सुन्वतः कदु स्तुवत आ गमः ||
kad u stuvanta ṛtayanta devata ṛṣiḥ ko vipra ohate | kadā havam maghavann indra sunvataḥ kad u stuvata ā gamaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • u: or
    • stuvanta: praising
    • ṛtayanta: those who are righteous
    • devata: deities
    • ṛṣiḥ: sages or seers
    • ko: who
    • vipra: wise or learned
    • ohate: invokes or calls
    • kadā: when
    • havam: our offerings
    • maghavann: powerful or mighty
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sunvataḥ: who listens
    • kad: when
    • u: or
    • stuvata: praising
    • ā gamaḥ: come to us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a longing for divine connection and the presence of the deities.
    • It questions when the righteous beings (ṛṣis) and wise individuals (vipras) will invoke the deities through their praises.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the hope that the mighty Indra will listen to these offerings and come to the devotees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke blessings from deities through songs and prayers, similar to the praises mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and support from higher powers during challenging times, hoping that their prayers will be heard and answered.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the human desire for divine connection and the importance of righteousness and wisdom in invoking blessings.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals or communities seek support, guidance, and blessings from higher powers through sincere offerings and praises.
    • The essence of this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate righteousness and wisdom in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine.
