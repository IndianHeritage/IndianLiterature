वास्तोष पते प्रति जानिह्य अस्मान स्वावेयो अनमीवो भव नः |यत त्वेमहे प्रति तन् नो जुषस्व शं नो भव द्विपदे शं चतुष्पदे ||
vāstoṣ pate prati jānīhy asmān svāveśo anamīvo bhavā naḥ | yat tvemahe prati tan no juṣasva śaṁ no bhava dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||
  • vāstoṣ pate: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord of the dwelling" or "O Lord of the house." It signifies a divine presence that governs and protects our homes and living spaces.
  • prati jānīhy asmān: This means "may you know us." It expresses a desire for the divine to recognize and acknowledge our existence and our needs.
  • svāveśo anamīvo bhavā naḥ: This translates to "may you be our protector, free from any harm." It emphasizes the need for safety and security in our lives, both physically and spiritually.
  • yat tvemahe: This means "whatever we offer to you." It indicates a sense of devotion and the act of giving, whether it be prayers, offerings, or good deeds.
  • prati tan no juṣasva: This translates to "please accept our offerings." It is a plea for the divine to receive and bless our contributions and efforts.
  • śaṁ no bhava dvipade: This means "may there be peace for us, both in the two-legged (humans) and the four-legged (animals)." It highlights the interconnectedness of all living beings and the importance of harmony in our environment.
  • śaṁ catuṣpade: This translates to "and peace for the four-legged." It further emphasizes the need for peace and well-being for all creatures, not just humans.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking protection, peace, and harmony in our lives and surroundings. It acknowledges the divine presence in our homes and the importance of living in unity with all beings.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, when moving into a new home, individuals often perform rituals to bless the space and invite positive energy. This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a safe and harmonious environment, not just for ourselves but for all living beings around us.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encourages us to recognize the divine in our daily lives, seek protection, and promote peace among all creatures. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and harmonious world.
