अक्रविहस्तā सुक्रते परस्पा यं त्रासाथे वरुणेळास्व अन्तः |राजानां क्षत्रम् अहृणीयमानā सहस्रस्थूणम् बिभृतः सह द्वौ ||
akravihastā sukṛte paraspā yaṁ trāsāthe varuṇeḻāsv antaḥ | rājānā kṣatram ahṛṇīyamānā sahasrasthūṇam bibhṛthaḥ saha dvau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akravihastā: One who is not hindered by obstacles.
    • sukṛte: In good deeds or actions.
    • paraspā: In the presence of others or in a communal setting.
    • yaṁ: Whom or which.
    • trāsāthe: In fear or in a state of anxiety.
    • varuṇeḻāsv: In the realm of Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • antaḥ: Within or inside.
    • rājānā: Of kings or rulers.
    • kṣatram: Power or authority.
    • ahṛṇīyamānā: Not being taken away or diminished.
    • sahasrasthūṇam: Of a thousand pillars or supports.
    • bibhṛthaḥ: Holding or sustaining.
    • saha: Together with.
    • dvau: Two.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the strength and resilience of individuals who engage in righteous actions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and support in overcoming fears and challenges.
    • The reference to Varuna suggests a connection to the moral and cosmic order, indicating that righteous actions are aligned with universal principles.
    • The mention of kings and power highlights the significance of leadership and authority in maintaining order and justice.
    • The imagery of a thousand pillars symbolizes stability and strength, suggesting that a strong foundation is necessary for sustaining authority and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team that collaborates effectively and supports each other can overcome challenges and fears, leading to successful project outcomes.
    • In community service, individuals who engage in good deeds together can create a supportive environment that fosters trust and cooperation.
    • In leadership, a ruler or manager who acts with integrity and upholds moral values can inspire their followers and maintain a harmonious environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of righteous actions, community support, and strong leadership in overcoming fears and challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as workplaces, communities, and leadership roles, where collaboration and integrity are essential for success.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that by upholding moral values and supporting one another, we can create a stable and just society.
