मन्त्र: विद्मा हि त्वा वृषन्तमं वाजेषु हवनश्रुतम् | वृषन्तमस्य हूमह ऊतिं सहस्रसातमाम् ||
vidmā hi tvā vṛṣantamaṁ vājeṣu havanaśrutam | vṛṣantamasya hūmaha ūtiṁ sahasrasātamām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidmā: We know
    • hi: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • vṛṣantamaṁ: the best among the strong
    • vājeṣu: in the offerings
    • havanaśrutam: heard in the invocations
    • vṛṣantamasya: of the best among the strong
    • hūmaha: we invoke
    • ūtiṁ: the wealth
    • sahasrasātamām: of a thousandfold
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a deep reverence and acknowledgment of a divine force or deity that is considered the strongest and most powerful. It emphasizes the importance of invoking this strength in the context of offerings and rituals, seeking abundance and prosperity that is vast and multifaceted.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the strength of their teams to achieve ambitious goals. For instance, during a project launch, a manager might say, "We know the strength of our team, and together we can achieve great success." This mirrors the invocation of strength in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek strength during challenging times, such as illness or loss. They might turn to spiritual practices or community support, echoing the call for divine strength and abundance.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and invoking strength, whether in personal endeavors, community efforts, or professional pursuits.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking support from a higher power or collective strength can lead to greater achievements and abundance.
    • In daily life, one can apply this mantra by acknowledging their strengths and the strengths of those around them, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in various endeavors.
