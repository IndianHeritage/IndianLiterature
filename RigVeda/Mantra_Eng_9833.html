ऋतस्य हि प्रसितिर द्यौर उरु व्याचो नमो मह्य आलरामतिः पानीयसी |इन्द्रो मित्रो वरुणः सं चिकीर्तिरे 'थो भागः सविता पूतदक्षसः ||
ṛtasya hi prasitir dyaur uru vyaco namo mahy a1ramatiḥ panīyasī | indro mitro varuṇaḥ saṁ cikitrire 'tho bhagaḥ savitā pūtadakṣasaḥ ||
  • ṛtasya hi prasitir: This phrase can be translated as "the truth is indeed the foundation." Here, "ṛta" refers to cosmic order or truth, which is fundamental to the universe.
  • dyaur uru vyaco: This means "the vast sky speaks." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine and the communication of higher truths through nature.
  • namo mahy a1ramatiḥ panīyasī: This translates to "I bow to the great and nourishing one." It reflects reverence towards the divine forces that sustain life.
  • indro mitro varuṇaḥ: This refers to the deities Indra, Mitra, and Varuna, who represent various aspects of nature and cosmic order. Indra is the god of rain and thunderstorms, Mitra represents friendship and contracts, and Varuna embodies the moral and cosmic order.
  • saṁ cikitrire: This means "they have taken care." It indicates the protective and nurturing roles of these deities in maintaining balance in the universe.
  • 'tho bhagaḥ savitā pūtadakṣasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the blessed Savitar, the all-knowing." Savitar is the sun god, symbolizing illumination and knowledge, and "pūtadakṣasaḥ" refers to the purity and skill in creation.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, cosmic order, and the divine forces that sustain life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to the understanding of natural laws and the interconnectedness of all beings. For instance, in environmental conservation, recognizing the "ṛta" or truth of nature can lead to sustainable practices that honor the balance of ecosystems. By acknowledging the roles of deities like Indra and Varuna, we can appreciate the significance of water and weather patterns in agriculture and daily life. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to live in harmony with the universe, respecting the truths that govern our existence.
