समिद्धश्चित्समिध्यसे देवभ्यो हव्यवाहन |आदित्यै रुद्रैः वसुभिः न आगहि मृलीकाय न आगहि ||
samiddhaś cit sam idhyase devebhyo havyavāhana | ādityai rudrair vasubhir na ā gahi mṛḻīkāya na ā gahi ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, you who are kindled, you who are the embodiment of the divine, may you carry our offerings to the gods. O Adityas, Rudras, and Vasus, do not let the earth hold back our offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samiddhaś: Kindled or ignited, referring to the fire that is lit for offerings.
    • cit: Consciousness or awareness, indicating the divine presence in the fire.
    • sam idhyase: You who are present or manifest, addressing Agni as a divine entity.
    • devebhyo: To the gods, indicating the recipients of the offerings.
    • havyavāhana: The one who carries offerings, referring to Agni as the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • ādityai: To the Adityas, a group of solar deities.
    • rudrair: To the Rudras, associated with storms and the destructive aspects of nature.
    • vasubhir: To the Vasus, representing the elemental forces of nature.
    • na ā gahi: Do not hold back or restrain.
    • mṛḻīkāya: Referring to the earth or soil, symbolizing the material world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Hindu ceremony, the fire (Agni) is lit to perform rituals, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms. This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni in carrying our intentions and offerings to the divine.
    • When individuals seek blessings for prosperity or success, they may perform homas (fire rituals) and recite this mantra, invoking the divine forces to ensure their offerings reach the gods without obstruction.
    • In environmental contexts, the mention of earth (mṛḻīkāya) can be interpreted as a reminder to respect and care for nature, ensuring that our actions do not harm the planet while seeking divine blessings.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Agni as a divine intermediary, emphasizing the importance of intention and purity in offerings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual worlds. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, personal prayers, and even in the context of environmental consciousness, urging individuals to act responsibly while seeking divine favor. By recognizing the role of Agni and the divine forces, practitioners can cultivate a deeper understanding of their relationship with the universe and the importance of their actions.
