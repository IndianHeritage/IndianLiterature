यद युञ्जते मरुतो रुक्मवक्षसोऽश्वान रथेषु भग आ सुदानवः |धेनुर न शिश्वे स्वसरेषु पिन्वते जनाय रातहविषे महीम इषम् ||
yad yuñjate maruto rukmavakṣaso 'śvān ratheṣu bhaga ā sudānavaḥ | dhenur na śiśve svasareṣu pinvate janāya rātahaviṣe mahīm iṣam ||
  • Mantra: "yad yuñjate maruto rukmavakṣaso 'śvān ratheṣu bhaga ā sudānavaḥ | dhenur na śiśve svasareṣu pinvate janāya rātahaviṣe mahīm iṣam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • yuñjate: they join or unite
    • maruto: the Maruts (storm deities)
    • rukmavakṣaso: with golden bodies
    • śvān: horses
    • ratheṣu: in chariots
    • bhaga: wealth or fortune
    • ā: come
    • sudānavaḥ: the well-born or noble
    • dhenur: the cow (symbol of nourishment)
    • na: not
    • śiśve: to be harmed
    • svasareṣu: in the waters
    • pinvate: they flow or move
    • janāya: for the people
    • rātahaviṣe: in the great sacrifice
    • mahīm: the earth or greatness
    • iṣam: the power or strength
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the Maruts, who are divine beings associated with storms and winds, often depicted as powerful and energetic.
    • The phrase "yad yuñjate maruto" suggests a unity or collaboration among these deities, emphasizing the importance of teamwork and collective effort.
    • The mention of "rukmavakṣaso" highlights their golden appearance, symbolizing purity, strength, and divine energy.
    • “śvān ratheṣu” refers to the horses in chariots, which can be seen as a metaphor for speed and progress in life, akin to how we harness resources to achieve our goals.
    • The term "bhaga" signifies wealth or fortune, indicating that such divine collaboration brings prosperity.
    • The latter part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment and protection, as represented by the cow, which is a symbol of sustenance and care.
    • “janāya rātahaviṣe” suggests that these efforts are for the benefit of the people, reinforcing the idea of service and community welfare.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, teamwork among employees can lead to greater innovation and success, much like the Maruts working together.
    • In community service, when individuals unite for a common cause, they can create significant positive change, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on collective effort for the benefit of all.
    • In personal life, nurturing relationships and supporting one another can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life, akin to the nourishment symbolized by the cow.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity, collaboration, and the importance of serving the community.
    • It teaches us that when we come together with a common purpose, we can achieve greatness and bring prosperity not just to ourselves but to society as a whole.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, community development, and a harmonious existence.
