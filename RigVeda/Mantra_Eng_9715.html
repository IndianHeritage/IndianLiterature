परā हīन्द्र धावसि वृषाकपेर अति व्यथिः |नो अह प्र विन्दस्य अन्यत्र सोमपीतये विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
parā hīndra dhāvasi vṛṣākaper ati vyathiḥ | no aha pra vindasy anyatra somapītaye viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: beyond, supreme
    • hīndra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • dhāvasi: you run or move
    • vṛṣākaper: with great strength or power
    • ati: exceedingly
    • vyathiḥ: disturbance or trouble
    • no: for us
    • aha: I
    • pra: towards
    • vindasy: you find or obtain
    • anyatra: elsewhere
    • somapītaye: for the sake of the soma drink
    • viśvasmād: from all
    • indra: O Indra
    • uttaraḥ: the best or supreme
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "parā hīndra dhāvasi" suggests that Indra moves with supreme speed and strength, indicating his ability to swiftly address challenges.
    • "ati vyathiḥ" refers to the disturbances or troubles that may arise in life, emphasizing the need for divine intervention to overcome them.
    • The mantra expresses a plea for assistance, asking Indra to help us find solutions and strength in times of difficulty.
    • The mention of "somapītaye" highlights the importance of the soma drink, which symbolizes nourishment, vitality, and spiritual awakening.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek strength and guidance from higher powers or spiritual practices, similar to invoking Indra for support.
    • In competitive environments, such as sports or business, people may draw on their inner strength and resilience, akin to the qualities attributed to Indra, to overcome challenges and achieve success.
    • Communities facing natural disasters often come together, invoking collective strength and support, reminiscent of the divine assistance sought in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking strength and guidance in times of trouble.
    • It emphasizes the belief in a higher power that can help individuals and communities overcome obstacles and disturbances in life.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate resilience, seek support from others, and remain hopeful in the face of adversity.
    • The significance of this mantra extends beyond its spiritual context, as it can be applied in everyday life to inspire courage and determination.
