समिद्धो अग्निर निहितः पृथिव्याम प्रत्यङ् विश्वानि भुवनानि अस्तात् | होतā पावकः प्रदिवः सुमेधा देवो देवान यजत्व अग्निर अर्हन् ||
samiddho agnir nihitaḥ pṛthivyām pratyaṅ viśvāni bhuvanāny asthāt | hotā pāvakaḥ pradivaḥ sumedhā devo devān yajatv agnir arhan ||
  • Mantra: "samiddho agnir nihitaḥ pṛthivyām pratyaṅ viśvāni bhuvanāny asthāt | hotā pāvakaḥ pradivaḥ sumedhā devo devān yajatv agnir arhan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddho: kindled or ignited
    • agnir: fire
    • nihitaḥ: placed or situated
    • pṛthivyām: in the earth
    • pratyaṅ: all around
    • viśvāni: all or universal
    • bhuvanāny: worlds or realms
    • asthāt: has established or set
    • hotā: the one who invokes or calls
    • pāvakaḥ: the purifier
    • pradivaḥ: in the heavens
    • sumedhā: wise or intelligent
    • devo: the divine or god
    • devān: the gods
    • yajatv: worshipping or offering
    • agnir: fire (repeated for emphasis)
    • arhan: worthy or deserving
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sacred fire (Agni) that is kindled and placed on the earth, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • The phrase "viśvāni bhuvanāny asthāt" indicates that this fire has established itself in all realms, suggesting its omnipresence and importance in various aspects of life.
    • Agni is referred to as the "hotā," the one who invokes, highlighting the role of fire in rituals and offerings to the gods.
    • The term "pāvakaḥ" emphasizes the purifying nature of fire, which is often used in various cultures to cleanse and sanctify.
    • In the context of worship, "yajatv agnir arhan" signifies that the fire is worthy of worship, representing the divine presence in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings or religious festivals, symbolizing purification and the presence of the divine.
    • Cooking food over a fire can be seen as a way of honoring Agni, as it transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, much like how the divine transforms our lives.
    • In modern times, the concept of "fire" can also be metaphorically applied to passion and creativity, where individuals ignite their inner fire to pursue their dreams and aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a bridge between the earthly and the divine, emphasizing its role in purification, worship, and transformation.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their spiritual practices, encouraging them to recognize the sacredness in everyday actions and the importance of intention in rituals.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all realms and the divine presence that permeates our lives, urging us to honor and respect the forces that sustain us.
