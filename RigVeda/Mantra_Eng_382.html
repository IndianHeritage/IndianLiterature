अहे यातारं कम अपश्य इन्द्र हृदि यत ते जग्ह्नुषो भीर अगच्छत् |नव च यन् नवतिं च श्रवन्तीः श्येनो न भीतो अतरो राजांसि ||
aher yātāraṁ kam apaśya indra hṛdi yat te jaghnuṣo bhīr agacchat | nava ca yan navatiṁ ca sravantīḥ śyeno na bhīto ataro rajāṁsi ||
  • Mantra: "aher yātāraṁ kam apaśya indra hṛdi yat te jaghnuṣo bhīr agacchat | nava ca yan navatiṁ ca sravantīḥ śyeno na bhīto ataro rajāṁsi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aher: O Indra,
    • yātāraṁ: the one who travels,
    • kam: who,
    • apaśya: has not seen,
    • indra: O Indra,
    • hṛdi: in the heart,
    • yat: that which,
    • te: of you,
    • jaghnuṣo: of the slayers,
    • bhīr: fear,
    • agacchat: went away,
    • nava: new,
    • ca: and,
    • yan: that which,
    • navatiṁ: ninety,
    • ca: and,
    • sravantīḥ: flowing,
    • śyeno: like a falcon,
    • na: not,
    • bhīto: afraid,
    • ataro: surpassing,
    • rajāṁsi: the kings.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, invoking his power and presence.
    • The phrase "yātāraṁ kam apaśya" suggests a quest for understanding or insight that has not yet been realized.
    • It emphasizes the importance of inner vision and awareness, as indicated by "hṛdi yat te," which refers to the heart or inner self.
    • The mention of "bhīr agacchat" indicates that fear can be overcome, and one can move beyond limitations.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "nava ca yan navatiṁ ca sravantīḥ," speaks to the idea of new beginnings and the flow of life, akin to the continuous cycle of renewal.
    • Finally, "śyeno na bhīto ataro rajāṁsi" suggests that like a fearless falcon, one can rise above challenges and surpass those in positions of power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek new opportunities and insights, overcoming fears that may hold them back from achieving their goals.
    • In personal development, it encourages self-reflection and the pursuit of inner strength, reminding us that we can rise above our fears and limitations.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder that true leaders are not afraid to challenge the status quo and can inspire others to do the same.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner strength, courage, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional endeavors, and leadership.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and the ability to navigate challenges with confidence.
