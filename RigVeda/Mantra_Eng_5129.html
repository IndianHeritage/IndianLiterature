आलक्तā या रुरुशीर्ष्ण्य अथो यस्यā अयो मुखम् |इदं पर्जन्यरेतस इष्वै देव्यै बृहन् नमः ||
ālāktā yā ruruśīrṣṇy atho yasyā ayo mukham | idam parjanyaretasa iṣvai devyai bṛhan namaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ālāktā: Unrestrained or unbound.
    • yā: Who.
    • ruruśīrṣṇy: With a roaring or thunderous head.
    • atho: Then or therefore.
    • yasyā: Whose.
    • ayo: Iron or strength.
    • mukham: Face or mouth.
    • idam: This.
    • parjanyaretasa: With the energy of the rain or the rain-bearing.
    • iṣvai: With the divine or the gods.
    • devyai: To the goddess or divine being.
    • bṛhan: Great or vast.
    • namaḥ: Salutation or homage.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful divine entity, often associated with rain and nourishment.
    • The phrase "who has a roaring head" can symbolize the might and authority of nature, particularly in the form of storms or rain.
    • The mention of "iron" signifies strength, suggesting that this divine force is not only powerful but also essential for life.
    • The invocation to the goddess indicates a plea for blessings, particularly in the context of agriculture and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers often pray for rain during droughts, recognizing the importance of water for crops. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of that need for divine intervention.
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor rain gods, showcasing the universal understanding of the necessity of rain for life.
    • In modern times, environmentalists advocate for the protection of natural resources, echoing the reverence for nature expressed in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature's forces, particularly rain, in sustaining life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the environment, urging respect and gratitude towards the divine aspects of nature.
    • In contemporary life, it can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices and to honor the natural world, recognizing its vital role in our existence.
