मन्त्र: उता नो धियः गोAGRāḥ पूषण विष्णवेवयावः | कर्ता नः स्वस्तिमतः ||
uta no dhiyo goagrāḥ pūṣan viṣṇav evayāvaḥ | kartā naḥ svastimataḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "O Pūṣan, O Viṣṇu, may our intellects be sharp and clear. May you, the creator, grant us well-being."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: A call or invocation, often used to address deities.
    • no: Our, indicating a collective aspect.
    • dhiyo: Intellects or understanding, referring to mental clarity and wisdom.
    • goagrāḥ: Sharp or keen, suggesting the desire for enhanced perception and insight.
    • pūṣan: A deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, often linked to the sun.
    • viṣṇav: Referring to Viṣṇu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology.
    • evayāvaḥ: May you come or may you be present, indicating a plea for divine presence.
    • kartā: Creator or doer, emphasizing the active role of the divine in our lives.
    • naḥ: Us, reinforcing the communal aspect of the prayer.
    • svastimataḥ: Well-being or auspiciousness, highlighting the desire for a prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, students may recite this mantra before an exam, seeking clarity of thought and sharpness of intellect to perform well.
    • During a family gathering, individuals may invoke this mantra to bless the family with prosperity and well-being, emphasizing the importance of collective harmony.
    • In a corporate environment, teams may use this mantra in meetings to foster a collaborative spirit and enhance creativity, aiming for successful outcomes in projects.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation for clarity of thought and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in our intellectual pursuits and the collective well-being of individuals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, family gatherings, and professional environments, serving as a reminder to seek wisdom and prosperity through divine support. By reciting this mantra, individuals align themselves with higher energies, fostering a conducive atmosphere for growth and success.
