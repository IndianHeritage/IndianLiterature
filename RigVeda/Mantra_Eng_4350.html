इमां ऊष्वासुरस्य श्रुतस्य महीं मायां वरुणस्य प्र वोचम् |मानेनैव तस्थिवां अन्तरिक्षे वि यो ममे पृथिवीं सूर्येण ||
imām ū ṣv āsurasya śrutasya mahīm māyāṁ varuṇasya pra vocam | māneneva tasthivām̐ antarikṣe vi yo mame pṛthivīṁ sūryeṇa ||
  • imām ū ṣv āsurasya śrutasya mahīm: This phrase can be translated to "I speak of the great glory of the divine, the one who is known and revered." Here, "imām" refers to "this," "ū" means "I," "ṣv" relates to "speak," "āsurasya" refers to "of the divine," "śrutasya" means "known," and "mahīm" signifies "greatness" or "glory." This emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and speaking about the divine qualities that inspire reverence.
  • māyāṁ varuṇasya pra vocam: This translates to "I declare the illusion of Varuna." "Māyāṁ" refers to "illusion" or "the cosmic illusion," "varuṇasya" means "of Varuna," who is the god of water and cosmic order, and "pra vocam" means "I declare." This suggests that the divine has a role in the cosmic order and that understanding this illusion is crucial for spiritual growth.
  • māneneva tasthivām̐ antarikṣe: This can be interpreted as "As if by respect, I stand in the space." "Māneneva" means "by respect," "tasthivām̐" refers to "I stand," and "antarikṣe" means "in the space" or "in the atmosphere." This indicates that respect and reverence for the divine allow one to find their place in the universe.
  • vi yo mame pṛthivīṁ sūryeṇa: This translates to "Whoever has the earth with the sun." "Vi" means "who," "yo" refers to "whoever," "mame" means "my," "pṛthivīṁ" refers to "earth," and "sūryeṇa" means "with the sun." This line emphasizes the interconnectedness of the earth and the sun, symbolizing the relationship between the material and spiritual worlds.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the importance of respect and reverence towards it. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Practicing mindfulness and gratitude for the natural world and its elements, recognizing their divine essence.
  • Engaging in rituals or practices that honor the cosmic order and promote harmony in our lives.
  • Understanding the illusions of life and striving for deeper spiritual knowledge and connection.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine's role in our existence and encourages us to cultivate respect and awareness in our daily lives.
