वृकाय चिज जसमानाय शक्तम उता श्रुतं शयवे हूयमानā |याव घन्यां अपिन्वतम अपो न स्तर्यं चिच शक्त्य अश्विनā शचीभिः ||
vṛkāya cij jasamānāya śaktam uta śrutaṁ śayave hūyamānā | yāv aghnyām apinvatam apo na staryaṁ cic chakty aśvinā śacībhiḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vṛkāya: Refers to the "wolf" or "predator," symbolizing strength and ferocity.
    • cij: Means "that which is," indicating existence or being.
    • jasamānāya: Relates to "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has strength."
    • śaktam: Translates to "power" or "energy," emphasizing the force behind actions.
    • uta: Means "or," suggesting an alternative or additional aspect.
    • śrutaṁ: Refers to "that which is heard" or "knowledge," indicating wisdom or understanding.
    • śayave: Means "to the one who is lying down," possibly referring to a state of rest or contemplation.
    • hūyamānā: Translates to "being invoked" or "called upon," indicating a plea or request for assistance.
    • yāv: Means "as long as" or "until," indicating a condition or duration.
    • aghnyām: Refers to "the one who is not to be harmed," suggesting protection or safety.
    • apinvatam: Means "to the one who is not to be disturbed," indicating stability and peace.
    • apo: Refers to "water," symbolizing purity and life.
    • na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • staryaṁ: Refers to "the one who is stable" or "the one who is firm."
    • cic: Means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing certainty.
    • chakty: Refers to "power" or "ability," indicating potential.
    • aśvinā: Refers to the "Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • śacībhiḥ: Means "with strength" or "with power," indicating the support of divine forces.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the strength and power of the divine, particularly through the symbolism of the wolf, which represents ferocity and survival instincts.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom, suggesting that true strength comes from understanding and awareness.
    • The mantra also highlights the need for protection and stability, indicating that one should seek refuge in the divine to avoid harm and disturbance.
    • Water is mentioned as a symbol of purity and life, suggesting that spiritual nourishment is essential for maintaining strength and stability.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins signifies a plea for health, vitality, and the ability to overcome challenges with divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek strength through meditation or prayer, invoking a higher power for guidance and protection.
    • In a professional setting, teams may draw upon collective knowledge and wisdom to navigate challenges, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and support.
    • During natural disasters, communities come together to provide safety and stability, reflecting the mantra's call for protection and resilience.
    • In health and wellness practices, individuals may focus on purifying their bodies and minds, akin to the symbolism of water in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, knowledge, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the call for protection and stability, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and strength from both within and from higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals and communities in their pursuit of resilience, health, and harmony.
