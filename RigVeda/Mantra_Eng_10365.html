ऋष्वस् त्वं इन्द्र शूर जातो दासीर विषः सूर्येण सह्याः |गुहा हितं गुह्यं गूढम् अप्सु बिभ्रमसि प्रस्रवणे न सोमम् ||
ṛṣvas tvam indra śūra jāto dāsīr viśaḥ sūryeṇa sahyāḥ | guhā hitaṁ guhyaṁ gūḻham apsu bibhṛmasi prasravaṇe na somam ||
  • ṛṣvas tvam indra: This phrase can be translated as "You are the swift one, O Indra." Here, "ṛṣvas" refers to swiftness or agility, and "Indra" is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with strength and power.
  • śūra jāto: This means "born of valor." It emphasizes the idea that true strength comes from courage and bravery, suggesting that one should embody these qualities in their actions.
  • dāsīr viśaḥ: This translates to "the servant of the people." It highlights the importance of serving others and being a protector of the community, which is a significant aspect of leadership.
  • sūryeṇa sahyāḥ: This means "with the sun as a companion." The sun symbolizes light, knowledge, and life, indicating that one should seek wisdom and enlightenment in their endeavors.
  • guhā hitaṁ guhyaṁ gūḻham: This phrase can be interpreted as "the hidden and secret place." It suggests that there are deeper truths and mysteries in life that require exploration and understanding.
  • apsu bibhṛmasi: This translates to "you hold in the waters." It signifies the nurturing aspect of nature and the importance of water as a life-giving force.
  • prasravaṇe na somam: This means "in the flow, you do not hold back the soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. This phrase encourages the free flow of creativity and inspiration.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, courage, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of serving others while embodying strength and swiftness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles where one must act swiftly and decisively for the benefit of the community. It also encourages individuals to seek deeper truths and wisdom, reminding us that nurturing and creativity are essential for growth. By embodying these principles, one can lead a life that is not only fulfilling but also impactful on others.
