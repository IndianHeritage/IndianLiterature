मित्रश्च नो वरुणश्च जुषेतां यज्ञं इष्टये |नि बर्हिषि सदतां सोमपीतये ||
mitraś ca no varuṇaś ca juṣetāṁ yajñam iṣṭaye | ni barhiṣi sadatāṁ somapītaye ||
  • Mitra and Varuna: The mantra begins by invoking two deities, Mitra and Varuna. Mitra represents friendship, harmony, and the light of day, while Varuna embodies the cosmic order, truth, and the waters. Together, they symbolize the balance between social and cosmic laws.
  • Yajna (Sacrifice): The term 'yajñam' refers to a ritual sacrifice or offering. In Vedic tradition, yajna is a means of connecting with the divine, fostering community, and ensuring prosperity. It signifies the importance of selfless actions for the greater good.
  • Ishtaye (Desire): The word 'iṣṭaye' indicates the purpose of the yajna, which is to fulfill desires or aspirations. This reflects the human tendency to seek blessings and support from the divine in achieving personal and communal goals.
  • Barhishi (Sacred Grass): 'Barhiṣi' refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine. It serves as a reminder of the sanctity of the offerings made during the yajna.
  • Somapītaye (Consumption of Soma): 'Somapītaye' relates to the consumption of Soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This aspect emphasizes the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for higher knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of community, divine connection, and the pursuit of harmony in life. It teaches us that through selfless actions and rituals, we can seek blessings from the divine for our desires and aspirations. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community service, environmental conservation, and fostering relationships based on mutual respect and understanding. By embodying the principles of Mitra and Varuna, we can create a balanced and harmonious society, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to the world around us.
