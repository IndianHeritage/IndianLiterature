वयं हि वां हवामह उक्षण्यन्तो व्यश्ववत् |सुमतिभिर उप विप्राव इहागतम् ||
vayaṁ hi vāṁ havāmaha ukṣaṇyanto vyaśvavat | sumatibhir upa viprāv ihā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • hi: indeed
    • vāṁ: you
    • havāmaha: we invoke
    • ukṣaṇyanto: with great energy
    • vyaśvavat: like the swift-moving
    • sumatibhir: with good thoughts
    • upa: towards
    • viprāv: the wise ones
    • ihā: here
    • gatam: come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a collective invocation, where the speaker (we) is calling upon a higher power or divine presence (you) with great energy and intention.
    • The phrase "with good thoughts" emphasizes the importance of positive intentions in our actions and prayers.
    • The reference to "the wise ones" suggests a seeking of guidance and wisdom from those who are knowledgeable and enlightened.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team meeting, when members come together to brainstorm ideas, they invoke the spirit of collaboration and creativity, much like the invocation in the mantra.
    • During a community event, people gather with good intentions to support a cause, reflecting the essence of coming together with positive thoughts.
    • In educational settings, students often seek guidance from teachers (the wise ones) to gain knowledge and wisdom, paralleling the call for wisdom in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of collective intention and the importance of invoking wisdom and positive energy in our endeavors.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, community service, and educational pursuits, where collaboration and positive thinking lead to fruitful outcomes.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that when we come together with good thoughts and seek wisdom, we can achieve greater heights and foster a harmonious environment.
