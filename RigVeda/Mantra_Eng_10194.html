अपसरा जारम् उपसिष्मियाणा योषा बिभर्ति परम व्योमन् |चरत प्रियस्य योनिषु प्रियः सन् सीदत पक्षे हिरण्यये स वेनः ||
apsarā jāram upasiṣmiyāṇā yoṣā bibharti parame vyoman | carat priyasya yoniṣu priyaḥ san sīdat pakṣe hiraṇyaye sa venaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsarā: celestial nymph or divine woman
    • jāram: old or aged
    • upasiṣmiyāṇā: one who is adorned or decorated
    • yoṣā: woman
    • bibharti: carries or holds
    • parame: supreme or highest
    • vyoman: in the sky or celestial realm
    • carat: moving or wandering
    • priyasya: of the beloved
    • yoniṣu: in the wombs or origins
    • priyaḥ: dear or beloved
    • san: being or existing
    • sīdat: sits or resides
    • pakṣe: in the wings or sides
    • hiraṇyaye: golden or radiant
    • sa: he or that
    • venaḥ: the one who is powerful or strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine woman, often associated with beauty and grace, who exists in the celestial realm.
    • The phrase "apsarā jāram" suggests a timeless beauty that transcends age, indicating that true beauty is not diminished by the passage of time.
    • The mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine feminine and the concept of love, as seen in "carat priyasya yoniṣu," which refers to the beloved residing in the origins of life.
    • The imagery of "hiraṇyaye" evokes a sense of radiance and purity, suggesting that the divine feminine embodies these qualities.
    • The mention of "venaḥ" indicates strength and power, suggesting that the divine feminine is not only beautiful but also a source of strength and resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the concept of beauty is often tied to youth, but this mantra reminds us that true beauty can be found in wisdom and experience, as seen in the respect given to elder women in many cultures.
    • The idea of the divine feminine can be observed in various forms of art and literature, where women are celebrated for their strength, grace, and nurturing qualities.
    • In relationships, the mantra highlights the importance of love and connection, reminding us that our bonds with loved ones are rooted in deeper origins and shared experiences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the timeless nature of beauty and the strength of the divine feminine.
    • It encourages us to appreciate the wisdom that comes with age and to recognize the power of love in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to celebrate the women in our lives, honor their contributions, and acknowledge the beauty that exists in all stages of life.
