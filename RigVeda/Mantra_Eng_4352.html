आर्यम्यं वरुण मित्र्यं वा सखायं वा सदमिद भ्रातरं वा | वेशं वा नित्यम् वरुणारणं वा यत सिम आगश चक्रिमा शिश्रथस् तत् ||
aryamyaṁ varuṇa mitryaṁ vā sakhāyaṁ vā sadam id bhrātaraṁ vā | veśaṁ vā nityaṁ varuṇāraṇaṁ vā yat sīm āgaś cakṛmā śiśrathas tat ||
  • Mantra: "aryamyaṁ varuṇa mitryaṁ vā sakhāyaṁ vā sadam id bhrātaraṁ vā | veśaṁ vā nityaṁ varuṇāraṇaṁ vā yat sīm āgaś cakṛmā śiśrathas tat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aryamyaṁ: noble or virtuous
    • varuṇa: the god of cosmic order and water
    • mitryaṁ: friend or ally
    • sakhāyaṁ: companion or comrade
    • sadam: dwelling or abode
    • id: this
    • bhrātaraṁ: brother
    • veśaṁ: home or place
    • nityaṁ: eternal or everlasting
    • varuṇāraṇaṁ: the abode of Varuna
    • yat: that which
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • āgaś: has come
    • cakṛmā: we have made
    • śiśrathas: with care or attention
    • tat: that
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of nobility and virtue, emphasizing the importance of friendship and brotherhood.
    • It suggests that whether in the presence of divine forces like Varuna or in our earthly relationships, we should strive for harmony and support.
    • The mention of "home" and "abode" signifies the importance of a safe and nurturing environment, where relationships can flourish.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another, especially in times of need, reflecting the essence of brotherhood and companionship.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of virtuous relationships and the importance of community support.
    • In today's world, it serves as a reminder to foster connections based on trust, friendship, and mutual respect.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as family gatherings, community service, and even in professional environments where teamwork and collaboration are essential.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to create a nurturing space where everyone feels valued and supported, reflecting the divine qualities of Varuna in our daily lives.
