को वोऽन्तर मरुत ऋष्टविद्युतो रेजति तमनā हन्वेव जिह्वया |धन्वच्युत इषां न यामनि पुरुप्रैषा अहन्यो नैतशः ||
ko vo 'ntar maruta ṛṣṭividyuto rejati tmanā hanveva jihvayā | dhanvacyuta iṣāṁ na yāmani purupraiṣā ahanyo3 naitaśaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • vo: of you
    • 'ntar: within
    • maruta: the winds or storm gods
    • ṛṣṭividyuto: shining with knowledge or wisdom
    • rejati: shines or radiates
    • tmanā: with the self or soul
    • hanveva: like a bow
    • jihvayā: with the tongue
    • dhanvacyuta: the one who is not fallen from the bow
    • iṣāṁ: of the command or authority
    • na: not
    • yāmani: to be restrained
    • purupraiṣā: with many offerings
    • ahanyo: day after day
    • na: not
    • eitaśaḥ: from this place
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the divine forces (the Maruts) that reside within us and how they radiate wisdom.
    • It suggests that our inner self, much like a bow, can express itself through our words (the tongue).
    • The phrase "dhanvacyuta" indicates a state of being that is steadfast and not fallen, implying strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of not being restrained by external forces, even when faced with numerous offerings or distractions.
    • It reflects on the continuous nature of life ("ahanyo") and the need to remain focused and true to oneself.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of maintaining one's integrity and wisdom amidst the pressures of work and societal expectations.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to express their true selves and communicate openly, much like the bow that releases its arrow with precision.
    • In moments of adversity, the mantra serves as a reminder to stay grounded and not be swayed by external opinions or distractions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and the expression of inner wisdom.
    • It teaches us to harness our inner strength and communicate effectively, ensuring that we remain true to ourselves in all aspects of life.
    • In a world filled with distractions and external pressures, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal growth and resilience.
