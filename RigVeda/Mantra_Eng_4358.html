तां वृद्धन्ताव अनु dyūn मर्ताय देवाव अदभा |अर्हन्ता चित पुरो दधे 'ंशेव देवाव अर्वते ||
tā vṛdhantāv anu dyūn martāya devāv adabhā | arhantā cit puro dadhe 'ṁśeva devāv arvate ||
  • tā vṛdhantāv anu dyūn martāya devāv adabhā
    • tā - "They" refers to the divine beings or deities.
    • vṛdhantāv - "who are increasing" or "who are growing," indicating the prosperity and abundance that the deities bring.
    • anu - "following" or "in accordance with," suggesting a connection or alignment.
    • dyūn - "the heavens" or "the celestial realm," representing the higher spiritual plane.
    • martāya - "for mortals" or "for human beings," indicating the focus on human existence.
    • devāv - "the gods" or "the divine," emphasizing the role of deities in human life.
    • adabhā - "who are not to be disregarded" or "who are worthy of respect," highlighting the reverence owed to the divine.
  • arhantā cit puro dadhe 'ṁśeva devāv arvate
    • arhantā - "worthy" or "deserving," referring to the qualities of the deities.
    • cit - "consciousness" or "awareness," indicating the divine intelligence.
    • puro - "in the front" or "in the forefront," suggesting leadership or guidance.
    • dadhe - "they place" or "they establish," indicating the action of the deities.
    • 'ṁśeva - "like a part" or "as a portion," suggesting a connection between the divine and the mortal.
    • devāv - "the gods," reiterating the focus on the divine beings.
    • arvate - "they are invoked" or "they are called upon," indicating the act of seeking divine assistance.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine and human realms. It highlights the importance of recognizing and respecting the deities who guide and support human existence. In real life, this can be seen in various practices such as prayer, rituals, and offerings made to deities in different cultures. By acknowledging the divine presence and seeking their blessings, individuals can foster a sense of connection and receive guidance in their lives. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interdependence between the divine and the mortal, encouraging individuals to cultivate reverence and gratitude towards the forces that shape their existence.
