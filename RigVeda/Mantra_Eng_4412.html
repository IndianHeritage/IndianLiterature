यो नः सन्तुयो अभिदासद अग्ने यो अन्तरो मित्रमहो वानुष्यāt |तम अजरेभिर वृषभिस तव स्वैस तपा तपिष्ठ तपसā तपस्वान् ||
yo naḥ sanutyo abhidāsad agne yo antaro mitramaho vanuṣyāt | tam ajarebhir vṛṣabhis tava svais tapā tapiṣṭha tapasā tapasvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: our
    • sanutyo: well-being
    • abhidāsad: may he bestow
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • yo: who
    • antaro: within
    • mitram: friend
    • aho: indeed
    • vanuṣyāt: among men
    • tam: him
    • ajarebhir: who is eternal
    • vṛṣabhis: with strength
    • tava: your
    • svais: with his own
    • tapā: by austerity
    • tapiṣṭha: the most powerful
    • tapasā: through penance
    • tapasvān: the ascetic
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings for our well-being.
    • It emphasizes the importance of Agni as a friend among men, highlighting the connection between the divine and human realms.
    • The mantra acknowledges Agni's eternal nature and strength, suggesting that through austerity and penance, one can attain power and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, people often seek blessings for health and prosperity, similar to how this mantra seeks Agni's blessings for well-being.
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and strength, used in rituals to invoke divine presence and support.
    • Practices like meditation and yoga can be seen as modern forms of austerity and penance, aimed at achieving personal growth and inner strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support for our well-being and the role of inner strength and discipline in achieving our goals.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals strive for personal development, health, and harmony in their lives.
    • By recognizing the connection between the divine and human efforts, we can cultivate a balanced approach to life, integrating spiritual practices with everyday actions.
