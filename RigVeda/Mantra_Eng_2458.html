इळाम अग्ने पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्ने सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "of great glory" or "of great fame," highlighting Agni's revered status in Vedic literature and his importance in rituals.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cattle," indicating Agni's role in safeguarding wealth and prosperity, often represented by cattle in ancient societies.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This means "the eternal" or "the most enduring," emphasizing the timeless nature of Agni and his presence in various aspects of life.
  • havamānāya: This translates to "to the one who is invoked," suggesting the act of calling upon Agni during rituals and ceremonies.
  • sādha: This means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," indicating the desire for Agni to help achieve one's goals and aspirations.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This phrase means "may he be our son," which can be interpreted as a wish for Agni to bless the family with a virtuous and wise offspring.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This translates to "the son who is wise," further emphasizing the importance of wisdom and intelligence in progeny.
  • sā te sumatir: This means "may there be good thoughts from you," indicating a prayer for positive and wise guidance from Agni.
  • bhūtv asme: This translates to "may we become," suggesting a transformation or aspiration to embody the qualities associated with Agni.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking his blessings for wisdom, prosperity, and the well-being of the family. It reflects the importance of fire in Vedic rituals and its symbolic representation of knowledge and purity.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be relevant in various contexts, such as family gatherings, educational pursuits, and professional endeavors. For instance, when starting a new project or family, invoking the qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to seek wisdom and prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the values of wisdom, protection, and the pursuit of good thoughts. By invoking Agni, individuals can aspire to cultivate these qualities in their lives, fostering a harmonious and prosperous existence.
