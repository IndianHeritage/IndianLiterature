मन्त्र: निर यद ईम बुद्धान महिषस्य वर्पस ईशानासः शवसां क्रान्त सूरयः | यद ईम अनु प्रदिवो मध्व आढवे गुहा संतं मातरिश्वा मथायति ||
nir yad īm budhnān mahiṣasya varpasa īśānāsaḥ śavasā kranta sūrayaḥ | yad īm anu pradivo madhva ādhave guhā santam mātariśvā mathāyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir: without
    • yad: that which
    • īm: we seek
    • budhnān: of the wise
    • mahiṣasya: of the great one (often referring to the Earth or a powerful entity)
    • varpasa: of the form or appearance
    • īśānāsaḥ: of the rulers or lords
    • śavasā: with strength or vigor
    • kranta: having crossed or transcended
    • sūrayaḥ: the wise or enlightened ones
    • yad: that which
    • īm: we seek
    • anu: following
    • pradivo: of the heavens
    • madhva: sweet or pleasant
    • ādhave: for the benefit
    • guhā: the cave or hidden place
    • santam: the one who is present
    • mātariśvā: the wind or the one who moves in the sky
    • mathāyati: stirs or churns
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the quest for wisdom and understanding, emphasizing the importance of seeking knowledge from the wise.
    • The reference to "the great one" can symbolize the Earth or a higher power, suggesting that true wisdom is rooted in a connection to the greater universe.
    • The phrase "having crossed" indicates overcoming challenges or obstacles, which is a common theme in personal growth and development.
    • The mention of "the heavens" and "sweet" signifies the pursuit of higher ideals and the joy that comes from achieving them.
    • The "hidden place" can represent the depths of our own consciousness, where true understanding resides.
    • The wind, represented by "mātariśvā," symbolizes change and the dynamic nature of life, reminding us that wisdom is not static but evolves with experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students are encouraged to seek knowledge from teachers (the wise) to navigate their academic journey successfully.
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that require them to "cross" their fears or limitations to achieve their goals.
    • In nature, the changing seasons can be seen as a metaphor for the wisdom gained through life's cycles, where each phase brings new insights.
    • In relationships, understanding and communication can be viewed as a "hidden place" where deeper connections are formed, leading to personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking wisdom and understanding in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true knowledge comes from overcoming challenges and connecting with the greater universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue education, personal growth, and deeper relationships, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
    • By recognizing the dynamic nature of wisdom, we can embrace change and continuously strive for improvement in our lives.
