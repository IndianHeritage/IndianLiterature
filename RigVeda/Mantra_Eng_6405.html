मन्त्र: अदितिर्नो दिवा पशुम् अदितिर्नक्तम् अद्वयाः | अदितिः पातु अंहसः सदावृद्धा ||
aditir no divā paśum aditir naktam advayāḥ | aditiḥ pātv aṁhasaḥ sadāvṛdhā ||
  • Mantra: "aditir no divā paśum aditir naktam advayāḥ | aditiḥ pātv aṁhasaḥ sadāvṛdhā ||"
  • Translation: "Aditi, may you protect our cattle by day, Aditi, may you protect our cattle by night; may Aditi grant us strength and prosperity always."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Aditi: Aditi is a significant goddess in Vedic literature, often associated with the infinite, the boundless, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • No: This word translates to "us" or "our," indicating a plea or request directed towards Aditi.
    • Divā: Meaning "by day," it signifies the time when the sun is up, representing light, clarity, and protection.
    • Paśum: Refers to "cattle" or "livestock," which were vital for sustenance and wealth in ancient agrarian societies.
    • Naktam: Meaning "by night," it represents the time of darkness, often associated with uncertainty and vulnerability.
    • Advayāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "without duality" or "unified," suggesting a holistic approach to life and existence.
    • Pātv: Translates to "may grant" or "may provide," indicating a request for blessings or protection.
    • Aṁhasaḥ: Refers to "strength" or "power," essential for overcoming challenges and achieving prosperity.
    • Sadāvṛdhā: Meaning "always growing" or "ever-increasing," it emphasizes the desire for continuous growth and abundance.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cattle are often seen as a symbol of wealth and prosperity. This mantra can be recited by farmers seeking protection for their livestock from diseases and predators.
    • In modern contexts, the essence of this mantra can be applied to the protection of one's resources, whether they be financial, emotional, or physical. For instance, a business owner may invoke this mantra to seek success and protection for their enterprise.
    • Individuals may also use this mantra during times of uncertainty, such as during a crisis or challenging period, to seek strength and guidance from a higher power.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine protection and blessings for both day and night, symbolizing a holistic approach to life. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and safeguarding one's resources, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek strength and prosperity in all endeavors, fostering a sense of unity and resilience in the face of challenges. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and protection, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
