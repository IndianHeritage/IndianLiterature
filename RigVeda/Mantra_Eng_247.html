अपो देवीर उप हवये यत्र गावाḥ पिबन्ति नः |सिन्धुभ्यः कर्त्वं हविः ||
apo devīr upa hvaye yatra gāvaḥ pibanti naḥ | sindhubhyaḥ kartvaṁ haviḥ ||
  • Translation: "O divine waters, may you flow towards us, where the cows drink. We offer our oblation to the rivers."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apo: waters
    • devīr: divine
    • upa: towards
    • hvaye: we invoke
    • yatra: where
    • gāvaḥ: cows
    • pibanti: drink
    • naḥ: for us
    • sindhubhyaḥ: to the rivers
    • kartvaṁ: offering
    • haviḥ: oblation

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence of water, recognizing its sacredness and importance in life.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, as cows are considered sacred and vital for agricultural societies.
    • The act of offering to the rivers signifies gratitude and respect for nature's resources, acknowledging their role in sustaining life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, water is revered as a life-giving force. For instance, in Hindu rituals, water is often used for purification and blessings.
    • Farmers depend on rivers and rainfall for irrigation, paralleling the mantra's invocation for divine waters to flow towards them.
    • In modern environmental movements, the significance of clean water sources is emphasized, reflecting the ancient wisdom of respecting and protecting water bodies.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life, nature, and spirituality.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor and protect our natural resources, particularly water, which is essential for survival.
    • In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices and a deeper appreciation for the environment, encouraging us to live in harmony with nature.
