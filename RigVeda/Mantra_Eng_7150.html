इयम् उ ते अनुṣ्टुतिः चक्रिषे तानि पौंस्याः |प्रावश चक्रस्य वर्तनिम ||
iyam u te anuṣṭutiś cakṛṣe tāni pauṁsyā | prāvaś cakrasya vartanim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iyam: this
    • u: indeed
    • te: your
    • anuṣṭutiś: praise or worship
    • cakṛṣe: I have made or created
    • tāni: those
    • pauṁsyā: of the men or beings
    • prāvaś: I have entered or penetrated
    • cakrasya: of the wheel or cycle
    • vartanim: I am revolving or moving
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of creation and the reverence associated with it.
    • The phrase "this indeed is your praise" suggests a deep acknowledgment of the divine or higher power.
    • The reference to "those of the men" indicates a connection to humanity and the collective experience.
    • "I have entered the cycle" implies an understanding of the continuous nature of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate achievements, they often express gratitude and praise for the efforts of individuals, similar to the praise mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, recognizing the contributions of team members can create a positive environment, reflecting the idea of collective acknowledgment.
    • The concept of cycles can be seen in nature, such as the changing seasons, which remind us of the continuous flow of life and the importance of adaptation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of praise, creation, and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the contributions of others while understanding their place within the larger cycle of life.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, teamwork, and personal growth, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
