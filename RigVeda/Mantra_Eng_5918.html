न ते विष्णो जायमानो न जातो देव महिम्नः परम अन्तम आप |उद अस्तभ्ना नाकं ऋष्वं बृहन्तं दाधर्थ प्राचीं ककुभं पृथिव्याः ||
na te viṣṇo jāyamāno na jāto deva mahimnaḥ param antam āpa | ud astabhnā nākam ṛṣvam bṛhantaṁ dādhartha prācīṁ kakubham pṛthivyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na te: not yours
    • viṣṇo: of Vishnu
    • jāyamāno: being born
    • na jāto: not born
    • deva: divine
    • mahimnaḥ: of greatness
    • param: supreme
    • antam: end
    • āpa: waters
    • ud: up
    • astabhnā: having established
    • nākam: the sky
    • ṛṣvam: lofty
    • bṛhantaṁ: vast
    • dādhartha: having supported
    • prācīṁ: the east
    • kakubham: the directions
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the nature of divinity, particularly focusing on Vishnu, who represents preservation and sustenance in the universe.
    • The phrase "na te viṣṇo jāyamāno" suggests that Vishnu, while being born, is not subject to the limitations of birth and death, indicating his eternal nature.
    • The mantra emphasizes the greatness of the divine, stating that it is beyond comprehension and exists at the ultimate end of all things ("param antam").
    • The reference to "waters" and "sky" signifies the vastness and omnipresence of the divine, which is foundational to the existence of the universe.
    • By mentioning directions and the earth, the mantra illustrates how divinity encompasses all aspects of existence, providing support and structure to the cosmos.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance and support from higher powers or principles, much like how this mantra acknowledges the divine's role in sustaining the universe.
    • In moments of uncertainty or challenge, individuals may turn to spiritual practices or beliefs that resonate with the idea of a greater force that is always present and supportive.
    • The concept of interconnectedness in nature, where all elements support one another, can be seen as a reflection of the divine structure mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of divinity and its omnipresence in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the support and guidance available to them from the universe, fostering a sense of connection and purpose.
    • In practical terms, this understanding can lead to a more profound appreciation for life, nature, and the relationships we cultivate, as we acknowledge the divine presence in all aspects of existence.
