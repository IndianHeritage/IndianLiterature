मन्त्र: उता द्वार उषतीर विष्रयन्ताम् उता देवाम् उषत आ वहेह ||
uta dvāra uśatīr vi śrayantām uta devām̐ uśata ā vaheha ||
  • Mantra: "uta dvāra uśatīr vi śrayantām uta devām̐ uśata ā vaheha"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "May the auspicious ones (the deities) come to us through the doors of prosperity; may they bring us well-being and happiness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: a particle often used to denote 'may' or 'let us.'
    • dvāra: means 'door' or 'gateway,' symbolizing opportunities or pathways in life.
    • uśatīr: refers to 'auspicious' or 'beneficial,' indicating positive energies or forces.
    • vi: a preposition meaning 'through' or 'by means of.'
    • śrayantām: means 'to come' or 'to arrive,' suggesting the arrival of good fortune.
    • devām̐: refers to 'deities' or 'gods,' representing divine forces or higher powers.
    • uśata: again means 'well-being' or 'happiness,' emphasizing the desired outcome.
    • ā: a preposition meaning 'towards' or 'to,' indicating direction.
    • vaheha: means 'to bring' or 'to carry,' suggesting the act of delivering blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be recited during a new venture, such as starting a business. The 'doors of prosperity' symbolize the opportunities that one hopes to open, inviting success and well-being.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during festivals or rituals to invoke blessings from deities, ensuring that the community thrives and experiences happiness.
    • In daily life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking divine intervention to guide them through difficulties and towards a path of prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine blessings and auspicious energies. It serves as a reminder to seek positivity and well-being in all aspects of life. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that is open to opportunities and blessings, reinforcing the belief that divine forces are at work in their lives. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal endeavors to communal celebrations, emphasizing the importance of inviting positivity and prosperity into our lives.
