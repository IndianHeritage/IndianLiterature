यो रयिवो रयिंतमो यो द्युम्नैर द्युम्नवत्‍तमः |सोमः सुतः स इन्द्र तेऽस्ति स्वधापते मदः ||
yo rayivo rayiṁtamo yo dyumnair dyumnavattamaḥ | somaḥ sutaḥ sa indra te 'sti svadhāpate madaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • rayivo - of wealth
    • rayiṁtamo - the best of wealth
    • yo - who
    • dyumnair - by glory
    • dyumnavattamaḥ - the best of glory
    • somaḥ - the moon or soma (a divine drink)
    • sutaḥ - son
    • sa - he
    • indra - Indra (the king of gods)
    • te - your
    • 'sti - is
    • svadhāpate - O lord of self-will
    • madaḥ - intoxication or delight
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of wealth and glory.
    • It acknowledges the importance of Indra, the king of gods, and his connection to prosperity and joy.
    • The reference to "soma" suggests a connection to divine nourishment and spiritual intoxication.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for prosperity and success in ventures, emphasizing the importance of ethical practices to achieve true wealth.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek glory through their actions, encouraging them to contribute positively to society.
    • During celebrations or rituals, invoking this mantra can enhance the sense of community and shared joy, much like how festivals bring people together to celebrate abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the pursuit of true wealth and glory, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and ethical dimensions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building, reminding us to seek a balance between material success and spiritual fulfillment.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra and the essence of soma, we are encouraged to cultivate a life filled with joy, prosperity, and ethical integrity.
