सुतः सोमो असुताद इन्द्र वस्यान अयं श्रेयान चिकीतुषे रणाय |एतं तितिर्व उप याहि यज्ञं तेन विश्वास तविषीर आ पृणस्व ||
sutaḥ somo asutād indra vasyān ayaṁ śreyāñ cikituṣe raṇāya | etaṁ titirva upa yāhi yajñaṁ tena viśvās taviṣīr ā pṛṇasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sutaḥ: born of, or produced by
    • somo: the moon or soma, a divine drink
    • asutād: from the non-born or the unproduced
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • vasyān: those who are controlled or subdued
    • ayaṁ: this
    • śreyāñ: auspicious or beneficial
    • cikituṣe: for the purpose of protection or care
    • raṇāya: for battle or conflict
    • etaṁ: this
    • titirva: to be vigilant or alert
    • upa: towards
    • yāhi: come or approach
    • yajñaṁ: sacrifice or offering
    • tena: by that
    • viśvās: trust or faith
    • taviṣīr: to be strong or powerful
    • ā: towards
    • pṛṇasva: fill or satisfy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of Indra and the beneficial aspects of the moon (soma).
    • It emphasizes the importance of vigilance and readiness for challenges, particularly in the context of battles or conflicts.
    • The mantra suggests that through offerings and sacrifices (yajña), one can gain strength and trust from the divine, which can help in overcoming obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to preparing for a major project (raṇāya) where teamwork (yajña) and trust (viśvās) among colleagues are essential for success.
    • In personal life, it can represent the need for vigilance and readiness to face challenges, such as preparing for an important exam or competition.
    • In community service, it reflects the idea of coming together (upa yāhi) to support a cause, where collective efforts lead to beneficial outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for readiness and the importance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches that through dedication and collective effort, one can achieve strength and success in various aspects of life.
    • This mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering trust, preparing for challenges, and engaging in acts of service and sacrifice for the greater good.
