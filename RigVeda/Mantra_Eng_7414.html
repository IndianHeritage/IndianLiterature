तुभ्यां यम अद्रिभिः सुतो गोभिः श्रीतो मदाय कम् |प्र सोम इन्द्र हूयते ||
tubhyāyam adribhiḥ suto gobhiḥ śrīto madāya kam | pra soma indra hūyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyāyam: To you (Indra)
    • adribhiḥ: With the mountains
    • suto: Son
    • gobhiḥ: With the cows
    • śrīto: Blessed or prosperous
    • madāya: For the intoxication or joy
    • kam: What
    • pra: Forth
    • soma: Soma (a sacred drink)
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • hūyate: Is invoked or called
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the king of the gods, to bless the worshippers with prosperity and joy.
    • The mention of "adribhiḥ" (mountains) and "gobhiḥ" (cows) signifies the importance of nature and agriculture in Vedic culture, where mountains represent stability and cows symbolize wealth and nourishment.
    • The phrase "madāya kam" suggests a desire for joy and intoxication, not in a literal sense, but as a metaphor for spiritual ecstasy and fulfillment.
    • The invocation of "soma" indicates the importance of this sacred drink in rituals, believed to bestow immortality and divine insight.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, the reverence for nature can be seen in sustainable farming practices that honor the land and its resources, akin to the respect shown in the mantra.
    • Communities often gather to celebrate harvests, invoking blessings for prosperity, similar to how the mantra calls upon Indra for abundance.
    • Spiritual practices today may include rituals that seek joy and fulfillment, reflecting the essence of "madāya" in personal growth and happiness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and community well-being.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of divine forces that govern prosperity and joy, encouraging individuals to seek harmony with the environment and each other.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the blessings of nature and to invoke positivity and abundance in our lives through gratitude and reverence.
