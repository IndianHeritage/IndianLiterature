प्र वीराय प्र तवसे तुरायाज यूथेव पशुरक्षिर अस्तम् |स पिपृशति तन्वी श्रुतस्य स्तृभिर न नाकं वचनस्य विपः ||
pra vīrāya pra tavase turāyājā yūtheva paśurakṣir astam | sa pispṛśati tanvi śrutasya stṛbhir na nākaṁ vacanasya vipaḥ ||
  • pra vīrāya: This phrase can be interpreted as "for the brave" or "to the hero." It signifies the importance of courage and valor in one's life. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice, such as activists or whistleblowers who risk their safety for a greater cause.
  • pra tavase: This translates to "for the powerful" or "to the mighty." It emphasizes the value of strength and influence. In contemporary society, this can relate to leaders or individuals in positions of power who use their influence to effect positive change, such as community leaders or politicians advocating for social reforms.
  • turāyājā: This term can mean "to the swift" or "to the agile." It highlights the importance of speed and adaptability. In a fast-paced world, this can be exemplified by entrepreneurs who quickly pivot their business strategies in response to market changes, showcasing the need for agility in decision-making.
  • yūtheva: This means "like a herd" or "together." It signifies the strength found in unity and collaboration. In real life, this can be seen in team sports where players work together to achieve a common goal, or in community initiatives where collective efforts lead to significant improvements in society.
  • paśurakṣir astam: This phrase translates to "the protector of the cattle." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the protector of resources or wealth. In modern contexts, this can relate to environmental activists who protect natural resources and advocate for sustainable practices to ensure the well-being of future generations.
  • sa pispṛśati: This means "he touches" or "he influences." It indicates the power of influence and connection. In real life, this can be seen in mentorship relationships where experienced individuals guide and shape the lives of others, impacting their personal and professional growth.
  • tanvi śrutasya: This translates to "of the delicate sound" or "of the subtle knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding. In today's world, this can be reflected in the pursuit of education and knowledge, where individuals seek to deepen their understanding of complex issues to make informed decisions.
  • stṛbhir na nākaṁ: This phrase can mean "with the words of the wise." It highlights the significance of wise counsel and communication. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of effective communication in leadership, where leaders articulate their vision and inspire others through their words.
  • vacanasya vipaḥ: This translates to "the words of the wise." It signifies the lasting impact of wise words and teachings. In contemporary society, this can be exemplified by influential speeches or writings that inspire movements, such as Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech, which continues to resonate with people advocating for equality and justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of bravery, power, agility, unity, protection, influence, wisdom, and the impact of wise words. Its significance lies in the reminder that these qualities are essential for personal growth and societal progress. In real life, individuals can apply these principles by embodying courage in their actions, using their influence for good, collaborating with others, protecting resources, seeking knowledge, and communicating effectively. By doing so, they contribute to a more just and harmonious society.
