एषा शुभ्रा न तंवो विदानोर्ध्वेवं स्नाति दृष्टये नो अस्तात् |अप द्वेषो बादमानां तमांस्य उषा दिवो दुहिताऽज्योतिषागात् ||
eṣā śubhrā na tanvo vidānordhveva snātī dṛśaye no asthāt | apa dveṣo bādhamānā tamāṁsy uṣā divo duhitā jyotiṣāgāt ||
  • eṣā - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
  • śubhrā - Meaning "pure" or "auspicious," it signifies something that is bright and beneficial.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not."
  • tanvo - Refers to "body" or "form," suggesting a physical aspect.
  • vidānordhveva - This compound word can be broken down into "vidāna" (knowledge) and "urdhva" (upward), implying a rise in knowledge or enlightenment.
  • snātī - Meaning "to bathe" or "to cleanse," it symbolizes purification.
  • dṛśaye - This translates to "for the sight" or "to be seen," indicating visibility or perception.
  • no - Meaning "not" or "no," reinforcing the negation.
  • asthāt - This means "to stand" or "to exist," suggesting a state of being.
  • apa - Meaning "away" or "from," indicating separation or distance.
  • dveṣo - This translates to "hatred" or "enmity," referring to negative emotions.
  • bādhamānā - Meaning "afflicting" or "causing pain," it suggests the impact of negative feelings.
  • tamāṁsy - Refers to "darkness" or "ignorance," symbolizing a lack of knowledge or clarity.
  • uṣā - Meaning "dawn" or "light," it represents new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • divo - This translates to "of the sky" or "heaven," indicating a divine or higher realm.
  • duhitā - Meaning "daughter," it symbolizes nurturing and care.
  • jyotiṣāgāt - This compound word can be broken down into "jyotiṣa" (light) and "gāt" (to go), suggesting the arrival of light or knowledge.

The mantra speaks of the importance of purity and enlightenment while distancing oneself from negativity and ignorance. It emphasizes the need for a clear vision and understanding, free from hatred and affliction. The imagery of dawn signifies hope and the arrival of knowledge, akin to how the morning light dispels darkness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the dawn brings light, individuals can seek knowledge and clarity in their lives, moving away from negative emotions and thoughts.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, this mantra encourages individuals to cleanse their minds of hatred and strive for understanding and peace.
  • Spiritual Practices: Many people use this mantra in meditation to focus on purity and enlightenment, fostering a sense of calm and clarity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity, knowledge, and the importance of overcoming negativity. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and clarity in their lives, promoting a harmonious existence both within oneself and in relation to others.
