अग्नि वीहि हविषा यक्षि देवान स्वध्वराः कृणुहि जातवेदः ||
agne vīhi haviṣā yakṣi devān svadhvarā kṛṇuhi jātavedaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings to the deities.
  • Vīhi: This term means "to come" or "to approach." It signifies a call for the divine presence of Agni.
  • Haviṣā: This word translates to "with offerings." It indicates the act of making offerings to the divine, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals.
  • Yakṣi: This means "to bestow" or "to grant." It is a request for Agni to grant blessings or fulfill desires.
  • Devān: This word means "the gods." It acknowledges the presence of divine beings and the interconnectedness of all deities.
  • Svadhvarā: This term can be interpreted as "with the right path" or "with proper conduct." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness in one's actions.
  • Kṛṇuhi: This means "to make" or "to create." It is a plea for Agni to create or manifest blessings in the devotee's life.
  • Jātavedaḥ: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It highlights Agni's role as a witness to all actions and the source of knowledge.

The mantra "agne vīhi haviṣā yakṣi devān svadhvarā kṛṇuhi jātavedaḥ" is a prayer to Agni, seeking his presence and blessings through offerings. It reflects the deep-rooted belief in the power of fire as a purifying and transformative element in Vedic rituals. The mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness and proper conduct in seeking divine blessings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Offerings: Individuals can recite this mantra during rituals to invoke the blessings of Agni, especially during ceremonies like weddings or housewarming, where fire plays a significant role.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on righteousness (svadhvarā) can inspire individuals to lead a life of integrity and moral values, guiding their decisions and actions.
  • Seeking Guidance: In times of uncertainty, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine guidance and clarity in one's path.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine presence through offerings, emphasizing righteousness and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the role of fire as a transformative force in both spiritual and everyday life.
