एष स्य परि षिच्यते मार्मृज्यमान आयुभिः |उरुगायः कविकृतुः ||
eṣa sya pari ṣicyate marmṛjyamāna āyubhiḥ | urugāyaḥ kavikratuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of him
    • pari: around
    • ṣicyate: is sprinkled
    • marmṛjyamāna: being wiped or cleansed
    • āyubhiḥ: with life or longevity
    • urugāyaḥ: of great fame or glory
    • kavikratuḥ: the wise or the poet with intent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine essence that surrounds and protects life.
    • The act of sprinkling signifies purification and the nurturing of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intent in achieving greatness.
    • The phrase "urugāyaḥ" suggests that true glory comes from a life lived with purpose and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to "cleanse" their lives of negativity, much like the "marmṛjyamāna" aspect of the mantra. This can be seen in practices such as meditation or therapy.
    • In a professional context, a leader who embodies "kavikratuḥ" inspires their team with vision and intent, leading to collective success and recognition ("urugāyaḥ").
    • In health and wellness, rituals that promote longevity and vitality can be seen as a modern interpretation of "āyubhiḥ," where individuals engage in activities that enhance their life force.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, wisdom, and the pursuit of greatness.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, guided by wisdom and intent, can lead to a fulfilling and celebrated life.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek purification, embrace wisdom, and strive for a life of purpose, ultimately leading to personal and communal glory.
