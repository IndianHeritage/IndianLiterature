मन्त्र: भद्राः ते हस्ताः सुखृतोता पाणिः प्रयन्ताराः स्तुवते राध इन्द्रः |का ते निषत्तिः किमु नो ममात्सि किम् नोदुदु हर्षसे दातवाः उ ||
bhadrā te hastā sukṛtota pāṇī prayantārā stuvate rādha indra | kā te niṣattiḥ kim u no mamatsi kiṁ nod-ud u harṣase dātavā u ||
  • bhadrā te hastā: This phrase translates to "May your hands be auspicious." In real life, this can be interpreted as a blessing for someone to have the ability to perform good deeds and actions. For example, when a person starts a new job, wishing them "bhadrā te hastā" signifies hope for their success and positive contributions.
  • sukṛtota pāṇī: This means "hands that are engaged in good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of actions that are righteous and beneficial. In a practical scenario, this could relate to volunteering or helping others in need, showcasing how one's actions can lead to positive outcomes in society.
  • prayantārā stuvate rādha indra: This can be interpreted as "those who praise you, O Indra, are moving forward." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often associated with strength and leadership. This line suggests that those who honor and respect powerful figures or deities will find progress in their endeavors. For instance, in a community, when people come together to support a leader or a cause, they often see collective growth and success.
  • kā te niṣattiḥ: This translates to "What is your intention?" It raises a question about purpose and motivation. In everyday life, understanding one's intentions can lead to more meaningful actions. For example, in a business setting, knowing the intention behind a project can help align the team's efforts towards a common goal.
  • kim u no mamatsi: This means "What do you not desire from us?" It reflects a sense of humility and the desire to understand the needs of others. In relationships, whether personal or professional, asking this question can foster better communication and understanding, leading to stronger bonds.
  • kiṁ nod-ud u harṣase dātavā u: This translates to "What do you not give us joy?" It speaks to the idea of sharing happiness and blessings. In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness, where sharing joy with others can create a positive environment. For instance, celebrating achievements together can enhance team spirit and morale.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of auspiciousness, good deeds, intention, humility, and the sharing of joy. Its significance lies in the reminder to engage in positive actions, understand our intentions, and foster relationships based on mutual respect and joy. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger communities, and a more harmonious society. By embodying the essence of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a collective well-being that resonates through their actions and interactions.
