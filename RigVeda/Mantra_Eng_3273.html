अवासृजन्त जिव्रयो न देवाः भुवः सम्राज् इन्द्र सत्ययोनिः |अहन्न अहिम परिशयानम् अर्णः प्र वर्तनीर अरदो विश्वधेनाः ||
avāsṛjanta jivrayo na devā bhuvaḥ samrāḻ indra satyayoniḥ | ahann ahim pariśayānam arṇaḥ pra vartanīr arado viśvadhenāḥ ||
  • avāsṛjanta jivrayo: This phrase can be translated as "they (the gods) created living beings." It signifies the divine act of creation, where the gods are seen as the architects of life. In real life, this can be related to the concept of birth and the emergence of life in various forms, from humans to animals and plants.
  • na devā bhuvaḥ samrāḻ: This translates to "the gods are not bound by the earth." It suggests that divine beings operate beyond the limitations of the physical world. In a practical sense, this can be understood as the pursuit of higher ideals and aspirations that transcend material concerns, such as the quest for knowledge, spirituality, and personal growth.
  • indra satyayoniḥ: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods, and "satyayoniḥ" means "the source of truth." This implies that Indra embodies truth and righteousness. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions, reflecting the idea that truth is foundational to a just society.
  • ahann ahim pariśayānam: This phrase can be interpreted as "I strike down the serpent." The serpent often symbolizes obstacles or negativity. In real life, this can represent overcoming challenges and adversities, whether they be personal struggles, societal issues, or internal conflicts.
  • arṇaḥ pra vartanīr: This translates to "the one who moves forward." It signifies progress and the continuous journey of life. In practical terms, this can be related to the importance of perseverance and resilience in achieving goals, whether in education, career, or personal development.
  • arado viśvadhenāḥ: This can be understood as "the one who knows all." It emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this reflects the value of education, learning from experiences, and the importance of being open-minded and curious about the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of creation, transcendence, truth, overcoming obstacles, progress, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in its encouragement to aspire for higher ideals, embrace challenges, and seek truth and wisdom in our lives. In a world often filled with distractions and negativity, this mantra serves as a reminder to focus on personal growth, integrity, and the continuous journey of life, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to societal progress.
