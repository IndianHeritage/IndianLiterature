मां न एकस्मिन्न आगसि मां द्वयोः उता त्रिषु |वधीर्मां शूर भूषिषु ||
mā na ekasminn āgasi mā dvayor uta triṣu | vadhīr mā śūra bhūriṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: do not
    • na: not
    • ekasminn: in one
    • āgasi: in the approach
    • mā: do not
    • dvayor: of two
    • uta: or
    • triṣu: in three
    • vadhīr: be killed
    • mā: do not
    • śūra: brave
    • bhūriṣu: in many
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and strength in numbers.
    • It suggests that one should not be divided in their approach, whether in a singular or dual manner, nor should they be overwhelmed by multiple challenges.
    • The repetition of "mā" (do not) serves as a strong admonition against fragmentation and fear.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, this mantra can be applied to encourage collaboration. For instance, in a workplace project, if team members work in silos (ekasminn), it can lead to inefficiencies. Instead, they should unite (mā dvayor) to tackle challenges together.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can remind individuals not to let misunderstandings (triṣu) create divisions. Open communication can prevent conflicts and foster stronger bonds.
    • In community initiatives, this mantra can inspire collective action. For example, during a natural disaster, communities that come together (bhūriṣu) to support each other are more resilient than those that do not.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and collective strength. It teaches that division can lead to vulnerability, while togetherness fosters resilience.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or community contexts, embracing collaboration and open communication can lead to more effective problem-solving and stronger relationships.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that we are stronger together, and by avoiding division, we can face challenges with courage and fortitude.
