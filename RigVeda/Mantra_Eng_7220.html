परोमात्रं ऋचिषमं इन्द्रं उग्रं सुराधसम् |ईशानं चिद् वसूनाम् ||
paromātram ṛcīṣamam indram ugraṁ surādhasam | īśānaṁ cid vasūnām ||
  • paromātram: This term can be translated as "the supreme measure" or "the highest limit." It signifies the ultimate standard or the most elevated state of being.
  • ṛcīṣamam: This word refers to "the one who is most worthy of praise" or "the most exalted." It highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring those who embody greatness.
  • indram: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength, power, and the protector of the heavens.
  • ugraṁ: This term means "fierce" or "powerful." It emphasizes the formidable nature of Indra, showcasing his might and authority.
  • surādhasam: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the best among the gods" or "the most excellent." It signifies Indra's superiority among divine beings.
  • īśānaṁ: This word translates to "the lord" or "the ruler." It indicates a position of leadership and control, suggesting that Indra holds a significant place in the cosmic order.
  • cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness." It implies a deeper understanding or realization of existence.
  • vasūnām: This refers to "the wealth" or "the treasures." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the abundance of life and the various blessings one can receive.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call to recognize and honor the supreme power and consciousness embodied by Indra, who is the ultimate protector and provider of wealth and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the highest forms of strength and leadership in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: In organizations or communities, recognizing and respecting strong leaders who guide and protect their people is essential. Just as Indra is revered for his strength, modern leaders can be honored for their contributions and guidance.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of striving for the "supreme measure" can inspire individuals to pursue excellence in their personal and professional lives. It encourages self-improvement and the pursuit of higher consciousness.
  • Community Support: The mantra can also serve as a reminder to support those who provide for the community, much like Indra provides for the heavens. Acknowledging and appreciating the efforts of caregivers, leaders, and providers fosters a sense of gratitude and unity.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the supreme powers in our lives, whether they be leaders, deities, or the principles of strength and abundance. By embodying these values, we can cultivate a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
