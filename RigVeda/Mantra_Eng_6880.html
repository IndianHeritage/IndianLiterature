मन्द्रं होतारं ऋत्विजं चित्रभानुं विभावसुम् |अग्निम् ईळे स उ श्रवत् ||
mandraṁ hotāram ṛtvijaṁ citrabhānuṁ vibhāvasum | agnim īḻe sa u śravat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandraṁ: A hymn or mantra, often referring to a sacred sound or chant.
    • hotāram: The one who invokes or calls upon, typically referring to a priest or a divine figure.
    • ṛtvijaṁ: The priest or officiant who performs rituals, especially in Vedic traditions.
    • citrabhānuṁ: The bright sun or a radiant being, symbolizing light and knowledge.
    • vibhāvasum: The one who illuminates or brings forth light, often associated with fire or the sun.
    • agnim: Fire, which is a central element in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and transformation.
    • īḻe: I invoke or I praise, indicating a call to the divine.
    • sa: He or that, referring to the divine being invoked.
    • u: And, a conjunction that connects thoughts.
    • śravat: To flow or to pour, often used in the context of offerings or blessings.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine fire (Agni) and the sun (Citrabhānu), invoking their blessings and illumination.
    • It emphasizes the role of the priest (ṛtvija) in performing rituals and calling upon divine forces for guidance and support.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, representing transformation, purification, and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of meditation or mindfulness, where individuals seek inner light and clarity.
    • During significant life events such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, fire is often used symbolically to represent the divine presence and blessings.
    • In a broader sense, the invocation of light and knowledge can be seen in educational pursuits, where individuals seek enlightenment and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and illumination in our lives.
    • It teaches us the importance of rituals and the role of spiritual leaders in connecting us with higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek knowledge, clarity, and transformation, reminding us of the light that guides our paths.
