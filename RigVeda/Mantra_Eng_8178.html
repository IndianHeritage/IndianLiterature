अस्मे वसूनि धारय सोम दिव्यानी पार्थिवा | इन्दो विश्वानि वार्याः ||
asme vasūni dhāraya soma divyāni pārthivā | indo viśvāni vāryā ||
  • asme: This word translates to "for us" or "to us." It signifies a sense of possession or belonging.
  • vasūni: This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." In the context of the mantra, it implies the abundance of resources and blessings.
  • dhāraya: This means "to hold" or "to sustain." It suggests the act of maintaining or nurturing what is received.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes spiritual nourishment.
  • divyāni: This translates to "divine" or "heavenly." It indicates that the wealth or blessings are of a higher, spiritual nature.
  • pārthivā: This means "earthly" or "material." It contrasts with the divine, suggesting a balance between spiritual and material wealth.
  • indo: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • viśvāni: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the idea of encompassing everything.
  • vāryā: This translates to "worthy" or "desirable." It suggests that the blessings sought are not just any blessings, but those that are truly valuable.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for the sustenance of both spiritual and material wealth. It calls upon divine forces to bestow upon us the riches of life, both in the physical and spiritual realms. The invocation of Indra emphasizes the need for strength and support in achieving these blessings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra while seeking personal development, aiming to balance their material ambitions with spiritual growth.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can use this mantra during gatherings or rituals to invoke collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Business Success: Entrepreneurs may chant this mantra to attract success and abundance in their ventures, ensuring they remain grounded and ethical in their pursuits.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and prosperity. It teaches us to seek not just material gains but also spiritual fulfillment. By recognizing the importance of both realms, we can lead a balanced and fulfilling life. This mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the divine blessings we receive and to strive for a harmonious existence.
