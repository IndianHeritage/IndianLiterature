पुरुकुत्सानी हि वाम अदाशद धव्येभिर इन्द्रावरुणा नमोभिः |अथा राजानं त्रसदस्युम अस्य वृत्रहणं ददथुर अर्धदेवम् ||
purukutsānī hi vām adāśad dhavyebhir indrāvaruṇā namobhiḥ | athā rājānaṁ trasadasyum asyā vṛtrahaṇaṁ dadathur ardhadevam ||
  • Purukutsānī hi vām adāśad dhavyebhir indrāvaruṇā namobhiḥ
    • Purukutsānī: Refers to a powerful figure or deity, often associated with strength and valor.
    • hi: A particle that emphasizes the statement, akin to "indeed" or "certainly."
    • vām: Means "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to the divine or a powerful entity.
    • adāśad: Translates to "has given" or "has bestowed," suggesting a gift or blessing.
    • dhavyebhir: Refers to offerings or sacrifices, often made to deities in Vedic rituals.
    • indrāvaruṇā: A compound of two deities, Indra (the king of gods) and Varuna (the god of cosmic order), symbolizing the balance of power and righteousness.
    • namobhiḥ: Means "with reverence" or "with salutations," indicating respect and devotion.
  • Athā rājānaṁ trasadasyum asyā vṛtrahaṇaṁ dadathur ardhadevam
    • Athā: Means "then" or "thereafter," indicating a sequence of events.
    • rājānaṁ: Refers to "kings" or "rulers," highlighting the importance of leadership.
    • trasadasyum: Suggests "one who is fearful" or "one who causes fear," possibly referring to a powerful adversary.
    • asyā: Means "of this" or "of this situation," indicating a specific context.
    • vṛtrahaṇaṁ: Refers to "the slayer of Vritra," a mythical serpent representing chaos and obstacles.
    • dadathur: Means "they have given" or "they have bestowed," indicating an act of granting or providing.
    • ardhadevam: Translates to "half-god" or "demigod," suggesting a being with divine qualities but not fully divine.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the role of powerful deities in overcoming obstacles and fears. It highlights the relationship between rulers and the divine, suggesting that true leadership is supported by spiritual strength and reverence. In real life, this can be applied to situations where individuals seek guidance and strength in leadership roles, whether in governance, business, or personal challenges. The invocation of divine support can inspire confidence and resilience, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles and that higher powers can aid us in our endeavors.
