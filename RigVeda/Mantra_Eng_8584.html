एवां देव देवताते पवस्व महे सोम प्सरसे देवपानः |महश्चिद्धि ष्मसि हिताः समर्ये कृधि सुस्थाने रोधसी पुनानः ||
evā deva devatāte pavasva mahe soma psarase devapānaḥ | mahaś cid dhi ṣmasi hitāḥ samarye kṛdhi suṣṭhāne rodasī punānaḥ ||
  • evā deva devatāte: This phrase can be translated as "O divine deities." It invokes the presence of the divine, acknowledging the multitude of gods and their roles in the universe.
  • pavasva mahe soma: "Pavasva" means "to purify" or "to flow," and "mahe soma" refers to the great soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This line emphasizes the importance of purification and the quest for spiritual enlightenment.
  • psarase devapānaḥ: "Psarase" means "in the waters," and "devapānaḥ" translates to "the drink of the gods." This suggests a connection between the divine and the natural world, highlighting the significance of water as a life-giving and purifying force.
  • mahaś cid dhi: "Mahaś" means "great," and "cid" refers to "consciousness" or "awareness." This line points to the profound nature of divine consciousness, suggesting that understanding this consciousness is essential for spiritual growth.
  • ṣmasi hitāḥ: "Ṣmasi" means "in the earth," and "hitāḥ" translates to "beneficial" or "auspicious." This indicates that the divine presence is also grounded in the physical world, and that earthly actions can lead to spiritual benefits.
  • samarye kṛdhi suṣṭhāne: "Samarye" means "in the battle" or "in the struggle," "kṛdhi" means "to do," and "suṣṭhāne" translates to "in a good place." This line encourages individuals to engage in righteous actions, even amidst challenges, to achieve a favorable outcome.
  • rodasī punānaḥ: "Rodasī" refers to "the heavens" or "the sky," and "punānaḥ" means "purifying." This suggests that the divine can purify and elevate one's spirit, connecting the earthly realm with the celestial.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human consciousness. It highlights the importance of purification, righteous action, and the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, encouraging individuals to seek purity in their thoughts and actions, engage in meaningful struggles, and recognize the divine presence in both the natural world and their own consciousness. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and contribute positively to the world around them.
