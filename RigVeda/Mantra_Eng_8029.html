आत सोम इन्द्रियो रसो वज्रः सहस्रसां भुवत् |उक्तं यद अस्य जायते ||
āt soma indriyo raso vajraḥ sahasrasā bhuvat | ukthaṁ yad asya jāyate ||
  • Transliteration: āt soma indriyo raso vajraḥ sahasrasā bhuvat | ukthaṁ yad asya jāyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āt: from, out of
    • soma: soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • indriyo: senses, faculties
    • raso: essence, taste, flavor
    • vajraḥ: thunderbolt, strength, power
    • sahasrasā: thousandfold, abundant
    • bhuvat: becomes, manifests
    • ukthaṁ: hymn, utterance, praise
    • yad: that which
    • asya: of this, of it
    • jāyate: is born, arises
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence and power derived from the sacred drink, soma, which is believed to enhance the senses and provide strength.
    • The phrase "sahasrasā bhuvat" suggests that this essence manifests in abundance, indicating a connection to the divine and the universe.
    • The mention of "ukthaṁ" emphasizes the importance of hymns and praises, suggesting that through devotion and expression, one can tap into this divine essence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be likened to experiences that elevate our consciousness, such as meditation, yoga, or even engaging in art and music. These activities can enhance our senses and provide a sense of strength and clarity.
    • For instance, a person practicing mindfulness may find that their awareness and appreciation of life’s beauty increase, akin to the "indriyo raso" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Additionally, the idea of "ukthaṁ" can be seen in the practice of gratitude, where expressing thanks can lead to a more fulfilling and abundant life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of connecting with the divine essence through our senses and expressions. It teaches us that by engaging in practices that elevate our consciousness, we can manifest strength and abundance in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the power of our words and actions, suggesting that through praise and gratitude, we can create a more meaningful existence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the potential for transformation through spiritual practices.
