तद् विष्णोः परमं पदं सदा पश्यन्ति सूरयः |दिवीव चक्षुर आततम् ||
tad viṣṇoḥ paramam padaṁ sadā paśyanti sūrayaḥ | divīva cakṣur ātatam ||
  • tad viṣṇoḥ: This phrase refers to "that of Vishnu," indicating the supreme aspect of the deity Vishnu, who is often associated with preservation and protection in Hinduism.
  • paramam padaṁ: Translated as "the highest abode" or "the ultimate state," this signifies a place of ultimate truth and enlightenment, often interpreted as the spiritual realm or moksha (liberation).
  • sadā: Meaning "always" or "eternally," this word emphasizes the perpetual nature of the truth or state being referred to.
  • paśyanti: This translates to "see" or "perceive," suggesting an active engagement with the truth or divine presence.
  • sūrayaḥ: Referring to "the wise" or "the enlightened ones," this term highlights those who have attained knowledge and understanding, often through spiritual practice.
  • divīva: Meaning "like the divine" or "heavenly," this word draws a comparison to a higher, celestial state of being.
  • cakṣur: Translated as "eye," it symbolizes perception, insight, and the ability to see beyond the physical realm.
  • ātatam: This means "extended" or "spread out," indicating the vastness of the divine vision or understanding.

In essence, this mantra speaks to the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment and the vision of the divine. It suggests that those who are wise and enlightened are able to perceive the ultimate truth of Vishnu's highest abode, which is eternal and transcendent. The comparison to a divine eye implies that this perception is not limited to the physical senses but extends into a deeper, spiritual understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals seeking spiritual growth can meditate on this mantra to cultivate a deeper understanding of their own existence and the universe.
  • Philosophical Reflection: It encourages reflection on the nature of reality and the pursuit of knowledge, prompting individuals to seek wisdom and insight in their lives.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders and educators can embody the qualities of the "sūrayaḥ" by guiding others towards enlightenment and understanding, fostering a community that values wisdom and insight.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and strive for the highest truth and understanding. It serves as a reminder that true perception goes beyond the material world, urging individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to cultivate wisdom in their lives. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can navigate life's challenges with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
