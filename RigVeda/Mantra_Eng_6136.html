प्रजाम ऋतस्य पिप्रतः प्र यद भरन्त वहनयः |विप्राः ऋतस्य वाहसः ||
prajām ṛtasya piprataḥ pra yad bharanta vahnayaḥ | viprā ṛtasya vāhasā ||
  • Mantra: "prajām ṛtasya piprataḥ pra yad bharanta vahnayaḥ | viprā ṛtasya vāhasā ||"
  • Translation: "The wise ones, who carry the truth, bring forth the progeny of righteousness, as they are nourished by the fire."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prajām: progeny or offspring, representing the fruits of one's actions and the continuation of life.
    • ṛtasya: of truth or righteousness, indicating the moral and ethical foundation of existence.
    • piprataḥ: bringing forth or nurturing, suggesting the act of fostering growth and development.
    • pra: a prefix meaning 'forth' or 'out,' emphasizing the action of bringing something into existence.
    • yad: that which, referring to the qualities or actions that are being discussed.
    • bharanta: those who carry or bear, indicating responsibility and the act of supporting something important.
    • vahnayaḥ: of the fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark that fuels life.
    • viprā: the wise or learned ones, referring to those who possess knowledge and understanding.
    • vāhasā: by the means of truth, indicating the method or path taken by the wise to achieve their goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents nurture their children (prajām) with values of honesty and integrity (ṛtasya), ensuring they grow up to be responsible individuals who contribute positively to society.
    • In a workplace, leaders who embody ethical practices (viprā) inspire their teams to work towards common goals (bharanta) while maintaining a commitment to truth and fairness (vāhasā).
    • In environmental conservation, activists bring forth initiatives (piprataḥ) that promote sustainable practices (ṛtasya) to protect the planet for future generations (prajām).
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing truth and righteousness in all aspects of life. It signifies that wise individuals play a crucial role in fostering growth and development, not just for themselves but for the community and future generations. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, leadership, and social responsibility, where the focus is on ethical practices and the well-being of others. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and just society, ensuring that the values of truth and righteousness are passed down through generations.
