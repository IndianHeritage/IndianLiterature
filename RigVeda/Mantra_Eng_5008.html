उता स्यां नः सरस्वती घोरा हिरण्यवर्तिनिः |वृत्रघ्नī वष्टि सुश्टुतिम् ||
uta syā naḥ sarasvatī ghorā hiraṇyavartaniḥ | vṛtraghnī vaṣṭi suṣṭutim ||
  • uta syā naḥ sarasvatī ghorā hiraṇyavartaniḥ
    • uta - This word can be interpreted as "may" or "let it be." It signifies a wish or a prayer for something to happen.
    • syā - This means "be" or "become," indicating a desire for a transformation or manifestation.
    • naḥ - This translates to "our," suggesting a collective aspect, emphasizing community or shared experience.
    • sarasvatī - Referring to the goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and learning. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and enlightenment.
    • ghorā - This means "terrible" or "fearsome," which can symbolize the challenges and obstacles one faces in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • hiraṇyavartaniḥ - This term can be understood as "golden" or "wealthy," often associated with prosperity and abundance, particularly in the context of knowledge and wisdom.
  • vṛtraghnī vaṣṭi suṣṭutim
    • vṛtraghnī - This means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes ignorance or obstacles. This can be related to overcoming challenges in life.
    • vaṣṭi - This word can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to manifest," indicating the act of realization or achievement.
    • suṣṭutim - This translates to "good praise" or "excellent praise," suggesting the importance of recognizing and appreciating the good in life.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for the blessings of Sarasvatī, the goddess of knowledge, to guide us through the challenges of ignorance and obstacles in our pursuit of wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of community in this journey, as we collectively seek enlightenment and prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, during examinations, or when facing difficult decisions, reminding us to seek knowledge and overcome challenges with the support of others. Ultimately, it signifies the transformative power of knowledge and the importance of recognizing and praising the good in our lives.
