आ नः सोम पवमानः किरा वस्व इन्दो भव मगवाः राधसो महः |शिक्षा वयोधो वसवे सु चेतुना मा नो गयम आरे अस्मत परा सिचः ||
ā naḥ soma pavamānaḥ kirā vasv indo bhava maghavā rādhaso mahaḥ | śikṣā vayodho vasave su cetunā mā no gayam āre asmat parā sicaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: May it come to us
    • soma: Soma, the divine nectar or elixir
    • pavamānaḥ: Purifying or invigorating
    • kirā: Bestow
    • vasv: All, everything
    • indo: O Indra, the king of gods
    • bhava: Become
    • maghavā: The powerful one
    • rādhaso: Of the wealth or riches
    • mahaḥ: Greatness
    • śikṣā: Instruction or teaching
    • vayodho: Of the wind or air
    • vasave: To the one who is worthy of worship
    • su: Good or auspicious
    • cetunā: With intelligence or wisdom
    • mā: May not
    • no: Our
    • gayam: Song or praise
    • āre: To be sung
    • asmat: From us
    • parā: Beyond
    • sicaḥ: Bestow or grant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, a symbol of divine energy and rejuvenation.
    • It seeks blessings from Indra, the god of rain and thunder, representing strength and power.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and instruction, suggesting that true wealth comes from knowledge and understanding.
    • It also highlights the need for purity and clarity in thoughts and actions, akin to how the wind carries messages and energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration, where the collective wisdom of the team leads to greater success.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, promoting personal growth and development.
    • During challenging times, invoking the essence of this mantra can serve as a reminder to stay focused on purity of thought and action, much like how one would seek clarity in a storm.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings, wisdom, and purity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to teamwork and leadership.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for greatness, guided by knowledge and the pursuit of higher ideals.
