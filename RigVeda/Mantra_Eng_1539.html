बळ इत्त्था तद वपुषे धायि दर्शतं देवस्य भार्गः सहसो यतो जनि |यद ईम उप ह्वरते साधते मतिः ऋतस्य धेनाऽनयन्त सश्रुतः ||
baḻ itthā tad vapuṣe dhāyi darśataṁ devasya bhargaḥ sahaso yato jani | yad īm upa hvarate sādhate matir ṛtasya dhenā anayanta sasrutaḥ ||
  • baḻ itthā tad vapuṣe: This phrase can be interpreted as "May that form (or body) be present here." It signifies a call for the divine presence to manifest in the physical realm.
  • dhāyi darśataṁ devasya bhargaḥ: This translates to "the brilliance of the divine is revealed." Here, "bhargaḥ" refers to the divine light or energy that illuminates the path of righteousness.
  • sahaso yato jani: This means "from where the creation emerges." It emphasizes the source of all creation and existence, suggesting a connection to the divine origin of life.
  • yad īm upa hvarate: This can be understood as "wherever the mind focuses." It highlights the importance of intention and concentration in achieving one's goals.
  • sādhate matir ṛtasya: This translates to "the intellect that strives for truth." It underscores the value of wisdom and discernment in the pursuit of righteousness.
  • dhenā anayanta sasrutaḥ: This phrase means "the nurturing force that brings forth abundance." It symbolizes the idea of prosperity and sustenance that comes from a higher power.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine presence and guidance in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of focusing our minds on truth and righteousness, which can lead to abundance and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during meditation, prayer, or moments of decision-making. By invoking the divine light and wisdom, individuals can navigate challenges with clarity and purpose, ultimately fostering a deeper connection with the universe and their own inner selves.
