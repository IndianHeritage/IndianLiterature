मां नो वधैर वरुण ये त इष्टाव एनः कृत्वन्तम असुर भ्रिणन्ति |मां ज्योतिषः प्रवसथानि गन्म विषू मृधः शिश्रथो जीवसे नः ||
mā no vadhair varuṇa ye ta iṣṭāv enaḥ kṛṇvantam asura bhrīṇanti | mā jyotiṣaḥ pravasathāni ganma vi ṣū mṛdhaḥ śiśratho jīvase naḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, may we not be harmed by those who are hostile; may they who are powerful not bring us down. May we not be struck by the rays of the sun; may we live without fear of death."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Varuna: A deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority, representing the natural laws of the universe.
    • Vadhair: Refers to harm or injury, indicating a plea for protection.
    • Asura: Often translated as 'demon' or 'powerful being,' representing forces that can cause harm.
    • Jyotiṣaḥ: Refers to light or rays, particularly those of the sun, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Viṣū: Refers to death or destruction, indicating a fear of mortality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, employees often face competition and challenges from colleagues. This mantra can be interpreted as a call for protection against negative influences and harmful competition.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may encounter toxic behaviors or conflicts. The plea for protection from Varuna can resonate with those seeking harmony and safety in their interactions.
    • In the context of health, the fear of illness or death is a common concern. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and resilience in the face of life's uncertainties.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection and safety from harm, both physical and emotional. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, whether in professional settings, personal relationships, or health concerns, invoking this mantra can serve as a source of strength and reassurance, encouraging individuals to face adversities with courage and hope. By recognizing the forces that threaten our well-being, we can cultivate a mindset of resilience and seek the light of knowledge and protection in our lives.
