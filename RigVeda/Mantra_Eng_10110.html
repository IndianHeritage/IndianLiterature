मन्त्र: चतुर्दशान्ये महिमानो अस्य तं धीराः वाचाः प्र णयन्ति सप्त | आप्नानं तीर्थं का इह प्र वोचद येन पथा प्रपिबन्ते सुतस्य ||
caturdaśānye mahimāno asya taṁ dhīrā vācā pra ṇayanti sapta | āpnānaṁ tīrthaṁ ka iha pra vocad yena pathā prapibante sutasya ||
  • Mantra: "caturdaśānye mahimāno asya taṁ dhīrā vācā pra ṇayanti sapta | āpnānaṁ tīrthaṁ ka iha pra vocad yena pathā prapibante sutasya ||"
  • Translation: "The wise speak of the greatness of this (knowledge) in fourteen ways; who here can declare the sacred place from which they drink the essence of the divine?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • caturdaśānye: In fourteen ways
    • mahimāno: Greatness or glory
    • asya: Of this (knowledge or truth)
    • taṁ: That
    • dhīrā: The wise or intelligent
    • vācā: By speech or words
    • pra ṇayanti: They declare or speak
    • sapta: Seven (often symbolizes completeness)
    • āpnānaṁ: Attaining or reaching
    • tīrthaṁ: Sacred place or pilgrimage site
    • ka: Who
    • iha: Here
    • pra vocad: Can declare or speak
    • yena: By which
    • pathā: Path or way
    • prapibante: They drink or consume
    • sutasya: Of the essence or divine nectar
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom, suggesting that true understanding can be articulated in multiple ways.
    • The reference to "fourteen ways" indicates the depth and multifaceted nature of knowledge, akin to how various disciplines (like science, philosophy, and art) can explain the same truth from different perspectives.
    • The "sacred place" symbolizes the source of wisdom and enlightenment, which can be interpreted as spiritual practices, education, or personal experiences that lead to deeper understanding.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In education, this mantra can be applied to the idea that students should be encouraged to explore subjects from various angles, fostering critical thinking and creativity.
    • In personal development, it highlights the importance of seeking knowledge from diverse sources, such as books, mentors, and life experiences, to gain a holistic understanding of life.
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to explore different paths to enlightenment, recognizing that each person's journey is unique and valuable.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the richness of knowledge and the importance of articulating and sharing wisdom in various forms.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and appreciate the diverse paths to understanding, whether in academic, personal, or spiritual contexts.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a quest for deeper truths and the sharing of insights that can benefit the broader community.
