अर्धं वीरस्य श्रितपाम अनिन्द्रम परा शर्धन्तं नुनुदे अभि क्षाम् |इन्द्रो मन्युम मन्युम्यो मिमाय भेजे पथो वर्तनिम पत्यमानः ||
ardhaṁ vīrasya śṛtapām anindram parā śardhantaṁ nunude abhi kṣām | indro manyum manyumyo mimāya bheje patho vartanim patyamānaḥ ||
  • Ardhaṁ vīrasya: This phrase translates to "half of the hero" or "the essence of valor." It signifies the importance of courage and strength in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who display bravery in difficult situations, such as firefighters or soldiers who risk their lives to save others.
  • Śṛtapām anindram: This means "the one who is not to be blamed" or "the one who is pure." It emphasizes the value of integrity and righteousness. For example, a person who stands up against corruption in their workplace embodies this quality, showing that moral clarity is essential in society.
  • Parā śardhantaṁ: This translates to "the supreme strength" or "the ultimate power." It refers to the idea that true strength comes from a higher source, often interpreted as divine or spiritual. In everyday life, this can be seen in people who draw strength from their faith or beliefs during tough times, such as those who find solace in prayer or meditation.
  • Nunude abhi kṣām: This phrase can be understood as "may we be granted the ability" or "may we receive the strength." It reflects a plea for empowerment and support. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking help from mentors or community leaders when facing personal or professional challenges.
  • Indro manyum manyumyo: This translates to "Indra, the lord of strength, who is full of vigor." Indra is often seen as a symbol of power and leadership. In real life, this can be likened to leaders who inspire and motivate others, such as a coach who encourages their team to strive for excellence.
  • Mimāya bheje: This means "he who has created" or "he who has manifested." It signifies the act of creation and the importance of innovation. In today's world, this can be related to entrepreneurs who bring new ideas to life, contributing to societal progress and development.
  • Patho vartanim patyamānaḥ: This translates to "the one who walks the path." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous or correct path in life. This can be seen in individuals who choose ethical careers or engage in social work, demonstrating commitment to a greater good.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of courage, integrity, divine strength, and the importance of following a righteous path. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to embody these qualities in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength from higher sources, act with integrity, and strive for personal and communal betterment. By embracing these values, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same.
