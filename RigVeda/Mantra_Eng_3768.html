मातेव यद्भरसे पप्रथानो जनं-जनं धायसे चक्षसे च | वयो-वयो जरेसे यद्दधानाḥ परित्मना विषुरूपो जिगासि ||
māteva yad bharase paprathāno janaṁ-janaṁ dhāyase cakṣase ca | vayo-vayo jarase yad dadhānaḥ pari tmanā viṣurūpo jigāsi ||
  • māteva: This word translates to "like a mother." It signifies nurturing, care, and unconditional love.
  • yad: This means "that which" or "what." It introduces a clause that describes the qualities of the subject.
  • bharase: This translates to "you bear" or "you support." It indicates the act of providing strength or sustenance.
  • paprathāno: This means "spreading" or "expanding." It suggests the idea of growth and dissemination.
  • janaṁ-janaṁ: This translates to "people" or "individuals." It emphasizes the collective nature of humanity.
  • dhāyase: This means "you run" or "you move." It indicates action and dynamism.
  • cakṣase: This translates to "you see" or "you perceive." It refers to awareness and understanding.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting the ideas presented.
  • vayo-vayo: This translates to "age after age" or "throughout time." It signifies continuity and the passage of time.
  • jarase: This means "with old age." It refers to the inevitable process of aging.
  • yad: Again, "that which," introducing another clause.
  • dadhānaḥ: This translates to "you hold" or "you possess." It indicates the act of carrying or maintaining something.
  • pari: This means "around" or "in all directions." It suggests a comprehensive or holistic approach.
  • tmanā: This translates to "with the self" or "by oneself." It emphasizes individuality and personal experience.
  • viṣurūpo: This means "of diverse forms" or "having many shapes." It indicates the multiplicity of existence.
  • jigāsi: This translates to "you strive" or "you seek." It refers to the pursuit of goals or understanding.

The mantra speaks to the nurturing aspect of existence, likening it to a mother's care. It emphasizes the importance of supporting and understanding individuals in their journey through life, especially as they age. The repeated references to perception and action highlight the dynamic nature of human experience, where individuals are constantly moving, growing, and seeking understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Parenting: Just as a mother nurtures her child, individuals can strive to support and uplift those around them, fostering a sense of community and care.
  • Education: Teachers and mentors can embody the qualities of this mantra by guiding students through their learning journeys, helping them to perceive and understand the world.
  • Aging: The acknowledgment of aging as a natural process encourages respect and care for the elderly, recognizing their wisdom and experiences.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own journeys, understanding that growth and striving for knowledge are lifelong pursuits.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity, the importance of nurturing relationships, and the continuous journey of growth and understanding throughout life. It encourages us to embrace our roles as caregivers, learners, and seekers of knowledge, fostering a compassionate and supportive environment for all.
