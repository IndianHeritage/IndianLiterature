इन्द्राग्नि युवाम इमेऽभि स्तोमा अनूषत |पिबतं शम्भुवाऽसुतम् ||
indrāgnī yuvām ime3 'bhi stomā anūṣata | pibataṁ śambhuvā sutam ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership, while Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Yuvām: This word means "you two," indicating a duality or partnership. It emphasizes the collaboration between Indra and Agni in their divine roles.
  • Ime 'bhi: This phrase translates to "these," referring to the offerings or praises directed towards the deities. It signifies the importance of devotion and recognition of divine forces.
  • Stomā: This term means "hymns" or "praises." It highlights the significance of chanting and expressing reverence towards the gods, which is a common practice in many cultures.
  • Anūṣata: This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It indicates the act of summoning the divine presence through prayer and ritual.
  • Pibataṁ: This term translates to "drink" or "consume." It symbolizes the act of receiving blessings or divine energy, akin to how one would partake in a sacred offering.
  • Śambhuvā: This word means "the son of prosperity" or "the auspicious one." It refers to the blessings and abundance that come from the divine.
  • Sutam: This term means "the son" or "the offspring." In this context, it refers to the results or fruits of the divine blessings that are being sought.

  • Meaning: The mantra essentially calls upon the combined powers of Indra and Agni to receive their blessings through hymns and offerings. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be likened to the practice of seeking guidance and support from mentors or leaders (like Indra) and harnessing energy and creativity (like Agni) in various endeavors, such as starting a new project or overcoming challenges.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of divine forces and human efforts. It teaches us the value of collaboration, respect for the divine, and the importance of seeking blessings in our pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in rituals, personal prayers, or even in teamwork, where invoking a higher purpose can lead to greater success and fulfillment.
