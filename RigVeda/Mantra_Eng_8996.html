यम् अग्ने मन्यसे रयिं सहसावन्न अमर्त्यः |तम ā नो वाजसातये वि वो मदे यज्ञेषु चित्रम ā भरā विवक्षसे ||
yam agne manyase rayiṁ sahasāvann amartya | tam ā no vājasātaye vi vo made yajñeṣu citram ā bharā vivakṣase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: who
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • manyase: you think of
    • rayiṁ: wealth
    • sahasāvann: abundant
    • amartya: immortal
    • tam: that
    • ā: may
    • no: us
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of strength
    • vi: you
    • vo: your
    • made: in joy
    • yajñeṣu: in sacrifices
    • citram: wonderful
    • ā: may
    • bharā: bestow
    • vivakṣase: you desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The speaker is asking Agni to bestow abundant wealth and immortality upon them, emphasizing the importance of strength and joy in their lives.
    • The phrase "yajñeṣu citram" suggests that the speaker seeks blessings during rituals and sacrifices, highlighting the significance of spiritual practices in attaining prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of success and abundance in various fields, such as business or personal development.
    • For instance, entrepreneurs often invoke the spirit of perseverance and strength, akin to the blessings sought from Agni, to overcome challenges and achieve their goals.
    • Additionally, during significant life events like weddings or festivals, people perform rituals (yajñas) to seek divine blessings for happiness and prosperity, resonating with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in the pursuit of wealth, strength, and joy.
    • It emphasizes the importance of spiritual practices and rituals in achieving one's desires and aspirations.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek balance between material pursuits and spiritual growth, encouraging individuals to cultivate both aspects for a fulfilling life.
