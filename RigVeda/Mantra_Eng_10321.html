अग्नि अच् छा वदिह नः प्रत्यङ नः सुमना भव |प्र नो यच्छ विशस पते धनदाऽसि नस् त्वम् ||
agne acchā vadeha naḥ pratyaṅ naḥ sumanā bhava | pra no yaccha viśas pate dhanadā asi nas tvam ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Acchā: This means "good" or "auspicious," indicating a desire for positive outcomes.
  • Vadeha: This translates to "speak" or "express," suggesting a call for communication or guidance.
  • Naḥ: This means "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Pratyaṅ: This can be interpreted as "towards us," implying a request for attention or favor.
  • Sumanā: This means "good thoughts" or "auspicious intentions," highlighting the importance of positive mindset.
  • Bhava: This translates to "become" or "manifest," indicating a desire for these qualities to materialize.
  • Pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," suggesting a proactive approach.
  • No: This means "us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the mantra.
  • Yaccha: This means "give" or "bestow," indicating a request for blessings or gifts.
  • Viśas: This refers to "the lord of the people" or "the protector," often associated with leadership and guidance.
  • Pate: This means "lord" or "master," emphasizing authority and respect.
  • Dhanadā: This translates to "giver of wealth," indicating a desire for prosperity and abundance.
  • Asi: This means "you are," affirming the presence of the divine or the higher power.
  • Nas: This means "us," again reinforcing the collective aspect.
  • Tvam: This means "you," addressing the divine directly.

This mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, seeking his blessings for good communication, positive thoughts, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being and the desire for auspiciousness in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions, fostering a mindset that attracts success and happiness.
  • Community Well-being: Groups or communities can use this mantra during gatherings or rituals to invoke collective blessings for prosperity and harmony.
  • Business and Prosperity: Entrepreneurs may chant this mantra before starting a new venture, seeking divine guidance and abundance in their endeavors.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and blessings for positive communication, thoughts, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of seeking higher wisdom in our pursuits. By invoking Agni, we align ourselves with the energies of light and purity, fostering an environment conducive to growth and abundance.
