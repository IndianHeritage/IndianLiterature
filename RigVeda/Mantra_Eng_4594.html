सनेमा ते 'वसा नव्य इन्द्र प्र पूरवः स्तवन्त एनाऽ यज्ञैः |सप्त यत पुरः शर्म शारदीर दर्ध धन दासीयः पुरुकुत्साय शिक्षन् ||
sanema te 'vasā navya indra pra pūravaḥ stavanta enā yajñaiḥ | sapta yat puraḥ śarma śāradīr dard dhan dāsīḥ purukutsāya śikṣan ||
  • Sanema te 'vasā navya indra pra pūravaḥ stavanta enā yajñaiḥ:
    • Sanema: This word translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies a plea or request for divine attention.
    • Te: This means "to you," indicating the direction of the invocation towards a deity.
    • Vasā: This can be interpreted as "dwelling" or "residing," suggesting a connection to the divine presence.
    • Navya: This word means "new" or "fresh," indicating a desire for renewal or rejuvenation.
    • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • Pra pūravaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the one who comes forth," emphasizing the active role of the deity in responding to the invocation.
    • Stavanta: This means "praising" or "worshipping," indicating the act of devotion and reverence.
    • Enā yajñaiḥ: This translates to "through these sacrifices," highlighting the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.
  • Sapta yat puraḥ śarma śāradīr dard dhan dāsīḥ purukutsāya śikṣan:
    • Sapta: This means "seven," which often symbolizes completeness or wholeness in Vedic texts.
    • Yat: This translates to "that which," indicating a reference to something significant.
    • Puraḥ: This means "in front" or "before," suggesting a forward-looking perspective.
    • Śarma: This word means "shelter" or "protection," indicating a desire for safety and security.
    • Śāradīr: This can be interpreted as "of the autumn," symbolizing a time of harvest and abundance.
    • Dard: This word can mean "to give" or "to bestow," indicating generosity and the act of sharing.
    • Dhan: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
    • Dāsīḥ: This means "servants" or "helpers," indicating the role of community and support in achieving goals.
    • Purukutsāya: This refers to a specific figure, possibly a king or a hero, symbolizing leadership and valor.
    • Śikṣan: This means "teaching" or "instruction," highlighting the importance of knowledge and guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for renewal, protection, and prosperity. It emphasizes the significance of rituals and community support in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new venture, seeking guidance in difficult times, or celebrating achievements with gratitude. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine support, human effort, and the importance of community in our journeys.
