यद धा स्याता इन्द्र श्रुष्टिर अस्ति यया बभूथा जरीतृभ्य ऊती |मा नः कामम् महायन्तम् आ धग विश्वा ते अश्यां पर्य आप आयोः ||
yad dha syā ta indra śruṣṭir asti yayā babhūtha jaritṛbhya ūtī | mā naḥ kāmam mahayantam ā dhag viśvā te aśyām pary āpa āyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad dha syā ta indra śruṣṭir asti yayā babhūtha jaritṛbhya ūtī | mā naḥ kāmam mahayantam ā dhag viśvā te aśyām pary āpa āyoḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever creation, O Indra, exists through your power, may it bring us strength and abundance. May our desires not be consumed, and may all your blessings flow towards us."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yad dha syā: "Whatever exists" - This phrase emphasizes the vastness of creation and the potential that lies within it.
    • ta indra: "O Indra" - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
    • śruṣṭir asti: "exists through your power" - This acknowledges that all creation is a result of divine energy and influence.
    • yayā babhūtha: "may it bring us" - A plea for the benefits of creation to be bestowed upon us.
    • jaritṛbhya ūtī: "strength and abundance" - This refers to the blessings of prosperity and vitality that we seek in life.
    • mā naḥ kāmam: "may our desires not be consumed" - A request for our aspirations to be fulfilled rather than lost or diminished.
    • mahayantam ā dhag: "may they be great" - This emphasizes the importance of our desires being significant and impactful.
    • viśvā te aśyām: "may all your blessings flow" - A call for the divine blessings of Indra to be abundant and all-encompassing.
    • pary āpa āyoḥ: "towards us" - A concluding plea for these blessings to be directed towards the supplicant.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked by entrepreneurs seeking success and prosperity in their ventures. They may recite it before important meetings or decisions to attract positive outcomes.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of uncertainty or when pursuing their goals, such as education or career advancements, to seek strength and guidance.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective blessings for the well-being and prosperity of the entire community.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine power to support human endeavors. It serves as a reminder that our aspirations and creations are interconnected with a higher force. By acknowledging this relationship, we cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards our desires and the world around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to communal harmony, emphasizing the importance of seeking strength and abundance through divine blessings.
