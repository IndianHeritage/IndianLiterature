स सुष्टुभा स स्तुभा सप्त विप्रैः स्वरेंआद्रिं स्वaryo3 नवग्वैः |सरण्युभिः फालिगं इन्द्र शक्र वलं रवेण दारयो दशग्वैः ||
sa suṣṭubhā sa stubhā sapta vipraiḥ svareṇādriṁ svaryo3 navagvaiḥ | saraṇyubhiḥ phaligam indra śakra valaṁ raveṇa darayo daśagvaiḥ ||
  • sa suṣṭubhā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is well-structured" or "that which is beautifully composed." In real life, this can refer to anything that is organized and aesthetically pleasing, such as a well-designed building or a harmonious piece of music.
  • sa stubhā: This means "that which is praised" or "that which is celebrated." In everyday life, this could relate to achievements that are recognized and honored, such as awards given for excellence in various fields like sports, arts, or academics.
  • sapta vipraiḥ: This translates to "with seven sages" or "with seven wise ones." The number seven often symbolizes completeness or perfection in various cultures. In a real-life context, this could refer to a group of experts or mentors who guide individuals in their personal or professional journeys.
  • svareṇādriṁ svaryo3 navagvaiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the sound of the mountain, the beautiful ones, and the nine-fold." This could symbolize the harmony of nature and the importance of listening to the wisdom of the environment. For example, the sounds of nature can inspire creativity and peace in our lives.
  • saraṇyubhiḥ: This means "with those who seek refuge." In real life, this can relate to individuals or communities that provide support and safety to those in need, such as shelters for the homeless or organizations that assist refugees.
  • phaligam: This translates to "fruit-bearing" or "that which yields results." In practical terms, this can refer to efforts that lead to tangible outcomes, such as a successful project at work or the fruits of one's labor in a garden.
  • indra śakra: Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods and a symbol of strength and power. In a modern context, this could represent leadership qualities in individuals who inspire and lead others effectively, such as a CEO or a community leader.
  • valaṁ raveṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the strength of the sun." The sun is a source of energy and life, symbolizing vitality and growth. In real life, this could relate to the importance of maintaining a positive attitude and energy in our daily lives.
  • darayo daśagvaiḥ: This translates to "with ten-fold support." This can signify the importance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving goals. In real life, this could be seen in group projects where the combined efforts of individuals lead to success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of harmony, support, and the beauty of structure in both the natural and social worlds. It highlights the importance of collaboration, wisdom, and the fruits of our efforts. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support systems, and the pursuit of personal goals. By embodying these values, individuals can create a more balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society as a whole.
