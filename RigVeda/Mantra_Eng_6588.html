ऋतावानां निःशेदतुः साम्राज्याय सुक्रतू |धृतव्रता क्षत्रियाः क्षत्रं आशतुः ||
ṛtāvānā ni ṣedatuḥ sāmrājyāya sukratū | dhṛtavratā kṣatriyā kṣatram āśatuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ṛtāvānā ni ṣedatuḥ sāmrājyāya sukratū | dhṛtavratā kṣatriyā kṣatram āśatuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛtāvānā: One who is truthful or adheres to the principle of Rta (cosmic order).
    • ni ṣedatuḥ: May he not be obstructed or hindered.
    • sāmrājyāya: For the kingdom or sovereignty.
    • sukratū: One who performs good deeds or has good intentions.
    • dhṛtavratā: One who is steadfast in vows or commitments.
    • kṣatriyā: The warrior or ruling class.
    • kṣatram: The realm or dominion.
    • āśatuḥ: May he attain or achieve.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and righteousness in leadership.
    • The term "ṛtāvānā" signifies the necessity for leaders to embody truth and uphold cosmic order, which can be likened to modern leaders who prioritize transparency and integrity in governance.
    • "ni ṣedatuḥ" suggests that such leaders should not face obstacles in their pursuit of justice and fairness, akin to how a just legal system should operate without bias.
    • "sāmrājyāya sukratū" highlights that good intentions and actions are essential for the prosperity of a kingdom, similar to how ethical business practices lead to sustainable success in the corporate world.
    • "dhṛtavratā kṣatriyā" refers to the commitment of leaders to their duties, reminiscent of how military leaders or public servants must remain dedicated to their responsibilities for the welfare of society.
    • "kṣatram āśatuḥ" conveys the aspiration for leaders to achieve a just and prosperous realm, reflecting the goal of modern governance to create equitable societies.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a guiding principle for leaders, emphasizing the need for truthfulness, commitment, and good deeds in their roles.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various leadership contexts, including politics, business, and community service, where ethical conduct and dedication are paramount.
    • Ultimately, the mantra underscores the significance of righteous leadership in fostering a just and harmonious society, reminding us that the foundation of any successful governance lies in the integrity and intentions of its leaders.
