अहं हि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म वाजयुर आजिं यामि सदोतिभिः |त्वामिदेव तममे सम अश्वयुर गव्युर अग्रे मथीनाम् ||
ahaṁ hi te harivo brahma vājayur ājiṁ yāmi sadotibhiḥ | tvām id eva tam ame sam aśvayur gavyur agre mathīnām ||
  • ahaṁ hi te - "I am indeed yours": This phrase signifies a deep sense of belonging and devotion. In real life, this can be compared to the relationship between a student and a teacher, where the student acknowledges the teacher's guidance and wisdom.
  • harivo - "O Lord": This term is often used to address a divine figure, indicating reverence. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people address their mentors or leaders with respect.
  • brahma - "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic spirit": This represents the universal consciousness that connects all beings. In practical terms, it can be related to the idea of unity in diversity, where different cultures and beliefs coexist harmoniously.
  • vājayur - "the one who brings strength": This word emphasizes empowerment and support. For instance, a coach who motivates their team to perform better embodies this concept.
  • ājiṁ - "I go": This indicates a journey or movement towards a goal. In life, this can be likened to pursuing one's dreams or aspirations, such as a student striving to achieve academic excellence.
  • yāmi - "I go" or "I move": This reinforces the idea of progress and action. It can be seen in the context of personal development, where individuals take steps to improve themselves.
  • sadotibhiḥ - "with the good ones": This phrase highlights the importance of companionship and community. In real life, it can be related to the support systems we build, such as friendships or professional networks that uplift us.
  • tvām id eva - "you alone": This emphasizes the singular importance of the divine or the guiding force in one's life. It can be compared to how individuals often look up to a particular role model or leader for inspiration.
  • tam ame - "that which is beyond": This suggests a pursuit of higher knowledge or truth. In practical terms, it can be related to the quest for understanding one's purpose in life.
  • sam aśvayur - "the one who is like a horse": Horses symbolize strength and vitality. This can be seen in the context of athletes who embody these qualities in their pursuit of excellence.
  • gavyur - "the one who brings nourishment": This word signifies sustenance and support. In real life, this can be related to caregivers or mentors who provide guidance and resources to help others thrive.
  • agre - "in the forefront": This indicates leadership and initiative. In a corporate setting, this can be likened to a leader who takes charge and leads their team towards success.
  • mathīnām - "of the thinkers": This refers to those who engage in deep contemplation and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be seen in scholars or philosophers who contribute to society through their insights.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, empowerment, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of community and guidance in our journeys. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as education, leadership, and personal development. By recognizing our connections to others and striving for growth, we can navigate our paths with purpose and strength.
