सुप्रवाचनं तव वीर वीर्यं यद एकेन क्रतुनā विन्दसे वसु |जातूष्ठिरस्य प्र वयः सहस्वतो या चकर्थ सेंद्र विश्वास्य उक्त्यः ||
supravācanaṁ tava vīra vīrya1ṁ yad ekena kratunā vindase vasu | jātūṣṭhirasya pra vayaḥ sahasvato yā cakartha sendra viśvāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • supravācanaṁ: This term can be broken down into "su" meaning good or auspicious, and "vācanaṁ" meaning speech or words. Together, it refers to the auspicious or powerful words.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or belonging.
  • vīra: This translates to "hero" or "warrior," symbolizing strength and valor.
  • vīryaṁ: This word means "strength" or "power," often associated with the heroic qualities of a warrior.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," referring to something specific that follows.
  • ekena: This translates to "by one" or "alone," indicating singularity or unity in action.
  • kratunā: This term means "by the power of sacrifice" or "by the strength of resolve," emphasizing the importance of intention and effort.
  • vindase: This means "you obtain" or "you achieve," indicating success or attainment.
  • vasu: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," often referring to material and spiritual abundance.
  • jātūṣṭhirasya: This can be broken down into "jātu" meaning "ever" or "always," and "ṣṭhira" meaning "stable" or "firm," suggesting a sense of permanence or reliability.
  • pra vayaḥ: This means "the great age" or "the vastness of life," indicating a broad perspective on existence.
  • sahasvato: This translates to "powerful" or "mighty," emphasizing strength and capability.
  • yā: This means "who" or "which," referring to a subject that follows.
  • cakartha: This means "you have done" or "you have accomplished," indicating action and achievement.
  • sendra: This term refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing authority and divine power.
  • viśvāsy: This means "of all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and comprehensiveness.
  • ukthyaḥ: This translates to "praises" or "invocations," referring to the act of worship or reverence.

The mantra essentially speaks to the power of auspicious words and the strength of a hero. It emphasizes that through singular resolve and sacrifice, one can achieve wealth and prosperity. The invocation of Indra suggests a connection to divine authority and universal support in one's endeavors.


Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of intention, strength, and divine support in achieving success. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and community building. For instance, when embarking on a new project or goal, one can reflect on the importance of clear communication (supravācanaṁ), personal strength (vīryaṁ), and the power of collective effort (kratunā) to achieve desired outcomes. It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and support, one can attain prosperity and fulfillment in life.
