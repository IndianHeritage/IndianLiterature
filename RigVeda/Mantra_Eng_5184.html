आ यो योनिं देवकृतं ससाद क्रत्वा ह्य अग्निर अमृताम् अतारीत् |तम ओषधिश च वनिनश च गर्भं भूमिश च विश्वधायसम बिभर्ति ||
ā yo yoniṁ devakṛtaṁ sasāda kratvā hy a1gnir amṛtām̐ atārīt | tam oṣadhīś ca vaninaś ca garbham bhūmiś ca viśvadhāyasam bibharti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yo: He who
    • yoniṁ: womb or source
    • devakṛtaṁ: created by the gods
    • sasāda: has seated or established
    • kratvā: through sacrifice or effort
    • hy: indeed
    • agniḥ: fire
    • amṛtām̐: of immortality
    • atārīt: has crossed over
    • tam: that
    • oṣadhīś: lord of herbs or plants
    • ca: and
    • vaninaś: of the forest
    • ca: and
    • garbham: the womb or embryo
    • bhūmiś: earth
    • ca: and
    • viśvadhāyasam: all-encompassing
    • bibharti: sustains or holds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine source of creation, emphasizing the role of fire (Agni) as a transformative force that leads to immortality.
    • The mention of "oṣadhīś" refers to the healing and nurturing aspects of nature, highlighting the importance of plants and herbs in sustaining life.
    • The mantra also acknowledges the earth as a nurturing entity that supports all forms of life, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the role of fire can be seen in practices like controlled burns that rejuvenate the soil, promoting new growth and sustainability.
    • Herbal medicine, which utilizes the "oṣadhīś," showcases how plants are essential for health and healing, reflecting the mantra's reverence for nature.
    • The concept of sustainability in environmental practices mirrors the idea of the earth (bhūmiś) as a life-sustaining force, reminding us of our responsibility to care for the planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, transformation, and sustenance, urging us to recognize the divine forces at play in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of our connection to nature and the importance of respecting and nurturing the environment.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that promote sustainability, health, and a deeper understanding of our place within the cosmos.
