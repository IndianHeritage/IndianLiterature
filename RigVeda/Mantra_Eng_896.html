यद उदीरत आजयो धृष्णवे धीयते धनाऽ |युक्ष्वा मदच्युताः हरि कं हनः कं वसौ दधोऽस्मां इन्द्र वसौ दधः ||
yad udīrata ājayo dhṛṣṇave dhīyate dhanā | yukṣvā madacyutā harī kaṁ hanaḥ kaṁ vasau dadho 'smām̐ indra vasau dadhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • udīrata: is raised or elevated
    • ājayo: victory or success
    • dhṛṣṇave: with courage or strength
    • dhīyate: is contemplated or thought upon
    • dhanā: wealth or prosperity
    • yukṣvā: may be united or connected
    • madacyutā: intoxicated with joy or bliss
    • harī: the divine or the Lord
    • kaṁ: whom
    • hanaḥ: should be struck down or defeated
    • kaṁ: whom
    • vasau: the wealth or resources
    • dadho: bestow or grant
    • 'smām̐: to us
    • indra: the king of gods
    • vasau: wealth or resources
    • dadhaḥ: grant or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the elevation of thoughts and actions that lead to success and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of courage and contemplation in achieving one's goals.
    • The mention of divine presence suggests that success is not solely dependent on individual effort but also on divine blessings.
    • The repeated questioning of "whom" indicates a reflection on adversaries or obstacles that may hinder progress.
    • Ultimately, it calls for the bestowal of wealth and resources, highlighting the interconnectedness of effort, divine support, and material success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new product. The team must elevate their ideas (udīrata) with courage (dhṛṣṇave) and contemplate (dhīyate) the market strategies to ensure success (ājayo).
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on their fears and obstacles (kaṁ hanaḥ) that prevent them from achieving their goals, seeking divine guidance (harī) to overcome these challenges.
    • In community service, organizations may unite (yukṣvā) to address social issues, contemplating the best ways to bring prosperity (dhanā) to those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of a holistic approach to success, combining personal effort, courage, and divine support.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional endeavors, reminding us that while we strive for success, we should also seek guidance and support from higher powers.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a mindset of reflection, unity, and the pursuit of prosperity, making it a valuable mantra for anyone seeking to achieve their goals.
