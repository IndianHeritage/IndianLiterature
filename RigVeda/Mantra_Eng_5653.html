सम उ वां यज्ञं महायं नमोभिर हुवे वाम मित्रावरुणा सबाधः |प्र वाम मन्मान्य ऋचसे नवानि कृतानि ब्रह्म जुञ्जुषन्न इमानी ||
sam u vāṁ yajñam mahayaṁ namobhir huve vām mitrāvaruṇā sabādhaḥ | pra vām manmāny ṛcase navāni kṛtāni brahma jujuṣann imāni ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "I invoke you, O great Yajna (sacrifice), with my salutations. I call upon you, O Mitra and Varuna, to grant us your blessings. May our offerings be accepted, and may we be endowed with the knowledge of the sacred hymns that lead us to the ultimate truth."
  • Breakdown of Key Terms:
    • Yajna: Refers to a ritual of offerings accompanied by chanting of Vedic mantras. In real life, this can be seen in various religious ceremonies where offerings are made to deities for blessings.
    • Mitra and Varuna: These are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. In everyday life, this can symbolize the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in relationships and society.
    • Namobhir: This term signifies reverence and respect. It highlights the importance of humility and gratitude in our interactions with others.
    • Manmāny: This refers to being worthy of respect or honor. In practical terms, it emphasizes the value of earning respect through actions and integrity.
    • Rituals and Offerings: The mantra speaks to the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine. In modern life, this can be reflected in various cultural practices that foster community and spirituality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • During festivals, people perform Yajnas to seek blessings for prosperity and peace, similar to how communities come together for celebrations.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the spirit of Mitra and Varuna can remind individuals to foster trust and uphold moral values in their interactions.
    • In workplaces, the principles of respect and harmony can lead to a more productive and positive environment, akin to the ideals expressed in this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, respect, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of rituals in fostering community and spiritual growth. In real life, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide individuals in their personal, social, and professional lives, promoting harmony and a deeper understanding of the world around them.
