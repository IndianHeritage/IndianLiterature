आद इद धोतारं वृणते दिविष्टिषु भागम इव पपृचानासा ऋञ्जते |देवान यत क्रत्वा मजनना पुरुष्टुतो मर्तं शंसं विश्वधा वेति धायसे ||
ād id dhotāraṁ vṛṇate diviṣṭiṣu bhagam iva papṛcānāsa ṛñjate | devān yat kratvā majmanā puruṣṭuto martaṁ śaṁsaṁ viśvadhā veti dhāyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: Here, it signifies "thus" or "in this way."
    • id: This means "this" or "now."
    • dhotāraṁ: Refers to "the one who purifies" or "the purifier."
    • vṛṇate: Means "chooses" or "selects."
    • diviṣṭiṣu: Refers to "in the heavens" or "in the divine realms."
    • bhagam: Means "wealth" or "prosperity."
    • iva: Translates to "like" or "as if."
    • papṛcānāsa: Means "having asked" or "inquiring."
    • ṛñjate: Refers to "is revealed" or "is made known."
    • devān: Means "the gods."
    • yat: Translates to "that" or "which."
    • kratvā: Refers to "by the sacrifice" or "through the ritual."
    • majmanā: Means "of the great one" or "of the powerful."
    • puruṣṭuto: Refers to "from the man" or "from the human perspective."
    • martaṁ: Means "the mortal" or "the human."
    • śaṁsaṁ: Refers to "the auspicious" or "the good."
    • viśvadhā: Means "in all directions" or "universally."
    • veti: Translates to "knows" or "understands."
    • dhāyase: Means "you support" or "you uphold."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine process of purification and the selection of prosperity in the heavenly realms.
    • It emphasizes the importance of inquiry and understanding in the pursuit of wealth and auspiciousness.
    • The mention of gods and sacrifice highlights the interconnectedness of human actions and divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek to purify their intentions and actions to foster a prosperous environment. This can be seen in ethical business practices that prioritize transparency and integrity.
    • In personal life, individuals may engage in self-reflection and inquiry to understand their desires and motivations, leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • Communities often come together in rituals or gatherings to invoke blessings and prosperity, reflecting the collective effort to connect with the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purification, inquiry, and the divine connection in achieving prosperity and auspiciousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community rituals, emphasizing the need for ethical actions and divine support.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that our actions, guided by purity and inquiry, can lead to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
