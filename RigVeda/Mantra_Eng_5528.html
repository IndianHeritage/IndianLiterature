सम उ वो यज्ञं महायन नमोभिः प्र होताम मन्द्रो रिरिच उपाके |यजस्व सु पूर्वणिका देवान आ यज्ञियाम अरमतिं ववृत्याः ||
sam u vo yajñam mahayan namobhiḥ pra hotā mandro ririca upāke | yajasva su purvaṇīka devān ā yajñiyām aramatiṁ vavṛtyāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Together, we offer this great sacrifice, with our salutations to the divine. O priest, may you inspire us to perform the ancient rites, and may we invoke the gods with our offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: Together, in unity.
    • u: Also, indicating inclusion.
    • vo: Your, referring to the divine or the gods.
    • yajñam: Sacrifice or offering, a central theme in Vedic rituals.
    • mahayan: Great, signifying the importance of the sacrifice.
    • namobhiḥ: With salutations or reverence.
    • pra hotā: O priest, the one who invokes the gods.
    • mandro: May you inspire or guide us.
    • ririca: To perform or enact.
    • upāke: The ancient rites or rituals.
    • yajasva: May you perform or offer.
    • su: Good or auspicious.
    • purvaṇīka: Ancient or traditional.
    • devān: The gods.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • yajñiyām: In the context of sacrifice.
    • aramatiṁ: The essence or spirit of the sacrifice.
    • vavṛtyāḥ: May it flourish or grow.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, community gatherings often involve collective efforts, such as charity events or festivals, where people come together to offer their time and resources for a common cause, reflecting the essence of "sam u vo yajñam."
    • In workplaces, team-building activities can be seen as a form of yajña, where employees collaborate and contribute to a shared goal, fostering unity and cooperation.
    • During religious ceremonies, individuals often express their reverence and gratitude through offerings, similar to the invocation of the gods in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity, reverence, and the importance of collective action in spiritual and communal practices. It teaches us that when we come together with a shared purpose, we can invoke positive energies and blessings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in collaborative efforts, whether in spiritual, social, or professional contexts, ultimately leading to growth, harmony, and fulfillment.
