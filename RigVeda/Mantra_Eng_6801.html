मन्त्र: श्यावाश्वस्य सुन्वतोऽत्रीणां श्रिणुतं हवम् | इन्द्राग्नि सोमपीतये ||
śyāvāśvasya sunvato 'trīṇāṁ śṛṇutaṁ havam | indrāgnī somapītaye ||
  • śyāvāśvasya: This term refers to the "dark horses" or "black steeds." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. They are often associated with the sun and the divine.
  • sunvato: This word means "of the one who is praised" or "of the one who is invoked." It indicates a connection to the divine or the gods, suggesting that the mantra is a call for blessings or assistance.
  • 'trīṇāṁ: This translates to "three." In the context of the mantra, it may refer to the three realms of existence: the physical, the mental, and the spiritual.
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This means "listen" or "hear." It is an invitation for the divine entities to pay attention to the offerings being made.
  • havam: This term refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering." In Vedic rituals, offerings are made to the gods to seek their favor and blessings.
  • indrāgnī: This compound word refers to two deities: Indra, the king of the gods and god of rain and thunderstorms, and Agni, the fire god. Both are significant in Vedic rituals and are invoked for their power and blessings.
  • somapītaye: This translates to "for the drink of Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the divine forces represented by Indra and Agni, asking them to listen to the offerings made and to bless the worshipper with strength, vitality, and spiritual nourishment. The invocation of the "dark horses" suggests a connection to the powerful and dynamic forces of nature, while the reference to the three realms emphasizes the holistic approach to existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking Strength and Vitality: Individuals may recite this mantra during times of physical or mental challenges, invoking the energy and strength of the divine to overcome obstacles.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In personal or communal rituals, this mantra can be used to enhance the spiritual significance of offerings made to the gods, fostering a deeper connection with the divine.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance in the physical, mental, and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to pursue holistic well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance and blessings through offerings and invocations. It highlights the importance of strength, vitality, and spiritual nourishment in our lives, making it relevant in various aspects of personal and communal practices.
