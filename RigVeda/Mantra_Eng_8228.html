मन्त्र: अप्सां इन्द्राय वायवे वरुणाय मरुद्भ्यः |सोमो अर्षति विष्णवे ||
apsā indrāya vāyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | somo arṣati viṣṇave ||
  • Apsā: This term refers to the waters or the celestial waters, often associated with purity and life-giving properties. In real life, water is essential for survival, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • Indrāya: Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. This signifies strength and power. In a modern context, Indra can represent leadership and authority in various fields, such as business or governance.
  • Vāyave: This refers to Vayu, the god of wind. Wind symbolizes movement and change. In real life, it can be seen in the context of innovation and progress, where new ideas and changes drive development.
  • Varuṇāya: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical aspects of life. In contemporary society, this can be related to the importance of justice and righteousness in our actions.
  • Marudbhyaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities associated with the winds and storms. They symbolize energy and dynamism. In real life, this can be likened to the youthful energy and enthusiasm that drive change and progress in various fields.
  • Somo: Soma is a sacred drink that is associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to the quest for education and self-improvement.
  • Arṣati: This term means to shine or to rise. It signifies growth and elevation. In real life, this can be seen in personal achievements and the pursuit of excellence in various endeavors.
  • Viṣṇave: Vishnu is the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity. He represents stability and preservation. In contemporary life, this can be related to the importance of sustainability and maintaining balance in our environment and society.

Conclusion: The mantra "apsā indrāya vāyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | somo arṣati viṣṇave ||" encapsulates the essence of various natural and divine forces that govern life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of water, wind, cosmic order, and divine protection. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of balance, ethical living, and the pursuit of knowledge and strength. It encourages individuals to seek harmony with nature and to strive for personal and collective growth, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from environmental sustainability to personal development and leadership.
