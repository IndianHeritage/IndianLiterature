आ भानुना पार्थिवानि ज्रयांसी महस् तोदस्य धृतता ततान्थ। स बाधस्वाप भयाः सहोभिः स्पृधो वनुष्यन् वनुषो न जूर्व।
ā bhānunā pārthivāni jrayāṁsi mahas todasya dhṛṣatā tatantha | sa bādhasvāpa bhayā sahobhiḥ spṛdho vanuṣyan vanuṣo ni jūrva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • bhānunā: the light
    • pārthivāni: earthly
    • jrayāṁsi: the weaknesses
    • mahas: of greatness
    • todasya: of this
    • dhṛṣatā: with courage
    • tatantha: may we overcome
    • sa: he
    • bādhasvāpa: may he remove
    • bhayā: fear
    • sahobhiḥ: with strength
    • spṛdho: with desire
    • vanuṣyan: of the human
    • vanuṣo: of the beings
    • ni: not
    • jūrva: may we be bound
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about seeking the light and strength to overcome earthly weaknesses.
    • It emphasizes the importance of courage and the removal of fear, suggesting that with strength and desire, one can transcend limitations.
    • The phrase "may we not be bound" indicates a desire for freedom from constraints that hinder personal growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges and fears regarding their capabilities. By invoking the essence of this mantra, they can find the courage to take on new projects and overcome self-doubt.
    • In personal relationships, one might experience insecurities. This mantra encourages individuals to seek strength and clarity, allowing them to communicate openly and build stronger connections.
    • In times of crisis, such as during a health scare or financial difficulty, the mantra serves as a reminder to focus on inner strength and resilience, helping individuals to navigate through tough times with courage.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength and the pursuit of greatness while overcoming fears and weaknesses.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to professional challenges, encouraging individuals to embrace courage and seek liberation from limitations.
    • By reflecting on this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset that fosters resilience, empowerment, and a proactive approach to life's challenges.
