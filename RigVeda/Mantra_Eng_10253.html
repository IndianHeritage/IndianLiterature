सहस्तोमाः सहचन्दस आवृतः सहप्रमा ऋषयः सप्त दैव्याः |पूर्वेषाम् पन्थाम् अनुदृश्य धीराः अन्वालेभिरे रथ्यो न रश्मीन् ||
sahastomāḥ sahachandasa āvṛtaḥ sahapramā ṛṣayaḥ sapta daivyāḥ | pūrveṣām panthām anudṛśya dhīrā anvālebhire rathyo3 na raśmīn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahastomāḥ: Those who are endowed with strength and vigor.
    • sahachandasa: Those who are united with the moon or have a harmonious relationship.
    • āvṛtaḥ: Surrounded or enveloped.
    • sahapramā: Those who possess great knowledge or wisdom.
    • ṛṣayaḥ: The sages or seers.
    • sapta: Seven.
    • daivyāḥ: Divine or celestial.
    • pūrveṣām: Of the ancients or predecessors.
    • panthām: The path or way.
    • anudṛśya: Unseen or not visible.
    • dhīrā: The wise or the courageous.
    • anvālebhire: They have attained or grasped.
    • rathyoḥ: Of the chariots or vehicles.
    • na: Not.
    • raśmīn: Rays or beams (often referring to light).
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a group of wise and strong individuals who are united and surrounded by divine energy.
    • The mention of "sahastomāḥ" and "sahachandasa" suggests a community that thrives on strength and harmony, much like a team working together towards a common goal.
    • The "sapta daivyāḥ" indicates the presence of divine guidance, which can be interpreted as the support of higher powers in our endeavors.
    • The "pūrveṣām panthām" refers to the paths laid down by our ancestors, emphasizing the importance of tradition and learning from the past.
    • The wise individuals, "dhīrā," who have grasped the unseen paths, symbolize those who are able to navigate through life's challenges with wisdom and courage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a successful team embodies the qualities of "sahastomāḥ" and "sahachandasa" by collaborating effectively, leveraging each member's strengths, and maintaining a harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be applied to friendships where mutual support and understanding lead to growth and resilience.
    • In education, students who learn from the wisdom of their predecessors and apply it to their studies exemplify the "pūrveṣām panthām" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, unity, wisdom, and the guidance of the divine in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek knowledge from the past while navigating our paths with courage and collaboration.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or educational pursuits, the principles embodied in this mantra can lead to success and fulfillment.
