इमे राध्रं चिन् मरुतो जुनेन्ति भृमिं चिद यथा वसवो जुषन्ति |अप बाधध्वं वृषणस तमांसि धत्त विश्वं तनयं तोकम अस्मे ||
ime radhraṁ cin maruto junanti bhṛmiṁ cid yathā vasavo juṣanta | apa bādhadhvaṁ vṛṣaṇas tamāṁsi dhatta viśvaṁ tanayaṁ tokam asme ||
  • ime radhraṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "these radiant ones" or "these bright beings." It signifies the presence of divine or powerful entities.
  • cin maruto: "Maruts" refers to the storm deities or winds. "Cin" can mean "to perceive" or "to understand." This suggests a connection with the forces of nature and their awareness.
  • junanti bhṛmiṁ: "Junanti" means "to join" or "to unite," while "bhṛmiṁ" refers to the earth or the ground. This indicates a harmonious relationship between the divine forces and the earth.
  • cid yathā vasavo juṣanta: "Cid" means "consciousness" or "awareness," and "vasavo" refers to the Vasus, another group of deities associated with abundance and prosperity. "Juṣanta" means "enjoy" or "delight in." This line suggests that the divine beings delight in the consciousness of abundance.
  • apa bādhadhvaṁ: "Apa" means "away" or "remove," and "bādhadhvaṁ" means "obstacles" or "hindrances." This is a call to remove any obstacles that may hinder progress or prosperity.
  • vṛṣaṇas tamāṁsi: "Vṛṣaṇas" refers to the strong or powerful ones, and "tamāṁsi" means "darkness" or "ignorance." This line emphasizes the need to overcome ignorance and embrace strength.
  • dhatta viśvaṁ tanayaṁ: "Dhattā" means "to hold" or "to support," "viśvaṁ" refers to the universe or all beings, and "tanayaṁ" means "offspring" or "descendants." This suggests a nurturing aspect towards all beings and the universe.
  • tokam asme: "Tokam" means "prosperity" or "wealth," and "asme" means "to us." This is a plea for prosperity to come to us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and humanity. It calls for the removal of obstacles and ignorance while nurturing prosperity and abundance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal growth, community development, and environmental stewardship. By recognizing the divine presence in our lives and striving for harmony with nature, we can foster a more prosperous and enlightened existence. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek unity, overcome challenges, and embrace the abundance that life offers.
