मन्त्र: अनेहसँ वो हवमानम् ऊतये मध्वः क्षरन्ति धीतयः |आ त्वा वसो हवमानास इन्दव उप स्तोत्रेषु दधिरे ||
anehasaṁ vo havamānam ūtaye madhvaḥ kṣaranti dhītayaḥ | ā tvā vaso havamānāsa indava upa stotreṣu dadhire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anehasaṁ: Without hesitation or doubt
    • vo: To you (plural)
    • havamānam: We are invoking or offering
    • ūtaye: For the purpose of nourishment or sustenance
    • madhvaḥ: Sweetness or pleasantness
    • kṣaranti: They flow or come forth
    • dhītayaḥ: Wisdom or understanding
    • ā: To or towards
    • tvā: You
    • vaso: Of the dwelling or home
    • havamānāsa: Invoking or offering
    • indava: The divine or celestial beings
    • upa: Near or close
    • stotreṣu: In praises or hymns
    • dadhire: They have established or placed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, expressing a desire for wisdom and nourishment.
    • The phrase "anehasaṁ vo havamānam" suggests a sincere and unhesitating offering to the divine, indicating the importance of intention in spiritual practices.
    • "Madhvaḥ kṣaranti dhītayaḥ" implies that wisdom flows sweetly, akin to how knowledge enriches our lives, much like sweet water nourishes the body.
    • The invocation of "indava" signifies a connection to higher powers, emphasizing the role of divine support in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach challenges. For instance, when facing a difficult decision, one might invoke clarity and wisdom through meditation or prayer, embodying the essence of "anehasaṁ."
    • In a community setting, when people come together to support a cause, they are collectively offering their intentions and efforts, similar to the "havamānam" aspect of the mantra.
    • In educational settings, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a flow of wisdom, where students and teachers engage in a mutual exchange of ideas, resonating with "madhvaḥ kṣaranti dhītayaḥ."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of intention, community, and the pursuit of wisdom in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that our offerings—whether they be prayers, actions, or thoughts—should be made with sincerity and clarity.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger community bonds, and a deeper connection to the divine.
