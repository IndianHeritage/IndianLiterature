अस्मे इन्द्रो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः dyumnaṁ यच्छन्तु महि शर्म सप्रथः |अवध्रं ज्योतिर अदितेर ऋतावृधो देवस्य श्लोकं सवितुर्मनाामहे ||
asme indro varuṇo mitro aryamā dyumnaṁ yacchantu mahi śarma saprathaḥ | avadhraṁ jyotir aditer ṛtāvṛdho devasya ślokaṁ savitur manāmahe ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • varuṇo: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority.
  • mitro: Means "friend," suggesting the importance of companionship and support in life.
  • aryamā: A deity associated with the sun and the concept of nobility, indicating high ideals and virtues.
  • dyumnaṁ: Translates to "glory" or "fame," emphasizing the pursuit of honor and recognition.
  • yacchantu: Means "may they grant," expressing a wish or prayer for blessings.
  • mahi: Refers to "great" or "vast," often associated with the universe or the earth.
  • śarma: Means "comfort" or "shelter," highlighting the need for safety and security.
  • saprathaḥ: Translates to "together" or "in unity," emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
  • avadhraṁ: Means "unharmed" or "safe," indicating a desire for protection from harm.
  • jyotir: Refers to "light," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • aditer: Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, representing nurturing and abundance.
  • ṛtāvṛdho: Means "upholding truth," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and moral integrity.
  • devasya: Means "of the divine," indicating a connection to higher powers or spiritual forces.
  • ślokaṁ: Refers to a "verse" or "hymn," indicating the poetic and sacred nature of the mantra.
  • savituh: Refers to Savitar, the sun god, symbolizing energy, life, and creativity.
  • manāmahe: Means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a focus on spiritual practice and reflection.

This mantra is a prayer that invokes the blessings of various deities, seeking strength, friendship, truth, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of community and the pursuit of glory while also highlighting the need for safety and enlightenment. The invocation of Indra, Varuna, and Savitar suggests a holistic approach to life, where physical strength, moral integrity, and spiritual wisdom are all valued.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with integrity and compassion.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on friendship and unity encourages people to foster strong relationships and support systems within their communities.
  • Personal Growth: The pursuit of glory and enlightenment can motivate individuals to strive for excellence in their personal and professional lives.
  • Safety and Protection: The desire for safety and comfort can guide individuals to create secure environments for themselves and their loved ones.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, truth, friendship, and enlightenment. It encourages individuals to seek blessings from the divine while fostering a sense of community and moral integrity in their lives. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
