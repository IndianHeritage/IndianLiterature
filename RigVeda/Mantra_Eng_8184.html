ते विश्वा दाशुषे वसु सोमाऽ दिव्यानि पार्थिवा |पवन्ताम् आन्तरिक्ष्याः ||
te viśvā dāśuṣe vasu somā divyāni pārthivā | pavantām āntarikṣyā ||
  • te viśvā dāśuṣe: This phrase can be translated as "all these (elements) are for the one who is a giver." Here, "te" means "these," "viśvā" refers to "all" or "universal," and "dāśuṣe" means "for the giver" or "for the one who bestows." This suggests a universal principle of generosity and sharing.
  • vasu somā: "Vasu" refers to wealth or prosperity, while "somā" can be interpreted as the divine nectar or the essence of life. Together, this phrase emphasizes the importance of divine wealth and the sustenance it provides to life.
  • divyāni pārthivā: "Divyāni" means "divine" or "heavenly," and "pārthivā" refers to "earthly" or "material." This juxtaposition highlights the connection between the spiritual and the material worlds, suggesting that both are essential for a balanced life.
  • pavantām āntarikṣyā: "Pavantām" means "may they flow" or "may they be purified," and "āntarikṣyā" refers to "in the atmosphere" or "in the space." This line invokes the idea of purification and the flow of positive energies in the environment, encouraging harmony and balance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of generosity, divine wealth, and the balance between the spiritual and material realms. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, where individuals share their resources for the greater good, or in personal development, where one seeks to balance material success with spiritual growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony and the flow of positive energies, reminding us that true prosperity comes from sharing and nurturing both our inner and outer worlds.
