नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणान इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. The invocation of Indra signifies the importance of divine support and guidance in human endeavors.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This translates to "we sing your praises." It emphasizes the act of worship and the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine. In real life, this can be likened to how people often express gratitude for the blessings they receive, whether through prayer, meditation, or acts of kindness.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This can be interpreted as "the rivers that flow, do not cease." Rivers are often symbolic of life, continuity, and sustenance. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of maintaining the flow of resources and opportunities in our lives, akin to how rivers nourish the land and support life.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "I have made for you, O Hariva." Hariva can be understood as a reference to the divine or a specific aspect of the divine. This line suggests the act of creation or offering, which can be seen in real life as the efforts individuals make to contribute positively to their communities or the world at large.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new Brahman." Brahman in Vedic philosophy represents the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit. The idea of "new Brahman" can signify innovation and the continuous evolution of understanding and spirituality. In modern contexts, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of adapting to new ideas and technologies.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This can be interpreted as "may we always be endowed with wisdom." The term "rathyaḥ" refers to the chariot, which symbolizes the journey of life. This line emphasizes the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges. In real life, this can be seen in the value placed on education, mentorship, and the sharing of knowledge to guide future generations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, expressing gratitude, and the importance of wisdom in our lives. It highlights the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support, reminding us that our actions, whether in worship or daily life, contribute to a greater cosmic order. In practical terms, this mantra encourages individuals to remain grateful, to innovate, and to seek wisdom as they navigate their personal and communal journeys. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community service and environmental stewardship.
