देवीः षल उर्वीर उरु नः कृत्नोता विश्वे देवासा इह वीरयध्वम् |मा हास्महि प्रजया मा तनूभिर मा रधाम द्विषते सोम राजा ||
devīḥ ṣaḻ urvīr uru naḥ kṛṇota viśve devāsa iha vīrayadhvam | mā hāsmahi prajayā mā tanūbhir mā radhāma dviṣate soma rājan ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the goddesses grant us strength; may all the gods here bestow upon us valor. Let us not be harmed by our progeny, let us not be harmed by our bodies, let us not be harmed by the enemies, O Soma, O King."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • devīḥ: Refers to goddesses, symbolizing divine feminine energy and strength.
    • ṣaḻ urvīr: Implies six forms of strength or valor, indicating a holistic approach to empowerment.
    • uru: Means vast or abundant, suggesting that the strength we seek is not limited.
    • kṛṇota: A call to action, meaning "may they grant" or "may they bestow."
    • viśve devāsa: Refers to all gods, emphasizing the collective support of divine forces.
    • vīrayadhvam: A plea for valor, urging the divine to instill courage and strength.
    • mā hāsmahi: A request to not be harmed or diminished.
    • prajayā: Refers to progeny or offspring, highlighting the importance of family and lineage.
    • tanūbhir: Relates to bodies or physical forms, indicating a concern for physical well-being.
    • radhāma: Means to be harmed or diminished, reinforcing the plea for protection.
    • dviṣate: Refers to enemies or adversaries, acknowledging the presence of challenges.
    • soma: A sacred drink associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • rājan: Means king, symbolizing authority and leadership.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek the support of their teams (the "goddesses") to foster a culture of collaboration and strength. This mantra can be a reminder to empower each other and work collectively towards common goals.
    • In personal life, parents may recite this mantra to invoke protection and strength for their children, ensuring that they grow up in a nurturing environment free from harm.
    • During challenging times, such as facing health issues or personal crises, individuals may reflect on this mantra to seek inner strength and resilience, reminding themselves of the vast support available from the universe.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to strength and protection. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges, whether they arise from within (like family dynamics or personal struggles) or from external sources (like adversaries). In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal development to community building, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles and that collective strength can lead to empowerment and resilience.
