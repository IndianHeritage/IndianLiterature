तं देवाः बुध्ने रजसः सुदंससं दिवस्पृथिव्योऽरतीं न्य एरिरे | रथमिव वेद्यम् शुक्रशोचिषम् अग्निम् मित्रं न क्षितिषु प्रशंस्यम् ||
taṁ devā budhne rajasaḥ sudaṁsasaṁ divaspṛthivyor aratiṁ ny erire | ratham iva vedyaṁ śukraśociṣam agnim mitraṁ na kṣitiṣu praśaṁsyam ||
  • taṁ devā - This phrase refers to "that divine" or "those deities." It signifies the presence of divine beings or forces that govern the universe.
  • budhne - This word means "to awaken" or "to enlighten." It suggests the idea of gaining knowledge or insight.
  • rajasaḥ - This term relates to "passion" or "activity." In the context of the mantra, it indicates the dynamic forces of nature and life.
  • sudaṁsasaṁ - This translates to "well-formed" or "beautiful." It emphasizes the aesthetic and harmonious aspects of creation.
  • divaspṛthivyor - This phrase means "of the heavens and the earth." It highlights the connection between the celestial and terrestrial realms.
  • aratiṁ - This word means "discontent" or "restlessness." It points to the human condition of seeking fulfillment and peace.
  • ny erire - This translates to "they have removed" or "they have dispelled." It suggests the act of overcoming obstacles or negativity.
  • ratham iva - This means "like a chariot." The chariot symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • vedyaṁ - This word means "to be known" or "to be understood." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • śukraśociṣam - This translates to "bright and shining." It refers to purity, clarity, and enlightenment.
  • agnim - This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine presence.
  • mitraṁ - This translates to "friend." It signifies the importance of relationships and harmony among beings.
  • na kṣitiṣu - This means "not on the earth." It suggests a transcendence beyond the material world.
  • praśaṁsyam - This word means "to be praised." It indicates the value of recognizing and honoring the divine and the good in life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking enlightenment and harmony in life. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, the pursuit of knowledge, and the need to overcome discontent. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, relationships, and the quest for a deeper understanding of existence. By recognizing the divine in ourselves and others, we can foster a sense of community and support, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, embrace beauty, and cultivate friendships, ultimately guiding us towards a more enlightened and peaceful existence.
