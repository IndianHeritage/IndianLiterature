इन्द्राय गावा आशिरं दुदुहरे वज्रीणि मधु |यत सीम उपह्वरे विदत ||
indrāya gāva āśiraṁ duduhre vajriṇe madhu | yat sīm upahvare vidat ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Gāva: This translates to "cows," which in Vedic culture are considered sacred and a symbol of wealth, nourishment, and abundance.
  • Āśiraṁ: This means "blessings" or "prosperity." It signifies the positive outcomes and benefits that one seeks in life.
  • Duduhre: This term means "to milk" or "to extract." It implies the act of drawing out the essence or benefits from something.
  • Vajriṇe: This refers to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," another epithet for Indra, emphasizing his strength and ability to protect and provide.
  • Madhu: This means "honey," symbolizing sweetness, nourishment, and the rewards of hard work.
  • Yat: This translates to "that which" or "whatever," indicating a connection to the preceding elements.
  • Sīm: This means "boundary" or "limit," suggesting the idea of protection and security.
  • Upahvare: This means "to offer" or "to present," indicating an act of devotion or giving.
  • Vidat: This translates to "knowledge" or "understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in achieving one's goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for blessings, prosperity, and the ability to extract the best from life. It highlights the importance of strength, nourishment, and wisdom in overcoming challenges and achieving success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Strength: Just as Indra is a symbol of strength, individuals can invoke this mantra when facing challenges, whether in personal life, career, or health, to gain the courage to overcome obstacles.
  • Desiring Prosperity: The reference to cows and honey signifies the desire for abundance. This can be relevant for entrepreneurs seeking success in their ventures or individuals looking to improve their financial situation.
  • Emphasizing Wisdom: The importance of knowledge in the mantra reminds us to seek wisdom in our decisions, whether in education, relationships, or career choices.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for strength, prosperity, and wisdom. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate their lives more effectively, ensuring a balanced and fulfilling existence. It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and support, one can achieve their goals and live a life of abundance and joy.
