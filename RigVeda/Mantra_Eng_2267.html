इमं स्तोमं सक्रतवो मे अद्य मित्रो आर्यमा वरुणो जुषन्ता |आदित्याशः शुचयो धारपूताः अवृजिना अनवद्याः अरिष्टाः ||
imaṁ stomaṁ sakratavo me adya mitro aryamā varuṇo juṣanta | ādityāsaḥ śucayo dhārapūtā avṛjinā anavadyā ariṣṭāḥ ||
  • imaṁ stomaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "this hymn" or "this praise." It signifies the act of offering a prayer or a hymn to the divine.
  • sakratavo me: This means "may they be gracious to me." It expresses a desire for the divine beings to bestow their blessings and grace upon the speaker.
  • adya: Translated as "today," it emphasizes the immediacy and urgency of the prayer, indicating that the speaker seeks divine intervention in the present moment.
  • mitro: Referring to "Mitra," a deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the sun. This highlights the importance of relationships and social bonds in life.
  • aryamā: This refers to "Aryama," a deity linked to the afterlife and the protection of the righteous. It signifies the hope for guidance and protection in one's journey.
  • varuṇo: This is "Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna represents the moral and ethical order of the universe, reminding us of the importance of righteousness.
  • juṣanta: Meaning "who delight," it indicates that the deities are pleased with the offerings and prayers made by the devotee.
  • ādityāsaḥ: Referring to the "Adityas," the solar deities, symbolizing light, truth, and knowledge. This emphasizes the pursuit of enlightenment and wisdom.
  • śucayo: This means "pure" or "clean," suggesting the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • dhārapūtā: Translated as "those who uphold," it signifies the strength and support provided by the divine in maintaining order and balance in life.
  • avṛjinā: Meaning "without sin," it emphasizes the aspiration to lead a life free from wrongdoing and negativity.
  • anavadyā: This translates to "without blemish," indicating the desire for purity and perfection in one's character and actions.
  • ariṣṭāḥ: Meaning "auspicious" or "beneficial," it signifies the hope for positive outcomes and blessings in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a deep yearning for divine grace, protection, and purity in one's life. It emphasizes the importance of relationships, moral order, and the pursuit of knowledge and righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, seeking guidance in relationships, or striving for ethical living. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can aspire to lead a life that is harmonious, pure, and aligned with higher principles, ultimately contributing to their personal growth and the well-being of society as a whole.
