त्वं ह्येक ईशिष इन्द्र वाजस्य गोमतः | स नो यन्धि महीमिषम् ||
tvaṁ hy eka īśiṣa indra vājasya gomataḥ | sa no yandhi mahīm iṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hy: indeed
    • eka: one
    • īśiṣa: the supreme ruler
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • vājasya: of strength or power
    • gomataḥ: of the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • sa: may he
    • no: us
    • yandhi: lead or guide
    • mahīm: to greatness or vastness
    • iṣam: the goal or purpose
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra addresses Indra, the supreme ruler and king of the gods, acknowledging his power and strength. It expresses a desire for guidance towards greatness and fulfillment of purpose. The mention of "gomataḥ" (the cow) symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and prosperity, indicating that the mantra seeks blessings for abundance and strength in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked by entrepreneurs seeking guidance and strength to achieve their goals and lead their ventures to success.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of uncertainty, asking for divine guidance to navigate challenges and reach their aspirations.
    • In agricultural communities, the reference to the cow can resonate deeply, as cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance. Farmers may use this mantra to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and guidance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking higher powers for support in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, whether in business, personal growth, or community welfare, this mantra encourages individuals to aspire for greatness and to trust in the guidance of a higher force. By acknowledging the divine and seeking blessings, one can cultivate a mindset of abundance and purpose, leading to a more fulfilling life.
