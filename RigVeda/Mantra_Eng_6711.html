मन्त्र: सूर्यो रश्मिं यथा सृजा त्वा यच्छन्तु मे गिरः |निम्नम आपो न साध्र्यक ||
sūryo raśmiṁ yathā sṛjā tvā yacchantu me giraḥ | nimnam āpo na sadhryak ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryo: Sun
    • raśmiṁ: Rays
    • yathā: As
    • sṛjā: Created
    • tvā: You
    • yacchantu: May they offer
    • me: To me
    • giraḥ: Words
    • nimnam: Low
    • āpo: Waters
    • na: Not
    • sadhryak: Be disturbed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Sun, symbolizing light, knowledge, and energy.
    • The phrase "as the rays of the sun are created" suggests a connection between divine energy and the speaker's own existence.
    • The request for words to be offered to the speaker indicates a desire for wisdom and guidance.
    • The mention of "low waters" signifies humility and the importance of remaining grounded, while "not be disturbed" emphasizes stability and peace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate to the Sun as a source of inspiration and motivation. Just as the sun rises each day, individuals can seek to rise above challenges with renewed energy.
    • The idea of offering words can be seen in the context of communication. In relationships, offering kind and wise words can strengthen bonds and foster understanding.
    • Staying grounded, like the "low waters," can be applied in stressful situations. Practicing mindfulness and humility can help individuals maintain their composure and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom while remaining humble and grounded.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, communication in relationships, and maintaining mental stability in challenging times.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a balanced and enlightened approach to life.
