अग्निं विश्वायुवेपसन् मर्यं न वाजिनं हितम् |सप्तिं न वाजयामसि ||
agniṁ viśvāyuvepasam maryaṁ na vājinaṁ hitam | saptiṁ na vājayāmasi ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Viśvāyuvepasam: This term can be broken down into "viśvā" meaning universal or all-encompassing, and "ayu" meaning life or vitality. "Vepasam" suggests movement or action. Together, it implies the universal life force that is dynamic and active.
  • Maryaṁ: This word refers to a hero or a noble person, often associated with valor and strength.
  • Na: A negation, meaning "not."
  • Vājinaṁ: This term means "the one who possesses strength or power," often associated with wealth and prosperity.
  • Hitam: Meaning "beneficial" or "good," indicating something that is favorable or advantageous.
  • Saptiṁ: This word refers to "seven," which can symbolize completeness or wholeness in various contexts.
  • Na vājayāmasi: This phrase translates to "we do not strive for strength or power," indicating a rejection of mere materialistic pursuits.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, asking for the universal life force to bless the noble and heroic qualities within us, while also emphasizing that we do not seek power for its own sake. Instead, we aim for a holistic and beneficial existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: It encourages individuals to focus on inner strength and virtues rather than external power or wealth. For example, a leader may prioritize integrity and service over mere authority.
  • Community Building: In a community context, it can inspire collective efforts towards the greater good, emphasizing cooperation and shared values rather than competition for resources.
  • Spiritual Growth: On a spiritual level, it serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and personal growth through selfless actions and a connection to the universal life force.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a balanced approach to life, where one seeks to embody noble qualities and contribute positively to the world, rather than merely pursuing power or material gain. It serves as a guiding principle for personal and communal harmony, encouraging individuals to align their actions with higher values and the greater good.
