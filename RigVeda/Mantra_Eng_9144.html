मन्त्र: त्रिपञ्चाशः क्रीळति व्रात एषां देव इव सविता सत्यधर्मा | उग्रस्य चिन् मन्यवे न नमन्ते राजा चिद एभ्यो नम इति कृत्नोति ||
tripañcāśaḥ krīḻati vrāta eṣāṁ deva iva savitā satyadharmā | ugrasya cin manyave nā namante rājā cid ebhyo nama it kṛṇoti ||
  • Tripañcāśaḥ: This term refers to the number fifty-three, which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle in various contexts.
  • Krīḻati: This word means "to play" or "to engage in activity." It suggests a dynamic and lively interaction with the world.
  • Vrāta: This term can be interpreted as a "group" or "community," often referring to a collective of individuals united by a common purpose or belief.
  • Eṣāṁ: This means "of these" or "of this," indicating a specific focus on the community or group being referred to.
  • Deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being," representing higher powers or spiritual entities that guide and influence human affairs.
  • Savitā: This term refers to the sun or solar deity, symbolizing illumination, life, and energy.
  • Satyadharmā: This means "truth and righteousness," emphasizing the importance of living a life aligned with truth and moral principles.
  • Ugrasya: This word can be interpreted as "of the fierce" or "powerful," suggesting strength and intensity.
  • Cin manyave: This phrase means "in the thought of the mind," indicating the importance of intention and mental focus in actions.
  • Nā namante: This translates to "do not bow down," suggesting a stance of strength and self-respect.
  • Rājā: This means "king" or "ruler," representing authority and leadership.
  • Cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the significance of being aware and mindful in one's actions.
  • Ebhyō: This means "to these" or "to them," indicating a connection to the community or group mentioned earlier.
  • Nama: This translates to "salutation" or "obeisance," representing respect and acknowledgment.
  • Kṛṇoti: This means "to make" or "to do," indicating the act of creating or establishing something.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of community, truth, and righteous action. It suggests that individuals should engage actively with their surroundings while maintaining a strong sense of self and respect for their community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, where collaboration and mutual respect are essential for success. It also highlights the significance of intention and awareness in our actions, encouraging individuals to act with purpose and integrity. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to live a life that is not only fulfilling personally but also beneficial to the community at large.
