अस्तेवा सु प्रातरं लायम अस्यन् भूषन्न इव प्र भरā स्तोमम् अस्मै |वाचां विप्रास तरत वाचम् आर्यो नि रामय जरितः सोम इन्द्रम् ||
asteva su prataraṁ lāyam asyan bhūṣann iva pra bharā stomam asmai | vācā viprās tarata vācam aryo ni rāmaya jaritaḥ soma indram ||
  • asteva su prataraṁ lāyam: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, the one who is well-established and prosperous." It emphasizes the importance of stability and prosperity in life.
  • asyan bhūṣann iva pra bharā stomam asmai: This translates to "Like a jewel adorning, let us offer our praises to him." Here, the metaphor of a jewel signifies the value of devotion and respect towards a higher power or authority.
  • vācā viprās tarata vācam: This means "The wise ones, through their words, navigate the path." It highlights the power of speech and wisdom in guiding individuals through life's challenges.
  • aryo ni rāmaya jaritaḥ soma indram: This can be understood as "May the noble ones, through their actions, invoke the strength of the divine." It suggests that noble actions can attract divine blessings and strength.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of stability, respect, wisdom, and divine strength. It serves as a reminder of the importance of being grounded in one's values while also seeking higher guidance.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In professional settings, being well-established and respected can lead to better opportunities and success.
    • In personal relationships, showing respect and admiration can strengthen bonds and create a supportive environment.
    • In decision-making, wise words and thoughtful communication can help navigate complex situations effectively.
    • In community service, noble actions can inspire others and invoke a sense of collective strength and purpose.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals seeking stability, wisdom, and divine support in their lives. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, one can cultivate a life of purpose, respect, and strength, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
