तुभ्यं अग्रे पर्य अवहन सूर्यां वहतुनā सह |पुनः पतिभ्यः जायाṁ दा अग्ने प्रजया सह ||
tubhyam agre pary avahan sūryāṁ vahatunā saha | punaḥ patibhyo jāyāṁ dā agne prajayā saha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyam: to you
    • agre: in front, ahead
    • pary: around, surrounding
    • avahan: may bring, may carry
    • sūryāṁ: the sun
    • vahatunā: with the one who carries
    • saha: together, with
    • punaḥ: again, once more
    • patibhyo: to the spouses, partners
    • jāyāṁ: the wife, the female partner
    • dā: give, grant
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • prajayā: with progeny, offspring
    • saha: together, with
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for blessings and prosperity.
    • The invocation of the sun symbolizes enlightenment, energy, and life force.
    • The phrase "may bring the sun" suggests a desire for illumination and guidance in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of partnership and family, as it calls for blessings for spouses and offspring.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, couples often seek blessings for a prosperous and harmonious life together, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • In daily life, individuals may seek guidance and clarity in their decisions, akin to asking for the sun's light to illuminate their path.
    • Parents often pray for the well-being and success of their children, reflecting the desire for progeny and family unity expressed in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in personal relationships and family life.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of individuals, their partners, and the divine, emphasizing the need for support and unity.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, and moments of reflection, reminding individuals to seek light, love, and prosperity in their lives.
