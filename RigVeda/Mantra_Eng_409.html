आ नासत्याः त्रिभिर एकादशैर इह देवैभिर यातं मधुपेयम् अश्विनाः | प्रायुः तारीषṭं नी रपांसि मृक्षतं सेधतं द्वेषो भवतं सचाभुवाः ||
ā nāsatyā tribhir ekādaśair iha devebhir yātam madhupeyam aśvinā | prāyus tāriṣṭaṁ nī rapāṁsi mṛkṣataṁ sedhataṁ dveṣo bhavataṁ sacābhuvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā nāsatyā: O Aśvins, the divine twins.
    • tribhir: with three.
    • ekādaśair: eleven.
    • iha: here.
    • devebhir: with the gods.
    • yātam: we have come.
    • madhupeyam: sweet like honey.
    • aśvinā: O Aśvins.
    • prāyus: may we attain.
    • tāriṣṭaṁ: the best.
    • nī rapāṁsi: the waters.
    • mṛkṣataṁ: may we be protected.
    • sedhataṁ: may we be united.
    • dveṣo: enmity.
    • bhavataṁ: may it not be.
    • sacābhuvā: with abundance.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Aśvins, the divine twin gods associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • The phrase "ā nāsatyā" invokes the Aśvins, asking for their blessings and guidance.
    • The mention of "tribhir ekādaśair" suggests a connection to the number eleven, which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle in various cultures.
    • "Iha devebhir yātam" indicates a collective journey or aspiration towards divine qualities, emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
    • "Madhupeyam" signifies the sweetness of life and experiences, akin to the joy we find in relationships and achievements.
    • The plea for protection and unity in "nī rapāṁsi mṛkṣataṁ sedhataṁ" highlights the human desire for safety and togetherness in a world often filled with challenges.
    • Finally, the mantra seeks to eliminate "dveṣo bhavataṁ," or enmity, promoting peace and harmony among individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration, encouraging employees to work together harmoniously towards common goals.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the essence of this mantra can help resolve conflicts and promote understanding, leading to stronger bonds.
    • In community gatherings, the principles of unity and protection can guide discussions and actions aimed at creating a supportive environment for all members.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine guidance, unity, and the pursuit of sweetness in life.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to seek harmony, protect one another, and eliminate negativity.
    • By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, we can create a more compassionate and connected world.
