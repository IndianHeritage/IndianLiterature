आयने ते परायणं दूर्वा रोहन्तु पुष्पिणीः | ह्रदाश च पुंडरीकाणि समुद्रस्य गृहाः इमे ||
āyane te parāyaṇe dūrvā rohantu puṣpiṇīḥ | hradāś ca puṇḍarīkāṇi samudrasya gṛhā ime ||
  • Mantra: āyane te parāyaṇe dūrvā rohantu puṣpiṇīḥ | hradāś ca puṇḍarīkāṇi samudrasya gṛhā ime ||
  • Translation: "May the sacred grass (dūrvā) flourish in your path; may the lotus flowers (puṇḍarīkāṇi) of the lakes be your home, O ocean."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • āyane: In the context of this mantra, it refers to the path or journey.
    • te: This means "your," indicating possession or connection.
    • parāyaṇe: This term signifies a destination or a place of refuge.
    • dūrvā: A type of sacred grass, often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and auspiciousness.
    • rohantu: This means "may they flourish" or "may they grow," indicating a wish for prosperity.
    • puṣpiṇīḥ: Refers to flowers, particularly those that bloom beautifully.
    • hradāś: This means "lakes" or "water bodies," symbolizing abundance and life.
    • puṇḍarīkāṇi: This refers specifically to lotus flowers, which are often associated with beauty and divinity.
    • samudrasya: This means "of the ocean," indicating vastness and depth.
    • gṛhā: This means "homes" or "abodes," suggesting a place of comfort and safety.
    • ime: This means "these," referring to the elements mentioned earlier.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal journey, one may seek blessings for growth and prosperity in their career, akin to the flourishing of dūrvā grass along their path.
    • In a community setting, the mantra can be invoked during festivals to bless the gathering with beauty and abundance, much like the blooming of lotus flowers in lakes.
    • In environmental contexts, this mantra can inspire efforts to protect water bodies, ensuring that they remain vibrant and full of life, similar to the imagery of the ocean's home.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of growth, beauty, and abundance in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek purity and prosperity in our journeys, both personal and communal.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in rituals, community gatherings, and environmental conservation efforts, promoting a harmonious relationship with nature and each other.
