मन्तर: नराद दंसीष्ठाव अतृये शुभ्रा सिषासतम् धियः | अथाहि वां दिवो नराः पुनः स्तोमो न विशसे ||
narā daṁsiṣṭhāv atraye śubhrā siṣāsataṁ dhiyaḥ | athā hi vāṁ divo narā punaḥ stomo na viśase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • narā: men or humans
    • daṁsiṣṭhāv: those who are the best or most excellent
    • atraye: in this context, it refers to the sage or seer
    • śubhrā: pure or auspicious
    • siṣāsataṁ: those who are endowed with wisdom or knowledge
    • dhiyaḥ: intellect or understanding
    • athā: therefore or thus
    • hi: indeed
    • vāṁ: your
    • divo: of the heavens or divine
    • punaḥ: again or once more
    • stomo: praise or hymn
    • na: not
    • viśase: to be doubted or questioned
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of human excellence and wisdom.
    • It suggests that those who are pure and knowledgeable are worthy of praise and recognition.
    • The phrase "athā hi vāṁ divo narā" indicates that the divine or heavenly qualities are reflected in humans who possess these virtues.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for excellence and wisdom, as these qualities are essential for spiritual and personal growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, employees who demonstrate excellence in their work and possess a strong understanding of their field often become leaders and are respected by their peers.
    • In education, students who seek knowledge and strive for academic excellence are often recognized and rewarded, serving as role models for others.
    • In community service, individuals who dedicate their time and resources to help others embody the qualities of wisdom and purity, inspiring others to follow suit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of striving for excellence and wisdom in our lives.
    • It highlights the connection between human qualities and divine attributes, suggesting that by embodying these virtues, we can elevate ourselves and contribute positively to society.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more harmonious community.
