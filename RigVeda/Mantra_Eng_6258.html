प्र बोधयोषो अश्विनाऽ प्र देवी सूनृते महि |प्र यज्ञहोतार आन्नुषक प्र मदाय श्रवो बृहत् ||
pra bodhayoṣo aśvinā pra devi sūnṛte mahi | pra yajñahotar ānuṣak pra madāya śravo bṛhat ||
  • pra bodhayoṣo aśvinā: This phrase can be translated as "May the Ashvins awaken us." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the awakening of consciousness and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • pra devi sūnṛte mahi: This translates to "May the goddess grant us great truth." Here, the term "Devi" refers to the divine feminine energy, representing wisdom and nurturing. The phrase emphasizes the importance of seeking truth and understanding in our lives.
  • pra yajñahotar ānuṣak: This means "May we be connected to the ritual and its officiant." Yajna refers to the Vedic rituals of sacrifice and offerings, which are essential for maintaining harmony in the universe. This line highlights the significance of participating in communal activities and rituals that foster unity and connection.
  • pra madāya śravo bṛhat: This translates to "May we attain the great fame." The word "madāya" signifies the joy or intoxication that comes from achieving something significant. This line encourages individuals to strive for greatness and recognition through their actions and contributions to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about awakening, truth, connection, and the pursuit of greatness. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and wisdom, engage in community rituals that promote unity, and strive for personal and collective achievements. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and enlightened society, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment in their lives.
