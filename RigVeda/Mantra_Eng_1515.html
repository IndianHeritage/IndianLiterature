यद ध क्राणā विवस्वति नाभा संदायि नव्यसी |अध प्र सू न उप यन्तु धीतयो देवाम् अच् छा न धीतयः ||
yad dha krāṇā vivasvati nābhā saṁdāyi navyasī | adha pra sū na upa yantu dhītayo devām̐ acchā na dhītayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • dha: is placed or established
    • krāṇā: in the context of creation or action
    • vivasvati: related to the sun or solar energy
    • nābhā: the center or source
    • saṁdāyi: to be gathered or assembled
    • navyasī: new or fresh
    • adha: below or beneath
    • pra: forth or forward
    • sū: to bring forth or produce
    • na: not
    • upa: near or close
    • yantu: may they go or proceed
    • dhītayo: thoughts or intellects
    • devām̐: divine or godly
    • acchā: good or auspicious
    • na: not
    • dhītayaḥ: thoughts or intellects (plural)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the establishment of new thoughts and ideas that are aligned with divine wisdom.
    • The reference to the sun (vivasvati) symbolizes enlightenment, clarity, and the source of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of gathering fresh, constructive thoughts that lead to positive outcomes.
    • The phrase "adha pra sū" suggests that these thoughts should not be limited or constrained but should be allowed to flourish and grow.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance in their intellectual pursuits and decision-making processes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams are brainstorming new ideas. Encouraging an open environment where fresh thoughts are welcomed can lead to innovative solutions.
    • In education, teachers can use this mantra to inspire students to think creatively and not be afraid to express new ideas, fostering a culture of learning and exploration.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on this mantra to seek clarity and guidance in their life choices, ensuring that their thoughts align with their higher purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing new, constructive thoughts that are aligned with divine wisdom.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and clarity in our intellectual endeavors, allowing us to make informed and auspicious decisions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, innovation in various fields, and a deeper connection with our inner wisdom and the divine.
