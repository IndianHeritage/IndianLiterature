यतā सुजुर्णी रातिनī घृताचī प्रदक्षिणिद देवतातिम उराणः |उद उ स्वरुर नवजā नाक्रः पश्वो अनक्ति सुधितः सुमेकः ||
yatā sujūrṇī rātinī ghṛtācī pradakṣiṇid devatātim urāṇaḥ | ud u svarur navajā nākraḥ paśvo anakti sudhitaḥ sumekaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yatā sujūrṇī rātinī ghṛtācī pradakṣiṇid devatātim urāṇaḥ | ud u svarur navajā nākraḥ paśvo anakti sudhitaḥ sumekaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatā: as, like
    • sujūrṇī: well-prepared, well-formed
    • rātinī: the one who brings joy or happiness
    • ghṛtācī: with ghee, or clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment and purity
    • pradakṣiṇid: circumambulating, moving around
    • devatātim: divine, pertaining to the gods
    • urāṇaḥ: the one who is great or vast
    • ud: up, above
    • svarur: the sound, the voice
    • navajā: newly born, fresh
    • nākraḥ: not to be neglected
    • paśvo: of the animals, creatures
    • anakti: does not harm
    • sudhitaḥ: well-thought-out, wise
    • sumekaḥ: well-formed, complete
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of being well-prepared and joyful in our actions.
    • The reference to "ghṛtācī" emphasizes the significance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that our actions should be pure and beneficial.
    • The act of "pradakṣiṇid" or circumambulating indicates respect and reverence towards the divine, reminding us to honor the forces that guide our lives.
    • The phrase "ud u svarur" suggests that our voice and intentions should rise above negativity, promoting positivity and clarity in communication.
    • "navajā" signifies the freshness of new beginnings, encouraging us to embrace change and renewal in our lives.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of being wise and thoughtful in our actions, as indicated by "sudhitaḥ".
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire employees to approach their tasks with joy and thorough preparation, leading to a more productive environment.
    • In personal relationships, embodying the qualities of nourishment and respect can strengthen bonds and foster a supportive atmosphere.
    • During significant life changes, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city, embracing the idea of "navajā" can help individuals adapt and thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of joy, preparation, and respect in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to living, where our actions are guided by wisdom and positivity.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper connection with the divine.
