स नो वृषन् सनीष्ठया सं घोरया द्रवित्न्वा |धियाविड्धि पुरंढ्या ||
sa no vṛṣan saniṣṭhayā saṁ ghorayā dravitnvā | dhiyāviḍḍhi puraṁdhyā ||
  • sa no vṛṣan saniṣṭhayā: This phrase can be translated as "May the strong one (vṛṣan) protect us with his steadfastness." Here, "vṛṣan" refers to a powerful entity, often interpreted as a deity or a force of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the protective qualities of a guardian or a leader who stands firm in the face of challenges.
  • saṁ ghorayā dravitnvā: This part means "with the fierce (ghorayā) and abundant (dravitnvā) energy." The term "ghorayā" suggests intensity or ferocity, while "dravitnvā" implies richness or abundance. In practical terms, this can be seen in situations where individuals or communities harness their fierce determination and resources to overcome obstacles, such as during natural disasters when communities come together to support each other.
  • dhiyāviḍḍhi puraṁdhyā: This translates to "may our intellect (dhiyā) be guided (viḍḍhi) by the supreme (puraṁdhyā)." The word "puraṁdhyā" often refers to a supreme being or higher wisdom. In everyday life, this can be related to seeking guidance from mentors, spiritual leaders, or even through education and knowledge to make informed decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and guidance in our lives. It signifies the need for a strong foundation and support system, whether it be from a higher power, community, or personal resilience. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community efforts, or when seeking wisdom in decision-making. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance in our endeavors, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in overcoming life's difficulties.
