स वह्निः पुत्रः pitroḥ पवित्रवान् पुनाति धीरः भुवनानि मायया |धेनुं च पृष्णिं वृषभं सुरेतसं विश्वाहाः शुक्रं पयो अस्य दुक्षत ||
sa vahniḥ putraḥ pitroḥ pavitravān punāti dhīro bhuvanāni māyayā | dhenuṁ ca pṛśniṁ vṛṣabhaṁ suretasaṁ viśvāhā śukram payo asya dukṣata ||
  • sa vahniḥ putraḥ - "He is the son of fire": This phrase signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly. In real life, this can be seen in the way children inherit qualities from their parents, much like how fire symbolizes transformation and energy.
  • pitroḥ pavitravān - "purified by the ancestors": This emphasizes the importance of lineage and the blessings of ancestors. In many cultures, honoring one's ancestors is a way to seek guidance and blessings, reflecting the idea that our actions are influenced by those who came before us.
  • punāti dhīro bhuvanāni māyayā - "the wise purifies the worlds through illusion": This suggests that wisdom has the power to transform and purify the world, even amidst the illusions of life. For example, a wise leader can inspire change and uplift society, demonstrating how knowledge can lead to positive outcomes.
  • dhenuṁ ca pṛśniṁ - "the cow and the earth": Cows are often seen as symbols of nourishment and abundance, while the earth represents stability and sustenance. In agriculture, the relationship between livestock and land is crucial for food production, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ suretasaṁ - "the bull of the divine": The bull symbolizes strength and fertility. In real life, this can be related to the importance of hard work and perseverance in achieving success, much like how farmers rely on strong animals for labor.
  • viśvāhā śukram payo asya dukṣata - "all beings are nourished by this pure milk": This line emphasizes the idea of nourishment and sustenance provided by the divine. In a broader sense, it can be related to how communities come together to support one another, sharing resources and knowledge for collective growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of connection between the divine, ancestors, and the earthly realm. It highlights the significance of wisdom, lineage, and the nurturing aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, these themes can be observed in family dynamics, community support, and the importance of knowledge in fostering growth and transformation. The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards our heritage and the impact of our actions on the world around us.
