मन्त्र: हिरण्यकेशो राजसो विसारे 'हिर धुनिर वाता इव ध्रजीमान् |शुचिभ्राजाऽ उषसो नवेद यशस्वतीर अपस्युवो न सत्याः ||
hiraṇyakeśo rajaso visāre 'hir dhunir vāta iva dhrajīmān | śucibhrājā uṣaso navedā yaśasvatīr apasyuvo na satyāḥ ||
  • hiraṇyakeśo: This term translates to "the golden-haired one," often referring to the sun or a divine figure associated with light and prosperity.
  • rajaso: This word means "of the realm of passion" or "of the material world," indicating the dynamic and active aspects of life.
  • visāre: This can be interpreted as "in the vastness" or "in the expanse," suggesting a sense of infinity or boundless nature.
  • 'hir: This is an exclamatory particle that can denote emphasis or importance.
  • dhanir: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it signifies abundance and the richness of life.
  • vāta: This translates to "wind," symbolizing movement, change, and the unseen forces of nature.
  • iva: This word means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • dhrajīmān: This term refers to "the one who is steadfast" or "the one who holds firm," indicating stability and strength.
  • śucibhrājā: This means "the one who shines with purity," often associated with clarity and enlightenment.
  • uṣaso: This translates to "of the dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • navedā: This can be interpreted as "new knowledge" or "fresh insights," indicating the importance of learning and growth.
  • yaśasvatīr: This means "those who are glorious" or "those who have fame," referring to individuals who achieve greatness.
  • apasyuvo: This translates to "those who do not see" or "those who are blind," often used metaphorically for ignorance or lack of awareness.
  • na satyāḥ: This means "not true" or "not real," indicating falsehood or deception.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the interplay between light and darkness, knowledge and ignorance, and the importance of steadfastness and purity in the pursuit of prosperity and glory. It emphasizes the need for awareness and the rejection of falsehood in order to achieve true success and enlightenment.


Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and truth while remaining grounded and steadfast in one's values. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and ethical decision-making. For instance, in a corporate setting, a leader who embodies these qualities can inspire their team to pursue innovation while maintaining integrity and transparency.
