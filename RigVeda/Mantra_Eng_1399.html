उषा उचन्ती समिधाने अग्ना उद्यान सूर्य उर्विय ज्योतिर अश्रेत् |देवो नो अत्र सविता nv अर्थं प्रासवीद द्विपत् प्र चतुष्पद इत्यै ||
uṣā ucchantī samidhāne agnā udyan sūrya urviyā jyotir aśret | devo no atra savitā nv artham prāsāvīd dvipat pra catuṣpad ityai ||
  • uṣā ucchantī: This phrase refers to the dawn or the arrival of light. In a metaphorical sense, it symbolizes new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and figuratively.
  • samidhāne: This term means 'in the act of kindling' or 'igniting'. It can be related to the idea of starting something new, such as a project or a relationship.
  • agnā: This word translates to 'fire', which is often associated with energy, transformation, and purification. Fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve goals.
  • udyān: This means 'to rise' or 'to ascend'. It signifies growth and progress, akin to how plants grow towards the sun.
  • sūrya: This is the sun, a powerful symbol of life, energy, and vitality. The sun is essential for growth and sustenance in nature.
  • urviyā: This term means 'wide' or 'broad', indicating expansiveness and abundance. It can refer to opportunities that are available to us.
  • jyotir: This translates to 'light' or 'radiance', symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • aśret: This word means 'to dwell' or 'to reside', suggesting a sense of stability and permanence in the presence of light and knowledge.
  • devo no atra savitā: This phrase means 'may the divine Savitar (the sun god) be with us'. It invokes the blessings of the divine for guidance and support.
  • nv artham: This translates to 'for our purpose' or 'for our benefit', indicating a desire for divine assistance in achieving our goals.
  • prāsāvīd: This term means 'to give birth' or 'to create', symbolizing the act of bringing forth new ideas, projects, or life itself.
  • dvipat: This means 'two-footed', often referring to humans or beings that walk upright, symbolizing our journey through life.
  • pra catuṣpad: This translates to 'four-footed', which can refer to animals or the natural world, highlighting the interconnectedness of all living beings.
  • ityai: This word signifies 'thus' or 'in this way', indicating a conclusion or summary of the preceding thoughts.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of new beginnings, the importance of divine guidance, and the interconnectedness of all life forms. It emphasizes the significance of light, knowledge, and growth in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when embarking on new ventures, seeking clarity in decision-making, or when one is in need of inspiration and motivation. By invoking the blessings of the divine, individuals can find strength and purpose in their endeavors, fostering a sense of community and harmony with the world around them.
