उत स्मैनाṁ वस्त्रमथिं न तायुम् अनु क्रोशन्ति क्षितयो भरेषु |नीचायमानं जसुरिं न श्येनं श्रवश्चाच्छा पशुमच च यूथम् ||
uta smainaṁ vastramathiṁ na tāyum anu krośanti kṣitayo bhareṣu | nīcāyamānaṁ jasuriṁ na śyenaṁ śravaś cācchā paśumac ca yūtham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: indeed, or certainly
    • smainaṁ: this (referring to a person or entity)
    • vastramathiṁ: the one who is clothed or covered
    • na: not
    • tāyum: to the one who is in distress
    • anu: after or following
    • krośanti: they cry out or shout
    • kṣitayo: of the earth or the ground
    • bhareṣu: in the burdens or loads
    • nīcāyamānaṁ: being brought low or humbled
    • jasuriṁ: the one who is glorious or has fame
    • na: not
    • śyenaṁ: the eagle or a powerful being
    • śravaś: of fame or reputation
    • cācchā: and also
    • paśumac: of the animal or creature
    • ca: and
    • yūtham: the group or herd
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of existence and the dynamics of power and humility.
    • It suggests that even those who are powerful and glorious (like the eagle) do not shout out in distress when faced with burdens.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining composure and dignity, even in challenging situations.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders who remain calm and collected during crises, providing strength to their followers.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra teaches us about resilience and the importance of not showing weakness in the face of adversity.
    • It encourages individuals to rise above their circumstances and maintain their dignity.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in workplaces where employees face challenges but must continue to perform their duties with integrity.
    • It also resonates in personal life, where individuals may face hardships but choose to remain strong for their families and communities.
