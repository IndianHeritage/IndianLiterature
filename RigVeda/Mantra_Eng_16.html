मित्रं हुवे पूतदक्षं वरुणं च ऋशादसम् |धियं घृताचीं साधन्ता ||
mitraṁ huve pūtadakṣaṁ varuṇaṁ ca riśādasam | dhiyaṁ ghṛtācīṁ sādhantā ||
  • Mitraṁ: This word translates to "friend" or "allied." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the importance of friendship and cooperation in life.
  • Huve: This means "I invoke" or "I call upon." It indicates a plea or a prayer to the divine forces.
  • Pūtadakṣaṁ: This term can be broken down into "pūta," meaning "pure," and "dakṣa," meaning "skilled" or "capable." Together, it refers to a pure and skilled being, often associated with divine qualities.
  • Varuṇaṁ: Varuṇa is a deity associated with the cosmic order and moral authority. He represents the law of nature and the universe.
  • Ca: This simply means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • Riśādasam: This word can be interpreted as "the one who removes obstacles" or "the one who protects." It emphasizes the protective aspect of the divine.
  • Dhiyaṁ: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It signifies the importance of wisdom and clarity of thought.
  • Ghṛtācīṁ: This term refers to "clarity" or "purity of thought," often associated with the ghee (ghṛta) used in rituals, symbolizing nourishment and enlightenment.
  • Sādhantā: This means "to achieve" or "to accomplish." It indicates the process of striving towards goals with the help of divine forces.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer invoking the divine qualities of friendship, purity, protection, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in achieving clarity of thought and overcoming obstacles in life.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering friendships and alliances that support personal and communal growth.
  • It encourages individuals to seek purity in their actions and thoughts, promoting ethical behavior and moral integrity.
  • By invoking the qualities of Varuṇa, one can strive for a deeper understanding of the natural laws and moral order, leading to a more harmonious life.
  • The emphasis on intellect and clarity can be particularly relevant in decision-making processes, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community engagements.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging a life of cooperation, wisdom, and moral integrity.
