आ वां राजानाव अध्वरे ववृत्यां हव्येभिर इन्द्रावरुणा नमोभिः |प्र वां घृताचि बाह्वोर दधानां परि तमनā विषुरूपा जिगाति ||
ā vāṁ rājānāv adhvare vavṛtyāṁ havyebhir indrāvaruṇā namobhiḥ | pra vāṁ ghṛtācī bāhvor dadhānā pari tmanā viṣurūpā jigāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: To you both
    • rājānāv: O kings (or rulers)
    • adhvare: In the sacrifice
    • vavṛtyāṁ: With the offerings
    • havyebhir: With the oblations
    • indrāvaruṇā: To Indra and Varuna
    • namobhiḥ: With our salutations
    • pra vāṁ: We invoke you
    • ghṛtācī: With clarified butter
    • bāhvor: Of the arms
    • dadhānā: Holding
    • pari: Around
    • tmanā: With the mind
    • viṣurūpā: With diverse forms
    • jigāti: May they come to us
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards the deities Indra and Varuna, who are often associated with kingship, order, and the natural elements.
    • The invocation of "ā vāṁ rājānāv" signifies a call to the rulers or divine forces to be present during the sacrificial rites, emphasizing the importance of leadership and divine guidance in human affairs.
    • The phrase "havyebhir" indicates the offerings made during the ritual, which are essential for establishing a connection with the divine.
    • By addressing Indra and Varuna, the mantra acknowledges the duality of nature and the balance required in life, as these deities represent different aspects of existence—Indra as the god of rain and thunder, and Varuna as the god of cosmic order.
    • The mention of "ghṛtācī" (clarified butter) symbolizes purity and nourishment, suggesting that the offerings should be made with the best intentions and materials.
    • The invocation of "viṣurūpā" reflects the diversity of forms and manifestations in the universe, reminding us to appreciate the variety of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, whether in business or community, invoking the principles of fairness and balance can lead to more harmonious and effective governance.
    • During rituals or significant life events, such as weddings or festivals, the act of making offerings can be seen as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings from higher powers.
    • In environmental practices, recognizing the balance of nature and the importance of diverse ecosystems can lead to more sustainable living and respect for the planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance, the importance of offerings, and the recognition of the diverse forms of existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives, whether in leadership, personal relationships, or our connection to nature.
    • By incorporating these principles into our daily lives, we can foster a sense of community, respect for the environment, and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
