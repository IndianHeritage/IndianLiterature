मन्त्र: तिस्रो देष्ट्राय निरृतीर उपासते दीर्घश्रुतो विहि जानन्ति वह्नयः | तासां नि चिक्युः कवयो निदानं परेषु या गुह्येषु व्रतेषु ||
tisro deṣṭrāya nirṛtīr upāsate dīrghaśruto vi hi jānanti vahnayaḥ | tāsāṁ ni cikyuḥ kavayo nidānam pareṣu yā guhyeṣu vrateṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tisro: three
    • deṣṭrāya: to the realms or directions
    • nirṛtīr: of the goddess of destruction or misfortune
    • upāsate: worship or meditate upon
    • dīrghaśruto: one who has long ears or is wise
    • vi: indeed
    • jānanti: know or understand
    • vahnayaḥ: the fire or the divine fire
    • tāsāṁ: of them
    • ni: down or below
    • cikyuḥ: have sought or searched
    • kavayo: the wise or the poets
    • nidānam: the source or origin
    • pareṣu: in the higher realms
    • yā: who
    • guhyeṣu: in the hidden or secret
    • vrateṣu: in the vows or commitments
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the worship of the three realms associated with the goddess of destruction, indicating a recognition of the cyclical nature of life, death, and rebirth.
    • The mention of "dīrghaśruto" suggests that those who are wise and perceptive can understand the deeper truths of existence.
    • The "vahnayaḥ" refers to the divine fire, symbolizing purification and transformation, which is essential in many spiritual practices.
    • The wise poets or sages ("kavayo") seek the source of knowledge and truth, which is often hidden from ordinary perception.
    • The reference to "vrateṣu" emphasizes the importance of commitment and vows in spiritual practice, suggesting that true understanding comes from dedication.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the concept of destruction is not merely negative; it is often seen as a necessary precursor to creation. For instance, in nature, wildfires can clear old growth, making way for new life.
    • Wisdom is often sought in various forms, such as through education, meditation, or mentorship. The idea of "dīrghaśruto" can be likened to the importance of listening and learning from those who have more experience.
    • The concept of "vahnayaḥ" can be seen in practices like yoga or rituals that involve fire, where participants seek purification and transformation of the self.
    • Commitment to personal growth, whether through spiritual practices, relationships, or career goals, reflects the essence of "vrateṣu," where dedication leads to deeper understanding and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of destruction and creation, the pursuit of wisdom, and the importance of commitment in spiritual and personal growth.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us that understanding the hidden aspects of life requires dedication and a willingness to explore beyond the surface.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace change, seek knowledge, and commit to their paths, ultimately leading to a more profound understanding of existence.
