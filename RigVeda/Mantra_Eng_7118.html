पौरो अश्वस्य पुरुकृत् गवाम् अस्य उत्सो देव हिरण्ययः |नकिर हि दानम् परिमर्धिषत तवे यद्-यद् यामि तद् आ भर ||
pauro aśvasya purukṛd gavām asy utso deva hiraṇyayaḥ | nakir hi dānam parimardhiṣat tve yad-yad yāmi tad ā bhara ||
  • pauro aśvasya purukṛd: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is the master of the horses." In ancient times, horses were symbols of wealth and power. For example, in modern life, a successful entrepreneur or leader can be seen as a "master of horses," guiding their resources effectively.
  • gavām asy utso deva hiraṇyayaḥ: This translates to "the divine energy of wealth and abundance." Here, "hiraṇya" refers to gold or wealth, which is often associated with prosperity. In real life, this can be related to individuals or organizations that create wealth through innovation and hard work, such as tech companies that revolutionize industries.
  • nakir hi dānam parimardhiṣat: This means "there is no greater gift than generosity." This highlights the importance of giving and sharing in society. For instance, charitable organizations that provide food, education, and healthcare embody this principle, showing how generosity can transform lives.
  • tve yad-yad yāmi tad ā bhara: This can be interpreted as "whatever I seek, may it come to me." It reflects the idea of manifestation and the power of intention. In practical terms, this can be seen in goal-setting practices where individuals visualize their aspirations, such as a student aiming for academic success or an athlete striving for victory.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of mastery, abundance, generosity, and the power of intention. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives. By striving to be masters of our resources, embracing abundance, practicing generosity, and setting clear intentions, we can create a positive impact on ourselves and those around us. This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
