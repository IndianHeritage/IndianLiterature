अभि श्यावं न कृशनेभिर अश्वं नक्षत्रेभिः पितरो द्यां अपिंशन |रात्र्यां तमो अदधुर ज्योतिर अहन बृहस्पतिर भिनद अद्रिं विदद गाः ||
abhi śyāvaṁ na kṛśanebhir aśvaṁ nakṣatrebhiḥ pitaro dyām apiṁśan | rātryāṁ tamo adadhur jyotir ahan bṛhaspatir bhinad adriṁ vidad gāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • śyāvaṁ: the dark or the black
    • na: not
    • kṛśanebhir: by the weak or the feeble
    • aśvaṁ: the horse
    • nakṣatrebhiḥ: by the stars
    • pitaro: the ancestors or forefathers
    • dyām: the sky or the heavens
    • apiṁśan: having a share or part
    • rātryāṁ: in the night
    • tamo: darkness
    • adadhur: they placed or established
    • jyotir: light
    • ahan: day
    • bṛhaspatir: the great teacher or Jupiter
    • bhinad: he split or divided
    • adriṁ: the mountain
    • vidad: he created or made
    • gāḥ: the cows or the wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the cosmic order and the balance between light and darkness.
    • The phrase "abhi śyāvaṁ na kṛśanebhir" suggests that the dark cannot be overcome by the weak, indicating the strength of resilience.
    • The mention of "aśvaṁ nakṣatrebhiḥ" connects the earthly with the celestial, emphasizing the importance of celestial guidance in our lives.
    • "Pitaro dyām apiṁśan" reflects the reverence for ancestors and the belief that they play a role in our lives, guiding us from the heavens.
    • The line "rātryāṁ tamo adadhur jyotir ahan" illustrates the transition from night (darkness) to day (light), symbolizing hope and renewal.
    • "Bṛhaspatir bhinad adriṁ" signifies the role of wisdom and knowledge in overcoming obstacles, represented by the mountains.
    • Finally, "vidad gāḥ" can be interpreted as the creation of wealth and prosperity through wisdom and effort.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal struggles, this mantra can remind individuals that challenges (darkness) cannot be overcome by weakness; rather, strength and resilience are essential.
    • In decision-making, seeking guidance from elders or ancestors can provide wisdom, much like the stars guiding travelers.
    • The transition from night to day can symbolize personal growth, where one moves from a phase of confusion to clarity.
    • In professional life, the wisdom of mentors (bṛhaspatir) can help navigate challenges (adriṁ) and lead to success (gāḥ).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance in life, the importance of strength, guidance from ancestors, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek resilience in the face of challenges, to honor their roots, and to strive for enlightenment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder that with wisdom and strength, one can navigate the complexities of existence and achieve prosperity.
