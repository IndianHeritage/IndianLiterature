आ च नो बर्हिः सदताविताः च नः स्पार्हाणि दातवे वसु |अस्रिधन्तो मरुतः सोम्ये मधौ स्वाहेह मादयाध्वै ||
ā ca no barhiḥ sadatāvitā ca naḥ spārhāṇi dātave vasu | asredhanto marutaḥ somye madhau svāheha mādayādhvai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā ca no: "and may it come to us"
    • barhiḥ: "the sacred grass" (often used in rituals)
    • sadatāvitā: "ever-present" or "always available"
    • ca naḥ: "and to us"
    • spārhāṇi: "desires" or "aspirations"
    • dātave: "to the giver" or "to the one who bestows"
    • vasu: "wealth" or "prosperity"
    • asredhanto: "those who are strong" or "powerful"
    • marutaḥ: "the winds" or "the storm gods"
    • somye: "in the soma" (a sacred drink)
    • madhau: "in the sweetness" or "in the essence"
    • svāheha: "may it be auspicious" or "may it be well"
    • mādayādhvai: "may we be delighted" or "may we rejoice"
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, invoking the blessings of the divine.
    • The mention of "barhiḥ" signifies the importance of sacred rituals and the connection to nature.
    • By asking for "spārhāṇi," the mantra emphasizes the importance of desires and aspirations in life.
    • The reference to "marutaḥ" highlights the power of natural forces and their role in our lives.
    • The invocation of "soma" suggests a connection to spiritual nourishment and the joy it brings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for success and prosperity in ventures.
    • In personal life, it can be interpreted as a desire for fulfillment and happiness in relationships.
    • During festivals or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for abundance and joy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of desires, nature, and divine blessings.
    • It encourages individuals to seek prosperity while remaining grounded in spiritual practices.
    • In essence, it teaches that true wealth comes from a harmonious balance between material and spiritual pursuits.
