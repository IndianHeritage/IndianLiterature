यः ककुभो निधारयः पृथिव्याम अधि दर्शतः |स माताऽपूर्व्यं पदं तद वरुणस्य सप्त्यं स हि गोपा इवेयो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
yaḥ kakubho nidhārayaḥ pṛthivyām adhi darśataḥ | sa mātā pūrvyam padaṁ tad varuṇasya saptyaṁ sa hi gopā iveryo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • kakubho: of the directions or the sky
    • nidhārayaḥ: who establishes or supports
    • pṛthivyām: on the earth
    • adhi: above
    • darśataḥ: who sees or perceives
    • sa: he
    • mātā: mother
    • pūrvyam: of the east
    • padaṁ: the place or position
    • tad: that
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of water and cosmic order)
    • saptyaṁ: the truth or the essence
    • sa hi: for he
    • gopā: the protector or guardian
    • iveryo: who is in the heavens
    • nabhantām: of the sky or the celestial realm
    • anyake: other
    • same: equal or similar
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that supports and sustains the world.
    • The reference to "kakubho" indicates the omnipresence of this divine force in all directions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and cosmic order, represented by Varuna, who is seen as a guardian of moral and natural law.
    • The mention of "mātā" (mother) signifies nurturing and protection, suggesting that this divine force is caring and supportive.
    • The mantra also highlights the interconnectedness of all beings, as it refers to the heavens and the earth, indicating a holistic view of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance and support from nature, which can be seen as a manifestation of this divine presence.
    • In moments of uncertainty, people may turn to their spiritual beliefs, reflecting the nurturing aspect of the divine as a "mother" figure.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be viewed as a way to honor the earth and the cosmic order, aligning with the principles of truth and protection mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support that exists in our lives, encouraging us to seek truth and harmony.
    • It highlights the significance of nurturing relationships, both with nature and within our communities.
    • By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of moral order, we can strive to live in a way that honors this divine presence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to be guardians of truth and protectors of the earth, fostering a sense of responsibility towards our environment and each other.
