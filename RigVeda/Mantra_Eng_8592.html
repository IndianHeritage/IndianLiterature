सोमं गावो धेनवो वावशानाः सोमं विप्राः मतििभिः पृच्छमानाः |सोमः सुतः पूयते अज्यमानः सोम अर्कास त्रिष्टुभः सं नवन्ते ||
somaṁ gāvo dhenavo vāvaśānāḥ somaṁ viprā matibhiḥ pṛcchamānāḥ | somaḥ sutaḥ pūyate ajyamānaḥ some arkās triṣṭubhaḥ saṁ navante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somaṁ: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and immortality.
    • gāvo: Cows, representing wealth and sustenance.
    • dhenavo: Milking cows, indicating the source of nourishment.
    • vāvaśānāḥ: Those who are eager or desirous.
    • viprā: The wise or learned ones, often referring to priests or scholars.
    • matibhiḥ: With intellect or understanding.
    • pṛcchamānāḥ: Inquiring or questioning.
    • sutaḥ: Offspring or progeny, often referring to the results of rituals.
    • pūyate: Purified or sanctified.
    • ajyamānaḥ: Being offered or presented.
    • arkās: Hymns or praises.
    • triṣṭubhaḥ: A specific meter in Vedic poetry.
    • saṁ navante: They rejoice or celebrate.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of Soma, which is not just a drink but a symbol of divine energy and spiritual nourishment.
    • The mention of cows signifies the importance of agriculture and pastoral life in Vedic culture, where cows are seen as a source of wealth and sustenance.
    • The wise individuals (viprā) are encouraged to seek knowledge and understanding, reflecting the value placed on inquiry and wisdom in society.
    • The act of purification (pūyate) suggests that through rituals and offerings, one can attain spiritual elevation and connection with the divine.
    • The joyous celebration (saṁ navante) indicates that the results of such spiritual practices lead to happiness and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of 'Soma' can be likened to practices that promote mental and physical well-being, such as yoga and meditation, which nourish the spirit.
    • The reverence for cows in many cultures today reflects the ongoing importance of sustainable agriculture and ethical treatment of animals.
    • Educational institutions encourage inquiry and critical thinking, mirroring the call for wisdom and understanding found in this mantra.
    • Rituals and community gatherings that celebrate harvests or significant life events can be seen as contemporary expressions of the joy and purification mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of nourishment, wisdom, and celebration in life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek knowledge, appreciate the sources of our sustenance, and engage in practices that elevate our spiritual and communal lives.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a deeper understanding of our environment, cherish our resources, and celebrate the joys of existence through community and spirituality.
