अबोधि जार उषसाम उपस्थाद धोत mandraḥ कवितमः पावकः | दधाति केतुम उभयस्य जन्तोर हव्यā देवेषु द्रविणं सुकृत्सु ||
abodhi jāra uṣasām upasthād dhotā mandraḥ kavitamaḥ pāvakaḥ | dadhāti ketum ubhayasya jantor havyā deveṣu draviṇaṁ sukṛtsu ||
  • abodhi: This term can be interpreted as "awareness" or "consciousness." It signifies the importance of being aware and enlightened in our actions and thoughts.
  • jāra: This word refers to "old age" or "decay." It reminds us of the transient nature of life and the inevitability of aging.
  • uṣasām: This translates to "of the dawn" or "of the morning." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • upasthād: Meaning "approaching" or "coming near," it indicates the arrival of something significant, often associated with divine presence or inspiration.
  • dhotā: This term means "the one who purifies" or "the purifier." It emphasizes the need for cleansing, both physically and spiritually, in our lives.
  • mandraḥ: This word can be understood as "melodious" or "sweet-sounding." It reflects the beauty of expression, particularly in poetry and music.
  • kavitamaḥ: This translates to "the best of poets" or "the most excellent in creativity." It highlights the value of creativity and artistic expression in human life.
  • pāvakaḥ: Meaning "the purifier" or "fire," it symbolizes transformation, energy, and the power to cleanse and renew.
  • dadhāti: This term means "to bestow" or "to grant." It signifies the act of giving, sharing, and the importance of generosity.
  • ketum: This translates to "the banner" or "the sign." It represents guidance, direction, and the importance of having a purpose in life.
  • ubhayasya: Meaning "of both," it indicates the duality of existence, encompassing both the material and spiritual realms.
  • jantor: This term refers to "beings" or "creatures." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living beings.
  • havyā: This translates to "sacred offerings" or "oblations." It signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • deveṣu: Meaning "to the gods," it indicates reverence and devotion towards divine entities.
  • draviṇaṁ: This term means "wealth" or "resources." It highlights the significance of material and spiritual wealth in our lives.
  • sukṛtsu: This translates to "in the virtuous" or "among the good." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and ethical living.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of awareness, creativity, purification, and the interconnectedness of life. It serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings, to be conscious of our actions, and to strive for both material and spiritual wealth. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, artistic pursuits, and spiritual practices. By recognizing the transient nature of life and the importance of living virtuously, we can cultivate a more meaningful existence, fostering a sense of purpose and connection with the world around us.
