इदं त एकं पर ऊ त एकं तृतीयेन ज्योतिषा सं विशस्व |संवेशने तन्वश चारुर एधि प्रियो देवानाम् परम जनित्रे ||
idaṁ ta ekam para ū ta ekaṁ tṛtīyena jyotiṣā saṁ viśasva | saṁveśane tanva1ś cārur edhi priyo devānām parame janitre ||
  • idaṁ ta ekam: This phrase translates to "this is one." It emphasizes the unity of existence, suggesting that all things are interconnected. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities function; when individuals come together for a common cause, they create a stronger, unified force.
  • para ū ta ekaṁ: This means "the supreme one is also one." It highlights the idea of a singular supreme reality or consciousness that pervades everything. For example, in various spiritual practices, individuals often seek to connect with a higher power or universal consciousness, reflecting this unity.
  • tṛtīyena jyotiṣā: This translates to "by the third light." The "third light" can symbolize enlightenment or knowledge that transcends ordinary understanding. In practical terms, this can relate to moments of insight or clarity that guide decision-making in life, such as choosing a career path or resolving personal conflicts.
  • saṁ viśasva: This means "enter into the essence." It encourages individuals to delve deeper into their true nature and the nature of reality. In everyday life, this can be applied through practices like meditation or self-reflection, which help individuals understand their motivations and desires better.
  • saṁveśane tanva1ś: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the gathering of bodies." It suggests the importance of community and collective effort. For instance, team sports or group projects in educational settings exemplify how collaboration can lead to greater achievements than individual efforts alone.
  • cārur edhi: This translates to "may you be beautiful." It is a call for beauty in both physical and spiritual forms. In real life, this can be seen in the appreciation of art, nature, and human relationships, where beauty enhances our experiences and connections with others.
  • priyo devānām: This means "beloved of the gods." It implies a connection to the divine or higher powers. In practical terms, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and compassion, which are often seen as ways to align oneself with higher moral or spiritual principles.
  • parame janitre: This translates to "to the supreme mother." It signifies reverence for the nurturing aspect of the universe, often associated with nature or the earth. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and communities work to protect and honor the planet as a nurturing force.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts of unity, enlightenment, community, beauty, divinity, and nurturing. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to seek deeper connections with themselves, others, and the universe. By embodying these principles, one can foster a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, promoting both personal growth and collective well-being.
