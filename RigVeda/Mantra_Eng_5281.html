इमा उ त्वा पस्पृधाना सो अत्र मन्द्रा गिरो देवयन्तीर उप स्थुः |अर्वाचि ते पथ्या राया एतु स्याम ते सुमताव इन्द्र शर्मन् ||
imā u tvā paspṛdhānāso atra mandrā giro devayantīr upa sthuḥ | arvācī te pathyā rāya etu syāma te sumatāv indra śarman ||
  • imā u tvā paspṛdhānāso: This phrase can be translated as "These words are directed towards you." It signifies the act of invoking or addressing a divine presence, in this case, Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • atra mandrā giro devayantīr upa sthuḥ: This translates to "Here, the sweet words of praise are offered to the divine." It emphasizes the importance of hymns and prayers in connecting with the divine, suggesting that the act of worship is a means to establish a relationship with higher powers.
  • arvācī te pathyā rāya: This means "May the paths of prosperity lead to you." It reflects the desire for guidance and support from the divine in achieving success and well-being in life.
  • etu syāma te sumatāv indra śarman: This can be interpreted as "May we attain your favor, O Indra, through our wise thoughts." It highlights the importance of wisdom and intelligence in seeking blessings and achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, the importance of prayer, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal rituals or prayers, individuals can recite this mantra to seek guidance and blessings from a higher power.
  • In community gatherings or religious ceremonies, it can serve as a reminder of the collective aspiration for prosperity and wisdom.
  • In moments of decision-making, invoking the essence of this mantra can encourage individuals to seek wise counsel and divine support.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine connection, the pursuit of prosperity, and the emphasis on wisdom, making it relevant in both spiritual and practical aspects of life.
