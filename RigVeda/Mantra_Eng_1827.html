न नूनमस्ति नो स्वः कस्तद्वेद यदाद्भुतम् |अन्यस्य चित्तमभिसंचरेंयं उतााधीतं विनाश्यति ||
na nūnam asti no śvaḥ kas tad veda yad adbhutam | anyasya cittam abhi saṁcareṇyam utādhītaṁ vi naśyati ||
  • Translation: "Surely, there is nothing that does not exist; who knows what is wonderful? The mind of another does not wander; it is not lost in what is learned."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na nūnam asti: "Surely, there is nothing" - This phrase emphasizes the existence of all things, suggesting that everything has a purpose or a reason for being.
    • no śvaḥ: "that does not exist" - Reinforces the idea that existence is universal and inescapable.
    • kas tad veda: "who knows what is wonderful?" - This line questions the understanding of the extraordinary, implying that true knowledge is rare and valuable.
    • yad adbhutam: "what is wonderful" - Refers to the marvels of the universe and life, encouraging exploration and curiosity.
    • anyasya cittam: "the mind of another" - Highlights the individuality of thought and perception, suggesting that each person's understanding is unique.
    • abhi saṁcareṇyam: "does not wander" - Indicates focus and concentration, essential for learning and understanding.
    • utādhītaṁ vi: "it is not lost in what is learned" - Suggests that knowledge, once acquired, remains with us and shapes our understanding.
    • naśyati: "does not perish" - Conveys the idea that true knowledge and understanding endure beyond time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, this mantra can be applied to the importance of learning and retaining knowledge. For instance, students who engage deeply with their subjects often find that their understanding grows, and they can apply this knowledge in various contexts, such as problem-solving in real life.
    • In personal development, the idea that "the mind of another does not wander" can be seen in mindfulness practices. When individuals focus on the present moment, they enhance their awareness and understanding of their thoughts and feelings, leading to better emotional regulation.
    • In scientific exploration, the phrase "who knows what is wonderful?" encourages curiosity and innovation. Scientists and researchers who seek to understand the unknown often make groundbreaking discoveries that change our perception of the world.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of existence, knowledge, and the individual mind's capacity to learn and retain information. It encourages us to appreciate the wonders of life and the importance of focused learning. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in education, personal growth, and scientific inquiry, reminding us that knowledge is a powerful tool that shapes our understanding of the world and ourselves. By recognizing the value of every experience and the uniqueness of our thoughts, we can foster a deeper connection with the universe and each other.
