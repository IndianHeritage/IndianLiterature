वायुर अस्मा उपामनथत पिनष्टि स्मा कुनन्नमाः |केशी विषस्य पात्रेण यद् रुद्रेणापिबत सह ||
vāyur asmā upāmanthat pinaṣṭi smā kunannamā | keśī viṣasya pātreṇa yad rudreṇāpibat saha ||
  • vāyur asmā upāmanthat: This phrase can be translated as "May the wind (vāyu) bring us together." Here, 'vāyu' symbolizes the vital force or energy that connects all living beings. In real life, this can be seen in how communication and relationships are fostered through shared experiences and interactions.
  • pinaṣṭi smā kunannamā: This part means "Let us not be separated from the nourishment." It emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual. For example, in a community, sharing food and resources strengthens bonds and ensures collective well-being.
  • keśī viṣasya pātreṇa: This translates to "With the vessel of the hair (keśī) of poison (viṣa)." The 'vessel' here can be interpreted as the body or the mind that holds both positive and negative experiences. In life, we often encounter challenges (poison) that we must navigate wisely to grow and learn.
  • yad rudreṇāpibat saha: This means "As Rudra (a form of Shiva) drinks together." Rudra represents transformation and the duality of creation and destruction. This can be related to how we must embrace both joy and sorrow in life, understanding that they coexist and contribute to our growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nourishment, and the duality of experiences. In real life, it teaches us to value our relationships, share resources, and navigate challenges with wisdom. By recognizing the vital forces that bind us and the lessons learned from both positive and negative experiences, we can foster a more harmonious existence. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, family meals, or even personal reflections, reminding us of the significance of unity and resilience in our lives.
