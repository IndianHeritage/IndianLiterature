मन्त्र: दिवस पृथिव्याऽधि भवेंदो द्युम्नवर्धनः |भवा वाजानाम् पतिः ||
divas pṛthivyā adhi bhavendo dyumnavardhanaḥ | bhavā vājānām patiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divas: of the heavens
    • pṛthivyā: of the earth
    • adhi: above
    • bhavendo: the lord of existence
    • dyumnavardhanaḥ: the one who increases glory or fame
    • bhavā: existence or being
    • vājānām: of the strong or powerful
    • patiḥ: lord or master
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine, recognizing the lord of existence who resides above the earth and heavens, and who enhances glory and strength. It acknowledges the divine as the master of all powerful beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, a good leader is often seen as someone who enhances the capabilities and strengths of their team, much like the divine figure in this mantra who increases glory.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to elevate their own existence and glory through education, self-improvement, and community service, reflecting the essence of striving for higher ideals.
    • In nature, the balance between the earth (pṛthivyā) and the heavens (divas) can be seen in ecosystems where every element plays a role in maintaining harmony, akin to the divine order mentioned in the mantra.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing a higher power or purpose in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek strength and glory through their actions.
    • It can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal aspirations, community leadership, and even in spiritual practices where one seeks to connect with a higher existence.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of striving for excellence and virtue in our endeavors.
