अधि यस् तस्थौ केशवन्ता व्यचस्वन्ता न पुष्ट्यै |वनोति शिप्राभ्यां शिप्रिणीवान् ||
adhi yas tasthau keśavantā vyacasvantā na puṣṭyai | vanoti śiprābhyāṁ śipriṇīvān ||
  • adhi - This word can be translated as "above" or "over." It signifies a higher state or a superior position.
  • yas - This means "who" or "that," referring to a person or entity.
  • tasthau - This translates to "stood" or "remained," indicating stability or presence.
  • keśavantā - This term refers to "one with hair" or "hairy," often symbolizing vitality and strength.
  • vyacasvantā - This can be interpreted as "one who is powerful" or "one who has great energy."
  • na - This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • puṣṭyai - This translates to "for nourishment" or "for growth," emphasizing the importance of sustenance.
  • vanoti - This means "to bring" or "to produce," suggesting an act of creation or manifestation.
  • śiprābhyāṁ - This term can be understood as "with sharpness" or "with precision," indicating clarity and focus.
  • śipriṇīvān - This translates to "one who is skilled" or "one who possesses expertise," highlighting the value of knowledge and skill.

The mantra speaks to the qualities of strength, stability, and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of being grounded while also possessing the energy and skill to create and produce effectively. The imagery of hair and sharpness suggests vitality and precision in one's actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Just as the mantra emphasizes stability and nourishment, individuals can focus on building a strong foundation in their lives through education and self-care.
  • Professional Growth: The idea of being skilled and precise can be applied in the workplace, where honing one's skills and being clear in communication can lead to better outcomes.
  • Community Building: The mantra's focus on nourishment can be reflected in community efforts to support one another, ensuring that everyone has the resources they need to thrive.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between strength and nourishment, the importance of skill and precision, and the need for stability in our lives. It encourages us to cultivate these qualities in our personal and professional endeavors, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and productive existence.
