नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणान इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be likened to seeking the blessings of a higher power or authority in times of need.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This means "we sing your praises." It reflects the importance of gratitude and recognition of the forces that help us in our lives. For example, in a community, people often come together to celebrate achievements, acknowledging the contributions of individuals or groups.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This translates to "the rivers that flow, do not cease." Rivers are often symbolic of life and continuity. In real life, this can be seen in the persistence of nature and the importance of water in sustaining life. Just as rivers flow, our efforts and actions should also be continuous and unwavering.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "I have made for you, O Hariva." Hariva can be interpreted as a reference to the divine or auspicious. This line emphasizes the act of creation and offering, akin to how individuals create opportunities or environments for success in their lives.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new Brahman." Brahman represents the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Vedic philosophy. The idea of "new" suggests innovation and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, this can be related to the importance of continuous learning and adaptation in a rapidly changing world.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This means "may we be endowed with wisdom and strength." This line highlights the significance of wisdom in guiding our actions and decisions. In practical terms, this can be seen in leadership roles where wise decision-making is crucial for the success of an organization or community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and praising higher powers, the continuity of life, the act of creation, the pursuit of knowledge, and the need for wisdom. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, environmental conservation, personal development, and leadership. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can foster a harmonious and progressive existence.
