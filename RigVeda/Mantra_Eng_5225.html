त्वां अग्ने समिधानो वसिष्ठो जरूथं हन् यक्षि रायें पुरंधिम् |पुरुणीथा जातवेदो जरस्व युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
tvām agne samidhāno vasiṣṭho jarūthaṁ han yakṣi rāye puraṁdhim | puruṇīthā jātavedo jarasva yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • tvām agne - "to you, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • samidhāno - "kindled" - This indicates the act of igniting or lighting the fire, which is essential in Vedic rituals for offerings and sacrifices.
  • vasiṣṭho - "the best" or "the most excellent" - This term signifies the supreme quality of Agni, highlighting his importance in the spiritual and material realms.
  • jarūthaṁ - "to the one who brings forth" - This suggests Agni's role in generating prosperity and abundance.
  • han - "to destroy" - This word implies the dual nature of Agni, who can both create and destroy, representing the cycle of life and death.
  • yakṣi - "to grant" - This indicates Agni's ability to bestow blessings and fulfill desires.
  • rāye - "wealth" - This refers to material and spiritual wealth, emphasizing the importance of prosperity in life.
  • puraṁdhim - "the one who is the best among the best" - This phrase elevates Agni to a position of supreme authority and reverence.
  • puruṇīthā - "you who are full" - This suggests completeness and abundance, reinforcing the idea of Agni as a source of all that is good.
  • jātavedo - "the one who knows all births" - This highlights Agni's omniscience and his role in the cycle of life.
  • jarasva - "may you be old" - This can be interpreted as a wish for longevity and stability, both for Agni and for those who worship him.
  • yūyam - "you all" - This addresses the collective, indicating that the blessings of Agni are for everyone.
  • pāta - "protect" - This is a plea for safeguarding and guidance from Agni.
  • svastibhiḥ - "with auspiciousness" - This emphasizes the desire for well-being and prosperity in life.
  • sadā - "always" - This indicates a continuous need for Agni's blessings.
  • naḥ - "for us" - This personalizes the mantra, making it a direct appeal to Agni for support and guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Agni, seeking his blessings for prosperity, protection, and well-being. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers where individuals seek guidance and support in their endeavors. The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the dual nature of fire as both a creator and destroyer, reminding us of the balance needed in life. By acknowledging Agni's role in our lives, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and reverence for the forces that sustain us, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
