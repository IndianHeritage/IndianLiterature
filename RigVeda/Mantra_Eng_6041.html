यस्मिन विश्वाश् चर्षणय उता च्यौत्ना ज्रयांसी च |अनु घेन मन्दी मघोनः ||
yasmin viśvāś carṣaṇaya uta cyautnā jrayāṁsi ca | anu ghen mandī maghonaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin - in which
    • viśvāś - all beings
    • carṣaṇaya - with the support
    • uta - and
    • cyautnā - the flow
    • jrayāṁsi - of decay
    • ca - and
    • anu - following
    • ghen - the powerful
    • mandī - the gentle
    • maghonaḥ - of the great
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a state of existence where all beings are supported and interconnected.
    • The term "viśvāś" signifies the unity of all life forms, suggesting that everything in the universe is interdependent.
    • "Carṣaṇaya" implies that this unity provides a foundation or support for all beings, much like how a community supports its members.
    • The mention of "cyautnā" and "jrayāṁsi" reflects the natural processes of life, including the flow of energy and the inevitability of decay.
    • The phrase "anu ghen mandī maghonaḥ" emphasizes the importance of gentleness and strength in the universe, suggesting that true power lies in harmony and balance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in how individuals support one another during difficult times, showcasing the interconnectedness of human experiences.
    • In nature, ecosystems demonstrate this principle, where various species rely on each other for survival, illustrating the balance of life.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be seen in the way love and compassion create a supportive environment, allowing individuals to thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, support, and the natural cycles of life.
    • It teaches us that strength comes from gentleness and that we are all part of a larger whole.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, a deeper understanding of our interconnectedness, and a greater appreciation for the balance of nature.
