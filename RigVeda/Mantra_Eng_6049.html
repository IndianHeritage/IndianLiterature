शिक्षा विभिन्दो अस्मै चत्वार्य आयुतā ददत् |अष्टा परः सहस्रā ||
śikṣā vibhindo asmai catvāry ayutā dadat | aṣṭā paraḥ sahasrā ||
  • śikṣā: This word translates to "instruction" or "education." It emphasizes the importance of learning and acquiring knowledge.
  • vibhindo: This means "to divide" or "to distribute." It suggests the act of sharing knowledge or wisdom among individuals.
  • asmai: This word means "to him" or "to that person." It indicates the recipient of the knowledge or instruction.
  • catvāry: This translates to "four." It signifies a specific number, which can represent various aspects of life or learning.
  • ayutā: This means "ten thousand." It denotes a large quantity, symbolizing abundance or vastness.
  • dadat: This translates to "giving" or "bestowing." It emphasizes the act of providing or offering something valuable.
  • aṣṭā: This means "eight." Like "catvāry," it represents another specific number, which can have various interpretations.
  • paraḥ: This translates to "beyond" or "higher." It suggests a sense of elevation or advancement.
  • sahasrā: This means "thousand." It reinforces the idea of abundance and multiplicity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to impart knowledge and wisdom in a structured manner, emphasizing the importance of education and the sharing of insights. It suggests that one should distribute knowledge in a way that is both abundant and meaningful, with specific numbers indicating the depth and breadth of learning.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various educational settings. For instance, in a classroom, a teacher may divide complex subjects into four main topics (catvāry) and then elaborate on each topic with ten thousand (ayutā) examples or applications, ensuring that students grasp the concepts thoroughly. Additionally, the idea of giving (dadat) knowledge can be seen in mentorship programs where experienced individuals share their insights with younger generations, fostering a culture of learning and growth.


In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of structured education and the importance of sharing knowledge generously. It serves as a reminder that learning is not just an individual pursuit but a communal responsibility, where the wisdom of the few can benefit the many. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can create a more informed and enlightened society.
