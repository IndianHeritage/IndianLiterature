यज्ञैर वा यज्ञवाहसो विप्रस्य वा मतीनाम् |मरुतः श्रिणुत हवं ||
yajñair vā yajñavāhaso viprasya vā matīnām | marutaḥ śṛṇutā havam ||
  • Mantra: "yajñair vā yajñavāhaso viprasya vā matīnām | marutaḥ śṛṇutā havam ||"
  • Translation: "O Maruts, hear our invocation, whether it is through the offerings of the yajñas or through the wisdom of the learned."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Yajñair: Refers to the rituals or sacrifices performed to invoke divine blessings.
    • Vā: Means "or," indicating an alternative approach.
    • Yajñavāhaso: Refers to those who carry out the yajñas, often the priests or learned individuals.
    • Viprasya: Means "of the wise" or "of the learned," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom in rituals.
    • Matīnām: Refers to "thoughts" or "intellects," emphasizing the role of mental focus and intention.
    • Marutaḥ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with strength and vitality.
    • Śṛṇutā: Means "hear" or "listen," a call for attention from the divine beings.
    • Havam: Refers to the offerings or invocations made during the rituals.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of both ritualistic offerings (yajñas) and the intellectual contributions of learned individuals (viprasya) in spiritual practices.
    • It highlights the belief that divine forces, represented by the Maruts, are responsive to both physical offerings and the mental intentions behind them.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where rituals are performed with a focus on both the physical act and the underlying thoughts or prayers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, ceremonies such as weddings or festivals involve both physical offerings (like food or flowers) and the heartfelt intentions of the participants, reflecting the duality expressed in the mantra.
    • In modern contexts, this can be likened to corporate rituals where teams come together to celebrate achievements, combining tangible rewards with motivational speeches that inspire and uplift the group.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of action and intention in spiritual and everyday practices.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in rituals with a clear mind and purpose, recognizing that both the physical and mental aspects are essential for invoking divine blessings.
    • In essence, it teaches that true devotion and effectiveness in any endeavor come from a harmonious blend of action and thought.
