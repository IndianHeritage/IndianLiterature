नहि वः शत्रुर विविदे अधि dyavi न भूम्यां ऋशादसः |युष्माकम अस्तु तविषी तनā युजा रुद्रासो नू चिदाधृषे ||
nahi vaḥ śatrur vivide adhi dyavi na bhūmyāṁ riśādasaḥ | yuṣmākam astu taviṣī tanā yujā rudrāso nū cid ādhṛṣe ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, there is no enemy who can be seen in the sky or on the earth; may your strength be united with the Rudras, and may you not be disturbed by any adversary."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • nahi: indeed, not
    • vaḥ: your
    • śatrur: enemy
    • vivide: who can be seen
    • adhi: in, upon
    • dyavi: the sky
    • na: not
    • bhūmyāṁ: on the earth
    • riśādasaḥ: adversaries, enemies
    • yuṣmākam: your
    • astu: may it be
    • taviṣī: strength, power
    • tanā: body, essence
    • yujā: united, joined
    • rudrāso: with the Rudras (a group of deities)
    • nū: not
    • cid: at all
    • ādhṛṣe: disturbed, troubled

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea of overcoming fear and adversity. It suggests that when one is united with strength and divine support, there is no enemy that can truly harm them.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various situations such as facing challenges in personal life, professional environments, or during conflicts. For instance, a student preparing for exams may feel overwhelmed by competition, but by focusing on their strengths and seeking support from mentors or peers, they can overcome these challenges.
    • In a workplace scenario, a team facing a tough project deadline may feel the pressure of competition. By collaborating effectively and leveraging each member's strengths, they can navigate through the challenges successfully.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of unity, strength, and divine support in overcoming adversities. It encourages individuals to recognize that perceived enemies or challenges can be faced with confidence when one is aligned with their inner strength and supported by a greater force.
    • In essence, it teaches that fear of adversaries can be mitigated through self-awareness, collaboration, and spiritual connection, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success.
