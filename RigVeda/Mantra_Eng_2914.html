मन्त्र: पुरोळाशम् पचत्यं जुषस्वेन्द्रा गुरुस्व च |तुभ्यं हव्यानि सिस्रते ||
puroḻāśam pacatyaṁ juṣasvendrā gurasva ca | tubhyaṁ havyāni sisrate ||
  • Mantra: "puroḻāśam pacatyaṁ juṣasvendrā gurasva ca | tubhyaṁ havyāni sisrate ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, the one who is the lord of the offerings, may you accept our offerings and grant us your blessings. To you, the divine, we offer our oblations."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • puroḻāśam: Refers to the divine being Agni, who is considered the lord of offerings.
    • pacatyaṁ: Means "to cook" or "to prepare," indicating the act of preparing offerings for the divine.
    • juṣas: Translates to "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," expressing a desire for the divine to accept the offerings.
    • indrā: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
    • gurasva: Means "be gracious" or "be kind," a plea for benevolence from the divine.
    • tubhyaṁ: Means "to you," indicating the recipient of the offerings.
    • havyāni: Refers to the offerings or oblations made to the divine.
    • sisrate: Means "to flow" or "to come forth," indicating the act of offerings being presented.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, offerings are made to deities during ceremonies, symbolizing gratitude and seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to the practice of giving back to the community or environment, where individuals or organizations offer their resources or time to support a cause, reflecting the spirit of generosity and gratitude.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • It highlights the act of giving and the belief that such acts can lead to blessings and positive outcomes in life.
    • In a broader sense, it teaches the value of gratitude, respect, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of acknowledging and honoring the forces that sustain us, whether they be divine, natural, or communal.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in acts of kindness, generosity, and gratitude, fostering a sense of community and connection.
    • Ultimately, it reflects the belief that by giving and sharing, we can cultivate a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
