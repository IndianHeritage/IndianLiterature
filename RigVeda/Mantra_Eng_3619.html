नū रोदसी बृहत्भिर नो वरूथैः पत्नीवद्भिर इषयन्ती सजोषाः |उरूची विश्वे यजते न पातं धियā स्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū rodasī bṛhadbhir no varūthaiḥ patnīvadbhir iṣayantī sajoṣāḥ | urūcī viśve yajate ni pātaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū rodasī: This phrase can be interpreted as "now the heavens" or "now the sky." It signifies the vastness and the divine nature of the cosmos.
  • bṛhadbhir: Meaning "with the great ones" or "by the mighty." This suggests the presence of powerful forces or deities that govern the universe.
  • no varūthaiḥ: Translates to "by the Varuna," referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna represents the moral and natural laws that maintain harmony in the universe.
  • patnīvadbhir: This means "like a wife" or "in the manner of a spouse." It indicates a nurturing and protective aspect, suggesting that the divine forces care for and support humanity.
  • iṣayantī: This word means "guiding" or "leading." It implies that these divine forces are actively involved in guiding human actions and decisions.
  • sajoṣāḥ: Translates to "with joy" or "with happiness." This indicates that the guidance provided by the divine is not only protective but also brings joy and fulfillment.
  • urūcī: Meaning "the wide" or "the expansive." This refers to the vastness of the universe and the abundance of opportunities available to humanity.
  • viśve: This means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the cosmos.
  • yajate: Translates to "worship" or "offer." This indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in acknowledging and honoring the divine forces.
  • ni pātaṁ: This phrase means "may we not fall" or "may we not be led astray." It expresses a desire for protection and guidance from the divine.
  • dhiyā: Meaning "with wisdom" or "with understanding." This emphasizes the importance of knowledge and insight in navigating life.
  • syāma: This translates to "may we be" or "let us be." It is a call for collective well-being and harmony.
  • rathyaḥ: Meaning "on the chariot" or "in the vehicle." This symbolizes the journey of life and the means by which we navigate our experiences.
  • sadāsāḥ: This means "always" or "eternally." It signifies the enduring nature of divine support and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the vastness of the universe and the nurturing forces that guide us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of uncertainty or decision-making, reminding us to seek wisdom and remain connected to the greater cosmic order. By acknowledging the divine and offering our gratitude, we can cultivate a sense of joy and fulfillment in our journey, ensuring that we navigate life with wisdom and purpose.
