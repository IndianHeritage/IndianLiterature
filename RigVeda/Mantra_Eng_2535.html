ये वृक्ष्णासो अधि क्षमि निमितासो यतस्रुचः |ते नो व्यन्तु वार्यं देवत्रा क्षेत्रसाधसः ||
ye vṛkṇāso adhi kṣami nimitāso yatasrucaḥ | te no vyantu vāryaṁ devatrā kṣetrasādhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • vṛkṇāso: who are strong or powerful
    • adhi: above or over
    • kṣami: who are capable or able
    • nimitāso: who are the cause or reason
    • yatasrucaḥ: from whom the offerings or sacrifices arise
    • te: they
    • no: for us
    • vyantu: may they come or approach
    • vāryaṁ: the powerful or the strong
    • devatrā: by the divine or the gods
    • kṣetrasādhasaḥ: in the field of action or in the realm of activity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful beings or forces that are above and capable of influencing our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine intervention and support in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "may they come for us" suggests a plea for assistance and guidance from these powerful entities.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for help in challenging situations, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities often come together to seek divine blessings for safety and recovery.
    • Individuals facing significant life changes, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city, may seek guidance through prayer or meditation, hoping for the support of higher powers.
    • In various cultures, rituals and offerings are made to invoke the favor of deities, reflecting the belief in divine assistance in human affairs.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking support from higher powers in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there are forces beyond our understanding that can aid us.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to remain open to guidance and assistance, whether through spiritual practices, community support, or personal reflection.
    • Ultimately, it reinforces the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine influence in achieving success and overcoming challenges.
