तिरः पुरू चिद अश्विनां राजांस्य आङ्गूषो वाम मगवानां जनेषु |एह यातम पथिभिर देवयानैर दसराव इमे वां निधयो मधूनाम ||
tiraḥ purū cid aśvinā rajāṁsy āṅgūṣo vām maghavānā janeṣu | eha yātam pathibhir devayānair dasrāv ime vāṁ nidhayo madhūnām ||
  • tiraḥ purū cid aśvinā: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Ashvins, who are the divine physicians, may you remove the obstacles." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and ability to bring light and clarity.
  • rajāṁsy āṅgūṣo vām maghavānā janeṣu: This translates to "May you bestow upon us the wealth and prosperity that is rightfully ours." Here, "rajāṁsy" refers to wealth or riches, and "maghavān" signifies the powerful or the mighty, indicating a plea for divine assistance in attaining prosperity.
  • eha yātam pathibhir devayānair: This means "May we be guided on the path of the divine." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path, guided by divine wisdom and support.
  • dasrāv ime vāṁ nidhayo madhūnām: This can be interpreted as "May we receive the treasures of sweetness and abundance." "Madhūnām" refers to sweetness, which can symbolize the joys and pleasures of life, while "nidhayo" indicates treasures or riches.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for guidance, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles. It reflects the human desire for health, wealth, and divine support in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be recited during times of uncertainty or when embarking on new ventures, such as starting a business or entering a new phase in life. It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and to remain open to the abundance that life has to offer.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of health, wealth, and divine guidance. By invoking the Ashvins, one seeks not only material prosperity but also spiritual clarity and support. It encourages individuals to trust in the divine path and to recognize the treasures of life that come from both material and spiritual abundance.
