यद अन्यासु वृषभो रोरवीति सो अन्यस्मिन यूथे न दधाति रेतः |स हि क्षपावान स भगः स राजा महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
yad anyāsu vṛṣabho roravīti so anyasmin yūthe ni dadhāti retaḥ | sa hi kṣapāvān sa bhagaḥ sa rājā mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • anyāsu: in others
    • vṛṣabho: the bull (symbolizing strength)
    • roravīti: roars or cries
    • so: he
    • anyasmin: in another
    • yūthe: in the group
    • ni dadhāti: does not place
    • retaḥ: the essence or energy
    • sa: he
    • hi: indeed
    • kṣapāvān: the one who brings light (the sun)
    • sa bhagaḥ: he is the blessed one
    • sa rājā: he is the king
    • mahad: great
    • devānām: of the gods
    • asuratvam: the essence of the divine
    • ekam: one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and essence of a powerful entity, often interpreted as a divine force or the sun.
    • The "bull" symbolizes strength and vitality, which can be seen in real life as the determination and resilience of individuals or communities.
    • The phrase "does not place the essence in another group" suggests the importance of maintaining one's unique identity and energy, akin to how a person should stay true to their values and beliefs despite external pressures.
    • The reference to the "king" and "blessed one" indicates a leadership quality that is revered and respected, much like how effective leaders in society inspire and guide others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader who embodies the qualities of the "bull" can motivate their team to overcome challenges, much like how a bull leads a herd.
    • In personal life, individuals who stay true to their principles, even when faced with peer pressure, exemplify the essence of this mantra.
    • Communities that come together to support one another during crises reflect the unity and strength described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, identity, and leadership in both personal and communal contexts.
    • It serves as a reminder to harness one's inner strength and remain steadfast in the face of adversity.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and a strong sense of community.
