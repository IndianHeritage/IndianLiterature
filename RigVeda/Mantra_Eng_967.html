एतत् त्यान न योजanam आचेति सस्वर ह यन् मरुतो गोतमो वः |पश्यन् हिरण्यचक्रान अयोदंष्ट्रान विधावतो वराहून ||
etat tyan na yojanam aceti sasvar ha yan maruto gotamo vaḥ | paśyan hiraṇyacakrān ayodaṁṣṭrān vidhāvato varāhūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etat: this
    • tyan: that
    • na: not
    • yojanam: plan or intention
    • aceti: does not understand
    • sasvar: with sound or voice
    • ha: indeed
    • yan: which
    • maruto: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • gotamo: the one who is known or recognized
    • vaḥ: you (plural)
    • paśyan: seeing
    • hiraṇyacakrān: golden wheels or circles
    • ayodaṁṣṭrān: unassailable or invincible
    • vidhāvato: of the one who moves or runs
    • varāhūn: the boar (a symbol of strength)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of understanding and recognizing the divine forces at play in the universe.
    • The phrase "etat tyan na yojanam aceti" suggests that one who does not comprehend the divine plan is missing out on a significant aspect of existence.
    • The reference to the Maruts indicates the powerful forces of nature, which can be seen as both destructive and creative.
    • The "hiraṇyacakrān" symbolizes prosperity and abundance, while "ayodaṁṣṭrān" refers to the invincible nature of these forces.
    • The mention of "varāhūn" (the boar) can be interpreted as a representation of strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, understanding the forces of nature can be likened to recognizing the impact of climate change. Just as the Maruts represent natural forces, we must acknowledge and adapt to these changes.
    • The concept of "hiraṇyacakrān" can be related to the pursuit of wealth and success in business. Those who understand the market dynamics (the divine plan) are more likely to succeed.
    • The resilience symbolized by "varāhūn" can be seen in individuals who overcome personal challenges, drawing strength from their experiences to emerge stronger.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding the divine forces and plans that govern our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be aware of their surroundings and the natural world, fostering a deeper connection with the universe.
    • In practical terms, this understanding can lead to better decision-making, resilience in adversity, and a more profound appreciation for the interconnectedness of life.
