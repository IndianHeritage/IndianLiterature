मन्त्र: सोम यास ते मयोभुव उतयः सन्ति दाशुषे |ताभिर नोऽविता भव ||
soma yās te mayobhuva ūtayaḥ santi dāśuṣe | tābhir no 'vitā bhava ||
  • Mantra: "soma yās te mayobhuva ūtayaḥ santi dāśuṣe | tābhir no 'vitā bhava ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Soma: Refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • yās: Means "who" or "that which."
    • te: Means "your" or "of you."
    • mayobhuva: Translates to "may I be endowed with your essence" or "may I be filled with your qualities."
    • ūtayaḥ: Refers to "uplifting" or "elevating."
    • santi: Means "exist" or "are."
    • dāśuṣe: Refers to "for the devotee" or "for the one who serves."
    • tābhir: Means "with these" or "by these."
    • no: Means "us" or "to us."
    • avitā: Means "not deprived" or "not lacking."
    • bhava: Means "become" or "may it be."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine blessings and inspiration, invoking the essence of Soma, which symbolizes spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The speaker seeks to be filled with the uplifting qualities of Soma, which can be understood as a desire for wisdom, strength, and clarity in life.
    • The phrase "for the devotee" emphasizes the importance of service and devotion in receiving these blessings.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be seen as a call for personal growth and the pursuit of knowledge, akin to how students seek inspiration from their teachers or mentors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the pursuit of spiritual and intellectual elevation through divine blessings.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during moments of self-reflection, in educational settings, or when seeking guidance in challenging situations.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of openness to learning and growth, fostering a deeper connection with their inner selves and the world around them.
