प्र देवम् अच्‍छा मधुमन्त इन्दवो 'सिष्यदन्त गावा आ न धेनवः |बरहिषदो वचनावन्त ऊधभिः परिसृतम् उष्रियं निर्णिजं धिरे ||
pra devam acchā madhumanta indavo 'siṣyadanta gāva ā na dhenavaḥ | barhiṣado vacanāvanta ūdhabhiḥ parisrutam usriyā nirṇijaṁ dhire ||
  • pra devam acchā madhumanta indavo: This phrase can be translated as "O divine ones, who are sweet like honey." Here, "devam" refers to the divine or celestial beings, while "madhumanta" signifies sweetness, often associated with the richness of life and abundance.
  • siṣyadanta gāva ā na dhenavaḥ: This part means "may the cows, the givers of nourishment, not be deprived." Cows are revered in Vedic culture as symbols of wealth and sustenance, representing the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • barhiṣado vacanāvanta: This translates to "those who speak with wisdom." "Barhiṣado" refers to the wise or learned individuals, emphasizing the importance of knowledge and guidance in life.
  • ūdhabhiḥ parisrutam usriyā: This can be interpreted as "may we be uplifted by the words of the wise." It highlights the significance of listening to and learning from those who possess wisdom and experience.
  • nirṇijaṁ dhire: This phrase means "with a steady mind." It suggests the importance of maintaining composure and clarity in thought, especially when faced with challenges.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, nourishment, wisdom, and mental steadiness. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where spiritual, physical, and intellectual aspects are interconnected.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in agriculture, the reverence for cows can translate into sustainable farming practices that respect animal welfare. In personal development, seeking wisdom from mentors and maintaining a calm mind can lead to better decision-making and emotional resilience.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance, appreciate the resources provided by nature, value wisdom, and cultivate a steady mind. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a more balanced and fulfilling life.
