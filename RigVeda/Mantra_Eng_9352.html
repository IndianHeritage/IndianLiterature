महत् तन् नाम गुह्यं पुरुष्पृग येन भूतं जनयो येन भव्यम् |प्रत्नं जातं ज्योतिर यद अस्य प्रियं प्रियाः सम अविशन्त पञ्च ||
mahat tan nāma guhyam puruspṛg yena bhūtaṁ janayo yena bhavyam | pratnaṁ jātaṁ jyotir yad asya priyam priyāḥ sam aviśanta pañca ||
  • mahat tan nāma guhyam: This phrase translates to "the great name is secret." It suggests that there is a profound and sacred essence in the name of the divine or the ultimate reality. In real life, this can be likened to the idea that true understanding or enlightenment often lies beyond mere words and requires deeper contemplation.
  • puruspṛg yena bhūtaṁ janayo yena bhavyam: This means "by which the being is born and by which the future is created." It emphasizes the connection between existence and creation. For example, in the context of parenting, the values and teachings imparted by parents shape not only the present but also the future of their children.
  • pratnaṁ jātaṁ jyotir: This translates to "the light that is born from the inquiry." It highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding. In modern life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and self-improvement, where individuals seek to illuminate their paths through learning.
  • yad asya priyam priyāḥ sam aviśanta pañca: This means "the five beloved ones enter into it." This can be interpreted as the idea that love and connection are fundamental to existence. In real life, this can be reflected in the relationships we build with family, friends, and community, which enrich our lives and provide support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of existence, creation, knowledge, and love. Its significance lies in the understanding that the divine or ultimate reality is both profound and accessible through inquiry and relationships. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek deeper meanings, foster connections, and recognize the sacredness in the act of creation and existence. It serves as a reminder that our actions and relationships shape not only our present but also the future, urging us to live with intention and awareness.
