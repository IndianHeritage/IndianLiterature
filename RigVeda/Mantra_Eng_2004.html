त्रिः सप्त मयूर्यः सप्त स्वसारो अग्रुवः |तास ते विषं वि जभ्रिर उदकं कुम्भिनीर इव ||
triḥ sapta mayūryaḥ sapta svasāro agruvaḥ | tās te viṣaṁ vi jabhrira udakaṁ kumbhinīr iva ||
  • Mantra: "triḥ sapta mayūryaḥ sapta svasāro agruvaḥ | tās te viṣaṁ vi jabhrira udakaṁ kumbhinīr iva ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • triḥ: three
    • sapta: seven
    • mayūryaḥ: peacocks (symbolizing beauty and grace)
    • svasāro: sisters or close ones
    • agruvaḥ: those who are strong or powerful
    • tās: those
    • te: your
    • viṣaṁ: poison or negativity
    • vi: away or apart
    • jabhrira: to remove or to dispel
    • udakaṁ: water (symbolizing purity and life)
    • kumbhinīr: like a pot or vessel
    • iva: like or as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interplay between strength, beauty, and the removal of negativity.
    • The mention of "three" and "seven" can symbolize completeness and the cycles of life, as both numbers hold significant meanings in various cultures.
    • The "mayūryaḥ" or peacocks represent grace and beauty, suggesting that one should embrace these qualities in life.
    • The term "svasāro" emphasizes the importance of relationships and support systems, highlighting that our close ones can provide strength.
    • The mantra suggests that through the power of unity and beauty, one can dispel negativity ("viṣaṁ") and purify their surroundings ("udakaṁ").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when individuals come together (like the "svasāro"), they can create a supportive environment that fosters positivity and dispels negativity.
    • In personal development, embracing qualities of beauty and grace (represented by "mayūryaḥ") can lead to a more fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to express themselves authentically.
    • In conflict resolution, the idea of removing negativity can be applied by fostering open communication and understanding, akin to "jabhrira" dispelling "viṣaṁ".
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, beauty, and strength in overcoming challenges and negativity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that by nurturing our relationships and embracing positive qualities, we can create a harmonious environment for ourselves and others.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through community engagement, personal growth, and conflict resolution strategies.
