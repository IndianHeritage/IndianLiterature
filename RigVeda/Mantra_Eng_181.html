त्वं तं ब्रह्मणस पते सोम इन्द्रश्च मार्त्यम् |दक्षिणा पात्व अंहसः ||
tvaṁ tam brahmaṇas pate soma indraś ca martyam | dakṣiṇā pātv aṁhasaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ: This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • tam: Meaning "that," it refers to the ultimate reality or the supreme being.
  • brahmaṇas: This term means "of the Brahman," which signifies the cosmic spirit or the ultimate source of all existence.
  • pate: Translated as "lord" or "master," it denotes authority and power over the universe.
  • soma: Referring to the sacred drink or plant, soma is associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • indraś: This is the genitive form of Indra, the king of gods, representing strength, power, and the warrior spirit.
  • ca: Meaning "and," it connects the various elements of the mantra.
  • martyam: This word means "mortal" or "human," highlighting the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • dakṣiṇā: Translated as "south," it can symbolize prosperity and auspiciousness.
  • pātv: Meaning "may you drink," it implies a request or invocation for the divine to partake in the offerings.
  • aṁhasaḥ: This term refers to "the essence" or "the vital energy," indicating the life force that sustains all beings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine, asking for blessings and strength. It acknowledges the connection between the divine (Brahman) and the mortal (martyam), emphasizing the importance of divine guidance in human life. The mention of Indra and soma highlights the need for strength and inspiration in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Guidance: Just as the mantra invokes the divine for support, individuals can seek guidance in challenging situations, such as during exams, job interviews, or personal crises.
  • Celebrating Achievements: The reference to soma can be likened to celebrating milestones in life, such as weddings or graduations, where one seeks blessings for continued success and happiness.
  • Fostering Strength: The invocation of Indra symbolizes the need for courage and strength in facing life's challenges, applicable in both personal and professional contexts.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine connection in human life, emphasizing the need for strength, guidance, and inspiration. It serves as a reminder that while we navigate our mortal existence, we can seek support from the divine to achieve our goals and overcome obstacles. By incorporating this understanding into our daily lives, we can foster a sense of purpose and resilience.
