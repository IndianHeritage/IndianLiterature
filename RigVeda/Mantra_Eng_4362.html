प्र ये दिवो बृहतः श्रिण्विरे गिरा सुशुक्वानः सुभ्व एवयामरुत् |न येषाम इरी सदस्थ īषṭा आम̐ अग्नयो न स्वविद्युतः प्र स्यन्द्रासो धुनीनाम् ||
pra ye divo bṛhataḥ śṛṇvire girā suśukvānaḥ subhva evayāmarut | na yeṣām irī sadhastha īṣṭa ām̐ agnayo na svavidyutaḥ pra syandrāso dhunīnām ||
  • Translation: "Those who listen to the great words from the heavens, who are well-versed in the beautiful sounds, O Maruts, may we be blessed by you. Those who are not present in the assembly of the wise, may they not be like the lightning that does not shine among the clouds."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pra ye: Those who
    • divo: of the heavens
    • bṛhataḥ: great or vast
    • śṛṇvire: listen or hear
    • girā: words or sounds
    • suśukvānaḥ: well-versed or skilled
    • subhva: beautiful or auspicious
    • evayāmarut: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • na yeṣām: those who are not
    • irī: present or in the assembly
    • sadhastha: wise or knowledgeable
    • īṣṭa: may they not be
    • ām̐: like
    • agnayo: the lightning or fire
    • na svavidyutaḥ: that does not shine
    • pra syandrāso: among the clouds
    • dhunīnām: of the storm or the powerful
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of listening to wise counsel and the teachings of those who are knowledgeable. For instance, in a corporate setting, employees who actively listen to their mentors and leaders are more likely to succeed and contribute positively to the organization.
    • In educational environments, students who pay attention to their teachers and engage in discussions are more likely to grasp complex concepts and excel academically.
    • The reference to the Maruts can be seen as a metaphor for the forces of nature and the importance of respecting and understanding them, which is crucial in fields like environmental science and meteorology.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for attentiveness to wisdom and the consequences of neglecting it. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, listen to those who guide us, and be present in the pursuit of understanding. It encourages individuals to be part of a community that values wisdom and to avoid isolation from the collective knowledge that can illuminate our paths, much like the lightning that shines brightly among the clouds. This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, education, and professional growth, highlighting the importance of collaboration and learning from others.
