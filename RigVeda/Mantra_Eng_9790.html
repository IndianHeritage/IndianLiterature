इन्द्रो दिव इन्द्र ईशे पृथिव्यां इन्द्रो अपां इन्द्र इत पर्वतानाम् |इन्द्रो वृद्धाम् इन्द्र इन् मेधिराणाम् इन्द्रः क्षेमे योगे हव्य इन्द्रः ||
indro diva indra īśe pṛthivyā indro apām indra it parvatānām | indro vṛdhām indra in medhirāṇām indraḥ kṣeme yoge havya indraḥ ||
  • Indro Diva: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with the sky and heavens. In real life, this can symbolize leadership and authority, akin to a CEO or a leader in a community.
  • Indra Īśe Pṛthivyā: Here, Indra is invoked as the ruler of the earth. This can be related to environmental stewardship, where individuals or organizations take responsibility for the health of the planet.
  • Indro Apām: This part signifies Indra's connection to water, which is essential for life. In practical terms, it highlights the importance of water conservation and management in our daily lives.
  • Indra It Parvatānām: This phrase refers to Indra as the one who resides in the mountains. Mountains can symbolize stability and strength, reminding us of the importance of having a strong foundation in our personal and professional lives.
  • Indro Vṛdhām: This indicates Indra's role in growth and prosperity. In a real-life context, it can be related to personal development and the pursuit of success in various endeavors.
  • Indra In Medhirāṇām: This refers to Indra as the source of wisdom and intelligence. It emphasizes the value of knowledge and education in achieving one's goals.
  • Indraḥ Kṣeme Yoge: This phrase highlights Indra's association with safety and well-being. In everyday life, it can be linked to the importance of creating a safe and supportive environment for ourselves and others.
  • Havya Indraḥ: This signifies the offerings made to Indra, representing gratitude and respect. In practical terms, it can be seen in acts of kindness and appreciation towards others in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the multifaceted nature of Indra, representing leadership, environmental responsibility, the importance of water, stability, growth, wisdom, safety, and gratitude. In real life, these qualities can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to lead with integrity, care for the environment, seek knowledge, and foster a sense of community and support. By embodying these principles, one can contribute positively to society and create a harmonious existence.
