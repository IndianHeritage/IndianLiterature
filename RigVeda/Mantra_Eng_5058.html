प्र चित्रम अर्कं गृणते तुराय मारुताय स्वतवसे भरध्वम् |ये सहांसी सहसा सहन्ते रेजते अग्ने पृथिवी मखभ्यः ||
pra citram arkaṁ gṛṇate turāya mārutāya svatavase bharadhvam | ye sahāṁsi sahasā sahante rejate agne pṛthivī makhebhyaḥ ||
  • Pra citram arkaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "the splendid light" or "the brilliant radiance." In a real-life context, this can refer to the beauty and brilliance of the sun, which is essential for life on Earth.
  • Gṛṇate: This means "to sing" or "to praise." In our daily lives, we often express gratitude and appreciation for the beauty around us, whether through art, music, or simple acts of kindness.
  • Turāya: This term can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick." It signifies the importance of speed and efficiency in our actions, akin to how we strive to achieve our goals in a timely manner.
  • Mārutāya: Referring to the "wind" or "air," this word symbolizes the vital force of nature. In real life, the wind is a powerful element that can bring change, much like how we adapt to new circumstances.
  • Swatavase: This translates to "with one's own strength" or "by one's own power." It emphasizes self-reliance and the importance of personal effort in achieving success.
  • Bharadhvam: This means "to bear" or "to carry." It suggests the idea of responsibility and the need to support one another in our endeavors, much like how communities come together to uplift each other.
  • Ye sahāṁsi: This phrase means "those who are strong" or "those who endure." It highlights resilience and the ability to withstand challenges, which is crucial in both personal and professional life.
  • Sahasā: This translates to "with great effort" or "with strength." It signifies the importance of perseverance and hard work in overcoming obstacles.
  • Sahante: Meaning "they endure" or "they bear," this word reinforces the theme of resilience and the capacity to face difficulties head-on.
  • Rejate: This means "they shine" or "they radiate." It reflects the idea that those who overcome challenges often emerge stronger and more radiant, inspiring others around them.
  • Agne: Referring to "fire," this symbolizes transformation and purification. In real life, fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve one's goals.
  • Pṛthivī: This translates to "the Earth," representing stability and grounding. It reminds us of our connection to nature and the importance of nurturing our environment.
  • Makhebhyaḥ: This means "from sacrifices" or "from offerings." It signifies the value of giving and the idea that our efforts and contributions can lead to greater rewards.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of light, strength, resilience, and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It teaches us to appreciate the beauty around us, to work diligently towards our goals, and to support one another in our journeys. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, personal development, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, we can create a more harmonious and thriving existence for ourselves and our communities.
