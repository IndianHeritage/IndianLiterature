तं सुप्रतीकं सुदृशं स्वञ्चम् अविद्वांसो विदुष्टारं सपेम |स यक्षद विश्व वयुनानि विद्वान् प्र हव्यं अग्निर अमृतेषु वाचत ||
taṁ supratīkaṁ sudṛśaṁ svañcam avidvāṁso viduṣṭaraṁ sapema | sa yakṣad viśvā vayunāni vidvān pra havyam agnir amṛteṣu vocat ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • supratīkaṁ - Meaning "well-known" or "famous," it signifies something that is recognized and revered.
  • sudṛśaṁ - This means "beautiful" or "well-seen," indicating something that is visually appealing or admirable.
  • svañcam - Translates to "self-illuminating" or "self-radiant," suggesting an inherent brilliance or light.
  • avidvāṁso - This means "those who are ignorant" or "the unwise," referring to individuals lacking knowledge or understanding.
  • viduṣṭaraṁ - Meaning "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," it contrasts with the previous term, highlighting the importance of wisdom.
  • sapema - This translates to "may we attain" or "let us achieve," expressing a desire for attainment or success.
  • sa - Meaning "he" or "that," it again refers to the divine or the force being addressed.
  • yakṣad - This means "he who protects" or "he who grants," indicating a benevolent force that provides safety and support.
  • viśvā - Translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and a holistic approach.
  • vayunāni - Meaning "the powers" or "the energies," it refers to the various forces at play in the universe.
  • vidvān - This means "the wise one," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in understanding and navigating life.
  • pra - Meaning "forth" or "out," it indicates a call to action or expression.
  • havyam - This translates to "offering" or "sacrifice," referring to the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • agnir - Meaning "fire," it symbolizes transformation, energy, and purification.
  • amṛteṣu - This means "in the immortal" or "in the eternal," suggesting a connection to the divine or everlasting truths.
  • vocat - Translates to "speaks" or "calls," indicating communication or expression of thoughts.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and understanding in a world filled with ignorance. It calls upon a divine force to guide individuals towards enlightenment and the attainment of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, personal growth, and spiritual development. By recognizing the value of wisdom and striving to attain it, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively and contribute positively to society. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, embrace beauty, and honor the divine forces that guide us towards a higher understanding of existence.
