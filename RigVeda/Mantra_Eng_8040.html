स न ऊर्जे व्यव्ययम् पवित्रं धावा धारया |देवासः श्रिणवन् हि कम ||
sa na ūrje vy a1vyayam pavitraṁ dhāva dhārayā | devāsaḥ śṛṇavan hi kam ||
  • sa na ūrje: This phrase translates to "may we be strong" or "may we have energy." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and strength in our lives.
  • vy a1vyayam: This term means "unfading" or "imperishable." It suggests that true strength and energy should be enduring and not fleeting.
  • pavitraṁ: This word means "pure" or "sacred." It indicates that the strength we seek should be rooted in purity, both in thought and action.
  • dhāva dhārayā: This can be interpreted as "may we be supported" or "may we be upheld." It reflects the idea of seeking support from higher powers or the universe.
  • devāsaḥ: This term refers to "the gods" or "divine beings." It highlights the connection between human efforts and divine assistance.
  • śṛṇavan hi kam: This phrase translates to "who listens to us." It implies that there is a divine presence that is attentive to our prayers and needs.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and purity in our lives while acknowledging the support of divine forces. It encourages individuals to cultivate enduring energy that is rooted in purity and to recognize the presence of the divine that listens to our aspirations and struggles.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by encouraging individuals to focus on their physical and mental well-being, striving for a balanced and healthy lifestyle.
  • In moments of challenge or adversity, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek inner strength and maintain purity of thought and action.
  • It can also be used in meditation or prayer, fostering a connection with the divine and inviting support in personal endeavors.
  • In a broader context, this mantra can inspire communities to work together towards common goals, emphasizing the importance of collective strength and purity in intentions.
