उक्तभृतं सामभृतं बिभर्ति ग्रावाणं बिभ्रत प्र वदात्य अग्रे |उपैनमाध्वं सुमनस्यमानाऽआ वो गच्छाति प्रतृदो वसिष्ठः ||
ukthabhṛtaṁ sāmabhṛtam bibharti grāvāṇam bibhrat pra vadāty agre | upainam ādhvaṁ sumanasyamānā ā vo gacchāti pratṛdo vasiṣṭhaḥ ||
  • ukthabhṛtaṁ: This term refers to the one who holds or possesses the sacred utterance or hymn. In a real-life context, this can be likened to a person who carries wisdom or knowledge, much like a teacher or a mentor who imparts valuable lessons to their students.
  • sāmabhṛtam: This word signifies harmony or balance. In everyday life, this can be seen in relationships where individuals strive for mutual understanding and cooperation, akin to a well-functioning team in a workplace where everyone contributes equally.
  • bibharti: This means to bear or carry. In practical terms, it can represent the responsibilities we take on in our lives, such as a parent caring for their children or a leader guiding their team through challenges.
  • grāvāṇam: This refers to the one who is vocal or speaks out. In a modern context, this can be compared to an advocate or activist who raises their voice for social justice or environmental issues, inspiring others to join the cause.
  • bibhrat: This term means to hold or possess. It can be related to the idea of holding onto one's values or beliefs, much like an individual who stands firm in their convictions despite societal pressures.
  • pra vadāty agre: This phrase translates to "speaks forth in front." It emphasizes the importance of communication and expression. In real life, this can be seen in public speaking or presentations where individuals share their ideas and insights with an audience.
  • upainam: This means to approach or come near. In a practical sense, it can represent the act of seeking knowledge or guidance, similar to a student approaching a teacher for help with a difficult subject.
  • ādhvaṁ: This term signifies a path or journey. In life, this can be likened to the various paths we take in our careers or personal lives, each leading us to different experiences and growth opportunities.
  • sumanasyamānā: This word refers to those who are of good mind or disposition. In real life, this can be associated with individuals who maintain a positive attitude, even in challenging situations, and inspire others to do the same.
  • ā vo gacchāti: This phrase means "may it go to you." It reflects the idea of sharing blessings or good fortune. In a community context, this can be seen in acts of kindness where individuals help one another, fostering a sense of unity and support.
  • pratṛdo: This term refers to the one who is a protector or guardian. In real life, this can be compared to a mentor or a leader who looks out for the well-being of their team or community, ensuring that everyone is safe and supported.
  • vasiṣṭhaḥ: This word signifies the best or the most excellent. It can be related to striving for excellence in our endeavors, whether in academics, sports, or any other field, encouraging individuals to reach their highest potential.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, harmony, responsibility, communication, and the pursuit of excellence in our lives. It encourages individuals to be vocal advocates for positive change, to seek knowledge, and to support one another in their journeys. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as education, leadership, community service, and personal development, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
