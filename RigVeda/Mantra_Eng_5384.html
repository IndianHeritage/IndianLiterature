अहा यद इन्द्र सुदिनā व्युच्छान दधो यत केतुम उपमं समत्सु |न्य अग्निः सीदद असुरो न होतā हुवानो अत्र सुभगाय देवान ||
ahā yad indra sudinā vyucchān dadho yat ketum upamaṁ samatsu | ny a1gniḥ sīdad asuro na hotā huvāno atra subhagāya devān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahā: O!
    • yad: that which
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sudinā: with good fortune
    • vyucchān: having been separated
    • dadho: placed or established
    • yat: that which
    • ketum: the banner or flag
    • upamaṁ: comparable or similar
    • samatsu: in the assembly or gathering
    • ny: not
    • agniḥ: fire
    • sīdad: has become weak
    • asuro: the demon or adversary
    • na: not
    • hotā: the priest or one who invokes
    • huvāno: calling or invoking
    • atra: here
    • subhagāya: for the auspicious
    • devān: the gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, emphasizing the importance of good fortune and the establishment of a strong presence in gatherings.
    • The mention of "vyucchān" suggests a separation or distinction, possibly indicating the need to rise above challenges or adversities.
    • The phrase "ny agniḥ sīdad asuro" implies that the fire (symbolizing energy, passion, or divine presence) should not weaken in the face of adversaries.
    • It highlights the role of the priest or the one who invokes divine blessings, suggesting that their efforts are crucial for the well-being of the community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be related to leadership. A leader (akin to Indra) must ensure that their team remains motivated and united, especially during challenging times.
    • In personal life, when facing adversities, one can draw strength from this mantra to maintain their passion and energy, ensuring that they do not succumb to negativity.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, invoking the spirit of togetherness and good fortune can help in fostering a positive environment, much like the assembly mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine support and the strength of community in overcoming challenges.
    • It emphasizes the need for leaders and individuals to remain steadfast and invoke positivity, ensuring that adversities do not weaken their resolve.
    • In essence, it teaches us to seek blessings for auspiciousness and to maintain our inner fire against all odds, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to collective endeavors.
