आ नः पूषा पवमानः सुरातयो मित्रो गच्छन्तु वरुणः सजोषसः |बृहस्पतिर मरुतो वायुर अश्विनाः त्वष्टा सविता सुमना सरस्वती ||
ā naḥ pūṣā pavamānaḥ surātayo mitro gacchantu varuṇaḥ sajoṣasaḥ | bṛhaspatir maruto vāyur aśvinā tvaṣṭā savitā suyamā sarasvatī ||
  • ā naḥ pūṣā pavamānaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the nourishing one, the wind, come to us." Here, "pūṣā" refers to nourishment, which can be interpreted as both physical and spiritual sustenance. In real life, this can relate to the importance of seeking nourishment in our lives, whether through food, knowledge, or emotional support.
  • surātayo mitro: This means "may the friends of the gods" or "may the divine friends" come to us. It emphasizes the significance of friendship and community. In modern life, this can be seen in the value of building strong relationships and networks that support us in our endeavors.
  • gacchantu varuṇaḥ sajoṣasaḥ: This translates to "may Varuna, the god of cosmic order, come to us with joy." Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order of the universe. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of justice and ethical living, encouraging individuals to act in harmony with the laws of nature and society.
  • bṛhaspatir maruto vāyur: This phrase refers to "Bṛhaspati, the lord of wisdom, the Maruts (storm gods), and Vayu (the wind god)." Each of these deities symbolizes different aspects of life: wisdom, strength, and movement. In everyday life, this can remind us to seek wisdom in our decisions, harness our inner strength, and remain adaptable to change.
  • aśvinā tvaṣṭā savitā: This means "the Aśvins (divine horsemen), Tvaṣṭā (the divine craftsman), and Savitā (the sun god)." The Aśvins represent health and vitality, Tvaṣṭā symbolizes creativity and craftsmanship, and Savitā embodies illumination and enlightenment. In real life, this can inspire us to pursue health, creativity, and knowledge in our daily lives.
  • suyamā sarasvatī: This translates to "the auspicious Sarasvatī." Sarasvatī is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, and wisdom. This highlights the importance of education and the arts in enriching our lives. In a practical sense, it encourages individuals to engage in learning and creative expression.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of nourishment, friendship, cosmic order, wisdom, health, creativity, and knowledge. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek balance in these areas, fostering a well-rounded and fulfilling existence. By embodying the principles represented in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life that is harmonious, ethical, and enriched with purpose.
