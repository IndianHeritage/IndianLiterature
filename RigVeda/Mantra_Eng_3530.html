को वाम अद्य कर्ते रातहव्य ऊतये वा सुतपेयाय वारकैः |ऋतस्य वा वनुषे पूर्व्याय नमो येमानो अश्विनां ववर्तत ||
ko vām adyā karate rātahavya ūtaye vā sutapeyāya vārkaiḥ | ṛtasya vā vanuṣe pūrvyāya namo yemāno aśvinā vavartat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • vām: You
    • adyā: Today
    • karate: Will do
    • rātahavya: For the night offering
    • ūtaye: For the prosperity
    • vā: Or
    • sutapeyāya: For the one who is well-prepared
    • vārkaiḥ: With the offerings
    • ṛtasya: Of truth
    • vā: Or
    • vanuṣe: For the one who is wise
    • pūrvyāya: To the ancient
    • namo: Salutations
    • yemāno: To those who are engaged
    • aśvinā: To the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • vavartat: Who have come forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically addressing the Ashvins, who are known as the deities of health and medicine in Vedic literature.
    • The speaker is inquiring about who will perform the rituals today for the night offering, seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • The mention of "sutapeyāya" emphasizes the importance of preparation and readiness in achieving one's goals.
    • The reference to "ṛtasya" signifies the adherence to truth and righteousness, which are essential for a fulfilling life.
    • By invoking the ancient wisdom ("pūrvyāya"), the mantra connects the present with the past, highlighting the continuity of knowledge and tradition.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as starting a new project or business. Just as the speaker seeks divine assistance, individuals often seek guidance and support from mentors or advisors.
    • In personal health, the invocation of the Ashvins can be likened to seeking medical help or adopting a healthy lifestyle, emphasizing the importance of being well-prepared and informed.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, the essence of this mantra can be seen in the collective effort to honor traditions and seek blessings for prosperity and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the significance of seeking divine guidance, the importance of preparation, and the adherence to truth in all endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our pursuits, whether personal or communal, we should remain connected to our roots and the wisdom of the past.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to engage actively in their lives, invoking support from higher powers while being grounded in truth and righteousness.
