वि त्वा नरः पुरुत्रा सपर्ण पितुर न जीव्रे वि वेदो भरन्त ||
vi tvā naraḥ purutrā saparyan pitur na jivrer vi vedo bharanta ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi: This prefix often denotes separation or distinction.
    • tvā: Refers to 'you' or 'your', indicating a personal address.
    • naraḥ: Means 'man' or 'human', representing humanity.
    • purutrā: Translates to 'in many ways' or 'in various forms', suggesting diversity.
    • saparyan: Refers to 'worship' or 'service', indicating reverence.
    • pitur: Means 'father', symbolizing lineage and respect for ancestors.
    • na: Means 'not', indicating negation.
    • jivrer: Translates to 'living' or 'alive', referring to existence.
    • vi: Again, this prefix emphasizes distinction.
    • vedo: Refers to 'knowledge' or 'wisdom', particularly sacred knowledge.
    • bharanta: Means 'to carry' or 'to bear', indicating responsibility.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between humanity and the divine, emphasizing the importance of worship and respect for one's lineage.
    • It suggests that while humans may worship in various forms, they should not forget the essence of life and the wisdom that guides them.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of honoring one's ancestors and the knowledge passed down through generations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and ceremonies are performed to honor ancestors, reflecting the idea of 'pitur' or respect for lineage.
    • In modern society, individuals often seek wisdom from elders or sacred texts, embodying the concept of 'vedo' or knowledge.
    • Community service and acts of kindness can be seen as forms of 'saparyan', where individuals contribute to the well-being of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity, the importance of honoring our roots, and the pursuit of wisdom.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in acts of service, respect their heritage, and seek knowledge that enriches their lives.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes that while we may worship in diverse ways, the core values of respect, wisdom, and service remain universal and essential for a harmonious existence.
