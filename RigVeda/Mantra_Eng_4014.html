ज्यायांसम अस्य यतुनस्य केतुन ऋषिस्वरं चरति यासु नाम ते |यादृश्मिन धायि तम अपस्यया विदद य उ स्वयं वहते सो अरं करत ||
jyāyāṁsam asya yatunasya ketuna ṛṣisvaraṁ carati yāsu nāma te | yādṛśmin dhāyi tam apasyayā vidad ya u svayaṁ vahate so araṁ karat ||
  • jyāyāṁsam: This term can be interpreted as "the greater" or "the superior." It signifies something that is elevated or of higher status.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of this particular." It indicates a specific context or subject matter.
  • yatunasya: This refers to "of the beings" or "of the creatures." It encompasses all living entities and their existence.
  • ketuna: This word can be translated as "by the mind" or "by the intellect." It emphasizes the role of consciousness and thought in understanding the world.
  • ṛṣisvaraṁ: This term means "the lord of the sages" or "the divine seer." It refers to a higher power or divine intelligence that guides and oversees.
  • carati: This means "moves" or "travels." It indicates the dynamic nature of existence and the continuous flow of life.
  • yāsu: This translates to "in which" or "in whom." It connects the previous concepts to a specific context or situation.
  • nāma te: This means "your name." It signifies the importance of identity and recognition in the spiritual or divine context.
  • yādṛśmin: This term can be interpreted as "in such a manner" or "in this way." It refers to the nature of existence and the way things unfold.
  • dhāyi: This means "the one who holds" or "the one who supports." It signifies stability and the foundation of existence.
  • tam: This translates to "that" or "him." It refers to the divine or the higher power being addressed.
  • apasyayā: This means "by not seeing" or "by ignorance." It indicates a lack of awareness or understanding.
  • vidad: This term means "to know" or "to understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • ya: This means "who" or "that which." It refers to the subject of the mantra.
  • u: This is a conjunction meaning "and." It connects ideas or concepts.
  • svayaṁ: This means "by oneself" or "self." It emphasizes self-reliance and individual agency.
  • vahate: This translates to "carries" or "bears." It signifies the act of supporting or sustaining.
  • so: This means "he" or "that." It refers to the divine or the higher power once again.
  • araṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way." It signifies the journey of life and existence.
  • karat: This means "to do" or "to act." It emphasizes the importance of action in the world.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine intelligence that guides them. It emphasizes the importance of awareness, knowledge, and action in navigating life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, decision-making, and understanding one's place in the universe. By recognizing the greater forces at play and our role within them, we can lead more meaningful and purposeful lives. This mantra encourages us to seek knowledge, act with intention, and remain aware of the divine presence in our lives.
