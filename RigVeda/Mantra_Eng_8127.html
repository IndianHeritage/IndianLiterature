त्वं इन्दो परि श्रव स्वादिष्टो अङ्गिरोभ्यः | वरिवोविद घृतं पयः ||
tvam indo pari srava svādiṣṭho aṅgirobhyaḥ | varivovid ghṛtam payaḥ ||
  • tvam indo: This phrase translates to "You, O Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can symbolize the power of nature and the importance of respecting and understanding natural forces.
  • pari srava: This means "surrounding or encompassing." It suggests a sense of protection and support. In our lives, this can be related to the idea of community and the support systems we build around ourselves, such as family and friends.
  • svādiṣṭho: This translates to "well-chosen" or "well-placed." It implies the importance of making wise decisions. In practical terms, this can be seen in career choices or personal relationships where thoughtful decisions lead to positive outcomes.
  • aṅgirobhyaḥ: This refers to "from the Angiras," a group of sages known for their wisdom. This highlights the value of knowledge and learning from those who are wise. In everyday life, this can be applied by seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals.
  • varivovid: This means "to know the essence" or "to understand deeply." It emphasizes the importance of insight and comprehension. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of education and the quest for deeper understanding in various fields, such as science or philosophy.
  • ghṛtam payaḥ: This translates to "ghee and milk." Both are considered sacred and nourishing substances in Vedic culture. In a modern context, this can symbolize the importance of nutrition and the need to nourish both the body and the mind for overall well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, wisdom, and nourishment in life. It emphasizes the importance of community support, wise decision-making, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by fostering strong relationships, making informed choices, and prioritizing both physical and mental health. The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to living a balanced and fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to draw from the wisdom of the past while nurturing their present and future.
