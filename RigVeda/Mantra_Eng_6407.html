उत त्या दैव्याः भीषजा शं नः करतो अश्विनाः |युयुयाताम इतो रपो अप श्रिधः ||
uta tyā daivyā bhiṣajā śaṁ naḥ karato aśvinā | yuyuyātām ito rapo apa sridhaḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • tyā - This means "by you" or "through you," referring to a divine or higher power.
  • daivyā - This translates to "divine" or "heavenly," suggesting a connection to the celestial or spiritual realm.
  • bhiṣajā - This word means "healers" or "physicians," indicating those who provide health and well-being.
  • śaṁ - This translates to "peace" or "well-being," emphasizing a state of harmony and health.
  • naḥ - This means "for us," indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • karato - This translates to "of our actions," referring to the deeds or efforts of individuals.
  • aśvinā - This refers to the "Aśvins," the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and benevolence.
  • yuyuyātām - This means "may they bring" or "may they bestow," indicating a desire for blessings or gifts.
  • ito - This translates to "from here" or "from this place," suggesting a point of origin or a request for intervention.
  • rapo - This means "water," which is often symbolic of life, nourishment, and purification.
  • apa - This translates to "away" or "from," indicating a movement or removal of something undesirable.
  • sridhaḥ - This means "prosperity" or "wealth," referring to abundance and well-being.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for divine healing and well-being, invoking the Aśvins to bless us with health and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of divine intervention in our lives, particularly in matters of health and wellness.


In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as:

  • During times of illness, individuals may recite this mantra to seek healing and support from higher powers.
  • In a community setting, it can be used in rituals or gatherings to invoke blessings for collective health and prosperity.
  • In personal meditation or reflection, one might use this mantra to focus on the importance of well-being and the role of divine forces in achieving it.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving health and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the blessings we seek. By invoking the Aśvins, we acknowledge the need for support beyond ourselves, fostering a sense of community and reliance on higher powers in our journey towards well-being.
