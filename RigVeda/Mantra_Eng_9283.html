स्वायुधं स्ववसं सुनीथं चतुःसमुद्रं धरणं रयीणाम् |चर्कृत्यं शंस्यम् भूर्वारम् अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयं दाह् ||
svāyudhaṁ svavasaṁ sunīthaṁ catuḥsamudraṁ dharuṇaṁ rayīṇām | carkṛtyaṁ śaṁsyam bhūrivāram asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • svāyudhaṁ: This term translates to "armed with one's own weapons." It signifies self-reliance and the importance of being prepared to face challenges in life.
  • svavasaṁ: Meaning "one's own strength," this emphasizes the value of personal power and the ability to harness one's inner resources to overcome obstacles.
  • sunīthaṁ: This word means "well-directed" or "well-guided." It suggests the importance of having a clear purpose and direction in life, akin to navigating a ship through stormy seas.
  • catuḥsamudraṁ: Translated as "four oceans," this phrase symbolizes vastness and abundance. It can represent the limitless opportunities and resources available to us.
  • dharuṇaṁ: This means "strong" or "firm." It highlights the necessity of resilience and steadfastness in the face of adversity.
  • rayīṇām: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the material and spiritual abundance that one can achieve through hard work and dedication.
  • carkṛtyaṁ: This translates to "performing actions" or "engaging in deeds." It emphasizes the importance of taking action and being proactive in life.
  • śaṁsyam: Meaning "praise" or "commendation," this word suggests the value of recognition and appreciation for one's efforts and achievements.
  • bhūrivāram: This term means "great abundance" or "plenty." It signifies the blessings and bounties that come from hard work and righteous living.
  • asmabhyaṁ: Translated as "to us," it indicates a collective aspect, emphasizing community and shared prosperity.
  • citraṁ: This means "varied" or "colorful," symbolizing diversity and the richness of experiences in life.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term refers to "strength" or "power," highlighting the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • rayiṁ: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it reiterates the mantra's focus on abundance.
  • dāḥ: This translates to "give" or "bestow," emphasizing the act of sharing and generosity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of self-reliance, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity through well-directed actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to harness our inner resources, remain resilient, and take proactive steps towards our goals. It encourages us to appreciate the abundance around us and to share our blessings with others. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and professional endeavors, where the focus is on collective growth and the importance of individual contributions to the greater good.
