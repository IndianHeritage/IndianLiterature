मां नो वधीर इन्द्र मां परा दा मां नः प्रिय भोजना प्र मोषीः |आण्डा मां नो मगवञ्च चक्र निर भेन मां नः पात्रा भेत्त सहजानुशाणि ||
mā no vadhīr indra mā parā dā mā naḥ priyā bhojanāni pra moṣīḥ | āṇḍā mā no maghavañ chakra nir bhen mā naḥ pātrā bhet sahajānuṣāṇi ||
  • mā no vadhīr indra: This phrase translates to "O Indra, do not harm us." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. In real life, this can be interpreted as a plea for protection from negative forces or harmful influences, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • mā parā dā: This means "do not give us anything that is beyond our reach." It reflects the human desire for balance and moderation in life. For example, in a professional setting, it can be seen as a call for realistic expectations and achievable goals, rather than overwhelming demands that can lead to stress and burnout.
  • mā naḥ priyā bhojanāni pra moṣīḥ: This translates to "do not take away our beloved sustenance." Here, "sustenance" can refer to not just food, but also emotional and spiritual nourishment. In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of maintaining relationships and connections that provide support and joy, such as family and friends.
  • āṇḍā mā no maghavañ chakra: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Maghavan, do not let our circle (or cycle) be broken." Maghavan is another name for Indra, and the "circle" can symbolize community and unity. In real life, this emphasizes the importance of community support and collaboration, whether in a neighborhood, workplace, or social group.
  • nir bhen mā naḥ pātrā bhet sahajānuṣāṇi: This means "do not let our vessels be broken." Vessels can symbolize our capacity to hold and share experiences, resources, and emotions. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of maintaining our mental and emotional health, ensuring that we are not overwhelmed or depleted by life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for protection, balance, sustenance, community, and emotional resilience. Its significance lies in its universal appeal to the human condition, reminding us to seek harmony in our lives and to cherish the connections that nourish us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions, encouraging us to foster supportive networks and maintain a balanced approach to life's challenges.
