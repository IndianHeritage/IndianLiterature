स वीरो अप्रतिष्कुत इन्द्रेण शूषुवे नृभिः |यस् ते गभीराऽसवनानि वृत्रहन् सुनोत्य आ च धावति ||
sa vīro apratiṣkuta indreṇa śūśuve nṛbhiḥ | yas te gabhīrā savanāni vṛtrahan sunoty ā ca dhāvati ||
  • sa vīro apratiṣkuta indreṇa śūśuve nṛbhiḥ
    • sa vīro: "He is a hero" - This refers to a person of great strength and valor, often seen as a protector or leader.
    • apratiṣkuta: "unconquered" - This signifies someone who remains undefeated, embodying resilience and determination.
    • indreṇa: "by Indra" - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing power, rain, and thunder.
    • śūśuve: "has been born" - This indicates the emergence or manifestation of strength and heroism.
    • nṛbhiḥ: "by men" - This suggests that the heroism is recognized and celebrated by humanity.
  • yas te gabhīrā savanāni vṛtrahan sunoty ā ca dhāvati
    • yas te: "who" - Referring to the hero or the divine force being addressed.
    • gabhīrā: "deep" - This can imply profound wisdom or strength that is not easily visible.
    • savanāni: "offerings" - This refers to the rituals and sacrifices made to honor the divine.
    • vṛtrahan: "slayer of Vritra" - Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology, symbolizing obstacles or chaos. The slaying of Vritra represents overcoming challenges.
    • sunoty: "brings forth" - This indicates the act of producing or manifesting something beneficial.
    • ā ca dhāvati: "and runs forth" - This suggests a dynamic action, indicating movement towards a goal or purpose.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the qualities of heroism, resilience, and the divine support that aids in overcoming challenges. It highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring the strength within ourselves and the divine forces that guide us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal challenges, where one must embody the spirit of a hero to overcome obstacles.
  • In leadership roles, where the qualities of strength and resilience inspire others to follow.
  • In community rituals and celebrations, where offerings are made to honor the divine and seek blessings for strength and success.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the inner strength we possess and the support we receive from the universe in our endeavors.
