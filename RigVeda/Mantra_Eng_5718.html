युवोः श्रीयम् परि योषावृणीत सूरो दुहितā परितकम्यायाम् |यद देवयन्तम् अवथः शचीभिः परि घ्रंसम् ओमनā वां वयो गात् ||
yuvoḥ śriyam pari yoṣāvṛṇīta sūro duhitā paritakmyāyām | yad devayantam avathaḥ śacībhiḥ pari ghraṁsam omanā vāṁ vayo gāt ||
  • yuvoḥ: This word refers to "youth" or "young ones," symbolizing vitality, energy, and potential.
  • śriyam: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," indicating not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • pari: Meaning "around" or "in all directions," it suggests a holistic approach to life and prosperity.
  • yoṣāvṛṇīta: This can be interpreted as "the one who chooses or selects," often referring to the divine or a higher power making choices for the benefit of humanity.
  • sūro: This word means "hero" or "brave one," representing strength and courage in the face of challenges.
  • duhitā: Translated as "daughter," it symbolizes nurturing, care, and the importance of feminine energy in creation and sustenance.
  • paritakmyāyām: This term can be understood as "in the midst of," indicating a presence that surrounds and supports.
  • yad: Meaning "that which," it introduces a clause that connects the previous ideas to a broader context.
  • devayantam: This refers to "the divine" or "the godly," emphasizing the connection between the material and spiritual realms.
  • avathaḥ: This means "to protect" or "to guard," highlighting the importance of safeguarding what is valuable.
  • śacībhiḥ: Translated as "with the powerful," it suggests collaboration with those who possess strength and wisdom.
  • pari: Again, this word emphasizes a comprehensive or all-encompassing approach.
  • ghraṁsam: This can be interpreted as "to receive" or "to accept," indicating openness to blessings and opportunities.
  • omanā: Meaning "with the mind" or "thoughtfully," it underscores the importance of intention in actions.
  • vāṁ: This translates to "your," indicating a personal connection to the message.
  • vayo: Meaning "age" or "time," it reflects the passage of time and the wisdom that comes with it.
  • gāt: This means "to go" or "to move forward," symbolizing progress and the journey of life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of youth, prosperity, and divine guidance in achieving a fulfilling life. It highlights the need for courage, nurturing, and collaboration with the powerful to protect and receive blessings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, community building, and spiritual growth. By embodying the qualities of bravery, nurturing, and openness, individuals can navigate life's challenges and create a prosperous existence. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance between material and spiritual wealth, fostering a holistic approach to life.
