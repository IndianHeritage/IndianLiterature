तं त्वाजनन्त मातरः कविं देवासो अंगिरः |हव्यवाहम अमर्त्यम् ||
taṁ tvājananta mātaraḥ kaviṁ devāso aṅgiraḥ | havyavāham amartyam ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • tvā - This means "you," indicating a direct address to the divine or the cosmic principle.
  • ajananta - This term can be interpreted as "born" or "created," suggesting the origin of the divine or the universe.
  • mātaraḥ - This translates to "mothers," symbolizing nurturing, creation, and the source of life.
  • kaviṁ - This means "the seer" or "the wise one," referring to a sage or a divine figure with profound knowledge.
  • devāso - This word means "gods" or "divine beings," indicating the presence of multiple divine entities.
  • aṅgiraḥ - This refers to "Angiras," a group of sages or a specific sage known for wisdom and knowledge.
  • havyavāham - This translates to "the one who carries offerings," symbolizing the act of giving and the importance of rituals.
  • amartyam - This means "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the divine nature that transcends the physical realm.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks to the divine essence that is both the creator and the nurturer of life. It acknowledges the wisdom of the seers and the gods, emphasizing the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the divine. The mention of immortality suggests a deeper understanding of existence beyond the physical world.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor the divine, reflecting the idea of havyavāham. For instance, during festivals, people offer food and prayers to deities, symbolizing gratitude and connection.
    • The concept of mātaraḥ can be seen in the reverence for mothers and mother figures in society, highlighting the nurturing aspect of life and the importance of familial bonds.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to kaviṁ, is evident in educational institutions where individuals seek enlightenment and understanding, much like the sages of ancient times.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nurturing, and the pursuit of wisdom. Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine in everyday life and the importance of rituals in maintaining a connection with the higher powers. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to honor their roots, seek knowledge, and engage in practices that foster a sense of community and spirituality.
