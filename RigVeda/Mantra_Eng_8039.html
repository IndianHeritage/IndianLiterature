घृतं पवस्व धारया यज्ञेषु देववीतमः |अस्मभ्यं वृष्टिम आपव ||
ghṛtam pavasva dhārayā yajñeṣu devavītamaḥ | asmabhyaṁ vṛṣṭim ā pava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ghṛtam: Ghee, a clarified butter used in rituals and offerings.
    • pavasva: May you flow or may you be purified.
    • dhārayā: By holding or sustaining.
    • yajñeṣu: In the sacrifices or rituals.
    • devavītamaḥ: O best of the gods or divine beings.
    • asmabhyaṁ: For us or to us.
    • vṛṣṭim: Rain or abundance.
    • ā pava: May you come or may you bestow.
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the divine to bless the offerings made in rituals with purity and abundance.
    • The use of ghee symbolizes nourishment and the essence of life, which is essential in Vedic rituals.
    • By asking the divine to "flow" and "sustain," the mantra emphasizes the importance of divine grace in our lives.
    • The reference to "yajñeṣu" highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
    • Asking for "vṛṣṭim" signifies a request for rain, which is a metaphor for prosperity and abundance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking rain is crucial for crop growth. This mantra can be recited during rituals to seek divine intervention for favorable weather.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during significant events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, to invite blessings and prosperity.
    • Communities may come together to perform collective rituals, reinforcing social bonds while seeking divine blessings for collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for sustenance and abundance in life.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine's role in our material and spiritual well-being.
    • In contemporary life, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of rituals, community, and the natural world, encouraging individuals to seek harmony and abundance through spiritual practices.
