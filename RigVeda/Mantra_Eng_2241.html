सेमाम अविढ्धि प्रभरितिं य ईशिषे 'या विद्हेम नवान महा गिरा |यथा नो मीढ्वान स्तवते सखा तव बृहस्पते सीषधः सोत नो मति ||
semām aviḍḍhi prabhṛtiṁ ya īśiṣe 'yā vidhema navayā mahā girā | yathā no mīḍhvān stavate sakhā tava bṛhaspate sīṣadhaḥ sota no matim ||
  • Semām aviḍḍhi prabhṛtiṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May we attain the knowledge and wisdom." Here, "semām" refers to knowledge or wisdom, while "aviḍḍhi" implies the act of attaining or achieving. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of education and understanding in various fields, such as science, philosophy, or arts.
  • Ya īśiṣe 'yā vidhema: This part translates to "O Lord, may we know that which is beneficial." The term "īśiṣe" refers to a divine or higher power, while "vidhema" means to know or understand. This can be related to seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who help individuals discern what is truly beneficial in life, such as making ethical decisions or choosing a career path.
  • Navayā mahā girā: This translates to "With new and great utterances." "Navayā" means new, and "mahā girā" refers to great words or expressions. In a contemporary context, this can be seen in the way new ideas and innovations emerge in society, such as technological advancements or new philosophies that challenge traditional beliefs.
  • Yathā no mīḍhvān: This phrase means "As we are praised." "Mīḍhvān" refers to being praised or honored. In real life, this can be associated with recognition in various fields, such as receiving awards for achievements in academics, sports, or community service, which can motivate individuals to strive for excellence.
  • Stavate sakhā tava: This translates to "Your friend praises." "Stavate" means to praise, and "sakhā" refers to a friend. This can be related to the importance of friendship and support systems in our lives, where friends uplift and encourage each other, fostering a positive environment for growth.
  • Bṛhaspate sīṣadhaḥ: This means "O Bṛhaspati, guide us." Bṛhaspati is a deity associated with wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking guidance from wise individuals or spiritual practices that help navigate life's challenges.
  • Sota no matim: This translates to "May we have clarity of thought." "Sota" means to have clarity or understanding, and "matim" refers to thought or intellect. This can be applied in situations where critical thinking and clear decision-making are essential, such as in business or personal relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge, the importance of guidance, and the value of friendship and support in achieving clarity and wisdom. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek out beneficial knowledge, surround ourselves with supportive individuals, and strive for excellence in our endeavors. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's complexities with greater understanding and purpose.
