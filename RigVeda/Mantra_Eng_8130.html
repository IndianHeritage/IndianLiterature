आ पवस्व सहस्रिणं रयिं गोमन्तम अश्विनम् |पुरुश्चन्द्रं पुरुष्पृहम् ||
ā pavasva sahasriṇaṁ rayiṁ gomantam aśvinam | puruścandram puruspṛham ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā pavasva: This can be interpreted as "come forth" or "approach." It signifies an invitation or a call to a divine presence.
    • sahasriṇaṁ: This word means "thousandfold" or "abundant." It suggests a vastness or a multitude of blessings.
    • rayiṁ: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It represents not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
    • gomantam: This term refers to "possessing cows" or "wealthy." In the Vedic context, cows symbolize wealth and nourishment.
    • aśvinam: This refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They are often invoked for their healing powers.
    • puruścandram: This can be interpreted as "the man who is like the moon." It symbolizes beauty, calmness, and a nurturing presence.
    • puruspṛham: This means "the man who is full of desires." It reflects human aspirations and the pursuit of goals.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call for abundance and prosperity, inviting the divine Aśvins to bestow their blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wealth, not just in material terms but also in terms of health and well-being.
    • The reference to the Aśvins highlights the need for healing and support in our lives, especially during challenging times.
    • The imagery of the moon and desires reflects the dual nature of human existence—seeking beauty and fulfillment while navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked during meetings or negotiations to attract prosperity and successful outcomes.
    • Individuals facing health challenges may recite this mantra as a form of meditation, seeking the healing energy of the Aśvins.
    • During festivals or family gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for abundance and harmony within the family.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and well-being, encompassing material, emotional, and spiritual aspects.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our desires and to invite divine support in our endeavors.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, from personal health to professional success, making it a versatile tool for invoking blessings.
