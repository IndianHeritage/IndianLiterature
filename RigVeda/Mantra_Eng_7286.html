चरन् वत्सो रुशन्न इह निदातारं न विन्दते |वेति स्तोतव अम्ब्यम् ||
caran vatso ruśann iha nidātāraṁ na vindate | veti stotava ambyam ||
  • Mantra: "caran vatso ruśann iha nidātāraṁ na vindate | veti stotava ambyam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • caran: moving or walking
    • vatso: calf or young one
    • ruśann: crying or lamenting
    • iha: here or in this world
    • nidātāraṁ: the one who provides or sustains
    • na vindate: does not find or does not attain
    • veti: knows or understands
    • stotava: praises or worships
    • ambyam: to the waters or the divine source
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of a young one (vatso) who, while moving (caran), may cry (ruśann) in this world (iha) when it does not find (na vindate) the sustainer (nidātāraṁ).
    • The second part emphasizes the importance of understanding (veti) and praising (stotava) the divine source (ambyam), which can be interpreted as the waters or the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a real-life scenario, a child (vatso) may feel lost or distressed (ruśann) when separated from their parents or guardians (nidātāraṁ). This reflects the natural instinct to seek comfort and security.
    • Similarly, in times of environmental crisis, communities may lament the loss of natural resources (ruśann) and seek to reconnect with the nurturing aspects of nature (ambyam) through conservation efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing our dependence on nurturing forces in our lives, whether they be familial, societal, or environmental.
    • It teaches us the importance of understanding and appreciating the sources of sustenance and support around us, encouraging a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards nature and our relationships.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in practices that honor and protect the environment, as well as to foster strong, supportive relationships that help individuals thrive.
