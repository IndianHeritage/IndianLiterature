अपाद एति प्रथमā पद्वतीनां कस् तद वाम मित्रावरुणा चिकेत |गर्भो भारम् भरत्य ā चिद अस्य ऋतम् पिपर्त्य अनृतं न तारीत ||
apād eti prathamā padvatīnāṁ kas tad vām mitrāvaruṇā ciketa | garbho bhāram bharaty ā cid asya ṛtam piparty anṛtaṁ ni tārīt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apād - from the foot or base
    • eti - goes or moves
    • prathamā - the first
    • padvatīnāṁ - of those who are on the path
    • kas - who
    • tad - that
    • vām - your
    • mitrāvaruṇā - Mitra and Varuna (deities)
    • ciketa - have understood or perceived
    • garbho - the womb or the essence
    • bhāram - burden
    • bharaty - carries or bears
    • ā - indeed
    • cid - consciousness or awareness
    • asya - of this
    • ṛtam - truth or order
    • piparty - nourishes or sustains
    • anṛtaṁ - falsehood or untruth
    • ni - not
    • tārīt - to be crossed or transcended
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of life and the importance of truth and righteousness.
    • The phrase "apād eti prathamā padvatīnāṁ" suggests that the first step on the path is crucial, emphasizing the importance of beginnings in any endeavor.
    • The mention of "Mitra and Varuna" highlights the divine forces that oversee truth and order in the universe, suggesting that they guide individuals in their actions.
    • "Garbho bhāram bharaty" indicates that the essence or core of existence carries a burden, which can be interpreted as the responsibilities and challenges we face in life.
    • The line "ṛtam piparty anṛtaṁ ni tārīt" reinforces the idea that truth nourishes and sustains life, while falsehood leads to obstacles that must be overcome.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, starting a new project or journey requires careful planning and a strong foundation, akin to the "first step" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In relationships, being truthful and transparent, as guided by Mitra and Varuna, fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
    • In professional settings, carrying the "burden" of responsibilities with integrity and commitment leads to success and respect among peers.
    • In societal contexts, promoting truth and righteousness can lead to a more harmonious community, where falsehoods are recognized and addressed.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth and the responsibilities we carry in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be mindful of their actions and the impact they have on themselves and others.
    • By embracing truth and righteousness, one can navigate life's challenges more effectively and contribute positively to society.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of individual actions and the greater cosmic order, urging us to align ourselves with truth for a fulfilling life.
