प्र ते अशनतु कुक्ष्योः प्रेन्द्र ब्रह्मणा शिरः |प्र बाहू शूर राधसे ||
pra te aśnotu kukṣyoḥ prendra brahmaṇā śiraḥ | pra bāhū śūra rādhase ||
  • pra te aśnotu: This phrase can be translated as "may we attain" or "may we receive." It signifies a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life.
  • kukṣyoḥ: This word refers to the "womb" or "source." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the origin of life and creativity.
  • prendra: This term can be interpreted as "the great one" or "the powerful." It denotes strength and authority, often associated with divine or cosmic forces.
  • brahmaṇā: This word means "by the divine" or "through the ultimate reality." It emphasizes the connection to the universal spirit or consciousness.
  • śiraḥ: This translates to "head" or "intellect." It represents wisdom, knowledge, and the guiding force of thought.
  • pra bāhū: This phrase means "may the arms" or "may the strength." It signifies the importance of physical power and capability in achieving one's goals.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "valiant." It highlights the qualities of courage and heroism, essential for overcoming challenges.
  • rādhase: This term can be interpreted as "for the purpose of prosperity" or "for the sake of abundance." It signifies the pursuit of success and well-being.

In summary, this mantra expresses a profound aspiration for strength, wisdom, and prosperity. It invokes the divine to bless individuals with the necessary qualities to achieve their goals and fulfill their potential. The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of both physical and intellectual strength, as well as the divine guidance that supports our endeavors.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can recite this mantra as a form of meditation or affirmation to cultivate inner strength and wisdom, helping them navigate life's challenges.
  • Teamwork and Leadership: In a professional setting, leaders can use this mantra to inspire their teams, fostering a culture of bravery and collaboration towards common goals.
  • Education: Students can invoke this mantra to seek clarity and focus in their studies, encouraging them to embrace knowledge and intellectual growth.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and divine support in the journey of life, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while remaining grounded in their values.
