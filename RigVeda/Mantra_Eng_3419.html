यच चिद्धि शाश्वताम् असिन्द्र साधारणस् त्वम् |तं त्वा वयं हवामहे ||
yac cid dhi śaśvatām asīndra sādhāraṇas tvam | taṁ tvā vayaṁ havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac: that which
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: intellect or understanding
    • śaśvatām: eternal or everlasting
    • asīndra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sādhāraṇas: common or universal
    • tvam: you
    • taṁ: that
    • tvā: you
    • vayaṁ: we
    • havāmahe: invoke or call upon
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, acknowledging his eternal nature and universal significance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine presence in our lives, recognizing that the divine is not just a distant entity but is intertwined with our understanding and existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and support from a higher power during challenging times, similar to how this mantra invokes Indra for assistance.
    • In moments of decision-making, people may reflect on their values and beliefs, akin to the "intellect" mentioned in the mantra, to guide their choices.
    • Communities often come together in prayer or meditation, invoking a sense of unity and shared purpose, much like the collective invocation in the mantra.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing the divine in our lives and the need for spiritual connection.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are part of a larger universe and that our actions and thoughts can resonate with the eternal.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation practices, or personal reflections to foster a deeper connection with the divine and to seek guidance in our endeavors.
