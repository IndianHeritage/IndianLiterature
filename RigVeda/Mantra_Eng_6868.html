यं त्वा जनासा इन्दते मनुष्वद अङ्गिरस्तम |अग्नि स बोधि मे वचः ||
yaṁ tvā janāsa indhate manuṣvad aṅgirastama | agne sa bodhi me vacaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • janāsa: the people
    • indhate: invoke or call upon
    • manuṣvad: with human-like qualities
    • aṅgirastama: the one who is the best among the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • sa: that
    • bodhi: knowledge or understanding
    • me: my
    • vacaḥ: words or speech
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The speaker is expressing a desire for Agni to grant them wisdom and understanding, which can be interpreted as a plea for guidance in life.
    • The invocation of Agni by the people signifies the collective need for enlightenment and clarity in their actions and thoughts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together to seek guidance on important decisions, they often invoke a higher power or collective wisdom, similar to how this mantra invokes Agni.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek clarity and understanding through meditation or prayer, asking for guidance in their choices, much like the speaker in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from a higher source, which is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, community decision-making, and spiritual practices.
    • By invoking Agni, the mantra highlights the human desire for wisdom and the need for guidance in navigating life's complexities.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that seeking knowledge and clarity is a universal pursuit, relevant in both individual and collective contexts.
