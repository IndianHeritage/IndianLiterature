इमा ब्रह्म बृहत्दिवो विवक्तीन्द्राय शूषम् अग्रियः स्वरषाः |महोगोत्रस्य क्षयति स्वराजो दुरश्च विश्वा अवृणोद अप स्वाह ||
imā brahma bṛhaddivo vivaktīndrāya śūṣam agriyaḥ svarṣāḥ | maho gotrasya kṣayati svarājo duraś ca viśvā avṛṇod apa svāḥ ||
  • imā - This word translates to "these" or "this," indicating a focus on the present or immediate context.
  • brahma - Refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, often associated with the source of all creation.
  • bṛhad - Means "great" or "vast," suggesting the expansive nature of the universe or consciousness.
  • divo - Translates to "of the heavens," indicating a connection to the divine or celestial realms.
  • vivaktī - Means "clarity" or "expression," referring to the articulation of truth or knowledge.
  • ndrāya - Refers to "Indra," the king of gods, symbolizing power and authority.
  • śūṣam - Means "the best" or "the foremost," indicating superiority or excellence.
  • agriyaḥ - Translates to "the foremost" or "the leader," emphasizing leadership qualities.
  • svarṣāḥ - Refers to "the shining" or "radiant," symbolizing enlightenment or divine light.
  • maho - Means "great" or "mighty," reinforcing the theme of vastness and power.
  • gotrasya - Refers to "lineage" or "clan," indicating the importance of heritage and community.
  • kṣayati - Means "to diminish" or "to reduce," suggesting the idea of overcoming obstacles.
  • svarājo - Refers to "the ruler of the heavens," indicating divine governance or authority.
  • duraś - Means "difficult" or "hard," referring to challenges or adversities.
  • ca - Translates to "and," connecting ideas or concepts.
  • viśvā - Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and wholeness.
  • avṛṇod - Means "to cover" or "to protect," suggesting safeguarding or shielding.
  • apa - Translates to "from," indicating a source or origin.
  • svāḥ - A term often used to conclude mantras, invoking blessings or auspiciousness.

This mantra speaks to the vastness of the universe and the divine authority that governs it. It emphasizes the importance of clarity in expression and the pursuit of excellence. The invocation of Indra signifies the need for strength and leadership in overcoming challenges. The mention of lineage highlights the significance of community and heritage in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: In positions of authority, leaders can draw inspiration from the qualities of Indra, striving for clarity and excellence in their decisions.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their lineage and community, recognizing the support systems that help them overcome challenges.
  • Spiritual Pursuits: The mantra encourages seekers to strive for enlightenment and understanding, illuminating their paths with divine wisdom.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for clarity, strength, and community. It serves as a reminder that, despite the challenges we face, we can draw upon the vastness of the universe and the support of our heritage to navigate our journeys. By embodying the qualities of leadership and excellence, we can contribute positively to our communities and the world at large.
