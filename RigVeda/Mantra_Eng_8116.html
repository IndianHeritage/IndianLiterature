पवस्वेन्दो वृषा सुतः कृधī नो यशसो जनैः |विश्वा अप द्विषो जहि ||
pavasvendo vṛṣā sutaḥ kṛdhī no yaśaso jane | viśvā apa dviṣo jahi ||
  • pavasvendo: This term can be broken down into two parts: "pava" meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "sendo," which refers to "the wind" or "the breath." Together, it signifies the pure essence or the vital force that sustains life.
  • vṛṣā: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It often refers to strength, vitality, or the ability to overcome challenges.
  • sutaḥ: This means "son" or "offspring." In a broader sense, it can refer to the results of one's actions or the fruits of one's labor.
  • kṛdhī: This term means "to create" or "to make." It emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence or manifesting one's desires.
  • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our." It indicates a sense of belonging or connection.
  • yaśaso: This word translates to "fame" or "glory." It signifies the recognition and respect one earns through their actions and virtues.
  • jane: This means "people" or "human beings." It refers to the collective society or community.
  • viśvā: This term means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • apa: This means "away" or "from." It indicates a sense of separation or removal.
  • dviṣo: This translates to "enemies" or "those who oppose." It refers to negativity or obstacles that hinder progress.
  • jahi: This means "to conquer" or "to overcome." It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges and adversities.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for strength and purity, asking for the ability to create glory and recognition in the world while overcoming negativity and opposition. It emphasizes the importance of community and the collective strength of humanity in achieving greatness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a source of motivation to pursue their goals, reminding themselves to harness their inner strength and creativity.
  • Community Building: It encourages collaboration and unity among people, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
  • Conflict Resolution: The mantra serves as a reminder to overcome adversities and negativity, promoting peace and understanding in relationships.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength, creativity, and unity among people. It serves as a powerful reminder that by overcoming challenges and working together, individuals can achieve greatness and contribute positively to society. In a world filled with obstacles, this mantra inspires resilience and the pursuit of collective glory.
