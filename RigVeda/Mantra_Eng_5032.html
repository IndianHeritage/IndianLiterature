युवं श्रीभिर दर्शताभिराभिः शुभे पुष्टिमूहतुः सूर्यायाः |प्र वां वयो वपुषेऽनु पप्तन नक्षद वाणी सुश्रुतता धिष्ण्यां वाम ||
yuvaṁ śrībhir darśatābhir ābhiḥ śubhe puṣṭim ūhathuḥ sūryāyāḥ | pra vāṁ vayo vapuṣe 'nu paptan nakṣad vāṇī suṣṭutā dhiṣṇyā vām ||
  • yuvaṁ - This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • śrībhir - This term means "with auspiciousness" or "with prosperity." It indicates the presence of good fortune and blessings.
  • darśatābhir - This can be interpreted as "by the sight" or "by the vision." It emphasizes the importance of perception and understanding in achieving one's goals.
  • ābhiḥ - This word means "with" or "by." It connects the previous concepts, suggesting that youth and auspiciousness come together.
  • śubhe - Translated as "auspicious" or "good," it highlights the positive aspects of life and the importance of good fortune.
  • puṣṭim - This means "nourishment" or "prosperity." It signifies the need for sustenance, both physical and spiritual, to thrive.
  • ūhathuḥ - This word translates to "may they bring" or "may they bestow." It expresses a desire for blessings and positive outcomes.
  • sūryāyāḥ - This refers to "the sun." The sun is often seen as a source of life, energy, and illumination, symbolizing clarity and enlightenment.
  • pra - This means "forward" or "towards." It indicates movement and progress.
  • vāṁ - This translates to "your" or "of you," personalizing the mantra and making it relevant to the individual.
  • vayo - This means "age" or "youth." It emphasizes the importance of being in the prime of life.
  • vapuṣe - This translates to "form" or "body." It signifies the physical aspect of existence.
  • 'anu - This means "after" or "following." It suggests continuity and progression.
  • paptan - This can be interpreted as "having attained" or "having achieved." It signifies accomplishment and success.
  • nakṣad - This word means "star" or "constellation." It symbolizes guidance and direction in life.
  • vāṇī - This translates to "speech" or "voice." It emphasizes the power of communication and expression.
  • suṣṭutā - This means "well praised" or "well spoken." It indicates the importance of positive affirmation and recognition.
  • dhiṣṇyā - This refers to "abode" or "place." It signifies a space of stability and security.
  • vām - This translates to "you" or "your," again personalizing the message.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, auspiciousness, and nourishment in achieving success and prosperity. It highlights the importance of perception, communication, and personal growth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, career advancement, and fostering positive relationships. By embodying the qualities of youth and auspiciousness, individuals can navigate their paths with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
