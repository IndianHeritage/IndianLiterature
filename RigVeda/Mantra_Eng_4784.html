अभूरु वीर गिरवणो महाम् इन्द्र धनें हिते |भरे विटन्तसायकः ||
abhūr u vīra girvaṇo mahām̐ indra dhane hite | bhare vitantasāyyaḥ ||
  • abhūr: This word can be interpreted as "may it be" or "let it be." It signifies a wish or a desire for something to manifest.
  • u: This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
  • vīra: Translated as "hero" or "brave one," it refers to someone who possesses courage and strength.
  • girvaṇo: This term means "of the singers" or "of the poets," indicating those who express thoughts and ideas through words, often in a spiritual or divine context.
  • mahām̐: This means "great" or "mighty," often used to describe a powerful entity or force.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • dhane: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance in various forms, whether material or spiritual.
  • hite: Meaning "for the good" or "beneficial," it emphasizes actions or desires that lead to positive outcomes.
  • bhare: This means "to fill" or "to bestow," suggesting the act of providing or granting something valuable.
  • vitantasāyyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are spread out" or "those who are expansive," referring to the vastness of the universe or the reach of one's influence.

This mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for the blessings of strength, prosperity, and beneficial outcomes. It expresses a desire for the great Indra, the embodiment of power and heroism, to fill our lives with wealth and goodness, while also acknowledging the role of poets and singers in conveying these divine messages.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking courage to face challenges, encouraging them to embody the qualities of a hero in their daily lives.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can use this mantra during gatherings or rituals to invoke blessings for collective wealth and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Creative Expression: Artists and poets may find inspiration in this mantra, using it as a source of motivation to create works that reflect strength and positivity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, prosperity, and the pursuit of goodness. It encourages individuals and communities to strive for greatness while recognizing the importance of creativity and expression in achieving their goals. By invoking the blessings of Indra, one can aspire to fill their life with abundance and positive energy, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal endeavors to communal aspirations.
