वयं घा त्वा सुतावन्त आपो न वृक्तबरहिषः |पवित्रस्य प्रस्रवणेषु वृत्रहन् परि स्तोतार आसते ||
vayaṁ gha tvā sutāvanta āpo na vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | pavitrasya prasravaṇeṣu vṛtrahan pari stotāra āsate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • gha: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • sutāvanta: who are endowed with good offspring
    • āpo: waters
    • na: not
    • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: those who are obstructed or hindered
    • pavitrasya: of the pure
    • prasravaṇeṣu: in the streams or flows
    • vṛtrahan: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • pari: all around
    • stotāra: the one who is praised
    • āsate: they sit or reside
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of the waters, emphasizing their purity and the blessings they bring.
    • The phrase "who are endowed with good offspring" suggests a connection between purity and prosperity, indicating that purity in thoughts and actions leads to fruitful outcomes.
    • The reference to "the slayer of Vritra" symbolizes the triumph of good over evil, where Vritra represents obstacles or challenges in life.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of purity in our lives, both in a physical and spiritual sense, as it is through purity that we can overcome challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, maintaining purity can be seen in practices such as cleanliness, ethical behavior, and mindfulness. For instance, a person who practices honesty and integrity in their work is likely to build trust and respect, leading to better relationships and opportunities.
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can be related to the importance of clean water and its role in sustaining life. Communities that prioritize clean water access often experience better health outcomes and economic growth.
    • On a personal level, individuals facing challenges can draw inspiration from the idea of overcoming obstacles, much like the slaying of Vritra. This can manifest in pursuing education, career goals, or personal development despite difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity and the acknowledgment of divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that by fostering purity in our thoughts, actions, and environments, we can attract positive outcomes and navigate through adversities.
    • In essence, this mantra encourages individuals to seek purity and strength, reinforcing the belief that with divine support, one can conquer obstacles and thrive.
