यम अत्यं इव वाजिनं मृजन्ति योषणो दश |वने क्रीळन्तं अत्यविम ||
yam atyam iva vājinam mṛjanti yoṣaṇo daśa | vane krīḻantam atyavim ||
  • Mantra: "yam atyam iva vājinam mṛjanti yoṣaṇo daśa | vane krīḻantam atyavim ||"
  • Translation: "Those who, like the swift horses, are driven by the women, are the ten who play in the forest."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yam: who
    • atyam: swift or fast
    • iva: like
    • vājinam: horses
    • mṛjanti: are driven or guided
    • yoṣaṇo: by women
    • daśa: ten
    • vane: in the forest
    • krīḻantam: playing
    • atyavim: are driven or guided
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a dynamic interplay between strength and guidance, represented by the swift horses and the women who drive them.
    • The "swift horses" symbolize energy, power, and the potential for rapid movement or progress in life.
    • The "women" represent nurturing, guidance, and the ability to direct that energy effectively.
    • The "ten" could symbolize completeness or a full set of attributes that are necessary for success.
    • The "forest" is a metaphor for the environment or the world in which we navigate, filled with challenges and opportunities.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios, such as a team working together where the leaders (women) guide the energetic members (horses) towards a common goal (playing in the forest).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collaboration between strength and guidance in achieving success.
    • It highlights that while energy and potential are crucial, effective direction and support are equally important.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can apply to various fields such as sports, business, and personal relationships, where teamwork and leadership play vital roles.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that success is not just about individual prowess but also about how we harness and direct that energy towards our goals.
