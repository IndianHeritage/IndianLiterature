तां नः शक्तं पार्थिवस्य महो रायो दिव्यस्य |महि वां क्षत्रं देवेषु ||
tā naḥ śaktam pārthivasya maho rāyo divyasya | mahi vāṁ kṣatraṁ deveṣu ||
  • tā naḥ - "that (which) for us": This phrase indicates a request or a desire for something beneficial or powerful.
  • śaktam - "powerful" or "capable": Refers to strength, energy, or capability that can be harnessed for various purposes.
  • pārthivasya - "of the earthly" or "of the material world": This term relates to the physical realm, encompassing all that is tangible and worldly.
  • maho - "great" or "mighty": Signifies something of immense importance or significance.
  • rāyo - "wealth" or "prosperity": Refers to abundance, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and emotional wealth.
  • divyasya - "of the divine": This word connects the earthly with the celestial, indicating a higher power or divine influence.
  • mahi - "great" or "vast": Similar to "maho," it emphasizes the enormity or significance of what is being referred to.
  • vāṁ - "your" or "of you": This indicates possession or connection to the divine or powerful entity being addressed.
  • kṣatraṁ - "kingdom" or "sovereignty": Refers to authority, governance, and the power to rule, often associated with leadership.
  • deveṣu - "among the gods": This phrase highlights the connection to divine beings or higher powers that influence the material world.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the acquisition of strength and prosperity from both earthly and divine sources. It emphasizes the importance of seeking power and wealth that is not only material but also spiritually significant. The mantra suggests that true prosperity comes from a harmonious relationship between the earthly realm and the divine.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek inner strength and capabilities while also striving for material success. For example, someone pursuing a career can meditate on this mantra to attract opportunities and enhance their skills.
  • Leadership: Leaders can invoke this mantra to inspire their teams, emphasizing the importance of both material success and ethical governance. A business leader might reflect on this mantra to ensure their decisions benefit both the company and the community.
  • Spiritual Growth: Practitioners of spirituality can use this mantra to connect with divine energies, seeking guidance and support in their journey. For instance, during meditation, one might chant this mantra to align their intentions with higher powers.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to power and prosperity. It teaches that true wealth encompasses not just material gains but also spiritual fulfillment and ethical leadership. By integrating the lessons of this mantra into daily life, individuals can strive for a balanced existence that honors both the earthly and the divine.
