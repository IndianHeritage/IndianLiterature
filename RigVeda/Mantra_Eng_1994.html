मन्त्र: नि गावो गोष्ठे असदन नि मृगासो अविक्षत | नि केतवो जनानां न्य अदृष्टा अलिप्सत ||
ni gāvo goṣṭhe asadan ni mṛgāso avikṣata | ni ketavo janānāṁ ny a1dṛṣṭā alipsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • gāvo: cows
    • goṣṭhe: in the herd
    • asadan: sitting
    • ni: not
    • mṛgāso: of the animals
    • avikṣata: looked around
    • ni: not
    • ketavo: the wise
    • janānāṁ: of the people
    • ny: not
    • a1dṛṣṭā: seen
    • alipsata: desired
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the absence of certain qualities or actions among different beings.
    • The first part indicates that the cows in the herd are not sitting peacefully, suggesting a lack of tranquility or harmony.
    • The second part reflects that animals are not looking around, implying a state of ignorance or unawareness.
    • The final part suggests that wise individuals among the people are not seen or desired, indicating a disconnect between wisdom and the masses.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, if employees (the cows) are not working together harmoniously (not sitting), it can lead to a chaotic workplace.
    • In society, if individuals (the animals) are not aware of their surroundings or the issues at hand, it can lead to ignorance about important social matters.
    • When wise leaders or thinkers (the wise) are not recognized or valued by the community, it can result in a lack of guidance and direction for the people.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of harmony, awareness, and the recognition of wisdom in society.
    • It serves as a reminder that without these qualities, both individuals and communities may struggle to thrive.
    • In real life, it encourages us to foster peaceful coexistence, stay aware of our surroundings, and value the insights of wise individuals to create a better society.
