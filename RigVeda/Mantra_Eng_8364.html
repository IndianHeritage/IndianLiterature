श्वेतं रूपं कृतुते यत् सिषासति सोमो मीढ्वाम् असुरो वेद भूमनः |धिया शमी सचते सेम अभि प्रवद दिवस कवन्धम् अव दर्षद उद्रिणम् ||
śvetaṁ rūpaṁ kṛṇute yat siṣāsati somo mīḍhvām̐ asuro veda bhūmanaḥ | dhiyā śamī sacate sem abhi pravad divas kavandham ava darṣad udriṇam ||
  • śvetaṁ rūpaṁ kṛṇute: This phrase translates to "the white form creates." The color white often symbolizes purity, clarity, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and truth, where individuals strive to attain a clear understanding of their surroundings.
  • yat siṣāsati somo: This means "that which the moon (Soma) nourishes." The moon is often associated with nourishment and growth, similar to how it influences tides and agricultural cycles. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how people rely on natural cycles for farming and harvesting, emphasizing the importance of harmony with nature.
  • mīḍhvām̐ asuro veda bhūmanaḥ: This translates to "the wise one knows the essence of the earth." Here, "asuro" refers to the wise or enlightened beings, while "bhūmanaḥ" relates to the earth. This can be connected to environmental awareness and the understanding that our actions impact the planet. For example, sustainable practices in agriculture and industry reflect this wisdom.
  • dhiyā śamī sacate: This means "with intellect, one attains peace." It highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in achieving tranquility in life. In real life, this can be seen in conflict resolution, where thoughtful dialogue and understanding lead to peaceful outcomes.
  • sem abhi pravad: This translates to "the essence speaks." It suggests that the core truth or essence of things reveals itself. In everyday life, this can be observed in moments of clarity or insight, where individuals come to understand deeper truths about themselves or their situations.
  • divas kavandham: This means "the day reveals." It signifies the idea that with the dawn of each day, new opportunities and insights are presented. This can be related to the concept of new beginnings, where each day offers a chance to start afresh and pursue one's goals.
  • ava darṣad udriṇam: This translates to "it shows the way." This phrase emphasizes guidance and direction. In practical terms, this can be seen in mentorship or leadership, where experienced individuals guide others towards success and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, clarity, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the importance of understanding our environment, seeking knowledge, and striving for peace through intellect. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in various fields such as education, environmental conservation, and personal development. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
