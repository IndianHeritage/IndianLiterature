अन्यं ऊषु त्वं यम्य अन्य उ त्वां परि स्वजाते लिबुजेव वृक्षम् |तस्य वा त्वं मन इच्छा स वा तवाधा कृणुष्व संविदं सुभद्राम ||
anyam ū ṣu tvaṁ yamy anya u tvām pari ṣvajāte libujeva vṛkṣam | tasya vā tvam mana icchā sa vā tavādhā kṛṇuṣva saṁvidaṁ subhadrām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anyam: another, different
    • ū: you
    • ṣu: in the sense of being
    • tvaṁ: you
    • yamy: whom
    • anya: another
    • u: and
    • tvām: you
    • pariṣvajāte: embraces
    • libujeva: like a tree
    • vṛkṣam: tree
    • tasya: of that
    • vā: or
    • tvam: you
    • mana: mind
    • icchā: desire
    • sa: he
    • vā: or
    • tavādhā: your support
    • kṛṇuṣva: make
    • saṁvidaṁ: knowledge
    • subhadrām: auspicious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of embracing one another.
    • The imagery of a tree signifies stability and support, suggesting that just as a tree provides shelter and sustenance, so should we support each other in our lives.
    • The phrase "make your support" emphasizes the active role we must take in fostering relationships and community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, colleagues who support each other create a positive environment, leading to increased productivity and job satisfaction.
    • In families, the act of embracing and supporting one another during difficult times strengthens bonds and fosters resilience.
    • In communities, initiatives that encourage collaboration and mutual aid reflect the essence of this mantra, promoting social harmony and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, support, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community building, reminding us of our shared responsibilities.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment for ourselves and others.
