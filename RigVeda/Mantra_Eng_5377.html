आयं सोम इन्द्र तुभ्यं सुन्वा आ तु प्र याहि हरिवस तदोकाः |पिबा त्व अस्य सुषुतस्य चारोर्दादो मग्हानि मगवन्न इयानः ||
ayaṁ soma indra tubhyaṁ sunva ā tu pra yāhi harivas tadokāḥ | pibā tv a1sya suṣutasya cāror dado maghāni maghavann iyānaḥ ||
  • ayaṁ soma indra tubhyaṁ: This phrase translates to "This Soma, O Indra, is for you." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In real life, it can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, which nourishes the mind and spirit.
  • sunva ā tu pra yāhi: This means "Listen and come forth." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and open to divine guidance. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of mindfulness and active listening, where individuals seek to understand and learn from their surroundings and experiences.
  • harivas tadokāḥ: This translates to "the best of the best." It signifies the pursuit of excellence and the highest ideals. In practical terms, this can relate to striving for personal and professional excellence, whether in academics, career, or personal development.
  • pibā tv a1sya suṣutasya: This means "Drink of this delightful one." It suggests the enjoyment and appreciation of life's offerings. In real life, this can be compared to savoring moments of joy and happiness, whether through relationships, achievements, or simple pleasures.
  • cāror dado maghāni: This translates to "bestow upon us great riches." It reflects the desire for abundance and prosperity. In a modern context, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of wealth, not just in material terms but also in knowledge, relationships, and experiences that enrich life.
  • maghavann iyānaḥ: This means "O powerful one, lead us." It is a call for guidance and strength from a higher power. In real life, this can be seen in the reliance on mentors, leaders, or spiritual guides who help navigate challenges and inspire growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine connection, guidance, and the pursuit of excellence and abundance in life. Its significance lies in the reminder to remain open to learning, to appreciate the richness of experiences, and to seek guidance from higher powers or mentors. In practical scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for personal growth, to cultivate mindfulness, and to appreciate the beauty of life while seeking prosperity in all its forms.
