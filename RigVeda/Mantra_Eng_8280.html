आयं त आघृणे सुतो घृतं न पवते शुचि |आ भक्षत कन्यासु नः ||
ayaṁ ta āghṛṇe suto ghṛtaṁ na pavate śuci | ā bhakṣat kanyāsu naḥ ||
  • ayaṁ ta āghṛṇe suto: This phrase can be translated as "This one, who is born from us." It signifies the connection between the individual and their lineage or family. In real life, this can be seen in the context of family gatherings where the importance of heritage and ancestry is celebrated.
  • ghṛtaṁ na pavate śuci: This translates to "the clarified butter does not become impure." Ghee (clarified butter) is often used in rituals and is considered pure and sacred in many cultures. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea that certain values or traditions remain untainted despite external influences, much like how ghee retains its purity.
  • ā bhakṣat kanyāsu naḥ: This means "may the maidens not consume us." This can be interpreted as a plea for protection and respect towards women. In contemporary society, this resonates with the ongoing discussions about women's rights and the importance of treating women with dignity and respect.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of lineage, purity, and respect for women. It highlights the importance of maintaining one's cultural values and traditions while advocating for the protection and honor of women in society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in family settings, cultural celebrations, and discussions surrounding gender equality, reminding us of our responsibilities towards our heritage and the respect we owe to all individuals, particularly women.
