अस्मै बहूनाम अवमाय सख्ये यज्ञैर विद्हेम नमसā हविर्भिः |सं सानु मार्ज्मि दिधिषामि बिल्मैर दधाम्य अन्नैः परि वन्द ऋग्भिः ||
asmai bahūnām avamāya sakhye yajñair vidhema namasā havirbhiḥ | saṁ sānu mārjmi didhiṣāmi bilmair dadhāmy annaiḥ pari vanda ṛgbhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmai: to him
    • bahūnām: of many
    • avamāya: by lowering
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • yajñair: through sacrifices
    • vidhema: we perform
    • namasā: with reverence
    • havirbhiḥ: with offerings
    • saṁ: together
    • sānu: with the essence
    • mārjmi: I cleanse
    • didhiṣāmi: I desire
    • bilmair: with the hidden
    • dadhāmy: I give
    • annaiḥ: with food
    • pari: all around
    • vanda: I honor
    • ṛgbhiḥ: with hymns
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "to him of many" suggests a focus on a higher power or divine entity that encompasses all.
    • It highlights the act of performing sacrifices and offerings with reverence, indicating a deep respect for the divine.
    • The mention of cleansing and giving food signifies the importance of purity and nourishment in spiritual rituals.
    • Overall, it reflects a harmonious relationship between individuals and the divine, achieved through collective worship and offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, community gatherings for festivals or religious ceremonies can be seen as a reflection of this mantra, where people come together to honor a higher power.
    • Charitable acts, such as community kitchens or food drives, embody the spirit of giving and nourishment mentioned in the mantra.
    • Environmental clean-up drives can be viewed as a form of "cleansing" the earth, aligning with the idea of mārjmi (to cleanse) in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, reverence, and collective action in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that through community efforts and shared intentions, we can create a positive impact both spiritually and socially.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger community bonds, greater respect for the environment, and a deeper connection to the divine.
