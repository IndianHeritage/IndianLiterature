तुभ्यं स्तोकाः घृतश्चुतो 'ग्ने विप्राय सन्त्यः |ऋषिः श्रेष्ठः सम इध्यसे यज्ञस्य प्राविता भव ||
tubhyaṁ stokā ghṛtaścuto 'gne viprāya santya | ṛṣiḥ śreṣṭhaḥ sam idhyase yajñasya prāvitā bhava ||
  • tubhyaṁ: This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion or offering directed towards a divine entity.
  • stokā: Meaning "small" or "little," it suggests humility in the offering, emphasizing that even a small gesture can be significant.
  • ghṛtaścuto: This term refers to "clarified butter" or "ghee," which is considered sacred and is often used in rituals and offerings in Hinduism.
  • 'gne: This word means "O fire," addressing Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes transformation and purification.
  • viprāya: This translates to "to the learned" or "to the wise," indicating respect towards those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • santya: Meaning "peace" or "tranquility," it reflects the desire for harmony and well-being.
  • ṛṣiḥ: This word means "sage" or "seer," referring to the wise individuals who have deep spiritual insight.
  • śreṣṭhaḥ: This translates to "the best" or "the greatest," indicating the highest quality or excellence.
  • sam idhyase: Meaning "you are established" or "you are present," it signifies the presence of the divine in the ritual.
  • yajñasya: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," highlighting the importance of offerings in Vedic traditions.
  • prāvitā: Meaning "may you be pleased" or "may you be satisfied," it expresses the hope that the offerings will be accepted.
  • bhava: This translates to "be" or "become," indicating a wish for the divine presence to manifest.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, humility, and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It emphasizes that even small offerings, when made with sincerity and respect, can hold great significance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal prayers, community rituals, or even in acts of kindness towards others. The mantra serves as a reminder that the intention behind our actions is what truly matters, and that we should strive for peace and harmony in our lives and relationships.
