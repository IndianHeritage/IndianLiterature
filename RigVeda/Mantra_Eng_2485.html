समित्-समित् सुमना बोध्य अस्मे शुचā-शुचā सुमतिं राशि वस्वः |आ देव देवान यजथाया वक्षि सखा सखीन सुमना यक्ष्य अग्ने ||
samit-samit sumanā bodhy asme śucā-śucā sumatiṁ rāsi vasvaḥ | ā deva devān yajathāya vakṣi sakhā sakhīn sumanā yakṣy agne ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samit-samit: Offering, or the act of giving.
    • sumanā: Good thoughts or intentions.
    • bodhy: To be awakened or enlightened.
    • asme: To us or for us.
    • śucā-śucā: Pure, or the essence of purity.
    • sumanim: Good understanding or wisdom.
    • rāsi: Abundance or collection.
    • vasvaḥ: All or everything.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • deva: God or divine beings.
    • devān: The gods.
    • yajathāya: For the purpose of worship or sacrifice.
    • vakṣi: You will speak or declare.
    • sakhā: Friend or companion.
    • sakhīn: Friends or companions (plural).
    • sumanā: Good thoughts or intentions (repeated for emphasis).
    • yakṣy: To be invoked or called upon.
    • agne: O Fire, or referring to the divine fire.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offering good thoughts and intentions to the divine.
    • It suggests that through purity of mind and heart, one can attain wisdom and abundance.
    • The invocation of divine beings signifies the need for guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the role of friendship and community in achieving collective goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of good intentions and teamwork can lead to greater productivity and success.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining purity of thought and intention can strengthen bonds and create a supportive environment.
    • During community service, invoking the spirit of cooperation and friendship can enhance the impact of collective efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of good intentions, purity, and community in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance while fostering positive relationships with others.
    • In essence, it teaches that through collective good will and purity of heart, we can achieve greater wisdom and abundance in our lives.
