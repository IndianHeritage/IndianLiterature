समुद्राद ऊर्मिर मधुमाम् उद आरद उपांशुना सम अमृतत्वम् आनट् |घृतस्य नाम गुह्यं यद अस्ति जिव्हा देवानाम् अमृतस्य नाभिः ||
samudrād ūrmir madhumām̐ ud ārad upāṁśunā sam amṛtatvam ānaṭ | ghṛtasya nāma guhyaṁ yad asti jihvā devānām amṛtasya nābhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "samudrād ūrmir madhumām̐ ud ārad upāṁśunā sam amṛtatvam ānaṭ | ghṛtasya nāma guhyaṁ yad asti jihvā devānām amṛtasya nābhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "From the ocean, the wave of sweetness arises; may it bring us immortality. The secret name of ghee is that which exists in the tongue of the gods, the navel of immortality."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • samudrād: From the ocean - symbolizes the source of life and abundance.
    • ūrmir: Wave - represents movement, energy, and the dynamic nature of existence.
    • madhumām̐: Sweetness - signifies joy, pleasure, and the essence of life.
    • ud ārad: May it bring - a prayer for blessings and positive outcomes.
    • upāṁśunā: With the subtle - indicates the importance of the unseen and the spiritual.
    • sam amṛtatvam: Immortality - the ultimate goal of existence, transcending the physical realm.
    • ānaṭ: To attain - the aspiration to reach a higher state of being.
    • ghṛtasya: Of ghee - ghee is a sacred substance in Vedic culture, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
    • nāma: Name - the significance of names and their power in creation.
    • guhyaṁ: Secret - the hidden truths that are often overlooked.
    • yad: That which - pointing to the essence of the subject.
    • asti: Exists - affirming the reality of the divine and the spiritual.
    • jihvā: Tongue - represents communication, expression, and the power of words.
    • devānām: Of the gods - the divine beings that embody higher wisdom.
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality - the eternal aspect of existence.
    • nābhiḥ: Navel - a symbol of origin and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The ocean is often seen as a metaphor for the vastness of human experience, where waves represent the ups and downs of life.
    • Sweetness can be related to moments of joy and happiness, such as celebrating a wedding or the birth of a child.
    • The concept of immortality can be reflected in the legacies we leave behind, such as art, literature, or impactful actions that inspire future generations.
    • Ghee, used in many Indian households, symbolizes nourishment and the importance of purity in our diets and lifestyles.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the pursuit of immortality, and the importance of spiritual nourishment. It teaches us to seek sweetness in our experiences, to recognize the hidden truths in our lives, and to aspire for a higher state of being. In real life, this can be applied by fostering positive relationships, pursuing knowledge, and striving for personal growth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
