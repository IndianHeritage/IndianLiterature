उष्टारेव फर्वरेषु श्रयेत प्रायोगेव श्वात्र्या शासुर एथः |दूतव हि ष्ठो यशसा जनेषु माप स्थातम महिषेवावपानात ||
uṣṭāreva pharvareṣu śrayethe prāyogeva śvātryā śāsur ethaḥ | dūteva hi ṣṭho yaśasā janeṣu māpa sthātam mahiṣevāvapānāt ||
  • uṣṭāreva: This word refers to the "camel," which is often seen as a symbol of endurance and resilience in harsh environments.
  • pharvareṣu: This term can be interpreted as "in the midst of the strong" or "among the powerful," indicating a context of strength and capability.
  • śrayethe: This means "to take shelter" or "to rely upon," suggesting the importance of seeking support from those who are strong.
  • prāyogeva: This can be understood as "like a practice" or "in the manner of application," emphasizing the need for practical application in life.
  • śvātryā: This word refers to "the one who is wise" or "the knowledgeable," highlighting the value of wisdom in decision-making.
  • śāsur: This term means "to guide" or "to instruct," indicating the role of a mentor or teacher in one's life.
  • ethaḥ: This translates to "come here" or "approach," suggesting an invitation to engage or participate.
  • dūteva: This can be interpreted as "like a messenger" or "as if sent," indicating the importance of communication and connection.
  • hi: This is a particle that emphasizes the preceding statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
  • ṣṭho: This means "to stand" or "to remain," emphasizing stability and presence.
  • yaśasā: This word refers to "fame" or "glory," indicating the pursuit of recognition and success.
  • janeṣu: This translates to "among people" or "in society," highlighting the social aspect of human existence.
  • māpa: This means "do not" or "let not," serving as a caution or warning.
  • sthātam: This translates to "to stand" or "to remain," reinforcing the idea of stability.
  • mahiṣevā: This term refers to "like a buffalo," which can symbolize strength and perseverance.
  • vapānāt: This means "from the act of drinking" or "from the source," suggesting nourishment or sustenance.

It highlights the need for practical application in life and the significance of guidance from mentors. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, where students seek guidance from teachers, or in professional settings, where individuals rely on experienced colleagues for support.
