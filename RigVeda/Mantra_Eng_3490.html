महश्चर्कर्म्यर्वतः क्रतुप्रादधिक्राव्णः पुरुवास्य वृष्णः |यम् पूरुभ्यो दीदिवांसं नाग्निं ददथुर मित्रावरुणा ततुरिम ||
mahaś carkarmy arvataḥ kratuprā dadhikrāvṇaḥ puruvārasya vṛṣṇaḥ | yam pūrubhyo dīdivāṁsaṁ nāgniṁ dadathur mitrāvaruṇā taturim ||
  • mahaś carkarmy: This phrase can be interpreted as "great actions" or "mighty deeds." It signifies the importance of undertaking significant and impactful actions in life.
  • arvataḥ: This word refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It emphasizes the strength and capability of individuals or forces in achieving their goals.
  • kratuprā: This term means "possessing wisdom" or "having a strong will." It highlights the necessity of wisdom and determination in decision-making and actions.
  • dadhikrāvṇaḥ: This can be translated as "the one who provides" or "the giver." It suggests the importance of generosity and support in relationships and communities.
  • puruvārasya: This phrase means "of the ancient ones" or "of the ancestors." It connects the present actions to the wisdom and traditions of those who came before us.
  • vṛṣṇaḥ: This word refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." It can symbolize resilience and strength in overcoming challenges.
  • yam pūrubhyo: This means "to whom the ancient ones" or "to whom the elders." It indicates respect and reverence for the wisdom of the past.
  • dīdivāṁsaṁ: This term translates to "the one who shines" or "the radiant one." It signifies enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • nāgniṁ: This word refers to "fire" or "the divine fire." Fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and the energy needed to achieve goals.
  • dadathur: This means "they have given" or "they have provided." It emphasizes the act of giving and sharing resources or knowledge.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase refers to "Mitra and Varuna," the deities representing friendship and cosmic order. It highlights the importance of harmony and balance in relationships.
  • taturim: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of strength, wisdom, generosity, and respect for tradition in our actions. It teaches us that by undertaking great deeds with determination and support from our community, we can achieve enlightenment and transformation. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, community service, and personal development. By embodying these values, we can create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of others, fostering harmony and progress in society.
