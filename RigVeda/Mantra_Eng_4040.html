मन्त्र: उता ज्ञा व्यन्तु देवपत्नीः इन्द्राण्य अग्नाय्य अश्विनीर राट् |आ रोडसी वरुणानी श्रिणोतु व्यन्तु देवीर य ऋतुर जनिनाम् ||
uta gnā vyantu devapatnīr indrāṇy a1gnāyy aśvinī rāṭ | ā rodasī varuṇānī śṛṇotu vyantu devīr ya ṛtur janīnām ||
  • Translation: "May the divine consorts of the gods, the Indras, and the Ashvins, come to us. Let the celestial beings hear us, and may the goddesses who govern the seasons listen to the prayers of the people."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • gnā: knowledge, understanding
    • vyantu: may they come, may they arrive
    • devapatnīr: divine consorts of the gods
    • indrāṇy: of Indra, the king of gods
    • aśvinī: the Ashvins, divine twin horsemen
    • rāṭ: ruler, king
    • ā: to, towards
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the heavens and the earth
    • varuṇānī: of Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • śṛṇotu: may they hear
    • vyantu: may they come
    • devīr: goddesses
    • ya: who
    • ṛtur: seasons
    • janīnām: of the people, of the beings
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine beings, specifically the consorts of the gods, to come and bless the speaker and the community.
    • The mention of Indra and the Ashvins highlights the importance of strength, health, and prosperity, as these deities are associated with power and healing.
    • The reference to the goddesses governing the seasons signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of harmony with nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking the deities for blessings can be seen during harvest festivals, where prayers are offered for a bountiful crop.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be recited during significant life events such as weddings or new beginnings, seeking divine guidance and support.
    • Environmental awareness can also be linked to this mantra, as it emphasizes the need to respect and listen to the natural world and its cycles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of humanity with the divine and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and blessings from higher powers in our endeavors and to remain attuned to the rhythms of nature.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to foster a sense of unity, respect, and gratitude towards the divine and the environment.
