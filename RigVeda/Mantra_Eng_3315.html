नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणाना इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance or blessings from a higher power or authority in times of need.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This means "we sing your praises." Singing praises can be seen as expressing gratitude and recognition of someone's efforts or achievements, similar to how we celebrate the accomplishments of leaders or mentors in our lives.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This translates to "the rivers that flow, do not dry up." Rivers symbolize sustenance and life. In a real-life context, this can represent the importance of maintaining resources and ensuring that our environment remains healthy and sustainable for future generations.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "you have made the horses." Horses in Vedic culture symbolize strength and power. In modern terms, this can be related to the development of technology and infrastructure that empowers societies, such as transportation systems that connect people and resources.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new and fresh creation." It signifies innovation and the continuous evolution of ideas. In real life, this can be seen in the context of startups and new technologies that aim to improve our lives and solve existing problems.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This means "may we always be endowed with wisdom." Wisdom is crucial in making informed decisions. In everyday life, this can be applied to the importance of education and lifelong learning, as well as seeking advice from experienced individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance, expressing gratitude, and recognizing the importance of resources and innovation. It highlights the need for wisdom in our actions and decisions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as environmental sustainability, technological advancement, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can strive for a balanced and prosperous existence.
