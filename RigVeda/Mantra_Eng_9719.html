न मत स्त्री सुभसत्तरा न सुयाशुतरा भुवत् |न मत प्रातिच्यवीयसी न शक्त्य उद्यमीयसी विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
na mat strī subhasattarā na suyāśutarā bhuvat | na mat praticyavīyasī na sakthy udyamīyasī viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Neither the woman of good fortune, nor the one of great fame, nor the one who is strong, nor the one who is capable, is superior to me; I am the one who is the best among all." This mantra emphasizes the idea of self-worth and the recognition of one's own capabilities.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • na mat: "not mine" or "not to me" - indicating a sense of detachment from external validations.
    • strī: "woman" - representing qualities often associated with femininity.
    • subhasattarā: "of good fortune" - referring to someone who is blessed or fortunate.
    • suyāśutarā: "of great fame" - indicating someone who is well-known or celebrated.
    • bhuvat: "is" - a form of the verb to be, indicating existence.
    • praticyavīyasī: "strong" or "capable" - referring to strength and capability.
    • sakthy: "power" - denoting strength or ability.
    • udyamīyasī: "capable" - indicating someone who is industrious or hardworking.
    • viśvasmād: "from all" - suggesting a universal perspective.
    • indra: "Indra" - the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
    • uttaraḥ: "the best" or "the highest" - indicating superiority or excellence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, an employee may feel overshadowed by colleagues who have more experience or recognition. This mantra serves as a reminder that one's own skills and contributions are valuable and should not be diminished by comparisons.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may sometimes feel inadequate when comparing themselves to others. This mantra encourages self-acceptance and the understanding that each person has unique strengths.
    • In competitive environments, such as sports or academics, this mantra can inspire individuals to focus on their own abilities rather than being intimidated by others' achievements.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its powerful message of self-empowerment and self-acceptance. It teaches that external accolades, fame, or strength do not define one's worth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster confidence, encourage personal growth, and promote a healthy self-image. By recognizing and valuing our own capabilities, we can navigate challenges with resilience and assertiveness, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
