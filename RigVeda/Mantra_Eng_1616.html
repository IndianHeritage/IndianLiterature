रेवद् वयो दधाथे रेवद् आशाथे नराः मायाभिर इतौति माहिनम् |न वां dyāvo 'हभिर नोत सिन्धवो न देवत्वं पाणयो नानशुर मगहम् ||
revad vayo dadhāthe revad āśāthe narā māyābhir itaūti māhinam | na vāṁ dyāvo 'habhir nota sindhavo na devatvam paṇayo nānaśur magham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • revad: to be strong or powerful
    • vayo: age or life
    • dadhāthe: you hold or maintain
    • āśāthe: you provide hope or aspiration
    • narā: men or humans
    • māyābhir: with illusions or deceptions
    • itaūti: they are led or guided
    • māhinam: to the great or the powerful
    • na: not
    • vāṁ: your
    • dyāvo: heaven
    • 'habhir: with the help of
    • nota: nor
    • sindhavo: rivers
    • na: not
    • devatvam: divinity or godliness
    • paṇayo: of the wealth or riches
    • nānaśur: not of the brave or the strong
    • magham: the great blessing or wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and power of life and how it is maintained through hope and aspiration.
    • It emphasizes that humans are often led by illusions and that true strength does not come from external sources like heaven or rivers.
    • The mantra suggests that divinity and true wealth are not merely about material possessions or bravery, but rather about inner strength and the ability to navigate through life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may face numerous challenges and illusions of success. However, true leadership is about maintaining hope and guiding the team through difficulties, rather than relying solely on external accolades.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter various distractions and illusions that lead them away from their true goals. The mantra reminds us to focus on our inner strength and aspirations rather than being swayed by superficial achievements.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true power and wealth come from within. It encourages individuals to cultivate inner strength and maintain hope in the face of challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, leadership, and overcoming obstacles, emphasizing the importance of resilience and inner guidance.
