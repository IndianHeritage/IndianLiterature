शरासः कुशरासो दर्भासः सैर्याऽउत |मौञ्जा अदृष्टा वैरिणाः सर्वे साकं न्यलिप्सत ||
śarāsaḥ kuśarāso darbhāsaḥ sairyā uta | mauñjā adṛṣṭā vairiṇāḥ sarve sākaṁ ny alipsata ||
  • śarāsaḥ: This term refers to "grass" or "herbs." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the natural world and the sustenance it provides.
  • kuśarāso: This can be interpreted as "the grass of the Kuśa," which is a sacred grass used in various rituals. It represents purity and the connection to the divine.
  • darbhāsaḥ: Similar to kuśarāsa, darbhāsa refers to "darba grass," which is also considered sacred and is often used in religious ceremonies.
  • sairyā: This word can be associated with "sacredness" or "purity," emphasizing the importance of maintaining a pure environment.
  • uta: This term means "and" or "also," indicating a continuation of thought or action.
  • mauñjā: Referring to "mauñjī," a type of sacred grass used in rituals, it symbolizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • adṛṣṭā: This means "invisible" or "unseen," suggesting that there are forces or energies at play that are not immediately apparent to us.
  • vairiṇāḥ: This term translates to "enemies" or "opponents," indicating the presence of challenges or adversities in life.
  • sarve: Meaning "all," it emphasizes inclusivity and the collective nature of existence.
  • sākaṁ: This means "together" or "with," highlighting the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • ny alipsata: This phrase can be interpreted as "did not desire" or "did not covet," suggesting a sense of contentment and detachment from material desires.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, purity, and the sacredness of rituals in our lives. It reminds us of the unseen forces that influence our existence and the importance of unity in facing challenges. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a connection with nature, engaging in rituals that promote mindfulness, and cultivating a sense of community to overcome adversities. By practicing contentment and detachment from material desires, we can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
