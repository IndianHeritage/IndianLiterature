स्वायुधः सोत्रभिः पूयमानोऽभ्यर्ष गूढं चारु नाम |अभि वाजं सप्तिरिव श्रवस्याभि वायुमभि गा देव सोम ||
svāyudhaḥ sotṛbhiḥ pūyamāno 'bhy arṣa guhyaṁ cāru nāma | abhi vājaṁ saptir iva śravasyābhi vāyum abhi gā deva soma ||
  • svāyudhaḥ: This term can be translated as "armed with one's own weapons." It signifies self-reliance and the importance of being prepared to face challenges in life.
  • sotṛbhiḥ: This means "with the help of the hearers" or "with the support of those who listen." It emphasizes the value of community and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • pūyamāno: This translates to "being purified" or "being made pure." It suggests the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • 'bhy: This is a preposition that can mean "from" or "by," indicating a source or origin.
  • arṣa: This word means "sacred" or "holy," often referring to knowledge that is revered and respected.
  • guhyaṁ: This translates to "secret" or "mystical," indicating that there are deeper truths and insights that may not be immediately apparent.
  • cāru: This means "beautiful" or "charming," suggesting that the pursuit of knowledge and truth can lead to beauty in life.
  • nāma: This translates to "name," indicating the importance of identity and recognition in the spiritual and material realms.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
  • vājaṁ: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • saptir: This translates to "seven," which can symbolize completeness or wholeness in various contexts.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • śravasyābhi: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy of praise," indicating the importance of recognition and honor.
  • vāyum: This translates to "air" or "wind," symbolizing the vital force of life and the importance of breath in spiritual practices.
  • abhi: Again, this indicates direction or purpose.
  • gā: This means "to sing" or "to praise," emphasizing the importance of expression and communication in spiritual and communal life.
  • deva: This translates to "god" or "divine," indicating the connection to the higher powers and the spiritual realm.
  • soma: This refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of self-reliance, community support, and the pursuit of purity and beauty in life. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the sacred knowledge that lies within and around us, and how this knowledge can lead to prosperity and divine connection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, teamwork, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with strength, seek wisdom, and foster harmonious relationships with others, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and enriched life.
