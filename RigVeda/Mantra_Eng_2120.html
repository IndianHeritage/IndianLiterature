येनमā विश्वा च्यवनाकृतानि यो दासं वर्णमधरणं गुहाकः |श्वघ्निवा यो जिगीवाम् लक्षम् आदद आर्यः पुष्टानि स जनास इन्द्रः ||
yenemā viśvā cyavanā kṛtāni yo dāsaṁ varṇam adharaṁ guhākaḥ | śvaghnīva yo jigīvām̐l lakṣam ādad aryaḥ puṣṭāni sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yenemā: by whom
    • viśvā: all
    • cyavanā: fallen
    • kṛtāni: deeds
    • yo: who
    • dāsaṁ: servant
    • varṇam: color or quality
    • adharaṁ: lower or base
    • guhākaḥ: hidden or secret
    • śvaghnīva: like a dog
    • yo: who
    • jigīvām̐l: lives
    • lakṣam: goal or aim
    • ādad: takes
    • aryaḥ: noble or honorable
    • puṣṭāni: nourished or prosperous
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: of the people
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that revives and supports all beings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of noble actions and the role of a higher power in guiding and nourishing humanity.
    • The reference to "servant" and "hidden" suggests that true strength often lies in humility and the unseen forces that work for the greater good.
    • The comparison to a dog may imply loyalty and the instinct to protect and serve, highlighting the virtues of dedication and service.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals who work selflessly for the welfare of others embody the essence of this mantra. For instance, volunteers in disaster relief efforts often act without seeking recognition, reflecting the noble qualities mentioned.
    • In the workplace, leaders who support their teams and foster a nurturing environment can be seen as embodying the qualities of Indra, guiding their people towards success.
    • In personal relationships, those who provide emotional support and care for their loved ones demonstrate the principles of loyalty and service, akin to the metaphor of the dog.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support that sustains life.
    • It teaches us the value of humility, service, and the importance of noble actions in our daily lives.
    • By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, reflecting the essence of the mantra in our actions and interactions.
