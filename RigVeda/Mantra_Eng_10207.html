अहं रुद्रैभिर वसुभिः चराम्य अहम् आदित्यैः उता विश्वदेवैः |अहम मित्रावरुणोभा बिभर्म्य अहम् इन्द्राग्नी अहम् अश्विनोभा ||
ahaṁ rudrebhir vasubhiś carāmy aham ādityair uta viśvadevaiḥ | aham mitrāvaruṇobhā bibharmy aham indrāgnī aham aśvinobhā ||
  • ahaṁ - "I" or "I am": This word signifies the self or the individual consciousness.
  • rudrebhir - "with the Rudras": Refers to the fierce deities associated with storms and destruction, representing the transformative aspects of nature.
  • vasubhiś - "with the Vasus": The Vasus are deities of abundance and prosperity, symbolizing the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • carāmy - "I move" or "I wander": Indicates the dynamic nature of existence and the journey of life.
  • aham - "I": Reiterating the self-awareness and presence of the individual.
  • ādityair - "with the Adityas": The Adityas are solar deities, representing light, truth, and cosmic order.
  • uta - "and": A conjunction that connects different aspects of divinity.
  • viśvadevaiḥ - "with the universal gods": Refers to the collective divine forces that govern the universe.
  • aham - "I": Again emphasizing the self.
  • mitrāvaruṇobhā - "I bear the friendship of Mitra and Varuna": Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and moral law.
  • bibharmy - "I uphold": Indicates the responsibility of maintaining balance and harmony.
  • aham - "I": Repeated for emphasis on the self's role.
  • indrāgnī - "I am Indra and Agni": Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification.
  • aham - "I": Again emphasizing the self.
  • aśvinobhā - "I am the Ashvins": The Ashvins are twin gods associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, representing duality and balance.

Conclusion:

This mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness between the individual and the divine forces of nature. It emphasizes the role of the self in relation to various deities, highlighting the balance between destruction and creation, abundance and scarcity, and the importance of harmony in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of responsibility towards nature and society, encouraging individuals to recognize their role in maintaining balance and harmony in their surroundings. It serves as a reminder that we are part of a larger cosmic order, and our actions can influence the world around us.
