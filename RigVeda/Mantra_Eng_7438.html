इमं मे स्तोमं अश्विनेमं मे श्रिणुतं हवम् |मध्वः सोमस्य पीतये ||
imam me stomam aśvinemam me śṛṇutaṁ havam | madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||
  • Translation: "This is my hymn to the Aśvins; listen to my call. Sweet is the drink of the Soma."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imam: This - referring to the hymn or prayer being offered.
    • me: My - indicating personal ownership or connection to the hymn.
    • stomam: Hymn - a sacred song or chant, often used in rituals.
    • aśvinem: Aśvins - the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • śṛṇutaṁ: Listen - a call for attention, urging the deities to hear the prayer.
    • havam: Call or offering - a term used for the invocation or offering made to the gods.
    • madhvaḥ: Sweet - describing the pleasant nature of the offering.
    • somasya: Of Soma - referring to the sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • pītaye: For drinking - indicating the act of consuming or partaking in the offering.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • The Aśvins are often invoked for their healing powers and are seen as bringers of health and well-being. In modern life, this can be related to seeking help in times of illness or distress, whether through medical professionals or holistic practices.
    • The mention of Soma, a drink that symbolizes divine knowledge and immortality, can be likened to the pursuit of wisdom and enlightenment in contemporary society. Just as the ancient sages sought the benefits of Soma, individuals today seek knowledge through education, meditation, and self-discovery.
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of communication with the divine, which can be paralleled with the practice of mindfulness and prayer in various spiritual traditions today. It highlights the need for individuals to express their needs and desires, whether to a higher power or within their community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of health, knowledge, and spirituality. It encourages individuals to seek support from both the divine and their community in times of need.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can manifest as reaching out for help during challenging times, engaging in practices that promote well-being, and fostering a sense of community through shared rituals or gatherings.
    • Ultimately, the hymn underscores the significance of listening—both to the divine and to one another—as a pathway to healing and enlightenment.
