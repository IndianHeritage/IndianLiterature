दधिक्राव्णो अकारीषं जीष्णोर अश्वस्य वाजिनः |सुरभि नो मुखा करत प्र ण आयूषि तारीषत ||
dadhikrāvṇo akāriṣaṁ jiṣṇor aśvasya vājinaḥ | surabhi no mukhā karat pra ṇa āyūṁṣi tāriṣat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhikrāvṇo: One who is rich in nourishment or abundance.
    • akāriṣaṁ: Not causing harm or injury.
    • jiṣṇor: Of the victorious or the strong.
    • aśvasya: Of the horse, symbolizing speed and power.
    • vājinaḥ: The one who is swift or a winner.
    • surabhi: Fragrant or pleasing, often referring to divine or celestial qualities.
    • no: Our.
    • mukhā: Faces or expressions.
    • karat: May create or bring forth.
    • pra ṇa: Life or vitality.
    • āyūṁṣi: Long life or longevity.
    • tāriṣat: May be protected or preserved.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of abundance and nourishment, emphasizing the importance of prosperity that does not harm others.
    • The reference to the horse symbolizes strength and speed, suggesting that one should strive for success and victory in life.
    • The term "surabhi" indicates a desire for pleasantness and positivity in our endeavors, highlighting the importance of a good attitude and approach.
    • By asking for the creation of life and longevity, the mantra reflects a wish for not just personal success but also for the well-being of the community and the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to ethical entrepreneurship, where success is achieved without harming competitors or the environment.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages nurturing connections that foster growth and happiness, rather than conflict or negativity.
    • In community service, it inspires individuals to contribute positively to society, ensuring that their actions lead to the well-being of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to success, emphasizing that true victory is achieved through ethical means and a focus on the greater good.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions should promote life, longevity, and prosperity for all, not just for oneself.
    • In today's world, where competition can often lead to unethical practices, this mantra encourages a balanced and compassionate approach to achieving one's goals.
