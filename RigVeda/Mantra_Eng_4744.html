यस्य त्याच चम्बरं मादे दिवोदासाय रन्धयः |अयं स सोम इन्द्र ते सुतः पिब ||
yasya tyac chambaram made divodāsāya randhayaḥ | ayaṁ sa soma indra te sutaḥ piba ||
  • Mantra: "yasya tyac chambaram made divodāsāya randhayaḥ | ayaṁ sa soma indra te sutaḥ piba ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tyac: the one who has given up
    • chambaram: the veil or covering
    • made: in the intoxication or ecstasy
    • divodāsāya: to the divine beings or gods
    • randhayaḥ: to be prepared or made ready
    • ayaṁ: this
    • sa: he or that
    • soma: the sacred drink or elixir
    • indra: the king of gods
    • te: your
    • sutaḥ: offspring or son
    • piba: drink
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine offering, where the essence of the sacred drink, Soma, is being prepared and offered to Indra, the king of gods.
    • The phrase "yasya tyac chambaram made" suggests the removal of ignorance or obstacles (the veil) in a state of divine ecstasy.
    • The invocation to Indra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the role of gods in human affairs.
    • In real life, this can be likened to moments when individuals seek clarity and divine guidance in their lives, often through rituals, meditation, or prayer.
    • The act of drinking Soma symbolizes the acceptance of divine wisdom and strength, akin to how people today might seek inspiration from spiritual practices or community gatherings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and the transformative power of spiritual practices.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges and seek clarity, strength, and inspiration from higher powers.
    • By invoking the blessings of the divine, one can find the courage to overcome obstacles and embrace a path of wisdom and enlightenment.
