तां अस्य नमसाह सहः सपर्ण्यन्ति प्रचेतसः |व्रतान्य अस्य सश्चिरे पुरूणि पूर्वचित्तये वस्वीर अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
tā asya namasā sahaḥ saparyanti pracetasaḥ | vratāny asya saścire purūṇi pūrvacittaye vasvīr anu svarājyam ||
  • tā asya namasā sahaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "to them, with reverence and respect." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and acknowledging the divine or the forces of nature that govern our lives.
  • saparyanti pracetasaḥ: This means "those who are wise and enlightened worship." It suggests that true wisdom comes from recognizing the divine and engaging in acts of worship or reverence.
  • vratāny asya saścire: This translates to "the vows and commitments of this are long-lasting." It indicates that the promises made in devotion or spiritual practice have enduring significance and impact.
  • purūṇi pūrvacittaye: This means "the ancient traditions and thoughts." It highlights the importance of historical wisdom and practices that have been passed down through generations.
  • vasvīr anu svarājyam: This can be interpreted as "those who follow the path of righteousness attain sovereignty." It suggests that living a life aligned with divine principles leads to true power and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence, wisdom, and the significance of ancient traditions in guiding one's life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their values and commitments, ensuring they align with their spiritual beliefs.
  • In community settings, honoring traditions and engaging in collective worship can foster unity and shared purpose.
  • In leadership, the mantra serves as a reminder that true authority comes from ethical practices and respect for the wisdom of the past.

Overall, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a life of reverence, wisdom, and commitment to higher principles, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
