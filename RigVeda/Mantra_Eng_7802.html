नित्यस्तोत्रो वनस्पतिः धीनाṃ अन्तः सबरदुगः |हिन्वानो मानुषा युगाः ||
nityastotro vanaspatir dhīnām antaḥ sabardughaḥ | hinvāno mānuṣā yugā ||
  • nityastotro: This term can be broken down into two parts: "nitya," meaning eternal or constant, and "stotra," which refers to praise or hymn. Together, it signifies an eternal hymn or praise.
  • vanaspatir: This word translates to "lord of the forest" or "tree." In a broader sense, it represents nature, growth, and the sustenance provided by the natural world.
  • dhīnām: This term means "of the wise" or "of the learned." It refers to those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • antaḥ: This word means "within" or "inside." It suggests an inner dimension or the essence of something.
  • sabardughaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "one who brings forth abundance" or "one who is rich in resources." It implies a source of prosperity and nourishment.
  • hinvāno: This word means "removing" or "dispelling." It indicates the act of eliminating negativity or obstacles.
  • mānuṣā: This term translates to "humans" or "mankind." It emphasizes the human experience and our connection to the divine and nature.
  • yugā: This word means "ages" or "cycles." It refers to the different epochs of time and the changes that occur within them.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the eternal essence of nature, praising its ability to provide wisdom and abundance to humanity. It emphasizes the importance of inner knowledge and the removal of obstacles that hinder our growth and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "vanaspatir" highlights the significance of nature. In today's context, it encourages us to respect and protect our environment, recognizing that it sustains us.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of "dhīnām" suggests that wisdom is essential for personal development. Individuals can strive to gain knowledge and understanding to navigate life's challenges.
  • Overcoming Obstacles: The term "hinvāno" serves as a reminder that we have the power to remove negativity from our lives. This can be applied in mental health practices, where individuals work to dispel negative thoughts and emotions.
  • Community and Cooperation: The mention of "mānuṣā" emphasizes our shared humanity. It encourages collaboration and support within communities to foster growth and prosperity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, wisdom, and humanity. It encourages us to seek knowledge, respect our environment, and work together to overcome challenges. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous existence for ourselves and future generations.
