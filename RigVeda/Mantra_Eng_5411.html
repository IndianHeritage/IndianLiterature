मन्त्रं अक्षरवं सुदितं सुपेशसं दधात यज्ञियेष्वा |पूर्वीश चन प्रसितयस् तरन्ति तं य इन्द्रे कर्मणा भुवत ||
mantram akharvaṁ sudhitaṁ supeśasaṁ dadhāta yajñiyeṣv ā | pūrvīś cana prasitayas taranti taṁ ya indre karmaṇā bhuvat ||
  • Mantra: "mantram akharvaṁ sudhitaṁ supeśasaṁ dadhāta yajñiyeṣv ā | pūrvīś cana prasitayas taranti taṁ ya indre karmaṇā bhuvat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mantram: mantra, a sacred utterance or sound
    • akharvaṁ: unblemished, pure
    • sudhitaṁ: well thought out, wise
    • supeśasaṁ: well-formed, auspicious
    • dadhāta: may he bestow
    • yajñiyeṣv: in the sacrifices
    • pūrvīś: the ancient ones, ancestors
    • cana: and
    • prasitayas: those who have been invoked
    • taranti: they cross over, they transcend
    • taṁ: that
    • ya: who
    • indre: to Indra, the king of gods
    • karmaṇā: by action
    • bhuvat: may become, may manifest
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of a well-formed and pure mantra.
    • It suggests that when one engages in sacrifices or rituals (yajñas) with a pure and wise intention, they can invoke the blessings of the divine.
    • The reference to "pūrvīś" indicates the importance of honoring ancestors and the wisdom of those who came before us.
    • By invoking these ancient energies, individuals can transcend challenges and obstacles in their lives.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and power, and the mantra suggests that through righteous action, one can attain divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various situations such as starting a new venture, where one seeks guidance and blessings for success.
    • During significant life events like weddings or new beginnings, individuals often perform rituals to invoke blessings, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal development, the idea of transcending challenges through wise actions resonates with the journey of self-improvement and overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention, purity, and respect for tradition in our actions.
    • It teaches that through thoughtful and righteous actions, one can invoke divine support and transcend life's challenges.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to align their actions with higher principles, fostering a connection with the divine and the wisdom of ancestors.
