एष स्तोमो वरुण मित्र तुभ्यं सोमः शुक्रो न वायवे 'यामि |अविष्टं धियो जिगृतं पुरंधीः यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
eṣa stomo varuṇa mitra tubhyaṁ somaḥ śukro na vāyave 'yāmi | aviṣṭaṁ dhiyo jigṛtam puraṁdhīr yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • eṣa stomo varuṇa mitra tubhyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "This hymn is dedicated to you, Varuna and Mitra." Varuna and Mitra are deities associated with cosmic order and friendship, respectively. In real life, this can be seen as a call for harmony and balance in relationships, emphasizing the importance of trust and cooperation among individuals.
  • somaḥ śukro na vāyave 'yāmi: Here, "somaḥ" refers to the sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration, while "śukro" means bright or pure. "na vāyave 'yāmi" translates to "I do not go to the wind." This can be interpreted as a desire to remain grounded and connected to the divine rather than being swept away by the chaos of life. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek clarity and purity in their thoughts and actions.
  • aviṣṭaṁ dhiyo jigṛtam puraṁdhīr: This line means "May our intellects be unclouded and our understanding be sharp." It highlights the importance of wisdom and discernment in decision-making. In everyday life, this can be applied to situations where critical thinking is required, such as in professional settings or personal relationships, where clear communication and understanding are vital.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This translates to "May you protect us always with auspiciousness." It is a plea for divine protection and blessings. In real life, this can be seen as a reminder to seek positivity and well-being in our lives, whether through mindfulness practices, community support, or spiritual rituals that foster a sense of safety and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for harmony, wisdom, and divine protection. Its significance lies in its call for balance in relationships, clarity in thought, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life. In practical scenarios, this mantra can be applied in daily rituals, during moments of decision-making, or in fostering community ties, reminding us to seek the divine in our interactions and to cultivate a life of integrity and positivity.
