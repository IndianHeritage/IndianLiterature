प्रैते वदन्तु प्र वयं वदाम ग्रावभ्यो वाचं वदतां वदद्भ्यः |यद अद्रयः पर्वताः साकं आशवः श्लोकं घोषं भरतेन्द्राय सोमिनः ||
praite vadantu pra vayaṁ vadāma grāvabhyo vācaṁ vadatā vadadbhyaḥ | yad adrayaḥ parvatāḥ sākam āśavaḥ ślokaṁ ghoṣam bharathendrāya sominaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Let us speak together, let us speak with one voice, let us speak to the people. What the mountains and hills have spoken, let us bring forth the sound of the hymn to Bharata, the son of Soma."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Praite vadantu: This phrase encourages collective communication and unity in expression.
    • Grāvabhyo vācaṁ: Refers to speaking to the community or society, emphasizing the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom.
    • Adrayaḥ parvatāḥ: Mountains symbolize stability and strength, representing the foundational truths that guide us.
    • Ślokaṁ ghoṣam: The hymn or sound signifies the power of words and their ability to inspire and unite.
    • Bharathendrāya sominaḥ: Bharata, often associated with the idea of a unified nation or community, represents the collective identity and heritage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Community Gatherings: In modern society, community meetings or gatherings reflect the essence of "praite vadantu," where individuals come together to discuss issues, share ideas, and foster unity.
    • Social Movements: Movements advocating for social justice often embody the spirit of collective voice, where diverse groups unite to speak against injustice, much like the call to speak with one voice.
    • Environmental Initiatives: Efforts to protect nature, such as tree planting drives, resonate with the idea of learning from the "mountains and hills," emphasizing the importance of nature in our lives.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collective expression, and the power of words in shaping society. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of coming together as a community to address challenges, share knowledge, and uphold shared values. By invoking the strength of our collective voice, we can create a harmonious and progressive society, much like the ancient wisdom of the RigVeda encourages us to do.
