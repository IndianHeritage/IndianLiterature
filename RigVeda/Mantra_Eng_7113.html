उभयं श्रिणवच च न इन्द्रो अर्वाग इदं वचः |सत्राच्याऽ मगवाऽ सोमपीतये धियā श्रविष्ठ आ गमत् ||
ubhayaṁ śṛṇavac ca na indro arvāg idaṁ vacaḥ | satrācyā maghavā somapītaye dhiyā śaviṣṭha ā gamat ||
  • Ubhayaṁ śṛṇavac ca: This phrase translates to "May both (the speaker and the listener) hear." It emphasizes the importance of mutual understanding and communication. In real life, this can be seen in effective teamwork where all members listen to each other to achieve a common goal.
  • Na indro arvāg idaṁ vacaḥ: This means "Let not Indra (the king of gods) be far from this speech." Indra symbolizes strength and power. This line suggests that divine support is sought in the communication process. For example, in a business meeting, when leaders seek guidance or inspiration, they hope for a favorable outcome, akin to invoking divine presence.
  • Satrācyā maghavā: This translates to "May the powerful one (Maghavan) come to us." Maghavan is often associated with abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking success in endeavors, such as a student striving for academic excellence or an entrepreneur aiming for business growth.
  • Somapītaye: This means "for the sake of the drink of Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality. In modern contexts, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and experiences that enrich our lives, such as education or cultural experiences that enhance personal growth.
  • Dhiyā śaviṣṭha ā gamat: This translates to "May the best thoughts come to us." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and clarity in thought. In everyday life, this can be seen in decision-making processes where individuals or groups strive to make informed and wise choices, such as in ethical dilemmas or strategic planning.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, divine support, the pursuit of prosperity, the importance of nourishment (both physical and intellectual), and the quest for wisdom. Its significance lies in its application to various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and spiritual pursuits. By embodying the principles of mutual listening, seeking divine guidance, striving for abundance, nourishing our minds, and fostering wise thoughts, individuals can navigate their lives more effectively and meaningfully.
