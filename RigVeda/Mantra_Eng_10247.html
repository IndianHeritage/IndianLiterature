यो यज्ञो विश्वतस्तन्तुभिः ततो एकशतम् देवकर्मभिरायतः |इमे वयन्ति पितरो य आययुः प्र वयाप वयेत्य आसते तते ||
yo yajño viśvatas tantubhis tata ekaśataṁ devakarmebhir āyataḥ | ime vayanti pitaro ya āyayuḥ pra vayāpa vayety āsate tate ||
  • Translation: "He who is the sacrifice, who is spread out in all directions, is the one who is invoked by the divine actions of the gods. These ancestors who have come here, they are the ones who are invoked, and they are the ones who are present here."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: He who
    • yajño: sacrifice
    • viśvatas: in all directions
    • tantubhis: spread out
    • tata: that
    • ekaśataṁ: one hundred
    • devakarmebhir: by the actions of the gods
    • āyataḥ: invoked
    • ime: these
    • vayanti: come
    • pitaro: ancestors
    • ya: who
    • āyayuḥ: have come
    • pra: here
    • vayāpa: they are present
    • vayety: they say
    • āsate: they are seated
    • tate: that
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the concept of sacrifice (yajña) as a central theme in Vedic philosophy. It suggests that the act of sacrifice is not just a ritual but a universal principle that connects all beings.
    • The phrase "in all directions" signifies the omnipresence of the divine and the interconnectedness of all life. Just as a spider weaves its web, the divine is intricately woven into the fabric of existence.
    • The invocation of ancestors (pitaro) highlights the importance of lineage and the respect for those who came before us. It suggests that our actions are influenced by our forebears, and honoring them is essential for our spiritual growth.
    • In real life, this can be seen in practices such as family gatherings during festivals, where ancestral traditions are celebrated, and the wisdom of previous generations is passed down.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of sacrifice, respect for ancestors, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It teaches us that our actions have a ripple effect, influencing not only our lives but also the lives of those around us.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as through community service, environmental conservation, and maintaining family traditions. By recognizing our place in the larger web of life, we can cultivate a sense of responsibility and gratitude.
