त्वं वर्मासि सप्रथः पुरोयोधश्च वृत्रहन् |त्वया प्रति ब्रुवे युजा ||
tvaṁ varmāsi saprathaḥ puroyodhaś ca vṛtrahan | tvayā prati bruve yujā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • varmāsi: are the protector or shield
    • saprathaḥ: together with all
    • puroyodhaś: the one who is strong in the front
    • ca: and
    • vṛtrahan: the slayer of Vritra (the demon)
    • tvayā: by you
    • prati: towards
    • bruve: I speak or I declare
    • yujā: with strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses a divine entity, acknowledging their role as a protector and a source of strength.
    • The term "vṛtrahan" refers to the slayer of the demon Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
    • The phrase "tvaṁ varmāsi" emphasizes the protective nature of the divine, suggesting that they shield us from harm.
    • By invoking this mantra, one seeks the strength and protection of the divine in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as facing a health issue or a financial setback, individuals often seek spiritual guidance and protection, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace or academic setting, one may call upon the strength and support of a higher power to overcome challenges and succeed.
    • During significant life events, like marriage or childbirth, people often recite mantras to invoke blessings and protection for themselves and their loved ones.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the protective and empowering nature of the divine.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking strength and support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals seek guidance, protection, and empowerment, reinforcing the belief in a higher power that aids in the journey of life.
