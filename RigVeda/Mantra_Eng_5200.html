कविं केतुं धासिम भानुम अद्रेर हिन्वन्ति शं राज्यं रोडस्योः |पुरंदरस्य गीरभिरा विवासे 'ग्नेर व्रतानी पूर्व्या महानि ||
kaviṁ ketuṁ dhāsim bhānum adrer hinvanti śaṁ rājyaṁ rodasyoḥ | puraṁdarasya gīrbhir ā vivāse 'gner vratāni pūrvyā mahāni ||
  • kaviṁ ketuṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the wise one" or "the seer" (kavi) and "the banner" or "the guiding principle" (ketu). In real life, this can relate to leaders or visionaries who guide society with their wisdom.
  • dhāsim: This word means "to serve" or "to be devoted." It emphasizes the importance of service and dedication in our lives, whether in personal relationships or professional commitments.
  • bhānum: This translates to "the sun" or "light." The sun is often seen as a source of energy and life, symbolizing positivity and growth. In our lives, it can represent hope and enlightenment.
  • adreḥ: This means "of the mountains" or "the high places." Mountains symbolize stability and strength, reminding us of the importance of having a strong foundation in our lives.
  • hinvanti: This word means "to bring forth" or "to produce." It signifies the act of creation and manifestation, which is essential in achieving our goals and aspirations.
  • śaṁ rājyaṁ: This translates to "peaceful kingdom" or "prosperous state." It reflects the desire for harmony and prosperity in society, which can be achieved through collective effort and cooperation.
  • rodasyoḥ: This means "of the heavens" or "the celestial realms." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, reminding us of the spiritual aspects of our existence.
  • puraṁdarasya: This refers to "the destroyer of cities," often associated with Lord Indra, the king of gods. It symbolizes the power to overcome obstacles and challenges in life.
  • gīrbhir: This means "by the words" or "through speech." It highlights the importance of communication and expression in our interactions and relationships.
  • ā vivāse: This translates to "to dwell" or "to reside." It signifies the importance of finding a place of belonging and community in our lives.
  • 'gner: This refers to "of fire," symbolizing transformation and purification. Fire is often associated with change and renewal, reminding us of the need to embrace change in our lives.
  • vratāni: This means "vows" or "commitments." It emphasizes the significance of promises and dedication in achieving our goals.
  • pūrvyā: This translates to "ancient" or "previous." It signifies the wisdom of the past and the lessons we can learn from history.
  • mahāni: This means "great" or "magnificent." It reflects the potential for greatness within each of us and the impact we can have on the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, service, light, strength, and the pursuit of a harmonious and prosperous life. It emphasizes the importance of communication, community, and the transformative power of commitment. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and community building. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can strive for greatness and contribute positively to society, fostering a peaceful and prosperous environment for all.
