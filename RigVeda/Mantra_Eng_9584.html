मन्त्र: अश्वाद इयायेति यद वदन्त्य ओजसो जातम उता मन्य एनम् | मन्योः इयाय हर्म्येषु तस्थौ यतः प्रजज्ञ इन्द्रो अस्य वेद ||
aśvād iyāyeti yad vadanty ojaso jātam uta manya enam | manyor iyāya harmyeṣu tasthau yataḥ prajajña indro asya veda ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvād: From the horse
    • iyāyeti: It moves or goes
    • yad: What
    • vadanty: They say
    • ojas: Energy or strength
    • jātam: Born or produced
    • uta: Or
    • manya: Considered or thought
    • enam: This
    • manyor: Of the mind
    • iyāya: It moves or goes
    • harmyeṣu: In the abodes or homes
    • tasthau: It stood or remained
    • yataḥ: From where
    • prajajña: The wise or knowledgeable
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • asya: Of this
    • veda: Knows or understands
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the energy and strength that is born from the horse, a symbol of power and vitality in Vedic literature.
    • It suggests that what is considered by the wise is not just physical strength but also the mental and spiritual energy that moves through various realms of existence.
    • The reference to Indra indicates the importance of divine knowledge and wisdom in understanding the essence of life and existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, athletes often draw upon their physical strength (ojas) and mental focus to achieve their goals, much like the energy derived from the horse.
    • In leadership, a wise leader (like Indra) must understand the dynamics of their team and the energy that drives them, ensuring that they harness both physical and mental strengths for success.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate their inner strength and wisdom, moving through life's challenges with resilience and insight.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of both physical and mental strength in achieving success and understanding the world.
    • It teaches us that true wisdom comes from recognizing the energy that exists within and around us, guiding our actions and decisions.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as sports, leadership, and personal growth, reminding us to balance our physical capabilities with mental clarity and wisdom.
