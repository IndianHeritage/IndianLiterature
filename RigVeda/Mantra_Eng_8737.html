एतं उ त्यं मदाच्युतं सहस्रधारं वृषभं दिवो दूहुः |विश्वा वसूनि बिभ्रतम् ||
etam u tyam madacyutaṁ sahasradhāraṁ vṛṣabhaṁ divo duhuḥ | viśvā vasūni bibhratam ||
  • etam: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something significant in the immediate context.
  • u: This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
  • tyam: Meaning "that," it refers to something that is beyond the immediate context, perhaps something aspirational or divine.
  • madacyutaṁ: This term can be broken down into "mada," meaning "intoxication" or "joy," and "acyuta," meaning "infallible" or "imperishable." Together, it refers to the infallible joy or bliss that is eternal.
  • sahasradhāraṁ: This word means "one who has a thousand streams" or "one who is abundant." It symbolizes richness and the flow of life or resources.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This translates to "bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in Vedic literature.
  • divo: Meaning "of the heavens" or "divine," it indicates a connection to the celestial or spiritual realm.
  • duhuḥ: This means "to milk" or "to draw out," suggesting the act of extracting nourishment or essence from something.
  • viśvā: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
  • vasūni: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," referring to the abundance of resources and blessings.
  • bibhratam: Meaning "to hold" or "to bear," it suggests the act of carrying or sustaining something valuable.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and embrace the divine joy and abundance that exists in the universe. It emphasizes the importance of both the immediate and the aspirational, suggesting that one should draw from the infinite resources of the divine while being grounded in the present.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as one draws nourishment from the divine, individuals can seek joy and strength from their experiences and relationships, fostering personal development.
  • Community Building: The idea of holding universal wealth can inspire communities to work together, sharing resources and supporting one another to create a prosperous environment.
  • Spiritual Practices: In meditation or prayer, one can focus on the joy and abundance represented in this mantra, cultivating a sense of gratitude and connection to the divine.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of joy, abundance, and the divine. It encourages individuals to recognize the wealth of experiences and resources available to them, both in the material and spiritual realms. By embracing this perspective, one can lead a more fulfilling and enriched life, contributing positively to themselves and their communities.
