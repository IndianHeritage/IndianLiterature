शचीभिर नः शचीवसू दिवा नक्तं दशस्यतम् |मा वां रात्रि उप दासत कदा चनास्मद रातिः कदा चन ||
śacībhir naḥ śacīvasū divā naktaṁ daśasyatam | mā vāṁ rātir upa dasat kadā canāsmad rātiḥ kadā cana ||
  • śacībhir: This word refers to the "power" or "strength" that comes from divine or celestial sources. In real life, this can be likened to the support we receive from our community or mentors that empowers us to achieve our goals.
  • naḥ: This means "to us" or "for us," indicating a collective aspect. It emphasizes the importance of community and shared experiences in our lives.
  • śacīvasū: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with strength." It suggests that we should seek the company of those who inspire and uplift us, much like surrounding ourselves with positive influences in our personal and professional lives.
  • divā: Meaning "by day," this word signifies clarity, awareness, and the pursuit of knowledge. In a practical sense, it can represent the importance of being proactive and making informed decisions during our waking hours.
  • naktaṁ: This translates to "by night," symbolizing introspection, rest, and the subconscious mind. It reminds us of the value of reflection and rest in our daily lives, allowing us to recharge and gain insights.
  • daśasyatam: This word means "the best of ten" or "the highest." It suggests striving for excellence and aiming for the best outcomes in our endeavors, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • mā vāṁ: This phrase translates to "may not," indicating a plea or a wish. It reflects our desire to avoid negative experiences or outcomes.
  • rātir: This means "night" or "darkness," which can symbolize challenges or difficulties we face. It serves as a reminder that challenges are a part of life, and we must navigate through them.
  • upa dasat: This phrase means "approach" or "come near." It signifies the importance of being open to new experiences and learning opportunities that come our way.
  • kadā canāsmad: This translates to "at any time" or "whenever." It emphasizes the unpredictability of life and the need to be prepared for various situations.
  • rātiḥ: This word refers to "delight" or "joy." It highlights the importance of seeking happiness and fulfillment in our lives.
  • kadā cana: This means "at any time" or "whenever," reinforcing the idea that joy and challenges can arise unexpectedly.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength and support from divine sources and the community while navigating the dualities of life—day and night, joy and challenges. It encourages us to strive for excellence, embrace new experiences, and remain open to the unpredictability of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, relationships, and professional growth, reminding us to cultivate positive influences, reflect on our experiences, and seek joy amidst challenges.
