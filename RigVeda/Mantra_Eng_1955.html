उप नः पितव आ चर शिवः शिवाभिर ऊतिभिः |मयोभुर अद्विषेण्यः सखा सुशेवं अद्वयाः ||
upa naḥ pitav ā cara śivaḥ śivābhir ūtibhiḥ | mayobhur adviṣeṇyaḥ sakhā suśevo advayāḥ ||
  • upa naḥ: This phrase translates to "may it come to us" or "may it approach us." It signifies a call for blessings or positive energies to come into one's life.
  • pitav: This word means "father" or "paternal." It represents guidance, protection, and the nurturing aspect of a father figure.
  • ā cara: This translates to "to act" or "to move." It implies the importance of taking action in life, suggesting that one should not remain passive but actively pursue their goals.
  • śivaḥ: This term means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It is often associated with the divine and represents the idea of goodness and positivity.
  • śivābhir ūtibhiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "with auspicious blessings." It emphasizes the need for divine support and positive influences in our endeavors.
  • mayobhur: This means "may I be filled" or "may I be endowed." It reflects a desire for abundance and fulfillment in life.
  • adviṣeṇyaḥ: This translates to "without enmity" or "free from hatred." It highlights the importance of harmony and peace in relationships and interactions.
  • sakhā: This word means "friend." It signifies companionship, support, and the value of having trustworthy allies in life.
  • suśevo: This translates to "well-doing" or "good deeds." It emphasizes the significance of positive actions and intentions in one's life.
  • advayāḥ: This means "non-dual" or "without duality." It suggests unity and the interconnectedness of all beings, promoting a sense of oneness.

Conclusion: The mantra "upa naḥ pitav ā cara śivaḥ śivābhir ūtibhiḥ | mayobhur adviṣeṇyaḥ sakhā suśevo advayāḥ ||" encapsulates a profound message about seeking blessings, taking action, and fostering positive relationships. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to actively pursue their goals while seeking divine guidance and support.
  • In relationships, it emphasizes the importance of nurturing friendships and maintaining harmony, free from hatred and conflict.
  • In community building, it promotes unity and collaboration, reminding us that we are all interconnected and should work towards the common good.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to embrace positivity, take action, and cultivate meaningful relationships in our lives, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
