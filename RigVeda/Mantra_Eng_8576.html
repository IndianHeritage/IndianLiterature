जुष्टो मदाय देवताता इन्दो परि ष्णुना धन्व सानो अव्ये |सहस्रधारः सुरभिर अदब्धः परि श्रव वाजसातौ नृषह्ये ||
juṣṭo madāya devatāta indo pari ṣṇunā dhanva sāno avye | sahasradhāraḥ surabhir adabdhaḥ pari srava vājasātau nṛṣahye ||
  • juṣṭo: This word can be translated as "pleasing" or "enjoyable." It signifies the importance of satisfaction and joy in life.
  • madāya: This term refers to "intoxication" or "ecstasy," often in a spiritual sense. It suggests a state of bliss that can be achieved through devotion or connection with the divine.
  • devatāta: This means "to the gods" or "divine." It emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • indo: This word refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • pari ṣṇunā: This can be interpreted as "with offerings" or "with reverence." It highlights the importance of gratitude and respect in spiritual practices.
  • dhanva: This translates to "bow" or "weapon," symbolizing readiness and the ability to defend oneself or one's beliefs.
  • sāno: This word means "to be present" or "to exist." It signifies the importance of being aware and conscious in one's actions.
  • avyé: This term can be understood as "unharmed" or "untouched," suggesting a state of purity and protection.
  • sahasradhāraḥ: This means "with a thousand streams" or "abundant." It symbolizes prosperity and the flow of resources in life.
  • surabhir: This translates to "fragrant" or "sweet," often associated with the beauty of nature and the pleasures of life.
  • adabdhaḥ: This means "unharmed" or "untouched," reinforcing the idea of safety and security in one's endeavors.
  • pari srava: This can be interpreted as "flowing all around," indicating abundance and the interconnectedness of life.
  • vājasātau: This term refers to "in the context of strength" or "in the realm of power," emphasizing the importance of courage and resilience.
  • nṛṣahye: This means "for the benefit of mankind" or "for the welfare of people," highlighting the mantra's focus on collective well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual fulfillment, abundance, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the importance of joy, devotion, and respect towards the divine, while also highlighting the need for strength and resilience in the face of challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or community gatherings, where individuals seek to connect with the divine and foster a sense of unity and prosperity. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with joy, gratitude, and a commitment to the welfare of all beings.
