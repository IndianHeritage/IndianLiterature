अप्स्व अंतर अमृतं अप्सु भेषजं अपाम उता प्रशस्तये |देवा भवत वाजिनः ||
apsv a1ntar amṛtam apsu bheṣajam apām uta praśastaye | devā bhavata vājinaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsu: in the waters
    • amṛtam: the immortal or the nectar
    • bheṣajam: the healing or medicine
    • apām: of the waters
    • uta: and
    • praśastaye: for the praise or glory
    • devā: the gods
    • bhavata: may you become
    • vājinaḥ: strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters as a source of immortality and healing.
    • The waters are not just physical; they symbolize purity, life, and sustenance.
    • The invocation to the gods suggests a desire for strength and vitality, both physically and spiritually.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, water is considered sacred and is used in rituals for purification and healing.
    • Modern medicine often looks to nature, including water, for healing properties, such as in hydrotherapy.
    • In daily life, staying hydrated is essential for maintaining health and vitality, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the importance of water.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water as a life-giving and healing force.
    • It encourages us to recognize the divine qualities of nature and seek strength and health through its elements.
    • In a broader sense, it reminds us of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of respecting and preserving our natural resources.
