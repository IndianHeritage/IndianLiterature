इमा अस्य प्रतूर्तयः पदं जुषन्त यद दिवि |नाभा यज्ञस्य सं दधुर यथा विदे ||
imā asya pratūrtayaḥ padaṁ juṣanta yad divi | nābhā yajñasya saṁ dadhur yathā vide ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imā: these
    • asya: of this
    • pratūrtayaḥ: the offerings or the results
    • padaṁ: position or place
    • juṣanta: enjoying or cherishing
    • yad: which
    • divi: in the heavens
    • nābhā: the center or the essence
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice or offering
    • saṁ dadhur: they hold or maintain
    • yathā: as
    • vide: to know or to understand
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings that are cherished in the heavens.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the essence of sacrifices and how they are maintained.
    • The mantra suggests a connection between earthly actions (sacrifices) and their divine significance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this can be related to the concept of giving back to the community through charitable acts.
    • Just as sacrifices in the Vedic context were meant to please the deities, modern acts of kindness can be seen as offerings to the greater good.
    • For instance, participating in community service or environmental conservation can be viewed as a way of cherishing the world we live in.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and their impact on the divine and the world around us.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in meaningful actions that contribute to the welfare of society and the environment.
    • In essence, it teaches that our offerings, whether material or spiritual, should be cherished and maintained for the greater good.
