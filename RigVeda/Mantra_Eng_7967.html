स सुतः पीतये वृषा सोमः पवित्रे अर्षति |विघ्नान रक्षांसी देवयुः ||
sa sutaḥ pītaye vṛṣā somaḥ pavitre arṣati | vighnan rakṣāṁsi devayuḥ ||
  • sa sutaḥ: This phrase translates to "he (the soma) is the son." In the context of the Rigveda, Soma is often personified and revered as a divine entity. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • pītaye: This means "for the drinker" or "for the one who consumes." It signifies the act of consuming Soma, which is believed to bestow strength and immortality.
  • vṛṣā: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It emphasizes the potency of Soma and its ability to invigorate and empower those who partake in it.
  • somaḥ: Referring directly to Soma, this term is central to the mantra. Soma is not just a drink but symbolizes the divine nectar that nourishes the soul and spirit.
  • pavitre: This means "pure" or "sacred." It highlights the sanctity of Soma and the purity of the rituals associated with its consumption.
  • arṣati: This translates to "shines" or "radiates." It suggests that the effects of Soma are illuminating and transformative, bringing clarity and enlightenment.
  • vighnan: This word means "obstacles" or "hindrances." It acknowledges the challenges and barriers that one may face in life.
  • rakṣāṁsi: This translates to "protection" or "guardians." It refers to the divine forces or energies that safeguard individuals from harm.
  • devayuḥ: This means "the divine beings" or "gods." It signifies the connection between the individual and the divine, invoking the presence of higher powers in the process of seeking protection and strength.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa sutaḥ pītaye vṛṣā somaḥ pavitre arṣati | vighnan rakṣāṁsi devayuḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine strength and protection through the sacred drink of Soma. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call to seek purity and strength in our actions and thoughts, while also recognizing the obstacles we face. Just as the consumption of Soma is believed to empower and purify, individuals can draw on their inner strength and seek divine guidance to overcome challenges. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual nourishment and the protective forces that surround us, encouraging us to remain connected to the divine in our daily lives.
