अश्विनां वर्तिर अस्मद आ गोमद दसराऽ हिरण्यवत् |अर्वाग् रथं समनसां न यच्छतम् ||
aśvinā vartir asmad ā gomad dasrā hiraṇyavat | arvāg rathaṁ samanasā ni yacchatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • vartir: To be present or to exist.
    • asmad: Us or our.
    • ā: From or towards.
    • gomad: Wealthy or prosperous, often associated with cattle.
    • dasrā: Swift or quick.
    • hiraṇyavat: Golden or possessing gold, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • arvāg: Forward or ahead.
    • rathaṁ: Chariot, symbolizing movement and progress.
    • samanasā: With harmony or balance.
    • ni: Not or without.
    • yacchatam: To grant or to give.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Aśvins, asking for their presence and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and swiftness in achieving goals.
    • The mention of a chariot symbolizes the journey of life, which should be harmonious and balanced.
    • It suggests that one should seek wealth and prosperity, but not at the cost of harmony and balance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of success. A company may strive for rapid growth (dasrā) while ensuring that its operations remain ethical and balanced (samanasā).
    • In personal life, individuals may seek to achieve their goals quickly but should also focus on maintaining healthy relationships and personal well-being.
    • In community development, the mantra can inspire initiatives that promote both economic growth (gomad) and social harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking prosperity and success while maintaining balance and harmony in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also includes health, relationships, and community well-being.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals and societies to pursue their goals with swiftness and determination, but always with a mindful approach to harmony and balance.
