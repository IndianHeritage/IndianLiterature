आदित्याऽव हि ख्याताधि कूलाद iva स्पशः |सुतीरथम अरवतों यथानु नो नेषथा सुगम अनहसो वा ऊतयः सूतयः वा ऊतयः ||
ādityā ava hi khyatādhi kūlād iva spaśaḥ | sutīrtham arvato yathānu no neṣathā sugam anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityā: O Adityas (the solar deities)
    • ava: come down
    • hi: indeed
    • khyatādhi: known or recognized
    • kūlād: from the banks or shores
    • iva: like
    • spaśaḥ: the touch or contact
    • sutīrtham: well-constructed or well-formed
    • arvato: from the heights
    • yathā: as
    • anu: following
    • no: us
    • neṣathā: lead or guide
    • sugam: easy or pleasant
    • anehaso: without darkness or obscurity
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: the rising or ascent
    • suūtayo: the good rising or ascent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Adityas, the solar deities, asking them to come down and provide their blessings.
    • The phrase "from the banks" suggests a source of abundance and nourishment, akin to how rivers provide life to the land.
    • The reference to "the touch" indicates a desire for divine contact, which can be interpreted as a longing for spiritual connection.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance, asking the deities to lead us towards a path that is easy and free from darkness.
    • It highlights the significance of rising or ascent, both physically and spiritually, suggesting a journey towards enlightenment and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance during challenging times, much like the invocation of the Adityas for support.
    • People may turn to nature, such as rivers or mountains, as sources of inspiration and strength, paralleling the metaphor of banks providing nourishment.
    • In spiritual practices, many seek a connection with the divine through meditation or prayer, reflecting the desire for "the touch" of the divine.
    • In leadership, guiding others towards a clear and positive path is essential, resonating with the mantra's call for guidance towards an easy journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and the pursuit of a life that is abundant, enlightened, and free from obstacles.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to leadership roles, emphasizing the need for support and clarity.
    • By invoking the blessings of the Adityas, individuals can aspire to rise above challenges and seek a path that leads to fulfillment and joy.
