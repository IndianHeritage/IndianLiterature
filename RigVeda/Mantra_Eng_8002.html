गोमन नः सोम वीरवत अश्वावत वाजवत सुतः |पवस्व बृहतीर इषः ||
goman naḥ soma vīravad aśvāvad vājavat sutaḥ | pavasva bṛhatīr iṣaḥ ||
  • goman naḥ - "May the cows (or wealth) be ours": This phrase signifies the importance of prosperity and abundance in life. Cows in ancient times were a symbol of wealth and nourishment. In modern terms, this can relate to the pursuit of financial stability and resources that sustain us.
  • soma vīravad - "Like the Soma, strong and heroic": Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, associated with immortality and divine inspiration. This part of the mantra emphasizes the need for strength and valor, akin to the qualities of a hero. In real life, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of courage and resilience in facing challenges.
  • aśvāvad vājavat sutaḥ - "Like the horses, swift and powerful": Horses symbolize speed, power, and freedom. This phrase encourages us to embody these qualities in our endeavors. In contemporary life, this can relate to the drive for success and the ability to navigate through life's challenges with agility and strength.
  • pavasva bṛhatīr iṣaḥ - "May you flow, O great one, with strength": This line invokes a sense of divine energy and flow. It suggests that we should seek to harness our inner strength and allow it to guide us. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of self-awareness and the ability to channel our energies towards constructive goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of wealth, strength, speed, and divine energy. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to pursue prosperity, embody courage, strive for success, and harness our inner strength. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can cultivate a balanced and fulfilling existence, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
