सुष्टामाऽ रथः स्यमाऽ हरि ते मिम्यक्षा वज्रो नृपते गभस्तौ |शीभं राजान् सुपथा याह्य अर्वाङ् वर्धाम ते पपुषो वृष्ण्याऽनि ||
suṣṭhāmā rathaḥ suyamā harī te mimyakṣa vajro nṛpate gabhastau | śībhaṁ rājan supathā yāhy arvāṅ vardhāma te papuṣo vṛṣṇyāni ||
  • suṣṭhāmā rathaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the well-constructed chariot." In life, this can symbolize the importance of having a strong foundation or support system, much like a chariot needs a sturdy structure to function effectively.
  • suyamā: This means "well-directed" or "well-guided." It emphasizes the need for proper guidance in our endeavors. For example, in a career, having a mentor can help steer one in the right direction.
  • harī te mimyakṣa: This translates to "may the divine grant you." It reflects the idea of seeking blessings or divine intervention in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek guidance and support from a higher power.
  • vajro nṛpate gabhastau: "The thunderbolt, O king, is your brilliance." This signifies strength and power. In a leadership context, a strong leader is often compared to a thunderbolt, capable of making impactful decisions that illuminate the path for others.
  • śībhaṁ rājan: This means "O king, the auspicious one." It highlights the qualities of a good leader who brings prosperity and well-being to their people. For instance, a leader who prioritizes the welfare of their community can be seen as a "śībhaṁ rājan."
  • supathā yāhy: This translates to "may you walk on the good path." It encourages individuals to choose the right path in life, akin to making ethical decisions in business or personal relationships.
  • arvāṅ vardhāma: This means "may you grow in strength." It signifies the importance of personal growth and development. In real life, this can relate to continuous learning and self-improvement in various aspects of life.
  • te papuṣo vṛṣṇyāni: "May you attain the fruits of your efforts." This emphasizes the idea that hard work leads to rewards. In a practical sense, this can be seen in academic achievements or career advancements resulting from dedication and effort.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, guidance, and prosperity in life. It serves as a reminder to build a solid foundation, seek divine blessings, and choose the right path. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, leadership, and ethical decision-making, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
