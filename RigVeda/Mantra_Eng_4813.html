इन्द्र त्रिधातु शरणं त्रिवरूथं स्वस्तिमत् |छर्दिर यच्छ मघवद्भ्यश्च मह्यम् च यावयाऽदिद्युम् एभ्यः ||
indra tridhātu śaraṇaṁ trivarūthaṁ svastimat | chardir yaccha maghavadbhyaś ca mahyaṁ ca yāvayā didyum ebhyaḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Tridhātu: This term refers to the three realms or worlds: the physical, the mental, and the spiritual. It signifies the holistic nature of existence.
  • Śaraṇaṁ: This means refuge or shelter. It indicates seeking protection and guidance from a higher power.
  • Trivarūthaṁ: This can be interpreted as the three forms or manifestations. It suggests the diversity of existence and the various ways in which divinity can be perceived.
  • Svastimat: This word denotes auspiciousness and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of health, prosperity, and harmony in life.
  • Chardir: This term refers to the act of giving or bestowing. It highlights the significance of generosity and sharing.
  • Yaccha: This means "give" or "grant." It is a call to the divine for blessings and support.
  • Maghavadbhyaś: This refers to the powerful or mighty ones, often associated with wealth and abundance. It signifies the importance of strength in achieving goals.
  • Mahyaṁ: This means "to me" or "for me," indicating a personal plea for assistance and blessings.
  • Tāvayā: This term means "by your grace" or "through your power," emphasizing reliance on divine intervention.
  • Didyum: This refers to light or illumination, symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • Ebhyḥ: This means "from these" or "from them," indicating a connection to the divine sources of support and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for protection, guidance, and blessings from Indra, the king of gods, while acknowledging the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual realms. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of uncertainty or challenge, where individuals seek strength and support. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, generosity, and the pursuit of well-being. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of trust in the divine and foster a harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
