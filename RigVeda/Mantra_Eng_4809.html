इन्द्र ज्येष्ठं न आ भरम् ओजिष्टम् पपुरि श्रवः |येनेमे चित्र वज्रहस्त रोडसी ओभे सुशिप्र प्राह ||
indra jyeṣṭhaṁ na ā bharam̐ ojiṣṭham papuri śravaḥ | yeneme citra vajrahasta rodasī obhe suśipra prāḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Jyeṣṭham: Means "the eldest" or "the most powerful," indicating Indra's supreme position among the deities.
  • Na ā bharam̐: Translates to "do not burden" or "do not weigh down," suggesting a plea for support without overwhelming pressure.
  • Ojiṣṭham: Means "the most energetic" or "the most vigorous," highlighting the dynamic nature of Indra's power.
  • Papuri: Refers to "the one who brings forth" or "the creator," emphasizing the generative aspect of Indra's energy.
  • Śravaḥ: Means "fame" or "glory," indicating the importance of reputation and recognition in one's actions.
  • Yeneme: Translates to "by whom" or "through whom," pointing to the source of strength and support.
  • Citra: Means "varied" or "colorful," suggesting diversity and richness in experiences.
  • Vajrahasta: Refers to "the one with a thunderbolt in hand," symbolizing Indra's might and ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Rodasī: Means "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," indicating the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • Obhe: Translates to "both" or "together," emphasizing unity and harmony.
  • Suśipra: Means "swift" or "quick," highlighting the importance of timely action and response.
  • Prāḥ: Refers to "the dawn" or "the new beginning," symbolizing hope and renewal.

The mantra essentially invokes Indra, the powerful deity, asking him not to burden the devotee while providing strength and energy. It emphasizes the importance of glory and fame in one's actions, while also recognizing the diverse experiences of life. The imagery of Indra with a thunderbolt signifies the ability to face challenges and overcome them, connecting the divine with the earthly existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from Indra's strength and seek to empower their teams without overwhelming them.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own strengths and the importance of maintaining a balance between ambition and humility.
  • Community Support: The mantra encourages collective action and support, reminding us of the importance of unity in overcoming challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between power and responsibility, urging individuals to seek strength while being mindful of the burdens they place on themselves and others. It highlights the significance of unity, swift action, and the pursuit of glory in a way that is harmonious and supportive.
