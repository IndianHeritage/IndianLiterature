युध्मस्य ते वृषभस्य स्वराज उग्रस्य यूनः स्थविरस्य घृष्वेः |अजूर्यतो वज्रीणो वीर्याणीन्द्र श्रुतस्य महतो महानि ||
yudhmasya te vṛṣabhasya svarāja ugrasya yūnaḥ sthavirasya ghṛṣveḥ | ajūryato vajriṇo vīryā3ṇīndra śrutasya mahato mahāni ||
  • yudhmasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the battle" or "of the struggle." It signifies the challenges and conflicts one faces in life.
  • te: This means "your," indicating possession or connection to the subject being addressed.
  • vṛṣabhasya: Translated as "of the bull," it symbolizes strength, power, and virility. The bull is often seen as a symbol of hard work and determination.
  • svarāja: This word means "self-rule" or "sovereignty." It emphasizes the importance of autonomy and self-governance in one's life.
  • ugrasya: Meaning "fierce" or "intense," this term reflects the passionate and vigorous approach one should take towards challenges.
  • yūnaḥ: This translates to "youth" or "young," representing vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and change.
  • sthavirasya: Meaning "of the aged" or "of the wise," it highlights the value of experience and wisdom that comes with age.
  • ghṛṣveḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of the strong" or "of the powerful," reinforcing the idea of resilience and strength in overcoming obstacles.
  • ajūryato: This means "from the unbroken" or "from the eternal," suggesting a source of strength that is constant and unwavering.
  • vajriṇo: Translated as "of the thunderbolt," it symbolizes immense power and the ability to overcome challenges with force.
  • vīryāṇī: This term means "of the brave" or "of the courageous," emphasizing the importance of bravery in facing life's battles.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic literature, Indra represents leadership, strength, and the ability to protect and provide.
  • śrutasya: This means "of the heard" or "of the known," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding one's actions.
  • mahato: Meaning "of the great," it signifies the importance of greatness in character and deeds.
  • mahāni: This translates to "the great things" or "the great achievements," emphasizing the significance of striving for greatness in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of facing life's battles with strength, wisdom, and courage. It encourages individuals to embrace their struggles, drawing on their inner power and the wisdom gained from experience. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various challenges, such as pursuing career goals, overcoming personal obstacles, or leading a community. By embodying the qualities of strength, bravery, and wisdom, one can navigate through life's complexities and achieve greatness. The mantra serves as a reminder that true sovereignty comes from within, and with determination and resilience, one can conquer any challenge that arises.
