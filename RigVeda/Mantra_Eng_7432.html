दाशेम कस्य मनसा यज्ञस्य सहसो यहो |कदु वोच इदं नमः ||
dāśema kasya manasā yajñasya sahaso yaho | kad u voca idaṁ namaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dāśema kasya manasā yajñasya sahaso yaho | kad u voca idaṁ namaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Whose mind shall we invoke for the strength of the sacrifice? When shall we call upon this (divine) name?"
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dāśema: "We shall invoke" - This indicates a call to action, a desire to connect with the divine.
    • kasya: "Whose" - This word signifies the search for a higher power or deity.
    • manasā: "Mind" - Refers to the mental and spiritual focus required in rituals.
    • yajñasya: "Of the sacrifice" - Represents the act of giving, whether in a ritualistic or metaphorical sense.
    • sahaso: "Strength" - Indicates the power and energy derived from the divine.
    • yaho: "When" - A question of timing, reflecting the importance of the right moment in spiritual practices.
    • kad: "When" - Reiterates the inquiry about the appropriate time for invocation.
    • u: "And" - A conjunction that connects thoughts.
    • voca: "Shall we call" - A direct appeal to the divine.
    • idaṁ: "This" - Refers to the specific name or aspect of the divine being invoked.
    • namaḥ: "Salutation" - A respectful greeting or acknowledgment of the divine presence.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and focus in spiritual practices.
    • It reflects the human quest for connection with the divine, seeking guidance and strength.
    • The invocation of a higher power is a common theme in many cultures, illustrating the universal desire for support in times of need.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and guidance through prayer or meditation, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • During challenging times, people may reflect on their values and beliefs, calling upon a higher power for support, akin to the essence of this mantra.
    • In community rituals or gatherings, the collective invocation of strength and unity can be seen as a modern-day application of this ancient practice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to focus their minds and intentions, fostering a deeper connection with the spiritual realm.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of community and shared beliefs in navigating life's challenges.
