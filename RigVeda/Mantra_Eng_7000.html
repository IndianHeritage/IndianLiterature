अपाम सोमम् अमृताऽभूमागन्म ज्योतिर अविदाम देवान् |किं नूनम् अस्मान् कृत्वद् अरातिः किं उ धूर्तिर अमृत मर्त्यस्य ||
apāma somam amṛtā abhūmāganma jyotir avidāma devān | kiṁ nūnam asmān kṛṇavad arātiḥ kim u dhūrtir amṛta martyasya ||
  • Translation: "We have drunk the Soma, we have become immortal; we have attained the light of the gods. What, indeed, is our enmity? What is the deceit of the mortal?"

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • apāma: We have drunk
    • somam: Soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • amṛtā: Immortal
    • abhūmāganma: We have attained
    • jyotir: Light
    • avidāma: We have known
    • devān: The gods
    • kiṁ: What
    • nūnam: Indeed
    • asmān: Us
    • kṛṇavad: Is our enmity
    • arātiḥ: Enmity
    • kim: What
    • u: Or
    • dhūrtir: Deceit
    • amṛta: Immortal
    • martyasya: Of the mortal

  • Contextual Significance:
    • The mantra speaks to the transformative power of the Soma, symbolizing spiritual enlightenment and the quest for immortality.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, which can elevate one's existence beyond the mundane.
    • For example, individuals who engage in lifelong learning or spiritual practices often report a sense of enlightenment and connection to something greater than themselves.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher truths and understanding our place in the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to transcend conflicts and deceit, fostering unity and enlightenment.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in daily life by promoting harmony in relationships and striving for personal growth through education and self-reflection.
