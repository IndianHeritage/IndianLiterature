न यं विविक्तो रोडसी नान्तरिक्षाणि वज्रीणम् |अमाद इद अस्य तित्विषे सम ओजसः ||
na yaṁ vivikto rodasī nāntarikṣāṇi vajriṇam | amād id asya titviṣe sam ojasaḥ ||
  • na yaṁ vivikto rodasī: This phrase translates to "He who is not separated from the two worlds." Here, "rodaśī" refers to the two realms of existence: the physical world and the spiritual world. This suggests a unity between the material and the spiritual, emphasizing that true understanding encompasses both realms.
  • nāntarikṣāṇi vajriṇam: This means "the one who is not confined to the space between." "Antarikṣa" refers to the space or the atmosphere, and "vajriṇam" can be interpreted as the powerful or the mighty. This line indicates that the divine or the ultimate reality transcends all physical limitations and boundaries.
  • amād id asya titviṣe: This translates to "the one who is beyond the reach of death." "Amād" signifies immortality or eternal existence, while "titviṣe" refers to the essence or the nature of being. This line emphasizes the eternal nature of the divine, suggesting that it is beyond the cycle of birth and death.
  • sam ojasaḥ: This phrase means "with all its power." "Sam" indicates completeness or totality, and "ojasaḥ" refers to energy or strength. This suggests that the divine presence is not only eternal but also immensely powerful, encompassing all forms of energy and vitality.

  • Real-Life Example: In our daily lives, we often find ourselves caught between the material and spiritual aspects of existence. For instance, a successful businessperson may achieve great wealth (material) but may feel unfulfilled without a sense of purpose or connection to something greater (spiritual). This mantra reminds us to seek balance and unity between these two realms.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to look beyond the physical limitations and to understand the eternal nature of existence. This understanding can lead to a more profound sense of peace and fulfillment.
  • Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in meditation practices where one seeks to transcend the physical realm and connect with the spiritual essence. It can also serve as a reminder during challenging times, encouraging individuals to rise above their circumstances and recognize their eternal nature.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the unity between the material and spiritual worlds, the eternal nature of existence, and the immense power that lies within. By embracing these teachings, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the universe, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
