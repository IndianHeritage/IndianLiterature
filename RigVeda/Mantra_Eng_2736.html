अदेदिष्ट वृत्रहा गोपतीर गान्तः कृष्णाम् अरुषैर धामभिर गात् |प्र सूनृताः दिशमान ऋतेन दुरश्च विश्वा अवृणोद अप स्वाह ||
adediṣṭa vṛtrahā gopatir gā antaḥ kṛṣṇām̐ aruṣair dhāmabhir gāt | pra sūnṛtā diśamāna ṛtena duraś ca viśvā avṛṇod apa svāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • adediṣṭa: commanded or directed
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra (a dragon or serpent representing obstacles)
    • gopatir: the protector or lord of the cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • gā: song or hymn
    • antaḥ: within or inside
    • kṛṣṇām̐: black or dark (often referring to the divine)
    • aruṣair: red or bright (often symbolizing energy or vitality)
    • dhāmabhir: with the realms or abodes
    • gāt: to go or to move
    • pra: forth or forward
    • sūnṛtā: with truth or righteousness
    • diśamāna: directing or guiding
    • ṛtena: with the cosmic order or truth
    • duraś: difficult or hard
    • ca: and
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • avṛṇod: he covered or enveloped
    • apa: away or from
    • svāḥ: a term of reverence or affirmation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine command that overcomes obstacles (Vritra) and protects the essence of life (symbolized by cows).
    • The imagery of darkness (kṛṣṇām̐) and brightness (aruṣair) suggests a balance of energies, representing the duality of existence.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth (sūnṛtā) and cosmic order (ṛtena) in guiding our actions and decisions.
    • It suggests that even in difficult times (duraś), there is a universal force (viśvā) that envelops and protects us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing a health crisis or financial difficulties, individuals often seek guidance and strength from their beliefs, echoing the protective nature of the mantra.
    • In leadership, the balance of dark and light can represent the need for leaders to be both firm and compassionate, guiding their teams through challenges with integrity.
    • In environmental conservation, the protection of natural resources (symbolized by cows) reflects the mantra's call for stewardship and respect for life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine guidance in overcoming life's obstacles.
    • It highlights the significance of truth and righteousness in our actions, encouraging us to seek balance in our lives.
    • In a broader context, it calls for a collective responsibility to protect and nurture our environment and communities, resonating with the universal themes of care and protection.
