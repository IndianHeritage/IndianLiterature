तां अस्य ज्येष्ठम् इन्द्रियं सचन्ते ता ईं आ क्षेति स्वधया मदन्तीः |ता ईं विश्वो न राजानं वृणाना बीभत्सुवो अप वृत्राद अतिष्ठन् ||
tā asya jyeṣṭham indriyaṁ sacante tā īm ā kṣeti svadhayā madantīḥ | tā īṁ viśo na rājānaṁ vṛṇānā bībhatsuvo apa vṛtrād atiṣṭhan ||
  • tā asya jyeṣṭham indriyaṁ sacante: This phrase can be translated to "They who are the eldest among the senses." Here, "tā" refers to "they," "asya" means "of this," "jyeṣṭham" translates to "eldest" or "most important," and "indriyaṁ" means "senses." This suggests that there are certain senses or faculties that hold greater significance in our perception of the world.
  • tā īm ā kṣeti svadhayā madantīḥ: This part translates to "They who are endowed with their own power." "īṁ" refers to "they," "ā kṣeti" means "see" or "perceive," "svadhayā" translates to "by their own power," and "madantīḥ" can be interpreted as "those who are intoxicated with joy." This indicates that those who are aware of their own strengths and capabilities can perceive the world more clearly and joyfully.
  • tā īṁ viśo na rājānaṁ vṛṇānā: This translates to "They who do not choose the kings of the people." "viśo" means "of the people," "na" translates to "not," "rājānaṁ" means "kings," and "vṛṇānā" refers to "choosing." This suggests a sense of independence from authority or leadership, emphasizing the importance of self-governance and personal choice.
  • bībhatsuvo apa vṛtrād atiṣṭhan: This can be translated as "They who have overcome the fear of obstacles." "bībhatsuvo" means "those who are fearless," "apa" translates to "from," "vṛtrād" refers to "obstacles," and "atiṣṭhan" means "have overcome." This highlights the idea of courage and resilience in the face of challenges.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of self-awareness, personal power, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and to make choices that align with their true selves, rather than being swayed by external authorities.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their strengths and capabilities, fostering a sense of self-confidence and empowerment.
    • Leadership: Leaders can take inspiration from this mantra to encourage autonomy and self-governance within their teams, promoting a culture of trust and independence.
    • Overcoming Challenges: In facing personal or professional obstacles, one can draw strength from the idea of being fearless and resilient, motivating themselves to push through difficulties.
