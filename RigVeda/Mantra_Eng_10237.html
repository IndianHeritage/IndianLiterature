धाताऽधातृणां भुवनस्य यस पतिर देवं त्रातारं अभिमातिषाहम् |इमं यज्ञं अश्विनोभा बृहस्पतिर देवाः पान्तु यजमानं न्यर्थात् ||
dhātā dhātṝṇām bhuvanasya yas patir devaṁ trātāram abhimātiṣāham | imaṁ yajñam aśvinobhā bṛhaspatir devāḥ pāntu yajamānaṁ nyarthāt ||
  • dhātā dhātṝṇām: This phrase refers to the "sustainer" or "nourisher" of all beings. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a parent or guardian who provides for and nurtures their children, ensuring their growth and well-being.
  • bhuvanasya yas patir: This translates to "the lord of the universe." It signifies a higher power or divine force that governs the cosmos. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people look up to leaders or mentors who guide them through challenges.
  • devaṁ trātāram abhimātiṣāham: This means "I have the strength to protect the divine." It emphasizes the idea of being a protector or guardian of sacred values and principles. For instance, a teacher who defends the integrity of education embodies this protective spirit.
  • imaṁ yajñam aśvinobhā: This phrase refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering" made to the divine. In modern terms, this can be compared to acts of charity or community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, reflecting a spirit of selflessness.
  • bṛhaspatir devāḥ: This translates to "the great lord of the gods." It symbolizes wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be represented by scholars or wise individuals who share their insights and guide others towards enlightenment.
  • pāntu yajamānaṁ nyarthāt: This means "may the deities protect the one who offers." It highlights the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine, where offerings lead to blessings. In practical terms, this can be seen in how people often seek blessings or guidance through rituals and prayers in times of need.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of sustenance, protection, and divine guidance in our lives. It teaches us the importance of nurturing relationships, seeking wisdom, and contributing to the greater good through selfless acts. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to take on roles of responsibility, whether as caregivers, leaders, or community members, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their actions. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious environment that reflects the essence of this ancient wisdom.
