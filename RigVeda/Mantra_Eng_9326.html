प्रयाजान मे अनुयाजांश च केवलान ऊर्जस्वन्तं हविषो दत्त भागम् |घृतं चापाम पुरुषं चौषधीनाम अग्नेश च दीर्घम आयुर अस्तु देवाः ||
prayājān me anuyājām̐ś ca kevalān ūrjasvantaṁ haviṣo datta bhāgam | ghṛtaṁ cāpām puruṣaṁ cauṣadhīnām agneś ca dīrgham āyur astu devāḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the offerings that I make, along with the pure and powerful portions, be given to the divine Agni. May the ghee and the essence of the herbs be offered to the divine, and may the gods grant us long life."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prayājān: offerings or sacrifices made with devotion.
    • anuyājām̐ś: the pure and powerful portions of the offerings.
    • ūrjasvantaṁ: strong, powerful, or energetic.
    • haviṣo: the essence or substance of the offerings.
    • datta: given or bestowed.
    • bhāgam: portion or share.
    • ghṛtaṁ: ghee, a sacred offering in Vedic rituals.
    • cāpām: essence or substance of herbs.
    • puruṣaṁ: the divine or the universal spirit.
    • cauṣadhīnām: of the herbs or plants.
    • agneś: referring to Agni, the fire god.
    • dīrgham: long or extended.
    • āyur: life or longevity.
    • astu: may it be or let it be.
    • devāḥ: the gods or divine beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, offerings are made to deities during festivals or rituals, similar to how ghee is offered to Agni in this mantra. For instance, during Diwali, people light lamps and offer sweets to invite prosperity and blessings.
    • In modern wellness practices, the use of herbs and natural substances for health is prevalent. This aligns with the mantra's reference to the essence of herbs, emphasizing the importance of nature in our well-being.
    • In personal development, the idea of invoking strength and energy can be seen in motivational practices where individuals seek to harness their inner power to achieve goals, akin to the invocation of 'ūrjasvantaṁ' or powerful energy.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of offerings, the power of nature, and the divine connection to longevity and strength. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude, the use of natural resources for health, and the pursuit of inner strength. By acknowledging the divine and the natural world, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and well-being in their lives. This mantra can be applicable in daily rituals, health practices, and personal growth endeavors, reinforcing the interconnectedness of life, nature, and spirituality.
