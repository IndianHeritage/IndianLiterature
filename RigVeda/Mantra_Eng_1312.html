अजोहवीद अश्विनौ तौग्र्यो वाम प्रोषः समुद्रम अव्यथिर जगन्वान् |निषṭम ऊहथुः सुयुजा रथेन मनोजवसा वृषणा स्वस्ति ||
ajohavīd aśvinā taugryo vām proḻhaḥ samudram avyathir jaganvān | niṣ ṭam ūhathuḥ suyujā rathena manojavasā vṛṣaṇā svasti ||
  • ajohavīd: This term can be broken down into "a" (not) and "johavīd" (to be born or created). It signifies the eternal, uncreated nature of the divine.
  • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities. They symbolize duality and balance.
  • taugryo: This word suggests strength or power, indicating the might of the Aśvins.
  • vām: Meaning "your," it indicates a personal connection or invocation to the divine.
  • proḻhaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," emphasizing the act of seeking divine assistance.
  • samudram: Translates to "ocean," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the source of life. It can also represent the collective unconscious or the universe.
  • avyathir: Meaning "unperturbed" or "calm," it reflects the stability and tranquility that the divine brings to the chaos of life.
  • jaganvān: This term refers to the "universe" or "world," indicating the all-encompassing nature of the divine presence.
  • niṣ ṭam: Meaning "to be established" or "to be firm," it suggests the importance of grounding oneself in spiritual practices.
  • ūhathuḥ: This can be interpreted as "to be guided" or "to be led," indicating the divine guidance one seeks in life.
  • suyujā: Meaning "well-connected" or "well-yoked," it emphasizes the importance of harmony and unity in one's actions.
  • rathena: Translates to "by the chariot," symbolizing the journey of life and the means by which one travels through experiences.
  • manojavasā: This term means "swift as the mind," indicating the speed and agility of thought and intention.
  • vṛṣaṇā: Refers to "strength" or "power," reinforcing the idea of harnessing inner strength.
  • svasti: Meaning "well-being" or "prosperity," it is a blessing for health, happiness, and success.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and strength in navigating the complexities of life. It emphasizes the importance of connection with the divine, the pursuit of inner strength, and the quest for well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or challenge, serving as a reminder to seek balance, remain calm amidst chaos, and harness one's inner power to achieve prosperity and harmony. It encourages individuals to invoke the divine presence in their lives, fostering a sense of peace and direction in their personal journeys.
