स्तृणीत बर्हिरानुषग्घृतपृष्ठम् मनिषिणः |यत्रामृतस्य चक्षुणम् ||
stṛṇīta barhir ānuṣag ghṛtapṛṣṭham manīṣiṇaḥ | yatrāmṛtasya cakṣaṇam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • stṛṇīta: Spread or laid out
    • barhir: Grass or a covering
    • ānuṣag: Following or in accordance with
    • ghṛtapṛṣṭham: With a ghee-covered surface
    • manīṣiṇaḥ: Of the wise or the enlightened
    • yatra: Where
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality or eternal truth
    • cakṣaṇam: Vision or sight
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of spreading a covering, which can be interpreted as creating a sacred space or environment.
    • The use of "ghṛtapṛṣṭham" suggests that this space is not just any ordinary space but one that is sanctified or made pure, often associated with offerings in rituals.
    • The phrase "manīṣiṇaḥ" indicates that this act is performed by the wise, suggesting that wisdom and enlightenment are essential in creating such a space.
    • Finally, "yatrāmṛtasya cakṣaṇam" implies that in this sacred space, one can attain a vision of immortality or eternal truth, highlighting the spiritual significance of the environment created.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, creating a sacred space for meditation or prayer is common. For instance, a personal altar at home can be seen as a modern interpretation of "stṛṇīta barhir," where individuals lay out items that hold spiritual significance.
    • During festivals, people often prepare their homes with decorations and offerings, akin to "ghṛtapṛṣṭham," to invite positive energies and blessings.
    • In workplaces, creating a conducive environment for creativity and productivity can be likened to this mantra, where the wise (leaders) set the tone for a space that fosters innovation and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of creating a sacred and wise environment that allows individuals to connect with deeper truths and insights.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that our surroundings can influence our thoughts and spiritual experiences.
    • In real life, whether in personal spaces, workplaces, or during rituals, the principles of this mantra can guide us to cultivate environments that promote wisdom, clarity, and a connection to the eternal.
