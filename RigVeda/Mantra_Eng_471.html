वन्दस्व मारुतं गणं त्वेषम् पनस्युम अर्किणम् |अस्मे वृद्धाऽसन्न इह ||
vandasva mārutaṁ gaṇaṁ tveṣam panasyum arkiṇam | asme vṛddhā asann iha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vandasva: "Worship" or "Adore"
    • mārutaṁ: "O Wind" or "O Maruts" (the storm deities)
    • gaṇaṁ: "Group" or "Host"
    • tveṣam: "Your" or "Of you"
    • panasyum: "Destruction" or "Dissolution"
    • arkiṇam: "Radiance" or "Light"
    • asme: "To us"
    • vṛddhā: "Increase" or "Growth"
    • asann: "Not present" or "Absent"
    • iha: "Here" or "In this place"
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to the Maruts, the deities of wind and storms, to come and bless the worshippers with their presence. It expresses a desire for the radiance and energy of the Maruts to bring growth and abundance to the devotees, while also acknowledging the absence of these divine forces in their lives at that moment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, farmers often invoke the Maruts during rituals to ensure timely rains and favorable weather conditions for their crops. The mantra symbolizes their hope for divine intervention in their agricultural endeavors.
    • In personal growth contexts, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, seeking inspiration and energy to overcome obstacles in their lives, much like the winds that clear the path for new beginnings.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine forces to bring about positive change and growth. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek support from higher powers in their pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as during times of need for guidance, support in personal or communal endeavors, and in rituals aimed at fostering abundance and prosperity. By acknowledging the presence of divine energies, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and purpose in their lives.
