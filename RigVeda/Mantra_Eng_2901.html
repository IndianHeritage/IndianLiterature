चरṣणीधृतं मगवाणं उक्थ्यं इन्द्रं गीरो बृहतीर अभ्य अनूषत |वावृधाणं पुरुहूतं सुवृक्तिभिर अमर्त्यं जरामाणं दिवे-दिवे ||
carṣaṇīdhṛtam maghavānam ukthya1m indraṁ giro bṛhatīr abhy anūṣata | vāvṛdhānam puruhūtaṁ suvṛktibhir amartyaṁ jaramāṇaṁ dive-dive ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • carṣaṇīdhṛtam: That which is held or supported by the earth.
    • maghavānam: The one who is powerful or mighty.
    • ukthya1m: The one who is invoked or called upon.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • giro: Words or hymns.
    • bṛhatīr: The great or vast.
    • abhy anūṣata: Who is worshipped or revered.
    • vāvṛdhānam: The one who is nourished or increased.
    • puruhūtaṁ: The one who is invoked by many.
    • suvṛktibhir: With good offerings or sacrifices.
    • amartyaṁ: The immortal or divine.
    • jaramāṇaṁ: The one who ages or is subject to decay.
    • dive-dive: Day by day or continuously.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity, emphasizing the importance of worship and offerings.
    • It highlights the relationship between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that the support of the earth is essential for the sustenance of life.
    • The phrase "day by day" indicates the continuous nature of devotion and the need for regular offerings to maintain a connection with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Indra is crucial during planting and harvesting seasons, as rain is vital for crops.
    • Regular rituals and offerings in temples reflect the belief in sustaining a relationship with the divine, ensuring blessings in daily life.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and strength from higher powers during challenging times, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in our daily lives and the need for continuous devotion.
    • It teaches us that our actions, offerings, and prayers can lead to nourishment and support from higher powers.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine, reminding us of our interconnectedness with the universe.
