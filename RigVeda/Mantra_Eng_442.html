क्रीळं वः शर्धो मारुतम् अनर्वाणं रथे शुभम् |कण्वा अभि प्र गायत ||
krīḻaṁ vaḥ śardho mārutam anarvāṇaṁ ratheśubham | kaṇvā abhi pra gāyata ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "We invoke the powerful Maruts, the divine beings of the storm, who are auspicious and bring forth prosperity. Let the wise ones sing praises to them."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • krīḻaṁ: This term refers to invoking or calling upon.
    • vaḥ: This means "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to the Maruts.
    • śardho: This word signifies strength or power, often associated with the Maruts.
    • mārutam: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy and vitality.
    • anarvāṇaṁ: This term means auspicious or beneficial, indicating the positive nature of the Maruts.
    • ratheśubham: This can be interpreted as "bringing good fortune" or "auspiciousness in the chariot," symbolizing progress and movement.
    • kaṇvā: Refers to the wise or learned individuals, often associated with sages or seers.
    • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating an action of praise or invocation.
    • pra gāyata: This means "to sing" or "to chant," emphasizing the act of expressing reverence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking the Maruts can be seen as a way to seek favorable weather conditions for crops, highlighting the importance of nature's forces in daily life.
    • During festivals, people often chant mantras to invite blessings and prosperity, similar to how this mantra calls upon the Maruts for auspiciousness.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to harness collective strength for a common goal.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of the Maruts, symbolizing the harnessing of natural forces for positive outcomes. It teaches us the importance of recognizing and respecting the energies around us, whether in nature or in our personal endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance in challenging situations, fostering teamwork, or celebrating communal achievements. By invoking the auspiciousness of the Maruts, we align ourselves with the forces that promote growth, prosperity, and harmony in our lives.
