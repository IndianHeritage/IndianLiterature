परी सुवानो हरिर अंशुः पवित्रे रथो न सर्जि सनये हियानः |आपच्छ्लोकं इन्द्रियम् पूयमानः प्रति देवाम् अजुषत प्रयोभिः ||
pari suvāno harir aṁśuḥ pavitre ratho na sarji sanaye hiyānaḥ | āpac chlokam indriyam pūyamānaḥ prati devām̐ ajuṣata prayobhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pari suvāno harir aṁśuḥ pavitre ratho na sarji sanaye hiyānaḥ | āpac chlokam indriyam pūyamānaḥ prati devām̐ ajuṣata prayobhiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • suvāno: well-spoken, auspicious
    • harir: the divine, often referring to Vishnu
    • aṁśuḥ: ray, light, or part
    • pavitre: pure, sacred
    • ratho: chariot, symbolizing movement or progress
    • na: not
    • sarji: to be bound or tied
    • sanaye: to be made or created
    • hiyānaḥ: to be directed or guided
    • āpac: to bring forth, to manifest
    • shlokam: verse, hymn
    • indriyam: senses, faculties
    • pūyamānaḥ: being filled, being purified
    • prati: towards, in relation to
    • devām̐: the divine, gods
    • ajuṣata: to be nourished, to be sustained
    • prayobhiḥ: with offerings, with devotion
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence and the purity associated with it. The term "pari suvāno" suggests that the divine is all-encompassing and auspicious, much like how a supportive community surrounds an individual.
    • The reference to "harir aṁśuḥ" indicates that the divine light or essence is a guiding force, similar to how a mentor or teacher illuminates the path for a student.
    • "Pavitre ratho" symbolizes a pure chariot, representing the journey of life that should be undertaken with purity of intention and action, akin to how one might approach a significant life event, such as a wedding or a new job, with sincerity and integrity.
    • The phrase "na sarji sanaye" implies that one should not be bound by worldly attachments, much like how a bird must learn to fly free from its nest to explore the world.
    • "Āpac shlokam indriyam pūyamānaḥ" emphasizes the importance of purifying one's senses and faculties, akin to how meditation or mindfulness practices help individuals clear their minds and focus on the present moment.
    • Finally, "prati devām̐ ajuṣata prayobhiḥ" suggests that through devotion and offerings, one can nourish their connection with the divine, similar to how acts of kindness and charity can strengthen relationships within a community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of purity, guidance, and devotion in one's life. It teaches that by surrounding ourselves with positive influences and maintaining a pure heart, we can navigate life's journey with clarity and purpose.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and spiritual practices. It encourages individuals to seek purity in their actions and thoughts, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that through devotion and a commitment to purity, we can achieve a harmonious existence and contribute positively to the lives of others.
