अहम् प्रात्नेन मनमनाऽ गीरः शुम्भामि कण्ववत् |येन इन्द्रः शुष्मम् इद् दधे ||
aham pratnena manmanā giraḥ śumbhāmi kaṇvavat | yenendraḥ śuṣmam id dadhe ||
  • Aham: This word translates to "I" or "myself," indicating a sense of self-awareness and individuality.
  • Pratnena: This means "with effort" or "with determination," suggesting that the speaker is committed to a particular action or thought process.
  • Manmanā: This can be interpreted as "with my mind" or "in my thoughts," emphasizing the importance of mental focus and intention.
  • Giraḥ: This word means "words" or "speech," highlighting the power of communication and expression.
  • Śumbhāmi: This translates to "I declare" or "I proclaim," indicating an assertion or a strong statement being made.
  • Kaṇvavat: This term refers to "like a sage" or "wise," suggesting that the speaker is aligning themselves with wisdom and knowledge.
  • Yen: This means "by whom," indicating a connection or relationship with a higher power or authority.
  • Indraḥ: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Śuṣmam: This translates to "dryness" or "drought," which can represent a lack of vitality or energy.
  • Id: This means "this," pointing to the subject matter at hand.
  • Dadhe: This translates to "gives" or "provides," indicating an action of bestowing or granting something.

The mantra can be interpreted as a declaration of self-awareness and intention, where the speaker, through focused thought and wise communication, seeks to connect with a higher power (Indra) to overcome challenges (symbolized by dryness or lack of vitality). The speaker asserts their individuality and determination to proclaim their thoughts and intentions, aligning themselves with wisdom.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to focus their thoughts and intentions on their goals, emphasizing the importance of self-awareness and determination in achieving personal growth.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by communicating effectively and making wise decisions that inspire and uplift their teams, especially during challenging times.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, this mantra encourages individuals to express their thoughts clearly and assertively, while also seeking a higher understanding or resolution.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of self-awareness, focused intention, and wise communication in overcoming challenges and achieving personal and collective goals. It serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of aligning oneself with higher principles and the strength that comes from within.
