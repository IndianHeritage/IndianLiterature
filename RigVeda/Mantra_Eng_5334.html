स न इन्द्र त्वयतायाऽइषे धास तमनā च ये मगवाणो जुन्थि |वस्वीषु ते जारित्रे अस्तु शक्तिर यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
sa na indra tvayatāyā iṣe dhās tmanā ca ye maghavāno junanti | vasvī ṣu te jaritre astu śaktir yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • sa na indra tvayatāyā: This phrase can be translated as "May we, O Indra, be blessed by you." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine support in our endeavors, much like seeking guidance or blessings before starting a new project or journey.
  • iṣe dhās tmanā ca ye maghavāno junanti: This translates to "Those who are powerful and who are endowed with strength." Here, the mantra acknowledges the strength and power of those who are capable. In everyday life, this can relate to recognizing and valuing the contributions of strong leaders or mentors who guide us in our personal and professional lives.
  • vasvī ṣu te jaritre astu śaktir: This means "May your power be in all our endeavors." It emphasizes the importance of divine strength in all actions. In practical terms, this can be likened to the support we seek from our community or family when undertaking significant challenges, such as starting a business or facing personal trials.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This translates to "May you protect us always with auspiciousness." This is a plea for ongoing protection and well-being. In real life, this can be seen in the way people often seek blessings for safety and success in various aspects of life, such as during weddings, new ventures, or even daily routines.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine support, strength, and protection in all aspects of life. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when individuals seek guidance and strength in their personal and professional journeys, reminding them to acknowledge the support they receive from both the divine and their community. It serves as a reminder to remain humble and grateful for the forces that aid us in our pursuits.
