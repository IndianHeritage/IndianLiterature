यद इन्द्राग्नि परमस्यां पृथिव्यां मध्यमस्यां अवमस्यां उता स्थः |अतः परि वृषणाव आ हि यातम अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī paramasyām pṛthivyām madhyamasyām avamasyām uta sthaḥ | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing strength and fire)
    • paramasyām: in the supreme
    • pṛthivyām: in the earth
    • madhyamasyām: in the middle
    • avamasyām: in the lower
    • uta: and
    • sthaḥ: standing or residing
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: let us go
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • pibataṁ: drink
    • sutasya: of the brewed (Soma)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Agni, representing strength and fire, and acknowledges their presence in various realms of existence: the supreme, the earth, the middle, and the lower.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of these realms and the divine forces that reside within them.
    • The phrase "let us go" suggests a call to action, encouraging individuals to seek out and embrace the power of these deities.
    • The reference to Soma, a sacred drink, symbolizes the pursuit of spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can draw parallels to the strength and energy represented by Indra and Agni by engaging in activities that promote physical and mental well-being, such as exercise and meditation.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be observed in community building, where individuals come together to support one another, reflecting the unity of the different realms mentioned in the mantra.
    • Consuming nourishing foods and drinks can be likened to the act of drinking Soma, as it symbolizes the importance of taking care of one's body and spirit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine forces that influence our lives and the importance of seeking strength, nourishment, and unity.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to recognize and harness the power within ourselves and our surroundings, fostering a sense of connection with the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, community support, and a deeper understanding of our place in the world.
