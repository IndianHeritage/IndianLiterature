स रंहता उरुगायस्य जूतिं वृथा क्रीळन्तं मिमते न गावा: |परिणसं कृतुते तिग्मशृङ्गो दिवा हरिर ददृशे नक्तं ऋज्रः ||
sa raṁhata urugāyasya jūtiṁ vṛthā krīḻantam mimate na gāvaḥ | parīṇasaṁ kṛṇute tigmaśṛṅgo divā harir dadṛśe naktam ṛjraḥ ||
  • sa raṁhata: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is endowed with strength" or "he who is powerful." In real life, this can refer to individuals who possess great physical or mental strength, such as athletes or leaders who inspire others through their resilience.
  • urugāyasya: This term means "of the one with vast fame" or "the one who is widely known." This can relate to public figures or celebrities whose actions and words have a significant impact on society, such as activists or influential thinkers.
  • jūtiṁ: This word translates to "glory" or "brilliance." In everyday life, this can be seen in achievements that bring honor to individuals or communities, such as winning awards or recognition for contributions to society.
  • vṛthā krīḻantam: This phrase means "playing in vain" or "acting without purpose." It serves as a reminder to avoid futile actions and instead focus on meaningful endeavors. For example, engaging in activities that do not contribute to personal growth or societal benefit can be seen as "playing in vain."
  • mimate na gāvaḥ: This translates to "the cows do not stray." Cows are often symbolic of nourishment and sustenance in many cultures. This can be interpreted as a call to remain grounded and focused on what truly matters, such as family, community, and values.
  • parīṇasaṁ: This term means "the one who is complete" or "the one who is whole." In real life, this can refer to individuals who have achieved a sense of fulfillment and balance in their lives, such as those who practice mindfulness or holistic living.
  • kṛṇute tigmaśṛṅgo: This phrase translates to "the one with sharp horns." This can symbolize strength and protection. In a real-world context, it can refer to leaders or guardians who protect their communities or ideals with determination and courage.
  • divā harir: This means "the sun is the lord." The sun is often associated with life, energy, and enlightenment. In daily life, this can be seen in the importance of positivity and hope, as well as the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • dadṛśe naktam ṛjraḥ: This translates to "he who sees the night." This can symbolize the ability to navigate through darkness or challenges. In real life, it can refer to individuals who face adversity with courage and resilience, finding their way through difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, purpose, and fulfillment in life. It encourages individuals to focus on meaningful actions, remain grounded, and protect their values. In a world filled with distractions and challenges, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal growth and community well-being. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute positively to society and navigate their journeys with clarity and purpose.
