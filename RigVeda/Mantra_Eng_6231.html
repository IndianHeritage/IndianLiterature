आ नो विश्वानि अश्विनाः धत्तं राधांस्य अह्रयाः |कृतं न ऋत्वियावतो मा नो रीर्धतं निदे ||
ā no viśvāny aśvinā dhattaṁ rādhāṁsy ahrayā | kṛtaṁ na ṛtviyāvato mā no rīradhataṁ nide ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May (we) attain
    • no: our
    • viśvāny: all (that is) universal
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen)
    • dhattaṁ: grant
    • rādhāṁsy: our desires
    • ahrayā: with abundance
    • kṛtaṁ: what is done
    • na: not
    • ṛtviyāvato: for the priests
    • mā: may (we) not
    • no: our
    • rīradhataṁ: be deprived
    • nide: of wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The speaker is asking the Ashvins to grant them all universal desires and abundance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not being deprived of wealth and resources, especially in the context of rituals and offerings made by priests.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of holistic well-being, where individuals seek not just material wealth but also health, happiness, and fulfillment in life.
    • For instance, during a wedding ceremony, families often pray for the well-being and prosperity of the couple, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In corporate settings, teams may invoke similar sentiments when they seek success in projects, emphasizing collaboration and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional fulfillment.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal aspirations to communal rituals, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our desires and the importance of divine blessings in achieving them.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to remain grateful and to seek abundance in all aspects of life, fostering a sense of community and shared prosperity.
