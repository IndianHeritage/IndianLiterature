adbhiḥ soma papṛcānasya te raso 'vyo vāraṁ vi pavamāna dhāvati | sa mṛjyamānaḥ kavibhir madintama svadasvendrāya pavamāna pītaye ||

  • soma: Soma is a sacred plant and drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how we seek education and wisdom in our lives.
  • papṛcānasya: This term can be understood as the one who is being asked or invoked. It signifies the importance of communication and connection with the divine or higher powers, similar to how we seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in our lives.
  • te: This word means "your," indicating a personal connection to the divine. It reminds us of the importance of recognizing the presence of the divine in our daily lives, whether through prayer, meditation, or mindfulness.
  • raso: This translates to "essence" or "juice," symbolizing the vital energy or life force. In real life, this can be related to the essence of experiences that nourish our souls, such as love, friendship, and creativity.
  • 'vyo: This word can be interpreted as "to you" or "for you," emphasizing the act of offering and devotion. It reflects the importance of selflessness and giving in our relationships and communities.
  • vāraṁ: This means "protection" or "shelter." In contemporary life, it can be associated with the need for safety and security, both physically and emotionally, in our homes and communities.
  • vi: This word means "in" or "within," suggesting an internal journey or exploration. It encourages us to look within ourselves for answers and understanding, much like the practice of self-reflection and introspection.
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "sacred" aspect of the divine. In our lives, it can symbolize the cleansing of negative thoughts and emotions, akin to practices like yoga and meditation that promote mental clarity and peace.
  • dhāvati: This means "flows" or "runs," indicating the dynamic nature of life and energy. It reminds us that life is ever-changing, and we must adapt and flow with it, much like how rivers carve their paths through landscapes.
  • sa: This word means "he" or "that," referring to the divine presence. It emphasizes the connection we have with the divine in our lives, encouraging us to recognize and honor that relationship.
  • mṛjyamānaḥ: This term means "being purified" or "cleansed." It signifies the importance of personal growth and transformation, akin to how we learn from our experiences and strive to become better individuals.
  • kavibhir: This translates to "by the poets" or "by the wise." It highlights the role of wisdom and creativity in our lives, reminding us of the value of art, literature, and expression in shaping our understanding of the world.
  • madintama: This means "the most intoxicating" or "the best." It symbolizes the pursuit of the highest ideals and experiences in life, such as love, joy, and fulfillment.
  • svadasvendrāya: This term refers to the "delight of the divine" or "the pleasure of the gods." It signifies the joy that comes from aligning ourselves with higher principles and values, much like the happiness we feel when we contribute positively to society.
  • pavamāna: Repeated here, it reinforces the idea of purification and sacredness, emphasizing its significance in our spiritual journey.
  • pītaye: This means "for drinking" or "to consume." It symbolizes the act of taking in the essence of life, much like how we nourish ourselves with food, knowledge, and experiences.
