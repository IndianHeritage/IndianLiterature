मन्त्र: सूर्याचन्द्रमसौ धाता यथापूर्वम् अकल्पयत् |दिवं च पृथिवीं चान्तरिक्षम अथो स्वः ||
sūryācandramasau dhātā yathāpūrvam akalpayat | divaṁ ca pṛthivīṁ cāntarikṣam atho svaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūrya: Sun
    • candra: Moon
    • dhātā: The Creator or the one who sustains
    • yathā: As
    • pūrvam: Previously or in the beginning
    • akalpayat: Created or established
    • divaṁ: The sky or heaven
    • ca: And
    • pṛthivī: The Earth
    • cāntarikṣam: The space or atmosphere
    • atho: Then or thereafter
    • svaḥ: The realm of the gods or the higher planes
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the Creator (dhātā) who established the Sun (sūrya) and the Moon (candra) as they were in the beginning (yathāpūrvam).
    • It emphasizes the creation of the cosmos, including the sky (divaṁ), the Earth (pṛthivī), and the space (cāntarikṣam).
    • The phrase "atho svaḥ" suggests a connection to the divine or higher realms, indicating that the creation is not just physical but also spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Sun and Moon are essential for life on Earth, providing light and regulating natural cycles such as day and night, which can be seen in agriculture where farmers plan their planting and harvesting based on these cycles.
    • The concept of creation can be reflected in scientific understanding, such as the Big Bang theory, which describes the origin of the universe and the formation of celestial bodies.
    • In daily life, the balance between the physical (Earth) and the spiritual (higher realms) can be observed in practices like yoga and meditation, where individuals seek harmony between their material existence and spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of the universe, highlighting the importance of both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It serves as a reminder of the Creator's role in establishing order and balance in the cosmos, which is applicable in various aspects of life, from environmental awareness to personal well-being.
    • By understanding this mantra, individuals can appreciate the beauty of creation and strive for harmony in their own lives, recognizing the significance of both the material and spiritual dimensions.
