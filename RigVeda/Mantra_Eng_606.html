तवे विश्वा तविषी सध्र्यग घिता तव राधः सोमपीताय हरषते |तव वज्रश चिकीते बाह्वोर हितो वृश्चा शत्रोर अव विश्वानि वृष्ण्याः ||
tve viśvā taviṣī sadhryag ghitā tava rādhaḥ somapīthāya harṣate | tava vajraś cikite bāhvor hito vṛścā śatror ava viśvāni vṛṣṇyā ||
  • tve viśvā taviṣī: This phrase can be interpreted as "You are the all-encompassing force." In real life, this can relate to the idea of universal energy or the interconnectedness of all beings. For example, in nature, every element, from the smallest insect to the largest tree, plays a role in the ecosystem, demonstrating how everything is interconnected.
  • sadhryag ghitā tava rādhaḥ: This translates to "Your essence is the source of nourishment." This can be likened to the importance of food and sustenance in our lives. Just as we rely on food for physical strength, we also seek emotional and spiritual nourishment from relationships, experiences, and knowledge.
  • somapīthāya harṣate: This means "It rejoices in the essence of the moon." The moon is often associated with calmness and reflection. In our lives, we can find joy in moments of peace and tranquility, such as spending time in nature or meditating, which can rejuvenate our spirits.
  • tava vajraś cikite bāhvor: This can be interpreted as "Your strength shines forth." This reflects the idea of inner strength and resilience. For instance, individuals who overcome adversity, such as illness or personal loss, often find a newfound strength within themselves that empowers them to face future challenges.
  • hito vṛścā śatror: This translates to "May you protect us from enemies." In a broader sense, this can refer to the protection we seek in our lives, whether it be physical safety, emotional security, or the safeguarding of our values and beliefs. For example, communities often come together to support one another in times of crisis, showcasing collective strength against adversity.
  • ava viśvāni vṛṣṇyā: This means "May you grant us all that we desire." This reflects the human aspiration for fulfillment and happiness. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of goals, whether personal or professional, and the importance of setting intentions to manifest our desires.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of universal connection, nourishment, strength, protection, and the pursuit of desires. Its significance lies in reminding us of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of seeking balance and support in our journeys. In real-life scenarios, we can apply these teachings by fostering relationships, nurturing our well-being, and striving for personal growth while also supporting others in their endeavors. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for living harmoniously within ourselves and with the world around us.
