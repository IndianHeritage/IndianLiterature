अति त्रī सोम रोचनā रोहन न भ्राजसे दिवम् |इष्णन् सूर्यं न चोदयः ||
ati trī soma rocanā rohan na bhrājase divam | iṣṇan sūryaṁ na codayaḥ ||
  • ati trī soma rocanā: This phrase can be translated as "three times the brilliance of Soma." In Vedic literature, Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. The number three signifies completeness and harmony. In real life, this can be related to the idea of achieving a balanced state of mind through spiritual practices, such as meditation or yoga, which can enhance one's clarity and perception.
  • rohan na bhrājase divam: This translates to "you do not shine in the heavens." This line suggests a contrast between the brilliance of Soma and the lack of radiance in the heavens. It can be interpreted as a reminder that external appearances or worldly achievements do not necessarily equate to true enlightenment or inner peace. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of material success versus spiritual fulfillment, where the latter often leads to a more profound sense of happiness.
  • iṣṇan sūryaṁ na codayaḥ: This means "do not awaken the sun." The sun symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the source of life. This line can be understood as a caution against forcing enlightenment or understanding prematurely. In practical terms, it reflects the importance of patience in personal growth and learning. Just as the sun rises in its own time, individuals must allow their understanding and wisdom to develop naturally.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of inner brilliance and the journey towards enlightenment. It teaches that true radiance comes from within and cannot be forced or rushed. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals are encouraged to seek balance, patience, and inner peace rather than merely chasing external validation or success. The mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate one's inner self, leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
