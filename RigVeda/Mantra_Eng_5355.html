एव न इन्द्र वार्यस्य पूर्धि प्र ते महीं सुमतिं वेविदाम |इषं पिन्व मगवद्भ्यः सुवीरां यूयम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
evā na indra vāryasya pūrdhi pra te mahīṁ sumatiṁ vevidāma | iṣam pinva maghavadbhyaḥ suvīrāṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • evā na indra vāryasya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, O Indra, of great power." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. In real life, this can be likened to seeking the support of a powerful leader or a guiding force in challenging situations.
  • pūrdhi pra te mahīṁ: This means "we seek your greatness." Here, "pūrdhi" refers to abundance or greatness, and "mahīṁ" signifies the earth or the vastness of existence. In practical terms, this can be understood as striving for prosperity and well-being in our lives, akin to setting ambitious goals for personal or community development.
  • sumatiṁ vevidāma: This translates to "we know the good thought." "Sumati" refers to wisdom or good thoughts. In everyday life, this emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and wise decision-making, similar to how individuals or organizations might prioritize ethical practices and thoughtful strategies in their endeavors.
  • iṣam pinva maghavadbhyaḥ: This phrase means "may you fill us with strength." "Iṣam" refers to strength or power, and "maghavadbhyaḥ" relates to the bountiful or generous. This can be related to the idea of empowerment, where individuals or communities seek to enhance their capabilities, much like how teams in workplaces often engage in training and development to boost their skills.
  • suvīrāṁ yūyam pāta: This translates to "may you protect the strong." "Suvīrāṁ" refers to the valiant or strong individuals. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the way societies protect their leaders, warriors, or those who contribute significantly to the community, ensuring their safety and well-being.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you always grant us well-being." "Svastibhiḥ" refers to auspiciousness or well-being. This highlights the universal desire for health and prosperity, akin to how communities come together to support each other during times of crisis, ensuring collective well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, wisdom, and protection from a higher power, represented by Indra. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the need for guidance and support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community support, and leadership. By invoking these qualities, individuals and societies can strive for a prosperous and harmonious existence, fostering a culture of mutual respect and empowerment.
