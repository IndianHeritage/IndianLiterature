एतानि वाम अश्विनाऽ वर्धनानि ब्रह्म स्तोमं गृत्समदासो अक्रन् |तानि नराः जुजुषाणोप यातं बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
etāni vām aśvinā vardhanāni brahma stomaṁ gṛtsamadāso akran | tāni narā jujuṣāṇopa yātam bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • etāni - This word translates to "these" or "these things." It indicates a focus on specific qualities or attributes that are being addressed.
  • vām - Meaning "your," this word signifies possession or connection, often referring to the divine or celestial beings.
  • aśvinā - Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities. They symbolize health, vitality, and the duality of nature.
  • vardhanāni - This means "that which increases" or "that which promotes growth." It suggests a nurturing aspect, enhancing life and well-being.
  • brahma - Translates to "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic spirit." It represents the source of all creation and the essence of the universe.
  • stomaṁ - This word means "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of offering reverence or acknowledgment to the divine.
  • gṛtsamadāso - Refers to "those who are devoted" or "those who are engaged in the sacred." It emphasizes the importance of dedication and commitment to spiritual practices.
  • akran - This means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to the divine for assistance or blessings.
  • tāni - Meaning "those," it refers back to the qualities or blessings being sought from the divine.
  • narā - This translates to "men" or "humans," indicating the recipients of the blessings or the ones who are invoking the divine.
  • jujuṣāṇo - Meaning "desiring" or "seeking," it reflects the human aspiration for divine support and guidance.
  • upa - This means "near" or "towards," suggesting a closeness or approach to the divine.
  • yātam - Translates to "let us go" or "let us move," indicating a call to action or a journey towards a goal.
  • bṛhad - Meaning "great" or "vast," it signifies the expansive nature of the blessings being sought.
  • vadema - This means "we speak" or "we declare," indicating the act of vocalizing intentions or prayers.
  • vidathe - Refers to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," suggesting a communal aspect of worship or invocation.
  • suvīrāḥ - Meaning "the strong" or "the valiant," it emphasizes the qualities of courage and strength that are desired from the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a prayer invoking the Aśvins for blessings that promote growth, health, and strength. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective aspiration towards the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as during rituals for health and well-being, in communal gatherings seeking strength in unity, or in personal prayers for guidance and support in challenging times. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of human aspirations and divine blessings, fostering a sense of hope and resilience in everyday life.
