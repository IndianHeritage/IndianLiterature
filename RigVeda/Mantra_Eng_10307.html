अयुद्धसेनो विभ्वा विभिन्दता दाशद वृत्रहा तुज्यानी तेजते |इन्द्रस्य वज्राद अबिभेद अभिष्णतः प्राक्रामच छुन्द्यूर अजहाद उषा अनः ||
ayuddhaseno vibhvā vibhindatā dāśad vṛtrahā tujyāni tejate | indrasya vajrād abibhed abhiśnathaḥ prākrāmac chundhyūr ajahād uṣā anaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ayuddhaseno vibhvā vibhindatā dāśad vṛtrahā tujyāni tejate | indrasya vajrād abibhed abhiśnathaḥ prākrāmac chundhyūr ajahād uṣā anaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ayuddhasena: With the strength of battle
    • vibhvā: The one who is powerful
    • vibhindatā: The one who divides or separates
    • dāśad: Ten (often refers to the ten directions or realms)
    • vṛtrahā: The slayer of Vritra (a demon representing chaos)
    • tujyāni: The one who shines or radiates
    • tejate: To shine or to be powerful
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • vajrād: From the thunderbolt (symbol of strength)
    • abibhed: Did not break or shatter
    • abhiśnathaḥ: With great force
    • prākrāmac: The one who is powerful
    • chundhyūr: The one who is invincible
    • ajahād: Did not abandon
    • uṣā: Dawn or light
    • anaḥ: The eternal or the infinite
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and strength associated with the divine, particularly focusing on Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "ayuddhaseno vibhvā" emphasizes the strength derived from battle, suggesting that true power comes from overcoming challenges and adversities.
    • In real life, this can be likened to individuals who face struggles, such as athletes training for competitions or soldiers preparing for battle, where their strength is tested and ultimately defined by their perseverance.
    • The mention of "vṛtrahā" refers to the slaying of Vritra, symbolizing the triumph over chaos and obstacles. This can be seen in personal journeys where individuals overcome significant life challenges, such as addiction or mental health issues.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of radiating strength and positivity ("tujyāni tejate"), which can be reflected in leaders who inspire others through their actions and resilience.
    • Furthermore, "indrasya vajrād abibhed" suggests that with divine strength, one can withstand challenges without breaking, akin to how a strong tree withstands storms.
    • The concept of "uṣā" or dawn signifies new beginnings and hope, reminding us that after every struggle, there is a possibility of renewal and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that comes from facing and overcoming challenges. It encourages individuals to harness their inner power and resilience.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building, where the focus is on overcoming obstacles and inspiring others.
    • Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to motivate individuals to embrace their struggles, emerge stronger, and shine brightly in their endeavors.
