अग्न आ याहि वीटये गृणानो हव्यदातये |निहोता सति बरहिषि ||
agna ā yāhi vītaye gṛṇāno havyadātaye | ni hotā satsi barhiṣi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agna: O Fire
    • ā yāhi: come here
    • vītaye: for the purpose of offering
    • gṛṇāno: singing praises
    • havyadātaye: for the one who gives offerings
    • ni hotā: the priest or the one who invokes
    • satsi: in the assembly
    • barhiṣi: in the sacred place or altar
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to Agni, the fire god, to come forth and accept the offerings made during a ritual.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine presence and the role of the priest in conducting the ritual.
    • The act of singing praises signifies the reverence and respect towards the divine, which is essential in any spiritual practice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices), where offerings are made to the fire as a witness and a medium to the divine.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of invoking a higher power can be seen in various forms of meditation and prayer, where individuals seek guidance and blessings.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals, often include rituals that honor the divine, similar to the assembly mentioned in the mantra.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the community.
    • It teaches the importance of intention and reverence in our actions, whether in spiritual practices or daily life.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek connection and harmony with the universe, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.
