व्य आनाḻ इन्द्रः पृतनाः स्वोजा आस्मै यतन्ते सख्याय पूर्वीः |आ स्मा रथं न पृतनासु तिष्ठ यं भद्रया सुमत्याः चोदयासे ||
vy ānaḻ indraḥ pṛtanāḥ svojā āsmai yatante sakhyāya pūrvīḥ | ā smā rathaṁ na pṛtanāsu tiṣṭha yam bhadrayā sumatyā codayāse ||
  • vy ānaḻ indraḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Indra, the powerful one." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. In real life, this can symbolize the call for strength and support in challenging situations.
  • pṛtanāḥ svojā: This translates to "the one who is worthy of praise." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to our lives. For example, in a workplace, acknowledging a colleague's hard work can foster a supportive environment.
  • āsmai yatante sakhyāya pūrvīḥ: This means "may they strive for friendship and unity." It highlights the value of collaboration and camaraderie. In real life, this can be seen in team sports where players work together towards a common goal, showcasing the power of unity.
  • ā smā rathaṁ na pṛtanāsu tiṣṭha: This translates to "let our chariot not stand still in the midst of challenges." It signifies the importance of perseverance and moving forward despite obstacles. For instance, in personal development, one must continue to strive for growth even when faced with setbacks.
  • yam bhadrayā sumatyā codayāse: This means "may we be guided by auspicious thoughts." It emphasizes the significance of positive thinking and intentions. In everyday life, maintaining a positive mindset can lead to better decision-making and overall well-being.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, unity, perseverance, and positive thinking. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, such as personal relationships, teamwork, and self-improvement. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and foster a supportive community around them.
