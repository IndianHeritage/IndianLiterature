इन्द्रावरुणाव अभ्यात्मन्ति मागान्य आर्यो वनुषाम अरातयः |युवं हि वस्व उभयस्य राजथोऽध स्मा नोऽवतं पार्ये दिवि ||
indrāvaruṇāv abhy ā tapanti māghāny aryo vanuṣām arātayaḥ | yuvaṁ hi vasva ubhayasya rājatho 'dha smā no 'vatam pārye divi ||
  • Indrāvaruṇāv abhy ā tapanti: This phrase refers to the divine forces of Indra and Varuna, who are believed to be the guardians of cosmic order and natural laws. They are often invoked for their strength and wisdom.
  • Māghāny aryo vanuṣām arātayaḥ: This part speaks of the noble and virtuous individuals who are worthy of respect and honor. It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and moral integrity in society.
  • Yuvaṁ hi vasva ubhayasya rājatho 'dha: Here, the mantra acknowledges the youth and vitality of life, suggesting that the energy of the young is essential for the prosperity of both the material and spiritual realms.
  • Smā no 'vatam pārye divi: This concluding line is a plea for protection and guidance from the divine, asking for blessings to navigate through life's challenges and to achieve success in both earthly and heavenly pursuits.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine forces for guidance and protection. It highlights the importance of moral integrity and the vitality of youth in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for seeking blessings for new ventures, encouraging the youth to uphold righteousness, or invoking divine support in times of uncertainty. It serves as a reminder that both spiritual and material success require a balance of strength, virtue, and youthful energy.
