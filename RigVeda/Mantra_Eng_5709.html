आयं ह यद वां देवया उ अद्रिर ऊर्ध्वो विवक्ति सोमसुद युवभ्यां |आ वलगू विप्रो ववृतीत हव्यैः ||
ayaṁ ha yad vāṁ devayā u adrir ūrdhvo vivakti somasud yuvabhyām | ā valgū vipro vavṛtīta havyaiḥ ||
  • ayaṁ ha yad vāṁ devayā: This phrase can be translated as "This is what you, O Deity, have." It signifies a connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that what is being offered or discussed is sacred and has divine approval.
  • u adrir ūrdhvo vivakti: This translates to "the mountain rises above." Mountains often symbolize stability, strength, and the connection between the earth and the heavens. In real life, we can relate this to personal growth and aspirations, where individuals strive to rise above challenges, much like a mountain stands tall against the sky.
  • somasud yuvabhyām: This can be interpreted as "with the two young ones of the Soma." Soma is a significant element in Vedic rituals, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The mention of "two young ones" could symbolize duality in life, such as joy and sorrow, success and failure, or the balance of opposites that we encounter in our daily lives.
  • ā valgū vipro vavṛtīta havyaiḥ: This phrase means "the wise one should be invoked with offerings." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we seek guidance from mentors, teachers, or even through literature and education to make informed decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine connection, personal growth, and the importance of wisdom in our lives. It reminds us that we are part of a larger cosmic order and encourages us to rise above our challenges while remaining grounded in knowledge and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of decision-making, personal development, and spiritual practices, guiding individuals to seek higher truths and maintain balance in their lives.
