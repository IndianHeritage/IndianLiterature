अत्यासो न ये मरुतः स्वञ्चो यक्षदृशो न शुभयन्त मर्याः |ते हरम्येष्ठाः शिशवो न शुभ्राः वत्सासो न प्रक्रीळिनः पयोधाः ||
atyāso na ye marutaḥ svañco yakṣadṛśo na śubhayanta maryāḥ | te harmyeṣṭhāḥ śiśavo na śubhrā vatsāso na prakrīḻinaḥ payodhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "atyāso na ye marutaḥ svañco yakṣadṛśo na śubhayanta maryāḥ | te harmyeṣṭhāḥ śiśavo na śubhrā vatsāso na prakrīḻinaḥ payodhāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atyāso: Those who are beyond (or transcendent)
    • na: Not
    • ye: Who
    • marutaḥ: The winds or storm gods
    • svañco: Those who are self-sufficient or independent
    • yakṣadṛśo: Those who are like the Yakṣas (nature spirits)
    • na: Not
    • śubhayanta: Bringing auspiciousness or goodness
    • maryāḥ: The mortals or human beings
    • te: They
    • harmyeṣṭhāḥ: Who dwell in the heavenly abodes
    • śiśavo: The young ones or calves
    • na: Not
    • śubhrā: Pure or auspicious
    • vatsāso: The calves or young ones
    • na: Not
    • prakrīḻinaḥ: Those who are diminished or weakened
    • payodhāḥ: The waters or oceans
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of divine beings and their relationship with the material world.
    • The phrase "atyāso na ye marutaḥ" suggests that those who are transcendent do not belong to the realm of the storm gods, indicating a separation from chaos and turbulence.
    • "svañco yakṣadṛśo na śubhayanta maryāḥ" implies that self-sufficient beings, akin to nature spirits, do not bring goodness to mortals, highlighting a distinction between divine and human experiences.
    • The latter part, "te harmyeṣṭhāḥ śiśavo na śubhrā," refers to those who reside in heavenly realms, suggesting purity and auspiciousness, while "vatsāso na prakrīḻinaḥ payodhāḥ" emphasizes that the young ones or calves are not diminished by the waters, symbolizing abundance and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of seeking higher consciousness and understanding one's place in the universe, much like how individuals strive for personal growth and self-sufficiency.
    • The distinction between divine and mortal can be seen in the way people often look to nature or spirituality for guidance, yet must navigate the chaos of everyday life.
    • The concept of purity and abundance can be reflected in sustainable living practices, where individuals aim to live in harmony with nature, ensuring that resources are not diminished.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of transcending chaos and seeking purity and abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to strive for self-sufficiency and to recognize the divine aspects of existence while navigating the material world.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to pursue personal growth, maintain a connection with nature, and foster a sense of community that uplifts rather than diminishes.
