कुहा यान्ता सुष्टुतिं काव्यस्य दिवो नपातā वृषणा शयुत्रा |हिरण्यस्येव कलशं निखातम उद ūपथुर दशमे अश्विनाहन ||
kuha yāntā suṣṭutiṁ kāvyasya divo napātā vṛṣaṇā śayutrā | hiraṇyasyeva kalaśaṁ nikhātam ud ūpathur daśame aśvināhan ||
  • kuha yāntā: This phrase can be interpreted as "where are they going?" It reflects a sense of inquiry about the direction or purpose of a journey.
  • suṣṭutiṁ: This means "well-formed praise" or "good praise." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and recognition for the divine or the forces of nature.
  • kāvyasya: This translates to "of poetry" or "of the poetic." It suggests a connection to creativity, art, and the beauty of expression.
  • dīvo napātā: This phrase means "the offspring of heaven." It refers to divine beings or celestial entities, indicating a connection to the spiritual realm.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word can mean "strong" or "powerful." It signifies strength and vitality, possibly referring to the forces of nature or divine powers.
  • śayutrā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are resting" or "those who are settled." It may refer to the stability and peace that comes from divine blessings.
  • hiraṇyasyeva: This means "like gold." It symbolizes purity, value, and the richness of life experiences.
  • kalaśaṁ: This translates to "a pot" or "a vessel." In many cultures, a pot symbolizes abundance and the nurturing of life.
  • nikhātam: This means "buried" or "hidden." It suggests that there are treasures or valuable insights that may not be immediately visible.
  • ud ūpathur: This phrase can be interpreted as "they have emerged" or "they have come forth." It indicates the revelation of knowledge or blessings.
  • daśame: This means "tenth." It may refer to a specific time or occasion, possibly indicating a cycle or completion.
  • aśvināhan: This refers to the "Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance of opposites.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of inquiry, praise, creativity, divine connection, strength, stability, and the emergence of hidden treasures. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek purpose in their journeys, express gratitude for the beauty around them, and recognize the divine presence in everyday experiences. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to appreciate the richness of life, to acknowledge the dualities we encounter, and to remain open to the blessings that emerge from our pursuits. It can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal reflection, creative endeavors, or communal celebrations, fostering a deeper connection with oneself and the universe.
