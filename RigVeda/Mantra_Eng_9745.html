तīkṣṇenāgne चक्षुषा रक्ष यज्ञं प्राञ्चं वसुभ्यः प्र णय प्रचेतः |हिंस्रं रक्षांस्य अभि शोशुचानं मा त्वा दभन यातुधान नृचक्षः ||
tīkṣṇenāgne cakṣuṣā rakṣa yajñam prāñcaṁ vasubhyaḥ pra ṇaya pracetaḥ | hiṁsraṁ rakṣāṁsy abhi śośucānam mā tvā dabhan yātudhānā nṛcakṣaḥ ||
  • tīkṣṇenāgne: This term refers to the "sharp" or "intense" nature of Agni, the fire god. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the intensity of passion or determination in one's pursuits.
  • cakṣuṣā: Meaning "with the eye" or "with vision," this word emphasizes the importance of perception and awareness. In everyday life, having a clear vision can lead to better decision-making.
  • rakṣa: This translates to "protect" or "guard." It signifies the need for protection in various aspects of life, such as emotional, physical, or spiritual safety.
  • yajñam: Referring to "sacrifice" or "offering," this term highlights the importance of selflessness and contributing to the greater good, akin to community service or charitable acts.
  • prāñcaṁ: This word means "life" or "vitality." It underscores the essence of living fully and embracing life’s experiences.
  • vasubhyaḥ: Meaning "to the good" or "to the auspicious," this term suggests a focus on positive outcomes and well-being in life.
  • pra ṇaya: This translates to "lead" or "guide," indicating the importance of leadership and direction in one’s life journey.
  • pracetaḥ: Meaning "with intelligence" or "with wisdom," this term emphasizes the value of thoughtful actions and decisions.
  • hiṁsraṁ: This word means "harmful" or "injurious," reminding us to be aware of negative influences or actions that can lead to harm.
  • rakṣāṁsy: Referring to "protections" or "safeguards," this term highlights the need for various forms of protection in life.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "against," indicating a direction or focus in our actions.
  • śośucānam: This term translates to "purifying" or "cleansing," suggesting the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions.
  • mā: Meaning "not," this word serves as a negation, emphasizing the importance of avoiding certain actions or influences.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," indicating a personal address, which can be interpreted as a call to self-awareness.
  • dabhan: Meaning "to bind" or "to restrain," this term suggests the need to control negative impulses or harmful influences.
  • yātudhānā: Referring to "evil spirits" or "malicious beings," this term highlights the presence of negativity in life that one must guard against.
  • nṛcakṣaḥ: This translates to "human eyes" or "human perception," emphasizing the importance of human insight and understanding in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, awareness, and the pursuit of positive actions in life. It serves as a reminder to maintain clarity of vision, to protect oneself from harmful influences, and to engage in selfless acts for the greater good. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and fostering a positive environment. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can lead a more fulfilling and purposeful life, while also contributing positively to society.
