गृभीतं ते मन इन्द्र द्विभर्हाः सुतः सोमः परिषिक्ता मधूनि | विसृष्टधेनाऽभरते सुवृक्तिर इयं इन्द्रं जोहुवती मनिषा ||
gṛbhītaṁ te mana indra dvibarhāḥ sutaḥ somaḥ pariṣiktā madhūni | visṛṣṭadhenā bharate suvṛktir iyam indraṁ johuvatī manīṣā ||
  • gṛbhītaṁ te mana indra: This phrase translates to "May your mind be grasped, O Indra." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call for focus and clarity of thought, akin to how leaders in various fields, such as business or politics, must maintain a clear vision to guide their teams.
  • dvibarhāḥ sutaḥ somaḥ: This means "the son of the two-fold (or double) barhāḥ, the soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic culture, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The "two-fold" could symbolize duality in life, such as joy and sorrow. In modern life, this can be seen in how individuals balance their personal and professional lives, drawing strength from both aspects.
  • pariṣiktā madhūni: This translates to "anointed with sweet (madhūni) substances." The act of anointing signifies blessing and sanctification. In contemporary terms, this can relate to rituals or practices that bring positivity and good fortune, such as meditation or mindfulness, which help individuals to cultivate a positive mindset.
  • visṛṣṭadhenā bharate: This means "the cow that has been released (or set free) brings prosperity." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth and nourishment. In today's context, this can be likened to the idea of sustainable living and the importance of nurturing our environment to ensure prosperity for future generations.
  • suvṛktir iyam indraṁ johuvatī manīṣā: This translates to "this intellect (manīṣā) that invokes Indra is excellent (suvṛktir)." Here, the invocation of Indra represents the pursuit of wisdom and strength. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge and innovation, where individuals or organizations strive to harness their intellect to achieve greatness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of clarity of thought, balance in life, the sanctification of our actions, the nurturing of our environment, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to seek clarity, balance, and prosperity while invoking their inner strength and intellect. By embodying these values, one can navigate the complexities of modern life with purpose and intention.
