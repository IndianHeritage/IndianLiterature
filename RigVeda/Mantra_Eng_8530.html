हरिः सृजनः पथ्याम ऋतस्येयरति वाचं अरितेव नावम् |देवो देवानां गुह्यानी नामाविष्कृतोति बर्हिषि प्रवाचे ||
hariḥ sṛjānaḥ pathyām ṛtasyeyarti vācam ariteva nāvam | devo devānāṁ guhyāni nāmāviṣ kṛṇoti barhiṣi pravāce ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hariḥ: A term often associated with Lord Vishnu, representing the divine or the supreme being.
    • sṛjānaḥ: Creating or manifesting.
    • pathyām: The path or way.
    • ṛtasyeyarti: The essence of truth or righteousness.
    • vācam: Speech or words.
    • ariteva: Like a boat or vessel.
    • nāvam: A boat or ship.
    • devo: The divine or gods.
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods.
    • guhyāni: Secret or hidden.
    • nāmāviṣ: Names or identities.
    • kṛṇoti: Creates or establishes.
    • barhiṣi: In the sacred space or in the rituals.
    • pravāce: Speaks or declares.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine creation and the establishment of truth and righteousness in the world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of words and communication, likening them to a vessel that carries meaning and intention.
    • The reference to the gods and their hidden names suggests that there is a deeper understanding and connection to the divine that can be accessed through spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, the words we choose can significantly impact our relationships and interactions. Just as a boat navigates through water, our words can help us navigate through life's challenges.
    • In a professional setting, clear and truthful communication is essential for teamwork and collaboration, reflecting the essence of righteousness mentioned in the mantra.
    • Spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, can help individuals connect with their inner selves and the divine, uncovering the hidden truths of their existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine creation, the power of words, and the pursuit of truth in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to communicate thoughtfully and to seek deeper understanding in our spiritual journeys.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a more harmonious and meaningful existence, both personally and collectively.
