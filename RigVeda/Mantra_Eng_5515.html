आयं हि नेताऽवरुण ऋतस्य मित्रो राजानो आर्यमापो धुः |सुहवा देव्य अदितिर अनर्वा ते नो अंहो अति पर्षन्न अरिष्टान् ||
ayaṁ hi netā varuṇa ṛtasya mitro rājāno aryamāpo dhuḥ | suhavā devy aditir anarvā te no aṁho ati parṣann ariṣṭān ||
  • ayaṁ hi netā varuṇa ṛtasya: This phrase translates to "This is indeed the leader of the truth, Varuna." Varuna is a deity associated with cosmic order and truth (ṛta). In real life, this can be likened to the importance of leadership that upholds truth and justice in society, such as judges or community leaders who ensure fairness.
  • mitro rājāno aryamāpo dhuḥ: This means "the friend of kings, Aryama, who brings forth prosperity." Aryama is another deity representing friendship and prosperity. In contemporary terms, this can be seen in the relationships between nations or businesses that foster mutual growth and support, akin to diplomatic ties that enhance economic prosperity.
  • suhavā devy aditir anarvā: This translates to "the auspicious goddess Aditi, who is unparalleled." Aditi is the mother of the gods and symbolizes boundless space and potential. In everyday life, this can represent the nurturing aspect of motherhood or the idea of limitless possibilities in education and personal growth, where individuals are encouraged to reach their full potential.
  • te no aṁho ati parṣann ariṣṭān: This means "may you grant us strength to overcome obstacles." This is a plea for divine assistance in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be related to seeking support during difficult times, whether through community support systems, therapy, or personal resilience strategies that help individuals navigate life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, friendship, prosperity, nurturing, and overcoming obstacles. It highlights the interconnectedness of these values in both spiritual and practical realms. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and communities in fostering justice, building supportive relationships, nurturing potential, and seeking strength in adversity. By embodying these ideals, one can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
