गोमायुर अदाद अजमायुर अदात पृष्णिर अदाद धरितो नो वसूनि |गवाम मण्डूकाद ददतः शतानि सहस्रसावे प्र तिरन्त आायुः ||
gomāyur adād ajamāyur adāt pṛśnir adād dharito no vasūni | gavām maṇḍūkā dadataḥ śatāni sahasrasāve pra tiranta āyuḥ ||
  • gomāyur: This term refers to the longevity or life span of cows. In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • adād: This word means "to give" or "to bestow." It indicates the act of providing or granting something valuable.
  • ajamāyur: Similar to gomāyur, this term refers to the life span of goats. Goats are also important in agricultural societies for their milk and meat.
  • pṛśnir: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the one who supports." It signifies strength and stability.
  • dharito: This term means "the earth" or "the one who bears." It represents the nurturing aspect of nature that sustains life.
  • vasūni: This word refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It encompasses all forms of abundance in life.
  • gavām: This means "of cows," emphasizing the importance of cattle in sustaining human life and agriculture.
  • maṇḍūkā: This term refers to frogs, which are often seen as indicators of ecological health and balance in nature.
  • dadataḥ: This means "giving" or "providing," similar to adād, emphasizing the act of sharing and abundance.
  • śatāni: This word means "hundreds," indicating a large quantity or multitude.
  • sahasrasāve: This term means "thousands," further emphasizing abundance and prosperity.
  • pra tiranta: This phrase can be interpreted as "to cross over" or "to transcend," suggesting overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • āyuḥ: This word means "life" or "longevity," highlighting the importance of a long and fulfilling life.

The mantra essentially invokes the blessings of longevity and prosperity, drawing on the symbolism of cows, goats, and nature. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, where the well-being of animals and the earth contributes to human prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Agriculture: Farmers can recite this mantra to seek blessings for their livestock and crops, ensuring a bountiful harvest and healthy animals.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mention of frogs highlights the importance of ecological balance. This mantra can inspire individuals to protect their environment and promote biodiversity.
  • Community Well-being: The focus on sharing and abundance encourages communities to support one another, fostering a sense of unity and collective prosperity.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing life, both human and animal, and the interconnectedness of all beings. By recognizing the value of nature and the blessings it provides, individuals can cultivate a life of abundance, health, and harmony.
