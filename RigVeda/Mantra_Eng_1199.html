तत् सविता वो 'मृतत्वम् आसुवद अगोह्यं यच्छ्रवयन्त ऐतन |तयं चिच्चमसम असुरस्य भक्षणम् एकं सन्तम् अकृतनुत चतुर्वयम् ||
tat savitā vo 'mṛtatvam āsuvad agohyaṁ yac chravayanta aitana | tyaṁ cic camasam asurasya bhakṣaṇam ekaṁ santam akṛṇutā caturvayam ||
  • tat savitā: This phrase refers to "that Savitar," where Savitar is the Sun God, symbolizing illumination, energy, and life-giving force.
  • vo 'mṛtatvam: This translates to "may grant you immortality," indicating a desire for eternal life or spiritual enlightenment.
  • āsuvad: This word means "goodness" or "auspiciousness," suggesting a wish for positive outcomes and blessings in life.
  • agohyaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "inaccessible" or "beyond reach," which may refer to the divine or higher truths that are not easily comprehensible.
  • yac chravayanta aitana: This means "that which is heard," implying the importance of knowledge and wisdom that is passed down through generations.
  • tyaṁ cic: This phrase can be understood as "that which is," emphasizing the existence of a singular truth or reality.
  • camasam: This word refers to "the essence" or "the core," suggesting a focus on the fundamental aspects of life.
  • asurasya bhakṣaṇam: This translates to "the consumption of the Asura," where Asuras are often seen as demonic or negative forces, indicating a struggle against negativity.
  • ekaṁ santam: This means "one, peaceful," highlighting the importance of unity and tranquility in life.
  • akṛṇutā caturvayam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the fourfold path," which may refer to the various paths of life or the four goals of human existence (Dharma, Artha, Kama, Moksha).

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound spiritual and philosophical concepts. It emphasizes the pursuit of immortality and goodness through the illumination provided by the divine (Savitar). In real life, this can be applied to the quest for knowledge, wisdom, and positive living. The mantra encourages individuals to seek higher truths, maintain unity and peace, and navigate the challenges posed by negative forces (Asuras) in their lives. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual growth and the pursuit of a balanced life, aligning with the four goals of human existence.
