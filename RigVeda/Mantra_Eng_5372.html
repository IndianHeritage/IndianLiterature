ब्रह्मा ण इन्द्रोप याहि विद्वान् अर्वांचस ते हरयः सन्तु युक्ताः |विश्वे चिद्धि त्वा विहवन्त मार्ता अस्माकं इच्छृणुहि विश्वमिन्वा ||
brahmā ṇa indropa yāhi vidvān arvāñcas te harayaḥ santu yuktāḥ | viśve cid dhi tvā vihavanta martā asmākam ic chṛṇuhi viśvaminva ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Brahman, may the wise ones who are knowledgeable and aware of the truth, come to us. May all beings be united and may they listen to us, just as the universe listens."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Brahmā: Refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy.
    • Indropa: This can be interpreted as a call to the divine or higher powers.
    • Vidvān: Means wise or knowledgeable, indicating the importance of wisdom in understanding the universe.
    • Arvāñcas: Refers to beings or entities that are aware and enlightened.
    • Harayaḥ: Represents the forces of nature or divine energies.
    • Yuktāḥ: Means being united or connected, emphasizing the importance of harmony.
    • Viśve: Refers to all beings or the universe as a whole.
    • Cid: Represents consciousness or awareness.
    • Vihavanta: Means to listen or pay attention.
    • Martā: Refers to mortals or human beings.
    • Asmākam: Means "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity.
    • Ic: Means "wish" or "desire," indicating intention.
    • Shṛṇuhi: Means "listen" or "hear," emphasizing the importance of communication.
    • Viśvaminva: Refers to the universe or the totality of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when people come together to solve a common problem, emphasizing the need for wisdom and unity.
    • In educational institutions, teachers and students can embody the essence of this mantra by fostering an environment of mutual respect and understanding, where knowledge is shared and valued.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages open communication and active listening, which are essential for building strong connections.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, wisdom, and awareness. It emphasizes the importance of collective consciousness and the need for individuals to come together in harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, education, and personal relationships, where the principles of understanding, respect, and collaboration are essential for growth and progress. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
