रक्षोहनं वाजिनं आ जिघर्मि मित्रं प्रतिष्ठम् उप यामि शर्मा | शिशानो अग्निः क्रतुभिः समिद्धः स नो दिवा स ऋषः पातु नक्तम् ||
rakṣohaṇaṁ vājinam ā jigharmi mitram prathiṣṭham upa yāmi śarma | śiśāno agniḥ kratubhiḥ samiddhaḥ sa no divā sa riṣaḥ pātu naktam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "I approach the protector, the powerful one, the friend who is established. May the fire, which is kindled by our efforts, protect us by day and by night."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rakṣohaṇaṁ: Protector or one who wards off evil.
    • vājinam: Powerful or strong, often associated with horses, symbolizing strength and speed.
    • ā jigharmi: I approach or I seek.
    • mitram: Friend or ally, indicating a relationship of trust and support.
    • prathiṣṭham: Established or grounded, suggesting stability and reliability.
    • upa yāmi: I go towards or I seek out.
    • śarma: Comfort, protection, or shelter.
    • śiśāno: The one who is kindled or ignited, referring to Agni (the fire).
    • agniḥ: Fire, a significant element in Vedic rituals and symbolism.
    • kratubhiḥ: By our efforts or actions, indicating the importance of intention and work.
    • samiddhaḥ: Kindled or lit, referring to the fire being ready for use.
    • sa no: May he (Agni) protect us.
    • divā: By day.
    • sa riṣaḥ: By night.
    • pātu: Protect or guard.
    • naktam: Night.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek protection and support from friends and family during challenging times. This mantra emphasizes the importance of having reliable allies who can provide comfort and security.
    • In a professional setting, teamwork and collaboration can be seen as a modern interpretation of the mantra, where colleagues support each other to achieve common goals, akin to the protective and powerful forces described.
    • During rituals or significant life events, invoking the presence of Agni symbolizes the purification of intentions and the seeking of divine protection, much like lighting a candle for hope and guidance.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection and support from both divine and earthly allies. It highlights the importance of community, friendship, and the power of collective efforts in overcoming challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance during difficult times, fostering teamwork in professional environments, and invoking spiritual protection during rituals. By recognizing the strength in unity and the importance of reliable support systems, individuals can navigate life's challenges with greater resilience and confidence.
