किम्मयः स्विच् चमस एष आस यं काव्येन चतुरो विचक्र |अथा सुनुध्वं सवनं मदाय पात ऋभवो मधुनः सोम्यस्य ||
kimmayaḥ svic camasa eṣa āsa yaṁ kāvyena caturo vicakra | athā sunudhvaṁ savanam madāya pāta ṛbhavo madhunaḥ somyasya ||
  • kimmayaḥ svic camasa eṣa āsa: This phrase can be translated to "What is this that has come to us?" It reflects a sense of curiosity and inquiry about the nature of existence and the experiences we encounter in life.
  • yaṁ kāvyena caturo vicakra: This part means "which has been crafted by the wise." It suggests that the experiences and knowledge we gain are shaped by the wisdom of those who came before us, much like a poem or a piece of art that conveys deeper meanings.
  • athā sunudhvaṁ savanam madāya: Translated, this means "Now, let us offer our praise with joy." It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and celebration in our lives, encouraging us to acknowledge and appreciate the blessings we receive.
  • pāta ṛbhavo madhunaḥ somyasya: This can be interpreted as "Let us drink the sweet nectar of the divine." It symbolizes the pursuit of joy and fulfillment, akin to savoring the sweetness of life and the divine experiences that enrich our existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of inquiry, wisdom, gratitude, and the pursuit of joy. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, appreciate the contributions of others, and celebrate the beauty of life. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal reflections, communal gatherings, or spiritual practices, where one can express gratitude and seek deeper connections with the world around them. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling life experience.
