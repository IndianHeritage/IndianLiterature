आयं देवाय जनमने स्तोमो विप्रेभिर आसयाः |अकारि रत्नधातमः ||
ayaṁ devāya janmane stomo viprebhir āsayā | akāri ratnadhātamaḥ ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
  • devāya - Meaning "to the deity" or "for the divine," it emphasizes the connection to the divine or spiritual realm.
  • janmane - This means "for birth" or "for the purpose of birth," suggesting a creation or a new beginning.
  • stomo - Refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating the act of worship or reverence towards the divine.
  • viprebhir - This translates to "by the learned" or "by the wise," highlighting the role of knowledgeable individuals in spiritual practices.
  • āsayā - Meaning "with intention" or "with purpose," it suggests that actions are taken with a specific goal in mind.
  • akāri - This word means "to create" or "to make," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • ratnadhātamaḥ - This translates to "the one who possesses the most precious gems" or "the one who is the best among the valuable," symbolizing the highest form of wealth or virtue.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation directed towards the divine, asking for blessings and guidance in the process of creation and life. It emphasizes the importance of intention and wisdom in spiritual practices, suggesting that true creation comes from a place of knowledge and purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of creation and intention, individuals can focus on their personal development by setting clear goals and seeking knowledge to achieve them.
  • Community Building: The reference to learned individuals highlights the importance of wisdom in community efforts. Engaging knowledgeable members can lead to more effective and meaningful initiatives.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals and prayers, this mantra serves as a reminder to approach the divine with respect and intention, fostering a deeper connection with spirituality.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for intentional creation and the pursuit of wisdom. It encourages individuals to recognize the divine in their actions and to approach life with purpose. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a life rich in meaning and connection, both personally and within the community.
