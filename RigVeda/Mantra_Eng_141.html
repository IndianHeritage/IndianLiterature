तान यजत्राम् ऋतावृधो 'ग्ने पत्निवतस् कृधि |मध्वः सुजिह्व पायय ||
tān yajatrām̐ ṛtāvṛdho 'gne patnīvatas kṛdhi | madhvaḥ sujihva pāyaya ||
  • tān yajatrām̐: This phrase can be translated as "those who perform the sacrifice." In the context of the RigVeda, it refers to individuals or groups who engage in rituals and offerings to the deities, seeking blessings and prosperity.
  • ṛtāvṛdho: This term means "abiding by truth" or "upholding righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living a life aligned with dharma (moral order) and truthfulness, which is a core principle in Vedic philosophy.
  • 'gne: This word refers to "O Agni," the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. Agni is often invoked in Vedic rituals as the one who carries offerings to the gods.
  • patnīvatas: This term can be interpreted as "with the qualities of a husband" or "in a nurturing manner." It suggests a sense of care and responsibility, particularly in relationships and community.
  • kṛdhi: This means "do" or "make." It is a call to action, urging the deity or the practitioners to engage in the act of creation or offering.
  • madhvaḥ: This word translates to "sweet" or "pleasant." It signifies the positive outcomes and blessings that arise from righteous actions and offerings.
  • sujihva: This term means "well-being" or "prosperity." It reflects the desire for a fruitful and harmonious life, both individually and collectively.
  • pāyaya: This translates to "protect" or "nourish." It is a plea for divine protection and sustenance, highlighting the need for support in one’s endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra "tān yajatrām̐ ṛtāvṛdho 'gne patnīvatas kṛdhi | madhvaḥ sujihva pāyaya ||" encapsulates the essence of performing righteous actions and rituals with sincerity and care. It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and the nurturing aspect of relationships, particularly in the context of community and family. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family gatherings, community service, or any situation where individuals come together to support one another. By invoking the qualities of Agni, practitioners are reminded of the significance of their actions and the blessings that arise from living a life of integrity and compassion. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for fostering harmony and well-being in both personal and communal spheres.
