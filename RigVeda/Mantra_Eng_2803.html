नामानि ते शतक्रतो विश्वाभिर गीरभिर इमहे |इन्द्राभिमातिषाह्ये ||
nāmāni te śatakrato viśvābhir gīrbhir īmahe | indrābhimātiṣāhye ||
  • nāmāni te - "Your names": This phrase refers to the various names or attributes of a divine being, in this case, Indra, who is often associated with strength and power.
  • śatakrato - "O Hundred-Strength": This is a title for Indra, emphasizing his immense power and capability. The term "hundred" symbolizes completeness and abundance.
  • viśvābhir - "with all beings": This indicates inclusivity, suggesting that the invocation is made in the presence of all creatures and entities in the universe.
  • gīrbhir - "with hymns": This refers to the act of chanting or singing praises, which is a common practice in many cultures to honor deities or significant figures.
  • īmahe - "we invoke": This is a call to action, expressing the desire to connect with the divine through prayer or invocation.
  • indrābhimātiṣāhye - "for the strength of Indra": This phrase highlights the purpose of the invocation, seeking the strength and support of Indra in overcoming challenges.

The mantra can be understood as a collective invocation to Indra, asking for his strength and support through the power of names and hymns. It emphasizes the importance of community and the shared experience of seeking divine assistance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios where individuals or groups seek strength and support, such as:

  • Teamwork: In a workplace setting, when a team faces a challenging project, they can come together, invoking the spirit of collaboration and strength to achieve their goals.
  • Community Support: During difficult times, such as natural disasters or personal crises, communities can unite, invoking collective strength to support one another.
  • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing personal struggles can recite this mantra as a form of meditation or prayer, seeking inner strength and resilience.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to foster unity and invoke strength from a higher power. It serves as a reminder that in times of need, we can draw upon the collective strength of our community and the divine to overcome obstacles. Whether in personal or communal contexts, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to seek support and strength through connection and shared purpose.
