अभी षतस तद आ भरेंद्र ज्यायः कनीयसः |पुरूवसुर हि मगवन सनाद असि भरे-भरे च हव्यः ||
abhī ṣatas tad ā bharendra jyāyaḥ kanīyasaḥ | purūvasur hi maghavan sanād asi bhare-bhare ca havyaḥ ||
  • abhī - This word can be interpreted as "towards" or "in the direction of." It signifies an intention or a goal that one is aiming for.
  • ṣatas - Meaning "hundred," this term often symbolizes abundance or a multitude of blessings.
  • tad - Translated as "that," it refers to something specific that is being acknowledged or called upon.
  • ā - This preposition can mean "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • bharendra - A compound word where "bhara" means "to fill" and "indra" refers to the king or lord, often associated with power and authority. Thus, it can be understood as "the lord who fills with abundance."
  • jyāyaḥ - This term means "greater" or "superior," suggesting a comparison or a desire for something of higher quality or status.
  • kanīyasaḥ - Meaning "smaller" or "lesser," it contrasts with "jyāyaḥ," indicating a spectrum of value or importance.
  • purūvasur - Referring to a figure from Vedic mythology, often associated with prosperity and abundance.
  • hi - A particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • maghavan - This term refers to the deity of wealth and abundance, often invoked for blessings in material and spiritual wealth.
  • sanād - Meaning "from the beginning" or "eternally," it suggests a timeless quality to the blessings being sought.
  • asi - Translated as "you are," indicating a presence or a state of being.
  • bhare-bhare - This repetition emphasizes the act of filling or providing, suggesting a continuous flow of abundance.
  • ca - Meaning "and," it connects ideas or entities together.
  • havyaḥ - Referring to offerings or sacrifices, often in the context of rituals aimed at invoking divine blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance and blessings from divine sources. It reflects a desire for both material and spiritual wealth, recognizing the interplay between greater and lesser values in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for prosperity, in personal affirmations for abundance, or in community gatherings where collective blessings are sought. The significance lies in the acknowledgment of divine support in our endeavors, encouraging individuals to strive for greater achievements while remaining grounded in humility and gratitude.
