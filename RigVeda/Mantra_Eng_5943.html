अन्योन्यं अनु गृभ्णात्य एनोर अपाम प्रसर्गे यद अमन्दिषाताम् |मण्डूको यद अभिवृष्टः कणिष्कन पृष्णिः सम्पृङ्क्ते हरितेन वाचम् ||
anyo anyam anu gṛbhṇāty enor apām prasarge yad amandiṣātām | maṇḍūko yad abhivṛṣṭaḥ kaniṣkan pṛśniḥ sampṛṅkte haritena vācam ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • anyo anyam anu gṛbhṇāty - This phrase can be interpreted as "one takes another" or "one receives from another." It signifies the interconnectedness of beings and the mutual support that exists in relationships.
    • enor apām prasarge - This translates to "in the flow of waters." It symbolizes the essence of life and the nurturing aspect of nature, emphasizing the importance of water in sustaining life.
    • yad amandiṣātām - This can be understood as "that which is not obstructed." It suggests the idea of freedom and the unobstructed flow of energy or life force.
    • maṇḍūko yad abhivṛṣṭaḥ - This refers to "the frog that is drenched." Frogs are often associated with rain and fertility, indicating the blessings of nature and the cycles of life.
    • kaniṣkan pṛśniḥ - This phrase can be interpreted as "the small one of the earth." It highlights the significance of humility and the importance of the earth in our existence.
    • sampṛṅkte haritena vācam - This translates to "the speech that is connected with the green." It signifies the connection between nature and communication, suggesting that our words and actions should be in harmony with the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The interconnectedness of people can be seen in community support systems, where individuals help each other in times of need, reflecting the idea of "one takes another."
    • Water conservation efforts highlight the significance of "enor apām prasarge," as communities work together to preserve this vital resource for future generations.
    • The concept of "yad amandiṣātām" can be applied in personal growth, where individuals strive to remove obstacles in their lives to achieve their goals.
    • Frogs, as indicators of environmental health, remind us of the importance of biodiversity and the need to protect ecosystems, resonating with "maṇḍūko yad abhivṛṣṭaḥ."
    • Humility, as suggested by "kaniṣkan pṛśniḥ," can be practiced in daily life by recognizing our place in the larger context of the world and respecting all forms of life.
    • Environmental activism embodies "sampṛṅkte haritena vācam," where individuals advocate for sustainable practices and communicate the importance of protecting nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nature, and the need for harmonious relationships. It serves as a reminder that our actions and words should reflect respect for the environment and for each other.
    • In real life, the teachings of this mantra can guide us in fostering community, promoting sustainability, and living in harmony with the natural world. By embracing these principles, we can contribute to a more balanced and nurturing existence for all beings.
