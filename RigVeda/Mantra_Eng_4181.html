मन्त्र: सनत् साश्व्यम् पशुम् उता गव्यं शतावयम् |श्यावाश्वस्तुताय या दुर् वीरायोपबरभृत् ||
sanat sāśvyam paśum uta gavyaṁ śatāvayam | śyāvāśvastutāya yā dor vīrāyopabarbṛhat ||
  • Sanat: This word translates to "eternal" or "everlasting." It signifies the timeless nature of existence and the universe.
  • Sāśvyam: This can be interpreted as "with strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength in both physical and spiritual realms.
  • Paśum: Meaning "the animal" or "the living being," this term reflects the connection between humans and nature, highlighting the significance of all living creatures.
  • Uta: This word means "and" or "also," serving as a conjunction that links ideas together.
  • Gavyaṁ: Referring to "cattle" or "the wealth of cattle," this term underscores the importance of livestock in ancient agrarian societies, symbolizing prosperity and sustenance.
  • Śatāvayam: This translates to "a hundred," often used to denote abundance or a large quantity, suggesting the idea of plentiful resources.
  • Śyāvā: Meaning "dark" or "blue," this word can symbolize depth, mystery, or the vastness of the sky and the ocean.
  • Śvastutāya: This term can be interpreted as "for the well-being" or "for prosperity," indicating a focus on health and success.
  • Yā: This means "who" or "that," referring to a subject or entity being discussed.
  • Dor: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful," emphasizing the qualities of strength and resilience.
  • Vīrāyopabarbṛhat: This can be understood as "to the great hero" or "to the mighty one," celebrating valor and heroism in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of eternal strength, the interconnectedness of life, and the importance of prosperity and heroism. In real life, these concepts can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal development, embracing the idea of sanat encourages individuals to seek lasting values and principles that guide their lives.
  • The emphasis on sāśvyam reminds us to cultivate inner strength and resilience, especially during challenging times.
  • Recognizing the significance of paśum fosters a sense of responsibility towards nature and all living beings, promoting environmental stewardship.
  • The notion of gavyaṁ highlights the importance of sustainable practices in agriculture and animal husbandry, ensuring food security and economic stability.
  • Lastly, the celebration of vīrāyopabarbṛhat inspires individuals to act courageously and stand up for what is right, embodying the spirit of heroism in everyday life.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values that contribute to a harmonious and prosperous existence, encouraging us to live with purpose and integrity.
