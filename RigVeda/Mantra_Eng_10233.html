मयि देवाः द्रविणम् ā यजन्ताम् मय्य् आशीर् अस्तु मयि देवहूतिः |दैव्याः होतारो वनुषन्त पूर्वे 'रिष्टाः स्याम तन्मा सुवीराः ||
mayi devā draviṇam ā yajantām mayy āśīr astu mayi devahūtiḥ | daivyā hotāro vanuṣanta pūrve 'riṣṭāḥ syāma tanvā suvīrāḥ ||
  • mayi devā draviṇam ā yajantām: This phrase translates to "May the gods who are worshipped by me grant me wealth." Here, "devā" refers to the deities, "draviṇam" means wealth or resources, and "yajantām" indicates the act of worship or offering. In real life, this can be seen as a prayer for prosperity and abundance, where individuals seek blessings from higher powers for their material needs.
  • mayy āśīr astu: This means "May there be blessings upon me." The word "āśīr" signifies blessings or good fortune. In practical terms, this can be related to the desire for positive outcomes in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and career. For example, before starting a new venture, one might seek blessings for success and well-being.
  • mayi devahūtiḥ: This translates to "May the divine offerings be with me." "Devahūtiḥ" refers to the offerings made to the divine. This can be understood as a call for spiritual connection and the importance of giving back to the universe or the divine forces that support us. In everyday life, this could manifest as acts of charity or kindness, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • daivyā hotāro vanuṣanta: This phrase means "The divine priests who perform the rituals." "Daivyā" refers to the divine, "hotāro" are the priests or those who invoke the deities, and "vanuṣanta" means to perform or carry out. This highlights the role of spiritual leaders and rituals in connecting individuals with the divine. In modern contexts, this can be seen in various religious ceremonies where community leaders guide individuals in their spiritual practices.
  • pūrve 'riṣṭāḥ syāma tanvā suvīrāḥ: This translates to "May we, the strong ones, be blessed as the ancients were." "Pūrve" refers to the ancients or predecessors, "riṣṭāḥ" means those who are blessed, "syāma" indicates a wish or hope, and "suvīrāḥ" means strong or valiant. This line emphasizes the continuity of blessings and strength from past generations to the present. In real life, this can be reflected in the values and teachings passed down through families, encouraging individuals to embody strength and resilience.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine blessings, prosperity, and strength, both for oneself and the community. It highlights the importance of worship, gratitude, and the continuity of blessings through generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, community gatherings, or rituals aimed at invoking prosperity and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual practices, and the pursuit of a fulfilling and abundant existence.
