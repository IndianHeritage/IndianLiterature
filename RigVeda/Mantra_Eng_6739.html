आ त्वा ग्रावां वदन्न इह सोमि घोषेण यच्छतु |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यया दिवावसो ||
ā tvā grāvā vadann iha somī ghoṣeṇa yacchatu | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • tvā: you
    • grāvā: the call or invocation
    • vadann: speaking or uttering
    • iha: here
    • somī: the one who is associated with Soma (a sacred drink)
    • ghoṣeṇa: with the sound or noise
    • yacchatu: may grant or bestow
    • divo: of the heavens
    • amuṣya: of this (or that) one
    • śāsato: who rules or governs
    • divaṁ: the sky or heaven
    • yaya: by which
    • divāvaso: dwelling in the day or daytime
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is an invocation that seeks blessings and guidance from divine forces.
    • The mention of "Soma" indicates a connection to spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "may grant" suggests a plea for assistance in achieving higher states of consciousness or understanding.
    • The reference to "the heavens" and "daytime" symbolizes clarity, truth, and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be recited during meditation or prayer to seek clarity in decision-making.
    • In a communal setting, it can be used in rituals to invoke blessings for collective well-being and harmony.
    • Professionally, individuals may use this mantra to inspire creativity and innovation, drawing on divine guidance for their work.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with higher spiritual realms, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, community bonding, and professional development.
    • By invoking divine assistance, individuals can navigate challenges with greater wisdom and insight, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
