प्रातर्यावाण प्रथमा यजध्वं पुरा गृध्राद अररुषः पिबातः | प्रातर हि यज्ञं अश्विनाः दधाते प्र शंसन्ति कवयः पूर्वभाजः ||
prātaryāvāṇā prathamā yajadhvam purā gṛdhrād araruṣaḥ pibātaḥ | prātar hi yajñam aśvinā dadhāte pra śaṁsanti kavayaḥ pūrvabhājaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prātaryāvāṇā prathamā yajadhvam purā gṛdhrād araruṣaḥ pibātaḥ | prātar hi yajñam aśvinā dadhāte pra śaṁsanti kavayaḥ pūrvabhājaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "In the morning, you who are the first to perform the sacrifice, drink from the cup of the eagle, for the Aśvins bestow the sacrifice in the morning, and the wise ones praise the previous offerings."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prātaryāvāṇā: In the morning, those who are active or engaged.
    • prathamā: First or foremost.
    • yajadhvam: Perform the sacrifice or offering.
    • purā: Previously or in the past.
    • gṛdhrād: From the eagle, symbolizing strength and vision.
    • araruṣaḥ: Drink or consume.
    • pibātaḥ: To drink or imbibe.
    • prātar: In the morning.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • yajñam: The sacrifice or offering.
    • aśvinā: The Aśvins, divine twin horsemen associated with health and medicine.
    • dadhāte: They bestow or provide.
    • pra: Before or prior.
    • śaṁsanti: Praise or commend.
    • kavayaḥ: The wise or learned ones.
    • pūrvabhājaḥ: Those who partake of previous offerings.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of morning rituals and the act of sacrifice as a means of connecting with the divine.
    • The reference to the eagle symbolizes clarity of vision and the ability to rise above challenges, suggesting that one should approach their duties with a clear mind and purpose.
    • The Aśvins, as divine figures, represent health and well-being, indicating that performing rituals in the morning can lead to blessings in these areas.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be interpreted as a call to start each day with intention and purpose, perhaps through morning rituals such as meditation, exercise, or setting daily goals.
    • Just as the eagle is a symbol of strength and vision, individuals can strive to gain clarity in their personal and professional lives by reflecting on their goals and aspirations each morning.
    • The idea of sacrifice can be seen in the willingness to put in effort and dedication towards one's responsibilities, whether in work, family, or community service.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of starting the day with a sense of purpose and connection to the divine.
    • By engaging in morning rituals and reflecting on our intentions, we can cultivate a mindset that promotes well-being and success.
    • Ultimately, the teachings of this mantra encourage us to honor our past efforts while striving for a brighter future, guided by wisdom and clarity.
