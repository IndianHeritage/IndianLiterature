स हि द्वारो द्वारीषु वव्र ūधनि चन्द्रबुध्नो मदवृद्धो मनिषिभिः | इन्द्रं तं अह्वे स्वपस्यया धियā मंहिष्ठरातिं स हि पप्रिर अन्धसः ||
sa hi dvaro dvariṣu vavra ūdhani candrabudhno madavṛddho manīṣibhiḥ | indraṁ tam ahve svapasyayā dhiyā maṁhiṣṭharātiṁ sa hi paprir andhasaḥ ||
  • sa hi dvaro dvariṣu: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, he is the door among the doors." Here, "dvaro" refers to a door, symbolizing opportunities or pathways in life. In real life, this can be likened to the various choices we face, where each door represents a different opportunity or direction we can take.
  • vavra ūdhani: This means "the one who is powerful in the waters." Water often symbolizes emotions and the subconscious. In a real-life context, this could refer to someone who is adept at navigating their emotions or the emotional landscape of others, much like a skilled mediator or counselor.
  • candrabudhno madavṛddho: Translated, this means "the moon, who is full of intoxication." The moon is often associated with calmness and reflection, while "madavṛddho" suggests a state of heightened awareness or inspiration. This can be seen in artists or thinkers who draw inspiration from nature and the cosmos, leading to creative expressions.
  • manīṣibhiḥ: This term refers to "the wise ones" or "the thinkers." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intellect in decision-making. In real life, this can be related to the value of seeking advice from knowledgeable individuals when faced with important choices.
  • indraṁ tam ahve: This translates to "I call upon Indra." Indra is a deity associated with strength, rain, and storms, symbolizing power and authority. In practical terms, this can represent invoking strength or support in challenging situations, much like seeking help from a mentor or leader during difficult times.
  • svapasyayā dhiyā: This means "with the vision of the mind." It highlights the importance of clarity of thought and vision in achieving one's goals. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of setting clear intentions and visualizing success in personal or professional endeavors.
  • maṁhiṣṭharātiṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the best of the best." It signifies striving for excellence and the pursuit of the highest ideals. In real life, this can be related to individuals who aim for the highest standards in their work or personal lives, inspiring others to do the same.
  • sa hi paprir andhasaḥ: This translates to "he indeed removes darkness." This symbolizes the dispelling of ignorance and confusion. In practical terms, this can be likened to the role of education and knowledge in illuminating our paths and helping us make informed decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, clarity, and the pursuit of excellence in navigating life's opportunities and challenges. It encourages individuals to seek guidance from wise mentors, to invoke strength in difficult times, and to strive for the highest ideals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, decision-making processes, and the pursuit of one's goals, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
