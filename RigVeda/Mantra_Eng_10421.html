मम पुत्राः शत्रुहणोऽथो मे दुहितā विराट् |उताहमस्मि संजयā पत्यु मे श्लोक उत्तमः ||
mama putrāḥ śatruhaṇo 'tho me duhitā virāṭ | utāham asmi saṁjayā patyau me śloka uttamaḥ ||
  • mama putrāḥ: This phrase translates to "my sons." It signifies the importance of progeny and the responsibilities that come with parenthood. In real life, this can be seen in the way parents nurture and guide their children, instilling values and ethics.
  • śatruhaṇo: This means "destroyers of enemies." It reflects the idea of strength and protection. In contemporary terms, this can relate to individuals or communities that stand up against adversities or threats, such as activists fighting for social justice or communities uniting against crime.
  • 'tho me duhitā: This translates to "and my daughter." It emphasizes the equal importance of daughters alongside sons. In modern society, this can be seen in movements advocating for gender equality and the empowerment of women, highlighting that daughters are equally valued and capable.
  • virāṭ: This word means "great" or "universal." It signifies the vastness and interconnectedness of life. In real life, this can be related to the idea of global citizenship, where individuals recognize their role in a larger community and the importance of collective well-being.
  • utāham asmi saṁjayā: This translates to "and I am victorious." It reflects a sense of triumph and resilience. In everyday life, this can be seen in personal achievements, such as overcoming challenges in one's career or personal life, embodying the spirit of perseverance.
  • patyau me: This means "my husband." It signifies partnership and support in relationships. In real life, this can be observed in healthy marriages where both partners support each other's aspirations and work together towards common goals.
  • śloka uttamaḥ: This translates to "the best verse." It indicates the significance of wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of education and the importance of learning in making informed decisions in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of family, strength, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of both sons and daughters, the value of supportive relationships, and the triumph of the human spirit. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as advocating for gender equality, fostering strong family bonds, and striving for personal and communal victories. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the collective responsibility we hold towards each other in our journey towards success and fulfillment.
