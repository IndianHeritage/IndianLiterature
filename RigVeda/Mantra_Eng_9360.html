तनूष ṭे वाजिन तन्वं नयन्ती वामम् अस्मभ्यं धातु शर्मा तुभ्यम् |अहरुतो महो धरुणाय देवान दिवीव ज्योतिः स्वम् आमिमीयाः ||
tanūṣ ṭe vājin tanva1ṁ nayantī vāmam asmabhyaṁ dhātu śarma tubhyam | ahruto maho dharuṇāya devān divīva jyotiḥ svam ā mimīyāḥ ||
  • tanūṣ ṭe vājin tanvaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "Your body is strong and powerful." Here, "tanūṣ" refers to the body or form, and "vājin" signifies strength or power. In real life, this can be related to the importance of physical health and fitness. For example, athletes train rigorously to maintain a strong body, which is essential for their performance.
  • nayantī vāmam asmabhyaṁ: This means "leading us towards prosperity." "Nayantī" indicates guidance or leadership, while "vāmam" refers to the direction of good or auspiciousness. In a practical context, this can be seen in the role of mentors or leaders in our lives who guide us towards success, such as teachers or coaches who help us achieve our goals.
  • dhātu śarma tubhyam: This translates to "bestowing upon you the essence of happiness." "Dhātu" refers to the essence or substance, and "śarma" means happiness or comfort. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of joy and contentment, such as spending time with loved ones or engaging in activities that bring us joy, like hobbies or travel.
  • ahruto maho dharuṇāya devān: This phrase means "the great ones who are revered." "Ahruto" signifies those who are honored, "maho" means great, and "dharuṇāya" refers to the divine or celestial beings. This can be connected to the respect we give to leaders, elders, or spiritual figures in our communities, such as parents or religious leaders who guide us with their wisdom.
  • divīva jyotiḥ svam ā mimīyāḥ: This translates to "like the light of the heavens, may you shine." "Divīva" refers to the divine or heavenly, "jyotiḥ" means light, and "svam ā mimīyāḥ" indicates the act of shining or radiating. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who inspire others through their actions and character, such as activists or philanthropists who illuminate the path for others through their selfless deeds.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, guidance, happiness, reverence, and inspiration in our lives. It signifies that by nurturing our physical and mental well-being, seeking guidance from wise leaders, and striving to bring joy to ourselves and others, we can lead a fulfilling life. This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, community leadership, and spiritual growth, reminding us to cultivate these qualities for a harmonious existence.
