यो अग्निः सप्तमानुषः श्रितो विश्वेषु सिन्धुषु |तम आगन्म त्रिपस्त्यम मन्धातुर दस्युहन्तमम् अग्निं यज्ञेषु पूर्व्यं नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
yo agniḥ saptamānuṣaḥ śrito viśveṣu sindhuṣu | tam āganma tripastyam mandhātur dasyuhantamam agniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyaṁ nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • agniḥ: fire
    • saptamānuṣaḥ: seventh among men
    • śrito: who is invoked
    • viśveṣu: in all
    • sindhuṣu: waters
    • tam: him
    • āganma: we approach
    • tripastyam: threefold
    • mandhātur: of Mandhata
    • dasyuhantamam: the slayer of the Dasyus
    • agniṁ: fire
    • yajñeṣu: in sacrifices
    • pūrvyaṁ: the ancient
    • nabhantām: of the heavens
    • anyake: other
    • same: in the same way
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "saptamānuṣaḥ" suggests that Agni is the seventh among men, indicating a special status or role in the cosmic order.
    • By saying "śrito viśveṣu sindhuṣu," it emphasizes that Agni is invoked in all waters, symbolizing purity and the essential nature of fire in various aspects of life.
    • "Tam āganma" expresses the desire to approach Agni, seeking his blessings and guidance.
    • The term "tripastyam" refers to the threefold nature of Agni, which can be interpreted as the physical fire, the spiritual fire, and the fire of knowledge.
    • "Mandhātur dasyuhantamam" refers to Agni as the slayer of the Dasyus, representing the triumph of good over evil.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to Agni in sacrifices, highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas, where offerings are made to the fire, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The concept of fire as a purifying agent can be seen in various cultures, where fire is used in cleansing rituals, signifying the removal of negativity and the welcoming of positive energies.
    • In modern life, the metaphor of fire can represent passion and motivation, encouraging individuals to pursue their goals with fervor and dedication.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of Agni as a divine force that connects humanity with the cosmos.
    • It teaches us the value of rituals and the role of fire in our lives, both literally and metaphorically.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking the spirit of Agni can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, purity, and the strength to overcome challenges, embodying the essence of transformation and growth.
