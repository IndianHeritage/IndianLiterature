पीपाय दhenुर अदितिः ऋताय जनाय मित्रावरुणा हविर्दे |हिनोति यद वां विदथे सपर्णं स रातहव्यः मानवः न होता ||
pīpāya dhenur aditir ṛtāya janāya mitrāvaruṇā havirde | hinoti yad vāṁ vidathe saparyan sa rātahavyo mānuṣo na hotā ||
  • pīpāya dhenur: This phrase can be translated as "the cow that gives milk." In Vedic culture, the cow is a symbol of nourishment and abundance. It represents the sustenance that is essential for life.
  • aditir: Aditi is often referred to as the mother of the gods and represents the infinite, the boundless, and the nurturing aspect of the universe. She embodies the concept of motherhood and protection.
  • ṛtāya: This term refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." It signifies the importance of living in harmony with the natural laws and the universe's inherent order.
  • janāya: This word means "for the people" or "for humanity." It emphasizes the collective aspect of existence and the need for community and cooperation.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This refers to the duality of Mitra and Varuna, representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. They symbolize the balance between social relationships and the moral order of the universe.
  • havirde: This term is associated with offerings or sacrifices made to the divine. It highlights the importance of gratitude and recognition of the divine forces in our lives.
  • hinoti: This means "to harm" or "to injure." It serves as a reminder of the consequences of our actions and the importance of ethical behavior.
  • yad vāṁ vidathe saparyan: This phrase can be interpreted as "whatever you worship in your gatherings." It emphasizes the significance of collective worship and the power of community in spiritual practices.
  • sa rātahavyo mānuṣo na hotā: This translates to "that person who does not perform rituals is not a true human." It underscores the importance of rituals and their role in connecting individuals to the divine and to each other.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of community, nourishment, and the cosmic order. It highlights the significance of ethical behavior, collective worship, and the nurturing aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to promote community service, environmental sustainability (symbolized by the cow), and the importance of maintaining harmony in relationships. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for ethical conduct, individuals can contribute to a more balanced and harmonious society.
