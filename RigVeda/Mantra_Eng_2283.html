इदं कवेर आदित्यस्य स्वराजो विश्वानि सान्त्य अभ्य अस्तु मह्ना |अति यो मन्द्रो यजथाय देवः सुकीर्तिम भिक्षे वरुणस्य भूरेः ||
idaṁ kaver ādityasya svarājo viśvāni sānty abhy astu mahnā | ati yo mandro yajathāya devaḥ sukīrtim bhikṣe varuṇasya bhūreḥ ||
  • idaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the present moment or the immediate context.
  • kaver: Refers to "the poet" or "the seer," indicating a person with deep insight or wisdom.
  • ādityasya: This means "of the sun" or "of the divine light," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and the source of life.
  • svarājo: Translates to "the radiant" or "the shining," emphasizing brilliance and clarity.
  • viśvāni: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • sānty: This word means "peace" or "calmness," suggesting a state of tranquility.
  • abhy: Translates to "towards" or "for," indicating direction or purpose.
  • astu: Means "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or intention.
  • mahnā: This means "great" or "mighty," referring to something of significant importance.
  • ati: Translates to "beyond" or "exceeding," indicating something that surpasses limits.
  • yo: Means "who," referring to a person or entity.
  • mandro: This word means "the hymn" or "the chant," indicating a sacred expression or prayer.
  • yajathāya: Refers to "for the sacrifice" or "for the offering," indicating a ritualistic context.
  • devaḥ: Means "the divine" or "the god," referring to a higher power or spiritual entity.
  • sukīrtim: Translates to "good fame" or "auspicious reputation," indicating the importance of positive recognition.
  • bhikṣe: Means "in alms" or "in offerings," suggesting a context of giving and receiving.
  • varuṇasya: Refers to "of Varuna," the god of water and cosmic order, symbolizing balance and harmony.
  • bhūreḥ: This means "of abundance" or "of plenty," indicating richness and prosperity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for peace, abundance, and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and enlightenment, invoking the blessings of the sun and the divine for a harmonious existence. The reference to Varuna highlights the need for balance in life, suggesting that one should seek not only material wealth but also spiritual fulfillment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During personal meditation or reflection, to seek clarity and peace in one's thoughts.
  • In community gatherings or rituals, to invoke blessings for collective harmony and prosperity.
  • In educational settings, to inspire students to pursue knowledge and wisdom, fostering a culture of learning.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking peace and wisdom in our lives. It encourages us to strive for a balance between our material and spiritual pursuits, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and harmonious existence.
