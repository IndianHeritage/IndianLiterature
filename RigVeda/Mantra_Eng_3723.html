त्वां अग्ने धार्णसिं विश्वधा वयं गीरभिर गृणन्तो नमसोपा सेदिमा |स नो जुषस्व समिधानो अंगिरो देवो मार्तस्य यशसा सुदीतिभिः ||
tvām agne dharṇasiṁ viśvadhā vayaṁ gīrbhir gṛṇanto namasopa sedima | sa no juṣasva samidhāno aṅgiro devo martasya yaśasā sudītibhiḥ ||
  • tvām agne: This phrase translates to "to you, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of fire in our daily lives, such as cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • dharṇasiṁ: This means "you who hold." It signifies Agni's role as a sustainer and protector. In our lives, we often rely on various forms of support, whether it be family, friends, or community, to help us through challenges.
  • viśvadhā: This translates to "the one who encompasses all." It indicates Agni's universal presence and significance. In a broader context, this can be seen in how fire is a common element across cultures, representing purification and transformation.
  • vayaṁ gīrbhir gṛṇanto: This means "we sing praises with our words." It emphasizes the importance of expression and gratitude. In real life, expressing appreciation can strengthen relationships and foster a positive environment.
  • namasopa sedima: This translates to "we bow down to you." It reflects humility and respect. In our daily lives, showing respect to others, whether in personal or professional settings, can lead to mutual understanding and cooperation.
  • sa no juṣasva: This means "may you be pleased with us." It signifies a desire for divine favor. In real life, seeking approval or support from mentors, leaders, or even peers can be crucial for personal and professional growth.
  • samidhāno aṅgiro: This translates to "the one who is kindled by the Angiras." Angiras refers to a group of sages known for their wisdom. This can be related to how we seek knowledge and guidance from wise individuals in our lives.
  • devo martasya yaśasā sudītibhiḥ: This means "O deity, grant us the glory of the mortals." It reflects a wish for success and recognition. In real life, striving for excellence in our endeavors can lead to achievements that bring honor and respect.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a symbol of energy, transformation, and universal presence. It teaches us the values of respect, gratitude, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering relationships, seeking guidance, and striving for personal excellence. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and successful life, much like the transformative power of fire in our daily existence.
