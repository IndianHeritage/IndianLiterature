अपश्यम् अस्य महतो महित्वम् अमर्त्यस्य मार्त्यासु विक्षु |नानाहनू विभृते सम भरेते असिन्वती बाप्सती भूय अत्तः ||
apaśyam asya mahato mahitvam amartyasya martyāsu vikṣu | nānā hanū vibhṛte sam bharete asinvatī bapsatī bhūry attaḥ ||
  • apaśyam - This word translates to "I have not seen" or "I do not perceive." It indicates a lack of direct experience or understanding.
  • asya - Meaning "of this" or "of this entity," it refers to something significant that is being discussed.
  • mahato - This means "great" or "mighty," suggesting the importance or vastness of the subject at hand.
  • mahitvam - Translated as "greatness," it emphasizes the grandeur or elevated status of the entity being referred to.
  • amartyasya - This word means "of the immortal," indicating a connection to the eternal or divine aspect of existence.
  • martyāsu - Meaning "among mortals," it contrasts the immortal with the mortal realm, highlighting the human experience.
  • vikṣu - This term can be interpreted as "in the midst of" or "among," suggesting a presence or influence within a certain context.
  • nānā - Meaning "various" or "many," it indicates diversity or multiplicity in the subject matter.
  • hanū - This translates to "jaws" or "mouths," which can symbolize communication or the act of speaking.
  • vibhṛte - Meaning "that which is carried" or "that which is borne," it suggests the idea of support or sustenance.
  • sam - This word means "together" or "in harmony," indicating unity or collective action.
  • bharete - Translated as "to bear" or "to carry," it emphasizes the act of supporting or enduring.
  • asinvatī - This term can be interpreted as "with strength" or "powerful," suggesting resilience and capability.
  • bapsatī - Meaning "abundant" or "rich," it indicates a state of plenty or prosperity.
  • bhūry - This translates to "great" or "many," reinforcing the idea of abundance.
  • attaḥ - Meaning "from" or "of," it indicates a source or origin of the qualities being discussed.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra reflects on the contrast between the mortal and the immortal, emphasizing the greatness and abundance of the divine or eternal aspects of existence. It suggests that while mortals may not perceive the full extent of this greatness, it is present and influential in their lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, individuals often feel limited by their mortal experiences. However, by recognizing their potential for greatness and the abundance of opportunities around them, they can strive for higher achievements.
    • In community settings, the mantra can be applied to emphasize unity and collective strength. When individuals come together, they can create a powerful force that reflects the greatness of their combined efforts.

  • Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the vastness of existence and the potential for greatness within the mortal realm. It encourages individuals to look beyond their immediate perceptions and recognize the abundance and strength that can be harnessed through unity and awareness of the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can inspire personal development, community collaboration, and a deeper understanding of one's place in the universe.
