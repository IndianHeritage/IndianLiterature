अहन वृतं वृततरं व्यंसं इन्द्रो वज्रेण महता वधेन |स्कन्धांसीव कुलिशेन विवृक्णाहिḥ शयत उपपृक पृथिव्याः ||
ahan vṛtraṁ vṛtrataraṁ vyaṁsam indro vajreṇa mahatā vadhena | skandhāṁsīva kuliśenā vivṛkṇāhiḥ śayata upapṛk pṛthivyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahan: today
    • vṛtraṁ: the obstacle or the demon
    • vṛtrataraṁ: the one who is more powerful than the obstacle
    • vyaṁsam: the one who is to be invoked or called upon
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • vajreṇa: with the thunderbolt
    • mahatā: with great strength
    • vadhena: by killing or overcoming
    • skandhāṁsīva: like the mountain
    • kuliśenā: with the weapon (thunderbolt)
    • vivṛkṇāhiḥ: to open or to reveal
    • śayata: lying down or resting
    • upapṛk: upon the earth
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, to overcome obstacles and challenges.
    • The term "vṛtra" symbolizes any form of obstruction or negativity in life, such as fear, doubt, or external challenges.
    • By calling upon Indra with the strength of his thunderbolt, the mantra emphasizes the need for divine intervention to conquer these obstacles.
    • The imagery of mountains and the earth signifies stability and grounding, suggesting that overcoming challenges leads to a more solid foundation in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might face significant challenges such as competition or project failures. This mantra can be a reminder to seek strength and support, whether through teamwork or divine guidance, to overcome these hurdles.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter emotional or psychological barriers, such as anxiety or depression. The invocation of Indra can symbolize the need to confront these issues with courage and resilience.
    • During times of natural disasters or crises, communities can come together, invoking the spirit of Indra to seek protection and strength to rebuild and recover.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that can be summoned in the face of adversity.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking help, whether from divine sources or through community support, to overcome life's challenges.
    • In essence, it teaches us that with determination and the right support, we can conquer our obstacles and emerge stronger, much like the earth that stands firm against the forces of nature.
