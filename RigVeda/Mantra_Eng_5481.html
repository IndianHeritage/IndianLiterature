इमां वाम मित्रावरुणा सुवृक्तिम इषं न कृत्वे असुरा नवीयः |इनो वाम अन्यः पदवीर अदब्धो जनं च मित्रो यतति ब्रुवाणः ||
imāṁ vām mitrāvaruṇā suvṛktim iṣaṁ na kṛṇve asurā navīyaḥ | ino vām anyaḥ padavīr adabdho janaṁ ca mitro yatati bruvāṇaḥ ||
  • imāṁ - This word translates to "this" or "these," indicating a focus on the present or immediate context.
  • vām - Meaning "your," it signifies a connection or relationship with the addressed entities.
  • mitrāvaruṇā - This compound word refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship, harmony, and cosmic order.
  • su-vṛktim - Translated as "well-spoken" or "good speech," it emphasizes the importance of communication and expression.
  • iṣaṁ - Meaning "desire" or "wish," it reflects the aspirations or intentions of individuals.
  • na kṛṇve - This phrase means "do not create" or "do not make," suggesting a caution against negative actions.
  • asurā - Referring to "demons" or "negative forces," it highlights the presence of challenges or adversities.
  • navīyaḥ - Meaning "new" or "fresh," it indicates the potential for renewal or new beginnings.
  • ino - This word translates to "this" or "these," again emphasizing the immediate context.
  • vām - Reiterating the connection to the addressed entities.
  • anyaḥ - Meaning "other," it suggests the existence of alternatives or different paths.
  • padavīr - Translated as "paths" or "ways," it refers to the various choices one can make in life.
  • adabdho - Meaning "unhindered" or "unobstructed," it signifies the importance of freedom and clarity in one's journey.
  • janaṁ - Referring to "people" or "individuals," it emphasizes the collective aspect of human experience.
  • ca - Meaning "and," it connects different ideas or entities.
  • mitro - Again referring to "Mitra," symbolizing friendship and support.
  • yatati - Meaning "strives" or "endeavors," it reflects the effort individuals put into their pursuits.
  • bruvāṇaḥ - Translated as "speaking" or "declaring," it emphasizes the power of words and communication.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of harmonious relationships, effective communication, and the pursuit of positive intentions while being aware of the challenges that may arise. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as fostering teamwork in a workplace, promoting understanding in personal relationships, or encouraging open dialogue in community discussions. The mantra serves as a reminder to strive for clarity and positivity in our interactions, while also recognizing the potential for renewal and new beginnings in our endeavors.
