अग्नि शुक्रम् शोचिषोऽरु प्रथयसे बृहत् |अभिक्रन्दन् वृषायसे वि वो मदे गर्भं दधासि जामिषु विवक्षसे ||
agne śukreṇa śociṣoru prathayase bṛhat | abhikrandan vṛṣāyase vi vo made garbhaṁ dadhāsi jāmiṣu vivakṣase ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Śukreṇa: Meaning "with brightness" or "with purity," it emphasizes the importance of clarity and righteousness.
  • Śociṣoru: This term can be interpreted as "illuminating" or "radiating light," suggesting the enlightening nature of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Prathayase: This means "you spread" or "you manifest," indicating the act of bringing forth or revealing something significant.
  • Bṛhat: Translated as "great" or "vast," it signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.
  • Abhikrandan: This word can be understood as "calling out" or "invoking," which reflects the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • Vṛṣāyase: Meaning "you who are powerful" or "you who are strong," it acknowledges the strength and might of the divine.
  • Vi: This is a prefix that can mean "in" or "among," indicating a sense of presence or involvement.
  • Vo: This means "your," referring to the divine or the higher powers being addressed.
  • Made: This term translates to "joy" or "delight," highlighting the importance of happiness and fulfillment.
  • Garbhaṁ: Meaning "womb" or "embryo," it symbolizes creation, potential, and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • Dhadhāsi: This means "you hold" or "you support," indicating the sustaining power of the divine.
  • Jāmiṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the beings" or "among the living," emphasizing the connection between the divine and all forms of life.
  • Vivakṣase: Meaning "you express" or "you reveal," it suggests the act of communication and the sharing of knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda invokes Agni, the fire god, as a source of purity, strength, and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in our lives, encouraging us to seek clarity and joy in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection, reminding us to embrace the light of knowledge and the strength of the divine in our daily endeavors. By acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings and the nurturing aspect of creation, we can foster a sense of community and support in our lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
