इन्द्रस् ते सोम सुतस्य पेयाः क्रत्वे दक्षाय विश्वे च देवाः ||
indras te soma sutasya peyāḥ kratve dakṣāya viśve ca devāḥ ||
  • Indras: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Te: Means "your," indicating possession or connection.
  • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Sutasya: Refers to the "son" or "offspring," often associated with the offerings made to the gods.
  • Peyāḥ: Means "drink" or "beverage," indicating the act of consuming the soma.
  • Krutve: Refers to "action" or "deed," emphasizing the importance of righteous actions.
  • Dakṣāya: Means "to the skilled" or "to the capable," highlighting the value of competence and expertise.
  • Viśve: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the collective nature of the divine.
  • Ca: Means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • Devāḥ: Refers to "gods," representing the divine forces that govern the universe.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra and the gods, asking for their blessings through the sacred soma drink. It emphasizes the importance of righteous actions and the need for skill and capability in achieving one's goals. The mantra reflects a deep connection between the divine and human efforts, suggesting that success is not solely based on individual merit but also on divine grace.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom.
  • Skill Development: The emphasis on "dakṣāya" encourages people to hone their skills and strive for excellence in their respective fields.
  • Community and Inclusivity: The reference to "viśve ca devāḥ" reminds us of the importance of community and working together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In spiritual practices, this mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings and divine support for one's endeavors.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, combining the divine with human effort. It teaches us that while we must strive for skill and righteousness, we should also seek the blessings of the divine to achieve our goals. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community, skill, and divine grace in our lives.
