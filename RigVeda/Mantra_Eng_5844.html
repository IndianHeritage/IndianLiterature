मो षु वरुण मृन्मयं गृहम् राजन् अहं गमम् |मृळा सुखात्र मृळय ||
mo ṣu varuṇa mṛnmayaṁ gṛhaṁ rājann ahaṁ gamam | mṛḻā sukṣatra mṛḻaya ||
  • mo ṣu varuṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth. Varuna represents the moral and natural order of the universe.
  • mṛnmayaṁ gṛhaṁ: This translates to "the house made of clay." In a metaphorical sense, it signifies the physical body or the material world, which is transient and impermanent.
  • rājann ahaṁ gamam: This means "O King, I am going." Here, the speaker acknowledges Varuna as a sovereign figure and expresses a desire to embark on a journey or a quest.
  • mṛḻā sukṣatra: This can be understood as "may I be blessed with good fortune." It reflects a wish for prosperity and well-being in one's endeavors.
  • mṛḻaya: This word can be interpreted as "to be blessed" or "to be fulfilled." It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving success.

  • Real-Life Example: In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and blessings from higher powers or deities before starting new ventures, such as a business or a journey. This mantra encapsulates that sentiment, where one seeks the favor of a higher authority (like Varuna) for success and protection.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the relationship between the material world (the clay house) and the divine order (Varuna). It serves as a reminder that while we navigate through life, we should remain aware of the moral and ethical dimensions that govern our actions.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in various life scenarios, such as during important life transitions (like marriage, starting a new job, or moving to a new place) where one seeks blessings for a smooth journey and positive outcomes.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation for guidance and blessings from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions with the cosmic order and seeking prosperity through ethical living. In a world filled with uncertainties, such invocations can provide comfort and a sense of direction.
