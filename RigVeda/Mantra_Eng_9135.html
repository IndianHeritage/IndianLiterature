यद īशीयामृतानाम् उता वा मार्त्यानाम् | जीवेद इन मगवाऽ मम ||
yad īśīyāmṛtānām uta vā martyānām | jīved in maghavā mama ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • īśīyām: is desired or wished for
    • ṛtānām: of the immortal ones
    • uta: or
    • vā: or
    • martyānām: of the mortals
    • jīved: may live
    • in: in
    • maghavā: by the powerful one (often referring to the divine or a deity)
    • mama: my
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for life, whether it be for the immortal or the mortal beings.
    • It reflects a yearning for vitality and existence, invoking the blessings of a powerful force or deity.
    • The phrase "jīved in maghavā mama" suggests a personal connection to this divine power, indicating that the speaker seeks to live under its influence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone might recite this mantra during a challenging time, seeking strength and longevity in their endeavors.
    • In a communal setting, it could be chanted during rituals or ceremonies that celebrate life, health, and prosperity, such as weddings or festivals.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of goals, where individuals wish for success and longevity in their efforts, whether in careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for life and vitality, transcending the boundaries of mortality.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings, both mortal and immortal, and the divine forces that influence our existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength and resilience in their lives, encouraging them to embrace both the challenges and blessings that come their way.
