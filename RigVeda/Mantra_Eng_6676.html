प्रति प्राशव्यां इतः सम्यञ्चा बरहिर आशाते |न ता वाजेषु वायतः ||
prati prāśavyām̐ itaḥ samyañcā barhir āśāte | na tā vājeṣu vāyataḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prati: towards, in response to
    • prāśavyām̐: the act of offering or giving
    • itaḥ: from here, from this place
    • samyañcā: in harmony, in balance
    • barhir: the sacred grass, often used in rituals
    • āśāte: to be desired, to be wished for
    • na: not
    • tā: those
    • vājeṣu: in the context of strength or power
    • vāyataḥ: to be blown or carried away
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of offering and the importance of harmony in our actions.
    • It emphasizes that offerings made with intention and balance are more valuable than those that are not.
    • The reference to "barhir" suggests the use of sacred elements in rituals, highlighting the significance of purity and intention.
    • The phrase "na tā vājeṣu vāyataḥ" indicates that offerings devoid of true intention or strength are not effective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when a team presents a project, the intention behind their work and the harmony within the team can determine the project's success.
    • In personal relationships, the sincerity of one's actions—like giving gifts or support—matters more than the material value of those actions.
    • In spiritual practices, the rituals performed with genuine devotion and mindfulness yield deeper connections and experiences than those done mechanically.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us the significance of intention and harmony in our actions.
    • It reminds us that true value lies not in the act itself but in the spirit with which it is performed.
    • In everyday life, whether in work, relationships, or spiritual practices, approaching tasks with sincerity and balance can lead to more meaningful outcomes.
