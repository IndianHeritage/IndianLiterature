अव सिंधुं वरुणो द्यौर इव स्थाद द्रप्सो न श्वेतो मृगस् तुविष्मान् |गम्भीरशंसो रजसो विमानः सुपारक्षित्रः सतो अस्य राजा ||
ava sindhuṁ varuṇo dyaur iva sthād drapso na śveto mṛgas tuviṣmān | gambhīraśaṁso rajaso vimānaḥ supārakṣatraḥ sato asya rājā ||
  • ava sindhuṁ: This phrase translates to "the river" or "the ocean." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the vastness and depth of knowledge and resources available in nature.
  • varuṇo: Referring to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth. Varuna represents the moral and natural laws that govern the universe.
  • dyaur iva sthād: This means "like the sky stands firm." It signifies stability and permanence, suggesting that just as the sky is unchanging, so too should our principles and values be steadfast.
  • drapso na śveto: This can be interpreted as "the white deer does not flee." The white deer symbolizes purity and innocence, indicating that one should remain calm and composed in the face of challenges.
  • mṛgas tuviṣmān: This translates to "the animals are in awe." It suggests that all beings, including animals, recognize and respect the divine order and the power of nature.
  • gambhīraśaṁso: Meaning "with deep praise," this phrase emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and honoring the greatness of the divine and the natural world.
  • rajaso vimānaḥ: This refers to "the vehicle of passion." It symbolizes the aspirations and desires that drive human actions, reminding us to channel our passions wisely.
  • supārakṣatraḥ: This means "well-protected." It signifies the importance of safeguarding our values, relationships, and environment, ensuring they thrive in a nurturing space.
  • sato asya rājā: This translates to "the king of truth." It emphasizes the supremacy of truth and righteousness in governance and leadership, suggesting that true authority comes from adhering to moral principles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the relationship between nature, divinity, and human conduct. It teaches us to respect the natural order, remain steadfast in our values, and channel our passions towards constructive ends. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, environmental conservation, and personal development. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society that honors truth and nurtures the world around them.
