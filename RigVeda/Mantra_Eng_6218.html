अग्निः हि ज्ञानी पूर्व्यश् छन्दो न सूरो आर्चिषा |ते भानुभिः वि तस्थिरे ||
agnir hi jāni pūrvyaś chando na sūro arciṣā | te bhānubhir vi tasthire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, often symbolizing energy, purity, and transformation.
    • hi: Indeed, emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • jāni: I know or I recognize.
    • pūrvyaś: From the past or ancient.
    • chando: The meter or rhythm, often referring to the structure of Vedic verses.
    • na: Not.
    • sūro: The sun or a deity associated with light and brilliance.
    • arciṣā: With light or radiance.
    • te: They.
    • bhānubhir: By the rays of the sun.
    • vi: Apart or separately.
    • tasthire: They stood or they established themselves.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the recognition of fire as a fundamental element that has existed since ancient times.
    • It emphasizes the connection between fire and the sun, suggesting that both are sources of light and energy.
    • The phrase "they stood by the rays of the sun" indicates a relationship between these elements, highlighting their importance in the cosmic order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying force. For instance, in Hindu rituals, fire (Agni) is central to ceremonies, symbolizing the divine and the transformation of offerings into spiritual energy.
    • The sun is often associated with life and growth. For example, in agriculture, sunlight is essential for photosynthesis, which is crucial for plant growth and food production.
    • In modern science, the principles of energy transformation can be seen in renewable energy sources, such as solar power, which harnesses sunlight to generate electricity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the eternal nature of fire and light, representing knowledge, transformation, and the interconnectedness of natural elements.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where we recognize the importance of energy sources, both natural and spiritual, in our lives.
    • Understanding this connection can inspire us to respect and harness these elements wisely, promoting sustainability and spiritual growth.
