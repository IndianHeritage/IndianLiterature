उताहं नक्तम् उता सोम ते दिवा सख्याय बभ्र ūधनि |घृणा तपन्तम् अति सूर्यं परः शकुनाऽ इव पप्तिमा ||
utāhaṁ naktam uta soma te divā sakhyāya babhra ūdhani | ghṛṇā tapantam ati sūryam paraḥ śakunā iva paptima ||
  • utāhaṁ naktam: This phrase can be translated as "I have come to the night." It signifies the transition from day to night, representing the cyclical nature of time and the balance between light and darkness.
  • uta soma te divā: This means "and the moon is yours by day." Here, the moon symbolizes calmness and tranquility, suggesting that even during the day, one can find peace and serenity.
  • sakhyāya babhra ūdhani: This translates to "for friendship, I have come." It emphasizes the importance of companionship and social bonds, highlighting that relationships are vital for a fulfilling life.
  • ghṛṇā tapantam: This phrase means "the one who is compassionate." Compassion is a key virtue in human interactions, reminding us to be empathetic towards others.
  • ati sūryam: This translates to "beyond the sun." It suggests striving for higher ideals and aspirations, pushing beyond the ordinary to achieve greatness.
  • paraḥ śakunā iva paptima: This means "like a bird that soars high." It symbolizes freedom and the pursuit of one's dreams, encouraging individuals to rise above challenges and limitations.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, friendship, compassion, and the pursuit of higher ideals. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embrace both light and darkness, to nurture relationships, and to act with empathy. It encourages individuals to aspire for greatness while remaining grounded in their connections with others. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to daily life, where one can strive for personal growth while fostering meaningful relationships and compassion in their community.
