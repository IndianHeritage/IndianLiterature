अस्य वामस्य पलितस्य होतुस तस्य भ्राता मध्यमो अस्त्य अशनः | तृतीयो भ्राता घृतपृष्ठो अस्यात्रापश्यं विश्वतिं सप्तपुत्रम् ||
asya vāmasya palitasya hotus tasya bhrātā madhyamo asty aśnaḥ | tṛtīyo bhrātā ghṛtapṛṣṭho asyātrāpaśyaṁ viśpatiṁ saptaputram ||
  • Mantra: "asya vāmasya palitasya hotus tasya bhrātā madhyamo asty aśnaḥ | tṛtīyo bhrātā ghṛtapṛṣṭho asyātrāpaśyaṁ viśpatiṁ saptaputram ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • vāmasya: of the left (or auspicious)
    • palitasya: of the one who is gray-haired (or aged)
    • hotus: of the priest (or one who offers)
    • tasya: of him
    • bhrātā: brother
    • madhyamo: the middle one
    • asty: exists
    • aśnaḥ: eater (or one who consumes)
    • tṛtīyo: the third
    • bhrātā: brother
    • ghṛtapṛṣṭho: one who is buttered (or nourished)
    • asyātrā: of this place
    • paśyaṁ: see
    • viśpatiṁ: lord of the universe
    • saptaputram: seven sons
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationships and roles within a family or community, particularly focusing on the dynamics between brothers.
    • The mention of "vāmasya" and "palitasya" suggests a reverence for the elder or wise members of the community, indicating that wisdom and experience are valued.
    • The "madhyamo" (middle brother) signifies balance and harmony, suggesting that in any group, the middle ground is essential for unity.
    • The "tṛtīyo bhrātā" (third brother) who is "ghṛtapṛṣṭho" implies nourishment and support, highlighting the importance of caring for one another.
    • The phrase "viśpatiṁ saptaputram" can be interpreted as a call to recognize the divine or universal principles that govern relationships, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the elder siblings often take on the role of mentors, guiding the younger ones, much like the "palitasya" in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, the middle management often acts as a bridge between the upper management and the employees, ensuring that communication flows smoothly, akin to the "madhyamo" role.
    • In community service, individuals who take on the role of caretakers or volunteers embody the spirit of "ghṛtapṛṣṭho," providing support and nourishment to those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of familial and communal bonds, highlighting the roles of wisdom, balance, and support.
    • It teaches us that every member of a community has a role to play, and that nurturing these relationships is essential for harmony and growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger family ties, better teamwork in professional settings, and a more compassionate society.
