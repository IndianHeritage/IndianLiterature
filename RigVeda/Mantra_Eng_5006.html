यस्त्वा देवी सरस्वती उपब्रूते धन हिते |इन्द्रं न वृततूर्ये ||
yas tvā devi sarasvaty upabrūte dhane hite | indraṁ na vṛtratūrye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • devi: goddess
    • sarasvaty: Saraswati (the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning)
    • upabrūte: speaks or addresses
    • dhane: wealth or prosperity
    • hite: for the good or benefit
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms)
    • na: not
    • vṛtratūrye: in the context of overcoming obstacles or enemies
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the goddess Saraswati, asking her to bless the speaker with wealth and prosperity for the greater good, while also seeking protection from adversities, symbolized by Indra's strength against obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Education and Knowledge: Just as students seek the blessings of Saraswati before exams, this mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success and prosperity in life.
    • Business and Wealth Creation: Entrepreneurs often invoke Saraswati for guidance in their ventures, highlighting the need for wisdom in financial decisions and the pursuit of wealth that benefits society.
    • Overcoming Challenges: In times of difficulty, individuals may reflect on this mantra to find strength and resilience, akin to how Indra is depicted as a protector against adversities.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnection between knowledge, prosperity, and overcoming challenges. It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom and the ability to navigate life's obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational pursuits, business endeavors, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and act for the greater good while being resilient in the face of challenges.
