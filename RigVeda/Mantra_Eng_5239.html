प्राग्नये विश्वशुचेद्धियंढे 'सुरघ्ने मन्म धीतिम् भरध्वम् |भरे हविः न बरहिषि प्रीणानो वैश्वानराय यतये मतीनाम् ||
prāgnaye viśvaśuce dhiyaṁdhe 'suraghne manma dhītim bharadhvam | bhare havir na barhiṣi prīṇāno vaiśvānarāya yataye matīnām ||
  • prāgnaye: This term refers to the state of being wise or possessing knowledge. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of education and understanding, where individuals seek to gain wisdom through learning and experience.
  • viśvaśuce: This means "of universal purity." It signifies the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. For example, in a community, promoting cleanliness and ethical behavior can lead to a harmonious environment.
  • dhiyaṁdhe: This word translates to "may we be endowed with intelligence." It emphasizes the need for mental clarity and insight. In practical terms, this can be seen in decision-making processes where individuals or groups analyze situations critically before arriving at conclusions.
  • 'suraghne: This term can be interpreted as "to the slayer of the enemies." It symbolizes overcoming obstacles and challenges. In everyday life, this could relate to personal struggles, such as overcoming fears or adversities in one's career or personal relationships.
  • manma: This means "my mind" or "my thoughts." It highlights the significance of focusing one's thoughts towards positive and constructive outcomes. For instance, practicing mindfulness can help individuals channel their thoughts towards achieving their goals.
  • dhītim: This refers to "intellect" or "understanding." It underscores the value of wisdom in guiding actions. In a corporate setting, for example, leaders often rely on their intellect to navigate complex business challenges.
  • bharadhvam: This translates to "let us bring forth" or "let us carry." It suggests a collective effort towards a common goal. In community service, for instance, people come together to support a cause, demonstrating the power of collaboration.
  • bhare: This means "to fill" or "to provide." It can be related to the act of nurturing and supporting others, such as parents providing for their children or mentors guiding their mentees.
  • haviḥ: This term refers to offerings or sacrifices. In a modern context, it can symbolize the contributions individuals make to society, such as volunteering time or resources for the greater good.
  • na barhiṣi: This means "not in the sacred grass." It suggests a caution against misusing sacred or important resources. In real life, this can be seen in the responsible use of natural resources and the environment.
  • prīṇāno: This translates to "pleasing" or "satisfying." It emphasizes the importance of creating joy and satisfaction in life. For example, acts of kindness can bring happiness to both the giver and the receiver.
  • vaiśvānarāya: This term refers to the universal spirit or the divine essence that connects all beings. It signifies the interconnectedness of life. In practice, this can be observed in the way communities come together during crises, showcasing solidarity and support.
  • yataye: This means "those who strive" or "those who seek." It highlights the importance of effort and determination in achieving goals. For instance, athletes train rigorously to achieve excellence in their sport.
  • matīnām: This translates to "of the minds." It signifies the collective consciousness and shared thoughts of a community. In a workplace, fostering a culture of open communication can enhance collaboration and innovation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, purity, collective effort, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder to strive for knowledge, maintain ethical standards, and work together towards common goals. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, community service, and personal development, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
