मन्त्र: न हि ते शूर राधसोऽन्तं विन्दामि सत्रा | दशस्याऽनो मघवन नू चिद अद्रिवो धियो वाजेभिर आविथ ||
nahi te śūra rādhaso 'ntaṁ vindāmi satrā | daśasyā no maghavan nū cid adrivo dhiyo vājebhir āvitha ||
  • Translation: "I do not find the end of the brave Radha; O Maghavan, may we be covered by the thoughts of the ten (the ten directions) with the strength of the steeds."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • te: your
    • śūra: brave or valiant
    • rādhaso: of Radha (often associated with divine love and devotion)
    • antaṁ: end or limit
    • vindāmi: I find
    • satrā: sacrifice or offering
    • daśasyā: of the ten (often referring to the ten directions or aspects of existence)
    • no: our
    • maghavan: O Maghavan (a name for Indra, the king of gods)
    • nū: indeed
    • cid: whether
    • adrivo: thoughts or intellect
    • dhiyo: minds or intellects
    • vājebhir: with strength or power
    • āvitha: may we be covered or protected

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the quest for understanding and the pursuit of bravery and strength in life.
    • Radha symbolizes divine love and devotion, suggesting that the journey towards understanding and bravery is endless and profound.
    • Maghavan, representing Indra, signifies the need for divine support and guidance in our endeavors.
    • The mention of the ten directions emphasizes the holistic approach to life, encompassing all aspects of existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might face challenges that require bravery and innovative thinking. The mantra encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and support while navigating these challenges.
    • In personal relationships, the endless pursuit of love and understanding, akin to Radha's devotion, can lead to deeper connections and emotional growth.
    • In community service, the idea of being covered by the thoughts of the ten can inspire individuals to consider the needs of all directions—social, economic, and environmental—when making decisions that affect the community.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of bravery, divine guidance, and holistic thinking in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to pursue our goals with courage while remaining open to the support of the divine and the interconnectedness of all aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal growth, professional challenges, and community engagement, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
