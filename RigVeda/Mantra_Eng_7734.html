बरिः प्राचीनम् ओजसा पवमानः स्तृणन् हरिः |देवेषु देव ईयते ||
barhiḥ prācīnam ojasā pavamānaḥ stṛṇan hariḥ | deveṣu deva īyate ||
  • Barhiḥ: This term refers to the sacred grass used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • Prācīnam: Meaning "ancient" or "old," this word emphasizes the timeless wisdom and traditions that have been passed down through generations.
  • Ojasā: This word signifies "energy" or "vitality," representing the life force that sustains all beings.
  • Pavamānaḥ: Translated as "the purifying one," this term refers to the wind or breath, which is essential for life and symbolizes the cleansing of the mind and spirit.
  • Stṛṇan: This means "to spread" or "to cover," indicating the act of enveloping or protecting.
  • Hariḥ: A name often associated with the divine, particularly Vishnu, representing the protector and sustainer of the universe.
  • Deveṣu: This term means "among the gods," indicating the divine realm and the presence of higher powers.
  • Deva: Referring to "god" or "deity," it signifies the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • Īyate: This means "is invoked" or "is worshipped," indicating the act of calling upon the divine for blessings and guidance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the ancient energies and divine forces to purify and protect. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with the sacred traditions and invoking the divine presence in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or rituals to seek purification and divine guidance.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "pavamānaḥ" (the purifying wind) can inspire people to respect and protect nature, recognizing the vital energy it provides.
  • Cultural Heritage: The emphasis on ancient wisdom encourages the preservation of cultural traditions and practices that promote harmony and balance in society.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of purity, and the need to invoke divine energies for guidance and protection. It highlights the significance of ancient wisdom in contemporary life, encouraging individuals to seek spiritual growth and environmental consciousness.
