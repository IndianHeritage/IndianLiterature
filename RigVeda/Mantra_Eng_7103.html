आ नो अग्नि वयोवृद्धं रयिम पावक शंस्यम् |रास्वा च न उपमाते पुरुष्पृहं सुनीती स्वयशस्तरम् ||
ā no agne vayovṛdhaṁ rayim pāvaka śaṁsyam | rāsvā ca na upamāte puruspṛhaṁ sunītī svayaśastaram ||
  • ā no agne: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, may you lead us." Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a messenger between humans and the gods. In real life, this can symbolize the guidance we seek in our lives, whether through mentors, education, or spiritual practices.
  • vayovṛdhaṁ: This term means "increasing our vitality." It emphasizes the importance of health and well-being. In practical terms, this can relate to maintaining a balanced lifestyle through proper nutrition, exercise, and mental health practices.
  • rayim: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also the richness of experiences and relationships. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of fulfilling careers, nurturing relationships, and contributing to community well-being.
  • pāvaka: Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this term highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In real life, this can be applied by striving for honesty, integrity, and ethical behavior in personal and professional interactions.
  • śaṁsyam: This translates to "may we be praised." It reflects the human desire for recognition and appreciation. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of acknowledging others' contributions and fostering a culture of gratitude in our communities.
  • rāsvā ca: This phrase means "and may we be nourished." It emphasizes the need for sustenance, both physical and emotional. In real life, this can relate to the importance of community support, healthy relationships, and self-care practices.
  • na upamāte: This translates to "without comparison." It suggests the idea of individuality and the unique path each person must take. In practical terms, this can be applied by encouraging self-acceptance and celebrating diversity in talents and perspectives.
  • puruspṛhaṁ: This term means "the desire for greatness." It reflects the human aspiration to achieve and excel. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal goals, professional ambitions, and the drive to make a positive impact in the world.
  • sunītī: This translates to "good guidance." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and mentorship. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking advice from experienced individuals and learning from their journeys.
  • svayaśastaram: This means "may we attain glory." It reflects the aspiration for success and recognition in our endeavors. In real life, this can be applied by setting high standards for ourselves and striving to achieve our dreams while contributing positively to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of guidance, health, prosperity, purity, recognition, nourishment, individuality, aspiration, wisdom, and glory. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of our lives, encouraging us to seek balance and fulfillment. By embodying these principles, we can create a meaningful existence that not only benefits ourselves but also positively impacts those around us.
