असावी देवं गोर्जीकमन्दो न्य अस्मिन्न इन्द्रो जनुषेम उवोच | बोधामसि त्वा हर्यश्व यज्ञैर बोधा नः स्तोमं अन्धसो मदेषु ||
asāvi devaṁ goṛjīkam andho ny asminn indro januṣem uvoca | bodhāmasi tvā haryaśva yajñair bodhā naḥ stomam andhaso madeṣu ||
  • asāvi devaṁ goṛjīkam: This phrase can be translated as "This divine being, the one who is powerful." The term "asāvi" refers to something that is present or existing, while "devaṁ" means divine or godly. "Goṛjīkam" suggests a sense of strength or might.
  • andho ny asminn indro januṣem uvoca: This part translates to "In this darkness, Indra, the lord, has spoken." Here, "andho" means darkness, "ny" implies 'in this', and "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition. "Januṣem" means to speak or declare, indicating that even in challenging times, divine guidance is available.
  • bodhāmasi tvā haryaśva: This translates to "We awaken you, O swift one." "Bodhāmasi" means 'we awaken' or 'we realize', while "tvā" refers to 'you', and "haryaśva" can be interpreted as 'the swift one', often associated with the speed of thought or action.
  • yajñair bodhā naḥ stomam andhaso madeṣu: This means "Through our sacrifices, we awaken the hymn in the darkness." "Yajñair" refers to sacrifices or offerings, "bodhā" means to awaken, "naḥ" means 'our', "stomam" refers to hymns or praises, and "andhaso madeṣu" indicates the darkness of ignorance or confusion.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the power of awakening knowledge through sacrifice and devotion. In real life, it signifies that even in times of confusion or darkness, one can seek enlightenment through spiritual practices and community efforts. The invocation of Indra symbolizes the strength and support available to individuals who strive for wisdom and clarity. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or spiritual rituals, where the collective effort to seek knowledge and understanding is paramount.
