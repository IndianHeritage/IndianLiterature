आ यस ते अग्न इधते अनीकं वसिष्ठ शुक्र दीदिवः पावक |उतो न एभिः स्तवथैर इह स्याः ||
ā yas te agna idhate anīkaṁ vasiṣṭha śukra dīdivaḥ pāvaka | uto na ebhiḥ stavathair iha syāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • yas - who
    • te - your
    • agna - O Fire
    • idhate - is established
    • anīkaṁ - in a multitude
    • vasiṣṭha - the best
    • śukra - pure
    • dīdivaḥ - shining
    • pāvaka - O Purifier
    • uto - and
    • na - not
    • ebhiḥ - with these
    • stavathair - praises
    • iha - here
    • syāḥ - may be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the Vedic fire god, who is revered as a divine force that purifies and sustains life.
    • The phrase "O Fire, who is established in a multitude" suggests that Agni is present in various forms and manifestations, symbolizing the omnipresence of fire in nature and life.
    • “The best, pure, and shining” refers to Agni's qualities, emphasizing the importance of purity and brightness in spiritual and physical realms.
    • The latter part of the mantra, “not with these praises may be here,” indicates a humble approach to worship, suggesting that mere words are insufficient to capture the essence of the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and energy. It symbolizes transformation and renewal, much like how challenges in life can lead to personal growth.
    • In many cultures, fire rituals are performed to purify spaces and invoke blessings, reflecting the significance of Agni in spiritual practices.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the qualities of Agni can be seen in individuals who embody purity, strength, and the ability to inspire others, much like a leader or a mentor.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a purifying force and a source of life, reminding us of the importance of purity in our thoughts and actions.
    • It teaches us to approach the divine with humility, recognizing that true connection goes beyond mere words and requires sincere intention and action.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire us to seek purity in our endeavors, whether in personal relationships, professional pursuits, or spiritual practices, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
