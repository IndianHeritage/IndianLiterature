यस् ते मदः पृतनाशाल अमृध्र इन्द्र तं न आ भर शूशुवांसम् |येना तोकस्य तनयस्य सातौ मंसिमहि जिगीवांसस तवोताः ||
yas te madaḥ pṛtanāṣāḻ amṛdhra indra taṁ na ā bhara śūśuvāṁsam | yena tokasya tanayasya sātau maṁsīmahi jigīvāṁsas tvotāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • madaḥ: intoxication or delight
    • pṛtanāṣāḻ: of the one who is powerful
    • amṛdhra: immortal or divine
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • taṁ: that
    • na: not
    • ā: to
    • bhara: bring or carry
    • śūśuvāṁsam: the one who is full of strength
    • yena: by whom
    • tokasya: of the offspring
    • tanayasya: of the child
    • sātau: in the presence
    • maṁsīmahi: we shall be strong
    • jigīvāṁsas: of the living beings
    • tvotāḥ: we praise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine strength and power of Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the importance of divine support in achieving strength and vitality.
    • The phrase "who is your intoxication" suggests that the divine presence brings joy and fulfillment, much like how a person feels uplifted in the company of loved ones or during moments of success.
    • The reference to "offspring" and "strength" highlights the significance of family and community support in nurturing and empowering individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the support and love from parents can be seen as a source of strength for children, enabling them to face challenges with confidence.
    • In a workplace, a supportive team can uplift an individual, much like the divine intoxication mentioned in the mantra, leading to greater productivity and job satisfaction.
    • During community gatherings or celebrations, the collective joy and strength derived from shared experiences can be likened to the divine presence invoked in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support and the strength derived from community and family. It teaches us that true power comes not just from individual effort but also from the connections we nurture with others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking support during difficult times, celebrating successes with loved ones, and fostering a sense of community in our daily lives.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us to appreciate the divine and communal aspects of our existence, encouraging us to cultivate relationships that empower and uplift us.
