न यस्य वार्ता जनुषा nv अस्ति न राधस आमरिता मगस्य |उद्वावृषाणस तविषीव उग्रास्मभ्यं दद्धि पुरुहूत रायः ||
na yasya vartā januṣā nv asti na rādhasa āmarītā maghasya | udvāvṛṣāṇas taviṣīva ugrāsmabhyaṁ daddhi puruhūta rāyaḥ ||
  • na yasya vartā januṣā nv asti: This phrase translates to "He who has no one to support him." It emphasizes the importance of community and support in one's life. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals rely on family, friends, or colleagues for emotional and practical support during challenging times.
  • na rādhasa āmarītā maghasya: This part means "He who does not have the strength of the mighty." It suggests that strength and resilience are crucial for overcoming obstacles. For example, in a workplace, a team that lacks strong leadership may struggle to achieve its goals, highlighting the need for capable individuals to guide and support others.
  • udvāvṛṣāṇas taviṣīva ugrā: This translates to "Like the powerful, may we be strong." It calls for the cultivation of strength and determination. In everyday life, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals strive to build their skills and confidence to face challenges head-on, much like athletes training for a competition.
  • asmabhyaṁ daddhi puruhūta rāyaḥ: This means "May the great one grant us strength." It reflects a plea for divine support and guidance. In real life, people often seek inspiration from higher powers or mentors when facing difficult decisions or transitions, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of community support, personal strength, and divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges. It teaches us that no one is truly alone and that we can draw strength from our relationships and higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek help when needed, cultivate their inner strength, and remain resilient in the face of adversity. By embodying these principles, we can navigate our lives more effectively and support others in their journeys as well.
