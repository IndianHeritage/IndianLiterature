उत तवाबधिरं वयं श्रुतकर्णं संतम ऊतये |दूराद इह हवामहे ||
uta tvābadhiraṁ vayaṁ śrutkarṇaṁ santam ūtaye | dūrād iha havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • tvā: you
    • abadhiraṁ: unshaken, steadfast
    • vayaṁ: we
    • śrutkarṇaṁ: with attentive ears
    • santam: peaceful, calm
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of invocation
    • dūrād: from afar
    • iha: here, in this place
    • havāmahe: we invoke, we call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a call to a divine presence, emphasizing the importance of being steadfast and attentive.
    • The phrase "we invoke from afar" suggests that even when the divine is distant, it can still be reached through sincere invocation.
    • The use of "peaceful" indicates a desire for tranquility and harmony in the process of invocation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a stressful situation, such as preparing for an important exam or presentation, one might recite this mantra to invoke calmness and focus.
    • During a community gathering or prayer meeting, participants may use this mantra to collectively call upon a higher power for guidance and support.
    • In personal meditation practices, individuals can reflect on the steadfastness and attentiveness that this mantra encourages, helping them to center their thoughts and emotions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to foster a connection with the divine, encouraging individuals to remain steadfast and attentive in their spiritual practices.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during moments of stress, communal prayers, or personal meditation, where invoking peace and focus is essential.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and the importance of seeking tranquility in our lives.
