इन्द्र क्रतुं न आ भर पितā पुत्रेभ्यो यथा |शिक्षा णो अस्मिन पुरुहूत यामिनि जीवाः ज्योतिर्म् अशीमहि ||
indra kratuṁ na ā bhara pitā putrebhyo yathā | śikṣā ṇo asmin puruhūta yāmani jīvā jyotir aśīmahi ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Kratuṁ: Means 'intellect' or 'wisdom', indicating the importance of mental strength and decision-making.
  • Na ā bhara: Translates to 'do not burden' or 'do not overwhelm', suggesting a plea for balance and moderation.
  • Pitā: Means 'father', representing guidance, protection, and nurturing.
  • Putrebhyo: Refers to 'sons' or 'children', indicating the next generation and the responsibilities towards them.
  • Yathā: Means 'as' or 'like', establishing a comparison or a standard.
  • Śikṣā: Means 'instruction' or 'education', emphasizing the importance of learning and knowledge.
  • Ṇo: Means 'our', indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Asmin: Means 'in this', referring to the current context or situation.
  • Puruhūta: Refers to 'one who is invoked', often used to describe a deity or a revered figure.
  • Yāmani: Means 'may we attain' or 'may we achieve', expressing a desire for success or fulfillment.
  • Jīvā: Means 'life' or 'living beings', highlighting the essence of existence.
  • Jyotir: Means 'light' or 'radiance', symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and positivity.
  • Aśīmahi: Means 'we aspire' or 'we seek', indicating a collective ambition or goal.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for wisdom and guidance from Indra, urging the father to not burden his children, but instead to provide them with the necessary education and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing the next generation with knowledge and wisdom, ensuring they are not overwhelmed by life's challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Parenting: Parents can take inspiration from this mantra to provide a balanced upbringing for their children, ensuring they are guided without being overwhelmed by expectations.
  • Education: Educators can reflect on the importance of teaching methods that empower students without causing stress, fostering a love for learning.
  • Leadership: Leaders in any field can learn from this mantra to lead with wisdom and compassion, ensuring their teams are supported and not overburdened.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, balance, and nurturing in relationships, especially between parents and children. It serves as a reminder that guidance should be provided in a way that empowers the next generation, fostering a culture of learning and enlightenment. In a world filled with challenges, this mantra encourages us to seek knowledge and light, ensuring a brighter future for all.
