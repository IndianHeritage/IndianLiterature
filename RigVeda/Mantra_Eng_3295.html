तस्येद इह स्तवथ वृष्ण्याणि तुविद्युम्नस्य तुविराधसो नृण् |यस्य क्रतुर विदथ्यो न सम्राट् साह्वान तरुत्रो अभ्य अस्ति कृष्टीः ||
tasyed iha stavatha vṛṣṇyāni tuvidyumnasya tuvirādhaso nṝn | yasya kratur vidathyo3 na samrāṭ sāhvān tarutro abhy asti kṛṣṭīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasyed: of that
    • iha: here
    • stavatha: praise
    • vṛṣṇyāni: of the Vṛṣṇis (a clan or tribe)
    • tuvidyumnasya: of Tuvidyumna (a name, possibly a leader or a deity)
    • tuvirādhaso: of the powerful
    • nṝn: of men
    • yasya: whose
    • kratur: intention or will
    • vidathyo: is well-formed or established
    • na: not
    • samrāṭ: the emperor or ruler
    • sāhvān: of the brave
    • tarutro: of the trees or plants
    • abhy: towards
    • asti: is
    • kṛṣṭīḥ: cultivation or harvest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of praise and recognition of the Vṛṣṇi clan and their leader, Tuvidyumna.
    • It emphasizes the significance of intention and will in achieving success, suggesting that a well-formed intention is crucial for any endeavor.
    • The phrase "not the emperor" indicates that true power and success do not solely rely on authority or position but rather on the strength of character and purpose.
    • The reference to "trees" symbolizes growth, stability, and the nurturing aspect of life, which is essential for cultivation and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who fosters a strong vision and encourages teamwork can achieve greater success than one who relies solely on their title.
    • In personal relationships, the intention behind actions—such as kindness and support—can lead to deeper connections than mere social status.
    • In agriculture, understanding the importance of nurturing the land (symbolized by trees) leads to better yields and sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of intention, character, and the collective strength of a community in achieving success.
    • It teaches us that true power lies not in authority but in the ability to inspire and cultivate growth, both in ourselves and in our surroundings.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to more meaningful achievements and a harmonious existence, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
