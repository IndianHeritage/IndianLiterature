यस्तां चकार स कुहा स्विद इन्द्रः कम ā जनं चरति कासु विक्षु |कस ते यज्ञो मनसे शं वराय को अर्क इन्द्र कतमः स होता ||
yas tā cakāra sa kuha svid indraḥ kam ā janaṁ carati kāsu vikṣu | kas te yajño manase śaṁ varāya ko arka indra katamaḥ sa hotā ||

  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses questions about the creation and movement of the divine, specifically Indra, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • It reflects on the nature of sacrifice and the mental focus required to achieve peace and harmony in life.
    • The inquiry into "who" and "where" suggests a search for understanding the divine presence in various aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek to understand the motivations and movements of their teams (akin to Indra's movements) to create a harmonious work environment.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on their actions and decisions, seeking peace and clarity in their relationships and endeavors.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, the focus on collective intention and mental alignment can lead to a more profound sense of unity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the divine's role in our lives and the significance of mental focus in achieving peace and harmony.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal introspection to community rituals, highlighting the universal quest for meaning and connection.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek clarity in their intentions and actions, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with each other.
