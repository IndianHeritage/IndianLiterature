पवमानस्य विश्ववित प्र ते सर्गा असृक्षत |सूर्यस्येव न रश्मयः ||
pavamānasya viśvavit pra te sargā asṛkṣata | sūryasyeva na raśmayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pavamānasya viśvavit pra te sargā asṛkṣata | sūryasyeva na raśmayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pavamānasya: of the purifying one, often associated with the wind or breath.
    • viśvavit: the all-knowing or omniscient.
    • pra: towards or for.
    • te: your or of you.
    • sargā: creations or manifestations.
    • asṛkṣata: protected or preserved.
    • sūryasyeva: like the sun.
    • na: not.
    • raśmayaḥ: rays or beams.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purifying force (pavamānasya) that is all-knowing (viśvavit) and emphasizes the importance of protection (asṛkṣata) of all creations (sargā).
    • The comparison to the sun (sūryasyeva) suggests that just as the sun's rays are essential for life and growth, so too is the purifying force essential for the preservation of existence.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way we seek knowledge and wisdom to protect our environment and society. For example, environmental conservation efforts aim to preserve the natural world, much like the mantra emphasizes the protection of creations.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of knowledge, purity, and protection in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom and understanding are crucial in safeguarding our surroundings and ensuring a sustainable future.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various fields such as education, environmental science, and community service, where the goal is to foster a deeper understanding of our responsibilities towards the world.
