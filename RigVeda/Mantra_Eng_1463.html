मनवे शासद अव्रतान त्वचं कृष्णाम अरन्धयत् |दक्षन् न विश्वं तत्रिषाणम् ओषति न्यार्शसाणम् ओषति ||
manave śāsad avratān tvacaṁ kṛṣṇām arandhayat | dakṣan na viśvaṁ tatṛṣāṇam oṣati ny arśasānam oṣati ||
  • manave: This word refers to "man" or "human." It signifies the essence of humanity and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • śāsad: This translates to "to govern" or "to rule." It implies the need for guidance and leadership in human affairs.
  • avratān: This means "those who are unrestrained" or "without discipline." It highlights the importance of self-control and adherence to moral values.
  • tvacaṁ: This word means "skin" or "surface." It can symbolize the outer appearance or the superficial aspects of life.
  • kṛṣṇām: This translates to "black" or "dark." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent ignorance or the darker aspects of human nature.
  • arandhayat: This means "to cover" or "to obscure." It suggests the idea of hiding the true nature of things or being blinded by ignorance.
  • dakṣan: This word means "skillful" or "capable." It emphasizes the need for competence and wisdom in leadership.
  • na viśvaṁ: This translates to "not the whole" or "not universal." It indicates that not all aspects of life are interconnected or understood.
  • tatṛṣāṇam: This means "desire" or "longing." It reflects the human tendency to seek fulfillment and satisfaction.
  • oṣati: This translates to "to nourish" or "to sustain." It signifies the importance of nurturing and supporting one another.
  • ny: This is a prefix that can mean "not" or "without." It negates the following term.
  • arśasānam: This means "the seat of the wise" or "the abode of knowledge." It represents the pursuit of wisdom and understanding.

The mantra speaks to the duality of human nature, emphasizing the need for governance and discipline in the face of ignorance and desire. It highlights the importance of self-awareness and the pursuit of wisdom in navigating life's complexities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Effective leaders must govern with wisdom and discipline, guiding their followers away from ignorance and towards enlightenment.
  • Personal Development: Individuals are encouraged to cultivate self-control and seek knowledge, recognizing the darker aspects of their nature and striving for improvement.
  • Community Support: The mantra underscores the importance of nurturing relationships and supporting one another in the pursuit of common goals.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with being human. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom, practice self-discipline, and support one another in the journey of life. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
