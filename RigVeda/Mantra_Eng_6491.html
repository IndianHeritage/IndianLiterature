नूतना इद इन्द्र ते वयम् ऊती अभूम न हि नू ते अद्रिवः |विद्मा पुरा परीनसः ||
nūtnā id indra te vayam ūtī abhūma nahi nū te adrivaḥ | vidmā purā parīṇasaḥ ||
  • nūtnā id indra te vayam ūtī abhūma nahi nū te adrivaḥ
    • nūtnā: This word translates to "new" or "fresh." It signifies the idea of renewal or innovation.
    • id: This means "here" or "now," indicating the present moment.
    • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the king of gods.
    • te: This means "your," referring to Indra in this context.
    • vayam: This translates to "we," indicating a collective group or community.
    • ūtī: This word means "strength" or "power," suggesting a call for empowerment.
    • abhūma: This means "we have become" or "we have attained," indicating a transformation or achievement.
    • nahi: This translates to "not," negating the following term.
    • nū: This is an emphatic form of "not," reinforcing the negation.
    • te: Again, this means "your," referring to Indra.
    • adrivaḥ: This means "without obstacles" or "free from hindrances," suggesting a state of clarity and freedom.
  • vidmā purā parīṇasaḥ:
    • vidmā: This translates to "we know," indicating awareness or understanding.
    • purā: This means "before" or "in the past," suggesting a connection to history or tradition.
    • parīṇasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is complete" or "the one who has achieved fullness," referring to a state of fulfillment or wholeness.

Conclusion:

The mantra expresses a collective aspiration for strength and empowerment, invoking the divine presence of Indra. It emphasizes the importance of renewal and overcoming obstacles, suggesting that through unity and understanding, individuals can achieve a state of completeness. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, where individuals seek to overcome challenges and attain their goals.
  • In community building, where collective strength and unity are essential for progress.
  • In leadership, where the qualities of empowerment and renewal are crucial for guiding others.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective effort and the potential for transformation when individuals come together with a shared purpose.
