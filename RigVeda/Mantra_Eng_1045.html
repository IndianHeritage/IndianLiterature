विषां गोपा अस्य चरन्ति जन्तवो द्विपच च यद उता चतुष्पद अक्तुभिः |चित्रः प्रकेत उषसो महांस्य अग्ने सख्ये मा ऋषामा वयं तव ||
viśāṁ gopā asya caranti jantavo dvipac ca yad uta catuṣpad aktubhiḥ | citraḥ praketa uṣaso mahām̐ asy agne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • viśāṁ gopā asya caranti: This phrase translates to "the protectors of the people wander." Here, "viśāṁ" refers to the community or people, and "gopā" signifies protectors or guardians. In real life, this can be likened to leaders or mentors who guide and safeguard their communities, such as community leaders or teachers who nurture the growth of their students.
  • jantavo dvipac ca yad uta catuṣpad: This part means "the beings, both two-footed and four-footed." "Jantavo" refers to living beings, "dvipac" means two-footed (humans), and "catuṣpad" refers to four-footed (animals). This highlights the interconnectedness of all life forms. For example, in a balanced ecosystem, humans, animals, and plants coexist and support each other, emphasizing the importance of biodiversity.
  • aktubhiḥ: This word can be interpreted as "with their actions." It suggests that the actions of these beings are significant. In a practical sense, this can relate to how our daily actions impact the environment and society, such as sustainable practices that protect wildlife and promote ecological balance.
  • citraḥ praketa uṣaso mahām̐: This translates to "the colorful dawns are great." "Citraḥ" means colorful or diverse, "praketa" refers to the dawn or new beginnings, and "mahām̐" signifies greatness. This can be seen in the context of new opportunities that arise each day, encouraging individuals to embrace change and pursue their goals, much like how a new day brings fresh possibilities.
  • asy agne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava: This phrase means "O Agni, may we not be angry with you." "Agni" represents the fire or divine energy, often associated with transformation and purification. This can be related to the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the forces of nature and the divine. In real life, it can be seen in practices like meditation or rituals that seek to align oneself with higher energies and promote inner peace.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of community, the interconnectedness of all living beings, and the importance of our actions. It encourages us to appreciate the beauty of new beginnings and to maintain a respectful relationship with the divine and nature. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, environmental conservation, and personal growth. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
