विष्णुर योनिं कल्पयतु त्वष्टा रूपाणि पिंशतु |आ सिञ्चतु प्रजापतिर धाता गर्भं दधातु ते ||
viṣṇur yoniṁ kalpayatu tvaṣṭā rūpāṇi piṁśatu | ā siñcatu prajāpatir dhātā garbhaṁ dadhātu te ||
  • viṣṇur yoniṁ kalpayatu: This phrase can be translated as "May Vishnu create the womb." Here, Vishnu represents the preserver and sustainer in the Hindu trinity. The term "yoni" refers to the source or womb, symbolizing creation and nurturing.
  • tvaṣṭā rūpāṇi piṁśatu: This translates to "May the divine craftsman shape forms." Tvaṣṭā is the divine artisan, responsible for creating and shaping the universe. This line emphasizes the importance of creativity and the role of divine forces in shaping existence.
  • ā siñcatu prajāpatir: This means "May the lord of creatures pour forth." Prajāpati is the lord of all beings, representing fertility and the continuation of life. This line signifies the nurturing aspect of creation, where life is sustained and nourished.
  • dhātā garbhaṁ dadhātu te: This translates to "May the sustainer give you a womb." Dhātā refers to the sustainer or the one who supports life. The term "garbha" means womb or embryo, indicating the potential for new life and the importance of nurturing it.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nurturing, and the divine forces that govern life. It highlights the interconnectedness of various aspects of existence, from the creative power of Vishnu to the nurturing role of Prajāpati.
  • Real-life Application: In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Parenting: Just as the mantra invokes divine forces to nurture life, parents are encouraged to create a loving and supportive environment for their children, fostering their growth and development.
    • Creativity: Artists and creators can draw inspiration from the idea of divine craftsmanship, understanding that their work is a reflection of a greater creative force.
    • Community Building: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships within communities, where individuals support one another in their personal and collective growth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of creation and the responsibilities that come with it. It encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in their lives and to actively participate in the nurturing and sustaining of life, whether through parenting, creativity, or community engagement.
