विश्वो यस्य व्रते जनो दाधार धर्मणस पतेः |पुनानस्य प्रभूवसोः ||
viśvo yasya vrate jano dādhāra dharmaṇas pateḥ | punānasya prabhūvasoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvo: universal, all-encompassing
    • yasya: whose
    • vrate: vow, commitment, or principle
    • jano: people, community
    • dādhāra: uphold, support
    • dharmaṇas: of righteousness, moral order
    • pateḥ: lord, master
    • punānasya: of the one who purifies
    • prabhūvasoḥ: of the powerful, the one who has authority
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the universal principles that bind a community together through shared vows and commitments.
    • It emphasizes the importance of upholding righteousness and moral order, suggesting that these values are essential for the well-being of society.
    • The reference to the "one who purifies" indicates a divine or higher power that supports and sustains these principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when individuals come together to support a common cause, such as environmental conservation, they embody the essence of "viśvo yasya vrate jano dādhāra." Their collective commitment to protecting nature reflects a shared moral responsibility.
    • In workplaces, teams that adhere to ethical practices and support each other in achieving common goals demonstrate the principles of dharma and community. For instance, a company that prioritizes fair trade and employee welfare aligns with the values expressed in this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, friends and family who uphold trust and loyalty create a supportive environment, reflecting the idea of upholding dharma in their interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and moral integrity within communities. It serves as a reminder that collective efforts towards righteousness can lead to a harmonious society.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, ethical business practices, and personal relationships, where the emphasis is on shared values and mutual support.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize their role in upholding the moral fabric of society, fostering a sense of responsibility towards one another and the greater good.
