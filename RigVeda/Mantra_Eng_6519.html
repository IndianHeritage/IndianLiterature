आ नो अश्वावद अश्विनाऽ वर्तिर यासिष्टं मधुपातमाऽ नराः |गोमद दसराऽ हिरण्यवत् ||
ā no aśvāvad aśvinā vartir yāsiṣṭam madhupātamā narā | gomad dasrā hiraṇyavat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: us
    • aśvāvad: like horses
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • vartir: you may come
    • yāsiṣṭam: the best
    • madhupātamā: the sweetest (honey)
    • narā: O men
    • gomad: possessing cows
    • dasrā: swift
    • hiraṇyavat: like gold
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, asking them to come towards us like swift horses.
    • It emphasizes the desire for the best and sweetest experiences in life, symbolized by honey.
    • The mention of possessing cows and being swift like gold suggests prosperity, wealth, and speed in achieving goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for success and prosperity. Just as the Ashvins are known for their swiftness and efficiency, individuals and organizations can strive for quick and effective results in their endeavors.
    • In personal life, it can be a reminder to seek out the best experiences and relationships, akin to the sweetness of honey, and to surround oneself with positivity and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine assistance in achieving prosperity and well-being.
    • It encourages individuals to aspire for excellence and to be open to the blessings that come from the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a motivational reminder to pursue one's goals with vigor and to appreciate the sweetness of life’s experiences.
