अपघ्नन्तो अरावणः पवमानाः स्वर्दृशः |योना ऋतस्य सीदत ||
apaghnanto arāvṇaḥ pavamānāḥ svardṛśaḥ | yonāv ṛtasya sīdata ||
  • Mantra: "apaghnanto arāvṇaḥ pavamānāḥ svardṛśaḥ | yonāv ṛtasya sīdata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apaghnanto: Those who are free from sin or obstacles.
    • arāvṇaḥ: The ones who are pure or untainted.
    • pavamānāḥ: The ones who are purified or sanctified.
    • svardṛśaḥ: Those who have a divine vision or insight.
    • yonāv: In the womb or source.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or cosmic order.
    • sīdata: To be established or to thrive.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the qualities of purity, sanctity, and divine insight.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being free from sin and obstacles in order to attain a higher state of consciousness.
    • The reference to "yonāv ṛtasya" suggests that these qualities are rooted in the fundamental truths of the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals who maintain integrity and ethical standards often find themselves thriving in their careers, akin to being "apaghnanto."
    • In personal relationships, those who practice honesty and transparency foster deeper connections, reflecting the essence of "arāvṇaḥ."
    • Spiritual practices such as meditation can lead to a clearer mind and enhanced insight, paralleling "pavamānāḥ svardṛśaḥ."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, truth, and divine insight in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for a state of being that is free from negativity and aligned with the cosmic order.
    • In practical terms, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
