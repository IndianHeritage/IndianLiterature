या ओजिष्ट इन्द्र तं सु नो दा मदो वृषन् स्वभिष्टर दास्वान् |सौवश्यं यो वनवत स्वश्वो वृत्राः समत्सु सासहद अमित्रान् ||
ya ojiṣṭha indra taṁ su no dā mado vṛṣan svabhiṣṭir dāsvān | sauvaśvyaṁ yo vanavat svaśvo vṛtrā samatsu sāsahad amitrān ||
  • ya ojiṣṭha indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, the most powerful." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. In real life, this can be likened to seeking the support of a strong leader or a powerful force in times of need.
  • taṁ su no dā: This means "give us that good." It reflects a desire for positive outcomes and blessings. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people pray or hope for good fortune, whether in personal endeavors or communal activities.
  • mado vṛṣan: Translated as "the one who brings joy." This highlights the importance of joy and happiness in life. For example, one might think of a friend or family member who brings joy into their lives, emphasizing the value of relationships that uplift us.
  • svabhiṣṭir dāsvān: This phrase means "may we receive our desires." It speaks to the human experience of longing and aspiration. In real life, this can be related to setting goals and working towards achieving them, whether in career, education, or personal growth.
  • sauvaśvyaṁ yo vanavat: This translates to "who is like a protector." It suggests the idea of guardianship and safety. In practical terms, this can be seen in the role of parents, mentors, or community leaders who protect and guide others.
  • svaśvo vṛtrā: This means "the one who overcomes obstacles." It emphasizes resilience and the ability to face challenges. In real life, this can be illustrated by individuals who overcome personal struggles, such as health issues or financial difficulties, demonstrating the strength of the human spirit.
  • samatsu sāsahad amitrān: This phrase translates to "may we conquer our enemies." It signifies the importance of overcoming adversities and challenges. In a broader context, this can relate to social justice movements where communities come together to fight against oppression and inequality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, joy, protection, and the ability to overcome challenges. Its significance lies in the universal human experience of seeking support and striving for positive outcomes in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to pursue their goals, support one another, and face adversities with courage and resilience. It serves as a reminder of the power of collective strength and the importance of nurturing positive relationships in our lives.
