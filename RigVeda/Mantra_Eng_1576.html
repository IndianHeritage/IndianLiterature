यम ईं द्वा सवयसा सपर्यतः समाने योना मिथुना समोकसा |दिवा न नक्तं पलितो युवाजनि पुरू चरन्न अजरो मानवः युगा ||
yam īṁ dvā savayasā saparyataḥ samāne yonā mithunā samokasā | divā na naktam palito yuvājani purū carann ajaro mānuṣā yugā ||
  • yam - This word translates to "whom" or "that which." It indicates a subject or a focus of attention.
  • īṁ - This is a sound that represents a sacred syllable, often associated with the divine or spiritual energy.
  • dvā - Meaning "two," it signifies duality or pairs, which can be interpreted in various contexts such as opposites or complementary forces.
  • savayasā - This term refers to "the well-formed" or "the complete," suggesting a sense of wholeness or perfection.
  • saparyataḥ - This means "worshipped" or "honored," indicating reverence towards the divine or sacred entities.
  • samāne - Translates to "equal" or "similar," suggesting harmony or balance.
  • yonā - This word means "womb" or "source," often symbolizing creation and nurturing.
  • mithunā - Refers to "couple" or "pair," emphasizing the importance of relationships and unity.
  • samokasā - This term can be interpreted as "with equal eyes," suggesting impartiality and fairness.
  • divā - Meaning "by day," it signifies the light, clarity, and consciousness.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not," indicating absence or denial.
  • naktam - Meaning "by night," it represents darkness, mystery, and the unconscious.
  • palito - This term means "aged" or "worn," suggesting the passage of time and experience.
  • yuvājani - Refers to "youthful" or "energetic," symbolizing vitality and potential.
  • purū - Meaning "many" or "abundant," indicating a multitude or diversity.
  • carann - This means "moving" or "wandering," suggesting a journey or exploration.
  • ajaro - Translates to "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the timeless nature of the spirit.
  • mānuṣā - Refers to "human beings," emphasizing the human experience and existence.
  • yugā - Meaning "ages" or "epochs," indicating the cyclical nature of time and existence.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, the balance between dualities, and the reverence for the divine. It emphasizes the importance of relationships, both in the physical and spiritual realms, and the journey of human existence through time.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Relationships: It highlights the significance of partnerships and unity, reminding us to honor and respect our connections with others.
  • Balance: The concept of duality encourages us to find harmony in our lives, balancing work and personal life, or light and dark aspects of our existence.
  • Spiritual Growth: The reference to the divine and the eternal encourages individuals to seek spiritual enlightenment and understanding of their place in the universe.
  • Life Journey: The idea of moving through different ages and experiences reminds us to embrace change and learn from our journeys.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings, the importance of balance in life, and the reverence for the divine. It encourages us to honor our relationships, seek harmony, and embrace the journey of life with an understanding of its cyclical nature.
