इन्द्रं मतिर्हृदा आवच्यमानाच्छा पतिं स्तोमतष्टा जिगाति |या जागृविर विदथे शस्यमानिन्द्र यत ते जायते विद्धि तस्य ||
indram matir hṛda ā vacyamānācchā patiṁ stomataṣṭā jigāti | yā jāgṛvir vidathe śasyamānendra yat te jāyate viddhi tasya ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Matir: Means intellect or understanding, indicating the importance of wisdom in decision-making.
  • Hṛda: Translates to heart, representing emotions and inner feelings.
  • Ā vacyamānācchā: Suggests the act of expressing or articulating thoughts and feelings.
  • Patiṁ: Means lord or master, indicating authority and guidance.
  • Stomataṣṭā: Refers to hymns or praises, emphasizing the significance of devotion and acknowledgment.
  • Jigāti: Means to move or progress, symbolizing the journey of life.
  • Yā: Refers to that which, indicating a connection to the previous elements.
  • Jāgṛvir: Means the awakened or aware, highlighting the importance of consciousness and awareness in life.
  • Vidathe: Refers to the assembly or gathering, symbolizing community and collaboration.
  • Śasyamān: Means flourishing or prosperous, indicating growth and abundance.
  • Indra: Again refers to the divine power, reinforcing the theme of strength and leadership.
  • Yat: Means that which, connecting the previous ideas to a result or outcome.
  • Te: Refers to you, indicating a personal connection to the divine or higher power.
  • Jāyate: Means to be born or to arise, symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities.
  • Viddhi: Means to know or understand, emphasizing the importance of knowledge.
  • Tasya: Refers to that, indicating a connection to the outcomes of the previous elements.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, emotional intelligence, and community in achieving prosperity and growth. It highlights the importance of expressing one's thoughts and feelings while acknowledging the divine guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in leadership roles, where understanding and articulating emotions can lead to better decision-making and collaboration within teams. It also serves as a reminder to remain aware and conscious of our surroundings, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. Ultimately, the mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, leading to personal and collective growth.
