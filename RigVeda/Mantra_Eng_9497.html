सरस्वान् धीभिर वरुणो धृतव्रतः पूषा विष्णुर महिमा वायुर अश्विनः |ब्रह्मकृतो अमृताः विश्ववेदसः शर्मा नो यंसन् त्रिवरूथम अंहसः ||
sarasvān dhībhir varuṇo dhṛtavrataḥ pūṣā viṣṇur mahimā vāyur aśvinā | brahmakṛto amṛtā viśvavedasaḥ śarma no yaṁsan trivarūtham aṁhasaḥ ||
  • Sarasvān: This term refers to the goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and learning, Saraswati. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and the importance of knowledge in personal and professional growth.
  • Dhībhir: This word means 'with intellect' or 'with understanding.' It emphasizes the need for wisdom in decision-making. For example, in business, making informed choices based on data and analysis reflects the application of dhībhir.
  • Varuṇo: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. This signifies the importance of maintaining balance and harmony in life. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation and the sustainable use of water resources.
  • Dhṛtavrataḥ: This means 'one who is steadfast in vows.' It highlights the importance of commitment and perseverance. For instance, an athlete training for a competition exemplifies dhṛtavrataḥ through their dedication and discipline.
  • Pūṣā: Pūṣa is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. This can be related to the importance of nurturing relationships and communities, as well as the role of agriculture in sustaining life.
  • Viṣṇur: Referring to Lord Vishnu, the preserver of the universe, this term signifies protection and maintenance. In real life, this can be seen in the efforts of individuals and organizations working towards social justice and community welfare.
  • Mahimā: This means 'greatness' or 'glory.' It reflects the pursuit of excellence and the recognition of achievements. For example, celebrating the accomplishments of scientists and innovators showcases mahimā in action.
  • Vāyur: Vāyu is the god of wind and air, symbolizing movement and change. In real life, this can be related to the dynamic nature of life and the need to adapt to new circumstances, such as technological advancements.
  • Aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. This highlights the importance of well-being and the advancements in healthcare that improve quality of life.
  • Brahmakṛto: This term means 'created by Brahman' or 'the ultimate reality.' It signifies the divine origin of all things. In real life, this can be reflected in the appreciation of art, culture, and spirituality.
  • Amṛtā: Meaning 'immortal' or 'nectar of immortality,' this signifies the pursuit of eternal truths and values. For example, the quest for knowledge and enlightenment in various philosophical traditions embodies amṛtā.
  • Viśvavedasaḥ: This means 'all-knowing' or 'possessing universal knowledge.' It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in understanding the world. In real life, this can be seen in the role of educators and mentors who guide others in their learning journeys.
  • Śarma: This term means 'comfort' or 'shelter.' It signifies the need for security and peace in life. For instance, creating safe spaces for communities reflects the essence of śarma.
  • No yaṁsan: This phrase can be interpreted as 'may we attain' or 'may we achieve.' It reflects the aspiration for success and fulfillment in life.
  • Trivarūtham: This means 'threefold' or 'triple form.' It can symbolize the interconnectedness of various aspects of life, such as mind, body, and spirit.
  • Aṁhasaḥ: This term refers to 'light' or 'radiance.' It signifies enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, this can be seen in the efforts of individuals striving to illuminate the minds of others through education and awareness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, knowledge, commitment, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in the reminder to pursue knowledge, maintain balance, and strive for excellence in all endeavors. In real life, these principles can be applied in various fields such as education, environmental conservation, healthcare, and community building, guiding individuals towards a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
