गो जिन नः सोमो रथजिद धिरण्यजित स्वर्जिद अभजित पवते सहस्रजित |यं देवासश चक्रिरे पीतये मदं स्वादिष्टं द्रप्सम् अरुणं मयोभुवम् ||
gojin naḥ somo rathajid dhiraṇyajit svarjid abjit pavate sahasrajit | yaṁ devāsaś cakrire pītaye madaṁ svādiṣṭhaṁ drapsam aruṇam mayobhuvam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the Soma, the one who conquers the chariot, the one who is rich in gold, the one who shines in the sky, the one who is victorious, who moves with a thousand powers, grant us the nectar of delight that the gods have prepared, the sweet drink that is red and born of the mind."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Gojin: Refers to the essence or the core of something, often associated with the divine or spiritual.
    • Naḥ: Means "to us," indicating a plea or request.
    • Somo: Refers to Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Rathajid: "Conqueror of the chariot," symbolizing mastery over one's journey or life path.
    • Dhiraṇyajit: "Rich in gold," representing abundance and prosperity.
    • Svarjid: "Shining in the sky," indicating a celestial or divine quality.
    • Abjit: "Victorious," symbolizing success and triumph.
    • Pavate: "Moves with a thousand powers," suggesting immense strength and capability.
    • Sahasrajit: "One who has a thousand victories," emphasizing the idea of overcoming challenges.
    • Yaṁ: "Whom," referring to the divine or the essence being invoked.
    • Devāsaś: "The gods," indicating the divine beings who are part of this invocation.
    • Cakrire: "Have prepared," suggesting a sense of creation or offering.
    • Pītaye: "For drinking," indicating the act of consuming or receiving.
    • Madaṁ: "Delight," representing joy and ecstasy.
    • Svādiṣṭhaṁ: "Sweet," indicating the pleasurable nature of the offering.
    • Drapsam: "Drink," referring to the sacred beverage.
    • Aruṇam: "Red," symbolizing vitality and life.
    • Mayobhuvam: "Born of the mind," indicating the mental or spiritual origin of the offering.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment through various means such as meditation, yoga, or even enjoying nature.
    • The idea of conquering one's "chariot" can be seen in personal development, where individuals strive to master their emotions, thoughts, and actions to navigate life's challenges effectively.
    • The reference to abundance and prosperity can be related to financial success, where individuals work hard to achieve their goals and enjoy the fruits of their labor.
    • The notion of divine inspiration can be found in creative pursuits, where artists, writers, and musicians seek to connect with a higher source of creativity to produce their work.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine qualities that can guide individuals towards a fulfilling and prosperous life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking joy, mastery, and abundance while recognizing the divine forces at play in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination, seek inner peace, and cultivate a sense of gratitude for the blessings they receive. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, one can navigate life's journey with grace and success.
