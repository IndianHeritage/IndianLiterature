प्रजावतिः सूयवसं ऋशन्तीः शुद्धा अपः सुप्रपाणे पिबन्तीः |मा वः स्तेन ईशत माघशंसः परि वो हेतī रुद्रस्य वृज्याः ||
prajāvatīḥ sūyavasaṁ riśantīḥ śuddhā apaḥ suprapāṇe pibantīḥ | mā vaḥ stena īśata māghaśaṁsaḥ pari vo hetī rudrasya vṛjyāḥ ||
  • prajāvatīḥ: This term refers to the concept of fertility and abundance. It signifies the nurturing aspect of nature, where life is sustained and flourishes.
  • sūyavasaṁ: This word can be interpreted as the essence of creation or the source of all beings. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the divine.
  • riśantīḥ: This term denotes the idea of being free or liberated. It suggests a state of purity and clarity, where one is unburdened by negativity.
  • śuddhā: Meaning 'pure', this word highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings. It is a call to maintain cleanliness in both physical and spiritual realms.
  • apaḥ: This refers to water, which is essential for life. Water symbolizes nourishment, cleansing, and the flow of energy.
  • suprapāṇe: This term means 'well-drinking' or 'having good sources of water'. It emphasizes the importance of having access to clean and pure resources.
  • pibantīḥ: This word means 'drinking' or 'imbibing'. It signifies the act of taking in nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
  • mā vaḥ: This phrase translates to 'may you not'. It is a plea or a request for protection against negativity or harm.
  • stenā: This term means 'thief' or 'deceiver'. It represents the forces that can disrupt harmony and steal away the purity of life.
  • īśata: This word means 'to be controlled' or 'to be governed'. It suggests the need for guidance and protection from negative influences.
  • māghaśaṁsaḥ: This term refers to 'great blessings' or 'auspiciousness'. It emphasizes the importance of seeking positive outcomes and divine favor.
  • pari vo: This phrase means 'around you' or 'in your vicinity'. It indicates the presence of divine protection and support in one's life.
  • hetī: This word means 'cause' or 'source'. It signifies the origin of actions and their consequences.
  • rudrasya: Referring to Rudra, a deity associated with storms and destruction, this term highlights the duality of nature—creation and destruction.
  • vṛjyāḥ: This term means 'to be avoided' or 'to be shunned'. It emphasizes the need to steer clear of negative influences and harmful actions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, purity, and the need for divine protection. It serves as a reminder to seek abundance and nourishment while maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal growth, community well-being, and environmental conservation. By fostering a sense of interconnectedness and seeking blessings for protection against negativity, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous life. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and mindfulness in our daily lives, encouraging us to cultivate a nurturing environment for ourselves and others.
