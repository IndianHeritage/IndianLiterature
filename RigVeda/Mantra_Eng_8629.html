अध क्षपाः परिष्कृतो वाजाम् अभि प्र गाहते |यदी विवस्वतो धियः हरिं हिन्वन्ति यातवे ||
adha kṣapā pariṣkṛto vājām̐ abhi pra gāhate | yadī vivasvato dhiyo hariṁ hinvanti yātave ||
  • Adha kṣapā pariṣkṛto: This phrase can be translated as "the night is well-prepared" or "the evening is adorned." It signifies the transition from day to night, a time when the world prepares for rest and reflection.
  • Vājām̐ abhi pra gāhate: This means "they call upon the powers of strength." Here, "vājām̐" refers to strength or energy, and "pra gāhate" indicates invoking or calling forth. This suggests a plea for strength and vitality during the night.
  • Yadī vivasvato dhiyo: This translates to "if the intellects of the wise are illuminated." "Vivasvato" refers to the sun or light, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment. This line emphasizes the importance of wisdom and clarity of thought.
  • Hariṁ hinvanti yātave: This means "those who seek the divine." "Hari" often refers to a deity or the divine essence, while "hinvanti" means to invoke or call upon. This line highlights the act of seeking divine guidance or support.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength and wisdom during the transition from day to night. It reflects the human experience of seeking guidance and support in times of uncertainty.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, we often face challenges that require us to seek strength and wisdom. For instance, during stressful times, such as preparing for an important exam or facing a personal crisis, we can reflect on this mantra to remind ourselves to seek inner strength and clarity of thought.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking strength and wisdom, especially during times of transition. It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and to prepare themselves mentally and spiritually for the challenges ahead.
