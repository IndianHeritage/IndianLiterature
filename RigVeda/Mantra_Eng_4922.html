विष्वदानीं सुमनसः स्याम पश्येम नु सूर्यं उच्छरन्तम् |तथा करद वसुपतिर वसूनां देवाम् ओहानोऽवसागमिष्टः ||
viśvadānīṁ sumanasaḥ syāma paśyema nu sūryam uccarantam | tathā karad vasupatir vasūnāṁ devām̐ ohāno 'vasāgamiṣṭhaḥ ||
  • viśvadānīṁ: This term can be interpreted as "all-giving" or "universal benefactor." It signifies the idea of abundance and generosity that is available to all.
  • sumanasaḥ: This means "with good intentions" or "with a pure mind." It emphasizes the importance of having a positive mindset and intentions in our actions.
  • syāma: Translated as "may we be," it expresses a wish or aspiration for a certain state of being.
  • paśyema: This means "may we see." It indicates a desire for perception, understanding, and awareness.
  • nu: This is an expression of emphasis, often translated as "indeed" or "truly."
  • sūryam: Referring to the "sun," it symbolizes light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • uccarantam: This means "rising" or "ascending," suggesting growth and elevation.
  • tathā: This translates to "thus" or "in this way," linking the previous thoughts to the next.
  • karad: This term can mean "with hands" or "action," indicating the importance of doing.
  • vasupatir: This refers to "the lord of wealth," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
  • vasūnāṁ: Meaning "of the wealth," it relates to material and spiritual riches.
  • devām̐: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings," representing higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • ohāno: This can be interpreted as "invoking" or "calling upon," suggesting a plea for divine assistance.
  • 'vasāgamiṣṭhaḥ: This means "may we attain" or "may we reach," indicating a desire for achievement and fulfillment.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for universal abundance, positive intentions, and enlightenment. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where one seeks not only personal growth but also the well-being of all.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a mindset of generosity and positivity. For example, in a workplace setting, fostering a collaborative environment where team members support each other can lead to collective success. Similarly, in personal relationships, approaching interactions with kindness and understanding can enhance connections and promote harmony.
  • Conclusion: The essence of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, action, and divine support in the pursuit of prosperity and enlightenment. By embodying its teachings, individuals can contribute to a more compassionate and abundant world, aligning their personal goals with the greater good.
