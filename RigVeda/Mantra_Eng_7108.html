मन्त्र: सप्त होतारस तं इद ईळते त्वाग्ने सुत्यजम् अह्रयम् |भिनत्स्य अद्रिं तपसा वि शोचिषा प्राग्ने तिष्ठ जनाम् अति ||
sapta hotāras tam id īḻate tvāgne sutyajam ahrayam | bhinatsy adriṁ tapasā vi śociṣā prāgne tiṣṭha janām̐ ati ||
  • Mantra: "sapta hotāras tam id īḻate tvāgne sutyajam ahrayam | bhinatsy adriṁ tapasā vi śociṣā prāgne tiṣṭha janām̐ ati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sapta: Seven
    • Hotāras: Priests or those who invoke
    • Tam: That
    • Id: Here
    • Īḻate: Shines or illuminates
    • Tvāgne: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • Sutyajam: Worthy of offerings
    • Ahrayam: I offer
    • Bhinatsy: Splitting or breaking
    • Adriṁ: The mountain
    • Tapasā: By austerity or penance
    • Vi: Indeed
    • Śociṣā: By radiance or brilliance
    • Prāgne: O wise one
    • Tiṣṭha: Stand or remain
    • Janām̐: For the people
    • Ati: Beyond or exceedingly
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "sapta hotāras" refers to the seven priests who perform rituals, symbolizing the collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • Agni is described as shining and worthy of offerings, indicating the importance of fire in rituals and its purifying qualities.
    • The mantra also speaks of breaking the mountain through austerity, which can be interpreted as overcoming obstacles through disciplined effort.
    • The call to "stand for the people" emphasizes the role of wisdom and guidance in leading society towards enlightenment and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of collective effort can be seen in community service projects where individuals come together to achieve a common goal.
    • The importance of rituals and offerings can be observed in various cultures where fire plays a central role in ceremonies, such as weddings or festivals.
    • Austerity and discipline are relevant in personal development, where individuals strive to overcome challenges through focused effort, such as in fitness or education.
    • Wisdom and guidance are crucial in leadership roles, where leaders must make decisions that benefit the community, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective spiritual practices, the power of fire as a purifying element, and the importance of wisdom in guiding society.
    • It serves as a reminder that through disciplined effort and community collaboration, individuals can overcome obstacles and contribute positively to the world.
    • In essence, the mantra encourages us to seek enlightenment and act for the greater good, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community service.
