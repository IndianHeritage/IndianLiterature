आ सुग्म्याय सुग्म्यम् प्रातः रथेनाश्विनाऽ वा सक्षणि |हुवे पितेव शोभरि ||
ā sugmyāya sugmyam prātā rathenāśvinā vā sakṣaṇī | huve piteva sobharī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • sugmyāya: to the auspicious or favorable
    • sugmyam: auspiciousness or goodness
    • prātā: in the morning
    • rathenā: by the chariot
    • āśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • vā: or
    • sakṣaṇī: with the power to see or perceive
    • huve: I invoke or call upon
    • piteva: like a father
    • sobharī: who is radiant or glorious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for auspiciousness and blessings, particularly in the morning, which is often considered a sacred time for new beginnings.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins signifies a call for health, vitality, and well-being, as they are known as divine physicians.
    • The comparison to a father suggests a deep sense of care, protection, and guidance, emphasizing the nurturing aspect of divine forces in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one might recite this mantra during morning rituals or meditation to set a positive tone for the day ahead.
    • Individuals facing health challenges may invoke the blessings of the Ashvins for recovery and strength, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on health and auspiciousness.
    • Parents might use this mantra to seek divine guidance and protection for their children, paralleling the nurturing aspect of a father.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for auspiciousness and health, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal well-being to familial care.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of starting each day with positive intentions and seeking divine support in our endeavors.
    • In a broader context, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection to the divine, fostering a holistic approach to life and well-being.
