तं सुश्रुत्यā विवासे ज्येष्ठराजं भरे कृत्नुम् |महो वाजिनं सानिभ्यः ||
taṁ suṣṭutyā vivāse jyeṣṭharājam bhare kṛtnum | maho vājinaṁ sanibhyaḥ ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • suṣṭutyā - This means "with good praise" or "through excellent commendation," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • vivāse - This can be interpreted as "to dwell" or "to reside," suggesting a connection or relationship with the divine.
  • jyeṣṭharājam - This term means "the elder king" or "the supreme ruler," symbolizing a powerful and authoritative figure, often associated with the divine or cosmic order.
  • bhare - This translates to "may you fill" or "may you bestow," indicating a request for blessings or abundance.
  • kṛtnum - This means "the act" or "the deed," referring to actions or efforts that are undertaken.
  • maho - This word means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of the subject being addressed.
  • vājinaṁ - This translates to "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful one," often referring to a divine force that provides energy and vitality.
  • sanibhyaḥ - This means "from the gathering" or "from the assembly," suggesting a collective or communal aspect in the invocation.

Conclusion: The mantra "taṁ suṣṭutyā vivāse jyeṣṭharājam bhare kṛtnum | maho vājinaṁ sanibhyaḥ ||" emphasizes the importance of praising and invoking the divine presence in our lives. It highlights the need for gratitude and recognition of a higher power that governs the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, where individuals seek blessings, strength, and guidance from the divine. By acknowledging the greatness of the divine and expressing our intentions through commendation, we can foster a deeper connection with the spiritual realm, leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
