समुद्राद ऊर्मिमुदियर्ति वेनो नभोजा: पृष्ठं हर्यतस्य दर्शि |ऋतस्य सानाव अधि विष्टपी भ्राट समानं योनिम अभ्य अनूषत व्राह: ||
samudrād ūrmim ud iyarti veno nabhojāḥ pṛṣṭhaṁ haryatasya darśi | ṛtasya sānāv adhi viṣṭapi bhrāṭ samānaṁ yonim abhy anūṣata vrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudrād: from the ocean
    • ūrmim: the wave
    • ud iyarti: rises up
    • veno: of the wind
    • nabhojāḥ: from the sky
    • pṛṣṭhaṁ: the back
    • haryatasya: of the horse
    • darśi: to see
    • ṛtasya: of truth
    • sānāv: with the two
    • adhi: above
    • viṣṭapi: in the vastness
    • bhrāṭ: brother
    • samānaṁ: equal
    • yonim: womb
    • abhy: towards
    • anūṣata: they have approached
    • vrāḥ: the seekers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dynamic relationship between the ocean, waves, wind, and the sky, symbolizing the interconnectedness of nature.
    • The phrase "from the ocean, the wave rises up" can be likened to how challenges in life (waves) emerge from a vast source (ocean) and how we must rise to meet them.
    • The mention of "the back of the horse" can represent strength and support, suggesting that we should rely on our inner strength and the support of others in our journey.
    • The reference to "truth" and "the two" indicates the importance of duality in life, such as the balance between opposing forces (e.g., joy and sorrow, success and failure).
    • The seekers approaching the "womb" signifies the quest for knowledge and understanding, emphasizing the importance of seeking wisdom and nurturing our inner selves.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges (waves) that arise from their past experiences (ocean). By acknowledging these challenges, they can rise above them and grow.
    • In teamwork, the "back of the horse" symbolizes the support team members provide each other, highlighting the importance of collaboration and mutual assistance.
    • In relationships, understanding the duality of emotions can help individuals navigate conflicts and appreciate the balance necessary for harmony.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge (the womb) encourages students to seek wisdom and understanding, nurturing their intellectual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life's journey, emphasizing the importance of rising to challenges, seeking truth, and nurturing our inner selves.
    • Its significance lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the balance required to navigate life's complexities.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace challenges, support one another, and continuously seek knowledge and understanding.
