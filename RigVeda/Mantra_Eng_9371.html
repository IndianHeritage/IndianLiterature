वयं सोम व्रते तव मनस तनूषु बिभ्रतः |प्रजावन्तः सचेमहि ||
vayaṁ soma vrate tava manas tanūṣu bibhrataḥ | prajāvantaḥ sacemahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • soma: Soma (a sacred drink in Vedic tradition)
    • vrate: In the vow or commitment
    • tava: Your
    • manas: Mind or consciousness
    • tanūṣu: In our bodies or forms
    • bibhrataḥ: Holding or carrying
    • prajāvantaḥ: Those who are full of progeny or creation
    • sacemahi: We aspire or we seek
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a collective aspiration for divine blessings and the nurturing of life.
    • The reference to "Soma" signifies the importance of spiritual nourishment and the sacredness of life.
    • By invoking the "mind" and "bodies," it emphasizes the holistic connection between thought, spirit, and physical existence.
    • The term "prajāvantaḥ" highlights the value of progeny and creation, suggesting a focus on family, community, and continuity of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to the importance of family values and the nurturing of future generations.
    • In a corporate setting, it can symbolize teamwork and collaboration, where the collective mind and efforts lead to success.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to align their thoughts and actions towards a higher purpose, fostering a sense of community and responsibility.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, spiritual nourishment, and the importance of nurturing life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for a harmonious existence, fostering love, respect, and continuity in life.
