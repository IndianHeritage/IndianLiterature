यā त ūतिर अवमा यā परमā यā मध्यमेन्द्र शुष्मिन्न अस्ति |ताभिर ū शु वृत्रहत्ये 'वीर न एभिś च वाजैर महान न उग्र ||
yā ta ūtir avamā yā paramā yā madhyamendra śuṣminn asti | tābhir ū ṣu vṛtrahatye 'vīr na ebhiś ca vājair mahān na ugra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: who/that
    • ta: this/that
    • ūtir: the ascent/height
    • avamā: the lower/lesser
    • paramā: the highest/supreme
    • madhyam: the middle/medium
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • śuṣminn: in the shining/bright
    • asti: exists
    • tābhir: by these
    • ū: the heights
    • vṛtrahatye: in the slaying of Vritra (the dragon)
    • avīr: the hero
    • na: not
    • ebhiś: by these
    • ca: and
    • vājair: by the steeds/forces
    • mahān: great
    • na: not
    • ugra: fierce/terrible
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the different levels of existence and the divine forces that govern them.
    • The mention of "yā ta ūtir avamā" suggests a journey or progression from the lower to the higher realms.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and power, represents the ability to overcome challenges, such as the slaying of Vritra, which can be seen as overcoming obstacles in life.
    • The phrase "tābhir ū ṣu vṛtrahatye" emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving greatness and overcoming adversities.
    • The mantra concludes with a reminder that true strength does not come from fierce aggression but from wisdom and the right use of power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that seem insurmountable. Just as Indra slayed Vritra, people can overcome their fears and obstacles through perseverance and support from others.
    • In leadership, the mantra can be applied to the idea that effective leaders do not rely solely on force or aggression but on wisdom, strategy, and the ability to inspire and uplift others.
    • In community building, the concept of rising from lower to higher states can be seen in social movements where collective action leads to significant change, emphasizing unity and strength in diversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the journey from lower to higher states of existence, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance and inner strength.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that true power is not just about aggression but about wisdom, support, and the ability to rise above challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek growth, overcome obstacles, and lead with compassion and strength.
