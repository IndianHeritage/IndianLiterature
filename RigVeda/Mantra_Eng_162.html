उप नः सुतम आगहि हरिभिर इन्द्र केशिभिः |सुते हि त्वा हवामहे ||
upa naḥ sutam ā gahi haribhir indra keśibhiḥ | sute hi tvā havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: near, towards
    • naḥ: our
    • sutam: son, offspring
    • ā: to, towards
    • gahi: grasp, hold
    • haribhir: with the yellow ones (often referring to the divine or celestial beings)
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • keśibhiḥ: with hair (often symbolizing strength or power)
    • sute: in the offspring
    • hi: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • havāmahe: we invoke, we call upon
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, to bless and protect our offspring. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in nurturing and guiding the next generation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the well-being and success of their children, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During significant life events such as weddings or naming ceremonies, invoking blessings for the future generations is a common practice, aligning with the call to Indra for protection and guidance.
    • In educational settings, teachers and mentors often seek divine inspiration and support for their students, paralleling the invocation of divine assistance in nurturing the next generation.
  • Significance and Application: The mantra highlights the importance of divine intervention in the upbringing of children. It serves as a reminder that while human efforts are essential, seeking blessings from higher powers can provide additional strength and guidance. In contemporary life, this can be applied in various contexts such as parenting, education, and community support, where the collective effort to nurture future generations is paramount.
