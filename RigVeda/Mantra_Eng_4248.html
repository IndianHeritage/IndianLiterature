मां कस्याद्भुतकृतु यक्षं भुजेमां तनुभिः |मां शेषसां मां तनसा ||
mā kasyādbhutakratū yakṣam bhujemā tanūbhiḥ | mā śeṣasā mā tanasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: Do not
    • kasyādbhutakratū: O one with extraordinary powers
    • yakṣam: To the one who is a Yaksha (a nature spirit)
    • bhujemā: May I not be harmed
    • tanūbhiḥ: By your body or form
    • mā: Do not
    • śeṣasā: By the remaining or leftover
    • mā: Do not
    • tanasā: By your physical body
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection from a powerful entity, specifically a Yaksha, who is often associated with nature and the unseen forces of the universe.
    • The repetition of "mā" emphasizes a strong desire for safety and the avoidance of harm.
    • The mention of "extraordinary powers" suggests a recognition of the divine or supernatural forces that can influence our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek protection from various forms of harm, whether physical, emotional, or spiritual. For instance, people may carry protective amulets or engage in rituals to ward off negative energies.
    • In a corporate setting, employees may seek protection from workplace politics or stress by fostering a supportive environment and building strong relationships with colleagues.
    • Parents often pray for the safety and well-being of their children, reflecting the universal desire to shield loved ones from harm.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking protection and safety in our lives.
    • It highlights the need to acknowledge the forces around us, both seen and unseen, and to cultivate a sense of security through spiritual practices or community support.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to be mindful of their surroundings and to actively seek harmony and protection in their lives.
