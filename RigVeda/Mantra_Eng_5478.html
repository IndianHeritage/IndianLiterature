आदित्याः रुद्राः वसवो जुषन्तेदं ब्रह्म क्रियमाणं नवीयः |श्रिण्वन्तु नो दिव्याः पार्थिवासो गोजाताः उता ये यज्ञियासः ||
ādityā rudrā vasavo juṣantedam brahma kriyamāṇaṁ navīyaḥ | śṛṇvantu no divyāḥ pārthivāso gojātā uta ye yajñiyāsaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityā: Refers to the Adityas, the solar deities in Vedic tradition.
    • rudrā: Refers to Rudra, a form of Shiva, representing storm and wind.
    • vasavo: Refers to the Vasus, deities of the elements and natural phenomena.
    • juṣant: Means to enjoy or to take pleasure in.
    • edam: This or this world.
    • brahma: Refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit.
    • kriyāmāṇaṁ: That which is being done or performed.
    • navīyaḥ: New or fresh.
    • śṛṇvantu: Let them listen or hear.
    • no: Our.
    • divyāḥ: Divine or celestial.
    • pārthivāso: Those who dwell on earth.
    • gojātā: Cattle or animals.
    • uta: And or also.
    • ye: Those who.
    • yajñiyāsaḥ: Those who are engaged in sacrifice or worship.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of various deities, including the solar deities (Adityas), Rudra, and the Vasus, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature and divinity.
    • It highlights the importance of performing righteous actions (kriyāmāṇaṁ) in a new and fresh manner, suggesting that one should always strive for innovation and improvement in their deeds.
    • The call for divine beings and earthly dwellers to listen signifies a collective consciousness, urging all beings to be aware of their actions and their impact on the world.
    • The mention of cattle (gojātā) symbolizes prosperity and sustenance, indicating that the blessings of the deities are essential for the well-being of all living beings.
    • Engagement in sacrifice (yajñiyāsaḥ) reflects the importance of rituals and offerings in maintaining harmony with the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to deities for a good harvest, reflecting the belief in divine intervention in their work.
    • In modern corporate settings, the idea of innovation (navīyaḥ) can be seen in companies that prioritize sustainable practices, aligning their business goals with environmental consciousness.
    • Community gatherings and rituals, such as festivals, serve as a reminder of collective responsibility and the importance of listening to one another for the greater good.
    • Animal welfare initiatives can be seen as a modern interpretation of the respect for cattle and nature, emphasizing the need for compassion in our actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of divine blessings in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in righteous actions and to innovate in their pursuits while being mindful of their impact on the world.
    • By invoking the divine and recognizing our responsibilities towards nature and each other, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits all.
