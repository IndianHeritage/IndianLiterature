श्रीणन्न उप स्थाद दिवं भुरण्युः स्थातुश्चरतं अक्तून व्य ऊर्णोत ||
śrīṇann upa sthād divam bhuraṇyuḥ sthātuś caratham aktūn vy ūrṇot ||
  • śrīṇann: This word can be interpreted as "hearing" or "listening." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • upa sthād: This phrase means "to stand near" or "to approach." It suggests the act of coming closer to something significant, whether it be knowledge, divinity, or a higher purpose.
  • divam: Translated as "heaven" or "the divine realm," this word signifies the spiritual or higher state of existence that one aspires to reach.
  • bhuraṇyuḥ: This term can be understood as "the vastness" or "the expanse." It represents the limitless nature of the universe and the infinite possibilities that lie within it.
  • sthātuś: Meaning "to establish" or "to create," this word indicates the act of forming or solidifying something, whether it be a thought, a belief, or a physical structure.
  • caratham: This word translates to "to move" or "to act." It emphasizes the importance of action and the dynamic nature of life.
  • aktūn: This term can be interpreted as "the energies" or "the forces." It refers to the various powers and influences that shape our lives and the world around us.
  • vy ūrṇot: This phrase means "to be filled" or "to be abundant." It signifies the idea of abundance and the fulfillment of desires and needs.

Conclusion: The mantra "śrīṇann upa sthād divam bhuraṇyuḥ sthātuś caratham aktūn vy ūrṇot" encapsulates a profound message about the journey of life. It encourages individuals to listen attentively to the wisdom around them, to approach the divine with sincerity, and to recognize the vastness of existence. By establishing a connection with the divine and acting upon the energies available to us, we can fill our lives with abundance and purpose.
In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, spiritual practices, and community building. For instance, in a workplace setting, being attentive to colleagues' ideas (śrīṇann) and fostering a collaborative environment (upa sthād) can lead to innovative solutions (bhuraṇyuḥ). Similarly, in personal relationships, actively listening and engaging with loved ones can strengthen bonds and create a fulfilling life (vy ūrṇot). Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of mindful action in achieving our goals.
