तां सम्राजागृहात्सुतीय यज्ञे-यज्ञ उपस्तुताः |अथैनोः क्षत्रं न कुतश्चनाधृषे देवत्वं नू चिदाधृषे ||
tā samrājā ghṛtāsutī yajñe-yajña upastutā | athainoḥ kṣatraṁ na kutaś canādhṛṣe devatvaṁ nū cid ādhṛṣe ||
  • tā samrājā ghṛtāsutī: This phrase can be translated as "those who are the rulers, born of ghee." In Vedic culture, ghee (clarified butter) is considered a sacred substance, often used in rituals and offerings. The term "samrājā" refers to a ruler or king, indicating a person of high status and responsibility.
  • yajñe-yajña upastutā: This part means "in the sacrifice, the sacrifice is praised." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices in Vedic traditions, where offerings to deities are made to seek blessings and prosperity.
  • athainoḥ kṣatraṁ: This translates to "therefore, their power." It suggests that the rulers or leaders possess a certain authority or strength that is derived from their connection to the divine through rituals.
  • na kutaś canādhṛṣe: This means "not from anywhere else." It implies that the power and authority of the rulers are not derived from any mundane source but are instead rooted in their spiritual practices and adherence to dharma (righteousness).
  • devatvaṁ nū cid ādhṛṣe: This can be interpreted as "the divine nature is not to be disregarded." It highlights the significance of recognizing and respecting the divine qualities that leaders should embody, reinforcing the idea that true leadership is intertwined with spirituality.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the connection between leadership, spirituality, and the importance of rituals in Vedic culture. It signifies that true rulers derive their authority from their adherence to dharma and their connection to the divine through sacrifices. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to modern leadership, where ethical governance, respect for traditions, and a commitment to the welfare of the community are essential. Leaders today can draw inspiration from this mantra to cultivate a sense of responsibility, integrity, and spiritual awareness in their roles, ensuring that their decisions benefit society as a whole.
