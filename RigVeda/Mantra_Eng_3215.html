एष वां देवाव अश्विनाऽ कुमारः साहदेव्यः |दीर्घायुर अस्तु सोमकः ||
eṣa vāṁ devāv aśvinā kumāraḥ sāhadevyaḥ | dīrghāyur astu somakaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "eṣa vāṁ devāv aśvinā kumāraḥ sāhadevyaḥ | dīrghāyur astu somakaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May this divine being, the Ashvins, the youthful ones, grant you long life, O Somaka."
  • Explanation:
    • eṣa vāṁ: This phrase translates to "may this (being) be for you." It indicates a blessing or a wish directed towards the recipient.
    • devāv: Refers to "divine beings" or "gods," specifically invoking the Ashvins, who are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.
    • aśvinā: The Ashvins are often depicted as youthful and benevolent, symbolizing vitality and rejuvenation.
    • kumāraḥ: This term means "youthful" or "young," emphasizing the energy and potential of youth.
    • sāhadevyaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "of the Somaka," referring to the individual or group being blessed.
    • dīrghāyur: This means "long life," a common wish in many cultures, signifying health and longevity.
    • astu: This is a term of affirmation, meaning "let it be" or "may it be so."
    • somakaḥ: This term can refer to a specific individual or a group, often associated with the moon or soma, which symbolizes nourishment and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Indian wedding, blessings are often invoked for the couple's long life and happiness, similar to the blessings of the Ashvins in this mantra.
    • During health rituals or ceremonies, individuals may chant this mantra to seek divine intervention for good health and longevity.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be used in motivational speeches or wellness programs to inspire individuals to seek vitality and a long, fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings for health and longevity, reflecting a universal desire for well-being.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, from personal health practices to communal rituals, highlighting the interconnectedness of spirituality and daily life.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, individuals are reminded of the value of youthfulness, vitality, and the pursuit of a long, healthy life.
