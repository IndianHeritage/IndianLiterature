वसू रुद्रā पुरुमन्तū वृधन्तā दशस्यतं नो वृषणाव अभिष्टौ |दस्रā ह यद रेक्ण औचथ्यो वाम प्र यत सस्राथे अकवाभिर ऊती ||
vasū rudrā purumantū vṛdhantā daśasyataṁ no vṛṣaṇāv abhiṣṭau | dasrā ha yad rekṇa aucathyo vām pra yat sasrāthe akavābhir ūtī ||
  • Vasū: This term refers to the deities associated with the elements and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to the natural resources we depend on, such as water, air, and earth, which are essential for our survival and well-being.
  • Rudrā: Rudra is often associated with storms and the fierce aspects of nature. This can be likened to the challenges and adversities we face in life, which can be both destructive and transformative.
  • Purumantū: This word signifies the wise or knowledgeable. In our daily lives, wisdom is crucial for making informed decisions, whether in personal relationships or professional endeavors.
  • Vṛdhantā: This term means to grow or increase. It can be related to personal growth, such as developing skills or nurturing relationships, which are vital for a fulfilling life.
  • Daśasyataṁ: This refers to the ten directions or completeness. In a broader sense, it can symbolize the holistic approach to life, where one must consider all aspects—physical, emotional, spiritual, and social.
  • No vṛṣaṇāv abhiṣṭau: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for blessings and protection. In real life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek guidance and support from higher powers during challenging times.
  • Dasrā ha yad rekṇa: This suggests the importance of remembrance and acknowledgment. In our lives, remembering our roots, traditions, and the lessons learned from past experiences can guide us in our current actions.
  • Aucathyo: This term can be associated with purity or clarity. In practical terms, it emphasizes the need for honesty and integrity in our dealings with others.
  • Vām pra yat: This phrase indicates a call for cooperation and unity. In real life, it highlights the importance of working together towards common goals, whether in communities, workplaces, or families.
  • Sasrāthe akavābhir ūtī: This can be interpreted as a plea for strength and support in overcoming obstacles. In everyday life, this resonates with the idea of seeking help from friends, family, or mentors when faced with difficulties.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking balance, wisdom, and support in life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, personal growth, and community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources around us, face challenges with courage, and foster unity and cooperation in our endeavors. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's complexities with grace and resilience.
