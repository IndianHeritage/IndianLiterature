यथा कण्वे मगवन मेधे अध्वरे दीर्घनीथे दामूनसि |यथा गोशर्ये असिषासो अद्रिवो मयि गोत्रं हरिश्रियं ||
yathā kaṇve maghavan medhe adhvare dīrghanīthe damūnasi | yathā gośarye asiṣāso adrivo mayi gotraṁ hariśriyam ||
  • yathā - This word translates to "as" or "like," indicating a comparison or a way of understanding something.
  • kaṇve - Refers to the sage Kaṇva, who is often associated with wisdom and knowledge in Vedic literature.
  • maghavan - This term is often used to refer to the deity Indra, who is known as the lord of wealth and abundance.
  • medhe - Meaning "in the mind" or "intellect," it emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and understanding.
  • adhvare - This word relates to rituals or sacrifices, highlighting the significance of spiritual practices.
  • dīrghanīthe - Translates to "long-lasting" or "eternal," suggesting the enduring nature of certain truths or principles.
  • damūnasi - Refers to the act of restraining or controlling, often in the context of desires or impulses.
  • yathā - Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the comparative nature of the mantra.
  • gośarye - This term can be interpreted as "in the cow's shelter," symbolizing nourishment and care.
  • asiṣāso - Meaning "blessings" or "prosperity," indicating the positive outcomes of righteous actions.
  • adrivo - Refers to "the mountain," symbolizing strength and stability.
  • mayi - Meaning "in me," indicating a personal connection to the divine or the principles being discussed.
  • gotraṁ - Refers to "lineage" or "family," emphasizing the importance of heritage and community.
  • hariśriyam - This term can be interpreted as "the glory of the divine," suggesting a connection to higher spiritual truths.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, mental clarity, and the practice of rituals in achieving prosperity and stability in life. It suggests that by following the path of righteousness and controlling one's desires, one can attain blessings and a strong foundation, much like the stability of a mountain. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals strive for self-control, seek knowledge, and engage in practices that promote well-being and community. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individual actions and their impact on the broader community and spiritual realm.
