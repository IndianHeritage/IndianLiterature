स्वयं दधिध्वे तविषीं यथा विद बृहन महान्त उर्वियā वि राजथ |उतान्तरिक्षं ममिरे व्य ओजसा शुभं याताम अनु रथा अवृत्तसत ||
svayaṁ dadhidhve taviṣīṁ yathā vida bṛhan mahānta urviyā vi rājatha | utāntarikṣam mamire vy ojasā śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • svayaṁ dadhidhve: This phrase can be translated as "you who are self-sufficient" or "you who possess your own strength." It emphasizes the importance of self-reliance and inner strength.
  • taviṣīṁ: This word refers to "power" or "might." It signifies the ability to overcome challenges and obstacles through one's own capabilities.
  • yathā vida: This translates to "as one knows" or "as one understands." It suggests the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding one's actions.
  • bṛhan: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word highlights the significance of greatness in character and deeds.
  • mahānta: This term means "noble" or "magnanimous." It points to the virtues of kindness and generosity.
  • urviyā: This can be interpreted as "with vastness" or "with expansiveness," indicating a broad perspective and understanding.
  • vi rājatha: This phrase means "may you rule" or "may you govern." It implies leadership and the responsibility that comes with it.
  • utāntarikṣam: This translates to "and the sky" or "and the heavens," symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • mamire: Meaning "to be filled" or "to be imbued," it suggests a state of being enriched or empowered.
  • vy ojasā: This phrase means "with energy" or "with vigor," emphasizing the importance of vitality in achieving one's goals.
  • śubhaṁ: This translates to "auspicious" or "good," indicating the positive outcomes that arise from virtuous actions.
  • yātām: Meaning "may we go" or "may we proceed," it encourages movement towards a goal or aspiration.
  • anu rathā: This phrase means "following the chariot," symbolizing the journey of life and the importance of companionship and support.
  • avṛtsata: This translates to "may it be fulfilled" or "may it be accomplished," indicating the desire for success and completion of one's endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of self-reliance, wisdom, and noble leadership. It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and knowledge to navigate life's challenges while fostering a sense of community and support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership roles, and collaborative efforts. By embodying the principles of self-sufficiency, greatness, and auspiciousness, individuals can strive for success and fulfillment in their endeavors, ultimately contributing to a harmonious and prosperous society.
