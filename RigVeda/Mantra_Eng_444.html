मन्त्र: इहेव श्रिण्व एषां कशा हस्तेषु यद वदान् |न यामञ्ज चित्रं ऋञ्जते ||
iheva śṛṇva eṣāṁ kaśā hasteṣu yad vadān | ni yāmañ citram ṛñjate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iheva: Here itself, in this world
    • śṛṇva: Listening, hearing
    • eṣāṁ: Of these, of this
    • kaśā: Who, which
    • hasteṣu: In the hands, in the actions
    • yad: What, that which
    • vadān: Speak, utter
    • ni yāmañ: Do not restrain, do not hold back
    • citram: Wonderful, colorful, diverse
    • ṛñjate: Is revealed, is manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to the world around us.
    • It suggests that by listening to the words and actions of others, we can uncover the beauty and diversity of life.
    • The phrase "do not restrain" encourages openness and the willingness to engage with the richness of experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, actively listening to colleagues can lead to innovative ideas and solutions. For instance, a team brainstorming session where everyone shares their thoughts can result in a more comprehensive project outcome.
    • In personal relationships, being attentive to a partner's feelings and expressions can strengthen the bond and lead to a deeper understanding of each other.
    • In education, teachers who listen to their students' feedback can adapt their teaching methods to better suit the learning styles of their students, creating a more effective learning environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and engagement with the world.
    • It teaches us that by being present and attentive, we can appreciate the richness of life and the diversity of perspectives.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and educational settings, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enriched existence.
