अग्निं देवासो मानविषु विक्षु प्रियं धुः क्षेष्यन्तो न मित्रम् |स दीद्यद उषतीर ऊर्म्याऽ आ दक्षाय्यो यो दास्वते दमा आ ||
agniṁ devāso mānuṣīṣu vikṣu priyaṁ dhuḥ kṣeṣyanto na mitram | sa dīdayad uśatīr ūrmyā ā dakṣāyyo yo dāsvate dama ā ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Devāso: Means 'of the gods', indicating a divine connection or influence.
  • Mānuṣīṣu: Translates to 'among humans', suggesting the presence of divine qualities in human actions.
  • Vikṣu: Means 'to seek or to desire', indicating the pursuit of something valuable or essential.
  • Priyaṁ: Means 'beloved' or 'dear', emphasizing the importance of what is being sought.
  • Dhuḥ: Refers to 'to draw out' or 'to extract', suggesting the act of bringing forth something valuable.
  • Kṣeṣyanto: Means 'those who are engaged in', indicating active participation in a process.
  • Na mitram: Translates to 'not a friend', suggesting a caution against false friendships or alliances.
  • Sa: Means 'he' or 'that one', referring to Agni or the divine force.
  • Dīdayad: Means 'to shine' or 'to illuminate', indicating the enlightening power of the divine.
  • Uśatīr: Refers to 'the best' or 'the most excellent', highlighting the quality of what is being sought.
  • Ūrmyā: Means 'waves' or 'currents', symbolizing the dynamic nature of life and energy.
  • Ā: A preposition meaning 'towards' or 'to', indicating direction or purpose.
  • Dakṣāyyo: Refers to 'skillful' or 'capable', emphasizing the importance of competence in actions.
  • Yo: Means 'who', referring to the one who possesses certain qualities.
  • Dāsvate: Means 'to give' or 'to offer', indicating the act of sharing or bestowing.
  • Dama: Refers to 'self-control' or 'discipline', highlighting the importance of restraint in life.

This mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine (Agni) and humanity, suggesting that through the pursuit of what is beloved and valuable, one can attain illumination and excellence. It warns against false friendships and highlights the importance of skill and self-discipline in achieving one's goals.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to seek out what is truly valuable in life, to engage actively in their pursuits, and to maintain discipline and skill. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, professional growth, and building genuine relationships. By embodying the qualities of Agni—energy, transformation, and illumination—individuals can navigate their lives with purpose and integrity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
