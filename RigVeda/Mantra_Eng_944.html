ऊर्ध्वं नुनुद्रे 'वतं त ओजसा दादृहाणं चिद् बिभिदुर विं पर्वतम् |धमन्तो वाणं मरुतः सुदानवो मदे सोमस्य रण्यानि चक्रिरे ||
ūrdhvaṁ nunudre 'vataṁ ta ojasā dādṛhāṇaṁ cid bibhidur vi parvatam | dhamanto vāṇam marutaḥ sudānavo made somasya raṇyāni cakrire ||
  • ūrdhvaṁ: This word translates to "upward" or "higher." It signifies the aspiration to rise above the mundane and reach for higher ideals or spiritual goals.
  • nunudre: This can be interpreted as "to awaken" or "to inspire." It suggests a call to action or a reminder to be alert and aware of one's surroundings.
  • 'vataṁ: This term refers to "the wind" or "air," often symbolizing the life force or energy that moves through all living beings.
  • ta: This is a pronoun meaning "that," indicating a connection to the previous elements mentioned.
  • ojasā: This word means "with energy" or "vigor." It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality in our endeavors.
  • dādṛhāṇaṁ: This can be translated as "to give" or "to bestow," indicating the act of sharing or providing support to others.
  • cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the significance of being mindful and present in our actions.
  • bibhidur: This word translates to "they are filled" or "they are imbued," suggesting a state of abundance or fulfillment.
  • vi: This is a preposition meaning "in" or "within," indicating a sense of inclusion or presence.
  • parvatam: This term means "mountain," often symbolizing stability, strength, and the challenges one must overcome in life.
  • dhamanto: This can be interpreted as "those who are steadfast" or "those who are determined," emphasizing the importance of perseverance.
  • vāṇam: This word refers to "speech" or "voice," highlighting the power of communication and expression.
  • marutaḥ: This term refers to "the winds" or "storm gods," symbolizing the forces of nature and their impact on human life.
  • sudānavo: This can be translated as "the good ones" or "the benevolent," indicating a positive influence or guidance.
  • made: This word means "in joy" or "in ecstasy," suggesting a state of happiness and fulfillment.
  • somasya: This term refers to "of the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual awakening.
  • raṇyāni: This can be interpreted as "the battles" or "the struggles," indicating the challenges one faces in life.
  • cakrire: This word means "they have created" or "they have established," suggesting the act of bringing something into existence through effort and intention.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for higher ideals, being aware of our surroundings, and harnessing our inner strength and energy. It encourages us to communicate effectively, remain steadfast in our pursuits, and find joy in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges with resilience and create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others.
