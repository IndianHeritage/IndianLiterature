सोमः पवते जनिता मतीनां जनिता दिवो जनिता पृथिव्याः |जनिताग्नेर जनिता सूर्यस्य जनितेन्द्रस्य जनितोता विष्णोः ||
somaḥ pavate janitā matīnāṁ janitā divo janitā pṛthivyāḥ | janitāgner janitā sūryasya janitendrasya janitota viṣṇoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somaḥ: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizes vitality and immortality.
    • pavate: He who purifies or makes sacred.
    • janitā: The creator or originator.
    • matīnāṁ: Of the minds or intellects.
    • divo: Of the heavens or celestial realms.
    • pṛthivyāḥ: Of the earth.
    • janitāgner: The creator of fire.
    • janitā sūryasya: The creator of the sun.
    • janitendrasya: The creator of the gods.
    • janitota viṣṇoḥ: The creator of Vishnu, the preserver.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine origins of various elements and deities in the universe.
    • Soma, as a symbol of life and rejuvenation, represents the essence of existence that purifies and nourishes.
    • The mention of "janitā" emphasizes the act of creation, highlighting that all aspects of life, intellect, and nature are interconnected and stem from a divine source.
    • By invoking the creator of fire, sun, and the gods, the mantra acknowledges the fundamental forces that sustain life and the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the concept of purification can be seen in practices such as meditation and yoga, which aim to cleanse the mind and body.
    • The sun, as a source of energy, is crucial for agriculture, reminding us of our dependence on natural elements for sustenance.
    • In modern spirituality, the acknowledgment of a higher power or divine source can be reflected in various religious practices that seek connection with the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all creation and the divine forces that govern it.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and honor the elements of nature and the divine, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to sustainable living practices and a deeper appreciation for the world around us.
