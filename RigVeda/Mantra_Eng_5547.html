स हि क्षयेण क्षम्यस्य जनमनः साम्राज्येन दिव्यस्य चेतति |अवन्न अवन्तीर उप नो दुरश्चराणमीवो रुद्र जासु नो भव ||
sa hi kṣayeṇa kṣamyasya janmanaḥ sāmrājyena divyasya cetati | avann avantīr upa no duraś carānamīvo rudra jāsu no bhava ||
  • sa hi kṣayeṇa kṣamyasya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, through the destruction of the unworthy." It suggests that in order to achieve greatness or divinity, one must overcome or eliminate negative traits or influences.
  • janmanaḥ sāmrājyena divyasya: This means "by the birth of the divine sovereignty." It emphasizes the importance of spiritual birth or awakening that leads to a higher state of existence or authority.
  • cetati: This word translates to "one thinks" or "one contemplates." It indicates the necessity of reflection and deep thought in the pursuit of spiritual and worldly goals.
  • avann avantīr upa no duraś: This can be interpreted as "may the obstacles not come near us." It expresses a desire for protection from challenges and adversities that may hinder progress.
  • carānamīvo rudra jāsu no bhava: This phrase translates to "like the moving ones, may Rudra not be angry with us." Here, Rudra represents a powerful force or deity, and the plea is for favor and protection from divine wrath.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming negativity and striving for spiritual elevation. It highlights the importance of self-reflection and the need for divine protection in our endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and conflict resolution. For instance, in a workplace setting, one might face challenges that require introspection and the elimination of unproductive behaviors to achieve success. By invoking the spirit of this mantra, individuals can seek guidance and strength to navigate through life's obstacles, fostering a sense of resilience and divine support in their journey.
