सं च त्वे जग्मुर गिर इन्द्र पूर्वीर विच च त्वद यान्ति विभ्वो मनीषाः |पुरा नूनं च स्तुतय ऋषीणाम पस्पृध्र इन्द्र अध्य उक्तार्काः ||
saṁ ca tve jagmur gira indra pūrvīr vi ca tvad yanti vibhvo manīṣāḥ | purā nūnaṁ ca stutaya ṛṣīṇām paspṛdhra indre adhy ukthārkā ||
  • saṁ ca tve jagmur gira: This phrase can be translated as "and they have come together with their words." It signifies the unity of thought and expression, emphasizing the importance of collective communication and agreement.
  • indra pūrvīr vi ca tvad yanti: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and leadership. The phrase suggests that the wise and powerful (the "pūrvīr") are drawn towards Indra, indicating the respect and reverence for authority and wisdom.
  • vibhvo manīṣāḥ: This translates to "the thoughts of the wise." It highlights the significance of wisdom and intellect in guiding actions and decisions, suggesting that wisdom is a guiding force in life.
  • purā nūnaṁ ca stutaya ṛṣīṇām: This part means "indeed, in the past, the sages have praised." It reflects the tradition of honoring and acknowledging the contributions of wise individuals (ṛṣīṇām) in society, which is essential for cultural continuity and respect for knowledge.
  • paspṛdhra indre adhy ukthārkā: This can be interpreted as "the hymns that are offered to Indra." It signifies the act of devotion and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine, suggesting that offerings and prayers are a means to seek blessings and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, wisdom, reverence for authority, and the importance of rituals in spiritual practice. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In teamwork, the importance of collective communication and agreement can lead to successful outcomes.
  • In leadership, respecting and valuing the wisdom of experienced individuals can guide decision-making processes.
  • In cultural practices, acknowledging the contributions of past sages fosters a sense of identity and continuity.
  • In spiritual practices, rituals and offerings can serve as a means to connect with higher powers and seek guidance.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of thought, action, and spirituality in our lives, encouraging us to seek wisdom and unity in our endeavors.
