ऋभुश् चक्र ईड्यं चारु नाम विश्वानि देवो वयुनानि विद्वान् |ससस्य चरमा घृतवत् पदं वेश तदिद अग्नि रक्षत्य अप्रयुच्छन् ||
ṛbhuś cakra īḍyaṁ cāru nāma viśvāni devo vayunāni vidvān | sasasya carma ghṛtavat padaṁ ves tad id agnī rakṣaty aprayucchan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛbhuś: The wise or learned ones.
    • cakra: Circle or cycle, often representing completeness or wholeness.
    • īḍyaṁ: Worthy of worship or praise.
    • cāru: Beautiful or pleasing.
    • nāma: Name or identity.
    • viśvāni: All or universal.
    • devo: Gods or divine beings.
    • vayunāni: Powers or energies.
    • vidvān: The knowledgeable or wise.
    • sasasya: Of the harvest or abundance.
    • carma: Skin or covering.
    • ghṛtavat: Like ghee or clarified butter, symbolizing richness.
    • padaṁ: Footstep or path.
    • ves: To dwell or reside.
    • tad: That.
    • id: Here or now.
    • agnī: Fire, often representing energy or transformation.
    • rakṣaty: Protects or safeguards.
    • aprayucchan: Without being abandoned or forsaken.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the wise and learned beings, acknowledging their role in the cycle of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worshipping the divine and recognizing the beauty in all aspects of existence.
    • The mention of "viśvāni devo" suggests a universal connection to the divine, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The reference to "sasasya carma" indicates a focus on abundance and prosperity, which can be interpreted as a call for a fruitful life.
    • “Agnī” symbolizes the transformative power of fire, representing energy and the ability to change and protect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to foster unity and collaboration, encouraging individuals to work together for a common goal.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, leading to personal growth and a deeper understanding of the world.
    • In agricultural practices, the emphasis on abundance can motivate farmers to adopt sustainable practices that ensure a bountiful harvest.
    • In spiritual practices, the invocation of divine protection can be a source of comfort and strength during challenging times.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, beauty, and divine connection in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to seek knowledge, foster abundance, and embrace transformation.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages a holistic approach to living, where one recognizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces at play.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a life that is rich in understanding, beauty, and protection, guiding individuals towards a harmonious existence.
