पवमानाऽसृक्षत पवित्रमति धारया | मरुत्वन्तो मत्सरा इन्द्रियाः हयाः मेधाम् अभि प्रयांसी च ||
pavamānā asṛkṣata pavitram ati dhārayā | marutvanto matsarā indriyā hayā medhām abhi prayāṁsi ca ||
  • pavamānā: This term refers to the "purifying" or "cleansing" aspect. In a real-life context, it can be likened to the process of self-reflection and personal growth, where one seeks to cleanse their mind and spirit of negativity.
  • asṛkṣata: This word means "to protect" or "to guard." In everyday life, this can be seen in the way individuals protect their loved ones or their values, ensuring that they remain safe from harm.
  • pavitram: This translates to "pure" or "sacred." In a practical sense, it can refer to maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings, such as keeping a clean environment or engaging in ethical practices.
  • ati dhārayā: This phrase means "with great support" or "with utmost strength." It can be related to the support systems we have in life, such as family, friends, or community, which help us navigate challenges.
  • marutvanto: This term refers to the "storm gods" or "winds." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the forces of change and transformation in our lives, akin to how storms can bring about renewal.
  • matsarā: This means "jealousy" or "envy." In real life, it highlights the importance of overcoming negative emotions that can hinder personal growth and relationships.
  • indriyā: This word refers to the "senses." It emphasizes the significance of being aware of our sensory experiences and how they influence our perceptions and actions.
  • hayā: This translates to "horses," often symbolizing strength and vitality. In life, this can be related to harnessing our inner strength to pursue our goals.
  • medhām: This means "intellect" or "wisdom." It underscores the value of knowledge and understanding in making informed decisions in life.
  • abhi prayāṁsi: This phrase means "to strive towards" or "to aim for." It reflects the human endeavor to reach goals and aspirations, emphasizing the importance of determination.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purification, protection, and the pursuit of wisdom. It signifies the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions while being aware of the forces of change and the emotions that can affect us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, relationships, and community interactions, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and harmonious life. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can foster a sense of inner peace and resilience in the face of life's challenges.
