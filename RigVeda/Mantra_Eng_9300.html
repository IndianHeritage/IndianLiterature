आदित्यानां वसूनां रुद्रियाणां देवो देवानां न मिनामि धाम | ते मां भद्राय शवसे तातक्षुर अपराजितम अस्तृतम अशाल्हम् ||
ādityānāṁ vasūnāṁ rudriyāṇāṁ devo devānāṁ na mināmi dhāma | te mā bhadrāya śavase tatakṣur aparājitam astṛtam aṣāḻham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityānāṁ: Of the Adityas (solar deities)
    • vasūnāṁ: Of the Vasus (elements or deities of material wealth)
    • rudriyāṇāṁ: Of the Rudras (storm deities)
    • devo: God
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods
    • na mināmi: I do not diminish
    • dhāma: The abode or realm
    • te: You
    • mā: Not
    • bhadrāya: For auspiciousness
    • śavase: In the body
    • tatakṣur: That which is invincible
    • aparājitam: Unconquered
    • astṛtam: Unshaken
    • aṣāḻham: Unassailable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powers of the Adityas, Vasus, and Rudras, acknowledging their divine nature and strength.
    • The speaker expresses a commitment to not diminish the essence of these divine beings, emphasizing respect and reverence.
    • The mantra seeks blessings for auspiciousness and strength, asking for protection and invincibility in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal challenges, such as health issues or career setbacks, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke strength and resilience.
    • During significant life events, like weddings or new ventures, people may seek blessings for auspicious beginnings, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be used in meditation to connect with divine energies, fostering a sense of protection and empowerment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support and the strength derived from acknowledging higher powers.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, protection, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of resilience and positivity, fostering a deeper connection with the divine.
