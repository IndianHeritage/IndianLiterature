स नो भगाय वायवे पूष्णे पवस्व मधुमान् |चारुर मित्रे वरुणे च ||
sa no bhagāya vāyave pūṣṇe pavasva madhumān | cārur mitre varuṇe ca ||
  • sa no bhagāya: This phrase translates to "may He (the divine) bestow upon us prosperity." Here, "sa" refers to the divine or the higher power, while "bhagāya" signifies wealth, prosperity, or abundance.
  • vāyave: This word means "to the wind" or "to the vital force." In a broader sense, it can represent the life force or energy that sustains all living beings.
  • pūṣṇe: This term refers to "the nourisher" or "the one who provides sustenance." It is often associated with the divine aspect that nurtures and supports life.
  • pavasva: This means "may you purify" or "may you cleanse." It indicates a desire for spiritual and physical purification, which is essential for growth and prosperity.
  • madhumān: This translates to "the sweet one" or "the one who brings sweetness." It symbolizes joy, happiness, and the pleasant aspects of life.
  • cārur: This word means "beautiful" or "charming." It reflects the aesthetic and harmonious aspects of existence.
  • mitre: This means "to the friend" or "to the ally." It signifies the importance of relationships and camaraderie in life.
  • varuṇe: This refers to "to Varuna," the deity associated with cosmic order, water, and the moral law. Varuna represents the ethical and moral dimensions of life.

In essence, this mantra is a prayer for divine blessings that encompass prosperity, nourishment, purification, joy, beauty, friendship, and moral integrity. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving a fulfilling life.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life. It teaches us that prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about nurturing relationships, maintaining moral values, and seeking joy and beauty in our surroundings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering friendships and alliances that bring joy and support.
  • In professional settings, striving for ethical practices and nurturing a positive work environment.
  • In personal growth, focusing on spiritual and physical purification to enhance overall well-being.
  • In community building, promoting harmony and beauty in our surroundings to create a more pleasant living environment.

By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a balanced and prosperous life that resonates with the divine qualities it invokes.
