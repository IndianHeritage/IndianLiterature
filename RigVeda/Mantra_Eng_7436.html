क्षेति क्षेमैभिः साधुभिर नकिर्म यं घ्नन्ति हन्ति यः |अग्नि सुवीर एधते ||
kṣeti kṣemebhiḥ sādhubhir nakir yaṁ ghnanti hanti yaḥ | agne suvīra edhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kṣeti: to dwell or reside
    • kṣemebhiḥ: with auspiciousness or well-being
    • sādhubhir: by the virtuous or good people
    • nakir: not (or no one)
    • yaṁ: whom
    • ghnanti: harm or injure
    • hanti: to kill or destroy
    • yaḥ: who
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • suvīra: with great strength or valor
    • edhate: may you shine or be exalted
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the protective and nurturing qualities of Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between the divine and human realms.
    • The essence of the mantra suggests that those who dwell in auspiciousness and are virtuous are shielded from harm and destruction.
    • It emphasizes the idea that negativity and harm cannot touch those who are aligned with goodness and virtue.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community where people support each other and engage in positive actions, individuals often feel a sense of safety and well-being. For instance, a neighborhood watch program can create a protective environment where crime is reduced.
    • In personal relationships, surrounding oneself with supportive and virtuous friends can lead to a more fulfilling and secure life. For example, having friends who encourage healthy habits can lead to better physical and mental health.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its affirmation of the protective power of virtue and goodness. It teaches that by cultivating positive qualities and surrounding ourselves with virtuous individuals, we can create a shield against negativity and harm.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, personal relationships, and even in professional environments where collaboration and support are key to success.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to strive for goodness and to foster environments that promote well-being and protection from harm.
