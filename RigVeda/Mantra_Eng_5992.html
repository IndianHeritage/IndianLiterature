अध ज्मो अध वाऽ दिवो बृहतो रोचनाद अधि |अयā वर्धस्व तन्बा गिरा ममाजातā सुक्रतो पृण ||
adha jmo adha vā divo bṛhato rocanād adhi | ayā vardhasva tanvā girā mamā jātā sukrato pṛṇa ||
  • Adha jmo adha vā divo bṛhato rocanād adhi: This phrase can be translated to "From below and from above, from the great radiance, I invoke." Here, "adha" means below, "vā" means or, "divo" refers to the sky or heavens, "bṛhato" means great, and "rocanād" refers to radiance or light. This invocation suggests a connection between the earthly and the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking blessings from both realms.
  • Ayā vardhasva tanvā girā mamā jātā sukrato pṛṇa: This part translates to "May you grow strong in my body, born of good deeds." "Ayā" means may you come, "vardhasva" means grow strong, "tanuvā" refers to the body, "girā" means speech or sound, "mamā" means my, "jātā" means born, "sukrato" means of good deeds, and "pṛṇa" means to nourish or fill. This line emphasizes the importance of good actions and their impact on one's physical and spiritual well-being.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual worlds. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and nourishment from both the earth and the heavens, reinforcing the idea that our actions have consequences that affect our overall well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, when facing challenges, one might reflect on the need for support from both their community (earthly) and their spiritual beliefs (heavenly). Additionally, it encourages individuals to engage in good deeds, as these actions contribute to personal growth and strength.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the duality of existence and the importance of nurturing both our physical and spiritual selves. By invoking strength from both realms, we can lead a more balanced and fulfilling life, grounded in good deeds and connected to the greater universe.
