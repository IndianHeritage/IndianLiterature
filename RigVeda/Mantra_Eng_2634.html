अमन्तिष्ठाम् भारतā रेवद अग्निं देवश्रवा देववातः सुदक्षम् |अग्ने वि पश्य बृहताभि रायेषां नो नेतā भवताद अनु dyūn ||
amanthiṣṭām bhāratā revad agniṁ devaśravā devavātaḥ sudakṣam | agne vi paśya bṛhatābhi rāyeṣāṁ no netā bhavatād anu dyūn ||
  • amanthiṣṭām: This term can be interpreted as "may we be united" or "may we come together." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration among people.
  • bhāratā: Refers to the people of Bharat (India), symbolizing a collective identity and cultural heritage.
  • revad: This word can mean "to be prosperous" or "to thrive," indicating a desire for abundance and well-being.
  • agniṁ: Agni represents fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • devaśravā: This term means "of the divine" or "glorious," suggesting a connection to the divine and the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • devavātaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the will of the gods," indicating that the speaker seeks alignment with divine intentions.
  • sudakṣam: Meaning "skilled" or "capable," this word highlights the importance of competence and expertise in achieving goals.
  • agne: Again referring to Agni, reinforcing the significance of fire and its transformative power.
  • vi paśya: This phrase means "may you see" or "may you perceive," suggesting a call for awareness and insight.
  • bṛhatābhi: This term can be translated as "with great abundance" or "with vast resources," indicating a desire for prosperity.
  • rāyeṣāṁ: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • no netā: This means "may you lead us," indicating a request for guidance and direction.
  • bhavatād: This can be interpreted as "may you become" or "may you manifest," suggesting a desire for transformation and growth.
  • anu dyūn: This phrase means "after the heavens" or "in accordance with the celestial," indicating a connection to higher realms and divine order.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for unity, prosperity, and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration among the people of Bharat, invoking the blessings of Agni, the divine fire, to lead them towards abundance and insight. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, spiritual rituals, or even in personal reflections where individuals seek to align their actions with higher principles and collective well-being. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the importance of striving for a prosperous and harmonious existence, guided by divine wisdom.
