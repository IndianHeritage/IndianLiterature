मन्त्र: उता स्वराजे अदितिः स्तोमं इन्द्राय जिजानत् |पुरुप्रशस्तम् ऊतय ऋतस्य यत् ||
uta svarāje aditiḥ stomam indrāya jījanat | purupraśastam ūtaya ṛtasya yat ||
  • uta svarāje: This phrase can be translated as "in the realm of the heavens" or "in the divine governance." It signifies the higher powers and the celestial order that governs the universe.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi is often referred to as the mother of the gods in Vedic literature. She represents infinity, boundlessness, and the nurturing aspect of the universe. Aditi embodies the concept of the divine feminine and the source of all creation.
  • stomam: This term refers to a hymn or praise. It indicates the act of offering reverence and gratitude, often through chanting or singing, to the divine forces.
  • indrāya: Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He symbolizes strength, power, and the warrior spirit. The invocation of Indra suggests a plea for strength and support in overcoming challenges.
  • jījanat: This word means "to create" or "to bring forth." It implies the act of manifestation, where divine energies are called upon to bring about desired outcomes.
  • purupraśastam: This term can be understood as "the most excellent" or "the most praised." It highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring the highest virtues and qualities in life.
  • ūtaya: This word means "to bring forth" or "to produce." It signifies the act of generating or manifesting something into existence.
  • ṛtasya: Rta refers to the cosmic order or truth. It represents the natural law that governs the universe, emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with this order.
  • yat: This word translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the preceding concepts and emphasizing their significance.

Conclusion: The mantra "uta svarāje aditiḥ stomam indrāya jījanat | purupraśastam ūtaya ṛtasya yat ||" encapsulates a profound invocation of divine energies and cosmic order. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the higher powers that govern our lives and the need to align ourselves with these forces. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of seeking strength and guidance, whether in personal challenges, community endeavors, or spiritual practices. By acknowledging the divine and the natural order, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, harmony, and resilience in their lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in accordance with the greater cosmic truth.
