इमं नरो मरुतः सश्चतानु दिवोदासं न पितरं सुदासः |अविष्टना पैजवन्स्य केतं दूनाशं क्षत्रं अजरो दुयोऽयु ||
imaṁ naro marutaḥ saścatānu divodāsaṁ na pitaraṁ sudāsaḥ | aviṣṭanā paijavanasya ketaṁ dūṇāśaṁ kṣatram ajaraṁ duvoyu ||
  • imaṁ naro marutaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "these men, O Maruts." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in the Rigveda, often associated with strength and power. In real life, this can symbolize the collective strength of individuals working together for a common cause.
  • saścatānu divodāsaṁ: This means "with the wise Divodasa." Divodasa is a legendary king known for his wisdom and leadership. This highlights the importance of wise leadership in guiding a community or organization, akin to how effective leaders in modern society inspire and direct their teams.
  • na pitaraṁ sudāsaḥ: Translated as "not the father Sudasa." This suggests a departure from traditional authority or lineage, emphasizing the idea that wisdom and merit can come from unexpected sources. In contemporary contexts, this can relate to the idea of meritocracy, where individuals are recognized for their abilities rather than their backgrounds.
  • aviṣṭanā paijavanasya ketaṁ: This phrase means "the abode of the swift." It can be interpreted as a call to action, urging individuals to be quick and decisive in their endeavors. In real life, this can be seen in the fast-paced nature of modern business, where timely decisions can lead to success.
  • dūṇāśaṁ kṣatram ajaraṁ: This translates to "the imperishable power." It signifies enduring strength and resilience. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of sustainable practices in business and life, where long-term success is prioritized over short-term gains.
  • duvoyu: This word can be interpreted as "twofold" or "dual." It suggests the importance of balance and harmony in life, such as balancing work and personal life or the duality of nature. In real life, this can be reflected in the need for individuals to find equilibrium in their pursuits.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of collective strength, wise leadership, merit over lineage, swift action, enduring power, and the importance of balance in life. In today's world, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in organizations, the value of wise decision-making, the recognition of talent regardless of background, the necessity for quick responses in business, the pursuit of sustainable practices, and the quest for personal balance. By embodying these values, individuals and communities can strive for success and harmony in their endeavors.
