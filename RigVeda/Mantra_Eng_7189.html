वयं घा ते अपूर्व्येन्द्र ब्रह्माणि वृत्रहन् |पुरूतमासः पुरुहूत वज्रिवो भृतिं न प्र भरामसि ||
vayaṁ ghā te apūrvyendra brahmāṇi vṛtrahan | purūtamāsaḥ puruhūta vajrivo bhṛtiṁ na pra bharāmasi ||
  • vayaṁ - This word translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • ghā - This means "to speak" or "to call," suggesting communication or invocation.
  • te - This translates to "your," referring to a divine entity, likely Indra in this context.
  • apūrvyendra - This can be broken down into "apūrva," meaning "unprecedented" or "unique," and "Indra," the king of gods, indicating the unparalleled nature of Indra's power.
  • brahmāṇi - This refers to "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic order," emphasizing the connection between the divine and the universe.
  • vṛtrahan - This means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or chaos, highlighting Indra's role as a protector and liberator.
  • purūtamāsaḥ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is most revered" or "the most honored," indicating the high status of the divine being addressed.
  • puruhūta - This means "the one who is invoked frequently," suggesting the importance of regular worship and connection with the divine.
  • vajrivo - This translates to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," a symbol of strength and power associated with Indra.
  • bhṛtiṁ - This means "support" or "sustenance," indicating the need for divine assistance in life.
  • na pra bharāmasi - This translates to "we do not bear," suggesting a plea for help or relief from burdens.

This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the need for divine support and guidance in overcoming obstacles. It reflects a communal approach to spirituality, where the collective seeks the blessings of a higher power to navigate life's challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Challenges: When a community faces difficulties, such as natural disasters or economic hardships, invoking the spirit of unity and divine support can foster resilience and collective strength.
  • Personal Struggles: Individuals facing personal challenges, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, can find solace in the idea of seeking divine assistance and support from a higher power.
  • Teamwork and Collaboration: In professional settings, this mantra can symbolize the importance of working together and seeking guidance to overcome obstacles, much like a team rallying for a common goal.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of community, divine support, and the strength found in unity. It encourages individuals and groups to seek help from higher powers in times of need, reinforcing the belief that together, with divine guidance, they can overcome any challenge.
