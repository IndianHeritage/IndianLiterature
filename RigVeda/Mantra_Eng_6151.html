य इमे रोदसी मही समीची समजग्रभीत् |तमोभिर इन्द्र तं गुहः ||
ya ime rodasī mahī samīcī samajagrabhīt | tamobhir indra taṁ guhaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ya ime rodasī mahī samīcī samajagrabhīt | tamobhir indra taṁ guhaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are the vast expanse of the earth, who are well-established, may Indra, the powerful one, protect us from darkness."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ya: who/that
    • ime: these
    • rodasī: the vast expanse or the earth
    • mahī: great or vast
    • samīcī: well-established or firm
    • samajagrabhīt: may they protect or may they hold
    • tamobhir: from darkness
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, associated with power and strength
    • taṁ: that
    • guhaḥ: hidden or secret
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the protection of Indra, who symbolizes strength and power, against the darkness that can represent ignorance, fear, or adversity.
    • The mention of "rodasī" and "mahī" emphasizes the vastness and stability of the earth, suggesting a grounding force that can be relied upon in times of trouble.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as loss or uncertainty, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power, much like invoking Indra for protection.
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can be seen as a call to respect and protect the earth, recognizing its vastness and the need for sustainable practices to combat the 'darkness' of pollution and climate change.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking strength and protection in the face of challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to connect with the earth and the divine for guidance, fostering a sense of responsibility towards both personal well-being and the environment.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the need for unity and support within communities to overcome collective challenges, reinforcing the idea that together, we can dispel darkness.
