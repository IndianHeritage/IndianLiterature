शिशुं न त्वा जेन्यं वर्धयन्ती माता बिभर्ति सचनस्यमानā | धनोर अधि प्रवता यासि हर्यञ् जिगीषसे पशुर इवावसृष्टः ||
śiśuṁ na tvā jenyaṁ vardhayantī mātā bibharti sacanasyamānā | dhanor adhi pravatā yāsi haryañ jigīṣase paśur ivāvasṛṣṭaḥ ||
  • śiśuṁ na tvā jenyaṁ: This phrase translates to "O child, may you be nourished." Here, the term "śiśu" refers to a child or offspring, symbolizing innocence and potential. In real life, this can be related to the nurturing of children by parents, emphasizing the importance of care and support in their formative years.
  • vardhayantī mātā: This means "the mother who nourishes." The role of a mother is crucial in the upbringing of a child, providing not just physical nourishment but also emotional and psychological support. For example, a mother’s encouragement can significantly impact a child's self-esteem and aspirations.
  • bibharti sacanasyamānā: This translates to "she bears the child with care." It highlights the responsibility and dedication of a mother in raising her child. In a broader context, this can be seen in various cultures where maternal figures are revered for their sacrifices and efforts in nurturing the next generation.
  • dhonor adhi pravatā: This phrase means "you move above the wealth." It suggests that the child is destined for greatness and prosperity. In real life, this can be interpreted as the potential for success that every child holds, which can be realized through education and opportunities.
  • yāsi haryañ: This translates to "you go forth with strength." It signifies the journey of life that the child will undertake, filled with challenges and achievements. For instance, as children grow, they face various life experiences that shape their character and resilience.
  • jigīṣase paśur ivāvasṛṣṭaḥ: This means "like a beast that is released." It symbolizes the freedom and independence that comes with maturity. In real life, this can be likened to young adults stepping into the world, ready to explore and make their own choices, much like a bird leaving the nest.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the nurturing role of a mother and the potential of a child. It signifies the importance of care, support, and guidance in the early stages of life, which are crucial for the child's development into a successful and independent individual. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in parenting practices, educational approaches, and community support systems that aim to foster the growth and well-being of children. It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with nurturing the next generation and the impact of love and guidance on their journey through life.
