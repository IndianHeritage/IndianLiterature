सुइग वहन्ति प्रति वाम ऋतेनोर्ध्वा भवन्ति पितरेव मेधा: |जरेथाम अस्मद विपणेर मनीषां युवोर अवश चक्रमा यातम अरवाक् ||
suyug vahanti prati vām ṛtenordhvā bhavanti pitareva medhāḥ | jarethām asmad vi paṇer manīṣāṁ yuvor avaś cakṛmā yātam arvāk ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suyug: well-connected or well-joined
    • vahanti: they carry or they bring
    • prati: towards or in accordance with
    • vām: your
    • ṛtena: with truth or righteousness
    • urdhvā: upwards or elevated
    • bhavanti: they become or they manifest
    • pitareva: like a father
    • medhāḥ: intelligence or wisdom
    • jarethām: may they be nourished or cultivated
    • asmad: our
    • vi: in or among
    • paṇer: of the wise or learned
    • manīṣāṁ: thoughts or intellect
    • yuvor: of the young or youthful
    • avaś: by force or necessity
    • cakṛmā: we have made or we have created
    • yātam: may it go or may it proceed
    • arvāk: openly or clearly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving higher goals.
    • The phrase "suyug vahanti" suggests that when individuals are well-connected and work together, they can achieve great things.
    • The reference to "ṛtena" indicates that this collaboration should be based on truth and righteousness, which is essential for success.
    • By likening wisdom to a father figure ("pitareva medhāḥ"), the mantra highlights the nurturing aspect of knowledge and intelligence.
    • The call to cultivate our intellect ("jarethām asmad vi paṇer manīṣāṁ") suggests that we should actively seek to develop our understanding and wisdom.
    • The phrase "yuvor avaś cakṛmā" implies that the youth have a crucial role in shaping the future, and their thoughts and actions are vital for progress.
    • Finally, "yātam arvāk" encourages openness and clarity in communication and action, which is essential for effective collaboration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, teams that communicate openly and work collaboratively tend to achieve better results than those that do not.
    • In educational environments, students who engage in group studies and share knowledge often perform better academically.
    • Community initiatives that bring people together for a common cause, such as environmental clean-ups or charity events, exemplify the power of collective action.
    • In family dynamics, nurturing wisdom and intelligence in children, akin to a father's role, fosters a supportive environment for growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, truth, and the nurturing of wisdom in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the need for collaboration and open communication.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious and successful existence.
