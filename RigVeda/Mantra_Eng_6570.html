अगोरुधाय गविषे द्युक्षाय दसम्यं वचः |घृतात् स्वादीयो मधुनश्च वोचत ||
agorudhāya gaviṣe dyukṣāya dasmyaṁ vacaḥ | ghṛtāt svādīyo madhunaś ca vocata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agorudhāya: To the one who is powerful and mighty.
    • gaviṣe: In the cow or in the context of nourishment.
    • dyukṣāya: To the one who is bright or shining.
    • dasmyaṁ: To the one who is worthy of praise.
    • vacaḥ: Words or speech.
    • ghṛtāt: From ghee (clarified butter).
    • svādīyo: Sweet or delightful.
    • madhunaś: From honey.
    • ca: And.
    • vocata: Speak or utter.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of strength and nourishment.
    • The reference to "gaviṣe" highlights the importance of cows in Vedic culture, symbolizing sustenance and wealth.
    • The phrase "ghṛtāt svādīyo madhunaś" suggests that the words spoken should be as sweet and nourishing as ghee and honey, emphasizing the value of kind and uplifting speech.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders who embody strength and provide nourishment (both physical and emotional) can uplift the entire group.
    • In personal relationships, using kind and sweet words can strengthen bonds and create a nurturing environment.
    • In educational settings, teachers who inspire and encourage students with positive speech can foster a love for learning.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of powerful and nourishing speech in our lives.
    • It teaches us to be mindful of our words, ensuring they are uplifting and beneficial to others.
    • In a world where communication is key, embodying the qualities of strength and sweetness in our speech can lead to more harmonious relationships and communities.
