त्वां इद वृत्रहन्तम जनासो वृत्कतबरिषः | उग्रं पूर्वीषु पूर्व्यं हवन्ते वाजसातये ||
tvām id vṛtrahantama janāso vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | ugram pūrvīṣu pūrvyaṁ havante vājasātaye ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" or "to you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • id - This means "now," suggesting immediacy and relevance in the current context.
  • vṛtrahantama - This compound word can be broken down into "vṛtra," which refers to a demon or obstacle, and "hantama," meaning "the one who kills." Thus, it signifies "the one who kills the demon Vṛtra," often associated with the removal of obstacles and negativity.
  • janāso - This translates to "of the people" or "of the community," indicating a collective aspect.
  • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in the sacred fire" or "those who perform rituals," emphasizing the importance of ritualistic practices in connecting with the divine.
  • ugram - This word means "fierce" or "powerful," suggesting a strong and dynamic force.
  • pūrvīṣu - This translates to "in the earlier times" or "in the past," indicating a historical or traditional context.
  • pūrvyaṁ - This means "the earlier" or "the first," reinforcing the idea of tradition and the importance of ancestral practices.
  • havante - This word means "those who offer" or "those who invoke," referring to the act of making offerings to the divine.
  • vājasātaye - This translates to "for the purpose of strength" or "for the purpose of power," indicating the intention behind the rituals and offerings.

The mantra can be understood as a call to a powerful divine force that removes obstacles and empowers the community through ritualistic practices. It emphasizes the importance of tradition and collective effort in invoking strength and overcoming challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When communities come together to address common challenges, invoking collective strength can lead to effective solutions.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: Engaging in rituals that honor traditions can foster a sense of belonging and empowerment among participants.
  • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing obstacles can draw inspiration from this mantra, reminding them of the power of resilience and the support of their community.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine strength to overcome obstacles, emphasizing the role of community and tradition in achieving empowerment. It serves as a reminder that through collective effort and ritualistic practices, individuals and communities can harness the power to face challenges and emerge victorious.
