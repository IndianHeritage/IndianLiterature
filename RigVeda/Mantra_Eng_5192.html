तवद्भियā विष āयन्न असिक्नीर असमानā जहतीर भोजानानि |वैश्वानर पूरवे शोशुचानः पुरो यद अग्ने दारयन्न अदीदः ||
tvad bhiyā viśa āyann asiknīr asamanā jahatīr bhojanāni | vaiśvānara pūrave śośucānaḥ puro yad agne darayann adīdeḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvad: from you
    • bhiyā: out of fear
    • viśa: of the universe
    • āyann: coming forth
    • asiknīr: of the river (often interpreted as a metaphor for nourishment)
    • asamanā: not equal
    • jahatīr: that which is to be abandoned
    • bhojanāni: food or nourishment
    • vaiśvānara: the universal fire or the divine energy
    • pūrave: in the beginning
    • śośucānaḥ: purifying
    • puro: in front
    • yad: that which
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • darayann: who brings forth
    • adīdeḥ: the ancient one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between the divine (Agni) and the sustenance of life.
    • The phrase "from you out of fear of the universe" suggests that all beings are interconnected and derive their existence from a higher power.
    • The mention of "food or nourishment" indicates the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, in our lives.
    • "Vaiśvānara" refers to the universal fire, symbolizing the energy that sustains all life and purifies it.
    • The invocation of Agni emphasizes the need for divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek nourishment not just from food but also from relationships, knowledge, and experiences. This mantra reminds us to appreciate the sources of our sustenance.
    • In times of uncertainty or fear, turning to spiritual practices or community can provide the support we need, much like the invocation of Agni for guidance.
    • Environmental awareness can be linked to this mantra, as it emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of respecting nature as a source of nourishment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine source of our sustenance and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages us to seek purification and guidance from higher powers, especially in challenging times.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder to respect and nurture our environment, as it is the source of our physical and spiritual nourishment.
