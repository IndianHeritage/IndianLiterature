तं नो अग्नि मघवद्भ्यः पुरुक्षुं रयिं न वाजं श्रुत्यं युवस्व |वैश्वानर महि नः शर्म यच्छ रुद्रैभिर अग्नि वसुभिः सजोषाः ||
taṁ no agne maghavadbhyaḥ purukṣuṁ rayiṁ ni vājaṁ śrutyaṁ yuvasva | vaiśvānara mahi naḥ śarma yaccha rudrebhir agne vasubhiḥ sajoṣāḥ ||
  • taṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "that (Agni) may be ours." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • maghavadbhyaḥ: This refers to the "magnificent" or "powerful" ones, often associated with the deities who bestow wealth and prosperity.
  • purukṣuṁ: This means "the one who has many forms" or "the one who is multifaceted," indicating the diverse manifestations of Agni.
  • rayiṁ: This word signifies "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of abundance in life.
  • ni vājaṁ: This translates to "may we have the best," suggesting a desire for the highest quality of blessings and resources.
  • śrutyaṁ: This means "by hearing" or "through knowledge," highlighting the importance of wisdom and understanding in achieving one's goals.
  • yuvasva: This is an invocation for Agni to "be youthful" or "to be invigorated," symbolizing the need for vitality and energy in life.
  • vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal aspect of Agni, representing the fire that connects all beings and the cosmos.
  • mahi naḥ: This means "may you grant us," indicating a plea for blessings and support from the divine.
  • śarma: This translates to "protection" or "shelter," emphasizing the need for safety and security in life.
  • yaccha: This means "give" or "bestow," a direct request for divine gifts.
  • rudrebhir: This refers to the "Rudras," a group of deities associated with storms and transformation, indicating the dynamic nature of life.
  • agne: Again invoking Agni, reinforcing the central role of fire in rituals and life.
  • vasubhiḥ: This means "with the Vasus," another group of deities associated with material wealth and prosperity.
  • sajoṣāḥ: This translates to "with joy" or "with happiness," indicating that blessings should come with a sense of fulfillment and joy.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a profound invocation for prosperity, protection, and divine blessings through the energy of Agni. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, vitality, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals for new beginnings, seeking guidance in challenging times, or when one desires to cultivate a sense of abundance and joy in their life. By invoking these divine energies, individuals can align themselves with the universal forces that promote growth, protection, and happiness.
