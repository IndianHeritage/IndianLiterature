सम īṁ रेब्हासो अस्वरन्न इन्द्रं सोमस्य पीतये |स्वर्पतिं यद īṁ वृधे धृतव्रतो ह्य ओजसा सम ऊतिभिः ||
sam īṁ rebhāso asvarann indraṁ somasya pītaye | svarpatiṁ yad īṁ vṛdhe dhṛtavrato hy ojasā sam ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we invoke Indra, the powerful one, who is the lord of the heavens, for the sake of the soma, the divine nectar. He who is strong and steadfast, may he grant us strength and vigor through our prayers."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam īṁ: Together, in harmony.
    • rebhāso: Radiant, shining.
    • asvarann: Powerful, strong.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms.
    • somasya: Of soma, a sacred drink that grants immortality and strength.
    • pītaye: For the sake of drinking, consuming.
    • svarpatiṁ: Lord of the heavens.
    • yad: Who, that.
    • īṁ: Strength, power.
    • vṛdhe: In growth, increase.
    • dhṛtavrato: Steadfast in vows, determined.
    • hy: Indeed, surely.
    • ojasā: With strength, energy.
    • sam ūtibhiḥ: Through our prayers, invocations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people may chant this mantra to invoke strength and unity among themselves, especially during challenging times.
    • During a sports event, athletes might recite this mantra to seek the blessings of Indra for strength and victory.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may use this mantra to foster collaboration and determination towards achieving a common goal.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and support in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the connection between spiritual practices and the pursuit of physical and mental vigor.
    • By calling upon Indra, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and collective effort in achieving success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals or groups seek strength, support, and unity.
    • It encourages a mindset of determination and resilience, reminding us that divine assistance can be sought through sincere prayers and collective efforts.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that strength is not just physical but also spiritual, and that invoking higher powers can lead to greater achievements.
