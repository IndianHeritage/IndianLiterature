पृतुपाजा अमर्त्यो घृतनिर्णिक स्वाहुतः |अग्निर यज्ञस्य हव्यवाट ||
pṛthupājā amartyo ghṛtanirṇik svāhutaḥ | agnir yajñasya havyavāṭ ||
  • pṛthupājā: This term can be broken down into two parts: "pṛthu" meaning "broad" or "vast," and "pājā," which refers to "worship" or "offering." Together, it signifies a broad or universal form of worship.
  • amartyo: This word translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It suggests a connection to the divine or the everlasting nature of the soul.
  • ghṛtanirṇik: This term can be understood as "the one who is clarified or purified by ghee (clarified butter)." Ghee is often used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the essence of offerings.
  • svāhutaḥ: This is a term used in Vedic rituals, often uttered during offerings to the fire. It signifies the act of offering and surrendering to the divine.
  • agnir: This word means "fire," which is a central element in Vedic rituals. Fire is seen as a purifying force and a medium through which offerings are made to the gods.
  • yajñasya: This translates to "of the sacrifice" or "of the ritual." It emphasizes the importance of sacrificial acts in connecting with the divine.
  • havyavāṭ: This term refers to the "sacred space" or "the place where offerings are made." It signifies the sanctity of the space where rituals are performed.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the significance of broad and universal worship, the immortality of the divine, and the purity of offerings made to the sacred fire during rituals. It highlights the importance of sacrifice and the sanctity of the space where these acts take place.


Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of purity in our actions, and the eternal nature of the soul. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Practicing mindfulness and purity in our daily actions, akin to the purity of ghee in offerings.
  • Engaging in community rituals or gatherings that promote unity and collective worship.
  • Recognizing the significance of sacrifice in personal growth, whether it be time, resources, or effort for the greater good.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of reverence and connection to the divine through their actions and intentions, fostering a deeper understanding of life and spirituality.
