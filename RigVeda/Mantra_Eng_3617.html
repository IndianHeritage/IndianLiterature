देवी देवैभिर्यजते यजत्रैरामिनती तस्थतुरुक्षमाणे |ऋतावरि अद्रुहा देवपुत्रे यज्ञस्य नेत्री शुचयद्भिरर्कैः ||
devī devebhir yajate yajatrair aminatī tasthatur ukṣamāṇe | ṛtāvarī adruhā devaputre yajñasya netrī śucayadbhir arkaiḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The goddess is worshipped by the gods, and she stands firm in the offerings made by the worshippers. She, who is the embodiment of truth, is the guiding force of the sacrifice, and she is pure, leading with her rays of light."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • devī: Goddess, representing divine feminine energy.
    • devebhir: By the gods, indicating the collective worship of divine beings.
    • yajate: Is worshipped, emphasizing the act of devotion and ritual.
    • yajatrair: By the worshippers, referring to those who perform the rituals.
    • aminatī: Stands firm, suggesting stability and strength in the divine presence.
    • tasthatur: They stand, indicating the steadfastness of the goddess in the rituals.
    • ukṣamāṇe: In the offerings, referring to the sacrifices made to the divine.
    • ṛtāvarī: Embodiment of truth, highlighting the importance of righteousness.
    • adruhā: Without deceit, emphasizing purity and honesty.
    • devaputre: Daughter of the gods, indicating a close relationship with the divine.
    • yajñasya: Of the sacrifice, referring to the rituals performed for spiritual gain.
    • netrī: Guiding force, suggesting leadership and direction in spiritual matters.
    • śucayadbhir: With purity, indicating the need for cleanliness in thoughts and actions.
    • arkaiḥ: With rays of light, symbolizing enlightenment and divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to deities to seek blessings, similar to how the mantra describes the worship of the goddess by the gods.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek guidance and strength from a higher power during challenging times, reflecting the goddess's role as a guiding force.
    • Practices such as meditation and mindfulness can be seen as modern-day sacrifices that promote purity of thought and action, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on righteousness.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of divine guidance, purity, and the collective effort of worshippers in seeking spiritual fulfillment. It teaches that true strength and stability come from a connection with the divine and that rituals, when performed with sincerity and purity, can lead to enlightenment and blessings in life. This mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios where individuals seek to cultivate a deeper spiritual connection, engage in community worship, or strive for personal growth through righteous actions and intentions.
