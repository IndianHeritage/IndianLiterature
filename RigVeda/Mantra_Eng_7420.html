ते नः सन्तु युजः सदा वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः |वृद्धासश्च प्रचेतसः ||
te naḥ santu yujaḥ sadā varuṇo mitro aryamā | vṛdhāsaś ca pracetasaḥ ||
  • te naḥ santu: This phrase translates to "may they be favorable to us." It expresses a desire for blessings and positive influences in our lives.
  • yujaḥ: This word means "connected" or "united." It signifies the importance of unity and harmony among individuals and communities.
  • sadā: This means "always" or "forever," indicating a continuous state of being or a lasting relationship.
  • varuṇo: Referring to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and moral authority. Varuna represents the natural laws and the ethical framework of the universe.
  • mitro: This translates to "friend." It emphasizes the value of friendship and supportive relationships in our lives.
  • aryamā: This term refers to a deity associated with the sun and the principles of truth and righteousness. Aryamā embodies the ideals of justice and moral integrity.
  • vṛdhāsaś: This word means "those who are powerful" or "those who are strong." It highlights the importance of strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the various elements of the mantra together.
  • pracetasaḥ: This translates to "those who are wise" or "those who have knowledge." It signifies the value of wisdom and understanding in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra "te naḥ santu yujaḥ sadā varuṇo mitro aryamā | vṛdhāsaś ca pracetasaḥ ||" encapsulates a profound wish for unity, friendship, strength, and wisdom. It calls upon divine forces to bless us with favorable conditions and to foster connections that are beneficial and harmonious. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in community building, fostering friendships, and promoting ethical behavior in personal and professional relationships. By embodying the principles of unity, strength, and wisdom, individuals can create a more supportive and just society, reflecting the ideals represented by the deities invoked in the mantra.
