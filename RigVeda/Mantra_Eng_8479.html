ऋषिः विप्रः पुरेताः जनानां ऋभर धीर उशनā काव्येन |स चिद् विवेद निहितं यद् आसाम् अपीच्यं गुह्यं नाम गोनाम् ||
ṛṣir vipraḥ puraetā janānām ṛbhur dhīra uśanā kāvyena | sa cid viveda nihitaṁ yad āsām apīcya1ṁ guhyaṁ nāma gonām ||
  • ṛṣir vipraḥ: This phrase refers to the sage or seer who possesses deep knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to a teacher or mentor who guides others through their insights and understanding.
  • puraetā janānām: This means "the one who leads the people." It signifies a leader or a visionary who inspires and directs a community towards a common goal. For example, a community leader who organizes efforts for social change embodies this concept.
  • ṛbhur dhīra: This translates to "the wise and composed." It emphasizes the importance of calmness and wisdom in decision-making. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who remain composed during crises, making rational choices rather than succumbing to panic.
  • uśanā kāvyena: This phrase can be interpreted as "through the poetic expression of knowledge." It highlights the power of art and literature in conveying profound truths. For instance, a poet or writer who uses their craft to address social issues exemplifies this idea.
  • sa cid viveda: This means "he knows the truth." It underscores the importance of knowledge and understanding in navigating life. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who seek knowledge through education and experience to make informed decisions.
  • nihitaṁ yad āsām: This translates to "that which is hidden." It suggests that there are deeper truths and insights that may not be immediately apparent. In real life, this can refer to the hidden potential within individuals or communities that can be uncovered through exploration and effort.
  • apīcyaṁ guhyaṁ nāma gonām: This phrase can be understood as "the secret name of the cows." In a metaphorical sense, it may refer to the nurturing aspects of life, such as sustenance and care. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of agriculture and the nurturing of resources for community well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, leadership, and the pursuit of hidden truths. It emphasizes the significance of knowledge and the role of individuals who guide and inspire others. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as education, community leadership, and personal development. By embodying the qualities of wisdom, composure, and a commitment to uncovering deeper truths, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a culture of understanding and growth.
