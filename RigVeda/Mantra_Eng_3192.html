इध्मं यस ते जभारच छश्रमानो महो अग्ने अनीकम आ सपर्ण्यन |स इधानः प्रति दोषाम उषासम पुष्यन रयिं सचते घ्नन्न अमित्रान ||
idhmaṁ yas te jabharac chaśramāṇo maho agne anīkam ā saparyan | sa idhānaḥ prati doṣām uṣāsam puṣyan rayiṁ sacate ghnann amitrān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idhmaṁ: This refers to the sacrificial offering or the fuel for the fire.
    • yas: Who or that which.
    • te: Your.
    • jabharac: Has been invoked or called upon.
    • chaśramāṇo: With great brilliance or radiance.
    • maho: Great or powerful.
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god.
    • anīkam: The host or assembly.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • saparyan: Worship or reverence.
    • sa: He or that.
    • idhānaḥ: Here or in this place.
    • prati: Against or towards.
    • doṣām: Faults or obstacles.
    • uṣāsam: The dawn or new beginnings.
    • puṣyan: Nourishing or flourishing.
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • sacate: He/she/it sits or resides.
    • ghnann: Destroying or overcoming.
    • amitrān: Enemies or adversaries.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the energy of life.
    • The phrase "idhmaṁ yas te jabharac" emphasizes the offering made to Agni, highlighting the act of devotion and the importance of rituals in spiritual practice.
    • The mantra also speaks of overcoming obstacles ("doṣām") and adversaries ("amitrān"), suggesting that with the blessings of Agni, one can achieve success and prosperity.
    • The reference to "uṣāsam puṣyan rayiṁ" indicates the dawn of new opportunities and the flourishing of wealth, symbolizing hope and renewal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of setting intentions or goals. Just as one offers to Agni, individuals can set their intentions for success and prosperity in their personal and professional lives.
    • In a corporate setting, teams can invoke the spirit of collaboration and unity, much like the assembly ("anīkam") mentioned in the mantra, to overcome challenges and achieve common goals.
    • During personal challenges, individuals can reflect on the mantra to find strength and resilience, invoking the energy of Agni to burn away negativity and obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of devotion, the importance of rituals, and the potential for transformation in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek the blessings of divine forces to overcome challenges and to embrace new beginnings with hope and determination.
    • In essence, it highlights the interconnectedness of spiritual practice and everyday life, urging us to recognize the divine in our pursuits and to strive for prosperity and well-being.
