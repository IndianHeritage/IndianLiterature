हविः हविष्मो महि सद्म दैव्यं नभो वसानः परि यास्य अध्वरम् |राजा पवित्ररथो वाजम् आरुहः सहस्रभृष्टिर जयसि श्रवो बृहत् ||
havir haviṣmo mahi sadma daivyaṁ nabho vasānaḥ pari yāsy adhvaram | rājā pavitraratho vājam āruhaḥ sahasrabhṛṣṭir jayasi śravo bṛhat ||
  • Havir haviṣmo: This phrase can be interpreted as "the offering is made with intention." In real life, this emphasizes the importance of sincerity and purpose in our actions. For example, when we help others, it should be done with genuine intent rather than for recognition.
  • Mahi sadma: Translated as "the great abode," this refers to a place of significance or a higher state of being. In practical terms, this could relate to our aspirations for a better life or a peaceful home environment, where love and respect thrive.
  • Daivyaṁ nabho vasānaḥ: This means "the divine presence dwelling in the sky." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine in our lives. For instance, when we look at the vast sky, it can remind us of the greater forces at play in the universe, encouraging us to seek connection with something larger than ourselves.
  • Pari yāsy adhvaram: This translates to "may we reach the sacred ritual." It highlights the journey towards spiritual fulfillment. In everyday life, this can be seen in our pursuit of knowledge, self-improvement, and the quest for meaning in our actions.
  • Rājā pavitraratho: Meaning "the king on a pure chariot," this symbolizes leadership and purity of purpose. In a real-world context, it can refer to leaders who govern with integrity and a commitment to the welfare of their people, much like a responsible CEO or a community leader.
  • Vājam āruhaḥ: This phrase means "mounting the chariot of strength." It signifies the importance of harnessing our inner strength and resources. For example, athletes train rigorously to harness their physical strength, which can be a metaphor for anyone striving to achieve their goals through hard work and perseverance.
  • Sahasrabhṛṣṭir: Translated as "thousand-fold strength," this emphasizes the power of collective effort. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as in community service projects or collaborative work environments.
  • Jayasi śravo bṛhat: This means "you conquer with great fame." It signifies the idea that true success is not just about personal gain but also about the legacy we leave behind. For instance, philanthropists who dedicate their wealth to charitable causes often achieve lasting fame through their contributions to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purposeful living, spiritual aspiration, and the importance of integrity in leadership. Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions should be driven by genuine intent, and that we should strive for a higher purpose in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to reflect on our motivations, work collaboratively, and aspire to leave a positive impact on the world around us. Whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or community involvement, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards a more meaningful existence.
