कविम इव प्रचेतसां यं देवासो अध द्विताः |नि मर्त्येष्व आदधुः ||
kavim iva pracetasaṁ yaṁ devāso adha dvitā | ni martyeṣv ādadhuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kavim: The wise or the poet, often referring to a seer or one who has deep insight.
    • Iva: Like or as.
    • Pracetasaṁ: With great intelligence or perception.
    • Yaṁ: Whom.
    • Devāso: The gods or divine beings.
    • Adha: Below or in this world.
    • Dvitā: The second or duality.
    • Ni: In or among.
    • Martyeṣv: Among mortals or in the realm of humans.
    • Ādadhuḥ: They have taken or accepted.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise seer, likened to a poet, who is recognized by the gods and is accepted among mortals.
    • It emphasizes the connection between divine wisdom and human experience, suggesting that those who possess insight are acknowledged by higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, individuals like philosophers, scientists, and artists can be seen as "kavim" who provide insights that shape our understanding of the world.
    • For instance, a scientist discovering a new principle in physics may be viewed as having divine insight, as their work can lead to advancements that benefit humanity.
    • Similarly, poets and writers who capture the essence of human experience often resonate with both the divine and the mortal, bridging the gap between the two realms.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wisdom and insight in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding.
    • It serves as a reminder that those who strive for wisdom are recognized and valued, both in the spiritual and material worlds.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and creativity, where the pursuit of knowledge and insight leads to progress and enlightenment.
