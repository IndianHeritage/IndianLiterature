सं-सम इद् युवसे वृषन्न अग्नि विश्वानि आर्य आ |इळस पदे सम इध्यसे स नो वसूनी आ भर ||
saṁ-sam id yuvase vṛṣann agne viśvāny arya ā | iḻas pade sam idhyase sa no vasūny ā bhara ||
  • saṁ-sam id yuvase vṛṣann agne viśvāny arya ā
    • saṁ-sam: This term can be interpreted as "together" or "in harmony." It signifies unity and collective effort.
    • id: This word means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or situation.
    • yuvase: Referring to "youth" or "young," it emphasizes vitality, energy, and the potential for growth.
    • vṛṣann: This term means "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful." It often refers to a deity or force that provides support and sustenance.
    • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • viśvāny: Meaning "all" or "universal," it suggests inclusivity and the idea that the divine encompasses everything.
    • arya ā: "Arya" refers to the noble or honorable, indicating a call to higher values and ethics.
  • iḻas pade sam idhyase sa no vasūny ā bhara
    • iḻas: This term can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance.
    • pade: Meaning "in the place" or "in the realm," it refers to a specific context or environment.
    • sam idhyase: This phrase means "may you be established" or "may you come together," emphasizing the importance of unity and collaboration.
    • sa: This word means "that" or "he," referring back to Agni or the divine force being addressed.
    • no: Meaning "our," it indicates a collective ownership or shared experience.
    • vasūny: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the desire for abundance.
    • ā bhara: Meaning "may you bring" or "may you fill," it is a request for the divine to bestow blessings and abundance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity through divine blessings. It calls upon Agni, the fire god, to bring together the energies of youth and strength, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In a workplace, fostering teamwork and collaboration to achieve common goals.
  • In community initiatives, encouraging collective efforts to improve living conditions and promote prosperity.
  • In personal relationships, emphasizing the importance of support and unity in overcoming challenges.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective action and the blessings that come from aligning with higher values and seeking abundance together.
