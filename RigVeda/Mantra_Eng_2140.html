शतं वा यस्य दश साकं आद्य एकस्य श्रुष्टौ यद ध कोदाम आविथा |अरज्जौ दस्यून सम उनब दभीतये सुप्राव्यो अभवः सास्य उक्त्यः ||
śataṁ vā yasya daśa sākam ādya ekasya śruṣṭau yad dha codam āvitha | arajjau dasyūn sam unab dabhītaye suprāvyo abhavaḥ sāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • śataṁ vā yasya daśa sākam: This phrase can be translated to "a hundred or ten of which are the first." It suggests abundance and the idea of multiple aspects or forms of something. In real life, this can be related to the concept of diversity in nature or society, where a single entity can manifest in various forms.
  • ādya ekasya śruṣṭau: This means "the first in creation." It emphasizes the origin or the primary source of existence. For example, in a family, the parents are the first creators of the next generation, symbolizing the continuity of life.
  • yad dha codam āvitha: This translates to "that which is invoked." It refers to the power of invocation or prayer. In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals or ceremonies where individuals seek blessings or guidance from a higher power, such as during weddings or festivals.
  • arajjau dasyūn: This phrase can be interpreted as "the unyielding enemies." It signifies challenges or obstacles that one may face. In a real-life context, this could relate to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or facing societal pressures.
  • sam unab dabhītaye: This means "to be free from fear." It highlights the importance of courage and resilience. For instance, individuals facing job loss or health issues must find the strength to confront their fears and move forward.
  • suprāvyo abhavaḥ sāsy ukthyaḥ: This translates to "well-spoken words lead to prosperity." It emphasizes the significance of communication and positive expression. In real life, effective communication in relationships or business can lead to successful outcomes and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, diversity, invocation, challenges, courage, and the power of words. Its significance lies in the understanding that life is a complex interplay of various elements, and by recognizing the importance of our origins, facing our fears, and communicating effectively, we can navigate through life's challenges. This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as personal development, conflict resolution, and fostering relationships, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence.
