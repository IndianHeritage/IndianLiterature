जन्मञ-जनमन निहितो जातवेदाः विश्वामित्रेभिर इध्यते अजस्रः |तस्य वयं सुमतौ यज्ञियस्यापि भद्रे सुमनसे स्याम ||
janmañ-janman nihito jātavedā viśvāmitrebhir idhyate ajasraḥ | tasya vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyasyāpi bhadre saumanase syāma ||
  • janmañ-janman: This phrase translates to "from birth to birth," indicating the continuity of life and the cycle of existence.
  • nihito: This means "established" or "fixed," suggesting a sense of permanence or stability in the context of life and existence.
  • jātavedā: Referring to the "one who knows all births," this term is often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • viśvāmitrebhir: This can be interpreted as "with all beings," emphasizing the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of harmony.
  • idhyate: Meaning "is invoked" or "is called upon," this word highlights the act of seeking blessings or guidance from the divine.
  • ajasraḥ: This translates to "constantly" or "perpetually," indicating an ongoing process or a continuous state of being.
  • tasya: Meaning "of that," it refers back to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • vayaṁ: This means "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • sumatau: Translated as "with good thoughts," it emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and intentions.
  • yajñiyasyāpi: This refers to "of the one who is worthy of sacrifice," suggesting the significance of selflessness and devotion in our actions.
  • bhadre: Meaning "auspicious" or "good," it highlights the pursuit of positive outcomes and well-being.
  • saumanase: This translates to "with a pleasant mind," indicating the importance of mental peace and harmony.
  • syāma: Meaning "may we be," it expresses a desire for collective well-being and harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, knowledge, and interconnectedness. It emphasizes the importance of positive thoughts, selflessness, and the pursuit of harmony among all beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, fostering relationships, and promoting mental well-being. By invoking these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society, reflecting the core values of the mantra in their daily lives.
