यो वां गर्तं मनसा तक्षद एतम ऊर्ध्वां धीतिं कृतवान धारणाच च |उक्षेथां मित्रावरुणा घृतेन ता राजानां सुख्षितीष तरप्येथां ||
yo vāṁ gartam manasā takṣad etam ūrdhvāṁ dhītiṁ kṛṇavad dhārayac ca | ukṣethām mitrāvaruṇā ghṛtena tā rājānā sukṣitīs tarpayethām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vāṁ: your
    • gartam: the pit or the depth
    • manasā: with the mind
    • takṣad: has created
    • etam: this
    • ūrdhvāṁ: upward
    • dhītiṁ: intellect or understanding
    • kṛṇavad: has made
    • dhārayac: and sustains
    • ca: and
    • ukṣethām: may they nourish
    • mitrāvaruṇā: by Mitra and Varuna (deities)
    • ghṛtena: with ghee (clarified butter)
    • tā: those
    • rājānā: the kings or rulers
    • sukṣitīs: with good fortune
    • tarpayethām: may they satisfy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine creation and sustenance of intellect and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing one's mind and intellect, suggesting that it is a divine act.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna signifies the balance of light and water, representing harmony and order in the universe.
    • The use of ghee symbolizes purity and nourishment, indicating that our thoughts and intellect should be nurtured with purity.
    • The reference to kings suggests that leaders should be wise and just, ensuring the well-being of their subjects.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, this mantra can be applied to the importance of nurturing a student's mind with knowledge and wisdom.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to act with integrity and to make decisions that benefit the greater good.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their thoughts and strive to cultivate a positive and constructive mindset.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of intellect and understanding in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals and leaders alike to seek wisdom and to nourish their minds with pure and constructive thoughts.
    • In a broader sense, it calls for harmony and balance in society, urging us to act with integrity and care for one another.
