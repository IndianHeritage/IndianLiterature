यम अग्निम मेध्यातिथिः कण्व ईधा ऋताद अधि |तस्य प्रेषो दीदियुस तं इमा ऋचस तं अग्निं वर्धयामसि ||
yam agnim medhyātithiḥ kaṇva īdha ṛtād adhi | tasya preṣo dīdiyus tam imā ṛcas tam agniṁ vardhayāmasi ||
  • yam - This word translates to "whom" or "that which." It indicates a focus on a specific entity or concept.
  • agnim - This means "fire" or "Agni," the Vedic deity representing fire, energy, and transformation. Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • medhyātithiḥ - This term can be broken down into "medhya," meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "ātithiḥ," meaning "guest." Together, it refers to the sacred guest or the divine presence that is welcomed.
  • kaṇva - This refers to a sage or a wise person, often associated with the Kaṇva clan in Vedic literature.
  • īdha - This means "may you be invoked" or "may you come forth," indicating a call to the divine or the fire to manifest.
  • ṛtād - This translates to "from truth" or "from the cosmic order," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and truth in the invocation.
  • adhi - This means "upon" or "over," suggesting a sense of elevation or transcendence.
  • tasya - This means "of him" or "of that," referring back to Agni or the divine presence being invoked.
  • preṣo - This translates to "messenger" or "one who sends," indicating the role of Agni as a communicator between the earthly and the divine.
  • dīdiyus - This means "to shine" or "to illuminate," suggesting the enlightening qualities of Agni.
  • tam - This means "that" or "him," again referring to Agni.
  • imā - This translates to "these," referring to the offerings or hymns being presented.
  • ṛcas - This means "verses" or "hymns," indicating the sacred words being offered to Agni.
  • vardhayāmasi - This translates to "we increase" or "we nourish," suggesting the act of enhancing or promoting the divine presence.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the sacred fire, as a divine guest and messenger, emphasizing the importance of truth and righteousness in the process. It calls for the illumination and nourishment of the divine presence through hymns and offerings.


Conclusion: This mantra holds significant relevance in various aspects of life. It can be applied in rituals, ceremonies, and personal practices where one seeks to invite divine energy and guidance. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace truth and righteousness, fostering a sense of connection with the divine. By invoking Agni, one can seek clarity, transformation, and enlightenment in their journey, making it a powerful tool for spiritual growth and understanding.
