द्विताविवव्रे सञ्जना सनीले आयास्यः स्तवमानैभिरर्कैः |भागो न मेने परम व्योमननधारयद्रोदसी सुदंसाः ||
dvitā vi vavre sanajā sanīḻe ayāsyaḥ stavamānebhir arkaiḥ | bhago na mene parame vyomann adhārayad rodasī sudaṁsāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dvitā vi vavre sanajā sanīḻe ayāsyaḥ stavamānebhir arkaiḥ | bhago na mene parame vyomann adhārayad rodasī sudaṁsāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvitā: two, duality
    • vi: indeed, certainly
    • vavre: chose, selected
    • sanajā: born, created
    • sanīḻe: in the well-formed, well-structured
    • ayāsyaḥ: without effort, effortlessly
    • stavamānebhir: by praises, through hymns
    • arkaiḥ: with rays, light
    • bhago: wealth, prosperity
    • na: not
    • mene: thought, considered
    • parame: supreme, highest
    • vyomann: in the sky, in the heavens
    • adhārayad: supported, upheld
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the earth and the sky
    • sudaṁsāḥ: beautiful, auspicious
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the duality of existence and the selection of the well-formed aspects of creation.
    • The phrase "dvitā vi vavre" emphasizes the importance of recognizing duality in life, such as light and darkness, joy and sorrow.
    • "Sanajā sanīḻe" suggests that everything created is structured and has a purpose, much like how a well-designed building serves its function.
    • The mention of "ayāsyaḥ" indicates that the divine or the ultimate truth can be realized effortlessly, akin to how a flower blooms naturally without force.
    • "Stavamānebhir arkaiḥ" highlights the significance of praise and acknowledgment, similar to how we celebrate achievements in our lives.
    • The term "bhago na mene" reflects the idea that true wealth is not merely material but encompasses spiritual and emotional richness.
    • "Parame vyomann adhārayad" suggests that the supreme reality supports all existence, much like how the atmosphere sustains life on Earth.
    • "Rodasī sudaṁsāḥ" indicates the beauty and harmony of the universe, reminding us to appreciate the world around us.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us to recognize and appreciate the dualities in life, urging us to find balance and harmony.
    • It emphasizes the importance of praise and gratitude, which can enhance our relationships and overall well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or during significant life events where acknowledgment of duality and beauty is essential.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that the universe is a beautifully structured entity, and by recognizing its intricacies, we can lead more fulfilling lives.
