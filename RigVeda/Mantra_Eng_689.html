अस्य शासुर उभयासः साचन्ते हविष्मन्त उशिजो ये च मार्ताः |दिवश्चित पूर्वो न्यसादि होतापृच्छ्यो विश्वपितर विक्षु वेधाः ||
asya śāsur ubhayāsaḥ sacante haviṣmanta uśijo ye ca martāḥ | divaś cit pūrvo ny asādi hotāpṛcchyo viśpatir vikṣu vedhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • śāsur: teacher or guide
    • ubhayāsaḥ: both sides or aspects
    • sacante: those who are engaged or involved
    • haviṣmanta: those who offer oblations
    • uśijo: the wise or learned
    • ye: who
    • ca: and
    • martāḥ: mortals or human beings
    • divaś: of the heavens
    • cit: conscious or aware
    • pūrvo: the ancient or previous
    • ny: not
    • asādi: to be attained or achieved
    • hotāpṛcchyo: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • viśpatir: the lord of the universe
    • vikṣu: the seer or sage
    • vedhāḥ: the knowledge or wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the guidance and wisdom provided by a teacher or a divine entity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of both the material and spiritual aspects of life, suggesting that true understanding comes from engaging with both.
    • The mention of "haviṣmanta" indicates the significance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • It acknowledges the role of wise individuals ("uśijo") in imparting knowledge to mortals ("martāḥ").
    • The reference to the heavens ("divaś") and the ancient ("pūrvo") suggests a connection to a higher consciousness and timeless wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, teachers embody the role of "śāsur," guiding students through both academic and moral lessons.
    • In spiritual practices, rituals and offerings can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, similar to "haviṣmanta."
    • Mentorship in professional environments reflects the relationship between the wise ("uśijo") and those seeking knowledge ("martāḥ").
    • Community leaders often serve as "viśpatir," guiding their communities with wisdom and insight.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of guidance, wisdom, and the interplay between the material and spiritual realms.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including education, spirituality, and community leadership.
    • By recognizing the importance of both the teacher and the learner, as well as the rituals that connect us to the divine, we can foster a deeper understanding of our existence and purpose.
