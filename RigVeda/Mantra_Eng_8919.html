उद īरताम् अवर उत परास् उन मध्यमाः पितरः सोम्यासः |असुं य इयुर अवृका ऋतज्ञास् ते नो 'वन्तु पितरो हवेषु ||
ud īratām avara ut parāsa un madhyamāḥ pitaraḥ somyāsaḥ | asuṁ ya īyur avṛkā ṛtajñās te no 'vantu pitaro haveṣu ||
  • ud īratām: This phrase can be translated as "may we be uplifted" or "may we rise up." It signifies a desire for elevation, both spiritually and materially.
  • avara ut parāsa: This means "from the lower to the higher." It reflects the journey of moving from a state of ignorance or lower consciousness to a higher state of awareness and understanding.
  • un madhyamāḥ: This translates to "the middle path." It suggests a balanced approach to life, avoiding extremes and finding harmony in our actions and thoughts.
  • pitaraḥ somyāsaḥ: This refers to "the fathers who are peaceful." It acknowledges the wisdom and guidance of our ancestors, emphasizing the importance of their teachings in our lives.
  • asuṁ ya īyur avṛkā: This can be interpreted as "those who are wise and knowledgeable." It highlights the value of wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge in our journey through life.
  • ṛtajñās: This word means "those who know the truth." It signifies the importance of truth and righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • te no 'vantu pitaro haveṣu: This translates to "may the fathers protect us in our endeavors." It is a plea for guidance and protection from our ancestors as we navigate through life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking elevation in life, moving from ignorance to knowledge, and finding balance in our actions. It acknowledges the wisdom of our ancestors and the importance of truth in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, decision-making, and seeking guidance during challenging times. By invoking the blessings of our forebears and striving for wisdom, we can navigate our paths with clarity and purpose, ensuring that our actions are aligned with higher principles and values.
