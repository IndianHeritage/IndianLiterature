स तु श्रुधि श्रुत्या यो दुवोयुर द्यौर न भूमाभि रायो आर्यः |असो यथा नः शवसा चकानो युगे-युगे वयसा चेकितानः ||
sa tu śrudhi śrutyā yo duvoyur dyaur na bhūmābhi rāyo aryaḥ | aso yathā naḥ śavasā cakāno yuge-yuge vayasā cekitānaḥ ||
  • sa tu śrudhi śrutyā: This phrase can be translated as "He who is to be heard, through the hearing." It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding the teachings or wisdom that is being imparted. In real life, this can be related to the value of active listening in communication, where one must truly hear and comprehend what others are saying to foster better relationships.
  • yo duvoyur dyaur na bhūmābhi rāyo aryaḥ: This part means "He who is the two worlds, the sky and the earth, is the noble one." It signifies the connection between the spiritual (sky) and the material (earth) realms. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals balance their spiritual beliefs with their everyday lives, striving to find harmony between their aspirations and responsibilities.
  • aso yathā naḥ śavasā cakāno: This translates to "As he who has made us strong." It speaks to the idea of empowerment and strength that comes from a higher source or divine power. In a real-life context, this can be related to how people draw strength from their faith, community, or personal experiences to overcome challenges.
  • yuge-yuge vayasā cekitānaḥ: This means "In every age, he is the one who knows." It highlights the timeless nature of wisdom and knowledge that transcends generations. In modern life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of lifelong learning and the importance of adapting to new knowledge and experiences as society evolves.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of listening, understanding the connection between the spiritual and material worlds, drawing strength from divine sources, and the pursuit of knowledge across ages. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to be active listeners, seek balance in their lives, draw strength from their beliefs, and commit to lifelong learning. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life, contributing positively to society and personal growth.
