महो यस पतिक् शवसो असाम्य आ महो नृम्णस्य तूतुजिः |भार्ता वज्रस्य धृष्णोः पिता पुत्रम् इव प्रियम् ||
maho yas patiḥ śavaso asāmy ā maho nṛmṇasya tūtujiḥ | bhartā vajrasya dhṛṣṇoḥ pitā putram iva priyam ||
  • maho: This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies the vastness and importance of the subject being discussed.
  • yas: Meaning "who" or "that," it introduces the subject of the mantra.
  • patiḥ: This term means "lord" or "master," indicating a figure of authority or power.
  • śavaso: This word can be interpreted as "of the dead" or "of the deceased," referring to the realm of the departed souls.
  • asāmy: Meaning "without equality" or "unequal," it suggests a unique or unparalleled nature.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "from" or "towards," indicating direction or origin.
  • nṛmṇasya: This translates to "of man" or "of humanity," emphasizing the human aspect of the mantra.
  • tūtujiḥ: This term can be understood as "the one who protects" or "the guardian," highlighting a protective role.
  • bhartā: Meaning "the supporter" or "the provider," it refers to someone who sustains or nurtures.
  • vajrasya: This word means "of the thunderbolt" or "of strength," symbolizing power and resilience.
  • dhṛṣṇoḥ: This translates to "of the brave" or "of the courageous," indicating valor and strength in character.
  • pitā: Meaning "father," it signifies a paternal figure, representing care and guidance.
  • putram: This word means "son," indicating a relationship of love and affection.
  • iva: Meaning "like" or "as," it draws a comparison, emphasizing similarity.
  • priyam: This translates to "beloved" or "dear," indicating a deep emotional connection.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between a powerful figure (the lord) and humanity, emphasizing the protective and nurturing roles that are essential in life. It highlights the importance of strength, bravery, and love in relationships, particularly between a father and son.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Family Relationships: The father-son relationship is a prime example of the nurturing and protective bond that is celebrated in this mantra. It reminds us of the importance of love and support within families.
  • Leadership: The qualities of a good leader, such as strength, bravery, and the ability to protect and nurture those they lead, are encapsulated in this mantra. It serves as a reminder for leaders to embody these traits.
  • Community Support: The mantra can also be applied to the broader community, where individuals take on roles of support and protection for one another, fostering a sense of unity and strength.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, protection, and nurturing in relationships, particularly within families and communities. It serves as a timeless reminder of the values that bind us together and the roles we play in supporting one another in our journeys through life.
