pra kṣodasā dhāyasā sasra eṣā sarasvatī dharuṇam āyasī pūḥ | prabābadhānā rathyeva yāti viśvā apo mahinā sindhur anyāḥ ||
  • pra kṣodasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with great effort" or "with determination." It signifies the importance of putting in hard work and dedication to achieve one's goals.
  • dhāyasā: This word refers to "thought" or "intellect." It emphasizes the role of wisdom and contemplation in decision-making and actions.
  • sasra eṣā: This can be translated as "this flow" or "this stream." It symbolizes the continuous flow of knowledge and creativity, akin to a river that nourishes life.
  • sarasvatī: Sarasvatī is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning in Hindu mythology. Her invocation here highlights the importance of education and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • dharuṇam: This term means "strength" or "support." It suggests that one should seek strength in their endeavors, whether through personal resilience or support from others.
  • āyasī pūḥ: This phrase can be understood as "may you be blessed" or "may you prosper." It is a wish for success and well-being in one's pursuits.
  • prabābadhānā: This word means "with great effort" or "with determination." It reinforces the idea that success requires hard work and perseverance.
  • rathyeva yāti: This translates to "like a chariot moves." It symbolizes progress and the journey of life, suggesting that one should move forward with purpose and direction.
  • viśvā apo: This phrase means "all waters" or "universal waters." It represents the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of collaboration and unity.
  • mahinā sindhur anyāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the great river and other waters." It signifies the abundance of resources and opportunities available to those who seek them.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of hard work, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge in achieving success. It encourages individuals to seek strength and support in their endeavors while recognizing the interconnectedness of all life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, career development, and personal growth. By embodying the principles of determination, collaboration, and continuous learning, individuals can navigate their journeys with purpose and achieve their goals. The invocation of Sarasvatī serves as a reminder to value knowledge and creativity as essential components of success.
