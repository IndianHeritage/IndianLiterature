यथा विप्रस्य मनुषो हविभिर देवां आयजः कविभिः कविः सन् |एवा होतः सत्यतर त्वम् अद्याग्ने मन्द्रया जुह्वा यजस्व ||
yathā viprasya manuṣo havirbhir devām̐ ayajaḥ kavibhiḥ kaviḥ san | evā hotaḥ satyatara tvam adyāgne mandrayā juhvā yajasva ||
  • Mantra: "yathā viprasya manuṣo havirbhir devām̐ ayajaḥ kavibhiḥ kaviḥ san | evā hotaḥ satyatara tvam adyāgne mandrayā juhvā yajasva ||"
  • Translation: "As the wise (vipra) man offers his oblations (havi) to the gods (devā), so too, O Agni, you, the poet (kavi), are invoked. Today, as the most truthful (satyatara), may you, O Agni, be pleased with our offerings and grant us your blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yathā: As
    • viprasya: Of the wise or learned
    • manuṣo: Man or human
    • havirbhir: With offerings
    • devām̐: To the gods
    • ayajaḥ: Invoked or worshipped
    • kavibhiḥ: By the poets or seers
    • kaviḥ: The poet or seer
    • san: Being or existing
    • evā: Thus or indeed
    • hotaḥ: The priest or one who invokes
    • satyatara: More truthful
    • tvam: You
    • adyāgne: Today, O Agni
    • mandrayā: With our offerings
    • juhvā: We offer
    • yajasva: May you accept or grant
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the role of the individual in connecting with the divine through offerings.
    • It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, where the wise man (vipra) invokes the gods (devā) through sincere offerings.
    • Agni, as the fire god, symbolizes purity and the transformative power of fire, representing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of intention and sincerity in our actions, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual practices.
    • For example, when we engage in community service or charitable acts, approaching these with a wise and sincere heart can lead to more meaningful outcomes.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who invoke the principles of wisdom and truthfulness in their decision-making processes can foster a culture of integrity and trust within their organizations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, truth, and divine blessings.
    • By embodying these principles in our daily lives, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both for ourselves and for those around us.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek a deeper connection with the divine through our actions and intentions, fostering a sense of purpose and community.
