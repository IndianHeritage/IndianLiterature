वि ते विश्वग वातजूतासो अग्ने भामासः शुचे शुचयश्चरण्ति |तुविमरक्षासो दिव्या नवग्वा वनाः वनन्ति धृतता रुजन्तः ||
vi te viṣvag vātajūtāso agne bhāmāsaḥ śuce śucayaś caranti | tuvimrakṣāso divyā navagvā vanā vananti dhṛṣatā rujantaḥ ||
  • vi te viṣvag vātajūtāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "to you, O universal one, who is connected with the winds." Here, "viṣvag" signifies universality, while "vātajūtāso" refers to the connection with the winds, symbolizing the omnipresence of the divine.
  • agne bhāmāsaḥ: "O Agni, the radiant one." Agni represents fire, which is not only a physical element but also a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification in life. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing the transformative power of energy.
  • śuce śucayaś caranti: This translates to "the pure ones move in purity." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In everyday life, this can be seen in practices like meditation, yoga, and ethical living, where individuals strive to maintain purity in their lives.
  • tuvimrakṣāso divyā: "Those who protect you are divine." This suggests that there are divine forces or energies that safeguard and guide us. In real life, this can be related to the support we receive from family, friends, and mentors who act as protectors in our journey.
  • navagvā vanā vananti: "The ninefold cows roam in the forests." Cows are often seen as symbols of abundance and nourishment. The "ninefold" can represent completeness or wholeness. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of seeking sustenance and abundance in nature, as well as the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment.
  • dhṛṣatā rujantaḥ: "With courage, they overcome suffering." This phrase highlights the importance of courage in facing life's challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who confront adversity with strength, whether it be in personal struggles, health issues, or societal challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of universality, purity, divine protection, abundance, and courage. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected with the universe and that maintaining purity in our lives can lead to divine support and abundance. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal growth, relationships, and interactions with the world around us, encouraging us to live with intention, seek nourishment from nature, and face challenges with bravery.
