स नो विश्वेभिर देवभिर ऊर्जो नपाद भद्रशोच || रयिं देहि विश्ववारम् ||
sa no viśvebhir devebhir ūrjo napād bhadraśoce | rayiṁ dehi viśvavāram ||
  • sa no viśvebhir devebhir: This phrase can be translated as "May all the gods (devas) be favorable to us." It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the interconnectedness of humanity with the divine forces. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people seek blessings from higher powers for success and well-being, such as during religious ceremonies or rituals.
  • ūrjo napād bhadraśoce: This part means "May we be endowed with strength and brilliance." Here, "ūrjo" refers to energy or strength, while "napād" signifies the act of being blessed or endowed. "Bhadraśoce" implies auspiciousness and brightness. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals striving for personal growth, health, and enlightenment, such as through education, fitness, or spiritual practices.
  • rayiṁ dehi viśvavāram: This translates to "Grant us wealth and prosperity." "Rayiṁ" refers to wealth or resources, while "viśvavāram" suggests universal or all-encompassing blessings. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a prayer for abundance, whether it be financial stability, emotional richness, or fulfilling relationships. Many people engage in practices like gratitude journaling or community service to attract positive energy and abundance into their lives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "sa no viśvebhir devebhir ūrjo napād bhadraśoce | rayiṁ dehi viśvavāram ||" encapsulates a profound desire for divine favor, strength, and prosperity. Its significance lies in the recognition of the need for support from higher powers, the pursuit of personal and collective strength, and the aspiration for abundance in various forms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek blessings through prayer, engage in self-improvement, and foster a mindset of abundance, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and harmonious life.
