युवं मित्रेण जनं यतथः सं च नयथः |मा मघोनः परि ख्यातं मो अस्माकं ऋषीणां गोपीथे न उरुष्यतं ||
yuvam mitremaṁ janaṁ yatathaḥ saṁ ca nayathaḥ | mā maghonaḥ pari khyatam mo asmākam ṛṣīṇāṁ gopīthe na uruṣyatam ||
  • yuvam: This word translates to "you" in the plural form, indicating a collective or community.
  • mitremaṁ: This means "friendship" or "allies," emphasizing the importance of unity and camaraderie among people.
  • janaṁ: This translates to "people" or "community," highlighting the social aspect of human existence.
  • yatathaḥ: This can be interpreted as "striving" or "working together," suggesting a collective effort towards a common goal.
  • saṁ ca: This means "and" or "together," reinforcing the idea of unity.
  • nayathaḥ: This translates to "lead" or "guide," indicating the role of leadership in a community.
  • mā: This means "not," serving as a negation in the context of the mantra.
  • maghonaḥ: This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting the importance of abundance in life.
  • pari khyatam: This can be interpreted as "well-known" or "renowned," indicating the value of reputation and recognition.
  • mo: This means "us" or "our," emphasizing a sense of belonging and community.
  • asmākam: This translates to "our," further reinforcing the collective identity.
  • ṛṣīṇāṁ: This means "of the sages" or "wise ones," indicating the importance of wisdom and guidance from learned individuals.
  • gopīthe: This can be interpreted as "in the protection" or "under the care," suggesting a sense of security within the community.
  • na: This means "not," again serving as a negation.
  • uruṣyatam: This translates to "become weak" or "diminish," indicating a warning against losing strength or unity.

The mantra emphasizes the significance of community, friendship, and collective effort. It calls for unity among people, urging them to work together under wise leadership to achieve prosperity and maintain strength. The negation of weakness suggests that a community must remain vigilant and united to avoid decline.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Building: In neighborhoods or local organizations, fostering friendships and collaboration can lead to a stronger, more supportive environment.
  • Teamwork in Workplaces: Encouraging teamwork and collective effort in professional settings can enhance productivity and create a positive work culture.
  • Social Movements: Activism often relies on unity and collective action to bring about social change, highlighting the importance of working together for a common cause.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, friendship, and collective effort in achieving prosperity and strength within a community. It encourages individuals to lead and guide one another, ensuring that the community remains resilient and vibrant.
