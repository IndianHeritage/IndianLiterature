समिद्धो अद्य मनुषो दुरोणे देवो देवान यजसि जातवेदः |आ च वह मित्रमहश्चिकित्वान त्वं दूतः कविर असि प्रचेताः ||
samiddho adya manuṣo duroṇe devo devān yajasi jātavedaḥ | ā ca vaha mitramahaś cikitvān tvaṁ dūtaḥ kavir asi pracetāḥ ||
  • Translation: "Today, the man who is kindled (with knowledge) offers to the gods, the divine beings, the one who knows all (the Jātaveda). And you, O messenger, who are wise and knowledgeable, bring forth the great friend."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • samiddho: kindled, ignited, representing enlightenment or knowledge.
    • adya: today, indicating the present moment, the importance of the now.
    • manuṣo: man or human, emphasizing the role of humanity in rituals.
    • duroṇe: to the gods, referring to divine beings or higher powers.
    • devo devān: divine beings, highlighting the reverence for deities.
    • yajasi: you offer, indicating the act of worship or sacrifice.
    • jātavedaḥ: the one who knows all, often referring to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • ā ca vaha: and bring forth, a call to action.
    • mitramahaś: great friend, symbolizing the importance of friendship and community.
    • cikitvān: wise, knowledgeable, indicating the qualities of a good messenger.
    • tvaṁ: you, addressing the messenger directly.
    • dūtaḥ: messenger, representing communication between the divine and human realms.
    • kavir: wise one, indicating a person of great knowledge.
    • asi: you are, affirming the identity of the messenger.
    • pracetāḥ: knowledgeable, wise, emphasizing the importance of wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of being "kindled" can be seen in individuals who pursue education and self-improvement, igniting their potential to contribute positively to society.
    • The idea of offering to the gods can be paralleled with acts of kindness and charity, where individuals give back to their communities, reflecting the essence of yajna (sacrifice).
    • The role of a "messenger" can be likened to leaders or mentors who guide others with wisdom and knowledge, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, community, and the act of offering. It highlights the importance of being present in the moment and recognizing the divine in everyday actions. In real life, this can be applied through education, community service, and mentorship, fostering a society that values wisdom and connection. The call to action encourages individuals to be proactive in their relationships and contributions, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
