इति चिद्धि त्वा धनां जयंते मादे-मादे अनुमदन्ति विप्राः |ओजीयो धृष्णो स्थिरम् आ तनुष्व मा त्वा दभन यातुधानाः दुरेवाः ||
iti cid dhi tvā dhanā jayantam made-made anumadanti viprāḥ | ojīyo dhṛṣṇo sthiram ā tanuṣva mā tvā dabhan yātudhānā durevāḥ ||
  • iti cid dhi tvā dhanā jayantam made-made anumadanti viprāḥ
    • iti - This word signifies "thus" or "in this way," indicating a conclusion or a statement of affirmation.
    • cid - Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it refers to the state of being aware or enlightened.
    • dhi - This translates to "intellect" or "understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in one's actions.
    • tvā - This means "you," addressing the divine or the higher consciousness.
    • dhanā - Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," symbolizing abundance in life.
    • jayantam - This means "victorious" or "triumphant," suggesting success and achievement.
    • made-made - This phrase can be interpreted as "with joy" or "in ecstasy," indicating a state of blissful awareness.
    • anumadanti - Meaning "they approve" or "they support," referring to the wise or learned individuals who endorse this state of being.
    • viprāḥ - This translates to "the wise" or "the learned," indicating those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • ojīyo dhṛṣṇo sthiram ā tanuṣva mā tvā dabhan yātudhānā durevāḥ
    • ojīyo - This means "energetic" or "full of vigor," emphasizing the importance of vitality in life.
    • dhṛṣṇo - Refers to "courageous" or "bold," highlighting the need for bravery in facing challenges.
    • sthiram - Meaning "steadfast" or "stable," it suggests the importance of being grounded and firm in one's beliefs.
    • ā - This word means "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
    • tanuṣva - This translates to "become" or "manifest," encouraging the realization of one's potential.
    • mā - Meaning "do not," it serves as a caution against negative influences.
    • tvā - Again referring to "you," addressing the individual or the divine.
    • dabhan - This means "to bind" or "to restrain," indicating the forces that may limit one's potential.
    • yātudhānā - Refers to "evil spirits" or "obstacles," symbolizing challenges that one may face.
    • durevāḥ - Meaning "far away" or "distant," suggesting the need to keep negative influences at bay.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of consciousness, wisdom, and courage in achieving prosperity and success. It encourages individuals to remain steadfast and energetic while being aware of the potential obstacles that may arise. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as pursuing career goals, personal development, or overcoming challenges in relationships. By embodying the qualities of awareness, courage, and stability, one can navigate through life's complexities and achieve a state of fulfillment and joy. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek support from the wise and to remain vigilant against negative influences, ultimately guiding individuals towards a prosperous and victorious life.
