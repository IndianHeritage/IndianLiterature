त्वमित्सप्रथाऽस्य अग्नेत्रातार ऋतस कविः |त्वां विप्रासः समिधाना दीदिवा आ विवासन्ति वेधसः ||
tvam it saprathā asy agne trātar ṛtas kaviḥ | tvāṁ viprāsaḥ samidhāna dīdiva ā vivāsanti vedhasaḥ ||
  • tvam it saprathā asy agne trātar ṛtas kaviḥ
    • tvam - "You" or "Thou": This refers to the divine or the sacred fire, Agni, which is a central figure in Vedic rituals.
    • it - "here" or "in this place": Indicates the presence of Agni in the ritual space.
    • saprathā - "with great strength" or "with all your might": This emphasizes the power and energy of Agni.
    • asy - "of this": Refers to the offerings and the context of the ritual.
    • agne - "O Agni": Directly addressing the fire deity.
    • trātar - "protector" or "savior": Agni is invoked as a protector in the lives of the devotees.
    • ṛtas - "truth" or "cosmic order": This signifies the importance of maintaining harmony and truth in the universe.
    • kaviḥ - "the wise one" or "the poet": Agni is also seen as a source of inspiration and wisdom.
  • tvāṁ viprāsaḥ samidhāna dīdiva ā vivāsanti vedhasaḥ
    • tvāṁ - "you": Again referring to Agni.
    • viprāsaḥ - "the learned ones" or "the wise": This refers to the sages and scholars who perform the rituals.
    • samidhāna - "offering" or "kindling": This indicates the act of making offerings to Agni.
    • dīdiva - "shining" or "illuminating": Refers to the light and warmth that Agni provides.
    • ā - "from" or "out of": Indicates the source of the offerings.
    • vivāsanti - "they bring forth" or "they invoke": This signifies the action of invoking Agni through offerings.
    • vedhasaḥ - "of the wise" or "of the learned": Refers to the knowledge and wisdom associated with the Vedas.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of Agni as a divine protector and a source of wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in invoking divine presence and maintaining cosmic order. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where fire is used in ceremonies, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds. The mantra serves as a reminder of the need for wisdom and truth in our actions, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and uphold righteousness in their lives.
