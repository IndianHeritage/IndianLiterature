आ यो विवाय साचथाय दैव्य इन्द्राय विष्णुः सुखृत सुखृत्तरः |वेधा अजिन्वत त्रिषधस्थ आर्यम ऋतस्य भागे यजमानं आब्जत ||
ā yo vivāya sacathāya daivya indrāya viṣṇuḥ sukṛte sukṛttaraḥ | vedhā ajinvat triṣadhastha āryam ṛtasya bhāge yajamānam ābhajat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yo: He who
    • vivāya: by uniting
    • sacathāya: for the purpose of truth
    • daivya: divine
    • indrāya: to Indra
    • viṣṇuḥ: to Vishnu
    • sukṛte: through good deeds
    • sukṛttaraḥ: even better deeds
    • vedhā: the one who knows
    • ajinvat: who is unblemished
    • triṣadhastha: who is in three places
    • āryam: noble
    • ṛtasya: of truth
    • bhāge: in the part
    • yajamānam: the one who is being worshipped
    • ābhajat: may he accept
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies of Indra and Vishnu, emphasizing the importance of unity and truth.
    • The phrase "He who by uniting for the purpose of truth" suggests that collaboration and harmony are essential for achieving higher goals.
    • Indra represents strength and power, while Vishnu symbolizes preservation and protection, indicating a balance between these forces in life.
    • The mention of "good deeds" and "even better deeds" highlights the significance of moral actions and their impact on one's spiritual journey.
    • The term "unblemished" refers to purity of character, which is crucial in both personal and communal contexts.
    • "In three places" can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual realms, suggesting that one should strive for balance in all aspects of life.
    • The invocation of "noble" and "truth" emphasizes the importance of integrity and righteousness in one's actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, collaboration among team members for a common goal reflects the essence of "uniting for the purpose of truth." Successful projects often arise from teamwork and shared values.
    • In community service, individuals coming together to help those in need exemplify the divine qualities of Indra and Vishnu, showcasing strength and preservation of societal values.
    • Practicing good deeds, such as volunteering or helping others, aligns with the mantra's emphasis on moral actions leading to spiritual growth.
    • Maintaining integrity in personal relationships, where honesty and trust are paramount, reflects the noble qualities mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, truth, and moral integrity in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a path of righteousness, encouraging them to engage in good deeds and maintain purity of character.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various contexts, including personal relationships, community involvement, and professional environments, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
