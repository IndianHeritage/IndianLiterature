उच्चा दिभो दुधितः प्रात्नवान नो भरद्वाजवत विधते मगhoni |सुवीरं रयिं गृणते रिरिह्य उरुगायम अधि धेहि श्रवो नः ||
ucchā divo duhitaḥ pratnavan no bharadvājavad vidhate maghoni | suvīraṁ rayiṁ gṛṇate rirīhy urugāyam adhi dhehi śravo naḥ ||
  • ucchā divo duhitaḥ: This phrase translates to "the daughter of the high heavens." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent aspirations or goals that are lofty and noble, akin to aiming for the stars in our endeavors.
  • pratnavan no bharadvājavad: This means "may she, like Bharadvaja, bestow upon us." Bharadvaja is a revered sage in Vedic literature, known for his wisdom and contributions. This part emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and blessings from wise figures in our lives.
  • vidhate maghoni: This translates to "may she create abundance." It signifies the desire for prosperity and wealth, not just in material terms but also in knowledge, relationships, and overall well-being.
  • suvīraṁ rayiṁ: This means "the excellent wealth." It highlights the value of quality over quantity, suggesting that true wealth encompasses not just riches but also virtues, health, and happiness.
  • gṛṇate rirīhy: This translates to "may she sing praises." It indicates the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation for the blessings we receive, fostering a positive and uplifting environment.
  • urugāyam adhi dhehi: This means "may she place us under the protection of the vast." It suggests seeking shelter and guidance from a higher power or the universe, emphasizing the need for support in our journeys.
  • śravo naḥ: This translates to "may our fame be established." It signifies the aspiration for recognition and respect in society, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence in their pursuits.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking guidance, abundance, and recognition while emphasizing the importance of lofty aspirations and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, career growth, and community building. For instance, when pursuing a career, one might look up to mentors (like Bharadvaja) for wisdom, aim for excellence in their work, and express gratitude for the support received. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to align our goals with higher values and to seek a holistic form of wealth that enriches our lives and the lives of those around us.
