अर्वाण्चं दैव्यं जनम अग्ने यक्ष्व सहूतिभिः |अयं सोमः सुदानवस् तं पात तिरोहण्यं ||
arvāñcaṁ daivyaṁ janam agne yakṣva sahūtibhiḥ | ayaṁ somaḥ sudānavas tam pāta tiroahnyam ||
  • arvāñcaṁ: This term refers to the "divine" or "celestial" aspect of existence. It signifies the higher realms of consciousness and the spiritual dimensions of life.
  • daivyaṁ: Meaning "divine" or "godly," this word emphasizes the connection between humanity and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • janam: This translates to "people" or "community." It highlights the importance of collective existence and the role of society in spiritual and material pursuits.
  • agne: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, this term symbolizes transformation, energy, and the sacred fire that is central to Vedic rituals.
  • yakṣva: This means "to grant" or "to bestow." It indicates a plea for blessings and support from the divine.
  • sahūtibhiḥ: This term translates to "with the help of the assembled." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals.
  • ayaṁ: Meaning "this," it brings focus to the present moment and the immediate reality we are addressing.
  • somaḥ: Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, it symbolizes nourishment, vitality, and the essence of life.
  • sudānavas: This term means "well-nourished" or "prosperous." It signifies abundance and the blessings of health and wealth.
  • tam: Meaning "that," it points to the object of our focus or desire.
  • pāta: This translates to "protect" or "guard." It is a plea for divine protection and guidance.
  • tiroahnyam: This term means "in the night" or "during the dark." It signifies the challenges and uncertainties that one may face.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for divine assistance and protection, emphasizing the importance of community and collective effort in the pursuit of prosperity and well-being. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, rituals, or even in personal endeavors where support from others is crucial. It reminds us that while we seek individual success, it is often through the strength of our connections and the blessings of the divine that we can navigate the challenges of life, especially during difficult times. This mantra serves as a reminder to honor both the spiritual and communal aspects of our existence, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
