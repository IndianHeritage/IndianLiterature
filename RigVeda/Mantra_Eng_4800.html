पुरूतमम् पुरूणां स्तोत्रिणां विवाचि |वाजेभिर वाजयताम् ||
purūtamam purūṇāṁ stotṝṇāṁ vivāci | vājebhir vājayatām ||
  • Mantra: "purūtamam purūṇāṁ stotṝṇāṁ vivāci | vājebhir vājayatām ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "The most excellent among the excellent, the one who is praised by the praisers, may He grant us strength and vigor."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Purūtamam: Refers to the "most excellent" or "supreme" among all.
    • Purūṇāṁ: Means "among the many" or "among the excellent."
    • Stotṝṇāṁ: Refers to "those who praise" or "the praisers."
    • Vivāci: Means "to speak" or "to express."
    • Vājebhir: Refers to "strength" or "vigor."
    • Vājayatām: Means "may He grant" or "may He bestow."
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring excellence in various forms, whether in individuals, ideas, or actions.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various fields such as sports, where athletes strive to be the best, or in academics, where students aim for excellence in their studies.
    • For example, a student who consistently performs well in exams and is praised by teachers embodies the essence of "purūtamam purūṇāṁ." Their achievements inspire others to pursue excellence.
    • In the workplace, a team that collaborates effectively and produces outstanding results can be seen as "the praisers" of their field, contributing to the overall success of the organization.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to strive for excellence in all endeavors, whether personal or professional.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the efforts of others who excel, fostering a culture of praise and motivation.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in daily life by setting high standards for oneself and acknowledging the achievements of peers, thereby creating an environment that promotes growth and success.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires a collective pursuit of excellence, urging individuals to seek strength and vigor in their actions and contributions.
