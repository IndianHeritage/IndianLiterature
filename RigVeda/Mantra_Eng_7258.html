न सīm अदेव आपद इषं दीर्घायो मर्त्यः |एतग्वा चिद य एतशा युयोजते हरि इन्द्रो युयोजते ||
na sīm adeva āpad iṣaṁ dīrghāyo martyaḥ | etagvā cid ya etaśā yuyojate harī indro yuyojate ||
  • na sīm adeva āpad iṣaṁ dīrghāyo martyaḥ
    • na sīm: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not let" or "may not be." It suggests a plea or a wish for protection.
    • adeva: This term refers to "the divine" or "gods." It indicates a connection to higher powers or spiritual entities.
    • āpad: This word means "calamity" or "disaster." It signifies challenges or adversities that one may face in life.
    • iṣaṁ: This translates to "the aim" or "the goal." It implies a focus on what one desires or strives for.
    • dīrghāyo: This term means "long life" or "longevity." It emphasizes the importance of health and vitality.
    • martyaḥ: This refers to "mortals" or "human beings." It highlights the human condition and our vulnerabilities.
  • etagvā cid ya etaśā yuyojate harī indro yuyojate
    • etagvā: This can be understood as "whoever" or "anyone." It indicates inclusivity in the message.
    • cid: This word means "indeed" or "certainly." It adds emphasis to the statement that follows.
    • ya etaśā: This translates to "whoever connects with this." It suggests the importance of establishing a relationship or connection.
    • yuyojate: This means "is united" or "is joined." It signifies the act of coming together or aligning with a purpose.
    • harī: This term refers to "the divine" or "the Lord." It indicates a connection to a higher power.
    • indro: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • yuyojate: Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the idea of unity and connection.

Conclusion:

The mantra expresses a profound desire for protection from calamities and the pursuit of longevity for mortals. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine and aligning oneself with higher powers to achieve one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking spiritual guidance during challenging times, such as illness or personal crises.
  • Engaging in practices that promote health and well-being, thereby striving for a long and fulfilling life.
  • Building a community or support system that fosters unity and strength, much like the connection to the divine emphasized in the mantra.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between human effort, divine support, and the importance of community in overcoming life's challenges and achieving longevity.
