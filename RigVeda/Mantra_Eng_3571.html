स इति क्षेति सुधिता ओकसि स्वे तस्मा इळा पिन्वते विश्वदानीम् |तस्मै विश्वः स्वयम एव नमन्ते यस्मिन् ब्रह्मा राजनि पूर्व एति ||
sa it kṣeti sudhita okasi sve tasmā iḻā pinvate viśvadānīm | tasmai viśaḥ svayam evā namante yasmin brahmā rājani pūrva eti ||
  • sa it kṣeti: This phrase can be translated as "He who knows" or "He who perceives." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in understanding the world around us.
  • sudhita okasi: "In the well-thought-out place" suggests a space or state of mind that is conducive to wisdom and clarity. It implies that knowledge flourishes in an environment that is nurtured and thoughtfully cultivated.
  • sve tasmā iḻā pinvate: This can be interpreted as "therefore, the divine energy (or inspiration) flows." It indicates that when one is in a state of knowledge and awareness, divine inspiration or creativity can manifest.
  • viśvadānīm: This term refers to "the all-giving" or "the universal benefactor." It signifies a force or entity that provides abundance and support to all beings.
  • tasmai viśaḥ svayam evā namante: This translates to "to him, all beings bow down." It highlights the reverence and respect that all creatures have for the source of knowledge and wisdom.
  • yasmin brahmā rājani pūrva eti: This phrase means "in whom the supreme consciousness (Brahman) resides, and who is the ruler." It points to the idea that true authority and leadership come from a deep understanding of the universe and oneself.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, awareness, and divine inspiration in our lives. It teaches us that when we cultivate a thoughtful and nurturing environment, we can access universal wisdom and creativity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and personal development. For instance, educators who create a supportive learning environment foster knowledge and creativity in their students. Similarly, leaders who embody wisdom and understanding inspire their teams to achieve greater heights. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, reverence, and the divine, guiding us towards a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
