यस्याश्वासः प्रदिशि यस्य गावो यस्य ग्रामाः यस्य विश्वे रथासः |यः सूर्यं य उषसं जजाना यो अपां नेता स जनास इन्द्रः ||
yasyāśvāsaḥ pradiśi yasya gāvo yasya grāmā yasya viśve rathāsaḥ | yaḥ sūryaṁ ya uṣasaṁ jajāna yo apāṁ netā sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • yasyāśvāsaḥ pradiśi: This phrase translates to "whose breath is in the directions." It signifies the omnipresence of a divine force that is present in all directions, much like the air we breathe. In real life, this can be likened to the idea that energy and life force are everywhere around us, influencing our environment and experiences.
  • yasya gāvo: This means "whose cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture. In modern terms, this can represent the resources and abundance one has in life, such as financial stability or supportive relationships.
  • yasya grāmā: This translates to "whose villages." It emphasizes community and the importance of social connections. In today's context, it can be related to the significance of belonging to a community or network that supports and nurtures individuals.
  • yasya viśve rathāsaḥ: This means "whose all chariots." Chariots symbolize movement, progress, and the journey of life. In real life, this can be interpreted as the various paths we take in our personal and professional lives, and the importance of having the right tools and support to navigate these journeys.
  • yaḥ sūryaṁ: This translates to "who has the sun." The sun is a powerful symbol of energy, vitality, and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can represent the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, as well as the importance of maintaining a positive outlook in life.
  • ya uṣasaṁ jajāna: This means "who has given birth to the dawn." Dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In everyday life, this can be seen as the potential for growth and renewal, encouraging individuals to embrace change and seek new experiences.
  • yo apāṁ netā: This translates to "who is the leader of the waters." Water is essential for life and represents nourishment and emotional well-being. In a real-life context, this can signify the importance of emotional intelligence and the ability to navigate one's feelings and relationships effectively.
  • sa janāsa indraḥ: This means "that Indra is the leader of the people." Indra is often regarded as a king of gods and a symbol of strength and leadership. In modern terms, this can represent the qualities of a good leader, such as wisdom, courage, and the ability to inspire others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, abundance, and the divine presence in all aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of community, resources, and leadership while encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and embrace new beginnings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to appreciate the support systems around us, to strive for personal growth, and to lead with integrity and compassion in our communities.
