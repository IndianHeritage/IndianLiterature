दुहान ऊधर दिव्यम् मधु प्रियं प्रात्नं सदस्थम् आसदत् |आप्रच्छ्यं धरणं वाज्य अर्षति नृभिर धूतो विचक्षणः ||
duhāna ūdhar divyam madhu priyam pratnaṁ sadhastham āsadat | āpṛcchyaṁ dharuṇaṁ vājy arṣati nṛbhir dhūto vicakṣaṇaḥ ||
  • duhāna: This term can be interpreted as "the act of milking" or "extraction." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the process of deriving nourishment or benefits from a source.
  • ūdhar: This word means "to lift" or "to elevate." It signifies the act of raising something to a higher state or condition.
  • divyam: Translated as "divine" or "heavenly," this term refers to something that is beyond the ordinary, often associated with spiritual or celestial qualities.
  • madhu: Meaning "honey," it represents sweetness, pleasure, and the rewards of life. Honey is often used as a metaphor for the joys and richness of existence.
  • pryatnaṁ: This translates to "effort" or "endeavor." It emphasizes the importance of striving and working towards a goal.
  • sadhastham: This word means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It indicates a collective effort or the importance of community.
  • āsadat: This term means "to sit" or "to dwell." It suggests a state of being present or engaged in a particular situation.
  • āpṛcchyaṁ: This can be interpreted as "inquiry" or "questioning." It highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding.
  • dharuṇaṁ: Meaning "strong" or "powerful," this term signifies resilience and the ability to withstand challenges.
  • vājy: This word refers to "the one who speaks" or "the voice." It emphasizes communication and expression.
  • arṣati: Translated as "to shine" or "to radiate," it indicates the manifestation of brilliance or excellence.
  • nṛbhir: This term means "by men" or "among people," emphasizing human agency and action.
  • dhūto: Meaning "shaken" or "disturbed," it suggests a state of change or transformation.
  • vicakṣaṇaḥ: This word translates to "wise" or "perceptive," indicating the importance of wisdom and insight in navigating life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for higher knowledge and understanding through collective effort and inquiry. It emphasizes the importance of community, resilience, and wisdom in achieving one's goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, or personal development. By working together and seeking knowledge, individuals can elevate themselves and their communities, leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life.
