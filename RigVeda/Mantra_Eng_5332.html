कीरिश्चिद्धि त्वामवसे जुहावेशानमिन्द्र सौभगस्य भूरेः |अवो बभूथा शतमूते अस्मे अभिक्षत्तुस त्वावतो वरूताः ||
kīriś cid dhi tvām avase juhāveśānam indra saubhagasya bhūreḥ | avo babhūtha śatamūte asme abhikṣattus tvāvato varūtā ||
  • kīriś cid dhi tvām avase juhāveśānam indra saubhagasya bhūreḥ
    • kīriś: This word refers to "fame" or "glory." In real life, this can be related to the recognition one receives for their achievements, such as awards in various fields like sports, academics, or arts.
    • cid: This means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the certainty of the statement being made.
    • dhi: This translates to "intellect" or "wisdom." In practical terms, it can be associated with the ability to make sound decisions based on knowledge and experience.
    • tvām: This means "you," indicating a direct address to a deity or a higher power, in this case, Indra.
    • avase: This means "to invoke" or "to call upon." In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek blessings or guidance.
    • juhāveśānam: This refers to "offering" or "sacrifice." In modern contexts, this can be likened to acts of charity or service to others.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength and power. In real life, this can symbolize leadership qualities in individuals.
    • saubhagasya: This means "of good fortune" or "prosperity." It relates to the blessings of wealth and success in various endeavors.
    • bhūreḥ: This translates to "of abundance" or "plenty," indicating a state of having more than enough.
  • avo babhūtha śatamūte asme abhikṣattus tvāvato varūtā
    • avo: This means "may you" or "let there be," expressing a wish or desire.
    • babhūtha: This translates to "become" or "come to pass," indicating a transformation or realization of potential.
    • śatamūte: This refers to "hundredfold" or "many," suggesting a multiplication of blessings or benefits.
    • asme: This means "to us," indicating a collective appeal or prayer for blessings.
    • abhikṣattus: This translates to "may you protect" or "may you bestow," indicating a request for safeguarding or support.
    • tvāvato: This means "by your grace" or "through your power," emphasizing reliance on divine assistance.
    • varūtā: This refers to "protection" or "shelter," symbolizing safety and security in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for invoking the blessings of Indra, the deity of strength and prosperity, to bestow fame, wisdom, and abundance upon the devotees. It signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in achieving success and well-being in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during significant events such as starting a new venture, seeking success in exams, or during rituals for prosperity and protection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace in the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
