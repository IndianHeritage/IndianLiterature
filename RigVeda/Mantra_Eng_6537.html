उशनां काव्यस् त्वा निहोतारम् असादयत् |आयजिं त्वा मनवे जातवेदसम् ||
uśanā kāvyas tvā ni hotāram asādayat | āyajiṁ tvā manave jātavedasam ||
  • uśanā: This term refers to the dawn or the light of the morning. In a broader sense, it symbolizes new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • kāvyas: This word is associated with poetry or the poetic arts. It signifies creativity, expression, and the beauty of language.
  • tvā: This means "you" and is often used to address a deity or a revered figure, indicating a direct invocation.
  • ni hotāram: This phrase translates to "the one who is invoked" or "the priest." It refers to the person who performs rituals and calls upon the divine.
  • asādayat: This means "to bring forth" or "to manifest." It indicates the act of making something present or real.
  • āyajiṁ: This term means "to worship" or "to offer." It signifies the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • tvā: Again, this refers to "you," emphasizing the direct relationship with the divine being addressed.
  • manave: This word means "to the mind" or "to the thinker." It highlights the importance of intellect and consciousness in spiritual practices.
  • jātavedasam: This term refers to the "one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It is often associated with Agni, the fire god, who is a symbol of knowledge and transformation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine light (uśanā) and the creative arts (kāvyas), invoking the presence of a priest (ni hotāram) to bring forth (asādayat) worship (āyajiṁ) directed towards the mind (manave) and the all-knowing (jātavedasam). It emphasizes the importance of creativity, intellect, and the divine in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Creative Pursuits: Artists, writers, and musicians can invoke this mantra to seek inspiration and clarity in their creative processes.
  • Rituals and Worship: During religious ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invite divine presence and blessings, enhancing the spiritual atmosphere.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the themes of enlightenment and knowledge, using the mantra as a reminder to seek wisdom and understanding in their daily lives.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interplay between creativity, intellect, and the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking higher knowledge and inspiration in our lives, whether through artistic expression, spiritual practices, or personal development. By embracing these elements, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
