कुहा स्थः कुहा जग्मथुः कुहा श्येनैव पेत्तथुः |अन्ति षड् भूतु वाम अवः ||
kuha sthaḥ kuha jagmathuḥ kuha śyeneva petathuḥ | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kuha sthaḥ kuha jagmathuḥ kuha śyeneva petathuḥ | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kuha: Where
    • sthaḥ: Are (or is located)
    • jagmathuḥ: They move or wander
    • śyeneva: Like a hawk
    • petathuḥ: They fly or soar
    • anti: May they come
    • ṣad: Six
    • bhūtu: Beings or entities
    • vām: Your
    • avaḥ: May they be present or come to you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the whereabouts and movements of various entities.
    • It suggests a quest for understanding the nature of existence and the forces that govern life.
    • The imagery of a hawk soaring through the sky symbolizes clarity, vision, and the ability to rise above challenges.
    • The invocation of "six beings" can be interpreted as a call for the presence of various aspects of life, such as physical, emotional, mental, spiritual, social, and environmental well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek clarity and vision (like the hawk) to navigate challenges and guide their teams effectively.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on their own existence and the various roles they play, striving for balance among their responsibilities.
    • In environmental discussions, the mantra can remind us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our surroundings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking clarity and understanding in our lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize the various aspects of our existence and strive for harmony among them.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in meditation, self-reflection, and decision-making processes, guiding us to be more aware of our surroundings and the forces at play in our lives.
