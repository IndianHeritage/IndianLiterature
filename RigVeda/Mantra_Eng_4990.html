तां हुवे ययोर इदं पाप्ने विश्वं पुरा कृतम् | इन्द्राग्नि न मर्धतः ||
tā huve yayor idam papne viśvam purā kṛtam | indrāgnī na mardhataḥ ||
  • tā huve yayor idam papne viśvam purā kṛtam: This phrase can be translated to "I invoke that which was created in the past, which is universal." Here, the speaker is calling upon a universal force or energy that has existed since ancient times. It emphasizes the connection to the past and the importance of ancient wisdom.
  • indrāgnī na mardhataḥ: This part translates to "May Indra and Agni not be harmed." Indra and Agni are significant deities in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and fire. This invocation seeks protection and blessings from these powerful forces, ensuring that they remain unharmed and continue to provide their benefits.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of invoking ancient wisdom and the universal forces that govern our existence. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the need to respect and honor the energies that have shaped our world.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Environmental Awareness: Just as the mantra invokes universal forces, we can draw parallels to the need for environmental conservation. By respecting nature and its ancient wisdom, we can ensure a sustainable future.
    • Spiritual Practices: Many individuals engage in meditation or rituals that honor ancient traditions. This mantra can be used in such practices to connect with the past and seek guidance from powerful energies.
    • Community and Relationships: The invocation of Indra and Agni can symbolize the importance of strength and unity in relationships. By fostering strong bonds and protecting one another, we can create a harmonious community.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of ancient wisdom and the universal forces that shape our lives. By invoking these energies, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of our place in the world and strive for harmony in our relationships with nature and each other.
