नराशंसस्य महिमानमेषामुप स्तोषाम यजतस्य यज्ञैः |ये सुक्रतवः शुचयो धियंढाः स्वदन्ति देवाऽउभयानि हव्याः ||
narāśaṁsasya mahimānam eṣām upa stoṣāma yajatasya yajñaiḥ | ye sukratavaḥ śucayo dhiyaṁdhāḥ svadanti devā ubhayāni havyā ||
  • Translation: "We praise the greatness of the Narashamsa, who is to be worshipped with sacrifices. Those who are righteous and pure in their thoughts, the gods enjoy both kinds of offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Narāśaṁsa: Refers to a divine being or a person of great virtue and honor.
    • Mahimānam: Signifies greatness or glory.
    • Upa stoṣāma: Means to praise or worship.
    • Yajatasya: Pertains to the act of sacrifice or worship.
    • Yajñaiḥ: Refers to the offerings made during sacrifices.
    • Sukratavaḥ: Denotes those who are righteous or virtuous.
    • Śucayo: Means pure or clean, often in a moral or spiritual sense.
    • Dhiyaṁdhāḥ: Refers to those who possess wisdom or intelligence.
    • Svādanti: Means to enjoy or relish.
    • Devā: Refers to the gods or divine beings.
    • Ubhayāni: Means both kinds or aspects.
    • Havyā: Refers to offerings or sacrifices that are pleasing to the gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, including Hinduism, the act of making offerings or sacrifices is a way to express gratitude and seek blessings from the divine. For instance, during festivals like Diwali, people perform rituals and offer sweets and flowers to deities, embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, ethical business practices can be seen as a form of 'sacrifice' where companies prioritize social responsibility over mere profit. This aligns with the idea of being righteous (sukratavaḥ) and pure (śucayo) in one's actions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of righteousness, purity, and the act of worship. It teaches that true greatness is not just in divine beings but also in the virtues of individuals. In real life, this mantra encourages us to engage in ethical practices, be mindful of our thoughts and actions, and recognize the value of offering our best to the world around us. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious environment that is pleasing to both the divine and our community.
