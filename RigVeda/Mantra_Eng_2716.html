शासद वाह्निर दूहितुर नप्त्यं गाद विद्वाम् ऋतस्य दीधितिं सपर्यन् |पिता यत्र दूहितुः सेकम् ऋञ्जन सं शग्म्येन मनसा दधन्वे ||
śāsad vahnir duhitur naptyaṁ gād vidvām̐ ṛtasya dīdhitiṁ saparyan | pitā yatra duhituḥ sekam ṛñjan saṁ śagmyena manasā dadhanve ||
  • śāsad: This word can be interpreted as "to command" or "to direct." It signifies the authority or power to guide.
  • vahnir: Translated as "fire," it symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a source of life and sustenance.
  • duhitur: Meaning "of the daughter," it refers to the feminine aspect, often associated with nurturing and care.
  • naptyaṁ: This word means "of the granddaughter," indicating the continuation of lineage and the importance of family ties.
  • gād: Translated as "to know" or "to understand," it emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • vidvām̐: This term means "wise" or "knowledgeable," highlighting the value of wisdom in guiding actions.
  • ṛtasya: Meaning "of truth" or "of order," it signifies the cosmic order and the importance of living in harmony with it.
  • dīdhitiṁ: This word can be interpreted as "brightness" or "radiance," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • saparyan: Meaning "to worship" or "to honor," it emphasizes the importance of reverence in life.
  • pitā: Translated as "father," it represents authority, protection, and guidance within the family structure.
  • yatra: This word means "where," indicating a place or state of being.
  • duhituḥ: Again referring to "the daughter," reinforcing the significance of women in the family and society.
  • sekam: Meaning "to pour" or "to offer," it symbolizes the act of giving and sharing.
  • ṛñjan: This term can be interpreted as "to purify" or "to cleanse," emphasizing the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions.
  • saṁ: Meaning "together" or "with," it signifies unity and collaboration.
  • śagmyena: This word can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with vigor," highlighting the importance of strength in achieving goals.
  • manasā: Meaning "with the mind," it emphasizes the role of intention and thought in actions.
  • dadhanve: This term means "to wield" or "to carry," indicating the act of taking responsibility and action.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of family, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth. It highlights the roles of both men and women in nurturing and guiding the family unit, as well as the importance of knowledge and purity in one's actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as family dynamics, education, and community leadership. It serves as a reminder to honor our relationships, seek wisdom, and act with intention and strength in our daily lives.
