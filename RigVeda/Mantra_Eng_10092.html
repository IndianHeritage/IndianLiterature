निषु सीद गणपते गणेषु त्वाम आहुर विप्रतमं कवीनाम् |न ऋते त्वत क्रियते किं चनारे महाम् अर्कम् मगवञ्चित्रम् अर्च ||
ni ṣu sīda gaṇapate gaṇeṣu tvām āhur vipratamaṁ kavīnām | na ṛte tvat kriyate kiṁ canāre mahām arkam maghavañ citram arca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni ṣu sīda: "Do not sit" or "Do not remain."
    • gaṇapate: "O Lord of the multitudes" or "O Lord of the hosts."
    • gaṇeṣu: "In the groups" or "Among the assemblies."
    • tvām: "You" or "Your presence."
    • āhur: "They say" or "They declare."
    • vipratamaṁ: "The best among the wise" or "The foremost of the learned."
    • kavīnām: "Of the poets" or "Of the seers."
    • na ṛte: "Without you" or "In your absence."
    • tvat: "Your" or "Of you."
    • kriyate: "Is done" or "Is performed."
    • kiṁ cana: "Anything" or "Any action."
    • āre: "O Lord" or "O deity."
    • mahām: "Great" or "Mighty."
    • arkam: "Sun" or "Light."
    • maghavañ: "O bounteous one" or "O generous one."
    • citram: "Wonderful" or "Marvelous."
    • arca: "Worship" or "Adoration."
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine presence of Ganapati, the Lord of the multitudes, emphasizing the importance of his presence in any assembly or gathering.
    • The phrase "Do not sit" suggests an active engagement with the divine, urging devotees to remain vigilant and connected.
    • Ganapati is recognized as the foremost among the wise and learned, indicating that wisdom and knowledge are essential in any endeavor.
    • The mantra highlights that without the divine presence, no action can be successfully performed, underscoring the belief that spiritual support is crucial in all aspects of life.
    • The invocation of the "great sun" symbolizes enlightenment, guidance, and the dispelling of darkness, which can be interpreted as ignorance or obstacles in life.
    • The mention of "wonderful worship" signifies the beauty and significance of devotion, suggesting that true worship leads to a deeper understanding and connection with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, before starting a significant project, teams often seek blessings or conduct rituals to ensure success, reflecting the belief that divine presence is essential for fruitful outcomes.
    • During educational pursuits, students may recite this mantra to invoke wisdom and clarity of thought, recognizing that knowledge is enhanced through spiritual connection.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may turn to this mantra for guidance and strength, believing that divine support can help them navigate difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine presence in all aspects of life, from personal endeavors to communal gatherings.
    • It emphasizes that wisdom, knowledge, and spiritual support are vital for success and fulfillment.
    • By invoking Ganapati, devotees seek not only blessings but also a deeper understanding of their purpose and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In essence, this mantra encourages individuals to remain engaged with the divine, fostering a sense of community, wisdom, and purpose in their lives.
