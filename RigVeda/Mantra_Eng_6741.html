आ त्वा कण्वा इहावसे हवन्ते वाजसातये |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
ā tvā kaṇvā ihāvase havante vājasātaye | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā tvā: To you
    • kaṇvā: O Kaṇva (a sage or a seer)
    • ihāvase: Here, in this world
    • havante: Invoking or calling upon
    • vājasātaye: For the purpose of strength or power
    • divo: Of the heavens
    • amuṣya: Of this (or that) world
    • śāsato: By the command or authority
    • divaṁ: Heaven
    • yaya: By which
    • divāvaso: Dwelling in the day or in the light
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards the sage Kaṇva, invoking his strength and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and power from higher realms, particularly the heavens.
    • The phrase "divo amuṣya" suggests a connection between the earthly and the divine, indicating that the wisdom of the heavens can influence our lives here on Earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call for seeking guidance from mentors or wise individuals who can provide strength and direction in challenging situations.
    • For instance, when facing a difficult decision in one's career, one might seek advice from a trusted advisor, akin to invoking the wisdom of the sage Kaṇva.
    • Additionally, the mantra can be applied in the context of spiritual practices, where individuals seek divine guidance through meditation or prayer, aiming to connect with higher powers for strength and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of the earthly and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and wisdom from higher sources, whether through spiritual practices or by learning from the experiences of others.
    • In real life, this can manifest as a pursuit of knowledge, mentorship, and spiritual growth, ultimately leading to a more empowered and enlightened existence.
