को देवयन्तम अशनवज जनं को वृक्तबरहिषम् |प्र-प्र दाश्वान पस्त्याभिर अस्तितान्तर्वावत क्षयम् दधे ||
ko devayantam aśnavaj janaṁ ko vṛktabarhiṣam | pra-pra dāśvān pastyābhir asthitāntarvāvat kṣayaṁ dadhe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • devayantam: Divine or celestial
    • aśnavaj: Who consumes or partakes
    • janaṁ: People or beings
    • ko: Who
    • vṛktabarhiṣam: With offerings or sacrifices
    • pra-pra: To the one who is the source
    • dāśvān: Of the horses or steeds
    • pastyābhir: With the help of the past
    • asthitāntarvāvat: Established within
    • kṣayaṁ: Destruction or decline
    • dadhe: He places or establishes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the divine beings who partake in the offerings made by people.
    • It reflects on the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the importance of offerings and sacrifices.
    • The mention of "horses" symbolizes strength, vitality, and the means of achieving goals, often seen in rituals and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "established within" suggests a deeper connection to the divine, indicating that the divine presence is inherent in the world around us.
    • The concept of "destruction" implies that neglecting these divine connections can lead to decline or loss.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude and respect towards nature and the universe.
    • For instance, when people engage in rituals or ceremonies to honor the earth, they are acknowledging the divine presence in their lives.
    • In a corporate setting, recognizing the contributions of employees (akin to offerings) can lead to a more harmonious and productive work environment.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a way of making offerings to the earth, ensuring its vitality and preventing decline.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces in our lives.
    • It teaches us that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, have consequences on our relationship with the divine and the world around us.
    • By understanding and applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their environment, promote sustainability, and enhance their spiritual well-being.
