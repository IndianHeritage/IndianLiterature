पिपर्तु नो अदिती राजपुत्राति द्वेषांस्य आर्यमा सुगेभिः |बृहन् मित्रस्य वरुणस्य शर्मोप स्याम पूर्ववीराः अरिष्टाः ||
pipartu no aditī rājaputrāti dveṣāṁsy aryamā sugebhiḥ | bṛhan mitrasya varuṇasya śarmopa syāma puruvīrā ariṣṭāḥ ||
  • pipartu no aditī rājaputrāti: This phrase can be translated as "May Aditi, the mother of the gods, grant us strength and prosperity." Aditi is often associated with the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe. In real life, this can be likened to seeking blessings from a higher power or the universe for guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • dveṣāṁsy aryamā sugebhiḥ: This part means "May we be free from enmity and obstacles." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and the removal of negativity in our lives. For example, in a workplace, fostering a collaborative environment can lead to better productivity and innovation, highlighting the need to overcome rivalry and conflict.
  • bṛhan mitrasya varuṇasya: This translates to "May we be protected by the great Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. In everyday life, this can be seen as a call for ethical living and maintaining good relationships with others, akin to the importance of trust and integrity in personal and professional relationships.
  • śarmopa syāma puruvīrā ariṣṭāḥ: This means "May we attain peace and security, like the ancient heroes." It suggests a desire for safety and well-being, drawing parallels to how we seek stability in our lives. For instance, in times of crisis, individuals often look for community support and solidarity, reflecting the need for collective strength and resilience.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for strength, harmony, protection, and peace. Its significance lies in the universal quest for well-being and the importance of fostering positive relationships while overcoming adversities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, community building, and conflict resolution, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our lives and the need for mutual support and understanding.
