तवद विश्वा शुभग सौभागान्य अग्ने वि यन्ति वनिनो न वयाः |श्रुष्टि रयिर वाजो वृततूर्ये दिवो वृष्टिर ईड्यो रीतिर्प अपाम ||
tvad viśvā subhaga saubhagāny agne vi yanti vanino na vayāḥ | śruṣṭī rayir vājo vṛtratūrye divo vṛṣṭir īḍyo rītir apām ||
  • tvad viśvā subhaga saubhagāny agne vi yanti vanino na vayāḥ
    • tvad - "Your" or "of you," referring to Agni, the fire god.
    • viśvā - "All" or "universal," indicating the all-encompassing nature of Agni.
    • subhaga - "Auspicious" or "fortunate," suggesting that Agni brings good fortune.
    • saubhagāny - "Prosperous" or "blessings," emphasizing the wealth and prosperity associated with Agni.
    • agne - "O Agni," directly addressing the fire god.
    • vi yanti - "They go," indicating movement or progression.
    • vanino - "Those who dwell in the forest," referring to the natural world and its inhabitants.
    • na vayāḥ - "Not the winds," suggesting a distinction from the forces of nature.
  • śruṣṭī rayir vājo vṛtratūrye divo vṛṣṭir īḍyo rītir apām
    • śruṣṭī - "Creation" or "manifestation," referring to the act of bringing forth life.
    • rayir - "Wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance in life.
    • vājo - "Strength" or "energy," often associated with the power of Agni.
    • vṛtratūrye - "In the overcoming of obstacles," suggesting the ability to conquer challenges.
    • divo - "Of the heavens," indicating a divine or celestial aspect.
    • vṛṣṭir - "Rain" or "blessing," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • īḍyo - "Worthy of praise," highlighting the reverence for Agni.
    • rītir - "Order" or "truth," suggesting a harmonious existence.
    • apām - "Of the waters," connecting to the life-giving properties of water.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a universal force that brings prosperity, strength, and creation. It highlights the relationship between nature and the divine, suggesting that the blessings of Agni are essential for overcoming obstacles and achieving harmony in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to rituals and practices that seek to invoke blessings for prosperity, health, and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements in nature and the importance of respecting and nurturing these relationships for a fulfilling life.
