मन्त्र: पदं देवस्य नमसां व्यन्तः श्रवस्यवः श्रव आपनं अमृतम् |नामानि चिद दधिरे यज्ञियानि भद्रायां ते रणयन्त संदृष्टौ ||
padaṁ devasya namasā vyantaḥ śravasyavaḥ śrava āpann amṛktam | nāmāni cid dadhire yajñiyāni bhadrāyāṁ te raṇayanta saṁdṛṣṭau ||
  • padaṁ devasya: This phrase translates to "the foot of the divine." In a real-life context, it can be understood as seeking guidance or support from a higher power or the universe. For example, when someone is facing challenges, they may pray or meditate to find strength and direction.
  • namasā: This means "with reverence" or "with humility." In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of respect towards elders, teachers, or anyone deserving of honor. For instance, bowing down to greet someone or showing gratitude can be a form of namasā.
  • vyantaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are free" or "liberated." In practical terms, it refers to individuals who have overcome their limitations or fears. For example, someone who has conquered their anxiety to speak in public embodies vyantaḥ.
  • śravasyavaḥ: This translates to "those who are worthy of praise." In real life, this can refer to individuals who contribute positively to society, such as community leaders or activists. Their actions inspire others and deserve recognition.
  • śrava āpann: This means "having attained fame" or "achieving recognition." In a modern context, this could relate to someone who has worked hard to achieve success in their career, such as an artist or scientist who gains acclaim for their work.
  • amṛktam: This word signifies "immortal" or "eternal." In life, this can be associated with the legacy one leaves behind, such as the impact of a teacher on their students or a leader on their community.
  • nāmāni cid dadhire: This translates to "they hold the names." This can be understood as the importance of identity and reputation. In real life, a person's name can carry weight, influencing how they are perceived in society, such as a brand name that signifies quality.
  • yajñiyāni: This refers to "sacrificial offerings" or "rituals." In contemporary life, this can be seen in charitable acts or community service, where individuals give back to society, reflecting the spirit of yajñiyāni.
  • bhadrāyāṁ: This means "for auspiciousness" or "for well-being." In practical terms, this can relate to actions taken for the greater good, such as environmental conservation efforts aimed at ensuring a healthy planet for future generations.
  • te raṇayanta: This translates to "they who are engaged in battle." This can symbolize the struggles individuals face in life, whether personal or professional. For example, someone fighting for social justice embodies this spirit of raṇayanta.
  • saṁdṛṣṭau: This means "in vision" or "in sight." In real life, this can refer to having a clear vision or goal, such as an entrepreneur who has a vision for their business and works diligently to achieve it.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, showing reverence, and striving for liberation and recognition. It emphasizes the importance of identity, sacrifice for the greater good, and the battles we face in life. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various contexts, such as personal growth, community service, and the pursuit of one's goals. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can lead a life of purpose, contributing positively to themselves and society.
