उप नो वाजाऽध्वरम् ऋभुक्षा देवाऽयात पथिभिर देवयानैः |यथा यज्ञं मनुषो विक्ष्व ā3सु दधिध्वे रण्वाः सुदिनेष्व अह्नाम् ||
upa no vājā adhvaram ṛbhukṣā devā yāta pathibhir devayānaiḥ | yathā yajñam manuṣo vikṣv ā3su dadhidhve raṇvāḥ sudineṣv ahnām ||
  • upa no vājā adhvaram: This phrase can be translated as "May the horses (vājā) come to us for the sacrifice (adhvaram)." In Vedic rituals, horses were often symbolic of strength and vitality, representing the energy needed for successful endeavors.
  • ṛbhukṣā devā yāta pathibhir devayānaiḥ: This translates to "May the divine beings (devā) come to us along the paths (pathibhir) of the divine (devayānaiḥ)." It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and support in our pursuits, suggesting that we should seek the blessings of higher powers in our actions.
  • yathā yajñam manuṣo vikṣv: This means "As the human (manuṣo) performs the sacrifice (yajñam) with focus and intention." It highlights the significance of human effort and intention in achieving success, indicating that our actions should be purposeful and aligned with our goals.
  • ā3su dadhidhve raṇvāḥ sudineṣv ahnām: This can be interpreted as "May we be endowed (dadhidhve) with strength (raṇvāḥ) in the auspicious (sudineṣv) days (ahnām)." It conveys a wish for strength and prosperity during favorable times, reinforcing the idea that success is often linked to the right timing and conditions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and strength in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of intention, effort, and the auspiciousness of timing in achieving our goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new project, seeking guidance in difficult times, or performing rituals for success. By invoking the blessings of the divine and aligning our actions with our intentions, we can navigate our paths more effectively and achieve our desired outcomes.
