यत् ते पवित्रं आर्चिष्य अग्नि विततम अन्तर आ |ब्रह्म तेन पुनिहि नः ||
yat te pavitram arciṣy agne vitatam antar ā | brahma tena punīhi naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yat te pavitram arciṣy agne vitatam antar ā | brahma tena punīhi naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, may that which is pure and radiant, which is spread out within us, purify us with that divine essence."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yat: that which
    • te: your
    • pavitram: pure
    • arciṣy: radiant or light
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vitatam: spread out or extended
    • antar: within
    • ā: us
    • brahma: divine essence or ultimate reality
    • tena: with that
    • punīhi: purify
    • naḥ: us

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the god of fire, who symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine light.
    • The phrase "yat te pavitram" emphasizes the importance of purity, suggesting that the essence of Agni is inherently pure and radiant.
    • "Vitatam antar ā" indicates that this divine light is not just external but is also present within us, highlighting the connection between the divine and the individual.
    • The invocation to "brahma tena punīhi naḥ" is a plea for purification, asking for the divine essence to cleanse and elevate our being.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during meditation or yoga practices, where individuals seek to connect with their inner self and attain a state of purity and clarity.
    • In moments of stress or negativity, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek the inner light and purity that exists within, promoting mental and emotional well-being.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, invoking Agni symbolizes the transformation of negative energies into positive ones, akin to how fire transforms materials through combustion.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking purity and divine light within ourselves, encouraging a journey of self-discovery and spiritual growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal meditation to communal rituals, emphasizing the universal quest for inner peace and enlightenment.
    • By invoking Agni, we acknowledge the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically, as a means to purify our thoughts, actions, and spirit.
