त्वं नः सोम विश्वतो गोपा अदाभ्यो भव |सेध राजन अप श्रिधो वि वो मदे मा नो दु:शंस ईशता विवक्षसे ||
tvaṁ naḥ soma viśvato gopā adābhyo bhava | sedha rājann apa sridho vi vo made mā no duḥśaṁsa īśatā vivakṣase ||
  • tvaṁ naḥ soma viśvato gopā: This phrase can be translated to "You, O Soma, are our protector from all sides." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and is considered a deity in the Rigveda. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of seeking protection and nourishment from a higher power or source, much like how people turn to spirituality or community for support.
  • adābhyo bhava: This means "be free from all obstacles." In our daily lives, we often face challenges and hurdles. This line encourages us to seek a state of being that is unencumbered by difficulties, similar to how individuals strive for mental clarity and emotional resilience in the face of adversity.
  • sedha rājann apa sridho: Translated, this means "O King, grant us prosperity." Here, the term "King" can symbolize authority or leadership. In a modern context, this could refer to leaders in various fields—politics, business, or community—who are expected to provide for the welfare of their people. It emphasizes the importance of good governance and responsible leadership.
  • vi vo made mā no duḥśaṁsa: This translates to "May we not be harmed by your wrath." This line reflects the human desire for safety and protection from negative consequences. In real life, it can be seen in the way people seek forgiveness or understanding from authority figures, whether in personal relationships or in broader societal contexts.
  • īśatā vivakṣase: This means "You express your will." It highlights the importance of intention and communication. In everyday life, this can be related to how individuals express their desires and aspirations, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community engagements.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for protection, prosperity, and understanding from a higher power or authority. Its significance lies in the universal human experience of seeking support and guidance in times of need. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community struggles, or when seeking clarity in leadership. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, urging us to seek harmony and support in our lives.
