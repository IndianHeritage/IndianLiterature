आ वां नराः मनोयुजोऽश्वासः प्रुषितप्सवः |वयो वहन्तु पीतये सह सुम्नैभिर अश्विनौ माध्वी मम श्रुतं हवम् ||
ā vāṁ narā manoyujo 'śvāsaḥ pruṣitapsavaḥ | vayo vahantu pītaye saha sumnebhir aśvinā mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: May we (us)
    • narā: men (humans)
    • manoyujo: united in thought or mind
    • 'śvāsaḥ: breath or life force
    • pruṣitapsavaḥ: those who are strong or powerful
    • vayo: our age or lifespan
    • vahantu: may they carry or bring
    • pītaye: for our nourishment or sustenance
    • saha: together with
    • sumnebhir: auspicious or favorable
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen)
    • mādhvī: sweet or pleasant
    • mama: my
    • śrutaṁ: heard or known
    • havam: offering or invocation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for unity and strength among people.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective thought and action, suggesting that when individuals come together with a common purpose, they can achieve greater things.
    • The reference to the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities, indicates a desire for divine assistance in achieving these goals.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that a harmonious community thrives on mutual support and shared resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members collaborate effectively, they can innovate and solve problems more efficiently, much like the unity called for in this mantra.
    • In community service, when individuals come together to support a cause, such as feeding the homeless, they embody the essence of this mantra by working towards a common goal.
    • In family settings, fostering open communication and shared responsibilities can lead to a nurturing environment, reflecting the mantra's call for collective strength and nourishment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity and collective effort in achieving common goals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and mutual support.
    • By invoking the blessings of the divine, as represented by the Ashvins, individuals and communities can aspire to create a harmonious and prosperous existence.
