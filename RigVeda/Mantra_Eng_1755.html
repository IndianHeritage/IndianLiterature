सूयवसाद भगवती हि भूयाऽथो वयं भगवन्तः स्याम |अद्धि तृणम् अग्न्ये विश्वदानीं पिब शुद्धम् उदकम् आचरन्ती ||
sūyavasād bhagavatī hi bhūyā atho vayam bhagavantaḥ syāma | addhi tṛṇam aghnye viśvadānīm piba śuddham udakam ācarantī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūyavasād: From the auspiciousness or goodness.
    • bhagavatī: Divine or blessed.
    • hi: Indeed.
    • bhūyā: May we become greater or more powerful.
    • atho: Therefore or thus.
    • vayam: We.
    • bhagavantaḥ: Those who are blessed or divine.
    • syāma: May we be.
    • addhi: In the midst of.
    • tṛṇam: Grass or the humble.
    • aghnye: Not to be harmed or sacred.
    • viśvadānīm: All-giving or universal benefactor.
    • piba: Drink or consume.
    • śuddham: Pure.
    • udakam: Water.
    • ācarantī: Acting or performing.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of seeking blessings and auspiciousness in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being in the company of the divine and those who are virtuous.
    • The reference to drinking pure water symbolizes the need for purity in thoughts and actions.
    • It suggests that by aligning ourselves with the divine and acting with purity, we can elevate our existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation, they embody the essence of this mantra by working towards a greater good.
    • In personal life, practicing mindfulness and purity in thoughts can lead to better mental health and well-being, reflecting the idea of drinking pure water.
    • In professional environments, fostering a culture of respect and support among colleagues can lead to collective success, resonating with the idea of being in the company of the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine blessings and striving for purity in our actions and thoughts.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community efforts, emphasizing the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can enhance our lives and contribute positively to the world around us.
