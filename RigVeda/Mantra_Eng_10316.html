पावकवर्चाः शुक्रवर्चा अनूनवर्चा उद इयर्षि भानुना | पुत्रो मातराऽविचरन्न उपावसि पृथक्शि रोधसी उभे ||
pāvakavarcāḥ śukravarcā anūnavarcā ud iyarṣi bhānunā | putro mātarā vicarann upāvasi pṛṇakṣi rodasī ubhe ||
  • pāvakavarcāḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "pāvak" meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "varcāḥ" meaning "radiance" or "light." Together, it signifies the pure and radiant energy that is essential for life.
  • śukravarcā: "śukra" translates to "bright" or "clear," and "varcā" again refers to "radiance." This phrase emphasizes the importance of clarity and brightness in our thoughts and actions, suggesting that purity of intention leads to positive outcomes.
  • anūnavarcā: "anūna" means "not diminished" or "everlasting," while "varcā" again refers to "radiance." This indicates a continuous and unyielding source of light or energy, symbolizing resilience and the enduring nature of truth.
  • ud iyarṣi bhānunā: "ud" means "up," "iyarṣi" can be interpreted as "to rise" or "to elevate," and "bhānunā" refers to "the sun." This phrase suggests the elevation of consciousness and the pursuit of enlightenment, akin to the rising sun that dispels darkness.
  • putro mātarā: "putro" means "son" and "mātarā" means "by the mother." This signifies the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the bond between a mother and her child, highlighting the importance of familial relationships and support.
  • vicarann upāvasi: "vicarann" means "wandering" or "moving about," and "upāvasi" means "to dwell" or "to reside." This suggests a journey of exploration and the idea of finding one's place in the world, emphasizing the importance of both movement and stability.
  • pṛṇakṣi rodasī ubhe: "pṛṇakṣi" means "to fill" or "to nourish," "rodasī" refers to "the two worlds" (often interpreted as the physical and spiritual realms), and "ubhe" means "both." This phrase highlights the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence and the need for nourishment in both physical and spiritual dimensions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of purity, clarity, resilience, and the nurturing aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking enlightenment and understanding one's place in the world while maintaining strong familial bonds. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, family relationships, and the pursuit of knowledge and truth. It serves as a reminder to cultivate purity in our intentions and actions, to seek clarity in our thoughts, and to nourish both our physical and spiritual selves for a balanced and fulfilling life.
