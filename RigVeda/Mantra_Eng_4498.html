त्वं इमा वार्याः पुरु दिवोदासाय सुन्वते |भरद्वाजाय दाशुषे ||
tvam imā vāryā puru divodāsāya sunvate | bharadvājāya dāśuṣe ||
  • tvam imā vāryā: This phrase translates to "You are the one who is worthy." It signifies the recognition of a divine or powerful presence that is deserving of respect and reverence. In real life, this can be likened to acknowledging leaders or mentors who guide us towards success.
  • puru divodāsāya: This means "for the one who is a great seer or sage." It refers to individuals who possess deep wisdom and insight. In contemporary terms, this could relate to teachers, philosophers, or even scientists whose knowledge shapes our understanding of the world.
  • sunvate: This word translates to "who is praised" or "who is celebrated." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society. For example, community leaders or activists who work tirelessly for social change deserve our praise and support.
  • bharadvājāya: This refers to the sage Bharadvaja, a revered figure in Vedic literature known for his wisdom and contributions to knowledge. In modern contexts, this could symbolize the importance of learning from historical figures and applying their teachings to our lives.
  • dāśuṣe: This term means "to the one who gives." It highlights the value of generosity and sharing. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of charity, where individuals or organizations provide support to those in need, fostering a sense of community and compassion.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and honoring those who possess wisdom, offer guidance, and contribute positively to society. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the leaders, teachers, and generous individuals in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to cultivate gratitude, seek knowledge, and engage in acts of kindness, ultimately fostering a more harmonious and supportive community.
