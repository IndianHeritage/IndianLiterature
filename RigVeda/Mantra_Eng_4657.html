वयं ते अस्यां इन्द्र द्युम्नहूतौ सखायः स्याम महिना प्रेष्ठाः |प्रातर्दनिः क्षत्रश्रीर अस्तु श्रेष्ठो घने वृत्राणां सनये धनानाम् ||
vayaṁ te asyām indra dyumnahūtau sakhāyaḥ syāma mahina preṣṭhāḥ | prātardaniḥ kṣatraśrīr astu śreṣṭho ghane vṛtrāṇāṁ sanaye dhanānām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • te: Your
    • asyām: In this
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • dyumna: Glory or fame
    • hūtau: In the offerings
    • sakhāyaḥ: Friends or companions
    • syāma: May we be
    • mahina: Great or powerful
    • preṣṭhāḥ: The best or foremost
    • prātardaniḥ: O destroyer of enemies
    • kṣatraśrīr: The glory of power or sovereignty
    • astu: Let there be
    • śreṣṭho: The best
    • ghane: In abundance
    • vṛtrāṇāṁ: Of the obstacles or enemies
    • sanaye: To grant or bestow
    • dhanānām: Of wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, seeking his blessings for glory and strength.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to be among the best companions in the offerings made to Indra, emphasizing the importance of unity and friendship.
    • It highlights the aspiration for power and prosperity, wishing for the removal of obstacles and the attainment of wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team working together towards a common goal, where each member supports one another to achieve success.
    • In personal relationships, it reflects the importance of having strong bonds with friends and family, where mutual support leads to overcoming challenges.
    • In community initiatives, it signifies the collective effort to bring about positive change, where the strength of unity can lead to prosperity for all.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the pursuit of excellence.
    • It serves as a reminder that through collaboration and support, individuals can overcome obstacles and achieve greater heights.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the value of teamwork and shared aspirations.
