वयं ते अद्य ररिमाः हि कामम् उत्तानहस्ताः नमसोपसद्य | यजिष्ठेन मनसा यक्षि देवान अस्रेदहतā मनमनā विप्रो अग्नि ||
vayaṁ te adya rarimā hi kāmam uttānahastā namasopasadya | yajiṣṭhena manasā yakṣi devān asredhatā manmanā vipro agne ||
  • vayaṁ te adya rarimā hi kāmam uttānahastā namasopasadya
    • vayaṁ te: "We are yours" - This phrase signifies a sense of belonging and devotion. In real life, this can be compared to the loyalty one shows towards family, friends, or a community.
    • adya: "today" - This emphasizes the immediacy of the devotion and the importance of the present moment. For example, in a business meeting, being fully present and engaged can lead to better outcomes.
    • rarimā: "to be strong" - This suggests a call for strength and resilience. In everyday life, this can relate to overcoming challenges, such as facing a difficult situation at work or in personal life.
    • hi kāmam: "for desires" - This indicates that the strength sought is for fulfilling one's desires. For instance, pursuing a career goal or a personal dream requires both strength and determination.
    • uttānahastā: "with uplifted hands" - This symbolizes readiness to receive blessings or support. In a practical sense, it can be likened to being open to opportunities and help from others.
    • namasopasadya: "with reverence" - This highlights the importance of humility and respect in one's pursuits. In real life, showing respect to mentors or elders can lead to valuable guidance and support.
  • yajiṣṭhena manasā yakṣi devān asredhatā manmanā vipro agne
    • yajiṣṭhena: "with the best of offerings" - This refers to the quality of what one offers, whether in terms of effort, time, or resources. For example, in a project, giving your best effort can lead to success.
    • manasā: "with the mind" - This emphasizes the importance of intention and focus. In daily life, being mindful in actions, such as practicing mindfulness in relationships, can enhance connections.
    • yakṣi devān: "to the gods" - This can be interpreted as seeking divine support or guidance. In real life, this could relate to seeking inspiration from role models or spiritual figures.
    • asredhatā: "may we be blessed" - This is a plea for blessings and positive outcomes. For instance, before starting a new venture, many people seek blessings for success.
    • manmanā: "with a focused mind" - This reinforces the idea of concentration and clarity of purpose. In practical terms, setting clear goals and focusing on them can lead to achievement.
    • vipro agne: "O wise one, Agni" - Agni represents the divine fire and is often associated with transformation and purification. In life, this can symbolize the transformative power of knowledge and wisdom.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, strength, intention, and the pursuit of desires with respect and mindfulness. Its significance lies in the reminder to approach life with a sense of belonging, to seek blessings, and to offer one's best in all endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to be present, focused, and respectful in their pursuits, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
