आ ये विश्वा पार्थिवानि पप्रथन् रोचनाः दिवः |मरुतः सोमपीतये ||
ā ye viśvā pārthivāni paprathan rocanā divaḥ | marutaḥ somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "ā ye viśvā pārthivāni paprathan rocanā divaḥ | marutaḥ somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: "O Maruts, may you who are the universal forces, who spread out the earthly delights, grant us the nectar of the moon."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix indicating direction or purpose.
    • ye: Those who.
    • viśvā: Universal or all-encompassing.
    • pārthivāni: Earthly or related to the earth.
    • paprathan: To spread or distribute.
    • rocanā: Delight or brightness.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • marutaḥ: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • somapītaye: For the drinking of the soma, a sacred drink.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra invokes the Maruts, who are considered the forces of nature, particularly associated with storms and winds.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the earthly and the celestial, suggesting that the delights of the earth are interconnected with the divine forces of the universe.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates a desire for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment, as soma is often associated with immortality and divine consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the Maruts can be seen as the forces that bring rain, essential for crops. Farmers often pray for rain, recognizing the importance of these natural forces in their livelihood.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek balance between their earthly desires and spiritual aspirations, much like the mantra seeks a connection between the physical and the divine.
    • During festivals, rituals may be performed to honor nature and seek blessings for prosperity, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the forces of nature that sustain life on earth.
    • It teaches us to seek a harmonious relationship with the environment, understanding that our earthly pleasures are intertwined with spiritual growth.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire sustainable living practices, encouraging individuals to respect nature while pursuing their desires.
