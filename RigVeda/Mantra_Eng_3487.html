उत स्मास्य पनयन्ति जनाः जूतिं कृष्टिप्रो अभिभूतिम आशोः |उतैन्म आहुः समिथे वियन्तः परा दधिक्राः असरत सहस्रैः ||
uta smāsya panayanti janā jūtiṁ kṛṣṭipro abhibhūtim āśoḥ | utainam āhuḥ samithe viyantaḥ parā dadhikrā asarat sahasraiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • smāsya: of the dead
    • panayanti: they lead or guide
    • janā: people or beings
    • jūtiṁ: life or vitality
    • kṛṣṭipro: through the efforts of the cultivators
    • abhibhūtim: prosperity or abundance
    • āśoḥ: of hope or desire
    • utainam: they call it
    • āhuḥ: they say
    • samithe: in the assembly or gathering
    • viyantaḥ: those who are moving or wandering
    • parā: beyond or superior
    • dadhikrā: they who bear or support
    • asarat: with strength or power
    • sahasraiḥ: with thousands
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the guidance and support provided by the living to those who have passed away.
    • It emphasizes the role of community and collective effort in achieving prosperity and abundance.
    • The mention of "cultivators" signifies the importance of hard work and dedication in nurturing life and hope.
    • The phrase "they call it" suggests that the community recognizes and honors the contributions of both the living and the dead.
    • The idea of "moving or wandering" reflects the transient nature of life and the importance of connection among individuals.
    • The reference to "thousands" indicates the strength found in unity and collective action.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor ancestors, reflecting the belief that the living can guide the spirits of the deceased.
    • Community farming initiatives demonstrate how collective effort can lead to prosperity, echoing the mantra's emphasis on the role of cultivators.
    • Support groups for grieving individuals illustrate how people come together to share strength and hope, embodying the mantra's message of unity.
    • In workplaces, teamwork and collaboration often lead to greater success, mirroring the idea of collective abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life and death, emphasizing the importance of community support and collective effort.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions and relationships have a lasting impact, not only on our lives but also on those who have passed.
    • In real life, it encourages us to work together, honor our heritage, and support one another in the pursuit of prosperity and hope.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that through unity and shared purpose, we can overcome challenges and create a meaningful existence.
