सुरथां ātithigve स्वभिषूम्र आरक्षे |आश्वमेधे सुपेशसः ||
surathām̐ ātithigve svabhīśūm̐r ārkṣe | āśvamedhe supeśasaḥ ||
  • Surathām̐: This term can be translated as "of the good and virtuous." It refers to individuals who are righteous and possess noble qualities.
  • Ātithigve: This word signifies "the guest" or "the one who is welcomed." In many cultures, hospitality is a revered virtue, and this term emphasizes the importance of treating guests with respect and honor.
  • Svabhīśūm̐r: This can be interpreted as "those who are self-possessed" or "those who have control over themselves." It highlights the value of self-discipline and inner strength.
  • Ārkṣe: This term relates to "the sun" or "the celestial body." The sun is often seen as a source of life, energy, and enlightenment in various cultures.
  • Āśvamedhe: This refers to the "Ashvamedha" sacrifice, an ancient Vedic ritual that symbolizes power, sovereignty, and the establishment of a king's authority.
  • Supeśasaḥ: This term means "of good appearance" or "well-formed." It can refer to both physical beauty and the beauty of character, suggesting that both are important in life.

The mantra as a whole emphasizes the qualities of virtue, hospitality, self-control, and the significance of the sun as a life-giving force. It also highlights the importance of rituals like the Ashvamedha in establishing authority and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, striving to embody the qualities of surathām̐ and svabhīśūm̐r can lead to a more fulfilling and respected life.
  • Practicing ātithigve by welcoming guests with warmth and generosity can strengthen social bonds and create a sense of community.
  • Recognizing the significance of ārkṣe can inspire individuals to appreciate nature and the life-giving forces around them, promoting environmental consciousness.
  • Engaging in rituals or traditions that symbolize personal or communal achievements, akin to the āśvamedhe, can foster a sense of identity and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues that lead to a harmonious and prosperous life. By embodying these qualities and recognizing their significance in our daily interactions and rituals, we can contribute to a more virtuous and connected society.
