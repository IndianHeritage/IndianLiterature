मन्त्र: नि ग्रामासो अविक्षत नि पद्वन्तो नि पक्षिणः |नि श्येनासश्चिदर्थिनः ||
ni grāmāso avikṣata ni padvanto ni pakṣiṇaḥ | ni śyenāsaś cid arthinaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • grāmāso: of the village or community
    • avikṣata: scattered or dispersed
    • ni: not
    • padvanto: those who walk or travel
    • ni: not
    • pakṣiṇaḥ: of the birds
    • ni: not
    • śyenāsaś: of the hawks or predatory birds
    • cid: indeed
    • arthinaḥ: those who seek or desire
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra conveys a sense of unity and protection within a community.
    • It emphasizes that there should be no scattering or division among the people, travelers, or even the birds.
    • It suggests that those who seek or desire should not be preyed upon or harmed.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to promote harmony and cooperation among its members. For instance, in a neighborhood watch program, residents come together to ensure safety and security, embodying the idea of not being scattered or divided.
    • In the context of travel, it can be seen in the way travelers support each other, such as in travel groups or communities that share resources and information to ensure safety and well-being.
    • In nature, the mantra reflects the balance of ecosystems where birds and other creatures coexist without harming each other, highlighting the importance of mutual respect and protection.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, protection, and mutual respect within communities and among individuals.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our daily lives, we should strive to support one another, ensuring that no one is left vulnerable or isolated.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious coexistence, whether in human societies or in the natural world, promoting a sense of collective responsibility and care.
