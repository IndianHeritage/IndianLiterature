अक्षन्न अमीमदन्त ह्य अव प्रिया अधूषत |अस्तोषत स्वभावनो विप्रā नविष्टयā मती योजा nv इन्द्र ते हरī ||
akṣann amīmadanta hy ava priyā adhūṣata | astoṣata svabhānavo viprā naviṣṭhayā matī yojā nv indra te harī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣann: Not blind, clear-sighted
    • amīmadanta: Those who are not obstructed or hindered
    • hy: Indeed
    • ava: Downward, towards
    • priyā: Beloved, dear
    • adhūṣata: They have not been harmed or diminished
    • astoṣata: They are not to be disturbed
    • svabhānavo: Those of their own nature
    • viprā: The wise or learned ones
    • naviṣṭhayā: With a new understanding or insight
    • matī: Intellect, thought
    • yojā: To connect or join
    • nv: Indeed
    • indra: The king of gods, representing power and strength
    • te: Your
    • harī: The one who brings joy or happiness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the clarity of vision and understanding that comes from wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being unimpeded in one's thoughts and actions, akin to how a clear stream flows without obstruction.
    • The reference to "viprā" highlights the value of learned individuals who possess insight and can guide others.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who inspire others through their wisdom and clear vision.
    • The invocation of "Indra" symbolizes the strength and power that comes from such clarity and wisdom.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra encourages individuals to seek clarity and wisdom in their lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength lies in understanding and insight, rather than mere power.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in decision-making processes, where clear thinking and wise counsel lead to better outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the importance of open communication and understanding, fostering deeper connections.
