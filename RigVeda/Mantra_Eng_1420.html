शतं राज्ञो नाधमानस्य निष्काञ्च चतं अश्वान प्रयतान सद्य आदम |शतं कक्षीवां असुरस्य गोनाṁ दिवि श्रवोऽजरम आ ततान ||
śataṁ rājño nādhamānasya niṣkāñ chatam aśvān prayatān sadya ādam | śataṁ kakṣīvām̐ asurasya gonāṁ divi śravo 'jaram ā tatāna ||
  • śataṁ rājño: This phrase translates to "a hundred of the king." It signifies abundance and prosperity associated with leadership and governance.
  • nādhamānasya: This means "of the one who is not lowly" or "not degraded." It emphasizes the importance of integrity and high moral standing in leadership.
  • niṣkāñ: This word refers to "freedom" or "liberation." It suggests that true leadership allows for the liberation of the people and the kingdom.
  • chatam aśvān: This translates to "a hundred horses." In ancient times, horses were symbols of wealth, power, and speed, indicating the strength of the kingdom.
  • prayatān: This means "efforts" or "endeavors." It highlights the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving success.
  • sadya: This translates to "immediate" or "quick." It suggests that the results of efforts should be seen promptly.
  • ādam: This word means "to attain" or "to achieve." It signifies the goal of reaching success through efforts.
  • śataṁ kakṣīvām̐: This means "a hundred of the strong." It emphasizes the need for strength and resilience in leadership.
  • asurasya gonāṁ: This translates to "of the demon's cattle." It symbolizes the challenges and adversities that leaders must face.
  • divi: This means "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm." It indicates a connection to higher powers or divine support.
  • śravo: This translates to "fame" or "glory." It signifies the importance of reputation and honor in leadership.
  • 'jaram: This means "aging" or "decay." It suggests that true strength and glory are everlasting and not subject to decline.
  • ā tatāna: This translates to "that which has been attained." It signifies the achievements and successes that come from wise leadership.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the qualities of effective leadership, such as integrity, strength, and the ability to liberate and uplift the people. It highlights the importance of hard work and the immediate results of efforts in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various leadership roles, whether in politics, business, or community service. Leaders who embody these qualities can inspire their followers, face challenges with resilience, and attain lasting success and glory. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless wisdom, reminding us that true leadership is not just about power but about serving and uplifting others.
