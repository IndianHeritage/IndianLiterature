तवम् इन्द्र श्रवितवा अपस कः परिष्टिता अहिना शूर पूर्वीः |त्वद वावक्रे रथ्यो न धेनाऽ रेजन्ते विश्व कृतिमाणि भीषा ||
tvam indra sravitavā apas kaḥ pariṣṭhitā ahinā śūra pūrvīḥ | tvad vāvakre rathyo3 na dhenā rejante viśvā kṛtrimāṇi bhīṣā ||
  • tvam indra sravitavā: This phrase translates to "You, Indra, who pours forth." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. This can be likened to a leader or a powerful figure in society who provides resources and support to their community.
  • apas kaḥ pariṣṭhitā: This means "who is established in the waters." Water is a vital resource for life, symbolizing sustenance and nourishment. In real life, this can be compared to the importance of clean water sources for communities, emphasizing the need for environmental conservation and management.
  • ahinā śūra pūrvīḥ: This translates to "the brave ones of the past." It refers to the heroes and warriors who have come before us, highlighting the importance of history and legacy. In modern contexts, this can relate to honoring past leaders and their contributions to society, inspiring current and future generations.
  • tvad vāvakre rathyo3: This means "your chariots are not hindered." Chariots symbolize progress and movement. In a contemporary sense, this can be seen in the context of technological advancements and the importance of innovation in driving society forward.
  • na dhenā rejante: This translates to "the cows do not thrive." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in many cultures. This phrase can be interpreted as a warning against neglecting resources that contribute to well-being and prosperity, such as agriculture and livestock management.
  • viśvā kṛtrimāṇi bhīṣā: This means "all artificial fears." It suggests that there are fears that are not based on reality but are instead constructed by society. In real life, this can relate to societal pressures and anxieties that people face, emphasizing the need for mental health awareness and resilience.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, resource management, honoring the past, progress, and addressing societal fears. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of these themes in our daily lives. By recognizing the role of strong leadership (like Indra), ensuring sustainable resources (like water and livestock), and learning from history, we can foster a more prosperous and harmonious society. Additionally, by confronting and understanding our fears, we can create a supportive environment that encourages growth and innovation. This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that can guide us in our personal and communal lives.
