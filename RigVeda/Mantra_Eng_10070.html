आ नो यज्ञं भारती तुयम् एत्व इळा मनुष्वद इह चेतयन्ती |तिस्रो देवीर बर्हिर एदं स्योनं सरस्वती स्वपसः सदन्तु ||
ā no yajñam bhāratī tūyam etv iḻā manuṣvad iha cetayantī | tisro devīr barhir edaṁ syonaṁ sarasvatī svapasaḥ sadantu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: our
    • yajñam: sacrifice or worship
    • bhāratī: the goddess of speech and knowledge
    • tūyam: you (feminine)
    • etv: may you grant
    • iḻā: the divine will or intention
    • manuṣvad: human-like or relatable
    • iha: here
    • cetayantī: inspiring or guiding
    • tisro: three
    • devīr: goddesses
    • barhir: the sacred grass or altar
    • edaṁ: this
    • syonaṁ: auspicious or beneficial
    • sarasvatī: the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning
    • svapasaḥ: of the wise or enlightened
    • sadantu: may they be present or may they bless
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and inspiration from the divine, particularly from the goddess of knowledge, Sarasvatī.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our lives, urging us to seek divine assistance in our endeavors.
    • The mention of "three goddesses" signifies the multifaceted nature of knowledge, encompassing various aspects such as speech, wisdom, and creativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In academic settings, students often recite prayers or mantras before exams to seek clarity and focus, similar to the invocation of Sarasvatī for knowledge.
    • Artists and musicians may invoke Sarasvatī before performances, hoping to channel creativity and inspiration.
    • In daily life, individuals may seek guidance in decision-making, reflecting the essence of this mantra by asking for wisdom in their choices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from education to creative endeavors, reminding us of the importance of seeking inspiration and clarity.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our quest for understanding and to honor the interconnectedness of knowledge, creativity, and human experience.
