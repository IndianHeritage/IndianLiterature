अग्नि तव त्यद उक्त्यं देवेष्व अस्त्य आप्यम् |स नः सत्तो मनुष्वद् आ देवान् यक्षि विदुष्टरो वित्तम् मे अस्य रोधसी ||
agne tava tyad ukthyaṁ deveṣv asty āpyam | sa naḥ satto manuṣvad ā devān yakṣi viduṣṭaro vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Tava: Meaning "your," it indicates possession or connection to Agni.
  • Tyad: This translates to "that," referring to the offerings or praises directed towards Agni.
  • Ukthyaṁ: This means "praise" or "hymn," emphasizing the act of worship and reverence.
  • Deveṣv: This means "among the gods," indicating the divine context of the mantra.
  • Asty: Translates to "there is," affirming existence or presence.
  • Āpyam: This means "abundance" or "wealth," suggesting a request for prosperity.
  • Sa: Meaning "he" or "that," referring back to Agni.
  • Naḥ: This means "our," indicating a collective appeal.
  • Satto: This translates to "truth" or "goodness," emphasizing moral integrity.
  • Manuṣvad: Meaning "like a human," it connects the divine with human experience.
  • Ā: This is a preposition meaning "to" or "towards," indicating direction.
  • Devān: This means "gods," referring to the divine beings in the Vedic pantheon.
  • Yakṣi: This translates to "may grant" or "may bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
  • Viduṣṭaro: Meaning "knowledgeable" or "wise," it refers to the enlightened beings.
  • Vittam: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for material and spiritual abundance.
  • Me: This means "my," indicating personal ownership or connection.
  • Asya: Meaning "of this," it refers to the context or situation at hand.
  • Rodasī: This translates to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," symbolizing the entirety of existence.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking blessings of truth, prosperity, and wisdom. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and human realms, highlighting the importance of moral integrity and the pursuit of abundance in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, personal prayers, or during significant life events where one seeks guidance and blessings for success and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the importance of aligning one's actions with truth and seeking the support of higher powers in achieving one's goals.
