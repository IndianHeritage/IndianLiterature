ऋतावानं यज्ञियं विप्रमुक्त्यं ā यं दधे मातरिश्वा दिवि क्षयम् | तं चित्रयामं हरिकेशं ईमहे सुदीतिम अग्निं सुविताय नव्यसे ||
ṛtāvānaṁ yajñiyaṁ vipram ukthya1m ā yaṁ dadhe mātariśvā divi kṣayam | taṁ citrayāmaṁ harikeśam īmahe sudītim agniṁ suvitāya navyase ||
  • ṛtāvānaṁ: This term refers to the one who is established in truth or righteousness. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold ethical standards and integrity in their actions, such as a judge who makes fair decisions based on the law.
  • yajñiyaṁ: This word signifies something that is worthy of worship or sacrifice. In contemporary terms, it can relate to acts of selflessness, such as volunteering for community service or donating to charity, which are seen as noble sacrifices for the greater good.
  • vipram: This refers to a learned or wise person, often a priest or sage. In modern society, this can be likened to educators or mentors who guide others through knowledge and wisdom, such as teachers who inspire students to pursue their dreams.
  • ukthyaṁ: This term means a hymn or a sacred utterance. In everyday life, this can be compared to motivational speeches or affirmations that inspire individuals to strive for their goals, similar to how a coach motivates a sports team before a game.
  • mātariśvā: This refers to the wind or the divine force that connects the earth and the heavens. In a practical sense, it can symbolize the unseen forces in our lives, such as intuition or inspiration that guide us in decision-making.
  • divi kṣayam: This phrase means the destruction or dissolution in the heavens. It can be interpreted as the transient nature of life and the universe, reminding us to cherish our moments and relationships, much like how we appreciate fleeting experiences like a beautiful sunset.
  • taṁ: This word means "that" and refers to the divine or the ultimate truth. In real life, this can be seen as the pursuit of one's purpose or calling, akin to individuals who dedicate their lives to a cause they believe in, such as environmental activists fighting for climate change.
  • citrayāmaṁ: This term signifies the colorful or diverse aspects of life. It can be related to the richness of human experiences, such as the variety of cultures and traditions that make our world vibrant and interesting.
  • harikeśam: This refers to the divine or auspicious being, often associated with Lord Vishnu. In a modern context, it can symbolize the ideals of compassion and protection, similar to how a community leader advocates for the welfare of its members.
  • sudītim: This means good intelligence or wisdom. In practical terms, it can be seen in individuals who make informed decisions based on knowledge and experience, such as a business leader who navigates challenges with strategic thinking.
  • agniṁ: This refers to fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the passion and drive that individuals have for their goals, such as an artist who pours their heart into their work.
  • suvitāya: This means well-nourished or well-prepared. It can relate to the importance of being ready and equipped for challenges, much like how athletes train rigorously to perform at their best during competitions.
  • navyase: This term signifies newness or freshness. In contemporary life, it can represent innovation and creativity, such as how startups bring fresh ideas to the market, challenging the status quo.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, wisdom, and the divine in our lives. It encourages individuals to engage in selfless acts, seek knowledge, and embrace the diverse experiences life offers. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide us in making ethical decisions, fostering community, and pursuing our passions with integrity. By embodying the essence of this mantra, we can contribute positively to society and cultivate a deeper understanding of our purpose in the universe.
