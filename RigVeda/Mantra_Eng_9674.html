मन्त्र: स्तोमा आसन प्रतिधयः कुरीरं चन्द उपशः |सूर्यायाऽ अश्विनā वराग्निर आसीत पुरोगवः ||
stomā āsan pratidhayaḥ kurīraṁ chanda opaśaḥ | sūryāyā aśvinā varāgnir āsīt purogavaḥ ||
  • stomā āsan pratidhayaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the hymns are established in the heart." Here, "stomā" refers to hymns or praises, while "āsan" means to be seated or established. This suggests that the essence of these hymns resides within us, indicating the importance of internalizing spiritual teachings.
  • kurīraṁ chanda opaśaḥ: This part can be interpreted as "the song of the seer is a source of inspiration." "Kurīraṁ" refers to a song or chant, "chanda" relates to rhythm or meter, and "opaśaḥ" means to bring forth or to reveal. This emphasizes the power of poetic expression in conveying spiritual truths and inspiring individuals.
  • sūryāyā aśvinā varāgnir: This translates to "the radiant sun, the twin Asvins, and the noble fire." "Sūryāyā" refers to the sun, "aśvinā" to the Asvins (twin deities associated with health and medicine), and "varāgnir" means the noble fire, often symbolizing purity and transformation. This highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements and divine forces in our lives.
  • āsīt purogavaḥ: This can be understood as "was the foremost among them." "Āsīt" means to be or to exist, and "purogavaḥ" refers to being the leader or the best. This suggests that the divine forces mentioned are not only present but also lead and guide us in our spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual practice, emphasizing the importance of internalizing hymns, drawing inspiration from poetic expressions, and recognizing the divine forces in nature. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in practices that foster inner peace and connection to the divine, such as meditation, chanting, or spending time in nature. By acknowledging the guiding forces in our lives, we can navigate challenges with greater wisdom and clarity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
