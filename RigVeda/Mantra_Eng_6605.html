युवोरु षूरथं हुवे सधस्तुत्याय सूरिषु |अतूर्तदक्षā वृषणा वृषणवसू ||
yuvor u ṣū rathaṁ huve sadhastutyāya sūriṣu | atūrtadakṣā vṛṣaṇā vṛṣaṇvasū ||
  • yuvor: This word translates to "of the young" or "of the youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential that comes with youth.
  • uṣū: This means "in the dawn" or "in the early morning." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the promise of a new day.
  • rathaṁ: This translates to "chariot." In Vedic literature, the chariot often represents the journey of life, the vehicle that carries us through our experiences.
  • huve: This means "I invoke" or "I call upon." It indicates a plea or a prayer directed towards a higher power or divine force.
  • sadhastutyāya: This can be broken down into "sadhastut" (praise) and "yāya" (for). It refers to the act of praising or honoring, particularly in the context of seeking blessings.
  • sūriṣu: This translates to "among the heroes" or "among the brave." It emphasizes the qualities of courage, strength, and valor.
  • atūrtadakṣā: This word can be understood as "skilled in overcoming obstacles." It highlights the importance of resilience and the ability to navigate challenges.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This means "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It signifies strength, both physical and mental, and the ability to endure.
  • vṛṣaṇvasū: This can be interpreted as "the essence of strength." It refers to the inherent qualities that contribute to one's power and capability.

In summary, this mantra invokes the youthful energy and vitality represented by the dawn, calling upon the divine for blessings and strength. It emphasizes the importance of courage, resilience, and the ability to overcome obstacles in our journey through life. The chariot symbolizes our life's journey, and by seeking the blessings of the heroes and the strong, we are reminded of the qualities we must embody to navigate our challenges successfully.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various life scenarios, such as during times of personal challenges or when embarking on new ventures. It serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength and resilience.
  • In a professional context, invoking this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, encouraging individuals to support one another in overcoming obstacles.
  • In educational settings, it can motivate students to embrace their youthful energy and approach learning with courage and determination.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to recognize our potential, seek divine guidance, and embody the qualities of strength and resilience in our daily lives.
