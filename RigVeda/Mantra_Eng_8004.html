तं नो विश्वा अवश्युवो गिरः शुम्भन्ति पूर्वथा |इन्दुम इन्द्राय पीतये ||
taṁ no viśvā avasyuvo giraḥ śumbhanti pūrvathā | indum indrāya pītaye ||
  • taṁ no viśvā avasyuvo giraḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May all the universal sounds (or words) come to us." Here, "taṁ" refers to "that" or "him," "no" means "to us," "viśvā" signifies "all" or "universal," "avasyuvo" implies "those who are destined," and "giraḥ" translates to "sounds" or "words." This part emphasizes the importance of communication and the collective wisdom that can be shared among individuals.
  • śumbhanti pūrvathā: This translates to "they sing (or speak) in the beginning." "Śumbhanti" means "they sing" or "they speak," and "pūrvathā" refers to "in the beginning" or "from the past." This suggests that the wisdom and knowledge we seek are rooted in ancient traditions and teachings, which have been passed down through generations.
  • indum indrāya pītaye: This can be interpreted as "for the sake of Indra, the drinker (or the one who enjoys)." "Indum" refers to "the moon" or "nectar," "indrāya" means "for Indra," and "pītaye" translates to "for drinking" or "for enjoyment." Indra is often associated with strength, power, and the enjoyment of life, symbolizing the pursuit of joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of collective wisdom, communication, and the importance of ancient knowledge in our lives. It encourages us to seek out and share universal truths and insights that have been cherished throughout history. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, community gatherings, and cultural celebrations where sharing knowledge and experiences enriches our lives. By invoking the spirit of Indra, we are reminded to enjoy life and seek fulfillment in our pursuits, fostering a sense of unity and joy among individuals.
