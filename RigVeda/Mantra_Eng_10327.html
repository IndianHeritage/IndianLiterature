आयम् अग्नि जरीता त्वे अभूद अपि सहसः सूनो नह्य अन्यद अस्त्य आप्यम् |भद्रं हि शर्म त्रिवरूथम् अस्ति त आरे हिंसाणाम् अप दिद्युम् आ कृत्हि ||
ayam agne jaritā tve abhūd api sahasaḥ sūno nahy a1nyad asty āpyam | bhadraṁ hi śarma trivarūtham asti ta āre hiṁsānām apa didyum ā kṛdhi ||
  • ayam agne jaritā tve abhūd: This phrase can be translated as "This fire, O Agni, has been invoked." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is often associated with warmth, cooking, and light, serving as a vital element for human survival and comfort.
  • api sahasaḥ sūno nahy a1nyad asty āpyam: This part means "Even the son of the mighty one has no other refuge." It emphasizes the idea that even the strongest or most powerful individuals rely on something greater than themselves. For example, in modern life, successful leaders often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual practices, acknowledging that they are not invincible.
  • bhadraṁ hi śarma trivarūtham asti: This translates to "Indeed, there is a threefold shelter that is auspicious." The concept of a threefold shelter can be interpreted as the protection provided by physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being. In everyday life, people often seek balance in these areas to achieve overall happiness and security.
  • ta āre hiṁsānām apa didyum ā kṛdhi: This means "May you create a space free from harm." It calls for the establishment of a safe and nurturing environment. In practical terms, this can relate to creating a peaceful home, a supportive workplace, or a community that fosters kindness and understanding.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection through Agni, the fire deity. It highlights the need for a supportive environment and the acknowledgment of our vulnerabilities. In real life, this can be applied by fostering relationships that provide emotional and spiritual support, creating safe spaces in our communities, and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. By invoking these principles, individuals can cultivate a life that is harmonious, balanced, and enriched by the divine presence.
