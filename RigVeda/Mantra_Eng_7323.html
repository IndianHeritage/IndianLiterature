सबाधो यं जनाः इमेऽग्निं हव्यैर्भिर ईळते |जुह्वानासो यतस्रुचः ||
sabādho yaṁ janā ime3 'gniṁ havyebhir īḻate | juhvānāso yatasrucaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sabādho yaṁ janā ime3 'gniṁ havyebhir īḻate | juhvānāso yatasrucaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "These people, who are united, invoke Agni with their offerings; those who are performing the sacrifice with their oblations."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sabādho: united, together
    • yaṁ: whom
    • janā: people, community
    • ime: these
    • agniṁ: Agni, the fire god
    • havyebhir: with offerings, oblations
    • īḻate: invoke, call upon
    • juhvānāso: those who perform the sacrifice
    • yatasrucaḥ: with their oblations, offerings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • Agni, as the fire god, symbolizes transformation, purification, and the connection between the earthly and divine realms.
    • The phrase "united people" suggests that when individuals come together with a common purpose, their collective energy is more potent.
    • Real-life example: In a community gathering, such as a wedding or a festival, when people come together to celebrate and perform rituals, the shared energy and intentions can create a powerful atmosphere of joy and connection.
    • The act of invoking Agni with offerings signifies the importance of gratitude and respect towards the divine, which can be seen in various cultures where offerings are made to deities during rituals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and collective action in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that when we come together with a shared intention, we can create a more profound impact, whether in spiritual rituals or in everyday life.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied to teamwork in professional settings, community service, or any collaborative effort where the combined strength of individuals leads to greater outcomes.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of community and the importance of honoring the divine through our collective actions.
