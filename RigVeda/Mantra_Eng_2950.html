कविर नृचक्षाः अभि शीम अचष्ट ऋतस्य योना विघृत मदन्ती |नानां चक्राते सदनं यथा वेः समानेन क्रतुनā संविदाने ||
kavir nṛcakṣā abhi ṣīm acaṣṭa ṛtasya yonā vighṛte madantī | nānā cakrāte sadanaṁ yathā veḥ samānena kratunā saṁvidāne ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kavir: The wise or the all-knowing.
    • Nṛcakṣā: One who has human-like eyes or perception.
    • Abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • Ṣīm: To protect or to guard.
    • Acaṣṭa: He has seen or perceived.
    • Ṛtasya: Of truth or righteousness.
    • Yonā: The source or womb.
    • Vighṛte: Disturbed or agitated.
    • Madantī: Intoxicated or filled with joy.
    • Nānā: Various or diverse.
    • Cakrāte: Creates or manifests.
    • Sadanaṁ: Abode or dwelling place.
    • Yathā: Just as or in the manner that.
    • Veḥ: The wind or air.
    • Samānena: Equally or uniformly.
    • Kratu: Intention or will.
    • Saṁvidāne: In the arrangement or organization.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise being who perceives the truth and protects it.
    • The reference to "various" suggests the diversity of life and experiences.
    • The "abode" created by this wise being is a harmonious place, akin to how the wind moves freely and uniformly.
    • It emphasizes the importance of intention and organization in creating a balanced life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a wise leader (kavir) perceives the needs of their team and protects their interests, creating a harmonious work environment.
    • In community building, diverse voices (nānā) come together to create a supportive and joyful space (sadanaṁ) for all members.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their intentions (kratu) and how they organize their lives to achieve balance and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, perception, and intention in creating a harmonious existence.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from leadership and community building to personal growth.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more balanced and joyful world.
