को नानाम वचसā सोम्याय मनायुर वा भवति वस्त उष्राः |का इन्द्रस्य युज्यं कः सखित्वं को भ्रात्रं वष्टि कवये का ऊती ||
ko nānāma vacasā somyāya manāyur vā bhavati vasta usrāḥ | ka indrasya yujyaṁ kaḥ sakhitvaṁ ko bhrātraṁ vaṣṭi kavaye ka ūtī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • nā nāma: is not named
    • vacasā: by speech
    • somyāya: to the pleasant one (often referring to the moon or a deity)
    • manāyur: with the mind or intellect
    • vā: or
    • bhavati: becomes
    • vasta: the essence or substance
    • usrāḥ: of the dawn or light
    • ka: who
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • yujyaṁ: is connected or united
    • kaḥ: who
    • sakhitvaṁ: friendship or companionship
    • ko: who
    • bhrātraṁ: brother
    • vaṣṭi: is invoked or called
    • kavaye: for the poet or sage
    • ka: who
    • ūtī: is the wealth or abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about identity and relationships.
    • It reflects on the nature of communication and connection, asking who can truly understand or name the essence of the divine or the relationships we hold.
    • The reference to Indra suggests a connection to power, leadership, and the divine, emphasizing the importance of unity and friendship.
    • It also highlights the significance of invoking the right energies and relationships in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, understanding who your allies are (sakhitvaṁ) can lead to better collaboration and success.
    • In personal relationships, recognizing the essence of each person (vasta) can deepen connections and foster a supportive environment.
    • In community settings, invoking the right energies (kavaye) can lead to collective growth and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding our relationships and the essence of those around us.
    • It encourages us to seek unity and connection, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
    • By reflecting on these questions, we can cultivate deeper relationships and a more harmonious existence.
