आयं कृत्नुर अगृभीतो विश्वजिद उद्भिद इत सोमः |ऋषिर विप्रः काव्येन ||
ayaṁ kṛtnur agṛbhīto viśvajid udbhid it somaḥ | ṛṣir vipraḥ kāvyena ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something immediate or relevant.
  • kṛtnur - This term can be interpreted as "the one who is complete" or "the whole." It suggests a sense of fulfillment or totality.
  • agṛbhīto - Meaning "not grasped" or "unattached," this word conveys a sense of freedom from material possessions or desires.
  • viśvajid - This translates to "the conqueror of all" or "one who has achieved everything." It implies a sense of mastery over the universe.
  • udbhid - This word means "to break forth" or "to emerge." It indicates the idea of new beginnings or revelations.
  • it - A simple word meaning "that" or "thus," often used to connect ideas.
  • somaḥ - Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • ṛṣir - This means "sage" or "seer," representing wisdom and insight.
  • vipraḥ - Translated as "the learned" or "the wise," it denotes a person of knowledge and understanding.
  • kāvyena - This word means "through poetry" or "by means of verse," highlighting the importance of artistic expression in conveying deeper truths.

The mantra can be interpreted as a celebration of completeness and mastery over the universe, achieved through wisdom and artistic expression. It emphasizes the importance of being unattached to material possessions while striving for knowledge and enlightenment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom while remaining unattached to material gains.
  • In creative fields, it highlights the significance of artistic expression as a means to convey profound truths and insights.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to break free from worldly attachments and strive for a higher understanding of existence.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a holistic approach to life, where one seeks knowledge and mastery while remaining unattached to the material world. It encourages individuals to express themselves creatively and to pursue wisdom, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
