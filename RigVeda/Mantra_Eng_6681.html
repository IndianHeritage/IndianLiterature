ऐतु पूषा रयिः भगः स्वस्ति सर्वधातमः |उरुर अध्वा स्वस्तये ||
aitu pūṣā rayir bhagaḥ svasti sarvadhātamaḥ | urur adhvā svastaye ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Pūṣan, grant us wealth and prosperity; may all beings be auspicious. May our paths be broad and conducive to well-being."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Aitu: A call or invocation, often used to address a deity.
    • Pūṣā: Refers to Pūṣan, the deity associated with nourishment, prosperity, and the sun.
    • Rayir: Wealth or riches.
    • Bhagaḥ: Blessings or fortune.
    • Svasti: Auspiciousness or well-being.
    • Sarvadhātamaḥ: The one who is the essence of all beings or the ultimate source.
    • Urur: Broad or expansive.
    • Adhvā: Path or journey.
    • Svastaye: For well-being or auspiciousness.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for prosperity and well-being, invoking the blessings of Pūṣan, who is often associated with the sun and nourishment.
    • In real life, this can be related to seeking success in various endeavors, whether in personal life, business, or community welfare.
    • For example, a farmer may chant this mantra before sowing seeds, hoping for a bountiful harvest, or a student may recite it before exams to seek clarity and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to well-being, encompassing material wealth, spiritual prosperity, and communal harmony.
    • It teaches us to seek not just personal gain but also the welfare of all beings, promoting a sense of interconnectedness.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in business ethics, community service, and personal development, reminding us to pursue success while ensuring the well-being of others.
