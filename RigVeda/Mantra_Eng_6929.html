यā वृत्रहा परावती सनā नवā च चुक्युवे |तā संसत्सु प्र वोचत ||
yā vṛtrahā parāvati sanā navā ca cucyuve | tā saṁsatsu pra vocata ||
  • yā vṛtrahā: This phrase can be translated as "who has slain Vritra." In the context of the Rigveda, Vritra is often depicted as a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstruction. The act of slaying Vritra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos.
  • parāvati: This term means "the one who is supreme" or "the highest." It signifies a divine power or force that is beyond the ordinary, often associated with the ultimate reality or the supreme being.
  • sanā: This word translates to "eternal" or "everlasting." It emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine or the truth being referred to in the mantra.
  • navā ca cucyuve: This can be interpreted as "newly born" or "freshly manifested." It suggests the idea of renewal and the continuous cycle of creation and rebirth.
  • tā saṁsatsu pra vocata: This phrase means "that which is spoken in the assembly." It indicates the importance of communication and sharing of knowledge within a community or group.

  • Real-Life Example: In a modern context, the slaying of Vritra can be likened to overcoming personal challenges or societal issues. For instance, when a community comes together to address a problem such as poverty or discrimination, they are collectively "slaying Vritra" by restoring order and harmony.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the power of divine intervention in overcoming chaos and the importance of unity in addressing challenges. It serves as a reminder that even in the face of adversity, there is a higher power that can guide and support us.
  • Application: This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in leadership, where a leader embodies the qualities of the supreme being by guiding their team through difficulties. It can also be relevant in personal development, encouraging individuals to seek renewal and transformation in their lives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming chaos through divine strength and communal effort. It inspires individuals and communities to strive for order, unity, and renewal, reminding us of the eternal nature of truth and the power of collective action in the face of challenges.
