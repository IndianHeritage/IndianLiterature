विश्वेत ता विष्णुराभरद उरुक्रमस्त्वेषितः |शतम् महिषान् क्षीरपाकम् ओदनं वराहम् इन्द्र एमुषम् ||
viśvet tā viṣṇur ābharad urukramas tveṣitaḥ | śatam mahiṣān kṣīrapākam odanaṁ varāham indra emuṣam ||
  • viśvet tā viṣṇur ābharad urukramas tveṣitaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May Vishnu, the all-pervading one, who is of great strides, bestow upon us." Here, Vishnu represents the divine force that sustains and protects the universe. The term "urukrama" refers to his vast and powerful strides, symbolizing his ability to encompass all aspects of existence.
  • śatam mahiṣān kṣīrapākam odanaṁ varāham indra emuṣam: This part translates to "A hundred powerful beings, like the bull, who are the essence of nourishment, may Indra, the king of gods, bring forth." The "mahiṣān" refers to strong beings, often symbolizing strength and stability. "Kṣīrapākam" indicates the process of making something nourishing, like milk being transformed into a wholesome food. "Odanam" refers to rice or food, while "varāham" signifies the boar, a symbol of abundance and fertility. Indra, as the king of gods, is invoked to provide these blessings.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings for sustenance and strength. It reflects the interconnectedness of the divine with the material world, highlighting how spiritual forces influence our physical well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals or prayers for prosperity and nourishment. For instance, during festivals or family gatherings, invoking this mantra can be a way to seek blessings for abundance in food and health. It can also serve as a reminder to appreciate the sustenance we receive and to share it with others, fostering a sense of community and gratitude.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support for material needs. It teaches us to recognize the role of spirituality in our daily lives and encourages us to cultivate gratitude for the abundance we receive. By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, we can enhance our connection to the divine and promote a harmonious existence.
