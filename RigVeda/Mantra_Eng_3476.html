मन्त्र: सेद ऋभवो यम् अवथा यūयम् इन्द्रश च मार्त्यम् |स धीरभिर अस्तु सन्तिता मेधसातां सो अरवतां ||
sed ṛbhavo yam avatha yūyam indraś ca martyam | sa dhībhir astu sanitā medhasātā so arvatā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sed: but
    • ṛbhavo: those who are righteous
    • yam: whom
    • avatha: you protect
    • yūyam: you (plural)
    • indraś: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ca: and
    • martyam: mortal
    • sa: he
    • dhībhir: with wisdom
    • astu: may it be
    • sanitā: well-being
    • medhasātā: of intelligence
    • so: he
    • arvatā: of strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the protection of the righteous by Indra, the god of strength and rain.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intelligence in achieving well-being.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are righteous and seek protection will be blessed with strength and intelligence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who acts with righteousness and integrity can inspire their team, leading to a productive and harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who prioritize honesty and support for one another often find their bonds strengthened, reflecting the protection and blessings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In education, students who approach their studies with diligence and integrity are often rewarded with knowledge and wisdom, akin to the blessings of intelligence and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of righteousness, wisdom, and strength in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that by embodying these qualities, we can seek protection and blessings from higher powers, leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or academic—applying the principles of this mantra can lead to positive outcomes and a sense of well-being.
