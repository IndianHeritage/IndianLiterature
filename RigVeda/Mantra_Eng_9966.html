युनक्त सीराः वि युगात तनुध्वं कृत्य योनौ वपतिह बीजम् |गिरा च श्रुष्टिः सभराः असन नो नेदीय इति श्रण्यः पक्वम् एयात् ||
yunakta sīrā vi yugā tanudhvaṁ kṛte yonau vapateha bījam | girā ca śruṣṭiḥ sabharā asan no nedīya it sṛṇyaḥ pakvam eyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yunakta: to unite or bring together
    • sīrā: the head or the top
    • vi: apart or separately
    • yugā: the yoke or the bond
    • tanudhvaṁ: you should stretch or expand
    • kṛte: for the sake of or in the act of
    • yonau: in the womb or source
    • vapateha: the seed or essence
    • bījam: the seed
    • girā: through speech or sound
    • ca: and
    • śruṣṭiḥ: creation or manifestation
    • sabharā: with abundance or fullness
    • asan: we are or we exist
    • no: our
    • nedīya: not to be neglected or overlooked
    • it: thus or in this way
    • sṛṇyaḥ: the one who listens or hears
    • pakvam: ripened or matured
    • eyāt: may it come or arrive
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and connection between different elements of existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing the seed of creation, which can be interpreted as ideas, relationships, or projects.
    • The phrase "yunakta sīrā vi yugā" suggests that while we may have different paths or perspectives, there is a need to come together for a common purpose.
    • The mention of "girā ca śruṣṭiḥ" indicates that through communication and expression, we can manifest our intentions and bring forth creation.
    • The idea of "sabharā asan no" highlights the abundance that can arise when we work together and support one another.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, collaboration among team members can lead to innovative solutions and successful projects, reflecting the unity and nurturing of ideas.
    • In community service, individuals coming together to support a cause can create a significant impact, demonstrating the power of collective effort.
    • In personal relationships, open communication fosters understanding and strengthens bonds, allowing for growth and maturity in the relationship.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, communication, and nurturing in all aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that by coming together and supporting one another, we can create a more abundant and fulfilling existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in professional settings, community initiatives, or personal relationships, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards success and harmony.
