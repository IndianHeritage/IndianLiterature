अभि विप्राः अनूषत मूर्धन यज्ञस्य कारवः |दधानाश चक्षसि प्रियम् ||
abhi viprā anūṣata mūrdhan yajñasya kāravaḥ | dadhānāś cakṣasi priyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • viprā: the wise, the learned
    • anūṣata: who are engaged, who are devoted
    • mūrdhan: in the head, in the highest position
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice, of the ritual
    • kāravaḥ: those who perform, those who create
    • dadhānāḥ: holding, possessing
    • cakṣasi: in the sight, in the vision
    • priyam: dear, beloved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of wise individuals who are dedicated to performing rituals and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "abhi viprā anūṣata" emphasizes the role of learned people who are committed to spiritual practices.
    • "Mūrdhan yajñasya kāravaḥ" suggests that these wise individuals hold a significant position in the context of rituals, indicating their leadership and influence.
    • The concluding part "dadhānāś cakṣasi priyam" implies that those who engage in these practices possess a vision that is dear and valuable, highlighting the importance of insight and wisdom in spiritual endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, educators and mentors can be seen as the "viprā" who guide others in their personal and professional lives, emphasizing the value of knowledge and wisdom.
    • Community leaders who organize cultural or spiritual events embody the essence of "yajñasya kāravaḥ," as they create spaces for collective growth and understanding.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who prioritize ethical practices and employee well-being reflect the qualities of those who "hold dear" the vision of a harmonious workplace.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the vital role of wise individuals in society who engage in meaningful practices that benefit the community.
    • It highlights the importance of leadership, vision, and ethical conduct in both spiritual and secular contexts.
    • In real life, this can be applied by recognizing and supporting those who contribute positively to society, whether through education, community service, or ethical leadership.
