वि तद ययुर अरुणयुग्भिर अश्वैश् चित्रं भान्ति उषसश् चन्द्ररथाः | अग्रं यज्ञस्य बृहतो नयन्तीर वि ता बाधन्ते तम ऊर्म्यायाः ||
vi tad yayur aruṇayugbhir aśvaiś citram bhānty uṣasaś candrarathāḥ | agraṁ yajñasya bṛhato nayantīr vi tā bādhante tama ūrmyāyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: indeed
    • tad: that
    • yayur: they have gone
    • aruṇayugbhir: with the red dawns
    • aśvaiś: with horses
    • citram: colorful or splendid
    • bhānty: shining
    • uṣasaś: of the dawns
    • candrarathāḥ: the chariots of the moon
    • agraṁ: the foremost
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice
    • bṛhato: of the great
    • nayantīr: leading
    • vi: indeed
    • tā: they
    • bādhante: dispel
    • tama: darkness
    • ūrmyāyāḥ: of the waves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dawns that bring light and dispel darkness.
    • The "red dawns" symbolize new beginnings and opportunities, much like how each day offers a fresh start.
    • The "chariots of the moon" represent the cyclical nature of time and the balance between light and darkness.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifices (yajñas) in achieving greatness and leading towards enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the arrival of dawn can be seen as a metaphor for hope and renewal, encouraging individuals to pursue their goals.
    • Just as the dawn dispels the darkness of night, personal challenges can be overcome through perseverance and effort.
    • In various cultures, rituals and sacrifices are performed to seek blessings for prosperity and success, reflecting the essence of yajñas.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of light over darkness and the importance of new beginnings.
    • It teaches us that through dedication and sacrifice, we can achieve our aspirations and lead ourselves out of difficult times.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a mindset of optimism and resilience, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community development.
