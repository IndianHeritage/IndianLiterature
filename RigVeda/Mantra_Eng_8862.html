अप्सु मे सोमो अब्रवीद अन्तर विश्वानि भेषजाः | अग्निं च विश्वशम्भुवम् ||
apsu me somo abravīd antar viśvāni bheṣajā | agniṁ ca viśvaśambhuvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsu: in the waters
    • me: my
    • somo: soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • abravīd: spoke or declared
    • antar: within
    • viśvāni: all or every
    • bheṣajā: healing or remedies
    • agniṁ: Agni (the fire god)
    • ca: and
    • viśvaśambhuvam: all-pervading or universal auspiciousness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the sacred drink Soma and the healing properties found in water.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals and its role as a purifying force.
    • The phrase "antar viśvāni bheṣajā" suggests that within all things, there exists a healing essence, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern herbal medicine, many remedies are derived from plants that grow near water sources, illustrating the healing properties of nature.
    • Rituals involving fire, such as lighting candles or bonfires, are often used in various cultures to symbolize purification and the dispelling of negativity.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be seen in holistic health practices, where the mind, body, and spirit are treated as a unified whole.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nature's elements—water and fire—as sources of healing and purification.
    • It encourages us to recognize the healing properties within our environment and the importance of rituals in connecting with these forces.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire practices that promote well-being, such as spending time in nature, engaging in rituals that foster mindfulness, and seeking holistic approaches to health.
