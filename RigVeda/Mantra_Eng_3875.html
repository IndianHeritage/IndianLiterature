प्र ते पूर्वाणि करणानि वोकम् प्र नूतना मगवन या चकर्थ |शक्तीवो यद् विभराऽरोदसी उभे जयन्न अपो मनवे दानुचित्राः ||
pra te pūrvāṇi karaṇāni vocam pra nūtanā maghavan yā cakartha | śaktīvo yad vibharā rodasī ubhe jayann apo manave dānucitrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra te: for you
    • pūrvāṇi: the ancient
    • karaṇāni: actions or deeds
    • vocam: I speak or I declare
    • pra nūtanā: the new
    • maghavan: O bountiful one (often referring to the deity Indra)
    • yā: which
    • cakartha: you have accomplished
    • śaktīvo: with power
    • yad: that
    • vibharā: you have filled
    • rodasī: the two worlds
    • ubhe: both
    • jayann: conquering
    • apo: waters
    • manave: for the mind or intellect
    • dānucitrāḥ: with various gifts or offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine, acknowledging both the ancient and the new actions that have been accomplished.
    • It emphasizes the power of the divine in filling the two worlds, suggesting a balance between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The reference to "waters" can symbolize knowledge, emotions, or the flow of life, which is essential for the mind's growth and understanding.
    • The phrase "dānucitrāḥ" indicates the various gifts that life offers, which can be interpreted as opportunities, experiences, and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might reflect on past experiences (pūrvāṇi) while also embracing new opportunities (nūtanā) to innovate and grow.
    • In personal relationships, acknowledging the history of interactions while being open to new ways of connecting can lead to deeper bonds.
    • In education, combining traditional knowledge with modern techniques can enhance learning experiences, much like the balance of ancient and new in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the past while being open to the future, highlighting the power of the divine in guiding our actions.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the gifts of life and the knowledge we gain through experiences, encouraging a holistic approach to personal and spiritual growth.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as decision-making, personal development, and fostering relationships, reminding us to balance tradition with innovation.
