स नो भगाय वायवे विप्रवीरः सदावृद्धः |सोमो देवेष्वा यमत ||
sa no bhagāya vāyave vipravīraḥ sadāvṛdhaḥ | somo deveṣv ā yamat ||
  • sa no bhagāya vāyave: This phrase can be translated as "May the wind (Vāyu) bring us prosperity (bhagā)." Here, Vāyu represents the vital force or energy that sustains life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of clean air and the environment in promoting health and well-being.
  • vipravīraḥ: This term means "the wise and brave." It emphasizes the qualities of wisdom and courage. In contemporary life, this can be seen in leaders or individuals who make informed decisions and take bold actions for the betterment of society, such as activists fighting for climate change or social justice.
  • sadāvṛdhaḥ: This translates to "always growing" or "ever-increasing." It signifies continuous growth and development. In a practical sense, this can relate to personal development, such as lifelong learning and self-improvement, where individuals strive to enhance their skills and knowledge throughout their lives.
  • somo deveṣv ā yamat: This part can be interpreted as "May the soma (a sacred drink) be with the gods." Soma symbolizes nourishment and divine inspiration. In modern contexts, this can be likened to the pursuit of spiritual or mental nourishment through practices like meditation, yoga, or engaging in creative activities that uplift the spirit.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of environmental health, wisdom, courage, continuous growth, and spiritual nourishment. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek prosperity through sustainable practices, make informed and brave choices, commit to personal development, and engage in activities that foster spiritual well-being. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving a fulfilling and meaningful life.
