यम् मृत्युः पुरुष्पृहं विदद् विश्वस्य धायसे |प्र स्वादनं पितूनाम् अस्तातातिं चिद् आयवे ||
yam martyaḥ puruspṛhaṁ vidad viśvasya dhāyase | pra svādanam pitūnām astatātiṁ cid āyave ||
  • yam martyaḥ: This phrase translates to "whom the mortal (human)". It refers to the human condition and the transient nature of life. In real life, this can be seen in the inevitability of aging and death, reminding us to cherish our time.
  • puruspṛhaṁ: This means "the one who desires (or longs for) the fullness of life". It signifies the human aspiration for fulfillment and completeness. For example, people often seek happiness through relationships, careers, and personal achievements, reflecting this deep-seated desire.
  • vidad viśvasya dhāyase: This translates to "you who support the universe". It indicates a divine or cosmic force that sustains all existence. In practical terms, this can be likened to the interconnectedness of life, where every action has a ripple effect on the world around us, such as environmental conservation efforts that support the planet.
  • pra svādanam: This means "the taste of the offerings". It suggests the importance of experiencing and appreciating the fruits of our labor. In everyday life, this can be seen in the joy of sharing a meal with loved ones, where the act of eating becomes a celebration of life and relationships.
  • pitūnām: This translates to "of the ancestors". It emphasizes the connection to our forebears and the wisdom they impart. In real life, honoring our ancestors can be seen in cultural practices, such as festivals or rituals that celebrate family heritage and history.
  • astatātiṁ: This means "the eternal". It refers to the timeless aspect of existence that transcends the physical realm. In practical terms, this can be understood through the legacy we leave behind, such as the impact of our actions on future generations.
  • cid āyave: This translates to "in the consciousness of life". It highlights the awareness and mindfulness that come with living fully. In real life, this can be applied through practices like meditation or mindfulness, which encourage individuals to be present and aware of their thoughts and actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the transient nature of life, the pursuit of fulfillment, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder to appreciate our experiences, honor our heritage, and live mindfully. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek deeper connections with themselves, their families, and the universe, ultimately leading to a more meaningful and conscious existence.
