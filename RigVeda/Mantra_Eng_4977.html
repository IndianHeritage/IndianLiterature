प्र नु वोचाऽ सुतेषु वां वीर्याणि चक्रथुः |हतासो वां पितरो देवशत्रव इन्द्राग्नि जीवथो युवम् ||
pra nu vocā suteṣu vāṁ vīryā3 yāni cakrathuḥ | hatāso vām pitaro devaśatrava indrāgnī jīvatho yuvam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "We invoke you, O Indra and Agni, to grant us strength and valor, as you have done for our forefathers. May you, who are the slayers of our enemies, keep us alive and prosperous."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra nu vocā: "We invoke" - This signifies a call to the divine, seeking assistance and blessings.
    • suteṣu: "in the offerings" - Refers to the rituals and offerings made to the deities.
    • vāṁ: "you" - Directly addressing the deities, Indra and Agni.
    • vīryāni: "strength" - Represents physical and mental strength, valor, and courage.
    • cakrathuḥ: "you have done" - Acknowledges the past actions of the deities in granting strength.
    • hatāso: "slayers" - Refers to the deities as protectors who vanquish enemies.
    • vām: "you" - Again addressing the deities.
    • pitaro: "forefathers" - Acknowledges the lineage and the blessings received from ancestors.
    • devaśatrava: "enemy of the gods" - Refers to those who oppose divine will, emphasizing the need for protection.
    • indrāgnī: "Indra and Agni" - Indra is the king of gods, and Agni is the fire god, both representing power and purification.
    • jīvatho: "may you keep us alive" - A plea for protection and sustenance.
    • yuvam: "you both" - Referring to the dual invocation of Indra and Agni.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be recited during significant life events such as weddings or new ventures, where individuals seek strength and protection from challenges.
    • In times of conflict or personal struggle, individuals may invoke the essence of this mantra to find courage and resilience, much like how athletes prepare mentally before a competition.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the collective strength derived from ancestors and the importance of unity in facing adversities.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and protection. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing our roots and the blessings of our ancestors while seeking guidance from powerful deities like Indra and Agni. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various situations where individuals or communities face challenges, reminding them of their inner strength and the support they can draw from their heritage and divine forces. It serves as a powerful affirmation of resilience, courage, and the pursuit of prosperity in the face of adversity.
