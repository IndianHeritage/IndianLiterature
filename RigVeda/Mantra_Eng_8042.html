उत ते शुष्मासा ईरते सिन्धोर ऊर्मेर इव स्वनः |वाणस्य चोदयापविम ||
ut te śuṣmāsa īrate sindhor ūrmer iva svanaḥ | vāṇasya codayā pavim ||
  • Mantra: "ut te śuṣmāsa īrate sindhor ūrmer iva svanaḥ | vāṇasya codayā pavim ||"
  • Translation: "May the sound of the waves of the ocean, like the sweet sound of the flute, inspire you to speak eloquently."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ut: a particle often used to denote a wish or desire.
    • te: means "your" or "to you."
    • śuṣmāsa: refers to "sweet" or "pleasant," often used to describe sounds.
    • īrate: means "to inspire" or "to move."
    • sindhor: means "of the ocean" or "of the sea."
    • ūrmer: refers to "waves" or "the sound of waves."
    • iva: means "like" or "as."
    • svanaḥ: means "sound" or "voice."
    • vāṇasya: means "of speech" or "of eloquence."
    • codayā: means "to inspire" or "to urge."
    • pavim: refers to "to speak" or "to express."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In public speaking, the ability to convey thoughts eloquently can be likened to the sweet sound of a flute, captivating the audience much like the waves of the ocean draw attention.
    • In music, the harmonious sounds produced can inspire creativity and expression, similar to how the ocean's waves can evoke emotions and thoughts.
    • In everyday conversations, when one speaks with clarity and beauty, it can create a positive impact on relationships, akin to the soothing sounds of nature.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of eloquence and the power of sound in communication. It suggests that just as the waves of the ocean can inspire and move us, so too can the beauty of speech. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as public speaking, artistic expression, and interpersonal communication. By striving to communicate with clarity and beauty, we can enhance our connections with others and inspire them in return.
