येन वंसाम पृतनासु शर्धतस तरन्तो आर्य आदिशः |स त्वं नो वर्ध prayasā शचिवसो जिन्वा धियो वसुविदः ||
yena vaṁsāma pṛtanāsu śardhatas taranto arya ādiśaḥ | sa tvaṁ no vardha prayasā śacīvaso jinvā dhiyo vasuvidaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yena: by whom
    • vaṁsāma: we are nourished
    • pṛtanāsu: in our endeavors
    • śardhatas: with strength
    • taranto: those who are swift
    • arya: noble or honorable
    • ādiśaḥ: who directs or guides
    • sa: he
    • tvaṁ: you
    • no: us
    • vardha: may you increase
    • prayasā: through effort
    • śacīvaso: of the wise
    • jinvā: may you inspire
    • dhiyo: our intellects
    • vasuvidaḥ: those who know wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine force that nourishes and guides us in our endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and wisdom in achieving our goals.
    • The invocation of the noble ones suggests a call for guidance from those who have attained wisdom and success.
    • It highlights the relationship between effort and the increase of knowledge and wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking guidance from mentors or leaders who have achieved success in their fields.
    • Students can invoke this mantra while studying, asking for the strength and wisdom to understand complex subjects and excel in their exams.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on this mantra when setting goals, recognizing that effort and guidance are essential for growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of effort, guidance, and success.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and support from those who have walked the path before them.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal and professional growth, fostering a mindset of continuous learning and collaboration.
