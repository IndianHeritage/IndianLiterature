भुवनस्य पितरं गीरभिराभि रुद्रं दिवा वर्धयाऽरुद्रमक्तौ |बृहन्तं ऋष्वं अजराṁ सुषुम्नं ऋधग्घुवेम कवीनेषितासः ||
bhuvanasya pitaraṁ gīrbhir ābhī rudraṁ divā vardhayā rudram aktau | bṛhantam ṛṣvam ajaraṁ suṣumnam ṛdhag ghuvema kavineṣitāsaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Rudra, the father of the universe, may we be nourished by your words, and may you be exalted during the day. We invoke the great, powerful, ageless, and ever-vibrant Rudra, who is the source of all inspiration."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhuvanasya: of the universe
    • pitaraṁ: father
    • gīrbhir: by words
    • ābhī: nourished
    • rudraṁ: O Rudra
    • divā: during the day
    • vardhayā: may you be exalted
    • aktau: in action
    • bṛhantam: great
    • ṛṣvam: powerful
    • ajaraṁ: ageless
    • suṣumnam: ever-vibrant
    • ṛdhag: source
    • ghuvema: we invoke
    • kavineṣitāsaḥ: who is the source of all inspiration
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and inspiration in our lives.
    • Rudra, often associated with transformation and healing, represents the forces of nature and the universe.
    • By invoking Rudra, we seek strength, wisdom, and the ability to navigate life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In moments of uncertainty or difficulty, individuals can recite this mantra to seek clarity and strength.
    • It can be used in meditation practices to connect with the universal energies and find inner peace.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be chanted during gatherings to invoke collective strength and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking divine guidance.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength and wisdom, fostering resilience in the face of challenges.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of words and intentions in shaping our reality and connecting with the universe.
