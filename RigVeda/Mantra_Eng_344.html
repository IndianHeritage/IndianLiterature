मन्त्र: शाश्वद इन्द्रः पोप्रुथद्भिर् जिगाय नानदद्भिः शाश्वसद्भिर् धनानि | स नो हिरण्यरथं दंसनावान स नः सन्तिता सन् ये स नो 'दात ||
śaśvad indraḥ popruthadbhir jigāya nānadadbhiḥ śāśvasadbhir dhanāni | sa no hiraṇyarathaṁ daṁsanāvān sa naḥ sanitā sanaye sa no 'dāt ||
  • śaśvad indraḥ: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles. The term "śaśvad" means eternal or everlasting, indicating the timeless nature of Indra's qualities.
  • popruthadbhir: This word can be broken down into "poprutha," which means to be spread out or to be vast. It suggests the expansive nature of Indra's influence and power.
  • jigāya nānadadbhiḥ: "Jigāya" means to conquer or to achieve victory, while "nānada" refers to joy or happiness. This part of the mantra emphasizes that through Indra's strength, one can achieve victory that brings joy and prosperity.
  • śāśvasadbhir dhanāni: "Dhanāni" means wealth or resources, and "śāśvasadbhir" implies that these resources are everlasting. This suggests that the blessings of Indra lead to sustainable wealth and prosperity.
  • sa no hiraṇyarathaṁ: "Hiraṇyarathaṁ" refers to the golden chariot, symbolizing wealth and abundance. This line expresses a desire for Indra to bless us with this golden chariot, representing both material and spiritual wealth.
  • daṁsanāvān: This term can be interpreted as one who is powerful and capable of overcoming challenges. It signifies the strength needed to face life's adversities.
  • sa naḥ sanitā sanaye: "Sanitā" means to protect or to guide, while "sanaye" refers to the act of bringing together or uniting. This line calls for Indra's guidance and protection in our endeavors.
  • sa no 'dāt: This phrase means "may he give us," indicating a plea for blessings and support from Indra.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for Indra, invoking his eternal strength and blessings for prosperity, joy, and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during times of struggle or when seeking success in endeavors. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra before starting a new business venture, hoping to attract wealth and overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher powers in our pursuits, emphasizing that true success is not just about material wealth but also about joy and fulfillment in life.
