इमं यज्ञं चनो धा अग्न उषन् यं त आसानो जुहुते हविष्मान् |भरद्वाजेषु दधिषे सुवृक्तिम अवीर वाजस्य गध्यस्य सातौ ||
imaṁ yajñaṁ cano dhā agna uśan yaṁ ta āsāno juhute haviṣmān | bharadvājeṣu dadhiṣe suvṛktim avīr vājasya gadhyasya sātau ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, the one who is invoked, may you accept this sacrifice. Those who are seated here, may they offer their oblations with devotion. O Bharadvaja, may you bestow upon us the excellent qualities of strength and valor."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ yajñaṁ: this sacrifice
    • cano: we invoke
    • dhā: may you accept
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • uśan: the one who is invoked
    • yaṁ: who
    • ta: those
    • āsāno: seated here
    • juhute: may they offer
    • haviṣmān: oblations
    • bharadvājeṣu: O Bharadvaja (a sage or a clan)
    • dadhiṣe: may you bestow
    • suvṛktim: excellent qualities
    • avīr: of strength
    • vājasya: of valor
    • gadhyasya: of the one who is strong
    • sātau: in the battle
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies, particularly Agni, who represents the transformative power of fire and is a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It highlights the communal aspect of rituals, where individuals come together to offer their prayers and sacrifices, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
    • The mention of Bharadvaja signifies the importance of wisdom and strength in achieving success, both in spiritual and worldly endeavors.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various communal activities, such as team-building exercises, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal.
    • It can also be relevant in personal development, where one seeks to cultivate inner strength and wisdom, drawing inspiration from the qualities of Agni and Bharadvaja.
    • In times of challenge, invoking the spirit of this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek support from the community and to harness one's inner strength to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the transformative power of divine energies. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and the qualities we must cultivate to navigate life's challenges. By invoking these principles, we can foster a sense of purpose and resilience in our daily lives.
