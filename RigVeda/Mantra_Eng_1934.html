अताप्यमाने अवसावन्ती अनु ष्यामा रोधसी देवपुत्रे |उभे देवानाम उभयैभिर अह्नां dyावā रक्षतं पृथिवी नो अभ्वात ||
atapyamāne avasāvantī anu ṣyāma rodasī devaputre | ubhe devānām ubhayebhir ahnāṁ dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||
  • Mantra: "atapyamāne avasāvantī anu ṣyāma rodasī devaputre | ubhe devānām ubhayebhir ahnāṁ dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atapyamāne: When the sun is shining or when it is hot
    • avasāvantī: The one who provides shelter or protection
    • anu: Following or in accordance with
    • ṣyāma: The dark or the night
    • rodasī: The two realms, often interpreted as the sky and the earth
    • devaputre: O son of the gods
    • ubhe: Both
    • devānām: Of the gods
    • ubhayebhir: By both
    • ahnām: Of the day and night
    • dyāvā: The sky
    • rakṣatam: May protect
    • pṛthivī: The earth
    • no: Our
    • abhvāt: May not be harmed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the protective powers of the divine, particularly in the context of the sun's heat and the shelter it provides.
    • It emphasizes the balance between day and night, and the need for protection from the extremes of nature.
    • The phrase "O son of the gods" suggests a direct appeal to divine beings for safeguarding both the sky and the earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for protection from harsh weather conditions that can affect their crops. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine intervention to ensure a bountiful harvest.
    • In daily life, individuals may seek protection from various challenges, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual. This mantra serves as a reminder of the need for support from higher powers.
    • During natural disasters, communities often come together to pray for safety and protection, reflecting the essence of this mantra in seeking divine shelter.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection and balance in life.
    • It teaches us to seek shelter not only from physical elements but also from emotional and spiritual turmoil.
    • In a world filled with uncertainties, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence that watches over us, encouraging us to maintain harmony between the forces of nature.
