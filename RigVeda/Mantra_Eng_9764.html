देवेभिर nv इषितो यज्ञियेबिर अग्निं स्तोषाण्य अजराम बृहन्तम् |यो भानुना पृथिवीं द्यां उतेमां आतताण रोडसी अन्तरिक्षम् ||
devebhir nv iṣito yajñiyebhir agniṁ stoṣāṇy ajaram bṛhantam | yo bhānunā pṛthivīṁ dyām utemām ātatāna rodasī antarikṣam ||
  • Translation: "May the gods, who are invoked through sacrifices, inspire us to praise Agni, the eternal and great one, who has spread the earth and the sky with his light, and who has established the realms of the atmosphere and the heavens."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • nv: indeed
    • iṣito: invoked
    • yajñiyebhir: through sacrifices
    • agniṁ: Agni (the fire god)
    • stoṣāṇy: to praise
    • ajaram: eternal
    • bṛhantam: great
    • yo: who
    • bhānunā: with light
    • pṛthivīṁ: the earth
    • dyām: the sky
    • utemām: has spread
    • ātatāna: has established
    • rodasī: the realms
    • antarikṣam: the atmosphere
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni, not just as a deity but as a symbol of light, energy, and transformation.
    • Agni represents the fire that purifies and sustains life, making it a central figure in Vedic rituals and daily life.
    • The invocation of the gods through sacrifices highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and the material world.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, the essence of this mantra can be seen in the way we honor and respect nature and the elements, recognizing their role in our existence.
    • For example, in environmental conservation efforts, we acknowledge the importance of fire (energy) in maintaining ecological balance.
    • Additionally, the practice of gratitude and reverence for the forces that sustain us can be reflected in daily rituals, such as lighting a candle or a fire during family gatherings, symbolizing warmth and unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of energy and light in our lives, urging us to recognize and honor the divine forces that support our existence.
    • By integrating the principles of this mantra into our lives, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of maintaining harmony with the natural world.
