प्रजाः ह तिस्रो अत्यायम् ईयुर न्यान्याः अर्कम् अभितो विविश्रे |बृहद् ध तस्थौ भुवनेषु अन्तः पवमानो हरिता आ विवेश ||
prajā ha tisro atyāyam īyur ny a1nyā arkam abhito viviśre | bṛhad dha tasthau bhuvaneṣv antaḥ pavamāno harita ā viveśa ||
  • prajā ha tisro atyāyam īyur ny a1nyā arkam abhito viviśre
    • prajā: This term refers to "creatures" or "beings." It signifies the diversity of life forms in the universe.
    • ha: An emphatic particle, often used to assert or emphasize a statement.
    • tisro: Meaning "three," it can symbolize various triads in life, such as the three stages of existence: birth, life, and death.
    • atyāyam: This word means "beyond" or "transcending," indicating a state that surpasses ordinary existence.
    • īyur: Translates to "may they live" or "may they thrive," expressing a wish for the well-being of all beings.
    • ny a1nyā: This can be interpreted as "other" or "different," suggesting the uniqueness of each being.
    • arkam: Refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with the sun or divine illumination.
    • abhito: Meaning "around" or "in all directions," indicating the omnipresence of light and energy.
    • viviśre: This term means "to enter" or "to pervade," suggesting the idea of light and energy permeating all aspects of life.
  • bṛhad dha tasthau bhuvaneṣv antaḥ pavamāno harita ā viveśa
    • bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," it signifies the expansive nature of the universe.
    • dha: This word can mean "to hold" or "to support," indicating stability and strength.
    • tasthau: Translates to "stood" or "remained," suggesting permanence in the face of change.
    • bhuvaneṣv: Refers to "in the worlds" or "in the realms," indicating the various dimensions of existence.
    • antaḥ: Meaning "within," it emphasizes the inner essence of beings and the universe.
    • pavamāno: This term means "the purifying one," often associated with the wind or breath, symbolizing life force.
    • harita: Refers to "green" or "life-giving," symbolizing growth, fertility, and renewal.
    • ā viveśa: Meaning "entered," it suggests the integration of life force into the fabric of existence.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the vital forces that sustain them. It highlights the importance of light, growth, and the purifying energies that permeate our existence. In real life, this can be seen in the way we nurture our environment, support each other, and strive for personal and collective growth. The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the world around us and to recognize the divine energy that flows through all living things. By embracing these principles, we can foster harmony and well-being in our lives and communities.
