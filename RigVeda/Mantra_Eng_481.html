मन्त्र: असाम्य ओजो बिभृताः सुदानवो 'सामि धूतयः शवः |ऋषिद्विषे मरुतः परिमण्यव इषुं न सृजत द्विषम् ||
asāmy ojo bibhṛthā sudānavo 'sāmi dhūtayaḥ śavaḥ | ṛṣidviṣe marutaḥ parimanyava iṣuṁ na sṛjata dviṣam ||
  • asāmy ojo bibhṛthā: This phrase can be translated as "the unequaled energy that is held." Here, "asāmy" refers to something that is unmatched or unequal, while "ojo" signifies energy or vitality. In real life, this can be likened to the unique strengths and talents that individuals possess, which can be harnessed for personal and communal growth.
  • sudānavo 'sāmi dhūtayaḥ: This part translates to "the well-formed and pure ones." "Sudānavo" implies something that is well-formed or excellent, and "dhūtayaḥ" refers to those who are pure or cleansed. In a practical context, this can relate to individuals or communities that strive for purity in their actions and intentions, promoting ethical behavior and integrity.
  • śavaḥ: This term means "the dead" or "the lifeless." It can symbolize the absence of vitality or spirit. In a real-life scenario, this could represent situations or environments that lack enthusiasm or motivation, highlighting the importance of fostering a lively and engaging atmosphere in various aspects of life.
  • ṛṣidviṣe marutaḥ: This translates to "the seers who are opposed to the winds." "ṛṣidviṣe" refers to the wise or enlightened ones, while "marutaḥ" signifies the winds, often associated with change and movement. This can be interpreted as the need for wisdom and foresight when facing challenges or changes in life, akin to how leaders or mentors guide others through turbulent times.
  • parimanyava: This word means "those who are vigilant or watchful." In real life, this can be related to individuals or groups who remain alert and aware of their surroundings, ensuring safety and preparedness in various situations, such as in community safety programs or disaster preparedness initiatives.
  • iṣuṁ na sṛjata dviṣam: This phrase translates to "do not create enmity." "Iṣuṁ" refers to arrows or weapons, and "dviṣam" means enmity or hostility. This emphasizes the importance of peace and harmony in society, advocating for conflict resolution and understanding rather than aggression. In practical terms, this can be applied in conflict mediation, community building, and promoting dialogue among differing groups.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of harnessing unique energies for the greater good, promoting purity in actions, and fostering wisdom in the face of challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of vigilance and the need to avoid creating enmity in our interactions. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in personal development, community engagement, and conflict resolution, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious and productive society.
