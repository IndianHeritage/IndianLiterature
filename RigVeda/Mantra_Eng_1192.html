युवाभ्यां देवी धिषणा मदायेंद्राग्नī सोमं उषती सुनोति |ताव अश्विनाः भद्रहस्ताः सुपाणि आ धावतं मधुनā पृङ्क्तं अप्सु ||
yuvābhyāṁ devī dhiṣaṇā madāyendrāgnī somam uśatī sunoti | tāv aśvinā bhadrahastā supāṇī ā dhāvatam madhunā pṛṅktam apsu ||
  • yuvābhyāṁ: This word refers to "two young ones" or "youths." In the context of the mantra, it symbolizes the divine energies or forces that are youthful and vibrant.
  • devī: This translates to "goddess" or "divine." It signifies the presence of divine feminine energy, which is often associated with nurturing and creation.
  • dhiṣaṇā: This means "intellect" or "wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in achieving one's goals.
  • madāyendrāgnī: This can be broken down into "madā" (intoxication or joy), "indra" (the king of gods), and "agnī" (fire). Together, it suggests a powerful, joyful energy that is both divine and transformative.
  • somam: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine bliss.
  • uśatī: This means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It indicates the presence of light and clarity in one's life.
  • sunoti: This translates to "to nourish" or "to support." It signifies the nurturing aspect of the divine energies.
  • tāv: This means "those" or "these," referring to the divine forces being invoked.
  • aśvinā: This refers to the "Aśvins," the twin gods of medicine and health in Vedic mythology, symbolizing healing and well-being.
  • bhadrahastā: This means "with auspicious hands," indicating the blessings and positive actions of the divine.
  • supāṇī: This translates to "good feet," symbolizing the ability to move forward positively in life.
  • ā dhāvatam: This means "to run towards" or "to approach," indicating a proactive approach to life and challenges.
  • madhunā: This translates to "with sweetness" or "honey," symbolizing the sweetness of life and the rewards of hard work.
  • pṛṅktam: This means "to be woven" or "to be connected," indicating the interconnectedness of all aspects of life.
  • apsu: This refers to "in the waters," symbolizing the source of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.

Conclusion: This mantra invokes the divine energies represented by the youthful Aśvins, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, nourishment, and auspicious actions in our lives. It highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors, particularly in health and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of decision-making, healing, and personal growth, reminding us to embrace the positive energies around us and to act with intention and clarity. By acknowledging the divine forces in our lives, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
