यस्त्वद धोतā पूर्वो अग्ने यजीयान द्वितā च सत्तā स्वधया च शम्भुः |तस्यानु धर्म प्र यजाऽ चिकीर्त्वोऽथा नो धा अध्वरं देववीतौ ||
yas tvad dhotā pūrvo agne yajīyān dvitā ca sattā svadhayā ca śambhuḥ | tasyānu dharma pra yajā cikitvo 'thā no dhā adhvaraṁ devavītau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvad: from you
    • dhotā: the one who purifies
    • pūrvo: the ancient
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • yajīyān: worthy of worship
    • dvitā: the second
    • ca: and
    • sattā: existence
    • svadhayā: by one's own will
    • ca: and
    • śambhuḥ: auspicious
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a purifier and a divine presence in rituals.
    • The phrase "yas tvad dhotā" refers to the one who purifies and is connected to the divine essence of Agni.
    • "Pūrvo agne" emphasizes the ancient and timeless nature of Agni, highlighting the continuity of spiritual practices.
    • "Yajīyān" indicates the importance of worship and reverence towards Agni, suggesting that he is worthy of offerings.
    • The mention of "sattā" and "svadhayā" reflects the idea of existence and self-determination, implying that through worship, one can align with the divine will.
    • "Śambhuḥ" signifies auspiciousness, indicating that the blessings of Agni lead to prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the sacred fire (Havan) is used to purify offerings and invoke blessings.
    • In modern life, the concept of "purification" can be seen in practices like meditation and mindfulness, where individuals seek to cleanse their minds and spirits.
    • The idea of self-determination ("svadhayā") resonates with personal development practices, where individuals take charge of their lives and decisions, aligning with their true purpose.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine through worship and purification.
    • It serves as a reminder of the ancient traditions that continue to hold relevance in contemporary spiritual practices.
    • In real life, the principles of purification, worship, and self-determination can guide individuals towards a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to seek auspiciousness and harmony in our lives through our actions and intentions.
