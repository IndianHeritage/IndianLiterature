एवेद एष तुविकूर्मिर वाजाम् एको वज्रहस्तः |सनाद अमृत्क्तो दयते ||
eved eṣa tuvikūrmir vājām̐ eko vajrahastaḥ | sanād amṛkto dayate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eved: indeed, this
    • eṣa: this, he
    • tuvikūrmir: the one who is strong, powerful
    • vājām̐: of the horses, strength
    • eko: one, alone
    • vajrahastaḥ: with a hand like thunder, powerful
    • sanād: from the beginning, eternally
    • amṛkto: immortal, free from death
    • dayate: gives, bestows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity, often interpreted as a divine force or deity, who is strong and capable of bestowing strength and blessings.
    • The phrase "vajrahastaḥ" suggests that this entity has the power of thunder, symbolizing immense strength and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The term "amṛkto" indicates that this force is eternal and beyond the limitations of mortality, emphasizing the idea of everlasting support and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals often seek strength and guidance during challenging times, much like how this mantra invokes a powerful force for support.
    • In leadership, a strong leader can be seen as a "vajrahastaḥ," guiding their team with strength and decisiveness, inspiring confidence and resilience.
    • In nature, the thunderstorm can be viewed as a manifestation of the "vajrahastaḥ," where the power of nature reminds us of the forces beyond our control that can also provide renewal and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking strength and guidance from powerful forces in our lives, whether they be spiritual, natural, or within ourselves.
    • It teaches us that there is an eternal source of support available to us, encouraging us to tap into our inner strength and the strength of the universe.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering resilience, seeking mentorship, and recognizing the powerful forces that aid us in our journeys.
