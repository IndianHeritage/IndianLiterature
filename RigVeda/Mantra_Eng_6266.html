ययोर्धि प्र यज्ञा असूरे सन्ति सूरयः |तā यज्ञस्याध्वरस्य प्रचेतसā स्वधाभिर यā पिबतः सोम्यम् मधु ||
yayor adhi pra yajñā asūre santi sūrayaḥ | tā yajñasyādhvarasya pracetasā svadhābhir yā pibataḥ somyam madhu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yayor: of whom
    • adhi: above
    • pra: in front of
    • yajñā: sacrifices
    • asūre: in the non-divine
    • santi: exist
    • sūrayaḥ: the divine beings
    • tā: those
    • yajñasyādhvarasya: of the sacrifice's ritual
    • pracetasā: with intelligence
    • svadhābhir: with offerings
    • yā: who
    • pibataḥ: drink
    • somyam: the soma (sacred drink)
    • madhu: honey
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine beings who exist above the sacrifices made by mortals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of performing rituals with intelligence and proper offerings.
    • The reference to "soma" and "madhu" symbolizes the spiritual nourishment and bliss that come from divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of mindfulness in our actions. Just as the divine beings are invoked through intelligent rituals, we can approach our daily tasks with awareness and intention.
    • For instance, in a corporate setting, a team that collaborates with a clear purpose and mutual respect can achieve greater success, akin to a well-performed yajña.
    • Moreover, the concept of "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment in life, where we seek experiences that nourish our spirit, such as spending time with loved ones or engaging in creative pursuits.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of performing our duties with awareness and intention, recognizing the divine presence in our actions.
    • It teaches us that through mindful rituals—whether in personal life, work, or community—we can connect with something greater than ourselves.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek spiritual nourishment and joy in our lives, reminding us that our actions can lead to a deeper connection with the divine.
