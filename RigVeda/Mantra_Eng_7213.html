मां नो हेतिर विवस्वता आदित्याः कृत्रिमा शरुः |पुरा नु जरसो वधीत ||
mā no hetir vivasvata ādityāḥ kṛtrimā śaruḥ | purā nu jaraso vadhīt ||
  • mā no hetir vivasvata ādityāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the sun (Āditya) not be our enemy." Here, "mā" means "not," "no" refers to "our," "hetir" means "enemy," and "vivasvata" refers to the sun or the radiant one. The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, and in this context, it symbolizes positivity and strength.
  • kṛtrimā śaruḥ: This part translates to "may the artificial (or man-made) be removed." "Kṛtrimā" means "artificial" or "not natural," and "śaruḥ" can be interpreted as "removal" or "destruction." This suggests a desire to eliminate falsehoods or artificial constructs that may hinder our progress or well-being.
  • purā nu jaraso vadhīt: This translates to "may the old age (jarasa) not kill us." "Purā" means "formerly" or "in the past," "nu" is an expression of hope or desire, and "vadhīt" means "to kill" or "to destroy." This line expresses a wish to overcome the limitations and decay associated with aging, emphasizing the importance of vitality and youthfulness.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the sun can be seen as a metaphor for positivity and energy. For instance, people often seek sunlight for its health benefits, such as Vitamin D production, which is essential for bone health and overall well-being.
    • The call to remove artificial constructs can be related to the current trend of seeking authenticity in various aspects of life, such as social media, where individuals strive to present their true selves rather than curated personas.
    • The desire to overcome the effects of aging is evident in the wellness industry, where many invest in health supplements, fitness regimes, and skincare products to maintain youthfulness and vitality.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for harmony with natural forces, the pursuit of authenticity, and the aspiration to transcend the limitations of aging. In a world where artificiality often prevails, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek genuine experiences and maintain a positive outlook on life. Its significance lies in its applicability to personal growth, health, and the quest for a fulfilling existence, encouraging individuals to embrace their true selves while striving for vitality and connection with the natural world.
