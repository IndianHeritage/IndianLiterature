कृणोति अस्मै वरिवो य इत्तेन्द्राय सोमं उषते सुनोति | सदृचीनैना मनसाविवेनेन तं इत् सखायं कृणुते समत्सु ||
kṛṇoty asmai varivo ya itthendrāya somam uśate sunoti | sadhrīcīnena manasāvivenan tam it sakhāyaṁ kṛṇute samatsu ||
  • kṛṇoty asmai varivo: This phrase can be translated as "He who bestows great wealth." The term "kṛṇoty" means to create or to make, while "varivo" refers to wealth or abundance. In real life, this can be seen in the context of individuals or organizations that contribute to the prosperity of others, such as philanthropists who donate to education or healthcare.
  • ya itthendrāya somam uśate: This translates to "who offers the soma to Indra." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, associated with immortality and divine inspiration. Indra is the king of the gods, representing strength and power. In modern terms, this can be likened to the act of offering support or resources to leaders or influential figures who can effect positive change in society.
  • sunoti: This word means "he hears" or "he listens." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and attentive. In everyday life, this can relate to the value of active listening in communication, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • sadhrīcīnena manasāvivenan: This phrase means "with a harmonious mind." "Sadhrīcīnena" refers to harmony or agreement, while "manasāvivenan" relates to the mind or consciousness. This highlights the significance of mental alignment and cooperation in achieving common goals, such as teamwork in a workplace or community projects.
  • tam it sakhāyaṁ kṛṇute samatsu: This translates to "he creates friendship among the united." The term "sakhāyaṁ" means friendship, and "samatsu" refers to those who are united or together. This underscores the importance of fostering relationships and camaraderie in any group setting, be it in families, workplaces, or social organizations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of generosity, support, active listening, mental harmony, and friendship. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community building, teamwork, and leadership. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute to a more prosperous and harmonious society, fostering connections that lead to collective success and well-being.
