प्रातर् देवīm अदितिं जोहवीमि मध्यंदिन उदिता सूर्यस्य |राये मित्रावरुणा सर्वतातेले तोकाय तनयाय शं योः ||
prātar devīm aditiṁ johavīmi madhyaṁdina uditā sūryasya | rāye mitrāvaruṇā sarvatāteḻe tokāya tanayāya śaṁ yoḥ ||
  • Translation: "In the morning, I invoke the goddess Aditi, who rises at midday with the sun. May she, along with Mitra and Varuna, grant us prosperity and protect our offspring."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prātar: morning
    • devīm: goddess
    • aditiṁ: Aditi, the mother of gods
    • johavīmi: I invoke
    • madhyaṁdina: midday
    • uditā: risen
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • rāye: prosperity
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Mitra and Varuna, deities of friendship and cosmic order
    • sarvatāteḻe: in all directions
    • tokāya: for offspring
    • tanayāya: for children
    • śaṁ yoḥ: may they grant peace
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings at the start of the day, particularly in the morning when the sun rises.
    • Aditi represents the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe, symbolizing the motherly care that encompasses all beings.
    • Mitra and Varuna are invoked for their roles in maintaining harmony and order in the universe, highlighting the interconnectedness of all life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • Morning rituals or prayers can be a way to set a positive tone for the day, similar to how this mantra invokes divine presence and blessings.
    • In family life, invoking blessings for children and prosperity can be seen in practices such as naming ceremonies or family gatherings where prayers are offered for well-being.
    • In a broader societal context, the invocation of harmony and order can be reflected in community efforts to promote peace and cooperation among diverse groups.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our daily lives.
    • It highlights the significance of nurturing relationships, both within families and communities, and the need for harmony in our interactions.
    • By invoking these deities, we acknowledge our dependence on the greater forces of the universe and our responsibility to foster peace and prosperity in our lives.
