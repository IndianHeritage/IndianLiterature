आदाय श्येनो अभरत सोमं सहस्रं सवाम् आयुतं च साकम् |अत्रा पुरन्धिर अजहाद अरातीर मदे सोमस्य मूरा अमूरः ||
ādāya śyeno abharat somaṁ sahasraṁ savām̐ ayutaṁ ca sākam | atrā puraṁdhir ajahād arātīr made somasya mūrā amūraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādāya: Taking or bringing
    • śyena: Eagle or a powerful being
    • abharat: Brought or carried
    • somaṁ: Soma, a sacred drink
    • sahasraṁ: Thousand
    • savam: Offering or sacrifice
    • ayutaṁ: Ten thousand
    • ca: And
    • sākam: Together or in unison
    • atrā: Here or in this context
    • puraṁdhir: The creator or the architect
    • ajahād: Has not abandoned
    • arātīr: Enemies or obstacles
    • made: In the intoxication or joy
    • somasya: Of Soma
    • mūrā: The essence or the core
    • amūraḥ: Without limits or boundaries
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of bringing forth the sacred Soma, which is often associated with divine inspiration and spiritual ecstasy.
    • The reference to "śyena" (eagle) symbolizes strength and the ability to rise above challenges, much like how an eagle soars high in the sky.
    • The phrase "sahasraṁ savām̐ ayutaṁ" indicates a vast offering, suggesting that the act of devotion and sacrifice should be abundant and generous.
    • The mention of "puraṁdhir" refers to the divine architect or creator, emphasizing the importance of creation and the role of divine forces in our lives.
    • By stating "ajahād arātīr," the mantra reassures that the divine presence does not abandon us in the face of obstacles, encouraging resilience and faith.
    • The concluding part highlights the limitless nature of the essence of Soma, suggesting that true joy and spiritual fulfillment are boundless.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for teamwork and collaboration, where each member brings their strengths (like the eagle) to achieve a common goal (the Soma).
    • During challenging times, such as a personal crisis or a global pandemic, the mantra serves as a reminder that we are not alone and that divine support is always present, encouraging us to persevere.
    • In spiritual practices, the act of offering (sacrifice) can be seen in rituals where individuals dedicate their time and resources to community service, reflecting the abundance mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, strength, and the boundless nature of spiritual fulfillment.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to rise above challenges, work together in unity, and remain connected to the divine source of inspiration.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a guiding principle for resilience, teamwork, and the pursuit of spiritual and personal growth.
