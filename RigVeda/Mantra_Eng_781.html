यस्तुभ्यं दाशाद्यो वा ते शिक्षात्तस्मै चिकीत्वान् रयिं दयस्व ||
yas tubhyaṁ dāśād yo vā te śikṣāt tasmai cikitvān rayiṁ dayasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • dāśād: from the giver
    • yo: who
    • vā: or
    • te: to you
    • śikṣāt: teaches
    • tasmai: to him
    • cikitvān: having thought
    • rayiṁ: wealth
    • dayasva: grant compassion
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine entity or teacher.
    • The speaker acknowledges the importance of the one who imparts knowledge and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the relationship between the giver of knowledge and the seeker, highlighting the need for compassion and generosity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a teacher who imparts knowledge to students embodies the essence of this mantra. The students are encouraged to seek wisdom and, in return, the teacher is asked to be compassionate and generous in sharing their knowledge.
    • In a mentorship relationship, a mentor who guides a mentee through challenges and provides insights reflects the spirit of this mantra. The mentee is encouraged to appreciate the mentor's efforts and to seek their guidance with humility.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mutual respect and compassion in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, mentorship, and any relationship where knowledge is shared.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can foster a culture of learning and generosity, ensuring that knowledge is not only acquired but also shared with kindness.
