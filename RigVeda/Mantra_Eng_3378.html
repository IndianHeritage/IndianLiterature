एतदस्यानः शये सुसम्पिष्टं विपाश्याऽ। ससार सिम परावतः॥
etad asyā anaḥ śaye susampiṣṭaṁ vipāśy ā | sasāra sīm parāvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etad: this
    • asyā: of this
    • anaḥ: breath or life force
    • śaye: in the resting or sleeping state
    • susampiṣṭaṁ: well-formed or well-structured
    • vipāśy: to see or to perceive
    • ā: towards
    • sasāra: has moved or has gone
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • parāvataḥ: of the highest or supreme
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of life and consciousness.
    • It suggests that the life force (anaḥ) rests in a well-structured state (susampiṣṭaṁ) and can be perceived (vipāśy).
    • The movement (sasāra) towards the boundaries (sīm) of the supreme (parāvataḥ) indicates a journey or evolution of consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In meditation, individuals often experience a state where they feel their life force resting and rejuvenating, similar to the "śaye" state mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal development, people strive to structure their lives (susampiṣṭaṁ) in a way that allows them to perceive their true potential (vipāśy) and move towards their goals (sasāra).
    • In nature, the cycle of life and death can be seen as a movement towards the boundaries of existence, reflecting the idea of parāvataḥ.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the life force within us and the journey of consciousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, meditation, and understanding the cycles of life.
    • By recognizing the structured nature of our existence and the movement towards higher consciousness, we can lead more fulfilling lives.
