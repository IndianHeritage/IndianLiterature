घर्मा समन्ता त्रिवृतं व्यापतुस् तयोः जुष्टिम् मातरिश्वा जगाम |दिवस् पयो दिधिषाणाऽवेक्षण विदुर देवाः सहसामानम् अर्कम् ||
gharmā samantā trivṛtaṁ vy āpatus tayor juṣṭim mātariśvā jagāma | divas payo didhiṣāṇā aveṣan vidur devāḥ sahasāmānam arkam ||
  • gharmā: This word refers to warmth or heat, often associated with the sun or fire. In real life, warmth can symbolize comfort, safety, and the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • samantā: This means 'from all sides' or 'surrounding'. It indicates a holistic approach, suggesting that warmth or energy is present everywhere around us.
  • trivṛtaṁ: This term can be interpreted as 'threefold' or 'triple'. It may refer to the three realms of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual, or the three aspects of life: creation, preservation, and destruction.
  • vy āpatus: This phrase means 'may it spread' or 'may it pervade'. It suggests a desire for warmth and energy to envelop and influence all aspects of life.
  • tayor: This means 'of them' or 'of those'. It refers to the energies or forces being discussed in the mantra.
  • juṣṭim: This word translates to 'delight' or 'enjoyment'. It signifies the joy and satisfaction derived from the warmth and energy that surrounds us.
  • mātariśvā: This term refers to the 'wind' or 'air', often personified as a deity. It represents the vital force of life and the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • jagāma: This means 'went' or 'arrived'. It indicates the movement or flow of energy and warmth through the environment.
  • divas: This translates to 'day' or 'heaven'. It symbolizes light, clarity, and the divine aspects of existence.
  • payo: This word means 'milk', often associated with nourishment and sustenance. It represents the nurturing qualities of nature.
  • didhiṣāṇā: This term can be interpreted as 'desiring' or 'seeking'. It indicates a longing for abundance and fulfillment.
  • aveṣan: This means 'to cover' or 'to envelop'. It suggests the idea of being surrounded by positive energies.
  • vidur: This translates to 'knowing' or 'wise'. It refers to the enlightened beings or deities who understand the nature of existence.
  • devāḥ: This means 'gods' or 'divine beings'. It signifies the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • sahasāmānam: This term can be interpreted as 'abundant' or 'plentiful'. It suggests a richness in the energies and blessings that surround us.
  • arkam: This word refers to 'light' or 'radiance', often associated with the sun. It symbolizes enlightenment and the source of life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of warmth, energy, and divine presence in our lives. It encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence and to seek joy and nourishment from the world around us. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of community, appreciating nature, and nurturing our relationships. By embracing the warmth and abundance that surrounds us, we can cultivate a fulfilling and harmonious life.
