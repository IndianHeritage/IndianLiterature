तद ū षु वाम एनā कृतं विश्वा यद वाम अनु ष्टवे |नानā जाताव अरेपसā सम अस्मे बन्धुम एयतुḥ ||
tad ū ṣu vām enā kṛtaṁ viśvā yad vām anu ṣṭave | nānā jātāv arepasā sam asme bandhum eyathuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • ū: indeed
    • ṣu: in you
    • vām: your
    • enā: by this
    • kṛtaṁ: done
    • viśvā: all
    • yad: which
    • vām: you
    • anu: after
    • ṣṭave: praise
    • nānā: various
    • jātāv: from different origins
    • arepasā: with great strength
    • sam: together
    • asme: we
    • bandhum: kinship
    • eyathuḥ: may come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about unity and the collective strength that arises when individuals come together.
    • The phrase "tad ū ṣu vām" emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine presence in each other.
    • It suggests that by acknowledging and praising each other, we can create a harmonious environment.
    • The mention of "nānā jātāv" indicates that people from various backgrounds and origins can unite.
    • It highlights the idea that diversity, when embraced, leads to a stronger community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members from different departments collaborate on a project, they bring unique perspectives that enhance creativity and problem-solving.
    • In community service, individuals from various cultural backgrounds come together to address social issues, demonstrating how collective efforts can lead to significant change.
    • In sports, diverse teams often perform better because they leverage the strengths of each member, showcasing the power of unity in diversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and collaboration among diverse individuals.
    • It serves as a reminder that by recognizing the strengths in one another and working together, we can achieve greater goals.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to foster kinship and support, leading to a more harmonious and productive society.
