अर्वाङ् नराः दैव्यानावसागतम् श्रिणुतं हवम् यदि मे जुजोषथः |युवोः हि सख्यम् उता वा यद आप्यम् मार्डीकम् इन्द्रावरुणा न यच्छतम् ||
arvāṅ narā daivyenāvasā gataṁ śṛṇutaṁ havaṁ yadi me jujoṣathaḥ | yuvor hi sakhyam uta vā yad āpyam mārḍīkam indrāvaruṇā ni yacchatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvāṅ: all, every
    • narā: men, people
    • daivyenā: divine, heavenly
    • avasā: having come down, descended
    • gataṁ: gone, departed
    • śṛṇutaṁ: listen, hear
    • havaṁ: invocation, call
    • yadi: if
    • me: my
    • jujoṣathaḥ: you are pleased, you accept
    • yuvor: of the two, of you
    • hi: indeed
    • sakhyam: friendship, companionship
    • uta: or
    • vā: or
    • yad: whatever, that which
    • āpyam: attainable, achievable
    • mārḍīkam: of the Maruts, storm deities
    • indrāvaruṇā: Indra and Varuna, deities of rain and water
    • ni: not
    • yacchatam: grant, give
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, specifically addressing the collective of people and invoking their attention.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine companionship and friendship, suggesting that when people come together with a common purpose, they can invoke divine blessings.
    • The mention of Indra and Varuna highlights the significance of natural elements, particularly water and rain, which are essential for life and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, people often invoke divine blessings for harmony and prosperity, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In environmental movements, the call for unity among people to protect natural resources can be seen as a modern application of invoking divine forces for a common cause.
    • Friendship and collaboration in workplaces can lead to successful projects, mirroring the idea of divine companionship leading to fruitful outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of collective invocation and the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • It teaches us that when we come together with a sense of purpose and friendship, we can achieve great things and attract positive energies.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from community service to environmental conservation, emphasizing the need for unity and divine guidance in our actions.
