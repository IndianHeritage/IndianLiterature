बृहस्पते प्रथमं वाचो अग्रं यत प्रैरत नामधेयं दधानाः |यद एषां श्रेष्ठं यद अरिप्रम आसीत प्रेणा तद एषां निहितं गुहाविः ||
bṛhaspate prathamaṁ vāco agraṁ yat prairata nāmadheyaṁ dadhānāḥ | yad eṣāṁ śreṣṭhaṁ yad aripram āsīt preṇā tad eṣāṁ nihitaṁ guhāviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhaspate: Referring to Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence.
    • prathamaṁ: First or foremost.
    • vāco: Speech or words.
    • agraṁ: The best or the foremost.
    • yat: That which.
    • prairata: Is inspired or directed.
    • nāmadheyaṁ: Name or identity.
    • dadhānāḥ: Holding or possessing.
    • yad: That which.
    • eṣāṁ: Of these or their.
    • śreṣṭhaṁ: The best or the most excellent.
    • yad: That which.
    • aripram: The enemy or adversary.
    • āsīt: Was or existed.
    • preṇā: Inspiration or encouragement.
    • tad: That.
    • eṣāṁ: Of these or their.
    • nihitaṁ: Hidden or concealed.
    • guhāviḥ: In the cave or secret place.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Brihaspati, the god of wisdom, to guide and inspire speech that is meaningful and impactful.
    • It emphasizes the importance of possessing the best words and names, which can lead to effective communication and understanding.
    • The reference to hidden wisdom suggests that true knowledge and insight may often be found in unexpected places, requiring introspection and contemplation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, effective communication can lead to successful negotiations. For instance, a skilled negotiator uses the best words to convey their message, much like the invocation of Brihaspati for eloquence.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and empathy can be seen as the 'best words' that foster connection and resolve conflicts.
    • In education, teachers who inspire students with their words can unlock hidden potential, akin to the 'hidden wisdom' mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, eloquence, and the power of words in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding, both in ourselves and in the world around us.
    • By invoking the qualities of Brihaspati, we can strive to communicate effectively, inspire others, and uncover the hidden truths that guide our actions.
