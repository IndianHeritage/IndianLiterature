तā हि क्षत्रम् अविहृतं सम्यग् असुर्यम् आशाते |अध व्रतेव मानवं स्वर्ण धायि दर्शतम् ||
tā hi kṣatram avihrutaṁ samyag asurya1m āśāte | adha vrateva mānuṣaṁ sva1r ṇa dhāyi darśatam ||
  • tā hi kṣatram avihrutaṁ samyag asurya1m āśāte
    • tā hi - "Indeed, they" refers to a group or collective.
    • kṣatram - "Kṣatra" signifies power, authority, or rulership, often associated with warriors or kings.
    • avihrutaṁ - "Unhindered" or "unobstructed," indicating a state of freedom or purity.
    • samyag - "Properly" or "correctly," suggesting that the power is exercised in a rightful manner.
    • asurya1m - "Asurya" can refer to the divine or celestial, often associated with light and truth.
    • āśāte - "Desires" or "longs for," indicating a yearning for something higher or noble.
  • adha vrateva mānuṣaṁ sva1r ṇa dhāyi darśatam
    • adha - "Below" or "under," suggesting a contrast or a lower state.
    • vrateva - "Like a vow," indicating a commitment or promise.
    • mānuṣaṁ - "Human," referring to humanity or human qualities.
    • sva1r - "Own" or "self," indicating personal responsibility or ownership.
    • ṇa dhāyi - "Does not hold" or "does not support," suggesting a lack of foundation or stability.
    • darśatam - "Visible" or "manifest," indicating something that can be seen or understood.

The mantra speaks to the nature of power and authority, emphasizing that true power is unobstructed and exercised correctly, aligned with divine principles. It contrasts this with a lower state of being, where human qualities may lack commitment and stability.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Leaders should embody the qualities of kṣatra, exercising their authority in a manner that is just and beneficial for all. For example, a community leader who listens to the needs of their constituents and acts with integrity exemplifies this principle.
  • Personal Development: Individuals striving for personal growth should seek to align their desires with higher values, ensuring that their actions are not obstructed by negative influences. For instance, someone pursuing a career in social work may focus on helping others rather than personal gain.
  • Community Engagement: The mantra encourages collective responsibility, where individuals work together towards a common goal, ensuring that their actions are visible and impactful. An example would be a group of volunteers coming together to address local issues, demonstrating commitment and unity.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of exercising power and authority in a manner that is just, unobstructed, and aligned with higher values. It serves as a reminder for individuals and leaders alike to act with integrity and commitment, fostering a sense of responsibility towards oneself and the community.
