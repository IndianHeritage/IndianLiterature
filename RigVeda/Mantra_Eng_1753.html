अपाङ् प्राङ् एति स्वधया गृभीतो 'मर्त्यो मर्त्येनासयोनिः |ताश्शश्वन्ता विषूचीनां वियन्ता न्यन्यं चिकीुर न न चिकीुर अन्यम् ||
apāṅ prāṅ eti svadhayā gṛbhīto 'martyo martyenā sayoniḥ | tā śaśvantā viṣūcīnā viyantā ny a1nyaṁ cikyur na ni cikyur anyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāṅ: not (or without)
    • prāṅ: life or breath
    • eti: goes or moves
    • svadhayā: by one's own will or desire
    • gṛbhīto: having been taken or grasped
    • 'martyo: mortal or human
    • martyenā: by the mortal
    • sayoniḥ: womb or source of birth
    • tā: those
    • śaśvantā: shining or radiant
    • viṣūcīnā: those who are wise or knowledgeable
    • viyantā: those who are liberated or free
    • ny: not
    • anyaṁ: another
    • cikyur: they do
    • na: not
    • ni: not
    • cikyur: they do
    • anyam: another
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of life and existence, emphasizing the importance of individual will and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The phrase "apāṅ prāṅ eti svadhayā" suggests that life moves forward through one's own will, indicating the power of personal agency.
    • The mention of "martyo martyenā sayoniḥ" reflects the human condition, where mortals are born from the womb, highlighting the cycle of life and death.
    • The concluding lines "tā śaśvantā viṣūcīnā viyantā" refer to those who are enlightened and liberated, suggesting that wisdom leads to freedom from the cycle of rebirth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that require them to exercise their willpower to overcome obstacles, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In a workplace setting, a team member who takes initiative and drives projects forward embodies the essence of "svadhayā," showcasing the impact of personal agency.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be seen in community service, where individuals come together to support one another, reflecting the idea that "ny a1nyaṁ cikyur" – we do not act alone.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of personal will and the interconnectedness of life, reminding us that our actions have consequences not just for ourselves but for others as well.
    • It encourages individuals to take charge of their lives, seek wisdom, and strive for liberation from the cycles of suffering.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more compassionate society.
