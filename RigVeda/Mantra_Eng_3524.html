कां वाम भूद उपमातिः कया न आश्विनाऽ गमथो हूयमानाः |को वाम महश्चित्त्यजसो अभीक उरुष्यतम माध्वी दसरा न ऊती ||
kā vām bhūd upamātiḥ kayā na āśvinā gamatho hūyamānā | ko vām mahaś cit tyajaso abhīka uruṣyatam mādhvī dasrā na ūtī ||
  • kā vām bhūd upamātiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "What is the nature of your existence?" It questions the essence of being and invites introspection about one's identity and purpose.
  • kayā na āśvinā gamatho hūyamānā: This part translates to "By what means do you, O Ashvins, come to us when invoked?" The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine, symbolizing the arrival of help and healing in times of need.
  • ko vām mahaś cit tyajaso abhīka: This translates to "Who among you, O great ones, is willing to abandon us?" It reflects a plea for support and the fear of abandonment in difficult times.
  • uruṣyatam mādhvī dasrā na ūtī: This can be interpreted as "May the most potent and sweet blessings come to us." It expresses a desire for abundance and positivity in life.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding one's existence and seeking divine assistance in overcoming challenges. It highlights the interconnectedness of human experience and the divine.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied during moments of self-doubt or when facing obstacles. For instance, when someone is struggling with a personal crisis, reflecting on their purpose and seeking support from friends, family, or spiritual practices can be a way to invoke the blessings of the Ashvins.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder to seek clarity about our existence and to reach out for help when needed. It encourages individuals to embrace their vulnerabilities and to trust in the support that can come from both the divine and the community around them.
