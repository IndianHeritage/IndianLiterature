मिमीहि श्लोकम् आस्ये पार्जन्य इव ततनः |गाय गायत्रम उक्त्यम् ||
mimīhi ślokam āsye parjanya iva tatanaḥ | gāya gāyatram ukthyam ||
  • mimīhi: This word can be interpreted as "to listen" or "to pay attention." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and aware of one's surroundings and the teachings of the universe.
  • ślokam: Refers to a "verse" or "hymn." In the context of the Rigveda, it signifies the sacred verses that convey deep spiritual truths and wisdom.
  • āsye: This term means "in the mouth" or "in speech." It highlights the power of words and the significance of vocalizing thoughts and prayers.
  • parjanya: This word denotes "rain" or "the rain god." It symbolizes nourishment, abundance, and the life-giving force of nature.
  • iva: Translated as "like" or "as," it draws a comparison, suggesting a relationship between two entities.
  • tatanaḥ: This term can be understood as "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." It refers to a divine force or energy that manifests in the world.
  • gāya: Meaning "to sing" or "to chant," it emphasizes the importance of expression and the act of sharing one's thoughts and feelings through sound.
  • gāyatram: This refers to a "sacred chant" or "melody." It signifies the spiritual significance of music and sound in connecting with the divine.
  • ukthyam: This word means "to be uttered" or "to be spoken." It underscores the importance of vocalizing prayers and intentions.

  • Meaning: The mantra encourages individuals to listen attentively to the sacred verses, to express themselves through song, and to recognize the divine forces that nourish and sustain life, much like rain nourishes the earth.
  • Real-life Example: In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life. For instance, during a community gathering or a spiritual ceremony, individuals can come together to chant and sing, fostering a sense of unity and connection. Just as rain brings life to crops, the collective energy of shared expressions can nourish the spirit of the community.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, expression, and connection with the divine. It teaches us the value of listening, the power of words, and the importance of community. In real-life scenarios, it can inspire individuals to engage in collective rituals, celebrate nature's abundance, and foster a deeper connection with themselves and others. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can cultivate a more harmonious and spiritually enriched life.
