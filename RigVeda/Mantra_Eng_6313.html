स प्रथमं व्योमनि देवानां सदने वृद्धः |सुपारः सुश्रवस्तमः सम अप्सुजित ||
sa prathame vyomani devānāṁ sadane vṛdhaḥ | supāraḥ suśravastamaḥ sam apsujit ||
  • sa prathame vyomani: This phrase translates to "In the beginning, in the sky." It signifies the primordial state of existence, where everything originates. In real life, this can be likened to the concept of a beginning or a starting point in any endeavor, such as the inception of a project or the birth of a new idea.
  • devānāṁ sadane vṛdhaḥ: This means "the abode of the gods is vast." It suggests that the divine or spiritual realm is expansive and filled with possibilities. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of seeking guidance or inspiration from higher powers or mentors in our lives, emphasizing the importance of community and support systems.
  • supāraḥ: This word translates to "well-formed" or "excellent." It indicates the quality of something being well-prepared or well-structured. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of planning and organization, whether in personal goals, professional projects, or even in relationships.
  • suśravastamaḥ: This means "of the best fame" or "renowned." It highlights the value of reputation and the impact of being well-regarded in society. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who strive for excellence in their fields, gaining respect and recognition for their contributions.
  • sam apsujit: This phrase translates to "together with the waters." It symbolizes unity and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe. In practical terms, this can be related to teamwork and collaboration, where collective efforts lead to greater achievements, much like how rivers converge to form a larger body of water.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of beginnings, the vastness of divine support, the importance of quality and reputation, and the power of unity. Its significance lies in reminding us to seek inspiration from higher realms, to strive for excellence in our endeavors, and to work collaboratively with others. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, professional teamwork, and community building, encouraging individuals to recognize their potential and the importance of collective efforts in achieving greatness.
