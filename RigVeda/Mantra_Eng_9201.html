इयं वाम अह्वे श्रिणुतम मे अश्विनाऽ पुत्रायेव पितराः मह्यम् शिक्षतम् |अनापीरजñा असजात्यामतिः पुरा तस्याः अभिशस्तेर अव स्पृतम् ||
iyaṁ vām ahve śṛṇutam me aśvinā putrāyeva pitarā mahyaṁ śikṣatam | anāpir ajñā asajātyāmatiḥ purā tasyā abhiśaster ava spṛtam ||
  • iyaṁ vām ahve - This phrase translates to "I call upon you." It signifies an invocation or a plea directed towards the divine, in this case, the Aśvins, who are considered the twin gods of medicine and health in Vedic literature.
  • śṛṇutam me - This means "listen to me." It emphasizes the importance of being heard, suggesting a deep desire for connection and understanding between the devotee and the divine.
  • aśvinā - Referring to the Aśvins, these deities are often associated with healing, protection, and the welfare of humanity. They symbolize the dual aspects of life, such as light and darkness, health and illness.
  • putrāyeva pitarā - This translates to "like sons to a father." It indicates a relationship of care and guidance, where the divine is seen as a nurturing figure, akin to a parent who provides wisdom and support.
  • mahyaṁ śikṣatam - Meaning "teach me," this phrase expresses a yearning for knowledge and enlightenment. It highlights the importance of learning and growth in one's spiritual journey.
  • anāpir ajñā - This can be interpreted as "without ignorance." It suggests a desire to be free from confusion and misunderstanding, seeking clarity in thoughts and actions.
  • asajātyāmatiḥ purā - This phrase means "the mind that is not bound by birth." It signifies the transcendence of social and material limitations, encouraging individuals to rise above their circumstances and seek a higher understanding.
  • tasyā abhiśaster ava spṛtam - This translates to "may the blessings of that knowledge touch me." It expresses a wish for divine grace and the transformative power of wisdom to manifest in one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual yearning for guidance, knowledge, and healing from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of listening, learning, and transcending ignorance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of seeking clarity in difficult situations, asking for guidance in personal or professional challenges, or during times of illness when one seeks healing. By invoking the Aśvins, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine, fostering a sense of support and nurturing in their lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and understanding in our journey, encouraging us to rise above our limitations and embrace the blessings of knowledge.
