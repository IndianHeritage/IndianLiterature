वास्तोष पते प्रतरणो न एधि गयस्पहानो गोभिर अश्वेभिर इन्दो |अजरासस ते सख्ये स्याम पितेव पुत्रान प्रति नो जुषस्व ||
vāstoṣ pate prataraṇo na edhi gayasphāno gobhir aśvebhir indo | ajarāsas te sakhye syāma piteva putrān prati no juṣasva ||
  • vāstoṣ pate: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord of the dwelling" or "O Lord of the house." It signifies a divine presence that governs and protects the home and its inhabitants.
  • prataraṇo: This word means "one who helps" or "one who supports." It emphasizes the role of the divine in providing assistance and guidance to those who seek it.
  • na edhi: Translated as "do not harm" or "do not bring misfortune," this phrase expresses a plea for protection from negative influences or dangers.
  • gayasphāno: This term refers to "the one who is full of joy" or "the one who brings happiness." It highlights the importance of joy and positivity in life.
  • gobhir aśvebhir indo: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the cows and horses, O Indra." It invokes the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, who is associated with strength, prosperity, and abundance.
  • ajarāsas: Meaning "immortal" or "everlasting," this word signifies the eternal nature of the divine and its blessings.
  • te sakhye syāma: This translates to "may we be your friends" or "may we be united with you." It expresses a desire for a close relationship with the divine.
  • piteva putrān: This phrase means "like a father to his sons," indicating a nurturing and protective relationship between the divine and its devotees.
  • prati no juṣasva: Translated as "please be pleased with us," this is a call for the divine to accept and bless the offerings and prayers of the devotees.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine protection, joy, and a nurturing relationship between the divine and humanity. It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, support, and blessings in their homes and lives. For instance, families may recite this mantra during housewarming ceremonies or family gatherings to invoke peace and prosperity. The significance lies in fostering a sense of community, gratitude, and connection with the divine, encouraging individuals to live harmoniously and seek joy in their daily lives.
