पुष्टिर्न रण्वा क्षitir न पृथ्वी गिरिर्न भुज्म क्षोदो न शम्भु ||
puṣṭir na raṇvā kṣitir na pṛthvī girir na bhujma kṣodo na śambhu ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • puṣṭir: This word translates to "nourishment" or "prosperity." It signifies the importance of sustenance and growth in life.
    • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • raṇvā: This refers to "battle" or "conflict." It suggests the struggles and challenges one faces.
    • kṣitir: This means "earth" or "land," representing the physical world and our connection to it.
    • na pṛthvī: Similar to kṣitir, pṛthvī also means "earth," emphasizing the grounding aspect of existence.
    • girir: This translates to "mountain," symbolizing strength, stability, and the challenges that rise in life.
    • na bhujma: This means "not to be consumed" or "not to be devoured," indicating resilience against adversities.
    • kṣodo: This refers to "suffering" or "pain," highlighting the inevitable hardships in life.
    • na śambhu: This translates to "not to be destroyed" or "not to be harmed," suggesting the idea of enduring strength and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal growth, the mantra can be seen as a reminder that despite the challenges (raṇvā) we face in our careers (kṣitir), we should seek nourishment (puṣṭir) through learning and resilience.
    • In relationships, the mantra emphasizes that conflicts (raṇvā) should not consume our love (na bhujma) and that we should strive for stability (girir) and support each other through hardships (kṣodo).
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can be interpreted as a call to protect our earth (kṣitir, pṛthvī) from destruction (na śambhu) and to ensure that it remains a source of nourishment (puṣṭir) for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a profound reminder of the balance between challenges and sustenance in life. It encourages individuals to cultivate resilience and strength while navigating through conflicts and hardships.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that nourishment and prosperity can coexist with struggles, and that one can emerge stronger from adversities.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, relationships, and environmental stewardship, guiding individuals to maintain their integrity and strength in the face of challenges.
