परā ह यत् स्थिरं हत नरो वर्तयथā गुरु |वियाथन वनिनः पृथिव्यā व्याशाः पर्वतानाम् ||
parā ha yat sthiraṁ hatha naro vartayathā guru | vi yāthana vaninaḥ pṛthivyā vy āśāḥ parvatānām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: beyond, supreme
    • ha: indeed
    • yat: that which
    • sthiraṁ: stable, firm
    • hatha: having been struck down
    • naro: man, human
    • vartayathā: you should conduct, you should lead
    • guru: teacher, guide
    • vi: apart, separately
    • yāthana: effort, endeavor
    • vaninaḥ: of the forest, of nature
    • pṛthivyā: of the earth
    • vy: indeed
    • āśāḥ: hopes, aspirations
    • parvatānām: of the mountains
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of stability and guidance in the face of challenges.
    • It emphasizes the role of a teacher or guide (guru) in helping individuals navigate through life's difficulties.
    • The mention of "man" (naro) suggests that this guidance is essential for human beings, especially when they face adversities.
    • The reference to "efforts" (yāthana) indicates that while guidance is important, personal effort is also crucial in achieving stability and success.
    • The connection to nature (vaninaḥ) and the earth (pṛthivyā) signifies that our aspirations and hopes are grounded in the natural world, and we must respect and understand it.
    • The mountains (parvatānām) symbolize challenges or obstacles that one must overcome, and the mantra suggests that with the right guidance and effort, these can be navigated successfully.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, a teacher (guru) plays a vital role in guiding students (naro) through their academic challenges, helping them to achieve stability (sthiraṁ) in their knowledge and skills.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek mentors who can provide guidance and support as they strive to overcome life's obstacles (parvatānām) and achieve their goals (āśāḥ).
    • In environmental conservation, understanding the relationship between humanity (naro) and nature (vaninaḥ) is crucial for sustainable living, where aspirations for a better future must align with the health of the earth (pṛthivyā).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of guidance and personal effort in achieving stability and overcoming challenges in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may face difficulties (parvatānām), with the right support (guru) and our own endeavors (yāthana), we can navigate through them successfully.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious relationship with nature and the earth, emphasizing that our aspirations should be grounded in reality and respect for the environment.
