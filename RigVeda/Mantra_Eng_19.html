अश्विनाऽ यज्वरीर इषो द्रवत्पाणि शुभस पती |पुरुभुजाऽ चनस्यतम ||
aśvinā yajvarīr iṣo dravatpāṇī śubhas patī | purubhujā canasyatam ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā yajvarīr iṣo dravatpāṇī śubhas patī | purubhujā canasyatam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, who are the bestowers of wealth and prosperity, with swift hands and auspicious powers, may you come to us with your bountiful blessings."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are the twin deities in Hindu mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Yajvarīr: This term refers to those who perform sacrifices or rituals. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
    • Dravatpāṇī: This means "swift-handed" or "quick to act." It signifies the promptness of the Ashvins in responding to the prayers and needs of devotees.
    • Śubhas Patī: Translated as "lord of auspiciousness," this phrase highlights the benevolent and positive nature of the Ashvins, who bring good fortune and blessings.
    • Purubhujā: This term means "one who enjoys many things" or "the one who has many enjoyments." It reflects abundance and the richness of life.
    • Canasyatam: This can be interpreted as "may you come to us" or "may you be present." It is a call for the divine presence and blessings in one’s life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the Ashvins can be seen as symbols of health and well-being. For instance, when people seek medical help or wellness treatments, they are invoking the blessings of the Ashvins for recovery and health.
    • The concept of performing rituals or sacrifices (Yajvarīr) can be related to community service or charitable acts today. Just as the Ashvins are called upon for blessings, individuals can engage in acts of kindness to bring positivity into their lives and the lives of others.
    • The idea of "swift hands" (Dravatpāṇī) can be applied to the fast-paced nature of modern life, where quick decision-making and responsiveness are valued in both personal and professional settings.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity, health, and well-being. It serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers while also engaging in positive actions in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking the Ashvins can inspire individuals to pursue health, engage in community service, and remain open to the blessings of abundance and prosperity.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages a harmonious balance between seeking divine assistance and taking proactive steps in our lives to create a fulfilling and prosperous existence.
