इन्द्र तुभ्यं इद अद्रिवोऽनुत्तं वज्रिन वीर्यम् |यद ध त्यम मायिनं मृगं तं उ त्वं माययावधीर आर्चन् अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
indra tubhyam id adrivo 'nuttaṁ vajrin vīryam | yad dha tyam māyinam mṛgaṁ tam u tvam māyayāvadhīr arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Hindu mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Tubhyam: Means "to you," indicating a direct address to Indra, showing reverence and devotion.
  • Id: Translates to "this," pointing to the offerings or praises being presented.
  • Adrivo: Means "mountains," which can symbolize stability, strength, and the challenges one faces in life.
  • Anuttaṁ: Means "unmatched" or "supreme," highlighting the unparalleled nature of Indra's power.
  • Vajrin: Refers to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," a metaphor for strength and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Vīryam: Means "valor" or "strength," emphasizing the importance of courage in facing life's challenges.
  • Yad: Translates to "that," referring to the qualities or actions being discussed.
  • Hā: Means "that which," indicating a connection to the previous qualities mentioned.
  • Māyinam: Refers to "the one who creates illusions," suggesting the complexities and challenges of life.
  • Mṛgaṁ: Means "the deer," often symbolizing agility, grace, and the pursuit of goals.
  • Tam: Means "that," referring to the qualities or entities being addressed.
  • U: Means "you," again addressing Indra directly.
  • Tvam: Means "you," reinforcing the personal connection to the divine.
  • Māyayāvadhīr: Refers to "the one who is beyond illusion," indicating a higher state of consciousness or understanding.
  • Arcann: Means "worship," emphasizing the act of devotion and reverence.
  • Anu: Means "following" or "in accordance with," suggesting alignment with divine will.
  • Svarājyam: Refers to "the realm of sound" or "the kingdom of the divine," symbolizing a higher state of existence or enlightenment.

The mantra essentially calls upon Indra, the powerful deity, to bestow strength and valor upon the devotee. It acknowledges the challenges of life, represented by the mountains and the illusions we face, while also recognizing the grace and agility needed to navigate these challenges. The worship of Indra is a plea for divine support in overcoming obstacles and achieving a higher state of consciousness.


Conclusion: This mantra is significant as it highlights the importance of strength, courage, and divine support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied when facing challenges, whether personal or professional. For instance, when someone is preparing for a difficult exam or a major life decision, invoking the qualities of Indra can serve as a reminder to harness inner strength and seek guidance. It encourages individuals to remain steadfast and courageous, ultimately leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
