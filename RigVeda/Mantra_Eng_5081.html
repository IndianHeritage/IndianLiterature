इन्द्रावरुणा सुतपाव इमं सुतं सोमं पिबतं मद्यं धृतव्रता |युवो रथो अध्वरं देववीतये प्रति स्वसारम उप याती पीतये ||
indrāvaruṇā sutapāv imaṁ sutaṁ somam pibatam madyaṁ dhṛtavratā | yuvo ratho adhvaraṁ devavītaye prati svasaram upa yāti pītaye ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This refers to the deities Indra and Varuna, who are often associated with rain, thunder, and the cosmic order. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power, while Varuna embodies the moral and cosmic order.
  • Sutapāv: This term can be interpreted as "well-prepared" or "well-formed," indicating the importance of preparation and intention in rituals and offerings.
  • Imam sutam somam: "This soma, the drink of immortality," refers to the sacred beverage used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine inspiration and spiritual awakening.
  • Pibatam madyaṁ: "Let them drink the intoxicating beverage," suggests the act of consuming soma, which is believed to bestow wisdom and strength upon the drinker.
  • Dhṛtavratā: This term means "those who are steadfast in their vows," highlighting the importance of commitment and discipline in spiritual practices.
  • Yuvo ratho adhvaraṁ: "The young ones, the chariot of the sacrifice," signifies the energy and enthusiasm of youth in performing rituals and sacrifices.
  • Devavītaye: "For the sake of the gods," emphasizes the purpose of the rituals, which is to please and honor the divine.
  • Prati svasaram upa yāti pītaye: "Approaching the drink for the sake of enjoyment," indicates the communal aspect of rituals, where participants come together to share in the experience and blessings of the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of preparation, commitment, and community in spiritual practices. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the significance of intention and discipline in our endeavors, whether they be personal, professional, or spiritual. The act of coming together to celebrate and honor the divine can foster a sense of unity and purpose, encouraging individuals to strive for higher ideals and collective well-being. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, celebrations, or any situation where individuals seek to connect with a higher purpose and each other.
