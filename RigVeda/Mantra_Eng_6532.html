स त्वं न ऊर्जाम् पते रयिं रास्व सुवीर्यम् |प्राव नस् तोके तनये समत्स्वा ||
sa tvaṁ na ūrjām pate rayiṁ rāsva suvīryam | prāva nas toke tanaye samatsv ā ||
  • sa tvaṁ - "You" or "Thou" refers to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • na ūrjām - "not the energy" or "not the strength" indicates a negation of something that is typically expected.
  • pate - "O Lord" or "O Master" is a term of reverence, addressing the divine.
  • rayiṁ - "wealth" or "prosperity" signifies material and spiritual abundance.
  • rāsva - "short" or "brief" suggests something that is limited or not extensive.
  • suvīryam - "good strength" or "excellent power" refers to the quality of strength that is beneficial.
  • prāva - "may we attain" or "may we reach" indicates a desire for achievement or success.
  • nas - "us" or "our" signifies a collective or communal aspect.
  • toke - "offspring" or "children" refers to the next generation or progeny.
  • tanaye - "in the offspring" or "in the children" emphasizes the importance of future generations.
  • samatsv - "in equality" or "in balance" suggests harmony and fairness.
  • ā - "to" or "towards" indicates direction or intention.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine power, asking for the right kind of strength and prosperity that is beneficial and balanced, particularly for the future generations. It emphasizes the importance of not just seeking material wealth but also the quality of strength that supports harmony and equality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Family and Parenting: Parents can reflect on the importance of nurturing their children not just with material wealth but also with values that promote strength, equality, and harmony.
  • Community Development: Leaders and community members can use this mantra as a guiding principle to ensure that development efforts are equitable and beneficial for all, especially for future generations.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can meditate on this mantra to focus on cultivating inner strength that is not just powerful but also constructive and supportive of others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to strength and prosperity. It teaches us that true wealth is not merely material but encompasses the well-being of future generations and the importance of balance and equality in all aspects of life. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
