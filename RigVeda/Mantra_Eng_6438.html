ते घेद अग्नि स्वाध्यो ये त्वा विप्र निदधिरे नृचक्षसम् |विप्रासो देव सुक्रतुम् ||
te ghed agne svādhyo3 ye tvā vipra nidadhire nṛcakṣasam | viprāso deva sukratum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: those
    • ghed: who have invoked
    • agne: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • svādhyo: who are worthy
    • ye: who
    • tvā: you
    • vipra: O wise one
    • nidadhire: have established
    • nṛcakṣasam: among men
    • viprāso: O wise ones
    • deva: O divine
    • sukratum: with good intentions
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra addresses Agni, the deity of fire, acknowledging those who have invoked him with worthy intentions. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and good intentions in establishing a connection with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often light a fire as a symbol of unity and purity. This act can be seen as invoking Agni with good intentions.
    • In personal development, individuals who set goals with a clear and positive mindset are akin to the wise ones mentioned in the mantra. Their intentions guide their actions towards success.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of intention and wisdom in our actions. It serves as a reminder that our efforts should be directed towards positive outcomes.
    • In daily life, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or community service, approaching situations with a wise and good-hearted mindset can lead to better results and harmony.
