यसम् आरासत क्षयम् जीवातुं च प्रचेतसः |मनोर विश्वस्य घेद इमा आदित्याः राया ईशते 'नेहसो व ऊतयः सुतयः व ऊतयः ||
yasmā arāsata kṣayaṁ jīvātuṁ ca pracetasaḥ | manor viśvasya ghed ima ādityā rāya īśate 'nehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • yasmā - "because of which" or "from which": This word indicates a cause or reason, suggesting that the following ideas are derived from a specific source.
  • arāsata - "of the non-existent" or "of the void": This term refers to the absence of something, highlighting the importance of existence and creation.
  • kṣayaṁ - "destruction" or "decay": This word signifies the process of deterioration or the end of something, emphasizing the transient nature of life.
  • jīvātuṁ - "to live" or "to exist": This term represents the essence of life and the desire for survival and continuity.
  • ca - "and": A conjunction that connects ideas, indicating a relationship between the concepts presented.
  • pracetasaḥ - "of the wise" or "of the intelligent": This word refers to those who possess wisdom and understanding, suggesting that knowledge is crucial for navigating life.
  • manor - "of the mind": This term emphasizes the role of thought and consciousness in shaping our experiences and reality.
  • viśvasya - "of the universe" or "of all": This word indicates inclusivity, referring to the entirety of existence.
  • ghed - "to be established" or "to be created": This term suggests the act of bringing something into being, highlighting the creative aspect of existence.
  • ima - "these" or "this": A demonstrative pronoun that points to specific entities or concepts.
  • ādityā - "the sun" or "the solar deity": This term represents the source of light and life, symbolizing energy and vitality.
  • rāya - "wealth" or "prosperity": This word signifies abundance and the positive aspects of life.
  • īśate - "rules" or "governs": This term indicates authority and control, suggesting that certain forces govern existence.
  • 'nehaso - "of the non-illuminated" or "of the dark": This word refers to ignorance or the absence of knowledge.
  • va - "or": A conjunction that presents alternatives or choices.
  • ūtayaḥ - "the rising" or "the ascendant": This term signifies growth, development, and the emergence of new possibilities.
  • suūtayo - "the well-established" or "the prosperous": This word refers to stability and success in life.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between existence, wisdom, and the forces that govern life. It emphasizes the importance of understanding the transient nature of life and the necessity of knowledge in navigating the complexities of existence. The reference to the sun symbolizes the source of life and energy, while the concepts of wealth and prosperity highlight the positive outcomes of wisdom and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom to navigate life's challenges effectively.
  • In leadership, it emphasizes the importance of understanding the dynamics of existence and the impact of decisions on the collective.
  • In environmental awareness, it reminds us of the transient nature of resources and the need for sustainable practices to ensure prosperity for future generations.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of existence, the importance of wisdom, and the role of conscious choices in shaping our reality. By embracing knowledge and understanding, we can navigate the complexities of life and contribute to a prosperous and harmonious existence.
