निर यत् पूतेव स्वधितिः शुचिर गात् स्वयाऽ कृपा तंवा3 रोचमानः |आ यो मात्रोः उशेन्यो जनिष्ट देवयज्ञाय सुक्रतुः पावकः ||
nir yat pūteva svadhitiḥ śucir gāt svayā kṛpā tanvā3 rocamānaḥ | ā yo mātror uśenyo janiṣṭa devayajyāya sukratuḥ pāvakaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir yat: wherever
    • pūteva: pure
    • svadhitiḥ: self-knowledge or self-awareness
    • śucir: clean or pure
    • gāt: to go or to move
    • svayā: by oneself
    • kṛpā: compassion or grace
    • tanvā: body or form
    • rocamānaḥ: shining or radiant
    • ā yo: who
    • mātror: of the mother
    • uśenyo: of the powerful
    • janiṣṭa: has been born
    • devayajyāya: for the worship of the divine
    • sukratuḥ: of good intention or good will
    • pāvakaḥ: the purifying or sacred fire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the journey towards purity and self-awareness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of compassion and grace in our actions.
    • The reference to the mother signifies the nurturing aspect of life and the source of strength.
    • The idea of being radiant suggests that when one is pure and compassionate, they shine brightly in the world.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of good intentions in our endeavors, especially in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of the sacred fire symbolizes transformation and purification, essential for spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, practicing self-awareness can lead to better decision-making and healthier relationships.
    • Acts of kindness and compassion, such as volunteering or helping those in need, reflect the essence of this mantra.
    • In a family setting, nurturing and supporting one another, especially by mothers, creates a strong foundation for growth.
    • In professional environments, maintaining good intentions and a positive attitude can foster teamwork and collaboration.
    • Engaging in spiritual practices, such as meditation or rituals, can serve as a means of personal transformation and purification.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, compassion, and self-awareness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth and to act with good intentions in all aspects of life.
    • By embodying these principles, one can contribute positively to their community and the world at large.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine, urging us to live in harmony with ourselves and others.
