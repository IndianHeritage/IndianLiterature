विद्यामेषि रजस् पृथ्वहामिमानो अक्तुभिः |पश्यञ्जन्मानि सूर्य ||
vi dyām eṣi rajas pṛthv ahā mimāno aktubhiḥ | paśyañ janmāni sūrya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: apart, away
    • dyām: the sky, heavens
    • eṣi: you go, you move
    • rajas: the atmosphere, the space
    • pṛthv: the earth, the ground
    • ahā: this day, this moment
    • mimāno: having measured, having understood
    • aktubhiḥ: with the energies, with the forces
    • paśyañ: seeing, observing
    • janmāni: births, lives
    • sūrya: the sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the heavens, the earth, and the sun.
    • It suggests a journey or movement through the atmosphere and the earth, emphasizing the importance of understanding and measuring the forces at play in our lives.
    • The reference to "seeing births" indicates a recognition of the cycles of life and the role of the sun as a source of energy and life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we observe nature and its cycles, such as the changing seasons, which are influenced by the sun.
    • For instance, farmers rely on the sun's position and the weather to determine the best times for planting and harvesting crops, illustrating the connection between the earth and the sun.
    • Additionally, in a metaphorical sense, this mantra can apply to personal growth and transformation, where individuals measure their experiences and learn from them, akin to the sun illuminating their path.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements of life—heaven, earth, and the sun.
    • It encourages us to observe and understand the forces that shape our existence, promoting a sense of awareness and mindfulness.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate the cycles of life, harness the energy of the sun for growth, and measure their experiences for personal development.
