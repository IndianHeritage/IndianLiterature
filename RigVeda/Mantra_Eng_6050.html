उत सुत्ये पयोवृद्धा माकी रणस्य नप्त्या |जनित्वनाय मामहे ||
uta su tye payovṛdhā mākī raṇasya naptyā | janitvanāya māmahe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
    • su: Meaning "good" or "well," it signifies positivity and auspiciousness.
    • tye: This can be interpreted as "to you" or "for you," suggesting a direction of devotion or offering.
    • payovṛdhā: This term refers to "the increase of nourishment" or "the growth of sustenance," often associated with prosperity and abundance.
    • mākī: This word can mean "to be born" or "to arise," indicating the emergence of something new.
    • raṇasya: Translated as "of battle" or "of conflict," it relates to challenges or struggles in life.
    • naptyā: This term can mean "through offspring" or "by progeny," emphasizing the importance of lineage and continuity.
    • janitvanāya: This word signifies "for the purpose of birth" or "for the act of creation," highlighting the significance of creation and existence.
    • māmahe: Meaning "we invoke" or "we call upon," it expresses a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for nourishment and prosperity, particularly in the context of overcoming challenges and ensuring the continuity of life through progeny.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in times of struggle and the role of nourishment—both physical and spiritual—in achieving success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking growth and prosperity in a competitive market. Entrepreneurs often face challenges (raṇasya) and must invoke (māmahe) support from mentors or divine forces to navigate these battles successfully.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of family challenges, invoking blessings for the well-being and growth of their children (naptyā) and ensuring a prosperous future.
    • In agricultural practices, farmers may use this mantra to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest (payovṛdhā), especially during difficult seasons when the threat of failure looms large.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nourishment, struggle, and the continuity of life through progeny.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and to appreciate the blessings of sustenance and growth.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or spiritual—this mantra encourages individuals to remain hopeful and proactive in their pursuits, invoking support for a prosperous and fulfilling existence.
