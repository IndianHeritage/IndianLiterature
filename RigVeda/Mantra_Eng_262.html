शतं ते राजन् भीषजः सहस्रं उर्वी गभीराऽसुमतिष्टे अस्तु |बाधस्व दूरे निरृतिम् पराचैः कृतं चिदेनः प्र मुमुग्ध्य अस्मत् ||
śataṁ te rājan bhiṣajaḥ sahasram urvī gabhīrā sumatiṣ ṭe astu | bādhasva dūre nirṛtim parācaiḥ kṛtaṁ cid enaḥ pra mumugdhy asmat ||
  • śataṁ te rājan bhiṣajaḥ: This phrase translates to "A hundred (healers) for you, O King." Here, "rājan" refers to a king or leader, and "bhiṣajaḥ" means healers or physicians. This can be interpreted as a prayer for abundant health and well-being for the leader and his people.
  • sahasram urvī gabhīrā: This means "a thousand deep and wide (resources)." "Sahasram" signifies abundance, while "urvī" refers to the earth or resources. This part of the mantra emphasizes the need for vast and deep resources, which can be understood as a call for prosperity and richness in life.
  • sumatiṣ ṭe astu: This translates to "may there be good thoughts for you." "Sumatiṣ" refers to good or auspicious thoughts, and "astu" means may it be so. This highlights the importance of positive thinking and wisdom in leadership.
  • bādhasva dūre nirṛtim: This means "keep away misfortune from a distance." "Bādhasva" is a command to keep away, while "nirṛtim" refers to misfortune or calamity. This part of the mantra is a plea for protection against negativity and adversity.
  • parācaiḥ kṛtaṁ cid enaḥ pra mumugdhy asmat: This translates to "may whatever has been done by others be undone for us." "Parācaiḥ" refers to others, "kṛtaṁ" means done, and "mumugdhy" means to free or release. This signifies a desire for liberation from the consequences of actions taken by others that may negatively affect us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound prayer for health, prosperity, wisdom, protection, and liberation from negativity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, community well-being, and personal growth. For instance, leaders can invoke this mantra to seek the welfare of their people, ensuring that they have the resources and positive mindset to thrive. Additionally, individuals can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to cultivate good thoughts, protect themselves from misfortune, and seek freedom from negative influences in their lives. Overall, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of health, prosperity, and positive thinking in achieving a harmonious life.
