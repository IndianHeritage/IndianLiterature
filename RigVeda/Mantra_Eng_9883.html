विद्युः न याः पतन्ती दविद्योद्भरन्ती मे अप्याः काम्यानि |जनीष्टो अपो नार्यः सुजातः प्रोरवशी तिरता दीर्घमायुः ||
vidyun na yā patantī davidyod bharantī me apyā kāmyāni | janiṣṭo apo naryaḥ sujātaḥ prorvaśī tirata dīrgham āyuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidyun: lightning
    • na: not
    • yā: which
    • patantī: falling
    • davidyod: without knowledge
    • bharantī: carrying
    • me: my
    • apyā: also
    • kāmyāni: desires
    • janiṣṭo: born
    • apo: waters
    • naryaḥ: noble
    • sujātaḥ: well-born
    • prorvaśī: leading to prosperity
    • tirata: may flow
    • dīrgham: long
    • āyuḥ: life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of knowledge and the importance of noble intentions.
    • The imagery of lightning suggests a sudden illumination or insight, which is not to be confused with ignorance.
    • It emphasizes that desires should be aligned with wisdom and noble qualities.
    • The reference to waters signifies nourishment and the flow of life, suggesting that a well-born individual can lead a prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be likened to the lightning that illuminates the mind, guiding individuals away from ignorance.
    • In personal development, aligning one's desires with noble intentions can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • In community service, individuals who act with noble intentions can inspire others, creating a ripple effect of positive change.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of knowledge, noble intentions, and the pursuit of a meaningful life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, personal growth, and community engagement.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can lead a life that is not only prosperous but also enriching for themselves and others.
