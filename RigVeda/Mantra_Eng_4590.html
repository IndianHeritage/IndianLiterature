प्र श्येनो न मदिराम अंशुम अस्मै शिरो दासस्य नमुचेर मथायन | प्रावन नमीं साप्यं ससन्तं पृणग राया सम इषा सं स्वस्ति ||
pra śyeno na madiram aṁśum asmai śiro dāsasya namucer mathāyan | prāvan namīṁ sāpyaṁ sasantam pṛṇag rāyā sam iṣā saṁ svasti ||
  • pra śyeno: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be blessed" or "may we attain prosperity." It signifies a desire for well-being and success.
  • na madiram: This translates to "not intoxicated" or "not under the influence." It suggests a state of clarity and awareness, emphasizing the importance of being grounded and rational.
  • aṁśum: Meaning "light" or "radiance," this word symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine presence that guides us.
  • asmai: This means "to him" or "to that being," indicating a direction of reverence or devotion towards a higher power or deity.
  • śiro: Translated as "head," it represents authority, leadership, and the capacity to make decisions.
  • dāsasya: This means "of the servant" or "of the devotee," highlighting humility and the importance of service in spiritual practice.
  • namucer: Referring to "the one who is bound" or "the one who is restrained," it can symbolize the challenges and limitations we face in life.
  • mathāyan: This word means "to churn" or "to extract," suggesting the process of seeking wisdom and understanding through effort and perseverance.
  • prāvan: Meaning "to breathe" or "to live," it signifies the essence of life and vitality.
  • namīṁ: This translates to "to bow" or "to submit," indicating humility and respect towards the divine or higher principles.
  • sāpyaṁ: This means "to be fulfilled" or "to be satisfied," representing the attainment of desires and goals.
  • sasantam: This word means "to be prosperous" or "to be flourishing," emphasizing abundance and success in life.
  • pṛṇag: Translated as "to be filled" or "to be nourished," it signifies the importance of sustenance and support in our lives.
  • rāyā: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the significance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • sam: Meaning "together" or "in harmony," it emphasizes unity and collective well-being.
  • iṣā: This translates to "power" or "authority," indicating the strength and influence we can wield in our lives.
  • saṁ: Meaning "with" or "together," it reinforces the idea of community and collaboration.
  • svasti: This word means "well-being" or "good fortune," representing the ultimate goal of health, happiness, and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for clarity, prosperity, and spiritual fulfillment. It emphasizes the importance of humility, service, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and leadership. By embodying the principles of awareness, respect, and collaboration, individuals can navigate life's challenges and work towards a harmonious and prosperous existence. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and to contribute positively to the world around us, ultimately leading to a state of well-being and abundance.
