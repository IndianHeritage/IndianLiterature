पुष्णश्चक्रं न ऋश्यति न कोशोऽव पद्यते। नोऽस्य व्यथते पविः॥
pūṣṇaś cakraṁ na riṣyati na kośo 'va padyate | no asya vyathate paviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pūṣṇa: Nourisher or sustainer, often associated with the Sun or a divine force that provides life and sustenance.
    • cakraṁ: Wheel or cycle, symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the universe.
    • na: Not, indicating negation.
    • riṣyati: To be disturbed or affected.
    • kośo: A sheath or covering, often referring to the layers of existence or the physical body.
    • 'va: Or, used for comparison.
    • padyate: To fall or to be affected.
    • no: Not, again indicating negation.
    • asya: Of this, referring to the subject at hand.
    • vyathate: To be troubled or disturbed.
    • paviḥ: The divine or the sacred, often referring to the spirit or the essence of life.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea that the nourishing force (pūṣṇa) is unyielding and stable (cakraṁ) and is not disturbed (na riṣyati) by external factors.
    • It emphasizes that the essence of life (paviḥ) remains unaffected by the physical layers (kośo) or the troubles of existence (vyathate).
    • In essence, it conveys a message of resilience and stability in the face of life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a person going through a difficult phase in life, such as losing a job. The mantra reminds them that their inner strength and essence remain intact, and they can draw upon this inner nourishment to overcome challenges.
    • In nature, the Sun continues to rise and set, providing sustenance to all living beings, regardless of the storms or challenges that may arise. This reflects the unwavering nature of the nourishing force.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its message of inner strength and resilience. It teaches us that while external circumstances may change and challenge us, our core essence and the nourishing forces in our lives remain constant.
    • This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal crises, health challenges, or any situation where one feels overwhelmed. It serves as a reminder to connect with one's inner strength and the sustaining forces of life.
