प्रति यद आपो अदृश्रम आयतीर घृतं पयांसि बिभ्रतीर मधूनि |अध्वर्युभिर मनसा संविदानां इन्द्राय सोमं सुषुतं भरन्तीः ||
prati yad āpo adṛśram āyatīr ghṛtam payāṁsi bibhratīr madhūni | adhvaryubhir manasā saṁvidānā indrāya somaṁ suṣutam bharantīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in relation to
    • yad: whatever
    • āpo: waters
    • adṛśram: unseen, hidden
    • āyatīr: flowing, moving
    • ghṛtam: clarified butter, ghee
    • payāṁsi: milk, nourishing liquid
    • bibhratīr: holding, containing
    • madhūni: sweet, honey-like substances
    • adhvaryubhir: by the priests (who perform the rituals)
    • manasā: with the mind, mentally
    • saṁvidānā: knowing, understanding
    • indrāya: for Indra (the king of gods)
    • somaṁ: soma (a sacred drink)
    • suṣutam: well-prepared, excellent
    • bharantīḥ: carrying, bringing forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the offerings made to the divine, particularly to Indra, who is associated with strength and power.
    • The waters (āpo) that are unseen (adṛśram) symbolize the hidden potential and resources in life that can be harnessed.
    • The flowing nature (āyatīr) of these waters represents the continuous flow of life and opportunities.
    • Ghee (ghṛtam) and milk (payāṁsi) are symbols of nourishment and sustenance, indicating the importance of nurturing oneself and others.
    • The sweet substances (madhūni) signify the rewards of life that come from hard work and devotion.
    • The priests (adhvaryubhir) performing the rituals with understanding (manasā saṁvidānā) highlight the importance of intention and mindfulness in our actions.
    • Ultimately, the mantra emphasizes the act of offering (bharantīḥ) to the divine, which can be interpreted as giving back to the universe or contributing positively to society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize and utilize their hidden talents and resources for personal growth.
    • In a community setting, it encourages people to come together, share their skills, and support one another, much like the priests in the ritual.
    • In professional environments, the mantra can serve as a reminder to approach work with mindfulness and intention, ensuring that efforts are directed towards meaningful goals.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the importance of nurturing connections and offering support, akin to the nourishing elements mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of offering, nurturing, and the flow of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to recognize the unseen potential within ourselves and others, to act with intention, and to contribute positively to the world around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where we are mindful of our actions and their impact on ourselves and the community.
