अवः परेण पर एनावरें पदा वत्सं बिभ्रती गौर उद अस्तात् |सा कद्रीची कं स्विद अर्धं परागात् क्व स्वित सूते नहि यूथे अन्तः ||
avaḥ pareṇa para enāvareṇa padā vatsam bibhratī gaur ud asthāt | sā kadrīcī kaṁ svid ardham parāgāt kva svit sūte nahi yūthe antaḥ ||
  • avaḥ pareṇa para enāvareṇa padā: This phrase can be translated as "May we be protected by the supreme power that is beyond our understanding." Here, "avaḥ" signifies protection, "pareṇa" refers to the supreme or higher, and "para enāvareṇa" indicates something that is beyond our comprehension.
  • vatsam bibhratī gaur ud asthāt: This part means "The cow, which is a symbol of nourishment and abundance, stands firm." In many cultures, especially in India, the cow is revered as a sacred animal that provides sustenance and is associated with prosperity.
  • sā kadrīcī kaṁ svid ardham parāgāt: This translates to "Where does that which is powerful and beneficial go?" It raises a question about the nature of strength and its direction, prompting reflection on how we utilize our strengths in life.
  • kva svit sūte nahi yūthe antaḥ: This can be interpreted as "Where does it give birth, if not within the group?" This emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals and nurturing growth.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking protection from higher powers while recognizing the importance of community and nourishment. It reflects a holistic view of life where individual strength is intertwined with collective well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:
    • Community Support: Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of collective strength, it can be seen in community initiatives where individuals come together to support one another, such as food banks or local charities.
    • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the cow symbolizes the need for sustainable practices. In real life, this can translate to efforts in protecting the environment and promoting sustainable agriculture.
    • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek guidance from higher powers or wisdom while also recognizing the strength that comes from being part of a community. This can be reflected in mentorship programs or collaborative projects.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking higher guidance, and the strength found in community. It encourages individuals to reflect on their roles within their communities and the impact of their actions on the collective well-being.
