असावी सोम इन्द्र ते शविष्ठ धृष्णव आ गहि |आ त्वा पृणक्त्व इन्द्रियं रजः सूर्यो न रश्मिभिः ||
asāvi soma indra te śaviṣṭha dhṛṣṇav ā gahi | ā tvā pṛṇaktv indriyaṁ rajaḥ sūryo na raśmibhiḥ ||
  • asāvi soma indra te: This phrase can be translated as "May the Soma, O Indra, be invigorating for you." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and experiences that invigorate our spirit and mind.
  • śaviṣṭha dhṛṣṇav ā gahi: This means "the most powerful, may you grasp." Here, "śaviṣṭha" refers to the highest or the most potent, while "dhṛṣṇav" implies strength or power. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals strive to harness their strengths and capabilities to achieve their goals, whether in personal development or professional endeavors.
  • ā tvā pṛṇaktv indriyaṁ: This translates to "May we fill you, O Indra, with our senses." This line emphasizes the importance of sensory experiences and awareness. In everyday life, this can relate to being fully present in our experiences, whether it’s enjoying a meal, appreciating art, or engaging in nature.
  • rajaḥ sūryo na raśmibhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "the energy of the sun, not with its rays." This suggests a deeper understanding of energy and vitality that goes beyond mere physical manifestations. In real life, this can be seen in how we harness our inner energy and potential, rather than relying solely on external sources for motivation or inspiration.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine inspiration and strength through the sacred Soma, while also emphasizing the importance of sensory awareness and inner energy. In real life, it encourages individuals to pursue knowledge, harness their strengths, and be fully present in their experiences. This mantra serves as a reminder that true power and vitality come from within, and by cultivating our inner resources, we can achieve our highest potential.
