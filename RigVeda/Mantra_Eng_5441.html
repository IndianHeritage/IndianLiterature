विदुः पृथिव्या दिवो जनित्रं श्रिण्वन्त्य आपो अध क्षरन्तीः ||
viduḥ pṛthivyā divo janitraṁ śṛṇvanty āpo adha kṣarantīḥ ||
  • Viduḥ: This word translates to "they know" or "they understand." It signifies awareness and knowledge.
  • Pṛthivyā: Refers to "the earth." It symbolizes the physical realm and our connection to nature.
  • Divo: Means "of the sky" or "heaven." It represents the spiritual or celestial realm.
  • Janitraṁ: Translates to "the mother" or "the source." It indicates nurturing and creation.
  • Śṛṇvanty: Means "they hear" or "they listen." This emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • Āpo: Refers to "the waters." Water is often associated with life, purity, and sustenance.
  • Adha: Means "below" or "beneath." It indicates the grounding aspect of existence.
  • Kṣarantīḥ: Translates to "flowing" or "streaming." This suggests movement, change, and the dynamic nature of life.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of knowledge, nature, and the divine. It highlights the importance of understanding both the earthly and celestial realms, recognizing the nurturing aspects of creation, and being receptive to the wisdom that flows through life.
  • Real-Life Example: In our daily lives, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach learning and growth. For instance, a student who actively listens to their teachers (śṛṇvanty) and seeks knowledge (viduḥ) about the world (pṛthivyā) and the universe (divo) embodies the essence of this mantra. Additionally, environmental awareness and conservation efforts can be seen as a way of honoring the earth (pṛthivyā) and the waters (āpo) that sustain us.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for holistic understanding and respect for both the material and spiritual aspects of life. It encourages us to be aware of our surroundings, to listen to the wisdom of nature, and to recognize our role in the greater cosmic order. In real-life scenarios, this can manifest in practices such as mindfulness, environmental stewardship, and a commitment to lifelong learning.
