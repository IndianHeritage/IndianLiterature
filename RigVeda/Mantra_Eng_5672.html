प्रति वां सूर उदिते सूक्तैर मित्रं हुवे वरुणं पूतदक्षम् |ययोः असुर्यं अक्षितं ज्येष्ठं विश्वस्य याम्न्न आचिता जिगत्नु ||
prati vāṁ sūra udite sūktair mitraṁ huve varuṇam pūtadakṣam | yayor asurya1m akṣitaṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ viśvasya yāmann ācitā jigatnu ||
  • Mantra: "prati vāṁ sūra udite sūktair mitraṁ huve varuṇam pūtadakṣam | yayor asuryaṁ akṣitaṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ viśvasya yāmann ācitā jigatnu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • vāṁ: you
    • sūra: brave or heroic
    • udite: arising or emerging
    • sūktair: with hymns or praises
    • mitraṁ: friend
    • huve: I invoke or call upon
    • varuṇam: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • pūtadakṣam: pure and capable
    • yayor: of whom
    • asuryaṁ: the non-solar or dark aspect
    • akṣitaṁ: imperishable
    • jyeṣṭhaṁ: the elder or supreme
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • yāmann: whom
    • ācitā: who is worshipped
    • jigatnu: who is powerful or strong
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, specifically invoking Varuna, who represents the cosmic order and moral authority.
    • The phrase "prati vāṁ sūra udite" suggests a direction towards the brave, indicating the importance of courage in facing challenges.
    • By using "sūktair mitraṁ huve," the speaker emphasizes the role of friendship and community in spiritual practices, highlighting that collective efforts in praise can lead to divine blessings.
    • Varuna is described as "pūtadakṣam," which signifies purity and capability, reminding us that divine forces are not only powerful but also embody moral integrity.
    • The mention of "asuryaṁ akṣitaṁ" points to the eternal nature of the divine, suggesting that true strength lies in the imperishable aspects of existence, beyond the material world.
    • Finally, "jigatnu" reflects the idea of strength and resilience, encouraging individuals to harness their inner power in the face of adversity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the spirit of teamwork and courage can lead to successful project outcomes, much like the collective invocation of divine forces in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, fostering friendships based on mutual respect and support can create a strong foundation, akin to the bond with Varuna as a friend and protector.
    • In times of crisis, individuals can draw upon their inner strength and moral values, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on resilience and purity of purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of courage, community, and moral integrity in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the invocation of divine support, encouraging individuals to seek strength from both within and from their connections with others.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide us in personal growth, relationship building, and facing challenges with resilience.
