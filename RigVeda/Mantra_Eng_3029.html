उषो देव्य अमर्त्या वि भाहि चन्द्ररथा सूनृतā ईरयन्ती |आ त्वा वहन्तु सुमासो अश्वा हिरण्यवर्णाम पृथुपाजसो ये ||
uṣo devy amartyā vi bhāhi candrarathā sūnṛtā īrayantī | ā tvā vahantu suyamāso aśvā hiraṇyavarṇām pṛthupājaso ye ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the goddess of dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • devy: This means divine or goddess-like, indicating the sacred nature of the dawn.
  • amartyā: This term signifies immortality or the eternal aspect of the divine, suggesting that the dawn is a timeless phenomenon.
  • vi bhāhi: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you shine" or "may you illuminate," emphasizing the light and clarity that dawn brings.
  • candrārathā: This translates to "the chariot of the moon," symbolizing the connection between the sun and the moon, representing balance in nature.
  • sūnṛtā: This means "truthful" or "righteous," indicating that the dawn brings forth honesty and clarity.
  • īrayantī: This word means "nourishing" or "sustaining," suggesting that the dawn provides sustenance to life.
  • ā tvā vahantu: This phrase translates to "may they carry you," indicating a desire for the forces of nature to support and uplift the dawn.
  • suyamāso: This means "well-formed" or "beautiful," referring to the aesthetic beauty of the dawn.
  • aśvā: This word means "horses," often symbolizing strength and speed, representing the energy of the dawn.
  • hiraṇyavarṇām: This translates to "golden-colored," describing the radiant and vibrant hues of the dawn.
  • pṛthupājaso: This means "of great worship," indicating the reverence and respect that the dawn commands in the natural order.
  • ye: This word means "those," referring to the forces or entities that are being invoked.

Conclusion: The mantra invokes the goddess of dawn, celebrating her beauty, nourishment, and the truth she brings. In real life, this can be seen as a reminder to embrace new beginnings, seek clarity in our lives, and appreciate the beauty of nature. The dawn symbolizes hope and renewal, encouraging individuals to start each day with positivity and purpose. This mantra can be applicable in daily rituals, meditation practices, or moments of reflection, serving as a reminder of the eternal cycle of life and the importance of light and truth in our journeys.
