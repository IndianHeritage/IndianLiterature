स घा नो देवः सविता सहावा सावीषद वसुपतिर वसूनी |विश्रयमाणो अमतिम उरूचिम मर्तभोजनम अध रासते नः ||
sa ghā no devaḥ savitā sahāvā sāviṣad vasupatir vasūni | viśrayamāṇo amatim urūcīm martabhojanam adha rāsate naḥ ||
  • sa ghā no devaḥ savitā: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine Savita (the Sun) illuminate us." Here, Savita represents the life-giving energy of the sun, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
  • sahāvā sāviṣad vasupatir vasūni: This part means "May he (Savita) bestow upon us wealth and prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving material success and abundance in life.
  • viśrayamāṇo amatim urūcīm: This translates to "May we attain a state of fearlessness and great wisdom." It highlights the aspiration for knowledge and courage, which are vital for overcoming life's challenges.
  • martabhojanam adha rāsate naḥ: This phrase means "May we not be deprived of the sustenance of life." It reflects the desire for health and well-being, ensuring that we have the necessary resources to thrive.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for divine guidance, prosperity, wisdom, and sustenance. It acknowledges the interconnectedness of spiritual and material aspects of life.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be invoked during moments of uncertainty or when seeking clarity in decision-making. For instance, before starting a new venture or project, one might recite this mantra to seek blessings for success and wisdom.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors. It encourages individuals to strive for knowledge, prosperity, and well-being while remaining connected to the spiritual source of life. By integrating these principles into our lives, we can navigate challenges with courage and wisdom.
