वृष्टिं दिवः परि श्रव द्युम्नं पृथिव्याऽधि |सहो नः सोम पृत्सु धाह ||
vṛṣṭiṁ divaḥ pari srava dyumnam pṛthivyā adhi | saho naḥ soma pṛtsu dhāḥ ||
  • vṛṣṭiṁ: This word translates to "rain" or "precipitation." In a real-life context, rain is essential for agriculture, replenishing water sources, and maintaining ecological balance.
  • dvaḥ: This means "of the heavens" or "from the sky." It signifies the divine source of rain, emphasizing the connection between the celestial and terrestrial realms.
  • pari srava: This phrase means "to flow all around" or "to pour forth." It suggests abundance and the idea that rain should not be limited but should cover the earth generously.
  • dyumnam: This translates to "glory" or "brightness." It indicates that the rain brings not just water but also prosperity and vitality to the land.
  • pṛthivyā: This means "of the earth." It highlights the relationship between the rain and the earth, where the rain nourishes the soil and supports life.
  • adhi: This word means "upon" or "over." It signifies the action of rain falling upon the earth, reinforcing the connection between the heavens and the earth.
  • saho: This translates to "together" or "with strength." It implies a collective effort or unity in the act of receiving rain.
  • naḥ: This means "our." It personalizes the request for rain, indicating a communal need for sustenance and support.
  • soma: This refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine nourishment. In this context, it symbolizes the life-giving properties of rain.
  • pṛtsu: This means "in the fields" or "in the crops." It emphasizes the importance of rain for agriculture and food production.
  • dhāḥ: This translates to "to bestow" or "to grant." It is a plea for the divine to provide the necessary rain for growth and sustenance.

Conclusion: The mantra "vṛṣṭiṁ divaḥ pari srava dyumnam pṛthivyā adhi | saho naḥ soma pṛtsu dhāḥ ||" is a prayer for abundant rain from the heavens, recognizing its vital role in sustaining life on earth. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, where rain nourishes the soil, supports agriculture, and brings prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during agricultural festivals, rituals for rain, or in times of drought, reminding us of our dependence on natural elements for survival and the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and collective responsibility in seeking the blessings of nature for a fruitful and harmonious existence.
