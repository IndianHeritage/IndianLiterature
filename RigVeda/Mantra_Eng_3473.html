त्र्युदायं देवहितं यथा वः स्तोमो वाजाऽ ऋभुक्षाणो दद वः |जुह्वे मनुष्वद उपरासु विक्षु युष्मे सचा बृहद् दिवेषु सोमम् ||
tryudāyaṁ devahitaṁ yathā vaḥ stomo vājā ṛbhukṣaṇo dade vaḥ | juhve manuṣvad uparāsu vikṣu yuṣme sacā bṛhaddiveṣu somam ||
  • tryudāyaṁ: This term can be broken down into "tryu" meaning three and "dāyaṁ" meaning giving or bestowing. It signifies the act of bestowing threefold blessings or benefits.
  • devahitaṁ: This translates to "divine welfare" or "benefit from the gods." It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from divine entities for well-being.
  • yathā vaḥ: This phrase means "as you (gods) wish." It indicates a submission to divine will, acknowledging that the desires of the divine are paramount.
  • stomo: This refers to a hymn or praise. It highlights the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence through words and songs.
  • vājā: This word means "strength" or "power." It signifies the importance of strength in various aspects of life, including physical, mental, and spiritual.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who provides nourishment." It emphasizes the role of sustenance and support in life.
  • dade vaḥ: This means "may he give to you." It is a prayer for receiving blessings and support from the divine.
  • juhve: This translates to "I invoke" or "I offer." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine for assistance.
  • manuṣvad: This term means "human-like" or "of humans." It emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine, suggesting that divine blessings are accessible to all.
  • uparāsu: This means "above" or "in the higher realms." It indicates a connection to higher spiritual planes or divine entities.
  • vikṣu: This translates to "to the seeker" or "to the one who desires." It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and blessings.
  • yuṣme: This means "to you." It is a direct address to the divine, reinforcing the personal connection in the invocation.
  • sacā: This translates to "and also." It indicates the inclusion of additional blessings or aspects in the prayer.
  • bṛhaddiveṣu: This means "in the great heavens." It signifies the vastness of the divine and the blessings that can be found in the higher realms.
  • somam: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine ecstasy. It represents the ultimate spiritual nourishment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for divine blessings, strength, and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine favor and recognizing the interconnectedness of humans and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of need, such as during challenges or transitions, where individuals seek strength and guidance. It serves as a reminder to express gratitude and reverence, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the universe. By invoking these blessings, individuals can cultivate resilience and a sense of purpose in their lives.
