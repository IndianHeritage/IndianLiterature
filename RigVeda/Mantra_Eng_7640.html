वर्षिष्ठक्षत्रा उरुचक्षसा नराः राजानां दीर्घश्रुत्तमा |तां बाहुता न दंशना रथर्यतः साकं सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः ||
varṣiṣṭhakṣatrā urucakṣasā narā rājānā dīrghaśruttamā | tā bāhutā na daṁsanā ratharyataḥ sākaṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • varṣiṣṭhakṣatrā: The most excellent or supreme star/constellation.
    • urucakṣasā: With wide vision or perception.
    • narā: Men or people.
    • rājānā: Kings or rulers.
    • dīrghaśruttamā: With long-lasting fame or reputation.
    • tā: Those.
    • bāhutā: Many or abundant.
    • na: Not.
    • daṁsanā: By biting or harming.
    • ratharyataḥ: Of the chariot or vehicle.
    • sākaṁ: Together with.
    • sūryasya: Of the sun.
    • raśmibhiḥ: By the rays or beams.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme qualities of rulers and leaders who possess a broad vision and long-lasting fame.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength, suggesting that true power does not come from harm or division but from collaboration and enlightenment.
    • The reference to the sun's rays symbolizes knowledge, clarity, and guidance, which are essential for effective leadership.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders who prioritize collaboration and have a clear vision for the future often achieve greater success and respect.
    • Business leaders who foster teamwork and innovation, rather than competition and conflict, tend to create more sustainable and thriving organizations.
    • Community leaders who unite people for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice, exemplify the qualities described in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for enlightened leadership that values vision, unity, and the well-being of the community.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength comes from collaboration and the pursuit of knowledge, much like the sun's rays that illuminate and nurture life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more effective leadership, stronger communities, and a more harmonious society.
