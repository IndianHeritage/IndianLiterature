यन् नासत्याऽपराके अर्वाके अस्ति भेषजम् |तेन नूनं विमदाय प्रचेतसा छर्दिर वत्साय यच्छतम् ||
yan nāsatyā parāke arvāke asti bheṣajam | tena nūnaṁ vimadāya pracetasā chardir vatsāya yacchatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yan: that which
    • nāsatyā: of the Nāsatyas (the twin deities, often associated with healing and medicine)
    • parāke: in the higher realms
    • arvāke: in the lower realms
    • asti: exists
    • bheṣajam: medicine or healing
    • tena: by that
    • nūnaṁ: certainly
    • vimadāya: to remove ignorance or confusion
    • pracetasā: with wisdom or intelligence
    • chardir: sickness or affliction
    • vatsāya: for the calf (often symbolizing nourishment or care)
    • yacchatam: may be given or provided
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the existence of healing and medicine that transcends both the higher and lower realms.
    • The Nāsatyas, as divine figures, symbolize the connection between the spiritual and physical aspects of healing.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in overcoming ignorance and afflictions, suggesting that true healing comes from understanding and knowledge.
    • The reference to "vatsāya" indicates a nurturing aspect, highlighting the need for care and compassion in the healing process.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the integration of holistic approaches (spiritual and physical) can be seen in practices like yoga and meditation, which promote overall well-being.
    • Healthcare professionals often emphasize the importance of patient education, which aligns with the idea of removing ignorance for better health outcomes.
    • Support systems, such as family and community care, reflect the nurturing aspect of the mantra, where collective wisdom aids in healing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the holistic approach to healing, where both spiritual and physical realms are acknowledged.
    • It teaches us that true healing requires wisdom and understanding, not just medical intervention.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as healthcare, mental well-being, and community support, emphasizing the need for compassion and knowledge in the healing process.
