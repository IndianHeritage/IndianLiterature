इन्द्रावरुणा यद इमानी चक्रथुर विश्वा जातानि भुवनस्य मज्मना |क्षेमेण मित्रो वरुणं दुवस्यति मरुद्भिर उग्रः शुभं अन्य ईयते ||
indrāvaruṇā yad imāni cakrathur viśvā jātāni bhuvanasya majmanā | kṣemeṇa mitro varuṇaṁ duvasyati marudbhir ugraḥ śubham anya īyate ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This refers to the deities Indra and Varuna, who represent the forces of nature and cosmic order. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna embodies the moral and cosmic law.
  • Yad imāni cakrathur: This phrase translates to "whatever they have created." It signifies the manifestations of the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Viśvā jātāni bhuvanasya majmanā: This means "all beings born from the cosmic essence." It emphasizes the idea that all life forms are interconnected and originate from a common source.
  • Kṣemeṇa mitro: "With safety and friendship." This highlights the importance of harmony and cooperation among beings for a prosperous existence.
  • Varuṇaṁ duvasyati: This translates to "Varuna protects." It signifies the protective nature of the divine, ensuring the well-being of all creatures.
  • Marudbhir ugraḥ: "The fierce winds." This can be interpreted as the powerful forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • Śubham anya īyate: "May auspiciousness come from elsewhere." This suggests seeking blessings and positive outcomes from various sources.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, divine protection, and the balance of nature. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony among individuals and the environment. For instance, in community living, fostering friendships and cooperation can lead to a safer and more prosperous society. Additionally, recognizing the forces of nature and respecting them can lead to sustainable living practices. The mantra encourages us to seek positivity and auspiciousness in our lives, reminding us that we are all part of a larger cosmic order. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced world.
