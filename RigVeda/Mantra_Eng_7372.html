मन्त्र: पुरोळाशं नो अन्धसा इन्द्र सहस्रं आ भर |शता च शूर गोनाम ||
puroḻāśaṁ no andhasa indra sahasram ā bhara | śatā ca śūra gonām ||
  • puroḻāśaṁ: This term refers to the "noble" or "virtuous" qualities of a person. In real life, this can be associated with individuals who exhibit integrity, honor, and moral strength.
  • no: This is a pronoun meaning "our," indicating a collective aspiration or desire.
  • andhasa: This word translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It symbolizes the challenges and obstacles that one faces in life, such as confusion or lack of knowledge.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing power, strength, and leadership. In a modern context, this can be likened to a leader or a guiding force in our lives.
  • sahasram: This means "thousand," often used to denote abundance or a multitude of blessings. It signifies the vastness of resources or support available to us.
  • ā bhara: This phrase translates to "bring forth" or "bestow." It is a call to action, inviting the divine or higher powers to provide assistance.
  • śatā: This means "hundred," which can represent a significant number of qualities or virtues that one should aspire to embody.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the qualities mentioned.
  • śūra: This term means "brave" or "valiant." It refers to the courage and strength needed to face life's challenges.
  • gonām: This word translates to "of the herd" or "of the community." It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and support within a community.

Conclusion: The mantra "puroḻāśaṁ no andhasa indra sahasram ā bhara | śatā ca śūra gonām ||" encapsulates a profound aspiration for guidance, strength, and the dispelling of ignorance. It calls upon the divine to bestow noble qualities and virtues upon individuals and communities, encouraging bravery and collective support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership roles, community building, and personal development. By embodying the qualities of integrity, courage, and wisdom, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and contribute positively to their communities. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher powers while striving for personal and communal growth.
