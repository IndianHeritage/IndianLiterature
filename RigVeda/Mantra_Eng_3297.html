मन्त्र: स्थूरस्य रायो बृहतो य ईशे तं उष्टवाम विदथेष्व इन्द्रम् |यो वायुनā जयति गोमतीषु प्र धृष्णुयā नयति वस्यो अच् छ ||
sthūrasya rāyo bṛhato ya īśe tam u ṣṭavāma vidatheṣv indram | yo vāyunā jayati gomatīṣu pra dhṛṣṇuyā nayati vasyo accha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sthūrasya: of the solid or substantial
    • rāyo: wealth or prosperity
    • bṛhato: of the great or vast
    • ya: who
    • īśe: rules or governs
    • tam: that
    • u: indeed
    • ṣṭavāma: we praise
    • vidatheṣv: in the assemblies or gatherings
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • yo: who
    • vāyunā: by the wind or air
    • jayati: conquers or triumphs
    • gomatīṣu: among the cows or wealth
    • pra: with great effort
    • dhṛṣṇuyā: with strength or courage
    • nayati: leads or guides
    • vasyo: under control or governed
    • accha: good or auspicious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of prosperity and strength, emphasizing the importance of wealth that is substantial and governed by a higher power.
    • It praises Indra, the deity associated with rain, storms, and strength, suggesting that true wealth comes from divine blessings and the ability to harness natural forces.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes wealth in ancient times, as cows were a primary source of sustenance and economic stability.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of courage and strength in achieving success and controlling one's resources effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern business, the mantra can be related to the idea of seeking divine guidance and strength in leadership to achieve prosperity.
    • Farmers may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest, recognizing the role of nature and divine forces in their success.
    • In personal finance, individuals may reflect on this mantra to remind themselves of the importance of managing their resources wisely and with courage.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine blessings, personal strength, and the management of resources.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance from higher powers while also emphasizing the importance of courage and control in their endeavors.
    • In essence, it teaches that true prosperity is achieved through a combination of divine favor, personal effort, and wise management of one's wealth.
