शिक्षेयं अस्मै दित्सेयं शचीपते मनिषिणे |यद अहं गोपतिः स्याम ||
śikṣeyam asmai ditseyaṁ śacīpate manīṣiṇe | yad ahaṁ gopatiḥ syām ||
  • śikṣeyam: This word translates to "may I be taught" or "let me learn." It signifies the importance of education and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • asmai: This means "to him" or "to that person," indicating a direction towards a teacher or a guiding figure.
  • ditseyaṁ: This can be interpreted as "may I give" or "may I bestow," suggesting a reciprocal relationship in learning where knowledge is shared.
  • śacīpate: This term refers to "the lord of strength" or "the powerful one," often associated with divine or authoritative figures who possess wisdom.
  • manīṣiṇe: This means "to the wise one" or "to the thinker," emphasizing the value of wisdom and intellect in guiding others.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," serving as a connector in the mantra.
  • ahaṁ: This means "I," indicating the speaker's personal involvement and intention.
  • gopatiḥ: This term means "the lord of the cows" or "the protector," symbolizing a caretaker or leader role, often associated with responsibility and nurturing.
  • syām: This translates to "may I be" or "let me be," expressing a desire or aspiration.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or a request for guidance and wisdom from a knowledgeable figure. The speaker expresses a desire to learn and to be taught, while also wishing to share that knowledge with others. It highlights the importance of mentorship and the role of wise leaders in society.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In educational settings, students can embody the spirit of this mantra by seeking knowledge from their teachers and mentors, while also being encouraged to share their insights with peers.
  • In professional environments, leaders can adopt the principles of this mantra by fostering a culture of learning and collaboration, where knowledge is freely exchanged and everyone is encouraged to grow.
  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their own journeys of learning and teaching, recognizing the value of both roles in their lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the cyclical nature of learning and teaching. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to share it, fostering a community of growth and understanding. In various aspects of life, from education to leadership, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide us towards becoming better learners and teachers, ultimately contributing to a more knowledgeable and compassionate society.
