अंसेषु व ऋष्टयः पात्सु खादयो वक्षःसु रुक्मा मरुतो रथे शुभः | अग्निभ्राजसो विद्युतो गभस्त्योः शिप्राः शीरषु वितता हिरण्ययीः ||
aṁseṣu va ṛṣṭayaḥ patsu khādayo vakṣaḥsu rukmā maruto rathe śubhaḥ | agnibhrājaso vidyuto gabhastyoḥ śiprāḥ śīrṣasu vitatā hiraṇyayīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṁseṣu: in the limbs
    • va: and
    • ṛṣṭayaḥ: the seers or sages
    • patsu: in the horses
    • khādayo: the ones who consume
    • vakṣaḥsu: in the chests
    • rukmā: golden
    • maruto: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • rathe: in the chariot
    • śubhaḥ: auspicious
    • agnibhrājaso: of the fiery brilliance
    • vidyuto: of the lightning
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the luminous ones
    • śiprāḥ: swift
    • śīrṣasu: in the heads
    • vitātā: spread out
    • hiraṇyayīḥ: golden (feminine form)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies associated with the Maruts, the storm deities known for their speed and power.
    • The reference to "golden" suggests purity, wealth, and auspiciousness, indicating that these energies bring prosperity and good fortune.
    • The mention of "limbs," "chests," and "heads" symbolizes the holistic nature of divine blessings, affecting all aspects of life.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for strength and vitality in our endeavors, much like athletes preparing for a competition or individuals striving for success in their careers.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in achieving our goals and aspirations.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are interconnected with the forces of nature and the universe, and that invoking these energies can lead to success and fulfillment.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited during times of challenge or when embarking on new ventures, such as starting a business or pursuing a new project.
