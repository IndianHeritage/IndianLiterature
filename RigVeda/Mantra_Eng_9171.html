उप हवये सुहवम् मारुतं गणं पावकम ऋष्वं सख्याय शम्भुवम् |रायस पोषं सौश्रवसाया धीमहि तद देवानाम् अवो अद्या वृणीमहे ||
upa hvaye suhavam mārutaṁ gaṇam pāvakam ṛṣvaṁ sakhyāya śambhuvam | rāyas poṣaṁ sauśravasāya dhīmahi tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • upa hvaye: This phrase can be translated as "I call upon" or "I invoke." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • suhavam: This word means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It emphasizes the positive qualities that one seeks in the divine or in the situation being invoked.
  • mārutaṁ: Referring to the "Māruts," the storm deities or winds in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • gaṇam: This translates to "group" or "assembly," indicating a collective force or community of divine beings.
  • pāvakam: Meaning "purifying" or "sacred," it highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • ṛṣvaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," referring to the strength and resilience that one seeks.
  • sakhyāya: Meaning "for friendship" or "to foster companionship," it emphasizes the importance of relationships and community.
  • śambhuvam: This term refers to "auspiciousness" or "blessing," indicating a desire for divine favor and goodwill.
  • rāyas: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • poṣaṁ: Meaning "nourishment" or "sustenance," it signifies the need for support and growth in various aspects of life.
  • sauśravasāya: This word means "for good fame" or "renown," indicating the desire for recognition and respect in society.
  • dhīmahi: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," emphasizing the importance of reflection and mindfulness.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the divine qualities and blessings being invoked.
  • devānām: This translates to "of the gods," indicating the divine nature of the beings being called upon.
  • avo: Meaning "to us," it signifies the personal connection and appeal to the divine.
  • adyā: This word means "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the invocation.
  • vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose" or "we select," indicating an active participation in the process of seeking blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for divine assistance, emphasizing the importance of auspiciousness, strength, friendship, and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as during rituals, personal meditations, or communal gatherings where individuals seek to foster positive relationships, enhance their well-being, and invite blessings into their lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of community and the divine, encouraging individuals to reflect on their intentions and actions while striving for a harmonious and prosperous existence.
