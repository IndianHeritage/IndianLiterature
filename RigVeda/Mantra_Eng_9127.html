एतानि भद्राः कलश क्रियाम कुरुश्रवण ददतो मग्हानि |दान इद् वो मगवाणः सो अस्त्व अयं च सोमो हृदि यं बिभर्मि ||
etāni bhadrā kalaśa kriyāma kuruśravaṇa dadato maghāni | dāna id vo maghavānaḥ so astv ayaṁ ca somo hṛdi yam bibharmi ||
  • etāni bhadrā: This phrase translates to "these auspicious" or "these good things." It signifies the importance of positive and beneficial actions in life.
  • kalaśa: A "kalaśa" refers to a pot or vessel, often used in rituals to hold sacred water or offerings. It symbolizes abundance and prosperity.
  • kriyāma: This means "we perform" or "we do." It emphasizes the act of performing rituals or actions that lead to positive outcomes.
  • kuruśravaṇa: This term can be interpreted as "to hear" or "to listen." It suggests the importance of being receptive to wisdom and guidance.
  • dadato maghāni: This translates to "giving great wealth" or "bestowing abundance." It highlights the act of generosity and sharing one's resources with others.
  • dāna: This word means "donation" or "charity." It underscores the value of giving and supporting those in need.
  • id vo maghavānaḥ: This phrase means "may the powerful one (Maghavan) grant you." It invokes the blessings of a deity associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • so astv ayaṁ ca somo: This translates to "may this Soma (a sacred drink) be with you." Soma represents nourishment and vitality, suggesting the importance of sustenance in life.
  • hṛdi yam bibharmi: This means "I hold in my heart." It signifies the personal connection and commitment to the values of generosity and abundance.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of performing auspicious actions, being generous, and sharing wealth with others. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as charitable giving, community service, and fostering a spirit of cooperation and support among individuals. By embodying these principles, one can create a positive impact on society and cultivate a sense of abundance and prosperity in their own life. The mantra serves as a reminder to be mindful of our actions and their effects on ourselves and others, promoting a harmonious and prosperous existence.
