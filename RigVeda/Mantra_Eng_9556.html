सक्तुम iva तितुंना पुनन्तो यत्र धीराः मनसा वाचं अक्रत |अत्रा सखायः सख्यानि जानते भद्रैषां लक्ष्मीः निहिताधि वाचि ||
saktum iva titaünā punanto yatra dhīrā manasā vācam akrata | atrā sakhāyaḥ sakhyāni jānate bhadraiṣāṁ lakṣmīr nihitādhi vāci ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saktum - strength or power
    • iva - like or as
    • titaünā - by the wise or learned
    • punanto - purifying or cleansing
    • yatra - where
    • dhīrā - the wise or intelligent
    • manasā - with the mind
    • vācam - speech or words
    • akrata - have created or made
    • atrā - here
    • sakhāyaḥ - friends or companions
    • sakhyāni - friendships or bonds
    • jānate - know or understand
    • bhadraiṣāṁ - auspicious or good
    • lakṣmīr - wealth or prosperity
    • nihitādhi - placed or established
    • vāci - in speech or words
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of wise individuals who purify their thoughts and words.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creating meaningful relationships and understanding among friends.
    • The mantra suggests that true wealth and prosperity are found in the bonds of friendship and the purity of speech.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team that communicates openly and supports each other can achieve greater success than one that does not.
    • In personal relationships, friends who uplift and encourage each other create a positive environment that fosters growth.
    • In community settings, when individuals come together with a shared purpose and understanding, they can create lasting change.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of wisdom, purity of thought, and the value of friendships.
    • It teaches us that true prosperity is not just material wealth but also the richness of our relationships and the quality of our interactions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both personally and collectively.
