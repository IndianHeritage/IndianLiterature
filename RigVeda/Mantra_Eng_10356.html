गाम अङ्गैषा आ ह्वयति दार्व अङ्गैषो अपावधीत् |वसन्न अरण्याण्यां सायम् अकृक्षद इति मन्यते ||
gām aṅgaiṣa ā hvayati dārv aṅgaiṣo apāvadhīt | vasann araṇyānyāṁ sāyam akrukṣad iti manyate ||
  • gām: This word translates to "the world" or "the community." It signifies the collective existence of beings and the interconnectedness of life.
  • aṅgaiṣa: This term means "with the limbs" or "with the parts." It suggests that every individual is a part of a larger whole, emphasizing unity and cooperation.
  • ā hvayati: This phrase means "calls upon" or "invokes." It indicates a plea or a request for assistance or guidance from a higher power or the universe.
  • dārv: This word refers to "the tree" or "the plant." It symbolizes nature, growth, and sustenance, highlighting the importance of the natural world in our lives.
  • apāvadhīt: This term means "has not been harmed" or "is protected." It conveys a sense of safety and preservation, suggesting that the natural world and community should be safeguarded.
  • vasann: This word translates to "dwelling" or "residing." It signifies a sense of belonging and stability within a community or environment.
  • araṇyānyāṁ: This term means "in the wilderness" or "in the forest." It represents the untamed aspects of nature and the importance of respecting and coexisting with it.
  • sāyam: This word translates to "evening" or "twilight." It symbolizes a time of reflection, transition, and the closing of the day, which can be a moment for introspection.
  • akrukṣad: This term means "did not harm" or "did not injure." It emphasizes the importance of non-violence and compassion towards all living beings.
  • iti manyate: This phrase translates to "thus it is thought" or "this is the belief." It indicates a shared understanding or philosophy within a community.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, nature, and the importance of non-violence. It teaches us to recognize our interconnectedness with the world around us and to invoke protection for both our communities and the natural environment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as promoting environmental conservation, fostering community support systems, and practicing compassion in our daily interactions. By embracing these principles, we can create a harmonious existence that respects both humanity and nature.
