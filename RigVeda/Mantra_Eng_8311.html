इषुर न धन्वन् प्रति धीयते मतिर्वत्सो न मातुर उप सर्ज्य ऊधनि |उरुधारेव दुहे अग्र आयत्य अस्य व्रतेष्व अपि सोम इष्यते ||
iṣur na dhanvan prati dhīyate matir vatso na mātur upa sarjy ūdhani | urudhāreva duhe agra āyaty asya vrateṣv api soma iṣyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iṣur: arrow
    • na: not
    • dhanvan: bow
    • prati: against
    • dhīyate: is directed
    • matir: mind or intellect
    • vatso: calf or young one
    • na: not
    • mātur: mother’s
    • upa: towards
    • sarjy: gathering or collection
    • ūdhani: in the water
    • urudhāreva: like a strong one
    • duhe: milking
    • agra: foremost or best
    • āyaty: is obtained
    • asya: of this
    • vrateṣv: in vows or commitments
    • api: also
    • soma: soma (a sacred drink)
    • iṣyate: is desired or sought
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between the mind, intentions, and actions.
    • The first part suggests that an arrow (representing intention or action) is not directed against the bow (representing the mind or intellect). This implies that our actions should align with our thoughts.
    • The second part compares a young calf that does not go towards its mother, indicating that sometimes our desires or intentions may not align with our natural instincts or nurturing sources.
    • The imagery of milking and obtaining the best from a strong source emphasizes the importance of commitment and effort in achieving our goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, if an employee's actions (like working on a project) do not align with their intentions (like wanting to succeed), it can lead to failure. This mantra reminds us to ensure our actions reflect our true intentions.
    • In personal relationships, if one desires closeness but does not communicate or act in a nurturing way, the relationship may suffer. The mantra encourages us to align our actions with our emotional needs.
    • In spiritual practices, the commitment to a path (like meditation or yoga) should be matched with the intention to grow and learn, similar to how one would seek the best results from a strong source.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of aligning our thoughts, intentions, and actions. It serves as a reminder that our desires should be nurtured through appropriate actions.
    • In real life, this alignment can lead to greater success, fulfillment, and harmony in various aspects, including personal growth, relationships, and professional endeavors.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches us that to achieve our goals, we must ensure that our actions are in harmony with our intentions, much like the relationship between a calf and its mother or the process of milking from a strong source.
