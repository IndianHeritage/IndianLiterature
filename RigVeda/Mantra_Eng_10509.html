इन्द्रस्येव रातिम आजोहुवानाः स्वस्तये नावमिवा रुहेमा |उर्वी न पृथ्वी बहुले गभीरें मा वाम एतौ मा परेतौ ऋषाम ||
indrasyeva rātim ājohuvānāḥ svastaye nāvam ivā ruhema | urvī na pṛthvī bahule gabhīre mā vām etau mā paretau riṣāma ||
  • Indrasyeva: This term refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Rātim: This word means 'joy' or 'delight,' indicating a state of happiness and fulfillment.
  • Ājohuvānāḥ: This can be interpreted as 'those who invoke' or 'those who call upon,' suggesting a communal effort or collective prayer.
  • Svastaye: This term translates to 'for well-being' or 'for prosperity,' emphasizing the importance of health and success.
  • Nāvam: Meaning 'boat' or 'vessel,' it symbolizes a means of navigation through life's challenges.
  • Ivā: This word means 'like' or 'as if,' drawing a comparison to illustrate a point.
  • Ruhema: This term means 'may we prosper' or 'may we thrive,' expressing a desire for growth and success.
  • Urvī: This refers to 'the earth' or 'the vast expanse,' symbolizing stability and grounding.
  • Pṛthvī: Another term for 'earth,' reinforcing the idea of a solid foundation in life.
  • Bahule: This means 'abundant' or 'plentiful,' indicating a desire for richness in life.
  • Gabhīre: This translates to 'deep' or 'profound,' suggesting a depth of experience or understanding.
  • Mā: This word means 'not,' serving as a negation in the context of the mantra.
  • Vām: This means 'you' or 'your,' indicating a personal connection to the listener or the community.
  • Etau: This term means 'these,' referring to the subjects of the invocation.
  • Mā: Again, this serves as a negation, emphasizing the importance of avoiding negativity.
  • Paretau: This means 'the other' or 'the external,' suggesting a focus on the internal rather than external influences.
  • Riṣāma: This translates to 'we may not be harmed' or 'may we be protected,' expressing a desire for safety and security.

The mantra essentially calls upon the divine for joy, prosperity, and protection, invoking the strength of Indra to navigate through life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being, suggesting that when individuals come together in prayer and intention, they can create a powerful force for good.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for festivals or rituals, invoking this mantra can enhance the sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation to focus on their goals and aspirations, seeking guidance and protection in their endeavors.
  • In Times of Crisis: During challenging times, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from the community and the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra is significant as it encapsulates the essence of seeking joy, prosperity, and protection through collective effort and divine invocation. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the power of shared intentions in overcoming life's challenges.
