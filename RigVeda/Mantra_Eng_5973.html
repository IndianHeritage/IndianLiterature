इन्द्र जहि पुमाṁसं यातुधानम् उता स्त्रियं मायया शाशदानाम् |विग्रीवासो मूरदेव ऋदन्तु मा ते दृष्टं सूर्यं उच्छरन्तम् ||
indra jahi pumāṁsaṁ yātudhānam uta striyam māyayā śāśadānām | vigrīvāso mūradevā ṛdantu mā te dṛśan sūryam uccarantam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Jahi: Means "to conquer" or "to defeat," indicating a call to overcome obstacles or adversaries.
  • Pumāṁsaṁ: Refers to "the male" or "the man," often representing human strength or ego.
  • Yātudhānam: Refers to "the sorcerer" or "the evil spirit," symbolizing negative forces or challenges in life.
  • Uta: Means "and" or "also," connecting thoughts or ideas.
  • Striyam: Refers to "the woman," symbolizing femininity, nurturing, and wisdom.
  • Māyayā: Means "by illusion" or "through deception," indicating the challenges posed by falsehoods or distractions.
  • Śāśadānām: Refers to "the eternal" or "the everlasting," symbolizing stability and permanence.
  • Vigrīvāso: Refers to "the one who wears the skin," often symbolizing transformation or the ability to adapt.
  • Mūradevā: Refers to "the great god," indicating a higher power or divine influence.
  • Ṛdantu: Means "to be pleased" or "to rejoice," indicating a state of happiness or fulfillment.
  • Mā te: Means "may you not," indicating a wish or a prayer for protection.
  • Dṛśan: Refers to "sight" or "vision," symbolizing perception and understanding.
  • Sūryam: Refers to "the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Uccarantam: Means "rising" or "ascending," indicating growth, progress, or elevation.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for protection and strength against negative forces and illusions in life. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges, whether they come from within (ego) or from external sources (evil spirits). The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support in this endeavor.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in personal or professional life, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and clarity.
  • Overcoming Illusions: In situations where one feels deceived or misled, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek the truth and not be swayed by false appearances.
  • Empowerment: It encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength and wisdom, fostering a sense of empowerment and resilience.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for courage and clarity in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that with divine support and inner strength, one can overcome any challenge, rise above illusions, and achieve enlightenment. This mantra is applicable in daily life as a source of inspiration and guidance, encouraging individuals to confront their fears and strive for personal growth.
