या एको अस्ति दंशना महां उग्रो अभि व्रतैः |गमत स शिप्री न स योषद आ गमद धवं न परि वरजति ||
ya eko asti daṁsanā mahām̐ ugro abhi vrataiḥ | gamat sa śiprī na sa yoṣad ā gamad dhavaṁ na pari varjati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • eko: is one
    • asti: exists
    • daṁsanā: with a bite or sting
    • mahām̐: great
    • ugro: fierce or powerful
    • abhi: towards
    • vrataiḥ: with vows or commitments
    • gamat: goes
    • sa: he
    • śiprī: swift or quick
    • na: not
    • sa: he
    • yoṣad: woman or female
    • ā: to
    • gamad: goes
    • dhavaṁ: to the house or home
    • na: not
    • pari: around
    • varjati: avoids or shuns
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a singular entity that is powerful and fierce, yet also committed to its vows.
    • The phrase "who is one" suggests the idea of unity or singularity, which can be interpreted as the oneness of existence or the divine.
    • The mention of "with a bite" indicates that this entity possesses strength and the ability to impact its surroundings significantly.
    • The swift nature of this entity implies that it acts quickly and decisively, which can be likened to how opportunities in life often require prompt action.
    • The latter part of the mantra suggests that this entity does not avoid the feminine aspect or the home, indicating a balance between strength and nurturing.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a strong leader embodies the qualities of decisiveness and commitment, much like the "great fierce one" in the mantra. For instance, a CEO who makes bold decisions while remaining committed to the company's values.
    • In personal relationships, the balance between strength and nurturing can be seen in a parent who provides for their family while also being emotionally supportive.
    • In nature, a lion can be seen as a representation of this mantra, being both powerful and protective of its pride, while also nurturing its young.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength combined with commitment and nurturing qualities.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in dominance but also in the ability to care and support others.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for a balance between assertiveness and compassion, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community involvement.
