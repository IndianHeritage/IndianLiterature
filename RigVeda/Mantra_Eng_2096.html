श्रुधी हवम् इन्द्र मा ऋषण्यः स्याम ते दावने वसूनाम् |इमा हि त्वाम ऊर्जो वर्धयन्ति वसूयवः सिन्धवो न क्षरन्तः ||
śrudhī havam indra mā riṣaṇyaḥ syāma te dāvane vasūnām | imā hi tvām ūrjo vardhayanti vasūyavaḥ sindhavo na kṣarantaḥ ||
  • śrudhī havam indra: This phrase can be translated as "We call upon you, Indra, with our offerings." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be likened to how people seek blessings or guidance from higher powers or leaders when facing challenges.
  • mā riṣaṇyaḥ syāma te dāvane vasūnām: This translates to "May we not be harmed; may we be strong in the presence of the divine." This reflects a desire for protection and strength, similar to how individuals or communities come together in times of crisis, seeking safety and resilience.
  • imā hi tvām ūrjo vardhayanti: This means "These offerings increase your power." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the belief that sincere offerings can enhance the divine's strength. In modern life, this can be seen in how people contribute to causes they believe in, hoping to amplify their impact.
  • vasūyavaḥ sindhavo na kṣarantaḥ: This translates to "The rivers of wealth do not diminish." It suggests that true abundance and prosperity are sustainable. In practical terms, this can relate to environmental sustainability, where the health of natural resources is crucial for long-term prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support, the power of community, and the sustainability of resources. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate strength through unity, to offer gratitude, and to ensure that our actions contribute to the well-being of both ourselves and the environment. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during community gatherings, environmental initiatives, or personal reflections on resilience and abundance.
