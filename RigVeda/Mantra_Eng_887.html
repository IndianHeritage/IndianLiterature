इन्द्रो वृत्रस्य तविषीं निर अहन सहसा सहः |महत तद अस्य पौंस्यं वृत्रं जगहन्वाम् अषृजद आर्चन् अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
indro vṛtrasya taviṣīṁ nir ahan sahasā sahaḥ | mahat tad asya pauṁsyaṁ vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ asṛjad arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Vṛtrasya: Vṛtra is a demon or a serpent that represents obstacles, chaos, or ignorance that one must overcome.
  • Taviṣīṁ: This term signifies strength or might, indicating the power needed to confront challenges.
  • Nir ahan: Translates to "without effort" or "effortlessly," suggesting that true strength can lead to victory without struggle.
  • Sahasā: Means "with force" or "suddenly," indicating the swift action required to overcome obstacles.
  • Sahaḥ: Refers to "together" or "with," emphasizing the importance of unity and collaboration in overcoming challenges.
  • Mahāt: Means "great" or "significant," highlighting the importance of the task at hand.
  • Tad: Translates to "that," referring to the strength or power being invoked.
  • Pauṁsyaṁ: Refers to "masculine strength" or "vitality," indicating the essence of power and courage.
  • Vṛtraṁ: Again refers to the demon or obstacle that needs to be defeated.
  • Jaghanvām̐: Means "we have defeated" or "we have conquered," indicating a successful overcoming of challenges.
  • Asṛjad: Translates to "created" or "brought forth," suggesting the act of manifesting strength and power.
  • Arcann: Refers to "worship" or "offering," indicating the reverence for the divine forces at play.
  • Anu: Means "following" or "in accordance with," suggesting alignment with higher principles.
  • Svarājyam: Refers to "sovereignty" or "kingdom," symbolizing the ultimate goal of achieving control and mastery over one's life and circumstances.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming obstacles through strength, unity, and divine support. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal challenges, professional hurdles, or societal issues. For instance, when facing a difficult project at work, invoking the spirit of Indra can inspire teamwork and collective effort to achieve success. The mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and collaboration, one can conquer the Vṛtras of life, leading to personal sovereignty and fulfillment.
