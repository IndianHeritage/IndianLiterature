त्वां इद वृत्रहन्तम सुतावन्तो हवामहे |यद इन्द्र मृळयासि नः ||
tvām id vṛtrahantama sutāvanto havāmahe | yad indra mṛḻayāsi naḥ ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to a divine entity, often Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • id - This means "now" or "here," indicating immediacy and presence.
  • vṛtrahantama - This compound word can be broken down into "vṛtra," which means "the obstructer" or "the demon," and "hantama," meaning "the one who kills." Thus, it refers to the one who destroys obstacles or enemies.
  • sutāvanto - This translates to "the ones who have good offspring" or "the ones who are blessed with progeny," indicating prosperity and continuity.
  • havāmahe - This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," suggesting a prayer or a plea for assistance.
  • yad - This means "that" or "which," referring to a specific action or quality.
  • indra - Referring to Indra, the god of rain, thunder, and war, symbolizing strength and power.
  • mṛḻayāsi - This means "you make calm" or "you soothe," indicating a quality of bringing peace or resolution.
  • naḥ - This translates to "our," indicating possession or belonging.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, invoking his power to remove obstacles and bring prosperity. The speaker acknowledges Indra's ability to calm and soothe, asking for his blessings for themselves and their lineage.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Overcoming Challenges: Just as Indra is invoked to destroy obstacles, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek strength and support when facing difficulties in life, such as career challenges or personal struggles.
  • Family and Legacy: The reference to "good offspring" emphasizes the importance of family and the desire for a prosperous lineage. This can resonate with individuals who prioritize family values and seek blessings for their children.
  • Seeking Peace: The aspect of calming and soothing can be applied in stressful situations, such as during conflicts or emotional turmoil. It serves as a reminder to seek inner peace and resolution.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support to overcome life's challenges, ensure prosperity, and foster peace. It serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that can be drawn from faith and the importance of community and family in achieving a fulfilling life.
