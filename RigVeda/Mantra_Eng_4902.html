उदु त्याच चक्षुर महि मित्रयोः आमं एति प्रियं वरुणयोः अदब्दम् |ऋतस्य शुचि दर्शतम अनीकं रुक्मो न दिव उदीता व्य अद्यौत ||
ud u tyac cakṣur mahi mitrayor ām̐ eti priyaṁ varuṇayor adabdham | ṛtasya śuci darśatam anīkaṁ rukmo na diva uditā vy adyaut ||
  • ud u tyac cakṣur: This phrase can be translated as "Let the eye be opened." It signifies the importance of perception and awareness in our lives. Just as we need our physical eyes to see the world, we also need our inner vision to understand deeper truths.
  • mahi mitrayor: This means "the great friendship." It emphasizes the value of relationships and alliances in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in the bonds we form with friends, family, and colleagues, which help us navigate challenges and celebrate successes.
  • ām̐ eti priyaṁ: Translated as "comes to the beloved," this phrase suggests the idea of attraction and connection. In a practical sense, it can refer to how we are drawn to people, ideas, or goals that resonate with us emotionally and spiritually.
  • varuṇayor adabdham: This can be interpreted as "the unspoken bond of Varuna." Varuna is often associated with cosmic order and truth. This phrase highlights the significance of unexpressed understanding and respect in relationships, akin to the silent agreements we have with those we trust.
  • ṛtasya śuci darśatam: This translates to "the pure vision of truth." It underscores the importance of clarity and honesty in our perceptions. In everyday life, this can be applied to our pursuit of knowledge and integrity in our actions and decisions.
  • anīkaṁ rukmo: This phrase means "the shining one without a host." It symbolizes individuality and the unique light each person brings to the world. In real life, this can be seen in how each person contributes their talents and perspectives to society.
  • na diva uditā vy adyaut: This translates to "not yet has the day dawned." It suggests the idea of potential and the anticipation of new beginnings. In practical terms, it can represent the hope and possibilities that lie ahead, encouraging us to strive for our goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound insights about perception, relationships, truth, individuality, and potential. Its significance lies in reminding us to cultivate awareness, cherish our connections, pursue truth, and embrace our unique contributions to the world. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can guide us in personal development, fostering meaningful relationships, and navigating the complexities of life with integrity and hope.
