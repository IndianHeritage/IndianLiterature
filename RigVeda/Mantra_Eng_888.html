मन्त्र: इमे चित तव मन्यवे वेपते भियसां मही |यद इन्द्र वज्रिन्न ओजसा वृत्रम मरुत्वां अवधीर्ज आर्चन् अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
ime cit tava manyave vepete bhiyasā mahī | yad indra vajrinn ojasā vṛtram marutvām̐ avadhīr arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • ime cit tava manyave vepete bhiyasā mahī: This line can be translated to "These thoughts of yours, O Indra, tremble with great fear." Here, "ime cit" refers to "these thoughts," "tava" means "yours," "manyave" translates to "in the mind," "vepete" means "tremble," and "bhiyasā" signifies "with fear." The term "mahī" refers to the earth or the great expanse.
  • yad indra vajrinn ojasā vṛtram marutvām̐ avadhīr: This part translates to "When Indra, with his thunderbolt, struck down the Vritra, the demon of drought." "Yad" means "when," "indra" refers to the deity Indra, "vajrinn" translates to "with the thunderbolt," "ojasā" means "with power," "vṛtram" refers to the demon Vritra, and "marutvām̐ avadhīr" means "the one who was struck down by the Maruts." This line highlights the power of Indra in overcoming obstacles and challenges.
  • arcann anu svarājyam: This translates to "Worshipping, he attained sovereignty." "Arcann" means "worshipping," "anu" translates to "after," and "svarājyam" refers to "sovereignty" or "kingdom." This line emphasizes the importance of devotion and worship in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the themes of fear, power, and devotion. It signifies how the thoughts and fears of individuals can be overcome through strength and determination, much like how Indra defeated the demon Vritra. In real life, this can be applied to various situations where individuals face challenges or fears. For instance, a student preparing for an important exam may feel anxious (vepete bhiyasā), but with hard work and determination (vajrinn ojasā), they can overcome their fears and achieve success (arcann anu svarājyam). The mantra serves as a reminder that through courage and devotion, one can conquer obstacles and attain their aspirations.
