नकीष ṭं कर्मणा नशन न प्र योषन न योषति |देवानां य इन् मनो यजमान इयक्षत्य अभीद अयजनो भुवत ||
nakiṣ ṭaṁ karmaṇā naśan na pra yoṣan na yoṣati | devānāṁ ya in mano yajamāna iyakṣaty abhīd ayajvano bhuvat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakiṣ ṭaṁ: Not by action
    • karmaṇā: By deeds
    • naśan: Not by destruction
    • na pra yoṣan: Not by offering
    • na yoṣati: Not by sacrifice
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods
    • ya: Who
    • in mano: In the mind
    • yajamāna: The worshipper
    • iyakṣaty: Who performs
    • abhīd: Is known
    • ayajvano: Of the non-worshipper
    • bhuvat: Becomes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes that true spiritual attainment and connection with the divine cannot be achieved merely through rituals, sacrifices, or actions.
    • It suggests that the essence of devotion lies in the purity of the mind and intention rather than in the external acts of worship.
    • For example, a person may perform elaborate rituals but if their mind is not aligned with the divine purpose, those actions may not hold true significance.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and relationships.
    • For instance, a leader may engage in numerous team-building activities (actions) but if they do not genuinely care for their team members (intention), the efforts may be futile.
    • Similarly, in personal relationships, one may give gifts or perform acts of kindness, but if these actions are not backed by sincere feelings, they may not foster true connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that the quality of our thoughts and intentions is paramount in our spiritual journey.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate a sincere and devoted mindset, which ultimately leads to a deeper connection with the divine and a more meaningful existence.
    • In essence, it teaches that true worship and devotion come from within, and that external actions should be a reflection of our inner state.
