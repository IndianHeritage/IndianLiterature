कश्मा अद्य सुजाताय रातहव्याय प्र ययुः |एनā यामेन मरुतः ||
kasmā adya sujātāya rātahavyāya pra yayuḥ | enā yāmena marutaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kasmā adya sujātāya rātahavyāya pra yayuḥ | enā yāmena marutaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kasmā: Why
    • adya: Today
    • sujātāya: To the well-born or noble
    • rātahavyāya: To the one who is worthy of offerings
    • pra yayuḥ: They have gone forth
    • enā: By this
    • yāmena: By the path or way
    • marutaḥ: Of the Maruts (storm deities)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the actions taken today towards a noble being who is worthy of offerings.
    • It reflects on the idea of seeking guidance or blessings from divine forces, specifically the Maruts, who are associated with storms and strength.
    • The phrase "pra yayuḥ" suggests movement or progress, indicating that the actions taken are significant and lead to a greater purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to the idea of recognizing and rewarding employees who demonstrate noble qualities and contribute positively to the organization.
    • In personal relationships, it can signify the importance of acknowledging and supporting friends or family members who are striving for their goals and deserving of encouragement.
    • In community service, it emphasizes the need to uplift those who are working towards the betterment of society, thus fostering a spirit of collaboration and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and honoring those who are noble and deserving of respect and offerings.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and the paths they choose, urging them to align with positive forces and contribute to the greater good.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of community, support, and the pursuit of noble endeavors in our daily lives.
