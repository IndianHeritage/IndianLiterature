त्रीणि जाना परि भूषन्त्य अस्य समुद्र एकं दिव्य एकम् अप्सु |पूर्वाम् अनु प्र दिशम् पार्थिवानाम् ऋतून प्रशासद विदधाव अनुष्ठु ||
trīṇi jānā pari bhūṣanty asya samudra ekaṁ divy ekam apsu | pūrvām anu pra diśam pārthivānām ṛtūn praśāsad vi dadhāv anuṣṭhu ||
  • Mantra: "trīṇi jānā pari bhūṣanty asya samudra ekaṁ divy ekam apsu | pūrvām anu pra diśam pārthivānām ṛtūn praśāsad vi dadhāv anuṣṭhu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trīṇi: three
    • jānā: beings or entities
    • pari: around or surrounding
    • bhūṣanty: adorn or embellish
    • asya: of this
    • samudra: ocean or sea
    • ekaṁ: one
    • divy: divine or celestial
    • ekam: one
    • apsu: in the waters
    • pūrvām: towards the east or the past
    • anu: following or after
    • pra: forth or forward
    • diśam: direction or space
    • pārthivānām: of the earthly beings
    • ṛtūn: seasons
    • praśāsad: governs or rules
    • vi: indeed
    • dadhāv: moves or flows
    • anuṣṭhu: in accordance with or in harmony
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence that surrounds and governs the three realms of existence.
    • The "three beings" can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual aspects of life.
    • The "ocean" symbolizes the vastness of existence, while the "one divine" refers to the singularity of the ultimate reality or consciousness.
    • The mention of "waters" signifies the source of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • The phrase "governs the seasons" indicates the cyclical nature of life and the importance of harmony with the natural world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the seasons is crucial for planting and harvesting crops, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the cyclical nature of life.
    • In personal development, recognizing the balance between physical, mental, and spiritual well-being can lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • Environmental conservation efforts highlight the importance of nurturing our natural resources, akin to the divine essence that sustains life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine essence that governs life.
    • It serves as a reminder to live in harmony with nature and to recognize the cyclical patterns in our lives.
    • By applying these principles, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and foster a more sustainable and balanced way of living.
