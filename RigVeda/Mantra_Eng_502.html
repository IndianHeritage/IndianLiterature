त्वं तस्य द्वयाविनो 'घशंसस्य कस्य चित् |पदाभि तिष्ठ तपुषिम ||
tvaṁ tasya dvayāvino 'ghaśaṁsasya kasya cit | padābhi tiṣṭha tapuṣim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tasya: of that
    • dvayāvino: of the two (dual)
    • 'ghaśaṁsasya: of the one who is free from sin or fault
    • kasya: of whom
    • cit: indeed
    • padābhi: by the feet
    • tiṣṭha: stand or remain
    • tapuṣim: in the light or brilliance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses a divine entity, invoking the presence of a higher power that is free from sin and duality.
    • The phrase "tvaṁ tasya dvayāvino" suggests a connection to the duality of existence, emphasizing the importance of balance in life.
    • The term "'ghaśaṁsasya" highlights the purity and faultlessness of the divine, encouraging individuals to aspire towards such qualities.
    • "padābhi tiṣṭha tapuṣim" can be interpreted as a call to stand firm in the light of truth and righteousness, symbolizing the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for integrity and ethical behavior, reminding them to remain steadfast in their principles.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to seek balance and harmony, recognizing the dual nature of human interactions and the importance of understanding and compassion.
    • In the pursuit of knowledge, it serves as a reminder to seek truth and wisdom, standing firm against ignorance and falsehood.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, balance, and the pursuit of truth in various aspects of life.
    • Its application can be seen in personal growth, ethical decision-making, and the quest for knowledge, making it relevant in contemporary society.
    • By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened world.
