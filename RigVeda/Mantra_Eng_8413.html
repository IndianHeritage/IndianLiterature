आ यस्तस्थौ भुवनानि अमर्त्यो विश्वानि सोमः परि तान्य अर्षति |कृत्वान संचितं विचृतं अभिष्टय इन्दुः सिषक्त्य उषसं न सूर्यः ||
ā yas tasthau bhuvanāny amartyo viśvāni somaḥ pari tāny arṣati | kṛṇvan saṁcṛtaṁ vicṛtam abhiṣṭaya induḥ siṣakty uṣasaṁ na sūryaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • yas - who
    • tasthau - stood
    • bhuvanāny - the worlds
    • amartyo - the immortal
    • viśvāni - all
    • somaḥ - the soma (a sacred drink)
    • pari - around
    • tāny - those
    • arṣati - he rules
    • kṛṇvan - creating
    • saṁcṛtaṁ - gathered
    • vicṛtam - dispersed
    • abhiṣṭaya - for the purpose
    • induḥ - the moon
    • siṣakty - has power
    • uṣasaṁ - of dawn
    • na - not
    • sūryaḥ - the sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence that governs the universe.
    • The phrase "who stood from the worlds" suggests a being or force that is foundational to existence.
    • The term "amartyo" indicates immortality, hinting at the eternal nature of this essence.
    • "Soma" represents a sacred drink, often associated with divine inspiration and immortality.
    • The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all things, as it mentions "creating" and "gathering" energies.
    • The reference to "induḥ" (the moon) and "uṣasaṁ" (dawn) signifies the cyclical nature of time and existence.
    • It suggests that the moon has a power that is distinct from the sun, indicating different aspects of divinity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the cycle of day and night can be seen as a representation of the eternal forces at play, similar to the "soma" that nourishes life.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be observed in ecosystems, where every element plays a role in maintaining balance.
    • In personal growth, the idea of gathering experiences and dispersing knowledge reflects the journey of learning and sharing wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of understanding the divine forces that govern our lives and the universe.
    • It encourages us to recognize our connection to all beings and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • In real life, this can inspire us to seek harmony with nature, appreciate the wisdom of experiences, and embrace the eternal aspects of our being.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the divine presence in all aspects of life, urging us to live with awareness and reverence.
