वयः सुपर्णा उप सेदुर इन्द्रं प्रियमेधा ऋषयो नाधमानाः |अप ध्वान्तम ऊर्णुहि पूर्धि चक्षुर मुमुग्ध्य अस्मान निधयेव बद्धान ||
vayaḥ suparṇā upa sedur indram priyamedhā ṛṣayo nādhamānāḥ | apa dhvāntam ūrṇuhi pūrdhi cakṣur mumugdhy a1smān nidhayeva baddhān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaḥ: our, of us
    • suparṇā: well-winged, auspicious
    • upa: near, close
    • sedur: to sit, to dwell
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • priyamedhā: with pleasing intellect
    • ṛṣayo: sages, seers
    • nādhamānāḥ: not falling, not declining
    • apa: away from
    • dhvāntam: darkness, ignorance
    • ūrṇuhi: to remove, to dispel
    • pūrdhi: fullness, completeness
    • cakṣur: eye, vision
    • mumugdhy: to free, to liberate
    • a1smān: us, our
    • nidhayeva: like a treasure, as if stored
    • baddhān: bound, tied
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the collective strength and wisdom of individuals (vayaḥ) who are auspicious (suparṇā) and are close (upa) to the divine (Indra).
    • It emphasizes the importance of having a pleasing intellect (priyamedhā) and the role of sages (ṛṣayo) who remain steadfast (nādhamānāḥ) in their pursuit of knowledge.
    • The mantra calls for the removal (ūrṇuhi) of darkness (dhvāntam) and ignorance, urging for a vision (cakṣur) that liberates (mumugdhy) us from the bonds (baddhān) that tie us down.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team that collaborates effectively and supports each other can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra. Their collective wisdom and positive mindset can lead to innovative solutions.
    • In educational settings, teachers and students who engage in meaningful discussions and share knowledge reflect the qualities of the sages mentioned in the mantra, fostering an environment of learning and growth.
    • On a personal level, individuals who strive to overcome ignorance and seek enlightenment through reading, meditation, or community service are practicing the principles of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and to support one another in overcoming ignorance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork, education, and personal development, reminding us of the importance of collective effort and the pursuit of higher understanding.
