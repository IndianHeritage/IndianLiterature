आ गावो अग्मन्न उता भद्रम अक्रन् सीदन्तु गोष्ठे रणयन्त्व अस्मे | प्रजावतिः पुरुरूपा इह स्युर इन्द्राय पूर्वीर उषसो दूहानाः ||
ā gāvo agmann uta bhadram akran sīdantu goṣṭhe raṇayantv asme | prajāvatīḥ pururūpā iha syur indrāya pūrvīr uṣaso duhānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā gāvo: O cows (or cattle)
    • agmann: may they come
    • uta: and
    • bhadram: auspiciousness
    • akran: may they produce
    • sīdantu: may they sit down
    • goṣṭhe: in the herd
    • raṇayantv: may they be active or lively
    • asme: for us
    • prajāvatīḥ: may they be fruitful or productive
    • pururūpā: manifold or diverse
    • iha: here
    • syur: may they be
    • indrāya: for Indra (the deity)
    • pūrvīr: from the ancients
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • duhānāḥ: milking or producing milk
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the blessings of cows, which are considered sacred and a symbol of wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • The phrase "ā gāvo agmann" expresses a desire for the cows to come to the speaker, indicating a longing for abundance and nourishment.
    • "Bhadram akran" signifies the hope that these cows will produce auspiciousness, which can be interpreted as a wish for good fortune and health.
    • The line "sīdantu goṣṭhe" emphasizes the importance of community and togetherness, as it calls for the cows to gather in the herd, symbolizing unity and cooperation.
    • "Raṇayantv asme" suggests that the cows should be lively and active, reflecting the idea that vitality and energy are essential for prosperity.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "prajāvatīḥ pururūpā iha syur," highlights the desire for the cows to be fruitful and diverse, which can be seen as a metaphor for the richness of life and the variety of blessings one can receive.
    • Finally, the invocation to Indra, "indrāya pūrvīr uṣaso duhānāḥ," connects the blessings of the cows to divine favor, suggesting that the prosperity derived from them is a gift from the gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the health and productivity of livestock directly impact the livelihood of farmers. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of the farmers' hopes for a bountiful harvest and healthy animals.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for sustainable practices in farming, where the well-being of animals and the environment is prioritized to ensure long-term prosperity.
    • The emphasis on community in the mantra can be related to cooperative farming practices, where farmers work together to support each other and share resources, leading to collective success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity, health, and community through the blessings of nature and divine favor.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of life, where the well-being of animals, humans, and the environment are all linked.
    • In contemporary life, the principles derived from this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards sustainable living, cooperation, and respect for nature, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
