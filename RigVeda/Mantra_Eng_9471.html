रण्वः संदृष्टौ पितुमाम् इव क्षयो भद्राऽ रुद्राणाम् मरुताम् उपस्तुतिः |गोभिः श्याम यशसो जनेषु आ सदाऽ देवास इळया सचेमहि ||
raṇvaḥ saṁdṛṣṭau pitumām̐ iva kṣayo bhadrā rudrāṇām marutām upastutiḥ | gobhiḥ ṣyāma yaśaso janeṣv ā sadā devāsa iḻayā sacemahi ||
  • raṇvaḥ saṁdṛṣṭau: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the sight of the battlefield" or "in the presence of conflict." It signifies the challenges and struggles one faces in life.
  • pitumām̐ iva kṣayo: This translates to "like the loss of a father." It evokes deep emotions related to loss and the impact it has on individuals and families.
  • bhadrā rudrāṇām marutām upastutiḥ: This means "a prayer of praise to the auspicious Rudras and Maruts." Rudras are often associated with storm and wind, while Maruts are the storm deities. This highlights the importance of seeking blessings from powerful forces in nature.
  • gobhiḥ ṣyāma yaśaso janeṣv: This can be understood as "with the strength of the cows, may we attain fame among people." Cows symbolize wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture, indicating the importance of community and sustenance.
  • ā sadā devāsa iḻayā sacemahi: This translates to "may we always be blessed by the gods." It emphasizes the need for divine support and guidance in our endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of facing life's battles with the support of divine forces and the community. It acknowledges the pain of loss while simultaneously seeking strength and blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as personal losses, conflicts, or societal struggles. It serves as a reminder to seek support from both the divine and the community, fostering resilience and hope.

  • Conclusion: The mantra teaches us the importance of recognizing our struggles, honoring our losses, and seeking blessings from higher powers. It encourages us to remain connected to our community and to draw strength from both spiritual and social resources in times of need.
