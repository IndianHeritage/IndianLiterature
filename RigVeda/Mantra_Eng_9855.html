एतं शंसम् इन्द्रास्मयुष त्वं कूचित सन्तं सहसावन्न अभिष्टये |सदा पाह्य अभिष्टये मेदतां वेदतां वसो ||
etaṁ śaṁsam indrāsmayuṣ ṭvaṁ kūcit santaṁ sahasāvann abhiṣṭaye | sadā pāhy abhiṣṭaye medatāṁ vedatā vaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etaṁ: this
    • śaṁsam: praise or commendation
    • indrāsmayuṣ: Indra, the king of gods, associated with strength and power
    • ṭvaṁ: you
    • kūcit: having been invoked or called upon
    • santaṁ: peaceful or tranquil
    • sahasāvann: with great strength or might
    • abhiṣṭaye: for the purpose of achieving or attaining
    • sadā: always
    • pāhy: protect
    • medatāṁ: of wisdom or intellect
    • vedatā: of knowledge or understanding
    • vaso: of the dwelling or abode
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking his protection and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and wisdom in our lives.
    • By calling upon Indra, the mantra suggests that one can attain peace and strength in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek strength through prayer or meditation, similar to invoking Indra for support.
    • In leadership roles, one may call upon the qualities of wisdom and strength to guide their decisions, akin to the blessings sought in this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often seek divine blessings for protection and success, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges and seek protection and wisdom.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can cultivate a sense of peace and strength, essential for personal and communal growth.
