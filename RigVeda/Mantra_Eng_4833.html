का ईं स्तवत कः पृणात को यजाते यद उग्रं इन मगवाऽविश्वहावेत् |पादाव इव प्रहरन्न अन्यं-अन्यं कृत्नोति पूर्वम् अपरं शचीभिः ||
ka īṁ stavat kaḥ pṛṇāt ko yajāte yad ugram in maghavā viśvahāvet | pādāv iva praharann anyam-anyaṁ kṛṇoti pūrvam aparaṁ śacībhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ka: who
    • īṁ: this (often used as a particle)
    • stavat: praises
    • kaḥ: who
    • pṛṇāt: fulfills or provides
    • ko: who
    • yajāte: sacrifices or worships
    • yad: that
    • ugram: fierce or powerful
    • in: in
    • maghavā: the mighty one (often referring to Indra)
    • viśvahāvet: who pervades everything
    • pādāv: feet
    • iva: like
    • praharann: striking or hitting
    • anyam-anyaṁ: one another
    • kṛṇoti: creates or makes
    • pūrvam: before
    • aparaṁ: after
    • śacībhiḥ: with the powerful ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the nature of divinity and the roles of individuals in the cosmic order.
    • It reflects on the idea of who truly praises, fulfills, and worships the powerful forces of the universe.
    • The mention of "ugram" suggests a recognition of the fierce and formidable aspects of divinity, particularly in the context of Indra, who is often seen as a warrior god.
    • The imagery of feet striking one another symbolizes the interconnectedness of all beings and their actions, emphasizing that every action has a reaction.
    • The phrase "with the powerful ones" indicates that collaboration and unity among strong individuals or forces are essential for achieving greater goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to teamwork where individuals with different strengths come together to achieve a common goal, much like how the powerful forces in nature work in harmony.
    • In community service, the idea of individuals fulfilling their roles to support one another reflects the essence of this mantra, where each person's contribution is vital for the success of the collective effort.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of mutual support and recognition of each other's strengths can lead to a more profound connection, akin to the divine interplay described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine forces at play in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, whether in praise, fulfillment, or sacrifice, contribute to the greater cosmic order.
    • In real life, applying the principles of collaboration, recognition of strength, and mutual support can lead to more harmonious and successful outcomes in various aspects of life.
