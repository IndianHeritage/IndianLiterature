हस्ते दधानो नृम्णा विश्वाण्य अमे देवान धाद गुहा निषीदन ||
haste dadhāno nṛmṇā viśvāny ame devān dhād guhā niṣīdan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • haste: in the hand
    • dadhāno: holding or possessing
    • nṛmṇā: of men or humanity
    • viśvāny: all or universal
    • ame: these
    • devān: the gods
    • dhād: bestow or grant
    • guhā: the cave or hidden place
    • niṣīdan: to sit or dwell
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between humanity and the gods.
    • It suggests that humans, by holding the universal truths in their hands, can invoke the blessings of the gods.
    • The mention of "guhā" indicates that there are hidden aspects of existence that can be accessed through spiritual practice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the act of prayer or meditation, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power.
    • For instance, during a challenging time, a person may recite this mantra while reflecting on their inner strength and the support of the divine.
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective blessings and unity among participants.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their potential to access spiritual wisdom and blessings.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, community gatherings, and personal spiritual practices, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
