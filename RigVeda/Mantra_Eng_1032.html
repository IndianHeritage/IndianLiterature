अग्निषोमा चेदिति तद् वीर्यं वां यद् अमुष्णीतम् अवसन् पाणिं गाः |अवातिरतम् बृहस्य शेषोऽविन्दतं ज्योतिर् एकं बहुभ्यः ||
agnīṣomā ceti tad vīryaṁ vāṁ yad amuṣṇītam avasam paṇiṁ gāḥ | avātiratam bṛsayasya śeṣo 'vindataṁ jyotir ekam bahubhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnīṣomā: Refers to the divine energies of fire (Agni) and the moon (Soma).
    • ceti: This can be interpreted as "that which is known" or "that which is understood."
    • tad: Means "that" or "that which."
    • vīryaṁ: Refers to "strength" or "power."
    • vāṁ: Means "of you" or "your."
    • yad: Means "what" or "that which."
    • amuṣṇītam: Refers to "that which has been achieved" or "that which has been attained."
    • avasam: Means "to be under control" or "to be subdued."
    • paṇiṁ: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity."
    • gāḥ: Means "cows," which symbolize wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
    • avātiratam: Refers to "having crossed over" or "having transcended."
    • bṛsayasya: Means "of the great" or "of the vast."
    • śeṣo: Refers to "the remainder" or "the residue."
    • 'vindataṁ: Means "to find" or "to attain."
    • jyotir: Refers to "light" or "radiance."
    • ekam: Means "one" or "the singular."
    • bahubhyaḥ: Means "to many" or "to various."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interplay between the energies of fire and the moon, symbolizing the balance of strength and nourishment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and attaining strength, which can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, career growth, and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The reference to wealth and cows signifies the importance of prosperity and sustenance in life, highlighting the need for both material and spiritual wealth.
    • The idea of transcending and finding light suggests a journey towards enlightenment and self-realization, which is applicable in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, one can relate this mantra to the journey of self-improvement, where understanding one's strengths and weaknesses leads to greater achievements.
    • In a professional context, the balance of fire (passion) and moon (calmness) can be seen in effective leadership, where a leader must be both assertive and nurturing.
    • In spiritual practices, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek enlightenment through meditation and self-reflection, aiming to find the "light" within themselves.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of balance between strength and nourishment, urging individuals to seek both material and spiritual wealth.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various life scenarios, encouraging personal growth, effective leadership, and spiritual enlightenment.
    • By understanding and embodying the principles within this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with greater purpose and clarity.
