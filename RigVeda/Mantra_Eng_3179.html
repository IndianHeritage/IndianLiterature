एभिर् नो अर्कैर् भवā नो अर्वाङ् स्वर्णा ज्योतिः |अग्नि विश्वेभिः सुमना अनीकैः ||
ebhir no arkair bhavā no arvāṅ sva1r ṇa jyotiḥ | agne viśvebhiḥ sumanā anīkaiḥ ||
  • ebhir no arkair: This phrase can be translated as "with these rays" or "with these lights." The term "arkair" refers to the rays of light or energy, often associated with the sun or divine illumination.
  • bhavā no arvāṅ: This means "may you become our protector" or "may you be our support." It expresses a plea for guidance and protection from a higher power.
  • sva1r ṇa jyotiḥ: This translates to "the light of the heavens" or "the celestial light." It signifies the divine light that brings knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • agne: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • viśvebhiḥ: This means "with all beings" or "with all the universe." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the collective nature of existence.
  • sumanā: This translates to "with good thoughts" or "with auspicious intentions." It highlights the importance of positive thinking and intentions in our actions.
  • anīkaiḥ: This means "with many" or "with a multitude." It suggests the idea of unity and collaboration among various entities or individuals.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer for divine guidance and protection, invoking the light of knowledge and wisdom from Agni, the fire god. It emphasizes the importance of positive intentions and the interconnectedness of all beings. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and support from the divine while fostering unity and collaboration in our lives.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life as a reminder to seek guidance and clarity in decision-making, especially during challenging times.
  • It encourages individuals to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions, which can lead to better outcomes in personal and professional relationships.
  • The invocation of Agni symbolizes the transformative power of fire, reminding us to embrace change and purification in our lives.
  • In a broader context, the mantra highlights the importance of community and collaboration, urging us to work together for common goals and mutual support.
