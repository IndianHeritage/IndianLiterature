आ यज्ञैर देव मार्त्य इत्त्था तव्यांसम ऊतये |अग्निं कृते स्वध्वरे पूरुर ईळीतावसे ||
ā yajñair deva martya itthā tavyāṁsam ūtaye | agniṁ kṛte svadhvare pūrur īḻītāvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • yajñair: sacrifices
    • deva: gods
    • martya: mortals
    • itthā: thus
    • tavyāṁsam: to be performed
    • ūtaye: for prosperity
    • agniṁ: Agni (the fire god)
    • kṛte: for the sake of
    • svadhvare: in the proper manner
    • pūrur: abundant
    • īḻītāvase: may we invoke
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra emphasizes the importance of performing sacrifices (yajñas) for the benefit of both the gods and mortals. It highlights the role of Agni, the fire god, as a mediator in these rituals, ensuring that they are conducted properly and lead to prosperity and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Community Gatherings: Just as communities come together for festivals and rituals, this mantra reflects the idea of collective effort for a common good, fostering unity and shared purpose.
    • Environmental Responsibility: The concept of yajña can be likened to sustainable practices today, where individuals and communities perform actions that benefit the environment, akin to a sacrifice for future generations.
    • Personal Growth: The mantra can also be interpreted in the context of personal sacrifices made for self-improvement, such as dedicating time to education or health, which ultimately leads to personal prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmonious coexistence between the divine and the mortal realms through the act of yajña. It teaches us that our actions, when performed with intention and respect, can lead to prosperity not just for ourselves but for the community and the environment. In modern life, this mantra encourages us to engage in practices that promote well-being, sustainability, and collective growth, reminding us that our contributions matter in the larger tapestry of life.
