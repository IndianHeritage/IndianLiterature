परि धामानि यानि ते त्वं सोमासि विश्वतः |पवमान ऋतुभिः कवे ||
pari dhāmāni yāni te tvaṁ somāsi viśvataḥ | pavamāna ṛtubhiḥ kave ||
  • Mantra: "pari dhāmāni yāni te tvaṁ somāsi viśvataḥ | pavamāna ṛtubhiḥ kave ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, surrounding
    • dhāmāni: the abodes, realms, or places
    • yāni: which
    • te: your
    • tvaṁ: you
    • somāsi: you are the essence of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • viśvataḥ: from all directions, universally
    • pavamāna: the purifying one, the one that cleanses
    • ṛtubhiḥ: with the seasons, with the rhythms of nature
    • kave: O poet, O seer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine essence that permeates all realms and directions.
    • The reference to "Soma" symbolizes the life-giving and nourishing aspects of nature, akin to how water and sunlight sustain life on Earth.
    • The phrase "pavamāna ṛtubhiḥ" emphasizes the connection between the divine and the natural cycles, suggesting that the divine is present in the changing seasons and rhythms of life.
    • In a real-life context, this can be seen in how we rely on nature's cycles for agriculture, health, and well-being. For instance, farmers plant crops according to the seasons, understanding that the divine essence of nature supports their growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and the natural world, reminding us to respect and honor the cycles of nature.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition that the divine is not separate from our daily lives; rather, it is present in every aspect of our existence.
    • In practical terms, this mantra encourages mindfulness and gratitude for the resources provided by nature, urging us to live in harmony with our environment.
    • By acknowledging the divine in the natural world, we can foster a deeper appreciation for life and promote sustainable practices that honor this connection.
