प्रोरोर मित्रावरुणा पृथिव्याः प्र दिव ऋष्वाद बृहतः सुदानु |स्पशो दधाथे ओषधिषु विक्ष्व ऋधग यतो अनिमिषं रक्षमाणा ||
proror mitrāvaruṇā pṛthivyāḥ pra diva ṛṣvād bṛhataḥ sudānū | spaśo dadhāthe oṣadhīṣu vikṣv ṛdhag yato animiṣaṁ rakṣamāṇā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • proror: of the two (Mitra and Varuna)
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Mitra and Varuna (the deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the Earth
    • pra: towards
    • dīva: the heavens
    • ṛṣvād: with strength
    • bṛhataḥ: the great
    • sudānū: the well-nourished
    • spaśo: of the senses
    • dadhāthe: you establish
    • oṣadhīṣu: in the plants
    • vikṣv: in the world
    • ṛdhag: with abundance
    • yato: from where
    • animiṣaṁ: without blinking
    • rakṣamāṇā: protecting
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize the principles of friendship, harmony, and cosmic order.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the Earth and the heavens, suggesting a balance between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The mention of "well-nourished" indicates the importance of sustenance and growth, both physically and spiritually.
    • By establishing a connection with the plants and the world, it highlights the significance of nature in our lives.
    • The phrase "without blinking" suggests vigilance and constant protection, indicating the need for awareness in our actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the principles of Mitra and Varuna can be seen in community-building efforts where individuals come together to support one another, fostering friendship and cooperation.
    • The mantra's emphasis on nature can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where protecting the Earth is seen as a sacred duty.
    • In personal growth, the idea of being "well-nourished" can be applied to mental and emotional health, where individuals seek balance and nourishment in their lives through relationships, education, and self-care.
    • The concept of vigilance can be reflected in mindfulness practices, where individuals are encouraged to be present and aware of their surroundings and actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of maintaining harmony within ourselves and with the world around us.
    • Its significance lies in promoting values of friendship, protection, and respect for nature, which are essential for a balanced and fulfilling life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to stronger communities, healthier relationships, and a deeper appreciation for the environment.
