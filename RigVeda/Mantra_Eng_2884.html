ये त्वाहिहत्ये मगवन्न अवर्धन ये शांबर हरिवो ये गविष्टौ |ये त्वा नूनम अनुमदन्ति विप्राः पिबेन्द्र सोमं सगणो मरुद्भिः ||
ye tvāhihatye maghavann avardhan ye śāmbare harivo ye gaviṣṭau | ye tvā nūnam anumadanti viprāḥ pibendra somaṁ sagaṇo marudbhiḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those who have invoked you, O Maghavan (Indra), who have praised you, who have sought you in the sky, those who are indeed the wise ones, they drink the soma, together with the Maruts."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • tvā: You
    • hihatye: Have invoked
    • maghavann: O Maghavan (Indra, the god of rain and thunder)
    • avardhan: Who have praised
    • śāmbare: In the sky
    • harivo: Who have sought
    • gaviṣṭau: In the cattle (or wealth)
    • nūnam: Indeed
    • anumadanti: The wise ones
    • viprāḥ: The learned or wise
    • pibendra: Drink, O Indra
    • somaṁ: Soma (a sacred drink)
    • sagaṇo: Together with
    • marudbhiḥ: The Maruts (storm deities)
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance in achieving prosperity and abundance.
    • The mention of "soma" refers to a ritual drink that was believed to confer immortality and divine insight, symbolizing the pursuit of higher knowledge and spiritual awakening.
    • The "Maruts" are storm deities, representing the forces of nature that aid in the growth and sustenance of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra during rituals is akin to seeking favorable weather conditions for crops, highlighting the reliance on divine forces for sustenance.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for collaboration and unity among individuals (the "wise ones") to achieve common goals, much like teamwork in a corporate environment.
    • The concept of "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in contemporary life, where individuals seek wisdom through education and experience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine powers for guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us the value of wisdom, collaboration, and the pursuit of knowledge, which are essential in both personal and professional realms.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that seeking higher assistance and working together can lead to prosperity and fulfillment in life.
