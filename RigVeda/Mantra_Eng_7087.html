निष्सिध्वरीरोषधीराप आस्तामिन्द्रावरुणा महिमानमाशत || या सिस्रतू रजसः पारि अध्वनो ययोः शत्रुर नकीर आदेव ओहते ||
niṣṣidhvarīr oṣadhīr āpa āstām indrāvaruṇā mahimānam āśata | yā sisratū rajasaḥ pāre adhvano yayoḥ śatrur nakir ādeva ohate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niṣṣidhvarīr: One who is free from obstacles or barriers.
    • oṣadhīr: Herbs or plants, often symbolizing healing and nourishment.
    • āpa: Waters, representing purity and life.
    • āstām: May they come or may they be present.
    • indrāvaruṇā: Indra and Varuna, deities representing power and cosmic order.
    • mahimānam: Greatness or glory.
    • āśata: May they grant or bestow.
    • yā: Which.
    • sisratū: Flowing or moving.
    • rajasaḥ: Of the dust or the material world.
    • pāre: Beyond.
    • adhvano: Paths or ways.
    • yayoḥ: Of which.
    • śatrur: Enemy or adversary.
    • nakir: Not or without.
    • ādeva: Like a god or divine.
    • ohate: To be invoked or called upon.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Varuna, asking for their blessings to remove obstacles and provide nourishment.
    • The mention of "oṣadhīr" and "āpa" emphasizes the importance of nature and water in sustaining life, suggesting a holistic approach to health and well-being.
    • The phrase "yā sisratū rajasaḥ pāre adhvano" indicates a desire to transcend the material world and its challenges, seeking a higher path free from adversities.
    • By calling upon the divine, the mantra suggests that one can find strength and support in facing life's difficulties.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to nature for solace, finding peace in gardens or parks, which can be seen as a modern interpretation of seeking the blessings of "oṣadhīr."
    • People may engage in meditation or prayer, invoking divine support to overcome challenges, reflecting the essence of calling upon Indra and Varuna for guidance.
    • In environmental movements, the emphasis on preserving water and plant life resonates with the mantra's call for the presence of "āpa" and "oṣadhīr," highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming life's obstacles and the need to respect and nurture the natural world.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, emphasizing the balance between spiritual and material aspects.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek harmony with nature and the divine, fostering resilience and well-being.
