उप मां मतिर्मास्थित वाश्रां पुत्रम् इव प्रियम् |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
upa mā matir asthita vāśrā putram iva priyam | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • upa mā matir asthita vāśrā putram iva priyam
    • upa: This word can be interpreted as "towards" or "up to," indicating a direction or intention.
    • mā: This translates to "not" or "do not," often used to express a negation or caution.
    • matir: This means "intellect" or "understanding," referring to the mental faculties that guide decision-making.
    • asthita: This word means "established" or "firm," suggesting stability and reliability in thought or action.
    • vāśrā: This can be interpreted as "belonging to the family" or "of the household," indicating a sense of belonging or connection.
    • putram: This translates to "son," symbolizing a cherished relationship, often representing love and care.
    • iva: This means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
    • priyam: This translates to "dear" or "beloved," emphasizing affection and attachment.
  • kuvit somasyāpām iti
    • kuvit: This can be interpreted as "what is good" or "what is beneficial," suggesting a search for positive outcomes.
    • somasyāpām: This phrase refers to "the waters of the soma," where "soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
    • iti: This means "thus" or "therefore," indicating a conclusion or summary of the preceding thoughts.

The mantra can be understood as a plea for the establishment of a firm and loving intellect, akin to the affection one has for a beloved son, while also seeking what is beneficial and nourishing, much like the waters of the sacred soma. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing our intellect and relationships with care and stability.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In parenting, it highlights the importance of nurturing a child's intellect and emotional well-being, ensuring they feel loved and secure.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to cultivate a stable and positive mindset, focusing on beneficial thoughts and actions.
  • In community building, it underscores the significance of fostering strong, loving relationships that contribute to the collective well-being.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a balanced approach to intellect and emotion, advocating for love, stability, and the pursuit of what is beneficial. It serves as a reminder to cherish our relationships and to cultivate a nurturing environment for growth, both personally and within our communities.
