अस्मे तद इन्द्रावरुणा वसु श्याद अस्मे रयिः मरुतः सर्ववीरः |अस्मान वरूत्रीः शरणैर अवन्त्व अस्मान होत्रा भारती दक्षिणाभिः ||
asme tad indrāvaruṇā vasu ṣyād asme rayir marutaḥ sarvavīraḥ | asmān varūtrīḥ śaraṇair avantv asmān hotrā bhāratī dakṣiṇābhiḥ ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a plea or request directed towards divine powers.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the divine qualities or blessings being sought.
  • indrāvaruṇā: This compound word combines "Indra," the king of gods, and "Varuna," the god of cosmic order, symbolizing the balance of power and righteousness.
  • vasu: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a desire for abundance in life.
  • ṣyād: Translates to "may it be" or "let it be," expressing hope or intention.
  • rayir: This word means "wealth" or "resources," emphasizing the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • marutaḥ: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm gods, representing strength and vitality.
  • sarvavīraḥ: Meaning "all heroes," it signifies the collective strength and valor of individuals.
  • asmān: Again means "us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the plea.
  • varūtrīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "protectors" or "guardians," indicating a desire for safety and security.
  • śaraṇair: Meaning "with protection," it emphasizes the need for divine safeguarding.
  • avantv: Translates to "may they protect," a direct request for divine intervention.
  • hotrā: Refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers sacrifices," symbolizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • bhāratī: This word can mean "the goddess of speech" or "knowledge," highlighting the significance of wisdom and communication.
  • dakṣiṇābhiḥ: Meaning "with offerings," it indicates the importance of gratitude and reciprocity in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra is a profound invocation for protection, prosperity, and strength from divine forces. It emphasizes the importance of community, the need for safeguarding, and the role of rituals in spiritual life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or when seeking guidance in personal or communal endeavors. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging us to seek blessings not just for ourselves but for the collective well-being. By reciting this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of unity and purpose, invoking the support of higher powers in their daily lives.
