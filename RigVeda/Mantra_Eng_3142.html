स्तीर्ये बर्हिषि समिधाने अग्ना ऊर्ध्वो अध्वर्युर जुजुषाणो अस्तात् | पर्य अग्निः पशुपा न होतā त्रिविष्ट्ये एति प्रदिव उराणः ||
stīrṇe barhiṣi samidhāne agnā ūrdhvo adhvaryur jujuṣāṇo asthāt | pary agniḥ paśupā na hotā triviṣṭy eti pradiva urāṇaḥ ||
  • stīrṇe barhiṣi samidhāne: This phrase refers to the act of placing offerings on a prepared altar or sacred space. In real life, this can be likened to setting up a space for important rituals or ceremonies, such as weddings or religious observances, where careful preparation is essential.
  • agnā ūrdhvo adhvaryur jujuṣāṇo asthāt: Here, 'Agnā' refers to the fire, which is a central element in Vedic rituals. 'Adhvaryur' is the priest who performs the rituals. This line signifies the invocation of fire and the presence of the priest, emphasizing the importance of guidance and leadership in any significant endeavor, much like how a project manager leads a team to success.
  • pary agniḥ paśupā na hotā: This part speaks to the omnipresence of fire ('Agni') and its role as a protector ('paśupā'). In modern life, this can be interpreted as the protective forces in our lives, such as family, friends, or community, who support us in our endeavors.
  • triviṣṭy eti pradiva urāṇaḥ: This line suggests that the fire reaches the heavens ('triviṣṭy') and connects the earthly realm with the divine. It can be seen as a metaphor for aspirations and goals that elevate us, similar to how individuals strive for higher education or personal growth to achieve their dreams.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of ritual, community, and aspiration. It highlights the importance of preparation, guidance, protection, and the connection between the earthly and the divine. In real life, these elements are applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, community support, and the pursuit of goals. By understanding and embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, much like the rituals that connect us to our traditions and values.
