अग्निर इव मन्यो त्विषितः सहस्व सेनानीर नः सहुरे हूता एधि |हत्वा य शत्रून विभजस्व वेद ओजो मिमानो विगृह नुदस्व ||
agnir iva manyo tviṣitaḥ sahasva senānīr naḥ sahure hūta edhi | hatvāya śatrūn vi bhajasva veda ojo mimāno vi mṛdho nudasva ||
  • Mantra: "agnir iva manyo tviṣitaḥ sahasva senānīr naḥ sahure hūta edhi | hatvāya śatrūn vi bhajasva veda ojo mimāno vi mṛdho nudasva ||"
  • Translation: "Like fire, O mighty one, you are our leader; come forth with your powerful army. Having defeated our enemies, may you bestow upon us strength and vigor, and may you drive away our foes."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Agnir: Fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • Iva: Like, indicating a comparison.
    • Manyo: Mighty or powerful, referring to strength and capability.
    • Tviṣitaḥ: Shining or radiant, suggesting brilliance and clarity.
    • Sahasva: Be strong or powerful, a call for strength.
    • Senānīr: Leader of the army, representing guidance and leadership.
    • Naḥ: Our, indicating possession or belonging.
    • Sahure: Come forth, a request for action.
    • Hūta: Invoked or called upon, suggesting a plea for assistance.
    • Edhi: May you bestow, a wish for blessings.
    • Hatvāya: Having defeated, indicating victory over adversaries.
    • Śatrūn: Enemies, referring to obstacles or challenges.
    • Vi: Indeed, emphasizing certainty.
    • Bhajasva: May you bestow or grant, a request for generosity.
    • Veda: Knowledge or wisdom, indicating understanding.
    • Ojo: Strength or energy, referring to vitality.
    • Mimāno: May you increase, a wish for growth.
    • Vi: Again, emphasizing certainty.
    • Mṛdho: From the earth, suggesting grounding or stability.
    • Nudasva: Drive away, a plea for removal of negativity.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, one might invoke the spirit of this mantra to seek strength and guidance in overcoming challenges and achieving success.
    • During personal struggles, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, individuals may reflect on the essence of this mantra to find inner strength and resilience.
    • In team sports, athletes often rally together, drawing on collective strength and leadership to overcome opponents, akin to the call for a powerful army in the mantra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength, leadership, and the drive to overcome obstacles. It serves as a reminder that, like fire, we can harness our inner power to face challenges head-on. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or competitive environments, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to seek strength, unity, and resilience. By embodying the qualities of the mantra, one can navigate through life's adversities with confidence and vigor.
