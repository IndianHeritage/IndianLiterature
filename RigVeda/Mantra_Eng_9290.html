अहम् भूवँ वसुनः पूर्व्यस् पतिर् अहम् धनानि सं जयामि शाश्वतः |मां हवन्ते पितरं न जन्तवोऽहम् दाशुषे विभजामि भोजन् ||
aham bhuvaṁ vasunaḥ pūrvyas patir ahaṁ dhanāni saṁ jayāmi śaśvataḥ | māṁ havante pitaraṁ na jantavo 'haṁ dāśuṣe vi bhajāmi bhojanam ||
  • Aham bhuvaṁ vasunaḥ: This phrase translates to "I am the one who exists in the realm of wealth." Here, "aham" means "I," "bhuvaṁ" refers to "existence" or "realm," and "vasunaḥ" signifies "wealth" or "prosperity." This can be related to individuals who strive for success and abundance in their lives, such as entrepreneurs who create businesses to generate wealth.
  • Pūrvyas patir: This means "the lord of the east." "Pūrvyas" refers to "the east," and "patir" means "lord" or "master." In real life, this can symbolize the importance of new beginnings and opportunities that come with the dawn, much like how a new day brings fresh chances for growth and success.
  • Aham dhanāni saṁ jayāmi: This translates to "I conquer wealth." "Dhanāni" means "wealth," and "saṁ jayāmi" means "I conquer." This can be seen in the context of individuals who overcome challenges to achieve financial stability, such as someone who works hard to pay off debts and build savings.
  • Śaśvataḥ: This word means "eternal" or "everlasting." It signifies the pursuit of lasting wealth and prosperity, which can be compared to investments that yield returns over time, such as real estate or stocks that appreciate in value.
  • Māṁ havante pitaraṁ: This translates to "the fathers invoke me." "Māṁ" means "me," "havante" refers to "invoke," and "pitaraṁ" means "fathers" or "ancestors." This can be related to the respect and reverence for one's ancestors, who often guide and inspire future generations in their pursuits.
  • Na jantavo 'haṁ: This means "I am not a creature." "Na" means "not," "jantavo" refers to "creatures," and "'haṁ" means "I." This can symbolize the idea of transcending limitations and striving for higher goals, much like individuals who break free from societal constraints to achieve their dreams.
  • Dāśuṣe vi bhajāmi bhojanam: This translates to "I partake in the offerings." "Dāśuṣe" means "offerings," "vi" means "in," "bhajāmi" means "I partake," and "bhojanam" means "food" or "nourishment." This can be seen in the context of community and sharing, where individuals come together to celebrate and support one another, much like a communal meal that fosters connection and unity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of self-identity, the pursuit of wealth, and the connection to one's roots and community. It signifies that true prosperity comes not just from material wealth but also from the relationships we build and the respect we hold for our ancestors. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for success while remaining grounded in their values and community, reminding them that their journey is not just about personal gain but also about contributing to the greater good.
