इन्द्रः सु पूषा वृषणा सुहस्ता दिवो न प्रीताः शशयं दुदुहरे |विश्वे यद अस्यां रणयन्त देवाः प्र वो 'त्र वसवः सुम्नम अश्यां ||
indraḥ su pūṣā vṛṣaṇā suhastā divo na prītāḥ śaśayaṁ duduhre | viśve yad asyāṁ raṇayanta devāḥ pra vo 'tra vasavaḥ sumnam aśyām ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • su pūṣā: Translates to "well-nourished" or "prosperous," indicating abundance and well-being.
  • vṛṣaṇā: Means "the one who brings rain," highlighting the importance of rain for agriculture and sustenance.
  • suhastā: Signifies "having good hands," suggesting the ability to create and nurture.
  • divo na prītāḥ: Translates to "not pleased with the heavens," indicating a disconnect or a need for attention from the divine.
  • śaśayaṁ duduhre: Means "they have drawn forth the milk," symbolizing the extraction of nourishment and resources.
  • viśve: Refers to "all" or "universal," emphasizing inclusivity and collective effort.
  • yad asyāṁ: Translates to "that which is in this," pointing to the present moment or the current situation.
  • raṇayanta: Means "those who are engaged in battle," symbolizing struggle or effort in achieving goals.
  • devāḥ: Refers to "the gods," representing divine forces or higher powers.
  • pra vo 'tra: Translates to "for you here," indicating a call to action or a request for divine intervention.
  • vasavaḥ: Refers to "the wealth-bringers," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
  • sumnam: Means "auspiciousness" or "good fortune," highlighting the desire for positive outcomes.
  • aśyām: Refers to "in this world," indicating the earthly realm and our experiences within it.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and the collective efforts of individuals in achieving prosperity and well-being. It highlights the need for nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and calls upon the divine to bless the present moment with abundance and auspiciousness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, agricultural practices, and personal endeavors where collaboration and divine guidance are sought for success and fulfillment. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking blessings for a prosperous life.
