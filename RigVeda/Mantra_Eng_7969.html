स वाजी रोचनादिवः पवमानो वि धावति |रक्षोहा वारम् अव्ययम् ||
sa vājī rocanā divaḥ pavamāno vi dhāvati | rakṣohā vāram avyayam ||
  • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a divine entity.
  • vājī - This term means "the one who is strong" or "the one who possesses strength." It can also refer to "the one who brings prosperity."
  • rocanā - This word means "brilliance" or "radiance," often associated with light and clarity.
  • divaḥ - This translates to "of the heavens" or "of the sky," indicating a celestial or divine context.
  • pavamāno - This term means "the one who is purifying" or "the one who brings purity," often associated with the wind or air.
  • vi - This word can mean "in" or "within," suggesting a sense of presence or location.
  • dhāvati - This means "runs" or "flows," indicating movement or action.
  • rakṣohā - This term translates to "protection" or "defense," often referring to safeguarding against negative forces.
  • vāram - This means "barrier" or "shield," indicating a protective measure.
  • avyayam - This translates to "imperishable" or "eternal," suggesting something that cannot be destroyed or diminished.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to a divine force that embodies strength, brilliance, and purity, which moves through the heavens and offers protection against negative influences. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in maintaining purity and strength in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of strength and brilliance, individuals can strive to cultivate their inner strength and clarity in their personal and professional lives. For example, someone facing challenges at work can meditate on this mantra to invoke a sense of resilience and focus.
  • Protection from Negativity: The protective aspect of the mantra can be relevant in situations where one feels overwhelmed by negative influences, whether from people or circumstances. Practicing this mantra can serve as a reminder to create boundaries and seek positive environments.
  • Spiritual Practices: In spiritual rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke divine protection and purity, especially during meditation or prayer sessions, enhancing the spiritual atmosphere.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength and purity that can be sought from the divine. It encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength, seek clarity, and protect themselves from negativity, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to spiritual practices.
