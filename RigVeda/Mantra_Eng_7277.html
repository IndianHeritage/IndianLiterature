अग्निं सूनं सहसो जातवेदसं दानाय वार्याणाम् |द्वितायो भूद अमृतो मर्त्येषु आ होत mandratमो विशि ||
agniṁ sūnuṁ sahaso jātavedasaṁ dānāya vāryāṇām | dvitā yo bhūd amṛto martyeṣv ā hotā mandratamo viśi ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Sūnuṁ: Means "son" or "descendant," indicating a lineage or connection.
  • Sahaso: Translates to "with strength" or "powerful," emphasizing the vigor associated with Agni.
  • Jātavedasaṁ: Refers to the one who knows all births, a title for Agni, highlighting his role as a mediator between the divine and human realms.
  • Dānāya: Means "for the purpose of giving" or "offering," indicating the act of sacrifice or sharing.
  • Vāryāṇām: Refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," suggesting the importance of strength in offerings.
  • Dvitā: Means "second" or "twice," possibly indicating a dual aspect or a second opportunity.
  • Yo: Translates to "who," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • Bhūd: Means "to be" or "exist," indicating the essence of being.
  • Amṛto: Refers to "immortal," symbolizing the eternal nature of the soul or spirit.
  • Martyeṣv: Means "among mortals," indicating the human condition.
  • Ā hotā: Translates to "the one who invokes" or "the priest," referring to the role of the one performing the ritual.
  • Mandratamo: Means "the most gentle" or "the softest," suggesting a compassionate approach.
  • Viśi: Refers to "distinguished" or "special," indicating a unique quality or characteristic.

This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing his role as a powerful and transformative force in the universe. It highlights the importance of offerings and sacrifices, suggesting that through these acts, one can connect with the divine and attain immortality. The mention of "mortals" indicates the human experience and the need for guidance and support from the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings and strength, emphasizing the importance of intention and offering in spiritual practices.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to embrace transformation and purification in their lives, encouraging them to let go of negativity and embrace positive change.
  • Community and Sharing: The concept of giving and sharing in the mantra can be reflected in community service and charitable acts, promoting a sense of unity and support among individuals.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in our lives, symbolizing the transformative power of fire, the importance of offerings, and the connection between the divine and human realms. It encourages individuals to seek strength, compassion, and immortality through their actions and intentions.
