रुद्रस्य ये मीळ्हुषः सन्ति पुत्रा यांश चो नु दाधृविर भरध्यै |विदे हि माता महो मही षा सेत पृष्णिः सुभवे3 गर्भम आधात ||
rudrasya ye mīḻhuṣaḥ santi putrā yām̐ś co nu dādhṛvir bharadhyai | vide hi mātā maho mahī ṣā set pṛśniḥ subhve3 garbham ādhāt ||
  • Rudrasya: Refers to Rudra, a deity associated with storms, wind, and the hunt, often seen as a fierce aspect of Shiva.
  • ye mīḻhuṣaḥ: Translates to "those who are born" or "those who are produced," indicating the offspring or creations of Rudra.
  • santi putrā: Means "there are sons," emphasizing the progeny or descendants of Rudra.
  • yām̐ś co nu dādhṛvir bharadhyai: This phrase can be interpreted as "who hold the strength and power," suggesting that these offspring possess significant qualities or abilities.
  • vide hi mātā: Translates to "the mother who is great," indicating a powerful maternal figure, possibly referring to nature or the earth.
  • maho mahī: Means "great earth," emphasizing the vastness and importance of the earth in sustaining life.
  • ṣā set pṛśniḥ: Refers to "the one who is the source of nourishment," indicating the nurturing aspect of the earth or nature.
  • subhve3 garbham ādhāt: Translates to "who holds the good womb," suggesting the idea of creation and the nurturing of life within the womb of the earth or nature.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the connection between divine forces, nature, and the cycle of life. It emphasizes the importance of progeny, strength, and the nurturing aspects of the earth.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of our relationship with nature and the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment. It encourages us to recognize the strength within ourselves and our communities, as well as the vital role of motherhood and nurturing in society.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reflection on the interconnectedness of life, the divine, and nature. It encourages us to honor our roots, the strength we inherit, and the nurturing aspects of our existence, reminding us that we are all part of a larger cycle of life.
