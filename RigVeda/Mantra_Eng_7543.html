भद्रम्-भद्रं न आ भरेषम् ऊर्जं शतक्रतो |यद इन्द्र मृळयासि नः ||
bhadram-bhadraṁ na ā bhareṣam ūrjaṁ śatakrato | yad indra mṛḻayāsi naḥ ||
  • Translation: "May we receive auspiciousness; may we not be burdened with any obstacles. O Indra, grant us strength and protection."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Bhadram: Auspiciousness or well-being.
    • Bhadraṁ: A repetition emphasizing the importance of auspiciousness.
    • Na: Not.
    • Ā bhareṣam: To be burdened or weighed down.
    • Ūrjaṁ: Strength or energy.
    • Śatakrato: A name for Indra, meaning "the one with a hundred powers."
    • Yad: That which.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing power and strength.
    • Mṛḻayāsi: To grant or bestow.
    • Naḥ: Us or our.

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for auspiciousness and strength, invoking the divine energy of Indra, who is often associated with rain, storms, and the power of nature.
    • In real life, this mantra can be recited during challenging times, such as starting a new venture, facing personal difficulties, or seeking protection from negative influences.
    • For example, a student preparing for exams might recite this mantra to seek clarity and strength, hoping to overcome the stress and obstacles in their path.

  • Significance:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking positivity and strength in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that while challenges may arise, we can call upon higher powers for support and guidance.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to cultivate a mindset of resilience and optimism, fostering a sense of community and shared strength.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra is applicable in various life scenarios, from personal struggles to collective challenges faced by communities.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, individuals can find the courage to face obstacles and strive for auspicious outcomes in their endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us the value of seeking strength and positivity, both for ourselves and for those around us.
