त इन निञ्यं हृदयस्य प्रकेतैः सहस्रवल्शम् अभि सं चरन्ति |यमेना ततम् परिधिं वयन्तो 'प्सरसा उप सेदुर वसिष्ठाः ||
ta in niṇyaṁ hṛdayasya praketaiḥ sahasravalśam abhi saṁ caranti | yamena tatam paridhiṁ vayanto 'psarasa upa sedur vasiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • ta in niṇyaṁ hṛdayasya praketaiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who dwell in the heart with their essence." It suggests a deep connection to one's inner self and emotions. In real life, this can be related to the importance of self-awareness and emotional intelligence, where understanding one's feelings can lead to better relationships and decision-making.
  • sahasravalśam abhi saṁ caranti: This translates to "they move about with a thousand strengths." This can be seen as a metaphor for resilience and the ability to adapt to various challenges. For example, individuals who face adversity, such as overcoming illness or personal loss, often find inner strength and support from their community, embodying this idea of moving with strength.
  • yamena tatam paridhiṁ: This means "with Yama, they spread the boundary." Yama represents moral order and discipline. In practical terms, this can relate to setting personal boundaries in life, such as maintaining a work-life balance or establishing healthy relationships. It emphasizes the importance of discipline in achieving goals.
  • vayanto 'psarasa upa sedur vasiṣṭhāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the wise ones who dwell in the waters of wisdom." This suggests that true knowledge and wisdom come from deep contemplation and understanding. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of mindfulness or meditation, where individuals seek clarity and insight through reflection.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of self-awareness, resilience, discipline, and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, these qualities are essential for personal growth and navigating life's challenges. By cultivating these attributes, individuals can lead more fulfilling lives, make informed decisions, and contribute positively to their communities. The mantra serves as a reminder to connect with one's inner self, harness strength, set boundaries, and seek wisdom in all endeavors.
