यन् न इन्द्रो जुजुषे यच च वष्टि तन् नो महान् करोति शुष्म्य ā चित् |ब्रह्म स्तोमं मगवाऽ सोमं उक्था यो अश्मानं शवसां बिभ्रद एति ||
yan na indro jujuṣe yac ca vaṣṭi tan no mahān karati śuṣmy ā cit | brahma stomam maghavā somam ukthā yo aśmānaṁ śavasā bibhrad eti ||
  • yan na indro jujuṣe: This phrase can be translated as "what Indra desires." Indra, in Vedic literature, is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the aspirations and goals that individuals strive for, whether in personal or professional realms.
  • yac ca vaṣṭi: This means "and what he bestows." It signifies the blessings or gifts that come from the divine or higher powers. In everyday life, this can be seen in the form of opportunities, support from others, or unexpected good fortune that helps us achieve our goals.
  • tan no mahān karati: Translated as "that great one makes for us." This refers to the idea that the divine or the universe facilitates our endeavors. For example, when we work hard towards a goal, we often find that circumstances align in our favor, which can be interpreted as divine support.
  • šuṣmy ā cit: This phrase can be understood as "may we be aware of the subtle." It emphasizes the importance of being mindful and perceptive to the nuances of life. In practical terms, this could relate to being aware of our surroundings, the feelings of others, and the opportunities that may not be immediately obvious.
  • brahma stomam maghavā somam ukthā: This translates to "the great hymn of praise, the powerful one, the soma." Here, "soma" refers to a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. In real life, this can be seen as the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, which nourishes our spirit and intellect.
  • yo aśmānaṁ śavasā bibhrad eti: This means "who carries the stones of the dead." This phrase can be interpreted as a metaphor for overcoming obstacles and challenges in life. Just as stones can represent burdens, this line encourages us to face and carry our challenges with strength and resilience.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in our endeavors, being mindful of our surroundings, and recognizing the blessings we receive. Its significance lies in the reminder that while we strive for our goals, we are not alone; there is a greater force that aids us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to remain hopeful and resilient in the face of challenges, to appreciate the opportunities that come their way, and to cultivate a sense of awareness and gratitude in their daily lives.
