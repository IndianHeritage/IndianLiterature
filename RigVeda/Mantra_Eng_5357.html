मन्त्र: न दुर्गा इन्द्र श्नथिह्य अमित्राम् अभि ये नो मर्तासो अमन्ति |आरे तं शंसं कृतुहि निनित्सोर आ नो भर सम्भरणं वसूनाम् ||
ni durga indra śnathihy amitrām̐ abhi ye no martāso amanti | āre taṁ śaṁsaṁ kṛṇuhi ninitsor ā no bhara sambharaṇaṁ vasūnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • durga: difficult, hard
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • śnathihy: may he protect
    • amitrām̐: from enemies
    • abhi: towards
    • ye: those who
    • no: us
    • martāso: mortals
    • amanti: who are in danger
    • āre: O
    • taṁ: that
    • śaṁsaṁ: auspiciousness
    • kṛṇuhi: may you create
    • ninitsor: of the enemies
    • ā: for
    • no: us
    • bhara: bring
    • sambharaṇaṁ: abundance
    • vasūnām: of wealth
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the protection of Indra, asking him to shield us from difficulties and enemies.
    • It emphasizes the need for auspiciousness and abundance in our lives, particularly in times of danger.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek divine protection and guidance, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • Communities facing natural disasters may recite such mantras to seek safety and abundance in recovery efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in overcoming challenges and ensuring prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal hardships, community crises, or even in daily prayers for protection and abundance.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and resilience, reinforcing their belief in the support of higher powers in their lives.
