आ त्वा विप्राः अचुच्यवुः सुतसोमा अभि प्रयः | बृहद्भा बिभ्रतो हविः अग्ने मर्ताय दाशुषे ||
ā tvā viprā acucyavuḥ sutasomā abhi prayaḥ | bṛhad bhā bibhrato havir agne martāya dāśuṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: to, towards
    • tvā: you
    • viprā: the wise, the learned
    • acucyavuḥ: have called upon
    • sutasomā: the Soma (a sacred drink) that is well-prepared
    • abhi: towards, for
    • prayaḥ: effort, endeavor
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • bhā: light, brilliance
    • bibhrato: holding, possessing
    • havir: offering, oblation
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • martāya: for the mortal, for the human
    • dāśuṣe: for the one who gives, for the benefactor
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of "viprā" signifies the respect for wisdom and knowledge, suggesting that the learned individuals are calling upon Agni for blessings.
    • The term "sutasomā" refers to the sacred drink Soma, which symbolizes vitality and divine inspiration, indicating that the offerings made are pure and well-prepared.
    • "Bṛhad bhā" emphasizes the vastness of the light and knowledge that Agni embodies, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just for the individual but for the community as a whole.
    • The phrase "martāya dāśuṣe" highlights the importance of generosity and support for humanity, indicating that the offerings are made for the benefit of all mortals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often light a fire and offer prayers, similar to how Agni is invoked in this mantra. This act symbolizes unity and the sharing of blessings.
    • In modern times, the concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and well-being through education and healthy living, where individuals strive to prepare themselves for greater contributions to society.
    • When leaders or influential figures in society make efforts to support the less fortunate, they embody the spirit of "dāśuṣe," acting as benefactors for the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine blessings for wisdom, community well-being, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us that our efforts should not only be for personal gain but also for the upliftment of society as a whole.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek knowledge, share our resources, and support one another, fostering a sense of community and collective growth.
