नूनं सा ते प्रति वरं जरीत्रे दूहीयद इन्द्र दक्षिणा मगहोनी |शिक्षा स्तोत्रिभ्यो माती धग भगो नो बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ jaritre duhīyad indra dakṣiṇā maghonī | śikṣā stotṛbhyo māti dhag bhago no bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, that is your best offering." It emphasizes the importance of giving one's best in any endeavor, whether it be in personal relationships, work, or spiritual practices.
  • jaritre duhīyad indra: This part refers to "the one who is strong and powerful, Indra." Indra is often seen as a symbol of strength and leadership. In real life, this can relate to individuals who take charge in difficult situations, demonstrating leadership qualities.
  • dakṣiṇā maghonī: This translates to "the generous offering." It signifies the importance of generosity and sharing one's resources with others. For example, in community service, individuals who donate time or money to help those in need embody this principle.
  • śikṣā stotṛbhyo: This means "the teachings to the worshippers." It highlights the value of education and learning from those who guide us. In modern life, this can be seen in mentorship programs where experienced individuals help guide the younger generation.
  • māti dhag bhago: This phrase can be interpreted as "may our intellect and wealth be blessed." It suggests a desire for wisdom and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and financial stability, which are essential for personal growth and success.
  • no bṛhad vadema: This translates to "let us speak of the great." It encourages discussions about significant and impactful ideas. In everyday life, this can be applied in forums or discussions that aim to address important social issues, fostering a culture of awareness and change.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This means "in the assembly of the brave." It signifies the importance of courage and unity in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in movements where individuals come together to fight for a common cause, demonstrating bravery and solidarity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of offering one's best, demonstrating strength and leadership, practicing generosity, valuing education, seeking wisdom and prosperity, discussing impactful ideas, and fostering courage and unity. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, leadership roles, educational settings, and social movements. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same.
