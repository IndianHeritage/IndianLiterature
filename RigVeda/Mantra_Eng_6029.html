मन्त्र: विद्मा ह्यस्य वीरस्य भूरिदावरिं सुमतिम् |त्रिषु जातस्य मनांसि ||
vidmā hy asya vīrasya bhūridāvarīṁ sumatim | triṣu jātasya manāṁsi ||
  • vidmā: This word translates to "we know" or "we are aware." It signifies knowledge and understanding.
  • hy: This is a conjunction meaning "for" or "indeed," emphasizing the importance of the knowledge being discussed.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of him," referring to the subject of the mantra, which is a person or a being of great valor.
  • vīrasya: This translates to "of the hero" or "of the brave one," indicating a person who possesses courage and strength.
  • bhūridāvarīṁ: This term means "of great wisdom" or "of abundant intellect," suggesting that the hero is not only brave but also wise.
  • sumatim: This translates to "good thought" or "auspicious understanding," indicating a positive mindset and clarity of thought.
  • triṣu: This means "in three," referring to the three realms or aspects of existence.
  • jātasya: This translates to "of the born" or "of the created," indicating the manifestation of thoughts or ideas.
  • manāṁsi: This means "minds" or "thoughts," referring to the mental faculties and consciousness.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for wisdom and understanding in the context of bravery and heroism. It emphasizes the importance of having a courageous heart coupled with a wise mind, suggesting that true valor is not just about physical strength but also about mental clarity and good intentions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: A leader who embodies the qualities of bravery and wisdom can inspire their team to achieve great things. For example, a manager who takes bold decisions while considering the well-being of their employees exemplifies this mantra.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, approaching the issue with courage and a clear mind can lead to effective resolutions. For instance, a mediator who bravely addresses the concerns of both parties while maintaining a fair perspective can help resolve disputes amicably.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals striving for self-improvement can reflect on this mantra by cultivating both courage to face challenges and the wisdom to make informed decisions. For example, someone overcoming personal fears while seeking knowledge and understanding in their field of interest.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between bravery and wisdom. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding while being courageous in their actions. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from leadership and conflict resolution to personal growth, highlighting the importance of a well-rounded character in achieving success and harmony.
