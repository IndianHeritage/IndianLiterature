इन्द्रो मह्ना महतो अर्णवस्य व्रतामिनाद अंगिरोभिर गृणानः |पुरूणि चिन् नि ततान राजांसी दाधार यो धरणं सत्यताता ||
indro mahnā mahato arṇavasya vratāminād aṅgirobhir gṛṇānaḥ | purūṇi cin ni tatānā rajāṁsi dādhāra yo dharuṇaṁ satyatātā ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Mahnā: Means 'great' or 'mighty', emphasizing the vastness of Indra's power.
  • Mahato: Translates to 'of the great', indicating the significance of the subject being discussed.
  • Arṇavasya: Refers to the 'ocean' or 'vastness', symbolizing the depth and breadth of existence.
  • Vratāminād: Implies 'by the vow' or 'commitment', suggesting the importance of dedication and resolve.
  • Aṅgirobhir: Refers to the 'Angiras', a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge.
  • Gṛṇānaḥ: Means 'singing' or 'praising', indicating the act of worship or reverence.
  • Purūṇi: Translates to 'many' or 'various', suggesting diversity in experiences or aspects of life.
  • Cin: Means 'to gather' or 'to collect', indicating the act of bringing together knowledge or experiences.
  • Ni tatānā: Refers to 'he who has spread', suggesting the dissemination of knowledge or power.
  • Rajāṁsi: Means 'kingdoms' or 'realms', indicating the various domains of existence.
  • Dādhāra: Translates to 'he who supports' or 'holds', emphasizing the role of a leader or protector.
  • Yo: Means 'who', referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • Dharuṇaṁ: Refers to 'truth' or 'reality', indicating the importance of authenticity.
  • Satyatātā: Means 'truthfulness' or 'steadfastness', emphasizing the value of honesty and integrity.

This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, strength, and the pursuit of truth. It highlights the role of a leader (Indra) who, through commitment and wisdom, supports and nurtures various realms of existence. The invocation of the Angiras sages signifies the importance of knowledge and guidance in leadership.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership in Organizations: Leaders in businesses can embody the qualities of Indra by being strong, wise, and committed to their teams, fostering an environment of trust and support.
  • Community Building: Community leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to gather diverse perspectives and support the collective well-being of their members.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on the values of truthfulness and commitment in their personal lives, striving to be authentic and dedicated in their pursuits.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the qualities that define effective leadership and the importance of truth and commitment in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to strive for greatness while supporting and uplifting those around them.
