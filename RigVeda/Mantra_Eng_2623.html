इमं नो यज्ञं अमृतेषु धेहिमाः हव्याः जातवेदो जुषस्व |स्टोकानाम अग्नि मेदसो घृतस्य होतः प्राशान प्रथमो निषद्य ||
imaṁ no yajñam amṛteṣu dhehīmā havyā jātavedo juṣasva | stokānām agne medaso ghṛtasya hotaḥ prāśāna prathamo niṣadya ||
  • Translation: "May we offer this sacrifice to the Immortal; O Jātavedas, enjoy our offerings. You, O Agni, the first to be invoked, are the one who brings forth the essence of the ghee (clarified butter) from the offerings."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • no: our
    • yajñam: sacrifice
    • amṛteṣu: to the Immortal
    • dhehīmā: may we offer
    • havyā: offerings
    • jātavedo: O Jātavedas (a name for Agni)
    • juṣasva: enjoy
    • stokānām: of the small (or few)
    • agne: O Agni
    • medaso: essence
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee
    • hotaḥ: the one who brings forth
    • prāśāna: the first
    • prathamo: first
    • niṣadya: seated
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine. In Vedic rituals, Agni is invoked to carry offerings to the gods.
    • The phrase "may we offer this sacrifice to the Immortal" emphasizes the importance of connecting with the eternal and the divine through our actions and offerings.
    • The mention of "ghee" signifies purity and nourishment, as ghee is often used in Hindu rituals and is considered sacred.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to engage in acts of service and sacrifice, whether through community service, charitable acts, or simply being kind and generous to others.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • The mantra highlights the importance of intention behind our actions. Just as offerings are made with care and devotion, our daily actions should be performed with mindfulness and purpose.
    • In a modern context, this can translate to being conscious of our contributions to society, whether through environmental stewardship, social justice, or personal relationships.
    • By invoking the spirit of Agni, we are reminded to ignite the fire of passion and commitment in our endeavors, ensuring that our efforts are directed towards the greater good.
