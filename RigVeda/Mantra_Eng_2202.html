नूनं सा ते प्रति वरं जरीत्रे दूहीयद इन्द्र दक्षिणा मगहोनी |शिक्षा स्तोत्रिभ्यो माती धग भगो नो बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ jaritre duhīyad indra dakṣiṇā maghonī | śikṣā stotṛbhyo māti dhag bhago no bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, that is your excellent gift." It suggests a recognition of the divine blessings that one receives.
  • jaritre duhīyad indra: This translates to "O Indra, the one who supports and nourishes." Indra is often seen as a deity who provides strength and sustenance, akin to how a leader supports their community.
  • dakṣiṇā maghonī: This means "the generous offering." It emphasizes the importance of giving and sharing resources, similar to how communities come together to support those in need.
  • śikṣā stotṛbhyo: This can be understood as "the teachings to the worshippers." It highlights the value of education and guidance in spiritual and practical matters.
  • māti dhag bhago: This translates to "may our intellect be bright." It signifies the importance of wisdom and clarity in decision-making, much like how critical thinking is essential in everyday life.
  • no bṛhad vadema: This means "let us speak of the great." It encourages discussions about significant and impactful ideas, similar to how communities engage in dialogues about progress and innovation.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This translates to "in the assembly of the brave." It refers to the gathering of courageous individuals who strive for a common goal, akin to teamwork in various fields such as sports or community service.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, community support, education, wisdom, and collective courage. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, leadership, and personal development. By recognizing the gifts we receive, supporting one another, valuing education, and fostering courage, we can create a more harmonious and progressive society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the strength found in unity and shared purpose.
