इमा इन्द्रं वरुणं मे मनिषा अग्मन्न उप द्रविणं इच्छमानाः |उपेम अस्तुर जोष्टार इव वस्वो रघवीर इव श्रवसो भिक्षमानाः ||
imā indraṁ varuṇam me manīṣā agmann upa draviṇam icchamānāḥ | upem asthur joṣṭāra iva vasvo raghvīr iva śravaso bhikṣamāṇāḥ ||
  • imā - This word translates to "these" or "this." It indicates a focus on the subjects being addressed.
  • indraṁ - Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • varuṇam - Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and ethical aspects of the universe.
  • me - Means "my," indicating a personal connection or request.
  • manīṣā - Translates to "intellect" or "thought," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in decision-making.
  • agmann - Means "to be born" or "to arise," suggesting the emergence of new ideas or actions.
  • upa - Means "towards" or "near," indicating a direction or intention.
  • draviṇam - Refers to "wealth" or "resources," highlighting the material aspects of life.
  • icchamānāḥ - Means "desiring" or "wishing," indicating a longing for something.
  • upem - Translates to "we shall attain" or "we shall reach," suggesting a goal or aspiration.
  • asthur - Means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," emphasizing resilience.
  • joṣṭāra - Refers to "those who are strong" or "warriors," symbolizing courage and valor.
  • iva - Means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • vasvo - Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance.
  • raghvīr - Refers to "the brave" or "the heroic," symbolizing valor and strength.
  • iva - Again, means "like" or "as."
  • śravaso - Refers to "fame" or "glory," indicating the pursuit of recognition.
  • bhikṣamāṇāḥ - Means "seeking" or "requesting," indicating a desire for something.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation, where the speaker is seeking the blessings of Indra and Varuna for wisdom, strength, and resources. The desire for wealth and prosperity is coupled with a call for courage and recognition, suggesting that one should not only seek material gains but also strive for moral integrity and valor.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with wisdom and integrity.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on intellect and the desire for wealth can motivate individuals to pursue education and self-improvement, aiming for both material success and ethical living.
  • Community Building: The call for strength and courage can inspire collective efforts in communities to work towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance in the pursuit of wisdom, strength, and prosperity. It encourages individuals to balance their material desires with ethical considerations and to strive for personal and communal growth. In a world where challenges abound, invoking the qualities of Indra and Varuna can serve as a reminder to act with integrity and courage in all endeavors.
