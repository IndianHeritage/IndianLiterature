दिवश्चिदस्य वरिमाविपप्रथ इन्द्रं न मह्ना पृथिवी चन प्रतिः |भीमस्तुविष्माञ्चर्चणिभ्य आत्मपः शिशीते वज्रं तेजसे न वंसगः ||
divaś cid asya varimā vi papratha indraṁ na mahnā pṛthivī cana prati | bhīmas tuviṣmāñ carṣaṇibhya ātapaḥ śiśīte vajraṁ tejase na vaṁsagaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divaś: Of the heavens
    • Cid: Indeed
    • Asya: Of this
    • Varimā: Abundance or wealth
    • Vi: Apart or separately
    • Papratha: Spread or distributed
    • Indraṁ: To Indra (the king of gods)
    • Na: Not
    • Mahnā: By great power
    • Pṛthivī: Earth
    • Cana: Also
    • Prati: Towards
    • Bhīmas: Mighty or powerful
    • Tuviṣmāñ: In the great strength
    • Carṣaṇibhya: From the great expanse
    • Ātapaḥ: Heat or energy
    • Śiśīte: Shines or radiates
    • Vajraṁ: Thunderbolt or strength
    • Tejase: With brilliance
    • Na: Not
    • Vaṁsagaḥ: Of the lineage or descendants
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine abundance that is spread from the heavens to the earth.
    • It emphasizes the power of Indra, who is often associated with strength and rain, suggesting that true wealth comes from divine sources.
    • The mention of "bhīmas" and "tuviṣmāñ" highlights the importance of strength and might in achieving one's goals.
    • The imagery of "vajraṁ" signifies the indomitable force that can overcome obstacles, akin to how a thunderbolt can break through the sky.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain, symbolizing their reliance on divine abundance for a fruitful harvest.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek strength and resilience to overcome challenges, drawing inspiration from the metaphor of the thunderbolt.
    • In leadership, the qualities of Indra can be mirrored in leaders who inspire and empower their teams, fostering a sense of abundance and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine abundance and human strength.
    • It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses strength, resilience, and the support of the divine.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by seeking strength in adversity, recognizing the abundance around us, and understanding that our efforts are often supported by greater forces.
