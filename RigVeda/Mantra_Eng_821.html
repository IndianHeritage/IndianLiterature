संजानानां उप सीदन्न अभिज्ञु पत्निवन्तो नमस्यं नमस्यन् |रिरिक्वांसस तन््वः कृत्वता स्वाह सखा सख्युर निमिषि रक्षमाणाः ||
saṁjānānā upa sīdann abhijñu patnīvanto namasyaṁ namasyan | ririkvāṁsas tanvaḥ kṛṇvata svāḥ sakhā sakhyur nimiṣi rakṣamāṇāḥ ||
  • saṁjānānā: This term can be translated as "understanding" or "perceiving." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and comprehension in our interactions.
  • upa sīdann: This phrase means "coming together" or "approaching." It signifies the act of gathering or uniting, which is essential in building relationships.
  • abhijñu: This word refers to someone who is "knowledgeable" or "wise." It highlights the value of wisdom in guiding our actions and decisions.
  • patnīvanto: This term translates to "those who have partners" or "spouses." It reflects the significance of companionship and partnership in life.
  • namasyaṁ namasyan: This phrase means "salutations" or "respectful greetings." It underscores the importance of showing respect and honor to others.
  • ririkvāṁsas: This word can be interpreted as "those who are aware" or "those who are vigilant." It suggests the need for attentiveness in our relationships and surroundings.
  • tanvaḥ: This term means "bodies" or "forms." It indicates the physical aspect of existence and the importance of taking care of our physical selves.
  • kṛṇvata: This word translates to "creating" or "making." It emphasizes the active role we play in shaping our lives and relationships.
  • svāḥ: This is often used as an affirmation or a call for blessings. It signifies a positive intention or a wish for well-being.
  • sakhā: This term means "friend" or "companion." It highlights the value of friendship and support in our lives.
  • sakhyur: This word refers to "those who are friendly" or "those who are in harmony." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships.
  • nimiṣi: This term means "to blink" or "to be vigilant." It suggests the need for awareness and mindfulness in our actions.
  • rakṣamāṇāḥ: This word translates to "those who protect" or "those who safeguard." It highlights the importance of protection and care in relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding, unity, wisdom, respect, vigilance, and protection in our relationships and interactions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as family dynamics, friendships, and professional relationships. By fostering awareness and respect, we can create harmonious environments that promote well-being and support. This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that underpin meaningful connections and the importance of actively participating in the creation of positive relationships.
