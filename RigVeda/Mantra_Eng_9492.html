देवान वसिष्ठो अमृतान ववन्दे ये विश्वा भुवनाभि प्रतस्थुः |ते नो रासन्ताम उरुगायम अद्य यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
devān vasiṣṭho amṛtān vavande ye viśvā bhuvanābhi pratasthuḥ | te no rāsantām urugāyam adya yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • devān vasiṣṭho amṛtān vavande: This phrase can be translated as "the divine beings, the most excellent, who are immortal, we honor." Here, "devān" refers to the divine or celestial beings, "vasiṣṭho" signifies the best or most excellent, and "amṛtān" means immortal. In real life, this can be seen as a call to respect and honor those who embody wisdom and virtue, such as teachers, leaders, or spiritual guides.
  • ye viśvā bhuvanābhi pratasthuḥ: This translates to "those who have established themselves in all worlds." "Viśvā" means all or universal, "bhuvanābhi" refers to the worlds or realms, and "pratasthuḥ" means to stand or establish. This can be related to individuals who have a broad understanding of life and can navigate various aspects of existence, such as scientists, philosophers, or community leaders who contribute to the welfare of society.
  • te no rāsantām urugāyam: This means "may they grant us abundance and prosperity." "Te" refers to them, "no" means us, "rāsantām" signifies abundance or wealth, and "urugāyam" can be interpreted as vast or extensive. In practical terms, this can be seen as a prayer for the blessings of prosperity in our endeavors, whether in business, personal growth, or community development.
  • adya yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This translates to "today, may you protect us with your auspiciousness always." "Adya" means today, "yūyam" refers to you (the divine beings), "pāta" means to protect, "svastibhiḥ" signifies auspiciousness or well-being, and "sadā naḥ" means always for us. This can be understood as a request for ongoing guidance and protection in our daily lives, emphasizing the importance of seeking divine support in our actions and decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of honoring divine beings and wise individuals who contribute positively to the world. It serves as a reminder to seek their blessings for abundance and protection in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal prayers, or community gatherings, where individuals come together to express gratitude and seek guidance for a prosperous and harmonious life. By acknowledging the wisdom of those who have established themselves in various realms, we cultivate a sense of respect and interconnectedness that can lead to collective growth and well-being.
