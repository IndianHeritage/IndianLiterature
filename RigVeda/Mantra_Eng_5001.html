मन्त्र: इन्द्राग्नि श्रिणुतं हवं यजमानस्य सुन्वतः |वीतं हव्याण्यागतम् पिबतं सोम्यम् मधु ||
indrāgnī śṛṇutaṁ havaṁ yajamānasya sunvataḥ | vītaṁ havyāny ā gatam pibataṁ somyam madhu ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the deities Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and rain, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This means "listen" or "pay attention." It is a call to the deities to be attentive to the offerings being made.
  • havaṁ: This translates to "the offering" or "the invocation." It signifies the act of making a sacred offering to the gods.
  • yajamānasya: This word means "of the one who is performing the sacrifice" or "the worshipper." It indicates the person who is conducting the ritual.
  • sunvataḥ: This means "who is well-versed" or "who is knowledgeable." It refers to the worshipper's understanding and sincerity in performing the ritual.
  • vītaṁ: This translates to "the offerings" or "the oblations." It refers to the items being offered to the deities.
  • havyāny: This means "the offerings that are to be consumed" or "the sacred items." It emphasizes the importance of the offerings in the ritual.
  • ā gatam: This translates to "having come" or "that which has arrived." It signifies the presence of the divine in response to the offerings.
  • pibataṁ: This means "drink" or "consume." It is an invitation for the deities to partake in the offerings made to them.
  • somyam: This refers to "the soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • madhu: This translates to "honey" or "sweetness," symbolizing the sweetness of life and the joy of divine blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the relationship between the worshipper and the divine. It signifies a call for the deities Indra and Agni to listen to the offerings made by the knowledgeable worshipper. In real life, this can be seen as a reminder of the significance of intention and sincerity in our actions, whether in spiritual practices or daily life. Just as the worshipper seeks the blessings of the gods through offerings, individuals can seek to cultivate positive relationships and intentions in their interactions with others. The mantra teaches us that our actions, when performed with understanding and devotion, can lead to divine blessings and a fulfilling life.
