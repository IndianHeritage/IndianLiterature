त्वं तां अग्न उभयान् वि विद्वान् वेषि प्रपित्वे मनुषो यजत्र |अभिपित्वे मनवे शास्यो भूर्मर्मृजेन्य उशिग्भिर नाक्रः ||
tvaṁ tām̐ agna ubhayān vi vidvān veṣi prapitve manuṣo yajatra | abhipitve manave śāsyo bhūr marmṛjenya uśigbhir nākraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tām̐: That
    • agna: O Fire (Agni)
    • ubhayān: Both
    • vi: Indeed
    • vidvān: Knowledgeable
    • veṣi: Protector
    • prapitve: In the act of sacrifice
    • manuṣo: Human
    • yajatra: One who performs sacrifice
    • abhipitve: In the act of worship
    • manave: To Man (the human)
    • śāsyo: Governed
    • bhūr: Abundance
    • marmṛjenya: May be attained
    • uśigbhir: By the wise
    • nākraḥ: Not to be neglected
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the divine fire, symbolizing knowledge, purity, and the transformative power of sacrifice.
    • It emphasizes the importance of both knowledge and action in the process of worship and sacrifice.
    • The phrase "ubhayān vi vidvān" suggests that true understanding comes from both knowledge and practical application.
    • It highlights the role of humans as yajatra (sacrificers) who must engage in rituals with a sense of purpose and dedication.
    • The mantra calls for the wise to govern their actions and intentions, ensuring that they are aligned with the greater good.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the concept of sustainable living, where knowledge about environmental issues must be coupled with actionable steps to protect the planet.
    • In a workplace setting, it can be seen in the importance of teamwork, where knowledge sharing and collaborative efforts lead to successful project outcomes.
    • In personal development, it emphasizes the need for continuous learning and applying that knowledge to improve oneself and contribute positively to society.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious balance between knowledge and action.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wisdom is not just theoretical but must be practiced in daily life.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can lead more meaningful lives, contribute to their communities, and foster a sense of responsibility towards the world.
