वायो शुक्रो आयामि ते मध्वो अग्रं दिविष्टिषु |आ याहि सोमपीतये स्पार्हो देव नियुत्वता ||
vāyo śukro ayāmi te madhvo agraṁ diviṣṭiṣu | ā yāhi somapītaye spārho deva niyutvatā ||
  • vāyo: This word refers to "Vayu," the wind god in Vedic literature. Vayu symbolizes the vital breath and the life force that sustains all living beings.
  • śukro: Translated as "pure" or "bright," this term signifies clarity, purity of thought, and spiritual enlightenment.
  • ayāmi: This means "I come" or "I approach," indicating a movement towards something desirable or beneficial.
  • te: This is a pronoun meaning "your," referring to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • madhvo: This word translates to "sweetness" or "delight," often associated with the essence of life and joy.
  • agraṁ: Meaning "the foremost" or "the best," it signifies the highest or most important aspect of something.
  • diviṣṭiṣu: This term refers to "in the heavens" or "in the divine realms," indicating a connection to the spiritual or celestial.
  • ā yāhi: This phrase means "come here" or "approach," inviting the divine presence or blessings.
  • somapītaye: This refers to "for the sake of drinking soma," where soma is a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • spārho: This word means "desire" or "wish," indicating a longing for something that is beneficial or uplifting.
  • deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being," representing the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • niyutvatā: This term means "with abundance" or "with great strength," suggesting a powerful and generous presence.

In essence, this mantra is a prayer or invocation to the divine forces, particularly to Vayu, to come forth with purity and sweetness, bringing forth blessings and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with a pure heart and a desire for spiritual nourishment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • During meditation or spiritual practices, one can recite this mantra to invite clarity and purity into their thoughts and actions.
  • In moments of stress or confusion, invoking the essence of Vayu can help in finding calmness and clarity, much like a gentle breeze that clears the air.
  • In rituals or ceremonies, this mantra can be used to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and spiritual growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine forces of nature, promoting purity, clarity, and abundance in life. By invoking these qualities, one can enhance their spiritual journey and foster a deeper connection with the universe.
