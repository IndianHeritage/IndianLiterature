प्र गायताभ्य आर्चाम देवान सोमं हिनोत महते धनाय |स्वादुः पवाते अतिवारम अव्यम आ सिदाति कलशं देवयुर नः ||
pra gāyatābhy arcāma devān somaṁ hinota mahate dhanāya | svāduḥ pavāte ati vāram avyam ā sīdāti kalaśaṁ devayur naḥ ||
  • pra gāyatābhy arcāma devān: This phrase translates to "We sing praises to the gods." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and reverence towards divine beings. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people express gratitude and seek blessings through prayers and rituals.
  • somaṁ hinota mahate dhanāya: Here, "somaṁ" refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "hinota mahate dhanāya" means "may it bring us great wealth." This suggests that through spiritual practices and offerings, one can attract prosperity. For example, in many cultures, rituals are performed to invoke abundance, such as harvest festivals where communities come together to celebrate and give thanks for their bountiful crops.
  • svāduḥ pavāte: This translates to "the sweet winds blow." It symbolizes the positive and nourishing forces of nature. In a real-life context, this can be related to the idea of good fortune and favorable circumstances that come our way when we are in harmony with our environment. For instance, when a person is in a positive mindset, they often attract good opportunities and relationships.
  • ati vāram avyam: This phrase means "may we be protected from harm." It highlights the human desire for safety and security. In everyday life, this can be seen in the various protective measures people take, such as wearing seatbelts, practicing safety protocols at work, or even seeking spiritual protection through prayers and rituals.
  • ā sīdāti kalaśaṁ devayur naḥ: This translates to "may the divine essence reside in us." It signifies the aspiration for divine presence and guidance in our lives. In practical terms, this can be reflected in the way individuals seek to embody virtues such as compassion, wisdom, and integrity, often looking to spiritual teachings for inspiration.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of devotion, prosperity, protection, and the aspiration for divine presence. Its significance lies in the reminder that through reverence and spiritual practices, individuals can cultivate a life filled with abundance and safety. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages people to engage in rituals, express gratitude, and seek harmony with nature, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and prosperous existence.
