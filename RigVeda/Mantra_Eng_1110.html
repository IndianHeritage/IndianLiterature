यस्य द्यावापृथिवी पौंस्यं महद यस्य व्रते वरुणो यस्य सूर्यः |यस्येन्द्रस्य सिन्धवः सच्चति व्रतम् मरुत्वन्तं सख्याय हवामहे ||
yasya dyāvāpṛthivī pauṁsyam mahad yasya vrate varuṇo yasya sūryaḥ | yasyendrasya sindhavaḥ saścati vratam marutvantaṁ sakhyāya havāmahe ||
  • Translation: "Whose sky and earth are of great importance, whose vow is Varuna, whose sun is the source of light, whose rivers are under the command of Indra, we invoke the Maruts for friendship."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Yasya: Whose
    • Dyāvāpṛthivī: Sky and Earth
    • Pauṁsyam: Importance or greatness
    • Mahad: Great
    • Vrata: Vow or promise
    • Varuṇa: The god of cosmic order and water
    • Sūryaḥ: The Sun
    • Indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods
    • Sindhavaḥ: Rivers
    • Marutvantaṁ: The Maruts, storm deities
    • Sakhyāya: For friendship
    • Havāmahe: We invoke
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and divine forces. The sky and earth represent the physical world, while the gods symbolize the spiritual and moral order.
    • Varuna, as the god of cosmic order, signifies the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in life. For example, in real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts where humans strive to maintain the balance of nature.
    • The sun (Sūrya) is a source of light and energy, essential for life. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of renewable energy sources, such as solar power, in combating climate change.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and leadership. In a corporate setting, this can be likened to effective leadership that guides teams towards achieving common goals.
    • The Maruts, associated with storms and winds, symbolize the dynamic forces of nature. In life, this can be interpreted as the challenges and changes we face, which can lead to growth and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting the natural world and the divine forces that govern it. It encourages individuals to seek harmony in their lives and relationships.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various fields such as environmentalism, leadership, and personal development.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of community and friendship, urging us to work together in harmony with nature and each other for a better future.
