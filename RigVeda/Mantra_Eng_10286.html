कः कुमारम् अजनयद रथं को निर अवर्तयत् |कः स्वित तद अद्य नो ब्रूयाद अनुदेयी यथाभवत् ||
kaḥ kumāram ajanayad rathaṁ ko nir avartayat | kaḥ svit tad adya no brūyād anudeyī yathābhavat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaḥ: Who
    • kumāram: The child or young one
    • ajanayad: Gave birth to or produced
    • rathaṁ: The chariot
    • ko: Who
    • nir: Without
    • avartayat: Turned or directed
    • kaḥ svit: Who indeed
    • tad: That
    • adya: Today
    • no: Us
    • brūyād: Should speak
    • anudeyī: Unfaltering or unerring
    • yathābhavat: As it has come to be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of rhetorical questions that reflect on the origins and guidance of life.
    • It questions who has given birth to the child (the future) and who has directed the chariot (the journey of life).
    • It emphasizes the uncertainty of knowledge and the quest for understanding our beginnings and direction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, one might reflect on the question of who influences the upbringing of a child and the values instilled in them.
    • In leadership, the mantra can be applied to the idea of guiding a team or organization, questioning who is steering the direction and how effectively.
    • In personal development, individuals may ponder their origins, influences, and the paths they choose in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of understanding our roots and the forces that shape our lives.
    • It encourages introspection about our origins and the guidance we receive, prompting us to seek clarity in our journey.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to be aware of the influences in our lives and to take responsibility for the direction we choose.
