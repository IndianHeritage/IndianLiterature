मन्त्र: विद्मा हि यस् ते अद्रिवस् त्वादत्तः सत्य सोमपाः |विश्वासु दसम कृष्टिषु ||
vidmā hi yas te adrivas tvādattaḥ satya somapāḥ | viśvāsu dasma kṛṣṭiṣu ||
  • vidmā hi: This phrase translates to "we know indeed." It signifies a deep understanding or awareness of a particular truth or principle.
  • yas te: This means "who is yours" or "who belongs to you." It indicates a connection or relationship with a divine or higher power.
  • adrivas: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is not obstructed" or "the one who is free." It suggests a state of liberation or purity.
  • tvādattaḥ: This translates to "from you, O giver." It acknowledges the source of blessings or knowledge, emphasizing the importance of divine grace.
  • satya: This word means "truth." It highlights the significance of truthfulness and integrity in life.
  • somapāḥ: This term refers to "those who drink the soma," which is often associated with divine nectar or the essence of life. It symbolizes spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • viśvāsu: This means "in all things" or "in every aspect." It suggests a holistic view of existence, encompassing all elements of life.
  • dasma: This translates to "may we attain" or "may we achieve." It expresses a desire for accomplishment and fulfillment.
  • kṛṣṭiṣu: This term refers to "in the fields" or "in the endeavors." It signifies the practical aspects of life, including work, agriculture, and personal efforts.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge, truth, and divine connection in achieving success in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to seek understanding and liberation through spiritual practices while remaining grounded in their daily endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, professional development, and the pursuit of truth in relationships and work. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a fulfilling and meaningful life, harmonizing spiritual aspirations with practical actions.
