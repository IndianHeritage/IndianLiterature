धारा सुतस्य रोचते पुनानो अरुषो हरिः |विश्वा यद रूपा पर्याति ऋक्वभिः सप्ताश्येभिर ऋक्वभिः ||
dhārā sutasya rocate punāno aruṣo hariḥ | viśvā yad rūpā pariyāty ṛkvabhiḥ saptāsyebhir ṛkvabhiḥ ||
  • dhārā sutasya rocate: This phrase can be translated as "the flow of the offspring is pleasing." Here, "dhārā" refers to a flow or stream, while "sutasya" means offspring or progeny. This can be understood in a real-life context as the nurturing and development of future generations, which is a fundamental aspect of family and society.
  • punāno aruṣo hariḥ: This translates to "the red one, the vibrant, is the Lord." "Punāno" signifies something that is renewed or refreshed, "aruṣo" refers to the color red, often associated with vitality and energy, and "hariḥ" is a term for the divine or the Lord. In real life, this can symbolize the rejuvenation of life and the importance of maintaining energy and enthusiasm in our endeavors.
  • viśvā yad rūpā pariyāty: This means "all forms that pervade." "Viśvā" indicates universality, "rūpā" refers to forms or shapes, and "pariyāty" means to pervade or encompass. This can be related to the diversity of life and experiences in the world, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of embracing diversity in our communities.
  • ṛkvabhiḥ saptāsyebhir ṛkvabhiḥ: This translates to "with the seven hymns of the Ṛk." "Ṛkvabhiḥ" refers to the hymns or verses of the Ṛgveda, and "saptāsyebhir" indicates the number seven, which often symbolizes completeness or wholeness. This can be seen in real life as the importance of tradition, culture, and the wisdom passed down through generations, which guide us in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing future generations, the vitality of life, the universality of forms, and the wisdom of tradition. In real life, it encourages us to appreciate the flow of life, to rejuvenate our spirits, to embrace diversity, and to honor the teachings of our ancestors. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as family gatherings, community events, and personal reflections, reminding us of our responsibilities towards ourselves, our families, and society as a whole.
