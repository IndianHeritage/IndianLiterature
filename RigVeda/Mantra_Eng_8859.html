तस्मा अरं गमाम वो यस्य क्षयाय जिन्वथ |आपो जनयथा च नः ||
tasmā araṁ gamāma vo yasya kṣayāya jinvatha | āpo janayathā ca naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasmā: Therefore
    • araṁ: We go
    • gamāma: We shall go
    • vo: To you
    • yasya: Whose
    • kṣayāya: For the destruction
    • jinvatha: You may live
    • āpo: Waters
    • janayathā: May you generate
    • ca: And
    • naḥ: For us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or invocation to a higher power, asking for guidance and support.
    • The phrase "tasmā araṁ gamāma vo" indicates a collective movement towards a goal or purpose, suggesting unity and cooperation.
    • "yasya kṣayāya jinvatha" implies a desire for longevity and prosperity, while also acknowledging the inevitability of destruction or challenges.
    • The mention of "āpo janayathā ca naḥ" highlights the importance of water, symbolizing nourishment, life, and the essential resources needed for survival.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when a group comes together to achieve a common goal, such as environmental conservation efforts, where they collectively strive for a sustainable future.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and resilience to overcome obstacles while also recognizing the importance of support from others.
    • In a corporate environment, teams can embody the essence of this mantra by working collaboratively towards a shared vision, ensuring that all members contribute to the success of the organization.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, resilience, and the essential role of resources in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that while challenges may arise, collective efforts and support can lead to prosperity and longevity.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal to communal, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide individuals and groups towards achieving their goals while fostering a sense of interconnectedness.
