मन्त्र: भवā नो अग्नि 'वितोत गोपा भवā वयस्कृतु उता नो वयोधाः | रास्वा च नः सुमहो हव्यदातिं त्रास्वोत नस् तन्भोऽ अप्रयुच्छन् ||
bhavā no agne 'vitota gopā bhavā vayaskṛd uta no vayodhāḥ | rāsvā ca naḥ sumaho havyadātiṁ trāsvota nas tanvo3 aprayucchan ||
  • bhavā no agne: This phrase translates to "May you, O Agni (the fire god), be present with us." Agni represents not just physical fire but also the divine energy that purifies and transforms. In real life, this can be likened to the role of fire in cooking, warmth, and rituals, symbolizing transformation and sustenance.
  • 'vitota gopā: This means "may you protect us." Here, Agni is invoked as a protector. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we seek protection from various forms of danger, whether physical, emotional, or spiritual. For instance, parents often pray for the safety and well-being of their children.
  • bhavā vayaskṛd uta no vayodhāḥ: This translates to "may you grant us strength and vigor." This is a plea for vitality and energy, which is essential for overcoming challenges. In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of physical fitness and mental resilience in achieving personal and professional goals.
  • rāsvā ca naḥ sumaho havyadātiṁ: This means "may the offerings we make be accepted." It reflects the idea of reciprocity in relationships, where one gives and receives. In real life, this can be seen in community service or charitable acts, where individuals contribute to society and, in return, gain a sense of fulfillment and connection.
  • trāsvota nas tanvo3 aprayucchan: This translates to "may you protect our bodies and not abandon us." This is a call for physical and spiritual protection. In contemporary life, this can be related to the importance of health and wellness practices, such as regular exercise, meditation, and seeking medical care, to ensure our bodies are cared for and protected.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine presence, protection, strength, acceptance of offerings, and the safeguarding of our physical and spiritual well-being. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives through spiritual practices, community involvement, and personal health. By invoking these qualities, we can navigate the complexities of life with resilience and purpose, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
