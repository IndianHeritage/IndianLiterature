आ द्विभरहामिनो यात्व इन्द्रो वृषा हरिभ्यां परिषिक्तमन्धः |गव्याः सुतस्य प्रभृतस्य मध्वः सत्रा खेदाम् अरुशहां वृषस्व ||
ā dvibarhā amino yātv indro vṛṣā haribhyām pariṣiktam andhaḥ | gavy ā sutasya prabhṛtasya madhvaḥ satrā khedām aruśahā vṛṣasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • dvibarhā: two-fold, or having two wings
    • amino: of the mind or intellect
    • yātv: may you go or proceed
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • vṛṣā: the one who brings strength or power
    • haribhyām: from the strong or powerful
    • pariṣiktam: anointed or blessed
    • andhaḥ: blind or darkness
    • gavy: of the cow or cattle
    • ā: from
    • sutasya: of the son or offspring
    • prabhṛtasya: of the one who is abundant
    • madhvaḥ: sweet or pleasant
    • satrā: in the assembly or gathering
    • khedām: sorrow or grief
    • aruśahā: the one who removes
    • vṛṣasva: may you be strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The phrase "dvibarhā amino" suggests a duality in thought or action, emphasizing the importance of balance and wisdom in decision-making.
    • The reference to "gavy ā sutasya" highlights the significance of nurturing and caring for one's resources, akin to how a farmer tends to cattle for sustenance.
    • The mantra also speaks to overcoming darkness or ignorance ("andhaḥ") through the strength and blessings of the divine.
    • In a real-life context, this can be likened to seeking guidance and support in challenging times, much like a student seeking help from a teacher when faced with difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine strength and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our thoughts and actions, and to nurture our resources, whether they be relationships, knowledge, or material possessions.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of personal struggle, decision-making, or when one seeks to foster growth in their personal or professional life.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength and seek divine guidance to navigate through the complexities of life.
