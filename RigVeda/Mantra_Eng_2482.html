विभावादेवः सुरणः परि क्षितीर अग्निर बभूव शवसाऽ सुमद्रथः |तस्य व्रतानि भूर्पोषिणो वयं उप भूषेम दमा आ सुवृक्तिभिः ||
vibhāvā devaḥ suraṇaḥ pari kṣitīr agnir babhūva śavasā sumadrathaḥ | tasya vratāni bhūripoṣiṇo vayam upa bhūṣema dama ā suvṛktibhiḥ ||
  • Vibhāvā devaḥ suraṇaḥ: This phrase refers to the divine beings or gods who are manifest and powerful. The term "vibhāvā" signifies the presence and glory of the divine. In real life, this can be related to the way we perceive greatness in nature or in individuals who inspire us, such as leaders or visionaries.
  • Pari kṣitīr agnir babhūva: Here, "pari kṣitīr" means the encompassing earth, and "agnir babhūva" refers to the fire that has come into being. This can symbolize the fundamental elements of life—earth and fire—representing stability and transformation. For example, in cooking, fire transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, illustrating the importance of change and growth in our lives.
  • Śavasā sumadrathaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as the one who is strong and has a good chariot (symbolizing strength and capability). In a modern context, this could relate to individuals who are well-prepared and equipped to face challenges, much like athletes or professionals who train rigorously to achieve their goals.
  • Tasya vratāni bhūripoṣiṇo: "Tasya" refers to that divine being, and "vratāni" means vows or commitments. This suggests that the divine is associated with certain principles or commitments that guide us. In everyday life, this can be seen in the commitments we make to our families, careers, or communities, emphasizing the importance of integrity and responsibility.
  • Vayam upa bhūṣema dama ā suvṛktibhiḥ: This line translates to "we adorn ourselves with good conduct and virtues." It highlights the significance of ethical behavior and moral values in our lives. For instance, practicing kindness, honesty, and respect in our interactions with others can lead to a more harmonious society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine presence, the importance of elemental forces, strength, commitment to principles, and the value of virtuous living. Its significance lies in reminding us to recognize the divine in our lives, to embrace change and growth, to be prepared and strong in our endeavors, to uphold our commitments, and to live ethically. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to strive for personal and communal betterment, fostering a sense of responsibility towards ourselves and the world around us.
