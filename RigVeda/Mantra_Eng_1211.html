ईळे dyāvāpṛthivī पूर्वचित्तये 'ग्निं घरमं सुरुचं याम्न्न इष्टये |याभिर भरे कारम अंशाय जिवथस ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतं ||
īḻe dyāvāpṛthivī pūrvacittaye 'gniṁ gharmaṁ surucaṁ yāmann iṣṭaye | yābhir bhare kāram aṁśāya jinvathas tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • īḻe dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase refers to the invocation of the heavens (dyāva) and the earth (pṛthivī). It signifies the duality and unity of the cosmos, representing the balance between the spiritual and the material worlds. In real life, this can be seen in the way we seek harmony in our lives, balancing our aspirations (spiritual) with our responsibilities (material).
  • pūrvacittaye: This term translates to "with a focused mind" or "with intention." It emphasizes the importance of having a clear and determined mindset when approaching any task or goal. For example, in a professional setting, having a focused mind can lead to better decision-making and productivity.
  • 'gniṁ gharmaṁ surucaṁ: Here, 'gniṁ refers to fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Gharmaṁ means warmth or heat, and surucaṁ implies beauty or auspiciousness. Together, this can be interpreted as invoking the transformative power of fire to bring about positive change in our lives. In practical terms, this could relate to the way we use passion and enthusiasm (fire) to achieve our goals.
  • yāmann iṣṭaye: This phrase means "for the purpose of our desires." It highlights the importance of intention behind our actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we set goals and work towards them, ensuring that our actions align with our desires.
  • yābhir bhare kāram aṁśāya: This translates to "with which we fill the actions." It suggests that our intentions and desires should be filled with purpose and meaning. For instance, in community service, when individuals engage in actions that benefit others, they are filling their deeds with a sense of purpose.
  • jinvathas tābhir ū ṣu: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we thrive through these." It speaks to the idea of flourishing and succeeding through the right intentions and actions. In real life, this can be related to personal growth and development, where individuals thrive by aligning their actions with their values.
  • ūtibhir aśvinā gatam: This refers to the blessings of the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being. It signifies the importance of divine support in our endeavors. In practical terms, this can be seen in how people seek guidance and support from mentors or spiritual leaders in their journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of intention, balance, and divine support in our actions. It teaches us that by aligning our desires with purposeful actions and seeking harmony between the spiritual and material aspects of life, we can thrive and achieve our goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and community service, where the focus on intention and purpose leads to meaningful outcomes.
