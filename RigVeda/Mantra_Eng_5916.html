बृहस्पते युवं इन्द्रश्च वस्वो दिव्यस्येशाथे उता पार्थिवस्य | धत्तं रयिं स्तुवते कीरये चिद् यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
bṛhaspate yuvam indraś ca vasvo divyasyeśāthe uta pārthivasya | dhattaṁ rayiṁ stuvate kīraye cid yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate yuvam indraś ca vasvo divyasyeśāthe uta pārthivasya | dhattaṁ rayiṁ stuvate kīraye cid yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Bṛhaspati, you and Indra, the divine and the earthly, grant us wealth and prosperity. We praise you, may you protect us always with auspiciousness."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity of wisdom and eloquence, often associated with knowledge and learning.
    • Yuvam: You (plural), referring to the divine entities being addressed.
    • Indra: The king of gods, symbolizing strength, power, and protection.
    • Vasvo: Referring to the earthly or material aspects of life.
    • Divyasyeśāthe: The divine or celestial realm, indicating a connection to higher powers.
    • Pārthivasya: Pertaining to the earthly or worldly aspects.
    • Dhattam: Grant or bestow, a request for blessings.
    • Rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity, both material and spiritual.
    • Stuvate: We praise or worship, indicating reverence towards the deities.
    • Kīraye: For glory or fame, seeking recognition and honor.
    • Cid: May it be so, an affirmation of the request.
    • Yūyam: You (plural), again addressing the divine beings.
    • Pāta: Protect or safeguard, a plea for divine protection.
    • Svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness, indicating a desire for good fortune.
    • Sadā: Always, a request for continuous blessings.
    • Naḥ: Us, referring to the devotees or worshippers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke this mantra during meetings to seek wisdom and prosperity for their business endeavors.
    • During family gatherings or celebrations, individuals may recite this mantra to bless their homes with wealth and harmony.
    • In educational institutions, students may chant this mantra before exams to invoke the blessings of knowledge and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in all aspects of life, whether material or spiritual.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and prosperity, suggesting that true wealth comes from a balance of these elements.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to approach challenges with reverence and gratitude, fostering a mindset of abundance and protection.
