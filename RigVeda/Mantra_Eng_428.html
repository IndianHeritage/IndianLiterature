तं घेम इत्त्था नमस्विन उप स्वराजम् आसते |होत्राभिर अग्निम मनुषः सम इन्दते तितिर्वांसो अति श्रिधः ||
taṁ ghem itthā namasvina upa svarājam āsate | hotrābhir agnim manuṣaḥ sam indhate titirvāṁso ati sridhaḥ ||
  • taṁ ghem itthā namasvina upa svarājam āsate: This phrase can be translated as "To that divine being, who is worthy of our reverence, we offer our salutations." Here, "taṁ" refers to the divine entity, "ghem" signifies the essence or the core, "itthā" means 'thus' or 'in this manner', "namasvina" indicates respect or reverence, and "upa svarājam āsate" suggests the presence of a higher power or divine authority.
  • hotrābhir agnim manuṣaḥ sam indhate: This part translates to "The human beings invoke the fire (Agni) with their offerings." "hotrābhir" refers to the priests or those who perform the rituals, "agnim" is the fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals, and "manuṣaḥ sam indhate" means that humans collectively engage in this invocation.
  • titirvāṁso ati sridhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "Those who are wise and knowledgeable attain prosperity." "titirvāṁso" refers to the wise or learned individuals, "ati" means 'beyond' or 'exceeding', and "sridhaḥ" signifies prosperity or wealth.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of reverence towards the divine and the act of invoking Agni, the fire, which symbolizes purity, transformation, and the connection between the earthly and the divine. It highlights the collective effort of humanity in seeking blessings and prosperity through rituals.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:
    • Rituals and Ceremonies: Just as the mantra suggests invoking Agni, people today still perform rituals to seek blessings for new beginnings, such as housewarming ceremonies or weddings.
    • Community Gatherings: The collective invocation of divine blessings can be seen in community events where people come together to celebrate festivals, reinforcing social bonds and shared values.
    • Personal Reflection: The idea of reverence can be applied in daily life through mindfulness practices, where individuals take a moment to express gratitude and respect for the forces that govern their lives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine. It encourages individuals to engage in respectful practices, seek knowledge, and work collectively towards prosperity. By invoking the divine through rituals and community efforts, people can foster a sense of unity and purpose in their lives.
