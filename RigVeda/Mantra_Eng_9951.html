एवामहॊ असुर वक्षथाय वम्रकः पद्भिर उप सर्पद इन्द्रम् |स इयानः करति स्वस्तिम अस्मा इषम् ऊर्जं सुख्षितिं विश्वम् आभाः ||
evā maho asura vakṣathāya vamrakaḥ paḍbhir upa sarpad indram | sa iyānaḥ karati svastim asmā iṣam ūrjaṁ sukṣitiṁ viśvam ābhāḥ ||
  • evā maho asura vakṣathāya vamrakaḥ paḍbhir upa sarpad indram
    • evā - This word can be interpreted as "thus" or "in this way," indicating a connection to the preceding thoughts or actions.
    • maho - Meaning "great" or "mighty," it signifies the importance of the subject being discussed.
    • asura - Refers to powerful beings, often associated with strength and might, but can also imply challenges or obstacles.
    • vakṣathāya - This term can be understood as "to the one who speaks" or "to the one who declares," suggesting communication or proclamation.
    • vamrakaḥ - This word can denote "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one," emphasizing the strength of the subject.
    • paḍbhir - Meaning "with words" or "through speech," it highlights the importance of verbal expression in achieving goals.
    • upa - Translates to "near" or "towards," indicating a direction or approach.
    • sarpad - This term can be interpreted as "to the serpent," which often symbolizes wisdom, transformation, or hidden knowledge.
    • indram - Refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, representing power, authority, and leadership.
  • sa iyānaḥ karati svastim asmā
    • sa - Meaning "he" or "that one," referring to a specific entity or force.
    • iyānaḥ - This can be interpreted as "who moves" or "who travels," suggesting progress or movement towards a goal.
    • karati - Meaning "does" or "makes," indicating action or creation.
    • svastim - Refers to "well-being" or "prosperity," emphasizing the positive outcomes of actions taken.
    • asmā - Meaning "for us," indicating a collective benefit or shared experience.
  • iṣam ūrjaṁ sukṣitiṁ viśvam ābhāḥ
    • iṣam - This word can be understood as "the goal" or "the aim," representing aspirations or objectives.
    • ūrjaṁ - Meaning "energy" or "vitality," it signifies the life force or strength needed to achieve goals.
    • sukṣitiṁ - Refers to "prosperity" or "well-being," emphasizing the importance of positive outcomes.
    • viśvam - Meaning "the universe" or "all," indicating a holistic view of existence and interconnectedness.
    • ābhāḥ - This term can be interpreted as "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and clarity.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, communication, and collective well-being in achieving one's goals. It highlights the role of powerful forces (both internal and external) in guiding us towards prosperity and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, teamwork, and personal development. For instance, in a corporate setting, effective communication and collaboration among team members can lead to successful project outcomes, while individual strength and determination can help one overcome personal challenges. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the shared journey towards achieving a prosperous and enlightened existence.
