अभि त्वा देवः सविताभि सोमो अवीवृतत् |अभि त्वा विश्व भूतानि अभिवर्तो यथाससि ||
abhi tvā devaḥ savitābhi somo avīvṛtat | abhi tvā viśvā bhūtāny abhīvarto yathāsasi ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tvā devaḥ savitābhi somo avīvṛtat | abhi tvā viśvā bhūtāny abhīvarto yathāsasi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • devaḥ: divine, god
    • savitā: the sun, the source of life
    • bhi: with, by
    • somo: the moon, representing nourishment and tranquility
    • avīvṛtat: has not been concealed, revealed
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • bhūtāny: beings, entities
    • abhīvarto: to be made manifest, to come forth
    • yathāsasi: as you wish, as you desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of the sun (Savitā) and the moon (Soma), emphasizing their roles in sustaining life and providing nourishment.
    • The phrase "abhi tvā" indicates a direct appeal to the divine, seeking blessings and guidance.
    • The mention of "viśvā bhūtāny" signifies the interconnectedness of all beings, highlighting the importance of harmony in the universe.
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of community and the need for individuals to support one another, much like the sun and moon support life on Earth.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine forces that govern our existence and the need to align ourselves with these forces for a harmonious life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and enlightenment, as represented by the sun and moon, which are symbols of wisdom and clarity.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited during meditation or rituals to invoke peace, prosperity, and unity among people.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being open and receptive to the divine guidance that is always present, just as the sun and moon are constant in the sky.
