इन्दुः पुनानः प्रजाम उराणः करद विश्वानि द्रविणानि नः ॥
induḥ punānaḥ prajām urāṇaḥ karad viśvāni draviṇāni naḥ ||
  • Induḥ: This word translates to "moon" or "nectar." In a broader sense, it symbolizes nourishment, prosperity, and the soothing qualities of nature.
  • Punānaḥ: Meaning "purifying" or "refreshing," this term emphasizes the cleansing and revitalizing aspects of life.
  • Prajām: This refers to "creatures" or "beings," indicating all forms of life, including humans, animals, and plants.
  • Urāṇaḥ: This word means "great" or "vast," suggesting the expansive nature of existence and the universe.
  • Karad: Translated as "may we obtain" or "may we achieve," it expresses a desire for attainment and success.
  • Viśvāni: This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Draviṇāni: Referring to "wealth" or "resources," this term highlights the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • Naḥ: This means "for us," indicating a personal or collective plea for blessings and prosperity.

The mantra "induḥ punānaḥ prajām urāṇaḥ karad viśvāni draviṇāni naḥ" can be interpreted as a prayer for the purification and nourishment of all beings, invoking the vastness of existence to grant us wealth and resources. It emphasizes the importance of holistic well-being, both materially and spiritually.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "prajām" and "urāṇaḥ" can inspire individuals to care for all forms of life and the environment, promoting sustainability and ecological balance.
  • Community Well-being: The desire for "draviṇāni" reflects the need for collective prosperity. This can manifest in community initiatives aimed at improving local resources, such as food banks or educational programs.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek personal purification and growth, whether through meditation, self-reflection, or engaging in activities that promote mental and emotional well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of seeking both material and spiritual wealth. It encourages us to purify our intentions and actions, fostering a sense of community and responsibility towards the environment and each other. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous world.
