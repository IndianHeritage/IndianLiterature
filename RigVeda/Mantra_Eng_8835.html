यो भानुभिर विभावा विभात्य अग्निर देवभिर ऋतावाजस्रः |आ यो विवाय सख्या सखिभ्योऽपरिह्वृतो अत्यो न सप्तिः ||
yo bhānubhir vibhāvā vibhāty agnir devebhir ṛtāvājasraḥ | ā yo vivāya sakhyā sakhibhyo 'parihvṛto atyo na saptiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • bhānubhir: by the rays (of the sun)
    • vibhāvā: manifests
    • vibhāty: shines
    • agnir: fire
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • ṛtāvājasraḥ: always in accordance with truth
    • ā: that
    • yo: who
    • vivāya: by joining
    • sakhyā: with friends
    • sakhibhyo: to friends
    • aparihṛto: unimpeded
    • aty: beyond
    • na: not
    • saptiḥ: the seven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) that shines brightly with the rays of the sun.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness, as Agni is always aligned with these principles.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of friendship and unity, suggesting that true strength comes from collaboration and support among friends.
    • The phrase "beyond the seven" can be interpreted as transcending limitations or boundaries, indicating a higher state of existence or consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, the concept of collaboration can be seen when team members come together to achieve a common goal, much like the unity expressed in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, the importance of supporting friends during difficult times reflects the essence of "sakhyā" and "sakhibhyo."
    • In environmental discussions, the reverence for fire (Agni) can be linked to the need for sustainable practices that honor nature and truth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of truth, friendship, and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in promoting values that foster unity and righteousness in our daily lives.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious environment that transcends challenges and limitations.
