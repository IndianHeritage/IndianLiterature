मां निन्दत य इमां मह्यं रातिं देवो ददौ मर्याय स्वधावान् |पाकाय गृत्सो अमृतो विचेताऽ वैश्वानरो नृतमो यह्वो अग्निः ||
mā nindata ya imām mahyaṁ rātiṁ devo dadau martyāya svadhāvān | pākāya gṛtso amṛto vicetā vaiśvānaro nṛtamo yahvo agniḥ ||
  • mā nindata: This phrase translates to "do not criticize" or "do not speak ill of." It emphasizes the importance of refraining from negative speech about others. In real life, this can be seen in the workplace where constructive feedback is encouraged, but gossip and slander can create a toxic environment.
  • ya imām mahyaṁ rātiṁ: This means "that which brings me joy" or "that which gives me happiness." It highlights the pursuit of joy and fulfillment in life. For example, engaging in hobbies or spending time with loved ones can bring happiness and should be prioritized.
  • devo dadau: This translates to "the divine has given." It suggests that blessings and gifts come from a higher power. In everyday life, one might recognize moments of serendipity or unexpected help as divine interventions, reinforcing the belief in a supportive universe.
  • martyāya svadhāvān: This phrase means "for the mortal being." It acknowledges the human condition and the need for guidance and support in our mortal lives. This can be related to seeking mentorship or guidance from elders or experienced individuals in various aspects of life.
  • pākāya gṛtso: This translates to "pure and nourishing." It refers to the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and food. In real life, this can be applied by choosing healthy, organic foods and maintaining a clean environment, which contributes to overall well-being.
  • amṛto vicetā: This means "the immortal one who knows." It signifies wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge. In practical terms, this can be seen in the value placed on education and lifelong learning, where individuals strive to gain knowledge that transcends their mortal existence.
  • vaiśvānaro nṛtamo: This translates to "the universal fire, the best among men." It symbolizes the vital energy that connects all living beings. In real life, this can be related to the idea of community and shared experiences, where collective efforts lead to greater achievements.
  • yahvo agniḥ: This means "that which is fire." Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation and purification. In everyday life, this can be reflected in personal growth, where challenges and hardships act as a catalyst for change and improvement.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of positive speech, the pursuit of joy, and the recognition of divine blessings in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek purity, wisdom, and community while acknowledging the transformative power of challenges. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can guide personal development, foster healthy relationships, and promote a supportive environment, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
