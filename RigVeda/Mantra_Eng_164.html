इमे सोमास इन्दवः सुतासो अधि बरहिषि |तां इन्द्र सहसे पिब ||
ime somāsa indavaḥ sutāso adhi barhiṣi | tām̐ indra sahase piba ||
  • ime somāsa indavaḥ: This phrase translates to "these soma drinks, O Indra." In the Vedic context, Soma refers to a sacred drink that was believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • sutāso adhi barhiṣi: This means "the well-prepared offerings on the sacred grass." Barhiṣi refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, indicating the importance of proper preparation and respect in spiritual practices.
  • tām̐ indra sahase piba: This translates to "drink it, O Indra, with great strength." Here, Indra represents the divine force or energy that empowers individuals to overcome challenges and achieve greatness.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the role of sacred offerings in spiritual practices. It highlights the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, suggesting that through proper rituals and offerings, one can invoke divine energy.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek inspiration and strength from higher powers or the universe. It encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote well-being, such as meditation, prayer, or community service, which can be viewed as offerings to the greater good.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of spiritual nourishment and the need to connect with the divine. By recognizing the sacredness of our actions and the energy we invoke, we can cultivate a more meaningful and empowered life. Whether through personal rituals or communal practices, the essence of this mantra encourages us to seek strength and inspiration from the divine, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
