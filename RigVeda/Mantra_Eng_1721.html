अचिकित्वां चिकीषुषश्चिदात्र कवीन् पृच्छामि विद्मने न विद्वान् |विजस तस्तम्भ षडिमाः राजांस्य अजस्य रूपे किमपि स्विदेकम् ||
acikitvāñ cikituṣaś cid atra kavīn pṛcchāmi vidmane na vidvān | vi yas tastambha ṣaḻ imā rajāṁsy ajasya rūpe kim api svid ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • acikitvāñ: One who is aware or knowledgeable.
    • cikituṣaś: One who is wise or has insight.
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • atra: Here or in this context.
    • kavīn: Poets or seers, those who have deep understanding.
    • pṛcchāmi: I ask or inquire.
    • vidmane: In the realm of knowledge or wisdom.
    • na: Not.
    • vidvān: The knowledgeable or wise person.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'who' or 'that'.
    • yas: Who or which.
    • tastambha: Has established or has set in place.
    • ṣaḻ: Six.
    • imā: These.
    • rajāṁsy: Kings or rulers.
    • ajasya: Of the unborn or eternal.
    • rūpe: Form or shape.
    • kim: What.
    • api: Also or even.
    • svid: Where or in what place.
    • ekam: One or singular.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a quest for knowledge and understanding.
    • The speaker is inquiring about the nature of wisdom and the essence of existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking answers from those who possess deep insight, the 'kavīn'.
    • The mention of 'six kings' could symbolize various aspects of leadership or different paths to wisdom.
    • It suggests that true knowledge is not easily grasped and requires inquiry and contemplation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a manager may seek advice from experienced leaders (the 'kavīn') to navigate complex challenges.
    • Students often ask teachers or mentors for guidance to gain deeper insights into their subjects.
    • In personal life, individuals may turn to philosophers or spiritual leaders when seeking answers to existential questions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inquiry and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us that wisdom is often found through dialogue and questioning those who are knowledgeable.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as education, leadership, and personal growth, reminding us to seek understanding and clarity in our lives.
