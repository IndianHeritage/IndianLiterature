यः शम्बरं पर्वतेषु क्षियन्तं चत्वारिंश्यां शरद् अन्वविन्दत् | ओजायमानं यो अहिं जगहान दानुं शयानं स जनासा इन्द्रः ||
yaḥ śambaram parvateṣu kṣiyantaṁ catvāriṁśyāṁ śarady anvavindat | ojāyamānaṁ yo ahiṁ jaghāna dānuṁ śayānaṁ sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaḥ śambaram parvateṣu kṣiyantaṁ catvāriṁśyāṁ śarady anvavindat | ojāyamānaṁ yo ahiṁ jaghāna dānuṁ śayānaṁ sa janāsa indraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • śambaram: the auspicious or peaceful
    • parvateṣu: in the mountains
    • kṣiyantaṁ: diminishing or reducing
    • catvāriṁśyāṁ: forty
    • śarady: in the autumn
    • anvavindat: found or discovered
    • ojāyamānaṁ: increasing strength or energy
    • yo: who
    • ahiṁ: the serpent or enemy
    • jaghāna: killed or defeated
    • dānuṁ: the demon or adversary
    • śayānaṁ: lying down or resting
    • sa: he
    • janāsa: of the people
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful figure, Indra, who is associated with strength and victory.
    • The phrase "yaḥ śambaram parvateṣu" suggests that Indra is found in the peaceful mountains, symbolizing stability and strength in nature.
    • "kṣiyantaṁ catvāriṁśyāṁ" indicates a time of reduction or decline, possibly referring to challenges faced during the autumn season.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of resilience, as it mentions "ojāyamānaṁ," which signifies the increase of strength even in difficult times.
    • Indra's action of "jaghāna dānuṁ" represents the triumph over adversities, akin to overcoming personal challenges in life, such as defeating fears or obstacles.
    • The imagery of "ahiṁ jaghāna" can be likened to facing and conquering one's inner demons or external threats, much like a person overcoming a significant life challenge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, resilience, and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • In real life, it can be applied to various situations, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues where one must find inner strength to prevail.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in times of decline or difficulty, one can discover and harness their strength to achieve victory.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embody the qualities of Indra, facing adversities with courage and determination.
