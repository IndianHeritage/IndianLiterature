शं नो अग्निर ज्योतिरनिको अस्तु शं नो मित्रावरुणाव अश्विनाः शम् |शं नः सुक्रतां सुक्रतानि सन्तु शं न इषिरो अभि वातु वातः ||
śaṁ no agnir jyotiranīko astu śaṁ no mitrāvaruṇāv aśvinā śam | śaṁ naḥ sukṛtāṁ sukṛtāni santu śaṁ na iṣiro abhi vātu vātaḥ ||
  • śaṁ no agnir: This phrase translates to "May the fire (Agni) be auspicious for us." In many cultures, fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and energy. For example, in a wedding ceremony, the fire is often considered a witness to the vows taken by the couple, signifying the sacredness of their union.
  • jyotiranīko astu: This means "May the light be favorable." Light is often associated with knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and personal growth, where individuals seek to illuminate their minds and lives with knowledge.
  • śaṁ no mitrāvaruṇāv: This translates to "May the friends (Mitra and Varuna) be auspicious." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order. In our lives, having supportive friends and maintaining harmonious relationships can lead to a more fulfilling and balanced life.
  • aśvinā śam: This means "May the Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen) be auspicious." The Ashvins symbolize health, vitality, and the duality of life. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of balance in our lives, such as balancing work and personal life to maintain overall well-being.
  • śaṁ naḥ sukṛtāṁ sukṛtāni santu: This translates to "May good deeds and actions be ours." This emphasizes the significance of performing righteous actions in our daily lives. For instance, volunteering for community service or helping those in need reflects the essence of this line.
  • śaṁ na iṣiro abhi vātu vātaḥ: This means "May the wind (vāta) protect us from harm." Wind is often seen as a force of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal. In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as seeking protection from negative influences and striving for a safe and nurturing environment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for auspiciousness, protection, and the pursuit of good deeds. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of relationships, knowledge, and righteous actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate positive relationships, seek knowledge, and engage in actions that contribute to the well-being of themselves and their communities. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
