तुभ्यं सोमाः सुताः इमे स्तीर्णं बर्हिर् विभावसो |स्तोत्रिभ्य इन्द्रं आ वह ||
tubhyaṁ somāḥ sutā ime stīrṇam barhir vibhāvaso | stotṛbhya indram ā vaha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • somāḥ: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • sutā: the sons or offspring
    • ime: these
    • stīrṇam: well-prepared or well-placed
    • barhir: the grass or the sacred grass
    • vibhāvaso: of the one who shines or illuminates
    • stotṛbhya: to the praise-singers or worshippers
    • indram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ā: towards
    • vaha: bring or carry
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, to accept the offerings made with the soma drink.
    • The mention of "soma" signifies the importance of this sacred drink in Vedic rituals, believed to bestow immortality and divine insight.
    • The phrase "well-prepared" indicates the care and reverence with which offerings should be made, emphasizing the significance of intention in rituals.
    • The invocation of Indra highlights the role of divine forces in human affairs, suggesting that the gods are active participants in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, the concept of offering can be seen in various forms, such as meditation, prayer, or acts of kindness, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power.
    • Just as the Vedic priests prepared offerings with care, people today often engage in rituals or ceremonies that require mindfulness and intention, such as weddings or memorials.
    • The idea of invoking divine assistance can be related to seeking guidance in challenging situations, where individuals may pray or meditate for clarity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • It teaches that rituals should be performed with sincerity and respect, as they are a means of connecting with higher powers.
    • In real life, the principles of intention, reverence, and seeking divine guidance can be applied in various aspects, from personal growth to community rituals, fostering a sense of connection and purpose.
