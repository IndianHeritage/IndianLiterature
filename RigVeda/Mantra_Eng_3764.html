अग्निं घृतेन वावृधुः स्तोमेभिर विश्वचर्षणिम् |स्वाधीभिर वचस्युभिः ||
agniṁ ghṛtena vāvṛdhuḥ stomebhir viśvacarṣaṇim | svādhībhir vacasyubhiḥ ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Ghṛtena: This means "with ghee" or "with clarified butter." Ghee is often used in Vedic rituals as an offering to the deities, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
  • Vāvṛdhuḥ: This translates to "may he be increased" or "may he grow." It expresses a desire for the enhancement of Agni's presence and blessings.
  • Stomebhir: This means "with hymns" or "with praises." It indicates the importance of chanting and invoking the divine through words of reverence.
  • Viśvacarṣaṇim: This term refers to "the all-pervading" or "the one who moves everywhere." It highlights the omnipresence of Agni and his role in the universe.
  • Svādhībhir: This means "with self-control" or "with mastery." It emphasizes the importance of discipline and control in one's actions and thoughts.
  • Vacasyubhiḥ: This translates to "with good speech" or "with beneficial words." It signifies the power of words and communication in shaping reality and relationships.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, asking for his blessings and presence in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of offering pure intentions (represented by ghee) and the power of words and self-discipline in invoking divine energy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek personal growth and improvement through self-discipline and positive communication.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In spiritual practices, this mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke the blessings of Agni, ensuring that offerings are made with pure intentions.
  • Conflict Resolution: The emphasis on good speech can be applied in resolving conflicts, encouraging individuals to communicate effectively and respectfully.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the enhancement of divine energy in our lives through purity, discipline, and positive communication. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster personal growth, improve relationships, and create a harmonious environment around them.
