हन्ता वृत्रम इन्द्रः शूशुवानः प्रावीन नु वीरो जरीतारम् ऊती |कर्त्ता सुदासे अह वा उ लोकं दाता वसु मुहुर आ दाशुषे भूत ||
hantā vṛtram indraḥ śūśuvānaḥ prāvīn nu vīro jaritāram ūtī | kartā sudāse aha vā u lokaṁ dātā vasu muhur ā dāśuṣe bhūt ||
  • hantā vṛtram: This phrase translates to "the slayer of Vritra." In the context of the Rigveda, Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and drought. Indra, the king of the gods, is often depicted as the one who defeats Vritra, restoring order and bringing rain.
  • indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and the warrior spirit. He is often invoked for protection and victory in battles.
  • śūśuvānaḥ: This word means "who has heard" or "who has listened." It signifies the importance of being attentive and responsive to the needs of the world and its people.
  • prāvīn nu vīro: Translates to "the strong hero." This emphasizes the qualities of bravery and valor, suggesting that true strength lies not just in physical power but also in moral courage.
  • jaritāram ūtī: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth abundance." It highlights the role of Indra as a provider of prosperity and sustenance, essential for life.
  • kartā sudāse: This means "the doer of good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of action and the impact of one's deeds on the community and the world.
  • aha vā u lokaṁ: Translates to "I, or the world." This phrase suggests a connection between the individual and the larger universe, indicating that one's actions can influence the world around them.
  • dātā vasu: This means "the giver of wealth." It signifies the importance of generosity and sharing resources for the betterment of society.
  • muhur ā dāśuṣe bhūt: This translates to "again and again, for the benefit of all." It emphasizes the cyclical nature of giving and receiving, suggesting that acts of kindness and generosity should be continuous.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, strength, and the importance of community. It highlights the role of Indra as a protector and provider, encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to take action against chaos and adversity, promote generosity, and strive for the greater good. It serves as a reminder that true strength lies in the ability to listen, act for the benefit of others, and contribute positively to society. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous environment for themselves and their communities.
