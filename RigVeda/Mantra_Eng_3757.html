त्वां अग्ने वाजसातमं विप्राः वर्धन्ति सुश्रुतम् | स नो रास्व सुवीर्यम् ||
tvām agne vājasātamaṁ viprā vardhanti suṣṭutam | sa no rāsva suvīryam ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature.
  • agne - This is the vocative form of Agni, emphasizing the invocation of the fire deity.
  • vājasātamaṁ - This term means "the best among those who provide strength or energy." It signifies Agni as a source of power and vitality.
  • viprā - This word refers to the wise or learned individuals, often the priests or sages who perform rituals and invoke divine blessings.
  • vardhanti - This means "they increase" or "they promote," indicating the action of enhancing or elevating something.
  • suṣṭutam - This translates to "well-praised" or "highly praised," suggesting that Agni is worthy of reverence and admiration.
  • sa - This means "he" or "that," referring back to Agni.
  • no - This translates to "our," indicating a personal connection or request for blessings.
  • rāsva - This word means "short" or "brief," often interpreted in the context of life or existence.
  • suvīryam - This translates to "good strength" or "excellent power," emphasizing the qualities of vitality and vigor.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings to enhance the strength and vitality of the wise individuals who praise him. It acknowledges Agni's role as a source of energy and power, and it seeks his favor for a life filled with vigor and purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni is a source of energy, individuals can seek to cultivate their inner strength and wisdom through education and self-improvement.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders and wise individuals can invoke the spirit of Agni to inspire and uplift their communities, promoting collective strength and well-being.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: This mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings for health, strength, and prosperity, reinforcing the connection between the divine and the material world.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Agni as a source of strength and vitality. It serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom and the power of collective praise. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to seek strength, promote well-being, and honor the divine forces that guide and support them.
