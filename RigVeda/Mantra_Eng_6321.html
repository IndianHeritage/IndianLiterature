मन्त्र: स्तुहि श्रुतं विपश्चितं हरिः यस्य प्रसाक्षिणा |गन्तारा दाशुषो गृहं नमस्विनः ||
stuhi śrutaṁ vipaścitaṁ harī yasya prasakṣiṇā | gantārā dāśuṣo gṛhaṁ namasvinaḥ ||
  • stuhi: This word translates to "praise" or "glorify." It signifies the act of acknowledging and honoring the divine or the wise.
  • śrutaṁ: Meaning "heard" or "known," this term emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom that comes from listening and understanding.
  • vipaścitaṁ: This refers to "the wise" or "the discerning." It highlights the value of insight and the ability to perceive deeper truths.
  • harī: Often associated with the divine, particularly Vishnu, this word represents the aspect of the divine that is protective and nurturing.
  • yasya: Meaning "whose," this word connects the qualities of wisdom and divinity to a specific entity or being.
  • prasakṣiṇā: This term means "witness" or "observer," indicating the presence of a higher consciousness that observes and guides.
  • gantārā: This translates to "the one who leads" or "the guide," suggesting a role of leadership and direction in life.
  • dāśuṣo: Meaning "of the servants" or "of the devotees," this term emphasizes humility and service to the divine.
  • gṛhaṁ: This word means "home" or "abode," symbolizing a place of safety, comfort, and belonging.
  • namasvinaḥ: This translates to "to the revered" or "to the worthy of respect," indicating a gesture of reverence and devotion.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to praise and honor the divine wisdom and guidance that leads us through life. It emphasizes the importance of listening to the wise, recognizing the protective nature of the divine, and serving with humility. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:


  • In education, valuing the insights of teachers and mentors who guide us in our learning journey.
  • In leadership, recognizing the importance of wise leaders who can guide their teams with discernment and compassion.
  • In personal relationships, fostering an environment of respect and reverence for one another, creating a supportive home.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, guidance, and reverence in our lives. It encourages us to seek knowledge, honor those who lead us, and create a nurturing environment where we can thrive. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with greater clarity and purpose.
