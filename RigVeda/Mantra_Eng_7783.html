अप द्वाराः मतीनां प्रात्ना ऋण्वन्ति कारवः |वृष्णो हरसा आयवः ||
apa dvārā matīnām pratnā ṛṇvanti kāravaḥ | vṛṣṇo harasa āyavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apa: away, from
    • dvārā: doors, gateways
    • matīnām: of the minds, of the intellects
    • pratnā: the thoughts, the ideas
    • ṛṇvanti: they flow, they come forth
    • kāravaḥ: the doers, the creators
    • vṛṣṇo: of Vishnu, the preserver
    • harasa: of joy, of happiness
    • āyavaḥ: the seekers, the aspirants
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flow of thoughts and ideas that come from the minds of individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creativity and action in the pursuit of knowledge and joy.
    • The reference to Vishnu suggests that these thoughts and actions are aligned with a higher purpose or divine will.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a collaborative work environment, when team members share their ideas (matīnām) freely, it leads to innovative solutions (pratnā) that benefit the entire organization.
    • In education, when students are encouraged to express their thoughts (ṛṇvanti), it fosters a culture of learning and creativity (kāravaḥ).
    • In personal development, individuals who seek joy (harasa) and purpose (āyavaḥ) in their actions often find fulfillment and success in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of open-mindedness and the flow of ideas in achieving personal and collective growth.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace creativity and action as pathways to joy and fulfillment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to enhanced collaboration, innovation, and a deeper sense of purpose in various aspects of life.
