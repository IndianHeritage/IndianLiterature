कीं नो भ्रातर अगस्त्य सखा सन्नाति मन्यसे |विद्मा हि ते यथा मनोऽस्मभ्यम् इन् न दित्ससि ||
kiṁ no bhrātar agastya sakhā sann ati manyase | vidmā hi te yathā mano 'smabhyam in na ditsasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ: What
    • no: to us
    • bhrātar: O brother
    • agastya: Agastya (a sage)
    • sakhā: friend
    • sann: being
    • ati: exceedingly
    • manyase: you think
    • vidmā: we know
    • hi: indeed
    • te: your
    • yathā: as
    • mano: mind
    • 'smabhyam: to us
    • in: not
    • ditsasi: you desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agastya, a revered sage in Hindu tradition, and expresses a sense of inquiry and connection.
    • The speaker is asking Agastya what he thinks of them, emphasizing a relationship of brotherhood and friendship.
    • It reflects a desire for understanding and knowledge, as the speaker acknowledges their awareness of Agastya's mind and intentions.
    • The phrase "you desire not" suggests a plea for goodwill and a request for positive intentions from Agastya.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be likened to a team member seeking feedback from a colleague, fostering a sense of camaraderie and collaboration.
    • In personal relationships, it reflects the importance of open communication and understanding each other's thoughts and feelings.
    • In a community context, it can symbolize the need for mutual respect and support among members, encouraging a harmonious environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of relationships built on trust, understanding, and goodwill.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as friendships, professional relationships, and community interactions.
    • By fostering open communication and positive intentions, individuals can create a supportive and collaborative atmosphere, leading to personal and collective growth.
