इदं त्यत् पात्रम् इन्द्रपानम् इन्द्रस्य प्रियं अमृतम् अपायि |मत्सद यथा सौमिनसाया देवं व्यास्मद द्वेषो युयवाद व्यंहः ||
idaṁ tyat pātram indrapānam indrasya priyam amṛtam apāyi | matsad yathā saumanasāya devaṁ vy a1smad dveṣo yuyavad vy aṁhaḥ ||
  • idaṁ tyat pātram: This phrase translates to "this is the vessel." In a real-life context, it can be understood as the container or medium through which we receive blessings or knowledge. For example, a teacher can be seen as a vessel of knowledge, imparting wisdom to students.
  • indrapānam: This means "the drink of Indra," referring to the divine nectar or elixir associated with the god Indra, who is often seen as the king of gods and a symbol of strength and power. In modern terms, it can represent the pursuit of excellence and the rewards that come from hard work, akin to achieving a significant milestone in one's career.
  • indrasya priyam: This translates to "beloved of Indra." It signifies something that is cherished or valued. In everyday life, this could relate to relationships or goals that we hold dear, such as family, friendships, or personal aspirations.
  • amṛtam apāyi: This means "the immortal essence has departed." It can symbolize the transient nature of life and the inevitability of change. For instance, it reminds us to cherish moments and relationships, as they may not last forever.
  • matsad yathā: This translates to "as I am." It emphasizes self-awareness and authenticity. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of being true to oneself, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • saumanasāya devaṁ: This means "to the divine with a pleasant mind." It suggests approaching the divine or higher ideals with positivity and goodwill. In practice, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and compassion towards others, fostering a harmonious environment.
  • vy a1smad dveṣo: This translates to "our enmity." It acknowledges the presence of conflict or negativity in relationships. In real life, it serves as a reminder to address and resolve conflicts constructively rather than allowing them to fester.
  • yuyavad vy aṁhaḥ: This means "may we strive together." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity. In a practical sense, this can be applied in team settings, where working together towards a common goal leads to greater success.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of receptivity, the pursuit of excellence, the value of relationships, and the importance of self-awareness and collaboration. Its significance lies in its call to embrace positivity, resolve conflicts, and work together harmoniously. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in personal growth, professional development, and fostering meaningful connections with others.
