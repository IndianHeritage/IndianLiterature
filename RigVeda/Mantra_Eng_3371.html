यत्रोता बाधितेभ्यश्चक्रं कुत्साय युध्यते |मुषाया इन्द्र सूर्यं ||
yatrota bādhitebhyaś cakraṁ kutsāya yudhyate | muṣāya indra sūryam ||
  • Mantra: "yatrota bādhitebhyaś cakraṁ kutsāya yudhyate | muṣāya indra sūryam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatra: where
    • uta: and
    • bādhitebhyaś: from those who are obstructed
    • cakraṁ: the wheel or cycle
    • kutsāya: to the one who is insulted or dishonored
    • yudhyate: fights or struggles
    • muṣāya: for the one who is hidden or concealed
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sūryam: the sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a scenario where a struggle or conflict arises against those who obstruct or hinder progress.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the cycle of life, challenges, and the ongoing nature of conflict.
    • The mention of "kutsāya" indicates that this struggle is often against dishonor or insult, suggesting a fight for dignity and respect.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and power, while "sūryam" (the sun) symbolizes enlightenment and truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, an employee may face obstacles from colleagues that hinder their progress. This mantra can inspire them to confront these challenges with the strength of Indra and the clarity of the sun.
    • In social movements, individuals often fight against systemic injustices that obstruct their rights. The mantra serves as a reminder to stand firm against dishonor and to seek truth and justice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of resilience in the face of adversity. It encourages individuals to confront obstacles and dishonor with strength and clarity.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various life situations, from personal struggles to broader societal issues, reminding us that the fight for dignity and truth is a universal endeavor.
