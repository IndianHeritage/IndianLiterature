देवो देवानाम् असि मित्रो अद्भुतो वसुर वसूनाम् असि चारुर अध्वरे |शर्मन् स्याम तव सप्रथस्तमे 'ग्ने सख्ये मा ऋषामाऽ वयं तव ||
devo devānām asi mitro adbhuto vasur vasūnām asi cārur adhvare | śarman syāma tava saprathastame 'gne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • devo devānām asi: This phrase translates to "You are the God of gods." It emphasizes the supreme nature of the divine, suggesting that there is a hierarchy among deities, and the addressed deity holds the highest position. In real life, this can be likened to the concept of leadership in organizations where a CEO or a leader is seen as the ultimate authority guiding the team.
  • mitro adbhuto: This means "wonderful friend." It highlights the importance of friendship and companionship in our lives. Just as a true friend supports and uplifts us, the divine is portrayed as a friend who provides guidance and assistance. In everyday life, we often rely on friends during challenging times, showcasing the value of strong relationships.
  • vasur vasūnām asi: This translates to "You are the wealth of the wealthy." It signifies that true wealth is not just material but also spiritual and emotional. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how people value experiences and relationships over material possessions, recognizing that true richness comes from within and from connections with others.
  • cārur adhvare: This phrase means "beautiful in the sacrifice." It suggests that the act of giving, whether through rituals or in daily life, is beautiful and noble. In real life, acts of charity and selflessness, such as volunteering or helping those in need, embody this beauty of sacrifice.
  • śarman syāma: This translates to "may we attain happiness." It reflects a universal desire for joy and contentment. In our daily lives, we often seek happiness through various means, such as pursuing passions, building relationships, or achieving personal goals.
  • tava saprathastame 'gne: This means "in your most excellent form, O fire." Fire is often a symbol of transformation and purification. In real life, we can relate this to the transformative experiences we undergo, such as overcoming challenges that lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
  • sakhye mā riṣāmā: This translates to "may we not be harmed in friendship." It emphasizes the importance of protecting and nurturing friendships. In our lives, we often face conflicts or misunderstandings, and this line serves as a reminder to approach relationships with care and understanding.
  • vayaṁ tava: This means "we are yours." It signifies devotion and surrender to the divine. In real life, this can be seen in the way individuals commit to their beliefs, communities, or causes, showcasing loyalty and dedication.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divinity, friendship, wealth, sacrifice, happiness, transformation, protection, and devotion. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. By recognizing the divine in our relationships and actions, we can strive for a more fulfilling and harmonious existence. This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as fostering strong friendships, engaging in selfless acts, and seeking personal growth through challenges, ultimately leading to a richer and more meaningful life.
