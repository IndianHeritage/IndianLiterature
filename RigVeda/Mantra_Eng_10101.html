विष्वे देवासो अध वृष्ण्याणि ते 'वर्धयन सोमवत्या वचस्यया | रद्धं वृतम् अहिम इन्द्रस्य हन्मनाग्निर न जम्बhais तृष्व अन्नम् आवयत ||
viśve devāso adha vṛṣṇyāni te 'vardhayan somavatyā vacasyayā | raddhaṁ vṛtram ahim indrasya hanmanāgnir na jambhais tṛṣv annam āvayat ||
  • viśve devāso: This phrase translates to "all the gods" or "universal deities." It signifies the collective presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • adha vṛṣṇyāni te 'vardhayan: This means "who increase the strength of the Vṛṣṇis." The Vṛṣṇis are a clan or group, often associated with the divine and heroic qualities. This line emphasizes the support and enhancement provided by the gods to those who embody strength and virtue.
  • somavatyā vacasyayā: This translates to "by the words of the one who is rich in soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. This line suggests that the blessings and wisdom of the gods are communicated through sacred words or hymns.
  • raddhaṁ vṛtram ahim indrasya: Here, "raddhaṁ" means "faith" or "trust," and "vṛtram" refers to obstacles or demons. This line indicates that with faith, one can overcome challenges, particularly those represented by Indra, the king of gods, who is known for his strength and ability to conquer adversities.
  • hanmanāgnir na jambhais: This means "may Agni (the fire god) not be disturbed by the demons." Agni represents transformation and purification. This line emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity and focus in the face of distractions or negative influences.
  • tṛṣv annam āvayat: This translates to "may you bring forth food for the thirsty." It signifies the need for sustenance and nourishment, both physical and spiritual, highlighting the importance of providing for oneself and others.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine support, strength, and the importance of faith in overcoming obstacles. It emphasizes the role of the gods in enhancing human capabilities and the necessity of sacred communication through hymns. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance during challenging times, fostering a sense of community and support, and recognizing the importance of spiritual nourishment. It serves as a reminder that with faith and divine assistance, one can navigate life's challenges and achieve personal growth and fulfillment.
