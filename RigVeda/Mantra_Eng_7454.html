पिबतं सोमं मधुमन्तं अश्विनाः बर्हिः सीदतं सुमत् |तावावृधाना उप सुष्टुतिं दिवो गन्तं गौराव इवेरीनम् ||
pibataṁ somam madhumantam aśvinā barhiḥ sīdataṁ sumat | tā vāvṛdhānā upa suṣṭutiṁ divo gantaṁ gaurāv iveriṇam ||
  • pibataṁ somam: This phrase translates to "drink the soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to seeking wisdom through education and experience.
  • madhumantam: Meaning "sweet," this word signifies the pleasant and beneficial aspects of life. In practical terms, it can refer to the joys and rewards that come from hard work and dedication, such as achieving personal goals or enjoying the fruits of one's labor.
  • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. This can be related to the importance of health and well-being in our lives. For example, taking care of one's physical and mental health can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • barhiḥ: This term refers to the sacred grass used in rituals. It symbolizes purity and the connection to the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen as the importance of maintaining a pure and ethical lifestyle, which can lead to spiritual growth.
  • sīdataṁ: This means "to be nourished" or "to be satisfied." It emphasizes the need for sustenance, both physically and spiritually. In real life, this can relate to the importance of nurturing relationships and personal growth.
  • sumat: This word means "good thought" or "wisdom." It highlights the significance of positive thinking and wise decision-making in our lives. For instance, cultivating a mindset focused on gratitude and positivity can lead to better mental health and relationships.
  • tā vāvṛdhānā: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are nourished." It suggests that those who seek knowledge and wisdom will thrive. In practical terms, this can be seen in how continuous learning and self-improvement can lead to success in various aspects of life.
  • upa suṣṭutiṁ: This means "to praise well." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation. In real life, this can be reflected in how acknowledging the efforts of others can strengthen relationships and foster a positive environment.
  • divo gantaṁ: This translates to "the song of the heavens." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine. In everyday life, this can be related to finding inspiration in nature and the universe, which can lead to a deeper understanding of our place in the world.
  • gaurāv iveriṇam: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the shining light." It symbolizes enlightenment and the pursuit of truth. In practical terms, this can be seen in the quest for knowledge and understanding, which can illuminate our paths in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, nurturing relationships, and maintaining a pure and ethical lifestyle. It encourages individuals to pursue wisdom and express gratitude, leading to personal growth and fulfillment. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, health, and interpersonal relationships, ultimately guiding us towards a more meaningful and enriched existence.
