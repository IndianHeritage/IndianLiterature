उप मां पेपीषत तमः कृष्णं व्यक्तम अस्तिता |उष ऋणेव यातय ||
upa mā pepiśat tamaḥ kṛṣṇaṁ vyaktam asthita | uṣa ṛṇeva yātaya ||
  • upa mā pepiśat: This phrase can be translated as "may we be drawn towards the light." The term "upa" means "towards," "mā" signifies "us," and "pepiśat" implies "to be drawn" or "to be attracted." This suggests a yearning for enlightenment and knowledge.
  • tamaḥ: This word means "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the challenges and obstacles that one faces in life, often symbolizing confusion or lack of clarity.
  • kṛṣṇaṁ: This term refers to "the black" or "the dark," often associated with the divine or the ultimate reality. In this context, it can symbolize the deeper truths that lie beneath the surface of our understanding.
  • vyaktam asthita: "vyaktam" means "manifest" or "evident," and "asthita" means "established" or "firm." Together, this phrase suggests that the truth or knowledge we seek is already present and established, waiting to be recognized.
  • uṣa: This word translates to "dawn" or "light." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of knowledge and understanding.
  • ṛṇeva: This term means "like a debt" or "as if bound." It implies a sense of obligation or connection to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • yātaya: This word means "to strive" or "to endeavor." It emphasizes the importance of effort and determination in the quest for understanding and clarity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the journey from darkness to light, from ignorance to knowledge. It encourages individuals to strive towards enlightenment, recognizing that the truth is already present and established within the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal growth, and spiritual development. For instance, students seeking knowledge can reflect on this mantra to motivate themselves to overcome challenges and embrace learning. Similarly, individuals facing personal struggles can use this mantra as a reminder to seek clarity and understanding in their lives. Ultimately, it serves as a guiding principle for anyone on a quest for truth and enlightenment.
