उक्तवाहसे विभवे मनिषां द्रुणा न पारम् ईरयाऽनदीनाम् |न स्पृश धियā तन्वी श्रुतस्य जुष्टतरस्य कुविद अङ्ग वेदत ||
ukthavāhase vibhve manīṣāṁ druṇā na pāram īrayā nadīnām | ni spṛśa dhiyā tanvi śrutasya juṣṭatarasya kuvid aṅga vedat ||
  • ukthavāhase: This term can be broken down into "uktha" meaning "sacred utterance" or "mantra" and "vāhase" meaning "to carry" or "to convey." It signifies the importance of spoken words and their power in the spiritual realm.
  • vibhve: This word translates to "in the vastness" or "in the expanse," indicating the limitless nature of the universe and the divine.
  • manīṣāṁ: This refers to "intellect" or "thought," emphasizing the role of wisdom and understanding in our lives.
  • druṇā: Meaning "with strength" or "with firmness," it suggests the need for resilience and determination in our pursuits.
  • na pāram: This translates to "not beyond," indicating that the goals we seek are attainable and within our reach.
  • īrayā: This word means "by the flow" or "by the current," symbolizing the natural flow of life and the importance of going with the current rather than against it.
  • nadīnām: This translates to "of the rivers," which can be seen as a metaphor for the continuous flow of life and experiences.
  • ni spṛśa: Meaning "do not touch," it suggests a sense of caution or mindfulness in our actions and thoughts.
  • dhiyā: This refers to "intellect" or "understanding," reinforcing the importance of wisdom in decision-making.
  • tanvi: This means "delicate" or "subtle," indicating the fragility of life and the need to handle situations with care.
  • śrutasya: This translates to "of the heard" or "of the knowledge," emphasizing the value of learning and wisdom gained through experience.
  • juṣṭatarasya: Meaning "more pleasing" or "more desirable," it suggests that the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom leads to greater fulfillment.
  • kuvid: This term means "of the unwise" or "of the ignorant," serving as a reminder to avoid ignorance and seek knowledge.
  • aṅga: This translates to "part" or "aspect," indicating that every part of our being is interconnected.
  • vedat: This refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of learning and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, resilience, and the natural flow of life. It teaches us to be mindful in our actions and to seek knowledge while remaining grounded in our pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various aspects such as education, personal growth, and decision-making. By understanding the interconnectedness of our thoughts and actions, we can navigate life's challenges with greater clarity and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder that our goals are within reach if we approach them with wisdom and determination.
