शतं दासे बल्बूथे विप्रस तरुक्षा आ ददे |ते ते वायव इमे जनाः मदन्तीन्द्रगोपाः मदन्ति देवगोपाः ||
śataṁ dāse balbūthe vipras tarukṣa ā dade | te te vāyav ime janā madantīndragopā madanti devagopāḥ ||
  • śataṁ dāse balbūthe vipras tarukṣa ā dade
    • śataṁ: This word means "hundred" or "many," indicating abundance or a large number.
    • dāse: Refers to "servants" or "those who serve," suggesting a community or group of people dedicated to a cause.
    • balbūthe: This term can be interpreted as "strength" or "power," emphasizing the importance of strength in service.
    • vipras: This word means "wise" or "learned," referring to those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
    • tarukṣa: This can be translated as "trees" or "plants," symbolizing nature and its importance in life.
    • ā dade: This phrase means "to give" or "to bestow," indicating an act of generosity or sharing.
  • te te vāyav ime janā madantīndragopā madanti devagopāḥ
    • te te: This phrase means "these" or "those," referring to specific groups or entities.
    • vāyav: This word translates to "winds," symbolizing movement, change, and the unseen forces of nature.
    • ime: This means "these" or "this," indicating a focus on the present or immediate context.
    • janā: Refers to "people" or "beings," emphasizing the collective aspect of humanity.
    • madantīndragopā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are joyful and protectors," suggesting guardianship and happiness.
    • madanti: This means "they rejoice" or "they are happy," indicating a state of joy or celebration.
    • devagopāḥ: This translates to "divine protectors," referring to higher powers or deities that safeguard and guide humanity.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of community, strength, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It highlights the role of learned individuals in guiding and protecting the community, akin to how trees provide shelter and sustenance. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as:

  • Community service initiatives where knowledgeable individuals lead efforts to support and uplift those in need.
  • Environmental conservation efforts that recognize the importance of nature and advocate for its protection.
  • Spiritual or religious gatherings that celebrate joy and foster a sense of unity among people.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the collective responsibility to nurture and protect both our communities and the environment, encouraging a harmonious existence with nature and each other.
