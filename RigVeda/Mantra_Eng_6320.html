मन्त्र: उतो पातिर य उच्यते कृष्णीनाम एक इद वशी | नमोवृद्धैर अवस्युभिः सुते रण ||
uto patir ya ucyate kṛṣṭīnām eka id vaśī | namovṛdhair avasyubhiḥ sute raṇa ||
  • Mantra: "uto patir ya ucyate kṛṣṭīnām eka id vaśī | namovṛdhair avasyubhiḥ sute raṇa ||"
  • Translation: "He who is called the Lord of the fields, the one who is singular and all-powerful, to whom we offer our salutations, may he grant us strength in battle."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • uto: a particle indicating a connection or relationship, often translated as "therefore" or "thus."
    • patir: means "lord" or "master," referring to a figure of authority or power.
    • ya: translates to "who," indicating a subject or a person being referred to.
    • ucyate: means "is called" or "is named," suggesting recognition or acknowledgment.
    • kṛṣṭīnām: refers to "of the fields," indicating a connection to agriculture or land.
    • eka: means "one" or "singular," emphasizing uniqueness or singularity.
    • id: translates to "here" or "now," indicating presence or immediacy.
    • vaśī: means "powerful" or "controlling," suggesting authority and strength.
    • namovṛdhair: translates to "to whom we offer our salutations," indicating respect and reverence.
    • avasyubhiḥ: means "with strength" or "with power," indicating a request for strength.
    • sute: translates to "in battle," indicating a context of conflict or challenge.
    • raṇa: means "battle" or "war," emphasizing the theme of struggle and conflict.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the blessings of the land and its deities to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine support in their efforts.
    • In competitive sports, athletes may invoke strength and power before a match, similar to how this mantra seeks strength in battle.
    • In leadership, individuals may refer to this mantra when seeking guidance and strength to lead their teams effectively, especially in challenging situations.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength and authority, particularly in the context of challenges and conflicts. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing a higher power that can provide support and guidance. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as agriculture, sports, and leadership, where individuals seek strength and direction to overcome obstacles and achieve success. By acknowledging a higher power, one can cultivate a sense of purpose and resilience in the face of adversity.
