शुष्मी शर्धो न मारुतं पवस्वानभिशस्तā दिव्यā यथा विट् |आपो न मक्षू सुमतिर भवā नः सहस्राप्साः पृतनाशाण न यज्ञः ||
śuṣmī śardho na mārutam pavasvānabhiśastā divyā yathā viṭ | āpo na makṣū sumatir bhavā naḥ sahasrāpsāḥ pṛtanāṣāṇ na yajñaḥ ||
  • śuṣmī: This word can be interpreted as "radiant" or "brilliant." It signifies the brilliance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • śardho: Refers to "the season" or "the time." It indicates the importance of timing in our actions and decisions.
  • na mārutam: "Mārut" refers to the wind or air, often associated with the divine forces of nature. This phrase suggests a call for the winds to be favorable and supportive.
  • pavasvānabhiśastā: This can be translated as "may the winds blow favorably." It is a plea for positive energy and support from nature.
  • divyā: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this word emphasizes the connection to higher powers and spiritual realms.
  • yathā viṭ: This phrase can be understood as "as the earth." It signifies grounding and stability, reminding us of our roots.
  • āpo: Refers to "waters," symbolizing nourishment, life, and purification.
  • na makṣū: "Makṣū" means "to be free from obstacles." This phrase expresses a desire for a clear path in life.
  • sumatir bhavā naḥ: This translates to "may our thoughts be auspicious." It emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and intentions.
  • sahasrāpsāḥ: Meaning "thousand waters," this symbolizes abundance and the richness of life.
  • pṛtanāṣāṇ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth," indicating the act of creation and manifestation.
  • na yajñaḥ: "Yajña" refers to sacrifice or offerings, often in the context of rituals. This phrase suggests the importance of our actions and contributions to the greater good.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divine forces, and human intentions. It calls for favorable conditions in life, urging us to align our thoughts and actions with positive energies. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important decisions, rituals, or when seeking clarity and support in challenging situations. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance, positivity, and harmony with the natural world, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
