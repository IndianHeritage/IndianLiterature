अध यद इमे पवमान रोदसी इमा च विश्वा भुवनाभि मजनना |यूथे न निष्ठा वृषभो वि तिष्ठसे ||
adha yad ime pavamāna rodasī imā ca viśvā bhuvanābhi majmanā | yūthe na niṣṭhā vṛṣabho vi tiṣṭhase ||
  • Adha yad ime pavamāna rodasī: This phrase can be translated to "Here, where the purifying (pavamāna) heavens (rodasī) are." The term "pavamāna" refers to the purifying or cleansing aspect, often associated with the wind or breath, symbolizing the vital force that sustains life.
  • Imā ca viśvā bhuvanābhi majmanā: This translates to "And all these worlds (viśvā bhuvanā) are created (majmanā)." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all realms of existence, suggesting that everything is part of a larger cosmic order.
  • Yūthe na niṣṭhā vṛṣabho vi tiṣṭhase: This means "In the assembly (yūthe), you (vṛṣabha) stand firm (tiṣṭhase)." The term "vṛṣabha" often refers to a bull, symbolizing strength and stability. This line suggests the importance of standing firm in unity and strength within a community or group.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the themes of purification, creation, and the importance of community. It reflects the belief that the universe is interconnected and that individual actions contribute to the greater whole.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, where the idea of purification can relate to efforts to clean and protect our planet. It also resonates in community building, where individuals come together to support one another, emphasizing the strength found in unity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards the environment and each other. It encourages us to recognize our place within the larger cosmos and to act with intention and integrity, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
