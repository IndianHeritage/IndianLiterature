स जायतामः परम व्योमन वायुर न पाथः परि पाशी सद्यः |त्वं भुवना जनयन्न अभि क्रन्न अपत्याय जातवेदो दशस्यन् ||
sa jāyamānaḥ parame vyoman vāyur na pāthaḥ pari pāsi sadyaḥ | tvam bhuvanā janayann abhi krann apatyāya jātavedo daśasyan ||
  • sa jāyamānaḥ: This phrase translates to "being born" or "coming into existence." It signifies the beginning of life or the emergence of new entities.
  • parame vyoman: This means "in the supreme sky" or "in the highest realm." It refers to the ultimate reality or the divine space where creation occurs.
  • vāyur na pāthaḥ: This translates to "the wind does not obstruct." It suggests that the forces of nature, like the wind, are free and unobstructed, allowing for the flow of life and energy.
  • pari pāsi sadyaḥ: This means "protect immediately." It emphasizes the need for immediate protection and support for new life as it emerges.
  • tvam bhuvanā: This translates to "you, the creator of the worlds." It acknowledges a divine force or deity responsible for the creation and sustenance of the universe.
  • janayann abhi krann: This means "giving birth and nurturing." It highlights the nurturing aspect of creation, where life is not only born but also cared for.
  • apatyāya: This translates to "for the offspring." It signifies the focus on the next generation and the importance of ensuring their well-being.
  • jātavedo: This refers to "the one who knows the birth." It is often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment regarding creation.
  • daśasyan: This means "of the ten." It can refer to the ten directions or the completeness of creation, encompassing all aspects of existence.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the themes of creation, protection, and nurturing of life. It highlights the divine role in the birth and sustenance of all beings, urging for immediate support and care for new life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various contexts such as parenting, community support, and environmental stewardship. Just as the mantra calls for the protection of new life, it reminds us of our responsibility to nurture and safeguard the future generations and the world we inhabit. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of care, responsibility, and the interconnectedness of all life forms.
