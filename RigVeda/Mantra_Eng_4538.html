उद अग्नि भारत द्युमद अजस्रेण दविद्युतत् |शोचावि भाह्य अजरा ||
ud agne bhārata dyumad ajasreṇa davidyutat | śocā vi bhāhy ajara ||
  • ud agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," where Agni represents the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a symbol of energy and transformation.
  • bhārata: This term refers to the people of Bharat (India) and signifies a sense of unity and collective identity among the inhabitants of the land.
  • dyumad: Meaning "brilliant" or "radiant," this word emphasizes the illuminating and enlightening qualities of Agni, suggesting that fire brings clarity and understanding.
  • ajasreṇa: This can be interpreted as "with unending strength" or "everlasting," indicating the eternal nature of fire and its role in sustaining life and energy.
  • davidyutat: This term means "shining" or "brilliant," reinforcing the idea of Agni as a source of light and guidance.
  • śocā: This word translates to "to shine" or "to illuminate," further emphasizing the enlightening aspect of Agni.
  • vi bhāhy: This phrase can be understood as "may we be protected" or "may we be guided," indicating a plea for safety and guidance through the light of Agni.
  • ajara: Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," this word highlights the timeless nature of the divine and the enduring qualities of fire.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his radiant and eternal light to guide and protect the people of Bharat. It reflects a deep reverence for the transformative power of fire, which is not only a physical element but also a metaphor for knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual illumination.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as fire transforms raw materials into something useful, individuals can seek the light of knowledge and wisdom to transform their lives and achieve personal growth.
  • Community Unity: The reference to "bhārata" emphasizes the importance of unity among people. In modern society, this can inspire collective efforts towards social harmony and cooperation.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be recited during rituals or meditation to invoke the protective and illuminating qualities of Agni, fostering a sense of peace and clarity.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of seeking guidance and protection through the illuminating power of Agni. It serves as a reminder of the transformative potential of knowledge and unity among people, applicable in personal, communal, and spiritual contexts.
