उत वा यस्य वाजिनोऽनु विप्रम अतक्षत |स गन्ता गोमती व्रजे ||
uta vā yasya vājino 'nu vipram atakṣata | sa gantā gomati vraje ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta vā: or indeed
    • yasya: whose
    • vājino: of the one who possesses strength or energy
    • anu: following
    • vipram: the wise or learned one
    • atakṣata: did not abandon or forsake
    • sa: he
    • gantā: who goes or travels
    • gomati: with wealth or prosperity
    • vraje: in the pasture or field
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between strength, wisdom, and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes that a person who possesses strength and follows the wise will not be forsaken.
    • The imagery of traveling in a prosperous field suggests a journey towards success and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who is strong in skills and follows the guidance of a mentor (the wise) is likely to succeed and not be overlooked in their career.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom from experienced elders often find themselves in better positions to achieve their goals.
    • Communities that support and uplift their members through shared knowledge and strength tend to thrive and prosper.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, wisdom, and community support in achieving prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal development, professional growth, and community building.
    • By recognizing the value of wisdom and strength, individuals and communities can navigate challenges and reach their goals more effectively.
