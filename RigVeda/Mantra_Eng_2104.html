इन्द्रो महां सिंधुम आशयानम् मायाविनं वृत्रम अस्फुरन् निः |अरेजेतां रोधसी भियाने कनिक्रदतो वृष्णो अस्य वज्रात ||
indro mahāṁ sindhum āśayānam māyāvinaṁ vṛtram asphuran niḥ | arejetāṁ rodasī bhiyāne kanikradato vṛṣṇo asya vajrāt ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Mahāṁ: Means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the grandeur of Indra.
  • Sindhum: Translates to "ocean" or "river," representing vastness and abundance.
  • Āśayānam: Means "one who resides" or "abode," indicating a place of safety or refuge.
  • Māyāvinaṁ: Refers to "the one who creates illusions" or "the magician," highlighting the deceptive nature of reality.
  • Vṛtram: Represents "the demon" or "obstacle," symbolizing challenges or adversities in life.
  • Asphuran: Means "to strike" or "to attack," indicating the action of overcoming obstacles.
  • Niḥ: Translates to "without" or "free from," suggesting liberation from challenges.
  • Arejetāṁ: Means "to be victorious" or "to conquer," emphasizing triumph over difficulties.
  • Rodasī: Refers to "the heavens" or "the sky," symbolizing the divine realm.
  • Bhiyāne: Means "with fear" or "in fear," indicating the presence of challenges that invoke fear.
  • Kanikradato: Refers to "the one who roars" or "the powerful," symbolizing strength and courage.
  • Vṛṣṇo: Refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," often associated with the divine.
  • Asya: Means "of this" or "of him," indicating possession or belonging.
  • Vajrāt: Refers to "from the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength and the ability to overcome challenges.

This mantra speaks to the themes of strength, overcoming obstacles, and the power of the divine. It invokes Indra, the mighty king of the gods, who is called upon to help conquer the challenges represented by Vṛtra, a demon symbolizing obstacles and fears. The imagery of the ocean and the heavens suggests a vastness of possibilities and the divine support available to those who seek it.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios where individuals face significant challenges or fears. For instance, a student preparing for a crucial exam may feel overwhelmed. By invoking the spirit of Indra through this mantra, they can find the strength and courage to overcome their fears and perform to the best of their abilities. Similarly, in a business context, an entrepreneur facing market challenges can draw inspiration from this mantra to confront and conquer obstacles with confidence.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire strength and resilience in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, individuals can summon their inner power to overcome challenges and fears. By embracing this mindset, one can navigate life's difficulties with courage and determination, ultimately leading to success and fulfillment.
