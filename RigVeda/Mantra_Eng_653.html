सो अर्णवो न नद्यः समुद्रियः प्रति गृभ्णाति विश्रिता वरिमभिः |इन्द्रः सोमस्य पीतये वृषायते सनात् स युध्म ओजसा पनस्यते ||
so arṇavo na nadyaḥ samudriyaḥ prati gṛbhṇāti viśritā varīmabhiḥ | indraḥ somasya pītaye vṛṣāyate sanāt sa yudhma ojasā panasyate ||
  • so arṇavo na nadyaḥ - This phrase can be translated as "the vast ocean and rivers." Here, the ocean symbolizes the depth of knowledge and the rivers represent the flow of life and experiences.
  • samudriyaḥ prati gṛbhṇāti - This means "the ocean receives." It signifies the idea of acceptance and the gathering of experiences, much like how the ocean collects water from various rivers.
  • viśritā varīmabhiḥ - Translated as "with the abundant resources." This refers to the richness of life and the resources we gather through our experiences and knowledge.
  • indraḥ somasya pītaye - "Indra, for the drinking of Soma." Indra is a deity associated with strength and power, and Soma represents the essence of life and vitality. This line emphasizes the importance of strength derived from life's experiences.
  • vṛṣāyate sanāt - This translates to "is ever powerful." It suggests that the strength gained from experiences is eternal and enduring.
  • sa yudhma ojasā panasyate - Meaning "he who fights with vigor." This line highlights the importance of actively engaging in life and utilizing one's strength and resources to overcome challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of gathering experiences and knowledge, much like how rivers flow into the ocean. It teaches us that life is a collection of experiences that enrich us, and through these experiences, we gain strength and vitality. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, where one learns from challenges and uses that knowledge to face future obstacles with vigor. The mantra encourages us to embrace our experiences, accept the resources life offers, and actively engage in our journey, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and empowered existence.
