दूरे तन् नाम गुह्यं पराचैर यत् त्वा भीते अह्वयेतां वयोद्धै |उद अस्तभ्नाः पृथिवीं द्यां अभीक भ्रातुः पुत्रान मघवन तित्विषाणः ||
dūre tan nāma guhyam parācair yat tvā bhīte ahvayetāṁ vayodhai | ud astabhnāḥ pṛthivīṁ dyām abhīke bhrātuḥ putrān maghavan titviṣāṇaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Far away is the name of the secret, which the wise invoke when they are afraid. May the winds, the earth, and the sky protect the sons of the brother, O Maghavan, with your brilliance."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dūre: Far away
    • tan: That
    • nāma: Name
    • guhyam: Secret
    • parācair: By the wise or learned
    • yat: Which
    • tvā: You
    • bhīte: When afraid
    • ahvayetāṁ: Invoke
    • vayodhai: With the winds
    • ud: Up
    • astabhnāḥ: Protect
    • pṛthivīṁ: The earth
    • dyām: The sky
    • abhīke: O protector
    • bhrātuḥ: Of the brother
    • putrān: Sons
    • maghavan: O Maghavan (a name for Indra)
    • titviṣāṇaḥ: With your brilliance
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty or fear, individuals often seek guidance or protection from higher powers, much like how the mantra invokes the divine for safety.
    • In a corporate setting, when facing challenges, teams may come together to brainstorm solutions, invoking collective wisdom to overcome fears of failure.
    • Parents often pray for the safety and well-being of their children, reflecting the essence of invoking protection for loved ones.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection and guidance during fearful times, highlighting the role of wisdom and collective strength.
    • It can be applied in various life scenarios, such as during personal crises, natural disasters, or any situation where one feels vulnerable.
    • The invocation of divine protection serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the support available from the universe.
