जुष्टī नरो ब्रह्मणा वः पितृणाम अक्षम् अव्ययम् न किलā ऋषाथ |यच् चक्वरीषु बृहता रवेणेंद्रे शुष्मम् अदधात वसिष्ठाः ||
juṣṭī naro brahmaṇā vaḥ pitṝṇām akṣam avyayaṁ na kilā riṣātha | yac chakvarīṣu bṛhatā raveṇendre śuṣmam adadhātā vasiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • juṣṭī naro: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is pleasing to men" or "the one who is agreeable to people." It emphasizes the importance of being amiable and fostering good relationships with others.
  • brahmaṇā vaḥ: This translates to "by the divine" or "through the sacred." It suggests that the actions and relationships we cultivate should be guided by a higher moral or spiritual principle.
  • pitṝṇām: This word refers to "ancestors" or "forefathers." It highlights the significance of honoring and remembering those who came before us, acknowledging their contributions and wisdom.
  • akṣam avyayaṁ: This can be understood as "the imperishable and eternal." It signifies the enduring nature of certain values and principles that transcend time and remain relevant.
  • na kilā riṣātha: This phrase means "there is no doubt" or "certainly not." It conveys a sense of certainty and assurance in the truths being expressed.
  • yac chakvarīṣu: This translates to "what is in the vastness" or "what exists in the universe." It suggests a connection to the larger cosmos and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • bṛhatā raveṇendre: This can be interpreted as "the great light of the sun" or "the powerful radiance." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the illumination that comes from understanding.
  • šuṣmam adadhātā: This phrase means "who has given life" or "who has provided sustenance." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of existence and the importance of providing for others.
  • vasiṣṭhāḥ: This refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge in guiding our actions and decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about human relationships, the importance of honoring our ancestors, and the pursuit of eternal values. It encourages us to cultivate amiable relationships guided by divine principles, to honor the wisdom of those who came before us, and to seek enlightenment and knowledge. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering community bonds, practicing gratitude towards our forebears, and striving for personal growth through learning and wisdom. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious and meaningful existence that resonates with the eternal truths of life.
