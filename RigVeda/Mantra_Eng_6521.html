ईळिष्वा हि प्रतिव्यं यजस्व जातवेदसम् |चरिष्णुधूमम् अगृभीतशोचिषम् ||
īḻiṣvā hi pratīvya1ṁ yajasva jātavedasam | cariṣṇudhūmam agṛbhītaśociṣam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • īḻiṣvā: This term can be interpreted as "to be worshipped" or "to be revered." It signifies the importance of devotion and respect towards the divine.
    • hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding word, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • pratīvyaṁ: This can mean "in the world" or "in the realm of existence," indicating the physical and spiritual dimensions of life.
    • yajasva: This is a command form meaning "perform sacrifice" or "offer worship," highlighting the act of devotion and ritual.
    • jātavedasam: Refers to "the one who knows all births," often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge, transformation, and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • cariṣṇu: This term means "moving" or "wandering," suggesting the dynamic nature of life and existence.
    • dhūmam: Translates to "smoke," which can symbolize the offerings made to the divine, representing the connection between the earthly and the divine through rituals.
    • agṛbhīta: Means "not to be grasped" or "ungraspable," indicating the transcendental nature of the divine and the ultimate reality.
    • śociṣam: This term means "radiant" or "illuminating," suggesting the purity and brilliance of the divine presence.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of worship and reverence towards the divine, particularly through the figure of Agni, who represents knowledge and transformation.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in rituals and sacrifices, which are seen as a means to connect with the divine and seek blessings.
    • The mention of "smoke" signifies the offerings made during rituals, which serve as a bridge between the material world and the spiritual realm.
    • The phrase "not to be grasped" highlights the idea that the ultimate reality or divine essence transcends human understanding and cannot be confined to physical forms.
    • The mantra calls for a dynamic engagement with life, suggesting that one should actively participate in the world while maintaining a connection to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in the practice of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or their inner selves through rituals and focused intention.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, embody the essence of this mantra by bringing people together to perform collective worship and celebrate the divine.
    • Acts of service and charity can also be seen as a form of worship, where individuals offer their time and resources to help others, thus honoring the divine presence in all beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of devotion, ritual, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage actively with the world while maintaining a spiritual connection, highlighting the balance between the material and spiritual realms.
    • In essence, it teaches that through worship and reverence, one can attain a deeper understanding of existence and the divine, leading to personal transformation and enlightenment.
