मन्त्र: एन्दो पार्थिवं रयिं दिव्यम् पवस्व धारणया | द्युमन्तं शुष्मं आ भर ||
endo pārthivaṁ rayiṁ divyam pavasva dhārayā | dyumantaṁ śuṣmam ā bhara ||
  • Endo: This word can be interpreted as "from within" or "internal." It signifies the importance of inner strength and resources.
  • Pārthivaṁ: This translates to "earthly" or "material." It refers to the physical world and the resources it provides.
  • Rayiṁ: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It encompasses not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • Divyam: This word means "divine" or "heavenly." It suggests a connection to higher powers or spiritual abundance.
  • Pavasva: This translates to "may you purify" or "may you cleanse." It indicates a desire for purification, both physically and spiritually.
  • Dhārayā: This means "to hold" or "to sustain." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining and nurturing what one possesses.
  • Dyumantaṁ: This word means "radiant" or "brilliant." It signifies the illumination that comes from knowledge and wisdom.
  • Śuṣmam: This translates to "dry" or "barren." It can refer to a state of lack or emptiness that needs to be addressed.
  • Ā bhara: This means "may you fill" or "may you bring forth." It expresses a wish for abundance and fulfillment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for the inner and outer wealth to be purified and sustained, leading to a radiant and fulfilling life. It emphasizes the balance between material and spiritual prosperity, urging individuals to seek both inner strength and external resources.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their inner strengths and resources, seeking to cultivate them while also pursuing material goals.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to earthly wealth can inspire people to take care of the environment, ensuring that natural resources are sustained for future generations.
  • Spiritual Growth: The divine aspect encourages individuals to connect with their spiritual side, seeking wisdom and enlightenment.
  • Community Support: The mantra can inspire collective efforts to purify and sustain the community's resources, promoting shared prosperity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of inner and outer wealth, urging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives. It highlights the significance of purification and sustainability, making it applicable in personal, environmental, and communal contexts.
