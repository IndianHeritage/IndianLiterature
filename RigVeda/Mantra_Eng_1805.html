पान्ति मित्रावरुणाव अवद्याच चयत इम आर्यमो अप्रशस्तान् |उत च्यवन्ते अच्युताः ध्रुवाणि वावृद्ध इम मरुतो दातिवारः ||
pānti mitrāvaruṇāv avadyāc cayata īm aryamo apraśastān | uta cyavante acyutā dhruvāṇi vāvṛdha īm maruto dātivāraḥ ||
  • pānti: This word can be interpreted as "to protect" or "to nourish." In real life, this can be seen in the way parents nurture their children, ensuring their safety and well-being.
  • mitrāvaruṇāv: This refers to the duality of Mitra and Varuna, representing friendship and cosmic order. In everyday life, this can be likened to the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships and upholding ethical standards in society.
  • avadyāc: This term means "without fault" or "pure." It emphasizes the significance of integrity and moral character in our actions, akin to how a trustworthy friend or colleague is valued in personal and professional relationships.
  • cayata: This word suggests "to gather" or "to assemble." In a practical context, it can relate to teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as in community service projects.
  • īm: This is a call for attention or invocation, often used to seek blessings or guidance. In real life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek divine support for their endeavors.
  • aryamo: This term can be interpreted as "noble" or "worthy." It highlights the importance of striving for excellence and virtue in our pursuits, similar to how leaders are expected to embody integrity and inspire others.
  • apraśastān: This means "those who are not praised" or "unworthy." It serves as a reminder to be cautious of negative influences or actions that do not align with our values, much like avoiding toxic relationships.
  • uta: This word means "or" and serves as a conjunction, indicating choices or alternatives. In life, we often face decisions that require careful consideration of our options.
  • cyavante: This term means "to fall away" or "to deviate." It can be related to the idea of straying from one's path or values, akin to how individuals may face temptations that lead them astray.
  • acyutā: This means "infallible" or "eternal." It signifies the pursuit of lasting truths and principles, much like the quest for knowledge and wisdom that transcends time.
  • dhruvāṇi: This term refers to "fixed" or "stable." It emphasizes the importance of having a strong foundation in life, such as building a stable career or nurturing lasting relationships.
  • vāvṛdha: This means "to grow" or "to increase." It can be related to personal development and the continuous pursuit of growth in various aspects of life, such as education or self-improvement.
  • īm: Again, this is a call for attention or invocation, reinforcing the need for awareness and mindfulness in our actions.
  • maruto: This refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with strength and power. In real life, this can symbolize the challenges and adversities we face, which can also lead to growth and resilience.
  • dātivāraḥ: This means "the giver of gifts" or "generous." It highlights the importance of generosity and sharing in our communities, akin to how charitable acts can uplift those in need.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, integrity, and collaboration in our lives. It serves as a reminder to uphold noble values, make wise choices, and strive for personal growth while being mindful of our actions and their impact on others. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide us in building strong relationships, fostering community spirit, and navigating the challenges we encounter, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
