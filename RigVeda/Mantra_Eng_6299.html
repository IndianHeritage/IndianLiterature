महीर अस्य प्रणीतयः पूर्वीर उता प्रशस्तयः |विश्वा वसूनि दाशुषे व्य आनशुः ||
mahīr asya praṇītayaḥ pūrvīr uta praśastayaḥ | viśvā vasūni dāśuṣe vy ānaśuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahīr: great, vast, or powerful
    • asya: of this
    • praṇītayaḥ: those who have been led or guided
    • pūrvīr: by the ancients or predecessors
    • uta: or
    • praśastayaḥ: those who are praised or honored
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • vasūni: wealth or good things
    • dāśuṣe: for the one who serves or gives
    • vy: indeed
    • ānaśuḥ: may they come or arrive
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness and vastness of the guidance provided by the ancients.
    • It emphasizes the importance of honoring those who have come before us and the wisdom they impart.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of universal wealth and prosperity that is available to those who serve and give selflessly.
    • It suggests that by following the path laid out by our predecessors, we can attract abundance and good fortune into our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often look to the strategies and practices of successful predecessors to guide their decisions, ensuring that they build on a foundation of proven success.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek mentorship from those who have achieved what they aspire to, learning from their experiences and insights.
    • Communities that honor their traditions and the wisdom of elders often find greater cohesion and prosperity, as they draw on the collective knowledge of their history.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting and learning from the past.
    • It encourages us to be open to the guidance of those who have come before us, recognizing that their experiences can lead us to greater success and fulfillment.
    • In a world that often values innovation over tradition, this mantra calls for a balance, suggesting that true progress is built on the wisdom of the past.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that by serving others and giving generously, we can attract abundance and prosperity into our lives.
