एष स्तोमो मारुतं शर्धो अच् छा रुद्रस्य सूनूम् युवन्यूम् उद अश्याः |कामो राये हवते मा स्वस्त्य उप स्तुहि पृषदश्वाम् अयासः ||
eṣa stomo mārutaṁ śardho acchā rudrasya sūnūm̐r yuvanyūm̐r ud aśyāḥ | kāmo rāye havate mā svasty upa stuhi pṛṣadaśvām̐ ayāsaḥ ||
  • eṣa stomo mārutaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "this hymn is for the Maruts," referring to the storm deities in Vedic literature. The Maruts are often associated with the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds.
  • śardho acchā rudrasya: This means "the powerful Rudra," where Rudra is a form of Shiva, representing destruction and transformation. The term "śardho" signifies strength and might.
  • sūnūm̐r yuvanyūm̐r ud aśyāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the youthful sons of Rudra," indicating the Maruts as the offspring of Rudra, embodying vitality and energy.
  • kāmo rāye havate: This translates to "may he who desires wealth and prosperity," suggesting a prayer for abundance and success in life.
  • mā svasty upa stuhi: This means "may we not be deprived of well-being," emphasizing the importance of health and prosperity in one's life.
  • pṛṣadaśvām̐ ayāsaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the swift steeds of the divine," symbolizing the speed and agility of divine forces that aid in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a prayer for strength, prosperity, and well-being, invoking the Maruts and Rudra for their blessings. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as during times of uncertainty or when seeking success in endeavors. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra before starting a new business or project, hoping to attract positive energy and divine support. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of nature, divine forces, and human aspirations, encouraging individuals to seek harmony and strength in their pursuits.
