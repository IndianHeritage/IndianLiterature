इयम् मनीषा इयम् अश्विनाः गीर इमां सुवृक्तिं वृषणा जुषेथाम् |इमा ब्रह्माणि युवयून्य अग्मन यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyam manīṣā iyam aśvinā gīr imāṁ suvṛktiṁ vṛṣaṇā juṣethām | imā brahmāṇi yuvayūny agman yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • iyam manīṣā: This phrase translates to "this is the thought" or "this is the intention." It emphasizes the importance of clarity in thought and purpose.
  • iyam aśvinā gīr: Referring to the "Aśvins," the divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, this part signifies the invocation of divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • imāṁ suvṛktiṁ vṛṣaṇā juṣethām: This means "may we enjoy this good thought." It highlights the aspiration for positive and constructive thinking, which can lead to beneficial actions.
  • imā brahmāṇi yuvayūny agman: This translates to "these thoughts are the Brahman, the ultimate reality." It suggests that our thoughts and intentions are connected to the universal truth.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you protect us always with auspiciousness." It is a plea for divine protection and blessings in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of positive thoughts and divine guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as decision-making, leadership, and personal development. For instance, when faced with challenges, invoking positive thoughts and seeking guidance can lead to better outcomes. The mantra serves as a reminder that our intentions shape our reality, and by aligning our thoughts with higher ideals, we can navigate life with purpose and receive blessings for our endeavors.
