एष स्य सोमः पवते सहस्रजिद धिन्वानो वाचम् इषिराम उषर्बुधम् |इन्दुः समुद्रम उद इयर्ति वायुभिर इन्द्रस्य हार्दि कलशेषु सीदति ||
eṣa sya somaḥ pavate sahasrajid dhinvāno vācam iṣirām uṣarbudham | induḥ samudram ud iyarti vāyubhir endrasya hārdi kalaśeṣu sīdati ||
  • eṣa sya somaḥ pavate: This phrase refers to the essence of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, which is believed to purify and invigorate. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of consuming nourishing foods and drinks that enhance our physical and mental well-being.
  • sahasrajid dhinvāno vācam: This part speaks of the power of speech and communication, suggesting that the one who has a thousand voices can express profound truths. In modern contexts, this can relate to the influence of social media and communication platforms where diverse voices can be amplified.
  • iṣirām uṣarbudham: This phrase can be interpreted as the swift and powerful nature of thoughts and intentions. In everyday life, this can be seen in how quickly ideas can spread and impact society, such as through viral trends or movements.
  • induḥ samudram ud iyarti: Here, 'Indu' refers to the moon or a source of light, and 'samudram' means ocean. This symbolizes the vastness of knowledge and wisdom that one can access. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of education and the quest for knowledge in various fields.
  • vāyubhir endrasya hārdi kalaśeṣu sīdati: This line suggests that the essence of divine energy resides in the hearts of beings, akin to how the wind (vāyu) carries life. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing our inner selves and connecting with our spiritual essence, which can lead to a more fulfilling life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, communication, thought, knowledge, and inner divinity. In our daily lives, it encourages us to seek nourishment for our bodies and minds, express ourselves clearly, harness the power of our thoughts, pursue knowledge, and connect with our inner selves. By embodying these principles, we can lead more meaningful and impactful lives, contributing positively to our communities and the world at large.
