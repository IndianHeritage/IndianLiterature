तवे विश्वा सरस्वति श्रितायूषि देव्यम् |शुनहोत्रेषु मत्स्व प्रजां देवी दिदिद्धि नः ||
tve viśvā sarasvati śritāyūṁṣi devyām | śunahotreṣu matsva prajāṁ devi didiḍḍhi naḥ ||
  • tve viśvā sarasvati: This phrase can be translated as "to you, O all-encompassing Sarasvati." Here, Sarasvati is revered as the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and the importance of knowledge in our daily lives.
  • śritāyūṁṣi devyām: This means "who is the source of life and vitality." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of Sarasvati, akin to how a teacher or mentor provides guidance and support to students. For example, a teacher who inspires students to learn and grow embodies this nurturing quality.
  • śunahotreṣu matsva: This translates to "in the offerings to the Sun and the sacred fire." It signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture, which can be likened to modern practices of gratitude and respect towards nature and the universe. For instance, celebrating Earth Day can be seen as a contemporary offering to nature.
  • prajāṁ devi didiḍḍhi naḥ: This means "O goddess, may you grant us progeny." It reflects the desire for prosperity and the continuation of life. In real life, this can relate to the importance of family, community, and the legacy we leave behind. For example, parents often strive to provide a better future for their children, ensuring the growth and prosperity of the next generation.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge, nurturing, respect for nature, and the desire for prosperity. In our daily lives, we can apply these principles by valuing education, supporting one another, practicing gratitude towards the environment, and fostering a sense of community. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, reflecting the essence of the mantra in our actions and interactions.
