अस्येद इन्द्रो वावृधे वृष्ण्यं शवो मदे सुतस्य विष्णवी |अद्यां तं अस्य महिमानम् आयवोऽनु स्तुवन्ति पूर्वथा ||
asyed indro vāvṛdhe vṛṣṇyaṁ śavo made sutasya viṣṇavi | adyā tam asya mahimānam āyavo 'nu ṣṭuvanti pūrvathā ||
  • Mantra: "asyed indro vāvṛdhe vṛṣṇyaṁ śavo made sutasya viṣṇavi | adyā tam asya mahimānam āyavo 'nu ṣṭuvanti pūrvathā ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May Indra, the great god, who is ever-growing, be pleased with our offerings. Today, those who are wise sing the praises of His greatness, as they did in the past."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • asyed: "of this" or "of this moment"
    • indro: "Indra," the king of gods, associated with strength and rain
    • vāvṛdhe: "who is ever-growing" or "who increases"
    • vṛṣṇyaṁ: "of the Vṛṣṇi," referring to a clan or lineage known for valor
    • śavo: "of the dead" or "of the sacrifice," indicating the importance of offerings
    • made: "in the joy" or "in the ecstasy," referring to the bliss of divine connection
    • sutasya: "of the son" or "of the offspring," often referring to the results of sacrifices
    • viṣṇavi: "of Vishnu," indicating the divine connection to preservation and sustenance
    • adyā: "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the worship
    • tam: "that," referring to the greatness of Indra
    • asya: "of this," again pointing to the present moment
    • mahimānam: "greatness" or "glory," highlighting the attributes of Indra
    • āyavo: "those who are wise" or "the learned," referring to the knowledgeable worshippers
    • 'nu: "now" or "indeed," reinforcing the present action
    • ṣṭuvanti: "sing praises" or "worship," indicating the act of devotion
    • pūrvathā: "as in the past," connecting the present worship to historical traditions
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, people often seek blessings from higher powers during significant life events, such as weddings or the birth of a child, similar to how the mantra invokes Indra's blessings.
    • Communities come together to celebrate festivals, offering prayers and songs to deities, reflecting the collective worship mentioned in the mantra.
    • In times of need, such as during droughts or natural calamities, people may turn to rituals and prayers, invoking the divine for support and sustenance, akin to the invocation of Indra's strength.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and recognition of the interconnectedness of past and present worship. It emphasizes the importance of community, tradition, and the pursuit of spiritual growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to seek strength and guidance from higher powers, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their spiritual practices.
