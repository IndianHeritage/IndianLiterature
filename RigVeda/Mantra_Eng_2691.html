प्र सप्तहोतā सनकाद अरोचत मातुर उपस्थे यद अशोचद ऊधनि |न नि मिषति सुरणो दिवे-दिवे यद असुरस्य जठराद अजायता ||
pra saptahotā sanakād arocata mātur upasthe yad aśocad ūdhani | na ni miṣati suraṇo dive-dive yad asurasya jaṭharād ajāyata ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • pra saptahotā: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the seventh among the hota (the priest or the one who offers prayers)." It signifies a special role in rituals.
    • sanakād: Refers to "the one who is wise or knowledgeable." This indicates the importance of wisdom in spiritual practices.
    • arocata: Means "to speak or to declare." This emphasizes the act of communication in rituals.
    • mātur upasthe: Translates to "in the presence of the mother." This highlights the significance of maternal figures in spiritual contexts.
    • yad aśocad: Means "that which is lamented." This suggests a connection to sorrow or loss.
    • ūdhani: Refers to "the waters" or "the ocean." This symbolizes the vastness of existence and the depths of emotions.
    • na ni miṣati: Translates to "does not blink." This indicates a state of alertness or awareness.
    • suraṇo: Refers to "the divine" or "the gods." This connects the mantra to higher spiritual beings.
    • dive-dive: Means "day by day." This emphasizes the continuity of existence and the daily rituals.
    • yad asurasya: Translates to "that which belongs to the asuras (demons)." This indicates a contrast between divine and demonic forces.
    • jaṭharād: Refers to "from the belly." This symbolizes the source of life and sustenance.
    • ajāyata: Means "was born." This signifies the beginning of existence or creation.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra reflects the interplay between divine and demonic forces, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and awareness in navigating life's challenges.
    • The mention of the mother signifies the nurturing aspect of spirituality, reminding us of the importance of compassion and care in our lives.
    • The continuous nature of existence is highlighted, suggesting that spiritual practices should be a daily endeavor.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom and remain aware of their surroundings, much like a leader who must be vigilant and wise in decision-making.
    • It encourages individuals to honor their roots and the nurturing figures in their lives, fostering a sense of gratitude and respect.
    • The mantra serves as a reminder to engage in daily spiritual practices, whether through meditation, prayer, or acts of kindness, to cultivate a deeper connection with the divine.
