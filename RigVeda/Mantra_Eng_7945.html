सूता इन्द्राय वायवे वरुणाय मरुद्भ्यः |सोमा अर्षन्ति विष्णवे ||
sutā indrāya vāyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | somā arṣanti viṣṇave ||
  • Sutā: This word refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Indrāya: This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods, associated with thunder, rain, and war. Indra represents strength and power.
  • Vāyave: This refers to Vayu, the god of wind, symbolizing movement, breath, and life force.
  • Varuṇāya: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and ethical aspects of the universe.
  • Marudbhyaḥ: This refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, who are associated with the forces of nature and the power of storms.
  • Somā: Again, this refers to the sacred soma, emphasizing its importance in rituals and spiritual practices.
  • Arṣanti: This means "to be offered" or "to be invoked," indicating a call to the deities for blessings.
  • Viṣṇave: This refers to Vishnu, the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity, symbolizing stability and sustenance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to various deities, seeking their blessings and support. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, calling upon the powers of Indra, Vayu, Varuna, the Maruts, and Vishnu. Each deity represents different aspects of life and the universe, highlighting the importance of harmony and balance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be recited during religious ceremonies to invoke divine blessings for prosperity, health, and protection.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of nature deities reminds us of our responsibility towards the environment and the need to live in harmony with nature.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities represented by these deities—strength, stability, and moral order—can inspire individuals to cultivate these traits in their own lives.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements of existence—spiritual, natural, and moral. By invoking these deities, we acknowledge their roles in our lives and strive to align ourselves with the cosmic order they represent. This mantra can be a source of inspiration and strength in our daily lives, encouraging us to seek balance and harmony in all our endeavors.
