नकी रेवन्तं सख्याय विन्दसे पीयन्ति ते सुराश्वः |यदा कृणोषि नदनुं सम ऊहस्यादित पितेव हूयसे ||
nakī revantaṁ sakhyāya vindase pīyanti te surāśvaḥ | yadā kṛṇoṣi nadanuṁ sam ūhasy ād it piteva hūyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakī: Notable or significant
    • revantaṁ: Radiant or shining
    • sakhyāya: For friendship or companionship
    • vindase: I seek or I find
    • pīyanti: They drink or they enjoy
    • te: Those
    • surāśvaḥ: Divine horses or celestial beings
    • yadā: When
    • kṛṇoṣi: You create or you perform
    • nadanuṁ: The river or flow
    • sam: Together or in harmony
    • ūhasy: You unite or you gather
    • ād: From the beginning or from the source
    • ite: Thus or in this way
    • piteva: Like a father
    • hūyase: You are called or you are invoked
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the significance of seeking companionship and the joy that comes from divine connections.
    • The "radiant" aspect suggests that true friendships are enlightening and uplifting.
    • The mention of "divine horses" symbolizes strength and nobility, indicating that such friendships are powerful and transformative.
    • The act of "creating" and "uniting" reflects the importance of bringing people together for a common purpose or goal.
    • Invoking the idea of a father suggests a nurturing and protective quality in these relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, forming strong bonds with colleagues can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
    • In community service, individuals coming together for a cause can create a significant impact, much like the divine beings in the mantra.
    • Friendships that provide emotional support during challenging times exemplify the nurturing aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking meaningful relationships and the joy that comes from unity and companionship.
    • It serves as a reminder that our connections with others can be both divine and transformative, guiding us through life's challenges.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger communities, supportive friendships, and a more fulfilling life.
