आदित्याः रुद्राः वसवः सुनीताः dyāvākṣāmā पृथिवी अन्तरिक्षम् |सजोषसो यज्ञं अवन्तु देवाः ऊर्ध्वं कृत्वन्तु अध्वरस्य केतुम् ||
ādityā rudrā vasavaḥ sunīthā dyāvākṣāmā pṛthivī antarikṣam | sajoṣaso yajñam avantu devā ūrdhvaṁ kṛṇvantv adhvarasya ketum ||
  • ādityā: Refers to the Adityas, a group of solar deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize light, truth, and the life-giving energy of the sun.
  • rudrā: Refers to Rudra, a fierce form of Shiva, representing the storm and the destructive aspects of nature. Rudra embodies the duality of creation and destruction.
  • vasavaḥ: Refers to the Vasus, a group of eight deities associated with various natural elements. They symbolize abundance and prosperity.
  • sunīthā: Means 'well-directed' or 'good guidance'. It emphasizes the importance of having a clear and righteous path in life.
  • dyāvākṣāmā: Refers to the heavens, indicating the celestial realm and the divine presence that oversees the earthly existence.
  • pṛthivī: Refers to the Earth, symbolizing stability, nourishment, and the physical realm where humans live.
  • antarikṣam: Refers to the space or the atmosphere, representing the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • sajoṣaso: Means 'with joy' or 'with enthusiasm', indicating the importance of positive energy and collective effort in rituals and life.
  • yajñam: Refers to the sacrificial ritual or offering, which is a central theme in Vedic practices, symbolizing the act of giving and sharing with the divine.
  • avantu: Means 'may they protect' or 'may they guide', indicating a plea for divine protection and guidance.
  • devā: Refers to the gods or divine beings, emphasizing the connection between humans and the divine forces.
  • ūrdhvaṁ: Means 'upward' or 'higher', indicating aspirations and the pursuit of higher knowledge and spiritual growth.
  • kṛṇvantv: Means 'may they create' or 'may they establish', indicating the active role of the divine in manifesting reality.
  • adhvarasya: Refers to the ritual or sacrifice, emphasizing the importance of performing duties and rituals in life.
  • ketum: Refers to the 'flag' or 'symbol', indicating the representation of goals and aspirations in life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the natural elements, the importance of rituals, and the pursuit of higher aspirations. In real life, this can be applied by recognizing the need for balance between our material and spiritual pursuits, engaging in community rituals that promote harmony, and striving for personal growth while being guided by ethical principles. The mantra serves as a reminder to align ourselves with the forces of nature and the divine, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our actions.
