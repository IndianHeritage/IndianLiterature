त्वां विश्वे अमृत जायतमानं शिशुं न देवाः अभि सं नवन्ते |त्व कृतुभिर अमृतत्वं आयन वैश्वानर यत पितरोर अदीदः ||
tvāṁ viśve amṛta jāyamānaṁ śiśuṁ na devā abhi saṁ navante | tava kratubhir amṛtatvam āyan vaiśvānara yat pitror adīdeḥ ||
  • tvāṁ viśve: This phrase translates to "You, O All." It signifies the universal presence of the divine, suggesting that the essence of the divine is present in all aspects of life.
  • amṛta jāyamānaṁ: This means "the immortal being being born." It refers to the idea of eternal life or the soul's journey through various forms of existence.
  • śiśuṁ: This word means "child." It symbolizes innocence, new beginnings, and the potential for growth and development.
  • na devā abhi saṁ navante: This translates to "the gods do not reject." It implies that the divine does not turn away from any being, emphasizing inclusivity and acceptance.
  • tava kratubhir: This means "by your intentions." It highlights the power of will and intention in shaping reality and influencing the world around us.
  • amṛtatvam: This translates to "immortality." It signifies the ultimate goal of existence, which is to attain a state beyond the cycle of birth and death.
  • āyan vaiśvānara: This phrase refers to the universal fire or the cosmic principle that sustains life. It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all beings through the life force.
  • yat pitror adīdeḥ: This means "which is the origin of the ancestors." It connects the present to the past, emphasizing the importance of lineage and heritage in shaping identity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about life, divinity, and existence. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of intention in achieving immortality. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as fostering inclusivity in communities, recognizing the potential in every individual (like a child), and understanding the significance of our actions and intentions in shaping our destinies. The mantra serves as a reminder that we are all part of a larger cosmic tapestry, and our lives are intertwined with the divine and with each other.
