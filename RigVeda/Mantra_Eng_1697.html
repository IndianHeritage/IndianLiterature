यत् ते सादे महसाऽ शूकृतस्य पार्ष्ण्यां वा कशायां वा तुतोद || स्रुचेव ता हविषो अध्वरेषु सर्वा ता ते ब्रह्मणा सूदयामि ||
yat te sāde mahasā śūkṛtasya pārṣṇyā vā kaśayā vā tutoda | sruceva tā haviṣo adhvareṣu sarvā tā te brahmaṇā sūdayāmi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • sāde: is pleasing
    • mahasā: with great power
    • śūkṛtasya: of the virtuous
    • pārṣṇyā: from the earth
    • vā: or
    • kaśayā: from the sky
    • vā: or
    • tutoda: has been offered
    • sruceva: like a stream
    • tā: those
    • haviṣo: offerings
    • adhvareṣu: in the sacrifices
    • sarvā: all
    • tā: those
    • te: of you
    • brahmaṇā: by the divine
    • sūdayāmi: I purify
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for the divine and the offerings made in rituals.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the earthly and the celestial, suggesting that both realms contribute to the spiritual experience.
    • The phrase "with great power" indicates the strength and significance of virtuous actions and offerings.
    • It highlights the importance of purity in intentions and actions, as the speaker seeks to purify their offerings through divine grace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in acts of kindness and generosity, where one offers help to others selflessly.
    • During festivals or religious ceremonies, individuals often make offerings, symbolizing their gratitude and connection to the divine.
    • In environmental practices, the idea of offerings can extend to caring for nature, where individuals contribute to the well-being of the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity, intention, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach life with reverence and to recognize the divine in everyday actions.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and harmonious existence, both personally and within their communities.
