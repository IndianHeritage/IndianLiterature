येनā नवग्वो दध्यङ्ग् अपोर्णुते येन विप्रास āपिरे |देवानां सुम्ने अमृतस्य चाऽरुणो येन श्रवांस्य आनाśुḥ ||
yenā navagvo dadhyaṅṅ aporṇute yena viprāsa āpire | devānāṁ sumne amṛtasya cāruṇo yena śravāṁsy ānaśuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yenā: By whom
    • navagvo: The new-born (or fresh) cows
    • dadhyaṅṅ: Are nourished
    • aporṇute: Are made complete
    • yena: By whom
    • viprāsa: The wise or learned ones
    • āpire: Are inspired or invoked
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods
    • sumne: In auspiciousness
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality
    • cāruṇo: Beautiful or delightful
    • yena: By whom
    • śravāṁsy: The glories or praises
    • ānaśuḥ: Are brought forth or manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine force that nourishes and completes the new-born, symbolized by cows, which are often seen as a source of sustenance and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • The invocation of the wise (viprāsa) suggests that knowledge and wisdom are essential for invoking divine blessings.
    • The reference to gods (devānāṁ) and immortality (amṛtasya) indicates a connection to the divine and the eternal truths that govern life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness (sumne) in our endeavors and the beauty (cāruṇo) of the divine qualities that we seek to manifest in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the nurturing of new crops can be likened to the nurturing of new-born cows. Farmers often pray for divine blessings to ensure a bountiful harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In education, teachers (the wise) inspire students to seek knowledge, paralleling the invocation of wisdom in the mantra. This highlights the role of education in shaping future generations.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate positive qualities and virtues, akin to invoking divine blessings for a fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nourishment, wisdom, and divine blessings in achieving completeness and prosperity in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to education and personal growth, reminding us of the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to cultivate auspiciousness and beauty in our actions, leading to a harmonious and fulfilling existence.
