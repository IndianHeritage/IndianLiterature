महī atra mahinā vāram ṛṇvatho 'reṇavas tuja ā sadman dhenavaḥ | svaranti tā uparatāti sūryam ā nimruca uṣasas takvavīr iva ||
mahī atra mahinā vāram ṛṇvatho 'reṇavas tuja ā sadman dhenavaḥ | svaranti tā uparatāti sūryam ā nimruca uṣasas takvavīr iva ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Here, the great ones, the powerful ones, may we be free from the bonds of sin. May the cows (representing wealth and nourishment) be present in our abode. They who are free from sin, may they shine like the sun and the dawn, like the brave ones."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mahī: great, vast, or powerful.
    • atra: here, in this place.
    • mahinā: with power or strength.
    • vāram: may we be free, may we be liberated.
    • ṛṇvatho: from the bonds or debts.
    • 'reṇavas: sin or impurities.
    • tuja: may you be present.
    • ā: in, at, or towards.
    • sadman: abode or home.
    • dhenavaḥ: cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment.
    • svaranti: they who shine or radiate.
    • tā: they, those.
    • uparatāti: free from sin or impurities.
    • sūryam: sun, representing light and knowledge.
    • ā: towards or in the direction of.
    • nimruca: dawn, symbolizing new beginnings.
    • uṣasas: dawn or morning light.
    • takvavīr: brave or courageous ones.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and purity in our lives. For instance, in a community, when individuals work together with integrity and strength, they can overcome challenges and create a harmonious environment.
    • The mention of cows symbolizes wealth and nourishment. In agricultural societies, the presence of healthy livestock is crucial for sustenance and economic stability. This can be seen in rural areas where farming and animal husbandry are the backbone of the economy.
    • The idea of shining like the sun and the dawn can be related to personal growth and enlightenment. For example, individuals who pursue education and self-improvement often inspire others, creating a ripple effect of positivity and knowledge in their communities.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, purity, and prosperity. It encourages individuals and communities to strive for a life free from sin and negativity, fostering an environment where wealth (symbolized by cows) and knowledge (symbolized by the sun and dawn) can thrive. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, personal development, and sustainable living practices. By embodying the principles of strength, integrity, and enlightenment, we can create a better world for ourselves and future generations.
