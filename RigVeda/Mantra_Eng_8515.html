प्र सुमेधा गातुविद विश्वदेवः सोमः पुनानः सदा एति नित्यम् |भुवद विश्वेषु काव्येषु रन्तानु जनान यतते पञ्च धीरः ||
pra sumedhā gātuvid viśvadevaḥ somaḥ punānaḥ sada eti nityam | bhuvad viśveṣu kāvyeṣu rantānu janān yatate pañca dhīraḥ ||
  • pra sumedhā: This phrase can be translated as "with excellent intellect" or "with great wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of having a sharp and discerning mind in all endeavors.
  • gātuvid: This term refers to "the one who knows the path" or "the guide." It signifies the need for guidance and direction in life, suggesting that wisdom is not just about knowledge but also about knowing how to apply it.
  • viśvadevaḥ: This means "the universal gods" or "all the deities." It indicates a connection to the divine and the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers in our pursuits.
  • somaḥ punānaḥ: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and rejuvenation. "Punānaḥ" means "purifying." Together, this phrase suggests that divine blessings can purify and nourish our intellect and actions.
  • sada eti nityam: This translates to "always goes forth eternally." It implies that wisdom and divine guidance are constant and ever-present, encouraging individuals to seek them continuously.
  • bhuvad viśveṣu kāvyeṣu: "Bhuvad" means "in the world," and "viśveṣu kāvyeṣu" refers to "in all poetic expressions." This highlights the idea that wisdom and divine guidance are applicable in all aspects of life, including art, literature, and everyday experiences.
  • rantānu janān: This phrase can be interpreted as "the joyful beings" or "those who are happy." It suggests that wisdom and divine guidance lead to happiness and fulfillment in life.
  • yatate pañca dhīraḥ: "Yatate" means "strives," and "pañca dhīraḥ" refers to "the five wise ones." This indicates that those who are wise and strive for knowledge are the ones who truly understand and navigate life successfully.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, divine guidance, and the pursuit of knowledge in achieving a fulfilling life. It teaches that by seeking the blessings of the universal deities and striving for intellectual growth, individuals can navigate the complexities of life with clarity and joy. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, decision-making, and creative pursuits, reminding us to seek wisdom and guidance in all our endeavors for a more meaningful existence.
