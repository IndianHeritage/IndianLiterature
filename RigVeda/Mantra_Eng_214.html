अपां नपतं अवसे सवितारं उप स्तुहि |तस्य व्रतान्य उष्मसि ||
apāṁ napātam avase savitāram upa stuhi | tasya vratāny uśmasi ||
  • Mantra: "apāṁ napātam avase savitāram upa stuhi | tasya vratāny uśmasi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāṁ: of waters
    • napātam: the offspring or descendant
    • avase: to be invoked or called upon
    • savitāram: the sun or the divine light
    • upa: near or close
    • stuhi: praise or worship
    • tasya: of that
    • vratāny: vows or commitments
    • uśmasi: in warmth or energy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine essence of water and the sun, acknowledging their vital roles in life.
    • The phrase "apāṁ napātam" refers to the offspring of water, which can symbolize the life-giving properties of water, essential for all living beings.
    • "Savitāram" signifies the sun, representing light, energy, and growth, which are crucial for sustaining life on Earth.
    • The invocation "upa stuhi" suggests a call for closeness and connection with these divine elements, emphasizing the importance of recognizing their presence in our lives.
    • The latter part, "tasya vratāny uśmasi," indicates that through our commitments and actions, we harness the warmth and energy provided by these forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers rely on water and sunlight for crop growth. This mantra can be seen as a reminder to respect and honor these natural resources.
    • In daily life, individuals can reflect on their relationship with nature, understanding that water and sunlight are essential for health and well-being.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be used during meditation to connect with the elements, fostering a sense of gratitude and awareness of the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life and the natural elements that sustain it.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine presence in nature, encouraging individuals to cultivate respect and gratitude for water and sunlight.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices, mindfulness, and a deeper appreciation for the environment, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence with nature.
