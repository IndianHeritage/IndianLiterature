युवो रथस्य परि चक्रं ईयत ईर्माण्यद वाम इषण्यति |अस्मांं अच्‍छा सुमतिर वां शुभस पती आ धेनुर इव धावतु ||
yuvo rathasya pari cakram īyata īrmānyad vām iṣaṇyati | asmām̐ acchā sumatir vāṁ śubhas patī ā dhenur iva dhāvatu ||
  • yuvo rathasya: This phrase translates to "the young ones of the chariot." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the youth or the new generation that is full of potential and energy.
  • pari cakram: This means "surrounding the wheel." The wheel is often a symbol of progress and movement, indicating that the youth are encircled by opportunities and responsibilities.
  • īyata: This word can be interpreted as "may they be directed" or "may they be guided." It emphasizes the need for guidance in the journey of life.
  • īrmānyad: This translates to "may they be strong" or "may they be powerful." It highlights the importance of strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • vām iṣaṇyati: This phrase means "may they be endowed with wisdom." Wisdom is crucial for making informed decisions and navigating through life's complexities.
  • asmām̐ acchā: This translates to "may we have good fortune." It reflects a desire for prosperity and success in our endeavors.
  • sumatir: This means "good understanding" or "intellect." It emphasizes the importance of clarity of thought and sound judgment.
  • vāṁ: This word means "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to the audience or the divine.
  • śubhas patī: This translates to "the lord of auspiciousness." It signifies the presence of positive energy and blessings in our lives.
  • ā dhenur iva dhāvatu: This phrase means "may it run like a cow." Cows are often associated with nourishment and abundance, suggesting a desire for prosperity and sustenance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of youth, guidance, strength, wisdom, and good fortune. It serves as a reminder that the new generation must be nurtured and guided to harness their potential effectively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, where mentors and teachers play a crucial role in shaping the minds of young individuals. It also resonates in family dynamics, where parents strive to provide a supportive environment for their children to grow and thrive. Ultimately, the mantra encourages a collective effort to foster a prosperous and enlightened society, where wisdom and strength lead to a fulfilling life.
