रसाय्यः पयसा पिन्वमाना ईरयन्न एषि मधुमन्तम् अंशुम् |पवमानः संतानिम् एषि कृत्वन् इन्द्राय सोम परिषिच्यमानाḥ ||
rasāyyaḥ payasā pinvamāna īrayann eṣi madhumantam aṁśum | pavamānaḥ saṁtanim eṣi kṛṇvann indrāya soma pariṣicyamānaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rasāyyaḥ: Refers to the essence or the vital energy.
    • payasā: Means milk, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
    • pinvamāna: Indicates the act of being pressed or extracted.
    • īrayann: Suggests the act of moving or flowing.
    • eṣi: Means to go or to move towards.
    • madhumantam: Refers to the sweet or honey-like essence.
    • aṁśum: Means radiance or light.
    • pavamānaḥ: Refers to the purifying or cleansing force.
    • saṁtanim: Indicates the act of being steady or stable.
    • kṛṇvann: Means to create or to make.
    • indrāya: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing power and strength.
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • pariṣicyamānaḥ: Means being anointed or blessed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the process of extracting the essence of life (rasāyyaḥ) through the nourishing qualities of milk (payasā). It symbolizes the importance of nourishment in our lives, both physically and spiritually.
    • The act of pressing (pinvamāna) signifies the effort required to obtain this essence, reminding us that valuable things often require hard work and dedication.
    • The reference to the sweet essence (madhumantam) suggests that the rewards of our efforts can be sweet and fulfilling, akin to the sweetness of honey.
    • The mantra also invokes the purifying force (pavamānaḥ), which can be interpreted as the cleansing of our thoughts and actions, leading us towards a more stable and steady life (saṁtanim).
    • By creating (kṛṇvann) a connection with Indra (indrāya), the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and guidance from higher powers or the universe.
    • The mention of soma (sacred drink) highlights the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment and the quest for immortality, both in a literal and metaphorical sense.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek nourishment not just through food but also through knowledge, relationships, and experiences that enrich their lives.
    • It can serve as a reminder that achieving our goals often requires effort and perseverance, much like the process of extracting juice from fruits or milk from cows.
    • The idea of purification can be applied in practices such as meditation or yoga, where individuals seek to cleanse their minds and bodies to achieve a state of balance and harmony.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be relevant in community building, where individuals come together to support each other in their quests for personal and collective growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing nourishment, effort, purification, and the pursuit of strength and enlightenment.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to guide individuals towards a more fulfilling and balanced life, encouraging them to seek both physical and spiritual nourishment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our efforts, the rewards we reap, and the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers or the universe.
