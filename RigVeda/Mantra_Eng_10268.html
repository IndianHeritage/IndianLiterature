प्रोष्व अस्मै पुरोऽरथम इन्द्राय शूषम् आर्चत |अभीक चिदु लोककृत सङ्गे समत्सु वृत्रहास्माकं बोधि चोदिता नभन्ताम् अन्यकेषां ज्याकाद्धि धन्वसु ||
pro ṣv asmai puroratham indrāya śūṣam arcata | abhīke cid u lokakṛt saṁge samatsu vṛtrahāsmākam bodhi coditā nabhantām anyakeṣāṁ jyākā adhi dhanvasu ||
  • Mantra: "pro ṣv asmai puroratham indrāya śūṣam arcata | abhīke cid u lokakṛt saṁge samatsu vṛtrahāsmākam bodhi coditā nabhantām anyakeṣāṁ jyākā adhi dhanvasu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pro ṣv: to this, for this
    • asmai: to him
    • puroratham: the chariot of the past
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • śūṣam: the dry, barren
    • arcata: we worship
    • abhīke: in the presence of
    • cid: indeed
    • u: or
    • lokakṛt: the creator of the world
    • saṁge: in the assembly
    • samatsu: among the good
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • asmākam: our
    • bodhi: wisdom
    • coditā: inspired
    • nabhantām: in the sky
    • anyakeṣāṁ: of others
    • jyākā: the light
    • adhi: upon
    • dhanvasu: the bow (or strength)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "pro ṣv asmai puroratham" indicates a request for guidance and support from Indra, particularly in overcoming challenges that may arise from the past.
    • By invoking "śūṣam arcata," the mantra acknowledges the need to worship and seek blessings for overcoming difficulties, especially those that seem dry or barren, symbolizing life's challenges.
    • The mention of "lokakṛt" emphasizes the importance of creation and the interconnectedness of all beings, suggesting that wisdom and strength come from unity and collaboration.
    • In "vṛtrahā," the reference to the slayer of Vritra symbolizes the triumph over obstacles and adversities, encouraging individuals to confront their fears and challenges head-on.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, "bodhi coditā nabhantām," signifies the aspiration for enlightenment and wisdom that transcends individual struggles, aiming for a higher understanding and connection with the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing a challenging project, one might reflect on this mantra to seek the strength and wisdom to overcome obstacles, much like how Indra is invoked for support.
    • In personal life, during times of emotional turmoil or hardship, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to find inner strength and clarity, reminding themselves of the importance of community and support.
    • In educational pursuits, students can use the essence of this mantra to motivate themselves to overcome academic challenges, invoking the wisdom of past experiences to guide their current efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of all beings and the strength that comes from unity and collaboration.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or academic pursuits, this mantra encourages individuals to confront their fears, seek support, and strive for enlightenment and understanding.
