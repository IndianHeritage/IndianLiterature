बृहस्पते युवम् इन्द्रश्च वस्वो दिव्यस्येशाथे उता पार्थिवस्य | धत्तं रयिं स्तुवते कीरये चिद् यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
bṛhaspate yuvam indraś ca vasvo divyasyeśāthe uta pārthivasya | dhattaṁ rayiṁ stuvate kīraye cid yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate yuvam indraś ca vasvo divyasyeśāthe uta pārthivasya | dhattaṁ rayiṁ stuvate kīraye cid yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Bṛhaspati, you and Indra, along with the divine Vasus, grant us wealth and prosperity. We praise you for your glory; may you protect us always with your auspiciousness."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity of wisdom and eloquence, often associated with knowledge and learning.
    • Yuvam: You (plural), referring to the divine beings being addressed.
    • Indra: The king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Vasvo: Referring to the Vasus, a group of deities associated with material wealth and prosperity.
    • Divyasyeśāthe: In the realm of the divine, indicating a connection to higher spiritual powers.
    • Pārthivasya: Pertaining to the earthly or material realm.
    • Dhattam: Grant or bestow, a request for divine favor.
    • Rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity, both material and spiritual.
    • Stuvate: We praise, an expression of reverence and gratitude.
    • Kīraye: For glory or fame, indicating the importance of recognition.
    • Cid: May it be so, a term of affirmation.
    • Yūyam: You (plural), again addressing the divine beings.
    • Pāta: Protect, a plea for safeguarding.
    • Svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness, invoking blessings of well-being.
    • Sadā: Always, indicating a continuous need for divine support.
    • Naḥ: Us, referring to the devotees or worshippers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the essence of this mantra during meetings to seek wisdom and prosperity for their projects.
    • During family gatherings or celebrations, individuals may recite this mantra to bless their homes with wealth and harmony.
    • In educational institutions, students may chant this mantra before exams to invoke the blessings of knowledge and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of spiritual and material realms, reminding us that true wealth encompasses both knowledge and resources.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, professional success, and community well-being, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and auspiciousness in their endeavors.
