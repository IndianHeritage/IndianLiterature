मन्त्र: उता त्या सद्य आर्याः सरयोः इन्द्र पारतः |अर्णाचित्ररथावधिः ||
uta tyā sadya āryā sarayor indra pārataḥ | arṇācitrarathāvadhīḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to what has been previously mentioned.
  • tyā - This means "by you" or "through you," suggesting a sense of agency or action attributed to a divine being.
  • sadya - Translated as "immediately" or "suddenly," it conveys a sense of urgency or promptness in the context of the mantra.
  • āryā - This term refers to "noble" or "honorable," often used to describe individuals of high moral standing or virtue.
  • sarayor - This word means "of the rivers," indicating a connection to nature, specifically water bodies that are often revered in Vedic literature.
  • indra - Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic texts, known as the king of the gods and associated with thunder, rain, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • pārataḥ - This translates to "from the Paratas," which may refer to a specific group or lineage, emphasizing the importance of community or heritage.
  • arṇā - This word can mean "to be born" or "to arise," suggesting the emergence of something new or significant.
  • citrarathāvadhīḥ - This compound word can be broken down into "citra" (colorful or diverse) and "rathā" (chariots), indicating a sense of movement, diversity, and perhaps the journey of life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, to act swiftly and nobly, drawing upon the strength of the rivers and the heritage of the community. It emphasizes the importance of immediate action and the noble qualities that should guide such actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a leader among the gods, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to act decisively and with integrity.
  • Community Support: The reference to the rivers and the Paratas highlights the importance of community. In times of crisis, coming together as a community to support one another can lead to swift and effective solutions.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mention of rivers serves as a reminder of the importance of nature. In contemporary society, this can translate to efforts in environmental conservation and sustainable practices.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the virtues of promptness, nobility, and community. It encourages individuals to act with integrity and to recognize the interconnectedness of life, nature, and society. By embodying these principles, one can navigate the complexities of life with strength and purpose.
