मातली काव्यैर यमो अङ्गिरोभिर बृहस्पतिर ऋक्वभिर वावृधानाḥ |याम्̐श च देवā वावृधुर ये च देवान स्वाहान्ये स्वधायान्ये मदन्ति ||
mātalī kavyair yamo aṅgirobhir bṛhaspatir ṛkvabhir vāvṛdhānaḥ | yām̐ś ca devā vāvṛdhur ye ca devān svāhānye svadhayānye madanti ||
  • mātalī: This term refers to the divine charioteer or the one who drives the chariot of the gods. In a real-life context, this can symbolize guidance and support in our journeys, much like a mentor or a leader who helps navigate challenges.
  • kavyair: This word means "by the poets" or "through poetry." It highlights the importance of creativity and expression in understanding the divine and the world around us. In everyday life, poetry and art can serve as powerful tools for communication and emotional expression.
  • yamo: Yama is the god of death and moral order. This signifies the inevitability of death and the importance of living a righteous life. In real life, this can remind us to make ethical choices and to value our time and relationships.
  • aṅgirobhir: Referring to the Angirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. This emphasizes the value of learning and wisdom in our lives. In practice, seeking knowledge through education and experience is crucial for personal growth.
  • bṛhaspatir: Bṛhaspati is the guru of the gods and represents wisdom and knowledge. This can be related to the importance of having wise counsel in our lives, whether through teachers, mentors, or advisors who guide us in making informed decisions.
  • ṛkvabhir: This term refers to the hymns of the Rigveda, which are sacred texts that convey spiritual knowledge. It signifies the importance of sacred texts and traditions in guiding moral and ethical behavior in society. In real life, many people turn to religious or philosophical texts for guidance in their lives.
  • vāvṛdhānaḥ: This means "growing" or "increasing." It suggests the idea of growth, both spiritually and materially. In our lives, this can be seen in personal development, career advancement, and the nurturing of relationships.
  • yām̐ś ca devā vāvṛdhur: This phrase indicates that the gods themselves grow and evolve. It reflects the dynamic nature of divinity and existence. In real life, this can be interpreted as the need for adaptability and growth in our own lives, embracing change as a part of our journey.
  • ye ca devān svāhānye svadhayānye madanti: This part refers to those who worship the gods and partake in their divine essence. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and spiritual practice. In everyday life, this can manifest as rituals, meditation, or any practice that connects us to a higher purpose or understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of guidance, wisdom, and the importance of growth in both spiritual and material aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, embrace change, and maintain ethical conduct while navigating our journeys. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through mentorship, education, and spiritual practices that foster personal and communal growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to live a life of purpose, guided by wisdom and creativity, while remaining connected to the divine and the community around us.
