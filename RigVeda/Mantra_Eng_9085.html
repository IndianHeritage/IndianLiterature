वने न वा यो न्य अधायि चाकञ्च शुचिर वां स्तोमो भुरणाव अजीगः |यस्येद इन्द्रः पुरुदिनेषु होता नृणां नर्यो नृतमः क्षपावान् ||
vane na vā yo ny adhāyi cākañ chucir vāṁ stomo bhuraṇāv ajīgaḥ | yasyed indraḥ purudineṣu hotā nṛṇāṁ naryo nṛtamaḥ kṣapāvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vane: in the forest
    • na: not
    • vā: or
    • yo: who
    • ny adhāyi: has not offered
    • cākañ: with a pure heart
    • shucir: pure
    • vāṁ: your
    • stomo: praise
    • bhuraṇāv: to the one who is worthy
    • ajīgaḥ: who is not to be neglected
    • yasyed: whose
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • purudineṣu: in many days
    • hotā: the one who offers
    • nṛṇāṁ: of men
    • naryo: of women
    • nṛtamaḥ: the best among men
    • kṣapāvān: the one who brings light
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of offering and devotion.
    • It emphasizes that one who does not offer with a pure heart is not worthy of praise.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, is invoked as the one who recognizes and rewards the true offerings of both men and women.
    • The mantra suggests that true worthiness comes from sincerity and purity in one's actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who works diligently and with integrity is often recognized and rewarded, while those who do not contribute sincerely may be overlooked.
    • In personal relationships, genuine acts of kindness and support are valued more than superficial gestures.
    • In community service, volunteers who dedicate their time and effort with a pure heart often inspire others and create a positive impact.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of sincerity and purity in our actions.
    • It teaches us that true recognition comes from genuine efforts and contributions, whether in spiritual, personal, or professional realms.
    • By embodying these values, we can foster a more harmonious and respectful environment in our lives.
