यस्य शर्मन्न उप विश्वे जनासा एवैतस्थुः सुमतिम भिक्षमाणाः |वैश्वानरो वरम आ रोदस्योर आग्निः ससाद पित्रोर उपस्थम् ||
yasya śarmann upa viśve janāsa evais tasthuḥ sumatim bhikṣamāṇāḥ | vaiśvānaro varam ā rodasyor āgniḥ sasāda pitror upastham ||
  • Mantra: "yasya śarmann upa viśve janāsa evais tasthuḥ sumatim bhikṣamāṇāḥ | vaiśvānaro varam ā rodasyor āgniḥ sasāda pitror upastham ||"
  • Translation: "To whom all beings are drawn for shelter, may they, seeking wisdom, be established in good thoughts. The universal fire, the Vaiśvānara, has settled in the presence of the ancestors."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yasya: of whom
    • śarmann: for shelter or protection
    • upa: near or towards
    • viśve: all beings or creatures
    • janāsa: people or beings
    • evais: indeed or truly
    • tasthuḥ: may they be established
    • sumatim: in good thoughts or wisdom
    • bhikṣamāṇāḥ: seeking or desiring
    • vaiśvānaro: the universal fire
    • varam: the best or supreme
    • ā: from or out of
    • rodasyor: of the two worlds (heaven and earth)
    • āgniḥ: fire
    • sasāda: has settled
    • pitror: of the ancestors
    • upastham: in the presence or near

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking shelter and wisdom from a higher power or universal force.
    • It highlights the connection between humanity and the divine, suggesting that all beings are drawn towards a source of protection and guidance.
    • The reference to the "universal fire" symbolizes the transformative power of knowledge and the warmth of ancestral blessings.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In times of uncertainty or distress, individuals often seek guidance from spiritual practices, community support, or philosophical teachings, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a family setting, honoring ancestors and seeking their wisdom can provide a sense of belonging and direction, as suggested by the mention of "pitror upastham."
    • In professional environments, fostering a culture of good thoughts and wisdom can lead to better teamwork and innovation, aligning with the mantra's call for "sumatim bhikṣamāṇāḥ."

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking wisdom and protection from higher forces.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate good thoughts and to honor their roots, which can lead to personal growth and community harmony.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that by aligning ourselves with universal principles, we can navigate life's challenges with greater clarity and purpose.
