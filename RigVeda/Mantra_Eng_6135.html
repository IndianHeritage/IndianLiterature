महां इन्द्रो य ओजसा पार्जन्यो वृष्टिमां इव | स्तोमैर वत्सस्य वावृधे ||
mahām̐ indro ya ojasā parjanyo vṛṣṭimām̐ iva | stomair vatsasya vāvṛdhe ||
  • Mantra: "mahām̐ indro ya ojasā parjanyo vṛṣṭimām̐ iva | stomair vatsasya vāvṛdhe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahām̐: great, powerful
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms
    • ya: who
    • ojasā: with strength, energy
    • parjanyo: the rain-bringer, often referring to the clouds
    • vṛṣṭimām̐: with rain, or the act of raining
    • iva: like, as if
    • stomair: with hymns, praises
    • vatsasya: of the calf, symbolizing nourishment and growth
    • vāvṛdhe: may it increase, may it grow
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity who is often associated with rain and fertility.
    • The phrase "with strength" emphasizes the energy and vigor that Indra brings, particularly in the context of rain.
    • The reference to "parjanyo" highlights the importance of rain in agriculture and sustenance, as it is essential for crops to grow.
    • The comparison "like rain" suggests that the blessings or abundance we seek should come as naturally and generously as rain does.
    • The mention of "hymns" indicates that through devotion and praise, one can invoke the divine blessings of Indra.
    • The "calf" symbolizes nourishment and the growth of life, indicating that the blessings sought are not just for survival but for thriving and flourishing.
    • The concluding wish for increase signifies a desire for prosperity and abundance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during dry seasons, similar to how this mantra seeks the blessings of Indra for rain.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, hoping for strength and support in overcoming challenges.
    • Communities may come together to chant this mantra during festivals or rituals to invoke prosperity and abundance for the entire community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving growth and prosperity.
    • It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of nature and our reliance on it for sustenance.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers, especially during challenging times.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a sense of gratitude and reverence for the natural world and the abundance it provides.
