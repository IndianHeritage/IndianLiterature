तव त्विषो जनिमान रेजत द्यौ रेजद भूमिर भियसां स्वस्य मन्योः |ऋघायन्त सुभवः पर्वतास आर्दन धन्वानि सरयन्त आपः ||
tava tviṣo janiman rejata dyau rejad bhūmir bhiyasā svasya manyoḥ | ṛghāyanta subhva1ḥ parvatāsa ārdan dhanvāni sarayanta āpaḥ ||
  • tava tviṣo - "Your radiance" or "Your light": This phrase refers to the divine light or energy that emanates from the Supreme Being. In real life, we can relate this to the positive energy or aura that individuals exude, which can influence those around them.
  • janiman rejata - "is born and shines": This signifies the birth of new ideas, thoughts, or even life itself. For example, when a child is born, they bring joy and hope, shining brightly in the lives of their parents.
  • dyau rejad - "the sky shines": This can be interpreted as the vastness and beauty of the universe. In our daily lives, we often look up at the sky and find inspiration or solace, especially during moments of reflection.
  • bhūmir bhiyasā - "the earth is filled": This phrase indicates the abundance of resources and life on Earth. In practical terms, it reminds us of the importance of nature and the need to protect our environment for future generations.
  • svasya manyoḥ - "of one's own mind": This emphasizes the significance of self-awareness and introspection. In real life, understanding our thoughts and emotions can lead to personal growth and better decision-making.
  • ṛghāyanta - "they who are worthy": This refers to those who are deserving of respect and honor. In society, we often recognize individuals who contribute positively, such as teachers, healthcare workers, and community leaders.
  • subhvaḥ - "goodness": This highlights the importance of virtuous actions and intentions. In everyday life, acts of kindness and compassion can create a ripple effect, inspiring others to do the same.
  • parvatāsa - "mountains": Mountains symbolize strength and stability. In our lives, we face challenges that can be likened to climbing a mountain, requiring perseverance and resilience.
  • ārdan - "to be nourished": This signifies the need for sustenance, both physically and spiritually. In real life, we nourish our bodies with food and our souls with love, knowledge, and experiences.
  • dhanvāni - "weapons": This can be interpreted metaphorically as the tools we use to navigate life’s challenges. For instance, education and skills can be seen as weapons that empower us to succeed.
  • sarayanta - "flowing": This refers to the continuous flow of life and energy. In practical terms, it reminds us to go with the flow and adapt to changes, much like a river that carves its path through the landscape.
  • āpaḥ - "waters": Water is essential for life and symbolizes purity and renewal. In our lives, we often seek moments of refreshment and rejuvenation, whether through nature, meditation, or self-care.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, highlighting the interconnectedness of the universe, nature, and human experience. It serves as a reminder of the divine light within us, the importance of self-awareness, and the need to honor goodness and strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to embrace positivity, nurture our environment, and strive for personal growth, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
