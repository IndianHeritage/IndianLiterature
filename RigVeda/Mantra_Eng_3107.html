एभिर् भव सुमना अग्नि अर्कैर इमान स्पृश मनमभिः शूर वाजान् |उता ब्रह्माण्य अंगिरो जुषस्व सं ते शस्तिर देववाता जरेत ||
ebhir bhava sumanā agne arkair imān spṛśa manmabhiḥ śūra vājān | uta brahmāṇy aṅgiro juṣasva saṁ te śastir devavātā jareta ||
  • ebhir bhava sumanā: This phrase can be translated as "May you be endowed with good thoughts." Here, "ebhir" refers to "with these," and "sumanā" means "good thoughts" or "auspiciousness." In real life, this can be related to the importance of positive thinking and how it influences our actions and decisions.
  • agne: This word means "O Agni," referring to the fire god in Vedic tradition. Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine messenger. In our daily lives, we can see Agni as a representation of our inner drive and passion that fuels our ambitions.
  • arkair imān spṛśa: This translates to "touch these with your rays." "Arkair" refers to "rays" or "light," and "spṛśa" means "to touch." This can be likened to how sunlight nourishes plants, symbolizing the need for divine blessings to illuminate our paths and guide us toward success.
  • manmabhiḥ: This word means "with our minds." It emphasizes the role of intention and thought in our actions. In real life, this can be seen in how our mindset can shape our experiences and outcomes, highlighting the power of focused intention.
  • śūra vājān: This phrase translates to "the strong and powerful." "Śūra" means "brave" or "heroic," and "vājān" refers to "strength" or "power." This can be related to the qualities we admire in leaders and role models who inspire us to be courageous and resilient in the face of challenges.
  • uta brahmāṇy aṅgiro juṣasva: This means "and may you enjoy the blessings of the Brahman." "Brahmāṇy" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, and "aṅgiro" relates to the sages or seers. This highlights the importance of seeking wisdom and spiritual guidance in our lives, akin to how we seek mentorship from experienced individuals.
  • saṁ te śastir devavātā jareta: This translates to "may the divine powers protect you." "Saṁ te" means "to you," "śastir" refers to "protection," and "devavātā" means "divine beings." This can be seen in the way we seek protection and support from our community, family, or spiritual beliefs during difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of positive thoughts, divine guidance, and the pursuit of strength and wisdom. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate a positive mindset, seek spiritual and intellectual growth, and rely on the support of the community and divine forces. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with courage and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
