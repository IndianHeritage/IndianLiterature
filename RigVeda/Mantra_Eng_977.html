शतम् इनु शरदो अन्ति देवाः यत्र नश् चक्रा जरसं तनूनाम् |पुत्रासो यत्र पितरो भवन्ति मा नो मध्याः रीरीषतायुर गन्तोः ||
śatam in nu śarado anti devā yatrā naś cakrā jarasaṁ tanūnām | putrāso yatra pitaro bhavanti mā no madhyā rīriṣatāyur gantoḥ ||
  • śatam in nu śarado anti devā: This phrase can be translated to "In the hundred autumns, the gods are present." Here, "śatam" refers to a hundred, "śarado" signifies autumns, and "devā" means gods. This suggests a time of abundance and divine presence, emphasizing the importance of seasons in the cycle of life.
  • yatrā naś cakrā jarasaṁ tanūnām: This translates to "Where our bodies, worn by age, do not decay." The term "naś" means "our," "cakrā" refers to "bodies," "jarasaṁ" means "worn by age," and "tanūnām" signifies "of bodies." This line reflects the desire for longevity and the divine protection against the decay of the physical form.
  • putrāso yatra pitaro bhavanti: This means "Where sons become fathers." "Putrāso" refers to "sons," "yatra" means "where," and "pitaro" signifies "fathers." This line highlights the continuity of life and the generational cycle, emphasizing the importance of family and lineage.
  • mā no madhyā rīriṣatāyur gantoḥ: This translates to "May we not be cut off from the middle of our life." "Mā no" means "may we not," "madhyā" refers to "the middle," "rīriṣatāyur" signifies "cut off from life," and "gantoḥ" means "to go." This expresses a prayer for a long and fulfilling life, free from premature death.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, the divine presence in our existence, and the importance of family and continuity. It serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the blessings of longevity and health.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • In family gatherings, where the presence of multiple generations signifies the continuity of lineage and the blessings of the ancestors.
    • During festivals or seasonal celebrations, where the changing seasons remind us of the divine presence and the abundance of life.
    • In health and wellness practices, where the focus on longevity and vitality aligns with the desire expressed in the mantra for a life free from decay.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reflection on the human experience, emphasizing the importance of divine blessings, family, and the desire for a long and fulfilling life. It encourages individuals to appreciate the cycles of life and the connections that bind generations together, reminding us to seek harmony with the divine and the natural world.
