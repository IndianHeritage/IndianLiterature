या इमे dyāvāpṛthivī जनित्री रूपैर अपिṁशद भुवनानि विश्वा |तम अद्य होतर इषितो यजीयान देवं त्वष्टारम इह यक्षि विद्वान् ||
ya ime dyāvāpṛthivī janitrī rūpair apiṁśad bhuvanāni viśvā | tam adya hotar iṣito yajīyān devaṁ tvaṣṭāram iha yakṣi vidvān ||
  • Translation: "O Divine Architect, who is the creator of the heavens and the earth, who manifests in various forms, and who is the source of all worlds, we invoke you today as the priest of our offerings, the one who is worthy of worship, the skilled craftsman."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ya ime: "who are these" - referring to the divine presence.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: "heavens and earth" - symbolizing the duality of existence.
    • janitrī: "mother" - indicating the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • rūpair: "in forms" - acknowledging the various manifestations of the divine.
    • apiṁśad: "of all" - encompassing everything in creation.
    • bhuvanāni: "worlds" - referring to the different realms of existence.
    • viśvā: "all" - emphasizing inclusivity.
    • tam: "that" - pointing towards the divine being.
    • adya: "today" - indicating the immediacy of the invocation.
    • hotar: "priest" - the one who performs rituals and offerings.
    • iṣito: "worthy of worship" - highlighting the reverence due to the divine.
    • yajīyān: "the one who is worshipped" - reinforcing the idea of devotion.
    • devaṁ: "God" - the supreme being.
    • tvaṣṭāram: "the skilled craftsman" - referring to the creator aspect of the divine.
    • iha: "here" - indicating the present moment.
    • yakṣi: "to bestow" - the act of giving blessings.
    • vidvān: "the wise one" - acknowledging the intelligence and wisdom of the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see the duality of the heavens and the earth in the cycle of day and night, which can be seen as a reflection of the divine balance.
    • The nurturing aspect of the divine can be compared to a mother’s love, which is essential for growth and development.
    • In various cultures, the concept of a skilled craftsman can be seen in the way artisans create beautiful works of art, reflecting the divine creativity in human endeavors.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life. It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of the universe and the nurturing qualities of the divine. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence for nature, acknowledging the creativity in ourselves and others, and understanding the importance of community and support, much like a mother’s role in nurturing her children. By invoking the divine in our daily lives, we can cultivate a deeper connection to the world around us and to each other.
