गोचिता बाहू अमितक्रतुः सिमः कर्मन्-कर्मण चतामूतिः खजंकरः | अकल्प इन्द्रः प्रतिमानम् ओजसाथा जनाः वि हवयन्ते सिषासवः ||
gojitā bāhū amitakratuḥ simaḥ karman-karmañ chatamūtiḥ khajaṁkaraḥ | akalpa indraḥ pratimānam ojasāthā janā vi hvayante siṣāsavaḥ ||
  • gojitā bāhū: This phrase can be translated as "the arms that are victorious" or "the arms that have achieved success." In real life, this can refer to individuals or groups who have worked hard and achieved their goals, such as athletes who train rigorously to win competitions.
  • amitakratuḥ: This term means "one with boundless intellect" or "one with infinite resolve." It signifies the importance of wisdom and determination in achieving success. For example, a scientist conducting research must possess both intellect and perseverance to make groundbreaking discoveries.
  • simaḥ karman: This translates to "the boundary of action" or "the limits of deeds." It emphasizes the significance of understanding the consequences of our actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical decision-making, where individuals must consider the impact of their choices on others and the environment.
  • karmañ chatamūtiḥ: This phrase means "the culmination of actions" or "the ultimate result of deeds." It highlights that every action has a result, and one must be mindful of their actions. For instance, in business, the success of a project often depends on the quality of the work put in by the team.
  • khajaṁkaraḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who creates abundance" or "the one who brings forth prosperity." This reflects the idea of nurturing and fostering growth, whether in personal relationships, communities, or economies. A gardener, for example, cultivates plants to create a thriving garden.
  • akalpa indraḥ: This phrase means "the unbounded Indra" or "the limitless leader." Indra is often seen as a symbol of strength and leadership. In real life, this can relate to leaders who inspire and guide others towards achieving common goals, such as community leaders advocating for social change.
  • pratimānam ojasāthā: This translates to "the embodiment of strength and energy." It signifies the importance of vitality and vigor in pursuing one's goals. For example, athletes often rely on their physical and mental strength to excel in their sports.
  • janā vi hvayante siṣāsavaḥ: This phrase means "the people who invoke the divine energy." It reflects the idea of seeking inspiration and support from a higher power or the universe. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or prayer, where individuals seek guidance and strength in their endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, intellect, and the consequences of our actions in achieving success. It serves as a reminder that our efforts, guided by wisdom and determination, can lead to prosperity and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various fields such as sports, leadership, business, and personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence while being mindful of their actions and their impact on the world around them.
