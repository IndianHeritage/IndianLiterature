दश रथान प्रष्ठिमतः शतं गा अथर्वभ्यः |अश्वत्थः पायवे दात ||
daśa rathān praṣṭimataḥ śataṁ gā atharvabhyaḥ | aśvathaḥ pāyave 'dāt ||
  • Mantra: "daśa rathān praṣṭimataḥ śataṁ gā atharvabhyaḥ | aśvathaḥ pāyave 'dāt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • daśa: ten
    • rathān: chariots
    • praṣṭimataḥ: from the one who is praised
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • gā: cows
    • atharvabhyaḥ: to the Atharvans (a group of priests or followers of the Atharva Veda)
    • aśvathaḥ: the sacred fig tree (Ficus religiosa)
    • pāyave: to the protector or guardian
    • 'dāt: gave
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine offering or blessing involving ten chariots and a hundred cows, which are significant symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • The phrase "from the one who is praised" suggests that these offerings are made to a deity or a revered figure, indicating a relationship of respect and devotion.
    • The mention of "Atharvabhyaḥ" implies that this offering is directed towards those who follow the Atharva Veda, which is associated with practical knowledge, healing, and rituals.
    • The "aśvathaḥ" or sacred fig tree symbolizes wisdom and longevity, often regarded as a place of meditation and spiritual growth.
    • The act of giving or offering is emphasized with "pāyave 'dāt," indicating a protective and nurturing aspect of the divine, suggesting that the offerings are made for the well-being and protection of the community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of offering can be seen in charitable acts, where individuals or organizations donate resources (like money, food, or time) to help those in need, reflecting the spirit of giving found in this mantra.
    • The sacred fig tree is often found in temples and is a place where people gather for meditation and reflection, symbolizing the importance of nature and spirituality in daily life.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals where they offer prayers and resources to deities, similar to the offerings mentioned in the mantra, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of offerings and the relationship between the divine and the community.
    • It teaches the values of gratitude, respect, and the importance of giving for the welfare of others.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of kindness, support their communities, and seek spiritual growth through connection with nature and the divine.
