प्रत्याग्ने मिथुनā दह यातुधानā किमीदिनā |सं त्वा शिशामि जागृहि अदब्धं विप्र मन्मभिः ||
praty agne mithunā daha yātudhānā kimīdinā | saṁ tvā śiśāmi jāgṛhy adabdhaṁ vipra manmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • praty: towards
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • mithunā: with a pair or couple
    • daha: burn
    • yātudhānā: of the malevolent spirits or demons
    • kimīdinā: what is this (or what is happening)
    • saṁ: together
    • tvā: you
    • śiśāmi: I invoke or I call
    • jāgṛhy: having been grasped or held
    • adabdhaṁ: unhurt or unharmed
    • vipra: O wise one or sage
    • manmabhiḥ: with my thoughts or intentions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for protection and the ability to overcome malevolent forces.
    • The phrase "mithunā" suggests a duality or partnership, indicating that the speaker seeks strength in unity against adversities.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire as a purifying and protective element in Vedic rituals.
    • The mention of "yātudhānā" refers to the negative energies or spirits that can cause harm, highlighting the need for spiritual protection.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to remain unharmed ("adabdhaṁ") while invoking the wisdom and guidance of the sage ("vipra").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in situations where individuals face challenges or negativity, such as workplace conflicts or personal struggles.
    • People often seek the support of friends or family (the "mithunā") to navigate difficult times, emphasizing the importance of community and partnership.
    • Rituals involving fire, such as lighting candles or performing havans, can serve as a symbolic act of invoking Agni for protection and purification.
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals may turn to meditation or prayer, focusing their thoughts on positive outcomes and seeking guidance from wise mentors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine protection and guidance in the face of adversity.
    • It highlights the power of unity and the importance of remaining unharmed while confronting negative influences.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience, seek support from others, and engage in spiritual practices for protection and clarity.
