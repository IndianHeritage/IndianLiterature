त्वं एतान् रुदतो जक्षतश्चायोधयो रजस इन्द्र पारे |अवाधहो दिव आ दस्युमucciā प्र सुन्वतः स्तुवतः शंसम आवः ||
tvam etān rudato jakṣataś cāyodhayo rajasa indra pāre | avādaho diva ā dasyum uccā pra sunvataḥ stuvataḥ śaṁsam āvaḥ ||
  • tvam etān rudato jakṣataś cāyodhayo rajasa indra pāre
    • tvam - "You" or "Thou": This refers to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • etān - "these": Referring to the beings or entities that are being addressed.
    • rudato - "who are crying" or "who are in distress": This signifies beings in need of help or support.
    • jakṣataś - "who are watching" or "who are observing": This indicates the divine presence that is aware of the struggles of beings.
    • cāyodhayo - "and those who are fighting": This can refer to warriors or those engaged in conflict, seeking strength and victory.
    • rajasa - "in the realm of passion or activity": This highlights the dynamic nature of existence, where actions and emotions play a crucial role.
    • indra pāre - "beyond Indra": This suggests a transcendence or a higher state of being that is sought after.
  • avādaho diva ā dasyum uccā pra sunvataḥ stuvataḥ śaṁsam āvaḥ
    • avādaho - "I call upon": This is an invocation, a plea for assistance or intervention.
    • diva - "from the heavens": This indicates a divine source or celestial realm.
    • ā dasyum - "to remove the evil": This signifies the desire to eliminate negativity or malevolence.
    • uccā - "high" or "elevated": This refers to a state of being that is above the ordinary.
    • pra sunvataḥ - "for those who are devoted": This emphasizes the importance of devotion and sincerity in seeking divine help.
    • stuvataḥ - "who praise": This highlights the act of worship and reverence towards the divine.
    • śaṁsam āvaḥ - "bring peace or well-being": This is a request for harmony and prosperity in life.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention in times of distress and conflict. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the struggles of others and the need for a higher power to guide and protect. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of personal crisis, individuals may invoke this mantra to seek strength and support from a higher power.
  • Communities facing challenges, such as natural disasters or social unrest, can come together in prayer, invoking the divine for peace and resolution.
  • In a broader sense, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of compassion and support in overcoming adversity.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the pursuit of peace, making it relevant in both personal and collective contexts.
