ऋभुर ऋभुक्षा ऋभुर विधतो मद ā ते हरī जूजुवानस्य वाजिनाः |दुष्टरं यस्य साम चिद ऋधग यज्ञो न मानवः ||
ṛbhur ṛbhukṣā ṛbhur vidhato mada ā te harī jūjuvānasya vājinā | duṣṭaraṁ yasya sāma cid ṛdhag yajño na mānuṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛbhur: The one who is noble or virtuous.
    • ṛbhukṣā: The one who provides sustenance or nourishment.
    • ṛbhur vidhato: The one who creates or establishes virtue.
    • mada: Joy or ecstasy.
    • ā te: To you (addressing the divine).
    • harī: The divine or the one who is auspicious.
    • jūjuvānasya: Of the one who is engaged in the act of worship or devotion.
    • vājinā: The one who is powerful or strong.
    • duṣṭaraṁ: Difficult or challenging.
    • yasya: Whose.
    • sāma: Song or hymn.
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • ṛdhag: Prosperity or abundance.
    • yajño: Sacrifice or offering.
    • na mānuṣaḥ: Not human or beyond human.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of nobility, sustenance, and creation of virtue.
    • It emphasizes the joy and ecstasy that come from devotion and worship.
    • The reference to the divine as "harī" signifies auspiciousness and benevolence.
    • The mantra acknowledges the challenges faced in life and the power of divine support in overcoming them.
    • It suggests that true prosperity and abundance come from divine connection and sacrifice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when individuals come together to support each other, they embody the qualities of "ṛbhur" by being virtuous and providing sustenance.
    • During festivals, people engage in acts of devotion, experiencing "mada" through joy and celebration, which strengthens community bonds.
    • In challenging times, such as during a natural disaster, the collective effort to help one another reflects the essence of "vājinā," showcasing strength and resilience.
    • Practicing gratitude and mindfulness can lead to a deeper awareness of life's abundance, aligning with the concept of "cid" and "ṛdhag."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of virtue, community support, and divine connection in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in acts of devotion and sacrifice, leading to personal and communal prosperity.
    • In a world filled with challenges, the mantra inspires us to seek strength in our connections with the divine and with each other.
    • Ultimately, it highlights that true abundance is not just material but also spiritual, arising from our relationships and our commitment to virtuous living.
