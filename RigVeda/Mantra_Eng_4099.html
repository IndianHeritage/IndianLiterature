युṣ्माकं स्मा रथांऽ अनु मुडे दधे मरुतो जीरददानवः | वृष्टि dyāvo यतीर इव ||
yuṣmākaṁ smā rathām̐ anu mude dadhe maruto jīradānavaḥ | vṛṣṭī dyāvo yatīr iva ||
  • yuṣmākaṁ - This word translates to "your" or "yours," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
  • smā - This can be interpreted as "to you" or "for you," suggesting a direction of action or intention towards the addressed.
  • rathām̐ - This means "chariot," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • anu - This word means "after" or "following," indicating a sequence or a pursuit.
  • mude - This translates to "joy" or "happiness," emphasizing the emotional state associated with the action.
  • dadhe - This means "to place" or "to establish," indicating an act of creation or setting something in motion.
  • maruto - Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power, energy, and the forces of nature.
  • jīradānavaḥ - This can be interpreted as "the giver of life," suggesting a nurturing or life-sustaining aspect.
  • vṛṣṭī - This means "rain" or "shower," often associated with abundance and fertility.
  • dyāvo - This translates to "heaven" or "sky," representing the divine or higher realms.
  • yatīr - This word means "to flow" or "to move," indicating a dynamic process.
  • iva - This means "like" or "as," used for comparison or simile.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for joy and abundance, where the speaker is asking for the blessings of the Maruts to bring forth happiness and prosperity. The imagery of a chariot suggests a journey, and the act of placing joy in the chariot indicates a desire to carry happiness forward in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as one would seek joy and abundance in their personal journey, this mantra encourages individuals to pursue happiness actively.
  • Community Well-being: The invocation of the Maruts can symbolize the collective energy of a community working together to create a joyful and prosperous environment.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to rain and the forces of nature can remind us of the importance of nurturing our environment for sustainable living.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for joy, abundance, and the nurturing forces of nature. It serves as a reminder to actively seek happiness and to recognize the interconnectedness of our journeys with the divine and the natural world. By invoking these energies, we can foster a more fulfilling and prosperous life, both individually and collectively.
