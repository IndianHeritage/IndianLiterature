मन्त्र: उता द्युमत् सुवीर्यं बृहद् अग्ने विवाससि |देवेभ्यो देव दाशुषे ||
uta dyumat suvīryam bṛhad agne vivāsasi | devebhyo deva dāśuṣe ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • dyumat - This term means "shining" or "radiant," often used to describe something that emits light or brilliance.
  • suvīryam - This word translates to "great strength" or "valiant," referring to someone or something that possesses exceptional power or courage.
  • bṛhad - Meaning "great" or "vast," this term emphasizes the magnitude or importance of what is being described.
  • agne - This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in fire.
  • vivāsasi - This word means "you reside" or "you dwell," indicating a presence or existence in a particular space.
  • devebhyo - This translates to "to the gods," referring to the divine beings in the Vedic pantheon.
  • deva - This word means "god" or "divine," often used to denote a higher power or deity.
  • dāśuṣe - This term means "to the worshipper" or "to the devotee," indicating a relationship between the divine and those who seek to honor or serve them.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, the radiant and powerful fire god, asking for his presence and blessings. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the worshippers, highlighting the importance of strength and brilliance in both the divine and human realms.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Strength: Just as Agni represents strength and brilliance, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to cultivate their own inner strength and resilience in challenging situations.
  • Community and Collaboration: The invocation to the gods signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in communal efforts, such as teamwork in professional settings or collective community initiatives.
  • Spiritual Practices: This mantra can be recited during rituals or meditation to invoke the energy of Agni, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and enhancing spiritual growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of strength, brilliance, and divine connection in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek inner power while recognizing the importance of community and spirituality in achieving their goals. By invoking Agni, worshippers can aspire to embody the qualities of courage and radiance in their daily lives.
