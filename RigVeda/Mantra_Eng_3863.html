स्त्रियः हि दास आयुधानि चक्रे किम मा करण्न अबला अस्य सेनाः |अन्तर ह्य अख्यद उभे अस्य धेन अथोप प्रैद युद्धये दस्युम इन्द्रः ||
striyo hi dāsa āyudhāni cakre kim mā karann abalā asya senāḥ | antar hy akhyad ubhe asya dhene athopa praid yudhaye dasyum indraḥ ||
  • striyo hi dāsa āyudhāni cakre: This phrase translates to "Women indeed have made weapons for the servants." Here, "striyo" refers to women, "dāsa" refers to servants or those who serve, and "āyudhāni" means weapons. This can be interpreted as recognizing the role of women in supporting and empowering those who serve or protect.
  • kim mā karann abalā asya senāḥ: This translates to "What should the weak ones of this army do?" The term "abalā" refers to the weak or vulnerable, and "senāḥ" means army. This line raises a question about the role and agency of those who may be perceived as weak in a collective effort, emphasizing the need for inclusivity and support.
  • antar hy akhyad ubhe asya dhene: This means "Indeed, within, both are known." The word "antar" means within, "akhyad" means known, and "ubhe" refers to both. This suggests that there is an inner understanding or recognition of both strength and weakness, highlighting the importance of self-awareness and mutual respect.
  • athopa praid yudhaye dasyum indraḥ: This translates to "And thus, Indra leads the fight against the enemy." "Indra" is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and leadership. This line signifies the importance of strong leadership in overcoming challenges and adversaries.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the roles of women, the importance of recognizing both strength and vulnerability, and the necessity of strong leadership in facing challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various contexts such as gender equality, where the contributions of women are acknowledged and valued in all spheres, including leadership roles. It also highlights the need for support systems for those who may feel weak or marginalized, ensuring that everyone has a role in collective efforts. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inclusivity, empowerment, and the recognition of diverse strengths within a community, which is essential for achieving common goals and overcoming adversities.
