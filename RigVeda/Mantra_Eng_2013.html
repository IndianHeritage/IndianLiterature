तवम् अग्ने द्रविणोदाऽ अरंक्रते त्वं देवः सविता रत्नधा असि |त्वं भगो नृपतिः वस्व ईशिषे त्वं पायुर दमे यस् तेऽविदत् ||
tvam agne draviṇodā araṁkṛte tvaṁ devaḥ savitā ratnadhā asi | tvam bhago nṛpate vasva īśiṣe tvam pāyur dame yas te 'vidhat ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a divine force in Vedic literature. In real life, fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • draviṇodā - "who brings wealth" - This indicates the ability of Agni to bestow prosperity and abundance. In modern terms, this can be related to the pursuit of wealth through hard work and dedication.
  • araṁkṛte - "in the form of" - This suggests that Agni manifests in various forms, much like how energy can take different shapes in our lives, such as creativity, passion, or motivation.
  • tvaṁ devaḥ savitā - "You are the divine Savita" - Savita is another name for the sun, representing illumination and life. This can be likened to the role of the sun in agriculture, where it is essential for growth and sustenance.
  • ratnadhā asi - "you are the holder of jewels" - This phrase signifies that Agni possesses great treasures, which can be interpreted as the knowledge and wisdom that guide us in life. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of education and learning.
  • tvam bhago nṛpate - "You, O Lord of wealth" - This emphasizes the divine aspect of wealth and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we seek financial stability and success.
  • vasva īśiṣe - "who rules over all" - This indicates the omnipresence of Agni and its influence in all aspects of life. It can be compared to the universal laws of nature that govern our existence.
  • tvam pāyur dame - "You protect the household" - This highlights the protective nature of Agni, akin to how families seek safety and security in their homes. It reflects the importance of nurturing and safeguarding our loved ones.
  • yas te 'vidhat - "who has established" - This suggests that Agni has a role in creating order and structure in the universe. In real life, this can be related to the systems and rules we establish in society for harmony and progress.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the multifaceted nature of Agni as a divine force that brings wealth, protection, and order to our lives. It signifies the importance of seeking knowledge, prosperity, and security while recognizing the transformative power of energy in various forms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with dedication, protect their families, and appreciate the wealth of knowledge and resources available to them. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the divine presence that guides us through our journeys.
