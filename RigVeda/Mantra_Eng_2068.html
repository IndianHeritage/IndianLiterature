स नो वृष्टिं दिवस परि स नो वाजम् अनर्वाणम् |स नः सहस्रीणीः इषः ||
sa no vṛṣṭiṁ divas pari sa no vājam anarvāṇam | sa naḥ sahasriṇīr iṣaḥ ||
  • sa no vṛṣṭiṁ divas pari: This phrase can be translated to "May the rain (vṛṣṭi) come to us from the heavens (divas)." Here, "vṛṣṭi" refers to rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance, while "divas" signifies the sky or heavens. This invocation emphasizes the importance of rain in sustaining life on Earth.
  • sa no vājam anarvāṇam: This translates to "May we have the strength (vājam) that is invincible (anarvāṇam)." The term "vājam" refers to strength or vigor, and "anarvāṇam" implies something that cannot be defeated or is unassailable. This part of the mantra expresses a desire for resilience and fortitude in the face of challenges.
  • sa naḥ sahasriṇīr iṣaḥ: This can be interpreted as "May we be endowed with a thousand (sahasriṇīr) powers (iṣaḥ)." The word "sahasriṇīr" denotes abundance or a thousandfold, while "iṣaḥ" refers to power or capability. This line seeks blessings for abundant strength and capabilities to overcome obstacles.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra as a whole is a prayer for rain, strength, and abundant capabilities. It reflects the interconnectedness of nature and human existence, highlighting the reliance on natural elements for survival and prosperity.
  • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in agricultural communities where rain is vital for crops. Farmers may chant this mantra during droughts or before the planting season to invoke favorable weather conditions.
  • Moreover, the themes of resilience and strength resonate in various aspects of life, such as personal challenges, community struggles, or even in professional settings where teamwork and collaboration are essential for success.
  • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony with nature and the need for inner strength to navigate life's challenges.
