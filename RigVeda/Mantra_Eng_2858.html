आ याह्य अर्वाङ् उप वन्धुरेष्ठास तवेद अनु प्रदिवः सोमपेयं |प्रियā सखायā वि मुचोप बरहिस त्वाम इमे हव्यवाहो हवन्ते ||
ā yāhy arvāṅ upa vandhureṣṭhās taved anu pradivaḥ somapeyam | priyā sakhāyā vi mucopa barhis tvām ime havyavāho havante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāhy: "Come to us" or "May you come."
    • arvāṅ: "The one who is full of strength."
    • upa: "Near" or "Close."
    • vandhureṣṭhās: "The one who is worshipped."
    • taved: "Your power" or "Your strength."
    • anu: "Following" or "After."
    • pradivaḥ: "In the heavenly realm."
    • somapeyam: "The drink of immortality" or "Soma drink."
    • priyā: "Beloved" or "Dear."
    • sakhāyā: "Friend" or "Companion."
    • vi: "Out" or "Away."
    • mucopa: "Release" or "Liberate."
    • barhis: "The sacred grass" or "The altar."
    • tvām: "You."
    • ime: "These."
    • havyavāho: "Those who carry offerings."
    • havante: "Who bring offerings."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to a divine presence, inviting strength and blessings into the worshipper's life.
    • The reference to "Soma" signifies the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment and immortality, which can be likened to the quest for knowledge and wisdom in modern life.
    • The phrase "release you" suggests a desire for liberation from worldly attachments, akin to the modern practice of mindfulness and letting go of stress.
    • The invocation of friends and companions highlights the importance of community and support in achieving personal and spiritual goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams come together to seek guidance and strength for a project, emphasizing collaboration and shared goals.
    • In personal relationships, it can represent the importance of having supportive friends who help each other grow and overcome challenges.
    • During times of stress, individuals may find solace in meditation or spiritual practices that echo the themes of this mantra, seeking inner peace and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine strength, community support, and the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional collaboration, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our journeys.
    • By invoking these principles, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
