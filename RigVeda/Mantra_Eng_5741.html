अतारीष्म तमसस् पारम् अस्य प्रति स्तोमं देवयन्तो दधानाः |पुरुदंसां पुरुतमा पुराजामर्त्या हवते अश्विनाः गीः ||
atāriṣma tamasas pāram asya prati stomaṁ devayanto dadhānāḥ | purudaṁsā purutamā purājāmartyā havate aśvinā gīḥ ||
  • atāriṣma: This term can be understood as "to transcend" or "to go beyond." It signifies the act of overcoming obstacles or limitations.
  • tamasas: This word means "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the challenges and difficulties that one faces in life.
  • pāram: This translates to "the other side" or "beyond." It indicates a state of enlightenment or clarity that one seeks to achieve.
  • asya: Meaning "of this" or "of this world," it refers to the current existence and experiences we encounter.
  • prati: This means "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting a movement or progression towards a goal.
  • stomaṁ: This term refers to "praise" or "song," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • devayanto: This translates to "those who are divine" or "the gods," representing higher powers or forces that guide and support us.
  • dadhānāḥ: Meaning "holding" or "possessing," it implies the act of maintaining or nurturing divine qualities.
  • purudaṁsā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the strong one," symbolizing strength and capability.
  • purutamā: Meaning "the most excellent" or "the best," it signifies the highest ideals or virtues one should aspire to.
  • purājāmartyā: This translates to "the ancient immortal," representing timeless wisdom and knowledge that transcends mortality.
  • havate: This means "to invoke" or "to call upon," indicating the act of seeking assistance or blessings from higher powers.
  • aśvinā: Referring to "the Ashvins," the twin gods of medicine and health in Vedic tradition, symbolizing healing and well-being.
  • gīḥ: This translates to "song" or "hymn," emphasizing the importance of vocal expression in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of transcending darkness and ignorance to reach a state of enlightenment and divine connection. It emphasizes the importance of invoking higher powers, expressing gratitude, and aspiring towards excellence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, overcoming challenges, and seeking guidance in difficult times. By recognizing the divine forces at play and striving for higher ideals, individuals can navigate their lives with purpose and clarity.
