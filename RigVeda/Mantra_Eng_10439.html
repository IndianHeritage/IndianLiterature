यस्त्वा भ्राता पतिर्भूत्वा जारो भूत्वा निपद्यते |प्रजां यस्ते जिघांसति तं इतो नाशयामसि ||
yas tvā bhrātā patir bhūtvā jāro bhūtvā nipadyate | prajāṁ yas te jighāṁsati tam ito nāśayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • bhrātā: brother
    • patir: husband
    • bhūtvā: becoming
    • jāro: enemy
    • nipadyate: falls upon
    • prajāṁ: progeny
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • jighāṁsati: desires to harm
    • tam: him
    • ito: from here
    • nāśayāmasi: we destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses a protective invocation against those who wish harm upon one's family or loved ones.
    • The first part speaks of a person who may take on roles such as a brother or husband but then turns into an enemy.
    • The second part emphasizes the desire to protect one's progeny from harm, indicating a strong familial bond and the instinct to safeguard loved ones.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be relevant when dealing with conflicts where a trusted individual may betray the family, highlighting the need for vigilance and protection.
    • In a workplace, it can symbolize the importance of being cautious of colleagues who may appear friendly but have ulterior motives that could harm one's career or reputation.
    • In community relationships, it serves as a reminder to be aware of those who may pose a threat to the collective well-being, urging proactive measures to ensure safety.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of vigilance and protection against betrayal and harm, especially towards one's family and loved ones.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, reminding individuals to be aware of their surroundings and the intentions of those around them.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a proactive approach to safeguarding relationships and ensuring the well-being of one's community.
