उतासी मैत्रावरुणो वसिष्ठोर्वश्यā ब्रह्मन् मनसोऽधि जातः |द्रप्सं स्कन्नं ब्रह्मणा दैव्यम् विश्वे देवाः पुष्करे त्वाददन्त ||
utāsi maitrāvaruṇo vasiṣṭhorvaśyā brahman manaso 'dhi jātaḥ | drapsaṁ skannam brahmaṇā daivyena viśve devāḥ puṣkare tvādadanta ||
  • Mantra: "utāsi maitrāvaruṇo vasiṣṭhorvaśyā brahman manaso 'dhi jātaḥ | drapsaṁ skannam brahmaṇā daivyena viśve devāḥ puṣkare tvādadanta ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • utāsi: You have risen
    • maitrāvaruṇo: of the friendly Varuna
    • vasiṣṭha: of the sage Vasistha
    • or: or
    • vaśyā: under control
    • brahman: the ultimate reality or consciousness
    • manaso: of the mind
    • 'dhi: in the intellect
    • jātaḥ: born or created
    • drapsaṁ: the desire
    • skannam: to be fulfilled
    • brahmaṇā: by the divine
    • daivyena: divine
    • viśve: all
    • devāḥ: deities
    • puṣkare: in the pure
    • tvā: you
    • adanta: unbroken or unyielding
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Varuna, a deity associated with cosmic order and water, and Vasistha, a revered sage known for his wisdom.
    • The phrase "You have risen" signifies the awakening of consciousness and awareness, suggesting a moment of enlightenment or realization.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of controlling the mind and intellect, indicating that true understanding and fulfillment come from within.
    • The mention of "desire to be fulfilled" highlights the human experience of longing and aspiration, which can be directed towards higher spiritual goals.
    • The invocation of "all deities" suggests a holistic approach to spirituality, recognizing the interconnectedness of various divine forces.
    • The term "in the pure" indicates a state of clarity and purity of thought, essential for spiritual growth and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during meditation or mindfulness practices, where one seeks to rise above distractions and connect with their inner self.
    • When facing challenges, individuals can reflect on the idea of controlling their thoughts and desires, leading to more thoughtful and deliberate actions.
    • In moments of decision-making, invoking the wisdom of sages like Vasistha can inspire individuals to seek clarity and make choices aligned with their higher purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of inner awareness, control over the mind, and the pursuit of higher spiritual goals.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to guide individuals towards a more profound understanding of themselves and their place in the universe.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra in real life, one can cultivate a sense of peace, purpose, and connection with the divine.
