मन्त्र: वेर अध्वरस्य दूत्यानि विद्वान् उभे अन्ता रोडसी संचित्वान् |दूत ईयसे प्रदिव उराणो विदुष्टरो दिव आरोधनानि ||
ver adhvarasya dūtyāni vidvān ubhe antā rodasī saṁcikitvān | dūta īyase pradiva urāṇo viduṣṭaro diva ārodhanāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ver: to be strong or powerful
    • adhvarasya: of the sacrifice or ritual
    • dūtyāni: messengers or agents
    • vidvān: the wise or knowledgeable one
    • ubhe: both
    • antā: ends or boundaries
    • rodasī: the two realms or horizons
    • saṁcikitvān: having understood or comprehended
    • dūta: messenger
    • īyase: you move or go
    • pradiva: towards the sky or heavens
    • urāṇo: the ancient or eternal
    • viduṣṭaro: the wise or learned
    • diva: the sky or heavens
    • ārodhanāni: the ascensions or elevations
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and wisdom of the messengers who traverse the boundaries of the earthly and celestial realms.
    • The "wise one" understands the significance of both realms, indicating a deep comprehension of the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The mention of messengers suggests the importance of communication and the role of intermediaries in conveying messages between different planes of existence.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the role of teachers, mentors, or even technology that helps bridge gaps in understanding and knowledge.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
    • It highlights the role of communication in achieving harmony between different aspects of life, such as personal ambitions and communal responsibilities.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various fields such as education, where teachers act as messengers of knowledge, or in business, where effective communication is key to success.
    • Moreover, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, fostering a sense of interconnectedness with the world around them.
