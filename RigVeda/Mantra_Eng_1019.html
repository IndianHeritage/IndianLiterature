विश्वानि देवी भुवनाभिचक्ष्या प्रतिची चक्षुर उर्वियā वि भाति |विश्वं जीवं चरसे बोधयन्ती विश्वस्य वाचं अविदन मनायोः ||
viśvāni devī bhuvanābhicakṣyā pratīcī cakṣur urviyā vi bhāti | viśvaṁ jīvaṁ carase bodhayantī viśvasya vācam avidan manāyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvāni: all, universal
    • devī: goddess, divine
    • bhuvanābhicakṣyā: who sees all worlds
    • pratīcī: in the direction of the west
    • cakṣur: eye, vision
    • urviyā: wide, expansive
    • vi bhāti: shines, illuminates
    • jīvaṁ: living beings
    • carase: moves, travels
    • bodhayantī: enlightening, awakening
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • vācam: speech, voice
    • avidan: not knowing
    • manāyoḥ: of the minds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that encompasses all aspects of existence.
    • The "goddess" represents a universal consciousness that observes and understands all worlds.
    • The phrase "who sees all worlds" suggests an omniscient perspective, indicating that this divine force is aware of everything happening in the universe.
    • The "eye" symbolizes perception and awareness, while "wide" indicates the vastness of this understanding.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and awakening, suggesting that this divine presence helps living beings to realize their true nature.
    • It also highlights the significance of communication and understanding, as it refers to the "voice" of the universe and the minds that may not be aware of it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of this mantra can be seen as someone who has a broad vision and understands the needs of all employees, fostering an environment of enlightenment and growth.
    • In education, a teacher who inspires students to think critically and encourages them to explore the world around them reflects the essence of this mantra by awakening their minds.
    • In personal relationships, effective communication and understanding can lead to deeper connections, mirroring the mantra's emphasis on the importance of speech and awareness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of divine awareness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and understanding, both within themselves and in their interactions with others.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a greater sense of community.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the divine presence that guides and illuminates our paths, urging us to be more aware and conscious in our daily lives.
