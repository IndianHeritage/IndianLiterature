तं उ त्वा पाथ्यो वृषा सम ईधे दस्युहन्तमम् |धनञ्जयं रणेरणे ||
tam u tvā pāthyo vṛṣā sam īdhe dasyuhantamam | dhanaṁjayaṁ raṇe-raṇe ||
  • tam u tvā: This phrase can be translated as "to you" or "to that." It signifies a direction or focus towards a particular entity or concept.
  • pāthyo: This word means "the path" or "the way." It suggests guidance or a method to achieve something.
  • vṛṣā: This term translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It often refers to strength, valor, or might.
  • sam īdhe: This means "may I be inspired" or "may I be guided." It indicates a desire for divine inspiration or support.
  • dasyuhantamam: This word can be interpreted as "the one who destroys the enemies" or "the conqueror of foes." It refers to overcoming obstacles or adversaries.
  • dhanaṁjayaṁ: This translates to "the one who brings wealth" or "the victorious." It signifies success and prosperity.
  • raṇe-raṇe: This phrase means "in battle" or "in conflict." It emphasizes the context of struggle or competition.

The mantra can be understood as a call for strength and guidance in overcoming challenges and adversaries, particularly in the context of conflict or competition. It expresses a desire for divine support in achieving victory and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as career obstacles or personal conflicts, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance.
  • Competitive Situations: In competitive environments like sports or business, this mantra can serve as a source of motivation to overcome rivals and achieve success.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra can also be used in a spiritual context, where individuals seek inner strength and clarity to navigate life's battles.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and guidance in the face of challenges. It serves as a reminder that with divine support, one can overcome adversities and achieve success in various aspects of life. Whether in personal struggles, competitive endeavors, or spiritual journeys, the essence of this mantra is relevant and applicable, encouraging individuals to strive for victory and prosperity.
