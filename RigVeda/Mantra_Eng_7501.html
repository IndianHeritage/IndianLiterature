इन्द्राय मद्वने सुतं परि ष्टोभन्तु नो गिरः |अर्कं अर्चन्तु कारवः ||
indrāya madvane sutam pari ṣṭobhantu no giraḥ | arkam arcantu kāravaḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Madvane: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is sweet" or "to the one who brings joy." It signifies the positive attributes associated with Indra, emphasizing his benevolent nature.
  • Sutam: This means "the son" or "the offspring." In this context, it refers to the offerings or the results of one's actions that are dedicated to Indra.
  • Pariṣṭobhantu: This word means "may they be established" or "may they be fulfilled." It expresses a desire for the offerings to be accepted and for blessings to be bestowed.
  • No: This translates to "our" or "to us," indicating a collective appeal for blessings.
  • Giraḥ: This means "words" or "utterances." It signifies the importance of speech and communication in invoking divine blessings.
  • Arkam: This term refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with the sun or divine illumination. It symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Arcantu: This means "may they worship" or "may they honor." It indicates the act of reverence and devotion towards the divine.
  • Kāravaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in action" or "those who perform." It emphasizes the importance of active participation in rituals and devotion.

Conclusion: The mantra "indrāya madvane sutam pari ṣṭobhantu no giraḥ | arkam arcantu kāravaḥ ||" encapsulates a prayer for divine blessings and the fulfillment of desires through the auspices of Indra. It highlights the significance of communication, action, and devotion in seeking spiritual and material prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers where individuals seek guidance, strength, and success in their endeavors. By invoking the qualities of Indra, practitioners can cultivate resilience and positivity in their lives, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose in their spiritual journeys.
