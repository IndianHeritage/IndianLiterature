ऊर्जं गावो यवसे पीवो अत्तन ऋतस्य याः सदने कोशे अंग्ध्वे |तनूर एव तन्वो अस्तु भेषजम् आ सर्वतातिम अदितिं वृणीमहे ||
ūrjaṁ gāvo yavase pīvo attana ṛtasya yāḥ sadane kośe aṅgdhve | tanūr eva tanvo astu bheṣajam ā sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe ||
  • ūrjaṁ: This word translates to "energy" or "vitality." In real life, it can be associated with the energy we derive from food, exercise, and positive interactions.
  • gāvo: This means "cows," which in Vedic culture symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance. In modern terms, cows are often seen as a source of dairy products and are integral to agricultural economies.
  • yavase: This translates to "in the grains" or "in the harvest." It signifies the importance of agriculture and sustenance in our lives, highlighting how grains are fundamental to our diet.
  • pīvo: Meaning "to drink" or "to consume," this word emphasizes the act of taking in nourishment, whether it be food, knowledge, or experiences.
  • attana: This can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful." It reflects the strength we derive from our resources and relationships.
  • ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of order." It signifies the importance of living in harmony with the natural laws and truths of the universe.
  • yāḥ: This means "who" or "which," referring to the entities or aspects being discussed.
  • sadane: This translates to "in the abode" or "in the home," indicating a place of safety and comfort.
  • kośe: Meaning "in the treasure" or "in the wealth," it refers to the abundance we possess, both materially and spiritually.
  • aṅgdhve: This word can be interpreted as "to be fragrant" or "to be auspicious," suggesting the positive qualities of the resources we have.
  • tanūr: This means "body" or "form," indicating the physical aspect of existence.
  • eva: This translates to "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statement being made.
  • tanvo: This means "of the body," emphasizing the connection between our physical form and our experiences.
  • astu: This translates to "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or intention.
  • bheṣajam: Meaning "medicine" or "healing," it signifies the importance of health and wellness in our lives.
  • ā: This means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • sarvatātim: This translates to "all-pervading" or "universal," suggesting a connection to the greater cosmos.
  • aditiṁ: This word means "the infinite" or "the boundless," representing the limitless potential of existence.
  • vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating an active participation in the process of creation and manifestation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of energy, nourishment, strength, truth, and abundance in our lives. It calls upon the divine to bless us with health and prosperity, recognizing the importance of both physical and spiritual wealth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, yoga, and rituals that focus on gratitude for the resources we have. It serves as a reminder to live in harmony with nature and to seek balance in our lives, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous existence.
