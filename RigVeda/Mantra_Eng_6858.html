मन्त्र: उता त्वाग्ने मम स्तुतो वाश्राय प्रतिहर्यते |गोष्टं गावा इवाशत ||
uta tvāgne mama stuto vāśrāya pratiharyate | goṣṭhaṁ gāva ivāśata ||
  • Mantra: "uta tvāgne mama stuto vāśrāya pratiharyate | goṣṭhaṁ gāva ivāśata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: indeed, or also
    • tvāgne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • mama: my
    • stuto: praised or worshipped
    • vāśrāya: for protection or shelter
    • pratiharyate: is offered or is invoked
    • goṣṭhaṁ: the herd or group
    • gāva: of cows
    • ivāśata: like or as if
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the deity representing fire, which is often seen as a purifying and protective force.
    • The speaker acknowledges Agni as a source of praise and seeks his protection, similar to how a herd of cows is safeguarded.
    • In real life, this can be likened to how people seek the blessings of a higher power for safety and security in their lives, much like how a farmer ensures the safety of their livestock.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in our daily lives.
    • It reflects the human desire for safety, community, and the nurturing aspect of nature, represented by the herd of cows.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied in various situations, such as during family gatherings, community events, or even in personal endeavors where one seeks support and protection from challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the need for divine guidance.
    • It encourages individuals to seek protection not just for themselves but for their loved ones and their community, fostering a sense of unity and collective well-being.
    • In essence, it teaches us to honor and invoke the forces that protect and nurture us, much like how we care for our own families and communities.
