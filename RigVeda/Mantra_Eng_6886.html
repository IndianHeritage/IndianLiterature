अग्निः प्रात्नेना मनमनाऽ शुम्भानस् तन्‍वम् स्वाम् |कविः विप्रेण वावृधे ||
agniḥ pratnena manmanā śumbhānas tanva1ṁ svām | kavir vipreṇa vāvṛdhe ||
  • Agniḥ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Pratnena: This term means "with devotion" or "with intention." It emphasizes the importance of sincere effort and focus in one's actions.
  • Manmanā: This translates to "with the mind" or "with thought." It suggests that mental engagement and mindfulness are crucial in any endeavor.
  • Śumbhānas: This word can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be supported." It indicates the need for sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
  • Tanvaṁ: This means "body" or "form." It highlights the physical aspect of existence and the importance of taking care of one's body.
  • Svām: This translates to "lord" or "master." It signifies the idea of self-mastery and control over one's life and actions.
  • Kavir: This term means "the wise one" or "the poet." It represents knowledge, creativity, and the ability to express oneself.
  • Vipreṇa: This translates to "with the learned" or "with the wise." It emphasizes the value of learning and wisdom in guiding one's actions.
  • Vāvṛdhe: This means "to grow" or "to flourish." It signifies the potential for development and success through the right actions and mindset.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, mindfulness, and the pursuit of knowledge. It teaches us that by engaging our minds and bodies with intention and wisdom, we can nourish ourselves and grow in various aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, education, and spiritual practices. For instance, when pursuing a goal, one should approach it with dedication (pratnena), remain mindful (manmanā), and seek knowledge from wise mentors (vipreṇa) to achieve success (vāvṛdhe). This holistic approach can lead to a fulfilling and balanced life.
