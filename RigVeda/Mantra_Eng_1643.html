तं अस्य राजा वरुणस्तम अश्विनां क्रतुः सचन्त मारुतस्य वेधसः |दाधार दक्षं उत्तमम् अहर्विदं व्रजं च विष्णुः सखिवाम् अपोर्णुते ||
tam asya rājā varuṇas tam aśvinā kratuṁ sacanta mārutasya vedhasaḥ | dādhāra dakṣam uttamam aharvidaṁ vrajaṁ ca viṣṇuḥ sakhivām̐ aporṇute ||
  • tam asya rājā varuṇas: This phrase refers to Varuna, the king of the waters and the celestial ruler. Varuna is often associated with the moral order and cosmic law (Rita). In real life, this can be likened to the concept of justice and governance in society, where leaders are expected to uphold moral values.
  • tam aśvinā kratuṁ sacanta: The Aśvins are twin deities representing health, medicine, and the dawn. "Kratuṁ sacanta" implies the pursuit of wisdom and knowledge. This can be related to modern-day healthcare and the importance of scientific knowledge in improving human life, as seen in advancements in medicine and technology.
  • mārutasya vedhasaḥ: Mārutas are the storm deities, symbolizing strength and power. "Vedhasaḥ" refers to the wise or knowledgeable. This can be interpreted as the need for strength and wisdom in overcoming challenges, similar to how individuals or communities face natural disasters or personal adversities with resilience.
  • dādhāra dakṣam uttamam: This phrase means "he who holds the best and most capable." It signifies the importance of competence and excellence in leadership and action. In real life, this can be seen in successful leaders or organizations that strive for excellence in their fields, such as innovative companies that lead in technology.
  • aharvidaṁ vrajaṁ ca viṣṇuḥ: Here, "aharvidaṁ" refers to the daily sustenance or nourishment, while "vrajaṁ" means to go or to move. Viṣṇu is the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology. This can be related to the importance of sustainability and the preservation of resources in our daily lives, akin to how communities work towards environmental conservation.
  • sakhivām̐ aporṇute: This phrase suggests the idea of friendship and support. "Aporṇute" means to fulfill or complete. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of community and social bonds, where mutual support leads to collective well-being and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, wisdom, strength, and community support. Its significance lies in the reminder that effective governance and personal conduct should be rooted in moral values, knowledge, and resilience. In today's world, these principles are applicable in various scenarios, from political leadership to personal relationships, emphasizing the need for ethical behavior, continuous learning, and collaboration for a harmonious society.
