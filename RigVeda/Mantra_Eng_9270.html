त्वां अग्ने यजमानाऽनु dyūn विश्वा वसु दधिरे वारीयाणि |त्वया सह द्रविणम् इच्छमानाऽव्रजं गोमन्तम् उषिजो वि वव्रुः ||
tvām agne yajamānā anu dyūn viśvā vasu dadhire vāryāṇi | tvayā saha draviṇam icchamānā vrajaṁ gomantam uśijo vi vavruḥ ||
  • tvām agne - "to you, O Agni" - This phrase addresses Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. In real life, fire is often associated with purification and transformation, much like how we seek to transform our lives through various means.
  • yajamānā - "the one who offers" - This refers to the person performing the yajna (sacrifice). In modern terms, it can be likened to individuals who contribute to community welfare or engage in charitable acts, offering their resources for the greater good.
  • anu dyūn - "following the heavens" - This suggests a connection to higher realms or aspirations. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as striving for higher goals or ideals, such as pursuing education or personal growth.
  • viśvā vasu dadhire vāryāṇi - "all beings have established their wealth" - This line emphasizes the abundance of resources and blessings available to all. In practical terms, it can be seen in how communities come together to share resources, creating a supportive environment for everyone.
  • tvayā saha - "together with you" - This indicates a partnership or collaboration with the divine. In real life, this can be reflected in teamwork and cooperation in various aspects of life, such as in workplaces or community projects.
  • draviṇam icchamānā - "desiring wealth" - This highlights the human aspiration for prosperity. In contemporary society, this can relate to the pursuit of financial stability and success, as well as the ethical considerations that come with it.
  • vrajaṁ gomantam - "the one who moves with wealth" - This suggests a journey towards prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or businesses that actively seek opportunities for growth and development.
  • uśijo vi vavruḥ - "the wise ones have gathered" - This line signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in achieving success. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the value placed on education, mentorship, and the sharing of knowledge within communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity and wisdom through collaboration with the divine and the community. It emphasizes the importance of offering, striving for higher ideals, and recognizing the abundance of resources available to all. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of service, pursue personal growth, and foster cooperation within their communities, ultimately leading to a more prosperous and harmonious society.
