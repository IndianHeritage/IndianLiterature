यद īं सुतास इन्दवोऽभि प्रियं अमन्दिषुः |आपो न धायि सवनं मा आवसो दुधा इवोप दाशुषे ||
yad īṁ sutāsa indavo 'bhi priyam amandiṣuḥ | āpo na dhāyi savanam ma ā vaso dughā ivopa dāśuṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • īṁ: the essence or the best
    • sutāsa: of the sons or offspring
    • indavo: the divine beings or deities
    • 'bhi: with or through
    • priyam: dear or beloved
    • amandiṣuḥ: in the joyous or blissful
    • āpo: waters
    • na: not
    • dhāyi: to hold or to support
    • savanam: the offering or sacrifice
    • ma: not
    • ā: to
    • vaso: of the dwelling or home
    • dughā: milk or nourishment
    • ivopa: like or as if
    • dāśuṣe: to the giver or benefactor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings made to the divine beings, emphasizing the importance of joy and nourishment in the process of worship.
    • The phrase "yad īṁ sutāsa indavo 'bhi priyam amandiṣuḥ" suggests that whatever is offered to the deities should be cherished and filled with joy.
    • The mention of "āpo na dhāyi savanam" indicates that the waters (or the essence of life) should not be withheld from the offerings, symbolizing the importance of sharing and generosity.
    • The concluding part "ma ā vaso dughā ivopa dāśuṣe" implies that one should not hold back the nourishment, akin to how a nurturing mother provides milk to her child, highlighting the significance of giving and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often share food and resources, embodying the spirit of generosity and joy that this mantra promotes.
    • In a family setting, parents provide for their children selflessly, ensuring that they receive love and nourishment, which reflects the essence of the mantra.
    • In workplaces, fostering a culture of support and collaboration can lead to a more joyful and productive environment, resonating with the idea of offering and sharing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of joy, generosity, and nourishment in our offerings to the divine and to each other.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, including family, community, and professional settings, where the values of support and sharing can lead to a more harmonious existence.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more compassionate and joyful society.
