तं व अभि प्राचतेन्द्रं सोमस्य पीतये |तदिद ध्यस्य वर्धनम् ||
tam v abhi prārcatendraṁ somasya pītaye | tad id dhy asya vardhanam ||
  • tam v abhi prārcatendraṁ somasya pītaye
    • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
    • v - This is a particle meaning "you" or "to you," indicating a direction of reverence or prayer.
    • abhi - This means "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting an approach or invocation.
    • prārcat - This is derived from the root "arc," meaning "to praise" or "to worship." It indicates an act of devotion.
    • indraṁ - Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • somasya - This relates to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • pītaye - This means "for the drink" or "for the consumption," indicating the purpose of the offering or prayer.
  • tad id dhy asya vardhanam
    • tad - This translates to "that," referring back to the divine or the essence being invoked.
    • id - This means "this," indicating the immediate presence or relevance of the invocation.
    • dhy - This is derived from "dhyā," meaning "to meditate" or "to contemplate," suggesting a focus on the divine.
    • asya - This means "of this," indicating possession or connection to the subject of meditation.
    • vardhanam - This translates to "growth" or "nourishment," implying the enhancement or increase of spiritual or material wealth.

Conclusion:

The mantra "tam v abhi prārcatendraṁ somasya pītaye | tad id dhy asya vardhanam ||" is a prayer directed towards Indra, invoking his blessings through the sacred soma drink. It emphasizes the importance of worship and meditation in seeking divine support for growth and prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can meditate on their goals and seek strength and guidance from higher powers to achieve their aspirations.
  • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and prosperity, fostering unity and shared purpose.
  • In times of challenge, individuals can reflect on this mantra to find courage and resilience, drawing inspiration from the divine to overcome obstacles.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support, reminding us of the power of faith, meditation, and community in our journey towards growth and fulfillment.
