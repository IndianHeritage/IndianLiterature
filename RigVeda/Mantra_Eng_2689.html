मन्त्र: सुनिर्मथा निर्मथितः सुनिधा निहितः कविः |अग्नि स्वध्वराः कृणु देवान् देवयते यज ||
sunirmathā nirmathitaḥ sunidhā nihitaḥ kaviḥ | agne svadhvarā kṛṇu devān devayate yaja ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sunirmathā: well-thought-out or well-planned
    • nirmathitaḥ: churned or extracted
    • sunidhā: well-placed or well-arranged
    • nihitaḥ: hidden or contained
    • kaviḥ: the seer or the wise one
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • svadhvarā: with your own strength or power
    • kṛṇu: make or create
    • devān: the gods
    • devayate: for the divine or to the divine
    • yaja: worship or offer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of thoughtful planning and the extraction of wisdom.
    • The phrase "well-thought-out" suggests that actions should be deliberate and purposeful, akin to how one might plan a significant event in life, such as a wedding or a business venture.
    • "Churned or extracted" can be likened to the process of refining ideas or skills, similar to how one might refine a recipe or a business strategy through trial and error.
    • The mention of "the wise one" indicates the need for insight and foresight in our endeavors, much like a mentor guiding a young professional in their career.
    • Agni represents transformation and energy, reminding us that our actions should be fueled by passion and dedication, similar to how a chef uses fire to transform raw ingredients into a delicious meal.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • This mantra can be applied in personal development, where one must thoughtfully plan their goals and extract the necessary skills to achieve them.
    • In a corporate setting, it emphasizes the importance of strategic planning and teamwork, where ideas are churned and refined to create successful projects.
    • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to approach rituals and offerings with intention and clarity, ensuring that they are meaningful and directed towards the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for intentionality and wisdom in our actions.
    • It teaches us that success is not merely a result of hard work but also of thoughtful planning and the ability to harness our inner strength.
    • In everyday life, whether in personal goals, professional endeavors, or spiritual practices, this mantra encourages us to be mindful and deliberate, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful existence.
