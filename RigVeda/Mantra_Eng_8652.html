समु प्रियाः अनूषत गावो मदाय घृष्वयः |सोमासः कृत्वते पथः पवमानास इन्दवः ||
sam u priyā anūṣata gāvo madāya ghṛṣvayaḥ | somāsaḥ kṛṇvate pathaḥ pavamānāsa indavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sam u priyā anūṣata gāvo madāya ghṛṣvayaḥ | somāsaḥ kṛṇvate pathaḥ pavamānāsa indavaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The cows, having been gathered together, are dear; they are brought forth for the purpose of enjoyment. The Soma, the divine drink, is prepared, and the Indus flows along the paths."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: together, in unison
    • u: indeed, truly
    • priyā: dear, beloved
    • anūṣata: having been gathered, assembled
    • gāvo: cows, representing wealth and nourishment
    • madāya: for enjoyment, pleasure
    • ghṛṣvayaḥ: for the purpose of enjoyment, often associated with offerings
    • somāsaḥ: Soma, the sacred drink, symbolizing divine inspiration and ecstasy
    • kṛṇvate: they prepare, they make
    • pathaḥ: paths, ways, routes
    • pavamānāsa: flowing, purifying
    • indavaḥ: the Indus, often symbolizing abundance and prosperity
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and togetherness, as represented by the gathering of cows, which are a symbol of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • The act of preparing Soma signifies the pursuit of higher consciousness and spiritual ecstasy, which can be likened to modern practices of meditation and mindfulness that aim to elevate one's state of being.
    • The reference to paths suggests the various journeys one can take in life, highlighting the importance of choosing the right path for personal and spiritual growth.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied to communal gatherings, celebrations, and rituals where people come together to share joy and abundance, such as festivals, family reunions, or community service events.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of unity, joy, and the pursuit of higher ideals.
    • It encourages individuals to come together for a common purpose, fostering a sense of community and shared experience.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire us to engage in collective activities that promote well-being, harmony, and spiritual growth, reminding us of the importance of connection in our lives.
