यān वो नरो देवयन्तो निमिम्युर वनस्पते स्वधितिर वा तातक्ष || ते देवासः स्वरवस् तस्थिवांसः प्रजावद अस्मे दिधिषन्तु रत्नम् ||
yān vo naro devayanto nimimyur vanaspate svadhitir vā tatakṣa | te devāsaḥ svaravas tasthivāṁsaḥ prajāvad asme didhiṣantu ratnam ||
  • yān vo naro devayanto nimimyur: This phrase can be translated to "those who are devoted to the divine." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the connection between humans and the divine forces. In real life, this can be seen in various religious practices where individuals dedicate their actions and thoughts to a higher power, such as prayer or meditation.
  • vanaspate svadhitir vā tatakṣa: Here, "vanaspate" refers to the plant kingdom, and "svadhitir" signifies self-awareness or self-determination. This part of the mantra suggests that the divine presence is also found in nature and that self-awareness is crucial for personal growth. For example, when people engage in gardening or environmental conservation, they connect with nature and cultivate self-awareness about their impact on the environment.
  • te devāsaḥ svaravas tasthivāṁsaḥ: This translates to "may those divine beings who are established in their essence." It highlights the idea of stability and permanence in the divine. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of inner peace and stability through practices like yoga or mindfulness, where individuals seek to establish a strong foundation within themselves.
  • prajāvad asme didhiṣantu ratnam: This phrase means "may they bestow upon us the wealth of progeny." It signifies the desire for prosperity and abundance, not just in material terms but also in terms of knowledge, wisdom, and relationships. In real life, this can be seen in the aspirations of families to nurture their children and provide them with opportunities for growth and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, self-awareness, stability, and the desire for prosperity. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of humans with the divine and nature, emphasizing the importance of nurturing both spiritual and material wealth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that promote personal growth, environmental stewardship, and the cultivation of meaningful relationships, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
