ईयुष्टे ये पूर्वतराम् अपश्यन् व्युच्छन्तीम् उषसम् मार्त्याः |अस्माभिरू नु प्रतिचक्ष्याभूद् ओ ते यान्ति ये अपरिषु पश्यान् ||
īyuṣ ṭe ye pūrvatarām apaśyan vyucchantīm uṣasam martyāsaḥ | asmābhir ū nu praticakṣyābhūd o te yanti ye aparīṣu paśyān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īyuṣ: Life or vitality
    • ṭe: Your
    • ye: Those who
    • pūrvatarām: Earlier or previous
    • apaśyan: Have seen or perceived
    • vyucchantīm: Separating or distinguishing
    • uṣasam: Dawn or morning
    • martyāsaḥ: Mortals or human beings
    • asmābhir: By us
    • ū: Indeed
    • nu: Now or certainly
    • praticakṣyābhūd: May be revealed or made visible
    • o: O or oh
    • te: You
    • yanti: Go or proceed
    • ye: Those who
    • aparīṣu: In the later or subsequent
    • paśyān: Seeing or perceiving
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the vitality and life that is perceived by those who have experienced the dawn of understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the earlier truths and insights that guide mortals in their journey.
    • The dawn symbolizes new beginnings, enlightenment, and the clarity that comes with knowledge.
    • It suggests that through our collective efforts, we can reveal deeper truths and insights that may not be immediately visible.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often reflect on their past experiences to gain insights that help them navigate their current challenges.
    • In scientific research, breakthroughs often come from building upon previous discoveries, highlighting the importance of historical knowledge.
    • In community building, collective efforts to understand and address past injustices can lead to a more equitable future.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and learning from the past to enhance our present and future.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, education, and social justice.
    • By acknowledging the insights gained from previous experiences, we can foster a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us.
