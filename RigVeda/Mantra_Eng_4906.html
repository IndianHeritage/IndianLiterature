द्युंष पितः पृथिवी मातर अध्रुग अग्ने भ्रातर वसवो मृळता नः |विश्व आदित्याऽऽदिते सजोषा अस्मभ्यं शर्म बहुलं वि यन्त ||
dyau3ṣ pitaḥ pṛthivi mātar adhrug agne bhrātar vasavo mṛḻatā naḥ | viśva ādityā adite sajoṣā asmabhyaṁ śarma bahulaṁ vi yanta ||
  • dyauḥ pitaḥ - "Heavenly Father": This phrase refers to the sky or heaven as a paternal figure, symbolizing protection and guidance. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a father or a guardian who provides support and wisdom to their children.
  • pṛthivi mātar - "Earth Mother": This represents the Earth as a nurturing mother, emphasizing the importance of nature and the environment. In our daily lives, we can see this in the way we rely on the Earth for resources, food, and shelter, highlighting the need for environmental conservation.
  • adhrug agne - "O Agni, the Fire": Agni symbolizes fire, which is essential for warmth, cooking, and rituals. In modern life, fire represents energy and transformation, akin to how we use technology and innovation to improve our lives.
  • bhrātar vasavo - "Brothers of the Vasus": The Vasus are deities representing various natural elements. This phrase suggests unity and cooperation among different forces of nature. In real life, it can be seen in community efforts where individuals come together for a common cause, such as environmental sustainability.
  • mṛḻatā naḥ - "May they bless us": This is a plea for blessings and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as seeking goodwill and positive outcomes in our endeavors, whether in personal relationships or professional pursuits.
  • viśva ādityā - "All the Adityas": The Adityas are solar deities representing various aspects of life and vitality. This phrase emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms. In real life, it can be related to the importance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • adite sajoṣā - "With the support of Aditi": Aditi is the mother of the gods, symbolizing boundlessness and infinity. This suggests the idea of limitless potential and opportunities. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to pursue their dreams without fear of limitations.
  • asmabhyaṁ śarma bahulaṁ - "May we have abundant protection": This is a request for safety and security. In contemporary life, this can be seen in the importance of social safety nets, community support systems, and personal well-being.
  • vi yanta - "May they go forth": This phrase signifies action and movement towards a goal. In real life, it can be interpreted as the importance of taking initiative and making progress in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for the natural world and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the roles of protection, nurturing, and cooperation among the elements of nature and humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards the environment, the importance of community, and the pursuit of our aspirations with the support of the universe. By embodying these principles, we can foster a harmonious existence that respects both our individual journeys and our collective well-being.
