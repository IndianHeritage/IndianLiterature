यद इन्द्र दिवि पार्ये यद ऋधग यद वा स्वे सदने यत्र वासि |अतो नो यज्ञं अवसे नियुत्वान सजोषाः पाहि गिरवणो मरुद्भिः ||
yad indra divi pārye yad ṛdhag yad vā sve sadane yatra vāsi | ato no yajñam avase niyutvān sajoṣāḥ pāhi girvaṇo marudbhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad - whatever
    • indra - Indra, the king of gods
    • divi - in the heavens
    • pārye - in the expanse
    • yad - whatever
    • ṛdhag - is prosperous
    • yad - whatever
    • vā - or
    • sve - in one's own
    • sadane - abode
    • yatra - where
    • vāsi - you reside
    • ato - therefore
    • no - for us
    • yajñam - the sacrifice
    • avase - may you fulfill
    • niyutvān - with abundance
    • sajoṣāḥ - with joy
    • pāhi - protect
    • girvaṇo - the singers
    • marudbhiḥ - with the winds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for prosperity and abundance in both the heavens and one's own home.
    • The repetition of "yad" emphasizes the various aspects of life that are being addressed, highlighting the interconnectedness of divine blessings and human existence.
    • The call for protection and fulfillment of sacrifices signifies the importance of rituals and community in achieving harmony and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Indra is common during the planting season, as rain is crucial for crop growth. Farmers may perform rituals to seek his blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of community support and collaboration in achieving personal and collective goals.
    • In family settings, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for harmony and abundance within the household, encouraging family members to support each other in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for divine blessings, community support, and personal responsibility.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agricultural practices to family dynamics, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our actions and the divine.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek prosperity not just for ourselves, but for our communities and the world around us, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
