प्र सोम याहिंद्रस्य कुक्षा नृभिर येमानो अद्रिभिः सुतः ||
pra soma yāhīndrasya kukṣā nṛbhir yemāno adribhiḥ sutaḥ ||
  • Pra: This word can be understood as "for" or "towards," indicating a direction or purpose.
  • Soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • Yāhī: This term means "come" or "approach," suggesting an invitation or a call to action.
  • Indrasya: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. This word signifies the divine authority or energy.
  • Kukṣā: This translates to "womb" or "source," indicating a place of origin or creation.
  • Nṛbhir: This word means "by men" or "of humans," emphasizing the human aspect in the context of the mantra.
  • Yemāno: This term can be interpreted as "who are moving" or "who are striving," indicating action and effort.
  • Adribhiḥ: This translates to "by the mountains," symbolizing stability, strength, and the natural world.
  • Sutaḥ: This word means "born" or "produced," often referring to offspring or the results of actions.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine energy of Indra, inviting the essence of life (Soma) to come forth from the source (kukṣā) through the efforts of humanity (nṛbhir) and the strength of nature (adribhiḥ). It emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine, human, and natural forces.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to celebrate and invoke blessings for prosperity and health, similar to how this mantra calls for divine energy.
    • In environmental conservation efforts, the strength of nature (mountains) is recognized, and humans strive to protect and nurture the earth, reflecting the mantra's themes of action and respect for the natural world.

  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energy and recognizing the interconnectedness of all life. It teaches us that through our efforts and respect for nature, we can draw upon the strength of the divine to create a harmonious existence. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, environmental stewardship, and personal growth, reminding us to strive for balance and unity in our actions.
