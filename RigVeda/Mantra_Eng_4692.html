स वाह्निभिर ऋक्वभिर गोषु शश्वन् मित्रज्ञुभिः पुरुकृत्वा जिगाय |पुरः पुरोहा सखिभिः सखीयन् दृढा रुरोज कविभिः कविः सन् ||
sa vahnibhir ṛkvabhir goṣu śaśvan mitajñubhiḥ purukṛtvā jigāya | puraḥ purohā sakhibhiḥ sakhīyan dṛḻhā ruroja kavibhiḥ kaviḥ san ||
  • sa vahnibhir: This phrase refers to "with the fires" or "with the flames." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the transformative power of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how fire transforms raw materials into something valuable.
  • ṛkvabhir: This word means "with hymns" or "with verses." It emphasizes the importance of expression and communication, particularly through poetry and sacred texts, which can inspire and elevate human consciousness.
  • goṣu: Translated as "in the cows" or "among the cattle," this term symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and sustenance. Cows are often seen as a source of prosperity and are revered in many cultures, particularly in India.
  • śaśvan: This word means "everlasting" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of certain truths and values that persist through generations.
  • mitajñubhiḥ: This term translates to "with those who know the truth." It highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in our interactions and relationships.
  • purukṛtvā: Meaning "having accomplished many deeds," this phrase underscores the significance of action and achievement in life. It encourages individuals to engage in meaningful work and contribute positively to society.
  • jigāya: This word means "to conquer" or "to achieve." It reflects the idea of overcoming challenges and attaining success through perseverance and effort.
  • puraḥ purohā: Translated as "the chief priest in front," this phrase suggests leadership and guidance. It emphasizes the role of wise leaders in directing communities towards prosperity and harmony.
  • sakhibhiḥ sakhīyan: This means "with friends and companions." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • dṛḻhā: This word means "firm" or "steadfast." It signifies the strength of character and resolve needed to face life's challenges.
  • ruroja: Translated as "to shine" or "to illuminate," this term represents the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • kavibhiḥ kaviḥ san: This phrase means "the poet among poets." It signifies the value of creativity and artistic expression in shaping culture and society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, community, and the transformative power of knowledge and action. It encourages individuals to seek truth, work collaboratively, and strive for personal and collective achievements. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community building, and personal development. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, fostering relationships based on mutual respect and understanding.
