सत्याऽ सत्येभिर् महती महद्भिर् देवी देवैभिर् यजता यजत्रैः |रुजद् दृढानि ददद् उष्रियाणाम् प्रति गावा उषसं वावशन्त ||
satyā satyebhir mahatī mahadbhir devī devebhir yajatā yajatraiḥ | rujad dṛḻhāni dadad usriyāṇām prati gāva uṣasaṁ vāvaśanta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satyā: Truth
    • satyebhir: By truths
    • mahatī: Great
    • mahadbhir: By great ones
    • devī: Goddess
    • devebhir: By the gods
    • yajatā: Worshipped
    • yajatraiḥ: By sacrifices
    • rujad: Free from pain
    • dṛḻhāni: Firmness
    • dadad: Gives
    • usriyāṇām: Of the dawns
    • prati: Towards
    • gāva: Cows
    • uṣasaṁ: Dawn
    • vāvaśanta: They come forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and the divine connection between the worshipper and the divine forces.
    • The phrase "satyā satyebhir" suggests that truth is the foundation of all great things, and it is through truth that one can achieve greatness.
    • The mention of "devī" and "devebhir" indicates the role of divine beings in the process of worship and sacrifice, highlighting the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine.
    • The line "rujad dṛḻhāni dadad" signifies that through devotion and truth, one can attain a state free from pain and suffering, suggesting a path to spiritual and emotional well-being.
    • The reference to "gāva" and "uṣasaṁ" symbolizes abundance and the arrival of new beginnings, akin to the dawn bringing light and hope.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, practicing honesty and transparency can lead to stronger bonds and mutual respect, reflecting the essence of "satyā".
    • In professional settings, integrity in business practices fosters trust and long-term success, embodying the principle of "mahatī".
    • Community service and charitable acts can be seen as modern-day sacrifices ("yajatraiḥ") that contribute to the welfare of society, aligning with the mantra's call for worship through good deeds.
    • Mindfulness and meditation practices can help individuals achieve a state of peace and clarity, resonating with the idea of being "free from pain" ("rujad").
    • The arrival of a new day ("uṣasaṁ") can symbolize new opportunities and fresh starts, encouraging individuals to embrace change and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of truth and the divine in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek truth, engage in acts of worship and sacrifice, and strive for a life free from suffering.
    • Its significance lies in its universal applicability, urging us to cultivate honesty, integrity, and compassion in our daily lives, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can create a harmonious existence that aligns with both spiritual and worldly aspirations.
