दृतेर iva ते 'वृकम अस्तु सख्यम् |अच्छिद्रस्य दधन्वतः सुपूर्णस्य दधन्वतः ||
dṛter iva te 'vṛkam astu sakhyam | acchidrasya dadhanvataḥ supūrṇasya dadhanvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dṛter: of strength or courage
    • iva: like or as
    • te: your
    • 'vṛkam: protection or shield
    • astu: may it be or let it be
    • sakhyam: friendship or companionship
    • acchidrasya: of the one who is without flaws or defects
    • dadhanvataḥ: of the one who possesses or holds
    • supūrṇasya: of the one who is complete or fulfilled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for strength and protection in the context of friendship.
    • It emphasizes the importance of having strong bonds with others, suggesting that true companionship provides a shield against life's challenges.
    • The phrase "of the one who is without flaws" indicates that true friendship is characterized by purity and completeness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, having supportive colleagues can create a protective environment where individuals feel empowered to take risks and innovate.
    • In personal relationships, friends who stand by each other during tough times exemplify the strength and protection that this mantra speaks of.
    • In community settings, strong bonds among members can lead to collective resilience against social challenges, showcasing the power of unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength derived from companionship and the protective nature of true friendship.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that fostering strong, supportive relationships can lead to a more fulfilling and resilient life.
