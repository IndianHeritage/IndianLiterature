वयं तद वः सम्राज आ वृणीमहे पुत्रो न बहुपाय्यम् |अश्याम तद आदित्य जुघ्वतो हविः येन वस्योऽनशामहै ||
vayaṁ tad vaḥ samrāja ā vṛṇīmahe putro na bahupāyyam | aśyāma tad ādityā juhvato havir yena vasyo 'naśāmahai ||
  • vayaṁ tad vaḥ samrāja ā vṛṇīmahe: This phrase can be translated to "We invoke that great ruler (or king)". Here, "vayaṁ" means "we\ "tad" means "that\ "vaḥ" means "of you\ "samrāja" refers to a "great ruler\ and "ā vṛṇīmahe" means "we invoke" or "we choose". This invocation signifies the importance of leadership and guidance in our lives.
  • putro na bahupāyyam: This translates to "may we have a son who is not of many paths". "Putro" means "son\ "na" means "not\ and "bahupāyyam" refers to "many paths". This line emphasizes the desire for a child who is focused and has a clear purpose in life, rather than being scattered in many directions. In real life, this can relate to the importance of having a clear vision and direction in one's personal and professional life.
  • aśyāma tad ādityā juhvato havir: This means "we offer our oblation to the shining one (Aditya)". "Aśyāma" refers to "shining" or "radiant\ "tad" means "that\ "ādityā" refers to "Aditya" (the sun), "juhvato" means "offering\ and "havir" means "oblations". This line highlights the significance of offering gratitude and respect to the divine forces, particularly the sun, which is a source of life and energy. In practical terms, this can be seen in rituals and practices that honor nature and its elements.
  • yena vasyo 'naśāmahai: This translates to "by whom we are made prosperous". "Yena" means "by whom\ "vasyo" refers to "prosperous" or "wealthy\ and "naśāmahai" means "we are made". This line signifies the belief that divine blessings lead to prosperity and well-being. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of ethical living and the understanding that our actions can lead to positive outcomes.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance from a higher power, the importance of focused purpose in life, the act of offering gratitude, and the belief in divine prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in parenting, where the focus is on nurturing children with clear values and direction; in leadership, where guidance and vision are paramount; and in daily practices that honor nature and seek blessings for prosperity. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions, intentions, and the divine forces that influence our lives.
