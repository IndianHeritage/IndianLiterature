कात उपेतिर मनसो वराय भुवद अग्ने शान्तमा का मनिषा |को वा यज्ञैः परि दक्षं त आपा केन वा ते मनसा दाशेम ||
kā ta upetir manaso varāya bhuvad agne śaṁtamā kā manīṣā | ko vā yajñaiḥ pari dakṣaṁ ta āpa kena vā te manasā dāśema ||
  • kā ta upetir manaso varāya: This phrase can be translated as "What is that which is the goal of the mind?" It reflects the quest for understanding the ultimate purpose or aim of our thoughts and desires.
  • bhuvad agne śaṁtamā kā manīṣā: Here, "bhuvad agne" refers to the divine light or fire, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment. "śaṁtamā kā manīṣā" translates to "what is the best thought or intention." This suggests that the pursuit of knowledge and clarity of thought is essential for spiritual growth.
  • ko vā yajñaiḥ pari dakṣaṁ: This line means "Who is capable of performing sacrifices?" In the Vedic context, sacrifices (yajñas) are not merely rituals but represent selfless actions and contributions to the greater good.
  • ta āpa kena vā te manasā dāśema: This can be interpreted as "May we attain that through our minds." It emphasizes the power of intention and thought in achieving our goals and connecting with the divine.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community service. For instance, a leader who seeks to inspire their team must first understand the collective goals and aspirations (kā ta upetir manaso varāya) and then guide them towards enlightenment and clarity (bhuvad agne śaṁtamā kā manīṣā).
  • Significance: The mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their intentions and actions. It serves as a reminder that true fulfillment comes from aligning our thoughts with a higher purpose and contributing positively to society (ko vā yajñaiḥ pari dakṣaṁ).
  • Application: In daily life, one can practice mindfulness and self-reflection to ensure that their thoughts and actions are in harmony with their values and the greater good (ta āpa kena vā te manasā dāśema). This can lead to a more meaningful and impactful existence.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, understanding our intentions, and performing selfless actions. It is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community engagement, reminding us that our thoughts and actions should be directed towards a higher purpose for a fulfilling life.
