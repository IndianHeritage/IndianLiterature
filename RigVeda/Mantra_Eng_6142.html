गुहा सतीर उप तमन pra यच शोचन्त धीतयः |कण्वा ऋतस्य धारया ||
guhā satīr upa tmanā pra yac chocanta dhītayaḥ | kaṇvā ṛtasya dhārayā ||
  • guhā: This word translates to "cave" or "hidden place." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the depths of the mind or the subconscious where true knowledge and wisdom reside.
  • satīr: This term means "truth" or "truthful." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's thoughts and actions.
  • upa: This word means "near" or "approaching." It suggests a closeness or a journey towards something significant.
  • tmanā: This translates to "with the self" or "by oneself." It indicates the importance of self-awareness and introspection in the pursuit of truth.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progression.
  • yac: This word means "that which" or "what." It introduces the idea of inquiry or seeking knowledge.
  • shocanta: This term means "to lament" or "to grieve." It can refer to the emotional struggles one faces in the pursuit of truth.
  • dhītayaḥ: This word translates to "thoughts" or "intellect." It emphasizes the role of the mind in understanding and processing experiences.
  • kaṇvā: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It highlights the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of order." It signifies the underlying principles of the universe that govern existence.
  • dhārayā: This translates to "holding" or "sustaining." It suggests the need to maintain and uphold truth and wisdom in our lives.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to seek the hidden truths within ourselves, to approach them with honesty and self-awareness, and to navigate the emotional challenges that arise in this pursuit. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the sustaining power of truth in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their inner thoughts and emotions, seeking to understand their true selves and the motivations behind their actions.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of disagreement, approaching the matter with honesty and a desire to understand the underlying truths can lead to more effective resolutions.
  • Education: Students and educators can embody the principles of seeking knowledge and truth, fostering an environment of inquiry and critical thinking.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of introspection, honesty, and wisdom in our lives. By embracing these principles, we can navigate the complexities of existence and strive towards a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us.
