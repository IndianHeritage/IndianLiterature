पार्षद्वाणः प्रस्कण्वं सम असाद्यच छयानं जीव्रिम उद्धितम् |सहस्राण्य असिषासद गवाम ऋषिस त्वतो दस्यवे वृकः ||
pārṣadvāṇaḥ praskaṇvaṁ sam asādayac chayānaṁ jivrim uddhitam | sahasrāṇy asiṣāsad gavām ṛṣis tvoto dasyave vṛkaḥ ||
  • pārṣadvāṇaḥ: This term refers to the "companions" or "friends" who are often seen as supportive figures in one's life. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of having a strong support system, such as family and friends, who help us navigate challenges.
  • praskaṇvaṁ: This word means "to be well-prepared" or "to be ready." In a practical sense, it emphasizes the significance of preparation in achieving success, whether in academics, career, or personal goals.
  • sam asādayac: This phrase translates to "they have gathered together." It highlights the importance of unity and collaboration. For example, in a workplace, teamwork can lead to more innovative solutions and better outcomes.
  • chayānaṁ: This term means "shadow" or "protection." It signifies the need for safeguarding oneself and others. In real life, this can be seen in the context of mental health, where creating a safe space for individuals to express themselves is crucial.
  • jivrim uddhitam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the awakened life." It suggests the idea of living life to its fullest potential. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to pursue their passions and dreams actively.
  • sahasrāṇy: This word means "thousands." It symbolizes abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can relate to the idea of having multiple opportunities and resources available to us if we are open to them.
  • asiṣāsad: This term refers to "blessings" or "graces." It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and recognizing the positive aspects of life. Practicing gratitude can lead to improved mental well-being and happiness.
  • gavām: This word means "of cows," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes wealth and nourishment. In modern terms, it can represent the importance of sustainable living and caring for our environment to ensure future prosperity.
  • ṛṣis: This term refers to "sages" or "wise individuals." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, seeking guidance from mentors or learning from experiences can lead to personal growth.
  • tvoto: This word means "to you" or "for you." It signifies a personal connection and responsibility. In practical terms, it reminds us to take ownership of our actions and their consequences.
  • dasyave: This term refers to "the enemy" or "adversary." It acknowledges the presence of challenges in life. In real life, facing adversities can lead to resilience and strength.
  • vṛkaḥ: This word means "wolf," often symbolizing cunning and survival instincts. It serves as a reminder to be aware of our surroundings and to navigate life's challenges wisely.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, preparation, unity, protection, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; we have companions, resources, and wisdom to guide us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to foster relationships, prepare for challenges, and embrace opportunities for growth. By recognizing the blessings in our lives and learning from adversities, we can navigate our paths with resilience and purpose.
