यः शूरैः स्वः सनितā यो विप्रैः वाजं तरुतā |तम ईशानास इरधन्त वाजिनं पृक्षम् अत्यं न वाजिनं ||
yaḥ śūraiḥ sva1ḥ sanitā yo viprair vājaṁ tarutā | tam īśānāsa iradhanta vājinam pṛkṣam atyaṁ na vājinam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • śūraiḥ: by the brave or the strong
    • svaḥ: own, self
    • sanitā: having been made or created
    • yo: who
    • viprair: by the wise or learned
    • vājaṁ: wealth, prosperity, or nourishment
    • tarutā: by the trees or nature
    • tam: that
    • īśānāsa: the lord or ruler
    • iradhanta: who is to be worshipped or revered
    • vājinam: the one who is strong or powerful
    • pṛkṣam: the one who is to be sought or desired
    • atyam: beyond or surpassing
    • na: not
    • vājinam: the strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the qualities of strength and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking prosperity and nourishment through the guidance of the brave and the wise.
    • The reference to nature (trees) suggests that true strength and prosperity come from a harmonious relationship with the environment.
    • The mantra calls upon a higher power or lord, indicating that reverence and worship are essential in the pursuit of strength and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who are both brave and wise can inspire their teams to achieve greater success.
    • Environmental sustainability practices in businesses reflect the idea of seeking prosperity through a relationship with nature.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom often find themselves more resilient in the face of challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, wisdom, and reverence in achieving prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to corporate success and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying these qualities, individuals and communities can strive for a balanced and prosperous life.
