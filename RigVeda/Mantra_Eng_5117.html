वक्ष्यन्तीवेदा गनिगन्ति कर्णं प्रियं सखायं परिषस्वजानां |योषेव शिङ्क्ते वितताधि धन्वञ् ज्याऽयं समाने पारयन्ती ||
vakṣyantīved ā ganīganti karṇam priyaṁ sakhāyam pariṣasvajānā | yoṣeva śiṅkte vitatādhi dhanvañ jyā iyaṁ samane pārayantī ||
  • vakṣyantī vedā: This phrase translates to "the Vedas speak" or "the knowledge is revealed." It signifies the importance of sacred knowledge and wisdom in guiding human life.
  • ganīganti: This means "they come together" or "they unite." It emphasizes the idea of community and collective understanding, suggesting that knowledge is best shared and discussed among individuals.
  • karṇam priyaṁ sakhāyam: This translates to "the ear that listens to a dear friend." It highlights the value of listening and the importance of having trusted companions who share wisdom and insights.
  • pariṣasvajānā: This means "those who embrace" or "those who hold close." It suggests the act of cherishing knowledge and relationships, indicating that wisdom should be nurtured and valued.
  • yoṣeva: This translates to "like a woman" or "like a female." In this context, it may symbolize nurturing qualities, intuition, and the ability to create and sustain life.
  • śiṅkte: This means "to stretch" or "to extend." It can represent the expansion of knowledge and understanding, as well as the growth of relationships.
  • vitatādhi dhanvañ: This translates to "the bow that is drawn." It symbolizes readiness and the potential for action, suggesting that knowledge equips individuals to face challenges.
  • jyā iyaṁ: This means "the string of the bow." It represents the connection between knowledge and action, indicating that wisdom is the force that propels us forward.
  • samane pārayantī: This translates to "that which brings together." It signifies the unifying power of knowledge and the importance of collaboration in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, community, and the nurturing of relationships. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, teamwork, and personal development. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of open communication and collaboration can lead to greater innovation and success. Similarly, in personal relationships, valuing and listening to each other can strengthen bonds and enhance mutual understanding. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom is not just an individual pursuit but a collective journey that enriches our lives and communities.
