सत्यं इति तं न त्वावाम् अन्यो अस्तीन् द्र देवो न मर्त्यो ज्यायान् |अहन् अहिम् परिशयानम् अर्णो 'वासृजो अपो अच्छा समुद्रम् ||
satyam it tan na tvāvām̐ anyo astīndra devo na martyo jyāyān | ahann ahim pariśayānam arṇo 'vāsṛjo apo acchā samudram ||
  • Translation: "Truth is that which is not different from you; there is no other god than you, O Indra, who is greater than mortals. I, the one who is not harmed, have created the waters; may they flow towards the ocean."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • satyam: Truth; the ultimate reality that transcends illusion.
    • it: This; referring to the truth being discussed.
    • tan: That; indicating the essence of truth.
    • na: Not; negating the existence of anything else.
    • tvāvām̐: You; addressing the divine presence, specifically Indra.
    • anyo: Other; emphasizing the uniqueness of the divine.
    • astī: Is; affirming existence.
    • indra: A deity; representing strength and power.
    • devo: God; highlighting the divine nature.
    • na: Not; again negating the existence of others.
    • martyo: Mortal; referring to human beings.
    • jyāyān: Greater; indicating superiority.
    • ahann: I; the speaker or the individual.
    • ahim: Not harmed; signifying invulnerability.
    • pariśayānam: Creation; the act of bringing forth.
    • arṇo: Waters; symbolizing life and sustenance.
    • 'vāsṛjo: I have created; indicating agency in creation.
    • apo: Waters; again referring to the life-giving element.
    • acchā: Flow; the movement of water.
    • samudram: Ocean; representing the ultimate destination or unity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of truth as a guiding principle can be seen in various ethical frameworks, where honesty and integrity are valued in personal and professional relationships.
    • In leadership, the idea that there is no greater authority than the truth can be applied, where leaders are expected to uphold transparency and accountability.
    • The metaphor of water flowing towards the ocean can be likened to individuals striving towards their goals, where the ocean represents the culmination of their efforts and aspirations.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth as the ultimate reality and the divine presence that transcends all. It teaches us that in our pursuit of life, we must recognize the importance of truth and integrity, as they lead us towards our greater purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to act with honesty, foster unity, and strive for their goals with resilience, ultimately guiding them towards a fulfilling existence.
