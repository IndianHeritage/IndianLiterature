यā विश्वासां जनितारा मतीनाम इन्द्राविष्णू कलशा सोमधाना |प्र वां गिरः शस्यमानā अवन्तु प्र स्तोमासो गीयमानासो अर्कैः ||
yā viśvāsāṁ janitārā matīnām indrāviṣṇū kalaśā somadhānā | pra vāṁ giraḥ śasyamānā avantu pra stomāso gīyamānāso arkaiḥ ||
  • yā viśvāsāṁ janitārā matīnām: This phrase can be translated as "those who generate trust and understanding among people." It emphasizes the importance of fostering trust and wisdom in society.
  • indrāviṣṇū kalaśā somadhānā: Here, "Indra" and "Vishnu" are invoked, representing strength and preservation, respectively. "Kalaśā" refers to a pot or vessel, often symbolizing abundance and nourishment, while "somadhānā" relates to the offering of Soma, a sacred drink that symbolizes vitality and immortality.
  • pra vāṁ giraḥ śasyamānā avantu: This line suggests that the words and expressions of wisdom should be directed towards prosperity and abundance. "Giraḥ" means speech or words, and "avantu" implies that they should be protected or nurtured.
  • pra stomāso gīyamānāso arkaiḥ: This part refers to the hymns and praises that are sung, invoking the divine. "Stomāso" relates to hymns, and "arkaiḥ" refers to the light or energy, often associated with the sun, which is a source of life and sustenance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of trust, wisdom, and the nurturing of positive speech in society. It highlights the roles of divine figures like Indra and Vishnu in providing strength and preservation, while also invoking the idea of abundance and nourishment through the sacred Soma. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, leadership, and personal relationships. By fostering trust and understanding, individuals can create a harmonious environment that promotes growth and prosperity. The invocation of divine energies serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing positive communication for collective well-being.
