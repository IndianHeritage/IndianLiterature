अव तवे इन्द्र प्रवतो नर्मिर गीरो ब्रह्माणि नियुतो धवन्ते |उरू न राधः सवना पुरूण्य अपो गा वज्रिन युवसे सम इन्दून ||
ava tve indra pravato normir giro brahmāṇi niyuto dhavante | urū na rādhaḥ savanā purūṇy apo gā vajrin yuvase sam indūn ||
  • ava tve indra pravato normir giro brahmāṇi niyuto dhavante
    • ava: This word can be interpreted as "downward" or "towards us," indicating a call for attention or presence.
    • tve: This means "you," referring to the deity Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • indra: Indra is the god of rain, thunder, and war, symbolizing strength and power.
    • pravato: This term suggests "moving" or "flowing," indicating a dynamic action.
    • normir: This can be understood as "the sound" or "the voice," possibly referring to the divine utterances or hymns.
    • giro: This word means "of the songs" or "of the hymns," emphasizing the importance of sacred chants in invoking divine presence.
    • brahmāṇi: This refers to the "Brahman," the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy.
    • niyuto: This means "bound" or "controlled," suggesting a sense of order or discipline in the universe.
    • dhavante: This term means "they move" or "they flow," indicating the active nature of divine forces.
  • urū na rādhaḥ savanā purūṇy apo gā vajrin yuvase sam indūn
    • urū: This means "wide" or "broad," suggesting expansiveness.
    • na: This translates to "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • rādhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with abundance.
    • savanā: This refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices," highlighting the importance of rituals in Vedic culture.
    • purūṇy: This means "many" or "numerous," indicating a multitude of blessings or resources.
    • apo: This translates to "water," symbolizing life, nourishment, and purification.
    • gā: This means "to go" or "to move," suggesting action or progress.
    • vajrin: This refers to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," often associated with Indra, symbolizing strength and power.
    • yuvase: This means "to you," indicating a direct address to the deity.
    • sam: This translates to "together" or "in harmony," suggesting unity.
    • indūn: This refers to "the moons" or "the divine light," symbolizing illumination and guidance.

Conclusion:

This mantra invokes the presence of Indra, calling upon his strength and the divine forces that govern the universe. It emphasizes the importance of sacred hymns and rituals in connecting with the divine. In real life, this can be seen in the practice of prayer and meditation, where individuals seek guidance, strength, and abundance in their lives. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of harmony and balance in our pursuits. By acknowledging the divine and participating in rituals, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, drawing upon the strength of the universe to navigate life's challenges.
