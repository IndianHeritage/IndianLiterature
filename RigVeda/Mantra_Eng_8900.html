पञ्च पदानि रूपोऽन्व आरोहं चतुष्पदिम् अन्वेमि व्रतena |अक्षरेण प्रतिमिम एताम् ऋतस्य नाभाव् अधि सम्पुनामि ||
pañca padāni rupo anv arohaṁ catuṣpadīm anv emi vratena | akṣareṇa prati mima etām ṛtasya nābhāv adhi sam punāmi ||
  • pañca padāni: This phrase translates to "five feet" or "five limbs." In a broader context, it can refer to the five elements or the five senses that are fundamental to human experience.
  • rupo anv arohaṁ: This means "I ascend the form." It signifies the journey of the soul or consciousness towards higher understanding or enlightenment.
  • catuṣpadīm: This translates to "four-footed," which can symbolize stability and groundedness, often associated with the earth and material existence.
  • anv emi vratena: This means "I follow the vow." It indicates a commitment to a path or a set of principles that guide one's actions and decisions.
  • akṣareṇa: This translates to "with the imperishable." It refers to the eternal or unchanging aspect of reality, often associated with spiritual truths.
  • prati mima etām: This means "I approach this." It signifies the act of seeking or striving towards a goal or understanding.
  • ṛtasya nābhāv: This translates to "the essence of truth." It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in one's life.
  • adhi sam punāmi: This means "I restore upon." It indicates a sense of renewal or bringing back to a state of balance and harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the journey of self-discovery and the pursuit of truth. It emphasizes the importance of grounding oneself in reality while striving for higher understanding and enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, spiritual practices, and ethical decision-making. By committing to a path of truth and righteousness, individuals can restore balance in their lives and contribute positively to their communities. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence and the importance of following a principled path in the quest for knowledge and understanding.
