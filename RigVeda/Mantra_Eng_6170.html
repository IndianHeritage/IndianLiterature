आ नो याहि परावतो हरिभ्यां हर्यताभ्यां |इमं इन्द्र सुतं पिब ||
ā no yāhi parāvato haribhyāṁ haryatābhyām | imam indra sutam piba ||
  • Mantra: "ā no yāhi parāvato haribhyāṁ haryatābhyām | imam indra sutam piba ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, come to us from the farthest realms, drink the nectar of this offering."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā no yāhi: "Come to us" - A call for divine presence and assistance.
    • parāvato: "from the farthest realms" - Suggests reaching out to the divine from distant places, indicating the vastness of the universe.
    • haribhyāṁ: "from the Haris" - Refers to the divine beings or forces associated with nature and vitality.
    • haryatābhyām: "from the forces of life" - Emphasizes the connection to life-giving energies.
    • imam: "this" - Refers to the offering being presented.
    • indra: "Indra" - The king of gods, associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • sutam: "nectar" or "soma" - Represents the essence of life, nourishment, and divine bliss.
    • piba: "drink" - A request for the divine to partake in the offering.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be invoked during times of need, such as when seeking guidance or support in difficult situations. For instance, a student preparing for exams may recite this mantra to invoke clarity and strength.
    • In community rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invite prosperity and well-being, especially during harvest festivals where the community seeks blessings for a bountiful yield.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to "forces of life" can inspire actions towards sustainability, reminding individuals and communities to respect and nurture the natural world.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine presence and the nourishment of life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking support from higher powers in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal, communal, and environmental contexts, encouraging individuals to reach out for guidance, celebrate life, and foster a harmonious relationship with nature. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of purpose and connection to the divine, enhancing their journey through life.
