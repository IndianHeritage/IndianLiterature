याः सूर्यो रश्मिभिर आततान याभ्य इन्द्रो अरदद गातुम ऊर्मिम् |ते सिन्धवो वरिवो धातना नो युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
yāḥ sūryo raśmibhir ātatāna yābhya indro aradad gātum ūrmim | te sindhavo varivo dhātanā no yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāḥ: Those who
    • sūryo: The sun
    • raśmibhir: With rays
    • ātatāna: Have spread out
    • yābhya: From whom
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • aradad: Has given
    • gātum: To go
    • ūrmim: The waves (or the ocean)
    • te: You
    • sindhavo: Rivers
    • varivo: Abundant
    • dhātanā: Bestowing
    • no: To us
    • yūyam: You all
    • pāta: Protect
    • svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness
    • sadā: Always
    • naḥ: Us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the sun and its rays, symbolizing light, knowledge, and life.
    • It acknowledges Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is seen as a giver of strength and vitality.
    • The mention of rivers signifies abundance and the flow of life, suggesting a connection to nature and its resources.
    • The plea for protection with auspiciousness indicates a desire for well-being and harmony in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance and clarity, much like the rays of the sun that illuminate our path.
    • Indra's role can be likened to the support we receive from mentors or leaders who empower us to overcome challenges.
    • The rivers can represent the flow of opportunities in our lives, reminding us to be open to abundance and prosperity.
    • Seeking protection and auspiciousness can be seen in rituals or practices that promote well-being, such as meditation or community gatherings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human experience.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the forces that sustain life and the importance of seeking protection and guidance.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to appreciate the resources around us, seek knowledge, and foster a sense of community and well-being.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to live harmoniously with nature and to be grateful for the blessings we receive.
