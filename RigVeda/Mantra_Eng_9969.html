इष्कृताहावम अवतं सुवरात्रं सुशेचनम् |उद्रीणं सिञ्चे अक्षितम् ||
iṣkṛtāhāvam avataṁ suvaratraṁ suṣecanam | udriṇaṁ siñce akṣitam ||
  • iṣkṛtāhāvam: This term can be broken down into "iṣkṛta," meaning "created" or "made," and "āhāvam," which refers to "invocation" or "calling." Together, it signifies the act of invoking or calling forth something that has been created.
  • avataṁ: This word translates to "downward" or "to bring down." It suggests a movement or action that brings something from a higher state to a lower one, often implying a connection between the divine and the earthly.
  • suvaratraṁ: "Suvaratraṁ" can be understood as "good" or "auspicious" (su) and "golden" or "brilliant" (varatraṁ). This term evokes images of prosperity, wealth, and positive energy.
  • suṣecanam: This word means "nourishing" or "watering." It implies the act of providing sustenance or support, essential for growth and flourishing.
  • udriṇaṁ: This term refers to "uplifting" or "raising." It suggests an action that elevates or enhances something, often in a spiritual or emotional context.
  • siñce: This is a verb meaning "to sprinkle" or "to pour." It indicates the act of distributing or spreading something beneficial, like blessings or nourishment.
  • akṣitam: This word translates to "imperishable" or "eternal." It signifies something that is everlasting and cannot be destroyed, often referring to spiritual truths or divine qualities.

In summary, the mantra "iṣkṛtāhāvam avataṁ suvaratraṁ suṣecanam | udriṇaṁ siñce akṣitam ||" can be interpreted as a call to invoke the divine blessings that bring prosperity and nourishment, while also uplifting and spreading eternal truths. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with higher energies and nurturing them in our lives.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by recognizing the need for spiritual nourishment and the importance of invoking positive energies. For example, during a challenging time, one might recite this mantra to seek guidance and support from higher powers.
  • In a practical sense, it can be related to personal growth and development. Just as plants need water and sunlight to thrive, individuals require spiritual and emotional nourishment to flourish in their lives.
  • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts to uplift one another, fostering an environment of support and positivity, much like a community garden where everyone contributes to the growth of plants.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of creation, nourishment, and the pursuit of eternal truths, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives.
