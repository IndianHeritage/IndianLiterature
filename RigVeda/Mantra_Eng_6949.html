आ यस्य ते महिमानं शतमूते शतक्रतो |गीर्भिर गृणन्ति कारवः ||
ā yasya te mahimānaṁ śatamūte śatakrato | gīrbhir gṛṇanti kāravaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix indicating direction or movement towards.
    • yasya: Whose or of whom.
    • te: Your or of you.
    • mahimānaṁ: Greatness or glory.
    • śatamūte: With a hundred powers or abilities.
    • śatakrato: One who has a hundred intentions or resolutions.
    • gīrbhir: With hymns or praises.
    • gṛṇanti: They sing or chant.
    • kāravaḥ: Those who are engaged in action or work.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness and glory of a divine entity, emphasizing the multitude of powers and intentions that this entity possesses.
    • The phrase "gīrbhir gṛṇanti" indicates that those who are engaged in righteous actions or work sing praises of this greatness, highlighting the importance of acknowledging and honoring the divine through our actions and words.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who demonstrates integrity and vision can inspire their team to work diligently and praise their efforts, much like the "kāravaḥ" who sing praises.
    • In personal life, individuals who practice gratitude and acknowledge the support of family and friends often find their relationships strengthened, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring greatness, whether it be in a divine context or in our everyday lives.
    • It teaches us that through our actions and words, we can uplift and celebrate the qualities we admire in others, fostering a culture of respect and appreciation.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this principle can lead to stronger relationships, enhanced teamwork, and a more positive environment, whether at home, in the workplace, or within the community.
