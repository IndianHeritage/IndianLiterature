स्वप्नेनाभ्युप्याच्छुमुरीं धुनिं च जगन्त दस्युम्प्र दभीतिमावह |रम्भी चिदात्र विविदे हिरण्यं सोमस्य तामद इन्द्रश्चकार ||
svapnenābhyupyā cumuriṁ dhuniṁ ca jaghantha dasyum pra dabhītim āvaḥ | rambhī cid atra vivide hiraṇyaṁ somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • svapnenābhyupyā: This term can be broken down into "svapna" meaning "dream" and "abhyupya" meaning "to attain" or "to reach." It suggests the idea of achieving or realizing one's dreams.
  • cumuriṁ: This word refers to "the light" or "radiance." It symbolizes enlightenment or knowledge that dispels darkness.
  • dhuniṁ: This translates to "the sound" or "the vibration." It can represent the cosmic sound or the underlying rhythm of the universe.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas together.
  • jaghantha: This term means "to destroy" or "to eliminate." It implies the removal of obstacles or negativity.
  • dasyum: This word refers to "the enemy" or "the adversary." It can symbolize any form of negativity or challenges one faces in life.
  • pra dabhītim: "Pra" means "to" or "towards," and "dabhītim" refers to "fear" or "anxiety." This phrase suggests the act of overcoming fear.
  • āvaḥ: This means "to bring" or "to invoke." It indicates a call to action or a request for assistance.
  • rambhī: This term can refer to "the goddess" or "the divine." It symbolizes the nurturing and protective aspects of the divine feminine.
  • cid: This means "consciousness" or "awareness." It represents the state of being aware and enlightened.
  • atra: This translates to "here" or "in this place," indicating the present moment or current situation.
  • vivide: This means "various" or "diverse," suggesting the multiplicity of experiences and perspectives in life.
  • hiraṇyaṁ: This word means "gold" or "wealth." It symbolizes prosperity and abundance.
  • somasya: This refers to "of the moon" or "of the soma," which is often associated with nourishment and rejuvenation.
  • tā: This means "that" or "those," referring to the qualities or attributes being discussed.
  • mada: This translates to "intoxication" or "ecstasy," often in the context of spiritual bliss or joy.
  • indraś: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods, symbolizing power and authority.
  • cakāra: This means "to create" or "to make." It indicates the act of bringing something into existence.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the journey of realizing one's dreams through enlightenment and the removal of fears and obstacles. It invokes the divine feminine for support and emphasizes the importance of consciousness and awareness in navigating the diverse experiences of life. The reference to wealth and spiritual ecstasy suggests that true prosperity comes from a state of joy and connection with the divine.


Significance: This mantra is significant as it encourages individuals to pursue their dreams while remaining aware of their fears and challenges. It highlights the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in overcoming obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, career aspirations, and spiritual growth. By invoking the qualities of enlightenment, courage, and abundance, individuals can create a fulfilling and prosperous life.
