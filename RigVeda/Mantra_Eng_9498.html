वृषा यज्ञो वृषणः सन्तु यज्ञियā वृषणो देवā वृषणो हविष्कृतः |वृषणā द्यावापृथिवी ऋतावरī वृषा पार्जन्यो वृषणो वृषस्तुभः ||
vṛṣā yajño vṛṣaṇaḥ santu yajñiyā vṛṣaṇo devā vṛṣaṇo haviṣkṛtaḥ | vṛṣaṇā dyāvāpṛthivī ṛtāvarī vṛṣā parjanyo vṛṣaṇo vṛṣastubhaḥ ||
  • vṛṣā yajño vṛṣaṇaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the sacrifice (yajña) is powerful (vṛṣā) and brings forth strength (vṛṣaṇaḥ)." In real life, this can be related to the idea that when we put effort into something meaningful, such as community service or personal development, we gain strength and resilience.
  • santu yajñiyā vṛṣaṇo devā: This translates to "may the gods (devā) who are worthy of sacrifice (yajñiyā) grant us strength (vṛṣaṇo)." This can be seen in the context of seeking blessings or support from higher powers or the universe when embarking on significant endeavors, such as starting a new business or pursuing a challenging goal.
  • vṛṣaṇo haviṣkṛtaḥ: This means "the strength (vṛṣaṇo) that is offered (haviṣkṛtaḥ) in the sacrifice." It emphasizes the importance of dedication and the offerings we make in our lives, whether they are time, resources, or emotional investment in relationships or projects.
  • vṛṣaṇā dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase can be understood as "the strength (vṛṣaṇā) of the heavens (dyāvā) and the earth (pṛthivī)." It signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of life and how our actions resonate with the universe. For example, when we act with integrity and kindness, we contribute positively to the world around us.
  • ṛtāvarī vṛṣā: This translates to "the truth (ṛtāvarī) is powerful (vṛṣā)." It highlights the significance of truthfulness in our lives. In practical terms, being honest in our dealings fosters trust and builds strong relationships, whether in personal life or business.
  • parjanyo vṛṣaṇo: This means "the rain (parjanyo) brings strength (vṛṣaṇo)." This can be related to the idea that nourishment and sustenance, much like rain for crops, are essential for growth. In life, this could refer to the support we receive from family and friends that helps us thrive.
  • vṛṣastubhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the powerful (vṛṣa) is exalted (stubhaḥ)." It suggests that strength and power are recognized and celebrated. In real life, this could be seen in how we honor achievements and milestones, whether in personal or communal contexts.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifice, strength, truth, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that our efforts, when aligned with truth and purpose, can yield strength and blessings from the universe. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various aspects such as personal growth, community service, and building relationships. By recognizing the power of our actions and the support we receive, we can navigate life's challenges with resilience and integrity.
