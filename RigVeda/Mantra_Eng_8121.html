कृत्वन्तो वरिवो गवेऽभ्यर्षन्ति सुश्रुतिम् |इळाम अस्मभ्यं संयतम् ||
kṛṇvanto varivo gave 'bhy arṣanti suṣṭutim | iḻām asmabhyaṁ saṁyatam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kṛṇvanto: This word can be translated as "those who create" or "those who make." It implies an active role in bringing something into existence.
    • varivo: This term refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." It denotes a sense of superiority or high quality.
    • gave 'bhy: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the cows" or "to the givers." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
    • arṣanti: This word means "they are praised" or "they are honored." It suggests recognition and appreciation for the actions taken.
    • suṣṭutim: This term translates to "good praise" or "excellent commendation." It emphasizes the quality of the praise being given.
    • iḻām: This word can mean "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the good things in life.
    • asmabhyaṁ: This translates to "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal connection or benefit.
    • saṁyatam: This term means "to be restrained" or "to be controlled." It implies a sense of discipline or moderation.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks about the act of creating excellence and the importance of recognizing and praising those who contribute positively to society.
    • It highlights the significance of nurturing and valuing resources, symbolized by cows, which are essential for sustenance and prosperity.
    • The call for "good praise" suggests that recognition should be given to those who work towards the betterment of the community, fostering a culture of appreciation.
    • The mention of wealth and restraint indicates that while abundance is desirable, it should be pursued with discipline and responsibility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, employees who go above and beyond in their roles should be recognized and praised for their contributions. This not only boosts morale but also encourages a culture of excellence.
    • In agricultural communities, the care and respect for livestock, such as cows, can lead to sustainable farming practices that benefit both the environment and the economy.
    • In personal finance, the mantra can be applied by seeking wealth while practicing restraint, ensuring that one lives within their means and invests wisely for the future.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of creating excellence and recognizing contributions to society. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have and to pursue prosperity with discipline.
    • In real life, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence, fostering a culture of appreciation and responsible stewardship.
