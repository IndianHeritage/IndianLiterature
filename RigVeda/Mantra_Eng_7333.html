युक्ष्वा हि देवहूतमाम् अश्वाम् अग्ने रथीर इव |निहोता पूर्व्यः सदा ||
yukṣvā hi devahūtamām̐ aśvām̐ agne rathīr iva | ni hotā pūrvyaḥ sadaḥ ||
  • yukṣvā hi devahūtamām̐: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, the divine one, who is invoked." Here, "yukṣvā" refers to the act of invoking or calling upon, while "devahūtamām̐" signifies the divine nature of Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
  • aśvām̐ agne rathīr iva: This part translates to "like a charioteer with horses." In this context, "aśvām̐" refers to horses, and "rathīr" means charioteer. This metaphor illustrates the idea of Agni guiding and controlling the forces of nature, much like a charioteer directs his horses to achieve a goal.
  • ni hotā pūrvyaḥ sadaḥ: This translates to "the one who is the priest of the past." "Ni hotā" refers to the priest or the one who performs the rituals, while "pūrvyaḥ" means 'of the past' and "sadaḥ" implies 'always' or 'eternally.' This emphasizes the timeless role of Agni in rituals and the continuity of tradition.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of Agni as a divine force that is invoked in rituals. It emphasizes the role of Agni as a guide and protector, akin to a charioteer who leads his horses. The invocation of Agni is not just a ritualistic act but a call for divine guidance in our lives.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of guidance and direction. Just as a charioteer must skillfully manage his horses to reach a destination, individuals must seek guidance from their inner wisdom or external mentors to navigate life's challenges. For example, in a professional setting, a mentor can provide the necessary guidance to help a mentee achieve their career goals.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of seeking guidance. It encourages individuals to invoke their inner strength and wisdom, much like invoking Agni, to navigate through life's complexities. By recognizing the significance of guidance, we can strive for a more purposeful and directed life.
