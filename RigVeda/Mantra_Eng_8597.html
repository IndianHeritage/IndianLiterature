अक्रान समुद्रः प्रथमं विधर्माण जनयन प्रजा भुवनस्य राजा |वृषा पवित्रे अधि सानो अव्ये बृहत् सोमो वावृधे सुवान इन्दुः ||
akrān samudraḥ prathame vidharmañ janayan prajā bhuvanasya rājā | vṛṣā pavitre adhi sāno avye bṛhat somo vāvṛdhe suvāna induḥ ||
  • Akrān samudraḥ prathame vidharmañ: This phrase can be translated as "The ocean, in the beginning, holds the essence of creation." Here, the ocean symbolizes the vastness and depth of potential and possibilities. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of a blank canvas or an untouched resource that holds infinite potential, much like the ocean that nurtures life.
  • Janayan prajā bhuvanasya rājā: This translates to "The king who generates beings in the world." This can be interpreted as the role of a leader or a creator who brings forth life and sustains it. In a modern context, this could refer to leaders in various fields—be it in governance, business, or community—who are responsible for nurturing and guiding their people towards growth and prosperity.
  • Vṛṣā pavitre adhi sāno avye: This means "The pure and powerful one, who is above all." This can be seen as a reference to a divine or supreme force that oversees and protects. In everyday life, this could represent the moral and ethical principles that guide individuals and societies, emphasizing the importance of purity in intentions and actions.
  • Bṛhat somo vāvṛdhe suvāna induḥ: This translates to "The great soma has increased, the good nectar." Soma is often associated with nourishment and immortality in Vedic texts. In contemporary terms, this could symbolize the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and well-being that enriches life. It highlights the importance of seeking out what is beneficial and nurturing for oneself and others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, leadership, purity, and the pursuit of nourishment in life. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the responsibilities that come with leadership and creation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their roles as creators and nurturers in their communities, to lead with integrity, and to seek out knowledge and wisdom that enriches not only their lives but also the lives of others. It serves as a guiding principle for ethical leadership and the importance of nurturing the potential within ourselves and the world around us.
