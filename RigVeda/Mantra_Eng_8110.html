स पवस्व य आविथेन्द्रं वृत्राय हन्तवे |वव्रिवांसं महीर अपः ||
sa pavasva ya āvithendraṁ vṛtrāya hantave | vavrivāṁsam mahīr apaḥ ||
  • sa pavasva: This phrase can be interpreted as "you who are the wind" or "O Wind, please come." In the context of the RigVeda, the wind is often seen as a powerful force of nature, symbolizing movement, change, and the breath of life.
  • ya āvithendraṁ: This translates to "who covers Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the heavens. The phrase suggests a connection between the wind and the divine, indicating that the wind has the power to influence or envelop even the mightiest of gods.
  • vṛtrāya hantave: This means "to slay Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon-like figure in Vedic mythology, representing drought and obstruction. The act of slaying Vritra symbolizes the removal of obstacles and the restoration of order and prosperity.
  • vavrivāṁsam: This word can be understood as "having great strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the might of the wind and its ability to bring about significant change.
  • mahīr apaḥ: This translates to "the great waters." In Vedic thought, water is essential for life, symbolizing nourishment, fertility, and abundance. The mention of "great waters" indicates the importance of rain and the life-giving properties of water.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the interplay between natural forces and divine intervention. It highlights the importance of the wind in bringing rain and overcoming obstacles, which is crucial for agriculture and sustenance.
  • Real-life Application: In agricultural societies, the arrival of rain is vital for crop growth. Farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions, invoking the forces of nature to ensure a bountiful harvest. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, encouraging respect for the environment and the elements.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the power of natural forces and their significance in human life. It teaches us to recognize the importance of harmony with nature and the divine, urging us to seek balance and respect for the elements that sustain us. In a world facing climate change and environmental challenges, this message is more relevant than ever, reminding us of our responsibility to protect and cherish the natural world.
