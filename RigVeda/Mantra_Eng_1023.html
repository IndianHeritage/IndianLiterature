उषस् तच् चित्रं आ भरास्मभ्यं वाजिनिवति |येना तोकं च तनयं च धामहे ||
uṣas tac citram ā bharāsmabhyaṁ vājinīvati | yena tokaṁ ca tanayaṁ ca dhāmahe ||
  • uṣas: This word refers to "dawn" or "the early morning light." In many cultures, dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • tac: This translates to "that," indicating something specific that is being referred to, often in a context of reverence or importance.
  • citram: Meaning "colorful" or "varied," this word suggests diversity and richness, often associated with the beauty of nature and life.
  • ā bharāsmabhyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you bestow upon us." It is a call for blessings or gifts from the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking support and abundance.
  • vājinīvati: This term means "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful." It signifies the importance of strength and vitality in life.
  • yena: This translates to "by which," indicating a means or method through which something is achieved.
  • tokaṁ: This word means "offspring" or "children," highlighting the significance of family and lineage in human life.
  • ca: This simply means "and," connecting ideas and emphasizing inclusivity.
  • tanayaṁ: Similar to "tokaṁ," this word also refers to "children" or "descendants," reinforcing the theme of progeny and continuity.
  • ca: Again, this is "and," further linking the concepts presented.
  • dhāmahe: This means "we meditate upon" or "we contemplate," indicating a reflective and thoughtful approach to life and its blessings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the blessings of dawn, asking for the colorful and vibrant aspects of life to be bestowed upon us. It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality, particularly in relation to our offspring and the future generations. The speaker is seeking divine support to nurture and protect their family, ensuring a prosperous and fulfilling life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Family Blessings: Parents can recite this mantra to seek blessings for their children, hoping for their well-being and success.
  • New Beginnings: Individuals starting a new venture or phase in life can use this mantra to invoke positivity and strength as they embark on their journey.
  • Morning Rituals: Practicing this mantra at dawn can serve as a reminder to embrace new opportunities and face the day with vigor and hope.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant meaning in the context of seeking blessings for strength, vitality, and the well-being of future generations. It encourages individuals to reflect on their lives, appreciate the beauty around them, and strive for a prosperous future, making it relevant in various aspects of daily life.
