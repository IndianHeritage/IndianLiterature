अतृप्णुवन्तं वियतम अबुध्यम अबुध्यमानं सुशुपाणम् इन्द्र |सप्त प्रति प्रवत आश्यानम् अहिं वज्रेण वि रिणा अपर्वन् ||
atṛpṇuvantaṁ viyatam abudhyam abudhyamānaṁ suṣupāṇam indra | sapta prati pravata āśayānam ahiṁ vajreṇa vi riṇā aparvan ||
  • atṛpṇuvantaṁ: This term can be broken down into "a" (not) and "tṛpṇu" (satisfied). It refers to a state of dissatisfaction or unfulfilled desire.
  • viyatam: This word means "the sky" or "the atmosphere," symbolizing the vastness and the infinite possibilities that exist beyond our immediate perception.
  • abudhyam: This translates to "awakened" or "enlightened," indicating a state of awareness or consciousness.
  • abudhyamānaṁ: This is a form of the previous word, suggesting a continuous state of awakening or realization.
  • suṣupāṇam: This term means "one who is in deep sleep," representing a state of ignorance or unawareness.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with power, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • sapta: This means "seven," which can symbolize completeness or a cycle, as in the seven days of the week or the seven colors of the rainbow.
  • prati: This translates to "towards" or "in relation to," indicating direction or focus.
  • pravata: This word means "flowing" or "moving," suggesting a dynamic process or change.
  • āśayānam: This term refers to "the abode" or "the place of rest," indicating a state of stability or security.
  • ahiṁ: This means "serpent" or "snake," often symbolizing hidden dangers or challenges that one must confront.
  • vajreṇa: This translates to "with the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to break through obstacles.
  • vi riṇā: This phrase can be interpreted as "to overcome" or "to conquer," indicating a sense of victory.
  • aparvan: This means "the unseen" or "the hidden," suggesting that there are aspects of life that are not immediately visible or understood.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the journey of awakening from a state of dissatisfaction and ignorance to one of enlightenment and realization. It emphasizes the importance of confronting challenges (symbolized by the serpent) and utilizing one's inner strength (represented by the thunderbolt) to navigate through life's complexities. In real life, this can be applied to personal growth, where individuals must recognize their unfulfilled desires and work towards self-awareness and empowerment. The mantra encourages us to embrace change and seek deeper understanding, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and meaningful existence.
