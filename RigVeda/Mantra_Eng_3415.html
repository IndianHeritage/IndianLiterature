अभि त्वा गोतमā गिरानूषत प्र दावने |इन्द्र वाजाय घृष्वये ||
abhi tvā gotamā girānūṣata pra dāvane | indra vājāya ghṛṣvaye ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tvā gotamā girānūṣata pra dāvane | indra vājāya ghṛṣvaye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • gotamā: of the Gotama (a sage or seer)
    • girānūṣata: of the songs or hymns
    • pra: forth, forward
    • dāvane: in the act of giving or offering
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • vājāya: for the strong, powerful
    • ghṛṣvaye: for the one who is invoked or called upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The phrase "abhi tvā gotamā" suggests a personal invocation, indicating a relationship between the speaker and the divine, emphasizing the importance of connection in spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "girānūṣata" highlights the significance of hymns and songs in invoking divine presence, showing how words and sounds can create a spiritual atmosphere.
    • "Pra dāvane" indicates the act of offering, which is a central theme in many spiritual traditions, where giving is seen as a way to receive blessings in return.
    • The invocation of Indra as "vājāya ghṛṣvaye" suggests a request for strength and support, which can be interpreted as a plea for help in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, similar to this mantra, seeking strength and guidance from a higher power.
    • Communities may gather to sing hymns or chants, creating a collective energy that fosters unity and support, much like the invocation of divine presence through song.
    • Acts of charity and giving can be seen as modern-day interpretations of "pra dāvane," where individuals offer their resources to help others, thereby inviting blessings into their own lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance and strength through personal connection, song, and the act of giving.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that in our struggles, we can reach out for support, whether through spiritual practices or community engagement.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate relationships with the divine and with each other, fostering a sense of belonging and shared strength.
