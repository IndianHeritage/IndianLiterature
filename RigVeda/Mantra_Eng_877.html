सहस्राक्षो विचर्षणिर अग्नि रक्षांसी सेधति |होतागृणीत उक्त्यः ||
sahasrākṣo vicarṣaṇir agnī rakṣāṁsi sedhati | hotā gṛṇīta ukthyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasrākṣo: One with a thousand eyes, often referring to the divine or universal vision.
    • vicarṣaṇir: The one who moves or travels, indicating a dynamic presence.
    • agnī: Fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • rakṣāṁsi: Protections or safeguards, representing security and safety.
    • sedhati: To establish or to set in place, indicating the act of creating stability.
    • hotā: The priest or the one who invokes, often associated with rituals and offerings.
    • gṛṇīta: To sing or to chant, emphasizing the importance of vocal expression in rituals.
    • ukthyaḥ: Invocations or hymns, referring to the sacred verses recited during rituals.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is all-seeing and ever-moving, symbolizing the omnipresence of the divine in our lives.
    • The mention of fire (agnī) signifies the transformative power of the divine, which can purify and protect.
    • The invocation of protection (rakṣāṁsi) suggests that the divine is a guardian, establishing safety and stability in our lives.
    • The role of the hotā (priest) and the act of singing hymns (gṛṇīta ukthyaḥ) highlight the importance of rituals and prayers in connecting with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty or danger, people often turn to prayers and rituals for protection, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, rituals are performed to invoke divine blessings and establish a safe environment.
    • The concept of fire as a purifying agent can be seen in various cultures where fire rituals are performed to cleanse spaces and individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine protection and the transformative power of rituals in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that the divine is always present, watching over us and guiding us through challenges.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to engage in spiritual practices that foster a sense of security and connection to the divine.
