पुनर् वै देवाः अददुः पुनर् मनुष्याः उता | राजानः सत्यं कृत्वाना ब्रह्मजायाम् पुनर् ददुः ||
punar vai devā adaduḥ punar manuṣyā uta | rājānaḥ satyaṁ kṛṇvānā brahmajāyām punar daduḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • punar: again, anew
    • vai: indeed
    • devā: the gods
    • adaduḥ: gave
    • manuṣyā: humans
    • uta: or
    • rājānaḥ: kings
    • satyaṁ: truth
    • kṛṇvānā: creating, making
    • brahmajāyām: in the realm of the divine, in the essence of Brahman
    • punar: again
    • daduḥ: gave
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the cyclical nature of existence, where the gods bestow gifts upon humanity and kings create truth.
    • The repetition of "punar" emphasizes renewal and the continuous cycle of giving and receiving.
    • The mention of "devā" (gods) and "manuṣyā" (humans) highlights the relationship between the divine and the mortal, suggesting that both realms are interconnected.
    • The role of "rājānaḥ" (kings) signifies leadership and the responsibility of rulers to uphold truth and righteousness.
    • "Brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, indicating that the actions of both gods and humans are rooted in a higher truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, leaders who act with integrity and truth can inspire trust and cooperation among the people, reflecting the essence of "rājānaḥ satyaṁ kṛṇvānā."
    • In personal relationships, the act of giving and receiving support mirrors the divine cycle mentioned in the mantra, where both parties contribute to each other's growth.
    • In environmental stewardship, the idea of renewal can be seen in sustainable practices that aim to restore balance to nature, akin to the gods giving back to humanity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, renewal, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, whether as leaders or individuals, have a profound impact on the world around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious society where truth and reciprocity are valued, fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility.
