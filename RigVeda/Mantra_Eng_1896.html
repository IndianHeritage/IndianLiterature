आ वां दानाय ववृतीय दसरā गोर ओहेण तौग्र्यो न जीव्रिः |अपः क्षोणी सचते माहिनā वां जूर्णो वाम अक्षुर अम्हसो यजत्रा ||
ā vāṁ dānāya vavṛtīya dasrā gor oheṇa taugryo na jivriḥ | apaḥ kṣoṇī sacate māhinā vāṁ jūrṇo vām akṣur aṁhaso yajatrā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: "to you" or "for you"
    • dānāya: "for the purpose of giving" or "to offer"
    • vavṛtīya: "may it be fulfilled" or "may it come to fruition"
    • dasrā: "the one who is strong" or "the powerful"
    • gor: "of the cow" or "nourishing"
    • oheṇa: "with strength" or "with vigor"
    • taugryo: "the one who is fierce" or "the powerful one"
    • na jivriḥ: "may not perish" or "may not be destroyed"
    • apaḥ: "water" or "the essence of life"
    • kṣoṇī: "the earth" or "the ground"
    • sacate: "may it be established" or "may it be firm"
    • māhinā: "with greatness" or "with abundance"
    • vāṁ: "to you" or "for you"
    • jūrṇo: "the one who is nourished" or "the one who is sustained"
    • vām: "to you" or "for you"
    • akṣur: "the imperishable" or "the eternal"
    • aṁhaso: "of the light" or "of the radiance"
    • yajatrā: "the one who is worshipped" or "the one who is revered"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offering and giving, suggesting that through acts of generosity, one can achieve fulfillment and strength.
    • The reference to "the powerful" and "the nourishing" indicates that strength and sustenance come from a connection to nature, particularly through elements like water and earth.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of permanence and resilience, as it speaks of not perishing and being established firmly, which can be related to the enduring nature of life and the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community service, when individuals come together to help those in need, they embody the spirit of "dānāya" by offering their time and resources, leading to a stronger, more connected community.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the essence of "apaḥ kṣoṇī," as protecting water sources and the earth ensures the sustenance of life for future generations.
    • In personal relationships, acts of kindness and support can be seen as a way to nourish bonds, ensuring that they do not "jivriḥ" or perish over time.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of generosity, and the need to nurture our environment and relationships.
    • Its significance lies in the call to action for individuals to contribute positively to their communities and the world, ensuring that both human connections and the natural world thrive.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to engage in sustainable practices, support one another, and recognize the value of what we offer to the world around us.
