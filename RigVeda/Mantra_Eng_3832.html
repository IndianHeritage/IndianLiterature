यस्य मापरुषाः शतमुद्धर्षयन्त्युक्षणः |अश्वमेधस्य दानाः सोमाऽइव त्र्याशिरः ||
yasya mā paruṣāḥ śatam uddharṣayanty ukṣaṇaḥ | aśvamedhasya dānāḥ somā iva tryāśiraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • mā: not
    • paruṣāḥ: harsh, cruel
    • śatam: hundred
    • uddharṣayanty: who elevate or uplift
    • ukṣaṇaḥ: the strong, powerful
    • aśvamedhasya: of the horse sacrifice
    • dānāḥ: gifts, donations
    • somā: like the soma (a sacred drink)
    • iva: like
    • tryāśiraḥ: three-headed (referring to the three realms or aspects)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power and benevolence of a person who does not exhibit cruelty, even when they possess great strength.
    • The reference to "hundred" suggests a multitude of strengths or virtues that can uplift others.
    • The "horse sacrifice" (aśvamedha) symbolizes a significant ritual in Vedic culture, representing prosperity, power, and the establishment of authority.
    • The mention of "soma" indicates the importance of spiritual nourishment and the divine connection that enhances one's capabilities.
    • The "three-headed" aspect can be interpreted as the integration of physical, mental, and spiritual strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A leader who uses their power to uplift their community rather than oppress it exemplifies the essence of this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, a manager who encourages their team and fosters a positive work environment, despite having authority, embodies the spirit of this teaching.
    • Philanthropists who use their wealth to support education, healthcare, and social causes reflect the idea of giving and uplifting others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of using one's strength and resources for the greater good, promoting kindness and generosity.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies not in domination but in the ability to uplift and support others.
    • In today's world, where challenges abound, this teaching is applicable in various scenarios, from personal relationships to global leadership, encouraging a more compassionate and equitable society.
