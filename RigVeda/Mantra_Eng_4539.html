वीति यो देवम् मार्तो दुवस्येद अग्निम ईळीताध्वरे हविष्मान् |होतारं सत्ययजं रोदस्योर उत्तानहस्तो नमसā विवासेत ||
vītī yo devam marto duvasyed agnim īḻītādhvare haviṣmān | hotāraṁ satyayajaṁ rodasyor uttānahasto namasā vivāset ||
  • vītī yo devam: This phrase can be translated as "He who knows the divine." It emphasizes the importance of understanding and recognizing the divine presence in our lives. In real life, this can relate to individuals who seek spiritual knowledge and strive to connect with a higher power through meditation, prayer, or philosophical inquiry.
  • marto duvasyed agnim: This translates to "the mortal who invokes the fire." Fire in Vedic literature symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In contemporary life, this can be seen in rituals, such as lighting candles or bonfires during celebrations, which represent the invocation of positive energy and the transformation of the self.
  • īḻītādhvare haviṣmān: This means "the one who offers the oblation with devotion." It highlights the significance of offering and sacrifice in spiritual practices. In modern contexts, this can be related to acts of charity or service, where individuals offer their time, resources, or skills to help others, reflecting devotion and selflessness.
  • hotāraṁ satyayajaṁ: This translates to "the priest who speaks the truth." It underscores the value of truthfulness and integrity in leadership and guidance. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by leaders, teachers, or mentors who inspire others through their honesty and ethical conduct.
  • rodasyor uttānahasto: This means "with hands raised to the heavens." It symbolizes aspiration and the pursuit of higher goals. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who strive for personal growth, whether through education, career advancement, or spiritual enlightenment, always reaching for their highest potential.
  • namasā vivāset: This translates to "may he be honored with reverence." It emphasizes the importance of respect and gratitude towards the divine and the forces of nature. In practical terms, this can be reflected in how we treat the environment, showing reverence for nature and acknowledging our interconnectedness with all living beings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual practice, emphasizing the importance of understanding the divine, invoking positive energies, offering selflessly, speaking the truth, aspiring for higher goals, and honoring the sacred. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a life of purpose, integrity, and connection with the greater universe. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community service, environmental stewardship, and ethical leadership, fostering a harmonious and meaningful existence.
