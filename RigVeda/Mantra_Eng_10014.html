अप योर इन्द्रः पापज आ मर्तो न शश्रमाणो बिभीवान् |शुभे यद युयुजे तविषीवान् ||
apa yor indraḥ pāpaja ā marto na śaśramāṇo bibhīvān | śubhe yad yuyuje taviṣīvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apa: away, from
    • yor: your
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • pāpaja: born of sin, sinful
    • ā: to, towards
    • marto: mortal, human
    • na: not
    • śaśramāṇo: one who is weary, tired
    • bibhīvān: fearful, afraid
    • śubhe: auspicious, good
    • yat: that which
    • yuyuje: unites, connects
    • taviṣīvān: powerful, strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity associated with strength and power, asking him to remove the sins and fears that bind mortals.
    • The phrase "apa yor indraḥ pāpaja" suggests a plea for liberation from the consequences of sinful actions.
    • "Marto na śaśramāṇo bibhīvān" emphasizes the desire for humans to be free from weariness and fear, promoting a state of courage and vitality.
    • The concluding part, "śubhe yad yuyuje taviṣīvān," highlights the importance of auspiciousness and strength in uniting individuals towards a common goal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, this mantra can be a reminder to seek strength and unity among colleagues, overcoming fears and doubts to achieve collective success.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a call to address past grievances and work towards a harmonious and supportive bond, free from the burdens of past mistakes.
    • During times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal loss, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire communities to come together, support one another, and rise above fear and despair.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of overcoming sin, fear, and weariness through the strength and auspiciousness provided by divine intervention.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals and communities to unite, support each other, and strive for a positive and powerful existence.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that through collective strength and auspicious intentions, one can transcend challenges and achieve greater harmony in life.
