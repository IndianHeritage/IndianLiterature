ऊर्जां देवां अवश्य ओजसा त्वां पिबा सोमं मदाय कं शतक्रतो |यं ते भागम् अधारयन विश्वाः सेहानः पृतना उरु ज्रयः सम अप्सुजिन मरुत्वां इन्द्र सत्पते ||
ūrjā devām̐ avasy ojasā tvām pibā somam madāya kaṁ śatakrato | yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin marutvām̐ indra satpate ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • ūrjā: Refers to energy or strength.
    • devām̐: Pertains to the divine or gods.
    • avasy: Implies necessity or requirement.
    • ojasā: Signifies vitality or power.
    • tvām: Means 'you' or 'to you'.
    • pibā: Translates to 'drink' or 'consume'.
    • somam: Refers to 'soma', a sacred drink believed to confer immortality.
    • madāya: Means 'for intoxication' or 'to induce joy'.
    • kaṁ: Refers to 'who' or 'whom'.
    • śatakrato: Refers to 'the one with a hundred powers', often associated with Indra.
    • yaṁ: Means 'whom'.
    • te: Means 'your'.
    • bhāgam: Refers to 'portion' or 'share'.
    • adhārayan: Means 'who have supported' or 'who have upheld'.
    • viśvāḥ: Refers to 'all' or 'universal'.
    • sehānaḥ: Means 'those who are strong' or 'powerful'.
    • pṛtanā: Refers to 'the one who is praised'.
    • uru: Means 'great' or 'vast'.
    • jrayaḥ: Refers to 'strength' or 'power'.
    • sam: Means 'together' or 'with'.
    • apsujin: Refers to 'the one who is born from water' or 'the aquatic'.
    • marutvām̐: Pertains to 'the storm' or 'the winds'.
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods, associated with thunder and rain.
    • satpate: Means 'the lord of truth' or 'the true lord'.
  • Significance of the Mantra:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies and strength, particularly focusing on Indra, the god of rain and thunder.
    • It emphasizes the importance of vitality and the consumption of 'soma', which symbolizes the pursuit of higher consciousness and joy.
    • The mantra highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the support they provide to one another.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek strength and energy from the divine, especially during challenging times.
    • It encourages individuals to uplift and support one another, fostering a sense of community and shared strength.
    • The concept of 'soma' can be related to the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment through spiritual practices, meditation, or community service.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, unity, and divine support.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek energy from the divine and to support one another in their journeys.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with vitality, joy, and a sense of belonging.
