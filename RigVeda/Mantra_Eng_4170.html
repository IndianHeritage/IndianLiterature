आ ये तस्थुः पृथतीषु श्रुतासु सुखेषु रुद्राः मरुतो रथेṣu | वनाऽचिद उग्राः जिहते न वो भियाः पृथिवी चिद रेजते पर्वतश्चित् ||
ā ye tasthuḥ pṛṣatīṣu śrutāsu sukheṣu rudrā maruto ratheṣu | vanā cid ugrā jihate ni vo bhiyā pṛthivī cid rejate parvataś cit ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā ye: O you who are (invocation)
    • tasthuḥ: who are established
    • pṛṣatīṣu: in the realms of the senses
    • śrutāsu: in the heard (knowledge)
    • sukheṣu: in happiness
    • rudrā: O Rudras (a form of Shiva)
    • maruto: O Maruts (storm deities)
    • ratheṣu: in the chariots
    • vanā: in the forests
    • cid: indeed
    • ugrā: fierce
    • jihate: may you be pleased
    • ni vo: to you
    • bhiyā: from fear
    • pṛthivī: the Earth
    • cid: indeed
    • rejate: shines
    • parvataś: like a mountain
    • cit: indeed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the Rudras and Maruts, deities associated with storms and the fierce aspects of nature.
    • It emphasizes the connection between divine forces and the natural world, highlighting the importance of harmony and balance.
    • The mention of "chariots" and "forests" symbolizes the journey of life and the challenges one faces, while also acknowledging the beauty of nature.
    • The phrase "may you be pleased" reflects a desire for divine favor and protection from fear, suggesting that the divine can provide solace in times of distress.
    • The imagery of the Earth shining like a mountain conveys strength, stability, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural disasters, people often turn to prayers and rituals to seek protection and guidance from higher powers, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a modern application of respecting the natural world, akin to the reverence expressed in the mantra.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may seek comfort in nature, finding peace in forests or mountains, reflecting the mantra's themes of seeking refuge and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity, nature, and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek harmony with the natural world and to invoke divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • In a contemporary context, it highlights the importance of environmental stewardship and the need for spiritual grounding in an increasingly chaotic world.
