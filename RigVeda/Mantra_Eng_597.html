उद्यन्नाद्य मित्रमहाऽरोहन्न उत्तराṁ दिवम् |हृद्रोगं मम सूर्य हरिमाणं च नाशय ||
udyann adya mitramaha ārohann uttarāṁ divam | hṛdrogam mama sūrya harimāṇaṁ ca nāśaya ||
  • Udyann adya mitramaha: This phrase can be translated as "Today, the great friend (the Sun) rises." Here, the Sun is personified as a friend, symbolizing warmth, guidance, and positivity. In real life, the rising sun can be seen as a new beginning, a fresh start to the day, encouraging individuals to embrace opportunities.
  • Ārohann uttarāṁ divam: This means "Ascending towards the northern sky." The northern direction is often associated with auspiciousness and prosperity in various cultures. In practical terms, this can represent aspirations and goals that one aims to achieve, akin to climbing a ladder towards success.
  • Hṛdrogam mama: Translated as "Remove the afflictions of my heart." This line speaks to the emotional and psychological burdens that individuals carry. In real life, it emphasizes the importance of mental health and the need for healing from emotional pain, whether through therapy, meditation, or support from loved ones.
  • Harimāṇaṁ ca nāśaya: This translates to "And destroy the one who is harmful." This can refer to negative influences or harmful entities in one’s life. In a contemporary context, it can symbolize the need to eliminate toxic relationships or detrimental habits that hinder personal growth and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a journey of renewal and healing. It signifies the importance of embracing new beginnings, setting aspirations, and seeking emotional and spiritual well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied during moments of reflection, such as at the start of a new day or during significant life changes, reminding individuals to focus on positivity, set goals, and prioritize mental health. By invoking these principles, one can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
