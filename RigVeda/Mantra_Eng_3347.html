अहम् भूमिम् अददाम् आर्यायाहं वृष्टिं दाशुषे मार्त्याय |अहम् अपो अनयम् वावशानाऽ मम देवासो अनु केतम् आयन ||
aham bhūmim adadām āryāyāhaṁ vṛṣṭiṁ dāśuṣe martyāya | aham apo anayaṁ vāvaśānā mama devāso anu ketam āyan ||
  • Translation: "I have given the earth to the noble; I have bestowed rain upon the mortal. I have brought forth the waters; may the divine beings lead me to the path."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • aham - I
    • bhūmim - the earth
    • adadām - have given
    • āryāyāhaṁ - to the noble
    • vṛṣṭiṁ - rain
    • dāśuṣe - to the mortal
    • martyāya - to the mortal being
    • apo - waters
    • anayaṁ - I have brought forth
    • vāvaśānā - may lead
    • mama - my
    • devāso - divine beings
    • anu - along with
    • ketam - path
    • āyan - to guide

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The act of giving the earth to the noble can be seen in sustainable practices where individuals or organizations work towards environmental conservation, ensuring that the earth is preserved for future generations.
    • Bestowing rain upon mortals can be likened to the importance of water in agriculture. Farmers rely on rain for their crops, and initiatives like rainwater harvesting are practical applications of this principle.
    • The invocation of divine beings to guide one’s path can be reflected in various cultures where people seek blessings or guidance through prayer or meditation, emphasizing the need for spiritual direction in life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity, nature, and the divine. It signifies the importance of giving and receiving, as well as the need for guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages sustainable living, respect for nature, and seeking spiritual guidance, reminding us that our actions have a profound impact on the world around us. This mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental activism, community service, and personal development, highlighting the significance of our choices and their effects on society and the planet.
