तं ईमहे पुरुष्टुतं यह्वम् प्रात्नाभिर ऊतिभिः |नि बर्हिषि प्रिय सादद अध द्विता ||
tam īmahe puruṣṭutaṁ yahvam pratnābhir ūtibhiḥ | ni barhiṣi priye sadad adha dvitā ||
  • tam īmahe: This phrase translates to "we meditate upon" or "we invoke." It signifies the act of focusing one's thoughts and intentions towards a higher power or truth.
  • puruṣṭutaṁ: This term refers to "the most excellent being" or "the supreme person." In the context of the RigVeda, it often denotes the universal spirit or the ultimate reality that encompasses all existence.
  • yahvam: This word can be understood as "who" or "that which." It indicates a connection to the divine or the higher consciousness that is being invoked.
  • pratnābhir ūtibhiḥ: This phrase means "with our prayers and hymns." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the power of words in connecting with the divine.
  • ni barhiṣi: This translates to "in the sacred place" or "in the holy space." It suggests a setting that is conducive to spiritual practices and the presence of the divine.
  • priye: This word means "beloved" or "dear." It reflects the affectionate relationship between the devotee and the divine, highlighting the emotional aspect of worship.
  • sadad: This term can be interpreted as "everlasting" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of the divine and the enduring connection that exists between the individual and the universal spirit.
  • adha dvitā: This phrase translates to "beyond duality" or "transcending opposites." It points to the idea of unity in diversity, suggesting that the ultimate reality is beyond the distinctions we often perceive in the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of spiritual invocation and the quest for understanding the supreme reality. It emphasizes the importance of meditation, prayer, and the sacredness of the space in which one seeks connection with the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation practices, rituals, or moments of reflection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the eternal nature of the spirit, encouraging individuals to transcend their dualistic perceptions and embrace a more holistic view of existence. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper sense of peace, purpose, and unity in their daily lives.
