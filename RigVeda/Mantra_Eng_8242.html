पवस्व जनयन्न इषोऽभि विश्वानि वार्याः |सखा सकिभ्य उतये ||
pavasva janayann iṣo 'bhi viśvāni vāryā | sakhā sakhibhya ūtaye ||
  • Mantra: "pavasva janayann iṣo 'bhi viśvāni vāryā | sakhā sakhibhya ūtaye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pavasva: "May you purify" or "May you cleanse."
    • janayann: "Generating" or "Creating."
    • iṣo: "Of the Lord" or "Of the Divine."
    • 'bhi: "With" or "By."
    • viśvāni: "All" or "Universal."
    • vāryā: "Strengths" or "Powers."
    • sakhā: "Friend" or "Companion."
    • sakhibhya: "To friends" or "To companions."
    • ūtaye: "For the purpose of nourishment" or "For the purpose of support."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine to purify and generate universal strengths.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and companionship in achieving goals.
    • The mantra suggests that through divine support and the strength of relationships, one can attain nourishment and fulfillment in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the mantra can be applied to foster collaboration and mutual support among team members, leading to collective success.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the significance of nurturing friendships that provide emotional and spiritual nourishment.
    • In community service, it encourages individuals to come together, purifying their intentions to create a positive impact on society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of divine support in our endeavors and the importance of strong, supportive relationships.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, teamwork, and community building.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can seek purification and strength, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their actions.
