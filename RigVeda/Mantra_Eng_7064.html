पुषा विष्णुर हवनाम मे सरस्वती अवन्तु सप्त सिन्धवः |आपो वातः पर्वतासो वनस्पतिः श्रिणोतु पृथिवी हवम् ||
pūṣā viṣṇur havanam me sarasvaty avantu sapta sindhavaḥ | āpo vātaḥ parvatāso vanaspatiḥ śṛṇotu pṛthivī havam ||
  • pūṣā: This term refers to the deity Pūṣan, who is associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of sustenance in our daily lives, whether it be through food, knowledge, or emotional support.
  • viṣṇur: Referring to Lord Vishnu, the preserver in Hindu mythology. This signifies the need for preservation and balance in our lives, akin to how we maintain relationships and responsibilities.
  • havanam me: This translates to "my offering." It emphasizes the act of giving and the importance of making offerings in various forms, such as gratitude, service, or contributions to society.
  • sarasvaty: This refers to Goddess Saraswati, the deity of knowledge, music, and arts. In a real-life context, this highlights the value of education and creativity in personal and professional growth.
  • avantū: Meaning "may it protect" or "may it guide." This reflects the human desire for guidance and protection in our endeavors, similar to seeking mentorship or support from loved ones.
  • sapta sindhavaḥ: This phrase means "the seven rivers." It symbolizes abundance and the flow of resources. In modern life, this can be related to the importance of water and natural resources for sustenance and agriculture.
  • āpo: This means "waters." It signifies the essential nature of water for life, akin to how we rely on clean water for health and well-being.
  • vātaḥ: Referring to the wind or air, which is vital for life. This can be compared to the importance of fresh air and a healthy environment for our physical and mental health.
  • parvatāso: Meaning "mountains," which represent stability and strength. In real life, this can be seen in the way we seek stability in our careers and personal lives.
  • vanaspatiḥ: This refers to plant life or vegetation, symbolizing growth and nourishment. It can be related to the importance of nature and agriculture in sustaining human life.
  • śṛṇotu: Meaning "may hear," indicating the importance of listening and understanding. This is crucial in communication and relationships, where active listening fosters connection.
  • pṛthivī: Referring to the Earth, which is our home and sustainer. This emphasizes the need for environmental consciousness and care for our planet.
  • havam: This means "offering" or "sacrifice," reinforcing the idea of giving back to the community and the universe, similar to charitable acts and social responsibility.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing nourishment, preservation, knowledge, and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek balance, offer gratitude, and protect our environment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in sustainable practices, pursue education, and contribute positively to society, fostering a harmonious existence with nature and each other.
