देवं-देवं राधसे चोदयन्त्य अस्मद्र्यक सूनृताऽ ईरयन्ती |व्युञ्चन्ती नः सानये धियः धा यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
devaṁ-devaṁ rādhase codayanty asmadryak sūnṛtā īrayantī | vyucchantī naḥ sanaye dhiyo dhā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • devaṁ-devaṁ: This phrase translates to "the divine among the divine." It emphasizes the omnipresence of divinity in various forms and aspects of life.
  • rādhase: Referring to the divine energy or essence that inspires and motivates individuals. In real life, this can be likened to the inner drive or passion that propels someone to achieve their goals.
  • codayanty: This means "to inspire" or "to guide." It signifies the role of divine forces in providing direction and encouragement in our lives.
  • asmadryak: This term can be interpreted as "our own" or "belonging to us." It highlights the personal connection individuals have with the divine and the guidance they receive.
  • sūnṛtā: This word means "truth" or "righteousness." It underscores the importance of living a life aligned with truth and moral values.
  • īrayantī: This translates to "to lead" or "to guide." It reflects the idea that divine forces lead us towards a path of righteousness and fulfillment.
  • vyucchantī: Meaning "to free" or "to liberate," this term suggests the divine's role in helping individuals overcome obstacles and challenges in life.
  • naḥ: This means "us" or "our," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra, indicating that the divine guidance is for all.
  • sanaye: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • dhiyo: This refers to "thoughts" or "intellects," highlighting the significance of mental clarity and focus.
  • dhā: Meaning "to hold" or "to maintain," it suggests the need to keep our thoughts aligned with divine guidance.
  • yūyam: This means "you" or "your," addressing the divine forces directly, acknowledging their presence and influence.
  • pāta: This translates to "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for divine protection and support.
  • svastibhiḥ: Meaning "well-being" or "auspiciousness," it signifies the desire for a life filled with positivity and good fortune.
  • sadā: This means "always," emphasizing the continuous nature of divine support and guidance.
  • naḥ: Reiterating "us," it reinforces the collective aspect of seeking divine blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, protection, and inspiration in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of aligning our thoughts and actions with truth and righteousness while acknowledging the omnipresence of divine forces. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of uncertainty or challenge, serving as a reminder to seek inner strength and clarity through divine connection. Whether in personal endeavors, professional challenges, or spiritual journeys, invoking this mantra can foster a sense of peace, purpose, and direction, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
