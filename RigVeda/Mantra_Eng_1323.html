आ वां रथो अश्विनाऽ श्येनपत्वा सुमृळीकः स्ववाम् यात्व अरवाङ् |यो मार्त्यस्य मनसो जवीयान् त्रिवन्धुरो वृषणा वातरंहाḥ ||
ā vāṁ ratho aśvinā śyenapatvā sumṛḻīkaḥ svavām̐ yātv arvāṅ | yo martyasya manaso javīyān trivandhuro vṛṣaṇā vātaraṁhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: O you (plural),
    • ratho: chariot,
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin gods),
    • śyenapatvā: like the eagle,
    • sumṛḻīkaḥ: well-formed,
    • svavām̐: may we go,
    • yātv: as you travel,
    • arvāṅ: with speed,
    • yo: who,
    • martyasya: of the mortal,
    • manaso: of the mind,
    • javīyān: the swiftest,
    • trivandhuro: threefold,
    • vṛṣaṇā: with strength,
    • vātaraṁhāḥ: the wind-born.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, who symbolize health, vitality, and swiftness.
    • The reference to "chariot" suggests a journey or movement, which can be interpreted as the journey of life.
    • The phrase "like the eagle" indicates a desire for sharp vision and clarity in one's pursuits.
    • “May we go” signifies a plea for guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • The mention of "the swiftest of the mind" emphasizes the importance of mental agility and quick thinking in overcoming challenges.
    • The term "threefold" can represent various aspects of life, such as body, mind, and spirit, or past, present, and future.
    • “Wind-born” suggests the influence of nature and the importance of being in harmony with the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a corporate setting, invoking the qualities of the Ashvins can inspire individuals to be swift and agile in decision-making.
    • In sports, athletes often seek mental clarity and physical strength, akin to the qualities described in this mantra, to enhance their performance.
    • In personal development, the mantra can serve as a reminder to maintain balance in various aspects of life, ensuring that one is not only focused on material success but also on mental and spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance in our journeys, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate mental agility, clarity, and strength, which are essential for navigating the complexities of life.
    • By embodying the qualities of the Ashvins, one can aspire to lead a balanced and fulfilling life, harmonizing the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions.
