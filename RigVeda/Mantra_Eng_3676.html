त्वं अङ्ग जरीतारं यविष्ठ विश्वान्य अग्ने दुरिताति पर्षि |स्तेनाऽदृश्रन् रिपवो जनासोऽज्ञातकेत वृजिनाऽभूवन ||
tvam aṅga jaritāraṁ yaviṣṭha viśvāny agne duritāti parṣi | stenā adṛśran ripavo janāso 'jñātaketā vṛjinā abhūvan ||
  • tvam aṅga jaritāraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Agni, are the one who brings forth strength." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the role of motivation and passion in achieving goals. For instance, a student preparing for exams draws strength from their determination to succeed.
  • yaviṣṭha viśvāny agne: This means "You are the one who is the best among all, O Agni." It emphasizes the supremacy of Agni among the deities. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of striving for excellence in various fields, such as sports or academics, where individuals aim to be the best in their endeavors.
  • duritāti parṣi: This translates to "Remove our difficulties." It is a plea for help in overcoming obstacles. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where individuals face challenges, such as financial difficulties or health issues, and seek support from friends, family, or community resources to overcome these hurdles.
  • stenā adṛśran: This phrase means "The thieves have not been seen." It suggests the idea of protection from harm or negativity. In real life, this can be related to the importance of security, whether it be physical safety or safeguarding one's reputation. For example, a community coming together to create a neighborhood watch program to protect against crime.
  • ripavo janāso 'jñātaketā: This translates to "The enemies of the people are not known." It highlights the need for awareness and vigilance against potential threats. In a modern context, this can be applied to social issues where individuals must be aware of injustices or discrimination in society and work towards addressing them.
  • vṛjinā abhūvan: This means "May we be free from all sins." It is a call for purity and righteousness. In real life, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of ethical living, where individuals strive to act with integrity and compassion in their daily interactions, such as volunteering for charitable causes or standing up against wrongdoing.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep spiritual and practical significance. It emphasizes the importance of strength, excellence, overcoming difficulties, protection from harm, awareness of threats, and the pursuit of righteousness. In real life, these themes resonate in various aspects, from personal development to community engagement. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can find inspiration to lead a life filled with purpose, resilience, and ethical values, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
