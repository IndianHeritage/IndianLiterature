ऊर्जो नपात् सहसावन्न इति त्वोपस्तुतस्य वन्दते वृषा वाक् |त्वां स्तोषाम तवया सुवीराः द्राघीय आयुः प्रतरं दधानाः ||
ūrjo napāt sahasāvann iti tvopastutasya vandate vṛṣā vāk | tvāṁ stoṣāma tvayā suvīrā drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ūrjo: energy, strength, or vitality
    • napāt: offspring or progeny
    • sahasāvann: with great power or might
    • iti: thus or so
    • tvopastutasya: of you who is being worshipped
    • vandate: we bow or pay homage
    • vṛṣā: the strong or powerful
    • vāk: speech or word
    • tvāṁ: you
    • stoṣāma: we praise or extol
    • tvayā: by you
    • suvīrā: the valiant or heroic
    • drāghīya: long-lasting or enduring
    • āyuḥ: life or longevity
    • prataraṁ: crossing or overcoming
    • dadhānāḥ: holding or possessing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of energy and strength, recognizing the divine source of vitality.
    • It emphasizes the importance of honoring and praising the divine, which is seen as the source of all power and life.
    • The mention of "suvīrā" suggests that true strength comes from valor and courage, qualities that are essential in overcoming life's challenges.
    • The reference to longevity ("drāghīya āyuḥ") indicates a desire for a long and prosperous life, which is often sought through spiritual practices and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and energy through various means such as exercise, nutrition, and meditation, reflecting the essence of "ūrjo."
    • Parents may invoke blessings for their children, wishing for them to be strong and successful, akin to the "napāt" aspect of the mantra.
    • In challenging situations, people may turn to prayer or meditation, seeking divine support to overcome obstacles, resonating with the idea of "tvāṁ stoṣāma."
    • Communities often come together to celebrate and honor their heroes, recognizing the importance of valor and strength in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of energy, strength, and life.
    • It highlights the significance of honoring the divine and seeking blessings for strength and longevity.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their pursuit of health, courage, and resilience.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a life of devotion, strength, and the pursuit of noble qualities that contribute to personal and communal well-being.
