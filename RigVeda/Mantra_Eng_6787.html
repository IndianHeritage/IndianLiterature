मन्त्र: प्रेदम् ब्रह्म वृततूर्येष्व आविथ प्र सुन्वतः शचीपत इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः | मध्यंदिनस्य सवनस्य वृत्रहन्न अनेद्य पिबा सोमस्य वज्रिवः ||
predam brahma vṛtratūryeṣv āvitha pra sunvataḥ śacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya vṛtrahann anedya pibā somasya vajrivaḥ ||
  • Predam: This word can be interpreted as "to be prepared" or "to be ready." In life, being prepared is crucial for success, whether it’s in academics, career, or personal relationships.
  • Brahma: Refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and understanding of the universe.
  • Vṛtratūryeṣv: This term relates to overcoming obstacles or challenges. In everyday life, we face various hurdles, and this mantra encourages us to confront and conquer them.
  • Āvitha: Means "to cover" or "to envelop." This can be seen in how we protect our loved ones or our values in a chaotic world.
  • Pra sunvataḥ: Translates to "those who are devoted." This emphasizes the importance of dedication and commitment in achieving our goals.
  • Śacīpata: Refers to the lord of strength or power. In real life, this can be associated with harnessing our inner strength to face adversities.
  • Indra: The king of gods, symbolizing leadership and authority. This can be related to taking charge in our lives and making impactful decisions.
  • Viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ: This phrase means "with all powers." It signifies the importance of utilizing all available resources and support systems in our endeavors.
  • Mādhyaṁdinasya: Refers to the midday or the peak of energy. This can be a metaphor for the prime time in our lives when we should be most productive.
  • Savanasy: Relates to offerings or sacrifices. In life, this can be interpreted as the sacrifices we make for our goals and the greater good.
  • Vṛtrahann: Means "the slayer of Vritra," symbolizing the triumph over evil or negativity. This can be seen in personal battles against fears or doubts.
  • Anedya: Translates to "unfailing" or "constant." This reflects the idea of resilience and perseverance in the face of challenges.
  • Pibā: Means "to drink." This can symbolize the act of absorbing knowledge or experiences that nourish our growth.
  • Somasya: Refers to the divine nectar or elixir, often associated with immortality. In life, this can represent the pursuit of wisdom and enlightenment.
  • Vajrivaḥ: Means "the one who wields the thunderbolt." This symbolizes strength and the ability to make impactful changes in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of preparation, overcoming challenges, and harnessing inner strength. It encourages individuals to be dedicated and resilient while utilizing all available resources to achieve their goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a guiding principle for personal development, leadership, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, one can navigate life's complexities with confidence and purpose.
