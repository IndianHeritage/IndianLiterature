तुभ्यं भरन्ति क्षितयो यविष्ट बलिम अग्ने अन्तित ओत दूरात् |आ भन्दिष्टस्य सुमतिं चिकीद्धि बृहत् ते अग्ने महि शर्मा भद्रम् ||
tubhyam bharanti kṣitayo yaviṣṭha balim agne antita ota dūrāt | ā bhandiṣṭhasya sumatiṁ cikiddhi bṛhat te agne mahi śarma bhadram ||
  • tubhyam bharanti kṣitayo yaviṣṭha balim agne antita ota dūrāt
    • tubhyam - "to you" or "for you"
    • bharanti - "they bear" or "they carry"
    • kṣitayo - "the earth" or "the ground"
    • yaviṣṭha - "the best" or "the most excellent"
    • balim - "offering" or "sacrifice"
    • agne - "O Agni" (the fire god)
    • antita - "from afar" or "from a distance"
    • ota - "food" or "nourishment"
    • dūrāt - "from far away"
  • ā bhandiṣṭhasya sumatiṁ cikiddhi bṛhat te agne mahi śarma bhadram
    • ā - "from" or "out of"
    • bhandiṣṭhasya - "the one who is well-established" or "the one who is strong"
    • sumatiṁ - "good thought" or "wisdom"
    • cikiddhi - "may you achieve" or "may you attain"
    • bṛhat - "great" or "vast"
    • te - "your"
    • agne - "O Agni"
    • mahi - "great" or "mighty"
    • śarma - "comfort" or "shelter"
    • bhadram - "auspicious" or "good fortune"

This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. The first part of the mantra expresses the idea that the earth provides offerings to Agni from a distance, symbolizing the connection between the material world and the spiritual realm. It highlights the importance of sacrifice and the nourishment that comes from the earth, which can be seen in real life through agricultural practices where farmers offer their harvests as a form of gratitude to nature.


The second part of the mantra seeks wisdom and good fortune from Agni, emphasizing the need for guidance and protection in life. This can be related to how individuals often seek blessings for success in their endeavors, whether in personal relationships, career paths, or spiritual journeys. For instance, people may light a candle or a fire during significant events, such as weddings or festivals, to invoke the presence of Agni and seek his blessings for a prosperous future.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its dual focus on the material and spiritual aspects of life. It teaches us the value of gratitude for the resources we receive from the earth and the importance of seeking wisdom and protection in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, celebrations, and personal reflections, reminding us to honor the balance between our earthly existence and our spiritual aspirations.
