स नो नियुद्भिः पुरुहूत वेधो विश्ववाराभिर आगहि प्रयज्यो |न यā अदेवो वरते न देव आभिर याहि तुयम् आ मद्र्यद्रिक ||
sa no niyudbhiḥ puruhūta vedho viśvavārābhir ā gahi prayajyo | na yā adevo varate na deva ābhir yāhi tūyam ā madryadrik ||
  • sa no niyudbhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May He (the divine) guide us." It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in our lives.
  • puruhūta vedho: "Puruhūta" refers to the one who is invoked or called upon, often associated with the divine or a deity. "Vedho" relates to knowledge or wisdom. Together, this suggests invoking the divine for wisdom and understanding.
  • viśvavārābhir ā gahi prayajyo: "Viśvavārā" means "all-encompassing" or "universal protection." "Ā gahi" translates to "may you come" or "may you be present." "Prayajyo" refers to offerings or sacrifices. This line expresses a desire for universal protection and the presence of the divine in our lives through our offerings.
  • na yā adevo varate: This translates to "the one who does not turn away from us." It signifies the importance of a divine presence that remains steadfast and supportive.
  • na deva ābhir yāhi tūyam: "Na deva" means "not the gods," and "ābhir yāhi" translates to "come with your blessings." This line suggests a call for blessings that are not just from the gods but from a higher, more universal source.
  • ā madryadrik: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be united in our purpose." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort in achieving our goals.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound appeal for divine guidance, wisdom, and protection in our lives. It highlights the significance of invoking the divine not just for personal gain but for universal well-being and collective purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or reflection to seek guidance and clarity in their life decisions.
  • Community Gatherings: It can be used in community events to foster unity and collective purpose, encouraging participants to work together for a common goal.
  • Rituals and Offerings: During religious or spiritual rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings and protection for the community and the environment.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors.
