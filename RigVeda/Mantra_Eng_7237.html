अभि प्र गोपतिं गिरेंद्रम् अर्च यथा विदे |सूनुं सत्यस्य सत्पतिम् ||
abhi pra gopatiṁ girendram arca yathā vide | sūnuṁ satyasya satpatim ||
  • Mantra: "abhi pra gopatiṁ girendram arca yathā vide | sūnuṁ satyasya satpatim ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "I invoke the Lord of the Earth, the great Indra, as I worship him, just as the son of truth, the master of the righteous path."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • pra: forth, forward
    • gopatiṁ: the lord of the earth or protector
    • girendram: Indra, the king of the gods, often associated with strength and rain
    • arca: to worship or to honor
    • yathā: as, in the manner of
    • vide: knowledge or understanding
    • sūnuṁ: son
    • satyasya: of truth
    • satpatim: the master of the righteous path
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine protection and guidance in our lives.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles, which can be likened to seeking support in challenging times.
    • The reference to "the son of truth" signifies the importance of honesty and righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or support from a higher power, similar to invoking Indra for strength.
    • In professional settings, leaders who embody truth and righteousness inspire their teams, much like the "master of the righteous path."
    • Community leaders who act with integrity and seek the welfare of their people can be seen as modern-day embodiments of the principles in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and embodying truth and righteousness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to look towards higher ideals and to act with integrity, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • In a world filled with challenges, invoking such principles can lead to personal growth and collective harmony.
