मित्रो अग्निः भवति यत् समिद्धो मित्रो होतā वरुणो जातवेदाः |मित्रो अध्वर्युः इषिरो दामूनā मित्रः सिन्धूनाम् उता पर्वतानाम् ||
mitro agnir bhavati yat samiddho mitro hotā varuṇo jātavedāḥ | mitro adhvaryur iṣiro damūnā mitraḥ sindhūnām uta parvatānām ||
  • Mitro Agnir Bhavati: This phrase translates to "Friendship is like fire." Here, 'Mitro' means friend, and 'Agnir' refers to fire. Just as fire provides warmth and light, friendship brings joy and support in our lives.
  • Yat Samiddho Mitro Hotā: This means "When ignited, friendship becomes a source of nourishment." It emphasizes that when we nurture our friendships, they become more fulfilling and beneficial, much like how a fire needs fuel to burn brightly.
  • Varuṇo Jātavedāḥ: 'Varuṇa' is the god of water and celestial order, while 'Jātavedāḥ' refers to the one who knows all. This suggests that true friendship is guided by a higher moral order and understanding, akin to how water sustains life.
  • Mitro Adhvaryur Iṣiro Damūnā: This translates to "Friendship is the priest who performs the rituals." Here, friendship is likened to a priest who facilitates important life events, highlighting the role of friends in our significant life moments.
  • Mitraḥ Sindhūnām Uta Parvatānām: This means "Friendship is found in rivers and mountains." It signifies that friendship is a natural and essential part of life, just as rivers and mountains are integral to the earth's landscape.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship in our lives, comparing it to fire, water, and the natural world. It teaches us that nurturing friendships can lead to a fulfilling and supportive life. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in how friends support each other during difficult times, celebrate achievements together, and create lasting memories. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to value and cultivate our friendships, as they are essential for our emotional and spiritual well-being.
