त्यं सुमेषं महयाऽस्वरविदं शतं यस्य सुभ्वः साकं ईरते |अत्यं न वाजं हवनस्यदं रथम इन्द्रं ववृत्यां अवसे सुवृक्तिभिः ||
tyaṁ su meṣam mahayā svarvidaṁ śataṁ yasya subhvaḥ sākam īrate | atyaṁ na vājaṁ havanasyadaṁ ratham endraṁ vavṛtyām avase suvṛktibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyaṁ: that
    • su: good
    • meṣam: sheep (often symbolizes wealth or prosperity)
    • mahayā: great
    • svarvidaṁ: knowledge of the sound or voice
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • yasya: of whom
    • subhvaḥ: auspicious
    • sākam: together
    • īrate: they worship
    • atyaṁ: beyond
    • na: not
    • vājaṁ: the prize or reward
    • havanasyadaṁ: of the sacrifice
    • ratham: chariot
    • endram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • vavṛtyām: in the midst of
    • avase: to attain
    • suvṛktibhiḥ: with good offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the worship of a divine entity, likely Indra, who is associated with strength, rain, and prosperity.
    • The mention of "sheep" symbolizes wealth and abundance, indicating a desire for prosperity in life.
    • The phrase "knowledge of the sound" suggests the importance of understanding and communication in spiritual practices.
    • It emphasizes the collective worship and offerings made to attain blessings and rewards, particularly in the context of sacrifices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of community and collective efforts in achieving goals, such as in team projects or community service.
    • The idea of making good offerings can be seen in charitable acts, where individuals come together to support a cause, reflecting the essence of "sākam īrate."
    • Understanding the "knowledge of the sound" can be applied in effective communication, whether in personal relationships or professional settings, highlighting the need for clarity and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of collective worship, the pursuit of prosperity, and the value of communication in spiritual and everyday life.
    • It teaches us that by coming together and making sincere offerings, we can attain greater rewards and blessings, both materially and spiritually.
    • In essence, it encourages us to foster community, seek knowledge, and strive for abundance in our lives.
