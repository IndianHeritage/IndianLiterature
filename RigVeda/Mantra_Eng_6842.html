मन्त्र: इमे विप्रस्य वेधसो 'ग्नेर अस्तृतयज्वनाḥ | गिरः स्तोमासा ईरते ||
ime viprasya vedhaso 'gner astṛtayajvanaḥ | giraḥ stomāsa īrate ||
  • ime viprasya vedhaso: This phrase can be translated as "these are the wise ones of the Vedas." Here, "vipra" refers to a learned or wise person, often a sage or a scholar who has deep knowledge of the Vedas. In real life, this can be likened to modern-day scholars or educators who impart wisdom and knowledge to others.
  • 'gner astṛtayajvanaḥ: This translates to "of the fire, the one who is the master of the sacrifice." In Vedic rituals, fire (Agni) is considered a divine entity and a mediator between humans and gods. The phrase emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices in spiritual practices. In contemporary life, this can be seen in various cultural ceremonies where fire plays a central role, such as weddings or religious festivals.
  • giraḥ stomāsa īrate: This means "the hymns and praises are invoked." "Giraḥ" refers to speech or sound, while "stomāsa" relates to hymns or praises. This part of the mantra highlights the significance of vocal expressions in spiritual practices. In modern contexts, this can be compared to the power of music, poetry, or spoken word in inspiring and uplifting communities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, ritual, and vocal expression in spiritual and communal life. It signifies that learned individuals play a crucial role in guiding society through knowledge and rituals, while the act of invoking hymns and praises fosters a sense of unity and reverence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in educational settings, cultural ceremonies, and community gatherings where the sharing of knowledge and collective worship enhances social bonds and spiritual growth.
