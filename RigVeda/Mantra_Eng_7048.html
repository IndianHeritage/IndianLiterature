यस्मै त्वं वसो दानाय मंहसे स रायस पोषम इन्बति |वसूयवो वसुपतिं शतक्रतुं स्तोमैर इन्द्रं हवामहे ||
yasmai tvaṁ vaso dānāya maṁhase sa rāyas poṣam invati | vasūyavo vasupatiṁ śatakratuṁ stomair indraṁ havāmahe ||
  • yasmai tvaṁ vaso dānāya maṁhase: This phrase can be translated to "To whom you, O Vasu, grant wealth and strength." Here, "Vasu" refers to the divine beings associated with wealth and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen as a prayer for abundance and the ability to share that wealth with others.
  • sa rāyas poṣam invati: This translates to "He who nourishes the wealth." This line emphasizes the importance of nurturing and sustaining wealth, not just acquiring it. For example, a business owner who reinvests profits into their community or employees exemplifies this principle.
  • vasūyavo vasupatiṁ: This means "the lord of the Vasus." The Vasus are a group of deities representing various aspects of nature and material wealth. This line acknowledges the divine source of prosperity and encourages respect for nature and its resources.
  • śatakratuṁ: This term refers to "the one with a hundred powers." It signifies the omnipotence of the divine, particularly in the context of Indra, the king of the gods. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea that individuals can harness their own potential and capabilities to achieve greatness.
  • stomair indraṁ havāmahe: This translates to "We invoke Indra with our hymns." This line is a call to the divine for blessings and support. In everyday life, this can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance and inspiration from higher powers or mentors when facing challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for wealth, strength, and guidance. It highlights the importance of nurturing prosperity and recognizing the divine sources of our resources. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in business practices that prioritize ethical growth, community support, and personal development. By invoking these principles, individuals and communities can strive for a balanced and prosperous life, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards wealth and resources.
