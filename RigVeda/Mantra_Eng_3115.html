मन्त्र: सेद अग्ने अस्तु शुभगः सुदानुर यस् त्वा नित्येन हविषा य उक्थैः |पिप्रीषति स्व आयुषि दुरोणे विश्वेद अस्मै सुदिना सासद इष्टिः ||
sed agne astu subhagaḥ sudānur yas tvā nityena haviṣā ya ukthaiḥ | piprīṣati sva āyuṣi duroṇe viśved asmai sudinā sāsad iṣṭiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sed: but
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • astu: may there be
    • subhagaḥ: auspicious
    • sudānur: well-nourished
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • nityena: with eternal
    • haviṣā: offerings
    • ya: who
    • ukthaiḥ: with hymns
    • piprīṣati: nourishes
    • sva: his own
    • āyuṣi: life
    • duroṇe: in the great abundance
    • viśved: all
    • asmai: to him
    • sudinā: with good fortune
    • sāsad: may he attain
    • iṣṭiḥ: the offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for auspiciousness and nourishment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of eternal offerings and hymns to sustain life and abundance.
    • The phrase "may he attain the offerings" signifies the desire for blessings and prosperity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude and appreciation for the resources we have.
    • For instance, during a family gathering, lighting a fire and offering prayers can symbolize unity and the sharing of blessings.
    • In a professional setting, recognizing the contributions of team members and celebrating achievements can be seen as a modern interpretation of making offerings to Agni.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for auspiciousness and nourishment through devotion and gratitude.
    • It teaches us the value of maintaining a connection with the divine through our actions and intentions.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a mindset of abundance, appreciation, and the importance of community, which can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
