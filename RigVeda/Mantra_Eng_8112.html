त्वोतासस तवावसā स्यामा वन्वन्त आमुरः |सोम व्रतेषु जागृहि ||
tvotāsas tavāvasā syāma vanvanta āmuraḥ | soma vrateṣu jāgṛhi ||
  • tvotāsas: This word can be broken down into "tva" meaning "your" and "tāsas" which refers to "glory" or "brilliance". It signifies the divine qualities or the radiant nature of the deity being addressed.
  • tavāvasā: This translates to "may we be protected by you". It expresses a plea for safety and guidance from the divine, indicating a relationship of trust and reliance.
  • syāma: This word means "may we become" or "let us be". It is an invocation for transformation or a change in state, often towards a more favorable condition.
  • vanvanta: This term can be interpreted as "those who possess" or "those who are rich in". It often refers to abundance, particularly in the context of nature or resources.
  • āmuraḥ: This word means "immortal" or "eternal". It signifies the quest for longevity or a connection to the eternal aspects of existence.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine ecstasy. It represents the spiritual nourishment that sustains life.
  • vrateṣu: This translates to "in the vows" or "in the rituals". It indicates the importance of commitment and discipline in spiritual practices.
  • jāgṛhi: This means "awaken" or "be vigilant". It is a call to remain alert and aware, both spiritually and in daily life.

In summary, this mantra is a profound invocation that seeks the divine's protection and guidance while aspiring for transformation and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual practices and the need for vigilance in one's spiritual journey. The reference to Soma highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment in achieving immortality and eternal wisdom.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life as a reminder to seek guidance and protection from higher powers, especially during challenging times.
  • It encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of abundance and gratitude for the resources they possess, fostering a mindset of positivity.
  • The emphasis on spiritual practices and rituals can inspire people to engage in regular meditation, prayer, or other forms of spiritual discipline, enhancing their overall well-being.
  • Being vigilant and aware in one's actions and thoughts can lead to better decision-making and a more fulfilling life.
