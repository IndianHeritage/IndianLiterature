आ यं हस्ते न खादिनं शिशुं जातं न बिभ्रति |विशाम अग्निं स्वध्वरम् ||
ā yaṁ haste na khādinaṁ śiśuṁ jātaṁ na bibhrati | viśām agniṁ svadhvaram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yaṁ: This refers to "that which" or "that which is present."
    • haste: Means "in the hand" or "by the hand."
    • na: Translates to "not."
    • khādinaṁ: Means "eating" or "consuming."
    • śiśuṁ: Refers to "child" or "infant."
    • jātaṁ: Means "born" or "created."
    • na bibhrati: Translates to "does not bear" or "does not carry."
    • viśām: Refers to "of the community" or "of the people."
    • agniṁ: Means "fire," often symbolizing energy, transformation, or divine presence.
    • svadhvaram: Refers to "the one who is self-illuminated" or "the one who is self-sustaining."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the protection and nurturing of the innocent and vulnerable, represented by the child.
    • It emphasizes the idea that one should not harm or consume the innocent, symbolized by the child, and that the community should safeguard its members.
    • The mention of "fire" (agni) signifies the transformative power of community and the divine energy that supports and protects the innocent.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be applied to the way parents nurture and protect their children, ensuring they grow up in a safe environment.
    • In a community, it can reflect the responsibility of society to protect its vulnerable members, such as the elderly, children, and those in need.
    • In a broader context, it can relate to social justice movements that advocate for the rights and protection of marginalized groups.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the moral obligation to protect the innocent and vulnerable in society.
    • It highlights the importance of community support and the role of divine energy in fostering a safe and nurturing environment.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to act with compassion and responsibility towards those who cannot protect themselves.
