यथा पूर्वेभ्यो जरीत्रिभ्य इन्द्र मय इवापो न तृष्यते बभूथा |ताम अनु त्वा निविदं जोहवीमि विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
yathā pūrvebhyo jaritṛbhya indra maya ivāpo na tṛṣyate babhūtha | tām anu tvā nividaṁ johavīmi vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • yathā pūrvebhyo: This phrase translates to "as in the past" or "like those who came before." It suggests a connection to tradition and the wisdom of ancestors.
  • jaritṛbhya: This word refers to "the singers" or "the poets," indicating those who express knowledge and wisdom through their art.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the sky. He represents the force that drives away obstacles and brings prosperity.
  • maya ivāpo: This can be interpreted as "like the waters," suggesting a fluidity and adaptability in life, akin to how water flows and nourishes.
  • na tṛṣyate babhūtha: This phrase means "it does not thirst," indicating a state of fulfillment and abundance, where needs are met without longing.
  • tām anu tvā: This translates to "following you," suggesting a sense of guidance and direction, as if one is being led by a higher power or wisdom.
  • nividaṁ johavīmi: This means "I invoke the knowledge," indicating a call to wisdom and understanding, seeking enlightenment.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: This refers to "this knowledge," emphasizing the importance of learning and education in achieving one's goals.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This word can mean "the group" or "the community," highlighting the significance of collective effort and support.
  • jīradānum: This translates to "the life-giving," suggesting that the knowledge and wisdom invoked are essential for vitality and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of learning from the past, invoking wisdom, and recognizing the interconnectedness of community and knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, leadership, and personal development. For instance, in a workplace, leaders can draw on the experiences of past leaders (yathā pūrvebhyo) to guide their teams effectively. Similarly, in personal growth, individuals can seek knowledge (nividaṁ johavīmi) from mentors and the community (vṛjanaṁ) to foster a fulfilling life. The mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom, when sought and shared, can lead to a life of abundance and purpose, free from unfulfilled desires (na tṛṣyate babhūtha).
