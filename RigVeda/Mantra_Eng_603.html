त्वं अपाम अपिधानावृणोर अपाधारयः पर्वते दानुमद वसु |वृत्रं यद इन्द्र शवसावधीर अहिमाद इति सूर्यं दिव्य आरोहयो दृष्टे ||
tvam apām apidhānāvṛṇor apādhārayaḥ parvate dānumad vasu | vṛtraṁ yad indra śavasāvadhīr ahim ād it sūryaṁ divy ārohayo dṛśe ||
  • tvam apām apidhānāvṛṇor: This phrase can be translated as "You, who are the cover of the waters." Here, "tvam" refers to the divine or a higher power, while "apām" means waters. In real life, this can symbolize the nurturing and life-giving aspects of water, which is essential for all living beings.
  • apādhārayaḥ: This term means "the one who supports." It signifies stability and strength. In our daily lives, we often rely on various forms of support, whether it be emotional, financial, or social. For instance, family and friends provide the necessary support during challenging times.
  • parvate dānumad vasu: This translates to "on the mountain, the bountiful wealth." Mountains are often seen as symbols of strength and permanence. In a real-life context, this can refer to the wealth of resources that nature provides, such as minerals, forests, and clean air, which are vital for our survival.
  • vṛtraṁ yad indra śavasāvadhīr: This means "the one who overcomes the obstacle, Indra, the strong." Indra is a deity associated with strength and power. In our lives, we face various obstacles, and this line encourages us to be resilient and confront challenges head-on, much like overcoming personal or professional hurdles.
  • ahim ād it sūryaṁ divy ārohayo dṛśe: This can be interpreted as "the divine sun that rises, dispelling the darkness." The sun symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and hope. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of education in illuminating our paths and dispelling ignorance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of support systems, and the strength to overcome obstacles. It highlights the significance of water, mountains, and the sun as vital elements in our lives. In a broader sense, it encourages us to seek knowledge, support one another, and face challenges with resilience. This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, community support, and environmental conservation, reminding us of our responsibilities towards nature and each other.
