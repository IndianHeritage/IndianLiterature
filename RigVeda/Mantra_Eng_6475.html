यथा रुद्रस्य सूनवो दिवो वशन्त्य असुरस्य वेधसः |युवानस तथेद असत ||
yathā rudrasya sūnavo divo vaśanty asurasya vedhasaḥ | yuvānas tathed asat ||
  • Mantra: "yathā rudrasya sūnavo divo vaśanty asurasya vedhasaḥ | yuvānas tathed asat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as, like
    • rudrasya: of Rudra (a form of Shiva, representing destruction and transformation)
    • sūnavo: sons, offspring
    • dīvo: of the sky, heavenly
    • vaśanty: dwell, reside
    • asurasya: of the Asura (often representing powerful beings, sometimes in opposition to the Devas)
    • vedhasaḥ: of the wise, knowledgeable
    • yuvānas: youthful, young
    • tathā: so, thus
    • asat: not true, false, or non-existent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between Rudra and his offspring, suggesting that just as the sons of Rudra reside in the heavenly realms, the powerful beings (Asuras) also have their place among the wise.
    • The mention of "yuvānas" indicates a sense of youthfulness and vitality, suggesting that the energy and potential of youth are significant in the cosmic order.
    • The phrase "tathā asat" implies a contrast between what is true and what is false, hinting at the importance of discernment in understanding the nature of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to the idea of mentorship and guidance. Just as Rudra's sons may learn from their father, young individuals today benefit from the wisdom of their elders in various fields, such as education, business, and personal development.
    • The concept of discernment is crucial in today's world, where misinformation can easily spread. The mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, much like the wise Asuras, to differentiate between truth and falsehood.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra can be applied to the balance of power and knowledge in society. The youth, representing potential and energy, must learn to navigate the complexities of life, much like the Asuras and Devas in their cosmic roles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, the role of youth, and the need for discernment in life. It serves as a reminder that while power and knowledge exist in various forms, it is essential to understand their true nature.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, leadership, and personal growth, where the guidance of the wise and the energy of the young can lead to a harmonious and enlightened society.
