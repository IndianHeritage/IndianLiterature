उत दासं कौलितरं बृहतः पर्वताद अधि |अवाहन् इन्द्र शम्बरम् ||
uta dāsaṁ kaulitaram bṛhataḥ parvatād adhi | avāhann indra śambaram ||
  • uta: This word translates to "or" or "also," indicating a choice or an alternative.
  • dāsaṁ: This means "servant" or "devotee," referring to someone who is devoted or submissive to a higher power.
  • kaulitaram: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is of the family" or "the one who belongs to a lineage," suggesting a connection to tradition or heritage.
  • bṛhataḥ: This means "great" or "vast," often used to describe something of immense size or significance.
  • parvatād: This translates to "from the mountain," indicating a source or origin that is elevated or lofty.
  • adhi: This word means "above" or "over," suggesting a position of superiority or elevation.
  • avāhann: This means "to bring down" or "to invoke," indicating a call to action or a request for assistance.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength.
  • śambaram: This term can be interpreted as "the auspicious" or "the benevolent," referring to something that brings good fortune or blessings.

The mantra can be understood as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, to bring down blessings from the lofty mountains, invoking his strength and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine, highlighting the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher powers.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking Guidance: Just as the mantra calls upon Indra for blessings, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in times of uncertainty.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The invocation of strength from a higher source can be likened to seeking motivation and support when facing personal or professional challenges.
  • Connection to Heritage: The reference to lineage reminds individuals of the importance of their roots and the values passed down through generations.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of humility, devotion, and the recognition of higher powers in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek blessings and guidance from the divine, especially when navigating the complexities of life. By invoking strength and auspiciousness, individuals can find the courage to face challenges and remain connected to their heritage.
