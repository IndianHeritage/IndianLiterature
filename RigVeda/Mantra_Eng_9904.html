अपाः पूर्वेषां हरिवः सुतानाम् अथो इदं सवनं केवलं ते |ममद्धि सोमं मधुमन्तम् इन्द्र सत्रा वृषाण जठर आ वृषस्व ||
apāḥ pūrveṣāṁ harivaḥ sutānām atho idaṁ savanaṁ kevalaṁ te | mamaddhi somam madhumantam indra satrā vṛṣañ jaṭhara ā vṛṣasva ||
  • Mantra: "apāḥ pūrveṣāṁ harivaḥ sutānām atho idaṁ savanaṁ kevalaṁ te | mamaddhi somam madhumantam indra satrā vṛṣañ jaṭhara ā vṛṣasva ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, may this offering of ours, which is the essence of the waters and the sweet nectar, be accepted by you. We invoke you to partake of this soma, which is rich and potent, and to bless us with your strength."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • apāḥ: waters, representing purity and life.
    • pūrveṣāṁ: of the ancients, indicating a connection to tradition and wisdom.
    • harivaḥ: sweet or delightful, often associated with joy and positivity.
    • sutānām: of the offerings, referring to the gifts made to the divine.
    • atho: therefore, indicating a cause-and-effect relationship.
    • idaṁ: this, pointing to the specific offering being made.
    • savanaṁ: the act of offering or sacrifice.
    • kevalaṁ: only or solely, emphasizing the exclusivity of the offering.
    • te: to you, addressing the divine being, Indra.
    • mamaddhi: may you accept, a plea for acceptance of the offering.
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and strength.
    • madhumantam: sweet or rich, describing the desirable qualities of the soma.
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and strength.
    • satrā: the ritual or sacrifice, a communal act of devotion.
    • vṛṣañ: strength or vigor, a request for empowerment.
    • jaṭhara: belly or womb, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • ā: towards, indicating direction or intention.
    • vṛṣasva: may you be strong, a call for divine strength and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to a prayer or invocation made during significant life events, such as weddings or festivals, where individuals seek blessings for strength and prosperity.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of teamwork and collaboration, akin to the offerings made to Indra, to foster a productive and harmonious work environment.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may reflect on the essence of this mantra, seeking inner strength and clarity, much like the ancient seekers who called upon Indra for support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and the importance of offerings in establishing a connection with the divine. It emphasizes the need for purity, intention, and community in our actions. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers, whether in personal endeavors, communal activities, or during times of need. By acknowledging our roots and the wisdom of the ancients, we can navigate our modern lives with purpose and resilience.
