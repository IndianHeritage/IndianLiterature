तां तु त इन्द्र महतो महानि विश्वेष्वित सवनेषु प्रवाच्याः |यच छूर धृष्णो धृषता दधृष्वान अहिं वज्रेण शवसाविवेषीः ||
tā tū ta indra mahato mahāni viśveṣv it savaneṣu pravācyā | yac chūra dhṛṣṇo dhṛṣatā dadhṛṣvān ahiṁ vajreṇa śavasāviveṣīḥ ||
  • tā tū ta indra mahato mahāni viśveṣv it savaneṣu pravācyā
    • tā tū ta: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which you are" or "that which you represent." It emphasizes the essence of the subject being addressed.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. He symbolizes power and authority.
    • mahato: This means "great" or "mighty," indicating the vastness of Indra's influence.
    • mahāni: This translates to "great things" or "great deeds," referring to the significant actions or qualities of Indra.
    • viśveṣv: This word means "in all" or "in every," suggesting a universal presence or influence.
    • it savaneṣu pravācyā: This can be understood as "in the offerings" or "in the invocations," indicating the context of rituals and prayers directed towards Indra.
  • yac chūra dhṛṣṇo dhṛṣatā dadhṛṣvān ahiṁ vajreṇa śavasāviveṣīḥ
    • yac: This means "that which" or "what," introducing a clause that describes a quality or action.
    • chūra: This translates to "brave" or "valiant," highlighting the qualities of courage and strength.
    • dhṛṣṇo: This word means "of the bold" or "of the daring," reinforcing the theme of bravery.
    • dhṛṣatā: This refers to "strength" or "power," emphasizing the importance of resilience.
    • dadhṛṣvān: This means "having taken" or "having held," indicating an active engagement with strength.
    • ahiṁ: This translates to "the serpent" or "the adversary," which can symbolize challenges or obstacles.
    • vajreṇa: This means "with the thunderbolt," a weapon associated with Indra, symbolizing power and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • śavasāviveṣīḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth life" or "the one who revives," indicating a transformative power.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, bravery, and the divine influence of Indra in overcoming challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of courage and resilience in the face of adversity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal challenges, invoking the qualities of Indra can inspire individuals to face their fears and overcome obstacles.
  • In leadership, the mantra can serve as a guiding principle for those in positions of authority to act with strength and integrity.
  • In community rituals, it can be used to invoke blessings for collective strength and unity in facing societal challenges.

Overall, this mantra encapsulates the essence of divine strength and the human spirit's capacity to rise above difficulties, making it relevant in both spiritual and practical aspects of life.
