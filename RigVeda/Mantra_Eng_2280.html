उभे अस्मै पीपयतः समीची दिवो वृष्टिं शुभगो नाम पुष्यन |उभा क्षयाव आजयन याति पृत्सूभाव अर्धौ भवतः साधू अस्मै ||
ubhe asmai pīpayataḥ samīcī divo vṛṣṭiṁ subhago nāma puṣyan | ubhā kṣayāv ājayan yāti pṛtsūbhāv ardhau bhavataḥ sādhū asmai ||
  • Mantra: "ubhe asmai pīpayataḥ samīcī divo vṛṣṭiṁ subhago nāma puṣyan | ubhā kṣayāv ājayan yāti pṛtsūbhāv ardhau bhavataḥ sādhū asmai ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ubhe: both
    • asmai: to him
    • pīpayataḥ: who is nourishing
    • samīcī: in a proper manner
    • divo: of the heavens
    • vṛṣṭiṁ: rain or abundance
    • subhago: auspicious or fortunate
    • nāma: by name
    • puṣyan: nourishing or fostering
    • ubhā: both
    • kṣayāv: in decline or destruction
    • ājayan: to bring forth
    • yāti: goes
    • pṛtsūbhāv: in the state of the earth
    • ardhau: in halves
    • bhavataḥ: of you
    • sādhū: good or virtuous
    • asmai: to him
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the duality of nourishment and abundance, emphasizing the importance of both aspects in life.
    • The phrase "ubhe asmai pīpayataḥ" suggests that both forms of nourishment, whether physical or spiritual, are essential for a balanced life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way we need both food (physical nourishment) and knowledge or wisdom (spiritual nourishment) to thrive.
    • The mention of "divo vṛṣṭiṁ" refers to the blessings from the heavens, which can be interpreted as the natural resources and opportunities that come our way.
    • For example, a farmer relies on rain ("vṛṣṭi") for crops, which symbolizes the abundance that nature provides.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "ubhā kṣayāv ājayan," indicates that both aspects of life can lead to decline if not managed properly, highlighting the need for balance.
    • In a modern context, this can relate to how overindulgence in material wealth without spiritual growth can lead to a sense of emptiness or decline in well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of balance between physical and spiritual nourishment in our lives.
    • It teaches us that both aspects are interconnected and essential for overall well-being.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek a harmonious life where material success is complemented by personal growth and ethical living.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can strive for a fulfilling life that honors both the physical and spiritual dimensions.
