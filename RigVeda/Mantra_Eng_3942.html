अस्मा इति काव्यं वच उक्तं इन्द्राय शंस्यम् |तस्मा उ ब्रह्मवाहसे गिरो वर्धन्त्यात्रयो गिरः शुम्भन्त्यात्रयः ||
asmā it kāvyaṁ vaca uktham indrāya śaṁsyam | tasmā u brahmavāhase giro vardhanty atrayo giraḥ śumbhanty atrayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā: from us
    • it: thus
    • kāvyaṁ: poetic or eloquent
    • vaca: speech or words
    • uktham: utterance or hymn
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • śaṁsyam: worthy of praise
    • tasmā: therefore
    • u: and
    • brahmavāhase: to the one who carries the divine knowledge
    • giro: words or sounds
    • vardhanty: they grow or increase
    • atrayo: these (referring to the hymns)
    • giraḥ: utterances or voices
    • śumbhanty: they are praised
    • atrayaḥ: these (referring to the hymns)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of eloquent speech and the importance of words in connecting with the divine.
    • It suggests that when we express ourselves poetically and with intention, our words become worthy offerings to the divine, particularly to Indra, who represents strength and leadership.
    • The phrase "brahmavāhase giro vardhanty" indicates that such words, when directed towards the divine, have the potential to grow and flourish, enhancing our spiritual connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In public speaking, a well-crafted speech can inspire and motivate an audience, much like how the mantra suggests that eloquent words can reach the divine.
    • In literature, poetry often serves as a medium to express deep emotions and thoughts, resonating with readers and creating a shared experience.
    • In daily life, expressing gratitude or intentions through spoken or written words can manifest positive changes, reflecting the idea that our words hold power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of words and their ability to connect us with higher powers.
    • It teaches us that our expressions, when filled with intention and reverence, can lead to personal growth and spiritual elevation.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in communication, art, or personal reflection, the essence of this mantra encourages us to be mindful of our words and their impact on ourselves and the world around us.
