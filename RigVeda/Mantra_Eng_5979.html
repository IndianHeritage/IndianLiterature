महें चन त्वां अद्रिवः परा शुल्काय देयाम् |न सहस्राय नायुताय वज्रिवो न शताय शतामघ ||
mahe cana tvām adrivaḥ parā śulkāya deyām | na sahasrāya nāyutāya vajrivo na śatāya śatāmagha ||
  • mahe cana tvām adrivaḥ parā śulkāya deyām
    • mahe - This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies the vastness and importance of the subject being addressed.
    • cana - This means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the certainty of the statement.
    • tvām - This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a deity or a higher power.
    • adrivaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "without obstacles" or "free from hindrances," suggesting a state of purity or clarity.
    • parā - Meaning "supreme" or "transcendent," it refers to the highest form of existence or consciousness.
    • śulkāya - This word means "for the price" or "for the offering," indicating a sense of exchange or reciprocity in spiritual practices.
    • deyām - This translates to "I give" or "I offer," signifying the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • na sahasrāya nāyutāya vajrivo na śatāya śatāmagha
    • na - This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • sahasrāya - This translates to "thousands," suggesting abundance or a large quantity.
    • nāyutāya - Meaning "ten thousands," it further emphasizes the idea of vastness.
    • vajrivo - This term refers to "thunderbolt" or "indestructible," symbolizing strength and power.
    • na - Again, this indicates negation.
    • śatāya - This translates to "hundreds," continuing the theme of numerical abundance.
    • śatāmagha - This means "hundredfold sin," suggesting the idea of moral or spiritual debt.

Meaning and Interpretation:

This mantra expresses a profound offering to a higher power, emphasizing the importance of purity and the act of giving. The speaker acknowledges the greatness of the divine and offers something valuable without any hindrances. The latter part of the mantra suggests that the offerings made are not to be measured in terms of quantity or material wealth, but rather in the purity of intention and the spiritual significance behind them.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to acts of charity where the intention behind the donation is more significant than the amount given. For instance, a person may choose to volunteer their time and skills to help others, which is a more profound offering than merely donating money.
  • In personal relationships, the mantra can be applied to the idea of giving love and support without expecting anything in return, highlighting the importance of selfless acts.

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the purity of intention and the spiritual value of offerings. It teaches that true giving transcends material wealth and is rooted in the quality of the act itself. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to focus on the essence of their contributions, whether in charity, relationships, or personal growth, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with others.
