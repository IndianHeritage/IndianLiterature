अग्नि कविः वेधाऽसि होतā पावक यक्ष्यः |मन्द्रो यजिष्ठो अध्वरिषु ईड्यो विप्रेभिः शुक्र मन्यमभिः ||
agne kavir vedhā asi hotā pāvaka yakṣyaḥ | mandro yajiṣṭho adhvareṣv īḍyo viprebhiḥ śukra manmabhiḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Kavir: This term means 'the wise one' or 'the poet,' indicating the creative and intellectual aspects of fire.
  • Vedhā: This translates to 'the one who knows' or 'the seer,' emphasizing the knowledge and insight associated with Agni.
  • Asi: This means 'you are,' affirming the identity of Agni as a divine presence.
  • Hotā: This refers to 'the one who invokes,' highlighting the role of Agni in rituals and sacrifices.
  • Pāvaka: Meaning 'the purifier,' this term signifies the cleansing and transformative power of fire.
  • Yakṣyaḥ: This word can be interpreted as 'the one who grants,' indicating Agni's role in bestowing blessings and prosperity.
  • Mandro: This means 'the sweet one,' suggesting the pleasant and beneficial nature of Agni.
  • Yajiṣṭho: This translates to 'the most worthy of worship,' emphasizing the importance of Agni in Vedic rituals.
  • Adhvareṣv: This means 'in the sacrifices,' indicating the context in which Agni is invoked.
  • Īḍyo: This translates to 'worthy of praise,' highlighting the reverence for Agni.
  • Viprebhiḥ: This means 'by the priests,' indicating the role of priests in invoking Agni during rituals.
  • Śukra: This term means 'bright' or 'pure,' further emphasizing the purity associated with Agni.
  • Manmabhiḥ: This translates to 'with our minds,' indicating the mental focus and intention behind the invocation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the multifaceted nature of Agni as a divine force that embodies wisdom, purity, and the power to grant blessings. In real life, the significance of this mantra can be seen in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: Agni is central to many Hindu rituals, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • Personal Transformation: The qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, purity, and wisdom in their lives.
  • Environmental Awareness: Fire represents energy and transformation, reminding us of the importance of respecting nature and using resources wisely.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of invoking positive energies for personal and communal growth.
