अनर्वाणं वृषभं मन्द्रजिह्वं बृहस्पतिं वर्धयाऽनव्यम् अर्कैः |गाथान्यः सुरुचो यस्य देवाः आशृण्वन्ति नवमानस्य मर्ताः ||
anarvāṇaṁ vṛṣabham mandrajihvam bṛhaspatiṁ vardhayā navyam arkaiḥ | gāthānyaḥ suruco yasya devā āśṛṇvanti navamānasya martāḥ ||
  • anarvāṇaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is not to be harmed" or "the invincible." It signifies a state of being that is beyond defeat or injury.
  • vṛṣabham: This word translates to "the bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in various cultures. In the context of the mantra, it represents a powerful force or deity.
  • mandrajihvam: This term means "with a sweet tongue" or "eloquent." It suggests the importance of communication and the ability to express oneself effectively.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ: Referring to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods and the deity of wisdom and knowledge. This highlights the significance of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • vardhayā: This means "to increase" or "to enhance." It indicates a desire for growth, prosperity, and improvement in various aspects of life.
  • navyam: This translates to "new" or "fresh," symbolizing innovation, renewal, and the potential for new beginnings.
  • arkaiḥ: This word refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with the sun or divine illumination. It signifies enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • gāthānyaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who sing" or "those who express." It emphasizes the importance of sharing knowledge and experiences through expression.
  • suruco: This means "well-formed" or "beautiful," indicating the value of aesthetics and harmony in life.
  • yasya: This translates to "whose," indicating possession or belonging, often referring to the divine or higher powers.
  • devā: This word means "gods" or "divine beings," representing higher consciousness and spiritual guidance.
  • āśṛṇvanti: This means "they listen" or "they hear," emphasizing the importance of attentiveness and receptivity to wisdom.
  • navamānasya: This term translates to "of the new mind" or "of the fresh spirit," indicating a state of renewal and openness to new ideas.
  • martāḥ: This word means "mortals" or "human beings," highlighting the connection between the divine and human experience.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking invincibility, wisdom, and renewal through eloquent expression and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of communication, growth, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By embodying the qualities of strength, eloquence, and receptivity to wisdom, individuals can navigate challenges and foster a harmonious environment. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a balanced approach to life, where the divine and human aspects coexist, leading to a fulfilling and enlightened existence.
