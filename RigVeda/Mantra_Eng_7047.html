यो नो दाताऽस नः पिता महाम् उग्र ईशानकृत् |अयामन् उग्रो मगवाऽपुरूवसुर गोर अश्वस्य प्र दातु नः ||
yo no dātā sa naḥ pitā mahām̐ ugra īśānakṛt | ayāmann ugro maghavā purūvasur gor aśvasya pra dātu naḥ ||
  • Translation: "He who is the giver, he is our great father, the powerful lord; may he grant us strength, the mighty one, the giver of wealth, the one who is the source of all that is good."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: He who
    • no: our
    • dātā: giver
    • sa: he
    • naḥ: to us
    • pitā: father
    • mahām̐: great
    • ugra: powerful
    • īśānakṛt: lord or ruler
    • ayāmann: may he grant
    • ugro: mighty
    • maghavā: giver of wealth
    • purūvasur: source of all that is good
    • gor: of strength
    • aśvasya: of the horse (symbolizing speed and power)
    • pra dātu: may he grant us
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the role of a higher power as a nurturing father figure.
    • The term "giver" signifies the abundance and generosity of the universe, encouraging individuals to seek and appreciate the gifts of life.
    • The reference to "strength" and "wealth" highlights the need for both physical and spiritual resources to thrive in life.
    • The mention of "the horse" symbolizes speed, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles, which can be interpreted as the drive to achieve one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, seeking strength and guidance from a higher power when facing challenges.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used in rituals or gatherings to invoke blessings for prosperity and unity among members.
    • In a corporate environment, leaders may draw inspiration from this mantra to foster a culture of generosity and support, encouraging teamwork and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking support from the divine and from one another.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the strength and resources available to us, urging us to be grateful and to share our blessings with others.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate resilience, generosity, and a sense of community, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to social harmony.
