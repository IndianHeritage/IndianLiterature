आ तु न इन्द्र वृत्रहन् अस्माकं अर्धं आ गहि |महान महीभिर ऊतिभिः ||
ā tū na indra vṛtrahann asmākam ardham ā gahi | mahān mahībhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • tū: you
    • na: not
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms)
    • vṛtrahann: slayer of Vritra (a demon representing drought and obstacles)
    • asmākam: our
    • ardham: half
    • ā: to
    • gahi: grasp or hold
    • mahān: great
    • mahībhir: with great (or vast) things
    • ūtibhiḥ: with praises or offerings
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a plea to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to not withhold his blessings from us. It emphasizes the importance of seeking his grace to overcome obstacles (symbolized by Vritra) and to attain prosperity and abundance in our lives. The invocation of "great things" and "praises" suggests a call for collective effort and reverence in seeking divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Agricultural Context: Farmers often pray for rain during droughts. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of their hopes for favorable weather conditions to ensure a good harvest.
    • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing difficulties in life, such as financial struggles or health issues, may invoke this mantra as a way to seek strength and support from a higher power to overcome their challenges.
    • Community Gatherings: In festivals or community events, people may come together to chant this mantra, symbolizing unity and collective aspiration for prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support in overcoming life's challenges. It highlights the importance of community, reverence, and the pursuit of greatness through collective efforts. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers, whether in personal struggles or communal endeavors, reinforcing the belief that with faith and unity, obstacles can be overcome.
