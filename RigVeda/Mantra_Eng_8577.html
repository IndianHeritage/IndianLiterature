अरश्मानो ये 'रथा आयुक्ता अत्यासो न ससृजानासा आजौ |एते शुक्रासो धन्वन्ति सोमादेवासस ताम् उप याता पिबध्यै ||
araśmāno ye 'rathā ayuktā atyāso na sasṛjānāsa ājau | ete śukrāso dhanvanti somā devāsas tām̐ upa yātā pibadhyai ||
  • Mantra: "araśmāno ye 'rathā ayuktā atyāso na sasṛjānāsa ājau | ete śukrāso dhanvanti somā devāsas tām̐ upa yātā pibadhyai ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are unbound by the reins, who are not driven by the chariots, do not create the great illusion. These are the bright ones, the divine beings, who drink the essence of the moon."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • araśmāno: Unbound or free from restraints.
    • ye: Those who.
    • 'rathā: Chariots, symbolizing the journey of life.
    • ayuktā: Not driven or controlled.
    • atyāso: Great illusion or delusion.
    • na: Not.
    • sasṛjānāsa: Create or manifest.
    • ājau: In the realm of the divine or spiritual.
    • ete: These.
    • śukrāso: Bright or luminous beings.
    • dhanvanti: Drink or consume.
    • somā: The essence of the moon, often associated with nourishment and immortality.
    • devāsas: Divine beings or gods.
    • tām̐: That.
    • upa: Towards or near.
    • yātā: Go or move.
    • pibadhyai: To drink or consume.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of being "unbound" can be related to individuals who pursue their passions without societal constraints, such as artists or entrepreneurs who follow their creative instincts rather than conforming to traditional paths.
    • The idea of "not creating great illusion" can be seen in mindfulness practices, where individuals strive to see reality as it is, free from the distractions and delusions of modern life.
    • The "bright ones" can be likened to enlightened individuals or spiritual leaders who inspire others through their wisdom and clarity, such as Mahatma Gandhi or the Dalai Lama.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of freedom from societal constraints and the pursuit of truth and enlightenment.
    • It encourages individuals to seek their own paths and to consume the essence of life, represented by the moon, which symbolizes nourishment and spiritual growth.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, spiritual practices, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us to remain true to ourselves and to seek deeper understanding beyond superficial illusions.
