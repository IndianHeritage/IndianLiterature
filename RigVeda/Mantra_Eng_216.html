सखाय आ नि शीदत सविता स्तोम्यो नु नः |दाता राधांसी शुम्भति ||
sakhāya ā ni ṣīdata savitā stomyo nu naḥ | dātā rādhāṁsi śumbhati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhāya: Friends or companions
    • ā: Here, indicating presence or direction
    • ni: Down or below
    • ṣīdata: To sit or dwell
    • savitā: The Sun, often personified as a deity
    • stomyo: Worthy of praise or hymns
    • nu: Indeed or surely
    • naḥ: Our
    • dātā: Giver or bestower
    • rādhāṁsi: Prosperity or wealth
    • śumbhati: To bestow or grant
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the Sun (Savitā) to bless us with prosperity and to be present among friends. It emphasizes the importance of companionship and the divine blessings that come from unity and shared experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, the presence of friends and family enhances the joy of the occasion, much like the Sun brightens the day.
    • In a workplace, collaboration among colleagues can lead to greater success, reflecting the idea of shared prosperity.
    • During festivals, people come together to celebrate, invoking blessings for abundance and happiness, similar to the invocation in the mantra.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of community and friendship in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings in our endeavors, especially when we are united with others.
    • In daily life, one can apply this mantra by fostering relationships and working collaboratively towards common goals, ensuring that the spirit of togetherness is maintained.
