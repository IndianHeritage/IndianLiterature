इन्द्र पिब स्वधया चित् सुतस्याग्नेर वा पाहि जिह्वया यजत्र |अध्वर्योः वा प्रयतं शक्र हस्ताद धोतुर वा यज्ञं हविषो जुषस्व ||
indra piba svadhayā cit sutasyāgner vā pāhi jihvayā yajatra | adhvaryor vā prayataṁ śakra hastād dhotur vā yajñaṁ haviṣo juṣasva ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Piba: Means "to drink," indicating the act of receiving or absorbing something beneficial.
  • Svadhayā: Translates to "by one's own will" or "with one's own effort," emphasizing personal agency and intention.
  • Cit: Means "consciousness" or "awareness," suggesting a state of heightened perception.
  • Sutasyāgner: Refers to the "soma" or "sacred drink" associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing purification and spiritual nourishment.
  • Vā: Means "or," presenting an alternative or choice.
  • Pāhi: Means "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for safety and support.
  • Jihvayā: Refers to the "tongue," symbolizing communication and expression.
  • Yajatra: Refers to the "sacrificial offering," highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Adhvaryor: Refers to the priests who perform the rituals, emphasizing the role of tradition and community in spiritual practices.
  • Prayataṁ: Means "with effort" or "with intention," reinforcing the need for active participation in spiritual endeavors.
  • Śakra: Another name for Indra, emphasizing his power and influence.
  • Hastād: Means "from the hand," indicating direct action or intervention.
  • Dhotur: Refers to the "one who stirs" or "activates," symbolizing the dynamic nature of creation and transformation.
  • Yajñaṁ: Refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals.
  • Haviṣo: Refers to the "offerings" made to the gods, symbolizing gratitude and devotion.
  • Juṣasva: Means "enjoy" or "accept," indicating a desire for divine blessings and favor.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking his protection and blessings through conscious effort and ritual offerings. It emphasizes the importance of personal agency in spiritual practices and the role of community in connecting with the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during significant life events (weddings, births, etc.) where individuals seek divine guidance and support. It serves as a reminder of the power of intention and the importance of rituals in fostering a sense of connection with the divine and the community.
