मां ते हरि वृषणा वीतपृष्ठा नि रीरामन यजमानासो अन्ये |अत्यायाहि शाश्वतो वयं ते 'रं सुतैभिः कृतवाम सोमैः ||
mā te harī vṛṣaṇā vītapṛṣṭhā ni rīraman yajamānāso anye | atyāyāhi śaśvato vayaṁ te 'raṁ sutebhiḥ kṛṇavāma somaiḥ ||
  • mā te harī vṛṣaṇā vītapṛṣṭhā ni rīraman yajamānāso anye
    • mā te - "Do not let your" or "May you not have". This phrase expresses a plea or a wish for something not to happen.
    • harī - "O Lord" or "O God". This term is often used to address divine beings, indicating reverence.
    • vṛṣaṇā - "of the strong" or "of the powerful". This word signifies strength and might.
    • vītapṛṣṭhā - "with a back that is free" or "without burden". It suggests a state of being unencumbered or liberated.
    • ni rīraman - "do not be disturbed" or "do not be troubled". This is a request for peace and stability.
    • yajamānāso - "the worshippers" or "the ones who perform rituals". This refers to those who engage in spiritual practices.
    • anye - "others". This indicates a distinction between the worshippers and others who may not share the same devotion.
  • atyāyāhi śaśvato vayaṁ te 'raṁ sutebhiḥ kṛṇavāma somaiḥ
    • atyāyāhi - "may we attain" or "may we achieve". This is a hopeful expression for reaching a goal.
    • śaśvato - "eternal" or "everlasting". This word conveys the idea of permanence and continuity.
    • vayaṁ - "we" or "us". This indicates a collective identity or community.
    • te - "your". This is a possessive form, indicating belonging or connection to the divine.
    • 'raṁ - "wealth" or "prosperity". This signifies abundance and well-being.
    • sutebhiḥ - "with offerings" or "with sacrifices". This refers to the acts of devotion and giving.
    • kṛṇavāma - "we create" or "we make". This indicates an active role in the process of worship.
    • somaiḥ - "with soma". Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine nourishment and spiritual ecstasy.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a deep spiritual plea for divine protection and the pursuit of eternal prosperity through worship. It emphasizes the importance of being unburdened and peaceful while engaging in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be applied to the idea of seeking balance and tranquility amidst the chaos of daily life. By focusing on our spiritual goals and performing acts of devotion, we can cultivate a sense of community and shared purpose, leading to a more fulfilling existence. The mantra serves as a reminder that through collective efforts and offerings, we can achieve lasting prosperity and connection with the divine.
