द्युर्भिरक्तुभिः परिपातमस्मान् अरिष्टेभिरश्विनासौभागेभिः |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
dyubhir aktubhiḥ pari pātam asmān ariṣṭebhir aśvinā saubhagebhiḥ | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • dyubhir aktubhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "by the powers of the heavens." It signifies the divine forces and energies that govern the universe.
  • pari pātam asmān: This means "may they surround us." It expresses a desire for protection and support from these divine forces.
  • ariṣṭebhir aśvinā: Referring to the "Aśvins," the twin deities associated with health and medicine, this part invokes their blessings to ward off misfortunes.
  • saubhagebhiḥ: This translates to "with auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of good fortune and prosperity in life.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo: "May that (auspiciousness) come to us from Mitra and Varuna." Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and truth.
  • māmahantām: This means "may they be great." It expresses a wish for the blessings of these deities to be abundant and impactful.
  • aditiḥ sindhuḥ: Aditi is the mother of the gods, and "sindhuḥ" refers to the ocean or a river. This part signifies the nurturing and life-giving aspects of nature.
  • pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: "The earth and the sky." This phrase acknowledges the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine protection, auspiciousness, and the interconnectedness of nature. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as during rituals for health and well-being, seeking blessings for prosperity, or even in moments of uncertainty when one seeks guidance and support from higher powers. The invocation of deities like Mitra and Varuna reminds us of the values of friendship, harmony, and truth, which are essential in our daily interactions and relationships. By recognizing the significance of nature and the divine, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and mindfulness in our lives.
