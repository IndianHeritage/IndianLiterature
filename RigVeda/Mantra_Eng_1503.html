ज्योतिष्मत क्षत्रम् आशाते आदित्य दानुनस पतिः |मित्रस तयोः वरुणो यातयज्जनो 'र्यमाऽ यातयज्जनः ||
jyotiṣmat kṣatram āśāte ādityā dānunas patī | mitras tayor varuṇo yātayajjano 'ryamā yātayajjanaḥ ||
  • jyotiṣmat: This word translates to "radiant" or "illuminated." It signifies the presence of light, knowledge, and wisdom. In real life, this can be related to individuals who bring clarity and enlightenment to others, such as teachers or leaders.
  • kṣatram: This term refers to "power" or "authority." It is often associated with the warrior class in Vedic society, representing strength and protection. In modern contexts, this can be seen in leaders or protectors of communities, such as law enforcement or military personnel.
  • āśāte: This word means "to desire" or "to wish for." It reflects the aspirations and hopes of individuals or communities. For example, people often aspire for peace, prosperity, and security in their lives.
  • ādityā: This refers to the "sons of Aditi," often associated with the sun and divine beings. It symbolizes vitality and life-giving energy. In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of the sun in agriculture and its role in sustaining life on Earth.
  • dānunas: This term can be interpreted as "the one who gives" or "the generous." It emphasizes the value of generosity and sharing in society. Real-life examples include charitable organizations and individuals who dedicate their resources to help others.
  • patī: This means "lord" or "master." It signifies authority and leadership. In contemporary society, this can be seen in various leadership roles, from business executives to community leaders.
  • mitras: This word translates to "friends" or "allies." It highlights the importance of relationships and cooperation among individuals. In real life, friendships and alliances are crucial for personal and professional success.
  • tayor: This means "of them" or "of those." It indicates a connection or relationship between the subjects mentioned.
  • varuṇo: This refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. It symbolizes the natural laws and moral order in the universe. In real life, this can be related to the importance of environmental conservation and respecting natural resources.
  • yātayajjano: This term means "the one who performs sacrifices" or "the one who acts with intention." It emphasizes the significance of purposeful actions in life. For example, individuals who volunteer their time for community service embody this principle.
  • 'aryamā: This word can be interpreted as "noble" or "honorable." It reflects the qualities of integrity and virtue. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold ethical standards and contribute positively to society.
  • yātayajjanaḥ: This refers to "the one who is engaged in sacrifice" or "the one who is dedicated." It signifies commitment and dedication to a cause or purpose. For instance, activists who fight for social justice exemplify this dedication.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of light, power, desire, generosity, friendship, and noble actions. It highlights the importance of leadership and cooperation in achieving a harmonious society. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, environmental conservation, and personal development. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more enlightened and just world.
