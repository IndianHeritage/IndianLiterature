अचित्ती यच चक्रिमा दैवये जनै दीनैर दक्षैः प्रभूतī पूरुषत्वता |देवेषु च सवितर मानुषेषु च त्वं नो अत्र सुवताद अनागसः ||
acittī yac cakṛmā daivye jane dīnair dakṣaiḥ prabhūtī pūruṣatvatā | deveṣu ca savitar mānuṣeṣu ca tvaṁ no atra suvatād anāgasaḥ ||
  • acittī: This term can be interpreted as "without consciousness" or "unconscious." It suggests a state of being that is not aware or enlightened.
  • yac: This means "that which" or "whatever." It introduces a clause that refers to the actions or qualities being discussed.
  • cakṛmā: This word translates to "we have done" or "we have created." It indicates the actions taken by individuals or a collective.
  • daivye: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this term refers to qualities or actions that are aligned with higher spiritual principles.
  • jane: This translates to "among people" or "in humanity," emphasizing the social aspect of the mantra.
  • dīnair: This means "by the humble" or "by the needy," highlighting the importance of compassion and support for those who are less fortunate.
  • dakṣaiḥ: This term means "by the skilled" or "by the capable," referring to those who possess the ability to act effectively.
  • prabhūtī: This translates to "prosperity" or "abundance," indicating a state of wealth or well-being.
  • pūruṣatvatā: This term refers to "the essence of humanity" or "human qualities," emphasizing the importance of human values and characteristics.
  • deveṣu: Meaning "among the gods," this term connects the divine with the earthly realm.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking different ideas together.
  • savitar: This refers to "the Sun" or "the divine light," symbolizing enlightenment and energy.
  • mānuṣeṣu: This means "among humans," reinforcing the connection between the divine and human actions.
  • ca: Again, this is a conjunction meaning "and."
  • tvaṁ: This translates to "you," addressing the divine or a higher power directly.
  • no: This means "to us," indicating a plea or request.
  • atra: This translates to "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment.
  • suvaṭād: This term means "goodness" or "well-being," emphasizing positive outcomes.
  • anāgasaḥ: This translates to "without sin" or "innocent," suggesting purity and moral integrity.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine qualities and human actions. It calls for a recognition of the divine in our daily lives and encourages us to act with compassion and skill, particularly towards those who are less fortunate.
  • Real-Life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, where individuals come together to support the needy. It can also be reflected in professional settings where skilled individuals mentor others, fostering a sense of abundance and prosperity for all.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of aligning our actions with higher principles, promoting compassion, skillfulness, and a sense of community. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
