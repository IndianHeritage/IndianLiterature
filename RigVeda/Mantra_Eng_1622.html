आ धेनवो मातेत्यम अवन्तीर ब्रह्मप्रियं पीपयन सस्मिन्न ऊधन |पित्वो भिक्षेत वयुनानि विद्वान् आसाविवासन् अदितिम उरुष्येत ||
ā dhenavo māmateyam avantīr brahmapriyam pīpayan sasminn ūdhan | pitvo bhikṣeta vayunāni vidvān āsāvivāsann aditim uruṣyet ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā dhenavo: "from the cows" - symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • māmateyam: "may it not be taken away" - a plea for protection and preservation.
    • avantīr: "may it be protected" - a call for safeguarding resources.
    • brahmapriyam: "dear to the divine" - indicating the sacredness of what is being requested.
    • pīpayan: "nourishing" - the act of providing sustenance.
    • sasminn: "in that" - referring to the context of the plea.
    • ūdhan: "boiling" - a metaphor for transformation and preparation.
    • pitvo: "having drunk" - signifies consumption and enjoyment.
    • bhikṣeta: "may it be obtained" - a request for acquisition.
    • vayunāni: "the good things" - referring to beneficial outcomes.
    • vidvān: "the wise one" - a reference to knowledge and understanding.
    • āsāvivāsann: "having settled" - indicating stability and permanence.
    • aditim: "the infinite" - representing boundless possibilities.
    • uruṣyet: "may it manifest" - a wish for realization and emergence.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing and protecting resources, particularly those that are sacred and beneficial.
    • It reflects a deep connection to nature, particularly through the symbolism of cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in many cultures.
    • The plea for protection and nourishment can be related to modern-day concerns about environmental sustainability and the preservation of natural resources.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be applied to agricultural practices, where farmers seek to protect their crops and livestock while ensuring they provide for their communities.
    • The invocation of wisdom suggests that knowledge and understanding are crucial in making decisions that affect resources and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the protection and nurturing of resources that are vital for life.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment and to seek wisdom in our actions.
    • In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, ensuring that future generations can also benefit from the earth's resources.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in harmony with nature.
