य इद्ध आ vivāsati सुम्नम् इन्द्रस्य मर्त्यः |द्यम्नाय सुतरा अपः ||
ya iddha āvivāsati sumnam indrasya martyaḥ | dyumnāya sutarā apaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ya iddha āvivāsati sumnam indrasya martyaḥ | dyumnāya sutarā apaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "He who, having been born, is endowed with the auspiciousness of Indra, may he bring us abundant wealth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ya: who
    • iddha: having been born
    • āvivāsati: is endowed or possesses
    • sumnam: auspiciousness or good fortune
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • martyaḥ: mortal or human
    • dyumnāya: for wealth or prosperity
    • sutarā: abundantly or greatly
    • apaḥ: water or resources
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and prosperity in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "having been born" signifies the potential and opportunities that come with life, suggesting that every individual has the capacity to achieve greatness.
    • The term "auspiciousness" reflects the idea that good fortune is not merely a matter of chance but can be cultivated through positive actions and intentions.
    • In real life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of success in various fields, such as education, career, and personal development, where one seeks the blessings of higher powers or the universe for guidance and support.
    • For example, a student preparing for exams may recite this mantra to invoke clarity of mind and success in their endeavors, believing that their hard work, combined with divine blessings, will lead to favorable outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking auspiciousness and prosperity in life, highlighting the belief that divine support can enhance human efforts.
    • It serves as a reminder that while individuals must strive for their goals, they can also seek blessings from higher powers to aid in their journey.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as starting a new business, embarking on a new project, or even during significant life transitions, where the desire for success and abundance is paramount.
