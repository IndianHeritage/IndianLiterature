प्रति स्तोमेभिर उषसं वसिष्ठा गीरभिर विप्रासः प्रथमā अबुध्रन |विवर्तयन्तीं रजसी समन्ते आविष्कृत्वातīm भुवनानि विश्वा ||
prati stomebhir uṣasaṁ vasiṣṭhā gīrbhir viprāsaḥ prathamā abudhran | vivartayantīṁ rajasī samante āviṣkṛṇvatīm bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • Mantra: "prati stomebhir uṣasaṁ vasiṣṭhā gīrbhir viprāsaḥ prathamā abudhran | vivartayantīṁ rajasī samante āviṣkṛṇvatīm bhuvanāni viśvā ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the dawn (Uṣas) as the first to awaken the world with her beautiful sounds and illuminations, bringing forth the various realms of existence.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Prati: Towards or in response to.
    • Stomebhir: With hymns or praises.
    • Uṣasaṁ: The dawn or the light of the morning.
    • Vasiṣṭhā: The best or the most excellent.
    • Gīrbhir: With words or sounds.
    • Viprāsaḥ: The wise or learned ones.
    • Prathamā: The first or foremost.
    • Abudhran: To awaken or to rouse.
    • Vivartayantīṁ: Causing to turn or to manifest.
    • Rajasī: The energy or the dynamic force.
    • Samante: All around or in all directions.
    • Āviṣkṛṇvatīm: Revealing or uncovering.
    • Bhuvanāni: The worlds or realms.
    • Viśvā: All or everything.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The dawn symbolizes new beginnings. Just as the sun rises each day, individuals can start anew, leaving behind past mistakes and embracing fresh opportunities.
    • In a corporate setting, the arrival of a new leader can be likened to the dawn, bringing new ideas and energy that can transform the organization.
    • In personal life, the act of waking up early to witness the sunrise can serve as a reminder of the beauty of life and the potential of each day.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge, wisdom, and the awakening of consciousness. It highlights how the dawn brings clarity and understanding, much like how knowledge illuminates our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the beauty of nature and the cycles of life, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment and wisdom in their daily lives.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be chanted during meditation or morning rituals to invoke positivity and clarity for the day ahead.
