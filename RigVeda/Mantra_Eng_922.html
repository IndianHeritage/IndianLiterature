मन्त्र: कदामर्तम आराधसम पदा क्षुम्पम इव स्फुरत् |कद नः शुश्रवद गीर इन्द्रो अङ्ग ||
kadā martam arādhasam padā kṣumpam iva sphurat | kadā naḥ śuśravad gira indro aṅga ||
  • Mantra: "kadā martam arādhasam padā kṣumpam iva sphurat | kadā naḥ śuśravad gira indro aṅga ||"
  • Translation: "When will the mortal, who is like a shining tree, be praised? When will Indra, the powerful, listen to our words?"
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kadā: When
    • martam: Mortal
    • arādhasam: Praised
    • padā: Like a foot or step
    • kṣumpam: Tree or something that shines
    • sphurat: Shining or illuminating
    • naḥ: Our
    • śuśravad: Listening or hearing
    • gira: Words or speech
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing power and strength
    • aṅga: O, or a term of address
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep yearning for recognition and communication with the divine.
    • The comparison of a mortal to a "shining tree" suggests that even in our transient existence, we possess the potential for greatness and illumination.
    • The plea for Indra to listen to our words signifies the desire for divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the context of individuals seeking validation and acknowledgment in their personal and professional lives.
    • For example, a student may seek recognition from a teacher (akin to Indra) for their hard work and dedication, hoping that their efforts will be acknowledged and rewarded.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for recognition and the need for divine support in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we are mortal and transient, our actions and words can shine brightly, impacting those around us.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek connection with higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or acts of kindness, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches us that our voices matter and that seeking acknowledgment is a natural part of the human experience.
