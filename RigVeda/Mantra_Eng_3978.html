उप स्तुहि प्रथमं रत्नधेम बृहस्पतिं सनितारं धनानाम् |यः शंसते स्तुवते शम्भविष्टः पुरूवसुर आगमज जोहुवानम् ||
upa stuhi prathamaṁ ratnadheyam bṛhaspatiṁ sanitāraṁ dhanānām | yaḥ śaṁsate stuvate śambhaviṣṭhaḥ purūvasur āgamaj johuvānam ||
  • upa stuhi: This phrase can be translated as "I praise" or "I offer my homage." It signifies the act of devotion and respect towards a higher power or deity.
  • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word emphasizes the importance of the subject being addressed, indicating that it holds a primary position in the speaker's reverence.
  • ratnadheyam: This term translates to "the one who possesses wealth" or "the one who is rich in treasures." It refers to a deity or divine figure who is abundant in blessings and prosperity.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ: Bṛhaspati is the name of the deity associated with wisdom, knowledge, and eloquence. He is often regarded as the guru of the gods and symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • sanitāraṁ: This word means "the one who leads" or "the guide." It suggests a figure who provides direction and support, helping individuals navigate through life's challenges.
  • dhanānām: This translates to "of wealth" or "of riches," reinforcing the theme of prosperity and abundance that is central to this mantra.
  • yaḥ: Meaning "who," this word introduces a relative clause, referring to the subject of the praise.
  • śaṁsate: This term means "to praise" or "to speak well of." It indicates the act of acknowledging the virtues and qualities of the deity being addressed.
  • stuvate: Similar to "śaṁsate," this word also means "to praise" or "to worship," emphasizing the importance of devotion in the spiritual practice.
  • śambhaviṣṭhaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is auspicious" or "the one who brings good fortune." It highlights the positive attributes of the deity and the blessings they bestow upon their devotees.
  • purūvasur: This word refers to a divine figure or a celestial being, often associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • āgamaj: Meaning "born of the sacred texts" or "derived from the scriptures," this term emphasizes the divine authority and wisdom that comes from ancient teachings.
  • johuvānam: This word means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to the divine for guidance and support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of devotion, knowledge, and the pursuit of wealth in a holistic sense—wealth not just in material terms but also in wisdom and guidance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during educational pursuits, business ventures, or any endeavor where guidance and prosperity are sought. By invoking the qualities of Bṛhaspati, individuals can aspire to achieve success and abundance in their lives, while also fostering a sense of gratitude and respect for the knowledge and resources they receive. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, wealth, and spiritual growth, encouraging individuals to seek balance in their pursuits.
