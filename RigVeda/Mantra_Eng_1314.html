शतम मेषान वृक्ये मामहाणं तमः प्रणीतम अशिवेन पितृा |आक्षी ऋज्राश्वे अश्विनाव अधत्तं ज्योतिर अन्धाय चक्रथुर विचक्षे ||
śatam meṣān vṛkye māmahānaṁ tamaḥ praṇītam aśivena pitrā | ākṣī ṛjrāśve aśvināv adhattaṁ jyotir andhāya cakrathur vicakṣe ||
  • śatam meṣān vṛkye: This phrase translates to "a hundred sheep in the pasture." Here, "śatam" means a hundred, "meṣān" refers to sheep, and "vṛkye" means in the pasture. This can symbolize abundance and prosperity in life, akin to having a wealth of resources or opportunities.
  • māmahānaṁ tamaḥ praṇītam: This means "the great darkness that has been brought forth." "Māmahānaṁ" refers to something vast or great, while "tamaḥ" means darkness. This can represent ignorance or challenges that one faces in life, which need to be overcome.
  • aśivena pitrā: This translates to "by the auspicious father." "Aśivena" means auspicious or beneficial, and "pitrā" refers to the father or ancestors. This can signify the guidance and blessings received from one's forebears, emphasizing the importance of lineage and heritage in overcoming life's challenges.
  • ākṣī ṛjrāśve: This phrase means "with the eyes of the Aśvins." The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. "Ākṣī" refers to eyes, and "ṛjrāśve" can imply clarity or vision. This suggests the need for insight and clarity in navigating through life's complexities.
  • aśvināv adhattaṁ: This translates to "the Aśvins have placed." This indicates the action of the Aśvins in providing support or assistance, reinforcing the idea of divine help in times of need.
  • jyotir andhāya: This means "light for the blind." "Jyotir" refers to light, and "andhāya" means for the blind. This symbolizes the quest for knowledge and enlightenment, particularly for those who are lost or in darkness.
  • cakrathur vicakṣe: This translates to "they who have the ability to see." "Cakrathur" refers to those who can perceive or understand, and "vicakṣe" means wise or discerning. This emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in making decisions and understanding the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey from ignorance to enlightenment, highlighting the importance of guidance from ancestors and divine beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking wisdom in difficult situations, relying on the support of family and heritage, and striving for clarity in decision-making. It serves as a reminder that while challenges (darkness) may arise, with the right guidance and insight, one can navigate through them towards prosperity and understanding. This mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and support, fostering a sense of community and connection to one's roots.
