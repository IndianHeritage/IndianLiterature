यā ते भीमान्य आयुधा तिग्मानि सन्ति धूर्वणे |रक्षा समस्य नो निदः ||
yā te bhīmāny āyudhā tigmāni santi dhūrvaṇe | rakṣā samasya no nidaḥ ||
  • yā te bhīmāny āyudhā: This phrase translates to "those fierce weapons of yours." Here, "yā" refers to "those," "te" means "your," "bhīmāny" translates to "fierce" or "terrible," and "āyudhā" means "weapons." In a real-life context, this can be understood as the challenges or adversities we face that can be overwhelming or daunting.
  • tigmāni santi dhūrvaṇe: This part means "that are sharp and powerful." "Tigmāni" refers to "sharp" or "piercing," "santi" means "are," and "dhūrvaṇe" can be interpreted as "in the midst of difficulties." This can relate to the sharpness of our intellect or the strength of our resolve when faced with life's challenges.
  • rakṣā samasya no nidaḥ: This translates to "protect us from all troubles." "Rakṣā" means "protection," "samasya" refers to "troubles" or "problems," and "nidaḥ" means "may you grant." This is a plea for divine protection against the adversities and challenges that life presents.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for strength and protection in the face of life's challenges. It acknowledges the fierce adversities we encounter and seeks divine assistance to navigate through them. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as during personal crises, professional challenges, or even in times of societal upheaval. By invoking this mantra, individuals can find solace and strength, reminding themselves that they are not alone in their struggles and that there is a higher power that can provide support and guidance. This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate resilience and seek help when needed, reinforcing the importance of community and spiritual support in overcoming life's difficulties.
