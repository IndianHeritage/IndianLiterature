तां नो वाजवतीर इषा आशून पिपृतम अरवतः | इन्द्रं अग्निं च वोळ्हवे ||
tā no vājavatīr iṣa āśūn pipṛtam arvataḥ | indram agniṁ ca voḻhave ||
  • tā no - This phrase translates to "may they" or "let them" and signifies a request or invocation.
  • vājavatīr - This word means "powerful" or "strong," often associated with the idea of strength and vigor.
  • iṣa - This term refers to "desire" or "wish," indicating a longing for something beneficial.
  • āśūn - This word means "swift" or "quick," suggesting the urgency or immediacy of the desires being invoked.
  • pipṛtam - This translates to "filled" or "satisfied," indicating a state of abundance or fulfillment.
  • arvataḥ - This term means "of the strong" or "of the powerful," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • indram - Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, authority, and protection.
  • agniṁ - This word means "fire," which is a sacred element in Vedic rituals, representing purity, transformation, and energy.
  • ca - This simply means "and," connecting the elements of the mantra.
  • voḻhave - This translates to "may we receive" or "may we attain," indicating a desire for blessings or gifts from the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for strength, fulfillment, and divine blessings. It expresses a desire for powerful and swift assistance from the divine forces, particularly from Indra and Agni, who are seen as protectors and sources of energy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges, individuals can recite this mantra to invoke strength and quick resolution to their problems.
  • Teamwork: In a group setting, such as a project team, this mantra can be used to foster a sense of unity and collective strength, encouraging members to support each other in achieving common goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: During rituals or meditation, this mantra can be chanted to seek divine guidance and blessings, enhancing the spiritual experience.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and fulfillment from divine sources. It serves as a reminder that in times of need, one can call upon higher powers for support and guidance, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
