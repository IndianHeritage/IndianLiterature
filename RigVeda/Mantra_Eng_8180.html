वृष्णस ते वृष्ण्यं शवो वृषा वनं वृषा मदः |सत्यं वृषन् वृषेद असि ||
vṛṣṇas te vṛṣṇyaṁ śavo vṛṣā vanaṁ vṛṣā madaḥ | satyaṁ vṛṣan vṛṣed asi ||
  • vṛṣṇas te: This phrase can be interpreted as "your strength" or "your power." In real life, this can relate to the inner strength that individuals possess, which helps them overcome challenges. For example, a person facing a difficult situation at work may draw upon their inner strength to persevere and succeed.
  • vṛṣṇyaṁ: This word signifies "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It can be associated with leaders or role models in society who inspire others through their strength and resilience. For instance, a community leader who advocates for social justice embodies this strength and inspires others to take action.
  • śavo: This term translates to "the dead" or "the lifeless." It can symbolize the challenges or obstacles that one faces in life, which may seem overwhelming or insurmountable. For example, a person dealing with grief may feel as though they are surrounded by lifelessness, yet they must find a way to move forward.
  • vṛṣā vanaṁ: This phrase can be understood as "the forest of strength." It represents a place or state where one can find support and resources to grow stronger. In real life, this could be a supportive community or a group of friends who uplift each other during tough times.
  • vṛṣā madaḥ: This translates to "the intoxication of strength." It refers to the exhilaration and empowerment that comes from recognizing one's own strength. For example, an athlete who achieves a personal best may feel a rush of confidence and motivation to continue pushing their limits.
  • satyaṁ: This word means "truth." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where individuals choose to act ethically, even when faced with difficult choices, such as a whistleblower exposing corruption.
  • vṛṣan: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It reinforces the idea of strength and resilience, encouraging individuals to embrace their capabilities. For instance, a student who works hard to achieve academic success exemplifies this strength.
  • vṛṣed asi: This phrase translates to "you are strong." It serves as a reminder of one's inherent strength and potential. In real life, this can be a motivational affirmation that individuals use to boost their confidence, such as a mantra repeated before a challenging task.

Conclusion: The mantra "vṛṣṇas te vṛṣṇyaṁ śavo vṛṣā vanaṁ vṛṣā madaḥ | satyaṁ vṛṣan vṛṣed asi ||" encapsulates the essence of inner strength, resilience, and the pursuit of truth. It serves as a powerful reminder that individuals possess the strength to overcome obstacles and challenges in their lives. By recognizing and embracing this strength, one can navigate through difficulties, inspire others, and act with integrity. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to leadership roles, encouraging individuals to harness their inner power and strive for truth and strength in their actions.
