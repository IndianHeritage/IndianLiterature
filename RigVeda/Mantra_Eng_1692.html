ये वाजिनं परिपश्यन्ति पक्वं य इम आहुः सुरभिर निर हरेति |ये चार्वतो मांसभिक्षां उपासते उतो तेषां अभिगूर्तिर न इन्बतु ||
ye vājinam paripaśyanti pakvaṁ ya īm āhuḥ surabhir nir hareti | ye cārvato māṁsabhikṣām upāsata uto teṣām abhigūrtir na invatu ||
  • Translation: "Those who see the well-cooked food, who are called 'surabhi' (fragrant), and who do not take away the essence; those who worship the food that is rich in meat, may their abundance not be diminished."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • vājinam: Well-cooked food
    • paripaśyanti: See or perceive
    • pakvaṁ: Cooked or prepared
    • ya: Who
    • īm: Essence or essence of life
    • āhuḥ: Are called
    • surabhir: Fragrant or pleasing
    • nir hareti: Do not take away
    • cārvato: Rich in meat
    • māṁsabhikṣām: Food that is meat-based
    • upāsata: Worship or honor
    • uto: May
    • teṣām: Their
    • abhigūrtir: Abundance or prosperity
    • na: Not
    • invatu: Be diminished
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and appreciating the nourishment provided by well-prepared food.
    • The term "surabhi" signifies not just the physical attributes of food but also its spiritual essence, suggesting that food should be treated with respect and gratitude.
    • The reference to "māṁsabhikṣām" indicates a recognition of diverse dietary practices, acknowledging that different cultures may have varying views on what constitutes nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, meals are a time for family gatherings, where the act of cooking and sharing food is a way to honor relationships and traditions.
    • Restaurants that focus on farm-to-table practices exemplify the idea of appreciating well-cooked food, as they emphasize quality ingredients and sustainable practices.
    • In spiritual practices, such as yoga or meditation, the preparation and consumption of food are often approached with mindfulness, recognizing the energy and intention behind the meal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of food in our lives, not just as sustenance but as a source of joy, community, and spiritual nourishment.
    • It encourages us to honor the food we consume, recognizing its preparation and the effort that goes into it, which can lead to a more mindful and appreciative approach to eating.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the interconnectedness of life, where the act of eating becomes a celebration of abundance and gratitude.
