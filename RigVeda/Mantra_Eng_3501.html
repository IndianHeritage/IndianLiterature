इन्द्रा ह यो वरुणा चक्र आपी देवौ मर्तः सख्याय प्रयस्वान् |स हन्ति वृत्रा समिथेषु शत्रून अवोभिर वा महद्भिः स प्र श्रिण्वे ||
indrā ha yo varuṇā cakra āpī devau martaḥ sakhyāya prayasvān | sa hanti vṛtrā samitheṣu śatrūn avobhir vā mahadbhiḥ sa pra śṛṇve ||
  • Indrā ha yo varuṇā cakra āpī devau martaḥ sakhyāya prayasvān:
    • Indrā: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Varuṇā: Represents Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, embodying the principles of truth and moral authority.
    • Cakra: Means 'wheel' or 'circle', often symbolizing the cycle of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Āpī: Translates to 'approaching' or 'coming near', indicating a call to divine presence.
    • Devau: Refers to the divine beings or gods, emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
    • Martaḥ: Means 'mortal' or 'human', highlighting the human condition and our limitations.
    • Sakhyāya: Translates to 'friendship' or 'alliance', suggesting the importance of unity and cooperation.
    • Prayasvān: Means 'effortful' or 'energetic', indicating the need for active participation in life.
  • Sa hanti vṛtrā samitheṣu śatrūn:
    • Sa: Means 'he' or 'that one', referring to the divine or powerful entity.
    • Hanti: Translates to 'destroys' or 'defeats', indicating the overcoming of obstacles.
    • Vṛtrā: Refers to Vritra, a dragon or serpent symbolizing chaos and obstacles in life.
    • Samitheṣu: Means 'in the assemblies' or 'in gatherings', emphasizing the importance of community.
    • Śatrūn: Means 'enemies', representing challenges or adversities faced in life.
  • Avobhir vā mahadbhiḥ sa pra śṛṇve:
    • Avobhir: Means 'with great strength' or 'with great power', indicating the might of the divine.
    • Vā: Translates to 'or', suggesting alternatives or choices in life.
    • Mahadbhiḥ: Means 'with the great ones', referring to powerful allies or forces.
    • Sa: Again refers to 'he' or 'that one', reinforcing the connection to the divine.
    • Pra: Means 'forth' or 'out', indicating the manifestation of power.
    • Śṛṇve: Means 'hears' or 'listens', emphasizing the importance of being receptive to divine guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of unity, effort, and the power of the divine in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during crises, or personal struggles where one seeks strength and guidance. The mantra serves as a reminder that through collective effort and divine support, we can overcome obstacles and achieve our goals.
