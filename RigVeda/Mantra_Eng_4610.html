मन्त्र: य एक इद धव्यश चर्षणीनाम इन्द्रं तं गीरभिर अभ्यर्च आभिः |यः पत्यते वृषभो वृष्ण्यावान सत्यः सत्वा पुरुमायः सहस्वान् ||
ya eka id dhavyaś carṣaṇīnām indraṁ taṁ gīrbhir abhy arca ābhiḥ | yaḥ patyate vṛṣabho vṛṣṇyāvān satyaḥ satvā purumāyaḥ sahasvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • eka: one
    • id: indeed
    • dhavyaś: of the divine
    • carṣaṇīnām: of the seers or sages
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • taṁ: him
    • gīrbhir: with hymns
    • abhy: with
    • arca: worship
    • ābhiḥ: with offerings
    • yaḥ: who
    • patyate: is worshipped
    • vṛṣabho: the bull, symbolizing strength
    • vṛṣṇyāvān: the one who belongs to the Vrishni clan
    • satyaḥ: the truth
    • satvā: the essence or being
    • purumāyaḥ: the supreme being
    • sahasvān: the powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the worship of Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "ya eka id dhavyaś carṣaṇīnām indraṁ" emphasizes the singularity and uniqueness of Indra as the divine force that is worshipped by the sages.
    • The use of "gīrbhir abhy arca ābhiḥ" indicates that this worship is performed through hymns and offerings, highlighting the importance of devotion and ritual in connecting with the divine.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "yaḥ patyate vṛṣabho vṛṣṇyāvān satyaḥ satvā purumāyaḥ sahasvān," describes Indra as the embodiment of truth and strength, reinforcing his role as a protector and provider.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the essence of this mantra can be seen in the way people seek strength and guidance through various forms of worship, whether in temples, through meditation, or in personal prayers.
    • For instance, athletes often invoke a sense of divine strength and support before competitions, paralleling the worship of Indra for power and success.
    • Additionally, leaders and decision-makers may draw inspiration from the qualities of truth and strength embodied by Indra, striving to lead with integrity and courage.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that guide and empower us in our lives.
    • It teaches us that through devotion, truth, and strength, we can connect with higher powers and draw upon their blessings to navigate challenges.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to embody the qualities of strength, truth, and integrity in their daily lives, fostering a sense of purpose and connection to the divine.
