यश्चिकेत स सुक्रतुर देवत्रा स ब्रवीतु नः |वरुणो यस्य दर्शतो मित्रो वा वनते गिरः ||
yaś ciketa sa sukratur devatrā sa bravītu naḥ | varuṇo yasya darśato mitro vā vanate giraḥ ||
  • yaś ciketa: This phrase translates to "whoever knows" or "he who understands." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in life.
  • sa sukratur: This means "he who has good intentions" or "the one with righteous actions." It highlights the significance of performing good deeds and having a virtuous character.
  • devatrā: This term refers to "the divine" or "the gods." It signifies the connection between human actions and divine observation or influence.
  • sa bravītu naḥ: This translates to "may he speak to us" or "may he guide us." It expresses a desire for guidance and wisdom from a higher power.
  • varuṇo: Varuna is a deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth. This word invokes the idea of divine authority and moral order in the universe.
  • yasya darśato: This means "whose sight" or "who is seen." It suggests that the divine presence is always watching and aware of human actions.
  • mitro vā: This translates to "or the friend." It indicates the importance of friendship and companionship in life, suggesting that divine guidance can come through relationships.
  • vanate giraḥ: This means "speaks with words" or "utters speech." It emphasizes the power of communication and the importance of expressing thoughts and intentions clearly.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of knowledge, good intentions, divine guidance, and the importance of communication. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, act righteously, and maintain a connection with the divine and our community. It encourages individuals to be aware of their actions and their impact on the world around them, fostering a sense of responsibility and moral integrity. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
