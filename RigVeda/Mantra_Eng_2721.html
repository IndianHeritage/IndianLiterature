विदद यदī सरमा रुग्णम् अद्रेर महि पाथः पूर्व्यं सध्र्यक कः | अग्रं नयत सुपद्य अक्षराणाम् अच्चा रवम् प्रथमā जानती गात् ||
vidad yadī saramā rugṇam adrer mahi pāthaḥ pūrvyaṁ sadhryak kaḥ | agraṁ nayat supady akṣarāṇām acchā ravam prathamā jānatī gāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidad: to give or to bestow
    • yadī: if
    • saramā: the one who brings forth or leads
    • rugṇam: the one who is afflicted or troubled
    • adreḥ: of the mountains or high places
    • mahi: great or vast
    • pāthaḥ: path or way
    • pūrvyaṁ: ancient or prior
    • sadhryak: with good fortune or auspiciousness
    • kaḥ: who
    • agraṁ: the foremost or the best
    • nayat: leads or guides
    • supady: good or auspicious path
    • akṣarāṇām: of the imperishable or eternal
    • acchā: good or excellent
    • ravam: sound or noise
    • prathamā: the first or foremost
    • jānatī: knows or understands
    • gāt: song or hymn
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of giving and leading those who are troubled towards a great and auspicious path.
    • It emphasizes the importance of guidance and support in overcoming difficulties, akin to how a mentor or a leader helps individuals navigate through challenges in life.
    • The reference to "mountains" can symbolize the high aspirations or challenges one faces, while the "great path" signifies the journey towards achieving those aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a manager who supports their team during tough projects embodies the essence of this mantra by leading them towards success.
    • In education, a teacher who guides students through difficult subjects helps them find their way to knowledge and understanding.
    • In personal life, friends who stand by each other during tough times exemplify the spirit of this mantra, providing emotional support and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion and leadership in times of trouble.
    • It teaches us the value of being a guiding light for others, encouraging us to help those who are struggling to find their path.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, reminding us of our responsibility to uplift and support one another.
