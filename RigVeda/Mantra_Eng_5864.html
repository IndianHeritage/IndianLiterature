प्र सोतां जीरो अध्वरेषु अस्तात सोमं इन्द्राय वायवे पिबध्यै |प्र यद वां मध्वो अग्रियम भरन्त्य अध्वर्यवो देवयन्तः शचीभिः ||
pra sotā jīro adhvareṣv asthāt somam indrāya vāyave pibadhyai | pra yad vām madhvo agriyam bharanty adhvaryavo devayantaḥ śacībhiḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the one who is the source of life, who stands in the rituals, offer the soma to Indra and Vayu. As the priests, endowed with strength, bring forth the best of the sweet offerings to the divine."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pra sotā: "the source of life" or "the one who brings forth."
    • jīro: "life" or "vitality."
    • adhvareṣv: "in the rituals" or "during the sacrifices."
    • asthāt: "stands" or "is present."
    • somam: "soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • indrāya: "to Indra," the king of gods, associated with strength and rain.
    • vāyave: "to Vayu," the god of wind and life force.
    • pibadhyai: "may they drink" or "may they consume."
    • pra yad: "as you bring forth."
    • vām: "your" or "of you."
    • madhvo: "sweetness" or "delight."
    • agriyam: "the best" or "the foremost."
    • bharanty: "they carry" or "they bring."
    • adhvaryavo: "the priests" or "the ritual performers."
    • devayantaḥ: "to the divine" or "to the gods."
    • śacībhiḥ: "with strength" or "with power."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals and ceremonies, the act of offering food or drink to deities can be seen as a way to seek blessings and express gratitude, similar to how the priests in the mantra offer soma to Indra and Vayu.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of collaboration and strength among team members, akin to the priests bringing forth the best offerings, to achieve collective goals.
    • During festivals, communities come together to celebrate and share sweet dishes, symbolizing the sweetness of life and the importance of unity, reflecting the essence of "madhvo" in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine energies and the importance of rituals in connecting with the spiritual realm. It emphasizes the role of community and collaboration in achieving common goals, whether in spiritual practices or everyday life. By recognizing the divine in our actions and offerings, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and unity, making this mantra applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to communal harmony.
