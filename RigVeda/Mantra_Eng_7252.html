यो राजा चर्षणीनां यात रथेभिर अध्रिगुः |विश्वासां तरुतां पृतनानां ज्येष्ठो यो वृत्रहा गृणे ||
yo rājā carṣaṇīnāṁ yātā rathebhir adhriguḥ | viśvāsāṁ tarutā pṛtanānāṁ jyeṣṭho yo vṛtrahā gṛṇe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • rājā: king
    • carṣaṇīnāṁ: of the seers or sages
    • yātā: who has gone
    • rathebhir: with chariots
    • adhriguḥ: unshaken or steadfast
    • viśvāsāṁ: of all beings
    • tarutā: of the trees or plants
    • pṛtanānāṁ: of the strong or powerful
    • jyeṣṭho: the eldest or the greatest
    • yo: who
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • gṛṇe: I sing or I praise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful king who is steadfast and has the ability to lead with wisdom, akin to a sage.
    • The reference to "who has gone with chariots" suggests a leader who is not only mobile but also capable of taking action.
    • The phrase "of all beings" indicates a universal connection, emphasizing the king's role in the welfare of all.
    • The mention of "the slayer of Vritra" symbolizes the triumph over chaos and obstacles, representing the king's strength and capability to protect his realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders who embody the qualities of wisdom, strength, and steadfastness are often revered. For instance, leaders who navigate crises with a calm demeanor and strategic foresight can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In the corporate world, a CEO who leads a company through challenging times, ensuring the welfare of employees and stakeholders, reflects the qualities of the "king" in this mantra.
    • Environmental leaders who advocate for sustainable practices and protect natural resources can be likened to the "slayer of Vritra," overcoming the chaos of environmental degradation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wise and steadfast leadership in all aspects of life, whether in governance, business, or community leadership.
    • It serves as a reminder that true leadership is not just about power but also about responsibility towards all beings and the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, embodying the qualities of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous society, where leaders are seen as protectors and nurturers.
