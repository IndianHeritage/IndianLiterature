वि ते वज्रासो अस्तिरन् नवतिं नाव्याः अनु |महत त इन्द्र वीर्यम बाह्वोस ते बलं हितम् अर्चन् अनु स्वराज्यम ||
vi te vajrāso asthiran navatiṁ nāvyā3 anu | mahat ta indra vīryam bāhvos te balaṁ hitam arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • vi te vajrāso asthiran: This phrase can be translated as "Your (Indra's) strength is unshakeable like a thunderbolt." Here, "vajra" symbolizes strength and power, often associated with the deity Indra, who is known for his might and ability to overcome challenges.
  • navatiṁ nāvyā3 anu: This part means "following the ninety." The number ninety can represent completeness or a significant milestone, suggesting that one should strive for a high level of achievement or success.
  • mahat ta indra vīryam: This translates to "Great is your (Indra's) valor." It emphasizes the importance of courage and strength in overcoming obstacles, much like how a leader must exhibit bravery in times of crisis.
  • bāhvos te balaṁ hitam: This means "Your strength is beneficial." It highlights the idea that true strength should not only be powerful but also constructive and helpful to others, akin to how a strong community leader uses their influence for the greater good.
  • arcann anu svarājyam: This translates to "Worship leads to sovereignty." It suggests that devotion and respect towards higher powers or principles can lead to personal and communal success, similar to how dedication in one's work can lead to career advancement.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, valor, and the importance of using one's power for the benefit of others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in leadership roles, where individuals are called to exhibit courage and strength while ensuring that their actions positively impact their community. It serves as a reminder that true leadership is not just about personal power but also about uplifting others and fostering a sense of unity and purpose. By embodying these principles, one can achieve not only personal success but also contribute to the greater good of society.
