आयं वाम अद्रिभिः सुतः सोमो नराः वृषण्वसुः |आ यातं सोमपीतये पिबतं दाशुषो गृहे ||
ayaṁ vām adribhiḥ sutaḥ somo narā vṛṣaṇvasū | ā yātaṁ somapītaye pibataṁ dāśuṣo gṛhe ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies presence and immediacy.
  • vām - Meaning "your," it indicates a connection or relationship with the audience or the divine.
  • adribhiḥ - This means "from the mountains." It symbolizes strength, stability, and the source of nourishment.
  • sutaḥ - Translated as "born" or "produced," it refers to something that has emerged or been created.
  • somo - This word refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • narā - Meaning "men" or "humans," it emphasizes the human aspect of the mantra.
  • vṛṣaṇvasū - This can be interpreted as "the one who brings prosperity" or "the one who is strong." It denotes strength and abundance.
  • ā yātaṁ - This phrase means "may it come" or "let it arrive," expressing a desire for the arrival of something beneficial.
  • somapītaye - This translates to "for the drinking of Soma," indicating the purpose of the invocation, which is to partake in the divine essence.
  • pibataṁ - Meaning "drink," it is a call to action, urging the audience to partake in the offering.
  • dāśuṣo - This word means "of the generous" or "of the bountiful," highlighting the qualities of abundance and generosity.
  • gṛhe - Translated as "in the home," it signifies the importance of the domestic space and the sanctity of the household.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, invoking the essence of Soma for prosperity and abundance. It highlights the importance of strength and nourishment derived from nature, particularly from the mountains. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as rituals for prosperity, celebrations of abundance, or even in personal reflections on gratitude for the resources we have. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the gifts of nature and the importance of community and home in fostering a prosperous life.
