यद इन्द्र प्राग अपाग उदङ न्यग हूयसे नृभिः |सिमā पुरū नृषūतो अस्य आनवे 'सि प्रशर्ध तुर्वशे ||
yad indra prāg apāg udaṅ nyag vā hūyase nṛbhiḥ | simā purū nṛṣūto asy ānave 'si praśardha turvaśe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • prāg: before
    • apāg: not taken away
    • udaṅ: upward
    • nyag: downward
    • vā: or
    • hūyase: you are invoked
    • nṛbhiḥ: by men
    • simā: boundary
    • purū: many
    • nṛṣūto: of men
    • asy: of this
    • ānave: in the unbounded
    • 'si: you are
    • praśardha: powerful
    • turvaśe: to the one who is strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, emphasizing the importance of his blessings in human endeavors.
    • The phrase "whatever Indra you are invoked by men" suggests that humans seek divine assistance in their actions and decisions.
    • The mention of boundaries ("simā") indicates the limits within which human actions take place, and the need for divine guidance to navigate these boundaries effectively.
    • The term "purū nṛṣūto" highlights the collective nature of humanity, suggesting that the strength of many is sought in the presence of the divine.
    • The phrase "in the unbounded" signifies the limitless potential that can be achieved with divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to Indra for rain, recognizing that their success depends on favorable weather conditions.
    • In community gatherings, people may invoke divine blessings for unity and strength, reflecting the collective effort needed to achieve common goals.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may seek blessings from higher powers to ensure success and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in all human endeavors.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of humanity and the need for collective strength in facing challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals or communities seek to align their efforts with a higher purpose, ensuring that their actions are fruitful and harmonious.
