अस्मे श्रेष्ठेभिर भानुभिर वि भाह्य उषो देवी प्रतिरन्ती न आयुः |इषं च नो दधती विश्ववारे गोमद अश्वावद रथवच च राधः ||
asme śreṣṭhebhir bhānubhir vi bhāhy uṣo devi pratirantī na āyuḥ | iṣaṁ ca no dadhatī viśvavāre gomad aśvāvad rathavac ca rādhaḥ ||
  • asme: This word translates to "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • śreṣṭhebhir: Meaning "by the best" or "by the most excellent," it suggests a desire for the highest quality or the best outcomes.
  • bhānubhir: This term refers to "the rays of the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • vi bhāhy: Translates to "may we be protected," indicating a plea for safety and security.
  • uṣo: Refers to "the dawn," representing new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • devi: This word means "goddess," invoking divine feminine energy and protection.
  • pratirantī: Meaning "may she come forth," it expresses a desire for the arrival of blessings or auspiciousness.
  • na āyuḥ: Translates to "not our lifespan," suggesting a focus beyond mere longevity to a more meaningful existence.
  • iṣaṁ: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a wish for abundance.
  • ca no dadhatī: Translates to "may she bestow upon us," expressing a request for divine generosity.
  • viśvavāre: Meaning "in all directions," it signifies a universal appeal for blessings.
  • gomad: Refers to "cattle," symbolizing wealth and sustenance in ancient agrarian societies.
  • aśvāvad: Meaning "like horses," it represents strength, speed, and vitality.
  • rathavac: Translates to "like chariots," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • ca rādhaḥ: This phrase means "and may we be fulfilled," indicating a desire for completion and satisfaction in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound appeal for divine blessings, prosperity, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of seeking the best in life, not just in terms of material wealth but also in spiritual and emotional fulfillment. The invocation of the dawn symbolizes hope and new beginnings, while the references to cattle, horses, and chariots highlight the significance of strength, sustenance, and progress.
In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for new ventures, prayers for safety and prosperity, or even in personal reflections on growth and fulfillment. It serves as a reminder to seek not just longevity but a life rich in experiences, knowledge, and divine blessings, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence in all aspects of life.
