सुगस ते अग्नि सानवित्तो अध्वा युक्ष्वा सुते हरितो रोहितश्च |ये वा सद्मन्न अरुषा वीरवाहो हुवे देवानां जनिमानि सत्तः ||
sugas te agne sanavitto adhvā yukṣvā sute harito rohitaś ca | ye vā sadmann aruṣā vīravāho huve devānāṁ janimāni sattaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sugas: auspicious, good
    • te: your
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • sanavitto: well-known, revered
    • adhvā: path, journey
    • yukṣvā: may you be united, connected
    • sute: in the offerings
    • harito: green, flourishing
    • rohitaś: red, vibrant
    • ca: and
    • ye: those who
    • vā: or
    • sadmann: in the good place, in the right context
    • aruṣā: red, bright
    • vīravāho: bearers of valor
    • huve: I invoke, I call upon
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • janimāni: births, origins
    • sattaḥ: of existence, reality
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine connection between the earthly and the spiritual.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, representing the energy that fuels life and sustains the universe.
    • The phrase "sugas te agne" emphasizes the auspicious nature of Agni, suggesting that the fire god brings good fortune and blessings.
    • By calling upon Agni to be united with the offerings ("yukṣvā sute"), the mantra highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • The mention of "vīravāho" refers to the valorous beings, indicating that the invocation is not just for personal gain but also for the strength and courage of the community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied during significant life events such as weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or any auspicious beginnings where fire is used in rituals to invoke blessings.
    • In a corporate setting, the essence of this mantra can be reflected in team-building exercises where the focus is on unity, strength, and collective success, akin to invoking the blessings of Agni for a prosperous journey.
    • Environmental initiatives can also draw inspiration from the "harito" aspect, promoting green and sustainable practices that honor the earth and its resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of rituals, and the invocation of divine energies for personal and communal growth.
    • Its significance lies in fostering a sense of unity, courage, and auspiciousness in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals and communities to seek blessings for their endeavors.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a life that honors tradition while embracing modernity, ensuring a harmonious existence.
