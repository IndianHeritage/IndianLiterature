गिरा य एता युनजद धरी त इन्द्र प्रियā सुरथा शूर धायू |प्र यो मन्युः रिरिक्षतो मिनात्य ā सुक्रतुम् आर्यमणं ववृत्याम् ||
girā ya etā yunajad dharī ta indra priyā surathā śūra dhāyū | pra yo manyuṁ ririkṣato mināty ā sukratum aryamaṇaṁ vavṛtyām ||
  • girā: This word translates to "speech" or "voice." It signifies the power of communication and expression.
  • ya: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who" or "that," connecting the subject to the action.
  • etā: This means "these" or "this," referring to the actions or qualities being discussed.
  • yunajad: This term means "to unite" or "to bring together," indicating a sense of harmony and collaboration.
  • dharī: This translates to "bearer" or "holder," often referring to someone who carries responsibilities or virtues.
  • ta: This is a demonstrative pronoun meaning "that," emphasizing the qualities or actions being highlighted.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • priyā: This means "beloved" or "dear," indicating affection and value in relationships.
  • surathā: This term refers to "good fortune" or "prosperity," suggesting a positive outcome or success.
  • śūra: This translates to "brave" or "heroic," highlighting qualities of courage and valor.
  • dhāyū: This means "one who is steadfast" or "enduring," indicating resilience and determination.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," suggesting movement or progression.
  • yo: Another relative pronoun meaning "who," connecting the subject to the action.
  • manyuṁ: This translates to "anger" or "wrath," indicating strong emotions that can drive actions.
  • rikṣato: This means "to observe" or "to watch," suggesting vigilance and awareness.
  • mināty: This term means "to overcome" or "to surpass," indicating triumph over challenges.
  • ā: This preposition means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • sukratum: This translates to "good intention" or "noble purpose," emphasizing the importance of positive motivations.
  • aryamaṇaṁ: This term refers to "the noble one" or "the virtuous," indicating high moral standards.
  • vavṛtyām: This means "in the midst of" or "in the presence of," suggesting a context or environment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and noble intentions in our actions and relationships. It encourages individuals to communicate effectively, work together harmoniously, and uphold virtues such as bravery and resilience. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, fostering strong family bonds, or engaging in community service. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute to a more prosperous and harmonious society.
