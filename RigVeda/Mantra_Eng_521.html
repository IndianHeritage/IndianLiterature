श्रेष्टं यविष्टम अतिथिं स्वाहुतं जुष्टं जनाय दाशुषे |देवां अच्छा यातवे जातवेदसम अग्निम ईळे व्युष्टिषु ||
śreṣṭhaṁ yaviṣṭham atithiṁ svāhutaṁ juṣṭaṁ janāya dāśuṣe | devām̐ acchā yātave jātavedasam agnim īḻe vyuṣṭiṣu ||
  • śreṣṭhaṁ: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the utmost importance.
  • yaviṣṭham: Meaning "the one who is most worthy" or "the one who is to be honored." This emphasizes the value of recognizing and respecting those who deserve it.
  • atithiṁ: This refers to "the guest" or "the visitor." In many cultures, guests are treated with great respect and hospitality, highlighting the importance of welcoming others.
  • svāhutaṁ: This word means "offered" or "sacrificed." It indicates the act of giving or dedicating something valuable, often in a spiritual context.
  • juṣṭaṁ: This translates to "enjoyed" or "delighted in." It suggests that the offerings made are not just rituals but are meant to bring joy and satisfaction.
  • janāya: Meaning "for the people" or "for humanity." This emphasizes the collective benefit of the actions taken.
  • dāśuṣe: This word means "to the giver" or "to the one who bestows." It highlights the importance of generosity and the act of giving.
  • devām̐: This translates to "to the gods." It signifies the divine aspect of the offerings and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • acchā: Meaning "good" or "auspicious." This word emphasizes the positive nature of the actions and intentions behind them.
  • yātave: This means "to the one who is invoked" or "to the one who is called upon." It indicates the act of seeking blessings or guidance from the divine.
  • jātavedasam: Referring to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." This highlights the omniscience of the divine and the importance of wisdom.
  • agnim: This translates to "fire," which is often a symbol of purity, transformation, and energy in many cultures.
  • īḻe: Meaning "I invoke" or "I praise." This indicates an active engagement in worship or reverence.
  • vyuṣṭiṣu: This word means "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices." It emphasizes the significance of rituals and the intention behind them.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of honoring guests, recognizing the divine, and the act of giving. It teaches us that our actions should be directed towards the greater good of humanity and that we should engage in rituals with sincerity and joy. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as hosting guests with warmth, participating in community service, or engaging in spiritual practices that foster a sense of connection with the divine and the community. The significance lies in the reminder that our actions, when rooted in respect, generosity, and joy, contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
