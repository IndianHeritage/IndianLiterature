स्तुहीन्द्रं व्यश्ववत अनूर्मिं वाजिनं यमम् |आर्यो गयम् मंहमानं विडाशुषे ||
stuhīndraṁ vyaśvavad anūrmiṁ vājinaṁ yamam | aryo gayam maṁhamānaṁ vi dāśuṣe ||
  • stuhīndraṁ: This term can be broken down into "stuhī" meaning "praise" and "indraṁ" referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature. Thus, it signifies the act of praising Indra.
  • vyaśvavad: This word can be interpreted as "like the one who is powerful" or "like the one who is strong." It emphasizes strength and capability.
  • anūrmiṁ: This term means "the one who is full of energy" or "the one who is vibrant." It suggests a sense of dynamism and liveliness.
  • vājinaṁ: This word translates to "the one who possesses wealth" or "the one who is rich." It indicates abundance and prosperity.
  • yamam: This term refers to "the one who is to be controlled" or "the one who is to be restrained." It implies a sense of governance or authority.
  • aryo: This word means "noble" or "worthy." It denotes a sense of respect and honor.
  • gayam: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is celebrated" or "the one who is praised." It signifies recognition and admiration.
  • maṁhamānaṁ: This word means "the one who is esteemed" or "the one who is honored." It reflects a sense of dignity and respect.
  • vi: This is a preposition meaning "in" or "with." It connects the following words to the context of the mantra.
  • dāśuṣe: This term translates to "for the one who serves" or "for the one who is devoted." It emphasizes the importance of service and dedication.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of praise, strength, energy, wealth, nobility, and service. It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring those who embody these qualities. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles where one must exhibit strength and nobility while serving others. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence and to appreciate the contributions of those who work diligently for the greater good. By embodying these principles, one can foster a harmonious and prosperous community.
