सुत एति पवित्रा त्विषिं दधाना ओजसा |विचक्षाणो विरोचयन ||
suta eti pavitra ā tviṣiṁ dadhāna ojasā | vicakṣāṇo virocayan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suta: son or offspring
    • eti: goes or moves
    • pavitra: pure or sacred
    • ā: towards
    • tviṣiṁ: brilliance or radiance
    • dadhāna: holding or possessing
    • ojasā: with energy or strength
    • vicakṣāṇo: wise or discerning
    • virocayan: shining or illuminating
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a son or offspring who moves towards purity and radiance.
    • The phrase "holding with energy" suggests that this purity and brilliance are not just inherent but are actively cultivated.
    • The wise and discerning nature of the offspring is emphasized, indicating that true brilliance comes from knowledge and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a child who is raised with values of honesty and integrity embodies the essence of "pavitra." For instance, a child who helps others in need demonstrates the purity of character.
    • In a professional environment, an employee who consistently seeks knowledge and applies it wisely can be seen as "vicakṣāṇo," contributing to the overall success of the team.
    • In education, a student who not only excels academically but also engages in community service reflects the idea of "ojasā," using their knowledge and energy for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the cultivation of purity, wisdom, and strength in one's character.
    • It serves as a reminder that true brilliance is not just about innate talent but also about the conscious effort to grow and contribute positively to society.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, education, and professional development, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence while maintaining integrity and wisdom.
