अधā हि विक्ष्व īḍ्यो 'सि प्रियो नो अतिथिḥ | रण्वः पुरीव जूर्यः सूनुर न त्रययाय्यः ||
adhā hi vikṣv īḍyo 'si priyo no atithiḥ | raṇvaḥ purīva jūryaḥ sūnur na trayayāyyaḥ ||
  • adhā hi vikṣv īḍyo 'si priyo no atithiḥ
    • adhā hi - "Indeed, here" or "truly, here". This phrase emphasizes the importance of the following statement.
    • vikṣv - "in all directions" or "everywhere". It signifies the omnipresence of the subject being discussed.
    • īḍyo 'si - "you are worthy of worship". This indicates that the subject is revered and holds a significant place in the hearts of people.
    • priyo no - "dear to us". This phrase expresses affection and closeness towards the subject.
    • atithiḥ - "guest" or "visitor". In Vedic culture, guests are treated with utmost respect and honor, symbolizing hospitality.
  • raṇvaḥ purīva jūryaḥ sūnur na trayayāyyaḥ
    • raṇvaḥ - "the one who brings joy" or "the one who is joyful". This suggests that the subject brings happiness and positivity.
    • purīva - "like a city" or "as a community". This indicates a sense of belonging and togetherness.
    • jūryaḥ - "prosperous" or "flourishing". This word conveys the idea of growth and abundance.
    • sūnur - "son" or "offspring". This signifies continuity and the passing of values from one generation to another.
    • na trayayāyyaḥ - "not to be trifled with" or "not to be taken lightly". This phrase emphasizes the seriousness and importance of the subject.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of hospitality and the reverence for guests in our lives. It highlights the idea that guests are not just visitors but are to be treated with love and respect, as they bring joy and prosperity to our homes and communities. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Hosting friends and family with warmth and generosity, ensuring they feel valued and cherished.
  • Creating a welcoming environment in workplaces or community gatherings, where everyone feels included and appreciated.
  • Recognizing the importance of relationships and connections, treating each interaction with care and respect.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values of hospitality, respect, and the joy that comes from nurturing relationships, which are essential for a harmonious and prosperous life.
