युवं च्यवानं जरसोऽमुमुक्तं निपेदव ऊहथुराशुम अश्वम् |निरअंहसस्तमसः स्पर्तमात्रिं नि जाहुषं शिथिरे धातमन्तः ||
yuvaṁ cyavānaṁ jaraso 'mumuktaṁ ni pedava ūhathur āśum aśvam | nir aṁhasas tamasaḥ spartam atriṁ ni jāhuṣaṁ śithire dhātam antaḥ ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • cyavānaṁ: Refers to "Cyavana," a sage known for his wisdom and rejuvenation. This indicates the importance of wisdom and experience that comes with age.
  • jaraso: Meaning "from old age," it highlights the contrast between youth and the inevitable aging process.
  • 'mumuktaṁ: This can be interpreted as "liberated" or "freed," suggesting a release from the constraints of aging and mortality.
  • ni pedava: Translates to "they have not held back," indicating a sense of freedom and the pursuit of one's desires without restraint.
  • ūhathur: Means "they have thought" or "they have considered," emphasizing the importance of contemplation and reflection in decision-making.
  • āśum: Refers to "swift" or "quick," symbolizing the urgency of life and the need to act promptly.
  • aśvam: Translates to "horse," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and speed in Vedic literature.
  • nir: Meaning "without," it denotes a state of absence or freedom from something.
  • aṁhasas: Refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," indicating a state of strength and resilience.
  • tamasaḥ: Meaning "of darkness," it symbolizes ignorance or the challenges one faces in life.
  • spartam: This can be interpreted as "to be awakened" or "to be enlightened," suggesting a journey towards knowledge and understanding.
  • atriṁ: Refers to "Atri," a revered sage, symbolizing wisdom and guidance.
  • ni jāhuṣaṁ: Meaning "they have not abandoned," it indicates a commitment to one's path and values.
  • śithire: Translates to "in the calm" or "in the stillness," suggesting the importance of peace and tranquility in life.
  • dhātam: Refers to "the essence" or "the foundation," indicating the core principles that guide one's life.
  • antaḥ: Meaning "within," it emphasizes introspection and the importance of looking inward for answers.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the journey of life, highlighting the contrast between youth and old age, the pursuit of wisdom, and the importance of acting swiftly while remaining grounded in one's values. It encourages individuals to seek liberation from the constraints of aging and ignorance, emphasizing the need for contemplation and inner strength.
  • Real-life Application: In today's fast-paced world, this mantra serves as a reminder to embrace our youthful energy while valuing the wisdom gained through experience. It encourages us to act decisively in our pursuits, whether in personal goals, career aspirations, or relationships, while remaining true to our core values and seeking knowledge.
