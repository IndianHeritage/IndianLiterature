गिरा यदī सबन्धवः पञ्च व्राताः अपस्यवः |परिष्कृत्वन्ति धरिणसिम ||
girā yadī sabandhavaḥ pañca vrātā apasyavaḥ | pariṣkṛṇvanti dharṇasim ||
  • girā: This word translates to "speech" or "words." It signifies the power of communication and expression.
  • yadī: This means "if" or "when," indicating a conditional or hypothetical situation.
  • sabandhavaḥ: This term refers to "those who are connected" or "relatives." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and community.
  • pañca: This translates to "five," which can symbolize completeness or a group of essential elements.
  • vrātā: This word means "vows" or "commitments," highlighting the significance of promises made within a community.
  • apasyavaḥ: This translates to "those who do not see" or "blind," which can refer to ignorance or lack of awareness.
  • pariṣkṛṇvanti: This means "they purify" or "they cleanse," indicating a process of refinement or improvement.
  • dharṇasim: This term refers to "the earth" or "the foundation," symbolizing stability and support.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the power of speech and the importance of community and relationships. It suggests that when individuals who are connected (relatives or community members) make vows or commitments, they may sometimes be blind to the consequences or the reality of their situation. However, through the act of communication and purification, they can refine their understanding and strengthen their foundation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Communication in Relationships: Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of speech, effective communication is crucial in personal relationships. For example, couples who openly discuss their feelings and commitments are more likely to build a strong and lasting bond.
  • Community Engagement: The idea of being connected to others can be seen in community service. When individuals come together to make commitments to help others, they can create a positive impact, even if they initially do not see the full effect of their actions.
  • Self-Reflection and Growth: The concept of purification can relate to personal growth. Individuals may need to reflect on their actions and commitments, recognizing areas of ignorance or misunderstanding, and work towards self-improvement.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of communication, community, and the process of purification in our lives. It serves as a reminder that through awareness and commitment, we can strengthen our relationships and contribute positively to our communities. The teachings of this mantra can be applied in everyday interactions, fostering a deeper understanding and connection with those around us.
