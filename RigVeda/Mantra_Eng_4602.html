इदा हि ते वेविषतः पुराजाः प्रात्नास आसुः पुरुकृत सखायः |ये मध्यमा सा उता नूतना सा उतावमस्य पुरुहूत बोधि ||
idā hi te veviṣataḥ purājāḥ pratnāsa āsuḥ purukṛt sakhāyaḥ | ye madhyamāsa uta nūtanāsa utāvamasya puruhūta bodhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idā: Here, in this context
    • hi: Indeed, certainly
    • te: Your
    • veviṣataḥ: Those who have entered
    • purājāḥ: The ancient ones, the ancestors
    • pratnāsa: The ones who are awakened or enlightened
    • āsuḥ: They are present, they exist
    • purukṛt: The one who has performed great deeds
    • sakhāyaḥ: Friends, companions
    • ye: Those who
    • madhyamāsa: The middle path, the balanced approach
    • uta: Or, also
    • nūtanāsa: The new ones, the innovators
    • utāvamasya: The highest, the supreme
    • puruhūta: The one who is invoked, the deity
    • bodhi: Enlightenment, wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the past and the present, emphasizing the importance of both ancient wisdom and new insights.
    • It highlights the role of enlightened beings (pratnāsa) who have entered the realm of knowledge and understanding, suggesting that they are companions (sakhāyaḥ) on the journey of life.
    • The mention of the "middle path" (madhyamāsa) indicates a balanced approach to life, which is crucial in today's fast-paced world where extremes often dominate.
    • It also acknowledges the presence of both the ancient (purājāḥ) and the new (nūtanāsa), suggesting that wisdom is not static but evolves over time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the integration of traditional knowledge with contemporary methods exemplifies the balance between ancient wisdom and new approaches.
    • In personal development, individuals often draw from historical philosophies (like Stoicism or Buddhism) while also embracing modern psychological practices to achieve a well-rounded perspective.
    • In community building, fostering relationships with elders (purājāḥ) while also encouraging the ideas of youth (nūtanāsa) can lead to a more harmonious society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of both heritage and innovation in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek enlightenment (bodhi) through a balanced approach, recognizing the value of both the past and the present.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, including education, personal growth, and community engagement, promoting a holistic understanding of our existence.
