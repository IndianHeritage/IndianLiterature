धर्तारो दिव ऋभवः सुहस्तā वातापर्जन्यā महिषस्य तान्यतोः |आप ओषधिः प्र तिरन्तु नो गीर भगो रातिर वाजिनो यन्तु मे हवम् ||
dhartāro diva ṛbhavaḥ suhastā vātāparjanyā mahiṣasya tanyatoḥ | āpa oṣadhīḥ pra tirantu no giro bhago rātir vājino yantu me havam ||
  • Translation: "May the Earth, the heavens, and the winds, which are the bearers of strength, bring forth the waters and the herbs for us. Let the sounds of our prayers reach the divine, and may the blessings of prosperity and abundance come to us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhartāro: Bearers or supporters, referring to the forces of nature that sustain life.
    • diva: Heavens or sky, symbolizing the divine and celestial forces.
    • ṛbhavaḥ: Strength or power, indicating the vital energy present in nature.
    • suhastā: Well-formed or auspicious, referring to the favorable conditions provided by nature.
    • vātāparjanyā: Winds and rains, essential elements for life and agriculture.
    • mahiṣasya: Of the earth, emphasizing the connection to the land and its resources.
    • tanyatoḥ: From the depths, suggesting the richness that comes from the earth.
    • āpa: Waters, crucial for sustenance and growth.
    • oṣadhīḥ: Herbs or plants, representing the flora that nourishes and heals.
    • pra tirantu: May they flow or come forth, a plea for abundance.
    • no: For us, indicating the personal nature of the prayer.
    • giro: Sounds or words, referring to the prayers and chants offered.
    • bhago: Blessings or prosperity, a request for divine favor.
    • rātir: Delight or joy, signifying the happiness that comes from abundance.
    • vājino: Of the strong or powerful, referring to the strength that comes from nature.
    • yantu: May they come, a hopeful invocation.
    • me: To me, personalizing the request.
    • havam: Offering or sacrifice, indicating the act of giving to receive blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers pray for rain and good weather to ensure a bountiful harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek blessings for prosperity and health, invoking the energies of nature through rituals and prayers.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals that honor the earth and its resources, embodying the spirit of gratitude expressed in this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It serves as a reminder to respect and honor the natural world, recognizing that our well-being is deeply tied to the health of the environment. In real life, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices, encourage gratitude for natural resources, and foster a sense of community in seeking abundance and prosperity. By acknowledging the divine forces at play in our lives, we can cultivate a harmonious relationship with nature, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
