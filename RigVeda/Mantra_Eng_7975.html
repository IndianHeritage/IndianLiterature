एतं त्यं हरितो दश मर्मृज्यन्ते अपस्युवः |याभिर मदाय शुम्भते ||
etaṁ tyaṁ harito daśa marmṛjyante apasyuvaḥ | yābhir madāya śumbhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etaṁ - this
    • tyaṁ - that
    • harito - of the green or flourishing
    • daśa - ten
    • marmṛjyante - are being wiped away or removed
    • apasyuvaḥ - those who do not see or perceive
    • yābhir - by whom
    • madāya - for the sake of intoxication or delight
    • śumbhate - are being praised or celebrated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the duality of perception and reality.
    • The phrase "this and that" suggests a comparison or contrast between two states or conditions.
    • The "green" symbolizes vitality, growth, and prosperity, while "ten" could represent completeness or a full cycle.
    • The idea of being "wiped away" indicates a loss or removal of something essential, perhaps due to ignorance or lack of awareness.
    • The mention of "those who do not see" highlights the importance of perception and awareness in understanding the world around us.
    • The latter part of the mantra suggests that there are forces or entities that are celebrated for their ability to bring joy or intoxication, possibly referring to the divine or nature itself.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a company may thrive (the "green") when its employees are aware of their roles and responsibilities. However, if they are oblivious to their impact (the "ten"), the company may face challenges.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and communication are vital. When one partner fails to perceive the needs of the other, it can lead to emotional disconnect, akin to being "wiped away."
    • In nature, the flourishing of ecosystems depends on the awareness of human actions. Ignorance can lead to environmental degradation, which is a loss of the "green."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness and perception in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to remain conscious of our surroundings and the impact of our actions.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or ecological contexts, being aware can lead to growth and prosperity.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to celebrate the beauty of life and the interconnectedness of all things, urging us to seek understanding and appreciation in our daily experiences.
