इन्द्र इषे ददातु न ऋभुक्षणं ऋभुं रयिम् |वाजी ददातु वाजिनम् ||
indra iṣe dadātu na ṛbhukṣaṇam ṛbhuṁ rayim | vājī dadātu vājinam ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Iṣe: This term refers to the act of granting or bestowing. It signifies a request for blessings or gifts.
  • Dadātu: This means "may he give" or "may he bestow." It is a plea for generosity and support.
  • Na: This translates to "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something undesirable.
  • Ṛbhukṣaṇam: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is deserving" or "the one who is worthy." It emphasizes the importance of merit and righteousness.
  • Ṛbhuṁ: This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the desire for abundance and success.
  • Rayim: This word signifies "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the theme of abundance.
  • Vājī: This term means "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful." It is often associated with victory and success.
  • Vājinam: This translates to "the one who is victorious" or "the one who is a winner," emphasizing the aspiration for triumph.

  • Meaning: The mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to bestow blessings of wealth and prosperity while ensuring that one remains deserving and righteous. It emphasizes the importance of merit in receiving blessings and the aspiration for strength and victory in life.
  • Real-Life Example: In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success in ventures. Entrepreneurs often pray for guidance and support, hoping to achieve prosperity while maintaining ethical practices. For instance, a startup founder may recite this mantra before a pitch to investors, seeking both financial backing and the strength to navigate challenges.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to success. It teaches that true prosperity comes not just from external blessings but also from internal righteousness and strength. In real life, it serves as a reminder to pursue goals with integrity and to seek support from higher powers while being deserving of the blessings one wishes to receive. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, including personal growth, professional endeavors, and spiritual journeys.
