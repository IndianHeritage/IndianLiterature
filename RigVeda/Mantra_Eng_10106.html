चतुष्कपर्दा युवतिः सुपेशाऽघृतप्रतिका वयुनानि वस्ते |तस्यां सुपर्णा वृषणा निषेदतुर्म यत्र देवाः दधिरे भागधेयम् ||
catuṣkapardā yuvatiḥ supeśā ghṛtapratīkā vayunāni vaste | tasyāṁ suparṇā vṛṣaṇā ni ṣedatur yatra devā dadhire bhāgadheyam ||
  • catuṣkapardā: This term can be broken down into "catuṣ" meaning four and "kapardā" which refers to a type of hair or locks. In a broader sense, it symbolizes completeness or wholeness.
  • yuvatiḥ: This word translates to "young woman" or "maiden," representing vitality, youth, and potential.
  • supeśā: Meaning "well-dressed" or "beautifully adorned," this term emphasizes the importance of aesthetics and presentation in life.
  • ghṛtapratīkā: This can be interpreted as "the essence of ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often associated with purity and nourishment in Vedic culture.
  • vayunāni: This word refers to "the powers" or "the strengths," indicating the capabilities or energies that one possesses.
  • vaste: Meaning "to dwell" or "to reside," this term suggests a sense of belonging or stability.
  • tasyāṁ: This translates to "in her" or "in that," referring back to the young woman mentioned earlier.
  • suparṇā: This term means "well-winged" or "having good wings," often symbolizing freedom, aspiration, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful," indicating strength and resilience.
  • ni ṣedatur: Meaning "to reside" or "to settle," this suggests a state of stability or permanence.
  • yatra: This translates to "where," indicating a location or a state of being.
  • devā: Meaning "gods" or "divine beings," this term signifies the spiritual or higher powers at play.
  • dadhire: This word means "to hold" or "to support," indicating a nurturing or protective aspect.
  • bhāgadheyam: This translates to "the share of fortune" or "the portion of wealth," suggesting abundance and prosperity.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the qualities of a young woman who embodies completeness, beauty, purity, strength, and divine support. It highlights the importance of nurturing one's strengths and aspirations while being grounded in stability and abundance.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be seen as a celebration of feminine energy and the qualities that contribute to personal and communal well-being.
  • In real life, it encourages individuals, especially women, to embrace their strengths and present themselves with confidence and grace.
  • The emphasis on divine support suggests that seeking spiritual guidance can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • In a broader context, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, community building, and fostering relationships.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of beauty, strength, and divine support in achieving a prosperous and meaningful life.
