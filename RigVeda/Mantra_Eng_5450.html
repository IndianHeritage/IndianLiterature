राजा राष्ट्राणाम् पेयो नदीनाम् अनुत्तम अस्मै क्षत्रं विश्वायुः ||
rājā rāṣṭrānām peśo nadīnām anuttam asmai kṣatraṁ viśvāyu ||
  • rājā: This word translates to "king" or "ruler." It signifies authority, leadership, and governance.
  • rāṣṭrānām: This means "of the states" or "of the nations." It indicates the collective aspect of governance over multiple regions or communities.
  • peśo: This term can be interpreted as "the best" or "the finest." It suggests a quality of excellence in leadership or governance.
  • nadīnām: This translates to "of the rivers." Rivers are often seen as lifelines for civilizations, symbolizing sustenance, prosperity, and the flow of resources.
  • anuttam: This means "the supreme" or "the unparalleled." It emphasizes the highest quality or status.
  • asmai: This word translates to "to him" or "to that one." It indicates a direction of reverence or offering.
  • kṣatraṁ: This term refers to "power" or "sovereignty." It is often associated with the warrior class and the protection of the realm.
  • viśvāyu: This means "universal energy" or "cosmic force." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the vital energy that sustains existence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the best leadership and governance over nations, akin to the flow of rivers that nourish and sustain life. It calls upon the supreme power to bestow sovereignty and universal energy to the ruler, ensuring that they govern with wisdom and excellence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership in Governance: It serves as a reminder for leaders to strive for excellence and to govern with integrity and wisdom, much like a king who is responsible for the welfare of his subjects.
  • Environmental Stewardship: The reference to rivers highlights the importance of natural resources and the need for sustainable practices in governance, ensuring that the environment is preserved for future generations.
  • Community Unity: The mantra emphasizes the collective aspect of governance, encouraging leaders to foster unity and collaboration among diverse communities for the greater good.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of righteous leadership and the responsibility that comes with power. It serves as a guiding principle for leaders to act with wisdom, ensuring the prosperity and well-being of their nations, much like the rivers that sustain life. Its application in real life can inspire leaders to prioritize excellence, sustainability, and unity in their governance.
