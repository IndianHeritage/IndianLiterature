इन्द्राविष्णु दृष्मिताः शम्बरस्य नव पुरो नवतिं च शनतिष्ठम् |शतम् वर्चिनः सहस्रं च साकं हतो अप्रत्य असुरस्य वीरान् ||
indrāviṣṇū dṛṁhitāḥ śambarasya nava puro navatiṁ ca śnathiṣṭam | śataṁ varcinaḥ sahasraṁ ca sākaṁ hatho apraty asurasya vīrān ||
  • Indrāviṣṇū: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra and Vishnu, two of the most significant deities in Hindu mythology. Indra is often seen as the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, while Vishnu is the preserver and protector of the universe.
  • Dṛṁhitāḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are established" or "those who are firm." It signifies stability and strength, suggesting that the powers of Indra and Vishnu are steadfast and reliable.
  • Śambarasya: Referring to Shambara, a demon in Vedic texts, this term indicates the challenges or adversities that one may face. It symbolizes the obstacles in life that need to be overcome.
  • Nava puro: This phrase translates to "new cities" or "new settlements," which can represent new beginnings or fresh opportunities in life.
  • Navatiṁ ca: Meaning "ninety," this can symbolize abundance or a large number of opportunities and resources available to one.
  • Śnathiṣṭam: This term can be understood as "those who are nourished" or "those who are sustained," indicating the importance of support and sustenance in overcoming challenges.
  • Śataṁ varcinaḥ: This translates to "a hundred glories," suggesting that there are numerous achievements and successes that can be attained through the blessings of the divine.
  • Sahasraṁ ca: Meaning "a thousand," this emphasizes the vastness of potential and the multitude of blessings that can be received.
  • Sākaṁ hatho: This phrase can be interpreted as "together with strength," indicating the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Apraty asurasya: This means "the non-demonic" or "beyond the demons," suggesting a state of purity and righteousness that one should strive for.
  • Vīrān: This term refers to "heroes" or "warriors," symbolizing the courage and valor needed to face life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and strength in overcoming obstacles in life. It highlights the need for unity, nourishment, and the pursuit of glory and righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal challenges, professional endeavors, or community efforts where collaboration and divine guidance are sought to achieve success. The mantra serves as a reminder that with the right support and determination, one can navigate through difficulties and emerge victorious, embodying the qualities of a true hero.
