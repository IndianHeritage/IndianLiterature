यस्य प्रस्वादसो गिर उपमश्रवसः पितुः |क्षेत्रं न रण्वम ऊचुशे ||
yasya prasvādaso gira upamaśravasaḥ pituḥ | kṣetraṁ na raṇvam ūcuṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • prasvādasaḥ: who is endowed with excellent qualities
    • gira: speech or words
    • upamaśravasaḥ: of great renown or fame
    • pituḥ: of the father
    • kṣetraṁ: the field or domain
    • na: not
    • raṇvam: to be fought or engaged in battle
    • ūcuṣe: has spoken or declared
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a person who possesses admirable qualities and whose words carry weight and significance.
    • The reference to the father indicates a lineage or heritage that is respected and esteemed.
    • The phrase "kṣetraṁ na raṇvam ūcuṣe" suggests that this individual does not engage in conflict or battle within their domain, implying a peaceful and harmonious existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a leader in a community who is known for their wisdom and integrity. Their words inspire trust and cooperation among the members, leading to a peaceful resolution of conflicts.
    • A teacher who embodies the qualities of patience and understanding can create a nurturing environment for students, fostering learning without the need for harsh discipline.
    • In a corporate setting, a manager who communicates effectively and values collaboration can lead a team to success without resorting to competition or conflict.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of possessing admirable qualities and the power of words in shaping relationships and environments.
    • It highlights that true strength lies not in conflict but in the ability to foster peace and understanding.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, education, and interpersonal relationships, where effective communication and admirable qualities can lead to harmonious outcomes.
