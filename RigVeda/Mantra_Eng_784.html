पितुर न पुत्राः क्रतुं जुषन्त श्रोषण ये अस्य शासं तुरासः ||
pitur na putrāḥ kratuṁ juṣanta śroṣan ye asya śāsaṁ turāsaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pitur: Refers to the father or ancestor.
    • Na: Means "not".
    • Putrāḥ: Means "sons" or "children".
    • Kratuṁ: Refers to "intellect" or "willpower".
    • Juṣanta: Means "enjoying" or "delighting in".
    • Śroṣan: Refers to "hearing" or "listening".
    • Ye: Means "those who".
    • Asya: Means "of this" or "of him".
    • Śāsaṁ: Refers to "command" or "order".
    • Turāsaḥ: Means "swift" or "quick".
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between fathers and their sons.
    • It suggests that those who do not listen to the commands or teachings of their fathers lack the willpower or intellect to enjoy life fully.
    • The essence of the mantra emphasizes the importance of respecting and heeding the wisdom of one's ancestors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the teachings of parents and elders are considered invaluable. For instance, a child who listens to their father's advice on financial management may avoid debt and achieve financial stability.
    • In professional settings, mentorship often mirrors this relationship. A young employee who seeks guidance from a seasoned mentor is more likely to succeed than one who disregards their advice.
    • In spiritual practices, many individuals find solace and direction by following the teachings of their ancestors, which can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the value of familial wisdom and the importance of listening to those who have come before us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to respect their heritage and learn from the experiences of their parents and ancestors.
    • By doing so, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of life, make informed decisions, and ultimately lead a more enriched existence.
