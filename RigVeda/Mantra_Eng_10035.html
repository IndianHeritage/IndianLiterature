उच्चा दिवि दक्षिणावन्तो अस्तुर ये अश्वदाः सह ते सूर्येण |हिरण्यदाऽमृतत्वं भजन्ते वासोदाः सोम प्र तिरन्त आहुः ||
uccā divi dakṣiṇāvanto asthur ye aśvadāḥ saha te sūryeṇa | hiraṇyadā amṛtatvam bhajante vāsodāḥ soma pra tiranta āyuḥ ||
  • Uccā divi dakṣiṇāvanto: This phrase can be translated as "those who are high in the sky and are favorable." It refers to celestial beings or deities that are benevolent and powerful.
  • Asthur ye aśvadāḥ saha te sūryeṇa: This means "those who are swift like horses, together with the sun." Here, the imagery of horses symbolizes speed, strength, and vitality, while the sun represents energy and life.
  • Hiraṇyadā amṛtatvam bhajante: This translates to "those who possess golden wealth attain immortality." The term "hiraṇyadā" signifies wealth or prosperity, and "amṛtatvam" refers to the state of being immortal or eternal.
  • Vāsodāḥ soma pra tiranta āyuḥ: This can be interpreted as "the ones who drink soma, may they attain long life." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer health and longevity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, vitality, prosperity, and longevity. In real life, it can be applied to the pursuit of a fulfilling and prosperous life, where one seeks the favor of higher powers or deities, embodies strength and vitality, and strives for wealth that contributes to well-being. The reference to soma highlights the significance of nourishment and health in achieving a long and fruitful life. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance between spiritual and material wealth, fostering a holistic approach to life.
