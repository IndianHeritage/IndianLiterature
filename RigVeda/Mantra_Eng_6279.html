मन्त्र: य इन्द्र सोमपातमः मदः शविष्ठ चेतति |येना हंसी न्य अत्रिणं तं ईमहे ||
ya indra somapātamo madaḥ śaviṣṭha cetati | yenā haṁsi ny a1triṇaṁ tam īmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • somapātamo: the one who drinks soma (a sacred drink)
    • madaḥ: intoxication or ecstasy
    • śaviṣṭha: the most powerful or the best
    • cetati: thinks or contemplates
    • yenā: by whom
    • haṁsi: you are made strong or empowered
    • ny a1triṇaṁ: the one who is not averse or hostile
    • tam: that
    • īmahe: we invoke or we meditate upon
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, who is associated with strength and power.
    • It highlights the importance of the soma drink, which symbolizes divine inspiration and ecstasy.
    • The mantra suggests that through the power of Indra, one can gain strength and overcome adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In challenging situations, individuals often seek inspiration or motivation, akin to the divine intoxication of soma, to empower themselves.
    • In leadership, invoking the qualities of strength and wisdom, similar to Indra, can help guide teams through difficulties.
    • In personal development, meditating on the qualities of strength and resilience can lead to overcoming personal challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine strength and inspiration in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and motivation.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find the strength to face adversities and achieve their goals.
