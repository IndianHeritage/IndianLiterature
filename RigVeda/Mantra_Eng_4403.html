द्योऽन यस्य पनयन्त्यभ्वं भासांसी वस्ते सूर्यो न शुक्रः |विय इनोत्यजरः पावकोऽश्नस्य चिच्छिष्णथत पूर्व्याणि ||
dyāvo na yasya panayanty abhvam bhāsāṁsi vaste sūryo na śukraḥ | vi ya inoty ajaraḥ pāvako 'śnasya cic chiśnathat pūrvyāṇi ||
  • dyāvo: This word refers to the heavens or the sky, symbolizing the vastness and the divine.
  • na: A negation, meaning "not" or "without."
  • yasya: Meaning "whose," indicating possession or relation.
  • panayanty: This can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to produce."
  • abhvam: Refers to "the past" or "that which has been."
  • bhāsāṁsi: Meaning "light" or "radiance," often associated with knowledge and enlightenment.
  • vaste: This means "to dwell" or "to reside," indicating a state of being.
  • sūryo: Refers to the sun, a symbol of energy, life, and illumination.
  • na: Again, a negation.
  • śukraḥ: Meaning "brightness" or "purity," often associated with clarity and wisdom.
  • vi: A prefix meaning "in" or "within."
  • ya: Meaning "who" or "that which."
  • inoty: This can be interpreted as "to lead" or "to guide."
  • ajaraḥ: Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," indicating something that transcends time.
  • pāvako: Refers to "the purifying fire," symbolizing transformation and purification.
  • 'śnasya: Meaning "of the eater," indicating consumption or absorption.
  • cic: Refers to "that which is," often associated with existence or reality.
  • chiśnathat: Meaning "to be known" or "to be understood."
  • pūrvyāṇi: Refers to "the ancient" or "the previous," indicating a connection to history or tradition.

This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of the cosmos, the eternal nature of existence, and the transformative power of knowledge and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of understanding the past while recognizing the guiding light of the present, represented by the sun.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the sun brings light and energy, individuals can seek knowledge and enlightenment to transform their lives. For example, pursuing education or self-improvement can lead to personal evolution.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the heavens and the sun can inspire a connection to nature. Understanding our environment and the impact of our actions can lead to a more sustainable lifestyle.
  • Spiritual Journey: The idea of immortality and the eternal can encourage individuals to explore their spiritual beliefs and seek a deeper understanding of their existence.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of knowledge, the importance of understanding our past, and the transformative power of enlightenment. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to recognize the interconnectedness of all things, guiding them towards a more meaningful and purposeful life.
