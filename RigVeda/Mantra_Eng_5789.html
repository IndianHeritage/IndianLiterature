उच्छन्ती या कृणोषि मंहनā महि प्रख्यै देवी स्वर दृष्टे |तस्यास ते रत्नभाजा ईमहे वयं स्याम मातुर न सूनवः ||
ucchantī yā kṛṇoṣi maṁhanā mahi prakhyai devi svar dṛśe | tasyās te ratnabhāja īmahe vayaṁ syāma mātur na sūnavaḥ ||
  • ucchantī: This word can be interpreted as "those who elevate" or "those who uplift." It signifies the act of raising or enhancing one's state of being.
  • yā: This means "who" or "that," referring to the subject of the mantra, which is a divine entity.
  • kṛṇoṣi: Translated as "you create" or "you perform," this word emphasizes the creative power of the divine.
  • maṁhanā: This term can be understood as "greatness" or "magnificence," indicating the vastness of the divine presence.
  • mahi: Meaning "great" or "mighty," it further emphasizes the strength and power of the divine.
  • prakhyai: This word translates to "manifest" or "shine," suggesting the visibility and clarity of the divine's influence.
  • devi: Referring to the "goddess" or "divine feminine," it acknowledges the nurturing and protective aspects of divinity.
  • svar: This means "sound" or "voice," often associated with the cosmic sound or the essence of creation.
  • dṛśe: Translated as "sight" or "vision," it indicates perception and understanding.
  • tasyās: Meaning "of her," it refers back to the divine feminine mentioned earlier.
  • te: This means "your," indicating a connection between the divine and the devotee.
  • ratnabhāja: Translated as "those who possess jewels," it symbolizes the blessings and riches bestowed by the divine.
  • īmahe: This means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a practice of reflection and devotion.
  • vayaṁ: Meaning "we," it emphasizes the collective aspect of the prayer or mantra.
  • syāma: This translates to "may we be" or "let us be," expressing a desire for a certain state of being.
  • mātur: Meaning "of the mother," it reinforces the nurturing aspect of the divine.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something.
  • sūnavaḥ: Translated as "sons" or "children," it refers to the offspring or the followers of the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to the divine feminine, asking for elevation, greatness, and blessings. It acknowledges the nurturing aspect of the goddess and expresses a desire for the community to be enriched by her presence. The invocation of "ratnabhāja" suggests that the blessings received are precious and valuable, akin to jewels.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation to seek elevation in their personal and spiritual lives, aiming for greatness and clarity in their actions.
  • Community Well-being: Groups or communities can use this mantra in gatherings to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and harmony, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Motherhood and Nurturing: Expecting mothers or caregivers can chant this mantra to seek divine support and blessings for the well-being of their children, emphasizing the protective nature of the divine feminine.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine's role in uplifting and nurturing individuals and communities. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine feminine's presence in our lives, encouraging us to seek greatness, clarity, and blessings in our journey. By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and enhance their spiritual growth.
