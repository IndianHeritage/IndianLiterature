स विप्रश्चरषणीनां शवसा मानवशाणाम् |अति क्षिप्रेव विध्यति ||
sa vipraś carṣaṇīnāṁ śavasā mānuṣāṇām | ati kṣipreva vidhyati ||
  • sa vipraś: This phrase can be translated as "that wise one" or "the learned one." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to a person who possesses knowledge and wisdom.
  • carṣaṇīnāṁ: This word means "of the seers" or "of the sages." It indicates a connection to those who have deep insight and understanding, often through spiritual or philosophical practices.
  • śavasā: This term translates to "with the body" or "in the physical form." It emphasizes the importance of the physical existence and the experiences that come with it.
  • mānuṣāṇām: This means "of humans" or "of mankind." It highlights the human condition and the shared experiences of humanity.
  • ati kṣipreva: This phrase can be interpreted as "very quickly" or "with great speed." It suggests a sense of urgency or immediacy in the actions or thoughts being described.
  • vidhyati: This word means "is created" or "is formed." It indicates the process of bringing something into existence or manifesting an idea or thought.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra speaks to the relationship between wisdom, human experience, and the rapid manifestation of thoughts or actions. It suggests that a wise person, connected to the insights of sages, can quickly bring forth ideas or actions that are beneficial to humanity.
  • Real-Life Example: Consider a leader in a community who embodies wisdom and understanding. When faced with a challenge, such as a natural disaster, this leader can quickly mobilize resources and inspire others to act, demonstrating the principle of "ati kṣipreva vidhyati." Their wisdom allows them to see the best course of action and implement it effectively.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of wisdom and the ability to act swiftly for the benefit of humanity. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate knowledge and insight, enabling them to respond effectively to challenges and opportunities. This mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom, when applied, can lead to rapid and positive change in the world.
