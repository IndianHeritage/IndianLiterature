श्रिये कं वो अधि तनूषु वाशीर मेधा वनान न कृणवन्त ऊर्ध्वा |युष्मभ्यं कम मरुतः सुजातास तुविद्युम्नासो धनयन्ते अद्रिम ||
śriye kaṁ vo adhi tanūṣu vāśīr medhā vanā na kṛṇavanta ūrdhvā | yuṣmabhyaṁ kam marutaḥ sujātās tuvidyumnāso dhanayante adrim ||
  • śriye: This word translates to "for prosperity" or "for wealth." It signifies the desire for abundance and success in life.
  • kaṁ: This means "who" or "whom," indicating a quest for a benefactor or a source of blessings.
  • vo: This translates to "to you," referring to the audience or the divine entities being addressed.
  • adhi: This means "upon" or "over," suggesting a sense of elevation or enhancement.
  • tanūṣu: This word means "in bodies" or "in forms," indicating the physical manifestation of beings.
  • vāśīr: This translates to "the one who is powerful" or "the ruler," referring to a divine force or deity.
  • medhā: This means "intellect" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of mental clarity and understanding.
  • vanā: This translates to "in the forest" or "in nature," symbolizing the connection to the natural world.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something undesirable.
  • kṛṇavanta: This translates to "those who create" or "those who make," referring to the active role in shaping one's destiny.
  • ūrdhvā: This means "upward" or "higher," suggesting aspirations towards higher goals or spiritual elevation.
  • yuṣmabhyaṁ: This translates to "to you all," addressing a collective audience.
  • kam: Again, this means "who," reinforcing the inquiry into the source of blessings.
  • marutaḥ: This refers to "the winds" or "the storm gods," symbolizing powerful forces of nature.
  • sujātās: This means "well-born" or "noble," indicating a lineage of virtue and goodness.
  • tuvidyumnāso: This translates to "those who are knowledgeable" or "those who have great wisdom," emphasizing the value of knowledge.
  • dhanayante: This means "bring wealth" or "bestow riches," indicating the act of granting prosperity.
  • adrim: This translates to "the mountain" or "the firm ground," symbolizing stability and strength.

The mantra expresses a prayer for prosperity, wisdom, and the elevation of one's being. It seeks blessings from powerful forces, urging them to bestow wealth and knowledge upon the individual and the community. The invocation of natural elements like the winds and mountains signifies a deep respect for nature and its role in human life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or reflection to seek clarity and wisdom in their decisions, aiming for personal and professional success.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can use this mantra in gatherings or rituals to invoke collective prosperity and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and shared goals.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to nature encourages a connection with the environment, promoting sustainable practices and respect for natural resources.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of prosperity, wisdom, and nature. It emphasizes the importance of seeking higher knowledge and the blessings of powerful forces to achieve a fulfilling and abundant life. By incorporating its teachings into daily life, individuals and communities can strive for growth, stability, and harmony with the world around them.
