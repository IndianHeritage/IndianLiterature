तद वां नराः शंस्यम् पज्रियेन कक्षीवतां नासत्याः परिज्मन् |शफाद अश्वस्य वाजिनो जनाय शतम् कुम्भांऽऽसिञ्चतं मधूनाम् ||
tad vāṁ narā śaṁsyam pajriyeṇa kakṣīvatā nāsatyā parijman | śaphād aśvasya vājino janāya śataṁ kumbhām̐ asiñcatam madhūnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vāṁ: speech or voice
    • narā: men or people
    • śaṁsyam: to be praised or celebrated
    • pajriyeṇa: with the help of or through
    • kakṣīvatā: like a womb or container
    • nāsatyā: the Asvins, divine twin deities
    • parijman: to be invoked or called upon
    • śaphād: from the abundance or plenty
    • aśvasya: of the horse
    • vājino: of the chariot or vehicle
    • janāya: for the people or community
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • kumbhām̐: pots or vessels
    • asiñcatam: to be poured or sprinkled
    • madhūnām: of the sweet or honey
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of speech and the importance of invoking divine blessings.
    • The phrase "tad vāṁ narā śaṁsyam" emphasizes the need for people to celebrate and praise the divine through their words.
    • The reference to "pajriyeṇa kakṣīvatā" suggests that just as a womb nurtures life, our words and intentions can nurture positive outcomes.
    • The invocation of the Asvins, "nāsatyā parijman," highlights the importance of calling upon divine forces for assistance in our endeavors.
    • The imagery of "śaphād aśvasya vājino" relates to the abundance and strength that can be derived from the divine, akin to the power of a horse in a chariot.
    • The mention of "janāya śataṁ kumbhām̐" signifies the desire for prosperity and abundance for the community, represented by the hundred vessels filled with sweetness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often share words of encouragement and praise, which can uplift spirits and foster unity.
    • In personal development, affirmations and positive speech can nurture one's self-esteem and lead to better outcomes in life.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, invoking divine blessings can be seen as a way to seek guidance and support in challenging times.
    • In teamwork, celebrating each member's contributions can create a more harmonious and productive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of speech and the power of invoking divine blessings for the well-being of individuals and communities.
    • It teaches us that our words can create a nurturing environment, much like a womb, and that we should seek divine assistance in our endeavors.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, personal affirmations, and teamwork, to foster positivity and abundance.
