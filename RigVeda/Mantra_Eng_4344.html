मन्त्र: स्तोमासस त्वा विचारीणि प्रति ष्टोभन्त्यक्तुभिः |प्र यां वाजं न हेषन्तम् पेरुम अस्यस्य अर्जुनि ||
stomāsas tvā vicāriṇi prati ṣṭobhanty aktubhiḥ | pra yā vājaṁ na heṣantam perum asyasy arjuni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • stomāsas: hymns or praises
    • tvā: you
    • vicāriṇi: contemplating or reflecting
    • prati: towards
    • ṣṭobhanty: they make to vibrate or resonate
    • aktubhiḥ: with offerings or sacrifices
    • pra: for
    • yā: which
    • vājaṁ: strength or vigor
    • na: not
    • heṣantam: to be neglected or overlooked
    • perum: to be attained or achieved
    • asyasy: of this
    • arjuni: of the one who is strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of hymns and praises directed towards a divine entity.
    • It suggests that through contemplation and reflection, one can resonate with the divine, enhancing the effectiveness of offerings.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of not neglecting the strength and vigor that can be attained through sincere devotion and worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, chanting or singing praises to deities is a common practice, such as in churches, temples, or during spiritual gatherings.
    • For instance, in a yoga class, participants often chant mantras to create a sense of unity and focus, enhancing their practice.
    • In personal life, individuals may use affirmations or positive mantras to cultivate strength and resilience during challenging times.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of words and intentions in connecting with the divine.
    • It teaches that through reflection and sincere offerings, one can achieve strength and overcome obstacles.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, spiritual practices, and community worship, reinforcing the idea that devotion and contemplation can lead to profound transformations.
