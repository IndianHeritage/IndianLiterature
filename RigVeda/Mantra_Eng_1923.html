युवां गोतमः पुरुमीळ्हो अत्रीर दश्रā हवते 'वसे हविष्मान् |दिशं न दिष्टाम् ऋजूयेव यन्ता मे हवं नासत्योप यातम् ||
yuvāṁ gotamaḥ purumīḻho atrir dasrā havate 'vase haviṣmān | diśaṁ na diṣṭām ṛjūyeva yantā me havaṁ nāsatyopa yātam ||
  • yuvāṁ gotamaḥ purumīḻho atrir dasrā havate 'vase haviṣmān
    • yuvāṁ: This word refers to "you" in a plural sense, indicating a collective or community.
    • gotamaḥ: This is a reference to the sage Gotama, symbolizing wisdom and knowledge.
    • purumīḻho: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is well-versed" or "the knowledgeable one."
    • atrir: Refers to the sage Atri, another figure of wisdom and enlightenment.
    • dasrā: This can mean "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has strength."
    • havate: This word means "to invoke" or "to call upon."
    • 'vase: This indicates "to be in control" or "to have power over."
    • haviṣmān: Refers to "the one who possesses offerings" or "the one who is worthy of offerings."
  • diśaṁ na diṣṭām ṛjūyeva yantā me havaṁ nāsatyopa yātam
    • diśaṁ: This means "direction" or "path."
    • na diṣṭām: This can be interpreted as "not obstructed" or "not hindered."
    • ṛjūyeva: This term means "straight" or "honest."
    • yantā: Refers to "the one who leads" or "the one who guides."
    • me: This means "my" or "to me."
    • havaṁ: This refers to "offering" or "sacrifice."
    • nāsatyopa: This can be interpreted as "the truthful ones" or "the honest ones."
    • yātam: This means "to go" or "to proceed."

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the pursuit of truth. It calls upon the collective strength of knowledgeable individuals to invoke the right path and ensure that offerings are made with sincerity and honesty. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In leadership roles, where guiding others with wisdom and integrity is crucial.
  • In community gatherings, where collective decision-making is enhanced by the input of knowledgeable individuals.
  • In personal development, where one seeks to follow a straight and honest path in life.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
