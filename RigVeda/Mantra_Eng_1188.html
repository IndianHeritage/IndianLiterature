एवेन्द्राग्नī पपिवांसā सुतस्य विश्वास्मभ्यं सं जयतां धनानि |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
evendrāgnī papivāṁsā sutasya viśvāsmabhyaṁ saṁ jayataṁ dhanāni | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • evendrāgnī: This term refers to the divine fire (Agni) that is invoked for blessings. In real life, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how we seek to transform our lives through knowledge and wisdom.
  • papivāṁsā: This word means "to drink" or "to consume." It signifies the act of taking in knowledge or resources. In a practical sense, it can relate to how we absorb information and experiences in our daily lives.
  • sutasya: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring." It emphasizes the importance of lineage and heritage. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how families pass down values and traditions through generations.
  • viśvāsmabhyaṁ: This means "to all of us" or "to everyone." It highlights the collective aspect of existence, reminding us of our interconnectedness. In today's world, this can be reflected in community efforts and social responsibility.
  • saṁ jayataṁ dhanāni: This phrase translates to "may wealth be victorious." It speaks to the aspiration for prosperity and success. In real life, this can be related to personal and communal goals for financial stability and abundance.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo: This means "may that (wealth) be ours, O Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This invokes the idea of seeking harmony and balance in our relationships and endeavors.
  • māmahantām: This translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies the pursuit of greatness in our actions and aspirations. In practical terms, it can relate to striving for excellence in our personal and professional lives.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi represents the infinite and the mother of all gods. This symbolizes the nurturing aspect of the universe. In real life, it can be seen in the care and support we provide to one another in our communities.
  • sindhuḥ: This means "ocean" or "river," symbolizing abundance and the flow of life. It can be related to the natural resources we depend on and the importance of preserving our environment.
  • pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: This translates to "the earth and the sky." It signifies the balance between the material and spiritual realms. In our lives, this can be reflected in the need to maintain harmony between our physical needs and spiritual growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, emphasizing the importance of knowledge, prosperity, community, and balance. It serves as a reminder to seek wealth not just in material terms but also in relationships, wisdom, and harmony with nature. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose. By invoking the blessings of divine forces, we can strive for a life that is rich in both material and spiritual wealth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
