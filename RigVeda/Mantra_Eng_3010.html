अश्विनाऽपरी वाम इषः पुरूचीर ईयुर गीरभिर यतमानाः अमृतध्राः | रथो ह वाम ऋतजाऽद्रिजूतः परि dyावापृथिवी याति सद्यः ||
aśvinā pari vām iṣaḥ purūcīr īyur gīrbhir yatamānā amṛdhrāḥ | ratho ha vām ṛtajā adrijūtaḥ pari dyāvāpṛthivī yāti sadyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā pari vām iṣaḥ purūcīr īyur gīrbhir yatamānā amṛdhrāḥ | ratho ha vām ṛtajā adrijūtaḥ pari dyāvāpṛthivī yāti sadyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra invokes the Aśvins, the divine twin horsemen, asking them to come swiftly with their chariots and bring forth their blessings through their songs and efforts.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • pari: Means 'around' or 'in all directions', indicating a comprehensive approach.
    • vām: Means 'you' (plural), addressing the Aśvins directly.
    • iṣaḥ: Refers to 'power' or 'strength', suggesting the divine energy of the Aśvins.
    • purūcīr: Means 'with many songs', indicating the importance of hymns and prayers.
    • īyur: Means 'to come' or 'to arrive', emphasizing the action of the Aśvins coming to aid.
    • gīrbhir: Refers to 'by songs', highlighting the role of music and chants in invoking divine presence.
    • yatamānā: Means 'striving' or 'efforting', indicating the need for human effort in conjunction with divine help.
    • amṛdhrāḥ: Refers to 'immortal' or 'eternal', suggesting the timeless nature of the blessings sought.
    • ratho: Means 'chariot', symbolizing movement and progress.
    • ha: An emphatic particle, reinforcing the statement.
    • ṛtajā: Refers to 'truth' or 'righteousness', indicating the moral foundation of the request.
    • adrijūtaḥ: Means 'born of the mountains', possibly referring to the strength and stability of the Aśvins.
    • pari: Again means 'around' or 'in all directions'.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Refers to 'heaven and earth', indicating the cosmic scope of the invocation.
    • yāti: Means 'to go' or 'to move', suggesting the action of the blessings being delivered.
    • sadyaḥ: Means 'immediately', emphasizing the urgency of the request.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of illness, people often seek divine intervention through prayers, similar to how the Aśvins are invoked for health and healing.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may chant mantras to invite blessings and ensure success, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of striving for success while seeking divine support can be seen in motivational practices where individuals set goals and pray for guidance and strength.
  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine assistance in human endeavors. It highlights the need for both effort and spiritual support, suggesting that while we strive for our goals, invoking higher powers can lead to success and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals seek guidance, health, and prosperity, reminding us of the interconnectedness of human effort and divine grace.
