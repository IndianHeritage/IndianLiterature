मां वां वृको मां वृकीर आ दधर्षीन मां परि वर्क्तम उता माति धक्तम |अयं वाम भागो निहित इयं गीर दसराव इमे वां निधयो मधूनाम ||
mā vāṁ vṛko mā vṛkīr ā dadharṣīn mā pari varktam uta māti dhaktam | ayaṁ vām bhāgo nihita iyaṁ gīr dasrāv ime vāṁ nidhayo madhūnām ||
  • mā vāṁ vṛko mā vṛkīr ā dadharṣīn: This phrase can be translated as "May the wolf not harm you, may the wolf not attack you." Here, the wolf symbolizes danger or adversities that one may face in life.
  • mā pari varktam uta māti dhaktam: This translates to "May you not be driven away, may you not be burned." This part emphasizes the desire for protection from external threats and internal turmoil.
  • ayaṁ vām bhāgo nihita: This means "This fortune is hidden for you." It suggests that there are blessings and opportunities that are yet to be revealed, waiting for the right moment to manifest.
  • iyaṁ gīr dasrāv ime vāṁ nidhayo madhūnām: This translates to "These songs and treasures of sweetness are for you." It indicates that there are joys and rewards in life that are meant for the individual, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation for protection, prosperity, and the unveiling of hidden blessings. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as during challenging times when one feels threatened or overwhelmed. For instance, when facing a difficult situation at work or in personal relationships, reciting this mantra can provide a sense of security and hope. It encourages individuals to remain resilient and trust that good fortune is on the horizon. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to instill confidence and a sense of safety, reminding us that despite adversities, there are always hidden treasures and opportunities waiting to be discovered.
