इमा उ वां दिविष्टय उष्रा हवन्ते अश्विनाः |अयं वाम अह्वे 'वसे शचिवसू विशं-विशं हि गच्छतः ||
imā u vāṁ diviṣṭaya usrā havante aśvinā | ayaṁ vām ahve 'vase śacīvasū viśaṁ-viśaṁ hi gacchathaḥ ||
  • imā u vāṁ: This phrase can be translated as "these (offerings) are for you." It signifies the act of offering and devotion, highlighting the importance of giving in spiritual practices.
  • diviṣṭaya: This word refers to "heavenly" or "divine." It indicates that the offerings are directed towards celestial beings or deities, emphasizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • usrā havante: "usrā" means "the strong" or "the powerful," and "havante" refers to "those who are invoked." Together, this suggests that the powerful beings (in this context, the Aśvins) are being called upon for assistance or blessings.
  • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • ayaṁ vām ahve: This translates to "I call upon you." It is a direct invocation, showing the speaker's desire to connect with the divine and seek their presence.
  • 'vase: This word means "to come" or "to arrive." It indicates a welcoming of the divine presence into the earthly realm.
  • śacīvasū: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with power." It highlights the strength and capability of the deities being invoked.
  • viśaṁ-viśaṁ hi gacchathaḥ: This phrase means "go to all beings." It signifies the universality of the blessings being sought, indicating that the divine assistance is not limited to a select few but is meant for all of creation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of invoking divine powers for assistance and blessings. It reflects the human desire to connect with the divine and seek support in various aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in rituals, prayers, and moments of need where individuals seek guidance, health, and well-being. The invocation of the Aśvins symbolizes the hope for balance and harmony in life, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community and support in our journeys. The mantra serves as a reminder that divine assistance is available to all, encouraging a sense of unity and shared purpose in the pursuit of well-being and strength.
