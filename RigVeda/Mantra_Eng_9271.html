मन्त्र: अस्ताव्य अग्निर नरां सुशेवं वैश्वानर ऋषिभिः सोमगोपाः | अद्वेषे dyावापृथिवी हुवेम देवाः धत्त रयिम अस्मे सुवीरम् ||
astāvy agnir narāṁ suśevo vaiśvānara ṛṣibhiḥ somagopāḥ | adveṣe dyāvāpṛthivī huvema devā dhatta rayim asme suvīram ||
  • Mantra: "astāvy agnir narāṁ suśevo vaiśvānara ṛṣibhiḥ somagopāḥ | adveṣe dyāvāpṛthivī huvema devā dhatta rayim asme suvīram ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • astāvy: May there be (or may we have)
    • agnir: Fire
    • narāṁ: Of men (or humanity)
    • suśevo: Well-being or auspiciousness
    • vaiśvānara: Universal or cosmic
    • ṛṣibhiḥ: By the sages or seers
    • somagopāḥ: Protectors of the soma (nectar of immortality)
    • adveṣe: Without enmity
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth
    • huvema: We invoke or call upon
    • devā: Deities or divine beings
    • dhatta: Grant or bestow
    • rayim: Wealth or prosperity
    • asme: For us
    • suvīram: Good strength or valor
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra begins with a call for the presence of fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire can be seen in various forms, such as the fire of knowledge, passion, or even the literal fire in rituals.
    • The mention of "narāṁ" emphasizes the collective aspect of humanity, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just for the individual but for all people.
    • "Suśevo" indicates a desire for auspiciousness and well-being, which can be related to personal health, happiness, and success in endeavors.
    • "Vaiśvānara" refers to the universal aspect of existence, reminding us of our connection to the cosmos and the larger universe. This can be reflected in our daily lives through practices that promote harmony with nature.
    • The term "ṛṣibhiḥ" highlights the wisdom of sages, suggesting that the guidance of wise individuals is essential for achieving the desired outcomes.
    • "Somagopāḥ" refers to the protectors of the soma, which symbolizes nourishment and immortality. In modern life, this can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing our bodies and minds through healthy practices.
    • The phrase "adveṣe dyāvāpṛthivī" calls for harmony between heaven and earth, advocating for a peaceful coexistence that is crucial in today's world, where conflicts often arise.
    • Finally, the invocation of deities for wealth and strength signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and resilience in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking collective well-being, harmony, and divine blessings in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, rituals for peace, or personal meditative practices aimed at fostering positivity and strength.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to strive for a life filled with auspiciousness, wisdom, and a deep connection to both the earthly and cosmic realms.
