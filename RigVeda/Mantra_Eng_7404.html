एतो nv इन्द्रं स्तवामेशानं वस्वः स्वराजम् |न राधसां मर्दिषन् नः ||
eto nv indraṁ stavāmeśānaṁ vasvaḥ svarājam | na rādhasā mardhiṣan naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "eto nv indraṁ stavāmeśānaṁ vasvaḥ svarājam | na rādhasā mardhiṣan naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke Indra, the supreme ruler of all, the one who is worthy of praise; may he not harm us with his wrath."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • eto: Here, this, or thus.
    • nv: Indeed or certainly.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, often associated with thunder and rain.
    • stavāmeśānaṁ: We praise or invoke; 'stava' means praise, and 'īśāna' refers to the lord or ruler.
    • vasvaḥ: All or everything; it signifies the totality of existence.
    • svarājam: The king of the heavens or the celestial realm.
    • na: Not.
    • rādhasā: With wrath or anger; 'rādha' refers to strength or power.
    • mardhiṣan: To harm or to strike.
    • naḥ: Us or our.

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, who is revered as a powerful deity in Vedic literature. Indra is often called upon for protection and blessings, especially in times of need.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a call for divine assistance and acknowledgment of his supreme power over the natural elements.
    • The plea for Indra not to harm us with his wrath reflects a common human concern about the forces of nature and the need for harmony with them.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra was crucial during the monsoon season, as he is associated with rain. Farmers would chant this mantra to seek favorable weather conditions for their crops.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be interpreted as a reminder to respect nature and seek balance in our interactions with the environment. For instance, climate change discussions often revolve around the consequences of human actions on natural systems.
    • On a personal level, this mantra can serve as a meditation for individuals seeking protection and guidance in their lives, encouraging them to reflect on their actions and their impact on the world around them.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and guidance while acknowledging the power of nature. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in harmony with the environment.
    • In today's world, where environmental issues are at the forefront, the teachings of this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to foster a respectful relationship with nature, ensuring a sustainable future for generations to come.
