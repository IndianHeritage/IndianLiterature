त्वं अग्ने द्युभिः त्वं āशुशुक्षणिस् त्वं adbhyas त्वं अश्मनस् परि |त्वं वनेभ्यः त्वं ओषधिभ्यः त्वं नृणां नृपतिः जायसे शुचिः ||
tvam agne dyubhis tvam āśuśukṣaṇis tvam adbhyas tvam aśmanas pari | tvaṁ vanebhyas tvam oṣadhībhyas tvaṁ nṛṇāṁ nṛpate jāyase śuciḥ ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and transformation.
  • dyubhis - "from the heavens" - This indicates the divine nature of Agni, suggesting that he is connected to the celestial realm.
  • tvam āśuśukṣaṇis - "You are swift and powerful" - This highlights Agni's ability to act quickly and effectively, much like how fire can spread rapidly and transform its surroundings.
  • tvam adbhyas - "You are from the waters" - This signifies the connection between fire and water, illustrating the balance of elements in nature.
  • tvam aśmanas pari - "You encompass the stones" - This suggests that Agni has the power to transform even the hardest materials, akin to how fire can turn stone into ash.
  • tvaṁ vanebhyas - "You are from the forests" - This indicates that Agni is also found in nature, particularly in the woods where fire can be kindled.
  • tvam oṣadhībhyas - "You are from the herbs" - This reflects the idea that fire is essential for cooking and processing plants, which are vital for sustenance.
  • tvaṁ nṛṇāṁ - "You are for mankind" - This emphasizes Agni's role in human life, as fire is crucial for warmth, cooking, and protection.
  • nṛpate - "O Lord of men" - This acknowledges Agni's authority and importance in human affairs, as he is often invoked in rituals for blessings and guidance.
  • jāyase śuciḥ - "You are born pure" - This signifies the purity associated with fire, which is often seen as a cleansing force in various cultures.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the multifaceted nature of Agni, representing not just a physical element but also a spiritual force that connects the heavens, earth, and humanity. In real life, the significance of this mantra can be seen in various contexts, such as in rituals where fire is used for purification and offerings, symbolizing the transformation of the mundane into the sacred. It teaches us about the interconnectedness of nature and the importance of respecting and harnessing these elements for our well-being. The invocation of Agni serves as a reminder of the balance we must maintain with the natural world, emphasizing the need for harmony between human activities and the environment.
