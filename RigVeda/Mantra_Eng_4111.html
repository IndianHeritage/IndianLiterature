प्र शर्धाय मारुताय स्वभानव इमां वाचम अनजा पर्वतच्युत |घर्मस्तुभे दिव आ पृष्ठयज्वने द्युम्नश्रवसे महि नृम्णम आर्चत ||
pra śardhāya mārutāya svabhānava imāṁ vācam anajā parvatacyute | gharmastubhe diva ā pṛṣṭhayajvane dyumnaśravase mahi nṛmṇam arcata ||
  • pra śardhāya: This phrase can be interpreted as "for the sake of strength" or "for the purpose of vigor." It emphasizes the importance of strength in various aspects of life.
  • mārutāya: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms. This signifies the dynamic and powerful forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • svabhānava: This term means "of one's own light" or "self-illuminating." It suggests the idea of inner strength and self-awareness, highlighting the importance of recognizing one's own potential.
  • imāṁ vācam: Translated as "this speech" or "this utterance," it indicates the significance of communication and expression in human interactions.
  • anajā: Meaning "unborn" or "eternal," this word reflects the idea of timelessness and the continuity of existence beyond physical forms.
  • parvatacyute: This can be understood as "the fall of the mountain," symbolizing challenges or obstacles that one may face in life.
  • gharmastubhe: This phrase refers to "the warmth" or "the heat," which can be associated with passion, energy, and the drive to achieve goals.
  • diva ā pṛṣṭha: Meaning "from the heavens above," this suggests a divine or higher influence guiding human actions and aspirations.
  • yajvane: This term relates to "the one who performs sacrifices," indicating the importance of dedication and effort in achieving success.
  • dyumnaśravase: Translated as "to the one who is renowned for glory," it emphasizes the pursuit of honor and recognition through one's deeds.
  • mahi nṛmṇam: This phrase means "great deeds of men," highlighting the significance of human actions and their impact on the world.
  • arcata: This term means "to worship" or "to honor," indicating the importance of reverence and gratitude in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, self-awareness, and the dynamic forces of nature that influence human life. It emphasizes the importance of communication, overcoming challenges, and the pursuit of glory through dedicated actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, leadership, and the pursuit of goals. By recognizing our inner strength and the challenges we face, we can navigate life's obstacles with resilience and honor the contributions of ourselves and others. This mantra serves as a reminder to strive for greatness while remaining grounded in humility and gratitude.
