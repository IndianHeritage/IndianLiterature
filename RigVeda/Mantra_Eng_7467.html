यज जायताऽअपूर्व्य मगवन वृत्रहत्याय |तत पृथिवीं अप्रथयस् तद अस्तभ्ना उता द्यां ||
yaj jāyathā apūrvya maghavan vṛtrahatyāya | tat pṛthivīm aprathayas tad astabhnā uta dyām ||
  • yaj jāyathā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is born from the sacrifice." In the context of the RigVeda, it refers to the idea that creation and prosperity arise from selfless actions and offerings.
  • apūrvya: This term signifies "unprecedented" or "unique." It suggests that the results of such sacrifices are extraordinary and beyond the ordinary experiences of life.
  • maghavan: This word refers to "the bountiful one" or "the generous one," often associated with the deity Indra, who is known for his strength and ability to bestow wealth and blessings upon his devotees.
  • vṛtrahatyāya: This term means "for the slaying of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology that represents chaos and obstruction. The act of slaying Vritra symbolizes overcoming obstacles and achieving victory over challenges.
  • tat pṛthivīm aprathayas: This translates to "that which spreads the earth." It indicates the expansion and flourishing of life and resources on Earth as a result of the sacrifices made.
  • tat astabhnā uta dyām: This phrase means "that which supports the heavens." It implies that the benefits of such sacrifices not only affect the earthly realm but also have a cosmic significance, supporting the balance of the universe.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of selfless actions and sacrifices in achieving prosperity and overcoming obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, charitable acts, and personal sacrifices for the greater good. By engaging in such actions, individuals can create a positive impact on their surroundings, leading to a flourishing community and a harmonious existence. The mantra serves as a reminder that our contributions, no matter how small, can lead to significant changes in both our lives and the lives of others, ultimately supporting the balance of the world around us.
