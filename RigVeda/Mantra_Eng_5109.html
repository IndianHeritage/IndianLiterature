जनाय चिद य ईवतः उ लोकं बृहस्पतिर देवहूतौ चकार |घ्नन् वृत्राणि वि पुरो दर्धरिति जयञ छत्रूम् अमित्रान पृत्सु साहन ||
janāya cid ya īvata u lokam bṛhaspatir devahūtau cakāra | ghnan vṛtrāṇi vi puro dardarīti jayañ chatrūm̐r amitrān pṛtsu sāhan ||
  • janāya cid ya īvata: This phrase can be interpreted as "for the benefit of the people" or "for the welfare of all beings." It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being and the need for actions that uplift society.
  • u lokam bṛhaspatir devahūtau cakāra: Here, "bṛhaspatir" refers to the deity Brihaspati, who is often associated with wisdom and knowledge. The phrase suggests that Brihaspati has created or established the world ("lokam") in a way that promotes harmony and prosperity.
  • ghnan vṛtrāṇi vi puro dardarīti: "Vṛtrāṇi" refers to obstacles or adversaries, and "ghnan" means to strike or overcome. This part of the mantra indicates the triumph over challenges and the removal of barriers that hinder progress.
  • jayañ chatrūm̐r amitrān pṛtsu sāhan: This phrase translates to "may we conquer our enemies and adversaries." It highlights the importance of resilience and strength in facing opposition, whether in personal life, community, or broader societal contexts.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of striving for collective welfare, wisdom, and the overcoming of obstacles. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community development projects, conflict resolution, and personal growth. For instance, in a community facing social issues, the principles of this mantra can inspire collective action towards improvement, fostering unity and resilience against challenges. The significance lies in its call for wisdom, strength, and the pursuit of a harmonious existence, reminding us that our actions should ultimately benefit the greater good.
