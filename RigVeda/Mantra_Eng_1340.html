युवं वन्दनं निरृतं जरणयया रथं न दश्रा करणā सम इन्बथः |क्षेत्राद ā विप्रं जनथो विपण्यया प्र वाम अत्र विधते दंसना भुवत ||
yuvaṁ vandanaṁ nirṛtaṁ jaraṇyayā rathaṁ na dasrā karaṇā sam invathaḥ | kṣetrād ā vipraṁ janatho vipanyayā pra vām atra vidhate daṁsanā bhuvat ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • vandanaṁ: This means "salutation" or "worship." It reflects the importance of respect and reverence in relationships and interactions.
  • nirṛtaṁ: This term refers to "destruction" or "chaos." It highlights the presence of challenges and obstacles in life.
  • jaraṇyayā: This word can be interpreted as "aging" or "decay." It serves as a reminder of the inevitable passage of time and the changes it brings.
  • rathaṁ: This means "chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the journey of life and the means by which we navigate our experiences.
  • na dasrā: This phrase translates to "not to be hindered" or "not obstructed." It emphasizes the importance of perseverance and determination.
  • karaṇā: This means "actions" or "deeds." It underscores the significance of our choices and the impact they have on our lives.
  • sam invathaḥ: This can be interpreted as "together" or "in unity." It highlights the value of collaboration and support from others.
  • kṣetrād: This word means "from the field" or "from the environment." It signifies the influence of our surroundings on our lives.
  • ā vipraṁ: This translates to "the wise one" or "the learned." It points to the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding our actions.
  • janatho: This means "from the people" or "from society." It reflects the interconnectedness of individuals within a community.
  • vipanyayā: This can be interpreted as "through knowledge" or "by understanding." It emphasizes the role of insight in overcoming challenges.
  • pra vām: This means "to you" or "towards you." It signifies a direction or intention in our actions.
  • atra: This translates to "here" or "in this place." It emphasizes the importance of being present in the moment.
  • vidhate: This means "to create" or "to establish." It highlights the power of intention in shaping our reality.
  • daṁsanā: This term refers to "teeth" or "biting." It can symbolize the challenges that can be faced and overcome.
  • bhuvat: This means "to become" or "to manifest." It signifies the process of transformation and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of youth, respect, and wisdom while acknowledging the challenges and changes that come with time. It teaches us to navigate our lives with determination and unity, drawing strength from our surroundings and the knowledge we acquire. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community building, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us to remain resilient and focused on our goals despite the obstacles we may face.
