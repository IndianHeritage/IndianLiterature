दनो विष इन्द्र मृध्रवाचः सप्त यत पुरः शर्म शारदीर दर्त् |ऋणोर अपो अनवद्यार्णा यूने वृतम् पुरुकुत्साय रन्धीः ||
dano viśa indra mṛdhravācaḥ sapta yat puraḥ śarma śāradīr dart | ṛṇor apo anavadyārṇā yūne vṛtram purukutsāya randhīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dano: wealth or riches
    • viśa: of the people or community
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, associated with strength and power
    • mṛdhravācaḥ: with sweet or pleasant words
    • sapta: seven
    • yat: which
    • puraḥ: in front or ahead
    • śarma: comfort, protection, or shelter
    • śāradīr: autumn or the season of harvest
    • dart: to strike or to overcome
    • ṛṇor: of debts or obligations
    • apo: waters or sources
    • anavadyārṇā: without blemish or pure
    • yūne: in the company or together
    • vṛtram: the demon or obstacle
    • purukutsāya: to Purukutsa, a legendary king
    • randhīḥ: to break or to remove
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to bless the community with wealth and prosperity.
    • The mention of "sweet words" suggests the importance of communication and diplomacy in achieving harmony and success.
    • The "seven" could symbolize completeness or the seven directions, indicating a holistic approach to well-being.
    • The reference to "comfort" and "protection" highlights the need for security in life, especially during challenging times.
    • The mantra also emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles (vṛtram) that hinder progress and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders can use the principles of this mantra to foster unity and collaboration, ensuring that everyone's voice is heard and valued.
    • In personal relationships, employing "sweet words" can help resolve conflicts and build stronger bonds.
    • During times of economic hardship, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek support from one another and work together to overcome financial challenges.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to "waters" can be linked to the importance of preserving natural resources for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, communication, and overcoming obstacles in the pursuit of prosperity and well-being.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community development and environmental stewardship.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, individuals and communities can strive for a harmonious and prosperous existence, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
