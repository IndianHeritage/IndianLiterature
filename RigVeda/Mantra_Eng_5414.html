तवेन्द्रावमं वसु त्वं पुष्यसि मध्यंमम् |सत्रा विश्वस्य परमस्य राजसि नकीषṭ्वा गोषु वृण्वते ||
taved indrāvamaṁ vasu tvam puṣyasi madhyamam | satrā viśvasya paramasya rājasi nakiṣ ṭvā goṣu vṛṇvate ||
  • taved indrāvamaṁ vasu tvam puṣyasi madhyamam
    • taved: This word can be interpreted as "you are" or "you are indeed." It emphasizes the presence and essence of the subject being addressed.
    • indrāvamaṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • vasu: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance and the good things in life.
    • tvam: Translates to "you," addressing the divine or the cosmic force directly.
    • puṣyasi: Means "you nourish" or "you promote growth," suggesting a nurturing aspect.
    • madhyamam: This word means "the middle" or "the center," indicating balance and harmony.
  • satrā viśvasya paramasya rājasi nakiṣ ṭvā goṣu vṛṇvate
    • satrā: Refers to a "sacrifice" or "ritual," which is a significant aspect of Vedic culture, symbolizing devotion and connection to the divine.
    • viśvasya: Means "of the universe" or "of all," indicating inclusivity and universality.
    • paramasya: Translates to "the supreme" or "the highest," referring to the ultimate reality or divine essence.
    • rājasi: Means "in the kingdom" or "in the realm," suggesting a place of authority and governance.
    • nakiṣ ṭvā: This term can be interpreted as "you are not" or "you do not," indicating a negation or absence.
    • goṣu: Refers to "cows," which in Vedic culture symbolize wealth, nourishment, and sustenance.
    • vṛṇvate: Means "they choose" or "they select," indicating a decision-making process.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the nurturing and prosperous qualities of the divine, particularly in the context of leadership and abundance. It highlights the importance of balance and harmony in the universe, suggesting that true wealth comes from a connection to the divine and the fulfillment of one's duties through rituals and sacrifices. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In leadership roles, where one must balance power with responsibility and nurture their community.
  • In personal growth, where individuals seek to nourish their talents and relationships, promoting a harmonious life.
  • In cultural practices, where rituals and traditions are observed to connect with the divine and express gratitude for abundance.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing both oneself and the community for a prosperous and balanced life.
