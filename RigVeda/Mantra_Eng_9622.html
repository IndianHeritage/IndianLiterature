ग्रावाणो न सूरयः सिंधुमातरा आदर्दिरासो अद्रयो न विश्वहा |शिशूलाऽ न क्रीळयः सुमातरो महाग्रामो न यामन्न उता त्विषा ||
grāvāṇo na sūrayaḥ sindhumātara ādardirāso adrayo na viśvahā | śiśūlā na krīḻayaḥ sumātaro mahāgrāmo na yāmann uta tviṣā ||
  • grāvāṇo na sūrayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the powerful ones do not harm." It suggests that those who possess strength or power should use it wisely and not inflict harm on others. In real life, this can be seen in leaders or individuals in positions of authority who choose to protect and uplift rather than oppress.
  • sindhumātara ādardirāso: This translates to "the mother of rivers, the ocean, does not abandon." It symbolizes the nurturing and sustaining qualities of nature, particularly water. For example, rivers and oceans provide life and resources to communities. This reminds us of the importance of preserving our natural resources and respecting the environment.
  • adrayo na viśvahā: This means "the mountains do not bear witness." It implies that nature is indifferent to human actions. Mountains stand as silent observers of human history, reminding us that our actions have consequences, and we should act with integrity. In real life, this can be related to the idea that our choices, whether good or bad, will ultimately shape our legacy.
  • śiśūlā na krīḻayaḥ: This phrase translates to "the children do not play." It can signify the seriousness of life and the responsibilities that come with maturity. In a real-world context, it can be seen in the transition from childhood to adulthood, where individuals must take on responsibilities and make decisions that affect their future.
  • sumātaro mahāgrāmo: This means "the great community of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and collective knowledge in a community. In modern society, this can be reflected in collaborative efforts, such as community projects or educational initiatives, where shared wisdom leads to better outcomes for all.
  • na yāmann uta tviṣā: This translates to "not for anyone's glory." It suggests that actions should not be motivated by personal gain or recognition. In real life, this can be seen in selfless acts of service, where individuals contribute to society without seeking fame or reward.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of using power wisely, nurturing the environment, acting with integrity, embracing responsibility, valuing collective wisdom, and performing selfless acts. Its significance lies in guiding individuals and communities towards a harmonious existence, where actions are taken for the greater good rather than personal gain. In today's world, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, environmental conservation, community service, and personal development, fostering a more compassionate and responsible society.
