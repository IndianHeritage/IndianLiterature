मन्त्र: सोमः सुतो धारणात्योन न हित्वा सिन्धुर न निम्नम अभि वाज्य अक्षाः |आ योनिं वन्यम असदत पुनानः सम इन्दुर गोभिर असरत सम अद्भिः ||
somaḥ suto dhārayātyo na hitvā sindhur na nimnam abhi vājy akṣāḥ | ā yoniṁ vanyam asadat punānaḥ sam indur gobhir asarat sam adbhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somaḥ: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • suto: Son or offspring, indicating something that is born or produced.
    • dhārayātyo: Sustaining or holding; it implies support or maintenance.
    • na hitvā: Not abandoning or leaving behind.
    • sindhur: River or ocean, symbolizing flow and abundance.
    • na nimnam: Not sinking or descending, suggesting stability.
    • abhi vājy: Towards the goal or purpose, indicating direction.
    • akṣāḥ: Eyes or vision, often representing perception or awareness.
    • ā yoniṁ: The source or origin, often referring to the womb or creative force.
    • vanyam: Wild or natural, indicating something that is untamed or free.
    • asadat: Not existing or absent, suggesting a state of lack.
    • punānaḥ: Purifying or cleansing, indicating renewal.
    • sam indur: Together with the moon, symbolizing harmony and unity.
    • gobhir: With cows or wealth, often representing prosperity.
    • asarat: Moving or flowing, indicating action or dynamism.
    • sam adbhiḥ: Together with waters, suggesting collaboration and unity.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence of Soma, which is not only a drink but also a metaphor for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The idea of sustaining and not abandoning signifies the importance of perseverance and commitment in our endeavors.
    • The river symbolizes the flow of life and abundance, reminding us to remain stable and not get overwhelmed by challenges.
    • The reference to vision and awareness emphasizes the need for clarity in our goals and the importance of perception in achieving them.
    • The concept of the source or origin highlights the significance of understanding our roots and the natural world around us.
    • Purification and renewal are essential for growth, indicating that we must cleanse ourselves of negativity to embrace new beginnings.
    • The unity with the moon and waters suggests that collaboration and harmony with nature and others are vital for success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to stay committed to their goals, much like a river that flows steadily towards the ocean.
    • In environmental conservation, the reference to nature encourages us to protect our natural resources and recognize their importance in sustaining life.
    • In relationships, the emphasis on unity and collaboration can guide us to work together harmoniously, much like the interconnectedness of ecosystems.
    • In spiritual practices, the idea of purification can motivate individuals to engage in meditation or rituals that cleanse the mind and spirit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of sustenance, commitment, and harmony with nature.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to pursue their goals with clarity and perseverance while remaining connected to their roots and the world around them.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to embrace challenges, seek renewal, and foster unity in our personal and communal endeavors.
