पुरुत्रा चिद्धि वां नराः विह्वयन्ते मनिषिणः |वाघद्भिर अश्विनागतम् ||
purutrā cid dhi vāṁ narā vihvayante manīṣiṇaḥ | vāghadbhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Purutrā: In various ways or from all sides.
    • Cid: Indeed or certainly.
    • Hī: This word can be interpreted as a particle that emphasizes the preceding word.
    • Vāṁ: Your or of you.
    • Narā: Men or people.
    • Vihvayante: They are inspired or motivated.
    • Manīṣiṇaḥ: Wise or intelligent beings.
    • Vāghadbhir: With words or through speech.
    • Aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • Gatam: Gone or departed.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks about how wise individuals are inspired from all directions and how they communicate effectively, often invoking the blessings of the Aśvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team of diverse individuals (representing "narā") can come together to brainstorm ideas ("vihvayante") from various perspectives ("purutrā"). This collaboration can lead to innovative solutions.
    • In education, teachers (wise beings) inspire students through effective communication ("vāghadbhir"), encouraging them to think critically and creatively.
    • In healthcare, practitioners may call upon the wisdom of ancient practices (symbolized by the Aśvins) to heal patients, blending traditional and modern medicine.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of collective wisdom and effective communication. It highlights the importance of drawing inspiration from various sources and the role of wise individuals in guiding others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in teamwork, education, and healthcare, reminding us that collaboration and communication are key to achieving success and well-being.
