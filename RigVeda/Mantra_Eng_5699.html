अवोर वां नूनम अश्विनाऽ युवाकुर हुवे यद वां सुते माध्वी वसुइयुः |आ वां वहन्तु स्थविरासो अश्वाः पिबाथो अस्मे सुषुतā मधूनि ||
avor vāṁ nūnam aśvinā yuvākur huve yad vāṁ sute mādhvī vasūyuḥ | ā vāṁ vahantu sthavirāso aśvāḥ pibātho asme suṣutā madhūni ||
  • Mantra: "avor vāṁ nūnam aśvinā yuvākur huve yad vāṁ sute mādhvī vasūyuḥ | ā vāṁ vahantu sthavirāso aśvāḥ pibātho asme suṣutā madhūni ||"
  • Translation: "O Ashvins, may you come to us quickly; may your sweet and nourishing gifts flow to us. May the strong steeds bring you to us, and may we drink the delightful honey."
  • Explanation:
    • Avor vāṁ nūnam: This phrase expresses a call for the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, to come quickly. In real life, this can be likened to seeking help or guidance in times of need, much like calling upon friends or mentors during challenging situations.
    • Aśvinā yuvākur huve: The Ashvins are known for their youthful energy and vitality. This can be related to the idea of youthfulness in our lives, where we often seek the vigor and enthusiasm that comes with being young, whether in age or spirit.
    • Yad vāṁ sute mādhvī vasūyuḥ: This line refers to the sweet and nourishing gifts that the Ashvins provide. In a practical sense, this can be seen as the blessings and resources we receive in life, such as love, knowledge, and support from our community.
    • Ā vāṁ vahantu sthavirāso aśvāḥ: Here, the mantra invokes the strong steeds to bring the Ashvins to us. This can symbolize the importance of having strong foundations and support systems in our lives, akin to having reliable friends or family who help us navigate challenges.
    • Pibātho asme suṣutā madhūni: This line expresses a desire to partake in the delightful honey, which can be interpreted as enjoying the fruits of our labor and the sweetness of life. It emphasizes the importance of savoring the good moments and experiences that come our way.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine assistance and the celebration of life's blessings. It teaches us to seek help when needed, appreciate the support we receive, and enjoy the sweetness of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during times of transition, such as starting a new job, moving to a new place, or facing personal challenges, reminding us to call upon our inner strength and the support of others while cherishing the positive experiences that come our way.
