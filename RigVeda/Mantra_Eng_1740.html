जगतā सिंधुं दिव्य अस्तभायद रथं tare सूर्यं पर्य अपश्यत् |गायत्रस्य समिधस् तिस्र आहुस ततो मह्ना प्र रिरिचे महित्वा ||
jagatā sindhuṁ divy astabhāyad rathaṁtare sūryam pary apaśyat | gāyatrasya samidhas tisra āhus tato mahnā pra ririce mahitvā ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "He who sees the divine ocean of the world, the sun shining in the chariot of the sky, and who is called the Gayatri, is the one who is revered for his greatness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • jagatā: of the world
    • sindhuṁ: ocean
    • divy: divine
    • astabhāyad: shining or illuminating
    • rathaṁtare: in the chariot of the sky
    • sūryam: sun
    • pary: around or surrounding
    • apaśyat: saw or perceived
    • gāyatrasya: of the Gayatri
    • samidhas: revered or honored
    • tisra: three
    • āhus: they say
    • tato: therefore
    • mahnā: by greatness
    • pra ririce: he who is revered
    • mahitvā: greatness
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The "ocean of the world" can be seen as a metaphor for the vastness of human experience and knowledge. Just as the ocean is deep and full of mysteries, so too is the world filled with opportunities for learning and growth.
    • The "sun shining in the chariot of the sky" symbolizes enlightenment and guidance. In our lives, we often look to mentors or leaders who illuminate our paths, much like the sun provides light and warmth.
    • The concept of Gayatri represents the pursuit of wisdom and understanding. In modern contexts, this can relate to education and the quest for knowledge, where individuals strive to gain insights that can lead to personal and societal betterment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on perception, enlightenment, and reverence for knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek understanding and wisdom in their lives, recognizing the vastness of the world and the importance of guidance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to pursue education, seek mentors, and appreciate the beauty and complexity of the world around them. It serves as a reminder that through knowledge and enlightenment, one can achieve greatness and contribute positively to society.
