स मृज्यमानो दशभिः सुखर्मभिः प्र मध्यमासु मातृषु प्रमे सचा |व्रतानि पानो अमृतस्य चाऽरुण उभे नृचक्षां अनु पश्यते विश्वौ ||
sa mṛjyamāno daśabhiḥ sukarmabhiḥ pra madhyamāsu mātṛṣu prame sacā | vratāni pāno amṛtasya cāruṇa ubhe nṛcakṣā anu paśyate viśau ||
  • sa mṛjyamāno: This phrase can be interpreted as "being purified" or "being cleansed." In real life, this can relate to the process of self-improvement or personal growth, where individuals strive to rid themselves of negative traits or habits.
  • daśabhiḥ sukarmabhiḥ: This translates to "by ten good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of performing virtuous actions. For example, in a community service context, engaging in ten acts of kindness can significantly impact the lives of others and foster a sense of community.
  • pra madhyamāsu mātṛṣu: This means "among the mothers who are in the middle." It can symbolize the nurturing aspect of life, where mothers or caregivers play a central role in the development of individuals. In real life, this can be seen in the way families support each other through challenges.
  • prame sacā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the measure of truth." It suggests the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions. For instance, in business, maintaining transparency and ethical practices can lead to long-term success and trust from clients.
  • vratāni pāno: This translates to "the vows of drinking." It can symbolize the commitments we make in life, whether personal or professional. For example, someone may take a vow to maintain a healthy lifestyle, which can lead to improved well-being.
  • amṛtasya cāruṇa: This means "the beautiful nectar of immortality." It signifies the pursuit of eternal truths or wisdom. In real life, this can relate to the quest for knowledge and understanding that transcends time, such as the teachings of great philosophers or spiritual leaders.
  • ubhe nṛcakṣā anu paśyate viśau: This translates to "both the human eyes see the universe." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of perspective. In real life, this can be seen in how diverse viewpoints contribute to a richer understanding of the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of personal growth through virtuous actions, the nurturing role of caregivers, the importance of truth and commitments, and the pursuit of wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, perspectives, and relationships shape our understanding of the universe. In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness among individuals.
