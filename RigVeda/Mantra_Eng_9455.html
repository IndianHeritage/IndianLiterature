अपामिवाम अप विश्वाम अनाहुतिम अपारातिं दुर्विदात्राम अग्हायतः |आरे देवाः द्वेषो अस्मद युयोतनोऽरु णः शर्मा यच्छता स्वस्तये ||
apāmīvām apa viśvām anāhutim apārātiṁ durvidatrām aghāyataḥ | āre devā dveṣo asmad yuyotanoru ṇaḥ śarma yacchatā svastaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāmīvām: "of the waters" - Refers to the divine essence of water, symbolizing purity and life.
    • apa: "away" - Suggests a removal or distancing from negativity or obstacles.
    • viśvām: "all" - Indicates inclusiveness, encompassing everything in existence.
    • anāhutim: "uninvited" - Represents the unforeseen or unwanted elements in life.
    • apārātiṁ: "without obstacles" - Signifies a state of being free from hindrances.
    • durvidatrām: "difficult to overcome" - Refers to challenges that are hard to face.
    • aghāyataḥ: "may we be protected" - A plea for safety and security.
  • Deities and Forces:
    • āre: "O deities" - A call to the divine forces for assistance.
    • devā: "gods" - Represents the higher powers that govern the universe.
    • dveṣo: "hatred" - Acknowledges the presence of negativity and conflict.
    • asmad: "us" - Refers to the collective human experience.
    • yuyotanoru: "may they unite" - A wish for harmony and cooperation among beings.
    • ṇaḥ: "for us" - Emphasizes the personal aspect of the prayer.
    • śarma: "safety" - Represents protection and well-being.
    • yacchatā: "may grant" - A request for blessings and support.
    • svastaye: "for welfare" - A focus on overall health and prosperity.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from negative influences and obstacles in life.
    • In real life, individuals often face challenges such as stress, conflict, and unforeseen circumstances. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine assistance and maintain a positive outlook.
    • For example, during difficult times, such as a health crisis or personal loss, reciting this mantra can provide comfort and a sense of security, encouraging individuals to overcome their struggles.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be applied in community settings where cooperation and unity are essential for overcoming societal challenges, such as poverty or discrimination.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates a universal plea for protection, unity, and the removal of obstacles, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
    • By invoking the divine and seeking harmony, individuals can foster resilience and a sense of community, ultimately leading to a more peaceful and prosperous existence.
