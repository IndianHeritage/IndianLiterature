माहं मघोनो वरुण प्रियस्य भूридाव्न आ विदं शूनम् आपेः |मा रायो राजन सुमाद अव स्थाम बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
māham maghono varuṇa priyasya bhūridāvna ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ | mā rāyo rājan suyamād ava sthām bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • māham maghono varuṇa: This phrase can be translated as "O Varuna, the great one." Varuna is a deity associated with the cosmic order and moral authority. In real life, this can be seen as a call for justice and righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • priyasya bhūridāvna: This means "the one who is dear and generous." It emphasizes the importance of being kind and generous in our relationships. For example, in a community, being generous with time and resources can strengthen bonds and foster a supportive environment.
  • ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ: This translates to "may we not be deprived of wealth." It reflects a desire for abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of education and skills that lead to better job opportunities and financial stability.
  • mā rāyo rājan suyamād: This phrase means "may we attain wealth and prosperity." It highlights the aspiration for success and well-being. In everyday life, this can be seen in the efforts individuals make to achieve their goals, whether in careers, education, or personal development.
  • ava sthām bṛhad vadema: This translates to "let us speak of great things." It encourages the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be applied to discussions that inspire innovation and creativity, such as brainstorming sessions in a workplace.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This means "in the assembly of the brave." It signifies the importance of community and collaboration. In practical terms, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, drawing on each other's strengths.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the values of justice, generosity, prosperity, wisdom, and community. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to strive for righteousness and abundance while fostering strong relationships and collaboration. In real life, these principles can guide personal and professional interactions, encouraging a harmonious and prosperous society.
