तं इच छ्यौत्नैर आर्यन्ति तं कृतैभिः चर्षणयः |एष इन्द्रो वरिवस्कृत ||
tam ic cyautnair āryanti taṁ kṛtebhiś carṣaṇayaḥ | eṣa indro varivaskṛt ||
  • tam ic cyautnair āryanti: This phrase can be translated to "They who are noble (ārya) seek him (tam) with their offerings (ic cyautnair)." Here, the term "ārya" refers to those who are virtuous or noble in character. The act of seeking with offerings signifies a quest for divine connection or favor.
  • taṁ kṛtebhiś carṣaṇayaḥ: This translates to "With their deeds (kṛtebhiś), they honor (carṣaṇayaḥ) him." The word "kṛtebhiś" refers to actions or deeds, indicating that the noble individuals are honoring the divine through their righteous actions.
  • eṣa indro varivaskṛt: This means "This is Indra, the one who is well adorned (varivaskṛt)." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and the warrior spirit. The term "varivaskṛt" suggests that Indra is adorned with glory and power, emphasizing his significance in the cosmic order.

  • Real-Life Example: In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the idea of seeking guidance and blessings from a higher power through virtuous actions. For instance, individuals who engage in community service or charitable acts may feel a sense of connection to a greater purpose, akin to the noble seekers in the mantra.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of noble intentions and righteous actions in seeking divine favor. It suggests that true honor comes from living a life of virtue and contributing positively to the world around us.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal development, leadership, and community engagement. By embodying the qualities of nobility and righteousness, individuals can inspire others and create a positive impact in their communities.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the divine. It encourages individuals to strive for nobility in their thoughts and deeds, reinforcing the idea that through virtuous living, one can attain a deeper connection with the divine and contribute to the greater good of society.
