अथा ते अङ्गिरस्तमाग्ने वेधस्तम प्रियं |वोचेम ब्रह्म सानसि ||
athā te aṅgirastamāgne vedhastama priyam | vocema brahma sānasi ||
  • athā te: This phrase translates to "now to you." It signifies a moment of addressing a higher power or divine entity, indicating a transition to a prayer or invocation.
  • aṅgirastamāgne: "Aṅgiras" refers to a group of sages or seers, and "tamāgne" means "O Agni," where Agni is the fire god in Vedic tradition. This part of the mantra acknowledges the divine presence of Agni, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the gods.
  • vedhastama priyam: "Vedhastama" can be interpreted as "the one who knows the essence" or "the one who is the best among the seers." "Priyam" means "beloved" or "dear." This phrase emphasizes the importance of Agni as a cherished entity who possesses profound knowledge.
  • vocema: This translates to "we speak" or "we invoke." It indicates the act of calling upon the divine, expressing a desire for connection and communication.
  • brahma sānasi: "Brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, while "sānasi" means "in the presence of." This part of the mantra signifies the aspiration to connect with the ultimate truth or divine consciousness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom. It reflects the human desire to connect with higher powers for knowledge and support in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during moments of decision-making, seeking clarity in challenging situations, or when embarking on new ventures. For instance, before starting a new project, individuals might recite this mantra to invoke inspiration and guidance from a higher source.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and guidance from the divine. It encourages individuals to recognize the presence of higher powers in their lives and to communicate their needs and aspirations. By invoking Agni and the ultimate reality, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their purpose and direction in life.
