जज्ञानो हरितो वृषा विश्वं आ भाति रोचनम् | हर्यश्वो हरितं धत्त आयुधम् आ वज्रम बाह्वोर हरिम ||
jajñāno harito vṛṣā viśvam ā bhāti rocanam | haryaśvo haritaṁ dhatta āyudham ā vajram bāhvor harim ||
  • jajñāno: This word can be translated as "born" or "created." It signifies the beginning of existence or the emergence of life.
  • harito: This term means "green" or "prosperous." It symbolizes growth, fertility, and abundance in nature.
  • vṛṣā: This word refers to "strength" or "power." It represents the vigor and vitality that is essential for life and sustenance.
  • viśvam: Translated as "the universe" or "all," it encompasses everything that exists, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ā bhāti: This phrase means "shines forth" or "illuminates." It indicates the presence of light, knowledge, and clarity in life.
  • rocanam: This word translates to "radiance" or "brightness." It signifies the beauty and brilliance found in nature and existence.
  • haryaśvo: This term can be interpreted as "the swift one" or "the one who moves quickly." It represents agility and the ability to adapt to changing circumstances.
  • haritaṁ: Meaning "green" or "fertile," it emphasizes the importance of nature and its role in sustaining life.
  • dhatta: This word means "to hold" or "to bear." It signifies the act of carrying or supporting something, which can relate to responsibilities in life.
  • āyudham: Translated as "weapon" or "tool," it symbolizes the means by which one can achieve goals or defend oneself.
  • ā vajram: This phrase means "the thunderbolt" or "the indestructible." It represents strength, resilience, and the power to overcome challenges.
  • bāhvor: This term means "of the arms." It signifies action, effort, and the physical means to achieve one's objectives.
  • harim: This word can be interpreted as "the divine" or "the one who brings prosperity." It represents the ultimate source of strength and abundance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, growth, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the importance of strength, prosperity, and the divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as personal growth, environmental sustainability, and the pursuit of knowledge. By recognizing the significance of nature and the divine in our lives, we can strive for a harmonious existence that fosters growth, resilience, and prosperity for ourselves and the world around us.
