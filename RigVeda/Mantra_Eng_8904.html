यमो नो गातुम् प्रथमो विवेद नैषा गव्यूतिर अपभर्तवा उ |यत्रा नः पूर्वे पितरः परेयुर एनाऽजजñानाः पथ्या अनु स्वाह ||
yamo no gātum prathamo viveda naiṣā gavyūtir apabhartavā u | yatrā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ pareyur enā jajñānāḥ pathyā3 anu svāḥ ||
  • yamo no gātum prathamo viveda: This phrase can be translated as "May Yama, the god of death, not take us away first." Yama represents the inevitable end of life, and this line expresses a desire for longevity and the hope to live a full life before facing death.
  • naiṣā gavyūtir apabhartavā u: This part can be interpreted as "May we not be taken away by the path of the cattle." In Vedic culture, cattle symbolize wealth and prosperity. This line suggests a wish to remain on the path of abundance and not be diverted from it.
  • yatrā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ pareyur: This translates to "Where our forefathers have gone." It acknowledges the ancestors and their journey, indicating a connection to lineage and heritage.
  • enā jajñānāḥ pathyā3 anu svāḥ: This can be understood as "May we follow the path that leads to our ancestors." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path that honors the teachings and values of those who came before us.

  • Significance: This mantra reflects a deep respect for life, the journey of ancestors, and the desire to live in accordance with their values. It serves as a reminder of the transient nature of life and the importance of making choices that lead to prosperity and fulfillment.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to reflect on their life choices and strive for a path that honors their heritage.
    • In family dynamics, it promotes the idea of passing down values and traditions from one generation to the next, ensuring that the wisdom of ancestors is not lost.
    • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts to create a prosperous environment that benefits all, much like the communal care for cattle in Vedic society.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, death, and the importance of lineage. It serves as a guiding principle for living a meaningful life, rooted in respect for the past while striving for a prosperous future. By reflecting on this mantra, individuals can find motivation to honor their ancestors and make choices that lead to a fulfilling existence.
