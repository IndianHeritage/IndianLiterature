त्वष्टा यद् वज्रं सुक्र्तं हिरण्ययं सहस्रभृष्टिं स्वपाऽवर्तयत् | धत्त इन्द्रो नारी अपांसी कर्तवेऽहन वृतं निर अपां औब्जद अर्णवम् ||
tvaṣṭā yad vajraṁ sukṛtaṁ hiraṇyayaṁ sahasrabhṛṣṭiṁ svapā avartayat | dhatta indro nary apāṁsi kartave 'han vṛtraṁ nir apām aubjad arṇavam ||
  • tvaṣṭā: This refers to the divine craftsman or the creator, often associated with the act of shaping and forming the universe.
  • yad vajraṁ: "vajra" means thunderbolt or diamond, symbolizing strength and indestructibility. This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is strong and unyielding."
  • sukṛtaṁ: This translates to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," emphasizing the importance of righteousness in one's actions.
  • hiraṇyayaṁ: Meaning "golden," this word signifies prosperity and abundance, often associated with wealth and success.
  • sahasrabhṛṣṭiṁ: This term can be understood as "thousands of blessings" or "multitudes of prosperity," indicating a vast and overwhelming abundance.
  • svapā avartayat: This phrase means "he who brings forth the dream" or "he who manifests visions," suggesting the power of creation and realization of aspirations.
  • dhatta: This means "to hold" or "to support," indicating the act of providing strength or stability.
  • indro: Referring to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing power, authority, and protection.
  • nary apāṁsi: This can be interpreted as "the waters of the earth," representing the life-giving and nurturing aspects of nature.
  • kartave 'han: This phrase means "to perform actions" or "to carry out duties," emphasizing the importance of responsibility.
  • vṛtraṁ: Referring to Vritra, a serpent or dragon that represents obstacles or challenges that need to be overcome.
  • nir apām: This translates to "without water," indicating a state of drought or lack of resources.
  • aubjad: Meaning "to be born" or "to emerge," suggesting the idea of new beginnings or creation.
  • arṇavam: This refers to the "ocean" or "vastness," symbolizing the depths of existence and the challenges that lie within.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, strength, and the overcoming of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and the blessings that come from them. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, where one must overcome challenges (like Vritra) through righteous actions and the support of the community (symbolized by Indra). It also highlights the significance of nurturing one's dreams and aspirations, akin to the abundance represented by "sahasrabhṛṣṭiṁ." Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of creation, responsibility, and the pursuit of prosperity in our lives.
