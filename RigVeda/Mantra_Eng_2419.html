इन्द्रश्च मृळयाति नो न नः पश्चादघं नशत् |भद्रम्भवाति नः पुरः ||
indraś ca mṛḻayāti no na naḥ paścād aghaṁ naśat | bhadram bhavāti naḥ puraḥ ||
  • Indraś ca mṛḻayāti: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. The term "mṛḻayāti" can be interpreted as "to soothe" or "to calm." Thus, this part of the mantra suggests that Indra has the power to calm or alleviate troubles.
  • No na naḥ paścād aghaṁ naśat: Here, "no" means "for us," and "naḥ" translates to "our." The term "paścād" means "after," while "aghaṁ" refers to "sin" or "evil." "Naśat" implies "may it not happen." This part of the mantra expresses a plea for protection from the consequences of past actions or sins.
  • Bhadram bhavāti naḥ puraḥ: "Bhadram" translates to "auspicious" or "good," and "bhavāti" means "may it become." "Naḥ" again refers to "for us," and "puraḥ" means "in front" or "ahead." This phrase is a hopeful invocation for good fortune and auspiciousness to manifest in the future.

  • Real-Life Example: In times of distress or uncertainty, individuals often seek solace and guidance. For instance, during a challenging period such as a job loss or health crisis, people may turn to spiritual practices or rituals that invoke divine support, similar to how the mantra calls upon Indra for relief and protection.
  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a universal human desire for peace, protection from past misdeeds, and the hope for a prosperous future. It reflects the importance of seeking divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges and the aspiration for a positive outcome.
  • Application in Real Life: The principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal struggles, community challenges, or even global issues. By invoking a sense of calm and seeking auspiciousness, individuals and communities can foster resilience and hope, encouraging a proactive approach to overcoming difficulties.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of seeking divine support in our lives. It encourages individuals to acknowledge their past, seek forgiveness, and look forward to a brighter future. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a mindset of positivity and resilience, essential for navigating the complexities of life.
