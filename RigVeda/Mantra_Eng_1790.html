शतभुजिभिः तं अभिहृतेर अगात पूर्बी रक्षता मरुतो यं आवत |जनं यं उग्रास तवसो विरप्शिनः पाठना शंसात तनयस्य पुष्टिषु ||
śatabhujibhis tam abhihruter aghāt pūrbhī rakṣatā maruto yam āvata | janaṁ yam ugrās tavaso virapśinaḥ pāthanā śaṁsāt tanayasya puṣṭiṣu ||
  • śatabhujibhis: This term refers to "those with a hundred arms," symbolizing strength and capability. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or organizations that possess diverse skills and resources, enabling them to tackle various challenges effectively.
  • tam abhihruter: This phrase means "to that which is being invoked." It signifies the act of calling upon a higher power or divine force for assistance. For example, in times of crisis, people often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, invoking a sense of peace and clarity.
  • aghāt: This word translates to "harm" or "injury." It highlights the importance of protection from negative influences or adversities. In everyday life, this can relate to safeguarding oneself from harmful situations, whether physical, emotional, or psychological.
  • pūrbhī: Meaning "from the past," this term emphasizes the lessons learned from previous experiences. In practical terms, reflecting on past mistakes can help individuals make better decisions in the future.
  • rakṣatā: This translates to "protection." It underscores the need for safety and security in various aspects of life, such as personal well-being, financial stability, and emotional health. For instance, communities often come together to support one another during difficult times, providing a sense of security.
  • maruto: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities, this term symbolizes the forces of nature and their power. In real life, this can be seen in how people respect and adapt to natural phenomena, such as preparing for storms or natural disasters.
  • yam āvata: This phrase means "to whom we offer our prayers." It reflects the act of devotion and surrender to a higher power. In daily life, this can manifest in various forms of worship or gratitude, fostering a sense of connection to something greater than oneself.
  • janaṁ: This word means "people" or "community." It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being and support. In real life, this can be observed in community initiatives aimed at improving the quality of life for all members.
  • yam ugrās: This translates to "to whom we offer strength." It signifies the need for empowerment and resilience. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development, where individuals seek to build their inner strength to face life's challenges.
  • tavaso: Meaning "of your power," this term highlights the significance of harnessing one's abilities. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals leverage their skills and talents to achieve their goals.
  • virapśinaḥ: This word refers to "those who are fierce." It symbolizes determination and courage. In everyday life, this can be likened to individuals who stand up for their beliefs and fight against injustice.
  • pāthanā: This translates to "through recitation." It emphasizes the power of words and their impact on reality. In practical terms, this can be observed in how affirmations and positive self-talk can influence one's mindset and outcomes.
  • śaṁsāt: Meaning "to praise," this term highlights the importance of recognition and appreciation. In real life, acknowledging the efforts of others fosters a positive environment and encourages collaboration.
  • tanayasya: This word means "of the offspring" or "descendants." It signifies the continuity of life and the importance of nurturing future generations. In practical terms, this can be seen in how families and communities invest in education and upbringing.
  • puṣṭiṣu: This translates to "in prosperity." It emphasizes the desire for abundance and well-being. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of a fulfilling life, where individuals strive for both material and spiritual wealth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking protection, strength, and prosperity through divine invocation and community support. Its significance lies in the reminder that individuals are not alone in their struggles; they can draw upon their inner strength, the support of their community, and the guidance of higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages people to reflect on their past, protect themselves from harm, and work collectively towards a prosperous future, fostering resilience and empowerment in the face of challenges.
