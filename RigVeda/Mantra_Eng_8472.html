विपश्चिते पवमानाय गायत मही न धारात्य अन्धो अर्षति |अहिर न जूर्णाम् अति सर्पति त्वचम् अत्यो न क्रीळन्न असरद वृषा हरिः ||
vipaścite pavamānāya gāyata mahī na dhārāty andho arṣati | ahir na jūrṇām ati sarpati tvacam atyo na krīḻann asarad vṛṣā hariḥ ||
  • vipaścite: This term refers to the wise or the knowledgeable. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in understanding the world around us.
  • pavamānāya: This word means "to the purifying one" or "to the one who is in motion." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the need for purification.
  • gāyata: This translates to "sing" or "chant." It highlights the significance of expression and communication in human experience.
  • mahī: Meaning "the great" or "the earth," it symbolizes the vastness of existence and the grounding force of nature.
  • na dhārāty: This phrase means "does not hold" or "does not support." It suggests the transient nature of material possessions and attachments.
  • andho: This word means "blind." It can refer to ignorance or lack of awareness in life.
  • arṣati: This term means "to rise" or "to ascend." It indicates the potential for growth and elevation in one's life.
  • ahir: This translates to "serpent." In many cultures, serpents symbolize transformation and renewal.
  • na jūrṇām: Meaning "not old" or "not decayed," it emphasizes the importance of freshness and vitality in life.
  • ati sarpati: This phrase means "exceedingly swift" or "moving quickly." It reflects the fast pace of life and the need to adapt.
  • tvacam: This word means "skin" or "body." It represents the physical aspect of existence.
  • atyō: This translates to "beyond" or "exceeding." It suggests transcending limitations.
  • na krīḻann: Meaning "not playing" or "not engaging," it indicates the seriousness of life and the need for focus.
  • asarad: This term means "without season" or "timeless." It signifies the eternal nature of certain truths.
  • vṛṣā: This translates to "the bull" or "strength." It symbolizes power and stability.
  • hariḥ: This word means "the remover of sins" or "the divine." It represents the spiritual aspect of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the nature of existence, emphasizing the importance of knowledge, purification, and the transient nature of life. It encourages individuals to rise above ignorance and material attachments, embracing transformation and vitality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, mindfulness practices, and spiritual journeys, reminding us to seek wisdom and remain grounded while navigating the complexities of life.
