प्रेष्ठं वो अतिथिं गृणीषे 'ग्निं शस्तिभिः तुर्वणिः सजोषाः |असद यथा नो वरुणः सुखीर्तिर इषश्च पार्षद अरिगूर्तः सूरीः ||
preṣṭhaṁ vo atithiṁ gṛṇīṣe 'gniṁ śastibhis turvaṇiḥ sajoṣāḥ | asad yathā no varuṇaḥ sukīrtir iṣaś ca parṣad arigūrtaḥ sūriḥ ||
  • Mantra: "preṣṭhaṁ vo atithiṁ gṛṇīṣe 'gniṁ śastibhis turvaṇiḥ sajoṣāḥ | asad yathā no varuṇaḥ sukīrtir iṣaś ca parṣad arigūrtaḥ sūriḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • preṣṭhaṁ: Most beloved or esteemed
    • vo: To you
    • atithiṁ: Guest or visitor
    • gṛṇīṣe: I sing or praise
    • agniṁ: Fire (often symbolizes divine presence)
    • śastibhis: With auspiciousness or blessings
    • turvaṇiḥ: The one who is powerful or strong
    • sajoṣāḥ: With joy or happiness
    • asad: As if or like
    • yathā: As or like
    • no: Us
    • varuṇaḥ: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • sukīrtir: Good fame or reputation
    • iṣaś: Of the powerful
    • ca: And
    • parṣad: Assembly or gathering
    • arigūrtaḥ: Not to be defeated or harmed
    • sūriḥ: The one who is radiant or brilliant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of honoring guests and the divine presence represented by fire (Agni).
    • It suggests that by praising and welcoming guests, one invites blessings and auspiciousness into their life.
    • The reference to Varuna highlights the significance of maintaining cosmic order and good reputation, which can be likened to the importance of integrity in personal and professional relationships.
    • The mantra also conveys a sense of protection and strength, suggesting that those who uphold these values will not be harmed.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, hospitality is a revered value. For instance, in Middle Eastern cultures, guests are treated with utmost respect and generosity, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, maintaining a good reputation (sukīrtir) can lead to successful partnerships and collaborations, akin to the blessings invoked in the mantra.
    • During festivals, lighting a fire (Agni) symbolizes the welcoming of divine energy and blessings, similar to the invocation in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of hospitality, respect for guests, and the pursuit of a good reputation.
    • It encourages individuals to create a welcoming environment, fostering joy and blessings in their lives.
    • In essence, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in everyday interactions, promoting harmony and strength in both personal and communal relationships.
