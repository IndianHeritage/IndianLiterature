आ ये राजांसी तविषीभिर अव्यत प्र व एवासः स्वयतासो अध्रजन |भयन्ते विश्वा भुवनानि हरम्याः चित्रो वो यामः प्रयतास्व ऋष्टिषु ||
ā ye rajāṁsi taviṣībhir avyata pra va evāsaḥ svayatāso adhrajan | bhayante viśvā bhuvanāni harmyā citro vo yāmaḥ prayatāsv ṛṣṭiṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā ye: O those who
    • rajāṁsi: kings or rulers
    • taviṣībhir: with your strength or power
    • avyata: have not been disturbed
    • pra va: come forth
    • evāsaḥ: indeed, you are
    • svayatāso: self-illuminated or self-sufficient
    • adhrajan: without any hindrance
    • bhayante: may they be free from fear
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • bhuvanāni: worlds or realms
    • harmyā: in the mansions or abodes
    • citro: colorful or diverse
    • vo: for you
    • yāmaḥ: may we attain
    • prayatāsv: through efforts
    • ṛṣṭiṣu: in the rituals or sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the rulers or leaders, invoking their strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of self-sufficiency and the ability to remain undisturbed in the face of challenges.
    • The mantra seeks to ensure that all beings are free from fear and can thrive in their respective realms.
    • It highlights the beauty and diversity of existence, suggesting that through collective efforts and rituals, one can attain harmony and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who remain calm and composed during crises can inspire their teams to overcome challenges, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • Community leaders who promote inclusivity and diversity can create a harmonious environment, ensuring that all members feel safe and valued.
    • In personal life, individuals who practice self-sufficiency and resilience can navigate life's difficulties without being easily disturbed.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strong, self-sufficient leadership that fosters a safe and harmonious environment for all.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to community building and organizational leadership.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and leaders can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous society.
