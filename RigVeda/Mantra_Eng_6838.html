इमां धियं शिक्षमानस्य देव क्रतुं दक्षं वरुण सं शिशाधि |ययाति विश्वा दुरिता tarema सुतर्माणम अधि नावं रुहेमा ||
imāṁ dhiyaṁ śikṣamāṇasya deva kratuṁ dakṣaṁ varuṇa saṁ śiśādhi | yayāti viśvā duritā tarema sutarmāṇam adhi nāvaṁ ruhema ||
  • imāṁ dhiyaṁ: This phrase translates to "this intellect" or "this understanding." It emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and wisdom in decision-making.
  • śikṣamāṇasya: This means "of one who is learning" or "of the one who is being educated." It highlights the value of continuous learning and growth.
  • deva kratuṁ: "Divine intention" or "the will of the gods." This suggests that our actions should align with a higher purpose or divine will.
  • dakṣaṁ: This word means "skilled" or "capable." It indicates the need for competence and skillfulness in our endeavors.
  • varuṇa: Varuṇa is a deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. Invoking Varuṇa signifies a call for righteousness and adherence to universal laws.
  • saṁ śiśādhi: This translates to "may you establish" or "may you set." It is a plea for the establishment of wisdom and understanding.
  • yayāti: This term refers to "to attain" or "to achieve." It signifies the pursuit of goals and aspirations.
  • viśvā: Meaning "all" or "universal," it emphasizes inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • duritā tarema: This phrase means "may we overcome difficulties." It is a prayer for strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • sutarmāṇam: This translates to "the well-constructed" or "the well-formed." It refers to the importance of building a strong foundation in life.
  • adhi nāvaṁ: Meaning "upon the boat," it symbolizes the journey of life and the need for guidance and support.
  • ruhema: This means "may we be prosperous" or "may we thrive." It is a wish for abundance and success in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, skill, and divine guidance in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of continuous learning and the pursuit of righteousness while navigating the challenges of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, professional development, and personal growth. By invoking the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for excellence, overcome obstacles, and achieve their goals with a sense of purpose and connection to the greater cosmos.
