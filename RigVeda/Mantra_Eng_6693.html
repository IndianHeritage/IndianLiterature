स गोर अश्वस्य वि व्रजं मन्दानः सोम्येभ्यः |पुरं न शूर दर्षसि ||
sa gor aśvasya vi vrajam mandānaḥ somyebhyaḥ | puraṁ na śūra darṣasi ||
  • sa: This word translates to "that" or "he," indicating a subject or focus in the mantra.
  • gor: This means "of the cow," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes nourishment, wealth, and abundance.
  • aśvasya: This translates to "of the horse," representing strength, speed, and vitality.
  • vi: This is a prefix meaning "in" or "among," suggesting a sense of inclusion or presence.
  • vrajam: This means "herd" or "group," often referring to a collective of beings or entities.
  • mandānaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "pleasant" or "delightful," indicating a positive quality.
  • somyebhyaḥ: This translates to "from the pleasant ones," which can refer to those who are joyful or blissful.
  • puraṁ: This means "city" or "place," symbolizing a community or a gathering of people.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • śūra: This translates to "brave" or "heroic," referring to qualities of courage and valor.
  • darṣasi: This means "you see" or "you perceive," indicating awareness or insight.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the abundance and strength present in a community, emphasizing the importance of unity and the positive qualities that arise from it. It suggests that in a place where there is no bravery or heroism, the essence of joy and pleasantness may be diminished.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra speaks of a herd and a city, it highlights the importance of coming together as a community. For example, in a neighborhood, when residents collaborate on community projects, they create a supportive environment that fosters joy and strength.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, the collective effort of employees can lead to a thriving environment. When team members support each other and exhibit bravery in their roles, the organization flourishes, much like the pleasantness described in the mantra.
  • Personal Relationships: In personal relationships, recognizing and nurturing the strengths of each individual can lead to a harmonious and joyful connection. Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of positive qualities, relationships thrive when partners uplift each other.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of community, strength, and positivity. It encourages individuals to foster bravery and unity in their surroundings, leading to a more joyful and abundant life. By applying these principles in various aspects of life, one can create a nurturing environment that benefits all.
