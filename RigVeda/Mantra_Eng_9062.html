बृहन्नच्छायो अपलाशो अर्वा तस्थौ माता विषितो अत्ति गर्भः |अन्यस्य वत्सं रिहती मिमाया कया भुवा नि दधे धेनुर ऊधः ||
bṛhann acchāyo apalāśo arvā tasthau mātā viṣito atti garbhaḥ | anyasyā vatsaṁ rihatī mimāya kayā bhuvā ni dadhe dhenur ūdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhann: vast, great
    • acchāya: covering, protection
    • apalāśo: without any hindrance
    • arvā: the one who is strong or powerful
    • tasthau: stood, established
    • mātā: mother
    • viṣito: surrounded, enveloped
    • atti: to consume, to take
    • garbhaḥ: womb, embryo
    • anyasyā: of another
    • vatsaṁ: calf, offspring
    • rihatī: to nourish, to support
    • mimāya: to create, to manifest
    • kayā: by which
    • bhuvā: earth, world
    • ni: not
    • dadhe: to place, to establish
    • dhenur: cow, nurturing
    • ūdhaḥ: upward, elevated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness and protective nature of the divine, often represented as a mother figure.
    • The imagery of a mother enveloping her child signifies nurturing and unconditional love.
    • The mention of the womb (garbhaḥ) indicates the potential for new life and creation, emphasizing the importance of nurturing environments.
    • The reference to the cow (dhenur) symbolizes abundance and sustenance, highlighting the role of nature in providing for life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a mother’s love and protection can be seen as a parallel to the divine nurturing described in the mantra.
    • In agriculture, the cow is revered for its role in providing milk and sustenance, reflecting the importance of nurturing and caring for the environment.
    • In community settings, the idea of supporting one another, much like a mother supports her child, can foster a sense of belonging and care.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing, protection, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of creating supportive environments, whether in families, communities, or nature.
    • In real life, applying the principles of nurturing and protection can lead to healthier relationships and a more sustainable world.
