स नः शक्रश्चिदाशकद् दानवां अन्तराभरः |इन्द्रो विश्वाभिरूतिभिः ||
sa naḥ śakraś cid ā śakad dānavām̐ antarābharaḥ | indro viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • sa naḥ - "May he (Indra) be with us" - This phrase expresses a plea for the presence and support of the divine, specifically Indra, who is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature.
  • śakraś - "the powerful" - Refers to Indra's strength and capability. In real life, this can be likened to seeking the strength and support of a leader or mentor who can guide us through challenges.
  • cid - "consciousness" or "awareness" - This word emphasizes the importance of awareness in our actions and decisions. In everyday life, being conscious of our surroundings and the impact of our choices is crucial.
  • ā - "to" or "towards" - This preposition indicates direction, suggesting that we are seeking something from Indra.
  • śakad - "able" or "capable" - This reinforces the idea of strength and capability, highlighting the need for assistance from a powerful source.
  • dānavām̐ - "the demons" - Symbolically represents obstacles, challenges, or negative forces in our lives. In a real-world context, this could refer to personal struggles, societal issues, or internal conflicts.
  • antarābharaḥ - "who is within" - This suggests that the divine presence is not only external but also internal, indicating that we have the strength within ourselves to overcome challenges.
  • indro - "Indra" - The deity being invoked, representing power, strength, and protection.
  • viśvābhir - "with all" - This phrase emphasizes inclusivity and the collective strength of the community or society.
  • ūtibhiḥ - "with praises" - This indicates that we should approach the divine with respect and gratitude, acknowledging the support we seek.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa naḥ śakraś cid ā śakad dānavām̐ antarābharaḥ | indro viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ ||" serves as a powerful invocation for strength, awareness, and divine support in overcoming challenges. It highlights the importance of seeking help from a higher power while also recognizing our inner strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of difficulty, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. By invoking the support of a powerful force like Indra, we remind ourselves of the collective strength we possess and the importance of gratitude in our journey. This mantra encourages us to be aware of our surroundings and to seek assistance, both from within and from the community around us.
