इन्द्रावरुणा युवम् अध्वराय नो विशे जनाय महि शर्म यच्छतम् |दीर्घप्रयज्युम् अति यो वनुष्यति वयं जयेम पृतनासु दूद्यः ||
indrāvaruṇā yuvam adhvarāya no viśe janāya mahi śarma yacchatam | dīrghaprayajyum ati yo vanuṣyati vayaṁ jayema pṛtanāsu dūḍhyaḥ ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This refers to the divine pair of Indra and Varuna, who are often associated with rain, thunder, and cosmic order. Indra is the king of gods, while Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order.
  • Yuvam: This means "you two" or "together," indicating a partnership or collaboration between the two deities.
  • Adhvarāya: This term refers to a ritual or sacrifice, suggesting that the mantra is invoked during a sacred ceremony.
  • No viśe janāya: This translates to "for the people," indicating that the blessings sought are for the community or society at large.
  • Mahi śarma yacchatam: This phrase means "may you grant great protection," emphasizing the desire for safety and well-being.
  • Dīrghaprayajyum: This means "long-lasting strength" or "endurance," suggesting a wish for sustained vitality and resilience.
  • Ati yo vanuṣyati: This translates to "whoever strives," indicating that effort and determination are essential for achieving success.
  • Vayaṁ jayema: This means "may we conquer," expressing a collective aspiration for victory and success.
  • Pṛtanāsu dūḍhyaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the battles," referring to challenges or struggles that one may face in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and community well-being in the face of challenges. It highlights the need for collaboration, effort, and resilience to achieve success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various situations such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, or personal challenges where support from others and a strong will are essential for overcoming obstacles. The invocation of Indra and Varuna symbolizes the hope for protection and strength, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles and that collective efforts can lead to triumph.
