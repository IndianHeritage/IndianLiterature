आ नो बृहन्ता बृहतीभिर ऊती इन्द्र यातं वरुण वाजसातौ |यद दिद्यवः पृथनासु प्रकीर्णान तस्य वां स्याम सनितारा आजेः ||
ā no bṛhantā bṛhatībhir ūtī indra yātaṁ varuṇa vājasātau | yad didyavaḥ pṛtanāsu prakrīḻān tasya vāṁ syāma sanitāra ājeḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: us
    • bṛhantā: with greatness
    • bṛhatībhir: with the great ones
    • ūtī: blessings or prosperity
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • yātaṁ: may you go
    • varuṇa: O Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • vājasātau: with strength and vigor
    • yad: whatever
    • didyavaḥ: we may achieve
    • pṛtanāsu: in our endeavors
    • prakrīḻān: in our actions
    • tasya: of that
    • vāṁ: we
    • syāma: may we be
    • sanitāra: with protection
    • ājeḥ: in our pursuits
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and support from the divine forces, specifically Indra and Varuna.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking greatness and blessings in our endeavors.
    • The invocation of Indra and Varuna signifies the need for strength, protection, and cosmic order in our actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited before starting a new project, seeking the blessings of prosperity and success.
    • Students may chant this mantra before exams, asking for guidance and protection in their academic pursuits.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals may invoke this mantra to ensure a harmonious and prosperous journey ahead.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving greatness and success in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek strength and protection from higher powers while engaging in our endeavors.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal, academic, and professional contexts, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
