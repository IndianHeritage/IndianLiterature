इळाम अग्ने पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्ने सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "of great glory" or "of great fame," highlighting Agni's revered status in Vedic literature and his importance in rituals.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cattle," indicating Agni's role in safeguarding wealth and prosperity, often represented by cattle in ancient societies.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This translates to "the eternal" or "the most enduring," emphasizing the timeless nature of Agni and his presence in the universe.
  • havamānāya: This means "to the one who is invoked," referring to the act of calling upon Agni during rituals and sacrifices.
  • sādha: This word means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," indicating the desire for Agni to help achieve one's goals.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This translates to "may our son be," expressing a wish for progeny and the continuation of lineage.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This means "may he be wise, O Agni," indicating the hope for wisdom and intelligence in the offspring.
  • sā te sumatir bhūtv asme: This translates to "may he have good thoughts from you," emphasizing the importance of positive thinking and guidance from the divine.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking blessings for wisdom, prosperity, and the well-being of one's family, particularly the children. It reflects the values of ancient Vedic society, where the divine was invoked for guidance and support in daily life.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be recited during family gatherings, rituals, or significant life events such as weddings or naming ceremonies. It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and positive intentions in raising children and nurturing relationships.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance for prosperity, wisdom, and the well-being of future generations. It highlights the importance of invoking higher powers in our lives, fostering a sense of connection to tradition, and the values of family and community.
