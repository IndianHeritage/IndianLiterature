तव व्रते सुभगासः स्याम स्वाध्यो वरुण तुष्टुवांसः |उपायन उषसां गोमतीनाम अग्नयो न जरामाणानु dyūn ||
tava vrate subhagāsaḥ syāma svādhyo varuṇa tuṣṭuvāṁsaḥ | upāyana uṣasāṁ gomatīnām agnayo na jaramāṇā anu dyūn ||
  • tava vrate subhagāsaḥ syāma: This phrase can be translated as "In your vow, O Varuna, may we be auspicious." Here, "tava vrate" refers to the vows or commitments made to the divine, while "subhagāsaḥ" signifies auspiciousness or good fortune. In real life, this can be related to the commitments we make in our personal and professional lives, where we seek blessings for success and positivity.
  • svādhyo varuṇa tuṣṭuvāṁsaḥ: This translates to "May we, who are devoted to you, O Varuna, be satisfied." "Svādhyo" indicates devotion or dedication, and "tuṣṭuvāṁsaḥ" refers to being content or fulfilled. This can be seen in the context of individuals who dedicate themselves to their work or spiritual practices, seeking fulfillment and satisfaction in their endeavors.
  • upāyana uṣasāṁ gomatīnām: This part means "The means of approach to the dawn, O cows." "Upāyana" refers to the means or methods, "uṣasāṁ" signifies dawn or new beginnings, and "gomatīnām" relates to the cows, which are often symbolic of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature. In a practical sense, this can be interpreted as the various paths we take to achieve new beginnings in our lives, whether through education, career changes, or personal growth.
  • agnayo na jaramāṇā anu dyūn: This translates to "May the fires not grow old and may they reach the heavens." "Agnayo" refers to the sacred fires, which symbolize energy and transformation, while "na jaramāṇā" means not growing old, and "anu dyūn" signifies reaching the heavens. This can be related to the idea of maintaining our inner fire or passion throughout life, ensuring that we continue to strive for our goals and aspirations without losing our enthusiasm.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion, auspiciousness, and the pursuit of fulfillment in our lives. It encourages individuals to remain committed to their vows and seek blessings for success and satisfaction. The references to dawn and sacred fires highlight the significance of new beginnings and maintaining passion. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to stay dedicated to our goals, embrace new opportunities, and nurture our inner drive, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
