अयं वेनश्चोदयत पृष्णिगर्भा ज्योतिजरायू रजसो विमाने |इमम् अपां संगमे सूर्यस्य शिशुं न विप्रा मतीभिः रिहन्ति ||
ayaṁ venaś codayat pṛśnigarbhā jyotirjarāyū rajaso vimāne | imam apāṁ saṁgame sūryasya śiśuṁ na viprā matibhī rihanti ||
  • ayaṁ venaś codayat: This phrase can be translated as "this (mantra) inspires the flow of energy." In real life, this can be likened to the motivation one feels when pursuing a goal, such as studying for an important exam or preparing for a significant presentation. Just as the mantra invokes energy, individuals often seek inspiration from mentors or motivational speakers to energize their pursuits.
  • pṛśnigarbhā jyotirjarāyū: This part refers to "the light born from the womb of the Earth." It symbolizes the nurturing aspect of nature, akin to how a mother nurtures her child. In practical terms, this can be seen in agriculture, where the Earth provides sustenance and life through crops, reflecting the nurturing qualities of nature.
  • rajaso vimāne: This translates to "in the vehicle of the mind." It suggests that our thoughts and intentions are like vehicles that carry us through life. For example, a person with a positive mindset can navigate challenges more effectively, similar to how a well-designed vehicle can traverse difficult terrains.
  • imam apāṁ saṁgame: This means "in the union of waters." It signifies the importance of collaboration and unity. In real life, this can be observed in teamwork, where individuals come together, pooling their resources and skills to achieve a common goal, much like rivers merging to form a larger body of water.
  • sūryasya śiśuṁ: This translates to "the child of the Sun." It symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge. In practical terms, this can be related to education, where knowledge is often seen as a guiding light, illuminating the path for individuals to follow in their personal and professional lives.
  • na viprā matibhī rihanti: This means "the wise do not lose their understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and clarity of thought. In real life, this can be seen in decision-making processes, where individuals who remain calm and collected are more likely to make sound choices, even in stressful situations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of inspiration, nurturing, collaboration, enlightenment, and wisdom. Its significance lies in its ability to guide individuals in their personal and collective journeys. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek motivation, appreciate the nurturing aspects of nature, work collaboratively, pursue knowledge, and maintain clarity in decision-making. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate the complexities of life more effectively and harmoniously.
