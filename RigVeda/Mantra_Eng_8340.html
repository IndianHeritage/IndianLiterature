हरिम मृजन्त्य अरुषो न युज्यते सं धेनुभिः कलशे सोमो अज्यते |उद वाचम ईरयति हिन्वते मती पुरुष्टुतस्य कति चित परिप्रियः ||
harim mṛjanty aruṣo na yujyate saṁ dhenubhiḥ kalaśe somo ajyate | ud vācam īrayati hinvate matī puruṣṭutasya kati cit paripriyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • harim: The divine or the one who removes obstacles.
    • mṛjanty: They cleanse or purify.
    • aruṣo: The red or the bright one, often referring to the sun.
    • na yujyate: Is not joined or connected.
    • saṁ dhenubhiḥ: With the cows or the nurturing beings.
    • kalaśe: In the pot or vessel.
    • somo: The moon or the soma, a sacred drink.
    • ajyate: Is poured or offered.
    • ud: Up or above.
    • vācam: Speech or words.
    • īrayati: Inspires or brings forth.
    • hinvate: They drive away or remove.
    • matī: Intellect or understanding.
    • puruṣṭutasya: Of the one who is praised or honored.
    • kati cit: Some or a few.
    • paripriyaḥ: Beloved or cherished.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence that purifies and brings clarity.
    • The reference to the sun and the moon symbolizes the balance of energy and nourishment.
    • The act of pouring soma signifies the offering of oneself to the divine, enhancing spiritual connection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of speech and intellect in driving away ignorance and confusion.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are honored and cherished are connected to this divine energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate this mantra to the practice of meditation, where individuals seek to purify their thoughts and emotions.
    • The act of offering food or drink in rituals can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, similar to the pouring of soma.
    • In relationships, the idea of cherishing and honoring others aligns with the mantra's message of connection and respect.
    • Using positive speech and communication can drive away negativity, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the power of words.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of purification, connection to the divine, and the power of speech and intellect.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek clarity and understanding in our lives, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and others.
    • Incorporating its teachings can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling life, where we honor the divine within and around us.
