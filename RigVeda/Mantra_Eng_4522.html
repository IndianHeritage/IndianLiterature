सुवीरं रयिम् आ भर जातवेदो विचर्षणे |जहि रक्षांसी सुक्रतो ||
suvīraṁ rayim ā bhara jātavedo vicarṣaṇe | jahi rakṣāṁsi sukrato ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suvīraṁ: Strong, valiant, or powerful.
    • rayim: Wealth or prosperity.
    • ā bhara: Bring forth or bestow.
    • jātavedo: O Agni, the one who knows all births or the divine fire.
    • vicarṣaṇe: In the act of moving or wandering.
    • jahi: Destroy or remove.
    • rakṣāṁsi: Obstacles or protections.
    • sukrato: By good deeds or virtuous actions.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the divine fire, asking for strength and prosperity. It seeks the removal of obstacles through virtuous actions, emphasizing the importance of good deeds in achieving success and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, an entrepreneur may recite this mantra to invoke strength and prosperity in their ventures, while also committing to ethical practices to overcome challenges.
    • In personal life, an individual facing difficulties may use this mantra as a reminder to focus on their strengths and virtues, seeking to eliminate negative influences through positive actions.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the connection between inner strength, ethical behavior, and external success.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity comes not just from material wealth but also from virtuous living and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • In daily life, individuals can apply this mantra by setting goals that align with their values, fostering resilience, and actively working to remove negativity from their lives.
