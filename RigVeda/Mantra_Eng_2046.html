घृतं मिमिक्षे घृतम् अस्य योनिर घृते श्रितो घृतम् व अस्य धाम | अनुष्वधम आ वह मादयस्व स्वाहाकृतं वृषभ वक्षि हव्यं ||
ghṛtam mimikṣe ghṛtam asya yonir ghṛte śrito ghṛtam v asya dhāma | anuṣvadham ā vaha mādayasva svāhākṛtaṁ vṛṣabha vakṣi havyam ||
  • ghṛtam mimikṣe: This phrase translates to "I invoke the ghee." Ghee, or clarified butter, is considered a sacred substance in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and nourishment.
  • ghṛtam asya yonir: This means "the source of ghee." It signifies the origin or the divine source from which nourishment and sustenance arise.
  • ghṛte śrito: This translates to "in the ghee, may we be established." It suggests a desire to be grounded in purity and abundance, reflecting the importance of being rooted in positive and nourishing elements in life.
  • ghṛtam v asya dhāma: This means "the abode of ghee." It indicates a place or state where purity and nourishment reside, emphasizing the significance of creating a harmonious environment.
  • anuṣvadham ā vaha: This translates to "bring forth the sweet." It is a call to manifest sweetness and positivity in life, akin to the sweetness of ghee, which is often used in offerings and celebrations.
  • mādayasva: This means "may you delight." It expresses a wish for joy and happiness, highlighting the importance of finding delight in the simple and pure aspects of life.
  • svāhākṛtaṁ: This translates to "offered with reverence." It signifies the act of offering with respect and devotion, which is a fundamental aspect of Vedic rituals.
  • vṛṣabha vakṣi havyam: This means "the bull, the offering." The bull symbolizes strength and fertility, and this phrase emphasizes the importance of strong and fruitful offerings in rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of purity, nourishment, and joy in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek out and establish themselves in environments that promote positivity and abundance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by creating spaces—both physical and emotional—that are nurturing and supportive. For instance, in a family setting, fostering a loving and caring atmosphere can lead to happiness and growth. In professional environments, promoting teamwork and collaboration can yield fruitful results. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to honor the sacredness of life and the importance of our offerings, both in rituals and in our daily interactions with others.
