अश्वासो न ये ज्येष्ठास आाशवो दिधिषवो न रथ्यः सुदानवः |आपो न निम्नैर उदभिर जिगत्नवो विश्वरूपा अंगिरसो न सामभिः ||
aśvāso na ye jyeṣṭhāsa āśavo didhiṣavo na rathyaḥ sudānavaḥ | āpo na nimnair udabhir jigatnavo viśvarūpā aṅgiraso na sāmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvāso: horses
    • na: not
    • ye: those who
    • jyeṣṭhāsa: are the eldest or superior
    • āśavo: swift or powerful
    • didhiṣavo: those who are determined
    • na: not
    • rathyaḥ: chariots
    • sudānavaḥ: well-formed or excellent
    • āpo: waters
    • na: not
    • nimnair: lower or inferior
    • udabhir: by the waters
    • jigatnavo: those who strive or work hard
    • viśvarūpā: of universal form
    • aṅgiraso: of the Angirasas (a group of sages)
    • na: not
    • sāmabhiḥ: by hymns or chants
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of strength, determination, and the essence of striving for excellence.
    • The mention of "horses" and "chariots" symbolizes power and the means to achieve goals.
    • The phrase "not those who are the eldest" suggests that age or seniority does not necessarily equate to superiority in capability or effectiveness.
    • "Waters" represent the flow of life and knowledge, indicating that one should not be limited by lower or inferior sources.
    • The term "universal form" implies a holistic approach to understanding and achieving one's goals.
    • The reference to "Angirasas" connects to wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge through chants and hymns, emphasizing the importance of learning and spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a young employee may outperform a senior colleague, demonstrating that talent and hard work can surpass experience.
    • In sports, a team that works cohesively and strives for excellence can defeat a more experienced team, showcasing the power of determination.
    • In education, students from diverse backgrounds can excel by leveraging their unique strengths and knowledge, rather than being held back by perceived limitations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of striving for excellence, regardless of age or status.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, and to work hard towards their goals without being hindered by inferior sources.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as education, career, and personal development, reminding us that determination and effort are key to success.
