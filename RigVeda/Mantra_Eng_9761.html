प्रत्याग्ने हरसां हरः श्रिणीहि विश्वतः प्रति |यातुधानस्य रक्षसो बलं वि रुज वीर्यम् ||
praty agne harasā haraḥ śṛṇīhi viśvataḥ prati | yātudhānasya rakṣaso balaṁ vi ruja vīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • praty: towards
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • harasā: with great strength
    • haraḥ: O remover (of obstacles)
    • śṛṇīhi: listen
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
    • prati: towards
    • yātudhānasya: of the sorcerer or evil spirit
    • rakṣaso: of the demons
    • balaṁ: strength
    • vi: indeed
    • ruja: pain or suffering
    • vīryam: valor or power
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his strength and protection against evil forces and sorcery. It emphasizes the need for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and challenges posed by malevolent entities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or protection, similar to invoking Agni for strength against adversities.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to ward off negative energies or influences, akin to the protective aspect of this mantra.
    • In modern contexts, people may turn to meditation or positive affirmations to combat feelings of fear or anxiety, paralleling the call for strength and valor in the face of challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and protection. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking help from higher powers when faced with difficulties, whether they be internal struggles or external threats. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various situations where individuals feel overwhelmed by negativity or adversity, encouraging them to find strength within and seek support from the divine or their community.
