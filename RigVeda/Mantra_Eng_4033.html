हयो न विद्वांऽ अयुजि स्वयṁ धुरी तां वहामि प्रतरणीम् अवस्युवम् |नास्याऽ वश्मि विमुचं नावृतं पुनर विद्वान् पथः पुरेत ऋजु नेषति ||
hayo na vidvām̐ ayuji svayaṁ dhuri tāṁ vahāmi prataraṇīm avasyuvam | nāsyā vaśmi vimucaṁ nāvṛtam punar vidvān pathaḥ puraeta ṛju neṣati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hayo: horse, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • na: not.
    • vidvām̐: I know, or I am knowledgeable.
    • ayuj: unrestrained or free.
    • svayaṁ: by myself or on my own.
    • dhuri: burden or responsibility.
    • tāṁ: that.
    • vahāmi: I carry or I bear.
    • prataraṇīm: the crossing or the path.
    • avasyuvam: I will surely achieve or attain.
    • nāsyā: of her (the path).
    • vaśmi: I am bound or tied.
    • vimucaṁ: release or liberation.
    • nāvṛtam: not turned back or not reversed.
    • punar: again.
    • vidvān: the knowledgeable one.
    • pathaḥ: of the path.
    • puraeta: may go forth or proceed.
    • ṛju: straight or direct.
    • neṣati: leads or guides.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of life and the responsibilities we carry.
    • The horse symbolizes strength and the ability to move forward, representing our potential to overcome obstacles.
    • The speaker expresses a sense of independence and self-reliance in navigating life's challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in choosing the right path.
    • The mantra suggests that while one may feel bound by circumstances, there is always a way to achieve liberation and move forward.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges that seem overwhelming. By relying on their knowledge and skills (like the horse's strength), they can navigate through these challenges and find success.
    • In personal relationships, one may feel constrained by misunderstandings. However, through open communication and wisdom, they can find a way to resolve conflicts and strengthen their bonds.
    • In education, students often face difficulties in their studies. By seeking knowledge and guidance, they can overcome these hurdles and achieve their academic goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength and knowledge we possess to navigate life's journey.
    • It encourages individuals to take responsibility for their paths and to seek liberation from constraints.
    • In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, including personal growth, professional development, and relationship management.
    • Ultimately, it inspires us to move forward with confidence and clarity, ensuring that we remain on the right path.
