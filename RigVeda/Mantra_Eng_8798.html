यत ते राजञ्छृतं हविस्तेन सोमाभि रक्ष नः |अरातिवा मा नस्तारीं मो च नः किं चनाममद इन्द्रायेंदो परि श्रव ||
yat te rājañ chṛtaṁ havis tena somābhi rakṣa naḥ | arātīvā mā nas tārīn mo ca naḥ kiṁ canāmamad indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Translation: "O Indra, may the offerings that we have prepared for you, protect us from our enemies. May we not be harmed by any adversary, and may we be freed from all that binds us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yat: that which
    • te: your
    • rājañ: O king (referring to Indra)
    • chṛtaṁ: prepared or arranged
    • havis: offerings or oblations
    • tena: by that
    • somābhi: with the soma (a sacred drink)
    • rakṣa: protect
    • naḥ: us
    • arātīvā: from enemies or adversaries
    • mā: may not
    • nas: us
    • tārīn: harm or trouble
    • mo ca: free us
    • naḥ: us
    • kiṁ canām: from anything
    • amad: that binds
    • indrāy: to Indra
    • endo: O lord
    • pari: all around
    • srava: flow or pour
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The invocation of offerings signifies the importance of gratitude and respect towards divine forces that govern nature and human life.
    • The plea for protection from enemies reflects a universal human concern for safety and security, relevant in both ancient and modern contexts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team seeking guidance and protection from challenges in a competitive market.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of conflict or uncertainty, seeking peace and resolution.
    • Communities facing external threats, such as natural disasters or social unrest, may find solace in the collective prayer for protection and unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine protection and the need for unity against adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace in overcoming challenges.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages individuals and communities to seek support, whether spiritual or communal, in facing life's difficulties.
