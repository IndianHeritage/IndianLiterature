प्र मन्महे शवसानाय शूषम आङ्गूषं गिरवणसे अंगिरस्वत् |सुवृक्तिभिः स्तुवत ऋग्मियायार्चामार्कं नरे विश्रुताय ||
pra manmahe śavasānāya śūṣam āṅgūṣaṁ girvaṇase aṅgirasvat | suvṛktibhiḥ stuvata ṛgmiyāyārcāmārkaṁ nare viśrutāya ||
  • pra manmahe: This phrase can be interpreted as "we meditate upon" or "we contemplate." It signifies the act of focusing one's mind and thoughts on a higher power or divine entity.
  • śavasānāya: This term refers to "the one who is the essence of life" or "the life-giving force." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and energy in our existence.
  • śūṣam: This word means "the one who is pure" or "the one who is free from impurities." It highlights the significance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • āṅgūṣaṁ: This can be understood as "the one who is strong" or "the one who possesses strength." It represents the idea of resilience and power in overcoming challenges.
  • girvaṇase: This term refers to "the one who is praised" or "the one who is worthy of worship." It signifies the importance of reverence and respect towards the divine.
  • aṅgirasvat: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is knowledgeable" or "the one who possesses wisdom." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and learning in our lives.
  • suvṛktibhiḥ: This phrase means "with good words" or "with auspicious speech." It highlights the power of positive communication and its impact on our relationships and surroundings.
  • stuvata: This term refers to "those who praise" or "those who offer devotion." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine.
  • ṛgmiyāyārcāmārkaṁ: This can be understood as "the one who is the source of light" or "the one who brings illumination." It represents the idea of enlightenment and guidance in our lives.
  • nare viśrutāya: This phrase means "to the one who is known among men" or "to the one who is recognized by all." It emphasizes the importance of being acknowledged and respected in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of meditation, reverence, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to focus on the divine, seek purity and strength, and communicate positively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as during meditation practices, in moments of reflection, or when seeking guidance in challenging situations. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the divine, enhance their personal growth, and contribute positively to their communities.
