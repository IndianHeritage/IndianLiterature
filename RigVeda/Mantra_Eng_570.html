उषो ये ते प्र यामेषु युञ्जते मनो दानाय सूरयः |अत्राह तात कण्व एषां कण्वतमो नाम गृणाति नृणाम ||
uṣo ye te pra yāmeṣu yuñjate mano dānāya sūrayaḥ | atrāha tat kaṇva eṣāṁ kaṇvatamo nāma gṛṇāti nṛṇām ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • ye: This translates to "those who," indicating a group of people or entities that are being addressed.
  • te: This means "your," referring to the divine or celestial beings associated with the dawn.
  • pra yāmeṣu: This phrase can be understood as "in the activities of the day," suggesting the actions and endeavors that take place during daylight.
  • yuñjate: This means "to connect" or "to unite," indicating a harmonious relationship or collaboration.
  • mano: This translates to "mind" or "intellect," emphasizing the importance of mental faculties in achieving goals.
  • dānāya: This word means "for the purpose of giving" or "generosity," highlighting the value of sharing and altruism.
  • sūrayaḥ: This refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened ones," often associated with those who possess knowledge and insight.
  • atrāha: This means "here" or "in this context," indicating a specific focus on the present moment.
  • tat: This translates to "that," referring to the subject being discussed.
  • kaṇva: This is a name that can refer to a sage or a wise person, often associated with wisdom and guidance.
  • eṣāṁ: This means "of these," referring to the group being addressed.
  • kaṇvatamo: This translates to "the most excellent" or "the best," indicating a high standard or quality.
  • nāma: This means "name," suggesting the importance of identity and recognition.
  • gṛṇāti: This translates to "sings" or "praises," indicating an expression of reverence or acknowledgment.
  • nṛṇām: This means "of men" or "of humans," emphasizing the human experience and the connection to the divine.

The mantra speaks to the connection between the dawn, the mind, and the wise individuals who engage in acts of generosity and collaboration. It highlights the importance of unity and the pursuit of knowledge as we navigate our daily lives. The dawn symbolizes new opportunities, and the wise are encouraged to use their intellect and generosity to uplift others.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In a workplace setting, fostering collaboration among team members to achieve common goals.
  • In community service, where individuals come together to support those in need, embodying the spirit of generosity.
  • In personal development, where one seeks knowledge and enlightenment to improve oneself and contribute positively to society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, wisdom, and generosity in our lives. It encourages us to embrace new beginnings with an open mind and to work together for the betterment of ourselves and others. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
