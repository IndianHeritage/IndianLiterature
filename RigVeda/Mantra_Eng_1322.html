एतानि वाम अश्विनाऽ वीर्याणि प्र पूर्व्याण्य आयवोऽवोचन |ब्रह्म कृत्वन्तो वृषणा युवभ्यां सुवीरासो विदथम आवदेम ||
etāni vām aśvinā vīryāṇi pra pūrvyāṇy āyavo 'vocan | brahma kṛṇvanto vṛṣaṇā yuvabhyāṁ suvīrāso vidatham ā vadema ||
  • etāni: This word translates to "these" or "these things." It indicates a focus on specific qualities or attributes.
  • vām: Meaning "your," it signifies possession or connection to the subject being addressed.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and life-giving powers.
  • vīryāṇi: This means "strengths" or "valorous deeds," highlighting the importance of courage and valor in life.
  • pra pūrvyāṇy: Translates to "the previous" or "the earlier," suggesting a connection to past actions or traditions.
  • āyavo: This word means "energies" or "vital forces," indicating the life force that drives actions and intentions.
  • 'vocan: Meaning "to speak" or "to declare," it emphasizes the act of communication and expression.
  • brahma: Referring to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, it signifies the foundation of existence.
  • kṛṇvanto: This means "creating" or "making," indicating the act of bringing something into being.
  • vṛṣaṇā: Meaning "with strength" or "powerful," it emphasizes the importance of strength in creation.
  • yuvabhyāṁ: This translates to "with the young" or "youthful," suggesting the vitality and energy of youth.
  • suvīrāso: Meaning "well-heroic" or "brave," it highlights the qualities of bravery and heroism.
  • vidatham: This means "to arrange" or "to organize," indicating the importance of structure and order.
  • ā vadema: Translates to "we may speak" or "we may declare," emphasizing the power of speech and intention.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, valor, and the creative forces of life. It calls upon the Aśvins, symbolizing healing and rejuvenation, to inspire individuals to harness their inner strengths and energies. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a corporate setting, leaders can draw upon the qualities of bravery and organization to inspire their teams to achieve common goals. The mantra serves as a reminder that by acknowledging our strengths and the energies around us, we can create a more harmonious and productive environment.
