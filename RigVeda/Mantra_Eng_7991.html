प्र ये गावो न भूर्णयस् त्वेषा अयासो अक्रमुः |घ्नन्तः कृष्णाम् अप तवचम् ||
pra ye gāvo na bhūrṇayas tveṣā ayāso akramuḥ | ghnantaḥ kṛṣṇām apa tvacam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • ye: those who
    • gāvo: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • na: not
    • bhūrṇayas: disturb, harm
    • tveṣā: your (referring to the divine)
    • ayāso: without effort, effortlessly
    • akramuḥ: unbroken, undisturbed
    • ghnantaḥ: striking, hitting
    • kṛṣṇām: black, dark (often referring to the divine or the cosmic)
    • apa: away, from
    • tvacam: skin, covering
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the protection of wealth and resources, symbolized by cows, which are vital for sustenance and prosperity.
    • The phrase "na bhūrṇayas" emphasizes the importance of not disturbing or harming these resources.
    • The reference to "tveṣā ayāso akramuḥ" suggests that the divine presence ensures that these resources remain undisturbed and effortlessly protected.
    • The latter part, "ghnantaḥ kṛṣṇām apa tvacam," can be interpreted as a call to remove any negative influences or obstacles that may threaten this protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the protection of their livestock and crops, recognizing their importance for livelihood.
    • In a corporate setting, businesses strive to protect their assets and resources, ensuring that they are not harmed by market fluctuations or unethical practices.
    • On a personal level, individuals may seek to safeguard their relationships and well-being, ensuring that they are not disturbed by external negativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of safeguarding resources and wealth, both in a material and spiritual sense.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and protect what is valuable in our lives, whether it be relationships, health, or material possessions.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, encouraging individuals and communities to foster an environment of protection and respect for their resources.
