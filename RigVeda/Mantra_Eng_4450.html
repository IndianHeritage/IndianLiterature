विध्वेषांसीनुहि वर्धयेळाम मदेम शतहिमाः सुवीराः ||
vi dveṣāṁsīnuhi vardhayeḻām madema śatahimāḥ suvīrāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vi dveṣāṁsīnuhi vardhayeḻām madema śatahimāḥ suvīrāḥ"
  • Translation: "May we, free from enmity, increase our strength a hundredfold, O strong ones."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi: a prefix meaning "apart" or "away from."
    • dveṣāṁsī: derived from "dveṣa," meaning "hatred" or "enmity."
    • nuhi: a form of the verb "to be," indicating a state of being.
    • vardhayeḻām: from "vardhaya," meaning "to increase" or "to grow."
    • madema: meaning "may we" or "let us."
    • śatahimāḥ: "a hundredfold" or "hundred times strong."
    • suvīrāḥ: meaning "strong ones" or "valiant ones."

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and the absence of hatred in achieving strength and success.
    • It calls for the removal of enmity, suggesting that personal and collective growth is hindered by negative feelings.
    • The phrase "increase our strength a hundredfold" signifies a desire for immense growth, both physically and spiritually.
    • In real life, this can be seen in team sports where cooperation and mutual respect among team members lead to better performance and success.
    • In workplaces, fostering a positive environment free from rivalry can enhance productivity and innovation.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and the detrimental effects of enmity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community building, teamwork, and conflict resolution.
    • By promoting harmony and collaboration, individuals and groups can achieve greater strength and success.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to strive for a collective upliftment, free from negativity, to realize our full potential.
