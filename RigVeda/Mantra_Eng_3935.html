शुष्मासो ये ते अद्रिवो मेहनā केतसापः |उभा देवाव अभिष्टये दिवश च गमश च राजथः ||
śuṣmāso ye te adrivo mehanā ketasāpaḥ | ubhā devāv abhiṣṭaye divaś ca gmaś ca rājathaḥ ||
  • śuṣmāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are radiant" or "those who shine brightly." It signifies the brilliance and clarity of thought or action.
  • ye: This means "who" or "those who," referring to a group of individuals or entities.
  • te: This translates to "you" or "those," indicating a direct address to the radiant beings.
  • adrivo: This word can be broken down into "a" (not) and "driva" (to be moved or shaken), suggesting stability or immovability.
  • mehanā: This term refers to "the great" or "the powerful," often associated with strength and authority.
  • ketasāpaḥ: This can be interpreted as "with intelligence" or "with wisdom," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and insight.
  • ubhā: This means "both" or "together," indicating a duality or partnership.
  • devāv: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings," representing higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • abhiṣṭaye: This term means "for the purpose of invocation" or "for the sake of blessings," indicating a desire for divine favor.
  • dvaś: This can be interpreted as "heaven" or "the sky," symbolizing the realm of the divine or the higher planes of existence.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting ideas or entities.
  • gmaś: This term can be understood as "to come" or "to arrive," suggesting movement towards a goal or destination.
  • ca: Again, this means "and," reinforcing the connection between concepts.
  • rājathaḥ: This translates to "you who are kings" or "you who are rulers," indicating authority and leadership.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the radiant beings, invoking their wisdom and strength for the purpose of achieving divine blessings and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of intelligence and stability in leadership, suggesting that true rulers are those who shine with clarity and possess the wisdom to guide others.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders in any field, whether in politics, business, or community, can embody the qualities of radiance, wisdom, and strength. They should strive to be stable and insightful, guiding their followers with clarity.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their own qualities, aiming to cultivate brilliance and wisdom in their actions and decisions. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and stability in life.
  • Spiritual Practices: In spiritual contexts, this mantra can be used to invoke divine guidance and blessings, encouraging practitioners to connect with higher powers for wisdom and strength.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of radiance, wisdom, and stability in leadership and personal growth. It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and to embody the qualities of a true ruler, fostering clarity and insight in all endeavors.
