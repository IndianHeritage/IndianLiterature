पिषङ्गभृष्टिं अम्भृतं पिशाचिं इन्द्र सम मृण | सर्वं रक्षो नि बरहय ||
piśaṅgabhṛṣṭim ambhṛṇam piśācim indra sam mṛṇa | sarvaṁ rakṣo ni barhaya ||
  • piśaṅgabhṛṣṭim: This term can be broken down into two parts: "piśaṅga," which refers to a yellowish or tawny color, and "bhṛṣṭim," meaning a covering or a cloak. Together, it suggests a protective covering that is perhaps deceptive or illusory.
  • ambhṛṇam: This word translates to "the one who is filled with water" or "the one who brings forth water." In a broader sense, it can symbolize nourishment, sustenance, or the essence of life.
  • piśācim: This term refers to a "piśāca," which is a type of spirit or ghost in Hindu mythology, often associated with darkness and malevolence. It represents negative energies or obstacles that can hinder progress.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, known as the king of the gods and the god of thunder, rain, and war. He symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • sam mṛṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as "to be killed" or "to be overcome." It suggests the act of vanquishing or eliminating obstacles.
  • sarvaṁ: This word means "all" or "everything," indicating a comprehensive approach to the subject at hand.
  • rakṣo: This term refers to "rakṣasas," which are malevolent beings or demons in Hindu mythology. It signifies the negative forces that need to be addressed.
  • ni barhaya: This phrase can be understood as "to drive away" or "to remove." It implies an action of cleansing or purifying the environment from negativity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra to protect and remove all negative energies and obstacles that may hinder one's progress. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming challenges and ensuring a safe and nurturing environment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers.
  • Environmental Cleansing: In spaces that feel heavy or negative, such as homes or workplaces, this mantra can be used during rituals to invoke protection and create a positive atmosphere.
  • Community Well-being: In times of social unrest or conflict, this mantra can be a collective prayer for peace and harmony, encouraging individuals to come together to drive away negativity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful invocation for protection and the removal of obstacles. Its significance lies in the recognition of the challenges we face and the need for divine assistance in overcoming them. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals can foster a sense of security and resilience, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
