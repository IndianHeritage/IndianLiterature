यो नः सन्तु युत उ वाऽजिघत्नुर अभिख्याय तं तिगितेन विध्य |बृहस्पत आयुधैर जेषि शत्रून द्रुहे रीषन्तं परि धेहि राजन् ||
yo naḥ sanutya uta vā jighatnur abhikhyāya taṁ tigitena vidhya | bṛhaspata āyudhair jeṣi śatrūn druhe rīṣantam pari dhehi rājan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: our
    • sanutya: with strength
    • uta: or
    • vā: or
    • jighatnur: who is powerful
    • abhikhyāya: by proclaiming
    • taṁ: him
    • tigitena: with strength
    • vidhya: may we know
    • bṛhaspata: O Bṛhaspati (the deity of wisdom and eloquence)
    • āyudhair: with weapons
    • jeṣi: may you conquer
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • druhe: who are hostile
    • rīṣantam: who are angry
    • pari: all around
    • dhehi: may you place
    • rājan: O King
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for strength and wisdom, invoking the deity Bṛhaspati to guide and protect us.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and strategy in overcoming challenges and adversaries.
    • The phrase "with strength" suggests that both physical and mental fortitude are necessary to face life's battles.
    • In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or communities face conflicts, whether personal, social, or political.
    • For example, a leader facing opposition may seek wisdom and strength to navigate challenges effectively.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the need for a balanced approach to conflict, combining strength with wisdom.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in physical might but also in the ability to think strategically and act wisely.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in business negotiations, conflict resolution, and personal challenges.
    • By invoking the qualities of Bṛhaspati, individuals can aspire to lead with intelligence and courage, fostering harmony and success.
