पुरुत्रा हि सदृङ्ग् असि विश्व विश्वा अनु प्रभुः |समत्सु त्वा हवामहे ||
purutrā hi sadṛṅṅ asi viśo viśvā anu prabhuḥ | samatsu tvā havāmahe ||
  • Mantra: "purutrā hi sadṛṅṅ asi viśo viśvā anu prabhuḥ | samatsu tvā havāmahe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Purutrā: Everywhere, in all directions
    • Hi: Indeed, certainly
    • Sadṛṅṅ: Like, similar to
    • Asi: You are
    • Viśo: Of the universe, of all beings
    • Viśvā: All, everything
    • Anu: Following, in accordance with
    • Prabhuḥ: Lord, master
    • Samatsu: In harmony, in unity
    • Tvā: You
    • Havāmahe: We invoke, we call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for a divine presence that is omnipresent and integral to the universe.
    • The phrase "purutrā hi sadṛṅṅ asi" emphasizes that the divine essence is everywhere and is akin to the very fabric of existence.
    • "Viśo viśvā anu prabhuḥ" suggests that this divine presence governs all beings and is the source of unity among them.
    • Finally, "samatsu tvā havāmahe" indicates a collective invocation, calling upon this divine presence to guide and support us in harmony.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke a sense of unity and shared purpose, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In environmental movements, the idea that all beings are interconnected can be seen as a modern interpretation of the mantra's call for harmony and respect for the universe.
    • In personal meditation practices, individuals may focus on the omnipresence of the divine, seeking to connect with a higher power that is both within and around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life and the importance of unity and harmony among all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate a sense of interconnectedness and respect for the universe, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as community building, environmental stewardship, and personal spiritual practices.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can foster a deeper understanding of their place in the cosmos and the importance of living in harmony with one another and the world around them.
