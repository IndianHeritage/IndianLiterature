नाभ्यां आसीद अन्तरिक्षं शीरष्णो द्यौः सम अवर्तत |पद्भ्याम् भूमिर् दिशः श्रोत्रात तथा लोकां अकल्पयन ||
nābhyā āsīd antarikṣaṁ śīrṣṇo dyauḥ sam avartata | padbhyām bhūmir diśaḥ śrotrāt tathā lokām̐ akalpayan ||
  • nābhyā āsīd antarikṣaṁ: This phrase translates to "From the navel, the space (universe) came into being." In this context, the navel symbolizes the source or origin of creation. It can be likened to the concept of a seed from which a tree grows, representing the idea that all existence originates from a singular point.
  • śīrṣṇo dyauḥ sam avartata: This means "From the head, the sky (heavens) revolved." Here, the head represents consciousness or the divine intellect, suggesting that the heavens are a manifestation of higher thought or awareness. An example in real life could be the way human creativity and innovation shape our environment, akin to how the heavens were formed from divine thought.
  • padbhyām bhūmir diśaḥ: This translates to "From the feet, the earth and the directions (cardinal points) emerged." The feet symbolize stability and grounding, indicating that the earth is the foundation of existence. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we build our lives and societies on the physical world, emphasizing the importance of a strong foundation in any endeavor.
  • śrotrāt tathā lokām̐ akalpayan: This means "From the ears, they created the worlds." The ears represent perception and understanding, suggesting that awareness and knowledge are essential for the creation of different realms of existence. In real life, this can be related to how education and communication shape our understanding of the world, leading to the development of diverse cultures and societies.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the process of creation, illustrating how different aspects of existence emerge from a singular source. It signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of the universe, from the cosmic to the earthly. In real life, this can be applied to various fields such as science, philosophy, and spirituality, where understanding the origins and connections of different phenomena can lead to a more holistic view of life. The mantra encourages us to recognize the importance of our roots (the earth), our consciousness (the heavens), and our perceptions (the worlds) in shaping our reality.
