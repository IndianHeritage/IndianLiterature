मन्त्र: कद ū nv a1syākṛtam इन्द्रस्यास्ति पौंस्यं | केनो नु कं श्रोमतेन न शुश्रुवे जनुषः परि वृत्रहा ||
kad ū nv a1syākṛtam indrasyāsti pauṁsyam | keno nu kaṁ śromatena na śuśruve januṣaḥ pari vṛtrahā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • ū: indeed
    • nv: or
    • a1syākṛtam: of this creation
    • indrasyāsti: of Indra, there is
    • pauṁsyam: masculinity or strength
    • keno: by whom
    • nu: indeed
    • kaṁ: whom
    • śromatena: by hearing
    • na: not
    • śuśruve: has heard
    • januṣaḥ: of men
    • pari: around
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of creation and the strength associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • It reflects on the idea of masculinity and strength, questioning who has truly heard or understood the essence of Indra's power and the creation he embodies.
    • The reference to "Vritra" symbolizes obstacles or challenges, suggesting that understanding Indra's strength is crucial for overcoming life's difficulties.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader (akin to Indra) must possess strength and wisdom to navigate challenges (Vritra) and inspire their team.
    • In personal life, individuals often face obstacles that require inner strength and understanding, much like the qualities attributed to Indra.
    • In sports, athletes embody the spirit of overcoming challenges, drawing parallels to the strength and valor of Indra in facing adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and understanding strength in various forms, whether in leadership, personal challenges, or competitive environments.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength is not just physical but also involves wisdom, understanding, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, resilience, and the ability to inspire others in the face of challenges.
