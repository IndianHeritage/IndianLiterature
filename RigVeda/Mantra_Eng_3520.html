मन्त्र: राया वयं ससवांसो मदेम हव्येन देवाः यवसेन गावा: |तां धेनुम इन्द्रावरुणा युवं नो विश्वाहा धत्तम अनपस्पुरन्तीम् ||
rāyā vayaṁ sasavāṁso madema havyena devā yavasena gāvaḥ | tāṁ dhenum indrāvaruṇā yuvaṁ no viśvāhā dhattam anapasphurantīm ||
  • Translation: "May we, through our offerings, attain wealth; may the gods, through our sacrifices, grant us abundance like the cows. May we, like Indra and Varuna, be youthful and may we be endowed with all that is beneficial."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rāyā: wealth or prosperity
    • vayaṁ: we
    • sasavāṁso: may we attain or achieve
    • madema: may we be filled or satisfied
    • havyena: through our offerings or sacrifices
    • devā: gods
    • yavasena: like the grains or abundance
    • gāvaḥ: cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • tāṁ: that
    • dhenum: cow or nurturing source
    • indrāvaruṇā: Indra and Varuna, deities representing strength and cosmic order
    • yuvaṁ: youthful or vigorous
    • no: our
    • viśvāhā: all-encompassing or universal
    • dhattam: may we be endowed or granted
    • anapasphurantīm: that which is beneficial or flourishing
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for prosperity and success in ventures. For instance, a startup may recite this mantra during a launch event, seeking divine blessings for growth and abundance.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during rituals or prayers to seek blessings for health, wealth, and happiness, similar to how people might pray for good fortune before starting a new job or moving to a new home.
    • In agricultural communities, the reference to cows and grains can resonate deeply, as these are symbols of sustenance and wealth. Farmers may chant this mantra during harvest festivals to invoke blessings for a bountiful yield.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and prosperity, emphasizing not just material gain but also spiritual and communal well-being. It serves as a reminder that true abundance comes from a harmonious relationship with the divine and the natural world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal aspirations to communal rituals, reinforcing the idea that prosperity is a collective endeavor that benefits all. By invoking the blessings of Indra and Varuna, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve a balanced and fulfilling life.
