अप्सु त्वा मधुमत्तमं हरिं हिन्वन्त्य अद्रिभिः |इन्दव इन्द्राय पीतये ||
apsu tvā madhumattamaṁ hariṁ hinvanty adribhiḥ | indav indrāya pītaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsu: In the waters
    • tvā: You
    • madhumattamaṁ: The sweetest
    • hariṁ: The divine or the Lord
    • hinvanty: They bring forth
    • adribhiḥ: By the mountains
    • indav: The Indus or the divine nectar
    • indrāya: For Indra
    • pītaye: For drinking
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence found in water, referring to the sweetness and nourishment it provides.
    • It highlights the connection between nature (represented by mountains) and the divine (Indra), suggesting that the natural world is a source of spiritual sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Water is essential for life, and its purity and sweetness can be likened to divine blessings. For instance, rivers and lakes are often seen as sacred in many cultures, symbolizing life and spirituality.
    • The mountains, which are often revered in various traditions, can be seen as guardians of the waters, providing a source of nourishment and inspiration.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of respecting and preserving natural resources, particularly water, as they are vital for our survival and spiritual well-being.
    • In modern life, this can be applied through environmental conservation efforts, promoting clean water initiatives, and recognizing the interconnectedness of nature and spirituality.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance between our material needs and spiritual growth, encouraging us to appreciate the gifts of nature.
