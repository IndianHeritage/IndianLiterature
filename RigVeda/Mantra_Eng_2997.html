प्र मे विविक्वाम् अविदन् मनिषां धेनुं चरन्तीम् प्रयुताम् अगोपाम् |सद्यश्चिद् या दुदुहे भूरी धासेर इन्द्रस् तद् अग्निः पनितारो अस्याः ||
pra me vivikvām̐ avidan manīṣāṁ dhenuṁ carantīm prayutām agopām | sadyaś cid yā duduhe bhūri dhāser indras tad agniḥ panitāro asyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra me: for me
    • vivikvām̐: to be separated or distinguished
    • avidan: not knowing or unaware
    • manīṣāṁ: of the mind or intellect
    • dhenuṁ: cow or nurturing entity
    • carantīm: moving or wandering
    • prayutām: well-prepared or equipped
    • agopām: without a protector or guardian
    • sadyaś: immediately or right away
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • yā: who
    • duduhe: has milked or drawn out
    • bhūri: abundant or plentiful
    • dhāser: of the earth or sustenance
    • indras: Indra, the king of gods
    • tad: that
    • agniḥ: fire or energy
    • panitāro: the one who is well-fed or nourished
    • asyāḥ: of this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the quest for knowledge and understanding, emphasizing the importance of being aware and discerning.
    • The "cow" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, representing the resources we seek in life.
    • The phrase "moving or wandering" suggests a journey, both physical and intellectual, towards enlightenment.
    • The mention of "Indra" and "Agni" signifies the divine forces that aid in our pursuit of wisdom and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often seek knowledge (the "cow") and must navigate through various challenges (the "wandering") to achieve their goals.
    • In personal development, individuals may feel lost or unaware (the "avidan") but can find guidance through mentors (the "Indra") who provide wisdom and support.
    • In business, companies strive to create value (the "bhūri dhāser") and must adapt to changing environments (the "carantīm") to thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of awareness, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of divine guidance in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek nourishment—both intellectual and material—while being mindful of their journey.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various fields such as education, personal growth, and business, reminding us to remain vigilant and open to learning.
