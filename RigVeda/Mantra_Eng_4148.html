वातत्विषो मरुतो वर्षनिर्णिजो यमा इव सुसदृशः सुपेशसः |पिषाङ्गाश्वा अरुणाश्वा अरेपसः प्रत्वक्षसो महिना द्यौर इवोर्वः ||
vātatviṣo maruto varṣanirṇijo yamā iva susadṛśaḥ supeśasaḥ | piśaṅgāśvā aruṇāśvā arepasaḥ pratvakṣaso mahinā dyaur ivoravaḥ ||
  • vātatviṣo: This term refers to the "wind" or "air" that is powerful and vital. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of air in our environment, as it is essential for life and sustains all living beings.
  • maruto: This word signifies the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with wind and storms. They represent the forces of nature that can be both destructive and nurturing, similar to how storms can bring rain to nourish crops but also cause destruction.
  • varṣanirṇijo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings rain." Rain is crucial for agriculture and sustenance, highlighting the importance of water in our daily lives and its role in food production.
  • yamā iva: This phrase means "like the Yamas," referring to the moral codes or restraints in Hindu philosophy. It emphasizes the importance of discipline and ethical living, which can be seen in various aspects of life, such as personal relationships and professional conduct.
  • susadṛśaḥ: This means "well-formed" or "beautiful." It can be related to the appreciation of beauty in nature and art, reminding us to recognize and cherish the aesthetics around us.
  • supeśasaḥ: This term translates to "well-formed" or "handsome," often used to describe something that is pleasing to the eye. In real life, this can refer to the importance of presentation in various fields, such as design, fashion, and even personal grooming.
  • piśaṅgāśvā: This refers to "yellow horses," which can symbolize energy and vitality. Horses are often associated with strength and freedom, reminding us of the importance of physical health and the spirit of adventure in our lives.
  • aruṇāśvā: This term means "red horses," which can symbolize passion and enthusiasm. In real life, this can relate to pursuing our passions and the drive to achieve our goals.
  • arepasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are swift." It emphasizes the importance of agility and adaptability in life, akin to how we must navigate challenges and changes in our personal and professional lives.
  • pratvakṣaso: This term means "those who are powerful." It signifies strength and resilience, qualities that are essential for overcoming obstacles and achieving success.
  • mahinā: This word translates to "with great power." It highlights the significance of harnessing our inner strength and capabilities to make a positive impact in the world.
  • dyaur ivoravaḥ: This phrase means "like the sky." The sky represents vastness and potential, reminding us of the limitless possibilities that life offers if we dare to dream and strive for them.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nature's forces, the importance of ethical living, and the celebration of beauty and strength. It serves as a reminder of our connection to the environment and the values we should uphold in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to appreciate the beauty around us, embrace our passions, and cultivate resilience and adaptability in the face of challenges. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
