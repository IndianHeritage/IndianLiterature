याभिः सिन्धुम मधुमन्तम असश्चतं वसिष्ठं याभिर अजराव अजन्वतम् |याभिः कुत्सं श्रुतर्यं नार्यम आवतं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतम् ||
yābhiḥ sindhum madhumantam asaścataṁ vasiṣṭhaṁ yābhir ajarāv ajinvatam | yābhiḥ kutsaṁ śrutaryaṁ naryam āvataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ - This word can be translated as "by whom" or "through whom." It indicates a connection or a means of achieving something.
  • sindhum - Refers to "the ocean" or "the sea," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • madhumantam - This means "sweet" or "honeyed," often used to describe something that is desirable or beneficial.
  • asaścataṁ - Translates to "the one who is unchanging" or "eternal," suggesting stability and permanence.
  • vasiṣṭhaṁ - Refers to "the best" or "the most excellent," often associated with wisdom and virtue.
  • yābhir - Similar to "yābhiḥ," it means "by whom" or "through whom," reinforcing the idea of connection.
  • ajarāv - Means "immortal" or "not subject to decay," emphasizing the eternal nature of certain truths or beings.
  • ajinvatam - Refers to "the one who is clothed" or "the one who has a covering," which can symbolize protection or the material world.
  • yābhiḥ - Again, this word appears to emphasize the means or connections.
  • kutsaṁ - This can mean "the one who is wise" or "the one who is knowledgeable," indicating a figure of authority or insight.
  • śrutaryaṁ - Refers to "the one who hears" or "the one who listens," suggesting the importance of communication and understanding.
  • naryam - This means "human" or "man," indicating the focus on humanity and its experiences.
  • āvataṁ - Translates to "the one who protects" or "the one who shelters," emphasizing the role of guardianship.
  • tābhir - Means "by those" or "through them," reinforcing the idea of collective action or influence.
  • ū - This can be interpreted as "the one who is great" or "the one who is powerful," indicating strength.
  • ṣu - Means "good" or "well," often used to denote positivity or beneficial outcomes.
  • ūtibhir - Refers to "the one who brings prosperity" or "the one who brings success," indicating a source of abundance.
  • aśvinā - Refers to "the Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being in Vedic literature.
  • gatam - Means "gone" or "departed," indicating a transition or movement from one state to another.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of various elements in life, such as the ocean (representing vastness), sweetness (representing desirability), and the eternal nature of wisdom and protection. It highlights the importance of communication, knowledge, and the role of divine beings like the Ashvins in bringing prosperity and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge, the importance of community and support, and the recognition of the divine in everyday experiences. It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, protect one another, and strive for a harmonious existence, drawing on the strengths of both the material and spiritual worlds.
