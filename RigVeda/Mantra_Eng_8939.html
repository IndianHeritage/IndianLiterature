अग्नेर् वर्म परि गोभिर् व्यायस्व सम् प्रोरुणुष्व पीवसा मेदसा च |नेत् त्वा धृष्णुर् हरसां जर्हृषाणो दधृग् विधक्ष्यन् पर्यङ्खयाते ||
agner varma pari gobhir vyayasva sam prorṇuṣva pīvasā medasā ca | net tvā dhṛṣṇur harasā jarhṛṣāṇo dadhṛg vidhakṣyan paryaṅkhayāte ||
  • Agner: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Varma: This means "protection" or "armor," indicating a shield or safeguard against negativity.
  • Pari: This translates to "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a comprehensive or all-encompassing nature.
  • Gobhih: This word means "with the cows," which can symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • Vyayasva: This means "to expand" or "to grow," indicating a desire for growth and abundance.
  • Sam: This translates to "together" or "in harmony," emphasizing unity and collective effort.
  • Prorṇuṣva: This means "to increase" or "to enhance," suggesting a call for greater strength and vitality.
  • Pīvasā: This translates to "with richness" or "with abundance," indicating a desire for prosperity.
  • Medasā: This means "with wisdom" or "intellect," highlighting the importance of knowledge in achieving goals.
  • Net: This word means "lead" or "guide," indicating a request for direction and support.
  • Tvā: This translates to "you," referring to the divine or higher power being addressed.
  • Dhṛṣṇur: This means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," indicating a plea for strength.
  • Harasā: This translates to "with joy" or "happiness," suggesting a desire for a joyful existence.
  • Jarhṛṣāṇo: This means "the one who is wise" or "the knowledgeable," indicating the importance of wisdom.
  • Dadṛg: This translates to "to see" or "to perceive," indicating a desire for clarity and understanding.
  • Vidhakṣyan: This means "to create" or "to manifest," suggesting a call for the realization of intentions.
  • Paryaṅkhayāte: This translates to "with a firm foundation," indicating stability and security.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation for protection, growth, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of surrounding oneself with positive energy and abundance while seeking guidance from a higher power. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks strength and clarity, or when embarking on new ventures that require a solid foundation and collective effort. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of prosperity, joy, and wisdom, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
