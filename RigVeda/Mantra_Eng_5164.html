उत योषणे दिव्ये मही न उषासनक्ता सुदुगहेव धेनुः |बरहिषदा पुरुहूते मगहोनी आ यज्ञिये सुविताया श्रयेताम् ||
uta yoṣaṇe divye mahī na uṣāsānaktā sudugheva dhenuḥ | barhiṣadā puruhūte maghonī ā yajñiye suvitāya śrayetām ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • yoṣaṇe - Refers to "the divine feminine" or "the goddess," symbolizing nurturing and creativity.
  • divye - Means "divine" or "heavenly," suggesting something that transcends the ordinary.
  • mahī - Translates to "earth" or "the great," representing the physical world and its abundance.
  • na - Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • uṣāsānaktā - Refers to "the dawn" or "the light of day," symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • sudugheva - Translates to "like a well-fed cow," representing nourishment and prosperity.
  • dhenuḥ - Means "cow," which is a symbol of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture.
  • barhiṣadā - Refers to "the sacred grass," often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and connection to the divine.
  • puruhūte - Means "to the many invoked," indicating the presence of multiple deities or forces in a ritual context.
  • maghonī - Translates to "the source of wealth," representing abundance and prosperity.
  • ā - Means "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • yajñiye - Refers to "sacred offerings" or "rituals," emphasizing the importance of spiritual practices.
  • suvitāya - Means "well-nourished" or "prosperous," indicating a state of abundance and well-being.
  • śrayetām - Translates to "may it support" or "may it uphold," suggesting a plea for divine assistance.

This mantra invokes the divine feminine and the earth, emphasizing the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual. It highlights the connection between the divine and the material world, suggesting that through rituals and offerings, one can attain prosperity and abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in rituals that honor the divine feminine and the earth can lead to a deeper connection with nature and oneself.
  • Community Gatherings: Celebrating festivals that emphasize abundance and gratitude can foster a sense of community and shared prosperity.
  • Personal Growth: Embracing the qualities of nurturing and creativity in daily life can lead to personal fulfillment and success.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving prosperity and well-being through spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human existence, encouraging individuals to seek balance and abundance in their lives.
