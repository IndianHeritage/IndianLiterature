ऊर्जो नपातं शुभगं सुदीदितिम अग्निं श्रेष्टशोचिषम् |स नो मित्रस्य वरुणस्य सो अपां आ सुम्नं यक्षते दिवि ||
ūrjo napātaṁ subhagaṁ sudīditim agniṁ śreṣṭhaśociṣam | sa no mitrasya varuṇasya so apām ā sumnaṁ yakṣate divi ||
  • ūrjo: This word translates to "energy" or "vitality." It signifies the life force that sustains all beings.
  • napātaṁ: Meaning "descendant" or "offspring," it refers to the progeny or the results of energy.
  • subhagaṁ: This means "auspicious" or "fortunate," indicating something that brings good fortune or blessings.
  • sudīditim: Translated as "brilliant" or "illuminated," it refers to the clarity and brightness of the divine light.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire," which in Vedic literature symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence.
  • śreṣṭhaśociṣam: This translates to "the best among the shining," indicating the supreme quality of the divine light.
  • sa no: This phrase means "may he" or "let him," expressing a wish or prayer.
  • mitrasya: Meaning "of Mitra," referring to the deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the sun.
  • varuṇasya: This means "of Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • so: This translates to "he" or "that," referring to the divine energy being invoked.
  • apām: Meaning "of waters," it signifies the life-giving and purifying aspects of water.
  • ā: This means "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • sumnaṁ: Translated as "auspiciousness" or "well-being," it refers to the positive outcomes one seeks.
  • yakṣate: This means "may grant" or "may bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
  • divi: Meaning "in the heavens," it refers to the divine realm or higher consciousness.

This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, seeking his blessings for energy, prosperity, and auspiciousness in life. It emphasizes the importance of divine energy and its role in our existence. The invocation of Mitra and Varuna highlights the need for harmony and moral order in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can seek the energy and clarity to transform their lives positively.
  • Relationships: Invoking the qualities of Mitra can help foster friendships and harmonious relationships, essential for personal and professional success.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to water reminds us of the importance of preserving our natural resources and maintaining balance in our ecosystems.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of energy, morality, and the natural world. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can aspire to lead a life filled with purpose, harmony, and well-being, making it applicable in various aspects of daily life.
