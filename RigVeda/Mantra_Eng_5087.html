इन्द्राविष्णु तत् पनयाय्यं वां सोमस्य मद उरु चक्रमाथे |अकृतम् अन्तरिक्षं वरियोऽप्रथतं जीवसे नो राजांसि ||
indrāviṣṇū tat panayāyyaṁ vāṁ somasya mada uru cakramāthe | akṛṇutam antarikṣaṁ varīyo 'prathataṁ jīvase no rajāṁsi ||
  • Indrāviṣṇū: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra and Vishnu, two of the most significant deities in Vedic literature. Indra is often associated with rain, storms, and war, while Vishnu represents preservation and protection. Together, they symbolize the balance of power and harmony in the universe.
  • Tat panayāyyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is to be attained" or "that which is desired." It signifies the aspirations and goals that individuals seek in life, whether they are material, spiritual, or emotional.
  • Vāṁ somasya mada: "Vāṁ" means "your," and "somasya mada" refers to the intoxication or bliss derived from Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This part of the mantra emphasizes the joy and enlightenment that come from spiritual practices and experiences.
  • Uru cakramāthe: "Uru" means "vast" or "great," and "cakramāthe" refers to the wheel or cycle of life. This suggests the expansive nature of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings within the cosmic order.
  • Akṛṇutam antarikṣaṁ: "Akṛṇutam" means "to create" or "to establish," and "antarikṣaṁ" refers to the space or the atmosphere. This part of the mantra speaks to the act of creation and the establishment of order in the universe, highlighting the importance of maintaining balance in our surroundings.
  • Varīyo 'prathataṁ: "Varīyo" means "the best" or "the most excellent," while "prathataṁ" refers to the act of spreading or expanding. This indicates the pursuit of excellence and the desire to share knowledge and goodness with others.
  • Jīvase no rajāṁsi: "Jīvase" means "to live" or "to thrive," and "no rajāṁsi" translates to "our desires" or "our aspirations." This emphasizes the importance of nurturing our dreams and ambitions in life, encouraging us to strive for fulfillment and purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking balance, joy, and excellence in life through the divine influences of Indra and Vishnu. It encourages individuals to pursue their aspirations while remaining connected to the greater cosmic order. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, leadership, and community building, reminding us to strive for harmony and to uplift those around us. By embodying these principles, we can create a more fulfilling and meaningful existence, both for ourselves and for others in our lives.
