अस्य व्रतानि नाधृषे पवमानस्य दूद्याः |रुजा यस् त्वा पृथन्यति ||
asya vratāni nādhṛṣe pavamānasya dūḍhyā | ruja yas tvā pṛtanyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • vratāni: vows or commitments
    • nādhṛṣe: not to be neglected or disregarded
    • pavamānasya: of the purified or the one who is in a state of purity
    • dūḍhyā: by the force or power
    • ruja: pain or suffering
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • pṛtanyati: removes or alleviates
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of maintaining one's vows and commitments.
    • It emphasizes that these commitments should not be neglected, especially by those who are in a state of purity or striving for spiritual growth.
    • The mantra suggests that through the power of these commitments, one can alleviate pain and suffering.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee who commits to a project and adheres to deadlines demonstrates the essence of this mantra. Their dedication not only leads to personal success but also alleviates stress for the entire team.
    • In personal relationships, keeping promises and being reliable can strengthen bonds and reduce conflicts, showcasing the power of commitment in alleviating emotional pain.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals who maintain their vows, such as meditation or ethical living, often find a sense of peace and reduced suffering in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of commitment and purity in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by honoring our vows, we can not only enhance our own well-being but also positively impact those around us.
    • This mantra is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional commitments, and spiritual practices, serving as a guiding principle for living a fulfilling and responsible life.
