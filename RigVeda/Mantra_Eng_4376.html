विषां कविं विश्वपतिं शाश्वतीनां नितोषणं वृषभं चर्षणीनाम् |प्रेतीषणिम इषयन्तं पावकं राजन्तं अग्निं यजतं रयीणाम् ||
viśāṁ kaviṁ viśpatiṁ śaśvatīnāṁ nitośanaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ carṣaṇīnām | pretīṣaṇim iṣayantam pāvakaṁ rājantam agniṁ yajataṁ rayīṇām ||
  • viśāṁ kaviṁ: This phrase refers to the "poet of the people" or "the one who knows all." In a real-life context, this can be likened to a leader or a visionary who understands the needs and aspirations of the community.
  • viśpatiṁ: This means "lord of the universe" or "master of all beings." It signifies a figure of authority and governance, akin to a wise ruler or a guiding force in society.
  • śaśvatīnāṁ: This term translates to "eternal" or "everlasting." It emphasizes the idea of permanence and stability, which can be seen in enduring values or principles that guide human behavior.
  • nitośanaṁ: This means "the one who brings prosperity" or "the provider." In everyday life, this can be related to individuals or systems that contribute to the welfare and abundance of society, such as educators or social workers.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This translates to "the bull," symbolizing strength and power. In a modern context, this could represent industries or sectors that drive economic growth, such as technology or agriculture.
  • carṣaṇīnām: This refers to "the one who is worshipped" or "the revered." It can be associated with figures of inspiration, such as mentors or spiritual leaders who guide others on their paths.
  • pretīṣaṇim: This term means "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." In real life, this can relate to the act of seeking guidance or support from a higher power or authority during challenging times.
  • iṣayantam: This translates to "the one who rules" or "the one who governs." It signifies leadership and the responsibility that comes with it, similar to how community leaders or elected officials manage societal affairs.
  • pāvakaṁ: This means "the purifying one" or "the fire." In a practical sense, this can be related to transformative processes, such as education or personal growth, that cleanse and elevate individuals or communities.
  • rājantam: This translates to "the one who shines" or "the radiant." It signifies excellence and brilliance, akin to individuals or organizations that stand out for their contributions to society.
  • agniṁ: This refers to "fire," which symbolizes energy, passion, and transformation. In real life, fire can represent the drive and motivation that fuels innovation and progress.
  • yajataṁ: This means "the one who is worshipped" or "the one who is offered sacrifices." It signifies devotion and the act of giving back to the community or the divine, similar to charitable acts or community service.
  • rayīṇām: This translates to "of wealth" or "of abundance." It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and well-being in society, highlighting the need for resources and support for all.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, community, and the pursuit of prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of wise governance, the role of strength and purity in achieving goals, and the need for devotion and service to others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life, such as community leadership, social responsibility, and personal growth. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the collective effort required to foster a thriving society.
