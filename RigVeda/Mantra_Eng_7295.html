ते जानता स्वं ओक्यं सं वत्सासो न मातृभिः |मिथो नasant जामिभिः ||
te jānata svam okya1ṁ saṁ vatsāso na mātṛbhiḥ | mitho nasanta jāmibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you (plural)
    • jānata: knowing
    • svam: your own
    • okyaṁ: speech or utterance
    • saṁ: together
    • vatsāso: like calves or young ones
    • na: not
    • mātṛbhiḥ: with mothers
    • mitho: among each other
    • nasanta: we are united
    • jāmibhiḥ: with offspring or progeny
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective understanding among individuals.
    • The phrase "te jānata svam okyaṁ" suggests that individuals should be aware of their own expressions and thoughts.
    • The reference to "vatsāso" indicates a nurturing aspect, akin to how young calves are cared for, highlighting the need for mutual support.
    • By stating "na mātṛbhiḥ," it implies that this unity is not solely dependent on maternal figures but is a collective responsibility.
    • The concluding part "mitho nasanta jāmibhiḥ" reinforces the idea of coming together, fostering relationships, and creating a supportive community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, team members must communicate openly and support each other to achieve common goals, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In families, children learn the value of cooperation and understanding from their parents, which aligns with the nurturing aspect of "vatsāso."
    • Community initiatives, such as neighborhood watch programs, exemplify "mitho nasanta," where individuals unite for the safety and well-being of all.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of collective awareness, support, and unity in various aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in personal relationships, workplaces, and communities, reminding us that our individual actions and expressions contribute to a larger whole.
    • By fostering understanding and cooperation, we can create a harmonious environment that benefits everyone involved.
