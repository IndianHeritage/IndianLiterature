विभ्राड् बृहत् सुभृतं वाजसातमं धर्मन् दिवो धरणे सत्यं अर्पितम् |अमित्रहा वृत्रहा दस्युहन्तमं ज्योतिर जजñe असुरहा शत्रुहा ||
vibhrāḍ bṛhat subhṛtaṁ vājasātamaṁ dharman divo dharuṇe satyam arpitam | amitrahā vṛtrahā dasyuhaṁtamaṁ jyotir jajñe asurahā sapatnahā ||
  • vibhrāḍ: This word signifies the vastness and expansiveness of the universe. It can be related to the idea of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • bṛhat: Meaning "great" or "large," this term emphasizes the importance of greatness in thought, action, and spirit. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire greatness in others.
  • subhṛtaṁ: This translates to "well-nourished" or "well-supported." It reflects the idea of being nurtured, akin to how a supportive community fosters individual growth.
  • vājasātamaṁ: This means "the best of strength" or "the most powerful." It can be likened to the strength found in unity, where collective effort leads to greater achievements.
  • dharman: Referring to "dharma," or righteousness, this word underscores the importance of ethical living. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold moral values in their decisions.
  • divo: Meaning "of the heavens," this term connects the earthly realm with the divine, suggesting that our actions should align with higher principles.
  • dharuṇe: This word can be interpreted as "the bearer" or "the one who supports." It signifies the role of individuals or forces that uphold and sustain life.
  • satyam: Meaning "truth," this term emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in all aspects of life. Real-life examples include whistleblowers who stand up for truth despite potential repercussions.
  • arpitam: This translates to "offered" or "dedicated," indicating the act of giving oneself or one's efforts for a greater cause.
  • amitrahā: Meaning "destroyer of enemies," this term can be interpreted as overcoming negativity or obstacles in life, akin to personal growth through challenges.
  • vṛtrahā: This signifies "the slayer of Vritra," a metaphor for conquering ignorance or adversity. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome personal struggles.
  • dasyuhaṁtamaṁ: This means "the most powerful among the enemies," suggesting the importance of recognizing and confronting our greatest challenges.
  • jyotir: Meaning "light," this term symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment, akin to the pursuit of education and wisdom in our lives.
  • jajñe: This translates to "born" or "manifested," indicating the emergence of new ideas or beings, similar to how innovation arises from creativity.
  • asurahā: Meaning "destroyer of demons," this term can be seen as the triumph of good over evil, reflecting the moral battles we face in society.
  • sapatnahā: This translates to "the one who overcomes rivals," emphasizing the importance of resilience and determination in achieving success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for greatness, truth, and righteousness while overcoming challenges and negativity. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected and that our actions should align with higher principles. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue ethical living, support one another, and face adversities with courage and resilience. It serves as a guiding light for personal and collective growth, urging us to contribute positively to the world around us.
