सुसंदृशं त्वा वयम् प्रति पश्येम सूर्य |विपश्येम नृचक्षसः ||
susaṁdṛśaṁ tvā vayam prati paśyema sūrya | vi paśyema nṛcakṣasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • susaṁdṛśaṁ: Beautifully or auspiciously seen
    • tvā: You (referring to the Sun)
    • vayam: We
    • prati: Towards
    • paśyema: May we see or perceive
    • sūrya: O Sun
    • vi: In a way or manner
    • paśyema: May we see
    • nṛcakṣasaḥ: With human eyes or with the eyes of humanity
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or a wish for the ability to perceive the Sun in its full glory and beauty. It emphasizes the desire to see the divine light and energy that the Sun represents, not just with physical eyes but with a deeper understanding and appreciation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Morning Rituals: Many cultures begin their day by greeting the Sun, acknowledging its importance in providing light and energy. This mantra can be recited during morning prayers to cultivate a sense of gratitude and awareness of the Sun's role in life.
    • Mindfulness and Awareness: In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals to practice mindfulness, encouraging them to see beauty in their surroundings and appreciate the natural world, much like how one would appreciate the Sun's rays on a bright day.
    • Environmental Awareness: The mantra can also serve as a reminder of the importance of the Sun in sustaining life on Earth. It can inspire actions towards environmental conservation, recognizing the Sun's role in ecosystems and the need to protect our planet.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and appreciation of the Sun, which symbolizes life, energy, and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate gratitude, mindfulness, and a deeper connection with nature. By recognizing the beauty and importance of the Sun, we can foster a greater appreciation for the world around us and our place within it.
