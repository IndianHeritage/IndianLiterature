इन्द्रं वर्धन्तो अप्तुरः कृत्वन्तो विश्वम् आर्यं |अपघ्नन्तो अराव्णः ||
indraṁ vardhanto apturaḥ kṛṇvanto viśvam āryam | apaghnanto arāvṇaḥ ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Vardhanto: Means to increase or enhance. It signifies growth and development.
  • Apturaḥ: Refers to those who are capable or skilled. It denotes competence and ability.
  • Kṛṇvanto: Means to create or make. It emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence.
  • Viśvam: Translates to the universe or all beings. It represents inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all life.
  • Āryam: Refers to the noble or honorable. It signifies virtue and righteousness.
  • Apaghnanto: Means to destroy or eliminate. It indicates the removal of negativity or obstacles.
  • Arāvṇaḥ: Refers to the enemies or adversaries. It symbolizes challenges or difficulties that one may face.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks of invoking the strength and leadership of Indra to enhance the capabilities of individuals, encouraging them to create a noble and inclusive environment while eliminating adversities and challenges.
  • Real-life Example: In a corporate setting, a leader (akin to Indra) can inspire their team (the capable individuals) to innovate and work collaboratively (creating a noble environment) while addressing challenges such as market competition (eliminating adversities).

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, collaboration, and the pursuit of noble goals. It encourages individuals to harness their strengths and work together for the greater good.
  • Application in Real Life: The principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, team projects, or personal development, where individuals strive to uplift themselves and others while overcoming challenges.
