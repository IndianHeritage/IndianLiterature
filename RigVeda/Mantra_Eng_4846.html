इन्द्रस्य वज्रो मरुताम् अनीकम् मित्रस्य गर्भो वरुणस्य नाभिः |सेमां नो हव्यदातिं जुषाणो देव रथ प्रति हव्याः गृभाय ||
indrasya vajro marutām anīkam mitrasya garbho varuṇasya nābhiḥ | semāṁ no havyadātiṁ juṣāṇo deva ratha prati havyā gṛbhāya ||
  • Indrasya vajro marutām anīkam: This phrase refers to the "thunderbolt of Indra," which symbolizes strength and power. Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and warfare. The term "marutām anīkam" refers to the Maruts, the storm deities who accompany Indra. This can be likened to a team of powerful forces working together to achieve a common goal, much like a sports team that relies on each member's strength to win a game.
  • Mitrasya garbho varuṇasya nābhiḥ: Here, "Mitrasya" refers to Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony, while "Varuṇasya" refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. "Garbho" means womb or source, and "nābhiḥ" means navel or center. This line suggests that the essence of friendship and cosmic order is at the core of existence. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of community and relationships, where harmony and order are essential for a peaceful society.
  • Semāṁ no havyadātiṁ juṣāṇo: "Semāṁ" means prosperity or well-being, while "havyadātiṁ" refers to offerings made to the gods. "Juṣāṇo" means enjoying or partaking. This line emphasizes the importance of making offerings or sacrifices to achieve prosperity. In modern life, this can be interpreted as the need to invest time and resources into our goals, whether in business, education, or personal relationships, to reap the benefits.
  • Deva ratha prati havyā gṛbhāya: "Deva" means god, "ratha" refers to a chariot, and "prati" means towards. "Havyā" refers to offerings, and "gṛbhāya" means to receive or accept. This line suggests that the divine chariot is moving towards the offerings made by devotees. In practical terms, this can be seen as the idea that when we put forth our best efforts and intentions, we attract positive outcomes and blessings in our lives, much like how a well-prepared meal attracts guests to a dinner party.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, harmony, prosperity, and divine connection. It teaches us that by aligning ourselves with the forces of nature and the divine, we can achieve our goals and live in harmony with others. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, nurturing relationships in our personal lives, and making sacrifices for the greater good. Ultimately, it reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of contributing positively to the world around us.
