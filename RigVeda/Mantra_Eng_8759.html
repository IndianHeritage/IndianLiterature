स वाज्य अक्षाः सहस्ररेता अद्भिर मृजानो गोभिः श्रीणानः ||
sa vājy akṣāḥ sahasraretā adbhir mṛjāno gobhiḥ śrīṇānaḥ ||
  • sa vājy akṣāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "those who are endowed with strength and power." In real life, this can refer to individuals or groups who possess the ability to influence or lead, such as community leaders or influential figures in society.
  • sahasraretā: This term means "with a thousand rays" or "with abundant energy." It symbolizes vitality and the potential for growth. For example, a person who is passionate about their work and inspires others can be seen as having "thousand rays" of energy, illuminating the path for those around them.
  • adbhir mṛjāno: This translates to "purified by waters." Water is often associated with cleansing and renewal. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of maintaining mental and physical health through practices like meditation, exercise, and proper hydration, which help in rejuvenating the mind and body.
  • gobhiḥ: This word means "with cows" or "with wealth." In ancient times, cows were a symbol of wealth and prosperity. In modern terms, this can represent the importance of nurturing resources, whether they be financial, emotional, or social, to ensure a prosperous life.
  • śrīṇānaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "being blessed" or "prosperous." It signifies the attainment of success and well-being. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who achieve their goals through hard work and dedication, leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa vājy akṣāḥ sahasraretā adbhir mṛjāno gobhiḥ śrīṇānaḥ" emphasizes the importance of strength, vitality, purification, wealth, and prosperity. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate our inner strength and energy, maintain our health, nurture our resources, and strive for success. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a balanced and prosperous life, contributing positively to their communities and the world around them.
