ते हि यज्ञेषु यज्ञियास ऊमा आदित्येन नाम्ना शम्भविष्टाः |ते नोऽवन्तु रथतूर मनिषाम महश्च यामन्न अध्वरे चकानाः ||
te hi yajñeṣu yajñiyāsa ūmā ādityena nāmnā śambhaviṣṭhāḥ | te no 'vantu rathatūr manīṣām mahaś ca yāmann adhvare cakānāḥ ||
  • Translation: "They who are the essence of sacrifices, who are invoked in the rituals, may they protect us with their divine presence. May they grant us the wisdom and strength to achieve our goals, just as they have done in the past."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • te hi: "They indeed" - Referring to the divine beings or forces that are invoked in rituals.
    • yajñeṣu: "in sacrifices" - Highlighting the importance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine.
    • yajñiyāsa: "essence of sacrifices" - Suggesting that these beings embody the very purpose of the rituals.
    • ūmā: "with auspiciousness" - Indicating the positive and beneficial nature of these divine forces.
    • ādityena: "by the sun" - The sun is often seen as a symbol of enlightenment and vitality.
    • nāmnā: "by name" - Emphasizing the importance of names and identities in spiritual practices.
    • śambhaviṣṭhāḥ: "those who are established in auspiciousness" - Referring to the divine beings who are rooted in positive energy.
    • te no 'vantu: "may they protect us" - A plea for divine protection and guidance.
    • rathatūr: "with strength" - Suggesting the need for strength in our endeavors.
    • manīṣām: "of wisdom" - Highlighting the importance of wisdom in achieving our goals.
    • mahaś ca: "and greatness" - Acknowledging the pursuit of greatness in our actions.
    • yāmann: "those who" - Referring back to the divine beings.
    • adhvare: "in the rituals" - Reinforcing the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • cakānāḥ: "who have acted" - Acknowledging the actions of these divine beings in the past.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, rituals can take many forms, such as weddings, graduations, or even daily practices like meditation. Each of these rituals serves to connect individuals with a greater purpose or community.
    • When facing challenges, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking divine forces for wisdom and strength.
    • In the workplace, teamwork and collaboration can be seen as a form of ritual where collective efforts lead to success, echoing the essence of sacrifices mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals and the divine forces that guide and protect us in our endeavors. It serves as a reminder that through collective efforts, wisdom, and strength, we can achieve our goals and navigate life's challenges. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in meaningful practices that foster community, seek wisdom, and strive for greatness in their personal and professional lives.
