एष कविर अभिष्टुतः पवित्रे अधि तोषते |पुनानो घ्नन्न अप श्रिधः ||
eṣa kavir abhiṣṭutaḥ pavitre adhi tośate | punāno ghnann apa sridhaḥ ||
  • eṣa: This word translates to "this" or "he," indicating a specific entity or being.
  • kavir: Meaning "the poet" or "the wise one," it refers to a figure of great knowledge and creativity, often associated with divine inspiration.
  • abhiṣṭutaḥ: This term means "praised" or "worshipped," suggesting that the entity is revered and acknowledged for its qualities.
  • pavitre: Translated as "pure," it signifies a state of cleanliness or sanctity, both physically and spiritually.
  • adhi: This word means "above" or "upon," indicating a position of elevation or superiority.
  • tośate: Meaning "satisfied" or "pleased," it conveys a sense of contentment or fulfillment.
  • punāno: This term can be interpreted as "renewing" or "reviving," suggesting a process of restoration or rejuvenation.
  • ghnann: Meaning "striking" or "destroying," it implies an action that can eliminate negativity or obstacles.
  • apa: Translated as "away" or "from," it indicates a movement or separation from something.
  • sridhaḥ: This word means "prosperity" or "wealth," often associated with abundance and success.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to a wise and revered entity, acknowledging its purity and the satisfaction it brings. It speaks of renewal and the ability to remove obstacles, leading to prosperity and abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of renewal, individuals can use it as a reminder to seek personal development and transformation in their lives.
  • Creative Endeavors: The reference to the "poet" encourages creativity and the pursuit of artistic expression, inspiring individuals to embrace their creative potential.
  • Spiritual Practices: The emphasis on purity and satisfaction can guide individuals in their spiritual journeys, encouraging practices that promote inner peace and clarity.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The idea of striking down obstacles can serve as motivation for individuals facing difficulties, reminding them of their strength and ability to overcome adversity.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, purity, and renewal in life. It serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of creativity, personal growth, and the ability to overcome challenges, ultimately leading to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
