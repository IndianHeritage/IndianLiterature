अग्नि वाजस्य गोमता ईशानः सहसो यहो |अस्मे धेहि जातवेदो महि श्रवः ||
agne vājasya gomata īśānaḥ sahaso yaho | asme dhehi jātavedo mahi śravaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Vājasya: This term means "of strength" or "of vigor." It signifies the power and energy that Agni brings to life.
  • Gomata: Translated as "the one who is like a cow," it represents nourishment and abundance. Cows are revered in Vedic culture for their milk and agricultural contributions.
  • Īśānaḥ: This means "the lord" or "the ruler." It indicates Agni's supreme authority and control over the elements and life.
  • Sahaso: This word translates to "with strength" or "with might." It emphasizes the powerful nature of Agni.
  • Yaho: This can be interpreted as "to us" or "for us," indicating a plea or invocation directed towards Agni.
  • Asme: This means "to us" or "for us," reinforcing the personal connection and request for blessings.
  • Dhehi: This term means "bestow" or "grant." It is a request for Agni to provide something valuable.
  • Jātavedo: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It highlights Agni's omniscience and ability to understand the essence of life.
  • Mahi: This means "great" or "vast," indicating the expansive nature of Agni's blessings.
  • Śravaḥ: This translates to "fame" or "glory." It signifies the importance of recognition and honor in life.

The mantra can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to Agni, asking for strength, nourishment, and wisdom. It reflects a deep respect for the divine forces that govern life and the natural world.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and guidance during challenging times, much like invoking Agni for support.
  • In agricultural communities, the cow symbolizes sustenance and prosperity, paralleling the reverence for Agni as a source of nourishment.
  • In modern contexts, the mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking clarity and insight in decision-making, akin to asking for the wisdom of Agni.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, in community rituals, or when making important life decisions. By invoking Agni, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, clarity, and connection to the greater forces of life.
