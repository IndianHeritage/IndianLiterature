अग्नि पावक रोचिषा मन्द्रया देव जिह्वया |आ देवान वक्षि यक्षि च ||
agne pāvaka rociṣā mandrayā deva jihvayā | ā devān vakṣi yakṣi ca ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Pāvaka: Meaning 'purifier', this term emphasizes the cleansing and purifying qualities of fire, both physically and spiritually.
  • Rociṣā: This translates to 'with brightness' or 'radiance', indicating the illuminating nature of fire and knowledge.
  • Mandrayā: This word can be interpreted as 'with sweetness' or 'gentleness', suggesting a nurturing aspect of fire that provides warmth and comfort.
  • Deva: Meaning 'god', this term signifies the divine nature of the forces we invoke, highlighting the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • Jihvayā: This refers to the 'tongue', symbolizing communication and the expression of thoughts and prayers, particularly in the context of offerings to the divine.
  • Ā: A preposition that can mean 'to' or 'towards', indicating directionality in the invocation.
  • Devān: This means 'gods', referring to the various divine entities that are being invoked in the mantra.
  • Vakṣi: This translates to 'I will speak' or 'I will declare', emphasizing the act of communication and the importance of vocalizing prayers and intentions.
  • Yakṣi: This can mean 'to bestow' or 'to grant', indicating the desire for blessings and support from the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for purification, illumination, and divine blessings. It highlights the importance of fire not just as a physical element but as a spiritual force that connects humans to the divine. The invocation of Agni is a call for clarity, warmth, and the ability to communicate effectively with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can help purify the space and participants, creating a conducive environment for spiritual practices.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to seek personal transformation, illuminating their path and providing the warmth of support from the community and the divine.
  • Communication: The emphasis on speaking and declaring intentions can be applied in daily life, encouraging individuals to express their thoughts and desires clearly and confidently.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of fire as a purifying and transformative force. It encourages individuals to seek clarity, warmth, and divine blessings in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the spiritual realm and enhancing their personal and communal experiences.
