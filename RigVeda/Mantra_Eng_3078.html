यस्त इध्मं जभरत सिष्विदानो मूर्धानं वा तातपते त्वायाः |भुवस तस्य स्वतवामः पायुर अग्ने विश्वस्मात सीम अग्हायत उरुष्य ||
yas ta idhmaṁ jabharat siṣvidāno mūrdhānaṁ vā tatapate tvāyā | bhuvas tasya svatavām̐ḥ pāyur agne viśvasmāt sīm aghāyata uruṣya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • ta: that
    • idhmaṁ: the offering (sacrifice)
    • jabharat: has brought forth
    • siṣvidāno: with knowledge or wisdom
    • mūrdhānaṁ: the head or the highest point
    • vā: or
    • tatapate: you shine or radiate
    • tvāyā: for you
    • bhuvas: of the earth
    • tasya: of that
    • svatavām̐ḥ: may you protect
    • pāyur: life or vitality
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • viśvasmāt: from all directions
    • sīm: the boundary or limit
    • aghāyata: may you remove
    • uruṣya: the obstacles or difficulties
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "yas ta idhmaṁ jabharat" refers to the one who has brought forth the offering, emphasizing the importance of intention and knowledge in rituals.
    • "Mūrdhānaṁ vā tatapate tvāyā" suggests that Agni shines at the highest point, symbolizing enlightenment and divine presence.
    • The line "bhuvas tasya svatavām̐ḥ pāyur agne" calls for Agni to protect life and vitality, highlighting the role of fire in sustaining life through warmth and energy.
    • "Viśvasmāt sīm aghāyata uruṣya" expresses a desire for Agni to remove obstacles from all directions, indicating the need for guidance and support in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the importance of setting intentions in our actions, whether in personal goals or professional endeavors.
    • For instance, when starting a new project, one might reflect on the knowledge and wisdom required to succeed, akin to the offering made to Agni.
    • In moments of difficulty, individuals can invoke the spirit of this mantra by seeking clarity and support, much like calling upon Agni to remove obstacles.
    • In a broader sense, communities can come together in rituals that honor Agni, reinforcing the collective intention to protect and nurture life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention, knowledge, and divine support in our lives.
    • It teaches us to seek guidance and protection from challenges, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life and the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to come together in shared purpose, fostering resilience and vitality.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of our actions and the importance of invoking higher powers for support and guidance.
