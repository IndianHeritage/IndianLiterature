स नः क्षुमन्तं सदने व्यूर्णुहि गोर्णसं रयिम इन्द्र श्रवाय्यम् |स्याम ते जयतः शक्र मेदिनो यथा वयम् उष्मसि तद वसो कृधि ||
sa naḥ kṣumantaṁ sadane vy ūrṇuhi goarṇasaṁ rayim indra śravāyyam | syāma te jayataḥ śakra medino yathā vayam uśmasi tad vaso kṛdhi ||
  • sa naḥ kṣumantaṁ sadane vy ūrṇuhi: This phrase can be translated as "May you, Indra, who is the giver of wealth, bestow upon us abundance in our homes." Here, "sa naḥ" refers to "may you," and "kṣumantaṁ" signifies "wealth" or "prosperity." The term "sadane" means "in our homes," indicating a desire for prosperity within one's household.
  • goarṇasaṁ rayim indra śravāyyam: This part translates to "May we receive the wealth that is as vast as the sky." "Goarṇasaṁ" refers to "vastness" or "greatness," while "rayim" means "wealth." "Indra" is the deity associated with strength and abundance, and "śravāyyam" implies "may we be blessed with fame and glory." This line emphasizes the aspiration for not just material wealth but also recognition and respect in society.
  • syāma te jayataḥ śakra medino: This translates to "May we be victorious, O Indra, in our endeavors." "Syāma" means "may we be," "te" refers to "you," and "jayataḥ" signifies "victorious." "Śakra" is another name for Indra, and "medino" means "in our efforts." This line expresses a desire for success in all pursuits, whether personal or communal.
  • yathā vayam uśmasi tad vaso kṛdhi: This can be interpreted as "As we strive for success, grant us your blessings." "Yathā" means "as," "vayam" refers to "we," and "uśmasi" signifies "strive." "Tad vaso kṛdhi" translates to "grant us your blessings." This line highlights the importance of divine support in achieving one's goals.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity, success, and divine blessings in one's life. It reflects the human desire for abundance not just in material terms but also in terms of recognition and success in endeavors.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • During a new business venture, entrepreneurs may recite this mantra to invoke prosperity and success.
    • In family gatherings or rituals, individuals may chant this mantra to seek blessings for a harmonious and prosperous household.
    • Students may use this mantra before exams or important presentations to wish for success and recognition in their academic pursuits.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of material wealth, success, and divine blessings. It encourages individuals to strive for their goals while recognizing the importance of seeking support from higher powers. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, one can cultivate a mindset focused on abundance, success, and gratitude.
