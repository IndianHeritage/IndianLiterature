अपश्यं गोपाम अनिपद्यमानम् आ च परा च पथिभिः चरन्तम् |स सदृचिः स विषूचीर वसाना आ वरिवर्ति भुवनेष्व अन्तः ||
apaśyaṁ gopām anipadyamānam ā ca parā ca pathibhiś carantam | sa sadhrīcīḥ sa viṣūcīr vasāna ā varīvarti bhuvaneṣv antaḥ ||
  • apaśyaṁ: This word translates to "not seen" or "invisible." It signifies the unseen aspects of life or the universe that are beyond our immediate perception.
  • gopām: Refers to "the protector" or "the one who guards." In a broader sense, it can symbolize guardianship or the divine presence that watches over us.
  • anipadyamānam: This term means "not being born" or "not having a form." It suggests the idea of the eternal or the formless aspect of existence.
  • ā ca parā ca: Translates to "and also the other." This phrase indicates the duality of existence, encompassing both the seen and the unseen, the material and the spiritual.
  • pathibhiś carantam: This means "moving along the paths." It signifies the journey of life, where individuals navigate through various experiences and choices.
  • sa sadhrīcīḥ: This can be interpreted as "he who is of the same nature." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the shared essence of existence.
  • sa viṣūcīr: This term refers to "he who is all-pervading." It highlights the omnipresence of the divine or the universal consciousness that permeates everything.
  • vasāna: This means "dwelling" or "residing." It signifies the idea of being rooted in a place or a state of being.
  • ā varīvarti: Translates to "moving through the vastness." This phrase suggests the ability to traverse through the infinite aspects of existence.
  • bhuvaneṣv antaḥ: This means "within the worlds." It indicates the presence of the divine or the essence of life within all realms of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about the nature of existence, the divine, and the journey of life. It emphasizes the unseen forces that guide and protect us, the interconnectedness of all beings, and the omnipresence of the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during moments of uncertainty or fear, reminding individuals of the protective forces around them. It encourages a deeper understanding of one's journey and the importance of recognizing both the seen and unseen aspects of life. This awareness can lead to a more harmonious existence, fostering a sense of peace and connection with the universe.
