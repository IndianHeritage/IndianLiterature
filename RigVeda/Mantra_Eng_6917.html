मन्त्र: विद्मा हि त्वा धनञ्जयम् इन्द्र दृढा चिद् आरुजम् |आदारीणं यथा गयम् ||
vidmā hi tvā dhanaṁjayam indra dṛḻhā cid ārujam | ādāriṇaṁ yathā gayam ||
  • vidmā hi tvā - "We know you" - This phrase expresses a sense of recognition and understanding. In real life, this can be likened to acknowledging someone's strengths or contributions, similar to how a community recognizes a leader for their guidance.
  • dhanaṁjayam - "the one who conquers wealth" - This refers to a person who is victorious in acquiring wealth or prosperity. In modern terms, this could relate to successful entrepreneurs or individuals who have achieved financial stability through hard work and perseverance.
  • indra - "Indra" - In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and represents power, strength, and authority. In everyday life, this could symbolize a strong leader or a person who commands respect in their field, such as a CEO or a community leader.
  • dṛḻhā cid ārujam - "who is steadfast and unyielding" - This phrase highlights the qualities of resilience and determination. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges head-on, such as activists fighting for social justice or athletes training rigorously to achieve their goals.
  • ādāriṇaṁ yathā gayam - "like the one who is steadfast in their resolve" - This suggests the importance of commitment and dedication. For example, a student who diligently studies for exams or an artist who tirelessly works on their craft embodies this principle.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing strength, resilience, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that success is not just about wealth but also about the qualities of determination and steadfastness. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, personal development, and community building. By embodying these values, individuals can inspire others and contribute positively to society.
