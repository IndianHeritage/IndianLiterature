आशुरर्ष बृहन्मते परि प्रियेन धाम्ना |यत्र देवाः इति ब्रवन ||
āśur arṣa bṛhanmate pari priyeṇa dhāmnā | yatra devā iti bravan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āśur: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the divine being."
    • arṣa: This word signifies "the seer" or "the sage," often referring to those who possess deep wisdom.
    • bṛhanmate: This means "the one with great intellect" or "the one who is wise and expansive in thought."
    • pari: This translates to "all around" or "in every direction."
    • priyeṇa: This means "with love" or "affectionately."
    • dhāmnā: This refers to "by the abode" or "through the dwelling place."
    • yatra: This means "where" or "in which place."
    • devā: This translates to "the gods" or "divine beings."
    • iti: This is a particle that means "thus" or "so."
    • bravan: This means "speaking" or "declaring."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • The mantra speaks of a powerful divine being, a sage with great wisdom, who is present everywhere and is approached with love and reverence.
    • It emphasizes the idea that in the presence of the divine, the gods declare their existence and their connection to the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke the presence of the divine with love and respect, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal meditation or prayer, individuals may seek the guidance of wise figures or deities, reflecting the pursuit of wisdom and connection to the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, love, and the divine presence in our lives.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, community, and a deeper connection to the spiritual realm.
    • By recognizing the divine in our surroundings and approaching it with love, we can foster a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
