विष्वे देवाः शास्तना मा यथेह होतā वृतो मनवाऽयन् निषद्य |प्र मे ब्रूता भागधेयं यथा वो येन पथā हव्यं आ वो वहानि ||
viśve devāḥ śāstana mā yatheha hotā vṛto manavai yan niṣadya | pra me brūta bhāgadheyaṁ yathā vo yena pathā havyam ā vo vahāni ||
  • Translation: "All the gods, who are the rulers, may the one who is the priest here, who is chosen, speak to me. Let me know the share of the offerings, so that you may lead us on the path where the offerings are to be brought."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśve devāḥ: All the gods
    • śāstana: Rulers or those who govern
    • mā: May
    • yat: Who
    • heha: Here
    • hotā: Priest or one who offers
    • vṛto: Chosen or selected
    • manavai: Speak to me
    • yan: Let me know
    • niṣadya: Share or portion
    • pra me: For me
    • brūta: Speak or tell
    • bhāgadheyaṁ: Share of the offerings
    • yathā: So that
    • vo: You
    • yena: By which
    • pathā: Path
    • havyam: Offerings
    • ā: To
    • vo: You
    • vahāni: Lead us
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the presence of all gods, acknowledging their authority and seeking their guidance.
    • The reference to the priest signifies the importance of rituals and the role of a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The request for knowledge about the share of offerings highlights the significance of understanding one's duties and responsibilities in spiritual practices.
    • In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from mentors or leaders in various fields, whether in personal development, career, or community service.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective worship, reminding us that we are part of a larger whole.
    • It teaches us to seek wisdom and guidance from those who are more knowledgeable, fostering a sense of humility and respect.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in team settings, where collaboration and shared goals lead to success.
    • Moreover, it encourages us to be mindful of our contributions and the impact they have on the collective, whether in family, workplace, or society.
