यज्ञेन यज्ञं अयजन्त देवास तानि धर्माणि प्रथमाण्य आसन |ते ह नाकं महिमानः साचन्त यत्र पूर्वे साध्याः सन्ति देवाः ||
yajñena yajñam ayajanta devās tāni dharmāṇi prathamāny āsan | te ha nākam mahimānaḥ sacanta yatra pūrve sādhyāḥ santi devāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yajñena: Through sacrifice or offering
    • yajñam: The sacrifice itself
    • ayajanta: They performed or conducted
    • devās: The gods or divine beings
    • tāni: Those
    • dharmāṇi: The righteous duties or principles
    • prathamāny: The foremost or primary
    • āsan: They established or seated
    • te: They
    • ha: Indeed
    • nākam: The heavenly realm or abode
    • mahimānaḥ: Of great glory or magnificence
    • sacanta: They attained or reached
    • yatra: Where
    • pūrve: In the past
    • sādhyāḥ: Those who have achieved or accomplished
    • santi: Are
    • devāḥ: The gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of yajña (sacrifice) as a means of connecting with the divine.
    • It suggests that the gods themselves performed sacrifices to establish the principles of dharma (righteousness).
    • The mantra indicates that through these sacrifices, the gods attained a glorious heavenly realm.
    • It highlights the idea that the actions of the divine beings serve as a model for human behavior, encouraging individuals to engage in selfless acts for the greater good.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, community service can be seen as a form of yajña, where individuals come together to help others, reflecting the principles of dharma.
    • Environmental conservation efforts, such as tree planting or clean-up drives, can be viewed as sacrifices made for the benefit of future generations, aligning with the idea of establishing a better world.
    • Acts of kindness, charity, and social responsibility resonate with the essence of this mantra, as they contribute to the collective well-being of society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of selfless actions and the importance of contributing to the welfare of others.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote harmony, righteousness, and a sense of community.
    • By embodying the principles of yajña and dharma in our daily lives, we can aspire to reach a state of fulfillment and connection with the divine, just as the gods did in the past.
