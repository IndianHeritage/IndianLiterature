प्र यं अन्तर वृषसवासो अग्मन तीव्राः सोमाः बहुलान्तास इन्द्रम् |नाह दामानम् मगवाऽन्यंसन नि सुन्वते वहति भूरी वामम् ||
pra yam antar vṛṣasavāso agman tīvrāḥ somā bahulāntāsa indram | nāha dāmānam maghavā ni yaṁsan ni sunvate vahati bhūri vāmam ||
  • pra yam antar vṛṣasavāso agman tīvrāḥ somā bahulāntāsa indram
    • pra yam: This phrase can be interpreted as "to whom" or "for whom." It indicates a direction or purpose.
    • antar: Meaning "within" or "inside," it suggests an inner quality or essence.
    • vṛṣasavāso: This term refers to "the one who resides in strength" or "the powerful." It can symbolize a deity or a strong force.
    • agman: Translated as "has come" or "arrived," indicating the presence of a divine or powerful force.
    • tīvrāḥ: Meaning "intense" or "fierce," it describes the potency of the force or energy being invoked.
    • somā: Referring to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and divine energy.
    • bahulāntāsa: This can be interpreted as "many" or "abundant," suggesting a plentiful or overwhelming presence.
    • indram: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and leadership.
  • nāha dāmānam maghavā ni yaṁsan ni sunvate vahati bhūri vāmam
    • nāha: Meaning "not" or "no," indicating a negation or absence.
    • dāmānam: Referring to "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
    • maghavā: This term refers to "the mighty" or "the powerful," often associated with divine forces.
    • ni yaṁsan: Meaning "who is not," indicating a lack or absence of something.
    • ni sunvate: Translated as "who do not listen," suggesting a failure to heed divine guidance or wisdom.
    • vahati: Meaning "carries" or "brings," indicating the act of delivering or manifesting something.
    • bhūri: Referring to "abundant" or "plentiful," emphasizing the richness of what is being discussed.
    • vāmam: Meaning "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or relationship.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the invocation of divine strength and abundance, particularly through the figure of Indra, who represents power and leadership. It highlights the importance of recognizing and embracing the divine forces that bring prosperity and nourishment into our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals seek strength in their endeavors, whether in personal challenges, professional pursuits, or spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder to remain open to divine guidance and to appreciate the abundance that surrounds us, while also acknowledging the importance of listening to wisdom and heeding the call of the powerful forces in our lives.
