मन्त्र: असौ च या न उर्वराद इमां तन्वं मम |अथो तातस्य यच्छिरः सर्वा ता रोमशा कृधि ||
asau ca yā na urvarād imāṁ tanva1m mama | atho tatasya yac chiraḥ sarvā tā romaśā kṛdhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asau: that
    • ca: and
    • yā: who/which
    • na: not
    • urvarād: from the earth (often interpreted as from the womb or the source)
    • imāṁ: this
    • tanva1m: body
    • mama: my
    • atho: then
    • tatasya: of that
    • yac: whatever
    • shiraḥ: head
    • sarvā: all
    • tā: those
    • romaśā: hairs
    • kṛdhi: may you create
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for the divine to bless and protect the physical body.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the individual and the earth, suggesting that our physical existence is rooted in nature.
    • The invocation of the divine to create and protect all parts of the body, including the head and hair, signifies a holistic approach to well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during yoga or meditation to enhance focus on physical health and spiritual well-being.
    • It can also be used in rituals for protection, such as before important events or challenges, to invoke strength and resilience.
    • Individuals may find comfort in this mantra during times of illness or vulnerability, seeking divine support for recovery and health.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and holistic health.
    • It serves as a reminder of our connection to the earth and the importance of nurturing our physical bodies.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in practices of mindfulness, health rituals, and personal empowerment, fostering a sense of safety and well-being.
