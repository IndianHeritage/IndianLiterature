सरस्वति देवनिदो नि बरहय प्रजां विश्वस्य बृशयस्य मायिनः |उत क्षितिभ्यो 'वनीर अविन्दो विषम एभ्यो अस्रवो वाजिनीवति ||
sarasvati devanido ni barhaya prajāṁ viśvasya bṛsayasya māyinaḥ | uta kṣitibhyo 'vanīr avindo viṣam ebhyo asravo vājinīvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sarasvati: Refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • Devanido: A term that can be interpreted as "the one who is divine" or "belonging to the gods."
    • Ni: A prefix that can imply "down" or "towards."
    • Barhaya: Means "to increase" or "to enhance."
    • Prajāṁ: Refers to "creatures" or "beings."
    • Viśvasya: Means "of the universe" or "of all."
    • Bṛsayasya: Can be interpreted as "of the great" or "of the powerful."
    • Māyinaḥ: Refers to "the one who creates illusions" or "the one who is magical."
    • Uta: Means "and" or "also."
    • Kṣitibhyo: Refers to "from the earth" or "of the earth."
    • Avanīr: Means "the earth" or "the ground."
    • Avindo: Can be interpreted as "the one who is not found" or "the one who is elusive."
    • Viṣam: Refers to "poison" or "danger."
    • Ebho: Means "from these" or "from this."
    • Asravo: Refers to "flowing" or "emerging."
    • Vājinīvati: Can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who is powerful."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess Sarasvati, seeking her blessings to enhance knowledge and wisdom in all beings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine knowledge in overcoming challenges and dangers in life.
    • The reference to the earth and the elusive nature of certain truths suggests that while knowledge is essential, it can sometimes be difficult to grasp.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often seek the blessings of Sarasvati before exams, believing that her grace will help them understand complex subjects.
    • Professionals in creative fields, such as artists and musicians, may invoke Sarasvati to inspire their work and enhance their skills.
    • In times of crisis or uncertainty, individuals may turn to spiritual practices that include this mantra to seek clarity and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine knowledge and wisdom in navigating the complexities of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking knowledge and understanding can help us overcome challenges and dangers.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue education, creativity, and spiritual growth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
