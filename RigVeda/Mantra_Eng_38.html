अस्य पीत्वा शतक्रतो घनो वृत्राणाम् अभवः |प्रावो वाजेषु वाजिनम् ||
asya pītvā śatakrato ghano vṛtrāṇām abhavaḥ | prāvo vājeṣu vājinam ||
  • Mantra: "asya pītvā śatakrato ghano vṛtrāṇām abhavaḥ | prāvo vājeṣu vājinam ||"
  • Translation: "Having drunk this, the powerful one (Shatakratu) became the slayer of Vritra; he who is swift in the races of strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • pītvā: having drunk
    • śatakrato: the one with a hundred powers (often referring to Indra)
    • ghano: the powerful or the dense
    • vṛtrāṇām: of Vritra (the demon or obstacle)
    • abhavaḥ: became or was
    • prāvo: swift or quick
    • vājeṣu: in the races or contests of strength
    • vājinam: the strong or powerful one
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra refers to the mythological figure Indra, who is often depicted as the king of the gods and a slayer of demons.
    • The act of "drinking" symbolizes the acquisition of strength, wisdom, or divine energy, which empowers Indra to overcome obstacles, represented by Vritra.
    • Vritra is often seen as a metaphor for challenges or barriers in life, such as ignorance, fear, or adversity.
    • The phrase "swift in the races of strength" highlights the importance of agility and readiness in overcoming life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals often face challenges that can be likened to Vritra. By "drinking" knowledge and skills (as Indra did), they can become more effective in overcoming these challenges.
    • In sports, athletes prepare rigorously (akin to drinking the divine energy) to enhance their performance and outpace their competitors, embodying the spirit of being "swift in the races of strength."
    • In personal development, individuals may seek wisdom through education and experience, empowering themselves to tackle life's obstacles with confidence and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of acquiring strength and wisdom to overcome obstacles in life.
    • It teaches that through preparation, knowledge, and agility, one can face and conquer challenges, much like Indra faced Vritra.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek personal growth and resilience, reminding them that they have the power to overcome their own Vritras.
