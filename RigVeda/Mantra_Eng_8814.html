कृष्णां यद एनिम् अभि वर्पसां भूज जनयन योषां बृहतः पितुः जाम् |ऊर्ध्वं भानुं सूर्यस्य स्तभायन् दिवो वसुभिः अरतिर् वि भाति ||
kṛṣṇāṁ yad enīm abhi varpasā bhūj janayan yoṣām bṛhataḥ pitur jām | ūrdhvam bhānuṁ sūryasya stabhāyan divo vasubhir aratir vi bhāti ||
  • kṛṣṇāṁ yad enīm abhi varpasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the black one (Krishna) who is endowed with great energy." In real life, this can symbolize individuals who possess immense potential and strength, much like leaders or innovators who inspire others through their actions.
  • bhūj janayan yoṣām bṛhataḥ pitur jām: This translates to "who generates strength in the women, the offspring of the great father." This can be related to the role of nurturing and empowering women in society, highlighting the importance of family and community support in raising strong individuals.
  • ūrdhvam bhānuṁ sūryasya stabhāyan: This means "who raises the sun high in the sky." This can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, where individuals strive to elevate themselves and others, much like educators or mentors who guide the next generation.
  • divo vasubhir aratir vi bhāti: This phrase can be understood as "the divine light shines with the wealth of the heavens." It signifies the abundance and prosperity that comes from spiritual and moral values, reminding us that true wealth is not just material but also includes wisdom and virtue.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of strength, empowerment, enlightenment, and divine abundance. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing potential in ourselves and others, particularly in women, and striving for knowledge and moral integrity. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings, community development programs, and personal growth initiatives, where the focus is on uplifting individuals and fostering a supportive environment for all.
