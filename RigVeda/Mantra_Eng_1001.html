यः सोम सख्ये तव रारणद देव मर्त्यः |तं दक्षः सचते कविः ||
yaḥ soma sakhye tava rāraṇad deva martyaḥ | taṁ dakṣaḥ sacate kaviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • soma: the moon or a divine drink associated with immortality
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • tava: your
    • rāraṇad: having been invoked or called upon
    • deva: god or divine being
    • martyaḥ: mortal or human
    • taṁ: him
    • dakṣaḥ: skilled or capable
    • sacate: he approaches or attends
    • kaviḥ: the poet or seer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine connection between the mortal and the divine.
    • The reference to "soma" suggests a relationship with the moon or a divine elixir, symbolizing nourishment and immortality.
    • The phrase "in friendship" indicates a bond that transcends the ordinary, suggesting that the divine is approachable and can be invoked in times of need.
    • The skilled one, "dakṣaḥ," represents the idea that those who are capable and wise can connect with the divine and receive guidance.
    • The "kaviḥ" or poet signifies the importance of creativity and expression in understanding and relating to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, invoking the spirit of friendship can lead to deeper connections, much like how the mantra suggests a divine friendship.
    • In creative endeavors, artists and poets often seek inspiration from a higher power, paralleling the role of the "kaviḥ" in the mantra.
    • In moments of challenge, individuals may call upon their inner strength and wisdom ("dakṣaḥ") to navigate difficulties, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on skill and capability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine friendship and the connection between the mortal and the divine.
    • It teaches us that through skill, creativity, and invoking the divine, we can navigate life's challenges and foster deeper relationships.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance, cultivate friendships, and express themselves creatively, reinforcing the idea that the divine is always accessible to those who seek it.
