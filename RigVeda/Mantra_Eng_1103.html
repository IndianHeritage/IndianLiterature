न यस्य देवā देवतā न मर्तā आपश्चन शवसो अन्तम आपुः |स प्रarik्वा त्वक्षसा क्षमो दिवश्च मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
na yasya devā devatā na martā āpaś cana śavaso antam āpuḥ | sa prarikvā tvakṣasā kṣmo divaś ca marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • Translation: "He who has no gods as his deities, nor mortals as his kin, may he, with his body, attain the end of the earth; may he, O Indra, grant us strength and vigor."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na yasya: "He who does not have"
    • devā devatā: "gods as his deities"
    • na martā: "nor mortals"
    • āpaś: "as his kin"
    • cana: "at all"
    • śavaso: "of the dead"
    • antam: "the end"
    • āpuḥ: "may he attain"
    • sa: "he"
    • prarikvā: "with his body"
    • tvakṣasā: "may he attain"
    • kṣmo: "the earth"
    • dvaś: "of the heavens"
    • ca: "and"
    • marutvān: "the powerful"
    • no: "for us"
    • bhavatv: "may he grant"
    • indra: "O Indra"
    • ūtī: "strength and vigor"

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the idea of self-reliance and the pursuit of strength and vigor without dependence on external deities or kin. It suggests that true power comes from within and that one can achieve greatness through personal effort and determination.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to personal development and self-improvement. For instance, individuals striving for success in their careers often rely on their own skills and determination rather than waiting for external validation or support.
    • In the context of sports, athletes often embody this mantra by training rigorously and pushing their limits, relying on their own strength and perseverance to achieve their goals.
    • In mental health, the mantra can serve as a reminder to individuals that they have the inner strength to overcome challenges and that self-belief is crucial in the face of adversity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of self-reliance and inner strength. It encourages individuals to look within themselves for power and resilience, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success. By embodying the essence of this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of independence and confidence that leads to achieving one's goals.
