तां घा तां भद्राः उषसः पुरासुर अभिष्टिद्युम्ना ऋतजातसत्याः | यास्व ईजानाः शशमान उक्थैः स्तुवञ्छंसन द्रविणं सद्य आप ||
tā ghā tā bhadrā uṣasaḥ purāsur abhiṣṭidyumnā ṛtajātasatyāḥ | yāsv ījānaḥ śaśamāna ukthaiḥ stuvañ chaṁsan draviṇaṁ sadya āpa ||
  • tā ghā tā bhadrā uṣasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the auspicious dawn. The word "bhadrā" signifies something that is beneficial or auspicious, while "uṣasaḥ" refers to the dawn or the early morning light. In real life, the dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. For example, starting a new day with positive intentions can lead to a productive and fulfilling day.
  • purāsur abhiṣṭidyumnā: This part refers to the ancient beings or deities who are invoked for blessings and prosperity. "Abhiṣṭidyumnā" suggests the attainment of desired goals or success. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders to achieve one's aspirations, much like how individuals often turn to inspirational figures for motivation.
  • ṛtajātasatyāḥ: This phrase emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness. "Ritajāta" refers to the natural order or cosmic law, while "satyāḥ" means truth. In everyday life, adhering to truth and ethical principles can lead to harmony and trust in relationships, whether in personal or professional settings.
  • yāsv ījānaḥ śaśamāna ukthaiḥ: This line speaks to the idea of those who are wise and knowledgeable. "Ijānaḥ" refers to those who understand or are enlightened, while "ukthaiḥ" suggests the act of praising or celebrating. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities honor educators and leaders who impart wisdom, fostering a culture of learning and respect.
  • stuvañ chaṁsan draviṇaṁ sadya āpa: This final part can be interpreted as a call to praise and seek abundance. "Stuvañ" means to praise, "chaṁsan" refers to wealth or resources, and "sadya āpa" implies the immediate arrival of prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of gratitude and recognition of abundance in one’s life, which can attract more positivity and resources.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of auspicious beginnings, the pursuit of truth, the importance of wisdom, and the celebration of abundance. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to embrace new opportunities, uphold ethical values, seek knowledge, and express gratitude for the resources they have. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as starting a new project, fostering honest relationships, learning from mentors, and practicing gratitude to attract more positivity into one’s life.
