त्वा युजा तव तत सोम सख्य इन्द्रो अपो मनवे सश्रुतस् कः |अहन्न अहिम अरिणात सप्त सिन्धून अपावृणोद अपिहितेव खानि ||
tvā yujā tava tat soma sakhya indro apo manave sasrutas kaḥ | ahann ahim ariṇāt sapta sindhūn apāvṛṇod apihiteva khāni ||
  • tvā yujā: This phrase can be translated as "with you united." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in team sports where players must work together to win a game.
  • tava tat soma sakhya: This translates to "your friendship, O Soma." Soma is often associated with the divine and represents nourishment and vitality. This highlights the significance of friendship and support in our lives, akin to how friends uplift each other during challenging times.
  • indro apo manave sasrutas kaḥ: This can be interpreted as "Indra, the lord of the waters, who is known to man." Indra is a powerful deity associated with rain and storms, symbolizing strength and protection. In a real-life context, this can relate to leaders or mentors who guide and protect their followers, much like a teacher guiding students through their education.
  • ahann ahim ariṇāt: This means "I have struck down the enemy." It signifies overcoming obstacles and challenges. In everyday life, this can be seen in personal struggles, such as overcoming fears or achieving career goals despite setbacks.
  • sapta sindhūn: This translates to "the seven rivers." The seven rivers symbolize abundance and prosperity. In modern terms, this can represent the various resources and opportunities available to us, such as education, technology, and community support, which can lead to success.
  • apāvṛṇod apihiteva khāni: This means "he has opened the hidden places." It signifies the discovery of knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of education and self-improvement, where individuals seek to uncover their potential and capabilities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, friendship, strength, and the pursuit of knowledge. It teaches us that by working together and supporting one another, we can overcome challenges and achieve prosperity. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, the value of friendships in personal growth, and the continuous quest for knowledge and self-improvement. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message that resonates across cultures and eras, encouraging individuals to strive for collective success and personal development.
