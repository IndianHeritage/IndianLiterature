दधिक्रामु नमसā बोधयन्त उदीराणां यज्ञं उपप्रयन्तः |इळां देवīm बरहिषि सादयन्तो 'श्विनā विप्रā सुहवā हुवेम ||
dadhikrām u namasā bodhayanta udīrāṇā yajñam upaprayantaḥ | iḻāṁ devīm barhiṣi sādayanto 'śvinā viprā suhavā huvema ||
  • Transliteration: dadhikrām u namasā bodhayanta udīrāṇā yajñam upaprayantaḥ
  • Translation: "With reverence, we invoke the divine energies, awakening the sacred rituals and guiding them towards the offerings."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • dadhikrām: To invoke or call upon, often associated with the act of summoning divine presence.
    • namasā: With reverence or respect, indicating a humble approach towards the divine.
    • bodhayanta: Awakening or enlightening, suggesting the act of bringing awareness or consciousness.
    • udīrāṇā: Those who are elevated or exalted, referring to the divine beings or energies.
    • yajñam: Sacrifice or ritual, a central theme in Vedic traditions, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
    • upaprayantaḥ: Guiding or directing, indicating the intention behind the rituals.
    • iḻāṁ: The divine energy or goddess, often representing prosperity and well-being.
    • devīm: Goddess, a feminine divine presence.
    • barhiṣi: In the sacred space or altar, where offerings are made.
    • sādayanto: Those who are engaged in the act of worship or offering.
    • śvinā: Referring to the divine twins, often associated with health and well-being.
    • viprā: The wise or learned ones, often referring to priests or sages.
    • suhavā: Auspicious or beneficial, indicating the positive outcomes of the rituals.
    • huvema: We invoke or call upon, emphasizing the act of prayer or supplication.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, individuals often invoke divine blessings with respect and reverence, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • During community gatherings, people may perform rituals to awaken a sense of unity and purpose, akin to the collective invocation of divine energies.
    • In personal meditation practices, individuals may focus on awakening their inner consciousness and connecting with the divine, reflecting the essence of 'bodhayanta'.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies with respect and intention. It highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal meditation, community rituals, and significant life events, reminding us of the sacredness of our intentions and actions. By engaging in such practices, we cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and foster a sense of harmony and purpose in our lives.
