यद ईम इन्द्र श्रवाय्यं इषं शविष्ठ दधिषे |पप्रथे दीर्घश्रुत्तमं हिरण्यवर्ण दुष्टारम् ||
yad īm indra śravāyyam iṣaṁ śaviṣṭha dadhiṣe | paprathe dīrghaśruttamaṁ hiraṇyavarṇa duṣṭaram ||
  • yad īm indra: This phrase can be translated as "that which we invoke, O Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine assistance or strength in times of need, similar to how people pray for help during difficult situations.
  • śravāyyam: This word refers to "glory" or "fame." It emphasizes the importance of reputation and the impact of one's actions on how they are perceived by others. In modern life, this can relate to how individuals strive for recognition in their careers or communities, highlighting the value of integrity and positive contributions.
  • iṣaṁ: This term means "power" or "might." It signifies the strength that one possesses or seeks. In everyday life, this can be likened to personal empowerment, where individuals work to harness their abilities to overcome challenges, such as pursuing education or developing skills to achieve their goals.
  • śaviṣṭha: This translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It suggests striving for excellence in all endeavors. In a real-world context, this can be seen in the pursuit of excellence in various fields, such as sports, academics, or arts, where individuals aim to be the best versions of themselves.
  • dadhiṣe: This word means "to bestow" or "to grant." It implies a request for blessings or gifts from the divine. In practical terms, this can be related to the act of giving and receiving support from others, whether through mentorship, friendship, or community service.
  • paprathe: This means "to spread" or "to expand." It signifies the idea of sharing knowledge or resources. In contemporary life, this can be reflected in the importance of collaboration and networking, where sharing ideas leads to innovation and growth.
  • dīrghaśruttamaṁ: This phrase translates to "the long-lasting fame." It emphasizes the desire for enduring recognition and legacy. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals and organizations strive to create a lasting impact, such as through charitable work or groundbreaking achievements that benefit future generations.
  • hiraṇyavarṇa: This term means "golden-colored" or "radiant." It symbolizes prosperity and wealth. In a modern context, this can relate to the pursuit of financial stability and success, as well as the importance of ethical wealth creation that benefits society.
  • duṣṭaram: This word means "difficult" or "challenging." It acknowledges the obstacles one may face in life. In real life, this can be related to the challenges encountered in personal and professional journeys, emphasizing resilience and the importance of overcoming adversity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for strength, excellence, and enduring recognition while acknowledging the challenges of life. Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek divine support in our endeavors, strive for personal and communal excellence, and foster a legacy that positively impacts future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination, seek collaboration, and contribute to the greater good, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and impactful life.
