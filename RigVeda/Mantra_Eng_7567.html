तं उष्टवाम यं गिर इन्द्रं उक्थानि वावृधुः |पुरूण्य अस्य पौंस्याः सिषासन्तो वनामहे ||
tam u ṣṭavāma yaṁ gira indram ukthāni vāvṛdhuḥ | purūṇy asya pauṁsyā siṣāsanto vanāmahe ||
  • tam u ṣṭavāma: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke that" or "We praise that." It signifies the act of calling upon a higher power or divine entity, which in this context is Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature.
  • yaṁ gira indram ukthāni vāvṛdhuḥ: This part translates to "whom the hymns have praised as Indra." Here, "gira" refers to the hymns or words of praise, and "ukthāni" indicates the offerings or invocations made to Indra. This emphasizes the importance of words and hymns in connecting with the divine.
  • purūṇy asya pauṁsyā: This translates to "the many powers of this being." It highlights the various attributes and strengths of Indra, suggesting that he embodies multiple forms of power and influence.
  • siṣāsanto vanāmahe: This can be interpreted as "we seek to know" or "we desire to understand." It indicates a quest for knowledge and understanding of the divine and the universe.

In summary, this mantra is a call to Indra, acknowledging his power and the significance of hymns in worship. It reflects the human desire to connect with the divine and seek understanding of the greater forces at play in the universe.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of words and the importance of invoking divine energies in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in various contexts such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to connect with higher energies and seek guidance.
  • Community Gatherings: In group settings, reciting such mantras can foster a sense of unity and collective purpose, invoking shared values and aspirations.
  • Personal Reflection: The mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their own strengths and the powers they possess, promoting self-awareness and growth.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our spiritual journeys.
