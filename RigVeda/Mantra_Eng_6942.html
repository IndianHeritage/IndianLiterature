एवारे वृषभा सुत एषिन्वन् भूय आवयः |श्वघ्निव निवारत चरन् ||
evāre vṛṣabhā sute 'sinvan bhūry āvayaḥ | śvaghnīva nivatā caran ||
  • evāre: This word can be interpreted as "in this way" or "thus." It sets the tone for the mantra, indicating a method or approach to be followed.
  • vṛṣabhā: This term refers to "the bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in Vedic literature. The bull represents the vital energy and the force of nature.
  • sute: This word means "born" or "produced." It signifies the act of creation or manifestation, often in the context of divine or natural processes.
  • 'sinvan: This can be understood as "to be filled" or "to be endowed." It suggests a state of abundance or richness, whether in material or spiritual terms.
  • bhūry: This word translates to "great" or "abundant." It emphasizes the idea of plenty and the richness of life.
  • āvayaḥ: This term means "to protect" or "to preserve." It indicates a sense of safeguarding what is valuable or essential.
  • śvaghnīva: This word can be interpreted as "like a dog." In Vedic texts, dogs are often associated with loyalty and guardianship, suggesting a protective quality.
  • nivatā: This means "calm" or "peaceful." It conveys a sense of tranquility and stability, essential for a harmonious existence.
  • caran: This word means "to move" or "to act." It signifies the importance of action and movement in life, suggesting that one must engage with the world actively.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the themes of strength, abundance, protection, and action. It encourages individuals to embrace their inner power (symbolized by the bull), to act with purpose, and to protect what is valuable in their lives. The imagery of the dog adds a layer of loyalty and guardianship, suggesting that one should be both a protector and a participant in the world.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, where one seeks to harness their inner strength and act with intention.
  • In professional settings, it encourages individuals to protect their values and work towards abundance, fostering a sense of loyalty and teamwork.
  • In relationships, it highlights the importance of loyalty and protection, urging individuals to act with care and commitment towards their loved ones.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to live with strength, purpose, and a sense of responsibility towards oneself and others, creating a balanced and fulfilling life.
