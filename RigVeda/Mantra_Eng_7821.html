परि दिव्यानी मर्मृशद विश्वानी सोम पार्थिवा |वसूनि याह्य अस्मयुः ||
pari divyāni marmṛśad viśvāni soma pārthivā | vasūni yāhy asmayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • divyāni: divine, celestial
    • marmṛśad: touching, reaching
    • viśvāni: all, universal
    • soma: the sacred drink, often associated with immortality
    • pārthivā: earthly, pertaining to the earth
    • vasūni: wealth, prosperity
    • yāhy: may come, may arrive
    • asmayuḥ: with strength, with vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine and universal energies that surround us.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the celestial and earthly realms, suggesting that divine blessings can manifest in our material lives.
    • The mention of "soma" symbolizes the pursuit of spiritual and physical nourishment, indicating that both are essential for a balanced life.
    • By calling upon "vasūni," the mantra seeks prosperity and abundance, encouraging individuals to welcome wealth and resources into their lives.
    • The phrase "asmayuḥ" highlights the importance of strength and vigor in achieving one's goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to foster a culture of collaboration and support, where team members work together to achieve common goals, drawing on both divine inspiration and earthly resources.
    • In personal development, individuals can meditate on this mantra to attract positive energies and abundance, focusing on both spiritual growth and material success.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, chanting this mantra can invoke blessings for prosperity and strength, creating a sacred space for intentions to manifest.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and the material world.
    • It encourages individuals to seek balance in their lives, recognizing the importance of both spiritual nourishment and earthly prosperity.
    • By embracing the energies invoked in this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with abundance, strength, and divine blessings.
