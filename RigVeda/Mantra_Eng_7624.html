अनु ते शुष्मं तुरयन्तम् ईयतुः क्षोणी शिशुं न मातराः |विश्वास ते स्पृधः शनथयन्त मन्यवे वृतं यद इन्द्र तूरवसी ||
anu te śuṣmaṁ turayantam īyatuḥ kṣoṇī śiśuṁ na mātarā | viśvās te spṛdhaḥ śnathayanta manyave vṛtraṁ yad indra tūrvasi ||
  • anu te śuṣmaṁ turayantam īyatuḥ kṣoṇī śiśuṁ na mātarā
    • anu: This word means "following" or "after." It signifies continuity and the idea of progression.
    • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection.
    • śuṣmaṁ: Refers to "the subtle" or "the finest," often associated with the essence of things.
    • turayantam: This means "swift" or "quick," suggesting speed and efficiency in action.
    • īyatuḥ: This can be interpreted as "may it go" or "may it move," indicating a desire for progress or movement.
    • kṣoṇī: Refers to "earth" or "ground," symbolizing stability and foundation.
    • śiśuṁ: This means "child" or "young one," representing innocence and potential.
    • na mātarā: Translates to "not by the mother," which can imply independence or self-sufficiency.
  • viśvās te spṛdhaḥ śnathayanta manyave vṛtraṁ yad indra tūrvasi
    • viśvās: This means "trust" or "faith," indicating a strong belief in something or someone.
    • te: Again, this means "your," reinforcing the connection to the subject.
    • spṛdhaḥ: Refers to "desire" or "wish," indicating aspiration or longing.
    • śnathayanta: This means "to break" or "to shatter," suggesting overcoming obstacles.
    • manyave: Refers to "thought" or "intellect," indicating the role of wisdom in actions.
    • vṛtraṁ: This translates to "the obstacle" or "the demon," symbolizing challenges or adversities.
    • yad: Means "that," referring to something specific.
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods, symbolizing power and authority.
    • tūrvasi: This can mean "in the battle" or "in the conflict," indicating a struggle or challenge.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of trust, independence, and the overcoming of obstacles. It suggests that by following the subtle and swift path, one can achieve their potential without relying solely on external support. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, where individuals strive to achieve their goals through their own efforts and intellect, rather than depending on others. It encourages self-reliance and the belief that with determination and wisdom, one can overcome challenges, much like how a child learns to walk independently. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength and the pursuit of one's aspirations with faith and courage.
