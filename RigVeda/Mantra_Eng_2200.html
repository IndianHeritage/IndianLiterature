एव त इन्द्रोचथम् अहेम श्रवस्य न त्मना वाजयन्तः |अश्याम तत सप्तम् आशुषाणां ननमो वधार अदेवस्य पीयोः ||
evā ta indrocatham ahema śravasyā na tmanā vājayantaḥ | aśyāma tat sāptam āśuṣāṇā nanamo vadhar adevasya pīyoḥ ||
  • evā ta indrocatham: This phrase can be interpreted as "Thus, we invoke the divine Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine intervention or support in times of need, much like how people seek blessings or guidance in challenging situations.
  • ahema śravasyā: This translates to "the one who is adorned with glory." It signifies the importance of honor and reputation. In contemporary life, this can relate to how individuals strive to build their reputations through hard work and ethical behavior, aiming to be recognized and respected in their communities.
  • na tmanā vājayantaḥ: This means "they do not act with selfishness." It emphasizes the value of selflessness and community spirit. In today's world, this can be reflected in acts of charity, volunteering, and helping others without expecting anything in return, fostering a sense of unity and support among people.
  • aśyāma tat sāptam: This can be interpreted as "that which is dark or hidden." It suggests the idea of uncovering truths or facing challenges. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, where individuals seek to illuminate the unknown, whether in personal growth or scientific discovery.
  • āśuṣāṇā: This term refers to "those who are swift or quick." It highlights the importance of being proactive and responsive. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how businesses and individuals adapt quickly to changes in technology or market demands, emphasizing agility and innovation.
  • nanamo vadhar adevasya pīyoḥ: This phrase can be understood as "we bow down to the divine." It signifies humility and reverence towards higher powers. In everyday life, this can manifest in practices of gratitude, where individuals acknowledge the contributions of others, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine invocation, honor, selflessness, the pursuit of truth, agility, and humility. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to seek divine support while embodying virtues that foster community and personal growth. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to act with integrity, seek knowledge, and remain humble in their achievements. By applying these teachings, one can contribute positively to society and cultivate a fulfilling life.
