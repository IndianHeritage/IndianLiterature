आ शर्म पर्वतानां वृणीमहे नदीनाम् |आ विष्णोः सचाभुवः ||
ā śarma parvatānāṁ vṛṇīmahe nadīnām | ā viṣṇoḥ sacābhuvaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ā śarma parvatānāṁ vṛṇīmahe nadīnām | ā viṣṇoḥ sacābhuvaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • śarma: comfort, joy, or protection
    • parvatānāṁ: of the mountains
    • vṛṇīmahe: we choose or we invoke
    • nadīnām: of the rivers
    • ā: from
    • viṣṇoḥ: of Vishnu
    • sacābhuvaḥ: who is the source of all existence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation for comfort and protection from the mountains and rivers.
    • Mountains are often seen as symbols of stability and strength, while rivers represent the flow of life and nourishment.
    • By invoking Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, the mantra seeks divine support and blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural disasters, such as floods or landslides, people often seek refuge in the mountains or near rivers, highlighting the importance of these natural elements for safety and sustenance.
    • In personal life, individuals may turn to nature for solace during stressful times, finding peace in the mountains or by the riverside, which aligns with the mantra's call for comfort.
    • Communities often gather near rivers for festivals and rituals, invoking blessings for prosperity and protection, resonating with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking comfort and protection from nature, represented by mountains and rivers.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine (Vishnu) and the natural world, reminding us of the need for harmony with our environment.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or environmental conservation efforts, where the blessings of nature and the divine are invoked for well-being and stability.
