त्वं न इन्द्र वाजयुस् त्वं गव्युः शतक्रतुः |त्वं हिरण्ययुर् वसो ||
tvaṁ na indra vājayus tvaṁ gavyuḥ śatakrato | tvaṁ hiraṇyayur vaso ||
  • tvaṁ na indra vājayus: This phrase can be translated as "You, Indra, are the one who bestows strength." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be likened to the support and strength we receive from mentors or leaders in our lives, who empower us to overcome challenges.
  • tvaṁ gavyuḥ śatakrato: This translates to "You are the one who possesses a hundred powers." The term "śatakrato" refers to someone with a hundred abilities or powers, symbolizing versatility and capability. In a modern context, this can be seen in individuals who excel in multiple fields, such as a polymath or a leader who can navigate various challenges effectively.
  • tvaṁ hiraṇyayur vaso: This means "You are the golden abode." The word "hiraṇya" signifies gold, which is often associated with prosperity and wealth. This can be interpreted as a metaphor for a place or state of being that is rich in resources and opportunities. In real life, this could refer to a thriving community or a successful organization that fosters growth and prosperity for its members.

  • Significance of the Mantra: This mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, versatility, and prosperity. It acknowledges the divine as a source of empowerment and abundance.
  • Real-Life Application: In our daily lives, we can invoke the essence of this mantra by seeking strength in our endeavors, embracing our diverse skills, and striving for prosperity in our personal and professional lives. It encourages us to recognize the support we receive from others and to cultivate an environment that promotes growth and success.
