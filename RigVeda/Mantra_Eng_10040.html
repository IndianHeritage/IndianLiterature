दक्षिणाश्वं दक्षिणा गां ददाति दक्षिणा चन्द्रम् उता यद धिरण्यम् |दक्षिणान्नं वनुते यो न आत्मा दक्षिणां वर्म कृतुते विजानन ||
dakṣiṇāśvaṁ dakṣiṇā gāṁ dadāti dakṣiṇā candram uta yad dhiraṇyam | dakṣiṇānnaṁ vanute yo na ātmā dakṣiṇāṁ varma kṛṇute vijānan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dakṣiṇāśvaṁ: The southern horse, symbolizing prosperity and auspiciousness.
    • dakṣiṇā: The direction south, often associated with wealth and blessings.
    • gāṁ: The cow, representing nourishment and sustenance.
    • dadāti: Gives or bestows.
    • candram: The moon, symbolizing beauty and tranquility.
    • uta: And, or also.
    • yad: That which.
    • dhiraṇyam: Golden or wealth.
    • dakṣiṇānnaṁ: The southern food, indicating nourishment from the south.
    • vanute: Enjoys or partakes.
    • yo: Who.
    • na: Not.
    • ātma: Self or soul.
    • dakṣiṇāṁ: The southern direction again, emphasizing its importance.
    • varma: Armor or protection.
    • kṛṇute: Creates or establishes.
    • vijānan: Knowing or understanding.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the blessings and gifts that come from the southern direction, which is often associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • The mention of the horse and cow signifies the importance of these animals in Vedic culture, representing strength and nourishment respectively.
    • The moon symbolizes calmness and beauty, suggesting that wealth should be pursued with a sense of peace and harmony.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding and knowledge in acquiring wealth and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the south is considered a direction of prosperity. For instance, in Feng Shui, the southern area of a home is often associated with fame and recognition.
    • In agriculture, the cow is revered for its ability to provide milk and other resources, highlighting the importance of nurturing and sustaining life.
    • In modern business, understanding market trends (vijānan) is crucial for success, akin to the wisdom mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking blessings from the southern direction, which is associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • It teaches us that true wealth comes not just from material possessions but also from understanding, knowledge, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied by fostering a mindset of abundance, seeking knowledge, and appreciating the resources we have, much like the reverence for cows and horses in Vedic culture.
