आ देवो यातु सविता सुरत्नोऽन्तरिक्षप्रा वहमानो अश्वैः |हस्ते दधानो नार्यां पुरूणि निवेशयञ च प्रसूवञ च भूम ||
ā devo yātu savitā suratno 'ntarikṣaprā vahamāno aśvaiḥ | haste dadhāno naryā purūṇi niveśayañ ca prasuvañ ca bhūma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • devo: the divine
    • yātu: may he come
    • savitā: the sun or the life-giver
    • suratno: of the one who is full of light
    • 'ntarikṣaprā: who traverses the sky
    • vahāmāno: carrying
    • aśvaiḥ: with horses
    • haste: in his hands
    • dadhāno: holding
    • naryā: for the man
    • purūṇi: the many
    • niveśayañ: may he place
    • ca: and
    • prasuvañ: may he produce
    • ca: and
    • bhūma: the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine sun, Savitā, asking for his presence and blessings.
    • The phrase "may he come" signifies a call for divine intervention and guidance.
    • The reference to "carrying with horses" symbolizes strength and speed, suggesting that the divine energy is powerful and swift.
    • The mention of "holding in his hands" indicates the divine's ability to bestow gifts and blessings upon humanity.
    • The terms "may he place" and "may he produce" reflect a desire for abundance and prosperity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the sun's blessings to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal endeavors, individuals may seek divine guidance when starting new projects, hoping for success and prosperity.
    • During festivals, rituals are performed to honor the sun, symbolizing gratitude for life and sustenance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers in our endeavors, whether in personal, professional, or communal aspects.
    • By acknowledging the divine's role in our lives, we cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection to the universe.
