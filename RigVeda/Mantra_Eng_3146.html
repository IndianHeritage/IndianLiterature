द्विर्यं पञ्च जिजानन संवासानाः स्वसारो अग्निम मानविषु विक्षु |उषर्बुधम अथर्यो न दन्तं शुक्रं स्वासं परशुं न तिग्मम् ||
dvir yam pañca jījanan saṁvasānāḥ svasāro agnim mānuṣīṣu vikṣu | uṣarbudham atharyo3 na dantaṁ śukraṁ svāsam paraśuṁ na tigmam ||
  • Mantra: "dvir yam pañca jījanan saṁvasānāḥ svasāro agnim mānuṣīṣu vikṣu | uṣarbudham atharyo3 na dantaṁ śukraṁ svāsam paraśuṁ na tigmam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvir: Twice
    • yam: Who
    • pañca: Five
    • jījanan: Creating or generating
    • saṁvasānāḥ: Those who are gathered together
    • svasāro: Relatives or kin
    • agnim: Fire
    • mānuṣīṣu: Among humans
    • vikṣu: In the midst of
    • uṣarbudham: The one who is awakened or enlightened
    • atharyo: The one who is not hindered
    • na: Not
    • dantaṁ: Tusk or tooth
    • śukraṁ: Bright or pure
    • svāsam: Breath or life
    • paraśuṁ: Axe or tool
    • na: Not
    • tigmam: Sharp or fierce
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation and the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of community and kinship.
    • The phrase "dvir yam pañca jījanan" suggests the idea of generating life or existence in a collective manner, highlighting the significance of collaboration and unity.
    • “Agnim mānuṣīṣu vikṣu” indicates the presence of fire (symbolizing energy, transformation, and purity) among humans, which can be interpreted as the inner spark of life that connects us all.
    • The latter part of the mantra, “uṣarbudham atharyo3 na dantaṁ,” suggests a state of enlightenment and freedom from obstacles, where one is not hindered by the sharpness of life’s challenges.
    • “Śukraṁ svāsam paraśuṁ na tigmam” implies a state of purity and balance, where one is not overwhelmed by the harshness of existence but instead embraces a life of clarity and purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, collaboration among team members can lead to innovative solutions, reflecting the idea of "dvir yam pañca jījanan" where collective effort generates success.
    • In family gatherings, the warmth of shared experiences and kinship can be likened to "agnim mānuṣīṣu vikṣu," where the fire of love and connection is present among relatives.
    • In personal development, overcoming challenges and achieving enlightenment can be seen as embodying “uṣarbudham atharyo,” where one learns to navigate life’s difficulties with resilience.
    • Practicing mindfulness and maintaining a balanced life can resonate with “śukraṁ svāsam paraśuṁ na tigmam,” where one seeks purity and clarity amidst the chaos of daily life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the pursuit of enlightenment.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to embrace collaboration, maintain purity in our actions, and navigate life’s challenges with resilience.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, personal growth, and a more harmonious existence.
