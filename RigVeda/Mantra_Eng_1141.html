युयोप नाभिर उपरस्यायोः प्र पूर्वाभिस तिरते राष्ट्रि शूरः |अञ्जसी कुलिशी वीरपत्नी पयो हिन्वाना उदभिर भरण्ते ||
yuyopa nābhir uparasyāyoḥ pra pūrvābhis tirate rāṣṭi śūraḥ | añjasī kuliśī vīrapatnī payo hinvānā udabhir bharante ||
  • yuyopa: This term can be interpreted as "to unite" or "to bring together." It signifies the importance of collaboration and harmony in achieving common goals.
  • nābhir: Refers to the "navel" or "center." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the core or essence of something, such as the heart of a community or the center of a relationship.
  • uparasyāyoḥ: This word means "above" or "higher." It suggests aspirations or goals that are elevated, indicating the pursuit of higher ideals or values.
  • pra pūrvābhis: This phrase can be translated as "with the previous ones" or "from the past." It emphasizes the importance of learning from history and the wisdom of ancestors.
  • tirate: Meaning "to cross over" or "to traverse," this word signifies overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • rāṣṭi: This term refers to "nation" or "community." It highlights the collective aspect of society and the importance of unity among its members.
  • śūraḥ: Translated as "brave" or "heroic," it denotes the qualities of courage and valor that are essential for facing life's challenges.
  • añjasī: This word means "easily" or "swiftly," suggesting that certain actions or achievements can be accomplished with grace and efficiency.
  • kuliśī: Referring to "the one who wields a thunderbolt," it symbolizes strength and power, often associated with divine forces.
  • vīrapatnī: This term translates to "the wife of a hero," representing support and partnership in the journey of life.
  • payo: Meaning "milk," it symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • hinvānā: This word means "to bring forth" or "to produce," indicating the act of creation or manifestation.
  • udabhir: Referring to "waters," it can symbolize the flow of life, emotions, and the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • bharante: This term means "to bear" or "to carry," suggesting the responsibilities and burdens that come with life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, courage, and the importance of learning from the past while striving for higher ideals. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal relationships. By embracing collaboration and drawing strength from our history and partnerships, we can navigate challenges more effectively and contribute positively to our communities. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength and the nurturing of relationships, which are essential for personal and societal growth.
