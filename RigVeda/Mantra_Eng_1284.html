तद वां नराः सनये दंष उग्रं आविष्कृणोमि तान्यतुर न वृष्टिम् | दध्यङ् ह यन् मध्व् आथर्वणो वाम अश्वस्य शीरष्णा प्र यद इम उवाच ||
tad vāṁ narā sanaye daṁsa ugram āviṣ kṛṇomi tanyatur na vṛṣṭim | dadhyaṅ ha yan madhv ātharvaṇo vām aśvasya śīrṣṇā pra yad īm uvāca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vāṁ: your
    • narā: men or people
    • sanaye: to be protected or saved
    • daṁsa: fierce or powerful
    • ugram: intense or strong
    • āviṣ: to manifest or reveal
    • kṛṇomi: I create or make
    • tanyatur: with great effort
    • na: not
    • vṛṣṭim: rain or abundance
    • dadhyaṅ: I hold or maintain
    • ha: indeed
    • yan: that which
    • madhv: sweet or pleasant
    • ātharvaṇo: of the Atharva Veda
    • vām: your
    • aśvasya: of the horse
    • śīrṣṇā: with the head or top
    • pra: forward or towards
    • yad: what
    • īm: I speak or say
    • uvāca: said
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation and manifestation of strength and protection for people.
    • The phrase "tad vāṁ narā sanaye" emphasizes the importance of safeguarding humanity.
    • It suggests that through intense effort and creation, one can bring forth abundance and prosperity, as indicated by "na vṛṣṭim."
    • The reference to "madhv ātharvaṇo" connects to the sweetness of life and the wisdom of the Atharva Veda, which is often associated with practical knowledge and healing.
    • The mention of "aśvasya śīrṣṇā" symbolizes strength and vitality, akin to the power of a horse, which is often seen as a symbol of energy and movement.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to initiatives aimed at protecting and uplifting people, such as social welfare programs.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to work diligently towards their goals, creating abundance through hard work and perseverance.
    • In environmental contexts, it can be interpreted as a call to protect nature and ensure sustainable practices that benefit all living beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, protection, and the creation of abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that through collective effort and wisdom, we can achieve prosperity and safeguard our communities.
    • In essence, it encourages us to harness our inner strength and work towards the greater good, reflecting the interconnectedness of all beings.
