सुगुर् असत् सुहिरण्यः स्वश्वो बृहद् अस्मै वय इन्द्रो दधाति |यस् त्वायन्तं वसुनā प्रातरित्वो मुक्षीजयेव पदिम् उत्सिनाति ||
sugur asat suhiraṇyaḥ svaśvo bṛhad asmai vaya indro dadhāti | yas tvāyantaṁ vasunā prātaritvo mukṣījayeva padim utsināti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sugur: good, auspicious
    • asat: not false, true
    • suhiraṇyaḥ: well-being, prosperity
    • svaśvo: one's own, personal
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • asmai: to him, to that person
    • vaya: our, us
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • dadhāti: bestows, grants
    • yas: who
    • tvāyantaṁ: you, to you
    • vasunā: with wealth, with riches
    • prātaritvo: in the morning, at dawn
    • mukṣījayeva: as if liberating
    • padim: path, way
    • utsināti: leads, guides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspiciousness and truthfulness of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of personal well-being and prosperity.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, is invoked to bestow blessings and wealth.
    • The mention of dawn signifies new beginnings and opportunities.
    • The idea of liberation suggests a path to enlightenment and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for ethical practices that lead to true prosperity.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek their own happiness and well-being, fostering a positive environment.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for a prosperous start.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking truth and prosperity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings with a positive mindset.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to personal growth, ethical business practices, and the pursuit of happiness.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek guidance and blessings from higher powers for a fulfilling life journey.
