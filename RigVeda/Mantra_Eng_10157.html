अभि द्यां महिना भुवम् अभिमाम पृथिवीं महीम् |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
abhi dyām mahinā bhuvam abhī3mām pṛthivīm mahīm | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • Mantra: "abhi dyām mahinā bhuvam abhī3mām pṛthivīm mahīm | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • dyām: the sky, the heavens
    • mahinā: with greatness, with power
    • bhuvam: the world, the earth
    • abhī3mām: towards me, to me
    • pṛthivīm: the earth, the physical realm
    • mahīm: the great, the vast
    • kuvit: what is good, auspicious
    • somasyāpām: of the waters of the moon (or nectar)
    • iti: thus, so
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the vastness and greatness of both the heavens and the earth.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine (represented by the sky) and the earthly realm.
    • The phrase "kuvit somasyāpām" suggests a yearning for the auspicious qualities of the moon's waters, which can be interpreted as a desire for nourishment, prosperity, and spiritual enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek balance between our spiritual aspirations (the sky) and our earthly responsibilities (the earth).
    • For instance, a person striving for success in their career while also nurturing their relationships and personal growth embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In nature, the cycle of water (from the sky to the earth) reflects the interconnectedness of all life, reminding us of the importance of harmony in our environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the relationship between the spiritual and the material worlds.
    • It encourages individuals to aspire for greatness while remaining grounded in reality, fostering a holistic approach to life.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, environmental consciousness, and community building, promoting a balanced and fulfilling existence.
