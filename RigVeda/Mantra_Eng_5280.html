राजेवा हि जनिभिः क्षेष्य एवाव द्युबिर अभि विदुष कविः सन् |पिशा गिरो मघवन गोभिर अश्वैस् त्वायतः शिशीहि राये अस्मान् ||
rājeva hi janibhiḥ kṣeṣy evāva dyubhir abhi viduṣ kaviḥ san | piśā giro maghavan gobhir aśvais tvāyataḥ śiśīhi rāye asmān ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, the king is known by his subjects; the wise poet, through his knowledge, is aware of the heavens. O Maghavan, with your powerful words, may you bestow upon us wealth and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rājeva: Refers to a king or ruler, symbolizing authority and leadership.
    • janibhiḥ: Means subjects or people, indicating the relationship between a ruler and his followers.
    • kṣeṣy: To be known or recognized, emphasizing the importance of reputation.
    • evāva: Indeed or truly, reinforcing the statement.
    • dyubhir: Refers to the heavens or celestial realms, suggesting a higher knowledge or wisdom.
    • viduṣ: The wise or knowledgeable, indicating the importance of wisdom in leadership.
    • kaviḥ: A poet or seer, symbolizing creativity and insight.
    • piśā: Powerful or strong, referring to the potency of words.
    • giro: Words or speech, highlighting the significance of communication.
    • maghavan: A name for the deity associated with wealth and abundance.
    • gobhir: With cattle or wealth, representing prosperity.
    • aśvais: With horses, symbolizing strength and mobility.
    • tvāyataḥ: May you bestow or grant, indicating a plea for blessings.
    • śiśīhi: Bestow or grant, a call for generosity.
    • rāye: Wealth or prosperity, the ultimate goal of the invocation.
    • asmān: Us or our, indicating a collective plea.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a CEO is often judged by how well they lead their team and the success of the company, similar to how a king is known by his subjects.
    • In community leadership, a wise leader who understands the needs of their people can create a harmonious environment, akin to the wise poet who understands the heavens.
    • In personal relationships, effective communication (the power of words) can strengthen bonds and foster prosperity in friendships and family ties.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wise leadership and the power of words in achieving prosperity. It highlights the relationship between a ruler and their subjects, suggesting that true leadership is recognized through the well-being of the people. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as business, community leadership, and personal relationships, where effective communication and understanding lead to success and abundance. The invocation to Maghavan for wealth serves as a reminder that prosperity is not just material but also encompasses wisdom, relationships, and community well-being.
