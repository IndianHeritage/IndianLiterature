मन्त्र: एं एनं सृजता सुते मन्दिम इन्द्राय मन्दिने |चक्रिं विश्वानि चक्रये ||
em enaṁ sṛjatā sute mandim indrāya mandine | cakriṁ viśvāni cakraye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • em: This word can be interpreted as "this" or "here." It signifies presence or focus.
    • enaṁ: Meaning "him" or "that," referring to a divine entity or force.
    • sṛjatā: Translates to "creating" or "producing," indicating the act of bringing forth.
    • sute: Means "for the benefit" or "for the sake of," suggesting a purpose behind the creation.
    • mandim: Refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the mighty," often associated with deities.
    • indrāya: This is a reference to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • mandine: Another form of "mighty" or "powerful," reinforcing the attributes of Indra.
    • cakriṁ: Meaning "the one who wields a discus" or "the one who has a wheel," symbolizing control and mastery.
    • viśvāni: Translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness.
    • cakraye: Means "I create" or "I establish," suggesting an active role in the process of creation.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of creation and the invocation of divine power, particularly that of Indra, to manifest strength and universality.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creating for the benefit of all, highlighting a sense of responsibility in the act of creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may embody the essence of this mantra by creating a work environment that benefits all employees, fostering collaboration and innovation.
    • In community service, individuals who work to create programs for the betterment of society reflect the principles of this mantra by focusing on universal welfare.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing and creating a supportive atmosphere for family and friends aligns with the mantra's call for beneficial creation.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to harness divine power for the greater good, reminding us of our responsibility in the act of creation.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from leadership and community service to personal relationships, encouraging us to create with intention and purpose.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that our actions should aim to benefit all, reflecting the interconnectedness of life.
