कुहा त्या कुहा नु श्रुता दिवि देवā नासत्यā |कस्मिन्न ā यतथो जनें को वां नदीनां सचा ||
kuha tyā kuha nu śrutā divi devā nāsatyā | kasminn ā yatatho jane ko vāṁ nadīnāṁ sacā ||
  • Kuha tyā kuha nu śrutā divi devā nāsatyā: This line can be translated to "Where are you, O divine beings, who are known as the Nāsatyas?" The term "Nāsatyas" refers to the twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with truth and healing. This invocation reflects a quest for divine guidance and presence.
  • Kasminn ā yatatho jane: This translates to "In which place do you reside among the people?" This line emphasizes the search for the divine in the midst of humanity, suggesting that the divine is not distant but rather integrated within the human experience.
  • Ko vāṁ nadīnāṁ sacā: This can be interpreted as "Who among the rivers is the true one?" Rivers in Vedic literature often symbolize the flow of life, knowledge, and sustenance. This line may imply a quest for understanding the true essence of life and the sources of wisdom.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a profound inquiry into the nature of divinity and its relationship with humanity. It reflects a yearning for connection with the divine and an understanding of one's place in the universe.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Spiritual Seeking: Individuals often seek a deeper connection with the divine or a higher power, much like the invocation of the Nāsatyas. This can manifest in practices like meditation, prayer, or community worship.
    • Community and Belonging: The question of where the divine resides among people can lead to discussions about community, shared values, and the importance of social connections in spiritual journeys.
    • Environmental Awareness: The reference to rivers can inspire a respect for nature and the environment, prompting individuals to consider the sources of life and the importance of preserving natural resources.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, humanity, and nature. It encourages individuals to seek truth, foster community, and respect the environment, highlighting the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom in modern life.
