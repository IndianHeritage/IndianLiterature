मन्त्र: तिस्रो वाच उद ईरते गावो मिमन्ति धेनवः |हरिर एति कणिक्रदत ||
tisro vāca ud īrate gāvo mimanti dhenavaḥ | harir eti kanikradat ||
  • tisro vāca ud īrate: This phrase translates to "the three words rise up." In this context, "vāca" refers to speech or words, and "tisro" signifies the number three. This can be interpreted as the importance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality.
  • gāvo mimanti dhenavaḥ: Here, "gāvo" means "cows," and "mimanti" can be understood as "they moo" or "they express." "Dhenavaḥ" refers to "the milch cows" or "nourishing." This part of the mantra emphasizes the nurturing aspect of life, symbolized by cows, which provide sustenance and nourishment.
  • harir eti kanikradat: "Harir" refers to "the Lord" or "the divine," while "eti" means "comes" or "approaches." "Kanikradat" can be interpreted as "from the sound" or "from the call." This suggests that the divine presence is invoked through sound or communication, highlighting the connection between the spiritual and the material world.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, nourishment, and divine presence. It teaches us that our words have power and that nurturing relationships, much like the relationship between humans and cows, is essential for a fulfilling life.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, in a family setting, the way we communicate with each other can either strengthen or weaken our bonds. Just as cows provide milk, our words can provide emotional nourishment. In professional environments, effective communication can lead to better teamwork and productivity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of our words and the nurturing relationships we cultivate. By being mindful of our communication and fostering connections, we can invite divine presence and harmony into our lives. This principle is applicable in all aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional interactions, emphasizing that our words and actions have the power to shape our reality.
