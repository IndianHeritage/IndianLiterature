पातां वृत्रहा सुतं आ घा गमन नारे अस्मत् |नियमते शतमूतिः ||
pātā vṛtrahā sutam ā ghā gaman nāre asmat | ni yamate śatamūtiḥ ||
  • pātā: This word translates to "O protector" or "O guardian." It signifies a plea for protection and guidance.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "slayer of Vritra," referring to the deity Indra, who is known for defeating the serpent Vritra, symbolizing chaos and obstacles.
  • sutam: This means "son" or "offspring," indicating a connection to lineage and the importance of progeny in Vedic culture.
  • ā ghā: This phrase can be interpreted as "come forth" or "approach," suggesting a call for divine presence or assistance.
  • gaman: This word means "movement" or "journey," indicating the importance of progress and the journey of life.
  • nāre: This translates to "to us" or "for us," emphasizing the personal nature of the plea.
  • asmat: This means "our" or "us," reinforcing the collective aspect of the invocation.
  • ni yamate: This phrase means "may you control" or "may you govern," indicating a desire for divine guidance and order.
  • śatamūtiḥ: This term translates to "hundredfold wisdom" or "great intelligence," suggesting the aspiration for abundant knowledge and understanding.

In essence, this mantra is a prayer for protection, guidance, and wisdom. It invokes the divine to assist in overcoming obstacles and to bless the speaker and their lineage with knowledge and strength.


Real-Life Application: This mantra can be applied in various life situations, such as during challenging times when one seeks clarity and direction. For instance, a student facing difficulties in their studies might recite this mantra to seek guidance and wisdom in their academic journey. Similarly, individuals embarking on new ventures, such as starting a business or moving to a new city, can use this mantra to invoke protection and support from the divine.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in the face of life's challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and wisdom, not just for oneself but for the collective. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of connection to the divine and a deeper understanding of their life's journey.
