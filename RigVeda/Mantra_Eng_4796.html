इमा उ त्वा शतक्रतोऽभि प्रणोनुवुर गिरः |इन्द्र वत्सं न मातरः ||
imā u tvā śatakrato 'bhi pra ṇonuvur giraḥ | indra vatsaṁ na mātaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imā: these
    • u: and
    • tvā: you
    • śatakrato: O hundred-fold (referring to Indra, the king of gods)
    • 'bhi: with
    • pra: forth
    • ṇonuvur: we invoke
    • giraḥ: words or hymns
    • indra: O Indra
    • vatsaṁ: the calf (symbolizing nourishment or care)
    • na: not
    • mātaraḥ: mothers (referring to the nurturing aspect)
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer invoking Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking him to listen to the hymns and words of praise offered by the devotees. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the nurturing aspects of life, symbolized by the term "vatsaṁ," which can be interpreted as a calf, representing care and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to Indra for rain, as it is essential for crop growth. This mantra can be recited during rituals to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In family settings, the nurturing aspect can be related to the care provided by mothers. This mantra can be a reminder to honor and appreciate the nurturing roles in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can be applied in any situation where support and sustenance are needed, such as during challenging times in personal or professional life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and the recognition of nurturing relationships in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers while also valuing the care provided by those around us. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in rituals for prosperity, during times of need, or simply as a daily reminder to appreciate the nurturing aspects of life. By reciting this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of connection with the divine and the community, promoting gratitude and support in their lives.
