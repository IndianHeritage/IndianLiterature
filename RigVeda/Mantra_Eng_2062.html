स्वः स्वाय धायसे कृतुता ऋत्विग ऋत्विजम् |स्तोमं यज्ञं चाद अरं वनेमा ररिमा वयम् ||
svaḥ svāya dhāyase kṛṇutām ṛtvig ṛtvijam | stomaṁ yajñaṁ cād araṁ vanemā rarimā vayam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svaḥ: Heaven or the celestial realm.
    • svāya: To oneself or for oneself.
    • dhāyase: You who are meditating or contemplating.
    • kṛṇutām: May you create or establish.
    • ṛtvig: The priest or the one who performs rituals.
    • ṛtvijam: The one who is engaged in the sacred rites.
    • stomaṁ: Praise or hymn.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering.
    • cād: And also.
    • araṁ: The one who is worthy or deserving.
    • vanemā: May we attain or reach.
    • rarimā: The one who is powerful or strong.
    • vayam: We.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the celestial powers and emphasizes the importance of self-reflection and meditation.
    • It encourages individuals to create and establish their own paths in life, much like a priest who performs rituals to connect with the divine.
    • The mention of hymns and sacrifices signifies the importance of gratitude and offerings in our lives, whether they are material or spiritual.
    • It highlights the collective strength of the community ("vayam") in achieving goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams come together to create innovative solutions, reflecting the idea of collective effort and individual contributions.
    • In personal development, individuals can meditate on their goals and aspirations, using the mantra as a reminder to establish their own paths and make sacrifices for their dreams.
    • In community service, the mantra can inspire groups to come together for a common cause, emphasizing the power of unity and shared purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individual efforts and collective strength.
    • It encourages self-reflection, the importance of rituals in daily life, and the need for community support in achieving personal and collective goals.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can foster a sense of purpose, gratitude, and collaboration in their endeavors.
