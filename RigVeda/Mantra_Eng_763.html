यमो ह जातो यमो जनित्वं जारः कनिनाम् पतिर जनिनाम् ||
yamo ha jāto yamo janitvaṁ jāraḥ kanīnām patir janīnām ||
  • Mantra: "yamo ha jāto yamo janitvaṁ jāraḥ kanīnām patir janīnām"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "He who is born, he who is the creator, is the lord of the young ones, the lord of the mothers."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Yamo: Refers to the one who is born or the one who has come into existence.
    • Jāto: Means 'born' or 'created'.
    • Janitvaṁ: Refers to the act of creation or birth.
    • Jāraḥ: Indicates 'lord' or 'master'.
    • Kanīnām: Refers to 'young ones' or 'children'.
    • Patir: Means 'lord' or 'husband'.
    • Janīnām: Refers to 'mothers' or 'those who give birth'.
  • Contextual Meaning: This mantra emphasizes the significance of creation and the roles of both the creator and the created. It highlights the relationship between parents and children, where the creator (often interpreted as a divine force or God) is seen as the protector and lord of the young ones, as well as the mothers who nurture them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often take on the role of protectors and guides for their children, embodying the essence of the mantra as they nurture and support the young ones.
    • In a broader societal context, educators and mentors can be seen as modern-day embodiments of this mantra, as they guide and shape the future generations.
    • In nature, the relationship between animals and their offspring reflects this mantra, where the mother protects and nurtures her young, ensuring their survival and growth.
  • Significance: The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacred bond between parents and children, emphasizing the responsibilities that come with creation and nurturing. It encourages respect for the roles of both mothers and fathers in the upbringing of children, highlighting the importance of love, care, and guidance in fostering a healthy environment for growth.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • In parenting, it encourages parents to be mindful of their roles and responsibilities towards their children.
    • In community building, it inspires individuals to take on mentorship roles, guiding the younger generation.
    • In spiritual practices, it can be a reminder to honor the divine aspects of creation and the interconnectedness of life.
