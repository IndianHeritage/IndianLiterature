अवोचाम कवये मेध्याय वचो वन्दारु वृषभाय वृष्णे |गविष्टिरो नमसā स्तोमम् अग्नौ दिवीव रुक्मम् उर्व्यञ्चम् अश्रेत् ||
avocāma kavaye medhyāya vaco vandāru vṛṣabhāya vṛṣṇe | gaviṣṭhiro namasā stomam agnau divīva rukmam uruvyañcam aśret ||
  • avocāma: This word translates to "we have spoken" or "we have declared." It signifies the act of expressing thoughts or intentions, highlighting the importance of communication in human relationships.
  • kavaye: This means "to the poet" or "to the wise one." It refers to those who possess knowledge and insight, suggesting that wisdom is essential in guiding our words and actions.
  • medhyāya: This term means "for the pure" or "for the wise." It emphasizes the need for purity of thought and intention in our expressions, aligning our words with our values.
  • vaco: This translates to "words." Words hold power, and this part of the mantra reminds us of the significance of what we say and how it can impact others.
  • vandāru: This means "to the revered" or "to the worthy." It suggests that our words should be directed towards those who deserve respect and honor, reinforcing the idea of gratitude and acknowledgment.
  • vṛṣabhāya: This translates to "to the strong" or "to the bull." It symbolizes strength and stability, indicating that our expressions should be grounded in strength and resilience.
  • vṛṣṇe: This refers to "to Vishnu" or "to the divine." It signifies the connection between our words and the divine, suggesting that our expressions should be aligned with higher principles.
  • gaviṣṭhiro: This means "with great effort" or "with determination." It highlights the importance of putting in effort and dedication in our pursuits, whether in communication or other aspects of life.
  • namasā: This translates to "with reverence" or "with respect." It emphasizes the need to approach our expressions and interactions with humility and honor.
  • stomam: This means "praise" or "hymn." It signifies the act of celebrating and acknowledging the positive aspects of life and the divine.
  • agnau: This translates to "in the fire." Fire symbolizes transformation and purification, indicating that our words and intentions should lead to positive change.
  • divīva: This means "like the heavens." It suggests that our expressions should aspire to the heights of virtue and goodness, aiming for a higher purpose.
  • rukmam: This translates to "golden" or "precious." It signifies the value of our words and thoughts, encouraging us to treat them as treasures.
  • uruvyañcam: This means "with great abundance." It emphasizes the idea of sharing our thoughts and expressions generously, contributing positively to the world around us.
  • aśret: This translates to "may it be established" or "may it be realized." It signifies the hope that our words and intentions manifest in reality.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wise and pure communication, urging us to express our thoughts with respect, strength, and reverence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and spiritual practices. By aligning our words with higher principles and values, we can foster positive interactions and contribute to a harmonious society. The mantra serves as a reminder that our expressions hold power and should be used to uplift and inspire, ultimately leading to transformation and growth in ourselves and others.
