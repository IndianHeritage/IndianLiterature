यज्ञं पृच्छाम्य अवमं स तद दूतो वि वोकति |क्व ऋतम पूर्व्यं गतं कस्तद bibharति नूतनं वित्तं मे अस्य रोदसी ||
yajñam pṛcchāmy avamaṁ sa tad dūto vi vocati | kva ṛtam pūrvyaṁ gataṁ kas tad bibharti nūtano vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • yajñam: This term refers to a sacrificial offering or ritual, often associated with the Vedic tradition. In real life, it can symbolize any act of selfless giving or service to others.
  • pṛcchāmy: This means "I ask" or "I inquire." It reflects the human quest for knowledge and understanding, akin to seeking answers in our daily lives through education or mentorship.
  • avamaṁ: This word translates to "the lowest" or "the least." It can represent humility and the importance of recognizing one's place in the world, reminding us to be grounded and modest.
  • sa: This means "that" or "he." It refers to the divine or the higher power that guides us.
  • dūto: This translates to "messenger." In a modern context, it can symbolize communication, whether through technology or personal interactions, emphasizing the importance of conveying messages effectively.
  • vi vocati: This means "speaks" or "declares." It highlights the power of words and communication in shaping our reality and relationships.
  • kva: This means "where." It prompts us to consider our surroundings and the context of our actions.
  • ṛtam: This term signifies "truth" or "order." It represents the universal principles that govern life, akin to the laws of nature or ethics in society.
  • pūrvyaṁ: This means "previous" or "ancient." It connects us to our heritage and the wisdom of our ancestors, reminding us to learn from the past.
  • gataṁ: This translates to "gone" or "departed." It signifies the transient nature of life and the importance of cherishing the present moment.
  • kas: This means "who." It invites introspection about our identity and purpose.
  • tad: This means "that." It can refer to the divine or the ultimate truth we seek.
  • bibharti: This translates to "carries" or "supports." It emphasizes the idea of sustenance, whether physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • nūtano: This means "new" or "fresh." It represents renewal and the potential for growth and change in our lives.
  • vittam: This translates to "wealth" or "resources." It can refer to both material and spiritual wealth, highlighting the importance of balance in our pursuits.
  • me: This means "my." It personalizes the mantra, making it relevant to the individual.
  • asya: This means "of this." It connects the individual to the broader universe.
  • rodasī: This term refers to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all existence.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of inquiry, humility, and the pursuit of truth. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding while recognizing their place in the universe. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, personal growth, and community service. By embodying the principles of selflessness, communication, and respect for the past, we can navigate our lives with purpose and integrity, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
