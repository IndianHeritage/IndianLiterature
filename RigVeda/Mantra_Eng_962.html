श्रियसे कम भानुभिः सम मिमिक्षिरे ते रश्मिभिस त ऋक्वभिः सुखादयः |ते वाशीमन्त इष्मिणो अभीरवो विद्रे प्रियस्य मारुतस्य धाम्नः ||
śriyase kam bhānubhiḥ sam mimikṣire te raśmibhis ta ṛkvabhiḥ sukhādayaḥ | te vāśīmanta iṣmiṇo abhīravo vidre priyasya mārutasya dhāmnaḥ ||
  • śriyase: This word refers to prosperity, wealth, or abundance. In real life, it can be associated with financial stability or success in one's endeavors.
  • kam: This translates to "who" or "what," indicating a search for understanding or inquiry into the nature of prosperity.
  • bhānubhiḥ: This means "by the sun" or "radiant," symbolizing enlightenment, knowledge, and clarity. For example, just as the sun illuminates the world, knowledge can illuminate our paths in life.
  • sam mimikṣire: This phrase can be interpreted as "they have looked upon" or "they have observed," suggesting a careful consideration or awareness of prosperity.
  • te: This means "you" or "those," referring to the divine or higher powers that bestow blessings.
  • raśmibhis: This translates to "with rays," indicating the influence of light or energy in achieving prosperity.
  • ta ṛkvabhiḥ: This can be understood as "with hymns" or "with verses," emphasizing the importance of spiritual practices and devotion in attaining wealth and happiness.
  • sukhādayaḥ: This means "sources of happiness," highlighting that true prosperity is not just material wealth but also includes emotional and spiritual well-being.
  • te vāśīmanta: This phrase suggests "you who are all-pervading," indicating the omnipresence of divine energy in our lives.
  • iṣmiṇo: This translates to "in this," referring to the current state of existence or reality.
  • abhīravo: This means "fearless" or "courageous," suggesting that true prosperity comes with confidence and bravery in facing life's challenges.
  • vidre: This can be interpreted as "to break through," indicating the need to overcome obstacles to achieve prosperity.
  • priyasya: This means "of the beloved," suggesting that prosperity is often linked to relationships and connections with others.
  • mārutasya: This refers to the "winds" or "forces of nature," symbolizing the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life that can influence our journey towards prosperity.
  • dhāmnaḥ: This translates to "of the abode" or "of the dwelling," indicating a place of stability and security that prosperity can provide.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the multifaceted nature of prosperity, which encompasses not only material wealth but also emotional and spiritual fulfillment. It highlights the importance of knowledge, courage, and relationships in achieving a prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their pursuits, reminding them that true wealth comes from a harmonious blend of financial success, personal happiness, and strong connections with others. By invoking the divine energies represented in the mantra, one can cultivate a mindset that attracts abundance and joy into their life.
