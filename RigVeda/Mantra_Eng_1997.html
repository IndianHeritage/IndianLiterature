ये अंश्या ये अङ्ग्याः सूचिका ये प्रकङ्कताḥ | अदृष्टाḥ किं चनेह वः सर्वे साकं निजस्यत ||
ye aṁsyā ye aṅgyāḥ sūcīkā ye prakaṅkatāḥ | adṛṣṭāḥ kiṁ caneha vaḥ sarve sākaṁ ni jasyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • aṁsyā: who are parts or limbs
    • ye: those who
    • aṅgyāḥ: who are vital or essential
    • sūcīkā: who are indicators or signs
    • ye: those who
    • prakaṅkatāḥ: who are manifest or evident
    • adṛṣṭāḥ: unseen or not perceived
    • kiṁ: what
    • caneha: is here
    • vaḥ: for you
    • sarve: all
    • sākaṁ: together
    • ni: not
    • jasyata: will be separated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of all beings and elements.
    • It emphasizes that all parts, whether seen or unseen, are integral to the whole.
    • The use of terms like "limbs" and "indicators" suggests that every individual has a role to play in the larger context of existence.
    • It highlights the importance of unity and collective existence, where separation is not an option.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, each member plays a unique role, contributing to the overall harmony and functioning of the household.
    • In a workplace, every employee, regardless of their position, is essential for the success of the organization.
    • In nature, every species, whether large or small, has a role in maintaining the ecosystem's balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing our interconnectedness and the roles we play in the larger scheme of life.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the contributions of others and to work together harmoniously.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to better relationships, teamwork, and a more compassionate society.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that separation is an illusion, and unity is the essence of existence.
