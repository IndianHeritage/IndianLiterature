दधिक्रावण इद उ नु चर्किराम विश्वा इन माम उषसः सूदयन्तु | अपाम अग्नेर उषसः सूर्यस्य बृहस्पतेर आङ्गिरसस्य जित्नोः ||
dadhikrāvṇa id u nu carkirāma viśvā in mām uṣasaḥ sūdayantu | apām agner uṣasaḥ sūryasya bṛhaspater āṅgirasasya jiṣṇoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dadhikrāvṇa id u nu carkirāma viśvā in mām uṣasaḥ sūdayantu | apām agner uṣasaḥ sūryasya bṛhaspater āṅgirasasya jiṣṇoḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhikrāvṇa: May the forces of nature be strong and powerful.
    • id: Here, in this moment.
    • u nu: Indeed, truly.
    • carkirāma: We are invoking or calling upon.
    • viśvā: All, the entirety of existence.
    • in mām: In me, within myself.
    • uṣasaḥ: Of the dawn, the new beginning.
    • sūdayantu: May they purify or cleanse.
    • apām: Of the waters.
    • agner: Of fire.
    • sūryasya: Of the sun.
    • bṛhaspater: Of the great sage or teacher.
    • āṅgirasasya: Of the Angiras, a group of sages.
    • jiṣṇoḥ: Of the victorious or the one who conquers.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for strength and purification, invoking the powers of nature and the divine.
    • The phrase "May the forces of nature be strong" suggests a call for resilience and support from the universe.
    • By saying "in me," it emphasizes the importance of internal strength and self-awareness.
    • The mention of "dawn" symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the potential for transformation.
    • Invoking the elements of water, fire, and the sun signifies the holistic approach to life, recognizing the interconnectedness of all elements.
    • The reference to sages like Bṛhaspati and Angiras highlights the importance of wisdom and guidance in our journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, one might recite this mantra to seek inner strength and clarity, much like how individuals turn to meditation or prayer for support.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during rituals or gatherings to invoke collective strength and unity, similar to how teams come together for motivation before a big event.
    • In environmental contexts, the invocation of natural elements can remind us of our responsibility to protect and respect nature, akin to movements advocating for sustainability and ecological balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that lies within us and the support we can draw from the universe.
    • Its significance is profound in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community bonding and environmental consciousness.
    • By integrating the essence of this mantra into our daily lives, we can cultivate resilience, wisdom, and a deeper connection to the world around us.
