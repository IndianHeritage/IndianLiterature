आ पप्रौ पार्थिवं रजो बद्बदहे रोचनā दिवि |न त्वावाम् इन्द्र कश्चन न जातो न जनिष्यते 'ति विश्वं ववक्षिथ ||
ā paprau pārthivaṁ rajo badbadhe rocanā divi | na tvāvām̐ indra kaś cana na jāto na janiṣyate 'ti viśvaṁ vavakṣitha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā paprau: "I have attained" or "I have reached."
    • pārthivaṁ: "earthly" or "material."
    • rajo: "the essence" or "the quality."
    • badbadhe: "to be made manifest" or "to be revealed."
    • rocanā: "brightness" or "radiance."
    • divi: "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm."
    • na: "not."
    • tvāvām̐: "you two" or "both of you."
    • indra: "O Indra," referring to the king of gods.
    • kaś cana: "anyone" or "anyone at all."
    • na jāto: "not born" or "not created."
    • na janiṣyate: "will not be born" or "will not come into existence."
    • ati: "beyond" or "exceeding."
    • viśvaṁ: "the universe" or "all that exists."
    • vavakṣitha: "you have declared" or "you have proclaimed."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the attainment of earthly qualities and the manifestation of divine brightness.
    • It emphasizes the relationship between the material and the divine, suggesting that true understanding and enlightenment come from recognizing both realms.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the importance of divine guidance and strength in achieving one's goals.
    • The phrase "not born" suggests that certain truths or qualities are eternal and unchanging, existing beyond the physical realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, one might seek to attain qualities such as wisdom and compassion (earthly qualities) while also striving for spiritual enlightenment (divine brightness).
    • In leadership, a leader may embody both practical skills (material) and visionary thinking (divine), guiding their team towards success.
    • In scientific pursuits, researchers often seek to understand the material world while also contemplating the deeper truths of existence, reflecting the duality expressed in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of balancing earthly pursuits with spiritual aspirations.
    • It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment while remaining grounded in reality, fostering a holistic approach to life.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal growth, leadership, or scientific inquiry—this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the material and the divine.
