चतस्र इं घृतदुहः साचन्ते समाने अन्तर धरणे निषत्ताः |तां इमर्षन्ति नमसā पुनानास तां इं विश्वतः परि शन्ति पूर्वीः ||
catasra īṁ ghṛtaduhaḥ sacante samāne antar dharuṇe niṣattāḥ | tā īm arṣanti namasā punānās tā īṁ viśvataḥ pari ṣanti pūrvīḥ ||
  • catasra: This word translates to "four." In various contexts, the number four can symbolize stability and foundation, much like the four corners of a square or the four cardinal directions.
  • īṁ: This is a sacred syllable often associated with the divine or spiritual energy. It represents the essence of creation and consciousness.
  • ghṛtaduhaḥ: This term refers to "the one who milks ghee." Ghee is a symbol of purity and nourishment in Vedic literature, representing the essence of life and sustenance.
  • sacante: This means "to be present" or "to exist." It emphasizes the importance of being in the moment and recognizing the divine presence in our lives.
  • samāne: This translates to "equal" or "similar." It suggests harmony and balance, which are essential for a peaceful existence.
  • antar: Meaning "within," this word encourages introspection and the understanding that true knowledge and divinity reside within us.
  • dharuṇe: This term can be interpreted as "the one who supports" or "the bearer." It signifies strength and stability, akin to the earth that supports all life.
  • niṣattāḥ: This word means "to be firm" or "to be resolute." It highlights the importance of determination and steadfastness in our pursuits.
  • tā: This translates to "those" or "they," referring to the divine forces or energies being invoked.
  • īm: Similar to the earlier mention, this syllable reinforces the connection to the divine and the spiritual realm.
  • arṣanti: This means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It suggests the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • namasā: This translates to "with reverence" or "with respect." It emphasizes the importance of humility and gratitude in our interactions with the divine.
  • punānās: This term means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It signifies the process of spiritual growth and the importance of purification in our lives.
  • tā: Again, referring to the divine energies or forces.
  • īm: Reiterating the connection to the divine.
  • viśvataḥ: This means "from all sides" or "universally." It suggests the omnipresence of the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • pari: This translates to "around" or "surrounding," indicating the encompassing nature of divine energy.
  • ṣanti: Meaning "to be" or "to exist," reinforcing the idea of presence and existence.
  • pūrvīḥ: This term means "the ancients" or "the elders," referring to the wisdom of those who came before us and the importance of learning from history.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine presence, stability, and the pursuit of knowledge and purity. It encourages individuals to seek balance and harmony within themselves and their surroundings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as meditation practices, rituals, or daily affirmations that promote mindfulness and spiritual growth. By recognizing the divine within and around us, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of our purpose and foster a sense of interconnectedness with the universe.
