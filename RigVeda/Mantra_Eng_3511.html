मम द्विता राष्ट्रं क्षत्रियस्य विश्वायोर्विश्वे अमृताः यथा नः |क्रतुं साचन्ते वरुणस्य देवाः राजामि कृष्टर उपमस्य वव्रेः ||
mama dvitā rāṣṭraṁ kṣatriyasya viśvāyor viśve amṛtā yathā naḥ | kratuṁ sacante varuṇasya devā rājāmi kṛṣṭer upamasya vavreḥ ||
  • Translation: "May I have a second kingdom, like the Kshatriyas, who are immortal in all directions. The gods, who are capable of great deeds, have chosen me as the ruler of the land of agriculture."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mama: my
    • dvitā: second
    • rāṣṭraṁ: kingdom
    • kṣatriyasya: of the Kshatriyas (warriors)
    • viśvāyor: in all directions
    • viśve: all
    • amṛtā: immortal
    • yathā: as
    • naḥ: ours
    • kratuṁ: power or capability
    • sacante: who are capable
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of water and cosmic order)
    • devā: gods
    • rājāmi: I rule
    • kṛṣṭer: of agriculture
    • upamasya: of the comparison
    • vavreḥ: have chosen
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the desire for prosperity and strength, akin to the Kshatriyas, who were known for their valor and governance.
    • The mention of "immortal" signifies a longing for enduring legacy and impact, much like leaders who strive to create lasting change in society.
    • The invocation of Varuna emphasizes the importance of cosmic order and moral authority in leadership, suggesting that true power comes from righteousness.
    • The reference to agriculture highlights the significance of sustenance and the role of the ruler in ensuring the welfare of the people.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders often seek to create policies that ensure the welfare of their citizens, much like the desire expressed in the mantra for a prosperous kingdom.
    • Business leaders can draw parallels from this mantra by striving for ethical practices and sustainable growth, ensuring their companies contribute positively to society.
    • Community leaders who work towards the betterment of their neighborhoods embody the spirit of this mantra by fostering unity and resilience among residents.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with power and leadership. It emphasizes the importance of striving for a greater good, ensuring that one's actions benefit not just oneself but the community at large.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals in positions of authority to act with integrity, seek prosperity for all, and maintain a balance between power and moral duty.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires a vision of leadership that is both aspirational and grounded in the well-being of society, making it relevant in various contexts, from politics to business and community service.
