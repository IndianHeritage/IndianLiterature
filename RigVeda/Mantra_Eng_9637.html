अग्निं विशा ईळते मानवीर या अग्निम मनुषो नहुषो वि जाताः | अग्निर गान्धर्वीम् पथ्याम ऋतस्याग्नेर गव्यूतिर घृत आ निषत्ताः ||
agniṁ viśa īḻate mānuṣīr yā agnim manuṣo nahuṣo vi jātāḥ | agnir gāndharvīm pathyām ṛtasyāgner gavyūtir ghṛta ā niṣattā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agniṁ: Fire
    • Viśa: To shine or illuminate
    • Īḻate: To be worshipped or revered
    • Mānuṣīr: Human or pertaining to humans
    • Yā: Who
    • Agnim: Fire (again)
    • Manuṣo: Of humans
    • Nahuṣo: A reference to a king or a powerful being
    • Vi: In this context, it can mean 'in' or 'with'
    • Jātāḥ: Born or created
    • Agniḥ: Fire (again)
    • Gāndharvīm: Celestial beings or divine musicians
    • Pathyām: In the path or way
    • Ṛtasyāgner: Of the truth of fire
    • Gavyūtir: Abundance or richness
    • Ghṛta: Ghee or clarified butter
    • Ā: To or towards
    • Niṣattā: Strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine spark within humans.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire in human life, not just as a physical element but as a spiritual guide.
    • The reference to "Gāndharvīm" suggests that fire is also connected to the divine and the arts, indicating that creativity and inspiration are ignited by this element.
    • The mantra highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, suggesting that through reverence and worship of Agni, one can attain strength and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals and ceremonies, symbolizing purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'igniting' passion or creativity can be seen in various fields such as art, music, and entrepreneurship, where individuals draw inspiration from their inner 'fire' to create and innovate.
    • Fire safety and respect for fire in daily life, such as in cooking or heating, reflects the reverence for Agni as a life-giving force that must be respected and managed wisely.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of fire as a source of life, creativity, and divine connection.
    • It teaches us to respect and revere the elements that sustain us, recognizing their role in our spiritual and physical existence.
    • In real life, the principles derived from this mantra can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to seek inspiration, embrace transformation, and cultivate strength through our actions and intentions.
