पराणुदस्व मघवन्न अमित्रान सुवेदा नो वसू कृधि |अस्माकं बोध्य अविता महाधने भव वृद्धः सखीनाम् ||
parā ṇudasva maghavann amitrān suvedā no vasū kṛdhi | asmākam bodhy avitā mahādhane bhavā vṛdhaḥ sakhīnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: beyond, supreme
    • ṇudasva: may you inspire or awaken
    • maghavann: O powerful one, referring to a deity or divine force
    • amitrān: against our enemies
    • suvedā: with good knowledge or wisdom
    • no: our
    • vasū: wealth or prosperity
    • kṛdhi: may you create or bestow
    • asmākam: our
    • bodhy: may you enlighten or make us aware
    • avitā: protector or guardian
    • mahādhane: in great wealth or abundance
    • bhavā: may you become
    • vṛdhaḥ: great or powerful
    • sakhīnām: of friends or companions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine assistance and protection.
    • It calls upon a powerful deity to inspire and awaken the supreme knowledge within us.
    • It seeks the creation of wealth and prosperity while ensuring protection against adversaries.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and enlightenment in achieving success and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking guidance and protection in competitive environments.
    • Individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times to invoke strength and wisdom to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, it can be a reminder to foster supportive and prosperous connections with friends and family.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for divine guidance, wisdom, and protection.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional success, highlighting the interconnectedness of knowledge, wealth, and relationships.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that attracts prosperity and fosters resilience against challenges.
