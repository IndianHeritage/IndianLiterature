विष्वा द्वेषांसी जहि चावा च कृत्‍हि विश्वे संवन्त्वा वसु |शीष्टेषु चित्ते मदिरासो अंशवो यत्रा सोमस्य तृम्पसि ||
viśvā dveṣāṁsi jahi cāva cā kṛdhi viśve sanvantv ā vasu | śīṣṭeṣu cit te madirāso aṁśavo yatrā somasya tṛmpasi ||
  • viśvā dveṣāṁsi jahi: This phrase translates to "overcome all enmities." It emphasizes the importance of eliminating hatred and negativity from our lives. In real life, this can be seen in conflict resolution, where individuals or groups work to resolve their differences through dialogue and understanding.
  • cāva cā kṛdhi: This means "and also act with good intentions." It encourages individuals to not only avoid negativity but also to engage in positive actions. For example, volunteering for community service or helping a neighbor in need reflects this principle.
  • viśve sanvantv ā vasu: This translates to "may all beings be prosperous." It highlights the interconnectedness of all life and the desire for collective well-being. In practice, this can be seen in initiatives aimed at sustainable development, where the focus is on ensuring that resources are available for future generations.
  • śīṣṭeṣu cit te madirāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "may your consciousness be filled with wisdom." It suggests the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be applied through education and lifelong learning, where individuals strive to expand their horizons and make informed decisions.
  • aṁśavo yatrā somasya tṛmpasi: This means "where the essence of the divine is experienced." It signifies the pursuit of spiritual fulfillment and connection with the divine. In everyday life, this can manifest through practices such as meditation, prayer, or engaging in nature, where individuals seek a deeper understanding of their existence and purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of overcoming negativity, acting with good intentions, promoting collective prosperity, seeking wisdom, and experiencing the divine. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to foster harmony, engage in positive actions, and pursue knowledge and spiritual growth. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous world, both for ourselves and for future generations.
