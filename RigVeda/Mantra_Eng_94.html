एहि स्तोमां अभि स्वराभि गृणीहि आ रुva |ब्रह्म च नो वसो साचिन्द्र यज्ञं च वर्धय ||
ehi stomām̐ abhi svarābhi gṛṇīhy ā ruva | brahma ca no vaso sacendra yajñaṁ ca vardhaya ||
  • ehi stomām̐: This phrase can be translated as "come, O hymn" or "come, O praise." It signifies an invitation to the divine or the sacred, calling upon the power of the hymn to manifest.
  • abhi svarābhi: This means "with the sounds" or "with the melodies." It emphasizes the importance of sound and vibration in spiritual practices, suggesting that the act of chanting or singing has a profound impact.
  • gṛṇīhy ā ruva: This can be interpreted as "let us sing" or "let us chant." It encourages collective participation in the act of worship or praise, highlighting the communal aspect of spirituality.
  • brahma ca no vaso: This translates to "may Brahman (the ultimate reality) be our abode." It reflects the desire for unity with the divine and the aspiration to dwell in a state of higher consciousness.
  • sacendra: This term refers to "the true lord" or "the true ruler." It signifies the importance of aligning oneself with truth and righteousness, suggesting that true leadership comes from a place of integrity.
  • yajñaṁ ca vardhaya: This means "may the sacrifice (or offering) be increased." In Vedic tradition, yajña represents selfless action and offerings to the divine, indicating that such acts should be amplified for greater spiritual benefit.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of collective worship, the power of sound, and the aspiration for unity with the divine. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, spiritual retreats, or any form of collective action aimed at uplifting the community. The significance lies in recognizing the importance of coming together in harmony, using the power of sound and intention to elevate our consciousness and foster a sense of belonging. By engaging in collective practices, we can enhance our spiritual growth and contribute positively to our surroundings, embodying the principles of truth and selfless service.
