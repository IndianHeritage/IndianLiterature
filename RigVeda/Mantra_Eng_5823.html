उत स्वयां तंवा सं वदेत तद् कदां न्व अन्तर्वरुणे भुवानि |किम मे हव्यं अहृणानो जुषेत कदां मृळीकं सुमना अभि ख्यम् ||
uta svayā tanvā3 saṁ vade tat kadā nv a1ntar varuṇe bhuvāni | kim me havyam ahṛṇāno juṣeta kadā mṛḻīkaṁ sumanā abhi khyam ||
  • uta svayā tanvā: This phrase can be translated as "with my own body" or "with my own self." It emphasizes the importance of personal agency and the connection between the individual and the divine.
  • saṁ vade tat kadā nv a1ntar varuṇe bhuvāni: This part translates to "when shall I speak that which is within me to Varuna?" Varuna is the Vedic god associated with cosmic order and truth. This line reflects the desire to communicate one's innermost thoughts and feelings to a higher power.
  • kim me havyam ahṛṇāno juṣeta: This translates to "what offering shall I make that is not taken away?" It raises the question of what is truly valuable and worthy of offering to the divine, suggesting a search for authenticity in one's actions and intentions.
  • kadā mṛḻīkaṁ sumanā abhi khyam: This can be interpreted as "when shall I be blessed with a good mind?" It expresses a longing for mental clarity and wisdom, which are essential for making meaningful contributions to the world.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of self-awareness, communication with the divine, and the pursuit of authenticity and wisdom. It encourages individuals to reflect on their inner thoughts and the offerings they make in life.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in self-reflection, seek clarity in their intentions, and strive for authenticity in their actions. For example, in a professional setting, one might consider what genuine contributions they can make to their team or organization, rather than simply going through the motions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with one's inner self and the divine. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and authenticity in their lives, fostering a deeper understanding of their purpose and the impact of their actions on the world around them.
