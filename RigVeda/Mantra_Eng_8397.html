प्र सोमस्य पवमानस्योर्मय इन्द्रस्य यन्ति जठरं सुपेशसः | दध्ना यद ईम उन्नीताऽयशसा गवां दानाय शूरम उदमन्दिषुः सुताः ||
pra somasya pavamānasyormaya indrasya yanti jaṭharaṁ supeśasaḥ | dadhnā yad īm unnītā yaśasā gavāṁ dānāya śūram udamandiṣuḥ sutāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pra somasya pavamānasyormaya indrasya yanti jaṭharaṁ supeśasaḥ | dadhnā yad īm unnītā yaśasā gavāṁ dānāya śūram udamandiṣuḥ sutāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the essence of the divine, invoking the energy of Indra, the king of gods, and the sacred drink, Soma. It highlights the importance of strength, valor, and the blessings that come from divine sources.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pra: Towards, for the sake of.
    • Somasya: Of Soma, the divine nectar.
    • Pavamānasyor: Of the purifying essence.
    • Maya: Our, belonging to us.
    • Indrasya: Of Indra, the god of rain and thunder.
    • Yanti: Go, move towards.
    • Jaṭharaṁ: The belly, the source of nourishment.
    • Supeśasaḥ: Well-formed, auspicious.
    • Dadnā: By giving, through offering.
    • Yad: That which.
    • Īm: We desire, we seek.
    • Unnītā: Elevated, uplifted.
    • Yaśasā: With glory, fame.
    • Gavāṁ: Of cows, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • Dānāya: For the purpose of giving, generosity.
    • Śūram: Brave, valiant.
    • Udamandiṣuḥ: In the waters, in the flow of life.
    • Sutāḥ: The sons, offspring, or products.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the importance of community and support systems. Just as the essence of Soma nourishes and purifies, so do our relationships and connections with others.
    • In a corporate setting, the invocation of strength and valor can be seen in teamwork, where individuals uplift each other to achieve common goals, much like the blessings of Indra.
    • In personal life, the mantra encourages individuals to seek glory and success through hard work and generosity, akin to how farmers nurture their crops for a bountiful harvest.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors and the need for strength and valor in our pursuits.
    • It teaches us to seek nourishment not just from physical sources but also from spiritual and communal connections.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to be generous, to uplift others, and to strive for glory through hard work and collaboration.
