प्र तद विष्णुः स्तवते वीर्येण मृगो न भीमः कुचरो गिरिष्ठाः |यस्योरुषु त्रिषु विक्रमणेष्व अधिक्षियन्ति भुवनानि विश्वा ||
pra tad viṣṇuḥ stavate vīryeṇa mṛgo na bhīmaḥ kucaro giriṣṭhāḥ | yasyoruṣu triṣu vikramaṇeṣv adhikṣiyanti bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for, towards
    • tad: that
    • viṣṇuḥ: Vishnu, the preserver
    • stavate: praises, worships
    • vīryeṇa: with strength, power
    • mṛgo: the deer, symbolizing gentleness
    • na: not
    • bhīmaḥ: fearful, terrifying
    • kucaro: the one who roams
    • giriṣṭhāḥ: on the mountains, high places
    • yasy: whose
    • oruṣu: in the thighs
    • triṣu: in three
    • vikramaṇeṣv: in the acts of movement
    • adhikṣiyanti: dominate, control
    • bhuvanāni: the worlds
    • viśvā: all, universal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and strength of Lord Vishnu, who is revered as the preserver of the universe.
    • The imagery of a deer, which is often seen as gentle and non-threatening, contrasts with the idea of fearlessness and strength.
    • The phrase "whose thighs dominate the worlds" suggests that the strength of Vishnu is such that it governs all realms of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Vishnu can inspire their team to work fearlessly and with strength, much like how Vishnu governs the universe.
    • In personal life, individuals can draw strength from this mantra during challenging times, reminding themselves to be fearless and to act with purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, fearlessness, and the ability to govern one's life and surroundings.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like Vishnu, we can harness our inner strength to navigate the challenges of life.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be chanted during meditation or yoga practices to instill a sense of courage and empowerment.
