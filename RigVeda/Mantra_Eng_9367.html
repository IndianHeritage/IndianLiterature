यो यज्ञस्य प्रसाधनस् तन्तुर देवेष्व आततः |तम आहुतं नशीमहि ||
yo yajñasya prasādhanas tantur deveṣv ātataḥ | tam āhutaṁ naśīmahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice
    • prasādhanas: the one who prepares or offers
    • tantur: the thread or connection
    • deveṣv: among the gods
    • ātataḥ: who is steadfast or firm
    • tam: that
    • āhutaṁ: invoked or called
    • naśīmahi: we meditate upon or we invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of sacrifice (yajña) and the connection (tantur) it creates with the divine (deveṣv).
    • It emphasizes the role of the one who prepares the sacrifice, highlighting the significance of intention and dedication in spiritual practices.
    • The term "āhutaṁ" indicates the act of invoking the divine presence, suggesting that through sincere offerings, one can connect with higher powers.
    • The phrase "naśīmahi" reflects a collective intention to meditate upon or invoke this divine connection, indicating the communal aspect of spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, rituals and ceremonies such as weddings, festivals, or community gatherings can be seen as forms of yajña, where people come together to celebrate and invoke blessings.
    • In personal life, acts of kindness and charity can be viewed as sacrifices that create a connection with the community and the divine, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • Practices like meditation and prayer can be seen as a way to prepare oneself spiritually, aligning with the essence of "prasādhanas" in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention and community in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches that through sincere offerings and actions, one can establish a connection with the divine, leading to personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in acts of service, meditation, and community rituals, fostering a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and their relationship with the divine.
