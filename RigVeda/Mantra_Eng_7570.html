इन्द्र शुद्धो हि नो रयिं शुद्धो रत्नानि दाशुषे |शुद्धो वृत्राणि जिघ्नसे शुद्धो वाजं सिषाससि ||
indra śuddho hi no rayiṁ śuddho ratnāni dāśuṣe | śuddho vṛtrāṇi jighnase śuddho vājaṁ siṣāsasi ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Śuddho: Means 'pure' or 'clean', indicating a state of clarity and righteousness.
  • Hi: A particle that emphasizes the statement, often translated as 'indeed' or 'certainly'.
  • No: Means 'our', indicating possession or belonging.
  • Rayiṁ: Refers to wealth or prosperity, often associated with material and spiritual abundance.
  • Ratnāni: Means 'gems' or 'precious things', symbolizing valuable resources or qualities.
  • Dāśuṣe: Refers to the act of giving or bestowing, often in the context of generosity or sharing.
  • Vṛtrāṇi: Refers to obstacles or enemies, often symbolizing challenges that need to be overcome.
  • Jighnase: Means 'to slay' or 'to destroy', indicating the act of overcoming challenges.
  • Vājaṁ: Refers to 'strength' or 'power', often associated with success in endeavors.
  • Siṣāsasi: Means 'to bestow' or 'to grant', indicating the act of providing or giving strength.

This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity, to bless us with purity in our wealth and resources. It emphasizes the importance of having a clear and righteous approach to prosperity and the act of giving. The mantra also seeks the strength to overcome obstacles and challenges in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In business, it encourages ethical practices and the importance of sharing wealth with others, promoting a culture of generosity.
  • In personal life, it serves as a reminder to maintain purity in intentions and actions, ensuring that one's pursuits are aligned with righteousness.
  • In facing challenges, it inspires individuals to seek strength and clarity in overcoming difficulties, reinforcing the belief that obstacles can be conquered with the right mindset.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, strength, and generosity. It teaches us that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about the purity of our intentions and the strength to overcome challenges. By embodying these principles, we can lead a more fulfilling and impactful life.
