तन् नस् तुरीपम् अद्भुतम् पुरु वारम् पुरु तमनाऽ |त्वष्टा पोषाय विष्यतु राये नाभा नो अस्मयुः ||
tan nas turīpam adbhutam puru vāram puru tmanā | tvaṣṭā poṣāya vi ṣyatu rāye nābhā no asmayuḥ ||
  • tan nas turīpam adbhutam puru vāram puru tmanā
    • tan - "that" or "this," referring to the divine or the ultimate reality.
    • nas - "to us," indicating a personal connection or request.
    • turīpam - "the wonderful" or "the extraordinary," suggesting something beyond the ordinary experience.
    • adbhutam - "marvelous" or "wondrous," emphasizing the awe-inspiring nature of the divine.
    • puru - "man" or "being," indicating the human aspect of existence.
    • vāram - "protection" or "shelter," implying a need for safety and security.
    • puru tmanā - "with a great spirit," suggesting a connection to the higher self or consciousness.
  • tvaṣṭā poṣāya vi ṣyatu
    • tvaṣṭā - "the creator" or "the divine craftsman," referring to the aspect of the divine that shapes and forms.
    • poṣāya - "for nourishment" or "for sustenance," indicating a request for support and sustenance in life.
    • vi ṣyatu - "may it come forth" or "may it manifest," expressing a desire for the divine to act in the world.
  • rāye nābhā no asmayuḥ
    • rāye - "for wealth" or "for prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance in life.
    • nābhā - "may it not be lacking," suggesting a plea for completeness and fulfillment.
    • no - "to us," reinforcing the personal nature of the request.
    • asmayuḥ - "of our existence" or "of our life," connecting the request to the essence of being.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound request for divine intervention in our lives. It emphasizes the need for protection, nourishment, and prosperity while acknowledging the extraordinary nature of the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or when seeking guidance in personal or professional endeavors. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra during significant life changes, such as starting a new job, moving to a new place, or embarking on a new relationship, to invoke a sense of security and abundance. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human experience, encouraging us to seek support from a higher power in our journey through life.
