आद ऋध्नोति हविष्कृतिम प्राञ्चं कृत्नोति अध्वरम् |होत्रा देवेषु गच्छति ||
ād ṛdhnoti haviṣkṛtim prāñcaṁ kṛṇoty adhvaram | hotrā deveṣu gacchati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: "from the beginning" or "first"
    • ṛdhnoti: "he/she/it increases" or "grows"
    • haviṣkṛtim: "the offering" or "sacrifice"
    • prāñcaṁ: "the one who is wise" or "intelligent"
    • kṛṇoty: "makes" or "creates"
    • adhvaram: "the ritual" or "sacred act"
    • hotrā: "the priest" or "the one who invokes"
    • deveṣu: "to the gods" or "in the divine realm"
    • gacchati: "goes" or "moves"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
    • The phrase "ād ṛdhnoti" suggests that from the very beginning, there is a process of growth and increase through the act of offering.
    • "Haviṣkṛtim" refers to the offerings made during rituals, which are essential for spiritual growth and connection.
    • The term "prāñcaṁ" highlights the role of wisdom in performing these rituals effectively.
    • "Kṛṇoty adhvaram" indicates that the wise individual creates or performs the sacred acts, which are crucial for spiritual elevation.
    • The "hotrā" or priest plays a vital role in invoking the divine presence through these rituals.
    • Finally, "deveṣu gacchati" signifies that the offerings and rituals reach the divine, establishing a connection between the earthly and the spiritual realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of mindfulness and intentionality in our actions. Just as rituals require focus and dedication, so do our daily tasks.
    • For instance, when preparing a meal, one can approach it as a sacred act, putting love and care into the process, which can enhance the experience and the nourishment it provides.
    • In professional settings, the mantra can inspire individuals to approach their work with wisdom and intention, leading to greater productivity and fulfillment.
    • Community rituals, such as weddings or festivals, can also be seen as modern interpretations of this mantra, where offerings and prayers are made to honor the divine and seek blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in fostering a connection with the divine.
    • It teaches us that through intentional actions and wise choices, we can enhance our spiritual growth and well-being.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more meaningful existence, where our daily actions are infused with purpose and reverence.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that our offerings, whether in the form of prayers, actions, or intentions, have the power to reach the divine and transform our lives.
