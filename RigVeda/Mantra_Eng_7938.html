आद इं त्रितस्य योषणो हरिं हिन्वन्त्य अद्रिभिः |इन्दुम इन्द्राय पीतये ||
ād īṁ tritasya yoṣaṇo hariṁ hinvanty adribhiḥ | indum indrāya pītaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: This can be interpreted as "to lead" or "to guide."
    • īṁ: Often associated with "the essence" or "the spirit."
    • tritasya: Refers to "the third" or "the one who is beyond the two."
    • yoṣaṇo: Means "of the woman" or "of the feminine energy."
    • hariṁ: Refers to "the divine" or "the Lord," often associated with Vishnu.
    • hinvanty: Means "to drive away" or "to remove."
    • adribhiḥ: Refers to "the mountains" or "the obstacles."
    • indum: Means "the moon" or "the nectar."
    • indrāya: Refers to "to Indra," the king of gods.
    • pītaye: Means "for drinking" or "to be consumed."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine feminine energy that guides and leads us.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles (represented by mountains) to reach a higher state of consciousness.
    • The reference to Indra and the moon suggests a connection to divine blessings and nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be seen as a call to embrace one's inner strength and femininity, guiding oneself through challenges.
    • In a community setting, it can represent the collective effort to support and uplift each other, driving away negativity and obstacles.
    • In spiritual practices, invoking this mantra can help individuals seek divine guidance and clarity in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of feminine energy and the importance of overcoming challenges to attain spiritual nourishment.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, community support, and spiritual practices.
    • By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals can find strength in unity and divine guidance in their journeys.
