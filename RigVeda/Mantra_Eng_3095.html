आश्रिण्वते अदृपिताय मनमा नृचक्षसे सुमृळीकाय वेधः |देवाय शस्तिम अमृताय शंस ग्रावेव सोता मधुशूद यम् ईळे ||
āśṛṇvate adṛpitāya manma nṛcakṣase sumṛḻīkāya vedhaḥ | devāya śastim amṛtāya śaṁsa grāveva sotā madhuṣud yam īḻe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āśṛṇvate: To those who listen or hear.
    • adṛpitāya: To the one who is not disturbed or agitated.
    • manma: My mind or thoughts.
    • nṛcakṣase: To the one who has the eyes of a human.
    • sumṛḻīkāya: To the one who is well-versed or knowledgeable.
    • vedhaḥ: Knowledge or understanding.
    • devāya: To the divine or godly being.
    • śastim: Prosperity or well-being.
    • amṛtāya: To the immortal or eternal.
    • śaṁsa: Praise or commendation.
    • grāveva: Like a song or chant.
    • sotā: To the one who is a giver or provider.
    • madhuṣud: Sweetness or nectar.
    • yam: Whom.
    • īḻe: I invoke or worship.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding.
    • It calls upon a divine being who is knowledgeable and provides prosperity.
    • The reference to "human eyes" suggests a connection to human experience and perception.
    • The mantra invokes the idea of eternal wisdom and the sweetness of life, akin to nectar.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a teacher embodies the qualities of "sumṛḻīkāya" by sharing knowledge and encouraging students to listen and understand.
    • In a community, leaders who promote well-being and prosperity reflect the essence of "devāya" and "śastim."
    • During meditation or prayer, individuals may invoke this mantra to seek clarity and wisdom in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of listening, understanding, and invoking divine wisdom in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, leadership, and personal growth.
    • By embodying the qualities of knowledge, prosperity, and eternal wisdom, individuals can enhance their lives and the lives of those around them.
