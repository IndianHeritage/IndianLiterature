अभि प्रियाणि पवते पुनानो देवो देवान स्वेन रसेन पृञ्चन |इन्दुर धर्माण्य ऋतुथा वसानो दश क्षिपो अव्यत सानो अव्ये ||
abhi priyāṇi pavate punāno devo devān svena rasena pṛñcan | indur dharmāṇy ṛtuthā vasāno daśa kṣipo avyata sāno avye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in favor of
    • priyāṇi: beloved, dear
    • pavate: blows, moves (referring to the wind)
    • punāno: purifying, refreshing
    • devo: god, divine being
    • devān: gods, deities
    • svena: by its own, inherent
    • rasena: essence, flavor, taste
    • pṛñcan: to create, to manifest
    • indur: moon, light
    • dharmāṇy: righteous, moral
    • ṛtuthā: in accordance with truth, order
    • vasāno: dwelling, residing
    • daśa: ten
    • kṣipo: swift, quick
    • avyata: unrestrained, free
    • sāno: to be, to exist
    • avye: in the pure, untainted state
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that purify and refresh the environment.
    • The wind, symbolizing change and movement, is described as blowing towards what is beloved and dear.
    • The gods are invoked to manifest their essence, bringing forth righteousness and truth.
    • The mention of the moon signifies illumination and guidance, suggesting that moral and ethical living is essential.
    • The phrase "ten swift" indicates the rapidity with which divine forces can act in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the wind can be seen as a purifying force, clearing away pollution and bringing fresh air, much like how we seek to clear negativity from our lives.
    • In personal relationships, we often strive to create an environment that is loving and supportive, akin to the "beloved" that the mantra refers to.
    • In ethical decision-making, the guidance of moral principles can be likened to the light of the moon, helping us navigate through life's challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the purifying forces in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek righteousness and truth in our actions, fostering a harmonious environment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper connection with the divine.
