स्वस्तये वाजिभिः च प्रणेताḥ सं यन् महीर इषा आसत्सि पूर्वीḥ | रायो वन्तारो बृहतः स्यामास्मे अस्तु भग इन्द्र प्रजावान् ||
svastaye vājibhiś ca praṇetaḥ saṁ yan mahīr iṣa āsatsi pūrvīḥ | rāyo vantāro bṛhataḥ syāmāsme astu bhaga indra prajāvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Svastaye: For well-being or prosperity.
    • Vājibhiḥ: With strength or vigor.
    • Ca: And.
    • Praṇetaḥ: The leader or guide.
    • Saṁ: Together or in unity.
    • Yan: That which.
    • Mahīr: Of the earth or the great.
    • Iṣa: The ruler or lord.
    • Āsatsi: May we be seated or established.
    • Pūrvīḥ: In the past or ancient.
    • Rāyo: Of wealth or prosperity.
    • Vantāro: Those who possess or bring forth.
    • Bṛhataḥ: Of greatness or vastness.
    • Syāmāsme: May we become or may we be.
    • Asthu: Let there be or may there be.
    • Bhaga: Blessing or fortune.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing power and strength.
    • Prajāvān: The one who is wise or knowledgeable.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of well-being and prosperity in life.
    • It calls upon a leader or guide to unite people for a common purpose, reflecting the need for collaboration and teamwork.
    • The reference to the earth and its ruler signifies the connection between humanity and nature, urging respect for the environment.
    • It highlights the significance of ancient wisdom and the blessings of the divine, particularly invoking Indra, who symbolizes strength and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration among employees, ensuring that everyone works towards a common goal for the prosperity of the organization.
    • In community initiatives, leaders can use this mantra to inspire unity and collective action, emphasizing the importance of working together for the betterment of society.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on their connection to nature and the importance of sustainable living, aligning their actions with the well-being of the planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of well-being, leadership, and the environment.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to seek prosperity through unity and respect for ancient wisdom.
    • In today's world, where collaboration and sustainability are crucial, this mantra is highly applicable, guiding us towards a harmonious and prosperous existence.
