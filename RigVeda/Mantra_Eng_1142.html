प्रति यत् स्य नीतादर्शि दस्योर ओको नाच्छा सदनं जानती गात् |अध स्मा नो मगवञ्च चर्कृताद इन मां नो मगहेव निष्शपी परा दाह् ||
prati yat syā nīthādarśi dasyor oko nācchā sadanaṁ jānatī gāt | adha smā no maghavañ carkṛtād in mā no magheva niṣṣapī parā dāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati yat syā nīthādarśi dasyor oko nācchā sadanaṁ jānatī gāt | adha smā no maghavañ carkṛtād in mā no magheva niṣṣapī parā dāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: each, every
    • yat: that which
    • syā: may be
    • nīthādarśi: one who shows the path
    • dasyor: of the two
    • oko: of the abode
    • nācchā: not falling
    • sadanaṁ: dwelling, home
    • jānatī: knows
    • gāt: song, hymn
    • adha: below, down
    • smā: may we
    • no: our
    • maghavañ: O mighty one
    • carkṛtād: from the actions
    • in: may
    • mā: not
    • magheva: like the mighty
    • niṣṣapī: to be freed
    • parā: supreme
    • dāḥ: gifts, boons
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the guidance and knowledge that leads to a stable and secure home.
    • The phrase "nīthādarśi" refers to a guide or a leader who shows the right path, which can be likened to a mentor in real life.
    • The mention of "dasyor oko" suggests a duality, perhaps indicating the balance between two aspects of life, such as material and spiritual.
    • "Nācchā sadanaṁ jānatī gāt" emphasizes the importance of knowing one's home or foundation, which can be interpreted as understanding one's roots and values.
    • The plea to "maghavañ" indicates a request for strength and support from a higher power, akin to seeking help from a community or family during challenging times.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a mentor can guide an individual through career challenges, much like the "nīthādarśi" in the mantra.
    • Balancing work and personal life reflects the "dasyor" aspect, where one must navigate between responsibilities and personal well-being.
    • Understanding one's cultural heritage and values can provide a strong foundation, similar to knowing one's "sadanaṁ."
    • In times of crisis, reaching out for support from friends or family mirrors the invocation of "maghavañ" for strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of guidance, stability, and support in life.
    • It teaches us the importance of knowing our roots and seeking help when needed, which is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys and that seeking guidance and support is a strength, not a weakness.
