नराशंसो नोऽवतु प्रयाजे शं नो अस्त्व अनुयाजो हवेषु |क्षिपद अशस्तिम अप दुरमतीं हन्न अथा करद यजमानाय शं योः ||
narāśaṁso no 'vatu prayāje śaṁ no astv anuyājo haveṣu | kṣipad aśastim apa durmatiṁ hann athā karad yajamānāya śaṁ yoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "narāśaṁso no 'vatu prayāje śaṁ no astv anuyājo haveṣu | kṣipad aśastim apa durmatiṁ hann athā karad yajamānāya śaṁ yoḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the divine beings protect us; may they grant us peace and prosperity. May they remove our obstacles and negative thoughts, and may our offerings be accepted."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • narāśaṁso: Refers to divine beings or deities, often associated with protection and guidance.
    • no: Means "us," indicating a collective appeal for support.
    • 'vatu: Translates to "protect," emphasizing the need for safeguarding.
    • prayāje: Means "to grant" or "to bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
    • śaṁ: Refers to "peace" or "well-being," highlighting the desire for harmony.
    • no astv: "May there be for us," reinforcing the collective wish for good fortune.
    • anuyājo: Means "following" or "in accordance with," suggesting alignment with divine will.
    • haveṣu: Refers to "offerings," indicating the importance of rituals and devotion.
    • kṣipad: Means "to remove," indicating the desire to eliminate obstacles.
    • aśastim: Refers to "negative thoughts" or "ill will," emphasizing the need for mental clarity.
    • apa: Means "away from," indicating a distancing from negativity.
    • durmatiṁ: Refers to "bad thoughts" or "misguided intentions," highlighting the importance of positive thinking.
    • hann: Means "to destroy," indicating a strong desire to eradicate negativity.
    • athā: Means "and then," suggesting a continuation of the request.
    • karad: Refers to "action" or "effort," emphasizing the need for proactive measures.
    • yajamānāya: Refers to "to the one who offers," indicating the importance of the devotee's role.
    • śaṁ yoḥ: "May there be peace," reiterating the central theme of harmony and well-being.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often chant this mantra to invoke blessings for collective peace and prosperity, fostering a sense of unity.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to seek mental clarity and protection from negative influences, helping them to focus on positive outcomes.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, this mantra is used to ensure that offerings are made with pure intentions, aligning the devotee's actions with divine will.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for divine protection, mental clarity, and the importance of positive intentions. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community gatherings, serving as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine. By invoking this mantra, one seeks to create a harmonious environment, free from negativity, and aligned with the greater good.
