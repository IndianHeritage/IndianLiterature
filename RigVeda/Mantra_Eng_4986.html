मन्त्र: इन्द्राग्नī उक्तवाहसā स्तोमेभिर हवनश्रुतā | विश्वाभिर गीरभिर आगतम अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
indrāgnī ukthavāhasā stomebhir havanaśrutā | viśvābhir gīrbhir ā gatam asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the deities Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and rain, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Ukthavāhasā: This word means "those who carry the hymns" or "the ones who are invoked." It signifies the importance of reciting hymns and prayers to connect with the divine.
  • Stomebhir: This refers to "with praises" or "through hymns." It emphasizes the role of vocal expression in worship and the significance of chanting in spiritual practices.
  • Havanaśrutā: This term means "those who are heard in the offerings." It indicates the act of making offerings to the gods, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals.
  • Viśvābhir: This means "with all" or "universal." It suggests inclusivity and the idea that the divine is accessible to everyone.
  • Gīrbhir: This translates to "with songs" or "with words." It highlights the power of sound and language in spiritual practices.
  • Ā gatam: This means "having come" or "arrived." It signifies the presence of the divine or the fulfillment of a prayer.
  • Asya: This translates to "of this" or "of this offering." It refers to the specific offerings made to the deities.
  • Somasya: This term refers to "of Soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Pītaye: This means "for drinking" or "to be consumed." It indicates the act of receiving blessings or nourishment from the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic worship, emphasizing the importance of hymns, offerings, and the connection between the divine and humanity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various spiritual practices, such as meditation, rituals, and community gatherings where collective prayers and offerings are made. The significance lies in fostering a sense of unity, reverence, and gratitude towards the divine forces that govern the universe. By invoking Indra and Agni, practitioners seek strength, transformation, and blessings, which can be reflected in personal growth, community well-being, and environmental harmony.
