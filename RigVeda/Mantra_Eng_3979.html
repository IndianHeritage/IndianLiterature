तवोतिभिः सचमानā अरिष्टा बृहस्पते मगवानः सुवीराः |ये अश्वदā उता वा सन्ति गोदā ये वस्त्रदāः सुभगास तेषु रायः ||
tavotibhiḥ sacamānā ariṣṭā bṛhaspate maghavānaḥ suvīrāḥ | ye aśvadā uta vā santi godā ye vastradāḥ subhagās teṣu rāyaḥ ||
  • tavotibhiḥ: This term can be understood as "by your powers" or "through your strength." It signifies the divine energy or influence that one possesses.
  • sacamānā: This word translates to "those who are in harmony" or "who are united." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective strength.
  • ariṣṭā: Meaning "without obstacles" or "free from hindrances," this term highlights the idea of overcoming challenges and achieving success.
  • bṛhaspate: Referring to the deity Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence, this term invokes the qualities of intelligence and guidance.
  • maghavānaḥ: This translates to "the powerful one" or "the mighty." It signifies strength and capability, often associated with prosperity and abundance.
  • suvīrāḥ: Meaning "the good heroes" or "the valiant," this term celebrates those who exhibit courage and valor in their actions.
  • ye aśvadā: This phrase means "those who possess horses," symbolizing wealth, power, and status in ancient times.
  • uta vā: This translates to "or those who," indicating an alternative group or category.
  • santi godā: Meaning "those who have cows," this term represents wealth and sustenance, as cows were considered valuable in Vedic society.
  • ye vastradāḥ: This translates to "those who give clothing," symbolizing generosity and the act of providing for others.
  • subhagās: Meaning "those who are fortunate" or "blessed," this term refers to individuals who are favored by the divine.
  • teṣu rāyaḥ: This phrase means "in them is wealth" or "in those individuals is prosperity," indicating that such qualities lead to abundance and success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine strength, unity, and the qualities of wisdom, courage, and generosity. It highlights that true prosperity comes from collective harmony and the virtues of individuals who contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork in organizations, promote community service, and encourage individuals to embody these qualities for personal and collective growth. By recognizing the importance of unity and the virtues that lead to success, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
