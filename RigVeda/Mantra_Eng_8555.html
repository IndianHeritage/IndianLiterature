प्रास्य धाराऽबृहतीर असृग्रन्न अक्तो गोभिः कलशाम् ā विवेश |साम कृत्वान सामन्यो विपश्चित क्रन्दन्न एत्य अभि सख्युर न जामिम् ||
prāsya dhārā bṛhatīr asṛgrann akto gobhiḥ kalaśām̐ ā viveśa | sāma kṛṇvan sāmanyo vipaścit krandann ety abhi sakhyur na jāmim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāsya: of the offering or sacrifice
    • dhārā: flow or stream
    • bṛhatīr: great or vast
    • asṛgrann: having been poured out
    • akto: unbroken or undisturbed
    • gobhiḥ: with the cows or through the cattle
    • kalaśām̐: the pot or vessel
    • ā viveśa: entered or came into
    • sāma: song or melody
    • kṛṇvan: creating or making
    • sāmanyo: common or universal
    • vipaścit: wise or knowledgeable
    • krandann: crying out or calling
    • ety: coming or approaching
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • sakhyur: friends or companions
    • na: not
    • jāmim: I shall be born or I shall come into being
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a great flow of offerings that are made in a sacred context, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
    • The mention of cows signifies nourishment and wealth, as cows were considered sacred and a source of sustenance in ancient Indian culture.
    • The act of entering the pot (kalaśa) represents the gathering of resources or blessings, which can be seen as a metaphor for collecting wisdom and experiences in life.
    • The creation of a universal song (sāma) indicates the importance of harmony and unity among people, suggesting that wisdom is shared and celebrated collectively.
    • The call to friends (sakhyur) emphasizes the significance of community and companionship in the pursuit of knowledge and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people come together to share their resources, knowledge, and experiences, much like the offerings made in the mantra.
    • In modern society, the importance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals reflects the essence of creating a universal song.
    • In personal development, the idea of entering a pot of wisdom can be likened to the pursuit of education and learning from various life experiences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of shared knowledge and resources.
    • It teaches us that true prosperity comes from collaboration, harmony, and the collective pursuit of wisdom.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by fostering relationships, sharing our knowledge, and working together towards common goals, thereby enriching our lives and the lives of those around us.
