वैश्वानरं कवयो यज्ञियासो 'ग्निं देवाऽजनयनन अजुर्म् |नक्षत्रं प्रात्नम् अमिनच चरिष्णु यक्षस्याध्यक्षं तविषं बृहन्तम् ||
vaiśvānaraṁ kavayo yajñiyāso 'gniṁ devā ajanayann ajuryam | nakṣatram pratnam aminac cariṣṇu yakṣasyādhyakṣaṁ taviṣam bṛhantam ||
  • vaiśvānaraṁ: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, representing the divine energy that sustains life and the universe.
  • kavayo: This word means "the wise" or "the seers," indicating those who possess knowledge and insight into the workings of the universe.
  • yajñiyāso: This refers to the sacrificial or ritualistic aspect, emphasizing the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • 'gniṁ: This is the word for "fire," which symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in various cultures.
  • devā: This means "gods," representing the divine beings that govern various aspects of existence.
  • ajanayann: This term means "gave birth to" or "created," indicating the act of bringing forth life or existence.
  • ajuryam: This word signifies "immortal" or "eternal," highlighting the everlasting nature of the divine and the universe.
  • nakṣatram: This refers to "constellation" or "star," symbolizing guidance and the cosmic order.
  • pratnam: This means "the first" or "the foremost," indicating the primary importance of certain elements in the universe.
  • aminac: This term suggests "to move" or "to travel," representing the dynamic nature of existence.
  • cariṣṇu: This word means "the moving" or "the active," emphasizing the constant change and flow of life.
  • yakṣasyādhyakṣaṁ: This refers to "the overseer of the yakṣas," indicating a guardian or protector role in the cosmic order.
  • taviṣam: This means "powerful" or "strong," highlighting the strength and potency of the divine forces.
  • bṛhantam: This term signifies "the great" or "the vast," representing the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the role of fire as a transformative force, and the interconnectedness of the cosmos. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in acknowledging the divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during religious ceremonies, personal reflections on the nature of existence, or even in environmental discussions where the balance of nature and the universe is considered. By recognizing the divine in everyday life, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the world around them, promoting harmony and understanding.
