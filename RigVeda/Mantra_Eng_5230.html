मन्द्रं होतारं उशिजो यविष्टम् अग्निं विशा ईळते अध्वरेषु |स हि क्षपावां अभवद रयीणाम अतन्द्रो दूतो यजथाय देवान् ||
mandraṁ hotāram uśijo yaviṣṭham agniṁ viśa īḻate adhvareṣu | sa hi kṣapāvām̐ abhavad rayīṇām atandro dūto yajathāya devān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandraṁ: a hymn or mantra
    • hotāram: the one who offers (the priest)
    • uśijo: the one who is born of the light
    • yaviṣṭham: the most excellent or the best
    • agniṁ: fire
    • viśa: to be invoked or called upon
    • īḻate: to shine or to illuminate
    • adhvareṣu: in the rituals or sacrifices
    • sa: he
    • hi: indeed
    • kṣapāvām̐: the one who brings prosperity
    • abhavad: has become
    • rayīṇām: of wealth or riches
    • atandro: without fatigue or effort
    • dūto: a messenger
    • yajathāya: for the purpose of sacrifice
    • devān: the gods
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of Agni, the fire god, who is invoked during rituals.
    • The term "hotāram" refers to the priest who performs the offerings, emphasizing the importance of the ritualistic role in connecting the material and spiritual worlds.
    • Agni is described as "uśijo," indicating his divine origin and purity, which is essential for the acceptance of offerings.
    • The phrase "yaviṣṭham agniṁ" highlights the excellence of Agni, suggesting that he is the most revered among the deities invoked in rituals.
    • Agni is not just a physical fire but symbolizes transformation, purification, and the energy that sustains life.
    • The mantra also indicates that Agni is a "dūto," a messenger who carries the offerings to the gods, thus playing a crucial role in the communication between humans and the divine.
    • The reference to "rayīṇām" signifies the wealth and prosperity that can be attained through proper rituals and offerings, suggesting a direct correlation between spiritual practices and material well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, people often perform rituals during significant life events such as weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or festivals, invoking Agni for blessings and prosperity.
    • Corporate rituals or ceremonies may also invoke similar principles, where leaders seek divine blessings for success and abundance in their ventures.
    • Individuals may light candles or perform fire rituals at home to symbolize the purification of their space and invite positive energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the spiritual realm.
    • It highlights the importance of rituals in achieving prosperity and well-being, suggesting that spiritual practices can lead to tangible benefits in life.
    • In essence, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual worlds, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that honor this relationship.
