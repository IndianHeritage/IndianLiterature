सोमो धेनुं सोमो अर्वन्तम् आशुं सोमो वीरं कर्मण्यं ददाति |सादन्यं विदथ्यं सभेयं पितृश्रवणं यो ददाशद अस्मै ||
somo dhenuṁ somo arvantam āśuṁ somo vīraṁ karmaṇyaṁ dadāti | sādanyaṁ vidathyaṁ sabheyam pitṛśravaṇaṁ yo dadāśad asmai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somo: Refers to the divine nectar or soma, often associated with immortality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • dhenuṁ: Means 'cow', symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • arvantam: Refers to 'the one who is strong or powerful'.
    • āśuṁ: Means 'swift' or 'quick', indicating speed and efficiency.
    • vīraṁ: Translates to 'hero' or 'brave one', representing valor and courage.
    • karmaṇyaṁ: Refers to 'in actions' or 'in deeds', emphasizing the importance of action.
    • dadāti: Means 'gives', indicating the act of bestowing or providing.
    • sādanyaṁ: Refers to 'the one who is worthy' or 'deserving'.
    • vidathyaṁ: Means 'the one who is well-prepared' or 'organized'.
    • sabheyam: Refers to 'the one who is respected' or 'honored'.
    • pitṛśravaṇaṁ: Means 'the one who listens to the ancestors' or 'heeding the wisdom of forefathers'.
    • yo: Means 'who', referring to the subject of the mantra.
    • dadāśad: Means 'has given' or 'has bestowed'.
    • asmai: Refers to 'to him' or 'to that person'.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of soma, symbolizing the divine qualities of nourishment, strength, and valor.
    • The reference to 'cow' signifies the importance of sustenance and the nurturing aspect of life.
    • It emphasizes the need for swift action and the courage to undertake tasks, highlighting the significance of being proactive in life.
    • The mantra also speaks to the importance of being worthy and well-prepared, suggesting that respect and honor come to those who are diligent and organized.
    • Listening to the wisdom of ancestors is a crucial aspect, indicating that one should value the teachings and experiences of those who came before.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of strength, quick decision-making, and respect for their team's input can inspire and motivate others.
    • In personal life, nurturing relationships with family and friends, akin to the nurturing aspect of the cow, can lead to a fulfilling and supportive environment.
    • Students preparing for exams can relate to the idea of being well-prepared and deserving of success through hard work and dedication.
    • Listening to the advice of elders in the family can provide valuable insights and guidance, helping individuals navigate challenges in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, nourishment, and respect for wisdom, making it applicable in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to embody these qualities in our actions, fostering a life of purpose and fulfillment.
    • By integrating the teachings of this mantra into daily life, individuals can cultivate a sense of honor, courage, and preparedness, leading to personal and communal growth.
