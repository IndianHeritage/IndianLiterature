मां नो अस्मिन मघवन पृत्स्व अंहसी नहि ते अन्तः शवसः परीनशे |अक्रन्दयो नद्यः रोरुवद वनाः कथाऽ न क्षोणीर्भियसां सम आरत ||
mā no asmin maghavan pṛtsv aṁhasi nahi te antaḥ śavasaḥ parīṇaśe | akrandayo nadyo3 roruvad vanā kathā na kṣoṇīr bhiyasā sam ārata ||
  • mā no asmin maghavan: This phrase can be translated as "May the bountiful one (Maghavan) not harm us." Here, Maghavan refers to the deity of wealth and abundance, often associated with Indra. In real life, this can be interpreted as a plea for protection from misfortunes that may hinder prosperity.
  • pṛtsv aṁhasi: This means "in the midst of the earth." It signifies our existence and the challenges we face in the material world. For example, in a business context, this could relate to navigating the complexities of the market.
  • nahi te antaḥ śavasaḥ parīṇaśe: This translates to "there is no end to your inner strength." It emphasizes the idea that true power and resilience come from within. In personal development, this can be seen in individuals who overcome adversity through inner strength and determination.
  • akrandayo nadyo3: This phrase means "the rivers that cry out." It symbolizes the natural world and its emotional responses to human actions. For instance, environmental degradation can lead to the suffering of ecosystems, akin to rivers crying out for help.
  • roruvad vanā: This translates to "the forests that lament." It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and the impact of human activities on the environment. In real life, deforestation leads to loss of biodiversity and ecological balance, causing a 'lament' in nature.
  • kathā na kṣoṇīr bhiyasā sam ārata: This means "the stories of the earth are not to be taken lightly." It suggests that the narratives of our planet, including its history and struggles, are significant. In a contemporary context, this can relate to the importance of understanding climate change and its implications for future generations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of humanity and nature. It serves as a reminder to seek protection from misfortunes, acknowledge our inner strength, and respect the stories of the earth. In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, personal resilience in the face of challenges, and the pursuit of prosperity while being mindful of our impact on the world around us. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious existence with nature and each other.
