अनु कृष्णे वसुधिती जिहाते उभे सूर्यस्य मंहनā यजत्रे |परि यत ते महिमानं वृजध्यै सखाया इन्द्र काम्या ऋजिप्याः ||
anu kṛṣṇe vasudhitī jihāte ubhe sūryasya maṁhanā yajatre | pari yat te mahimānaṁ vṛjadhyai sakhāya indra kāmyā ṛjipyāḥ ||
  • anu kṛṣṇe: This phrase can be translated as "following Krishna" or "in accordance with Krishna." Krishna, in Hindu philosophy, represents the divine consciousness and the ultimate truth. In real life, this can be seen as aligning oneself with higher principles or moral values, much like how individuals strive to live by ethical standards in their personal and professional lives.
  • vasudhitī jihāte: This means "the earth is being nourished." It signifies the importance of taking care of our environment and resources. For example, sustainable farming practices and conservation efforts reflect this idea, as they aim to nourish the earth for future generations.
  • ubhe sūryasya maṁhanā yajatre: This translates to "both the sun's radiance and the offerings made." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In practical terms, this can relate to how we harness solar energy today, reflecting our respect for natural resources and the importance of renewable energy in combating climate change.
  • pari yat te mahimānaṁ: This means "as your greatness is." It acknowledges the vastness and power of the divine. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the potential within ourselves and others, encouraging personal growth and the pursuit of excellence.
  • vṛjadhyai sakhāya: This phrase can be understood as "to those who seek friendship." It emphasizes the value of relationships and community. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of networking and building supportive relationships in both personal and professional contexts.
  • indra kāmyā ṛjipyāḥ: This translates to "may we be blessed by Indra's desires." Indra is often associated with strength and power. This can be related to the aspiration for success and the pursuit of one's goals, reflecting the human desire for achievement and recognition.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, emphasizing the importance of aligning with higher principles, nurturing the earth, harnessing natural resources, recognizing personal potential, valuing relationships, and aspiring for success. Its significance lies in its applicability to modern life, where individuals are encouraged to live ethically, care for the environment, pursue personal growth, and build meaningful connections. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
