इदं यमस्य साधनं देवमानं यद उच्यते |इयम् अस्य धाम्यते नालीर अयं गीरभिः परिष्कृतः ||
idaṁ yamasya sādanaṁ devamānaṁ yad ucyate | iyam asya dhamyate nāḻīr ayaṁ gīrbhiḥ pariṣkṛtaḥ ||
  • idaṁ yamasya sādanaṁ: This phrase translates to "this is the abode of Yama." Yama is the god of death and the underworld in Hindu mythology. The term "sādanaṁ" refers to a place or abode, indicating a sacred space associated with Yama.
  • devamānaṁ yad ucyate: This means "the divine being that is spoken of." It suggests that Yama is not just a deity of death but also a divine figure who has a significant role in the cosmic order.
  • iyam asya dhamyate nāḻīr: This translates to "this is the path that is followed." The word "dhamyate" implies a journey or a way of life, while "nāḻīr" can refer to the channels or pathways, possibly indicating the spiritual paths that lead to Yama.
  • ayaṁ gīrbhiḥ pariṣkṛtaḥ: This means "this is adorned with hymns." The term "gīrbhiḥ" refers to hymns or chants, suggesting that the abode of Yama is revered and celebrated through sacred verses.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of Yama as a divine figure who governs the cycle of life and death. It highlights the importance of recognizing death as a natural part of existence and the need to honor it through spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to live a life of purpose and mindfulness, understanding that our actions have consequences beyond this life. It encourages individuals to reflect on their mortality and the spiritual paths they choose, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the universe.
