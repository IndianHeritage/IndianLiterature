यद ईम ऋतस्य पयसा पियानो नयन्न ऋतस्य पथिभी रजिष्ठैः |आर्यमा मित्रो वरुणः परिज्मा त्वचं पृñचन्त्य उपरस्य योनौ ||
yad īm ṛtasya payasā piyāno nayann ṛtasya pathibhī rajiṣṭhaiḥ | aryamā mitro varuṇaḥ parijmā tvacam pṛñcanty uparasya yonau ||
  • yad īm ṛtasya payasā piyāno: This phrase can be translated as "that which is nourished by the essence of truth." Here, "ṛta" refers to the cosmic order or truth, and "payasā" signifies nourishment. In real life, this can be related to the idea that truth and righteousness provide sustenance to our lives, much like food nourishes our bodies.
  • nayann ṛtasya pathibhī rajiṣṭhaiḥ: This translates to "leading along the paths of truth." It emphasizes the importance of following the righteous path in life. For example, in a professional setting, adhering to ethical practices can lead to long-term success and respect.
  • aryamā mitro varuṇaḥ: This part refers to the deities Aryaman, Mitra, and Varuna, who symbolize friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. In our daily lives, fostering friendships and maintaining harmony with others can lead to a more fulfilling and peaceful existence.
  • parijmā tvacam pṛñcanty uparasya yonau: This can be interpreted as "they who are enveloped in the skin of the cosmic being." It suggests a connection to the universe and the importance of understanding our place within it. In practical terms, this can relate to environmental consciousness and the need to protect our planet, recognizing that we are all part of a larger whole.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, righteousness, and harmony in our lives. It teaches us that by nourishing ourselves with truth and following a righteous path, we can foster meaningful relationships and contribute positively to the universe. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal relationships, professional ethics, and environmental stewardship, guiding us towards a more harmonious existence.
