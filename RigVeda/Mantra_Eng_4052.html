स जिह्वया चतुरनीका ऋञ्जते चारु वसानो वरुणो यतन्न अरिम् |न तस्य विद्म पुरुषत्वता वयं यतो भागः सविता दाति वार्यम् ||
sa jihvayā caturanīka ṛñjate cāru vasāno varuṇo yatann arim | na tasya vidma puruṣatvatā vayaṁ yato bhagaḥ savitā dāti vāryam ||
  • sa jihvayā: This phrase can be translated as "with the tongue." It signifies the power of speech and communication. In real life, effective communication is essential in various aspects, such as personal relationships, professional settings, and public speaking.
  • caturanīka: This term means "the one who is fourfold" or "having four aspects." It can refer to the multifaceted nature of existence or the different dimensions of life, such as physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual. For example, a balanced life requires attention to all these aspects to achieve overall well-being.
  • ṛñjate: This word translates to "shines" or "radiates." It implies the idea of brilliance or excellence. In a real-life context, this can be seen in individuals who excel in their fields, inspiring others through their achievements and character.
  • cāru vasāno: This phrase means "the one who is beautifully adorned." It can symbolize the importance of inner beauty and virtues over mere external appearances. For instance, a person who is kind and compassionate is often seen as more beautiful than someone who is merely physically attractive.
  • varuṇo: Referring to Varuna, the deity associated with water and cosmic order, this term emphasizes the significance of natural elements and their role in maintaining balance in life. In real life, this can relate to the importance of environmental conservation and respecting nature.
  • yatann arim: This translates to "who conquers the enemy." It signifies overcoming obstacles and challenges. In everyday life, this can be applied to personal struggles, such as overcoming fears, doubts, or adversities in pursuit of one's goals.
  • na tasya vidma puruṣatvatā: This means "we do not know his true nature." It reflects the idea that the divine or ultimate reality is beyond human comprehension. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, acknowledging that there are limits to what we can know.
  • vayaṁ yato bhagaḥ: This phrase translates to "we are where the blessings are." It emphasizes the importance of being in alignment with positive energies and blessings in life. For example, surrounding oneself with supportive and uplifting people can lead to personal growth and happiness.
  • savitā dāti vāryam: This means "the sun gives strength." It symbolizes the life-giving energy of the sun and its role in sustaining life. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of sunlight for health, agriculture, and overall well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound truths about communication, the multifaceted nature of existence, the importance of inner beauty, the role of nature, overcoming challenges, the limits of human understanding, the significance of blessings, and the life-giving energy of the sun. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a balanced and harmonious life, encouraging them to cultivate virtues, respect nature, and seek knowledge while recognizing the divine's mysterious nature. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can inspire personal growth, resilience, and a deeper connection with the world around us.
