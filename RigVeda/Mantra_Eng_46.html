त्वं सुतस्य पीतये सद्यो वृद्धो अजायथाḥ |इन्द्र ज्यैष्ठ्याय सुक्रतो ||
tvaṁ sutasya pītaye sadyo vṛddho ajāyathāḥ | indra jyaiṣṭhyāya sukrato ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you" or "your," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, in this case, Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature.
  • sutasya - This means "of the son" or "of the offspring," suggesting a connection to progeny or lineage.
  • pītaye - This translates to "for the sake of nourishment" or "for the sake of sustenance," indicating a purpose related to providing support or sustenance.
  • sadyo - This means "immediately" or "at once," emphasizing the urgency or immediacy of the action or request being made.
  • vṛddho - This translates to "grown" or "mature," referring to someone who has attained wisdom or age, often associated with strength and authority.
  • ajāyathāḥ - This means "you were born" or "you came into being," indicating the origin or birth of the divine being being addressed.
  • indra - Referring to the deity Indra, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength, symbolizing power and authority.
  • jyaiṣṭhyāya - This translates to "for the superiority" or "for the greatness," indicating a pursuit of excellence or higher status.
  • sukrato - This means "of good deeds" or "of virtuous actions," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and moral integrity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for nourishment and support, particularly in the context of lineage and the virtues of good deeds. It highlights the importance of divine assistance in achieving greatness and the role of moral actions in attaining success.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Family and Lineage: The emphasis on "sutasya" suggests the importance of nurturing and supporting one's family and descendants. In modern life, this can translate to the responsibility of parents to provide for their children and ensure their well-being.
  • Leadership and Authority: The reference to "vṛddho" and "jyaiṣṭhyāya" can be seen in the context of leadership roles, where experienced leaders are expected to guide and support their teams with wisdom and integrity.
  • Ethical Living: The mention of "sukrato" underscores the significance of living a life of virtue. In today's world, this can be reflected in the importance of ethical business practices and social responsibility.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine support, family responsibilities, ethical living, and the pursuit of greatness. It encourages individuals to seek divine blessings while also emphasizing the importance of good deeds and moral integrity in their lives.
