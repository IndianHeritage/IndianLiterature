त्वां यज्ञैर अवीवृधन पवमान विधर्मणि |अथा नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
tvāṁ yajñair avīvṛdhan pavamāna vidharmaṇi | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and refers to the divine or the higher power that is being addressed in the mantra.
  • yajñair - This means "through sacrifices" or "by means of offerings." In the Vedic context, yajña represents a ritual of offerings to the deities, which is believed to maintain cosmic order.
  • avīvṛdhan - This term can be interpreted as "having been revealed" or "having been made manifest." It suggests the idea of the divine being made accessible or known through the rituals.
  • pavamāna - This word means "purifying" or "the purifier." It signifies the aspect of the divine that cleanses and sanctifies the offerings and the worshippers.
  • vidharmaṇi - This translates to "in accordance with righteousness" or "in the path of dharma." It emphasizes the importance of following the right path and moral principles in life.
  • athā - This means "therefore" or "then," indicating a conclusion or a result of the preceding statements.
  • no - This translates to "our," indicating a personal connection and a plea for the divine to consider the worshippers.
  • vasyasas - This term means "to be controlled" or "to be made favorable." It expresses a desire for the divine to grant favor and guidance.
  • kṛdhi - This means "do" or "make," serving as a request for action from the divine.

Conclusion:

The mantra "tvāṁ yajñair avīvṛdhan pavamāna vidharmaṇi | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance through righteous actions and offerings. It emphasizes the importance of rituals (yajñas) in connecting with the divine, purifying oneself, and adhering to dharma (righteousness).


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their actions and ensure they align with moral values, seeking purification and guidance in their life choices.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, such as festivals or ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and foster a sense of unity and purpose among participants.
  • Environmental Awareness: The idea of purification can extend to environmental practices, where individuals and communities engage in actions that promote sustainability and respect for nature.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of actions, morality, and divine guidance, encouraging individuals to live a life of purpose and righteousness.
