तुभ्यं सुतास तुभ्यम् उ सोत्वासस त्वां गिरः श्वात्र्या आ ह्वयन्ति |इन्द्रेदम् अद्य सवनं जुषाणो विश्वस्य विद्वां इह पाहि सोमम् ||
tubhyaṁ sutās tubhyam u sotvāsas tvāṁ giraḥ śvātryā ā hvayanti | indredam adya savanaṁ juṣāṇo viśvasya vidvām̐ iha pāhi somam ||
  • tubhyaṁ sutās tubhyam: This phrase translates to "to you, O Soma, we offer our praises." Here, "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing divine inspiration and the essence of life. In real life, this can be likened to the way we honor and appreciate the sources of inspiration in our lives, such as mentors, nature, or spiritual practices.
  • u sotvāsas tvāṁ giraḥ: This means "and the songs of praise call upon you." It emphasizes the importance of vocalizing our gratitude and reverence. In contemporary life, this can be seen in how people express appreciation through art, music, or even simple acts of kindness, reinforcing the idea that vocal acknowledgment can strengthen our connections with the divine or the universe.
  • śvātryā ā hvayanti: This translates to "the wise invoke you with their hymns." It suggests that wisdom and knowledge are essential in seeking the divine. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and understanding, where individuals seek knowledge to better their lives and the lives of others, invoking a sense of purpose and direction.
  • indredam adya savanaṁ juṣāṇo: This means "today, enjoying the offering to Indra." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. This line signifies the importance of celebrating achievements and the blessings we receive. In modern contexts, this can be seen during festivals or personal milestones where communities come together to celebrate and honor their successes.
  • viśvasya vidvām̐ iha pāhi somam: This translates to "O wise one, protect the Soma here." It is a plea for protection and guidance. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a call for safeguarding our well-being and the well-being of our loved ones, whether through community support, personal health practices, or spiritual guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of reverence, wisdom, celebration, and protection. Its significance lies in the reminder to honor the sources of inspiration in our lives, to express gratitude, to seek knowledge, and to celebrate our achievements while asking for guidance and protection. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various ways, such as through community gatherings, personal reflections, educational pursuits, and spiritual practices, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
