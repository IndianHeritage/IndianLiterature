अग्नेमाकिष्टे देवस्य रातिमादेवो युयोत |त्वमईशिषे वसूनाम् ||
agne mākiṣ ṭe devasya rātim adevo yuyota | tvam īśiṣe vasūnām ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a source of energy.
  • Mākiṣ: This term means "to bring forth" or "to manifest." It signifies the act of invoking or calling upon the divine.
  • Ṭe: This is a form of the pronoun "you," indicating a direct address to Agni.
  • Devasya: This means "of the divine" or "of the god." It emphasizes the sacred nature of Agni.
  • Rātim: This word translates to "delight" or "joy." It signifies the positive emotions and experiences associated with the divine.
  • Adevo: This means "the divine" or "the god." It reinforces the connection to the higher spiritual realm.
  • Yuyota: This term means "to unite" or "to come together." It suggests a gathering of energies or forces.
  • Tvam: This is another form of "you," again addressing Agni directly.
  • Īśiṣe: This means "to the ruler" or "to the lord." It indicates a position of authority and power.
  • Vasūnām: This translates to "of the wealth" or "of the treasures." It refers to the abundance and prosperity that can be derived from the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, asking for the manifestation of divine joy and unity, and recognizing Agni as a ruler of wealth and abundance. It reflects the importance of fire not only as a physical element but also as a spiritual symbol that brings people together and purifies their intentions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can help create a sacred space, promoting unity and joy among participants.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can meditate on this mantra to seek clarity and abundance in their lives, focusing on the positive energies that Agni represents.
  • Community Building: The idea of coming together (yuyota) can be applied in community initiatives, fostering collaboration and shared joy among members.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine energies for joy, unity, and abundance. It serves as a reminder of the power of spirituality in everyday life, encouraging individuals and communities to seek connection and prosperity through the divine. By recognizing the sacredness of fire and its role in our lives, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for the forces that unite us and bring us joy.
