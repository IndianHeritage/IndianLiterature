ते न आस्नो वृकाणाम् आदित्यासो मुमोचत |स्टेनं बद्धं इवादिते ||
te na āsno vṛkāṇām ādityāso mumocata | stenam baddham ivādite ||
  • Mantra: "te na āsno vṛkāṇām ādityāso mumocata | stenam baddham ivādite ||"
  • Translation: "May the sun (Āditya) release us from the bonds of the wicked, like a thief who is bound."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • te: you (plural or collective)
    • na: not
    • āsno: from the bonds or ties
    • vṛkāṇām: of the wicked or evil-doers
    • ādityāso: of the sun (Āditya)
    • mumocata: may release or liberate
    • stenam: thief
    • baddham: bound or tied
    • iv: like
    • ādite: the sun (as a source of light and truth)

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the sun, symbolizing light, truth, and purity, to free individuals from the negative influences of wickedness.
    • The phrase "like a thief who is bound" suggests that just as a thief is caught and restrained, so too can individuals be trapped by the actions of the wicked.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking liberation from negativity and harmful influences in life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, an employee may feel trapped by toxic colleagues or a negative work environment. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek the light of positivity and to distance oneself from harmful influences.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may find themselves in toxic situations. The invocation of the sun in this mantra can inspire them to seek clarity and liberation from such bonds.
    • On a broader scale, communities facing corruption or injustice can draw strength from this mantra, calling upon the forces of good to overcome the wickedness that binds them.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the need for liberation from negativity and the importance of seeking the light of truth and goodness in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to rise above the influences of wickedness and to strive for a life filled with positivity and integrity.
    • In practical terms, it encourages self-reflection and the pursuit of environments and relationships that foster growth and well-being.
