धिषा यदि धिषण्यन्तः सरण्यान सदन्तो अद्रिम औषिजस्य गोहे |आ दुरोषाः पास्त्यस्य होतā यो नो महān संवरणेṣu वह्निः ||
dhiṣā yadi dhiṣaṇyantaḥ saraṇyān sadanto adrim auśijasya gohe | ā duroṣāḥ pāstyasya hotā yo no mahān saṁvaraṇeṣu vahniḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhiṣā: direction, guidance
    • yadi: if
    • dhiṣaṇyantaḥ: those who are guided
    • saraṇyān: those who seek refuge
    • sadanto: with good intentions
    • adrim: the mountain, symbolizing strength
    • auśijasya: of the one who is pure or virtuous
    • gohe: in the abode, home
    • ā: from
    • duroṣāḥ: those who are not harmful
    • pāstyasya: of the protector
    • hotā: the one who offers, the priest
    • yo: who
    • no: for us
    • mahān: great
    • saṁvaraṇeṣu: in the protective realms
    • vahniḥ: fire, symbolizing energy and transformation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about seeking guidance and protection from higher powers.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good intentions and virtuous living.
    • The reference to "mountain" symbolizes strength and stability, suggesting that one should seek refuge in strong and virtuous principles.
    • The "fire" represents energy and transformation, indicating that through guidance and protection, one can achieve personal growth and transformation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a career change, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, reflecting the essence of "dhiṣā yadi dhiṣaṇyantaḥ."
    • People who practice mindfulness and meditation often embody the qualities of "sadanto," focusing on good intentions and virtuous living.
    • In times of crisis, communities come together to support each other, embodying the idea of seeking refuge and protection from harmful influences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It teaches us the value of good intentions and the strength found in virtuous living.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, community support, and a deeper connection to one's values and beliefs.
