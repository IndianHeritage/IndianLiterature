युवं पय उष्रियायाम् अधत्तम् पक्वम् आमायाम् अव पूर्व्यं गोः |अन्तर यद वनिनो वाम ऋतप्सू ह्वारो न शुचिर यजते हविष्मान् ||
yuvam paya usriyāyām adhattam pakvam āmāyām ava pūrvyaṁ goḥ | antar yad vanino vām ṛtapsū hvāro na śucir yajate haviṣmān ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • yuvam: You (plural), referring to a group or community.
    • paya: Nourishment or sustenance, often associated with milk or food.
    • usriyāyām: In the context of the dawn or the early morning, symbolizing new beginnings.
    • adhattam: To place or establish, indicating an action of setting something in motion.
    • pakvam: Ripe or mature, suggesting readiness or completeness.
    • āmāyām: In the context of the earth or the world, indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • ava: Downward or towards, suggesting a direction or focus.
    • pūrvyaṁ: Previous or ancient, referring to traditions or past knowledge.
    • goḥ: Of the cow, which is a symbol of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture.
    • antar: Within or inside, indicating an inner aspect.
    • yad: That which, referring to a specific quality or characteristic.
    • vanino: Of the forest or nature, suggesting a connection to the natural world.
    • vām: Your, indicating possession or belonging.
    • ṛtapsū: The essence of truth or righteousness, often associated with the cosmic order.
    • hvāro: Invoking or calling upon, suggesting a plea or request.
    • na: Not, indicating negation.
    • śucir: Pure or clean, referring to a state of being untainted.
    • yajate: To worship or offer, indicating a ritualistic aspect.
    • haviṣmān: The one who offers or sacrifices, often in a spiritual context.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the collective nourishment and growth of a community, emphasizing the importance of unity and shared resources.
    • The reference to dawn signifies new beginnings, suggesting that every day brings opportunities for renewal and growth.
    • The idea of maturity and readiness (pakvam) indicates that individuals and communities should strive to reach their full potential.
    • The connection to nature (vanino) highlights the importance of living in harmony with the environment, recognizing that our well-being is intertwined with the natural world.
    • The invocation of purity (śucir) and truth (ṛtapsū) suggests that spiritual practices should be grounded in honesty and integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to initiatives that promote sustainable living, where members work together to cultivate resources responsibly.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their own growth and maturity, setting goals that align with their values and the greater good.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra serves as a reminder to respect and protect nature, recognizing its role in our sustenance and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of community, growth, and harmony with nature. It encourages individuals to strive for maturity and purity in their actions while fostering a collective spirit.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless message that resonates in various aspects of life, from personal development to community engagement and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals and communities can create a more sustainable and harmonious existence.
