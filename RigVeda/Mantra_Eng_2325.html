यā सुभौः स्वङ्गुरिः सुशूमā बहुसूवरī | तस्यै विश्वपत्न्यै हविः सिनीवालयै जुहोतना ||
yā subāhuḥ svaṅguriḥ suṣūmā bahusūvarī | tasyai viśpatnyai haviḥ sinīvālyai juhotana ||
  • yā subāhuḥ: This phrase can be translated to "who is of good strength" or "who is powerful." In real life, this can refer to individuals or entities that possess great capabilities or resources, such as leaders, organizations, or even natural forces like rivers or mountains that provide sustenance and strength to communities.
  • svaṅguriḥ: This term means "who is of good form" or "who is well-formed." It can symbolize beauty, harmony, and balance in nature or human endeavors. For example, a well-designed building or a perfectly executed piece of art can embody this quality, reflecting the importance of aesthetics in our lives.
  • suṣūmā: This word translates to "very auspicious" or "very favorable." In practical terms, it can refer to moments or events that bring joy and prosperity, such as weddings, festivals, or the birth of a child, which are often celebrated for their positive impact on individuals and communities.
  • bahusūvarī: This phrase means "who is abundant" or "who has many resources." It can represent the richness of nature, such as fertile land or a bountiful harvest, which sustains life. In a broader sense, it can also refer to individuals who are generous and share their wealth or knowledge with others, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • tasyai viśpatnyai: This translates to "to her, the mistress of all." It signifies reverence towards a divine feminine force or a nurturing figure that governs and protects. In real life, this can be likened to the role of mothers, caregivers, or even nature itself, which nurtures and sustains life.
  • haviḥ: This word means "offering" or "sacrifice." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in various forms, whether it be through charitable acts, community service, or simply offering kindness to others. In many cultures, offerings are made to express gratitude and seek blessings.
  • sinīvālyai: This term can be interpreted as "who is rich in resources" or "who is full of abundance." It emphasizes the idea of prosperity and the importance of utilizing resources wisely for the benefit of all. For instance, sustainable practices in agriculture or business can lead to long-term benefits for communities and the environment.
  • juhotana: This word means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of prayer or invocation, where individuals seek connection with the divine or higher powers. In real life, this can be seen in various spiritual practices, rituals, or even moments of reflection where people seek guidance and support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, beauty, abundance, and reverence towards nurturing forces in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of gratitude, sharing, and invoking blessings. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal relationships, community building, and environmental stewardship, encouraging individuals to recognize and honor the resources and support systems that sustain them. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous world for ourselves and future generations.
