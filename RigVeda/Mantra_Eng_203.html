इहेंद्राग्नि उप हवये तयोः इत् स्तोमं उष्मसि |तां सोमं सोमपातमा ||
ihendrāgnī upa hvaye tayor it stomam uśmasi | tā somaṁ somapātamā ||
  • Mantra: "ihendrāgnī upa hvaye tayor it stomam uśmasi | tā somaṁ somapātamā ||"
  • Translation: "I invoke Indra and Agni; may they grant me their favor and strength. May this hymn reach the essence of Soma, the divine nectar."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ihendrāgnī: Refers to the deities Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • upa hvaye: Means "I invoke" or "I call upon," indicating a plea for divine assistance.
    • tayor: Translates to "of those two," referring to Indra and Agni.
    • it stomam: Means "this hymn" or "this praise," suggesting the act of offering reverence through words.
    • uśmasi: Refers to "warmth" or "energy," indicating a desire for the blessings of vitality and strength.
    • tā somaṁ: Refers to "that Soma," which is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment.
    • somapātamā: Means "the one who drinks Soma," indicating the aspirational state of attaining divine qualities through the consumption of this sacred nectar.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Invocation of Strength: Just as one might call upon a mentor or a leader for guidance in challenging situations, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers in times of need.
    • Transformation through Fire: In a practical sense, the element of fire (Agni) can be seen in various life transformations, such as the process of cooking food, which transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, symbolizing personal growth and development.
    • Seeking Enlightenment: The concept of Soma can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in modern life. Just as ancient seekers aspired to drink Soma for enlightenment, individuals today strive for education and self-improvement to achieve their goals.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and strength in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of invoking higher powers, whether in moments of personal struggle or in the pursuit of knowledge and transformation. In contemporary life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, prayer, or simply reflecting on one's goals and aspirations. By acknowledging the forces that guide us, we can cultivate resilience and strive for enlightenment, much like the ancient seekers of wisdom who revered Indra, Agni, and Soma.
