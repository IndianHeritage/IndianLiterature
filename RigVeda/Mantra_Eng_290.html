वसिष्वा हि मियेद्ध्य वस्त्राण्य ऊर्जाम् पते | सेमं नो अध्वरं यज ||
vasiṣvā hi miyedhya vastrāṇy ūrjām pate | semaṁ no adhvaraṁ yaja ||
  • Mantra: "vasiṣvā hi miyedhya vastrāṇy ūrjām pate | semaṁ no adhvaraṁ yaja ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vasiṣvā: All-encompassing, universal
    • hi: Indeed, certainly
    • miyedhya: May we attain, may we achieve
    • vastrāṇy: Garments, coverings
    • ūrjām: Energy, strength, vitality
    • pate: O Lord, O Master
    • semaṁ: Shelter, protection
    • no: Our
    • adhvaraṁ: Sacrifice, offering
    • yaja: To worship, to perform
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for universal protection and strength.
    • The invocation of "vasiṣvā" suggests a desire for inclusivity and unity, emphasizing the importance of collective well-being.
    • By seeking "vastrāṇy ūrjām," the mantra highlights the need for spiritual and physical sustenance, akin to how clothing provides warmth and protection.
    • The phrase "semaṁ no adhvaraṁ yaja" indicates a call to perform rituals or sacrifices that ensure safety and prosperity, reflecting the importance of community and shared values.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where people come together to celebrate and support one another, fostering a sense of unity.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and protection for themselves and their loved ones.
    • Organizations may use the essence of this mantra in team-building exercises, emphasizing the importance of collective effort and mutual support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking strength and protection through unity.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment that individual well-being is tied to the well-being of the community.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and groups to come together, support one another, and engage in practices that promote harmony and strength.
