उत त्वां अदिते मह्य अहं देव्य उप ब्रुवे |सुमृळीकाम अभिष्टये ||
uta tvām adite mahy ahaṁ devy upa bruve | sumṛḻīkām abhiṣṭaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tvām: you
    • adite: O Aditi (the mother of gods)
    • mahy: for me
    • ahaṁ: I
    • devy: O divine one
    • upa: near
    • bruve: I speak
    • sumṛḻīkām: with good intentions
    • abhiṣṭaye: for the purpose of blessings
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer addressed to Aditi, the divine mother, expressing the speaker's intention to communicate with her for blessings and good fortune. The speaker seeks her presence and guidance, emphasizing the importance of divine support in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, individuals often seek blessings from a higher power before starting a new venture, such as a business or a significant life change, similar to how the speaker seeks Aditi's blessings.
    • In community settings, people may gather for rituals or prayers to invoke divine support for collective well-being, reflecting the communal aspect of seeking blessings.
    • In moments of uncertainty or challenge, individuals may turn to spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, to find guidance and strength, akin to the speaker's appeal to Aditi for support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the divine as a source of guidance and blessings. It highlights the importance of seeking support from higher powers in both personal and communal contexts. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals or communities seek strength, clarity, and positive outcomes, reinforcing the belief in the interconnectedness of the divine and human endeavors.
