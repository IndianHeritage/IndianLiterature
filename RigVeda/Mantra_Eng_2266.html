इमा गिर आदित्येभ्यो घृतस्नूः सनाद राजभ्यो जुह्वा जुहोमि |श्रिणोतु मित्रो आर्यमा भगो नस् तुविजातो वरुणो दक्षो अंशः ||
imā gira ādityebhyo ghṛtasnūḥ sanād rājabhyo juhvā juhomi | śṛṇotu mitro aryamā bhago nas tuvijāto varuṇo dakṣo aṁśaḥ ||
  • imā gira: This phrase translates to "these words" or "these utterances." It signifies the importance of speech and communication in rituals and daily life.
  • ādityebhyo: Referring to the Adityas, the solar deities, this part emphasizes the connection to divine energy and light, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • ghṛtasnūḥ: This term means "one who is rich in ghee" or "nourishing." Ghee is a symbol of purity and is often used in Vedic rituals, representing sustenance and prosperity.
  • sanād: This word means "from the beginning" or "eternally." It indicates the timeless nature of the divine and the continuity of traditions.
  • rājabhyo: This translates to "to the kings" or "to the rulers," highlighting the importance of leadership and governance in society.
  • juhvā juhomi: This phrase means "I offer" or "I invoke." It signifies the act of offering prayers or sacrifices, which is central to Vedic rituals.
  • śṛṇotu: Meaning "may he hear," this word expresses the desire for divine attention and responsiveness to prayers.
  • mitro: Referring to "Mitra," the deity of friendship and contracts, this emphasizes the importance of relationships and trust in human interactions.
  • aryamā: This term refers to a deity associated with the sun and truth, symbolizing righteousness and moral integrity.
  • bhago: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," this word highlights the aspiration for abundance and success in life.
  • nas tuvijāto: This translates to "may he be born for us," indicating a plea for divine blessings and the manifestation of positive outcomes.
  • varuṇo: Referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, this signifies the importance of balance and harmony in the universe.
  • dakṣo: Meaning "skilled" or "capable," this word emphasizes the value of competence and expertise in various endeavors.
  • aṁśaḥ: This term means "part" or "aspect," indicating the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, leadership, and moral integrity. It highlights the significance of communication, relationships, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for success in business, governance, or personal relationships. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can aspire to lead fulfilling lives, foster trust, and contribute positively to society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of aligning oneself with higher principles for overall well-being.
