इन्द्रश्च वायवेषां सुतानां पीतिमर्हतः |तान्जुषेथामरेपसाव अभि प्रयः ||
indraś ca vāyav eṣāṁ sutānām pītim arhathaḥ | tāñ juṣethām arepasāv abhi prayaḥ ||
  • Indraś ca vāyav: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, and Vayu, the god of wind. Indra is often associated with strength, rain, and thunder, while Vayu represents the vital breath and movement. Together, they symbolize the forces of nature that are essential for life.
  • Eṣāṁ sutānām pītim arhathaḥ: This part translates to "may you enjoy the nourishment of these sons." Here, "sutānām" refers to the offspring or creations, and "pītim" signifies nourishment or satisfaction. This suggests a blessing for abundance and well-being for the progeny or creations of the divine.
  • Tāñ juṣethām arepasāv abhi prayaḥ: This line can be interpreted as "may you delight in the offerings and the strength of the divine." "Juṣethām" means to enjoy or delight in, while "arepasāv" refers to the offerings made to the gods. "Abhi prayaḥ" indicates a sense of strength or power that comes from these divine blessings.

  • Real-Life Example: In agricultural societies, the blessings of Indra and Vayu are crucial for a good harvest. Farmers often pray to these deities for rain and favorable winds, which are essential for crop growth. This mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke their blessings for prosperity and abundance.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the divine. It highlights the importance of seeking blessings for nourishment and strength, not just for oneself but for the community and future generations. It serves as a reminder of the gratitude owed to the forces that sustain life.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during family gatherings, community events, or agricultural festivals. It encourages individuals to appreciate the resources they have and to seek harmony with nature. By acknowledging the divine forces at play, people can foster a sense of responsibility towards the environment and their community.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for nourishment and strength. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and the interconnectedness of all life. By invoking these blessings, individuals can cultivate a sense of abundance and well-being, fostering a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine.
