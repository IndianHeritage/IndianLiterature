इनोता पृच्छ जनिमा कवीनाम मनोधृतः सुखृतस तक्षत द्याम् |इमा उ ते प्राण्यो वर्धमानाः मनोवाताः अध नु धर्मणि गमन ||
inota pṛccha janimā kavīnām manodhṛtaḥ sukṛtas takṣata dyām | imā u te praṇyo3 vardhamānā manovātā adha nu dharmaṇi gman ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • inota: to bring forth, to produce
    • pṛccha: to ask, to inquire
    • janima: birth, creation
    • kavīnām: of the wise, the poets, the seers
    • manodhṛtaḥ: held in the mind, mental focus
    • sukṛtas: good deeds, virtuous actions
    • takṣata: to create, to fashion
    • dyām: the sky, the heavens
    • imā: these
    • u: and
    • te: your
    • praṇyo: thoughts, intentions
    • vardhamānā: increasing, growing
    • manovātā: mental winds, thoughts
    • adha: below, in this world
    • nu: indeed, truly
    • dharmaṇi: in righteousness, in duty
    • gman: to be established, to be grounded
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inquiry and understanding in the process of creation and existence.
    • It suggests that wise individuals (kavīnām) should focus their minds (manodhṛtaḥ) on virtuous actions (sukṛtas) to create a better world (dyām).
    • The mantra encourages individuals to cultivate positive thoughts (praṇyo) that grow and flourish (vardhamānā) in their lives.
    • It highlights the significance of grounding these thoughts in righteousness (dharmaṇi) to ensure they manifest positively in the world (adha).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members focus on positive collaboration and virtuous actions, they create a productive environment that fosters creativity and innovation.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing good thoughts and intentions can lead to stronger bonds and mutual respect, enhancing the quality of interactions.
    • In community service, individuals who engage in selfless acts and focus on the well-being of others contribute to a harmonious society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of thought and intention in shaping our reality.
    • By focusing on virtuous actions and grounding our thoughts in righteousness, we can create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of others.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to be mindful of our mental processes and to actively engage in creating a better world through our actions and intentions.
