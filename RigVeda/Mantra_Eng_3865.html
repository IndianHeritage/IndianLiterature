यद īं सोमā बभ्रुधूतā अमन्दन्न अरोरवीद वृषभः साधनेषु |पुरंदरः पपिवाम् इन्द्रो अस्य पुनर गवाम् अददाद उष्रियाणाम् ||
yad īṁ somā babhrudhūtā amandann aroravīd vṛṣabhaḥ sādaneṣu | puraṁdaraḥ papivām̐ indro asya punar gavām adadād usriyāṇām ||
  • yad īṁ somā babhrudhūtā: This phrase refers to the "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. The term "babhrudhūtā" suggests that the soma is rich and nourishing.
  • amandann aroravīd vṛṣabhaḥ sādaneṣu: Here, "amandann" implies the act of providing sustenance or nourishment. "Aroravīd" can be interpreted as the one who knows the essence of life, while "vṛṣabhaḥ" refers to a bull, symbolizing strength and fertility. "Sādaneṣu" indicates the places or contexts where this nourishment is provided.
  • puraṁdaraḥ papivām̐: "Puraṁdaraḥ" is often associated with Indra, the king of gods, who is known for his strength and ability to overcome obstacles. "Papivām̐" means to drink or consume, suggesting that Indra partakes in the soma, gaining strength and vitality from it.
  • indro asya punar gavām adadād usriyāṇām: This line emphasizes Indra's role in bestowing blessings. "Indro asya" refers to Indra's influence, while "punar gavām" suggests the return or restoration of wealth and prosperity. "Adadād usriyāṇām" indicates the act of giving or granting, particularly in the context of abundance and nourishment.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of nourishment, strength, and divine blessings. It highlights the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, in achieving prosperity and overcoming challenges.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Health and Nutrition: Just as the soma is a source of nourishment, individuals can focus on maintaining a balanced diet that promotes physical and mental well-being.
    • Spiritual Growth: Engaging in practices that enhance spiritual awareness, such as meditation or yoga, can be seen as a modern equivalent of consuming soma for divine inspiration.
    • Community Support: The idea of providing sustenance can extend to community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, ensuring that everyone has access to resources and support.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nourishment, strength, and divine blessings in our lives. By recognizing the importance of these elements, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling existence, drawing inspiration from the ancient wisdom of the Rig Veda.
