त्वं मखस्य डोधतः शिरोऽव त्वचो भरः |अगच्छः सोमिनो गृहम् ||
tvam makhasya dodhataḥ śiro 'va tvaco bharaḥ | agacchaḥ somino gṛham ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • makhasya - Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the act of giving and devotion.
  • dodhataḥ - Means "the one who churns" or "the one who extracts," suggesting a process of transformation or refinement.
  • śiro - Translates to "head," symbolizing leadership, wisdom, and the pinnacle of thought.
  • 'va - A conjunction meaning "or," indicating a choice or alternative.
  • tvaco - Means "skin" or "body," representing the physical aspect of existence.
  • bharaḥ - Translates to "fill" or "load," suggesting the act of providing or bestowing something.
  • agacchaḥ - Means "go" or "move," indicating a journey or transition.
  • somino - Refers to "of the moon" or "nectar," often associated with nourishment and the divine essence.
  • gṛham - Translates to "home" or "abode," symbolizing a place of comfort, safety, and belonging.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to a divine presence, inviting it to partake in the offerings made through sacrifice. It emphasizes the importance of transformation and the journey towards enlightenment, where the divine essence nourishes the physical and spiritual aspects of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the churning process refines and transforms, individuals can reflect on their own lives, seeking to improve and evolve through experiences and challenges.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and divine presence, enhancing the collective spirit of the community.
  • Mindfulness Practices: The concepts of sacrifice and offering can be integrated into mindfulness practices, where individuals focus on giving back to society and nurturing their surroundings.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence in the act of sacrifice and transformation. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to seek growth and nourishment in their lives. By applying its teachings, one can foster a deeper understanding of their purpose and the importance of community and personal evolution.
