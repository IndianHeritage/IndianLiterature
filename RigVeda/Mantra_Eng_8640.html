मन्त्र: क्रत्वे दाक्षाय नः कव पवस्व सोम धारया | इन्द्राय पातवे सुतो मित्राय वरुणाय च ||
kratve dakṣāya naḥ kave pavasva soma dhārayā | indrāya pātave suto mitrāya varuṇāya ca ||
  • Kratve: This word refers to the concept of "sacrifice" or "ritual." In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where offerings are made to deities or ancestors, symbolizing gratitude and respect.
  • Dakṣāya: This term means "to the skilled" or "to the capable." It emphasizes the importance of skill and competence in any endeavor. For example, in a workplace, recognizing and rewarding skilled employees can lead to greater productivity and innovation.
  • Naḥ: This means "to us." It signifies a collective aspect, indicating that the mantra is a communal appeal or prayer.
  • Kave: This translates to "O poet" or "O sage." It highlights the role of wisdom and creativity in guiding actions and thoughts. In modern life, this can relate to the influence of artists, writers, and thinkers who inspire society.
  • Pavasva: This means "may you purify" or "may you cleanse." It suggests a desire for spiritual and moral purification, akin to seeking clarity and integrity in one's life choices.
  • Soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality. In contemporary terms, it can represent anything that rejuvenates the spirit, such as nature, art, or community.
  • Dhārayā: This means "may you sustain" or "may you hold." It reflects the need for support and stability in life, akin to the importance of family and community in providing emotional and social support.
  • Indrāya: Referring to Indra, the king of gods, this term invokes strength and power. In real life, it can symbolize leadership and the qualities needed to overcome challenges.
  • Pātave: This means "to the powerful" or "to the strong." It emphasizes the need for strength in various aspects of life, such as physical health, mental resilience, and emotional fortitude.
  • Suto: This translates to "the son" or "the offspring." It signifies the importance of lineage and heritage, reflecting how values and traditions are passed down through generations.
  • Mitrāya: This means "to the friend." It highlights the significance of friendship and alliances in life, emphasizing the need for supportive relationships.
  • Varuṇāya: Referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order, this term invokes the principles of justice and moral law. In modern contexts, it can relate to the importance of ethics and fairness in society.

Conclusion: The mantra "kratve dakṣāya naḥ kave pavasva soma dhārayā | indrāya pātave suto mitrāya varuṇāya ca ||" encapsulates a profound appeal for skill, purification, strength, and support from divine entities. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of community, the importance of moral values, and the need for guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and ethical leadership, reminding us to seek wisdom, nurture relationships, and uphold justice in our daily actions.
