सुत इन्द्राय विष्णवे सोमः कलशे अक्षरत् |मधुमां अस्तु वायवे ||
suta indrāya viṣṇave somaḥ kalaśe akṣarat | madhumām̐ astu vāyave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suta: son or offspring, often referring to a divine or noble lineage.
    • indrāya: to Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain, thunder, and war.
    • viṣṇave: to Vishnu, the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity.
    • somaḥ: soma, a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine qualities.
    • kalaśe: in the pot or vessel, symbolizing containment and nourishment.
    • akṣarat: imperishable or eternal, indicating something that transcends the physical.
    • madhumām̐: sweet or pleasant, often referring to the essence of life or divine nectar.
    • astu: may it be, a wish or blessing.
    • vāyave: to Vayu, the god of wind, representing movement and life force.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Vishnu, two principal deities in Hinduism, emphasizing the importance of divine protection and sustenance.
    • The mention of soma in a vessel signifies the nourishment of the soul and the body, suggesting that spiritual and physical well-being are interconnected.
    • The term "akṣarat" highlights the eternal nature of the divine, reminding us that while physical forms may perish, the essence of divinity remains everlasting.
    • The invocation to Vayu symbolizes the vital breath and the importance of air and life force in sustaining existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek divine guidance and protection in our endeavors, much like how individuals pray for success and safety before embarking on a journey.
    • The concept of soma can be related to the importance of nutrition and health in our lives, emphasizing that what we consume affects our physical and spiritual well-being.
    • In a broader sense, the invocation of Vayu can be linked to the significance of environmental awareness, as clean air and nature are essential for life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for health, protection, and sustenance in life.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it promotes, integrating physical, spiritual, and environmental well-being.
    • In contemporary life, it serves as a reminder to maintain a balance between our material pursuits and spiritual growth, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the world around us.
