अस्मा इद उ प्र भरā तूतुजानो वृत्राय वज्रम ईशानः कियेदहाः |गोर न पर्व विदाद तिरश्चेष्यन्न अर्णांस्य अपां चरध्यै ||
asmā id u pra bharā tūtujāno vṛtrāya vajram īśānaḥ kiyedhāḥ | gor na parva vi radā tiraśceṣyann arṇāṁsy apāṁ caradhyai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā: from us
    • id: this
    • u: indeed
    • pra: forward
    • bharā: bear or carry
    • tūtujāno: the one who is strong or powerful
    • vṛtrāya: against the obstacle or enemy
    • vajram: the thunderbolt or weapon
    • īśānaḥ: the lord or ruler
    • kiyedhāḥ: how many or how much
    • gor: of the cow or wealth
    • na: not
    • parva: the festival or occasion
    • vi: in the midst of
    • radā: the sacrifice or offering
    • tiraśceṣyann: crossing over
    • arṇāṁsy: of the waters
    • apāṁ: of the waters
    • caradhyai: for the purpose of moving or flowing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes strength and power from the divine to overcome obstacles.
    • It emphasizes the importance of carrying forward our intentions and actions with determination.
    • The reference to "vajram" symbolizes the strength and might needed to face challenges, akin to how a warrior uses a weapon in battle.
    • The mention of "cow" signifies wealth and prosperity, suggesting that overcoming obstacles leads to abundance.
    • The phrase "crossing over the waters" can be interpreted as navigating through life's challenges, much like how one must cross a river to reach the other side.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges in a project. Just as one must push through difficulties to achieve success, the mantra encourages perseverance.
    • In personal life, when dealing with emotional or financial obstacles, invoking the strength symbolized by "vajram" can inspire individuals to take decisive actions.
    • During festivals or significant life events, the mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on the positive outcomes of overcoming challenges, much like the joy experienced after a successful celebration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, determination, and the ability to overcome obstacles in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to professional endeavors, reminding us to harness our inner power and strive for success.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and a proactive approach to life's difficulties.
