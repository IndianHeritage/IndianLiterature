अभि वस्त्राः सुवसनान्य अर्षाभि धेनुः सुदुघाः पूयमानः |अभि चन्द्रा भार्तवे नो हिरण्याभ्य अश्वान रथिनो देव सोम ||
abhi vastrā suvasanāny arṣābhi dhenūḥ sudughāḥ pūyamānaḥ | abhi candrā bhartave no hiraṇyābhy aśvān rathino deva soma ||
  • Mantra: "abhi vastrā suvasanāny arṣābhi dhenūḥ sudughāḥ pūyamānaḥ | abhi candrā bhartave no hiraṇyābhy aśvān rathino deva soma ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • vastrā: garments, clothing
    • suvasanāny: well-clad, beautifully adorned
    • arṣābhi: with the strength of the earth
    • dhenūḥ: cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • sudughāḥ: very rich, abundant
    • pūyamānaḥ: being filled, overflowing
    • candrā: moon, representing beauty and prosperity
    • bhartave: for the sustainer, the provider
    • no: our
    • hiraṇyābhy: golden, wealth
    • aśvān: horses, symbolizing strength and speed
    • rathino: chariots, representing movement and progress
    • deva: divine, god
    • soma: a sacred drink, symbolizing vitality and energy
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of prosperity and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of being well-clad and adorned, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for being prepared and equipped for life's challenges.
    • The mention of cows (dhenūḥ) signifies wealth and nourishment, reflecting the agricultural society of ancient India where cattle were a primary source of livelihood.
    • The reference to the moon (candrā) symbolizes beauty and prosperity, suggesting that one should seek not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional fulfillment.
    • The horses (aśvān) and chariots (rathino) represent strength and progress, indicating that one should strive for advancement in life, both materially and spiritually.
    • The invocation of the divine (deva) and the sacred drink (soma) highlights the importance of seeking divine blessings and vitality in all endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of education and self-improvement. Just as one prepares for a journey by equipping themselves with the right tools, individuals should seek knowledge and skills to navigate their paths effectively.
    • In a business context, the mantra encourages entrepreneurs to create a nurturing environment (like the cows) that fosters growth and abundance, ensuring that their ventures are well-supported and prosperous.
    • On a personal level, it serves as a reminder to cultivate beauty and positivity in one's life, akin to the moon's glow, which can be achieved through mindfulness, gratitude, and self-care.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking abundance, strength, and divine support in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to be well-prepared, to nurture their resources, and to strive for both material and spiritual growth.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates—balancing material wealth with spiritual fulfillment, which is essential for a harmonious and prosperous life.
    • In essence, it serves as a guiding principle for living a life that is rich in both external achievements and internal peace.
