ये सोमासः परावति ये अर्वावति सुन्विरे |ये वादः शर्यणावति ||
ye somāsaḥ parāvati ye arvāvati sunvire | ye vādaḥ śaryaṇāvati ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Those who are the drinkers of Soma, who are exalted, who are the ones who bring forth the sun; those who are the speakers of truth, who are the ones who bring forth the light."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • somāsaḥ: Drinkers of Soma (Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration)
    • parāvati: Exalted or elevated
    • arvāvati: Those who bring forth or manifest
    • sunvire: The sun or light
    • vādaḥ: Speakers or utterers
    • śaryaṇāvati: Those who bring forth truth or light
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher knowledge and truth.
    • The "drinkers of Soma" symbolize those who pursue spiritual enlightenment and wisdom.
    • The exalted ones are those who have achieved a higher state of consciousness.
    • Bringing forth the sun represents the illumination of knowledge and truth in one’s life.
    • Speaking truth is a fundamental principle that leads to clarity and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody the qualities of truthfulness and enlightenment inspire their teams to achieve greater heights.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who communicate openly and honestly foster trust and deeper connections.
    • In education, teachers who encourage critical thinking and the pursuit of knowledge help students become enlightened individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of seeking truth and enlightenment in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for higher knowledge and to be truthful in their interactions.
    • In a world filled with misinformation, the principles outlined in this mantra are more relevant than ever, guiding us towards a path of integrity and wisdom.
