पिबा-पिबेद इन्द्र शूर सोमं मा ऋषाण्यो वसवान वसुः सन |उता त्रायस्व गृणतो मघोनो महश्च रायो रेवतस कृधī नः ||
pibā-pibed indra śūra somam mā riṣaṇyo vasavāna vasuḥ san | uta trāyasva gṛṇato maghono mahaś ca rāyo revatas kṛdhī naḥ ||
  • pibā-pibed: This term translates to "drink" or "consume." It signifies the act of taking in something beneficial, often associated with the consumption of soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and the warrior spirit. He is often invoked for protection and victory.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "heroic." It emphasizes the qualities of courage and valor, which are essential in overcoming challenges.
  • somam: Soma is a divine drink that is believed to confer immortality and strength. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • mā riṣaṇyo: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not let us be harmed." It is a plea for protection and safety from adversities.
  • vasavāna: This term refers to the "wealthy" or "prosperous." It signifies abundance and the blessings of material and spiritual wealth.
  • vasuḥ: Vasu means "wealth" or "prosperity." It is often associated with the material and spiritual riches that one seeks in life.
  • uta: This word means "and" or "also," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • trāyasva: This term translates to "protect" or "safeguard." It is a call for divine protection in times of need.
  • gṛṇato: This means "of those who sing" or "of those who praise." It highlights the importance of devotion and gratitude in one's life.
  • maghono: This word means "of the generous" or "of the bountiful." It refers to the blessings that come from generosity and abundance.
  • mahaś: This translates to "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of strength and power in achieving one's goals.
  • ca: This means "and," further connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • rāyo: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the theme of abundance.
  • revatas: This word means "of the prosperous" or "of the wealthy," again emphasizing the blessings of prosperity.
  • kṛdhī: This translates to "may you create" or "may you bring forth," indicating a desire for the manifestation of blessings.
  • naḥ: This means "for us," indicating a collective plea for blessings and protection.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for strength, protection, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of courage and the consumption of life-giving resources, symbolized by soma. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times when one seeks divine support and abundance. It serves as a reminder to cultivate bravery, express gratitude, and strive for both material and spiritual wealth. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and attract positive energies into their lives.
