अच्छा नो अंगिरस्तमं यज्ञासो यन्तु संयतः |होता यो अस्ति विक्ष्वा यशस्तमः ||
acchā no aṅgirastamaṁ yajñāso yantu saṁyataḥ | hotā yo asti vikṣv ā yaśastamaḥ ||
  • acchā no: This phrase can be translated as "may we be auspicious" or "may we be well." It signifies a desire for well-being and positivity in life.
  • aṅgirastamaṁ: Referring to "the best of the Angiras," this term connects to the Angiras, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. It implies seeking the highest wisdom or guidance.
  • yajñāso: This word means "of the sacrifices" or "of the offerings." In Vedic tradition, yajnas (sacrifices) are performed to please the deities and ensure harmony in the universe.
  • yantu: This translates to "may they go" or "may they proceed." It indicates a movement towards a goal or aspiration.
  • saṁyataḥ: Meaning "controlled" or "restrained," this term emphasizes the importance of self-discipline and moderation in one's actions.
  • hotā: This refers to "the priest" or "the one who invokes." In the context of a yajna, the hota is responsible for performing the rituals and invoking the deities.
  • yo asti: This means "who is" or "who exists." It highlights the presence of the hota and the significance of the role they play in the rituals.
  • vikṣv: This term can be interpreted as "in all directions" or "everywhere." It signifies the universal nature of the offerings and the reach of the rituals.
  • ā yaśastamaḥ: This phrase translates to "the best of fame" or "the highest glory." It indicates the aspiration for achieving great renown and respect through righteous actions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness, wisdom, and self-discipline in the performance of rituals and sacrifices. It highlights the role of the priest in invoking divine blessings and the universal impact of these actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios, such as personal development, community service, and leadership. By embodying the principles of well-being, wisdom, and self-control, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings and achieve a sense of fulfillment and respect in their endeavors. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious and disciplined approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek higher knowledge and act with purpose for the greater good.
