त्वं सूर्ये न आ भज तव क्रत्वा तवोतििभिः |अथा नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
tvaṁ sūrye na ā bhaja tava kratvā tavotibhiḥ | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • sūrye: O Sun
    • na: Not
    • ā: Come
    • bhaja: Worship
    • tava: Your
    • kratvā: Power/Strength
    • tavotibhiḥ: With your might
    • athā: Therefore
    • no: Us
    • vasyasas: Be favorable
    • kṛdhi: Do/Make
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer addressed to the Sun, asking for its strength and power to be directed towards the supplicant. The speaker implores the Sun not to withdraw its blessings and to be favorable to them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, people often seek the blessings of the Sun for health and vitality. For instance, many cultures have rituals at sunrise to honor the Sun, believing it brings energy and positivity for the day ahead.
    • In agriculture, farmers pray to the Sun for favorable weather conditions, as sunlight is crucial for crop growth. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of their dependence on the Sun's power for a successful harvest.
    • In personal development, individuals may invoke the energy of the Sun as a metaphor for inner strength and resilience, seeking to harness that power to overcome challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the Sun as a source of life and energy. It emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and favor from higher powers in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as health, agriculture, and personal growth, reminding us of our connection to nature and the forces that sustain us.
