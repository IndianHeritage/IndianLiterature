ते नो रत्नानि धत्तन त्रिर् आ सप्तानि सुन्वते |एकम्-एकं सुशस्तिभिः ||
te no ratnāni dhattana trir ā sāptāni sunvate | ekam-ekaṁ suśastibhiḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the treasures (ratnāni) that we seek (dhattana) be bestowed upon us (te no) three times (trir) and may the seven (sāptāni) be heard (sunvate) together with the one (ekam) and each (ekaṁ) in their own excellence (suśastibhiḥ)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • te: you, your
    • no: our
    • ratnāni: treasures, riches
    • dhattana: bestow, grant
    • trir: three times
    • sāptāni: seven
    • sunvate: to be heard, listened to
    • ekam: one
    • ekaṁ: each
    • suśastibhiḥ: in their own excellence, well-formed
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking and receiving blessings or treasures in life, not just in a material sense but also in terms of wisdom, health, and spiritual growth.
    • The repetition of "three times" signifies the importance of persistence and dedication in our pursuits.
    • The mention of "seven" can be interpreted as the seven chakras in the body, representing holistic well-being.
    • The phrase "each in their own excellence" suggests that every individual has unique strengths and qualities that should be recognized and celebrated.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to teamwork. Each member brings their unique skills (the treasures) to the table, and when they work together (the seven), they can achieve greater success (the one).
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their strengths and areas for improvement, seeking to enhance their skills (the treasures) through continuous learning and practice (three times).
    • In community service, the mantra can inspire collective efforts where each person's contribution is valued, leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individual efforts and collective success. It encourages us to seek out and appreciate the unique contributions of ourselves and others.
    • Its significance lies in fostering a mindset of gratitude and recognition, which can lead to personal fulfillment and community harmony.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance relationships, improve teamwork, and promote a culture of excellence and appreciation in various aspects of life.
