तद् वो वाजाऽऋभवः सुप्रवाचनं देवेषु विभ्वो अभवन महित्वनम् |जीव्री यत् संतापितराऽसनाजुराऽ पुनर् युवानाऽ चरथाय तक्षथ ||
tad vo vājā ṛbhavaḥ supravācanaṁ deveṣu vibhvo abhavan mahitvanam | jivrī yat santā pitarā sanājurā punar yuvānā carathāya takṣatha ||
  • tad vo vājā ṛbhavaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is your strength." Here, "vājā" refers to strength or power, often associated with the energy that drives success and prosperity.
  • supravācanaṁ deveṣu: This means "well-spoken among the gods." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in divine contexts, suggesting that what is spoken holds significant weight.
  • vibhvo abhavan mahitvanam: This translates to "the great ones have become powerful." It indicates that those who are revered or respected have attained greatness through their actions and virtues.
  • jivrī yat santā pitarā: This can be interpreted as "the living beings that are descendants of the ancestors." It highlights the connection between generations and the importance of lineage and heritage.
  • sanājurā punar yuvānā: This means "the ancient ones who are young again." It suggests a cycle of renewal and rejuvenation, where wisdom from the past is revitalized in the present.
  • carathāya takṣatha: This translates to "you shall strive for progress." It encourages action and effort towards achieving goals and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, communication, and the connection between generations. It teaches us that our words and actions can lead to greatness and that we should strive for progress while honoring our heritage. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, where effective communication and strength of character are essential for inspiring others. It also highlights the importance of learning from our ancestors and using that wisdom to navigate modern challenges, fostering a sense of continuity and purpose in our lives.
