तं त्वा विप्राः वचोविदः परिष्कृत्वन्ति वेधसः |सं त्वा मृजन्त्यायवः ||
taṁ tvā viprā vacovidaḥ pari ṣkṛṇvanti vedhasaḥ | saṁ tvā mṛjanty āyavaḥ ||
  • taṁ tvā - This phrase translates to "that you" or "to you." It signifies a direct address to a higher power or divine entity, indicating a relationship of reverence and devotion.
  • viprā - This term refers to the wise or learned individuals, often associated with the priests or sages in Vedic tradition. They are the ones who possess knowledge and understanding of the Vedas.
  • vacovidaḥ - This means "those who know the words" or "those who are knowledgeable in speech." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in conveying knowledge and wisdom.
  • pari ṣkṛṇvanti - This translates to "they prepare" or "they arrange." It suggests an active role in organizing or structuring knowledge, possibly referring to the rituals or teachings that the wise impart.
  • vedhasaḥ - This term relates to the Vedas, the ancient sacred texts of Hinduism. It signifies the source of knowledge and wisdom that guides individuals in their spiritual and moral lives.
  • saṁ tvā - This means "they cleanse you" or "they purify you." It indicates a process of spiritual purification, which is essential for attaining higher knowledge and understanding.
  • mṛjanty - This translates to "they wipe" or "they remove." It suggests the act of removing impurities or obstacles that hinder spiritual growth and enlightenment.
  • āyavaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "those who seek" or "those who aspire." It refers to individuals who are on a quest for knowledge, truth, and spiritual fulfillment.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the relationship between the seeker of knowledge and the wise individuals who guide them. It highlights the importance of words and communication in the pursuit of wisdom, as well as the necessity of spiritual purification for those who aspire to understand the deeper truths of existence.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra serves as a reminder of the value of wisdom and the role of learned individuals in society. In real life, it encourages us to seek guidance from those who possess knowledge and experience.
  • It underscores the importance of clear communication and the power of words in shaping our understanding and relationships. For example, effective communication in personal and professional settings can lead to better collaboration and problem-solving.
  • The concept of purification is relevant in various aspects of life, such as mental clarity, emotional well-being, and ethical living. Engaging in practices like meditation, self-reflection, and ethical behavior can help individuals remove obstacles to their personal growth.
  • Ultimately, this mantra inspires individuals to be lifelong learners, to seek knowledge, and to strive for spiritual and personal development, fostering a more enlightened and harmonious society.
