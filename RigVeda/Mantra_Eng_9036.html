त्वं नः सोम सुखृतुर वयोद्धेयाय जागृहि |क्षेत्रवित्तरो मनुषो वि वो मदे द्रुहो नः पाहि अंहसो विवक्षसे ||
tvaṁ naḥ soma sukratur vayodheyāya jāgṛhi | kṣetravittaro manuṣo vi vo made druho naḥ pāhy aṁhaso vivakṣase ||
  • tvaṁ naḥ: This phrase translates to "You are ours," indicating a deep connection or relationship with the divine or a higher power.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • sukratur: This word means "one who does good deeds" or "benefactor." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and virtuous actions in life.
  • vayodheyāya: This term can be interpreted as "for the purpose of strength" or "to gain vitality." It suggests a quest for physical and mental strength.
  • jāgṛhi: This means "awaken" or "be alert." It calls for awareness and mindfulness in our actions and thoughts.
  • kṣetravittaro: This phrase refers to "the one who possesses wealth in the field," symbolizing prosperity and abundance in life.
  • manuṣo: This means "human" or "person," highlighting the human experience and the importance of human actions.
  • vi vo made: This can be interpreted as "in your joy" or "in your delight," suggesting that joy and happiness are essential aspects of life.
  • druho: This word means "enemy" or "obstacle." It signifies the challenges and adversities one may face in life.
  • naḥ: This means "us" or "our," reinforcing the communal aspect of the mantra.
  • pāhy: This translates to "protect" or "safeguard," indicating a plea for divine protection.
  • aṁhaso: This refers to "our strength" or "our power," emphasizing the importance of inner strength.
  • vivakṣase: This means "you express" or "you reveal," suggesting the importance of communication and expression in life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a plea for divine guidance, protection, and the pursuit of righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions while seeking strength and prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, community building, and spiritual growth. By embodying the principles of good deeds, awareness, and seeking divine support, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively and foster a sense of unity and purpose within their communities.
