पवितारः पुनीतन सोमं इन्द्राय पातवे |अथा नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
pavītāraḥ punītana somam indrāya pātave | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • pavītāraḥ: This word translates to "purifying" or "sacred." It signifies the essence of cleanliness and sanctity, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
  • punītana: Meaning "to purify" or "to cleanse," this term emphasizes the act of removing impurities, whether they be moral, spiritual, or physical.
  • somam: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • indrāya: This word means "to Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • pātave: Translates to "to be offered" or "to be given," indicating the act of devotion and surrender to the divine.
  • athā: This word means "then" or "therefore," suggesting a transition or a consequence of the preceding actions.
  • no: Meaning "our," it indicates a collective identity or community.
  • vasyasas: This term translates to "to be controlled" or "to be subdued," implying a desire for harmony and order in life.
  • kṛdhi: Meaning "to create" or "to make," it signifies the act of bringing something into existence or manifesting intentions.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for purification and divine blessings. It invokes the purifying essence of Soma, asking for the strength and guidance of Indra to cleanse and elevate the collective spirit of the community. The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, both in thought and action, and the need for divine intervention to achieve harmony and control over one's life circumstances.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use the essence of this mantra to seek purification of their thoughts and actions, striving for a more virtuous life.
  • Community Harmony: In group settings, such as families or organizations, this mantra can serve as a reminder to work together towards common goals, fostering unity and cooperation.
  • Spiritual Practices: During rituals or meditation, reciting this mantra can help individuals focus on cleansing their inner selves and inviting divine guidance.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant importance as it encapsulates the essence of purification, divine connection, and the pursuit of harmony. It serves as a reminder of the need for both personal and collective efforts to achieve a balanced and fulfilling life, making it applicable in various aspects of daily living.
