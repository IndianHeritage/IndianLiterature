उत स्मा हि त्वां आहुर इन मगवानं शचीपते |दाताрам अविदीधयुम ||
uta smā hi tvām āhur in maghavānaṁ śacīpate | dātāram avidīdhayum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: indeed, or certainly
    • smā: we, or us
    • hi: for, or because
    • tvām: you
    • āhur: they say, or they declare
    • in: in, or within
    • maghavānaṁ: of the powerful, or the mighty
    • śacīpate: O Lord of strength, or O Lord of power
    • dātāram: the giver, or the bestower
    • avidīdhayum: who is not to be neglected, or who is not to be disregarded
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for a powerful deity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "uta smā hi tvām āhur" indicates that the speaker acknowledges the divine presence and power of this deity.
    • By referring to the deity as "maghavānaṁ," it highlights the might and strength associated with this divine figure.
    • The term "śacīpate" signifies the lordship over strength and power, suggesting that this deity is a protector and a source of strength.
    • The concluding part, "dātāram avidīdhayum," emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the giver of strength and blessings, who should not be overlooked or neglected.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and guidance during challenging times, much like how one might pray to a higher power for support.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may embody the qualities of "maghavānaṁ" by empowering their teams, providing resources, and fostering an environment of strength and collaboration.
    • In personal relationships, acknowledging the contributions of others, akin to recognizing the "dātāram," can strengthen bonds and promote gratitude.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and honoring the sources of strength and support in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to appreciate the powerful forces, whether divine or human, that contribute to their well-being and success.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the interconnectedness of strength, support, and gratitude, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
