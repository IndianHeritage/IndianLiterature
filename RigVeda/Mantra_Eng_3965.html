आ दैव्याणि पार्थिवाणि जन्मापश्चाच्छा सुमखाय वोचम् |वर्धन्तां द्यावो गिरश्चन्द्राग्राः उदा वर्धन्ताम् अभिषाताः अर्णाः ||
ā daivyāni pārthivāni janmāpaś cācchā sumakhāya vocam | vardhantāṁ dyāvo giraś candrāgrā udā vardhantām abhiṣātā arṇāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • daivyāni: divine
    • pārthivāni: earthly
    • janmāpaś: born of
    • cācchā: auspicious
    • sumakhāya: good fortune
    • vocam: I speak
    • vardhantāṁ: may they flourish
    • dyāvo: the heavens
    • giraś: with words
    • candrāgrā: the moon's peak
    • udā: exalted
    • vardhantām: may they increase
    • abhiṣātā: with blessings
    • arṇāḥ: the oceans
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes both divine and earthly energies, suggesting a harmonious relationship between the spiritual and material worlds.
    • The phrase "may they flourish" emphasizes the desire for growth and prosperity in all aspects of life.
    • By mentioning "the heavens" and "the moon's peak," it highlights the importance of celestial influences on earthly existence.
    • The reference to "blessings" from the oceans signifies the abundance and nurturing qualities of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for divine blessings to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the desire for prosperity and growth.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek guidance from spiritual practices to align their earthly actions with divine intentions, promoting a balanced life.
    • Communities often come together in rituals to invoke blessings for collective well-being, showcasing the interconnectedness of individuals and nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking harmony between the divine and earthly realms, encouraging individuals to cultivate good fortune and prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, community well-being, and environmental stewardship.
    • By recognizing the interconnectedness of all life, this mantra serves as a reminder to honor both spiritual and material aspects of existence.
