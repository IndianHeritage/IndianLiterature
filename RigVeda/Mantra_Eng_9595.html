अभि त्वा सिन्धो शिशुम इन न मातरो वाश्रा अर्षन्ति पयसेव धेनवः |राजेव युध्वा नयसि त्वम् इति सिचौ यद आसाम अग्रं प्रवताम इनक्षसि ||
abhi tvā sindho śiśum in na mātaro vāśrā arṣanti payaseva dhenavaḥ | rājeva yudhvā nayasi tvam it sicau yad āsām agram pravatām inakṣasi ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tvā sindho śiśum in na mātaro vāśrā arṣanti payaseva dhenavaḥ | rājeva yudhvā nayasi tvam it sicau yad āsām agram pravatām inakṣasi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • tvā: you
    • sindho: of the ocean or waters
    • śiśum: child or offspring
    • in: not
    • mātaro: mothers
    • vāśrā: those who are protective
    • arṣanti: are disturbed
    • payaseva: like milk
    • dhenavaḥ: cows
    • rājeva: like a king
    • yudhvā: fighting
    • nayasi: you lead
    • tvam: you
    • it: this
    • sicau: in purity
    • yad: that
    • āsām: of those
    • agram: foremost
    • pravatām: of those who flow
    • inakṣasi: you see
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine essence of water, likening it to a nurturing mother and a powerful king.
    • The phrase "abhi tvā sindho" suggests a call towards the waters, indicating their importance in life and sustenance.
    • It emphasizes the protective nature of water, akin to a mother who nurtures her child, while also highlighting its strength and purity.
    • The comparison of water to milk ("payaseva dhenavaḥ") illustrates its life-giving properties, essential for growth and nourishment.
    • The latter part of the mantra speaks to the leadership and guidance that water provides, akin to a king leading his subjects.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, water is vital for crops, much like a mother providing sustenance to her child. Farmers often pray for rain, recognizing the divine nature of water in their livelihoods.
    • In many cultures, rivers are considered sacred and are treated with reverence, reflecting the mantra's acknowledgment of water as a nurturing and protective force.
    • Water conservation efforts highlight the importance of preserving this precious resource, as it is essential for life, akin to the nurturing aspect of a mother.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the essential role of water in our lives, portraying it as both a nurturing and powerful entity.
    • Its application in real life is evident in our dependence on water for survival, agriculture, and spiritual practices.
    • By recognizing the significance of water, we are reminded of our responsibility to protect and cherish this vital resource for future generations.
