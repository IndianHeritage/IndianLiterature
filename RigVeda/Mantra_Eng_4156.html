युयं राजानम इर्यं जनाय विभ्वतष्टं जनयथाऽ यजत्राः |युष्मद एति मुष्ठिहा बाहुजूतो युष्मत सदश्वो मरुतः सुवीरः ||
yūyaṁ rājānam iryaṁ janāya vibhvataṣṭaṁ janayathā yajatrāḥ | yuṣmad eti muṣṭihā bāhujūto yuṣmat sadaśvo marutaḥ suvīraḥ ||
  • yūyaṁ rājānam: This phrase translates to "you (plural) are the king." It signifies the collective power and responsibility of a group, suggesting that leadership is not just an individual trait but a shared duty among many.
  • iryaṁ janāya: This means "for the people." It emphasizes that the actions and decisions of leaders should be for the benefit of the community, highlighting the importance of service and welfare in leadership.
  • vibhvataṣṭaṁ: This can be interpreted as "having great wealth or resources." It suggests that true leadership involves not just authority but also the ability to provide and share resources for the betterment of society.
  • janayathā yajatrāḥ: This translates to "may you generate (or create) offerings." It implies that leaders should inspire and encourage contributions from the community, fostering a spirit of cooperation and collective effort.
  • yuṣmad eti muṣṭihā: This phrase means "your strength is like a fist." It symbolizes unity and strength in action, indicating that when people come together, they can achieve great things.
  • bāhujūto: This translates to "with strong arms." It signifies the physical and metaphorical strength required to support and protect the community.
  • yuṣmat sadaśvo: This means "your steeds (horses) are always with you." It symbolizes readiness and the importance of being prepared to act swiftly and decisively for the welfare of the people.
  • marutaḥ suvīraḥ: This translates to "the Maruts (storm gods) are strong." The Maruts represent power and dynamism, suggesting that true leadership is also about harnessing energy and enthusiasm for progress.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of collective leadership and responsibility. It emphasizes that true leaders are those who act for the benefit of their people, share resources, and inspire collective action. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in community leadership, organizational management, and even in family dynamics, where the focus should be on collaboration, support, and shared goals. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and proactive engagement in the welfare of society, reminding us that effective leadership is a communal effort that thrives on cooperation and shared vision.
