यस्य विश्वानि हस्तयोः पञ्च क्षितीनां वसु |स्पाशयस्व यो अस्मदृग दिव्येवाशनिर जहि ||
yasya viśvāni hastayoḥ pañca kṣitīnāṁ vasu | spāśayasva yo asmadhrug divyevāśanir jahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • viśvāni: all (the) worlds
    • hastayoḥ: of the hands
    • pañca: five
    • kṣitīnāṁ: of the earths
    • vasu: wealth or good things
    • spāśayasva: may you bestow
    • yo: who
    • asmadhrug: our protector
    • divyevā: like the divine
    • śanir: the one who is powerful
    • jahi: may you conquer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine force that possesses the ability to bestow wealth and prosperity.
    • The reference to "five earths" can symbolize the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that are essential for life.
    • The phrase "may you bestow" indicates a plea for blessings and abundance in all aspects of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine protection and guidance in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited before starting a new venture, seeking divine blessings for success and prosperity.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, individuals may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for a harmonious and prosperous life.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on this mantra as a reminder to seek guidance and protection in their daily lives, fostering a sense of security and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and protection in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements and the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to business ventures, emphasizing the need for divine support in our pursuits.
