यो अद्रिभित् प्रथमजा ऋतावा बृहस्पतिर् आङ्गिरसो हविष्मान् |द्विबर्हज्मा प्राघर्मसत् पिता न आ रोडसी वृषभो रोरवीति ||
yo adribhit prathamajā ṛtāvā bṛhaspatir āṅgiraso haviṣmān | dvibarhajmā prāgharmasat pitā na ā rodasī vṛṣabho roravīti ||
  • Mantra: "yo adribhit prathamajā ṛtāvā bṛhaspatir āṅgiraso haviṣmān | dvibarhajmā prāgharmasat pitā na ā rodasī vṛṣabho roravīti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • adribhit: by the mountains
    • prathamajā: the first-born
    • ṛtāvā: the one who upholds truth
    • bṛhaspatir: the great lord (Bṛhaspati)
    • āṅgiraso: of the Angirasas (a group of sages)
    • haviṣmān: the one who is worthy of offerings
    • dvibarhajmā: the one who is twice invoked
    • prāgharmasat: the one who is the father of the ancient ones
    • pitā: father
    • na: not
    • ā rodasī: in the sky
    • vṛṣabho: the bull (symbol of strength)
    • roravīti: roars or cries
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of Bṛhaspati, who is considered the god of wisdom and eloquence.
    • The phrase "by the mountains" suggests stability and strength, indicating that the wisdom of Bṛhaspati is as enduring as the mountains.
    • "The first-born who upholds truth" emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in life, akin to how honesty is valued in personal and professional relationships.
    • The mention of "the one who is worthy of offerings" signifies the respect and reverence that should be given to wisdom and knowledge.
    • "The father of the ancient ones" connects to the idea of tradition and the wisdom passed down through generations, similar to how elders in families are often seen as repositories of knowledge.
    • The imagery of the "bull" symbolizes strength and power, which can be related to the strength of character needed to uphold truth and wisdom in challenging situations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Bṛhaspati can inspire their team to uphold ethical standards and make decisions based on truth.
    • In personal life, individuals who prioritize honesty and integrity often find themselves respected and trusted by their peers, much like the revered qualities of Bṛhaspati.
    • In education, teachers who impart wisdom and encourage critical thinking reflect the essence of this mantra, guiding students to seek truth and knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, truth, and strength in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and uphold ethical values, which are essential for personal growth and societal harmony.
    • By embodying the qualities of Bṛhaspati, we can navigate life's challenges with integrity and wisdom, ultimately contributing to a more just and enlightened world.
