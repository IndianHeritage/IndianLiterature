मन्त्र: सकृत् धा द्यौर अजायत सकृत् भूमिर अजायत | पृष्ण्या दुग्धं सकृत् पयस् तद अन्यो नानु जायते ||
sakṛd dha dyaur ajāyata sakṛd bhūmir ajāyata | pṛśnyā dugdhaṁ sakṛt payas tad anyo nānu jāyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakṛd: once
    • dha: placed or established
    • dyaur: the sky or heaven
    • ajāyata: was born or came into being
    • bhūmir: the earth
    • pṛśnyā: from the cow or the source of nourishment
    • dugdhaṁ: milk or nourishment
    • payas: the essence or substance
    • tad: that
    • anyo: another
    • nānu: not
    • jāyate: is born or comes into being
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation of the universe, emphasizing the simultaneous birth of the sky and the earth.
    • The phrase "pṛśnyā dugdhaṁ" suggests that nourishment, represented by milk, is a fundamental aspect of existence.
    • It implies that once the sky and earth were established, they provided the necessary sustenance for life.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that nothing else is born that is not derived from this fundamental essence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the earth (bhūmir) provides the soil necessary for crops to grow, while the sky (dyaur) provides sunlight and rain, illustrating the interdependence of these elements.
    • In a family, parents (the earth) provide nourishment and support (the sky) to their children, who are the new life that emerges from this foundation.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra can be related to the concept of sustainability, where the earth and sky work together to provide resources that support life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe, highlighting the importance of both the earth and the sky in the creation of life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the essential resources that sustain us and the need to respect and nurture our environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work towards sustainable practices that honor the balance of nature.
