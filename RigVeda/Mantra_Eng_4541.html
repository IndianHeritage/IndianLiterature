अग्निं देवासो अग्रियम इन्धते वृत्रहन्तमम् |येना वसून्याभृतā तृḻहā रक्षांसी वाजिना ||
agniṁ devāso agriyam indhate vṛtrahantamam | yenā vasūny ābhṛtā tṛḻhā rakṣāṁsi vājinā ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Devāso: This means "the gods" or "divine beings," indicating the presence of higher powers or deities.
  • Agriyam: Translated as "the foremost" or "the best," it signifies the supreme nature of Agni among the gods.
  • Indhate: This means "to ignite" or "to inspire," suggesting the act of invoking or awakening divine energy.
  • Vṛtrahantamam: This term refers to "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or ignorance. It highlights the power of Agni to overcome challenges.
  • Yenā: Meaning "by whom," it indicates the agency or means through which something is achieved.
  • Vasūny: This translates to "the treasures" or "the wealth," representing the abundance and prosperity that can be attained.
  • Ābhṛtā: Meaning "brought forth" or "produced," it signifies the manifestation of wealth and resources.
  • Tṛḻhā: This word means "protection" or "safeguarding," emphasizing the importance of security in life.
  • Rakṣāṁsi: This translates to "the protectors" or "defenses," indicating the need for guardianship against adversities.
  • Vājinā: Meaning "with strength" or "powerful," it suggests the necessity of vigor and resilience in facing life's challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that ignites and inspires, helping to overcome obstacles and bring forth prosperity and protection. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, invoking the energy of Agni can symbolize the drive to pursue goals and overcome challenges.
  • In community settings, the mantra can inspire collective efforts to safeguard resources and ensure prosperity.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder of the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically, in purifying and protecting one's life.

Thus, the mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, protection, and the pursuit of abundance in various aspects of life.
