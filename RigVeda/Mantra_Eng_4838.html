अगव्यūति क्षेत्रम् आगन्म देवाः उर्वी सती भूमिः अंहूरणाभूत् | बृहस्पते प्र चिकीर्त्सा गविष्टाव इत्तथा सते जरीत्र इन्द्र पन्थाम् ||
agavyūti kṣetram āganma devā urvī satī bhūmir aṁhūraṇābhūt | bṛhaspate pra cikitsā gaviṣṭāv itthā sate jaritra indra panthām ||
  • agavyūti: This term can be interpreted as "the path of the righteous" or "the way of the virtuous." It signifies a journey or direction that is aligned with moral and ethical values.
  • kṣetram: This word means "field" or "area." In a broader sense, it can refer to the environment or the context in which actions take place.
  • āganma: This translates to "we have come" or "we have arrived." It indicates a sense of purpose and intention in the journey.
  • devā: This means "gods" or "divine beings." It represents higher powers or forces that guide and influence human actions.
  • urvī: This term refers to "the earth" or "the vast expanse." It symbolizes the physical world and the resources it provides.
  • satī: This word means "truth" or "reality." It emphasizes the importance of authenticity and integrity in one's actions.
  • bhūmir: This translates to "earth" or "ground." It signifies stability and the foundation upon which life is built.
  • aṁhūraṇābhūt: This phrase can be interpreted as "having been made manifest" or "having come into being." It suggests the realization of potential and the emergence of new possibilities.
  • bṛhaspate: This refers to "Bṛhaspati," the deity of wisdom and knowledge. It symbolizes the pursuit of understanding and enlightenment.
  • pra cikitsā: This means "to heal" or "to remedy." It indicates the importance of addressing issues and finding solutions.
  • gaviṣṭāv: This term can be understood as "in the midst of" or "within." It suggests being present and engaged in the current situation.
  • itthā: This translates to "thus" or "in this way." It emphasizes the manner in which actions are taken.
  • sate: This word means "truth" or "existence." It reinforces the idea of being grounded in reality.
  • jaritra: This term refers to "the path" or "the way." It signifies the journey one undertakes in life.
  • indra: This is the name of the king of gods, representing strength, power, and leadership.
  • panthām: This means "path" or "way." It symbolizes the direction one chooses in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path, grounded in truth and wisdom, while being aware of the environment and the divine influences around us. It encourages individuals to seek healing and solutions in their lives, embodying authenticity and integrity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building, where individuals strive to make ethical choices and contribute positively to society. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can navigate life's challenges with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
