मन्त्र: एता ते अग्न उचथानि वेधो 'वोचाम कवेये ता जुषस्व |उच शोचस्व कृणुहि वस्यसो नो महो रायः पूर्वार प्र यन्धि ||
etā te agna ucathāni vedho 'vocāma kavaye tā juṣasva | uc chocasva kṛṇuhi vasyaso no maho rāyaḥ puruvāra pra yandhi ||
  • etā te agna ucathāni vedho: This phrase can be translated as "These are the invocations to you, O Agni (the Fire God), which we have spoken." Here, Agni represents the divine force of fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • 'vocāma kavaye tā juṣasva: This means "We have called upon you, O Poet (Kavi), to enjoy these offerings." The term "Kavi" refers to a seer or a wise person, indicating the importance of wisdom and creativity in our lives.
  • uc chocasva: This translates to "Shine forth." It is a call for illumination, both literally and metaphorically, suggesting the need for knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
  • kṛṇuhi vasyaso no maho rāyaḥ: This means "Make us worthy of great wealth." It emphasizes the desire for prosperity and abundance, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and intellectual wealth.
  • puruvāra pra yandhi: This can be interpreted as "Lead us towards the path of the ancients." It signifies the importance of tradition and the wisdom of our ancestors in guiding our current actions and decisions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of invoking divine energy (Agni) for transformation and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, creativity, and the pursuit of prosperity in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:


  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their goals and aspirations, seeking knowledge and guidance from wise mentors.
  • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts towards prosperity and well-being, encouraging collaboration and shared wisdom.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek inner illumination and transformation through rituals and meditative practices.

Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, tradition, and the pursuit of a prosperous life.
