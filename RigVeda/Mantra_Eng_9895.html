मन्त्र: दिवि न केतुर्विधायि हर्यतो विव्यचद् वज्रो हरितो न रंह्या |तुदद् अहिं हरिशिप्रो य आयसः सहस्रशोकाः अभवत् धरिम्भरः ||
divi na ketur adhi dhāyi haryato vivyacad vajro harito na raṁhyā | tudad ahiṁ hariśipro ya āyasaḥ sahasraśokā abhavad dharimbharaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divi: in the sky or heaven
    • na: not
    • ketur: a banner or flag
    • adhi: above or over
    • dhāyi: placed or established
    • haryato: of the horses or steeds
    • vivyacad: separated or distinguished
    • vajro: thunderbolt or strong
    • harito: of the green or flourishing
    • na: not
    • raṁhyā: hidden or concealed
    • tudad: to strike or hit
    • ahiṁ: the serpent or adversary
    • hariśipro: of the one who is powerful or strong
    • ya: who
    • āyasaḥ: of iron or metal
    • sahasraśokā: of a thousand sorrows
    • abhavad: became or manifested
    • dharimbharaḥ: the one who bears the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is established above, symbolized by a banner in the sky.
    • The imagery of horses suggests strength and vitality, indicating a force that is both powerful and visible.
    • The mention of the thunderbolt (vajra) signifies an unyielding strength that cannot be hidden or subdued.
    • The reference to the serpent (ahiṁ) can symbolize challenges or adversities that one must confront.
    • The phrase "sahasraśokā" indicates the presence of numerous sorrows, suggesting that life is filled with challenges.
    • Ultimately, the mantra emphasizes the strength and resilience required to bear the weight of the earth and its burdens.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, individuals often face numerous challenges, akin to the "sahasraśokā." For instance, someone may deal with the loss of a loved one, financial difficulties, or health issues.
    • In a professional context, a leader may encounter adversities while guiding their team through a crisis, requiring the strength symbolized by the "vajra."
    • In nature, the resilience of ecosystems can be seen as they recover from disasters, embodying the idea of "dharimbharaḥ," where the earth bears its burdens and continues to thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength and resilience needed to face life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to rise above adversities, much like the banner in the sky, symbolizing hope and perseverance.
    • In real life, it can be applied in moments of struggle, urging one to harness inner strength and confront difficulties with courage.
