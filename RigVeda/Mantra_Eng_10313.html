विष्वावसुर अभि तन् नो गृणातु दिव्यो गन्धर्वो रजसो विमानः |यद वा घा सत्यं उता यन् न विद्म धियः हिन्वानो धिया इनो अव्याः ||
viśvāvasur abhi tan no gṛṇātu divyo gandharvo rajaso vimānaḥ | yad vā ghā satyam uta yan na vidma dhiyo hinvāno dhiya in no avyāḥ ||
  • viśvāvasur: This term can be broken down into "viśvā" meaning "all" or "universal" and "vasu" which refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." Together, it signifies the universal wealth or abundance that is sought after.
  • abhi tan no: "Abhi" means "towards" or "for," and "tan" refers to "that" or "that which." "No" means "us" or "our." This phrase can be interpreted as "may that come towards us" or "may that be for us."
  • gṛṇātu: This word means "may it sing" or "may it chant." It implies a call for blessings or praises to be sung in our favor.
  • divyo gandharvo: "Divyo" means "divine" and "gandharvo" refers to "celestial beings" or "singers of the heavens." This suggests a connection to higher spiritual realms or divine entities.
  • rajaso vimānaḥ: "Rajas" refers to "passion" or "activity," and "vimānaḥ" means "vehicles" or "aerial chariots." This can be interpreted as the vehicles of passion or the means through which divine energy is manifested.
  • yad vā ghā satyam: "Yad" means "that which," "vā" means "or," "ghā" refers to "speak" or "say," and "satyam" means "truth." This phrase can be understood as "that which is spoken or that which is true."
  • uta yan na vidma: "Uta" means "or," "yan" means "that which," and "na vidma" translates to "we do not know." This indicates a sense of humility and acknowledgment of the limits of human understanding.
  • dhiyo hinvāno: "Dhiyo" refers to "intellects" or "thoughts," and "hinvāno" means "leading" or "guiding." This suggests a plea for guidance in our thoughts and intellects.
  • dhiya in no avyāḥ: "Dhiya" again refers to "thoughts," "in no" means "in us," and "avyāḥ" means "unharmed" or "unobstructed." This can be interpreted as a desire for our thoughts to remain clear and unobstructed.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking universal wealth and divine blessings while acknowledging the limitations of human understanding. It calls for guidance in our thoughts and intellects, urging us to remain open to divine wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during meditation, prayer, or rituals where individuals seek clarity, abundance, and divine support in their endeavors. It serves as a reminder to remain humble and receptive to the higher forces that guide our lives, fostering a sense of connection with the universe and the divine.
