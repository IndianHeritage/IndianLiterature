सम ī वत्सं न मातृभिः सृजता गयसाधनम् |देवाव्यं मदम् अभि द्विषवसम ||
sam ī vatsaṁ na mātṛbhiḥ sṛjatā gayasādhanam | devāvya1m madam abhi dviśavasam ||
  • Mantra: "sam ī vatsaṁ na mātṛbhiḥ sṛjatā gayasādhanam | devāvya1m madam abhi dviśavasam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • ī: a prefix indicating direction or intention
    • vatsaṁ: calf, symbolizing new beginnings or offspring
    • na: not
    • mātṛbhiḥ: by mothers, indicating nurturing or care
    • sṛjatā: created, produced
    • gayasādhanam: a place of abundance or prosperity
    • devāvya1m: divine, pertaining to the gods
    • madam: intoxication, joy, or bliss
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • dviśavasam: the one who is hostile or has enmity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of harmony and unity in creation.
    • The reference to "vatsaṁ" (calf) symbolizes new beginnings, suggesting that nurturing and care are essential for growth.
    • The phrase "na mātṛbhiḥ sṛjatā" indicates that without the nurturing aspect (mothers), true prosperity (gayasādhanam) cannot be achieved.
    • The invocation of divine joy ("devāvya1m madam") suggests that true happiness comes from a connection with the divine.
    • Finally, the mention of "dviśavasam" warns against hostility and enmity, highlighting the need for peace and cooperation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the nurturing role of parents is crucial for the development of children. Just as the mantra suggests, without care and love, children may not thrive.
    • In a workplace, collaboration and harmony among team members lead to greater productivity and success, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on unity.
    • In community building, fostering a sense of belonging and support can create a prosperous environment, aligning with the idea of "gayasādhanam."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of nurturing, unity, and divine connection in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true prosperity and joy come from harmonious relationships and the avoidance of hostility.
    • In practical terms, applying these principles can lead to healthier families, more effective teams, and stronger communities.
