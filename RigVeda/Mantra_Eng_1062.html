उभे भद्रे जोषयते न मेने गावो न वाश्राः उप तस्थुर एविः |स दाक्षाणां दाक्षपतिर्भभूवाञ्जन्ति यं दक्षिणतो हविर्भिः ||
ubhe bhadre joṣayete na mene gāvo na vāśrā upa tasthur evaiḥ | sa dakṣāṇāṁ dakṣapatir babhūvāñjanti yaṁ dakṣiṇato havirbhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ubhe bhadre joṣayete na mene gāvo na vāśrā upa tasthur evaiḥ | sa dakṣāṇāṁ dakṣapatir babhūvāñjanti yaṁ dakṣiṇato havirbhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Both the auspicious ones who are invoked do not neglect the cows and the offerings; he who is the master of the skilled ones, may he be invoked with offerings from the south."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ubhe: Both
    • bhadre: Auspicious or good
    • joṣayete: Who are invoked or called upon
    • na mene: Do not neglect
    • gāvo: Cows
    • na vāśrā: Not the offerings
    • upa tasthur: They who are present
    • evaiḥ: Indeed
    • sa: He
    • dakṣāṇāṁ: Of the skilled ones
    • dakṣapatir: Master of the skilled
    • babhūvāñ: May he be invoked
    • janti: They who are called
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • dakṣiṇato: From the south
    • havirbhiḥ: With offerings

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking auspicious energies and deities while ensuring that essential elements, such as cows and offerings, are not neglected. In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
    • The phrase "master of the skilled ones" refers to a deity or divine force that governs those who are adept in their crafts and professions. This highlights the value of skill and expertise in various fields.
    • The direction "from the south" can symbolize prosperity and abundance, as many cultures associate the south with positive attributes.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as business and personal development. For instance, when starting a new venture, one might invoke the energies of prosperity and skill, ensuring that all necessary resources (like support from skilled individuals) are not overlooked.
    • In agricultural practices, respecting and caring for livestock, particularly cows, can lead to sustainable farming and economic stability. This aligns with the mantra's emphasis on not neglecting cows.
    • In personal growth, individuals can reflect on their skills and seek to enhance them, invoking the support of mentors or guides who can help them on their journey.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of auspiciousness, skill, and the resources we rely on. By invoking positive energies and ensuring that we do not overlook essential elements in our lives, we can foster growth, prosperity, and well-being.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates, encouraging individuals to be mindful of their surroundings, the resources they have, and the skills they possess, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
