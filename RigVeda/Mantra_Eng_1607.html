स चन्द्रो विप्र मार्त्यो महो व्राधन्तमो दिवि |प्र-प्रेत ते अग्ने वानुषः स्याम ||
sa candro vipra martyo maho vrādhantamo divi | pra-pret te agne vanuṣaḥ syāma ||
  • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a focus in the mantra.
  • candro - This means "moon," symbolizing calmness, beauty, and the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • vipra - Refers to a "wise person" or "sage," someone who possesses knowledge and insight.
  • martyo - This translates to "mortal" or "human," emphasizing the human condition and our transient nature.
  • maho - Meaning "great" or "mighty," it signifies strength and importance.
  • vrādhantamo - This word can be interpreted as "the most powerful" or "the greatest," indicating a peak of strength or capability.
  • divi - Translates to "in the heavens" or "in the sky," suggesting a divine or celestial context.
  • pra-pret - This means "to the departed" or "to the ancestors," indicating a connection to those who have passed away.
  • te - This translates to "to you," indicating a direction or offering towards a deity or divine entity.
  • agne - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • vanuṣaḥ - This means "of the forest" or "of nature," connecting the divine to the natural world.
  • syāma - This translates to "may we be" or "let us be," indicating a desire or aspiration.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that seeks the blessings of the divine, particularly Agni, the fire god, while acknowledging the connection between the mortal and the celestial. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and the nurturing aspects of nature, represented by the moon and the forest.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the wise sage (vipra) seeks knowledge, individuals can strive for wisdom in their own lives, learning from experiences and the world around them.
  • Connection to Nature: The reference to the forest (vanuṣaḥ) encourages people to appreciate and protect the natural environment, recognizing its importance in our lives.
  • Honoring Ancestors: The mention of the departed (pra-pret) serves as a reminder to honor and remember those who came before us, fostering a sense of gratitude and continuity.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of Agni (agne) can be relevant in rituals and practices that seek purification and transformation, whether in personal meditation or communal ceremonies.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of wisdom, nature, and the divine. It serves as a reminder of our mortal existence while encouraging us to seek greatness and honor our roots. By applying its teachings in our daily lives, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and our place in the universe.
