यज्ञो हीळो वो अन्तर आदित्य अस्ति मृळत |युष्मे इद वो अपि ष्मसि सजात्ये ||
yajño hīḻo vo antara ādityā asti mṛḻata | yuṣme id vo api ṣmasi sajātye ||
  • Mantra: "yajño hīḻo vo antara ādityā asti mṛḻata | yuṣme id vo api ṣmasi sajātye ||"
  • Translation: "Indeed, the sacrifice (yajña) is the essence of the divine (Adityas) that resides within you; may you be united in harmony and purpose."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Yajña: Refers to a ritual of offerings, often associated with fire, symbolizing the act of giving and selflessness.
    • Hīḻo: Represents the essence or core, indicating the fundamental nature of something.
    • Antara: Means 'within' or 'inside', suggesting an internal connection.
    • Ādityā: Refers to the divine or celestial beings, often associated with the sun and light.
    • Asti: Means 'is' or 'exists', affirming the presence of something.
    • Mṛḻata: Implies 'to be united' or 'to come together', emphasizing harmony.
    • Yuṣme: Means 'in you' or 'within you', indicating a personal connection.
    • Id: Means 'this', referring to the current context or situation.
    • Api: Means 'also' or 'too', suggesting inclusivity.
    • Ṣmasi: Means 'to be united' or 'to come together', reinforcing the idea of unity.
    • Sajātye: Refers to 'in harmony' or 'in the same kind', indicating a shared purpose or goal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together for a common cause, such as a charity event, they embody the essence of yajña by contributing their time and resources for the greater good.
    • In a workplace, teamwork and collaboration reflect the idea of being united in purpose, where each member contributes their unique skills towards a shared goal.
    • In personal relationships, the act of giving and supporting one another during difficult times exemplifies the essence of sacrifice and unity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifice and unity in achieving harmony and purpose in life. It teaches us that true fulfillment comes from selfless acts and working together towards common goals. In real-life scenarios, whether in communities, workplaces, or personal relationships, embodying the principles of yajña can lead to stronger bonds and a more harmonious existence. By recognizing the divine essence within ourselves and others, we can foster a sense of belonging and collective purpose, ultimately contributing to a more compassionate and unified world.
