मन्त्र: सुनोतā सोमपाव्ने सोमं इन्द्राय वज्रीणि | पचतā पक्तीर अवसे कृत्वं इति पृणन्न इति पृणते मयः ||
sunotā somapāvne somam indrāya vajriṇe | pacatā paktīr avase kṛṇudhvam it pṛṇann it pṛṇate mayaḥ ||
  • sunotā: This word can be translated as "he who listens" or "the one who hears." It signifies the importance of being receptive and attentive to divine guidance.
  • somapāvne: This term refers to "the one who purifies with Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • somam: This means "the Soma," which represents the essence of life and vitality, often associated with the moon and its calming influence.
  • indrāya: This word means "to Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • vajriṇe: This translates to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," referring to Indra's power and ability to overcome obstacles.
  • pacatā: This means "to cook" or "to prepare," symbolizing the process of transformation and creation.
  • paktīr: This refers to "the offerings" or "the prepared food," which signifies the fruits of one's labor and devotion.
  • avase: This means "to bring forth" or "to manifest," indicating the act of creation and realization of potential.
  • kṛṇudhvam: This translates to "you should make" or "you should create," emphasizing the active role of individuals in shaping their destinies.
  • it: This word means "this," pointing to the immediate context of the mantra.
  • pṛṇann: This means "to fill" or "to satisfy," indicating the fulfillment of desires and needs.
  • it: Again, this word means "this," reinforcing the focus on the present moment.
  • pṛṇate: This translates to "to nourish" or "to sustain," highlighting the importance of nurturing oneself and others.
  • mayaḥ: This means "by me" or "through me," indicating personal agency and responsibility in the process of creation and nourishment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of listening, purification, and active participation in the process of creation and nourishment. It highlights the relationship between the individual and the divine, urging one to be receptive to spiritual guidance while also taking responsibility for their actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, where one must listen to their inner voice and seek spiritual nourishment through practices like meditation or mindfulness. It also underscores the importance of community and sharing resources, as one prepares offerings not just for oneself but for the greater good. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that guide us in our journey of life.
