यो नो रसं दिप्सति पित्वो अग्ने यो अश्वानां यो गवां यस् तनूनाम् | रिपुः स्तेनः स्तेनकृत् दभ्रम एतु नि ष हीयतां तन्वा तना च ||
yo no rasaṁ dipsati pitvo agne yo aśvānāṁ yo gavāṁ yas tanūnām | ripuḥ stenaḥ steyakṛd dabhram etu ni ṣa hīyatāṁ tanvā3 tanā ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • rasaṁ: essence or juice
    • dipsati: desires or longs for
    • pitvo: having drunk
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • yo: who
    • aśvānāṁ: of horses
    • yo: who
    • gavāṁ: of cows
    • yas: who
    • tanūnām: of bodies
    • ripuḥ: enemy
    • stenah: thief
    • stekyakṛt: the one who commits theft
    • dabhram: let it be removed
    • etu: may it come
    • ni: not
    • ṣa: six
    • hīyatāṁ: may it be destroyed
    • tanvā: body
    • tanā: body
    • ca: and
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The essence of life, represented by "rasaṁ," is something that people long for, symbolizing the pursuit of knowledge, nourishment, and spiritual fulfillment.
    • The mention of horses and cows signifies wealth and prosperity, as these animals were vital for sustenance and status in ancient times.
    • The mantra also addresses the removal of enemies and thieves, indicating a desire for protection and security in one's life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and the need to eliminate negative influences that can harm one's body and spirit.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the pursuit of knowledge and personal growth can be seen in educational endeavors, where individuals seek to enrich their lives and contribute positively to society.
    • The importance of community and family can be likened to the significance of livestock in ancient times, where people today strive for financial stability and emotional support from loved ones.
    • In a corporate environment, the mantra's call for the removal of enemies can be interpreted as a desire for ethical practices and a healthy work culture, free from deceit and malice.
    • Personal wellness practices, such as meditation and yoga, reflect the mantra's emphasis on maintaining a pure body and mind, promoting overall well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking knowledge, and the need for protection against negativity.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, encouraging individuals to strive for personal and communal growth while safeguarding their well-being.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal development to community building, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide individuals toward a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
