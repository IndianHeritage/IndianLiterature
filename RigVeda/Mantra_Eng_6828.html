स क्षपः परि षस्वजे न्य उ1स्रो मायया दधे स विश्वम् परि दर्शतः |तस्य वेनीर अनु व्रतम उषस तिस्रो अवर्धयन नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
sa kṣapaḥ pari ṣasvaje ny u1sro māyayā dadhe sa viśvam pari darśataḥ | tasya venīr anu vratam uṣas tisro avardhayan nabhantām anyake same ||
  • sa kṣapaḥ pari ṣasvaje: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is the master of the night" or "the one who encompasses the darkness." In real life, this can symbolize the challenges and uncertainties we face, akin to the night that brings fear and doubt.
  • ny u1sro māyayā dadhe: This translates to "he who brings forth the dawn through illusion." Here, "dawn" represents new beginnings and hope, while "illusion" suggests that our perceptions can sometimes cloud our understanding of reality. For example, in personal growth, we often face illusions about our capabilities, but overcoming them leads to enlightenment.
  • sa viśvam pari darśataḥ: This means "he who sees everything around." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and perception in our lives. In a practical sense, being observant and aware of our surroundings can lead to better decision-making and understanding of the world.
  • tasya venīr anu vratam: This can be interpreted as "following the path of his guidance." It suggests the importance of following a righteous path or guidance in life. For instance, mentors and leaders in our lives can provide direction and wisdom that help us navigate challenges.
  • uṣas tisro avardhayan: This translates to "the three dawns that arise." The number three can symbolize various aspects of life, such as past, present, and future, or mind, body, and spirit. In real life, this can remind us to balance different aspects of our existence for holistic well-being.
  • nabhantām anyake same: This phrase means "the heavens and the earth are in harmony." It signifies the interconnectedness of all things. In practical terms, this can be seen in environmental awareness, where understanding our connection to nature leads to sustainable living practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey from darkness to light, emphasizing awareness, guidance, and harmony. Its significance lies in the reminder that despite the challenges we face (the night), there is always the potential for new beginnings (the dawn). In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and environmental consciousness, encouraging us to seek balance and understanding in our lives.
