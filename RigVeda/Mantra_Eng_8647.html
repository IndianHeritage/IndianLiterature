तं दुरोषम् अभि नरः सोमं विश्वाच्यां धियाः |यज्ञं हिन्वन्त्य अद्रिभिः ||
taṁ duroṣam abhī naraḥ somaṁ viśvācyā dhiyā | yajñaṁ hinvanty adribhiḥ ||
  • taṁ: This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • duroṣam: This means "difficult to approach" or "hard to attain," indicating the challenges in reaching spiritual or divine understanding.
  • abhī: This translates to "with reverence" or "with devotion," suggesting the attitude one should have when seeking the divine.
  • naraḥ: This means "man" or "human," emphasizing the human condition and the quest for higher knowledge.
  • somaṁ: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • viśvācyā: This translates to "all-encompassing" or "universal," indicating the broad reach of divine wisdom.
  • dhiya: This means "intellect" or "understanding," highlighting the importance of mental clarity in spiritual pursuits.
  • yajñaṁ: Refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," a key concept in Vedic culture that signifies the act of giving and devotion.
  • hinvanty: This means "they bring forth" or "they carry," indicating the active role of individuals in manifesting their spiritual goals.
  • adribhiḥ: This translates to "by the mountains," symbolizing strength, stability, and the challenges one faces in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for humans to approach the divine with reverence, acknowledging the challenges in attaining spiritual wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of intellect and understanding in this pursuit, while also highlighting the role of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine. The reference to "Soma" suggests that through devotion and the right practices, one can attain a state of enlightenment or immortality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their challenges and approach their goals with reverence and dedication.
  • In community service, the concept of sacrifice can inspire people to contribute selflessly for the greater good.
  • In education, the emphasis on intellect encourages a pursuit of knowledge and understanding in all aspects of life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of devotion, intellect, and the willingness to face challenges in the pursuit of spiritual and personal growth. It encourages individuals to engage in rituals and sacrifices that foster a deeper connection with the divine and the universe.
