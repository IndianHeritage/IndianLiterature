कृष्णं त एमा रुशतः पुरो भाष चरिष्ण्व आर्चिर वपुषामिद एकम् |यद अप्रविता दधते ह गर्भं सद्यश्चिज्जातो भवसीदु दूतः ||
kṛṣṇaṁ ta ema ruśataḥ puro bhāś cariṣṇv a1rcir vapuṣām id ekam | yad apravītā dadhate ha garbhaṁ sadyaś cij jāto bhavasīd u dūtaḥ ||
  • kṛṣṇaṁ ta ema ruśataḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically to Lord Krishna, who represents the ultimate truth and consciousness. In real life, this can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers or wisdom in times of confusion or distress.
  • puro bhāś cariṣṇv a1rcir vapuṣām id ekam: This part emphasizes the unity of all beings and the importance of light (knowledge) in dispelling darkness (ignorance). In practical terms, it suggests that collaboration and understanding among people can lead to enlightenment and progress. For example, in a workplace, teamwork and open communication can lead to innovative solutions.
  • yad apravītā dadhate ha garbhaṁ: This line refers to the nurturing aspect of creation, where potential (garbha) is held within. It signifies the importance of nurturing ideas and relationships. In everyday life, this can be related to parenting, where a child’s potential is nurtured through love and education.
  • sadyaś cij jāto bhavasīd u dūtaḥ: This concluding phrase speaks to the immediacy of existence and the role of messengers (dūtaḥ) in conveying important truths. In a modern context, this can be likened to the role of educators or mentors who guide individuals towards their purpose and help them realize their potential.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, the importance of unity and collaboration, nurturing potential, and the role of mentors in our lives. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, such as personal growth, community building, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a more harmonious and enlightened society.
