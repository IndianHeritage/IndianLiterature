भूरि चक्र मरुतः पितृयाण्युक्तानि या वः शस्यन्ते पुरा चित् |मरुद्भिर उग्रः पृतनासु साळ्हा मरुद्भिर इति सानितā वाजम् अर्वा ||
bhūri cakra marutaḥ pitryāṇy ukthāni yā vaḥ śasyante purā cit | marudbhir ugraḥ pṛtanāsu sāḻhā marudbhir it sanitā vājam arvā ||
  • bhūri cakra marutaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the abundant circle of the Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. The term "bhūri" signifies abundance or plenty, while "cakra" refers to a circle or cycle, indicating a continuous flow of energy and vitality.
  • pitryāṇy ukthāni: This translates to "the hymns dedicated to the ancestors." In many cultures, including Vedic traditions, honoring ancestors is crucial. It reflects respect for lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations. This can be seen in modern practices such as family gatherings during festivals where stories of ancestors are shared.
  • yā vaḥ śasyante purā cit: This means "those that were sung in the past." It emphasizes the importance of tradition and the oral transmission of knowledge. In real life, this can be likened to how stories, values, and teachings are passed down through families and communities, shaping identities and moral frameworks.
  • marudbhir ugraḥ pṛtanāsu sāḻhā: This can be interpreted as "the fierce ones among the Maruts in the battles." Here, "ugraḥ" signifies fierceness or intensity, while "pṛtanāsu" refers to battles or struggles. This reflects the idea that challenges are a part of life, and facing them with strength is essential. In contemporary life, this can be seen in how individuals confront personal or professional challenges, drawing on inner strength and resilience.
  • marudbhir it sanitā vājam arvā: This translates to "the Maruts who bring forth the strength of the sacrifice." The term "vājam" refers to strength or vigor, and "arvā" can imply abundance or prosperity. This highlights the connection between effort (sacrifice) and reward (strength and prosperity). In modern contexts, this can be related to the idea that hard work and dedication lead to success, whether in careers, education, or personal goals.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of abundance, respect for ancestors, the importance of tradition, the necessity of facing challenges, and the relationship between effort and reward. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as family gatherings that honor heritage, personal struggles that require resilience, and the pursuit of goals that demand hard work. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to embrace our roots, face life's challenges with courage, and recognize that our efforts can lead to prosperity and strength.
