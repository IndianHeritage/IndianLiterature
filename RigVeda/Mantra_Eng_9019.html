सो चिन् नु वृष्टिर यूथ्याः स्वा सच्चाम् इन्द्रः शमश्रूणि हरिताभि प्रुष्णुते |अव वेति सुख्षयं सुते मधूद इद धूनोति वातो यथा वनम् ||
so cin nu vṛṣṭir yūthyā3 svā sacām̐ indraḥ śmaśrūṇi haritābhi pruṣṇute | ava veti sukṣayaṁ sute madhūd id dhūnoti vāto yathā vanam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: that
    • cin: may he (Indra) know
    • nu: indeed
    • vṛṣṭir: rain
    • yūthyā: in a group
    • svā: our
    • sacām̐: with wealth
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • śmaśrūṇi: with a beard
    • haritābhi: with green (or flourishing)
    • pruṣṇute: may he bestow
    • ava: down
    • veti: he brings
    • sukṣayaṁ: prosperity
    • sute: in the offering
    • madhūd: sweet
    • id: this
    • dhūnoti: he stirs up
    • vāto: the wind
    • yathā: as
    • vanam: the forest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, asking him to bestow prosperity and abundance.
    • The phrase "may he know" suggests a plea for awareness and attention from the divine, emphasizing the importance of connection between humans and the divine.
    • The mention of "rain in a group" signifies the collective nature of blessings, indicating that prosperity is often shared within a community.
    • The reference to "sweet offerings" highlights the significance of gratitude and the act of giving back to the divine for the blessings received.
    • The imagery of the wind stirring up the forest symbolizes the dynamic and transformative power of nature, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during dry seasons, reflecting the reliance on divine blessings for a good harvest.
    • Communities come together to celebrate festivals, sharing resources and offerings, which mirrors the collective aspect of the mantra.
    • Environmental initiatives that focus on preserving forests and nature resonate with the idea of nurturing the earth, akin to the blessings invoked in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity, nature, and the divine, highlighting the importance of community and gratitude.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition that prosperity is not just an individual pursuit but a collective journey that requires harmony with nature and the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to seek blessings through unity, gratitude, and respect for the environment, fostering a sustainable and prosperous future.
