विष्वे देवाः सह धीभिः पुरन्ध्या मनो यजत्रा अमृताः ऋतज्ञाः | रातिषाचो अभिषाचः स्वरविदः स्वर्गिरो ब्रह्म सूक्तं जुषेरत ||
viśve devāḥ saha dhībhiḥ puraṁdhyā manor yajatrā amṛtā ṛtajñāḥ | rātiṣāco abhiṣācaḥ svarvidaḥ sva1r giro brahma sūktaṁ juṣerata ||
  • viśve devāḥ: This phrase translates to "all the gods" or "universal deities." It signifies the collective presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • saha dhībhiḥ: This means "together with wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective intelligence among the divine beings.
  • puraṁdhyā: This term refers to "the creator" or "the architect." It highlights the role of a supreme being in the creation and maintenance of the universe.
  • manor yajatrā: This translates to "the mind is the altar of sacrifice." It suggests that our thoughts and intentions are crucial in the process of worship and offerings to the divine.
  • amṛtā: This word means "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting nature of the divine and the ultimate goal of human existence.
  • ṛtajñāḥ: This translates to "those who know the truth." It refers to the enlightened beings who understand the laws of the universe and live in accordance with them.
  • rātiṣāco: This term can be interpreted as "those who bring joy." It emphasizes the positive and uplifting nature of divine blessings.
  • abhiṣācaḥ: This means "to bestow blessings." It signifies the act of the divine in showering grace and favor upon humanity.
  • svarvidaḥ: This translates to "those who know the sound" or "the essence of sound." It highlights the importance of sound and vibration in the universe, particularly in spiritual practices.
  • svar giro: This means "the voice of the divine." It signifies the communication and guidance that comes from the higher realms.
  • brahma sūktaṁ: This refers to "the hymn of the absolute." It emphasizes the ultimate reality and the essence of existence.
  • juṣerata: This means "may they enjoy" or "may they be pleased." It expresses a desire for the divine to be satisfied with our offerings and intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, wisdom, and the divine presence in our lives. It teaches us that our thoughts and intentions are powerful tools in connecting with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as during meditation, rituals, or daily affirmations. By focusing on collective wisdom and the eternal nature of the divine, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their purpose and foster a sense of community. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek truth, joy, and divine connection in their lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
