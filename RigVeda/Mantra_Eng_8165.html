तं ई मृजन्त्य आयवो हरिं नदिषु वाजिनम् |इन्दुम इन्द्राय मत्सरम् ||
tam ī mṛjanty āyavo hariṁ nadīṣu vājinam | indum indrāya matsaram ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • ī - This is a prefix that can denote action or movement, often implying a sense of direction or purpose.
  • mṛjanty - This means "they cleanse" or "they purify," indicating a process of purification or renewal.
  • āyavo - This term can be interpreted as "long life" or "vitality," suggesting a connection to health and longevity.
  • hariṁ - This refers to "Hari," a name often associated with the divine, particularly in the context of Vishnu or Krishna.
  • nadīṣu - This means "in the rivers," indicating a natural setting, often associated with life-giving properties.
  • vājinam - This translates to "the one who is swift" or "the horse," symbolizing strength, speed, and vitality.
  • indum - This word means "the moon," which is often associated with calmness, beauty, and the passage of time.
  • indrāya - This refers to "to Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
  • matsaram - This translates to "jealousy" or "envy," indicating a negative emotion that can hinder growth and harmony.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for purification and vitality, invoking divine forces to cleanse and renew life. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming negative emotions like jealousy, which can obstruct one's path to success and fulfillment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as rivers cleanse and nourish the land, individuals can seek to purify their thoughts and emotions to foster personal growth. For example, practicing mindfulness and meditation can help in overcoming feelings of jealousy and resentment.
  • Community Harmony: In a community setting, this mantra can serve as a reminder to work together harmoniously, setting aside envy and competition. Initiatives that promote collaboration and support can lead to a more vibrant and thriving community.
  • Health and Well-being: The reference to "long life" and "vitality" can inspire individuals to adopt healthier lifestyles, focusing on physical and mental well-being through balanced diets, exercise, and positive relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of purification, vitality, and overcoming negative emotions. By embracing these principles, individuals and communities can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, aligning themselves with divine energies that promote growth and well-being.
