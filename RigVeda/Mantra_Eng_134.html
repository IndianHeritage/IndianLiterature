स्वाहा यज्ञं कृतोतेन्द्राय यजनो गृहे |तत्र देवां उप ह्वये ||
svāhā yajñaṁ kṛṇotanendrāya yajvano gṛhe | tatra devām̐ upa hvaye ||
  • svāhā: This term is often used in Vedic rituals and signifies an offering or a salutation to the divine. It represents the act of surrendering oneself and one's actions to a higher power.
  • yajñaṁ: This word translates to "sacrifice" or "offering." In the context of the RigVeda, it refers to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities, which are believed to maintain cosmic order and harmony.
  • kṛṇotan: This means "to perform" or "to make." It indicates the action of carrying out the yajña or sacrifice with intention and devotion.
  • endrāya: This term refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature. The invocation of Indra signifies the desire for his blessings and protection.
  • yajvano: This word means "the one who performs the sacrifice." It emphasizes the role of the individual or community engaging in the ritual, highlighting the importance of intention behind the actions.
  • gṛhe: This translates to "in the home" or "in the household." It signifies the domestic sphere where rituals and offerings are made, emphasizing the importance of spirituality in everyday life.
  • tatra: This means "there" or "in that place," indicating the location where the offerings are made.
  • devām̐: This term refers to "the deities" or "gods." It acknowledges the divine presence and the need for their blessings in the rituals performed.
  • upa hvaye: This phrase translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon." It expresses the act of inviting the divine presence into the ritual space, seeking their attention and blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra "svāhā yajñaṁ kṛṇotanendrāya yajvano gṛhe | tatra devām̐ upa hvaye ||" encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of intention, devotion, and the invocation of divine blessings in both personal and communal practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during family gatherings, festivals, or any significant event where individuals seek harmony, prosperity, and divine guidance. By performing rituals with sincerity and inviting the divine into our homes, we create a sacred space that fosters spiritual growth and strengthens our connection to the universe. This practice not only enriches our lives but also promotes a sense of community and shared purpose, reflecting the timeless wisdom of the RigVeda in contemporary life.
