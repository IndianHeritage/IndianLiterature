तुविग्रीवो वृषभो वावृद्धानो 'शत्र्व आर्यः सम अजाति वेदः |इतीमम् अग्निम अमृताऽवोचन बरहिष्मते मनवे शर्मा यं सद धविष्मते मनवे शर्मा यं सत् ||
tuvigrīvo vṛṣabho vāvṛdhāno 'śatrv a1ryaḥ sam ajāti vedaḥ | itīmam agnim amṛtā avocan barhiṣmate manave śarma yaṁsad dhaviṣmate manave śarma yaṁsat ||
  • tuvigrīvo: This term can be interpreted as "one who is strong and powerful." In real life, this can refer to individuals or entities that possess great strength or influence, such as leaders or organizations that drive change.
  • vṛṣabho: Meaning "the bull," this symbolizes strength, fertility, and productivity. In agriculture, a strong bull is essential for plowing fields, representing hard work and the foundation of sustenance.
  • vāvṛdhāno: This word suggests "one who increases or enhances." In a modern context, this could relate to innovation and growth in businesses or personal development, where individuals strive to improve their skills and knowledge.
  • 'śatrv a1ryaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the noble one who overcomes enemies." It signifies resilience and the ability to face challenges, akin to individuals who confront adversities in their lives, such as overcoming personal struggles or societal issues.
  • sam ajāti vedaḥ: This translates to "the knowledge that is universally accepted." In today's world, this can refer to shared values and truths that unite people across cultures, such as the importance of compassion and understanding.
  • itīmam agnim: "This fire" symbolizes energy, passion, and transformation. In real life, it can represent the drive that fuels individuals to pursue their goals, akin to the passion of an artist or entrepreneur.
  • amṛtā avocan: This means "they spoke of immortality." It reflects the human quest for lasting impact and legacy, as seen in the works of great thinkers, artists, and leaders who strive to leave a mark on the world.
  • barhiṣmate: This term refers to "the one who is worthy of offerings." In a practical sense, it can relate to the respect and honor given to those who contribute positively to society, such as community leaders or philanthropists.
  • manave śarma: This translates to "the comfort of the mind." It emphasizes the importance of mental peace and well-being, which is crucial in today's fast-paced world where stress and anxiety are prevalent.
  • yaṁsad dhaviṣmate: This phrase means "who brings happiness." It highlights the role of individuals or actions that promote joy and positivity in life, such as acts of kindness or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, resilience, knowledge, and the pursuit of a meaningful life. Its significance lies in the encouragement to embody these qualities in our daily lives, whether through personal growth, community involvement, or the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to strive for excellence, support one another, and seek a deeper understanding of our shared humanity. By embracing these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
