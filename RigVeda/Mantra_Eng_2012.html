त्वं अग्नि रुद्रः असुरो महो दिवस त्वं शर्धो मारुतं पृक्ष ईशिषे |त्वं वातैः अरुणैः यासि शङ्गयस् त्वं पूषा विधतः पासि नु तमनः ||
tvam agne rudro asuro maho divas tvaṁ śardho mārutam pṛkṣa īśiṣe | tvaṁ vātair aruṇair yāsi śaṁgayas tvam pūṣā vidhataḥ pāsi nu tmanā ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a symbol of energy and transformation. In real life, fire represents both physical and metaphorical energy, such as passion and creativity.
  • rudro asuro - "Rudra, the divine" - Rudra is often associated with storms and the wildness of nature. This can be likened to the unpredictable aspects of life, such as challenges and adversities that one faces.
  • maho divas - "Greatness of the heavens" - This phrase emphasizes the vastness and majesty of the universe. In our lives, it can remind us of our place in the cosmos and the importance of humility.
  • tvaṁ śardho - "You, in the autumn" - Autumn symbolizes change and the cycle of life. It can be related to personal growth and the acceptance of change in our lives.
  • mārutam pṛkṣa - "The winds that blow" - Winds can represent change and movement. In real life, this can be seen in how we adapt to new circumstances and the importance of resilience.
  • īśiṣe - "You who rules" - This signifies authority and control. In our lives, it can be interpreted as taking charge of our own destiny and making conscious choices.
  • tvaṁ vātair aruṇair - "You who travel with the red winds" - This can symbolize the journey of life and the experiences that shape us. The 'red winds' may represent passion and vitality.
  • yāsi śaṁgayas - "You who brings together" - This highlights the importance of unity and collaboration in our lives, whether in personal relationships or community efforts.
  • tvaṁ pūṣā - "You, O Pusha" - Pusha is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. This can be related to the importance of nurturing ourselves and others in our lives.
  • vidhataḥ pāsi nu tmanā - "You who governs, protect us with your essence" - This is a plea for guidance and protection. In real life, it can signify the need for support and wisdom in our decisions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine energy, transformation, and the interconnectedness of life. It serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience, unity, and the guidance we seek in our journey. In real life, we can apply these teachings by embracing change, nurturing relationships, and taking charge of our destinies, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
