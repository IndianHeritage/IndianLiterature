नकीः सुदासो रथम पर्य आसा न रीरामत् |इन्द्रो यस्याविताऽ यस्य मरुतो गमत् स गोमती व्रजे ||
nakiḥ sudāso ratham pary āsa na rīramat | indro yasyāvitā yasya maruto gamat sa gomati vraje ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakiḥ: Not (or no one)
    • sudāso: Of the good (or noble) servants
    • ratham: Chariot
    • pary āsa: Has surrounded (or encompassed)
    • na: Not
    • rīramat: To be disturbed (or troubled)
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • yasyāvitā: Whose protection (or care)
    • yasy: Whose
    • maruto: The Maruts (storm deities)
    • gamat: Go (or move)
    • sa: He (or that)
    • gomati: Wealthy (or prosperous)
    • vraje: In the pasture (or field)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the protection and support provided by divine forces, particularly Indra and the Maruts, to those who are noble and virtuous.
    • The phrase "nakiḥ sudāso ratham pary āsa" suggests that no one can disturb the chariot of those who are under the protection of these divine beings.
    • It emphasizes the idea that when one is aligned with righteousness and virtue, they are shielded from negativity and disturbances.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who consistently demonstrates integrity and hard work may find that they are supported by their colleagues and superiors, creating a protective environment around them.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who practice honesty and kindness often find that they are surrounded by supportive friends and family, making it difficult for negativity to penetrate their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of living a virtuous life and the protective power that comes from aligning oneself with divine principles.
    • It teaches that righteousness attracts support and shields one from harm, applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and spiritual journeys.
    • By embodying the qualities of goodness and integrity, individuals can create a safe and prosperous space for themselves, much like the "gomati vraje" that flourishes in the fields under divine protection.
