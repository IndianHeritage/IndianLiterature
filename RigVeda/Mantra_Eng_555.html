युवोर उषा अनु श्रीयम् परिज्मनोः उपाचरत् |ऋता वनथो अक्तुभिः ||
yuvor uṣā anu śriyam parijmanor upācarat | ṛtā vanatho aktubhiḥ ||
  • yuvor: This word translates to "of the young" or "of the youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential that comes with youth.
  • uṣā: This means "dawn" or "the first light of day." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • anu: This word means "following" or "after." It indicates a sequence or a progression, suggesting that something comes after another.
  • śriyam: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It represents not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • parijmanor: This term can be understood as "of the two young ones" or "of the youthful couple." It emphasizes relationships and partnerships.
  • upācarat: This means "to approach" or "to serve." It indicates an action of coming closer or offering something.
  • ṛtā: This word signifies "truth" or "order." It represents the natural law and the cosmic order that governs the universe.
  • vanatho: This translates to "of the forest" or "of nature." It connects the mantra to the natural world and its significance.
  • aktubhiḥ: This term refers to "with the powers" or "with the energies." It suggests the involvement of divine or natural forces in the process.

The mantra can be interpreted as a celebration of youth and the dawn, symbolizing the arrival of new opportunities and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of relationships and the support that comes from nature and the universe. The invocation of truth and order suggests that these elements work together harmoniously to create a fulfilling life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as dawn signifies a new day, individuals can embrace new beginnings in their personal and professional lives, seeking prosperity and growth.
  • Relationships: The emphasis on youthful couples highlights the importance of nurturing relationships, whether in friendships, family, or romantic partnerships.
  • Environmental Awareness: The connection to nature encourages individuals to respect and care for the environment, recognizing its role in our well-being.
  • Truth and Integrity: Upholding truth and order in our actions can lead to a more harmonious life, aligning with the natural laws of the universe.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of youth, nature, relationships, and truth. It encourages individuals to embrace new beginnings, foster meaningful connections, and live in harmony with the world around them. By applying these principles, one can cultivate a life filled with prosperity and fulfillment.
