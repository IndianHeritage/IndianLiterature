ऋष्यो न तृष्यन्न अवपानम् आगहि पिबा सोमं वशाम् अनु |निमेघमानो मगवन् दिवे-दिव ओजिष्ठं दधिषे सहः ||
ṛśyo na tṛṣyann avapānam ā gahi pibā somaṁ vaśām̐ anu | nimeghamāno maghavan dive-diva ojiṣṭhaṁ dadhiṣe sahaḥ ||
  • ṛśyo: This word refers to the sages or seers, who are often depicted as wise and enlightened beings in Vedic literature.
  • na tṛṣyann: This phrase means "not thirsty," indicating a state of fulfillment or satisfaction.
  • avapānam: This term refers to the act of drinking or consuming, particularly in the context of sacred substances.
  • ā gahi: This means "take" or "hold," suggesting an invitation to partake in something valuable.
  • pibā: This is a command form meaning "drink," emphasizing the act of consumption.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • vaśām̐ anu: This phrase translates to "following the will," indicating alignment with divine or cosmic order.
  • nimeghamāno: This term can be interpreted as "one who brings rain," symbolizing abundance and nourishment.
  • maghavan: Referring to the deity of wealth and abundance, often associated with prosperity and blessings.
  • dive-diva: This phrase means "day by day," suggesting a continuous or daily practice.
  • ojiṣṭhaṁ: This word means "the strongest" or "the most powerful," indicating a source of great strength.
  • dadhiṣe: This term means "you bestow," indicating a giving or sharing of blessings.
  • sahaḥ: This means "together" or "with," suggesting unity and collaboration.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the sages to partake in the sacred Soma, a drink that symbolizes spiritual nourishment and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of being fulfilled and aligned with divine will, while also invoking the blessings of abundance and strength from the deities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or rituals to seek guidance and fulfillment in their spiritual journey.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, this mantra can be recited to foster unity and shared purpose, encouraging participants to work together towards common goals.
  • Personal Growth: The themes of abundance and strength can inspire individuals to pursue their aspirations with confidence and resilience.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for spiritual nourishment, alignment with divine will, and the pursuit of abundance. It serves as a reminder to seek fulfillment not just in material wealth but in spiritual and communal connections, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
