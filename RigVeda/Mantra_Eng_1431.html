आदद धव्याण्य आददिर यज्ञस्य केतुर्अर्हणा |अध स्मास्य हर्षतो हृषीवतो विश्वे जुषन्त पन्थां नरः शुभे न पन्थाम् ||
ādad dhavyāny ādadir yajñasya ketur arhaṇā | adha smāsya harṣato hṛṣīvato viśve juṣanta panthāṁ naraḥ śubhe na panthām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādad: to take or to accept
    • dhavyāny: offerings or sacrifices
    • ādadir: the beginning or origin
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice
    • ketur: the banner or flag
    • arhaṇā: worthy or deserving
    • adha: below or beneath
    • smāsya: of the dead or the departed
    • harṣato: of the joyful or happy
    • hṛṣīvato: of the joyful or delighted
    • viśve: all or universal
    • juṣanta: enjoying or cherishing
    • panthāṁ: path or way
    • naraḥ: man or human
    • śubhe: auspicious or good
    • na: not
    • panthām: path or way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the acceptance of offerings in a sacrificial context, emphasizing the importance of beginning a ritual with the right intentions.
    • The "banner of the sacrifice" symbolizes the guiding principles and values that should lead one's actions.
    • The reference to the departed and the joyful suggests a connection between the living and the ancestors, highlighting the importance of honoring those who have passed.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to cherish the paths they choose, ensuring they are auspicious and beneficial.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach significant events, such as weddings or festivals, where rituals are performed with respect and intention.
    • In a corporate setting, the idea of starting a project with a clear vision and values can be seen as a contemporary interpretation of this mantra, ensuring that the team remains aligned with its goals.
    • In personal development, the mantra serves as a reminder to honor our past experiences while striving for joy and fulfillment in our current paths.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for intentionality in our actions and the importance of honoring our connections to the past.
    • It teaches us that the paths we choose should be guided by values that are worthy and beneficial, both for ourselves and for the community.
    • In essence, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where joy, respect, and auspiciousness are integral to our journey.
