प्रियमेधवाद अत्रीवज जातवेदो विरूपवत् |अङ्गिरस्वन महिव्रत प्रस्कण्वस्य श्रुधी हवम् ||
priyamedhavad atrivaj jātavedo virūpavat | aṅgirasvan mahivrata praskaṇvasya śrudhī havam ||
  • Priyamedhavad: This term can be broken down into two parts: "Priya" meaning dear or beloved, and "Medhavad" which refers to one who is wise or intelligent. Together, it suggests a person who is beloved for their wisdom.
  • Atrivaj: This term can be interpreted as "one who is worthy of praise" or "one who is revered." It signifies a person or entity that commands respect and admiration.
  • Jātavedo: This word refers to the "one who is born of knowledge" or "the one who knows all." It is often associated with the divine or the ultimate source of wisdom and knowledge.
  • Virūpavat: This term means "having many forms" or "multifaceted." It indicates the complexity and diversity of existence, suggesting that wisdom can manifest in various ways.
  • Aṅgirasvan: This refers to the "one who is associated with the Aṅgiras," a group of sages known for their profound knowledge and spiritual insight. It emphasizes the connection to ancient wisdom.
  • Mahivrata: This term means "great vow" or "sacred commitment." It signifies a deep dedication to a cause or principle, often associated with spiritual or moral pursuits.
  • Praskaṇvasya: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is pure" or "the one who is free from impurities." It highlights the importance of purity in thought and action.
  • Śrudhī: This term means "to hear" or "to listen." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • Havam: This refers to the act of offering or invocation, often in the context of rituals. It signifies the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, reverence, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of being beloved for one's intelligence, the multifaceted nature of wisdom, and the commitment to purity and dedication in one's pursuits. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings where the value of wisdom and knowledge is celebrated, or in personal development where individuals strive to embody these qualities. It serves as a reminder to honor and seek wisdom in all aspects of life, fostering a deeper understanding of oneself and the world around us.
