त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम् |उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान् मृत्योः मुक्षीय मामृतात् ||
tryambakaṁ yajāmahe sugandhim puṣṭivardhanam | urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya māmṛtāt ||
  • Mantra: "tryambakaṁ yajāmahe sugandhim puṣṭivardhanam | urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya māmṛtāt ||"
  • Translation: "We meditate upon the three-eyed one (Lord Shiva), who permeates and nourishes all like a fragrance. May he liberate us from the bondage of death, just as a cucumber is severed from its vine."

  • Tryambakaṁ: Refers to the "three-eyed one," symbolizing Lord Shiva. The three eyes represent the sun, moon, and fire, signifying the ability to see beyond the physical realm.
  • Yajāmahe: Means "we meditate" or "we worship." This indicates a conscious effort to connect with the divine.
  • Sugandhim: Translates to "fragrant" or "nourishing." This suggests that the divine presence is beneficial and uplifting, much like a pleasant aroma that enhances our surroundings.
  • Puṣṭivardhanam: Means "nourisher" or "one who promotes growth." This highlights the nurturing aspect of the divine, encouraging prosperity and well-being.
  • Urvarukam: Refers to a "cucumber," which is often used as a metaphor for something that is bound but can be easily released.
  • Bandhanān: Means "from bondage." This signifies the constraints and limitations we face in life, particularly the fear of death.
  • Mṛtyor: Translates to "of death." This emphasizes the inevitability of mortality and the desire to transcend it.
  • Mukṣīya: Means "may we be liberated." This is a plea for freedom from the cycle of life and death.
  • Māmṛtāt: Translates to "from immortality." This suggests a longing for eternal life or spiritual liberation.

  • Real-Life Example: In times of grief or loss, individuals often seek solace in spirituality. This mantra can be recited to find comfort and to remind oneself of the transient nature of life. For instance, during a funeral, family members may chant this mantra to honor the deceased and to seek liberation for their soul.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in overcoming life's challenges, particularly the fear of death. It serves as a reminder that while physical existence is temporary, spiritual liberation is attainable through devotion and meditation.
  • Application: This mantra can be applied in various life scenarios, such as during meditation practices, rituals, or even in personal reflections when facing fears or uncertainties. It encourages individuals to cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and to seek strength in times of adversity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "tryambakaṁ yajāmahe sugandhim puṣṭivardhanam | urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya māmṛtāt ||" serves as a powerful invocation for spiritual growth and liberation. It highlights the nurturing and protective qualities of the divine while addressing the universal fear of death. By meditating on this mantra, individuals can find peace, strength, and a sense of connection to the greater cosmos, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
