मां न इन्द्र पीयत्नवे मां शर्धते परा दाहः |शिक्षा शचीवः शचीभिः ||
mā na indra pīyatnave mā śardhate parā dāḥ | śikṣā śacīvaḥ śacībhiḥ ||
  • mā na indra pīyatnave: This phrase can be translated as "May Indra not be angry." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for harmony and peace, urging individuals to avoid actions that may provoke anger or conflict.
  • mā śardhate parā dāḥ: This translates to "May he not be hostile towards us." This line emphasizes the importance of maintaining good relationships and avoiding hostility. In everyday life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in workplaces, families, or communities, where cooperation and understanding are essential for harmony.
  • śikṣā: This word means "instruction" or "teaching." It highlights the value of learning and guidance in life. For instance, in educational settings, the role of teachers is crucial in shaping the minds and characters of students.
  • śacīvaḥ: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in decision-making. In real life, wise individuals often serve as mentors or leaders, guiding others through their experiences and insights.
  • śacībhiḥ: This word means "with the wise." It suggests the idea of collaboration and learning from those who possess knowledge. In practical terms, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals come together to share their expertise for a common goal.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of peace, wisdom, and learning in our lives. It serves as a reminder to foster positive relationships, seek knowledge, and collaborate with others. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to a more harmonious and productive environment, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community interactions. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can strive for a life filled with understanding, cooperation, and growth.
