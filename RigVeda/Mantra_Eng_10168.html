मन्त्र: तुषेय्यम् पुरुवरपसम् ऋभ्वम् इनतमम् आप्त्यम् आप्त्यानाम् |आ दर्षते शवसाऽ सप्त दानून् प्र साक्षते प्रतिमानानि भूरी ||
stuṣeyyam puruvarpasam ṛbhvam inatamam āptyam āptyānām | ā darṣate śavasā sapta dānūn pra sākṣate pratimānāni bhūri ||
  • stuṣeyyam: This word can be interpreted as "may I be praised" or "let there be praise." It signifies the importance of recognition and appreciation in our lives.
  • puruvarpasam: This term refers to "the one who is of great worth" or "the noble one." It highlights the value of noble qualities and virtuous behavior in individuals.
  • ṛbhvam: This word means "the one who is righteous" or "the one who follows the path of truth." It emphasizes the significance of righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • inatamam: This can be translated as "the most excellent" or "the highest." It suggests striving for excellence in all endeavors.
  • āptyam: This term means "fulfillment" or "attainment." It indicates the importance of achieving one's goals and aspirations.
  • āptyānām: This refers to "those who have attained" or "the fulfilled ones." It signifies the community of individuals who have reached their goals.
  • ā darṣate: This phrase means "it is revealed" or "it is shown." It suggests that the truth or the essence of something becomes apparent over time.
  • śavasā: This word can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
  • sapta: This means "seven." In many cultures, the number seven is considered sacred and represents completeness or perfection.
  • dānūn: This term refers to "gifts" or "offerings." It highlights the importance of generosity and sharing with others.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progress.
  • sākṣate: This word means "to witness" or "to observe." It signifies the importance of being aware and conscious of our actions and their consequences.
  • pratimānāni: This can be translated as "the measures" or "the standards." It emphasizes the need for benchmarks in our pursuits.
  • bhūri: This word means "abundant" or "plentiful." It signifies the richness of life and the rewards of hard work and virtue.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for excellence, righteousness, and fulfillment in life. It encourages individuals to be noble, to seek truth, and to appreciate the journey towards achieving their goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, professional growth, and community service. By embodying the qualities of strength, generosity, and awareness, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the importance of living a life of purpose and virtue.
