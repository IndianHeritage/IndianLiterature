सहस्रेणेव साचते यवीयुधा यस्त आनाḻ उपस्तुतिम् |पुत्रं प्रावर्गं कृतुते सुवीर्ये दाश्नोति नमुक्तिभिः ||
sahasreṇeva sacate yavīyudhā yas ta ānaḻ upastutim | putram prāvargaṁ kṛṇute suvīrye dāśnoti namaüktibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasreṇeva: Like a thousand
    • sacate: He who is strong or powerful
    • yavīyudhā: With the best of weapons
    • yas: Who
    • ta: That
    • ānaḻ: To whom
    • upastutim: Praise or worship
    • putram: A son
    • prāvargaṁ: One who is well-versed or knowledgeable
    • kṛṇute: Creates or makes
    • suvīrye: In great strength or valor
    • dāśnoti: He who is capable
    • namaüktibhiḥ: With respectful words or salutations
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful individual who is likened to a thousand warriors, emphasizing immense strength and capability.
    • The phrase "yavīyudhā" suggests that this strength is not just brute force but is complemented by skill and strategy, akin to a well-trained soldier in a battle.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom, as indicated by "prāvargaṁ," suggesting that true strength comes from understanding and intelligence.
    • Furthermore, the reference to "putram" indicates the significance of lineage and the role of a son, who embodies the virtues of strength and wisdom.
    • The concluding part emphasizes the importance of respect and reverence, as one should approach such powerful individuals with humility and honor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who possesses both strength in decision-making and wisdom in strategy can be compared to the figure described in this mantra. Such leaders inspire their teams and drive success.
    • In sports, a coach who combines physical training with mental preparation exemplifies the "yavīyudhā" aspect, preparing athletes not just to compete but to excel.
    • In family dynamics, a father who imparts knowledge and values to his children embodies the essence of "putram," ensuring that the next generation is strong and capable.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, wisdom, and respect in various aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that true power lies not just in physical might but in the ability to think critically and act wisely.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, sports, or family, embodying these qualities can lead to success and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to honor those who possess such qualities, recognizing their contributions to society and our lives.
