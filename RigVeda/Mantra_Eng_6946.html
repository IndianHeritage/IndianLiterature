यस्य ते विश्वमानुषो भूरेर दत्तस्य वेदति |वसु स्पार्हं तद आ भर ||
yasya te viśvamānuṣo bhūrer dattasya vedati | vasu spārhaṁ tad ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • viśvamānuṣo: universal human
    • bhūrer: of the earth
    • dattasya: of the one who gives
    • vedati: knows or understands
    • vasu: wealth or good things
    • spārhaṁ: desire or aspiration
    • tad: that
    • ā: to
    • bhara: bring or fill
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a universal connection and the importance of giving and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the idea that true wealth comes from sharing and understanding the needs of others.
    • The phrase "of the one who gives" suggests that generosity is a key virtue.
    • The mantra encourages us to aspire for good things, not just for ourselves but for the collective well-being of humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community service, individuals often come together to help those in need, embodying the spirit of giving and understanding.
    • Philanthropists who donate to education or healthcare initiatives demonstrate the principle of sharing wealth for the greater good.
    • In workplaces, fostering a culture of collaboration and support reflects the essence of this mantra, where understanding and generosity lead to collective success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of generosity and understanding in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also lies in our relationships and contributions to society.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, we can create a more compassionate and connected world, where aspirations for good are shared and fulfilled collectively.
