विष्वे देवाः अनमस्यन् भियानास् त्वाम् अग्ने तमसि तस्थिवांसम् |वैश्वानरोऽवतूतये नोऽमर्त्योऽवतूतये नः ||
viśve devā anamasyan bhiyānās tvām agne tamasi tasthivāṁsam | vaiśvānaro 'vatūtaye no 'martyo 'vatūtaye naḥ ||
  • viśve devā: This phrase translates to "all the gods" or "universal deities." It signifies the collective presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • anamasyan: This means "without enmity" or "free from hostility." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and peace among all beings.
  • bhiyānās: This term can be interpreted as "fearing" or "being afraid." It suggests a sense of caution or reverence towards the divine.
  • tvām agne: "O Agni," referring to the fire god, who is a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification.
  • tamasi: This means "in darkness" or "in ignorance." It highlights the challenges and obstacles that one may face in life.
  • tasthivāṁsam: This translates to "standing firm" or "remaining steadfast." It indicates the need for resilience and strength in the face of difficulties.
  • vaiśvānaro: This refers to the universal fire or the cosmic energy that sustains life.
  • 'vatūtaye: This means "may you protect" or "may you guide." It expresses a plea for divine guidance and protection.
  • no 'martyo: "Not mortal," indicating a desire to transcend human limitations and connect with the divine.
  • 'vatūtaye naḥ: This translates to "may you protect us." It is a call for divine safeguarding and support.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity among all divine forces and the need for harmony in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection from the divine, especially in times of darkness and uncertainty. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges, encouraging individuals to remain steadfast and seek support from a higher power. It highlights the significance of community, cooperation, and the pursuit of peace, which are essential for personal and collective well-being.
