अनु त्वा मही पाजसी अचक्रे dyāvākṣāmā मदताम इन्द्र कर्मन् |त्वं वृत्रम आशयानं सिरासु महो वज्रेण सिष्वपो वराहुम ||
anu tvā mahī pājasī acakre dyāvākṣāmā madatām indra karman | tvaṁ vṛtram āśayānaṁ sirāsu maho vajreṇa siṣvapo varāhum ||
  • anu tvā: This phrase translates to "following you" or "after you." It signifies the idea of seeking guidance or inspiration from a higher power.
  • mahī pājasī: "mahī" means "great" or "vast," and "pājasī" refers to "glory" or "splendor." Together, they denote the immense glory of the divine.
  • acakre: This word means "to create" or "to establish." It implies the act of bringing something into existence or manifesting it.
  • dyāvākṣāmā: "dyā" refers to "heaven" or "sky," and "kṣāmā" means "to be able" or "to possess." This can be interpreted as the divine presence that encompasses the heavens.
  • madatām: This translates to "of the intoxication" or "of the ecstasy." It suggests a state of bliss or divine joy.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and rain. He represents the force that drives change and transformation.
  • karman: This word means "action" or "deed." It emphasizes the importance of actions in the pursuit of goals and the manifestation of desires.
  • tvaṁ: This means "you," referring directly to the divine or the higher power being addressed.
  • vṛtram: "vṛtra" refers to a demon or obstacle that hinders progress. It symbolizes challenges or adversities in life.
  • āśayānaṁ: This means "one who resides" or "one who dwells." It indicates the presence of the divine in our lives.
  • sirāsu: This translates to "in the heads" or "in the minds." It suggests the importance of thoughts and consciousness in overcoming challenges.
  • maho: This means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the power of the divine.
  • vajreṇa: "vajra" means "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing strength and indestructibility. It represents the power to overcome obstacles.
  • siṣvapo: This translates to "to strike down" or "to defeat." It indicates the action of overcoming challenges.
  • varāhum: This means "to protect" or "to save." It signifies the divine's role in safeguarding and guiding us through difficulties.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of actions and thoughts in manifesting our desires and achieving our goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing obstacles, whether in personal endeavors, professional challenges, or spiritual journeys. By invoking the divine presence and strength, individuals can find the courage and clarity needed to navigate through difficulties, ultimately leading to personal growth and transformation.
