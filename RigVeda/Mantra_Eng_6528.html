यज्ञेभिर अद्भुतक्रतुः यं कृपा सूदयन्त इति |मित्रं न जने सुदितं ऋतावनि ||
yajñebhir adbhutakratuṁ yaṁ kṛpā sūdayanta it | mitraṁ na jane sudhitam ṛtāvani ||
  • yajñebhir: This word refers to "sacrifices" or "offerings." In the context of the RigVeda, yajñas are rituals performed to please the deities and ensure harmony in the universe.
  • adbhutakratuṁ: This term can be broken down into "adbhuta," meaning "wonderful" or "extraordinary," and "kratu," which refers to "intellect" or "will." Together, it signifies a divine intellect or extraordinary will that is invoked through sacrifices.
  • yaṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "who" or "that," referring to the divine or the deity being addressed.
  • kṛpā: This word means "compassion" or "grace." It indicates the benevolent nature of the deity, who is capable of bestowing blessings upon devotees.
  • sūdayanta: This term means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It suggests the act of removing impurities or obstacles, both physically and spiritually.
  • it: This is a particle that can mean "thus" or "therefore," linking the previous concepts to the following statement.
  • mitraṁ: This word means "friend" or "ally." In the Vedic context, it often refers to the sun or a deity that brings light and warmth, symbolizing friendship and support.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • jane: This term means "to know" or "to recognize." It implies awareness or understanding.
  • sudhitam: This word means "well-thought-out" or "wise." It refers to the quality of being intelligent or having good judgment.
  • ṛtāvani: This term can be translated as "in the realm of truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order." It signifies the importance of living in harmony with the universal laws.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine entity, asking for the extraordinary intellect and compassion that can purify and guide individuals towards wisdom and truth. It emphasizes the importance of sacrifices and rituals in connecting with the divine and receiving blessings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of purification and wisdom, individuals can strive for self-improvement through reflection, learning, and seeking guidance from mentors.
  • Community Harmony: The concept of yajña can be seen in community service and collective efforts to support one another, fostering a sense of unity and cooperation.
  • Environmental Awareness: The idea of living in accordance with the cosmic order can inspire individuals to respect nature and engage in sustainable practices, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance, the importance of compassion, and the pursuit of wisdom. It serves as a reminder to engage in practices that promote personal and communal well-being, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence in alignment with universal truths.
