तं इन नरो वि हवयन्ते समीके रिरिक्वांसस् तन्‍वः कृत्वता त्राम् |मिथो यत् त्यागम् उभयासो अग्मन् नरस् तोकस्य तनयस्य सातौ ||
tam in naro vi hvayante samīke ririkvāṁsas tanvaḥ kṛṇvata trām | mitho yat tyāgam ubhayāso agman naras tokasya tanayasya sātau ||
  • tam in naro vi hvayante: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is invoked by men." It suggests a collective call or prayer from humanity towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • samīke ririkvāṁsas tanvaḥ kṛṇvata trām: This part translates to "may they create a body of strength and protection." It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength in the community, where individuals come together to form a protective force.
  • mitho yat tyāgam ubhayāso agman: This means "when they come together, they abandon their differences." It highlights the significance of overcoming personal or societal divisions for a greater cause.
  • naras tokasya tanayasya sātau: This can be understood as "for the well-being of the offspring of humanity." It reflects the idea that the actions taken today are for the benefit of future generations.

In conclusion, this mantra from the RigVeda emphasizes the power of collective action, unity, and the importance of working together for the greater good. It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together, setting aside their differences, they can create a strong and protective community that benefits not only themselves but also future generations. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community service, social movements, and collaborative projects where the focus is on collective well-being and progress. The mantra encourages us to recognize our shared humanity and work towards a common goal, fostering a sense of belonging and responsibility towards one another.
