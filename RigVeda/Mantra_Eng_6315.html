इयं त इन्द्र गिरवणो रातिः क्षरति सुन्वतः |मन्दानो अस्य बरहिषो वि राजसि ||
iyaṁ ta indra girvaṇo rātiḥ kṣarati sunvataḥ | mandāno asya barhiṣo vi rājasi ||
  • iyaṁ ta indra girvaṇo rātiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "This is the song of Indra, the lord of the heavens." Here, "Indra" represents the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power. The term "girvaṇo" refers to the divine hymns or praises sung in honor of Indra.
  • kṣarati sunvataḥ: This translates to "who flows for the good of the worshippers." The word "kṣarati" means to flow or to move, indicating the blessings and abundance that Indra provides to those who worship him. "Sunvataḥ" refers to the devotees or worshippers, emphasizing the reciprocal relationship between the divine and the faithful.
  • mandāno asya barhiṣo: This can be interpreted as "the one who is adorned with offerings." "Mandāno" suggests being decorated or adorned, while "barhiṣo" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity. This line highlights the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.
  • vi rājasi: This means "you reign supreme." It signifies the authority and power of Indra as the king of the gods, reinforcing his role as a protector and provider of prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the significance of offerings, and the blessings that come from divine grace. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as rituals, prayers, and community gatherings where individuals seek the blessings of higher powers for prosperity and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of faith, the act of giving, and the belief in a higher authority that governs the universe. By understanding and practicing these principles, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection to the divine, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
