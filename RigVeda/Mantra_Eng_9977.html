उत स्म वातो वहति वासोऽस्याधिरथं यदजायत सहस्रम् |रथीरभून्मुद्गलानी गविष्टौ भरे कृतं व्यचेद इन्द्रसेना ||
ut sma vāto vahati vāso 'syā adhirathaṁ yad ajayat sahasram | rathīr abhūn mudgalānī gaviṣṭau bhare kṛtaṁ vy aced indrasenā ||
  • Mantra: "ut sma vāto vahati vāso 'syā adhirathaṁ yad ajayat sahasram | rathīr abhūn mudgalānī gaviṣṭau bhare kṛtaṁ vy aced indrasenā ||"
  • Translation: "The wind carries the essence of this abode, which has conquered a thousand chariots. The charioteers have become powerful, and the Indra's army has achieved great feats."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ut sma: "Indeed, now" - signifies a moment of realization or awakening.
    • vāto: "the wind" - represents the force of nature, often seen as a carrier of messages and energy.
    • vahati: "carries" - indicates movement and transmission of energy or ideas.
    • vāso: "essence" or "abode" - refers to the core or dwelling place of existence.
    • adhirathaṁ: "conqueror of chariots" - symbolizes strength and victory over challenges.
    • yad: "which" - a connector that refers back to the essence mentioned earlier.
    • ajayat: "has conquered" - denotes achievement and success.
    • sahasram: "thousand" - emphasizes the magnitude of the victory.
    • rathīr: "charioteers" - represents those who guide and control the forces of life.
    • abhūn: "have become" - indicates transformation and growth.
    • mudgalānī: "powerful" - signifies strength and capability.
    • gaviṣṭau: "in the great battle" - refers to the challenges faced in life.
    • bhare: "achieved" - indicates accomplishment and fulfillment.
    • kṛtaṁ: "done" - signifies actions taken towards achieving goals.
    • vy aced: "has been cut" - suggests overcoming obstacles.
    • indrasenā: "Indra's army" - symbolizes collective strength and unity in facing challenges.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works together to overcome challenges can be seen as the "Indra's army," where each member plays a crucial role in achieving success.
    • In personal life, individuals who face and conquer their fears or obstacles can relate to the idea of "conquering a thousand chariots," symbolizing their strength and resilience.
    • Environmental movements that aim to protect nature can be likened to the "wind" carrying the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of preserving our abode.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of unity, strength, and the ability to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder that with collective effort and determination, one can achieve great feats in life.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional endeavors, or social movements, the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals and groups to harness their inner strength and work together towards common goals.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of resilience and the transformative power of collaboration in achieving success and overcoming adversity.
