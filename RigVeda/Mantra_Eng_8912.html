अति द्रव सारमेयौ श्वानौ चतुरक्षौ शबलौ साधुनā पथā |अथा पितृन् सुविदात्राम् उपेहि यमेना ये सद्धमादम् मदन्ति ||
ati drava sārameyau śvānau caturakṣau śabalau sādhunā pathā | athā pitṝn suvidatrām̐ upehi yamena ye sadhamādam madanti ||
  • ati drava: The term "ati" means "exceedingly" or "very," and "drava" refers to something that is fluid or flowing. This can symbolize the importance of adaptability and flexibility in life.
  • sārameyau: This word refers to "dogs," which are often seen as loyal companions. In many cultures, dogs symbolize fidelity and protection.
  • śvānau: Another term for "dogs," emphasizing their significance in the mantra. Dogs are often associated with guardianship and loyalty.
  • caturakṣau: This means "four-eyed," which can symbolize perception and awareness. It suggests the importance of being observant and aware of one's surroundings.
  • śabalau: This term can refer to "strong" or "powerful," indicating the strength and resilience needed in life.
  • sādhunā pathā: "Sādhunā" means "by the righteous" or "good," and "pathā" means "path." This suggests that one should follow the path of righteousness and virtue.
  • athā: This word means "then" or "therefore," indicating a transition or conclusion to the previous thoughts.
  • pitṝn: Referring to "ancestors" or "forefathers," this highlights the importance of honoring and remembering those who came before us.
  • suvidatrām̐: This means "well-disposed" or "favorable," suggesting that one should seek the blessings of the ancestors for a positive outcome.
  • upehi: This term means "approach" or "come near," indicating a call to action or a request for guidance.
  • yamena: Referring to "Yama," the god of death and the moral order, this emphasizes the importance of adhering to moral principles.
  • ye: This means "those who," indicating a group of people who follow a certain path.
  • sadhamādam: This term can be interpreted as "those who are righteous" or "virtuous," reinforcing the idea of following a good path.
  • madanti: This means "they rejoice" or "they are happy," suggesting that following the righteous path leads to joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of loyalty, adaptability, and righteousness in life. It encourages individuals to honor their ancestors and seek their blessings while adhering to moral principles. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as fostering strong relationships, being adaptable in challenging situations, and making ethical decisions. By following the path of righteousness and honoring our roots, we can find joy and fulfillment in our lives.
