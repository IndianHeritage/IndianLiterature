याभिः कृशानुम असने दुवस्यथो जवे याभिर यूनो अर्वन्तम आवतम् |मधु प्रियं भरतो यत सरड्भ्यस ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतम् ||
yābhiḥ kṛśānum asane duvasyatho jave yābhir yūno arvantam āvatam | madhu priyam bharatho yat saraḍbhyas tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ - This word can be translated as "by whom" or "through which." It indicates a connection or means of action.
  • kṛśānum - Refers to "the one who is slender" or "the one who is agile." This can symbolize flexibility and adaptability in life.
  • asane - Means "in the seat" or "in the position." It suggests stability and the importance of being grounded in one's actions.
  • duvasyatho - This can be interpreted as "swiftly" or "with speed." It emphasizes the need for quickness in thought and action.
  • jave - Translates to "in speed" or "with swiftness." This reinforces the idea of agility and responsiveness.
  • yābhir - Similar to "yābhiḥ," it means "by whom" or "through which," indicating a collective action or influence.
  • yūno - Refers to "the two" or "the pair." This can symbolize partnership and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • arvantam - Means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and resilience in facing challenges.
  • āvatam - Translates to "protect" or "safeguard." This highlights the importance of protection and care in relationships and endeavors.
  • madhu - Means "sweet" or "pleasant." It symbolizes joy and positivity in life.
  • priyam - Refers to "beloved" or "dear." This emphasizes the value of love and affection in human connections.
  • bharatho - Can be interpreted as "the one who carries" or "the bearer." It signifies responsibility and the act of supporting others.
  • yat - Means "that" or "which." It indicates a reference to something significant.
  • saraḍbhyas - Refers to "from the waters" or "from the flow." This can symbolize the source of life and nourishment.
  • tābhir - Means "by those" or "through them," indicating a collective effort or influence.
  • ū - Can be interpreted as "to" or "towards," suggesting direction or purpose.
  • ṣu - Means "good" or "well." It emphasizes the importance of positive outcomes.
  • ūtibhir - Refers to "by the powers" or "through the strengths." This highlights the role of strength and capability in achieving goals.
  • aśvinā - Refers to "the Ashvins," who are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and vitality. They symbolize the importance of well-being and support.
  • gatam - Means "gone" or "departed." It signifies the completion of a journey or the achievement of a goal.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of agility, strength, collaboration, and protection in our lives. It teaches us that by working together and supporting one another, we can achieve our goals swiftly and effectively. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in teamwork settings, such as in workplaces or community projects, where collective effort leads to success. The mantra also highlights the significance of nurturing relationships and providing care, which is essential for personal and communal well-being. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the positive impact we can have on each other.
