अहं गुङ्गुभ्यो अतिथिग्वम् इष्करम् इषं न वृत्रतुरं विक्षु धारणम् |यत पार्णयघ्न उता वा करञ्जहे प्राहम् महे वृत्रहत्ये अशुश्रवि ||
ahaṁ guṅgubhyo atithigvam iṣkaram iṣaṁ na vṛtraturaṁ vikṣu dhārayam | yat parṇayaghna uta vā karañjahe prāham mahe vṛtrahatye aśuśravi ||
  • ahaṁ - "I" or "myself": This word signifies the self or the individual consciousness.
  • guṅgubhyo - "from the depths" or "from the hidden": This can refer to the hidden aspects of life or the subconscious mind.
  • atithigvam - "the guest" or "the visitor": This represents the idea of hospitality and welcoming others into our lives.
  • iṣkaram - "the creator" or "the maker": This word emphasizes the role of creation and the act of bringing forth new ideas or forms.
  • iṣaṁ - "the power" or "the authority": This refers to the strength or influence one holds in various aspects of life.
  • na - "not": A negation that indicates the absence of something.
  • vṛtraturaṁ - "the slayer of Vritra": Vritra is a mythical serpent representing obstacles or challenges; thus, this phrase signifies overcoming difficulties.
  • vikṣu - "the seeker" or "the one who searches": This represents the quest for knowledge and understanding.
  • dhārayam - "I hold" or "I support": This indicates the act of sustaining or maintaining something.
  • yat - "that which": A reference to a specific quality or characteristic.
  • parṇayaghna - "the one who destroys the leaves": This can symbolize the act of removing superficial layers to reach the core of an issue.
  • uta - "or": A conjunction that presents an alternative.
  • vā - "or": Similar to "uta," it offers another option.
  • karañjahe - "the one who acts": This emphasizes the importance of action in achieving goals.
  • prāham - "I declare" or "I proclaim": This indicates the act of expressing or stating something with conviction.
  • mahe - "great" or "mighty": This signifies strength and power.
  • vṛtrahatye - "in the slaying of Vritra": This reinforces the theme of overcoming challenges.
  • aśuśravi - "I have heard" or "I have listened": This indicates the importance of being receptive and attentive to knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the journey of self-awareness, the importance of hospitality, the act of creation, and the power of overcoming obstacles. In real life, it encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength, support others, and actively seek knowledge while being open to new experiences. The significance lies in recognizing that challenges (symbolized by Vritra) can be overcome through conscious effort and action. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, community building, and leadership, where one must navigate difficulties and foster a supportive environment for themselves and others.
