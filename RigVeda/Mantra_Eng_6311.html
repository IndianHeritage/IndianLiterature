सुवीर्यं स्वश्व्यं सुगव्यं इन्द्र दद्धि नः |होतेव पूर्वचित्तये प्राध्वरे ||
suvīryaṁ svaśvyaṁ sugavyam indra daddhi naḥ | hoteva pūrvacittaye prādhvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suvīryaṁ: Good strength or valor
    • svaśvyaṁ: Good health or well-being
    • sugavyam: Good wealth or prosperity
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, often associated with power and rain
    • daddhi: Grant or bestow
    • naḥ: To us
    • hoteva: Like a priest or a benefactor
    • pūrvacittaye: For the previous intentions or thoughts
    • prādhvare: In the context of worship or offering
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking for strength, health, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving one's goals and intentions, much like a priest who invokes the gods during a ritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, employees may recite this mantra before a big presentation, seeking the strength and clarity of mind to perform well.
    • During a wedding ceremony, families may invoke this mantra to bless the couple with a prosperous and healthy life together.
    • In sports, athletes might use this mantra as a form of motivation, asking for the strength and endurance needed to succeed in their competitions.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of strength, health, and prosperity in achieving success in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine assistance in our endeavors, acknowledging that while hard work is essential, spiritual support can enhance our efforts.
    • In daily life, individuals can apply this mantra by setting positive intentions and seeking blessings for their goals, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual pursuits.
