ध्रुवं ध्रुवेण हविषाभि सोमं मृषामसि |अथो त इन्द्रः केवलीर विषो बलिहृतस करत ||
dhruvaṁ dhruveṇa haviṣābhi somam mṛśāmasi | atho ta indraḥ kevalīr viśo balihṛtas karat ||
  • dhruvaṁ: This word translates to "fixed" or "constant." It signifies stability and reliability, much like the North Star, which is a guiding point in the night sky.
  • dhruveṇa: This means "by the fixed" or "through the constant." It emphasizes the importance of steadfastness in our actions and thoughts.
  • haviṣābhi: This refers to "with offerings" or "through sacrifices." In a real-life context, it can relate to the idea of giving back to the community or contributing to a cause.
  • somam: This word means "the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
  • mṛśāmasi: This translates to "we are deceived" or "we are misled." It serves as a reminder to be cautious and discerning in our choices and beliefs.
  • atho: This means "therefore" or "then," indicating a conclusion or a result of the previous statements.
  • ta: This translates to "that" or "those," referring to the divine or higher powers.
  • indraḥ: This refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • kevalīr: This means "the only" or "the sole," emphasizing exclusivity and singularity in purpose or action.
  • viśo: This translates to "of the people" or "of the community," highlighting the importance of collective effort and unity.
  • balihṛtas: This means "having taken the offerings" or "having accepted the sacrifices," indicating the acceptance of devotion and contributions.
  • karat: This translates to "he does" or "he acts," emphasizing the importance of action in achieving goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of stability, offerings, and divine guidance in our lives. It teaches us that while we may face deception or challenges, remaining steadfast and contributing to the greater good can lead to divine acceptance and strength. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community service, personal development, and leadership, where the focus is on consistent effort, collective action, and the pursuit of higher ideals. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's complexities with purpose and integrity.
