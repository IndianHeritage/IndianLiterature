परी यद इन्द्र रोदसी उभे अबुभोजीर महिना विश्वतः सीम |अमन्यमानाम् अभि मन्यमानैर निर ब्रह्मभिर अधमो दस्युम इन्द्र ||
pari yad indra rodasī ubhe abubhojīr mahinā viśvataḥ sīm | amanyamānām̐ abhi manyamānair nir brahmabhir adhamo dasyum indra ||
  • Mantra: "pari yad indra rodasī ubhe abubhojīr mahinā viśvataḥ sīm | amanyamānām̐ abhi manyamānair nir brahmabhir adhamo dasyum indra ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • yad: that which
    • indra: a deity, often associated with strength and power
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • ubhe: both
    • abubhojīr: having been nourished or sustained
    • mahinā: by the great
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • amanyamānām̐: for those who do not think
    • abhi: towards
    • manyamānair: those who think
    • nir: without
    • brahmabhir: the ultimate reality or truth
    • adhamo: the lowest
    • dasyum: the enemy or adversary
    • indra: again referring to the deity
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the omnipresence of Indra, the powerful deity, who encompasses both realms of existence.
    • It emphasizes the nourishment and sustenance provided by the divine from all directions.
    • The mantra contrasts those who are unaware or unthinking with those who are aware and contemplative, suggesting that awareness leads to a higher understanding of reality.
    • It warns against the lowest forms of existence, represented by the adversary, and encourages seeking the ultimate truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire and nourish their team, fostering an environment where everyone feels supported from all sides.
    • In personal relationships, being aware and thoughtful can lead to deeper connections, while ignorance can create barriers and misunderstandings.
    • In a broader societal context, the mantra can be seen as a call to seek truth and justice, standing against adversities and challenges that threaten harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of awareness, strength, and the pursuit of truth in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to be mindful of their surroundings and the impact of their actions, promoting a harmonious existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more just society.
