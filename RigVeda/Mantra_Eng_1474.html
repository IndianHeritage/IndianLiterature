अहन्न इन्द्रो यथा विदे शीरष्णा-शीरष्णोपवाच्यः |अस्मत्रा ते सदृक सन्तु रातयो भद्रा भद्रस्य रातयः ||
ahann indro yathā vide śīrṣṇā-śīrṣṇopavācyaḥ | asmatrā te sadhryak santu rātayo bhadrā bhadrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahann: I am
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • yathā: as
    • vide: to know
    • śīrṣṇā-śīrṣṇopavācyaḥ: with the head (or intellect) and the head being revered
    • asmatrā: from us
    • te: your
    • sadhryak: good fortune
    • santu: may they be
    • rātayo: the rulers or leaders
    • bhadrā: auspicious
    • bhadrasya: of the auspicious
    • rātayaḥ: the leaders or rulers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between the individual and the divine, particularly invoking the presence of Indra, who symbolizes strength, power, and leadership.
    • The phrase "I am Indra" suggests a realization of one's own potential and strength, encouraging individuals to embody the qualities of leadership and wisdom.
    • The reference to "head" signifies the importance of intellect and wisdom in guiding one's actions and decisions.
    • The invocation for "good fortune" and "auspicious leaders" emphasizes the need for positive influences and guidance in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to achieve greater heights, fostering an environment of collaboration and innovation.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on their own strengths and capabilities, using this mantra as a reminder to lead with wisdom and integrity in their relationships and decisions.
    • In community leadership, this mantra can serve as a call to action for leaders to be benevolent and to seek the welfare of their community, ensuring that their leadership is both effective and compassionate.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of self-awareness, leadership, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their own potential and to strive for wisdom in their actions, fostering a sense of responsibility towards themselves and others.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that true leadership is not just about authority, but about guiding others towards a path of goodness and prosperity.
