मां सख्युः शूनम् आ विदे मां पुत्रस्य प्रभूवसो |आवृत्वद भूत्व ते मनः ||
mā sakhyuḥ śūnam ā vide mā putrasya prabhūvaso | āvṛtvad bhūtu te manaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: do not
    • sakhyuḥ: of friends
    • śūnam: be empty or devoid
    • ā vide: let it not be known
    • mā: do not
    • putrasya: of the son
    • prabhūvaso: of the powerful or the lord
    • āvṛtvad: let it be covered
    • bhūtu: of existence or being
    • te: your
    • manaḥ: mind or thoughts
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or a prayer to not let the mind be devoid of friends and to not let the son be without the influence of the powerful.
    • It emphasizes the importance of companionship and support in life, suggesting that a person should not feel isolated or empty.
    • The phrase "let it be covered" indicates a desire for protection over one's thoughts and feelings, ensuring they are nurtured and supported.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of teamwork. Just as friends support each other, colleagues should collaborate and help one another to achieve common goals.
    • In family dynamics, the mantra highlights the need for strong familial bonds. Parents should ensure that their children feel loved and supported, preventing feelings of loneliness or neglect.
    • In friendships, it serves as a reminder to be present for one another, ensuring that no one feels abandoned or unsupported during tough times.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for connection and support in human relationships.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the need for community and support systems.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that our mental and emotional well-being is deeply intertwined with the relationships we cultivate, urging us to foster connections that uplift and protect us.
