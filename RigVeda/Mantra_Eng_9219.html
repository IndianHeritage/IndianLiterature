जीवं रुदन्ति वि मयन्ते अध्वरे दीर्घाम् अनु प्रसितिं दीधियुर नरः |वामम् पितृभ्यो य इदं समेरिरे मयः पतिभ्यो जनयः परिष्वजे ||
jīvaṁ rudanti vi mayante adhvare dīrghām anu prasitiṁ dīdhiyur naraḥ | vāmam pitṛbhyo ya idaṁ samerire mayaḥ patibhyo janayaḥ pariṣvaje ||
  • jīvaṁ rudanti: This phrase can be translated as "the living beings cry out." It signifies the emotional and spiritual expressions of life. In real life, this can be seen in moments of joy, sorrow, or struggle, where individuals express their feelings openly, such as during a wedding or a funeral.
  • vi mayante adhvare: This means "they are united in the sacrifice." It refers to the collective effort and unity in performing rituals or sacrifices. In modern contexts, this can be likened to community service or group activities where people come together for a common cause, such as environmental clean-up drives or charity events.
  • dīrghām anu prasitiṁ: This translates to "long-lasting prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of seeking enduring success and well-being. In everyday life, this can be reflected in sustainable practices, such as investing in education or health, which ensure long-term benefits for individuals and communities.
  • dīdhiyur naraḥ: This phrase means "the wise ones among men." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who guide others through their experiences and insights, such as teachers or community leaders who inspire positive change.
  • vāmam pitṛbhyo: This translates to "to the ancestors." It signifies respect and gratitude towards one's forebears. In contemporary society, this can be observed during festivals or family gatherings where traditions are honored, such as Diwali or Thanksgiving, where families come together to remember their roots.
  • ya idaṁ samerire: This means "who have come together." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork within organizations or groups working towards a shared goal, such as a sports team or a project group in a workplace.
  • mayaḥ patibhyo: This translates to "to the partners." It signifies the bonds and relationships we form with others. In modern life, this can be reflected in partnerships in business or personal relationships, where mutual support and understanding are crucial for success.
  • janayaḥ pariṣvaje: This means "may they embrace the people." It highlights the importance of community and connection. In real life, this can be seen in social initiatives that aim to bring people together, such as community festivals or support groups that foster a sense of belonging.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of unity, wisdom, and respect for traditions. It serves as a reminder that our actions and relationships have a profound impact on our communities and the world around us. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to engage in collective efforts, honor their heritage, and foster meaningful connections with others, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
