एता विश्वा सवनā तूतुमां कृषे स्वयṁ सूनो सहसो यानि दधिषे |वराय ते पात्रं धर्मणे तना यज्ञो मन्त्रो ब्रह्मोदयतं वचः ||
etā viśvā savanā tūtumā kṛṣe svayaṁ sūno sahaso yāni dadhiṣe | varāya te pātraṁ dharmaṇe tanā yajño mantro brahmodyataṁ vacaḥ ||
  • etā viśvā savanā tūtumā: This phrase can be interpreted as "all these offerings are directed towards you." It signifies the act of offering and the importance of collective participation in rituals.
  • kṛṣe svayaṁ sūno sahaso yāni dadhiṣe: This translates to "you who bestow strength and nourishment." It emphasizes the divine's role in providing sustenance and support to humanity.
  • varāya te pātraṁ dharmaṇe tanā: This means "we offer you the best vessel for righteousness." It highlights the importance of purity and intention in offerings, suggesting that the vessel (or the person) making the offering should embody dharma (righteousness).
  • yajño mantro brahmodyataṁ vacaḥ: This can be understood as "the mantra of the sacrifice is elevated by the divine word." It indicates that the spoken word in rituals holds significant power and that the mantras used in sacrifices are sacred and transformative.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to support a cause, such as a charity event. Just as the mantra emphasizes collective offerings, community members contribute their resources and efforts for a common good.
  • Significance: The mantra underscores the importance of intention, community, and the divine in our actions. It teaches that our offerings, whether material or spiritual, should be made with purity and a sense of righteousness.
  • Application: In daily life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family gatherings, where members come together to celebrate and support each other, or in workplaces where teamwork and collaboration are essential for success.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine. It encourages us to approach our actions with sincerity and a sense of purpose, ensuring that our contributions to society are meaningful and aligned with higher principles of righteousness.
