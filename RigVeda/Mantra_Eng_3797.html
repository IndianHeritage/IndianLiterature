देवं वो देवयज्ञयाग्निम ईळीत मर्त्यः |समिद्धः शुक्र दिदिह्य ऋतस्य योनिम आसदः ससस्य योनिम आसदः ||
devaṁ vo devayajyayāgnim īḻīta martyaḥ | samiddhaḥ śukra dīdihy ṛtasya yonim āsadaḥ sasasya yonim āsadaḥ ||
  • devaṁ vo devayajyayāgnim īḻīta martyaḥ: This line can be translated to "May we invoke the divine fire (Agni) through our worship." Here, "devaṁ" refers to the divine or gods, "devayajyayā" signifies the act of worship, and "agnim" refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • samiddhaḥ śukra dīdihy: This phrase means "May the fire be kindled bright and pure." "Samiddhaḥ" indicates that the fire is lit or kindled, "śukra" means bright or pure, and "dīdihy" suggests the desire for the fire to shine or illuminate.
  • ṛtasya yonim āsadaḥ: This translates to "May we sit at the source of truth." "Rtasya" refers to truth or cosmic order, "yonim" means source or womb, and "āsadaḥ" implies sitting or dwelling. This line emphasizes the importance of being grounded in truth and righteousness.
  • sasasya yonim āsadaḥ: This can be interpreted as "May we dwell at the source of abundance." "Sasasya" refers to abundance or prosperity, and "yonim" again means source. This line highlights the aspiration for a life filled with prosperity and well-being.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energy through worship, seeking purity and brightness in our actions, and grounding ourselves in truth and abundance. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where spirituality, morality, and prosperity are interconnected.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in personal development, one can strive to cultivate purity of thought and action, ensuring that their intentions are aligned with truth and righteousness. In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts towards creating a harmonious and prosperous environment, where individuals support each other in their spiritual and material pursuits.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds. By invoking divine energy, seeking purity, and grounding ourselves in truth and abundance, we can lead a balanced and fulfilling life. It encourages individuals to reflect on their values and actions, fostering a sense of responsibility towards themselves and the community.
