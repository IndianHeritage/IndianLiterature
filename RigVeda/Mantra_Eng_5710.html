मन्त्र: चित्रं ह यद वाम भोजने न्व अस्ति न्यात्रये महिष्वन्तं युयोतम् |यो वाम ओमानं दधते प्रियः सन् ||
citraṁ ha yad vām bhojanaṁ nv asti ny atraye mahiṣvantaṁ yuyotam | yo vām omānaṁ dadhate priyaḥ san ||
  • citraṁ ha yad vām bhojanaṁ: This phrase can be translated to "how wonderful is the food that you have." Here, "citraṁ" signifies something that is colorful or diverse, while "bhojanaṁ" refers to food. This can be related to the variety of cuisines we experience in our daily lives, showcasing the richness of culture and tradition in food.
  • nv asti ny atraye mahiṣvantaṁ yuyotam: This part translates to "there is no one who can match your strength." "Mahiṣvantaṁ" refers to someone who is powerful or strong. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as athletes who train rigorously to achieve excellence, or leaders who inspire and motivate others through their strength of character.
  • yo vām omānaṁ dadhate priyaḥ san: This translates to "whoever offers you this with love." "Omānaṁ" refers to respect or honor, and "priyaḥ san" means with affection. This can be related to the act of sharing food with loved ones, where the intention behind the offering is filled with love and respect, creating a bond among people.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of food as a source of joy and connection among individuals. It highlights the beauty of diversity in food, the strength of individuals, and the importance of love and respect in relationships. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as family gatherings, community feasts, or even in professional settings where sharing a meal can foster collaboration and camaraderie. The essence of this mantra encourages us to appreciate the richness of our experiences and the bonds we create through the simple act of sharing food.
