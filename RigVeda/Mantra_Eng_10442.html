ग्रीवाभ्यस्त उष्णिहाभ्यः कीकसाभ्यो अनूक्यात् |यक्ष्मं दोषण्यं अंसाभ्यां बाहुभ्यां वि वृहामि ते ||
grīvābhyas ta uṣṇihābhyaḥ kīkasābhyo anūkyāt | yakṣmaṁ doṣaṇya1m aṁsābhyām bāhubhyāṁ vi vṛhāmi te ||
  • Mantra: "grīvābhyas ta uṣṇihābhyaḥ kīkasābhyo anūkyāt | yakṣmaṁ doṣaṇya1m aṁsābhyām bāhubhyāṁ vi vṛhāmi te ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • grīvābhyas: From the neck
    • ta: That
    • uṣṇihābhyaḥ: From the warmth or heat
    • kīkasābhyo: From the kīkasas (a type of bird or possibly a metaphor for something)
    • anūkyāt: By the utterance or declaration
    • yakṣmaṁ: Illness or disease
    • doṣaṇya: Defects or faults
    • aṁsābhyām: From the limbs
    • bāhubhyāṁ: From the arms
    • vi: I
    • vṛhāmi: I strengthen or enhance
    • te: You
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a prayer for health and strength, invoking the divine to remove ailments and enhance physical well-being.
    • The reference to "neck," "arms," and "limbs" suggests a holistic approach to health, emphasizing the importance of the entire body in maintaining wellness.
    • The mention of "warmth" may symbolize vitality and energy, which are essential for a healthy life.
    • By invoking the "kīkasas," it may also imply seeking protection or blessings from nature or the divine forces represented by these entities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be recited during yoga or meditation sessions to promote physical health and mental clarity.
    • It can be used in healing rituals or during times of illness to invoke strength and recovery.
    • Individuals may also use this mantra as a daily affirmation to encourage a positive mindset towards health and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on holistic health, encouraging individuals to seek balance and strength in their physical and spiritual lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of body and spirit, and the importance of nurturing both for overall well-being.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various health and wellness practices, making it relevant in today's fast-paced world where health is often compromised.
