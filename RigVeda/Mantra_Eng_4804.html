तत् सु नो विश्वे आर्य आ सदा गृणन्ति कारवः |बृबुं सहस्रदातमं सूरिं सहस्रसातमम् ||
tat su no viśve arya ā sadā gṛṇanti kāravaḥ | bṛbuṁ sahasradātamaṁ sūriṁ sahasrasātamam ||
  • tat: This word translates to "that" or "that which," indicating a reference to something significant or divine.
  • su: Meaning "good" or "well," it emphasizes positivity and auspiciousness.
  • no: This means "our," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
  • viśve: Translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the collective.
  • arya: Refers to "noble" or "honorable," highlighting the qualities of virtue and righteousness.
  • ā: A prefix that can denote direction or emphasis, often used to enhance the meaning of the following word.
  • sadā: Meaning "always" or "eternally," it signifies continuity and permanence.
  • gṛṇanti: This means "they sing" or "they praise," indicating an act of devotion or acknowledgment.
  • kāravaḥ: Refers to "those who act" or "doers," emphasizing action and engagement in the world.
  • bṛbuṁ: This word means "great" or "powerful," often used to describe something of immense significance.
  • sahasradātamaṁ: Translates to "the one who gives a thousand," indicating abundance and generosity.
  • sūriṁ: Meaning "the sun" or "radiant one," it symbolizes light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • sahasrasātamam: This means "the one who is the best among thousands," further emphasizing excellence and superiority.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the collective well-being and enlightenment of all noble beings. It expresses a desire for universal goodness and praises the divine source of abundance and knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common cause, such as a charity event, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the collective effort and noble intentions behind their actions.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation to cultivate a sense of connection with the universe and to seek guidance in their actions, ensuring they align with noble values.
  • Educational Settings: In schools or learning environments, this mantra can inspire students to strive for excellence and to support one another in their educational journeys.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, virtue, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals and communities to act with nobility and to recognize the abundance that comes from collective efforts. By embodying these principles in daily life, one can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
