यासु राजा वरुणो यासु सोमो विश्वे देवाः यासूरजं मदन्ति |वैश्वानरो यास्व अग्निः प्रवेश्टस ता आपो देवीर इह माम् अवन्तु ||
yāsu rājā varuṇo yāsu somo viśve devā yāsūrjam madanti | vaiśvānaro yāsv agniḥ praviṣṭas tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||
  • yāsu rājā varuṇo: This phrase refers to Varuna, the king of the cosmic order and the god of water. Varuna represents the moral and natural laws that govern the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the rule of law in society, where justice and order are maintained.
  • yāsu somo: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality. It can be compared to the importance of nutrition and health in our daily lives, emphasizing the need for a balanced diet to sustain our physical and mental well-being.
  • viśve devā: This translates to "all the gods," indicating the collective divine presence. In a real-world context, this can be seen as the acknowledgment of diverse beliefs and the importance of unity among different cultures and religions.
  • yāsūrjam madanti: This phrase suggests the joy and bliss derived from the divine. It can be related to moments of happiness and fulfillment in life, such as achieving personal goals or experiencing love and friendship.
  • vaiśvānaro: Referring to the universal fire, this symbolizes energy and transformation. In practical terms, it can be associated with the drive for personal growth and the transformative power of education and knowledge.
  • yāsv agniḥ praviṣṭas: This means "where the fire has entered," indicating the presence of divine energy. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as the inspiration and passion that fuels our endeavors, whether in art, science, or personal relationships.
  • tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu: This line calls upon the divine waters (āpo) to protect and nurture the speaker. It signifies the importance of seeking support and guidance from higher powers or the community in times of need, much like how we rely on friends and family during challenging times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in various aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of order, nourishment, unity, joy, transformation, inspiration, and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to cultivate these values in our daily lives, fostering a sense of community, personal growth, and spiritual connection. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
