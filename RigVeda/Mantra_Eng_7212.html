नास्माकम अस्ति तत तारा आदित्यासो अतिष्कदे |यूम asmabhyam मृळता ||
nāsmākam asti tat tara ādityāso atiṣkade | yūyam asmabhyam mṛḻata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nāsmākam: "not ours" or "not belonging to us"
    • asti: "there is" or "exists"
    • tat: "that"
    • tara: "beyond" or "further"
    • ādityāso: "of the sun" or "solar"
    • atiṣkade: "in the highest place" or "in the supreme position"
    • yūyam: "you" (plural)
    • asmabhyam: "to us"
    • mṛḻata: "may you grant" or "may you bestow"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a sense of humility and recognition of the divine.
    • It acknowledges that what is beyond us, particularly the power and light of the sun (ādityā), is not ours to claim.
    • It calls upon the divine forces to bestow blessings upon us, indicating a desire for guidance and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in moments of gratitude, where individuals recognize that their achievements are not solely their own but are supported by greater forces, such as community, nature, or divine intervention.
    • For instance, a student may acknowledge that their success in exams is not just due to their hard work but also the support of teachers, family, and the resources available to them.
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may express appreciation for their team's efforts, recognizing that the success of a project is a collective achievement rather than an individual one.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of humility and the recognition of forces greater than ourselves.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek blessings and support from the divine, fostering a sense of interconnectedness with the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the essence of this mantra can lead to a more grateful and collaborative approach to life, enhancing personal and communal well-being.
