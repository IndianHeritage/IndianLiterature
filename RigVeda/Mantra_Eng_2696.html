इन्द्रः सुशिप्रो मगवाऽतरुत्रो महाव्रातस् तुविकूर्मिर ऋघावान् |यद उग्रो धा बाधितो मर्त्येषु क्व त्या ते वृषभ वीर्याणि ||
indraḥ suśipro maghavā tarutro mahāvrātas tuvikūrmir ṛghāvān | yad ugro dhā bādhito martyeṣu kva1 tyā te vṛṣabha vīryāṇi ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Suśipro: Means "well-formed" or "well-structured," indicating the importance of having a solid foundation in life.
  • Maghavā: Another name for Indra, often associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • Tarutro: Suggests the idea of overcoming obstacles, akin to a tree that stands tall despite storms.
  • Mahāvrātas: Refers to great vows or commitments, emphasizing the significance of dedication and discipline.
  • Tuvikūrmir: Implies a powerful being, possibly a reference to the tortoise (Kūrma) in Hindu mythology, symbolizing stability and endurance.
  • Ṛghāvān: Indicates someone who is wise or knowledgeable, highlighting the value of wisdom in decision-making.
  • Yad ugro: Translates to "when fierce," suggesting the presence of challenges or adversities in life.
  • Dhā bādhito: Means "when bound" or "restricted," indicating moments when one feels trapped or limited.
  • Martyeṣu: Refers to mortals or human beings, emphasizing the human experience.
  • Kva: Means "where," posing a question about the location or state of being.
  • Tyā: Translates to "by you," indicating a call for divine intervention or support.
  • Te: Means "your," referring to the divine or higher power.
  • Vṛṣabha: Symbolizes strength and virility, often associated with the bull in Vedic texts.
  • Vīryāṇi: Refers to "strengths" or "powers," indicating the qualities that one should cultivate.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and resilience in the face of challenges. It calls upon divine support to overcome obstacles and highlights the significance of commitment and discipline in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership roles, and overcoming adversities in both professional and personal contexts. By embodying the qualities of Indra and seeking divine guidance, individuals can navigate life's challenges with confidence and strength.
