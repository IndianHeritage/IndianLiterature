स हि क्षत्रस्य मनसस्य चित्तिभिर एवावदस्य यजतस्य साध्रेः |अवत्सारस्य स्पृणवाम रण्वभिः शविष्टं वाजं विदुषा चिदर्ध्यम् ||
sa hi kṣatrasya manasasya cittibhir evāvadasya yajatasya sadhreḥ | avatsārasya spṛṇavāma raṇvabhiḥ śaviṣṭhaṁ vājaṁ viduṣā cid ardhyam ||
  • sa hi kṣatrasya manasasya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, for the warrior's mind." Here, "kṣatra" refers to the warrior class or the ruling power, emphasizing the importance of mental strength and clarity in leadership.
  • cittibhir evāvadasya: This means "with the thoughts that are directed." It suggests that the thoughts and intentions of a leader or warrior should be focused and purposeful.
  • yajatasya sadhreḥ: This translates to "of the one who performs sacrifices." In Vedic culture, sacrifices (yajnas) were essential for maintaining cosmic order and harmony, indicating that a leader must be selfless and dedicated to the greater good.
  • avatsārasya spṛṇavāma: This can be interpreted as "we are touched by the one who protects." It highlights the role of a protector or guardian, suggesting that true leadership involves safeguarding others.
  • raṇvabhiḥ śaviṣṭhaṁ: This phrase means "the most excellent in battle." It emphasizes the valor and excellence required in a warrior, indicating that true strength lies not just in physical prowess but also in moral integrity.
  • vājaṁ viduṣā cid ardhyam: This translates to "the wealth that is known by the wise." It suggests that true wealth is not merely material but encompasses wisdom, knowledge, and understanding.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, emphasizing the importance of mental clarity, selflessness, protection, valor, and wisdom. It serves as a reminder that true leaders must cultivate their minds and intentions to serve their communities effectively.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to various leadership roles, such as in politics, business, or community service. For instance, a business leader who prioritizes ethical practices and the well-being of employees embodies the principles outlined in this mantra. Similarly, a community leader who focuses on the needs of the people and acts with integrity reflects the qualities of a true warrior as described in the RigVeda.
