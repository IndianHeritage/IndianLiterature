उक्तं इन्द्राय शंस्यं वर्धनं पुरुनिष्किधे | शक्रो यथा सुतेषु णो रारणत सख्येषु च ||
uktham indrāya śaṁsyaṁ vardhanam puruniṣṣidhe | śakro yathā suteṣu ṇo rāraṇat sakhyeṣu ca ||
  • uktham: This word translates to "the utterance" or "the spoken word." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the importance of speech and communication in achieving one's goals.
  • indrāya: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, this term symbolizes power, strength, and leadership. Indra is often associated with rain, storms, and the warrior spirit.
  • śaṁsyaṁ: This means "praise" or "commendation." It highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the divine or powerful forces in our lives.
  • vardhanam: This translates to "growth" or "increase." It emphasizes the idea of nurturing and fostering development, whether in personal life, community, or spirituality.
  • puruniṣṣidhe: This term can be interpreted as "in the fulfillment of desires" or "in the accomplishment of goals." It suggests that the mantra is aimed at achieving success and satisfaction in life.
  • śakro: Another reference to Indra, this word signifies strength and capability, particularly in overcoming challenges.
  • yathā: Meaning "as" or "like," it draws a comparison, indicating that the following actions or qualities are to be emulated.
  • suteṣu: This translates to "in the offerings" or "in the gifts." It suggests the importance of generosity and sharing in the pursuit of success.
  • ṇo: This is a form of the pronoun "we," indicating a collective aspect, emphasizing community and togetherness.
  • rāraṇat: This term can mean "to strive" or "to work hard." It underscores the necessity of effort and determination in achieving one's goals.
  • sakhyeṣu: This translates to "in friendships" or "in alliances." It highlights the importance of relationships and collaboration in the journey of life.
  • ca: Meaning "and," it connects the ideas presented in the mantra, reinforcing the interconnectedness of the concepts.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the power of words, the importance of leadership, and the necessity of growth and fulfillment in life. It teaches us that by praising and recognizing the strengths around us, we can foster our own development and that of our community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In the workplace, effective communication and recognition of team members' strengths can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
  • In personal relationships, nurturing friendships and alliances can help individuals achieve their goals through mutual support.
  • In community initiatives, collective efforts and shared resources can lead to significant growth and fulfillment of common desires.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions, relationships, and aspirations, encouraging us to strive for success while uplifting those around us.
