आ न इन्द्र पृक्षसे 'स्माकं ब्रह्मोद्यतम् |तत् त्वा याचामहे 'वः शुष्णं यद धनं अमानुषम् ||
ā na indra pṛkṣase 'smākam brahmodyatam | tat tvā yācāmahe 'vaḥ śuṣṇaṁ yad dhann amānuṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - O
    • na - not
    • indra - Indra (the king of gods, representing strength and power)
    • pṛkṣase - we seek or we ask
    • smākam - our
    • brahmodyatam - that which is raised or elevated by the divine
    • tat - that
    • tvā - you
    • yācāmahe - we request or we pray
    • vaḥ - for you
    • śuṣṇaṁ - the best or the most excellent
    • yad - which
    • dhann - wealth or prosperity
    • amānuṣam - beyond human or superhuman
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, asking for his blessings and support.
    • The phrase "brahmodyatam" signifies a divine elevation, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just material but also spiritual in nature.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and power in overcoming challenges.
    • The request for "śuṣṇaṁ" indicates a desire for the highest form of prosperity, which can be interpreted as both material wealth and spiritual fulfillment.
    • The term "amānuṣam" suggests that the blessings sought are extraordinary, transcending ordinary human capabilities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek strength and support from higher powers, similar to how this mantra seeks the blessings of Indra.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of teamwork and strength to overcome challenges, paralleling the essence of this mantra.
    • During personal struggles, people may turn to spiritual practices or prayers to seek guidance and strength, reflecting the essence of seeking divine support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine assistance in achieving both material and spiritual goals.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength, prosperity, and support can come from higher powers, encouraging individuals to remain hopeful and resilient.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, in professional environments, or in spiritual practices, reinforcing the belief in the power of divine intervention.
