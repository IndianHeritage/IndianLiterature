श्वित्यञ्चो मा दक्षिणतस्कपर्दा धियंजिन्वासो अभि हि प्रमन्दुः | उत्तिष्ठन वोचे परि बरहिषो नृन न मे दूराद अवितवे वसिष्ठाः ||
śvityañco mā dakṣiṇataskapardā dhiyaṁjinvāso abhi hi pramanduḥ | uttiṣṭhan voce pari barhiṣo nṝn na me dūrād avitave vasiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • śvityañco: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is bright or radiant." In real life, this can refer to individuals who bring positivity and enlightenment to their surroundings, much like a teacher or a mentor who inspires others.
  • mā dakṣiṇataskapardā: This phrase can be understood as "do not let the southern winds disturb me." The southern winds can symbolize challenges or negative influences that may come from unexpected directions. In life, this can relate to staying focused and not allowing external distractions to sway one's purpose.
  • dhiyaṁjinvāso: This can be translated as "the one who possesses wisdom." Wisdom is crucial in making informed decisions in everyday life, whether in personal relationships, career choices, or community involvement.
  • abhi hi pramanduḥ: This phrase suggests "may the great one come to me." It reflects the desire for divine guidance or support in times of need. In real life, this can be seen in moments when individuals seek help from a higher power or from their community during difficult times.
  • uttiṣṭhan voce: This means "rise and speak." It encourages individuals to take action and voice their thoughts. In a practical sense, this can be applied in situations where one must advocate for themselves or others, such as in social justice movements or personal conflicts.
  • pari barhiṣo: This can be interpreted as "surrounding the sacred." It emphasizes the importance of creating a respectful and sacred environment, whether in a physical space like a home or in a community setting. This can be seen in practices like maintaining cleanliness and respect in places of worship.
  • nṝn na me dūrād: This translates to "do not let me be far from the people." It highlights the significance of community and connection with others. In real life, this can be reflected in the importance of maintaining relationships with family and friends, especially during challenging times.
  • avitave vasiṣṭhāḥ: This phrase can be understood as "may the best come to me." It expresses a hope for positive outcomes and blessings in life. This can be related to the aspirations individuals have for success, happiness, and fulfillment in their personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of wisdom, community, resilience, and the pursuit of positivity. Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek enlightenment, maintain focus amidst challenges, and foster connections with others. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal growth, relationships, and contributions to society, reminding them of the importance of rising to speak and creating a sacred space for themselves and their communities.
