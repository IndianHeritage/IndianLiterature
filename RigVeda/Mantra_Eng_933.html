त्वं अङ्ग प्र शंसीषो देवः शविष्ठ मार्त्यम् |न त्वद अन्यो मगवन्न अस्ति मर्दितेन्द्र ब्रवीमि ते वचः ||
tvam aṅga pra śaṁsiṣo devaḥ śaviṣṭha martyam | na tvad anyo maghavann asti marḍitendra bravīmi te vacaḥ ||
  • tvam: This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • aṅga: Meaning "part" or "aspect," it signifies that the addressed deity is an integral part of the universe.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," suggesting a sense of movement or action.
  • śaṁsiṣo: This term can be interpreted as "praise" or "worship," indicating the act of honoring the divine.
  • devaḥ: This means "god" or "deity," referring to a higher power or divine being.
  • śaviṣṭha: This word translates to "most powerful" or "most capable," emphasizing the strength and capability of the deity.
  • martyam: Meaning "mortal" or "human," it contrasts the divine with the human condition.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • tvad: This translates to "your," reinforcing the connection to the addressed deity.
  • anyo: Meaning "other," it suggests exclusivity or uniqueness.
  • maghavann: This term refers to "the powerful one" or "the mighty," often associated with wealth and prosperity.
  • asti: This means "is" or "exists," affirming the presence of the subject.
  • marḍitendra: This can be interpreted as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is revered," indicating a high status.
  • bravīmi: Meaning "I speak" or "I declare," it indicates the act of proclaiming or expressing something important.
  • te: This translates to "to you," reinforcing the address to the deity.
  • vacaḥ: Meaning "words" or "speech," it signifies the importance of communication and expression in the context of devotion.

The mantra can be understood as a profound expression of devotion and recognition of the divine. It emphasizes the unique and unparalleled nature of the deity being addressed, highlighting the relationship between the divine and the mortal. The speaker acknowledges the deity's power and capability, asserting that there is no other being like them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to connect with the divine and seek guidance.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of the deity by being powerful and capable, inspiring their followers through their actions and words.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to recognize their unique strengths and capabilities, fostering self-confidence and empowerment.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire devotion, recognize the divine's unique qualities, and encourage individuals to embrace their own strengths. It serves as a reminder of the connection between the mortal and the divine, urging us to seek higher ideals in our lives.
