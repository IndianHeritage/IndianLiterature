अश्विनाऽ मधुशुत्तमो युवाकुः सोमस्तं पातमागतम् दुरोणे |रथो ह वां भूरी वर्पः करिकृत सुतावतो निष्कृतमागमिष्टः ||
aśvinā madhuṣuttamo yuvākuḥ somas tam pātam ā gataṁ duroṇe | ratho ha vām bhūri varpaḥ karikrat sutāvato niṣkṛtam āgamiṣṭhaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā madhuṣuttamo yuvākuḥ somas tam pātam ā gataṁ duroṇe | ratho ha vām bhūri varpaḥ karikrat sutāvato niṣkṛtam āgamiṣṭhaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • madhuṣuttamo: The best or sweetest (madhu) among the Aśvins.
    • yuvākuḥ: Young or youthful, indicating vitality and energy.
    • somas: Referring to Soma, a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine qualities.
    • tam: That or him, referring to the divine essence or energy.
    • pātam: The drink or the essence that is to be consumed.
    • ā gataṁ: Having come or arrived.
    • duroṇe: In the vessel or container, symbolizing the body or the physical form.
    • ratho: Chariot, representing the journey of life.
    • ha: Indeed or certainly, emphasizing the statement.
    • vām: Your or of you, indicating a personal connection.
    • bhūri: Abundant or plentiful, suggesting richness in experience or blessings.
    • varpaḥ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • karikrat: Having been made or created, indicating action or effort.
    • sutāvato: Of the well-spoken or eloquent, referring to wisdom or knowledge.
    • niṣkṛtam: Freed or liberated, suggesting a release from bondage or suffering.
    • āgamiṣṭhaḥ: The one who is to be attained or reached, indicating a goal or aspiration.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Aśvins, who are often seen as the divine physicians, symbolizing health and rejuvenation. In real life, this can be related to seeking medical help or healing in times of illness.
    • The reference to "madhuṣuttamo" emphasizes the sweetness of life and the importance of enjoying the good moments, akin to savoring a delicious meal or a joyful experience.
    • The mention of "somas" and the act of drinking it can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, much like how one seeks education or enlightenment in life.
    • The "ratho" symbolizes the journey of life, where each individual is on their own chariot, navigating through various experiences, challenges, and achievements.
    • The idea of "bhūri varpaḥ" reflects the abundance of opportunities and blessings one can receive, similar to how a farmer reaps a bountiful harvest after diligent work.
    • "Karikrat" and "sutāvato" highlight the importance of effort and eloquence in achieving one's goals, akin to how a student must study hard and articulate well to succeed in exams.
    • Finally, "niṣkṛtam āgamiṣṭhaḥ" signifies the aspiration for liberation and fulfillment, which can be seen in the pursuit of personal growth, spiritual enlightenment, or achieving one's dreams.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of health, wisdom, and the journey of life. It encourages individuals to seek healing, embrace the sweetness of life, and strive for abundance and fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as health and wellness practices, educational pursuits, and personal development efforts.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes the interconnectedness of body, mind, and spirit, urging individuals to live a balanced and purposeful life.
