अवर्षीर वर्षम् उदु षू गृभायाकर धन्वान्य अत्येतवा उ |अजीजन ओषधीर भोजना य कमुत प्रजाभ्यो विदो मनीषाम् ||
avarṣīr varṣam ud u ṣū gṛbhāyākar dhanvāny atyetavā u | ajījana oṣadhīr bhojanāya kam uta prajābhyo 'vido manīṣām ||
  • Mantra: "avarṣīr varṣam ud u ṣū gṛbhāyākar dhanvāny atyetavā u | ajījana oṣadhīr bhojanāya kam uta prajābhyo 'vido manīṣām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avarṣīr: to rain down or to shower
    • varṣam: rain or abundance
    • ud: up or above
    • u: and
    • ṣū: to shine or to illuminate
    • gṛbhāyākar: to grasp or to hold
    • dhanvāny: wealth or resources
    • atyetavā: beyond or surpassing
    • ajījana: to give birth or to produce
    • oṣadhīr: herbs or plants
    • bhojanāya: for nourishment or food
    • kam: who or what
    • uta: or
    • prajābhyo: to beings or creatures
    • vido: knowing or knowledgeable
    • manīṣām: of wisdom or intellect
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the abundance of rain and resources that nourish life.
    • The phrase "avarṣīr varṣam" emphasizes the importance of rain as a source of sustenance, akin to how farmers rely on seasonal rains for their crops.
    • "gṛbhāyākar dhanvāny" suggests that wealth and resources are to be grasped and utilized wisely, similar to how businesses must manage their resources effectively to thrive.
    • The mention of "oṣadhīr bhojanāya" highlights the significance of plants and herbs in providing nourishment, reflecting the modern emphasis on healthy eating and the use of natural ingredients in diets.
    • Finally, the inquiry "kam uta prajābhyo 'vido manīṣām" raises questions about who possesses true wisdom, reminding us of the value of knowledge and understanding in making informed decisions in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, resources, and wisdom.
    • In real life, it encourages us to appreciate the abundance provided by nature and to use our resources wisely.
    • It also serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding, as these are essential for making informed choices that benefit ourselves and society.
    • Overall, the mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, from agriculture and business to personal growth and community well-being.
