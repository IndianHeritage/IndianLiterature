विहि होत्रा अवीतā विपो न रायो आर्यः |वायव आ चन्द्रेण रथेन याहि सुतस्य पीतये ||
vihi hotrā avītā vipo na rāyo aryaḥ | vāyav ā candreṇa rathena yāhi sutasya pītaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vihi: to go or to proceed
    • hotrā: the priest or the one who offers sacrifices
    • avītā: protected or safeguarded
    • vipo: of the wise or learned
    • na: not
    • rāyo: wealth or prosperity
    • aryaḥ: noble or honorable
    • vāyav: related to the wind or air
    • ā: towards
    • candreṇa: with the moon
    • rathena: with the chariot
    • yāhi: come or go
    • sutasya: of the son or offspring
    • pītaye: for the enjoyment or nourishment
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the idea of a wise and noble individual who is protected and safeguarded.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking prosperity and wealth, but not at the cost of one's honor or nobility.
    • The reference to the wind and the moon suggests a connection to nature and the cosmos, indicating that true wealth comes from harmony with the universe.
    • The chariot symbolizes movement and progress, suggesting that one should actively pursue their goals while remaining grounded in noble values.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A business leader who prioritizes ethical practices while striving for financial success embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who support and uplift their loved ones while pursuing their own ambitions reflect the balance of wisdom and nobility.
    • Environmental activists who advocate for sustainable practices demonstrate the connection to nature and the importance of protecting our planet for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to pursue wealth and success with integrity and honor.
    • It highlights the significance of being wise and noble in our actions, ensuring that our pursuits do not harm others or the environment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
