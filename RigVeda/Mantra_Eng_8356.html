ऋतस्य गोपा न दभाय सुक्रतुस् त्रि ष पवित्रा हृद्य अन्तर आ दधे |विद्वान् स विश्वा भुवनाभि पश्यत्य अवाजुष्टान् विध्यति कर्ते अव्रतान् ||
ṛtasya gopā na dabhāya sukratus trī ṣa pavitrā hṛdy a1ntar ā dadhe | vidvān sa viśvā bhuvanābhi paśyaty avājuṣṭān vidhyati karte avratān ||
  • ṛtasya gopā: This phrase translates to "the protector of truth." In real life, this can be likened to individuals or leaders who uphold honesty and integrity in their actions, such as a whistleblower who exposes corruption for the greater good.
  • na dabhāya: This means "not to be harmed." It signifies the idea that those who follow the path of truth and righteousness are shielded from harm. For example, a person who stands firm in their ethical beliefs may face challenges but ultimately finds strength and protection through their convictions.
  • sukratus: This term refers to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." It emphasizes the importance of performing righteous acts in daily life, such as volunteering for community service or helping those in need, which contributes to personal and societal well-being.
  • trī ṣa pavitrā: This translates to "three pure ones." It can represent the mind, body, and spirit, highlighting the need for holistic purity. In practice, this can be seen in wellness practices that promote mental clarity, physical health, and spiritual growth, such as yoga and meditation.
  • hṛdy a1ntar ā dadhe: This means "they place within the heart." It suggests that true understanding and wisdom come from within. In real life, this can be related to introspection and self-awareness, where individuals reflect on their values and beliefs to guide their actions.
  • vidvān sa viśvā bhuvanābhi paśyaty: This translates to "the wise one sees all worlds." It implies that a person with wisdom can perceive the interconnectedness of all life. For instance, an environmentalist who understands the impact of human actions on the planet embodies this wisdom by advocating for sustainable practices.
  • avājuṣṭān vidhyati karte avratān: This means "he who acts without transgression." It emphasizes the importance of adhering to one's principles and duties. In real life, this can be exemplified by professionals who maintain ethical standards in their work, such as doctors who prioritize patient care over profit.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life rooted in truth, righteousness, and wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that by embodying these values, individuals can navigate life's challenges with integrity and purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to act ethically, reflect on our inner selves, and recognize our interconnectedness with the world around us, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
