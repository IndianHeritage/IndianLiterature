हिरण्यदन्तं शुचिवर्णम आरात क्षेत्राद अपश्याम आयुधा मिमानम् |ददानो अस्मा अमृतं विपृक्वत किम माम अनिन्द्राः कृत्वन्न अनुक्थाः ||
hiraṇyadantaṁ śucivarṇam ārāt kṣetrād apaśyam āyudhā mimānam | dadāno asmā amṛtaṁ vipṛkvat kim mām anindrāḥ kṛṇavann anukthāḥ ||
  • hiraṇyadantaṁ: This term translates to "golden-toothed" or "having golden teeth." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent something that is precious, valuable, or auspicious. For example, in real life, a person who is kind and generous can be seen as having a "golden heart."
  • śucivarṇam: This means "of pure color" or "radiant." It signifies purity and brightness. In everyday life, this can relate to individuals who embody integrity and honesty, shining brightly in their communities.
  • ārāt: This word can be interpreted as "from afar" or "from a distance." It suggests a sense of observation or awareness from a broader perspective. For instance, a leader who can see the bigger picture and guide their team effectively embodies this quality.
  • kṣetrād: This translates to "from the field" or "from the realm." It can refer to the physical world or the environment we inhabit. In practical terms, this could relate to someone who is grounded and connected to their surroundings, such as a farmer who understands the land.
  • apaśyam: Meaning "I have not seen" or "I have not perceived." This can indicate a lack of awareness or understanding. In real life, it can reflect moments when we fail to recognize opportunities or challenges around us.
  • āyudhā: This term means "with weapons" or "armed." It can symbolize preparedness or the tools we use to face challenges. For example, education and skills can be seen as weapons in the battle of life.
  • mimānam: This translates to "to measure" or "to evaluate." It suggests the importance of assessment in our actions and decisions. In real life, this can relate to setting goals and measuring progress towards achieving them.
  • dadāno: Meaning "giving" or "bestowing." This emphasizes the act of generosity and sharing. In everyday life, acts of kindness, such as volunteering or helping others, embody this principle.
  • asmā: This means "to us" or "for us." It indicates a sense of community or collective experience. In real life, this can relate to teamwork and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • amṛtaṁ: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies something that transcends time, such as wisdom or love. In practical terms, the impact of our actions can create a legacy that lasts beyond our lifetime.
  • vipṛkvat: This term means "in a reversed manner" or "in a contrary way." It can suggest the idea of looking at things from a different perspective. In real life, this can relate to innovative thinking and problem-solving.
  • kim mām: This translates to "what about me?" It reflects a sense of inquiry or self-reflection. In everyday life, it can relate to moments of introspection where we question our purpose and direction.
  • anindrāḥ: This means "without fault" or "blameless." It signifies purity of intention and action. In real life, striving for ethical behavior and integrity in our actions embodies this quality.
  • kṛṇavann: This translates to "they make" or "they create." It emphasizes the power of action and creation. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who take initiative to bring about positive change in their communities.
  • anukthāḥ: This means "without being spoken" or "unexpressed." It can signify the importance of unspoken thoughts and feelings. In real life, this can relate to the significance of non-verbal communication and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of value, purity, awareness, preparedness, generosity, community, immortality, perspective, self-reflection, integrity, action, and unspoken communication. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their lives. By recognizing the importance of these attributes, we can strive to create a more harmonious and meaningful existence, both personally and collectively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to act with intention, to be aware of our surroundings, and to contribute positively to our communities, ultimately leading to a legacy that transcends time.
