सनापुराणमध्येम्यआराणमहःपितुर्जनितुर्जामितन्नः। देवासो यत्र पनितारा एवैरुरौ पथि व्युते तस्थुरन्तः॥
sanā purāṇam adhy emy ārān mahaḥ pitur janitur jāmi tan naḥ | devāso yatra panitāra evair urau pathi vyute tasthur antaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanā: ancient, eternal
    • purāṇam: the old, the traditional
    • adhy: I meditate, I contemplate
    • emy: I approach, I come near
    • ārān: the great, the powerful
    • mahaḥ: greatness, majesty
    • pitur: of the father, of the creator
    • janitur: the one who gives birth, the origin
    • jāmi: I know, I understand
    • tan: that, which
    • naḥ: our, of us
    • devāso: the divine beings, the gods
    • yatra: where, in which
    • panitāra: the pure, the sacred
    • evair: indeed, truly
    • urau: in the heavens, in the sky
    • pathi: on the path, in the way
    • vyute: in the midst, in the company
    • tasthur: they reside, they dwell
    • antaḥ: within, inside
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal and ancient wisdom that one seeks to understand and connect with.
    • The reference to the "father" and "origin" suggests a connection to a higher power or divine source, emphasizing the importance of understanding our roots.
    • The mention of divine beings indicates that there is a spiritual dimension to our existence, and that we are not alone in our journey.
    • The phrase "where the pure dwell" suggests a place of sanctity and purity, which can be interpreted as a state of mind or a spiritual realm.
    • The idea of "path" signifies the journey of life, where one seeks knowledge and understanding amidst the challenges faced.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek ancient wisdom through meditation and reflection, similar to the contemplation mentioned in the mantra.
    • In family structures, understanding our origins and the role of our ancestors can provide a sense of identity and belonging.
    • In spiritual practices, many people connect with divine beings through prayer, seeking guidance and support in their life journey.
    • In environmental contexts, the idea of purity can relate to the importance of preserving nature and recognizing the sacredness of the earth.
    • In career paths, individuals often navigate their professional journeys by seeking mentorship and wisdom from those who have walked similar paths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking ancient wisdom and understanding our connection to the divine and our origins.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their life paths and the purity of their intentions as they navigate through challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire personal growth, spiritual exploration, and a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that wisdom is timeless and that we can draw strength from our roots as we journey through life.
