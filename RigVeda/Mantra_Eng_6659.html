वाशीम एको बिभर्ति हस्त आयसीम अन्तर देवेषु निध्रुविः ||
vāśīm eko bibharti hasta āyasīm antar deveṣu nidhruviḥ ||
  • vāśīm: This word can be translated to "the one who possesses" or "the one who controls." It signifies a sense of authority and mastery over one's surroundings.
  • eko: Meaning "one" or "single," this term emphasizes unity and singularity, suggesting that there is a singular force or entity that governs or influences.
  • bibharti: This translates to "carries" or "supports." It indicates the act of holding or sustaining something, which can be interpreted as the support provided by a higher power or universal force.
  • hasta: Meaning "hand," this word symbolizes action, effort, and the ability to manipulate or create. It represents the human capacity to act and influence the world.
  • āyasīm: This term refers to "iron" or "strength." It symbolizes resilience, durability, and the power to withstand challenges.
  • antar: Meaning "within" or "inside," this word suggests an inner dimension or the essence of something, pointing to the internal aspects of existence.
  • deveṣu: This translates to "among the gods" or "in the divine realm." It indicates a connection to the spiritual or divine forces that govern the universe.
  • nidhruviḥ: This term can be interpreted as "unwavering" or "steady." It signifies stability and consistency, suggesting that the force being referred to is reliable and constant.

The mantra "vāśīm eko bibharti hasta āyasīm antar deveṣu nidhruviḥ" encapsulates the idea of a singular, powerful force that governs and supports the universe, both externally and internally. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing this force and understanding our relationship with it.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Empowerment: Just as the mantra speaks of a singular force, individuals can harness their inner strength and capabilities to overcome challenges. For example, someone facing a difficult situation at work can draw upon their resilience (āyasīm) and take decisive action (hasta) to navigate through it.
  • Unity in Diversity: The concept of "eko" highlights the importance of unity. In a diverse society, recognizing that we are all part of a larger whole can foster harmony and cooperation. Community initiatives that bring people together for a common cause exemplify this principle.
  • Spiritual Connection: The reference to "deveṣu" suggests a connection to the divine. Engaging in practices such as meditation or prayer can help individuals feel grounded and connected to a higher power, providing guidance and support in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the strength that comes from recognizing and aligning with the universal force that supports us. By understanding and applying its teachings, individuals can cultivate resilience, foster unity, and deepen their spiritual connections in their everyday lives.
