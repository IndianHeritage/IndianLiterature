ववक्षुर अस्य केतव उता वज्रो गभस्त्योः |यत सूर्यो न रोदसी अवर्धयत ||
vavakṣur asya ketava uta vajro gabhastyoḥ | yat sūryo na rodasī avardhayat ||
  • Mantra: "vavakṣur asya ketava uta vajro gabhastyoḥ | yat sūryo na rodasī avardhayat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vavakṣur: to be strong or powerful
    • asya: of this
    • ketava: the wise or knowledgeable ones
    • uta: and
    • vajro: thunderbolt or strength
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the luminous or radiant
    • yat: that which
    • sūryo: the sun
    • na: not
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • avardhayat: has expanded or increased
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and strength that comes from knowledge and wisdom.
    • The phrase "vavakṣur asya ketava" suggests that those who are wise possess a strength that is formidable.
    • The mention of "vajro gabhastyoḥ" indicates a connection to the radiant energy, akin to the power of a thunderbolt, which can be seen as a metaphor for enlightenment or divine knowledge.
    • The sun, represented by "sūryo," is a symbol of illumination and life, suggesting that wisdom brings light to the darkness of ignorance.
    • The idea that "na rodasī avardhayat" implies that true wisdom transcends the limitations of the physical world, expanding one's understanding beyond the ordinary realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge empowers individuals, much like the strength described in the mantra. For instance, a student who dedicates themselves to learning can overcome challenges and achieve great success.
    • In leadership, wise leaders who possess knowledge and insight can guide their teams effectively, akin to the "vajro" strength that can break through obstacles.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek self-awareness and understanding can illuminate their paths, similar to how the sun brings light to the world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and knowledge as sources of strength and illumination in life.
    • It teaches us that by seeking knowledge, we can transcend our limitations and expand our understanding of the world.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and a deeper understanding of our existence.
