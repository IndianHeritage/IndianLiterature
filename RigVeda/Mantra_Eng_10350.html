नह्य अस्यां नाम गृभ्णामि नो अस्मिन रमते जनः |परामेव परावतं sapatnīं गमयामसि ||
nahy asyā nāma gṛbhṇāmi no asmin ramate jane | parām eva parāvataṁ sapatnīṁ gamayāmasi ||
  • Translation: "I do not grasp the name of this one; in this, the people do not delight. We shall lead the supreme one, the highest, to the rival."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • nahy: "not" or "do not"
    • asyā: "of this" or "this one"
    • nāma: "name"
    • gṛbhṇāmi: "I grasp" or "I hold"
    • no: "not" or "in this"
    • asmin: "in this" or "in this context"
    • ramate: "delight" or "enjoy"
    • jane: "people" or "among people"
    • parām: "supreme" or "highest"
    • eva: "indeed" or "certainly"
    • parāvataṁ: "the highest" or "the supreme one"
    • sapatnīṁ: "to the rival" or "to the competitor"
    • gamayāmasi: "we shall lead" or "we shall guide"

  • Contextual Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a philosophical inquiry into the nature of identity and existence. It suggests a detachment from superficial names and identities, emphasizing the importance of the essence over the label.
    • In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or groups may focus too much on titles, roles, or societal labels rather than the true qualities and values of a person or situation. For example, in a workplace, a manager may be more focused on the title of an employee rather than their actual contributions and skills.
    • The mention of "the supreme one" indicates a pursuit of higher truths or ideals, which can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, spirituality, or leadership. It encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and connection beyond surface-level interactions.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to transcend superficial identities and to seek a deeper understanding of ourselves and others. It encourages us to focus on the essence of our interactions and relationships.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in everyday life by fostering empathy, understanding, and a focus on shared values rather than differences. It serves as a reminder to look beyond names and titles to appreciate the true nature of individuals and situations.
    • Ultimately, this mantra invites us to lead ourselves and others towards higher ideals, promoting unity and deeper connections in a world often divided by superficial distinctions.
