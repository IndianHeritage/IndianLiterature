मातुर दिधिषुम अब्रवम् स्वसुर जारः श्रिणोतु नः |भ्रातेंद्रस्य सखा मम ||
mātur didhiṣum abravaṁ svasur jāraḥ śṛṇotu naḥ | bhrātendrasya sakhā mama ||
  • mātur didhiṣum abravaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "I speak the intention of my mother." Here, the term "mātur" refers to "mother," and "didhiṣum" implies a desire or intention. This highlights the importance of maternal guidance and wisdom in one's life.
  • svasur jāraḥ śṛṇotu naḥ: This translates to "May my mother-in-law listen to us." The word "svasur" means "mother-in-law," and "jāraḥ" refers to a companion or friend. This part emphasizes the significance of familial relationships and the need for understanding and communication within the family structure.
  • bhrātendrasya sakhā mama: This means "the friend of my brother." "Bhrātendrasya" refers to "brother" and "sakhā" means "friend." This indicates the value of brotherhood and friendship, suggesting that these relationships are vital for support and companionship.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of familial bonds, emphasizing the roles of mothers, mothers-in-law, and brothers in our lives. It reflects the importance of communication, understanding, and support within these relationships.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary society, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In family gatherings, where open communication fosters stronger relationships.
    • In conflict resolution, where understanding the perspectives of mothers and mothers-in-law can lead to harmony.
    • In friendships, where the support of brothers and close friends can help navigate life's challenges.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the foundational role that family and close relationships play in our lives. By valuing and nurturing these connections, we can create a supportive environment that enhances our well-being and fosters personal growth.
