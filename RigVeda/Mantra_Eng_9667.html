सत्येनोत्तभिता भूमिः सूर्येणोत्तभिता द्यौः |ऋतेनादित्यास्तिष्ठन्ति दिवि सोमो अधि श्रितः ||
satyenottabhitā bhūmiḥ sūryeṇottabhitā dyauḥ | ṛtenādityās tiṣṭhanti divi somo adhi śritaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satyena: By truth
    • uttabhitā: Established or raised
    • bhūmiḥ: The earth
    • sūryeṇa: By the sun
    • uttabhitā: Established or raised
    • dyauḥ: The sky or heaven
    • ṛtena: By the cosmic order or truth
    • ādityās: The sun gods
    • tiṣṭhanti: Stand or exist
    • divi: In the heavens
    • somo: The moon
    • adhi: Above or upon
    • śritaḥ: Dependent or resting
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the establishment of the earth and the sky through truth and cosmic order.
    • It highlights the significance of the sun and moon, representing the balance of energies in the universe.
    • The mantra emphasizes that the earth is grounded in truth, while the heavens are upheld by the sun and the moon, symbolizing the harmony of natural forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Environmental Awareness: Just as the earth is established by truth, we must strive for sustainable practices that honor the truth of our environment. For instance, using renewable energy sources like solar power reflects the sun's role in this mantra.
    • Personal Integrity: In our daily lives, living truthfully and with integrity can lead to a solid foundation, much like the earth established by truth. For example, being honest in our relationships fosters trust and stability.
    • Balance in Life: The interplay of the sun and moon can be seen in the balance of work and rest in our lives. Just as the sun provides energy and the moon offers calm, we must balance our efforts with moments of reflection and relaxation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of truth and cosmic order in establishing a harmonious existence.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions should align with the principles of truth and balance, both in our personal lives and in our interactions with the world.
    • By embodying these values, we contribute to a more stable and harmonious society, reflecting the eternal truths represented in the RigVeda.
