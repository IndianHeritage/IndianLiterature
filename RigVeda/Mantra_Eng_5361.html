एव न इन्द्र वार्यस्य पूर्धि प्र ते महीं सुमतिं वेविदाम |इषं पिन्व मगवद्भ्यः सुवीरां यूयम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
evā na indra vāryasya pūrdhi pra te mahīṁ sumatiṁ vevidāma | iṣam pinva maghavadbhyaḥ suvīrāṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • evā na indra vāryasya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, O Indra, of great power." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. In real life, this can be likened to seeking the support of a powerful leader or a guiding force in challenging situations.
  • pūrdhi pra te mahīṁ: This means "we seek your abundant blessings." It reflects the human desire for prosperity and abundance. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people pray for success in their endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal growth.
  • sumatiṁ vevidāma: This translates to "we understand the good thoughts." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and positive thinking. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of mindfulness and the impact of a positive mindset on one's life and decisions.
  • iṣam pinva maghavadbhyaḥ: This phrase means "may you bestow strength upon us." It signifies the need for support and strength in overcoming obstacles. In real life, this can be compared to teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to support each other in achieving common goals.
  • suvīrāṁ yūyam pāta: This translates to "may you protect the brave." It highlights the value of courage and bravery in facing challenges. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the recognition of individuals who stand up for justice, equality, and truth, often at great personal risk.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you always grant us well-being." It reflects a universal wish for health and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to the importance of community support systems, healthcare, and the pursuit of a balanced life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, wisdom, and protection from a higher power, represented by Indra. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the need for support in our lives, whether from divine forces or from our communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength in unity, embrace positive thinking, and strive for well-being, ultimately fostering a sense of collective responsibility and resilience in the face of challenges.
