स विद्वां अङ्गिरोभ्य इन्द्रो गा अवृणोद अप |स्तुषे तद अस्य पौंस्यम् ||
sa vidvām̐ aṅgirobhya indro gā avṛṇod apa | stuṣe tad asya pauṁsyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he
    • vidvām̐: the wise or knowledgeable
    • aṅgirobhya: from the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • gā: song or hymn
    • avṛṇod: has chosen or selected
    • apa: for us
    • stuṣe: for praise or worship
    • tad: that
    • asya: of this
    • pauṁsyam: manliness or strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine selection and empowerment by Indra, the god of strength and rain.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge, suggesting that true strength comes from understanding and enlightenment.
    • The reference to Angiras, a group of wise sages, indicates that wisdom is often passed down through generations and is a collective effort.
    • The act of praising or worshiping is seen as a way to invoke divine blessings and strength in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who seeks knowledge and wisdom from experienced mentors (akin to the Angiras) can make better decisions, leading to the success of the organization.
    • In personal life, individuals who engage in continuous learning and self-improvement often find themselves more resilient and capable of overcoming challenges, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • Communities that come together to support and uplift each other, much like the collective wisdom of sages, can create a stronger, more harmonious environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, collective strength, and the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and resilience in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in physical strength but in the knowledge and wisdom we cultivate.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, community support, and a deeper connection with the divine.
