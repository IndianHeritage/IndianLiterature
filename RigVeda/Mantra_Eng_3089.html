सुकर्माणः सुरुचो देवयन्तो यो न देवाः जनिमा धमन्तः |शुचन्तो अग्निं ववृद्धन्त इन्द्रं ऊर्वं गव्यं परिषदन्तो अग्मन ||
sukarmāṇaḥ suruco devayanto 'yo na devā janimā dhamantaḥ | śucanto agniṁ vavṛdhanta indram ūrvaṁ gavyam pariṣadanto agman ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sukarmāṇaḥ: Those who perform good deeds
    • suruco: Beautiful in form
    • devayanto: Those who are devoted to the divine
    • 'yo: Who
    • na: Not
    • devā: The gods
    • janimā: Born
    • dhamantaḥ: Those who are righteous
    • śucanto: Pure
    • agniṁ: Fire
    • vavṛdhanta: They increase
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • ūrvaṁ: The earth
    • gavyam: Cow, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • pariṣadanto: Those who gather
    • agman: They come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of individuals who are righteous and devoted to the divine, emphasizing the importance of good deeds and purity.
    • The mention of "sukarmāṇaḥ" highlights the value of performing virtuous actions in our daily lives, such as helping others, being honest, and contributing positively to society.
    • "Surucho" suggests that those who embody these qualities are not only virtuous but also possess a beautiful character, which can be seen in people who inspire others through their actions.
    • The reference to "agniṁ" (fire) symbolizes transformation and purity, indicating that righteous actions can lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and leadership, suggesting that those who are righteous and pure can attain great heights and influence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A community leader who organizes charitable events to help the underprivileged embodies the qualities of "sukarmāṇaḥ" and "devayanto."
    • Teachers who inspire their students to pursue knowledge and ethical behavior reflect the essence of "surucho" and "śucanto."
    • Environmental activists working towards a sustainable future demonstrate the importance of purity and righteousness in their actions, akin to the qualities described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of living a life of virtue, purity, and devotion to the divine.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in good deeds, foster a sense of community, and strive for personal growth.
    • By embodying these principles, one can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, reflecting the ideals presented in the RigVeda.
