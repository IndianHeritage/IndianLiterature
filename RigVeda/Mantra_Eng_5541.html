आ नो दधिक्राह् पथ्याम् अनक्त्व ऋतस्य पन्थाम् अन्वेतवा उ |श्रिणोतु नो दैव्यम् शर्धो अग्निः श्रिण्वन्तु विश्वे महिषा अमूराः ||
ā no dadhikrāḥ pathyām anaktv ṛtasya panthām anvetavā u | śṛṇotu no daivyaṁ śardho agniḥ śṛṇvantu viśve mahiṣā amūrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we (us)
    • dadhikrāḥ: be endowed (or be nourished)
    • pathyām: with the path (or way)
    • anaktv: of the night (or darkness)
    • ṛtasya: of truth (or righteousness)
    • panthām: the path (or way)
    • anvetavā: may it lead us (or guide us)
    • u: and
    • śṛṇotu: may (he) hear (or listen)
    • no: our
    • daivyaṁ: divine (or heavenly)
    • śardho: strength (or power)
    • agniḥ: O Agni (the fire god)
    • śṛṇvantu: may they listen (or hear)
    • viśve: all (or universal)
    • mahiṣā: great (or mighty)
    • amūrāḥ: immortal (or eternal)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and enlightenment.
    • It seeks nourishment and support on the righteous path, emphasizing the importance of truth and virtue.
    • The invocation of Agni symbolizes the divine light that dispels darkness and ignorance.
    • It calls upon all divine beings to listen and assist in the journey towards truth and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a moral dilemma at work, one might recite this mantra to seek clarity and guidance.
    • Students preparing for exams may use this mantra to invoke focus and the strength to pursue knowledge.
    • During community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to foster unity and collective pursuit of truth and righteousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and support in the pursuit of truth and righteousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals seek clarity, strength, and direction in their lives.
    • By invoking the divine and seeking the path of righteousness, individuals can navigate challenges with wisdom and integrity.
