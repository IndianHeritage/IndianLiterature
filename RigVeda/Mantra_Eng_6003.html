मम त्वा सूर उदिते मम मध्यंदिनि दिवः |मम प्रपित्वे अपिशर्वरे वसव आ स्तोमासो अवृत्सत ||
mama tvā sūra udite mama madhyaṁdine divaḥ | mama prapitve apiśarvare vasav ā stomāso avṛtsata ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Surya (Sun), you rise for me in the morning; in the midday, you shine for me. Even in the evening, may the Vasus (deities of abundance) bless me with their presence."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mama: my
    • tvā: you
    • sūra: O Sun
    • udite: rising
    • madhyaṁdine: at midday
    • divaḥ: of the day
    • prapitve: in the evening
    • apiśarvare: in the evening
    • vasav: the Vasus, deities of abundance
    • stomāso: blessings
    • avṛtsata: may they bestow
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the Sun, a symbol of energy, vitality, and life. It acknowledges the Sun's role in the cycle of day and night, emphasizing its importance in daily life.
    • The mention of the Vasus signifies a request for abundance and prosperity, highlighting the interconnectedness of nature and divine forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to the Sun for favorable weather conditions, as sunlight is crucial for crop growth. This mantra can be recited to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In daily life, individuals may recite this mantra during morning rituals to invoke positivity and energy for the day ahead, recognizing the Sun's role in providing light and warmth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the Sun as a source of life and abundance. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the natural cycles and the divine forces that support our existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as agriculture, personal well-being, and spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to seek harmony with nature and the universe.
