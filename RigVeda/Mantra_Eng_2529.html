अञ्जन्ति त्वां अध्वरे देवयन्तो वनस्पते मधुनā दैव्यम् |यद ऊर्ध्वस तिष्ठा द्रविणेह धत्ताद यद वा क्षयो मातुर अस्याऽ उपस्थे ||
añjanti tvām adhvare devayanto vanaspate madhunā daivyena | yad ūrdhvas tiṣṭhā draviṇeha dhattād yad vā kṣayo mātur asyā upasthe ||
  • añjanti tvām adhvare: This phrase can be translated as "They invoke you in the rituals." Here, "añjanti" refers to the act of invoking or honoring, while "adhvare" pertains to the sacred rituals performed in Vedic traditions. In real life, this can be likened to how people today participate in religious ceremonies, seeking blessings or guidance from a higher power.
  • devayanto vanaspate: This translates to "O divine one, the lord of the forest." "Devayanto" signifies the divine aspect, and "vanaspate" refers to the lord of plants or trees. This can be related to the reverence for nature and the environment in contemporary society, where many cultures celebrate Earth Day or engage in conservation efforts, recognizing the divine presence in nature.
  • madhunā daivyena: This means "with sweetness and divinity." "Madhunā" refers to sweetness, often symbolizing the positive and nurturing aspects of life, while "daivyena" indicates a divine quality. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and compassion, where individuals strive to bring sweetness into their interactions with others.
  • yad ūrdhvas tiṣṭhā: This translates to "that which stands above." "Yad" means "that," "ūrdhvas" refers to "above," and "tiṣṭhā" means "stands." This can be interpreted as striving for higher ideals or aspirations in life, akin to setting personal goals or seeking enlightenment.
  • draviṇeha dhattād: This means "may it grant wealth." "Draviṇeha" refers to wealth or resources, and "dhattād" implies the act of granting or bestowing. In a modern context, this can relate to the pursuit of prosperity and abundance, whether in financial terms or in the richness of experiences and relationships.
  • yad vā kṣayo mātur asyā upasthe: This translates to "or may it protect the womb of the mother." "Yad vā" means "or may," "kṣayo" refers to protection or preservation, "mātur" means "of the mother," and "asyā upasthe" indicates "in her womb." This highlights the importance of motherhood and the nurturing aspect of life, which can be seen in contemporary discussions around maternal health and the sanctity of life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human aspirations. It calls for the invocation of divine blessings in rituals, the reverence for nature, the pursuit of higher ideals, and the protection of motherhood. In real life, these themes resonate with contemporary values such as environmental conservation, compassion, and the importance of family. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message that encourages individuals to seek harmony with the divine, nurture their relationships, and strive for a prosperous and meaningful life.
