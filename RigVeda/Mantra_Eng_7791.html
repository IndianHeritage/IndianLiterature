हस्तच्युतैर्भिराद्रिभिः सुतं सोमं पुनीतन |मधावाद्धावतामधु ||
hastacyutebhir adribhiḥ sutaṁ somam punītana | madhāv ā dhāvatā madhu ||
  • Mantra: "hastacyutebhir adribhiḥ sutaṁ somam punītana | madhāv ā dhāvatā madhu ||"
  • Translation: "With hands that are free from sin, let us bring forth the pure Soma, which flows like honey."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Hastacyutebhir: Refers to hands that are not tainted by sin or wrongdoing.
    • Adribhiḥ: Means 'from the mountains' or 'from the rocks', indicating a source of purity and strength.
    • Sutaṁ: Refers to 'the one who is poured out', often associated with the Soma drink, which is considered sacred.
    • Somam: The divine drink, Soma, which is believed to confer immortality and enlightenment.
    • Punītana: Means 'pure' or 'purifying', emphasizing the importance of purity in the offering.
    • Madhāv: Refers to 'honey', symbolizing sweetness and nourishment.
    • Ā dhāvatā: Means 'let it flow' or 'let it come forth', indicating a desire for abundance.
    • Madhu: Also means 'honey', representing the sweetness of life and the rewards of virtuous actions.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purity and virtuous actions in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, suggesting that pure intentions lead to divine rewards.
    • The reference to Soma signifies the pursuit of knowledge, enlightenment, and the essence of life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in actions that are morally and ethically sound, ensuring that their 'hands' are free from wrongdoing.
    • It encourages the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to seeking the 'Soma' of life, which can be interpreted as personal growth and enlightenment.
    • The metaphor of honey can be applied to relationships and interactions, reminding us to cultivate sweetness and positivity in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity in our actions and thoughts.
    • It encourages us to seek knowledge and enlightenment while fostering positive relationships.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life, contributing to our personal growth and the well-being of those around us.
