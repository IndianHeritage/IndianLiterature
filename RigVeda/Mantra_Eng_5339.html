न ते गिरो अपि मृष्ये तुरस्य न सुष्टुतिम असूर्यस्य विद्वान् |सदा ते नाम स्वयशो विवक्मि ||
na te giro api mṛṣye turasya na suṣṭutim asuryasya vidvān | sadā te nāma svayaśo vivakmi ||
  • na te giro api mṛṣye: This phrase translates to "Your words are not false." It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness in speech. In real life, this can be seen in the context of personal relationships, where honesty is crucial for building trust. For example, in a friendship, if one person consistently tells the truth, it strengthens their bond and fosters a deeper connection.
  • turasya na suṣṭutim asuryasya vidvān: This part means "The wise one does not praise the non-divine." It suggests that true wisdom lies in recognizing and appreciating what is divine or virtuous, rather than being swayed by superficial or materialistic values. In a modern context, this can relate to the idea of valuing integrity and character over wealth or status. For instance, a leader who prioritizes ethical practices over profit maximization exemplifies this wisdom.
  • sadā te nāma svayaśo vivakmi: This translates to "I always speak of your name and glory." It reflects the idea of devotion and the importance of acknowledging and celebrating the virtues of the divine or the admirable qualities in others. In everyday life, this can be seen in practices such as gratitude journaling, where individuals reflect on and express appreciation for the positive aspects of their lives and the people around them.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truthfulness, wisdom, and devotion. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their interactions and decisions, promoting a culture of honesty, ethical behavior, and appreciation for the good in others. By embodying these values, one can contribute to a more harmonious and respectful society, fostering deeper connections and a sense of community.
