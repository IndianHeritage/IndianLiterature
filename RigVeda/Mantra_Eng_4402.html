स नो विभावा चक्षणिर्न वस्तोर अग्निर वन्दारु वेद्यश्चनो धात् |विश्वायुर्यो अमृतो मर्त्येषूषर्भुद भूद अतिथिर जातवेदाः ||
sa no vibhāvā cakṣaṇir na vastor agnir vandāru vedyaś cano dhāt | viśvāyur yo amṛto martyeṣūṣarbhud bhūd atithir jātavedāḥ ||
  • sa no vibhāvā cakṣaṇir: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine light illuminate us." Here, "vibhāvā" refers to the divine or the illuminating force, while "cakṣaṇir" means "to see" or "to perceive." In real life, this can be understood as a call for clarity and understanding in our thoughts and actions.
  • na vastor agnir: This translates to "the fire of knowledge." "Agnir" refers to fire, which symbolizes knowledge and transformation. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of education and wisdom, where knowledge acts as a transformative force in our lives.
  • vandāru vedyaś cano dhāt: This means "may we be worthy of the blessings of the universe." "Vandāru" implies reverence, while "vedyaś" refers to the knowledge or wisdom that is to be attained. This can be seen in our daily lives when we seek to respect and learn from the world around us, acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • viśvāyur yo amṛto: This translates to "the one who grants universal life." "Viśvāyur" means universal life, and "amṛto" refers to immortality or eternal existence. In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing life and promoting well-being for all, emphasizing the value of health and longevity.
  • martyeṣūṣarbhud: This phrase means "among mortals." It highlights our human condition and the transient nature of life. In practical terms, it serves as a reminder to cherish our time on Earth and to live meaningfully, recognizing the impermanence of life.
  • bhūd atithir jātavedāḥ: This translates to "may we be guests of the universe, enlightened by knowledge." "Atithir" refers to a guest, and "jātavedāḥ" means one who is knowledgeable or wise. This can be related to the idea of being humble and open to learning from every experience and interaction we have in life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, clarity, and understanding in our lives. It encourages us to respect the wisdom of the universe and to live in harmony with all beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in education, personal growth, and fostering relationships. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can strive to create a more enlightened and compassionate world.
