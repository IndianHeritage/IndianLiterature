जज्ञानाṁ सप्त मातरो वेधाम अशासत श्रीये |अयं ध्रुवो रयीणां चिकेत यत ||
jajñānaṁ sapta mātaro vedhām aśāsata śriye | ayaṁ dhruvo rayīṇāṁ ciketa yat ||
  • jajñānaṁ: This word translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." It signifies the importance of understanding and enlightenment in life.
  • sapta: Meaning "seven," this number often represents completeness or a holistic approach in Vedic literature.
  • mātaro: This translates to "mothers," symbolizing nurturing, care, and the foundational aspects of life that support growth and development.
  • vedhām: This word means "to know" or "to perceive," indicating the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of awareness in our lives.
  • aśāsata: This can be interpreted as "to establish" or "to create," emphasizing the role of knowledge in building a stable and prosperous life.
  • śriye: This word means "prosperity" or "wealth," highlighting the connection between knowledge and material success.
  • ayaṁ: Translates to "this," indicating a focus on the present and the immediate relevance of the mantra.
  • dhruvo: Meaning "constant" or "eternal," it suggests that true knowledge and wisdom are unchanging and reliable.
  • rayīṇāṁ: This word means "wealth" or "resources," pointing to the material aspects of life that are influenced by knowledge.
  • ciketa: This translates to "to seek" or "to understand," emphasizing the active pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • yat: Meaning "that" or "which," it refers to the knowledge and wisdom being discussed in the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge and wisdom as foundational elements in achieving prosperity and stability in life. It highlights the nurturing role of mothers and the completeness represented by the number seven, suggesting that a holistic approach to learning and understanding is essential. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and family dynamics. By valuing knowledge and nurturing relationships, individuals can create a prosperous and fulfilling life. The mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom and understanding, which are vital for long-term success and happiness.
