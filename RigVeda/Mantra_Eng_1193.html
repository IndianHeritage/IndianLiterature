युवाम इन्द्राग्नī वसुनो विभागे तवस्तमा शुश्रव वृत्रहत्ये |ताव आसद्यā बरहिषि यज्ञे अस्मिन प्र चर्षणी मादयेतां सुतस्य ||
yuvām indrāgnī vasuno vibhāge tavastamā śuśrava vṛtrahatye | tāv āsadyā barhiṣi yajñe asmin pra carṣaṇī mādayethāṁ sutasya ||
  • yuvām: This word translates to "you two," indicating a duality or partnership. In real life, this can be seen in relationships, teamwork, or collaborations where two entities work together towards a common goal.
  • indrāgnī: Referring to the deities Indra and Agni, symbolizing strength and fire, respectively. Indra represents power and leadership, while Agni signifies transformation and energy. In everyday life, this can be related to the balance of strength and passion in achieving success.
  • vasuno: This term means "of the wealth" or "prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of abundance in life. For instance, in a business context, wealth can be seen as not just financial gain but also the richness of experiences and relationships.
  • vibhāge: Meaning "in the division" or "in the distribution." This can relate to the fair distribution of resources and opportunities in society, highlighting the need for equity and justice in various aspects of life.
  • tavastamā: This translates to "your greatness" or "your power." It signifies the recognition of one's potential and capabilities. In personal development, acknowledging one's strengths is crucial for growth and self-improvement.
  • śuśrava: Meaning "heard" or "listened to." This emphasizes the importance of communication and understanding in relationships. In real life, effective listening can lead to better connections and conflict resolution.
  • vṛtrahatye: Referring to the "slaying of Vritra," a metaphor for overcoming obstacles. This can be applied to personal challenges, where individuals must confront and overcome their fears or difficulties to achieve their goals.
  • tāv: Meaning "those two," reinforcing the idea of partnership and collaboration. This can be seen in various partnerships, such as business alliances or friendships, where mutual support is essential.
  • āsadyā: This term means "having gathered" or "assembled." It signifies the importance of coming together for a common purpose, such as community gatherings or team meetings that foster collaboration.
  • barhiṣi: Referring to "the sacred grass" used in rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity. In real life, this can relate to the importance of maintaining integrity and ethical standards in our actions.
  • yajñe: Meaning "in the sacrifice" or "in the offering." This highlights the significance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good, such as volunteering or charitable acts.
  • asmin: This translates to "in this," indicating presence and participation in the moment. It encourages mindfulness and being present in our actions and interactions.
  • pra carṣaṇī: Meaning "the sacred chant" or "the invocation." This emphasizes the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality, akin to affirmations or positive thinking in personal development.
  • mādayethāṁ: This term means "may you be pleased" or "may you be joyful." It reflects the desire for happiness and fulfillment in life, which can be pursued through personal passions and relationships.
  • sutasya: Referring to "of the offspring" or "of the children." This highlights the importance of nurturing future generations and the legacy we leave behind, emphasizing education and mentorship.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of partnership, strength, prosperity, and overcoming obstacles. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of collaboration in achieving common goals. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, nurturing relationships, and contributing to the community. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence, both personally and collectively.
