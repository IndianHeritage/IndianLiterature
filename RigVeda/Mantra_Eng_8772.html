दिवः पीयूषम् पूर्व्यं यदुक्त्यम महो गाहाद दिव आ निर अधुक्षत |इन्द्रम् अभि जातमानं सम अस्वरन् ||
divaḥ pīyūṣam pūrvyaṁ yad ukthyam maho gāhād diva ā nir adhukṣata | indram abhi jāyamānaṁ sam asvaran ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divaḥ: Of the heavens
    • Pīyūṣam: Nectar or essence
    • Pūrvyaṁ: Former or previous
    • Yad: That which
    • Ukthyam: Is spoken or uttered
    • Mahā: Great
    • Gāhād: From the great
    • Diva: Heaven
    • Ā: Indeed
    • Nir: Without
    • Adhukṣata: Being restrained or held back
    • Indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • Abhi: Towards
    • Jāyamānaṁ: Being born or generated
    • Sam: Together
    • Asvaran: To be made to sound or resonate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence of the heavens, referring to the divine nectar that is spoken of in ancient texts.
    • The phrase "that which is spoken" suggests the power of words and intentions in manifesting reality.
    • The mention of Indra indicates the importance of divine support and strength in our endeavors.
    • The idea of being "without restraint" emphasizes the need for freedom and openness in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to speak positively and with intention, as our words shape our reality.
    • In leadership, invoking the strength of Indra can symbolize the need for courage and decisiveness in guiding others.
    • In creative endeavors, the essence of the heavens can inspire artists and thinkers to break free from limitations and express their true selves.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine essence, the power of spoken words, and the importance of freedom in our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength and to communicate with purpose, allowing us to manifest our desires and aspirations.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal growth to leadership and creativity, the teachings of this mantra can guide us towards a more fulfilling existence.
