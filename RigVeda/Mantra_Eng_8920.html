इदं पितृभ्यो नमो अस्त्व अद्य ये पूर्वासो य उपरास इयुः |ये पार्थिवे रजस्य आ निषत्तā ये वा नूनं सुवृजनासु विक्षु ||
idam pitṛbhyo namo astv adya ye pūrvāso ya uparāsa īyuḥ | ye pārthive rajasy ā niṣattā ye vā nūnaṁ suvṛjanāsu vikṣu ||
  • Translation: "To these ancestors, I offer my salutations today, those who have gone before and those who are above. Those who are the rulers of the earthly realm, who are indeed the well-wishers of the prosperous."
  • Breakdown of Key Terms:
    • idam: This means "this" or "here," indicating a present moment of reverence.
    • pitṛbhyo: Refers to "ancestors" or "forefathers," highlighting the importance of lineage and heritage.
    • namo astv: A phrase of salutation, meaning "salutations to" or "I bow to."
    • adya: Means "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the offering.
    • ye pūrvāso: "Those who have gone before," referring to the ancestors who have passed away.
    • ya uparāsa: "Those who are above," possibly referring to spiritual beings or deities.
    • īyuḥ: Indicates "who are present" or "who exist," suggesting a connection to the living and the spiritual.
    • ye pārthive rajasy: "Those who are the rulers of the earthly realm," referring to powerful figures or deities that govern the material world.
    • ā niṣattā: "Who are indeed," reinforcing the certainty of their existence and influence.
    • ye vā nūnaṁ: "Those who are indeed," further emphasizing the importance of these figures.
    • suvṛjanāsu: "Well-wishers of the prosperous," indicating those who support and nurture prosperity.
    • vikṣu: "In the prosperous," suggesting a connection to abundance and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, honoring ancestors is a common practice. For instance, during the Day of the Dead in Mexico, families create altars to remember and celebrate their deceased loved ones, reflecting the same reverence expressed in this mantra.
    • In modern society, the concept of mentorship can be seen as a parallel to the idea of ancestors guiding the living. Successful individuals often credit their mentors or predecessors for their achievements, acknowledging the influence of those who came before them.
    • In business, leaders often pay homage to the foundational figures of their industry, recognizing their contributions and the legacy they left behind, similar to the respect shown to ancestors in this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on respect for ancestors and the acknowledgment of their influence in our lives. It serves as a reminder to honor our roots and the wisdom passed down through generations. In real life, this can manifest in various ways, such as family traditions, cultural practices, and the recognition of those who have paved the way for our current successes. By invoking the blessings of our ancestors and recognizing the interconnectedness of life, we cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards our heritage and future.
