मन्‍त्र: मनुष्वत् त्वा न धीमहि मनुष्वत् सम इधीमहि | अग्ने मनुष्वद् अङ्गिरो देवान् देवयते यज ||
manuṣvat tvā ni dhīmahi manuṣvat sam idhīmahi | agne manuṣvad aṅgiro devān devayate yaja ||
  • manuṣvat tvā ni dhīmahi: This phrase can be translated as "We meditate upon you, O Manushvat." The term "manuṣvat" refers to the quality of being human or possessing human-like qualities. It emphasizes the importance of humanity in spiritual practices.
  • manuṣvat sam idhīmahi: This translates to "We invoke you, O Manushvat." Here, the act of invocation signifies a call for guidance and support from the divine, highlighting the connection between humans and the divine.
  • agne manuṣvad aṅgiro: This part translates to "O Agni, the divine fire, we call upon you." Agni represents the sacred fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • devān devayate yaja: This means "Worship the gods with devotion." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and worship in connecting with the divine forces that govern the universe.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence and the relationship between humanity and the divine. It encourages individuals to recognize their human qualities and seek guidance from higher powers.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, during challenging times, individuals can meditate on their human qualities, such as compassion and resilience, while seeking divine guidance through prayer or meditation. It can also be used in rituals to invoke positive energies and foster a sense of community through collective worship.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine. By acknowledging our human qualities and seeking divine support, we can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose. It highlights the importance of devotion and the transformative power of spiritual practices in our lives.
