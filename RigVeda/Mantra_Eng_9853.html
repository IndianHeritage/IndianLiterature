कृधī नो अह्रयो देव सविताḥ स च स्तुषे मग्नाम् |सहो न इन्द्रो वह्निभिर न येषां चर्षणीनां चक्रं रश्मिं न योयुवे ||
kṛdhī no ahrayo deva savitaḥ sa ca stuṣe maghonām | saho na indro vahnibhir ny eṣāṁ carṣaṇīnāṁ cakraṁ raśmiṁ na yoyuve ||
  • kṛdhī no ahrayo deva savitaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine Savita, the Sun God, inspire us." Here, "Savita" represents the sun, which is often associated with energy, life, and illumination. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of seeking inspiration and guidance from higher powers or natural forces, much like how we look to the sun for light and warmth.
  • sa ca stuṣe maghonām: This translates to "and may He be pleased with our offerings." This line emphasizes the significance of gratitude and respect towards divine forces. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we express appreciation for the resources and opportunities we receive, whether through rituals, personal reflection, or acts of kindness.
  • saho na indro vahnibhir: This means "may Indra, the king of gods, protect us with his fiery strength." Indra is often associated with power and protection. In a modern context, this can be likened to seeking strength and support from leaders or mentors in our lives who guide us through challenges.
  • ny eṣāṁ carṣaṇīnāṁ cakraṁ raśmiṁ na yoyuve: This translates to "may we not be deprived of the circle of prosperity." The "circle of prosperity" symbolizes abundance and well-being. In real life, this can be interpreted as striving for a balanced and fulfilling life, ensuring that we maintain our health, relationships, and resources.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for inspiration, gratitude, protection, and prosperity. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for living a balanced life, where one seeks divine guidance, expresses appreciation, relies on strength from others, and aims for abundance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in daily practices such as meditation, gratitude journaling, and community building, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and with each other.
