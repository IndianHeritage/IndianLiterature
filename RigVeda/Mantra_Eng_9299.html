प्र नेमस्मिन ददृशे सोमो अन्तर गोपा नेमम् आवीर अस्त ह कृत्नोति |स तिग्मशृङ्गं वृषभं युयुत्सन् द्रुहस तस्थौ बहुले बद्धो अन्तः ||
pra nemasmin dadṛśe somo antar gopā nemam āvir asthā kṛṇoti | sa tigmaśṛṅgaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ yuyutsan druhas tasthau bahule baddho antaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pra nemasmin dadṛśe somo antar gopā nemam āvir asthā kṛṇoti | sa tigmaśṛṅgaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ yuyutsan druhas tasthau bahule baddho antaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the divine essence of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, which is perceived in the inner self. It describes the presence of a powerful bull, symbolizing strength and vitality, ready to engage in battle, bound by the forces of nature.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra nemasmin: "in this direction" or "towards this goal."
    • dadṛśe: "is seen" or "is perceived."
    • somo: "Soma," representing the divine nectar or essence.
    • antar: "within" or "inside."
    • gopā: "the protector" or "the guardian."
    • nemam: "this" or "this essence."
    • āvir: "manifest" or "appear."
    • asthā: "established" or "firm."
    • kṛṇoti: "creates" or "makes."
    • sa: "he" or "that one."
    • tigmaśṛṅgaṁ: "with sharp horns," symbolizing strength.
    • vṛṣabhaṁ: "the bull," representing power and vitality.
    • yuyutsan: "engaged in battle" or "ready to fight."
    • druhas: "the enemy" or "the adversary."
    • tasthau: "stood" or "remained."
    • bahule: "in abundance" or "in plenty."
    • baddho: "bound" or "tied."
    • antaḥ: "within" or "inside."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of Soma can be likened to the pursuit of inner peace and spiritual fulfillment. Just as Soma is a divine essence, individuals often seek experiences that nourish their soul, such as meditation or nature walks.
    • The bull symbolizes strength and resilience. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges head-on, like athletes training for competitions or leaders guiding their teams through difficult times.
    • The idea of being "bound" can relate to commitments we make in life, such as family responsibilities or professional obligations, which require us to stand firm and face adversities.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength, the pursuit of spiritual essence, and the readiness to confront challenges. In our daily lives, it serves as a reminder to seek inner peace, embrace our strengths, and remain steadfast in the face of adversity. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's battles with resilience and purpose.
