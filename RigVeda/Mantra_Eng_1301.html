अश्वं न गूढम् अश्विना दुरेवैर ऋषिं नराः वृषणा रेबहम् अप्सु |सं तं ऋणीथो विपृतं दंसोभिः न वां जूर्यन्ति पूर्व्या कृतानि ||
aśvaṁ na gūḻham aśvinā durevair ṛṣiṁ narā vṛṣaṇā rebham apsu | saṁ taṁ riṇītho viprutaṁ daṁsobhir na vāṁ jūryanti pūrvyā kṛtāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvaṁ: horse
    • na: not
    • gūḻham: hidden
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods)
    • durevair: by the far-reaching
    • ṛṣiṁ: sage
    • narā: men
    • vṛṣaṇā: by the strong
    • rebham: the one who is powerful
    • apsu: in the waters
    • saṁ: together
    • taṁ: that
    • riṇītho: may we invoke
    • viprutaṁ: the one who is open
    • daṁsobhir: with the sounds
    • na: not
    • vāṁ: you
    • jūryanti: may they be harmed
    • pūrvyā: by the past
    • kṛtāni: deeds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking the divine powers of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • The phrase "aśvaṁ na gūḻham" suggests that the strength and vitality (symbolized by the horse) should not be hidden, indicating the importance of revealing one's true potential.
    • The mention of "durevair ṛṣiṁ narā" emphasizes the connection between humans and sages, suggesting that wisdom and strength can be achieved through divine guidance.
    • The line "saṁ taṁ riṇītho viprutaṁ" indicates a collective invocation for protection and support, highlighting the importance of community and collaboration.
    • The concluding part warns against the negative influences of past deeds, suggesting that one should strive to overcome past mistakes and seek a path of righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when a team comes together to tackle a challenging project, invoking the collective strength and wisdom of its members.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their past actions and seek to improve themselves, much like the mantra encourages overcoming past deeds.
    • In health and wellness, the invocation of the Ashvins can symbolize seeking healing and vitality, reminding individuals to take care of their physical and mental well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support and wisdom in our lives.
    • It teaches us to reveal our true potential, work collaboratively, and learn from our past to create a better future.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that seeking guidance from higher powers can lead to strength and healing.
