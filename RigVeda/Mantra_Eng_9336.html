साध्वीम् अकर देववीतिं नो अद्य यज्ञस्य जिव्हाम् अविदाम् गुह्याम् |स आयुर आगात सुरभिर वसानो भद्राम् अकर देवहूतिं नो अद्य ||
sādhvīm akar devavītiṁ no adya yajñasya jihvām avidāma guhyām | sa āyur āgāt surabhir vasāno bhadrām akar devahūtiṁ no adya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sādhvīm: auspicious, good, or well-formed
    • akar: created or made
    • devavītiṁ: divine or godly offering
    • no: our
    • adya: today
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice or ritual
    • jihvām: tongue or speech
    • avidāma: we know or we understand
    • guhyām: secret or hidden
    • sa: he or that
    • āyur: life or longevity
    • āgāt: may come or arrive
    • surabhir: fragrant or pleasing
    • vasāno: dwelling or residing
    • bhadrām: auspicious or beneficial
    • akar: created or made
    • devahūtiṁ: divine offering or sacrifice
    • no: our
    • adya: today
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of creating auspicious offerings to the divine.
    • It suggests that through our speech and actions, we can invoke divine blessings and understanding.
    • The reference to "life" and "fragrance" indicates that a life lived in harmony with divine principles is pleasing and beneficial.
    • The mantra calls for awareness of the hidden aspects of life and spirituality, encouraging us to seek deeper understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied by engaging in acts of kindness and generosity, which can be seen as offerings to the divine.
    • Practicing mindfulness in our speech can lead to more harmonious relationships, reflecting the idea of creating auspicious communication.
    • Incorporating rituals or practices that honor the divine, such as meditation or prayer, can help us connect with the deeper meanings of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to create a life filled with auspicious actions and divine offerings.
    • It teaches us that our words and actions have the power to invoke blessings and understanding, both for ourselves and others.
    • In a world often filled with chaos, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek harmony, understanding, and connection with the divine in our everyday lives.
