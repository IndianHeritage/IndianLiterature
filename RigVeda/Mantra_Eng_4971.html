तां पूष्णः सुमतिं वयं वृक्षस्य प्र वयाम इव |इन्द्रस्य चा रभामहे ||
tām pūṣṇaḥ sumatiṁ vayaṁ vṛkṣasya pra vayām iva | indrasya cā rabhāmahe ||
  • tām pūṣṇaḥ: This phrase refers to "that Pusha," where Pusha is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of seeking sustenance and support from nature and the universe.
  • sumatiṁ vayaṁ: "We seek good understanding" or "wisdom." This emphasizes the value of wisdom in decision-making and life choices. For example, in a business context, having a clear understanding of market trends can lead to successful outcomes.
  • vṛkṣasya pra vayām iva: This translates to "like the branches of a tree." Trees symbolize growth, stability, and interconnectedness. In real life, this can be seen in how communities grow and thrive together, much like the branches of a tree that support each other.
  • indrasya cā rabhāmahe: "We invoke the strength of Indra." Indra is the king of gods and represents power and strength. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking strength and courage in challenging situations, such as overcoming obstacles in personal or professional life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking nourishment, wisdom, interconnectedness, and strength. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate these qualities within ourselves and our communities. By invoking these principles, we can navigate life's challenges more effectively and foster a supportive environment for growth and prosperity.
