ज्येष्ठेन सोतार इन्द्राय सोमं वीराय शक्राय |भरā पिबन् नार्याय ||
jyeṣṭhena sotar indrāya somaṁ vīrāya śakrāya | bharā piban naryāya ||
  • Mantra: "jyeṣṭhena sotar indrāya somaṁ vīrāya śakrāya | bharā piban naryāya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "With the elder (jyeṣṭha), we offer the soma to Indra, the hero (vīra), the powerful (śakra). May he drink it, O Narya (the noble one)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • jyeṣṭhena: Refers to the elder or the most respected, symbolizing wisdom and experience.
    • sotar: Indicates the act of offering or pouring, often associated with rituals.
    • indrāya: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength and power.
    • somaṁ: Refers to soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • vīrāya: Means "to the hero," emphasizing valor and courage.
    • śakrāya: Refers to the powerful or mighty, often associated with Indra's strength.
    • bharā: Means "may you fill" or "may you drink," indicating a request for abundance.
    • piban: Means "to drink," emphasizing the act of consuming the soma.
    • naryāya: Refers to the noble or virtuous one, possibly addressing a divine or revered figure.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wisdom and respect for elders in seeking strength and guidance.
    • It emphasizes the role of divine inspiration (soma) in achieving valor and power, which can be applied in various aspects of life, such as leadership, decision-making, and personal growth.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the aspiration for overcoming challenges and achieving success through courage and strength.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can inspire leaders to seek wisdom from experienced mentors (jyeṣṭha) while making decisions that require courage (vīra) and strength (śakra).
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on the importance of learning from elders and drawing upon their experiences to navigate challenges.
    • During times of adversity, invoking the qualities of Indra can serve as a reminder to harness inner strength and courage to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and divine inspiration in our lives.
    • By honoring the elders and seeking their guidance, we can cultivate the courage and power needed to face life's challenges.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to embrace the qualities of valor and nobility in our pursuits, leading to personal and communal growth.
