त्वां जनाः ममसत्येष्व इन्द्र संतस्थाना वि हवयन्ते समीकः |अत्र युजं कृतुते यो हविष्मान नासुन्वता सख्यं वष्टि शूरः ||
tvāṁ janā mamasatyeṣv indra saṁtasthānā vi hvayante samīke | atrā yujaṁ kṛṇute yo haviṣmān nāsunvatā sakhyaṁ vaṣṭi śūraḥ ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to a divine entity, in this case, Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • janā - This means "people" or "beings," indicating the collective of humanity or a community.
  • mamasatyeṣv - This can be broken down into "mama" (my), "satya" (truth), and "eṣv" (in these), suggesting a connection between the divine and the truth held by individuals.
  • indra - Refers to Indra, the god of rain, thunder, and war, symbolizing strength and power.
  • saṁtasthānā - This word means "who is established" or "who stands firm," indicating stability and reliability.
  • vi - This is a prefix that can mean "in" or "with," often used to denote a relationship or connection.
  • hvayante - This means "invoke" or "call upon," suggesting a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • samīke - This translates to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," indicating a communal aspect of worship or invocation.
  • atrā - Meaning "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the immediacy of the invocation.
  • yujaṁ - This means "to unite" or "to join," suggesting a coming together of people or energies.
  • kṛṇute - This translates to "makes" or "creates," indicating an action of bringing something into existence.
  • yo - Meaning "who," it introduces a subject that is being described.
  • haviṣmān - This refers to "the one who offers," often associated with sacrifices or offerings to the divine.
  • nāsunvatā - This means "with strength" or "with vigor," indicating a powerful presence or action.
  • sakhyaṁ - This translates to "friendship" or "alliance," suggesting a bond or relationship.
  • vaṣṭi - This means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating a giving nature.
  • śūraḥ - This translates to "the brave" or "the hero," emphasizing qualities of courage and valor.

The mantra essentially invokes Indra, asking for his presence and support in the assembly of people who are united in truth. It emphasizes the importance of community, strength, and the divine connection that binds individuals together. The call for Indra's assistance highlights the need for divine intervention in human affairs, particularly in times of need or challenge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common cause, invoking a higher power can foster unity and strength among participants.
  • Teamwork: In professional settings, the mantra can symbolize the importance of collaboration and support among team members, encouraging them to work together towards a shared goal.
  • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing difficulties can use the essence of this mantra to seek strength and guidance, reminding them of the support available from their community and the divine.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and divine support. It serves as a reminder that in times of need, individuals can come together, invoke higher powers, and create a bond that empowers them to overcome challenges. Whether in personal struggles or communal efforts, the principles embodied in this mantra are applicable in fostering resilience and cooperation in various aspects of life.
