अहं हुवान आरक्षे श्रुतर्वणि मदच्युति |शर्धांसीव स्तुकाविनाम मृक्षां शीरषा चतुर्णाम ||
ahaṁ huvāna ārkṣe śrutarvaṇi madacyuti | śardhāṁsīva stukāvinām mṛkṣā śīrṣā caturṇām ||
  • ahaṁ - This word translates to "I" or "myself," indicating a personal invocation or a call to the divine.
  • huvāna - This means "calling" or "invoking," suggesting an act of prayer or a plea for attention from the divine.
  • ārkṣe - This refers to "the sky" or "heavens," symbolizing the vastness and the divine realm.
  • śrutarvaṇi - This can be interpreted as "the one who hears" or "the one who is known," indicating a divine presence that is aware of our calls.
  • madacyuti - This term means "my intoxication" or "my ecstasy," which can refer to the blissful state achieved through spiritual connection.
  • śardhāṁsīva - This translates to "like the rays of light," symbolizing enlightenment, knowledge, and divine guidance.
  • stukāvinām - This can be understood as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is celebrated," referring to the divine being worthy of worship.
  • mṛkṣā - This word means "the stars" or "the celestial bodies," representing the cosmic order and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • śīrṣā - This translates to "the head" or "the top," symbolizing leadership, authority, and the pinnacle of spiritual attainment.
  • caturṇām - This means "of the four," which could refer to the four directions, elements, or aspects of existence, emphasizing balance and harmony.

In this mantra, the speaker is invoking a divine presence from the heavens, expressing a personal connection and a desire for spiritual intoxication and enlightenment. The imagery of light and stars suggests a quest for knowledge and understanding, while the reference to the four aspects emphasizes the importance of balance in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation, prayer, or moments of reflection. It encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. For example, when facing challenges, one might recite this mantra to invoke strength and guidance from a higher power, fostering a sense of peace and clarity.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual connection, the pursuit of knowledge, and the need for balance in our lives. It encourages us to reach out to the divine, seek enlightenment, and recognize our place within the cosmic order.
