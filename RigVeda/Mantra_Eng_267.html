अव ते हेळो वरुण नमोभिर अव यज्ञेभिर इमहे हविर्भिः |क्षयन्न अस्मभ्यम असुर प्रचेताऽ राजन एनांसी शिश्रथः कृतानि ||
ava te heḻo varuṇa namobhir ava yajñebhir īmahe havirbhiḥ | kṣayann asmabhyam asura pracetā rājann enāṁsi śiśrathaḥ kṛtāni ||
  • ava te heḻo varuṇa namobhir: This phrase can be translated as "We bow to you, Varuna, with our salutations." Varuna is the Vedic deity associated with the cosmic order and the waters. In real life, this can be seen as a call for respect and acknowledgment of the natural laws and the universe.
  • ava yajñebhir īmahe havirbhiḥ: This means "We invoke you with our offerings." In ancient times, yajñas (sacrificial rituals) were performed to please the gods and ensure prosperity. In modern life, this can be related to the idea of giving back to nature and society, such as through charitable acts or environmental conservation efforts.
  • kṣayann asmabhyam asura pracetā: This translates to "O Asura, the wise one, remove our troubles." The term 'Asura' here refers to a powerful being, often associated with challenges or obstacles. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as seeking guidance or support in overcoming difficulties, whether they be personal, professional, or societal.
  • rājann enāṁsi śiśrathaḥ kṛtāni: This means "O King, may you protect our actions." This line emphasizes the importance of divine protection over our deeds. In a contemporary context, it can be seen as a reminder to act ethically and responsibly, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to the community and the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for the divine, a call for support in overcoming challenges, and a plea for protection over our actions. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of a higher power that governs the universe and the importance of ethical conduct in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in selfless acts, seek guidance in times of trouble, and strive for harmony with the natural and social order. By invoking such principles, we can foster a more compassionate and responsible society.
