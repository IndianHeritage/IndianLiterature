अजो न क्षां दाधार पृथिवीं तस्तम्भ द्यां मन्त्रैभिः सत्यैः ||
ajo na kṣāṁ dādhāra pṛthivīṁ tastambha dyām mantrebhiḥ satyaiḥ ||
  • ajo: This word translates to "the unborn" or "eternal." It signifies the idea of something that is beyond birth and death, representing the eternal nature of the universe or the divine.
  • na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the permanence of the eternal aspect.
  • kṣāṁ: This term refers to "earth" or "the physical world." It represents the material realm that we inhabit.
  • dādhāra: This means "held" or "supported." It indicates the sustaining force that maintains the earth and the universe.
  • pṛthivīṁ: Another word for "earth," emphasizing its significance in the mantra.
  • tastambha: This translates to "upheld" or "stabilized." It suggests the idea of stability and support provided to the earth and the cosmos.
  • dyām: This word means "the sky" or "heaven." It represents the celestial realm and the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • mantrebhiḥ: This refers to "mantras" or "sacred sounds." It signifies the power of sound and vibration in creation and sustenance.
  • satyaiḥ: This means "with truth" or "by truth." It emphasizes the importance of truth in the process of creation and existence.

The mantra "ajo na kṣāṁ dādhāra pṛthivīṁ tastambha dyām mantrebhiḥ satyaiḥ" can be interpreted as a profound statement about the relationship between the eternal, the material world, and the divine. It suggests that the eternal (ajo) does not perish (na) and that it supports (dādhāra) the earth (kṣāṁ) and stabilizes (tastambha) the heavens (dyām) through the power of sacred sounds (mantrebhiḥ) and truth (satyaiḥ).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: The mantra reminds us of the importance of sustaining our planet (pṛthivīṁ) and recognizing the interconnectedness of all life. Just as the eternal supports the earth, we must support our environment through sustainable practices.
  • Spiritual Practices: The reference to mantras and truth highlights the significance of spiritual practices in our lives. Chanting mantras can help us connect with the eternal and find stability in our lives.
  • Philosophical Reflection: The mantra encourages us to reflect on the nature of existence, the eternal versus the temporary, and the role of truth in our lives. It invites us to seek deeper understanding and wisdom.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of existence, emphasizing the eternal nature of the universe and the importance of truth and sacred sound in sustaining life. It serves as a reminder of our responsibility towards the earth and encourages us to engage in practices that honor both the material and spiritual aspects of our existence.
