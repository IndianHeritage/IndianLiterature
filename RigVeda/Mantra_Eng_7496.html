तवे सु पुत्र शवसो 'वृत्रन कामकातयः |न त्वां इन्द्राति रिच्यते ||
tve su putra śavaso 'vṛtran kāmakātayaḥ | na tvām indrāti ricyate ||
  • tve - This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction of reverence or supplication towards a divine entity.
  • su - Meaning "good" or "well," it signifies positivity and auspiciousness.
  • putra - This translates to "son," symbolizing progeny, lineage, and the continuation of family or community.
  • śavaso - This word can mean "of the dead" or "of the body," often referring to the physical form or the essence of life.
  • 'vṛtran - This term refers to "the one who obstructs" or "the enemy," often symbolizing challenges or obstacles in life.
  • kāmakātayaḥ - This can be interpreted as "those who fulfill desires," indicating the divine ability to grant wishes or aspirations.
  • na - Meaning "not," it negates the following statement.
  • tvām - This translates to "you," again addressing the divine presence.
  • indrāti - Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and authority.
  • ricyate - This means "is disturbed" or "is affected," indicating a state of being influenced or impacted.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine entity, asking for protection and the fulfillment of desires while acknowledging the presence of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as career setbacks or personal losses, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine assistance and maintain a positive outlook.
  • Family and Relationships: The mention of "putra" highlights the significance of family bonds. It can be used in family prayers to seek blessings for the well-being and success of children.
  • Goal Setting: The aspect of "kāmakātayaḥ" encourages individuals to pursue their desires and aspirations, reinforcing the belief that with divine support, one can overcome any obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges and fulfilling one's desires. It serves as a reminder that while obstacles may arise, with faith and devotion, one can navigate through difficulties and achieve their goals. The mantra is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to family well-being, reinforcing the interconnectedness of spirituality and daily living.
