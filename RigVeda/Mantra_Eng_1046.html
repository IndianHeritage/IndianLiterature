त्वं अध्वर्युः उता होतासि पूर्व्यः प्रशास्ता पोता जनुषा पुरोहितः | विश्वा विद्वां आर्त्विज्याः धीर पूष्यस्य अग्ने सख्ये मा ऋषामा वयं तव ||
tvam adhvaryur uta hotāsi pūrvyaḥ praśāstā potā januṣā purohitaḥ | viśvā vidvām̐ ārtvijyā dhīra puṣyasy agne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • tvam adhvaryur: This phrase refers to the divine presence that is the officiant of the sacrifice. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or a guide who orchestrates important events, such as a wedding or a community gathering.
  • uta hotāsi pūrvyaḥ: This means "you are the one who invokes the gods." In contemporary terms, this can be seen as someone who inspires or motivates others, like a teacher or a mentor who calls forth the potential in their students.
  • praśāstā potā: This translates to "the one who is the master of the offerings." In everyday life, this could represent a person who is responsible for managing resources, such as a project manager who ensures that all elements of a project are aligned and delivered effectively.
  • januṣā purohitaḥ: This means "the one who is the priest for the people." This can be related to community leaders or spiritual guides who provide wisdom and support to their communities, similar to a counselor or a community organizer.
  • viśvā vidvām̐: This phrase means "the one who knows everything." In modern contexts, this could refer to experts or scholars who possess extensive knowledge in their fields, such as scientists or researchers who contribute to societal advancement.
  • ārtvijyā dhīra: This translates to "the wise one among the priests." This can be compared to a wise elder or a respected figure in a community who offers guidance and insight based on experience.
  • puṣyasy agne: This means "O Agni, the one who nourishes." In real life, this can symbolize the importance of nurturing relationships and environments, akin to a caregiver or a parent who provides love and support.
  • sakhye mā riṣāmā: This translates to "may we not be angry with you." This reflects the importance of maintaining harmony and understanding in relationships, similar to conflict resolution in personal or professional settings.
  • vayaṁ tava: This means "we are yours." This signifies a sense of belonging and commitment, akin to teamwork in a workplace or loyalty in friendships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the roles of leadership, guidance, and community in both spiritual and practical contexts. It highlights the importance of wisdom, nurturing, and harmony in relationships. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, workplace environments, and community interactions. By embodying the qualities of the divine presence described in the mantra, individuals can foster a more supportive and collaborative society.
