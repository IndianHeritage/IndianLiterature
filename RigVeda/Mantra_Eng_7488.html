अस्य पीत्वा मदानां देवो देवस्यौजसा | विश्वाभि भुवना भुवत ||
asya pītvā madānāṁ devo devasyaujasā | viśvābhi bhuvanā bhuvat ||
  • Mantra: "asya pītvā madānāṁ devo devasyaujasā | viśvābhi bhuvanā bhuvat ||"
  • Translation: "Having drunk the nectar of the gods, may the divine one, with his strength, bring forth all worlds."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • pītvā: having drunk
    • madānāṁ: of the intoxication or nectar
    • devo: the divine one or god
    • devasyaujasā: with the strength of the divine
    • viśvābhi: with all
    • bhuvanā: worlds or realms
    • bhuvat: may come into being or manifest
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine intoxication that comes from the blessings of the gods.
    • The act of "drinking" symbolizes receiving divine knowledge, inspiration, or energy that empowers individuals to create and manifest their desires.
    • The phrase "with his strength" emphasizes the power that comes from the divine, suggesting that true strength and capability are derived from a higher source.
    • The invocation of "all worlds" indicates a holistic approach, where the divine energy is not limited to one aspect of life but encompasses all realms of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a creative process, artists often speak of being "inspired" or "touched by the muse," which can be likened to drinking the nectar of creativity.
    • In leadership, a leader who draws strength from their values and vision can inspire their team to achieve great things, akin to the divine strength mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom from spiritual practices often find themselves empowered to manifest their goals and dreams.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine inspiration and strength in the process of creation and manifestation.
    • It teaches us that by connecting with higher energies or values, we can bring forth positive changes in our lives and the world around us.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek inspiration from various sources—be it nature, spirituality, or community—to empower their actions and decisions.
