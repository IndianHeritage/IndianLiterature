असौ य एषि वीरको गृहम्-गृहम् विचाकशद् |इमं जम्बसुतं पिब धानावन्तं करम्भिणम् अपूपवन्तम् उक्तिनम् ||
asau ya eṣi vīrako gṛhaṁ-gṛhaṁ vicākaśad | imaṁ jambhasutam piba dhānāvantaṁ karambhiṇam apūpavantam ukthinam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asau: that
    • ya: who
    • eṣi: goes
    • vīrako: the brave one
    • gṛhaṁ-gṛhaṁ: from house to house
    • vicākaśad: has wandered
    • imaṁ: this
    • jambhasutam: son of Jambha (a mythical figure)
    • piba: drink
    • dhānāvantaṁ: rich in grains
    • karambhiṇam: with a good harvest
    • apūpavantam: with offerings
    • ukthinam: of the sacred hymn
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a brave individual who travels from house to house.
    • The reference to "son of Jambha" suggests a connection to strength and valor.
    • The act of drinking "rich in grains" signifies abundance and prosperity.
    • The mention of "good harvest" and "offerings" indicates the importance of gratitude and sharing wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to community leaders or social workers who go from home to home, helping those in need.
    • It can also symbolize the importance of sharing resources, such as food drives or community kitchens that provide for the less fortunate.
    • The idea of abundance can be seen in successful businesses that give back to their communities through charitable initiatives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the values of bravery, community service, and the importance of sharing resources.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in individual success but in uplifting others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more compassionate and prosperous society.
