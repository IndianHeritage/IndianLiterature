सुतम्भरो यजमानस्य सत्पतिर विश्वासाम ऊधः स धियाम उदञ्चनः |भरद्धेनू रसवच्छिश्रिये पयोऽनुब्रुवाणो अध्य एति न स्वपन ||
sutambharo yajamānasya satpatir viśvāsām ūdhaḥ sa dhiyām udañcanaḥ | bharad dhenū rasavac chiśriye payo 'nubruvāṇo adhy eti na svapan ||
  • Mantra: "sutambharo yajamānasya satpatir viśvāsām ūdhaḥ sa dhiyām udañcanaḥ | bharad dhenū rasavac chiśriye payo 'nubruvāṇo adhy eti na svapan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sutambharo: One who is endowed with auspiciousness or good fortune.
    • yajamānasya: Of the one who is performing the sacrifice or ritual.
    • satpatir: The true path or the righteous way.
    • viśvāsām: Of all beings or universal trust.
    • ūdhaḥ: Rising or elevating.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • dhiyām: Of the intellect or understanding.
    • udañcanaḥ: One who elevates or uplifts.
    • bharad: Bestowing or providing.
    • dhenū: The cow, symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • rasavac: Of essence or flavor.
    • chiśriye: In the realm of prosperity or wealth.
    • payo: Milk, representing nourishment.
    • anubruvāṇo: One who speaks or conveys.
    • adhy: Above or beyond.
    • eti: Goes or reaches.
    • na: Not.
    • svapan: Sleeping or being inactive.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of a person who is engaged in rituals and sacrifices, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and trust.
    • The term "sutambharo" suggests that the individual is blessed with good fortune, which can be seen in real life as someone who is successful in their endeavors due to their positive actions and intentions.
    • "Yajamānasya" refers to the one performing the ritual, highlighting the significance of intention and dedication in any task, such as a student preparing for exams or an athlete training for a competition.
    • The phrase "satpatir viśvāsām" indicates that the true path is one that fosters trust among all beings, akin to a leader who inspires confidence in their team, leading to collective success.
    • "Udañcanaḥ" signifies the act of uplifting others, which can be exemplified by mentors or teachers who guide their students towards achieving their goals.
    • The imagery of "bharad dhenū" and "payo" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, akin to how a supportive family or community provides the necessary resources for growth and development.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that one who embodies these qualities does not remain inactive ("na svapan"), suggesting that proactive engagement in life is essential for success and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness, trust, and proactive engagement in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that success is not just about individual effort but also about uplifting others and fostering a supportive environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and community building, where the focus is on collective growth and well-being.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages individuals to embody these virtues to create a harmonious and prosperous life.
