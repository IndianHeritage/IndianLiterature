ऐच्छाम तव बहुधा जातवेदः प्रविष्टम अग्ने अप्स्व ओषधिषु |तं तव यमो अचिकेच चित्रभानो दशान्तरुष्याद अतिरोचमानम् ||
aicchāma tvā bahudhā jātavedaḥ praviṣṭam agne apsv oṣadhīṣu | taṁ tvā yamo acikec citrabhāno daśāntaruṣyād atirocamānam ||
  • Mantra: "aicchāma tvā bahudhā jātavedaḥ praviṣṭam agne apsv oṣadhīṣu | taṁ tvā yamo acikec citrabhāno daśāntaruṣyād atirocamānam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aicchāma: We desire
    • tvā: You
    • bahudhā: In many ways
    • jātavedaḥ: O Agni, the one who knows all births
    • praviṣṭam: Entered
    • agne: O Agni (Fire)
    • apsv: In the waters
    • oṣadhīṣu: In the plants
    • taṁ: That
    • tvā: You
    • yamo: Yama (the god of death)
    • acikec: May he not harm
    • citrabhāno: O radiant one
    • daśāntaruṣyād: From the ten directions
    • atirocamānam: Most brilliant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "aicchāma tvā bahudhā" expresses a desire for Agni to manifest in various forms, indicating the multifaceted nature of divine energy.
    • "jātavedaḥ" refers to Agni as the one who knows all births, symbolizing the source of life and knowledge.
    • The invocation of Agni in "apsv oṣadhīṣu" highlights the connection between fire, water, and plants, emphasizing the interdependence of natural elements.
    • The latter part of the mantra, invoking Yama, suggests a plea for protection from death and misfortune, seeking the blessings of the radiant Agni to ward off negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek knowledge and wisdom in various forms, much like how we learn from different experiences and perspectives.
    • The interconnection of elements can be reflected in sustainable living practices, where we recognize the importance of preserving nature and its resources.
    • In moments of fear or uncertainty, invoking the protective qualities of Agni can serve as a metaphor for finding inner strength and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all elements in nature and the need for balance.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to cultivate wisdom, resilience, and a harmonious relationship with the environment and the divine.
