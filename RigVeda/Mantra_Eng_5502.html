अनु तन् नो जास्पतिर मंसीष्ठ रत्नं देवस्य सवितुर इयानः |भागं उग्रो 'वसे जोहवीति भागं अनुग्रो अध याती रत्नम् ||
anu tan no jāspatir maṁsīṣṭa ratnaṁ devasya savitur iyānaḥ | bhagam ugro 'vase johavīti bhagam anugro adha yāti ratnam ||
  • anu tan no jāspatir maṁsīṣṭa ratnaṁ devasya savitur iyānaḥ
    • anu: This word means "following" or "after." It signifies continuity and the idea of progression.
    • tan: This translates to "that" or "that which." It refers to the object of focus or the essence of what is being discussed.
    • no: This means "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • jāspatir: This term refers to "the lord of speech" or "the divine teacher," often associated with wisdom and knowledge.
    • maṁsīṣṭa: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating a high standard or quality.
    • ratnaṁ: This translates to "jewel" or "precious thing," symbolizing value and worth.
    • devasya: This means "of the divine" or "of the god," referring to a higher power or spiritual essence.
    • savituh: This term refers to "the sun" or "the source of light," symbolizing illumination and enlightenment.
    • iyānaḥ: This means "may it lead" or "may it guide," indicating a desire for direction and purpose.
  • bhagam ugro 'vase johavīti
    • bhagam: This translates to "the portion" or "the share," indicating a part of something larger.
    • ugro: This means "mighty" or "powerful," suggesting strength and intensity.
    • 'vase: This term means "to control" or "to govern," indicating authority and mastery.
    • johavīti: This translates to "may it be invoked" or "may it be called upon," suggesting a plea for assistance or intervention.
  • bhagam anugro adha yāti ratnam
    • anugro: This means "the favorable" or "the benevolent," indicating kindness and support.
    • adha: This translates to "below" or "beneath," suggesting humility or a grounded approach.
    • yāti: This means "to go" or "to move," indicating progress or journey.
    • ratnam: This again refers to "jewel" or "precious thing," emphasizing the value of what is being sought.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom, guidance, and the invocation of divine support in our endeavors. It reflects the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment from higher powers while recognizing the value of our collective identity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In educational settings, where students seek knowledge and guidance from teachers and mentors.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive for self-improvement and enlightenment.
  • In community gatherings, where collective efforts are made to invoke blessings and support from a higher power.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, community, and divine guidance in our journey through life.
