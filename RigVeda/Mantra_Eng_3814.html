अग्निस तुविश्रवस्तमं तुविब्रह्माणम उत्तमम् |अतूर्तं श्रावयत्पतिम पुत्रं ददाति दाशुषे ||
agnis tuviśravastamaṁ tuvibrahmāṇam uttamam | atūrtaṁ śrāvayatpatim putraṁ dadāti dāśuṣe ||
  • Agnis: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purity.
  • Tuviśravastamaṁ: This term can be broken down into "tu" (you) and "viśravastamaṁ" (the one who is most renowned or famous). It signifies the greatness and revered status of Agni.
  • Tuvibrahmāṇam: This means "you, the supreme Brahman," indicating that Agni is not just a deity but also represents the ultimate reality or cosmic principle.
  • Uttamam: This translates to "the highest" or "the best," emphasizing Agni's exalted position among the deities.
  • Atūrtaṁ: This word means "quick" or "swift," suggesting the immediacy and responsiveness of Agni to prayers and offerings.
  • Śrāvayat: This means "to make known" or "to proclaim," indicating the act of revealing or communicating something important.
  • Patim: This translates to "lord" or "master," referring to Agni as the ruler or protector.
  • Putraṁ: This means "son," which can symbolize the blessings or progeny that come from the divine.
  • Dadāti: This means "gives," indicating the act of bestowing or granting something valuable.
  • Dāśuṣe: This term refers to "the devotee" or "the worshipper," highlighting the relationship between the divine and the individual who seeks blessings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, acknowledging his supreme status and asking for his blessings. It emphasizes the importance of Agni in the Vedic tradition as a mediator between the divine and human realms. The mantra suggests that Agni, as the embodiment of energy and transformation, is capable of granting prosperity and blessings to his devotees.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the transformation of offerings into divine blessings.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to embrace change and transformation in their lives, encouraging them to pursue their goals with energy and determination.
  • Community and Family: The mention of "putraṁ" signifies the importance of family and community, reminding individuals to seek blessings for their loved ones and to foster harmony within their relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that not only represents the physical element of fire but also embodies the principles of transformation, purity, and divine communication. By invoking Agni, individuals can seek guidance, blessings, and the strength to navigate life's challenges, making it a relevant and powerful prayer in both spiritual and everyday contexts.
