मन्त्र: होत्राद् अहं वरुण बिभ्यद् आयं नेद् एव मा युनजन्न अत्र देवाः |तस्य मे तन्भो बहुधा निविष्टा एतमर्थं न चिकेताहम् अग्निः ||
hotrād ahaṁ varuṇa bibhyad āyaṁ ned eva mā yunajann atra devāḥ | tasya me tanvo bahudhā niviṣṭā etam arthaṁ na ciketāham agniḥ ||
  • hotrād ahaṁ varuṇa bibhyad: This phrase translates to "From the priest, I fear Varuna." Here, "hotrād" refers to the priest or the one who performs the rituals, and "Varuna" is the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. This indicates a recognition of the power of divine forces and the importance of rituals in maintaining harmony.
  • āyaṁ ned eva mā yunajann atra devāḥ: This means "May the gods not take away my strength." It reflects a plea for protection and the desire to retain one's vitality and strength. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for seeking support from higher powers or the universe when facing challenges.
  • tasya me tanvo bahudhā niviṣṭā: This translates to "My body is established in many ways." This suggests the multifaceted nature of existence and the various roles one plays in life. For example, an individual may be a parent, a professional, and a friend, each role requiring different strengths and attributes.
  • etam arthaṁ na ciketāham agniḥ: This means "I do not understand this purpose, O Agni." Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing transformation and the divine spark within. This line expresses a sense of confusion or lack of clarity regarding one's purpose or direction in life, which is a common human experience.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and understanding one's place in the universe. It highlights the importance of rituals, the acknowledgment of divine forces, and the multifaceted nature of human existence. In real-life scenarios, individuals often find themselves in situations where they seek clarity and strength, whether in personal challenges, professional endeavors, or spiritual quests. The mantra serves as a reminder to connect with the divine, seek support, and embrace the complexity of life while striving for understanding and purpose.
