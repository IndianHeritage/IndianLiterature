सुत इन्द्राय वायवे वरुणाय मरुद्भ्यः |सोमो अर्षति विष्णवे ||
suta indrāya vāyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | somo arṣati viṣṇave ||
  • Mantra: "suta indrāya vāyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | somo arṣati viṣṇave ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suta: son or offspring, often referring to a divine or noble lineage.
    • indrāya: to Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain, thunder, and war.
    • vāyave: to Vayu, the god of wind, representing movement and life force.
    • varuṇāya: to Varuna, the god of water and cosmic order, symbolizing truth and moral authority.
    • marudbhyaḥ: to the Maruts, the storm deities, representing strength and power.
    • somo: to Soma, the divine nectar or drink, associated with immortality and ecstasy.
    • arṣati: shines or radiates, indicating a state of brilliance or enlightenment.
    • viṣṇave: to Vishnu, the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity, symbolizing stability and sustenance.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes various deities, each representing different aspects of life and nature.
    • By addressing Indra, the mantra seeks strength and courage, much like how a leader inspires their team to overcome challenges.
    • Vayu symbolizes the essential breath of life, akin to how fresh air revitalizes us in our daily lives.
    • Varuna represents the moral compass and the importance of truth, similar to how ethical practices guide businesses and personal relationships.
    • The Maruts embody the power of storms, reminding us of nature's might, much like how we respect and prepare for natural disasters.
    • Soma, as a symbol of ecstasy, reflects the pursuit of joy and fulfillment in life, akin to how people seek happiness through various means.
    • Vishnu's invocation emphasizes the need for preservation and balance, similar to how we strive for sustainability in our environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of various forces in nature and life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek strength, truth, and balance in our lives, drawing inspiration from the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in personal growth, leadership, environmental sustainability, and ethical decision-making.
    • By invoking these deities, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, aligning their actions with higher principles.
