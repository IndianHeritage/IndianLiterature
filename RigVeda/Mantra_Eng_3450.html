ये अश्विनाः ये पितरः य ऊती धेनुं तातक्षुर ऋभवः ये अश्वा |ये अंशत्रा य ऋधग rodasī ये विभवो नरः स्वपत्यानि चक्रुः ||
ye aśvinā ye pitarā ya ūtī dhenuṁ tatakṣur ṛbhavo ye aśvā | ye aṁsatrā ya ṛdhag rodasī ye vibhvo naraḥ svapatyāni cakruḥ ||
  • ye aśvinā: This phrase refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and medicinal powers. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • ye pitarā: This translates to "those who are ancestors" or "forefathers." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and remembering one's lineage and heritage.
  • ya ūtī dhenuṁ tatakṣur: This part can be interpreted as "those who have the power to bestow abundance." The term "dhenu" refers to a cow, which is a symbol of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture.
  • ṛbhavo ye aśvā: "ṛbhavo" means "those who are swift" or "who move quickly." The "aśvā" refers to horses, which are often associated with strength, speed, and vitality.
  • ye aṁsatrā: This phrase can be understood as "those who are free from enmity." It highlights the value of peace and harmony in relationships.
  • ya ṛdhag rodasī: This translates to "those who have established the heavens." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, suggesting a higher purpose or goal in life.
  • ye vibhvo naraḥ svapatyāni cakruḥ: This means "those who are wise among men have created prosperity." It emphasizes the role of wisdom and intelligence in achieving success and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking blessings from divine forces, honoring one's ancestors, and striving for abundance and peace in life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence—spiritual, familial, and material. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, or meditative practices aimed at fostering gratitude, promoting harmony in relationships, and aspiring for prosperity. By invoking the qualities of the Aśvins and honoring our forefathers, we can cultivate a life filled with abundance, wisdom, and peace.
