मन्त्र: एतो nv इन्द्रं स्तवाम सखायः स्तोम्यं नरम् | कृष्टीर यो विश्वा अभ्य अस्त्य एक इति ||
eto nv indraṁ stavāma sakhāyaḥ stomyaṁ naram | kṛṣṭīr yo viśvā abhy asty eka it ||
  • eto nv indraṁ: This phrase translates to "We invoke Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and support in challenging situations.
  • stavāma: This means "we praise" or "we extol." It emphasizes the act of worship and recognition of divine qualities. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people express gratitude or admiration towards mentors, leaders, or even nature.
  • sakhāyaḥ: This translates to "as friends." It suggests a relationship of camaraderie and support. In real life, this can be reflected in friendships and partnerships where mutual support is essential for overcoming obstacles.
  • stomyaṁ: This means "worthy of praise." It indicates that the subject of the praise possesses qualities that are admirable. In practical terms, this can relate to recognizing and appreciating the efforts of individuals in various fields, such as community service or professional achievements.
  • naram: This translates to "the man" or "human." It signifies the human aspect of the divine, suggesting that the qualities we admire in deities can also be found in humanity. This can be seen in leaders who embody strength and wisdom.
  • kṛṣṭīr: This word means "strength" or "power." It highlights the importance of inner strength and resilience. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who overcome personal challenges, such as illness or adversity, through sheer willpower.
  • yo viśvā abhy asty: This translates to "who is present everywhere." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine or the universal spirit. In a modern context, this can relate to the idea of interconnectedness among all beings and the environment.
  • eka it: This means "one" or "unity." It emphasizes the concept of oneness and collective strength. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts where individuals come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine strength and support while recognizing the importance of friendship, unity, and the admirable qualities present in humanity. Its significance lies in the reminder that we can draw strength from both the divine and our relationships with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek support from their communities, appreciate the strengths of those around them, and strive for unity in the face of challenges. It serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the strength that comes from collective effort.
