क्रीळुर मखो न मंहयुः पवित्रं सोम गच्छसि |दधत स्तोत्रे सुवीर्यम् ||
krīḻur makho na maṁhayuḥ pavitraṁ soma gacchasi | dadhat stotre suvīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • krīḻur: This can be interpreted as "the one who is joyful" or "the one who brings joy."
    • makho: Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," often associated with rituals in Vedic traditions.
    • na: Means "not," indicating negation.
    • maṁhayuḥ: Translates to "to be harmed" or "to be diminished."
    • pavitraṁ: Means "pure" or "sacred," often used to describe something that is spiritually uplifting.
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • gacchasi: Means "you go" or "you move," indicating a journey or transition.
    • dadhat: Translates to "holding" or "possessing," often used in the context of holding virtues or qualities.
    • stotre: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating a form of worship or acknowledgment.
    • suvīryam: Means "excellent strength" or "great valor," often associated with heroic qualities.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of joy and purity in the context of offerings and sacrifices.
    • It suggests that true offerings should not diminish one's spirit or essence, but rather enhance it.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates that the divine inspiration and strength come from pure intentions and actions.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to hold onto virtues and to praise those qualities that lead to excellence and valor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when teams come together for a project, the joy and purity of their intentions can lead to successful outcomes. For instance, a team that collaborates with a positive mindset and mutual respect is likely to achieve its goals more effectively.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining purity of thought and intention can strengthen bonds. For example, when friends support each other genuinely, it fosters a joyful and resilient friendship.
    • In community service, individuals who engage in acts of kindness with pure intentions often inspire others, creating a ripple effect of positivity and strength within the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity and joy in our actions and offerings.
    • It serves as a reminder that our intentions should uplift rather than diminish ourselves and others.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, successful collaborations, and a more harmonious community.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embody virtues that promote excellence and valor in all aspects of life.
