तīv्रस्याभिवयसो अस्य पाही सर्वरथā वि हरī इह मुञ्च |इन्द्र मा त्वा यजमानासो अन्ये नि रीरमन तुभ्यम इमे सुताः ||
tīvrasyābhivayaso asya pāhi sarvarathā vi harī iha muñca | indra mā tvā yajamānāso anye ni rīraman tubhyam ime sutāsaḥ ||
  • tīvrasyābhivayaso: This term can be broken down into two parts: "tīvrasyā" meaning "of the intense" or "powerful" and "abhivayaso" which refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." This suggests a plea for the attention of a powerful deity.
  • asya pāhi: "asya" means "of this" and "pāhi" means "protect" or "guard." Together, this phrase is a request for protection, indicating a desire for safety and security.
  • sarvarathā vi harī: "sarvarathā" translates to "all chariots" or "all vehicles," and "vi harī" can be interpreted as "may you grant" or "may you bestow." This implies a wish for the divine to provide all means of progress and success.
  • iha muñca: "iha" means "here" or "in this place," and "muñca" means "release" or "let go." This phrase expresses a desire for liberation or freedom in the current context.
  • indra mā tvā: "indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, and "mā tvā" means "do not" or "may you not." This indicates a plea directed towards Indra, asking him not to withhold his blessings.
  • yajamānāso: This term means "the worshippers" or "those who perform rituals." It signifies the community or individuals who are engaged in spiritual practices.
  • anye ni rīraman: "anye" means "others" and "ni rīraman" translates to "may they not disturb" or "may they not interfere." This suggests a desire for harmony and peace, free from external disturbances.
  • tubhyam ime sutāsaḥ: "tubhyam" means "to you," "ime" means "these," and "sutāsaḥ" refers to "sons" or "offspring." This phrase indicates a dedication or offering of one's progeny or achievements to the divine.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine protection, guidance, and the removal of obstacles. It reflects the human condition of seeking support from higher powers in times of need.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be invoked during challenging times, such as during a significant life transition, a new venture, or when facing uncertainties. For instance, a student preparing for exams might recite this mantra to seek clarity and focus, while a businessperson might use it to invoke success and protection for their enterprise.
  • Conclusion: The essence of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human efforts, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance and protection in our endeavors. By acknowledging the presence of higher powers, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace and confidence in their pursuits.
