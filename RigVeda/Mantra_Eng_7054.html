मन्त्र: य आयुः कुत्सम् अतिथिग्वम् अर्दयो वावृद्धानो दिवे-दिवे |तं त्वा वयं हर्यश्वं शतक्रतुम् वाजयन्तो हवामहे ||
ya āyuṁ kutsam atithigvam ardayo vāvṛdhāno dive-dive | taṁ tvā vayaṁ haryaśvaṁ śatakratuṁ vājayanto havāmahe ||
  • ya āyuṁ kutsam atithigvam ardayo vāvṛdhāno dive-dive: This line can be translated to mean "May we attain the long life that is free from ailments and may we be blessed with prosperity every day." Here, "āyuṁ" refers to life or longevity, "kutsam" implies being free from ailments or suffering, and "atithigvam" suggests hospitality or the welcoming of guests, which is a significant aspect of Vedic culture.
  • taṁ tvā vayaṁ haryaśvaṁ śatakratuṁ vājayanto havāmahe: This part translates to "We invoke you, O powerful one, who is like a swift horse, endowed with a hundred powers, and we offer our prayers." "Haryaśva" symbolizes strength and speed, akin to a horse, while "śatakratu" refers to someone with a hundred capabilities or powers, indicating a divine being or force that we seek blessings from.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of health, longevity, and prosperity in life. It reflects the Vedic belief in the interconnectedness of physical well-being and spiritual growth. The invocation of divine powers signifies a plea for assistance in achieving these goals.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be recited during health-related rituals or ceremonies, such as weddings or housewarming events, where blessings for a long and prosperous life are sought. It can also be used in personal meditation practices to focus on health and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the values of health, hospitality, and divine support in our lives. By invoking these qualities, individuals can cultivate a positive mindset and attract prosperity and longevity, making it relevant in various aspects of daily life, from personal health to community relationships.
