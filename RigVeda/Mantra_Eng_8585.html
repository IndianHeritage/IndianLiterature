अश्वो न क्रदो वृषभिर युजानाḥ सिंहो न भीमो मनसो जवीयान् |अर्वाचीनैः पथिभिर ये रजिष्ठा आ पवस्व सौमन्सं न इन्दो ||
aśvo na krado vṛṣabhir yujānaḥ siṁho na bhīmo manaso javīyān | arvācīnaiḥ pathibhir ye rajiṣṭhā ā pavasva saumanasaṁ na indo ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvo: horse
    • na: not
    • krado: to be defeated
    • vṛṣabhir: with the strong
    • yujānaḥ: united
    • siṁho: lion
    • na: not
    • bhīmo: fearful
    • manaso: of the mind
    • javīyān: swift
    • arvācīnaiḥ: from the east
    • pathibhir: by the paths
    • ye: those who
    • rajiṣṭhā: who are in control
    • ā: towards
    • pavasva: may you flow
    • saumanasaṁ: with a pleasant mind
    • na: not
    • indo: O Indra
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes strength and courage, symbolized by the horse and lion, which are often associated with power and fearlessness.
    • The phrase "not to be defeated" emphasizes resilience and the importance of unity in overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to the mind being swift suggests the need for quick thinking and adaptability in difficult situations.
    • The mention of "those who are in control" indicates the importance of leadership and guidance in navigating life's paths.
    • The call to "flow with a pleasant mind" highlights the significance of maintaining a positive attitude and mental clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a corporate setting, the qualities of resilience and unity can lead to successful teamwork and project completion.
    • In sports, athletes often embody the spirit of the horse and lion, demonstrating strength, speed, and fearlessness in the face of competition.
    • In personal challenges, maintaining a swift and adaptable mindset can help individuals navigate through crises, such as job loss or health issues.
    • Leadership in community initiatives can reflect the essence of this mantra, where guiding others with a positive and pleasant approach fosters collaboration and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, unity, and a positive mindset in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability across various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success.
    • By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and leadership, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
