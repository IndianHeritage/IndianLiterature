प्रत्याग्निरुषसो जातवेदाः अख्यद् देवो रोचमानः महोभिः |आ नासत्योरुगायाः रथेनेमं यज्ञं उप नो यातमच्छ ||
praty agnir uṣaso jātavedā akhyad devo rocamānā mahobhiḥ | ā nāsatyorugāyā rathenemaṁ yajñam upa no yātam accha ||
  • Mantra: "praty agnir uṣaso jātavedā akhyad devo rocamānā mahobhiḥ | ā nāsatyorugāyā rathenemaṁ yajñam upa no yātam accha ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • praty: towards
    • agnir: fire
    • uṣaso: of dawn
    • jātavedā: the one who knows all births (a name for Agni)
    • akhyad: has declared
    • devo: the divine
    • rocamānā: shining
    • mahobhiḥ: with great powers
    • ā: from
    • nāsatyorugāyā: of the great (or vast) non-being
    • rathenemaṁ: by this chariot
    • yajñam: the sacrifice
    • upa: towards
    • no: us
    • yātam: may we go
    • accha: well
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is also seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "uṣaso jātavedā" refers to Agni as the one who brings forth the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • "Devo rocamānā mahobhiḥ" highlights the divine nature of Agni, emphasizing his brilliance and power.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "ā nāsatyorugāyā rathenemaṁ yajñam upa no yātam accha," suggests a desire for the divine to guide us towards our sacrifices, ensuring that our efforts are fruitful and beneficial.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the importance of setting intentions at the start of a new day, much like the dawn that Agni represents.
    • When embarking on new projects or endeavors, invoking the qualities of Agni can inspire clarity, focus, and the energy needed to succeed.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, the mantra can be recited to seek blessings for the success of the undertaking, ensuring that the efforts are aligned with higher purposes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of fire (Agni) as a transformative force in our lives, representing both physical and spiritual illumination.
    • It teaches us to seek divine guidance in our actions and to recognize the potential for renewal and growth in every new beginning.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to approach our endeavors with reverence and intention, fostering a connection between the material and spiritual realms.
