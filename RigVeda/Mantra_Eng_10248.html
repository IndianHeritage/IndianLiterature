पुमां एणं तनुत उ कृत्नाति पुमान् वि तत्ने अधि नाके अस्मिन् |इमे मयूखाः उप सेदुर ū सदः सामानि चक्रुस तसराण्य ओतवे ||
pumām̐ enaṁ tanuta ut kṛṇatti pumān vi tatne adhi nāke asmin | ime mayūkhā upa sedur ū sadaḥ sāmāni cakrus tasarāṇy otave ||
  • Mantra: "pumām̐ enaṁ tanuta ut kṛṇatti pumān vi tatne adhi nāke asmin | ime mayūkhā upa sedur ū sadaḥ sāmāni cakrus tasarāṇy otave ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pumām̐: The masculine principle or the divine male energy.
    • enaṁ: This (referring to the universe or creation).
    • tanuta: The body or form.
    • ut: And.
    • kṛṇatti: Creates or manifests.
    • pumā: The man or the divine being.
    • vi: Indeed or truly.
    • tatne: He who supports or sustains.
    • adhi: Above or in the higher realms.
    • nāke: In the sky or heavens.
    • asmin: In this (referring to the present existence).
    • ime: These.
    • mayūkhā: Rays or beams of light.
    • upa: Near or close to.
    • sedur: They have settled or established.
    • ū: And.
    • sadaḥ: Ever or always.
    • sāmāni: Harmonies or melodies.
    • cakrus: They have created.
    • tasarāṇy: The various forms or manifestations.
    • otave: To flow or to move.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine masculine energy that creates and sustains the universe.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the physical form and the higher realms, suggesting that everything is interconnected.
    • The "rays of light" symbolize knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment that guide individuals in their journey through life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way people draw inspiration from nature, art, and relationships, which can illuminate their paths.
    • For example, a person may find clarity and purpose in their life through meditation or by spending time in nature, reflecting the idea of divine energy manifesting in their existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the divine presence in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of harmony in their lives.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through mindfulness practices, fostering relationships, and seeking knowledge.
    • By acknowledging the divine energy within and around us, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of our purpose and the world we inhabit.
