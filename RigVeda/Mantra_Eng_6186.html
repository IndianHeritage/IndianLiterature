वापन्ति मरुतो मिहं प्र वेपयन्ति पर्वतान् |यद यामं यान्ति वायुभिः ||
vapanti maruto miham pra vepayanti parvatān | yad yāmaṁ yānti vāyubhiḥ ||
  • Vapanti: This word translates to "they blow" or "they move." It signifies the action of the winds or the forces of nature.
  • Maruto: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities or the winds in Vedic literature. They are often associated with strength and power.
  • Miham: This can be interpreted as "I am" or "I exist," indicating a sense of presence or being.
  • Pra vepayanti: This phrase means "they shake" or "they cause to tremble." It suggests a powerful influence or impact.
  • Parvatān: This word means "mountains." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the great natural formations that stand tall and strong.
  • Yad: Translates to "that" or "which," serving as a connector in the mantra.
  • Yāmaṁ: This word means "the path" or "the way," indicating a journey or direction.
  • Yānti: Means "they go" or "they move," suggesting action or progression.
  • Vāyubhiḥ: Refers to "by the winds," indicating the influence of the winds on the journey or movement.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the power of the winds (Maruts) and their ability to influence the world around them, including the mountains and the paths taken by beings. It highlights the dynamic relationship between nature and existence, emphasizing how the forces of nature can cause significant changes and movements in the environment.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for the challenges and changes we face. Just as the winds can shake the mountains, our experiences can profoundly impact our lives. For example, consider a person facing a major life change, such as moving to a new city or starting a new job. The uncertainties and challenges they encounter can be likened to the winds that shake the mountains, testing their strength and resilience.


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the powerful forces of nature and their influence on our lives. It serves as a reminder of the inevitability of change and the importance of resilience in the face of challenges. By acknowledging the impact of these forces, we can better navigate our own paths and embrace the journey of life, understanding that just as the winds shape the mountains, our experiences shape who we are.
