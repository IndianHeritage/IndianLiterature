प्र यज्ञ एत्वानुषग अद्य देवव्यचस्तमः | स्तृणीत बर्हिर आसदे ||
pra yajña etv ānuṣag adyā devavyacastamaḥ | stṛṇīta barhir āsade ||
  • Mantra: "pra yajña etv ānuṣag adyā devavyacastamaḥ | stṛṇīta barhir āsade ||"
  • Translation: "Today, let us perform the sacrifice; may the divine beings be present and may they grant us their blessings."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: signifies 'before' or 'in front of', indicating the initiation of an action.
    • yajña: refers to 'sacrifice' or 'offering', a central concept in Vedic rituals that symbolizes the act of giving and devotion.
    • etv: means 'this' or 'here', pointing to the present moment of action.
    • ānusag: translates to 'may it be fulfilled' or 'may it be accomplished', expressing a desire for the success of the ritual.
    • adyā: means 'today', emphasizing the immediacy of the action.
    • devavyacastamaḥ: refers to 'the divine beings' or 'gods', invoking their presence and blessings.
    • stṛṇīta: means 'to cover' or 'to spread', indicating the preparation of the space for the ritual.
    • barhir: refers to 'grass' or 'sacred grass', which is often used in Vedic rituals as a symbol of purity.
    • āsade: means 'to sit' or 'to reside', suggesting the establishment of a sacred space for the divine presence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, people often perform rituals or ceremonies to mark significant life events such as weddings, births, or housewarmings. These rituals can be seen as a form of 'yajña', where individuals offer their intentions and seek blessings from higher powers.
    • In a corporate setting, team-building activities can be viewed as a modern-day 'yajña', where employees come together to strengthen their bonds and work towards a common goal, invoking a sense of unity and purpose.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and community in rituals. It highlights that the act of coming together to perform a sacrifice or offering is not just about the physical act but also about the collective energy and focus of the participants.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our daily lives, whether through personal or communal actions, we can invoke positivity and blessings by being mindful of our intentions and the energy we bring into our actions.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of performing rituals with a sense of purpose and community. It teaches us that every action, when performed with the right intention, can lead to positive outcomes and divine blessings.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community service, reminding us to approach our endeavors with mindfulness and a spirit of giving.
