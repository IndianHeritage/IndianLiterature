परा व्यक्तो अरुषो दिवः कविर वृषा त्रिपृष्टो अनविष्ट गा अभि |सहस्रणीति यतिः परायति रेबो न पूर्वीर उषसो वि राजति ||
parā vyakto aruṣo divaḥ kavir vṛṣā tripṛṣṭho anaviṣṭa gā abhi | sahasraṇītir yatiḥ parāyatī rebho na pūrvīr uṣaso vi rājati ||
  • parā vyakto: This phrase can be translated as "the supreme manifestation" or "the ultimate expression." It signifies the highest form of existence or consciousness that transcends ordinary understanding.
  • aruṣo divaḥ: "aruṣo" means "red" or "brilliant," and "divaḥ" refers to "heaven" or "the sky." Together, they evoke imagery of a radiant sky, symbolizing enlightenment and divine presence.
  • kavir vṛṣā: "kavir" translates to "the wise one" or "the poet," while "vṛṣā" means "the strong" or "the powerful." This suggests a figure of great wisdom and strength, often associated with divine knowledge.
  • tripṛṣṭho anaviṣṭa: "tripṛṣṭho" can be interpreted as "threefold" or "having three aspects," and "anaviṣṭa" means "not confined" or "not limited." This indicates a being that encompasses multiple dimensions and is not bound by physical limitations.
  • gā abhi: "gā" means "to sing" or "to praise," and "abhi" implies "towards" or "in the direction of." This suggests an act of devotion or reverence directed towards the divine.
  • sahasraṇītir: This term translates to "the one who has a thousand paths" or "the one who leads to many directions." It symbolizes the multitude of ways in which one can seek knowledge or truth.
  • yatiḥ parāyatī: "yatiḥ" refers to "the ascetic" or "the seeker," and "parāyatī" means "the one who goes beyond." This indicates a spiritual journey that transcends ordinary existence.
  • rebho: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines." It emphasizes the brilliance of the divine or the enlightened state.
  • na pūrvīr uṣaso vi rājati: "na" means "not," "pūrvīr" refers to "the previous" or "the past," "uṣaso" means "dawn" or "light," and "vi rājati" translates to "shines." This suggests that the current state of enlightenment or divine presence is unique and not merely a repetition of the past.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound spiritual concepts, emphasizing the nature of the divine as a supreme, radiant force that transcends ordinary understanding. It highlights the journey of the seeker towards enlightenment, suggesting that there are countless paths to truth and wisdom. In real life, this can be applied to various spiritual practices, such as meditation, self-reflection, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek their own unique path towards understanding and enlightenment, recognizing that each person's journey is valid and significant. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the potential for transformation through spiritual exploration.
