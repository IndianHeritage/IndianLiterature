गावो यवम् प्रयुता आर्यो अक्षन् ता अपश्यं सहगोपाश चरन्तीः |हवा इद आर्यो अभितः सम आयान कियद आसु स्वपतिश चन्दयाते ||
gāvo yavam prayutā aryo akṣan tā apaśyaṁ sahagopāś carantīḥ | havā id aryo abhitaḥ sam āyan kiyad āsu svapatiś chandayāte ||
  • gāvo yavam prayutā aryo akṣan: This phrase can be translated to "The cows, having been brought together, are seen by the noble ones." Here, "gāvo" refers to cows, which are often symbolic of wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature. "Yavam" means grain or food, indicating sustenance. "Aryo" refers to the noble or the wise, and "akṣan" means to see or perceive.
  • tā apaśyaṁ sahagopāś carantīḥ: This translates to "They (the cows) move together with the herdsmen." "Apaśyaṁ" means to move or wander, while "sahagopāś" refers to the herdsmen or caretakers of the cows. This line emphasizes the relationship between the cows and their caretakers, highlighting the importance of community and cooperation.
  • havā id aryo abhitaḥ sam āyan: This can be interpreted as "The noble ones gather around the offerings." "Havā" refers to offerings or sacrifices, which are central to Vedic rituals. "Id" means 'here' or 'now', and "abhitaḥ" means 'from all sides', indicating a communal gathering for a purpose.
  • kiyad āsu svapatiś chandayāte: This translates to "How many are the lords who are pleased with the offerings?" "Kiyad" means 'how many', "āsu" refers to 'in this', "svapatiś" means 'lords' or 'rulers', and "chandayāte" means 'to be pleased'. This line reflects the idea of seeking favor from higher powers through offerings and rituals.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, community, and the divine. It highlights the importance of nurturing relationships, whether with nature (represented by cows) or with fellow beings (the herdsmen). The act of coming together for offerings signifies unity and shared purpose.
  • Real-life Application: In modern society, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life. For instance, in agricultural communities, the care for livestock and the cooperation among farmers can lead to prosperity. Additionally, in any community setting, the act of gathering for a common cause—be it a festival, a charity event, or a communal meal—reflects the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of community, cooperation, and the respect for nature. It encourages individuals to come together, appreciate their resources, and seek harmony with both the environment and each other. In a world that often emphasizes individualism, this mantra calls for a return to collective values and shared responsibilities.
