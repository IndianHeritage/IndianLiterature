अग्नि त्वं नो अन्तम उता त्राताऽशिवो भव वरूथ्यः ||
agne tvaṁ no antama uta trātā śivo bhavā varūthyaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • Tvaṁ: This means "you," addressing Agni directly. It signifies a personal connection and reverence towards the divine.
  • No: This translates to "us," indicating a collective appeal or prayer from the community or individuals seeking blessings.
  • Antama: This word means "the ultimate" or "the highest." It suggests a quest for the highest truth or enlightenment.
  • Uta: This can be interpreted as "and" or "also," linking the previous and following concepts together.
  • Trātā: This means "protector" or "savior." It emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • Śivo: This word translates to "auspicious" or "blessed." It reflects the desire for well-being and harmony.
  • Bhavā: This means "become" or "may you be." It expresses a wish or hope for the divine to manifest in a certain way.
  • Varūthyaḥ: This term refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." It highlights the act of invoking divine presence in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "agne tvaṁ no antama uta trātā śivo bhavā varūthyaḥ" is a prayer for divine guidance and protection. It signifies the importance of seeking the blessings of Agni, the fire god, for purity, transformation, and auspiciousness in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers where individuals seek strength, protection, and a connection to the divine. It reminds us of the significance of invoking higher powers for support in our journey towards enlightenment and well-being.
