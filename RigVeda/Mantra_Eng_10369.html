सविता यन्त्रैः पृथिवीं अरम्णाद अस्कम्भने सविता dyām अदृṁहत् |अश्वम् इवाधुक्षद धुनिम अन्तरिक्षम् अतूर्ते बद्धं सविता समुद्रम् ||
savitā yantraiḥ pṛthivīm aramṇād askambhane savitā dyām adṛṁhat | aśvam ivādhukṣad dhunim antarikṣam atūrte baddhaṁ savitā samudram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Savitā: The Sun or the divine light.
    • Yantraiḥ: By means of instruments or tools.
    • Pṛthivīm: The Earth.
    • Aramṇād: From the act of plowing or cultivation.
    • Askambhane: In the act of stabilizing or supporting.
    • Dyām: The sky or the heavens.
    • Adṛṁhat: He has made or created.
    • Aśvam: A horse, symbolizing strength and speed.
    • Ivādhukṣad: Like a plow or a cultivator.
    • Dhunim: The atmosphere or the space.
    • Antarikṣam: The intermediate space or the sky.
    • Atūrte: In the act of binding or connecting.
    • Baddhaṁ: Bound or tied.
    • Samudram: The ocean or the vast waters.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Savitā, the Sun, as a source of energy and life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of tools and instruments in cultivating the Earth, symbolizing human effort and innovation.
    • The reference to the sky and the atmosphere highlights the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
    • The imagery of a horse and plowing suggests the need for strength and diligence in our endeavors.
    • Binding the ocean signifies the vast potential and resources available to us when we harness the power of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the use of modern tools and techniques can significantly enhance productivity, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on instruments for cultivation.
    • In environmental conservation, understanding the interconnectedness of ecosystems can lead to more sustainable practices, akin to the harmony suggested in the mantra.
    • In personal development, the strength and diligence symbolized by the horse can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination.
    • In community building, the idea of binding resources together can lead to collaborative efforts that harness collective strengths for greater impact.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine energy, human effort, and the interconnectedness of nature.
    • It encourages us to utilize our tools and resources wisely, fostering growth and stability in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can guide us in agriculture, environmental stewardship, personal development, and community collaboration.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility to harmonize our actions with the natural world for a sustainable future.
