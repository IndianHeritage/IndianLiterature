प्र द्यावाऽयज्ञैः पृथिवी ऋतावृद्धा मही तुषे विदथेṣu प्रचेतसा |देवेभिर्ये देवपुत्रे सुदंससेत्था धियā वारीयाणि प्रभूषतः ||
pra dyāvā yajñaiḥ pṛthivī ṛtāvṛdhā mahī stuṣe vidatheṣu pracetasā | devebhir ye devaputre sudaṁsasetthā dhiyā vāryāṇi prabhūṣataḥ ||
  • pra dyāvā yajñaiḥ: This phrase translates to "the heavens (dyāvā) are nourished by sacrifices (yajñaiḥ)." In real life, this can be understood as the idea that our actions, especially those that are selfless and for the benefit of others, contribute to the greater good of the universe. For example, community service or charitable acts can be seen as sacrifices that enrich the social fabric.
  • pṛthivī ṛtāvṛdhā: Here, "pṛthivī" refers to the Earth, and "ṛtāvṛdhā" means "nurtured by truth." This suggests that the Earth thrives on truth and righteousness. In practical terms, this can be related to sustainable living and environmental conservation, where living in harmony with nature and being truthful in our dealings with it leads to a healthier planet.
  • mahī stuṣe vidatheṣu pracetasā: "mahī" means "great," "stuṣe" means "praise," and "vidatheṣu" refers to "in gatherings." "pracetasā" implies "with wisdom." This part emphasizes the importance of wisdom in communal gatherings and the act of praising the great. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how we celebrate achievements in communities, such as award ceremonies or public acknowledgments of contributions, where wisdom and recognition play crucial roles.
  • devebhir ye devaputre sudaṁsasetthā: This translates to "those who are among the gods (devebhir) and are the offspring of the divine (devaputre) are well-placed." This can be interpreted as recognizing the divine potential within every individual. In real life, this can be related to mentorship and leadership, where individuals are encouraged to realize their potential and contribute positively to society.
  • dhiyā vāryāṇi prabhūṣataḥ: "dhiyā" means "with intellect," "vāryāṇi" refers to "the best," and "prabhūṣataḥ" means "to be praised." This suggests that the best actions are those guided by intellect and wisdom. In practical terms, this can be applied in decision-making processes in various fields, such as business, education, and governance, where informed and wise choices lead to better outcomes.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the universe, the importance of truth, wisdom, and selfless actions. It teaches us that our contributions, whether through sacrifices, sustainable practices, or wise decisions, have a profound impact on the world around us. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community service, practice environmental stewardship, and make informed choices that benefit society as a whole. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous world.
