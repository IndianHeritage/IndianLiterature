परीष्य सुवानो अक्षाऽ इन्दुरव्ये मदाच्युतः |धारा य ऊर्ध्वो अध्वरे भ्राजां नैति गव्ययुः ||
pari ṣya suvāno akṣā indur avye madacyutaḥ | dhārā ya ūrdhvo adhvare bhrājā naiti gavyayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pariṣya: Surrounding or encompassing
    • suvāno: Good or auspicious
    • akṣā: Eyes or vision
    • indur: Moon or lunar
    • avye: Not to be disturbed or unshaken
    • madacyutaḥ: One who is intoxicated with joy or bliss
    • dhārā: Flow or stream
    • ya: Who or that
    • ūrdhvo: Above or upward
    • adhvare: In the sacrifice or ritual
    • bhrājā: Shining or radiant
    • naiti: Does not go or does not lead
    • gavyayuḥ: Of the cows or related to cattle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is all-encompassing and auspicious.
    • The reference to the moon suggests a calming, nurturing force that brings joy and stability.
    • The idea of being unshaken indicates a state of peace and tranquility amidst chaos.
    • The flow or stream mentioned can be interpreted as the continuous flow of life and energy, which is essential in rituals and sacrifices.
    • The shining aspect emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and clarity in one's spiritual journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate to the idea of being surrounded by positive influences, akin to a supportive community that fosters growth and happiness.
    • The moon's symbolism can be seen in how people often seek solace and inspiration from nature, especially during nighttime, reflecting on their thoughts and emotions.
    • In stressful situations, maintaining a state of calmness and being unshaken can lead to better decision-making and emotional resilience.
    • Rituals in various cultures, such as weddings or festivals, often embody the flow of joy and community, where the collective energy uplifts everyone involved.
    • Seeking enlightenment through education, meditation, or self-reflection can lead to personal growth and a clearer understanding of one's purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of surrounding oneself with positive energies and maintaining inner peace.
    • It highlights the role of rituals and community in fostering joy and stability in life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, where one is able to navigate challenges with grace and clarity.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and joy in both personal and communal experiences.
