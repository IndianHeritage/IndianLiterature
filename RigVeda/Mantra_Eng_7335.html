त्वं ह यद् यविष्ठ्य सहसः सूनव आहुत |ऋतावा यज्ञियो भुवः ||
tvaṁ ha yad yaviṣṭhya sahasaḥ sūnav āhuta | ṛtāvā yajñiyo bhuvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: indeed
    • yad: whatever
    • yaviṣṭhya: is established
    • sahasaḥ: with strength
    • sūnav: of the sons
    • āhuta: invoked
    • ṛtāvā: in accordance with truth
    • yajñiyo: related to sacrifice
    • bhuvaḥ: of the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence and the strength that comes from invoking it.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in rituals and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "with strength" suggests that the power of the divine is invoked through sincere and truthful actions.
    • The mention of "sons" can be interpreted as the continuation of lineage and the importance of family in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as family gatherings, where the values of truth and strength are celebrated.
    • During significant life events like weddings or births, invoking the divine presence with sincerity can bring blessings and strength to the family.
    • In professional settings, adhering to truth and integrity in business practices can lead to success and respect, akin to the strength mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for truthfulness and strength in our actions, particularly in rituals and family life.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking the divine with sincerity can lead to blessings and prosperity.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to live a life of integrity, fostering strong relationships and a harmonious community.
