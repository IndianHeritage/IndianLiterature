यजिष्ठं त्वा ववृमहे देवं देवत्रा होतारम अमर्त्यम् |अस्य यज्ञस्य सुक्रतुम् ||
yajiṣṭhaṁ tvā vavṛmahe devaṁ devatrā hotāram amartyam | asya yajñasya sukratum ||
  • yajiṣṭhaṁ: This word translates to "the most excellent" or "the best." It signifies the highest form of worship or offering.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the deity being addressed in the mantra.
  • vavṛmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we praise." It indicates a collective effort in worship.
  • devaṁ: This means "divine" or "god." It emphasizes the sacred nature of the being being worshipped.
  • devatrā: This can be interpreted as "among the gods" or "of the divine beings," highlighting the importance of the deity in the pantheon.
  • hotāram: This refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers," indicating the role of the individual performing the ritual.
  • amartyam: This means "immortal" or "eternal," suggesting the timeless nature of the divine being.
  • asya: This translates to "of this," referring to the specific ritual or sacrifice being performed.
  • yajñasya: This means "of the sacrifice," emphasizing the importance of the ritual in connecting with the divine.
  • sukratum: This translates to "good action" or "beneficial deed," indicating the positive outcomes expected from the ritual.

The mantra essentially expresses a collective invocation to the divine, acknowledging the deity as the most excellent and immortal being, and emphasizing the significance of the ritual being performed. It highlights the role of the priest and the intention behind the sacrifice, which is to achieve beneficial outcomes.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, individuals can recite this mantra to invoke blessings and positive energy, ensuring that their intentions are aligned with the divine.
  • Personal Growth: The essence of the mantra can inspire individuals to strive for excellence in their actions and decisions, promoting a life of integrity and purpose.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal events, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the collective effort towards achieving common goals, fostering unity and cooperation.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking the divine in our actions, recognizing the role of rituals in connecting with higher powers, and striving for beneficial outcomes in our lives. It serves as a reminder that our intentions and actions should be aligned with the pursuit of excellence and goodness.
