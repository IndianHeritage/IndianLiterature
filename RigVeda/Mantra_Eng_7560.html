त्यान नु ये विघ rodasī तस्तभुर मरुतो हुवे |अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
tyān nu ye vi rodasī tastabhur maruto huve | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Mantra: "tyān nu ye vi rodasī tastabhur maruto huve | asya somasya pītaye ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are in the sky, may they bestow their blessings upon us; O Maruts, we invoke you for the enjoyment of this Soma."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • tyān: Those (who are)
    • nu: indeed
    • ye: who
    • vi: in the sky
    • rodasī: the heavens
    • tastabhur: may they bestow
    • maruto: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • huve: we invoke
    • asya: of this
    • somasya: Soma (the sacred drink)
    • pītaye: for enjoyment
  • Contextual Significance:
    • The mantra addresses the Maruts, who are considered the deities of storms and winds in Vedic literature. They symbolize strength, vitality, and the forces of nature.
    • Soma, in this context, refers to a ritual drink that was believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the offering to the gods during Vedic rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the invocation of natural forces can be seen in environmental movements that seek to honor and protect nature, much like the Vedic respect for the Maruts.
    • The concept of seeking blessings from higher powers can be related to various cultural practices where people pray for prosperity, health, and well-being, akin to the invocation of the Maruts for blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine forces of nature and seeking their blessings for sustenance and enjoyment in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interdependence between humanity and nature, encouraging respect and reverence for the natural world.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of rituals and prayers in fostering a sense of community and shared values, which is applicable in various cultural and spiritual practices today.
