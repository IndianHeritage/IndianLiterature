प्रो द्रोणे हरयः कर्मागमन पुनानास ऋज्यन्तो अभूवन् |इन्द्रो नो अस्य पूर्व्यः पपीयाद द्युक्षो मदस्य सोम्यस्य राजा ||
pro droṇe harayaḥ karmāgman punānāsa ṛjyanto abhūvan | indro no asya pūrvyaḥ papīyād dyukṣo madasya somyasya rājā ||
  • pro droṇe: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the presence of the great ones" or "in the company of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and wisdom from those who are knowledgeable.
  • harayaḥ: This term refers to "the enlightened" or "those who are free from ignorance." It signifies the qualities of clarity and understanding that one should aspire to embody.
  • karmāgman: This word translates to "actions that lead to progress" or "deeds that bring about success." It highlights the significance of performing righteous actions that contribute to personal and communal growth.
  • punānāsa: This can be understood as "purifying" or "cleansing." It suggests the need for self-reflection and the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions.
  • ṛjyanto: This term means "those who are righteous" or "virtuous." It emphasizes the value of living a life of integrity and moral uprightness.
  • abhūvan: This word translates to "they have become" or "they have manifested." It indicates the realization of one's potential through virtuous living and wise actions.
  • indro: Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods, this term symbolizes strength, power, and leadership. It represents the qualities of a good leader who protects and guides others.
  • no asya pūrvyaḥ: This phrase means "may he grant us the blessings of the past." It signifies the importance of learning from history and the wisdom of our ancestors.
  • papīyād: This term translates to "may he be greater" or "may he excel." It expresses the desire for growth and excellence in one's endeavors.
  • dyukṣo: This word means "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It highlights the importance of strength, both physical and mental, in overcoming challenges.
  • madasya: This term refers to "of joy" or "of bliss." It signifies the importance of joy and happiness in life, which can be achieved through righteous living.
  • somyasya: This word translates to "of the gentle" or "of the kind." It emphasizes the value of compassion and kindness in our interactions with others.
  • rājā: This term means "king" or "ruler." It symbolizes authority and responsibility, reminding us of the importance of leadership in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, performing righteous actions, and embodying virtues such as strength, joy, and kindness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles where one must guide others with integrity and compassion. It also serves as a reminder to learn from the past and strive for personal growth and excellence. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and foster a harmonious society.
