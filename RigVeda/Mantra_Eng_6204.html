समुत्ये महतीर अपः संक्षोणी समुसूर्यम् |सं वज्रम पर्वशो दधुः ||
sam u tye mahatīr apaḥ saṁ kṣoṇī sam u sūryam | saṁ vajram parvaśo dadhuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • u: and, also
    • tye: to you, for you
    • mahatīr: great, vast
    • apaḥ: waters, streams
    • saṁ: together, in union
    • kṣoṇī: earth, land
    • u: and, also
    • sūryam: sun, light
    • saṁ: together, in union
    • vajram: thunderbolt, strength
    • parvaśo: of the powerful, mighty
    • dadhuḥ: they have given, bestowed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and harmony of natural elements.
    • The phrase "sam u tye mahatīr apaḥ" suggests that the great waters are in harmony with each other.
    • "saṁ kṣoṇī" indicates the earth's connection with these waters, emphasizing the importance of land and water in sustaining life.
    • The mention of "sūryam" highlights the sun's role in providing light and energy, essential for growth and sustenance.
    • Finally, "saṁ vajram parvaśo dadhuḥ" signifies that the powerful forces of nature, represented by the thunderbolt, have bestowed their strength upon the earth and its elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the harmony between water (irrigation) and sunlight is crucial for crop growth.
    • Environmental conservation efforts emphasize the interconnectedness of land, water, and sunlight in maintaining ecosystems.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be recited to invoke the balance of natural elements in one's life, promoting peace and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity and balance among the elements of nature.
    • It teaches us to respect and maintain the harmony between water, earth, and sunlight, which is vital for life.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can guide sustainable practices, environmental awareness, and personal well-being.
