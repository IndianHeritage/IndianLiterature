इमा उ वाम भृमयो मन्यमाना युवावते न तुज्या अभूवन |क्व1 त्यद इन्द्रावरुणा यशो वां येन स्मा सिनं भरतः सकिभ्यः ||
imā u vām bhṛmayo manyamānā yuvāvate na tujyā abhūvan | kva1 tyad indrāvaruṇā yaśo vāṁ yena smā sinam bharathaḥ sakhibhyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "imā u vām bhṛmayo manyamānā yuvāvate na tujyā abhūvan | kva1 tyad indrāvaruṇā yaśo vāṁ yena smā sinam bharathaḥ sakhibhyaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imā: these
    • u: and
    • vām: you
    • bhṛmayo: we are thinking
    • manyamānā: considering
    • yuvāvate: in youth
    • na: not
    • tujyā: to be neglected
    • abhūvan: have become
    • kva: where
    • tyad: that
    • indrāvaruṇā: Indra and Varuna
    • yaśo: fame
    • vāṁ: your
    • yena: by whom
    • smā: we
    • sinam: to be protected
    • bharathaḥ: O Bharata
    • sakhibhyaḥ: to friends
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep sense of camaraderie and the importance of youth and friendship.
    • The phrase "imā u vām bhṛmayo" suggests a collective thought process, indicating that the speaker is addressing a group, possibly friends or companions.
    • The mention of "yuvāvate" emphasizes the vitality and energy of youth, which is often associated with creativity, ambition, and the pursuit of dreams.
    • The line "na tujyā abhūvan" implies that the thoughts and actions of the youth should not be neglected or dismissed, highlighting the value of their contributions.
    • Furthermore, invoking "Indra and Varuna" symbolizes the divine protection and guidance that one seeks in their endeavors, suggesting that friendship and collaboration can lead to success and fame.
    • The concluding part, "yena smā sinam bharathaḥ sakhibhyaḥ," reinforces the idea of seeking protection and support from friends, which is crucial in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project at work, the collaboration and ideas of all members, especially the younger ones, can lead to innovative solutions that might not have been considered otherwise.
    • In sports, the bond between teammates often drives them to perform better, as they support and motivate each other, reflecting the essence of friendship highlighted in the mantra.
    • In educational settings, mentoring relationships between older and younger students can foster growth, where the wisdom of experience meets the fresh perspectives of youth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, friendship, and collective thought in achieving success and navigating life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that the contributions of young individuals should be valued and that collaboration can lead to greater achievements.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork, mentorship, and community building, where the strength of relationships and shared goals can lead to positive outcomes.
