अस्मे धेहि श्रवो बृहद् द्युम्नं सहस्रसातमम् |इन्द्र ता रथिनीर इषः ||
asme dhehi śravo bṛhad dyumnaṁ sahasrasātamam | indra tā rathinīr iṣaḥ ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us." It signifies a request or a plea directed towards a higher power.
  • dhehi: Meaning "bestow" or "grant," this word indicates a desire for something valuable or beneficial to be given.
  • śravo: This term refers to "fame" or "glory." It emphasizes the importance of recognition and honor in one's life.
  • bṛhad: Translated as "great" or "vast," it signifies something of immense value or significance.
  • dyumnaṁ: This word means "splendor" or "brilliance," highlighting the beauty and radiance associated with glory.
  • sahasrasātamam: This term can be broken down into "sahasra," meaning "thousand," and "sātama," meaning "the best." Together, it conveys the idea of the highest or the best among thousands.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic literature, Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership.
  • tā: This word means "that" or "those," indicating a connection to the previous terms.
  • rathinīr: This term refers to "charioteers" or "drivers," symbolizing guidance and direction in life.
  • iṣaḥ: Meaning "desire" or "wish," it signifies the aspirations and goals one seeks to achieve.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the bestowal of great fame and glory upon the individual or community. It seeks the blessings of Indra, the powerful deity, to guide and support the aspirant in their endeavors.
    • The invocation of "sahasrasātamam" suggests a yearning for excellence that stands out among many, emphasizing the desire to achieve greatness in a competitive world.
    • The mention of "rathinīr" indicates the importance of having skilled guides or mentors who can steer one towards success, much like a charioteer guides a chariot.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may recite this mantra before a significant presentation or project, seeking recognition and success in their career.
    • A student preparing for exams might use this mantra to invoke the blessings of wisdom and guidance, hoping to achieve top marks among peers.
    • In sports, athletes often seek motivation and support from higher powers, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for strength and guidance in competition.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving greatness and recognition. It serves as a reminder of the importance of guidance and mentorship in our pursuits.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various fields, including education, career, and personal development, where individuals seek to excel and make a mark.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to strive for excellence while acknowledging the need for support and guidance from others, be it divine or human.
