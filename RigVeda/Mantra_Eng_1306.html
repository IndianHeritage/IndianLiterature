पुरू वर्पांस्य अश्विनाऽ दधाना निपेदव उहथुर आशुम अश्वम् |सहस्रसां वाजिनम् अप्रतीतम अहिहनं श्रवस्यं तरुत्रम् ||
purū varpāṁsy aśvinā dadhānā ni pedava ūhathur āśum aśvam | sahasrasāṁ vājinam apratītam ahihanaṁ śravasya1ṁ tarutram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • purū: many, abundant
    • varpāṁsy: offerings, gifts
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen
    • dadhānā: bestowing, providing
    • ni: not, without
    • pedava: obstacles, hindrances
    • ūhathur: they have brought forth, they have created
    • āśum: swiftly, quickly
    • aśvam: horse, symbolizing speed and power
    • sahasrasāṁ: of thousands, numerous
    • vājinam: charioteer, one who drives
    • apratītam: unperceived, unknown
    • ahihanaṁ: of the serpent, symbolizing strength and resilience
    • śravasya: of fame, glory
    • tarutram: the swift, the quick
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The phrase "purū varpāṁsy aśvinā dadhānā" suggests that the Ashvins are bestowing numerous offerings, indicating abundance and generosity.
    • "Ni pedava ūhathur" implies that they create or bring forth without any obstacles, emphasizing the removal of hindrances in life.
    • The mention of "āśum aśvam" symbolizes the swift and powerful nature of the blessings being sought, akin to the speed of a horse.
    • "Sahasrasāṁ vājinam" refers to the multitude of charioteers, suggesting that there are many paths to success and glory.
    • "Apratītam ahihanaṁ" indicates the unknown strength that lies within, akin to the hidden potential in every individual.
    • "Śravasyaṁ tarutram" emphasizes the swift attainment of fame and glory, suggesting that with the right blessings, success can come quickly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a job market, individuals often seek blessings for success and the removal of obstacles, much like the invocation of the Ashvins.
    • In sports, athletes may visualize their swift performance and the removal of barriers to achieve their goals, paralleling the themes of speed and overcoming challenges in this mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may strive to unlock their hidden potential, akin to "apratītam ahihanaṁ," by seeking guidance and support from mentors or coaches.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving success swiftly.
    • It highlights the belief in the abundance of blessings available to those who seek them with sincerity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as career advancement, personal growth, and athletic performance, reminding us of the power of intention and the support we can receive from the universe.
