मरत्वां इन्द्र मीढ्वः पिबा सोमं शतक्रतो | अस्मिन यज्ञे पुरुष्टुत ||
marutvām̐ indra mīḍhvaḥ pibā somaṁ śatakrato | asmin yajñe puruṣṭuta ||
  • Marutvām̐: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with wind and storms. They symbolize strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods and the god of thunder, rain, and war. He represents leadership, courage, and the protector of humanity.
  • Mīḍhvaḥ: This word means "to enjoy" or "to partake." It signifies the act of consuming or experiencing something beneficial.
  • Pibā: This term translates to "drink." It is often used in the context of consuming soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Somaṁ: Soma is a ritual drink in Vedic tradition, associated with the moon and considered to be a source of divine energy and inspiration.
  • Śatakrato: This epithet of Indra means "the one with a hundred powers" or "the one who has a hundred victories." It emphasizes his strength and capability.
  • Asmin: This word means "in this" or "in this context," indicating the specific ritual or offering being performed.
  • Yajñe: Yajña refers to a Vedic ritual of offerings accompanied by chanting of Vedic mantras. It is a sacred act of worship and connection with the divine.
  • Puruṣṭuta: This term means "well praised" or "honored." It signifies the respect and reverence given to the deities during the ritual.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the Maruts and Indra to partake in the offerings made during a yajña, specifically inviting them to enjoy the soma, which is a symbol of divine energy and inspiration. The invocation highlights the importance of nature's forces and divine protection in human endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the need for strength and support from higher powers in our pursuits. For example, during challenging times, individuals may seek inspiration and courage from their beliefs, much like invoking the Maruts and Indra for strength. In a corporate setting, leaders may draw upon the qualities of Indra to inspire their teams, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine support and the acknowledgment of natural forces in our lives. It teaches us to respect and honor the energies around us, whether in personal challenges or communal efforts. By invoking these deities, we align ourselves with the greater forces of the universe, seeking strength and inspiration in our endeavors.
