त्वं होतā मनुर्हितो वह्निर् आसā विदुष्टरः | अग्ने यक्षि दिवो विशः ||
tvaṁ hotā manurhito vahnir āsā viduṣṭaraḥ | agne yakṣi divo viśaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, in this case, Agni, the fire god.
  • hotā - This means "the one who is invoked" or "the priest," referring to Agni as the one who is called upon during rituals.
  • manurhito - This can be interpreted as "the one who is established by Manu," where Manu represents the archetypal human or the progenitor of mankind in Vedic texts.
  • vahnir - This word means "fire," symbolizing purity, energy, and transformation.
  • āsā - This translates to "hope" or "aspiration," indicating a longing for divine blessings.
  • viduṣṭaraḥ - This means "the one who is knowledgeable" or "the wise one," emphasizing the wisdom associated with Agni.
  • agne - A direct invocation to Agni, reinforcing the focus on the fire god.
  • yakṣi - This translates to "may you bestow" or "may you grant," indicating a request for blessings.
  • divo - This means "of the heavens," suggesting a connection to the divine or celestial realms.
  • viśaḥ - This translates to "the community" or "the people," indicating a collective aspect of the invocation.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is invoked for blessings, wisdom, and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of fire not only as a physical element but also as a spiritual force that connects humanity with the divine. The invocation of Agni signifies the hope for enlightenment and the well-being of the community.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the seeking of divine guidance.
  • Personal Aspirations: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking clarity and wisdom in their personal or professional lives, hoping for the fire of knowledge to illuminate their paths.
  • Community Well-being: The collective aspect of the mantra can be used in community gatherings, emphasizing unity and the shared aspiration for prosperity and harmony.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that not only purifies and transforms but also connects individuals and communities to higher wisdom and aspirations. It serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking divine energies in our lives to foster growth, understanding, and unity.
