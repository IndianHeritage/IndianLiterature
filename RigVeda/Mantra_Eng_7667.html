उप त्वा जामयो गिरो देदिशतीर हविष्कृतः |वायोर अनीक अस्तिरन् ||
upa tvā jāmayo giro dediśatīr haviṣkṛtaḥ | vāyor anīke asthiran ||
  • upa tvā - This phrase translates to "I approach you" or "I come to you." It signifies a sense of seeking connection or guidance.
  • jāmayo - This word can be interpreted as "born of the womb" or "offspring." It often refers to the essence of life and creation.
  • giro - This term means "words" or "speech." It emphasizes the power of communication and the importance of expressing thoughts and intentions.
  • dediśatīr - This can be understood as "may you grant" or "may you bestow." It reflects a plea for blessings or support from a higher power.
  • haviṣkṛtaḥ - This word refers to "sacrificial offerings" or "that which is prepared for the divine." It highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • vāyor - This means "of the wind" or "of the air." It symbolizes the vital force and the unseen energies that surround us.
  • anīke - This term translates to "in the multitude" or "in the group." It suggests the importance of community and collective efforts.
  • asthiran - This word means "unsteady" or "unstable." It reflects the transient nature of life and the challenges we face.

In essence, this mantra expresses a deep yearning for connection with the divine, seeking guidance and blessings for the offspring and the community. It acknowledges the power of words and the importance of rituals in establishing a relationship with the divine forces that govern our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Parenting: Parents can recite this mantra to seek blessings for their children, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and guiding them through life's challenges.
  • Community Gatherings: During community events or rituals, this mantra can be invoked to foster unity and collective strength, reminding individuals of their interconnectedness.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra in meditation or prayer to seek clarity and guidance in their personal journeys, acknowledging the transient nature of life and the need for stability.

In conclusion, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for connection, guidance, and the recognition of the collective human experience. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, communication, and the divine forces that influence our lives.
