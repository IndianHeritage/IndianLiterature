उप छायाम iva घृणेर अगन्म शरमा ते वयम् |अग्नि हिरण्यसंदृशः ||
upa cchāyām iva ghṛṇer aganma śarma te vayam | agne hiraṇyasaṁdṛśaḥ ||
  • upa cchāyām iva ghṛṇer aganma śarma te vayam
    • upa - towards, near
    • cchāyām - shadow, protection
    • iva - like, as if
    • ghṛṇer - of love, affection
    • aganma - we have come, we have arrived
    • śarma - comfort, joy, shelter
    • te - your
    • vayam - we
  • agne hiraṇyasaṁdṛśaḥ
    • agne - O Agni, the divine fire
    • hiraṇya - golden, wealth
    • saṁdṛśaḥ - of good sight, auspicious

This mantra can be translated as: "We have come to your shelter, like the shadow of love. O Agni, who is of golden appearance, may we find comfort in you."


The mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing warmth, light, and the divine presence. The imagery of "shadow of love" suggests a protective and nurturing aspect, indicating that the speaker seeks refuge and comfort in Agni's presence. The reference to "golden appearance" signifies purity, wealth, and auspiciousness, emphasizing the divine qualities of Agni.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Protection: Just as one seeks shelter from the sun or rain, individuals can invoke this mantra during times of uncertainty or fear, asking for divine protection and guidance.
  • Creating a Safe Space: In personal relationships, the idea of being a "shadow of love" can be reflected in how we support and protect our loved ones, creating a safe and nurturing environment.
  • Spiritual Practices: During rituals or meditation, reciting this mantra can help individuals connect with the divine, seeking comfort and clarity in their spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and comfort. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking shelter in times of need, whether it be emotional, spiritual, or physical. By invoking Agni, individuals can cultivate a sense of safety and warmth in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with each other.
