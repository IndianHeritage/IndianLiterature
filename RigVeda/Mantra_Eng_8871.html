को अस्य वेद प्रथमस्याह्नः का ईं ददर्श का इह प्र वोचत् |बृहन् मित्रस्य वरुणस्य धाम कद उ ब्रव आहनो वीच्यां नृन् ||
ko asya veda prathamasyāhnaḥ ka īṁ dadarśa ka iha pra vocat | bṛhan mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma kad u brava āhano vīcyā nṝn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • asya: of this
    • veda: knows or understands
    • prathamasyāhnaḥ: of the first day
    • ka: who
    • īṁ: what
    • dadarśa: saw
    • ka: who
    • iha: here
    • pra vocat: speaks out
    • bṛhan: great
    • mitrasya: of Mitra
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna
    • dhāma: abode or realm
    • kad: when
    • u: or
    • brava: speak
    • āhano: of the mortals
    • vīcyā: of the wise
    • nṝn: of men
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about knowledge and understanding.
    • It reflects on the nature of existence and the divine, specifically invoking the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent cosmic order and moral law.
    • The mantra asks who truly knows the essence of the first day of creation and who has seen or spoken about it.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in human life, suggesting that true knowledge is rare and valuable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a scientific context, this mantra can be related to the quest for knowledge about the universe's origins, akin to the questions posed by cosmologists about the Big Bang.
    • In a philosophical sense, it resonates with the inquiries of great thinkers like Socrates, who famously stated, "The unexamined life is not worth living," highlighting the importance of self-reflection and understanding.
    • In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek deeper truths in their personal experiences, relationships, and the world around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It encourages individuals to question their understanding of existence and to seek wisdom from both divine and earthly sources.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, personal development, and spiritual practices, reminding us that the journey of knowledge is ongoing and essential for a meaningful life.
