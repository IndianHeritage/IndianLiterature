सुब्रह्माणं देववंतं बृहन्तं उरुं गभीरं पृथुबुध्नं इन्द्र |श्रुतर्षिम उग्रं अभिमातिषाहं अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयं दाह ||
subrahmāṇaṁ devavantam bṛhantam uruṁ gabhīram pṛthubudhnam indra | śrutaṛṣim ugram abhimātiṣāham asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • Subrahmāṇaṁ: This term refers to the auspicious and divine aspect of the Supreme Being. In real life, it can be related to the pursuit of goodness and virtue in our actions.
  • Devavantam: Meaning 'possessing divine qualities', this suggests that individuals should strive to embody qualities such as compassion, wisdom, and integrity, much like revered leaders or mentors in our lives.
  • Bṛhantam: This translates to 'the great one' or 'the vast one', symbolizing the infinite nature of the universe. In practical terms, it encourages us to think beyond our immediate surroundings and consider the larger impact of our actions.
  • Uruṁ: Meaning 'broad' or 'expansive', this can be seen in the context of broadening our horizons through education and experiences, allowing us to grow and adapt in a changing world.
  • Gabhīram: This word means 'deep' or 'profound', suggesting the importance of deep understanding and introspection in our lives. For example, engaging in meditation or reflective practices can lead to greater self-awareness.
  • Pṛthubudhnam: This term refers to 'one with vast intelligence'. In real life, it emphasizes the value of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging us to seek learning opportunities and share our insights with others.
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and leadership. This can be related to the qualities of a good leader in our communities or workplaces, who inspires and guides others.
  • Śrutaṛṣim: Meaning 'the seer of what is heard', this highlights the importance of listening and understanding different perspectives. In practice, active listening in conversations can lead to better relationships and collaboration.
  • Ugram: This translates to 'fierce' or 'powerful', suggesting that we should harness our inner strength to overcome challenges. For instance, facing fears or obstacles with determination can lead to personal growth.
  • Abhimātiṣāham: This term means 'I am the one who is determined'. It encourages us to cultivate a strong will and perseverance in pursuing our goals, much like athletes training for a competition.
  • Asmabhyaṁ: This translates to 'for us', indicating a sense of community and shared purpose. It reminds us of the importance of working together for common goals, such as community service or collaborative projects.
  • Citraṁ: Meaning 'varied' or 'colorful', this reflects the diversity of life and experiences. Embracing diversity in our communities can lead to richer interactions and a more inclusive society.
  • Vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term refers to 'the one who brings prosperity'. It signifies the importance of contributing positively to society, whether through our careers, volunteer work, or personal relationships.
  • Rayiṁ: Meaning 'wealth' or 'prosperity', this emphasizes the pursuit of not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness in life.
  • Dāḥ: This translates to 'give' or 'bestow', highlighting the importance of generosity and sharing with others, which can lead to a more fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing virtues such as wisdom, strength, community, and generosity. Its significance lies in guiding individuals to lead a life of purpose and integrity, fostering personal growth while contributing positively to society. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, community service, and personal development, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence for all.
