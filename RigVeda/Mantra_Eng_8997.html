अग्निर जातो अथर्वणा विदद विश्वानि काव्याः |भुवद दूतो विवस्वतो वि वो मदे प्रियो यमस्य काम्यो विवक्षसे ||
agnir jāto atharvaṇā vidad viśvāni kāvyā | bhuvad dūto vivasvato vi vo made priyo yamasya kāmyo vivakṣase ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Jāto: Meaning "born" or "arisen," it signifies the emergence of Agni as a vital force.
  • Atharvaṇā: This term relates to the Atharva Veda, which emphasizes knowledge and wisdom, particularly in practical and worldly matters.
  • Vidad: Translated as "to know" or "to provide," indicating the role of Agni in imparting knowledge and guidance.
  • Viśvāni: Meaning "all" or "universal," it suggests the inclusiveness of Agni's influence.
  • Kāvyā: Referring to "poetic" or "creative," it highlights the artistic and expressive aspects of existence.
  • Bhuvad: Meaning "earthly" or "material," it connects the divine with the physical world.
  • Dūto: Translated as "messenger," indicating Agni's role in communication between the divine and human realms.
  • Vivasvato: Referring to "the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Vi: Meaning "you," addressing the audience or the divine.
  • Made: Translated as "joy" or "delight," indicating the positive emotions associated with Agni's presence.
  • Priyo: Meaning "beloved," suggesting the cherished nature of Agni in rituals and daily life.
  • Yamasya: Referring to "Yama," the god of death and moral order, indicating the balance between life and death.
  • Kāmyo: Meaning "desired" or "wishful," indicating the aspirations and goals of individuals.
  • Vivakṣase: Translated as "to express" or "to declare," emphasizing the importance of communication and intention.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Agni as a transformative force that connects the material and spiritual realms. It highlights the importance of knowledge, creativity, and communication in achieving one's desires and aspirations. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In rituals and ceremonies, invoking Agni for blessings and guidance.
  • In personal development, using the principles of knowledge and creativity to achieve goals.
  • In fostering communication and understanding in relationships, reflecting the messenger aspect of Agni.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of seeking knowledge and transformation through the divine presence of Agni.
