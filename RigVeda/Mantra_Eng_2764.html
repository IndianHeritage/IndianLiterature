आ ते कारो श्रिणवामाः वचांसि ययाथ दूरत् अनसा रथेन |नि ते नंसै पीप्यानेव योषा मर्यायेव कन्या शाश्वतै ते ||
ā te kāro śṛṇavāmā vacāṁsi yayātha dūrād anasā rathena | ni te naṁsai pīpyāneva yoṣā maryāyeva kanyā śaśvacai te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: From you
    • kāro: Action or work
    • śṛṇavāmā: We listen
    • vacāṁsi: Words or speech
    • yayātha: By which
    • dūrād: From afar
    • anasā: Without any hindrance
    • rathena: By the chariot
    • ni te: To you
    • naṁsai: We offer
    • pīpyāneva: Like a thirsty one
    • yoṣā: Woman
    • maryāyeva: Like a maiden
    • kanyā: Daughter or girl
    • śaśvacai: Everlasting or eternal
    • te: To you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep yearning for connection and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of listening to divine words and guidance, which can be seen as a metaphor for seeking wisdom in our lives.
    • The reference to a chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where we navigate through challenges and seek support from higher powers.
    • The imagery of a thirsty woman reflects the human desire for fulfillment and the need for spiritual nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of listening to mentors and leaders who guide us in our careers.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the need for open communication and understanding between partners, akin to listening to each other's needs.
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom from sacred texts or teachings, fostering a deeper connection with the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking guidance and wisdom from higher sources, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual contexts.
    • It teaches us to be receptive to the messages that come from afar, reminding us that true understanding often requires patience and openness.
    • In essence, it encourages us to embark on our life's journey with a thirst for knowledge and a willingness to listen, ensuring that we remain connected to our purpose and the divine.
