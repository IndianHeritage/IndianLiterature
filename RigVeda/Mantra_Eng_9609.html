अभ्रपृषो न वाचां पृषा वसु हविष्मन्तो न यज्ञा विजानुषः |सुमारुतं न ब्रह्माणं अर्हसे गणम अस्तोष्य एषां न शोभसे ||
abhrapruṣo na vācā pruṣā vasu haviṣmanto na yajñā vijānuṣaḥ | sumārutaṁ na brahmāṇam arhase gaṇam astoṣy eṣāṁ na śobhase ||
  • abhrapruṣo: This term can be broken down into "abhr" meaning cloud and "pruṣo" which can be interpreted as the one who is associated with or comes from the clouds. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the nurturing aspect of nature, as clouds bring rain which is essential for agriculture.
  • na vācā pruṣā: "na" means not, "vācā" refers to speech or words, and "pruṣā" can be understood as the one who is associated with. This phrase suggests that the essence or the true nature of something cannot be captured merely by words. For example, the beauty of a sunset cannot be fully described; it must be experienced.
  • vasu haviṣmanto: "vasu" refers to wealth or abundance, and "haviṣmanto" relates to offerings or sacrifices. This indicates that true wealth comes from the offerings we make, whether they are material or spiritual. In modern life, this can be seen in charitable acts where giving leads to a sense of fulfillment and community well-being.
  • na yajñā vijānuṣaḥ: "yajñā" means sacrifice or ritual, and "vijānuṣaḥ" refers to those who are knowledgeable or wise. This suggests that true wisdom is not just in performing rituals but in understanding their significance. For instance, in many cultures, rituals are performed to honor ancestors, and understanding their importance can deepen familial bonds.
  • sumārutaṁ: This term can be interpreted as the good or auspicious winds. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of favorable circumstances or opportunities that come our way. For example, a job opportunity that aligns perfectly with one’s skills can be seen as a "sumārutaṁ".
  • na brahmāṇam: "brahmāṇam" refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. This phrase suggests that the essence of the universe is beyond mere understanding. In real life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of knowledge and the realization that some truths are beyond human comprehension.
  • arhase gaṇam: "arhase" means deserving or worthy, and "gaṇam" refers to a group or community. This indicates that a community that is deserving of respect and honor should be acknowledged. In contemporary society, this can relate to recognizing the contributions of various communities, such as healthcare workers during a pandemic.
  • astoṣy eṣāṁ: "astoṣy" means to be satisfied or pleased, and "eṣāṁ" refers to these offerings or actions. This implies that the satisfaction derived from our actions is significant. For example, volunteering for a cause can bring immense personal satisfaction and joy.
  • na śobhase: "na" means not, and "śobhase" refers to beauty or radiance. This suggests that without the right actions and intentions, beauty is superficial. In real life, this can be seen in the idea that true beauty comes from kindness and compassion rather than just physical appearance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper meanings behind words, actions, and rituals. It teaches that true wealth and wisdom come from our intentions and the offerings we make to the world. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, personal relationships, and spiritual practices. The significance lies in recognizing that our actions, driven by genuine intentions, lead to true fulfillment and a harmonious existence with the universe.
