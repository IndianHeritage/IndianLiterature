माचिदन्यद्विशंसतसखायोमारिषण्यत। इन्द्रमित्स्तोतावृषणं सचा सुतेमुहुर्वुक्ताश्च शंसत॥
mā cid anyad vi śaṁsata sakhāyo mā riṣaṇyata | indram it stotā vṛṣaṇaṁ sacā sute muhur ukthā ca śaṁsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā cid: do not (mā) any (cid)
    • anyad: anything else
    • vi: apart
    • śaṁsata: speak of (or praise)
    • sakhāyo: friends (or companions)
    • mā: do not
    • riṣaṇyata: be angry (or quarrel)
    • indram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • it: thus
    • stotā: the one who praises
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: the strong (or powerful)
    • sacā: and with
    • sute: the offerings (or gifts)
    • muhur: repeatedly
    • ukthā: hymns (or praises)
    • ca: and
    • śaṁsata: speak of (or praise)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony among friends and companions.
    • It advises against speaking ill of others or harboring anger, promoting a sense of community and support.
    • The reference to Indra signifies the strength and power that comes from collective praise and acknowledgment.
    • It encourages the repetition of hymns and praises, suggesting that consistent appreciation strengthens bonds.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of appreciation can lead to better teamwork and productivity. For instance, regularly acknowledging colleagues' efforts can enhance collaboration.
    • In friendships, avoiding gossip and negativity can strengthen relationships. For example, celebrating each other's successes rather than competing can create a supportive environment.
    • In community settings, collective efforts in praising local heroes or initiatives can build a stronger community spirit, as seen in neighborhood events that honor volunteers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, appreciation, and positive communication in our lives.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, highlighting the power of collective praise and harmony.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a more supportive and collaborative atmosphere, ultimately leading to greater success and fulfillment.
