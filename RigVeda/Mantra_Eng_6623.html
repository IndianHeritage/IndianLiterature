स्मद एतया सुखीरत्याश्विनā श्वेतया धियā |वहेते शुभ्रयावानā ||
smad etayā sukīrtyāśvinā śvetayā dhiyā | vahethe śubhrayāvānā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • smad: "with us" or "together with us"
    • etayā: "by this" or "through this"
    • sukīrtyā: "with good fame" or "with auspicious reputation"
    • śvinā: "with the Ashvins" (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • śvetayā: "with purity" or "with clarity"
    • dhiyā: "with intellect" or "with understanding"
    • vahethe: "may we be endowed" or "may we possess"
    • śubhrayāvānā: "with auspiciousness" or "with purity and brightness"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good reputation and purity in thought and action.
    • The phrase "may we be endowed" suggests a desire for divine grace and support in achieving a life filled with auspiciousness and clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, maintaining a good reputation (sukīrtyā) can lead to better opportunities and collaborations. For instance, a company known for its ethical practices attracts more clients and partners.
    • In personal relationships, being pure in intentions (śvetayā) fosters trust and strengthens bonds. For example, friends who communicate openly and honestly build deeper connections.
    • Seeking knowledge and understanding (dhiyā) is crucial in education. Students who approach their studies with clarity and dedication often achieve better results and personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the values of purity, good reputation, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek divine blessings while striving for personal and communal well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and socially.
