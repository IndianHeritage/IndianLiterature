मन्त्र: चक्राणासः परीनहम् पृथिव्या हिरण्येन मणिना शुम्भमानाः |न हिन्वानासस तितिरुस त इन्द्रं परि स्पशो अदधात सूर्येण ||
cakrāṇāsaḥ parīṇaham pṛthivyā hiraṇyena maṇinā śumbhamānāḥ | na hinvānāsas titirus ta indram pari spaśo adadhāt sūryeṇa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakrāṇāsaḥ: Those who are endowed with the wheel (or cycle) of life.
    • parīṇaham: I am surrounded or encompassed.
    • pṛthivyā: By the earth.
    • hiraṇyena: With gold or wealth.
    • maṇinā: By the jewel.
    • śumbhamānāḥ: Being adorned or decorated.
    • na hinvānāsas: Do not harm or do not destroy.
    • titirus: Those who are striving or seeking.
    • ta: They.
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods.
    • pari: All around.
    • spaśo: Of the touch or contact.
    • adadhāt: He established or created.
    • sūryeṇa: With the sun.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of life and the divine.
    • The phrase "cakrāṇāsaḥ parīṇaham" suggests that we are all part of a larger cycle, influenced by the earth and the cosmos.
    • The mention of "hiraṇyena maṇinā" indicates the importance of wealth and beauty in our lives, but it also implies that these should not lead to harm.
    • "Na hinvānāsas" serves as a reminder to avoid destruction and to seek harmony.
    • The reference to "Indra" symbolizes strength and leadership, suggesting that we should strive for greatness while being mindful of our actions.
    • Finally, "sūryeṇa" connects us to the sun, representing life, energy, and the source of all existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can embody the essence of this mantra by fostering a culture of collaboration and respect, ensuring that their pursuit of success does not harm their employees or the environment.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra encourages individuals to appreciate the beauty and wealth of love and friendship while being cautious not to let jealousy or greed destroy those bonds.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions should protect the earth and its resources, promoting sustainability rather than exploitation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of balance in life, urging us to recognize our place within the larger cycle of existence.
    • It teaches us to value wealth and beauty while being responsible stewards of our environment and relationships.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a harmonious existence, where our actions contribute positively to the world around us, reflecting the divine order represented by Indra and the life-giving sun.
