नवग्वासः सुतसोमास इन्द्रं दशग्वासो अभ्यर्चन्त्यर्कैः |गव्यं चिदूर्वमपिधानवन्तं तं चिन् नरः शशमानाऽप व्रण ||
navagvāsaḥ sutasomāsa indraṁ daśagvāso abhy arcanty arkaiḥ | gavyaṁ cid ūrvam apidhānavantaṁ taṁ cin naraḥ śaśamānā apa vran ||
  • navagvāsaḥ: This term refers to the "ninefold" or "nine kinds of" offerings or aspects. In a broader sense, it can symbolize the various dimensions of life and existence.
  • sutasomāsa: This can be interpreted as "the sons of the moon" or "the descendants of the moon," which may represent the connection to divine or celestial influences in our lives.
  • indraṁ: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens. He symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • daśagvāso: This term means "tenfold" or "ten kinds of" offerings, indicating a comprehensive approach to worship and devotion.
  • abhy arcanty: This phrase means "they worship" or "they offer reverence," highlighting the act of devotion and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • arkaiḥ: This refers to "hymns" or "praises," emphasizing the significance of words and expressions in spiritual practices.
  • gavyaṁ: This term can be understood as "that which is related to cows" or "nourishing," symbolizing sustenance and abundance.
  • cid: This word means "that which is conscious" or "awareness," pointing to the importance of mindfulness and consciousness in our actions.
  • ūrvam: This can be interpreted as "the earth" or "the ground," representing stability and the physical realm.
  • apidhānavantaṁ: This term suggests "that which is covered" or "that which is hidden," indicating the deeper truths and mysteries of life.
  • taṁ: This means "that" or "him," referring to the divine presence or the ultimate reality.
  • cin: This word means "to know" or "to perceive," emphasizing the importance of awareness and understanding.
  • naraḥ: This term means "man" or "human," highlighting the human experience and our connection to the divine.
  • śaśamānā: This can be interpreted as "those who are prosperous" or "those who are flourishing," indicating the rewards of devotion and righteousness.
  • apa vran: This phrase means "to remove obstacles" or "to eliminate suffering," emphasizing the transformative power of faith and worship.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the multifaceted nature of existence, and the connection between the divine and humanity. It signifies the importance of worship, mindfulness, and the pursuit of knowledge in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in spiritual practices, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine. It encourages us to recognize the abundance in our lives and to seek deeper understanding and awareness, ultimately leading to personal growth and the removal of obstacles in our paths.
