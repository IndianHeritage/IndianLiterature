पञ्चपादं पितरं द्वादशाकृतिं दिव आहुः परे अर्धे पुरीषिणम् |अथेमे अन्य उपरे विचक्षणं सप्तचक्रे षडर आहुर अर्पितम् ||
pañcapādam pitaraṁ dvādaśākṛtiṁ diva āhuḥ pare ardhe purīṣiṇam | atheme anya upare vicakṣaṇaṁ saptacakre ṣaḻara āhur arpitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pañcapādam: Five-footed or having five aspects.
    • pitaraṁ: Father or progenitor.
    • dvādaśākṛtiṁ: Twelve forms or manifestations.
    • diva: Heaven or celestial realm.
    • āhuḥ: They say or declare.
    • pare: Beyond or in the higher realms.
    • ardhe: In the middle or center.
    • purīṣiṇam: The one who is the essence of the city or the being.
    • atheme: In this context, it refers to 'this' or 'here.'
    • anya: Other or different.
    • upare: Above or higher.
    • vicakṣaṇaṁ: Wise or discerning.
    • saptacakre: Seven wheels or circles.
    • ṣaḻara: Six-fold or six aspects.
    • āhur: They say or declare.
    • arpitam: Offered or dedicated.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity that embodies multiple aspects and forms, suggesting a complex nature that transcends simple definitions.
    • The "five-footed" aspect may symbolize the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that constitute the universe, indicating a holistic view of existence.
    • The "twelve forms" could represent the twelve zodiac signs or the twelve months, emphasizing the cyclical nature of time and existence.
    • The reference to "heaven" suggests a connection to the divine or spiritual realms, indicating that this entity is revered in higher consciousness.
    • The "wise" and "discerning" qualities highlight the importance of wisdom in navigating life's complexities, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding.
    • The "seven wheels" may symbolize the chakras in the human body, representing the energy centers that govern physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of being "five-footed" can be seen in holistic health practices that consider physical, mental, emotional, spiritual, and social well-being.
    • The idea of "twelve forms" can be related to the twelve months of the year, reminding us of the importance of cycles in nature and our lives, such as seasons of growth and rest.
    • Wisdom and discernment are crucial in decision-making processes, whether in personal relationships, career choices, or financial investments.
    • The concept of chakras is widely embraced in yoga and meditation practices, promoting balance and harmony within oneself.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness in the universe, urging individuals to recognize the multifaceted nature of existence.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek wisdom, embrace cycles, and understand the deeper aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more balanced, harmonious, and fulfilling life, fostering a deeper connection with oneself and the universe.
