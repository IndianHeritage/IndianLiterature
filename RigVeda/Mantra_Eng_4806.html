स त्वं नश् चित्र वज्रहस्त धृष्णुया महः स्तवानो अद्रिवः |गाम अश्वं रथ्यम इन्द्र सं किरा सत्रा वाजं न जिग्युषे ||
sa tvaṁ naś citra vajrahasta dhṛṣṇuyā mahaḥ stavāno adrivaḥ | gām aśvaṁ rathyam indra saṁ kira satrā vājaṁ na jigyuṣe ||
  • sa tvaṁ naś: This phrase translates to "You are our" or "You are with us." It signifies a call for divine presence and support.
  • citra vajrahasta: "Citra" means "colorful" or "varied," and "vajrahasta" refers to "one who holds a thunderbolt." This can be interpreted as a reference to Indra, the king of gods, who wields a powerful weapon, symbolizing strength and protection.
  • dhṛṣṇuyā mahaḥ: "Dhṛṣṇuyā" means "with great strength" or "with courage," and "mahaḥ" means "great." This part emphasizes the need for strength and valor in facing challenges.
  • stavāno adrivaḥ: "Stavāno" refers to "praising" or "worshiping," and "adrivaḥ" means "the one who is beyond the mountains." This suggests that the divine is worthy of praise and is beyond the physical realm.
  • gām aśvaṁ rathyam: "Gām" means "the cow" (symbolizing wealth and nourishment), "aśvaṁ" means "the horse" (symbolizing power and speed), and "rathyam" refers to "the chariot." This indicates a request for prosperity, strength, and the means to achieve goals.
  • indra saṁ kira: "Indra" refers to the god of rain and thunderstorms, and "saṁ kira" means "to bestow." This part is a plea for Indra to grant blessings and abundance.
  • satrā vājaṁ na jigyuṣe: "Satrā" means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," "vājaṁ" means "wealth" or "resources," and "na jigyuṣe" translates to "do not let us be defeated." This emphasizes the importance of community and the need for divine support to overcome obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a powerful invocation for divine assistance, strength, and prosperity. It highlights the importance of community, courage, and the need for divine blessings in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when individuals or communities seek strength and support. It serves as a reminder to acknowledge the divine presence in our lives and to strive for collective well-being, prosperity, and resilience in the face of adversity.
