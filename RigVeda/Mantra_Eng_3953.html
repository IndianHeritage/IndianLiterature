ते नो मित्रो वरुणो आर्यमायुर इन्द्र ऋभुक्षा मरुतो जुषन्ता |नमोभिर वा ये दधते सुवृक्तिं स्तोमा रुद्राय मीळ्हुषे सजोषाः ||
te no mitro varuṇo aryamāyur indra ṛbhukṣā maruto juṣanta | namobhir vā ye dadhate suvṛktiṁ stomaṁ rudrāya mīḻhuṣe sajoṣāḥ ||
  • te no mitro varuṇo aryamāyur indra ṛbhukṣā maruto juṣanta: This line invokes several deities, highlighting their roles and attributes.
    • Mitro: Represents friendship and harmony. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of building strong relationships in personal and professional settings.
    • Varuṇa: Symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority. This can relate to the importance of ethics and justice in society.
    • Aryamā: Associated with the ancestors and the guidance they provide. This reflects the value of family heritage and wisdom passed down through generations.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power. In everyday life, this can be likened to leadership qualities that inspire and motivate others.
    • Ṛbhukṣā: Refers to the nourishment provided by the earth. This can be connected to sustainable living and the importance of caring for our environment.
    • Maruto: Represents the storm gods, symbolizing change and dynamism. This can be seen in how we adapt to life's challenges and embrace change.
  • namobhir vā ye dadhate suvṛktiṁ stomaṁ rudrāya mīḻhuṣe sajoṣāḥ: This line expresses reverence and gratitude towards those who contribute positively to the world.
    • Namobhir: A term of respect, indicating the importance of humility and acknowledgment of others' contributions.
    • Suvṛktiṁ: Refers to good deeds or virtuous actions. This emphasizes the significance of ethical behavior in our daily lives.
    • Stomaṁ: Represents praise or offerings. This can be related to the practice of gratitude in our lives, recognizing the good around us.
    • Rudrāya: Refers to Lord Rudra, associated with transformation and healing. This highlights the importance of resilience and recovery in the face of adversity.
    • Mīḻhuṣe: Suggests harmony and unity. This can be seen in community efforts where collaboration leads to greater outcomes.
    • Sajoṣāḥ: Indicates collective effort and joy. This reflects the significance of teamwork and shared success in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of various aspects of life, including friendship, ethics, leadership, and community. It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships, acting with integrity, and working together for the common good. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, fostering a sense of responsibility and collaboration that leads to a harmonious society. The invocation of deities symbolizes the aspiration for divine guidance in these endeavors, encouraging us to strive for a balanced and virtuous life.
