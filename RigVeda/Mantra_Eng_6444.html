यदिः घृतैः आहुतो वाशीं अग्निः भरत उच चाव च |असुर इव निर्णिजम् ||
yadī ghṛtebhir āhuto vāśīm agnir bharata uc cāva ca | asura iva nirṇijam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadī: if
    • ghṛtebhir: with ghee (clarified butter)
    • āhuto: invoked or offered
    • vāśīm: the one who is powerful or the lord
    • agnir: fire
    • bharata: O Bharata (a term often referring to the people or the nation)
    • uc: high or elevated
    • cāva: and also
    • ca: and
    • asura: divine or powerful beings
    • iva: like
    • nirṇijam: unshaken or steady
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about invoking the divine fire (Agni) with offerings of ghee.
    • It emphasizes the power of Agni as a deity who is both high and mighty, akin to the strength of divine beings (Asuras).
    • The phrase "if invoked with ghee" suggests that the quality of offerings matters in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, ghee is often used in fire ceremonies (Homa) to invoke blessings from deities.
    • The significance of offerings can be seen in various cultures where food or other items are offered to spirits or ancestors, reflecting respect and the desire for divine favor.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of invoking higher powers can be related to seeking guidance or strength in challenging situations, such as during personal crises or community challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of intention and quality in spiritual offerings.
    • It signifies the connection between the material (ghee) and the spiritual (Agni), illustrating how physical acts can invoke divine presence and support.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, should be performed with sincerity and respect to achieve desired outcomes.
