ऋतावानं विचेतसम् पश्यन्तो द्यां इव स्त्रिभिः | विश्वेषाम् अध्वराणां हस्कर्तारं दमे-दमे ||
ṛtāvānaṁ vicetasam paśyanto dyām iva stṛbhiḥ | viśveṣām adhvarāṇāṁ haskartāraṁ dame-dame ||
  • ṛtāvānaṁ: This term refers to the one who is established in truth or righteousness. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who uphold ethical standards and integrity in their actions, such as a judge or a community leader who makes decisions based on fairness.
  • vicetasam: This word means one who is wise or possesses intelligence. In practical terms, this can be seen in educators or mentors who guide others with their knowledge and insight, helping them navigate complex situations.
  • paśyanto: This translates to "seeing" or "perceiving." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and observation. For example, a scientist observing natural phenomena or a parent noticing changes in their child's behavior demonstrates this quality.
  • dyām: This word means "sky" or "heaven." It symbolizes the vastness of knowledge and the infinite possibilities that lie ahead. In everyday life, this can be related to aspirations and dreams that individuals strive to achieve, much like an artist envisioning their masterpiece.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as." It serves as a comparative term, linking the previous concepts to something relatable.
  • stṛbhiḥ: This term refers to "the stars" or "the celestial bodies." It signifies guidance and inspiration. In real life, this can be compared to role models or mentors who illuminate the path for others, much like stars guiding travelers at night.
  • viśveṣām: This means "of all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the collective nature of existence. In a community context, this can be seen in collaborative efforts where everyone contributes to a common goal, such as community service projects.
  • adhvarāṇāṁ: This refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals." It highlights the importance of dedication and commitment in achieving goals. For instance, athletes who train rigorously for competitions embody this spirit of sacrifice and hard work.
  • haskartāraṁ: This term means "the one who brings joy" or "the giver of happiness." In real life, this can be represented by individuals who spread positivity and uplift others, such as volunteers in charitable organizations.
  • dame-dame: This phrase can be interpreted as "in every place" or "in every moment." It emphasizes the omnipresence of these qualities in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life rooted in truth, wisdom, and awareness. It encourages individuals to strive for righteousness, observe their surroundings, and draw inspiration from the universe. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, community service, and personal development. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same, creating a harmonious and enlightened community.
