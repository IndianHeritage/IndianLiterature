स जातिभिर वृत्रहा सेद उ हव्यैर उद उष्रियā असृजद इन्द्रो अर्कैः |उरूच्य अस्मै घृतवत् भरन्ती मधु स्वाद्म दुदुहे जेन्यā गौः ||
sa jātebhir vṛtrahā sed u havyair ud usriyā asṛjad indro arkaiḥ | urūcy asmai ghṛtavad bharantī madhu svādma duduhe jenyā gauḥ ||
  • sa jātebhir vṛtrahā: This phrase can be translated as "He who is the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology that represents chaos and obstruction. In real life, this can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges that hinder progress.
  • sed u havyair ud usriyā: This translates to "with offerings and with the light of the dawn." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture, which can be related to the idea of gratitude and recognition of the divine in our daily lives. For example, starting the day with gratitude can set a positive tone for the day ahead.
  • asṛjad indro arkaiḥ: This means "Indra, with his rays, has created." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. This can be seen in real life as the creative force behind innovation and progress, where individuals harness their strengths to create something new and beneficial.
  • urūcy asmai ghṛtavad bharantī: This translates to "the cows, rich in ghee, are bringing." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. In modern terms, this can represent the abundance and resources we have in our lives that we can share with others, promoting community and support.
  • madhu svādma duduhe jenyā gauḥ: This means "the sweet milk is being drawn from the cows." Milk is a symbol of nourishment and sustenance. In real life, this can be interpreted as the benefits we derive from our hard work and the sweetness of life that comes from nurturing relationships and experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of overcoming obstacles, the importance of rituals, the power of creation, the value of abundance, and the sweetness of life. In real life, it encourages us to recognize and appreciate the resources we have, to be grateful, and to strive for progress while nurturing our relationships and communities. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the divine, urging us to live with intention and purpose.
