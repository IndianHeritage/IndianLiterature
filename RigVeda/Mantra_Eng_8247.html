मृजन्ति त्वां सम अग्रुवोऽव्ये जीराव अधि ष्वणि |रेभो यद अज्ञसे वने ||
mṛjanti tvā sam agruvo 'vye jīrāv adhi ṣvaṇi | rebho yad ajyase vane ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mṛjanti: They cleanse or purify.
    • tvā: You.
    • sam: Together or completely.
    • agruvo: The one who is foremost or the best.
    • 'vye: In the context of being invoked or called upon.
    • jīrāv: To be rejuvenated or made fresh.
    • adhi: Above or upon.
    • ṣvaṇi: In the context of sound or voice.
    • rebho: The one who is powerful or strong.
    • yad: That which.
    • ajyase: You are being invoked or called upon.
    • vane: In the forest or wilderness.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of purification and invocation of a powerful entity.
    • The phrase "mṛjanti tvā" suggests a collective effort to cleanse or purify the essence of the divine or the revered.
    • It emphasizes the importance of coming together to invoke the best qualities of a higher power.
    • The mention of "vane" indicates a connection to nature, suggesting that this purification and invocation can take place in a natural setting, such as a forest.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed in natural settings to connect with the divine. For instance, Native American ceremonies often take place in sacred spaces, emphasizing the importance of nature in spiritual practices.
    • Yoga and meditation retreats often occur in serene environments, allowing individuals to cleanse their minds and spirits, aligning with the essence of this mantra.
    • Community service or collective efforts to clean up natural spaces can be seen as a modern interpretation of "mṛjanti," where people come together to purify their surroundings, invoking a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective purification and the invocation of higher powers in natural settings.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and nature, encouraging individuals to seek rejuvenation and strength through unity and respect for the environment.
    • In real life, this can be applied through community gatherings, environmental conservation efforts, and spiritual practices that honor both the divine and the natural world.
