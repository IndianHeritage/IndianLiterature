तं उक्षमानं रजसि स्व आ दमे चन्द्रम iva सुरुचं ह्वार आ दधुः |पृश्न्याः पतरं चितयन्तं अक्षभिः पाठो न पायुं जनसी उभे अनु ||
tam ukṣamāṇaṁ rajasi sva ā dame candram iva surucaṁ hvāra ā dadhuḥ | pṛśnyāḥ pataraṁ citayantam akṣabhiḥ pātho na pāyuṁ janasī ubhe anu ||
  • tam ukṣamāṇaṁ: This phrase refers to the one who is rising or elevating. In a real-life context, this can symbolize personal growth or the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • rajasi: This word means 'in the realm of action' or 'in the material world'. It signifies the importance of engaging with the world around us and taking action to achieve our goals.
  • sva ā dame: This translates to 'in one's own abode' or 'within oneself'. It emphasizes the significance of self-awareness and understanding one's inner self as a foundation for growth.
  • candram iva: Meaning 'like the moon', this phrase suggests beauty, calmness, and illumination. The moon is often associated with tranquility and guidance, reflecting how one should navigate through life's challenges.
  • surucaṁ: This term means 'well-formed' or 'beautiful'. It can be related to the idea of cultivating beauty in our lives, whether through relationships, art, or personal development.
  • hvāra ā dadhuḥ: This means 'they have given forth' or 'they have produced'. It signifies the act of creation and contribution, highlighting the importance of sharing one's gifts and talents with the world.
  • pṛśnyāḥ: This word refers to 'the wise' or 'the knowledgeable'. It underscores the value of wisdom and learning from experiences, which can guide us in making informed decisions.
  • pataraṁ: Meaning 'the one who flies' or 'the one who transcends', this can symbolize ambition and the desire to rise above challenges, akin to how birds soar in the sky.
  • citayantam: This translates to 'the one who contemplates' or 'the thinker'. It emphasizes the importance of reflection and mindfulness in our daily lives.
  • akṣabhiḥ: This term means 'with clarity' or 'with vision'. It suggests the need for clear sight and understanding in our actions and decisions.
  • pātho na pāyuṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as 'the path is not obstructed'. It signifies the idea that with determination and clarity, one can navigate through life's obstacles.
  • janasī ubhe anu: This means 'both the people' or 'the community'. It highlights the importance of community and collective effort in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the journey of personal growth and the importance of self-awareness, wisdom, and community. It teaches us that by rising above challenges and engaging with the world, we can create beauty and contribute positively to society. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development workshops, community service initiatives, or even in artistic endeavors where collaboration and shared vision are key. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to strive for clarity, beauty, and connection in our lives.
