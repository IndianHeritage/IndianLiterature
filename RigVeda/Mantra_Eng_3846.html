सखा सख्ये अपचत तुयम् अग्निर अस्य क्रत्वा महिषा त्रि शतानि |त्री साकं इन्द्रो मनुष्यः सरांसि सुतं पिबद वृत्रहत्याय सोमम् ||
sakhā sakhye apacat tūyam agnir asya kratvā mahiṣā trī śatāni | trī sākam indro manuṣaḥ sarāṁsi sutam pibad vṛtrahatyāya somam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhā: friend or companion
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • apacat: has been prepared or made ready
    • tūyam: for you
    • agnir: fire
    • asya: of this
    • kratvā: by the power or strength
    • mahiṣā: great or mighty
    • trī: three
    • śatāni: hundreds
    • trī: three
    • sākam: together
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • manuṣaḥ: of humans
    • sarāṁsi: streams or waters
    • sutam: the drink or essence
    • pibad: drink
    • vṛtrahatyāya: for the slaying of Vritra (the dragon or demon)
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of friendship and collaboration.
    • The reference to "fire" symbolizes energy and transformation, suggesting that through collective effort, great things can be achieved.
    • The mention of "Indra" highlights the role of divine support in human endeavors, emphasizing that success often requires both human effort and divine grace.
    • The act of drinking "soma" represents the nourishment and rejuvenation that comes from shared experiences and victories.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, teamwork can be likened to the "friendship" mentioned in the mantra. When colleagues collaborate effectively, they can achieve goals that would be impossible individually.
    • In community service, individuals coming together to help those in need exemplifies the power of collective action, akin to the "three hundred" working together for a common cause.
    • In sports, a team that works in harmony, supporting each other, can overcome formidable opponents, much like Indra overcoming Vritra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, friendship, and collaboration in achieving success.
    • It teaches that through collective effort, supported by divine grace, individuals can overcome challenges and attain their goals.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community initiatives, reminding us of the strength found in togetherness.
