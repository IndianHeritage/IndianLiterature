न्य अग्निं जातवेदसं दधात देवम् ऋत्विजम् |प्र यज्ञ एत्व आनुषग अद्य देवव्यचस्तमः ||
ny a1gniṁ jātavedasaṁ dadhātā devam ṛtvijam | pra yajña etv ānuṣag adyā devavyacastamaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ny - not
    • a1gniṁ - the fire
    • jātavedasaṁ - the one who knows all births
    • dadhātā - who brings forth
    • devam - the divine
    • ṛtvijam - the priest or the one who performs rituals
    • pra - for
    • yajña - sacrifice or offering
    • etv - this
    • ānusag - following
    • adyā - today
    • devavyacastamaḥ - the divine has manifested
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is also known as Jātavedas, the one who knows all births and is a witness to all actions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices performed by priests (ṛtvijam) to connect with the divine.
    • The mantra suggests that through these rituals, one can invoke the divine presence in their lives today.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is considered sacred and is used in various rituals, such as lighting candles during religious ceremonies or bonfires during festivals.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'sacrifice' can be seen in the dedication people show towards their work, family, or community, where they put in effort for a greater good.
    • Rituals, whether in the form of meditation, prayer, or community service, can help individuals feel connected to something larger than themselves, similar to the invocation of the divine in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a purifying and transformative force in rituals.
    • It teaches us the value of intention and dedication in our actions, encouraging us to perform our duties with a sense of purpose.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied by engaging in meaningful rituals, whether personal or communal, that foster a connection to the divine and promote harmony in our lives.
