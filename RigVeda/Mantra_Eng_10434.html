आहार्षं त्वाविदं त्वा पुनर आगाह पुनर्नव |सर्वाङ्ग सर्वं ते चक्षुः सर्वम् आयुः च ते 'विदम् ||
āhārṣaṁ tvāvidaṁ tvā punar āgāḥ punarnava | sarvāṅga sarvaṁ te cakṣuḥ sarvam āyuś ca te 'vidam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āhārṣaṁ: nourishment or sustenance
    • tvā: you
    • vidaṁ: know or understand
    • tvā: you (repeated for emphasis)
    • punar: again or anew
    • āgāḥ: come or arrive
    • punarnava: renewal or rejuvenation
    • sarvāṅga: all parts or aspects
    • sarvaṁ: all or everything
    • te: your
    • cakṣuḥ: eyes or vision
    • sarvam: all or everything (repeated for emphasis)
    • āyuś: life or longevity
    • ca: and
    • te: your (repeated for emphasis)
    • 'vidam: know or understand (repeated for emphasis)
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment and renewal in life.
    • It suggests that understanding and awareness are crucial for holistic well-being.
    • The repetition of "tvā" and "sarvam" highlights the significance of the individual and their complete experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Nourishment: Just as a plant needs water, sunlight, and nutrients to grow, humans require physical, emotional, and spiritual nourishment to thrive.
    • Renewal: In nature, seasons change, and life rejuvenates. Similarly, individuals can experience personal growth and transformation through learning and new experiences.
    • Awareness: Mindfulness practices, such as meditation, help individuals become more aware of their thoughts and feelings, leading to better decision-making and overall health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nourishment, awareness, and renewal in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek holistic well-being by nurturing all aspects of themselves—physical, mental, and spiritual.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied through healthy eating, continuous learning, and self-reflection, leading to a more fulfilling and balanced life.
