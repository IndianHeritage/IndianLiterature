तं-तम इद् राधसे मह इन्द्रं चोदामि पीतये |यः पूर्व्यां अनुṣṭुतिम ईशे कृष्णीनां नृतुः ||
taṁ-tam id rādhase maha indraṁ codāmi pītaye | yaḥ pūrvyām anuṣṭutim īśe kṛṣṭīnāṁ nṛtuḥ ||
  • taṁ-tam: This phrase can be interpreted as "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • id: This word means "here" or "now," indicating the present moment or the immediate context.
  • rādhase: This term is associated with "wealth" or "prosperity," often linked to the blessings of the divine.
  • maha: Meaning "great" or "mighty," it emphasizes the power and significance of the entity being addressed.
  • indraṁ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, rain, and thunder. He represents the force of nature and the protector of humanity.
  • codāmi: This translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon," indicating a plea or request for divine intervention.
  • pītaye: This word means "for the drink" or "for nourishment," suggesting a desire for sustenance or spiritual fulfillment.
  • yaḥ: Meaning "who," it introduces a relative clause that describes the qualities of the entity being invoked.
  • pūrvyām: This term means "in the past" or "ancient," connecting the present invocation to historical or traditional practices.
  • anuṣṭutim: This refers to "praise" or "worship," indicating the act of honoring the divine through words and actions.
  • īśe: Meaning "to the lord" or "to the master," it signifies reverence towards a higher power.
  • kṛṣṭīnāṁ: This word relates to "the cultivated" or "the plowed," symbolizing the efforts of humanity in agriculture and sustenance.
  • nṛtuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "of the people" or "of humanity," emphasizing the collective aspect of the invocation.

In summary, this mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, the great deity associated with prosperity and nourishment. It calls upon him to bless the present moment with abundance and sustenance, linking the past praises and efforts of humanity in agriculture to the divine. The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the interconnectedness between divine forces and human endeavors.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • Agricultural Practices: Farmers may recite this mantra during planting or harvesting seasons, seeking blessings for a bountiful crop.
  • Community Gatherings: In festivals or communal events, this invocation can be used to foster unity and collective prosperity.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals may use this mantra for meditation, focusing on gratitude for the sustenance they receive and the efforts of those before them.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of acknowledging divine support in our daily lives and the significance of community and tradition in achieving prosperity.
