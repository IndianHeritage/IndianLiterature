इन्द्रस्य हार्दि सोमधानम आ विश समुद्रम इव सिन्धवः |जुष्टो मित्राय वरुणाय वायवे दिवो विष्टम्भ उत्तमः ||
indrasya hārdi somadhānam ā viśa samudram iva sindhavaḥ | juṣṭo mitrāya varuṇāya vāyave divo viṣṭambha uttamaḥ ||
  • Indrasya: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Hārdi: Means 'in the heart' or 'within the essence', indicating a deep connection or core aspect.
  • Somadhānam: Refers to the 'soma', a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight, suggesting nourishment for the spirit.
  • Ā viśa: Translates to 'may enter' or 'may pervade', indicating a desire for divine presence or influence.
  • Samudram iva sindhavaḥ: Means 'like the ocean and rivers', symbolizing vastness and abundance, suggesting a connection to nature and the universe.
  • Juṣṭo: Means 'enjoyed' or 'pleasing', indicating satisfaction or fulfillment.
  • Mitrāya: Refers to 'Mitra', the god of friendship and harmony, emphasizing the importance of relationships.
  • Varuṇāya: Refers to 'Varuna', the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing balance and the natural laws of the universe.
  • Vāyave: Refers to 'Vayu', the god of wind, representing movement and the breath of life.
  • Divo: Means 'of the sky' or 'heavenly', indicating a connection to the divine or higher realms.
  • Viṣṭambha: Means 'support' or 'foundation', suggesting stability and strength.
  • Uttamaḥ: Means 'the best' or 'supreme', indicating the highest quality or excellence.

This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking for the essence of soma to enter the heart, much like the vastness of the ocean and rivers. It emphasizes the importance of harmony, friendship, and balance in life, calling upon the gods Mitra, Varuna, and Vayu for support and strength.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as soma nourishes the spirit, individuals can seek experiences that enrich their inner selves, such as meditation, learning, and self-reflection.
  • Relationships: The invocation of Mitra highlights the importance of nurturing friendships and building harmonious relationships, which can lead to a supportive community.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to nature encourages individuals to respect and protect the environment, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.
  • Spiritual Connection: Seeking divine guidance and support can help individuals navigate challenges and find purpose in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of inner nourishment, harmonious relationships, and a deep connection to the natural and divine world. It encourages individuals to seek strength and support from the universe while fostering a sense of community and balance in their lives.
