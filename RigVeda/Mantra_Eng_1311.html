युवं तुग्राय पूर्व्येभिर एविः पुनर्मन्याव अभवतं युवाना |युवं भुज्युम अर्णसो निः समुद्राद विभिर ऊहथुर ऋज्रेभिर अश्वैः ||
yuvaṁ tugrāya pūrvyebhir evaiḥ punarmanyāv abhavataṁ yuvānā | yuvam bhujyum arṇaso niḥ samudrād vibhir ūhathur ṛjrebhir aśvaiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ tugrāya pūrvyebhir evaiḥ punarmanyāv abhavataṁ yuvānā | yuvam bhujyum arṇaso niḥ samudrād vibhir ūhathur ṛjrebhir aśvaiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • tugrāya: to the strong or powerful
    • pūrvyebhir: by the previous ones, ancestors
    • evaiḥ: indeed, thus
    • punarmanyāv: again, in a new way
    • abhavataṁ: became, came into being
    • yuvānā: of the young
    • bhujyum: strength, power
    • arṇaso: from the ocean
    • niḥ: from, out of
    • samudrād: from the sea
    • vibhir: by the various
    • ūhathur: they have drawn, they have taken
    • ṛjrebhir: by the righteous, by the just
    • aśvaiḥ: by the horses, symbolizing strength and speed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the youth and their potential, emphasizing the strength and power that comes from the past generations.
    • The phrase "yuvaṁ tugrāya" highlights the vigor and energy of youth, suggesting that they are destined to achieve great things.
    • It acknowledges the contributions of ancestors ("pūrvyebhir evaiḥ") and how their legacies empower the current generation.
    • The mention of "bhujyum arṇaso" signifies that the strength of the youth is akin to drawing power from the vast ocean, symbolizing limitless potential.
    • The reference to "aśvaiḥ" connects the youth's strength to the qualities of horses, which are often associated with speed, agility, and power in various cultures.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, young entrepreneurs often draw inspiration from the achievements of previous generations, using their legacies to fuel innovation and creativity.
    • Sports teams often rely on the energy and strength of young athletes, who bring fresh perspectives and vigor to the game, much like the "yuvānā" in the mantra.
    • In education, mentors and teachers guide the youth, helping them harness their potential and encouraging them to build upon the knowledge of their predecessors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of youth and their role in shaping the future, while also honoring the wisdom and strength inherited from past generations.
    • It encourages young individuals to recognize their potential and the resources available to them, urging them to strive for greatness.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire youth to take initiative, embrace their strengths, and contribute positively to society, ensuring a legacy for future generations.
