द्वितā व्यूर्ण्वन्न अमृतस्य धाम स्वरविदे भुवनानि प्रथन्त || धियः पिन्वानाः स्वसरे न गावा ऋतायन्तीर अभि वावश्र इन्दुम ||
dvitā vyūrṇvann amṛtasya dhāma svarvide bhuvanāni prathanta | dhiyaḥ pinvānāḥ svasare na gāva ṛtāyantīr abhi vāvaśra indum ||
  • Mantra: "dvitā vyūrṇvann amṛtasya dhāma svarvide bhuvanāni prathanta | dhiyaḥ pinvānāḥ svasare na gāva ṛtāyantīr abhi vāvaśra indum ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvitā: twofold or dual
    • vyūrṇvann: expanding or spreading
    • amṛtasya: of immortality or eternal
    • dhāma: abode or realm
    • svarvide: pertaining to the divine or celestial
    • bhuvanāni: worlds or realms
    • prathanta: manifesting or revealing
    • dhiyaḥ: thoughts or intellects
    • pinvānāḥ: nourishing or enriching
    • svasare: in the self or within
    • na: not
    • gāva: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • ṛtāyantīr: those who uphold truth or righteousness
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • vāvaśra: may we attain or achieve
    • indum: the moon or the essence of nourishment
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dual nature of existence and the eternal realm of immortality.
    • The phrase "dvitā vyūrṇvann" suggests the idea of duality in life, such as the balance between material and spiritual pursuits.
    • For example, in real life, individuals often find themselves balancing their professional responsibilities with personal growth and spiritual practices.
    • The term "amṛtasya dhāma" refers to the abode of immortality, which can be interpreted as the ultimate goal of human existence—achieving a state of eternal bliss or enlightenment.
    • "Dhiyaḥ pinvānāḥ" emphasizes the importance of nourishing our intellect and thoughts, akin to how we nourish our bodies with food.
    • In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge through education, meditation, and self-reflection.
    • The mention of "gāva ṛtāyantīr" symbolizes the importance of truth and righteousness, akin to how ethical practices in business lead to sustainable success.
    • Finally, "abhi vāvaśra indum" expresses a desire to attain the essence of nourishment, which can be seen as a quest for fulfillment and happiness in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balancing dualities in life, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of truth and righteousness.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a holistic approach to life, where both material and spiritual aspects are nurtured.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek a balanced lifestyle, engage in ethical practices, and continuously strive for personal and spiritual growth.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true fulfillment comes from nourishing both the mind and the spirit while living in harmony with the world around us.
