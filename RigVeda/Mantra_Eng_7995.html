स पवस्व विचर्षण आ मही रोधसी पृण |उषः सूर्यो न रश्मिभिः ||
sa pavasva vicarṣaṇa ā mahī rodasī pṛṇa | uṣāḥ sūryo na raśmibhiḥ ||
  • sa pavasva: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you (the wind) blow." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the importance of movement and change in our lives. Just as the wind brings freshness and new beginnings, we too must embrace change to grow.
  • vicarṣaṇa: This term refers to "the one who moves or travels." It signifies the journey of life, where each individual is on a path of discovery. For example, consider a student who travels to a new city for education; this journey is not just physical but also a journey of personal growth and learning.
  • ā mahī rodasī: This translates to "the great earth and the sky." It emphasizes the connection between the earth and the cosmos. In our daily lives, this can be seen in our relationship with nature. For instance, when we engage in environmental conservation, we acknowledge our responsibility towards the earth and the universe.
  • pṛṇa: This word means "to nourish" or "to provide." It highlights the importance of sustenance in our lives. In a practical sense, this can relate to how we nourish our bodies with food, our minds with knowledge, and our spirits with love and compassion.
  • uṣāḥ: This term means "dawn" or "the beginning of a new day." It symbolizes hope and new opportunities. For example, every morning offers a chance to start anew, whether it’s in our personal goals or professional endeavors.
  • sūryo: This refers to "the sun." The sun is a source of energy and life, representing vitality and strength. In real life, we can draw parallels to how sunlight is essential for plants to grow, just as positivity and encouragement are vital for human development.
  • na raśmibhiḥ: This phrase translates to "without rays." It can signify the absence of light or guidance. In our lives, this can relate to times when we feel lost or uncertain. It reminds us of the importance of seeking guidance and clarity in challenging situations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life’s journey, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of nourishment, and the hope that each new day brings. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to embrace change, seek knowledge, and maintain a harmonious relationship with the environment. By understanding and applying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives.
