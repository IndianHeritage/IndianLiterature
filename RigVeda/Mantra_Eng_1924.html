अतारीष्म तमसस् पारम् अस्य प्रति वां स्तोमो अश्विनाव अधायि |एह यातम् पथिभिर देवयानैर विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
atāriṣma tamasas pāram asya prati vāṁ stomo aśvināv adhāyi | eha yātam pathibhir devayānair vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • atāriṣma: This word can be interpreted as "to transcend" or "to overcome." It signifies the act of rising above challenges or obstacles.
  • tamasas: This translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the state of being lost or unaware, often associated with confusion or lack of knowledge.
  • pāram: Meaning "beyond" or "across," it indicates a journey or transition from one state to another, often implying enlightenment or clarity.
  • asya: This word means "of this" or "of this world," grounding the mantra in the present reality.
  • prati: Translated as "towards" or "in the direction of," it suggests movement or intention towards a goal.
  • vāṁ: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or address to the listener or seeker.
  • stomo: This can be understood as "praise" or "song," referring to the act of expressing reverence or gratitude.
  • aśvināv: Referring to the twin deities Ashvins, who symbolize health, vitality, and the dawn. They represent the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • adhāyi: This means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating a divine intervention or blessing.
  • eha: Translated as "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the immediacy of the experience or the presence of the divine in the current moment.
  • yātam: This means "to go" or "to move," suggesting action or progress towards a goal.
  • pathibhir: Meaning "by the paths," it refers to the various ways or methods one can take in life.
  • devayānair: This translates to "by the divine beings," indicating the influence or guidance of higher powers.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: Meaning "this knowledge," it emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in one's journey.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the group" or "the community," highlighting the significance of collective effort and support.
  • jīradānum: This translates to "the essence of life," suggesting the vital force or energy that sustains existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a journey from ignorance to enlightenment, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance and collective effort. It encourages individuals to transcend their limitations and seek knowledge, supported by the blessings of higher powers. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, community development, and spiritual practices. By acknowledging the darkness of ignorance and striving towards the light of knowledge, individuals can foster a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
