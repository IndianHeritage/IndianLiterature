सजोषा इन्द्र सगणो मरुद्भिः सोमं पिब वृत्रहा शूर विद्वान् |जहि शत्रूम् अप मृधो नुदस्वाथाभयं कृणुहि विश्वतो नः ||
sajoṣā indra sagaṇo marudbhiḥ somam piba vṛtrahā śūra vidvān | jahi śatrūm̐r apa mṛdho nudasvāthābhayaṁ kṛṇuhi viśvato naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sajoṣā: With joy or happiness
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • sagaṇo: Together with his companions
    • marudbhiḥ: With the Maruts, the storm deities
    • somam: The soma, a sacred drink that grants immortality
    • piba: Drink
    • vṛtrahā: Slayer of Vritra, a dragon or serpent representing chaos
    • śūra: Brave or valiant
    • vidvān: Wise or knowledgeable
    • jahi: Conquer or defeat
    • śatrūm̐r: Enemies
    • apa: Away or remove
    • mṛdho: From the earth or from the ground
    • nudasvāthābhayaṁ: Grant us fearlessness and safety
    • kṛṇuhi: Make or create
    • viśvato: From all sides
    • naḥ: Our
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, invoking him to drink the soma with his companions, the Maruts, and to bestow bravery and wisdom upon us. It calls for the removal of enemies and the creation of safety and fearlessness from all directions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing challenges at work or in relationships, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and wisdom to overcome obstacles.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, invoking Indra can symbolize unity and collective strength, encouraging participants to work together harmoniously.
    • In moments of fear or anxiety, such as public speaking or facing a difficult situation, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek courage and support from the divine.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and fears. It emphasizes the importance of community, bravery, and wisdom in facing adversities. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied as a source of inspiration and strength, reminding individuals to seek support from higher powers and to cultivate courage in their daily lives. By invoking the blessings of Indra and the Maruts, one can foster a sense of safety and resilience, essential for navigating the complexities of life.
