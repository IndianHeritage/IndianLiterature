अधā मातुर उषसः सप्त विप्रā जायतामहि प्रथमā वेधसो नृण् |दिवस पुत्रā अङ्गिरसो भवेमाद्रिं रुजेम धनिनं शुचन्तः ||
adhā mātur uṣasaḥ sapta viprā jāyemahi prathamā vedhaso nṝn | divas putrā aṅgiraso bhavemādriṁ rujema dhaninaṁ śucantaḥ ||
  • adhā mātur uṣasaḥ: This phrase translates to "from the mother dawn." In this context, "mother" symbolizes the nurturing aspect of nature, while "dawn" represents new beginnings and enlightenment. Just as dawn brings light and clarity, we seek knowledge and wisdom from the universe.
  • sapta viprā jāyemahi: This means "may we be born as the seven wise ones." The number seven often symbolizes completeness and perfection in various cultures. Here, it refers to the aspiration to embody wisdom and knowledge, akin to the seven sages or rishis in Vedic tradition who are revered for their profound understanding of the cosmos.
  • prathamā vedhaso nṝn: This translates to "the first among the wise men." It emphasizes the importance of being a leader in wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who take initiative in learning and sharing knowledge, such as educators or thought leaders who inspire others.
  • divas putrā aṅgiraso: This means "sons of the sky, of the Angiras." The Angiras are a group of sages known for their spiritual insight. This phrase signifies a connection to the divine and the pursuit of higher knowledge. In modern terms, it can relate to those who seek to understand the universe through science and spirituality.
  • bhavemādriṁ rujema dhaninaṁ: This translates to "may we overcome obstacles and attain wealth." Here, "wealth" is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and intellectual richness. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal and professional goals, where overcoming challenges leads to success.
  • śucantaḥ: This word means "pure" or "clean." It signifies the importance of purity in thought, action, and intention. In real life, this can be reflected in ethical living, where individuals strive to maintain integrity and clarity in their actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for wisdom, purity, and overcoming challenges. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment while maintaining ethical integrity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, personal development, and leadership. By embodying the qualities of the wise and striving for purity in our actions, we can contribute positively to society and foster a culture of learning and growth.
