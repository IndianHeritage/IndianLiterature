आ वात वाहि भेषजं विबात वाहि यद् रपः |त्वं हि विश्वभेषजो देवानां दूत ईयसे ||
ā vāta vāhi bheṣajaṁ vi vāta vāhi yad rapaḥ | tvaṁ hi viśvabheṣajo devānāṁ dūta īyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāta: O Wind
    • vāhi: Carrying
    • bheṣajaṁ: Healing or medicine
    • vi vāta: O Wind, indeed
    • vāhi: Carrying
    • yad: Whatever
    • rapaḥ: Is beneficial or good
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hi: Indeed
    • viśvabheṣajo: Universal healer
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods
    • dūta: Messenger
    • īyase: You move or travel
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses the Wind as a carrier of healing and beneficial qualities.
    • It acknowledges the Wind as a universal healer and a messenger of the gods.
    • The invocation suggests a request for the Wind to carry healing energies and beneficial elements to the seeker.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In traditional medicine, the concept of "prana" or life force is often associated with the Wind. Practices like yoga and Ayurveda emphasize the importance of breath and air in maintaining health.
    • In nature, the Wind plays a crucial role in dispersing seeds and pollen, which is essential for the growth of plants and the overall health of ecosystems.
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor the Wind, seeking its blessings for good health and prosperity, reflecting the belief in its healing powers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the Wind as a vital force in both physical and spiritual healing.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and health, encouraging individuals to seek harmony with the natural elements.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in practices such as meditation, healing rituals, and holistic health approaches, where the focus is on harnessing the healing powers of nature.
