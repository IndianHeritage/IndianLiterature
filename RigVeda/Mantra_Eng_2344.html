उक्षन्ते अश्वां अत्यां इवाजीषु नदस्य कर्णैस् तुरयन्त आशीभिः |हिरण्यशिप्रा मरुतो दविध्वतः पृक्षं याथ पृषतीभिः समन्यवः ||
ukṣante aśvām̐ atyām̐ ivājiṣu nadasya karṇais turayanta āśubhiḥ | hiraṇyaśiprā maruto davidhvataḥ pṛkṣaṁ yātha pṛṣatībhiḥ samanyavaḥ ||
  • ukṣante: This word can be translated as "they awaken" or "they rouse." It signifies the act of bringing forth energy or life.
  • aśvām̐: Refers to "horses," which in Vedic literature often symbolize strength, speed, and vitality.
  • atyām̐: This term means "beyond" or "transcending," indicating a state that surpasses ordinary limits.
  • ivājiṣu: Translates to "in the swift ones," suggesting a connection to those who are quick or agile, possibly referring to the forces of nature or divine beings.
  • nadasya: Means "of the sound" or "of the flow," which can be interpreted as the rhythm of life or the cosmic sound.
  • karṇais: This word means "with ears," indicating the act of listening or being receptive to the sounds of the universe.
  • turayanta: Translates to "they drive away" or "they remove," suggesting a protective or purifying action.
  • āśubhiḥ: Refers to "inauspicious things" or "obstacles," indicating the removal of negativity or hindrances.
  • hiraṇyaśiprā: This term means "golden" or "radiant," often associated with prosperity and auspiciousness.
  • maruto: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and dynamic energy.
  • davidhvataḥ: This means "they who are swift" or "those who move quickly," emphasizing the active nature of the Maruts.
  • pṛkṣaṁ: Translates to "the path" or "the way," indicating a journey or direction in life.
  • yātha: Means "as" or "like," suggesting a comparison or analogy.
  • pṛṣatībhiḥ: Refers to "with the strong ones," indicating a connection to powerful forces or beings.
  • samanyavaḥ: This term means "those who are united" or "together," emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the awakening of strength and vitality, transcending ordinary limits, and the removal of obstacles through the dynamic forces of nature, represented by the Maruts. It highlights the importance of being receptive to the sounds and rhythms of the universe while emphasizing unity and collaboration among powerful forces.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of awakening and transcending limits, individuals can strive for personal development by overcoming challenges and embracing their inner strength.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on unity suggests that working together with others can lead to greater achievements, whether in a workplace, community project, or family setting.
  • Spiritual Practice: The mantra encourages individuals to listen to the deeper rhythms of life, which can be practiced through meditation, mindfulness, or connecting with nature.
  • Overcoming Obstacles: The idea of driving away inauspicious things can inspire individuals to actively remove negativity from their lives, whether through positive thinking, seeking support, or making lifestyle changes.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of awakening one's potential, the importance of community, and the ability to overcome challenges through unity and strength.
