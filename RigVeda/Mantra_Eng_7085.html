ज्योतिष्मन्तं केतुमन्तं त्रिचक्रं सुखं रथं सुषदं भूर्वारम् |चित्रामघा यस्य योगे 'धिजज्ञे तं वां हुवे अति रिक्तं पिबध्यै ||
jyotiṣmantaṁ ketumantaṁ tricakraṁ sukhaṁ rathaṁ suṣadam bhūrivāram | citrāmaghā yasya yoge 'dhijajñe taṁ vāṁ huve ati riktam pibadhyai ||
  • jyotiṣmantaṁ: This word refers to the one who is full of light or radiance. In a real-life context, this can symbolize individuals who bring positivity and enlightenment to others, such as teachers or mentors.
  • ketumantaṁ: This term signifies the one who has a banner or a flag. It can represent leadership and guidance, akin to a leader who inspires and directs a group towards a common goal.
  • tricakraṁ: This means 'three wheels' or 'three circles'. In practical terms, it can be related to the balance of mind, body, and spirit, which is essential for holistic well-being.
  • sukhaṁ: This translates to 'happiness' or 'joy'. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of contentment, such as spending time with loved ones or achieving personal goals.
  • rathaṁ: This word means 'chariot'. It symbolizes the journey of life, where one navigates through various experiences and challenges, much like a charioteer guiding their chariot through different terrains.
  • suṣadam: This means 'beneficial' or 'auspicious'. In real life, this can refer to actions or decisions that lead to positive outcomes, such as making healthy lifestyle choices.
  • bhūrivāram: This term signifies 'abundant wealth' or 'great resources'. It can be related to the importance of sharing resources and wealth within a community to foster growth and support.
  • citrāmaghā: This refers to 'the one who is colorful and diverse'. In a real-life context, it can symbolize the richness of cultural diversity and the beauty of different perspectives in society.
  • yoge 'dhijajñe: This phrase means 'in the union of knowledge and wisdom'. It emphasizes the importance of combining theoretical knowledge with practical wisdom in decision-making.
  • taṁ vāṁ huve: This translates to 'I invoke you'. It signifies a call for guidance or support, which can be seen in various rituals or personal prayers for assistance in times of need.
  • ati riktam: This means 'extremely full' or 'overflowing'. In real life, this can represent a state of abundance, whether it be in love, knowledge, or resources.
  • pibadhyai: This translates to 'may you drink' or 'may you absorb'. It signifies the act of taking in knowledge or experiences, akin to learning from life’s lessons.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of enlightenment, leadership, balance, joy, and abundance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of guiding oneself and others through the journey of life while embracing diversity and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, community leadership, and fostering harmonious relationships. By embodying the qualities described in the mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings and lead fulfilling lives.
