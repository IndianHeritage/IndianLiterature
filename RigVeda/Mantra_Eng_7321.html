आगन्म वृत्रहन्तमं ज्येष्ठम अग्निम आनवम् |यस्य श्रुतर्वा बृहन्न अर्क्षो अनीक एधते ||
āganma vṛtrahantamaṁ jyeṣṭham agnim ānavam | yasya śrutarvā bṛhann ārkṣo anīka edhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āganma: We have come.
    • vṛtrahantamaṁ: The slayer of Vritra (the demon of drought).
    • jyeṣṭham: The eldest or the most powerful.
    • agnim: To the fire (Agni).
    • ānavam: The one who is to be invoked or called upon.
    • yasya: Whose.
    • śrutarvā: The one who is heard or listened to.
    • bṛhann: The great or vast.
    • ārkṣo: The one who is like the sun or radiant.
    • anīka: The host or multitude.
    • edhate: They gather or assemble.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a powerful force in Vedic literature.
    • Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing the transformative power of fire.
    • The reference to "vṛtrahantamaṁ" highlights Agni's role in overcoming obstacles and challenges, akin to how fire can clear a path through dense underbrush.
    • The phrase "yasya śrutarvā" suggests that Agni is attentive and responsive to those who call upon him, much like how a good listener pays attention to the needs of others.
    • The imagery of "bṛhann ārkṣo" evokes the vastness and brilliance of the sun, symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
    • The term "anīka" indicates a gathering or community, suggesting that the invocation of Agni brings people together, much like a campfire that draws individuals for warmth and connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes purification and the presence of the divine, similar to how Agni is invoked in the mantra.
    • In personal challenges, individuals often seek strength and clarity, akin to invoking Agni to overcome their "Vritra" or obstacles in life.
    • Community gatherings around a fire can foster unity and communication, reflecting the idea of "anīka" where people come together for a common purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a powerful force for transformation, unity, and overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking higher powers in times of need and the role of community in personal and collective growth.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in seeking strength, fostering connections, and navigating obstacles with resilience.
