मन्त्र: अम्बितमे नदीतमे देवितमे सरस्वती | अप्रशस्ताऽ इव स्मसि प्रशस्तिम अम्ब नस् कृधि ||
ambitame nadītame devitame sarasvati | apraśastā iva smasi praśastim amba nas kṛdhi ||
  • Mantra: "ambitame nadītame devitame sarasvati | apraśastā iva smasi praśastim amba nas kṛdhi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ambitame: O Mother, the one who encompasses all.
    • nadītame: O River, the one who flows abundantly.
    • devitame: O Goddess, the divine feminine energy.
    • sarasvati: Referring to Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • apraśastā: Those who are not praised or recognized.
    • iva: Like or as if.
    • smasi: You are (in the present tense).
    • praśastim: Praise or recognition.
    • amba: Mother.
    • nas: Us.
    • kṛdhi: Do or create.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine feminine energy represented by Sarasvati, emphasizing her role as a nurturing and encompassing force.
    • It acknowledges the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our lives, suggesting that these qualities are essential for growth and development.
    • The phrase "apraśastā iva smasi" highlights the idea that even those who are not recognized or praised have value and should be acknowledged.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, this mantra can inspire students and teachers to value knowledge and creativity, encouraging a culture of learning.
    • In community service, it reminds us to recognize and uplift those who may not receive acknowledgment for their contributions, such as volunteers or unsung heroes.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages us to appreciate the nurturing qualities of those around us, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of knowledge, recognition, and the nurturing aspects of the divine feminine.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from education to community service, emphasizing the importance of valuing every individual's contribution.
    • By invoking this mantra, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for wisdom and the interconnectedness of all beings, fostering a more harmonious society.
