एवां अग्निम अजुर्मुर गीरभिर यज्ञेभिर अनुषक | दधद अस्मे सुवीर्यम उता त्यद आश्वश्व्यम इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
evām̐ agnim ajuryamur gīrbhir yajñebhir ānuṣak | dadhad asme suvīryam uta tyad āśvaśvyam iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • evām̐ agnim: This phrase refers to the invocation of Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals. Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • ajuryamur: This term can be interpreted as "unfailing" or "eternal." It emphasizes the reliability and constancy of Agni in rituals and life.
  • gīrbhir yajñebhir ānuṣak: This part highlights the importance of hymns (gīrbhir) and sacrifices (yajñebhir) in invoking Agni. It suggests that through proper rituals and chants, one can connect with the divine.
  • dadhad asme suvīryam: This phrase means "may he bestow upon us strength." It reflects the desire for vitality and courage in life, which can be achieved through the blessings of Agni.
  • uta tyad āśvaśvyam: This can be interpreted as "and may he grant us the swiftness of horses." It symbolizes the need for speed, agility, and progress in our endeavors.
  • iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara: This final part translates to "may he bring forth the power to those who praise him." It emphasizes the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine, where devotion leads to divine support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking Agni for strength, speed, and divine support through rituals and hymns. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking motivation in challenging times, striving for excellence in personal and professional endeavors, and fostering a sense of community through shared rituals and celebrations. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness between human efforts and divine blessings, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that uplift and empower them.
