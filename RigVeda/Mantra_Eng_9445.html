विष्वा हि वो नमस्यानि वन्द्या नामानि देवाऽउत यज्ञियानि वः |ये स्थ जाताऽअदितेर अद्भ्यस परी ये पृथिव्याः ते म इह श्रुताऽहवम् ||
viśvā hi vo namasyāni vandyā nāmāni devā uta yajñiyāni vaḥ | ye stha jātā aditer adbhyas pari ye pṛthivyās te ma iha śrutā havam ||
  • Translation: "All of you, the divine beings, are worthy of our salutations and respect. Your names are to be honored, and you are the ones who are offered our sacrifices. Those who are born from Aditi, and those who dwell in the earth, we invoke you here with our offerings."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvā: all, every
    • hi: indeed, certainly
    • vo: your
    • namasyāni: salutations, respects
    • vandyā: worthy of worship
    • nāmāni: names
    • devā: divine beings, gods
    • uta: and
    • yajñiyāni: those who are to be offered sacrifices
    • vaḥ: to you
    • ye: those who
    • stha: dwell, reside
    • jātā: born
    • aditer: from Aditi (the infinite, mother of gods)
    • adbhyas: from the waters
    • pari: around, about
    • ye: those who
    • pṛthivyās: in the earth
    • te: you
    • ma: us
    • iha: here
    • śrutā: heard, invoked
    • havam: our offerings
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and respecting the divine forces in our lives.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the reverence we should have for the natural world and its elements.
    • In real life, this can be seen in practices such as environmental conservation, where we honor the earth and its resources.
    • It also reflects the idea of community and collective worship, reminding us that we are part of a larger whole.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to practice gratitude and respect towards nature, promoting sustainable living.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, it serves as a reminder to honor the contributions of all members, fostering unity and cooperation.
    • In personal reflection, it encourages individuals to recognize the divine in themselves and others, leading to a more compassionate and respectful society.
