अहम् अटकं कवये शिष्णथं हठैरहम् कुत्सम् आवम् आबिर ऊतिभिः |अहम् शुष्णस्य श्नथिता वधर यमं न यो ररा आर्यं नाम दस्यवे ||
aham atkaṁ kavaye śiśnathaṁ hathair ahaṁ kutsam āvam ābhir ūtibhiḥ | ahaṁ śuṣṇasya śnathitā vadhar yamaṁ na yo rara āryaṁ nāma dasyave ||
  • Aham atkaṁ kavaye: The phrase translates to "I am the one who knows the truth." Here, "aham" means "I," "atkaṁ" refers to "truth" or "knowledge," and "kavaye" means "the knower" or "the wise one." This emphasizes the importance of self-awareness and understanding one's own truth.
  • Śiśnathaṁ hathair: This can be interpreted as "with hands that are strong." "Śiśnathaṁ" refers to strength or power, while "hathair" means "with hands." This suggests that knowledge and truth must be pursued with strength and determination.
  • Ahaṁ kutsam āvam ābhir ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "I am the one who is protected by the forces of nature." "Kutsam" refers to protection, "āvam" means "we," and "ūtibhiḥ" refers to the energies or forces. This indicates that when one seeks truth, they are supported by the natural forces around them.
  • Ahaṁ śuṣṇasya śnathitā: This means "I am the one who is aware of the hidden." "Śuṣṇasya" refers to the hidden or the unseen, while "śnathitā" means "awareness." This highlights the importance of being aware of the deeper truths that may not be immediately visible.
  • Vadhar yamaṁ na yo rara: This can be interpreted as "I do not fear the challenges." "Vadhar" means "to face," "yamaṁ" refers to "challenges" or "obstacles," and "na yo rara" means "I do not fear." This encourages individuals to confront their fears and challenges head-on.
  • Āryaṁ nāma dasyave: This translates to "the noble one is not to be defeated." "Āryaṁ" refers to the noble or the righteous, "nāma" means "name," and "dasyave" refers to "defeat." This emphasizes the idea that those who seek truth and act nobly will ultimately prevail.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of self-discovery and the pursuit of truth. It emphasizes the importance of self-awareness, strength, and the support of natural forces in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, professional challenges, and moral dilemmas. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with courage and integrity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence. The significance lies in the reminder that truth and nobility will always triumph over adversity.
