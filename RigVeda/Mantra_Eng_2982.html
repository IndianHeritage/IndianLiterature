आ धेनवो धुनयन्ताम् अषिष्वीः सबरदुग्धाः शशयाऽप्रदुग्धाः |नव्याऽनव्याऽयुवतयो भवन्तीर महद् देवानाम् असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
ā dhenavo dhunayantām aśiśvīḥ sabardughāḥ śaśayā apradugdhāḥ | navyā-navyā yuvatayo bhavantīr mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā dhenavo: "from the cows" - symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • dhunayantām: "who are being milked" - representing the act of extracting value or sustenance.
    • aśiśvīḥ: "the strong" - indicating power and vitality.
    • sabardughāḥ: "with good qualities" - referring to those who possess virtues.
    • śaśayā: "with a good yield" - suggesting prosperity and fruitful outcomes.
    • apradugdhāḥ: "not disturbed" - indicating stability and peace.
    • navyā-navyā: "new and fresh" - symbolizing renewal and innovation.
    • yuvatayo: "of the young women" - representing youth, beauty, and potential.
    • bhavantīr: "who become" - indicating transformation and growth.
    • mahad: "great" - referring to something of significant importance.
    • devānām: "of the gods" - symbolizing divine qualities and blessings.
    • asuratvam: "the essence of the non-divine" - indicating the balance between divine and earthly aspects.
    • ekam: "one" - suggesting unity and oneness.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and extracting value from our surroundings.
    • The imagery of cows being milked can be seen as a metaphor for utilizing resources wisely, whether they be natural, emotional, or intellectual.
    • The qualities of strength, goodness, and stability highlight the virtues that are essential for a prosperous life.
    • The mention of youth and freshness suggests the importance of innovation and renewal in our endeavors.
    • Ultimately, the mantra conveys a message of unity, where divine and earthly elements coexist harmoniously.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers extract value from their crops (like milking cows) while ensuring sustainable practices that promote the health of the land.
    • In personal development, individuals can draw strength from their experiences and virtues to navigate challenges, much like the strong qualities mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community building, fostering a sense of unity and cooperation among diverse groups can lead to a harmonious and prosperous society.
    • In innovation, businesses that embrace new ideas and adapt to changing circumstances can thrive, reflecting the freshness and renewal emphasized in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our resources, embracing virtues, and fostering unity in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to community development and sustainable practices.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals and societies can achieve prosperity and harmony, reflecting the divine qualities that connect us all.
