प्र सु श्रुतं सुराधसम आर्चा शक्रम अभिष्टये |यः सुन्वते स्तुवते काम्यं वसु सहस्रेणेव मंहते ||
pra su śrutaṁ surādhasam arcā śakram abhiṣṭaye | yaḥ sunvate stuvate kāmyaṁ vasu sahasreṇeva maṁhate ||
  • pra su śrutaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "well heard" or "well understood." It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding knowledge or wisdom that is beneficial.
  • surādhasam: This term refers to "the one who is excellent among the gods" or "the best of the divine." It signifies the supreme power or deity that is being invoked.
  • arcā: This means "worship" or "adoration." It highlights the act of reverence and devotion towards the divine.
  • śakram: This word refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty." It denotes strength and capability, often associated with the deity Indra, who is known for his valor and might.
  • abhiṣṭaye: This translates to "for the purpose of achieving" or "for the attainment of." It indicates the intention behind the worship or invocation.
  • yaḥ sunvate: This means "those who listen" or "who hear." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to divine messages or teachings.
  • stuvate: This translates to "who praise" or "who extol." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine.
  • kāmyaṁ: This word means "desirable" or "wishful." It refers to the aspirations and desires that individuals seek to fulfill.
  • vasu: This term signifies "wealth" or "prosperity." It represents the material and spiritual abundance that one seeks in life.
  • sahasreṇeva: This translates to "like a thousand." It implies abundance and the idea of receiving blessings in great measure.
  • maṁhate: This means "to be fulfilled" or "to be achieved." It indicates the realization of desires and aspirations through divine blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of worship, understanding, and receptivity towards divine wisdom. It highlights the importance of praising the divine for the fulfillment of desires and the attainment of prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or personal reflections where individuals seek guidance, strength, and abundance in their lives. By invoking the divine and expressing gratitude, one can cultivate a mindset that attracts positive outcomes and enriches their spiritual journey.
