यस्मा ऊमासो अमृताऽरासत रायस पोषं च हविषा ददाशुषे |उक्षन्त्य अस्मै मरुतो हिताऽइव पुरू राजांसी पयसा मयोभुवः ||
yasmā ūmāso amṛtā arāsata rāyas poṣaṁ ca haviṣā dadāśuṣe | ukṣanty asmai maruto hitā iva purū rajāṁsi payasā mayobhuvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmā: From whom
    • ūmāso: The ones who are united
    • amṛtā: Immortal
    • arāsata: Who are not separated
    • rāyas: Wealth or prosperity
    • poṣaṁ: Nourishment
    • ca: And
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • dadāśuṣe: To the one who gives
    • ukṣanty: They who are active
    • asmai: To him
    • maruto: The Maruts (storm deities)
    • hitā: Beneficial
    • iva: Like
    • purū: Many
    • rajāṁsi: Kings or rulers
    • payasā: With nourishment
    • mayobhuvaḥ: Who are full of strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine source from which unity and immortality arise.
    • The phrase "ūmāso amṛtā" suggests a state of being where individuals are united and free from death, symbolizing a harmonious existence.
    • The mention of "rāyas poṣaṁ" indicates that this divine source provides nourishment and prosperity, essential for life.
    • The "Maruts" are invoked as powerful forces that bring beneficial outcomes, akin to how storms can bring rain that nourishes the earth.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and gratitude towards this divine source, which can be seen as a metaphor for acknowledging the forces of nature and the universe that sustain us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain, recognizing the Maruts as the bringers of rain, which is essential for their crops.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek unity and harmony in relationships, understanding that a strong bond can lead to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate harvest festivals, giving thanks for the nourishment provided by nature, reflecting the essence of offerings in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life, the importance of unity, and the need for gratitude towards the forces that sustain us.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the blessings we receive and to strive for harmony in our lives.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to recognize our dependence on nature and the universe, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the environment and each other.
