युष्मे देवाऽपि ष्मसि युध्यन्त इव वर्मसु |युयम् महो न एनसो युयम् अर्ब्हाद उरुष्यतानेहसो वा ऊतयः सुूतयः वा ऊतयः ||
yuṣme devā api ṣmasi yudhyanta iva varmasu | yūyam maho na enaso yūyam arbhād uruṣyatānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • yuṣme: This word translates to "for you" or "to you," indicating a direction of reverence or offering towards the divine.
  • devā: This means "gods" or "deities," referring to the divine beings that are worshipped and revered in Vedic tradition.
  • api: This word means "also" or "even," suggesting inclusivity or emphasis.
  • ṣmasi: This translates to "we are" or "we exist," indicating a state of being or presence.
  • yudhyanta: This means "fighting" or "struggling," which can refer to the challenges faced in life.
  • iva: This translates to "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • varmasu: This means "in the armor" or "in the protection," symbolizing safety and security.
  • yūyam: This word means "you all," addressing a collective group, often the deities or the audience of the mantra.
  • maho: This translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating strength and power.
  • na: This means "not," used for negation.
  • enaso: This translates to "of the enemies," referring to adversaries or obstacles.
  • arbhād: This means "from the young" or "from the children," indicating innocence or new beginnings.
  • uruṣyatānehaso: This translates to "of the great strength," referring to immense power or energy.
  • va: This means "or," used to present alternatives.
  • ūtayaḥ: This means "those who rise" or "those who elevate," indicating progress or ascension.
  • suūtayo: This translates to "well-risen" or "well-elevated," suggesting success and achievement.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, acknowledging the presence of gods who are engaged in the struggles of life, akin to warriors in battle. It emphasizes the importance of divine protection and strength in overcoming adversities, particularly those posed by enemies or challenges. The mention of "young" signifies the potential for growth and the importance of nurturing strength from an early stage.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, individuals can invoke this mantra to seek strength and protection from divine forces, much like a soldier preparing for battle.
  • Community Support: In times of collective struggle, such as during natural disasters or social issues, this mantra can serve as a reminder of unity and the strength derived from collective efforts.
  • Education and Growth: The reference to "young" can be applied in educational settings, where nurturing the potential of children is crucial for their development into strong individuals.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges, the strength found in unity, and the nurturing of potential from a young age. It serves as a reminder that, with the help of the divine, individuals and communities can rise above adversities and achieve greatness.
