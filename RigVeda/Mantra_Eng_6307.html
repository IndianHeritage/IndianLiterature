यदा ते मारुतीर विशस तुभ्यम इन्द्र नियेमिरे |आद इति ते विश्वा भुवनानि येमिरे ||
yadā te mārutīr viśas tubhyam indra niyemire | ād it te viśvā bhuvanāni yemire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadā: When
    • te: Your
    • mārutīr: Wind or Air (often associated with the deity Vayu)
    • viśas: Entered or penetrated
    • tubhyam: To you
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • niyemire: Controlled or governed
    • ād: Thus
    • it: Here
    • te: Your
    • viśvā: All or universal
    • bhuvanāni: Worlds or realms
    • yemire: Have been governed or ruled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of the wind (Māruti) and its connection to Indra, the king of the gods.
    • It suggests that when the winds enter or influence the realms, they are under the control of Indra.
    • The mantra highlights the relationship between natural forces and divine authority, emphasizing that all elements of nature are interconnected and governed by higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In meteorology, understanding wind patterns is crucial for predicting weather changes. Just as the mantra suggests control over the winds, scientists study these patterns to forecast storms and climate changes.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can be related to leadership and governance. Just as Indra governs the winds and realms, effective leaders must manage various elements within their organizations or communities to ensure harmony and progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting natural forces and understanding our place within the larger cosmos.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, acknowledging the influence of external forces while striving for personal and communal harmony.
