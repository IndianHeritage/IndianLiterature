वृषणश्वेन मरुतो वृषप्सुना रथेन वृषनाभिना |आ श्येनासो न पक्षिणो वृत्था नरो हव्याऽनो वीतये गता ||
vṛṣaṇaśvena maruto vṛṣapsunā rathena vṛṣanābhinā | ā śyenāso na pakṣiṇo vṛthā naro havyā no vītaye gata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣaṇaśvena: With the strength of the bull or powerful energy.
    • maruto: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities or winds.
    • vṛṣapsunā: With the energy of the bull or strength.
    • rathena: By the chariot or vehicle.
    • vṛṣanābhinā: With the strength of the bull.
    • ā śyenāso: O birds or those who soar.
    • na pakṣiṇo: Not like the birds.
    • vṛthā: In vain or without purpose.
    • naro: Man or human beings.
    • havyā: Offerings or sacrifices.
    • no: Our.
    • vītaye: For the purpose of liberation or attainment.
    • gata: Gone or departed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the strength and energy associated with the bull, symbolizing power and vitality.
    • The Maruts represent the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds, indicating the dynamic and uncontrollable aspects of life.
    • The mention of a chariot suggests a journey or movement, possibly towards a goal or aspiration.
    • The phrase "O birds" can be interpreted as a call to those who aspire to rise above, yet it warns against aimlessness or actions that lack purpose.
    • The reference to human beings and offerings emphasizes the importance of intention and meaningful actions in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to harness one's inner strength and energy (vṛṣaṇaśvena) to navigate challenges (maruto) effectively, much like a team working together towards a common goal (rathena).
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to rise above distractions (na pakṣiṇo) and focus on meaningful pursuits (havyā) that lead to personal growth and fulfillment (vītaye).
    • In community service, it highlights the importance of collective effort and intention in making a positive impact, ensuring that actions are not in vain (vṛthā) but contribute to the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of strength, purpose, and collective effort in our lives.
    • It encourages us to channel our energies wisely and to engage in actions that are meaningful and directed towards a higher purpose.
    • In essence, it teaches us that while we may have the strength to act, it is the intention behind our actions that truly matters in achieving our goals and contributing to the world around us.
