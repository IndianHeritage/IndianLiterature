त्वावतः पुरूवसो वयम् इन्द्र प्रणेताḥ | स्मसि स्थातार हरिणाम् ||
tvāvataḥ purūvaso vayam indra praṇetaḥ | smasi sthātar harīṇām ||
  • tvāvataḥ - This word can be translated as "from you" or "of you." It signifies a connection or relationship with a higher power, often referring to the divine or cosmic forces.
  • purūvaso - This term refers to "the many" or "the multitude." It indicates a collective or community aspect, emphasizing the importance of unity among individuals.
  • vayam - This means "we" or "us," highlighting the speaker's identity as part of a larger group or community.
  • indra - Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and leadership. He represents the force that guides and protects.
  • praṇetaḥ - This translates to "the leader" or "the guide." It signifies the role of a leader in directing and inspiring others.
  • smasi - This word means "you are" or "you exist," affirming the presence of the divine or the guiding force in our lives.
  • sthātar - This term can be understood as "the one who supports" or "the stabilizer," indicating a foundation or source of strength.
  • harīṇām - This refers to "the beings" or "the creatures," often symbolizing the various forms of life and existence in the universe.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for guidance and support from a higher power, acknowledging the collective strength of the community and the importance of leadership. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and humanity, where the divine serves as a source of strength and stability for the multitude.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: In organizations or communities, effective leaders are essential for guiding their teams. This mantra serves as a reminder for leaders to seek divine guidance and support while also fostering unity among their members.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on "we" highlights the importance of collective effort. In times of crisis, communities can come together to support one another, drawing strength from their unity.
  • Spiritual Connection: Individuals can use this mantra in their spiritual practices to invoke a sense of connection with the divine, seeking guidance and stability in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance, community unity, and effective leadership. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; we are supported by a higher power and the collective strength of those around us. By embracing these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with confidence and purpose.
