त्वं मायाभिर अप मायिनोऽधमः स्वधाभिर ये अधि शुप्ताव अजुह्वत |त्वं पिप्रोः नृमणः प्रारुजः पुरः प्र ऋजिश्वानं दस्युहत्येष्व आविथ ||
tvam māyābhir apa māyino 'dhamaḥ svadhābhir ye adhi śuptāv ajuhvata | tvam pipror nṛmaṇaḥ prārujaḥ puraḥ pra ṛjiśvānaṁ dasyuhatyeṣv āvitha ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • māyābhir - This term refers to "by illusions" or "by the power of illusion," suggesting the presence of deceptive forces in the world.
  • apa - Meaning "away" or "from," it indicates a separation or distancing from something.
  • māyino - This word means "of the illusionist," referring to a being or force that creates illusions.
  • 'dhamaḥ - Translates to "the lowest" or "the base," indicating a state of being that is not elevated or pure.
  • svadhābhir - This means "by one's own nature," suggesting an intrinsic quality or essence.
  • ye - Meaning "those who," it introduces a group of individuals or entities.
  • adhi - This translates to "upon" or "over," indicating a position or state of being.
  • śuptāv - Meaning "in sleep," it refers to a state of unconsciousness or ignorance.
  • ajuhvata - This means "they did not offer," suggesting a lack of action or sacrifice.
  • tvam - Reiterating the address to the divine.
  • pipror - This term means "you who are the source," indicating a provider or sustainer.
  • nṛmaṇaḥ - Meaning "of mankind," it refers to human beings and their nature.
  • prārujaḥ - This translates to "who have arisen," indicating a state of awakening or realization.
  • puraḥ - Meaning "in front" or "before," it suggests a position of leadership or guidance.
  • pra - A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progression.
  • ṛjiśvānaṁ - This term refers to "the one who is righteous," indicating a moral or ethical quality.
  • dasyuhatyeṣv - Meaning "in the midst of enemies," it refers to challenges or adversities faced.
  • āvitha - This means "to cover" or "to envelop," suggesting protection or concealment.

The mantra speaks to the duality of existence, addressing the divine while acknowledging the illusions and challenges faced by humanity. It emphasizes the importance of self-awareness and the need to rise above ignorance and deception. The reference to "those who have arisen" suggests a call to action for individuals to awaken to their true nature and responsibilities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to recognize and overcome their illusions and limitations.
  • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to guide others with righteousness and integrity, especially in challenging situations.
  • In spiritual practices, it highlights the importance of self-awareness and the pursuit of truth beyond superficial appearances.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-realization and ethical living. It encourages individuals to navigate through life's illusions and challenges with awareness and righteousness, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
