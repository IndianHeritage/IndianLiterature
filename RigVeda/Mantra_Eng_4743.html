अस्मास्मा इदन्धसोऽध्वर्यो प्र भरā सुतम् |कुवित समस्य जेन्यस्य शर्धतोऽभिशस्तेर अवस्परत् ||
asmā-asmā id andhaso 'dhvaryo pra bharā sutam | kuvit samasya jenyasya śardhato 'bhiśaster avasparat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā-asmā: "from us, from us" - indicating a sense of unity or collective identity.
    • id: "this" - referring to the present moment or situation.
    • andhaso: "of the blind" - symbolizing ignorance or lack of awareness.
    • 'dhvaryo: "the one who performs" - referring to an individual who acts or performs duties.
    • pra: "forth" - suggesting movement or progression.
    • bharā: "bear" or "carry" - indicating responsibility or support.
    • sutam: "the son" or "offspring" - representing the next generation or continuation of lineage.
    • kuvit: "bad deeds" - referring to negative actions or consequences.
    • samasya: "of the problem" - indicating challenges or difficulties.
    • jenyasya: "of the one who is born" - referring to those who come into existence.
    • śardhato: "with strength" - suggesting resilience or power.
    • 'bhiśaster: "of the healer" - referring to someone who provides healing or resolution.
    • avasparat: "may it be fulfilled" - a wish for completion or realization.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective responsibility and awareness in overcoming ignorance.
    • It suggests that individuals must bear the weight of their actions and support the next generation.
    • The reference to "bad deeds" highlights the need to confront and resolve challenges with strength and healing.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when faced with social issues such as poverty or discrimination, members must come together to support each other and work towards solutions.
    • Parents have a responsibility to guide their children, ensuring they learn from past mistakes and grow into responsible adults.
    • In a workplace, team members must acknowledge their roles in both successes and failures, fostering an environment of accountability and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of collective action in addressing ignorance and challenges.
    • It encourages us to take responsibility for our actions and to support one another in the journey of life.
    • By embodying the principles of awareness, strength, and healing, we can create a more harmonious and supportive society.
