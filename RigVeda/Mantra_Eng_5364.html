मन्त्र: चकार ता कृणवन् नूनम अन्यā यानि ब्रुवन्ति वेधसः सुतेषु | जनीर इव पतिर एकः समानो न मामृजे पुर इन्द्रः सु सर्वाः ||
cakāra tā kṛṇavan nūnam anyā yāni bruvanti vedhasaḥ suteṣu | janīr iva patir ekaḥ samāno ni māmṛje pura indraḥ su sarvāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakāra: He did, he created
    • tā: those
    • kṛṇavan: who are doing
    • nūnam: indeed
    • anyā: other
    • yāni: which
    • bruvanti: speak, say
    • vedhasaḥ: of the wise, the learned
    • suteṣu: in the offerings, in the sacrifices
    • janīr: like mothers
    • iva: like
    • patir: lord, master
    • ekaḥ: one
    • samāno: equal, similar
    • ni: not
    • māmṛje: he has removed me
    • pura: from the city, from the place
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • su: good, well
    • sarvāḥ: all
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the actions of the wise and learned individuals who create and speak about the offerings made in sacrifices.
    • It emphasizes the unity and equality among those who are engaged in such noble pursuits, likening them to a singular lord or master.
    • The mention of Indra suggests a divine presence or blessing that is invoked in these actions, highlighting the importance of community and shared purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be related to teamwork where individuals contribute their unique skills towards a common goal, much like the wise individuals in the mantra.
    • In educational institutions, teachers and students working together to create knowledge and understanding can be seen as a reflection of the unity and equality mentioned.
    • In community service, volunteers coming together to serve a cause exemplify the idea of collective effort and shared purpose, akin to the offerings in sacrifices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on collaboration, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
    • It teaches us that while individual contributions are valuable, the collective effort is what truly leads to success and fulfillment.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to work together harmoniously, respecting each other's contributions, and striving towards common goals, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or community initiatives.
