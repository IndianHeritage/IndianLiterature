तं अमृक्षन्त वाजिनं उपस्थे अदितेर अधि |विप्रासो अण्व्या धियाः ||
tam amṛkṣanta vājinam upasthe aditer adhi | viprāso aṇvyā dhiyā ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • amṛkṣanta - This term means "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the everlasting nature of the divine.
  • vājinam - This word means "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," often associated with strength and power.
  • upasthe - This translates to "in the presence" or "near," suggesting a close relationship or connection with the divine.
  • aditer - This refers to "Aditi," the mother of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing the infinite and the boundless.
  • adhi - This means "above" or "over," indicating a higher state or level of existence.
  • viprāso - This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned," often denoting those who possess knowledge and insight.
  • aṇvyā - This means "with understanding" or "with wisdom," emphasizing the importance of intellect in spiritual pursuits.
  • dhiyā - This translates to "with thought" or "with contemplation," highlighting the role of meditation and reflection in connecting with the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, acknowledging the eternal and powerful nature of the higher power, while also emphasizing the importance of wisdom and contemplation in understanding and connecting with this divine presence. It suggests that through thoughtful reflection and the pursuit of knowledge, one can attain a closer relationship with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation to focus on the eternal nature of the divine and seek guidance in their spiritual journey.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on wisdom and understanding encourages individuals to pursue knowledge and self-improvement, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders and educators can embody the qualities of the "viprāso," using their knowledge and insight to guide others towards a higher understanding of life and spirituality.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine through wisdom and contemplation. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, fostering a deeper relationship with the eternal and powerful aspects of existence. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with greater insight and purpose.
