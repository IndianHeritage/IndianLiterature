ऋभुर न रथ्यं नवं दधातā केतम आदिशे |शुक्राः पवध्वम् अर्णसā ||
ṛbhur na rathyaṁ navaṁ dadhātā ketam ādiśe | śukrāḥ pavadhvam arṇasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛbhur: The one who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • na: Not.
    • rathyaṁ: The chariot or vehicle.
    • navaṁ: New or fresh.
    • dadhātā: One who creates or brings forth.
    • ketam: The goal or destination.
    • ādiśe: To direct or guide.
    • śukrāḥ: The pure or bright ones.
    • pavadhvam: To purify or cleanse.
    • arṇasā: From the depths or the ocean.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wise individuals who do not create a new chariot (or path) but instead guide towards a goal.
    • The mention of "śukrāḥ" refers to those who are pure and enlightened, suggesting that purity of thought and intention is essential in guiding others.
    • The term "arṇasā" implies that the guidance comes from deep knowledge or experience, akin to drawing wisdom from the depths of the ocean.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a mentor who has vast experience (arṇasā) does not simply create new strategies (navaṁ) but instead guides their team towards achieving the company's goals (ketam) through their wisdom (ṛbhur).
    • In education, a teacher (śukrāḥ) who purifies the minds of students (pavadhvam) by imparting knowledge does not just provide new information but helps them understand the deeper concepts (arṇasā) that lead to true learning.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and purity in guiding others towards their goals.
    • It teaches that true leadership is not about creating new paths but about guiding others with deep knowledge and integrity.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as education, mentorship, and leadership, where the focus should be on nurturing and guiding rather than merely directing.
