विष्णुं स्तोमासः पुरुदसमं अर्का भागस्येव कारीणो यामनि गमन् |उरुक्रमः ककुहो यस्य पूर्वीर न मर्धन्ति युवतयो जनित्रीः ||
viṣṇuṁ stomāsaḥ purudasmam arkā bhagasyeva kāriṇo yāmani gman | urukramaḥ kakuho yasya pūrvīr na mardhanti yuvatayo janitrīḥ ||
  • viṣṇuṁ: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology. He embodies the principles of order and harmony in the universe.
  • stomāsaḥ: This term can be interpreted as praises or hymns. It signifies the act of worship and reverence towards the divine.
  • purudasmam: This word suggests vastness or abundance. It can be related to the infinite nature of the universe and the resources it provides.
  • arkā: Refers to light or radiance, often associated with the sun. It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the dispelling of ignorance.
  • bhagasyeva: This term relates to prosperity and wealth. It emphasizes the importance of abundance in life.
  • kāriṇo: Meaning 'doer' or 'creator', it highlights the active role of the divine in the world.
  • yāmani: This can be interpreted as 'those who are engaged in' or 'those who are devoted to'. It signifies dedication and commitment.
  • gman: This term can be understood as 'to know' or 'to understand', emphasizing the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • urukramaḥ: Refers to the one with great strides, often associated with Lord Vishnu's ability to encompass the universe in his steps.
  • kakuho: This term can be interpreted as 'the one who is hidden' or 'the mysterious one', indicating the deeper, often unseen aspects of divinity.
  • yasya: Meaning 'whose', it denotes possession or belonging, often referring to the divine attributes of Vishnu.
  • pūrvīr: Refers to the ancestors or those who came before us, emphasizing the connection between generations.
  • na mardhanti: This phrase means 'do not harm' or 'do not destroy', indicating protection and preservation.
  • yuvatayo: Refers to young women or maidens, symbolizing fertility, creativity, and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • janitrīḥ: Meaning 'mothers', it signifies the nurturing and protective qualities associated with motherhood.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, abundance, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of reverence towards the divine, the interconnectedness of generations, and the nurturing qualities of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom while remaining grounded in their cultural and familial roots.
  • In community settings, it promotes the idea of collective prosperity and the importance of supporting one another, especially the younger generations.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder of the divine presence in everyday life, encouraging individuals to engage in acts of worship and gratitude.

Overall, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life of harmony, respect, and abundance, reflecting the values that are essential for personal and communal well-being.
