मरतः पिबत ऋतुनā पोत्राद यज्ञं पुनीतन |यूयं हि ष्ठा सुदानवः ||
marutaḥ pibata ṛtunā potrād yajñam punītana | yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā sudānavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "marutaḥ pibata ṛtunā potrād yajñam punītana | yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā sudānavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Marutaḥ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities or winds in Vedic literature.
    • Pibata: Means "drink" or "imbibe," suggesting the act of taking in or absorbing.
    • Ṛtunā: Translates to "with the seasons," indicating the cyclical nature of time and life.
    • Potrād: Means "from the pot," symbolizing nourishment or sustenance.
    • Yajñam: Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals.
    • Punītana: Means "purifying," suggesting the act of cleansing or sanctifying.
    • Yūyaṁ: Translates to "you all," addressing a collective group.
    • Hi: A particle that emphasizes the statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • Ṣṭhā: Means "to be firm" or "to stand," indicating stability or strength.
    • Sudānavaḥ: Refers to "good or noble people," suggesting a virtuous or righteous character.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, encouraging them to "drink" or absorb the essence of the offerings made during rituals.
    • The reference to "with the seasons" highlights the importance of timing and the natural cycles in our lives, reminding us to align our actions with the rhythms of nature.
    • The act of purifying the offerings signifies the need for sincerity and purity in our intentions when making sacrifices or offerings, whether in rituals or in daily life.
    • Addressing the collective group emphasizes community and shared responsibility, suggesting that together, we can achieve greater strength and stability.
    • The mention of "noble people" encourages us to aspire to virtuous qualities and to surround ourselves with those who uplift and inspire us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they embody the spirit of this mantra by sharing food and offerings, thus creating a sense of unity and collective joy.
    • In personal development, aligning one's actions with the natural cycles (like planting in spring) can lead to more fruitful outcomes, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on timing and nature.
    • In professional settings, fostering a culture of integrity and purity in intentions can lead to a more harmonious and productive work environment, resonating with the idea of noble character.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, community, and personal integrity.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to engage in rituals and offerings with a pure heart, to recognize the importance of timing, and to build a supportive community of noble individuals.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both individually and collectively.
