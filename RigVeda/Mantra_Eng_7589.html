स सुक्रतूर रणिता यः सुतेष्व अनुत्तमन्युर यो अहेव रेवान् |य एक इन नार्य अपांसी कर्ता स वृत्रहा प्रतीद अन्यं आहुः ||
sa sukratū raṇitā yaḥ suteṣv anuttamanyur yo aheva revān | ya eka in nary apāṁsi kartā sa vṛtrahā pratīd anyam āhuḥ ||
  • sa sukratū raṇitā: This phrase can be translated as "he who is virtuous and has fought bravely." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and valor in one's actions. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice, such as activists or soldiers who fight for their country's freedom.
  • yaḥ suteṣv anuttamanyur: This means "who has the best offspring and is free from anger." It highlights the value of having a peaceful disposition and nurturing good qualities in the next generation. For example, parents who instill values of kindness and empathy in their children contribute to a more harmonious society.
  • yo aheva revān: This translates to "who is indeed the one who brings forth prosperity." It signifies the role of individuals who contribute to the well-being of their community, such as entrepreneurs who create jobs or philanthropists who support social causes.
  • ya eka in nary apāṁsi kartā: This phrase means "who alone is the doer among the women." It can be interpreted as recognizing the strength and capability of women in society. For instance, women leaders in various fields, such as politics or business, exemplify this strength by breaking barriers and leading initiatives.
  • sa vṛtrahā pratīd anyam āhuḥ: This translates to "he who is the slayer of Vritra is called by others." Vritra is a mythological serpent representing obstacles or challenges. This part of the mantra signifies overcoming difficulties and being recognized for one's achievements. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face adversity, such as cancer survivors or those who have overcome personal struggles, and are celebrated for their resilience.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the ideals of virtue, bravery, peacefulness, prosperity, and overcoming challenges. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their lives. In today's world, this mantra can inspire people to act with integrity, support their communities, and strive for personal growth. By recognizing and celebrating these values, we can contribute to a more just and compassionate society.
