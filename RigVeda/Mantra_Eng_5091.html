घृतवती भुवनानाम् अभिश्रियोर्वी पृथ्वी मधुदुग्धे सुपेशसा | dyावापृथिवी वरुणस्य धर्मणा विष्कभिते अजरे भूरेतसā ||
ghṛtavatī bhuvanānām abhiśriyorvī pṛthvī madhudughe supeśasā | dyāvāpṛthivī varuṇasya dharmaṇā viṣkabhite ajare bhūriretasā ||
  • ghṛtavatī: This word translates to "rich in ghee" or "nourishing." Ghee is often associated with purity and nourishment in Indian culture.
  • bhuvanānām: This means "of the worlds" or "of all beings." It signifies the universality of the mantra's message.
  • abhiśriyorvī: This can be interpreted as "the earth of great glory." It emphasizes the magnificence and importance of the earth.
  • pṛthvī: This directly translates to "earth." It represents the physical world and our connection to it.
  • madhudughe: This means "milk that is sweet" or "nourishing." It symbolizes abundance and the sweetness of life.
  • supeśasā: This translates to "with good qualities" or "well-formed." It indicates the ideal state of being or existence.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase means "heaven and earth." It signifies the duality and balance of the universe.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "of Varuna," the deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth.
  • dharmaṇā: This translates to "by virtue" or "by righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living a life of virtue.
  • viṣkabhite: This means "spread out" or "expanded." It suggests the vastness of existence and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • ajare: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of the universe and existence.
  • bhūriretasā: This means "with great energy" or "with abundant vitality." It emphasizes the dynamic and vibrant nature of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of nourishment, abundance, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It highlights the importance of living in harmony with the earth and the cosmos, emphasizing virtues such as righteousness and purity. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate the natural world, promote sustainability, and cultivate a sense of responsibility towards the environment. It serves as a reminder of the richness of life and the importance of nurturing both ourselves and the world around us, fostering a sense of unity and respect for all forms of existence.
