अस्याजरासो दमाम अरित्रा अर्चद्धूमासो अग्नयः पावकाः |श्वितीचयः श्वात्रासो भुरण्यवो वनर्षदो वायवो न सोमाः ||
asyājarāso damām aritrā arcaddhūmāso agnayaḥ pāvakāḥ | śvitīcayaḥ śvātrāso bhuraṇyavo vanarṣado vāyavo na somāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "asyājarāso damām aritrā arcaddhūmāso agnayaḥ pāvakāḥ | śvitīcayaḥ śvātrāso bhuraṇyavo vanarṣado vāyavo na somāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asyājarāso: "of this eternal" - Refers to the timeless and unchanging nature of the universe.
    • damām: "of the restraining" - Suggests control or moderation, possibly in the context of desires or actions.
    • aritrā: "of the unyielding" - Indicates strength and resilience.
    • arcaddhūmāso: "of the worshipful smoke" - Represents the offerings made to the divine, often through fire rituals.
    • agnayaḥ: "of the fires" - Refers to the sacred fire, a central element in Vedic rituals.
    • pāvakāḥ: "the purifiers" - Symbolizes the cleansing and transformative power of fire.
    • śvitīcayaḥ: "the shining" - Represents brightness, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • śvātrāso: "the black" - Could symbolize the unknown or the hidden aspects of existence.
    • bhuraṇyavo: "the bearers of wealth" - Refers to those who provide abundance and prosperity.
    • vanarṣado: "the forest dwellers" - Represents nature and its inhabitants, emphasizing harmony with the environment.
    • vāyavo: "the winds" - Symbolizes movement, change, and the unseen forces of nature.
    • na somāḥ: "not the moon" - Suggests a distinction from the lunar influences, possibly indicating a focus on the solar or fiery aspects of divinity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of asyājarāso can be seen in the way we strive for stability in our lives amidst constant change, such as maintaining relationships or careers.
    • damām reflects the importance of self-control in modern life, such as resisting temptations in a consumer-driven society.
    • The aritrā aspect can be related to personal resilience, like overcoming challenges in health or career.
    • arcaddhūmāso is akin to the rituals we perform in our lives, such as celebrating festivals or family traditions that honor our heritage.
    • agnayaḥ and pāvakāḥ can be compared to the role of fire in cooking and warmth, essential for survival and comfort.
    • śvitīcayaḥ represents the pursuit of knowledge, as seen in education and personal development.
    • bhuraṇyavo can be likened to the efforts of entrepreneurs who create wealth and opportunities for others.
    • vanarṣado emphasizes the importance of environmental conservation, as seen in movements to protect forests and wildlife.
    • vāyavo symbolizes the need for adaptability in life, much like how we adjust to changing circumstances.
    • na somāḥ can be interpreted as a reminder to focus on the tangible and practical aspects of life rather than getting lost in illusions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance between the eternal and the transient, urging us to find harmony in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements of existence, from the physical to the spiritual.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to practice moderation, resilience, and respect for nature, fostering a holistic approach to living.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a guide for personal growth and societal well-being, emphasizing the importance of both individual and collective responsibility.
