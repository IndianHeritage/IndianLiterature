अरुणो मा सकृत् वृकः पथā यन्तं ददर्श हि |उज जिहीते निचाय्य तष्टेव पृष्ठ्यामयी वित्तम् मे अस्य रोदसी ||
aruṇo mā sakṛd vṛkaḥ pathā yantaṁ dadarśa hi | uj jihīte nicāyyā taṣṭeva pṛṣṭyāmayī vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aruṇo: The reddish dawn or the early morning light.
    • mā: Not or do not.
    • sakṛd: Once or at least once.
    • vṛkaḥ: The wolf.
    • pathā: On the path or way.
    • yantaṁ: Going or moving.
    • dadarśa: Saw or perceived.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • uj: Bright or shining.
    • jihīte: To be desired or sought after.
    • nicāyyā: With a firm foundation or stability.
    • taṣṭeva: Like a firm support or base.
    • pṛṣṭyāmayī: Related to the back or support.
    • vittam: Wealth or resources.
    • me: My or mine.
    • asya: Of this or this.
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities.
    • The mention of the wolf can represent challenges or obstacles that one may encounter on their path.
    • The phrase "not once did the wolf see" suggests that one should not be deterred by fear or challenges.
    • The "bright" aspect emphasizes the importance of clarity and vision in pursuing one's goals.
    • The idea of stability and support indicates the need for a strong foundation in life, whether it be emotional, financial, or social.
    • Wealth and resources are acknowledged as essential for navigating life's challenges and achieving success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, the mantra can be applied to someone starting a new job. The dawn represents the new opportunity, while the wolf symbolizes the challenges of adapting to a new environment.
    • In personal life, it can relate to someone overcoming fears, such as public speaking. The mantra encourages them to face their fears with clarity and stability.
    • Financially, it can be interpreted as a reminder to build a strong foundation before pursuing wealth, ensuring that one is prepared for any obstacles that may arise.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of facing challenges with courage and clarity.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek stability and support in all endeavors, whether personal or professional.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embrace new beginnings while being mindful of the resources and foundations they build upon.
