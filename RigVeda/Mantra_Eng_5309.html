सना ता त इन्द्र भोजानानि रातहव्याय दाशुषे सुदासे |वृष्णे ते हरि वृषणा युनज्मि व्यन्तु ब्रह्माणि पुरुषाक वाजम् ||
sanā tā ta indra bhojanāni rātahavyāya dāśuṣe sudāse | vṛṣṇe te harī vṛṣaṇā yunajmi vyantu brahmāṇi puruśāka vājam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanā: Together, in harmony
    • tā: That, those
    • ta: You, your
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • bhojanāni: Foods, nourishment
    • rātahavyāya: For the one who is invoked during the sacrifice
    • dāśuṣe: To the one who gives
    • Sudāse: To Sudāsa, a king or a person of significance
    • vṛṣṇe: To Vṛṣṇa, another name for Lord Vishnu
    • te: Your
    • harī: The divine, often associated with Vishnu
    • vṛṣaṇā: The strong, powerful
    • yunajmi: I unite, I connect
    • vyantu: May they come, may they arrive
    • brahmāṇi: The divine, the ultimate reality
    • puruśāka: The one who is the essence of all beings
    • vājam: Wealth, strength, or vigor
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer invoking the blessings of Indra and Vṛṣṇa for nourishment and strength. It emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony in seeking divine support for sustenance and prosperity. The mantra reflects a deep respect for the divine forces that govern the universe and acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to share food and celebrate unity, reflecting the essence of "sanā" and "tā".
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities like Indra and Vṛṣṇa, symbolizing gratitude for the nourishment and prosperity received.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, seeking strength and support from the divine to overcome challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving harmony, nourishment, and strength. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community gatherings, personal prayers, and during significant life events. By invoking the blessings of Indra and Vṛṣṇa, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity and purpose, ensuring that they are supported in their endeavors and nourished in body and spirit.
