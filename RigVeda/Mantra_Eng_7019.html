यद ध नूनं यद वा यज्ञे यद वा पृथिव्यां अधि | अतो नो यज्ञं आशुभिर महेमत उग्र उग्रेभिर आ गहि ||
yad dha nūnaṁ yad vā yajñe yad vā pṛthivyām adhi | ato no yajñam āśubhir mahemata ugra ugrebhir ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • dha: is placed or established
    • nūnaṁ: indeed
    • yad vā: or whatever
    • yajñe: in the sacrifice
    • yad vā: or whatever
    • pṛthivyām: on the earth
    • adhi: above
    • ato: therefore
    • no: for us
    • yajñam: the sacrifice
    • āśubhir: with inauspicious things
    • mahemata: may we be protected
    • ugra: fierce
    • ugrebhir: with fierce ones
    • ā gahi: may you hold or grasp
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a plea for protection and guidance in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • The speaker acknowledges the various forms of offerings made to the divine, whether in the form of rituals (yajña) or through the natural world (pṛthivyām).
    • It emphasizes the importance of being shielded from negative influences (āśubhir) and the need for divine support in overcoming challenges (ugra).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the concept of seeking protection from negative energies or influences, such as stress or anxiety.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, individuals often perform rituals to invoke blessings and safeguard against misfortunes.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may engage in motivational activities or rituals to foster unity and protect against competition, akin to the yajña.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection and guidance in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions (yajña) with the world around us (pṛthivyām) and the divine.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek positive influences and support while being mindful of the challenges they may face.
