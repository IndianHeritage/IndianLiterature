दोहेन गाम उप शिक्षा सखायम् प्र बोधय जरीतर जारम् इन्द्रम् |कोषं न पूर्णं वसुनā न्यृष्टम् ā च्यावय मगधेयाय शूरम् ||
dohena gām upa śikṣā sakhāyam pra bodhaya jaritar jāram indram | kośaṁ na pūrṇaṁ vasunā nyṛṣṭam ā cyāvaya maghadeyāya śūram ||
  • Mantra: "dohena gām upa śikṣā sakhāyam pra bodhaya jaritar jāram indram | kośaṁ na pūrṇaṁ vasunā nyṛṣṭam ā cyāvaya maghadeyāya śūram ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dohena: By the power of nourishment or sustenance.
    • gām: The goal or the path.
    • upa: Near or towards.
    • śikṣā: Instruction or teaching.
    • sakhāyam: Friends or companions.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • bodhaya: Enlighten or awaken.
    • jaritar: Old age or decay.
    • jāram: Aging or deterioration.
    • indram: The powerful or the king (often referring to Indra, the king of gods).
    • kośaṁ: The treasure or wealth.
    • na: Not.
    • pūrṇaṁ: Full or complete.
    • vasunā: By wealth or riches.
    • nyṛṣṭam: Having been touched or affected.
    • ā: From or out of.
    • cyāvaya: Remove or cast away.
    • maghadeyāya: For the one who is strong or mighty.
    • śūram: The brave or the valiant.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment and guidance in achieving one's goals.
    • It suggests that through proper instruction and companionship, one can overcome the challenges of aging and decay.
    • The reference to Indra symbolizes the strength and power needed to face life's adversities.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of wealth not being merely material but also encompassing knowledge and wisdom.
    • It encourages the removal of obstacles that prevent one from attaining their full potential.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, mentorship can be seen as a form of nourishment that helps individuals grow and succeed in their careers.
    • In personal relationships, having supportive friends can provide the necessary encouragement to overcome life's challenges.
    • In education, teachers play a crucial role in enlightening students, guiding them towards their goals and helping them navigate difficulties.
    • In health, maintaining a balanced diet and lifestyle can be viewed as a way to combat the effects of aging and promote longevity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of guidance, companionship, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true wealth lies not just in material possessions but in the strength of our relationships and the wisdom we acquire.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive to overcome obstacles and achieve their fullest potential in various aspects of life.
