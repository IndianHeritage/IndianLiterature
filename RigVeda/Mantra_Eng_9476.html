एव कविस तुवीरवां ऋतज्ञा द्रविणस्युर द्रविणसश चकाणः |उक्तेभिर अत्र मतीभिः च विप्रोऽपिपयद गयो दिव्यानि जन्म ||
evā kavis tuvīravām̐ ṛtajñā draviṇasyur draviṇasaś cakānaḥ | ukthebhir atra matibhiś ca vipro 'pīpayad gayo divyāni janma ||
  • evā kavis tuvīravām̐: This phrase can be translated as "Thus, the poets, the brave ones." Here, "kavis" refers to poets or seers, and "tuvīravām̐" signifies those who are courageous or valiant. In real life, this can relate to individuals who express their thoughts and creativity boldly, such as artists, writers, and leaders who inspire others through their work.
  • ṛtajñā draviṇasyur: This part means "who know the truth and the wealth." "ṛtajñā" refers to those who are knowledgeable about the truth or righteousness, while "draviṇasyur" indicates wealth or resources. In a practical context, this can be seen in entrepreneurs or innovators who understand the ethical implications of their wealth and use it to benefit society.
  • draviṇasaś cakānaḥ: This translates to "who have created wealth." It emphasizes the act of generating resources or prosperity. For example, successful business leaders who create jobs and contribute to the economy embody this idea, as they not only accumulate wealth but also share it with the community.
  • ukthebhir atra matibhiś ca: This phrase means "by the utterances and thoughts here." It suggests that the words and ideas expressed hold significance. In everyday life, this can relate to the power of communication, where the way we articulate our thoughts can influence others and shape our environment.
  • vipro 'pīpayad: This translates to "the wise one has also nourished." "Vipra" refers to a learned or wise person, and "pīpayad" implies nurturing or fostering growth. This can be seen in mentors or educators who guide and support others in their personal and professional development.
  • gayo divyāni janma: This means "the divine birth of the cattle." In a broader sense, it can symbolize prosperity and abundance. In real life, this can be related to sustainable farming practices that promote the well-being of livestock and the environment, leading to a harmonious relationship between humans and nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of creativity, truth, wealth creation, effective communication, wisdom, and prosperity. It highlights how these elements are interconnected and essential for personal and societal growth. In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and impactful existence, where individuals not only seek their own success but also contribute positively to the community and the world at large.
