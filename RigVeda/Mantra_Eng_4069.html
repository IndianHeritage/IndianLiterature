सूता इन्द्राय वायवे सोमासो दध्याशिरः |निम्नं न यान्ति सिन्धवोऽभि प्रयः ||
sutā indrāya vāyave somāso dadhyāśiraḥ | nimnaṁ na yanti sindhavo 'bhi prayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sutā indrāya vāyave somāso dadhyāśiraḥ | nimnaṁ na yanti sindhavo 'bhi prayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sutā: The one who is well-prepared or well-made.
    • indrāya: To Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • vāyave: To Vayu, the god of wind, symbolizing movement and life force.
    • somāso: Of the soma, a sacred drink that represents immortality and divine energy.
    • dadhyāśiraḥ: Bestowing or granting strength and prosperity.
    • nimnaṁ: Low or humble.
    • na: Not.
    • yanti: Go or move.
    • sindhavo: Rivers or bodies of water.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • prayaḥ: Efforts or endeavors.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Vayu, emphasizing the importance of strength and vitality in our lives.
    • The reference to "soma" highlights the pursuit of spiritual and physical nourishment, akin to how we seek sustenance in our daily lives.
    • The phrase "nimnaṁ na yanti sindhavo" suggests that even the humble or lowly do not get swept away by the currents of life, symbolizing resilience and stability.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a job loss, one can draw strength from the mantra, reminding oneself to remain resilient and not be overwhelmed by the circumstances.
    • In community service, individuals can embody the spirit of this mantra by helping those in need, ensuring that even the less fortunate are not left behind.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of knowledge and self-improvement can be seen as a way to embody the essence of "soma," nurturing one's mind and spirit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, vitality, and resilience in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek divine blessings while also emphasizing the importance of supporting one another, especially those who may be struggling.
    • In essence, it teaches us that true strength lies not just in power, but in compassion and the ability to uplift others.
