आ भरतं शिक्षतं वज्रबाहू अस्मां इन्द्राग्नि अवतं शचीभिः |इमे नु ते रश्मयः सूर्यस्य येभिः सपित्वं पितरो न आसन ||
ā bharataṁ śikṣataṁ vajrabāhū asmām̐ indrāgnī avataṁ śacībhiḥ | ime nu te raśmayaḥ sūryasya yebhiḥ sapitvam pitaro na āsan ||
  • Mantra: "ā bharataṁ śikṣataṁ vajrabāhū asmām̐ indrāgnī avataṁ śacībhiḥ | ime nu te raśmayaḥ sūryasya yebhiḥ sapitvam pitaro na āsan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā bharataṁ: O Bharata (a term often referring to the people of India or the land itself)
    • śikṣataṁ: may you be taught or educated
    • vajrabāhū: with arms like thunder (symbolizing strength and power)
    • asmām̐: for us
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (representing divine forces)
    • avataṁ: may they protect or guide us
    • śacībhiḥ: with the wise or the learned
    • ime nu: these indeed
    • te: your
    • raśmayaḥ: rays or light
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • yebhiḥ: by which
    • sapitvam: the power of the fathers
    • pitaro: of the ancestors
    • na āsan: were not present
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of divine forces, specifically Indra and Agni, to provide strength and guidance to the people.
    • The phrase "vajrabāhū" symbolizes the need for strength and resilience in facing life's challenges, akin to how a person might rely on their inner strength during difficult times, such as overcoming personal loss or adversity.
    • The mention of "śacībhiḥ" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and learning, suggesting that education and knowledge are vital for personal and communal growth. For example, in modern society, education empowers individuals to make informed decisions and contribute positively to their communities.
    • The reference to "raśmayaḥ sūryasya" highlights the significance of light and enlightenment, akin to how the sun provides energy and sustenance to life. This can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and truth in our lives, guiding us towards better choices and understanding.
    • The phrase "sapitvam pitaro na āsan" reflects on the legacy of our ancestors and the importance of honoring their wisdom and teachings. In real life, this can be seen in practices such as family traditions, where the values and lessons passed down through generations shape our identities and moral compass.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and the guidance of divine forces in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the call for education, the honoring of ancestral wisdom, and the pursuit of enlightenment, which are essential for personal development and societal progress.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, embrace their inner strength, and respect the teachings of their forebears, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
