शंसावाद्वार्यो प्रतिमे गृणीहीन्द्राय वाहः कृणवावा जुष्टम् |एदं बर्हिर यजमानस्य सिदाथा च भूद उक्थम् इन्द्राय शस्तम् ||
śaṁsāvādhvaryo prati me gṛṇīhīndrāya vāhaḥ kṛṇavāva juṣṭam | edam barhir yajamānasya sīdāthā ca bhūd uktham indrāya śastam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • śaṁsāvādhvaryo: A call to the priest (adhvaryu) to perform the ritual.
    • prati me: For me, or in my favor.
    • gṛṇīhī: Sing or chant.
    • indrāya: To Indra, the king of gods.
    • vāhaḥ: The one who carries or brings.
    • kṛṇavāva: May we create or establish.
    • juṣṭam: That which is pleasing or desirable.
    • edam: This.
    • barhir: The sacred grass or altar.
    • yajamānasya: Of the one who is performing the sacrifice (yajamana).
    • sīdāthā: May it sit or be established.
    • ca: And.
    • bhūd: May it be or exist.
    • uktham: The hymn or utterance.
    • indrāya: To Indra.
    • śastam: The best or most excellent.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, rain, and the power of the storm.
    • The invocation of the priest (adhvaryu) signifies the importance of ritual and the role of the priest in connecting the devotee with the divine.
    • The phrase "may we create or establish that which is pleasing" emphasizes the desire for harmony and prosperity in life, reflecting a common human aspiration.
    • The mention of "sacred grass" (barhir) indicates the physical elements used in rituals, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of sacrifice (yajna), which is not just a ritual but a way of life that promotes community, sharing, and gratitude.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to celebrate and share resources, akin to a communal feast or festival.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek harmony in their relationships, fostering an environment where everyone feels valued and appreciated.
    • In environmental contexts, the call for establishing what is pleasing can translate into efforts for sustainability and ecological balance, reflecting a respect for nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rituals in fostering a connection with the divine and the community.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of intention in our actions, encouraging us to create a life that is harmonious and fulfilling.
    • In essence, it teaches us that our prayers and actions should aim for the greater good, aligning our personal aspirations with the welfare of the community and the environment.
