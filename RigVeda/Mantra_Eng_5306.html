त्वं धृष्णो धृतात वीतहव्यं प्रावो विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः सुदासम् | प्र पौरुकुत्सिं त्रसदस्युम आवः क्षेत्रसातां वृत्रहत्येषु पूरुम ||
tvaṁ dhṛṣṇo dhṛṣatā vītahavyam prāvo viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ sudāsam | pra paurukutsiṁ trasadasyum āvaḥ kṣetrasātā vṛtrahatyeṣu pūrum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • dhṛṣṇo: of the brave
    • dhṛṣatā: of the strong
    • vītahavyam: who has been offered (to the gods)
    • prāvo: who is invoked
    • viśvābhir: with all (the powers)
    • ūtibhiḥ: with praises
    • sudāsam: the good servant
    • pra: towards
    • paurukutsiṁ: the one who is strong in the city
    • trasadasyum: the one who is swift
    • āvaḥ: may you come
    • kṣetrasātā: the one who protects the fields
    • vṛtrahatyeṣu: in the slaying of Vritra (the dragon)
    • pūrum: the abundant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a powerful deity, asking for strength and protection.
    • The phrase "tvaṁ dhṛṣṇo dhṛṣatā" emphasizes the qualities of bravery and strength, which are essential in overcoming challenges in life.
    • "Vītahavyam" suggests the importance of offerings and respect towards the divine, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of "sudāsam" indicates the desire for a benevolent force to assist in our endeavors.
    • By calling upon "trasadasyum," the mantra seeks swiftness and agility, qualities that are beneficial in both physical and mental pursuits.
    • "Kṣetrasātā" refers to the protection of one's resources and environment, which is crucial for prosperity.
    • The mention of "vṛtrahatyeṣu" symbolizes the triumph over obstacles and adversities, akin to slaying a dragon that represents fears or challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, invoking the qualities of bravery and strength can help individuals face challenges and assert themselves effectively.
    • During times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities often come together to offer prayers and seek divine intervention for protection and recovery.
    • In sports, athletes often channel their inner strength and agility to overcome opponents, much like the qualities invoked in this mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on their fears and challenges, using the metaphor of slaying a dragon to symbolize overcoming personal obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of inner strength, bravery, and the need for divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to communal efforts in times of need.
    • By invoking these qualities, individuals can cultivate resilience and a proactive approach to life's adversities, ultimately leading to personal growth and success.
