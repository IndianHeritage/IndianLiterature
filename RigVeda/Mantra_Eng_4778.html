ब्रह्माणं ब्रह्मवाहसं गीरभिः सखायं ऋग्मियम् |गां न दोहसे हुवे ||
brahmāṇam brahmavāhasaṁ gīrbhiḥ sakhāyam ṛgmiyam | gāṁ na dohase huve ||
  • Brahmāṇam: This term refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit, often associated with the concept of Brahman in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the source of all creation and the essence of the universe.
  • Brahmavāhasaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "one who resides in Brahman" or "the one who embodies the divine." It emphasizes the connection between the individual and the universal consciousness.
  • Gīrbhiḥ: This translates to "through hymns" or "by means of words." It highlights the importance of expression, communication, and the power of sound in spiritual practices.
  • Sakhāyam: Meaning "friend" or "companion," this term suggests the idea of unity and companionship in the pursuit of spiritual knowledge and understanding.
  • Ṛgmiyam: This refers to the "sacred verses" or "mantras" that are recited in Vedic rituals. It underscores the significance of sacred texts in connecting with the divine.
  • Gāṁ: This word means "earth" or "the material world." It represents the physical realm and our existence within it.
  • Na dohase: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be separated" or "may we not be divided." It conveys a desire for unity and harmony.
  • Huve: This term means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking connection with the divine or the higher consciousness.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the individual with the universal spirit (Brahman) and the importance of communication through sacred hymns. It calls for unity and harmony in our relationships and interactions with the world around us. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as:


  • In personal relationships, fostering a sense of companionship and understanding among friends and family.
  • In community building, promoting unity and collaboration among diverse groups.
  • In spiritual practices, using sacred texts and hymns to connect with the divine and enhance one's spiritual journey.

The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony and connection, reminding us that our individual existence is part of a larger cosmic reality. By embracing this understanding, we can cultivate a more compassionate and unified world.
