ये युध्यन्ते प्रधानेषु शूरासो ये तनूत्यजः |ये वा सहस्रदक्षिणास तांष्चिद एवापि गच्छतात् ||
ye yudhyante pradhaneṣu śūrāso ye tanūtyajaḥ | ye vā sahasradakṣiṇās tām̐ś cid evāpi gacchatāt ||
  • Translation: "Those who fight against the powerful, those who abandon their bodies, and those who are a thousand times more capable, may they attain that."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • yudhyante: fight or struggle
    • pradhaneṣu: against the powerful or prominent
    • śūrāso: the brave or courageous
    • tanūtyajaḥ: those who abandon their bodies (sacrifice themselves)
    • vā: or
    • sahasradakṣiṇās: those who are a thousand times more capable
    • tām̐ś: that
    • cid: indeed
    • evāpi: even
    • gacchatāt: may they attain
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the valor and courage of individuals who stand up against powerful adversaries.
    • It acknowledges the sacrifices made by those who are willing to give up their lives for a greater cause.
    • The mention of "a thousand times more capable" suggests that true strength lies not just in physical prowess but also in moral and ethical courage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Activism: Many activists fight against oppressive regimes or social injustices, often at great personal risk. For instance, Malala Yousafzai stood up for girls' education in Pakistan, facing life-threatening challenges.
    • Military Sacrifice: Soldiers who fight in wars often embody the spirit of this mantra, risking their lives for their country and its values.
    • Whistleblowers: Individuals who expose corruption or wrongdoing in organizations often face severe repercussions, yet they choose to act for the greater good, exemplifying the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of courage and sacrifice in the face of adversity.
    • It encourages individuals to stand up for what is right, even when the odds are against them.
    • In a world where power dynamics often overshadow justice, this mantra inspires us to be brave and to act with integrity, reinforcing the idea that true strength is found in moral courage.
