कृधि रत्नं यजमानाय सुक्रतो त्वं हि रत्नधा असि |आ न ऋते शिशीहि विश्वं ऋत्विजं सुशंसो यश च दक्षते ||
kṛdhi ratnaṁ yajamānāya sukrato tvaṁ hi ratnadhā asi | ā na ṛte śiśīhi viśvam ṛtvijaṁ suśaṁso yaś ca dakṣate ||
  • kṛdhi ratnaṁ: This phrase translates to "create or produce wealth" or "bring forth the jewel." In a real-life context, this can be understood as the pursuit of prosperity and success in various endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal growth.
  • yajamānāya: This means "for the worshipper" or "for the one who is performing the sacrifice." It signifies the importance of intention and dedication in any action. For example, when someone works hard for a cause they believe in, they are acting as a yajamāna, focusing their efforts towards a greater good.
  • sukrato: This word means "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." It emphasizes the significance of ethical behavior and moral integrity. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness, community service, or simply making choices that benefit others.
  • tvaṁ hi ratnadhā asi: This translates to "you are indeed the holder of jewels." It acknowledges the divine or higher power that bestows blessings and wealth upon those who are deserving. In practical terms, this can be related to the belief that hard work and righteousness attract positive outcomes and opportunities.
  • ā na ṛte: This means "without you" or "in your absence." It highlights the idea that nothing can be accomplished without the support or presence of the divine or a guiding force. For instance, many people express gratitude to a higher power for their achievements, recognizing that their success is not solely their own doing.
  • śiśīhi: This word can be interpreted as "protect" or "nurture." It suggests the need for safeguarding one's achievements and values. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals protect their families, communities, and personal values from negative influences.
  • viśvam ṛtvijaṁ: This translates to "all the priests" or "all the sacred ones." It signifies the collective effort of those who contribute to the spiritual and moral fabric of society. In a modern context, this can refer to community leaders, educators, and mentors who guide and inspire others.
  • suśaṁso: This means "well-spoken" or "praiseworthy." It emphasizes the importance of reputation and the impact of words. In everyday life, this can be related to how individuals are perceived based on their actions and the way they communicate with others.
  • yaś ca dakṣate: This translates to "and who is capable." It highlights the value of skill and competence. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals strive to develop their talents and abilities to achieve their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity through virtuous actions, recognizing the divine support in our endeavors, and the importance of community and reputation. In real life, it serves as a reminder that success is not just about personal achievement but also about ethical conduct, community involvement, and the acknowledgment of a higher power guiding our paths. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in business ethics, personal development, and community service, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and purpose while seeking prosperity and fulfillment.
