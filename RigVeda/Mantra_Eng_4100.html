आ यं नरः सुदानवो ददाशुषे दिवः कोशम् अचुच्यवुः |विपर्जन्यं सृजन्ति रोदसी अनु धन्वना यान्ति वृष्टयः ||
ā yaṁ naraḥ sudānavo dadāśuṣe divaḥ kośam acucyavuḥ | vi parjanyaṁ sṛjanti rodasī anu dhanvanā yanti vṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yaṁ: that which
    • naraḥ: man or human
    • sudānavo: who is endowed with good qualities
    • dadāśuṣe: for the one who is generous
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • kośam: treasure or wealth
    • acucyavuḥ: they have not lost
    • vi parjanyaṁ: the rain or the clouds
    • sṛjanti: they create or produce
    • rodasī: the two realms (heaven and earth)
    • anu: following
    • dhanvanā: with wealth or riches
    • yanti: they go or proceed
    • vṛṣṭayaḥ: the rains or showers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of a good human being who is generous and possesses good qualities.
    • It emphasizes that such individuals are blessed with wealth and prosperity, which they do not lose.
    • The connection to rain signifies abundance and nourishment, suggesting that those who are virtuous and generous will attract blessings from the heavens.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A person who donates to charity and helps those in need often finds that their life is filled with abundance, whether in terms of financial wealth or emotional fulfillment.
    • Communities that come together to support each other during difficult times often see a resurgence of prosperity and well-being, akin to the rains that nourish the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of generosity and good character in attracting prosperity and blessings.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder that our actions and qualities can lead to abundance, not just for ourselves but for our communities as well.
    • By embodying the virtues described in this mantra, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others, fostering a cycle of generosity and abundance.
