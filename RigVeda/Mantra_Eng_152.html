युवं दक्षं धृतव्रत मित्रावरुण दूलभम् |ऋतुनां यज्ञं आशाथे ||
yuvaṁ dakṣaṁ dhṛtavrata mitrāvaruṇa dūḻabham | ṛtunā yajñam āśāthe ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development. In real life, youth is often associated with innovation and new ideas, as seen in young entrepreneurs who bring fresh perspectives to the business world.
  • dakṣaṁ: Meaning "skilled" or "capable," this term emphasizes the importance of competence and expertise. For example, a skilled craftsman or a talented musician showcases how mastery in a field can lead to success and recognition.
  • dhṛtavrata: This word means "one who is steadfast in vows" or "determined." It highlights the significance of commitment and perseverance. In real life, individuals who set goals and remain dedicated to achieving them, such as athletes training for competitions, exemplify this quality.
  • mitrāvaruṇa: This term refers to the duality of friendship and cosmic order, represented by the deities Mitra and Varuna. It signifies the importance of harmony and balance in relationships and the universe. For instance, in teamwork, collaboration and mutual respect among members lead to successful outcomes.
  • dūḻabham: This word means "rare" or "precious." It suggests that certain qualities or experiences are unique and valuable. In life, moments of genuine connection or extraordinary achievements can be seen as dūḻabham, reminding us to cherish them.
  • ṛtunā: This translates to "by the seasons" or "in accordance with the cycles of nature." It emphasizes the importance of timing and natural rhythms. For example, farmers who plant crops according to seasonal changes demonstrate the significance of aligning actions with natural cycles.
  • yajñam: Meaning "sacrifice" or "offering," this term highlights the concept of giving for a greater cause. In modern life, charitable acts and community service reflect the spirit of yajñam, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • āśāthe: This word can be interpreted as "to be fulfilled" or "to achieve." It signifies the culmination of efforts and the realization of goals. In personal development, achieving a long-term aspiration, such as completing a degree or starting a business, embodies āśāthe.

Conclusion: The mantra "yuvaṁ dakṣaṁ dhṛtavrata mitrāvaruṇa dūḻabham | ṛtunā yajñam āśāthe ||" encapsulates essential qualities for success and fulfillment in life. It emphasizes the importance of youthfulness, skill, determination, harmonious relationships, and the value of rare experiences. By aligning our actions with natural cycles and contributing to the greater good, we can achieve our goals and lead a meaningful life. This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these qualities in our daily lives, fostering personal growth and positive relationships within our communities.
