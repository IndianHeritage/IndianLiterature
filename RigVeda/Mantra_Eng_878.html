इथ्था हि सोम इन मडे ब्रह्मा चकार वर्धनम् | शविष्ट वज्रिन्न ओजसा पृथिव्या निः शशा अहिम अर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
itthā hi soma in made brahmā cakāra vardhanam | śaviṣṭha vajrinn ojasā pṛthivyā niḥ śaśā ahim arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • itthā hi soma in made brahmā cakāra vardhanam: This phrase can be translated to "Thus, the Soma, in its intoxication, created the great expansion." Here, 'Soma' refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The term 'brahmā' signifies the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, while 'vardhanam' means growth or expansion. This suggests that through the divine essence of Soma, a profound expansion of consciousness or existence occurs.
  • śaviṣṭha vajrinn ojasā: This translates to "with the might of the strongest, the powerful." 'Śaviṣṭha' refers to the best or the strongest, and 'vajrinn' relates to the concept of thunderbolt or strength. 'Ojasā' signifies energy or vitality. This part emphasizes the importance of harnessing strength and energy in our endeavors, suggesting that true power comes from a deep connection to the divine.
  • pṛthivyā niḥ śaśā ahim arcann anu svarājyam: This can be interpreted as "the earth, having been worshipped, brings forth the serpent (or the divine) to the realm of sovereignty." 'Pṛthivyā' refers to the earth, 'niḥ' means 'from', 'śaśā' can mean 'serpent' or 'divine energy', and 'svarājyam' refers to sovereignty or rule. This indicates that by honoring the earth and its resources, one can attain a higher state of existence or authority.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, strength, and the relationship between humanity and the earth. It highlights the importance of divine inspiration (Soma) in fostering growth and expansion in life. The invocation of strength and energy suggests that individuals should harness their inner power to achieve their goals.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and environmental stewardship. For instance, individuals can draw inspiration from the concept of Soma to cultivate creativity and innovation in their work. Leaders can embody the strength and vitality described in the mantra to inspire their teams. Additionally, by respecting and nurturing the earth, we can create a sustainable future, aligning with the idea of sovereignty over our environment.
