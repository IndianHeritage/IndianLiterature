उरुं नृभ्य उरुं गव उरुं रथाय पन्थाम् |देववीतिम मनामहे ||
uruṁ nṛbhya uruṁ gava uruṁ rathāya panthām | devavītim manāmahe ||
  • uruṁ: This word translates to "broad" or "vast." It signifies expansiveness and abundance.
  • nṛbhya: This means "for men" or "to humans." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • gava: This refers to "cows," which are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
  • rathāya: This translates to "for the chariot." Chariots symbolize movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • panthām: This means "path" or "way." It represents the journey one takes in life, both physically and spiritually.
  • devavītim: This term refers to "the divine protection" or "the favor of the gods." It highlights the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers.
  • manāmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate." It signifies the act of reflection and devotion.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for guidance and blessings on the vast paths of life. It calls upon the divine to provide support and protection as one navigates through various aspects of existence, whether it be in personal endeavors, relationships, or spiritual journeys.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: When embarking on a new venture, such as starting a business or pursuing education, one can recite this mantra to seek guidance and blessings for success.
  • Community and Relationships: In fostering relationships, whether in family or community, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek harmony and support from one another.
  • Spiritual Practice: During meditation or prayer, this mantra can be used to invoke a sense of connection with the divine and to reflect on one's life path.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support as we navigate the vast and complex paths of life. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys and that the blessings of the divine can lead us toward fulfillment and abundance.
