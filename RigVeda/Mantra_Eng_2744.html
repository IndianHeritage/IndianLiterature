यजाम इन् नमसां वृद्धम् इन्द्रं बृहन्तम् ऋष्वम् अजरो युवानम् |यस्य प्रियं ममतुर यज्ञियस्य न रोदसी महिमानं ममाते ||
yajāma in namasā vṛddham indram bṛhantam ṛṣvam ajaraṁ yuvānam | yasya priye mamatur yajñiyasya na rodasī mahimānam mamāte ||
  • yajāma: This word translates to "we worship" or "we offer." It signifies the act of devotion and reverence towards a higher power.
  • in: A preposition that can mean "in" or "to," indicating direction or focus in worship.
  • namasā: This means "with reverence" or "with bowing." It emphasizes the humility and respect with which one approaches the divine.
  • vṛddham: Translated as "the elder" or "the wise," it refers to a being of great knowledge and experience, often associated with divinity.
  • indram: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • bṛhantam: Meaning "the great" or "the vast," it highlights the immense nature of the divine being being worshipped.
  • ṛṣvam: This word means "the strong" or "the powerful," indicating the might and capability of the divine.
  • ajaraṁ: Translated as "immortal" or "eternal," it signifies the timeless nature of the divine, free from decay and death.
  • yuvānam: Meaning "the youthful," it represents vitality and energy, suggesting that the divine is both ancient and ever-renewing.
  • yasya: This means "whose," indicating possession or connection to the divine being.
  • priye: Translated as "beloved," it signifies the affection and bond between the worshipper and the divine.
  • mamatur: This means "may it be for me," expressing a personal plea or desire for the divine's blessings.
  • yajñiyasya: Referring to "the one who is to be worshipped," it emphasizes the importance of the divine in rituals and offerings.
  • na rodasī: This phrase means "not in the two worlds," suggesting a transcendence beyond the physical and spiritual realms.
  • mahimānam: Translated as "greatness" or "glory," it refers to the magnificence of the divine being.
  • mamāte: This means "to me," indicating a personal connection and the desire for the divine's presence in one's life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, reverence, and the quest for divine connection. It highlights the attributes of the divine, such as wisdom, strength, immortality, and youthfulness, which serve as ideals for individuals to aspire to in their own lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of prayer, meditation, or rituals where individuals seek guidance, strength, and blessings from a higher power. It reminds us of the importance of humility and respect in our spiritual practices, encouraging us to cultivate a personal relationship with the divine while recognizing its vastness and glory. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the worshipper and the divine, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose in the spiritual journey.
