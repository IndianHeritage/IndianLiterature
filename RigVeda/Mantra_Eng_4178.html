मन्त्र: क्व वो 'श्वाः क्वाभीशवः कथं शेक कथाय यया |पृष्ठे सदो नसो यमः ||
kva1 vo 'śvāḥ kvā3bhīśavaḥ kathaṁ śeka kathā yaya | pṛṣṭhe sado nasor yamaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kva: Where
    • vo: Your
    • śvāḥ: Dogs (often symbolizing loyalty and protection)
    • kvā: Where
    • bhīśavaḥ: Those who are fearful or those who are in fear
    • kathaṁ: How
    • śeka: To be able or to have the ability
    • kathā: Story or narrative
    • yaya: By which
    • pṛṣṭhe: On the back
    • sado: Of the house or dwelling
    • nasor: Of us
    • yamaḥ: The god of death or restraint
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the whereabouts of loyalty (symbolized by dogs) and those who are fearful.
    • It inquires how one can possess the ability to tell a story or narrative that leads to understanding or enlightenment.
    • It reflects on the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, particularly in the context of life and death.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the loyalty of friends and family can be likened to the 'dogs' mentioned in the mantra. Their presence provides protection and support during challenging times.
    • Fear is a common human experience. Understanding how to navigate fear can empower individuals to tell their own stories of resilience and growth.
    • The concept of 'yamaḥ' can be related to the inevitability of death, prompting individuals to reflect on their lives and the legacies they wish to leave behind.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encourages introspection about loyalty, fear, and the narratives we create in our lives.
    • It highlights the importance of understanding our relationships and the stories we tell ourselves and others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and coping with loss, reminding us to cherish our connections and the stories that shape our existence.
