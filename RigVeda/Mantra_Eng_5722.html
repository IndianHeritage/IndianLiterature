नू मे हवम् आ श्रिणुतं युवाना यासिष्टं वर्तिर अश्विनाव इरावत् | धत्तं रत्नानि जरतं च सूरīn यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū me havam ā śṛṇutaṁ yuvānā yāsiṣṭaṁ vartir aśvināv irāvat | dhattaṁ ratnāni jarataṁ ca sūrīn yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • nū me havam ā śṛṇutaṁ: This phrase translates to "O Ashvins, listen to my invocation." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. This line emphasizes the importance of calling upon divine beings for assistance and guidance.
  • yuvānā yāsiṣṭaṁ vartir aśvināv irāvat: Here, "yuvānā" means "young" or "youthful," and "yāsiṣṭaṁ" refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." The phrase suggests that the Ashvins are youthful and embody the best qualities. "Irāvat" can be interpreted as "the one who brings prosperity." This line highlights the qualities of vitality and excellence that one should aspire to in life.
  • dhattaṁ ratnāni jarataṁ ca sūrīn: "Dhattam" means "bestow," "ratnāni" refers to "jewels" or "treasures," and "sūrīn" means "heroes" or "valiant ones." This part of the mantra is a plea for the Ashvins to bestow treasures and virtues upon the worshipper, emphasizing the importance of seeking both material and spiritual wealth.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: "Yūyam" means "you" (referring to the Ashvins), "pāta" means "protect," and "svastibhiḥ" translates to "with auspiciousness." This line is a request for the Ashvins to protect the worshipper with their blessings and auspiciousness, indicating the desire for a safe and prosperous life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for health, prosperity, and protection. It reflects the human desire for connection with the divine and the hope for blessings in various aspects of life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • During times of illness or distress, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke healing and support from higher powers.
    • In moments of uncertainty or challenge, such as starting a new venture or facing personal struggles, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, this mantra can be used to invoke blessings for health, prosperity, and well-being for oneself and the community.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness between humans and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of seeking help, embodying virtues, and striving for a prosperous life. By invoking the Ashvins, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and resilience, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
