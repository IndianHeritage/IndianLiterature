इयम् अददाद रभसम ऋणच्युतं दिवोदासं वध्र्यश्वाय दाशुषे |या शश्वन्तम् आचखादावसम पाणिं ता ते दात्राणि तविषा सरस्वति ||
iyam adadād rabhasam ṛṇacyutaṁ divodāsaṁ vadhryaśvāya dāśuṣe | yā śaśvantam ācakhādāvasam paṇiṁ tā te dātrāṇi taviṣā sarasvati ||
  • iyam adadād rabhasam: This phrase can be interpreted as "this has been given with vigor." It signifies the act of offering or giving something with enthusiasm and energy. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or communities come together to support a cause, such as fundraising events where people contribute generously and passionately.
  • ṛṇacyutaṁ divodāsaṁ: This translates to "the one who is free from debt, O Divodasa." Here, "Divodasa" refers to a person or a deity who is free from obligations or debts. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of financial responsibility and the value of living within one's means, which is crucial in modern society to avoid the stress of debt.
  • vadhryaśvāya dāśuṣe: This can be understood as "to the one who is strong and powerful." It emphasizes the qualities of strength and capability. In everyday life, this can be reflected in leaders or mentors who inspire others through their strength of character and ability to overcome challenges, motivating those around them to strive for greatness.
  • yā śaśvantam ācakhādāvasam: This phrase means "who has revealed the one who is full of prosperity." It highlights the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom that leads to prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in educational settings where teachers impart knowledge to students, enabling them to achieve success and prosperity in their future endeavors.
  • paṇiṁ tā te dātrāṇi taviṣā sarasvati: This translates to "may the giver of wealth, Sarasvati, grant you abundance." Sarasvati is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. This part of the mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom as a pathway to abundance. In contemporary life, this can be applied in the pursuit of education and continuous learning, which often leads to better opportunities and financial stability.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of generosity, financial responsibility, strength, knowledge, and the pursuit of prosperity. Its significance lies in the encouragement to give with enthusiasm, live responsibly, seek wisdom, and strive for personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, fostering a culture of support, learning, and abundance. By embodying these values, one can contribute positively to society and achieve a fulfilling life.
