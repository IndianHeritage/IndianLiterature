आ रोडसी अपृणद आ स्वर महज जातं यद एनम अपसो अधारयन |सो अध्वराय परि नीयते कविर अत्यो न वाजसातये चनोहितः ||
ā rodasī apṛṇad ā svar mahaj jātaṁ yad enam apaso adhārayan | so adhvarāya pari ṇīyate kavir atyo na vājasātaye canohitaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rodasī: From the sky or the heavens
    • apṛṇad: Has provided or has given
    • ā svar: From the light or the luminous
    • mahaj: Great or vast
    • jātaṁ: Born or created
    • yad: That which
    • enam: This
    • apaso: Waters or the flow of life
    • adhārayan: Sustained or supported
    • so: He or that
    • adhvarāya: To the sacrifice or offering
    • pari ṇīyate: Is brought forth or is led
    • kavir: The wise or the seer
    • atyō: Beyond or transcending
    • na: Not
    • vājasātaye: For the purpose of strength or power
    • canohitaḥ: Who is not obstructed or hindered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine provision from the heavens and the light, emphasizing the vastness of creation.
    • It highlights the importance of water as a life-sustaining force, suggesting that all life is interconnected through this essential element.
    • The mantra also refers to the act of sacrifice, indicating that offerings are a means to connect with the divine and to receive blessings.
    • The mention of the wise seer suggests that knowledge and wisdom are crucial in understanding the universe and our place within it.
    • Finally, it emphasizes the idea of strength and the importance of being unobstructed in one's pursuits, whether in spiritual or material endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the reliance on water for crops can be seen as a direct application of the mantra's emphasis on the sustaining power of water.
    • In personal development, the idea of sacrifice can be related to the effort and dedication one puts into achieving goals, whether in education, career, or relationships.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and wisdom can be reflected in the importance of education and lifelong learning in modern society.
    • In community building, the concept of being unobstructed can relate to the importance of collaboration and support among individuals to achieve common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine provision, and the role of wisdom in navigating existence.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless message that resonates in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community development.
    • By understanding and applying the principles within this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the universe and enhance their journey through life.
