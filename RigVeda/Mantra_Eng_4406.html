आ सूर्यो न भानुमद्भिरर्कैर अग्ने ततान्थ रोदसी वि भासा |चित्रो नयत परि तमांस्यक्तः शोचिषा पत्मन्न औशिजो न दीयन् ||
ā sūryo na bhānumadbhir arkair agne tatantha rodasī vi bhāsā | citro nayat pari tamāṁsy aktaḥ śociṣā patmann auśijo na dīyan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • sūryo: the sun
    • na: not
    • bhānumadbhir: by those who shine
    • arkair: by the rays
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • tatantha: you have spread
    • rodasī: the two worlds
    • vi: in
    • bhāsā: light
    • citro: colorful
    • nayat: lead
    • pari: around
    • tamāṁsy: the darkness
    • aktaḥ: having been made
    • śociṣā: with brightness
    • patmann: the one who is the lord
    • auśijo: of the auspicious
    • na: not
    • dīyan: to be given
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, and emphasizes the importance of light and knowledge.
    • The phrase "ā sūryo na bhānumadbhir arkair" suggests that the light of the sun is not just physical but also represents knowledge and enlightenment.
    • "Agne tatantha rodasī vi bhāsā" indicates that Agni spreads light across the two worlds, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The mantra encourages the removal of darkness ("pari tamāṁsy aktaḥ") and the pursuit of brightness and clarity in life.
    • It highlights the role of Agni as a guide, leading us away from ignorance and towards wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and education. Just as Agni spreads light, individuals can seek to illuminate their minds through learning.
    • In moments of confusion or darkness, one can invoke the essence of this mantra by seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to how Agni leads us away from ignorance.
    • In a broader societal context, this mantra can inspire communities to work towards enlightenment and progress, fostering environments where knowledge and wisdom are valued.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It serves as a reminder that just as Agni dispels darkness, we too can strive to overcome ignorance in our lives.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and societies can work towards a brighter, more informed future.
