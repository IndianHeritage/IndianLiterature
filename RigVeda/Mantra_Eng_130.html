तां सुजिह्वा उप हवये होतारा दैव्याः कवि |यज्ञं नो यक्षताम इमम् ||
tā sujihvā upa hvaye hotārā daivyā kavī | yajñaṁ no yakṣatām imam ||
  • tā sujihvā: This phrase can be translated as "those who are well-versed" or "those who have good understanding." It refers to individuals who possess wisdom and knowledge.
  • upa hvaye: This means "I call upon" or "I invoke." It signifies a plea or request directed towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • hotārā: This term refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers sacrifices." In the context of Vedic rituals, the hotārā plays a crucial role in performing yajñas (sacrificial rituals) and invoking deities.
  • daivyā: This translates to "divine" or "heavenly." It indicates the connection to the divine realm and the spiritual aspect of the invocation.
  • kavī: This word means "poet" or "sage." It represents those who have the ability to articulate profound truths and insights, often through poetry or hymns.
  • yajñaṁ no yakṣatām imam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may this sacrifice be accepted by us." It expresses the hope that the offerings made in the yajña will be favorably received by the divine.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call to the wise and knowledgeable individuals to come together and invoke the divine through the performance of a yajña. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, divine connection, and the role of the priest in facilitating spiritual practices.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Gatherings: Just as the mantra calls for collective wisdom, community events can benefit from the participation of knowledgeable individuals who can guide discussions and decisions.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In modern spiritual practices, invoking the divine through rituals can still hold significance, reminding participants of the importance of intention and reverence.
  • Education and Learning: The emphasis on wisdom in the mantra highlights the value of education and the pursuit of knowledge in personal and communal growth.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine wisdom and the collective effort in spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder that knowledge, community, and reverence for the divine are essential components of a fulfilling life. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves, our communities, and the divine.
