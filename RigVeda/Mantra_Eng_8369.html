अव द्युतानः कलशाम् अचिक्रदन् नृभिर येमानः कोश आ हिरण्यये |अभिम ऋतस्य दोहनाऽअनूषताधि त्रिपृष्ट उषसो वि राजति ||
ava dyutānaḥ kalaśām̐ acikradan nṛbhir yemānaḥ kośa ā hiraṇyaye | abhīm ṛtasya dohanā anūṣatādhi tripṛṣṭha uṣaso vi rājati ||
  • ava dyutānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the shining ones" or "those who bring light." In a real-life context, this can refer to individuals or forces that illuminate our path, such as teachers, mentors, or guiding principles.
  • kalaśām̐ acikradan: "kalaśām̐" refers to a pot or vessel, often symbolizing abundance or nourishment. "acikradan" can mean "who has filled." Together, this suggests the idea of being filled with abundance or knowledge. For example, a person who seeks education and wisdom fills their vessel of knowledge.
  • nṛbhir yemānaḥ: "nṛbhir" means "by men" or "by humanity," and "yemānaḥ" can be interpreted as "those who are striving." This emphasizes the collective effort of humanity in pursuit of goals, such as community service or collaborative projects that aim to improve society.
  • kośa ā hiraṇyaye: "kośa" refers to a sheath or layer, while "hiraṇyaye" means "of gold" or "golden." This can symbolize the layers of richness in life, such as experiences, relationships, and achievements that contribute to a person's wealth of life.
  • abhīm ṛtasya dohanā: "abhīm" can mean "fearless" or "brave," while "ṛtasya" refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." "dohanā" means "milking" or "extracting." This suggests the idea of bravely seeking and extracting truth from the universe, akin to scientists or philosophers who pursue knowledge despite challenges.
  • anūṣatādhi: This term can be interpreted as "beyond" or "transcending." It implies a journey towards higher understanding or enlightenment, similar to personal growth journeys where individuals strive to overcome their limitations.
  • tripṛṣṭha uṣaso vi rājati: "tripṛṣṭha" means "threefold" or "triple," and "uṣaso" refers to "dawn" or "morning." "vi rājati" means "shines" or "rules." This can symbolize the dawn of new beginnings, where the threefold aspect could represent mind, body, and spirit, shining together in harmony as one embarks on a new day or phase in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, truth, and abundance in life. It emphasizes the importance of collective human effort, the pursuit of enlightenment, and the richness of experiences that contribute to personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for education, collaborate with others for a common cause, and embrace new beginnings with courage and wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all part of a larger cosmic order, and our actions can illuminate the path for ourselves and others.
