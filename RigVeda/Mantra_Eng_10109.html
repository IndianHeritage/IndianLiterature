षट्त्रिंशांश च चतुरः कल्पयन्तश्चन्दांसी च दधता अद्वादशम् |यज्ञं विमाय कवयो मनीषा ऋक्सामाभ्यां प्र रथं वर्तयन्ति ||
ṣaṭtriṁśām̐ś ca caturaḥ kalpayantaś chandāṁsi ca dadhata ādvādaśam | yajñaṁ vimāya kavayo manīṣa ṛksāmābhyām pra rathaṁ vartayanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṣaṭtriṁśām̐ś: thirty-six
    • ca: and
    • caturaḥ: four
    • kalpayantaś: those who create or arrange
    • chandāṁsi: the meters or rhythms
    • ca: and
    • dadhata: who hold or maintain
    • ādvādaśam: twelve
    • yajñaṁ: the sacrifice or offering
    • vimāya: with wisdom or insight
    • kavayo: the poets or seers
    • manīṣa: with thought or intelligence
    • ṛksāmābhyām: with the two verses
    • pra: forward
    • rathaṁ: the chariot
    • vartayanti: they drive or guide
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and arrangement of various elements in a structured manner.
    • The "thirty-six" and "four" likely refer to specific aspects of Vedic knowledge or rituals, emphasizing the importance of organization in spiritual practices.
    • The "twelve" could symbolize completeness or a cycle, often seen in various cultural contexts, such as the twelve months of the year.
    • The mention of "yajña" signifies the importance of sacrifice and offerings in Vedic traditions, which can be related to selfless actions in daily life.
    • The "poets" or "seers" represent those who possess wisdom and insight, guiding others through their knowledge.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, where the guidance of wise individuals helps navigate through challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the "thirty-six" and "four" can represent the various departments and teams working together to achieve a common goal, emphasizing teamwork and collaboration.
    • In personal development, the "yajña" can be seen as the sacrifices one makes for self-improvement, such as time spent learning or practicing a skill.
    • Mentorship can be likened to the "poets" guiding the "chariot" of one's career, where experienced individuals help navigate through professional challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of structure, wisdom, and sacrifice in both spiritual and everyday life.
    • It teaches us that organized efforts, guided by knowledgeable individuals, can lead to successful outcomes.
    • In essence, it encourages us to embrace collaboration, wisdom, and selflessness in our pursuits, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or community service.
