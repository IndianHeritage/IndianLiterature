शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek help or guidance from a higher power or authority when facing challenges.
  • maghavānam indram: This translates to "Indra, the mighty one." Indra is a significant deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. In contemporary terms, this can symbolize the need for strength and resilience in overcoming obstacles, much like how a leader inspires their team during tough times.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This means "in this burden, we seek the best." "Bhare" refers to a burden or challenge, while "nṛtamaṁ" implies seeking the best or the most excellent. This can relate to situations in life where one must navigate difficulties, such as in a career or personal life, and strive to find the best solutions or outcomes.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: This translates to "listening to the fierce for the purpose of upliftment." "Śṛṇvantam" means listening, and "ūgr" refers to something fierce or intense. This can be applied in real life when one must pay attention to strong advice or warnings, such as in a mentor-mentee relationship where the mentor provides tough love to help the mentee grow.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This means "in the midst of the strong, we conquer the obstacles." "Samatsu" refers to being among the strong or powerful, and "vṛtrāṇi" signifies obstacles or challenges. This can be seen in competitive environments, such as sports or business, where being among strong competitors pushes individuals to excel and overcome their limitations.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This translates to "gathering wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means to gather or accumulate, and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources. In a practical sense, this can relate to financial planning and investment strategies where individuals or organizations work to accumulate resources for future stability and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking strength and guidance in the face of challenges. It highlights the need to listen to wise counsel, strive for excellence, and work collaboratively with others to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional growth, and community building. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate their burdens more effectively and achieve their goals with resilience and determination.
