ऋतधीतय आगत सत्यधर्माणो अध्वरम् |अग्निः पिबत जिव्हया ||
ṛtadhītaya ā gata satyadharmāṇo adhvaram | agneḥ pibata jihvayā ||
  • ṛtadhītaya: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ṛta," which signifies cosmic order or truth, and "dhītaya," meaning understanding or knowledge. Together, it refers to the understanding of the cosmic order or the pursuit of truth.
  • ā gata: This phrase translates to "having arrived" or "having come forth." It indicates a state of realization or attainment.
  • satyadharmāṇo: "Satya" means truth, and "dharma" refers to righteousness or duty. Thus, "satyadharmāṇo" signifies those who are committed to truth and righteousness.
  • adhvaram: This term refers to a ritual or sacrifice. In the Vedic context, it often denotes the sacred acts performed to honor the divine.
  • agneḥ: This word means "of Agni," the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • pibata: This translates to "drink" or "consume." In this context, it suggests the act of receiving or partaking in something sacred.
  • jihvayā: This term means "with the tongue." It emphasizes the importance of speech and expression in the act of worship or ritual.

In summary, the mantra can be interpreted as a call to understand and embrace the cosmic order and truth, to engage in righteous actions, and to partake in the divine through rituals, particularly invoking Agni. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, truth, and the sacredness of speech in our spiritual practices.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to align themselves with truth and righteousness while engaging in spiritual practices. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by promoting honesty in personal and professional relationships, participating in community service, and engaging in rituals that foster a connection with the divine. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society that values truth and righteousness, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
