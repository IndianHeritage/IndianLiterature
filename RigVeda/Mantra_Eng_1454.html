ओजिष्ट त्रातार अविता रथं कं चिद अमर्त्य |अन्यं अस्मद रिरिषेः कं चिद अद्रिवो रिरिक्षन्तं चिद अद्रिवः ||
ojiṣṭha trātar avitā rathaṁ kaṁ cid amartya | anyam asmad ririṣeḥ kaṁ cid adrivo ririkṣantaṁ cid adrivaḥ ||
  • ojiṣṭha: This word can be translated to "the most powerful" or "the strongest." It signifies the highest form of strength or capability.
  • trātar: This means "to protect" or "to save." It implies a sense of guardianship or safeguarding.
  • avitā: This translates to "not abandoned" or "not forsaken." It suggests a sense of continuity and support.
  • rathaṁ: This word means "chariot." In the Vedic context, a chariot symbolizes the journey of life, the vehicle that carries one through various experiences.
  • kaṁ cid: This phrase means "anyone" or "anyone at all," indicating inclusivity.
  • amartya: This translates to "immortal" or "divine." It refers to the eternal aspect of existence.
  • anyam: This means "another" or "other," suggesting a comparison or contrast.
  • asmad: This translates to "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity.
  • ririṣeḥ: This means "desire" or "wish." It reflects the aspirations or longings of individuals.
  • adrivo: This translates to "the one who is not a river," which can symbolize stability or permanence.
  • rikṣantaṁ: This means "to seek" or "to strive for." It indicates an active pursuit of goals or desires.
  • cid: This word means "indeed" or "certainly," adding emphasis to the preceding words.
  • adrivaḥ: This translates to "those who are not rivers," suggesting a group that is stable and enduring.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for strength and protection in the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of being supported and not abandoned, while also recognizing the desires and aspirations of individuals. The imagery of the chariot represents the path we take, and the idea of immortality suggests that our actions and intentions have lasting significance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can seek strength and protection in their endeavors, ensuring they are not forsaken in their pursuits.
  • In leadership, the mantra can serve as a reminder for leaders to support their teams and protect their interests, fostering a sense of unity and strength.
  • In community building, it highlights the importance of collective identity and the need to support one another in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, protection, and support in the journey of life. It encourages individuals to strive for their desires while ensuring they are not abandoned in their pursuits. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building, emphasizing the importance of unity and resilience.
