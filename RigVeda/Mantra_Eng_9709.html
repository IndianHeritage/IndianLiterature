आ नः प्रजां जनयतु प्रजापतिर आजरसाय सम अनक्त्व अर्यमाः |अदुर्मङ्गलीः पतिलोकम् आ विश शं नो भव द्विपादे शं चतुष्पादे ||
ā naḥ prajāṁ janayatu prajāpatir ājarasāya sam anaktv aryamā | adurmaṅgalīḥ patilokam ā viśa śaṁ no bhava dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||
  • Mantra: "ā naḥ prajāṁ janayatu prajāpatir ājarasāya sam anaktv aryamā | adurmaṅgalīḥ patilokam ā viśa śaṁ no bhava dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: May he (the deity) for us
    • prajāṁ: progeny or offspring
    • janayatu: may he generate or create
    • prajāpatir: the lord of creatures
    • ājarasāya: who is ageless or eternal
    • sam anaktv: may he grant us prosperity
    • aryamā: the noble one
    • adurmaṅgalīḥ: may he remove all inauspiciousness
    • patilokam: the world of the husband or protector
    • ā viśa: may he enter or come to us
    • śaṁ: peace or well-being
    • no: for us
    • bhava: may you become
    • dvipade: among the bipeds (humans)
    • śaṁ: peace or well-being
    • catuṣpade: among the quadrupeds (animals)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the generation of healthy and prosperous offspring. It invokes the deity known as Prajāpati, the lord of creatures, who is eternal and ageless.
    • The phrase "may he generate for us progeny" emphasizes the importance of family and continuity in life. In real life, this can be seen in the way families celebrate the birth of children, viewing them as blessings and the continuation of lineage.
    • The invocation of "may he grant us prosperity" reflects a desire for abundance and success in life. For example, in modern society, people often seek blessings for their careers and financial stability, paralleling the ancient wish for prosperity.
    • The mention of "removing all inauspiciousness" highlights the human desire to overcome obstacles and challenges. In practical terms, this can relate to rituals and practices that people engage in to ward off negativity, such as housewarming ceremonies or protective amulets.
    • The concluding lines about peace among bipeds and quadrupeds signify a harmonious existence not just for humans but for all living beings. This can be related to contemporary movements advocating for animal rights and environmental conservation, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, family, prosperity, and harmony with nature. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, reminding us of our responsibilities towards our families, communities, and the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to seek balance, prosperity, and peace.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that our actions and intentions can create a positive impact not only on ourselves but also on the world around us.
