त्वां यज्ञेषु ऋत्विजं चारुम अग्ने नि षेदिरे |घृतप्रतīkं मनुषो वि वो मदे शुक्रं चेतिष्ठम अक्षभिर विवक्षसे ||
tvāṁ yajñeṣv ṛtvijaṁ cārum agne ni ṣedire | ghṛtapratīkam manuṣo vi vo made śukraṁ cetiṣṭham akṣabhir vivakṣase ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
  • yajñeṣv - This means "in the sacrifices," indicating the context of rituals and offerings made to deities.
  • ṛtvijaṁ - This refers to "the priests" or "ritual officiants," who perform the sacred rites and ensure that the rituals are conducted properly.
  • cārum - This translates to "beautiful" or "pleasant," describing the nature of Agni and the offerings made to him.
  • agne - This is the name of the fire god, Agni, who is a symbol of purity and the divine messenger between humans and gods.
  • ni ṣedire - This means "have settled" or "have taken their place," indicating the presence of Agni in the rituals.
  • ghṛtapratīkam - This translates to "the essence of ghee," which is a sacred offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
  • manuṣo - This means "of humans," emphasizing the connection between humans and the divine through rituals.
  • vi vo - This phrase can be interpreted as "for you all," indicating a collective offering or prayer.
  • made - This translates to "in joy" or "in ecstasy," reflecting the emotional state of the participants in the ritual.
  • śukraṁ - This means "pure" or "bright," referring to the clarity and purity of the offerings and intentions.
  • cetiṣṭham - This translates to "you are invoked" or "you are called upon," indicating the act of inviting Agni's presence.
  • akṣabhir - This means "with the unbroken," symbolizing the continuity and permanence of the rituals.
  • vivakṣase - This translates to "you express" or "you reveal," indicating the communication between the divine and the human.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni in Vedic rituals, highlighting the role of priests and the offerings made to invoke divine presence. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where fire is used in ceremonies, such as weddings or religious festivals, symbolizing purification and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention, purity, and community in our rituals and daily lives, encouraging us to seek divine guidance and express our gratitude through meaningful actions.
