यथा चिन् मन्यसे हृदा तद इन मे जग्मुर आशसः |ये ते नेदिष्टं हवनान्य आगमन तान वर्ध भीमसंदृशः ||
yathā cin manyase hṛdā tad in me jagmur āśasaḥ | ye te nediṣṭhaṁ havanāny āgaman tān vardha bhīmasaṁdṛśaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as, in the way
    • cin: whatever
    • manyase: you think, you believe
    • hṛdā: with the heart
    • tad: that
    • in: in
    • me: me, my
    • jagmur: they have come
    • āśasaḥ: of blessings, of prosperity
    • ye: those who
    • te: you
    • nediṣṭham: the best, the most desirable
    • havanāni: offerings, sacrifices
    • āgaman: have come, have arrived
    • tān: those
    • vardha: may you increase, may you grow
    • bhīmasaṁdṛśaḥ: of terrifying appearance, formidable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between thought, intention, and the manifestation of blessings.
    • It suggests that whatever one thinks with sincerity and from the heart can lead to the arrival of prosperity and blessings.
    • The phrase "those who have come" indicates that the blessings or positive outcomes are already on their way, contingent upon the right mindset and intentions.
    • The call to "may you increase" emphasizes the importance of nurturing these blessings and intentions, especially when they appear formidable or challenging.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, if an individual believes in their capabilities and visualizes success, they are more likely to take actions that lead to career advancement.
    • In personal relationships, when one approaches interactions with love and positivity, it often results in stronger bonds and mutual respect.
    • In health and wellness, maintaining a positive mindset can significantly impact one's physical and mental well-being, leading to better health outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the power of intention and belief in shaping our reality.
    • It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and feelings can attract positive outcomes, and we should be mindful of what we cultivate in our hearts.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to focus on positive thinking and to actively nurture their aspirations, especially when faced with challenges.
