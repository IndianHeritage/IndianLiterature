यां त्वा दिवो दुहितर वर्धयन्त्य उषः सुजाते मतीभिर वसिष्ठाः |सास्मासु धा रयिम ऋष्वं बृहन्तं यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
yāṁ tvā divo duhitar vardhayanty uṣaḥ sujāte matibhir vasiṣṭhāḥ | sāsmāsu dhā rayim ṛṣvam bṛhantaṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yāṁ tvā divo duhitar vardhayanty uṣaḥ sujāte matibhir vasiṣṭhāḥ | sāsmāsu dhā rayim ṛṣvam bṛhantaṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Daughter of the Sky, whom the auspicious dawns nourish, endowed with wisdom by the seers, may you bring us wealth that is vast and abundant; may you protect us always with your blessings."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yāṁ: "whom" - Referring to the divine feminine energy or the dawn.
    • tvā: "you" - Addressing the divine presence.
    • divo: "of the sky" - Indicating a celestial or divine origin.
    • duhitar: "daughter" - Symbolizing nurturing and care.
    • vardhayanty: "nourish" - The act of providing sustenance and growth.
    • uṣaḥ: "dawn" - Represents new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • su-jāte: "well-born" - Signifying purity and auspiciousness.
    • matibhir: "with wisdom" - The knowledge and insight provided by sages.
    • vasiṣṭhāḥ: "the seers" - Referring to wise sages or rishis.
    • sāsmāsu: "from this" - Indicating a source of abundance.
    • dhā: "bring" - A call for the arrival of blessings.
    • rayim: "wealth" - Not just material wealth, but also spiritual and emotional richness.
    • ṛṣvam: "vast" - Emphasizing the magnitude of the blessings.
    • bṛhantaṁ: "abundant" - Suggesting a continuous flow of prosperity.
    • yūyam: "you all" - A collective address to the divine forces.
    • pāta: "protect" - A plea for safeguarding and guidance.
    • svastibhiḥ: "with blessings" - Invoking auspiciousness and well-being.
    • sadā: "always" - A desire for perpetual support.
    • naḥ: "for us" - A communal aspect, seeking blessings for all.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during the dawn, symbolizing the start of a new day filled with opportunities and hope.
    • In a family setting, parents can invoke this mantra for the well-being and prosperity of their children, nurturing them with wisdom and love.
    • In a community gathering, this mantra can be used to seek collective blessings for prosperity and harmony among members.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine blessings for wealth, wisdom, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships, both with the divine and within our communities. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and gratitude, fostering a positive environment in their personal and communal lives. Its application in real-life scenarios serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and prosperity not just for oneself, but for the collective well-being of all.
