मन्त्र: त्रिकद्रुकेषु चेतनं देवासो यज्ञं अत्नत || तं इद वर्धन्तु नो गिरः सदावृधम् ||
trikadrukeṣu cetanaṁ devāso yajñam atnata | tam id vardhantu no giraḥ sadāvṛdham ||
  • Trikadrukeṣu: This term can be broken down into "tri" meaning three, and "kadrukeṣu" which refers to the three realms or aspects of existence. In a broader sense, it can signify the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of life.
  • Cetanaṁ: This word translates to consciousness or awareness. It emphasizes the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions and thoughts.
  • Devāso: This refers to the divine beings or deities. In the context of the mantra, it suggests invoking the presence and blessings of the divine.
  • Yajñam: This term means sacrifice or offering. In Vedic tradition, yajña is a ritual of offerings to the deities, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of selfless action.
  • Atnata: This word can be interpreted as eternal or everlasting. It signifies the timeless nature of the divine and the actions performed in devotion.
  • Tam id vardhantu: This phrase means "may that (divine presence) increase or grow." It is a prayer for the enhancement of divine qualities and blessings in our lives.
  • No giraḥ: This translates to "our words" or "our expressions." It emphasizes the significance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality.
  • Sadāvṛdham: This term means "always flourishing" or "ever-growing." It suggests a continuous state of growth and prosperity, both materially and spiritually.

Conclusion: The mantra "trikadrukeṣu cetanaṁ devāso yajñam atnata | tam id vardhantu no giraḥ sadāvṛdham ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine consciousness across the three realms of existence. It highlights the importance of awareness, selfless action, and the power of words in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, meditation, or even in daily affirmations. By focusing on the divine and expressing our intentions through positive words and actions, we can foster a sense of growth and harmony in our lives and the lives of those around us. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our spiritual and material well-being through conscious living and communication.
