यो हत्वाहिम अरिणात सप्त सिन्धून यो गा उदादजद अपधā वलस्य |यो अश्मनोः अन्तर अग्निं जजाना संवृक समत्सु स जनास इन्द्रः ||
yo hatvāhim ariṇāt sapta sindhūn yo gā udājad apadhā valasya | yo aśmanor antar agniṁ jajāna saṁvṛk samatsu sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • hatvā: having killed
    • ahiṁ: the serpent (often symbolizing obstacles or enemies)
    • ariṇāt: the one who is hostile or adversarial
    • sapta: seven
    • sindhūn: rivers (often representing abundance and resources)
    • yo: who
    • gā: has gone
    • udājad: has risen or emerged
    • apadhā: from the path or way
    • valasya: of the strong or powerful
    • yo: who
    • aśmanor: of stones (often symbolizing stability)
    • antar: within
    • agniṁ: fire (symbolizing energy and transformation)
    • jajāna: has been born or created
    • saṁvṛk: surrounded or enveloped
    • samatsu: in harmony or together
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: of the people
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods, representing strength and power)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity (Indra) who has overcome adversaries and obstacles (symbolized by the serpent) and has emerged victorious.
    • The mention of "seven rivers" can be interpreted as the abundance of resources and opportunities that one can access when they are strong and determined.
    • The "fire within stones" symbolizes the inner strength and potential that lies within each individual, waiting to be ignited and utilized.
    • Indra, as a representation of strength and leadership, signifies the importance of unity and harmony among people to achieve greatness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who overcomes challenges (akin to the serpent) and leads their team (the people) towards success can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals facing hardships can draw inspiration from this mantra, reminding them that they possess the inner strength (the fire) to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
    • Communities that come together in harmony to support each other during difficult times reflect the unity and strength emphasized in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that lies within each individual and the importance of unity in overcoming challenges.
    • Its significance is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, leadership, and community support.
    • By embodying the qualities of resilience, strength, and harmony, individuals and communities can navigate through life's adversities and emerge victorious.
