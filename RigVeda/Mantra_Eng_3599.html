आगन देव ऋतुभिर वर्धतु क्षयम् दधातु नः सविता सुप्रजाम इषम् |स नः क्षपाभिर अहभिष्च जिन्वतु प्रजावन्तं रयिम अस्मे सम इन्वतु ||
āgan deva ṛtubhir vardhatu kṣayaṁ dadhātu naḥ savitā suprajām iṣam | sa naḥ kṣapābhir ahabhiś ca jinvatu prajāvantaṁ rayim asme sam invatu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āgan: May come
    • deva: O God
    • ṛtubhir: With the seasons
    • vardhatu: May increase
    • kṣayaṁ: Destruction
    • dadhātu: May grant
    • naḥ: To us
    • savitā: O Sun
    • suprajām: Good progeny
    • iṣam: Power
    • sa: May he
    • naḥ: To us
    • kṣapābhir: With the nights
    • ahabhiś: And days
    • ca: And
    • jinvatu: May he protect
    • prajāvantaṁ: Those who have progeny
    • rayim: Wealth
    • asme: For us
    • sam: Together
    • invatu: May he bestow
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine, specifically to the Sun God (Savitā), asking for blessings of prosperity, protection, and the increase of good progeny.
    • It emphasizes the importance of harmony with nature and the cycles of life, seeking divine intervention to ensure that destruction is kept at bay and that wealth and progeny flourish.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, this mantra can be recited during planting seasons to invoke favorable weather and bountiful harvests, reflecting the connection between divine blessings and human sustenance.
    • Families may chant this mantra during auspicious occasions like weddings or childbirth, seeking blessings for a prosperous and harmonious family life.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of divine support in achieving prosperity and well-being in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human life with nature and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek harmony and balance.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in personal prayers, community gatherings, and rituals aimed at fostering unity, peace, and abundance.
