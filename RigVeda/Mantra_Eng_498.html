मन्त्र: चतुरश्चिद्ददमानाद्बिभीयादानिधातोः |न दुरुक्ताय स्पृहयेत ||
caturaś cid dadamānād bibhīyād ā nidhātoḥ | na duruktāya spṛhayet ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • caturaś: clever, wise, or intelligent
    • cid: indeed, certainly
    • dadamānād: from the one who gives or bestows
    • bibhīyād: may fear or be afraid
    • ā: from, or away from
    • nidhātoḥ: of the one who is the source or origin
    • na: not
    • duruktāya: to harsh or unkind words
    • spṛhayet: should desire or aspire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the nature of giving.
    • It suggests that a wise person, who is generous and bestows gifts, should not fear the source of their generosity.
    • Furthermore, it advises against desiring or aspiring for harsh or unkind words, indicating that one should strive for kindness and compassion in communication.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a manager who is wise and generous with their praise and support fosters a positive environment. They do not fear sharing their knowledge or resources, as this builds trust and collaboration among team members.
    • In personal relationships, a person who communicates with kindness and avoids harsh words strengthens their bonds with others. For instance, during a disagreement, choosing to express feelings without resorting to insults can lead to a more constructive resolution.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and generosity in our interactions.
    • It teaches that true strength comes from kindness and that one should not fear the act of giving or sharing knowledge.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, both personally and professionally, fostering an environment of respect and understanding.
