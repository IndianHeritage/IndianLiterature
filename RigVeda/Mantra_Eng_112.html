अग्निम्-आग्निं हविमभिः सदा हवन्त विश्वपतिम् |हव्यवाहम् पुरुप्रियम् ||
agnim-agniṁ havīmabhiḥ sadā havanta viśpatim | havyavāham purupriyam ||
  • Mantra: "agnim-agniṁ havīmabhiḥ sadā havanta viśpatim | havyavāham purupriyam ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke Agni, the divine fire, with our offerings; may he always bring us prosperity and be the lord of all beings. He who carries our offerings is dear to all."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Agnim: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • Agniṁ: The same as above, emphasizing the importance of Agni in rituals.
    • Havīmabhiḥ: Refers to offerings or oblations made to the divine, indicating the act of giving.
    • Sadā: Means 'always' or 'eternally', suggesting a continuous relationship with the divine.
    • Havanta: Refers to those who bring offerings, highlighting the role of the devotee.
    • Viśpatim: Means 'lord of all beings', indicating Agni's universal significance.
    • Havyavāham: Refers to the one who carries offerings, emphasizing the importance of Agni in receiving and transforming offerings.
    • Purupriyam: Means 'dear to all', suggesting that Agni is beloved and respected by all beings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings), where offerings are made to the fire as a witness and a medium to the divine.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'Agni' can be seen in the importance of fire in cooking, where it transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, symbolizing transformation and sustenance.
    • Agni can also represent the inner fire or passion within individuals, driving them to pursue their goals and aspirations, much like how one might invoke motivation and energy in their daily lives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that transforms offerings into blessings. It highlights the importance of continuous devotion and the act of giving in our relationship with the divine. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize the transformative power of their actions, whether in rituals, personal aspirations, or communal gatherings. By invoking Agni, one acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships through offerings, whether they be material, emotional, or spiritual.
