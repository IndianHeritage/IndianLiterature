अमाय वो मरुतो यातवे द्यौर जिहीता उत्तरā बृहत् |यत्रा नरो देदिशते तनूष्व ā त्वक्षांसी बाह्वोजसः ||
amāya vo maruto yātave dyaur jihīta uttarā bṛhat | yatrā naro dediśate tanūṣv ā tvakṣāṁsi bāhvojasaḥ ||
  • amāya: This word can be interpreted as "without fear" or "free from fear." It signifies a state of confidence and security.
  • vo: This is a pronoun meaning "your" or "to you," indicating a connection or relationship with the Maruts, the storm deities.
  • maruto: Refers to the Maruts, the group of storm gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • yātave: This term can be understood as "to the one who travels" or "to the one who moves," suggesting dynamism and action.
  • dyaur: Meaning "sky" or "heaven," it represents the vastness and the divine realm.
  • jihīta: This word means "to be favorable" or "to be kind," indicating a benevolent aspect of the divine.
  • uttarā: Translated as "the northern" or "the higher," it suggests elevation or a superior state.
  • bṛhat: Meaning "great" or "vast," it emphasizes the enormity of the divine presence or the universe.
  • yatrā: This word means "where," indicating a location or a state of being.
  • naro: Refers to "man" or "human," highlighting the human experience and existence.
  • dediśate: This term means "to give" or "to bestow," suggesting generosity and the act of sharing.
  • tanūṣv: Meaning "in bodies" or "in forms," it refers to the physical existence of beings.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating direction or intention.
  • tvakṣāṁsi: This word means "skin" or "body," referring to the physical aspect of beings.
  • bāhvojasaḥ: Meaning "of the strong arms" or "of the powerful," it emphasizes strength and capability.

This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, asking for their blessings and protection. It emphasizes the importance of being fearless and confident in the face of challenges. The reference to the sky and the vastness of the universe suggests a connection to the divine and the greater forces at play in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Facing personal challenges: When individuals encounter difficulties, invoking the spirit of the Maruts can inspire courage and resilience.
  • Community support: The mantra highlights the importance of collective strength, encouraging communities to come together and support one another.
  • Environmental awareness: The Maruts represent the forces of nature, reminding us of the need to respect and protect our environment.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, fearlessness, and unity. It serves as a reminder that, with the blessings of the divine, individuals and communities can overcome obstacles and thrive. By embodying the qualities of the Maruts, we can navigate life's challenges with confidence and grace.
