मां नो वधाय हतनवे जिहीळानस्य रीरधः |मां हृणानस्य मन्यवे ||
mā no vadhāya hatnave jihīḻānasya rīradhaḥ | mā hṛṇānasya manyave ||
  • mā no vadhāya: This phrase translates to "do not let us be harmed" or "may we not be killed." It expresses a plea for protection and safety.
  • hatnave: This word means "to the one who is killed" or "to the one who is slain." It emphasizes the consequences of violence and the impact it has on individuals and society.
  • jihīḻānasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the one who is angry" or "of the one who is hostile." It highlights the emotions that can lead to conflict and harm.
  • rīradhaḥ: This word means "to the one who is in distress" or "to the one who is suffering." It acknowledges the pain and suffering that can arise from violence and conflict.
  • mā hṛṇānasya: This phrase translates to "do not let us be taken away" or "do not let us be deprived." It signifies a desire for preservation and the importance of maintaining one's existence and dignity.
  • manyave: This word means "of the mind" or "of the thought." It refers to the mental state and the importance of positive thinking and intentions in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "mā no vadhāya hatnave jihīḻānasya rīradhaḥ | mā hṛṇānasya manyave ||" serves as a profound reminder of the need for protection, compassion, and understanding in our interactions with others. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In conflict resolution, where individuals seek to understand each other's perspectives and avoid harm.
  • In community building, where the focus is on creating a safe and supportive environment for all members.
  • In personal relationships, where empathy and kindness can prevent misunderstandings and emotional distress.

This mantra encourages us to foster a mindset of peace and protection, emphasizing the significance of our thoughts and actions in shaping a harmonious existence. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more compassionate and understanding world.
