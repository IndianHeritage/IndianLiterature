इमा उ त्वा पुरुतमस्य कारोर्व हव्यं वीर हव्याः हवन्ते |धियो रथेष्टां अजरो नवीयो रयिः विभूतिर ईयते वचस्याः ||
imā u tvā purutamasya kāror havyaṁ vīra havyā havante | dhiyo ratheṣṭhām ajaraṁ navīyo rayir vibhūtir īyate vacasyā ||
  • imā u tvā - This phrase can be translated as "these (offerings) are for you." It signifies the act of devotion and the intention behind the offerings made to the divine.
  • purutamasya - This word means "of the most excellent" or "the best." It refers to the supreme quality of the divine being addressed.
  • kāror - This term can be interpreted as "of the one who is worthy" or "the one who is deserving." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the worthiness of the divine.
  • havyaṁ - This means "that which is offered" or "sacred offerings." It represents the physical manifestations of devotion, such as rituals and prayers.
  • vīra - Translated as "hero" or "brave one," this term highlights the qualities of strength and valor associated with the divine.
  • havyā havante - This phrase can be understood as "the offerings that are to be received." It indicates the expectation of divine acceptance of the offerings made.
  • dhiyo - This word means "intellect" or "understanding." It signifies the importance of wisdom and clarity in one's thoughts and actions.
  • ratheṣṭhām - This term refers to "the one who is seated in the chariot." It symbolizes the divine guiding the journey of life.
  • ajaraṁ - Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," this word emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine and its qualities.
  • navīyo - This translates to "new" or "fresh," indicating the continuous renewal and rejuvenation that the divine brings into life.
  • rayir - This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • vibhūtir - Translated as "glory" or "splendor," this word highlights the magnificence of the divine presence.
  • īyate - This means "is invoked" or "is called upon," indicating the act of reaching out to the divine for support and guidance.
  • vacasyā - This term refers to "speech" or "words," emphasizing the power of communication and expression in connecting with the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, recognizing the divine as the source of strength, wisdom, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of offering our best intentions and actions to the divine, seeking guidance and blessings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power for support in their personal and communal journeys. By acknowledging the divine's qualities and offering our sincere intentions, we can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and fulfillment in our lives.
