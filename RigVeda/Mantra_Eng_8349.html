मन्त्र: श्रक्वे द्रप्सस्य धमतः सम अस्वरन्न ऋतस्य योना सम अरन्त नाभयः | त्रिन् स मूर्ध्नो असुरश चक्र आरभे सत्यस्य नावः सुक्रितम् अपीपरन् ||
srakve drapsasya dhamataḥ sam asvarann ṛtasya yonā sam aranta nābhayaḥ | trīn sa mūrdhno asuraś cakra ārabhe satyasya nāvaḥ sukṛtam apīparan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • srakve: In the context of the mantra, it refers to the essence or the beauty of creation.
    • drapsasya: This can be interpreted as the sustenance or nourishment that comes from the earth.
    • dhamataḥ: This signifies the power or strength that governs the universe.
    • sam: Meaning 'together' or 'in harmony.'
    • asvarann: This refers to the sound or vibration that resonates through creation.
    • ṛtasya: This denotes the principle of truth or cosmic order.
    • yonā: This means the source or origin.
    • sam aranta: This can be interpreted as 'those who are united' or 'those who come together.'
    • nābhayaḥ: This signifies the absence of fear or the state of being fearless.
    • trīn: Meaning 'three,' which can symbolize various triads in life.
    • sa: This means 'that' or 'he.'
    • mūrdhno: Referring to the head or the highest point.
    • asuraś: This can be interpreted as 'divine' or 'powerful beings.'
    • cakra: Meaning 'wheel' or 'cycle,' often representing the cycles of life.
    • ārabhe: This means 'to initiate' or 'to begin.'
    • satyasya: Referring to 'truth' or 'reality.'
    • nāvaḥ: This means 'boat' or 'vessel,' symbolizing a means of journey or transition.
    • sukṛtam: This signifies 'good deeds' or 'virtuous actions.'
    • apīparan: This can be interpreted as 'to attain' or 'to achieve.'
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all elements of creation, emphasizing harmony and the cosmic order.
    • The mention of 'three' can symbolize various triads such as mind, body, and spirit, or past, present, and future, highlighting the importance of balance in life.
    • The idea of being 'fearless' suggests that when one is aligned with truth and cosmic order, they can navigate life's challenges with confidence.
    • The 'boat' symbolizes the journey of life, where good deeds and virtuous actions serve as the means to navigate through the complexities of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when individuals come together in harmony, they can achieve greater goals, much like the mantra suggests unity and strength.
    • In personal development, embracing truth and acting with integrity can lead to a fulfilling life, free from fear and doubt.
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life forms can inspire collective action towards sustainability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony, truth, and virtuous actions in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that we are all part of a greater cosmic order, and by aligning ourselves with these principles, we can navigate life's journey with purpose and confidence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, community cohesion, and a deeper connection with the world around us.
