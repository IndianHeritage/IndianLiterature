विभिर्द्वा चरता एकया सह प्र प्रवासेव वसतः ||
vibhir dvā carata ekayā saha pra pravāseva vasataḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vibhir: This word can be interpreted as "spread out" or "pervading." It suggests a sense of abundance or distribution.
    • dvā: This means "two" or "dual." It often signifies a pair or a duality in existence.
    • carata: This translates to "moving" or "wandering." It indicates a sense of action or dynamism.
    • ekayā: This means "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of coming together as one.
    • saha: This translates to "with" or "together with." It reinforces the idea of companionship or partnership.
    • pra: This prefix often means "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progression.
    • pravāseva: This can be interpreted as "in the journey" or "in the process of traveling." It suggests a state of transition or movement.
    • vasataḥ: This means "dwelling" or "residing." It indicates a place of existence or being.
  • Meaning: The mantra can be understood as a call for unity and movement, suggesting that even in a state of duality, there is a need to come together and progress forward in life. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and shared journeys.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, teams often consist of individuals with diverse skills (dvā) who must work together (ekayā saha) to achieve common goals. The success of a project often depends on how well these individuals can collaborate and move forward together (carata).
    • In community service, volunteers from different backgrounds come together (saha) to address social issues. Their collective efforts (vibhir) can lead to significant changes in the community, demonstrating the power of unity in action (pravāseva).
    • In personal relationships, couples often face challenges (dvā) but must navigate these together (ekayā saha) to strengthen their bond. Their journey (pravāseva) through life's ups and downs is a testament to their commitment to each other.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity and collaboration in the face of duality. It serves as a reminder that while we may have differences, our collective efforts can lead to progress and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, whether in professional settings, community initiatives, or personal relationships, the principles of coming together and moving forward are essential for achieving shared goals and fostering harmony.
