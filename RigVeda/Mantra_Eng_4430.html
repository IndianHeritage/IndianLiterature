पृक्षस्य वृष्णो अरुषस्य नू सहः प्र नु वोचं विदथा जातवेदसः |वैश्वानराय मतिर्व नव्यसी शुचिः सोम इव पवते चारुर अग्नये ||
pṛkṣasya vṛṣṇo aruṣasya nū sahaḥ pra nu vocaṁ vidathā jātavedasaḥ | vaiśvānarāya matir navyasī śuciḥ soma iva pavate cārur agnaye ||
  • pṛkṣasya: This word refers to the act of seeking or searching. In real life, it can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge or truth, much like a student searching for answers in their studies.
  • vṛṣṇo: This term is associated with the Vṛṣṇi clan, often linked to Lord Krishna. It symbolizes strength and valor, akin to a leader who inspires courage in their followers.
  • aruṣasya: Meaning 'the red one' or 'the bright one', it can represent the sun or fire, which are essential for life. In daily life, this can be seen in the way sunlight nurtures plants, symbolizing growth and vitality.
  • nū sahaḥ: This phrase translates to 'now together' or 'let us unite'. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity, similar to a team working together to achieve a common goal.
  • pra nu vocaṁ: This means 'let us speak forth' or 'let us express'. It highlights the significance of communication, much like how sharing ideas in a discussion can lead to innovation.
  • vidathā: Referring to 'the assembly' or 'the gathering', it signifies the importance of community. In real life, this can be seen in family gatherings or community meetings where collective decisions are made.
  • jātavedasaḥ: This term refers to the 'one who knows all births', often associated with Agni, the fire god. It symbolizes wisdom and the ability to understand the past, present, and future, akin to a mentor guiding a young professional.
  • vaiśvānarāya: This represents the universal aspect of divinity, suggesting inclusiveness. In practice, it can be seen in multicultural societies where diverse backgrounds come together harmoniously.
  • matir: Meaning 'intellect' or 'thought', it emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making, similar to how a wise leader navigates challenges with foresight.
  • navyasī: This word means 'new' or 'fresh', symbolizing innovation and progress. In real life, it can be related to startups that bring new ideas to the market.
  • śuciḥ: This translates to 'pure' or 'clean', representing integrity and clarity of purpose. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical business practices that prioritize honesty.
  • soma iva: Referring to 'like soma', it symbolizes nourishment and rejuvenation, akin to how a balanced diet nourishes the body and mind.
  • pavate: This means 'to purify' or 'to cleanse', highlighting the importance of purification in various aspects of life, such as mental clarity through meditation.
  • cārur: Meaning 'beautiful' or 'pleasant', it signifies the appreciation of beauty in life, much like how art and nature enrich our experiences.
  • agnaye: Referring to 'to Agni', it symbolizes the divine presence in fire, representing transformation and energy, similar to how challenges can lead to personal growth.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to come together, communicate effectively, and embrace innovation while maintaining purity of thought and action. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal development. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and progressive society.
