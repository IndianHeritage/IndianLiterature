अनायतो अनिबद्धः कथायं न्यङ्ग उत्तानोऽव पद्यते न |कया याति स्वधया को ददर्श दिवः स्कम्भः समृतः पाति नाकम् ||
anāyato anibaddhaḥ kathāyaṁ nyaṅṅ uttāno 'va padyate na | kayā yāti svadhayā ko dadarśa divaḥ skambhaḥ samṛtaḥ pāti nākam ||
  • anāyato: This word translates to "without being bound" or "unrestrained." It signifies freedom and the ability to act without limitations.
  • anibaddhaḥ: Meaning "unattached" or "not bound," this term emphasizes the state of being free from obligations or constraints.
  • kathāyaṁ: This translates to "in what story" or "in what narrative," indicating a quest for understanding or knowledge through stories.
  • nyaṅṅ: This word can be interpreted as "who" or "which," often used to inquire about identity or existence.
  • uttāno: Meaning "elevated" or "uplifted," it suggests a state of rising above challenges or limitations.
  • 'va: This is a conjunction meaning "or," used to present alternatives or choices.
  • padyate: This translates to "is placed" or "is situated," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • na: Meaning "not," it negates the following statement.
  • kayā: This word means "by what" or "with what," often used to inquire about the means or method.
  • yāti: This translates to "goes" or "moves," indicating action or progression.
  • svadhayā: Meaning "by one's own nature" or "by one's own self," it emphasizes individuality and self-determination.
  • ko: This means "who," again inquiring about identity or agency.
  • dadarśa: This translates to "saw" or "perceived," indicating awareness or realization.
  • divaḥ: Meaning "of the sky" or "heavenly," it refers to the celestial realm.
  • skambhaḥ: This word means "pillar" or "support," symbolizing stability and strength.
  • samṛtaḥ: Meaning "remembered" or "recalled," it suggests the importance of memory and legacy.
  • pāti: This translates to "protects" or "sustains," indicating a nurturing or safeguarding presence.
  • nākam: Meaning "the sky" or "heaven," it refers to the vastness and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra reflects the themes of freedom, self-awareness, and the quest for knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of being unbound and unattached, allowing individuals to rise above their circumstances and seek their own paths. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, career choices, and spiritual journeys. For instance, someone may feel constrained by societal expectations but can find empowerment by embracing their individuality and pursuing their passions. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies in self-determination and the ability to navigate one's own narrative in life.
