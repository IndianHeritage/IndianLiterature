आ नो यातं दिवस पर्य आन्तरिक्षाद अधप्रियाः |पुत्रः कण्वस्य वाम इह सुशाव सोम्यम् मधु ||
ā no yātaṁ divas pary āntarikṣād adhapriyā | putraḥ kaṇvasya vām iha suṣāva somyam madhu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no yātaṁ: May we go forth
    • divas: from the heavens
    • pary: all around
    • āntarikṣād: from the atmosphere
    • adhapriyā: to the beloved
    • putraḥ: the son
    • kaṇvasya: of Kanva
    • vām: your
    • iha: here
    • suṣāva: has produced
    • somyam: the pleasant
    • madhu: honey
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for prosperity and well-being, invoking the blessings of the heavens and the atmosphere.
    • The reference to "the son of Kanva" signifies lineage and the importance of family in achieving success.
    • The term "honey" symbolizes sweetness and abundance, suggesting that the outcomes of our efforts should be fruitful and enjoyable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be recited during auspicious occasions like weddings or the birth of a child, emphasizing the importance of blessings from the divine for a prosperous future.
    • In a professional context, it can be used in team meetings to foster a collaborative spirit, encouraging team members to work together harmoniously for shared success.
    • During festivals, families may chant this mantra to invoke divine blessings for happiness and abundance in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of family, community, and the divine in achieving prosperity.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to approach our goals with a sense of unity and purpose, ensuring that our efforts lead to sweet and fulfilling outcomes.
