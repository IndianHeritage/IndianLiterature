आ यः पुरं नार्मिणीम अदीदेद अत्यः कविर नभन्योऽ नार्वा |सूरो न रुरुक्वां शतात्मा ||
ā yaḥ puraṁ nārmiṇīm adīded atyaḥ kavir nabhanyo3 nārvā | sūro na rurukvāñ chatātmā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: This can be interpreted as "from" or "out of."
    • yaḥ: "who" or "that."
    • puraṁ: "city" or "place."
    • nārmiṇīm: "of the women" or "feminine."
    • adīded: "has created" or "has established."
    • atyah: "beyond" or "transcending."
    • kavir: "the wise one" or "the poet."
    • nabhanyo: "of the sky" or "heavenly."
    • nārvā: "the one who is not afraid" or "fearless."
    • sūro: "the hero" or "the brave one."
    • na: "not."
    • rurukvāñ: "who is troubled" or "who suffers."
    • chatātmā: "with a hundred souls" or "with multiple aspects."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a creator or wise being who has established a place or city that transcends the ordinary.
    • The reference to "nārmiṇīm" suggests a connection to femininity, possibly indicating the nurturing aspect of creation.
    • The "kavir" represents wisdom and creativity, suggesting that true creation comes from a place of deep understanding.
    • The mention of "nabhanyo" indicates a connection to the divine or celestial, suggesting that this creation is not just earthly but has a higher significance.
    • The "sūro" implies bravery and strength, indicating that those who inhabit this space are courageous and resilient.
    • The phrase "na rurukvāñ" suggests that in this place, suffering or trouble is absent, highlighting a state of peace and fulfillment.
    • Finally, "chatātmā" indicates a multifaceted existence, suggesting that the essence of life is complex and rich.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern urban settings, the concept of a "city" can be seen as a place of diverse cultures and communities, where creativity and wisdom thrive.
    • Women often play a crucial role in nurturing and establishing communities, reflecting the "nārmiṇīm" aspect of the mantra.
    • Artists and thinkers who create from a place of deep understanding can be seen as modern-day "kavirs," contributing to society's growth.
    • In times of crisis, individuals who embody the "sūro" spirit inspire others to be brave and resilient, creating a supportive environment.
    • Communities that foster peace and understanding can be seen as embodying the essence of "na rurukvāñ," where suffering is minimized.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, creativity, and community in establishing a harmonious existence.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength lies in nurturing and supporting one another, transcending ordinary experiences.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to create spaces that are inclusive, peaceful, and rich in diversity, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of embracing our multifaceted nature for a fulfilling life.
