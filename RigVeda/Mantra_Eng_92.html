यत्सानोः सानुम् आरुहद् भूयः अस्पष्ट कर्त्वम् |तद् इन्द्रो अर्थं चेतति यूथेन वृष्णिः एजति ||
yat sānoḥ sānum āruhad bhūry aspaṣṭa kartvam | tad indro arthaṁ cetati yūthena vṛṣṇir ejati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • sānoḥ: of the two (often interpreted as the duality of existence)
    • sānum: the essence or nature
    • āruhad: has risen or ascended
    • bhūry: abundant or plentiful
    • aspaṣṭa: unclear or indistinct
    • kartvam: action or deed
    • tad: that
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • arthaṁ: purpose or meaning
    • cetati: thinks or contemplates
    • yūthena: with the group or community
    • vṛṣṇir: the one who is strong or powerful
    • ejati: moves or acts
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the duality of existence and the essence that has risen abundantly.
    • It suggests that even when actions are unclear or indistinct, there is a higher purpose that is contemplated by the divine (Indra).
    • The importance of community and collective strength is emphasized, as the mantra indicates that true power and action come from unity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when a team faces challenges, the unclear roles and responsibilities can lead to confusion. However, when the team comes together, they can find clarity and purpose, much like the essence that rises in the mantra.
    • In social movements, individuals may feel lost or unclear about their roles, but when they unite for a common cause, they embody the strength of the community, reflecting the idea of 'yūthena vṛṣṇir ejati.'
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and collective action in overcoming challenges and finding purpose.
    • It teaches us that even in times of uncertainty, we can find strength in community and shared goals.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and mutual support.
