वह्निं यशसां विदथस्य केतुं सुप्राव्यं दूतं सद्योर्थम् |द्विजन्मानं रयिम इव प्रशस्तं रातिम भरद भृगवे मातरिश्वा ||
vahniṁ yaśasaṁ vidathasya ketuṁ suprāvyaṁ dūtaṁ sadyoartham | dvijanmānaṁ rayim iva praśastaṁ rātim bharad bhṛgave mātariśvā ||
  • Vahniṁ: This word refers to fire or the divine energy associated with it. In real life, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how challenges can lead to personal growth.
  • Yaśasaṁ: This means fame or glory. In contemporary terms, it can relate to achieving recognition in one’s field, such as an artist gaining acclaim for their work.
  • Vidathasya: This refers to the assembly or gathering, often implying a community or collective effort. For example, community service projects where people come together for a common cause.
  • Ketuṁ: This word signifies a banner or a flag, symbolizing leadership and direction. In a modern context, it can be likened to a leader guiding a team towards a shared goal.
  • Suprāvyaṁ: This means well-spoken or eloquent. In real life, effective communication is crucial in various fields, such as a politician delivering a compelling speech to inspire voters.
  • Dūtaṁ: This translates to messenger. In today’s world, this could refer to anyone who conveys important information, like a journalist reporting news that impacts society.
  • Sadyoartham: This means immediate or urgent. It highlights the importance of timely action, such as responding quickly to a crisis situation, like a natural disaster response team.
  • Dvijanmānaṁ: This refers to the twice-born or those who have undergone a significant transformation, akin to individuals who have experienced a profound change in their lives, such as recovering from addiction.
  • Rayim: This means wealth or prosperity. In a practical sense, it can relate to financial success achieved through hard work and dedication, like a small business thriving in a competitive market.
  • Iva: This means like or as. It draws comparisons, emphasizing the importance of relatable experiences in understanding concepts.
  • Praśastaṁ: This means praised or commendable. It can refer to actions or behaviors that are recognized positively in society, such as acts of kindness or bravery.
  • Rātim: This translates to joy or delight. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of happiness shared with loved ones, like celebrating a family gathering.
  • Bharad: This means to bear or carry. It signifies responsibility, much like how parents bear the responsibility of nurturing their children.
  • Bhṛgave: This refers to the divine or the exalted. It can be associated with individuals who inspire others through their actions, like a mentor guiding a young professional.
  • Mātariśvā: This is a reference to the wind or the divine force that moves through the universe. In real life, it can symbolize change and the unseen forces that influence our lives, like the impact of technology on communication.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of transformation, community, leadership, and the importance of timely action. It emphasizes the significance of eloquence and the role of messengers in society. In real life, these concepts are applicable in various scenarios, from personal growth and community service to effective communication and leadership. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the impact they have on our lives and the lives of others, encouraging us to strive for excellence and contribute positively to society.
