ईलेन्यो वो मनुषो युगेषु समानगा अशुचज जातवेदाः | सुसंधृशा भानुना यो विभाति प्रति गावः समिधानम् बुद्धन्त ||
īḻenyo vo manuṣo yugeṣu samanagā aśucaj jātavedāḥ | susaṁdṛśā bhānunā yo vibhāti prati gāvaḥ samidhānam budhanta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īḻenyo: With great effort or strength
    • vo: Your
    • manuṣo: Human beings
    • yugeṣu: In the ages or epochs
    • samanagā: United or in harmony
    • aśucaj: Impure or unclean
    • jātavedāḥ: The one who knows all births (often referring to Agni, the fire god)
    • susaṁdṛśā: Well-seeing or perceptive
    • bhānunā: By the sun or light
    • yo: Who
    • vibhāti: Shines or illuminates
    • prati: Towards
    • gāvaḥ: Cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • samidhānam: The act of kindling or offering
    • budhanta: Those who are wise or enlightened
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the unity and strength of humanity across different ages.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and the role of Agni (the fire god) as a source of knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The reference to cows symbolizes prosperity and sustenance, highlighting the connection between spiritual and material wealth.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are wise and enlightened can see the truth and are able to kindle the fire of knowledge and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when people come together with a common purpose, they can achieve great things, much like the unity described in the mantra.
    • In modern society, the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can be seen in educational institutions where individuals strive to better themselves and their communities.
    • The importance of purity can be reflected in practices such as mindfulness and ethical living, which promote a clean and harmonious life.
    • In agriculture, the reverence for cows as symbols of wealth and nourishment can be observed in many cultures, emphasizing the connection between nature and sustenance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, purity, and the pursuit of knowledge in human life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community building to personal development and ethical living.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and societies can strive for a harmonious existence, fostering both spiritual and material prosperity.
