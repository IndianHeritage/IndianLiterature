त्वं होतā मनुर्हितो 'ग्ने यज्ञेषु सीदसि |सेमं नो अध्वरं यज ||
tvaṁ hotā manurhito 'gne yajñeṣu sīdasi | semaṁ no adhvaraṁ yaja ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hotā: O Invoker or Priest
    • manurhito: Inspired by Manu (the first man or progenitor)
    • 'gne: O Agni (Fire)
    • yajñeṣu: In the sacrifices
    • sīdasi: You sit or reside
    • semaṁ: Our protection or shelter
    • no: Our
    • adhvaraṁ: The sacred ritual or sacrifice
    • yaja: Perform or offer
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The term "hotā" refers to the priest or the one who invokes the deities during a ritual, emphasizing the importance of the role of the priest in Vedic rituals.
    • "Manurhito" suggests that the invocation is inspired by the principles laid down by Manu, who represents order and righteousness in the universe.
    • The phrase "yajñeṣu sīdasi" indicates that Agni resides in the sacrifices, highlighting the belief that fire is a vital element in rituals, serving as a conduit for offerings to reach the gods.
    • "Semaṁ no adhvaraṁ yaja" expresses a plea for protection and guidance in the performance of rituals, indicating the need for divine support in achieving success and safety in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of invoking a higher power during significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, mirrors the essence of this mantra. People often light a fire or a candle, symbolizing Agni, to seek blessings.
    • The role of a priest in various cultures today can be compared to the "hotā" in the mantra, where the priest acts as a mediator, ensuring that the rituals are performed correctly to invite divine favor.
    • In personal development, the idea of seeking guidance and protection while embarking on new ventures resonates with the plea for divine support in the mantra, reminding individuals to remain grounded and seek help from higher powers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection through rituals, emphasizing the importance of fire as a sacred element in connecting with the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the need for support in our endeavors, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, reminding us to invoke higher powers for guidance and to perform our duties with righteousness and integrity.
