मन्त्र: किम अस्य मादे किम व अस्य पीताव इन्द्रः किम अस्य सख्ये चकार | रणा वा ये निषदि किम ते अस्य पुरा विविध्रे किम उ नूतनासः ||
kim asya made kim v asya pītāv indraḥ kim asya sakhye cakāra | raṇā vā ye niṣadi kiṁ te asya purā vividre kim u nūtanāsaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • asya: of this
    • made: intoxication or delight
    • v: and
    • pītāv: drinking
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • sakhye: friendship
    • cakāra: did or created
    • raṇā: in battle
    • vā: or
    • ye: those who
    • niṣadi: sitting or dwelling
    • kiṁ: what
    • te: for you
    • purā: in the past
    • vividre: have they achieved
    • nūtanāsaḥ: new or fresh
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions, reflecting on the nature of intoxication and the actions of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and victory.
    • It questions the significance of intoxication and friendship, suggesting that these elements may have a deeper meaning in the context of life and relationships.
    • The reference to battle indicates the struggles and challenges one faces, and the inquiry about past achievements suggests a reflection on one's journey and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a social context, the mantra can be related to gatherings where people celebrate and bond over shared experiences, much like how friends come together to enjoy a festival or a wedding.
    • In a professional setting, it can reflect on teamwork and collaboration, where individuals support each other in overcoming challenges, akin to a project team working together to achieve a common goal.
    • In personal growth, it encourages individuals to reflect on their past experiences, learning from both successes and failures, similar to how one might evaluate their life choices during a significant life transition.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its exploration of the themes of intoxication, friendship, and the struggles of life.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their relationships and the impact of their past on their present and future.
    • In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to cherish friendships, learn from experiences, and face challenges with courage, much like the way one would approach life's battles with resilience and support from loved ones.
