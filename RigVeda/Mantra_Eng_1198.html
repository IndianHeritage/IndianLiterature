आभोगयम् प्र यद इच्छन्त आइतनापाकाः प्राञ्चो मम के चिद आपयः |सौधन्वनासश चरितस्य भूमानागच्छत सवितुर्दाशुषो गृहम् ||
ābhogayam pra yad icchanta aitanāpākāḥ prāñco mama ke cid āpayaḥ | saudhanvanāsaś caritasya bhūmanāgacchata savitur dāśuṣo gṛham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ābhogayam: The state of enjoyment or fulfillment.
    • pra: For, towards.
    • yad: What, that which.
    • icchanta: Desiring, wishing.
    • aitanāpākāḥ: Those who are not bound by worldly attachments.
    • prāñco: The breath of life, vital energy.
    • mama: My, of me.
    • ke cid: Anyone, any one.
    • āpayaḥ: To bring, to lead.
    • saudhanvanāsa: The one who is rich in wealth or resources.
    • caritasya: Of the one who acts, of the doer.
    • bhūmanā: The great, the vast.
    • agacchata: May come, may arrive.
    • savitur: Of the sun, the source of light.
    • dāśuṣo: Of the one who is devoted, the servant.
    • gṛham: Home, dwelling place.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the fulfillment of desires and the importance of detachment from worldly possessions.
    • It emphasizes the need for vital energy and the role of the divine (represented by the sun) in guiding individuals towards their true home or purpose.
    • The phrase "those who are not bound by worldly attachments" suggests that true wealth lies in spiritual fulfillment rather than material possessions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often chase material success, believing it will bring happiness. This mantra reminds us that true contentment comes from within and through spiritual growth.
    • Consider a person who has achieved great wealth but feels unfulfilled. They may find greater satisfaction in helping others or engaging in community service, aligning with the idea of detachment from materialism.
    • In a workplace setting, a leader who prioritizes the well-being of their team over profits embodies the essence of this mantra, creating a supportive environment that fosters growth and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual wealth over material wealth.
    • It encourages individuals to seek fulfillment through self-awareness, community, and connection with the divine.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more balanced and meaningful existence, where one finds joy in giving and serving rather than merely accumulating possessions.
