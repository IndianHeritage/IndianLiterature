द्वितायो वृत्रहन्तमो विद इन्द्रः शतक्रतुः |उप नो हरिभिः सुतम् ||
dvitā yo vṛtrahantamo vida indraḥ śatakratuḥ | upa no haribhiḥ sutam ||
  • Mantra: "dvitā yo vṛtrahantamo vida indraḥ śatakratuḥ | upa no haribhiḥ sutam ||"
  • Translation: "Indra, the one who has a hundred powers, knows the two who are the slayers of Vritra. May he bring us the son of the divine, the one who is nourished by the gods."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • dvitā: Refers to "two" or "the duality" in existence.
    • vṛtrahantamo: "Vritra" symbolizes obstacles or challenges, and "hantamo" means "the slayer." Thus, it refers to the one who overcomes challenges.
    • vida: Means "knows" or "understands," indicating wisdom and awareness.
    • indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • śatakratuḥ: "One with a hundred powers," symbolizing the multifaceted nature of strength and capability.
    • upa: Means "towards" or "near," indicating a desire for closeness or connection.
    • no: Means "us," indicating a collective or community aspect.
    • haribhiḥ: Refers to "the divine" or "the gods," emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the request.
    • sutam: Means "son" or "offspring," often symbolizing new beginnings or potential.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be applied in situations where individuals face challenges or obstacles in their lives, such as pursuing a difficult career path or overcoming personal struggles. Just as Indra is invoked to overcome Vritra, individuals can seek strength and wisdom to face their own challenges.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during collective efforts, such as community service or social initiatives, where the strength of the group is needed to overcome societal issues.
    • In educational contexts, students can recite this mantra to invoke the wisdom and strength of Indra as they face academic challenges, symbolizing the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and community in overcoming obstacles. It serves as a reminder that challenges are a part of life, but with the right mindset and support, they can be conquered.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together, harness their collective strengths, and strive for new beginnings, much like the offspring nourished by the divine.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the journey of life, where understanding and overcoming challenges lead to growth and fulfillment.
