मरत्सु वो दधिमहि स्तोमं यज्ञं च धृष्णुया |विश्वे ये मानवाः युगां पान्ति मृत्यं ऋषः ||
marutsu vo dadhīmahi stomaṁ yajñaṁ ca dhṛṣṇuyā | viśve ye mānuṣā yugā pānti martyaṁ riṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • marutsu: In the context of the mantra, this refers to the "winds" or "air," symbolizing the vital forces of nature.
    • vo: This means "to you," indicating a sense of offering or dedication.
    • dadhīmahi: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," suggesting a deep reflection or focus.
    • stomaṁ: This means "praise" or "hymn," referring to the act of worship or reverence.
    • yajñaṁ: This translates to "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals.
    • ca: This means "and," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
    • dhṛṣṇuyā: This means "with strength" or "with determination," emphasizing the power behind the actions.
    • viśve: This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity.
    • ye: This translates to "who," referring to the subjects of the mantra.
    • mānuṣā: This means "humans" or "mankind," highlighting the focus on human beings.
    • yugā: This translates to "ages" or "cycles," referring to the different periods of time.
    • pānti: This means "nourish" or "sustain," indicating the act of providing sustenance.
    • martyaṁ: This refers to "mortals" or "human beings," emphasizing the human condition.
    • riṣaḥ: This means "sages" or "wise ones," referring to those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a collective intention to meditate on the vital forces of nature (winds) and to offer praise and sacrifices with strength and determination.
    • It acknowledges the universal presence of humanity across different ages and emphasizes the importance of nourishing and sustaining human life through wisdom and knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in environmental conservation efforts, where we meditate on the importance of nature and make sacrifices for its preservation.
    • In community service, individuals can embody the spirit of this mantra by working together to nourish and support those in need, reflecting the idea of universal humanity.
    • In personal development, one can meditate on their strengths and the wisdom of past generations to navigate challenges in life, embodying the essence of the sages mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and reverence for both nature and humanity.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on the world, promoting a sense of responsibility and interconnectedness.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to engage in practices that nourish both ourselves and the environment, fostering a sustainable and harmonious existence.
