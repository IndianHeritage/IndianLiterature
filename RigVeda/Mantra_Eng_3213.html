बोधद यन् मां हरिभ्यां कुमारः साहदेव्यः |अच्छा न हूता उद अरम् ||
bodhad yan mā haribhyāṁ kumāraḥ sāhadevyaḥ | acchā na hūta ud aram ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the wise one, the son of the divine, be protected from harm and may he not be disturbed." This invocation seeks blessings for a young one, often interpreted as a child or a new beginning.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bodhad: Refers to wisdom or enlightenment. It signifies the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.
    • yan: This word can be understood as "who" or "that," indicating a subject of reverence.
    • mā: A term used to denote negation, meaning "not" or "may not."
    • haribhyāṁ: This term relates to the divine or the gods, often associated with protection and blessings.
    • kumāraḥ: Refers to a young one or a child, symbolizing innocence and potential.
    • sāhadevyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is protected" or "the one who is supported."
    • acchā: Means "good" or "well," indicating a positive state.
    • na: Again, a negation, reinforcing the idea of protection from harm.
    • hūta: Refers to being disturbed or harmed, emphasizing the need for safety.
    • ud: Can mean "up" or "out," suggesting a movement away from danger.
    • aram: This term can be interpreted as "to be free" or "to be liberated," indicating a state of peace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, this mantra can be a prayer for the safety and well-being of children, reflecting the universal desire for protection and guidance in their formative years.
    • In educational settings, teachers may invoke this mantra to wish for the enlightenment and growth of their students, hoping they remain unharmed by negative influences.
    • During significant life transitions, such as starting a new job or moving to a new place, individuals may recite this mantra to seek protection and wisdom in navigating new challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on wisdom, protection, and the nurturing of potential. It serves as a reminder of the importance of safeguarding the innocent and guiding them towards enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, education, and personal growth, reinforcing the idea that wisdom and protection are essential for a fulfilling life journey.
