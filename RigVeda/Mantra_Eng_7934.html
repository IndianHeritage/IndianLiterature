आ प्यायस्व सम एतु ते विश्वतः सोम वृष्ण्यम् |भवā वाजस्य संगथे ||
ā pyāyasva sam etu te viśvataḥ soma vṛṣṇyam | bhavā vājasya saṁgathe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix indicating a sense of direction or movement towards.
    • pyāyasva: Derived from the root "pyāy," meaning to protect or to nourish.
    • sam: Meaning together or in harmony.
    • etu: A form of the verb "to come" or "to arrive."
    • te: Meaning "your" or "of you."
    • viśvataḥ: Meaning "from all sides" or "universally."
    • soma: Referring to the sacred drink or plant, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • vṛṣṇyam: Meaning "strength" or "power," often associated with the qualities of the divine.
    • bhavā: Meaning "become" or "may you become."
    • vājasya: Meaning "of strength" or "of vigor."
    • saṁgathe: Meaning "in union" or "together."
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes a sense of protection and nourishment from all directions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony in achieving strength and power.
    • The reference to "soma" suggests a connection to divine inspiration and the pursuit of higher consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, this mantra can be applied to foster collaboration and mutual support among team members, ensuring that everyone contributes to a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the importance of nurturing and protecting each other, creating a safe and harmonious environment.
    • In community building, it serves as a reminder to come together from diverse backgrounds to create a stronger, united front.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength and protection, emphasizing that true power comes from unity and mutual support.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, relationships, and community efforts, reminding us that together we can achieve greater heights.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and groups can cultivate a sense of belonging and shared purpose, leading to a more harmonious existence.
