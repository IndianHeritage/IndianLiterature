अश्व्यो वारो अभवस् तद इन्द्र सृके यत त्वा प्रत्यहन देव एकः |अजयो गा अजयः शूर सोमम् अवासृजः सर्तवे सप्त सिन्धून ||
aśvyo vāro abhavas tad indra sṛke yat tvā pratyahan deva ekaḥ | ajayo gā ajayaḥ śūra somam avāsṛjaḥ sartave sapta sindhūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvyo: horses
    • vāro: protector or guardian
    • abhavas: became or manifested
    • tad: that
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sṛke: in the sacrifice or offering
    • yat: which
    • tvā: you
    • pratyahan: every day or continuously
    • deva: god
    • ekaḥ: one
    • ajayo: invincible
    • gā: songs or hymns
    • ajayaḥ: unconquerable
    • śūra: brave or heroic
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink
    • avāsṛjaḥ: released or poured out
    • sartave: for the purpose of gathering
    • sapta: seven
    • sindhūn: rivers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of Indra, who is often associated with strength, protection, and the power of nature.
    • The mention of "aśvyo vāro" suggests the importance of horses, which symbolize speed, power, and nobility in ancient cultures.
    • Indra is invoked as a protector who continuously supports and blesses the devotee, emphasizing the idea of divine guardianship.
    • The reference to "ajayo gā" highlights the significance of songs and hymns in invoking divine presence and strength, suggesting that art and expression are powerful tools for connection with the divine.
    • The "seven rivers" symbolize abundance and the flow of life, representing the interconnectedness of nature and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of seeking protection and guidance from a higher power can be seen in various spiritual practices, where individuals pray or meditate for strength and support in their daily challenges.
    • The importance of community and collective strength can be likened to teamwork in organizations, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, much like the rivers flowing together.
    • Artistic expressions, such as music and poetry, continue to play a vital role in human culture, serving as a means to connect with emotions and the divine, similar to the hymns mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, strength, and the importance of community and artistic expression.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; there is a higher power that supports us, and we can draw strength from our connections with others.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek support, express themselves creatively, and recognize the abundance that surrounds them, fostering a sense of gratitude and interconnectedness.
