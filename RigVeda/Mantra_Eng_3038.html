बृहस्पते जुषस्व नो हव्यानि विश्वदेव्यः |रास्व रत्नानि दाशुषे ||
bṛhaspate juṣasva no havyāni viśvadevya | rāsva ratnāni dāśuṣe ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate juṣasva no havyāni viśvadevya | rāsva ratnāni dāśuṣe ||"
  • Translation: "O Bṛhaspati, may you be pleased with our offerings, O all-gods; grant us the short and precious treasures."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity representing wisdom, knowledge, and eloquence, often associated with the planet Jupiter.
    • juṣasva: A plea for favor or acceptance, asking the deity to be pleased.
    • no: "our," indicating a personal connection to the speaker's community or group.
    • havyāni: Refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the gods.
    • viśvadevya: "O all-gods," acknowledging the presence and importance of all divine beings.
    • rāsva: Meaning "short" or "concise," suggesting a desire for simplicity or clarity.
    • ratnāni: "Treasures" or "gems," symbolizing valuable gifts or blessings.
    • dāśuṣe: Referring to the act of giving or bestowing, often in the context of receiving blessings.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and guidance from higher powers, particularly in the context of community and shared offerings.
    • It reflects a desire for divine favor in the pursuit of knowledge and prosperity, highlighting the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine blessings.
    • The mention of "short and precious treasures" suggests that the speaker values quality over quantity, seeking meaningful and impactful blessings rather than superficial ones.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be applied in educational settings where students seek guidance and wisdom from teachers or mentors, acknowledging the importance of learning and knowledge.
    • In personal development, individuals may recite this mantra as a reminder to focus on meaningful goals and to seek wisdom in their decisions, rather than getting lost in trivial pursuits.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can serve as a reminder to honor collective efforts and to seek blessings for the community's growth and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our endeavors, emphasizing the importance of community and meaningful offerings.
    • By invoking Bṛhaspati and acknowledging the presence of all gods, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where knowledge, wisdom, and community support are valued.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for clarity and purpose in our pursuits, seeking treasures that enrich our lives and the lives of those around us.
