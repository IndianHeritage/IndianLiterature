सोमो अस्मभ्यं द्विपादे चतुष्पादे च पशवे |अनमिवा इषस करात ||
somo asmabhyaṁ dvipade catuṣpade ca paśave | anamīvā iṣas karat ||
  • Mantra: "somo asmabhyaṁ dvipade catuṣpade ca paśave | anamīvā iṣas karat ||"
  • Translation: "May the Soma (sacred drink) be for us, for the two-footed and the four-footed creatures; may it bring us strength and vitality."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Soma: A ritual drink of importance in Vedic culture, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Asmabhyaṁ: "For us," indicating a collective benefit or blessing.
    • Dvipade: "Two-footed," referring to humans.
    • Catuṣpade: "Four-footed," referring to animals.
    • Paśave: "Creatures," encompassing all living beings.
    • Anamīvā: "May it bring," suggesting a desire for the effects of Soma.
    • Iṣas: "Strength" or "vitality," indicating the desired outcome of the mantra.
    • Karat: "To do" or "to bring forth," implying action or manifestation.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Health and Wellness: Just as Soma is believed to provide strength, modern health practices emphasize the importance of nutrition and hydration for vitality. Consuming nutrient-rich foods and staying hydrated can be seen as a contemporary equivalent of seeking strength.
    • Animal Welfare: The mention of both two-footed and four-footed beings highlights the interconnectedness of all life. In real life, this can translate to practices such as animal husbandry, where the health of animals is prioritized for the benefit of both humans and animals.
    • Community Support: The collective aspect of "for us" can be reflected in community initiatives that aim to support both people and animals, such as local shelters or food banks that provide for those in need.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and vitality not just for oneself but for all living beings. It reflects a holistic view of health and well-being that is applicable in various aspects of life.
    • In today's world, this can inspire individuals and communities to work towards the well-being of all, fostering a sense of responsibility towards both human and animal life.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the shared pursuit of strength and vitality in a harmonious existence.
