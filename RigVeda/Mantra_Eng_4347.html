वनेषु व्यन्तरिक्षं ततान वाजम् अरवत्सु पयः उष्रियासु |हृत्सु क्रतुं वरुणो अप्सु अग्निं दिवि सूर्यं अदधात सोमं अद्रौ ||
vaneṣu vy a1ntarikṣaṁ tatāna vājam arvatsu paya usriyāsu | hṛtsu kratuṁ varuṇo apsv a1gniṁ divi sūryam adadhāt somam adrau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vaneṣu: in the forests
    • vy a1ntarikṣaṁ: the atmosphere or the space
    • tatāna: he spread or extended
    • vājam: the strength or power
    • arvatsu: in the waters
    • paya: the nourishing or life-giving substance
    • usriyāsu: in the streams or rivers
    • hṛtsu: in the hearts
    • kratuṁ: the will or intention
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the deity of water and cosmic order
    • apsv: in the waters
    • a1gniṁ: the fire
    • divi: in the sky or heaven
    • sūryam: the sun
    • adadhāt: he placed or established
    • somam: the moon or soma, a sacred drink
    • adrau: in the mountains
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra describes the cosmic order and the interconnectedness of nature.
    • It highlights how Varuna, the deity associated with water and moral order, spreads strength and nourishment across various realms: forests, waters, and the atmosphere.
    • The mention of fire, the sun, and the moon signifies the essential elements that sustain life and maintain balance in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the balance of elements like water (rain), sunlight, and soil health is crucial for crop growth, reflecting the interconnectedness described in the mantra.
    • In environmental conservation, protecting forests and water bodies is essential for maintaining the ecological balance, akin to the cosmic order emphasized by Varuna.
    • In personal well-being, nurturing our inner strength (hṛtsu kratuṁ) and maintaining a harmonious relationship with nature can lead to a balanced and fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all elements in nature and the universe.
    • It teaches us to respect and maintain balance in our environment, which is applicable in various fields such as agriculture, environmental science, and personal development.
    • By understanding and applying these principles, we can contribute to a sustainable future and a harmonious existence with nature.
