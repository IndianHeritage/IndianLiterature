त्वं नो गोपाः पथिकृत् विचक्षणस् तव व्रताय मतिभिर् जरामहे |बृहस्पते यो नो अभि ह्वरो दधे स्वा तम् मार्मर्तु दुच्छुनाऽ हरस्वती ||
tvaṁ no gopāḥ pathikṛd vicakṣaṇas tava vratāya matibhir jarāmahe | bṛhaspate yo no abhi hvaro dadhe svā tam marmartu ducchunā harasvatī ||
  • tvaṁ no gopāḥ: This phrase translates to "You, O protector" or "You, O shepherd." In this context, it refers to a divine figure who guides and protects. In real life, this can be likened to a mentor or a leader who provides guidance and support to individuals or communities.
  • pathikṛd vicakṣaṇas: This means "the wise one who knows the path." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's journey. For example, a seasoned traveler or an experienced professional can be seen as a "pathikṛd vicakṣaṇas," guiding others through their expertise.
  • tava vratāya: This translates to "for your vow" or "for your promise." It signifies commitment and dedication to a cause or principle. In everyday life, this can be related to individuals who uphold their promises, such as a parent’s commitment to their child's education or a leader's dedication to their community.
  • matibhir jarāmahe: This means "we meditate with our minds." It highlights the importance of focused thought and intention in achieving goals. For instance, students preparing for exams often meditate or concentrate on their studies to achieve success.
  • bṛhaspate: Referring to the deity of wisdom and eloquence, this term invokes the idea of seeking divine guidance in matters of intellect and communication. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals seek advice from wise elders or experts before making important decisions.
  • yo no abhi hvaro dadhe: This translates to "who bestows upon us." It signifies the act of giving or granting blessings. In practical terms, this can be related to mentors or leaders who provide opportunities and resources to help others succeed.
  • svā tam marmartu: This means "may he protect us." It emphasizes the desire for safety and security. In real life, this can be seen in the protective instincts of parents towards their children or the efforts of communities to safeguard their members.
  • ducchunā harasvatī: This phrase can be interpreted as "with great strength." It signifies the power and resilience needed to overcome challenges. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by individuals who face adversity with courage and determination, such as activists fighting for social justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance, wisdom, and protection from a higher power. It emphasizes the importance of commitment, focused intention, and the strength to overcome challenges. In real life, these principles are applicable in various scenarios, such as in education, leadership, and personal development. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can navigate their paths with wisdom and resilience, fostering a supportive and protective environment for themselves and others.
