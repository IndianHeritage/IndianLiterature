वृकश्चिदस्य वारण उरामथिरा वयुनेषु भूषति |सेमं नः स्तोमं जुजुषाण आ गहिंद्र प्र चितरया धियā ||
vṛkaś cid asya vāraṇa urāmathir ā vayuneṣu bhūṣati | semaṁ naḥ stomaṁ jujuṣāṇa ā gahīndra pra citrayā dhiyā ||
  • vṛkaś cid asya vāraṇa urāmathir ā vayuneṣu bhūṣati
    • vṛkaś: This word can be interpreted as "wolf" or "predator," symbolizing strength and ferocity.
    • cid: This means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the certainty of the statement.
    • asya: Translates to "of this," indicating possession or relation.
    • vāraṇa: Refers to "protection" or "guardian," suggesting a protective force.
    • urāmathir: This can be understood as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one," indicating a source of strength.
    • ā vayuneṣu: This phrase means "in the auspiciousness," referring to positive or beneficial qualities.
    • bhūṣati: This means "adorns" or "decorates," suggesting enhancement or beautification.
  • semaṁ naḥ stomaṁ jujuṣāṇa
    • semaṁ: This translates to "safety" or "protection," indicating a desire for security.
    • naḥ: Means "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
    • stomaṁ: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," suggesting a form of worship or acknowledgment.
    • jujuṣāṇa: This means "desiring" or "longing for," indicating a yearning for something.
  • ā gahīndra pra citrayā dhiyā
    • ā: This can mean "from" or "towards," indicating direction.
    • gahīndra: Refers to "the great lord" or "the powerful one," suggesting a divine or supreme being.
    • pra: This means "before" or "in front of," indicating precedence.
    • citrayā: This translates to "with variety" or "with brilliance," suggesting diversity and richness.
    • dhiyā: Refers to "intellect" or "understanding," indicating wisdom or insight.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and communal safety. It reflects a desire for divine guidance and wisdom in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of strength and protection, individuals can seek inner strength and resilience in their personal journeys.
  • Community Support: The collective aspect of "naḥ" highlights the importance of community and support systems in achieving safety and well-being.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of divine guidance can be seen in various spiritual practices where individuals seek wisdom and protection in their lives.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, community, and divine guidance in achieving a harmonious and secure life.
