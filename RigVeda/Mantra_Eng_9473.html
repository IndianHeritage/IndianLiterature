कुविद अङ्ग प्रति यथा चिद अस्य नः सजात्यस्य मरुतो बुबोधथ |नाभा यत्र प्रथमं संनसामहे तत्र जामित्वम् अदितिर दधातु नः ||
kuvid aṅga prati yathā cid asya naḥ sajātyasya maruto bubodhatha | nābhā yatra prathamaṁ saṁnasāmahe tatra jāmitvam aditir dadhātu naḥ ||
  • kuvid aṅga prati yathā: This phrase can be interpreted as "as the limbs of a body are connected." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe, much like how different parts of a body work together harmoniously.
  • cid asya naḥ sajātyasya: This translates to "may we be aware of our shared essence." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing our commonality and unity with others, regardless of differences.
  • maruto bubodhatha: Here, "maruto" refers to the winds or the forces of nature, and "bubodhatha" means "may they awaken." This suggests a call to the natural forces to inspire and enlighten us, urging us to be aware of the world around us.
  • nābhā yatra prathamaṁ: This can be understood as "where the center is first." It points to the idea of a central truth or core principle that guides our actions and thoughts.
  • saṁnasāmahe: This means "we come together." It highlights the importance of unity and collaboration among individuals and communities.
  • tatra jāmitvam: This translates to "there, let there be stability." It signifies the need for a solid foundation in our lives, whether it be in relationships, communities, or personal beliefs.
  • aditir dadhātu naḥ: "May the infinite (Aditi) grant us." Aditi represents the boundless and the nurturing aspect of the universe, suggesting that we seek blessings from the infinite for our growth and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of unity, interconnectedness, and the pursuit of stability in life. It serves as a reminder that we are all part of a larger whole, and our actions should reflect this understanding. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in workplaces, promoting community service, or encouraging environmental stewardship. By recognizing our shared essence and working together towards common goals, we can create a more harmonious and stable society. The invocation of Aditi signifies the importance of seeking guidance and support from the universe, reminding us to remain grounded and connected in our endeavors.
