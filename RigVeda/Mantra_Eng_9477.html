एवā प्लेटेः सूनुर अवीवृद्धद् वो विश्व आदित्यā अदिते मनिषी |ईशानासो नरो अमर्त्येनास्तावी जनो दिव्यो गयेन ||
evā plateḥ sūnur avīvṛdhad vo viśva ādityā adite manīṣī | īśānāso naro amartyenāstāvi jano divyo gayena ||
  • evā plateḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "thus, from the plate" or "from the dish." In a broader sense, it signifies the source or origin of nourishment and sustenance.
  • sūnur avīvṛdhad: This translates to "the son who has not been diminished." It suggests the idea of growth, prosperity, and the continuation of lineage or knowledge without decline.
  • vo viśva ādityā: This means "to you, O all-pervading Adityas." The Adityas are a group of solar deities in Vedic tradition, representing various aspects of the sun and cosmic order.
  • adite manīṣī: This can be understood as "the wise one, born of Aditi." Aditi is the mother of the gods and symbolizes infinity and the boundless universe. This phrase emphasizes wisdom and the divine connection to the cosmos.
  • īśānāso naro: This translates to "the men who are the lords." It refers to individuals who possess authority, leadership, or mastery over their circumstances.
  • amartyenāstāvi: This means "immortal, they are." It signifies the eternal nature of the spirit or soul, transcending physical existence.
  • jano divyo gayena: This translates to "the divine people, through the song." It suggests that through expression, creativity, or art (symbolized by 'song'), individuals can connect with the divine or higher consciousness.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of nourishment, growth, wisdom, leadership, and the eternal nature of the soul. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In family and community settings, it emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships and ensuring the growth of future generations.
  • In leadership roles, it highlights the responsibility of leaders to guide and inspire others, ensuring that their influence is positive and enduring.
  • In creative pursuits, it encourages individuals to express themselves through art, music, or literature, fostering a connection to the divine and the universal truths.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of wisdom, and the pursuit of a higher purpose through our actions and expressions.
