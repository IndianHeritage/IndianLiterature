यद दधिषे मनस्यसि मन्दानः प्रेद इयक्षसि |मा तत कर इन्द्र मृळय ||
yad dadhiṣe manasyasi mandānaḥ pred iyakṣasi | mā tat kar indra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • dadhiṣe: you bestow
    • manasyasi: in your mind
    • mandānaḥ: those who are weak or feeble
    • pred: to be protected or saved
    • iyakṣasi: you are capable of
    • mā: do not
    • tat: that
    • kar: do
    • indra: O Indra (a deity)
    • mṛḻaya: please soothe or calm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, a prominent deity in the Rigveda, known for his strength and power.
    • The speaker is requesting Indra to bestow kindness and protection upon those who are weak or vulnerable.
    • It emphasizes the importance of compassion and support for the less fortunate in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when a natural disaster strikes, it is crucial for the stronger members to support those who are affected, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In workplaces, leaders are often called to uplift and protect their team members, especially those who may be struggling with their tasks.
    • In personal relationships, being there for friends or family during tough times embodies the spirit of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of compassion, protection, and support for the weak and vulnerable in society.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength should be used to uplift others rather than dominate them.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to foster an environment of care and support, ensuring that no one is left behind.
