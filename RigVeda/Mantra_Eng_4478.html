द्युताानं वो अतिथिं स्वर्णरम् अग्निं होतारम् मनुष्यः स्वध्वरम् |विप्रं न द्युक्षवचसं सुवृक्तिभिर हव्यवाहम् अरतिं देवम् ऋञ्जसे ||
dyutānaṁ vo atithiṁ svarṇaram agniṁ hotāram manuṣaḥ svadhvaram | vipraṁ na dyukṣavacasaṁ suvṛktibhir havyavāham aratiṁ devam ṛñjase ||
  • dyutānaṁ: This word refers to the "shining" or "radiant" aspects, often associated with light or brilliance.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating a direction of reverence or offering.
  • atithiṁ: This translates to "guest," emphasizing the importance of hospitality and welcoming others.
  • svarṇaram: This means "golden," symbolizing wealth, prosperity, and purity.
  • agniṁ: Refers to "fire," which is a sacred element in Vedic rituals, representing transformation and energy.
  • hotāram: This means "the one who invokes," referring to the priest or the person performing the ritual.
  • manuṣaḥ: This translates to "human" or "mankind," highlighting the role of humans in the cosmic order.
  • svadhvaram: This means "self-formed" or "self-created," indicating the importance of individual effort and self-reliance.
  • vipraṁ: Refers to a "sage" or "wise person," someone who possesses knowledge and insight.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • dyukṣavacasaṁ: This translates to "with the words of the wise," emphasizing the power of speech and wisdom.
  • suvṛktibhir: This means "with good utterances," highlighting the importance of positive and constructive communication.
  • havyavāham: Refers to "the one who carries offerings," indicating the act of giving and sharing.
  • aratiṁ: This translates to "discontent" or "dissatisfaction," pointing to the human condition of seeking fulfillment.
  • devam: This means "divine" or "god," representing the higher powers and spiritual aspects of life.
  • ṛñjase: This translates to "to please" or "to satisfy," indicating the goal of the offerings and prayers.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of hospitality, the sacredness of fire, and the importance of wisdom and positive communication in human life. It highlights the interconnectedness of humans with the divine and the cosmic order. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Practicing hospitality by welcoming guests with warmth and generosity.
  • Engaging in rituals that honor the elements, particularly fire, as a source of transformation and energy.
  • Valuing the wisdom of sages and incorporating their teachings into daily life.
  • Fostering positive communication and constructive dialogue in personal and professional relationships.
  • Recognizing the importance of self-reliance and individual effort in achieving personal goals.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values that promote harmony, prosperity, and spiritual fulfillment in our lives.
