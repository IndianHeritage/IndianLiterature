मन्त्र: वेदा यो वीनाम् पदम् अन्तरिक्षेण पतताम् | वेद नावः समुद्रियः ||
vedā yo vīnām padam antarikṣeṇa patatām | veda nāvaḥ samudriyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vedā yo vīnām padam antarikṣeṇa patatām | veda nāvaḥ samudriyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The one who knows the path of the flying beings in the sky, knows the ship that sails in the ocean."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vedā: Knowledge or wisdom.
    • yo: Who.
    • vīnām: Of the flying beings or birds.
    • padam: Path or way.
    • antarikṣeṇa: In the sky or space.
    • patatām: Of those who fly.
    • nāvaḥ: Ship or vessel.
    • samudriyaḥ: Of the ocean or sea.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in navigating both the physical and metaphysical realms.
    • The "flying beings" can be interpreted as the aspirations and goals we aim for in life, while the "ship" represents the means or tools we use to achieve those goals.
    • In real life, this can be likened to how individuals pursue their dreams (flying beings) using education, skills, and resources (the ship) to navigate through challenges (the ocean).
  • Real-Life Example:
    • A student who aspires to become a doctor (flying being) must acquire knowledge through education and training (the ship) to navigate the challenges of medical school and the healthcare field (the ocean).
    • Similarly, an entrepreneur with a vision (flying being) needs a solid business plan and resources (the ship) to successfully launch and sustain their business in a competitive market (the ocean).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of knowledge, aspiration, and the means to achieve one's goals.
    • It teaches us that understanding the path we wish to take and equipping ourselves with the right tools is essential for success in any endeavor.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom as a foundation for navigating life's complexities, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual pursuits.
