मन्त्र: ये त्रिंशति त्रयस् परो देवासो बर्हिर आसदन् |विदन्न अह द्वितासनन् ||
ye triṁśati trayas paro devāso barhir āsadan | vidann aha dvitāsanan ||
  • Mantra: "ye triṁśati trayas paro devāso barhir āsadan | vidann aha dvitāsanan ||"
  • Translation: "Those thirty-three divine beings who sit on the highest seat, knowing the one who is beyond duality."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: those
    • triṁśati: thirty-three
    • trayas: three
    • paro: supreme or beyond
    • devāso: divine beings or gods
    • barhir: seat or place
    • āsadan: sitting or residing
    • vidann: knowing or understanding
    • aha: I or the self
    • dvitāsanan: one who is beyond duality
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra refers to the thirty-three divine beings, which in Vedic tradition are often associated with the various aspects of existence and the cosmos.
    • The number thirty-three can be interpreted as representing the three realms of existence (heaven, earth, and the underworld) multiplied by the three main deities (Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva), symbolizing the interconnectedness of all life.
    • The phrase "who sit on the highest seat" suggests a position of authority and wisdom, indicating that these divine beings have a comprehensive understanding of the universe.
    • "Knowing the one who is beyond duality" points to the realization of a higher truth that transcends the dualities of existence, such as good and evil, pleasure and pain, or life and death.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. For instance, scientists and philosophers strive to understand the fundamental truths of the universe, much like the divine beings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who understand the bigger picture and the interconnectedness of their decisions with the well-being of their employees and the environment embody the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek to transcend their ego and dualistic thinking often find peace and clarity, aligning with the idea of knowing the one who is beyond duality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the interconnectedness of all beings and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond superficial differences and dualities, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal growth, leadership, and community building, promoting a holistic approach to existence.
