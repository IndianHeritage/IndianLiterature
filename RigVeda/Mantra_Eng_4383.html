त्वां हि स्मा चर्षणयो यज्ञेभिर गीरभिर ईळते |त्वां वाजी यात्य अवृको रजस्तूर विश्वचर्षणिः ||
tvāṁ hi ṣmā carṣaṇayo yajñebhir gīrbhir īḻate | tvāṁ vājī yāty avṛko rajastūr viśvacarṣaṇiḥ ||
  • tvāṁ hi ṣmā carṣaṇayo: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, you are the one who is invoked in the sacred chants." Here, "tvāṁ" refers to the divine or the ultimate reality, while "carṣaṇayo" signifies the sacred hymns or chants used in rituals.
  • yajñebhir gīrbhir īḻate: This means "You are praised by the offerings and the hymns." "Yajñebhir" refers to the sacrifices or offerings made during rituals, and "gīrbhir" indicates the hymns or praises sung in honor of the divine.
  • tvāṁ vājī yāty avṛko: This translates to "You, O divine, are the one who brings strength." "Vājī" means strength or vigor, and "avṛko" can be interpreted as the one who is unrestrained or free, suggesting that the divine provides boundless energy and power.
  • rajastūr viśvacarṣaṇiḥ: This phrase can be understood as "You are the one who encompasses all realms." "Rajastūr" refers to the essence of activity and dynamism, while "viśvacarṣaṇiḥ" indicates the universal nature of the divine, suggesting that it pervades all aspects of existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of the divine in rituals and the power of sacred chants. It highlights the relationship between the divine and the practitioners, illustrating how offerings and hymns invoke strength and universal presence. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal or communal rituals where individuals seek strength, guidance, and connection with the divine. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and reverence in our actions, encouraging us to recognize the sacred in our everyday lives and to seek empowerment through our spiritual practices.
