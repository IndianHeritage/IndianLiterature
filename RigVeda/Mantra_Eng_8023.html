मन्त्र: एते सोमास इन्दवः प्रयस्वन्तश्चमू सुताः | इन्द्रं वर्धन्ति कर्मभिः ||
ete somāsa indavaḥ prayasvantaś camū sutāḥ | indraṁ vardhanti karmabhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ete somāsa indavaḥ prayasvantaś camū sutāḥ | indraṁ vardhanti karmabhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "These are the invigorating soma juices, the sons of the Indus, who, through their efforts, enhance Indra with their deeds."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • somāsa: Soma juices, which are considered sacred and invigorating
    • indavaḥ: Sons of Indus, referring to the divine or celestial beings
    • prayasvantaś: Those who are vigorous or full of energy
    • camū: Armies or groups, often referring to collective efforts
    • sutāḥ: Sons or offspring, indicating lineage or results of actions
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • vardhanti: They enhance or increase
    • karmabhiḥ: By deeds or actions
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks to the power of collective effort and the importance of actions in enhancing divine qualities.
    • Soma, in Vedic tradition, symbolizes not just a drink but also the essence of vitality and inspiration.
    • Indra represents strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome challenges, which can be seen in various aspects of life, such as leadership in a community or organization.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team working together on a project can be seen as the "camū" that enhances the "Indra" of the organization, which is its success and reputation.
    • In community service, volunteers coming together to help those in need exemplify the collective effort that enhances the well-being of society.
    • In sports, a team that trains hard and works together can achieve victory, enhancing the glory of their sport, akin to enhancing Indra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of collective effort and the impact of our actions on greater powers or outcomes.
    • It teaches us that through our deeds, we can enhance not only our own lives but also the lives of others and the world around us.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and groups to work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
