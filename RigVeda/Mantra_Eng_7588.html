त्वं ह त्यद वृषभ चर्षणीनां घनो वृत्राणां तविषो बभूथा |त्वं सिन्धूम् असृजस्तस्तभानान् त्वम् अपो अजयो दासपत्नीः ||
tvaṁ ha tyad vṛṣabha carṣaṇīnāṁ ghano vṛtrāṇāṁ taviṣo babhūtha | tvaṁ sindhūm̐r asṛjas tastabhānān tvam apo ajayo dāsapatnīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: indeed
    • tyad: that
    • vṛṣabha: the bull, symbolizing strength and power
    • carṣaṇīnāṁ: of the earth, or the one who moves on the earth
    • ghano: dense or thick
    • vṛtrāṇāṁ: of the Vritra, a demon representing obstacles or challenges
    • taviṣo: of the powerful
    • babhūtha: you became
    • sindhūm̐r: the oceans or rivers
    • asṛjas: you created
    • tastabhānān: you established
    • tvam: you
    • apo: waters
    • ajayo: invincible
    • dāsapatnīḥ: the mistress of the servants or the one who governs
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine power and strength that governs the natural world.
    • The reference to "vṛṣabha" symbolizes the strength and stability that is essential for life.
    • The mention of "vṛtrāṇāṁ" indicates the challenges and obstacles that one faces in life, akin to the struggles we encounter in our daily existence.
    • The creation of "sindhūm̐r" signifies the importance of water and rivers, which are vital for sustenance and growth.
    • The phrase "apo ajayo" emphasizes the invincibility of the divine force that governs these elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the strength of the earth ("vṛṣabha") is crucial for growing crops, which can be seen in the way farmers depend on fertile land.
    • In personal life, individuals often face "vṛtrāṇāṁ" or challenges, such as job loss or health issues, which require resilience and strength to overcome.
    • The significance of water ("sindhūm̐r") can be observed in communities that rely on rivers for drinking water, irrigation, and transportation.
    • The concept of "dāsapatnīḥ" can be related to leadership roles in organizations where effective governance is essential for success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of strength, challenges, and the natural elements that sustain life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the divine power that supports us in overcoming obstacles and achieving our goals.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and resilience while recognizing the importance of nature and community in their journey.
    • Ultimately, it signifies that with the right mindset and support, one can navigate through life's challenges and emerge victorious.
