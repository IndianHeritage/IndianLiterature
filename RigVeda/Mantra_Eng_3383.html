उत त्यं पुत्रं अग्रुवः परावृक्तं शतक्रतुः |उक्तेष्व इन्द्र आभजत ||
uta tyam putram agruvaḥ parāvṛktaṁ śatakratuḥ | uktheṣv indra ābhajat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • tyam: that
    • putram: son
    • agruvaḥ: who is born, who is produced
    • parāvṛktaṁ: who is well-protected, who is surrounded
    • śatakratuḥ: of the one with a hundred powers (referring to Indra)
    • uktheṣv: in the utterances, in the words
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • ābhajat: he worshipped, he adored
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the relationship between Indra, the king of gods, and his son, who is described as being well-protected and endowed with great powers.
    • The phrase "uta tyam putram agruvaḥ" emphasizes the significance of the son, suggesting that he is a product of divine will and strength.
    • The term "parāvṛktaṁ" indicates that this son is surrounded by protection, which can be interpreted as the blessings and support from the divine.
    • Indra's act of worshipping or adoring his son signifies the importance of familial bonds and the recognition of one's lineage and heritage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the relationship between a father and son is celebrated, where the father often plays a protective role, guiding and nurturing the son to become strong and capable.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of parental support in a child's life, where the blessings and guidance from parents can lead to the child's success and well-being.
    • In leadership roles, the idea of being well-protected and supported can be applied to mentorship, where experienced leaders guide and protect their mentees, helping them to grow and thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine protection and the importance of familial relationships, particularly between a father and son.
    • It serves as a reminder that support and guidance from elders can empower the younger generation to achieve their potential.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to value their relationships, seek guidance, and provide support to others, fostering a sense of community and strength.
