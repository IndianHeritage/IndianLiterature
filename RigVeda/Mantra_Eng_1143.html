स त्वं न इन्द्र सूर्ये सो अप्स्व अनागात्वा आ भज जीवशंसे |मांतराम् भुजम् आ रीरीषो नः श्रद्धितं ते महत इन्द्रियाय ||
sa tvaṁ na indra sūrye so apsv anāgāstva ā bhaja jīvaśaṁse | māntarām bhujam ā rīriṣo naḥ śraddhitaṁ te mahata indriyāya ||
  • sa tvaṁ: This phrase translates to "you" or "that you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra or a higher power.
  • na indra sūrye: "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, while "sūrye" means "sun." This part emphasizes the connection between divine forces and natural elements.
  • so apsv anāgāstva: "so" means "that," "apsv" refers to "waters," and "anāgāstva" can be interpreted as "without sin" or "pure." This suggests a plea for purity and divine grace.
  • ā bhaja jīvaśaṁse: "ā bhaja" means "to worship" or "to serve," and "jīvaśaṁse" refers to "the essence of life." This indicates a call to honor the life force and its divine origin.
  • māntarām bhujam: "māntarām" can be understood as "the one who is to be worshipped," and "bhujam" means "to be grasped" or "to be held." This signifies the importance of holding onto divine wisdom.
  • ā rīriṣo naḥ: "ā rīriṣo" translates to "from the desires" or "from the cravings," while "naḥ" means "for us." This part expresses a desire to be free from worldly attachments.
  • śraddhitaṁ te: "śraddhitaṁ" means "faith" or "trust," and "te" means "in you." This emphasizes the importance of having faith in the divine.
  • mahata indriyāya: "mahata" means "great" or "powerful," and "indriyāya" refers to "senses" or "perceptions." This suggests a connection between divine power and human perception.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual message that emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine, seeking purity, and cultivating faith. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as meditation practices, where individuals seek to purify their thoughts and intentions, or in moments of decision-making, where one might call upon divine guidance to navigate desires and attachments. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the divine, and the natural world, encouraging individuals to live with intention and reverence for the greater forces at play in their lives.
