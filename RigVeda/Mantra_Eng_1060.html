मन्त्र: का इमं वो निण्यम् ā चिकेट वत्सो मातृर जनयत स्वधाभिः |बह्वीनां गर्भो अपसाम् उपस्थाण महान् कविर निश्चरति स्वधावान् ||
ka imaṁ vo niṇyam ā ciketa vatso mātṝr janayata svadhābhiḥ | bahvīnāṁ garbho apasām upasthān mahān kavir niś carati svadhāvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ka: who
    • imaṁ: this
    • vo: for you
    • niṇyam: the one who is to be born
    • ā: indeed
    • ciketa: has thought or considered
    • vatso: O calf (a term of endearment, often referring to a child)
    • mātṝr: mother
    • janayata: gives birth
    • svadhābhiḥ: with her own will or intention
    • bahvīnāṁ: of many
    • garbho: the embryo or fetus
    • apasām: from the depths
    • upasthān: the coming forth or manifestation
    • mahān: great
    • kavir: the wise or the poet
    • niś: at night
    • carati: moves or wanders
    • svadhāvān: with his own will or intention
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of birth and creation, emphasizing the role of the mother and the divine will in bringing forth life.
    • The term "vatso" signifies a nurturing relationship, akin to how a mother cares for her child, highlighting the importance of maternal love and intention in the act of creation.
    • The phrase "bahvīnāṁ garbho" suggests that from many potentialities, a single life emerges, which can be likened to the myriad choices and paths in life that lead to a specific outcome.
    • The reference to the "mahān kavir" indicates a higher wisdom or divine intelligence that guides the process of creation, akin to how nature operates with intricate laws and patterns.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of parenting, this mantra can be reflected in the nurturing environment that parents create for their children, emphasizing the importance of intention and love in raising a child.
    • In a broader sense, it can be applied to the creative processes in various fields, such as art, science, and innovation, where the initial idea (the "garbho") is nurtured and brought to fruition through careful thought and intention.
    • In nature, the cycle of life can be observed where many seeds (potential lives) are sown, but only a few grow into mature plants, illustrating the selective process of creation and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the profound significance of creation, emphasizing the roles of intention, love, and divine wisdom in the process of birth and life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of nurturing relationships, whether in family, community, or the natural world.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to approach their creative endeavors and relationships with mindfulness and intention, recognizing the potential for greatness that lies within each moment of creation.
