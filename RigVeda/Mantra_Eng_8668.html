मन्त्र: क्रत्वा शुक्रेभिर अक्षभिर ऋणोर अप व्रजं दिवः |हिन्वन्न ऋतस्य दीधितिम प्राध्वरे ||
kratvā śukrebhir akṣabhir ṛṇor apa vrajaṁ divaḥ | hinvann ṛtasya dīdhitim prādhvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kratvā: By the power of action or effort.
    • śukrebhir: With the pure or auspicious.
    • akṣabhir: With the eyes or vision.
    • ṛṇor: Of the debt or obligation.
    • apa: Away or to remove.
    • vrajaṁ: To go or to move.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or divine.
    • hinvann: Invoking or calling forth.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or cosmic order.
    • dīdhitim: The brilliance or radiance.
    • prādhvare: In the context of the priestly or sacred.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of taking action with purity and clarity of vision.
    • It suggests that by performing righteous actions, one can remove the burdens of obligations and debts.
    • The invocation of divine brilliance signifies the pursuit of truth and cosmic order in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when individuals work with integrity and clarity, they often find success and fulfillment, akin to removing the burdens of their responsibilities.
    • In personal relationships, approaching conflicts with a pure heart and clear vision can lead to resolution and harmony, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community service, acting with the intention of uplifting others can create a ripple effect of positivity, embodying the principles of truth and cosmic order.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of righteous action and the importance of purity in our intentions.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for clarity and truth in their endeavors, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can help individuals navigate challenges, foster positive relationships, and contribute to a harmonious society.
