ऊर्जो नपातं स हिनायम् अस्मयुर दाशेम हव्यदातये |भुवद वाजेष्व अविता भुवद वृध उता त्रातā तनूनाम् ||
ūrjo napātaṁ sa hināyam asmayur dāśema havyadātaye | bhuvad vājeṣv avitā bhuvad vṛdha uta trātā tanūnām ||
  • ūrjo: This word translates to "energy" or "vitality." It signifies the life force that sustains all beings.
  • napātaṁ: This means "descendant" or "offspring." It refers to the continuation of life and the generational transfer of energy.
  • sa hināyam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be free from harm." It emphasizes the need for safety and security in life.
  • asmayur: This term refers to "stone" or "rock," symbolizing strength and stability.
  • dāśema: This means "may we attain" or "may we achieve." It expresses a desire for success and fulfillment.
  • havyadātaye: This translates to "for the offering" or "for the divine." It indicates the importance of devotion and gratitude in life.
  • bhuvad: This word means "may it be" or "let it be." It reflects a hopeful outlook towards the future.
  • vājeṣv: This refers to "in the chariots" or "in the vehicles of strength." It symbolizes the means through which we achieve our goals.
  • avitā: This means "protector" or "guardian." It highlights the need for guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • bhuvad vṛdha: This phrase can be interpreted as "may it grow" or "may it prosper." It signifies the aspiration for growth and abundance.
  • uta: This means "or" and serves as a conjunction, linking ideas together.
  • trātā: This translates to "savior" or "rescuer." It emphasizes the importance of having a protective force in our lives.
  • tanūnām: This means "of the bodies" or "of the beings." It refers to the physical existence and the need for care and protection.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, energy, protection, and the pursuit of success. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of vitality, safety, and the divine, urging individuals to seek strength and guidance in their endeavors. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our physical and spiritual well-being while striving for growth and fulfillment.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use the principles of this mantra to focus on their personal growth, seeking energy and vitality in their daily lives.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on protection and guidance can inspire communities to come together to support one another, ensuring safety and well-being for all members.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be incorporated into meditation or prayer, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and a reminder of the importance of gratitude.
  • Goal Setting: The aspiration for success and growth can motivate individuals to set and achieve their personal and professional goals.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the vital forces at play in our lives and the importance of seeking balance, protection, and growth in all aspects of existence.
