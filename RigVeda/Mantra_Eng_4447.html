आ यः पप्रौ जायमान उर्वी दूरदृष्टा भासा कृष्णाध्वा |अध बहु चित तम ऊर्म्यायास तिरः शोचिषा ददृशे पावकः ||
ā yaḥ paprau jāyamāna urvī dūredṛśā bhāsā kṛṣṇādhvā | adha bahu cit tama ūrmyāyās tiraḥ śociṣā dadṛśe pāvakaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yaḥ: That which
    • paprau: has been heard or perceived
    • jāyamāna: being born or arising
    • urvī: the vastness or the earth
    • dūredṛśā: with a distant vision
    • bhāsā: light or illumination
    • kṛṣṇādhvā: the dark path or the path of ignorance
    • adha: below
    • bahu: many or numerous
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • tama: darkness
    • ūrmyāyās: waves or currents
    • tiraḥ: beyond or across
    • śociṣā: with brightness or purity
    • dadṛśe: has been seen or perceived
    • pāvakaḥ: the purifying fire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the emergence of light and consciousness from the vastness of the earth.
    • It highlights the contrast between the dark path of ignorance and the illumination that comes from awareness.
    • The reference to "pāvakaḥ" signifies the purifying nature of fire, which can be seen as a metaphor for knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal growth, individuals often experience a journey from ignorance (darkness) to knowledge (light). For instance, someone may start with misconceptions about a subject and, through study and experience, gain clarity and understanding.
    • In a broader societal context, communities may face challenges (darkness) but can overcome them through education and awareness, leading to progress and enlightenment.
    • The metaphor of fire can be related to the transformative power of knowledge, much like how fire transforms raw materials into something useful.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and awareness to dispel ignorance and darkness in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that enlightenment is a journey that requires effort and the willingness to perceive beyond the surface.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to pursue education, self-awareness, and personal development, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
