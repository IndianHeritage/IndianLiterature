इमां त इन्द्र सुष्टुतिं विप्र इयर्ति धीतिभिः |जामिम पदेव पिप्रतीं प्राध्वरे ||
imāṁ ta indra suṣṭutiṁ vipra iyarti dhītibhiḥ | jāmim padeva pipratīm prādhvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imāṁ: this
    • ta: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • suṣṭutiṁ: excellent praise
    • vipra: the wise or learned one
    • iyarti: may invoke or inspire
    • dhītibhiḥ: with thoughts or intellect
    • jāmim: the one who is born or produced
    • padeva: like a foot or step
    • pipratīm: the one who is to be praised
    • prādhvare: in the act of worship or offering
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, to inspire and invoke the wisdom of the learned. It emphasizes the importance of excellent praise and the role of intellect in worship. The mantra suggests that through thoughtful invocation, one can connect with the divine and receive blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the wisdom of their teams to make informed decisions. This is akin to calling upon Indra for guidance.
    • In educational institutions, teachers inspire students to think critically and creatively, paralleling the invocation of intellect in the mantra.
    • During rituals or prayers, individuals often seek divine inspiration to guide their actions, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Significance and Application: The mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and thoughtful praise in connecting with the divine. It teaches that invoking higher intellect can lead to better outcomes in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional success, and spiritual fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in decision-making processes, educational pursuits, and during moments of reflection or prayer, reminding us to seek wisdom and clarity in our actions.
