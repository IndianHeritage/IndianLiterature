त्वं वरो सुषाम्णे 'ग्ने जनाय चोदय |सदा वसो रातिं यविष्ठ शाश्वते ||
tvaṁ varo suṣāmṇe 'gne janāya codaya | sadā vaso rātiṁ yaviṣṭha śaśvate ||
  • tvaṁ varo suṣāmṇe 'gne janāya codaya
    • tvaṁ - You
    • varo - the best or the most excellent
    • suṣāmṇe - of good fame or auspicious
    • 'gne - O Agni (the fire god)
    • janāya - for the people or for humanity
    • codaya - inspire or motivate
  • sadā vaso rātiṁ yaviṣṭha śaśvate
    • sadā - always
    • vaso - may you dwell
    • rātiṁ - in joy or happiness
    • yaviṣṭha - the best or the most excellent
    • śaśvate - eternal or everlasting

This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between the divine and humanity. The speaker is asking Agni to inspire and motivate the people towards good actions and to always dwell in joy and happiness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as the mantra calls for inspiration from Agni, leaders can seek to inspire their teams to achieve excellence and foster a positive environment.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on joy and happiness can be a guiding principle for community leaders to create inclusive and supportive spaces for all members.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek inspiration in their daily lives, striving for excellence and maintaining a positive outlook.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine inspiration and the pursuit of joy and excellence. It serves as a reminder that we can seek guidance from higher powers in our endeavors, whether in leadership, community, or personal growth. By embodying the principles of joy and motivation, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
