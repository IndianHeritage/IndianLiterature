मन्त्र: गिरा वज्रो न संभृतः सबलो अनपच्युतः | ववक्ष ऋष्वो अस्तृतः ||
girā vajro na sambhṛtaḥ sabalo anapacyutaḥ | vavakṣa ṛṣvo astṛtaḥ ||
  • girā: This word translates to "speech" or "voice." In a broader sense, it represents communication and the power of words. In real life, effective communication is crucial in various fields such as business, education, and personal relationships.
  • vajro: Meaning "thunderbolt" or "diamond," this term symbolizes strength, resilience, and indestructibility. For example, a person who overcomes significant challenges in life can be likened to a diamond, showcasing the idea that true strength is often forged through adversity.
  • na sambhṛtaḥ: This phrase means "not gathered" or "not collected." It suggests a state of being unrestrained or free. In a real-life context, this can relate to the idea of creativity and innovation, where ideas flow freely without being confined by rigid structures.
  • sabalo: This word can be interpreted as "full of strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience. For instance, individuals who advocate for social change often draw upon their inner strength to challenge the status quo.
  • anapacyutaḥ: This term means "not falling" or "not declining." It signifies stability and consistency. In practical terms, this can be seen in the context of personal growth, where individuals strive to maintain their values and principles despite external pressures.
  • vavakṣa: This word translates to "to express" or "to declare." It highlights the importance of articulating thoughts and feelings. In everyday life, expressing oneself clearly can lead to better understanding and stronger relationships.
  • ṛṣvo: This term refers to "sages" or "wise ones." It signifies the value of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, seeking guidance from mentors or learning from the experiences of others can lead to personal and professional growth.
  • astṛtaḥ: This word means "to be established" or "to be set." It represents the idea of grounding oneself in principles and beliefs. In practice, this can be seen in individuals who remain committed to their goals and values, even in challenging circumstances.

Conclusion: The mantra "girā vajro na sambhṛtaḥ sabalo anapacyutaḥ | vavakṣa ṛṣvo astṛtaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of strength, resilience, and the power of communication. It emphasizes the importance of expressing oneself freely while remaining grounded in wisdom and inner strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to harness our voice, embrace our resilience, and seek wisdom in our journey, ultimately leading to personal growth and meaningful connections with others.
