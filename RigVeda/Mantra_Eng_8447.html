वृषा मतीनां पवते विचक्षणः सोमो अहनः प्रतरीतोषसो दिवः |क्राणā सिंधूनां कलशाम् अवीवशद इन्द्रस्य हार्द्य आविशन् मनीषिभिः ||
vṛṣā matīnām pavate vicakṣaṇaḥ somo ahnaḥ pratarītoṣaso divaḥ | krāṇā sindhūnāṁ kalaśām̐ avīvaśad indrasya hārdy āviśan manīṣibhiḥ ||
  • vṛṣā matīnām: This phrase can be translated as "the strong one of thoughts." Here, "vṛṣā" signifies strength or power, while "matīnām" refers to thoughts or intellect. In real life, this can be related to individuals who possess strong analytical skills or wisdom, such as leaders or thinkers who guide others with their insights.
  • pavate vicakṣaṇaḥ: This means "the wise one who blows (or inspires)." "Pavate" refers to the act of blowing or inspiring, and "vicakṣaṇaḥ" means wise or perceptive. This can be likened to a mentor or a teacher who inspires students to think critically and creatively, much like how a breeze can inspire movement and change.
  • somo ahnaḥ pratarītoṣaso divaḥ: This translates to "the moon (Soma) of the day, who rises in the sky." "Soma" is often associated with the moon and represents nourishment and tranquility. This can be seen in the way the moon influences tides and human emotions, symbolizing calmness and reflection in our daily lives.
  • krāṇā sindhūnāṁ kalaśām̐ avīvaśad: This means "the vessels of the rivers are filled." "Sindhūnāṁ" refers to rivers, and "kalaśām̐" means vessels or containers. This can be related to the abundance of resources in nature, such as rivers providing water for agriculture, which is essential for sustenance and growth in communities.
  • indrasya hārdy āviśan manīṣibhiḥ: This translates to "the wise ones enter the heart of Indra." "Indra" is a deity associated with strength and power, and "manīṣibhiḥ" refers to the wise or learned individuals. This can be interpreted as the pursuit of wisdom and strength in leadership, where wise individuals contribute to the well-being of society, much like how leaders should embody the qualities of strength and compassion.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and inspiration in our lives. It highlights how strong thoughts and wise actions can lead to abundance and nourishment, both physically and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in leadership, education, and community building, where individuals are encouraged to think critically, inspire others, and contribute positively to society. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to embody these qualities, fostering a harmonious and prosperous environment for all.
