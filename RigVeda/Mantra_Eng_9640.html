कीं स्विद आसीद अधिष्ठानम् आरम्भणं कटमत स्वित कथासीत | यतो भूमिं जनयन विश्वकर्मा विं द्यां और्णोन महिना विश्वचक्षाः ||
kiṁ svid āsīd adhiṣṭhānam ārambhaṇaṁ katamat svit kathāsīt | yato bhūmiṁ janayan viśvakarmā vi dyām aurṇon mahinā viśvacakṣāḥ ||
  • kiṁ svid āsīd adhiṣṭhānam: This phrase translates to "What was the foundation?" It prompts us to reflect on the origins and the basis of existence. In real life, this can relate to questioning the foundations of our beliefs, values, and actions.
  • ārambhaṇaṁ katamat svit kathāsīt: This means "What was the beginning of creation?" It encourages us to explore the stories and narratives that shape our understanding of the world. For example, in science, the Big Bang theory serves as a narrative for the beginning of the universe, while in various cultures, creation myths provide insight into their worldview.
  • yato bhūmiṁ janayan: This translates to "From where did the earth arise?" It signifies the inquiry into the origins of our planet and existence. In a practical sense, this can relate to environmental awareness and the importance of understanding our planet's resources and ecosystems.
  • viśvakarmā: This term refers to the "Universal Architect" or "Cosmic Creator." It symbolizes the creative force behind the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the role of innovators and creators in society who shape our world through their ideas and inventions.
  • vi dyām aurṇon mahinā: This phrase means "and the sky with its vastness." It highlights the grandeur of the universe and the interconnectedness of all things. In everyday life, this can remind us of our place in the cosmos and the importance of humility in the face of nature's vastness.
  • viśvacakṣāḥ: This translates to "the all-seeing." It suggests a perspective that encompasses all aspects of existence. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our daily lives, encouraging us to see beyond our immediate surroundings and consider the broader implications of our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra invites us to reflect on the origins of existence, the foundations of our beliefs, and the interconnectedness of all things. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of curiosity and awareness about our place in the universe. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, innovation, and personal growth. By understanding the roots of our existence and the stories that shape us, we can make more informed decisions and contribute positively to the world around us.
