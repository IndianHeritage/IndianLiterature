यो मार्त्येष्व अमृत ऋतावा देवो देवेष्व अरतिर निधायि |होतā यजिष्ठो मह्ना शुचध्यै हव्यैर अग्निर मनुषा ईरयध्यै ||
yo martyeṣv amṛta ṛtāvā devo deveṣv aratir nidhāyi | hotā yajiṣṭho mahnā śucadhyai havyair agnir manuṣa īrayadhyai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • martyeṣv: among mortals
    • amṛta: immortal
    • ṛtāvā: the one who is righteous
    • devo: the divine
    • deveṣv: among the gods
    • aratir: non-attachment
    • nidhāyi: establishes
    • hotā: the one who invokes
    • yajiṣṭho: the most excellent in sacrifice
    • mahnā: with great power
    • śucadhyai: for purity
    • havyair: with offerings
    • agnir: fire
    • manuṣa: among humans
    • īrayadhyai: may you inspire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities that one should aspire to embody while living among mortals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of righteousness (ṛtāvā) and non-attachment (aratir) in one's life.
    • The invocation of fire (agnir) symbolizes the transformative power of purity and sacrifice.
    • It suggests that through sincere offerings and actions, one can connect with the divine and inspire others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies righteousness and non-attachment can inspire their team to work ethically and collaboratively.
    • In personal relationships, practicing non-attachment can lead to healthier interactions, where love is given freely without expectations.
    • During community service, individuals who offer their time and resources selflessly can ignite a spirit of giving and inspire others to contribute.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues that lead to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to cultivate qualities that connect us with the divine and with each other.
    • In real-life scenarios, embodying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
