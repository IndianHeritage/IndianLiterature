अभि प्र दद्रुर जनयो न गर्भं रथा इव प्र ययुः साकं अद्रयः |अतर्पयो विसृत उभ्ज ऊर्मीन त्वं वृताम् अरीणां इन्द्र सिंधून ||
abhi pra dadrur janayo na garbhaṁ rathā iva pra yayuḥ sākam adrayaḥ | atarpayo visṛta ubja ūrmīn tvaṁ vṛtām̐ ariṇā indra sindhūn ||
  • Mantra: "abhi pra dadrur janayo na garbhaṁ rathā iva pra yayuḥ sākam adrayaḥ | atarpayo visṛta ubja ūrmīn tvaṁ vṛtām̐ ariṇā indra sindhūn ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • pra: forth, forward
    • dadrur: they have come, they have arrived
    • janayo: of the people, of the beings
    • na: not
    • garbhaṁ: womb, source
    • rathā: like a chariot
    • iva: like, as if
    • pra yayuḥ: they have moved forward
    • sākam: together, in unison
    • adrayaḥ: the mountains, the strong ones
    • atarpayo: they have satisfied, they have pleased
    • visṛta: released, set free
    • ubja: born, produced
    • ūrmīn: waves, currents
    • tvaṁ: you
    • vṛtām̐: surrounded, enveloped
    • ariṇā: by the enemy, by the adversary
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sindhūn: rivers, streams
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the arrival of beings or people who are not born from a womb, likening their movement to that of a chariot.
    • The imagery of mountains suggests strength and stability, indicating that these beings are powerful and significant.
    • The phrase "they have satisfied" implies a sense of fulfillment or contentment, possibly referring to the blessings of the divine.
    • The mention of "waves" and "currents" can symbolize the flow of life and the challenges that come with it, while "you" refers to Indra, the deity who is invoked for protection and strength.
    • The overall essence of the mantra is a call for divine intervention and support in overcoming adversities, represented by the "enemy" or challenges faced in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a difficult project at work, one might invoke the spirit of teamwork and collaboration, akin to the "movement in unison" described in the mantra.
    • During times of personal struggle, individuals may seek strength and guidance from a higher power, similar to how the mantra calls upon Indra for support against adversities.
    • In community efforts, such as disaster relief, the collective action of people coming together can be seen as a manifestation of the mantra's themes of unity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like the waves and currents, life is dynamic and requires resilience and collaboration.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to seek strength in unity and divine guidance in their endeavors.
