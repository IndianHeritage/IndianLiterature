यत्र नारी अपच्यवम् उपच्यवं च शिक्षते |उलूकहलसुतानाम् अवेद व इन्द्र जलगुलः ||
yatra nāry apacyavam upacyavaṁ ca śikṣate | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yatra nāry apacyavam upacyavaṁ ca śikṣate | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Where women are honored, there the gods are pleased; but where they are not honored, no sacred rite yields rewards."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yatra: where
    • nāry: women
    • apacyavam: are honored
    • upacyavaṁ: are respected
    • ca: and
    • śikṣate: are educated
    • ulūkhalasutānām: of those who are wise (often interpreted as the learned or enlightened)
    • aved: knowledge or wisdom
    • v: indeed
    • indra: a deity, often associated with strength and power
    • jalgulaḥ: rewards or benefits
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of honoring and respecting women in society.
    • It suggests that the presence of respect and education for women leads to divine blessings and prosperity.
    • In contrast, where women are not honored, the sacred rituals and efforts made by individuals yield no fruitful results.
    • For example, in many cultures, when women are given equal opportunities in education and leadership, communities thrive, as seen in countries that prioritize gender equality.
    • Conversely, societies that marginalize women often face social and economic challenges, highlighting the mantra's relevance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for gender equality and respect for women.
    • It serves as a reminder that the well-being of society is closely tied to the treatment of women.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in advocating for women's rights, promoting education for girls, and ensuring their representation in decision-making processes.
    • Ultimately, honoring women leads to a more harmonious and prosperous society, aligning with the divine will as suggested in the mantra.
