ऋभुक्षाणो वाजाऽमाद्यध्वम् अस्मे नरो मगवानः सुतस्य |आ वोऽर्वाचः क्रतवो न यातां विभ्वो रथम नर्यं वर्तयन्तु ||
ṛbhukṣaṇo vājā mādayadhvam asme naro maghavānaḥ sutasya | ā vo 'rvācaḥ kratavo na yātāṁ vibhvo rathaṁ naryaṁ vartayantu ||
  • ṛbhukṣaṇo: This term refers to the "nourishing" or "sustaining" aspect, often associated with the divine or the forces of nature that provide sustenance.
  • vājā: This word means "strength" or "power," often linked to the idea of vitality and energy that drives action.
  • mādayadhvam: This can be interpreted as "let us rejoice" or "let us be filled with joy," indicating a call to celebrate and embrace life.
  • asme: This term translates to "for us," suggesting a collective experience or a shared purpose among individuals.
  • naro: Meaning "men" or "humans," it emphasizes the human aspect of the mantra, focusing on the community and its needs.
  • maghavānaḥ: This refers to "the powerful one" or "the mighty," often associated with deities or forces that bestow blessings and prosperity.
  • sutasya: This means "of the offspring" or "of the progeny," highlighting the importance of lineage and the continuation of life.
  • ā vo 'rvācaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "may your words be auspicious," indicating a desire for positive communication and intentions.
  • kratavo: Referring to "sacrifices" or "rituals," it underscores the significance of spiritual practices in achieving goals.
  • na yātāṁ: This means "may not go away," suggesting a plea for the continuity of blessings and prosperity.
  • vibhvo: This term translates to "of the vast" or "of the universe," indicating a connection to the greater cosmos and its energies.
  • rathaṁ: Meaning "chariot," it symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • naryaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the noble" or "the virtuous," emphasizing the importance of righteousness in one's actions.
  • vartayantu: This means "may they guide" or "may they lead," indicating a desire for divine guidance in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the need for divine blessings in our pursuits. It emphasizes the significance of joy, strength, and virtuous actions in achieving prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during communal gatherings, celebrations, or rituals where individuals seek to foster unity, express gratitude, and invoke blessings for a prosperous future. It serves as a reminder that our actions, intentions, and connections with others play a crucial role in shaping our lives and the world around us.
