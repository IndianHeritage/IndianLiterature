अधाकृत्नोः प्रथमं वीर्यं महद यद अस्याग्रे ब्रह्मणा शुष्मम् ऐरयः |रत्हेष्ठेन हर्यश्वेन विच्युताः प्र जीरयः सिस्रते सदृक पृतक ||
adhākṛṇoḥ prathamaṁ vīryam mahad yad asyāgre brahmaṇā śuṣmam airayaḥ | ratheṣṭhena haryaśvena vicyutāḥ pra jīrayaḥ sisrate sadhrya1k pṛthak ||
  • adhākṛṇoḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is below" or "the one who is humble." It signifies the importance of humility in one's actions and thoughts.
  • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word emphasizes the priority of certain values or actions in life.
  • vīryam: This translates to "strength" or "valor." It represents the inner strength and courage that individuals must cultivate to face challenges.
  • mahad: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this word refers to the vastness of the universe and the greatness of the divine.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the preceding concepts.
  • asyāgre: Translated as "in the beginning" or "at the forefront," it suggests the importance of foundational principles in life.
  • brahmaṇā: This term refers to "the divine" or "the ultimate reality," highlighting the spiritual aspect of existence.
  • śuṣmam: Meaning "dry" or "barren," it can symbolize a lack of spiritual or emotional nourishment.
  • airayaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to manifest," indicating the act of creation or realization.
  • ratheṣṭhena: This translates to "by the charioteer," symbolizing guidance and direction in one's journey through life.
  • haryaśvena: Meaning "by the horse," it represents the energy and drive needed to propel oneself forward.
  • vicyutāḥ: This term means "scattered" or "dispersed," indicating the challenges of maintaining focus and unity in life.
  • pra jīrayaḥ: Translated as "to revive" or "to rejuvenate," it emphasizes the importance of renewal and growth.
  • sisrate: This means "to flow" or "to stream," symbolizing the continuous nature of life and experiences.
  • sadhrya1k: This term can be interpreted as "together" or "in harmony," highlighting the significance of community and collaboration.
  • pṛthak: Meaning "separate" or "distinct," it suggests the individuality of each person's journey while still being part of a larger whole.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of humility, strength, and guidance in our lives. It teaches us that while we may face challenges and feel scattered, we must strive for renewal and work together in harmony. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in a workplace, community service, or personal growth. By recognizing the divine and the interconnectedness of our journeys, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
