सखाय आ नि शीदत पुनानाय प्र गायत |शिशुं न यज्ञैः परिभूषत श्रीये ||
sakhāya ā ni ṣīdata punānāya pra gāyata | śiśuṁ na yajñaiḥ pari bhūṣata śriye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhāya: Friends or companions
    • ā: towards
    • ni: down or below
    • ṣīdata: to sit or dwell
    • punānāya: for purification or renewal
    • pra: forth or forward
    • gāyata: to sing or chant
    • śiśuṁ: the child or offspring
    • na: not
    • yajñaiḥ: with sacrifices or offerings
    • pari: all around
    • bhūṣata: to adorn or decorate
    • śriye: for prosperity or wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of companionship and community in the process of renewal and purification.
    • The phrase "sakhāya ā ni ṣīdata" suggests that friends should come together and dwell in harmony.
    • "Punānāya pra gāyata" indicates that through collective singing or chanting, one can achieve a state of purification.
    • The mention of "śiśuṁ" highlights the significance of nurturing the younger generation, ensuring they are not just adorned with material wealth but also with spiritual and moral values.
    • The phrase "na yajñaiḥ pari bhūṣata" implies that true adornment comes not from mere rituals or sacrifices but from genuine relationships and community support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, friends and family coming together to support a child’s education can be seen as a practical application of this mantra. The collective effort to nurture the child’s growth is a form of purification and renewal.
    • In modern workplaces, teamwork and collaboration can be viewed through the lens of this mantra. When colleagues support each other, they create a harmonious environment that fosters personal and professional growth.
    • During festivals or community events, singing and celebrating together can serve as a reminder of the importance of unity and shared joy, aligning with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound impact of companionship and community in achieving personal and collective growth.
    • It teaches us that true wealth lies not in material possessions but in the relationships we build and the support we offer one another.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous society, where individuals thrive through mutual respect and collaboration.
