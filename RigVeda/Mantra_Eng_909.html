अश्वावति प्रथमो गोषु गच्छति सुप्रवीर इन्द्र मार्त्यस तवोतििभिः |तम इति पृणक्षि वसुनाभवiyasā सिन्धुम आपो यथाभितो विचेतसः ||
aśvāvati prathamo goṣu gacchati suprāvīr indra martyas tavotibhiḥ | tam it pṛṇakṣi vasunā bhavīyasā sindhum āpo yathābhito vicetasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvāvati: One who possesses horses or is associated with horses.
    • prathamo: The first or foremost.
    • goṣu: Among the cows or in the realm of cattle.
    • gacchati: Goes or moves.
    • suprāvīr: With great strength or valor.
    • indra: The king of gods, often associated with power and strength.
    • martyas: Mortals or human beings.
    • tavotibhiḥ: By your great powers or abilities.
    • tam: That or him.
    • it: Here or in this context.
    • pṛṇakṣi: You fill or you provide.
    • vasunā: With wealth or abundance.
    • bhavīyasā: With greatness or in a grand manner.
    • sindhum: The ocean or a large body of water.
    • āpo: Waters or streams.
    • yathābhito: From all sides or in all directions.
    • vicetasaḥ: With intelligence or wisdom.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities associated with strength, abundance, and wisdom.
    • The mention of "aśvāvati" and "goṣu" highlights the importance of horses and cattle in ancient society, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate power and authority, suggesting that divine support is essential for success in human endeavors.
    • The phrase "tavotibhiḥ" indicates that it is through divine powers that one can achieve greatness and abundance.
    • The imagery of the ocean and waters signifies the vastness of resources and opportunities available to those who seek them wisely.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of livestock (cattle and horses) is still relevant today, as they contribute to farming and economic stability.
    • In business, the mantra can be interpreted as a reminder to seek divine guidance and strength in pursuing ventures, much like how entrepreneurs often rely on their networks and resources.
    • The concept of wisdom in decision-making can be applied in various fields, such as education and leadership, where informed choices lead to success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine support, strength, and wisdom in achieving success and abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers and to recognize the resources available to us, whether they be material or intellectual.
    • In modern contexts, it encourages individuals to harness their strengths and seek wisdom in their pursuits, ensuring a balanced approach to achieving their goals.
