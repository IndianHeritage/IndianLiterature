इमां त्वं इन्द्र मीढ्वः सुपुत्रां सुभगां कृणु |दशास्यां पुत्रान् आ धेहि पतिम एकादशं कृधि ||
imāṁ tvam indra mīḍhvaḥ suputrāṁ subhagāṁ kṛṇu | daśāsyām putrān ā dhehi patim ekādaśaṁ kṛdhi ||
  • Transliteration: imāṁ tvam indra mīḍhvaḥ suputrāṁ subhagāṁ kṛṇu | daśāsyām putrān ā dhehi patim ekādaśaṁ kṛdhi ||
  • Meaning:
    • imāṁ: this
    • tvam: you
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • mīḍhvaḥ: may you bestow
    • suputrāṁ: good sons
    • subhagāṁ: auspicious
    • kṛṇu: create or grant
    • daśāsyām: in the tenfold (or ten) ways
    • putrān: sons
    • ā dhehi: grant or bestow
    • patim: a lord or master
    • ekādaśaṁ: eleven
    • kṛdhi: make or create
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in the Vedic pantheon, asking for the blessing of good and auspicious sons.
    • The phrase "daśāsyām putrān" suggests a request for sons who embody the qualities of strength and virtue, possibly referring to the ten directions or aspects of life.
    • The mention of "ekādaśaṁ" indicates a desire for abundance, specifically asking for eleven sons, which can symbolize prosperity and fulfillment in family life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the birth of children is celebrated as a significant blessing. This mantra reflects the universal desire for healthy and virtuous offspring.
    • Parents often pray for their children to grow up to be successful and morally upright individuals, similar to the request for "suputrāṁ subhagāṁ" in the mantra.
    • In modern contexts, this can also relate to the aspirations parents have for their children to excel in various fields, be it academics, sports, or arts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of family and the blessings associated with having children who are not only numerous but also virtuous and prosperous.
    • It serves as a reminder of the values of nurturing and guiding the next generation, ensuring they grow up to contribute positively to society.
    • In a broader sense, it can be applied to any endeavor where one seeks to cultivate success and virtue, whether in personal relationships, professional aspirations, or community involvement.
