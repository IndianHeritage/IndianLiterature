अश्विनाव एह गच्छतं नासत्यां मा वि वेनतम् |हंसाव इव पततम् आ सुतां उप ||
aśvināv eha gacchataṁ nāsatyā mā vi venatam | haṁsāv iva patatam ā sutām̐ upa ||
  • Mantra: "aśvināv eha gacchataṁ nāsatyā mā vi venatam | haṁsāv iva patatam ā sutām̐ upa ||"
  • Translation: "O Ashvins, come here; do not let the untruthful (Nāsatyā) deceive us. Like swans, may you swiftly come to us with your blessings."
  • Explanation:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Nāsatyā: This term refers to the untruthful or deceitful aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to seek truth and clarity in our actions and thoughts.
    • Swans: Swans are often seen as symbols of purity and grace. In this context, they represent the swift arrival of divine blessings and wisdom.
    • Invocation: The mantra is an invocation for the Ashvins to come and assist the seeker, emphasizing the importance of truth and the rejection of falsehood.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a medical context, one might call upon the Ashvins for healing and health, especially when facing a serious illness. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek truth in medical advice and treatment options.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be applied to encourage honesty and transparency, urging individuals to avoid deceit and foster trust.
    • In decision-making, invoking the Ashvins can symbolize the pursuit of clarity and wisdom, helping individuals navigate complex choices with integrity.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance in the pursuit of truth and health. It serves as a reminder to remain vigilant against deceit and to embrace the blessings of clarity and wisdom in our lives. The Ashvins, as embodiments of healing and duality, encourage us to balance our actions with truthfulness, making this mantra applicable in various real-life scenarios, from health to relationships and decision-making.
