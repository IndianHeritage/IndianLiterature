उरौ वा ये अन्तरिक्षे मदन्ति दिवो वा ये रोचने सन्ति देवाः |ऊमा वा ये सुहवासो यजत्रा आयेमिरे रथ्यो अग्ने अश्वाः ||
urau vā ye antarikṣe madanti divo vā ye rocane santi devāḥ | ūmā vā ye suhavāso yajatrā āyemire rathyo agne aśvāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • urau: in the heavens
    • vā: or
    • ye: those who
    • antarikṣe: in the atmosphere
    • madanti: rejoice
    • divo: of the sky
    • vā: or
    • ye: those who
    • rocane: in the shining
    • santi: exist
    • devāḥ: the gods
    • ūmā: the auspicious
    • vā: or
    • ye: those who
    • suhavāso: who dwell in beauty
    • yajatrā: in the sacrifices
    • āyemire: have come forth
    • rathyo: in the chariots
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • aśvāḥ: the horses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine beings, the gods, who exist in the heavens and the atmosphere, rejoicing in their celestial abode.
    • It highlights the beauty and auspiciousness of these divine entities, suggesting that they are present in the rituals and sacrifices performed by humans.
    • The mention of Agni, the fire god, signifies the importance of fire in Vedic rituals, where offerings are made to invoke divine blessings.
    • The reference to horses and chariots symbolizes strength, speed, and the journey of life, indicating that divine support is essential for success in endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in various aspects, such as starting a new venture or during significant life events like weddings.
    • Just as the gods are invoked in rituals, people today often seek spiritual support through prayer or meditation, believing that it brings peace and clarity.
    • The symbolism of horses and chariots can be seen in the context of personal growth and ambition, where individuals strive to achieve their goals with the help of their inner strength and external support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and human actions, illustrating that the gods are ever-present and supportive in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to acknowledge and honor the spiritual forces that guide us, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the universe.
    • In practical terms, it serves as a call to integrate spirituality into daily life, fostering a sense of purpose and direction in our pursuits.
