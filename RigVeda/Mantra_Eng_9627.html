प्र मातुः प्रतरं गुह्यं इच्छन् कुमारो न वीरुधः सर्पद उर्वीः |ससं न पक्वम अविदच्छुचन्तं रिरिह्वांसं रिप उपस्थे अन्तः ||
pra mātuḥ prataraṁ guhyam icchan kumāro na vīrudhaḥ sarpad urvīḥ | sasaṁ na pakvam avidac chucantaṁ ririhvāṁsaṁ ripa upasthe antaḥ ||
  • pra mātuḥ prataraṁ guhyam: This phrase can be translated as "the secret of the mother is to be revealed." It emphasizes the importance of maternal wisdom and the hidden knowledge that mothers possess. In real life, this can be seen in the way mothers often have an intuitive understanding of their children's needs and emotions.
  • icchan kumāro na vīrudhaḥ: This translates to "the young one desires and does not falter." It signifies the aspirations and determination of youth. For example, a young student pursuing their dreams of becoming a doctor showcases this desire and unwavering spirit.
  • sarpad urvīḥ: This means "the serpent of the earth." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent challenges or obstacles that one must navigate in life. For instance, the struggles faced by individuals in their careers can be likened to the serpent that one must confront and overcome.
  • sasaṁ na pakvam avidac: This phrase translates to "the one who is not yet ripe does not know." It suggests that maturity and experience are necessary for understanding certain truths. A real-life example could be a young entrepreneur who, despite having innovative ideas, lacks the experience to execute them effectively.
  • shucantaṁ ririhvāṁsaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the pure one who is eager." It highlights the importance of purity of intention and eagerness to learn. For instance, a volunteer working selflessly for a cause embodies this purity and eagerness to make a difference.
  • ripa upasthe antaḥ: This translates to "the enemy is within." It serves as a reminder that often our greatest challenges come from within ourselves, such as self-doubt or fear. A person struggling with imposter syndrome in their professional life exemplifies this concept.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound insights about life, emphasizing the significance of maternal wisdom, youthful aspirations, the challenges we face, the necessity of experience, the purity of intention, and the internal battles we must confront. In real-life scenarios, these themes resonate with individuals as they navigate their personal and professional journeys, reminding them of the importance of self-awareness, determination, and the wisdom that comes from experience. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal growth and understanding the complexities of human existence.
