आदित्याः विश्वे मरुतः च विश्वे देवाः च विश्व ऋभवः च विश्वे |इन्द्रो अग्निः अश्विनौ तुष्टुवानाः यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ādityā viśve marutaś ca viśve devāś ca viśva ṛbhavaś ca viśve | indro agnir aśvinā tuṣṭuvānā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Mantra: ādityā viśve marutaś ca viśve devāś ca viśva ṛbhavaś ca viśve | indro agnir aśvinā tuṣṭuvānā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Adityas, O all-encompassing Maruts, O all gods, O universal Ribhus, may you protect us always with your blessings."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Adityas: Refers to the solar deities, representing light, truth, and cosmic order. In real life, this can be likened to the sun's role in providing energy and life to the Earth.
    • Maruts: The storm deities, symbolizing power and strength. They can be compared to the forces of nature that can both create and destroy, reminding us of the balance in life.
    • Devas: The divine beings or gods, representing various aspects of life and nature. They can be seen as the guiding principles in our lives, akin to mentors or role models.
    • Ribhus: The divine artisans, symbolizing creativity and craftsmanship. This can relate to human innovation and the importance of skill in our daily lives.
    • Indra: The king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms, representing leadership and authority. In a real-world context, this can be likened to effective leadership in organizations.
    • Agni: The fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification. This can be compared to the role of fire in cooking and warmth, as well as the metaphorical fire of passion in our pursuits.
    • Aśvinā: The twin gods of medicine and health, representing healing and well-being. This can be related to the importance of health in our lives and the advancements in medical science.
    • Pāta: To protect or safeguard, emphasizing the need for security in our lives.
    • Svastibhiḥ: Blessings or auspiciousness, highlighting the importance of positive energy and good fortune.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer for protection and blessings from various divine forces. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence. By invoking these deities, the mantra seeks to create a harmonious balance in life.
  • Real-Life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals, ceremonies, or even in personal meditation to seek guidance and protection. It serves as a reminder to respect the forces of nature and the divine, encouraging individuals to live in harmony with their surroundings.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in all aspects of life. It teaches us the importance of gratitude, respect for nature, and the pursuit of harmony. By acknowledging the various forces at play in our lives, we can cultivate a sense of peace and well-being, making it applicable in both personal and communal contexts.
