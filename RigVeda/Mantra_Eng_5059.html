त्विषीमान्तो अध्वरस्येव दिद्युत तृषुच्यवसो जुह्वोऽनाग्निः |अर्चत्रयो धुनयो न वीराः भ्राजज्जन्मानो मरुतो अधृष्टाः ||
tviṣīmanto adhvarasyeva didyut tṛṣucyavaso juhvo3 nāgneḥ | arcatrayo dhunayo na vīrā bhrājajjanmāno maruto adhṛṣṭāḥ ||
  • tviṣīmanto: This word can be interpreted as "radiant" or "brilliant." It signifies the presence of light and energy, often associated with divine qualities.
  • adhvarasyeva: This term refers to the "sacrificial ritual" or "sacred act." It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the universe.
  • didyut: Meaning "shining" or "glowing," this word highlights the brilliance and illumination that comes from divine blessings.
  • tṛṣucyavaso: This can be understood as "those who are thirsty" or "those who seek." It represents the human condition of longing for spiritual fulfillment and knowledge.
  • juhvo3: This term relates to the act of offering or invoking, particularly in the context of a ritual or prayer.
  • nāgneḥ: Meaning "of the fire," this word symbolizes the sacred fire, which is central to many Vedic rituals and represents transformation and purification.
  • arcatrayo: This refers to "three forms of worship" or "three offerings," indicating the multifaceted nature of devotion and the various ways one can connect with the divine.
  • dhunayo: This term can be interpreted as "those who are moving" or "those who are active," suggesting the dynamic nature of life and spirituality.
  • na vīrā: Meaning "not the heroes," this phrase may imply that true strength lies not just in physical prowess but in spiritual wisdom and understanding.
  • bhrājajjanmāno: This can be translated as "those who shine" or "those who are born of light," indicating individuals who embody divine qualities and virtues.
  • maruto: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic mythology, this word symbolizes power, energy, and the forces of nature.
  • adhṛṣṭāḥ: This term means "unseen" or "invisible," suggesting that there are spiritual forces and energies at work that may not be immediately apparent to us.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine connection through rituals and offerings. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual thirst and the pursuit of enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in practices that foster spiritual growth, such as meditation, prayer, and community service. By recognizing the radiant qualities within ourselves and others, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of our place in the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies in our spiritual pursuits and the light we bring into the world.
