वृषा न क्रुद्धः पतयद राजा स्वायो आर्यपत्नीः अकृणोद इमा अपः |स सुन्वते मगवाँ जीरदानवे 'विन्दज ज्योतिः मनवे हविष्मते ||
vṛṣā na kruddhaḥ patayad rajaḥsv ā yo aryapatnīr akṛṇod imā apaḥ | sa sunvate maghavā jīradānave 'vindaj jyotir manave haviṣmate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: bull or strong being
    • na: not
    • kruddhaḥ: angry or wrathful
    • patayad: he who flies or moves
    • rajaḥsv: in the realm of the atmosphere
    • ā: towards
    • yo: who
    • aryapatnīr: the noble wife or the revered partner
    • akṛṇod: did not create or did not make
    • imā: these
    • apaḥ: waters or streams
    • sa: he
    • sunvate: who is praised or celebrated
    • maghavā: the powerful or the great one
    • jīradānave: the giver of life or vitality
    • 'vindaj: who brings forth or produces
    • jyotir: light or illumination
    • manave: for the mind or intellect
    • haviṣmate: for the one who offers or sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity, often interpreted as a divine force or deity, who is not angry and moves gracefully through the atmosphere.
    • The reference to "aryapatnīr" suggests a connection to noble or revered relationships, emphasizing harmony and respect.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of water ("apaḥ") as a life-giving force, which is celebrated and praised.
    • The mention of "maghavā" and "jīradānave" indicates the presence of a powerful force that provides life and vitality, akin to the sun or nature's energy.
    • Finally, the mantra connects light ("jyotir") with the mind ("manave"), suggesting that illumination and knowledge are essential for those who offer or sacrifice ("haviṣmate").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach challenges. Instead of reacting with anger, we can choose to respond with calmness and grace, much like the "vṛṣā" that moves through the atmosphere.
    • The importance of relationships is highlighted here; in our personal and professional lives, fostering respect and harmony can lead to more fruitful collaborations.
    • Water is a vital resource, and this mantra reminds us of its significance. For instance, in agriculture, understanding the importance of water management can lead to better crop yields and sustainability.
    • The concept of vitality can be seen in our health practices. Engaging in activities that promote physical and mental well-being can be viewed as a way of honoring the life-giving forces mentioned in the mantra.
    • Lastly, the connection between light and knowledge encourages us to seek enlightenment through education and self-improvement, which can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of calmness, respect in relationships, the life-giving properties of water, the importance of vitality, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • In real life, these principles can guide us in our interactions, decision-making, and personal growth, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
    • By embodying the teachings of this mantra, we can create a positive impact on ourselves and those around us, fostering a sense of community and well-being.
