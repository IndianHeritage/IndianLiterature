मन्त्र: दिवो न तुभ्यम् अन्व इन्द्र सत्रासूर्यं देवैभिर् धायि विश्वम् |अहिं यद् वृत्रम् अपो वव्रिवांसं हन् ऋजीषिण विष्णुना सचानः ||
divo na tubhyam anv indra satrāsuryaṁ devebhir dhāyi viśvam | ahiṁ yad vṛtram apo vavrivāṁsaṁ hann ṛjīṣin viṣṇunā sacānaḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Indra, may we not be deprived of your protection, as you are the one who sustains the universe with the light of the sun and the blessings of the gods. Just as you have vanquished the serpent Vritra, may we also overcome our obstacles with the strength of Vishnu."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or divine realm.
    • Na: Not.
    • Tubhyam: To you.
    • Anv: Following or in accordance with.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • Satrāsuryaṁ: The light of the sun, symbolizing knowledge and clarity.
    • Devebhir: By the gods.
    • Dhāyi: Sustainer or protector.
    • Viśvam: The universe or all that exists.
    • Ahiṁ: Serpent or obstacle.
    • Yad: That which.
    • Vṛtram: Vritra, the serpent representing chaos and obstacles.
    • Apo: From or away from.
    • Vavrivāṁsaṁ: Having overcome or vanquished.
    • Hann: To strike or defeat.
    • Ṛjīṣin: With strength or power.
    • Viṣṇunā: By Vishnu, the preserver and protector.
    • Sacānaḥ: With blessings or grace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power, similar to invoking Indra for protection.
    • In a community facing natural disasters, the collective effort to overcome challenges can be likened to the vanquishing of Vritra, where unity and strength lead to recovery and resilience.
    • In educational settings, students may face obstacles in their learning journey. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and clarity (Satrāsuryaṁ) to overcome these challenges.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and strength in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we can seek support from higher powers and work together to vanquish our personal Vritras. In real life, this can manifest in seeking guidance during tough times, fostering community support, and striving for knowledge and clarity in our pursuits. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal applicability to human struggles, encouraging resilience and unity in the face of adversity.
