आ च त्वां एता वृषणा वहातो हरि सखायाः सुदुराः स्वङ्गा |धानावद इन्द्रः सवनं जुषाणः सखा सख्युः श्रिणवद वन्दनानि ||
ā ca tvām etā vṛṣaṇā vahāto harī sakhāyā sudhurā svaṅgā | dhānāvad indraḥ savanaṁ juṣāṇaḥ sakhā sakhyuḥ śṛṇavad vandanāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā ca tvām: and you
    • etā: these
    • vṛṣaṇā: of the strong
    • vahāto: who carry
    • harī: O Lord
    • sakhāyā: friend
    • sudhurā: very difficult
    • svaṅgā: of the self
    • dhānāvad: like a treasure
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • savanaṁ: the offering
    • juṣāṇaḥ: enjoying
    • sakhā: friend
    • sakhyuḥ: of friends
    • śṛṇavad: listening
    • vandanāni: salutations
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, emphasizing the importance of friendship and support in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "O Lord, you who carry the strong" suggests that divine strength is essential in our lives, especially when facing difficulties.
    • The reference to "like a treasure" indicates that the blessings and support we receive from friends and the divine are invaluable.
    • Listening and acknowledging the contributions of friends and divine forces is crucial for personal growth and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, having supportive friends can make a significant difference, much like the divine support mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a professional setting, teamwork and collaboration can lead to overcoming challenges, reflecting the essence of friendship and support highlighted in the mantra.
    • During community service or social initiatives, the collective effort of individuals can be seen as a manifestation of the divine strength and friendship that the mantra speaks of.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support and friendship in navigating life's challenges.
    • It teaches us to value the relationships we have and to recognize the strength that comes from unity and collaboration.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, resilience, and a deeper appreciation for the connections we share with others.
