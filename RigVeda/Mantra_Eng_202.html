अधारयन्त वहनयोऽभजन्त सुखृत्यया |भागं देवेषु यज्ञियम् ||
adhārayanta vahnayo 'bhajanta sukṛtyayā | bhāgaṁ deveṣu yajñiyam ||
  • adhārayanta: This term can be translated as "supporting" or "upholding." It signifies the act of providing strength or stability.
  • vahnayo: This word refers to "the fires" or "the divine fire." In Vedic tradition, fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative element, representing energy and vitality.
  • 'bhajanta: This means "worshipping" or "serving." It indicates an act of devotion or reverence towards the divine.
  • sukṛtyayā: This term translates to "through good deeds" or "by virtuous actions." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct in one's life.
  • bhāgaṁ: This word means "portion" or "share." It signifies the idea of receiving or partaking in something valuable or sacred.
  • deveṣu: This refers to "the gods" or "divine beings." It highlights the connection between humans and the divine, suggesting a relationship of respect and devotion.
  • yajñiyam: This term can be understood as "sacrificial" or "pertaining to sacrifice." It reflects the concept of offering and the importance of rituals in Vedic culture.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to uphold the divine fire through virtuous actions and to partake in the blessings of the gods through sacrificial offerings. It emphasizes the significance of maintaining a connection with the divine through good deeds and rituals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Personal Development: By focusing on good deeds and ethical behavior, individuals can create a positive impact in their communities, much like how the divine fire symbolizes transformation and energy.
  • Community Service: Engaging in acts of service and charity can be seen as a way to "worship" the divine, as it reflects the essence of selflessness and compassion.
  • Spiritual Practices: Incorporating rituals and offerings in daily life can help individuals connect with their spiritual beliefs, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of upholding virtues and maintaining a connection with the divine through our actions. It encourages individuals to engage in good deeds and rituals, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can contribute positively to society and cultivate a deeper spiritual connection.
