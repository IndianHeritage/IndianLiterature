तिस्रो वाच इरयति प्र वह्निर ऋतस्य धीतिं ब्रह्मणो मनिषाम् |गावो यन्ति गोपतीं पृच्छमानाः सोमं यन्ति मतयो वावशानाः ||
tisro vāca īrayati pra vahnir ṛtasya dhītim brahmaṇo manīṣām | gāvo yanti gopatim pṛcchamānāḥ somaṁ yanti matayo vāvaśānāḥ ||
  • tisro vāca: This phrase translates to "three words" or "three utterances." In a broader context, it can refer to the three fundamental aspects of communication or expression in life.
  • īrayati: This means "to inspire" or "to invoke." It suggests the power of words to motivate and bring forth action or thought.
  • pra vahnir: "Pra" means "before" or "in front of," and "vahnir" refers to "fire." This can symbolize the illuminating power of knowledge and truth, akin to how fire brings light and warmth.
  • ṛtasya dhītim: "ṛtasya" means "of truth" or "of order," and "dhītim" refers to "intellect" or "understanding." Together, this emphasizes the importance of wisdom grounded in truth.
  • brahmaṇo manīṣām: "Brahmaṇo" relates to "Brahman," the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, and "manīṣām" means "thought" or "intention." This suggests that true understanding comes from a connection to the divine or universal consciousness.
  • gāvo yanti gopatim: "gāvo" means "cows," which are often symbolic of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature, and "gopatim" refers to "the lord of the cows" or "the protector." This can be interpreted as the pursuit of sustenance and protection in life.
  • pṛcchamānāḥ: This means "inquiring" or "asking." It highlights the importance of curiosity and seeking knowledge.
  • somaṁ yanti: "somaṁ" refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. "yanti" means "to go" or "to reach." This indicates the journey towards enlightenment and higher states of consciousness.
  • matayo vāvaśānāḥ: "matayo" means "thoughts" or "intellects," and "vāvaśānāḥ" suggests "those who are aware" or "those who understand." This emphasizes the importance of awareness and understanding in the pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking truth and wisdom through communication, inquiry, and connection to the divine. In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in meaningful conversations, seek knowledge, and remain curious. The metaphor of fire symbolizes the illumination that comes from understanding and the pursuit of higher consciousness, akin to how education and enlightenment can transform lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our intellect and spirit, guiding us towards a more profound understanding of ourselves and the universe.
