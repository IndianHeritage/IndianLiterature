कृष्णं नियानं हरयः सुपर्णा अपो वसाना दिवं उत पतन्ति |ता आववृतन सदनाद ऋतस्याद इद घृतेन पृथिवी व्य उद्यते ||
kṛṣṇaṁ niyānaṁ harayaḥ suparṇā apo vasānā divam ut patanti | ta āvavṛtran sadanād ṛtasyād id ghṛtena pṛthivī vy udyate ||
  • kṛṣṇaṁ niyānaṁ harayaḥ suparṇā: This phrase can be translated as "the black one (Kṛṣṇa) who is the guide, the divine beings (harayaḥ) with beautiful wings (suparṇā)." Here, Kṛṣṇa represents a divine force or consciousness that guides individuals through life. The term "harayaḥ" refers to divine beings or energies that assist in this guidance, while "suparṇā" symbolizes the beauty and grace of these divine forces.
  • apo vasānā divam ut patanti: This translates to "the waters (apo) that dwell (vasānā) in the heavens (divam) and flow down (ut patanti)." This line signifies the connection between the celestial and earthly realms, suggesting that divine energies flow into the material world, nourishing it like rain nourishes the earth.
  • ta āvavṛtran sadanād ṛtasyād: This means "they (ta) who have come forth (āvavṛtran) from the abode (sadanād) of truth (ṛtasyād)." This line emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness as foundational elements in life. It suggests that those who embody truth are connected to the divine and are able to manifest their potential.
  • id ghṛtena pṛthivī vy udyate: This translates to "with this ghee (ghṛtena), the earth (pṛthivī) is uplifted (udyate)." Ghee, a symbol of purity and nourishment, represents the essence of life and spiritual practice. The uplifting of the earth signifies the transformation and elevation of human consciousness through spiritual practices and the pursuit of truth.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, the flow of spiritual energy, the importance of truth, and the transformative power of spiritual practices. It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and the material world, emphasizing that spiritual nourishment leads to personal and collective upliftment.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance from their inner selves or higher powers when faced with challenges. It encourages the pursuit of truth and righteousness in personal and professional relationships. For example, in a workplace setting, adhering to ethical practices and fostering a supportive environment can lead to collective success and harmony.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of aligning with truth and righteousness. By embracing these principles, individuals can experience personal growth and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
