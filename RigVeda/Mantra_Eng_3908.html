यस्यावधीत पितरं यस्य मातरं यस्य शक्रो भ्रातरं नात ईषते |वेतीद वस्य प्रयता यतंकरो न किल्बिषाद ईषते वस्व आकरः ||
yasyāvadhīt pitaraṁ yasya mātaraṁ yasya śakro bhrātaraṁ nāta īṣate | vetīd v asya prayatā yataṁkaro na kilbiṣād īṣate vasva ākaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasyā: of whom
    • avadhīt: has honored
    • pitaraṁ: the father
    • yasya: of whom
    • mātaraṁ: the mother
    • yasya: of whom
    • śakro: the powerful (often referring to Indra)
    • bhrātaraṁ: the brother
    • nāta: not
    • īṣate: is disturbed
    • vetīd: knows
    • v: and
    • asya: of him
    • prayatā: effort
    • yataṁkaro: who acts
    • na: not
    • kilbiṣād: from sin
    • īṣate: is disturbed
    • vasva: all
    • ākaraḥ: actions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of honoring one's parents and family.
    • It emphasizes that a person who respects their father, mother, and powerful figures (like Indra) will not be disturbed by negative influences.
    • The mantra suggests that through righteous actions and efforts, one can remain free from sin and its consequences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, honoring parents is a fundamental value. For instance, in Indian culture, touching the feet of elders is a sign of respect and gratitude.
    • In a workplace, respecting authority figures and colleagues can lead to a harmonious environment, reducing conflicts and enhancing productivity.
    • Engaging in community service or helping others can be seen as a way to honor the teachings of one's family and culture, leading to a positive impact on society.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for respect and honor towards family and community.
    • It teaches that through respect and righteous actions, one can lead a life free from disturbances and negativity.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional settings, and community interactions, promoting a balanced and harmonious existence.
