स्वध्वरासो मधुमन्तो अग्नय उष्रा जarantे प्रति वस्तोर अश्विनाः |यन् निक्तहस्तस् तरणिर विचक्षणः सोमं सुशाव मधुमन्तम् अद्रिभिः ||
svadhvarāso madhumanto agnaya usrā jarante prati vastor aśvinā | yan niktahastas taraṇir vicakṣaṇaḥ somaṁ suṣāva madhumantam adribhiḥ ||
  • svadhvarāso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are self-possessed" or "those who are in their own essence." It signifies individuals who are grounded and aware of their true nature.
  • madhumanto: This word translates to "sweet" or "pleasant." It often refers to the sweetness of life, experiences, or even the divine essence that brings joy.
  • agnaya: This refers to "the fires" or "the divine light." In a broader sense, it symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the transformative power of wisdom.
  • usrā: This term means "the cows" or "nourishing elements." Cows are often seen as symbols of abundance and sustenance in Vedic literature.
  • jarante: This means "to flow" or "to move." It indicates the dynamic nature of life and the continuous flow of energy and resources.
  • prati vastor: This phrase translates to "in every place" or "in every aspect." It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the twin deities, the Aśvins, who are associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of existence and the balance of opposites.
  • yan niktahastas: This can be interpreted as "that which is grasped by the hand." It signifies tangible experiences and the importance of engaging with the material world.
  • taraṇir: This term means "the one who crosses" or "the one who transcends." It represents the journey of overcoming obstacles and achieving higher states of consciousness.
  • vicakṣaṇaḥ: This translates to "the wise" or "the perceptive." It highlights the importance of wisdom and discernment in navigating life's challenges.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. It represents the pursuit of higher states of being and spiritual enlightenment.
  • suṣāva: This means "to enjoy" or "to relish." It emphasizes the joy found in experiences and the importance of savoring life.
  • madhumantam: Again referring to the "sweet" or "pleasant," reinforcing the idea of joy and fulfillment in life.
  • adribhiḥ: This translates to "with the mountains" or "with the strong." It symbolizes stability, strength, and the support of nature in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of self-awareness, joy, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It teaches us to appreciate the sweetness of life while navigating its challenges with discernment and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, relationships, and community building. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and harmonious existence, drawing on the support of nature and the wisdom of the universe.
