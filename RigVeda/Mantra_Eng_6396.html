शाचिगो शाचिपूजनायं रणाय ते सुतः |आखण्डल प्र हूयसे ||
śācigo śācipūjanāyaṁ raṇāya te sutaḥ | ākhaṇḍala pra hūyase ||
  • śācigo: This term can be interpreted as "one who is pure" or "one who is righteous." In real life, this can refer to individuals who uphold moral values and integrity in their actions.
  • śācipūjanāyaṁ: This phrase suggests "for the worship of the pure" or "to honor the righteous." It emphasizes the importance of respecting and honoring those who embody purity and righteousness in society.
  • raṇāya: This word translates to "for battle" or "for conflict." It signifies the challenges and struggles one faces in life, which can be both external (like societal conflicts) and internal (personal struggles).
  • te sutaḥ: This means "your son" or "your offspring." It can symbolize the legacy and values passed down through generations, highlighting the importance of nurturing the next generation with righteousness.
  • ākhaṇḍala: This term can be understood as "unbroken" or "whole." It represents unity and completeness, suggesting that a community or family should remain united in their values and purpose.
  • pra hūyase: This translates to "you are invoked" or "you are called upon." It indicates the act of seeking guidance or support from a higher power or from those who embody righteousness.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, righteousness, and unity in the face of challenges. It calls for honoring those who uphold moral values and encourages individuals to seek guidance from them. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in family dynamics, community building, and personal development. By fostering an environment that values integrity and unity, individuals can navigate life's battles more effectively, ensuring that the legacy of righteousness is passed on to future generations.
