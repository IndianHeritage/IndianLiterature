मन्त्र: रक्षां सु नो अररुषः स्वनात समस्य कस्य चित् |निदो यत्र मुमुच्महे ||
rakṣā su no araruṣaḥ svanāt samasya kasya cit | nido yatra mumucmahe ||
  • Translation: The mantra "rakṣā su no araruṣaḥ svanāt samasya kasya cit | nido yatra mumucmahe ||" can be translated to mean: "May protection come to us from all directions, may we be free from all troubles, and may we attain peace and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rakṣā: Protection or safeguarding.
    • su: Good or well.
    • no: Us or our.
    • araruṣaḥ: Free from troubles or afflictions.
    • svanāt: From all directions or sources.
    • samasya: Problems or difficulties.
    • kasya cit: From anyone or anything.
    • nido: Peace or tranquility.
    • yatra: Where or in which.
    • mumucmahe: We attain or we achieve.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be invoked during team meetings to foster a sense of unity and protection against workplace challenges, ensuring that all team members feel secure and supported.
    • During personal challenges, such as health issues or financial difficulties, individuals may recite this mantra to seek mental peace and protection from negative influences, helping them to focus on recovery and stability.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be used to promote collective well-being, encouraging members to support each other and work together to overcome communal issues.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection and peace. It emphasizes the importance of safeguarding oneself and others from troubles, fostering a sense of community and support. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek and provide protection, ensuring a harmonious and prosperous environment for all. By invoking this mantra, individuals and groups can cultivate a mindset focused on overcoming challenges and achieving collective well-being.
