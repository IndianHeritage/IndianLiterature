ध्रुवं ते राजा वरुणो ध्रुवं देवो बृहस्पतिः |ध्रुवं त इन्द्रश्चाग्निश्च राष्ट्रं धारयतां ध्रुवम् ||
dhruvaṁ te rājā varuṇo dhruvaṁ devo bṛhaspatiḥ | dhruvaṁ ta indraś cāgniś ca rāṣṭraṁ dhārayatāṁ dhruvam ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, the king, you are steadfast; O Brihaspati, you are steadfast; O Indra and Agni, may you steadfastly uphold the realm."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Dhruvaṁ: Steadfast, unchanging, eternal.
    • Te: Your.
    • Rājā: King.
    • Varuṇa: A deity associated with cosmic order and water.
    • Devo: God, divine being.
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity of wisdom and knowledge.
    • Indra: The king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms.
    • Agni: The fire god, representing energy and transformation.
    • Rāṣṭraṁ: Realm, territory, or nation.
    • Dhārayatāṁ: May uphold, support, or sustain.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the steadfastness of various deities, emphasizing the importance of stability and support in governance and society.
    • In real life, this can be related to the qualities of a good leader who remains firm and reliable in times of uncertainty.
    • For example, during a crisis, a leader who embodies the qualities of Varuna, Brihaspati, Indra, and Agni can provide guidance and strength to their people.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of divine support in maintaining order and stability in society.
    • It serves as a reminder for leaders to embody steadfastness and wisdom, ensuring the welfare of their realm.
    • In contemporary scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership training, governance, and community building, where the qualities of reliability and support are essential for progress and harmony.
