सम इन्द्र राया सम इषा रभेमहि सं वाजेभिः पुरुषचन्द्रैर अभिद्युभिः |सं देव्या प्रमत्यā वीरशुष्मया गोअग्रयाश्ववत्या रभेमहि ||
sam indra rāyā sam iṣā rabhemahi saṁ vājebhiḥ puruścandrair abhidyubhiḥ | saṁ devyā pramatyā vīraśuṣmayā goagrayāśvāvatyā rabhemahi ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Together, may we invoke Indra, the powerful one; may we be blessed with strength and prosperity, like the swift horses and the valiant heroes. Together, may we be united with the divine energy that brings forth abundance."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Indra: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • Rāyā: This term refers to wealth, prosperity, and abundance. It signifies the material and spiritual richness one seeks in life.
    • Iṣā: This word denotes power or authority. It emphasizes the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
    • Vājebhiḥ: This refers to horses, which symbolize speed, agility, and the ability to move forward swiftly in life.
    • Puruścandrair: This term can be interpreted as "valiant heroes" or "brave men," highlighting the importance of courage and valor in overcoming challenges.
    • Devyā: This refers to the divine or goddess energy, representing the nurturing and protective aspects of the universe.
    • Vīraśuṣmayā: This term signifies strength and vitality, suggesting the importance of being vigorous and full of life.
    • Goagrayāśvāvatyā: This phrase can be understood as "the best of the cows and horses," symbolizing the best resources and qualities one can possess.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the qualities of Indra can be likened to a team coming together to achieve a common goal, leveraging each member's strengths to overcome challenges and achieve success.
    • In personal life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek prosperity and strength in their endeavors, whether it be in education, career, or personal relationships.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and unity, encouraging participants to work together for the common good.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and divine support in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal aspirations, professional endeavors, or community initiatives, this mantra serves as a reminder that together, with strength and divine guidance, we can overcome obstacles and attain our goals. It encourages individuals to harness their inner power and work collaboratively towards a prosperous future.
