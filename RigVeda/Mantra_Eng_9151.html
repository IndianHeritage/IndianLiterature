अबुध्रमु त्य इन्द्रवन्तो अग्नयो ज्योतिर भरन्त उषसो व्युष्टिषु |महī dyāvāpṛthivī cetatām अपो 'द्य देवानाम् अव आ वृणीमहे ||
abudhram u tya indravanto agnayo jyotir bharanta uṣaso vyuṣṭiṣu | mahī dyāvāpṛthivī cetatām apo 'dyā devānām ava ā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abudhram: May we be endowed with wisdom.
    • u: And.
    • tya: O Indra.
    • indravanto: Those who are powerful like Indra.
    • agnayo: O Agni (the fire god).
    • jyotir: Light.
    • bharanta: Who carry or bring forth.
    • uṣaso: The dawn.
    • vyuṣṭiṣu: In the brightening or illumination.
    • mahī: The great.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth.
    • cetatām: Of the conscious beings.
    • apo: Water.
    • 'dyā: Today.
    • devānām: Of the gods.
    • ava: Downward or towards us.
    • ā: To us.
    • vṛṇīmahe: We choose or we invoke.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for wisdom and enlightenment.
    • It invokes the powerful deities, particularly Indra and Agni, to bless us with light and knowledge.
    • The mention of dawn signifies new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the heavens, earth, and water, highlighting the importance of nature in our quest for understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity in decision-making, such as choosing a career path or resolving conflicts.
    • It can be recited during moments of uncertainty, invoking the divine for guidance and wisdom.
    • In educational settings, students can use this mantra to inspire focus and enlightenment in their studies.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, it can be a reminder of the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine wisdom and illumination.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements of nature and the divine.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, clarity, and guidance in their personal and communal lives.
    • By invoking the blessings of the gods, we align ourselves with higher consciousness and purpose.
