इमे दिवो अनिमिषा पृथिव्याश्च चिकीत्वांसो अचेतसं नयन्ति |प्रव्राजे चिन् नद्यो गाधम अस्ति पारं नो अस्य विष्पितस्य पर्वण ||
ime divo animiṣā pṛthivyāś cikitvāṁso acetasaṁ nayanti | pravrāje cin nadyo gādham asti pāraṁ no asya viṣpitasya parṣan ||
  • ime divo animiṣā: This phrase translates to "these (things) of the heavens, without blinking." It suggests a sense of vigilance and awareness in the celestial realm.
  • pṛthivyāś cikitvāṁso: This means "of the earth, having knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of understanding and wisdom that comes from the earthly experience.
  • acetasaṁ nayanti: This translates to "they lead the unmindful." It indicates that there are forces or entities that guide those who may not be fully aware or conscious of their surroundings.
  • pravrāje cin nadyo: This means "when the rivers flow." Rivers are often symbolic of life, movement, and change, representing the flow of time and experiences.
  • gādham asti pāraṁ: This translates to "there is a depth (or richness) beyond." It suggests that there is more to discover beyond the surface of things.
  • no asya viṣpitasya parṣan: This means "of this vast expanse, the connection." It highlights the interconnectedness of all things within the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of awareness, knowledge, and interconnectedness in both the celestial and earthly realms. It serves as a reminder to remain vigilant and conscious of our surroundings while recognizing the deeper connections that bind us to the universe. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as environmental awareness, where understanding the impact of our actions on the earth and its ecosystems is crucial. It also encourages mindfulness in our daily lives, urging us to be present and aware of our thoughts and actions, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence with ourselves and the world around us.
