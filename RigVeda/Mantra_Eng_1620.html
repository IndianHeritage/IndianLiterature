प्रयन्तं इति परि जारं कनिनाम् पश्यामसि नोपनिपद्यमानम् |अनवपृग्णा वितता वसानम् प्रियं मित्रस्य वरुणस्य धाम ||
prayantam it pari jāraṁ kanīnām paśyāmasi nopanipadyamānam | anavapṛgṇā vitatā vasānam priyam mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma ||
  • Mantra: "prayantam it pari jāraṁ kanīnām paśyāmasi nopanipadyamānam | anavapṛgṇā vitatā vasānam priyam mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prayantam: moving towards
    • it: this
    • pari: around
    • jāraṁ: the one who is old or aged
    • kanīnām: of the young or the youthful
    • paśyāmasi: we see
    • nopanipadyamānam: not being obstructed
    • anavapṛgṇā: without any hindrance
    • vitatā: spread out
    • vasānam: dwelling or abode
    • priyam: dear or beloved
    • mitrasya: of the friend
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of water and cosmic order)
    • dhāma: abode or realm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey towards a state of harmony and understanding.
    • The phrase "moving towards" suggests a proactive approach to life, where one is encouraged to seek out knowledge and wisdom.
    • The mention of "the old and the young" signifies the importance of intergenerational relationships and learning from both the wisdom of the elders and the fresh perspectives of the youth.
    • "We see not being obstructed" implies that true understanding and enlightenment come when we are free from distractions and obstacles.
    • The idea of "without any hindrance" emphasizes the need for a clear mind and open heart to receive the teachings of life.
    • The "beloved abode of the friend" refers to the sacred space where mutual respect, love, and understanding exist, particularly in the context of relationships with others and with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering an environment where both experienced employees and newcomers can share knowledge leads to innovation and growth.
    • In family dynamics, valuing the insights of both grandparents and grandchildren can create a stronger family bond and a richer understanding of traditions.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining open communication without barriers allows for deeper connections and mutual support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding through the lens of both youth and age.
    • It teaches us the importance of creating spaces free from hindrances, where love and friendship can flourish.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to harmonious relationships, personal growth, and a deeper connection with the world around us.
