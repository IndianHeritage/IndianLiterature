अग्निं वो देवयज्ञयाग्निम प्रयत्य अध्वरे |अग्निं धीषु प्रथमम् अग्निम अर्वत्य अग्निं क्षैत्राय साधसे ||
agniṁ vo devayajyayāgnim prayaty adhvare | agniṁ dhīṣu prathamam agnim arvaty agniṁ kṣaitrāya sādhase ||
  • Agniṁ vo devayajyayāgnim prayaty adhvare: This line translates to "We invoke Agni (the Fire God) through our sacred rituals." Here, Agni represents not just the physical fire but also the divine energy that purifies and transforms. In real life, this can be likened to the way we use fire in various rituals, such as lighting a lamp during festivals, symbolizing the dispelling of darkness and ignorance.
  • Agniṁ dhīṣu prathamam: This means "Agni is the first among the wise." It signifies that fire is essential for knowledge and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can be seen in how fire has been a catalyst for human development—cooking food, providing warmth, and enabling the creation of tools. Just as fire ignites physical materials, knowledge ignites the mind.
  • Agnim arvaty: This translates to "Agni is invoked for strength." Fire is often associated with strength and power. In everyday life, we can relate this to the strength we derive from our passions and pursuits. For instance, athletes often speak of the 'fire' within them that drives them to excel, much like how Agni symbolizes the inner strength that propels us forward.
  • Agnim kṣaitrāya sādhase: This means "Agni is offered for the prosperity of the fields." Here, Agni is linked to agriculture and abundance. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the importance of sustainable farming practices that respect nature and promote growth. Farmers often perform rituals to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest, reflecting the ancient reverence for Agni as a source of sustenance.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the multifaceted role of Agni in our lives—serving as a symbol of purification, knowledge, strength, and prosperity. In real life, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by recognizing the importance of our actions and intentions in rituals, the pursuit of knowledge, the cultivation of inner strength, and the respect for nature in our agricultural practices. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives, much like the ancient sages who revered Agni as a divine force guiding humanity.
