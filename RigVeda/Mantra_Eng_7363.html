समित तान वृत्रहाखिदत खे अराम् इव खेदयाः |प्रवृद्धो दस्युहाभवत ||
sam it tān vṛtrahākhidat khe arām̐ iva khedayā | pravṛddho dasyuhābhavat ||
  • Mantra: "sam it tān vṛtrahākhidat khe arām̐ iva khedayā | pravṛddho dasyuhābhavat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • it: this, here
    • tān: those, them
    • vṛtrahā: slayer of Vritra (a demon representing chaos and obstruction)
    • akhidat: has cut, has removed
    • khe: in the sky, in the atmosphere
    • arām̐: like a bird, free
    • iva: like, as if
    • khedayā: in the sky, in the open
    • pravṛddho: having grown, having increased
    • dasyuhā: slayer of the Dasyus (a term for enemies or obstructors)
    • abhavat: has become, has manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of unity and harmony, suggesting that when we come together, we can overcome obstacles.
    • The reference to "vṛtrahā" indicates the removal of chaos and negativity, akin to overcoming personal challenges in life.
    • The imagery of being "like a bird" suggests freedom and liberation, which can be related to achieving one's goals and aspirations.
    • The phrase "pravṛddho dasyuhā" implies that through collective effort, we can grow stronger and more capable of facing adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, when individuals work together harmoniously, they can achieve greater success than when working alone. For instance, a sports team that collaborates effectively can win championships.
    • In community service, when people unite to address social issues, they can create significant change, such as organizing a clean-up drive to improve local environments.
    • In personal development, overcoming fears or challenges often requires support from friends or mentors, illustrating the importance of collective strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of unity and collaboration in overcoming obstacles and achieving freedom.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that together, we can rise above challenges, much like the slayer of Vritra who removes chaos.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, from personal relationships to professional environments, highlighting the importance of teamwork and support.
