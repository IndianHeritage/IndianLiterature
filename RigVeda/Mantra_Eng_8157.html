मन्त्र: उता त्या हरितो दश सूरो आयुक्त यातवे | इन्दुर इन्द्र इति ब्रुवन् ||
uta tyā harito daśa sūro ayukta yātave | indur indra iti bruvan ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • tyā - This means "by you" or "through you," suggesting a sense of agency or influence.
  • harito - This term refers to "the green" or "the flourishing," often associated with nature, vitality, and prosperity.
  • daśa - This translates to "ten," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle in various contexts.
  • sūro - Meaning "hero" or "brave one," this word signifies strength, valor, and the qualities of a leader.
  • ayukta - This can be interpreted as "not united" or "disconnected," indicating a lack of harmony or coherence.
  • yātave - This word means "to the one who travels" or "to the one who moves," suggesting a journey or progression.
  • indur - This translates to "the moon," often symbolizing calmness, reflection, and the passage of time.
  • indra - Referring to the king of gods in Vedic literature, Indra represents power, authority, and the force of nature.
  • iti - This means "thus" or "so," indicating a conclusion or a statement of fact.
  • bruvan - This translates to "speaking" or "declaring," suggesting communication or expression of thoughts.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for unity and strength in the face of challenges. It emphasizes the importance of flourishing and vitality, while also acknowledging the potential disconnection that can occur in life. The mention of Indra and the moon suggests a balance between power and calmness, urging individuals to find harmony in their pursuits.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from the qualities of Indra, embodying strength and authority while also fostering unity among their team members.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their journeys, recognizing the importance of both vitality and calmness in achieving their goals.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "the green" can inspire actions towards sustainability and the flourishing of nature, reminding us of our responsibility to the environment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, vitality, and harmony. It encourages individuals to embrace their journeys while fostering unity and reflecting on the balance between power and tranquility in their lives.
