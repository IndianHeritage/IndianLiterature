कण्वा इव भृगवः सूर्याऽ इव विश्वम् इद् धीतम् आनशुः | इन्द्रं स्तोमेभिर महयन्त आयवः प्रियमेधासो अस्वरन् ||
kaṇvā iva bhṛgavaḥ sūryā iva viśvam id dhītam ānaśuḥ | indraṁ stomebhir mahayanta āyavaḥ priyamedhāso asvaran ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaṇvā: Like the Kanva (a sage or a group of sages)
    • iva: Like
    • bhṛgavaḥ: The Bhrigus (another group of sages)
    • sūryā: The Sun
    • iva: Like
    • viśvam: All or the universe
    • id: This
    • dhītam: That which is understood or realized
    • ānaśuḥ: Those who are not greedy or who do not desire
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • stomebhir: With hymns or praises
    • mahayanta: Making great or glorifying
    • āyavaḥ: Those who are devoted or who seek
    • priyamedhāso: With loving thoughts or intentions
    • asvaran: They have spoken or expressed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between the sages (Kanvas and Bhrigus) and the Sun, suggesting that just as the Sun illuminates the universe, the wisdom of the sages enlightens the world.
    • The phrase "ānaśuḥ" indicates a state of being free from greed, emphasizing the importance of selflessness in the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and power, and the mantra calls upon him to be glorified through hymns, highlighting the significance of devotion and praise in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the wisdom of sages can be likened to the teachings of philosophers or spiritual leaders who guide individuals towards self-realization and enlightenment.
    • The concept of selflessness can be observed in acts of charity and community service, where individuals work for the betterment of society without seeking personal gain.
    • In various cultures, the act of praising or honoring leaders or deities through songs and hymns is a common practice, reflecting the importance of gratitude and reverence in human relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of wisdom, selflessness, and devotion. It serves as a reminder that true enlightenment comes from understanding, sharing knowledge, and honoring those who guide us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger community bonds, and a deeper appreciation for the wisdom that exists in the world around us.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, practice selflessness, and express gratitude, fostering a harmonious and enlightened society.
