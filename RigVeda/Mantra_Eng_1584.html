स ईं मृगो अप्यो वनर्गुर उप त्वच्य उपमस्यां नि धायि |व्य अब्रवीद वयुनां मार्त्येभ्यो 'ग्निर विद्वां ऽ ऋतचिद् धि सत्यः ||
sa īm mṛgo apyo vanargur upa tvacy upamasyāṁ ni dhāyi | vy abravīd vayunā martyebhyo 'gnir vidvām̐ ṛtacid dhi satyaḥ ||
  • sa īm mṛgo apyo vanargur upa tvacy upamasyāṁ ni dhāyi
    • sa - This word translates to "that" or "he." It indicates a subject or a focus in the mantra.
    • īm - This can mean "to us" or "for us," suggesting a sense of inclusion or benefit.
    • mṛgo - This means "deer" or "animal," symbolizing grace, agility, and the natural world.
    • apyo - This translates to "from the waters," indicating a connection to life and sustenance.
    • vanargur - This term refers to "the one who roams in the forest," representing freedom and the untamed aspects of nature.
    • upa - Meaning "near" or "towards," it suggests a movement or approach.
    • tvacy - This means "skin" or "surface," which can symbolize the outer layer of existence.
    • upamasyāṁ - This can be interpreted as "in the likeness of," indicating a comparison or similarity.
    • ni dhāyi - This means "may I hold" or "may I possess," expressing a desire for connection or ownership.
  • vy abravīd vayunā martyebhyo 'gnir vidvām̐ ṛtacid dhi satyaḥ
    • vy - This means "he" or "that one," referring to a speaker or a divine entity.
    • abravīd - This translates to "spoke" or "declared," indicating communication or revelation.
    • vayunā - This can mean "with wisdom" or "with knowledge," suggesting a thoughtful approach.
    • martyebhyo - This means "to mortals" or "to humans," indicating the audience of the message.
    • 'gnir - This translates to "fire," symbolizing transformation, energy, and purification.
    • vidvām̐ - This means "the wise one" or "the knowledgeable," referring to a person of insight.
    • ṛtacid - This can be interpreted as "truthful" or "righteous," emphasizing moral integrity.
    • dhi - This means "intellect" or "understanding," highlighting the importance of wisdom.
    • satyaḥ - This translates to "truth," which is a central theme in many spiritual and philosophical traditions.

Conclusion:

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of nature, wisdom, and truth. It emphasizes the importance of understanding our relationship with the natural world (symbolized by the deer and the forest) and the wisdom that comes from this connection. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Promoting environmental conservation and respect for wildlife.
  • Encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom in their actions and decisions.
  • Fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility among people.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of living in harmony with nature and upholding truth and wisdom in our lives.
