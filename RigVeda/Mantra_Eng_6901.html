यज्ञानां रथ्ये वयं तिग्मजम्भाय वीळवे |स्तोमैर इषेमाग्नये ||
yajñānāṁ rathye vayaṁ tigmajambhāya vīḻave | stomair iṣemāgnaye ||
  • yajñānāṁ: This word refers to "sacrifices" or "offerings." In the context of the Rigveda, yajñas are rituals performed to please the deities and ensure harmony in the universe.
  • rathye: This term means "in the chariot" or "on the path." It symbolizes the journey of life and the means by which we navigate through our experiences.
  • vayaṁ: This translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • tigmajambhāya: This word can be broken down into "tigma," meaning "sharp" or "intense," and "jambhāya," which refers to "the one who is fierce" or "the one who is powerful." It signifies strength and determination.
  • vīḻave: This term means "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who deserves." It indicates respect and recognition of merit.
  • stomair: This word refers to "hymns" or "praises." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence through words.
  • iṣemā: This translates to "may we attain" or "may we achieve." It reflects a desire for success and fulfillment.
  • agnaye: This term means "to Agni," the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals. Agni represents transformation, purification, and the connection between the earthly and divine realms.

The mantra can be interpreted as a collective invocation for strength and success through the performance of sacrifices, recognizing the power of the divine, and expressing gratitude through hymns. It emphasizes the importance of community and shared goals in achieving greatness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork: Just as the mantra emphasizes collective effort, in workplaces or group projects, collaboration and mutual support can lead to greater success.
  • Rituals and Celebrations: The concept of yajña can be seen in modern celebrations where people come together to honor traditions, such as weddings or festivals, reinforcing community bonds.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of striving for achievement (iṣemā) can inspire individuals to set goals and work diligently towards them, recognizing their own strengths (tigmajambhāya).

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of community, the importance of rituals in our lives, and the need to recognize and honor the divine forces that guide us. It encourages us to work together towards common goals, celebrate our achievements, and express gratitude for the blessings we receive.
