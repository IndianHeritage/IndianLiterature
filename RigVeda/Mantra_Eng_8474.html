असर्जि स्कम्भो दिव उद्यतो मदः परि त्रिधातुर भुवनान्य अर्षति |अंशुं रिहन्ति मतयः पाणिपातं गिरा यदि निर्णिजम् ऋग्मिणो ययुः ||
asarji skambho diva udyato madaḥ pari tridhātur bhuvanāny arṣati | aṁśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ panipnataṁ girā yadi nirṇijam ṛgmiṇo yayuḥ ||
  • asarji skambho diva udyato madaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the mighty Skambha, rising in the sky, is filled with intoxication." Here, "Skambha" represents a cosmic pillar or support, symbolizing stability and strength. In real life, this can be likened to a strong leader or a foundational principle that supports a community or organization.
  • pari tridhātur bhuvanāny arṣati: This translates to "the three worlds are shaken by it." The "three worlds" refer to the physical, mental, and spiritual realms. This can be seen in real life when a significant event, such as a natural disaster or a major political change, impacts all aspects of life, causing a ripple effect across society.
  • aṁśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ: This means "the thoughts gather like rays." This can be interpreted as the collective consciousness or ideas coming together to form a greater understanding or insight. In modern contexts, this can be compared to collaborative efforts in innovation, where diverse ideas converge to create new technologies or solutions.
  • panipnataṁ girā yadi nirṇijam ṛgmiṇo yayuḥ: This translates to "if the words of the seers are not lost." This emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations. In real life, this can be seen in the preservation of cultural heritage, where ancient teachings and philosophies continue to influence contemporary thought and practice.

  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the significance of strength, the interconnectedness of different realms, the power of collective thought, and the importance of preserving wisdom. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership, community resilience, innovation, and cultural preservation. By understanding and embodying these principles, individuals and societies can navigate challenges and foster growth and harmony.
