उत तवाम अरुणं वयं गोभिर अञ्जमो मदाय कम् |वि नो राय दुरो वृधि ||
uta tvām aruṇaṁ vayaṁ gobhir añjmo madāya kam | vi no rāye duro vṛdhi ||
  • uta tvām - This phrase translates to "we invoke you" or "we call upon you." It signifies a plea or a request for divine presence or assistance.
  • aruṇaṁ - This word means "the reddish dawn" or "the light of dawn." In a broader sense, it symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • vayaṁ - This translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community coming together in prayer or invocation.
  • gobhir - This term refers to "cows" or "wealth." In the Vedic context, cows are often seen as symbols of prosperity and nourishment.
  • añjmo - This means "to be adorned" or "to be blessed." It suggests a desire for divine grace and enrichment.
  • madāya - This translates to "for the sake of joy" or "for the purpose of delight." It emphasizes the importance of happiness and fulfillment in life.
  • kam - This word means "who" or "what," indicating a quest for understanding or knowledge.
  • vi no - This translates to "for us" or "to us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the invocation.
  • rāye - This means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the aspiration for abundance in life.
  • duro - This translates to "far" or "distant," suggesting the pursuit of goals that may seem out of reach.
  • vṛdhi - This means "growth" or "increase," indicating a desire for progress and development.

In summary, this mantra is a collective invocation for divine assistance, particularly in the context of prosperity, growth, and joy. It emphasizes the importance of community and the pursuit of abundance in life. The imagery of the dawn signifies hope and new beginnings, while the reference to cows symbolizes wealth and nourishment.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for festivals or celebrations, invoking this mantra can enhance the sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may recite this mantra when starting new ventures, seeking divine blessings for prosperity and growth.
  • Personal Development: Individuals may use this mantra as a daily affirmation to attract positivity, abundance, and joy into their lives.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire hope, foster community, and encourage the pursuit of prosperity and happiness in various aspects of life.
