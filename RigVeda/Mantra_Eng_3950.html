ग्राव्णो ब्रह्मा युयुजानाḥ सपर्ण कीरिणा देवान नमसोपशिक्षन् |अत्रिः सूर्यस्य दिवि चक्षुराधात स्वर्भानोर अप मायाग्हुक्षत ||
grāvṇo brahmā yuyujānaḥ saparyan kīriṇā devān namasopaśikṣan | atriḥ sūryasya divi cakṣur ādhāt svarbhānor apa māyā aghukṣat ||
  • grāvṇo brahmā yuyujānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the Brahman (the ultimate reality) is united with the world." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation efforts where individuals unite to protect nature.
  • saparyan kīriṇā devān: This translates to "with reverence, we praise the gods." It emphasizes the importance of respect and gratitude towards the divine forces that govern our lives. For example, during festivals like Diwali, people express their gratitude through prayers and offerings, acknowledging the blessings they receive.
  • namasopaśikṣan: This means "we bow down in humility." It reflects the attitude of humility and surrender before the divine. In everyday life, this can be observed when individuals seek guidance from mentors or elders, recognizing their wisdom and experience.
  • atriḥ sūryasya divi cakṣur ādhāt: This phrase can be understood as "Atri (a sage) has placed the eye of the sun in the sky." It symbolizes enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to the quest for education and understanding, where individuals strive to gain insight and clarity in their lives, much like students seeking knowledge in schools and universities.
  • svarbhānor apa māyā aghukṣat: This translates to "may the illusion of darkness be dispelled." It signifies the removal of ignorance and the pursuit of truth. In real life, this can be seen in movements advocating for social justice and equality, where individuals work to eliminate misconceptions and bring light to hidden issues.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, reverence, humility, enlightenment, and the dispelling of ignorance. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to recognize their interconnectedness with the divine and each other, to express gratitude, and to seek knowledge while striving to overcome ignorance. In contemporary society, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, education, and social activism, fostering a more harmonious and enlightened world.
