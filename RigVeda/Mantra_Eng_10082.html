सृजः सिन्धूम् अहिना जाग्रसाणाम् आद इद एताः प्र विविज्रे जवेना |मुमुक्षमाणाऽउता या मुमुच्रे 'धेद एता न रमन्ते नितिक्ताः ||
sṛjaḥ sindhūm̐r ahinā jagrasānām̐ ād id etāḥ pra vivijre javena | mumukṣamāṇā uta yā mumucre 'dhed etā na ramante nitiktāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sṛjaḥ: to create or to bring forth
    • sindhūm̐r: rivers or streams
    • ahinā: with the serpent or the great power
    • jagrasānām̐: of those who are awake or vigilant
    • ād: thus or so
    • etāḥ: these
    • pra vivijre: have been separated or distinguished
    • javena: with speed or swiftness
    • mumukṣamāṇā: desiring liberation or freedom
    • uta: or
    • yā: who
    • mumucre: have released or let go
    • 'dhed: from
    • etā: these
    • na: not
    • ramante: enjoy or delight
    • nitiktāḥ: those who are detached or indifferent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and flow of rivers, symbolizing the abundance of life and resources.
    • The mention of "ahinā" suggests a connection to powerful forces, possibly representing the divine or nature's energy.
    • The phrase "jagrasānām̐" emphasizes the importance of being vigilant and aware in life.
    • The idea of separation and distinction ("pra vivijre") indicates the need to discern between different paths or choices in life.
    • "mumukṣamāṇā" reflects the human desire for liberation, a common theme in many spiritual traditions.
    • The concluding part suggests that those who are detached ("nitiktāḥ") do not find joy in worldly pleasures, highlighting the value of detachment in the pursuit of higher truths.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a fast-paced world, being "jagrasānām̐" can be likened to the importance of mindfulness and awareness in our daily activities, such as being present during conversations or while working.
    • The rivers can symbolize the flow of opportunities in life; just as rivers carve their paths, individuals must navigate their journeys with discernment and purpose.
    • The concept of "mumukṣamāṇā" can be seen in those who seek personal growth and freedom from societal expectations, such as individuals pursuing unconventional careers or lifestyles.
    • Detachment, as mentioned in the mantra, can be observed in people who prioritize inner peace over material possessions, such as minimalists or those engaged in spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, awareness, and the pursuit of liberation.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to be vigilant, make conscious choices, and seek deeper truths beyond material existence.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to balance our engagement with the world while nurturing our inner selves, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
