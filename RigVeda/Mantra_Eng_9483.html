यā गौर वर्तनिम पर्येति निष्कृतम् पयो दूहानां व्रतनीर अवारतः | सा प्रब्रुवाणā वरुणाय दाशुषे देवभ्यः दाशद धविषा विवस्वते ||
yā gaur vartanim paryeti niṣkṛtam payo duhānā vratanīr avārataḥ | sā prabruvāṇā varuṇāya dāśuṣe devebhyo dāśad dhaviṣā vivasvate ||
  • yā gaur vartanim paryeti: This phrase can be translated as "She who moves gracefully." The term "gaur" refers to a radiant or luminous being, often associated with divinity or purity. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who embody grace and poise, such as dancers or leaders who inspire others through their actions.
  • niṣkṛtam payo duhānā vratanīr avārataḥ: This part translates to "the one who brings forth the pure essence of milk." Here, "niṣkṛtam" signifies purity, while "payo" refers to milk, a symbol of nourishment and sustenance. In everyday life, this can be seen in the context of mothers nurturing their children or caregivers providing for those in need, emphasizing the importance of purity in intentions and actions.
  • sā prabruvāṇā varuṇāya dāśuṣe: This translates to "She who speaks to Varuna, the deity of cosmic order." Varuna represents the moral and natural order of the universe. In practical terms, this can be related to individuals who advocate for justice and righteousness in society, such as activists or leaders who strive to maintain harmony and balance.
  • devebhyo dāśad dhaviṣā vivasvate: This phrase means "to the gods, she offers her devotion." It highlights the act of giving and devotion to higher powers or principles. In real life, this can be seen in various forms of worship, community service, or acts of kindness that individuals perform in honor of something greater than themselves.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of grace, purity, cosmic order, and devotion. Its significance lies in the reminder to embody these qualities in our daily lives. By striving for grace in our actions, purity in our intentions, advocating for justice, and offering our devotion to higher ideals, we contribute to a harmonious and balanced society. This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community service, and personal relationships, where these values can foster a sense of unity and purpose.
