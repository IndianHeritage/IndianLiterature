कियतī योषा मर्यतो वधूयोः परिप्रीता पण्यसा वारीयेण |भद्रा वधूर भवति यत सुपेशाः स्वयṁ सा मित्रं वनुते जन चित ||
kiyatī yoṣā maryato vadhūyoḥ pariprītā panyasā vāryeṇa | bhadrā vadhūr bhavati yat supeśāḥ svayaṁ sā mitraṁ vanute jane cit ||
  • kiyatī: This word can be translated to "how many" or "how much," indicating a sense of inquiry or curiosity.
  • yoṣā: Refers to "woman" or "female," highlighting the importance of femininity in the context of the mantra.
  • maryato: This term can mean "of the married" or "of the wife," emphasizing the role of a woman in a marital context.
  • vadhūyoḥ: This word translates to "of the bride," further reinforcing the focus on women in the context of marriage.
  • pariprītā: Meaning "satisfied" or "fulfilled," this term suggests a sense of contentment and happiness.
  • panyasā: This can be interpreted as "with wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the importance of material well-being.
  • vāryeṇa: This word means "with strength" or "with power," suggesting that strength is essential in achieving fulfillment.
  • bhadrā: Translated as "auspicious" or "good," this term signifies positivity and favorable conditions.
  • vadhūr: Again referring to "the bride," it emphasizes the significance of women in the context of blessings and auspiciousness.
  • bhavati: This means "becomes" or "is," indicating a transformation or realization of potential.
  • yat: Translated as "that which," it introduces a clause that elaborates on the preceding ideas.
  • supeśāḥ: This term means "well-dressed" or "beautiful," suggesting that external appearance can reflect inner qualities.
  • svayaṁ: Meaning "by oneself," it indicates self-sufficiency and independence.
  • sā: This word translates to "she," referring back to the woman or bride.
  • mitraṁ: Meaning "friend," it highlights the importance of companionship and support in relationships.
  • vanute: This means "to desire" or "to wish for," indicating aspirations and goals.
  • jane: Translated as "people" or "in society," it emphasizes the social context of the mantra.
  • cit: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," suggesting a deeper understanding of one's desires and relationships.

The mantra speaks to the roles and qualities of women, particularly in the context of marriage and society. It emphasizes the importance of satisfaction, prosperity, and strength in achieving a fulfilling life. The mantra suggests that a woman who is well-dressed and self-sufficient can become a source of friendship and support within her community.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to the empowerment of women, encouraging them to seek fulfillment in their personal and professional lives. It highlights the significance of self-awareness and the pursuit of happiness, which can lead to positive relationships and a supportive community.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role women play in society and the importance of their well-being. It encourages individuals to recognize and support the aspirations of women, fostering an environment where they can thrive and contribute positively to their communities.
