हंसा इव श्रेणिशो यतानाः शुक्रा वसानाḥ स्वरवो न आगुḥ |उन्नीयमानाः कविभिः पुरस्ताद देवā देवानाम् अपि यन्ति पाथः ||
haṁsā iva śreṇiśo yatānāḥ śukrā vasānāḥ svaravo na āguḥ | unnīyamānāḥ kavibhiḥ purastād devā devānām api yanti pāthaḥ ||
  • haṁsā iva: This phrase translates to "like swans." Swans are often seen as symbols of purity and grace. In real life, this can be related to individuals who strive for purity in their thoughts and actions, much like a person who practices mindfulness and seeks to maintain a clear and focused mind amidst distractions.
  • śreṇiśo: This word means "of the group" or "of the assembly." It signifies the importance of community and collaboration. In modern life, this can be seen in team projects at work or community service initiatives where collective effort leads to greater outcomes.
  • yatānāḥ: This translates to "those who strive" or "those who exert effort." It emphasizes the value of hard work and determination. For example, athletes training for a competition embody this principle, as their success is a result of consistent effort and dedication.
  • śukrā vasānāḥ: This phrase means "those who are clothed in brightness" or "radiant beings." It can refer to individuals who embody positivity and enlightenment. In real life, this could be likened to leaders or mentors who inspire others through their actions and wisdom.
  • svaravo na āguḥ: This translates to "the sound does not come." It suggests a state of silence or contemplation. In today's fast-paced world, this can be related to the practice of meditation or quiet reflection, which allows individuals to connect with their inner selves and find clarity.
  • unnīyamānāḥ: This means "those who are uplifted" or "those who are elevated." It signifies growth and progress. In a real-life context, this can be seen in personal development journeys where individuals seek to improve themselves through education, self-reflection, or spiritual practices.
  • kavibhiḥ: This translates to "by the poets" or "by the wise." It highlights the role of wisdom and creativity in guiding individuals. In modern society, this can be reflected in the influence of authors, artists, and thinkers who inspire change and provoke thought through their works.
  • purastād: This means "in front" or "before." It indicates a forward-looking perspective, focusing on future possibilities. In real life, this can be related to goal-setting and planning, where individuals envision their desired outcomes and work towards achieving them.
  • devā devānām api yanti pāthaḥ: This phrase translates to "the gods themselves follow the path." It signifies that even the divine are guided by certain principles or paths. In a practical sense, this can be interpreted as the idea that everyone, regardless of their status, must adhere to universal truths and moral values in their lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for purity, community, effort, positivity, silence, upliftment, wisdom, forward-thinking, and adherence to universal principles. Its significance lies in the reminder that personal growth and enlightenment are achievable through collective effort and individual determination. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with integrity, collaborate with others, and remain grounded in their values, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
