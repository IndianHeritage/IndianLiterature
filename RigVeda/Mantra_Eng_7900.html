आ पवस्व मदिन्तम पवित्रं धारणाय कवे |अर्कस्य योनिṁ आसदं ||
ā pavasva madintama pavitraṁ dhārayā kave | arkasya yonim āsadam ||
  • ā pavasva: This phrase can be translated as "come forth" or "be present." It signifies an invitation for divine energy or inspiration to manifest.
  • madintama: This word means "the most intoxicating" or "the best." It refers to the highest form of bliss or ecstasy that can be experienced.
  • pavitraṁ: This translates to "pure" or "sacred." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings.
  • dhārayā: This means "to hold" or "to sustain." It suggests the act of maintaining or nurturing something valuable.
  • kave: This term refers to "the poet" or "the seer." It indicates a person who has the ability to perceive deeper truths and express them through creativity.
  • arkasya: This translates to "of the light" or "of the sun." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the life-giving force of the sun.
  • yonim: This word means "the source" or "the womb." It represents the origin from which creation emerges.
  • āsadam: This means "to sit" or "to dwell." It implies a state of being present and grounded in a particular space or energy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for divine inspiration and purity, inviting the presence of the highest form of bliss and creativity. It emphasizes the importance of sustaining this purity and creativity, drawing from the source of light and knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Creative Pursuits: Artists, writers, and musicians can recite this mantra to invoke inspiration and clarity in their work, ensuring that their creations are pure and meaningful.
  • Meditation and Mindfulness: Practicing this mantra during meditation can help individuals connect with their inner selves, fostering a sense of peace and purity in their thoughts and actions.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals seeking personal development can use this mantra as a reminder to maintain purity in their intentions and actions, allowing them to grow and evolve positively.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of purity, creativity, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. By invoking these qualities in our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us, leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
