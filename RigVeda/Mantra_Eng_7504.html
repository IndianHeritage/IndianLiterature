आ त्वा विशन्तु इन्दवः समुद्रम इव सिन्धवः |न त्वां इन्द्राति रिच्यते ||
ā tvā viśantv indavaḥ samudram iva sindhavaḥ | na tvām indrāti ricyate ||
  • Mantra: "ā tvā viśantv indavaḥ samudram iva sindhavaḥ | na tvām indrāti ricyate ||"
  • Translation: "May the Indus rivers flow towards you, like the waters of the ocean; may you not be disturbed by the forces of Indra."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: towards
    • tvā: you
    • viśantv: may flow
    • indavaḥ: the Indus rivers
    • samudram: ocean
    • iva: like
    • sindhavaḥ: rivers
    • na: not
    • tvām: you
    • indrāti: by the forces of Indra
    • ricyate: disturbed
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the flow of prosperity and abundance, symbolized by the rivers, towards the individual or the deity being addressed.
    • The comparison of the rivers to the ocean signifies vastness and depth, suggesting that the blessings being sought are not just superficial but profound and life-sustaining.
    • The mention of Indra, the king of gods associated with storms and rain, indicates a desire for protection from overwhelming forces that could disrupt peace and stability.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a prayer for stability and abundance in one’s life, akin to seeking a steady flow of resources, opportunities, and support.
    • For example, a farmer may recite this mantra before the planting season, hoping for favorable weather and a bountiful harvest, while also seeking protection from natural calamities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its dual invocation of abundance and protection, making it relevant in various aspects of life, including personal, professional, and spiritual realms.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance and harmony, encouraging individuals to welcome positive influences while remaining resilient against challenges.
    • In contemporary settings, this mantra can be applied in contexts such as business ventures, family well-being, and community development, where the flow of resources and stability are essential for growth and success.
