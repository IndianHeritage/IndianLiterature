वृत्रखादो वलंरुजः पुरां धर्मो अपाम अजः |स्थाता रथस्य हर्योः अभिस्वर इन्द्रो दृढा चिद आरुजः ||
vṛtrakhādo valaṁrujaḥ purāṁ darmo apām ajaḥ | sthātā rathasya haryor abhisvara indro dṛḻhā cid ārujaḥ ||
  • vṛtrakhādo: This term refers to the one who has defeated Vritra, a serpent or dragon symbolizing chaos and obstacles. In real life, this can be likened to overcoming personal challenges or adversities that hinder progress.
  • valaṁrujaḥ: This can be interpreted as the one who has the strength to protect and support. In everyday life, this could represent a leader or a mentor who provides guidance and strength to others.
  • purāṁ: Meaning 'ancient' or 'of the past', this word emphasizes the importance of tradition and history in shaping our present. For instance, understanding historical events can help us make better decisions today.
  • darmo: This refers to righteousness or duty. In a practical sense, it can be seen in the actions of individuals who uphold ethical standards in their personal and professional lives.
  • apām: This word means 'of the waters', symbolizing the source of life and nourishment. In real life, it can be related to the importance of water conservation and the role of water in sustaining life.
  • ajaḥ: This term means 'unborn' or 'eternal', suggesting the idea of something that transcends time. This can be related to concepts like legacy and the impact of our actions on future generations.
  • sthātā: Meaning 'standing firm', this word signifies stability and resilience. In real life, it can be seen in individuals or communities that remain steadfast in the face of adversity.
  • rathasya: This refers to a 'chariot', symbolizing movement and progress. In a modern context, it can represent the journey of life and the various paths we take to achieve our goals.
  • haryor: This term can be interpreted as 'of the horses', which are often associated with strength and speed. In real life, this can symbolize the importance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving success.
  • abhisvara: Meaning 'with a powerful voice', this word emphasizes the importance of communication and expression. In today's world, effective communication is crucial in both personal and professional relationships.
  • indro: Referring to Indra, the king of gods, this term symbolizes leadership and authority. In real life, it can represent the qualities of a good leader who inspires and guides others.
  • dṛḻhā: This means 'firm' or 'strong', indicating the importance of determination and perseverance. In practical terms, it can be seen in individuals who pursue their goals despite obstacles.
  • cid: This term means 'consciousness' or 'awareness', highlighting the significance of mindfulness in our actions and decisions. In real life, being aware of our surroundings and choices can lead to better outcomes.
  • ārujaḥ: Meaning 'ascended' or 'risen', this word signifies growth and elevation. In a real-life context, it can represent personal development and the journey towards self-improvement.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of overcoming obstacles, the importance of duty and righteousness, and the significance of communication and leadership. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to remain resilient, uphold ethical standards, and strive for growth. By embodying these values, one can navigate the complexities of life with strength and purpose.
