या उक्तेभिर न विन्दते चिकीद य ऋषिचोदनः | इन्द्रं तं अच्छा वद नव्यस्यामत्य अरिष्यन्तं न भोजसे ||
ya ukthebhir na vindhate cikid ya ṛṣicodanaḥ | indraṁ tam acchā vada navyasyā maty ariṣyantaṁ na bhojase ||
  • Mantra: "ya ukthebhir na vindhate cikid ya ṛṣicodanaḥ | indraṁ tam acchā vada navyasyā maty ariṣyantaṁ na bhojase ||"
  • Translation: "He who does not find the one who is spoken of, who is the one who inspires the sages, speak to Indra, that one who is new and whose thoughts are not to be consumed."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ukthebhir: by the spoken words
    • na: not
    • vindhate: finds
    • cikid: who inspires
    • ya: who
    • ṛṣicodanaḥ: the one who inspires the sages
    • indraṁ: to Indra
    • tam: that one
    • acchā: speak
    • vada: say
    • navyasyā: new
    • maty: thoughts
    • ariṣyantaṁ: not to be consumed
    • na: not
    • bhojase: to be consumed
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine figure Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "who does not find the one who is spoken of" suggests a search for truth or understanding that remains elusive.
    • The reference to "the one who inspires the sages" indicates a higher wisdom or knowledge that guides those who seek enlightenment.
    • By calling upon Indra, the speaker seeks clarity and insight, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • The term "new" signifies fresh perspectives or ideas that are not yet fully realized or understood.
    • The concluding part, "whose thoughts are not to be consumed," implies that true wisdom is not something that can be easily grasped or taken for granted; it requires deep contemplation and respect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing challenges, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or leaders (akin to invoking Indra) to gain new insights and perspectives.
    • Students in academia may struggle to grasp complex concepts, prompting them to seek help from teachers or peers, reflecting the search for the "one who inspires the sages."
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate or reflect on their experiences to gain clarity, paralleling the quest for understanding and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our lives.
    • It teaches us that understanding and knowledge are not always readily available and require effort and introspection.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, education, or professional challenges, invoking higher wisdom can lead to greater clarity and insight.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us to respect the process of learning and the pursuit of truth, recognizing that true understanding is a journey rather than a destination.
