ते न इन्द्रः पृथिवी क्षामा वर्धन पूषा भगो अदितिः पाँच जनाः |सुशर्माणः स्ववसः सुनीथा भवन्तु नः सूत्रात्रासः सुगोपाः ||
te na indraḥ pṛthivī kṣāma vardhan pūṣā bhago aditiḥ pañca janāḥ | suśarmāṇaḥ svavasaḥ sunīthā bhavantu naḥ sutrātrāsaḥ sugopāḥ ||
  • te na indraḥ - "May Indra be with us" - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength and power.
  • pṛthivī kṣāma - "the Earth is strong" - This refers to the resilience and nurturing quality of the Earth, which sustains life.
  • vardhan - "may it grow" - A call for growth and prosperity, both in nature and in human endeavors.
  • pūṣā bhago - "may Pūṣā, the nourisher, bless us" - Pūṣā is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of sustenance.
  • aditiḥ pañca janāḥ - "Aditi, the mother of all, may she bless us with fivefold prosperity" - Aditi represents the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe, and the number five symbolizes completeness and balance.
  • suśarmāṇaḥ svavasaḥ - "may we have good fortune and well-being" - This line expresses a desire for happiness and security in life.
  • sunīthā bhavantu naḥ - "may they be well-disposed towards us" - A plea for favorable conditions and relationships in our lives.
  • sutrātrāsaḥ sugopāḥ - "may we be protected and guided" - This emphasizes the need for guidance and protection in our journey through life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for strength, nourishment, prosperity, and protection. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking blessings for a new venture, ensuring the well-being of family and community, or during significant life events like weddings or festivals. The significance lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, divine blessings, and human endeavors. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and positivity, fostering a harmonious relationship with the world around them.
