प्रति ष्यां सूनरी जनि व्युच्छन्ती परि स्वसुः |दिवो अदर्शि दुहिताः ||
prati ṣyā sūnarī janī vyucchantī pari svasuḥ | divo adarśi duhitā ||
  • Mantra: "prati ṣyā sūnarī janī vyucchantī pari svasuḥ | divo adarśi duhitā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in relation to
    • ṣyā: may be, could be
    • sūnarī: beautiful woman, often interpreted as a goddess or divine feminine
    • janī: mother, creator
    • vyucchantī: releasing, liberating
    • pari: all around, completely
    • svasuḥ: sister, often referring to a close female relative
    • divo: of the heavens, divine
    • adarśi: unseen, hidden
    • duhitā: daughter, offspring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a beautiful woman, possibly a divine figure, who is a mother and liberator.
    • The phrase "prati ṣyā sūnarī" suggests a connection to the divine feminine, emphasizing beauty and grace.
    • "Janī vyucchantī" indicates the role of a mother in providing freedom and nurturing, which can be seen in real-life examples of mothers who empower their children.
    • The mention of "svasuḥ" highlights the importance of sisterhood and familial bonds, reflecting how women support each other in society.
    • "Divo adarśi duhitā" suggests that the divine daughter, though unseen, plays a crucial role in the cosmic order, akin to how many women contribute silently to the fabric of society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, mothers are seen as the backbone of the family, providing emotional and financial support, similar to the nurturing aspect of "janī."
    • Women’s empowerment movements reflect the idea of "vyucchantī," where women are encouraged to break free from societal constraints.
    • The bond between sisters can be observed in various support systems, such as women’s groups or organizations that promote solidarity and empowerment.
    • In spiritual contexts, the concept of the divine feminine can be seen in various traditions, where goddesses represent strength, wisdom, and nurturing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of femininity, motherhood, and the unseen yet powerful role women play in society.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships and the strength found in female bonds.
    • In real life, it encourages us to recognize and honor the contributions of women, both seen and unseen, in our communities.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires a deeper appreciation for the divine feminine and its significance in our lives, urging us to foster love, support, and empowerment among women.
