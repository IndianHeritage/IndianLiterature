इन्द्रा याहि चित्रभानो सुताः इमे त्वायवः |अण्वीभिः तना पूतासः ||
indrā yāhi citrabhāno sutā ime tvāyavaḥ | aṇvībhis tanā pūtāsaḥ ||
  • Indrā: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • yāhi: This is a call or invitation, meaning "come" or "approach." It signifies a request for divine presence or assistance.
  • citrabhāno: This term translates to "one with a brilliant or colorful light." It represents the radiant and vibrant nature of the divine, often associated with enlightenment and knowledge.
  • sutā: This means "sons" or "offspring." In this context, it refers to the followers or devotees of Indra, highlighting the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • ime: This translates to "these" or "this," indicating a specific group or community that is being addressed.
  • tvāyavaḥ: This means "we invoke you" or "we call upon you," expressing a desire for divine intervention or support.
  • aṇvībhis: This term can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with vigor," emphasizing the need for power and energy in the invocation.
  • tanā: This means "body" or "form," referring to the physical manifestation of the divine or the community of devotees.
  • pūtāsaḥ: This translates to "purified" or "sacred," indicating a state of spiritual cleanliness and readiness to receive divine blessings.

  • Meaning: The mantra is a call to Indra, inviting him to come and bless the community of his devotees. It emphasizes the need for divine strength and purification, highlighting the relationship between the divine and humanity.
  • Real-life Example: In times of difficulty or when facing challenges, individuals often seek divine guidance or support. For instance, during a significant life event such as a wedding or a new business venture, people may recite prayers or mantras to invoke blessings and strength from the divine, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and strength. It serves as a reminder of the connection between the divine and humanity, encouraging individuals to seek assistance in their endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during challenging times, rituals, or significant life events where one seeks guidance, strength, and purification. By invoking the divine, individuals can find the courage and clarity needed to navigate their paths.
