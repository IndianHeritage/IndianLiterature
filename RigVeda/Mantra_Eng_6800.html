प्रातर्यावभिरागतम्देवभिरजेन्यावसू |इन्द्राग्नीसोमपीतये ||
prātaryāvabhir ā gataṁ devebhir jenyāvasū | indrāgnī somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "prātaryāvabhir ā gataṁ devebhir jenyāvasū | indrāgnī somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "In the morning, we invoke the divine beings who have come to us, Indra and Agni, for the purpose of enjoying the essence of the Soma."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prātaryāvabhir: Refers to the morning or dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and the arrival of light.
    • ā gataṁ: Means "who have come," indicating the presence of divine beings.
    • devebhir: Refers to "the gods" or divine entities, emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the invocation.
    • jenyāvasū: Can be interpreted as "for the purpose of enjoying," suggesting a desire for blessings and abundance.
    • indrāgnī: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god, both of whom are significant in Vedic rituals.
    • somapītaye: Relates to "the essence of Soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic culture, symbolizing immortality and divine nourishment.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Morning rituals in various cultures often symbolize new beginnings, similar to how this mantra invokes the dawn for divine blessings.
    • In modern life, people often seek guidance and blessings at the start of a new venture, akin to invoking Indra and Agni for support.
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of well-being and nourishment in our lives, whether through physical health or spiritual fulfillment.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of invoking divine energies at the start of the day, symbolizing hope, renewal, and the pursuit of blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and support from higher powers in our daily endeavors, fostering a sense of connection with the divine.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace new beginnings with positivity and to seek nourishment for both body and spirit, reflecting the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom.
