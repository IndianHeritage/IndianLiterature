तव द्रप्सा उदप्रुत इन्द्रं मदाय वावृधुः |त्वां देवासो अमृताय कम पपुः ||
tava drapsā udapruta indram madāya vāvṛdhuḥ | tvāṁ devāso amṛtāya kam papuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tava: Your
    • Drapsā: Desire or longing
    • Udapruta: Overflowing or abundant
    • Indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • Madāya: For the sake of joy or intoxication
    • Vāvṛdhuḥ: They have increased or grown
    • Tvāṁ: You
    • Devāso: The gods
    • Amṛtāya: For immortality
    • Kam: What
    • Papuḥ: They have taken or consumed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the overflowing desires that are directed towards the divine, particularly Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and abundance.
    • The phrase "tava drapsā udapruta" suggests that the desires are not just present but are abundant and overflowing, indicating a deep yearning for connection with the divine.
    • The mention of "madāya" implies that these desires are not merely for material gain but are also for spiritual joy and intoxication, reflecting a higher purpose.
    • The second part of the mantra highlights the role of the gods in bestowing immortality, suggesting that the divine connection leads to a transcendence beyond the physical realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often seek fulfillment through various means such as relationships, careers, or spiritual practices. This mantra reminds us that true fulfillment comes from a connection with the divine.
    • For instance, someone who practices meditation may experience an "overflowing" sense of peace and joy, akin to the "udapruta" feeling described in the mantra.
    • In the context of community, when people come together for a common cause, their collective desires and efforts can lead to significant positive change, reflecting the idea of "madāya" where joy is derived from unity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of directing our desires towards the divine and recognizing that true joy and fulfillment come from spiritual connections rather than material pursuits.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our quest for happiness, we should seek to cultivate a relationship with the divine, which can lead to a sense of immortality through legacy, impact, and spiritual growth.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in daily life by engaging in practices that foster spiritual awareness, community involvement, and a focus on higher values, ultimately leading to a more meaningful existence.
