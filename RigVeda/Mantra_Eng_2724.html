निगव्यतā मनसा सेदुर अर्कैः कृत्वानासो अमृतत्वाय गातुम् |इदं चिन् नु सदनं भूय एषां येन मासांऽसिषासन ऋतेन ||
ni gavyatā manasā sedur arkaiḥ kṛṇvānāso amṛtatvāya gātum | idaṁ cin nu sadanam bhūry eṣāṁ yena māsām̐ asiṣāsann ṛtena ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni gavyatā: "from the cow" or "of the cow"
    • manasā: "with the mind"
    • sedur: "to be established" or "to be made firm"
    • arkaiḥ: "with the rays" or "with the light"
    • kṛṇvānāso: "those who are creating" or "those who are making"
    • amṛtatvāya: "for immortality"
    • gātum: "to sing" or "to chant"
    • idaṁ: "this"
    • cin: "if" or "whether"
    • nu: "indeed" or "certainly"
    • sadanam: "the abode" or "the dwelling place"
    • bhūry: "of abundance" or "of plenty"
    • eṣāṁ: "of these"
    • yena: "by which"
    • māsām̐: "of the months"
    • asiṣāsann: "you have been blessed" or "you have been granted"
    • ṛtena: "with truth" or "by the principle of order"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the mind, the divine light, and the pursuit of immortality.
    • The reference to "the cow" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, which can be related to the sustenance we receive from nature.
    • The phrase "with the mind" emphasizes the importance of intention and thought in our actions.
    • Creating with the "rays" suggests that our actions should be enlightened and guided by higher principles.
    • The pursuit of "immortality" can be interpreted as striving for a legacy or impact that transcends our physical existence.
    • The mention of "the abode of abundance" indicates a place or state of being where prosperity and fulfillment are present.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a reminder that all these pursuits should be grounded in truth and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can inspire entrepreneurs to create ventures that not only seek profit but also contribute positively to society, reflecting the idea of abundance and nourishment.
    • In personal development, individuals can focus on cultivating a mindset that seeks enlightenment and truth, leading to a more fulfilling life.
    • In community building, the mantra encourages collaboration and collective efforts that are rooted in shared values and principles, fostering a sense of belonging and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of our thoughts, actions, and the pursuit of a meaningful existence.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to align their intentions with higher principles, fostering a life that is not only prosperous but also rooted in truth and righteousness.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can guide personal growth, ethical business practices, and community engagement, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and abundant life.
