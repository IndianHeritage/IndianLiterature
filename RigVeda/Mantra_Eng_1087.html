वैश्वानर तव तत् सत्यं अस्त्व अस्मान् रायो मगवाणः साचन्ताम् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
vaiśvānara tava tat satyam astv asmān rāyo maghavānaḥ sacantām | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence that sustains life. In real life, we can relate this to the warmth and energy we receive from the sun, which is essential for all living beings.
  • Tava tat satyam astv: This phrase translates to "May that truth of yours be." It emphasizes the importance of truth in our lives. For example, in personal relationships, honesty fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
  • Asmān rāyo maghavānaḥ sacantām: This means "May the powerful one (Maghavan) grant us wealth." It reflects the human desire for prosperity and abundance. In a modern context, this can be seen in the pursuit of career success and financial stability.
  • Tan no mitro varuṇo: "May that which is friendly and protective (Mitro and Varuṇa) be with us." This highlights the need for companionship and protection in our lives. For instance, having supportive friends and family can provide a sense of security and belonging.
  • Māmahantām: This term signifies the greatness or vastness of the divine. It reminds us of the importance of humility and recognizing forces greater than ourselves, such as nature or the universe.
  • Aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: This phrase refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, and the elements of water (Sindhu), earth (Pṛthivī), and sky (Dyauḥ). It signifies the interconnectedness of all elements in nature. In real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where we strive to protect our planet and its resources.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking truth, prosperity, protection, and the interconnectedness of all life. Its significance lies in the reminder to live harmoniously with nature and to value relationships built on trust and support. In today's world, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue ethical living, foster community ties, and engage in sustainable practices that honor the earth and its resources.
