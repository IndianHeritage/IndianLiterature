यत्संवत्सं ऋभवो गाम् अरक्षन् यत्संवत्सं ऋभवो मा अपिंशन् |यत्संवत्सं अभरण भासो अस्यास्ताभिः शमीभिर अमृतत्वम् आशुः ||
yat saṁvatsam ṛbhavo gām arakṣan yat saṁvatsam ṛbhavo mā apiṁśan | yat saṁvatsam abharan bhāso asyās tābhiḥ śamībhir amṛtatvam āśuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: whatever
    • saṁvatsam: year or cycle
    • ṛbhavo: the wise or enlightened ones
    • gām: the world or community
    • arakṣan: protection or safeguarding
    • mā: not
    • apiṁśan: to be harmed or affected
    • abharan: to bring or carry
    • bhāso: light or radiance
    • asyās: of this
    • tābhiḥ: with these
    • śamībhir: by the peaceful or tranquil
    • amṛtatvam: immortality or eternal life
    • āśuḥ: quickly or soon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the cyclical nature of life and the importance of wisdom in protecting the community.
    • It emphasizes that through the guidance of the wise, one can safeguard against harm and bring forth light and peace.
    • The mention of immortality suggests that by following the path of wisdom and protection, one can achieve a state of eternal well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, wise leaders often take steps to protect their members from external threats, much like how this mantra suggests safeguarding the community.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom often find themselves in better positions to navigate challenges, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on enlightenment.
    • Practices such as meditation and mindfulness can be seen as ways to achieve the 'light' mentioned in the mantra, leading to a more peaceful existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and protection in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by embracing knowledge and safeguarding our communities, we can achieve a state of peace and immortality in spirit.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community leadership to personal development, highlighting the timeless relevance of its teachings.
