मन्त्र: श्यावाश्वस्य सुन्वतस् तथा श्रिणु यथाश्रिणोऽत्रेः कर्माणि कृत्वतः |प्र त्रसदस्युम आविध त्वम् एक इन नृषाह्य इन्द्र ब्रह्माणि वर्धयन ||
śyāvāśvasya sunvatas tathā śṛṇu yathāśṛṇor atreḥ karmāṇi kṛṇvataḥ | pra trasadasyum āvitha tvam eka in nṛṣāhya indra brahmāṇi vardhayan ||
  • śyāvāśvasya: This term refers to the "dark horses" or "black steeds," symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or entities that possess hidden potential or capabilities that may not be immediately visible.
  • sunvatas: This word means "of the prosperous" or "of the successful." It emphasizes the importance of prosperity in life, which can be seen in successful businesses or individuals who contribute positively to society.
  • tathā: This translates to "thus" or "so," indicating a continuation or connection between ideas.
  • śṛṇu: Meaning "listen" or "hear," this word calls for attention and awareness, akin to the importance of active listening in communication and relationships.
  • yathāśṛṇor: This term means "as heard" or "according to what is heard," suggesting the significance of knowledge and wisdom gained through listening and learning.
  • atreḥ: Referring to "Atreya," a sage or a lineage, this highlights the importance of tradition and teachings passed down through generations.
  • karmāṇi: This means "actions" or "deeds," emphasizing the significance of one's actions in shaping their destiny and the world around them.
  • kṛṇvataḥ: This translates to "those who perform" or "those who do," reinforcing the idea that action is essential for success and fulfillment.
  • pra trasadasyum: This phrase can be interpreted as "to overcome obstacles" or "to remove difficulties," which is a common aspiration in life, whether in personal challenges or professional endeavors.
  • āvitha: Meaning "cover" or "protect," this word suggests the need for safeguarding one's interests and well-being, similar to how individuals protect their families or communities.
  • tvam: This means "you," addressing the individual directly, which personalizes the message and makes it relevant to each person.
  • eka: This translates to "one" or "unity," highlighting the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • in nṛṣāhya: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the human realm," indicating the context of human experiences and interactions.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes power, authority, and leadership, representing the qualities needed to lead effectively in various aspects of life.
  • brahmāṇi: This term refers to "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic spirit," suggesting a connection to the divine or the greater universe.
  • vardhayan: Meaning "to increase" or "to enhance," this word emphasizes growth and development, whether in personal skills, knowledge, or community welfare.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, prosperity, and the importance of actions in overcoming obstacles and achieving unity. It serves as a reminder that through listening, learning, and performing righteous deeds, individuals can enhance their lives and contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building, encouraging individuals to harness their potential and work collaboratively towards common goals.
