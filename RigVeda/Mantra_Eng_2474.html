वैश्वानराय पृथुपाजसे विपो रत्ना विधन्त धरणेषु गातवे |अग्निर हि देवाम् अमृतो दुवस्यत्य अथ धर्माणि सनताऽ न दूदुषत ||
vaiśvānarāya pṛthupājase vipo ratnā vidhanta dharuṇeṣu gātave | agnir hi devām̐ amṛto duvasyaty athā dharmāṇi sanatā na dūduṣat ||
  • vaiśvānarāya: This term refers to the universal aspect of the divine, often associated with the all-pervading nature of the Supreme Being. In real life, this can be seen as the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, emphasizing unity in diversity.
  • pṛthupājase: This means "to the one who is worshipped widely." It signifies the importance of reverence and devotion in various forms, akin to how people from different cultures and backgrounds honor their deities or principles.
  • vipo: This word translates to "of the wise" or "of the learned." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge in guiding one's actions and decisions, similar to how we seek advice from mentors or experts in our lives.
  • ratnā: Meaning "jewels" or "precious things," this term symbolizes the treasures of life, such as love, friendship, and knowledge. In everyday life, these can be the relationships and experiences that enrich our existence.
  • vidhanta: This means "to create" or "to establish." It reflects the creative force in the universe, akin to how individuals strive to build their dreams and aspirations through hard work and perseverance.
  • dharuṇeṣu: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." It can be related to the strength we find in ourselves and others, especially during challenging times, much like how communities come together to support one another in adversity.
  • gātave: This means "to those who sing" or "to those who praise." It emphasizes the importance of expression and gratitude, similar to how we celebrate achievements and milestones in our lives through songs and gatherings.
  • agnir: This word means "fire," which symbolizes transformation and purification. In real life, fire can represent the passion and drive that fuels our ambitions, as well as the challenges that lead to personal growth.
  • hi: This is a particle that emphasizes the preceding statement, akin to saying "indeed" or "certainly." It reinforces the significance of the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • devām̐: This translates to "the divine" or "the gods," representing the higher powers that guide and protect us. In our lives, this can be seen in the moral and ethical principles that govern our actions.
  • amṛto: Meaning "immortal," this term signifies the eternal nature of the soul and the essence of life. It reminds us of the legacy we leave behind and the impact we have on future generations.
  • duvasyaty: This means "to be desired" or "to be sought after." It reflects the aspirations and goals we pursue in life, similar to how we strive for success and fulfillment in our personal and professional endeavors.
  • athā: This word means "therefore" or "thus," indicating a conclusion or result. It connects the ideas presented in the mantra, showing how they lead to a greater understanding of life.
  • dhar māṇi: This translates to "the principles of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living a life aligned with ethical values, much like how we strive to act with integrity and fairness in our interactions.
  • sanatā: Meaning "eternal" or "everlasting," this term signifies the timeless nature of truth and righteousness. It reminds us that certain values and principles remain constant throughout history.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence. It emphasizes the importance of avoiding negative actions or thoughts that can lead us astray.
  • dūduṣat: This translates to "to be tainted" or "to be corrupted." It serves as a warning against moral decay and the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of universal divinity, wisdom, and the pursuit of righteousness. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living a life guided by ethical principles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, foster meaningful relationships, and strive for personal growth while remaining true to their values. By embodying these teachings, we can contribute to a more harmonious and just society.
