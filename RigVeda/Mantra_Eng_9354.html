यद उष औच्छः प्रथमा विभानाम् अजनयो येन पुष्टस्य पुष्टम् |यत ते जातिमत्वम् अवरम् परस्य महन् महत्याः असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
yad uṣa aucchaḥ prathamā vibhānām ajanayo yena puṣṭasya puṣṭam | yat te jāmitvam avaram parasyā mahan mahatyā asuratvam ekam ||
  • yad uṣa aucchaḥ prathamā vibhānām: This phrase can be translated as "that which is the first light of dawn, the highest among the shining ones." Here, "uṣa" refers to dawn or light, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • ajanayo yena puṣṭasya puṣṭam: This means "by which the nourished is nourished." It emphasizes the idea of sustenance and support, suggesting that the source of nourishment is vital for growth and prosperity.
  • yat te jāmitvam avaram: This translates to "that which is your greatness, the lesser of the great." It indicates a recognition of one's own potential and the importance of humility in acknowledging the greatness of others.
  • parasyā mahan mahatyā asuratvam ekam: This can be interpreted as "the greatness of the other, the supreme, is one." It speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the unity in diversity, highlighting that despite differences, there is a singular essence that binds everyone together.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of enlightenment, nourishment, humility, and unity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing the light within ourselves and others, and the need for mutual support in our journeys.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and enlightenment, much like the dawn brings light after darkness.
    • In relationships, it emphasizes the importance of supporting one another, akin to how nourishment is essential for growth.
    • In community building, it highlights the need for humility and recognition of each other's strengths, fostering a sense of unity and collaboration.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of enlightenment, and the need for mutual support. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and nurturing society.
