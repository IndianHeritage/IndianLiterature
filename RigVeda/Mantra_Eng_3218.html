अव स्य शूराध्वनो नान्ते 'स्मिन नो अद्य सवने मन्दध्यै |शंसात्य उक्तम् उषणेव वेधाश चिकीतुषे असुर्याय मनमा ||
ava sya śūrādhvano nānte 'smin no adya savane mandadhyai | śaṁsāty uktham uśaneva vedhāś cikituṣe asuryāya manma ||
  • Ava sya: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be safeguarded." It emphasizes the importance of seeking protection and support in our endeavors.
  • Śūrādhvano: This term refers to the "path of the brave" or "the way of the heroes." It signifies the journey one takes in life, often filled with challenges that require courage and strength.
  • Nānte 'smin: This translates to "in this world" or "in this existence." It highlights the context of our lives and the environment we navigate.
  • No adya: Meaning "not today," it suggests a sense of urgency or immediacy in the request for protection and guidance.
  • Savane mandadhyai: This phrase can be understood as "in the gathering of the divine" or "in the assembly of the sacred." It points to the importance of community and collective efforts in achieving goals.
  • Śaṁsāty uktham: This means "the utterance of praise" or "the expression of devotion." It emphasizes the significance of acknowledging and honoring the divine or higher powers.
  • Uśaneva: This term refers to "like the sun" or "as the sun does." It symbolizes illumination, guidance, and the dispelling of darkness in our lives.
  • Vedhāś: This translates to "the seer" or "the wise one." It represents the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek understanding and insight.
  • Cikituṣe: Meaning "to be aware" or "to be mindful," it stresses the importance of consciousness and awareness in our actions and decisions.
  • Asuryāya: This term can be interpreted as "to the non-divine" or "to the unworthy." It serves as a reminder to be cautious of negative influences or distractions that may lead us astray.
  • Manma: This translates to "my mind" or "my thoughts," indicating the personal nature of the prayer and the focus on one's inner self.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking protection and guidance on the courageous path of life. It emphasizes the importance of community, wisdom, and mindfulness while being aware of negative influences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as facing personal or professional obstacles, where one seeks strength and support from both the divine and the community. It serves as a reminder to remain focused on positive thoughts and actions, fostering a sense of resilience and purpose in our daily lives.
