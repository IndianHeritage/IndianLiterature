मन्त्र: इन्द्राग्नि युवोर अपि वसु दिव्यानी पार्थिवा |आ न इह प्र यच्छतं रयिं विश्वायुपोषसम ||
indrāgnī yuvor api vasu divyāni pārthivā | ā na iha pra yacchataṁ rayiṁ viśvāyupoṣasam ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and rain, while Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Yuvor: This word means "of the young" or "of the youthful." It signifies vitality, freshness, and the potential for growth and development.
  • Api: This term translates to "also" or "even," indicating inclusivity or addition.
  • Vasu: Vasu refers to wealth, prosperity, and material abundance. It is often associated with the elements that provide sustenance and comfort.
  • Divyāni: This word means "divine" or "heavenly," suggesting something that is beyond the ordinary, imbued with spiritual significance.
  • Pārthivā: This term means "earthly" or "terrestrial," referring to the physical world and its resources.
  • Ā na: This phrase translates to "may we not" or "let us not," indicating a plea or a desire to avoid something negative.
  • Iha: This word means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment and context.
  • Pra yacchataṁ: This term means "may grant" or "may provide," expressing a wish for generosity and abundance.
  • Rayiṁ: This word refers to wealth or resources, often in the context of prosperity and well-being.
  • Viśvāyupoṣasam: This term can be interpreted as "to all beings" or "to the universe," indicating a universal appeal for abundance and support.

The mantra essentially invokes the combined powers of Indra and Agni to bestow divine and earthly wealth upon the young and vibrant. It emphasizes the importance of both spiritual and material prosperity, suggesting that true wealth encompasses both divine blessings and earthly resources.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Young individuals can recite this mantra to seek guidance and blessings for their personal and professional development, encouraging them to harness their youthful energy for positive outcomes.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can use this mantra during rituals or gatherings to invoke blessings for collective wealth and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to both divine and earthly wealth can inspire individuals to appreciate and protect natural resources, recognizing their importance in sustaining life and prosperity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual and material wealth. It encourages individuals and communities to seek balance and harmony in their pursuits, ensuring that both divine blessings and earthly resources contribute to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
