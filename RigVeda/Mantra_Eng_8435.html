यज्ञस्य केतुः पवते स्वध्वरः सोमो देवानाम उप याती निष्कृतम् |सहस्रधारः परि कोशम् अर्षति वृषा पवित्रम् अत्य एति रोर्वत ||
yajñasya ketuḥ pavate svadhvaraḥ somo devānām upa yāti niṣkṛtam | sahasradhāraḥ pari kośam arṣati vṛṣā pavitram aty eti roruvat ||
  • yajñasya ketuḥ: This phrase refers to the "flag" or "banner" of the sacrifice (yajña). In Vedic tradition, yajñas are rituals performed to invoke divine blessings. The "flag" symbolizes the purpose and intention behind these rituals.
  • pavate svadhvaraḥ: This means "the wind blows with its own nature." Here, "pavate" signifies the wind, and "svadhvaraḥ" indicates its inherent qualities. This can be interpreted as the natural forces acting according to their own laws, which can be likened to how individuals should act according to their true nature.
  • somo devānām upa yāti niṣkṛtam: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, associated with immortality and divine consciousness. "Devānām" means "of the gods," and "upa yāti niṣkṛtam" suggests that it approaches the divine in a purified state. This can be seen as a metaphor for how our actions, when performed with purity and intention, can lead us closer to the divine.
  • sahasradhāraḥ: This term means "having a thousand streams." It symbolizes abundance and the flow of resources or blessings. In real life, this can be related to the idea of having multiple avenues of support or prosperity.
  • pari kośam arṣati: "Pari" means "around," and "kośam" refers to "the sheath" or "the covering." "Arṣati" indicates the act of shaking or moving. This can be interpreted as the idea that the divine presence surrounds and supports us, even when we may not be aware of it.
  • vṛṣā pavitram aty eti roruvat: "Vṛṣā" refers to strength or power, "pavitram" means "pure," and "aty eti roruvat" suggests transcending or going beyond. This can be understood as the strength of purity that allows one to rise above challenges and obstacles, akin to how individuals can overcome difficulties through integrity and virtue.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of intention, purity, and the natural flow of life. It signifies that through our actions (yajñas), we can connect with the divine and receive blessings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, or any endeavor where one seeks to contribute positively. By acting with purity and purpose, individuals can create a ripple effect of positivity and abundance in their lives and the lives of others. The mantra serves as a reminder that our true nature and intentions can lead us to greater heights and deeper connections with the universe.
