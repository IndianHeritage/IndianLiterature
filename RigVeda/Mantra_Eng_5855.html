अर्वन्तो न श्रवसो भिक्षमाणाः इन्द्रवायू सुश्रुतिभिर वसिष्ठाः |वाजयन्तः स्व अवसे हुवेम यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
arvanto na śravaso bhikṣamāṇā indravāyū suṣṭutibhir vasiṣṭhāḥ | vājayantaḥ sv avase huvema yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvanto: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • na: Not.
    • śravaso: Of fame or glory.
    • bhikṣamāṇā: Seeking or desiring.
    • indravāyū: Indra, the god of rain and thunder, and Vayu, the god of wind.
    • suṣṭutibhir: With excellent praises.
    • vasiṣṭhāḥ: The best or the most excellent.
    • vājayantaḥ: Those who bring strength or nourishment.
    • sv: Our or own.
    • avase: In our abode or dwelling.
    • huvema: We invoke or call upon.
    • yūyam: You (plural).
    • pāta: Protect or safeguard.
    • svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness or well-being.
    • sadā: Always.
    • naḥ: Us or our.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful deities Indra and Vayu, seeking their protection and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength, glory, and nourishment in our lives.
    • The mantra calls upon these deities to safeguard us with auspiciousness and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek strength and support from higher powers, similar to how this mantra invokes divine protection.
    • Communities come together to celebrate festivals, invoking blessings for prosperity and well-being, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal challenges, people may recite this mantra or similar prayers to find courage and strength, illustrating the universal need for divine support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the human desire for strength, protection, and auspiciousness in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal struggles, community gatherings, and spiritual practices.
    • By invoking the blessings of powerful deities, individuals and communities can find solace, strength, and a sense of security in their lives.
