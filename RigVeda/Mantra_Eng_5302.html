यस्य श्रवो रोधसी अन्तर उर्वी शीरष्णे-शीरष्णे विभभाजां विभक्तां | सप्तेद इन्द्रं न श्रवतः गृणन्ति न युध्यामधिम अशिशाद अभीक ||
yasya śravo rodasī antar urvī śīrṣṇe-śīrṣṇe vibabhājā vibhaktā | sapted indraṁ na sravato gṛṇanti ni yudhyāmadhim aśiśād abhīke ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: whose
    • śravo: fame or glory
    • rodasī: in the two worlds (heavens and earth)
    • antar: within
    • urvī: the vastness or expanse
    • śīrṣṇe-śīrṣṇe: at the head or top
    • vibabhājā: is divided or distributed
    • vibhaktā: separated or differentiated
    • sapted: seven
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • na: not
    • sravato: of those who flow or stream
    • gṛṇanti: they sing or praise
    • ni: not
    • yudhyāmadhim: in the battle or conflict
    • aśiśād: he has commanded or ordered
    • abhīke: in the face of danger
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the glory and fame of a powerful entity, possibly a deity, whose influence is felt across the two worlds—heaven and earth.
    • The mention of "seven" could symbolize completeness or perfection, often associated with the seven realms or seven elements in various cultures.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges, suggesting that the mantra is a call for divine support in times of conflict.
    • The phrase "in the face of danger" emphasizes the importance of courage and resilience when confronted with adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may invoke the spirit of this mantra when facing tough competition, seeking the strength and wisdom to navigate challenges effectively.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on this mantra during times of crisis, reminding themselves of their inner strength and the support of their community or faith.
    • In sports, athletes often draw inspiration from powerful figures or deities, channeling their energy to overcome obstacles and achieve victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, courage, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to seek help from higher powers while also recognizing one's own capabilities.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts—be it personal struggles, professional challenges, or competitive situations—where resilience and support are crucial for success.
