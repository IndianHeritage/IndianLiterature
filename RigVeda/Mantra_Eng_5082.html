इन्द्रावरुणा मधुमत्तमस्य वृष्णः सोमस्य वृषणां वृषेथाम् |इदं वाम अन्धः परिषिक्तम् अस्मे आसद्यास्मिन बर्हिषि मादयेताम् ||
indrāvaruṇā madhumattamasya vṛṣṇaḥ somasya vṛṣaṇā vṛṣethām | idaṁ vām andhaḥ pariṣiktam asme āsadyāsmin barhiṣi mādayethām ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This term refers to the deities Indra and Varuna, who represent the forces of nature and cosmic order. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna embodies the moral and cosmic order of the universe.
  • Madhumattamasya: This word can be broken down into "madhu," meaning honey or sweetness, and "mattamasya," which implies the essence or the best of sweetness. It signifies the richness and abundance of life.
  • Vṛṣṇaḥ: This term refers to the divine or the powerful, often associated with strength and vitality. It can also denote the qualities of a hero or a champion.
  • Somasyā: "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and represents nourishment for the soul.
  • Vṛṣaṇā: This term relates to the concept of strength and vigor, often linked to the idea of fertility and abundance in nature.
  • Vṛṣethām: This word suggests the act of flourishing or thriving, indicating a state of prosperity and well-being.
  • Idaṁ: This means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or the immediate surroundings.
  • Vām: This term translates to "your" or "of you," suggesting a connection or relationship with the divine or the community.
  • Andhaḥ: This word means "blind" or "dark," often symbolizing ignorance or lack of awareness.
  • Pariṣiktam: This term means "consecrated" or "sacred," indicating something that has been blessed or made holy.
  • Asme: This means "to us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Āsadyāsmin: This term refers to "in this place" or "in this context," emphasizing the importance of the environment or situation.
  • Barhiṣi: This word refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection to the divine.
  • Mādayethām: This term means "may we be delighted" or "may we rejoice," indicating a desire for joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for abundance, strength, and divine blessings in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the forces of nature and the divine in our daily existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, celebrations, or moments of reflection where one seeks to connect with the divine and express gratitude for the richness of life. It serves as a reminder to cultivate awareness, joy, and a sense of community, encouraging individuals to thrive in harmony with the world around them.
