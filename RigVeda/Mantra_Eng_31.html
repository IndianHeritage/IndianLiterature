मन्त्र: सुरूपकृत्नुम ऊतये सुदुघाम इव गोदुहे | जुहूमसि द्यवि-द्यवि ||
surūpakṛtnum ūtaye sudughām iva goduhe | juhūmasi dyavi-dyavi ||
  • Mantra: "surūpakṛtnum ūtaye sudughām iva goduhe | juhūmasi dyavi-dyavi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • surūpakṛtnum: Beautifully formed or well-shaped.
    • ūtaye: For the purpose of nourishment or sustenance.
    • sudughām: Difficult to obtain or rare.
    • iva: Like or as if.
    • goduhe: In the cow's udder, referring to the source of milk.
    • juhūmasi: We offer or we invoke.
    • dyavi-dyavi: In the heavens or celestial realms.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the beauty and nourishment that comes from a well-formed source, likening it to the rare and precious milk found in a cow's udder.
    • The invocation suggests a desire to connect with the divine or celestial realms, seeking blessings and sustenance from higher powers.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and appreciating the sources of nourishment in our lives, both physical and spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often seek the best breeds of livestock that produce high-quality milk, symbolizing the pursuit of excellence and nourishment.
    • In personal development, individuals strive to cultivate their talents and skills, akin to nurturing a well-formed source of potential that can provide sustenance for their growth.
    • In relationships, the mantra can be applied to the importance of nurturing connections that provide emotional and spiritual nourishment, much like the milk from a cow.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking beauty and nourishment in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the rare and precious sources of sustenance, both material and spiritual, that contribute to their well-being.
    • By invoking the divine, it serves as a reminder to appreciate and nurture the connections and resources that enrich our lives.
