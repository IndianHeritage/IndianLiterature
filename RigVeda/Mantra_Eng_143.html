आकीं सूर्यस्य रोचनाद विश्वान देवाम् उषर्बुधः |विप्रो होतेह वक्षति ||
ākīṁ sūryasya rocanād viśvān devām̐ uṣarbudhaḥ | vipro hoteha vakṣati ||
  • Transliteration: ākīṁ sūryasya rocanād viśvān devām̐ uṣarbudhaḥ | vipro hoteha vakṣati ||
  • Meaning:
    • ākīṁ: This word can be interpreted as "to be awakened" or "to be enlightened."
    • sūryasya: Refers to "of the Sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and energy.
    • rocanād: Means "from the radiance" or "from the brilliance," indicating the source of illumination.
    • viśvān: Translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
    • devām̐: Refers to "gods" or "divine beings," representing higher powers or spiritual entities.
    • uṣarbudhaḥ: Can be interpreted as "awakened at dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • vipro: Refers to a "sage" or "wise person," indicating a seeker of knowledge.
    • hoteha: Means "to invoke" or "to call upon," suggesting a plea for divine assistance.
    • vakṣati: Translates to "speaks" or "proclaims," indicating the act of expressing or sharing wisdom.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and awakening through the divine light of the Sun.
    • It suggests that by invoking the universal divine beings, one can gain wisdom and knowledge.
    • The reference to the sage indicates that wisdom is not just about knowledge but also about the ability to share and communicate that knowledge effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity in difficult situations. For instance, during a challenging decision-making process, one might meditate on this mantra to seek guidance and enlightenment.
    • In educational settings, teachers can embody the essence of this mantra by enlightening their students, invoking a sense of curiosity and wisdom.
    • In personal development, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding from various sources, including nature, experiences, and spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge through divine guidance.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom is not only about acquiring knowledge but also about sharing it with others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek clarity, invoke divine assistance, and strive for personal and collective enlightenment.
