स न इन्द्राय यज्यवे वरुणाय मरुद्भ्यः |वरिवोवित परि श्रव ||
sa na indrāya yajyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | varivovit pari srava ||
  • Mantra: "sa na indrāya yajyave varuṇāya marudbhyaḥ | varivovit pari srava ||"
  • Translation: "May we be blessed by Indra, the lord of sacrifices, and Varuna, the god of cosmic order, along with the Maruts (storm deities). May the waters flow abundantly."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Indra: The king of the gods, associated with rain, storms, and war. In real life, Indra represents the power of nature and the importance of respecting it.
    • Varuna: The god of water and the celestial ocean, symbolizing the moral and cosmic order. Varuna teaches us about the balance and harmony in our lives.
    • Marudbhyaḥ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm gods, representing the forces of nature that can be both destructive and nurturing.
    • Yajyave: Pertaining to sacrifices or offerings, highlighting the importance of gratitude and giving back to the universe.
    • Varivovit: Abundant or flowing, indicating the need for prosperity and nourishment in our lives.
    • Pari srava: To flow around or to spread, suggesting the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of harmony with nature and the divine forces that govern our world.
    • It calls for blessings from powerful deities, reminding us to seek guidance and support from higher powers in our endeavors.
    • The invocation of water signifies the essential role of resources in sustaining life, urging us to respect and protect our environment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agriculture, this mantra can be recited to invoke rain and ensure a good harvest, reflecting the dependence of human life on natural resources.
    • In personal life, it serves as a reminder to maintain balance and harmony in our actions, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the environment.
    • During rituals and ceremonies, this mantra can be used to express gratitude for the abundance of nature and to seek blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for abundance and harmony in life.
    • It teaches us the significance of gratitude, respect for nature, and the interconnectedness of all living beings.
    • By incorporating the teachings of this mantra into our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with the environment and the divine, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
