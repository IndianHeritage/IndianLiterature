गोभिष् टरेमामतिं दुरेवां यवेना क्षुधं पुरुहूत विश्वाम् |वयं राजभिः प्रथमां धनान्य अस्माकेन वृजनेन जयेम ||
gobhiṣ ṭaremāmatiṁ durevāṁ yavena kṣudham puruhūta viśvām | vayaṁ rājabhiḥ prathamā dhanāny asmākena vṛjanenā jayema ||
  • gobhiṣ: This word can be interpreted as "with the cows" or "with the herds." In ancient Vedic society, cows were considered sacred and a symbol of wealth and prosperity.
  • ṭaremāmatiṁ: This translates to "may we attain wisdom" or "may we achieve understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and insight in life.
  • durevāṁ: This means "from afar" or "from a distance." It suggests the idea of reaching out to something that may seem unattainable or difficult to grasp.
  • yavena: This word refers to "barley," which is a staple grain in many cultures. It symbolizes nourishment and sustenance.
  • kṣudham: This translates to "hunger" or "thirst." It represents the basic human needs and desires for sustenance and fulfillment.
  • puruhūta: This term can be understood as "the one who is invoked often" or "the one who is called upon." It signifies the importance of prayer and invocation in seeking blessings.
  • viśvām: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates a sense of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vayaṁ: This translates to "we." It emphasizes community and collective effort.
  • rājabhiḥ: This means "with kings" or "with rulers." It signifies the importance of leadership and guidance in achieving goals.
  • prathamā: This translates to "first" or "foremost." It indicates priority and the importance of taking the lead in endeavors.
  • dhanāny: This means "wealth" or "riches." It represents material prosperity and abundance.
  • asmākena: This translates to "by us" or "through us." It emphasizes personal agency and responsibility in achieving success.
  • vṛjanenā: This means "by the means" or "through the efforts." It signifies the importance of action and effort in attaining goals.
  • jayema: This translates to "may we conquer" or "may we triumph." It expresses the aspiration for success and victory.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, nourishment, and prosperity through collective effort and leadership. It highlights the importance of invoking divine blessings and emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings in the pursuit of success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in community initiatives, business ventures, or personal growth endeavors. By fostering collaboration, seeking knowledge, and striving for abundance, individuals and communities can work towards achieving their goals and overcoming challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and the significance of striving for a prosperous and fulfilling life.
