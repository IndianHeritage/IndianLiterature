श्रुधी हवं विपिपानस्याद्रेर बोधा विप्रस्यार्चतो मनिषाम् |कृष्वा दुवांस्य अन्तमा सचेमा ||
śrudhī havaṁ vipipānasyādrer bodhā viprasyārcato manīṣām | kṛṣvā duvāṁsy antamā sacemā ||
  • śrudhī: This term can be translated as "hearing" or "listening." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • havaṁ: This word means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to the divine or higher powers for guidance and support.
  • vipipānasyādrer: This phrase can be broken down into "vipipāna," which refers to the "wise" or "intelligent," and "ādrer," which means "of the earth" or "of the material world." Together, it suggests invoking the wisdom that is grounded in reality.
  • bodhā: This term means "awareness" or "understanding." It highlights the importance of gaining insight and clarity in one's thoughts and actions.
  • viprasyārcato: Here, "vipra" refers to a "sage" or "learned person," and "arcato" means "worship" or "honor." This indicates the reverence for those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • manīṣām: This word translates to "thoughts" or "intellect." It emphasizes the significance of deep contemplation and the pursuit of wisdom.
  • kṛṣvā: This term means "to cultivate" or "to nurture." It suggests the importance of actively engaging in the process of growth and development.
  • duvāṁsy: This word can be interpreted as "the two paths" or "the dual aspects." It signifies the choices and decisions one faces in life.
  • antamā: This term means "the ultimate" or "the end." It refers to the final goal or destination one seeks in their journey.
  • sacemā: This word translates to "may we attain" or "let us achieve." It expresses a desire for success and fulfillment in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of listening, invoking wisdom, and honoring learned individuals. It encourages individuals to cultivate their intellect and make conscious choices in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, personal development, and decision-making processes. By being attentive to knowledge and seeking guidance from wise individuals, one can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve their ultimate goals. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, reverence for wisdom, and the active pursuit of growth and understanding in both personal and communal aspects of life.
