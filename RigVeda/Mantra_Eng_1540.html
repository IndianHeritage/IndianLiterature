पृक्षो वपुः पितुमान नित्य आ शये द्वितीयम् आ सप्तशिवासु मातृषु |तृतीयम् अस्य वृषभस्य दोहसे दशप्रमतिं जनयन्त योषणाḥ ||
pṛkṣo vapuḥ pitumān nitya ā śaye dvitīyam ā saptaśivāsu mātṛṣu | tṛtīyam asya vṛṣabhasya dohase daśapramatiṁ janayanta yoṣaṇaḥ ||
  • pṛkṣo vapuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the form of the one who is perceptive." It suggests a connection to the divine or a higher consciousness that is aware and observant of the world around us.
  • pitumān: This word means "one who is like a father" or "fatherly." It signifies nurturing, protection, and guidance, akin to the role of a father in a family.
  • nitya: Translated as "eternal" or "everlasting," this term emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine presence and its constant support in our lives.
  • ā śaye: This can be understood as "in the abode" or "in the dwelling." It indicates a place of safety and comfort, where one can find peace and solace.
  • dvitīyam: Meaning "the second," this word may refer to duality in existence, such as the balance between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • ā saptaśivāsu mātṛṣu: This phrase translates to "in the seven mothers" or "in the divine feminine." It highlights the importance of feminine energy and the nurturing aspects of creation.
  • tṛtīyam: This means "the third," which can symbolize the triad of existence—creation, preservation, and destruction—reflecting the cyclical nature of life.
  • asya vṛṣabhasya: This refers to "of the bull," which is often a symbol of strength and fertility in various cultures, representing the power of creation.
  • dohase: This word means "of the milking" or "nourishing." It signifies the act of drawing sustenance from the divine or the universe.
  • daśapramatiṁ: Translated as "tenfold understanding," this term suggests a deep and comprehensive wisdom that encompasses various aspects of life.
  • janayanta yoṣaṇaḥ: This phrase means "the women who give birth" or "the creative forces." It emphasizes the role of women in nurturing and bringing forth life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, nurturing, and the balance of energies in the universe. It highlights the importance of both masculine and feminine principles in the process of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our understanding of family dynamics, the roles of parents, and the significance of nurturing relationships. It encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings. By embracing these principles, we can foster a more harmonious existence, appreciating the roles we play in the cycle of life and the wisdom we can gain from our experiences.
