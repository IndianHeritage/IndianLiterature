मन्त्र: चन्द्रमा अप्स्व अन्तर आ सुपर्णो धावते दिवि |न वो हिरण्यनेमयः पदं विन्दन्ति विद्युतो वित्तं मे अस्य रोधसी ||
candramā apsv a1ntar ā suparṇo dhāvate divi | na vo hiraṇyanemayaḥ padaṁ vindanti vidyuto vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • candrama: Refers to the moon, symbolizing calmness, serenity, and the mind's reflective nature.
  • apsu: Means in the waters, indicating the depths of emotions and the subconscious.
  • antar: Translates to within, suggesting introspection and inner exploration.
  • suparṇo: Represents the well-winged one, often associated with the divine or higher consciousness.
  • dhāvate: Means to run or move swiftly, indicating the dynamic nature of life and thoughts.
  • divi: Refers to the heavens or the divine realm, symbolizing higher aspirations and spiritual goals.
  • na vo: Translates to "not for you," indicating a separation or distinction.
  • hiraṇyanemayaḥ: Refers to the golden thread or path, symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
  • padaṁ: Means a step or a place, indicating a journey or a destination.
  • vindanti: Translates to "they find," suggesting discovery or realization.
  • vidyuto: Refers to lightning, symbolizing sudden insight or enlightenment.
  • vittam: Means wealth or resources, indicating material and spiritual abundance.
  • me: Translates to "my," indicating personal ownership or connection.
  • asya: Means "of this," suggesting a specific context or situation.
  • rodasī: Refers to the two worlds or realms, indicating the balance between the material and spiritual.

This mantra speaks to the interplay between the inner self and the external world. It emphasizes the importance of introspection (candrama apsu antar) while recognizing the swift movement of life (dhāvate divi) and the pursuit of prosperity (hiraṇyanemayaḥ padaṁ). The mention of lightning (vidyuto) suggests that enlightenment can come suddenly, illuminating our path to wealth (vittam) in both material and spiritual forms.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During meditation, focusing on the calmness of the mind (candrama) while exploring one's emotions (apsu).
  • In decision-making, recognizing the swift changes in life (dhāvate) and the need for clarity (vidyuto) to find the right path (padaṁ).
  • In financial planning, understanding that true wealth (vittam) comes from a balance of material success and spiritual fulfillment (rodasī).

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of inner reflection and the pursuit of enlightenment. It encourages individuals to seek a balance between their material desires and spiritual aspirations, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
