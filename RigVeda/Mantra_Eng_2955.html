मन्त्र: हिरण्यपाणिः सविता सुजिह्वस त्रिः आद दिवो विदथे पत्यमानः |देवेषु च सवितः श्लोकम् अश्रे राद अस्मभ्यम् आ सुव सर्वतातिम् ||
hiraṇyapāṇiḥ savitā sujihvas trir ā divo vidathe patyamānaḥ | deveṣu ca savitaḥ ślokam aśrer ād asmabhyam ā suva sarvatātim ||
  • hiraṇyapāṇiḥ: This term translates to "the one with golden hands." In a metaphorical sense, it represents abundance and prosperity. For example, a successful entrepreneur who creates wealth and opportunities for others can be seen as having "golden hands."
  • savitā: This refers to the Sun God, a source of life and energy. In real life, the sun is essential for agriculture, providing the energy needed for plants to grow, which in turn sustains life on Earth.
  • sujihvas: This word means "well-disposed" or "beneficial." It signifies the importance of positive intentions and actions. For instance, a community leader who works for the welfare of the people embodies this quality by fostering a supportive environment.
  • trir ā divo: This phrase can be interpreted as "three times in the heavens." It suggests a connection to the divine or higher realms. In practical terms, it can be seen in the practice of meditation or prayer, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or consciousness.
  • vidathe patyamānaḥ: This means "being established in the assembly." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration. For example, a team working together on a project exemplifies this idea, as their collective efforts lead to success.
  • deveṣu ca savitaḥ: This translates to "among the gods, O Savita." It highlights the divine presence in all aspects of life. In real life, this can be seen in moments of inspiration or creativity, where individuals feel a connection to something greater than themselves.
  • ślokam aśrer: This means "the verse that is unshaken." It signifies the enduring nature of truth and wisdom. For instance, timeless teachings from great philosophers or spiritual leaders continue to inspire and guide people across generations.
  • ād asmabhyam: This translates to "may it come to us." It expresses a desire for blessings and abundance. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the practice of gratitude, where individuals acknowledge and appreciate the good in their lives.
  • ā suva sarvatātim: This means "may it be good for all." It emphasizes the importance of universal well-being. For example, initiatives aimed at environmental sustainability benefit not just individuals but the entire planet, showcasing a commitment to collective welfare.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, divine connection, community, and universal well-being. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek abundance not just for oneself but for the greater good. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to act with positive intentions, collaborate with others, and appreciate the blessings in their lives, ultimately fostering a harmonious existence that benefits all. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life that is both fulfilling and beneficial to society as a whole.
