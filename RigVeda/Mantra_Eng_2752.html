आपूर्णोऽस्य कलशः स्वाहा सेक्तेव कोशं सिसिचे पिबध्यै |समु प्रियाऽआववृतन् मदाय प्रदक्षिणिद अभि सोमास इन्द्रम् ||
āpūrṇo asya kalaśaḥ svāhā sekteva kośaṁ sisice pibadhyai | sam u priyā āvavṛtran madāya pradakṣiṇid abhi somāsa indram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpūrṇo: Full, complete
    • asya: Of this
    • kalaśaḥ: Pot, vessel
    • svāhā: An expression of offering, often used in Vedic rituals
    • sekteva: As if pouring
    • kośaṁ: Wealth, treasure
    • sisice: Poured
    • pibadhyai: To drink, to consume
    • sam: Together, in harmony
    • u: And
    • priyā: Beloved, dear
    • āvavṛtran: They have come forth, manifested
    • madāya: For the purpose of joy, intoxication
    • pradakṣiṇid: In a circular manner, often referring to ritualistic circumambulation
    • abhi: Towards
    • somāsa: Of the Soma, a sacred drink
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the fullness and completeness of a vessel (kalaśa) that is being offered in a ritual.
    • The act of pouring (sekteva) symbolizes the sharing of wealth and abundance (kośaṁ) with the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and harmony (sam) in the act of offering.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that the offerings made are to invoke blessings and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the act of sharing resources within a community, such as food drives or charity events where individuals come together to support those in need.
    • During festivals, people often prepare offerings (like sweets or fruits) and share them with family and friends, embodying the spirit of abundance and togetherness.
    • In corporate settings, the idea of sharing success and wealth with employees and stakeholders can be seen as a modern interpretation of this mantra, where the 'vessel' of success is filled and shared.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of abundance, sharing, and community in both spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that true wealth is not just in possession but in the act of sharing and celebrating together.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, a sense of belonging, and a more harmonious society.
