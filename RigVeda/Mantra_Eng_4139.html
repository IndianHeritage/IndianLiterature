मन्त्र: न ये रिणन्त्य ओजसा वृथा गावो न दुर्धुरः |अश्मानं चित स्वर्म्यम् पर्वतं गिरिम् प्र च्यावयन्ति यामभिः ||
ni ye riṇanty ojasā vṛthā gāvo na durdhuraḥ | aśmānaṁ cit svarya1m parvataṁ girim pra cyāvayanti yāmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni ye: those who do not
    • riṇanty: sing or chant
    • ojasā: with energy or strength
    • vṛthā: in vain
    • gāvo: cows (symbolizing wealth and prosperity)
    • na: not
    • durdhuraḥ: difficult to attain
    • aśmānaṁ: stones or rocks
    • cit: gathered or collected
    • svaryaṁ: heavenly or divine
    • parvataṁ: mountain
    • girim: hill or peak
    • pra: forward or towards
    • cyāvayanti: they push away or remove
    • yāmabhiḥ: by those who are wise or knowledgeable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the futility of actions that lack energy and purpose. It suggests that those who do not engage with vigor and enthusiasm in their pursuits will find their efforts to be in vain.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth and prosperity, indicating that without the right approach, even valuable resources may not yield benefits.
    • The reference to "stones" and "mountains" can be interpreted as obstacles or challenges in life. The wise individuals are those who can navigate these challenges effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee who works without passion or commitment may find their contributions overlooked, similar to how cows that are not tended to may not produce milk.
    • In personal relationships, if one does not invest energy and care, the relationship may falter, akin to trying to push away heavy stones without the right tools.
    • In education, students who do not engage actively with their studies may struggle to grasp concepts, just as a mountain remains unscaled without the right preparation and effort.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of engaging with life’s challenges energetically and purposefully. It serves as a reminder that success and prosperity require not just resources but also the right attitude and effort.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in work, relationships, or personal growth, the mantra encourages individuals to approach their endeavors with enthusiasm and determination to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
