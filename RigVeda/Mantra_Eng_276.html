वेद मासो धृतव्रतो द्वादश प्रजावतः |वेदाः य उपजायते ||
veda māso dhṛtavrato dvādaśa prajāvataḥ | vedā ya upajāyate ||
  • veda: This term refers to knowledge or wisdom, particularly the sacred knowledge contained in the Vedas, which are ancient Indian scriptures.
  • māso: This word translates to "month," indicating a period of time that is significant in various rituals and practices.
  • dhṛtavrato: This term means "one who is steadfast in vows" or "determined in purpose," highlighting the importance of commitment and discipline in one's life.
  • dvādaśa: This translates to "twelve," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle, as in the twelve months of the year.
  • prajāvataḥ: This word means "of the progeny" or "of the offspring," emphasizing the importance of family, lineage, and the continuation of life.
  • vedā: Reiterating the significance of the Vedas, this term reinforces the idea of sacred knowledge.
  • ya upajāyate: This phrase means "that which is attained" or "that which is realized," suggesting the outcome of following the teachings of the Vedas.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to embrace the sacred knowledge of the Vedas, to remain steadfast in one's commitments over the course of time, and to recognize the importance of family and progeny in the pursuit of wisdom. It emphasizes the cyclical nature of life and the importance of discipline in achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Just as the mantra emphasizes steadfastness in vows, individuals can apply this by setting personal goals and remaining committed to them, whether in education, career, or personal growth.
  • Family Values: The focus on progeny highlights the importance of family. In modern society, this can translate to nurturing relationships and ensuring the well-being of future generations.
  • Time Management: The reference to months can serve as a reminder to manage time effectively, setting monthly goals to achieve larger objectives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a profound reminder of the significance of knowledge, commitment, and the cyclical nature of life. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can foster personal growth, strengthen family bonds, and achieve our aspirations with discipline and purpose.
