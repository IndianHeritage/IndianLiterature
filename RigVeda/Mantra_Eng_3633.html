मन्त्र: चत्वारि श्रिङ्गा त्रयो अस्य पादाः द्वे शीरषे सप्त हस्तासो अस्य | त्रिधा बद्धो वृषभो रोरवीति महो देवो मर्यां ā विवेश ||
catvāri śṛṅgā trayo asya pādā dve śīrṣe sapta hastāso asya | tridhā baddho vṛṣabho roravīti maho devo martyām̐ ā viveśa ||
  • Mantra: "catvāri śṛṅgā trayo asya pādā dve śīrṣe sapta hastāso asya | tridhā baddho vṛṣabho roravīti maho devo martyām̐ ā viveśa ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • catvāri: four
    • śṛṅgā: horns
    • trayo: three
    • asya: of this
    • pādā: feet
    • dve: two
    • śīrṣe: heads
    • sapta: seven
    • hastāso: of the hands
    • tridhā: in three ways
    • baddho: bound
    • vṛṣabho: the bull
    • roravīti: roars
    • maho: great
    • devo: god
    • martyām̐: into mortals
    • ā: into
    • viveśa: entered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra describes a divine being with a complex form, symbolizing various aspects of existence.
    • The "four horns" can represent the four cardinal directions, indicating omnipresence.
    • The "two heads" could signify duality in nature, such as good and evil, or the balance between material and spiritual pursuits.
    • The "seven hands" might represent the seven continents or the seven chakras, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all life.
    • The "bull" symbolizes strength and fertility, often associated with agricultural prosperity.
    • The phrase "maho devo martyām̐ ā viveśa" suggests that the divine enters the mortal realm, indicating the presence of the divine in everyday life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the bull is a vital animal, representing strength and the ability to cultivate land, which is essential for food production.
    • The concept of balance in life can be seen in practices like yoga, where physical, mental, and spiritual aspects are harmonized.
    • In modern society, the interconnectedness of people across the globe can be likened to the "seven hands," where collaboration and unity are crucial for progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the complexity of existence and the divine's role in the mortal world.
    • It serves as a reminder of the balance between various aspects of life and the importance of recognizing the divine in our daily experiences.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek harmony in their lives, appreciate the strength of nature, and acknowledge the interconnectedness of all beings.
