आन्त्रेब्यस ते गुडाभ्यो वनिष्ठोर हृदयाद अधि |यक्ष्मं मतस्नाभ्यां यक्नः प्लाशिभ्यो वि वृहामि ते ||
āntrebhyas te gudābhyo vaniṣṭhor hṛdayād adhi | yakṣmam matasnābhyāṁ yaknaḥ plāśibhyo vi vṛhāmi te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āntrebhyas: From the depths or the innermost.
    • te: Your.
    • gudābhyo: From the hidden or secret.
    • vaniṣṭhor: The best or most excellent.
    • hṛdayād: From the heart.
    • adhi: Above or over.
    • yakṣmam: To the one who is being invoked or worshipped.
    • matasnābhyāṁ: With the thoughts or intentions.
    • yaknaḥ: Of the sacrifice or offering.
    • plāśibhyo: To the ones who are performing the action.
    • vi: Indeed or truly.
    • vṛhāmi: I offer or I present.
    • te: To you.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of offering and devotion, emphasizing the importance of heartfelt intentions in rituals.
    • The phrase "from the depths" suggests that true offerings come from a place of sincerity and inner truth.
    • It highlights the connection between the heart and the act of worship, indicating that the most profound offerings are those that resonate with genuine feelings.
    • The mention of "sacrifice" signifies the importance of selflessness and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in acts of kindness and charity, where individuals give not just material possessions but also their time and compassion.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of offering from the heart can be seen in how people express love and support for one another, prioritizing emotional connection over material gifts.
    • In professional settings, the mantra can inspire leaders to approach their teams with genuine intentions, fostering a culture of trust and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of heartfelt offerings and the purity of intention in all actions.
    • It serves as a reminder that true value lies not in the material aspects of offerings but in the sincerity and love behind them.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to deeper connections, more meaningful relationships, and a greater sense of community and purpose.
