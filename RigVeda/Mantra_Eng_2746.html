अद्रोघ सत्यं तव तन् महित्वं सद्यो यज जातो अपिबो ह सोमम् |न dyāva इन्द्र तवसस त ओजो नाहां न मासाः शरदो वरन्त ||
adrogha satyaṁ tava tan mahitvaṁ sadyo yaj jāto apibo ha somam | na dyāva indra tavasas ta ojo nāhā na māsāḥ śarado varanta ||
  • adrogha: This term translates to "without malice" or "free from enmity." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and goodwill in human interactions.
  • satyaṁ: Meaning "truth," this word highlights the value of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or association, often referring to a divine or higher power.
  • mahitvaṁ: Translated as "greatness" or "majesty," it signifies the grandeur and significance of the subject being addressed, often a deity.
  • sadyo: This word means "immediately" or "at once," suggesting promptness in response or action.
  • yaj: Referring to "sacrifice" or "worship," it underscores the importance of devotion and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • jāto: Meaning "born" or "arisen," it indicates the emergence or manifestation of something significant.
  • apibo: This term can be interpreted as "even" or "also," suggesting inclusivity or additional significance.
  • ha: A particle often used for emphasis, reinforcing the preceding statement.
  • somam: Referring to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • na: Meaning "not," it negates the following terms, indicating absence or denial.
  • dyāva: This translates to "heaven," representing the celestial realm or divine space.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority.
  • tavasas: Meaning "strength" or "might," it emphasizes the power associated with the divine.
  • ta: A demonstrative pronoun meaning "that," referring to something previously mentioned.
  • ojo: This word signifies "energy" or "vitality," highlighting the importance of life force.
  • nāhā: Meaning "not," it negates the following terms, indicating absence.
  • māsāḥ: Referring to "months," it signifies the passage of time and cycles in nature.
  • śarado: Meaning "autumn," it represents a season of change and transition.
  • varanta: This term can be interpreted as "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the act of giving or providing.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, harmony, and divine greatness in our lives. It calls for immediate devotion and the importance of spiritual nourishment, symbolized by soma. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to promote honesty in personal relationships, encourage community harmony, and inspire individuals to seek spiritual growth through worship and sacrifice. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a life of integrity, strength, and connection to the divine, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
