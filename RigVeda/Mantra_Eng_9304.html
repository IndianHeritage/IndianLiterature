अहम् पितेव वेतसूम् अभिष्टये तुग्रं कुत्साय स्मदिभं च रन्धयम् |अहम् भुवं यजमानस्य राजनि प्र यद् भरे तुजये न प्रियाधृषे ||
aham piteva vetasūm̐r abhiṣṭaye tugraṁ kutsāya smadibhaṁ ca randhayam | aham bhuvaṁ yajamānasya rājani pra yad bhare tujaye na priyādhṛṣe ||
  • Translation: "I, like a father to the plants, offer my prayers to the divine. I bind together the offerings and the desires of the worshipper. I am the one who brings forth the wealth of the earth, and I do so for the one who is devoted, so that they may not be deprived of their desires."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • aham: I
    • piteva: like a father
    • vetasūm̐r: to the plants or vegetation
    • abhiṣṭaye: for the purpose of offering
    • tugraṁ: binding or connecting
    • kutsāya: to the desires or aspirations
    • smadibhaṁ: the offerings
    • ca: and
    • randhayam: I bring forth or create
    • aham bhuvaṁ: I am the one who brings forth
    • yajamānasya: of the worshipper
    • rājani: in the realm of wealth or prosperity
    • pra: for
    • yad: that
    • bhare: I may provide
    • tujaye: for you
    • na: not
    • priyādhṛṣe: deprived of desires
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the nurturing aspect of nature, akin to how a father nurtures his children. For instance, a farmer tends to his crops with care, ensuring they receive the right amount of water and nutrients, much like a father providing for his family.
    • The act of binding offerings and desires can be seen in community rituals where people come together to share their hopes and aspirations, such as during harvest festivals where gratitude is expressed for the bounty of the earth.
    • The idea of bringing forth wealth can be related to entrepreneurship, where individuals work diligently to create businesses that not only fulfill their desires but also contribute to the prosperity of their communities.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the interconnectedness between humans and nature. It teaches us the importance of nurturing our environment and being grateful for the resources it provides. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in sustainable practices, support local agriculture, and participate in community-building activities that honor both personal and collective aspirations. By embodying the spirit of this mantra, we can create a harmonious relationship with the world around us, ensuring that our desires are met without depleting the resources we depend on.
