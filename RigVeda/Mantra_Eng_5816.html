इयम् इन्द्रं वरुणम् अष्ट मे गीः प्रावत तोके तनये तूतुजानाः |सुरत्नासो देववीतिं गमेमा यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyam indraṁ varuṇam aṣṭa me gīḥ prāvat toke tanaye tūtujānā | suratnāso devavītiṁ gamema yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • iyam indraṁ varuṇam: This phrase refers to the invocation of two significant deities in Vedic tradition, Indra and Varuna. Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and war, while Varuna represents the cosmic order and moral authority.
  • aṣṭa me gīḥ: This translates to "eight hymns" or "eight verses." The number eight is often considered auspicious in various cultures, symbolizing completeness and harmony.
  • prāvat toke tanaye tūtujānā: This part can be interpreted as a request for blessings and protection for one's offspring or descendants. It emphasizes the importance of family and the continuity of life.
  • suratnāso devavītiṁ gamema: This phrase suggests a desire to attain divine knowledge or wisdom. It reflects the aspiration to connect with the divine and gain insights that can guide one's life.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This is a plea for protection and well-being. "Pāta" means to protect, and "svastibhiḥ" refers to auspiciousness or welfare. The mantra seeks the constant safeguarding of the individual and their community.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings, protection, and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable during significant life events such as childbirth, weddings, or any new beginnings where the well-being of family and community is paramount. The invocation of Indra and Varuna signifies the balance between strength and moral order, reminding us of the importance of both in our lives. By reciting this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of connection to the divine, seek guidance, and ensure the safety and prosperity of their loved ones.
