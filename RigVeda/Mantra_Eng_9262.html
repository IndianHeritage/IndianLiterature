समुद्रे त्वा नृमणा अप्स्व अन्तर नृचक्षाऽ इधि दिवो अग्न उर्धन |तृतीये त्वा रजसि तस्थिवांसम् अपाम उपस्थे महिषा अवर्धन ||
samudre tvā nṛmaṇā apsv a1ntar nṛcakṣā īdhe divo agna ūdhan | tṛtīye tvā rajasi tasthivāṁsam apām upasthe mahiṣā avardhan ||
  • Mantra: "samudre tvā nṛmaṇā apsv a1ntar nṛcakṣā īdhe divo agna ūdhan | tṛtīye tvā rajasi tasthivāṁsam apām upasthe mahiṣā avardhan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudre: in the ocean
    • tvā: you
    • nṛmaṇā: by the men
    • apsv: in the waters
    • a1ntar: within
    • nṛcakṣā: with human eyes
    • īdhe: I invoke
    • divo: of the sky
    • agna: O Fire
    • ūdhan: who is the source of light
    • tṛtīye: in the third
    • rajasi: in the atmosphere
    • tasthivāṁsam: who stands
    • apām: of the waters
    • upasthe: in the lap
    • mahiṣā: of the great one
    • avardhan: who nourishes
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Agni (Fire) and acknowledges his role in the natural world.
    • The phrase "samudre tvā" suggests a connection to the vastness of the ocean, symbolizing depth and the source of life.
    • By saying "nṛmaṇā apsv," it emphasizes the human relationship with nature, particularly how humans interact with water and fire.
    • The invocation of Agni as "the source of light" highlights the importance of fire in providing warmth, energy, and purification.
    • The mention of "rajasi" and "tasthivāṁsam" indicates the presence of divine energy in the atmosphere, suggesting that the elements are interconnected.
    • Finally, "mahiṣā avardhan" refers to the nurturing aspect of nature, reminding us of the importance of respecting and preserving our environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the relationship between water (apsv) and fire (agna) is crucial for crop growth. Farmers often rely on seasonal rains and sunlight to cultivate their fields.
    • In modern science, the study of ecosystems shows how interconnected elements like water, fire, and air are essential for sustaining life.
    • In daily life, rituals involving fire, such as lighting a lamp or a bonfire, symbolize purification and the invocation of positive energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements in nature and the importance of respecting them.
    • It highlights the significance of fire as a source of life and energy, urging us to acknowledge our dependence on natural resources.
    • In a broader context, it encourages sustainable practices and a harmonious relationship with the environment, applicable in various fields such as agriculture, environmental science, and spirituality.
