तस्मिन्न आवेशया गिरो य एकश् चर्षणीनाम् |अनु स्वधां यम् उप्यते यवं न चर्कृषद् वृषा ||
tasminn ā veśayā giro ya ekaś carṣaṇīnām | anu svadhā yam upyate yavaṁ na carkṛṣad vṛṣā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tasminn: In that (context or situation)
    • Ā veśayā: With the power of speech or words
    • Giro: The utterances or sounds
    • Ya: Who
    • Ekaś: Alone or singularly
    • Carṣaṇīnām: Of the seers or sages
    • Anu: Following or in accordance with
    • Svadhā: The offering or sacrifice
    • Yam: Which
    • Upyate: Is utilized or employed
    • Yavaṁ: Barley or grain
    • Na: Not
    • Carkṛṣad: Is cultivated or grown
    • Vṛṣā: The bull or strength
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of words and the significance of speech in the context of spiritual and material offerings.
    • The phrase "In that (context) with the power of speech" suggests that words have a profound impact on reality and can shape our experiences.
    • The reference to "the utterances of the seers" indicates that wise individuals or sages possess a unique ability to influence the world through their words.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of following the right path (anu svadhā) and making offerings that are meaningful and not merely superficial.
    • The mention of barley (yavaṁ) symbolizes sustenance and nourishment, indicating that true offerings should lead to growth and abundance.
    • Finally, the phrase "not cultivated by strength" suggests that true success and prosperity come from wisdom and intention rather than brute force.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In everyday life, the power of words can be seen in how leaders inspire their followers. For instance, a motivational speaker can uplift an audience and encourage them to pursue their dreams.
    • In relationships, the way we communicate can either strengthen or weaken our bonds. Thoughtful and kind words can foster love and understanding, while harsh words can lead to conflict.
    • In business, effective communication is crucial. A well-articulated vision can rally a team and drive success, while poor communication can lead to misunderstandings and failure.
    • In spiritual practices, mantras and affirmations are used to manifest intentions and create positive change in one’s life, demonstrating the practical application of the power of speech.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of words and their ability to shape our reality. It teaches us that wise speech, aligned with intention and purpose, can lead to growth and fulfillment.
    • In real life, we can apply this wisdom by being mindful of our words, using them to uplift ourselves and others, and making meaningful contributions to our communities.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies not in force, but in the wisdom of our words and the sincerity of our actions.
