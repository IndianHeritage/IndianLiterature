अस्मभ्यं तद् दिवो अद्भ्यः पृथिव्याः त्वया दत्तं काम्यं राधा आ गात् |शं यत् स्तोत्रिभ्य आपये भवात्य उरुशंसाया सवितर जरीत्रे ||
asmabhyaṁ tad divo adbhyaḥ pṛthivyās tvayā dattaṁ kāmyaṁ rādha ā gāt | śaṁ yat stotṛbhya āpaye bhavāty uruśaṁsāya savitar jaritre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • tad: that
    • divo: of the heavens
    • adbhyaḥ: from the waters
    • pṛthivyās: of the earth
    • tvayā: by you
    • dattaṁ: given
    • kāmyaṁ: desired
    • rādha: prosperity
    • ā gāt: may it come
    • śaṁ: peace
    • yat: which
    • stotṛbhya: to the worshippers
    • āpaye: may it come
    • bhavāty: may it become
    • uruśaṁsāya: of great praise
    • savitār: O Sun
    • jaritre: in the act of creation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for blessings and prosperity.
    • It acknowledges the divine source of abundance, asking for what is desired to be granted.
    • The reference to the heavens and the earth signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of life.
    • The invocation of the Sun (Savitār) highlights the importance of light and energy in creation and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, seeking guidance and support in their endeavors.
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective prosperity and harmony.
    • In environmental contexts, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of nature and the need to respect and nurture the earth and its resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving peace and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of gratitude towards the natural world and the divine forces that govern it.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals or communities seek to align their desires with the greater good, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
