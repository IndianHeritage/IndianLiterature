pṛthū karasnā bahulā gabhastī asmadrya1k sam mimīhi śravāṁsi | yūtheva paśvaḥ paśupā damūnā asmām̐ indrābhy ā vavṛtsvājau ||
  • pṛthū karasnā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with broad hands" or "with expansive capabilities." It signifies the ability to embrace and nurture various aspects of life.
  • bahulā gabhastī: Translated as "many lights" or "abundant brilliance," this part emphasizes the presence of knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment in our lives.
  • asmadrya1k: This term refers to "our wealth" or "our resources," indicating the importance of recognizing and valuing what we possess.
  • sam mimīhi śravāṁsi: This can be understood as "may you bestow upon us fame" or "may you grant us renown," highlighting the desire for recognition and respect in society.
  • yūtheva paśvaḥ: This phrase translates to "like a herd of cattle," symbolizing community, cooperation, and the strength found in unity.
  • paśupā damūnā: This can be interpreted as "the protector of the animals," referring to a guardian or a leader who ensures the well-being of the community.
  • asmām̐ indrābhy ā vavṛtsvājau: This part means "may Indra, the god of rain and thunder, shower us with blessings," invoking divine support and abundance in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of community, abundance, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and the nurturing of resources for collective growth. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and environmental conservation efforts. By recognizing our shared wealth and working together, we can achieve greater heights and ensure a prosperous future for all. The invocation of divine blessings serves as a reminder to remain humble and grateful for the support we receive from the universe.
