आसीनासो अरुणीनाम उपस्थे रयिं धत्त दाशुषे मर्यायाः |पुत्रेभ्यः पितरस् तस्य वस्वः प्र यच्छत त इहोरजं दधात ||
āsīnāso aruṇīnām upasthe rayiṁ dhatta dāśuṣe martyāya | putrebhyaḥ pitaras tasya vasvaḥ pra yacchata ta ihorjaṁ dadhāta ||
  • Transliteration: āsīnāso aruṇīnām upasthe rayiṁ dhatta dāśuṣe martyāya | putrebhyaḥ pitaras tasya vasvaḥ pra yacchata ta ihorjaṁ dadhāta ||
  • Meaning:
    • āsīnāso: "of those who are seated" - refers to individuals who are in a position of stability or authority.
    • aruṇīnām: "of the red ones" - symbolizes the dawn or the beginning of a new phase, often associated with hope and renewal.
    • upasthe: "in the presence" - indicates being in a sacred or significant space.
    • rayiṁ: "wealth" or "prosperity" - signifies material and spiritual abundance.
    • dhatta: "bestow" or "grant" - an action of giving or sharing.
    • dāśuṣe: "to the mortal" - refers to human beings, emphasizing our transient nature.
    • martyāya: "to the mortal" - reiterates the focus on human existence.
    • putrebhyaḥ: "to the sons" - highlights the importance of lineage and family.
    • pitaras: "the fathers" - signifies ancestors or parental figures.
    • tasya: "of that" - refers back to the wealth or blessings being discussed.
    • vasvaḥ: "all" or "the entirety" - indicates inclusiveness.
    • pra yacchata: "may they give" - a plea for generosity and sharing.
    • ta ihorjaṁ: "that here energy" - refers to the life force or vitality present in the environment.
    • dadhāta: "may it be established" - a wish for stability and permanence.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sharing wealth and blessings among family and community.
    • It highlights the connection between the living and their ancestors, suggesting that prosperity is a collective responsibility.
    • The invocation of energy and vitality suggests that the blessings bestowed should lead to a flourishing life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be applied in family gatherings where wealth and resources are shared, reinforcing bonds and responsibilities.
    • In community initiatives, such as charity events, the essence of this mantra can inspire collective efforts to uplift those in need.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to recognize their roots and the importance of contributing to the well-being of their families and communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging us to share our blessings and support one another.
    • It teaches that true prosperity is not just about individual wealth but about the collective well-being of society.
    • By embodying the principles of generosity and connection, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous world.
