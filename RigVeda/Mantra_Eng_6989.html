यद देवाः शर्म शरणं यद भद्रं यद अनातुरम् |त्रिधातु यद वरूथ्यं तद अस्मासु वि यन्तनानेहसो व उतयः सुतयः व उतयः ||
yad devāḥ śarma śaraṇaṁ yad bhadraṁ yad anāturam | tridhātu yad varūthya1ṁ tad asmāsu vi yantanānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • yad devāḥ - "that which the gods" - This phrase refers to the divine or celestial beings, emphasizing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • śarma - "comfort" or "shelter" - This word signifies the protection and peace that one seeks in life, akin to the safety of a home or community.
  • śaraṇaṁ - "refuge" - This term indicates a place of safety or a source of support, much like turning to family or friends in times of need.
  • yad bhadraṁ - "that which is auspicious" - This phrase highlights the importance of seeking good fortune and positive outcomes in life.
  • yad anāturam - "that which is unshakable" - This refers to stability and resilience, qualities that are essential in facing life's challenges.
  • tridhātu - "three realms" - This can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of existence, suggesting a holistic approach to life.
  • yad varūthyaṁ - "that which is well-structured" - This emphasizes the importance of order and organization in our lives, akin to a well-planned project or community.
  • tad asmāsu - "that which is within us" - This phrase encourages introspection and self-awareness, reminding us to look within for strength and guidance.
  • vi yantanānehaso - "as the winds blow" - This metaphor illustrates the ever-changing nature of life and the need to adapt to circumstances.
  • va ūtayaḥ suūtayo - "or the good winds" - This suggests the presence of positive influences and opportunities that can guide us towards success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the interplay between the divine, the individual, and the world around us. It emphasizes the importance of seeking comfort, stability, and auspiciousness in our lives while also recognizing the need for introspection and adaptability. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks refuge in family or community, or when making decisions that require a balance of structure and flexibility. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to align ourselves with positive energies and to cultivate resilience in the face of life's uncertainties.
