उशन्तस्त्वा न धिमहि उशन्तः सम इधिमहि |उशन्न उशता आवह पितृन् हविषे अत्तवे ||
uśantas tvā ni dhīmahy uśantaḥ sam idhīmahi | uśann uśata ā vaha pitṝn haviṣe attave ||
  • uśantas tvā ni dhīmahy: This phrase can be translated as "We meditate upon you, O Uśanta." Here, "uśantas" refers to a state of peace or tranquility, and "tvā" means "you." The term "ni dhīmahy" indicates a deep contemplation or meditation.
  • uśantaḥ sam idhīmahi: This translates to "We invoke the peaceful one." The repetition of "uśantaḥ" emphasizes the importance of peace and calmness in our lives. "Sam idhīmahi" suggests a collective invocation, indicating that this is a communal effort to seek peace.
  • uśann uśata ā vaha: This part means "May the peaceful one bring us prosperity." "Uśann" refers to the one who is peaceful, while "uśata" signifies well-being or prosperity. "Ā vaha" implies a request for the peaceful one to carry or bring forth these blessings.
  • pitṝn haviṣe atendimento: This translates to "We offer our oblations to the ancestors." "Pitṝn" refers to the ancestors or forefathers, and "haviṣe" means offerings. This line highlights the importance of honoring our ancestors and seeking their blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of peace, meditation, and the connection to our ancestors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal life, individuals can practice meditation to cultivate inner peace and tranquility, which can lead to better mental health and emotional stability.
  • In community settings, collective meditation or prayer can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, promoting harmony among individuals.
  • Honoring ancestors through rituals or remembrance can strengthen family bonds and provide a sense of identity and belonging.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking peace within ourselves and in our relationships, while also acknowledging the wisdom and guidance of those who came before us.
