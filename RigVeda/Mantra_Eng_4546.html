येभिः सूर्यं उषसम् मन्दसानोऽवासयोऽप दृषाणि दर्ध्रत् |महाम् अद्रिम् परि गा इन्द्र सन्तं नुत्था अच्युतं सदसस् परि स्वात् ||
yebhiḥ sūryam uṣasam mandasāno 'vāsayo 'pa dṛḻhāni dardrat | mahām adrim pari gā indra santaṁ nutthā acyutaṁ sadasas pari svāt ||
  • yebhiḥ - This word refers to "those who" or "by whom." It indicates a group or collective force.
  • sūryam - This means "the sun." The sun is often seen as a source of light, energy, and life in many cultures.
  • uṣasam - This translates to "the dawn" or "the morning." It symbolizes new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • mandasāno - This word means "gently" or "softly." It suggests a calm and peaceful approach.
  • 'vāsayo - This means "to dwell" or "to reside." It indicates a sense of belonging or presence.
  • 'pa - This is a prefix that can mean "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • dṛḻhāni - This translates to "firm" or "strong." It signifies stability and resilience.
  • dardrat - This means "to tremble" or "to shake." It can represent the challenges or disturbances in life.
  • mahām - This means "great" or "mighty." It refers to something of significant power or importance.
  • adrim - This translates to "the mountain." Mountains often symbolize strength, permanence, and challenges to overcome.
  • pari - This means "around" or "about." It indicates encompassing or surrounding something.
  • gā - This translates to "to sing" or "to praise." It reflects the act of expressing gratitude or reverence.
  • indra - This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
  • santaṁ - This means "peaceful" or "calm." It represents tranquility and harmony.
  • nutthā - This translates to "to awaken" or "to rise." It signifies the act of coming into awareness or consciousness.
  • acyutaṁ - This means "infallible" or "eternal." It represents something that is unchanging and reliable.
  • sadasas - This translates to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It indicates community and togetherness.
  • pari - Again, this means "around" or "about," emphasizing the idea of inclusivity.
  • svāt - This means "well-being" or "prosperity." It signifies a state of health and abundance.

The mantra speaks to the collective forces that bring light and new beginnings, represented by the sun and dawn. It emphasizes the importance of a gentle approach to life, the strength found in stability, and the challenges that may arise. The invocation of Indra suggests a call for divine support in overcoming obstacles and achieving peace and prosperity within a community.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Starting a new project or venture, where the dawn symbolizes new beginnings.
  • Seeking strength and resilience in times of personal or communal challenges.
  • Fostering a sense of community and togetherness in efforts to achieve common goals.
  • Practicing mindfulness and gratitude, recognizing the gentle forces that support our well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of resilience, and the power of community. It encourages us to embrace new beginnings with a gentle heart, seek strength in unity, and strive for peace and prosperity in our lives.
