पिब स्वधैिनवानाम् उता यस् तुग्र्ये सचा |उतायम् इन्द्र यस् तव ||
piba svadhainavānām uta yas tugrye sacā | utāyam indra yas tava ||
  • Mantra: "piba svadhainavānām uta yas tugrye sacā | utāyam indra yas tava ||"
  • Translation: "Drink the nectar of your own essence, O Indra, and let it be the source of your strength and power."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • piba: Drink
    • svadhainavānām: of your own essence or nature
    • uta: and
    • yas: who
    • tugrye: strong or powerful
    • sacā: and also
    • utāyam: let it be the source
    • indra: O Indra (a deity representing strength and power)
    • yas: who
    • tava: is yours
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of self-awareness and self-empowerment. It encourages individuals to tap into their own inner resources and strengths.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the concept of self-care and personal development. Just as one must nourish their body with food and drink, they must also nourish their spirit and mind with positive thoughts and actions.
    • For example, a person facing challenges in their career may find strength by reflecting on their past achievements and skills, thus "drinking" from their own essence to fuel their motivation.
    • The reference to Indra signifies the pursuit of strength and power, suggesting that by embracing one's true self, one can achieve greatness and overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to recognize and utilize their inherent strengths. It serves as a reminder that true power comes from within.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various life scenarios, such as during personal challenges, professional endeavors, or even in creative pursuits. By acknowledging and embracing one's unique qualities, individuals can navigate life's complexities with confidence and resilience.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages a journey of self-discovery and empowerment, urging individuals to "drink" from their own well of potential and strength.
