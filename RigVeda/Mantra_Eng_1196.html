मन्त्र: पुरन्दराः शिक्षतां वज्रहस्तास्मान् इन्द्राग्निः अवतम् भरेषु |तन् नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
puraṁdarā śikṣataṁ vajrahastāsmām̐ indrāgnī avatam bhareṣu | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • puraṁdarā: This term refers to "the remover of obstacles," often associated with Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. In real life, this can be likened to a mentor or guide who helps individuals overcome challenges.
  • śikṣataṁ: This means "to teach" or "to instruct." In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of education and learning in our lives, emphasizing the need for guidance in personal and professional growth.
  • vajrahastāsmām̐: Translated as "with the hand of thunder," this phrase symbolizes strength and power. In everyday life, this can represent the force of nature or the strength we find within ourselves to face adversities.
  • indrāgnī: This refers to the combined powers of Indra and Agni, the fire god. Together, they symbolize the dual forces of strength and transformation. In real life, this can be seen in the way challenges can lead to personal growth and transformation.
  • avatam bhareṣu: This phrase means "may they protect us." It highlights the need for divine protection and support in our endeavors. In a modern context, this can be related to seeking support from family, friends, or community during difficult times.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo: "May that (strength) come to us from Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This can be interpreted as a call for harmony and balance in our relationships and interactions with others.
  • māmahantām: This means "the great one" or "the supreme." It signifies the importance of striving for greatness in our pursuits, whether in personal achievements or contributions to society.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi represents the infinite and the mother of gods. This can symbolize the nurturing aspect of life, reminding us of the importance of compassion and care in our relationships.
  • sindhuḥ: This translates to "ocean" or "river," symbolizing abundance and the flow of life. In real life, this can represent the vast opportunities and resources available to us if we are open to them.
  • pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: This means "the earth and the sky." It signifies the connection between the material and spiritual realms. In our lives, this can remind us to balance our earthly responsibilities with our spiritual aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance, strength, protection, and harmony in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, overcome obstacles, and strive for greatness while maintaining balance in our relationships and responsibilities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, personal development, and community building, encouraging individuals to seek support and strive for a harmonious existence.
