यद अत्त्य उपजिह्विका यद वम्रो अतिसर्पति |सर्वं तद अस्तु ते घृतम् ||
yad atty upajihvikā yad vamro atisarpati | sarvaṁ tad astu te ghṛtam ||
  • Mantra: "yad atty upajihvikā yad vamro atisarpati | sarvaṁ tad astu te ghṛtam ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever is eaten by the one with the long tongue, whatever the snake that moves swiftly, may all that be for you as ghee."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad: whatever
    • atty: is eaten
    • upajihvikā: the one with the long tongue (often interpreted as a metaphor for a glutton or a greedy person)
    • vamro: snake
    • atisarpati: moves swiftly
    • sarvaṁ: all
    • tad: that
    • astu: may it be
    • te: for you
    • ghṛtam: ghee (clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment and purity)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of consumption and the nature of what is taken in, both physically and metaphorically.
    • The "one with the long tongue" can represent those who are greedy or consume excessively, while the "snake" symbolizes cunning and swift action.
    • By invoking ghee, a symbol of nourishment and purity, the mantra suggests a desire for wholesome sustenance, both in terms of food and spiritual nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the importance of mindful eating. For instance, when we consume food, we should be aware of its source and its impact on our health and environment.
    • In a corporate setting, the "long tongue" could represent individuals who take more than their fair share of resources or credit, while the "snake" could symbolize competitors who act swiftly to outmaneuver others. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek integrity and fairness in our dealings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of what we consume, both materially and spiritually. It encourages us to seek purity and nourishment in our lives.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to be mindful of our choices, whether in our diet, our relationships, or our professional conduct.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a balance between consumption and integrity, urging us to strive for a life that is fulfilling and wholesome.
