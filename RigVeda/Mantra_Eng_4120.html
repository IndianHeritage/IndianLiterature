यन् मरुतः सभरसः स्वर्णरः सूर्य उदिते मदथा दिवो नरः |न वो अश्वाः श्रथयन्ताह सिस्रतः सद्यो अस्याध्वनः पारम् अशनुथ ||
yan marutaḥ sabharasaḥ svarṇaraḥ sūrya udite madathā divo naraḥ | na vo 'śvāḥ śrathayantāha sisrataḥ sadyo asyādhvanaḥ pāram aśnutha ||
  • yan marutaḥ: This phrase refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities or winds in Vedic literature. They symbolize strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • sabharasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with great energy" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the dynamic and potent nature of the Maruts.
  • svarṇaraḥ: This word means "golden" or "radiant." It signifies purity, wealth, and the brilliance of the divine.
  • sūrya udite: "When the sun rises." The sun is often associated with enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • madathā: This term can be translated as "may we be intoxicated" or "may we be filled with joy." It suggests a state of bliss or ecstasy.
  • divo naraḥ: "The man of heaven" or "the divine being." This phrase indicates a connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • na vo 'śvāḥ: "May your horses not be hindered." Horses in Vedic culture symbolize speed, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • śrathayantāha: This can be interpreted as "those who are pulling" or "those who are guiding." It suggests a sense of direction and leadership.
  • sisrataḥ: "Those who are striving" or "those who are eager." This indicates a proactive approach to life and challenges.
  • sadyo: "Immediately" or "without delay." This emphasizes the urgency and importance of the actions being taken.
  • asyādhvanaḥ: "On this path." It refers to the journey or the way one is traveling in life.
  • pāram aśnutha: "May you attain the other side." This signifies reaching one's goals or achieving success in endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of harnessing natural forces, like the Maruts, and the enlightenment brought by the sun. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, leadership, and the pursuit of goals. For instance, in a professional setting, one might invoke this mantra to inspire teamwork and collective effort, ensuring that all members are motivated and directed towards a common objective. The mantra serves as a reminder to embrace challenges with vigor and to strive for success with the support of both the divine and the community.
