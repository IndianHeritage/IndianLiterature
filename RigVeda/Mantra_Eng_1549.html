अस्मे रयिं न स्वार्थं दामूनसम भगं दक्षं न पप्रचासि धरिणसिम् |रश्मींर इव यो यमति जनमनी उभे देवानां शंसम् ऋत आ च सुक्रतुः ||
asme rayiṁ na svarthaṁ damūnasam bhagaṁ dakṣaṁ na papṛcāsi dharṇasim | raśmīm̐r iva yo yamati janmanī ubhe devānāṁ śaṁsam ṛta ā ca sukratuḥ ||
  • asme rayiṁ: This phrase can be translated as "our wealth" or "our prosperity." It signifies the importance of collective well-being and abundance.
  • na svarthaṁ: This means "not for selfish purposes." It emphasizes the idea of selflessness and the need to act for the greater good rather than personal gain.
  • damūnasam: This term refers to "the one who is restrained" or "self-controlled." It highlights the value of discipline and moderation in one's actions.
  • bhagaṁ dakṣaṁ: This translates to "the skillful fortune" or "the auspiciousness that is skillful." It suggests that true prosperity comes from skill and effort, not just luck.
  • na papṛcāsi: This means "you do not ask." It indicates a sense of confidence and assurance in one's path, suggesting that one should not seek validation from others.
  • dharṇasim: This term can be interpreted as "the one who holds" or "the supporter." It signifies the importance of stability and support in life.
  • raśmīm̐r iva: This translates to "like the rays." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the illumination of the mind.
  • yo yamati: This means "who controls" or "who governs." It emphasizes the importance of self-governance and personal responsibility.
  • janmanī ubhe: This phrase translates to "in both births" or "in both lives," indicating the continuity of existence and the impact of actions across lifetimes.
  • devānāṁ: This means "of the gods." It signifies a connection to the divine and the higher principles that guide human life.
  • śaṁsam: This translates to "blessings" or "auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of seeking and recognizing the positive aspects of life.
  • ṛta ā ca sukratuḥ: This means "truth and good intention." It highlights the significance of living a life based on truthfulness and good deeds.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life of purpose, selflessness, and discipline. It teaches us that true wealth is not merely material but is rooted in our actions and intentions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in community service, where individuals work together for the common good without seeking personal gain. It also resonates in professional settings, where collaboration and ethical practices lead to collective success. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to align our actions with higher principles, fostering a harmonious existence both individually and collectively.
