त्वं-त्वम अहर्त्यथा उपस्तुतः पूर्वेभिर इन्द्र हरिकेश यज्वभिः |त्वं हर्यसि तव विश्वम् उक्त्यम असामि राधो हरिजात हर्यतम ||
tvaṁ-tvam aharyathā upastutaḥ pūrvebhir indra harikeśa yajvabhiḥ | tvaṁ haryasi tava viśvam ukthya1m asāmi rādho harijāta haryatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ-tvam: You, the essence of all.
    • aharyathā: Daily, consistently.
    • upastutaḥ: Praised, worshipped.
    • pūrvebhir: By the ancients, the wise ones of the past.
    • indra: A powerful deity, often associated with strength and leadership.
    • harikeśa: One with a golden mane, a reference to the divine.
    • yajvabhiḥ: By the worshippers, those who perform rituals.
    • haryasi: You are the one who brings joy.
    • tava: Your, belonging to you.
    • viśvam: The universe, all that exists.
    • ukthya: Praise, hymns.
    • asāmi: I am, I exist.
    • rādho: To be praised, to be honored.
    • harijāta: Born of joy, happiness.
    • haryatam: The most joyful, the supreme joy.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the divine presence that is consistently praised by ancient sages and worshippers.
    • It acknowledges the strength and leadership of Indra, a deity who symbolizes power and protection.
    • The phrase "You are the one who brings joy" highlights the importance of joy and happiness in life, suggesting that the divine essence is intertwined with our experiences of joy.
    • By invoking the divine, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals can find joy in simple moments, such as spending time with loved ones or engaging in creative pursuits, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, the collective praise of the divine can foster a sense of unity and shared joy among participants.
    • In leadership roles, embodying the qualities of Indra—strength, protection, and joy—can inspire others and create a positive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives, which brings joy and unity.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote joy, whether through worship, community, or personal reflection.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that joy is a fundamental aspect of existence, and by embracing it, we can connect more deeply with ourselves and the universe.
