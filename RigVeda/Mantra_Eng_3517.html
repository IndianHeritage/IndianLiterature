विदुष् ṭे विश्वा भुवनानि तस्य ता प्र ब्रवीषि वरुणाय वेधः |त्वं वृत्राणि श्रिण्विषे जगहन्वान त्वं वृताम् अरीणां इन्द्र सिंधून् ||
viduṣ ṭe viśvā bhuvanāni tasya tā pra bravīṣi varuṇāya vedhaḥ | tvaṁ vṛtrāṇi śṛṇviṣe jaghanvān tvaṁ vṛtām̐ ariṇā indra sindhūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viduṣ: of the wise or knowledgeable
    • ṭe: your
    • viśvā: all
    • bhuvanāni: worlds or realms
    • tasya: of that
    • tā: those
    • pra: forth
    • bravīṣi: you speak
    • varuṇāya: to Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • vedhaḥ: knowledge or understanding
    • tvaṁ: you
    • vṛtrāṇi: the obstacles or enemies
    • śṛṇviṣe: you hear
    • jaghanvān: the one who brings down
    • tvaṁ: you
    • vṛtām̐: the truth or reality
    • ariṇā: with the enemy
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • sindhūn: the rivers or streams
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine forces, particularly Varuna and Indra, invoking their wisdom and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
    • The reference to "all worlds" signifies the interconnectedness of existence and the need for divine guidance in navigating through it.
    • Real-life example: In times of uncertainty or difficulty, individuals often seek wisdom from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking divine guidance.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the role of divine intervention in human affairs, particularly in overcoming adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding, which are essential for personal growth and overcoming life's challenges.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as decision-making, conflict resolution, and personal development.
    • For instance, when faced with a difficult choice, one might reflect on their values and seek guidance, much like invoking the wisdom of the divine.
