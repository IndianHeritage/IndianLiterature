तरत स मन्दी धावति धारा सुतस्यांधसः |तरत स मन्दी धावति ||
tarat sa mandī dhāvati dhārā sutasyāndhasaḥ | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||
  • Mantra: "tarat sa mandī dhāvati dhārā sutasyāndhasaḥ | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tarat: to flow, to move swiftly
    • sa: that, he, she, it
    • mandī: a term often associated with a gentle or soothing quality
    • dhāvati: runs, flows, or moves
    • dhārā: a stream, flow, or current
    • sutasyāndhasaḥ: of the son of the blind, referring to a lineage or heritage
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a flowing or moving current that is gentle yet powerful.
    • The repetition of "tarat sa mandī dhāvati" emphasizes the continuous and dynamic nature of this flow.
    • The phrase "sutasyāndhasaḥ" can be interpreted as a reference to the potential and capabilities that arise from a lineage, even if it is perceived as limited or blind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a river that flows gently yet persistently, shaping the landscape over time. This can symbolize how consistent effort can lead to significant change.
    • In personal development, individuals may come from backgrounds that seem limiting (like the "son of the blind"), yet they can achieve great things through determination and the right guidance.
    • The mantra can also reflect the idea of nurturing qualities in ourselves and others, promoting growth and understanding despite challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of persistence and the gentle yet powerful nature of progress.
    • It teaches us that regardless of our origins or perceived limitations, we can flow towards our goals with determination and grace.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, community development, and even in professional settings where collaboration and support can lead to success.
