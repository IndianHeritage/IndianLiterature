उप नः सवना गहि सोमस्य सोमपाः पिब |गोडा इद् रेवतो मदः ||
upa naḥ savanā gahi somasya somapāḥ piba | godā id revato madaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "upa naḥ savanā gahi somasya somapāḥ piba | godā id revato madaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O you who partake of the Soma, come to us, drink the Soma, and let the divine intoxication of the gods flow into us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • upa: come near, approach
    • naḥ: to us
    • savanā: of the offerings, the sacred rituals
    • gahi: grasp, hold
    • somasya: of the Soma, the divine drink
    • somapāḥ: those who drink the Soma
    • piba: drink
    • godā: divine intoxication
    • id: indeed, truly
    • revato: of the divine, the radiant
    • madaḥ: intoxication, bliss
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, inviting them to partake in the offerings made during rituals.
    • Soma, in Vedic tradition, is not just a drink but symbolizes the essence of life, inspiration, and divine connection.
    • The act of drinking Soma is metaphorical for receiving divine wisdom and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking inspiration and guidance in our daily actions.
    • For instance, artists and writers often seek a muse or inspiration, akin to drinking the Soma, to create their best work.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke this spirit when making decisions, seeking clarity and divine guidance to lead their teams effectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine connection and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek inspiration and guidance from higher powers in our personal and professional lives.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset open to receiving wisdom and creativity, enhancing their life experiences.
