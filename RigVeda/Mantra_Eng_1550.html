उत नः सुद्योत्मा जीराश्वो होत mandraḥ श्रिणवच चन्द्ररथः |स नो नेषन् नेषतमैर अमूरो 'ग्निर वामं सुवितं वस्यो अच् छ ||
uta naḥ sudyotmā jīrāśvo hotā mandraḥ śṛṇavac candrarathaḥ | sa no neṣan neṣatamair amūro 'gnir vāmaṁ suvitaṁ vasyo accha ||
  • uta naḥ sudyotmā: This phrase can be translated as "may the bright one (or the enlightened one) come to us." Here, "sudyotmā" signifies brightness or enlightenment, suggesting a call for wisdom and clarity in our lives.
  • jīrāśvo hotā mandraḥ: This part refers to "the ancient horse (or energy) that is the priest." The horse symbolizes strength and vitality, while "hotā" indicates a priest or one who invokes. This can be seen as a metaphor for harnessing our inner strength and wisdom to guide us.
  • śṛṇavac candrarathaḥ: Translated, this means "the one who listens, the moon charioteer." The moon often represents calmness and intuition. This line emphasizes the importance of listening and being receptive to guidance, much like how the moon influences tides and emotions.
  • sa no neṣan neṣatamair: This phrase means "may he lead us with the best of guidance." It highlights the need for divine or wise guidance in our journey through life, suggesting that we should seek out mentors or sources of wisdom.
  • amūro 'gnir vāmaṁ suvitaṁ vasyo accha: This can be interpreted as "may the fire (or energy) that is not obstructed, be favorable and beneficial to us." Fire symbolizes transformation and energy, indicating that we should embrace change and seek positive outcomes in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda emphasizes the importance of seeking enlightenment, strength, and guidance in our lives. It encourages us to listen to our inner selves and the wisdom around us, while also embracing change and transformation. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and decision-making. By invoking these qualities, we can navigate challenges more effectively and foster a more fulfilling life.
