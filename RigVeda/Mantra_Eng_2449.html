बृहन्त इद्भानवो भार्जीकम् अग्निं साचन्त विद्युतो न शुक्राः | गुहेव वृद्धं सदसि स्वे अन्तर अपार ऊर्वे अमृतं दूहानाः ||
bṛhanta id bhānavo bhāṛjīkam agniṁ sacanta vidyuto na śukrāḥ | guheva vṛddhaṁ sadasi sve antar apāra ūrve amṛtaṁ duhānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhanta: Great, vast, or expansive.
    • id: Here, this.
    • bhānavo: Of the luminous or radiant ones.
    • bhāṛjīkam: The one who is powerful or strong.
    • agniṁ: Fire, representing energy or divine presence.
    • sacanta: They who are connected or united.
    • vidyuto: Lightning, symbolizing illumination or knowledge.
    • na: Not.
    • śukrāḥ: The pure or bright ones.
    • guheva: Like a cave or hidden place.
    • vṛddhaṁ: The elder or wise one.
    • sadasi: In the assembly or gathering.
    • sve: In oneself or within.
    • antar: Inside or within.
    • apāra: Boundless or infinite.
    • ūrve: Earth or the physical realm.
    • amṛtaṁ: Immortal or eternal.
    • duhānāḥ: Those who churn or extract.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness and power of the divine, represented by fire (Agni) and lightning (Vidyuto).
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and connection among beings, suggesting that true strength comes from collective energy.
    • The reference to the hidden or cave-like nature of wisdom indicates that true knowledge often lies within, waiting to be discovered.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of immortality and the eternal nature of existence, suggesting that through understanding and connection, one can access deeper truths.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In teamwork, the collective effort of individuals can lead to greater achievements, much like the unity mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal growth, introspection and self-discovery can reveal hidden strengths and wisdom, akin to the cave metaphor.
    • In scientific pursuits, the quest for knowledge often requires collaboration, reflecting the idea of connecting energies to illuminate understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by connecting with others and looking within ourselves, we can access the boundless wisdom and strength that exists in the universe.
    • In practical terms, it encourages collaboration, self-reflection, and the recognition of the eternal truths that guide our existence.
