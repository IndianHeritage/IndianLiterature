आर्यमा णो अदितिर यज्ञियासो 'दब्धानि वरुणस्य व्रतानि |युयोत नो अनपत्यानि गन्तोः प्रजावान नः पशुमां अस्तु गातुः ||
aryamā ṇo aditir yajñiyāso 'dabdhāni varuṇasya vratāni | yuyota no anapatyāni gantoḥ prajāvān naḥ paśumām̐ astu gātuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aryamā ṇo aditir yajñiyāso 'dabdhāni varuṇasya vratāni | yuyota no anapatyāni gantoḥ prajāvān naḥ paśumām̐ astu gātuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aryamā: A divine being, often associated with the sun or light.
    • ṇo: Our.
    • aditir: Aditi, the mother of gods, representing infinity and the cosmos.
    • yajñiyāso: Of the sacrifices or offerings.
    • 'dabdhāni: Bound or tied.
    • varuṇasya: Of Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • vratāni: Vows or commitments.
    • yuyota: May unite or connect.
    • no: Our.
    • anapatyāni: Without offspring or progeny.
    • gantoḥ: Of the one who goes or travels.
    • prajāvān: One who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • naḥ: To us.
    • paśumām̐: Of the cattle or animals.
    • astu: Let there be.
    • gātuḥ: Of the path or journey.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly Aditi and Varuna, to bless the offerings made in sacrifices.
    • Aditi symbolizes the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe, while Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order.
    • The phrase "may unite" suggests a desire for harmony and connection among people, emphasizing the importance of community and relationships.
    • The mention of "without offspring" can be interpreted as a plea for prosperity and continuity, highlighting the significance of family and lineage.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a call for unity in families and communities, encouraging individuals to come together for a common purpose.
    • For example, during festivals or communal gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for harmony and prosperity among participants.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity, prosperity, and the importance of divine blessings in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our relationships and to honor the commitments we make to one another.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various life scenarios, such as family gatherings, community events, and rituals, where the focus is on collective well-being and spiritual growth.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing relationships for a prosperous future.
