यथा वशन्ति देवास तथेद असत तद एषां नकिरे मिनत् |अरावाः चन मर्त्यः ||
yathā vaśanti devās tathed asat tad eṣāṁ nakir ā minat | arāvā cana martyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yathā vaśanti devās tathed asat tad eṣāṁ nakir ā minat | arāvā cana martyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "As the gods are pleased, so may that which is not true be far from us; let not the mortal be deceived."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yathā: As
    • vaśanti: are pleased or are controlled
    • devās: gods
    • tathed: so
    • asat: that which is not true or falsehood
    • tad: that
    • eṣāṁ: of these
    • nakir: may it not be
    • ā minat: deceived or misled
    • arāvā: let not
    • cana: or
    • martyaḥ: mortal or human being
  • Meaning and Context:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and the divine influence of the gods in guiding human actions.
    • It suggests that just as the gods are pleased with truth and righteousness, humans should strive to distance themselves from falsehood.
    • The phrase "let not the mortal be deceived" serves as a reminder to remain vigilant against deception and to seek clarity in thoughts and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to ethical business practices. For instance, a company that values transparency and honesty in its dealings is more likely to earn the trust of its clients and stakeholders.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful fosters stronger bonds. For example, open communication between friends or partners can prevent misunderstandings and build a foundation of trust.
    • In the context of social media, where misinformation can spread rapidly, this mantra serves as a reminder to verify facts before sharing information, thus promoting a culture of truthfulness.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for integrity and the pursuit of truth in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance in their actions and to remain steadfast against the temptations of falsehood.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more honest and harmonious society.
