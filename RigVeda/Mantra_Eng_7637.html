देवीं वाचम जनयन्त देवास तां विश्वरूपाः पशवो वदन्ति |सा नो मन्द्रेषम् ऊर्जं दूहानां धेनुर वाग अस्मान उप सुष्टुतैतु ||
devīṁ vācam ajanayanta devās tāṁ viśvarūpāḥ paśavo vadanti | sā no mandreṣam ūrjaṁ duhānā dhenur vāg asmān upa suṣṭutaitu ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to: "The gods gave birth to the divine speech; the universal forms speak of it. May that speech, which is rich in energy, nourish us like a cow that gives milk, and may it come to us with great praise."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • devīṁ vācam: "divine speech" - Refers to the sacred and powerful words that have the ability to create and transform.
    • ajanayanta: "gave birth" - Indicates the act of creation or manifestation.
    • devās: "gods" - Represents divine beings or forces that govern the universe.
    • tāṁ viśvarūpāḥ: "that universal form" - Suggests the all-encompassing nature of the divine speech.
    • paśavo vadanti: "the beings speak" - Implies that all creatures recognize and express this divine speech.
    • sā no mandreṣam: "may that speech" - A prayer for the divine speech to bless us.
    • ūrjaṁ duhānā: "rich in energy" - Refers to the vitality and power inherent in divine speech.
    • dhenur vāg: "like a cow that gives milk" - A metaphor for nourishment and sustenance provided by divine speech.
    • asmān upa suṣṭutaitu: "may it come to us with great praise" - A request for the divine speech to approach us positively and beneficially.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In communication, the power of words can uplift and inspire individuals, much like the divine speech described in the mantra. For instance, a motivational speaker can influence and energize an audience through their words.
    • In education, teachers use their words to impart knowledge and wisdom, nourishing the minds of students similar to how a cow nourishes with milk.
    • In relationships, kind and encouraging words can strengthen bonds and foster understanding, demonstrating the transformative power of speech.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the power of divine speech as a source of nourishment and energy. It emphasizes the importance of using words wisely and positively in our daily lives. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can strive to communicate effectively, inspire others, and create a harmonious environment. The mantra serves as a reminder that our words have the potential to shape reality and influence the world around us.
