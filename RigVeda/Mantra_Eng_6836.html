अस्तभ्नाद् dyām asuro विश्ववेदाऽमिमीत वरिमाणम् पृथिव्याः |आसीदद् विश्व भुवनानि सम्राड् विश्वेत तानि वरुणस्य व्रतानि ||
astabhnād dyām asuro viśvavedā amimīta varimāṇam pṛthivyāḥ | āsīdad viśvā bhuvanāni samrāḍ viśvet tāni varuṇasya vratāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • astabhnād: He has established or set in place.
    • dyām: The sky or heavens.
    • asuro: The divine or celestial beings.
    • viśvavedā: All-knowing or omniscient.
    • amimīta: He has created or manifested.
    • varimāṇam: The vastness or expanse.
    • pṛthivyāḥ: Of the earth.
    • āsīdad: He has become or has taken form.
    • viśvā: All or everything.
    • bhuvanāni: The worlds or realms.
    • samrāḍ: The ruler or sovereign.
    • viśvet: All or everyone.
    • tāni: Those.
    • varuṇasya: Of Varuna, the god of cosmic order.
    • vratāni: The vows or principles.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the establishment of the heavens and the earth by divine beings.
    • It emphasizes the omniscience of the divine, suggesting that all knowledge and creation stem from a higher power.
    • The reference to Varuna indicates the importance of cosmic order and the principles that govern existence.
    • It reflects the interconnectedness of all realms and the sovereignty of the divine over them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, the idea of a sovereign ruler can be likened to leaders who establish laws and principles that maintain order in society.
    • The concept of cosmic order can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where humans recognize their role in maintaining the balance of nature.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek knowledge and wisdom, reflecting the pursuit of understanding that is alluded to in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine order and knowledge in the universe.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the principles that govern our existence.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, uphold principles of order, and recognize the divine influence in their lives.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a sense of responsibility towards the world and a commitment to living in harmony with the cosmic order.
