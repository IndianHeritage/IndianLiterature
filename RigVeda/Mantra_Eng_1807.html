वयं अद्येन्द्रस्य प्रेष्ठा वयं श्वो वोचेमहि समर्ये |वयं पुरा महि च नो अनु द्यून तन् न ऋभुक्षा नराम् अनु ष्यāt ||
vayam adyendrasya preṣṭhā vayaṁ śvo vocemahi samarye | vayam purā mahi ca no anu dyūn tan na ṛbhukṣā narām anu ṣyāt ||
  • Vayam adyendrasya preṣṭhā: This phrase translates to "We are the beloved of Indra today." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking the blessings of a higher power or authority to achieve success in our endeavors.
  • Vayaṁ śvo vocemahi samarye: This means "We will speak tomorrow in the assembly." This highlights the importance of communication and collaboration in achieving goals. In a modern context, it can relate to team meetings or discussions where collective decisions are made, emphasizing the need for unity and shared purpose.
  • Vayam purā mahi ca no anu dyūn: This translates to "We have been great in the past, and may we continue to be so." It reflects on the achievements and strengths of the past while looking forward to future successes. In everyday life, this can serve as a reminder to learn from past experiences and build upon them for future growth.
  • Tan na ṛbhukṣā narām anu ṣyāt: This means "May we not be deprived of our strength." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and maintaining one's power and capabilities. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development, where individuals strive to enhance their skills and overcome challenges to remain strong in their pursuits.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of seeking divine support, the importance of communication, learning from the past, and maintaining strength. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership roles, teamwork, personal development, and resilience in the face of challenges. By embodying these values, individuals and communities can strive for success and harmony in their endeavors.
