अध ग्मन्तोषना पृच्छते वां कदर्था न आ गृहम् |आ जग्मथुः पराकाद दिवश च ग्मश च मार्त्यम् ||
adha gmantośanā pṛcchate vāṁ kadarthā na ā gṛham | ā jagmathuḥ parākād divaś ca gmaś ca martyam ||
  • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a sense of direction or movement towards a lower state.
  • Gmantośanā: This can be broken down into "gmanta," meaning "to go" or "to move," and "śanā," which can imply "to inquire" or "to ask." Together, it suggests a movement towards inquiry or seeking knowledge.
  • Pṛcchate: This means "to ask" or "to inquire," emphasizing the importance of questioning and seeking understanding.
  • Vāṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "you," indicating the subject of the inquiry.
  • Kadarthā: This translates to "what is the purpose" or "what is the meaning," highlighting the quest for understanding the reason behind actions or existence.
  • Na: This means "not," negating the previous inquiry.
  • Ā gṛham: This translates to "to the house" or "to the home," suggesting a return to a place of safety or comfort.
  • Ā jagmathuḥ: This means "they have gone" or "they have moved," indicating a departure or transition.
  • Parākād: This can be interpreted as "from the beyond" or "from the higher realms," suggesting a connection to the divine or spiritual.
  • Divaś: This means "of the heavens" or "of the sky," indicating a celestial aspect.
  • Ca: This means "and," connecting ideas or concepts.
  • Gmaś: This can be understood as "to go" or "to move," reinforcing the theme of movement.
  • Martyam: This translates to "mortal" or "human," indicating the earthly or human condition.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of inquiry and understanding one's purpose. It suggests that while we may seek knowledge and understanding, we must also recognize the transient nature of existence and the connection between the earthly and the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals often seek to understand their purpose in life. This mantra encourages self-inquiry and reflection, prompting one to ask meaningful questions about their existence.
  • Education: In academic settings, the importance of questioning and seeking knowledge is paramount. This mantra serves as a reminder for students and educators alike to foster an environment of inquiry.
  • Spiritual Practices: Many spiritual traditions emphasize the connection between the earthly and the divine. This mantra can inspire individuals to explore their spiritual beliefs and seek a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra highlights the significance of inquiry and the quest for understanding in the journey of life. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, reflect on our purpose, and recognize the interconnectedness of all existence, both mortal and divine.
