वैश्वानरो महिम्ना विश्वकृष्टिर भरद्वाजेषु यजतो विभावा | शातवनेये शतिनीभिर अग्निः पुरुणीथे जरते सूनृतावान ||
vaiśvānaro mahimnā viśvakṛṣṭir bharadvājeṣu yajato vibhāvā | śātavaneye śatinībhir agniḥ puruṇīthe jarate sūnṛtāvān ||
  • Vaiśvānaro: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, representing the divine energy that sustains life and the universe.
  • Mahimnā: This means "with great glory" or "in a magnificent manner," indicating the powerful and awe-inspiring nature of this divine fire.
  • Viśvakṛṣṭir: This can be interpreted as "the one who pervades all" or "the one who is present everywhere," emphasizing the omnipresence of this divine energy.
  • Bharadvājeṣu: Referring to the Bharadvaja clan, this highlights the importance of community and tradition in the worship of this divine fire.
  • Yajato: This means "to those who perform sacrifices," indicating the ritualistic aspect of honoring the divine fire through offerings and prayers.
  • Vibhāvā: This term signifies "the one who manifests" or "the one who reveals," suggesting that the divine fire brings forth abundance and prosperity.
  • Śātavaneye: This refers to the "one who is nourishing" or "the one who provides sustenance," highlighting the life-giving properties of fire.
  • Śatinībhir: This means "with strong and powerful forms," indicating the various manifestations of fire in different aspects of life.
  • Agniḥ: This is the direct reference to fire, which is considered sacred in Vedic traditions and symbolizes transformation and purification.
  • Puruṇīthe: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is complete" or "the one who fulfills," suggesting that the divine fire fulfills the needs of those who honor it.
  • Jarate: This means "to grow old" or "to age," indicating the cyclical nature of life and the role of fire in the process of creation and destruction.
  • Sūnṛtāvān: This term signifies "the one who is truthful" or "the one who embodies truth," emphasizing the moral and ethical dimensions associated with the worship of fire.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Agni, the divine fire, as a vital force in the universe. It highlights the significance of community, ritual, and the transformative power of fire in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Rituals and ceremonies where fire is used as a medium to connect with the divine.
  • Understanding the importance of community and tradition in spiritual practices.
  • Recognizing the transformative power of fire in personal growth and change.
  • Emphasizing the need for truth and integrity in our actions, as symbolized by Agni.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of fire and its role in sustaining life, fostering community, and promoting ethical living.
