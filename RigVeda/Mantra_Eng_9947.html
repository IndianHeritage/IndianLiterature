सो अभ्रियो न यवसा उदन्यन् क्षयाय गातुं विदन नो अस्मे |उप यत सीदद इन्दुं शरीरैः श्येनो 'योपाष्टिर हन्ति दस्यून् ||
so abhriyo na yavasa udanyan kṣayāya gātuṁ vidan no asme | upa yat sīdad induṁ śarīraiḥ śyeno 'yopāṣṭir hanti dasyūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: that
    • abhriyo: of the rich or prosperous
    • na: not
    • yavasa: the one who is strong or powerful
    • udanyan: rising or elevating
    • kṣayāya: for destruction or downfall
    • gātuṁ: to go or to move
    • vidan: knowing or understanding
    • no: us
    • asme: we are
    • upa: towards
    • yat: that which
    • sīdad: may sit or reside
    • induṁ: the moon or nectar
    • śarīraiḥ: with bodies or physical forms
    • śyena: the eagle or a powerful being
    • 'yopāṣṭir: who worships or adores
    • hanti: destroys or eliminates
    • dasyūn: the wicked or evil ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of prosperity and strength, emphasizing that those who are strong and prosperous should not be led to destruction.
    • The reference to the moon (indu) symbolizes nourishment and the essence of life, suggesting that we should strive for a higher state of being.
    • The eagle (śyena) represents a powerful force that can overcome evil, indicating that through strength and wisdom, one can eliminate negativity and malevolence from their life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader (yavasa) who understands the dynamics of the market (vidan) can guide their team away from potential failures (kṣayāya) and towards success.
    • In personal life, individuals who cultivate positive relationships and environments (indu) can rise above challenges and negativity (dasyūn) through their strength and resilience (śyena).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, wisdom, and positivity in overcoming challenges and negativity in life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek prosperity and to use their strength to protect themselves and others from harm.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of self-awareness and the power of collective strength in achieving a harmonious and prosperous life.
