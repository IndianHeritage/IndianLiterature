देव्याः होतारः प्रथमā सुवाचā मिमानā यज्ञं मनुषो यजध्यै |प्रचोदयन्ता विदथेṣu कारू प्राचीनं ज्योतिः प्रदिशा दिशन्ता ||
daivyā hotārā prathamā suvācā mimānā yajñam manuṣo yajadhyai | pracodayantā vidatheṣu kārū prācīnaṁ jyotiḥ pradiśā diśantā ||
  • daivyā hotārā: This phrase refers to the divine priests or the celestial beings who perform rituals. In real life, this can be likened to spiritual leaders or guides who help individuals connect with the divine through various practices.
  • prathamā suvācā: This means "the first to speak well" or "the one who articulates the truth." In everyday life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who communicate wisdom and inspire others through their words.
  • mimānā yajñam: This translates to "understanding the sacrifice." In a practical sense, it can refer to the importance of selflessness and the sacrifices we make for the greater good, such as volunteering or helping those in need.
  • manuṣo yajadhyai: This means "humans should perform sacrifices." It emphasizes the role of humanity in contributing to the welfare of society. For example, community service or charitable acts can be seen as modern-day sacrifices that benefit others.
  • pracodayantā vidatheṣu kārū: This phrase suggests "those who inspire action in gatherings." It highlights the importance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals, such as in workplaces or community projects where collective effort leads to success.
  • prācīnaṁ jyotiḥ: This translates to "the ancient light." It symbolizes wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations. In real life, this can be reflected in the value of education and learning from the experiences of our ancestors.
  • pradiśā diśantā: This means "guiding in all directions." It signifies the importance of guidance and direction in life. For instance, mentors and teachers play a crucial role in shaping the paths of individuals, helping them navigate their journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance, the importance of communication, and the value of selfless actions in society. It teaches us that through collaboration, wisdom, and sacrifice, we can create a harmonious and prosperous community. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community service, and educational settings, where the focus is on uplifting others and working together for a common purpose.
