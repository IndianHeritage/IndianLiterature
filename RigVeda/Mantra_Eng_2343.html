द्योऽन स्तृभिः चितयन्त खादिनो व्य अभ्रियाऽन द्युतयन्त वृष्टयः |रुद्रो यद् वो मरुतो रुक्मवक्षसो वृष्टाजनि पृष्ण्याः शुक्र ऊधनि ||
dyāvo na stṛbhiś citayanta khādino vy a1bhriyā na dyutayanta vṛṣṭayaḥ | rudro yad vo maruto rukmavakṣaso vṛṣājani pṛśnyāḥ śukra ūdhani ||
  • dyāvo: This word refers to the heavens or the sky, symbolizing the divine and the higher realms of existence.
  • na: A negation, meaning "not" or "no," indicating the absence of something.
  • stṛbhiś: This term can be interpreted as "with the stars" or "by the stars," suggesting a connection to celestial bodies.
  • citayanta: Meaning "those who are aware" or "those who contemplate," it implies a sense of mindfulness or consciousness.
  • khādino: This word means "those who consume" or "those who partake," indicating a sense of action or engagement with the world.
  • vy: A prefix that can denote "in various ways" or "in different forms."
  • abhriyā: This term suggests "those who are nourished" or "those who are sustained," indicating a sense of abundance or support.
  • na dyutayanta: Meaning "not shining" or "not illuminating," it suggests a lack of clarity or enlightenment.
  • vṛṣṭayaḥ: Referring to "rains" or "showers," symbolizing nourishment and the sustenance of life.
  • rudro: A name for the deity associated with storms and the wild, representing power and transformation.
  • yad: Meaning "that" or "which," indicating a connection to what follows.
  • vo: Meaning "to you," indicating a direct address to the audience or the divine.
  • maruto: Referring to the storm gods, symbolizing energy and movement.
  • rukmavakṣaso: Meaning "with golden chests," suggesting richness and vitality.
  • vṛṣājani: This term can be interpreted as "those who bring forth rain," indicating the life-giving aspect of nature.
  • pṛśnyāḥ: Referring to the "clouds," symbolizing the source of rain and nourishment.
  • śukra: Meaning "bright" or "pure," indicating clarity and positivity.
  • ūdhani: Referring to "the one who uplifts" or "the one who elevates," suggesting a sense of growth and elevation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of the heavens, nature, and the divine forces that sustain life. It highlights the importance of mindfulness, nourishment, and the transformative power of storms and rain. In real life, this can be applied to our understanding of the environment and our relationship with nature. Just as the rains nourish the earth, our actions and awareness can contribute to the well-being of our surroundings. The mantra serves as a reminder to be conscious of our impact on the world and to seek harmony with the natural forces that sustain us.
