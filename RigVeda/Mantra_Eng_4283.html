अभूद उषा रुशत्पशुर आग्निर अधाय्य ऋत्वियः |अयोिज वां वृषणवसू रथो दसराव अमर्त्यो माध्वी मम श्रुतं हवम् ||
abhūd uṣā ruśatpaśur āgnir adhāyy ṛtviyaḥ | ayoji vāṁ vṛṣaṇvasū ratho dasrāv amartyo mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||
  • abhūd - This word translates to "has arisen" or "has come into being." It signifies the emergence of something new, often associated with light or knowledge.
  • uṣā - Referring to "dawn" or "the morning light," it symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • ruśat - This term means "shining" or "radiant," often used to describe the brilliance of the sun or the dawn.
  • paśur - Meaning "the animal" or "the creature," it can represent the natural world and its interconnectedness with human life.
  • āgnir - This translates to "fire," which in Vedic literature symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • adhāyy - Meaning "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," it indicates a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • ṛtviyaḥ - This word refers to "the priests" or "those who perform rituals," highlighting the importance of spiritual guidance and tradition.
  • ayojī - Meaning "to be united" or "to be connected," it emphasizes the idea of harmony and collaboration.
  • vāṁ - This translates to "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or relationship.
  • vṛṣaṇvasū - This term can be interpreted as "the powerful" or "the strong," often associated with divine strength and protection.
  • ratho - Meaning "the chariot," it symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • dasrāv - This word means "the one who is swift" or "the quick," representing agility and responsiveness.
  • amartyo - Translating to "immortal," it signifies the eternal aspect of existence and the divine.
  • mādhvī - This term means "sweet" or "pleasant," often associated with joy and happiness.
  • mama - Meaning "my," it indicates possession or personal connection.
  • śrutaṁ - This translates to "that which is heard" or "knowledge," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and learning.
  • havam - Referring to "the offering" or "the invocation," it signifies the act of giving and connecting with the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of new beginnings, the importance of spiritual guidance, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the need for divine assistance in our journeys, highlighting the significance of rituals and offerings in fostering a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during significant life events such as weddings, new ventures, or personal transformations, where invoking blessings and guidance is essential. It serves as a reminder to embrace new opportunities with hope and to seek harmony in our relationships and endeavors.
