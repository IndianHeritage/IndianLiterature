यो मा इति प्रवोचत्य अश्वमेधाय सूरये |ददद ऋचां सनीं यते ददन मेधाम ऋतायते ||
yo ma iti pravocaty aśvamedhāya sūraye | dadad ṛcā saniṁ yate dadan medhām ṛtāyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • ma - this
    • iti - thus
    • pravocaty - is spoken
    • aśvamedhāya - for the horse sacrifice
    • sūraye - for the brave
    • dadad - gives
    • ṛcā - with hymns
    • saniṁ - together
    • yate - striving
    • dadan - giving
    • medhām - intelligence
    • ṛtāyate - leads to truth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the significance of the horse sacrifice (aśvamedha) in ancient Vedic culture, which was a ritual performed by kings to assert their sovereignty and power.
    • The phrase "who is spoken thus" indicates the importance of the words and hymns used in the ritual, emphasizing that they are not mere sounds but carry profound meaning and intention.
    • The mention of "brave" (sūraye) highlights the valor associated with those who perform such sacrifices, suggesting that courage is a vital quality in leadership.
    • The act of giving (dadad) with hymns (ṛcā) signifies the communal aspect of the ritual, where the collective effort leads to the attainment of intelligence (medhām) and truth (ṛtāyate).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in various forms, such as community service or selfless acts for the betterment of society. For instance, individuals who volunteer for social causes embody the spirit of giving and striving for a greater good.
    • The importance of words and intentions in rituals can be paralleled with modern-day ceremonies, such as weddings or inaugurations, where the spoken vows and commitments hold significant meaning and impact the future of the individuals involved.
    • Leadership today still requires bravery and the ability to inspire others, much like the leaders of ancient times who performed the aśvamedha to demonstrate their strength and capability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, community, and the power of words. It serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in authority but in the ability to inspire and uplift others through collective efforts.
    • In contemporary life, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various fields, including leadership, community service, and personal development, emphasizing the importance of courage, intention, and the pursuit of truth.
