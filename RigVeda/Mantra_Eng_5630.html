सस्वश्चिद्धितन्वः शुम्भमानाः आ हंसासो नीलपृष्ठा अपप्तन |विश्वं शर्धो अभितो मा निसेद नरो न रण्वाः सवने मदन्तः ||
sasvaś cid dhi tanva1ḥ śumbhamānā ā haṁsāso nīlapṛṣṭhā apaptan | viśvaṁ śardho abhito mā ni ṣeda naro na raṇvāḥ savane madantaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sasvaś: always, eternal
    • cid: consciousness, awareness
    • dhi: intellect, understanding
    • tanvaḥ: body, form
    • śumbhamānā: being praised, celebrated
    • ā: towards, in the direction of
    • haṁsāso: swans, symbolizing purity and wisdom
    • nīlapṛṣṭhā: blue-backed, often referring to the sky or the divine
    • apaptan: having attained, achieved
    • viśvaṁ: the universe, all
    • śardho: in harmony, together
    • abhito: around, in all directions
    • mā: not
    • niṣeda: to be restrained, held back
    • naro: man, human
    • na: not
    • raṇvāḥ: those who are engaged in battle, warriors
    • savane: in the offering, in the sacrifice
    • madantaḥ: intoxicated, filled with joy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal nature of consciousness and the importance of understanding one's true self.
    • The reference to "haṁsāso" (swans) symbolizes purity and wisdom, suggesting that one should strive for clarity and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "nīlapṛṣṭhā" can be interpreted as a connection to the divine or the vastness of the universe, indicating that our existence is part of a larger cosmic order.
    • It emphasizes the need for harmony and unity ("śardho") among all beings, urging individuals not to be restrained or held back from their true potential.
    • The mention of "raṇvāḥ" and "savane madantaḥ" suggests that even in the midst of challenges or battles, one should remain joyful and engaged in the act of giving or offering.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, encouraging individuals to work together harmoniously towards a common goal without being held back by personal conflicts.
    • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to seek inner wisdom and clarity, much like a person meditating to achieve a deeper understanding of themselves and their purpose.
    • During challenging times, such as conflicts or crises, this mantra encourages individuals to maintain a positive outlook and find joy in the process of overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness, unity, and joy in the face of adversity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building, reminding us of our interconnectedness and the importance of maintaining a positive spirit.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to embrace our true nature and contribute to the greater good, fostering a sense of peace and fulfillment in our lives.
