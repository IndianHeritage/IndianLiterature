प्र वाता इव डोधत उं मापीताऽयंसत |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
pra vātā iva dodhata un mā pītā ayaṁsata | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • pra vātā iva dodhata: This phrase can be translated as "like the winds that blow." Here, "pra" means "forth" or "out," "vātā" refers to "winds," and "dodhata" means "to move" or "to stir." This imagery evokes the idea of the winds being a powerful force that can influence and change the environment.
  • un mā pītā ayaṁsata: This part translates to "may the waters flow freely." "Un" means "may," "mā" refers to "not" or "without," "pītā" means "to drink" or "to consume," and "ayaṁsata" can be interpreted as "to flow" or "to move." This suggests a desire for abundance and the free flow of resources, akin to the life-giving properties of water.
  • kuvit somasyāpām iti: This phrase can be understood as "the essence of the soma (sacred drink) of the waters." "Kuvit" refers to "essence" or "nature," "soma" is a significant ritual drink in Vedic culture, and "āpām" means "of the waters." This indicates a connection between the sacred and the natural, emphasizing the importance of both in sustaining life.

  • Significance: This mantra reflects the interconnectedness of nature and the divine. It highlights the importance of natural elements like wind and water in sustaining life and the spiritual essence that can be derived from them.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, where the free flow of water is essential for ecosystems. It can also be relevant in discussions about climate change, where the winds and waters are affected by human actions. Additionally, it serves as a reminder of the sacredness of nature and the need to respect and preserve it.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the relationship between humanity and the natural world. It encourages us to recognize the vital forces of nature and to seek harmony with them. By acknowledging the significance of wind and water, we can foster a deeper appreciation for the environment and strive for sustainable practices that honor these essential elements of life.
