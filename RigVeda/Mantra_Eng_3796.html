त्वां विश्वे सजोषसो देवासो दूतम अकृत |सपर्ण्यन्तस त्वा कवये यज्ञेषु देवम् ईळते ||
tvāṁ viśve sajoṣaso devāso dūtam akrata | saparyantas tvā kave yajñeṣu devam īḻate ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and signifies the divine presence or the ultimate reality that is being addressed in the mantra.
  • viśve - Meaning "all" or "universal," it indicates the inclusiveness of the divine, encompassing everything in existence.
  • sajoṣaso - This term can be interpreted as "with joy" or "with harmony," suggesting a state of blissful existence that is associated with the divine.
  • devāso - Referring to "gods" or "divine beings," it emphasizes the connection between the speaker and the celestial entities.
  • dūtam - This means "messenger," indicating a role of communication or connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • akrata - Translated as "created" or "made," it suggests the act of bringing forth or manifesting something into existence.
  • saparyantas - This word means "worshiping" or "serving," highlighting the importance of devotion and reverence in the relationship with the divine.
  • tvā - Again, "you," reinforcing the direct address to the divine.
  • kave - Meaning "poet" or "sage," it signifies the role of the wise in understanding and conveying the divine message.
  • yajñeṣu - This translates to "in sacrifices" or "in offerings," indicating the rituals and practices that connect humans with the divine.
  • devam - Meaning "divine" or "god," it reiterates the focus on the sacred aspect of the addressed entity.
  • īḻate - This means "is worshiped" or "is honored," suggesting the reverence that is due to the divine presence.

The mantra essentially speaks to the universal divine presence that is acknowledged and worshiped by all beings. It emphasizes the joy and harmony that comes from recognizing this divine connection and the importance of rituals and offerings in maintaining that relationship. The role of the poet or sage is highlighted, indicating that wisdom and understanding are crucial in the practice of devotion.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can incorporate this mantra into their daily prayers or meditations, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and promoting inner peace.
  • Community Gatherings: During festivals or communal worship, this mantra can be recited to unify participants in a shared experience of reverence and joy.
  • Creative Expression: Artists, poets, and musicians can draw inspiration from the themes of this mantra, creating works that celebrate the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence that permeates all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to engage in worship and devotion, fostering a harmonious relationship with the universe. By recognizing and honoring this connection, one can experience a deeper sense of purpose and fulfillment in their daily lives.
