तं गावो अभ्य अनूषत सहस्रधारम अक्षितम् |इन्दुं धार्तारम आ दिवः ||
taṁ gāvo abhy anūṣata sahasradhāram akṣitam | induṁ dhartāram ā divaḥ ||
  • taṁ gāvo abhy anūṣata: This phrase can be translated to "those cows that are being invoked." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance. The invocation of cows can be seen as a call for prosperity and sustenance.
  • sahasradhāram akṣitam: This translates to "the one who holds a thousand streams." This can be interpreted as a reference to a divine entity or force that provides endless resources and support. In real life, this can be likened to the abundance of nature, such as rivers and streams that nourish the land and sustain life.
  • induṁ dhartāram ā divaḥ: This means "the one who supports the moon." The moon is often associated with calmness, reflection, and the passage of time. In a broader sense, this can symbolize the importance of stability and guidance in our lives, much like how the moon influences tides and cycles.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, abundance, and divine support. It highlights the importance of invoking prosperity and recognizing the resources that sustain us.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have, whether they are natural, emotional, or spiritual. It encourages gratitude for the abundance in our lives and the importance of nurturing our environment.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and recognizing the abundance around us. It teaches us to be mindful of our resources and to invoke prosperity not just for ourselves, but for the community and the environment as a whole.
