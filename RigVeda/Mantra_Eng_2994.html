त्रिर ā दिवः सवितर वार्याणि दिवे-दिव ā सुव त्रिर नो अह्नः |त्रिधातु राया ā सुवा वसूनि भग त्रातर धिषणे सातये धाः ||
trir ā divaḥ savitar vāryāṇi dive-diva ā suva trir no ahnaḥ | tridhātu rāya ā suvā vasūni bhaga trātar dhiṣaṇe sātaye dhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "trir ā divaḥ savitar vāryāṇi dive-diva ā suva trir no ahnaḥ | tridhātu rāya ā suvā vasūni bhaga trātar dhiṣaṇe sātaye dhāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a prayer to Savitar, the Sun God, asking for his blessings and the bestowal of prosperity and well-being.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • trir: three times or thrice, indicating a sense of completeness or fullness.
    • ā divaḥ: from the heavens, suggesting a divine origin or influence.
    • savitar: referring to Savitar, the Sun God, who represents light, energy, and life.
    • vāryāṇi: blessings or boons, indicating the gifts one seeks.
    • dive-diva: day by day, emphasizing the continuity of blessings.
    • suva: auspicious or good, referring to the nature of the blessings desired.
    • tridhātu: three realms or dimensions, often referring to the physical, mental, and spiritual aspects of existence.
    • rāya: prosperity or wealth, indicating the material and spiritual abundance one seeks.
    • vasūni: wealth or riches, often associated with material success.
    • bhaga: fortune or divine grace, suggesting a connection to higher powers.
    • trātar: to protect or save, indicating a plea for safety and security.
    • dhiṣaṇe: in the realm of knowledge or wisdom, suggesting the importance of understanding.
    • sātaye: for the sake of truth or righteousness, emphasizing moral integrity.
    • dhāḥ: to bestow or grant, indicating the act of giving blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek blessings for success in their endeavors, whether it be in their careers, studies, or personal relationships. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support in all aspects of life.
    • During significant life events such as weddings, new business ventures, or the birth of a child, people may recite this mantra to invoke prosperity and well-being for the future.
    • In times of uncertainty or challenge, individuals may turn to this mantra as a source of strength and reassurance, trusting in the divine to provide protection and guidance.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine blessings for prosperity, protection, and wisdom. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of decision-making, celebrations, or challenges, serving as a reminder of the importance of seeking higher guidance and maintaining moral integrity in all actions.
