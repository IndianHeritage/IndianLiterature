शृण्वे वृष्टर इव स्वनः पवमानस्य शुष्मिणः |चरन्ति विद्युतो दिवि ||
śṛṇve vṛṣṭer iva svanaḥ pavamānasya śuṣmiṇaḥ | caranti vidyuto divi ||
  • śṛṇve: This word translates to "I hear" or "I listen." It signifies the importance of being attentive and receptive to the sounds and messages around us.
  • vṛṣṭer: This means "of the rain" or "of the downpour." It symbolizes nourishment, abundance, and the life-giving force of nature.
  • iva: This translates to "like" or "as if," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • svanaḥ: This means "sound" or "voice." It emphasizes the significance of sound in communication and the natural world.
  • pavamānasya: This word refers to "of the purifying" or "of the wind." It represents the cleansing and refreshing qualities of the wind, which can also symbolize change and transformation.
  • śuṣmiṇaḥ: This translates to "of the dry" or "of the arid." It can refer to the state of being devoid of moisture, highlighting the contrast between dryness and the nourishing rain.
  • caranti: This means "they move" or "they travel." It indicates action and the dynamic nature of existence.
  • vidyuto: This translates to "of lightning." It symbolizes power, energy, and the sudden illumination that can come from insight or revelation.
  • divi: This means "in the sky" or "in the heavens." It represents the vastness of the universe and the connection between the earthly and the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to listen to the sounds of nature, particularly the rain and the wind, which are essential for life. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of the natural phenomena around us and recognizing their significance in our lives. The imagery of lightning and the sky further enhances the idea of connection to a greater power and the dynamic forces of nature.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Environmental Awareness: By listening to the sounds of nature, we become more aware of our environment and the importance of preserving it. For example, the sound of rain can remind us of the need for water conservation.
  • Mindfulness and Presence: The mantra encourages us to be present and attentive in our daily lives. Practicing mindfulness can lead to better mental health and well-being.
  • Spiritual Connection: The reference to the divine and the heavens can inspire individuals to seek a deeper spiritual connection, whether through meditation, prayer, or simply spending time in nature.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of being attuned to the natural world. It encourages us to listen, reflect, and appreciate the beauty and power of nature, which can lead to personal growth and a greater understanding of our place in the universe.
