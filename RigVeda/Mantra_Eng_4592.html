स वेतसुः दशमायं दशोणिं तूतुजिम इन्द्रः स्वभिष्टिसुम्नः |आ तुग्रं शाश्वद इभं द्योतनाय मातुर न सीम उप सृजा इयध्यै ||
sa vetasuṁ daśamāyaṁ daśoṇiṁ tūtujim indraḥ svabhiṣṭisumnaḥ | ā tugraṁ śaśvad ibhaṁ dyotanāya mātur na sīm upa sṛjā iyadhyai ||
  • sa vetasuṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is the master of the waters" or "he who knows the essence of life." In real life, this can relate to individuals who have a deep understanding of nature and its resources, such as environmentalists or water conservationists.
  • daśamāyaṁ daśoṇiṁ: This translates to "the one who is tenfold" or "the one who has ten aspects." This can symbolize completeness or wholeness. For example, a well-rounded individual who excels in multiple areas of life—such as career, family, health, and spirituality—embodies this concept.
  • tūtujim indraḥ: "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. "Tūtujim" can mean "the one who brings forth." This can be likened to leaders or influential figures in society who inspire and empower others, such as community leaders or educators.
  • svabhiṣṭisumnaḥ: This phrase means "with auspicious blessings." It signifies the importance of positive intentions and blessings in our endeavors. In real life, this can be seen in rituals or practices where people seek blessings for success, such as starting a new business or embarking on a significant life change.
  • ā tugraṁ: This can be interpreted as "to the strong" or "to the powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength in overcoming challenges. For instance, athletes or individuals who face adversity and emerge stronger exemplify this idea.
  • śaśvad ibhaṁ: "Śaśvad" means "everlasting" or "eternal," while "ibhaṁ" refers to "the elephant," a symbol of wisdom and strength. This can relate to the idea of enduring wisdom that guides us through life, much like the teachings of elders or mentors.
  • dyotanāya: This translates to "for illumination" or "for enlightenment." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be seen in educational pursuits, where individuals seek to illuminate their minds and the minds of others through learning.
  • mātur na sīm upa sṛjā iyadhyai: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be separated from the nurturing mother." It emphasizes the importance of connection to our roots and the nurturing aspects of life, such as family and community support. In real life, this can be reflected in the bonds we maintain with our families and the importance of community in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, wisdom, nurturing, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek balance in our lives, to draw strength from our connections, and to pursue enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for completeness in their personal and professional lives, to seek blessings in their endeavors, and to maintain strong ties with their roots and communities. It serves as a guiding principle for living a fulfilling and meaningful life.
