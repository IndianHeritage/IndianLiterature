मन्त्र: दिवो रुक्म उरुचक्षाऽ उद एति दूरार्थस तरणिर्भ्राजमानः | नूनं जनाः सूर्येण प्रसूता अयन्न अर्थानि कृत्वन्न अपांसी ||
divo rukma urucakṣā ud eti dūrearthas taraṇir bhrājamānaḥ | nūnaṁ janāḥ sūryeṇa prasūtā ayann arthāni kṛṇavann apāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Rukma: Golden or radiant
    • Urucakṣā: With wide eyes
    • Ud: Up or forth
    • Eti: Goes
    • Dūre: Far away
    • Arthas: Wealth or resources
    • Taraṇir: The one who shines
    • Bhrājamānaḥ: Shining or illuminating
    • Nūnaṁ: Certainly
    • Janāḥ: People or beings
    • Sūryeṇa: By the sun
    • Prasūtā: Born or produced
    • Ayann: They bring
    • Arthāni: Resources or benefits
    • Kṛṇavann: Creating or making
    • Apāṁsi: Of the waters
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the radiant and powerful presence of the sun, which is depicted as a source of wealth and resources.
    • The phrase "divo rukma urucakṣā" suggests the sun's golden and wide-eyed nature, symbolizing its all-seeing and illuminating qualities.
    • "Ud eti dūre" indicates that the sun rises and shines far and wide, spreading its light and energy across the earth.
    • The mantra emphasizes that people, born of the sun, are capable of creating and bringing forth resources and benefits, particularly in relation to water ("apāṁsi").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The sun is essential for agriculture; its light and warmth enable plants to grow, which in turn provides food and sustenance for people.
    • In many cultures, the sun is revered as a deity or a symbol of life, reflecting its importance in daily life and natural cycles.
    • Solar energy is a modern application of the sun's power, harnessing its rays to generate electricity and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the sun as a source of life, wealth, and resources.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, emphasizing the importance of respecting and utilizing natural resources wisely.
    • In real life, the teachings of this mantra can inspire sustainable practices, such as solar energy use and conservation of water, aligning with the idea of creating benefits for all beings.
