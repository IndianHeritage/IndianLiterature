उत स्मासु प्रथमः सरीष्यन् न वेवेति श्रेणिभी रथानाम् |स्रजं कृत्वानो जन्यो न शुभ्वा रेणुं रेरिहत किरणं ददश्वान् ||
uta smāsu prathamaḥ sariṣyan ni veveti śreṇibhī rathānām | srajaṁ kṛṇvāno janyo na śubhvā reṇuṁ rerihat kiraṇaṁ dadaśvān ||
  • uta smāsu prathamaḥ sariṣyan: This phrase can be translated as "the first among those who are united." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in team sports where players must work together to win a game.
  • ni veveti śreṇibhī rathānām: This translates to "he who moves with the groups of chariots." It emphasizes the idea of progress and movement in harmony with others. For example, in a corporate setting, a successful project often requires the coordinated efforts of various departments working together like a well-oiled machine.
  • srajaṁ kṛṇvāno janyo: This means "creating a garland." A garland symbolizes beauty and the fruits of labor. In life, this can be related to the achievements we create through our hard work, such as graduating from school or completing a significant project.
  • na śubhvā reṇuṁ rerihat: This translates to "not desiring the unworthy dust." It suggests the importance of focusing on what is valuable and meaningful rather than getting distracted by trivial matters. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal goals, where one must avoid negative influences and distractions.
  • kiraṇaṁ dadaśvān: This means "having seen the rays." It symbolizes enlightenment and awareness. In practical terms, this can relate to gaining knowledge and insight through experience, much like how a student learns from both successes and failures.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collaboration, and the pursuit of meaningful goals. It teaches us that success is often a collective effort and that we should focus on what truly matters in our lives. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community efforts, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us toward achieving our aspirations while fostering a sense of togetherness and purpose.
