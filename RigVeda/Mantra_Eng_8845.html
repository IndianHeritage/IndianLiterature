द्युर्भिर हितं मित्रं इव प्रयोगं प्रात्नं ऋत्विजं अध्वरस्य जारम् | बाहुभ्यां अग्निम आयवो जनन्त विक्षु होतारं न्य असादयन्त ||
dyubhir hitam mitram iva prayogam pratnam ṛtvijam adhvarasya jāram | bāhubhyām agnim āyavo 'jananta vikṣu hotāraṁ ny asādayanta ||
  • dyubhir hitam: This phrase can be translated as "that which is beneficial from the heavens." It signifies the positive influences and blessings that come from the celestial realm.
  • mitram iva: This means "like a friend." It suggests that the divine or celestial forces act in a friendly and supportive manner towards humanity.
  • prayogam pratnam: This translates to "the application of the ritual." It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and harnessing their benefits.
  • ṛtvijam adhvarasya jāram: This refers to "the priest of the sacrifice." It highlights the role of the priest in performing rituals and ensuring that they are conducted properly to invoke divine blessings.
  • bāhubhyām agnim āyavo 'jananta: This means "with their arms, they bring forth the fire." It symbolizes the active participation of individuals in the rituals, emphasizing the need for human effort in spiritual practices.
  • vikṣu hotāraṁ ny asādayanta: This translates to "they do not neglect the one who invokes." It signifies the importance of acknowledging and honoring those who perform the rituals and invoke the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of rituals and the role of the divine in our lives. It teaches us that the celestial forces are like friends, offering their support and blessings when we engage in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Participating in community rituals and celebrations that foster a sense of togetherness and connection with the divine.
  • Recognizing the efforts of spiritual leaders and practitioners who guide us in our spiritual journeys.
  • Understanding that our active participation and intention in rituals can lead to positive outcomes in our lives.

The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that spirituality is a collaborative effort between the divine and humanity, and that through rituals, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and its benevolent forces.
