इन्द्रे भुजं शशमानास आषत सूरो दृष्टिके वृषणश च पौंस्ये |प्र ये nv अस्याऽर्हणाऽ तातक्षिरे युजं वज्रं नृषदनेषु कारवः ||
indre bhujaṁ śaśamānāsa āśata sūro dṛśīke vṛṣaṇaś ca pauṁsye | pra ye nv asyārhaṇā tatakṣire yujaṁ vajraṁ nṛṣadaneṣu kāravaḥ ||
  • Indre: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Bhujaṁ: Means 'arm' or 'strength', indicating the might and capability of Indra.
  • Śaśamānāsa: This term can be interpreted as 'one who is like the moon', suggesting a sense of calmness and stability.
  • Āśata: Means 'to be strong' or 'to be powerful', reinforcing the idea of strength.
  • Sūro: Translates to 'hero' or 'brave one', emphasizing valor and courage.
  • Dṛśīke: Refers to 'the one who sees', indicating wisdom and insight.
  • Vṛṣaṇaś: Means 'the one who is strong', again highlighting the theme of power.
  • Pauṁsye: Refers to 'in manliness' or 'in strength', connecting to the idea of masculinity and vigor.
  • Pra ye: Means 'those who', indicating a collective or community aspect.
  • Nv: Can be interpreted as 'to' or 'towards', suggesting direction or purpose.
  • Asyārhaṇā: Refers to 'the worthy offerings', indicating the importance of giving and sacrifice.
  • Tatakṣire: Means 'they have accepted', suggesting a sense of acknowledgment or approval.
  • Yujaṁ: Translates to 'to join' or 'to unite', emphasizing the importance of unity.
  • Vajraṁ: Refers to 'thunderbolt' or 'diamond', symbolizing indestructibility and strength.
  • Nṛṣadaneṣu: Means 'in the gatherings of men', indicating social or communal contexts.
  • Kāravaḥ: Refers to 'those who act' or 'those who perform', emphasizing action and initiative.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, unity, and valor. It highlights the importance of collective action and the acknowledgment of worthy offerings in a community. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, or in community service, where the strength of unity is harnessed to uplift society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collaboration and the significance of recognizing and valuing contributions within a group, ultimately leading to greater achievements and harmony.
