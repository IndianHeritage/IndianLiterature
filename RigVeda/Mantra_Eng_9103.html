हिनोतā नो अध्वरं देवयज्ञा हिनोत ब्रह्म सनये धनानाम् |ऋतस्य योगे विष्यध्वं ऊधः श्रुष्टिवारीर भूतनास्मभ्यम् आपः ||
hinotā no adhvaraṁ devayajyā hinota brahma sanaye dhanānām | ṛtasya yoge vi ṣyadhvam ūdhaḥ śruṣṭīvarīr bhūtanāsmabhyam āpaḥ ||
  • Hinotā no adhvaraṁ devayajyā hinota brahma sanaye dhanānām
    • Hinotā: This term can be understood as "to bring forth" or "to invoke." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies.
    • No: This means "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
    • Adhvaraṁ: Refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," emphasizing the importance of sacred actions in connecting with the divine.
    • Devayajyā: This translates to "through divine worship," highlighting the role of devotion and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • Hinota: Again, this means "to bring forth," reinforcing the idea of invoking divine blessings.
    • Brahma: Refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, representing the source of all creation.
    • Sanaye: This means "to grant" or "to bestow," indicating the act of receiving divine grace.
    • Dhanānām: This translates to "of wealth," suggesting a focus on prosperity and abundance.
  • Ṛtasya yoge vi ṣyadhvam ūdhaḥ
    • Ṛtasya: Refers to "truth" or "cosmic order," emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with universal laws.
    • Yoge: This means "in the union" or "in the practice," indicating the significance of spiritual practices in achieving higher states of consciousness.
    • Vi ṣyadhvam: This can be interpreted as "to be established" or "to be grounded," suggesting the need for stability in one's spiritual journey.
    • Ūdhaḥ: This means "to rise" or "to elevate," indicating the aspiration for spiritual growth and enlightenment.
  • Śruṣṭīvarīr bhūtanāsmabhyam āpaḥ
    • Śruṣṭīvarīr: This term refers to "the creator" or "the one who brings forth," highlighting the creative aspect of the divine.
    • Bhūtanāsmabhyam: This translates to "to us beings," indicating a connection to all living entities.
    • Āpaḥ: This means "water," symbolizing nourishment, purity, and the essential nature of life.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies through rituals and worship, seeking prosperity and abundance while living in accordance with cosmic truths. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in practices that promote spiritual growth, such as meditation, community service, and ethical living. By aligning ourselves with the principles of truth and harmony, we can cultivate a life filled with blessings and a deeper connection to the universe. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that support our journey.
