स जायतामः परम व्योमनि व्रतान्य अग्निर व्रतपाः अरक्षत |व्य आन्तरिक्षम् अमिमीत सुक्रतुर वैश्वानरः महिना नाकम् अस्पृशत ||
sa jāyamānaḥ parame vyomani vratāny agnir vratapā arakṣata | vy a1ntarikṣam amimīta sukratur vaiśvānaro mahinā nākam aspṛśat ||
  • sa jāyamānaḥ: This phrase translates to "he who is being born" or "the one who is coming into existence." It signifies the beginning of life or creation.
  • parame vyomani: This means "in the supreme sky" or "in the highest realm." It refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic space where everything exists.
  • vratāny: This word means "vows" or "sacred commitments." It indicates the importance of discipline and dedication in life.
  • agnir: This translates to "fire," which is often seen as a purifying and transformative element in many cultures, including Hinduism.
  • vratapā: This means "the one who protects the vows." It emphasizes the role of fire as a guardian of sacred commitments.
  • arakṣata: This translates to "he protected" or "he safeguarded." It indicates the protective nature of the divine or cosmic forces.
  • vy a1ntarikṣam: This refers to "the intermediate space" or "the atmosphere." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • amimīta: This means "he expanded" or "he manifested." It suggests the idea of growth and evolution in the universe.
  • sukratur: This translates to "the one with good intentions" or "the benevolent one." It highlights the importance of positive actions and thoughts.
  • vaiśvānaro: This refers to "the universal being" or "the cosmic entity." It represents the interconnectedness of all life forms.
  • mahinā: This means "with greatness" or "with power." It signifies the strength and influence of the divine forces.
  • nākam: This translates to "the sky" or "the heavens." It represents the ultimate goal or aspiration in life.
  • aspṛśat: This means "he did not touch" or "he did not affect." It indicates the transcendence of the divine over the material world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, protection, and the pursuit of higher ideals. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining sacred commitments and the role of divine forces in safeguarding these vows. In real life, this can be applied to various aspects such as personal integrity, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of community and relationships. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and striving for positive intentions, individuals can contribute to a harmonious existence. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the significance of our actions in the broader cosmic context.
