क्षेमस्य च प्रयुजश च त्वं ईशिषे शचीपत इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः |माध्यंदिनस्य सवनस्य वृत्रहन्न अनेद्य पिबा सोमस्य वज्रिवः ||
kṣemasya ca prayujaś ca tvam īśiṣe śacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya vṛtrahann anedya pibā somasya vajrivaḥ ||
  • kṣemasya ca prayujaś ca: This phrase can be translated to "You who are the bestower of well-being and prosperity." In real life, this can be related to the importance of seeking peace and stability in our lives, whether through personal relationships, career choices, or community involvement.
  • tvam īśiṣe śacīpata: This means "O Indra, the lord of strength and power." Indra is often seen as a symbol of strength and leadership. In our daily lives, we can draw inspiration from this to take charge of our own lives, make strong decisions, and lead by example in our communities.
  • indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "Indra, with all your powers." It emphasizes the idea of harnessing all available resources and strengths to achieve our goals. In practical terms, this could mean collaborating with others, utilizing technology, or drawing on personal skills to overcome challenges.
  • mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya: This phrase refers to "the midday offering." It symbolizes the importance of timing and the right moment for action. In real life, this can be seen in the significance of seizing opportunities when they arise, whether in business, education, or personal growth.
  • vṛtrahann anedya: This means "the slayer of Vritra, the unassailable." Vritra represents obstacles and challenges. This part of the mantra encourages us to confront and overcome our fears and difficulties, much like facing challenges in our careers or personal lives head-on.
  • pibā somasya vajrivaḥ: This translates to "Drink the soma, O Indra, with your thunderbolt." Soma is often associated with divine inspiration and vitality. This can be interpreted as a call to seek inspiration and rejuvenation in our lives, whether through nature, art, or spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, well-being, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It serves as a reminder that we can draw on our inner resources and the support of others to achieve our goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, personal development, and community engagement. By embodying the qualities of Indra, we can strive for prosperity, face challenges with courage, and seek inspiration in our daily lives.
