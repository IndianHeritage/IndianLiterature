इमा गिरः सवितारं सुजिह्वम् पूर्णगभस्तिम ईळते सुपाणिम् |चित्रं वयो बृहद अस्मे दधातु यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
imā giraḥ savitāraṁ sujihvam pūrṇagabhastim īḻate supāṇim | citraṁ vayo bṛhad asme dadhātu yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • imā giraḥ - This phrase translates to "these words" or "these hymns." It signifies the importance of speech and communication in our lives. In real life, effective communication is essential in various fields such as business, education, and personal relationships.
  • savitāraṁ - This refers to "the sun" or "the solar deity." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In practical terms, it reminds us of the importance of nature and the environment, encouraging us to respect and protect our planet.
  • sujihvam - This means "well-formed" or "auspicious." It emphasizes the need for positive intentions and actions in our endeavors. For example, in a workplace, fostering a positive environment can lead to better teamwork and productivity.
  • pūrṇagabhastim - This translates to "full of light" or "radiant." It symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment. In real life, pursuing education and self-improvement can lead to personal growth and a brighter future.
  • īḻate - This means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It suggests the idea of spreading knowledge and wisdom. For instance, mentoring others can help illuminate their paths and contribute to their success.
  • supāṇim - This translates to "having good hands" or "skillful." It highlights the importance of skill and craftsmanship in our work. In practical terms, honing one's skills through practice and education can lead to mastery in any field.
  • citraṁ vayo - This means "varied ages" or "different stages of life." It acknowledges the diversity of experiences and perspectives. In real life, embracing diversity in teams can lead to innovative solutions and creativity.
  • bṛhad asme - This translates to "may we be great" or "may we achieve greatness." It encourages ambition and striving for excellence. For example, setting high goals in personal or professional life can lead to significant achievements.
  • dadhātu - This means "may they grant" or "may they bestow." It reflects the idea of seeking blessings and support from higher powers or the universe. In real life, many people find strength in community support and spiritual practices.
  • yūyam pāta - This translates to "may you protect" or "may you safeguard." It emphasizes the importance of protection and care in relationships and communities. For instance, looking out for one another in a neighborhood fosters a sense of security and belonging.
  • svastibhiḥ - This means "with auspiciousness" or "with well-being." It signifies the desire for health and prosperity. In practical terms, promoting wellness in our lives through healthy habits can lead to a better quality of life.
  • sadā naḥ - This translates to "always for us" or "forever for us." It reflects the idea of continuous support and blessings. In real life, maintaining strong relationships with family and friends can provide lasting support throughout life’s challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of communication, positivity, knowledge, skill, diversity, ambition, protection, and well-being in our lives. It serves as a reminder to strive for greatness while supporting and uplifting one another. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community building, and professional environments, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous existence.
