आयं यो वज्रः पुरुधा विवृत्तोऽवः सूर्यस्य बृहतः पुरीषात् |श्रव इद एनापरो अन्यद अस्ति तदव्यथी जरीमाणस्तरन्ति ||
ayaṁ yo vajraḥ purudhā vivṛtto 'vaḥ sūryasya bṛhataḥ purīṣāt | śrava id enā paro anyad asti tad avyathī jarimāṇas taranti ||
  • ayaṁ yo vajraḥ - This phrase translates to "this is the thunderbolt." In a metaphorical sense, the thunderbolt represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles. For example, in real life, a person who faces challenges with resilience can be likened to a thunderbolt, striking through difficulties with determination.
  • purudhā vivṛtto 'vaḥ - This means "spread out in many ways." It signifies the diverse manifestations of strength and power in life. For instance, a community can come together in various forms—through teamwork, collaboration, and support—demonstrating the idea of collective strength.
  • sūryasya bṛhataḥ purīṣāt - This translates to "from the great expanse of the sun." The sun symbolizes life, energy, and vitality. In practical terms, this can be seen in how sunlight nurtures plants, which in turn sustain life on Earth. It emphasizes the importance of harnessing natural resources for growth and sustenance.
  • śrava id enā - This means "the sound of this." It refers to the resonance of strength and power in the universe. In everyday life, this can be related to the impact of a powerful speech or a motivating leader whose words inspire action and change.
  • paro anyad asti - This translates to "there is nothing greater." It suggests the ultimate power and significance of the forces at play in the universe. In real life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the importance of certain values or principles that guide our actions, such as love, compassion, and integrity.
  • tad avyathī - This means "that does not waver." It signifies stability and unwavering strength in the face of adversity. For example, a person who remains steadfast in their beliefs despite challenges exemplifies this quality.
  • jarimāṇas taranti - This translates to "they who overcome old age." It symbolizes the triumph over the limitations of time and the pursuit of eternal values. In practical terms, this can be seen in individuals who maintain their passion and enthusiasm for life, regardless of their age.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, resilience, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that true power lies not only in individual strength but also in collective efforts and the natural forces that sustain us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to harness their inner strength, work together, and remain steadfast in their values, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth. It serves as a reminder that, like the sun, we can radiate energy and positivity, overcoming challenges and nurturing life around us.
