आ न इन्दो शतग्विनं गवाम् पोषं स्वश्व्यम् |वहा भगत्तिम् ऊतये ||
ā na indo śatagvinaṁ gavām poṣaṁ svaśvyam | vahā bhagattim ūtaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From, towards
    • na: Not
    • indo: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • śatagvinaṁ: Hundred-fold, abundant
    • gavām: Of cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • poṣaṁ: Nourishment, sustenance
    • svaśvyam: Our own, personal
    • vahā: Carry, bring
    • bhagattim: Prosperity, fortune
    • ūtaye: For the purpose of, for the attainment of
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, asking for abundant nourishment and prosperity.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth and sustenance, which were vital in ancient agrarian societies.
    • The phrase "our own" emphasizes a personal connection to the blessings being sought.
    • The overall tone is one of gratitude and a plea for divine support in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra during the planting season, seeking a bountiful harvest and the prosperity that comes with it.
    • In business, entrepreneurs might use this mantra to invoke success and abundance in their ventures, hoping to attract good fortune and growth.
    • Families may chant this mantra during rituals for health and prosperity, reinforcing the importance of nourishment and well-being in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for abundance and prosperity, which resonates across various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nourishment, wealth, and personal well-being.
    • In modern contexts, it can be applied in personal development, business practices, and community welfare, emphasizing the importance of seeking divine blessings for success and sustenance.
