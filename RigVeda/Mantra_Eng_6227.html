आ वां विप्र इहावसे 'हवत स्तोमेभिर अश्विनाः | अरिप्रā वृत्रहन्तमा ता नो भूतम मयोभुवा ||
ā vāṁ vipra ihāvase 'hvat stomebhir aśvinā | ariprā vṛtrahantamā tā no bhūtam mayobhuvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: "We invoke you" or "We call upon you."
    • vipra: "O wise one" or "O learned one," often referring to a sage or a knowledgeable person.
    • ihāvase: "Here in this world" or "In this place."
    • hvat: "What" or "Which."
    • stomebhir: "With hymns" or "With praises."
    • aśvinā: "O Ashvins," referring to the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • ariprā: "O destroyer of enemies" or "O vanquisher of foes."
    • vṛtrahantamā: "The one who slays Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or challenges.
    • tā: "That" or "Those."
    • no: "For us" or "To us."
    • bhūtam: "Existence" or "Being."
    • mayobhuvā: "May it be fulfilled" or "May it come to us."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to the Ashvins, who are revered for their healing and protective qualities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in overcoming life's challenges.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins signifies a request for assistance in achieving well-being and success.
    • The reference to "the slayer of Vritra" symbolizes the removal of obstacles and difficulties that hinder progress.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone facing health issues might recite this mantra to seek healing and support from the divine.
    • In a professional setting, individuals may invoke this mantra during challenging projects, hoping for wisdom and guidance to overcome obstacles.
    • Communities facing natural disasters or crises might use this mantra in prayers for protection and recovery.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine wisdom and support.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking help and guidance in times of need.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals or communities face challenges, emphasizing the need for collective strength and divine intervention.
