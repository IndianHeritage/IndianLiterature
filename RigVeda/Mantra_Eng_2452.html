आ देवानाम् अभवः केतुर्ग्नेमन्द्रो विश्वानि काव्यानि विद्वान् |प्रति मर्ताम् आवासयो दमूनानु देवान् रथिरो यासि साधन ||
ā devānām abhavaḥ ketur agne mandro viśvāni kāvyāni vidvān | prati martām̐ avāsayo damūnā anu devān rathiro yāsi sādhan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • devānām: of the gods
    • abhavaḥ: I have become
    • ketur: a banner or a guide
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • mandro: gentle or soothing
    • viśvāni: all or universal
    • kāvyāni: poetic or creative
    • vidvān: the wise or knowledgeable
    • prati: towards
    • martām̐: the mortal beings
    • avāsayo: may I dwell or reside
    • damūnā: with restraint or control
    • anu: following
    • devān: the gods
    • rathiro: in the chariot
    • yāsi: you go
    • sādhan: with accomplishment or success
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire to become a guiding force among the gods, particularly invoking Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes knowledge and purification.
    • The speaker seeks to embody wisdom and creativity, aiming to influence the mortal realm positively.
    • The mention of restraint indicates the importance of self-control and discipline in achieving one's goals.
    • The reference to the chariot suggests a journey or path that one must take, guided by divine forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a leadership role, one can embody the qualities of Agni by guiding a team with wisdom and creativity, ensuring that decisions are made with clarity and purpose.
    • In personal development, practicing self-restraint and discipline can lead to success in various endeavors, such as education or career advancement.
    • In community service, individuals can act as a guiding light, helping others navigate challenges with knowledge and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of becoming a guiding force in both personal and communal aspects of life.
    • It highlights the importance of wisdom, creativity, and self-restraint in achieving success and positively influencing others.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in leadership, personal growth, and community engagement, making it a timeless source of inspiration.
