सम अश्विनोर अवसां नूतनेन मयोभुवा सुप्रणीति गमेमा |आ नो रयिं वहतम ओत वीरान आ विश्वानि अमृतā सौभगानि ||
sam aśvinor avasā nūtanena mayobhuvā supraṇītī gamema | ā no rayiṁ vahatam ota vīrān ā viśvāny amṛtā saubhagāni ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we, with the new strength of the Aśvins, attain the well-directed path. May we carry the wealth of the brave and may all beings be blessed with immortality and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • aśvinor: of the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine
    • avasā: strength, support
    • nūtanena: new, fresh
    • mayobhuvā: may we be endowed with strength
    • supraṇītī: well-directed, auspicious path
    • gamema: may we go, may we attain
    • ā: towards
    • no: our
    • rayiṁ: wealth, prosperity
    • vahatam: carrying, bringing
    • ota: of the brave, the valiant
    • vīrān: heroes, warriors
    • ā: towards
    • viśvāny: all beings, all creatures
    • amṛtā: immortal, eternal
    • saubhagāni: auspiciousness, prosperity
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration. For instance, in a team project at work, when everyone works together harmoniously, they can achieve greater success.
    • The reference to the Aśvins symbolizes the pursuit of health and well-being. In modern life, this can relate to seeking medical help or adopting a healthy lifestyle to ensure longevity and vitality.
    • The idea of carrying wealth and blessings can be seen in community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, thereby creating a prosperous environment for all.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength, health, and prosperity. It encourages individuals to seek a well-directed path in life, emphasizing the importance of community and support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork, health initiatives, and community welfare projects. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
