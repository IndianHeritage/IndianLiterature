एष शुष्म्य असिष्यदद अन्तरिक्षे वृषा हरिः | पुनान इन्दुर इन्द्रम् आ ||
eṣa śuṣmy asiṣyadad antarikṣe vṛṣā hariḥ | punāna indur indram ā ||
  • eṣa: This word translates to "this" or "he." It indicates a specific presence or entity that is being referred to.
  • śuṣmy: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who is capable." It denotes strength and capability.
  • asiṣyadad: This word can be broken down to mean "to bestow" or "to grant." It implies the act of giving or providing something valuable.
  • antarikṣe: This refers to "the space" or "the atmosphere." It signifies the realm that exists between the earth and the heavens.
  • vṛṣā: This word means "the one who is strong" or "the one who is mighty." It is often associated with power and strength.
  • hariḥ: This term is often associated with the divine, particularly in the context of Vishnu or the concept of the divine protector.
  • punāna: This means "purifying" or "cleansing." It suggests a process of renewal or making something pure.
  • indur: This translates to "the moon." In many cultures, the moon symbolizes calmness, reflection, and the passage of time.
  • indram: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing power, authority, and leadership.
  • ā: This is a preposition that can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or movement.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine presence, acknowledging the powerful forces in the universe and seeking purification and strength. It emphasizes the connection between the earthly realm and the divine, invoking the strength of Indra and the calming influence of the moon.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Strength: Just as the mantra speaks of strength and capability, individuals can draw inspiration from it to cultivate their own inner strength in challenging situations.
  • Purification and Renewal: The concept of cleansing can be applied in personal growth, where one seeks to let go of negative influences and embrace a more positive, renewed self.
  • Leadership and Authority: The reference to Indra can inspire individuals in positions of leadership to act with wisdom and strength, guiding others with integrity.
  • Connection to Nature: The mention of the moon encourages reflection and mindfulness, reminding us to connect with the natural rhythms of life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength within us, the importance of purification, and the connection to the divine. It encourages individuals to seek inner strength, embrace renewal, and lead with wisdom, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to leadership roles.
