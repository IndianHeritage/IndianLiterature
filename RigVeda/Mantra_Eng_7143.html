स पूर्व्यो महानां वेनः क्रतुभिर आनजे |यस्य द्वारा मनुष पिताः देवेषु धिया आनजे ||
sa pūrvyo mahānāṁ venaḥ kratubhir ānaje | yasya dvārā manuṣ pitā deveṣu dhiya ānaje ||
  • sa pūrvyo mahānāṁ venaḥ kratubhir ānaje
    • sa: This word translates to "that" or "he," indicating a reference to a divine or significant entity.
    • pūrvyo: Meaning "of the ancients" or "from the past," it suggests a connection to ancient wisdom or traditions.
    • mahānāṁ: This means "of the great ones," referring to powerful beings or deities.
    • venaḥ: Translates to "the flow" or "the stream," often symbolizing the flow of life or energy.
    • kratubhir: This word means "by the sacrifices" or "through the rituals," indicating the importance of actions and offerings in spiritual practices.
    • ānaje: This can be interpreted as "to be born" or "to arise," suggesting the emergence of something significant through these actions.
  • yasya dvārā manuṣ pitā deveṣu dhiya ānaje
    • yasya: Meaning "whose," it indicates possession or belonging.
    • dvārā: Translates to "through the doors," symbolizing access or entry points to higher realms or knowledge.
    • manuṣ: This means "human" or "mankind," emphasizing the connection between the divine and humanity.
    • pitā: Meaning "father," it signifies a paternal figure, often associated with guidance and protection.
    • deveṣu: This translates to "among the gods," indicating a divine context or presence.
    • dhiya: Meaning "intellect" or "thought," it highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding.
    • ānaje: Again, this suggests the idea of emergence or birth, reinforcing the theme of creation or realization.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the connection between ancient wisdom, divine energies, and human intellect. It suggests that through rituals and sacrifices, one can access higher knowledge and divine guidance. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Engaging in spiritual practices that honor ancient traditions, fostering a sense of connection to the past.
  • Recognizing the importance of community and familial bonds, as represented by the paternal figure, in guiding one's spiritual journey.
  • Utilizing intellect and wisdom in decision-making, ensuring that actions are aligned with higher principles and values.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between the divine, the ancient, and the human experience, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and connection through their actions and thoughts.
