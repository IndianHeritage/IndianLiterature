ऋषिर न स्तुभ्वा विक्षु प्रशस्तो वाजी न प्रीतो वयो दधाति ||
ṛṣir na stubhvā vikṣu praśasto vājī na prīto vayo dadhāti ||
  • ṛṣir: This term refers to a sage or seer, someone who has attained a deep understanding of the universe through meditation and insight. In real life, this can be likened to a wise mentor or teacher who guides others through their knowledge and experience.
  • na stubhvā: This phrase can be interpreted as "not in praise" or "not in flattery." It suggests that true wisdom and insight do not come from superficial praise but from genuine understanding. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where people seek honest feedback rather than empty compliments.
  • vikṣu: This word means "in the midst of" or "among." It indicates a sense of community or collective experience. For example, in a workplace, collaboration among team members can lead to greater innovation and success.
  • praśasto: This term means "well-spoken" or "praised." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the impact of words. In real life, effective communication can strengthen relationships and foster understanding among individuals.
  • vājī: This word translates to "one who possesses strength" or "powerful." It signifies the importance of inner strength and resilience. In real life, individuals who face challenges with courage and determination often inspire others.
  • na prīto: This phrase means "not pleased" or "not satisfied." It suggests that true fulfillment comes from deeper sources rather than superficial pleasures. In life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of meaningful goals rather than temporary gratifications.
  • vayo: This term refers to "age" or "time." It highlights the significance of experience and the wisdom that comes with it. In real life, older generations often provide valuable insights based on their life experiences.
  • dadhāti: This word means "to bestow" or "to give." It signifies the act of sharing knowledge, wisdom, or resources. In everyday life, acts of generosity and sharing can create a positive impact on communities and relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra "ṛṣir na stubhvā vikṣu praśasto vājī na prīto vayo dadhāti" emphasizes the importance of genuine wisdom, effective communication, inner strength, and the sharing of knowledge within a community. Its significance lies in the reminder that true fulfillment and understanding come from deeper connections and experiences rather than superficial praise or temporary pleasures. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings, workplaces, and personal relationships, where the focus should be on meaningful interactions and the sharing of wisdom for collective growth and understanding.
