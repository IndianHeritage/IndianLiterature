यदā वाजम् असनद् विश्वरूपम् ā द्यां अरुक्षद् उत्तराणि सद्म | बृहस्पतिं वृषणं वर्धयन्तो नानā सन्तो बिभ्रतो ज्योतिर आसā ||
yadā vājam asanad viśvarūpam ā dyām arukṣad uttarāṇi sadma | bṛhaspatiṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vardhayanto nānā santo bibhrato jyotir āsā ||
  • yadā vājam asanad viśvarūpam: This phrase can be translated as "When the speech (or voice) is established in its universal form." Here, "vājam" refers to speech or voice, and "viśvarūpam" indicates a universal or all-encompassing nature. In real life, this can be related to the power of communication and how effective speech can transcend boundaries, bringing people together.
  • ā dyām arukṣad uttarāṇi sadma: This translates to "When the sky (or heavens) is unshaken, and the higher abodes are established." The term "dyām" refers to the sky or heavens, while "uttarāṇi sadma" refers to higher or elevated places. This can be likened to stability in life, where a person remains grounded and secure despite external chaos, similar to how a strong tree stands firm against the wind.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vardhayanto: This means "Nurturing the great lord (Bṛhaspati) who is powerful." Bṛhaspati is often associated with wisdom and knowledge. In a real-life context, this can be seen as the importance of nurturing wisdom and intellect in our lives, akin to how students seek knowledge from teachers to grow and develop.
  • nānā santo bibhrato jyotir āsā: This translates to "Various beings (or saints) carry the light of hope." The word "nānā" indicates diversity, while "jyotir" refers to light. This can be interpreted as the idea that different individuals contribute to a collective hope or enlightenment, similar to how diverse cultures and perspectives enrich our understanding of the world.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the power of communication, stability, wisdom, and the collective contribution of diverse individuals towards enlightenment. In real life, it signifies the importance of effective communication in fostering unity, the need for stability in our lives amidst challenges, the nurturing of knowledge and wisdom, and the recognition of diverse contributions to a common goal. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings, community building, and personal development, highlighting the interconnectedness of all aspects of life.
