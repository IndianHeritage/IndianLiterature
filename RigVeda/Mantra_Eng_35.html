मन्त्र: उता ब्रुवन्तु नो निदो निर अन्यतश्चिदारत | दधानाऽ इन्द्र इद् दुवाह् ||
uta bruvantu no nido nir anyataś cid ārata | dadhānā indra id duvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • bruvantu: may they speak, may they declare
    • no: our
    • nido: not, no
    • nir: without
    • anyataś: otherwise, in any other way
    • cid: indeed, certainly
    • ārata: with joy, with enthusiasm
    • dadhānā: holding, possessing
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • id: this, here
    • duvaḥ: of the two, of the duality
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call for unity and clarity in communication.
    • It emphasizes the importance of speaking truthfully and joyfully, without any negativity or doubt.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a request for divine guidance and strength in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team meeting, when members are encouraged to share their thoughts openly, it fosters a collaborative environment.
    • In personal relationships, clear and honest communication can prevent misunderstandings and strengthen bonds.
    • In public speaking, addressing the audience with confidence and clarity can inspire and motivate them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of clear and joyful communication in all aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as in workplaces, personal relationships, and public engagements.
    • By invoking the spirit of Indra, we seek strength and clarity in our words, promoting harmony and understanding.
