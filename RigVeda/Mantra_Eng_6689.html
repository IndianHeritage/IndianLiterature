प्र कृतान्य ऋजिषिणः कण्वा इन्द्रस्य गाथया | मद सोमस्य वोचत ||
pra kṛtāny ṛjīṣiṇaḥ kaṇvā indrasya gāthayā | made somasya vocata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: before, in front of
    • kṛtāny: actions, deeds
    • ṛjīṣiṇaḥ: those who are righteous, the virtuous
    • kaṇvā: of the Kaṇva clan, a group of sages
    • indrasya: of Indra, the king of gods
    • gāthayā: through the hymn, song, or praise
    • made: in joy, ecstasy
    • somasya: of Soma, the divine nectar
    • vocata: they spoke, they declared
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the righteous actions performed by the virtuous sages of the Kaṇva clan.
    • It highlights the importance of praising Indra, the king of gods, through hymns.
    • The mention of "Soma" refers to a sacred drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and joy.
    • The phrase "they spoke" indicates a collective effort in expressing devotion and reverence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, we often see groups coming together to celebrate achievements or milestones, much like the Kaṇva sages who united in praise.
    • Rituals and ceremonies in various cultures serve as a way to honor deities, akin to the hymns sung to Indra.
    • Communities often engage in collective actions for social good, reflecting the righteous deeds mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of collective righteousness and the power of praise in connecting with the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and shared values in achieving spiritual and social harmony.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to come together for a common purpose, fostering unity and strength in diversity.
