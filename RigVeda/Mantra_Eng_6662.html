मन्त्र: पथ एकः पीपाय तस्करो यथाम् एष वेद निधीनाम् ||
patha ekaḥ pīpāya taskaro yathām̐ eṣa veda nidhīnām ||
  • Patha: This word translates to "path" or "way." It signifies the journey or the method one takes in life.
  • Ekaḥ: Meaning "one" or "single," it emphasizes the importance of unity and singular focus in one's endeavors.
  • Pīpāya: This term can be interpreted as "to nourish" or "to sustain." It suggests the act of providing sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • Taskaro: This word means "thief" or "robber." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent distractions or negative influences that divert one from their path.
  • Yathām̐: Translated as "as" or "like," it draws a comparison, indicating that the following statement will illustrate a principle or truth.
  • Eṣa: Meaning "this," it refers to the subject at hand, which is the knowledge or wisdom being imparted.
  • Veda: This term means "knowledge" or "wisdom," particularly in the context of sacred knowledge in Hinduism.
  • Nidhīnām: This translates to "of treasures" or "of riches," indicating the valuable insights or truths that can be found within the Vedas.

  • Meaning: The mantra suggests that there is a singular path that nourishes the seeker, and it warns against the distractions (the "thief") that can lead one astray from this path of wisdom and knowledge.
  • Real-Life Example: In a modern context, consider a student pursuing higher education. The "path" represents their academic journey, while "ekaḥ" signifies their focus on a specific field of study. "Pīpāya" reflects the support they receive from mentors and resources that nourish their learning. However, "taskaro" symbolizes the distractions of social media or peer pressure that can divert their attention from their studies.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to stay focused on one's path and to seek nourishment from knowledge while being aware of distractions that can hinder progress. In real life, this can be applied to various situations, such as career development, personal growth, or spiritual journeys. By recognizing the importance of a singular focus and the value of wisdom, individuals can navigate their lives more effectively and achieve their goals.
