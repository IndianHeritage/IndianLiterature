इन्द्र सोमं सोमपते पिबेमं मध्यंदिनं सवनं चारु यत ते | प्रप्रुथ्या शिप्रे मगवन्न ऋजिषिन विमुच्य हरि इह माधयस्व ||
indra somaṁ somapate pibemam mādhyaṁdinaṁ savanaṁ cāru yat te | prapruthyā śipre maghavann ṛjīṣin vimucyā harī iha mādayasva ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Somaṁ: Represents the sacred drink, Soma, which is associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Somapate: Means "lord of Soma," indicating a connection to the divine and the spiritual essence of life.
  • Pibemam: Translates to "may I drink," expressing a desire for divine nourishment and inspiration.
  • Mādhyaṁdinaṁ: Refers to the midday, a time of peak energy and vitality.
  • Savanaṁ: Indicates a ritual offering or sacrifice, often associated with the worship of deities.
  • Cāru: Means "beautiful" or "pleasant," suggesting the aesthetic and spiritual beauty of the offering.
  • Yat te: Translates to "that which is yours," indicating a connection to the divine and the offerings made to the gods.
  • Prapruthyā: Refers to the earth or the material world, emphasizing the connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • Śipre: Means "with strength" or "with vigor," highlighting the power of the divine in the material world.
  • Maghavan: Another name for Indra, emphasizing his role as a giver of wealth and prosperity.
  • Ṛjīṣin: Refers to the one who is righteous or virtuous, indicating the importance of moral integrity.
  • Vimucyā: Means "may I be freed," expressing a desire for liberation from worldly attachments.
  • Harī: Refers to the divine or the auspicious, symbolizing positivity and good fortune.
  • Iha: Means "here," indicating the present moment and the importance of being grounded in reality.
  • Mādayasva: Translates to "may you be pleased," expressing a wish for divine favor and joy.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine connection and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in establishing a relationship with the divine, particularly during significant times like midday when energy is at its peak. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, prayer, and acts of kindness, which help individuals connect with their spiritual selves and the world around them. The mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between the material and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to seek liberation from worldly attachments while embracing the beauty and joy of life. By invoking the blessings of Indra and Soma, one can aspire to lead a life filled with strength, righteousness, and divine inspiration.
