यथाभवद अनुदेयी ततो अग्रं अजायत |पुरस्ताद बुद्धन आततः पश्चान निरयनं कृतम् ||
yathābhavad anudeyī tato agram ajāyata | purastād budhna ātataḥ paścān nirayaṇaṁ kṛtam ||
  • yathābhavad anudeyī: This phrase can be translated as "as it has been, so it will be." It suggests a continuity or a reflection of past actions in the present and future. In real life, this can be seen in the concept of karma, where one's actions directly influence their circumstances. For example, a student who diligently studies will likely perform well in exams, reflecting their past efforts.
  • tato agram ajāyata: This translates to "from that, the foremost was born." It implies that from the continuity of actions, something significant or primary emerges. In a practical sense, this can be related to the idea of leadership or innovation arising from consistent effort and dedication. For instance, a successful entrepreneur often builds their business on the foundation of previous experiences and lessons learned.
  • purastād budhna ātataḥ: This means "from the front, the wise one is present." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and foresight in guiding actions. In everyday life, this can be observed in leaders or mentors who provide guidance based on their knowledge and experience. For example, a manager who anticipates market trends can steer their team towards success.
  • paścān nirayaṇaṁ kṛtam: This translates to "afterwards, the path to liberation is made." It signifies that through wise actions and decisions, one can achieve a state of freedom or enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who, after overcoming challenges and learning from their experiences, find a sense of peace and fulfillment. For instance, someone who has faced adversity and emerged stronger often shares their journey to inspire others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the cyclical nature of actions and consequences, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and foresight in shaping one's path. It teaches that our past actions influence our present and future, and through wise decisions, we can pave the way for liberation and success. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal growth, leadership, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us that our choices today will define our tomorrow.
