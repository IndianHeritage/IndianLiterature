यद इन्द्राहन् प्रथमजाम अहीनाम् आन मायिनाम् अमिनाः प्रोत मायाः |आत सूर्यं जनयन द्याम् उषासं तादीत्ना शत्रुं न किल विवित्से ||
yad indrāhan prathamajām ahīnām ān māyinām amināḥ prota māyāḥ | āt sūryaṁ janayan dyām uṣāsaṁ tādītnā śatruṁ na kilā vivitse ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indrāhan: Indra, the king of gods
    • prathamajām: the first-born
    • ahīnām: of the serpents
    • ān: to bring forth
    • māyinām: of the illusionists
    • amināḥ: not by the unworthy
    • prota: woven
    • māyāḥ: of illusion
    • āt: from
    • sūryaṁ: the sun
    • janayan: giving birth
    • dyām: to the sky
    • uṣāsaṁ: the dawn
    • tādītnā: by that light
    • śatruṁ: the enemy
    • na: not
    • kilā: indeed
    • vivitse: to desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power of Indra, who is invoked to bring forth the first-born, symbolizing the emergence of creation and life.
    • The mention of "serpents" can be interpreted as challenges or obstacles in life, which are often deceptive and illusory.
    • The phrase "not by the unworthy" emphasizes the importance of purity and righteousness in one's actions and intentions.
    • The sun and dawn represent enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness, which can be seen as ignorance or negativity.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with the idea that by invoking this divine light, one can overcome enemies or adversities without the desire to engage in conflict.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when faced with challenges (the "serpents"), one can seek guidance and clarity (the "sun") to navigate through difficulties without resorting to unethical practices.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can inspire individuals to rise above conflicts and misunderstandings by focusing on understanding and enlightenment rather than hostility.
    • In a broader societal context, it encourages communities to work towards harmony and peace, using wisdom and knowledge to address issues rather than engaging in divisive actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of divine intervention and the importance of righteousness in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches that by seeking knowledge and enlightenment, one can navigate through illusions and adversities without succumbing to negativity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and wisdom, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
