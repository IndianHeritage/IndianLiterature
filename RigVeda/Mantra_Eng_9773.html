विष्वस्मा अग्निम भुवनाय देवā वैश्वानरं केतुम अह्नाम अकृण्वन |आ यस ततानोषसो विभातीर अपो ऊर्णोति तमो आर्चिषा यन् ||
viśvasmā agnim bhuvanāya devā vaiśvānaraṁ ketum ahnām akṛṇvan | ā yas tatānoṣaso vibhātīr apo ūrṇoti tamo arciṣā yan ||
  • viśvasmā agnim: This phrase translates to "the fire from all directions." In a broader sense, it signifies the universal nature of fire, which is essential for life and sustenance.
  • bhuvanāya: This means "for the world" or "for the universe." It indicates that the fire is not just a physical element but a vital force that sustains the entire cosmos.
  • devā: This word refers to "gods" or divine beings. It suggests that fire is revered and considered sacred in various cultures, symbolizing purity and transformation.
  • vaiśvānaraṁ: This term denotes "the universal fire" or "the fire that belongs to all." It emphasizes the idea that fire is a common element that connects all living beings.
  • ketum: This translates to "the banner" or "the standard." It symbolizes guidance and direction, indicating that fire serves as a beacon for humanity.
  • ahnām akṛṇvan: This means "they create the day." It highlights the role of fire in bringing light and dispelling darkness, akin to how the sun rises each day.
  • ā yas tatānoṣaso: This phrase translates to "that which spreads in the dawn." It signifies the awakening of life and energy with the arrival of light, representing hope and new beginnings.
  • vibhātīr: This means "shining" or "illuminating." It emphasizes the illuminating quality of fire, both literally and metaphorically, as it brings clarity and understanding.
  • apo ūrṇoti: This translates to "the waters nourish." It signifies the interdependence of fire and water, two essential elements for life, highlighting the balance in nature.
  • tamo arciṣā yan: This means "that which dispels darkness with light." It symbolizes the triumph of knowledge and wisdom over ignorance and confusion.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the significance of fire as a universal element that sustains life, brings light, and symbolizes divine presence. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in various rituals where fire is used as a purifying agent, such as in Hindu ceremonies (yajnas) where offerings are made to the fire. The mantra also serves as a reminder of the balance between elements—fire and water—and the importance of light in dispelling darkness, both literally and metaphorically. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and clarity in their lives, fostering a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of all beings in the universe.
