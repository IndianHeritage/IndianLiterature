सम्राजो ये सुवृधो यज्ञं आययुर अपरीह्वृतā दधिरे दिवि क्षयम् |तां ā विवास नमसā सुवृक्तिभिर महो आदित्यां अदितिं स्वस्तये ||
samrājo ye suvṛdho yajñam āyayur aparihvṛtā dadhire divi kṣayam | tām̐ ā vivāsa namasā suvṛktibhir maho ādityām̐ aditiṁ svastaye ||
  • Mantra: "samrājo ye suvṛdho yajñam āyayur aparihvṛtā dadhire divi kṣayam | tām̐ ā vivāsa namasā suvṛktibhir maho ādityām̐ aditiṁ svastaye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samrājo: kings or rulers
    • ye: who
    • suvṛdho: well-versed or skilled
    • yajñam: sacrifice or offering
    • āyayur: have come or arrived
    • aparihṛtā: unimpeded or unhindered
    • dadhire: have established or upheld
    • divi: in the heavens
    • kṣayam: destruction or downfall
    • tām̐: that
    • ā: with
    • vivāsa: reverence or devotion
    • namasā: with salutation or bowing
    • suvṛktibhir: with good words or praises
    • maho: great
    • ādityām̐: to the sun or solar deity
    • aditiṁ: to Aditi, the mother of gods
    • svastaye: for well-being or prosperity
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the rulers who are skilled in performing sacrifices and how they uphold their duties without hindrance.
    • The mention of "yajñam" (sacrifice) signifies the importance of selfless acts and offerings in society, which can be related to community service or charitable actions in modern life.
    • The phrase "divi kṣayam" suggests a connection to the divine or higher realms, indicating that the actions of these rulers have cosmic significance.
    • The invocation of "ādityām̐ aditiṁ" highlights the reverence towards the sun and the mother of gods, symbolizing the source of life and abundance.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders who prioritize the welfare of their people and engage in actions that promote harmony and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the role of leadership and the importance of selfless service for the greater good.
    • It serves as a reminder for contemporary leaders to act with integrity, wisdom, and a sense of responsibility towards their communities.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of kindness, community service, and to uphold values that contribute to societal well-being.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a harmonious relationship between leaders and the divine, fostering an environment of peace and prosperity.
