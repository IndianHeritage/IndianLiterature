घृतप्रुषः सौम्या जीरददानवः सप्त स्वसारः सदन ऋतस्य |या ह वाम इन्द्रावरुणा घृतश्चुतस् ताभिर धत्तं यजमानाय शिक्षतम् ||
ghṛtapruṣaḥ saumyā jīradānavaḥ sapta svasāraḥ sadana ṛtasya | yā ha vām indrāvaruṇā ghṛtaścutas tābhir dhattaṁ yajamānāya śikṣatam ||
  • ghṛtapruṣaḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ghṛta" meaning ghee or clarified butter, and "puruṣa" meaning person or being. Together, it refers to a being or entity that is nourished or enriched by ghee, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • saumyā: This word translates to gentle or peaceful. It signifies a calm and serene nature, which is essential for harmony in life.
  • jīradānavaḥ: "Jīra" refers to a type of grain or food, and "dānava" means a being or entity. This can be interpreted as a being that is nourished by wholesome food, emphasizing the importance of a healthy diet.
  • sapta svasāraḥ: "Sapta" means seven, and "svasāraḥ" refers to siblings or close relations. This phrase can symbolize the seven vital energies or aspects of life that are interconnected and support one another.
  • sadana ṛtasya: "Sadana" means abode or dwelling, and "ṛta" refers to truth or cosmic order. This phrase suggests a dwelling that is aligned with truth and righteousness, indicating the importance of living in accordance with moral values.
  • yā ha vām indrāvaruṇā: This part can be interpreted as a call to the divine forces, Indra and Varuna, who represent strength and cosmic order, respectively. It signifies the invocation of divine support in our endeavors.
  • ghṛtaścutas: This term refers to the essence or qualities derived from ghee, symbolizing purity and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in our actions and thoughts.
  • tābhir dhattaṁ: "Tābhir" means by these, and "dhattaṁ" refers to the act of giving or bestowing. This suggests that the divine forces provide blessings and support to those who seek them sincerely.
  • yajamānāya: This term refers to the one who performs rituals or sacrifices, indicating the importance of dedication and commitment in spiritual practices.
  • śikṣatam: This word means to be taught or instructed. It emphasizes the value of learning and acquiring knowledge in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and the importance of living in harmony with the cosmic order. It highlights the interconnectedness of life and the need for purity, dedication, and learning. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to promote a balanced lifestyle, encouraging individuals to seek nourishment through healthy food, maintain peaceful relationships, and engage in spiritual practices that align with truth and righteousness. By invoking divine support and committing to personal growth, one can cultivate a fulfilling and harmonious life.
