पत्नीवन्तः सुताः इमा उशन्तो यान्ति वीतये | अपां जग्मिर निचुंपुणः ||
patnīvantaḥ sutā ima uśanto yanti vītaye | apāṁ jagmir nicumpuṇaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "patnīvantaḥ sutā ima uśanto yanti vītaye | apāṁ jagmir nicumpuṇaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The sons of the prosperous, who are calm and composed, move towards prosperity; they who are like the waters that flow smoothly."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • patnīvantaḥ: Those who are prosperous or have wealth.
    • sutā: Sons or offspring.
    • ima: These.
    • uśanto: Calm, composed, or peaceful.
    • yanti: Move towards or go.
    • vītaye: Prosperity or wealth.
    • apāṁ: Of waters.
    • jagmir: Flow or move.
    • nicumpuṇaḥ: Smoothly or gently.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the qualities of calmness and composure in achieving prosperity.
    • It suggests that those who are grounded and peaceful are more likely to attain success and wealth.
    • The comparison to water highlights the importance of adaptability and smoothness in one's approach to life.
    • In real life, individuals who remain calm in challenging situations often find better solutions and opportunities.
    • For example, a business leader who maintains composure during a crisis can inspire confidence in their team and navigate the company towards recovery.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of inner peace and composure in the pursuit of success.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and even in community leadership.
    • By embodying the qualities of calmness and adaptability, individuals can create a positive impact on their surroundings and achieve their goals more effectively.
