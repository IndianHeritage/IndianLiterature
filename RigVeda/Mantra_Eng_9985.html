आरे अग्हा को nv इ1त्था ददर्श यं युञ्जन्ति तं व आ स्थापयन्ति |नास्मै तृणं नोडकम आ भरन्त्य उत्तरो धुरो वहति प्रदेदिशत ||
āre aghā ko nv i1tthā dadarśa yaṁ yuñjanti tam v ā sthāpayanti | nāsmai tṛṇaṁ nodakam ā bharanty uttaro dhuro vahati pradediśat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āre: O, indeed
    • aghā: sin, wrongdoing
    • ko: who
    • nv: indeed
    • i1tthā: has established
    • dadarśa: has seen
    • yaṁ: whom
    • yuñjanti: they unite
    • tam: that
    • v: indeed
    • ā: to
    • sthāpayanti: they establish
    • nāsmai: for him
    • tṛṇaṁ: grass
    • nodakam: water
    • ā: to
    • bharanty: they carry
    • uttaro: the upper
    • dhuro: burden
    • vahati: carries
    • pradediśat: has shown
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of recognizing and addressing wrongdoing.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and support among individuals in overcoming challenges.
    • The imagery of carrying burdens and the mention of grass and water symbolize the basic necessities of life and the support we provide to one another.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when someone is facing difficulties, others come together to support them, reflecting the unity mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, teamwork is essential; when one member struggles, the team rallies to help, demonstrating the principle of carrying each other's burdens.
    • In personal relationships, friends and family often help each other through tough times, embodying the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective responsibility and support in the face of adversity.
    • It teaches us that by uniting and helping one another, we can overcome challenges and alleviate the burdens of life.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including community service, teamwork, and personal relationships, reminding us of the strength found in unity.
