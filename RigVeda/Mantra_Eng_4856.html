बृहद्भिर अग्ने अर्चिभिः शुक्रेण देव शोचिषा |भरद्वाजे समिधानो यविष्ठ्य रेवन् नः शुक्र दिदिहि द्युमत पावक दिदिहि ||
bṛhadbhir agne arcibhiḥ śukreṇa deva śociṣā | bharadvāje samidhāno yaviṣṭhya revan naḥ śukra dīdihi dyumat pāvaka dīdihi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhadbhir: With great or vast.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • arcibhiḥ: With hymns or praises.
    • śukreṇa: With purity or brightness.
    • deva: O divine one.
    • śociṣā: With radiance or light.
    • bharadvāje: In the presence of Bharadvaja (a sage or seer).
    • samidhāno: Offering or kindling.
    • yaviṣṭhya: With the best or most excellent.
    • revan: To us or for us.
    • naḥ: Our.
    • śukra: Bright or pure.
    • dīdihi: Shine or illuminate.
    • dyumat: O luminous one.
    • pāvaka: O purifying fire.
    • dīdihi: Shine or illuminate.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni with "bṛhadbhir" suggests a call for vast and powerful blessings.
    • The use of "arcibhiḥ" indicates that the speaker is offering praises and hymns, acknowledging the importance of devotion in spiritual practices.
    • "śukreṇa deva" emphasizes the purity and brightness of the divine, suggesting that one should strive for purity in thoughts and actions.
    • The mention of "bharadvāje" connects the prayer to the sage Bharadvaja, indicating the importance of wisdom and guidance in one's spiritual journey.
    • "samidhāno" refers to the act of kindling the fire, symbolizing the initiation of spiritual practices and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • The phrase "yaviṣṭhya revan naḥ" expresses a desire for the best blessings to come to us, highlighting the human aspiration for excellence and fulfillment.
    • Finally, the repetition of "dīdihi" reinforces the plea for illumination and guidance from the divine, urging the fire to shine brightly in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or ceremonies where fire is present, such as weddings or religious festivals, symbolizing the presence of Agni as a witness and purifier.
    • Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation to seek clarity and guidance in their personal or professional lives, invoking the qualities of purity and brightness.
    • In moments of decision-making, reflecting on the qualities of Agni can inspire one to act with integrity and wisdom, much like the sage Bharadvaja.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, devotion, and wisdom in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and strive for excellence in their actions.
    • By invoking Agni, one can cultivate a sense of clarity and purpose, illuminating the path towards spiritual and personal growth.
