यो मायातुं यातुधानित्याह यो वा रक्षाः शुचिर अस्मीत्याह |इन्द्रस्तं हन्तु महता वधेन विश्वस्य जन्तोर अधमस पदिष्ट ||
yo māyātuṁ yātudhānety āha yo vā rakṣāḥ śucir asmīty āha | indras taṁ hantu mahatā vadhena viśvasya jantor adhamas padīṣṭa ||
  • Mantra: "yo māyātuṁ yātudhānety āha yo vā rakṣāḥ śucir asmīty āha | indras taṁ hantu mahatā vadhena viśvasya jantor adhamas padīṣṭa ||"
  • Translation: "He who speaks of the one who is a master of illusions, and he who is pure, may Indra slay him with a great weapon, the lowest of all beings in the universe."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • māyātuṁ: master of illusions
    • yātudhānety: one who is pure
    • āha: speaks
    • rakṣāḥ: protection or guardian
    • śucir: pure
    • asmīty: may be
    • Indras: Indra, the king of gods
    • hantu: slay
    • mahatā: great
    • vadhena: weapon
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • jantor: beings
    • adhamas: lowest
    • padīṣṭa: placed or situated

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the power of Indra, who is often associated with strength and protection. It emphasizes the importance of purity and the ability to overcome illusions or deceit.
    • The phrase "master of illusions" can be interpreted as those who manipulate reality for their own gain, akin to individuals who engage in deceitful practices in modern society, such as fraudsters or corrupt leaders.
    • The call for Indra to slay such beings with a "great weapon" signifies the need for divine intervention against malevolence and injustice.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to situations where unethical practices are prevalent. For instance, whistleblowers who expose corruption can be seen as invoking the spirit of this mantra, seeking justice against those who manipulate the system.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to be vigilant against deceitful individuals who may not have pure intentions, encouraging one to seek clarity and truth.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of purity and the need to confront and eliminate deceit and malevolence in all forms.
    • Its significance lies in the call for divine protection and justice, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to societal structures.
    • By invoking the strength of Indra, individuals are encouraged to stand against injustice and uphold truth and integrity in their lives.
