यद इन्द्रेण सरथं याथो अश्विनाऽ यद वा वायुनā भवथः समोकसā |यद आदित्येभिर ऋभुभिः सजोषसā यद वा विष्णोर विक्रमणेषु तिष्ठथः ||
yad indreṇa sarathaṁ yātho aśvinā yad vā vāyunā bhavathaḥ samokasā | yad ādityebhir ṛbhubhiḥ sajoṣasā yad vā viṣṇor vikramaṇeṣu tiṣṭhathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indreṇa: by Indra (the king of gods)
    • sarathaṁ: chariots
    • yātho: you go
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (twin gods of medicine and health)
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • vāyunā: by the wind (Vayu)
    • bhavathaḥ: you become
    • samokasā: with a good place
    • yad: whatever
    • ādityebhir: by the Adityas (solar deities)
    • ṛbhubhiḥ: with the wise
    • sajoṣasā: with joy
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • viṣṇor: of Vishnu (the preserver)
    • vikramaṇeṣu: in the acts of valor
    • tiṣṭhathaḥ: you stand
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of various deities, emphasizing the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the roles of Indra, the Ashvins, Vayu, the Adityas, and Vishnu, suggesting that success in life comes from a combination of strength, health, wisdom, and valor.
    • For example, in a real-life scenario, a student preparing for exams may seek the blessings of these deities for strength (Indra), health (Ashvins), and wisdom (Adityas) to achieve success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to success, reminding us that we are not alone in our pursuits.
    • It encourages us to seek divine guidance and support in various aspects of life, whether in personal challenges, professional endeavors, or health-related issues.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applicable in moments of uncertainty or when facing challenges, serving as a reminder to seek help from higher powers and to cultivate strength, wisdom, and health.
