नीललोहितं भवति कृत्याशक्तिर व्यज्यते |एधन्ते अस्याः ज्ञातयः पतिर्बन्धेषु बध्यते ||
nīlalohitam bhavati kṛtyāsaktir vy ajyate | edhante asyā jñātayaḥ patir bandheṣu badhyate ||
  • nīlalohitam: This term can be broken down into two parts: "nīla" meaning blue or dark, and "lohita" meaning red or blood. Together, they symbolize the duality of existence, representing both the material and spiritual aspects of life.
  • bhavati: This translates to "becomes" or "is." It indicates a state of transformation or realization.
  • kṛtyāsaktir: This word refers to "the power of action" or "the ability to perform duties." It emphasizes the importance of being proactive and engaged in one's responsibilities.
  • vy ajyate: This phrase means "is not hindered" or "is not obstructed." It suggests that true action is free from obstacles and distractions.
  • edhante: This means "they are enlightened" or "they are awakened." It refers to those who have gained knowledge and understanding.
  • asyā: This translates to "of this" or "of this knowledge." It indicates a connection to the wisdom being discussed.
  • jñātayaḥ: This term means "the knowers" or "the wise." It refers to those who possess insight and understanding.
  • patir: This means "lord" or "master." It signifies authority and control over one's actions and thoughts.
  • bandheṣu: This translates to "in bonds" or "in attachments." It refers to the ties that bind individuals to worldly concerns and distractions.
  • badhyate: This means "is bound" or "is tied." It indicates a state of being restricted or limited by external factors.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the journey of self-realization and the importance of action in overcoming obstacles. It emphasizes that while one may be bound by worldly attachments, true knowledge and understanding can lead to liberation. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who, despite facing challenges, remain committed to their goals and responsibilities. They harness their inner strength and wisdom to navigate through life's complexities, ultimately achieving a state of enlightenment. This mantra serves as a reminder to embrace our duties while seeking deeper understanding, allowing us to transcend the limitations imposed by our circumstances.
