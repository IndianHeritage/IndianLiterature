अहाव्य अग्नि हविः आस्ये ते श्रुचिव घृतं चाम्वीव सोमः |वाजसनीं रयिम अस्मे सुवीरं प्रशस्तं धेहि यशसम बृहन्तम् ||
ahāvy agne havir āsye te srucīva ghṛtaṁ camvīva somaḥ | vājasaniṁ rayim asme suvīram praśastaṁ dhehi yaśasam bṛhantam ||
  • ahāvy agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, the divine fire." Agni is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing fire, energy, and transformation. In real life, fire symbolizes purification and the energy that fuels our daily activities.
  • havir āsye te: This means "we offer our oblation to you." An oblation is a ritual offering, often made to deities. In modern terms, this can be likened to acts of gratitude or offerings we make in our lives, such as helping others or contributing to our communities.
  • srucīva ghṛtaṁ: This translates to "like a ladle, ghee." Ghee, or clarified butter, is a sacred substance in Vedic rituals. It represents nourishment and abundance. In everyday life, it can symbolize the importance of nurturing ourselves and others, both physically and spiritually.
  • camvīva somaḥ: This means "like the Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In contemporary life, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to seeking wisdom through education and experience.
  • vājasaniṁ rayim asme: This translates to "bestow upon us strength and wealth." This line emphasizes the desire for prosperity and vitality. In real life, it reflects our aspirations for success and well-being, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • suvīram praśastaṁ: This means "the excellent hero." It signifies the qualities of bravery and valor. In modern contexts, this can relate to individuals who inspire us through their courage, whether in social justice, environmental activism, or personal challenges.
  • dhehi yaśasam bṛhantam: This translates to "grant us great fame." This line expresses the wish for recognition and honor. In today's world, it can be seen in the pursuit of achievements that leave a lasting impact, such as innovations, artistic contributions, or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine blessings in various aspects of life, including strength, prosperity, and recognition. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine forces that guide and support us in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, to offer gratitude, and to strive for excellence in their pursuits, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
