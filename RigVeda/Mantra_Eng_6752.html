आ नः सहस्रशो भरायुतानि शतानि च |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
ā naḥ sahasraśo bharāyutāni śatāni ca | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "May it be ours" - a plea for possession or benefit.
    • sahasraśo: "thousands" - indicating abundance or multitude.
    • bharāyutāni: "may you bestow" - a request for giving or providing.
    • śatāni: "hundreds" - further emphasizing the idea of plenty.
    • ca: "and" - a conjunction linking ideas.
    • divo: "of the heavens" - referring to the divine or celestial realm.
    • amuṣya: "of that" - indicating something that is beyond or transcendent.
    • śāsato: "eternal" - suggesting something that is everlasting or timeless.
    • divaṁ: "heaven" - again referring to the celestial or divine space.
    • yaya: "by which" - indicating a means or method.
    • divāvaso: "day and night" - representing the cycle of time or existence.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a deep yearning for abundance and blessings from the divine.
    • It emphasizes the desire for both material and spiritual wealth, asking for thousands and hundreds of blessings.
    • The reference to the heavens and eternity suggests a connection to the divine and the timeless nature of existence.
    • It acknowledges the cyclical nature of life, encompassing both day and night, and the continuous flow of time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, one might recite this mantra during a prayer for prosperity, seeking success in their career or business.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be invoked to bless collective efforts, such as during festivals or charitable events, aiming for abundance for all involved.
    • In a spiritual setting, practitioners may use this mantra to connect with the divine, seeking guidance and blessings for their spiritual journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for abundance and divine blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging individuals to seek not just personal gain but also the well-being of the community.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal aspirations to collective endeavors, reinforcing the idea that abundance is not just material but also spiritual.
