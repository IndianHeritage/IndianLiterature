यो न आगो अभ्य एनो भरात्य अधीद अगम् अगहशंसे दधात |जही चिकीर्त्वो अभिशस्तिम एताम अग्ने यो नो मर्चयति द्वयेना ||
yo na āgo abhy eno bharāty adhīd agham aghaśaṁse dadhāta | jahī cikitvo abhiśastim etām agne yo no marcayati dvayena ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • na: not
    • āgo: goes
    • abhy: towards
    • eno: this
    • bharāty: carries
    • adhīd: overcomes
    • agham: sin
    • aghaśaṁse: in the presence of sin
    • dadhāta: bestows
  • jahī: let go
  • cikitvo: of the wise
  • abhiśastim: the curse
  • etām: this
  • agne: O Fire (Agni)
  • yo: who
  • no: for us
  • marcayati: enlightens
  • dvayena: with two

  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine messenger.
    • The first part emphasizes the importance of overcoming sin and negativity, suggesting that one who does not succumb to evil and carries the burden of righteousness is blessed.
    • The second part calls upon Agni to remove curses and enlighten us, indicating a plea for guidance and protection from negative influences.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often face moral dilemmas where they must choose between right and wrong. This mantra serves as a reminder to choose righteousness and overcome temptations.
    • In a professional setting, when faced with unethical practices, one can invoke the essence of this mantra to stand firm against wrongdoing and promote integrity.
    • During challenging times, such as personal loss or failure, the invocation of Agni can symbolize the hope for renewal and the shedding of past burdens, leading to personal growth.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of overcoming negativity and sin in our lives while seeking guidance and enlightenment from the divine.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to ethical dilemmas, reminding us to uphold righteousness and seek transformation.
    • By invoking Agni, we not only seek protection from curses but also aspire for clarity and wisdom in our actions, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and virtuous life.
