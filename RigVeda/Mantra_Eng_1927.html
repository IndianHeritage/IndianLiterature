श्रियें पूषन्न इषुकृतेव देवाः नासत्याः वहतुः सूर्यायाः |वाच्यन्ते वां ककुहा अप्सु जाताः युगाः जूर्णेव वरुणस्य भूरेः ||
śriye pūṣann iṣukṛteva devā nāsatyā vahatuṁ sūryāyāḥ | vacyante vāṁ kakuhā apsu jātā yugā jūrṇeva varuṇasya bhūreḥ ||
  • śriye: This word translates to "for prosperity" or "for wealth." It signifies the desire for abundance and success in life.
  • pūṣann: This term refers to "the nourisher" or "the one who provides sustenance." It is often associated with the Sun God, who is seen as a source of life and nourishment.
  • iṣukṛteva: This can be interpreted as "like an arrow." It suggests precision and focus in achieving one's goals.
  • devā: This means "gods" or "divine beings." It indicates a connection to the spiritual or divine realm.
  • nāsatyā: This refers to "the Asvins," twin deities known for their healing and protective qualities. They symbolize duality and balance.
  • vahatuṁ: This means "may they carry" or "may they bring." It expresses a wish for the divine to bestow blessings.
  • sūryāyāḥ: This translates to "of the Sun." It emphasizes the importance of the Sun in providing light, energy, and life.
  • vacyante: This means "they are spoken" or "they are declared." It indicates the power of words and intentions.
  • vāṁ: This translates to "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • kakuhā: This refers to "the womb" or "the source." It symbolizes creation and the origin of life.
  • apsu: This means "in the waters." It signifies the importance of water as a life-giving element.
  • jātā: This translates to "born" or "arisen." It indicates the emergence of new life or ideas.
  • yugā: This refers to "ages" or "cycles." It signifies the passage of time and the cyclical nature of existence.
  • jūrṇeva: This means "like a well-worn path." It suggests familiarity and the wisdom gained through experience.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "of Varuna," the god of water and cosmic order. It emphasizes the importance of balance and harmony in life.
  • bhūreḥ: This means "of the vastness" or "of the great expanse." It signifies the infinite nature of the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity and nourishment from the divine, particularly through the Sun and the Asvins. It highlights the importance of intention, balance, and the cyclical nature of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for abundance, in meditation for clarity and focus, or in daily affirmations to attract positivity and success. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can align themselves with the greater cosmic order, fostering a sense of harmony and fulfillment in their lives.
