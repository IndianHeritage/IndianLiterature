अस्माकं इन्द्रेहि नो रथम अवा पुरन्ध्या |वयं शविष्ठ वारणं दिवि श्रवो दधिमहि दिवि स्तोमं मनामहे ||
asmākam indrehi no ratham avā puraṁdhyā | vayaṁ śaviṣṭha vāryaṁ divi śravo dadhīmahi divi stomam manāmahe ||
  • asmākam: This word translates to "our" or "of us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • indrehi: Refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • no: Means "not" or "to us," suggesting a plea or request directed towards Indra.
  • ratham: Translates to "chariot," which is often a metaphor for the journey of life or the means of achieving goals.
  • avā: Means "to protect" or "to guard," indicating a desire for safety and support.
  • puraṁdhyā: Refers to "the one who is the best," often associated with Indra's prowess and capability.
  • vayaṁ: Means "we," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • śaviṣṭha: Translates to "the most powerful" or "the strongest," emphasizing the need for strength in the face of challenges.
  • vāryaṁ: Means "the one who is worthy," indicating a call for recognition of merit and value.
  • divi: Refers to "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm," suggesting a connection to higher powers.
  • śravo: Means "fame" or "glory," indicating a desire for recognition and honor.
  • dadhīmahi: Translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," suggesting a reflective and thoughtful approach to life.
  • stomam: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • manāmahe: Means "we think" or "we intend," reinforcing the idea of conscious intention and focus.

This mantra can be interpreted as a collective invocation for strength, protection, and guidance from Indra, the divine leader. It emphasizes the importance of community, the pursuit of power and recognition, and the need for divine support in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork: In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teams to work together towards a common goal, seeking strength and support from each other.
  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on the qualities of Indra, striving to embody strength and wisdom while guiding their teams.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek inner strength and recognition of their own worth, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and divine guidance. It serves as a reminder that in our journeys, whether personal or collective, we can seek support from higher powers and strive for greatness together. By meditating on these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
