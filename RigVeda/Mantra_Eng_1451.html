दुर्मानमानं सुमन्तुभिर एषा प्रचामहि |आ सत्याभिर इन्द्रं द्युम्नहूतिभिर यजत्रं द्युम्नहूतिभिः ||
durmanmānaṁ sumantubhir em iṣā pṛcīmahi | ā satyābhir indraṁ dyumnahūtibhir yajatraṁ dyumnahūtibhiḥ ||
  • Durmanmānaṁ: This term refers to someone who is difficult to please or has a negative disposition. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who are often critical or pessimistic, making it challenging to engage with them positively.
  • Sumantubhir: This word means "with good thoughts" or "wise." It emphasizes the importance of approaching situations and individuals with a positive mindset. For example, in a workplace, having a team that fosters good thoughts can lead to better collaboration and productivity.
  • Em iṣā: This phrase translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking guidance or support from a higher power or authority. In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek blessings for their endeavors.
  • Pṛcīmahi: This means "we inquire" or "we ask." It highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding. For instance, in education, students are encouraged to ask questions to deepen their understanding of a subject.
  • Ā satyābhir: This translates to "with truths" or "through truth." It underscores the significance of honesty and integrity in our actions. In real life, this can be reflected in ethical business practices where transparency is valued.
  • Indraṁ: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and leadership. Invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and guidance in challenging situations, much like a leader guiding their team through difficulties.
  • Dyumnahūtibhir: This term refers to "with glorious offerings" or "with praise." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the contributions of others. In a community setting, acknowledging the efforts of volunteers can foster a sense of belonging and motivation.
  • Yajatraṁ: This means "the act of worship" or "sacrifice." It signifies the importance of dedication and commitment to a cause. In real life, this can be seen in charitable activities where individuals dedicate their time and resources for the betterment of society.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of approaching challenges with a positive mindset, seeking knowledge, and invoking strength and guidance from higher powers. It teaches us the value of truth, recognition, and dedication in our actions. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community service, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and productive existence.
