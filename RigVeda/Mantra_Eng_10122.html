इति त्वाग्ने वृष्टिहव्यस्य पुत्रा उपस्तुतास ऋषयोऽवोचन | तांश च पाहि गृणतश च सूरीन वषड वषळ इत्य ऊर्ध्वासो अनक्षन नमो नम इत्य ऊर्ध्वासो अनक्षन ||
iti tvāgne vṛṣṭihavyasya putrā upastutāsa ṛṣayo 'vocan | tām̐ś ca pāhi gṛṇataś ca sūrīn vaṣaḍ vaṣaḻ ity ūrdhvāso anakṣan namo nama ity ūrdhvāso anakṣan ||
  • iti tvāgne: This phrase can be translated as "thus, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, representing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • vṛṣṭihavyasya: This term refers to "the rain of offerings." It signifies the blessings and abundance that come from divine grace, often associated with the rituals performed to please the deities.
  • putrā upastutāsa: This translates to "the sons who are praised." It indicates the importance of progeny and the role of children in carrying forward the family lineage and performing rituals.
  • ṛṣayo 'vocan: This means "the sages have spoken." It highlights the wisdom of the ancient sages who impart knowledge and guidance through their teachings.
  • tām̐ś ca pāhi: This phrase translates to "protect them." It is a plea for divine protection and guidance for the offerings and the individuals involved in the rituals.
  • gṛṇataś ca sūrīn: This means "and the brave ones who sing." It emphasizes the importance of courage and the act of singing praises, which can be seen as a form of devotion and celebration.
  • vaṣaḍ vaṣaḻ: This is an invocation, often used in Vedic rituals, calling upon the divine forces to come forth and bless the offerings.
  • ity ūrdhvāso anakṣan: This translates to "thus, the upward-facing ones are unblemished." It signifies purity and the aspiration to rise above worldly concerns, seeking higher spiritual truths.
  • namo nama: This means "salutations, salutations." It is a common expression of reverence and humility towards the divine.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the importance of rituals, and the connection between the material and spiritual realms. It emphasizes the role of Agni as a mediator, the significance of offerings, and the wisdom of the sages. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Performing rituals during significant life events, like weddings or housewarming ceremonies, to invoke blessings and protection.
  • Encouraging a sense of gratitude and reverence in daily life, recognizing the divine presence in nature and relationships.
  • Fostering a sense of community through collective rituals and celebrations, strengthening bonds among family and friends.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual practices, and the pursuit of higher truths in our everyday existence.
