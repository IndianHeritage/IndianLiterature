मन्त्र: नानां चक्राते यम्याः वपूंषि तयोः अन्यद रोचते कृष्णं अन्यत् |श्यावी च यद अरुशी च स्वसारौ महद देवानाम् असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
nānā cakrāte yamyā3 vapūṁṣi tayor anyad rocate kṛṣṇam anyat | śyāvī ca yad aruṣī ca svasārau mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Mantra: "nānā cakrāte yamyā3 vapūṁṣi tayor anyad rocate kṛṣṇam anyat | śyāvī ca yad aruṣī ca svasārau mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • nānā: various or diverse
    • cakrāte: are created or manifested
    • yamyā: by whom or by which
    • vapūṁṣi: forms or bodies
    • tayor: of those
    • anyad: other or different
    • rocate: shines or is attractive
    • kṛṣṇam: the black or dark one (often referring to Lord Krishna)
    • anyat: other
    • śyāvī: the dark one
    • ca: and
    • yad: which
    • aruṣī: the reddish one
    • svasārau: the sisters
    • mahad: great
    • devānām: of the gods
    • asuratvam: the nature of the non-divine or demonic
    • ekam: one or singular
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the diversity of forms and manifestations in the universe.
    • It highlights how various forms (vapūṁṣi) are created and how they shine differently.
    • The reference to "kṛṣṇam" and "aruṣī" suggests the duality of existence, where both dark and light, or different aspects of divinity, coexist.
    • The mention of "svasārau" indicates a relationship, possibly between different forms of divinity or aspects of nature.
    • Ultimately, it points to the singular essence (ekam) that underlies all diversity, emphasizing unity in multiplicity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see various forms of life, from the smallest microorganisms to the largest mammals, all coexisting and contributing to the ecosystem.
    • In human society, individuals from diverse backgrounds and cultures come together, each bringing unique perspectives and strengths, yet all part of the larger human experience.
    • The concept of duality can be observed in the balance of emotions; joy and sorrow, love and hate, all are essential parts of the human experience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and appreciating diversity in the world while understanding that all forms are manifestations of a singular essence.
    • It teaches us to embrace differences and find unity in diversity, which is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in community building, conflict resolution, and personal relationships.
    • By acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings, we can foster a sense of harmony and respect in our interactions with others and the environment.
