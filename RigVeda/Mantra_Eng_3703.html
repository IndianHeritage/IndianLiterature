आ ते अग्न इधिमहि द्युमन्तं देवाजरम् |यद धा स्याऽते पानीयसी समिद दीदायति द्यवीषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
ā te agna idhīmahi dyumantaṁ devājaram | yad dha syā te panīyasī samid dīdayati dyavīṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • agna: O Fire
    • idhīmahi: We meditate or invoke
    • dyumantaṁ: Shining or radiant
    • devājaram: O divine, immortal one
    • yad: That which
    • dha: You give
    • syā: May it be
    • te: Your
    • panīyasī: Most excellent or pure
    • samid: The offering or fuel
    • dīdayati: Illuminates or shines
    • dyavīṣaṁ: The heavens
    • stotṛbhya: To the worshippers
    • ā: To
    • bhara: Bring or fill
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
    • The invocation of Agni as "dyumantaṁ" highlights his radiant and illuminating qualities, akin to how fire brings light and warmth to our lives.
    • The phrase "yad dha syā te panīyasī" suggests a request for the divine to provide the best offerings, akin to how we seek the best for ourselves and our loved ones in life.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the importance of fire in various cultures, such as in cooking, warmth, and as a symbol of life and energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the reverence for fire as a sacred element that connects the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the purity and transformative power of fire in our daily lives, whether in rituals, cooking, or as a source of energy.
    • In modern contexts, it can remind us of the importance of sustainability and respect for natural resources, as fire is both a tool and a force of nature.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to invoke the divine presence in our actions and to seek purity and excellence in our offerings to the world.
