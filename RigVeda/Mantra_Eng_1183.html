यद इन्द्राग्नि यदुषु तुर्वशेषु यद द्रुह्युष्व अनुषु पूरुषु स्थः |अतः परि वृषणाव आ हि यातम अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī yaduṣu turvaśeṣu yad druhyuṣv anuṣu pūruṣu sthaḥ | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing strength and fire)
    • yaduṣu: in the places where
    • turvaśeṣu: in the strong (or powerful)
    • yad: whatever
    • druhyuṣv: in the enemies
    • anuṣu: in the followers
    • pūruṣu: among the people
    • sthaḥ: standing or established
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: let us go
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • pibataṁ: let us drink
    • sutasya: of the son (or offspring)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Agni, two powerful deities in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and energy.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength among people, encouraging them to stand together against adversities.
    • The reference to drinking soma signifies the pursuit of spiritual and physical nourishment, which is essential for vitality and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community facing challenges, this mantra can inspire collective action and resilience, reminding individuals of their strength when united.
    • In personal development, the idea of seeking nourishment—both physical and spiritual—can be applied through practices like meditation, healthy eating, and community engagement.
    • In leadership, invoking the qualities of Indra and Agni can guide leaders to be strong and decisive while fostering a sense of community and support among their followers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, strength, and the importance of spiritual nourishment in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to come together, face challenges, and seek holistic well-being.
    • In modern contexts, it can be applied in various scenarios, from community building to personal growth, emphasizing the timeless wisdom of the Vedas.
