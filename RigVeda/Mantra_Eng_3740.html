त्वं नो अग्नि अङ्गिरः स्तुतः स्तवाना आ भर | होतर विभ्वासहं रयिं स्तोत्रिभ्यः स्तवसे च न उतैधि पृथ्सु नो वृधे ||
tvaṁ no agne aṅgiraḥ stutaḥ stavāna ā bhara | hotar vibhvāsahaṁ rayiṁ stotṛbhyaḥ stavase ca na utaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing transformation and sustenance.
  • aṅgiraḥ stutaḥ: "Praised by the Angiras." The Angiras are a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. This indicates that the mantra is invoking the blessings of both Agni and the wise sages, emphasizing the importance of knowledge and guidance in our lives.
  • stavāna ā bhara: "Bestow upon us your praises." This line is a request for Agni to grant us the ability to express gratitude and reverence. In everyday life, expressing gratitude can foster positive relationships and a sense of community.
  • hotar vibhvāsahaṁ rayiṁ: "The priest who offers the oblation, grant us wealth." Here, the term "hotar" refers to the priest who performs rituals. This highlights the significance of rituals in attracting prosperity and abundance in our lives, akin to how we seek blessings through hard work and dedication.
  • stotṛbhyaḥ stavase ca: "To those who praise you." This acknowledges the importance of community and collective worship. In real life, collaboration and support from others can lead to greater achievements and fulfillment.
  • na utaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe: "May we not be hindered in our growth." This line expresses a desire for unhindered progress and development. In practical terms, it reflects the universal aspiration for personal and communal growth, whether in education, career, or relationships.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of invoking divine energy (Agni) and the wisdom of sages (Angiras) to seek blessings for prosperity, knowledge, and growth. It highlights the importance of community, gratitude, and the role of rituals in achieving success. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate positive relationships, express gratitude, and engage in practices that promote personal and communal development, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
