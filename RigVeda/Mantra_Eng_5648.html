उद वां चक्षुर वरुण सुप्रतीकं देवयोर एति सूर्यस्ततन्वान् |अभि यो विश्वा भुवनानि चष्टे स मन्युम मर्त्येष्व आ चिकेट ||
ud vāṁ cakṣur varuṇa supratīkaṁ devayor eti sūryas tatanvān | abhi yo viśvā bhuvanāni caṣṭe sa manyum martyeṣv ā ciketa ||
  • ud vāṁ cakṣur varuṇa supratīkaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Varuna, may my eyes be opened." Here, Varuna is the deity associated with cosmic order and truth. The invocation suggests a desire for clarity and understanding in perception.
  • devayor eti sūryas tatanvān: This part means "the sun, radiant and powerful, approaches the divine." The sun symbolizes enlightenment, energy, and life. It represents the pursuit of knowledge and the illumination of the mind.
  • abhi yo viśvā bhuvanāni caṣṭe: This translates to "whoever moves through all the worlds." It indicates the omnipresence of the divine and the interconnectedness of all existence. It emphasizes the idea that the divine is present in every aspect of life.
  • sa manyum martyeṣv ā ciketa: This means "he who knows the essence of life among mortals." It suggests that true understanding and wisdom come from recognizing the deeper truths of existence and the transient nature of life.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, clarity, and understanding in life. It encourages individuals to open their minds and hearts to the divine and to the truths of the universe.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a meditation tool to seek clarity in their thoughts and actions, helping them make better decisions.
    • Education: Students can recite this mantra to invoke a sense of enlightenment and focus while studying, aiming to absorb knowledge more effectively.
    • Conflict Resolution: In situations of disagreement, invoking the essence of this mantra can remind individuals to seek understanding and truth rather than being driven by emotions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and understanding in our lives. By invoking the divine and striving for clarity, individuals can navigate the complexities of existence with greater awareness and purpose.
