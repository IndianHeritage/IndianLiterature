नित्ये चिन् नु यं सदने जगृभ्रे प्रशस्तिभिर दधिरे यज्ञियासः |प्र सूनयन्त गृभयन्त इष्टाव अश्वासो न रथ्यो रारहाणः ||
nitye cin nu yaṁ sadane jagṛbhre praśastibhir dadhire yajñiyāsaḥ | pra sū nayanta gṛbhayanta iṣṭāv aśvāso na rathyo rārahāṇāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nitye: eternal, constant
    • cin: indeed, certainly
    • nu: now, indeed
    • yaṁ: who, which
    • sadane: in the abode, dwelling
    • jagṛbhre: have grasped, have taken hold of
    • praśastibhir: with praises, commendations
    • dadhire: have established, have set
    • yajñiyāsaḥ: the offerings, sacrifices
    • pra: forward, towards
    • sū: good, auspicious
    • nayanta: leading, guiding
    • gṛbhayanta: taking, receiving
    • iṣṭāv: the desired, the worthy
    • aśvāso: horses, steeds
    • na: not
    • rathyo: chariots
    • rārahāṇāḥ: those who are swift, those who move quickly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal and constant nature of the divine, emphasizing the importance of recognizing and grasping this truth.
    • The phrase "who have grasped in the abode" suggests that those who understand and appreciate the divine presence in their lives are able to establish a connection through their actions and offerings.
    • The mention of "praises" indicates that acknowledgment and gratitude towards the divine are essential in fostering a relationship with it.
    • The term "yajñiyāsaḥ" refers to the offerings or sacrifices made, which can be interpreted as the good deeds and intentions we put forth in our daily lives.
    • The latter part of the mantra highlights the guidance and support that comes from the divine, leading us towards our goals and desires, represented by "the desired" and "the worthy."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when individuals work together with a sense of purpose and gratitude, they often achieve greater success. For instance, a team that acknowledges each other's contributions and celebrates their achievements is likely to perform better.
    • In personal relationships, expressing appreciation and love can strengthen bonds. A family that regularly shares gratitude for one another fosters a supportive and nurturing environment.
    • In spiritual practices, rituals and offerings made with sincerity and devotion can create a deeper connection with the divine, leading to a sense of peace and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the eternal presence of the divine in our lives and the importance of gratitude and good deeds.
    • It teaches us that by acknowledging the divine and making sincere offerings, we can receive guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of gratitude, teamwork, and sincerity can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
