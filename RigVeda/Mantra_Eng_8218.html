वृषा पवस्व धारणया मरुत्वते च मत्सरः |विश्वा दधाना ओजसा ||
vṛṣā pavasva dhārayā marutvate ca matsaraḥ | viśvā dadhāna ojasā ||
  • vṛṣā: This word can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and vitality, which are essential qualities in both physical and mental realms.
  • pavasva: This term means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It emphasizes the importance of purification, whether it be of the mind, body, or spirit, to achieve a higher state of being.
  • dhārayā: This word translates to "holding" or "sustaining." It suggests the idea of support and stability, which are crucial in maintaining balance in life.
  • marutvate: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, this term embodies the concept of dynamic energy and movement. It symbolizes the forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • ca: This simple conjunction means "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • matsaraḥ: This word means "jealousy" or "envy." It highlights the negative emotions that can hinder personal growth and harmony in relationships.
  • viśvā: This term translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • dadhāna: This word means "to establish" or "to create." It emphasizes the act of building and nurturing relationships, communities, and environments.
  • ojasā: This term refers to "vitality" or "energy." It represents the life force that sustains us and drives our actions and intentions.

In summary, this mantra calls upon the powerful forces of nature and the universe to purify and sustain us while reminding us to overcome jealousy and embrace inclusivity. It encourages us to harness our inner strength and vitality to create a harmonious existence.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by focusing on personal growth and self-improvement. For example, engaging in meditation or yoga can help purify the mind and body, leading to a more balanced life.
  • In relationships, it serves as a reminder to foster understanding and compassion, rather than allowing jealousy to create rifts. Practicing open communication can help establish stronger bonds.
  • In a broader context, this mantra can inspire community building and collaboration, encouraging individuals to work together for a common goal, thus creating a more inclusive society.
