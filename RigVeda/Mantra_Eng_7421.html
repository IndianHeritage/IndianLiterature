अति नो विष्पिता पुरु नौभिर अपो न पर्वथ || यूम ṛतस्य रथ्यः ||
ati no viṣpitā puru naubhir apo na parṣatha | yūyam ṛtasya rathyaḥ ||
  • ati no viṣpitā puru naubhir apo na parṣatha
    • ati: This word means "beyond" or "exceeding." It suggests a sense of surpassing limits or boundaries.
    • no: This is a pronoun meaning "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity.
    • viṣpitā: This term can be interpreted as "spread out" or "expanded," often referring to the vastness of the universe or the divine presence.
    • puru: Meaning "many" or "numerous," it emphasizes abundance or plurality.
    • naubhir: This word translates to "by the nine," which can refer to the nine forms of energy or the nine planets in Vedic cosmology.
    • apo: Meaning "water," it symbolizes life, nourishment, and purity.
    • na: A negation, meaning "not," indicating a refusal or absence.
    • parṣatha: This term can be understood as "to be disturbed" or "to be troubled," suggesting a state of peace or stability.
  • yūyam ṛtasya rathyaḥ
    • yūyam: This is a pronoun meaning "you" (plural), addressing a group or community.
    • ṛtasya: This word refers to "truth" or "cosmic order," representing the fundamental principles that govern the universe.
    • rathyaḥ: Meaning "charioteers" or "drivers," it symbolizes those who guide or lead, often in the context of navigating through life or challenges.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective strength and the pursuit of truth. It suggests that by transcending limitations and embracing the vastness of existence, individuals can navigate the challenges of life with wisdom and unity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to teamwork, community efforts, and leadership, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal while adhering to principles of truth and righteousness. The mantra serves as a reminder that harmony and cooperation are essential for overcoming obstacles and achieving success in any endeavor.
