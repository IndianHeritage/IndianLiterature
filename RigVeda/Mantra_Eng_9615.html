या उदृचि यज्ञे अध्वरेष्ठा मरुद्भ्यो न मानवो ददाशत् |रेवत स वयो दधते सुवीरं स देवानामपि गोपीथे अस्तु ||
ya udṛci yajñe adhvareṣṭhā marudbhyo na mānuṣo dadāśat | revat sa vayo dadhate suvīraṁ sa devānām api gopīthe astu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • udṛci: in the high place or in the heavens
    • yajñe: in the sacrifice or worship
    • adhvareṣṭhā: the best of the rituals
    • marudbhyo: to the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • na: not
    • mānuṣo: human
    • dadāśat: has given
    • revat: the one who is prosperous
    • sa: he
    • vayo: age or strength
    • dadhate: maintains or holds
    • suvīraṁ: excellent or strong
    • sa: he
    • devānām: of the gods
    • api: also
    • gopīthe: in the protection or care
    • astu: may it be so
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is exalted in the heavens and is involved in the highest forms of worship.
    • The reference to the Maruts indicates a connection to the forces of nature, particularly storms, which are often seen as powerful and transformative.
    • The phrase "not a human" suggests that this being transcends human limitations, embodying divine qualities.
    • The mantra emphasizes prosperity and strength, indicating that this divine presence supports and maintains the strength of the gods.
    • The concluding phrase "may it be so" reflects a wish or prayer for the divine presence to continue its protective and nurturing role.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of seeking higher guidance in times of difficulty, much like how people turn to spirituality or meditation for strength and clarity.
    • The Maruts can symbolize the forces of nature that we respect and seek to understand, such as climate change, where we look for divine or higher wisdom to guide our actions.
    • In community rituals or gatherings, invoking such divine qualities can foster a sense of unity and strength among participants, encouraging them to work together for a common goal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and strength in both personal and communal aspects of life.
    • It reminds us to seek higher wisdom and support from the universe, especially in challenging times.
    • In a broader context, it encourages respect for nature and the divine forces that govern our existence, promoting harmony and balance in our lives.
