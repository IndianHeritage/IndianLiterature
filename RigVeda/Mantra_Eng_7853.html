यत सोम चित्रं उक्त्यं दिव्यम पार्थिवं वसु |तन् नः पुनान आ भर ||
yat soma citram ukthyaṁ divyam pārthivaṁ vasu | tan naḥ punāna ā bhara ||
  • Mantra: "yat soma citram ukthyaṁ divyam pārthivaṁ vasu | tan naḥ punāna ā bhara ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • soma: soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • citram: beautiful, colorful, or splendid
    • ukthyaṁ: uttered or spoken (referring to hymns or praises)
    • divyam: divine or heavenly
    • pārthivaṁ: earthly or material
    • vasu: wealth or prosperity
    • tan: that
    • naḥ: for us
    • punāna: purifying or cleansing
    • ā bhara: may it fill or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of the soma, a divine and splendid drink that symbolizes spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The reference to "divyam" and "pārthivaṁ" suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly, indicating that spiritual wealth can manifest in our material lives.
    • The phrase "tan naḥ punāna ā bhara" expresses a desire for this divine essence to purify and enrich our lives, both spiritually and materially.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be likened to practices that promote mental and spiritual well-being, such as meditation, yoga, or engaging in creative arts.
    • Just as the soma is believed to bestow immortality, individuals often seek experiences that provide lasting joy and fulfillment, such as meaningful relationships or acts of kindness.
    • The mantra can be applied in rituals or personal practices where one seeks to harmonize their spiritual aspirations with their material existence, such as during festivals or personal reflections.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking a balance between the spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just in material possessions but also in spiritual richness and purity.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can aspire to cultivate a life that is both prosperous and spiritually fulfilling, leading to a holistic approach to well-being.
