मन्त्र: पुरं न धृष्णवाऽ रुज कृष्णया बाधितो विशा | अन्ति षड्भूत वाम अवः ||
puraṁ na dhṛṣṇav ā ruja kṛṣṇayā bādhito viśā | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • puraṁ: city or place
    • na: not
    • dhṛṣṇav: of the strong or powerful
    • ā: from
    • ruja: pain or suffering
    • kṛṣṇayā: by the black or dark (often associated with Lord Krishna)
    • bādhito: hindered or obstructed
    • viśā: of the community or society
    • anti: end or conclusion
    • ṣad: six
    • bhūtu: beings or entities
    • vām: your or of you
    • avaḥ: may you bring or lead
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the removal of pain and suffering from a community or society.
    • The phrase "puraṁ na dhṛṣṇav ā ruja" suggests that in a place where the strong are present, pain should not exist.
    • The invocation of "kṛṣṇayā" indicates a reliance on divine strength, particularly that of Lord Krishna, to overcome obstacles.
    • The latter part, "anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ," emphasizes the importance of bringing together the six essential beings or elements for harmony and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community facing challenges such as poverty or violence, this mantra can be a reminder to seek strength in unity and divine guidance.
    • In personal struggles, individuals can invoke this mantra to find resilience and support from their community, much like how people come together during crises.
    • In workplaces, fostering a supportive environment can help alleviate stress and promote well-being, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength and the removal of suffering through divine intervention.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community issues, emphasizing the importance of unity and support.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for a harmonious existence, free from pain and obstacles.
