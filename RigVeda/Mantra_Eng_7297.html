अधुक्षत पिप्युषीम इषम् ऊर्जं सप्तपदीं अरिः |सूर्यस्य सप्त रश्मिभिः ||
adhukṣat pipyuṣīm iṣam ūrjaṁ saptapadīm ariḥ | sūryasya sapta raśmibhiḥ ||
  • adhukṣat: This term can be interpreted as "he who has been invoked" or "he who has been called upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • pipyuṣīm: This word refers to something that is nourishing or life-giving, often associated with the essence of sustenance and abundance.
  • iṣam: This translates to "the arrow" or "the aim," symbolizing focus, direction, and purpose in one's endeavors.
  • ūrjaṁ: Meaning "energy" or "vitality," this term emphasizes the importance of strength and vigor in achieving one's goals.
  • saptapadīm: This can be understood as "the seven steps," which may represent the journey of life or the stages of progress one must undertake.
  • ariḥ: This word means "enemy" or "obstacle," indicating the challenges that one may face in life.
  • sūryasya: This translates to "of the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • sapta: Meaning "seven," this number often holds significance in various cultures, representing completeness or wholeness.
  • raśmibhiḥ: This term refers to "rays" or "beams," particularly those of the sun, which can symbolize guidance, clarity, and illumination.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving one's goals through energy and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of focus and direction in life, while also acknowledging the challenges that may arise along the way. The reference to the sun and its rays suggests that enlightenment and knowledge are essential in this journey.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can invoke this mantra to seek clarity and strength in pursuing their goals, whether in career, education, or personal growth.
  • In times of adversity, one can reflect on the challenges faced and draw upon the energy and nourishment needed to overcome them, much like a plant seeking sunlight to grow.
  • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts to overcome societal obstacles, emphasizing the importance of unity and shared purpose.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interplay between divine guidance, personal effort, and the challenges of life. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment and energy while remaining focused on their goals, ultimately leading to enlightenment and success.
