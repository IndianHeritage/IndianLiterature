मन्त्र: एते नरः स्वपसो अभूतन य इन्द्राय सुनुता सोमम् अद्रयः |वामं-वामं वो दिव्याय धाम्ने वसु-वसु वः पार्थिवाय सुन्वते ||
ete naraḥ svapaso abhūtana ya indrāya sunutha somam adrayaḥ | vāmaṁ-vāmaṁ vo divyāya dhāmne vasu-vasu vaḥ pārthivāya sunvate ||
  • Translation: "These men, who have awakened, have become the embodiment of Indra; they have offered the divine soma to the one who is worthy. May the divine and the earthly bestow upon you their blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete naraḥ: "These men" - Refers to individuals who are enlightened or awakened.
    • svapaso abhūtana: "who have awakened" - Indicates a state of consciousness or realization.
    • ya indrāya: "to Indra" - Indra is the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • sunutha somam: "offer the divine soma" - Soma represents a sacred drink that grants immortality and divine insight.
    • adrayaḥ: "to the one who is worthy" - Suggests that offerings should be made to those deserving of respect and honor.
    • vāmaṁ-vāmaṁ: "divine and earthly" - Represents the duality of existence, both spiritual and material.
    • vo divyāya dhāmne: "to you, divine abode" - Refers to the ultimate spiritual goal or state of being.
    • vasu-vasu vaḥ: "bestow upon you" - A call for blessings and prosperity.
    • pārthivāya sunvate: "earthly blessings" - Acknowledges the importance of material well-being alongside spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who are enlightened and aware of their responsibilities can inspire their teams, much like the "men who have awakened." They embody qualities of strength and wisdom, akin to Indra.
    • In personal relationships, offering support and kindness to those who are deserving can create a nurturing environment, similar to the offering of soma to the worthy.
    • In community service, individuals who work towards the betterment of society reflect the duality of divine and earthly, striving for both spiritual fulfillment and material improvement for others.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awakening to one's true potential and the significance of offering respect and blessings to those who embody strength and wisdom. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, relationships, and community service. By recognizing and honoring the divine qualities in ourselves and others, we can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence, both spiritually and materially.
