क्व स्याऽवो मरुतः स्वधासीद यन् मां एकं समधत्ताहिहत्ये |अहं ह्य उग्रस्तविषस्तुविष्मान् विश्वस्य शत्रोर अनमं वधस्नैः ||
kva1 syā vo marutaḥ svadhāsīd yan mām ekaṁ samadhattāhihatye | ahaṁ hy u1gras taviṣas tuviṣmān viśvasya śatror anamaṁ vadhasnaiḥ ||
  • kva1 syā vo marutaḥ svadhāsīd: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the Maruts, the storm deities, asking where they are and expressing a desire for their presence. In real life, this can symbolize the need for support and guidance during turbulent times.
  • yan mām ekaṁ samadhattāhihatye: This part suggests a plea for unity and strength, indicating that the speaker seeks to be supported and protected. In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of community and teamwork, where individuals come together to support one another in challenging situations.
  • ahaṁ hy u1gras taviṣas tuviṣmān: Here, the speaker identifies themselves as powerful and capable, emphasizing their strength and resilience. This can be likened to individuals who, despite facing adversity, harness their inner strength to overcome obstacles, such as athletes training for a competition or individuals recovering from illness.
  • viśvasya śatror anamaṁ vadhasnaiḥ: This line speaks to the idea of overcoming enemies or challenges without harm. In a modern context, this can be seen in conflict resolution, where individuals or groups strive to resolve disputes peacefully, emphasizing dialogue and understanding rather than aggression.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of seeking divine support, the importance of unity, personal strength, and peaceful conflict resolution. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during crises, and personal resilience in the face of challenges. By embodying these values, individuals can navigate life's difficulties with grace and strength, fostering a harmonious environment both personally and collectively.
