युवां नराः पश्यमानास आप्यम् प्राच गव्यन्तः पृथुपर्शवो ययुः |दासाः च वृत्राः हतम् आर्याणि च सुदासम् इन्द्रावरुणावसावतम् ||
yuvāṁ narā paśyamānāsa āpyam prācā gavyantaḥ pṛthuparśavo yayuḥ | dāsā ca vṛtrā hatam āryāṇi ca sudāsam indrāvaruṇāvasāvatam ||
  • yuvāṁ narā - "You two, O men": This phrase addresses two individuals, possibly representing a pair of leaders or warriors. In real life, this can symbolize teamwork and collaboration, as seen in successful partnerships in business or sports.
  • paśyamānāsa - "who are observing": This suggests awareness and vigilance. In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of being observant in various situations, such as in leadership roles where awareness of team dynamics is crucial.
  • āpyam - "to attain": This word indicates a goal or aspiration. In everyday life, this can be likened to setting personal or professional goals, such as achieving a promotion or completing a marathon.
  • prācā - "the ancient": This term refers to traditions or wisdom passed down through generations. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the value placed on cultural heritage and the lessons learned from history.
  • gavyantaḥ - "those who are rich in cows": Cows are a symbol of wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture. In modern terms, this can represent the importance of resources and assets in achieving success, such as financial stability or a supportive community.
  • pṛthuparśavo - "those with broad shoulders": This phrase signifies strength and capability. In real life, this can relate to individuals who take on significant responsibilities, such as leaders in organizations or community activists.
  • yayuḥ - "who have moved": This indicates action and progress. In a practical sense, it can be associated with taking initiative in personal or professional endeavors, such as starting a new project or pursuing education.
  • dāsā - "the servants": This term can refer to those who serve or support others. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the importance of teamwork and collaboration in workplaces, where every role contributes to the overall success.
  • ca vṛtrā hatam - "and the Vritra has been slain": Vritra represents obstacles or challenges. This can be interpreted as overcoming difficulties in life, such as facing and conquering fears or adversities in personal or professional settings.
  • āryāṇi ca sudāsam - "and the noble Sudasa": Sudasa symbolizes righteousness and virtue. In real life, this can relate to the importance of ethical leadership and integrity in various fields, such as politics or business.
  • indrāvaruṇāvasāvatam - "Indra and Varuna have come to dwell": This signifies divine support and guidance. In contemporary life, this can be seen in the belief in seeking higher guidance or inspiration, whether through spirituality, mentorship, or community support.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of collaboration, awareness, strength, and overcoming challenges. It highlights the value of ethical leadership and the support of community and tradition in achieving goals. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in the workplace, community service, and personal development. The mantra serves as a reminder that success is often a collective effort, supported by wisdom and ethical values.
