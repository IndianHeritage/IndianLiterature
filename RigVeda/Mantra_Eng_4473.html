अग्निः हि विद्मना निदो देवो मर्तं उरुष्यति |सहावाः यस्यावृतो रयिः वाजेषु अवृतः ||
agnir hi vidmanā nido devo martam uruṣyati | sahāvā yasyāvṛto rayir vājeṣv avṛtaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • hi: Indeed, emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • vidmanā: We know or we understand.
    • nido: The abode or the home.
    • devo: The divine or the god.
    • martam: Mortal or human beings.
    • uruṣyati: To expand or to grow.
    • sahāvā: Together or in unity.
    • yasyāvṛto: Whose covering or protection.
    • rayir: Wealth or prosperity.
    • vājeṣv: In the context of strength or power.
    • avṛtaḥ: Uncovered or revealed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine nature of fire (Agnir) and its role in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the understanding of fire as a source of energy that nurtures and expands life.
    • The phrase "sahāvā yasyāvṛto rayir" suggests that when we come together in unity, we can uncover and access the wealth of resources and strength available to us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people unite for a common cause, such as environmental conservation, they can harness collective energy (like fire) to create significant change.
    • In personal development, understanding one's inner strength and potential can lead to growth and transformation, much like how fire transforms materials.
    • In business, collaboration among team members can lead to innovative ideas and solutions, revealing the wealth of creativity and resources that were previously hidden.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a metaphor for energy, transformation, and unity.
    • It teaches us that by understanding and harnessing our collective strengths, we can achieve greater prosperity and growth.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community efforts, personal growth, and teamwork, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and shared purpose.
