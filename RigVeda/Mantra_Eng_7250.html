तं घेम इत्त्था नमस्विन उप स्वराजम् आसते |अर्थं चिद अस्य सुधितं यद एतव āवर्तयन्ति दावने ||
taṁ ghem itthā namasvina upa svarājam āsate | arthaṁ cid asya sudhitaṁ yad etava āvartayanti dāvane ||
  • taṁ ghem itthā namasvina upa svarājam āsate: This phrase can be broken down into several components:
    • taṁ: Refers to "that" or "him," indicating a focus on a specific entity or concept.
    • ghem: This word can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a connection to abundance.
    • itthā: Means "thus" or "in this manner," indicating a way of being or existing.
    • namasvina: This term translates to "to the worthy" or "to those who are deserving," implying respect or reverence.
    • upa: Means "near" or "towards," suggesting a movement or approach.
    • svarājam: This can be interpreted as "the king of sound" or "the ruler of the heavens," which may refer to a divine or celestial authority.
    • āsate: Means "sits" or "resides," indicating a state of being or presence.
  • arthaṁ cid asya sudhitaṁ: This part can also be analyzed:
    • arthaṁ: Refers to "meaning" or "purpose," indicating the significance of something.
    • cid: Means "consciousness" or "awareness," suggesting a state of understanding.
    • asya: Translates to "of this," pointing to a specific context or subject.
    • sudhitaṁ: This can be interpreted as "well thought out" or "refined," indicating clarity and wisdom.
  • yad etava āvartayanti dāvane: The final part can be understood as:
    • yad: Means "that which," indicating a specific quality or action.
    • etava: Refers to "this" or "these," pointing to something immediate or present.
    • āvartayanti: Means "they turn" or "they revolve," suggesting a cyclical nature or process.
    • dāvane: This can be interpreted as "in the fire" or "in the blaze," which may symbolize transformation or purification.

Conclusion:

The mantra speaks to the reverence for a divine presence that embodies wealth and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of approaching this entity with respect. It highlights the significance of understanding the purpose and consciousness behind our actions, suggesting that wisdom and clarity are essential in our pursuits. The cyclical nature of existence is also acknowledged, indicating that transformation often occurs through challenges (symbolized by fire).

In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek clarity and purpose in their goals, approaching them with respect and mindfulness.
  • In business, it can serve as a reminder to honor ethical practices and the well-being of all stakeholders while pursuing prosperity.
  • In spiritual practices, it emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper meanings behind rituals and the transformative power of challenges.

Overall, this mantra serves as a guide for living a life of purpose, respect, and transformation, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom in their actions and to honor the divine presence in their pursuits.
