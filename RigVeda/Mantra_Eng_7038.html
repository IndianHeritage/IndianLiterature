यस्मै त्वं वसो दानाय शिक्षसि स रायस पोषम अश्नुते |तं त्वा वयम मगवन्न इन्द्र गिरवणः सुतावन्तो हवामहे ||
yasmai tvaṁ vaso dānāya śikṣasi sa rāyas poṣam aśnute | taṁ tvā vayam maghavann indra girvaṇaḥ sutāvanto havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmai: To whom
    • tvaṁ: You
    • vaso: Wealth
    • dānāya: For giving
    • śikṣasi: You teach
    • sa: He
    • rāyas: Prosperity
    • poṣam: Nourishment
    • aśnute: Enjoys
    • taṁ: Him
    • tvā: You
    • vayam: We
    • maghavann: O Mighty One
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the singers
    • sutāvanto: Those who have offerings
    • havāmahe: We invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the material world.
    • It suggests that by giving wealth and teaching others, one can attain prosperity and nourishment.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a call for divine support and blessings in achieving success and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea of sharing knowledge and resources. For instance, a successful entrepreneur may mentor others, thereby creating a cycle of prosperity.
    • In community service, individuals who donate their time and resources to help others often find that they themselves receive support and abundance in return.
    • In personal relationships, teaching and sharing with loved ones can lead to stronger bonds and mutual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of giving, teaching, and receiving.
    • It highlights the importance of generosity and the role of divine blessings in achieving success.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to contribute positively to their communities and relationships, fostering an environment of mutual support and prosperity.
