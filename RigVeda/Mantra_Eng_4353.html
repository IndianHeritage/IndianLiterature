कितवासो यद् रिरिपुर न दीवि यद् वा घा सत्यं उता यन् न विद्मा |सर्वा ता विष्य शिथिरेव देवाधा ते स्याम वरुण प्रियासः ||
kitavāso yad riripur na dīvi yad vā ghā satyam uta yan na vidma | sarvā tā vi ṣya śithireva devādhā te syāma varuṇa priyāsaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kitavāso: Those who are deceitful or dishonest.
    • yad: Whatever.
    • riripur: They may have taken away or stolen.
    • na: Not.
    • dīvi: In the heavens or in the divine realm.
    • yad vā: Or whatever.
    • ghā: To speak or to declare.
    • satyam: Truth.
    • uta: And.
    • yan: That which.
    • na vidma: We do not know.
    • sarvā: All.
    • tā: Those.
    • viṣya: In the world.
    • śithireva: As if they are stable or firm.
    • devādhā: O deities.
    • te: Your.
    • syāma: May we be.
    • varuṇa: O Varuna (the god of cosmic order).
    • priyāsaḥ: Beloved or dear ones.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a plea for clarity and truth in a world where deceit may prevail.
    • The speaker acknowledges the presence of dishonesty and seeks divine guidance to discern the truth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and the need for divine support in navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, this mantra can be applied when facing ethical dilemmas, urging individuals to seek truth and integrity in their decisions.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to communicate openly and honestly, fostering trust and understanding.
    • In societal contexts, it encourages individuals to challenge dishonesty and advocate for transparency in governance and community interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for truth and integrity amidst deception.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, reminding individuals to seek divine guidance in their pursuit of truth.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a commitment to honesty, fostering a more just and harmonious society.
