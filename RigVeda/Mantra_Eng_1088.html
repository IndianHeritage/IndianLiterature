जातवेदसे सुनवाम सोमम अरातीयतो न दहाति वेदः |स नः पर्वशति दुर्गाणि विश्वा नावेव सिन्धुं दुरितात्य अग्निः ||
jātavedase sunavāma somam arātīyato ni dahāti vedaḥ | sa naḥ parṣad ati durgāṇi viśvā nāveva sindhuṁ duritāty agniḥ ||
  • jātavedase: This term refers to the "one who is born of the knowledge" or "the one who knows all births." In a broader sense, it signifies the divine consciousness or the universal spirit that is aware of all creation.
  • sunavāma: This means "we call upon" or "we invoke." It indicates a plea or a prayer directed towards the divine for assistance or blessings.
  • somam: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.
  • arātīyato: This term can be interpreted as "from the enemies" or "from those who oppose us." It highlights the challenges and adversities one may face in life.
  • ni dahāti: This phrase means "does not burn" or "does not destroy." It suggests protection and preservation from harm.
  • vedaḥ: This refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," particularly the sacred knowledge contained in the Vedas, which guide human life and spirituality.
  • sa naḥ: This translates to "may he" or "let him." It is a call for divine intervention or support.
  • parṣad: This means "council" or "assembly." It signifies the gathering of divine forces or energies that can aid in overcoming obstacles.
  • ati durgāṇi: This phrase means "extremely difficult" or "very challenging." It acknowledges the presence of significant hurdles in life.
  • viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal." It emphasizes inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • nāveva: This means "like a boat." It symbolizes the journey of life and the need for guidance and support to navigate through challenges.
  • sindhuṁ: This refers to "the ocean" or "the vastness." It represents the depth of experiences and emotions one encounters in life.
  • duritāty: This means "from misfortunes" or "from difficulties." It highlights the desire to be liberated from negative circumstances.
  • agniḥ: This translates to "fire." In Vedic literature, fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence.

Conclusion: The mantra "jātavedase sunavāma somam arātīyato ni dahāti vedaḥ | sa naḥ parṣad ati durgāṇi viśvā nāveva sindhuṁ duritāty agniḥ ||" encapsulates a profound invocation for divine guidance and protection. It acknowledges the challenges of life while seeking the wisdom and strength to overcome them. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of difficulty, such as facing personal crises, health issues, or emotional turmoil. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and resilience, invoking the divine to assist them in navigating through the turbulent waters of life, much like a boat sailing through a vast ocean. The significance lies in its ability to connect the individual with the universal spirit, fostering a sense of unity and support in overcoming adversities.
