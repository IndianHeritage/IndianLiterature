भूषण न यो 'धि बभ्रूषु नम्नते वृषेवा पत्नीर अभ्य एति रोर्वत् |ओजायमानस् तन्वश च शुम्भते भीमो न श्रिङ्गा दविधावा दुर्गृभिः ||
bhūṣan na yo 'dhi babhrūṣu namnate vṛṣeva patnīr abhy eti roruvat | ojāyamānas tanvaś ca śumbhate bhīmo na śṛṅgā davidhāva durgṛbhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhūṣan: adornment or ornament
    • na: not
    • yo: who
    • 'dhi: indeed
    • babhrūṣu: in the brows or in the face
    • namnate: bows down or submits
    • vṛṣeva: like a bull
    • patnīr: wives or partners
    • abhy: towards
    • eti: goes or approaches
    • roruvat: crying or lamenting
    • ojāyamānas: becoming powerful or strong
    • tanvaś: body or form
    • ca: and
    • śumbhate: shines or is glorious
    • bhīmo: fearsome or formidable
    • na: not
    • śṛṅgā: horns
    • davidhāva: to be divided or separated
    • durgṛbhiḥ: from difficult situations or obstacles
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of a person who is adorned with virtues and strength.
    • The phrase "bhūṣan na yo 'dhi babhrūṣu" suggests that true adornment is not merely external but is reflected in one's character and actions.
    • The comparison to a bull ("vṛṣeva") indicates strength and resilience, emphasizing that a person should approach challenges with determination.
    • The mention of "roruvat" implies that even in moments of sorrow or difficulty, one must strive to become powerful and shine through adversity.
    • The concluding part highlights that a truly formidable person does not let obstacles divide them from their goals, symbolized by "na śṛṅgā davidhāva durgṛbhiḥ".
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, an employee who faces challenges with a positive attitude and resilience can inspire others, embodying the strength described in the mantra.
    • A leader who supports their team during tough times, while maintaining their integrity and strength, reflects the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, someone who overcomes personal loss or hardship and emerges stronger can be seen as living the principles of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength, resilience, and the true nature of adornment being one's character.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges are a part of life, and how we respond to them defines our true worth.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
