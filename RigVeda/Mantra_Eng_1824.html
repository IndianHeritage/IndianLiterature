प्रति प्र याहिंद्र मीळ्हुषो नृन महः पार्थिवे सदने यतस्व |अध यद एषाम पृथुबुध्नासा एतास तीरथे नारीः पौंस्यानि तस्थुः ||
prati pra yāhīndra mīḻhuṣo nṝn mahaḥ pārthive sadane yatasva | adha yad eṣām pṛthubudhnāsa etās tīrthe nāryaḥ pauṁsyāni tasthuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati pra yāhīndra mīḻhuṣo nṝn mahaḥ pārthive sadane yatasva | adha yad eṣām pṛthubudhnāsa etās tīrthe nāryaḥ pauṁsyāni tasthuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • pra: forth, out
    • yāhīndra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • mīḻhuṣo: of the great ones
    • nṝn: of men
    • mahaḥ: greatness
    • pārthive: earthly
    • sadane: in the abode
    • yatasva: strive, exert
    • adha: below
    • yad: that which
    • eṣām: of these
    • pṛthubudhnāsa: of those with vast understanding
    • etās: these
    • tīrthe: in the sacred places
    • nāryaḥ: women
    • pauṁsyāni: of men
    • tasthuḥ: may they stand
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the king of gods, asking for his blessings and support.
    • The phrase "prati pra yāhīndra" signifies a call to Indra to come forth and assist humanity in their endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the importance of striving for greatness and understanding in the earthly realm ("pārthive sadane").
    • The mention of "pṛthubudhnāsa" refers to those who possess vast knowledge and wisdom, highlighting the value of intellect and insight.
    • The mantra also acknowledges the role of women ("nāryaḥ") in society, suggesting that they too contribute to the strength and stability of the community.
    • By invoking the sacred places ("tīrthe"), it connects the spiritual with the physical, indicating that divine blessings can be sought in places of worship and reverence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be applied in educational settings where students strive for knowledge and wisdom, much like the "pṛthubudhnāsa."
    • In workplaces, the call to Indra can be seen as a motivation for teamwork and collaboration, where individuals work together towards a common goal.
    • Community initiatives that empower women reflect the essence of this mantra, recognizing their vital role in societal development.
    • Spiritual gatherings in sacred places can serve as a reminder of the connection between the divine and the earthly, encouraging individuals to seek guidance and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in human endeavors, emphasizing the need for wisdom, collaboration, and the recognition of all members of society.
    • It serves as a reminder that striving for greatness and understanding is essential in both personal and communal contexts.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, individuals and communities can find strength and guidance in their pursuits, fostering a harmonious and prosperous existence.
