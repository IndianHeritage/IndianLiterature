यो रक्षांसी निजूर्वति वृषा शुक्रेण शोचिषा | स नः पार्षद अतिद्विषः ||
yo rakṣāṁsi nijūrvati vṛṣā śukreṇa śociṣā | sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • rakṣāṁsi: the protectors
    • nijūrvati: who are engaged in their duties
    • vṛṣā: with strength
    • śukreṇa: with purity
    • śociṣā: with brightness
    • sa: he
    • naḥ: our
    • parṣad: assembly or group
    • ati: beyond
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine protector who is engaged in their duties with strength, purity, and brightness.
    • The phrase "who are engaged in their duties" emphasizes the importance of responsibility and commitment in one's role.
    • The mantra calls upon this protector to assist our assembly in overcoming adversaries or challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a manager who leads with integrity and clarity can be seen as a protector of their team, ensuring that everyone is supported and guided.
    • In a community, leaders who work selflessly for the welfare of others embody the qualities of strength and purity, helping to unite people against common challenges.
    • In personal life, a parent or guardian who provides a safe and nurturing environment for their children acts as a protector, fostering growth and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of having protectors in our lives who embody strength, purity, and brightness.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek out and appreciate those who support us in overcoming challenges and adversities.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or communities, the qualities of responsibility and commitment are essential for fostering a supportive environment.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to embody these qualities ourselves, becoming protectors for others in our lives.
