मन्त्र: उता स्म ते वनस्पते वातो वि वात्य अग्रं इति |अथो इन्द्राय पातवे सुनु सोमं उलूखल ||
uta sma te vanaspate vāto vi vāty agram it | atho indrāya pātave sunu somam ulūkhala ||
  • Mantra: "uta sma te vanaspate vāto vi vāty agram it | atho indrāya pātave sunu somam ulūkhala ||"
  • Translation: "O Vanaspati (Lord of Vegetation), may the wind blow favorably towards you. O Indra, we offer you the soma (sacred drink) that is prepared in the mortar."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Uta: A term of invocation, often used to express reverence.
    • Sma: Indicates a sense of presence or existence.
    • Te: Means "your," indicating possession.
    • Vanaspate: Refers to the Lord of Vegetation, symbolizing nature and its abundance.
    • Vāto: Means "wind," representing the vital force and energy in nature.
    • Indrāya: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, associated with strength and power.
    • Pātave: Means "to the one who is powerful," indicating the offering to Indra.
    • Sunu: Means "listen" or "hear," a call to the divine to accept the offering.
    • Somam: Refers to soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Ulūkhala: Refers to the mortar used for preparing soma, symbolizing the process of transformation.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the elements, and the divine.
    • It highlights the importance of offering and gratitude towards the forces that sustain life.
    • The invocation of Vanaspati and Indra signifies the reverence for nature and the divine powers that govern it.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire environmental consciousness, encouraging individuals to respect and protect nature.
    • It can be applied in rituals or practices that promote sustainability and harmony with the environment.
    • The act of offering soma can be seen as a metaphor for sharing resources and fostering community well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nature and the divine in our lives.
    • It encourages us to cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the environment.
    • By acknowledging the forces that sustain us, we can foster a deeper connection with the world around us.
