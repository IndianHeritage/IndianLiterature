मनTRA: नृणामु त्वा नृतमं गीरभिरुक्तैः अभि प्र वीरम् अर्चता सवादः |सं सहसे पुरुमायो जिहीते नमो अस्य प्रदिव एक ईशे ||
nṛṇām u tvā nṛtamaṁ gīrbhir ukthair abhi pra vīram arcatā sabādhaḥ | saṁ sahase purumāyo jihīte namo asya pradiva eka īśe ||
  • nṛṇām: This word translates to "of men" or "of humans." It signifies the collective human experience and the importance of community.
  • u: This is a conjunction meaning "and," indicating a connection or addition.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to a divine entity or a higher power that is being addressed.
  • nṛtamaṁ: This translates to "the best among men," suggesting an ideal or exemplary figure, possibly a leader or a deity.
  • gīrbhir: This means "by hymns" or "by songs," indicating the power of words and expressions in worship and communication.
  • ukthair: This translates to "with utterances" or "with sounds," emphasizing the importance of vocal expression in rituals.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "for," indicating direction or purpose in the context of the mantra.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "out," suggesting a sense of movement or action.
  • vīram: This translates to "hero" or "brave one," referring to a person of valor or strength.
  • arcatā: This means "worshipped" or "adored," indicating the act of reverence towards the divine.
  • sabādhaḥ: This translates to "with all sounds" or "with all utterances," suggesting a comprehensive approach to worship.
  • saṁ: This means "together" or "with," indicating unity or collective action.
  • sahase: This translates to "with courage" or "with strength," emphasizing the importance of bravery in human endeavors.
  • purumāyo: This means "the ancient" or "the primordial," referring to timeless wisdom or the eternal nature of existence.
  • jihīte: This translates to "may he be pleased" or "may he accept," indicating a desire for divine favor.
  • namo: This means "salutation" or "obeisance," a respectful acknowledgment of the divine.
  • asya: This translates to "of this" or "of him," referring to the subject of the worship.
  • pradiva: This means "to the sky" or "to the heavens," indicating a connection to the divine or the cosmos.
  • eka: This translates to "one," emphasizing unity and singularity in purpose.
  • īśe: This means "to the lord" or "to the master," referring to a higher power or deity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, acknowledging the strength and valor of humanity while seeking divine favor and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of collective worship and the power of words in connecting with the divine. The invocation of courage and the acknowledgment of a higher power reflect a deep understanding of the human condition and the need for support from the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common cause, invoking the spirit of unity and strength can help foster collaboration and support.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from the idea of being the "best among men," striving to embody courage and valor in their actions.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The importance of hymns and utterances in worship can be seen in various cultural and religious ceremonies, where collective prayers are offered for peace and prosperity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine. It highlights the significance of courage, unity, and reverence in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek strength from one another and from a higher power. By embodying these principles, we can navigate the challenges of life with grace and purpose.
