आ यन् नः पत्नीर गमन्त्य अच्छा त्वष्टा सुपाणिर दधातु वीरान् ||
ā yan naḥ patnīr gamanty acchā tvaṣṭā supāṇir dadhātu vīrān ||
  • Mantra: "ā yan naḥ patnīr gamanty acchā tvaṣṭā supāṇir dadhātu vīrān"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from, out of
    • yan: that which
    • naḥ: our
    • patnīr: wives or partners
    • gamanty: may go or move
    • acchā: good, auspicious
    • tvaṣṭā: the creator or divine craftsman
    • supāṇir: with good hands or skillful
    • dadhātu: may grant or bestow
    • vīrān: heroes or strong ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the well-being and auspiciousness of our partners or spouses.
    • It invokes the divine craftsman, Tvaṣṭā, to bless us with strength and valor.
    • The phrase "may our wives go" suggests a desire for their safe and prosperous journey in life.
    • The emphasis on "good hands" indicates a request for skill and capability in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a marriage, this mantra can be recited during a wedding ceremony to invoke blessings for the couple's journey together.
    • In a family setting, it can be used to pray for the safety and success of family members as they pursue their goals.
    • In a professional context, it can be a reminder to support colleagues and partners in their endeavors, fostering a collaborative environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of relationships and the need for divine support in our personal and professional lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish our partners and to seek strength and skill in our actions.
    • By invoking blessings for ourselves and our loved ones, we create a positive and supportive atmosphere that can lead to success and fulfillment.
