यं त्वा देवापिः शुशुचानो अग्न आर्ष्टिषेणो मनुष्यः समीदहे |विश्वेभिर देवैर अनुमद्यमानः प्र पर्जन्यम ईरयावृष्टिमन्तम् ||
yaṁ tvā devāpiḥ śuśucāno agna ārṣṭiṣeṇo manuṣyaḥ samīdhe | viśvebhir devair anumadyamānaḥ pra parjanyam īrayā vṛṣṭimantam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • devāpiḥ: the gods
    • śuśucāno: who are pleased
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ārṣṭiṣeṇo: with the strength
    • manuṣyaḥ: the human
    • samīdhe: we offer
    • viśvebhir: with all
    • devair: the gods
    • anumadyamānaḥ: being pleased
    • pra: for
    • parjanyam: the rain
    • īrayā: with the strength
    • vṛṣṭimantam: who brings rain
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings and the blessings of all the gods.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine favor in human endeavors, particularly in relation to agriculture and sustenance.
    • The invocation of rain signifies the need for natural resources and the sustenance they provide.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking the gods for rain is a common practice, as rain is essential for crop growth.
    • Modern farmers may still perform rituals or prayers for favorable weather conditions, reflecting the same dependence on divine favor.
    • In times of drought, communities often come together to pray for rain, showcasing the collective belief in the power of divine intervention.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of humans and the divine, emphasizing the need for spiritual support in achieving material goals.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and respect towards nature and the forces that sustain life.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony with nature and recognize the significance of collective efforts in addressing environmental challenges.
