स विश्वा दाशुषे वसु सोमो दिव्यानी पार्थिवा |पवताम आन्तरिक्ष्याः ||
sa viśvā dāśuṣe vasu somo divyāni pārthivā | pavatām āntarikṣyā ||
  • sa viśvā dāśuṣe: This phrase can be translated as "all that is universal, for the one who gives." Here, "sa" means "that," "viśvā" refers to "all" or "universal," and "dāśuṣe" means "for the giver" or "to the one who bestows." This emphasizes the importance of generosity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vasu somo: "Vasu" translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," while "somo" refers to "the moon" or "soma," which is often associated with nourishment and immortality. Together, this can signify the abundance and prosperity that comes from divine blessings.
  • divyāni pārthivā: "Divyāni" means "divine" or "heavenly," and "pārthivā" means "earthly." This phrase suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly realms, indicating that the blessings and wealth we receive are both spiritual and material in nature.
  • pavatām āntarikṣyā: "Pavatām" means "of the purifiers" or "those who cleanse," and "āntarikṣyā" refers to "the atmosphere" or "the space between." This can be interpreted as a call for purification and clarity in our lives, both physically and spiritually, as we navigate the space around us.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa viśvā dāśuṣe vasu somo divyāni pārthivā | pavatām āntarikṣyā ||" encapsulates the essence of universal generosity, the blessings of prosperity, and the connection between the divine and earthly realms. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in acts of charity, community service, and personal growth. By embodying the principles of giving and seeking both spiritual and material wealth, individuals can create a harmonious balance in their lives. Furthermore, the emphasis on purification encourages us to cleanse our thoughts and actions, fostering a positive environment for ourselves and those around us. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing both our inner and outer worlds.
