आ च वहासि तां इह देवां उप प्रशस्तये |हव्यā सुचन्द्र वीतये ||
ā ca vahāsi tām̐ iha devām̐ upa praśastaye | havyā suścandra vītaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā ca vahāsi: "You carry forth" or "You bring forth."
    • tām̐: "that" or "those."
    • iha: "here" or "in this place."
    • devām̐: "gods" or "divine beings."
    • upa: "near" or "towards."
    • praśastaye: "for praise" or "for commendation."
    • havyā: "offering" or "sacred oblation."
    • suścandra: "well-shining" or "brilliant."
    • vītaye: "for the purpose of liberation" or "for the purpose of release."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a call to the divine, inviting the gods to come forth and be present in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and praises directed towards the divine, suggesting that such acts can lead to spiritual liberation and enlightenment.
    • The mention of "well-shining" indicates the purity and brilliance of the offerings made, which are essential for attracting divine attention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, one might recite this mantra during a prayer or meditation session, seeking guidance and blessings from the divine.
    • In community rituals, such as festivals or ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to invoke the presence of deities, enhancing the spiritual atmosphere.
    • In daily life, the essence of this mantra can be applied by performing acts of kindness and service, which can be seen as offerings to the divine in others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine, fostering a sense of community and spirituality.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of offering praise and gratitude, which can lead to personal growth and liberation.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and to recognize the sacredness in their actions and offerings.
