उप सर्प मातरं भूमिम एताम उर्व्यचसम पृथिवीं सुशेवाम् |ऊर्णम्रदा युवतिर दक्षिणावत एषा त्वां पातु निरृतेर उपस्थात् ||
upa sarpa mātaram bhūmim etām uruvyacasam pṛthivīṁ suśevām | ūrṇamradā yuvatir dakṣiṇāvata eṣā tvā pātu nirṛter upasthāt ||
  • upa sarpa mātaram bhūmim etām uruvyacasam pṛthivīṁ suśevām
    • upa sarpa: This phrase can be interpreted as "approaching" or "coming close." It signifies the act of seeking or moving towards something sacred or important.
    • mātaram bhūmim: "Mātaram" means "mother," and "bhūmim" refers to "earth." Together, this phrase acknowledges the Earth as our mother, emphasizing the nurturing and life-giving aspects of nature.
    • etām uruvyacasam: "etām" means "this," and "uruvyacasam" can be understood as "of vast expanse" or "broad." This highlights the vastness and richness of the Earth.
    • pṛthivīṁ suśevām: "pṛthivīṁ" refers to "the Earth," and "suśevām" means "well-nourished" or "prosperous." This indicates the abundance and fertility of the Earth, which sustains life.
  • ūrṇamradā yuvatir dakṣiṇāvata
    • ūrṇamradā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings prosperity" or "the giver of wealth." It signifies a source of abundance and well-being.
    • yuvatir: This word means "young woman" or "youthful." It symbolizes vitality, energy, and the potential for growth.
    • dakṣiṇāvata: This term refers to "from the south" or "southern direction." In many cultures, the south is associated with warmth, fertility, and prosperity.
  • eṣā tvā pātu nirṛter upasthāt
    • eṣā: This means "this" or "this one," referring to the Earth or the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • tvā pātu: "tvā" means "you," and "pātu" means "may protect." This phrase expresses a prayer for protection and blessings.
    • nirṛter upasthāt: "nirṛter" refers to "from misfortune" or "evil," and "upasthāt" means "may come near." This indicates a desire for the Earth to protect us from negativity and harm.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the relationship between humanity and the Earth, portraying the Earth as a nurturing mother that provides abundance and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment and recognize its significance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices of environmental conservation, sustainable living, and fostering a sense of gratitude towards nature. By acknowledging the Earth as our mother, we cultivate respect and a deeper connection to the natural world, which is essential for our survival and well-being.
