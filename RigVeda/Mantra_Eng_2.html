अग्निः पूर्वेभिर ऋषिभिर ईड्यो नूतनैर उता |स देवाम् एष वक्षति ||
agniḥ pūrvebhir ṛṣibhir īḍyo nūtanair uta | sa devām̐ eha vakṣati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agniḥ: Fire
    • Pūrvebhir: By the ancients
    • Ṛṣibhir: By the sages
    • Īḍyo: Worthy of worship
    • Nūtanair: By the new ones
    • Uta: Also
    • Sa: He
    • Devām̐: The divine
    • Eha: Here
    • Vakṣati: Speaks or reveals
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is revered by both ancient sages and new seekers.
    • It emphasizes the continuity of worship and the importance of fire as a divine presence that communicates with the devotees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the Agni is central to ceremonies like weddings, where it symbolizes the divine witness to the vows taken by the couple.
    • In modern contexts, fire can represent knowledge and enlightenment, akin to the phrase "the light of knowledge." Just as Agni is worshipped for its transformative power, education and learning are revered in society for their ability to change lives.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of tradition and the passing down of wisdom from one generation to the next.
    • It highlights the role of fire not just as a physical element but as a metaphor for knowledge, spirituality, and the divine connection.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom from both ancient texts and contemporary sources, fostering a holistic understanding of life.
