समिद्धस्य श्रयमानः पुरस्ताद् ब्रह्म वन्बानो अजरोऽसुवीरम् |आरे अस्मद् अमतिम बादमान उच् श्रयस्व महते सौभगाय ||
samiddhasya śrayamāṇaḥ purastād brahma vanvāno ajaraṁ suvīram | āre asmad amatim bādhamāna uc chrayasva mahate saubhagāya ||
  • samiddhasya: This term refers to something that is kindled or ignited. In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the awakening of knowledge or consciousness.
  • śrayamāṇaḥ: This means 'taking refuge' or 'seeking shelter.' It signifies the act of finding support or guidance in a higher power or wisdom.
  • purastād: Translated as 'in front' or 'before,' this word indicates the presence of something significant that leads or guides the way.
  • brahma: This term refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. It represents the source of all creation and the essence of existence.
  • vanvāno: Meaning 'seeking' or 'desiring,' it implies an active pursuit of something valuable or essential.
  • ajaraṁ: This word means 'immortal' or 'eternal,' suggesting a state of being that transcends the physical limitations of life.
  • suvīram: Translated as 'good hero' or 'valiant one,' it refers to a person of great strength and virtue, embodying qualities that are admirable and inspiring.
  • āre: This is an invocation, often used to call upon a deity or higher power for assistance or guidance.
  • asmad: Meaning 'our' or 'us,' it signifies a collective identity or community.
  • amatim: This term refers to 'intellect' or 'understanding,' highlighting the importance of wisdom in navigating life.
  • bādhamāna: This means 'overcoming' or 'conquering,' suggesting the ability to rise above challenges or obstacles.
  • uc: Translated as 'up' or 'higher,' it indicates an elevation or ascension towards something greater.
  • shrayasva: This is a call to 'take shelter' or 'seek refuge,' reinforcing the theme of finding support in higher wisdom.
  • mahate: Meaning 'great' or 'mighty,' it emphasizes the significance of the power or force being invoked.
  • saubhagāya: This term translates to 'for good fortune' or 'prosperity,' indicating the ultimate goal of seeking blessings for well-being and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual message about seeking refuge in the ultimate reality (Brahma) while pursuing wisdom and strength. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges through collective understanding and support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during times of personal struggle, when seeking guidance in decision-making, or when striving for success in endeavors. By invoking the qualities of wisdom, strength, and community, individuals can navigate life's complexities and work towards achieving their goals with a sense of purpose and resilience.
