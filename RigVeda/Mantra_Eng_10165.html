तवे क्रतुम् अपि वृञ्जन्ति विश्वे द्विर् यद् एते त्रिर् भवन्ति ऊमाः |स्वादोः स्वादीयः स्वादुनā सृजा सम अदः सु मधु मधुनाभि योद्धीः ||
tve kratum api vṛñjanti viśve dvir yad ete trir bhavanty ūmāḥ | svādoḥ svādīyaḥ svādunā sṛjā sam adaḥ su madhu madhunābhi yodhīḥ ||
  • tve kratum api vṛñjanti viśve dvir yad ete trir bhavanty ūmāḥ
    • tve - This word refers to "you," indicating a divine presence or a higher power.
    • kratum - This translates to "intellect" or "will," suggesting the importance of mental strength and determination.
    • api - Meaning "also," it emphasizes inclusivity in the context of the mantra.
    • vṛñjanti - This means "to choose" or "to select," indicating the act of making choices based on wisdom.
    • viśve - This translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting a collective aspect.
    • dvir - Meaning "twice," it implies repetition or duality in experiences.
    • yad - This means "that" or "which," referring to the subject of the mantra.
    • ete - This translates to "these," indicating specific entities or beings.
    • trir - Meaning "thrice," it emphasizes the significance of threefold actions or occurrences.
    • bhavanty - This means "become," indicating transformation or manifestation.
    • ūmāḥ - This refers to "the auspicious," suggesting a positive outcome or blessing.
  • svādoḥ svādīyaḥ svādunā sṛjā sam adaḥ su madhu madhunābhi yodhīḥ
    • svādoḥ - This translates to "of sweetness," indicating the pleasantness of experiences.
    • svādīyaḥ - Meaning "delicious," it emphasizes the enjoyment derived from life.
    • svādunā - This means "by sweetness," suggesting that positive experiences are created through joy.
    • sṛjā - This translates to "to create," indicating the act of bringing forth new experiences or ideas.
    • sam - Meaning "together," it emphasizes unity in creation.
    • adaḥ - This means "that," referring to the outcomes of creation.
    • su - This translates to "good," indicating positive results.
    • madhu - Meaning "honey," it symbolizes sweetness and richness in life.
    • madhunābhi - This means "with honey," suggesting that life should be enjoyed with sweetness.
    • yodhīḥ - This translates to "warriors," indicating strength and courage in facing life's challenges.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of intellect, choice, and the sweetness of life. It suggests that by making wise decisions and embracing positivity, one can transform their experiences into something auspicious and fulfilling. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Making informed decisions in personal and professional life, where intellect and willpower play a crucial role.
  • Embracing positivity and joy in daily activities, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • Working collaboratively with others to create a harmonious environment, reflecting the unity in creation.
  • Facing challenges with courage and strength, akin to warriors, to overcome obstacles and achieve success.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength, make wise choices, and savor the sweetness of life, leading to a more enriched existence.
