त्वां अग्न आदित्यास आस्यं त्वां जिव्हां शुचयश चक्रिरे कवे |त्वां रातिषाचो अध्वरेषु सश्चिरे त्वे देवाः हविः अदन्त्य आहुतम् ||
tvām agna ādityāsa āsya1ṁ tvāṁ jihvāṁ śucayaś cakrire kave | tvāṁ rātiṣāco adhvareṣu saścire tve devā havir adanty āhutam ||
  • tvām agna ādityāsa āsyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, you are the one who is invoked by the Adityas." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, a crucial element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • tvāṁ jihvāṁ śucayaś cakrire kave: This translates to "The poets have made you their tongue, pure and sacred." This indicates that Agni is not only a deity but also a source of inspiration for poets and sages, emphasizing the importance of communication and expression in spiritual practices.
  • tvāṁ rātiṣāco adhvareṣu saścire: This means "You are the one who is invoked in the rituals." It highlights Agni's role in Vedic sacrifices, where he acts as a mediator between humans and the divine, carrying offerings to the gods.
  • tve devā havir adanty āhutam: This translates to "The gods have accepted your offerings." This signifies the acceptance of the offerings made to Agni, reinforcing the idea that rituals and sacrifices are essential for maintaining harmony with the divine.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, communication, and the connection between humans and the divine.
  • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to the practice of mindfulness and intention in our actions, much like how Agni is invoked with purity and purpose in rituals.
  • It serves as a reminder of the importance of our words and actions, encouraging us to communicate with sincerity and to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth and connection.
  • Overall, this mantra highlights the role of divine forces in our lives and the importance of rituals in maintaining a harmonious relationship with the universe.
