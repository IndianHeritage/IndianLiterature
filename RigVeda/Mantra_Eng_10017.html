प्रास्तौद ऋष्वौजाऽ ऋष्वेभिः तातक्ष शूरः शवसाः |ऋभुर न क्रतुभिर मातरिश्वा ||
prāstaud ṛṣvaujā ṛṣvebhis tatakṣa śūraḥ śavasā | ṛbhur na kratubhir mātariśvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāstaud: He who has risen or ascended.
    • ṛṣvaujā: With the strength of the sage.
    • ṛṣvebhis: By the sages.
    • tatakṣa: He has conquered or achieved.
    • śūraḥ: The brave or the hero.
    • śavasā: With the strength of the body.
    • ṛbhur: The one who is strong or powerful.
    • na: Not.
    • kratubhir: By the sacrifices or efforts.
    • mātariśvā: The one who is the wind or the spirit.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful figure who has risen with the strength of sages and has achieved great feats.
    • The term "śūraḥ" emphasizes bravery, suggesting that true strength comes from courage and valor.
    • The phrase "mātariśvā" refers to the wind, symbolizing freedom and the spirit that drives one to achieve their goals.
    • It highlights that true power is not just physical but also comes from wisdom and the collective strength of the community (the sages).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to leaders who rise to power through wisdom and bravery, such as Mahatma Gandhi, who led India to independence through non-violent means.
    • In sports, athletes who overcome challenges and rise to the top, like Serena Williams, exemplify the spirit of "śūraḥ" through their determination and strength.
    • In everyday life, individuals who face personal struggles and emerge stronger, such as those overcoming health issues or personal loss, embody the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner strength, wisdom, and bravery in achieving one's goals.
    • It teaches that true power comes from a combination of physical strength and the wisdom of the community.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to rise above challenges, drawing on both personal courage and the support of others to achieve greatness.
