भूरिं द्वे अचरन्ती चरन्तं पद्वन्तं गर्भं अपदी दधाते |नित्यं न सूनुम पितरोर उपस्थे dyावā रक्षतं पृथिवी नो अभ्वात ||
bhūriṁ dve acarantī carantam padvantaṁ garbham apadī dadhāte | nityaṁ na sūnum pitror upasthe dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||
  • bhūriṁ: This word translates to "abundance" or "plenty." It signifies the richness of life and the universe.
  • dve: Meaning "two," it often represents duality in existence, such as the balance between opposites.
  • acarantī: This term means "those who move" or "those who act." It refers to the dynamic nature of life and the universe.
  • carantam: Similar to "acarantī," it emphasizes the act of moving or progressing.
  • padvantaṁ: This word means "the one who is walking" or "the one who is progressing." It symbolizes the journey of life.
  • garbham: Translated as "womb" or "embryo," it signifies creation, potential, and the beginning of life.
  • apadī: This term means "without obstacles" or "unhindered," indicating a smooth path in life.
  • dadhāte: Meaning "they hold" or "they support," it refers to the nurturing aspect of existence.
  • nityaṁ: This word translates to "eternal" or "perpetual," emphasizing the timeless nature of certain truths.
  • na: Meaning "not," it negates the following term.
  • sūnum: This term means "son" or "offspring," representing lineage and continuity.
  • pitror: Translated as "of the parents," it signifies the connection to ancestry and heritage.
  • upasthe: This word means "in the lap" or "in the presence," indicating closeness and protection.
  • dyāvā: Meaning "heaven," it represents the spiritual or divine aspect of existence.
  • rakṣatam: This term means "may protect" or "may safeguard," indicating a plea for protection.
  • pṛthivī: Translated as "earth," it signifies the physical world and our connection to it.
  • no: Meaning "our," it indicates possession or belonging.
  • abhvāt: This term means "may not perish" or "may not be lost," emphasizing the desire for continuity and preservation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, creation, and the interconnectedness of existence. It highlights the importance of abundance, the journey of life, and the nurturing aspects of both the earth and the heavens. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as family, community, and the environment. It serves as a reminder to cherish our roots, protect our lineage, and seek harmony between the spiritual and physical realms. The plea for protection of both the earth and our lineage emphasizes the significance of sustainability and the preservation of our heritage for future generations.
