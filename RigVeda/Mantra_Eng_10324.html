इन्द्रवायू बृहस्पतिं सुहवेह हवामहे |यथा नः सर्व इज जनः संगत्यां सुमना असत ||
indravāyū bṛhaspatiṁ suhaveha havāmahe | yathā naḥ sarva ij janaḥ saṁgatyāṁ sumanā asat ||
  • Indravāyū: This term refers to the combined forces of Indra, the king of gods, and Vayu, the god of wind. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the power of collaboration and teamwork, where different strengths come together to achieve a common goal.
  • Bṛhaspatiṁ: Bṛhaspati is the guru of the gods and represents wisdom and knowledge. This can be related to the importance of seeking guidance from mentors or wise individuals in our lives, such as teachers or experienced professionals, to navigate challenges effectively.
  • Suhaveha: This word can be interpreted as "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking blessings or support from divine forces. In everyday life, this can be likened to the practice of prayer or meditation, where individuals seek peace and guidance.
  • Havāmahe: This term means "we offer" or "we present." It emphasizes the importance of giving back, whether through acts of kindness, charity, or community service. For instance, volunteering at local shelters or participating in community clean-up drives can be seen as modern-day interpretations of this offering.
  • Yathā naḥ: This phrase translates to "as we." It suggests a collective approach, emphasizing unity and shared purpose. In a real-life scenario, this can be observed in team projects or community initiatives where collaboration leads to success.
  • Sarva ij janaḥ: This means "all beings" or "everyone." It highlights inclusivity and the importance of considering the well-being of all individuals in our actions. For example, in workplace settings, fostering an inclusive environment where every voice is heard can lead to better outcomes.
  • Saṁgatyāṁ: This term refers to "togetherness" or "unity." It underscores the significance of harmony and cooperation in achieving goals. Real-life examples include team sports, where players must work together to win, or community gatherings that strengthen social bonds.
  • Sumanā: This word means "good thoughts" or "positive intentions." It emphasizes the power of mindset and the impact of positive thinking on our lives. Practicing gratitude or maintaining a positive outlook can significantly influence our mental health and relationships.
  • Asat: This term translates to "not" or "without." It suggests the idea of overcoming negativity or obstacles. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development, where individuals strive to eliminate negative habits or thoughts to achieve their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of collaboration, wisdom, and positive intention. It serves as a reminder of the importance of unity and collective effort in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community initiatives, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more harmonious and fulfilling existence. By invoking the support of higher powers and fostering good thoughts, we can navigate challenges and contribute positively to the world around us.
