त्रिर् नो अश्विनाऽ यजतā दिवे-दिवे परि त्रिधातु पृथिवīm अशायतम् |तिस्रो नासत्यā रथ्या परावत आत्मेव वातः स्वसराणि गच्छताम् ||
trir no aśvinā yajatā dive-dive pari tridhātu pṛthivīm aśāyatam | tisro nāsatyā rathyā parāvata ātmeva vātaḥ svasarāṇi gacchatam ||
  • Mantra: "trir no aśvinā yajatā dive-dive pari tridhātu pṛthivīm aśāyatam | tisro nāsatyā rathyā parāvata ātmeva vātaḥ svasarāṇi gacchatam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trir: three times
    • no: for us
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen)
    • yajatā: we worship
    • dive-dive: day by day
    • pari: all around
    • tridhātu: in three realms (heaven, earth, and the atmosphere)
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • aśāyatam: may you bestow
    • tisro: three
    • nāsatyā: O Nāsatyas (another name for the Ashvins)
    • rathyā: chariots
    • parāvata: may they go forth
    • ātmeva: as if by themselves
    • vātaḥ: wind
    • svasarāṇi: the sisters (or the various forms)
    • gacchatam: may they go
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The phrase "trir no aśvinā" indicates a request for blessings from the Ashvins three times, emphasizing the importance of their presence in our lives.
    • "Dive-dive" suggests a daily invocation, highlighting the need for constant connection with the divine for guidance and support.
    • The mention of "tridhātu" refers to the three realms of existence: the physical world, the spiritual realm, and the atmosphere, indicating a holistic approach to life.
    • The mantra also speaks of the "nāsatyā" and their chariots, symbolizing the swift and effective delivery of blessings and solutions to our problems.
    • Finally, the invocation of "vātaḥ svasarāṇi gacchatam" suggests a desire for the winds (or energies) to carry forth these blessings, indicating a trust in the natural flow of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one might recite this mantra during morning rituals to seek health and well-being for themselves and their family.
    • In times of uncertainty or challenges, invoking the Ashvins can serve as a reminder to trust in the process and seek divine guidance.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, this mantra can be used to bless the occasion and ensure a positive outcome.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in our daily lives and the interconnectedness of all realms of existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek blessings regularly and to trust in the natural flow of life, allowing energies to guide us towards our goals.
    • By incorporating this mantra into our routines, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and enhance our overall well-being.
