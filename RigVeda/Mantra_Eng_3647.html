अग्निर् होतā न्य असीदद् यजीयान् उपस्थे मातुः सुरभा उ लोके |युवā कविः पुरुनिष्ठ ऋतावā धार्ता कृष्तीनाम् उ त मध्य इद्धः ||
agnir hotā ny asīdad yajīyān upasthe mātuḥ surabhā u loke | yuvā kaviḥ puruniṣṭha ṛtāvā dhartā kṛṣṭīnām uta madhya iddhaḥ ||
  • Agnir hotā ny asīdad: This phrase translates to "Agni, the priest, has come forth." Agni, the fire god, is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a leader or a guide who facilitates communication and understanding among people.
  • yajīyān upasthe mātuḥ surabhā u loke: This means "the one who is worthy of worship, who is present in the homes of the mothers." This highlights the importance of reverence and respect for the nurturing aspects of life, akin to how we honor our parents and caregivers in our daily lives.
  • yuvā kaviḥ puruniṣṭha ṛtāvā: This translates to "the young sage, the one who is established in truth." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and truthfulness, which can be seen in leaders or mentors who guide others with integrity and knowledge.
  • dhartā kṛṣṭīnām uta madhya iddhaḥ: This means "the one who supports the crops and is in the midst of them." This can be related to the importance of agriculture and sustenance in our lives, reflecting how we depend on nature and the efforts of farmers for our survival.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Agni as a divine force that connects the earthly and the spiritual realms. It signifies the importance of leadership, reverence for nurturing figures, the pursuit of truth, and the sustenance provided by nature. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, community leadership, and environmental stewardship. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster harmony and growth in their personal and communal lives.
