प्र यत सिन्धवः प्रसवं यथायन्न आपः समुद्रं रथ्येव जग्मुः |अतश्चिद इन्द्रः सदसो वरीयान यद ईं सोमः पृणति दुग्धो अंशुः ||
pra yat sindhavaḥ prasavaṁ yathāyann āpaḥ samudraṁ rathyeva jagmuḥ | ataś cid indraḥ sadaso varīyān yad īṁ somaḥ pṛṇati dugdho aṁśuḥ ||
  • Translation: "As the rivers flow towards the ocean, so do the waters of the heavens, Indra, the most powerful, bestow upon us the strength of the soma, like the milk from the cow."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pra yat: As (the action of flowing or moving towards)
    • sindhavaḥ: Rivers (symbolizing the flow of life and energy)
    • prasavaṁ: Birth or creation (the act of bringing forth)
    • yathāyann: As it is (in its natural state)
    • āpaḥ: Waters (representing nourishment and sustenance)
    • samudraṁ: Ocean (the ultimate destination or source)
    • rathyeva: Like a chariot (symbolizing movement and progress)
    • jagmuḥ: They go (indicating the journey or flow)
    • ataś: Therefore (indicating a conclusion or result)
    • cid: Indeed (emphasizing certainty)
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods, representing power and strength)
    • sadaso: Of the assembly (the gathering of energies or forces)
    • varīyān: The most powerful (indicating superiority)
    • yad: That which (referring to the source of strength)
    • īṁ: The (indicating a specific quality)
    • somaḥ: Soma (a sacred drink symbolizing immortality and divine energy)
    • pṛṇati: Fills or nourishes (the act of providing sustenance)
    • dugdho: Milk (representing purity and nourishment)
    • aṁśuḥ: Rays or light (symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge)
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The rivers symbolize the various paths we take in life, all leading to a common goal, much like how individuals pursue different careers or passions that ultimately contribute to the greater good of society.
    • Indra represents the strength and support we seek in our lives, akin to the guidance we receive from mentors or leaders who empower us to achieve our goals.
    • The concept of soma can be likened to the nourishment we receive from our experiences, education, and relationships, which enrich our lives and help us grow.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the flow of energy, and the importance of seeking strength and nourishment from divine sources. In real life, it serves as a reminder to recognize the various paths we take, the support we receive, and the nourishment we gain from our experiences. It encourages us to strive for progress and to appreciate the journey towards our goals, much like rivers flowing towards the ocean, ultimately contributing to a larger purpose. This mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering a sense of community, seeking guidance, and valuing the experiences that shape us.
