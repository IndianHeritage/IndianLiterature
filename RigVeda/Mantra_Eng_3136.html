कां मर्यादा वयुनां कद ध वामम् अच् छा गमेमा राघवो न वाजम् |कदā नो देवीर अमृतस्य पत्निः सूरो वर्णेन ततान्न उषासः ||
kā maryādā vayunā kad dha vāmam acchā gamema raghavo na vājam | kadā no devīr amṛtasya patnīḥ sūro varṇena tatanann uṣāsaḥ ||
  • kā maryādā vayunā: This phrase can be translated to "What is the limit of our strength?" It questions the boundaries of human capability and potential.
  • kad dha vāmam acchā gamema: This means "When can we reach our goals?" It reflects the desire to achieve aspirations and the quest for success.
  • raghavo na vājam: This refers to "the Raghus, not the horses." The Raghus are a legendary dynasty in Hindu mythology, symbolizing nobility and valor, while horses often represent material wealth and power.
  • kadā no devīr amṛtasya patnīḥ: This translates to "When will the goddesses, the wives of immortality, be with us?" It signifies the longing for divine blessings and the pursuit of eternal wisdom.
  • sūro varṇena tatanann uṣāsaḥ: This means "The hero, by his color, brings forth the dawn." It emphasizes the role of a hero in bringing light and hope into the world, akin to the dawn dispelling darkness.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of human aspiration, strength, and the pursuit of divine blessings. It encourages individuals to reflect on their limits and strive for greatness while seeking the guidance of higher powers. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and community service. For instance, a leader may draw inspiration from this mantra to motivate their team to push beyond their limits and achieve collective goals. The significance lies in recognizing that while we may face challenges, the pursuit of our aspirations, supported by divine guidance, can lead to transformative outcomes.
