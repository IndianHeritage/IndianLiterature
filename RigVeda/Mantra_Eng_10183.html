जुषाणो अग्नि प्रति ह्यर्य मे वचो विश्वानि विद्वान् वयुनानि सुक्रतो |घृतनिर्णिग ब्रह्मणे गातुम एरय तव देवाऽजनयन्न अनु व्रतम् ||
juṣāṇo agne prati harya me vaco viśvāni vidvān vayunāni sukrato | ghṛtanirṇig brahmaṇe gātum eraya tava devā ajanayann anu vratam ||
  • juṣāṇo: This word translates to "pleasing" or "delighting." It signifies the act of being agreeable or favorable.
  • agne: Refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • prati: Means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating an intention or focus.
  • harya: This word can be interpreted as "to hear" or "to listen," emphasizing the importance of communication and receptivity.
  • me: Translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
  • vaco: Means "words" or "speech," highlighting the power of language and expression.
  • viśvāni: This term means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vidvān: Refers to "wise" or "knowledgeable," indicating the importance of wisdom and understanding.
  • vayunāni: This word can be interpreted as "actions" or "deeds," emphasizing the significance of righteous actions.
  • sukrato: Means "good deeds" or "virtuous," reinforcing the idea of ethical conduct.
  • ghṛtanirṇig: Refers to "clarity" or "purity," often associated with ghee (clarified butter) used in rituals, symbolizing nourishment and sanctity.
  • brahmaṇe: This word means "to the divine" or "to the ultimate reality," indicating a connection to the higher spiritual essence.
  • gātum: Translates to "to sing" or "to chant," emphasizing the importance of vocal expression in spiritual practices.
  • eraya: Means "to come" or "to arrive," suggesting a call for divine presence or intervention.
  • tava: Translates to "your," indicating a personal relationship with the divine.
  • devā: Refers to "gods" or "divine beings," highlighting the presence of the sacred in the mantra.
  • ajanayann: Means "to create" or "to generate," indicating the act of bringing forth or manifesting.
  • anu: Translates to "following" or "after," suggesting a sense of continuity or progression.
  • vratam: Refers to "vow" or "commitment," emphasizing the importance of dedication and resolve in spiritual practice.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking his blessings for wisdom, good deeds, and the ability to communicate effectively. It emphasizes the importance of righteous actions and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or when seeking guidance in decision-making. By invoking Agni's presence, individuals can aspire to lead a life filled with virtue, clarity, and divine connection, ultimately fostering a harmonious existence with themselves and the universe.
