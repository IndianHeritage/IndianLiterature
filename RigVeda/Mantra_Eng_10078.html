इन्द्रो दिवः प्रतिमानं पृथिव्याः विश्वा वेद सवनां हन्ति शुष्णम् |महिं चिद्द्यां आतनोत सूर्येण चास्कम्भ चित्कम्भनेन स्कभीयान् ||
indro divaḥ pratimānam pṛthivyā viśvā veda savanā hanti śuṣṇam | mahīṁ cid dyām ātanot sūryeṇa cāskambha cit kambhanena skabhīyān ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Divaḥ: Means "of the heavens" or "celestial," indicating a connection to the divine or higher realms.
  • Pratimānam: Translates to "the measure" or "the form," suggesting a representation or embodiment of something significant.
  • Pṛthivyā: Refers to "the earth," symbolizing stability, grounding, and the physical realm.
  • Viśvā: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • Veda: Refers to knowledge or wisdom, particularly the sacred knowledge contained in the Vedas.
  • Savanā: Means "offering" or "sacrifice," highlighting the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.
  • Hanti: Translates to "destroys" or "removes," indicating the power to eliminate negativity or obstacles.
  • Śuṣṇam: Refers to "drought" or "dryness," symbolizing lack or absence, particularly in a spiritual or emotional context.
  • Mahīṁ: Means "the great" or "the vast," often referring to the earth or the universe.
  • Cid: Translates to "consciousness" or "awareness," indicating a higher state of being or understanding.
  • Dyām: Refers to "the sky" or "heaven," representing the spiritual realm or higher consciousness.
  • Ātanot: Means "extends" or "expands," suggesting growth or reaching out towards something greater.
  • Sūryeṇa: Refers to "by the sun," symbolizing light, energy, and life-giving force.
  • Cāskambha: Means "supports" or "upholds," indicating strength and stability.
  • Cit: Refers to "consciousness" or "awareness," emphasizing the importance of mindfulness.
  • Kambhanena: Translates to "by the act of binding," suggesting the idea of connection or unity.
  • Skabhīyān: Means "the strongest" or "the most powerful," indicating supreme strength or authority.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine strength and the interconnectedness of all existence. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, offerings, and the removal of negativity in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to leadership roles where one must embody strength and wisdom, as well as in personal growth where one seeks to expand their consciousness and overcome obstacles. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and the importance of nurturing both the physical and spiritual realms in our lives.
