उत त्यन् नो मारुतं शर्ध आ गमद दिविक्षयं यजतम् बर्हिर आसदे | बृहस्पतिः शर्मा पूषोता नो यमद वरूथ्यं वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः ||
uta tyan no mārutaṁ śardha ā gamad divikṣayaṁ yajatam barhir āsade | bṛhaspatiḥ śarma pūṣota no yamad varūthya1ṁ varuṇo mitro aryamā ||
  • uta tyan no mārutaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the Maruts, the storm deities, to come forth and assist us. The Maruts are often associated with strength and power, representing the forces of nature.
  • śardha ā gamad: This means "may they come together." It signifies unity and collaboration, suggesting that the forces of nature and the divine should work in harmony for the benefit of humanity.
  • divikṣayaṁ: This term refers to the celestial or heavenly realms. It indicates a connection between the earthly and the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers.
  • yajatam barhir āsade: This can be translated as "may we worship and offer our prayers." It highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • bṛhaspatiḥ: Bṛhaspati is the deity of wisdom and knowledge. Invoking him signifies the importance of seeking wisdom in our endeavors and decisions.
  • śarma pūṣota: This phrase means "may he grant us protection and prosperity." It reflects the human desire for safety and abundance in life.
  • no yamad varūthyaṁ: This can be interpreted as a plea for guidance and support in overcoming obstacles. It emphasizes the need for divine assistance in navigating life's challenges.
  • varuṇo mitro aryamā: Varuna, Mitra, and Aryama are deities associated with order, friendship, and harmony. Their invocation signifies the importance of maintaining social order and fostering relationships based on trust and cooperation.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance, wisdom, and protection in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, humanity, and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection when individuals seek guidance in their personal or communal challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, cooperation, and the pursuit of knowledge in achieving a harmonious and prosperous life.
