वयं घा ते त्वे इद व इन्द्र विप्राऽपि ष्मसि |नहि त्वद अन्यः पुरुहूत कश्चन मगवन्न अस्ति मर्दिता ||
vayaṁ ghā te tve id v indra viprā api ṣmasi | nahi tvad anyaḥ puruhūta kaś cana maghavann asti marḍitā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • ghā: Praise
    • te: Your
    • tve: To you
    • id: Here
    • v: Indeed
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • viprā: Wise ones or seers
    • api: Also
    • ṣmasi: We are (or we exist)
    • nahi: Not
    • tvad: Your
    • anyaḥ: Other
    • puruhūta: Many invocations
    • kaś: Any
    • cana: At all
    • maghavan: O bounteous one
    • asti: Is
    • marḍitā: To be praised or worshipped
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, who represents strength, power, and the force of nature.
    • The phrase "We praise you" indicates a collective acknowledgment of Indra's greatness and the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • The line "Not your other invocations" emphasizes the uniqueness of Indra's power and the belief that no other deity can match his significance in the lives of the devotees.
    • It reflects the idea that true strength and prosperity come from a singular devotion to a higher power, which can be applied in various aspects of life, such as leadership, teamwork, and personal growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may invoke the spirit of collaboration and unity, much like the invocation of Indra, to achieve a common goal. The belief in a shared purpose can lead to greater success.
    • In personal development, individuals may focus their efforts on a single goal or aspiration, drawing inspiration from the mantra's emphasis on singular devotion, which can lead to more profound achievements.
    • In community building, the mantra can serve as a reminder that collective strength and support from a higher purpose can lead to resilience and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of devotion and the recognition of a higher power in achieving success and strength in life.
    • It teaches that focusing on a singular source of inspiration can lead to greater unity and purpose, whether in personal endeavors, professional settings, or community efforts.
    • By acknowledging the unique power of a higher force, individuals and groups can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, leading to fulfillment and achievement in various aspects of life.
