दश श्यावा ऋधद्रयो वीतवारासा आशवः |मात्रा नेमिं न वावृतुः ||
daśa śyāvā ṛdhadrayo vītavārāsa āśavaḥ | mathrā nemiṁ ni vāvṛtuḥ ||
  • daśa: This word translates to "ten." In various contexts, the number ten can symbolize completeness or a full cycle, such as the ten fingers on a human hand, representing human capability and potential.
  • śyāvā: This term means "dark" or "black." It can refer to the color of certain animals or aspects of nature, often symbolizing strength or mystery. For example, a black horse is often seen as powerful and majestic.
  • ṛdhadrayo: This word can be interpreted as "those who are rich" or "prosperous." In real life, this can relate to individuals or communities that have achieved wealth through hard work and dedication, such as successful entrepreneurs or thriving businesses.
  • vītavārāsa: This term can be broken down into "vīta" (free) and "vāra" (protection). It suggests a state of being free from fear or danger, akin to living in a safe and secure environment. For instance, a community that has low crime rates can be seen as having "vītavārāsa."
  • āśavaḥ: This word means "those who are swift" or "quick." In a modern context, this can refer to individuals or organizations that adapt quickly to change, such as tech companies that innovate rapidly to stay ahead in the market.
  • mathrā: This term can mean "measure" or "quantity." It emphasizes the importance of balance and moderation in life, similar to how one must measure ingredients carefully when cooking to achieve the desired flavor.
  • nemiṁ: This word translates to "wheel" or "circle." It symbolizes the cyclical nature of life, such as the seasons or the life cycle of birth and death. For example, the wheel of life in Buddhism represents the continuous cycle of life and rebirth.
  • ni vāvṛtuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may they not turn away." It suggests a plea for unity and focus, encouraging individuals to remain committed to their goals and values, much like a team working together towards a common objective.

Conclusion: The mantra "daśa śyāvā ṛdhadrayo vītavārāsa āśavaḥ | mathrā nemiṁ ni vāvṛtuḥ ||" encapsulates themes of completeness, strength, prosperity, safety, swiftness, balance, cyclical nature, and unity. Its significance lies in the encouragement to strive for a balanced and prosperous life while remaining united in purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, adapt to changes swiftly, and maintain a sense of security and prosperity in their endeavors. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of collaboration and resilience in achieving success.
