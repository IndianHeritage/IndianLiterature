वावृद्धान उप द्यवि वृषा वज्री अरोरवीत् |वृत्रहां सोमपातमः ||
vāvṛdhāna upa dyavi vṛṣā vajry aroravīt | vṛtrahā somapātamaḥ ||
  • vāvṛdhāna: This term can be interpreted as "the one who increases" or "the one who expands." It signifies growth and enhancement, which can be related to personal development or the expansion of knowledge.
  • upa dyavi: This phrase translates to "towards the sky" or "upwards." It suggests aspirations and reaching for higher goals, akin to striving for success in various aspects of life.
  • vṛṣā: This word means "the strong" or "the powerful." It represents strength and resilience, qualities that are essential in overcoming challenges in life.
  • vajry: This term refers to "the thunderbolt" or "the weapon of Indra," symbolizing power and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real life, it can be likened to the determination and courage needed to face adversities.
  • aroravīt: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." It emphasizes the importance of creativity and innovation in achieving success.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes ignorance or obstacles. It signifies the triumph over challenges and the removal of barriers in life.
  • somapātamaḥ: This phrase translates to "the one who drinks soma," which is associated with divine inspiration and enlightenment. It represents the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, aspiration, strength, creativity, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, it serves as a reminder to strive for personal and professional development, to face challenges with resilience, and to seek enlightenment through learning. Whether in the context of education, career, or personal relationships, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals towards achieving their goals and overcoming obstacles, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
