तं ईं हिन्वन्ति धीतयो दश वृषो देवम् मर्तास ऊतये हवामहे |धनोर अधि प्रवत आ स ऋण्वत्य अभिव्रजद्भिर वयुनā नवाधित ||
tam īṁ hinvanti dhītayo daśa vriśo devam martāsa ūtaye havāmahe | dhanor adhi pravata ā sa ṛṇvaty abhivrajadbhir vayunā navādhita ||
  • tam īṁ hinvanti: This phrase can be translated as "they who are inspired" or "they who are motivated." It refers to the divine forces or energies that guide and inspire individuals in their pursuits.
  • dhītayo: This word means "intellects" or "wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • daśa vriśo: Translated as "tenfold strength," this phrase signifies the power and resilience that can be harnessed through collective effort and unity.
  • devam martāsa: This means "the divine among mortals." It highlights the connection between the divine and human realms, suggesting that divine qualities can manifest in human actions.
  • ūtaye havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke for prosperity." It indicates a call for abundance and well-being, both materially and spiritually.
  • dhanoḥ adhi pravata: This phrase means "the wealth that flows." It symbolizes the abundance that comes from hard work and the blessings of the divine.
  • ā sa ṛṇvaty: This translates to "may it be fulfilled." It expresses a desire for the realization of aspirations and goals.
  • abhivrajadbhir: This word means "by those who are devoted." It emphasizes the role of dedication and commitment in achieving success.
  • vayunā navādhita: This translates to "with new vigor." It suggests the importance of renewal and fresh energy in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine inspiration and wisdom in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of collective strength, devotion, and the pursuit of prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in teamwork, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, or in personal development, where one seeks to harness their inner strength and wisdom to overcome challenges. By invoking these principles, individuals can strive for a fulfilling and prosperous life, both materially and spiritually.
