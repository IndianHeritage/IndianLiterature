विपिप्रोऽहिमायस्य दृढाः पुरो वज्रीं चवासान दर्दः |सुदामन् तद् रेक्णो अप्रमृष्यम ऋजिष्वने दात्रं दाशुषे दाः ||
vi pipror ahimāyasya dṛḻhāḥ puro vajriñ chavasā na dardaḥ | sudāman tad rekṇo apramṛṣyam ṛjiśvane dātraṁ dāśuṣe dāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The strong ones, who are like the mighty thunderbolt, do not break the unbreakable. The one who is endowed with good fortune, may he grant us the wealth that is beyond measure."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi pipror: Refers to the strong or powerful.
    • ahimāyasya: Suggests something that is unbreakable or invincible.
    • dṛḻhāḥ: Means strong or firm.
    • puro: Indicates the front or the forefront.
    • vajriñ: Relates to the thunderbolt, symbolizing power and strength.
    • chavasā: Refers to the one who is endowed with good fortune.
    • na dardaḥ: Means "do not break" or "remain unbroken."
    • sudāman: Refers to someone who is fortunate or blessed.
    • tad rekṇo: Indicates wealth or prosperity.
    • apramṛṣyam: Means beyond measure or unquantifiable.
    • ṛjiśvane: Refers to the one who is generous or giving.
    • dātraṁ: Means to grant or bestow.
    • dāśuṣe: Refers to the one who is deserving or worthy.
    • dāḥ: Indicates a plea or request.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader who embodies the qualities of strength and resilience can inspire their team to achieve unbreakable goals, much like the "strong ones" in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals who face challenges with unwavering determination can be seen as embodying the essence of being "unbreakable," encouraging others to remain steadfast in their pursuits.
    • The concept of "good fortune" can be related to the idea of being in the right place at the right time, such as a student receiving a scholarship due to their hard work and dedication, which aligns with the blessings of "sudāman."
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on strength, resilience, and the pursuit of unquantifiable wealth, both material and spiritual. It teaches us that true strength is not just about physical power but also about the ability to remain unbroken in the face of adversity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal challenges, professional endeavors, and the pursuit of prosperity. By embodying the qualities of strength and generosity, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
