मन्त्र: पाहि नो अग्नि रक्षसः पाहि धूर्तेर अराव्णः | पाहि रीषत उता व जिघांसतो बृहद्भानो यविष्ठ्य ||
pāhi no agne rakṣasaḥ pāhi dhūrter arāvṇaḥ | pāhi rīṣata uta vā jighāṁsato bṛhadbhāno yaviṣṭhya ||
  • pāhi no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, protect us." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the protective and transformative power of fire in our daily lives, such as in cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • rakṣasaḥ: This word means "from the demons" or "from evil forces." It signifies the need for protection from negative influences or harmful entities. In contemporary life, this can relate to the challenges we face, such as negative thoughts, toxic relationships, or harmful environments that we seek to be shielded from.
  • pāhi dhūrter arāvṇaḥ: This translates to "protect us from the deceitful." Here, "dhūrter" refers to deceit or trickery, and "arāvṇaḥ" implies the need for safeguarding against those who may wish to harm us through cunning means. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals may face manipulation or dishonesty, such as in business dealings or personal relationships.
  • pāhi rīṣata: This means "protect us from anger." Anger can lead to conflict and destruction, both in personal and societal contexts. For example, in a workplace, unchecked anger can lead to a toxic environment, while in personal relationships, it can cause rifts and misunderstandings.
  • uta vā jighāṁsato: This translates to "or from those who wish to harm us." It emphasizes the need for protection from those who harbor ill intentions. In real life, this can relate to situations where individuals may face bullying, harassment, or any form of aggression.
  • bṛhadbhāno: This means "the great light" or "the one who shines brightly." It symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can be associated with the pursuit of knowledge and truth in our lives, guiding us away from ignorance and towards understanding.
  • yaviṣṭhya: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is to be worshipped" or "the one who is revered." It signifies the importance of reverence and respect towards higher powers or principles in our lives. This can be seen in how we honor our values, traditions, and the wisdom of those who guide us.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from negative influences, deceit, anger, and harmful intentions while also recognizing the value of knowledge and reverence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering a positive environment at home and work, cultivating healthy relationships, and striving for personal growth and enlightenment. By invoking the protective qualities of Agni, we can aspire to create a life that is not only safe but also filled with wisdom and respect for ourselves and others.
