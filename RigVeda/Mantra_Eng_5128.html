अहिर्व इव भोगैः पर्य एति बाहुं ज्यायाः हेतिम् परिबाधमानः |हस्तघ्नो विश्वा वयुनानि विद्वान् पुमान् पुमांसम् परि पातु विश्वतः ||
ahir iva bhogaiḥ pary eti bāhuṁ jyāyā hetim paribādhamānaḥ | hastaghno viśvā vayunāni vidvān pumān pumāṁsam pari pātu viśvataḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ahir iva bhogaiḥ pary eti bāhuṁ jyāyā hetim paribādhamānaḥ | hastaghno viśvā vayunāni vidvān pumān pumāṁsam pari pātu viśvataḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahir iva: like a serpent
    • bhogaiḥ: with enjoyments or pleasures
    • pary eti: surrounds or encompasses
    • bāhuṁ: the arm
    • jyāyā: of the greater or superior
    • hetim: the cause or reason
    • paribādhamānaḥ: being restrained or bound
    • hastaghno: one who is struck by the hand
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • vayunāni: the powers or energies
    • vidvān: the wise or knowledgeable
    • pumāṁsam: the man or person
    • pari: all around
    • pātu: may protect
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra metaphorically compares the experience of life to a serpent that surrounds us with pleasures and challenges.
    • The phrase "like a serpent" suggests that life can be both beautiful and dangerous, requiring wisdom to navigate.
    • The "arm of the greater" signifies strength and support, indicating that we should seek help from those who are more knowledgeable or powerful.
    • The term "being restrained" implies that while we may face limitations, we can still find ways to overcome them through wisdom and understanding.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in protecting oneself from the various challenges of life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals seek guidance from mentors or experts when faced with difficult decisions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the dual nature of life, where both pleasures and challenges coexist.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom to navigate life's complexities.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to surround themselves with wise counsel and to be aware of the potential dangers that may arise.
    • Ultimately, the mantra calls for a holistic approach to life, where one seeks protection and guidance from all sides, ensuring a balanced and fulfilling existence.
