युष्मतो विप्रो मरुतः शतस्वी युष्मतो अर्वा सहुरिः सहस्री |युष्मोतः सम्राज् उता हन्ति वृत्रं प्र तद् वो अस्तु धूतयो देश्णम् ||
yuṣmoto vipro marutaḥ śatasvī yuṣmoto arvā sahuriḥ sahasrī | yuṣmotaḥ samrāḻ uta hanti vṛtram pra tad vo astu dhūtayo deṣṇam ||
  • yuṣmoto: This term can be translated as "for you" or "to you," indicating a sense of offering or devotion.
  • vipro: Refers to a "sage" or "wise person," often associated with knowledge and spiritual insight.
  • marutaḥ: This word denotes the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and the forces of nature.
  • śatasvī: Meaning "hundredfold" or "abundant," it suggests a sense of richness or prosperity.
  • arvā: This can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick," often associated with the speed of thought or action.
  • sahuriḥ: This term implies "with strength" or "powerful," indicating the might of the forces being invoked.
  • sahasrī: Meaning "thousand," it emphasizes abundance and the vastness of the blessings being sought.
  • samrāḻ: This word translates to "sovereign" or "ruler," indicating authority and control over one's life and circumstances.
  • uta: A conjunction meaning "and" or "also," linking thoughts or ideas together.
  • hanti: This means "to destroy" or "to overcome," often used in the context of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • vṛtram: Refers to "Vritra," a dragon or serpent in Vedic mythology, symbolizing obstacles or ignorance that need to be overcome.
  • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "out," often used to indicate direction or purpose.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the goal or the desired outcome of the invocation.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating the recipients of the blessings or the invocation.
  • astu: Translates to "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for the outcome.
  • dhūtayo: This term can be interpreted as "purified" or "cleansed," indicating a state of readiness or purity.
  • deṣṇam: Refers to "vision" or "sight," often associated with clarity and understanding.

The mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, asking for their blessings of strength, abundance, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the power of the sage in navigating life's challenges. The repetition of terms related to abundance and strength suggests a holistic approach to life, where one seeks not just material wealth but also spiritual insight and clarity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In times of personal struggle, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance from wise individuals or mentors.
  • During challenges in business or personal projects, invoking the Maruts can symbolize the need for dynamic action and overcoming obstacles.
  • In spiritual practices, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek clarity and purity of thought, allowing one to navigate life's complexities with wisdom.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine forces to gain strength, wisdom, and the ability to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and support, one can achieve abundance and clarity in life.
