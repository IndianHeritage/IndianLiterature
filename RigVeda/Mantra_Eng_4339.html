अभि क्रन्द स्तनय गर्भम् आ धा उदन्वता परि दीया रथेन |दृतीं सु कर्ष विषितं न्यञ्चं समा भवन्तूद्वतो निपादाः ||
abhi kranda stanaya garbham ā dhā udanvatā pari dīyā rathena | dṛtiṁ su karṣa viṣitaṁ nyañcaṁ samā bhavantūdvato nipādāḥ ||
  • abhi kranda: This phrase can be interpreted as "to cry out" or "to call forth." In a real-life context, this can relate to the act of seeking help or expressing a need, much like a child calling for their parent in times of distress.
  • stanaya: This word refers to "breast" or "nourishment." It symbolizes the source of sustenance and care, akin to a mother providing nourishment to her child, representing the nurturing aspect of life.
  • garbham: Meaning "womb" or "embryo," this term signifies the potential for new life and growth. In real life, it can be seen in the context of pregnancy, where a new life is developing and preparing to enter the world.
  • ā dhā: This translates to "to place" or "to establish." It reflects the idea of grounding or setting something in a stable position, similar to how one might establish a foundation for a building or a relationship.
  • udanvatā: This word means "uplifting" or "elevating." It can be related to the act of raising one's spirits or aspirations, much like a motivational speaker inspiring an audience to reach for their dreams.
  • pari dīyā: This phrase means "to give all around" or "to distribute." It emphasizes the importance of sharing and generosity, akin to community service where individuals come together to help those in need.
  • rathena: This translates to "by the chariot." The chariot symbolizes movement and progress, representing the journey of life and the means by which we navigate our paths, similar to how a vehicle helps us reach our destinations.
  • dṛtiṁ: Meaning "steadfastness" or "determination," this term highlights the importance of resilience in the face of challenges, much like an athlete training hard to achieve their goals despite obstacles.
  • su karṣa: This phrase can be interpreted as "good harvest" or "successful cultivation." It signifies the rewards of hard work and dedication, similar to a farmer reaping the benefits of their labor during harvest season.
  • viṣitaṁ: This word means "to be directed" or "to be focused." It emphasizes the importance of having a clear goal or direction in life, akin to a student pursuing a specific career path with determination.
  • nyañcaṁ: This translates to "to be free" or "to be liberated." It reflects the idea of breaking free from constraints, much like someone overcoming personal struggles to achieve their true potential.
  • samā: Meaning "equal" or "balanced," this term signifies harmony and equilibrium in life, similar to maintaining a work-life balance to ensure overall well-being.
  • bhavantūdvato: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you become elevated" or "may you rise." It encourages personal growth and the pursuit of higher ideals, much like a mentor guiding a mentee towards success.
  • nipādāḥ: This word means "to be grounded" or "to be established." It signifies the importance of having a solid foundation in life, akin to building strong relationships or a stable career.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of nurturing, growth, determination, and the importance of community and support. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, where each element plays a vital role in the journey of existence. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, personal development, community service, and professional endeavors. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to uplift themselves and others, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
