एकराल् अस्य भुवनस्य राजसि शचीपत इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः |माध्यंदिनस्य सवनस्य वृत्रहन्न अनेद्य पिबा सोमस्य वज्रिवः ||
ekarāḻ asya bhuvanasya rājasi śacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya vṛtrahann anedya pibā somasya vajrivaḥ ||
  • ekarāḻ asya bhuvanasya: This phrase translates to "the one who rules this universe." It signifies a singular, supreme authority or power that governs all aspects of existence.
  • rājasi: This word means "in the royal or kingly manner." It emphasizes the majesty and sovereignty associated with the divine power being addressed.
  • śacīpata: This term refers to "the lord of strength and power." It highlights the might and capability of the divine being, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • indra: Indra is the chief deity in the Rigveda, known for his valor and strength. He is often depicted as a warrior who protects and provides for his followers.
  • viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ: This phrase means "with all kinds of blessings." It suggests that the divine power bestows various forms of prosperity and well-being upon the world.
  • mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya: This translates to "of the midday offering." It refers to the rituals and sacrifices performed at noon, a time considered auspicious for invoking divine blessings.
  • vṛtrahann: This term means "the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology that represents chaos and obstruction. Indra's victory over Vritra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos.
  • anedya: This word means "unconquerable" or "invincible." It signifies the strength and resilience of the divine power being invoked.
  • pibā somasya: This translates to "drink of the soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, believed to confer immortality and divine insight. It represents the nourishment of the spirit.
  • vajrivaḥ: This term means "the one who wields the thunderbolt." It symbolizes the power and authority of the divine, often associated with Indra's weapon, the vajra, which represents strength and protection.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine authority, strength, and the blessings that come from a higher power. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine, particularly during auspicious times. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges where one seeks strength and guidance, or in communal rituals that aim to invoke prosperity and harmony. The significance lies in recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the power of faith and devotion in overcoming obstacles and achieving well-being.
