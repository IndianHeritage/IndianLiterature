नकिर ह्य एषां जनूंषि वेद ते अङ्ग विद्रे मिथो जनित्रम् ||
nakir hy eṣāṁ janūṁṣi veda te aṅga vidre mitho janitram ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nakir: Not known or not recognized.
    • hy: Indeed or certainly.
    • eṣāṁ: Of these or of those.
    • janūṁṣi: Bodies or forms.
    • veda: Knows or understands.
    • te: Your or of you.
    • aṅga: Parts or limbs.
    • vidre: To separate or to distinguish.
    • mitho: Among themselves or mutually.
    • janitram: Born or generated.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of understanding and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It emphasizes that while we may not fully know or recognize the forms of others, there is a deeper understanding that binds us together.
    • The phrase suggests that our individual parts (aṅga) are distinct yet part of a larger whole, and that we are born from mutual interactions (janitram).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals may have different roles and responsibilities, yet they contribute to the overall well-being of the group. For instance, in a workplace, each employee has unique skills that, when combined, lead to the success of the organization.
    • In nature, different species coexist and rely on each other for survival, demonstrating the interconnectedness of life. For example, bees pollinate flowers, which in turn provide food for other animals and humans.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the unity and interdependence of all beings. It encourages us to look beyond our individual differences and recognize the shared essence that connects us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can foster empathy, cooperation, and harmony within communities, promoting a sense of belonging and collective responsibility.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, we can work towards a more inclusive and compassionate society, where the strengths of individuals are celebrated as part of a greater whole.
