रेवतीर नः सदहमाद इन्द्रे सन्तु तुविवाजाः |क्षुमन्तो याभिर मदेम ||
revatīr naḥ sadhamāda indre santu tuvivājāḥ | kṣumanto yābhir madema ||
  • Mantra: "revatīr naḥ sadhamāda indre santu tuvivājāḥ | kṣumanto yābhir madema ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • revatīr: Wealth or prosperity
    • naḥ: Our
    • sadhamāda: May they be well-disposed or favorable
    • indre: To Indra, the king of gods
    • santu: May they be
    • tuvivājāḥ: Those who are endowed with strength or vigor
    • kṣumanto: Those who are hungry or in need
    • yābhir: With whom
    • madema: May we be satisfied or fulfilled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for prosperity and well-being, invoking the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and abundance.
    • The phrase "revatīr naḥ" emphasizes the desire for wealth and prosperity to be ours, indicating a collective aspiration for abundance.
    • By addressing Indra, the mantra seeks divine favor, suggesting that human efforts alone may not suffice without the blessings of higher powers.
    • The mention of "kṣumanto" highlights the plight of those who are in need, reminding us of the importance of compassion and support for the less fortunate.
    • Ultimately, the mantra culminates in a plea for satisfaction and fulfillment, indicating that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses emotional and spiritual well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during harvest festivals where people come together to celebrate abundance and share resources with those in need.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of financial difficulty, seeking divine intervention and support while also committing to help others who are struggling.
    • Organizations focused on social welfare can use the essence of this mantra to inspire their mission, emphasizing the importance of uplifting the needy while fostering a spirit of generosity and community support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of prosperity, compassion, and divine grace.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to seek not only their own well-being but also to extend support to those who are less fortunate.
    • In a world where material wealth often overshadows spiritual and emotional fulfillment, this mantra calls for a balanced approach to life, where true richness is found in shared abundance and collective happiness.
