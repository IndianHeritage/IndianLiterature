अग्निरप्सां ऋतीषहं वीरं ददाति सत्पतिम् |यस्य त्रसन्ति शवसः संचक्षि शत्रवो भियाः ||
agnir apsām ṛtīṣahaṁ vīraṁ dadāti satpatim | yasya trasanti śavasaḥ saṁcakṣi śatravo bhiyā ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and energy.
  • Apsām: This means "of the waters." In Vedic texts, water is often associated with life, nourishment, and the flow of energy.
  • Ṛtīṣahaṁ: This term can be translated as "the one who upholds truth." It signifies the importance of righteousness and moral order in the universe.
  • Vīraṁ: This means "the hero" or "the brave one." It represents strength, valor, and the qualities of a true leader.
  • Dadāti: This translates to "gives" or "bestows." It indicates the act of providing or sharing something valuable.
  • Satpatim: This means "the right path" or "the true way." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous and ethical path in life.
  • Yasya: This means "whose." It indicates possession or belonging.
  • Trasanti: This translates to "fear" or "tremble." It reflects the idea of being in awe or respect of something powerful.
  • Śavasaḥ: This term refers to "the dead" or "the lifeless." It signifies the concept of mortality and the transient nature of life.
  • Saṁcakṣi: This means "watches over" or "observes." It indicates vigilance and awareness.
  • Śatravo: This translates to "enemies" or "opponents." It represents challenges or adversities one may face.
  • Bhiyā: This means "with fear" or "in fear." It signifies the emotional response to threats or dangers.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, the fire god, asking for strength, protection, and guidance on the righteous path. It highlights the importance of truth, bravery, and the ability to overcome fear in the face of challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni transforms and purifies, individuals can seek to transform their lives by embracing truth and righteousness.
  • Leadership: The qualities of a hero (Vīraṁ) are essential for effective leadership. Leaders must inspire courage and uphold moral values.
  • Facing Challenges: The mantra reminds us that fear (Trasanti) is a natural response to adversity (Śatravo). However, with vigilance (Saṁcakṣi) and the right guidance (Satpatim), we can navigate through difficulties.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the values of truth, bravery, and the importance of following a righteous path. It encourages individuals to seek transformation and strength in their lives while being aware of the challenges they may face. By invoking these principles, one can cultivate a life of purpose and integrity.
