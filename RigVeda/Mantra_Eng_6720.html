मन्त्र: स्वरन्ति त्वा सुते नरो वसो निरेका उक्तिनः |कदā सुतं तृषाण ओका आगम इन्द्र स्वब्दिव वंसगः ||
svaranti tvā sute naro vaso nireka ukthinaḥ | kadā sutaṁ tṛṣāṇa oka ā gama indra svabdīva vaṁsagaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svaranti: They sing or praise
    • tvā: You
    • sute: In the offerings or sacrifices
    • naro: Men or people
    • vaso: Of the dwelling or home
    • nireka: Without any hindrance
    • ukthinaḥ: Those who have recited or chanted
    • kadā: When
    • sutaṁ: The offspring or the result
    • tṛṣāṇa: Thirst or desire
    • oka: Shelter or abode
    • ā gama: Come or arrive
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • svabdīva: Like a sound or voice
    • vaṁsagaḥ: Of the lineage or family
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of praising and invoking the divine, particularly Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and the welfare of the community.
    • The phrase "svaranti tvā sute" indicates that people are singing praises to the divine through their offerings, which can be likened to how communities today come together in festivals or rituals to honor their beliefs.
    • The mention of "naro vaso nireka ukthinaḥ" suggests that these praises are recited without any hindrance, emphasizing the importance of sincerity and devotion in spiritual practices.
    • "Kadā sutaṁ tṛṣāṇa" reflects the human desire for fulfillment and the quest for shelter or security, which resonates with modern-day aspirations for stability and prosperity.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the hope for divine intervention in achieving these desires, similar to how people today seek guidance or blessings in times of need.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to seek blessings for prosperity, much like the offerings made in the mantra.
    • Community gatherings, such as Diwali in India or Thanksgiving in the United States, reflect the collective expression of gratitude and the desire for abundance.
    • In times of crisis, people often turn to spiritual practices, invoking divine support, akin to the invocation of Indra in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of human longing for connection with the divine and the community's role in spiritual practices.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of the importance of collective worship and the pursuit of fulfillment through divine blessings.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages individuals to seek harmony within themselves and their communities, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.
