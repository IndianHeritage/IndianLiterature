तं īḻत प्रथमं यज्ञसाधं विश आरीर आहुतं ऋञ्जसानम् |ऊर्जः पुत्रम् भरतं सृप्रदाणुं देवाः अग्निं धारयन् द्रविणोदाम् ||
tam īḻata prathamaṁ yajñasādhaṁ viśa ārīr āhutam ṛñjasānam | ūrjaḥ putram bharataṁ sṛpradānuṁ devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām ||
  • tam īḻata: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is to be worshipped" or "that which is to be revered." In real life, this can refer to the values or principles that one holds dear, such as honesty, integrity, or compassion.
  • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word emphasizes the importance of prioritizing certain actions or beliefs in our lives. For example, in a business context, prioritizing customer satisfaction can lead to long-term success.
  • yajñasādhaṁ: This term refers to the "sacred offering" or "sacrificial act." In modern life, this can be likened to acts of service or charity, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, such as volunteering at a local shelter.
  • viśa ārīr: This phrase can be understood as "the essence of the universe" or "the cosmic body." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings. For instance, environmental conservation efforts highlight our responsibility towards the planet and all its inhabitants.
  • āhutam: This means "offered" or "sacrificed." In everyday life, this can represent the sacrifices we make for our loved ones, such as a parent working extra hours to provide for their family.
  • ṛñjasānam: This term can be interpreted as "the seat of joy" or "the place of happiness." It suggests that true fulfillment comes from within and can be achieved through positive actions and relationships.
  • ūrjaḥ: Meaning "energy" or "vitality," this word emphasizes the importance of maintaining one's physical and mental health. For example, engaging in regular exercise and mindfulness practices can enhance one's overall well-being.
  • putram: This translates to "son" or "child," symbolizing the future generation. It highlights the responsibility of nurturing and educating the youth to ensure a better tomorrow.
  • bharataṁ: This can be interpreted as "to bear" or "to carry." In a societal context, it can refer to the collective responsibility of individuals to support one another in times of need.
  • sṛpradānuṁ: This term signifies "the giver of abundance" or "the one who provides." It reflects the importance of generosity and sharing resources with others, such as donating to those less fortunate.
  • devā: Meaning "gods" or "divine beings," this word represents the higher ideals we aspire to in our lives. It can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and ethical living.
  • agniṁ: This translates to "fire," symbolizing transformation and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the challenges we face that ultimately lead to personal growth and development.
  • dhārayan: Meaning "to hold" or "to sustain," this word emphasizes the importance of maintaining our values and commitments in the face of adversity.
  • draviṇodām: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the importance of not just material wealth but also the richness of experiences and relationships in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence, sacrifice, and interconnectedness in our lives. It encourages individuals to prioritize values, engage in acts of service, and nurture future generations. In a world where individualism often prevails, this mantra serves as a reminder of our collective responsibility towards each other and the environment. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society, where the well-being of all is prioritized.
