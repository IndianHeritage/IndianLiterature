मन्त्र: परि प्रजाताḥ क्रत्वा बभूथा भुवो देवानाम् पिता पुत्रः सन् ||
pari prajātaḥ kratvā babhūtha bhuvo devānām pitā putraḥ san ||
  • Mantra: "pari prajātaḥ kratvā babhūtha bhuvo devānām pitā putraḥ san"
  • Translation: "By the power of the sacrifice, the progeny of the divine beings became manifest; the father of the gods became the son."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • prajātaḥ: born, progeny
    • kratvā: by the act of sacrifice, through ritual
    • babhūtha: became, manifested
    • bhuvo: of the worlds, of existence
    • devānām: of the gods, divine beings
    • pitā: father
    • putraḥ: son
    • san: being, existence
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of sacrifice (Yajna) in the creation and sustenance of life.
    • The term "prajātaḥ" refers to the offspring or progeny, indicating that through divine actions and sacrifices, life is generated.
    • The phrase "kratvā babhūtha" highlights the transformative power of rituals, suggesting that through dedicated actions, one can bring forth new beginnings.
    • The mention of "devānām pitā putraḥ" illustrates the relationship between the divine and the earthly, where the father (the divine) becomes the son (the earthly manifestation), symbolizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and sacrifices are performed to honor ancestors and seek blessings for future generations, reflecting the idea of continuity and connection.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to the importance of family and community bonds, where the actions of one generation impact the next.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can also be seen as a form of sacrifice for future generations, ensuring that the earth remains a viable home for all forms of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound relationship between sacrifice, creation, and the continuity of life.
    • It teaches us that our actions, especially those aimed at the greater good, have the power to shape the future.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in meaningful actions that benefit not only ourselves but also our families, communities, and the environment.
