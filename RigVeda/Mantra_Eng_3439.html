अपो ह्येषां अजुषन्त देवाः अभि क्रत्वा मनसा दीध्यानाः |वाजो देवानाम् अभवत् सुखर्मेन्द्रस्य ऋभुक्षा वरुणस्य विभ्वा ||
apo hy eṣām ajuṣanta devā abhi kratvā manasā dīdhyānāḥ | vājo devānām abhavat sukarmendrasya ṛbhukṣā varuṇasya vibhvā ||
  • Translation: "The waters, indeed, are those that the gods, having made their effort with their minds, have brought forth. The strength of the gods has become the bounty of the powerful Indra, the abundance of Varuna."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • apo: waters, symbolizing purity and life.
    • hy: indeed, emphasizing certainty.
    • eṣām: those, referring to the waters.
    • ajuṣanta: having made effort, indicating the active role of the gods.
    • devā: gods, representing divine forces.
    • abhi: towards, indicating direction or purpose.
    • kratvā: effort or action, highlighting the importance of intention.
    • manasā: with the mind, suggesting the power of thought.
    • dīdhyānāḥ: having brought forth, indicating creation or manifestation.
    • vājo: strength or power, often associated with Indra.
    • devānām: of the gods, reinforcing the divine aspect.
    • abhavat: has become, indicating transformation.
    • sukarmendrasya: of the powerful Indra, who is known for his deeds.
    • ṛbhukṣā: bounty or abundance, referring to the blessings received.
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna, the god of cosmic order.
    • vibhvā: abundance or wealth, indicating prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and effort in achieving goals. For instance, in a business setting, a team that collaborates with a clear vision and purpose is more likely to succeed than one that does not.
    • In personal development, the idea of using one's mind to manifest desires can be seen in practices like visualization and positive affirmations, where individuals focus their thoughts to create desired outcomes.
    • The reference to water symbolizes the essential resources we need in life. Just as water is vital for survival, the mantra reminds us to appreciate and manage our resources wisely.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of effort, intention, and the divine forces that support us. It teaches us that through focused thought and action, we can harness the strength of the universe to bring forth abundance in our lives. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community development, encouraging us to align our efforts with a higher purpose for greater fulfillment and success.
