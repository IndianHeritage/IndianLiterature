समानो राजा विभृतः पुरुत्रा शये शयासु प्रयुतो वनानु |अन्या वत्सं भरति क्षेति माता महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
samāno rājā vibhṛtaḥ purutrā śaye śayāsu prayuto vanānu | anyā vatsam bharati kṣeti mātā mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • samāno rājā: This phrase translates to "the same king." It signifies unity and equality among rulers or leaders. In a real-life context, this can be seen in democratic societies where leaders are elected to serve the common good, emphasizing the importance of collective leadership.
  • vibhṛtaḥ purutrā: This means "spread out everywhere." It suggests the omnipresence of a divine force or principle. For example, in nature, we see how ecosystems are interconnected, and the health of one part affects the whole, illustrating the idea of a universal presence.
  • śaye śayāsu: This translates to "in the beds of rest." It can refer to the idea of peace and tranquility. In modern life, this can be related to the importance of mental health and the need for rest and relaxation in our busy lives.
  • prayuto vanānu: This means "engaged in the forests." It symbolizes a connection to nature and the importance of environmental stewardship. In today's world, this can be seen in movements advocating for sustainability and conservation of natural resources.
  • anyā vatsam bharati kṣeti: This translates to "another mother carries the calf." It highlights the concept of nurturing and care, suggesting that love and support can come from various sources. In real life, this can be observed in communities where individuals support each other, akin to a village raising a child.
  • mātā mahad devānām: This means "the great mother of the gods." It signifies the nurturing aspect of divinity, representing the source of life and sustenance. In various cultures, the concept of a mother goddess is prevalent, symbolizing fertility, protection, and compassion.
  • asuratvam ekam: This translates to "the unity of the divine." It emphasizes the oneness of all beings and the interconnectedness of life. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the global movements for peace and unity, where people come together regardless of differences to work towards a common goal.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of unity, nurturing, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in promoting harmony among individuals and communities, encouraging a collective approach to leadership and care for the environment. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in fostering cooperation, supporting mental health, and advocating for sustainable practices, ultimately leading to a more compassionate and unified society.
