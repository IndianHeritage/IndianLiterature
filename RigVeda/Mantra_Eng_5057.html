नास्य वर्ता न तरुतां न्व अस्ति मरुतो यं अवथ वाजसातौ |तोक वा गोषु तनये यं अप्सु स व्रजं दर्ता पार्ये अध द्योः ||
nāsya vartā na tarutā nv asti maruto yam avatha vājasātau | toke vā goṣu tanaye yam apsu sa vrajaṁ dartā pārye adha dyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nāsya: not this
    • vartā: existence or being
    • na: not
    • tarutā: by the trees
    • nv: nor
    • asti: is
    • maruto: of the winds
    • yam: whom
    • avatha: you protect
    • vājasātau: in the strength of horses
    • toke: in the offspring
    • vā: or
    • goṣu: in the cows
    • tanaye: in the progeny
    • yam: whom
    • apsu: in the waters
    • sa: he
    • vrajaṁ: the one who goes
    • dartā: the one who strikes
    • pārye: in the surrounding
    • adha: below
    • dyoḥ: of the heavens
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the omnipresence and protective nature of divine forces.
    • It emphasizes that there is no existence without the divine, whether in nature (trees), in strength (horses), in livestock (cows), or in the waters.
    • The mention of "maruto" refers to the winds, symbolizing the unseen forces that govern life.
    • The mantra suggests that the divine is a protector, safeguarding all forms of life and their progeny.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the protection of their crops (trees) and livestock (cows) from natural calamities, recognizing the divine's role in their sustenance.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and protection in their endeavors, acknowledging that their success is intertwined with higher powers.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can inspire respect for nature, as it highlights the interconnectedness of all living beings and the divine presence in every aspect of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in all aspects of life, from nature to personal endeavors.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the protective forces around us and to seek harmony with the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages a holistic approach to living, where one acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine's role in sustaining life.
