नियुत्वन्तो ग्रामजितो यथा नरो 'र्यमणो न मरुतः कबन्धिनः |पिन्वन्त्य उत्सं यद इनासो अस्वरन व्युन्दन्ति पृथिवीं मध्वो अन्धसा ||
niyutvanto grāmajito yathā naro 'ryamaṇo na marutaḥ kabandhinaḥ | pinvanty utsaṁ yad ināso asvaran vy undanti pṛthivīm madhvo andhasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niyutvanto: Those who are united or connected.
    • grāmajito: The one who is victorious in the village or community.
    • yathā: Just as.
    • naro: A man or human being.
    • 'ryamaṇo: One who is engaged in righteous actions.
    • na: Not.
    • marutaḥ: The winds or forces of nature.
    • kabandhinaḥ: Those who are bound or restrained.
    • pinvanty: They enjoy or partake.
    • utsaṁ: The essence or the best part.
    • yad: That which.
    • ināso: Those who are free from illness or suffering.
    • asvaran: Without sound or noise.
    • vy: They.
    • undanti: They uplift or elevate.
    • pṛthivīm: The Earth.
    • madhvo: Of sweetness or joy.
    • andhasā: In darkness or ignorance.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength of unity and the importance of righteous actions.
    • It suggests that those who are united and victorious in their community, like a man engaged in righteous deeds, are not restrained by the forces of nature.
    • It emphasizes that those who are free from suffering and act without noise or disturbance can uplift the Earth and bring joy even in times of darkness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community project, when individuals come together with a common goal, they can achieve remarkable results, similar to how the mantra describes unity leading to victory.
    • In personal life, practicing righteousness and ethical behavior can lead to a fulfilling life, free from the burdens of guilt and negativity.
    • In environmental movements, collective efforts to protect the Earth can uplift communities and create a positive impact, resonating with the idea of bringing joy and light to darkness.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, righteousness, and the uplifting power of collective action.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community building to personal development and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can overcome challenges and create a harmonious and joyful existence.
