सो अग्निर यो वासुर गृणे सं यम आयन्ति धेनवः |सम अर्वन्तो रघुद्रुवः सं सुजातासः सूरय इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
so agnir yo vasur gṛṇe saṁ yam āyanti dhenavaḥ | sam arvanto raghudruvaḥ saṁ sujātāsaḥ sūraya iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Mantra: "so agnir yo vasur gṛṇe saṁ yam āyanti dhenavaḥ | sam arvanto raghudruvaḥ saṁ sujātāsaḥ sūraya iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: that
    • agnir: fire (often symbolizes energy, transformation, and purity)
    • yo: who
    • vasur: wealth or abundance
    • gṛṇe: sings or praises
    • saṁ: together
    • yam: whom
    • āyanti: approach or come
    • dhenavaḥ: cows (symbolizing nourishment and prosperity)
    • sam: together
    • arvanto: those who are strong or powerful
    • raghudruvaḥ: steadfast or unyielding
    • saṁ: together
    • sujātāsaḥ: well-born or noble
    • sūraya: the wise or learned
    • iṣaṁ: the desire or wish
    • stotṛbhya: to the praisers
    • ā: towards
    • bhara: bring or bestow
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of fire (Agni), which is a central figure in Vedic rituals, symbolizing transformation and the divine energy that sustains life.
    • The phrase "who sings praises" indicates the importance of gratitude and recognition of the divine forces that provide abundance and prosperity.
    • The mention of "cows" (dhenavaḥ) symbolizes nourishment and wealth, reflecting the agricultural society of the Vedic period where cattle were a primary source of sustenance.
    • The terms "strong" (arvanto) and "steadfast" (raghudruvaḥ) highlight the qualities of resilience and strength, essential for overcoming challenges in life.
    • "Well-born" (sujātāsaḥ) and "wise" (sūraya) emphasize the value of noble lineage and wisdom, suggesting that true wealth comes from character and knowledge.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to bring forth desires to the praisers, indicating the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the essence of this mantra can be seen in community gatherings where people come together to celebrate achievements and express gratitude for the resources they have.
    • In business, the mantra's emphasis on strength and wisdom can be applied to leadership, where successful leaders recognize the contributions of their teams and foster a culture of appreciation.
    • In personal development, the qualities of resilience and gratitude can help individuals navigate challenges, reminding them to appreciate the support they receive from others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of gratitude, and the strength found in community.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to recognize and honor the divine forces that contribute to their prosperity and well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, it inspires a mindset of appreciation, resilience, and collaboration, essential for personal and collective growth.
