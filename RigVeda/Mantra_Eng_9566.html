देवानां नु वयं जानाम प्र वोचाम विपण्यया |उक्तेषु शस्यमानेषु यः पश्याद उत्तरे युगे ||
devānāṁ nu vayaṁ jānā pra vocāma vipanyayā | uktheṣu śasyamāneṣu yaḥ paśyād uttare yuge ||
  • devānāṁ nu vayaṁ jānā: This phrase translates to "We know the gods." It emphasizes the importance of understanding divine forces and their influence in our lives. In real life, this can be related to the acknowledgment of higher powers or principles that guide our existence, such as nature, morality, or universal laws.
  • pra vocāma vipanyayā: This means "We speak with great wisdom." It suggests that communication and expression should be rooted in knowledge and insight. For example, in a workplace, effective communication can lead to better teamwork and project outcomes, highlighting the need for wisdom in our interactions.
  • uktheṣu śasyamāneṣu: This translates to "When the words are spoken." It signifies the power of words and their impact on reality. In everyday life, this can be seen in how affirmations or positive speech can influence our mindset and the attitudes of those around us, leading to constructive outcomes.
  • yaḥ paśyād uttare yuge: This means "He who sees in the later age." It refers to the ability to foresee or understand future implications. In practical terms, this can relate to planning for the future, whether in personal finance, career development, or environmental sustainability, where foresight is crucial for success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding divine influences, the importance of wise communication, the power of spoken words, and the ability to foresee future consequences. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By recognizing the interconnectedness of these elements, individuals can lead more meaningful and impactful lives, fostering a deeper connection with themselves, others, and the world around them.
