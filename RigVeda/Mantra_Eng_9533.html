घृतम् अग्नेर वध्र्यश्वस्य वर्धनं घृतम् अन्नं घृतम् व अस्य मेदनम् |घृतेनाहुत उर्वियā वि पप्रथे सूर्य इव रोचते सर्पिरासुतिः ||
ghṛtam agner vadhryaśvasya vardhanaṁ ghṛtam annaṁ ghṛtam v asya medanam | ghṛtenāhuta urviyā vi paprathe sūrya iva rocate sarpirāsutiḥ ||
  • ghṛtam: This word refers to ghee, a clarified butter that is considered sacred and is often used in rituals and offerings in Hinduism.
  • agner: This means "of Agni," the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes transformation and purification.
  • vadhryaśvasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the strong and powerful," indicating strength and vitality.
  • vardhanaṁ: This means "nourishing" or "increasing," suggesting growth and enhancement.
  • annaṁ: This translates to "food," which is essential for sustenance and life.
  • asya: This means "of this," referring to the offerings being made.
  • medanam: This word means "intellect" or "wisdom," indicating the importance of knowledge in conjunction with nourishment.
  • ghṛtenāhuta: This refers to offerings made with ghee, emphasizing the sacredness of the act.
  • urviyā: This means "with abundance" or "in a vast manner," suggesting a generous offering.
  • vi paprathe: This translates to "shining forth," indicating the radiance of the offerings and their divine acceptance.
  • sūrya iva: This means "like the sun," symbolizing brightness, energy, and life-giving properties.
  • rocate: This translates to "shines" or "illuminates," reinforcing the idea of radiance and positivity.
  • sarpirāsutiḥ: This refers to the "essence of ghee," which is considered to be a source of nourishment and vitality.

The mantra essentially speaks about the significance of ghee (ghṛtam) as an offering to Agni, the fire god, and highlights the importance of nourishment (annaṁ) and wisdom (medanam) in life. It emphasizes that offerings made with ghee are not only sacred but also have the power to enhance strength and vitality (vadhryaśvasya) and bring about growth (vardhanaṁ).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in a household, the act of cooking with ghee can be seen as a way to nourish the body and mind, promoting health and well-being. Additionally, during rituals and ceremonies, the use of ghee in offerings symbolizes the connection between the material and spiritual worlds, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence.


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and the role of sacred offerings in enhancing our lives. It teaches us to appreciate the simple yet profound aspects of life, such as food and wisdom, and encourages us to incorporate these values into our daily practices for a more fulfilling existence.
