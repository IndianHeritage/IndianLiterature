मन्त्र: और्वभृगुवच्छुचिमअप्नवानवदाहुवे | अग्निं समुद्रवाससम ||
aurvabhṛguvac chucim apnavānavad ā huve | agniṁ samudravāsasam ||
  • aurvabhṛguvac: This term can be broken down into two parts: "aurva" meaning "new" or "fresh," and "bhṛgu" referring to the sage Bhrigu, a revered figure in Vedic literature. This suggests a connection to wisdom and the transmission of knowledge.
  • chucim: This word translates to "pure" or "clean." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions, which is a recurring theme in many spiritual and philosophical traditions.
  • apnavānavad: This term can be interpreted as "to attain" or "to achieve." It signifies the pursuit of goals and aspirations, highlighting the importance of striving for success in life.
  • ā huve: This phrase means "I invoke" or "I call upon." It indicates a plea or a prayer directed towards a higher power, seeking guidance or blessings.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire," which in Vedic texts symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a divine element that connects the earthly realm with the spiritual.
  • samudravāsasam: This term can be understood as "one who resides in the ocean" or "ocean-dweller." It may refer to the vastness of knowledge and the depths of wisdom that can be found in nature and the universe.

In summary, this mantra invokes the essence of purity, knowledge, and the transformative power of fire. It emphasizes the importance of striving for purity in our thoughts and actions while seeking guidance from higher powers. The reference to fire and the ocean suggests a connection to the natural world, reminding us of the energy and wisdom that surrounds us.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life as a reminder to maintain purity in our intentions and actions, fostering a sense of integrity and authenticity.
  • It encourages individuals to pursue their goals with determination and to seek knowledge and wisdom from various sources, much like how one would explore the depths of the ocean.
  • The invocation aspect of the mantra serves as a reminder to connect with the divine or higher consciousness, which can provide guidance and support in challenging times.
  • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited during meditation or rituals to enhance focus, clarity, and a sense of purpose.
