मती जुष्टो धियाः हितः सोमो हिन्वे परावती |विप्रस्य धारणया कविः ||
matī juṣṭo dhiyā hitaḥ somo hinve parāvati | viprasya dhārayā kaviḥ ||
  • matī: This word refers to intelligence or understanding. It signifies the mental capacity to discern and make wise decisions.
  • juṣṭo: This means 'well-placed' or 'well-directed'. It indicates that the intelligence is not just present but is also applied effectively.
  • dhiyā: This term translates to 'thought' or 'intellect'. It emphasizes the importance of thoughtful consideration in decision-making.
  • hitaḥ: Meaning 'beneficial' or 'good', this word highlights the positive outcomes that arise from wise and directed intelligence.
  • somo: Referring to 'Soma', a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, it symbolizes nourishment and the essence of life that enhances consciousness.
  • hinve: This word means 'to inspire' or 'to invoke'. It suggests the act of calling forth or awakening the beneficial aspects of intelligence.
  • parāvati: This term can be interpreted as 'higher' or 'transcendent', indicating a connection to elevated states of being or consciousness.
  • viprasya: This means 'of the wise' or 'of the learned'. It refers to those who possess knowledge and insight.
  • dhārayā: This translates to 'holding' or 'sustaining'. It implies the responsibility of the wise to maintain and support beneficial thoughts and actions.
  • kaviḥ: Meaning 'poet' or 'sage', this word represents a person who has deep insight and the ability to express profound truths.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of directed intelligence and thoughtful consideration in achieving beneficial outcomes. It suggests that when our intellect is well-placed and nourished, it can inspire higher states of consciousness and wisdom. The wise individuals, or 'vipras', have the responsibility to sustain and promote these beneficial thoughts and actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Decision Making: In personal and professional life, applying directed intelligence can lead to better choices that benefit oneself and others.
  • Leadership: Leaders who embody the qualities of wisdom and thoughtful consideration can inspire their teams to achieve greater heights.
  • Education: Educators can use this principle to foster an environment where students are encouraged to think critically and apply their knowledge for the greater good.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of directed intelligence and the role of the wise in nurturing beneficial thoughts and actions. It encourages individuals to seek higher understanding and to apply their insights for the betterment of society.
