विष्वे हि त्वा सजोषसो जनासो वृक्तबरहिषः |होतारं सद्मसु प्रियं व्यन्ति वार्याः पुरु ||
viśve hi tvā sajoṣaso janāso vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | hotāraṁ sadmasu priyaṁ vyanti vāryā puru ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśve: all, everyone
    • hi: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • sajoṣaso: with joy, with enthusiasm
    • janāso: people, beings
    • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: with offerings, with oblations
    • hotāraṁ: the one who invokes, the priest
    • sadmasu: in the homes, in the dwelling places
    • priyaṁ: dear, beloved
    • vyanti: they go, they move
    • vāryā: with strength, with power
    • puru: many, numerous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the collective spirit of humanity, emphasizing the importance of unity and shared purpose.
    • The phrase "viśve hi tvā" suggests that all beings are connected and should come together in harmony.
    • The term "sajoṣaso" indicates that this unity should be filled with joy and enthusiasm, highlighting the positive energy that arises from collective action.
    • "vṛktabarhiṣaḥ" refers to the offerings made with sincerity and devotion, which can be seen as a metaphor for the contributions we make to society.
    • The "hotāraṁ" represents the guiding figures in our lives, such as leaders or mentors, who help us navigate our paths.
    • "sadmasu priyaṁ" emphasizes the importance of creating a loving and supportive environment in our homes and communities.
    • The concluding part "vyanti vāryā puru" suggests that when we come together with strength and purpose, we can achieve great things.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community service, individuals come together to help those in need, embodying the spirit of "viśve hi tvā" by working collectively for a common cause.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities foster a sense of unity and joy among employees, reflecting "sajoṣaso" as they collaborate towards shared goals.
    • During festivals, families and friends gather to celebrate, creating an atmosphere of love and support, akin to "sadmasu priyaṁ."
    • Mentorship programs in schools and organizations illustrate the role of "hotāraṁ," guiding individuals towards personal and professional growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, joy, and collective effort in achieving common goals.
    • It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together with a shared purpose, they can create a positive impact in their communities and beyond.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various settings, from family gatherings to community initiatives, fostering a sense of belonging and mutual support.
