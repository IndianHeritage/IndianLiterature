तवे धेनुः सुदुघा जातवेदो 'सश्चतेव समना सबरधुक् |त्वं नृभिर दक्षिणावद्भिर अग्ने सुमित्रेभिर इध्यसे देवयद्भिः ||
tve dhenuḥ sudughā jātavedo 'saścateva samanā sabardhuk | tvaṁ nṛbhir dakṣiṇāvadbhir agne sumitrebhir idhyase devayadbhiḥ ||
  • tve dhenuḥ sudughā jātavedo: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Agni, are like a cow that gives abundant milk." Here, Agni, the fire god, is compared to a nurturing cow, symbolizing the wealth and nourishment that fire provides in the form of warmth, energy, and sustenance.
  • ‘saścateva samanā sabardhuk: This part means "You are like a powerful force that is always present." It emphasizes the omnipresence and strength of Agni, akin to a force that is both protective and vital for life.
  • tvaṁ nṛbhir dakṣiṇāvadbhir agne: This translates to "You, O Agni, are invoked by humans with offerings." It highlights the relationship between humans and Agni, where offerings are made to seek blessings, protection, and guidance.
  • sumitrebhir idhyase devayadbhiḥ: This means "You are revered by those who are wise and virtuous." It suggests that Agni is honored by those who possess good qualities and wisdom, reinforcing the idea that righteousness attracts divine favor.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of nourishment, strength, and divine connection. In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and practices where fire is central, such as in Hindu ceremonies, where offerings are made to Agni to seek blessings for prosperity and protection. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of maintaining a respectful and nurturing relationship with the forces of nature, symbolized by Agni, and highlights the value of wisdom and virtue in attracting divine blessings. It encourages individuals to cultivate good qualities and to recognize the interconnectedness of life, nature, and spirituality.
