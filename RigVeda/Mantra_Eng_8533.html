इष्यन् वाचम् उपवक्तेव होतुः पुनान इन्दो वि ष्यां मनिषाम् | इन्द्रश्च यत क्षयथः सौभगाय सुवीर्यस्य पतयः स्याम ||
iṣyan vācam upavakteva hotuḥ punāna indo vi ṣyā manīṣām | indraś ca yat kṣayathaḥ saubhagāya suvīryasya patayaḥ syāma ||
  • iṣyan vācam upavakteva hotuḥ: This phrase can be translated to mean "May our speech be powerful and effective." Here, "iṣyan" refers to the strength or power of speech, while "vācam" means speech or words. In real life, this can be related to the importance of communication in various fields such as business, education, and personal relationships. For example, a leader who communicates effectively can inspire and motivate their team to achieve greater results.
  • punāna indo vi ṣyā manīṣām: This part translates to "May we be purified and endowed with wisdom." "Punāna" signifies purification, "indo" refers to the moon or a source of light, and "manīṣām" means wisdom or intellect. In practical terms, this can be seen in the context of self-improvement and learning. For instance, individuals who seek knowledge and strive for personal growth often find themselves making better decisions and leading more fulfilling lives.
  • indraś ca yat kṣayathaḥ saubhagāya: This translates to "And may Indra, the god of strength, grant us prosperity." "Indra" is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing power and success. "Saubhagāya" means prosperity or good fortune. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of success in various endeavors, such as career advancement or financial stability. For example, a business that seeks the blessings of prosperity may engage in ethical practices and community service, which can lead to long-term success.
  • suvīryasya patayaḥ syāma: This phrase means "May we become the leaders of great strength." "Suvīryasya" refers to great strength or valor, and "patayaḥ" means leaders or rulers. This can be applied in real-life scenarios where individuals or groups aspire to lead with integrity and strength. For instance, a community leader who advocates for social justice and empowers others can be seen as a "patayaḥ" of great strength, inspiring others to follow suit.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of powerful communication, the pursuit of wisdom, the blessings of prosperity, and the qualities of strong leadership. In real life, these elements are crucial for personal and collective success. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster better relationships, achieve their goals, and contribute positively to society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the values that lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
