तुभ्यं गावो घृतं पयो बभ्रो दुदुह्रे अक्षितम् |वर्षिष्ठे अधि सानवि ||
tubhyaṁ gāvo ghṛtam payo babhro duduhre akṣitam | varṣiṣṭhe adhi sānavi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • gāvo: cows
    • ghṛtam: clarified butter
    • payo: milk
    • babhro: that which is rich or abundant
    • duduhre: they have given or produced
    • akṣitam: imperishable or eternal
    • varṣiṣṭhe: in the best or most excellent
    • adhi: upon or in
    • sānavi: the sacred or divine
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses gratitude and reverence towards the divine for the abundance provided by nature, particularly through cows, which are considered sacred in Vedic culture.
    • The mention of "ghṛtam" (clarified butter) and "payo" (milk) signifies the importance of these substances in rituals and daily life, symbolizing nourishment and prosperity.
    • The term "akṣitam" emphasizes the eternal nature of these blessings, suggesting that the gifts of nature are everlasting and should be cherished.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cows are often seen as a source of livelihood. The milk and its by-products, such as ghee (clarified butter), are essential for both sustenance and economic stability.
    • In many cultures, including Indian, offerings of milk and ghee are made during religious ceremonies, highlighting the spiritual significance of these items.
    • The reverence for cows can be seen in practices such as cow protection and the celebration of festivals like Gopashtami, which honors the role of cows in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and spirituality. It encourages individuals to appreciate and respect the resources provided by the earth.
    • In a modern context, it can inspire sustainable practices and a deeper connection to the environment, urging people to recognize the value of natural resources and the importance of preserving them for future generations.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the significance of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives, fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and the divine.
