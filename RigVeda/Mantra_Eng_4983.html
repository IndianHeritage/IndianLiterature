मन्त्र: इन्द्राग्नि आ हि तन्मते नरो धन्वानि बाह्वोः |मा नो अस्मिन महाधने परा वर्क्तं गविष्टिषु ||
indrāgnī ā hi tanvate naro dhanvāni bāhvoḥ | mā no asmin mahādhane parā varktaṁ gaviṣṭiṣu ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. In Vedic literature, Indra represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles, while Agni symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine messenger.
  • Ā hi tanvate: This phrase can be translated as "may they bestow" or "may they grant." It expresses a plea for the blessings and support of the divine forces.
  • Naro dhanvāni bāhvoḥ: This part translates to "the men with strong arms" or "the warriors." It emphasizes the importance of physical strength and valor in achieving success and prosperity.
  • Mā no asmin mahādhane: This means "may we not be deprived of great wealth." It reflects a desire for abundance and prosperity in life, both materially and spiritually.
  • Parā varktaṁ gaviṣṭiṣu: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we attain the highest blessings in our endeavors." It signifies the aspiration to achieve the best outcomes in all pursuits, particularly in the context of wealth and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound request for divine support in the pursuit of strength, wealth, and success. It highlights the importance of collaboration between human effort (represented by the warriors) and divine grace (represented by Indra and Agni). In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when individuals seek strength and guidance to overcome obstacles, or when embarking on new ventures that require both courage and divine blessings. It serves as a reminder that while personal effort is crucial, seeking support from higher powers can lead to greater achievements and fulfillment in life.
