आग्ने गीरो दिव ā पृथिव्या मित्रं वह वरुणं इन्द्रं अग्निम् | आर्यमणं अदितिं विष्णुम् एषां सरस्वती मरुतो मादयन्ताम् ||
āgne giro diva ā pṛthivyā mitraṁ vaha varuṇam indram agnim | āryamaṇam aditiṁ viṣṇum eṣāṁ sarasvatī maruto mādayantām ||
  • āgne: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • giro: Means "words" or "songs," indicating the power of speech and communication.
  • diva: Translates to "heaven" or "sky," representing the divine realm.
  • ā pṛthivyā: Means "from the earth," connecting the divine with the material world.
  • mitraṁ: Refers to "friend," symbolizing harmony and cooperation.
  • vaha: Means "to carry" or "to bring," indicating the act of bringing forth or manifesting.
  • varuṇam: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • indram: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and leadership.
  • agniṁ: Again mentions Agni, emphasizing the importance of fire in rituals and life.
  • āryamaṇam: Refers to Aryaman, a deity associated with social order and hospitality.
  • aditiṁ: Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, symbolizing infinity and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • viṣṇum: Refers to Vishnu, the preserver, representing stability and protection.
  • eṣāṁ: Means "of these," indicating a connection to the divine entities mentioned.
  • sarasvatī: Refers to Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge, music, and arts, symbolizing wisdom and learning.
  • maruto: Refers to the Maruts, storm deities, representing power and the forces of nature.
  • mādayantām: Means "may they be pleased" or "may they be joyful," indicating a desire for harmony and blessings.

This mantra invokes various deities, emphasizing the interconnectedness of the divine and the earthly realms. It highlights the importance of communication, friendship, and moral order in life. The invocation of Agni, Varuna, Indra, and others signifies a holistic approach to life, where energy, ethics, strength, and wisdom play crucial roles.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony and balance in life. It teaches us to seek the blessings of various forces in nature and the divine, encouraging us to live ethically and harmoniously with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation, or daily affirmations to foster a sense of connection with the universe and to promote peace and understanding in our relationships.
