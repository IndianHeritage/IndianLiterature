वाजो नु ते शवसस् पात्व अन्तम् उरुं दोघं धरुणं देव रायः |पदं न तायुर गुहा दधानो महो राये चितयन्न अत्रिम अस्पः ||
vājo nu te śavasas pātv antam uruṁ doghaṁ dharuṇaṁ deva rāyaḥ | padaṁ na tāyur guhā dadhāno maho rāye citayann atrim aspaḥ ||
  • vājo nu te: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord, may your strength and energy be present." The term "vājo" refers to strength or energy, often associated with the divine power that drives the universe.
  • śavasas pātv antam: This means "may your power reach the ultimate end." Here, "śavasas" signifies power or might, and "antam" refers to the ultimate goal or end. This suggests a desire for divine strength to manifest in achieving one's highest aspirations.
  • uruṁ doghaṁ dharuṇaṁ: This translates to "the vast and nourishing." "Uruṁ" means vast or great, while "doghaṁ" refers to nourishment or sustenance. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of abundance and support from the divine.
  • deva rāyaḥ: This means "O divine ruler." "Deva" refers to the divine or godly, and "rāyaḥ" means ruler or king. This phrase acknowledges the supreme authority of the divine in guiding and protecting us.
  • padaṁ na tāyur guhā dadhāno: This translates to "the path is not hidden, as it is held in the cave." "Padaṁ" means path, "tāyur" refers to hidden, and "guhā" means cave. This suggests that the true path to enlightenment or success is not obscured but rather safeguarded within the depths of our being.
  • maho rāye citayann atrim aspaḥ: This means "may we be inspired by the great wealth." "Maho" refers to great, "rāye" means wealth, and "citayann" implies inspiration or awakening. This part of the mantra calls for the awakening of our potential and the pursuit of greatness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates a profound invocation for strength, guidance, and abundance from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine's role in our lives and the paths we choose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, career pursuits, or spiritual journeys. By reciting this mantra, individuals can seek divine support in achieving their goals, finding clarity in their paths, and nurturing their inner strength. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that the divine is always present, guiding us toward our highest potential and abundance.
