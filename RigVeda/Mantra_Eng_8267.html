एष सोमो अधि त्वचि गवां क्रीळत्य अद्रिभिः |इन्द्रं मदाय जोहुवत ||
eṣa somo adhi tvaci gavāṁ krīḻaty adribhiḥ | indram madāya johuvat ||
  • eṣa somo: This phrase refers to "this soma," where "soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • adhi tvaci: Translated as "upon your surface," this indicates a connection to the earth or the physical realm, suggesting that the effects of soma manifest in the material world.
  • gavāṁ: Meaning "of the cows," this symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and prosperity, as cows were considered valuable in Vedic society.
  • krīḻaty: This term means "to play" or "to rejoice," indicating a sense of joy and celebration that comes from the consumption of soma.
  • adribhiḥ: Translated as "with the mountains," this phrase connects the divine experience of soma with the grandeur and stability of nature.
  • indram: Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods, who represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • madāya: This means "for the intoxication," suggesting that the effects of soma lead to a state of bliss or heightened awareness.
  • johuvat: Translated as "they invoke," indicating a call to the divine, seeking blessings and support from Indra.

The mantra can be understood as a celebration of the divine qualities of soma, which brings joy and prosperity, while invoking the blessings of Indra. It emphasizes the connection between the divine, nature, and human experience.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of the interplay between the divine and the earthly. In real life, it can be applied in various contexts, such as rituals for prosperity, celebrations of abundance, or moments of seeking divine guidance. It reminds us of the importance of joy, connection to nature, and the pursuit of higher consciousness in our daily lives.
