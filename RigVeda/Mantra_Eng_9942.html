स वाजं यातापदुष्पदā यन् स्वरषातā परि षदत सनीष्यन् |अनर्वा यच छतदुरस्य वेदो घ्नञ् चिष्णदेवां अभि वर्पसā भूत ||
sa vājaṁ yātāpaduṣpadā yan svarṣātā pari ṣadat saniṣyan | anarvā yac chatadurasya vedo ghnañ chiśnadevām̐ abhi varpasā bhūt ||
  • sa vājaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is powerful" or "that which is strong." In real life, this can refer to the strength we find in our convictions, relationships, or even physical strength in athletes.
  • yātāpaduṣpadā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who moves towards the goal." It signifies the journey towards achieving one's objectives. For example, a student working hard to achieve academic success embodies this concept.
  • yan svarṣātā: This means "that which is in harmony with the universe." It suggests a connection with nature and the cosmos. For instance, individuals practicing mindfulness or meditation often seek this harmony in their lives.
  • pari ṣadat saniṣyan: This can be understood as "surrounding oneself with protection." In a practical sense, this could refer to the support systems we build, such as family and friends, that help us navigate life's challenges.
  • anarvā: This word translates to "without fear" or "fearless." In real life, this can be seen in individuals who take risks, such as entrepreneurs who start new businesses despite uncertainties.
  • yac chatadurasya: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is beyond the ordinary." It refers to extraordinary experiences or achievements. For example, someone who excels in their field, like a scientist making groundbreaking discoveries, embodies this idea.
  • vedo ghnañ: This can be understood as "the knowledge that is profound." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and learning. In everyday life, this can be seen in lifelong learners who seek knowledge through various means, such as education or self-study.
  • chiśnadevām̐: This term refers to "the divine essence" or "the spiritual aspect." It highlights the importance of spirituality in our lives. Many people find solace and guidance through spiritual practices, such as prayer or meditation.
  • abhi varpasā bhūt: This phrase can be translated as "may we be blessed with abundance." It signifies the desire for prosperity and well-being. In real life, this can be reflected in our aspirations for a fulfilling life, whether through career success, healthy relationships, or personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, purpose, harmony, protection, fearlessness, extraordinary achievements, profound knowledge, spirituality, and the pursuit of abundance. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to strive for their goals while remaining connected to the universe and their inner selves. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and spiritual practices, guiding individuals towards a fulfilling and meaningful life.
