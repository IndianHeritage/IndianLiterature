गार्हपत्यमेन सन्त्य ऋतुनā यज्ञनीर असि |देवान देवयते यज ||
gārhapatyena santya ṛtunā yajñanīr asi | devān devayate yaja ||
  • gārhapatyena: This term refers to the household fire, which is considered sacred in Vedic rituals. It symbolizes the warmth and nurturing aspect of home life.
  • santhya: This word means 'to be in harmony' or 'to be in balance'. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining equilibrium in life.
  • ṛtunā: This translates to 'with the seasons' or 'in accordance with the cycles of nature'. It highlights the significance of aligning our actions with the natural rhythms of the world.
  • yajñanīr: This term refers to the act of sacrifice or offering, which is central to Vedic rituals. It signifies the importance of giving and sharing in our lives.
  • asi: This means 'you are' or 'you exist', affirming the presence and essence of the divine in our lives.
  • devān: This word means 'gods' or 'divine beings', representing higher powers or forces that govern the universe.
  • devayate: This translates to 'to the divine' or 'to the gods', indicating the direction of our offerings and prayers.
  • yaja: This means 'to worship' or 'to perform rituals', emphasizing the importance of devotion and reverence in our lives.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to harmonize our lives with the sacredness of the household, the natural cycles, and the divine. It encourages us to engage in acts of sacrifice and worship, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Creating a nurturing home environment that promotes love and support among family members.
  • Practicing mindfulness and balance in daily activities, ensuring that we respect the natural rhythms of life, such as work-life balance.
  • Engaging in community service or charitable acts, reflecting the spirit of sacrifice and giving.
  • Incorporating rituals or practices that honor the divine, such as meditation, prayer, or family gatherings that celebrate shared values.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of living in harmony with our surroundings, nurturing our relationships, and honoring the divine through our actions. It serves as a reminder that our lives are interconnected with nature and the divine, and that through our offerings and worship, we can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.
