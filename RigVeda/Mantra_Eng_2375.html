आ वक्षि देवां इह विप्र यक्षि चोषन् होतर नि शदां योनिषु त्रिषु |प्रतिवीहि प्रस्थितं सोम्यम मधु पिबाग्निध्रात तव भागस्य तृप्णुहि ||
ā vakṣi devām̐ iha vipra yakṣi cośan hotar ni ṣadā yoniṣu triṣu | prati vīhi prasthitaṁ somyam madhu pibāgnīdhrāt tava bhāgasya tṛpṇuhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vakṣi: "I speak" or "I express."
    • devām̐: "to the gods."
    • iha: "here" or "in this world."
    • vipra: "the wise" or "the learned."
    • yakṣi: "may you grant" or "may you bestow."
    • cošan: "the essence" or "the nectar."
    • hotar: "the priest" or "the one who offers."
    • ni: "in" or "within."
    • ṣadā: "always" or "forever."
    • yoniṣu: "in the wombs" or "in the sources."
    • triṣu: "in the three" or "in the three realms."
    • prati: "towards" or "in response to."
    • vīhi: "bring" or "lead."
    • prasthitaṁ: "the one who has started" or "the one who has set forth."
    • somyam: "the soothing" or "the pleasant."
    • madhu: "honey" or "sweetness."
    • pibā: "drink" or "consume."
    • agnīdhrāt: "from the fire-holder" or "from the one who tends the fire."
    • tava: "your."
    • bhāgasya: "of the share" or "of the portion."
    • tṛpṇuhi: "satisfy" or "fulfill."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards the divine, invoking the gods and seeking their blessings.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for wisdom and nourishment, both spiritual and material.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the role of the priest in connecting the divine with the earthly realm.
    • The reference to "honey" symbolizes sweetness and abundance, suggesting a longing for prosperity and joy in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for prosperity and harmony among members.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, priests may chant this mantra to seek divine favor and ensure the well-being of participants.
    • In personal meditation, one might reflect on the themes of wisdom and nourishment, seeking to cultivate these qualities in their life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine connection, wisdom, and the pursuit of abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and the role of spiritual leaders in guiding individuals towards fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in rituals, personal reflections, and community gatherings to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose.
