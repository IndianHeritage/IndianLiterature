अध्वर्यवो यः शतम् आसहस्रम् भूम्या उपस्थे 'वपज जग्हन्वान् |कुत्सस्यायोर अतिथिग्वस्य वीरान् न्या आवृणग भरतā सोमम् अस्मै ||
adhvaryavo yaḥ śatam ā sahasram bhūmyā upasthe 'vapaj jaghanvān | kutsasyāyor atithigvasya vīrān ny āvṛṇag bharatā somam asmai ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals and sacrifices in the Vedic tradition. They are responsible for the proper execution of the rites.
  • yaḥ: This word means "who" and is used to refer to the subject of the mantra.
  • śatam: This translates to "hundred," indicating a large number or abundance.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "out of," often used to indicate a source or origin.
  • sahasram: Meaning "thousand," it emphasizes the vastness or multitude of something.
  • bhūmyā: This word means "earth," referring to the physical realm or the material world.
  • upasthe: This term can be interpreted as "in the lap" or "in the presence," suggesting a nurturing or supportive environment.
  • 'vapaj: This means "to give" or "to offer," indicating an act of generosity or sacrifice.
  • jaghanvān: Referring to "the one who supports" or "the one who upholds," it signifies strength and stability.
  • kutsasyāyor: This term refers to "the two of Kutsas," possibly indicating a lineage or a specific group of people.
  • atithigvasya: Meaning "of the guest," it highlights the importance of hospitality and welcoming others.
  • vīrān: This translates to "heroes" or "warriors," symbolizing strength, valor, and courage.
  • ny: A particle that can mean "not" or "no," often used for negation.
  • āvṛṇag: This means "to cover" or "to envelop," suggesting protection or safeguarding.
  • bharatā: Referring to "the Bharata," which can denote a lineage or a group of people, often associated with the Indian subcontinent.
  • somam: This term refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • asmai: Meaning "to him" or "to that one," indicating a direction of offering or devotion.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals, hospitality, and the nurturing of strength and valor within a community. It highlights the role of priests in connecting the material world with the divine through offerings and sacrifices. In real life, this can be applied to the significance of community gatherings, the importance of honoring guests, and the need for collective strength in facing challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a society and the importance of supporting one another in both spiritual and material pursuits.
