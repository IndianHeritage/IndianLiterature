आप्रुषायन मधुन ऋतस्य योनिम अवक्षिपन्न अर्क उल्काम इव द्योः | बृहस्पतिर उद्धरण्न अश्मनो गा भूम्या उद्नेव विट्वचम् बिभेद ||
āpruṣāyan madhuna ṛtasya yonim avakṣipann arka ulkām iva dyoḥ | bṛhaspatir uddharann aśmano gā bhūmyā udneva vi tvacam bibheda ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpruṣāyan: One who is not touched or affected.
    • madhuna: Of sweetness or honey.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or order.
    • yonim: The source or womb.
    • avakṣipann: Having been cast down or thrown.
    • arka: Light or radiance.
    • ulkām: Like a meteor or a shooting star.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • dyoḥ: Of the heavens.
    • bṛhaspatir: The great teacher or guru.
    • uddharann: Lifting or raising up.
    • aśmano: Of stones or obstacles.
    • gā: The earth or ground.
    • bhūmyā: Of the earth.
    • udneva: Like a wave or surge.
    • vi: Apart or asunder.
    • tvacam: The skin or surface.
    • bibheda: He tore or split.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force that is untouched by worldly influences, symbolized by sweetness and truth.
    • The imagery of light and meteors suggests a powerful, illuminating presence that can penetrate through obstacles, much like how a shooting star breaks through the night sky.
    • Bṛhaspati, often regarded as the deity of wisdom and knowledge, is depicted as lifting burdens and overcoming challenges, akin to how a teacher helps students rise above their difficulties.
    • The reference to tearing through the skin can symbolize breaking through superficial barriers to reach deeper truths or insights.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face obstacles that seem insurmountable. Just as Bṛhaspati lifts stones, mentors and educators can help guide individuals to overcome these challenges.
    • In moments of crisis, the idea of a divine light or guidance can provide comfort and clarity, much like how people often seek inspiration from spiritual texts or leaders during tough times.
    • The metaphor of a meteor can be related to moments of inspiration or epiphany that strike suddenly, illuminating one's path forward.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the power of truth in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, despite the obstacles we face, there is a higher wisdom that can help us navigate through difficulties.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, mentorship, and spiritual insight to rise above their circumstances and achieve their goals.
