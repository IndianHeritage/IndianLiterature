निखातं चिद यः पुरुषम्भृतं वसूदिद वपतिद दाशुषे |वज्री सुशिप्रो हर्यश्व इति करद इन्द्रः क्रत्वा यथा वशत ||
nikhātaṁ cid yaḥ purusambhṛtaṁ vasūd id vapati dāśuṣe | vajrī suśipro haryaśva it karad indraḥ kratvā yathā vaśat ||
  • nikhātaṁ: This word translates to "that which is established" or "that which is fixed." It signifies stability and permanence.
  • cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness." It represents the essence of being and understanding.
  • yaḥ: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who" or "that." It connects the subject to the action or state described.
  • purusambhṛtaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is endowed with the essence of humanity" or "the one who embodies the qualities of a person." It emphasizes the human aspect of existence.
  • vasūd: This word means "earth" or "the world." It signifies the physical realm and our connection to it.
  • id: This means "this" or "here," indicating presence and immediacy.
  • vapati: This translates to "he who rules" or "he who governs." It refers to authority and control.
  • dāśuṣe: This term means "for the servant" or "for the one who serves." It highlights the importance of service and humility.
  • vajrī: This word means "the one who wields the thunderbolt" or "the powerful one." It symbolizes strength and resilience.
  • suśipro: This translates to "well-formed" or "well-structured." It indicates order and harmony.
  • haryaśva: This term means "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It represents agility and responsiveness.
  • it: This means "thus" or "in this way," indicating a manner of action.
  • karad: This translates to "with hands" or "by action." It emphasizes the importance of doing and creating.
  • indraḥ: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
  • kratvā: This means "with strength" or "with effort." It signifies the importance of willpower and determination.
  • yathā: This translates to "as" or "in the way that," indicating a comparison or manner.
  • vaśat: This means "to be controlled" or "to be governed." It signifies the idea of mastery and influence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the importance of stability, consciousness, and service. It highlights the need for strength and agility in our actions, guided by a sense of purpose and authority. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership roles where one must balance power with responsibility, or in personal development where individuals strive to embody strength and resilience while serving others. It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in authority, but in the ability to uplift and support those around us, fostering a harmonious and well-structured community.
