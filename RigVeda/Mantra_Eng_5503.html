शं नो भवन्तु वाजिनो हवेषु देवताता मित्रद्रवः स्वर्काः |जम्भयन्तोऽहिं वृकं रक्षांसी सनेम्य अस्मद युयवन्न अमीवाः ||
śaṁ no bhavantu vājino haveṣu devatātā mitadravaḥ svarkāḥ | jambhayanto 'hiṁ vṛkaṁ rakṣāṁsi sanemy asmad yuyavann amīvāḥ ||
  • śaṁ no bhavantu: This phrase translates to "May peace be unto us." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and tranquility in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts to promote peace, such as conflict resolution programs or peace marches.
  • vājino haveṣu: This means "may we be strong and powerful." It reflects the desire for strength, both physically and mentally. For example, athletes train rigorously to build their physical strength, while individuals may engage in mental exercises like meditation to enhance their mental resilience.
  • devatātā mitadravaḥ svarkāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the divine essence of the benevolent ones." It suggests a connection to higher powers or deities that guide and protect us. In everyday life, people often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, hoping to connect with a higher purpose or divine presence.
  • jambhayanto 'hiṁ vṛkaṁ: This phrase means "driving away the harmful forces." It signifies the need to protect oneself from negativity and harm. In practical terms, this can relate to personal safety measures, such as self-defense classes or creating a supportive environment that discourages bullying and violence.
  • rakṣāṁsi sanemy: This translates to "we invoke the protective forces." It highlights the importance of seeking protection from external threats. In real life, this can be seen in the use of security systems in homes or communities coming together to support one another in times of crisis.
  • asmad yuyavann amīvāḥ: This means "may we unite against our adversaries." It emphasizes the strength found in unity and collaboration. In modern contexts, this can be observed in social movements where individuals come together to fight for a common cause, such as environmental protection or social justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking peace, strength, divine guidance, protection from harm, and unity against adversities. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal development to community building. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can foster a more harmonious and resilient existence, ultimately leading to a better world for all.
