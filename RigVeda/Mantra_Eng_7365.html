अभि गन्धर्वम् अतृणद अभुध्नेषु रजःस्व ā |इन्द्रो ब्रह्मभ्य इद वृह्दे ||
abhi gandharvam atṛṇad abudhneṣu rajaḥsv ā | indro brahmabhya id vṛdhe ||
  • Mantra: "abhi gandharvam atṛṇad abudhneṣu rajaḥsv ā | indro brahmabhya id vṛdhe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • gandharvam: the celestial musicians or beings associated with the heavens
    • atṛṇad: having been freed or liberated
    • abudhneṣu: in the waters or in the depths
    • rajaḥsv: the essence or the quality of the atmosphere
    • ā: a particle indicating direction or purpose
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms
    • brahmabhya: to the wise or the learned
    • id: this, here
    • vṛdhe: to grow, to increase, or to flourish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine connection and the liberation of celestial beings, suggesting a harmonious relationship between the earthly and the divine.
    • The mention of "gandharvam" indicates the presence of higher consciousness or artistic expression, which can be seen in real life through music, dance, and other forms of art that elevate the human spirit.
    • The reference to "Indra" signifies strength and the ability to overcome challenges, much like how individuals face adversities in life and emerge stronger.
    • The phrase "brahmabhya id vṛdhe" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in fostering growth and prosperity, applicable in educational pursuits and personal development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine support and the liberation of the spirit through artistic expression and wisdom.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, embrace creativity, and connect with the divine to overcome challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that through wisdom and the arts, one can achieve personal growth and contribute positively to society.
