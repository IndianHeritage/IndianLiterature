यस्मान्न ऋते विजयन्ते जनासो यं युध्यमानाऽवसे हवन्ते |यो विश्वस्य प्रतिमानं बभूव यो अच्युताच्युत स जनासा इन्द्रः ||
yasmān na ṛte vijayante janāso yaṁ yudhyamānā avase havante | yo viśvasya pratimānam babhūva yo acyutacyut sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmān: From whom
    • na: Not
    • ṛte: Without
    • vijayante: Victorious
    • janāso: People
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • yudhyamānā: Engaged in battle
    • avase: They invoke
    • havante: Offerings
    • yo: Who
    • viśvasya: Of the universe
    • pratimānam: The embodiment
    • babhūva: Became
    • yo: Who
    • acyutacyut: Unfailing
    • sa: That
    • janāsa: Of the people
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine figure, Indra, who is invoked by people engaged in battle.
    • It emphasizes that without the support of this divine presence, victory is unattainable.
    • Indra is portrayed as the embodiment of universal strength and reliability, symbolizing the power that guides and protects those who seek it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In competitive sports, athletes often invoke a sense of higher purpose or strength, believing that their success is supported by a greater force.
    • In challenging situations, such as exams or job interviews, individuals may seek blessings or guidance, reflecting the belief in a supportive presence that aids in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of divine support in achieving success and overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that while personal effort is crucial, the belief in a higher power can provide the strength and confidence needed to face adversities.
    • In modern life, this can be applicable in various scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in communities, reinforcing the idea that unity and faith can lead to triumph.
