यद् देवाऽ यतयो यथा भुवनानि अपिन्वत | अत्र समुद्र आ गूढम् आ सूर्यं अजभर्तन ||
yad devā yatayo yathā bhuvanāny apinvata | atrā samudra ā gūḻham ā sūryam ajabhartana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • devā: the gods
    • yatayo: the ascetics or those who strive for spiritual knowledge
    • yathā: as
    • bhuvanāny: the worlds or realms
    • apinvata: they enjoy or partake
    • atrā: here
    • samudra: the ocean
    • ā gūḻham: the hidden or concealed
    • ā: from
    • sūryam: the sun
    • ajabhartana: the one who sustains or supports
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of the divine, the ascetics, and the natural world.
    • It suggests that whatever the gods and ascetics enjoy or partake in, it is a reflection of the worlds they inhabit.
    • The mention of the ocean and the sun symbolizes vastness and sustenance, indicating that the divine presence is both hidden and sustaining.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see how the sun sustains life on Earth, much like how spiritual practices sustain the soul.
    • Ascetics often retreat into nature, finding solace and connection with the divine, similar to how one might find peace by the ocean.
    • The idea of enjoying the fruits of one's labor can be seen in how communities come together to celebrate harvests, reflecting the abundance of the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in all aspects of life, from nature to spiritual practices.
    • It encourages individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their connection to the universe and the divine.
    • In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude for the natural world and engaging in practices that promote spiritual growth and community.
