उत ते शुष्मा जिहताम् उत ते आर्चिर उत ते अग्ने शशमानस्य वाजाः |उच्छ्वञ्चस्व नि नम वर्धमान आ त्वाद्य विश्वे वसवः सदन्तु ||
ut te śuṣmā jihatām ut te arcir ut te agne śaśamānasya vājāḥ | uc chvañcasva ni nama vardhamāna ā tvādya viśve vasavaḥ sadantu ||
  • ut te śuṣmā jihatām: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the divine to bestow auspiciousness and prosperity. The word "śuṣmā" refers to something that is radiant or auspicious. In real life, this can be related to seeking blessings for success in endeavors, such as starting a new business or project.
  • ut te arcir: "Arcir" signifies light or illumination. This can be understood as a request for enlightenment and guidance. For example, when faced with difficult decisions, individuals often seek clarity and wisdom, akin to asking for the light of knowledge to guide their path.
  • ut te agne: "Agne" refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of maintaining passion and motivation in one's life, such as pursuing personal goals or overcoming challenges.
  • śaśamānasya vājāḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a request for strength and vigor. "Vājāḥ" refers to the qualities of a horse, which symbolizes power and speed. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of physical fitness or mental resilience, where individuals strive to enhance their strength and capabilities.
  • uc chvañcasva: This can be understood as a call to rise and elevate oneself. It signifies the importance of striving for higher goals and aspirations. For instance, in a professional context, this could relate to seeking promotions or advancements in one's career.
  • ni nama vardhamāna: "Vardhamāna" means to grow or increase. This phrase emphasizes the importance of growth and development in various aspects of life, such as personal relationships, education, or career. It encourages individuals to continuously seek improvement and expansion.
  • ā tvādya viśve vasavaḥ sadantu: This concluding part can be interpreted as a prayer for the well-being of all. "Viśve vasavaḥ" refers to the universal forces of nature and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts, where individuals come together to support each other, fostering a sense of unity and collective growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, enlightenment, strength, and growth. Its significance lies in the universal aspiration for improvement and well-being, both individually and collectively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community support, and the pursuit of knowledge and success. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can strive for a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
