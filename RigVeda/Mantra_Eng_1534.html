अधिवासम् परि मातु: रिहन्न अह तुविग्रेबिह: सत्वभिर याति वि ज्रय: | वयो दधत पद्वते रेरिहत सदानु श्येनी सचते वर्तनीर अह ||
adhīvāsam pari mātū rihann aha tuvigrebhiḥ satvabhir yāti vi jrayaḥ | vayo dadhat padvate rerihat sadānu śyenī sacate vartanīr aha ||
  • adhīvāsam: This term can be understood as "the dwelling" or "the abode." It signifies a place of residence or a state of being.
  • pari: Meaning "around" or "surrounding," this word indicates a sense of completeness or encompassing.
  • mātū: This translates to "of the mother," symbolizing nurturing, care, and the source of life.
  • rihann: This word means "to shine" or "to radiate," suggesting brightness and positivity.
  • aha: This is a pronoun meaning "I" or "my," indicating personal involvement or connection.
  • tuvigrebhiḥ: This term refers to "with strength" or "with vigor," emphasizing power and capability.
  • satvabhir: Meaning "with truth" or "with essence," it highlights the importance of authenticity and integrity.
  • yāti: This translates to "goes" or "moves," indicating progress or movement towards a goal.
  • vi jrayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the heights" or "to the peak," suggesting aspirations and achievements.
  • vayo: Meaning "age" or "time," it reflects the passage of life and experiences.
  • dadhat: This means "to hold" or "to bear," indicating responsibility and support.
  • padvate: This translates to "to walk" or "to progress," symbolizing the journey of life.
  • rerihat: Meaning "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," it emphasizes the importance of sustenance and care.
  • sadānu: This term means "always" or "eternally," indicating continuity and permanence.
  • śyenī: This translates to "like a bird" or "like a falcon," symbolizing freedom, vision, and elevation.
  • sacate: Meaning "to dwell" or "to reside," it signifies a state of being or existence.
  • vartanīr: This word means "in the present" or "in the current state," emphasizing the importance of the now.
  • aha: Again, this is a pronoun meaning "I" or "my," reinforcing personal connection.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, the nurturing aspect of the mother, and the journey of personal growth and aspiration. It emphasizes the importance of truth, strength, and the continuous journey of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, where one seeks to grow and evolve while being grounded in truth and authenticity. It encourages individuals to embrace their journey, nurture their aspirations, and remain connected to their roots, much like how a tree grows strong and tall while being nourished by the earth. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of balance, support, and the pursuit of one's goals with integrity and strength.
