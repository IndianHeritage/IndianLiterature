आ वो वाहिष्ठो वहतु स्तवध्यै रथो वाजाऽऋभुक्षणो अमृतः |अभि त्रिपृष्ठैः सवनेषु सोमैर मदे सुशिप्रा महाभिः पृणध्वम् ||
ā vo vāhiṣṭho vahatu stavadhyai ratho vājā ṛbhukṣaṇo amṛktaḥ | abhi tripṛṣṭhaiḥ savaneṣu somair made suśiprā mahabhiḥ pṛṇadhvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vo: To you all
    • vāhiṣṭho: The best or most excellent
    • vahatu: May he bring
    • stavadhyai: For the praise or worship
    • ratho: Chariot or vehicle
    • vājā: Wealth or abundance
    • ṛbhukṣaṇo: One who is rich or bountiful
    • amṛktaḥ: Immortal or eternal
    • abhi: Towards
    • tripṛṣṭhaiḥ: With three layers or aspects
    • savanesu: In the offerings or sacrifices
    • somair: With soma (a sacred drink)
    • made: In joy or ecstasy
    • suśiprā: Well-prepared or excellent
    • mahabhiḥ: With great abundance
    • pṛṇadhvam: May you be filled or satisfied
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces to bring forth the best and most excellent offerings for worship.
    • The mention of "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, where one seeks wealth and abundance.
    • The term "ṛbhukṣaṇo" refers to the richness of life, suggesting that the divine should bless us with prosperity.
    • "Amṛktaḥ" signifies the eternal nature of the soul, reminding us of our spiritual essence beyond material existence.
    • The phrase "tripṛṣṭhaiḥ savaneṣu somair" emphasizes the importance of offerings in rituals, particularly the sacred soma, which represents divine energy and joy.
    • The concluding part, "suśiprā mahabhiḥ pṛṇadhvam," is a prayer for abundance and fulfillment in life, urging the divine to fill our lives with joy and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, couples often invoke blessings for a prosperous and joyful life together, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • During festivals, people perform rituals to seek divine blessings for wealth and happiness, reflecting the mantra's call for abundance.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on the themes of this mantra to attract positivity and fulfillment in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine blessings for a prosperous and fulfilling life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as rituals, celebrations, and personal growth practices.
    • By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can cultivate a life filled with joy, abundance, and spiritual richness.
