ऋभुक्षणं न वर्तव उक्तेषु तुग्र्यावृधम् | इन्द्रं सोमे सचा सुते ||
ṛbhukṣaṇaṁ na vartava uktheṣu tugryāvṛdham | indraṁ some sacā sute ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ṛbhukṣaṇaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who nourishes" or "the provider of sustenance." It signifies a source of nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
    • na vartava: This phrase translates to "do not let it be obstructed" or "do not let it cease." It emphasizes the importance of continuity and flow in life.
    • uktheṣu: This word means "in the utterances" or "in the spoken words." It highlights the power of speech and intention in manifesting reality.
    • tugryāvṛdham: This can be understood as "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful." It refers to a force that is capable of overcoming obstacles.
    • indraṁ: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the warrior spirit. He represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • some: This term refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
    • sacā: This means "with truth" or "with sincerity," indicating the importance of authenticity in one's actions and intentions.
    • sute: This word translates to "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices," which signifies the act of giving and sharing as a means of connecting with the divine.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and the need to ensure that this nourishment is not obstructed.
    • It highlights the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality, suggesting that what we speak and express can manifest in our lives.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the call for strength and support in overcoming challenges, while the reference to soma emphasizes the pursuit of divine inspiration and immortality.
    • Overall, the mantra encourages a harmonious balance between sustenance, intention, and divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, one might reflect on the importance of maintaining healthy relationships and communication. Just as the mantra suggests nourishing connections, individuals can strive to keep their relationships strong and free from obstruction.
    • In a professional setting, the mantra can be applied to teamwork. Ensuring that all team members are heard and valued can lead to a more productive and harmonious work environment.
    • On a spiritual level, individuals can practice mindfulness and intention-setting, recognizing the power of their words and thoughts in shaping their experiences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nourishment, intention, and divine support in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to maintain a flow of positivity and strength, both within ourselves and in our interactions with others.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life that is rich in meaning, connection, and resilience.
