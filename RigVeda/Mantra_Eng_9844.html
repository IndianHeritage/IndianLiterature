रेभदात्र जनुषा पूर्वो अंगिरा ग्रावाण ऊर्ध्वा अभि चक्षुर अध्वरम् | येभिर विहायाऽभवद् विचक्षणः पाथः सुमेकं स्वधितिर वनन्वति ||
rebhad atra januṣā pūrvo aṅgirā grāvāṇa ūrdhvā abhi cakṣur adhvaram | yebhir vihāyā abhavad vicakṣaṇaḥ pāthaḥ sumekaṁ svadhitir vananvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rebhad: to be nourished or sustained
    • atra: here
    • januṣā: by the body or through the body
    • pūrvo: the ancient or the previous
    • aṅgirā: the divine or the enlightened
    • grāvāṇa: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • ūrdhvā: upward or higher
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • cakṣur: the eye or vision
    • adhvaram: the sacrifice or the ritual
    • yebhir: by whom
    • vihāyā: having abandoned or forsaken
    • abhavad: became or was
    • vicakṣaṇaḥ: wise or perceptive
    • pāthaḥ: the path or the way
    • sumekaṁ: the well-formed or the harmonious
    • svadhitir: self-knowledge or self-awareness
    • vananvati: flourishing or thriving
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nourishment and sustenance that comes from ancient wisdom and divine guidance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of vision and perception in understanding the rituals and sacrifices of life.
    • The phrase "having abandoned" suggests letting go of ignorance or distractions to become wise and perceptive.
    • The mantra encourages the pursuit of a harmonious path that leads to self-awareness and flourishing in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might seek guidance from mentors (the ancient wisdom) to navigate challenges (the rituals) effectively.
    • In personal development, individuals often abandon negative habits (forsaking) to gain clarity and insight (becoming wise).
    • In community building, fostering self-awareness and understanding among members can lead to a thriving environment (flourishing).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of drawing from ancient wisdom and divine guidance to navigate life's challenges.
    • It highlights the need for vision and perception in understanding our paths and making informed decisions.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, professional success, and harmonious community living.
