अवद्यं इव मन्यमाना गुहाकर इन्द्रं माता वीर्येणान्यृष्टम् |अथोद अस्तात स्वयं अत्कं वसाना आ रोडसी अपृणाज जात्यमानः ||
avadyam iva manyamānā guhākar indram mātā vīryeṇā nyṛṣṭam | athod asthāt svayam atkaṁ vasāna ā rodasī apṛṇāj jāyamānaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "avadyam iva manyamānā guhākar indram mātā vīryeṇā nyṛṣṭam | athod asthāt svayam atkaṁ vasāna ā rodasī apṛṇāj jāyamānaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avadyam: unblemished, pure
    • iva: like, as if
    • manyamānā: considering, thinking
    • guhākar: the one who resides in the cave (often referring to the mind or inner self)
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • mātā: mother
    • vīryeṇā: with strength, valor
    • nyṛṣṭam: having been established, set forth
    • athod: then, thereafter
    • asthāt: stood, remained
    • svayam: by oneself
    • atkaṁ: what, which
    • vasāna: dwelling, residing
    • ā: towards, in the direction of
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the heavens and the earth
    • apṛṇāj: did not cover, did not hide
    • jāyamānaḥ: being born, coming into existence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purity and strength of the inner self, represented by the metaphor of a mother.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and nurturing our inner strength, much like a mother nurtures her child.
    • The reference to Indra suggests that this inner strength is powerful and can overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "guhākar" indicates that this strength resides within us, often hidden like a treasure in a cave.
    • It encourages individuals to stand firm in their identity and purpose, not to hide their true selves.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that test their inner strength. This mantra serves as a reminder to tap into that strength, much like a mother draws on her love and resilience for her children.
    • In leadership, a leader must embody the qualities of Indra, showing strength and decisiveness while also nurturing their team, akin to a mother guiding her children.
    • In mental health, recognizing and embracing one's inner self can lead to healing and empowerment, reflecting the mantra's call to not hide one's true nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner strength and purity, encouraging individuals to embrace their true selves and stand firm in their identity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to leadership and mental health, serving as a guiding principle for resilience and authenticity.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true power comes from within, and by nurturing our inner selves, we can navigate the challenges of life with grace and strength.
