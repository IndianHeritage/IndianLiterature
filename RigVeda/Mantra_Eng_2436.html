सोमस्य माऽ तवसं वक्ष्य अग्ने वह्निं चकर्थ विदथे यजध्यै |देवाम् अच् छा दीद्यद युञ्जे अद्रिं शमाय अग्ने तन्वं जुषस्व ||
somasya mā tavasaṁ vakṣy agne vahniṁ cakartha vidathe yajadhyai | devām̐ acchā dīdyad yuñje adriṁ śamāye agne tanvaṁ juṣasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somasya: of Soma (a sacred drink in Vedic rituals)
    • mā: not
    • tavasaṁ: your strength
    • vakṣy: I will speak
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vahniṁ: the fire
    • cakartha: you have created
    • vidathe: in the assembly
    • yajadhyai: for the sacrifice
    • devām̐: the divine
    • acchā: may you shine
    • dīdyad: bright
    • yuñje: may I unite
    • adriṁ: the mountain
    • śamāye: for peace
    • agne: O Agni
    • tanvaṁ: your body
    • juṣasva: may you be pleased
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine connection between the earthly and the celestial.
    • The reference to "Soma" indicates the importance of this sacred drink in rituals, which is believed to bestow immortality and divine qualities.
    • The phrase "I will speak of your strength" suggests a recognition of Agni's power and the importance of acknowledging divine forces in our lives.
    • The mantra emphasizes the creation of fire in the assembly for the purpose of sacrifice, highlighting the communal aspect of worship and the significance of collective spiritual practices.
    • The invocation for Agni to shine brightly and for the speaker to unite with the mountain symbolizes a desire for stability, strength, and peace, drawing parallels to the natural world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire can symbolize the beginning of a new venture, such as a wedding or a significant life event, where the community gathers to witness and participate.
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the modern pursuit of wellness and spiritual experiences, where individuals seek out practices that enhance their physical and mental well-being.
    • In a corporate setting, the idea of uniting for a common goal can be seen in team-building exercises where members come together to create a harmonious and productive work environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring divine forces in our lives, particularly through communal practices and rituals.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the strength and purity that come from unity and the natural world, encouraging us to seek peace and stability in our personal and communal endeavors.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide us in fostering community, enhancing our spiritual practices, and striving for personal growth and harmony.
