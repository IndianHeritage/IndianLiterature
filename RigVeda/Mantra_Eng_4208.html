माया वाम मित्रावरुणा दिवि श्रिता सूर्यो ज्योतिष् चरति चित्रम् आयुधम् |तम अभ्रेण वृष्ट्या गूढतो दिवि पार्जन्य द्रप्सा मधुमन्त इरते ||
māyā vām mitrāvaruṇā divi śritā sūryo jyotiś carati citram āyudham | tam abhreṇa vṛṣṭyā gūhatho divi parjanya drapsā madhumanta īrate ||
  • māyā vām mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase can be interpreted as "O divine forces, you who are the friends of the universe." Here, "māyā" refers to the divine illusion or the creative power, while "mitrāvaruṇā" signifies the duality of friendship and cosmic order, represented by the deities Mitra and Varuna.
  • divi śritā sūryo jyotiś: This translates to "the sun, the source of light, who resides in the heavens." The sun is often seen as a symbol of knowledge, enlightenment, and life-giving energy. In real life, the sun is essential for agriculture, health, and the overall well-being of living beings.
  • carati citram āyudham: This means "moves with a wondrous weapon." The "wondrous weapon" can be interpreted as the various forces of nature that work in harmony to sustain life. For example, the balance of ecosystems, the water cycle, and the interplay of different elements in nature can be seen as these weapons that protect and nurture life.
  • tam abhreṇa vṛṣṭyā: This phrase translates to "that which is hidden in the clouds." It refers to the rain and the nourishing aspects of nature that are often concealed. In real life, rain is crucial for agriculture and sustaining water bodies, which in turn support life.
  • gūhatho divi parjanya: This means "the hidden one in the sky, the rain-bringer." Parjanya is a deity associated with rain and fertility. The significance of rain in agriculture cannot be overstated; it is vital for crop growth and food security.
  • drapsā madhumanta īrate: This translates to "may the sweet nectar flow." This phrase symbolizes abundance and prosperity. In real life, the concept of "nectar" can be related to the fruits of labor, the rewards of hard work, and the sweetness of life that comes from harmony with nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and human existence. It highlights the importance of harmony with the environment and the blessings that come from it, such as sunlight and rain. In real life, this mantra can be applied to our understanding of sustainability, agriculture, and the need to respect and nurture our natural resources. By recognizing the divine aspects of nature and our dependence on them, we can foster a more balanced and fulfilling life.
