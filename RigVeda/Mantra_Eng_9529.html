हिमेवं पर्णां मुषिता वनानि बृहस्पतिनाकृपयद वलो गाः |अनानुकृत्यम अपुनश्चकार यात सूर्यामासां मिथ उच्छरातः ||
himeva parṇā muṣitā vanāni bṛhaspatinākṛpayad valo gāḥ | anānukṛtyam apunaś cakāra yāt sūryāmāsā mitha uccarātaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "himeva parṇā muṣitā vanāni bṛhaspatinākṛpayad valo gāḥ | anānukṛtyam apunaś cakāra yāt sūryāmāsā mitha uccarātaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • himeva: indeed, like frost
    • parṇā: leaves
    • muṣitā: hidden or concealed
    • vanāni: forests
    • bṛhaspatinā: by the grace of Brihaspati (the deity of wisdom and knowledge)
    • akṛpayad: without any mercy or compassion
    • valo: the great or powerful
    • gāḥ: cows or wealth
    • anānukṛtyam: without any reciprocation or return
    • apunaś: again or once more
    • cakāra: did or performed
    • yāt: that which
    • sūryām: to the sun
    • āsā: hope or expectation
    • mitha: together or mutually
    • uccarātaḥ: from the high or elevated place
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the natural world, particularly the hidden aspects of nature, represented by frost-covered leaves and concealed forests.
    • Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom, is invoked, suggesting that knowledge and understanding are essential in navigating the complexities of life.
    • The mention of cows symbolizes wealth and prosperity, indicating that true wealth comes from nature and the blessings of the divine.
    • The phrase "without any reciprocation" implies that the gifts of nature and wisdom are given freely, and it is our duty to recognize and appreciate them.
    • The reference to the sun signifies hope and enlightenment, suggesting that through knowledge and understanding, we can rise above challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the cycles of nature and the importance of sustainable practices can lead to better yields and a healthier environment.
    • In personal development, seeking knowledge and wisdom without expecting immediate returns can lead to profound growth and enlightenment.
    • In community living, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of compassion can foster harmony and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the gifts of the natural world and the importance of knowledge in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both individually and collectively.
