आ ते पितर मरुतां सुम्नम एतु मा नः सूर्यस्य संदृशो युयोथाḥ |अभि नो वीरो अर्वति क्षमेत प्र जायेमहि रुद्र प्रजाभिः ||
ā te pitar marutāṁ sumnam etu mā naḥ sūryasya saṁdṛśo yuyothāḥ | abhi no vīro arvati kṣameta pra jāyemahi rudra prajābhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • pitar: O fathers (ancestors)
    • marutāṁ: Of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • sumnam: Auspiciousness
    • etu: May come
    • mā: Not
    • naḥ: To us
    • sūryasya: Of the Sun
    • saṁdṛśo: Of the one who is seen
    • yuyothāḥ: May you fight
    • abhi: For us
    • no: Our
    • vīro: Heroes
    • arvati: May they be strong
    • kṣameta: May they forgive
    • pra: For
    • jāyemahi: We may be born
    • rudra: O Rudra (a form of Shiva)
    • prajābhiḥ: With progeny
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the ancestors and the Maruts, invoking their blessings for auspiciousness and strength.
    • The phrase "ā te pitar marutāṁ sumnam etu" expresses a desire for the blessings of the ancestors and the storm deities to come to us, ensuring a prosperous and harmonious life.
    • "mā naḥ sūryasya saṁdṛśo yuyothāḥ" suggests a plea for protection and strength, asking the divine forces to fight for us against adversities, much like how the sun dispels darkness.
    • The invocation of "vīro arvati kṣameta" emphasizes the importance of courage and forgiveness, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges in life.
    • Finally, "pra jāyemahi rudra prajābhiḥ" signifies a wish for the birth of strong progeny, highlighting the importance of lineage and the continuation of values through generations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In family gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to honor ancestors, seeking their guidance and blessings for the family’s well-being.
    • In times of personal or communal struggle, such as during natural disasters or conflicts, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from both divine and ancestral forces.
    • Parents may reflect on this mantra when raising their children, emphasizing the values of courage, forgiveness, and the importance of a strong lineage.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine and ancestral support in our lives, emphasizing the importance of auspiciousness, strength, and the continuity of values through generations.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to connect individuals with their roots, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a powerful reminder of the values we uphold and the strength we draw from our heritage, guiding us through challenges and towards a prosperous future.
