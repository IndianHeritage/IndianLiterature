वद्मा हि सूनो अस्य अद्मसद्वा चक्रे अग्निर जनुषाज्मान्नम् |स तवं न ऊर्जसन ऊर्जं धा राजेव जेर अवृके क्षेष्य अन्तः ||
vadmā hi sūno asy admasadvā cakre agnir januṣājmānnam | sa tvaṁ na ūrjasana ūrjaṁ dhā rājeva jer avṛke kṣeṣy antaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vadmā: We speak or we declare
    • hi: indeed
    • sūno: of the son or offspring
    • asy: of this
    • admasadvā: the one who is born from the earth
    • cakre: has created or established
    • agnir: fire
    • januṣā: by birth or origin
    • ajmānnam: the one who is born
    • sa: he
    • tvaṁ: you
    • na: not
    • ūrjasana: of strength or energy
    • ūrjaṁ: energy or vigor
    • dhā: hold or maintain
    • rājeva: like a king
    • jer: may you be
    • avṛke: in the midst of
    • kṣeṣy: may you be protected
    • antaḥ: within
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of speech and the importance of declaring one's intentions and desires.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine (represented by fire) and the earthly realm, suggesting that our words can create reality.
    • The invocation of strength and energy indicates a call for empowerment and protection in one's endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, affirmations are a practical application of this mantra. By speaking positively about oneself, individuals can manifest their goals and aspirations.
    • In leadership, a leader who articulates a clear vision can inspire and mobilize their team, much like the invocation of strength in the mantra.
    • In conflict resolution, the act of openly communicating intentions can lead to understanding and harmony, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of speech and intention in shaping our reality.
    • It serves as a reminder that our words carry power, and by aligning our speech with our desires, we can create a more fulfilling life.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal growth to leadership, the principles embedded in this mantra can guide us towards achieving our goals and fostering positive relationships.
