साकं जाताः सुभवः साकम् उक्षिताḥ श्रीये चिद ā प्रतरं वावृद्धुर नरः |विरोकिणः सूर्यस्येव रश्मयः शुभं याताम् अनु रथा अवृत्सत ||
sākaṁ jātāḥ subhvaḥ sākam ukṣitāḥ śriye cid ā prataraṁ vāvṛdhur naraḥ | virokiṇaḥ sūryasyeva raśmayaḥ śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sākaṁ: Together, in unity
    • jātāḥ: Born, created
    • subhvaḥ: Auspicious, good
    • sākam: Together, in harmony
    • ukṣitāḥ: Nourished, cultivated
    • śriye: Prosperity, wealth
    • cid: Consciousness, awareness
    • ā: Towards, in the direction of
    • prataraṁ: Advancement, progress
    • vāvṛdhur: They have grown, they have increased
    • naraḥ: Human beings, people
    • virokiṇaḥ: Radiant, shining
    • sūryasyeva: Like the sun
    • raśmayaḥ: Rays, beams
    • śubhaṁ: Auspicious, good
    • yātām: May we go, may we proceed
    • anu: Following, in accordance with
    • rathā: Chariots, vehicles
    • avṛtsata: May they be protected, may they be shielded
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective growth among individuals.
    • It suggests that when people come together in harmony, they can achieve prosperity and progress.
    • The imagery of the sun's rays symbolizes enlightenment and the positive energy that comes from collaboration.
    • Just as the sun nourishes life on Earth, collective efforts can lead to a flourishing community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, teamwork can lead to innovative solutions and increased productivity, reflecting the mantra's call for unity.
    • Community service projects, where individuals come together to help those in need, exemplify the idea of collective growth and prosperity.
    • In family settings, supporting each other through challenges can lead to stronger bonds and a more harmonious environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.
    • Its significance lies in the belief that when individuals work together, they can create a positive impact on their surroundings.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, community development, and overall well-being.
