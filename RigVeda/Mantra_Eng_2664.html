वाजी वाजेषु धीयते 'ध्वरेषु प्रणीयते |विप्रो यज्ञस्य साधनः ||
vājī vājeṣu dhīyate 'dhvareṣu pra ṇīyate | vipro yajñasya sādhanaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vājī: One who is strong, powerful, or capable.
    • vājeṣu: In the context of strength or power.
    • dhīyate: Is meditated upon or contemplated.
    • 'dhvareṣu: In the context of offerings or sacrifices.
    • pra ṇīyate: Is directed or guided.
    • vipro: The learned or wise person.
    • yajñasya: Of the sacrifice or ritual.
    • sādhanaḥ: The means or method of accomplishment.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and wisdom in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "vājī vājeṣu" suggests that a person who is strong and capable is essential for the successful execution of rituals.
    • "dhīyate 'dhvareṣu" indicates that contemplation and meditation on these strengths are crucial during the performance of sacrifices.
    • The wise person ("vipro") plays a significant role in guiding the rituals, ensuring that they are performed correctly and meaningfully.
    • Overall, the mantra highlights the interplay between strength, wisdom, and the proper execution of sacred practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader (vājī) who is capable of guiding their team (vipro) through challenges (yajñasya) is essential for achieving success (sādhanaḥ).
    • During community events, the planning and execution require both strength (vājī) and thoughtful consideration (dhīyate) to ensure that everything goes smoothly (pra ṇīyate).
    • In personal development, individuals often meditate on their strengths (dhīyate) to overcome obstacles (dhvareṣu) and achieve their goals (sādhanaḥ).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of combining strength and wisdom in all endeavors, particularly in spiritual and communal practices.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their capabilities and the significance of guidance in achieving meaningful outcomes.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more effective leadership, successful teamwork, and personal growth.
