त्वं विक्षु प्रदिवः सीद आषु क्रत्वा रथीर अभवो वार्याणाम् |अत इनोषि विधते चिकीर्त्वो व्य आanusग जातवेदो वसूनि ||
tvaṁ vikṣu pradivaḥ sīda āsu kratvā rathīr abhavo vāryāṇām | ata inoṣi vidhate cikitvo vy ānuṣag jātavedo vasūni ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • vikṣu - This term can mean "the one who is all-pervading" or "the one who is everywhere," suggesting a universal presence.
  • pradivaḥ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is the source of all," emphasizing the idea of a creator or originator.
  • sīda - Meaning "to sit" or "to reside," it implies a sense of stability and presence.
  • āsu - This word means "quickly" or "immediately," indicating urgency or promptness.
  • kratvā - Referring to "with strength" or "with power," it highlights the importance of energy and effort in actions.
  • rathīr - This term means "charioteer," symbolizing guidance and control in one's journey through life.
  • abhavo - This translates to "may you become," suggesting a potential for transformation or evolution.
  • vāryāṇām - Meaning "of the strong" or "of the powerful," it emphasizes the qualities of strength and resilience.
  • ata - This word means "therefore" or "hence," indicating a conclusion or result.
  • inoṣi - This can be interpreted as "you bestow" or "you grant," suggesting a divine act of giving.
  • vidhate - Meaning "to create" or "to establish," it emphasizes the act of formation or construction.
  • cikitvo - This term refers to "wisdom" or "intelligence," highlighting the importance of knowledge in decision-making.
  • vy - This can mean "in all directions" or "everywhere," reinforcing the idea of omnipresence.
  • ānuṣag - Meaning "to follow" or "to pursue," it suggests a journey or path that one takes.
  • jātavedo - This term refers to "the one who knows all births," symbolizing a deep understanding of life and existence.
  • vasūni - Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it indicates the blessings of abundance and success.

In summary, this mantra addresses a divine presence that is all-pervading and powerful, urging it to reside among us and guide us with strength and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in our lives, particularly in our pursuits and endeavors. The invocation of the charioteer symbolizes the need for direction and control in our journeys, while the references to strength and prosperity highlight the qualities we should aspire to cultivate.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek guidance and wisdom in their decisions, much like a charioteer guiding a chariot.
  • In leadership, it emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in guiding teams or organizations towards success.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to connect with the divine for support and blessings in our endeavors.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence and guidance in our lives, urging us to harness our inner strength and wisdom to navigate our paths effectively.
