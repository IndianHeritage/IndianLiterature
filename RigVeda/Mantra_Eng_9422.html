स द्विबन्धुर वैतरणो यष्टा सबरधुं धेनुम अश्वं दुधाध्यै |सं यन् मित्रावरुणा वृञ्ज उक्थैर ज्येष्ठेभिर आर्यमणं वरूथैः ||
sa dvibandhur vaitaraṇo yaṣṭā sabardhuṁ dhenum asvaṁ duhadhyai | saṁ yan mitrāvaruṇā vṛñja ukthair jyeṣṭhebhir aryamaṇaṁ varūthaiḥ ||
  • sa dvibandhur vaitaraṇo: This phrase can be translated as "He who is bound by two (or has two bonds) is the Vaitaraṇa." The Vaitaraṇa is often associated with the river that separates the living from the dead, symbolizing the transition between life and death.
  • yaṣṭā sabardhuṁ dhenum asvaṁ duhadhyai: This part means "the one who offers the sacrifice, who has a cow and a horse, and who milks them." Here, the cow and horse symbolize wealth and strength, respectively. The act of milking represents the nurturing of resources and prosperity.
  • saṁ yan mitrāvaruṇā vṛñja: This translates to "He who is associated with Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship, order, and cosmic law. Their mention emphasizes the importance of harmony and balance in life.
  • ukthair jyeṣṭhebhir aryamaṇaṁ varūthaiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with the elder ones, the Aryaman and Varuna." Aryaman is a deity associated with social order and hospitality, reinforcing the idea of community and support among individuals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the balance between material wealth and spiritual harmony. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to the significance of maintaining relationships, nurturing resources, and upholding social order. For instance, in a business setting, fostering good relationships with partners (Mitra) and adhering to ethical practices (Varuna) can lead to sustainable success. Additionally, the mantra serves as a reminder to balance personal ambitions with communal responsibilities, ensuring that one's actions contribute positively to society as a whole.
