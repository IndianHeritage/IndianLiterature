तनूनापाद ऋतं यते मध्वा यज्ञः सम अज्यते |दधत सहस्रिणीर इषः ||
tanūnapād ṛtaṁ yate madhvā yajñaḥ sam ajyate | dadhat sahasriṇīr iṣaḥ ||
  • tanūnapād: This term can be broken down into two parts: "tanū" meaning body or form, and "napād" which can be interpreted as "without feet" or "without support." This suggests a state of being that transcends physical limitations.
  • ṛtaṁ: This word signifies truth, order, or cosmic law. In the context of the mantra, it emphasizes the importance of adhering to universal principles and righteousness.
  • yate: This translates to "striving" or "effort." It indicates the necessity of active engagement in the pursuit of truth and righteousness.
  • madhvā: This word means "sweetness" or "honey." It symbolizes the rewards of righteous actions and the sweetness of life that comes from living in harmony with the universe.
  • yajñaḥ: This term refers to sacrifice or ritual offering. In a broader sense, it represents the act of giving and the importance of selflessness in achieving spiritual and material goals.
  • sam ajyate: This phrase means "is performed together" or "is done in unity." It highlights the collective aspect of rituals and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • dadhat: This means "to hold" or "to bear." It suggests the responsibility of individuals to uphold and maintain the values of truth and righteousness.
  • sahasriṇīr: This term can be interpreted as "thousands" or "many." It indicates abundance and the multiplicity of efforts required to achieve a higher state of being.
  • iṣaḥ: This word means "desire" or "wish." It reflects the aspirations and intentions that drive individuals towards their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra "tanūnapād ṛtaṁ yate madhvā yajñaḥ sam ajyate | dadhat sahasriṇīr iṣaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of striving for truth and righteousness through collective efforts and selfless actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, or even in personal relationships where mutual support and shared values lead to a harmonious existence. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true fulfillment comes from living in accordance with universal principles and contributing positively to the world around us. By embodying these ideals, individuals can experience the 'sweetness' of life and foster a sense of unity and purpose within their communities.
