अव्यो वारे परि प्रियो हरिर वनेषु सिदति |रेभो वनुष्यते मती ||
avyo vāre pari priyo harir vaneṣu sīdati | rebho vanuṣyate matī ||
  • Mantra: "avyo vāre pari priyo harir vaneṣu sīdati | rebho vanuṣyate matī ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The beloved one, the divine, resides in the forests; the mind is inspired by the divine presence."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • avyo: Beloved or dear.
    • vāre: In the context of protection or shelter.
    • pari: Around or surrounding.
    • priyo: Loved or cherished.
    • harir: Referring to the divine or God, often associated with Vishnu.
    • vaneṣu: In the forests or natural surroundings.
    • sīdati: Resides or dwells.
    • rebho: A term that can imply inspiration or divine influence.
    • vanuṣyate: To be inspired or to have a mind that is influenced.
    • matī: Mind or intellect.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the presence of the divine in nature, particularly in forests, which can be seen as a metaphor for the purity and tranquility found in natural settings.
    • The idea of the divine residing in the forests suggests that nature is a sacred space where one can connect with higher consciousness.
    • The phrase "the mind is inspired by the divine presence" indicates that being in tune with nature can lead to spiritual awakening and intellectual clarity.
    • For example, many people find solace and inspiration while hiking in the mountains or walking through a serene forest, where they feel a connection to something greater than themselves.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in modern life by encouraging individuals to spend time in nature, which can lead to mental clarity and emotional healing.
    • Practices such as forest bathing (Shinrin-yoku) have gained popularity, highlighting the therapeutic benefits of immersing oneself in natural environments.
    • Incorporating moments of mindfulness in nature can enhance creativity and problem-solving abilities, as many artists and thinkers have found inspiration in the tranquility of the outdoors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the divine presence in nature and the importance of connecting with it for personal growth and inspiration.
    • By recognizing the sacredness of our natural surroundings, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us.
    • This mantra encourages us to seek out moments of peace and reflection in nature, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
