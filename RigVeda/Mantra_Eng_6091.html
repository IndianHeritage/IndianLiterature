वेमि त्वा पूषन्न ऋञ्जसे वेमि स्तोतव आगृह्णे |न तस्य वेम्य अरणं हि तद वसो तुषे पज्राय साम्ने ||
vemi tvā pūṣann ṛñjase vemi stotava āghṛṇe | na tasya vemy araṇaṁ hi tad vaso stuṣe pajrāya sāmne ||
  • Vemi tvā pūṣann ṛñjase: This phrase can be translated as "I invoke you, O Pusha, the nourisher." Here, "Pusha" refers to a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen as a call for sustenance and support in our daily lives, whether it be physical nourishment or emotional and spiritual support.
  • Vemi stotava āghṛṇe: This translates to "I invoke you, who is worthy of praise." This line emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who provide us with support and nourishment. In a practical sense, this can relate to expressing gratitude towards family, friends, mentors, or even nature for the sustenance they provide.
  • Na tasya vemy araṇaṁ hi: This means "I do not seek refuge in anyone else." It signifies a deep trust and reliance on the divine or the source of nourishment. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as having faith in one's own abilities and the support system around us, rather than seeking validation from external sources.
  • Tad vaso stuṣe pajrāya sāmne: This translates to "May that which is yours be praised and may it bring us prosperity." This line reflects the desire for abundance and well-being, not just for oneself but for the community as a whole. In real life, this can be seen in communal efforts to support local businesses, share resources, and uplift one another.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, gratitude, trust, and community. It serves as a reminder to acknowledge the sources of our sustenance, whether they are divine, familial, or communal. In a world where individualism often prevails, this mantra encourages us to foster connections and express gratitude, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous life. Its significance can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in family gatherings, community service, or personal reflection, where we recognize and appreciate the support we receive from others.
