जघने चोदा एषां वि शक्तानि नरो यमुः |पुत्रकृतhe न जनयः ||
jaghane coda eṣāṁ vi sakthāni naro yamuḥ | putrakṛthe na janayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jaghane: In the context of the mantra, it refers to the act of creating or generating.
    • coda: This can be interpreted as a directive or command.
    • eṣāṁ: This means "of these" or "of those," indicating a specific group or category.
    • vi: This is a prefix that can mean "in various ways" or "differently."
    • sakthāni: This translates to "powers" or "strengths," referring to the capabilities or energies present.
    • naro: This means "man" or "human," indicating the subject of the mantra.
    • yamuḥ: This can be interpreted as "to be born" or "to come into existence."
    • putrakṛthe: This refers to "for the sake of sons" or "for progeny," emphasizing the importance of offspring.
    • na: This means "not," indicating negation.
    • janayaḥ: This translates to "to generate" or "to produce."
  • Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks about the creative powers inherent in humans and the importance of generating life, particularly in the context of progeny.
    • It suggests that there are various strengths and capabilities within individuals that can lead to the creation of life.
    • However, it also implies a cautionary note that one should not generate life without purpose or intention, especially in the context of having children.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the decision to have children is often accompanied by considerations of financial stability, emotional readiness, and the ability to provide a nurturing environment.
    • Couples may reflect on their strengths and capabilities before deciding to start a family, ensuring they are prepared for the responsibilities that come with parenthood.
    • In many cultures, there is a strong emphasis on the importance of lineage and the legacy one leaves behind, which aligns with the mantra's focus on progeny.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of understanding one's strengths and the responsibilities that come with creating life.
    • It serves as a reminder that the act of generating life should be approached with intention and care, ensuring that future generations are brought into a world where they can thrive.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals and couples in their family planning decisions, encouraging them to reflect on their capabilities and the environment they wish to create for their children.
