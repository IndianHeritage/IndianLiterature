आ सिम अरोहत स्यमा भवन्तीः पतिक चिकीत्वान रयिविद रयीणाम् |प्र नीलपृष्ठो अतसस्य धासेस ता आवासयत पुरुधप्रतीकः ||
ā sīm arohat suyamā bhavantīḥ patiś cikitvān rayivid rayīṇām | pra nīlapṛṣṭho atasasya dhāses tā avāsayat purudhapratīkaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from, out of
    • sīm: boundary, limit
    • arohat: may ascend, rise
    • suyamā: well-formed, auspicious
    • bhavantīḥ: may become, may manifest
    • patiś: lord, master
    • cikitvān: knowing, wise
    • rayivid: wealth, prosperity
    • rayīṇām: of the wealthy, of riches
    • pra: forth, forward
    • nīlapṛṣṭho: blue-backed, possibly referring to the sky or a deity
    • atasasya: of the earth, of the ground
    • dhāses: may support, may uphold
    • tā: those
    • avāsayat: may dwell, may reside
    • purudhapratīkaḥ: having many forms, diverse manifestations
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the ascent and manifestation of auspiciousness and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in achieving wealth and success.
    • The reference to "blue-backed" could symbolize the vastness of the sky or divine presence, suggesting that prosperity is supported by higher powers.
    • The phrase "may dwell" indicates a desire for these auspicious qualities to take residence in one's life.
    • Overall, it reflects a holistic view of life where material wealth is intertwined with spiritual wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a company that values ethical practices and knowledge is more likely to achieve sustainable success.
    • Individuals who seek personal growth through education and self-awareness often find greater fulfillment and prosperity in their lives.
    • Communities that foster collaboration and support among members tend to thrive economically and socially.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of wisdom, prosperity, and auspiciousness.
    • It teaches that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses knowledge and ethical living.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a balanced and fulfilling existence, where success is achieved through integrity and wisdom.
