यस्मिन वृक्षे सुपलाशे देवैः सम्पिबते यमः |अत्र नो विश्वपतिः पिता पुराणाम् अनु वेनति ||
yasmin vṛkṣe supalāśe devaiḥ sampibate yamaḥ | atrā no viśpatiḥ pitā purāṇām̐ anu venati ||
  • Mantra: "yasmin vṛkṣe supalāśe devaiḥ sampibate yamaḥ | atrā no viśpatiḥ pitā purāṇām̐ anu venati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in which
    • vṛkṣe: tree
    • supalāśe: well-nourished or well-protected
    • devaiḥ: by the gods
    • sampibate: is being enjoyed or partaken
    • yamaḥ: Yama, the god of death
    • atrā: here
    • no: for us
    • viśpatiḥ: lord of the universe
    • pitā: father
    • purāṇām̐: of the ancients
    • anu: following
    • venati: leads or guides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a sacred tree that is nourished and protected by the divine.
    • It suggests that Yama, the god of death, partakes in the blessings of this tree.
    • The invocation of the "lord of the universe" as a father figure indicates a deep connection to the divine and the ancient wisdom.
    • The tree symbolizes life, nourishment, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, trees are seen as sacred and are often associated with life and spirituality. For instance, the Bodhi tree in Buddhism is revered as the place where Siddhartha attained enlightenment.
    • In modern environmental movements, the protection of trees and forests is crucial for sustaining life on Earth, reflecting the idea of a "well-nourished" environment.
    • The concept of Yama as a guide can be related to the idea of mortality and the importance of living a life that is in harmony with nature and the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, divine protection, and the wisdom of the ancients.
    • It serves as a reminder of our connection to the environment and the importance of nurturing it for future generations.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek guidance from the divine and to live in harmony with the world around us, recognizing the sacredness of life and death.
