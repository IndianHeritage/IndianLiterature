पुनर नः पितरो मनो ददातु दैव्यो जनः |जीवं व्रातं सचेमहि ||
punar naḥ pitaro mano dadātu daivyo janaḥ | jīvaṁ vrātaṁ sacemahi ||
  • Mantra: "punar naḥ pitaro mano dadātu daivyo janaḥ | jīvaṁ vrātaṁ sacemahi ||"
  • Translation: "May the divine beings grant us the wisdom of our ancestors; may we live a life of righteousness."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • punar: again, anew
    • naḥ: our
    • pitaro: ancestors, forefathers
    • mano: mind, intellect
    • dadātu: may grant, may bestow
    • daivyo: divine, celestial
    • janaḥ: beings, people
    • jīvaṁ: life
    • vrātaṁ: righteous, virtuous
    • sacemahi: may we attain, may we achieve
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and guidance from our ancestors. In many cultures, including Indian traditions, ancestors are revered for their knowledge and experiences.
    • The phrase "punar naḥ pitaro mano dadātu" suggests a desire for the divine to renew or enhance our understanding and intellect, drawing from the wisdom of those who came before us.
    • The second part, "jīvaṁ vrātaṁ sacemahi," highlights the aspiration to live a life that is not only long but also virtuous and aligned with righteousness. This can be related to the concept of Dharma in Hindu philosophy, which emphasizes living in accordance with moral and ethical principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, children often look up to their parents and grandparents for guidance. This mantra can be a reminder to seek their wisdom and learn from their experiences.
    • In professional life, mentors can be seen as modern-day ancestors. Seeking their advice can lead to better decision-making and a more righteous path in one's career.
    • In community settings, elders often hold a wealth of knowledge about traditions and values. Engaging with them can help preserve cultural heritage and promote a virtuous society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of generations. It encourages us to honor our ancestors by seeking their wisdom and applying it to our lives.
    • In today's fast-paced world, where individualism often overshadows collective wisdom, this mantra calls for a return to valuing the insights of those who have walked the path before us.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes the significance of living a life rooted in righteousness, which can lead to personal fulfillment and a harmonious society.
