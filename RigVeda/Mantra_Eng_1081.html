प्र यद अग्निः सहस्वतो विश्वतो यन्ति भानवः |अप नः शोशुचद अगम् ||
pra yad agneḥ sahasvato viśvato yanti bhānavaḥ | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • Mantra: "pra yad agneḥ sahasvato viśvato yanti bhānavaḥ | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||"
  • Translation: "As the rays of the powerful Agni (Fire) spread in all directions, may they remove our sins."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "outward," indicating movement or expansion.
    • yad: Meaning "that" or "which," referring to the rays or qualities of Agni.
    • agneḥ: The genitive form of "Agni," meaning "of Fire," which is a significant deity in Vedic literature.
    • sahasvato: Meaning "powerful" or "mighty," describing the strength of Agni.
    • viśvato: Meaning "from all sides" or "in all directions," indicating the omnipresence of Agni's light.
    • yanti: Meaning "go" or "move," suggesting the action of the rays spreading out.
    • bhānavaḥ: Meaning "rays" or "light," referring to the illuminating aspect of Agni.
    • apa: Meaning "away" or "from us," indicating a desire to remove something.
    • naḥ: Meaning "our," indicating possession or belonging.
    • śośucad: Meaning "to cleanse" or "to purify," referring to the act of removing impurities.
    • agham: Meaning "sin" or "fault," referring to negative actions or thoughts.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek to cleanse ourselves of negative experiences or thoughts. Just as the rays of Agni spread light, we can seek knowledge and wisdom to illuminate our paths and dispel ignorance.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the mantra can be applied in situations where individuals strive for personal growth. For instance, someone overcoming addiction may visualize the powerful rays of Agni helping them to shed their past and move towards a brighter future.
    • In a community context, this mantra can inspire collective efforts to remove societal sins, such as discrimination or injustice, by spreading awareness and promoting unity and understanding.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Agni, the divine fire, as a purifying force. It emphasizes the importance of seeking enlightenment and cleansing oneself of sins. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to pursue personal and communal growth, to seek knowledge, and to strive for purity in thoughts and actions. By embracing the qualities of Agni, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious existence, free from the burdens of negativity and ignorance.
