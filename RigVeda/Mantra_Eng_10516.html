इन्द्र क्षत्रम् अभि वामम् ओजो 'जायथा वृषभ चर्षणीनाम् | अपानुदो जनम् अमित्रयन्तम् उरुं देवभ्यो अकृत्नोरु लोकम् ||
indra kṣatram abhi vāmam ojo 'jāyathā vṛṣabha carṣaṇīnām | apānudo janam amitrayantam uruṁ devebhyo akṛṇor u lokam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Kṣatram: Represents the warrior class or the principle of protection and governance.
  • Abhi: Means 'towards' or 'in the direction of', indicating a focus or intention.
  • Vāmam: Translates to 'the left' or 'the auspicious', often associated with positive outcomes.
  • Ojo: Refers to 'energy' or 'vitality', essential for action and achievement.
  • 'Jāyathā: Means 'may be born' or 'may arise', indicating the potential for growth and development.
  • Vṛṣabha: Symbolizes strength and might, often associated with the bull, a powerful animal in Vedic culture.
  • Carṣaṇīnām: Refers to 'the powerful' or 'the strong', emphasizing the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
  • Apānudo: Means 'to remove' or 'to dispel', indicating the act of overcoming obstacles.
  • Janam: Refers to 'people' or 'humanity', highlighting the collective aspect of society.
  • Amitrayantam: Translates to 'the enemy' or 'those who oppose', indicating the presence of challenges or adversaries.
  • Uruṁ: Means 'vast' or 'great', suggesting the expansive nature of the universe or challenges.
  • Devebhyo: Refers to 'the gods', indicating divine support or intervention.
  • Akṛṇor: Means 'to create' or 'to establish', emphasizing the act of building or forming something new.
  • U Lokam: Translates to 'this world' or 'the earthly realm', grounding the mantra in the physical reality.

This mantra invokes the strength and protection of Indra, the king of gods, to overcome adversities and challenges faced by humanity. It emphasizes the importance of energy and vitality in achieving success and the need for divine support in the face of opposition.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from Indra's strength to guide their teams through challenges.
  • Conflict Resolution: The mantra serves as a reminder to dispel negativity and work towards harmony in communities.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use the energy and vitality symbolized in the mantra to pursue their goals and aspirations.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, protection, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges. It encourages individuals and communities to harness their inner strength and seek guidance from higher powers to create a better world.
