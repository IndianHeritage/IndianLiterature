ये द्रप्सा इव रोडसी धामन्त्य अनु वृष्टिभिः |उत्सं दूहन्तो अक्षितम् ||
ye drapsā iva rodasī dhamanty anu vṛṣṭibhiḥ | utsaṁ duhanto akṣitam ||
  • Mantra: "ye drapsā iva rodasī dhamanty anu vṛṣṭibhiḥ | utsaṁ duhanto akṣitam ||"
  • Translation: "Those who, like the clouds, move in the sky, bringing forth rain, are the ones who nourish the earth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • drapsā: Like clouds
    • iva: As if
    • rodasī: In the sky
    • dhamanty: Move or bring forth
    • anu: Following or after
    • vṛṣṭibhiḥ: With rains
    • utsaṁ: Nourish or sustain
    • duhanto: Those who bring forth
    • akṣitam: The earth or the unyielding
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between clouds and the forces that nourish the earth.
    • Clouds are essential for rain, which is vital for agriculture and sustaining life.
    • In a broader sense, it symbolizes the importance of nurturing and providing for the community.
    • For example, just as clouds bring rain to nourish crops, individuals in a society must contribute to the welfare of others.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agriculture, farmers rely on rain for their crops. This mantra can inspire farmers to respect and care for nature, understanding their dependence on it.
    • In community service, individuals can take this mantra as a call to action, encouraging them to support those in need, much like clouds support the earth.
    • In environmental conservation, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of protecting natural resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing and supporting one another, akin to how clouds nourish the earth with rain.
    • It teaches us the value of community, cooperation, and the responsibility we have towards each other and the environment.
    • In essence, it is a reminder that our actions can have a profound impact on the world around us, encouraging us to be like the clouds that bring sustenance and life.
