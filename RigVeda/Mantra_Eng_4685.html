अभूर् एको रयिपते रयीणाम् आ हस्तयोः अधिता इन्द्र कृष्टिः |वि तोके अप्सु तनये च सूरे 'वोचन्त कार्षाणयो विवाचः ||
abhūr eko rayipate rayīṇām ā hastayor adhithā indra kṛṣṭīḥ | vi toke apsu tanaye ca sūre 'vocanta carṣaṇayo vivācaḥ ||
  • abhūr eko: This phrase translates to "there is one" or "one who is". It emphasizes the concept of unity and singularity in the universe.
  • rayipate: This term refers to "the lord of wealth" or "the one who possesses riches". It signifies abundance and prosperity.
  • rayīṇām: This means "of the rich" or "of wealth". It highlights the importance of wealth in life, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and emotional richness.
  • ā hastayor adhithā: This translates to "from the hands" or "through the actions". It suggests that wealth and prosperity come through our actions and efforts.
  • indra kṛṣṭīḥ: Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. Kṛṣṭīḥ refers to "cultivation" or "nurturing". This phrase implies that true wealth is cultivated through strength and effort.
  • vi toke apsu: This means "in the waters" or "in the depths". It can symbolize the depths of knowledge or the subconscious mind, where true understanding and wisdom reside.
  • tanaye ca sūre: "Tanaye" means "in the offspring" or "in the descendants\ while "sūre" refers to "the brave" or "the strong". This suggests that the legacy of wealth and strength is passed down through generations.
  • 'vocanta carṣaṇayo vivācaḥ: This translates to "they speak of the great ones" or "they discuss the wise". It emphasizes the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom among people.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, wealth, and the importance of actions in achieving prosperity. It teaches that true wealth is not merely material but encompasses strength, wisdom, and the legacy we leave for future generations. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, community building, and family values. By recognizing the interconnectedness of our actions and the wealth we cultivate, we can strive for a more fulfilling and prosperous life, both individually and collectively.
