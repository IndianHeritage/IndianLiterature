मित्रं न यं सुदितं भृगवो दधुर वनस्पताव īḍ्यम ऊर्ध्वशोचिषम् |स त्वं सुप्रीतो वीतहव्ये अद्भुत प्रशस्तिभिर महायसे दिवे-दिवे ||
mitraṁ na yaṁ sudhitam bhṛgavo dadhur vanaspatāv īḍyam ūrdhvaśociṣam | sa tvaṁ suprīto vītahavye adbhuta praśastibhir mahayase dive-dive ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mitraṁ: friend, ally
    • na: not
    • yaṁ: who
    • sudhitam: well-thought-out, wise
    • bhṛgavo: the Bhṛgus, a family of sages
    • dadhur: have given, bestowed
    • vanaspatāv: in the trees, in nature
    • īḍyam: worthy of praise, revered
    • ūrdhvaśociṣam: shining upwards, elevated
    • sa: he, that
    • tvaṁ: you
    • suprīto: pleased, satisfied
    • vītahavye: in the sacred offerings
    • adbhuta: wonderful, extraordinary
    • praśastibhir: with praises, commendations
    • mahayase: great glory, magnificence
    • dive-dive: day by day, continuously
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of friendship and wisdom, emphasizing the value of nature and the divine.
    • The reference to the Bhṛgus suggests a connection to ancient wisdom and the importance of learning from those who have come before us.
    • The phrase "worthy of praise" indicates that nature and its elements should be revered and respected.
    • It highlights the idea that when one is pleased and satisfied with the offerings made, extraordinary blessings and praises will follow.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the importance of environmental conservation. Just as the mantra praises nature, we should strive to protect and respect our natural surroundings.
    • In relationships, the emphasis on friendship and wisdom can be seen in the way we nurture our connections with others, valuing those who offer us guidance and support.
    • In professional settings, the idea of being "pleased" with offerings can relate to the importance of recognition and appreciation in the workplace, fostering a positive environment that encourages growth and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of friendship, wisdom, and nature.
    • Its significance lies in the call to honor and respect the world around us, as well as the relationships we build with others.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra in our daily lives, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and reverence, leading to a more harmonious existence.
