प्र सम्राजो असुरस्य प्रशस्तिम पुंसः कृष्टीनाम अनुमाद्यस्य |इन्द्रस्येव प्र तवसस कृतानि वन्दे दारुं वन्दमानो विवक्मि ||
pra samrājo asurasya praśastim puṁsaḥ kṛṣṭīnām anumādyasya | indrasyeva pra tavasas kṛtāni vande dāruṁ vandamāno vivakmi ||
  • pra samrājo asurasya: This phrase can be translated as "to the great ruler of the Asuras." In Vedic literature, Asuras are often seen as powerful beings, sometimes in opposition to the Devas (gods). This can be likened to the concept of leadership in modern organizations where strong leadership is essential for success.
  • praśastim puṁsaḥ kṛṣṭīnām anumādyasya: This translates to "the excellence of the man who is engaged in noble deeds." It emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and character. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who contribute positively to society, such as community leaders or volunteers who work tirelessly for the welfare of others.
  • indrasyeva pra tavasas kṛtāni: This means "like the deeds of Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic texts, known for his strength and valor. This can be compared to modern heroes or leaders who take bold actions for the greater good, inspiring others through their courage and determination.
  • vande dāruṁ vandamāno vivakmi: This translates to "I bow to the wood, expressing my thoughts." Here, 'wood' can symbolize strength and stability, akin to the foundational values in our lives. In a practical sense, this can relate to respecting nature and understanding the importance of sustainability in our daily lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, virtue, courage, and respect for nature. Its significance lies in the reminder that true greatness comes from noble actions and the strength of character. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community service, and environmental conservation efforts. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
