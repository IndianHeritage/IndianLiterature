वावृद्धाना शुभस पती दसराऽ हिरण्यवर्तनी |पिबतं सोम्यम् मधु ||
vāvṛdhānā śubhas patī dasrā hiraṇyavartanī | pibataṁ somyam madhu ||
  • vāvṛdhānā: This term can be translated as "may we be nourished" or "may we grow." It signifies a desire for growth and prosperity in life.
  • śubhas patī: This phrase means "the lord of auspiciousness." It refers to a divine entity or force that brings good fortune and positive outcomes.
  • dasrā: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It symbolizes the speed and efficiency with which blessings and prosperity can come into our lives.
  • hiraṇyavartanī: This term translates to "golden wheel" or "the one who brings wealth." It represents abundance and the flow of prosperity.
  • pibataṁ: This word means "drink" or "consume." It suggests the act of taking in or receiving something beneficial.
  • somyam: This term refers to "the nectar" or "the essence of life." In Vedic tradition, soma is often associated with immortality and divine bliss.
  • madhu: This word translates to "honey" or "sweetness." It symbolizes joy, happiness, and the sweetness of life experiences.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for nourishment and growth, invoking the blessings of auspiciousness and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of receiving divine blessings swiftly, akin to how one would quickly consume something nourishing.
    • The imagery of a "golden wheel" suggests a continuous flow of wealth and abundance, much like the cycles of nature that provide sustenance.
    • The reference to "nectar" and "honey" highlights the sweetness of life that comes from divine blessings and prosperity.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be a reminder for entrepreneurs to seek growth and prosperity through ethical practices and divine guidance.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, hoping for swift solutions and blessings in their endeavors.
    • During festivals or auspicious occasions, families may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for prosperity and happiness in their homes.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing growth, prosperity, and the sweetness of experiences.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings and to be open to receiving abundance in various forms.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal aspirations, business ventures, and during significant life events, fostering a mindset of positivity and receptiveness to blessings.
