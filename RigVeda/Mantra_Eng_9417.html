पश्वा यत पश्चा वियुता बुद्धन्तेति ब्रवीति वक्तरी रराणः |वासोः वसुत्वा कारवो 'नेहा विश्वं विवेष्टि द्रविणम उप क्षु ||
paśvā yat paścā viyutā budhanteti bravīti vaktarī rarāṇaḥ | vasor vasutvā kāravo 'nehā viśvaṁ viveṣṭi draviṇam upa kṣu ||
  • paśvā yat paścā viyutā budhanteti bravīti vaktarī rarāṇaḥ
    • paśvā: Refers to the "animals" or "creatures." In a broader sense, it can symbolize all living beings.
    • yat: Means "that which" or "whatever."
    • paścā: Translates to "after" or "later," indicating a sequence or consequence.
    • viyutā: Means "separated" or "distinguished," suggesting a state of differentiation.
    • budhanteti: Refers to "those who are wise" or "intelligent beings," emphasizing the importance of wisdom.
    • bravīti: Means "speaks" or "declares," indicating communication or expression.
    • vaktarī: Refers to "the speaker" or "the one who articulates," highlighting the role of communication.
    • rarāṇaḥ: Means "the one who is active" or "the doer," suggesting action and engagement.
  • vasor vasutvā kāravo 'nehā viśvaṁ viveṣṭi draviṇam upa kṣu
    • vasor: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance.
    • vasutvā: Means "the state of being wealthy" or "prosperity itself."
    • kāravo: Refers to "the creators" or "those who make," emphasizing the role of creation and effort.
    • 'nehā: Means "here" or "in this place," indicating a specific context or environment.
    • viśvaṁ: Translates to "the whole" or "all," suggesting inclusivity.
    • viveṣṭi: Means "envelops" or "surrounds," indicating a comprehensive presence.
    • draviṇam: Refers to "wealth" or "resources," emphasizing material abundance.
    • upa kṣu: Means "to the one who is wise" or "to the knowledgeable," indicating a direction towards wisdom.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, communication, and the active pursuit of prosperity. It suggests that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom and the ability to articulate thoughts and ideas. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, where one seeks knowledge and understanding to make informed decisions.
  • In business, where effective communication and wise strategies lead to prosperity and success.
  • In community building, where the collective wisdom of individuals can create a supportive and thriving environment.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom and action are essential components of a prosperous life, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and engage actively in their pursuits.
