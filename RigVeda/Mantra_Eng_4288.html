सम अश्विनोर अवसां नूतनेन मयोभुवा सुप्रणीति गमेमा |आ नो रयिं वहतम ओत वीरान आ विश्वानि अमृतā सौभगानि ||
sam aśvinor avasā nūtanena mayobhuvā supraṇītī gamema | ā no rayiṁ vahatam ota vīrān ā viśvāny amṛtā saubhagāni ||
  • sam aśvinor avasā nūtanena mayobhuvā supraṇītī gamema
    • sam: This word signifies harmony or togetherness. It emphasizes the importance of unity in achieving goals.
    • aśvinor: Refers to the Ashvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • avasā: Means 'to be present' or 'to arrive.' It indicates the arrival of divine blessings or guidance.
    • nūtanena: Translates to 'with newness' or 'with freshness.' It suggests the idea of renewal or new beginnings.
    • mayobhuvā: This term can be interpreted as 'with our minds' or 'with our intellect.' It emphasizes the role of consciousness in our actions.
    • supraṇītī: Means 'well-directed' or 'well-guided.' It indicates the importance of having a clear and positive direction in life.
    • gamema: Translates to 'let us go' or 'let us move forward.' It signifies the action of progressing towards a goal.
  • ā no rayiṁ vahatam ota vīrān ā viśvāny amṛtā saubhagāni
    • ā: This word means 'to us' or 'for us,' indicating a request or invocation directed towards the divine.
    • no: Translates to 'our' or 'of us,' emphasizing a collective identity.
    • rayiṁ: Refers to 'wealth' or 'prosperity.' It signifies the material and spiritual abundance one seeks.
    • vahatam: Means 'to carry' or 'to bring.' It indicates the act of receiving blessings or abundance.
    • ota: Translates to 'strength' or 'power.' It signifies the importance of inner strength in achieving success.
    • vīrān: Refers to 'heroes' or 'warriors.' It symbolizes courage and valor in facing challenges.
    • ā: Again, this word emphasizes the invocation directed towards the divine.
    • viśvāny: Means 'all' or 'universal.' It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • amṛtā: Translates to 'immortal' or 'eternal.' It signifies the pursuit of everlasting values and truths.
    • saubhagāni: Refers to 'auspiciousness' or 'good fortune.' It indicates the blessings of prosperity and well-being.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, divine guidance, and the pursuit of prosperity and strength. It encourages individuals to seek renewal and direction in their lives while invoking blessings for collective well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek new beginnings and to move forward with clarity and purpose.
  • In community settings, it highlights the importance of working together harmoniously to achieve common goals.
  • In times of challenge, it serves as a reminder to invoke inner strength and courage, drawing upon the support of the community and divine forces.

Overall, the mantra serves as a powerful invocation for prosperity, strength, and auspiciousness in both personal and collective endeavors.
