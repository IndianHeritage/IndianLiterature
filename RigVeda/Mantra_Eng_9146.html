जायā तप्यते किटवस्य हीनā मातā पुत्रस्य चरतः क्व स्वित् |ऋणावā बिभ्यद धनम् इच्छमानो 'न्येषाम् अस्तम् उप नक्तम् एति ||
jāyā tapyate kitavasya hīnā mātā putrasya carataḥ kva svit | ṛṇāvā bibhyad dhanam icchamāno 'nyeṣām astam upa naktam eti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jāyā: wife
    • tapyate: is tormented
    • kitavasya: of the deceitful
    • hīnā: deprived
    • mātā: mother
    • putrasya: of the son
    • carataḥ: wandering
    • kva svit: where indeed
    • ṛṇāvā: in debt
    • bibhyat: fears
    • dhanam: wealth
    • icchamāno: desiring
    • anyeṣām: of others
    • astam: setting
    • upa: near
    • naktam: night
    • eti: goes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the struggles and fears associated with familial and social responsibilities.
    • The "wife" (jāyā) is depicted as suffering due to the deceitful actions of others (kitavasya), highlighting the emotional turmoil that can arise in relationships.
    • The mention of a "mother" (mātā) and "son" (putrasya) suggests a familial bond that is affected by external circumstances, emphasizing the interconnectedness of family dynamics.
    • The phrase "where indeed" (kva svit) indicates a search for answers or a place of refuge amidst turmoil.
    • The fear of debt (ṛṇāvā bibhyat) signifies the anxiety that comes with financial responsibilities and the desire for wealth (dhanam icchamāno), which can lead to moral dilemmas.
    • The concluding part, "of others" (anyeṣām) and "setting night" (astam upa naktam), suggests that one’s actions and desires can lead to unforeseen consequences, especially as one navigates through life’s challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, many individuals face the pressure of providing for their families while dealing with deceitful business practices or relationships that can lead to emotional distress.
    • Parents often worry about their children's future, fearing the burden of debt and the need to secure wealth for their well-being.
    • In relationships, the impact of dishonesty can lead to significant emotional suffering, as seen in cases of infidelity or betrayal, affecting not just the partners but also their families.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the complexities of human relationships and the emotional burdens that can arise from societal expectations and personal desires.
    • It highlights the importance of honesty and integrity in relationships, as well as the need for open communication to alleviate fears and anxieties.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and the potential impact on their loved ones, promoting a more compassionate and understanding approach to familial and social interactions.
