परी वाजे न वाजयुम अव्यो वारेषु सिञ्चत | इन्द्राय मधुमत्तमम् ||
pari vāje na vājayum avyo vāreṣu siñcata | indrāya madhumattamam ||
  • Mantra: "pari vāje na vājayum avyo vāreṣu siñcata | indrāya madhumattamam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the waters flow abundantly, may they not be obstructed; may they be poured forth for Indra, the one who is the bestower of sweetness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari vāje: "pari" means around or all around, and "vāje" refers to strength or power. Together, it suggests a comprehensive or abundant strength.
    • na vājayum: "na" means not, and "vājayum" refers to being obstructed or hindered. This phrase emphasizes the desire for unobstructed flow.
    • avyō vāreṣu: "avyō" means without hindrance, and "vāreṣu" refers to waters or streams. This indicates a wish for the waters to flow freely.
    • siñcata: This means to pour or to sprinkle, suggesting an act of offering or bestowing.
    • indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain and thunderstorms.
    • madhumattamam: "madhu" means sweetness or nectar, and "mattamam" means the best or the most excellent. This phrase highlights the desire for the best form of sweetness or abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the importance of water in agriculture. Just as the mantra calls for abundant and unobstructed waters, farmers pray for rain to nourish their crops, ensuring a good harvest.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can symbolize the flow of positive energy and resources in our lives. For instance, in a business context, it can represent the desire for unimpeded growth and success.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for open communication and understanding, allowing love and kindness to flow freely between individuals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation for abundance and the removal of obstacles. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking blessings for prosperity and sweetness in life. In real-life scenarios, whether in agriculture, business, or personal relationships, the essence of this mantra encourages us to foster an environment where resources and positive energies can flow freely, leading to growth and fulfillment.
