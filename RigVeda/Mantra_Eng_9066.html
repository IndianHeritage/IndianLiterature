विक्रोशनासो विश्वञ्च आयन पचाति नेमो न हि पक्षदर्धः |अयम् मे देवः सविता तद आह द्र्वन्न इद वनवत सर्पिरन्नः ||
vi krośanāso viṣvañca āyan pacāti nemo nahi pakṣad ardhaḥ | ayam me devaḥ savitā tad āha drvanna id vanavat sarpirannaḥ ||
  • vi krośanāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are calling out" or "those who are invoking." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • viṣvañca: This word means "all" or "universal." It emphasizes the inclusiveness of the divine presence in all aspects of life.
  • āyan: This term refers to "the path" or "the way." It suggests a journey or direction that one should follow in life.
  • pacāti: This means "to cook" or "to prepare." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the process of nurturing or developing something in life.
  • nemo nahi pakṣad ardhaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "there is no division in the half of the wings." It implies unity and the idea that there should be no separation in our pursuits or efforts.
  • ayam me devaḥ savitā: This translates to "this is my deity, Savitā." Savitā is often associated with the sun and represents illumination, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • tad āha: This means "that is said" or "that is declared." It indicates the importance of communication and the sharing of wisdom.
  • drvanna id vanavat: This can be interpreted as "like a tree in the forest." It symbolizes stability, growth, and the interconnectedness of life.
  • sarpirannaḥ: This word refers to "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in rituals. It represents nourishment, purity, and the essence of offerings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and recognizing the interconnectedness of all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to follow a path of unity and growth, nurturing their endeavors with care and devotion. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community building, and spiritual practices. By invoking the divine presence and striving for unity, individuals can cultivate a harmonious and fulfilling life, much like a tree that stands strong and interconnected within a forest. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for holistic living, where every action is infused with purpose and reverence for the divine.
