स रुद्रैभिर अशस्तवारा ऋभ्वा हित्वि गयम् आर्यवद्य आगात् |वम्रस्य मन्ये मिथुना विवव्री अन्नम् अभीत्यारोदयन् मुषायन् ||
sa rudrebhir aśastavāra ṛbhvā hitvī gayam āreavadya āgāt | vamrasya manye mithunā vivavrī annam abhītyārodayan muṣāyan ||
  • sa rudrebhir aśastavāra: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the fierce ones (Rudras) who are powerful." Rudra is often associated with storm and wind, symbolizing the uncontrollable forces of nature. In real life, this can relate to how we face challenges and adversities that seem overwhelming.
  • ṛbhvā hitvī gayam: This translates to "having overcome the obstacles." It signifies the importance of resilience and determination in the face of difficulties. For example, a student who struggles with a subject but continues to study hard and eventually excels embodies this idea.
  • āreavadya āgāt: This means "he has arrived at the desired goal." It reflects the achievement of one's aspirations. In a practical sense, this could be seen in someone who sets a career goal and works diligently to reach a position they aspire to, such as becoming a doctor or an engineer.
  • vamrasya manye: This phrase can be interpreted as "I consider the strength." It emphasizes the recognition of one's inner strength and capabilities. For instance, an athlete who acknowledges their physical and mental strength before a competition can harness that energy to perform better.
  • mithunā vivavrī: This translates to "the duality is revealed." It suggests the balance between opposites, such as success and failure, joy and sorrow. In life, we often experience both sides, and understanding this duality can lead to a more balanced perspective. For example, a business owner may face both profits and losses, learning valuable lessons from each experience.
  • annam abhītyārodayan muṣāyan: This means "the food that nourishes and sustains." It highlights the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual. In real life, this can relate to the significance of healthy eating and the nourishment of the mind through education and knowledge.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of overcoming challenges, recognizing inner strength, achieving goals, and understanding the dualities of life. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday scenarios, encouraging individuals to be resilient, strive for their aspirations, and maintain a balanced perspective. Whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or spiritual pursuits, the teachings of this mantra can guide individuals toward a fulfilling and meaningful life.
