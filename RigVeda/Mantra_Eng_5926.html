त्रिर देवः पृथिवीम् एष एतां विचक्रमे शतर्चसम महित्वा |प्र विष्णुर अस्तु तवसस तवीयान त्वेषं ह्य अस्य स्थविरस्य नाम ||
trir devaḥ pṛthivīm eṣa etāṁ vi cakrame śatarcasam mahitvā | pra viṣṇur astu tavasas tavīyān tveṣaṁ hy asya sthavirasya nāma ||
  • Mantra: "trir devaḥ pṛthivīm eṣa etāṁ vi cakrame śatarcasam mahitvā | pra viṣṇur astu tavasas tavīyān tveṣaṁ hy asya sthavirasya nāma ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trir: three times
    • devaḥ: gods
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • eṣa: this
    • etāṁ: this (feminine form)
    • vi: in various ways
    • cakrame: has moved or revolved
    • śatarcasam: with a hundred powers
    • mahitvā: greatness
    • pra: may
    • viṣṇur: Vishnu
    • astu: be
    • tavasas: of your strength
    • tavīyān: more powerful
    • tveṣaṁ: your
    • hy: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • sthavirasya: of the ancient
    • nāma: name
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that govern the earth and the universe.
    • The phrase "trir devaḥ" suggests the presence of three significant divine entities, which can be interpreted as the Trimurti: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva, representing creation, preservation, and destruction.
    • "pṛthivīm" refers to the earth, indicating the grounding aspect of these divine forces in our physical reality.
    • The term "śatarcasam" implies a hundred powers, symbolizing the vast capabilities and attributes of the divine.
    • "pra viṣṇur astu" invokes Lord Vishnu, the preserver, emphasizing the importance of preservation and balance in life.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of strength and stability, as seen in the phrase "tavasas tavīyān," which can be related to the strength we seek in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities often come together, invoking divine protection and strength, much like the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek inner strength and stability, reflecting the qualities of Vishnu as the preserver of life.
    • In environmental movements, the reverence for the earth ("pṛthivīm") aligns with the mantra's call for respect and care for our planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine forces and our earthly existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength, stability, and preservation in our lives, encouraging us to seek balance and harmony.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community resilience, highlighting the need for divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
