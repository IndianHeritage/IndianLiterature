तं इद् विप्राः अवस्यवः प्रवत्वतीभिर् ऊतिभिः | इन्द्रं क्षोणीः अवर्धयन वयाः इव ||
tam id viprā avasyavaḥ pravatvatībhir ūtibhiḥ | indraṁ kṣoṇīr avardhayan vayā iva ||
  • tam id viprā avasyavaḥ pravatvatībhir ūtibhiḥ
    • tam id: This phrase translates to "this" or "that," indicating a focus on a specific entity or concept.
    • viprā: Refers to the wise or learned individuals, often associated with priests or sages in Vedic literature.
    • avasyavaḥ: Means "those who are destined" or "those who are meant to," suggesting a sense of purpose or inevitability.
    • pravatvatībhir: This term can be understood as "with their efforts" or "through their actions," emphasizing the importance of active participation.
    • ūtibhiḥ: Refers to "strengths" or "powers," indicating the resources or capabilities that the wise individuals possess.
  • indraṁ kṣoṇīr avardhayan vayā iva
    • indraṁ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • kṣoṇīr: This term refers to "the earth" or "the land," symbolizing the physical realm and its sustenance.
    • avardhayan: Means "to increase" or "to enhance," suggesting a process of growth or improvement.
    • vayā iva: Translates to "like the wind," indicating a natural, effortless force that brings about change.

Meaning and Explanation:

This mantra speaks to the role of wise individuals (viprā) who, through their destined actions (avasyavaḥ) and strengths (ūtibhiḥ), work to enhance the earth (kṣoṇīr) and invoke the blessings of Indra. The comparison to the wind (vayā iva) suggests that this process is both natural and powerful, highlighting the importance of wisdom and effort in achieving growth and prosperity.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In a community, wise leaders who understand the needs of their people can bring about significant improvements in living conditions, much like how Indra brings rain to nourish the earth.
  • In a corporate setting, knowledgeable managers who utilize their skills and resources effectively can lead their teams to success, enhancing the overall productivity of the organization.
  • In personal development, individuals who actively seek knowledge and apply it in their lives can experience growth and transformation, akin to the natural forces that drive change.

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of wisdom and action in fostering growth and prosperity. It teaches us that through knowledge and effort, we can invoke positive change in our lives and communities. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from leadership and teamwork to personal development, reminding us that our actions, guided by wisdom, can lead to fruitful outcomes.
