एवां नृभिर इन्द्रः सुश्रवस्य प्रखादः पृक्षो अभि मित्रिणो भूत् |समर्य इषः स्तवते विवाचि सत्राकरो यजमानस्य शंसः ||
evā nṛbhir indraḥ suśravasyā prakhādaḥ pṛkṣo abhi mitriṇo bhūt | samarya iṣaḥ stavate vivāci satrākaro yajamānasya śaṁsaḥ ||
  • evā nṛbhir indraḥ: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, who is often associated with strength and power. The term "nṛbhir" means "by men" or "by humans," indicating that Indra is invoked or praised by people.
  • suśravasyā prakhādaḥ: "Suśravasyā" translates to "of good fame" or "renowned," while "prakhādaḥ" means "the one who is celebrated." This suggests that Indra is not only powerful but also well-regarded and respected among humans.
  • pṛkṣo abhi mitriṇo bhūt: "Pṛkṣo" means "to be vigilant" or "to observe," and "abhi mitriṇo" refers to "the friend of those who are vigilant." "Bhūt" can mean "to become" or "to exist." This part emphasizes that Indra is a protector and ally to those who are watchful and aware.
  • samarya iṣaḥ: "Samarya" means "to be united" or "to come together," while "iṣaḥ" refers to "the will" or "the intention." This indicates that there is a collective will or intention among people when they come together to worship or praise Indra.
  • stavate vivāci: "Stavate" means "to praise" or "to chant," and "vivāci" refers to "the one who speaks." This suggests that the act of praising Indra is a vocal and communal expression of devotion.
  • satrākaro yajamānasya śaṁsaḥ: "Satrākaro" means "the one who brings prosperity" or "the benefactor," "yajamānasya" refers to "of the worshipper," and "śaṁsaḥ" means "blessings" or "praises." This indicates that Indra is a source of blessings for those who engage in worship and rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of invoking Indra as a powerful and benevolent deity who supports and blesses those who are vigilant and united in their intentions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to communal gatherings, rituals, or any situation where people come together with a shared purpose, such as festivals, ceremonies, or even team-building activities. By recognizing the importance of collective effort and the power of praise, individuals can foster a sense of community and attract positive outcomes in their endeavors.
