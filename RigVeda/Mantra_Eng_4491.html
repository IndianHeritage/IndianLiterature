इमं उ त्यं अथर्ववद अग्निम मन्थन्ति वेधसः |यम् अङ्कूयन्तम आनयन्न अमूरं श्याव्याभ्यः ||
imam u tyam atharvavad agnim manthanti vedhasaḥ | yam aṅkūyantam ānayann amūraṁ śyāvyābhyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "They who are versed in the Atharva Veda invoke the fire, who is the one that brings forth the offerings, and who is the one that is invoked by the wise."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imam: This means "this" or "here," indicating the focus of the invocation.
    • u: A conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas together.
    • tyam: Refers to "that," pointing to the fire being invoked.
    • atharvavad: Pertaining to the Atharva Veda, one of the four Vedas, which contains hymns, spells, and incantations.
    • agnim: Means "fire," a central element in Vedic rituals symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • manthanti: Means "invoke" or "churn," suggesting an active engagement in the ritual.
    • vedhasaḥ: Refers to "the wise" or "those who know the Vedas," indicating the learned individuals performing the invocation.
    • yam: Again means "who," referring to the fire being invoked.
    • aṅkūyantam: Means "who brings forth" or "who is called," emphasizing the role of fire in the ritual.
    • ānayann: Means "bringing" or "leading," indicating the action of the fire in the ritual context.
    • amūraṁ: Refers to "the offerings" or "the essence," highlighting what is being offered to the fire.
    • śyāvyābhyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "from the wise" or "by the knowledgeable," reinforcing the connection between wisdom and the invocation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, fire is often invoked as a witness and purifier, similar to the Vedic practices.
    • In many cultures, fire symbolizes transformation and renewal, akin to how it is viewed in the Vedas, where it is seen as a medium to connect with the divine.
    • In scientific terms, fire represents energy and change, which can be related to personal growth and transformation in one’s life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the role of fire as a sacred element in rituals, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various rituals and personal transformations, reminding us of the importance of invoking wisdom and purity in our actions. The invocation of fire serves as a metaphor for the inner fire of knowledge and the transformative power of wisdom in our lives.
