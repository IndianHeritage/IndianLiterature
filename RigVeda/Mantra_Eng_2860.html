आ नो यज्ञं नमोवृद्धं सजोषा इन्द्र देव हरिभिर याहि तुयम् |अहं हि त्वा मतीभिर जोहवीमि घृतप्रयाः सदहमादे मधूनाम् ||
ā no yajñaṁ namovṛdhaṁ sajoṣā indra deva haribhir yāhi tūyam | ahaṁ hi tvā matibhir johavīmi ghṛtaprayāḥ sadhamāde madhūnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: us
    • yajñaṁ: sacrifice or worship
    • namovṛdhaṁ: to the great one (Indra)
    • sajoṣā: with joy
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • deva: divine
    • haribhir: with the help of the divine beings
    • yāhi: come
    • tūyam: to us
    • ahaṁ: I
    • hi: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • matibhir: with thoughts or intentions
    • johavīmi: I invoke
    • ghṛtaprayāḥ: with clarified butter (ghee)
    • sadhamāde: for the good things
    • madhūnām: of the sweet (nectar)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the king of gods, asking for his presence and blessings.
    • The invocation of "yajñaṁ" signifies the importance of rituals and sacrifices in seeking divine favor.
    • The phrase "ahaṁ hi tvā matibhir johavīmi" emphasizes the personal connection and intention behind the worship, indicating that the devotee is actively engaging in the process of invocation.
    • The use of "ghṛtaprayāḥ" symbolizes the offering of ghee, which is often associated with purity and nourishment in Vedic rituals.
    • The overall tone of the mantra is one of reverence and a call for divine assistance in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, people often perform rituals or prayers during significant life events such as weddings, births, or new ventures, seeking blessings similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • Communities may come together for festivals or ceremonies, reflecting the collective joy and intention to honor the divine, akin to the "sajoṣā" aspect of the mantra.
    • Individuals may engage in meditation or mindfulness practices, focusing their thoughts and intentions towards positive outcomes, paralleling the personal invocation of divine presence in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of intention, community, and the sacredness of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various cultural and spiritual practices that seek to invoke blessings and foster a sense of connection with higher powers.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of approaching life with reverence, gratitude, and a desire for harmony and prosperity.
