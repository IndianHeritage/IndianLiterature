युवं ह घर्मं मधुमन्तं अत्रये 'पो न क्षोदो 'वृणीतम एषे |तद वां नराव अश्विनां पश्वैष्टी रथ्येव चक्रा प्रति यन्ति मध्वः ||
yuvaṁ ha gharmam madhumantam atraye 'po na kṣodo 'vṛṇītam eṣe | tad vāṁ narāv aśvinā paśvaïṣṭī rathyeva cakrā prati yanti madhvaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ ha gharmam madhumantam atraye 'po na kṣodo 'vṛṇītam eṣe | tad vāṁ narāv aśvinā paśvaïṣṭī rathyeva cakrā prati yanti madhvaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the youthful, warm, and sweet essence of life, invoking the divine Aśvins to bestow their blessings. It emphasizes the importance of protection and the pursuit of well-being.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yuvaṁ: youthful, representing vitality and energy.
    • gharmam: warmth, symbolizing comfort and nurturing.
    • madhumantam: sweet, indicating the pleasant aspects of life.
    • atraye: invoking the divine, suggesting a call for assistance.
    • apo: water, representing purity and sustenance.
    • na kṣodo: without harm, emphasizing safety and protection.
    • eṣe: this, referring to the present moment or situation.
    • tad vāṁ: that which is yours, indicating possession or connection.
    • narāv: men, symbolizing humanity and collective strength.
    • aśvinā: the Aśvins, divine twin horsemen associated with health and medicine.
    • paśvaïṣṭī: abundance, referring to prosperity and well-being.
    • rathyeva: like a chariot, symbolizing movement and progress.
    • cakrā: wheels, representing cycles of life and continuity.
    • prati yanti: they move towards, indicating direction and purpose.
    • madhvaḥ: sweetness, reinforcing the theme of joy and positivity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals where people come together to celebrate life, health, and prosperity, invoking blessings for safety and abundance.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of stress or uncertainty, seeking warmth and protection from harm, while also focusing on the positive aspects of their lives.
    • In healthcare, the Aśvins symbolize the pursuit of health and wellness, reminding us to seek medical assistance and embrace a lifestyle that promotes well-being.
  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of youthfulness, warmth, and sweetness in life. It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings for protection and well-being, encouraging individuals and communities to focus on positivity and abundance. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in celebrations, personal reflections, and health pursuits, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and with each other.
