अतारीषुर भरतागव्यवः सम अभक्त विप्रः सुमतिं नदीनाम् |प्र पिन्वध्वम् इषयन्तीः सुराधा आ वक्षाणाः पृणध्वं यात शीभम् ||
atāriṣur bharatā gavyavaḥ sam abhakta vipraḥ sumatiṁ nadīnām | pra pinvadhvam iṣayantīḥ surādhā ā vakṣaṇāḥ pṛṇadhvaṁ yāta śībham ||
  • atāriṣur: This term can be interpreted as "those who have attained" or "those who have reached." It signifies the achievement of a higher state of consciousness or understanding.
  • bharatā: This word refers to "the one who is worthy" or "the one who is revered." It can also denote the idea of a collective identity, such as a nation or community.
  • gavyavaḥ: This term relates to "the one who possesses cows" or "wealth." In ancient times, cows were a symbol of prosperity and abundance.
  • sam abhakta: This phrase means "together, they are not divided." It emphasizes unity and collaboration among individuals or groups.
  • vipraḥ: This word refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • sumatiṁ: This term means "good thought" or "intelligence." It signifies the value of positive thinking and mental clarity.
  • nadīnām: This word translates to "of the rivers." Rivers are often seen as symbols of life, nourishment, and flow in various cultures.
  • pra pinvadhvam: This phrase means "may you nourish" or "may you cultivate." It encourages the act of nurturing and fostering growth.
  • iṣayantīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are striving" or "those who are aspiring." It reflects the human spirit's desire to achieve and improve.
  • surādhā: This word means "the one who is rich in good qualities." It emphasizes the importance of virtuous attributes in a person.
  • ā vakṣaṇāḥ: This phrase translates to "may you be protected" or "may you be safeguarded." It signifies the desire for safety and well-being.
  • pṛṇadhvaṁ: This term means "may you be filled" or "may you be satisfied." It reflects the aspiration for fulfillment and contentment.
  • yāta śībham: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you go towards prosperity" or "may you move towards abundance." It encourages the pursuit of success and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and positive thinking in achieving prosperity and fulfillment. It encourages individuals to work together, nurture their strengths, and strive for a higher purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, teamwork in professional settings, and personal development. By fostering collaboration and cultivating good thoughts, individuals and communities can move towards a more prosperous and harmonious existence.
