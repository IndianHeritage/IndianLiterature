स त्वं नो देव मनसा वायो मन्दानो अग्रियः |कृधि वाजाम् अपो धियः ||
sa tvaṁ no deva manasā vāyo mandāno agriyaḥ | kṛdhi vājām̐ apo dhiyaḥ ||
  • sa tvaṁ no deva: This phrase translates to "O Divine One, you are with us." It signifies a call to the divine presence, acknowledging the omnipresence of a higher power in our lives.
  • manasā vāyo mandāno agriyaḥ: This part can be interpreted as "with our minds, may we be inspired by the gentle wind." Here, "vāyo" refers to the wind, symbolizing the flow of thoughts and ideas, while "mandāno" suggests gentleness and calmness. "Agriyaḥ" means the foremost or the best, indicating the pursuit of the highest wisdom.
  • kṛdhi vājām̐ apo dhiyaḥ: This translates to "may you grant us strength and wisdom." "Vājām̐" refers to strength or power, and "apo dhiyaḥ" means the waters of wisdom, suggesting a request for clarity and insight in our endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the pursuit of wisdom in our lives. It reflects a deep connection with the divine and the natural world, encouraging individuals to seek inspiration and strength from both.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, such as during moments of decision-making, when seeking clarity in personal or professional challenges, or when striving for inner peace. For instance, before a crucial meeting or presentation, one might recite this mantra to invoke calmness and clarity of thought.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human consciousness. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, strength, and wisdom, guiding them through life's challenges and enhancing their overall well-being.
