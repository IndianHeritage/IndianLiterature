समिधा यस् त आहुतिं निश्चितिम मर्त्यो नशत् |वायावन्तं स पुष्यति क्षयम् अग्ने शतायुषम् ||
samidhā yas ta āhutiṁ niśitim martyo naśat | vayāvantaṁ sa puṣyati kṣayam agne śatāyuṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samidhā: kindled or ignited
    • yas: who
    • ta: that
    • āhutiṁ: offering or sacrifice
    • niśitim: the night or darkness
    • martyo: mortal or human
    • naśat: does not perish
    • vayāvantaṁ: the one who is powerful or strong
    • sa: he or that
    • puṣyati: nourishes or sustains
    • kṣayam: destruction or decay
    • agne: O Fire
    • śatāyuṣam: with a hundred years of life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of offerings made to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "samidhā yas ta āhutiṁ" emphasizes the importance of kindling the fire and making offerings, which symbolizes devotion and respect.
    • "niśitim martyo naśat" suggests that through these offerings, mortals can transcend the limitations of life and death, achieving a form of immortality or lasting legacy.
    • The line "vayāvantaṁ sa puṣyati" indicates that Agni, as a powerful force, nourishes those who honor him, leading to prosperity and well-being.
    • Finally, "kṣayam agne śatāyuṣam" highlights the desire for longevity and vitality, invoking Agni's blessings for a long and fruitful life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during rituals symbolizes purification and the presence of the divine. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is central to the ceremony, representing the couple's commitment and the divine witness to their vows.
    • In modern life, the mantra can be interpreted as a reminder of the importance of dedication and hard work. Just as offerings to Agni are made with care, individuals can achieve their goals through consistent effort and respect for their pursuits.
    • The concept of nourishing one's life through positive actions can be seen in community service, where individuals contribute to the well-being of others, thereby enriching their own lives and creating a legacy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the significance of devotion, the power of offerings, and the pursuit of longevity and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that through our actions and intentions, we can transcend our mortal limitations and create a lasting impact on the world around us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote well-being, both for themselves and their communities, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and to each other.
