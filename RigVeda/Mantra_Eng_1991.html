मन्त्र: कङ्कतो न कङ्कतोऽथो सतीनकङ्कतः | द्वाविति प्लुषी इति न्यदृष्टा अलिप्सता ||
kaṅkato na kaṅkato 'tho satīnakaṅkataḥ | dvāv iti pluṣī iti ny a1dṛṣṭā alipsata ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kaṅkato: This term can be interpreted as "desiring" or "longing." It reflects a state of yearning or aspiration.
    • na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It indicates the absence of something.
    • satīnakaṅkataḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the one who is truly desiring" or "the one who has genuine aspirations." It emphasizes authenticity in one's desires.
    • dvāv: This means "two." It suggests a duality or a choice between two paths or states.
    • iti: This is a marker that indicates a statement or conclusion, often translated as "thus" or "therefore."
    • pluṣī: This term can refer to "abundance" or "plenty," indicating a state of richness or fulfillment.
    • ny: This can be interpreted as "not" or "without," reinforcing the negation.
    • a1dṛṣṭā: This term suggests "unseen" or "invisible," indicating something that is not immediately apparent or recognized.
    • alipsata: This can be understood as "to desire" or "to aspire," reinforcing the theme of longing.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks to the nature of desire and aspiration. It suggests that one should be aware of their true desires and not be swayed by superficial longings.
    • The duality indicated by "dvāv" may represent the choice between genuine aspirations and fleeting desires. It encourages individuals to reflect on what they truly want in life.
    • The emphasis on authenticity in "satīnakaṅkataḥ" suggests that true fulfillment comes from understanding and pursuing one's real aspirations rather than being distracted by external influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a career context, an individual may feel pressured to pursue a high-paying job that does not align with their passions. This mantra encourages them to reflect on their true desires and seek a career that brings them joy and fulfillment.
    • In relationships, one might be drawn to superficial connections that lack depth. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek meaningful relationships that resonate with one's authentic self.
    • In personal development, individuals often set goals based on societal expectations rather than their own aspirations. This mantra encourages self-reflection to identify what truly matters to them.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and authenticity in our desires. It teaches that true fulfillment comes from understanding and pursuing what genuinely resonates with us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various aspects, including career choices, relationships, and personal growth. By aligning our actions with our true aspirations, we can lead more meaningful and satisfying lives.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a guiding principle to navigate the complexities of desire, urging us to seek depth and authenticity in our pursuits.
