को ददर्श प्रथमं जायमानं अस्तन्वन्तं यद अनस्था बिभर्ति |भूम्याऽसुर असृग आत्मा क्व स्वित को विद्वांसम् उप गात प्रष्टुम् एतत् ||
ko dadarśa prathamaṁ jāyamānam asthanvantaṁ yad anasthā bibharti | bhūmyā asur asṛg ātmā kva svit ko vidvāṁsam upa gāt praṣṭum etat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • dadarśa: has seen
    • prathamaṁ: first
    • jāyamānam: being born
    • asthanvantaṁ: without support
    • yad: that which
    • anasthā: without a place
    • bibharti: carries
    • bhūmyā: on the earth
    • asur: demon
    • asṛg: blood
    • ātma: soul
    • kva: where
    • svit: indeed
    • ko: who
    • vidvāṁsam: wise person
    • upa: near
    • gāt: goes
    • praṣṭum: to ask
    • etat: this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a profound question about existence and the nature of reality.
    • It reflects on the mystery of life, particularly the moment of birth and the conditions surrounding it.
    • The phrase "who has seen the first being being born" suggests a quest for knowledge about the origins of life.
    • The term "asthanvantaṁ" implies a state of being that lacks support, which can be interpreted as the vulnerability of life.
    • It questions the nature of existence, asking where the wise can find answers to these profound mysteries.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern science, the quest to understand the origins of life parallels the inquiry posed in this mantra. Scientists study the conditions that led to the emergence of life on Earth.
    • Philosophers and thinkers throughout history have grappled with questions of existence, much like the inquiry in this mantra, seeking to understand the meaning of life and our place in the universe.
    • In personal experiences, moments of vulnerability, such as facing challenges or uncertainties, can resonate with the idea of being "without support," prompting individuals to seek wisdom and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of questioning and seeking knowledge about our existence and the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their own lives, the support systems they have, and the wisdom they seek.
    • In a broader context, it highlights the universal human experience of grappling with the unknown and the quest for understanding, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal growth to scientific exploration.
