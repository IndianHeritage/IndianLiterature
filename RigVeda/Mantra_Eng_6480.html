मर्तश्चिद्वो नृतवो रुक्मवक्षस उप भ्रातृत्वमायति। अधिनो गात मरुतः सदा हि वाऽपित्वमस्ति निध्रुवि॥
martaś cid vo nṛtavo rukmavakṣasa upa bhrātṛtvam āyati | adhi no gāta marutaḥ sadā hi va āpitvam asti nidhruvi ||
  • martaś cid vo nṛtavo rukmavakṣasa upa bhrātṛtvam āyati
    • martaś: This word refers to the concept of mortality or death. It signifies the transient nature of life.
    • cid: This means 'indeed' or 'certainly', emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • vo: This is a pronoun meaning 'to you', indicating a direct address to the audience or the divine.
    • nṛtavo: This term can be interpreted as 'those who are truthful' or 'the righteous'. It highlights the importance of truthfulness in life.
    • rukmavakṣasa: This word can be broken down into 'rukma' (golden) and 'vakṣasa' (chest or heart), symbolizing the richness of character and virtue.
    • upa: This means 'near' or 'approaching', suggesting a closeness or connection.
    • bhrātṛtvam: This refers to 'brotherhood' or 'kinship', emphasizing the bonds of unity and support among individuals.
    • āyati: This means 'to attain' or 'to reach', indicating a goal or aspiration.
  • adhi no gāta marutaḥ sadā hi va āpitvam asti nidhruvi
    • adhi: This means 'over' or 'above', suggesting a higher perspective or guidance.
    • no: This is a pronoun meaning 'our', indicating a collective identity.
    • gāta: This refers to 'song' or 'hymn', symbolizing the power of expression and communication.
    • marutaḥ: This term refers to the 'winds' or 'storm gods', representing the forces of nature and their influence on life.
    • sadā: This means 'always' or 'eternally', indicating a timeless quality.
    • hi: This is a particle that adds emphasis, similar to 'indeed'.
    • va: This means 'or', suggesting an alternative or choice.
    • āpítvam: This refers to 'protection' or 'shelter', indicating a sense of security.
    • asti: This means 'exists' or 'is', affirming the presence of something.
    • nidhruvi: This term can be interpreted as 'unwavering' or 'constant', suggesting stability and reliability.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the transient nature of life and the importance of truthfulness, unity, and support among individuals. It calls for a higher perspective and the need for protection and stability in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster relationships based on trust and support, encouraging individuals to seek truth and maintain strong bonds with one another. It serves as a reminder that while life is fleeting, the values of truth, kinship, and mutual support are eternal and essential for a harmonious existence.
