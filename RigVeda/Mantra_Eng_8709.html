अनूपे गोमान गोभिर अक्षाः सोमो दुग्धाभिर अक्षाः |समुद्रं न संवराण्य अग्मन मन्दी मदाया तोषते ||
anūpe gomān gobhir akṣāḥ somo dugdhābhir akṣāḥ | samudraṁ na saṁvaraṇāny agman mandī madāya tośate ||
  • anūpe: This word translates to "in the beautiful" or "in the pleasant." It signifies a state of beauty and harmony.
  • gomān: Refers to "the one who possesses cows." In Vedic culture, cows are symbols of wealth and prosperity.
  • gobhir: This means "with the cows" or "by the cows," emphasizing the importance of cattle in sustenance and livelihood.
  • akṣāḥ: Translates to "eyes" or "sight," which can symbolize perception, awareness, or insight.
  • somo: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • dugdhābhir: This means "with milk," indicating nourishment, purity, and sustenance.
  • samudraṁ: Translates to "ocean," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • saṁvaraṇāny: Refers to "restrictions" or "barriers," suggesting limitations or obstacles.
  • agman: This means "they have come" or "arrived," indicating the presence or arrival of something significant.
  • mandī: Refers to "delight" or "joy," suggesting a state of happiness or fulfillment.
  • madāya: This translates to "in intoxication" or "in ecstasy," indicating a state of bliss or heightened awareness.
  • tośate: Means "to be pleased" or "to rejoice," indicating satisfaction and contentment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a celebration of abundance and the interconnectedness of life. It highlights the beauty of nature, the importance of cattle as a source of wealth, and the nourishing qualities of milk and the ocean. The arrival of joy and ecstasy signifies a state of fulfillment that transcends barriers.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Agriculture and Livestock: The emphasis on cows reflects the importance of sustainable farming and animal husbandry in providing for communities.
  • Spiritual Practices: The reference to Soma and the ocean can inspire individuals to seek deeper spiritual experiences and connection with nature.
  • Community and Relationships: The joy and fulfillment mentioned in the mantra can be seen in the importance of community gatherings and celebrations that bring people together.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the celebration of life, abundance, and the joy that comes from harmony with nature and community. It encourages individuals to appreciate the beauty around them, recognize the value of sustenance, and seek fulfillment in both spiritual and material aspects of life. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a life of richness and joy, transcending barriers and embracing the interconnectedness of all beings.
