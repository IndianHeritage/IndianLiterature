चोदयित्री सूनृतानां चेतन्ती सुमतिनाम् |यज्ञं दधे सरस्वती ||
codayitrī sūnṛtānāṁ cetantī sumatīnām | yajñaṁ dadhe sarasvatī ||
  • codayitrī: This word translates to "the one who inspires" or "the one who motivates." In a real-life context, this can refer to a mentor or a leader who encourages others to pursue their goals and aspirations.
  • sūnṛtānāṁ: This means "of those who are truthful" or "of the righteous." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts. For example, in a workplace, a team that values transparency and truthfulness fosters a positive environment and builds trust among its members.
  • cetantī: This word means "the one who thinks" or "the one who contemplates." It highlights the significance of reflection and thoughtful consideration in decision-making. In everyday life, taking time to think before acting can lead to better outcomes, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • sumatīnām: This translates to "of those with good intellect" or "of the wise." It signifies the value of wisdom and discernment. For instance, wise individuals often make informed choices that benefit themselves and those around them, such as community leaders who advocate for sustainable practices.
  • yajñaṁ: This word refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." In a broader sense, it can represent any act of giving or service to others. For example, volunteering time or resources to help those in need is a modern-day yajña that contributes to the welfare of society.
  • dadhe: This means "to establish" or "to create." It implies the act of bringing something into existence or making it happen. In real life, this can be seen in entrepreneurs who establish businesses that provide jobs and services to their communities.
  • sarasvatī: This refers to the goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and learning. In a practical sense, seeking knowledge through education and continuous learning is essential for personal and professional growth. For example, students who pursue higher education embody the essence of Sarasvatī by striving for knowledge and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra "codayitrī sūnṛtānāṁ cetantī sumatīnām | yajñaṁ dadhe sarasvatī ||" emphasizes the importance of inspiration, truthfulness, contemplation, wisdom, sacrifice, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, these qualities are applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership, education, and community service. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings and foster a culture of integrity and growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action for individuals to inspire and uplift one another while striving for wisdom and knowledge, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
