घृतेन द्यावापृथिवी अभिवृते घृतश्रियā घृतपृचā घृतावृद्धā |उर्वी पृथ्वी होत्रवूर्ये पुरोहिते ते इद् विप्रā ईळते सुम्नम इष्टये ||
ghṛtena dyāvāpṛthivī abhīvṛte ghṛtaśriyā ghṛtapṛcā ghṛtāvṛdhā | urvī pṛthvī hotṛvūrye purohite te id viprā īḻate sumnam iṣṭaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ghṛtena: with ghee (clarified butter)
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • abhīvṛte: having been filled or covered
    • ghṛtaśriyā: with the glory of ghee
    • ghṛtapṛcā: with the essence of ghee
    • ghṛtāvṛdhā: nourished by ghee
    • urvī: the vastness or expanse
    • pṛthvī: the earth
    • hotṛvūrye: in the strength of the priest
    • purohite: in the presence of the priest
    • te: your
    • id: this
    • viprā: the wise or learned
    • īḻate: invoke or call upon
    • sumnam: auspiciousness
    • iṣṭaye: for the purpose of offerings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of ghee (ghṛta) in rituals and offerings, symbolizing purity, nourishment, and prosperity.
    • The phrase "ghṛtena dyāvāpṛthivī abhīvṛte" suggests that heaven and earth are nourished and filled with the essence of ghee, indicating a connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
    • Ghee is often used in Hindu rituals as a medium to connect with the divine, representing the offering of one's best to the gods.
    • The mention of "hotṛvūrye" and "purohite" highlights the role of priests in rituals, who invoke blessings and auspiciousness on behalf of the community.
    • The invocation of "viprā" signifies the importance of wisdom and learned individuals in society, who guide and lead others towards prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu weddings, ghee is often used in the sacred fire (homa) to invoke blessings for the couple's future.
    • During festivals like Diwali, ghee lamps are lit to symbolize the dispelling of darkness and the welcoming of prosperity.
    • In daily life, cooking with ghee is considered beneficial for health, symbolizing nourishment and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and earthly realms through the medium of offerings, particularly ghee.
    • It highlights the role of learned individuals and priests in invoking blessings for the community, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and guidance.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in rituals, celebrations, and daily practices that promote purity, nourishment, and auspiciousness.
