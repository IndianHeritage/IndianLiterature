अरं क्षयाय नो महे विश्वा रूपाण्य आविशन् | इन्द्रं जैत्राय हर्षया शचीपतिम् ||
araṁ kṣayāya no mahe viśvā rūpāṇy āviśan | indraṁ jaitrāya harṣayā śacīpatim ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • araṁ: This word can be interpreted as "to us" or "for us," indicating a plea or invocation directed towards a higher power.
    • kṣayāya: This term means "for the destruction" or "for the removal," suggesting a desire to eliminate negativity or obstacles.
    • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our," emphasizing the personal connection to the request being made.
    • mahe: Translated as "great" or "mighty," it refers to the strength or power of the divine being invoked.
    • viśvā: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating a holistic approach to the invocation.
    • rūpāṇy: Meaning "forms" or "appearances," it suggests a recognition of the various manifestations of the divine or the universe.
    • āviśan: This term means "to enter" or "to pervade," indicating a desire for the divine presence to fill or envelop the surroundings.
    • indraṁ: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
    • jaitrāya: This word means "for victory," indicating a request for triumph over challenges.
    • harṣayā: Meaning "with joy" or "to bring happiness," it emphasizes the emotional aspect of the invocation.
    • śacīpatim: This term refers to "the lord of strength," often associated with Indra, highlighting the qualities of power and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for an important exam or presentation, individuals may recite this mantra to seek strength and clarity, hoping to remove any mental blocks and invite positive energy.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective strength and unity, aiming for a successful outcome in group endeavors.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on this mantra as a reminder to overcome obstacles in their lives, fostering a mindset of resilience and positivity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving success.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of universal forms and the power of collective strength, encouraging individuals to seek help from higher powers in their pursuits.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a motivational tool, reminding us to embrace positivity and resilience in the face of adversity.
