त्वां अग्ने प्रदिव आहुतं घृतैः सुम्नायवः सुशमिधा सम ईधिरे | स वावृधाना ओषधिभिर उक्षितोऽभि ज्रयांसी पार्थिवा विं तिष्ठसे ||
tvām agne pradiva āhutaṁ ghṛtaiḥ sumnāyavaḥ suṣamidhā sam īdhire | sa vāvṛdhāna oṣadhībhir ukṣito3 'bhi jrayāṁsi pārthivā vi tiṣṭhase ||
  • tvām - "You" or "to you": This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy and transformation.
  • agne - "O Agni": A direct address to the deity, invoking his presence and blessings.
  • pradiva - "in the sky" or "heavenly": This indicates the divine nature of Agni, connecting him to the celestial realm.
  • āhutaṁ - "offered" or "invoked": This signifies the act of making offerings to Agni, which is a crucial part of Vedic rituals.
  • ghṛtaiḥ - "with clarified butter": Ghee is a sacred substance in Hindu rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
  • sumnāyavaḥ - "with auspiciousness": This word emphasizes the positive and beneficial nature of the offerings made to Agni.
  • suṣamidhā - "with good kindling": This refers to the proper way of igniting the fire, which is essential for the rituals to be effective.
  • sam īdhire - "may they come together": This phrase expresses a desire for unity and harmony among the offerings and the divine.
  • sa - "he" or "that": Referring back to Agni, indicating his role in the process.
  • vāvṛdhāna - "may he increase": A plea for growth and abundance, both in spiritual and material aspects.
  • oṣadhībhir - "with herbs": This highlights the connection between Agni and the natural world, emphasizing the importance of plants and herbs in rituals.
  • ukṣito3 - "nourished" or "stimulated": This suggests that the offerings are meant to invigorate and energize Agni.
  • 'bhi - "with": A preposition indicating the means by which the offerings are made.
  • jrayāṁsi - "the mortal beings": This refers to humans and their connection to the divine through rituals.
  • pārthivā - "earthly" or "of the earth": This emphasizes the physical aspect of existence and the importance of grounding spiritual practices in the material world.
  • vi tiṣṭhase - "you stand": A declaration of Agni's presence and stability in the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects the earthly and celestial realms. It emphasizes the importance of offerings made with purity and intention, invoking blessings for growth and harmony. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or even in personal practices where one seeks to invite positive energy and transformation. The act of making offerings, whether in a spiritual or metaphorical sense, can lead to personal growth, community bonding, and a deeper connection with nature and the divine.
