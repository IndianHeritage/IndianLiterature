आवह कु्त्सम् इन्द्र यस्मिञ्चाकन् प्रावो युध्यन्तं वृषभं दशद्युम् | शफच्युतो रेणुर नक्षत द्यामुच् श्वैत्रेयो नृषाह्याय तस्थौ ||
āvaḥ kutsam indra yasmiñ cākan prāvo yudhyantaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ daśadyum | śaphacyuto reṇur nakṣata dyām uc chvaitreyo nṛṣāhyāya tasthau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āvaḥ: Invoke or bring forth
    • kutsam: The one who is worthy of respect
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power
    • yasmiñ: In whom or in which
    • cākan: The one who is capable or skilled
    • prāvo: The one who is powerful or strong
    • yudhyantaṁ: Engaged in battle or conflict
    • vṛṣabhaṁ: The bull, symbolizing strength and virility
    • daśadyum: Tenfold or multiple
    • śaphacyuto: The one who is free from obstacles
    • reṇur: Dust or particles, often symbolizing the earth
    • nakṣata: Stars or celestial bodies
    • dyām: The sky or heavens
    • uc: High or elevated
    • shvaitreyo: The one who is pure or virtuous
    • nṛṣāhyāya: For the benefit of humanity
    • tasthau: Stood or remained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, asking for his presence and support in overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to "yudhyantaṁ" signifies the struggles and battles we face in life, whether they are personal, social, or professional.
    • The "vṛṣabhaṁ" symbolizes strength and resilience, encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their endeavors.
    • The mention of "nakṣata dyām" connects the earthly struggles with the celestial, suggesting that our efforts are part of a larger cosmic order.
    • The phrase "nṛṣāhyāya" emphasizes the importance of these struggles for the greater good of humanity, highlighting the interconnectedness of individual and collective efforts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, when teams face challenges, invoking the spirit of Indra can symbolize rallying together to overcome obstacles, much like a team in a sports match.
    • In personal life, individuals may face battles such as health issues or relationship conflicts. This mantra serves as a reminder to draw upon inner strength and seek support from others.
    • In community service, the mantra can inspire collective action for social causes, emphasizing that individual efforts contribute to the welfare of society.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, unity, and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that our struggles are not isolated but part of a larger cosmic and social framework.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal challenges to collective efforts in community building, encouraging individuals to invoke their inner strength and work together for a common purpose.
