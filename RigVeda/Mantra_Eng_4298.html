विजिहीष्व वनस्पते योनिः सूष्यन्त्याऽ इव |श्रुतं मे अश्विनाः हवं सप्तवध्रिं च मुचताम् ||
vi jihīṣva vanaspate yoniḥ sūṣyantyā iva | śrutam me aśvinā havaṁ saptavadhriṁ ca muñcatam ||
  • Translation: "O Vanaspati (Lord of the Forest), may you grant me the strength to overcome obstacles, just as the plants thrive and flourish. I call upon the Aśvins to bestow upon me the knowledge that liberates me from the seven bonds of existence."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi jihīṣva: "may you grant" - a plea for assistance and strength.
    • vanaspate: "O Lord of the Forest" - a reference to nature and its nurturing qualities.
    • yoniḥ: "source" or "origin" - signifies the root or foundation of life.
    • sūṣyantyā iva: "just as the plants thrive" - a metaphor for growth and resilience.
    • śrutam: "knowledge" - the wisdom that leads to liberation.
    • me: "to me" - a personal appeal for guidance.
    • aśvinā: "O Aśvins" - invoking the twin deities associated with health and vitality.
    • havaṁ: "I call upon" - an invocation for divine assistance.
    • saptavadhriṁ: "seven bonds" - representing the various attachments and limitations in life.
    • ca: "and" - connecting the plea for knowledge and liberation.
    • muñcatam: "may you liberate" - a request for freedom from constraints.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as facing a difficult exam, one might invoke the essence of this mantra by seeking strength and clarity of mind, akin to how plants draw nourishment from the earth to grow.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek knowledge and wisdom to overcome their limitations, much like the Aśvins are called upon for guidance in achieving health and vitality.
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the importance of nature (vanaspati) can inspire actions that promote sustainability and respect for the ecosystem, reflecting the mantra's reverence for the natural world.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, knowledge, and liberation from the bonds that hold us back. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and human existence, reminding us that just as plants thrive with the right conditions, we too can flourish when we seek wisdom and support. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal challenges to broader societal issues, encouraging individuals to seek growth, resilience, and a deeper understanding of their place in the world.
