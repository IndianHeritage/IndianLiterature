मन्त्र: येन वृद्धो न शवसातुरो न स्वाभिरूतिभिः |सोमः सुतः स इन्द्र तेऽस्ति स्वधापते मदः ||
yena vṛddho na śavasā turo na svābhir ūtibhiḥ | somaḥ sutaḥ sa indra te 'sti svadhāpate madaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yena: by whom
    • vṛddho: the elder or the wise
    • na: not
    • śavasā: by the dead or lifeless
    • turo: swift or powerful
    • na: not
    • svābhir: by one's own
    • ūtibhiḥ: with strength or vigor
    • somaḥ: Soma, the divine drink or nectar
    • sutaḥ: son or offspring
    • sa: he
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • te: to you
    • 'sti: is
    • svadhāpate: lord of one's own self or destiny
    • madaḥ: intoxication or bliss
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and wisdom that comes from divine sources, particularly through the blessings of Indra and the essence of Soma.
    • The phrase "by whom the elder is not lifeless" suggests that true wisdom and strength come from a source that is alive and vibrant, not from mere tradition or lifeless customs.
    • The mention of "Soma" indicates the importance of spiritual nourishment and the divine intoxication that leads to enlightenment and joy.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate power and authority, and the mantra calls upon this divine force to bless the individual with strength and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies wisdom and strength can inspire their team to achieve greater heights, much like Indra inspires the gods.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek spiritual practices (akin to Soma) that provide them with clarity and purpose, leading to a more fulfilling life.
    • Communities that value the wisdom of their elders while also embracing new ideas can thrive, reflecting the balance of tradition and innovation suggested in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine wisdom and strength in our lives.
    • It teaches us to seek spiritual nourishment and to recognize the power of enlightened leadership.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and a harmonious community.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that true strength and wisdom come from a divine source, guiding us towards a life of purpose and fulfillment.
