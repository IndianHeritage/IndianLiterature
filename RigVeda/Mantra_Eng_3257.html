एव न इन्द्रो मगवाऽ विरप्शी करत सत्याऽ चर्षणिधृत् अनर्वा |त्वं राजा जनुषां धेहि अस्मे अधि श्रवो माहिनं यज जारित्रे ||
evā na indro maghavā virapśī karat satyā carṣaṇīdhṛd anarvā | tvaṁ rājā januṣāṁ dhehy asme adhi śravo māhinaṁ yaj jaritre ||
  • evā na indro maghavā virapśī: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, Indra, the mighty one, is powerful." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and authority. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or a figure of authority who possesses the ability to influence and guide others.
  • karat satyā carṣaṇīdhṛd anarvā: This translates to "He who holds the truth and is the protector of the earth." This emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and integrity in leadership. For example, a business leader who prioritizes ethical practices and transparency can inspire trust and loyalty among employees and customers.
  • tvaṁ rājā januṣāṁ dhehy asme: This means "You, O King, grant us strength." Here, the mantra calls upon Indra to bestow strength and support. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another during challenging times, such as natural disasters or social movements.
  • adhi śravo māhinaṁ yaj jaritre: This translates to "May we attain glory through our sacrifices." This highlights the significance of dedication and effort in achieving success. For instance, students who work hard and make sacrifices in their studies often reap the rewards in the form of academic achievements and future opportunities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, truthfulness, and the importance of community support and sacrifice. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership in organizations, ethical business practices, community resilience, and personal growth through dedication. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can strive for a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
