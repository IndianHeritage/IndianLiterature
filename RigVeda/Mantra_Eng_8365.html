अध श्वेतं कलशं गोभिरक्तं कर्ष्मन्नावाज्यक्रमीत् ससवान् |आ हिन्विरे मनसा देवयन्तः कक्षीवते शतहिमाय गोनाम् ||
adha śvetaṁ kalaśaṁ gobhir aktaṁ kārṣmann ā vājy akramīt sasavān | ā hinvire manasā devayantaḥ kakṣīvate śatahimāya gonām ||
  • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a direction or position.
  • Śvetaṁ: Meaning "white," it symbolizes purity, clarity, and auspiciousness.
  • Kalaśaṁ: Refers to a "pot" or "vessel," often used in rituals to hold sacred substances.
  • Gobhir: This means "by the cows," which in Vedic culture represents wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • Aktaṁ: Translates to "filled" or "saturated," suggesting abundance.
  • Kārṣmann: Refers to "the field" or "agriculture," indicating the importance of farming and sustenance.
  • Ā vājy: This means "not to be disturbed" or "unharmed," implying protection and stability.
  • Akramīt: Translates to "to be approached" or "to be reached," suggesting accessibility or attainability.
  • Sasavān: Meaning "having strength" or "powerful," indicating vitality and energy.
  • Ā hinvire: This means "they invoke" or "they call upon," suggesting a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • Manasā: Refers to "with the mind," indicating the importance of intention and thought in actions.
  • Devayantaḥ: Meaning "the divine beings" or "gods," representing higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • Kakṣīvate: This translates to "to the one who protects" or "to the guardian," indicating a plea for safeguarding.
  • Śatahimāya: Meaning "hundredfold" or "abundant," suggesting a desire for prosperity and success.
  • Gonām: Refers to "of the cows," again emphasizing the significance of cattle in Vedic culture as symbols of wealth and sustenance.

This mantra invokes the blessings of divine beings for prosperity, protection, and abundance in agricultural endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of purity, intention, and the nurturing aspects of nature, represented by the white pot filled with the essence of life, symbolized by cows.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Agricultural Practices: Farmers can recite this mantra to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest and protection from natural calamities.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke prosperity and success in their endeavors, ensuring their ventures are fruitful.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the mantra's themes of purity and intention, applying them to their personal and spiritual development.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving abundance and protection in various aspects of life. By aligning one's intentions with the principles of purity and nurturing, individuals can foster a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine, leading to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
