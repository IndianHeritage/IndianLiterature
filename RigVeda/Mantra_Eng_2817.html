त्रीणि राजाना विदथे पुरूणि परि विश्वानि भूषथः सदांसी |अपश्याम atra मनसा जगन्वान व्रते गन्धर्वाम् अपि वायुकेशान ||
trīṇi rājānā vidathe purūṇi pari viśvāni bhūṣathaḥ sadāṁsi | apaśyam atra manasā jaganvān vrate gandharvām̐ api vāyukeśān ||
  • trīṇi rājānā: This phrase translates to "three kings." In a broader context, it can symbolize the three primary aspects of existence or the three forces that govern life: creation, preservation, and destruction.
  • vidathe purūṇi: This means "in the assembly of the ancient." It refers to the gathering of wise individuals or sages who possess deep knowledge and understanding of the universe.
  • pari viśvāni bhūṣathaḥ: This translates to "adorn all the worlds." It signifies the importance of beautifying and enriching the universe through knowledge, wisdom, and positive actions.
  • sadāṁsi: This word means "eternal." It emphasizes the everlasting nature of truth and righteousness.
  • apaśyam atra manasā: This translates to "I have not seen here with my mind." It suggests a limitation of perception, indicating that there are truths and realities beyond our immediate understanding.
  • jaganvān: This means "the one who pervades the universe." It refers to the divine presence that exists in all things, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • vrate: This translates to "in the vow" or "in the commitment." It signifies the importance of dedication and discipline in one's spiritual and moral journey.
  • gandharvām̐ api vāyukeśān: This phrase means "even the celestial beings and the wind." It highlights the presence of divine forces and energies that influence our lives and the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts that are applicable in various real-life scenarios. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and knowledge, to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings, and to commit to a path of righteousness. In a world where distractions abound, this mantra serves as a reminder to focus on eternal truths and to strive for a deeper understanding of existence. By embodying these principles, one can contribute positively to the world, enriching not only their own life but also the lives of others. This mantra can be particularly relevant in contexts such as leadership, community building, and personal development, where the values of wisdom, commitment, and interconnectedness are essential for success and harmony.
