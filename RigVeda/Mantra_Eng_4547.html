तव क्रत्वा तव तद दṁसनाभिर आमासु पक्वं शच्य न दीधः |और्णोर दुर उष्रियाभ्यो विदृढोद ऊर्वाद गा असृजो अंगिरस्वान ||
tava kratvā tava tad daṁsanābhir āmāsu pakvaṁ śacyā ni dīdhaḥ | aurṇor dura usriyābhyo vi dṛḻhod ūrvād gā asṛjo aṅgirasvān ||
  • tava kratvā - "by your power" or "by your will": This phrase emphasizes the strength and authority of a higher power, suggesting that everything in existence is a result of divine will. In real life, this can be seen in the way people attribute their successes or failures to a higher power or fate.
  • tava tad daṁsanābhir - "by your fierce teeth": This metaphorical expression can be interpreted as the strength and ferocity of the divine. It signifies the ability to overcome obstacles and challenges. For example, in a competitive environment, individuals may channel their inner strength to face challenges, akin to the fierce determination suggested by this phrase.
  • āmāsu pakvaṁ - "in the ripe fruits": This can be understood as the results of one's actions or efforts. Just as ripe fruits are the outcome of a plant's growth, our achievements are the fruits of our labor. In everyday life, this can relate to the rewards one reaps from hard work, such as a promotion at work or the successful completion of a project.
  • śacyā ni dīdhaḥ - "may we not be harmed": This line expresses a plea for protection and safety. In real life, this resonates with the universal desire for security, whether it be physical, emotional, or financial. People often seek protection through various means, such as community support, legal systems, or spiritual practices.
  • aurṇor dura usriyābhyo - "from the distant and powerful": This phrase suggests seeking refuge or strength from forces that are beyond our immediate control. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking guidance from mentors, leaders, or even spiritual figures who possess wisdom and strength that we may lack.
  • vi dṛḻhod ūrvād - "with firm resolve": This emphasizes the importance of determination and steadfastness in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who persist through difficulties, such as athletes training for competitions or entrepreneurs facing business challenges.
  • gā asṛjo aṅgirasvān - "the Angiras, the seers": This refers to the wise and enlightened beings who guide humanity. In contemporary life, this can be likened to the role of teachers, philosophers, or spiritual leaders who provide insight and direction in times of uncertainty.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine power, the fruits of our actions, the need for protection, and the importance of determination and guidance. Its significance lies in the reminder that while we strive for success and safety, we are also part of a larger cosmic order. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength from within and from higher powers, to work diligently towards their goals, and to remain resilient in the face of challenges. It encourages a holistic approach to life, where one acknowledges both personal effort and the influence of greater forces at play.
