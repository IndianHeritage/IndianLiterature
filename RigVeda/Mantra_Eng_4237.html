सम्राजाऽय घृतयोनī मित्रश्चोभा वरुणश्च |देवाऽदेवेषु प्रशस्ताः ||
samrājā yā ghṛtayonī mitraś cobhā varuṇaś ca | devā deveṣu praśastā ||
  • Mantra: "samrājā yā ghṛtayonī mitraś cobhā varuṇaś ca | devā deveṣu praśastā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samrājā: The great ruler or king.
    • yā: Who or that which.
    • ghṛtayonī: Born of ghee or clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
    • mitraś: Friend or ally, often referring to the sun or a benevolent force.
    • cobhā: Both or together.
    • varuṇaś: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • ca: And.
    • devā: Gods or divine beings.
    • deveṣu: Among the gods.
    • praśastā: Praised or honored.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a supreme ruler who is nourished by purity and is a friend to all.
    • It highlights the duality of divine forces, represented by Mitra and Varuna, who work together in harmony.
    • The mention of gods being praised among other gods signifies the importance of reverence and acknowledgment of divine powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a good ruler (samrājā) is one who nurtures their people (ghṛtayonī) and fosters friendships (mitraś) for the greater good.
    • In environmental conservation, the balance of water (varuṇa) and sunlight (mitra) is crucial for sustaining life, reflecting the harmony mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community building, recognizing and honoring the contributions of all members (devā deveṣu praśastā) fosters unity and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of leadership that is nurturing, harmonious, and respectful of divine forces.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as governance, environmental stewardship, and community relations.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and leaders can create a more balanced and respectful society.
