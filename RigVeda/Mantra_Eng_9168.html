ग्रावां वदन्न अप रक्षांसी सेधतु दुष्ष्वप्न्यं निरृतिं विश्वम् अत्रिणम् |आदित्यं शर्मा मरुताम अशीमहि तद देवानाम अवो अद्या वृणीमहे ||
grāvā vadann apa rakṣāṁsi sedhatu duṣṣvapnyaṁ nirṛtiṁ viśvam atriṇam | ādityaṁ śarma marutām aśīmahi tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the protective forces that guard us, keep away all evil dreams and misfortunes. We invoke the Sun for shelter, and we seek the blessings of the Maruts (storm deities). Today, we choose the divine beings."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • grāvā: protective forces or guardians
    • vadann: speaking or uttering
    • apa: away or apart
    • rakṣāṁsi: protections or safeguards
    • sedhatu: may they establish or secure
    • duṣṣvapnyaṁ: evil dreams or nightmares
    • nirṛtiṁ: misfortune or calamity
    • viśvam: all or universal
    • atriṇam: to be free from
    • ādityaṁ: the Sun
    • śarma: shelter or protection
    • marutām: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • aśīmahi: we invoke or we seek
    • tad: that
    • devānām: of the divine beings
    • avo: may we call upon
    • adyā: today
    • vṛṇīmahe: we choose or we select
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of stress or anxiety, individuals often seek protection from negative thoughts or experiences. This mantra serves as a reminder to focus on positive energies and seek divine protection.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often recite mantras to invoke blessings and safeguard against misfortunes, similar to the invocation of the Sun and Maruts in this mantra.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to ward off evil spirits or bad dreams, reflecting the universal human desire for safety and security.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of protective forces and divine beings to shield individuals from negativity and misfortune. It emphasizes the importance of seeking shelter in higher powers during challenging times. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as personal challenges, communal gatherings, or spiritual practices, where individuals seek to create a positive and protective environment. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of security and peace, reinforcing the belief in the power of divine protection and guidance.
