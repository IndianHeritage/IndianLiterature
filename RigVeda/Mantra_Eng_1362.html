स्विध्मा यद् वनधितिर अपस्यात् सूरो अध्वरे परि रोधना गोः |यद् धा प्रभासि कृत्व्याम् अनु dyūn अनर्विशे पश्विषे तुराय ||
svidhmā yad vanadhitir apasyāt sūro adhvare pari rodhanā goḥ | yad dha prabhāsi kṛtvyām̐ anu dyūn anarviśe paśviṣe turāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svidhmā: shining, illuminating
    • yad: that which
    • vanadhitir: the forest or nature
    • apasyāt: may not be seen or perceived
    • sūro: the hero or the strong one
    • adhvare: in the sacrifice or ritual
    • pari: around or in all directions
    • rodhanā: the one who protects or guards
    • goḥ: of the cow or the sacred
    • yad: that which
    • dha: to hold or to maintain
    • prabhāsi: shines or illuminates
    • kṛtvyām̐: in the performance of duties
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • dyūn: the heavens or the celestial
    • anarviśe: without disturbance or chaos
    • paśviṣe: in the realm of living beings
    • turāya: swiftly or quickly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the illuminating power of nature and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of perception and awareness in recognizing the strength and protection offered by the natural world.
    • The reference to the "hero" in the context of sacrifice suggests that true strength lies in selfless actions and duties performed for the greater good.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes nourishment, purity, and the sacredness of life, which are essential in many cultures.
    • The mantra encourages us to maintain our duties while being aware of the divine presence in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the importance of nature's beauty and strength can inspire individuals to protect ecosystems.
    • In community service, performing selfless acts can be seen as a form of sacrifice that contributes to the well-being of others.
    • In personal development, being aware of one's duties and responsibilities can lead to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
    • In agriculture, respecting and nurturing livestock, such as cows, reflects the sacredness of life and the importance of sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine in nature and our responsibilities towards it.
    • It teaches us that true strength comes from selfless actions and a deep connection with the world around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, fostering respect for nature, community, and personal growth.
