सुषुप्वांसा ऋभवस्तद अपृच्छतागोह्य क इदं नो अबूबुधत |श्वानं बस्तो बोधयितारम अब्रवीत संवत्सर इदं अद्याव्याख्यात ||
suṣupvāṁsa ṛbhavas tad apṛcchatāgohya ka idaṁ no abūbudhat | śvānam basto bodhayitāram abravīt saṁvatsara idam adyā vy akhyata ||
  • suṣupvāṁsa: This term can be broken down into "su" meaning good or well, and "ṣupvāṁsa" which refers to a state of deep sleep or a state of being well-rested. In real life, this can be related to the importance of quality sleep for mental and physical health.
  • ṛbhavas: This word signifies the wise or the enlightened ones. It can be associated with individuals who possess knowledge and understanding, such as teachers or mentors in our lives who guide us through challenges.
  • tad apṛcchatāgohya: "Tad" means that, "apṛcchatā" refers to questioning or inquiry, and "gohya" means hidden or secret. This phrase suggests the act of seeking knowledge about the hidden aspects of life, akin to how scientists or philosophers explore the unknown.
  • ka idaṁ no abūbudhat: "Ka" means who, "idaṁ" means this, and "no abūbudhat" translates to "has not awakened us." This can be interpreted as a call to awareness, urging individuals to awaken to the truths around them, similar to how a mentor might challenge a student to think critically.
  • śvānam basto: "Śvānam" refers to the dog, a symbol of loyalty and companionship, while "basto" can mean the one who is in charge or the master. This can relate to the bond between humans and pets, emphasizing loyalty and trust in relationships.
  • bodhayitāram: This term means the one who enlightens or awakens. It can be likened to a teacher or a guide who helps others realize their potential, much like a coach who inspires athletes to achieve their best.
  • abravīt: This means "he spoke" or "he declared." It signifies the importance of communication and sharing knowledge, akin to how leaders or influential figures convey messages that inspire change.
  • saṁvatsara idam adyā vy akhyata: "Saṁvatsara" refers to a year or a cycle, "idyā" means today, and "vy akhyata" translates to "has been declared." This can be seen as a reminder of the cyclical nature of time and the importance of living in the present, similar to how we celebrate milestones and reflect on our growth over the years.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of awakening to knowledge, the importance of guidance from wise individuals, and the value of companionship and loyalty. In real life, it encourages us to seek deeper understanding, appreciate the relationships we have, and recognize the cyclical nature of our experiences. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, personal development, and fostering meaningful connections with others. It serves as a reminder to remain curious, engaged, and aware of the world around us.
