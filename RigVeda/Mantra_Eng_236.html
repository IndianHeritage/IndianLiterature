मरत्वन्तं हवामहे इन्द्रं आ सोमपीतये |सजूर गणेन तृम्पतु ||
marutvantaṁ havāmaha indram ā somapītaye | sajūr gaṇena tṛmpatu ||
  • Mantra: "marutvantaṁ havāmaha indram ā somapītaye | sajūr gaṇena tṛmpatu ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke the Maruts, the powerful ones, for the sake of Indra, who drinks the soma. May he be pleased with our offerings and come to us with his hosts."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Marutvantaṁ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with strength and power.
    • Havāmaha: Means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a prayer or offering.
    • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods, known for his valor and as a protector.
    • Ā: A preposition meaning "for" or "towards," indicating the purpose of the invocation.
    • Somapītaye: Refers to the one who drinks soma, a sacred drink that grants immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Sajūr: Means "with his hosts" or "with the assembly," referring to the collective power of the deities.
    • Gaṇena: Means "with the group" or "with the gathering," emphasizing community and collective effort.
    • Tṛmpatu: Means "may he be pleased" or "may he be satisfied," expressing a desire for divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to invoke blessings for a successful event, similar to how the mantra calls upon the Maruts and Indra for support.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities, akin to the invocation of Indra for his favor and blessings, reflecting the importance of gratitude and respect in various cultures.
    • In times of difficulty, individuals may seek strength and guidance from a higher power, paralleling the call for the Maruts' strength and Indra's protection.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine powers for strength, protection, and community support. It highlights the importance of collective effort in seeking blessings and the role of divine entities in human affairs. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community events, personal challenges, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine. The invocation serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers while fostering a sense of unity and purpose within communities.
