स्वयं चित् स मन्यते दाशुरिर जनो यत्रा सोमस्य तृम्पसि |इदं ते अन्नं युज्यं समुक्षितं तस्येहि प्र द्रवा पिब ||
svayaṁ cit sa manyate dāśurir jano yatrā somasya tṛmpasi | idaṁ te annaṁ yujyaṁ samukṣitaṁ tasyehi pra dravā piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svayaṁ: oneself
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • sa: he or that
    • manyate: thinks or believes
    • dāśurir: the one who is generous or a giver
    • jano: person or people
    • yatrā: where
    • somasya: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • tṛmpasi: is satisfied or fulfilled
    • idaṁ: this
    • te: for you
    • annaṁ: food or nourishment
    • yujyaṁ: suitable or appropriate
    • samukṣitaṁ: well-prepared or well-cooked
    • tasyehi: drink it
    • pra: forth or out
    • dravā: liquid or essence
    • piba: drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of self-awareness and generosity.
    • It suggests that a person who is aware of their own consciousness and is generous will find satisfaction in the sacred drink (soma), which symbolizes spiritual nourishment.
    • The phrase "this food is suitable for you" emphasizes the importance of nourishment that is not just physical but also spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, a person who shares resources and knowledge with others embodies the spirit of the mantra. For instance, a teacher who dedicates time to mentor students is practicing the generosity highlighted in the mantra.
    • In personal life, someone who takes time for self-reflection and mindfulness can experience a deeper sense of fulfillment, akin to the satisfaction derived from the soma.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and the importance of generosity in our interactions with others.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community service, education, and personal development, where the act of giving and being aware of oneself leads to a more fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that true nourishment comes not just from food but from our relationships and the consciousness we cultivate within ourselves.
