ऋज्राव इन्द्रोता आ ददē हरī ऋक्षस्य सूनवी |आश्वमेधस्य रोहितā ||
ṛjrāv indrota ā dade harī ṛkṣasya sūnavi | āśvamedhasya rohitā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛjrāv: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who is strong."
    • indrota: This refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon."
    • ā dade: This means "gives" or "bestows."
    • harī: This word signifies "the divine" or "the godly."
    • ṛkṣasya: This translates to "of the bear" or "of the constellation Ursa Major."
    • sūnavi: This means "the offspring" or "the progeny."
    • āśvamedhasya: This refers to "of the Ashvamedha sacrifice," a significant Vedic ritual.
    • rohitā: This translates to "the red" or "the one who is red," often associated with horses in the context of the Ashvamedha sacrifice.
  • Contextual Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the invocation of divine strength and blessings, particularly in the context of the Ashvamedha sacrifice, which was performed by kings to assert their sovereignty and power.
    • The mention of "the bear" can symbolize strength and protection, suggesting that the divine is called upon to safeguard the progeny and lineage of the performer.
    • The "red" aspect associated with horses signifies vitality and energy, essential for the success of the sacrifice and the prosperity of the kingdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the essence of this mantra can be seen in leadership and governance, where leaders invoke strength and wisdom to guide their people.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek divine blessings for their families, ensuring protection and prosperity, akin to the invocation of strength for one's lineage.
    • In cultural practices, rituals that honor ancestors and seek blessings for future generations reflect the same principles of invoking divine support for the well-being of one's progeny.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine invocation for strength and protection, particularly in the context of leadership and family.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of divine blessings, personal strength, and the prosperity of future generations.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance and support from higher powers in our endeavors, ensuring the well-being of ourselves and our families.
