युवाना पितरा पुनः सत्यमन्त्राः ऋजूयवः |ऋभवो विष्ट्य अकृत ||
yuvānā pitarā punaḥ satyamantrā ṛjūyavaḥ | ṛbhavo viṣṭy akrata ||
  • yuvānā: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential that comes with youth.
  • pitarā: This means "fathers" or "ancestors." It represents the lineage, heritage, and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • punaḥ: This translates to "again" or "once more." It indicates a sense of renewal or repetition, suggesting that certain values or teachings are revisited.
  • satyamantrā: This means "truthful mantras" or "sacred utterances of truth." It emphasizes the importance of truth and integrity in one's words and actions.
  • ṛjūyavaḥ: This can be interpreted as "straightforward" or "honest." It highlights the value of being upright and sincere in dealings.
  • ṛbhavo: This word refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened." It signifies those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • viṣṭy: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It indicates abundance and the blessings that come from living a righteous life.
  • akrata: This means "not created" or "not made." It suggests a state of being that is inherent or natural, rather than artificially constructed.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between youth, wisdom, truth, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of being truthful and straightforward, drawing from the wisdom of our ancestors while also recognizing the vitality of youth. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios:


  • In a family setting, the teachings of parents (pitarā) can guide the younger generation (yuvānā) to make honest choices (satyamantrā) that lead to a prosperous life (viṣṭy).
  • In a workplace, a culture of integrity (ṛjūyavaḥ) can foster an environment where young professionals thrive, learning from the experiences of their predecessors (punaḥ).
  • In community service, the wisdom of the elders (ṛbhavo) can inspire youth to engage in truthful and meaningful actions that benefit society (akrata).

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of intergenerational wisdom, the importance of truthfulness, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that by embracing these values, individuals can create a harmonious and thriving community. The teachings of the RigVeda are timeless and applicable in various aspects of life, encouraging us to honor our roots while striving for a better future.
