मन्त्र: चक्रिर दिवः पवते कृत्व्यो रसः महाम् अदब्दो वरुणो हुरुग्यते | असावी मित्रो वृजनेषु यज्ञियोऽत्यो न यूथे वृषयुः कनिक्रदत ||
cakrir divaḥ pavate kṛtvyo raso mahām̐ adabdho varuṇo hurug yate | asāvi mitro vṛjaneṣu yajñiyo 'tyo na yūthe vṛṣayuḥ kanikradat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakrir: He who moves or revolves
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • pavate: blows or moves
    • kṛtvyo: the actions or deeds
    • raso: essence or flavor
    • mahām̐: great or vast
    • adabdho: unrestrained or free
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • hurug: who is powerful
    • yate: strives or endeavors
    • asāvi: this one
    • mitro: friend or ally
    • vṛjaneṣu: in the realms of sacrifice
    • yajñiyo: pertaining to sacrifice or worship
    • 'tyo: who is beyond
    • na: not
    • yūthe: in the group or assembly
    • vṛṣayuḥ: the strong or powerful
    • kanikradat: who roars or thunders
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that govern the universe, particularly focusing on the actions of Varuna, the god of cosmic order.
    • The phrase "cakrir divaḥ pavate" suggests the movement of celestial bodies and the winds that are essential for life on Earth.
    • The mention of "raso mahām̐" indicates the importance of essence or flavor in our lives, symbolizing the richness of experiences.
    • Varuna is depicted as a powerful deity who is unrestrained and strives for cosmic order, reminding us of the balance in nature.
    • The term "asāvi mitro" emphasizes the idea of friendship and alliance, particularly in the context of sacrifices and rituals.
    • The mantra concludes with a reference to the powerful forces that can be both nurturing and destructive, akin to the duality of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the wind plays a crucial role in pollination and the dispersal of seeds, reflecting the divine movement mentioned in the mantra.
    • The essence of life can be seen in the flavors of food, which bring people together, much like the concept of friendship in the mantra.
    • In our daily lives, we often strive for balance, akin to Varuna's role in maintaining cosmic order, whether in personal relationships or environmental stewardship.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things in the universe, urging us to recognize the divine forces at play in our lives.
    • It highlights the importance of friendship, sacrifice, and the balance of nature, encouraging us to live harmoniously with our surroundings.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire us to engage in community service, environmental conservation, and fostering relationships that promote peace and understanding.
