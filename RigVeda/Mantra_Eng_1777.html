एकस्य चिन् मे विभ्व अस्त्व ओजो यां नु दधृष्वान कृण्वै मनिषा |अहं ह्य उग्रो मरुतो विदानो यानि च्यवम् इन्द्र इद ईश एषाम् ||
ekasya cin me vibhv a1stv ojo yā nu dadhṛṣvān kṛṇavai manīṣā | ahaṁ hy u1gro maruto vidāno yāni cyavam indra id īśa eṣām ||
  • ekasya: This word translates to "of one" or "of a single entity." It emphasizes unity and the importance of singular focus in our endeavors.
  • cin: Meaning "to know" or "to perceive," this word highlights the significance of awareness and understanding in our actions.
  • me: This translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or ownership of the knowledge and strength being referred to.
  • vibhv: This word means "wealth" or "abundance," suggesting that the knowledge and strength one possesses can lead to prosperity.
  • a1stv: This can be interpreted as "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a desire or intention for the manifestation of the qualities being invoked.
  • ojo: This translates to "strength" or "vigor," emphasizing the importance of physical and mental strength in achieving one's goals.
  • yā: Meaning "which," this word connects the qualities being discussed to the actions that follow.
  • nu: This word can be understood as "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statement being made.
  • dadhṛṣvān: This means "having held" or "having supported," indicating the importance of support and stability in one's pursuits.
  • kṛṇavai: This translates to "I create" or "I make," emphasizing the active role one must take in shaping their destiny.
  • manīṣā: Meaning "intellect" or "wisdom," this word highlights the necessity of thoughtful decision-making in the process of creation.
  • ahaṁ: This means "I," indicating the personal responsibility one has in their journey.
  • hy: This word can be translated as "indeed," reinforcing the assertion that follows.
  • ugro: This translates to "mighty" or "powerful," suggesting that one should harness their inner strength.
  • maruto: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities, this word symbolizes the dynamic and transformative forces in life.
  • vidāno: This means "knowing" or "understanding," emphasizing the importance of knowledge in navigating life's challenges.
  • yāni: Meaning "which," this word connects the knowledge to the actions that follow.
  • cyavam: This translates to "falling" or "declining," indicating the potential pitfalls one may encounter.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, this word symbolizes leadership and authority.
  • id: This means "this," pointing to the specific qualities or actions being discussed.
  • īśa: This translates to "lord" or "master," indicating a higher power or guiding force.
  • eṣām: Meaning "of these," this word refers to the qualities or actions previously mentioned.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, knowledge, strength, and personal responsibility in achieving one's goals. It encourages individuals to actively create their destiny while being aware of the dynamic forces in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and overcoming challenges. By embodying the qualities of wisdom, strength, and awareness, individuals can navigate their paths more effectively and achieve prosperity in their endeavors.
