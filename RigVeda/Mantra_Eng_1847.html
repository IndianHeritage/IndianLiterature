समत्सु त्वा शूर सताम उराणम् प्रपथिन्तमम् परितंस्यध्यै |सजोषसा इन्द्रं मादे क्षोणीः सूरीं चिद ये अनुमदन्ति वाजैः ||
samatsu tvā śūra satām urāṇam prapathintamam paritaṁsayadhyai | sajoṣasa indram made kṣoṇīḥ sūriṁ cid ye anumadanti vājaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samatsu: In harmony or together
    • tvā: You (referring to a deity or divine force)
    • śūra: Brave or heroic
    • satām: Of the good or virtuous
    • urāṇam: Of the great or powerful
    • prapathintamam: The best path or way
    • paritaṁsayadhyai: To be praised or honored
    • sajoṣasa: With joy or enthusiasm
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • made: In ecstasy or joy
    • kṣoṇīḥ: Earth or land
    • sūriṁ: The sun or a radiant being
    • cid: Who or that which
    • ye: Those who
    • anumadanti: Praise or support
    • vājaiḥ: With strength or vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of unity and harmony among the brave and virtuous.
    • It emphasizes the importance of following the best path and honoring those who deserve praise.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the call for divine support and strength in our endeavors.
    • The mention of the earth and the sun symbolizes the grounding and illuminating forces in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, when individuals work together harmoniously, they can achieve great success, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In community service, honoring and supporting those who contribute positively can lead to a more vibrant and supportive society.
    • In personal growth, following a virtuous path and seeking guidance from wise mentors can illuminate one's journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, virtue, and divine support in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, community building, and personal development.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
