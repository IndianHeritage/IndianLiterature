अत्याहियानां न हेतृभिर असृग्रम् वाजसातये | विवारमव्यं आशवः ||
atyā hiyānā na hetṛbhir asṛgraṁ vājasātaye | vi vāram avyam āśavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "atyā hiyānā na hetṛbhir asṛgraṁ vājasātaye | vi vāram avyam āśavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atyā: beyond, surpassing
    • hiyānā: those who are restrained or held back
    • na: not
    • hetṛbhir: by the doers or agents
    • asṛgraṁ: to be attained or achieved
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of strength or power
    • vi: indeed, truly
    • vāram: protection, refuge
    • avyam: without fear or danger
    • āśavaḥ: those who are swift or quick
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about transcending limitations and achieving strength.
    • It emphasizes that those who are restrained or held back cannot attain their goals through mere actions alone.
    • The mantra suggests that true strength comes from a state of fearlessness and swiftness in action.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who is held back by fear of failure may not achieve their full potential. By overcoming this fear and taking swift actions, they can surpass their limitations and contribute significantly to their organization.
    • In sports, an athlete who is overly cautious may miss opportunities to excel. Embracing a fearless attitude and acting decisively can lead to remarkable performances and victories.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of overcoming fear and limitations to achieve strength and success.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional development, and competitive environments.
    • By embodying the principles of fearlessness and swift action, individuals can unlock their true potential and achieve their goals.
