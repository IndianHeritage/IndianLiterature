यो न इदं-इदं पुरा प्र वस्य आनीनाय तं उ वः स्तुषे |सखाय इन्द्रं ऊतये ||
yo na idam-idam purā pra vasya ānināya tam u vaḥ stuṣe | sakhāya indram ūtaye ||
  • Mantra: "yo na idam-idam purā pra vasya ānināya tam u vaḥ stuṣe | sakhāya indram ūtaye ||"
  • Translation: "He who is not bound by this or that, who has come from afar, we invoke him; may we praise him, our friend Indra, for his strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • na: not
    • idam-idam: this or that
    • purā: from afar
    • pra: forth
    • vasya: bound
    • ānināya: having come
    • tam: him
    • u: and
    • vaḥ: we
    • stuṣe: praise
    • sakhāya: friend
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ūtaye: for strength
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of invoking a higher power, represented by Indra, who is not limited by worldly attachments or constraints.
    • The phrase "who is not bound by this or that" suggests a transcendence over dualities and material concerns, which can be likened to the concept of mindfulness in modern life, where one seeks to rise above distractions and focus on the present.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and power, represents the qualities we aspire to cultivate within ourselves. For example, in challenging situations, we often call upon our inner strength to overcome obstacles, much like invoking Indra's strength in this mantra.
    • The act of praising or invoking this higher power can be seen in various real-life scenarios, such as during moments of prayer, meditation, or even in motivational speeches where individuals seek inspiration from greater ideals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and guidance from a higher source, encouraging individuals to transcend their limitations and embrace their inner power.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and spiritual practices, where individuals strive to connect with their higher selves or seek inspiration from greater forces.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that we can invoke strength and wisdom from beyond ourselves, fostering resilience and empowerment in our daily lives.
