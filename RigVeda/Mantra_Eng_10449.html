यद् āशसā निःशसाभिशसोपरिम जाग्रतो यत् स्वपन्तः |अग्निर विश्वानि अप दुṣ्कृतानि अजुष्टानि आरे अस्मद दधातु ||
yad āśasā niḥśasābhiśasopārima jāgrato yat svapantaḥ | agnir viśvāny apa duṣkṛtāny ajuṣṭāny āre asmad dadhātu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • āśasā: by the blessings
    • niḥśasābhiśasopārima: free from all obstacles
    • jāgrato: of the awakened
    • yat: whatever
    • svapantaḥ: those who are dreaming
    • agnir: may the fire (representing energy and transformation)
    • viśvāny: all
    • apa: remove
    • duṣkṛtāny: the evil deeds
    • ajuṣṭāny: the unworthy actions
    • āre: may
    • asmad: us
    • dadhātu: may it establish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energy of Agni (fire) to bless and protect individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being free from obstacles in both waking and dreaming states.
    • The mantra seeks the removal of negative actions and thoughts, promoting a life of virtue and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges or obstacles in projects. By invoking the energy of Agni, one can seek clarity and strength to overcome difficulties.
    • In personal life, it can be used during meditation or reflection to cleanse oneself of negative thoughts and past mistakes, fostering a positive mindset.
    • In educational pursuits, students can recite this mantra to enhance focus and remove distractions, allowing for better learning and retention of knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and purifying one's actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to strive for goodness and to seek help in maintaining a virtuous path.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be a source of motivation and strength, encouraging individuals to rise above negativity and pursue their goals with clarity and purpose.
