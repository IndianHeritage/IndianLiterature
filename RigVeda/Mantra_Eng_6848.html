धासिं कृत्वाना ओषधीर बप्सद अग्निर न वायति |पुनर यन् तरुणीर अपि ||
dhāsiṁ kṛṇvāna oṣadhīr bapsad agnir na vāyati | punar yan taruṇīr api ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhāsiṁ: to create or to bring forth
    • kṛṇvāna: making or doing
    • oṣadhīr: herbs or plants
    • bapsad: in the presence of
    • agnir: fire
    • na: not
    • vāyati: blowing or moving
    • punar: again
    • yan: that which
    • taruṇīr: young or youthful
    • api: also
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation and nurturing of life through nature.
    • The phrase "dhāsiṁ kṛṇvāna oṣadhīr" emphasizes the importance of cultivating herbs and plants, which are essential for sustenance and health.
    • The mention of "agnir" (fire) suggests the transformative power of fire, which is vital in various processes, including cooking and rituals.
    • The line "na vāyati" indicates that there is a stillness or calmness, perhaps referring to a moment of peace in nature.
    • "Punar yan taruṇīr api" implies a renewal or rebirth, highlighting the cyclical nature of life and the rejuvenation that comes with each season.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the cultivation of herbs and plants is crucial for food security. Farmers work diligently to create a sustainable environment for crops to thrive.
    • In traditional medicine, many cultures rely on herbs for healing, showcasing the significance of "oṣadhīr" in promoting health and wellness.
    • Fire plays a central role in many cultures, from cooking food to performing rituals, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • The concept of renewal can be seen in nature, such as the changing of seasons, where plants die and are reborn, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on life's cyclical nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the elements.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of our reliance on nature for sustenance and the importance of nurturing our environment.
    • In real life, it encourages sustainable practices, respect for nature, and an understanding of the cycles of life, which are essential for our survival and well-being.
