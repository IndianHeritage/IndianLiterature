त्वं इन्द्र यशाऽस्य ऋजीषी शवसस पते |त्वं वृत्राणि हंस्य अप्रतीन्य एक इद अनुत्ता चर्षणिधृताः ||
tvam indra yaśā asy ṛjīṣī śavasas pate | tvaṁ vṛtrāṇi haṁsy apratīny eka id anuttā carṣaṇīdhṛtā ||
  • tvam indra yaśā asy ṛjīṣī śavasas pate: This phrase can be translated as "You, Indra, are the one who possesses glory and is the lord of strength." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, courage, and leadership.
  • tvaṁ vṛtrāṇi haṁsy: This means "You slay the Vṛtras." In Vedic mythology, Vṛtra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. Indra's victory over Vṛtra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos.
  • apratīny eka id anuttā carṣaṇīdhṛtā: This can be interpreted as "You alone are the one who holds the unyielding strength." It emphasizes the idea that true strength and resilience come from within and that one must rely on their inner power to overcome challenges.

  • Real-Life Example: In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to overcome challenges. For instance, during a crisis, a strong leader (like Indra) can rally their team, providing guidance and support to navigate through difficulties, much like Indra slaying Vṛtra to restore order.
  • Personal Application: On a personal level, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra when facing obstacles in their lives. By recognizing their inner strength and resilience, they can confront challenges head-on, much like Indra confronting Vṛtra.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of inner strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome chaos. In real life, it serves as a reminder that we all have the potential to be leaders in our own right, capable of facing and conquering the challenges that life presents. By embodying the qualities of Indra, we can strive for glory and strength in our personal and professional lives.
