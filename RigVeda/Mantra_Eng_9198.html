अमाजुरश्चिद्भवथो युवं भगोऽनाशोश्चिदवितारापमस्य चित् |अन्धस्य चिन् नासत्याकृष्णस्य चिद्युवामिदाहुर्भिषजां रुतस्य चित् ||
amājuraś cid bhavatho yuvam bhago 'nāśoś cid avitārāpamasya cit | andhasya cin nāsatyā kṛśasya cid yuvām id āhur bhiṣajā rutasya cit ||
  • amājuraś: This term can be broken down into "amā" meaning "not" and "jura" which can be interpreted as "old" or "aged". Thus, it signifies the idea of being ageless or eternal.
  • cid: This word translates to "consciousness" or "awareness". It represents the essence of being aware and present.
  • bhavatho: This means "of you" or "your". It indicates a connection or relationship with the subject being addressed.
  • yuvam: This translates to "you two" or "both of you\ suggesting a duality or partnership.
  • bhago: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity". It signifies abundance and the blessings that come with it.
  • 'nāśoś: This can be interpreted as "not perishing" or "immortal". It emphasizes the idea of permanence and continuity.
  • cid avitārāpamasya: This phrase can be understood as "the consciousness that does not divide or separate". It suggests unity and wholeness.
  • andhasya: This means "of the blind" or "darkness". It symbolizes ignorance or lack of awareness.
  • cin: Again, this refers to "consciousness" or "awareness".
  • nāsatyā: This term can be interpreted as "the truthful" or "the honest". It represents integrity and authenticity.
  • kṛśasya: This means "of the weak" or "the feeble". It signifies vulnerability or fragility.
  • cid yuvām: This translates to "you two are consciousness". It reinforces the idea of unity in awareness.
  • id āhur: This means "they say" or "it is said". It indicates a shared understanding or common belief.
  • bhiṣajā: This term refers to "healers" or "physicians". It symbolizes those who provide care and healing.
  • rutasya: This means "of the seasons" or "of the cycles". It represents the natural rhythms of life.
  • cit: Once again, this refers to "consciousness" or "awareness".

In summary, this mantra speaks to the eternal nature of consciousness and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the importance of unity, awareness, and the healing power of truth. The mantra suggests that despite the challenges of life, such as ignorance and vulnerability, there exists a deeper consciousness that binds us all together.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in personal development, encouraging individuals to seek awareness and understanding beyond superficial appearances.
  • In relationships, it highlights the importance of unity and shared consciousness, reminding us to support one another in times of weakness.
  • In the context of healing, it emphasizes the role of truth and awareness in the healing process, whether physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • In a broader societal context, it calls for collective awareness and responsibility, urging communities to work together for the common good.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of our shared existence and the importance of nurturing consciousness, truth, and unity in our lives.
