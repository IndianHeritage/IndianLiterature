आ वाम अश्वासो अभिमातिषाह इन्द्राविष्णू सदहमादो वहन्तु |जुषेथां विश्वा हवना मतीनाम उप ब्रह्माणि श्रिणुतं गिरो मे ||
ā vām aśvāso abhimātiṣāha indrāviṣṇū sadhamādo vahantu | juṣethāṁ viśvā havanā matīnām upa brahmāṇi śṛṇutaṁ giro me ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vām: "to you both" - indicating a duality or partnership.
    • aśvāso: "horses" - symbolizing strength, speed, and power.
    • abhimātiṣāha: "may they be endowed with strength" - a prayer for empowerment.
    • indrāviṣṇū: "Indra and Vishnu" - representing two powerful deities in Hinduism, embodying strength and preservation.
    • sadhamādo: "may they bring happiness" - a wish for joy and prosperity.
    • vahantu: "may they carry" - a request for support and guidance.
    • juṣethāṁ: "may you enjoy" - an invitation to partake in blessings.
    • viśvā: "all" - encompassing everything and everyone.
    • havanā: "sacrificial offerings" - referring to rituals that invoke divine presence.
    • matīnām: "of the minds" - relating to thoughts and intentions.
    • upa: "near" - suggesting closeness or approach.
    • brahmāṇi: "the ultimate reality" - referring to the supreme consciousness or truth.
    • śṛṇutaṁ: "may you hear" - a call for attention and receptivity.
    • giro me: "my words" - indicating personal expression or communication.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collaboration and support from powerful forces (Indra and Vishnu) in our lives.
    • It reflects a desire for empowerment, joy, and the fulfillment of intentions through divine assistance.
    • The invocation of "havanā" signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can be a reminder to seek support from loved ones and to empower each other in times of need.
    • In professional settings, it encourages teamwork and collaboration, invoking the strength of collective efforts to achieve common goals.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, performing rituals can help in channeling positive energies and blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking strength and joy through collaboration and ritualistic practices.
    • In essence, it encourages us to be open to divine guidance and to actively participate in the creation of a harmonious and prosperous life.
