पूर्वीर अहं शरदः शश्रमाणā दोषा वस्तोर उषसो जारयन्तीः |मिनाति श्रीयं जारिमा तनूनाम अप्य ū नु पत्नीर वृषणो जगम्युः ||
pūrvīr ahaṁ śaradaḥ śaśramāṇā doṣā vastor uṣaso jarayantīḥ | mināti śriyaṁ jarimā tanūnām apy ū nu patnīr vṛṣaṇo jagamyuḥ ||
  • pūrvīr ahaṁ - "I am the one who comes first" - This phrase signifies the importance of being a pioneer or a leader in one's endeavors. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who take initiative in their careers or communities, leading projects or movements that inspire others.
  • śaradaḥ - "of the autumn" - Autumn is often associated with harvest and abundance. This can be related to the idea of reaping the rewards of hard work, much like farmers who gather their crops after a season of labor.
  • śaśramāṇā - "the one who brings forth" - This term can be interpreted as a nurturing force, akin to a mother or caretaker. In everyday life, this could refer to individuals who support and uplift others, such as teachers or mentors who guide students towards success.
  • doṣā - "flaws" - Acknowledging imperfections is crucial for personal growth. In real life, this can be seen in self-reflection practices where individuals assess their strengths and weaknesses to improve themselves.
  • vastor - "of the essence" - This refers to the core nature of things. In practical terms, it encourages people to understand the fundamental aspects of their lives, such as values and beliefs, which guide their decisions.
  • uṣaso - "of the dawn" - Dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. This can be related to moments in life when individuals start fresh, such as beginning a new job or moving to a new city, filled with hope and potential.
  • jarayantīḥ - "that which ages" - This phrase reflects the inevitability of change and aging. In real life, it can be seen in the natural progression of life, where experiences shape individuals over time, leading to wisdom and maturity.
  • mināti - "brings forth" - This term emphasizes the act of creation or manifestation. In practical scenarios, it can relate to entrepreneurs who bring innovative ideas to life, contributing to society and the economy.
  • śriyaṁ - "prosperity" - Prosperity is a desired state for many. In real life, this can be seen in individuals striving for financial stability, career success, or personal happiness, often through hard work and dedication.
  • jarimā - "the one who ages" - This can signify the wisdom that comes with age. In real life, older generations often share valuable lessons and experiences with younger ones, fostering a sense of community and continuity.
  • tanūnām - "of the bodies" - This phrase can refer to the physical aspect of existence. In practical terms, it highlights the importance of health and well-being, encouraging individuals to take care of their bodies through exercise and nutrition.
  • apy ū nu - "even if" - This phrase introduces a sense of resilience. In real life, it can be seen in individuals who persevere through challenges, maintaining hope and determination despite obstacles.
  • patnīr - "the partner" - This term emphasizes the significance of relationships. In everyday life, it can refer to the importance of having supportive partners, whether in marriage, friendships, or professional collaborations.
  • vṛṣaṇo - "of the strong" - This signifies strength and power. In real life, it can be related to individuals who exhibit leadership qualities, inspiring others through their actions and decisions.
  • jagamyuḥ - "they have come" - This phrase indicates arrival or manifestation. In practical terms, it can refer to the realization of goals and dreams, where individuals achieve what they have worked towards.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of leadership, nurturing, self-awareness, and the cyclical nature of life. It encourages individuals to embrace their roles as pioneers, to acknowledge their flaws, and to strive for prosperity while valuing relationships and the wisdom that comes with age. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal development, foster community support, and inspire resilience in the face of challenges, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
