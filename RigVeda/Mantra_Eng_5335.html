पिबा सोमं इन्द्र मन्दतु त्वां यं ते सुशाव हर्यश्वाद्रिः |सोतुर बाहुभ्यां सुयतो नर्वा ||
pibā somam indra mandatu tvā yaṁ te suṣāva haryaśvādriḥ | sotur bāhubhyāṁ suyato nārvā ||
  • Translation: "Drink the soma, O Indra, may it invigorate you; the one who has been well prepared, the swift horse, may it be your strength with your strong arms."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pibā: Drink
    • Somam: Soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and strength
    • Indra: The king of gods, associated with thunder, rain, and war
    • Mandatu: May it invigorate or empower
    • Tvā: You
    • Suṣāva: Well-prepared or well-made
    • Haryaśvādriḥ: Swift horse, symbolizing strength and speed
    • Sotur: With strength
    • Bāhubhyāṁ: With strong arms
    • Suyato: Well-prepared or well-formed
    • Nārvā: May it be your strength
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the god of strength and power, to imbibe the soma, which is not just a drink but a symbol of divine energy and vitality.
    • The reference to the "swift horse" signifies the importance of speed and agility in achieving one's goals, much like how athletes train to enhance their performance.
    • The invocation of strength through "strong arms" can be related to the physical and mental strength required in various life challenges, such as overcoming obstacles in personal or professional life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, athletes often prepare themselves mentally and physically, akin to the preparation of soma, to achieve peak performance during competitions.
    • In business, leaders may draw on their inner strength and agility to navigate challenges, much like Indra drawing strength from the soma and the swift horse.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek motivation and empowerment through various means, such as meditation or self-reflection, paralleling the act of drinking soma for invigoration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and vitality in our endeavors, whether they be physical, mental, or spiritual.
    • It serves as a reminder that preparation and inner strength are crucial for success in any field, encouraging individuals to harness their potential and strive for excellence.
    • In essence, the mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, inspiring us to seek empowerment and resilience in our daily lives.
