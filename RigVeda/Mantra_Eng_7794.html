इन्द्राय सोम पातवे मदाय पर्यषिच्यसे |मनश्चिन मनसस पतिḥ ||
indrāya soma pātave madāya pari ṣicyase | manaścin manasas patiḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and leadership.
  • Soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • Pātave: This term means "to drink" or "to consume." It signifies the act of imbibing or taking in something beneficial.
  • Madāya: This word translates to "for intoxication" or "for joy." It indicates a state of bliss or ecstasy that comes from divine experiences.
  • Pari ṣicyase: This means "you are being poured" or "you are being anointed." It suggests a ritualistic act of offering or bestowing blessings.
  • Manaścin: This term refers to "of the mind" or "of consciousness." It emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and focus.
  • Manasas: This word means "of the mind" or "of thought." It highlights the significance of mental processes in achieving one's goals.
  • Patiḥ: This translates to "lord" or "master." It signifies authority and control over one's thoughts and actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for the divine essence of Soma to be poured into the mind, leading to joy and clarity of thought. It emphasizes the connection between divine blessings and mental strength.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of power, individuals in leadership positions can invoke the qualities of strength and wisdom to guide their teams effectively.
  • Creativity and Inspiration: The essence of Soma can be seen as a metaphor for inspiration. Artists, writers, and innovators can seek this divine inspiration to fuel their creative processes.
  • Mental Clarity: The focus on the mind suggests the importance of mental health and clarity in decision-making. Practices like meditation can help individuals achieve this state.
  • Joy and Celebration: The idea of intoxication for joy can be related to celebrating achievements and milestones in life, reminding us to appreciate the good moments.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving mental clarity, strength, and joy. It serves as a reminder to seek inspiration and guidance from higher powers in our daily lives, fostering a connection between the spiritual and the practical. By embodying these principles, individuals can enhance their leadership qualities, creativity, and overall well-being.
