पवस्व वृष्टिम आ सु नो 'पाम उर्मिं दिवस परि |अयक्ष्मा बृहतीर इषः ||
pavasva vṛṣṭim ā su no 'pām ūrmiṁ divas pari | ayakṣmā bṛhatīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pavasva vṛṣṭim ā su no 'pām ūrmiṁ divas pari | ayakṣmā bṛhatīr iṣaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Wind, bring us the rain; may we receive the waves of water from the sky. Let the great power of the heavens be unimpeded."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pavasva: O Wind, may you blow or bring.
    • vṛṣṭim: rain or precipitation.
    • ā: towards us or for us.
    • su: good or beneficial.
    • no: our or us.
    • 'pām: may we receive.
    • ūrmiṁ: waves or currents.
    • divas: from the sky or heavens.
    • pari: all around or completely.
    • ayakṣmā: unimpeded or without obstruction.
    • bṛhatīr: great or vast.
    • iṣaḥ: power or energy.
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Wind, asking for rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance.
    • In ancient times, and even today, communities rely on rain for their crops and livestock. A good monsoon can lead to prosperity, while a lack of rain can result in drought and famine.
    • The invocation of the Wind symbolizes the connection between nature and human existence, emphasizing the importance of harmony with the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers often perform rituals or prayers during the dry season, hoping for rain to nourish their fields.
    • In many cultures, festivals are held to celebrate the arrival of the monsoon, showcasing gratitude for the life-giving rains.
    • Environmental awareness campaigns emphasize the importance of preserving natural resources to ensure sustainable rainfall patterns.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interdependence of humans and nature, reminding us of our reliance on natural elements for survival.
    • It encourages respect for the environment and the understanding that our actions can impact weather patterns and ecological balance.
    • In modern times, this mantra can inspire efforts towards sustainable agriculture and environmental conservation, ensuring that future generations can also benefit from the blessings of nature.
