धारयन्त आदित्यासो जगत स्था देवा विश्वस्य भुवनस्य गोपाः |दीर्घाधियो रक्षमानाऽसूर्यम ऋतावानश चयमानाऽऋणानि ||
dhārayanta ādityāso jagat sthā devā viśvasya bhuvanasya gopāḥ | dīrghādhiyo rakṣamāṇā asuryam ṛtāvānaś cayamānā ṛṇāni ||
  • dhārayanta: This word translates to "holding" or "sustaining." It signifies the act of maintaining or supporting something.
  • ādityāso: Refers to the "Adityas," the solar deities in Vedic tradition, representing light, truth, and cosmic order.
  • jagat: Means "world" or "universe," encompassing all of creation.
  • sthā: Translates to "standing" or "established," indicating stability and permanence.
  • devā: Refers to "gods" or divine beings, who are often seen as protectors and guides.
  • viśvasya: Means "of the universe" or "of all," indicating inclusiveness and universality.
  • bhuvanasya: Refers to "of the worlds" or "of the realms," emphasizing the various dimensions of existence.
  • gopāḥ: Means "guardians" or "protectors," highlighting the role of divine beings in safeguarding creation.
  • dīrghādhiyo: Translates to "those with long understanding" or "wise," indicating the importance of wisdom and insight.
  • rakṣamāṇā: Means "protecting" or "safeguarding," emphasizing the act of preservation.
  • asuryam: Refers to "non-solar" or "darkness," often symbolizing ignorance or negativity.
  • ṛtāvānaś: Means "those who uphold truth" or "truthful," indicating the importance of righteousness.
  • cayamānā: Translates to "gathering" or "collecting," suggesting the accumulation of positive qualities or virtues.
  • ṛṇāni: Refers to "debts" or "obligations," which can symbolize the responsibilities we have in life.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine forces that sustain and protect the universe. The Adityas, as embodiments of light and truth, are seen as guardians of the world, ensuring its stability and order.
    • The emphasis on wisdom and protection suggests that those who possess deep understanding are crucial in maintaining harmony and safeguarding against ignorance and negativity.
    • The mention of "debts" indicates that we have responsibilities towards ourselves, others, and the universe, which we must acknowledge and fulfill.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, leaders who act with wisdom and integrity can be seen as modern-day Adityas, guiding their people towards a harmonious existence.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the idea of guardianship, where individuals and organizations work to protect the planet for future generations.
    • In personal relationships, being aware of our obligations to others—such as family, friends, and society—can help us navigate our interactions with care and respect.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine protection, wisdom, and the responsibilities we hold in life. It serves as a reminder that we are part of a larger cosmic order, and our actions have consequences.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, where we strive to be wise guardians of our communities and the environment, fulfilling our obligations with integrity.
