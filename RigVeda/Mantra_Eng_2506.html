इळाम अग्नि पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्नि सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "the one who is rich in offerings" or "the one who is well-fed." It signifies abundance and the importance of offerings in Vedic rituals.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This translates to "the one who is the protector of the cows." In Vedic culture, cows are sacred and symbolize wealth and nourishment.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This means "the eternal" or "the most enduring." It emphasizes the timeless nature of Agni and the fire's role in rituals.
  • havamānāya: This translates to "to the one who is invoked." It indicates the act of calling upon Agni during rituals.
  • sādha: This means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish." It reflects the desire for the fulfillment of prayers and intentions.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This translates to "may our son be." It expresses a wish for progeny and the continuation of lineage.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This means "the son who is wise." It highlights the importance of wisdom and intelligence in offspring.
  • sā te sumatir bhūtv asme: This translates to "may we have good thoughts from you." It signifies the aspiration for divine guidance and positive thinking.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the spiritual realm. It reflects the values of abundance, protection, wisdom, and the pursuit of good thoughts.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as:
    • In family rituals, invoking Agni for blessings on children and the family unit.
    • In personal development, seeking wisdom and positive thoughts to guide decisions and actions.
    • In agricultural practices, recognizing the importance of abundance and nourishment, akin to the reverence for cows in Vedic culture.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual guidance, and the pursuit of wisdom and abundance. It encourages individuals to seek divine blessings for their families and to cultivate positive thoughts in their daily lives.
