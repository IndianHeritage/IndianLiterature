माहं मघोनो वरुण प्रियस्य भूридाव्न आ विदं शूनम् आपेः |मा रायो राजन सुमाद अव स्थाम बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
māham maghono varuṇa priyasya bhūridāvna ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ | mā rāyo rājan suyamād ava sthām bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • māham maghono varuṇa priyasya bhūridāvna ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ
    • māham - "I" or "myself," indicating a personal invocation.
    • maghono - "of the bounteous," referring to abundance and prosperity.
    • varuṇa - "Varuna," the deity associated with cosmic order and water, symbolizing truth and moral authority.
    • priyasya - "of the beloved," suggesting a connection to something cherished or valued.
    • bhūridāvna - "the one who gives much," indicating generosity and the act of giving.
    • ā vidaṁ - "may I know," a plea for knowledge or understanding.
    • śūnam - "the void" or "emptiness," which can symbolize ignorance or lack of fulfillment.
    • āpeḥ - "from the waters," suggesting a source of life and nourishment.
  • mā rāyo rājan suyamād ava sthām bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ
    • mā - "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something.
    • rāyo - "wealth" or "prosperity," referring to material and spiritual abundance.
    • rājan - "O King," a respectful address to a ruler or authority.
    • suyamād - "by good deeds," emphasizing the importance of righteous actions.
    • ava sthām - "may I attain," a wish for achievement or success.
    • bṛhad - "great" or "vast," indicating something of significant magnitude.
    • vadema - "we speak," suggesting a collective expression or prayer.
    • vidathe - "in the assembly," referring to a gathering or community.
    • suvīrāḥ - "of the brave," indicating valor and strength in character.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from the divine, particularly from Varuna, who represents moral order and abundance. It reflects a desire for prosperity and success through righteous actions and community support. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can invoke this mantra to seek clarity and guidance in their life choices.
  • In community settings, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of collective efforts towards achieving common goals.
  • In leadership, it highlights the significance of generosity and moral integrity in guiding others.

Overall, the mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, prosperity, and ethical living, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for greatness through virtuous actions.
