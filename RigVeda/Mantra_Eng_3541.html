प्र वाम अवोचम अश्विनाऽ धियं धाधा रथः स्वश्वो अजरो यो अस्ति |येना सद्यः परि रजांसी याथो हविष्मन्तं तरणिम भोजम् अच् छ ||
pra vām avocam aśvinā dhiyaṁdhā rathaḥ svaśvo ajaro yo asti | yena sadyaḥ pari rajāṁsi yātho haviṣmantaṁ taraṇim bhojam accha ||
  • pra vām avocam: This phrase translates to "I speak to you." It signifies the act of communication and the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and medicinal powers. They symbolize duality and balance in life.
  • dhiyaṁdhā: This word means "with intelligence" or "with understanding." It emphasizes the need for wisdom in our actions and decisions.
  • rathaḥ: Meaning "chariot," it represents the journey of life and the means by which we navigate through challenges.
  • svaśvo: Translated as "one's own horse," it symbolizes personal strength and resources that one possesses to move forward in life.
  • ajaro: This means "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the pursuit of lasting values and truths that transcend time.
  • yo asti: Translates to "who exists," referring to the essence of being and the importance of existence in the universe.
  • yena sadyaḥ: This phrase means "by whom immediately," suggesting the immediacy of action and the need to respond promptly to life's situations.
  • pari rajāṁsi: Meaning "surrounding the realms," it indicates the vastness of the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • yātho: This translates to "wherever," emphasizing the idea of journeying through different experiences and places in life.
  • haviṣmantaṁ: Referring to "the one who is worthy of offerings," it highlights the importance of gratitude and recognition of those who contribute positively to our lives.
  • taraṇim: Meaning "the one who crosses over," it symbolizes overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
  • bhojam: This translates to "food" or "nourishment," representing the sustenance we seek for our physical and spiritual well-being.
  • accha: Meaning "good" or "well," it signifies the positive outcomes that arise from wise actions and decisions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, wisdom, and the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of understanding, personal strength, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, where effective communication and wisdom are crucial for guiding others. It also serves as a reminder to recognize and appreciate the contributions of those around us, fostering a sense of community and support. Ultimately, it encourages us to navigate life's challenges with intelligence and grace, seeking nourishment for both body and spirit.
