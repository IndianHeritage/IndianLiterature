शतम मेषान वृक्ये चक्षददानं ऋज्राश्वं तं पितांधं चकार |तस्मा अक्षी नासत्याः विचक्षे आधत्तं दश्राभि भिषजाव अनर्वण ||
śatam meṣān vṛkye cakṣadānam ṛjrāśvaṁ tam pitāndhaṁ cakāra | tasmā akṣī nāsatyā vicakṣa ādhattaṁ dasrā bhiṣajāv anarvan ||
  • śatam meṣān vṛkye: This phrase translates to "a hundred sheep in the pasture." Here, "meṣān" refers to sheep, which are often symbols of abundance and prosperity in Vedic literature. In real life, this can be likened to having a wealth of resources or a flourishing business.
  • cakṣadānam ṛjrāśvaṁ: This means "the gift of sight to the swift horse." The "swift horse" symbolizes speed and agility, while "cakṣadānam" refers to the ability to see or perceive. In practical terms, this can represent the importance of vision and foresight in achieving success, much like a business leader who anticipates market trends.
  • tam pitāndhaṁ cakāra: This translates to "he made the blind father." The term "pitāndhaṁ" suggests a father who is blind, which can symbolize ignorance or lack of awareness. This can be related to situations where individuals or organizations fail to recognize opportunities or threats, leading to their downfall.
  • tasmā akṣī nāsatyā: This means "therefore, the eyes are not deceitful." Here, "akṣī" refers to eyes, and "nāsatyā" implies truthfulness. This emphasizes the importance of clarity and honesty in perception. In real life, this can be seen in the context of ethical business practices where transparency leads to trust and long-term success.
  • vicakṣa ādhattaṁ: This translates to "the wise one gathers." "Vicakṣa" refers to wisdom or discernment, and "ādhattaṁ" means to gather or collect. This can be applied to the idea of wise decision-making, where individuals or leaders gather knowledge and insights before making important choices.
  • dasrā bhiṣajāv anarvan: This means "the ten physicians who are not to be ignored." "Dasrā" refers to ten, and "bhiṣajāv" means physicians or healers. This can symbolize the importance of seeking advice from multiple sources or experts before making decisions, akin to consulting various specialists in a medical scenario for a comprehensive understanding of a health issue.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness, wisdom, and ethical practices in life. It teaches us the value of having a clear vision, recognizing opportunities, and making informed decisions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as business, healthcare, and personal development, where clarity of thought and ethical considerations lead to success and well-being. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain vigilant, seek knowledge, and act with integrity in all endeavors.
