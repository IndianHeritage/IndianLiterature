मन्त्र: उता नो देव देवां अच्‍छा वोचो विदुष्टारः |श्रद् विश्वा वार्याः कृधि ||
uta no deva devām̐ acchā voco viduṣṭaraḥ | śrad viśvā vāryā kṛdhi ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "May the divine beings (devas) hear our words and grant us wisdom. Let us be endowed with faith and strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: A call for attention or invocation.
    • no: Our, indicating a personal connection.
    • deva: Divine beings or gods, representing higher powers.
    • devām̐: The plural form of deva, emphasizing multiple divine entities.
    • acchā: Good or beneficial, indicating a positive outcome.
    • voco: To speak or express, highlighting the importance of communication.
    • viduṣṭaraḥ: Wisdom or knowledge, suggesting a quest for understanding.
    • śrad: Faith or trust, essential for spiritual and personal growth.
    • viśvā: All or universal, indicating inclusivity.
    • vāryā: Strength or power, necessary for overcoming challenges.
    • kṛdhi: To create or make, emphasizing action and manifestation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to discuss important issues, invoking the divine for wisdom can lead to better decision-making.
    • In personal challenges, having faith in oneself and seeking guidance from higher powers can provide the strength needed to overcome obstacles.
    • In educational settings, students often pray for wisdom and understanding before exams, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in gaining wisdom and strength. It emphasizes the importance of faith and communication with the divine, which can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal growth, community decision-making, and educational pursuits. By invoking the blessings of the divine, individuals and communities can strive for a more enlightened and empowered existence.
