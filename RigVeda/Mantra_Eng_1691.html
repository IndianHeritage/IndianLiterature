यत् ते गात्राद अग्निना पच्यमानाद अभि शूलं निहतस्यावधावति |मा तद भूम्याम आ श्रिषन् मा तृणेषु देवभ्यस तद उषध्भ्यो रातम अस्तु ||
yat te gātrād agninā pacyamānād abhi śūlaṁ nihatasyāvadhāvati | mā tad bhūmyām ā śriṣan mā tṛṇeṣu devebhyas tad uśadbhyo rātam astu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: whatever
    • te: your
    • gātrād: from the body
    • agninā: by fire
    • pacyamānād: being cooked
    • abhi: towards
    • śūlaṁ: the spear or weapon
    • nihatasyāvadhāvati: of one who is killed
    • mā: do not
    • tad: that
    • bhūmyām: on the earth
    • ā: to
    • śriṣan: to be spread
    • mā: do not
    • tṛṇeṣu: in the grasses
    • devebhyas: to the gods
    • tad: that
    • uśadbhyo: from the herbs
    • rātam: the essence or strength
    • astu: may it be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between fire, the body, and the divine.
    • It suggests that whatever is being cooked or transformed by fire should not be harmed or affected negatively.
    • The reference to "the spear of one who is killed" indicates a caution against violence or harm that may arise from transformation.
    • The plea to not let this transformation affect the earth or the grasses signifies a respect for nature and the interconnectedness of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony with the divine and the natural world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In cooking, the process of transforming raw ingredients into a meal can be seen as a metaphor for personal growth and change. Just as food is transformed by fire, individuals undergo changes that should not harm their essence or values.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can be applied to the idea of sustainable practices. We should not exploit the earth or its resources in a way that leads to destruction, but rather seek to maintain balance and respect for nature.
    • In conflict resolution, the idea of avoiding harm resonates with the need for peaceful dialogue and understanding, rather than resorting to violence or aggression.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the delicate balance between transformation and preservation.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on themselves, others, and the environment.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can strive for a life that honors both personal growth and the sanctity of life, fostering a harmonious existence with the world around us.
