परिप्रयन्तं वय्यं सुशंसदं सोमं मनीषा अभ्य अनूषत स्तुभः |यो धारणाय मधुमाम् ūrmiṇā दिव इयर्ति वाचं रयिषाḻ अमर्त्यः ||
pariprayantaṁ vayyaṁ suṣaṁsadaṁ somam manīṣā abhy anūṣata stubhaḥ | yo dhārayā madhumām̐ ūrmiṇā diva iyarti vācaṁ rayiṣāḻ amartyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pariprayantaṁ vayyaṁ suṣaṁsadaṁ somam manīṣā abhy anūṣata stubhaḥ | yo dhārayā madhumām̐ ūrmiṇā diva iyarti vācaṁ rayiṣāḻ amartyaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pariprayantaṁ: One who is being invoked or called upon.
    • vayyaṁ: A term that can refer to a divine or celestial being.
    • suṣaṁsadaṁ: One who brings good fortune or auspiciousness.
    • somam: Referring to 'Soma', a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine energy.
    • manīṣā: Intellect or wisdom.
    • abhy anūṣata: Invoking or calling forth.
    • stubhaḥ: Praise or hymns.
    • yo: He who.
    • dhārayā: Sustains or holds.
    • madhumām̐: Sweet or pleasant.
    • ūrmiṇā: With waves or in abundance.
    • diva: In the heavens or divine realm.
    • iyarti: Moves or flows.
    • vācaṁ: Speech or words.
    • rayiṣāḻ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • amartyaḥ: Immortal or divine being.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine presence that is associated with wisdom and prosperity.
    • The reference to 'Soma' highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for immortality, which can be interpreted as the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
    • The phrase "who sustains with sweet waves" suggests that the divine provides abundance and sustenance, much like how nature provides for us through its resources.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another, sharing resources and knowledge to uplift each other.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and support in our pursuit of wisdom and prosperity.
    • It reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of sharing knowledge and resources for collective growth.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering a spirit of collaboration, seeking wisdom in our decisions, and recognizing the divine presence in our efforts to create a better world.
