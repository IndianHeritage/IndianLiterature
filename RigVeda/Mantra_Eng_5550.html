आपो यं वः प्रथमं देवयन्त इन्द्रपानम् ऊर्मिम अकृण्वतेषः |तं वो वयं शुचिम अरिप्रम अद्य घृतप्रुषम् मधुमन्तं वनेम ||
āpo yaṁ vaḥ prathamaṁ devayanta indrapānam ūrmim akṛṇvateḻaḥ | taṁ vo vayaṁ śucim aripram adya ghṛtapruṣam madhumantaṁ vanema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • yaṁ: Which
    • vaḥ: For you
    • prathamaṁ: First
    • devayanta: Divine
    • indrapānam: Indra's drink
    • ūrmim: Waves
    • akṛṇvateḻaḥ: They create
    • taṁ: That
    • vo: For you
    • vayaṁ: We
    • śucim: Pure
    • aripram: Enemy-destroying
    • adya: Today
    • ghṛtapruṣam: Ghee-filled
    • madhumantaṁ: Sweet
    • vanema: We invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the waters, which are considered sacred and divine in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "āpo yaṁ vaḥ prathamaṁ devayanta" suggests that the waters are the first among the divine entities, highlighting their importance in rituals and life.
    • "Indrapānam ūrmim akṛṇvateḻaḥ" refers to the waters that create waves, symbolizing the dynamic and life-giving properties of water.
    • The invocation of "śucim aripram" emphasizes the purity and the ability of these waters to destroy enemies, which can be interpreted as obstacles or negativity in life.
    • "Adya ghṛtapruṣam madhumantaṁ vanema" indicates a call to invoke the sweet, ghee-filled waters, representing nourishment and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, water is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, water is used for cleansing and sanctifying spaces.
    • The concept of invoking pure waters can be related to seeking clarity and purity in our thoughts and actions, akin to meditation practices that promote mental clarity.
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can inspire efforts to protect water bodies, recognizing their vital role in sustaining life and promoting health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of water as a life-giving and purifying force.
    • It teaches us to respect and invoke the natural elements for their beneficial qualities, encouraging a harmonious relationship with nature.
    • In real life, this can be applied by promoting environmental conservation, practicing mindfulness, and seeking purity in our daily lives.
