अकारि ब्रह्म समिधाना तुभ्यं शंसात्युक्तं यजते व्यू धाः |होतारं अग्निम मनुषो निषेदुर नमस्यन्त उशिजः शंसम आयोः ||
akāri brahma samidhāna tubhyaṁ śaṁsāty ukthaṁ yajate vy ū dhāḥ | hotāram agnim manuṣo ni ṣedur namasyanta uśijaḥ śaṁsam āyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akāri: created or made
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • samidhāna: offering or sacrifice
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • śaṁsāty: praise or commendation
    • ukthaṁ: hymn or chant
    • yajate: worship or perform a sacrifice
    • vy ū dhāḥ: those who are gathered or assembled
    • hotāram: the priest or the one who invokes
    • agnim: fire, often symbolizing the divine
    • manuṣo: human or mankind
    • ni ṣedur: have seated or established
    • namasyanta: those who bow or show reverence
    • uśijaḥ: the wise or learned
    • śaṁsam: praise or commendation
    • āyoḥ: of the two or duality
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of creation and the reverence shown towards the ultimate reality, Brahman.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and sacrifices made to the divine, represented by fire (Agni).
    • The gathering of people to perform these rituals signifies community and collective worship.
    • The role of the priest (hotāram) is crucial as he invokes the divine presence during the rituals.
    • It highlights the importance of wisdom and reverence in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, community gatherings for festivals or religious ceremonies can be seen as a continuation of this practice, where people come together to honor the divine.
    • In many cultures, the act of lighting a fire during rituals symbolizes the connection between the earthly and the divine, similar to the role of Agni in this mantra.
    • Educational institutions often emphasize the importance of wisdom and learning, akin to the 'uśijaḥ' who are revered for their knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of creation, worship, and community.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of reverence towards the divine and the role of collective efforts in spiritual practices.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to participate in community rituals, seek wisdom, and show respect towards the forces that govern existence.
