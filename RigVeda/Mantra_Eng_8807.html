वेṣi होत्रं उता पोत्रं जनानां मन्धातासि द्रविणोदाऽ ऋतावा |स्वाहा वयं कृत्नवामाः हविंषि देवो देवान यजत्व अग्निरर्हन् ||
veṣi hotram uta potraṁ janānām mandhātāsi draviṇodā ṛtāvā | svāhā vayaṁ kṛṇavāmā havīṁṣi devo devān yajatv agnir arhan ||
  • veṣi hotram uta potraṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for the offerings (hotra) and the pot (potraṁ) that are essential for rituals. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of preparation and the right tools in any endeavor, such as cooking or conducting a ceremony.
  • janānām mandhātāsi: Here, "janānām" refers to people or humanity, and "mandhātāsi" can be understood as a reference to a ruler or a leader. This suggests the importance of leadership and guidance in society. For example, effective leaders in communities or organizations help to inspire and direct people towards common goals.
  • draviṇodā ṛtāvā: "Draviṇodā" refers to wealth or resources, while "ṛtāvā" signifies truth or righteousness. This part emphasizes the need for ethical use of resources. In modern contexts, this can relate to sustainable practices in business or personal finance, where wealth is managed responsibly and ethically.
  • svāhā: A common term in Vedic rituals, "svāhā" is an invocation that signifies the offering being made to the fire. It represents the act of surrendering oneself to a higher power. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of gratitude or when individuals dedicate their efforts to a cause greater than themselves.
  • vayaṁ kṛṇavāmā havīṁṣi: This translates to "we create offerings." It highlights the collective effort in making contributions, whether in a spiritual context or in community service. For instance, volunteering for a local charity can be seen as creating offerings to society.
  • devo devān yajatv agnir arhan: This phrase can be interpreted as invoking the divine (devo) and the fire (agnir) as a sacred element in rituals. It signifies respect for the divine and the natural elements. In real life, this can be reflected in environmental conservation efforts, where people honor nature and its resources.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of preparation, leadership, ethical resource management, collective effort, and respect for the divine and nature. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as community leadership, sustainable practices, and acts of service. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can work towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
