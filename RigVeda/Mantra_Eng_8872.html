यमस्य माऽयम्यं काम आगन् समाने योनौ सहशेय्याय | जायेव पत्ये तन्वं रिरिच्यां विड् वृहेव रथ्येव चक्रा ||
yamasya mā yamya1ṁ kāma āgan samāne yonau sahaśeyyāya | jāyeva patye tanvaṁ riricyāṁ vi cid vṛheva rathyeva cakrā ||
  • yamasya: This word refers to the concept of restraint or control. In real life, it can be related to self-discipline, such as maintaining a healthy diet or managing one's emotions in stressful situations.
  • mā: This is a negation, meaning "not" or "do not." It emphasizes the importance of avoiding negative actions or thoughts.
  • yamyaṁ: This term suggests the act of controlling or restraining desires. For example, it can be seen in the practice of mindfulness, where individuals learn to observe their thoughts without acting on them impulsively.
  • kāma: This word translates to desire or longing. In everyday life, it can refer to various desires, such as the pursuit of career goals, relationships, or material possessions.
  • āgan: This means "to come" or "to arrive." It signifies the arrival of desires or experiences in one's life.
  • samāne: This term means "together" or "in harmony." It highlights the importance of balance in life, such as balancing work and personal life.
  • yonau: This refers to the source or origin. In a practical sense, it can relate to understanding the roots of one's desires and motivations.
  • sahaśeyyāya: This word suggests companionship or partnership. It can be applied to the importance of relationships and support systems in achieving one's goals.
  • jāyeva: This means "like a wife" or "in the manner of a spouse." It emphasizes loyalty and commitment in relationships, which can be seen in the dedication one shows towards family and loved ones.
  • patye: This translates to "husband" or "partner." It signifies the importance of mutual respect and understanding in partnerships.
  • tanvaṁ: This word means "body" or "form." It can relate to the physical aspect of life, such as health and well-being.
  • riricyāṁ: This term suggests the idea of longing or desire. It can be connected to the aspirations one has in life.
  • vi: This is a prefix that can mean "in" or "within." It indicates an internal aspect of experience.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness." It highlights the importance of being aware of one's thoughts and actions.
  • vṛheva: This translates to "like a vastness" or "expansiveness." It can be related to the idea of growth and expansion in one's life, such as pursuing education or personal development.
  • rathyeva: This means "like a chariot." It symbolizes the journey of life and the direction one chooses to take.
  • cakrā: This word refers to "wheel" or "cycle." It signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of adapting to changes.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of self-control, awareness, and harmony in the pursuit of desires. It teaches us to balance our aspirations with restraint and to cultivate meaningful relationships. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as maintaining a healthy lifestyle, nurturing relationships, and pursuing personal growth. By understanding our desires and their origins, we can navigate our lives more effectively, ensuring that we remain grounded while striving for our goals.
