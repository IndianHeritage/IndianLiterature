यस्य त्यत् ते महिमानम् मदेष्व इमे मही रोडसी नावीविक्ताम् |तद ओक आ हरिभिर इन्द्र युक्तैः प्रियैभिर याहि प्रियं अन्नम् अच् छ ||
yasya tyat te mahimānam madeṣv ime mahī rodasī nāviviktām | tad oka ā haribhir indra yuktaiḥ priyebhir yāhi priyam annam accha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tyat: that
    • te: your
    • mahimānam: greatness
    • madeṣv: in the intoxication
    • ime: these
    • mahī: great
    • rodasī: the two worlds
    • nāviviktām: not separated
    • tad: that
    • oka: abode
    • ā: come
    • haribhir: with the gods
    • indra: O Indra
    • yuktaiḥ: with those who are connected
    • priyebhir: with beloved ones
    • yāhi: go
    • priyam: dear
    • annam: food
    • accha: good
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness of a divine presence, suggesting that the essence of this greatness is intertwined with the world around us.
    • The phrase "in the intoxication" can be interpreted as being in a state of bliss or divine ecstasy, which is often experienced in moments of deep connection with the universe or spirituality.
    • The reference to "the two worlds" indicates a connection between the physical and spiritual realms, emphasizing that they are not separate but rather interconnected.
    • The call to "come with the gods" signifies an invitation to join in a higher state of existence, surrounded by divine beings and loved ones.
    • The concluding part about "good food" symbolizes nourishment, not just in a physical sense but also in a spiritual and emotional context, suggesting that true sustenance comes from love and divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of celebration, such as weddings or festivals, people often feel a sense of divine presence and connection with loved ones, reflecting the "intoxication" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Practices like meditation or yoga can help individuals experience the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual worlds, aligning with the idea of "the two worlds" being united.
    • Sharing meals with family and friends can be seen as a way to invite divine presence into our lives, as food often symbolizes love and togetherness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in our everyday lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out moments of bliss and connection, whether through spiritual practices, relationships, or communal gatherings.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true nourishment comes from love, connection, and the divine presence that surrounds us.
