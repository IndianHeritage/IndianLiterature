अचेति दिवो दुहितā मगोनī विश्वे पश्यन्त्य उषसं विभातिम् |आस्थाद रथं स्वधया युज्यमानम् आ यम अश्वासः सुयुजो वहन्ति ||
aceti divo duhitā maghonī viśve paśyanty uṣasaṁ vibhātīm | āsthād rathaṁ svadhayā yujyamānam ā yam aśvāsaḥ suyujo vahanti ||
  • aceti: This word can be interpreted as "she who is aware" or "she who knows." It signifies consciousness and awareness.
  • divo: This translates to "of the heavens" or "divine." It indicates a connection to the celestial or spiritual realm.
  • duhitā: Meaning "daughter," it often refers to a feminine aspect, symbolizing nurturing and creation.
  • maghonī: This word means "of abundance" or "prosperity." It represents wealth and the blessings of life.
  • viśve: Translated as "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusivity and the collective.
  • paśyanty: This means "they see" or "they perceive," indicating awareness and understanding.
  • uṣasaṁ: Referring to "the dawn," it symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
  • vibhātīm: This translates to "shining" or "illuminated," representing clarity and brilliance.
  • āsthād: Meaning "having taken" or "having established," it indicates a sense of grounding or foundation.
  • rathaṁ: This word means "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • svadhayā: Translated as "by one's own power," it emphasizes self-reliance and personal strength.
  • yujyamānam: This means "being yoked" or "joined together," indicating unity and collaboration.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
  • yam: This translates to "that" or "which," referring to a specific object or goal.
  • aśvāsaḥ: Meaning "horses," it symbolizes energy, speed, and vitality.
  • suyujo: This means "well yoked" or "harmoniously joined," indicating balance and teamwork.
  • vahanti: Translated as "they carry" or "they transport," it signifies the act of moving forward or progressing.

The mantra speaks to the essence of awareness, abundance, and the journey of life. It highlights the importance of consciousness and perception in recognizing the blessings of life, represented by the dawn. The imagery of the chariot suggests that we are all on a journey, and by harnessing our own power and working together, we can move towards our goals with energy and vitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to be aware of their strengths and to harness their inner power to achieve their goals.
  • In teamwork, it emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving common objectives.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and clarity in one's life journey.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of awareness, abundance, and the journey of life. It encourages individuals to recognize their potential, work collaboratively, and embrace new beginnings with hope and clarity. By applying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges and move towards a prosperous and fulfilling existence.
