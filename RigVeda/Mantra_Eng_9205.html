युवं श्वेतं पेदवे 'श्विनाश्वं नवभिर वाजैर नवती च वाजिनम् |चर्कृत्यं ददथुर द्रावयत्सखं भगं न नृभ्यो हव्यं मयोभुवम् ||
yuvaṁ śvetam pedave 'śvināśvaṁ navabhir vājair navatī ca vājinam | carkṛtyaṁ dadathur drāvayatsakham bhagaṁ na nṛbhyo havyam mayobhuvam ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality and energy, often associated with new beginnings and potential.
  • śvetam: Meaning "white," this term symbolizes purity, clarity, and auspiciousness. In many cultures, white is associated with peace and new beginnings.
  • pedave: This can be interpreted as "the one who is to be worshipped" or "the one who is exalted." It indicates a figure of reverence or importance.
  • 'śvināśvaṁ: This term refers to "the two horses of the Ashvins," who are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, representing speed, health, and the dawn.
  • navabhir: Meaning "with new," this word emphasizes freshness and innovation, suggesting a new approach or perspective.
  • vājair: This translates to "with steeds" or "with horses," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • navatī: This means "new" or "fresh," reinforcing the theme of renewal and rejuvenation.
  • ca: A conjunction meaning "and," linking ideas together.
  • vājinam: This term refers to "the one who possesses strength" or "the powerful one," indicating a source of energy and capability.
  • carkṛtyaṁ: This can be interpreted as "having been made" or "having been created," suggesting the act of bringing something into existence.
  • dadathur: Meaning "they have given," this word indicates generosity and the act of providing or bestowing something valuable.
  • drāvayatsakham: This phrase can be understood as "they who drive away obstacles," indicating the removal of hindrances and challenges.
  • bhagaṁ: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," symbolizing abundance and success.
  • na: A negation meaning "not," indicating the absence of something.
  • nṛbhyo: This means "to men" or "to people," indicating a focus on humanity and social connections.
  • havyam: Referring to "offerings" or "sacrifices," this term emphasizes the importance of giving and sharing.
  • mayobhuvam: This can be interpreted as "that which is born of me" or "my creation," indicating personal involvement and ownership in the process of creation.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the themes of youth, purity, strength, and the act of creation. It emphasizes the importance of new beginnings and the power of generosity in overcoming obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new project, embracing change, or fostering community support. The significance lies in recognizing the potential for renewal and the importance of contributing positively to society, encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength and work collaboratively for a prosperous future.
