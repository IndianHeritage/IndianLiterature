अयं सो अग्निर यस्मिन सोमं इन्द्रः सुतं दधे जटरे वावशानः | सहस्रिणं वाजम् अत्यं न सप्तिं ससवान सन स्तूयसे जातवेदः ||
ayaṁ so agnir yasmin somam indraḥ sutaṁ dadhe jaṭhare vāvaśānaḥ | sahasriṇaṁ vājam atyaṁ na saptiṁ sasavān san stūyase jātavedaḥ ||
  • ayaṁ so agnir: This phrase refers to the divine fire (Agni), which is considered a vital force in Vedic literature. Agni symbolizes transformation, energy, and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • yasmin somam indraḥ sutaṁ dadhe: Here, 'somam' refers to the sacred drink (Soma) that is associated with immortality and divine inspiration. 'Indraḥ' is the king of gods, and 'sutaṁ' means the offspring or the product. This line suggests that Indra, through the power of Agni, is able to create or manifest the essence of Soma.
  • jaṭhare vāvaśānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as the act of nurturing or containing. It implies that Agni has the capacity to hold and transform the essence of Soma within itself, much like how a mother nurtures her child.
  • sahasriṇaṁ vājam atyaṁ: 'Sahasriṇaṁ' means a thousandfold, and 'vājam' refers to strength or power. This line emphasizes the immense power and abundance that Agni possesses, which can be harnessed for various purposes.
  • na saptiṁ sasavān: This phrase suggests that Agni does not merely provide strength but also brings forth prosperity and success ('saptiṁ'). It indicates that the blessings of Agni lead to fruitful outcomes.
  • san stūyase jātavedaḥ: 'Jātavedaḥ' refers to the all-knowing Agni, who is praised and revered. This line highlights the importance of acknowledging and honoring Agni for the blessings it bestows upon humanity.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the significance of Agni as a transformative force that connects the divine with the earthly realm. In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and practices where fire is used as a medium for offerings, symbolizing purification and the transmission of intentions to the divine. The mantra teaches us the importance of nurturing our inner strength and potential (Agni) to achieve prosperity and success (Soma) in our lives. By recognizing and honoring the forces that contribute to our well-being, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and harness its energies for our growth and fulfillment.
