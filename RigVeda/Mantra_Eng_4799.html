इमा उ त्वा सुतेसुतें नक्षन्ते गिरवणो गिरः |वत्सं गावो न धेनवः ||
imā u tvā sute-sute nakṣante girvaṇo giraḥ | vatsaṁ gāvo na dhenavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imā: these
    • u: and
    • tvā: you
    • sute-sute: in the offerings
    • nakṣante: are shining
    • girvaṇo: of the singers
    • giraḥ: of the words
    • vatsaṁ: the calf
    • gāvo: of the cows
    • na: not
    • dhenavaḥ: the milking cows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine connection between offerings and the divine presence.
    • The phrase "imā u tvā" indicates a direct address to the divine, suggesting a relationship of reverence and devotion.
    • The term "sute-sute" refers to the offerings made during rituals, emphasizing their importance in spiritual practices.
    • "Nakṣante" implies that these offerings shine or are illuminated, indicating their purity and significance in the spiritual realm.
    • The mention of "vatsaṁ gāvo" symbolizes abundance and prosperity, as calves and cows are often associated with nourishment and wealth in agrarian societies.
    • The phrase "na dhenavaḥ" suggests a distinction between the milking cows and the calves, possibly indicating the importance of nurturing and growth in spiritual and material aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cows are revered not just for their milk but also as symbols of wealth and sustenance. This mantra can be seen as a reminder to honor the sources of our sustenance.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities, and this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and respect for the divine.
    • In personal development, the idea of nurturing one's potential (like a calf growing into a cow) can be reflected in this mantra, encouraging individuals to cultivate their skills and talents.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of offerings and the divine connection in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance around us and to nurture our relationships with both the material and spiritual aspects of life.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the importance of growth, gratitude, and the sacredness of our daily sustenance.
