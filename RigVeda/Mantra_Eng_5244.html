आ नो देवैभिर उप देवहूतिम अग्ने याहि वषट्कृतिं जुषाणः |तुभ्यं देवाय दाशतः स्याम यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ā no devebhir upa devahūtim agne yāhi vaṣaṭkṛtiṁ juṣāṇaḥ | tubhyaṁ devāya dāśataḥ syāma yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we be led or guided.
    • devebhir: By the gods.
    • upa: Near or towards.
    • devahūtim: The divine offering or invocation.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • yāhi: Come.
    • vaṣaṭkṛtiṁ: To the one who is invoked with the sacred sound "Vaṣaṭ".
    • juṣāṇaḥ: Enjoying or accepting.
    • tubhyaṁ: To you.
    • devāya: O divine one.
    • dāśataḥ: May we be worthy or deserving.
    • syāma: May we become.
    • yūyam: You (plural, referring to the gods).
    • pāta: Protect or safeguard.
    • svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness or well-being.
    • sadā: Always.
    • naḥ: For us.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation seeks guidance and protection from the gods, emphasizing the importance of divine support in our lives.
    • The phrase "May we be led by the gods" reflects a desire for divine direction in our actions and decisions.
    • By calling upon Agni, the mantra acknowledges the significance of fire in rituals and daily life, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy.
    • The use of "vaṣaṭ" is a sacred utterance that signifies the invocation of divine presence, often used in Vedic rituals.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for protection and well-being, highlighting the interconnectedness of divine favor and human prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people may perform rituals to invoke blessings from deities, akin to the offerings made to Agni.
    • In times of uncertainty or challenge, many turn to spiritual practices for protection and clarity, reflecting the essence of this mantra's plea for divine safeguarding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the need for spiritual connection and the role of rituals in fostering a sense of well-being and auspiciousness.
    • In a modern context, the principles of this mantra can be applied through mindfulness, prayer, and community support, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our journeys.
