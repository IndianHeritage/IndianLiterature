न्य अर्बुदस्य विष्टपं वर्ष्माणं बृहतस् तिरा |कृषे तद इन्द्र पौंस्यम् ||
ny arbudasya viṣṭapaṁ varṣmāṇam bṛhatas tira | kṛṣe tad indra pauṁsyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ny: a prefix indicating negation or absence.
    • arbudasya: of the mountain or the great mass.
    • viṣṭapaṁ: the place of dwelling or abode.
    • varṣmāṇam: of the vast or expansive.
    • bṛhatas: of the great or mighty.
    • tira: on the other side or beyond.
    • kṛṣe: in the act of cultivation or farming.
    • tad: that or those.
    • indra: the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • pauṁsyam: of manliness or virility.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the vastness and might of nature, represented by the mountains and the expansive landscapes.
    • It emphasizes the importance of cultivation and the role of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, in ensuring fertility and prosperity.
    • The mention of "manliness" suggests a call for strength and courage in the face of challenges, particularly in agricultural endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable conditions, much like invoking Indra for blessings on their crops.
    • The metaphor of mountains can be seen in the challenges people face in life, where overcoming these "mountains" requires strength and perseverance.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate not just land, but also their personal and professional lives, striving for growth and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, fertility, and the acknowledgment of nature's vastness.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek to overcome challenges, cultivate their potential, and rely on higher powers for support in their endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human effort, divine support, and the natural world.
