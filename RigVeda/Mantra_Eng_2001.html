इयत्तिका शकुन्तिका सका जगहास ते विषम् |सो चिन् नु न मराति नो वयम् मरामारे अस्य योजनाṁ हरिष्ठा मधु त्वा मधुला चकार ||
iyattikā śakuntikā sakā jaghāsa te viṣam | so cin nu na marāti no vayam marāmāre asya yojanaṁ hariṣṭhā madhu tvā madhulā cakāra ||
  • iyattikā: This term can be interpreted as "that which is connected" or "that which is related." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • śakuntikā: This word refers to "the bird" or "the creature." In many cultures, birds symbolize freedom, transcendence, and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • sakā: This can be understood as "friend" or "companion." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and support systems in our lives.
  • jaghāsa: This term means "to overcome" or "to conquer." It reflects the idea of triumphing over challenges and adversities.
  • te: This is a pronoun meaning "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or address to a higher power or the universe.
  • viṣam: This word translates to "difficult" or "poisonous." It represents the challenges and toxic elements we encounter in life.
  • so: This means "he" or "that one," referring to a divine entity or force that aids in overcoming difficulties.
  • cin: This term means "to know" or "to perceive," highlighting the importance of awareness and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
  • nu: This is an expression of inquiry or emphasis, often used to invoke thought or reflection.
  • na marāti: This phrase means "does not die" or "is not defeated," suggesting resilience and the enduring nature of the spirit.
  • no vayam: This translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective experience or shared journey.
  • marāmāre: This term means "to kill" or "to destroy," representing the struggles we face that threaten our well-being.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of this situation," referring to the context of the challenges we encounter.
  • yojanaṁ: This word translates to "plan" or "strategy," emphasizing the need for thoughtful approaches to overcome difficulties.
  • hariṣṭhā: This term means "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting the pursuit of excellence and the highest ideals.
  • madhu: This translates to "honey," symbolizing sweetness, nourishment, and the rewards of overcoming challenges.
  • tvā: This means "you," indicating a personal connection to the divine or the universe.
  • madhulā: This term refers to "sweetness" or "delight," representing the joy and fulfillment that comes from overcoming obstacles.
  • cakāra: This means "to create" or "to make," emphasizing the active role we play in shaping our lives and experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of life, highlighting the interconnectedness of beings, the importance of relationships, and the resilience required to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder that while we face difficulties (viṣam), we have the capacity to conquer them (jaghāsa) through awareness (cin) and collective strength (no vayam). The pursuit of excellence (hariṣṭhā) and the sweetness of life (madhu) are attainable through our efforts and strategies (yojanaṁ). In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek support from friends (sakā), remain resilient in the face of adversity, and strive for personal growth and fulfillment. It encourages us to create a life filled with joy and connection, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles and that there is always a path to sweetness and success.
