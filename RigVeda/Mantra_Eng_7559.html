त्यान नु पूतदक्षसो दिवो वो मरुतो हुवे |अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
tyān nu pūtadakṣaso divo vo maruto huve | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Mantra: "tyān nu pūtadakṣaso divo vo maruto huve | asya somasya pītaye ||"
  • Translation: "I invoke you, O Maruts, who are pure and powerful, for the sake of this Soma drink."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • tyān: "you" - referring to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • nu: "indeed" - an affirmation of the invocation.
    • pūtadakṣaso: "pure and powerful" - highlighting the divine qualities of the Maruts.
    • divo: "of the sky" - indicating their celestial nature.
    • vo: "to you" - a respectful address to the Maruts.
    • maruto: "O Maruts" - calling upon the storm gods.
    • huve: "I invoke" - a call to action, seeking their presence.
    • asya: "of this" - referring to the Soma drink being offered.
    • somasya: "of Soma" - the sacred drink associated with divine inspiration and immortality.
    • pītaye: "for drinking" - indicating the purpose of the invocation.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • The Maruts are often associated with storms, winds, and the power of nature. They symbolize strength, vitality, and the dynamic forces of the universe.
    • Soma is a ritual drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and divine insight. It represents the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • This mantra reflects the importance of invoking divine forces to bless rituals and offerings, emphasizing the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of seeking strength and purity in our actions and intentions.
    • It encourages individuals to connect with nature and the divine, recognizing the power of the elements in our lives.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, invoking higher powers can be a way to seek guidance, protection, and blessings for personal or communal endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength and purity, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the forces that govern our existence and the importance of aligning ourselves with these energies for personal growth and spiritual development.
    • In essence, it teaches us to honor the divine in our lives and to seek inspiration and strength from the natural world around us.
