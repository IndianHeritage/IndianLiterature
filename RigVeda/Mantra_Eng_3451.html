ये गोमन्तं वाजवन्तं सुवीरं रयिं धत्त वसुमन्तं पुरुक्षुम् |ते अग्रेपाऽऋभवो मन्दसाना अस्मे धत्त ये च रातिं गृणन्ति ||
ye gomantaṁ vājavantaṁ suvīraṁ rayiṁ dhattha vasumantam purukṣum | te agrepā ṛbhavo mandasānā asme dhatta ye ca rātiṁ gṛṇanti ||
  • ye gomantaṁ: This phrase refers to those who possess wealth and resources, often symbolized by cattle in ancient times. Cattle were a sign of prosperity and abundance.
  • vājavantaṁ: This term signifies strength and valor, often associated with warriors or those who are courageous and capable of overcoming challenges.
  • suvīraṁ: This word means "good hero" or "brave one," emphasizing the qualities of bravery and nobility in character.
  • rayiṁ dhattha: This phrase translates to "you hold wealth," indicating the importance of not just possessing wealth but also the responsibility that comes with it.
  • vasumantam: This term refers to those who are rich or prosperous, highlighting the value of abundance in life.
  • purukṣum: This word means "many people" or "a multitude," suggesting the importance of community and collective strength.
  • te agrepā: This phrase means "you are ahead," indicating leadership and the ability to guide others.
  • ṛbhavo: This term refers to those who are wise or knowledgeable, emphasizing the importance of wisdom in leadership.
  • mandasānā: This word means "those who are gentle" or "calm," suggesting that true strength also lies in gentleness and compassion.
  • asme dhatta: This phrase translates to "may you bestow upon us," indicating a plea for blessings and support.
  • ye ca rātiṁ gṛṇanti: This means "those who sing praises," highlighting the importance of gratitude and recognition of the good in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the qualities of wealth, strength, bravery, wisdom, and community. It teaches us that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about the virtues of leadership, gentleness, and gratitude. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as in leadership roles where one must balance strength with compassion, in community building where collective prosperity is valued, and in personal growth where one recognizes the importance of wisdom and gratitude in achieving true success. The mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these qualities in our lives for holistic well-being and harmony.
