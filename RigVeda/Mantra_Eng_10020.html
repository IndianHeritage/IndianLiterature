ऊर्ध्वा यत ते तретिनी भूद यज्ञस्य धूर्षु सद्मन |सजूर नावं स्वयशसं सचायोः ||
ūrdhvā yat te tretinī bhūd yajñasya dhūrṣu sadman | sajūr nāvaṁ svayaśasaṁ sacāyoḥ ||
  • ūrdhvā: This word translates to "upward" or "higher." It signifies the aspiration to rise above the mundane and reach for higher ideals or spiritual goals.
  • yat: Meaning "that" or "which," it connects the aspiration to the object of desire or intention.
  • te: This means "your," indicating possession or a personal connection to the higher ideals being referred to.
  • tretinī: This term can be interpreted as "the threefold path" or "the three realms," which may refer to the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of existence.
  • bhūd: This translates to "become" or "manifest," suggesting the process of realization or the act of bringing forth something into existence.
  • yajñasya: This word means "of the sacrifice" or "of the offering," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing selflessness and the act of giving.
  • dhūrṣu: This can be understood as "in the abode" or "in the dwelling," referring to a sacred space or a place of worship where spiritual practices occur.
  • sadman: Meaning "in the good place" or "in the right environment," it emphasizes the importance of the setting in which spiritual activities take place.
  • sajūr: This translates to "together" or "in harmony," indicating the collective effort and unity required in spiritual practices.
  • nāvaṁ: Meaning "boat" or "vessel," it symbolizes the means of navigating through life's challenges and the journey towards spiritual fulfillment.
  • svayaśasaṁ: This term means "with one's own glory" or "by one's own merit," highlighting the importance of personal effort and integrity in achieving success.
  • sacāyoḥ: This can be interpreted as "and of the two" or "and of the pairs," suggesting the duality present in life and the need for balance between opposing forces.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for higher ideals through collective effort and selfless actions. It highlights the significance of the environment in which spiritual practices occur and the personal responsibility one has in their journey. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, or personal development endeavors. By working together harmoniously and focusing on higher goals, individuals can navigate the challenges of life effectively, much like a boat navigating through waters, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
