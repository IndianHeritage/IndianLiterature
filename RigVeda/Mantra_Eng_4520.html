ते ते अग्ने त्वोताऽइषयन्तो विश्वम् आयुः |तरन्तो आर्यो अरातीर वन्वन्तो आर्यो अरातिः ||
te te agne tvotā iṣayanto viśvam āyuḥ | taranto aryo arātīr vanvanto aryo arātīḥ ||
  • te te agne: This phrase translates to "to you, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • tvotā iṣayanto: This means "who are invoked." It signifies the act of calling upon Agni for blessings and guidance.
  • viśvam āyuḥ: This translates to "all life" or "universal life." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of life energy.
  • taranto: This word means "to cross over" or "to transcend." It suggests overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
  • aryo arātīr: This can be interpreted as "noble ones" or "those who are worthy." It refers to individuals who embody virtue and righteousness.
  • vanvanto: This means "to flourish" or "to prosper." It indicates growth and success in various aspects of life.
  • aryo arātīḥ: This phrase reiterates the idea of noble individuals who are free from enmity or conflict, promoting peace and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energy (Agni) for the well-being of all life. It highlights the virtues of nobility, prosperity, and the ability to overcome challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for personal growth, community harmony, and environmental sustainability. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and striving for noble actions, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous world.
