उपेद अहं धनदाम अप्रतीतं जुष्टां न श्येनो वसतिम पतामि | इन्द्रं नमस्यन्न उपमेभिर अर्कैर यः स्तोत्रिभ्यो हव्यो अस्ति यामन ||
uped ahaṁ dhanadām apratītaṁ juṣṭāṁ na śyeno vasatim patāmi | indraṁ namasyann upamebhir arkair yaḥ stotṛbhyo havyo asti yāman ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uped: I have approached
    • ahaṁ: I
    • dhanadām: of the wealthy
    • apratītaṁ: not understood
    • juṣṭāṁ: filled with
    • na: not
    • śyena: like a falcon
    • vasatim: dwelling
    • patāmi: I fly
    • indraṁ: to Indra
    • namasyann: bowing
    • upamebhir: with comparisons
    • arkair: with hymns
    • yaḥ: who
    • stotṛbhyo: to the worshippers
    • havyo: worthy of offerings
    • asti: is
    • yāman: the one who is invoked
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence towards Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The speaker acknowledges the wealth and abundance that comes from the divine, indicating a relationship between material prosperity and spiritual acknowledgment.
    • The phrase "not understood" suggests a recognition of the complexities of wealth and prosperity, which may not always be clear or straightforward.
    • The imagery of flying like a falcon symbolizes aspiration and the pursuit of higher goals, while also indicating a sense of freedom and power.
    • The act of bowing to Indra with hymns signifies humility and the importance of worship in attaining blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in the practice of gratitude. For instance, individuals who acknowledge their blessings and express gratitude often find themselves more content and fulfilled.
    • In business, leaders who recognize the contributions of their teams and express appreciation can foster a positive work environment, leading to greater success and prosperity.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and understanding, as suggested by the phrase "not understood," can be seen in the continuous learning and adaptation that individuals and organizations undergo to thrive in a complex world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that contribute to our prosperity and well-being.
    • It teaches us the importance of humility, gratitude, and the pursuit of higher aspirations in our lives.
    • By integrating these values into our daily practices, we can cultivate a more meaningful and prosperous existence, both materially and spiritually.
