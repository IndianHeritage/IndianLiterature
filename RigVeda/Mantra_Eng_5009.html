यस्याऽनन्तो अहृतस्त्वेषश्चरिष्णुरर्णवः |अमश्चरति रोर्वत ||
yasyā ananto ahrutas tveṣaś cariṣṇur arṇavaḥ | amaś carati roruvat ||
  • yasyā - "of whom" or "whose": This word indicates possession or relation, referring to a divine entity or force.
  • ananto - "infinite" or "endless": This signifies the boundless nature of the divine, suggesting that the entity being referred to transcends all limitations.
  • ahrutas - "that which is drawn forth" or "that which is brought forth": This can imply the act of creation or manifestation, highlighting the process through which the infinite becomes tangible.
  • tveṣaś - "this" or "that": This word serves to specify the subject being discussed, linking it back to the divine force.
  • cariṣṇur - "moving" or "wandering": This suggests a dynamic quality, indicating that the divine is not static but is constantly in motion, influencing the world.
  • arṇavaḥ - "ocean" or "vast expanse": This metaphor represents the depth and vastness of existence, akin to the ocean that holds countless mysteries.
  • amaś - "the one who is not" or "the non-existent": This term can refer to the void or the absence that exists alongside creation, emphasizing the duality of existence.
  • carati - "moves" or "travels": This reinforces the idea of movement and change, suggesting that existence is in a constant state of flux.
  • roruvat - "roaring" or "crying": This evokes a sense of power and presence, indicating that the divine force has a voice and is actively engaged in the world.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of the divine and its relationship with the universe. It speaks to the infinite essence of the divine, which manifests in the world and is ever-moving, much like the vast ocean. The duality of existence is highlighted, where creation and non-existence coexist, suggesting that life is a balance of presence and absence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios. For instance, in moments of uncertainty or change, one can reflect on the idea that the divine is always present, guiding and influencing the course of events. Just as the ocean is vast and deep, our experiences are filled with layers of meaning and complexity. Understanding that there is a divine force at play can provide comfort and perspective during challenging times.


In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine's infinite nature and its active role in the world. It encourages individuals to embrace the dynamic aspects of life, acknowledging both creation and the void. By doing so, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of existence and find peace amidst the chaos of life.
