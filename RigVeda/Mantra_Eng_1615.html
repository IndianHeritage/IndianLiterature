युवां यज्ञैः प्रथमā गोभिर अञ्जत ऋतावानां मनसो न प्रयुक्तिषु |भरन्ति वाम मनमनā संयतā गिरो 'दृप्यतā मनसā रेवद आशाथे ||
yuvāṁ yajñaiḥ prathamā gobhir añjata ṛtāvānā manaso na prayuktiṣu | bharanti vām manmanā saṁyatā giro 'dṛpyatā manasā revad āśāthe ||
  • yuvāṁ yajñaiḥ: This phrase refers to the youthful energy and vigor that is often associated with the act of sacrifice or offerings (yajna). In real life, this can be seen in the enthusiasm of young people participating in community service or social causes, where their energy contributes to the greater good.
  • prathamā gobhir añjata: This translates to "first among the cows" or "the best among the offerings." Cows are considered sacred in Hindu culture, symbolizing nourishment and abundance. In a modern context, this can relate to prioritizing sustainable practices in agriculture and animal husbandry, ensuring that we respect and care for our environment and its resources.
  • ṛtāvānā manaso na prayuktiṣu: This means "the one who is righteous in thought does not falter in intentions." It emphasizes the importance of having a clear and righteous mindset. In everyday life, this can be applied to ethical decision-making in business or personal relationships, where integrity and honesty guide our actions.
  • bharanti vām manmanā: This phrase suggests that those who are focused and disciplined in their thoughts can achieve great things. It highlights the power of concentration and determination. For example, students who dedicate themselves to their studies often achieve academic success, demonstrating the importance of focus in achieving goals.
  • saṁyatā giro 'dṛpyatā: This translates to "controlled speech does not lead to arrogance." It teaches the value of humility and the importance of speaking wisely. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who listen more than they speak, fostering an environment of respect and collaboration.
  • manasā revad āśāthe: This means "with the mind, one should aspire for the highest." It encourages individuals to set lofty goals and strive for excellence. In practical terms, this can be related to personal development, where individuals aim for self-improvement and pursue their passions with dedication.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of youthful energy, righteousness in thought, focus, humility in speech, and the aspiration for higher goals. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, promoting ethical behavior, sustainable practices, and a commitment to self-improvement. By embodying these values, one can contribute positively to society and foster a harmonious environment for all.
