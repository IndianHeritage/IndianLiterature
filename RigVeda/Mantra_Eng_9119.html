प्र सु ग्मन्ता धियसानस्य सक्षणि वरेभिर वराम् अभि षु प्रसीदतः |अस्माकं इन्द्र उभयं जुजोषति यत सोम्यस्यान्धसो बुबोधति ||
pra su gmantā dhiyasānasya sakṣaṇi varebhir varām̐ abhi ṣu prasīdataḥ | asmākam indra ubhayaṁ jujoṣati yat somyasyāndhaso bubodhati ||
  • Mantra: "pra su gmantā dhiyasānasya sakṣaṇi varebhir varām̐ abhi ṣu prasīdataḥ | asmākam indra ubhayaṁ jujoṣati yat somyasyāndhaso bubodhati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • su: good, well
    • gmantā: the one who is going, the one who moves
    • dhiyasānasya: of the one who is seated in wisdom or intellect
    • sakṣaṇi: with the power, with the ability
    • varebhir: with the best, with the most excellent
    • varām̐: the best, the most excellent
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • ṣu: good, auspicious
    • prasīdataḥ: may it be pleased, may it be satisfied
    • asmākam: of us, our
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • ubhayaṁ: both, dual
    • jujoṣati: nourishes, supports
    • yat: that which
    • somyasyāndhaso: of the one who is blind to the pleasant
    • bubodhati: understands, perceives
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking for his blessings and support in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "pra su gmantā" suggests a movement towards goodness and positivity, indicating a journey or progress in life.
    • "Dhiyasānasya" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intellect in guiding our actions.
    • The mention of "sakṣaṇi varebhir" highlights the need for strength and excellence in our pursuits.
    • By seeking the satisfaction of the divine ("prasīdataḥ"), we acknowledge the role of higher powers in our success.
    • The latter part of the mantra reflects on the dual nature of existence and the importance of understanding both aspects ("ubhayaṁ jujoṣati").
    • It suggests that true understanding comes from recognizing the pleasant and the unpleasant, leading to a more holistic view of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when embarking on a new project. Seeking wisdom and strength can lead to better decision-making and successful outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, understanding both the positive and negative aspects of interactions can foster deeper connections and empathy.
    • In education, students can benefit from this mantra by recognizing the importance of both knowledge and wisdom in their learning journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, strength, and divine support in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek balance and understanding in all aspects of life, recognizing that both positive and negative experiences contribute to our growth.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, we align ourselves with higher energies that can guide us towards success and fulfillment.
