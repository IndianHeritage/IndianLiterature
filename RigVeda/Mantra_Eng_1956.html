तव त्ये पीतो रसांस्य अनु विष्ठिताः |दिवि वाताः इव श्रिताः ||
tava tye pito rasā rajāṁsy anu viṣṭhitāḥ | divi vātā iva śritāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tava: Your
    • Tye: In your
    • Pito: The essence or the taste
    • Rasā: Flavors or juices
    • Rajāṁsy: The kings or rulers
    • Anu: Following or in accordance with
    • Viṣṭhitāḥ: Established or situated
    • Divi: In the heavens or sky
    • Vātā: Winds or air
    • Iva: Like or as
    • Śritāḥ: Residing or resting
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence that is present in the flavors and qualities of life.
    • It suggests that the essence of life, much like the taste of food, is derived from a higher source.
    • The reference to "kings" indicates that this essence is not just for the common man but is universal, affecting all levels of society.
    • The comparison to winds in the heavens signifies the omnipresence of this divine essence, suggesting that it is everywhere, influencing all aspects of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In culinary arts, the flavors we experience can be seen as a manifestation of nature's bounty, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life.
    • In leadership, a good leader embodies the essence of wisdom and compassion, influencing their followers positively, much like the divine essence mentioned in the mantra.
    • In nature, the winds carry seeds and pollen, facilitating growth and life, symbolizing how the divine essence nurtures and sustains all living beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine essence in all aspects of life, from the flavors we enjoy to the leadership we follow.
    • It encourages us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all beings and the higher forces that govern our existence.
    • In practical terms, this can inspire us to seek harmony in our lives, cultivate gratitude for the simple pleasures, and strive to embody the qualities of wisdom and compassion in our interactions with others.
