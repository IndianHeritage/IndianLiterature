एष सूर्येण हासते पवमानो अधि dyavi |पवित्रे मत्सरो मदः ||
eṣa sūryeṇa hāsate pavamāno adhi dyavi | pavitre matsaro madaḥ ||
  • eṣa: This word translates to "this" or "he," indicating a specific subject or entity being referred to.
  • sūryeṇa: This means "with the sun" or "by the sun," highlighting the importance of the sun in the context of the mantra.
  • hāsate: This translates to "smiles" or "is joyful," suggesting a state of happiness or positivity.
  • pavamāno: This word means "the purifying one" or "the wind," which can symbolize the cleansing and refreshing aspects of nature.
  • adhi: This means "upon" or "over," indicating a position or state of being.
  • dyavi: This translates to "in the sky" or "heaven," referring to the celestial realm.
  • pavitre: This means "pure" or "sacred," emphasizing the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
  • matsaro: This translates to "jealous" or "envious," indicating a negative emotion that can arise in human interactions.
  • madaḥ: This means "intoxication" or "exuberance," often referring to a state of overwhelming joy or pride.

The mantra can be interpreted as a celebration of the sun's joyful presence in the sky, which purifies and uplifts the atmosphere. It suggests that while the sun brings happiness and purity, there are also human emotions like jealousy and intoxication that can cloud our judgment and interactions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the sun brings light and joy, individuals can strive to cultivate positivity and purity in their lives, focusing on uplifting thoughts and actions.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the purifying wind can inspire people to appreciate and protect nature, recognizing its role in maintaining balance and purity in the environment.
  • Interpersonal Relationships: The mention of jealousy serves as a reminder to be aware of negative emotions that can affect relationships. By fostering a spirit of joy and purity, individuals can create healthier connections with others.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of positivity, purity, and awareness of human emotions. It encourages individuals to embrace the uplifting qualities of nature while being mindful of the darker aspects of human behavior. By applying these teachings in daily life, one can strive for a more harmonious existence, both personally and in relation to others.
