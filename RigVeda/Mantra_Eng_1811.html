सोमासो न ये सुतास तृप्तांशवो हृत्सु पीतासो दुवसो नासते |ऐषाम अंसेषु रम्भिणीव रारभे हस्तेषु खादिष च कृतिष च सं दधे ||
somāso na ye sutās tṛptāṁśavo hṛtsu pītāso duvaso nāsate | aiṣām aṁseṣu rambhiṇīva rārabhe hasteṣu khādiś ca kṛtiś ca saṁ dadhe ||
  • Mantra: "somāso na ye sutās tṛptāṁśavo hṛtsu pītāso duvaso nāsate | aiṣām aṁseṣu rambhiṇīva rārabhe hasteṣu khādiś ca kṛtiś ca saṁ dadhe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somāso: Those who are intoxicated with Soma (a sacred drink).
    • na: Not.
    • ye: Who.
    • sutās: Have drunk.
    • tṛptāṁśavo: Satisfied, content.
    • hṛtsu: In their hearts.
    • pītāso: Those who have drunk.
    • duvaso: Not disturbed.
    • nāsate: They do not sit.
    • aiṣām: Of these.
    • aṁseṣu: In their parts.
    • rambhiṇīva: Like a flowing river.
    • rārabhe: I begin.
    • hasteṣu: In the hands.
    • khādiś: Eating.
    • ca: And.
    • kṛtiś: Actions, deeds.
    • ca: And.
    • saṁ dadhe: I establish.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the state of those who have consumed Soma, a drink associated with divine inspiration and ecstasy.
    • It emphasizes that those who are truly satisfied and content in their hearts do not remain idle or disturbed.
    • The imagery of a flowing river suggests a natural and effortless movement, indicating that when one is in harmony with their inner self, actions and creativity flow smoothly.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of taking action and establishing one's deeds in the world, akin to how a river nourishes the land it flows through.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals who are passionate about their work and feel fulfilled are more likely to be productive and innovative, much like the satisfied drinkers of Soma.
    • Artists and creators often describe a state of flow where their creativity pours out effortlessly, similar to the flowing river metaphor in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, those who are content and emotionally fulfilled tend to contribute positively to their surroundings, fostering a nurturing environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner satisfaction and contentment as a precursor to productive action and creativity.
    • It teaches us that when we are at peace within ourselves, we can engage with the world more effectively and meaningfully.
    • In real life, this can be applied by seeking fulfillment in our pursuits, whether in work, relationships, or creative endeavors, allowing our actions to flow naturally and positively impact those around us.
