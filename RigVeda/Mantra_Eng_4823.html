आयं विदच चित्रदृष्टिकम् अर्णः शुक्रसद्मनाम उषसाम अनीकः |आयम् महान महता स्कम्भनेनोद द्यां अस्तभ्नाद वृषभो मरुत्वान ||
ayaṁ vidac citradṛśīkam arṇaḥ śukrasadmanām uṣasām anīke | ayam mahān mahatā skambhanenod dyām astabhnād vṛṣabho marutvān ||
  • ayaṁ vidac citradṛśīkam: This phrase can be interpreted as "this one knows the colorful vision." Here, "vidac" means to know, and "citradṛśīkam" refers to a vibrant or colorful sight. In real life, this can relate to individuals who possess a keen insight or understanding of the world around them, much like artists or visionaries who see beauty and potential where others may not.
  • arṇaḥ śukrasadmanām: This translates to "the one who is in the abode of purity." "Arṇaḥ" refers to a being or entity, while "śukrasadmanām" indicates a place of brightness or purity. This can be likened to a person who embodies integrity and clarity in their actions, akin to a leader who inspires others through their virtuous conduct.
  • uṣasām anīke: This means "in the company of the dawn." "Uṣasām" refers to the dawn or new beginnings, and "anīke" suggests a group or assembly. This can symbolize the importance of collaboration and community in achieving new beginnings, much like how a team works together to launch a new project or initiative.
  • ayam mahān mahatā: This translates to "this is great, by greatness." "Mahān" signifies greatness, and "mahatā" emphasizes the concept of being significant or important. This can be seen in real life through influential figures who have made substantial contributions to society, such as scientists or activists whose work has a lasting impact.
  • skambhanenod dyām astabhnād: This phrase means "by the support of the great, the sky is established." "Skambhanenod" refers to support or foundation, while "dyām astabhnād" indicates the establishment of the sky or the heavens. This can be related to the foundational principles that support our lives, such as education and moral values, which help us reach our aspirations.
  • vṛṣabho marutvān: This translates to "the bull, the one of the winds." "Vṛṣabho" refers to strength and power, often symbolized by a bull, while "marutvān" relates to the winds or the forces of nature. This can be interpreted as the dynamic forces in our lives that drive change and progress, similar to how innovation and technology propel society forward.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of knowledge, purity, collaboration, greatness, foundational support, and dynamic forces. Its significance lies in the reminder that true understanding and progress come from a combination of insight, integrity, community, and the strength to support and uplift one another. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while supporting each other in their journeys.
