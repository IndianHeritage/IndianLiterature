प्र सूनव ऋभूणाम बृहन नवन्त वृजनाः |क्षामा ये विश्वधायसोऽशन धेनुं न मातरम् ||
pra sūnava ṛbhūṇām bṛhan navanta vṛjanā | kṣāmā ye viśvadhāyaso 'śnan dhenuṁ na mātaram ||
  • pra sūnava: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the sons" or "to the offspring." It signifies the importance of lineage and the continuation of knowledge and values through generations.
  • ṛbhūṇām: This word refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and knowledge in guiding one's actions and decisions.
  • bṛhan: Meaning "great" or "vast," this term suggests the expansive nature of wisdom and the universe. It indicates that true understanding encompasses a broad perspective.
  • navanta: This translates to "new" or "fresh." It highlights the importance of innovation and renewal in thought and action, suggesting that wisdom should evolve with time.
  • vṛjanā: This word means "to be protected" or "to be safeguarded." It implies the need for safeguarding knowledge and values for future generations.
  • kṣāmā: This term can be understood as "forgiveness" or "patience." It underscores the importance of these virtues in maintaining harmony and understanding in relationships.
  • ye viśvadhāyaso: This phrase means "those who are universal in their approach." It suggests that wisdom should not be limited to a particular group but should be inclusive and universal.
  • 'śnan: This word refers to "to eat" or "to consume." It can symbolize the act of absorbing knowledge and experiences, which is essential for personal growth.
  • dhenum: Meaning "the cow," this term is often associated with nourishment and sustenance. In a broader sense, it represents the resources and support systems that nurture individuals and communities.
  • na mātaram: This translates to "not the mother." It can imply a separation from traditional or maternal sources of wisdom, suggesting that one must seek knowledge independently.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, innovation, and the safeguarding of knowledge for future generations. It highlights the virtues of forgiveness and inclusivity, suggesting that true understanding transcends individual or familial boundaries. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, where the focus should be on nurturing a culture of learning that values both traditional wisdom and new ideas. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge independently while also recognizing the importance of community support and resources. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that wisdom is a collective journey that benefits all of humanity.
