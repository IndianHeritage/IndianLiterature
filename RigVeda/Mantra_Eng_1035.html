अग्निषोमा हविषः प्रस्थितस्य वीटं हर्यतं वृषणा जुषेथाम् |सुशर्माणां स्ववसा हि भूतम अथ धत्तं यजमानाय शं योः ||
agnīṣomā haviṣaḥ prasthitasya vītaṁ haryataṁ vṛṣaṇā juṣethām | suśarmāṇā svavasā hi bhūtam athā dhattaṁ yajamānāya śaṁ yoḥ ||
  • Agnīṣomā: This term refers to the duality of Agni (fire) and Soma (the sacred drink). In a real-life context, this can symbolize the balance between energy (Agni) and nourishment (Soma) in our lives.
  • Haviṣaḥ: This word means offerings or sacrifices. In modern terms, it can represent the efforts we make in our daily lives to achieve our goals, akin to investing time and resources into our aspirations.
  • Prasthitasya: This translates to 'one who has set forth' or 'embarked on a journey.' It signifies the beginning of a new venture or phase in life, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city.
  • Vītaṁ: This means 'freedom' or 'liberation.' In a contemporary context, it can refer to the liberation from fears or limitations that hold us back from achieving our true potential.
  • Haryataṁ: This term can be interpreted as 'to be joyful' or 'to rejoice.' It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a positive attitude and celebrating small victories in life.
  • Vṛṣaṇā: This word means 'strength' or 'power.' It can be related to personal strength, resilience, and the ability to overcome challenges in life.
  • Juṣethām: This translates to 'may you enjoy' or 'may you be pleased.' It suggests the importance of finding joy in our endeavors and appreciating the journey.
  • Suśarmāṇā: This term means 'well-being' or 'good fortune.' It highlights the significance of seeking happiness and prosperity in our lives.
  • Svavasā: This translates to 'one's own strength' or 'self-reliance.' It emphasizes the importance of self-sufficiency and trusting in one's abilities.
  • Bhūtam: This word means 'the past' or 'what has been.' It reminds us to learn from our experiences and use them to inform our future decisions.
  • Dhattam: This means 'to bestow' or 'to grant.' It signifies the act of giving, whether it be knowledge, resources, or support to others.
  • Yajamānāya: This term refers to 'the one who performs the sacrifice' or 'the devotee.' It can be seen as a metaphor for those who dedicate themselves to a cause or community.
  • Śaṁ yoḥ: This translates to 'peace' or 'well-being.' It underscores the ultimate goal of all actions: to achieve harmony and tranquility in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of embarking on a journey with joy, strength, and self-reliance while making offerings and seeking well-being. In real life, it serves as a reminder to balance our energies, invest in our goals, and celebrate our achievements. It encourages us to learn from the past, support one another, and strive for peace and happiness in our lives. By embodying these principles, we can navigate our personal and professional journeys with purpose and fulfillment.
