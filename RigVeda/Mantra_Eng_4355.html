यā पृतनासु दुष्टरा यā वाजेषु श्रवाय्यā |यā पाँच चर्षणीर अभिंद्राग्नī ता हवामहे ||
yā pṛtanāsu duṣṭarā yā vājeṣu śravāyyā | yā pañca carṣaṇīr abhīndrāgnī tā havāmahe ||
  • yā pṛtanāsu duṣṭarā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is difficult to attain in the battles." Here, "pṛtanā" refers to battles or struggles, and "duṣṭarā" means difficult or challenging. In real life, this can relate to the challenges we face in achieving our goals, whether in personal endeavors, professional careers, or even in social situations.
  • yā vājeṣu śravāyyā: This part translates to "that which is glorious in the context of strength." "Vājeṣu" refers to strength or power, and "śravāyyā" means glorious or renowned. This can be seen in real life when individuals or groups achieve recognition for their strength, such as athletes winning championships or leaders gaining respect for their powerful decisions.
  • yā pañca carṣaṇīr: This translates to "that which is associated with the five great elements." "Pañca" means five, and "carṣaṇīr" refers to the elements or aspects of nature. In a practical sense, this can relate to the interconnectedness of life and the environment, emphasizing the importance of nature in our existence and the need for sustainable practices.
  • abhīndrāgnī: This term can be interpreted as "the fire that is invincible." "Abhī" means invincible or unconquerable, and "āgnī" refers to fire. In real life, this can symbolize the inner strength and resilience that individuals possess, allowing them to overcome obstacles and challenges, much like a fire that cannot be extinguished.
  • tā havāmahe: This phrase means "we invoke that." It signifies a call to action or a prayer for the qualities mentioned earlier. In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals, prayers, or even motivational speeches where individuals or groups seek to embody the qualities of strength, resilience, and connection to nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for strength and resilience in the face of challenges, recognizing the glory in our efforts, and acknowledging our connection to the natural world. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate inner strength, respect the environment, and seek glory in our endeavors, whether they be personal, professional, or communal. By invoking these qualities, we can navigate the complexities of life with courage and purpose.
