वीति जनस्य दिव्यस्य काव्यैर अधि सुवानो नहुष्येभिर इन्दुः |प्र यो नृभिर अमृतो मार्त्येभिर मार्मृजानो 'विभिर गोभिर अद्भिः ||
vītī janasya divyasya kavyair adhi suvāno nahuṣyebhir induḥ | pra yo nṛbhir amṛto martyebhir marmṛjāno 'vibhir gobhir adbhiḥ ||
  • vītī janasya divyasya: This phrase can be translated as "the path of the divine people." It signifies the journey or the way of those who are enlightened or possess divine qualities.
  • kavyair adhi suvāno nahuṣyebhir induḥ: Here, "kavyair" refers to poets or sages, "adhi" means "above" or "over," "suvāno" implies "well spoken" or "auspicious," and "nahuṣyebhir" relates to the divine beings. "Induḥ" refers to the moon or nectar. This line suggests that the divine path is illuminated by the wisdom and eloquence of the sages.
  • pra yo nṛbhir amṛto martyebhir: "Pra yo" means "whoever," "nṛbhir" refers to humans, "amṛto" means "immortal," and "martyebhir" means "mortal beings." This indicates that those who follow the divine path can transcend mortality and attain immortality.
  • marmṛjāno 'vibhir gobhir adbhiḥ: "Marmṛjāno" means "those who are aware of the essence," "'vibhir" refers to "with the help of," "gobhir" means "cows" (symbolizing wealth and nourishment), and "adbhiḥ" means "with water." This line emphasizes the importance of understanding the essence of life and utilizing resources wisely for sustenance.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life guided by divine wisdom and the teachings of enlightened beings. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, understanding the deeper truths of existence, and striving for immortality through righteous living.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, individuals can seek guidance from wise mentors or spiritual leaders, engage in self-reflection, and pursue knowledge that elevates their consciousness. It encourages people to live harmoniously with nature and utilize resources sustainably, much like how ancient societies revered cows as symbols of wealth and nourishment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine path that leads to enlightenment and immortality. By following the teachings of sages and being mindful of our actions and resources, we can cultivate a life of purpose, wisdom, and harmony. It encourages us to transcend the mundane and strive for a higher understanding of existence, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
