यो नः स्वो अरणो यश च निष्ट्यो जिघांसति |देवास तं सर्वे धूर्वन्तु ब्रह्म वर्म ममांतरणम् ||
yo naḥ svo araṇo yaś ca niṣṭyo jighāṁsati | devās taṁ sarve dhūrvantu brahma varma mamāntaram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: our
    • svo: own
    • araṇo: protector
    • yaś: who
    • ca: and
    • niṣṭyo: the one who is steadfast
    • jighāṁsati: desires to harm
    • devās: the gods
    • taṁ: him
    • sarve: all
    • dhūrvantu: may they protect
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or truth
    • varma: armor or protection
    • mamāntaram: within me
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a plea for divine protection against those who wish to harm us.
    • It acknowledges the presence of both benevolent and malevolent forces in life.
    • The invocation of the gods signifies a call for higher powers to shield us from negativity.
    • The term "brahma varma" refers to a protective shield that is not just physical but also spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied when facing competition or negativity from colleagues. One might recite it to seek protection and maintain focus on their own work.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to protect oneself from toxic influences, encouraging individuals to surround themselves with supportive and positive people.
    • During challenging times, such as illness or financial difficulties, individuals may find solace in this mantra, invoking divine support to navigate through hardships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from higher powers against negativity and harm.
    • It serves as a reminder that while challenges may arise, there is a spiritual armor available to us through faith and divine support.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to be aware of their surroundings, to seek positivity, and to invoke protection when faced with adversity.
