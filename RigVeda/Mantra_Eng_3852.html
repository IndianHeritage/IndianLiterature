कथो नु ते परि चराणि विद्वान् वीर्याऽ मघवन् या चकर्थ याऽ चो नु नव्याः कृतवः शविष्ठ प्रेदु ता ते विदथेṣu ब्रवाम ||
katho nu te pari carāṇi vidvān vīryā maghavan yā cakartha | yā co nu navyā kṛṇavaḥ śaviṣṭha pred u tā te vidatheṣu bravāma ||
  • katho nu te pari carāṇi vidvān: This phrase can be translated as "O wise one, where are your actions directed?" Here, "katho" means "where," "nu" is an interrogative particle, "te" means "your," "pari" means "around" or "in all directions," "carāṇi" refers to "actions" or "movements," and "vidvān" means "the wise one" or "the knowledgeable." This line emphasizes the importance of wisdom in guiding one's actions.
  • vīryā maghavan yā cakartha: This translates to "the powerful ones, O Maghavan, who have acted." "Vīryā" means "powerful" or "strength," "Maghavan" is a name for the deity Indra, often associated with strength and abundance, and "yā cakartha" means "who have acted" or "who have performed." This line highlights the significance of strength and action in achieving success.
  • yā co nu navyā kṛṇavaḥ śaviṣṭha: This can be interpreted as "and those who create new things." "Yā" means "those," "co" is a conjunction meaning "and," "nu" again serves as an interrogative particle, "navyā" means "new," "kṛṇavaḥ" means "create" or "make," and "śaviṣṭha" refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." This line encourages innovation and the pursuit of excellence.
  • pred u tā te vidatheṣu bravāma: This translates to "we speak of you in the gatherings." "Pred" means "we speak," "u" is a particle that can mean "indeed," "tā" means "you," "te" means "your," "vidatheṣu" refers to "in the gatherings" or "in the assemblies," and "bravāma" means "we declare" or "we proclaim." This line emphasizes the importance of community and the sharing of knowledge and achievements.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, strength, innovation, and community. It signifies that wise actions, powered by strength and creativity, are essential for success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as business, education, and personal development. For instance, a successful entrepreneur must act wisely, harness their strength, innovate, and share their journey with others to inspire and lead. This mantra serves as a reminder that our actions should be guided by wisdom and that collaboration and communication within communities are vital for collective growth and achievement.
