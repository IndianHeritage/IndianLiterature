भूरी हि ते सवना मानवेषु भूरी मनिषी हवते त्वाम इति |मारे अस्मन् मगवञ् ज्योक कः ||
bhūri hi te savanā mānuṣeṣu bhūri manīṣī havate tvām it | māre asman maghavañ jyok kaḥ ||
  • bhūri hi te savanā: This phrase translates to "Indeed, your offerings are abundant." Here, "bhūri" means abundant or plentiful, and "savanā" refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the concept of generosity and the importance of giving back to the community, whether through charity, volunteering, or supporting those in need.
  • mānuṣeṣu bhūri manīṣī: This means "among humans, you are greatly revered." "Mānuṣeṣu" refers to humans, and "manīṣī" signifies wisdom or reverence. This highlights the idea that certain individuals or deities are held in high esteem for their wisdom and contributions. In a modern context, this can be seen in how we honor leaders, thinkers, and innovators who have made significant impacts on society.
  • havate tvām it: This translates to "they invoke you." "Havate" means to invoke or call upon, and "tvām" refers to you. This reflects the practice of seeking guidance or blessings from a higher power. In everyday life, people often turn to prayer, meditation, or reflection during challenging times, seeking support and direction.
  • māre asman maghavañ jyok kaḥ: This can be interpreted as "who can match your greatness?" "Māre" means who, "asman" refers to us or our, "maghavañ" signifies greatness or abundance, and "jyok" means to shine or illuminate. This line emphasizes the unparalleled nature of the divine or the revered figure being addressed. In real life, this can be related to the admiration we have for extraordinary individuals or forces in our lives, such as mentors, leaders, or even nature itself.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of abundance, reverence, invocation, and greatness. Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine or wise figures in our lives and the importance of gratitude and respect towards them. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to appreciate the contributions of others, to be generous in their actions, and to seek guidance from those they admire. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging us to cultivate wisdom and generosity in our daily lives.
