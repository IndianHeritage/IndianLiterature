अदितिर्द्यावापृथिवी ऋतम् महद् इन्द्राविष्णू मरुतः स्वर् बृहत् |देवाम् आदित्याम् अवसे हवामहे वसून् रुद्रान् सवितारं सुदंशसम् ||
aditir dyāvāpṛthivī ṛtam mahad indrāviṣṇū marutaḥ svar bṛhat | devām̐ ādityām̐ avase havāmahe vasūn rudrān savitāraṁ sudaṁsasam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "We invoke the great Indra and Vishnu, the Maruts, the vast heavens and the earth, the truth (ṛta), and the divine beings, the Adityas. We offer our prayers to the radiant Savitar, the Rudras, and the bountiful ones."
  • Breakdown of Key Terms:
    • Aditi: Represents the infinite, the mother of gods, symbolizing the cosmic order.
    • Dyāvāpṛthivī: Refers to the heavens (dyau) and the earth (pṛthivī), indicating the duality of existence.
    • Rtam: The concept of cosmic order and truth, essential for harmony in the universe.
    • Indra: The king of gods, associated with rain, storms, and strength.
    • Vishnu: The preserver, representing protection and sustenance in the universe.
    • Marutaḥ: The storm deities, symbolizing the forces of nature.
    • Svar: The celestial realm or the sky, often associated with the divine.
    • Devām̐ Ādityām̐: The divine beings, particularly the solar deities, representing light and life.
    • Savitā: The sun god, associated with creation and illumination.
    • Rudrān: The fierce deities, often associated with transformation and destruction.
    • Vasūn: The wealth and abundance, representing prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, invoking Indra for rain is a common practice among farmers, as rain is essential for crops.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek the protection of Vishnu during challenging times, symbolizing the need for support and sustenance.
    • During festivals, people often celebrate the forces of nature, acknowledging the Maruts and their role in maintaining ecological balance.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of cosmic forces and deities that govern the universe. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony (ṛta) in our lives and the world around us. By recognizing the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and humanity, we can cultivate a sense of respect and responsibility towards our environment. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from seeking blessings for prosperity and protection to fostering a deeper understanding of our place in the cosmos. It serves as a reminder of the balance we must strive for in our daily lives, encouraging us to live in accordance with the natural order and divine principles.
