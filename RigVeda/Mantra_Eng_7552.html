तत् सु नो विश्वे आर्य आ सदा गृणन्ति कारवः |मरुतः सोमपीतये ||
tat su no viśve arya ā sadā gṛṇanti kāravaḥ | marutaḥ somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "tat su no viśve arya ā sadā gṛṇanti kāravaḥ | marutaḥ somapītaye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tat: that
    • su: good, auspicious
    • no: our
    • viśve: all, universal
    • arya: noble, honorable
    • ā: may come
    • sadā: always, forever
    • gṛṇanti: sing, praise
    • kāravaḥ: those who perform, do
    • marutaḥ: the winds, storm deities
    • somapītaye: for the sake of the soma (sacred drink)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the universal noble beings (arya) who are always engaged in praise and good deeds.
    • The term "marutaḥ" refers to the storm deities or winds, symbolizing the dynamic forces of nature.
    • The mention of "somapītaye" connects to the sacred drink soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate achievements, they embody the spirit of "gṛṇanti" by praising each other's contributions.
    • Environmental movements can be seen as modern-day "marutaḥ," where individuals and groups work to protect nature and promote sustainability.
    • In spiritual practices, the consumption of soma can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, where individuals seek to elevate their consciousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, noble actions, and the reverence for nature.
    • It serves as a reminder to engage in positive actions and to appreciate the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In real life, it encourages us to foster a spirit of cooperation and respect for the environment, while also seeking personal growth and enlightenment.
