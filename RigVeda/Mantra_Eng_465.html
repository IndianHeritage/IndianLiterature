मन्त्र: दिवा चित तमः कृत्वन्ति पर्यञ्जेनोदवाहेन | यत् पृथिवीं व्युन्दन्ति ||
divā cit tamaḥ kṛṇvanti parjanyenodavāhena | yat pṛthivīṁ vyundanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divā: by day
    • cit: to create or to make
    • tamaḥ: darkness
    • kṛṇvanti: they make or they create
    • parjanyena: by the rain or by the clouds
    • udavāhena: by the one who brings forth or by the one who carries
    • yat: that which
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • vyundanti: they separate or they divide
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of creation and transformation in nature.
    • It highlights how the forces of nature, represented by rain and clouds, bring light to the darkness of the day.
    • It also suggests that these natural elements play a crucial role in the sustenance and separation of the earth's resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider the role of rain in agriculture. Just as the mantra suggests, rain nourishes the earth, allowing crops to grow and flourish, thus separating the barren land from fertile fields.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the mantra can be applied to personal growth. Just as darkness can represent ignorance or challenges, the 'rain' can symbolize knowledge or experiences that help individuals overcome obstacles and achieve clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of the cyclical nature of life and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the natural processes that sustain life and encourages us to seek knowledge and enlightenment to overcome our personal challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace change and transformation, recognizing that challenges can lead to growth and new opportunities.
