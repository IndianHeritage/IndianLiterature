ददानमिन न ददभन्त मन्माग्निर वरूथम मम तस्य चाकन |जुषन्त विश्वान्य अस्य कर्मोपस्तुतिम भरमानस्य कारोः ||
dadānam in na dadabhanta manmāgnir varūtham mama tasya cākan | juṣanta viśvāny asya karmopastutim bharamāṇasya kāroḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadānam: giving, donation
    • in: not
    • na: not
    • dadabhanta: those who give
    • manmāgnir: my fire (representing energy or passion)
    • varūtham: protection, support
    • mama: my
    • tasya: of that
    • cākan: also
    • juṣanta: enjoying, cherishing
    • viśvāny: all, universal
    • asya: of this
    • karmopastutim: praise of actions
    • bharamāṇasya: of the one who bears
    • kāroḥ: of the creator
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of giving and the spirit of generosity.
    • It suggests that those who do not give are not truly engaging with the divine energy represented by fire.
    • The mantra calls for cherishing and enjoying the universal actions that contribute to the well-being of all.
    • It highlights the significance of supporting and protecting one another in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals who volunteer their time and resources to help others exemplify the spirit of giving.
    • In workplaces, employees who share knowledge and support their colleagues contribute to a positive and productive environment.
    • Philanthropy, where wealthy individuals donate to causes that benefit society, reflects the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for generosity and mutual support.
    • It serves as a reminder that true fulfillment comes from giving and uplifting others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger communities, better relationships, and a more compassionate society.
