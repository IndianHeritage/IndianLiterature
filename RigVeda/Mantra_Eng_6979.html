अध स्याऽयोषणा मही प्रतिची वशम अश्व्यम् |अधिरुक्मा विनीयते ||
adha syā yoṣaṇā mahī pratīcī vaśam aśvyam | adhirukmā vi nīyate ||
  • Mantra: "adha syā yoṣaṇā mahī pratīcī vaśam aśvyam | adhirukmā vi nīyate ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The one who is in the depths of the earth, the great one, is under the control of the powerful. The one who is swift is led forth."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • adha: below or beneath, indicating something that is deep or hidden.
    • syā: is or exists, referring to the state of being.
    • yoṣaṇā: the great one or powerful, often associated with strength and authority.
    • mahī: the earth or the great expanse, symbolizing stability and foundation.
    • pratīcī: towards the west, which can symbolize direction or guidance.
    • vaśam: control or power, indicating dominance or influence.
    • aśvyam: swift or quick, often associated with speed and agility.
    • adhirukmā: the one who is elevated or lifted, suggesting a rise in status or position.
    • vi nīyate: is led forth or guided, indicating movement or direction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the concept of leadership in organizations. A leader (the great one) must have control (vaśam) over their team (the depths of the earth) to guide them effectively towards success (swift movement).
    • In personal development, individuals often need to dig deep (adha) within themselves to find their strengths (yoṣaṇā) and rise (adhirukmā) to new challenges, being guided by their goals (vi nīyate).
    • In nature, the earth (mahī) provides a foundation for all life, and understanding its depths can lead to sustainable practices that respect and harness its power (vaśam).
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the relationship between power, control, and guidance. It teaches us that true strength comes from understanding our foundations and being able to lead ourselves and others effectively. In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, personal growth, or environmental stewardship, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of harnessing our inner strength and guiding ourselves and others towards a purposeful direction.
