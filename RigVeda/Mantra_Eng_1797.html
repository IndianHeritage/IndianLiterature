एष वः स्तोमो मरुत इयं गीर मांदार्यस्य मान्यस्य कारोः |एषा यासिष्ट तंवे वयां विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ | eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta: This phrase can be translated as "This hymn is for you, O Maruts." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with the power of the wind and storms. They symbolize strength and vitality.
  • iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ: This translates to "This song is of the revered and honorable." Here, "gīr" refers to a song or hymn, and "māndāryasya" indicates something that is esteemed or respected. It emphasizes the importance of honoring those who are worthy.
  • eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ: This means "This is the one that has been desired for our bodies." It suggests a longing for strength and vitality, which can be interpreted as a prayer for health and well-being.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum: This can be understood as "We seek knowledge and the essence of life." "Vidyā" refers to knowledge, while "jīradānum" implies the essence or core of life. This highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in our existence.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, honor, and knowledge. It reflects a deep reverence for the forces of nature and the wisdom that comes from understanding them.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in personal development, individuals can draw inspiration from the Maruts to cultivate resilience and strength in the face of challenges. In a community setting, honoring those who contribute positively can foster a sense of respect and unity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, honor, and knowledge. By embodying these qualities, individuals and communities can thrive, creating a harmonious balance in life.
