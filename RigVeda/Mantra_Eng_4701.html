पुरुहूतः यः पुरुगूर्त ऋभ्वाम् एकः पुरुप्रशस्तोऽस्ति यज्ञैः | रथो न महे शवसे युजानोऽस्माभिर इन्द्रो अनुमाद्यो भूत्व् ||
puruhūto yaḥ purugūrta ṛbhvām̐ ekaḥ purupraśasto asti yajñaiḥ | ratho na mahe śavase yujāno3 'smābhir indro anumādyo bhūt ||
  • Mantra: "puruhūto yaḥ purugūrta ṛbhvām̐ ekaḥ purupraśasto asti yajñaiḥ | ratho na mahe śavase yujāno3 'smābhir indro anumādyo bhūt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • puruhūto: One who is invoked frequently or called upon.
    • yaḥ: Who.
    • purugūrta: One who is greatly praised or renowned.
    • ṛbhvām̐: The one who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • ekaḥ: The one or the singular.
    • purupraśasto: The one who is highly esteemed or honored.
    • asti: Is or exists.
    • yajñaiḥ: Through sacrifices or offerings.
    • ratho: Chariot.
    • na: Not.
    • mahe: Great or powerful.
    • śavase: In strength or might.
    • yujāno: Joined or united.
    • asmābhir: By us.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • anumādyo: One who is praised or approved.
    • bhūt: Be or exist.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and leadership.
    • The phrase "puruhūto" signifies the importance of calling upon divine forces in times of need, much like how people seek guidance or support from mentors or leaders in their lives.
    • Indra is described as "purugūrta" and "purupraśasto," indicating that he is not only revered but also recognized for his wisdom and strength, similar to how a respected leader is acknowledged for their contributions to society.
    • The mention of "yajñaiḥ" highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in establishing a connection with the divine, akin to how individuals engage in community service or charitable acts to foster goodwill and support.
    • The imagery of a chariot ("ratho") symbolizes the journey of life, where one must harness their strength and resources to navigate challenges, much like how people work together in teams to achieve common goals.
    • The mantra emphasizes unity and collaboration ("yujāno"), reflecting the idea that collective efforts lead to greater achievements, similar to how teamwork in a workplace can lead to successful project outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking divine support and wisdom in our endeavors.
    • It highlights the value of community, collaboration, and the pursuit of knowledge, which are essential in both personal and professional realms.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance from mentors, engage in community service, and work collaboratively towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the strength that comes from collective efforts.
