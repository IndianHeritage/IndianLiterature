अश्वावन्तं रथिनं वीरवन्तं सहस्रिणं शतिनं वाजम् इन्द्र | भद्रव्रातं विप्रवीरं स्वर्षाम अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयिं दाह ||
aśvāvantaṁ rathinaṁ vīravantaṁ sahasriṇaṁ śatinaṁ vājam indra | bhadravrātaṁ vipravīraṁ svarṣām asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • Mantra: aśvāvantaṁ rathinaṁ vīravantaṁ sahasriṇaṁ śatinaṁ vājam indra
  • Translation: "O Indra, grant us the wealth of the strong, the valiant, the one who rides the chariot, the one who is adorned with horses, and the one who possesses a thousand powers."
  • Explanation:
    • aśvāvantaṁ: Refers to the one who possesses horses, symbolizing strength and speed. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or organizations that have the resources and capabilities to achieve their goals efficiently.
    • rathinaṁ: The charioteer or the one who rides the chariot, representing leadership and guidance. For example, a successful business leader who steers their company towards success embodies this quality.
    • vīravantaṁ: The valiant or brave one, indicating courage and valor. This can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or take risks to achieve their dreams.
    • sahasriṇaṁ: The one with a thousand powers, symbolizing immense potential and capabilities. This can be compared to a team with diverse skills working together to achieve a common goal.
    • śatinaṁ: The one who possesses wealth or resources. In a modern context, this could refer to financial stability or the abundance of opportunities available to individuals or communities.
  • Continuing the Mantra: bhadravrātaṁ vipravīraṁ svarṣām asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ
  • Translation: "Bestow upon us the auspicious, the noble, the brave, the one who brings forth prosperity and abundance."
  • Explanation:
    • bhadravrātaṁ: The auspicious or beneficial one, indicating positivity and good fortune. This can be related to the importance of a positive mindset in achieving success.
    • vipravīraṁ: The noble and brave, representing integrity and honor. This can be seen in leaders who inspire others through their ethical actions.
    • svarṣām: The one who brings forth prosperity, akin to a person or entity that creates opportunities for growth and success.
    • asmabhyaṁ: Referring to 'us', indicating a collective request for blessings and support.
    • citraṁ: The colorful or diverse, symbolizing variety and richness in life experiences. This can be related to the importance of diversity in teams and communities.
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: The one who brings forth abundance, akin to a fruitful harvest or successful outcomes in endeavors.
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity, emphasizing the importance of material and spiritual wealth in life.
    • dāḥ: To bestow or grant, indicating a plea for divine blessings and support.
  • Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation for strength, leadership, courage, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and the blessings of divine forces in achieving success. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their strengths and seek to cultivate bravery and leadership qualities.
    • In business, organizations can use this mantra as a guiding principle to foster teamwork, integrity, and a positive work environment.
    • In community building, it serves as a reminder of the importance of diversity and collective prosperity.
