विभक्तासि चित्रभानो सिन्धोर ऊर्मा उपाक आ |सद्यो दाशुषे क्षरसि ||
vibhaktāsi citrabhāno sindhor ūrmā upāka ā | sadyo dāśuṣe kṣarasi ||
  • Vibhaktāsi: This word translates to "you are divided" or "you are manifest in various forms." It signifies the diversity and multiplicity of existence.
  • Citrabhāno: Meaning "of colorful rays" or "of varied light," this term represents the beauty and vibrancy of life, suggesting that existence is filled with different experiences and perspectives.
  • Sindhor: This translates to "of the ocean," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the source of life. The ocean is often seen as a metaphor for the unconscious mind or the collective consciousness.
  • Ūrmā: Meaning "waves," this word reflects the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life. Just as waves rise and fall, so do the experiences and emotions we encounter.
  • Upāka: This term can be interpreted as "to be brought forth" or "to emerge." It signifies the process of creation and manifestation in the world.
  • Sadyo: Meaning "immediately" or "suddenly," this word emphasizes the immediacy of experiences and the transient nature of life.
  • Dāśuṣe: This translates to "for the one who gives" or "to the benefactor," indicating the importance of generosity and the act of giving in our lives.
  • Kṣarasi: Meaning "you dissolve" or "you perish," this term reflects the impermanence of life and the inevitability of change and decay.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life as a dynamic interplay of diversity, beauty, and impermanence. It reminds us that while we experience life in various forms and colors, we are all part of a larger ocean of existence. The waves symbolize the ups and downs we face, and the importance of generosity is highlighted as a way to connect with others. In real life, this mantra can be applied to our understanding of relationships, personal growth, and the acceptance of change. By embracing the transient nature of life and recognizing the beauty in diversity, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for our experiences and the interconnectedness of all beings.
