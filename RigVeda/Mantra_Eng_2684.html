जातो अग्नि रोचते चेकितानो वाजी विप्रः कविशस्तः सुदानुः |यं देवासा ईड्यं विश्वविदं हव्यवाहम अदधुर अध्वरेषु ||
jāto agnī rocate cekitāno vājī vipraḥ kaviśastaḥ sudānuḥ | yaṁ devāsa īḍyaṁ viśvavidaṁ havyavāham adadhur adhvareṣu ||
  • jāto agnī rocate: This phrase translates to "the born fire shines." Here, "jāto" means born, "agnī" refers to fire, and "rocate" means shines or illuminates. In real life, fire symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and transformation. For example, in a household, the fire in the kitchen represents the sustenance and warmth of family life.
  • cekitāno vājī vipraḥ: This can be interpreted as "the wise one, the seeker of strength." "Cekitāno" refers to being wise or knowledgeable, "vājī" means one who seeks strength or power, and "vipraḥ" refers to a learned person or sage. In modern contexts, this can relate to individuals who pursue education and wisdom to empower themselves and their communities, such as teachers or leaders who inspire others.
  • kaviśastaḥ sudānuḥ: This translates to "the one who is a poet and has good knowledge." "Kavi" means poet or seer, "śastaḥ" refers to being knowledgeable, and "sudānuḥ" means one who has good understanding. This can be seen in contemporary society through artists, writers, and thinkers who use their creativity and insight to address social issues and inspire change.
  • yaṁ devāsa īḍyaṁ: This means "whom the gods praise." "Yaṁ" refers to whom, "devāsa" means gods, and "īḍyaṁ" means worthy of worship or praise. In real life, this can be related to individuals or leaders who are revered for their contributions to society, such as humanitarian figures or spiritual leaders who promote peace and understanding.
  • viśvavidaṁ havyavāham: This translates to "the one who knows everything and carries offerings." "Viśvavidaṁ" means all-knowing, and "havyavāham" refers to one who carries offerings to the divine. This can be likened to scientists or researchers who seek to understand the universe and contribute to the betterment of humanity through their discoveries and innovations.
  • adadhur adhvareṣu: This means "they have placed in the rituals." "Adadhur" refers to placing or establishing, and "adhvareṣu" means in the rituals or sacrifices. In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of rituals in various cultures, such as weddings, graduations, or community gatherings that foster unity and shared values.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and the pursuit of strength in life. It highlights the role of individuals who illuminate the path for others, whether through education, creativity, or spiritual guidance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to seek knowledge, appreciate the contributions of wise individuals, and recognize the importance of rituals that bind communities together. It serves as a reminder that enlightenment and strength come from understanding and collaboration, which are essential for personal and societal growth.
