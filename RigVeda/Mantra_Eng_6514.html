ताभिर ā यातं वृषणोप मे हवं विश्वाप्सुं विश्ववार्यम् |इषा मंहिष्ठा पुरुभूतमा नराः याभिः कृविं वावृद्धुस ताभिर ā गतम् ||
tābhir ā yātaṁ vṛṣaṇopa me havaṁ viśvapsuṁ viśvavāryam | iṣā maṁhiṣṭhā purubhūtamā narā yābhiḥ kriviṁ vāvṛdhus tābhir ā gatam ||
  • tābhir ā yātaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "by these, may we be led" or "may we be guided by them." It signifies the importance of guidance and support in our lives.
  • vṛṣaṇopa me havaṁ: This translates to "may the strength come to me." It emphasizes the need for inner strength and resilience to face challenges.
  • viśvapsuṁ viśvavāryam: This can be understood as "the all-encompassing, the universal protector." It highlights the idea of a protective force or energy that surrounds us.
  • iṣā maṁhiṣṭhā: This means "may I attain the highest." It reflects the aspiration for achieving the best in life, whether in personal goals or spiritual pursuits.
  • purubhūtamā narā: This translates to "the most excellent among men." It suggests the importance of striving to be the best version of oneself and recognizing the potential in others.
  • yābhiḥ kriviṁ vāvṛdhus: This can be interpreted as "by whom the actions are nourished." It signifies the role of supportive relationships and community in fostering growth and success.
  • tābhir ā gatam: This phrase means "by these, may we arrive." It encapsulates the journey towards achieving our goals with the help of guidance and support.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of guidance, strength, protection, aspiration, excellence, and community support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a workplace setting, a leader can embody the qualities of guidance and strength, inspiring their team to strive for excellence and support one another in achieving common goals. This mantra serves as a reminder that our journey towards success is often facilitated by the relationships we build and the strength we cultivate within ourselves.
