याभिर वम्रं विपिपानम उपस्तुतं कलीं याभिर वित्तजानीं दुवस्यथः |याभिर व्यश्वम उता पृथिम आवतं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनां गतम् ||
yābhir vamraṁ vipipānam upastutaṁ kaliṁ yābhir vittajāniṁ duvasyathaḥ | yābhir vyaśvam uta pṛthim āvataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhir: This word can be translated as "by whom" or "through which." It indicates a source or means of action.
  • vamraṁ: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and resilience.
  • vipipānam: This word means "the one who drinks" or "the one who consumes." It can symbolize the act of taking in knowledge or experiences.
  • upastutaṁ: This translates to "having been praised" or "that which is honored." It reflects the importance of recognition and respect.
  • kaliṁ: This term can refer to "the dark" or "the negative." It may symbolize challenges or obstacles in life.
  • yābhir: Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the idea of the source or means.
  • vittajāniṁ: This means "the one who knows wealth" or "the knowledgeable." It signifies wisdom and understanding, particularly in material or spiritual wealth.
  • duvasyathaḥ: This translates to "you should strive" or "you should work hard." It emphasizes the importance of effort and diligence.
  • yābhir: Again, this word is used to emphasize the means or sources.
  • vyaśvam: This term means "the universe" or "the whole world." It signifies the vastness of existence.
  • uta: This translates to "and" or "also," connecting ideas together.
  • pṛthim: This means "the earth" or "the ground." It symbolizes stability and foundation.
  • āvataṁ: This translates to "that which is protected" or "that which is sheltered." It reflects the idea of safety and security.
  • tābhir: This word means "by those" or "through them," indicating a collective effort.
  • ū: This can be interpreted as "the great" or "the powerful," emphasizing strength.
  • ṣu: This means "good" or "well," indicating positivity.
  • ūtibhir: This translates to "by the powers" or "through the energies," suggesting the influence of divine forces.
  • aśvinā: This term refers to "the Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being in Vedic literature.
  • gatam: This means "gone" or "departed," indicating a transition or change.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, knowledge, and collective effort in overcoming challenges and achieving well-being. It suggests that through the recognition of our sources of strength and wisdom, we can navigate the complexities of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during difficult times, or personal growth through learning and resilience. The mantra serves as a reminder that by acknowledging our strengths and working together, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
