आ पवमान नो भरार्यो अदाशुषो गयम् |कृधि प्रजावतीर इषः ||
ā pavamāna no bharāryo adāśuṣo gayam | kṛdhi prajāvatīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā pavamāna: "O purified one" - This refers to a divine or sacred entity that is pure and free from impurities.
    • no: "for us" - This indicates a plea or request directed towards the divine.
    • bharāryo: "bestow" - A call for the divine to grant or provide something valuable.
    • adāśuṣo: "without delay" - This emphasizes the urgency of the request.
    • gayam: "prosperity" or "wealth" - Refers to abundance, success, or well-being.
    • kṛdhi: "make" or "create" - A request for action to be taken.
    • prajāvatīr: "progeny" or "offspring" - This signifies the importance of family, lineage, and continuity.
    • iṣaḥ: "desire" or "wish" - This indicates the intention behind the request.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the divine to bestow prosperity and abundance upon us without delay, while also emphasizing the importance of progeny and the continuity of life.
    • It reflects a deep desire for not just material wealth but also for the growth and flourishing of families and communities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be invoked during rituals or ceremonies that celebrate new beginnings, such as weddings or the birth of a child, to seek blessings for prosperity and a healthy lineage.
    • In a community context, it can be used during festivals or gatherings where people come together to pray for collective well-being and abundance, reinforcing the idea of unity and shared prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to prosperity, encompassing both material wealth and the importance of family and community.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as family celebrations, community gatherings, and personal prayers, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our desires for wealth and the well-being of our loved ones.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek not just individual success but also the flourishing of those around us, fostering a sense of responsibility towards our families and communities.
