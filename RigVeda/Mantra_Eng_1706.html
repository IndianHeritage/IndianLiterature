त्रीणि त आहुर दिवि बन्धनानि त्रीण्य अप्सु त्रीण्य अन्तः समुद्रे |उतेव मे वरुणश चान्त्स्य अर्वन यत्रा त आहुः परमं जनित्रम् ||
trīṇi ta āhur divi bandhanāni trīṇy apsu trīṇy antaḥ samudre | uteva me varuṇaś chantsy arvan yatrā ta āhuḥ paramaṁ janitram ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to: "They say there are three bonds in the sky, three in the waters, and three in the depths of the ocean. O Varuna, may these bonds be loosened for me, wherever they say you are the supreme creator."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • trīṇi: Three - signifies a triad or a set of three.
    • ta: They say - indicates a statement or belief held by others.
    • āhur: They have said - a reference to traditional knowledge or wisdom.
    • divi: In the sky - refers to the celestial realm.
    • bandhanāni: Bonds or ties - signifies restrictions or limitations.
    • apsu: In the waters - refers to the earthly realm, particularly water bodies.
    • antaḥ: In the depths - signifies the inner or hidden aspects.
    • samudre: In the ocean - represents vastness and depth.
    • uteva: May it be - a plea or request.
    • me: For me - personalizes the request.
    • varuṇa: O Varuna - invokes the deity associated with cosmic order and water.
    • chantsy: Loosen or free - a desire for liberation from constraints.
    • arvan: Wherever - indicates a sense of universality.
    • yatrā: Wherever - emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine.
    • ta: They say - reiterates the belief held by others.
    • paramaṁ: Supreme - denotes the highest or ultimate.
    • janitram: Creator - refers to the source of all existence.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the concept of bonds or limitations that exist in various realms of existence—celestial, earthly, and deep within the ocean.
    • It reflects the human experience of feeling constrained by various factors in life, whether they be societal, emotional, or spiritual.
    • By invoking Varuna, the mantra seeks liberation from these constraints, emphasizing the importance of divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, individuals often feel bound by responsibilities, societal expectations, or personal fears. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek freedom from these limitations.
    • For example, someone may feel trapped in a job that does not fulfill them. By meditating on this mantra, they can find the courage to pursue their passions and break free from the constraints of their current situation.
    • Additionally, in times of emotional distress, reciting this mantra can provide solace and a sense of connection to a higher power, encouraging individuals to trust in the process of life and seek liberation from their struggles.
