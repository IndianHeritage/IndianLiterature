आ क्षोदो महि वृत्तं नदीनाम् परिष्टितम् असृज ऊर्मिम अपाम् |तासाम् अनु प्रवत इन्द्र पन्थाम् प्रार्दयो नीचीर अपसः समुद्रम् ||
ā kṣodo mahi vṛtaṁ nadīnām pariṣṭhitam asṛja ūrmim apām | tāsām anu pravata indra panthām prārdayo nīcīr apasaḥ samudram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • kṣodo: the source or origin
    • mahi: great or vast
    • vṛtaṁ: enveloped or surrounded
    • nadīnām: of the rivers
    • pariṣṭhitam: established or situated
    • asṛja: I create or I bring forth
    • ūrmim: waves or currents
    • apām: of the waters
    • tāsām: of them
    • anu: following or after
    • pravata: the path or way
    • indra: the deity or powerful one
    • panthām: the path or route
    • prārdayo: I invoke or I call upon
    • nīcīr: the low or humble
    • apasaḥ: of the waters
    • samudram: the ocean or sea
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness and the source of rivers, emphasizing their significance in the natural world.
    • The phrase "ā kṣodo mahi vṛtaṁ nadīnām" suggests that from the great source, the rivers are established, highlighting the interconnectedness of water bodies.
    • When it mentions "asṛja ūrmim apām," it indicates the creation of waves and currents, symbolizing the dynamic nature of water.
    • The invocation of "indra panthām" signifies calling upon a powerful force or deity to guide the path of these waters, which can be interpreted as seeking divine guidance in our endeavors.
    • The concluding part "nīcīr apasaḥ samudram" reflects the humility of the rivers as they flow towards the ocean, symbolizing the journey of life and the importance of humility in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, rivers are essential for sustaining life, providing water for agriculture, drinking, and supporting ecosystems. This mantra can remind us of the importance of preserving our water sources.
    • In personal life, the invocation of guidance can be seen in seeking mentorship or support from experienced individuals when navigating challenges.
    • The humility of rivers can be a metaphor for personal growth; just as rivers flow towards the ocean, we too should strive for growth while remaining humble.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of water, the interconnectedness of nature, and the importance of humility and guidance in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect natural resources, seek guidance in our journeys, and maintain humility as we strive for our goals.
    • In a broader context, it can inspire environmental stewardship and a deeper appreciation for the natural world around us.
