अग्नि विश्वेभिरागहि देवभिरहव्यदातये |होतारं त्वा वृष्णीमहे ||
agne viśvebhir ā gahi devebhir havyadātaye | hotāraṁ tvā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Viśvebhir: This term means "with all" or "universal," indicating the inclusiveness of the invocation.
  • Ā gahi: Translated as "come near" or "approach," it signifies a call for the divine presence.
  • Devebhir: This means "with the gods," emphasizing the connection between the individual and the divine.
  • Havyadātaye: This term refers to the act of offering or sacrifice, particularly in the context of rituals.
  • Hotāraṁ: This word means "the one who invokes" or "the priest," highlighting the role of the mediator in rituals.
  • Tvā: This means "you," referring directly to Agni, the deity being invoked.
  • Vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating a collective intention or desire.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, inviting him to come closer and be present among the worshippers, along with all the other gods. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective intention in spiritual practices. The invocation of Agni is significant as he is considered the mediator between humans and the divine, facilitating communication and offerings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra can serve as a reminder to seek guidance and support from higher powers in personal endeavors, fostering a sense of connection with the universe.
  • Environmental Awareness: Agni, as a symbol of fire, can also represent the importance of respecting nature and the elements, encouraging sustainable practices.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energy and the collective spirit in rituals. It highlights the role of Agni as a mediator and the necessity of community in spiritual practices. By understanding and applying this mantra in daily life, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the divine and the world around them.
