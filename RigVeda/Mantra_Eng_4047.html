तद अस्तु मित्रावरुणा तद अग्ने शं योर अस्मभ्यम इदं अस्तु शस्तम् |अशीमहि गाधम उता प्रतिष्ठां नमो दिवे बृहत सादनाय ||
tad astu mitrāvaruṇā tad agne śaṁ yor asmabhyam idam astu śastam | aśīmahi gādham uta pratiṣṭhāṁ namo dive bṛhate sādanāya ||
  • tad astu mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase can be translated as "Let it be so, O Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing the sun and the cosmic order, respectively. In real life, this can be seen as a call for harmony and balance in our relationships and interactions with others.
  • tad agne: This means "Let it be so, O Agni." Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of passion and motivation in our endeavors, whether in personal projects or professional pursuits.
  • śaṁ yor asmabhyam idam astu śastam: This translates to "May this be auspicious for us." It emphasizes the desire for well-being and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a wish for success in our actions and decisions, fostering a positive environment for growth.
  • aśīmahi gādham uta pratiṣṭhāṁ: This means "We strive for strength and stability." It highlights the importance of resilience and a solid foundation in life. For example, in a career context, this could refer to the need for continuous learning and adaptability to maintain a strong position in a competitive job market.
  • namo dive bṛhate sādanāya: This translates to "Salutations to the vast abode of the heavens." This can be seen as an acknowledgment of the greater universe and our place within it. In real life, this can inspire humility and gratitude for the opportunities we have, encouraging us to contribute positively to the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking harmony, strength, and auspiciousness in our lives. It encourages us to recognize the divine forces at play in our existence and to strive for balance and stability in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional endeavors, and our connection to the larger universe. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can foster a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
