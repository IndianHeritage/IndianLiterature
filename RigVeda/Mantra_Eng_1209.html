ऋभुक्षणम इन्द्रं आ हुव उतय ऋभून वाजान मरुतः सोमपीतये |उभा मित्रावरुणा नूनम अश्विनां ते नो हिन्वन्तु सातये धिये जिषे ||
ṛbhukṣaṇam indram ā huva ūtaya ṛbhūn vājān marutaḥ somapītaye | ubhā mitrāvaruṇā nūnam aśvinā te no hinvantu sātaye dhiye jiṣe ||
  • ṛbhukṣaṇam indram ā huva: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra, the lord of the rich harvest." Indra is often seen as the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to agricultural practices where farmers pray for rain to ensure a good harvest.
  • ūtaya ṛbhūn vājān marutaḥ somapītaye: This part means "May the Maruts, the storm gods, bring us strength and nourishment." The Maruts are associated with storms and winds, and they are often called upon for their power. In a modern context, this can be likened to the importance of natural elements in agriculture and how they contribute to the growth of crops.
  • ubhā mitrāvaruṇā: This translates to "Both Mitra and Varuna." Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and truth. Together, they signify the balance of nature and the importance of maintaining harmony in our relationships. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way communities work together to support one another.
  • nūnam aśvinā te no hinvantu: This means "May the Ashvins, the divine physicians, not hinder us." The Ashvins are twin gods associated with health and medicine. This line emphasizes the importance of health and well-being in our lives. In contemporary society, this can relate to the significance of healthcare and the need for access to medical services.
  • sātaye dhiye jiṣe: This translates to "May they grant us strength and wisdom." This final part highlights the desire for both physical strength and mental clarity. In real life, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals seek to improve their physical health and mental acuity through education, exercise, and mindfulness practices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, health, and community. It calls upon various deities to provide strength, nourishment, and harmony, reflecting the essential elements for a prosperous life. In today's world, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards sustainable living, health, and well-being, recognizing the importance of balance in all aspects of life.
