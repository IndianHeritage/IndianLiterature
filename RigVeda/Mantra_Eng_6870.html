तुभ्यं घेत ते जनाः इमे विश्वाः सुख्षितयः पृथक् |धासिं हिन्वन्त्य अतवे ||
tubhyaṁ ghet te janā ime viśvāḥ sukṣitayaḥ pṛthak | dhāsiṁ hinvanty attave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • ghet: we offer
    • te: to you
    • janā: people
    • ime: these
    • viśvāḥ: all
    • sukṣitayaḥ: well-being
    • pṛthak: separately
    • dhāsiṁ: we serve
    • hinvanty: they bring
    • attave: to you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep sense of devotion and service to a higher power or divine entity.
    • The speaker acknowledges the collective well-being of all people, emphasizing unity and harmony.
    • It highlights the importance of offering and serving, suggesting that true fulfillment comes from contributing to the welfare of others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community service, individuals come together to help those in need, reflecting the idea of serving for the greater good.
    • In workplaces, fostering a culture of collaboration and support among colleagues can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
    • In family settings, prioritizing the well-being of each member can strengthen bonds and create a nurturing atmosphere.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective responsibility and service.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond themselves and contribute to the well-being of others, fostering a sense of community.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, such as social work, teamwork, and family dynamics, promoting a more compassionate and interconnected society.
