इन्द्राग्नि अवसागतम् अस्मभ्यं चर्षणीसहाः |मा नो दुष्शंस इषत ||
indrāgnī avasā gatam asmabhyaṁ carṣaṇīsahā | mā no duḥśaṁsa īśata ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of the gods, and Agni, the fire god. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles, while Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Avasā gatam: This phrase can be interpreted as "having come to a state of refuge" or "having reached a place of safety." It suggests a sense of security and stability that one seeks in life.
  • Asmabhyaṁ: This word means "for us" or "to us," indicating a collective plea or request from a group of individuals, often the community or family.
  • Carṣaṇīsahā: This term refers to the "sacred space" or "the place of gathering." It signifies the importance of community and shared experiences in spiritual and social contexts.
  • Mā no duḥśaṁsa: This phrase translates to "may we not be harmed" or "may we be free from misfortune." It expresses a desire for protection and well-being.
  • Īśata: This word means "to command" or "to govern." It implies a request for divine guidance and authority in navigating life's challenges.

  • Real-Life Example: In a community gathering, individuals often seek blessings for safety and prosperity. For instance, during a wedding ceremony, families invoke the blessings of deities like Indra and Agni to ensure a harmonious and prosperous union.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and the strength found in community. It reflects the human desire for safety, guidance, and the collective well-being of society.
  • Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during family gatherings, community events, or even personal moments of reflection. It serves as a reminder to seek strength from both divine sources and communal support in overcoming life's challenges.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "indrāgnī avasā gatam asmabhyaṁ carṣaṇīsahā | mā no duḥśaṁsa īśata ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and the strength of community. It is a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of collective well-being in facing life's adversities.
