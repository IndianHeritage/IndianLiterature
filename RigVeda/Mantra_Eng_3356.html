ऋजिप्य इम् इन्द्रावतो न भुज्युं श्येनो जभारा बृहतो अधि ष्णोः |अन्तः पतत पतत्र्य अस्य पर्णम् अध यामनि प्रसितस्य तद वेः ||
ṛjipya īm indrāvato na bhujyuṁ śyeno jabhāra bṛhato adhi ṣṇoḥ | antaḥ patat patatry asya parṇam adha yāmani prasitasya tad veḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ṛjipya īm indrāvato na bhujyuṁ śyeno jabhāra bṛhato adhi ṣṇoḥ | antaḥ patat patatry asya parṇam adha yāmani prasitasya tad veḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛjipya: Straightforward, honest, or righteous.
    • īm: May we attain or may we have.
    • indrāvato: Of Indra, the king of gods, associated with power and strength.
    • na: Not.
    • bhujyuṁ: To be consumed or to be devoured.
    • śyeno: Like a falcon or eagle, symbolizing speed and agility.
    • jabhāra: To carry or to bear.
    • bṛhato: Of the great or vast.
    • adhi: Above or over.
    • ṣṇoḥ: Of the earth or ground.
    • antaḥ: Within or inside.
    • patat: Falling or descending.
    • patatry: A bird that falls or descends.
    • asya: Of this.
    • parṇam: Leaf or foliage.
    • adha: Below or beneath.
    • yāmani: To the one who is going or moving.
    • prasitasya: Of the one who is well-known or famous.
    • tad: That.
    • veḥ: Strength or power.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of righteousness and strength, asking for the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who represents power and authority.
    • The imagery of the falcon suggests agility and the ability to rise above challenges, while the mention of the earth signifies grounding and stability.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the ability to navigate through life's challenges without being consumed by them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges at work. By embodying the qualities of righteousness and strength, one can navigate through difficult situations with integrity.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages individuals to remain grounded and honest, fostering trust and respect among peers.
    • In sports, athletes can draw inspiration from the falcon's agility, striving to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals with determination and focus.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of inner strength, righteousness, and agility in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to remain true to their values while striving for success.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra in various aspects of life, one can cultivate resilience and integrity, ultimately leading to personal and professional growth.
