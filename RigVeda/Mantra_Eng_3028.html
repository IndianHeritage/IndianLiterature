उषो वाजेन वाजिनी प्रचेताः स्तोमं जुषस्व गृणतो मघोनि |पुराणी देवी युवतिः पुरंधिर अनु व्रतं चरसि विश्ववारे ||
uṣo vājena vājini pracetāḥ stomaṁ juṣasva gṛṇato maghoni | purāṇī devi yuvatiḥ puraṁdhir anu vrataṁ carasi viśvavāre ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • vājena: This term means "with strength" or "with vigor," indicating the power and energy associated with the dawn.
  • vājini: This is related to the concept of "victory" or "success," suggesting that the dawn brings opportunities for triumph.
  • pracetāḥ: This word means "with intelligence" or "with wisdom," emphasizing the importance of awareness and understanding as the day begins.
  • stomaṁ: This refers to a hymn or praise, indicating the act of expressing gratitude and reverence for the new day.
  • juṣasva: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you delight in," suggesting a call to embrace the blessings of the dawn.
  • gṛṇato: This word means "of those who sing" or "of those who praise," highlighting the communal aspect of celebrating the dawn.
  • maghoni: This term refers to "greatness" or "abundance," indicating the richness that the new day brings.
  • purāṇī: This means "ancient" or "eternal," suggesting that the dawn is a timeless phenomenon that has been revered throughout history.
  • devi: This word means "goddess," indicating a divine aspect to the dawn, often personified as a feminine figure.
  • yuvatiḥ: This means "young" or "youthful," symbolizing freshness and vitality associated with the morning.
  • puraṁdhir: This term refers to "the one who creates" or "the architect," suggesting that the dawn is a creator of new possibilities.
  • anu vrataṁ: This means "following the vow" or "adhering to the path," indicating a commitment to righteousness and purpose as the day unfolds.
  • carasi: This word means "you move" or "you travel," suggesting the journey of life that begins anew each day.
  • viśvavāre: This means "in the universal protection" or "in the care of all," indicating that the dawn brings a sense of safety and security.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of dawn as a powerful and divine force that brings new opportunities, wisdom, and vitality. It encourages individuals to embrace the day with gratitude and awareness, recognizing the potential for success and abundance. In real life, this mantra can be applied as a daily reminder to start each day with a positive mindset, to appreciate the beauty of new beginnings, and to commit to a path of purpose and righteousness. By acknowledging the dawn as a sacred time, individuals can cultivate a sense of mindfulness and intention in their daily lives, fostering a deeper connection to the world around them.
