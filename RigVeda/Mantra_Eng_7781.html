परी सुवानास इन्दवो मदाय बर्हणा गिरा |सुताः अर्षन्ति धारणया ||
pari suvānāsa indavo madāya barhaṇā girā | sutā arṣanti dhārayā ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari: Around, encompassing.
    • suvānāsa: Good or auspicious speech.
    • indavo: The divine or celestial beings, often referring to the gods.
    • madāya: For the purpose of joy or intoxication.
    • barhaṇā: With great praise or exaltation.
    • girā: Speech or sound.
    • sutā: The offerings or libations.
    • arṣanti: They are invoked or called upon.
    • dhārayā: By the act of holding or sustaining.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking the divine beings through auspicious speech.
    • It emphasizes the importance of joyful expression and exaltation in communication.
    • The offerings made are not just physical but also represent the thoughts and intentions behind them.
    • It suggests that through sincere and uplifting speech, one can connect with the divine and sustain that connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the vows exchanged are filled with love and joy, invoking blessings from the divine for a prosperous union.
    • During festivals, the prayers and chants are performed with great enthusiasm, creating a joyful atmosphere that invites divine presence.
    • In motivational speeches, the speaker uses uplifting language to inspire and connect with the audience, creating a shared sense of purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of positive and uplifting communication in our lives.
    • It teaches us that our words have the power to invoke divine blessings and create a harmonious environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community gatherings, or spiritual practices, the essence of this mantra can guide us to communicate with intention and joy.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that through our speech and offerings, we can foster connections that uplift ourselves and those around us.
