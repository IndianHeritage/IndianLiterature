प्राक्तुभ्य इन्द्रः प्र वृहद् अहभ्यः प्रान्तरिक्षात् प्र समुद्रस्य धासेः |प्र वातस्य प्रथसः प्र ज्मो अन्तात् प्र सिन्धुभ्यो रिरिचे प्र क्षितिभ्यः ||
prāktubhya indraḥ pra vṛdho ahabhyaḥ prāntarikṣāt pra samudrasya dhāseḥ | pra vātasya prathasaḥ pra jmo antāt pra sindhubhyo ririce pra kṣitibhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāktubhya: From the past or ancient times
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • vṛdho: The one who increases or grows
    • ahabhyaḥ: From the day or the light
    • prāntarikṣāt: From the atmosphere or the space
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • samudrasya: Of the ocean or the sea
    • dhāseḥ: Of the servant or the one who serves
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • vātasya: Of the wind
    • prathasaḥ: Of the dawn or the light
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • jmo: From the depths or the core
    • antāt: From the end or the limit
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • sindhubhyo: From the rivers or the waters
    • ririce: To be nourished or to grow
    • pra: Before or in front of
    • kṣitibhyaḥ: From the earth or the ground
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • It emphasizes the connection between various elements of nature: the atmosphere, the oceans, the winds, and the earth.
    • The repetition of "pra" signifies a call to these elements to come forth and provide their blessings.
    • It suggests a holistic view of nature, where all elements are interconnected and contribute to the sustenance of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain (Indra) to nourish their crops, recognizing the importance of weather in their livelihood.
    • Environmentalists advocate for the protection of oceans and rivers, understanding their vital role in sustaining ecosystems.
    • Wind energy is harnessed as a clean source of power, reflecting the significance of the wind in modern energy solutions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nature and its elements in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of our dependence on natural forces and the need to respect and protect them.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges, the teachings of this mantra encourage sustainable practices and a harmonious relationship with nature.
