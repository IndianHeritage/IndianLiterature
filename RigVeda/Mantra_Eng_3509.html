अश्व्यस्य तमनā रथ्यस्य पुष्टेर नित्यस्य रायः पतयः स्याम | तā चक्राणā ऊतिभिर नव्यसीभिर अस्मत्रा रायो नियुतः साचन्ताम् ||
aśvyasya tmanā rathyasya puṣṭer nityasya rāyaḥ patayaḥ syāma | tā cakrāṇā ūtibhir navyasībhir asmatrā rāyo niyutaḥ sacantām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvyasya: of the horse
    • tmanā: with the self
    • rathyasya: of the chariot
    • puṣṭer: of nourishment or prosperity
    • nityasya: of the eternal
    • rāyaḥ: wealth or riches
    • patayaḥ: may we become or may we be
    • syāma: we may be
    • tā: those
    • cakrāṇā: of the wheels
    • ūtibhir: with strength or vigor
    • navyasībhir: with newness or freshness
    • asmatrā: from our side
    • rāyo: of wealth
    • niyutaḥ: in abundance
    • sacantām: may we attain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of prosperity and abundance, symbolized by horses and chariots, which are traditional symbols of strength and wealth in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "aśvyasya tmanā" suggests a connection with the essence of the horse, which can be interpreted as a call for vitality and energy in our endeavors.
    • "Rāyaḥ patayaḥ syāma" indicates a desire to attain wealth and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of striving for success in life.
    • The mention of "cakrāṇā ūtibhir" highlights the significance of movement and progress, akin to the wheels of a chariot that propel us forward.
    • Overall, the mantra encourages us to seek nourishment and strength in our pursuits, ensuring that we remain dynamic and adaptable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of innovation and growth. Just as a chariot moves forward with strong wheels, a business must adapt and evolve to thrive in a competitive environment.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek continuous self-improvement and to embrace new opportunities, much like the freshness implied by "navyasībhir."
    • In community settings, it can serve as a reminder to work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and shared prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity, strength, and progress in various aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we must actively seek nourishment and wealth, both materially and spiritually, while remaining adaptable and open to new experiences.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to work towards collective growth and success, ensuring that we harness our inner strength and resources effectively.
