तवद् हि पुत्र सहसो वि पूर्वीर देवस्य यन्त्य ऊतयो वि वाजाः |त्वं देहि सहस्रिणं रयिं नो 'द्रोगेण वचसा सत्यं अग्ने ||
tvad dhi putra sahaso vi pūrvīr devasya yanty ūtayo vi vājāḥ | tvaṁ dehi sahasriṇaṁ rayiṁ no 'drogheṇa vacasā satyam agne ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvad: Your
    • dhi: Indeed
    • putra: Sons/Children
    • sahaso: With strength
    • vi: In various ways
    • pūrvīr: Of the ancient ones
    • devasya: Of the divine
    • yanty: Go towards
    • ūtayo: Prosperity
    • vi: In various ways
    • vājāḥ: Abundance
    • tvaṁ: You
    • dehi: Grant
    • sahasriṇaṁ: A thousandfold
    • rayiṁ: Wealth
    • no: To us
    • 'drogheṇa: Without deceit
    • vacasā: By speech
    • satyam: Truth
    • agne: O Fire (Agni)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The speaker is asking for strength and prosperity, invoking the blessings of Agni to grant them wealth and abundance.
    • The reference to "sons" signifies the importance of family and lineage, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just for the individual but for the entire family unit.
    • The phrase "without deceit" emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's pursuits, suggesting that true prosperity comes from righteous actions and truthful speech.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success in ventures. Entrepreneurs often pray for guidance and integrity in their dealings, ensuring that their business practices are honest.
    • In family life, parents may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for their children, hoping for their success and well-being in life.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to seek divine blessings for prosperity and harmony in the new phase of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings for strength, prosperity, and integrity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses family, honesty, and righteous living.
    • In contemporary life, the principles embedded in this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and professional endeavors, fostering a sense of responsibility and ethical conduct.
