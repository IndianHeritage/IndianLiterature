ते ते देवाय दाशतः स्याम महो नो रत्ना विडध इयानः ||
te te devāya dāśataḥ syāma maho no ratnā vi dadha iyānaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • te: You (plural) - addressing the divine beings.
    • devāya: To the gods - indicating reverence and devotion.
    • dāśataḥ: From the offerings - referring to the gifts or sacrifices made to the deities.
    • syāma: May we become - a wish or prayer for transformation or attainment.
    • maho: Great - signifying the importance or magnitude of the request.
    • no: Our - indicating a collective identity or community.
    • ratnā: Jewels or treasures - symbolizing valuable blessings or virtues.
    • vi: In various ways - suggesting diversity in the blessings sought.
    • dadhā: May we receive - a hopeful expression for receiving the blessings.
    • iyānaḥ: Moving forward - indicating progress or advancement.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards the divine, expressing a desire for blessings and treasures that can lead to greatness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the relationship between humans and the divine, suggesting that through devotion and sacrifice, one can attain spiritual and material wealth.
    • The collective "no" signifies that this prayer is not just for the individual but for the community, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during festivals or rituals where people come together to seek blessings for prosperity and harmony.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on this mantra when making significant life decisions, such as starting a new business or embarking on a new journey, seeking divine guidance and support.
    • In educational settings, students may invoke this mantra before exams or important presentations, wishing for the wisdom and knowledge to succeed.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the recognition of the importance of community and offerings in spiritual practice.
    • It serves as a reminder that through devotion and collective effort, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve greatness and receive the treasures of life.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to seek support from the divine while fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose within their communities.
