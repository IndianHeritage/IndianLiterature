यो वां यज्ञेभिर आवृतो 'धिवस्त्रā वधूर इव |सपर्यन्ता शुभे चक्राते अश्विनā ||
yo vāṁ yajñebhir āvṛto 'dhivastrā vadhūr iva | saparyantā śubhe cakrāte aśvinā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vāṁ: you
    • yajñebhir: by the sacrifices
    • āvṛto: surrounded
    • 'dhivastrā: like a bride
    • vadhūr: a woman
    • iva: like
    • saparyantā: with reverence
    • śubhe: auspicious
    • cakrāte: is made to shine
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin deities)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is surrounded by the offerings made in sacrifices.
    • The imagery of a bride suggests purity, beauty, and auspiciousness, indicating that the divine is adorned and revered.
    • The mention of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent nature, implies that this divine presence brings blessings and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, weddings are celebrated with great reverence, where the bride is adorned and surrounded by loved ones, symbolizing the importance of community and support.
    • In a corporate setting, when a new project is launched, it is often celebrated with a ceremony that honors the efforts of the team, akin to a sacrifice, where the success is surrounded by good wishes and blessings.
    • In personal life, when someone achieves a significant milestone, friends and family often gather to celebrate, creating an atmosphere of reverence and joy, similar to the auspiciousness described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of reverence and community in achieving auspiciousness and blessings in life.
    • It teaches us that just as a bride is celebrated and adorned, we too should honor and celebrate the divine presence in our lives through our actions and intentions.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal achievements, community gatherings, or corporate celebrations, the essence of this mantra can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude, reverence, and togetherness.
