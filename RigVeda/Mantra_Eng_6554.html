आ निरेकम् उता प्रियं इन्द्र दर्षि जनानाम् |धृतता धृत्ष्णो स्तवमान आ भर ||
ā nirekam uta priyam indra darṣi janānām | dhṛṣatā dhṛṣṇo stavamāna ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from, out of
    • nirekam: the one who is not hidden, the visible
    • uta: and, also
    • priyam: dear, beloved
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • darṣi: seeing, perceiving
    • janānām: of the people, of the beings
    • dhṛṣatā: with courage, with strength
    • dhṛṣṇo: of the brave, of the strong
    • stavamāna: praising, worshipping
    • ā: towards, to
    • bhara: bring, bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence of Indra, who is both visible and beloved by the people.
    • It emphasizes the importance of courage and strength in the act of worship and praise.
    • The invocation of Indra suggests a call for divine support and blessings in the lives of individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, people often turn to their faith or spiritual beliefs for strength, much like the invocation of Indra for courage.
    • Communities come together to celebrate festivals, praising deities, which fosters unity and strength among individuals.
    • In leadership, the qualities of courage and the ability to inspire others are essential, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and invoking divine support in our lives.
    • It teaches us that through courage and collective praise, we can seek blessings and strength to overcome challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles or community gatherings, the essence of this mantra can guide individuals towards resilience and unity.
