मन्त्र: उतो nv अस्य जोषम् आम् इन्द्रः सुतस्य गोमतः | प्रातः होतेव मत्सति ||
uto nv asya joṣam ām̐ indraḥ sutasya gomataḥ | prātar hoteva matsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uto: Now
    • nv: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • joṣam: the delight or enjoyment
    • ām̐: O
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • sutasya: of the one who is born (or produced)
    • gomataḥ: of the cow (or the one who is rich in resources)
    • prātar: in the morning
    • hoteva: like a priest or one who invokes
    • matsati: may he be pleased or may he be satisfied
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "uto nv asya joṣam" suggests a call for the enjoyment or delight that comes from the blessings of Indra.
    • By referring to "sutasya gomataḥ," it emphasizes the importance of resources, particularly the cow, which symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • The invocation "prātar hoteva matsati" indicates a desire for Indra's blessings to be present in the morning, a time associated with new beginnings and opportunities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the cow is often seen as a vital resource for milk, labor, and as a source of wealth. This mantra can be recited to seek blessings for prosperity and abundance in farming.
    • In modern contexts, individuals may recite this mantra during morning rituals or prayers to invoke positivity and strength for the day ahead, similar to how one might seek motivation or inspiration from a mentor or leader.
    • Communities may gather to chant this mantra during festivals or ceremonies to honor nature and seek blessings for a fruitful harvest, reflecting the interconnectedness of spirituality and daily life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, strength, and nourishment.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of resources, both material and spiritual, in achieving a fulfilling life.
    • In contemporary life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community well-being, and environmental stewardship, emphasizing the need for gratitude and respect towards nature and its resources.
