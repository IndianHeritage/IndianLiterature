मन्त्र: अत्राह तद वहेते मध्व आहुतिं यम् अश्वत्थम् उपतिष्ठन्त जायतवोऽस्मे ते सन्तु जायतवः |साकं गावाḥ सुवते पच्यते यवो न ते वाय उप दस्यन्ति धेनवो नाप दस्यन्ति धेनवः ||
atrāha tad vahethe madhva āhutiṁ yam aśvattham upatiṣṭhanta jāyavo 'sme te santu jāyavaḥ | sākaṁ gāvaḥ suvate pacyate yavo na te vāya upa dasyanti dhenavo nāpa dasyanti dhenavaḥ ||
  • atrāha tad vahethe: This phrase can be interpreted as "Here, that which is offered." It signifies the importance of offerings in rituals and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • madhva āhutiṁ: "Sweet offering" refers to the offerings made to deities, often symbolizing the sweetness of life and the joy of sharing. In real life, this can be likened to acts of kindness and generosity towards others.
  • yam aśvattham upatiṣṭhanta: This translates to "that which is established like the sacred fig tree." The aśvattha tree is a symbol of eternal life and wisdom. In everyday life, it can represent the importance of grounding oneself in knowledge and tradition.
  • jāyavo 'sme te santu jāyavaḥ: "May those who are born be blessed." This line emphasizes the significance of progeny and the continuity of life. It reflects the universal desire for prosperity and well-being for future generations.
  • sākaṁ gāvaḥ suvate: "Together, the cows are nourished." Cows are revered in many cultures, symbolizing abundance and sustenance. This can be related to the idea of community support and collective growth in society.
  • pacyate yavo: "The grains are cooked." This signifies the process of transformation and preparation, akin to how hard work leads to fruitful outcomes in life.
  • na te vāya upa dasyanti: "The winds do not harm you." This line conveys a sense of protection and safety, suggesting that with the right practices and offerings, one can shield themselves from negative influences.
  • dhenavo nāpa dasyanti dhenavaḥ: "The cows do not suffer." This reinforces the idea of care and protection for all living beings, highlighting the importance of compassion in our interactions with others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of offerings, protection, community, and the continuity of life. Its significance lies in the reminder of our responsibilities towards each other and the environment. In real life, it encourages us to engage in acts of kindness, nurture our relationships, and protect those who are vulnerable. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society, ensuring that future generations thrive in a nurturing environment.
