अहं ता विश्वा चकरं नकिर्मा दैव्यं सहो वरते अप्रतीतम् |यन् मा सोमासो ममदन यदुक्तोभे भयते रजसी अपारे ||
ahaṁ tā viśvā cakaraṁ nakir mā daivyaṁ saho varate apratītam | yan mā somāso mamadan yad ukthobhe bhayete rajasī apāre ||
  • ahaṁ: This word translates to "I" or "I am," indicating a sense of self or identity.
  • tā: This means "that" or "those," referring to something that is being acknowledged or recognized.
  • viśvā: This word means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
  • cakaraṁ: This translates to "I have made" or "I have created," indicating an act of creation or manifestation.
  • nakir: This means "not" or "no," negating what follows.
  • mā: This translates to "do not" or "may not," serving as a caution or a request.
  • daivyaṁ: This means "divine" or "heavenly," referring to something that is sacred or of a higher nature.
  • saho: This translates to "with strength" or "with power," indicating resilience or fortitude.
  • varate: This means "to bear" or "to carry," suggesting endurance or the ability to withstand challenges.
  • apratītam: This translates to "unseen" or "unknown," referring to aspects of life that are not immediately apparent or understood.
  • yan: This means "that which" or "what," introducing a clause that elaborates on a condition or situation.
  • mā: Again, this serves as a caution or request, reinforcing the previous negation.
  • somāso: This translates to "of the soma," referring to the sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
  • mamadan: This means "my joy" or "my delight," indicating personal happiness or fulfillment.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," linking the previous ideas to what follows.
  • ukthobhe: This translates to "in the utterances" or "in the words spoken," referring to the power of speech and expression.
  • bhayete: This means "to be afraid" or "to fear," indicating a sense of apprehension or concern.
  • rajasī: This translates to "of the rajas," referring to one of the three gunas (qualities) in Vedic philosophy, associated with activity and passion.
  • apāre: This means "infinite" or "boundless," suggesting something that transcends limits or boundaries.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of existence, creation, and the divine.
