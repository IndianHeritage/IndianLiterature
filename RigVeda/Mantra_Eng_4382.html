त्वं हि क्षैतवद् यशोऽग्ने मित्रो न पत्यसे |त्वं विचर्षणे श्रवो वसो पुष्टिं न पुष्यसि ||
tvaṁ hi kṣaitavad yaśo 'gne mitro na patyase | tvaṁ vicarṣaṇe śravo vaso puṣṭiṁ na puṣyasi ||
  • tvaṁ hi kṣaitavad yaśo 'gne mitro na patyase
    • tvaṁ hi - "You indeed" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of the subject, indicating that the focus is on the divine or the cosmic force being addressed.
    • kṣaitavad - "like the earth" - This word draws a parallel between the divine and the stability and nurturing qualities of the earth.
    • yaśo - "fame" or "glory" - This refers to the reputation or honor associated with the divine, suggesting that it is revered and respected.
    • 'gne - "O Agni" - Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine presence in rituals.
    • mitro - "friend" - This term indicates a relationship of trust and companionship, suggesting that the divine is a supportive ally.
    • na patyase - "you do not abandon" - This phrase conveys the idea of steadfastness and reliability of the divine presence.
  • tvaṁ vicarṣaṇe śravo vaso puṣṭiṁ na puṣyasi
    • tvaṁ - "You" - Again, the focus is on the divine entity being addressed.
    • vicarṣaṇe - "in the vastness" - This suggests the expansive nature of the divine, encompassing all of existence.
    • śravo - "glory" or "renown" - This reinforces the idea of the divine's esteemed status in the universe.
    • vaso - "of the abode" - This indicates a connection to a place of safety and comfort, suggesting that the divine provides shelter.
    • puṣṭiṁ - "nourishment" - This word signifies sustenance, both physical and spiritual, provided by the divine.
    • na puṣyasi - "you do not flourish" - This phrase implies that without the divine's influence, growth and prosperity are hindered.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine (Agni) and humanity, portraying the divine as a nurturing and steadfast presence that offers support, nourishment, and companionship. In real life, this can be reflected in the way individuals seek guidance and strength from spiritual beliefs or practices. For instance, during challenging times, people often turn to prayer or meditation, invoking the divine for support and stability, much like how the earth provides sustenance and shelter. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine, which can lead to personal growth, resilience, and a sense of community. It encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in their lives and to seek nourishment—both physical and spiritual—through this connection.
