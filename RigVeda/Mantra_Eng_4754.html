अविदद् दाक्षम् मित्रो नवीयान् पपानो देवभ्यः वस्यो अचैत् |ससवान् स्तौलाभिर् धौतरीभिर् उरुष्याऽ पायुर् अभवत् सकिभ्यः ||
avidad dakṣam mitro navīyān papāno devebhyo vasyo acait | sasavān staulābhir dhautarībhir uruṣyā pāyur abhavat sakhibhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avidad: to know or to understand
    • dakṣam: skilled or capable
    • mitro: friend or ally
    • navīyān: new or fresh
    • papāno: to be born or to arise
    • devebhyo: from the gods
    • vasyo: under control or governed
    • acait: not aware or not conscious
    • sasavān: possessing or having
    • staulābhir: with abundance or plenty
    • dhautarībhir: with purifying or cleansing
    • uruṣyā: with strength or power
    • pāyur: protector or guardian
    • abhavat: became or was
    • sakhibhyaḥ: to friends or companions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of understanding and skill in forming alliances and friendships.
    • The phrase "dakṣam mitro" emphasizes the value of having capable friends who can support and uplift one another.
    • The term "navīyān" suggests the idea of renewal and freshness in relationships, indicating that new connections can bring vitality.
    • It also highlights the divine aspect of friendships, as it mentions "devebhyo," suggesting that these relationships are blessed or guided by higher powers.
    • The mantra further implies that true friendship involves mutual support and protection, as indicated by "pāyur abhavat sakhibhyaḥ."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, having skilled colleagues (dakṣam mitro) can lead to successful projects and innovations.
    • Friendships that are renewed over time (navīyān) can provide emotional support during challenging life events.
    • Communities that come together to support one another (devebhyo vasyo) often thrive and create a sense of belonging.
    • In personal relationships, being aware of each other's strengths and weaknesses (acait) can lead to deeper connections and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of skilled and supportive friendships in our lives.
    • It teaches us that relationships, when nurtured and understood, can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance our social interactions, foster collaboration, and create a supportive network that benefits everyone involved.
