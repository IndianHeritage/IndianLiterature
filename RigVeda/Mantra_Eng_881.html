मन्त्र: निर इन्द्र भूम्या अधि वृत्रं जगन्त नीर दिवः | सृजा मरुत्वतीर अव जīvधन्या इमा अपो 'र्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
nir indra bhūmyā adhi vṛtraṁ jaghantha nir divaḥ | sṛjā marutvatīr ava jīvadhanyā imā apo 'rcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir: without
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • bhūmyā: on the earth
    • adhi: over
    • vṛtraṁ: Vritra, the serpent or dragon
    • jaghantha: who is defeated
    • nir: without
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • sṛjā: create
    • marutvatīr: like the Maruts, the storm deities
    • ava: down
    • jīvadhanyā: life-giving
    • imā: these
    • apo: waters
    • 'rcann: have been invoked
    • anu: after
    • svarājyam: sovereignty or rule
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of the divine to overcome obstacles and challenges, represented by Vritra, who is often seen as a symbol of drought or obstruction.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the earth and the heavens working together to create life and abundance.
    • The invocation of the waters signifies the essential role of nature in sustaining life, highlighting the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain to overcome drought conditions, similar to invoking the divine to defeat Vritra.
    • Environmental movements advocate for the protection of water resources, recognizing their vital role in sustaining life, akin to the life-giving waters mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek strength and support from their community or spiritual beliefs to overcome obstacles, reflecting the mantra's theme of divine assistance in overcoming difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the triumph of life and abundance over obstacles and challenges, urging us to recognize the importance of nature and divine support in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to personal struggles, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all life and the need for harmony between the earth and the heavens.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek strength and support in our endeavors, fostering a sense of unity with the natural world and the divine.
