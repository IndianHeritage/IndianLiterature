ऋतं येमाना ऋतमिद्वनोत्य ऋतस्य शुष्मस्तुरया उ गव्युः |ऋताय पृथ्वी बहुले गभीरें ऋताय धेनू परमेदुहाते ||
ṛtaṁ yemāna ṛtam id vanoty ṛtasya śuṣmas turayā u gavyuḥ | ṛtāya pṛthvī bahule gabhīre ṛtāya dhenū parame duhāte ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛtaṁ: Truth, order, or cosmic law.
    • yemāna: Those who follow or adhere to.
    • ṛtam: The same as above, emphasizing the concept of truth.
    • id: This.
    • vanoty: Brings forth or manifests.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or order.
    • śuṣmas: The finest or most subtle.
    • turayā: By the means of strength or power.
    • u: And.
    • gavyuḥ: Cows or wealth.
    • ṛtāya: For the sake of truth.
    • pṛthvī: Earth.
    • bahule: Abundant or plentiful.
    • gabhīre: Deep or profound.
    • dhenū: Cows, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • parame: Supreme or ultimate.
    • duhāte: Gives or provides milk.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of adhering to truth and cosmic order (ṛta) in life.
    • It suggests that those who follow the path of truth will manifest abundance and prosperity.
    • The reference to cows (dhenū) symbolizes nourishment, wealth, and the sustenance that comes from living in harmony with truth.
    • The earth (pṛthvī) is depicted as a source of deep and abundant resources when aligned with the principles of truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, companies that operate with transparency and integrity often see greater success and loyalty from customers.
    • In personal relationships, honesty and adherence to one's values lead to deeper connections and trust.
    • Environmental sustainability can be seen as a reflection of living in accordance with the natural order (ṛta), where respecting the earth leads to abundant resources for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to live a life rooted in truth and integrity.
    • It teaches that by aligning ourselves with cosmic order, we can access deeper resources and abundance in our lives.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal conduct, business ethics, and environmental stewardship, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
