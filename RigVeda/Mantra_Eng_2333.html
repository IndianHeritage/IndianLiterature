क्व स्य ते रुद्र मृळयाकुर हस्तो यो अस्ति भेषजो जलाशः | अपभर्ता रपसो दैव्यस्याभी नु मा वृषभ चक्षमीथाः ||
kva1 sya te rudra mṛḻayākur hasto yo asti bheṣajo jalāṣaḥ | apabhartā rapaso daivyasyābhī nu mā vṛṣabha cakṣamīthāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kva1 sya te rudra mṛḻayākur hasto yo asti bheṣajo jalāṣaḥ | apabhartā rapaso daivyasyābhī nu mā vṛṣabha cakṣamīthāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra is a prayer to Rudra, the deity associated with storms and healing. It seeks the removal of obstacles and the granting of health and well-being.
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kva1 sya te: Where is your presence?
    • rudra: O Rudra, the fierce and powerful deity.
    • mṛḻayākur: May you be appeased or calmed.
    • hasto: With your hand.
    • yo: Who.
    • asti: Is.
    • bheṣajo: The healer or remedy.
    • jalāṣaḥ: In the waters or the ocean.
    • apabhartā: The one who removes or dispels.
    • rapaso: Of the obstacles or troubles.
    • daivyasyābhī: Divine or celestial.
    • nu: Indeed or surely.
    • mā: Do not.
    • vṛṣabha: O bull, a symbol of strength.
    • cakṣamīthāḥ: May you grant vision or insight.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine intervention for healing and the removal of obstacles in life. It reflects the belief in the power of deities to influence health and well-being.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In times of illness, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke healing energies and seek relief from suffering.
    • During challenging situations, such as personal or professional obstacles, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to create a positive atmosphere and invite blessings for health and prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human experience. By invoking Rudra, individuals can find solace and strength in their struggles, fostering a sense of hope and resilience in the face of adversity.
