अनुकामं तर्पयेताम् इन्द्रावरुण राया आ |तां वां नेदिष्टम् ईमहे ||
anukāmaṁ tarpayethām indrāvaruṇa rāya ā | tā vāṁ nediṣṭham īmahe ||
  • Mantra: "anukāmaṁ tarpayethām indrāvaruṇa rāya ā | tā vāṁ nediṣṭham īmahe ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May Indra and Varuna, the rulers of the heavens, satisfy our desires; we invoke you for the best of what you can offer."
  • Explanation:
    • Indra and Varuna: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna is the god of water and the celestial ocean. They symbolize the forces of nature that are essential for life.
    • Anukāmaṁ: This term refers to desires or wishes. It signifies the human inclination towards seeking fulfillment and satisfaction in life.
    • Tarpayethām: This word means to satisfy or to please. It indicates the act of fulfilling desires or needs.
    • Nediṣṭham: This term refers to the best or the most desirable. It emphasizes the quality of what is being sought after.
    • Invocation: The mantra is essentially an invocation, a call to the divine forces to provide for human needs and desires. It reflects a deep understanding of the interdependence between humans and nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to Indra for rain, as it is crucial for their crops. This mantra can be recited to seek the blessings of Indra for a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of need, such as seeking guidance in career choices or personal relationships, invoking the divine for the best outcomes.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to foster unity and collective well-being, asking for the satisfaction of the community's desires and needs.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine intervention in fulfilling human desires. It teaches us to recognize our needs and the importance of nature's role in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious relationship with the environment, reminding us that our well-being is interconnected with the natural world.
    • By invoking Indra and Varuna, we acknowledge the forces that sustain us and express our gratitude, which is essential for a balanced and fulfilling life.
