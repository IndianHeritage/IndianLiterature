हरिं हि योनिṁ अभि ये समस्वरन् हिन्वन्तो हरī दिव्यं यथा सदाḥ |आ यम् पृणन्ति हरिभिर न धेनव इन्द्राय शूषं हरिवन्तम् अर्चत ||
hariṁ hi yonim abhi ye samasvaran hinvanto harī divyaṁ yathā sadaḥ | ā yam pṛṇanti haribhir na dhenava indrāya śūṣaṁ harivantam arcata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hariṁ: Refers to the divine or the supreme being.
    • hi: Indeed or certainly.
    • yonim: The source or womb of creation.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • ye: Those who.
    • samasvaran: Harmoniously singing or resonating.
    • hinvanto: Invoking or calling forth.
    • harī: The divine or the auspicious.
    • divyaṁ: Divine or celestial.
    • yathā: As or like.
    • sadaḥ: Always or eternally.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • yam: Whom or which.
    • pṛṇanti: Fulfill or nourish.
    • haribhir: By the divine beings.
    • na: Not.
    • dhenava: Cows or nurturing beings.
    • indrāya: To Indra, the king of gods.
    • śūṣaṁ: Dryness or lack.
    • harivantam: The one who possesses divinity.
    • arcata: Worship or offer reverence.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine source of creation and the harmonious invocation of the divine beings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and fulfilling the needs of the divine, akin to how a mother nurtures her child.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the significance of divine leadership and the need for sustenance in both the physical and spiritual realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when people come together to support each other, they are invoking the spirit of harmony and nurturing, similar to the divine beings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal life, when individuals engage in acts of kindness and generosity, they are fulfilling the essence of this mantra by nourishing the divine within themselves and others.
    • In environmental conservation, caring for nature can be seen as a way of worshipping the divine, as it sustains life and promotes harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing the divine essence in ourselves and the world around us.
    • It teaches us that by invoking harmony and fulfilling the needs of the divine, we can overcome challenges and foster a sense of community and support.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, environmental stewardship, and personal relationships, reminding us to act with compassion and reverence towards all forms of life.
