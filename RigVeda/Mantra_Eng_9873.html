मन्त्र: सुते अध्वरे अधि वाचं अकृताः क्रीलयोन मातरं तुदन्तः |विषू मुचां सुशुवुषो मनिषां वि वर्तन्ताम् अद्रयश्चायमानाः ||
sute adhvare adhi vācam akratā krīḻayo na mātaraṁ tudantaḥ | vi ṣū muñcā suṣuvuṣo manīṣāṁ vi vartantām adrayaś cāyamānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sute: In the context of offerings or sacrifices.
    • adhvare: Referring to the ritual or the act of worship.
    • adhi: Above or in addition to.
    • vācam: Speech or words.
    • akratā: Not performed or unfulfilled.
    • krīḻayo: Those who are engaged in the act of worship.
    • na: Not.
    • mātaraṁ: Mother or nurturing force.
    • tudantaḥ: Those who are causing disturbance or hindrance.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'to' or 'with'.
    • ṣū: A term often associated with the divine or auspiciousness.
    • muñcā: Release or liberate.
    • suṣuvuṣo: Those who are well-versed or knowledgeable.
    • manīṣāṁ: Wisdom or intellect.
    • vi: Again, 'to' or 'with'.
    • vartantām: May they exist or may they thrive.
    • adrayaś: Those who are steadfast or firm.
    • cāyamānāḥ: Those who are present or existing.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of performing rituals and sacrifices with sincerity and intention.
    • It suggests that when one engages in worship, it should be done with the right words and thoughts, avoiding any distractions or disturbances.
    • The invocation of wisdom and knowledge is crucial, as it guides the worshippers in their spiritual journey.
    • It also highlights the need for a nurturing environment, free from negativity, to foster spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when teams come together for a project, they must communicate effectively and work harmoniously, much like the mantra suggests for a successful ritual.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing communication and understanding can lead to a more profound connection, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In education, fostering an environment where knowledge is shared freely and distractions are minimized can enhance learning, akin to the wisdom invoked in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for intentionality in actions, especially in spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that rituals should be performed with clarity of thought and purpose, ensuring that the environment is conducive to spiritual growth.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork, and communication, emphasizing the importance of a positive and nurturing atmosphere for success.
