प्रातर अग्निम प्रातर इन्द्रं हवामहे प्रातर मित्रावरुणा प्रातर अश्विनाः |प्रातर भागं पूषणं ब्रह्मणस पतिम प्रातः सोमं उता रुद्रं हुवेम ||
prātar agnim prātar indraṁ havāmahe prātar mitrāvaruṇā prātar aśvinā | prātar bhagam pūṣaṇam brahmaṇas patim prātaḥ somam uta rudraṁ huvema ||
  • prātar agnim: This phrase translates to "in the morning, we invoke Agni." Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to starting the day with a positive mindset, igniting one's inner fire to pursue goals.
  • prātar indraṁ havāmahe: Here, "we invoke Indra in the morning." Indra is the king of gods and symbolizes strength and power. This can be related to seeking strength and courage to face daily challenges, much like a student preparing for an important exam or an athlete gearing up for a competition.
  • prātar mitrāvaruṇā: This means "in the morning, we call upon Mitra and Varuna." Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna embodies cosmic order and truth. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining good relationships and upholding integrity in our actions, such as in teamwork or community service.
  • prātar aśvinā: "in the morning, we invoke the Aśvins." The Aśvins are twin gods associated with health and medicine. This can be interpreted as a reminder to prioritize health and well-being, perhaps through morning exercise or a nutritious breakfast.
  • prātar bhagam pūṣaṇam: This translates to "in the morning, we invoke Bhaga and Pūṣaṇa." Bhaga represents prosperity and wealth, while Pūṣaṇa is associated with nourishment and growth. This can be related to setting intentions for abundance and personal growth at the start of the day, such as planning financial goals or personal development activities.
  • brahmaṇas patim: This means "the lord of creation." It signifies the importance of creativity and innovation in our lives. In a practical sense, this could be about engaging in creative projects or problem-solving in our work or personal lives.
  • prātaḥ somam uta rudraṁ huvema: "in the morning, we invoke Soma and Rudra." Soma represents the essence of life and vitality, while Rudra symbolizes transformation and healing. This can be seen in the context of seeking rejuvenation and healing, perhaps through meditation or self-care practices.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of starting the day with intention and reverence for various aspects of life, including energy, strength, relationships, health, prosperity, creativity, and healing. By invoking these divine qualities each morning, individuals can cultivate a positive mindset and align themselves with their goals and values. In real life, this practice can be applied through morning rituals, setting daily intentions, and fostering a holistic approach to well-being and personal growth.
