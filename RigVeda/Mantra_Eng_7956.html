मन्त्र: इन्दो समुद्रमीन्खय पवस्व विश्वमेजय | रायो धार्ता न ओजसा ||
indo samudramīṅkhaya pavasva viśvamejaya | rāyo dhartā na ojasā ||
  • Indo: This term refers to the divine or celestial, often associated with the god Indra, who is a symbol of strength and power.
  • Samudram: This word means 'ocean' or 'sea,' representing vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • Mīṅkhaya: This can be interpreted as 'to flow' or 'to move,' suggesting the dynamic nature of life and energy.
  • Pavasva: This term means 'to blow' or 'to inspire,' indicating the act of bringing forth energy or vitality.
  • Viśvamejaya: This word translates to 'conquering all' or 'victorious over the universe,' symbolizing ultimate success and achievement.
  • Rāyo: This refers to 'wealth' or 'prosperity,' highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • Dhartā: This means 'the bearer' or 'the supporter,' indicating a nurturing force that sustains life.
  • Na: This is a negation, meaning 'not,' which emphasizes the importance of the following term.
  • Ojasā: This word means 'with strength' or 'with energy,' suggesting the power that comes from vitality and vigor.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for divine energy and prosperity. It calls upon the celestial forces to flow like the ocean, inspiring strength and vitality, and ultimately leading to success and abundance in life. The emphasis on 'not' in relation to strength suggests that without this divine support, true prosperity cannot be achieved.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra to seek inspiration and strength in their personal endeavors, whether it be in career, education, or personal development.
  • Business Success: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke prosperity and success in their ventures, aligning their efforts with the divine flow of abundance.
  • Community Well-being: Communities can come together to chant this mantra, fostering a sense of unity and collective strength, which can lead to shared prosperity and support.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine forces of nature, encouraging them to harness strength and vitality for achieving success and prosperity. It serves as a reminder that true abundance is supported by a nurturing force, and that one must align themselves with this energy to thrive in life.
