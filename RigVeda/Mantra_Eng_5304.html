यस्तिग्मशृङ्गो वृषभो न भीमा एकः कृष्ण्तिश्च्यावयति प्र विश्वाः |यः शाश्वतो अदाशुषो गयस्य प्रयन्तासि सुष्वितराय वेदः ||
yas tigmaśṛṅgo vṛṣabho na bhīma ekaḥ kṛṣṭīś cyāvayati pra viśvāḥ | yaḥ śaśvato adāśuṣo gayasya prayantāsi suṣvitarāya vedaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tigmaśṛṅgo: with sharp horns
    • vṛṣabho: the bull
    • na: not
    • bhīma: fearful
    • ekaḥ: alone
    • kṛṣṭīś: the one who cultivates
    • cyāvayati: drives away
    • pra: forth
    • viśvāḥ: all
    • yaḥ: who
    • śaśvato: eternal
    • adāśuṣo: of the unshakable
    • gayasya: of the cow
    • prayantāsi: you approach
    • suṣvitarāya: for the good of all
    • vedaḥ: knowledge
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity, symbolized by a bull with sharp horns, who is not fearful and drives away negativity.
    • The bull represents strength and fertility, often associated with agricultural prosperity.
    • The phrase "who cultivates" suggests the importance of nurturing and caring for the land and its resources.
    • The eternal aspect signifies the timeless nature of knowledge and wisdom that guides humanity.
    • The mention of the cow indicates nourishment and sustenance, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers embody the spirit of the bull by working tirelessly to cultivate their fields, ensuring food security for their communities.
    • In personal development, individuals can take inspiration from the mantra to face challenges without fear, driving away negativity and focusing on growth.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra encourages a respectful relationship with nature, promoting sustainable practices that benefit all living beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, courage, and nurturing in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach challenges with confidence and to cultivate knowledge for the betterment of society.
    • In a world facing numerous challenges, the teachings of this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards resilience and harmony.
