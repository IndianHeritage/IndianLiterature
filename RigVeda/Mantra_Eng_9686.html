सुकिंशुकं शाल्मलिं विश्वरूपं हिरण्यवर्णं सुवृतं सुचक्रम् |आ रोह सूर्ये अमृतस्य लोकं स्योनं पत्ये वहतुं कृणुष्व ||
sukiṁśukaṁ śalmaliṁ viśvarūpaṁ hiraṇyavarṇaṁ suvṛtaṁ sucakram | ā roha sūrye amṛtasya lokaṁ syonam patye vahatuṁ kṛṇuṣva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sukiṁśukaṁ: Auspicious, beneficial
    • śalmaliṁ: Referring to the silk-cotton tree, symbolizing strength and resilience
    • viśvarūpaṁ: Universal form, representing the all-encompassing nature of existence
    • hiraṇyavarṇaṁ: Golden hue, symbolizing prosperity and wealth
    • suvṛtaṁ: Well-formed, indicating perfection and beauty
    • sucakram: Pure wheel, representing the cycle of life and the universe
    • ā roha: Ascend, rise up
    • sūrye: To the sun, symbolizing enlightenment and energy
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality, representing eternal life and truth
    • lokaṁ: World, realm of existence
    • syonam: To the lord, indicating a divine connection
    • patye: To the master, referring to a higher power or deity
    • vahatuṁ: May it carry, suggesting support and guidance
    • kṛṇuṣva: Make it happen, a call to action
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes auspiciousness and strength, symbolized by the silk-cotton tree, which is known for its resilience in nature.
    • The universal form signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing our place in the cosmos.
    • The golden hue represents prosperity, suggesting that one should strive for wealth not just in material terms but also in wisdom and virtue.
    • The pure wheel symbolizes the cyclical nature of life, reminding us of the importance of balance and harmony in our actions.
    • By calling upon the sun, the mantra emphasizes the pursuit of enlightenment and the energy needed to achieve our goals.
    • The reference to immortality speaks to the human desire for lasting impact and legacy, encouraging us to live meaningfully.
    • Finally, the plea for divine support highlights the importance of seeking guidance from a higher power in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate resilience and strength, akin to the silk-cotton tree, to overcome life's challenges.
    • In business, the pursuit of prosperity can be seen in entrepreneurs who strive for ethical practices while aiming for financial success.
    • In community service, the idea of interconnectedness can inspire collective efforts to address social issues, reflecting the universal form.
    • In spiritual practices, many people meditate on the sun as a source of energy and enlightenment, aligning with the mantra's call to rise towards it.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities we should aspire to embody: resilience, interconnectedness, prosperity, and enlightenment.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek a higher purpose and to act with intention in their lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide personal growth, ethical business practices, community engagement, and spiritual development.
