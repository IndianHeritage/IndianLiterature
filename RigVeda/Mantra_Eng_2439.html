अवर्धयन शुभगं सप्त यह्वीः श्वेतं जज्ञाणम् अरुषम् महित्वा |शिशुं न जातम् अभ्य आरुर अश्वा देवासो अग्निं जनिमन् वपुष्यन् ||
avardhayan subhagaṁ sapta yahvīḥ śvetaṁ jajñānam aruṣam mahitvā | śiśuṁ na jātam abhy ārur aśvā devāso agniṁ janiman vapuṣyan ||
  • avardhayan: This term can be understood as "to increase" or "to promote." It signifies the act of fostering growth or development.
  • subhagaṁ: This word translates to "auspicious" or "fortunate." It refers to something that brings good fortune or is blessed.
  • sapta: Meaning "seven," this number often symbolizes completeness or perfection in Vedic literature.
  • yahvīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the vital energies" or "the life forces." It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • śvetaṁ: Translated as "white," it symbolizes purity, clarity, and auspiciousness.
  • jajñānam: This word means "knowledge" or "wisdom." It signifies the understanding and awareness that one gains through experience.
  • aruṣam: This term can be interpreted as "red" or "radiant," often associated with energy, passion, and vitality.
  • mahitvā: Meaning "greatness" or "majesty," it refers to the significance and importance of something.
  • śiśuṁ: This word translates to "child" or "infant," symbolizing new beginnings, innocence, and potential.
  • na jātam: This phrase means "not born" or "not yet manifested," indicating something that is in a state of potentiality.
  • abhy ārur: This term can be understood as "to ascend" or "to rise up," suggesting progress or elevation.
  • aśvā: Meaning "horses," it often symbolizes strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • devāso: This word translates to "gods" or "divine beings," representing higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • agniṁ: This term means "fire," which is a symbol of transformation, energy, and purification.
  • janiman: This word means "to give birth" or "to create," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • vapuṣyan: This term can be interpreted as "in form" or "in body," referring to the manifestation of physical existence.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the themes of growth, auspiciousness, and the interplay of life forces. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and purity in the process of creation and manifestation. The imagery of horses and fire suggests strength and transformation, while the mention of a child symbolizes new beginnings and potential.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of increasing auspiciousness and knowledge, individuals can focus on personal development through education and self-improvement.
  • Parenting: The reference to a child highlights the importance of nurturing and guiding the next generation, ensuring they grow in a supportive and enriching environment.
  • Community Building: The idea of collective energies (the seven life forces) can be seen in community efforts where collaboration leads to greater outcomes.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of divine energies and the transformative power of fire can inspire individuals to engage in spiritual practices that promote inner growth and enlightenment.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nurturing knowledge, and the potential for transformation in both personal and communal contexts.
