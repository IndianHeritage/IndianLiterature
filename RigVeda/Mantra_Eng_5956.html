प्रति स्मरेथां तुज्यद्भिर एविर हतम् द्रुहो रक्षसो भङ्गुरावतः |इन्द्रासोमा दुष्कृतं मा सुगं भूद यो नः कदाचिद अभिदासति द्रुहा ||
prati smarethāṁ tujayadbhir evair hataṁ druho rakṣaso bhaṅgurāvataḥ | indrāsomā duṣkṛte mā sugam bhūd yo naḥ kadā cid abhidāsati druhā ||
  • Translation: "May we remember you, O Indra and Soma, who have vanquished the enemies and the malevolent spirits. May we be free from the harm of those who wish us ill, and may we never be attacked by any adversary."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Prati: Towards or in relation to.
    • Smarethāṁ: Remembering or recalling.
    • Tujayadbhir: By those who have conquered.
    • Evair: Indeed or truly.
    • Hataṁ: Defeated or vanquished.
    • Druho: Enemies or malevolent beings.
    • Rakṣaso: Spirits or demons.
    • Bhaṅgurāvataḥ: Those who are unstable or treacherous.
    • Indrāsomā: Referring to the deities Indra and Soma.
    • Duṣkṛte: For evil deeds or wrongdoings.
    • Mā: Not or may not.
    • Sugam: Easy or comfortable.
    • Bhūd: May it be or may it happen.
    • Yo: Who.
    • Naḥ: Us or our.
    • Kadā cid: At any time or ever.
    • Abhidāsati: Attacks or assails.
    • Druhā: Enmity or hostility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be invoked to seek protection from office politics and negative influences that may hinder one's career growth.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to focus on positive interactions and to shield oneself from toxic individuals who may wish to cause harm.
    • During challenging times, such as facing adversities or conflicts, individuals may recite this mantra to cultivate strength and resilience against those who oppose them.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for remembrance and protection from malevolent forces. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support in overcoming adversities and ensuring safety from those who harbor ill intentions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal, professional, and social situations, where individuals seek to maintain peace and harmony while warding off negativity. By reciting this mantra, one can foster a sense of security and empowerment, reinforcing the belief that divine forces are always present to assist in times of need.
