यं सोमं इन्द्र पृथिवीद्यावागर्भं न माताऽबिभृतस्त्वायाः |तं ते हिन्वन्ति तमु ते मृजन्त्यध्वर्यवो वृषभ पातवाः उ ||
yaṁ somam indra pṛthivīdyāvā garbhaṁ na mātā bibhṛtas tvāyā | taṁ te hinvanti tam u te mṛjanty adhvaryavo vṛṣabha pātavā u ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • somam: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • dyāvā: sky
    • garbhaṁ: womb or essence
    • na: not
    • mātā: mother
    • bibhṛtas: holds or carries
    • tvāyā: you
    • taṁ: him
    • te: your
    • hinvanti: they invoke
    • tam: him
    • u: and
    • te: your
    • mṛjanty: they purify
    • adhvaryavo: the priests
    • vṛṣabha: the strong one
    • pātavā: the powerful
    • u: and
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the god of rain and thunder, invoking his power and essence.
    • The reference to "soma" signifies the sacred drink that is believed to bestow immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The mention of "earth" and "sky" symbolizes the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "na mātā bibhṛtas tvāyā" suggests that the essence of the divine is not solely held by a mother figure, but is a universal force.
    • The invocation of priests ("adhvaryavo") indicates the importance of rituals and community in connecting with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, the use of offerings and prayers to invoke blessings can be seen as a continuation of this ancient practice.
    • In many cultures, the concept of a divine essence that connects the earth and sky can be observed in nature worship and environmental conservation efforts.
    • The idea of community rituals, such as harvest festivals, reflects the collective invocation of divine blessings for prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine powers for blessings and purification.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual worlds, urging individuals to recognize the divine essence in all aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire practices of gratitude, community bonding, and respect for nature, reminding us of our place within the larger cosmos.
