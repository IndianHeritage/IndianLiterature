न दक्षिणा वि चिकीते न सव्याऽ न प्राचीनम् आदित्याऽ नोत पश्चिम् | पाक्याऽ चिद वसवो धीर्याऽ चिद युष्मानीतो अभयं ज्योतिर अश्यां ||
na dakṣiṇā vi cikite na savyā na prācīnam ādityā nota paścā | pākyā cid vasavo dhīryā cid yuṣmānīto abhayaṁ jyotir aśyām ||
  • na dakṣiṇā vi cikite: This phrase translates to "neither the right hand nor the left hand is to be neglected." It emphasizes the importance of balance and harmony in actions. In real life, this can be seen in decision-making processes where one must consider multiple perspectives before arriving at a conclusion.
  • na savyā na prācīnam ādityā: This means "neither the left nor the ancient sun." Here, the reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge. The mantra suggests that one should not be limited by traditional or outdated beliefs. For example, in modern education, it is essential to challenge old paradigms and embrace new ideas to foster innovation.
  • nota paścā: This translates to "nor the west." This phrase can be interpreted as a call to look beyond geographical or cultural boundaries. In today's globalized world, this is particularly relevant as we learn from diverse cultures and practices, enriching our own lives and perspectives.
  • pākyā cid vasavo: This means "the pure ones, the Vasus." The Vasus are deities representing various natural elements. This part of the mantra highlights the significance of purity and the natural world. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where maintaining the purity of nature is crucial for sustainability.
  • dhīryā cid yuṣmānīto: This translates to "may you be endowed with wisdom." Wisdom is a vital quality that guides individuals in making sound decisions. In practical terms, this can be seen in leadership roles where wise decision-making is essential for the success of an organization or community.
  • abhayaṁ jyotir aśyām: This means "fearless light, not dark." It signifies the importance of courage and clarity in life. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who stand up against injustice, bringing light to dark situations and inspiring others to do the same.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, wisdom, and purity while encouraging individuals to transcend limitations and embrace enlightenment. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, including decision-making, environmental consciousness, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with clarity, courage, and a sense of responsibility towards themselves and the world around them.
