मन्त्र: मनोऽस्याऽन आसीद् द्यौर् आसीद् उता च्छदिः |शुक्राव् अनड्वाहाव् आस्तां यद् आयात् सूर्य गृहम् ||
mano asyā ana āsīd dyaur āsīd uta cchadiḥ | śukrāv anaḍvāhāv āstāṁ yad ayāt sūryā gṛham ||
  • Mano asyā ana āsīd dyaur āsīd uta cchadiḥ
    • Mano: Refers to the mind or consciousness.
    • asyā: This (referring to the mind).
    • ana āsīd: Did not exist or was not present.
    • dyaur: The sky or heaven.
    • āsīd: Was or existed.
    • uta: And.
    • cchadiḥ: The covering or veil.
  • Śukrāv anaḍvāhāv āstāṁ yad ayāt sūryā gṛham
    • Śukrāv: Bright or pure.
    • anaḍvāhāv: Without any obstruction or hindrance.
    • āstāṁ: May they be present or may they exist.
    • yad: That which.
    • ayāt: Has come or arrived.
    • sūryā: The sun.
    • gṛham: Home or abode.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of consciousness and existence. It suggests that before the mind (mano) came into being, there was a state of emptiness or non-existence (ana āsīd). The sky (dyaur) existed, but the mind was not yet present. This can be likened to the state of the universe before the emergence of life and consciousness, where everything was in a primordial state.


The second part of the mantra speaks of the brightness and purity (śukrāv) that exists without obstruction (anaḍvāhāv). It emphasizes the importance of clarity and unobstructed perception in our lives. The arrival of the sun (sūryā) signifies enlightenment, knowledge, and the illumination of our understanding, which can be seen as a metaphor for wisdom and insight that we seek in our daily lives.


Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of consciousness and the journey from non-existence to awareness. It teaches us the importance of seeking clarity and understanding in our lives, much like the sun brings light to the world. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, mindfulness practices, and the pursuit of knowledge. By recognizing the importance of a clear and unobstructed mind, we can navigate life's challenges with greater wisdom and insight.
