त्वं दिवो धरुणं धिषा ओजसा पृथिव्यां इन्द्र सदनेषु माहिनः |त्वं सुतस्य मादे अरिणा अपो वि वृत्रस्य समया पाष्यारुजः ||
tvaṁ divo dharuṇaṁ dhiṣa ojasā pṛthivyā indra sadaneṣu māhinaḥ | tvaṁ sutasya made ariṇā apo vi vṛtrasya samayā pāṣyārujaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ divo dharuṇaṁ dhiṣa ojasā: This phrase can be translated as "You, who are the strong support of the heavens." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," "divo" refers to "heaven," "dharuṇaṁ" means "support" or "strength," and "dhiṣa" signifies "with power." This line emphasizes the divine strength that upholds the universe.
  • pṛthivyā indra sadaneṣu māhinaḥ: This translates to "You, Indra, who are great in the abodes of the earth." "Pṛthivyā" means "of the earth," "indra" refers to the king of gods, and "sadaneṣu" means "in the abodes." This line highlights Indra's omnipresence and his role as a protector and provider on earth.
  • tvaṁ sutasya made ariṇā: This can be interpreted as "You, who are the source of joy and delight." "Sutasya" means "of the son" or "of the offspring," "made" refers to "joy," and "ariṇā" signifies "delight." This line reflects the idea that divine blessings bring happiness and prosperity to life.
  • apo vi vṛtrasya samayā pāṣyārujaḥ: This translates to "You, who are the waters that overcome obstacles." "Apo" means "waters," "vi" signifies "to overcome," "vṛtrasya" refers to "of the obstacles," and "samayā pāṣyārujaḥ" means "in the right time." This line symbolizes the cleansing and purifying power of water, which can remove barriers and bring forth new life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine strength, protection, joy, and the purifying power of water. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of difficulty, invoking the strength of the divine can provide support and guidance.
  • Recognizing the importance of nature, particularly water, in overcoming challenges and sustaining life.
  • Celebrating moments of joy and prosperity, acknowledging the divine influence in our achievements.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human experience, encouraging us to seek strength and joy in our lives while respecting the natural world around us.
