यथा वातः पुष्करिणीं समिङ्गयति सर्वतः |एवा ते गर्भ एजातु निरैतु दशमास्यः ||
yathā vātaḥ puṣkariṇīṁ samiṅgayati sarvataḥ | evā te garbha ejatu niraitu daśamāsyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as, like
    • vātaḥ: the wind
    • puṣkariṇīṁ: the lotus pond
    • samiṅgayati: stirs or moves
    • sarvataḥ: from all sides
    • evā: thus, in this way
    • te: your
    • garbha: womb, or essence
    • ejatu: may it be activated or energized
    • niraitu: may it come forth or manifest
    • daśamāsyaḥ: from the tenth month (referring to the time of birth)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between the wind and the lotus pond, suggesting that just as the wind stirs the waters of the pond from all directions, so too should the essence within a person be activated and brought forth.
    • The reference to the "tenth month" signifies the time of gestation, symbolizing the culmination of development and the moment of birth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the wind plays a crucial role in pollination and the dispersal of seeds, which can be likened to the nurturing of ideas and potential within individuals.
    • In personal development, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to harness one's inner potential and allow it to manifest in the world, much like a seed growing into a flower.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to activate and express one's inner essence, much like the natural processes that occur in the environment.
    • It serves as a reminder that just as the wind influences the pond, external influences and internal motivations can help bring forth our true potential.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, creative endeavors, and even in parenting, where nurturing and allowing a child to grow and express themselves is essential.
