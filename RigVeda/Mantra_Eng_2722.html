अगच्छद उ विप्रतमः सखीयन्न असूदयत सुखृतं गर्भं अद्रिः |ससाना मर्यो युवभिर मखस्यन्न अथाभवद अङ्गिराः सद्यो अर्चन ||
agacchad u vipratamaḥ sakhīyann asūdayat sukṛte garbham adriḥ | sasāna maryo yuvabhir makhasyann athābhavad aṅgirāḥ sadyo arcan ||
  • Mantra: "agacchad u vipratamaḥ sakhīyann asūdayat sukṛte garbham adriḥ | sasāna maryo yuvabhir makhasyann athābhavad aṅgirāḥ sadyo arcan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agacchad: went or moved
    • u: indeed
    • vipratamaḥ: the best among the wise or learned
    • sakhīyann: as a friend or companion
    • asūdayat: did not harm or injure
    • sukṛte: in good deeds or actions
    • garbham: the womb or the embryo
    • adriḥ: the mountain or the strong one
    • sasāna: commanded or directed
    • maryo: the mortal or human
    • yuvabhir: by the young or youthful
    • makhasyann: in the sacrifice or offering
    • athābhavad: then it became
    • aṅgirāḥ: the Angiras, a group of sages
    • sadyo: immediately
    • arcan: worship or adoration
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise individual who moves forward as a friend, ensuring that no harm is done in the process of good deeds.
    • The reference to the "womb" signifies the nurturing aspect of creation, where good actions lead to positive outcomes.
    • The "mountain" symbolizes strength and stability, indicating that such wisdom and friendship are grounded in solid principles.
    • The mention of "youthful" individuals suggests the importance of the younger generation in carrying forward traditions and values.
    • The act of "sacrifice" or "offering" highlights the significance of community and collective efforts in achieving goals.
    • The involvement of the Angiras, known for their wisdom, emphasizes the role of learned individuals in guiding society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who acts as a mentor and friend fosters a positive environment, ensuring that team members feel supported and valued.
    • In family settings, nurturing relationships and good deeds can lead to a harmonious household, where every member feels secure and loved.
    • Community service projects often bring together young people to work for a common cause, embodying the spirit of sacrifice and collaboration.
    • Educational institutions that promote mentorship programs encourage wise individuals to guide the youth, ensuring the transfer of knowledge and values.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, friendship, and good deeds in nurturing relationships and communities.
    • It teaches us that our actions, grounded in strength and guided by learned individuals, can lead to positive outcomes for future generations.
    • In real life, applying these principles can enhance personal relationships, workplace dynamics, and community engagement, ultimately leading to a more harmonious society.
