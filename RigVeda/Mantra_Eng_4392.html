अच्छा नो मित्रमहो देव देवान अग्नि वोचः सुमतिं रोडस्योः |वीहि स्वस्तिं सुखितिं दिवो नृन द्विषो अंहांसी दुरिता tarema ता tarema तवावसां tarema ||
acchā no mitramaho deva devān agne vocaḥ sumatiṁ rodasyoḥ | vīhi svastiṁ sukṣitiṁ divo nṝn dviṣo aṁhāṁsi duritā tarema tā tarema tavāvasā tarema ||
  • acchā no mitramaho deva devān: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine beings, the gods, be our friends." It emphasizes the importance of seeking friendship and support from higher powers or divine entities. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of community and support systems, where individuals seek guidance and help from mentors, leaders, or even spiritual figures.
  • agne vocaḥ sumatiṁ rodasyoḥ: Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. "Vocah" means to speak or invoke, and "sumatiṁ" translates to good thoughts or wisdom. This line suggests invoking Agni for wisdom and clarity. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking enlightenment or knowledge in challenging situations, much like consulting a wise friend or a teacher when faced with difficult decisions.
  • vīhi svastiṁ sukṣitiṁ divo nṝn: "Vīhi" means to bring or bestow, "svastiṁ" refers to well-being or prosperity, and "sukṣitiṁ" means good fortune. This line expresses a desire for well-being and prosperity to be bestowed upon humanity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the efforts of individuals and communities working towards social welfare, health, and prosperity for all.
  • dviṣo aṁhāṁsi duritā tarema: This translates to "May we overcome the obstacles posed by our enemies." "Dviṣo" refers to enemies or adversaries, and "duritā" means difficulties or misfortunes. This line highlights the importance of resilience and the need to confront challenges. In real life, this can be related to overcoming personal struggles, such as dealing with competition in the workplace or facing societal challenges.
  • tā tarema tavāvasā tarema: This phrase means "May we be liberated from these troubles." It emphasizes the desire for freedom from difficulties and the pursuit of a peaceful existence. In practical terms, this can be associated with the quest for mental peace, emotional stability, and the importance of seeking help when facing life's challenges, such as therapy or community support.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking divine support, wisdom, and well-being while emphasizing resilience against adversities. Its significance lies in the universal human experience of striving for peace, prosperity, and overcoming challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal struggles, community efforts for social welfare, or spiritual practices aimed at invoking guidance and support from higher powers. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals, communities, and the divine in the journey of life.
