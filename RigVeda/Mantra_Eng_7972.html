स देवः कविनेषितोऽभि द्रोणानि धावति |इन्दुर इन्द्राय मंहनाः ||
sa devaḥ kavineṣito3 'bhi droṇāni dhāvati | indur indrāya maṁhanā ||
  • sa devaḥ: This phrase translates to "that deity" or "that divine being." It signifies the presence of a higher power or divine force that guides and influences the world.
  • kavineṣito: This term means "inspired by the poet" or "guided by the wise." It suggests that the actions and thoughts of individuals are influenced by wisdom and creativity, often associated with divine inspiration.
  • abhi droṇāni dhāvati: This can be interpreted as "the divine being runs towards the goals" or "the deity moves towards the objectives." It indicates a sense of purpose and direction in the actions of the divine.
  • indur indrāya maṁhanā: This phrase translates to "the moon, for the sake of Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the heavens. The moon here symbolizes nourishment and the cyclical nature of life, which is essential for growth and sustenance.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance and the pursuit of goals with wisdom and inspiration. In real life, it can be applied to various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can seek inspiration from higher ideals or mentors, much like the divine influence mentioned in the mantra.
  • In leadership, the concept of guiding others towards a common goal resonates with the idea of a deity moving towards objectives, emphasizing the importance of vision and purpose.
  • In community building, the reference to nourishment and cyclical growth can be seen in sustainable practices that support the well-being of the environment and society.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine inspiration, purposeful action, and the interconnectedness of life, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and guidance in their endeavors.
