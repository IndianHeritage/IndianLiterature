चक्षुर नो धेहि चक्षुषे चक्षुर विख्यै तनूभ्यः |सं चेदं विं च पश्येम ||
cakṣur no dhehi cakṣuṣe cakṣur vikhyai tanūbhyaḥ | saṁ cedaṁ vi ca paśyema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakṣur: Eye or vision
    • no: Our
    • dhehi: Grant or bestow
    • cakṣuṣe: To the eye
    • cakṣur: Eye or vision (repeated for emphasis)
    • vikhyai: To perceive or to see clearly
    • tanūbhyaḥ: From our bodies or from our physical forms
    • saṁ: Together or in unity
    • cedaṁ: This or these
    • vi: Also or in addition
    • ca: And
    • paśyema: May we see or may we perceive
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for clarity of vision and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeing not just with the physical eyes but also with the inner eye, which represents wisdom and insight.
    • The repetition of "cakṣur" highlights the significance of vision in both a literal and metaphorical sense.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge. Students pray for clarity in understanding complex subjects, seeking not just to memorize but to truly comprehend.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may seek the ability to see beyond surface appearances, fostering empathy and deeper connections with others.
    • In leadership, this mantra can inspire leaders to have a clear vision for their organization, enabling them to guide their teams effectively towards common goals.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of vision—both physical and spiritual—in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek clarity, understanding, and insight in all aspects of life.
    • By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset that values perception and awareness, leading to more informed decisions and harmonious relationships.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of all beings, urging us to see the world with compassion and wisdom.
