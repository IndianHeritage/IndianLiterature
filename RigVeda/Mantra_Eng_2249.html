स सञ्जयः स विनयः पुरोहितः स सुश्रुतः स युधि ब्रह्मणस पतिः |चाक्ष्मो यद वाजं भरते मती धनाद इति सूर्यस तपति तप्यतुर वृथा ||
sa saṁnayaḥ sa vinayaḥ purohitaḥ sa suṣṭutaḥ sa yudhi brahmaṇas patiḥ | cākṣmo yad vājam bharate matī dhanād it sūryas tapati tapyatur vṛthā ||
  • sa saṁnayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings together" or "the one who unites." In real life, this can refer to a leader or a mediator who fosters collaboration among individuals or groups.
  • sa vinayaḥ: This means "the one who is humble" or "the one who has self-control." In practical terms, it highlights the importance of humility in leadership and personal relationships, reminding us that true strength lies in restraint and respect for others.
  • purohitaḥ: This word refers to a priest or a spiritual guide. In contemporary life, this can symbolize the role of mentors or advisors who provide wisdom and guidance in various aspects of life, including career and personal development.
  • sa suṣṭutaḥ: This translates to "the one who is well-spoken of" or "the one who is praised." It emphasizes the value of reputation and integrity, suggesting that one should strive to be a person of good character, earning respect and admiration from others.
  • sa yudhi brahmaṇas patiḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the one who is the lord of the wise in battle." It signifies the importance of wisdom and strategy in overcoming challenges, akin to how leaders must navigate complex situations with intelligence and foresight.
  • cākṣmo yad vājam bharate matī: This can be interpreted as "the one who has the vision to understand the essence of things." In real life, this speaks to the necessity of insight and perception in decision-making, encouraging individuals to look beyond the surface and grasp deeper truths.
  • dhanād it sūryas tapati tapyatur vṛthā: This translates to "the sun shines upon those who are worthy." It suggests that hard work and righteousness lead to success and recognition, much like how the sun provides light and energy to those who cultivate their fields diligently.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates essential qualities for personal and communal success, such as unity, humility, wisdom, and integrity. In real-life scenarios, these attributes are applicable in various contexts, including leadership, teamwork, and personal growth. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster harmonious relationships, navigate challenges effectively, and ultimately achieve their goals while contributing positively to society.
