अर्वाङ् त्रिचक्रो मधुवाहनो रथो जीराश्वो अश्विनोः यातु सुष्टुतः |त्रिवन्धुरो मगवाऽ विश्वसौभागः शं न आ वक्षद द्विपदे चतुष्पदे ||
arvāṅ tricakro madhuvāhano ratho jīrāśvo aśvinor yātu suṣṭutaḥ | trivandhuro maghavā viśvasaubhagaḥ śaṁ na ā vakṣad dvipade catuṣpade ||
  • arvāṅ: This term refers to the "swift" or "quick" aspect, often associated with speed and agility.
  • tricakro: This means "three-wheeled," symbolizing stability and balance in movement.
  • madhuvāhano: Translated as "honey-bearing," it signifies sweetness and abundance, often representing prosperity.
  • ratho: This word means "chariot," which is a vehicle of movement and progress, often used in a metaphorical sense for life's journey.
  • jīrāśvo: This term refers to a "well-trained horse," symbolizing strength and reliability in one's endeavors.
  • aśvinor: This refers to the "Ashvins," the twin deities of health and medicine, representing healing and well-being.
  • yātu: This means "may it go" or "may it proceed," indicating a desire for progress and movement towards goals.
  • suṣṭutaḥ: Translated as "well praised," it signifies the importance of recognition and appreciation in one's efforts.
  • trivandhuro: This term means "three-fold," suggesting a holistic approach to life and its challenges.
  • maghavā: This refers to "the mighty one," often associated with strength and power, indicating the importance of resilience.
  • viśvasaubhagaḥ: This means "universal prosperity," highlighting the aspiration for abundance and well-being for all.
  • śaṁ: This term signifies "peace" or "well-being," emphasizing the importance of harmony in life.
  • na ā vakṣad: Translated as "may it not be obstructed," indicating a desire for unobstructed progress.
  • dvipade: This means "two-footed," often referring to humans, symbolizing the human journey.
  • catuṣpade: This term means "four-footed," often referring to animals, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of speed, stability, abundance, and well-being. It encourages individuals to seek progress while maintaining harmony and resilience. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, professional endeavors, and community well-being. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the journey, recognize the contributions of others, and strive for universal prosperity and peace. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose.
