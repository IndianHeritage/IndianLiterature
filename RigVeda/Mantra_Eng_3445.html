अभूदु वो विधते रत्नधेम इदा नरो दाशुषे मार्त्याय |पिबत वाजाऽऋभवो ददेदु वो महि तृतीयं सवनं मदाय ||
abhūd u vo vidhate ratnadheyam idā naro dāśuṣe martyāya | pibata vājā ṛbhavo dade vo mahi tṛtīyaṁ savanam madāya ||
  • abhūd - This word can be interpreted as "may it be" or "let it be." It signifies a wish or a desire for something to come into existence.
  • u vo - This phrase translates to "for you all" or "to you." It indicates a collective address to a group, emphasizing community and shared experience.
  • vidhate - Meaning "to create" or "to establish," this word reflects the act of bringing something into being, often associated with divine or cosmic creation.
  • ratnadheyam - This term refers to "treasures" or "wealth." It symbolizes not just material wealth but also spiritual and intellectual riches.
  • idā - Translated as "now," it signifies the present moment, urging immediate action or realization.
  • naro - Meaning "man" or "human," it highlights the human condition and the role of individuals in the cosmic order.
  • dāśuṣe - This word can be interpreted as "to the giver" or "to the one who bestows." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and the act of giving.
  • martyāya - Referring to "mortality" or "humanity," it acknowledges the transient nature of human life and the inevitability of death.
  • pibata - This means "drink" or "consume," often used in a metaphorical sense to imply taking in knowledge or experiences.
  • vājā - Translated as "strength" or "power," it signifies the vital energy that drives life and action.
  • ṛbhavo - This term can mean "noble" or "excellent," suggesting a pursuit of high ideals and virtues.
  • dade - Meaning "to give," it reinforces the theme of generosity and sharing.
  • vo - Again, this means "to you," continuing the collective address.
  • mahi - This word translates to "great" or "mighty," often referring to the vastness of the universe or the greatness of the divine.
  • tṛtīyaṁ - Meaning "third," it can symbolize a triad or a balance among different aspects of life.
  • savanam - This refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating the importance of giving back to the universe or the divine.
  • madāya - Translated as "for joy" or "for delight," it emphasizes the joy that comes from sharing and giving.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of community, generosity, and the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth. It encourages individuals to create and share their treasures with others, acknowledging the transient nature of life while celebrating the joy of giving. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, charitable acts, and fostering a spirit of collaboration and support among individuals. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society, where the act of giving enriches both the giver and the receiver.
