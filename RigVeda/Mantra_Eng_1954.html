स्वादो पितो मधो पितो वयं त्वा ववृमहे |अस्माकं अविताभव ||
svādo pito madho pito vayaṁ tvā vavṛmahe | asmākam avitā bhava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svādo: sweet, pleasant
    • pito: drink, imbibe
    • madho: honey, nectar
    • vayaṁ: we, our
    • tvā: you, O divine essence
    • vavṛmahe: we invoke, we call upon
    • asmākam: our, belonging to us
    • avitā: protector, guardian
    • bhava: become, be
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation where the speaker expresses a desire for sweetness and nourishment, both physically and spiritually. The speaker calls upon a divine essence to provide protection and guidance, emphasizing the importance of community and shared experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a communal gathering, such as a family meal or festival, people often share sweet dishes, symbolizing unity and joy. This reflects the idea of "svādo" and "madho," where sweetness enhances relationships.
    • In a workplace, when team members support each other and create a positive environment, they embody the essence of "vayaṁ tvā vavṛmahe," invoking a collective spirit that fosters collaboration and success.
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking "avitā bhava," asking for protection and wisdom to navigate challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of sweetness in life, both in relationships and in the pursuit of knowledge and protection. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony and support within communities, whether in families, workplaces, or spiritual gatherings. By invoking divine protection and nourishment, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, making this mantra applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments.
