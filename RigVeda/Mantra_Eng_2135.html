प्रजाभ्यः पुष्टिं विभजन्त आसते रयिम इव पृष्ठम् प्रभवन्तम् आयते |असिन्वान दंष्ट्रैः पितुर अत्ति भोजने यस ताकृत्णोः प्रथमं सास्य उक्त्यः ||
prajābhyaḥ puṣṭiṁ vibhajanta āsate rayim iva pṛṣṭham prabhavantam āyate | asinvan daṁṣṭraiḥ pitur atti bhojanaṁ yas tākṛṇoḥ prathamaṁ sāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prajābhyaḥ: To the beings or creatures.
    • puṣṭiṁ: Nourishment or sustenance.
    • vibhajanta: They distribute or share.
    • āsate: They sit or reside.
    • rayim: Wealth or prosperity.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • pṛṣṭham: The back or support.
    • prabhavantam: The one who is powerful or radiant.
    • āyate: Approaches or comes near.
    • asinvan: With sharp teeth or fangs.
    • daṁṣṭraiḥ: With the teeth.
    • pitur: Of the father.
    • atti: Eats or consumes.
    • bhojanaṁ: Food or meal.
    • yas: Who.
    • tākṛṇoḥ: Who prepares or makes.
    • prathamaṁ: First or foremost.
    • sāsy: Grain or crops.
    • ukthyaḥ: Offerings or hymns.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of sharing nourishment and wealth among beings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the distribution of resources for collective prosperity.
    • The imagery of sharp teeth suggests the necessity of strength and protection in the act of sharing, akin to a father providing for his family.
    • It highlights the significance of preparing food and offerings, which can be seen as a metaphor for nurturing relationships and fostering growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often share their resources and knowledge with their children, ensuring their growth and well-being.
    • In communities, local farmers may share their harvests with neighbors, promoting a sense of unity and support.
    • In workplaces, leaders who distribute tasks and resources effectively contribute to the overall success of the team.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of sharing and nurturing within communities and families.
    • It teaches us that true prosperity comes from collective effort and support, rather than individual accumulation.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, healthier communities, and a more sustainable way of living.
