एतं मृजन्ति मार्ज्यं पवमानं दश क्षिपः |इन्द्राय मत्सरं मदम् ||
etam mṛjanti marjyam pavamānaṁ daśa kṣipaḥ | indrāya matsaram madam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etam: this
    • mṛjanti: they cleanse or purify
    • marjyam: the one that is to be purified
    • pavamānaṁ: the purifying or sacred essence
    • daśa: ten
    • kṣipaḥ: the swift or quick
    • indrāya: for Indra (the king of gods)
    • matsaram: jealousy or envy
    • madam: intoxication or pride
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of purification, emphasizing the importance of cleansing oneself from negative emotions such as jealousy and pride.
    • The term "pavamānaṁ" refers to a sacred essence that is essential for spiritual growth and enlightenment.
    • The mention of "Indra" signifies the need for divine support in overcoming these negative traits.
    • The phrase "daśa kṣipaḥ" suggests a swift action, indicating that purification should be done promptly and with determination.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, individuals may experience jealousy towards a colleague's success. This mantra encourages them to cleanse these feelings and focus on their own growth.
    • In personal relationships, feelings of pride can lead to misunderstandings. The mantra serves as a reminder to purify these emotions to foster better communication and understanding.
    • During meditation or spiritual practices, practitioners can recite this mantra to invoke a sense of purity and to seek divine assistance in overcoming negative emotions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-purification and the removal of negative emotions that hinder personal and spiritual growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including professional environments, personal relationships, and spiritual practices.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more positive mindset, leading to improved relationships and a deeper connection with the divine.
