द्वयाम् अग्ने रथिनो विंशतिं गा वधूमतो मगवाऽ मह्यम् सम्राट् |अभ्यावर्ति चायमानो ददाति दूनाशेयं दक्षिणा पार्थवानाम् ||
dvayām̐ agne rathino viṁśatiṁ gā vadhūmato maghavā mahyaṁ samrāṭ | abhyāvartī cāyamāno dadāti dūṇāśeyaṁ dakṣiṇā pārthavānām ||
  • dvayām̐ agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, the two." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the sacred. The number two can signify duality, such as the balance between opposites or the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • rathino viṁśatiṁ: "Twenty chariots" refers to strength and power. Chariots in ancient times were symbols of might and victory. In a modern context, this can be likened to the resources and capabilities one possesses to achieve success.
  • gā vadhūmato: This translates to "the song of the bride." The bride symbolizes new beginnings, unity, and the sacred bond of marriage. In real life, this can represent the importance of relationships and partnerships in achieving one's goals.
  • maghavā mahyaṁ samrāṭ: "May the mighty one grant me sovereignty." Here, "maghavā" refers to the powerful, often associated with the deity Indra, who embodies strength and leadership. This can be interpreted as a plea for empowerment and the ability to lead effectively in various aspects of life.
  • abhyāvartī cāyamāno: "This one, turning towards us." This phrase suggests a divine presence that is attentive and responsive to our needs. In everyday life, it can be seen as the importance of being open to guidance and support from the universe or those around us.
  • dadāti dūṇāśeyaṁ: "Gives abundant wealth." This signifies the blessings of prosperity and abundance. In practical terms, it can relate to the rewards of hard work, dedication, and the pursuit of one's passions leading to material and spiritual wealth.
  • dakṣiṇā pārthavānām: "The southern direction of the earth." The south is often associated with growth and fertility in various cultures. This can symbolize the importance of nurturing one's environment and relationships to foster growth and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, unity, divine guidance, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of balancing physical and spiritual aspects of life, the value of relationships, and the pursuit of empowerment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony in their endeavors, cultivate meaningful connections, and remain open to the abundance that life offers. By embodying these principles, one can navigate challenges and achieve personal and collective success.
