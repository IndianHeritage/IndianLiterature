एव न इन्दो अभि देववीतिम् परि श्रव नभो अर्णश् चमूषु |सोमो अस्मभ्यं काम्यम् बृहन्तं रयिं ददातु वीरवंतम् उग्रं ||
evā na indo abhi devavītim pari srava nabho arṇaś camūṣu | somo asmabhyaṁ kāmyam bṛhantaṁ rayiṁ dadātu vīravantam ugram ||
  • evā na indo: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra," where Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war.
  • abhi devavītim: This means "to the divine wealth" or "to the divine prosperity," indicating a desire for blessings and abundance from the divine.
  • pari srava nabho: This translates to "let the heavens pour down," suggesting a call for rain or divine blessings from the sky.
  • arṇaś camūṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the midst of the armies," which may refer to the protection and strength needed in battles or challenges.
  • somo asmabhyaṁ: This means "may the soma (a sacred drink) be for us," where soma symbolizes vitality, health, and divine inspiration.
  • kāmyam bṛhantaṁ rayiṁ: This translates to "the great wealth that is desired," indicating a longing for prosperity and abundance in life.
  • dadātu: This means "may it grant," expressing a wish or prayer for the fulfillment of desires.
  • vīravantam ugram: This can be interpreted as "the powerful and fierce," referring to strength and valor, qualities that are essential in overcoming obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a prayer for divine blessings, prosperity, and strength. It emphasizes the importance of seeking support from higher powers in times of need, whether in personal challenges or collective struggles. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as during difficult times in business, personal relationships, or even in community efforts where collective strength is required. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support, fostering a sense of hope and resilience in the face of adversity.
