मन्त्र: त्रिकद्रुकैभिः पतति षट् उर्वीर एकं इद् बृहत् |त्रिष्टुप् गायत्री छन्दांसि सर्वा ता यम आहिताः ||
trikadrukebhiḥ patati ṣaḻ urvīr ekam id bṛhat | triṣṭub gāyatrī chandāṁsi sarvā tā yama āhitā ||
  • Mantra: "trikadrukebhiḥ patati ṣaḻ urvīr ekam id bṛhat | triṣṭub gāyatrī chandāṁsi sarvā tā yama āhitā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trikadrukebhiḥ: By the three seers or sages
    • patati: Falls or descends
    • ṣaḻ: Six
    • urvīr: Vast or expansive
    • ekam: One
    • id: This
    • bṛhat: Great or vast
    • triṣṭub: A specific meter in Vedic poetry
    • gāyatrī: A sacred verse or hymn
    • chandāṁsi: The meters or rhythms
    • sarvā: All
    • tā: Those
    • yama: Restraint or control
    • āhitā: Established or set
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of the three sages who perceive the vastness of existence.
    • The phrase "falls or descends" suggests the manifestation of knowledge or wisdom into the material world.
    • The mention of "six" could symbolize completeness or the six directions (north, south, east, west, above, below).
    • "One" signifies unity amidst diversity, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The reference to "great" indicates the vastness of the universe and the depth of understanding that can be achieved.
    • The "triṣṭub" and "gāyatrī" refer to specific poetic forms that embody the essence of Vedic wisdom, suggesting that the structure of knowledge is as important as the content.
    • The term "restraint" implies the importance of self-control and discipline in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • Finally, "established" indicates that these principles are foundational and enduring in the quest for understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the idea of learning from multiple perspectives (the three sages) can be seen in collaborative learning environments where diverse viewpoints are valued.
    • The concept of unity in diversity is reflected in multicultural societies where individuals from different backgrounds come together to create a harmonious community.
    • Self-restraint is a key principle in personal development programs, emphasizing the need for discipline in achieving one's goals.
    • The structure of knowledge, akin to the poetic forms mentioned, can be observed in the way scientific theories are formulated and presented, highlighting the importance of methodology.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, unity, and the importance of structure in knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that true understanding comes from a combination of diverse perspectives, self-discipline, and a solid foundation of knowledge.
    • In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
