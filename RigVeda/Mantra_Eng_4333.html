अच्छा वद तवसं गीरभिराभिः स्तुहि पार्जन्यं नमसां विवासा |कनिक्रदद वृषभो जीरदादानु रेतो दधाति ओषधिषु गर्भम् ||
acchā vada tavasaṁ gīrbhir ābhiḥ stuhi parjanyaṁ namasā vivāsa | kanikradad vṛṣabho jīradānū reto dadhāty oṣadhīṣu garbham ||
  • acchā vada tavasaṁ gīrbhir ābhiḥ stuhi parjanyaṁ namasā vivāsa
    • acchā vada: This phrase translates to "speak well" or "speak positively." It emphasizes the importance of positive communication and the impact of words on our lives.
    • tavasaṁ: This means "your strength" or "your power," referring to the divine or cosmic energy that governs the universe.
    • gīrbhir ābhiḥ: This translates to "with hymns" or "with praises," indicating the act of worship and reverence through words.
    • stuhi parjanyaṁ: "Praise the rain-giver," where Parjanya symbolizes the rain, essential for life and agriculture.
    • namasā vivāsa: "With reverence, I bow," signifying humility and respect towards the divine forces.
  • kanikradad vṛṣabho jīradānū reto dadhāty oṣadhīṣu garbham
    • kanikradad: This term refers to "the one who roars" or "the powerful one," often associated with strength and vitality.
    • vṛṣabho: This means "the bull," symbolizing fertility, strength, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • jīradānū: This translates to "the giver of life," emphasizing the role of nature in sustaining life.
    • reto dadhāty: "He who holds the seed," referring to the potential for growth and reproduction in nature.
    • oṣadhīṣu: This means "in the plants," highlighting the connection between nature and life.
    • garbham: "The womb" or "the embryo," symbolizing creation and the nurturing aspect of the earth.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of positive communication, reverence for nature, and the interconnectedness of life. It highlights the importance of praising the divine forces that sustain us, particularly through the elements of rain and fertility. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Encouraging positive dialogue in personal relationships to foster understanding and harmony.
  • Recognizing the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable practices to ensure the health of our planet.
  • Valuing the role of agriculture and nature in our lives, promoting respect for the sources of our food and resources.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibility to communicate positively, respect nature, and acknowledge the divine forces that support life on Earth.
