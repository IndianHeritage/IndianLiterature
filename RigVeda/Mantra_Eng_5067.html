तां विग्रं धैते जठरं प्रिणध्या आ यत सद्म सभृतयः प्रिणन्ति |न मृष्यन्ते युवतयोऽवाता वियत पयो विश्वजिन्वा भरण्ते ||
tā vigraṁ dhaithe jaṭharam pṛṇadhyā ā yat sadma sabhṛtayaḥ pṛṇanti | na mṛṣyante yuvatayo 'vātā vi yat payo viśvajinvā bharante ||
  • tā vigraṁ dhaithe jaṭharam: This phrase can be interpreted as "they (the divine forces) strengthen the body." In real life, this can be related to the importance of physical health and well-being. Just as we nourish our bodies with food and exercise, the divine energies are believed to support our physical existence.
  • pṛṇadhyā ā yat sadma sabhṛtayaḥ pṛṇanti: This translates to "they fill the abode with abundance." This can be seen in the context of community and family life, where love, support, and resources are shared. For example, a family gathering during festivals where food and joy are shared exemplifies this abundance.
  • na mṛṣyante yuvatayo 'vātā: This means "the young do not suffer from the winds." Here, 'winds' can symbolize challenges or adversities in life. In a real-life scenario, this can be related to the resilience of youth who, despite facing difficulties, continue to thrive and pursue their dreams.
  • vi yat payo viśvajinvā bharante: This can be interpreted as "they who bear the universal essence." This speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the idea that we all share a common source of life. For instance, in environmental conservation, recognizing that all life forms are interconnected encourages us to protect our planet for future generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in our physical and communal lives, the resilience of youth in the face of challenges, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, it encourages us to nurture our bodies, foster community, support the younger generation, and recognize our shared responsibility towards the environment. This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our daily lives and the importance of living in harmony with ourselves and the world around us.
