इन्द्रो यातो 'वसितस्य राजा शमस्य च शृङ्गिणो वज्रबाहुः |सेद उ राजा क्षयति चर्षणीनाम अरान न नेमिः परि ता बभूव ||
indro yāto 'vasitasya rājā śamasya ca śṛṅgiṇo vajrabāhuḥ | sed u rājā kṣayati carṣaṇīnām arān na nemiḥ pari tā babhūva ||
  • Indro yāto 'vasitasya rājā: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods, who is depicted as a powerful ruler. The term "yāto" suggests movement or journey, indicating that Indra is on a mission or quest.
  • śamasya ca śṛṅgiṇo vajrabāhuḥ: Here, "śamasya" means peace or tranquility, while "śṛṅgiṇo" refers to the one with horns, often symbolizing strength and power. "Vajrabāhuḥ" translates to "one with a thunderbolt arm," emphasizing Indra's might and ability to protect and destroy.
  • sed u rājā kṣayati carṣaṇīnām: This line suggests that the king (Indra) is capable of overcoming obstacles or enemies ("kṣayati") and is associated with the "carṣaṇīnām," which can be interpreted as those who are in a state of turmoil or conflict.
  • arān na nemiḥ pari tā babhūva: The phrase indicates that Indra does not allow his enemies ("arān") to escape or evade him ("na nemiḥ"). "Pari tā babhūva" suggests that he surrounds or encompasses them, ensuring their defeat.

  • Real-Life Example: In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to a leader or a figure of authority who takes decisive action to maintain peace and order in a community. For instance, a police chief or a community leader who actively works to resolve conflicts and protect citizens embodies the qualities of Indra as described in this mantra.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of strength, leadership, and the pursuit of peace. It serves as a reminder that true leadership involves not only the ability to wield power but also the responsibility to protect and serve the community.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in governance, conflict resolution, and community service. Leaders can draw inspiration from Indra's qualities to foster a sense of security and harmony among people.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of effective leadership, emphasizing the balance between strength and peace. It encourages individuals in positions of power to act with responsibility and compassion, ensuring the well-being of those they lead. By embodying these principles, leaders can create a more harmonious and just society.
