युयं हि देवीर ऋतयुग्भिर अश्वैः परिप्रयाथ भुवनानि सद्यः | प्रबोधयन्तीर उषसः ससन्तं द्विपाच चतुष्पाच चरथाय जीवम् ||
yūyaṁ hi devīr ṛtayugbhir aśvaiḥ pariprayātha bhuvanāni sadyaḥ | prabodhayantīr uṣasaḥ sasantaṁ dvipāc catuṣpāc carathāya jīvam ||
  • yūyaṁ hi devīr: This phrase translates to "You, O Goddesses." It addresses divine beings, emphasizing their importance and presence in the universe.
  • ṛtayugbhir: This term refers to the "truth" or "righteousness" of the age. It signifies the moral and ethical standards that govern the universe.
  • aśvaiḥ pariprayātha: This means "with the horses." Horses in Vedic literature often symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. Here, it suggests a dynamic movement towards a goal.
  • bhuvanāni sadyaḥ: This translates to "the worlds immediately." It indicates the immediate impact of the divine on the various realms of existence.
  • prabodhayantīr: This means "awakening." It signifies the act of bringing awareness or enlightenment to beings.
  • uṣasaḥ: This term refers to "the dawn." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • sasantaṁ: This means "with the living." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and all living beings.
  • dvipāc catuṣpāc: This translates to "two-footed and four-footed." It represents all forms of life, including humans and animals, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • carathāya jīvam: This means "to move towards life." It signifies the journey of existence and the pursuit of vitality and purpose.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the role of divine forces in guiding and awakening all forms of life towards righteousness and vitality. It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of moral values in shaping our existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as promoting ethical behavior in communities, fostering awareness and enlightenment through education, and recognizing the significance of all life forms in our ecosystem. By invoking the divine and adhering to righteous principles, individuals and societies can strive for a harmonious and purposeful existence.
