नक्तोषासां वर्णम् आमेम्याने धापयते शिशुम एकं समीचि | dyावाक्षामां रुक्मो अन्तर विभाति देवाः अग्निं धारयन् द्रविणोदाम् ||
naktoṣāsā varṇam āmemyāne dhāpayete śiśum ekaṁ samīcī | dyāvākṣāmā rukmo antar vi bhāti devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām ||
  • naktoṣāsā: This term can be broken down into "nakta" meaning night and "uṣā" meaning dawn. Together, they signify the transition from night to day, representing the cycle of time and the balance between darkness and light.
  • varṇam: This word translates to "color" or "hue." In a broader sense, it can refer to the diversity of life and experiences that color our existence.
  • āmemyāne: This term suggests the act of gathering or bringing together. It can symbolize unity and the importance of community in achieving common goals.
  • dhāpayete: This means "to establish" or "to set." It indicates the act of creating a foundation or a stable environment for growth.
  • śiśum: This word means "child" or "young one." It represents innocence, potential, and the future generation that carries forward the legacy of knowledge and culture.
  • ekaṁ: This translates to "one" or "single." It emphasizes the importance of unity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • samīcī: This term means "proper" or "right." It signifies the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct in our actions.
  • dyāvākṣāmā: This can be interpreted as "heavenly" or "divine." It refers to the celestial realm and the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • rukmo: This word means "shining" or "brilliant." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the pursuit of truth.
  • antar vi bhāti: This phrase translates to "shines within." It suggests that true light and understanding come from within oneself.
  • devā: This means "gods" or "divine beings." It represents the spiritual aspect of existence and the connection to higher consciousness.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and the energy that drives life.
  • dhārayan: This translates to "holding" or "carrying." It indicates the responsibility of nurturing and sustaining life.
  • draviṇodām: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from a harmonious existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of unity, righteousness, and the nurturing of future generations. It highlights the cyclical nature of existence, where light overcomes darkness, and the divine presence guides us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to community building, ethical leadership, and the cultivation of knowledge and values in the younger generation. It serves as a reminder that our actions today shape the future, and by fostering a harmonious environment, we can ensure prosperity and well-being for all.
