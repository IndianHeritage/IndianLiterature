पुरू यत त इन्द्र सन्त्य उक्ताऽ गवे चकर्थोर्वरासु युध्यन् |ततक्षे सूर्याय चिद् ओकसि स्वे वृषा समत्सु दासस्य नाम चित् ||
purū yat ta indra santy ukthā gave cakarthorvarāsu yudhyan | tatakṣe sūryāya cid okasi sve vṛṣā samatsu dāsasya nāma cit ||
  • Purū yat ta indra santy ukthā: This phrase can be translated to "Whatever is abundant, O Indra, is praised." Here, "Purū" signifies abundance or plenty, and "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. In real life, this can relate to the idea of recognizing and appreciating the abundance in our lives, whether it be in relationships, resources, or opportunities.
  • Gave cakarthorvarāsu yudhyan: This translates to "The one who acts with strength in the battles of the cows." "Gave" refers to cows, which are a symbol of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. This line emphasizes the importance of taking action and being strong in the face of challenges. In modern life, this can be seen in the context of standing up for one's values or working hard to achieve one's goals, much like a farmer protecting their livestock.
  • Tatakṣe sūryāya cid okasi: This means "He who has the power to shine like the sun." "Sūryāya" refers to the sun, a symbol of energy, vitality, and enlightenment. This line suggests that one should strive to harness their inner strength and radiate positivity. In everyday life, this can be applied by being a source of inspiration and motivation for others, much like a leader who guides their team towards success.
  • Sve vṛṣā samatsu dāsasya nāma cit: This translates to "In the company of the strong, the name of the servant is known." This line highlights the importance of community and the influence of strong relationships. It suggests that being surrounded by capable and strong individuals can elevate one's own status and recognition. In real life, this can be seen in professional environments where collaboration and teamwork lead to greater achievements and recognition.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of abundance, strength, action, and community. It teaches us to appreciate what we have, to act with courage in the face of challenges, to shine brightly in our endeavors, and to surround ourselves with strong influences. In today's world, these principles can guide us in personal development, professional growth, and building meaningful relationships. By embodying these values, we can create a fulfilling and impactful life, much like the ideals presented in the RigVeda.
