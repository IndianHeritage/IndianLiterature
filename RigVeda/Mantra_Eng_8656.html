मन्त्र: एते पूता विपश्चितः सोमासो दध्याशिरः |सूर्यासो न दर्शतासो जिगत्नवो ध्रुवा घृते ||
ete pūtā vipaścitaḥ somāso dadhyāśiraḥ | sūryāso na darśatāso jigatnavo dhruvā ghṛte ||
  • Translation: "These pure, wise ones, who are like the moon, possess the essence of knowledge; they do not show themselves like the sun, but they are steadfast in their pursuit of truth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • pūtā: Pure
    • vipaścitaḥ: Wise or knowledgeable
    • somāso: Like the moon
    • dadhyāśiraḥ: Possessing the essence of knowledge
    • sūryāso: Like the sun
    • na: Not
    • darśatāso: Showing themselves
    • jigatnavo: Steadfast or determined
    • dhruvā: Unchanging or constant
    • ghṛte: In the pursuit of truth
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of individuals who embody purity and wisdom, akin to the moon, which is often associated with calmness and reflection.
    • In contrast, the sun represents visibility and outward expression. The wise individuals mentioned here do not seek to show off their knowledge or wisdom; instead, they embody it quietly and steadfastly.
    • The phrase "jigatnavo dhruvā ghṛte" emphasizes the importance of being unwavering in the pursuit of truth, suggesting that true wisdom is not about being in the spotlight but about a deep, internal commitment to understanding and knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a teacher who imparts knowledge to students without seeking recognition. Their focus is on the growth and understanding of their students rather than personal accolades.
    • A scientist working diligently in a lab, making discoveries that may not be immediately recognized by the public, exemplifies the steadfast pursuit of truth.
    • In everyday life, individuals who practice humility and seek knowledge for personal growth rather than external validation reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner wisdom and the importance of humility in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us that true wisdom is often quiet and unassuming, and that the most profound truths are discovered through steadfast dedication rather than public display.
    • In real life, applying this mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge for its own sake, to remain humble, and to contribute positively to society without the need for recognition.
