सिषक्तु न ऊर्जव्यस्य पुष्टेः ||
siṣaktu na ūrjavyasya puṣṭeḥ ||
  • Mantra: "siṣaktu na ūrjavyasya puṣṭeḥ"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "May we be endowed with the energy of nourishment."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • siṣaktu: This word refers to being endowed or blessed with something, particularly energy or strength.
    • na: This is a negation, meaning "not" or "without."
    • ūrjavyasya: This term relates to energy or vitality, often associated with life force or strength.
    • puṣṭeḥ: This word signifies nourishment, growth, or sustenance.
  • Contextual Meaning: The mantra emphasizes the importance of being blessed with energy that leads to nourishment and growth. It suggests that without this vital energy, one cannot achieve true sustenance or well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Physical Health: In the context of physical health, this mantra can be related to the importance of nutrition and energy in our daily lives. For instance, a balanced diet provides the necessary energy for our bodies to function optimally.
    • Mental Well-being: The mantra can also be applied to mental health, where positive energy and nourishment of the mind through education, meditation, and positive relationships are crucial for personal growth.
    • Spiritual Growth: In a spiritual context, being endowed with energy can refer to the pursuit of enlightenment and inner peace, which nourishes the soul and leads to a fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the vital energy that sustains life in all its forms. It serves as a reminder that nourishment—be it physical, mental, or spiritual—is essential for growth and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek balance in their lives, ensuring they are nourished in all aspects to thrive and flourish.
