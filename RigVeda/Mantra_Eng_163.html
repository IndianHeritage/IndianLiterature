सेमं नः स्तोमम् आ गह्य उपेदं सवनें सुतम् |गौरो न तृषितः पिब ||
semaṁ naḥ stomam ā gahy upedaṁ savanaṁ sutam | gauro na tṛṣitaḥ piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • semaṁ: shelter, protection, or a place of refuge.
    • naḥ: our, belonging to us.
    • stomam: praise, hymn, or song of worship.
    • ā gahy: may you accept or receive.
    • upedaṁ: this offering or sacrifice.
    • savanaṁ: the act of offering or the oblation.
    • sutam: well-prepared, or the essence of the offering.
    • gauro: bright, radiant, or auspicious.
    • na: not.
    • tṛṣitaḥ: thirsty or in need.
    • piba: drink or consume.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for divine acceptance of our offerings and praises.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking shelter and protection through worship.
    • The phrase "may you accept this offering" signifies the act of devotion and the desire for a connection with the divine.
    • The mention of "not thirsty" suggests that the divine is already fulfilled and does not require anything from us, yet we still offer our praises.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to celebrate and offer their gratitude through songs and prayers, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • During festivals, individuals may perform rituals that symbolize their offerings to the divine, seeking blessings and protection for their families.
    • In personal life, one might reflect on their achievements and express gratitude through meditation or journaling, acknowledging the support they have received.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of devotion and the act of offering in spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we seek blessings and protection, the essence of our offerings is rooted in gratitude and acknowledgment of the divine.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude, fostering a deeper connection with their spiritual beliefs and community.
