नकिर अस्य सहन्त्य पर्येत कयस्य चित् |वाजो अस्ति श्रवाय्यः ||
nakir asya sahantya paryetā kayasya cit | vājo asti śravāyyaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nakir: This can be interpreted as "not" or "no" in a negating sense.
    • asya: Meaning "of this" or "of him."
    • sahantya: Refers to "strength" or "power."
    • paryetā: Can be understood as "to be sought" or "to be desired."
    • kayasya: This translates to "of whom" or "whose."
    • cit: Meaning "thought" or "consciousness."
    • vājo: Refers to "strength" or "energy," often associated with horses or chariots in Vedic literature.
    • asti: Meaning "is" or "exists."
    • śravāyyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "of fame" or "of glory."
  • Meaning:
    • The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of strength and consciousness.
    • It suggests that true power and strength are not merely physical but are deeply connected to one's thoughts and consciousness.
    • The phrase "vājo asti śravāyyaḥ" emphasizes that strength exists in the realm of fame and glory, indicating that true power is recognized and celebrated.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, athletes often emphasize mental strength and focus as crucial components of their performance. For instance, a basketball player may practice visualization techniques to enhance their game, demonstrating the connection between consciousness and physical prowess.
    • In leadership, effective leaders are often those who possess not only strategic thinking but also the ability to inspire and motivate others. Their strength lies in their vision and the way they communicate it, aligning with the idea that true power is recognized and celebrated.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the interplay between strength, consciousness, and recognition in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true power is not just about physical might but also about the thoughts we cultivate and the impact we have on others.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal development, professional settings, or community leadership, understanding this connection can lead to more profound and lasting success.
