अभि सोमास आयवः पवन्ते मद्यम मदम् |समुद्रस्याधि विष्टपी मनीषिणो मत्सरासः स्वरविदः ||
abhi somāsa āyavaḥ pavante madyam madam | samudrasyādhi viṣṭapi manīṣiṇo matsarāsaḥ svarvidaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • somāsa: the essence of Soma, a sacred drink
    • āyavaḥ: those who are coming, arriving
    • pavante: they who are purifying, making pure
    • madyam: the intoxicating drink
    • madam: joy, ecstasy
    • samudrasyādhi: above the ocean, in the vastness
    • viṣṭapi: the expanse, the wide area
    • manīṣiṇo: the wise, the enlightened
    • matsarāsaḥ: those who are free from envy
    • svarvidaḥ: those who know the essence of sound, the wise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the arrival of the essence of Soma, which symbolizes divine inspiration and spiritual ecstasy.
    • The mention of "pavante" indicates a purification process, suggesting that through spiritual practices, one can attain a higher state of consciousness.
    • The "ocean" metaphorically represents the vastness of knowledge and wisdom, where the enlightened ones dwell.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being free from envy ("matsarāsaḥ") to truly understand and appreciate the divine knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the essence of Soma can be likened to moments of inspiration that artists, writers, and thinkers experience when they create their best work.
    • The purification process can be seen in practices like meditation and yoga, which help individuals clear their minds and connect with their inner selves.
    • The idea of the ocean of knowledge can be related to the pursuit of education and lifelong learning, where one seeks to expand their understanding of the world.
    • Being free from envy is crucial in personal and professional relationships, fostering collaboration and mutual growth rather than competition.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of spiritual awakening and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in practices that lead to purification of the mind and spirit, allowing for greater creativity and understanding.
    • In a world often filled with competition and envy, it serves as a reminder to cultivate wisdom and harmony, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened life.
