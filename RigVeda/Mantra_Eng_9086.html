प्र ते अस्याऽ उषसः प्रापरस्य नृतौ स्याम नृतमस्य नृणाम् |अनु त्रिशोकः शतम् आवहन नृन् कुत्सेन रथो यो असत् ससवान् ||
pra te asyā uṣasaḥ prāparasyā nṛtau syāma nṛtamasya nṛṇām | anu triśokaḥ śatam āvahan nṝn kutsena ratho yo asat sasavān ||
  • pra te asyā uṣasaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "for you, O Dawn." Here, "uṣasaḥ" refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • prāparasyā nṛtau syāma: This means "may we become part of the great rhythm." It suggests a desire to align oneself with the cosmic order and the natural cycles of life.
  • nṛtamasya nṛṇām: This translates to "of the best among men." It emphasizes the aspiration to be among the virtuous and noble individuals in society.
  • anu triśokaḥ: This can be interpreted as "after the three sorrows." It indicates a journey beyond suffering, suggesting resilience and the pursuit of joy.
  • śatam āvahan: This means "bringing forth a hundred." It symbolizes abundance and prosperity, indicating a wish for a fruitful life.
  • nṝn kutsena ratho yo asat sasavān: This phrase can be understood as "the chariot that brings forth the best of men." It signifies the importance of companionship and support in achieving greatness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking enlightenment, aligning with the cosmic order, and aspiring to be among the virtuous. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings, overcome challenges, and strive for abundance while supporting one another in our journeys. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal growth, community building, and fostering resilience in the face of adversity. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
