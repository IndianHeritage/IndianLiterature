या एक इति तं उष्ठुः कृष्टीनां विचर्षणिः |पतिर जजñe वृषक्रतुः ||
ya eka it tam u ṣṭuhi kṛṣṭīnāṁ vicarṣaṇiḥ | patir jajñe vṛṣakratuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • eka: one
    • it: here
    • tam: that
    • u: indeed
    • ṣṭuhi: praise
    • kṛṣṭīnāṁ: of the cultivators
    • vicarṣaṇiḥ: the one who moves in the fields
    • patir: lord
    • jajñe: was born
    • vṛṣakratuḥ: of the one with strong will
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a singular entity, a divine figure, who is praised by the cultivators of the land.
    • The term "vicarṣaṇiḥ" refers to the one who moves in the fields, symbolizing a connection to agriculture and the earth.
    • The phrase "patir jajñe vṛṣakratuḥ" indicates that this lord was born with a strong will, suggesting a powerful and determined nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, farmers often look to nature and divine forces for guidance and strength in their work. This mantra can be seen as a call for support from a higher power in their endeavors.
    • Community leaders who work tirelessly for the betterment of their communities can be likened to the "lord" mentioned in the mantra, embodying strong will and determination.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the divine presence in everyday life, particularly in the fields of agriculture and community service.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of strong will and determination in achieving one's goals, whether in farming or any other aspect of life.
    • This mantra can be applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek strength and guidance in their pursuits, encouraging them to connect with the earth and their community.
