अत्रेदु मे मांससे सत्यं उक्तं द्विपाच्च यच चतुष्पात संश्रिजानि |स्त्रीभिर यो अत्र वृषणं पृथन्याद अयुद्धो अस्य विभजानी वेदः ||
atred u me maṁsase satyam uktaṁ dvipāc ca yac catuṣpāt saṁsṛjāni | strībhir yo atra vṛṣaṇam pṛtanyād ayuddho asya vi bhajāni vedaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atred: here, in this context
    • u: and
    • me: to me
    • maṁsase: for the flesh
    • satyam: truth
    • uktaṁ: spoken
    • dvipāc: two-footed (referring to humans)
    • ca: and
    • yac: whatever
    • catuṣpāt: four-footed (referring to animals)
    • saṁsṛjāni: I create or I bring forth
    • strībhir: by women
    • yo: who
    • atra: here
    • vṛṣaṇam: the strong or powerful
    • pṛtanyād: from the earth
    • ayuddho: without conflict
    • asya: of this
    • vi: in this way
    • bhajāni: I worship or I honor
    • vedaḥ: knowledge or wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The reference to "two-footed" and "four-footed" signifies the relationship between humans and animals, highlighting the need for harmony.
    • The mention of women suggests the role of feminine energy in nurturing and sustaining life.
    • The phrase "without conflict" indicates a desire for peace and understanding among all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be reflected in the principles of animal rights and welfare, advocating for the humane treatment of animals.
    • Environmental movements that promote the protection of nature and the balance between human activities and wildlife resonate with the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, the emphasis on truth and harmony can guide individuals to communicate openly and resolve conflicts peacefully.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth, respect for all forms of life, and the importance of peaceful coexistence.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate wisdom and understanding in their interactions with others, fostering a more compassionate and harmonious world.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can contribute to a society that values integrity, empathy, and unity among all beings.
