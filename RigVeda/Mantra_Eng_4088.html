अधā नरो न्य ओहते 'धā नियुत ओहते |अधā पारावतā इति चित्रā रूपाणि दर्श्या ||
adhā naro ny ohate 'dhā niyuta ohate | adhā pārāvatā iti citrā rūpāṇi darśyā ||
  • adhā naro ny ohate: This phrase can be translated as "the men (or people) are drawn down." The term "adhā" means "down" or "below," while "naro" refers to "men" or "humans." The word "ny ohate" suggests a sense of being attracted or pulled towards something.
  • dhā niyuta ohate: Here, "dhā" means "to hold" or "to support," and "niyuta" can be interpreted as "to be guided" or "to be directed." This part of the mantra implies that there is a guiding force that holds or directs individuals.
  • adhā pārāvatā iti: The term "pārāvatā" refers to "the great or powerful," often associated with strength or might. "Adhā" again indicates a downward motion, suggesting that even the powerful can be drawn down or influenced by external forces.
  • citrā rūpāṇi darśyā: "Citrā" means "varied" or "colorful," while "rūpāṇi" refers to "forms" or "shapes." "Darśyā" means "to be seen" or "to be displayed." This part of the mantra emphasizes the diversity and beauty of forms that can be perceived in the world.

  • Real-Life Example: Consider the dynamics of society where individuals are often influenced by various factors such as culture, environment, and relationships. Just as the mantra suggests that people can be drawn down or guided by external forces, individuals in real life may find themselves swayed by societal norms or peer pressure.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reflection on the human condition and the interplay between individual agency and external influences. It serves as a reminder that while we may strive for power and individuality, we are often subject to the forces around us. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership, where a leader must navigate the influences of their team and environment while maintaining their vision. Understanding this balance can lead to more effective decision-making and a deeper appreciation of the diverse forms of life and experiences that shape our world.
