स हि श्रवस्युः सदनानि कृत्रिमाः क्षमया वृधाना ओजसा विनाशयन् |ज्योतिंषि कृत्वन्न अवृकाणि यज्यवेऽव सुक्रतुः सर्तवा अपः सृजत् ||
sa hi śravasyuḥ sadanāni kṛtrimā kṣmayā vṛdhāna ojasā vināśayan | jyotīṁṣi kṛṇvann avṛkāṇi yajyave 'va sukratuḥ sartavā apaḥ sṛjat ||
  • sa hi śravasyuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, the one who is worthy of praise." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.
  • sadanāni kṛtrimā: This translates to "the artificial abodes." It suggests a distinction between natural and man-made environments, urging us to appreciate the authenticity of nature over artificial constructs.
  • kṣmayā vṛdhāna ojasā: This means "with the strength of patience and growth." It highlights the virtues of patience and resilience, which are essential for personal and communal development.
  • vināśayan: This word means "destroying." It can refer to the destruction of negativity or harmful elements in our lives, promoting a path towards positivity and growth.
  • jyotīṁṣi kṛṇvann: This translates to "creating lights." It symbolizes the act of spreading knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment in the world.
  • avṛkāṇi: This means "the unblemished." It refers to purity and integrity, encouraging individuals to maintain their moral and ethical standards.
  • yajyave 'va sukratuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who performs sacrifices with good intentions." It emphasizes the importance of selfless actions and contributions to the welfare of others.
  • sartavā apaḥ sṛjat: This means "the waters that flow." It symbolizes the flow of life, knowledge, and resources, suggesting that sharing and collaboration are vital for progress.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life of integrity, patience, and selflessness. It encourages individuals to recognize and honor those who contribute positively to society while promoting the importance of authenticity over artificiality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, environmental conservation, and personal development. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can foster a more harmonious and enlightened society, ultimately leading to collective growth and well-being.
