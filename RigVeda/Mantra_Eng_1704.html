यमेना दत्तं तृत एनम् आयुनग इन्द्र एनम् प्रथमो अध्य अतिष्ठत् |गन्धर्वो अस्य रशनाम अगृभ्णात सूराद अश्वं वसवो निर अतष्ट ||
yamena dattaṁ trita enam āyunag indra eṇam prathamo adhy atiṣṭhat | gandharvo asya raśanām agṛbhṇāt sūrād aśvaṁ vasavo nir ataṣṭa ||
  • yamena dattaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "given by Yama," where Yama is the god of death and the underworld in Hindu mythology. It signifies the importance of life and the inevitability of death.
  • trita enam āyunag: "Trita" refers to a sage or a wise person, and "āyunag" means "long life." This part emphasizes the wisdom in seeking a long and fulfilling life.
  • indra eṇam prathamo adhy atiṣṭhat: "Indra" is the king of gods and represents strength and power. This line suggests that Indra is the first to support or uphold this life, indicating divine protection and strength in one's journey.
  • gandharvo asya raśanām agṛbhṇāt: "Gandharva" refers to celestial musicians and beings associated with joy and beauty. This line implies that the essence of life is tied to joy and the arts, which can uplift the human spirit.
  • sūrād aśvaṁ vasavo nir ataṣṭa: "Sūrā" means "brave" or "heroic," and "aśvaṁ" refers to "horse," a symbol of speed and power. "Vasavo" are the eight elemental gods. This part suggests that the brave and the elemental forces of nature support life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of courage and the natural world.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, death, and the divine support that guides individuals through their existence. It highlights the importance of wisdom, joy, strength, and bravery in navigating life's journey.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various life situations. For instance, when facing challenges, one can draw strength from the idea of divine support (Indra) and seek wisdom (Trita) to make informed decisions. Additionally, engaging in artistic pursuits (Gandharva) can provide joy and upliftment, while embodying bravery (Sūrā) can help overcome obstacles.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the multifaceted nature of life, encouraging individuals to embrace wisdom, seek joy, and draw strength from both divine and natural forces. It is applicable in daily life as a source of inspiration and guidance, helping individuals navigate their paths with courage and grace.
