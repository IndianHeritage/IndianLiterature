सम इन्द्रेरय गाम अनड्वाहं य आवहद उषीनराण्या अनः |भरताम अप यद रपो द्यौः पृथिवी क्षमा रपो मो षु ते किं चनाममत ||
sam indreraya gām anaḍvāhaṁ ya āvahad uśīnarāṇyā anaḥ | bharatām apa yad rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat ||
  • Mantra: "sam indreraya gām anaḍvāhaṁ ya āvahad uśīnarāṇyā anaḥ | bharatām apa yad rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • indreraya: to the lord of the senses (Indra)
    • gām: the path, the way
    • anaḍvāhaṁ: without obstacles
    • ya: who
    • āvahad: has brought forth
    • uśīnarāṇyā: from the realm of the dawn
    • anaḥ: the essence, the vital force
    • bharatām: of the Bharata (the people)
    • apa: from
    • yad: that which
    • rapo: of the waters
    • dyauḥ: the sky
    • pṛthivi: the earth
    • kṣamā: patience, endurance
    • rapo: of the waters
    • mo: the mind
    • ṣu: to you
    • te: your
    • kiṁ: what
    • canāmamat: can be thought of
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energy of Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the importance of harmony and unity.
    • The phrase "gām anaḍvāhaṁ" suggests a journey or path that is free from obstacles, which can be related to personal growth and overcoming challenges in life.
    • It highlights the connection between the earth (pṛthivi) and the sky (dyauḥ), symbolizing the balance between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The mention of "rapo" (waters) signifies the importance of emotional and spiritual nourishment, akin to how water sustains life.
    • The mantra encourages patience and endurance (kṣamā), which are essential qualities in facing life's adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, reminding individuals to work together harmoniously towards a common goal.
    • In personal development, it can serve as a reminder to navigate life's challenges with resilience and a positive mindset.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to earth and sky can encourage sustainable practices that respect the balance of nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, resilience, and the interconnectedness of all elements in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a harmonious existence that honors both the material and spiritual aspects of life.
