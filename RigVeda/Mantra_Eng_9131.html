मन्त्र: कुरुश्रवणम् आवृणि राजानं त्रासदस्यवम् |मंहिष्ठं वाघताम ऋषिः ||
kuruśravaṇam āvṛṇi rājānaṁ trāsadasyavam | maṁhiṣṭhaṁ vāghatām ṛṣiḥ ||
  • kuruśravaṇam: This term can be broken down into two parts: "kuru" meaning "to do" or "to make," and "śravaṇam" meaning "hearing" or "listening." Together, it suggests the act of making oneself attentive or receptive to wisdom and guidance.
  • āvṛṇi: This word means "to cover" or "to envelop." In the context of the mantra, it implies the act of seeking protection or guidance from a higher power or authority.
  • rājānaṁ: This translates to "kings" or "rulers." It signifies those in positions of power and authority, who are responsible for the welfare of their subjects.
  • trāsadasyavam: This term can be interpreted as "fearless" or "without fear." It suggests a state of being that is free from anxiety or trepidation, often associated with strength and confidence.
  • maṁhiṣṭhaṁ: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent." It denotes a sense of superiority or high quality, often in the context of virtues or characteristics.
  • vāghatām: This can be understood as "of speech" or "of words." It emphasizes the importance of communication and expression in conveying thoughts and ideas.
  • ṛṣiḥ: This term refers to a "sage" or "seer," someone who possesses deep knowledge and insight, often through spiritual practices or meditation.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra calls upon the rulers to be attentive and receptive to wisdom, seeking guidance and protection. It emphasizes the importance of being fearless and possessing excellent qualities, particularly in communication and expression. The mention of a sage highlights the value of wisdom in leadership.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who actively listen to their employees and seek their input create a more inclusive and productive work environment.
    • In community leadership, those who are fearless in addressing challenges and communicate effectively can inspire trust and confidence among their constituents.
    • In personal relationships, being open to listening and expressing oneself clearly can strengthen bonds and resolve conflicts.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for leaders to embody wisdom, fearlessness, and effective communication. In real-life scenarios, these qualities are essential for successful leadership, whether in business, community, or personal relationships. By embracing these principles, individuals can foster environments of trust, collaboration, and growth.
