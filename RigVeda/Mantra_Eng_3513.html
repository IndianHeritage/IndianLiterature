अहम् इन्द्रो वरुणस् ते महित्वोर्वी गभीरें रजसी सुमेके |त्वष्टेऽव विश्वा भुवनानि विद्वान् सम एयरयं रोदसी धारयं च ||
aham indro varuṇas te mahitvorvī gabhīre rajasī sumeke | tvaṣṭeva viśvā bhuvanāni vidvān sam airayaṁ rodasī dhārayaṁ ca ||
  • Aham Indro Varuṇas: The mantra begins with "Aham," meaning "I" or "I am." "Indro" refers to Indra, the king of the gods, symbolizing power and authority. "Varuṇas" refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. Together, this phrase signifies the speaker's connection to divine authority and the natural order.
  • Te Mahitvorvī Gabhīre Rajasī Sumeke: "Te" means "your," "Mahitvo" refers to greatness or majesty, "orvī" means "earth," "gabhīre" means "deep," "rajasī" refers to the atmosphere or space, and "sumeke" means "well-formed" or "beautiful." This part of the mantra expresses reverence for the vastness and beauty of the earth and the cosmos, acknowledging the divine presence in all aspects of nature.
  • Tvaṣṭeva Viśvā Bhuvanāni Vidvān: "Tvaṣṭeva" means "like Tvashtar," the divine craftsman, "viśvā" means "all," "bhuvanāni" refers to the worlds or realms, and "vidvān" means "the wise one." This line emphasizes the creative power of the divine, suggesting that just as Tvashtar creates and shapes the universe, so too does the speaker seek to understand and connect with this creative force.
  • Sam Airayaṁ Rodasī Dhārayaṁ Ca: "Sam" means "together," "airayaṁ" refers to the atmosphere or sky, "rodasī" means "the two worlds" (often interpreted as the earth and the heavens), and "dhārayaṁ" means "to hold" or "to support." This concluding line signifies the unity of all realms and the importance of maintaining balance and harmony between them.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine connection, the beauty of the natural world, and the creative power inherent in the universe. It serves as a reminder of our place within the cosmic order and encourages us to seek wisdom and harmony in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, where we recognize the importance of preserving the beauty of the earth, or in personal development, where we strive to harness our creative potential and connect with the greater forces of nature. By acknowledging the divine in our everyday lives, we can foster a sense of unity and purpose, leading to a more harmonious existence.
