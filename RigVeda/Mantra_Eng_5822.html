धीराऽ त्व अस्य महिना जनूंषि वि यस् तस्तम्भ रोदसी चिद उर्वी |प्र नाकं ऋष्वं नुनुदे बृहन्तं द्वितानक्षत्रं पप्रथच च भूमा ||
dhīrā tv asya mahinā janūṁṣi vi yas tastambha rodasī cid urvī | pra nākam ṛṣvaṁ nunude bṛhantaṁ dvitā nakṣatram paprathac ca bhūma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhīrā: Wise, intelligent, or composed.
    • tv: You (referring to a divine entity).
    • asya: Of this.
    • mahinā: Great or powerful.
    • janūṁṣi: Joints or connections.
    • vi: In various ways.
    • yas: Who.
    • tastambha: Has established or supported.
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms (often referring to the sky and the earth).
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • urvī: The vastness or expanse.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • nākam: Heaven or the celestial realm.
    • ṛṣvaṁ: The high or elevated.
    • nunude: Has declared or proclaimed.
    • bṛhantaṁ: The great or vast.
    • dvitā: The second or dual.
    • nakṣatram: Star or celestial body.
    • paprathac: Has spread or extended.
    • ca: And.
    • bhūma: The earth or ground.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine intelligence that governs the universe.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of all things, symbolized by the "joints" or "connections."
    • The reference to "two worlds" suggests a balance between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of consciousness and awareness in understanding the vastness of existence.
    • It also points to the divine's role in establishing order and harmony in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often encounter situations that require wisdom and composure, much like the "dhīrā" mentioned in the mantra. For instance, a leader making critical decisions during a crisis embodies this quality.
    • The interconnectedness of life can be seen in ecosystems, where every organism plays a role in maintaining balance, similar to the "joints" in the mantra.
    • In spiritual practices, the awareness of the duality of existence—material and spiritual—can lead to a more fulfilling life, reflecting the essence of the "two worlds."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the wisdom and intelligence that govern our lives and the universe.
    • It encourages us to cultivate awareness and understanding of our interconnectedness with all beings.
    • By embodying the qualities of wisdom and consciousness, we can navigate life's challenges and contribute to the harmony of the world around us.
