ये वां दंसांस्य अश्विनाः विप्रासः परिमामृशुः |एवेत काणवस्य बोधतम ||
ye vāṁ daṁsāṁsy aśvinā viprāsaḥ parimāmṛśuḥ | evet kāṇvasya bodhatam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • vāṁ: Your
    • daṁsāṁsy: Knowledge or wisdom
    • aśvinā: The Aśvins, twin deities associated with health and medicine
    • viprāsaḥ: The wise or learned ones
    • parimāmṛśuḥ: Who are engaged in or who have attained
    • evet: Indeed
    • kāṇvasya: Of the Kānva family or lineage
    • bodhatam: Awakening or enlightenment
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to those who possess wisdom and knowledge, particularly in the context of the Aśvins, who are revered for their healing abilities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and understanding, particularly as it relates to the Kānva lineage, suggesting a connection to tradition and learnedness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the Aśvins can be likened to doctors and healthcare professionals who strive to heal and provide knowledge to their patients.
    • In educational settings, teachers and mentors embody the qualities of the wise ones (viprāsaḥ) who guide students towards enlightenment and understanding.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom through reading, meditation, or mentorship reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and enlightenment, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding in their lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, healthcare, and personal growth, reminding us of the importance of learning and healing.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that wisdom is a collective journey, often guided by those who have come before us, and that enlightenment is a goal worth pursuing.
