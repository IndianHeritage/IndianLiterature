प्र चर्षणिभ्यः पृतनाहवेषु प्र पृथिव्यां रिरिचाथे दिवश्च |प्र सिंधुभ्यः प्र गिरिभ्यो महित्वा प्रेन्द्राग्नि विश्वा भुवनात्य अन्याः ||
pra carṣaṇibhyaḥ pṛtanāhaveṣu pra pṛthivyā riricāthe divaś ca | pra sindhubhyaḥ pra giribhyo mahitvā prendrāgnī viśvā bhuvanāty anyā ||
  • pra carṣaṇibhyaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "from the moving forces" or "from the energies that drive motion." In real life, this can relate to the dynamic forces in nature, such as wind, water, and even human actions that create change and progress.
  • pṛtanāhaveṣu: This term refers to "in the gatherings of the powerful" or "in the assemblies of strength." This can be seen in modern contexts such as community meetings, political gatherings, or any scenario where collective strength is harnessed for a common purpose.
  • pra pṛthivyā: This translates to "from the Earth." It signifies the importance of our planet and its resources. In today's world, this can be connected to environmental awareness and sustainability efforts, emphasizing our responsibility to care for the Earth.
  • riricāthe: This word means "to be nourished" or "to thrive." It highlights the need for sustenance and growth, applicable in personal development, education, and health. For instance, nourishing our bodies with healthy food or nurturing our minds with knowledge.
  • divaś ca: This means "and from the heavens." It suggests a connection to the divine or higher powers. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking inspiration or guidance from spiritual beliefs or practices, such as meditation or prayer.
  • pra sindhubhyaḥ: This translates to "from the oceans." It symbolizes vastness and depth, akin to the emotional and spiritual depths we experience in life. For example, the ocean can represent the vastness of human experience and the need to explore our inner selves.
  • pra giribhyo: This means "from the mountains." Mountains often symbolize strength, stability, and challenges. In real life, this can relate to overcoming obstacles or striving for high goals, much like climbing a mountain to reach a summit.
  • mahitvā: This term signifies "greatness" or "majesty." It can be associated with achievements and aspirations in life, such as striving for excellence in one's career or personal life.
  • prendrāgnī: This phrase can be interpreted as "the fire of the divine" or "the sacred fire." In many cultures, fire represents transformation and purification. In real life, this can relate to the passion and drive that fuels our ambitions and desires.
  • viśvā bhuvanāty anyā: This translates to "the various worlds" or "the diverse realms." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all existence. In modern life, this can be seen in the global community, where different cultures and societies interact and influence one another.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness between nature, humanity, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of drawing strength from various sources—earth, heavens, oceans, and mountains—to thrive and achieve greatness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, personal growth, community building, and spiritual practices. By recognizing and harnessing these forces, individuals can navigate life's challenges and contribute positively to the world around them.
