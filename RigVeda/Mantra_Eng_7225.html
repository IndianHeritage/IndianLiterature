यस्य ते स्वादु सख्यं स्वाद्वी प्रणीतिर्द्रिवः |यज्ञो वितन्तसाय्यः ||
yasya te svādu sakhyaṁ svādvī praṇītir adrivaḥ | yajño vitantasāyyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • svādu: sweet, pleasant
    • sakhyaṁ: friendship
    • svādvī: delightful, enjoyable
    • praṇītir: guidance, leadership
    • adrivaḥ: without obstacles, unhindered
    • yajño: sacrifice, offering
    • vitantasāyyaḥ: one who is expansive, vast
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sweetness of friendship and the guidance that comes from it.
    • The phrase "yasya te svādu sakhyaṁ" emphasizes the importance of having pleasant and sweet relationships.
    • "svādvī praṇītir adrivaḥ" suggests that such friendships provide guidance that is free from obstacles, allowing for a smooth journey in life.
    • The mention of "yajño" indicates that these relationships are akin to sacrifices or offerings, highlighting the value of nurturing and maintaining them.
    • "vitantasāyyaḥ" implies that the benefits of such friendships are vast and expansive, enriching our lives in numerous ways.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, having supportive colleagues can lead to a more productive and enjoyable environment. Their guidance can help navigate challenges, much like the "praṇītir" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal life, friendships that are built on trust and mutual respect can provide emotional support during tough times, illustrating the "svādu sakhyaṁ" aspect.
    • Community gatherings or social events can be seen as modern-day "yajños," where individuals come together to celebrate and strengthen their bonds, creating a sense of belonging and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nurturing sweet and supportive relationships in our lives.
    • It teaches us that such friendships not only provide joy but also guidance that helps us overcome obstacles.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger connections, a supportive community, and a more fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the value of friendship and the importance of fostering relationships that enrich our lives.
