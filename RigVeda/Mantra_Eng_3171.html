स सद्म परिणीयते होतामन्द्रो दिविष्टिषु |उत पोतानिषीदति ||
sa sadma pari ṇīyate hotā mandro diviṣṭiṣu | uta potā ni ṣīdati ||
  • sa sadma pari ṇīyate: This phrase can be translated to "the one who is in the abode of truth." Here, "sa" refers to "he" or "that," "sadma" means "abode" or "home," and "pari ṇīyate" implies "is surrounded" or "is encompassed." In a real-life context, this can refer to a person who lives in a state of truthfulness and integrity, surrounded by an environment that fosters honesty and virtue.
  • hotā mandro diviṣṭiṣu: This part translates to "the priest who is wise in the heavenly realms." "Hotā" refers to a priest or one who offers sacrifices, "mandro" means "wise" or "intelligent," and "diviṣṭiṣu" refers to "in the heavenly realms" or "in the divine." This can be related to individuals in our lives who guide us with wisdom, such as mentors or spiritual leaders, who help us navigate through life's challenges with their knowledge and insight.
  • uta potā ni ṣīdati: This translates to "and the one who is a protector sits." "Uta" means "and," "potā" refers to "the protector" or "guardian," and "ni ṣīdati" means "sits" or "remains." In practical terms, this can symbolize the presence of a guardian figure in our lives, such as a parent, teacher, or friend, who provides support and safety, ensuring that we feel secure in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, wisdom, and protection in our lives. It signifies that living in truth and integrity creates a supportive environment, while wise guidance from mentors helps us navigate our paths. Additionally, the presence of protective figures ensures our safety and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, educational settings, and spiritual practices, reminding us to seek truth, value wisdom, and appreciate the guardians in our lives who help us thrive.
