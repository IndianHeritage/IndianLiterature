बृहदु गायिषे वचोऽसूर्यानदीनाम् |सरस्वतीं इन महयां सुवृक्तिभिः स्तोमैर वसिष्ठ रोडसी ||
bṛhad u gāyiṣe vaco 'suryā nadīnām | sarasvatīm in mahayā suvṛktibhiḥ stomair vasiṣṭha rodasī ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bṛhad: Great or vast.
    • u: A particle often used for emphasis.
    • gāyiṣe: To sing or chant.
    • vaco: Words or speech.
    • 'suryā: Related to the sun.
    • nadīnām: Of the rivers.
    • sarasvatīm: Referring to the Sarasvati, a sacred river and goddess of knowledge.
    • mahayā: Great or significant.
    • suvṛktibhiḥ: With well-formed or well-spoken words.
    • stomair: With hymns or praises.
    • vasiṣṭha: The best or most excellent.
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness of the words sung in praise of the sun and the rivers.
    • It emphasizes the significance of the Sarasvati river, which symbolizes knowledge and wisdom.
    • The mantra suggests that through well-formed words and hymns, one can connect with the divine and the vastness of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rivers are seen as sources of life and knowledge. For instance, the Nile in Egypt is revered for its life-giving properties, similar to how the Sarasvati is honored in this mantra.
    • In modern times, the act of singing or chanting can be a form of meditation or spiritual practice, helping individuals connect with their inner selves and the universe.
    • Public speaking and effective communication are vital skills in today's world, reflecting the importance of "well-formed words" in achieving success and influence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the power of words and their ability to connect us with the divine and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of knowledge, communication, and reverence for nature in our lives.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to use our words wisely, to seek knowledge, and to appreciate the beauty of the world around us.
