तुविक्षं ते सुक्र्तं सूमयं धनुः साधुर बुण्डो हिरण्ययः |उभा ते बाहू रण्या सुसंस्कृत ऋदूपे चिद ऋदूवृद्धा ||
tuvikṣaṁ te sukṛtaṁ sūmayaṁ dhanuḥ sādhur bundo hiraṇyayaḥ | ubhā te bāhū raṇyā susaṁskṛta ṛdūpe cid ṛdūvṛdhā ||
  • tuvikṣaṁ te: This phrase can be interpreted as "your strength" or "your power." It signifies the inherent strength that one possesses, which can be both physical and mental.
  • sukṛtaṁ: This word means "good deeds" or "virtuous actions." It emphasizes the importance of performing righteous actions in life.
  • sūmayaṁ: This term refers to "well-formed" or "well-structured." It can relate to the idea of having a well-organized life or a well-structured approach to challenges.
  • dhanuḥ: This translates to "bow," symbolizing readiness for action and the ability to defend oneself or one's beliefs.
  • sādhur: Meaning "good" or "virtuous," this word reinforces the idea of righteousness and moral integrity.
  • bundo: This can be interpreted as "to bind" or "to connect," suggesting the importance of relationships and community.
  • hiraṇyayaḥ: This word means "golden" or "wealthy," symbolizing prosperity and abundance in life.
  • ubhā te bāhū: This phrase translates to "your arms," indicating strength and the ability to take action.
  • raṇyā: This means "battle" or "war," which can symbolize the struggles one faces in life.
  • susaṁskṛta: This term means "well-cultured" or "well-educated," highlighting the importance of knowledge and culture in personal development.
  • ṛdūpe: This can be interpreted as "in the form of truth," emphasizing the significance of truthfulness in one's actions.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," pointing to the importance of being aware of one's actions and their consequences.
  • ṛdūvṛdhā: This translates to "growth in truth," suggesting that as one grows in life, they should also grow in their understanding and practice of truth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, virtuous actions, and the pursuit of truth in life. It encourages individuals to cultivate their inner strength and engage in good deeds while being aware of their actions and their impact on the community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community service. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
