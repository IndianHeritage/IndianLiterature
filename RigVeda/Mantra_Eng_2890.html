शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek help or guidance from a higher power or a source of strength when facing challenges.
  • maghavānam indram: This translates to "Indra, the mighty one." Indra is a significant deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. In practical terms, this can represent the need for leadership and strength in our lives, whether in personal endeavors or in community leadership.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This means "in this place, we offer our best." "Nṛtamaṁ" refers to the best or the finest, and "vājasātau" relates to offerings or sacrifices. This can be interpreted as a reminder to give our best efforts in whatever we do, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: This translates to "listening to the fierce for prosperity." "Śṛṇvantam" means listening, and "ūgrā" refers to something fierce or powerful. This suggests that we should be attentive to strong, impactful ideas or teachings that can lead to our prosperity. In real life, this could mean being open to advice from mentors or learning from challenging experiences.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This means "in the assembly, we conquer the obstacles." "Samatsu" refers to a gathering or assembly, and "vṛtrāṇi" signifies obstacles or challenges. This highlights the importance of community and collaboration in overcoming difficulties. In practice, this can be seen in teamwork in workplaces or community efforts to address social issues.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This translates to "the gathered wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means collected or gathered, and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources. This can symbolize the importance of accumulating knowledge, resources, and support in our lives. In real life, this can be reflected in financial planning, education, and building relationships.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking strength and guidance from higher powers, giving our best efforts, listening to impactful teachings, collaborating in communities, and gathering resources. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, including personal development, leadership, teamwork, and resource management. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and work towards prosperity in their lives.
