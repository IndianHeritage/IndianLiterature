विमे कर्णा पतयतो विमे चक्षुर वी3दं ज्योतिर्हृदय आहितं यत् |विमे मनश्चरति दूराधीः किम् स्विद्वक्ष्यामि किमु नू मनिष्ये ||
vi me karṇā patayato vi cakṣur vī3daṁ jyotir hṛdaya āhitaṁ yat | vi me manaś carati dūraādhīḥ kiṁ svid vakṣyāmi kim u nū maniṣye ||
  • Translation: "May my ears be open to the words of wisdom; may my eyes be open to the light of knowledge; may my heart be filled with understanding; may my mind reach out to the truth. What should I speak, and what should I not speak?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • vi me: "May I" - a plea for openness and receptivity.
    • karṇā: "ears" - symbolizing the ability to listen and absorb knowledge.
    • patayato: "to the words" - referring to the teachings and wisdom shared by others.
    • vi cakṣur: "may my eyes" - a request for clarity and vision.
    • vī3daṁ: "light" - representing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • jyotir: "light" - further emphasizing the importance of illumination in understanding.
    • hṛdaya: "heart" - the center of emotions and understanding.
    • āhitaṁ: "filled" - a desire for fullness in understanding and compassion.
    • yat: "that" - referring to the knowledge and understanding sought.
    • vi me: "may my" - reiterating the plea for personal growth.
    • manaś: "mind" - the seat of thought and contemplation.
    • carati: "reaches out" - an active pursuit of truth.
    • dūraādhīḥ: "to the distant" - suggesting the pursuit of knowledge beyond immediate understanding.
    • kiṁ svid: "what should" - questioning the appropriateness of speech.
    • vakṣyāmi: "I speak" - the act of communication.
    • kim u: "and what" - further inquiry into the nature of expression.
    • nū: "not" - indicating restraint in speech.
    • maniṣye: "I should think" - reflecting on the importance of thoughtful consideration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a student may embody this mantra by actively listening to the teacher, engaging with the material, and asking questions to deepen their understanding.
    • In a workplace, an employee may apply this mantra by being open to feedback from colleagues, seeking clarity in communication, and ensuring that their contributions are thoughtful and constructive.
    • During personal relationships, one can practice this mantra by being attentive to the feelings and words of others, fostering empathy, and choosing words carefully to maintain harmony.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for holistic awareness—encouraging individuals to listen, see, feel, and think deeply. It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and understanding while also being mindful of one's words and actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals in their pursuit of wisdom, fostering better communication, empathy, and personal growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can navigate life's complexities with greater clarity and purpose.
