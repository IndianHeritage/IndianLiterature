पूर्वीर उषसः शरदश च गूर्ता वृतं जगहन्वां असृजद वि सिन्धून् | परिस्ठिता अतृणद बद्बधानाः सीरा इन्द्रः श्रवितवे पृथिव्या ||
pūrvīr uṣasaḥ śaradaś ca gūrtā vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ asṛjad vi sindhūn | pariṣṭhitā atṛṇad badbadhānāḥ sīrā indraḥ sravitave pṛthivyā ||
  • pūrvīr uṣasaḥ: This phrase refers to the "early dawns" or "the first light of day." It symbolizes new beginnings and the hope that comes with the arrival of a new day.
  • śaradaś ca gūrtā: "śarada" means "autumn" or "the season of harvest," while "gūrtā" can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth." This suggests a time of abundance and the importance of nature's cycles.
  • vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ asṛjad vi sindhūn: Here, "vṛtra" refers to a demon or obstacle, and "jaghanvām̐" means "we have defeated." The phrase indicates the overcoming of challenges, with "sindhūn" referring to rivers, symbolizing the flow of life and prosperity.
  • pariṣṭhitā atṛṇad: "pariṣṭhitā" means "established" or "firm," while "atṛṇad" suggests "to protect" or "to guard." This emphasizes the importance of stability and security in life.
  • badbadhānāḥ sīrā indraḥ: "badbadhānāḥ" can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful," and "indraḥ" refers to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and leadership.
  • sravitave pṛthivyā: This phrase means "to flow upon the earth," indicating the blessings and abundance that come from the divine, nourishing the land and its people.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of renewal, abundance, overcoming obstacles, and the importance of stability and strength. In real life, it can be applied to various situations such as personal growth, community resilience, and environmental stewardship. For instance, when facing challenges in life, one can draw inspiration from the idea of overcoming obstacles like "vṛtra" and embracing new beginnings with the dawn. Additionally, it highlights the significance of nurturing our environment, much like the autumn harvest, reminding us to appreciate and protect the resources that sustain us. Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the divine support available to us in our endeavors.
