तदिद्वदन्त्याद्रयो विमोचने यामन्न अञ्जस्पा इव घेद उपब्धिभिः |वापन्तो बीजमिव धान्याकृतः पृञ्चन्ति सोमं न मिनन्ति बाप्सतः ||
tad id vadanty adrayo vimocane yāmann añjaspā iva ghed upabdibhiḥ | vapanto bījam iva dhānyākṛtaḥ pṛñcanti somaṁ na minanti bapsataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • id: indeed
    • vadanty: they speak
    • adrayo: of the mountains
    • vimocane: in liberation
    • yāmann: whom
    • añjaspā: with great strength
    • iva: like
    • ghed: they are held
    • upabdibhiḥ: by the surrounding
    • vapanto: they who are growing
    • bījam: the seed
    • iva: like
    • dhānyākṛtaḥ: the grain that is cultivated
    • pṛñcanti: they inquire
    • somaṁ: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • na: not
    • minanti: they diminish
    • bapsataḥ: of the flourishing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between nature and the divine.
    • The mountains symbolize strength and stability, while the act of liberation suggests a release from constraints.
    • The reference to "great strength" indicates the power of nature and its ability to nurture life.
    • The "seed" and "grain" metaphorically represent potential and growth, emphasizing the importance of cultivation in life.
    • The mention of "soma" signifies the sacredness of certain experiences or substances that enhance life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the process of planting seeds and nurturing them to grow into crops reflects the mantra's theme of cultivation and growth.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek liberation from their fears and limitations, akin to the mountains providing strength.
    • In spiritual practices, the consumption of sacred substances (like soma) can symbolize the pursuit of enlightenment and connection with the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, strength, and spiritual growth.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our potential and seeking liberation from constraints.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate their talents and seek experiences that enhance their spiritual journey.
