परावतो ये दिधिषन्त आप्यम मनुप्रीतासो जनिमा विवस्वतः |ययातेर ये नहुषस्य बरहिषि देवाः आसते ते अधि ब्रुवन्तु नः ||
parāvato ye didhiṣanta āpyam manuprītāso janimā vivasvataḥ | yayāter ye nahuṣyasya barhiṣi devā āsate te adhi bruvantu naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "parāvato ye didhiṣanta āpyam manuprītāso janimā vivasvataḥ | yayāter ye nahuṣyasya barhiṣi devā āsate te adhi bruvantu naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Those who, like the winds, are powerful and who are pleased with the offerings, may they grant us the knowledge of the divine, as the gods who dwell in the sacred space of Nahuṣa do."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • parāvato: Refers to the powerful winds, symbolizing strength and movement.
    • ye: Those who.
    • didhiṣanta: Who are intent or focused.
    • āpyam: Knowledge or wisdom.
    • manuprītāso: Pleased with human offerings or actions.
    • janimā: Birth or creation.
    • vivasvataḥ: Of the radiant one, often referring to the sun or divine light.
    • yayāter: By which.
    • ye: Those who.
    • nahuṣyasya: Of Nahuṣa, a king who became a deity.
    • barhiṣi: In the sacred space or altar.
    • devā: The gods.
    • āsate: Dwell or reside.
    • te: They.
    • adhi: Upon or in regard to.
    • bruvantu: May they speak or grant.
    • naḥ: To us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from higher powers, akin to how individuals often turn to mentors or spiritual leaders for guidance in their personal and professional lives.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may seek the wisdom of experienced leaders to navigate challenges, reflecting the idea of divine guidance in decision-making.
    • In personal development, individuals may engage in practices like meditation or prayer, seeking clarity and insight, similar to the invocation of divine knowledge in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the quest for divine knowledge and the importance of being in harmony with the forces of nature and the universe.
    • It teaches us to be mindful of our actions and offerings, as they can attract positive energies and wisdom.
    • In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude, seeking knowledge, and being open to guidance from both the natural world and those around us.
