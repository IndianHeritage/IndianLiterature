इन्द्रः सहस्रदाव्नां वरुणः शंस्यानाम् |क्रतुर भवत्युक्त्यः ||
indraḥ sahasradāvnāṁ varuṇaḥ śaṁsyānām | kratur bhavaty ukthyaḥ ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sahasradāvnāṁ: This term can be broken down into "sahasra," meaning thousand, and "dāvnāṁ," which refers to those who are endowed or possess. Together, it signifies those who have a multitude of strengths or resources.
  • Varuṇaḥ: Varuna is another deity, representing cosmic order, truth, and moral authority. He is often associated with the waters and the celestial realm.
  • Śaṁsyānām: This word means those who are praiseworthy or worthy of praise. It indicates individuals or entities that have earned respect and admiration.
  • Kratur: This term refers to intention, will, or purpose. It signifies the mental and spiritual resolve behind actions.
  • Bhavaty: This means "becomes" or "is." It indicates a transformation or realization of potential.
  • Ukthyaḥ: This word refers to hymns, praises, or offerings. It signifies the act of expressing reverence or devotion through words.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the strength and leadership of Indra, who embodies the qualities of those who possess numerous strengths and resources. It acknowledges Varuna as a figure of cosmic order and praises those who are worthy of admiration. The mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and the transformative power of words and hymns in achieving one's goals.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader (Indra) who possesses a multitude of skills (sahasradāvnāṁ) can inspire and guide their team (śaṁsyānām) towards achieving a common goal. Their clear intentions (kratur) and effective communication (ukthyaḥ) can lead to successful outcomes.
    • In personal development, individuals who recognize their strengths and aim to improve themselves can embody the qualities of Indra and Varuna. By setting clear intentions and expressing their goals, they can transform their lives.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of strength, leadership, and the power of intention in both personal and communal contexts. It serves as a reminder that through clear intentions and the expression of praise and reverence, individuals can harness their potential and achieve greatness. The principles embodied in this mantra are applicable in various aspects of life, including leadership, personal growth, and community building.
