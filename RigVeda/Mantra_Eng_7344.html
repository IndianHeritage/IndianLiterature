मां नो अस्मिन महाधने परा वर्ग भारभृत यथा |संवर्गं सं रयिं जय ||
mā no asmin mahādhane parā varg bhārabhṛd yathā | saṁvargaṁ saṁ rayiṁ jaya ||
  • mā no asmin mahādhane: This phrase can be translated as "May we not be deprived of this great wealth." Here, "mā" means "not," "no" refers to "us," "asmin" means "in this," and "mahādhane" translates to "great wealth." This can be understood as a prayer for abundance and prosperity in life.
  • parā varg bhārabhṛd yathā: This part means "as the one who bears the burden of the highest class." "Parā" refers to "the highest," "varg" means "class" or "category," "bhārabhṛd" translates to "one who bears the burden," and "yathā" means "as." This suggests the importance of responsibility and the ability to carry the weight of one's duties and obligations.
  • saṁvargaṁ saṁ rayiṁ jaya: This can be interpreted as "Victory to the gathering of wealth." "Saṁvargaṁ" means "gathering" or "collection," "saṁ rayiṁ" translates to "wealth," and "jaya" means "victory." This emphasizes the idea of collective prosperity and the triumph that comes from unity and collaboration.

  • Real-Life Example: In a business context, this mantra can be applied to a team working together towards a common goal. For instance, a startup aiming to achieve financial success can embody the essence of this mantra by ensuring that all team members are aligned and working collaboratively, thus not being deprived of the wealth generated by their collective efforts.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of seeking abundance while also recognizing the responsibilities that come with it. It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also involves the strength of character and the ability to support one another in our endeavors.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in family settings where members support each other, in communities that work together for common welfare, or in workplaces where collaboration leads to success. It serves as a reminder that prosperity is best enjoyed when shared and that we must bear our responsibilities to achieve collective goals.
