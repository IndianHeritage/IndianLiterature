भूरी चकर्थ युज्येभिर अस्मे समानेभिर वृषभ पौंस्येभिः |भूरिणि हि कृणवामā शविष्ठेन्द्र क्रत्वा मरुतो यद वशाम ||
bhūri cakartha yujyebhir asme samānebhir vṛṣabha pauṁsyebhiḥ | bhūrīṇi hi kṛṇavāmā śaviṣṭhendra kratvā maruto yad vaśāma ||
  • bhūri cakartha: This phrase can be translated as "You have accomplished great deeds." The term "bhūri" signifies abundance or greatness, while "cakartha" refers to actions or deeds. In real life, this can relate to individuals or communities achieving significant milestones, such as a team completing a challenging project or a community coming together to build a school.
  • yujyebhir asme: This means "may we be united." The word "yujyebhir" implies connection or unity, and "asme" refers to us. This can be seen in real-life scenarios where teamwork is essential, such as in sports teams working together to win a championship or families supporting each other through difficult times.
  • samānebhir: This translates to "with those who are equal." It emphasizes the importance of equality and mutual respect among individuals. In a workplace, this can be reflected in a culture where all employees, regardless of their position, are valued and their opinions are heard.
  • vṛṣabha pauṁsyebhiḥ: "vṛṣabha" refers to strength or power, and "pauṁsyebhiḥ" relates to masculinity or virility. This can symbolize the strength found in unity and collaboration among individuals, akin to a group of friends supporting each other through life's challenges, showcasing the power of camaraderie.
  • bhūrīṇi hi kṛṇavāmā: This phrase means "we indeed create great things." It highlights the potential for greatness that exists within collective efforts. For example, a community coming together to create a sustainable garden can lead to environmental benefits and a sense of shared purpose.
  • śaviṣṭhendra kratvā: "śaviṣṭhendra" refers to the best or the most powerful, while "kratvā" means through our actions or efforts. This can be seen in leaders who inspire others through their actions, such as activists fighting for social justice, demonstrating the impact of dedicated efforts.
  • maruto yad vaśāma: This translates to "may we be under the influence of the Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities representing strength and vitality. In real life, this can symbolize the influence of positive forces in our lives, such as mentors or role models who guide us towards success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, strength, and collective action in achieving greatness. It teaches us that by working together, respecting one another, and being influenced by positive forces, we can accomplish remarkable things in our personal and communal lives. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from teamwork in professional settings to community initiatives, highlighting the importance of collaboration and mutual support in fostering growth and success.
