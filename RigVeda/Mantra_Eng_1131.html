स धारणत पृथिवीं पप्रथच च वज्रेण हत्वा निर अपः ससर्ज |अहन्न अहिम अभिनद रौहिणं व्य अहन व्यंसम् मगवाऽ शचीभिः ||
sa dhārayat pṛthivīm paprathac ca vajreṇa hatvā nir apaḥ sasarja | ahann ahim abhinad rauhiṇaṁ vy ahan vyaṁsam maghavā śacībhiḥ ||
  • sa dhārayat pṛthivīm: This phrase translates to "He who supports the Earth." Here, "sa" refers to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. The term "dhārayat" means to uphold or sustain, while "pṛthivīm" refers to the Earth or the physical world.
  • paprathac ca vajreṇa: This means "and he struck with the thunderbolt." "Paprathac" indicates the action of striking or hitting, and "vajreṇa" refers to the thunderbolt, a symbol of power and strength associated with Indra.
  • hatvā nir apaḥ sasarja: This translates to "having defeated the waters." "Hatvā" means to defeat or conquer, "nir" means without, and "apaḥ" refers to the waters or the oceans. This line signifies the control over chaotic elements, representing the triumph over obstacles.
  • ahann ahim abhinad: This means "I struck the serpent." "Ahann" translates to "I struck," "ahim" refers to a serpent or a dragon, often symbolizing chaos or evil. This line emphasizes the victory over malevolent forces.
  • rauhiṇaṁ vy ahan: This translates to "I struck the enemy." "Rauhiṇaṁ" can be interpreted as an enemy or adversary, and "vy ahan" means "I struck down." This reinforces the theme of overcoming challenges and adversaries.
  • vyaṁsam maghavā śacībhiḥ: This means "the powerful one, with strength." "Vyaṁsam" refers to the powerful or mighty, "maghavā" is often associated with Indra, and "śacībhiḥ" refers to strength or might. This line highlights the importance of strength and power in overcoming difficulties.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of strength, support, and victory over chaos and adversaries. It signifies the divine power that sustains the Earth and the ability to conquer challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals face obstacles, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. It serves as a reminder of the inner strength and resilience required to overcome difficulties, much like how leaders and individuals in history have triumphed over adversity. The mantra encourages us to seek strength within ourselves and to support one another in our endeavors, reflecting the interconnectedness of life and the importance of community in overcoming challenges.
