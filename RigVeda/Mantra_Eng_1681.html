मां नो मित्रो वरुणो आर्यमायुर इन्द्र ऋभुक्षा मरुतः परि ख्यन |यद वाजिनो देवजातस्य सप्तेः प्रवक्ष्यामो विदथे वीर्याणि ||
mā no mitro varuṇo aryamāyur indra ṛbhukṣā marutaḥ pari khyan | yad vājino devajātasya sapteḥ pravakṣyāmo vidathe vīryāṇi ||
  • mā no mitro varuṇo aryamāyur indra ṛbhukṣā marutaḥ pari khyan: This line invokes various deities, asking for their blessings and protection.
    • Mitro: Represents friendship and harmony, emphasizing the importance of social bonds.
    • Varuṇa: Symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority, reminding us of the need for righteousness in our actions.
    • Aryamā: Associated with the ancestors and the afterlife, highlighting the significance of honoring our heritage.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power, encouraging us to seek courage in our endeavors.
    • Ṛbhukṣā: Represents nourishment and sustenance, reminding us of the importance of food and resources.
    • Marutaḥ: The storm gods, symbolizing the forces of nature and the need to respect the environment.
  • yad vājino devajātasya sapteḥ pravakṣyāmo vidathe vīryāṇi: This line speaks of the divine qualities and strengths that we aspire to attain.
    • Vājino: Refers to the swift and powerful, indicating the pursuit of excellence and success.
    • Devajātasya: Suggests the divine origin of these qualities, reminding us that our strengths are a gift.
    • Sapteḥ: Implies the sevenfold path or aspects of life, encouraging a holistic approach to personal development.
    • Vidathe: Refers to the assembly or gathering, emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
    • Vīryāṇi: Represents valor and strength, inspiring us to cultivate courage in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, nourishment, and harmony in life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of moral values. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during communal gatherings, rituals, or personal reflections, to invoke a sense of unity, purpose, and strength. By recognizing the divine qualities we aspire to embody, we can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, honoring both our heritage and the environment around us.
