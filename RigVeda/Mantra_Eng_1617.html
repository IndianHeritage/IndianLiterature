युवं वस्त्राणि पीवसा वसाथे युवोर अच्‍छिद्रā मन्तवो ह सर्गाः |अवातिरतम अनृतानि विश्व ऋतेन मित्रावरुणा सचेथे ||
yuvaṁ vastrāṇi pīvasā vasāthe yuvor acchidrā mantavo ha sargāḥ | avātiratam anṛtāni viśva ṛtena mitrāvaruṇā sacethe ||
  • yuvaṁ - This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • vastrāṇi - Meaning "clothes" or "garments," this term symbolizes protection and identity. Just as clothing covers and protects the body, it can also represent the values and beliefs that shape an individual.
  • pīvasā - This word means "with abundance" or "richness." It suggests a state of fullness and prosperity, indicating that one should embrace the richness of life.
  • vasāthe - This translates to "in your abode" or "in your dwelling." It emphasizes the importance of a safe and nurturing environment for growth.
  • yuvor - This means "of the young" or "of the youth," reinforcing the focus on the younger generation and their role in society.
  • acchidrā - Meaning "unbroken" or "whole," this term signifies integrity and unity, suggesting that the youth should remain undivided in their purpose and actions.
  • mantavo - This translates to "thoughts" or "intellect." It highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in guiding one's actions.
  • ha - A particle often used for emphasis, indicating certainty or affirmation.
  • sargāḥ - Meaning "creations" or "origins," this term refers to the various forms of life and existence that arise from the divine.
  • avātiratam - This means "to be free from" or "to transcend." It suggests the idea of overcoming obstacles and limitations.
  • anṛtāni - Translating to "falsehoods" or "lies," this term emphasizes the importance of truth and honesty in one's life.
  • viśva - Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ṛtena - This translates to "by truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order," highlighting the significance of living in harmony with universal principles.
  • mitrāvaruṇā - Referring to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This emphasizes the importance of relationships and ethical conduct.
  • sacethe - Meaning "may you be established" or "may you thrive," it is a blessing for prosperity and success.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of youth, integrity, wisdom, and truth in life. It encourages the younger generation to embrace their potential, remain united in purpose, and live in accordance with universal truths. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, where the youth are encouraged to pursue knowledge and ethical values. It also serves as a reminder for individuals to cultivate a nurturing environment that fosters growth and prosperity, both personally and within the community. By embodying these principles, one can contribute positively to society and create a harmonious existence.
