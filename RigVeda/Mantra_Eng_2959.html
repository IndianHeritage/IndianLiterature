इन्द्रो विश्वैर वीर्यैः पत्यमान उभे आ पप्रौ रोदसी महित्वा |पुरंदरो वृत्रहा धृष्णुसेणः संगृभ्य न आ भर भूरी पश्वः ||
indro viśvair vīryai3ḥ patyamāna ubhe ā paprau rodasī mahitvā | puraṁdaro vṛtrahā dhṛṣṇuṣeṇaḥ saṁgṛbhyā na ā bharā bhūri paśvaḥ ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Viśvair: Means "with all" or "universal," indicating the all-encompassing nature of Indra's strength.
  • Vīryaiḥ: Translates to "with valor" or "with might," emphasizing the heroic qualities associated with Indra.
  • Patyamāna: Means "being invoked" or "being worshipped," suggesting that Indra is being called upon for assistance.
  • Ubhe: Means "both," referring to the dual aspects of existence, such as the physical and spiritual realms.
  • Ā paprau: Translates to "he has poured forth," indicating the act of bestowing blessings or abundance.
  • Rodasī: Refers to the "two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," symbolizing the connection between the divine and the earthly.
  • Mahitvā: Means "greatness" or "majesty," highlighting the grandeur of Indra's presence.
  • Puraṁdaro: Another name for Indra, meaning "the destroyer of cities," representing his role in overcoming obstacles.
  • Vṛtrahā: Means "slayer of Vritra," referring to Indra's victory over the serpent demon, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
  • Dhṛṣṇuṣeṇaḥ: Translates to "with strength," emphasizing the power and determination of Indra.
  • Saṁgṛbhyā: Means "having gathered," indicating the collection of resources or strength.
  • Na ā bharā: Translates to "do not burden," suggesting a plea for relief from difficulties.
  • Bhūri: Means "abundant" or "plentiful," indicating a desire for prosperity.
  • Paśvaḥ: Refers to "cattle" or "wealth," symbolizing material and spiritual abundance.

  • Significance: This mantra invokes Indra, seeking his blessings for strength, abundance, and protection from obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of valor and the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be relevant in various situations, such as during challenging times when one seeks strength and support. For example, individuals facing difficulties in their careers or personal lives can recite this mantra to invoke courage and resilience.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming challenges. It encourages individuals to embrace their inner strength and strive for abundance in all aspects of life.
