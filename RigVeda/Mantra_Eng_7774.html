अभि वाह्निर अमर्त्यः सप्त पश्यति वावहिः |क्रीविर देवीर अतर्पयत ||
abhi vahnir amartyaḥ sapta paśyati vāvahiḥ | krivir devīr atarpayat ||
  • Mantra: "abhi vahnir amartyaḥ sapta paśyati vāvahiḥ | krivir devīr atarpayat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • vahnir: fire
    • amartyaḥ: immortal or divine beings
    • sapta: seven
    • paśyati: sees or perceives
    • vāvahiḥ: those who carry or bring
    • krivir: the creative force or energy
    • devīr: divine or goddesses
    • atarpayat: satisfied or nourished
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire that is perceived by the immortal beings.
    • The "seven" could symbolize various aspects of life or the universe, such as the seven chakras, seven continents, or seven days of the week, indicating a holistic view of existence.
    • The phrase "those who carry" suggests the importance of individuals or forces that bring forth energy or creativity into the world.
    • The creative force, represented by "krivir," is nourished by divine energies, indicating a symbiotic relationship between the divine and the material world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the concept of "fire" can be seen as a metaphor for passion and creativity. For instance, artists and innovators often draw inspiration from their inner fire to create works that resonate with others.
    • The idea of "seven" can be reflected in various cultural practices, such as the seven wonders of the world, which remind us of the beauty and diversity of human achievement.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who "carry" the vision of the organization can be seen as the ones who inspire and motivate their teams, much like the divine forces mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine and the material world, highlighting the importance of creativity and inspiration in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are all part of a larger cosmic order, where our actions and energies contribute to the greater good.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek their inner fire, embrace creativity, and recognize the divine forces that nourish their endeavors, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
