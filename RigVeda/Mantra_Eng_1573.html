मन्त्र: एति प्र होतā व्रतम् अस्य माययोर्ध्वां दधानाḥ शुचिपेशसं धियम् |अभि श्रुचः क्रमते दक्षिणावृतो या अस्य धाम प्रथमं ह निंसते ||
eti pra hotā vratam asya māyayordhvāṁ dadhānaḥ śucipeśasaṁ dhiyam | abhi srucaḥ kramate dakṣiṇāvṛto yā asya dhāma prathamaṁ ha niṁsate ||
  • eti pra hotā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who invokes" or "the one who offers." In a real-life context, this can refer to a person who leads a community or family in rituals or prayers, emphasizing the importance of leadership and guidance in spiritual practices.
  • vratam asya: "vratam" means "vow" or "commitment." This suggests the significance of dedication and discipline in one's spiritual journey. For example, someone might take a vow to meditate daily or to engage in acts of charity, reflecting their commitment to personal growth and community service.
  • māyayor dhvāṁ dadhānaḥ: This can be translated as "holding the two aspects of illusion." It highlights the duality of existence—material and spiritual. In real life, this can be seen in the balance one must maintain between worldly responsibilities and spiritual pursuits, such as balancing work and family life with personal spiritual practices.
  • śucipeśasaṁ dhiyam: "śucipeśasaṁ" refers to purity and clarity of thought. This emphasizes the importance of having a clear and pure mind in spiritual practices. For instance, practicing mindfulness and meditation can help individuals achieve mental clarity and emotional stability.
  • abhi srucaḥ: This phrase means "to flow forth" or "to pour out." It can symbolize the act of sharing knowledge or blessings with others. In a practical sense, this could relate to educators or mentors who share their wisdom and experiences to uplift others in their community.
  • kramate dakṣiṇāvṛto: "kramate" means "to proceed" or "to move forward," and "dakṣiṇāvṛto" refers to the right path or direction. This suggests the importance of following a righteous path in life. For example, making ethical decisions in business or personal life reflects this principle.
  • yā asya dhāma prathamaṁ ha niṁsate: This can be interpreted as "the abode that shines first." It signifies the ultimate goal of spiritual practice, which is to attain a state of enlightenment or divine connection. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who strive for higher consciousness through various spiritual practices, such as yoga or meditation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of commitment, clarity of thought, and the balance between material and spiritual aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to lead with integrity, share knowledge, and strive for enlightenment. In today's fast-paced world, these principles can guide individuals in making ethical choices, fostering community, and pursuing personal growth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
