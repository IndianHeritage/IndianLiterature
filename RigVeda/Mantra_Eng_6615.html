वैयश्वस्य श्रुतं नरतो मे अस्य वेदथः |सजोषसां वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमा ||
vaiyaśvasya śrutaṁ naroto me asya vedathaḥ | sajoṣasā varuṇo mitro aryamā ||
  • Vaiyaśvasya: This term refers to the qualities or attributes associated with the Vaiśvadeva, which signifies universal welfare and the collective well-being of all beings.
  • Śrutaṁ: This word means "that which is heard" or "knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in our lives.
  • Naroto: This translates to "of the human beings" or "of men." It highlights the human experience and the need for connection among individuals.
  • Me: This means "my" or "to me," indicating a personal connection to the knowledge and wisdom being referred to.
  • Asya: This word means "of this" or "of this world," pointing to the relevance of the mantra in our current existence.
  • Vedathaḥ: This term refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom" derived from the Vedas, the ancient scriptures of India, which are a source of spiritual and philosophical guidance.
  • Sajoṣasā: This means "with harmony" or "in unity," emphasizing the importance of cooperation and collective effort among individuals.
  • Varuṇo: Varuna is the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority, representing the principles of truth and righteousness.
  • Mitro: This refers to "friend" or "allies," indicating the significance of friendship and support in human relationships.
  • Aryamā: Aryama is a deity associated with the path of righteousness and the protection of the moral order, symbolizing the importance of ethical living.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, unity, and moral values. It calls for individuals to seek knowledge and understanding while fostering harmonious relationships with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, conflict resolution, and personal development. By embodying the principles of cooperation, friendship, and ethical living, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and just society, reflecting the teachings of the Vedas in their daily lives.
