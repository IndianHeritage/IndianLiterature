मन्तर: नृचक्षाः रक्षः परि पश्य विक्षु तस्य त्रिणि प्रति श्रिणीहि अग्रा | तस्याग्ने पृष्टीर हरसा श्रिणीहि त्रेधा मूलं यातुधानस्य वृश्च ||
nṛcakṣā rakṣaḥ pari paśya vikṣu tasya trīṇi prati śṛṇīhy agrā | tasyāgne pṛṣṭīr harasā śṛṇīhi tredhā mūlaṁ yātudhānasya vṛśca ||
  • nṛcakṣā rakṣaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "O protector of men." It invokes a divine guardian who watches over humanity. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a parent or a mentor who safeguards and guides individuals through challenges.
  • pari paśya vikṣu: This means "look around and see." It encourages awareness and observation of one's surroundings. In modern life, this can be related to the importance of being mindful and aware of the environment, whether it be in nature or in social settings, to make informed decisions.
  • tasya trīṇi prati śṛṇīhy agrā: This translates to "listen to the three things that are foremost." It suggests paying attention to key aspects or principles that guide one's actions. In practical terms, this could refer to the importance of focusing on core values such as honesty, integrity, and compassion in personal and professional life.
  • tasyāgne pṛṣṭīr harasā śṛṇīhi: This means "O Agni, listen to the call." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification. This can be seen in real life through the process of personal growth, where individuals undergo challenges that lead to self-improvement and enlightenment.
  • tredhā mūlaṁ yātudhānasya vṛśca: This translates to "the root of the threefold is the demon." It suggests that there are underlying issues or negative forces that need to be addressed. In contemporary life, this can be interpreted as recognizing and confronting personal demons, such as fears or insecurities, to achieve a balanced and fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of protection, awareness, listening to core values, and confronting inner challenges. Its significance lies in guiding individuals to seek divine protection, remain mindful of their surroundings, adhere to fundamental principles, and engage in personal transformation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, personal development, and community building, where the focus is on nurturing a safe and supportive environment for growth and understanding.
