हारिद्रवेव पतथो वनेद उप सोमं सुतं महिषेवावा गच्छथः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च त्रिर वर्तिर यातम अश्विनाः ||
hāridraveva patatho vaned upa somaṁ sutam mahiṣevāva gacchathaḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca trir vartir yātam aśvinā ||
  • hāridraveva: This term can be broken down into "hāridra," which means "golden" or "yellow," and "veva," which implies "like" or "as." This suggests a quality of brightness or radiance.
  • patatho: This word translates to "of the path" or "of the way." It indicates a journey or movement along a specific route.
  • vaned: This term refers to "the forest" or "the wilderness," symbolizing nature and its untamed beauty.
  • upa: Meaning "towards" or "near," this word indicates direction or approach.
  • somaṁ: "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • sutam: This translates to "the son" or "the offspring," often referring to the results or products of a particular action.
  • mahiṣevāva: "Mahiṣa" means "great" or "powerful," and "eva" implies "indeed." This suggests a strong presence or influence.
  • gacchathaḥ: This is a command form meaning "go" or "move," indicating action or progression.
  • sajoṣasā: This term can be understood as "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting a positive emotional state.
  • uṣasā: Referring to "the dawn," this symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light.
  • sūryeṇa: This means "with the sun," indicating warmth, energy, and life-giving properties.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting ideas or elements.
  • trir: This translates to "three times," suggesting repetition or a cycle.
  • vartir: Meaning "to move" or "to flow," this indicates a continuous process or journey.
  • yātam: This means "to go" or "to travel," reinforcing the idea of movement.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the "Aśvins," the twin deities of medicine and health in Vedic mythology, symbolizing healing and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra speaks to the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of moving towards brightness and joy, akin to the rising sun that brings new beginnings. It encourages individuals to embrace the path of positivity and healing, much like the Aśvins who represent health and vitality. In real life, this can be applied to personal growth, where one seeks to move towards a brighter future, overcoming challenges with enthusiasm and support from the divine. The mantra serves as a reminder to pursue our goals with vigor and to remain open to the blessings that come with each new day.
