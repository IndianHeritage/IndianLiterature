द्युश्चिदस्यामवामं अहेः स्वनाद अयोयवीद भियसां वज्र इन्द्र ते |वृत्रस्य यद्बद्बधानस्य रोधसी मदे सुतस्य शवसाभिनच्छिरः ||
dyauś cid asyāmavām̐ aheḥ svanād ayoyavīd bhiyasā vajra indra te | vṛtrasya yad badbadhānasya rodasī made sutasya śavasābhinac chiraḥ ||
  • dyauś cid asyām avām̐ aheḥ svanād: This phrase can be interpreted as "the sky is indeed the sound of the great one." Here, "dyauś" refers to the sky or heavens, and "svaṇād" indicates sound or resonance. This suggests a connection between the divine and the cosmos, emphasizing the importance of celestial phenomena in our lives.
  • ayoyavīd bhiyasā vajra indra te: This translates to "may Indra, the wielder of the thunderbolt, protect us." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, storms, and protection. The term "vajra" symbolizes both power and the ability to overcome obstacles, akin to how a thunderbolt can clear the sky.
  • vṛtrasya yad badbadhānasya rodasī: This part means "the one who destroys Vritra, the cosmic serpent, in the sky." Vritra represents chaos and obstruction, and Indra's victory over him symbolizes the triumph of order over disorder. This can be likened to overcoming personal challenges in life, where one must confront and conquer their fears or obstacles.
  • made sutasya śavasābhinac chiraḥ: This translates to "may we be blessed with the long-lasting essence of the divine." "Made" refers to joy or bliss, while "sutasya" indicates offspring or progeny. This line emphasizes the importance of legacy and the continuity of joy and prosperity through generations.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and guidance in overcoming life's challenges. It highlights the relationship between the cosmos and the divine, urging individuals to seek strength and clarity in their endeavors.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be invoked during times of difficulty or uncertainty. For instance, when facing a significant life decision, such as a career change or personal challenge, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek inner strength and clarity. It can also be used in rituals or meditative practices to foster a sense of connection with the universe and divine protection.
