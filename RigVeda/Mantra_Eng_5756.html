मन्त्र: वाजिनीवती सूर्यस्य योषा चित्रामघा राया ईशे वसूनाम् | ऋषिष्टुतā जारयन्ती मघोन्य उषा उच्छति वह्निभिर गृणानā ||
vājinīvatī sūryasya yoṣā citrāmaghā rāya īśe vasūnām | ṛṣiṣṭutā jarayantī maghony uṣā ucchati vahnibhir gṛṇānā ||
  • vājinīvatī: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is swift and powerful," often associated with the idea of strength and vitality. In real life, this can be likened to a successful athlete or a leader who drives their team forward with energy and determination.
  • sūryasya: This means "of the sun." The sun is a symbol of life, energy, and illumination. In our daily lives, we can see the sun as a source of inspiration, reminding us to seek knowledge and enlightenment.
  • yoṣā: Translated as "woman" or "female," this term highlights the importance of femininity and the role of women in society. For example, women in leadership positions can inspire change and progress in various fields.
  • citrāmaghā: This can be understood as "the one who brings forth abundance and prosperity." In practical terms, this could refer to a successful entrepreneur who creates jobs and contributes to the economy.
  • rāya: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," this word emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual wealth in our lives. It reminds us to strive for a balanced life that includes both financial stability and personal fulfillment.
  • īśe: This translates to "to the lord" or "to the divine." It signifies a connection to a higher power or purpose, which can be seen in practices like meditation or prayer that help individuals find their path in life.
  • vasūnām: Meaning "of the treasures," this term refers to the various blessings and resources we have in life. It encourages us to appreciate and utilize our resources wisely, whether they are material possessions or personal talents.
  • ṛṣiṣṭutā: This can be interpreted as "the praise of the sages." It highlights the importance of wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations. In real life, this can be seen in the value of education and mentorship.
  • jarayantī: This means "nurturing" or "sustaining." It reflects the idea of care and support, akin to how parents nurture their children or how communities support one another.
  • maghony: This term can be understood as "of the generous" or "of the bountiful." It emphasizes the importance of generosity in our lives, encouraging us to give back to our communities and help those in need.
  • uṣā: Translated as "dawn," it symbolizes new beginnings and hope. In everyday life, this can represent the start of a new day filled with opportunities and possibilities.
  • ucchati: This means "rises" or "ascends," indicating growth and progress. It can be related to personal development, such as pursuing a new career or overcoming challenges.
  • vahnibhir: This translates to "by the fires," symbolizing transformation and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the challenges we face that ultimately lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • gṛṇānā: Meaning "singing" or "praising," this term reflects the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation in our lives. It can be seen in practices like journaling or sharing positive affirmations with others.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of strength, prosperity, femininity, and the connection to the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships, pursuing knowledge, and embracing new beginnings. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to strive for balance, generosity, and growth. By embodying these values, we can contribute positively to our communities and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
