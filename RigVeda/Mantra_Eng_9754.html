विषं गवां यातुधानाः पिबन्तु आ वृष्च्यन्ताम् अदितये दुरेवाः |परैणान देवः सविता ददातु परा भागम् ओषधीनां जयन्ताम् ||
viṣaṁ gavāṁ yātudhānāḥ pibantv ā vṛścyantām aditaye durevāḥ | parainān devaḥ savitā dadātu parā bhāgam oṣadhīnāṁ jayantām ||
  • viṣaṁ: This word refers to poison or harmful substances. In a broader sense, it can symbolize negativity or obstacles in life.
  • gavāṁ: This translates to "of the cows," which are often seen as symbols of nourishment, wealth, and prosperity in Vedic literature.
  • yātudhānāḥ: This term means "evil spirits" or "malevolent beings." It represents the challenges and adversities one may face.
  • pibantv: This means "may they drink." It suggests a desire for these negative forces to consume or be absorbed by something else.
  • ā vṛścyantām: This phrase can be interpreted as "may they be driven away." It expresses the wish for the removal of negativity and obstacles.
  • aditaye: This refers to "Aditi," the mother of the gods, symbolizing boundless space and the infinite. Aditi represents protection and nurturing.
  • durevāḥ: This means "difficult to reach" or "far away," indicating the challenges in overcoming negativity.
  • parainān: This translates to "may the divine" or "may the gods." It invokes divine intervention in our lives.
  • devaḥ: This means "god" or "deity," emphasizing the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges.
  • savitā: This refers to the sun god, Savitar, who represents energy, life, and illumination.
  • dadātu: This means "may he grant," expressing a wish for blessings and support.
  • parā bhāgam: This translates to "the highest share" or "the best portion," indicating a desire for the best outcomes in life.
  • oṣadhīnāṁ: This refers to "herbs" or "medicinal plants," symbolizing healing and nourishment.
  • jayantām: This means "may they thrive" or "may they be victorious," indicating a wish for success and prosperity.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer for protection and the removal of negativity and obstacles in life. It invokes divine support, particularly from Aditi and Savitar, to ensure that one receives the best outcomes and thrives in a nurturing environment. The significance of this mantra lies in its application to real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges, whether they be personal, professional, or spiritual. By reciting this mantra, one can seek the strength to overcome adversities and attract positive energies, much like how one would seek guidance and support from mentors or spiritual leaders in times of difficulty. It serves as a reminder of the importance of divine intervention and the power of positive thinking in achieving success and well-being.
