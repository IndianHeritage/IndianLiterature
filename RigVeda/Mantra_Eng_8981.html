परी वो विश्वतो दधातु ऊर्जां घृतेन पयसा |ये देवाः के च यज्ञियास ते रय्यां सं सृजन्तु नः ||
pari vo viśvato dadha ūrjā ghṛtena payasā | ye devāḥ ke ca yajñiyās te rayyā saṁ sṛjantu naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pari vo viśvato dadha ūrjā ghṛtena payasā | ye devāḥ ke ca yajñiyās te rayyā saṁ sṛjantu naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the energies (ūrjā) that are everywhere (viśvato) be bestowed upon us through the ghee (ghṛtena) and milk (payasā). May those deities (devāḥ) who are worthy of worship (yajñiyās) grant us prosperity (rayyā) and abundance."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari: around, everywhere
    • vo: to you (plural)
    • viśvato: from all sides, universally
    • dadha: bestow, grant
    • ūrjā: energy, strength, vitality
    • ghṛtena: with ghee, clarified butter
    • payasā: with milk
    • ye: who
    • devāḥ: deities, divine beings
    • ke ca: and those
    • yajñiyās: worthy of worship, those who are offered sacrifices
    • te: they
    • rayyā: prosperity, wealth, abundance
    • saṁ: together, in unison
    • sṛjantu: may they create, may they produce
    • naḥ: for us

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine energies and the role of deities in bestowing prosperity and abundance upon individuals and communities.
    • It highlights the connection between the physical offerings (like ghee and milk) and spiritual blessings, suggesting that material and spiritual realms are intertwined.
    • The invocation of deities signifies the need for reverence and gratitude towards higher powers that govern the universe.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies to invoke blessings for health, wealth, and success.
    • For example, during a family gathering or a festival, individuals may offer ghee and milk as part of their prayers, symbolizing their request for divine support in their endeavors.
    • In a broader context, communities can come together to perform collective rituals, fostering unity and shared intentions for prosperity.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual practices, and the need for collective efforts in seeking prosperity.
    • By acknowledging the divine and offering gratitude, individuals can cultivate a mindset that attracts abundance and positive energies into their lives.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a harmonious relationship with the universe, where both material and spiritual needs are recognized and fulfilled.
