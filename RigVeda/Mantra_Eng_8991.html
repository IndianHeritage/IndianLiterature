कृष्णः श्वेतो 'रुषो यामो अस्य ब्रध्न ऋज्र उता शोणो यशस्वान् |हिरण्यरूपं जनिता जजाना ||
kṛṣṇaḥ śveto 'ruṣo yāmo asya bradhna ṛjra uta śoṇo yaśasvān | hiraṇyarūpaṁ janitā jajāna ||
  • kṛṣṇaḥ: This word refers to the color black or dark, often associated with Lord Krishna, who embodies love, compassion, and divine playfulness.
  • śveto: Meaning white, this color symbolizes purity, peace, and spiritual enlightenment.
  • 'ruṣo: This term can be interpreted as red, representing passion, energy, and vitality.
  • yāmo: This word signifies the concept of restraint or control, often related to the practice of self-discipline.
  • asya: Meaning 'of this', it indicates possession or belonging.
  • bradhna: This term can be understood as 'the one who is strong' or 'powerful', often referring to a deity or a divine force.
  • ṛjra: This word signifies 'the one who is righteous' or 'truthful', emphasizing moral integrity.
  • uta: This is a conjunction meaning 'and' or 'also', linking ideas together.
  • śoṇo: Referring to the color red again, it can symbolize strength and courage.
  • yaśasvān: This term means 'one who is famous' or 'glorious', indicating a person of great renown.
  • hiraṇyarūpaṁ: This word translates to 'golden form', symbolizing wealth, prosperity, and divine beauty.
  • janitā: Meaning 'the one who gives birth' or 'creator', it refers to the source of life and existence.
  • jajāna: This term means 'has been born' or 'has come into being', indicating the manifestation of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of duality and the interplay of various qualities represented by colors and attributes. It highlights the importance of balance in life—embracing both the light (śveto) and the dark (kṛṣṇaḥ), the passionate (śoṇo) and the righteous (ṛjra). In real life, this can be applied to the concept of self-awareness and personal growth, where one must acknowledge and integrate different aspects of their personality. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength (bradhna) and glory (yaśasvān) come from understanding and harmonizing these diverse qualities within oneself. It encourages individuals to strive for a golden (hiraṇyarūpaṁ) existence, where they can create (janitā) and manifest (jajāna) their highest potential.
