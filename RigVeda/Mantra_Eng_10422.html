येनेंद्रो हविषा कृत्व्य अभवद् dyumny उत्तमः |इदं तद् अकृ देवाऽसपत्ना किलाभुवम् ||
yenendro haviṣā kṛtvy abhavad dyumny uttamaḥ | idaṁ tad akri devā asapatnā kilābhuvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yenendro: By whom the Lord Indra
    • haviṣā: With the offering
    • kṛtvy: Having performed
    • abhavad: Became
    • dyumny: Glorious
    • uttamaḥ: The best
    • idaṁ: This
    • tad: That
    • akri: Not done
    • devā: The gods
    • asapatnā: Without rivals
    • kilābhuvam: Indeed became
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power of Lord Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and the warrior spirit.
    • The phrase "by whom the Lord Indra became glorious with the offering" suggests that through proper offerings and devotion, one can attain greatness and divine favor.
    • The mention of "the gods without rivals" indicates a state of supreme achievement and recognition, where one stands out in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works diligently and presents innovative ideas can be seen as "becoming glorious" in the eyes of their superiors, much like Indra receiving offerings.
    • In sports, an athlete who trains hard and performs exceptionally well can be compared to Indra, achieving recognition and success without rivals.
    • In personal life, individuals who contribute positively to their community through selfless acts can also be seen as embodying the spirit of this mantra, gaining respect and admiration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of dedication, effort, and the right intentions in achieving greatness.
    • It serves as a reminder that through sincere offerings—whether they be in the form of hard work, creativity, or service—one can attain recognition and success.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal and professional growth, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
