तं īḻiṣ्व यॊ अर्चिषा वनā विश्वā परिष्वजत् |कृष्णā कृणोति जिव्हया ||
tam īḻiṣva yo arciṣā vanā viśvā pariṣvajat | kṛṣṇā kṛṇoti jihvayā ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • īḻiṣva - This can be interpreted as "may we worship" or "may we honor," indicating an act of reverence.
  • yo - Meaning "who," this word introduces a relative clause, connecting the divine to its attributes.
  • arciṣā - This word means "with light" or "with radiance," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and purity.
  • vanā - Translates to "the forest" or "the wilderness," representing nature and the interconnectedness of life.
  • viśvā - Meaning "all" or "universal," this word emphasizes inclusivity and the totality of existence.
  • pariṣvajat - This means "embracing" or "enveloping," suggesting a sense of unity and protection.
  • kṛṣṇā - This word refers to "black" or "dark," often associated with the divine, particularly in the context of Lord Krishna.
  • kṛṇoti - Meaning "creates" or "makes," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • jihvayā - This translates to "with the tongue," symbolizing communication, expression, and the power of words.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine force that embodies light, nature, and universality. It emphasizes the importance of worship and reverence towards this divine entity, which is associated with creation and communication.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "vanā" (forest) highlights the importance of nature. In today's world, where environmental issues are prevalent, this mantra can inspire individuals to respect and protect the natural world.
  • Unity and Inclusivity: The term "viśvā" (all) encourages a sense of community and belonging. In diverse societies, this mantra can serve as a reminder to embrace differences and work towards harmony.
  • Communication and Expression: The mention of "jihvayā" (with the tongue) underscores the significance of words. In personal relationships and professional settings, this can remind us of the power of communication in building connections and resolving conflicts.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of reverence towards the divine, and the power of communication. It encourages us to embrace nature, foster unity, and express ourselves thoughtfully in our daily lives.
