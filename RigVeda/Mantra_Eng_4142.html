उत स्य वाज्य अरुषस् तुविष्वणिर इह स्म धायि दर्शतः |मा वो यामेषु मरुतश्चिरं करत प्र तं रथेषु चोदत ||
uta sya vājy aruṣas tuviṣvaṇir iha sma dhāyi darśataḥ | mā vo yāmeṣu marutaś ciraṁ karat pra taṁ ratheṣu codata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • sya: of this
    • vājy: the one who is invoked
    • aruṣas: the red or bright one
    • tuviṣvaṇir: the one who is powerful
    • iha: here
    • sma: indeed
    • dhāyi: the one who supports
    • darśataḥ: of the seers
    • mā: may not
    • vo: to you
    • yāmeṣu: in the nights
    • marutaś: of the winds
    • ciraṁ: for a long time
    • karat: may do
    • pra: forward
    • taṁ: that
    • ratheṣu: in the chariots
    • codata: may inspire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly the powerful and bright ones, to support and guide us.
    • The phrase "may not to you in the nights" suggests a plea for protection and guidance during challenging times.
    • The reference to "winds" symbolizes change and movement, indicating the dynamic nature of life.
    • The mention of "chariots" can be seen as a metaphor for life's journey, where we seek inspiration and direction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, such as during a job search or a personal crisis, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual practices, akin to invoking divine support.
    • During significant life events, like weddings or graduations, people often pray for blessings and guidance, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In the context of teamwork, leaders may inspire their teams to navigate challenges, similar to the invocation of divine forces for support and direction.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; we can invoke higher powers for inspiration and direction.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to remain open to guidance from both spiritual and earthly sources, fostering resilience and adaptability in the face of life's challenges.
