एव सत्यं मगवानां युवं तद इन्द्रश च सोमोर्वम अश्व्यं गोः |आदर्दृतम अपिहितान्य अश्ना रिरिचथुः क्षाश चित्त तत्रिदानां ||
evā satyam maghavānā yuvaṁ tad indraś ca somorvam aśvyaṁ goḥ | ādardṛtam apihitāny aśnā riricathuḥ kṣāś cit tatṛdānā ||
  • evā satyam: This phrase translates to "this is the truth." It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness in life. In real life, being truthful can lead to trust and respect in relationships, whether personal or professional.
  • maghavānā yuvaṁ: "maghavānā" refers to the powerful or the mighty, and "yuvaṁ" means young or youthful. This can symbolize the energy and potential of youth. For example, young leaders in society often bring fresh ideas and vigor to social movements.
  • tad indraś ca somorvam aśvyaṁ goḥ: "indra" refers to the king of gods, "somorvam" relates to the essence of soma (a sacred drink), "aśvyaṁ" means horses, and "goḥ" refers to cows. This line can be interpreted as a call for abundance and prosperity, as horses and cows were symbols of wealth in ancient times. In modern contexts, this can relate to the importance of resources and wealth in achieving success.
  • ādardṛtam apihitāny: This means "those that are hidden or concealed." It suggests that there are truths or resources that may not be immediately visible. In life, this can relate to the idea that opportunities often lie beneath the surface and require effort to uncover.
  • aśnā riricathuḥ: "aśnā" means to consume or take in, and "riricathuḥ" can be interpreted as a desire or longing. This can reflect the human condition of seeking fulfillment and satisfaction in life, whether through knowledge, experiences, or material possessions.
  • kṣāś cit tatṛdānā: "kṣāś" refers to the earth or ground, and "tatṛdānā" can mean to be nourished or sustained. This emphasizes the connection between humanity and the earth, highlighting the importance of sustainability and care for our environment. In real life, this can be seen in movements towards environmental conservation and sustainable living.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of truth, youth, abundance, hidden opportunities, human desires, and the connection to the earth. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek truth, harness youthful energy for positive change, recognize the value of resources, uncover hidden opportunities, and maintain a sustainable relationship with our environment. In today's world, these principles can guide individuals and communities towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
