उपो षु जातम apturaṁ गोभिर भङ्गम् परिष्कृतम् |इन्दुं देवाऽयासिषुः ||
upo ṣu jātam apturaṁ gobhir bhaṅgam pariṣkṛtam | induṁ devā ayāsiṣuḥ ||
  • Upo: This word translates to "from above" or "from the higher realms." It signifies a connection to the divine or higher consciousness.
  • Ṣu: This term can mean "to be well" or "to be good," indicating a state of well-being or auspiciousness.
  • Jātam: Meaning "born" or "created," it refers to the emergence of something new or the manifestation of a particular state.
  • Apturaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "that which is attained" or "achieved," suggesting fulfillment or the realization of desires.
  • Gobhih: This term relates to "cows" or "wealth," often symbolizing prosperity and abundance in Vedic literature.
  • Bhaṅgam: Meaning "broken" or "disrupted," it can refer to obstacles or challenges that need to be overcome.
  • Pariṣkṛtam: This word means "refined" or "purified," indicating a process of improvement or enhancement.
  • Induṁ: This term refers to "the moon" or "nectar," symbolizing nourishment, beauty, and the soothing qualities of nature.
  • Devā: Meaning "gods" or "divine beings," it signifies the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • Ayāsiṣuḥ: This word can be interpreted as "you have come" or "you have arrived," indicating the presence or arrival of divine energy or blessings.

The mantra can be understood as a call for divine blessings and the attainment of prosperity and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles and refining one's life to achieve a state of fulfillment and abundance. The imagery of cows represents wealth, while the moon symbolizes nourishment and beauty, suggesting that true prosperity encompasses both material and spiritual aspects.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their goals and aspirations, seeking divine guidance to overcome challenges and attain their desires.
  • In business, entrepreneurs can use this mantra to invoke prosperity and success, focusing on refining their strategies and overcoming obstacles in their path.
  • In community settings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to work together harmoniously, seeking collective well-being and abundance for all.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to prosperity, emphasizing the need for both material success and spiritual fulfillment. By invoking divine blessings and striving for refinement in our lives, we can navigate challenges and achieve a state of well-being that benefits not only ourselves but also those around us.
