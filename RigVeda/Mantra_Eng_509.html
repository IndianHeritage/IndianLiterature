मन्त्र: कद् रुद्राय प्रचेतसे मीळ्हुष्टमाय तव्यसे | वोचेम शान्तमं हृदे ||
kad rudrāya pracetase mīḻhuṣṭamāya tavyase | vocema śaṁtamaṁ hṛde ||
  • Mantra: "kad rudrāya pracetase mīḻhuṣṭamāya tavyase | vocema śaṁtamaṁ hṛde ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: When
    • rudrāya: to Rudra (a form of Shiva, representing the storm and the hunt)
    • pracetase: to the one who is all-knowing or has great intellect
    • mīḻhuṣṭamāya: to the one who is most auspicious or beneficial
    • tavyase: to the one who is worthy of worship
    • vocema: we speak or we invoke
    • śaṁtamaṁ: the most peaceful or tranquil
    • hṛde: in the heart
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Rudra, invoking his auspicious and peaceful nature.
    • It emphasizes the importance of calling upon divine intelligence and benevolence in our lives.
    • The phrase "in the heart" suggests that this invocation is not just verbal but should resonate deeply within us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of distress or confusion, individuals may seek guidance through meditation or prayer, similar to invoking Rudra for clarity and peace.
    • In a community setting, when facing challenges, people often come together to seek collective wisdom and support, reflecting the essence of invoking a higher power for guidance.
    • In personal relationships, when conflicts arise, individuals may strive to approach the situation with a peaceful heart, embodying the mantra's call for tranquility.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our lives.
    • It teaches us to cultivate peace and auspiciousness in our hearts, which can lead to better decision-making and harmonious relationships.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through meditation, prayer, and conscious efforts to maintain peace and understanding in our interactions with others.
