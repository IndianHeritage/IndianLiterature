यो विश्वतः सुप्रतीकः सदृङ्ग् असि दूरे चित् तळिद् इवाति रोचसे |रात्र्याश् चिद् अन्धो अति देव पश्यस्य अग्ने सख्ये मा ऋषामाः वयं तव ||
yo viśvataḥ supratīkaḥ sadṛṅṅ asi dūre cit san taḻid ivāti rocase | rātryāś cid andho ati deva paśyasy agne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • viśvataḥ: from all directions
    • supratīkaḥ: well-known or well-formed
    • sadṛṅṅ: like or similar
    • asi: you are
    • dūre: far away
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • san: being or existence
    • taḻid: like lightning
    • ivāti: as it moves
    • rocase: shining or illuminating
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is omnipresent and illuminating, akin to the light of consciousness that dispels darkness.
    • The reference to "lightning" symbolizes sudden insight or enlightenment that can occur in our lives.
    • The phrase "from all directions" suggests that this divine energy is accessible to everyone, regardless of their circumstances.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of confusion or despair, individuals often seek clarity. This mantra reminds us that enlightenment can come from unexpected places, much like a flash of lightning in a storm.
    • In a team setting, when brainstorming ideas, the collective consciousness of the group can lead to innovative solutions that shine brightly, illuminating the path forward.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the omnipresence of divine consciousness and the potential for enlightenment in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek awareness and clarity, especially in challenging situations, and to recognize that help and insight can come from various sources.
    • This mantra can be applicable in daily life, particularly in moments of decision-making, problem-solving, or when one feels lost, serving as a reminder to remain open to the light of understanding.
