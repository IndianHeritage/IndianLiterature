तस्माद्यज्ञात्सर्वहुत ऋचः सामानि जज्निरे। छन्दांसि जज्निरे तस्माद्यजुस् तस्मादजायत।।
tasmād yajñāt sarvahuta ṛcaḥ sāmāni jajñire | chandāṁsi jajñire tasmād yajus tasmād ajāyata ||
  • tasmād yajñāt: This phrase translates to "from that sacrifice." In the context of the Rigveda, yajña (sacrifice) is a central theme, representing the act of offering to the divine. It signifies the importance of selfless actions and contributions to the greater good.
  • sarvahuta ṛcaḥ: This means "all the hymns (ṛca) were born." Here, ṛca refers to the sacred verses or hymns that are recited during rituals. The idea is that these hymns emerge from the act of sacrifice, emphasizing the connection between devotion and the creation of spiritual knowledge.
  • sāmāni jajñire: This translates to "the melodies (sāmāni) were born." Sāmāni are the musical chants that accompany the hymns. This indicates that music and harmony arise from the same source as the hymns, highlighting the integral relationship between sound, spirituality, and ritual.
  • chandāṁsi jajñire: This means "the meters (chandāṁsi) were born." In Vedic literature, chandāṁsi refers to the rhythmic patterns of the verses. This suggests that the structure and form of poetry and music are also derived from the act of sacrifice, reinforcing the idea that creativity is rooted in spiritual practice.
  • tasmād yajus: This translates to "from that, the yajus (sacred texts) emerged." Yajus refers to the prose mantras used in rituals. This indicates that the knowledge and guidelines for performing rituals also stem from the act of sacrifice, showing the comprehensive nature of spiritual wisdom.
  • tasmād ajāyata: This means "from that, it was born." This concluding phrase encapsulates the essence of creation and manifestation that arises from the act of sacrifice, suggesting that all forms of knowledge, art, and spirituality are interconnected and originate from a higher source.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of yajña (sacrifice) as the foundation of various forms of knowledge, creativity, and spirituality. In real life, this can be applied to the idea that selfless actions and contributions to society lead to the emergence of wisdom, art, and harmony. For instance, community service can be seen as a modern form of yajña, where individuals come together to support a cause, leading to the creation of a more harmonious society. The mantra teaches us that our actions, especially those that benefit others, have the power to create a ripple effect of positive outcomes in various aspects of life, including art, music, and spiritual growth.
