वृष्णः कोशः पवते मध्वा ऊर्मिर वृषभान्नाय वृषभाय पातवे |वृषणाध्वर्यू वृषभासो अद्रयो वृषाणं सोमं वृषभाय सुष्वति ||
vṛṣṇaḥ kośaḥ pavate madhva ūrmir vṛṣabhānnāya vṛṣabhāya pātave | vṛṣaṇādhvaryū vṛṣabhāso adrayo vṛṣaṇaṁ somaṁ vṛṣabhāya suṣvati ||
  • vṛṣṇaḥ kośaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the wealth of strength" or "the treasure of vigor." In real life, this can relate to the importance of physical and mental strength in achieving success. For example, athletes train rigorously to build their physical strength, which is essential for their performance.
  • pavate: This word means "to blow" or "to purify." It signifies the cleansing aspect of air or wind. In a practical sense, this can be related to the importance of fresh air and its role in maintaining health. For instance, spending time in nature can rejuvenate the mind and body.
  • madhva ūrmir: "Madhva" refers to sweetness or pleasantness, while "ūrmir" means wave or surge. Together, they can symbolize the sweet waves of life or the joys that come in waves. This can be seen in moments of happiness that come unexpectedly, like the joy of receiving good news or achieving a long-desired goal.
  • vṛṣabhānnāya: This can be interpreted as "to the strong one" or "to the bull." The bull is often a symbol of strength and hard work. In real life, this can be related to individuals who embody these qualities, such as leaders or mentors who guide others through challenges.
  • vṛṣabhāya pātave: This phrase means "to the one who protects strength." It emphasizes the importance of safeguarding one's strength and resources. In a modern context, this can relate to financial planning and the need to protect one's assets for future stability.
  • vṛṣaṇādhvaryū: This term can be understood as "the one who performs rituals with strength." It highlights the significance of rituals in various cultures. For example, wedding ceremonies often involve rituals that strengthen the bond between partners.
  • vṛṣabhāso adrayo: This phrase can mean "the strong one who resides in the mountains." Mountains symbolize stability and endurance. In real life, this can relate to individuals who are steadfast in their beliefs and values, much like a mentor who provides guidance through difficult times.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ somaṁ: "Soma" is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality or enlightenment. This can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. In practical terms, this can relate to education and lifelong learning as a means to achieve personal growth.
  • vṛṣabhāya suṣvati: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the strong one who brings prosperity." It emphasizes the connection between strength and success. In real life, this can be seen in entrepreneurs who leverage their strengths to build successful businesses that contribute to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, protection, and the pursuit of prosperity. It highlights the importance of physical and mental vigor, the cleansing power of nature, and the role of rituals in our lives. In real-life scenarios, these themes can be applied to personal development, health, financial planning, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges and work towards achieving their goals with resilience and purpose.
