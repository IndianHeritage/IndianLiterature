इहागतम् वृषण्वसुः श्रिणुतम् मा इमं हवम् |अन्ति षड्भूत वाम अवः ||
ihā gataṁ vṛṣaṇvasū śṛṇutam ma imaṁ havam | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ihā gataṁ vṛṣaṇvasū śṛṇutam ma imaṁ havam | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ihā: here, in this place
    • gataṁ: gone, arrived
    • vṛṣaṇvasū: the powerful, the strong
    • śṛṇutam: listen, hear
    • ma: my
    • imaṁ: this
    • havam: offering, invocation
    • anti: may they come
    • ṣad: six
    • bhūtu: beings, entities
    • vām: you, your
    • avaḥ: bring, lead
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is an invocation to the powerful beings present in the environment.
    • The speaker is calling upon these entities to listen to their offerings and to be present in their life.
    • The mention of "six beings" could symbolize various aspects of existence or different energies that one seeks to harmonize with.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be used during meditation or prayer, where an individual seeks guidance and support from higher powers or energies.
    • In a community setting, it can be chanted during rituals or gatherings to invoke collective strength and unity among participants.
    • In a professional environment, it can serve as a reminder to listen to diverse perspectives and collaborate effectively with colleagues.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for connection with powerful energies and beings that can aid in personal and collective growth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and open to guidance, which is applicable in various life scenarios, from personal development to community building.
    • By invoking these energies, individuals can foster a sense of purpose and direction in their lives, making it a valuable tool for spiritual and practical applications.
