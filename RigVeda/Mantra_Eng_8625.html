इन्द्राय सोम पातवे वृतघ्नै परि षिच्यसे |नरे च दक्षिणावत देवाय सदनासदे ||
indrāya soma pātave vṛtraghne pari ṣicyase | nare ca dakṣiṇāvate devāya sadanāsade ||
  • Indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
  • Soma: Soma is both a deity and a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Pātave: This term means "to drink" or "to consume," indicating the act of offering Soma to Indra.
  • Vṛtraghne: This translates to "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra is a serpent or dragon representing drought and obstacles. Indra is known for defeating Vritra, thus bringing rain and fertility.
  • Pari ṣicyase: This means "you are being poured" or "you are being anointed," suggesting a ritualistic offering to Indra.
  • Nare: This word means "to man" or "to human beings," indicating the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • Ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the various elements of the mantra.
  • Dakṣiṇāvate: This term refers to "the one who is favorable" or "the one who grants prosperity," emphasizing the benevolent nature of the divine.
  • Devāya: This means "to the deity," reinforcing the focus on divine worship and reverence.
  • Sadanāsade: This translates to "the one who resides in the abode," indicating the divine presence in the sacred space or the heart of the devotee.

The mantra essentially invokes Indra, asking him to partake of the Soma offering, which symbolizes the nourishment of the soul and the removal of obstacles (represented by Vritra). It highlights the relationship between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the need for divine favor and blessings for prosperity and well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Offerings: In Hindu rituals, offerings to deities are made to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and success. This mantra can be recited during such offerings to invoke divine grace.
  • Overcoming Challenges: Just as Indra defeated Vritra, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to overcome personal challenges and obstacles in their lives, seeking strength and support from the divine.
  • Community and Cooperation: The mention of "nare" emphasizes the importance of community. In modern society, this can translate to working together for common goals, invoking divine support for collective efforts.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful invocation for divine assistance, emphasizing the importance of rituals, community, and the pursuit of overcoming life's challenges. It reminds us of the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms, encouraging us to seek blessings for both personal and communal prosperity.
