उभा पिबताम अश्विनोभा नः शर्म यच्छताम् |अविद्रियाभिर ऊतिभिः ||
ubhā pibatam aśvinobhā naḥ śarma yacchatam | avidriyābhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ubhā pibatam aśvinobhā naḥ śarma yacchatam | avidriyābhir ūtibhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the two Ashvins, who are the divine physicians, drink our offerings and grant us protection and well-being with their healing powers."
  • Explanation:
    • Ubhā: This word means "two" or "both," referring to the duality of the Ashvins, who are often depicted as twin gods in Vedic literature.
    • Pibatam: This translates to "may they drink," indicating a request for the Ashvins to accept the offerings made to them.
    • Aśvinobhā: Refers to the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They are considered divine healers and are often invoked for their benevolence.
    • Naḥ: This means "our," indicating a personal connection and a plea for assistance directed towards the Ashvins.
    • Śarma: Translates to "protection" or "well-being," highlighting the desire for safety and health in life.
    • Yacchatam: This means "may they grant," expressing a wish or prayer for the blessings of the Ashvins.
    • Avidriyābhir: This word can be interpreted as "with healing powers" or "with remedies," emphasizing the Ashvins' role as healers.
    • Ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "with strength" or "with vigor," suggesting a request for vitality and robustness in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, we often seek the help of doctors and healthcare professionals, similar to how the Vedic people sought the blessings of the Ashvins for health and healing.
    • During times of illness or distress, people may turn to rituals or prayers for protection and recovery, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In various cultures, invoking divine assistance for health and well-being is common, akin to the invocation of the Ashvins in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine help for health and protection, emphasizing the belief in a higher power that can aid in our well-being.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals face health challenges or seek guidance and support, reminding us of the interconnectedness of spirituality and physical health.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, we acknowledge the need for both physical and spiritual healing, which is relevant in today's fast-paced and often stressful world.
