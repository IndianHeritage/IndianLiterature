अर्वाङ् एहि सोमकामं त्वाहुर अयं सुतस् तस्य पिबा मदाय |उरुव्यचā जटहर आ वृषस्व पितेव नः श्रिणुहि हूयमानः ||
arvāṅ ehi somakāmaṁ tvāhur ayaṁ sutas tasya pibā madāya | uruvyacā jaṭhara ā vṛṣasva piteva naḥ śṛṇuhi hūyamānaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "arvāṅ ehi somakāmaṁ tvāhur ayaṁ sutas tasya pibā madāya | uruvyacā jaṭhara ā vṛṣasva piteva naḥ śṛṇuhi hūyamānaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a call to the divine, inviting the essence of the soma (a sacred drink) to come forth. It expresses a desire for abundance and nourishment, asking for the blessings of prosperity and vitality.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • arvāṅ: Inviting or calling forth.
    • ehi: Come here or approach.
    • somakāmaṁ: The essence of soma, which symbolizes immortality and divine bliss.
    • tvāhur: We call upon you.
    • ayaṁ: This.
    • sutas: The drink or essence.
    • tasya: Of that.
    • pibā: Drink or consume.
    • madāya: For intoxication or bliss.
    • uruvyacā: With great abundance.
    • jaṭhara: The belly or womb, symbolizing nourishment.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • vṛṣasva: Be strong or powerful.
    • piteva: Like a father.
    • naḥ: Our.
    • śṛṇuhi: Listen or hear.
    • hūyamānaḥ: Being invoked or called upon.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies that seek blessings for prosperity, such as housewarming or starting a new business.
    • In personal life, individuals may chant this mantra when seeking strength and support during challenging times, invoking a sense of divine presence and guidance.
    • In communal gatherings, such as festivals, this mantra can be used to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, inviting abundance for all participants.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine abundance and nourishment. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of seeking blessings for strength and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal challenges to communal celebrations, reinforcing the idea that we are all part of a larger cosmic order that supports our growth and well-being.
