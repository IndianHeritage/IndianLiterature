अतश्चिदिन्द्र न उपा याहि शतवाजया |इषा सहस्रवाजया ||
ataś cid indra ṇa upā yāhi śatavājayā | iṣā sahasravājayā ||
  • ataś: This word translates to "from here" or "from this point." It signifies a starting point or a foundation for action.
  • cid: Meaning "indeed" or "certainly," it emphasizes the certainty of the statement being made.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the king of gods. He represents leadership and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • ṇa: This is a particle that can denote negation or a question, often used to seek clarity or affirmation.
  • upā: This word means "to come" or "to approach," indicating a movement towards something or someone.
  • yāhi: This is a command form of the verb "to go," suggesting an invitation or a call to action.
  • śatavājayā: This term can be broken down into "śata," meaning "hundred," and "vājayā," which relates to "victory" or "strength." Together, it implies a call for a hundred victories or a multitude of strengths.
  • iṣā: This word means "power" or "authority," indicating a source of strength or capability.
  • sahasravājayā: Similar to the previous term, "sahasra" means "thousand," and "vājayā" again relates to "victory." This suggests an even greater call for strength and triumph, emphasizing abundance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, asking him to come forth and bestow upon the seeker the strength and victories that are abundant and powerful. It reflects a desire for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, one can invoke the spirit of Indra, seeking strength and resilience to overcome obstacles.
  • Leadership Roles: For those in positions of leadership, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance and strength from higher powers to lead effectively and achieve collective victories.
  • Competitive Situations: In competitive environments, such as sports or business, the mantra can be a source of motivation, encouraging individuals to strive for multiple victories and harness their inner strength.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and the pursuit of numerous victories. It serves as a reminder that, with the right mindset and support, one can overcome challenges and achieve greatness in various aspects of life. By calling upon Indra, individuals can find the courage and power needed to navigate their journeys successfully.
