इमं जुषस्व गिरवणः समुद्र इव पिन्वते |इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिर ववक्षिथ ||
imaṁ juṣasva girvaṇaḥ samudra iva pinvate | indra viśvābhir ūtibhir vavakṣitha ||
  • imaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current situation.
  • juṣasva - This means "enjoy" or "take pleasure in," suggesting an invitation to appreciate or embrace something.
  • girvaṇaḥ - Refers to "the singers" or "the poets," often associated with the divine or the wise who convey knowledge through hymns.
  • samudra - This translates to "ocean," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • iva - Meaning "like" or "as," it draws a comparison, enhancing the imagery of the ocean.
  • pinvate - This means "to flow" or "to pour," indicating abundance and the act of sharing or giving.
  • indra - Refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • viśvābhir - This means "with all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the idea of collective strength.
  • ūtibhir - Translates to "with powers" or "with energies," indicating the various forces at play in the universe.
  • vavakṣitha - This means "you have spoken" or "you have declared," indicating the act of communication or the sharing of wisdom.

The mantra can be interpreted as an invocation to enjoy the present moment, much like the vast ocean that flows abundantly. It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and the sharing of wisdom among individuals, akin to the way poets and singers convey knowledge through their art. The reference to Indra suggests a call for leadership and strength, encouraging individuals to harness their inner power and work together for a common purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In teamwork settings, where collaboration and shared goals are essential for success.
  • In personal development, encouraging individuals to embrace their strengths and enjoy the journey of life.
  • In community building, fostering a sense of unity and collective effort towards common objectives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of enjoying the present, embracing collective strength, and sharing wisdom. It encourages individuals to recognize their interconnectedness and work together harmoniously, much like the vast ocean that nourishes all life. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to community engagement, promoting a holistic approach to living.
