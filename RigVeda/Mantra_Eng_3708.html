उभे सुचन्द्र सर्पिषो दर्वी श्रीणीष आसनिः |उतो न उत पूपूRYā उक्तेषु शवसस पत इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
ubhe suścandra sarpiṣo darvī śrīṇīṣa āsani | uto na ut pupūryā uktheṣu śavasas pata iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Mantra: "ubhe suścandra sarpiṣo darvī śrīṇīṣa āsani | uto na ut pupūryā uktheṣu śavasas pata iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ubhe: both
    • suścandra: auspicious moon
    • sarpiṣo: of ghee (clarified butter)
    • darvī: the sacred grass (often used in rituals)
    • śrīṇīṣa: prosperity or abundance
    • āsani: may it be seated or established
    • uto: may it be
    • na: not
    • ut: or
    • pupūryā: may it be filled
    • uktheṣu: in the utterances or invocations
    • śavasas: of the dead or the departed
    • pata: path or way
    • iṣaṁ: the desired
    • stotṛbhya: to the worshippers or those who praise
    • ā bhara: may it be brought or filled
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of both the auspicious moon and the sacred ghee, symbolizing nourishment and prosperity.
    • The mention of "darvī" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, emphasizing the importance of purity and sanctity in offerings.
    • It expresses a desire for abundance and prosperity to be established in one's life, highlighting the significance of spiritual and material wealth.
    • The phrase "may it be filled" suggests a longing for fulfillment in both spiritual and worldly aspects, indicating a balance between the two.
    • By addressing the departed, it acknowledges the connection between the living and the ancestors, emphasizing respect and remembrance.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to bring forth blessings to those who praise, reinforcing the idea that devotion and gratitude attract divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family gathering, invoking this mantra can create a sense of unity and abundance, reminding everyone of the importance of togetherness and shared prosperity.
    • During a religious ceremony, chanting this mantra can enhance the spiritual atmosphere, inviting blessings for health, wealth, and harmony.
    • In personal meditation, reflecting on the meanings of this mantra can help individuals focus on their goals, fostering a mindset of abundance and gratitude.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, prosperity, and spirituality.
    • Its significance lies in the balance it promotes between material and spiritual wealth, encouraging individuals to seek fulfillment in all aspects of life.
    • By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, one can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and abundance, attracting positive energies and blessings.
