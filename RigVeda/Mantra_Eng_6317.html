स्तोता यत ते विचर्षणिर अतिप्रशर्धयद गिरः |वयाः इवाऽनु रोहते जुषन्त यत ||
stotā yat te vicarṣaṇir atipraśardhayad giraḥ | vayā ivānu rohate juṣanta yat ||
  • Stotā: This word refers to a "praise" or "hymn." It signifies the act of offering reverence or commendation to a deity or a higher power.
  • Yat: This translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection to what follows in the mantra.
  • Te: Meaning "to you," this word establishes a direct address to the divine or the subject of praise.
  • Vicarṣaṇir: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who moves swiftly." It denotes strength and agility.
  • Atipraśardhayad: This word suggests "having great energy" or "exceeding in vigor." It emphasizes the intensity of the divine presence being addressed.
  • Giraḥ: Meaning "words" or "speech," this term highlights the importance of communication and expression in the context of devotion.
  • Vayā: This translates to "like the wind," symbolizing freedom, movement, and the intangible nature of the divine.
  • Ivānu: Meaning "as if" or "like," it draws a comparison to something familiar, enhancing understanding.
  • Rohate: This word means "to rise" or "to ascend," indicating growth, elevation, or the uplifting nature of the divine.
  • Juṣanta: This translates to "delighting" or "enjoying," suggesting a sense of joy and fulfillment derived from the divine presence.
  • Yat: Again, this word means "that" or "which," reinforcing the connection to the preceding phrases.

The mantra can be interpreted as a hymn that praises the divine for its powerful and energetic nature, likening it to the wind that rises and brings joy. It emphasizes the importance of words and communication in expressing devotion and reverence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to connect with the divine energy and seek inspiration.
  • Motivation: The idea of rising like the wind can serve as a metaphor for overcoming challenges and striving for personal growth.
  • Communication: The emphasis on words highlights the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation in relationships, fostering deeper connections.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize and celebrate the divine energy in their lives. By acknowledging the power of words and the uplifting nature of the divine, one can cultivate a sense of joy, motivation, and connection in everyday experiences.
