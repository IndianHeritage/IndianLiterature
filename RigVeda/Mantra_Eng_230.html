तīv्राः सोमास आ गह्य आशीर्वन्तः सुताः इमे | वायो तान प्रस्थितान पिब ||
tīvrāḥ somāsa ā gahy āśīrvantaḥ sutā ime | vāyo tān prasthitān piba ||
  • tīvrāḥ: This word translates to "intense" or "powerful." It signifies strength and vigor.
  • somāsa: Refers to "the drink of immortality," often associated with the sacred soma plant, which is believed to bestow vitality and divine qualities.
  • ā gahy: This means "having been taken" or "having been received," indicating the act of accepting or embracing something.
  • āśīrvantaḥ: Translates to "those who bless" or "those who confer blessings," referring to the divine or auspicious forces that provide support and prosperity.
  • sutā: This word means "sons" or "offspring," often used to denote the progeny or descendants who carry forward the lineage.
  • ime: This translates to "these," referring to the subjects or entities being discussed.
  • vāyo: Means "O Wind" or "O Vital Force," representing the life-giving energy that sustains all beings.
  • tān: This means "them," referring to the subjects previously mentioned.
  • prasthitān: Translates to "those who have set forth" or "those who have departed," indicating movement or progression.
  • piba: This means "drink" or "consume," suggesting the act of taking in or absorbing something vital.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to embrace the powerful blessings of life and vitality, symbolized by the sacred soma. It emphasizes the importance of receiving blessings from divine forces and recognizing the vital energy that propels us forward in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of existence:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of receiving blessings, individuals can seek guidance and support from mentors or spiritual leaders to enhance their personal development.
  • Community Support: The reference to "sons" can symbolize the importance of family and community. It highlights the need for collective support in achieving common goals.
  • Health and Well-being: The concept of consuming the "drink of immortality" can be related to the pursuit of health and wellness, encouraging individuals to adopt lifestyles that promote vitality.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of "O Wind" reminds us of the vital forces of nature. It calls for respect and care for the environment, recognizing our interconnectedness with the earth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for embracing the blessings of life, recognizing the vital forces that propel us forward, and fostering a sense of community and support. It serves as a reminder to seek vitality, both physically and spiritually, and to honor the connections we share with others and the environment. In a world that often feels disconnected, this mantra encourages us to cultivate relationships and embrace the blessings that life offers.
