अर्चा शक्राय शाकिने शचिवते श्रिण्वन्तं इन्द्रं महायन्न अभि ष्टुहि |यो धृष्णुना शवसा रोडसी उभे वृषा वृषत्वा वृषभो न्यृञ्जते ||
arcā śakrāya śākine śacīvate śṛṇvantam indram mahayann abhi ṣṭuhi | yo dhṛṣṇunā śavasā rodasī ubhe vṛṣā vṛṣatvā vṛṣabho nyṛñjate ||
  • arcā - This word refers to worship or invocation. It signifies the act of honoring or calling upon a deity.
  • śakrāya - This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, known for his strength and power.
  • śākine - This term can be interpreted as the one who possesses strength or energy, emphasizing Indra's dynamic nature.
  • śacīvate - This word means to be powerful or to have the ability to act, highlighting the capability of the deity being invoked.
  • śṛṇvantam - This means 'listening' or 'hearing', indicating the responsiveness of the divine to the prayers of the devotees.
  • indram - Again, this refers to Indra, reinforcing the focus on this particular deity.
  • mahayann - This term means 'great' or 'magnificent', suggesting the grandeur of the deity's actions.
  • abhi - This preposition means 'towards' or 'in the direction of', indicating the focus of the invocation.
  • ṣṭuhi - This means 'praise' or 'worship', emphasizing the act of honoring the deity.
  • yo - This word means 'who', introducing a relative clause that describes the qualities of the deity.
  • dhṛṣṇunā - This term refers to 'strength' or 'courage', highlighting the valor associated with the deity.
  • śavasā - This means 'with vigor' or 'with energy', further emphasizing the dynamic nature of the divine.
  • rodasī - This word refers to the 'two worlds' or 'the heavens and the earth', indicating the vastness of the deity's influence.
  • ubhe - This means 'both', reinforcing the idea of encompassing all realms.
  • vṛṣā - This term can mean 'bull' or 'strength', symbolizing power and fertility.
  • vṛṣatvā - This refers to 'the quality of being strong' or 'powerful', again emphasizing the strength of the deity.
  • vṛṣabho - This means 'the best among the strong', indicating Indra's supreme position among deities.
  • nyṛñjate - This means 'to be invoked' or 'to be called upon', indicating the act of summoning the divine presence.

The mantra essentially invokes Indra, praising his strength and power while acknowledging his responsiveness to the prayers of the devotees. It highlights the importance of worship and the belief that the divine listens to the calls of the faithful.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios where individuals seek strength and support in their endeavors. For instance:

  • In times of personal struggle, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke courage and resilience.
  • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to seek blessings for collective strength and unity.
  • In professional settings, it can serve as a reminder to harness one's inner strength and capabilities when facing challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine support available to individuals in their pursuits. It emphasizes the significance of invoking strength and courage, both in personal and communal contexts, reinforcing the belief that the divine is always present to assist those who seek it.
