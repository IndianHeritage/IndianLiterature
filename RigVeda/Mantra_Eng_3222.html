विष्वानि शक्रो नार्याणि विद्वान् अपो रिरेच सकिभिर निकामैः |अश्मानं चिद् ये बिभिदुर वचोभिर व्रजं गोमन्तं उशिजो वि ववृुः ||
viśvāni śakro naryāṇi vidvān apo rireca sakhibhir nikāmaiḥ | aśmānaṁ cid ye bibhidur vacobhir vrajaṁ gomantam uśijo vi vavruḥ ||
  • viśvāni: This word translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • śakro: Referring to "Shakra," a deity associated with strength and power, often identified with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • naryāṇi: This term means "the noble ones" or "the virtuous." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and moral integrity.
  • vidvān: Meaning "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," it highlights the value of wisdom and understanding in life.
  • apo: This word means "water," symbolizing purity, life, and sustenance.
  • rireca: Translates to "he has poured" or "he has bestowed," indicating the act of giving or sharing.
  • sakhibhir: Meaning "with friends" or "companions," it underscores the importance of community and relationships.
  • nikāmaiḥ: This term means "to gather" or "to assemble," suggesting the act of coming together for a common purpose.
  • aśmānaṁ: Referring to "stones" or "rocks," it symbolizes strength and stability.
  • cid: Meaning "that which is," it denotes existence or consciousness.
  • ye: This word means "those who," indicating a group or collective.
  • bibhidur: Translates to "who are aware" or "who understand," emphasizing the importance of awareness and knowledge.
  • vacobhir: Meaning "by words," it signifies the power of speech and communication.
  • vrajaṁ: This term means "the path" or "the way," indicating a journey or direction in life.
  • gomantam: Referring to "the one who possesses cows," it symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and nourishment.
  • uśijo: Meaning "the wise ones" or "the learned," it again emphasizes the value of wisdom.
  • vi: A prefix meaning "in all" or "throughout," reinforcing the idea of universality.
  • vavruḥ: Translates to "they have come" or "they have arrived," indicating the manifestation of ideas or beings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and community. It calls upon the divine to bestow strength and knowledge upon all beings, encouraging individuals to come together in harmony. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and fostering relationships based on mutual respect and understanding. The significance lies in recognizing that our collective strength and wisdom can lead to a more prosperous and harmonious existence, much like how a community thrives when its members support one another. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the power of collaboration in achieving common goals.
