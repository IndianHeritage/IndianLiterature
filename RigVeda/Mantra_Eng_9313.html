सो चिन् नु सख्या नर्य इनाḥ स्तुतश चर्कृत्य इन्द्रो मावते नरे |विश्वासु धूर्षु वाजकृत्येषु सत्पते वृत्रे वाप्स्व अभि शूर मन्दसे ||
so cin nu sakhyā narya inaḥ stutaś carkṛtya indro māvate nare | viśvāsu dhūrṣu vājakṛtyeṣu satpate vṛtre vāpsv a1bhi śūra mandase ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we, through our actions, be praised by Indra, the mighty one, who is the protector of the brave and the one who grants strength in our endeavors."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • so cin nu: "May we" - a call for collective action and unity.
    • sakhyā: "through our actions" - emphasizes the importance of deeds in achieving recognition.
    • narya: "praise" - signifies the acknowledgment of efforts and achievements.
    • inaḥ: "Indra" - the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
    • stutaś: "praised" - the act of being recognized for one's contributions.
    • carkṛtya: "through our actions" - reiterates the importance of proactive behavior.
    • māvate: "mighty" - refers to the strength and capability of Indra.
    • nare: "brave" - highlights the valor required in human endeavors.
    • viśvāsu: "in all" - signifies inclusivity in efforts and actions.
    • dhūrṣu: "in endeavors" - refers to the various pursuits in life.
    • vājakṛtyeṣu: "in our tasks" - emphasizes the importance of diligence in work.
    • satpate: "protector" - indicates the role of Indra as a guardian of the righteous.
    • vṛtre: "in challenges" - refers to the obstacles one faces.
    • vāpsv: "in waters" - symbolizes the flow of life and the challenges within it.
    • a1bhi: "with strength" - calls for resilience in facing difficulties.
    • śūra: "brave" - again emphasizes courage.
    • mandase: "mighty" - reiterates the strength of Indra.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project at work, when individuals contribute their skills and efforts, they collectively seek recognition from their superiors, akin to invoking Indra's praise.
    • In sports, athletes train hard and work together, hoping to be acknowledged for their teamwork and bravery in competitions, similar to the mantra's call for strength and valor.
    • In personal challenges, individuals often seek inner strength and support from their community, reflecting the mantra's essence of seeking protection and encouragement from a higher power.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on collective action, bravery, and the pursuit of strength in the face of challenges. It serves as a reminder that through our deeds, we can seek recognition and support from greater forces, whether in personal endeavors, professional settings, or communal activities. The mantra encourages individuals to act with courage and integrity, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their pursuits. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, from teamwork in workplaces to personal growth and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
