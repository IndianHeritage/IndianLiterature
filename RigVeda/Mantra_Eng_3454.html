आगन्न ऋभूणाम इह रत्नधेयम् अभूत सोमस्य सुषुतस्य पीतिः |सुकृत्यया यत स्वपस्यया चम् एकं विचक्र चामसं चतुर्धा ||
āgann ṛbhūṇām iha ratnadheyam abhūt somasya suṣutasya pītiḥ | sukṛtyayā yat svapasyayā cam̐ ekaṁ vicakra camasaṁ caturdhā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āgann: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ṛbhūṇām: of the righteous or the wise
    • iha: here
    • ratnadheyam: the treasure or wealth
    • abhūt: has been or has come into being
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • suṣutasya: of the well-prepared or well-made
    • pītiḥ: enjoyment or satisfaction
    • sukṛtyayā: through good deeds
    • yat: which
    • svapasyayā: through vision or insight
    • cam̐: the cup or vessel
    • ekaṁ: one
    • vicakra: having been made to revolve or turn
    • camasaṁ: the cup or vessel (again)
    • caturdhā: in four ways
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
    • It speaks of the wealth that arises from righteous actions and the enjoyment derived from the divine nectar, Soma, which represents spiritual bliss and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "through good deeds" emphasizes the importance of ethical living and the pursuit of righteousness in achieving fulfillment.
    • The mention of "one cup" that revolves in "four ways" can symbolize the multifaceted nature of life and the different paths one can take to achieve spiritual and material wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of ethical business practices. Companies that operate with integrity and contribute positively to society often find greater success and satisfaction.
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of happiness through mindfulness and self-awareness, where individuals seek joy not just in material possessions but in experiences and relationships.
    • The "four ways" can represent the various paths one can take in life—career, family, spirituality, and community service—each contributing to a holistic sense of fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of righteousness, ethical living, and the pursuit of spiritual and material wealth through good deeds.
    • It serves as a reminder that true satisfaction comes from a balanced approach to life, where one seeks to harmonize personal desires with the greater good.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on themselves and others, promoting a life of purpose and fulfillment.
