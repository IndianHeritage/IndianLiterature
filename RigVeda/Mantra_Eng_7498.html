यस् ते नूनं शतक्रतव इन्द्र द्युम्नितमो मदः |तेन नूनम मदे मदेः ||
yas te nūnaṁ śatakratav indra dyumnitamo madaḥ | tena nūnam made madeḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • nūnaṁ: indeed
    • śatakratav: O Indra, the one with a hundred powers
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • dyumnitamo: the most glorious
    • madaḥ: intoxication or ecstasy
    • tena: by that
    • nūnam: indeed
    • made: intoxication
    • madeḥ: of ecstasy
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, known for his strength and valor.
    • The phrase "O Indra, the one with a hundred powers" acknowledges his multifaceted abilities and divine strength.
    • The term "dyumnitamo" signifies the highest glory, suggesting that Indra's power is unparalleled.
    • The mantra speaks of "madaḥ," which refers to a state of ecstasy or intoxication, often associated with divine inspiration or spiritual awakening.
    • By invoking Indra's glory and power, the mantra suggests that one can attain a state of heightened awareness and joy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of great achievement, such as winning a competition or completing a challenging project, individuals often feel a sense of ecstasy and fulfillment, akin to the "madaḥ" described in the mantra.
    • In sports, athletes often channel the energy and strength of a figure like Indra to push through their limits, drawing inspiration from the idea of divine power.
    • During celebrations or festivals, people experience collective joy and intoxication, reflecting the communal aspect of "made" as they come together to honor their achievements and heritage.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and invoking divine strength and glory in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that by connecting with higher powers, we can experience profound joy and inspiration.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various life situations, such as seeking motivation during difficult times or celebrating successes with gratitude and reverence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages individuals to embrace their own potential and the ecstasy that comes from realizing their capabilities, much like the divine qualities attributed to Indra.
