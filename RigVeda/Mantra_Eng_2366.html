मन्त्र: हिरण्यरूपः स हिरण्यसंदृग अपां नपात सेदु हिरण्यवर्णः |हिरण्ययात परि योनेर निषद्यां हिरण्यदां ददाति अन्नं अस्मै ||
hiraṇyarūpaḥ sa hiraṇyasaṁdṛg apāṁ napāt sed u hiraṇyavarṇaḥ | hiraṇyayāt pari yoner niṣadyā hiraṇyadā dadaty annam asmai ||
  • hiraṇyarūpaḥ: This term translates to "golden form" or "golden appearance." In a real-life context, this can symbolize wealth, prosperity, and the beauty of nature. For example, a sunrise can be seen as a "golden form" that brings hope and new beginnings.
  • sa hiraṇyasaṁdṛg: This phrase means "he who has the golden vision." It suggests a perspective that sees the value and beauty in life. In everyday life, this can be likened to individuals who have a positive outlook, finding opportunities in challenges, much like entrepreneurs who see potential where others see obstacles.
  • apāṁ napāt: This translates to "the son of the waters." It can refer to the life-giving properties of water, essential for survival. In practical terms, this can be related to the importance of clean water sources in communities, emphasizing the need for environmental conservation.
  • sed u hiraṇyavarṇaḥ: This means "and he who is of golden color." This can symbolize purity and excellence. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of excellence in various fields, such as education or sports, where individuals strive to achieve their best.
  • hiraṇyayāt: This term can be interpreted as "from the golden one." It signifies the source of wealth and abundance. In a modern context, this could relate to the idea of investing in oneself through education and skills development to create a prosperous future.
  • pari yoner: This translates to "surrounding the womb." It can symbolize nurturing and protection. In real life, this can be seen in the care provided by families and communities to ensure the well-being of their members, especially children.
  • niṣadyā: This means "seated" or "established." It can refer to stability and security. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of having a stable job or a secure home environment, which provides a foundation for personal growth.
  • hiraṇyadā: This translates to "the giver of gold." It symbolizes generosity and the sharing of wealth. In real life, this can be exemplified by philanthropic efforts where individuals or organizations contribute to social causes, helping those in need.
  • dadaty annam asmai: This means "he gives food to him." It emphasizes the importance of sustenance and nourishment. In a broader context, this can relate to food security initiatives that ensure everyone has access to nutritious food, highlighting the need for community support systems.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, vision, nurturing, stability, and generosity. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek wealth not just in material terms but also in knowledge, community support, and environmental stewardship. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal development, community engagement, and sustainable living, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
