यूपव्रस्का उता ये यूपवाहाश चषालं ये अश्वयूपाय तक्षति |ये चार्वते पचनं संभरन्त्य उतो तेषाम अभिगूर्तिर न इन्बतु ||
yūpavraskā uta ye yūpavāhāś caṣālaṁ ye aśvayūpāya takṣati | ye cārvate pacanaṁ sambharanty uto teṣām abhigūrtir na invatu ||
  • yūpavraskā: This term refers to those who are engaged in the act of offering or sacrifice. In a broader sense, it symbolizes individuals who are dedicated to their duties and responsibilities.
  • uta: This word means "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to the previous thought.
  • ye yūpavāhāḥ: This phrase translates to "those who carry the sacrificial post." It signifies individuals who support and uphold traditions and values, much like how a community supports its cultural practices.
  • caṣālaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is skilled" or "the one who is adept." It highlights the importance of expertise and proficiency in any endeavor.
  • ye aśvayūpāya: This refers to "those who use the horse post," symbolizing strength and vitality. Horses are often associated with power and speed, representing the drive to achieve goals.
  • takṣati: This word means "to create" or "to build." It emphasizes the act of construction, whether it be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • ye cārvate: This translates to "those who are engaged in cooking" or "those who prepare." It signifies the nurturing aspect of life, where sustenance is provided for the community.
  • pacanaṁ: This term means "cooking" or "preparation," further emphasizing the importance of nourishment and care in society.
  • sambharanty: This word refers to "those who gather" or "those who collect." It highlights the importance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals.
  • uto: Similar to "uta," it means "and" or "also," connecting the ideas presented.
  • teṣām: This term means "of them," referring to the individuals mentioned earlier in the mantra.
  • abhigūrtir: This translates to "the blessing" or "the grace." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from the actions of those dedicated to their duties.
  • na invatu: This phrase means "may not be disturbed" or "may not be hindered." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining focus and dedication in one's pursuits.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of dedication, collaboration, and expertise in various aspects of life. It highlights how individuals who are committed to their responsibilities, whether in a community, family, or workplace, contribute to the overall well-being and success of society. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in teamwork within organizations, where each member plays a vital role in achieving common goals. The mantra serves as a reminder that when individuals work together with dedication and skill, they create a harmonious and prosperous environment, free from disturbances and distractions.
