अव क्षिप दिवो अश्मानम् उच्छा येन शत्रुम् मंदसानो निजूर्वाः |तोकस्य सातौ तनयस्य भूरेर अस्मां अर्धं कृताद इन्द्र गोनाम ||
ava kṣipa divo aśmānam uccā yena śatrum mandasāno nijūrvāḥ | tokasya sātau tanayasya bhūrer asmām̐ ardhaṁ kṛṇutād indra gonām ||
  • ava kṣipa: This phrase can be translated as "cast away" or "remove." It signifies the act of dispelling negativity or obstacles.
  • divo aśmānam: This refers to "the stone of the sky," which can symbolize the celestial or divine realm. It represents the higher powers or the universe.
  • uccā yena: This means "by which" or "through which," indicating a means or method to achieve something.
  • śatrum: This translates to "enemy" or "adversary." It can refer to any form of opposition or challenge one faces in life.
  • mandasāno: This word means "slow" or "gentle," suggesting a calm and composed approach to dealing with challenges.
  • nijūrvāḥ: This can be interpreted as "our own strength" or "our own power," emphasizing self-reliance and inner strength.
  • tokasya sātau: This phrase means "in the birth of the offspring," which can refer to new beginnings or the potential for growth and development.
  • tanayasya bhūrer: This translates to "of the son of the earth," indicating a connection to the material world and our roots.
  • asmām̐ ardhaṁ: This means "our share" or "our part," suggesting a sense of ownership and responsibility.
  • kṛṇutād: This translates to "may you create" or "may you bring forth," indicating a desire for manifestation and realization of potential.
  • indra gonām: This refers to "the strength of Indra," where Indra is a deity associated with power and leadership, symbolizing the support of divine forces in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of dispelling negativity and obstacles in our lives while relying on our inner strength and the support of higher powers. It encourages a calm and composed approach to challenges, highlighting the significance of new beginnings and personal responsibility. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing difficulties, such as in personal relationships, career challenges, or health issues. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can find the strength to overcome adversities and manifest their goals with the support of both their inner resources and divine guidance.
