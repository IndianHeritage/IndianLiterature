तवेन्द्राहमाशसाहस्ते दात्रं चना ददेम्। दिनस्य वा मघवन् संभरतस्य वा पूर्धि यवस्य काशिनः॥
taved indrāham āśasā haste dātraṁ canā dade | dinasya vā maghavan sambhṛtasya vā pūrdhi yavasya kāśinā ||
  • taved: This word can be interpreted as "you are powerful" or "you are capable." It signifies strength and capability.
  • indrāham: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing authority, power, and leadership.
  • āśasā: This term means "with blessings" or "with grace," indicating the importance of divine favor in achieving success.
  • haste: Translates to "in the hand," suggesting action or the means to accomplish something.
  • dātraṁ: This means "gift" or "offering," highlighting the significance of generosity and sharing.
  • canā: This word can mean "to give" or "to bestow," reinforcing the theme of generosity.
  • dade: Meaning "to provide" or "to grant," it emphasizes the act of giving.
  • dinasya: This translates to "of the day," indicating the importance of time and the daily cycle in our lives.
  • vā: Meaning "or," it presents an alternative or choice.
  • maghavan: Referring to the one who is rich or prosperous, often associated with abundance and wealth.
  • sambhṛtasya: This means "of the gathered" or "of the collected," suggesting the importance of community and collaboration.
  • vā: Again, meaning "or," indicating options or alternatives.
  • pūrdhi: This translates to "fullness" or "completeness," symbolizing the idea of wholeness in life.
  • yavasya: Referring to "of the grain" or "of the harvest," it connects to sustenance and nourishment.
  • kāśinā: This term can mean "with brightness" or "with clarity," suggesting enlightenment and understanding.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, generosity, and divine blessings in achieving success and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of community and the significance of sharing resources. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, where collaboration and sharing of ideas lead to success. It also resonates in personal relationships, where generosity and support foster stronger bonds. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and to act with kindness and strength in our daily lives.
