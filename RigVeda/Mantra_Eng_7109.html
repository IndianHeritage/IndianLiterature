अग्निम्- अग्निं वो अध्रिगुं हुवेम वृत्कबरिषः | अग्निं हितप्रयसः शाश्वतीष्व आ होतारं चर्षणीनाम् ||
agnim-agniṁ vo adhriguṁ huvema vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | agniṁ hitaprayasaḥ śaśvatīṣv ā hotāraṁ carṣaṇīnām ||
  • Agnim-agniṁ: This phrase refers to the fire (Agni), which is a central element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • Vo adhriguṁ huvema: This translates to "we invoke you, O Agni, with our prayers." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine fire for guidance and support.
  • Vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: This term can be understood as "with offerings and praises." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving in the relationship with the divine.
  • Agniṁ hitaprayasaḥ: This means "Agni, who is beneficial and auspicious." It highlights the positive attributes of fire, representing warmth, light, and nourishment.
  • Śaśvatīṣv ā hotāraṁ: This translates to "the eternal priest." It signifies Agni's role as a mediator between humans and the divine, always present and ready to accept offerings.
  • Carṣaṇīnām: This term refers to "the beings of the earth." It connects the divine fire to the earthly realm, indicating that Agni is a bridge between the spiritual and material worlds.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the spiritual realm. In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and ceremonies where fire is used as a medium for offerings, symbolizing purification and transformation. For example, during weddings or religious ceremonies, the sacred fire is invoked to bless the union and ensure prosperity. The mantra teaches us the importance of devotion, gratitude, and the recognition of the divine in our daily lives, encouraging us to seek guidance and support from higher powers while acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings.
