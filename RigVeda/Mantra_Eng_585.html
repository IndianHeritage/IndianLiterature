वयश्चित्ते पतत्रिणो द्विपच्चतुष्पदर्जुनि |उषः प्रारन्न ऋतूम्न् अनु दिवो अन्तेभ्यस् परि ||
vayaś cit te patatriṇo dvipac catuṣpad arjuni | uṣaḥ prārann ṛtūm̐r anu divo antebhyas pari ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaś: of the birds
    • cit: gathered
    • te: your
    • patatriṇo: of the winged ones
    • dvipac: of the two-footed (humans)
    • catuṣpad: of the four-footed (animals)
    • arjuni: the one who is strong or powerful
    • uṣaḥ: dawn
    • prārann: having arisen
    • ṛtūm̐r: seasons
    • anu: after
    • divo: of the sky
    • antebhyas: from the end
    • pari: all around
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of all living beings, including birds, humans, and animals.
    • The mention of "dawn" signifies new beginnings and the cyclical nature of life, as dawn brings light after darkness.
    • The reference to "seasons" indicates the importance of time and change in our lives, reminding us that everything is transient.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the arrival of dawn can be seen as a metaphor for hope and renewal, much like how people often feel rejuvenated at the start of a new day.
    • The changing seasons can be compared to the various phases in human life, such as childhood, adulthood, and old age, each bringing its own experiences and lessons.
    • In a community, the collaboration between different beings (humans, animals, and nature) is essential for a balanced ecosystem, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of harmony among all forms of life and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the dawn of each day and the changes that come with the seasons, encouraging us to embrace new beginnings and adapt to life's transitions.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire us to foster connections with our environment and recognize our role within the larger tapestry of life.
