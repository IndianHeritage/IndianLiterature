परी प्रियः कलशे देववात इन्द्राय सोमो रण्यो मदाया |सहस्रधारः शतवाज इन्दुर वाजी न सप्तिः समना जिगाति ||
pari priyaḥ kalaśe devavāta indrāya somo raṇyo madāya | sahasradhāraḥ śatavāja indur vājī na saptiḥ samanā jigāti ||
  • Mantra: "pari priyaḥ kalaśe devavāta indrāya somo raṇyo madāya | sahasradhāraḥ śatavāja indur vājī na saptiḥ samanā jigāti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari priyaḥ: beloved or cherished
    • kalaśe: in the pot or vessel
    • devavāta: divine wind or energy
    • indrāya: for Indra, the king of gods
    • somo: the moon or soma, a sacred drink
    • raṇyo: for the warrior or in battle
    • madāya: for intoxication or joy
    • sahasradhāraḥ: with a thousand streams or flows
    • śatavāja: with a hundred steeds or resources
    • indur: the moon
    • vājī: the one who brings strength or energy
    • na saptiḥ: not to be hindered
    • samanā: equal or balanced
    • jigāti: to move or to progress
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies associated with Indra and the moon (Soma), emphasizing the importance of strength, joy, and abundance.
    • The phrase "pari priyaḥ kalaśe" suggests that the offerings made in a vessel are cherished, indicating the significance of gratitude and respect in rituals.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents power and authority, and the mantra seeks his blessings for success in endeavors, particularly in battles or challenges.
    • The mention of "sahasradhāraḥ" and "śatavāja" symbolizes abundance and resources, suggesting that one should strive for prosperity and strength in life.
    • The idea of "na saptiḥ" implies that obstacles should not deter one from their path, promoting resilience and determination.
    • Overall, the mantra encourages individuals to seek divine support in their pursuits, ensuring that they remain balanced and focused on their goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new venture. Entrepreneurs often seek blessings for success and abundance, much like invoking Indra's strength.
    • In personal challenges, such as overcoming health issues or personal conflicts, individuals can recite this mantra to cultivate resilience and a positive mindset.
    • During festivals or rituals, offerings made with love and respect can be seen as a modern-day interpretation of "pari priyaḥ kalaśe," emphasizing the importance of intention in spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine support in our lives, encouraging us to seek strength and abundance while remaining resilient in the face of challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the balance it promotes between seeking external help and cultivating inner strength, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can navigate their journeys with confidence and purpose, ensuring that they remain aligned with their goals and values.
