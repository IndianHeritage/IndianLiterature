आयं देवानाम् अपसाम् अपस्तमो यो जजाना रोदसी विश्वशम्भुवाः |विः यो ममे रजसी सुक्रतूययाजरेभिः स्कम्भनेभिः सम आनृचे ||
ayaṁ devānām apasām apastamo yo jajāna rodasī viśvaśambhuvā | vi yo mame rajasī sukratūyayājarebhiḥ skambhanebhiḥ sam ānṛce ||
  • ayaṁ devānām - This phrase translates to "this is of the gods." It signifies the divine nature of the subject being discussed, indicating that it is connected to higher powers or celestial beings.
  • apasām apastamo - This means "the best among the waters." In a broader sense, it can refer to the most auspicious or beneficial aspects of nature, particularly water, which is essential for life.
  • yo jajāna rodasī - This translates to "who has given birth to the heavens." It emphasizes creation and the origin of the universe, suggesting a connection to cosmic forces.
  • viśvaśambhuvā - This means "the all-encompassing." It refers to the universal nature of the divine, indicating that the subject is present everywhere and in everything.
  • vi yo mame rajasī - This translates to "who is my desire." It reflects personal longing or aspiration, suggesting that the divine is also a source of individual motivation and purpose.
  • sukratūyayājarebhiḥ - This means "with good deeds and offerings." It highlights the importance of righteous actions and the act of giving, which are essential in spiritual practices.
  • skambhanebhiḥ - This translates to "with supports." It can refer to the foundational aspects of life, such as community, family, and values that uphold an individual.
  • sam ānṛce - This means "I sing together." It signifies unity and collective worship, emphasizing the importance of community in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine connection, creation, and the importance of righteous living. It serves as a reminder that the divine is not only a cosmic force but also a personal guide in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek a deeper connection with nature, engage in good deeds, and foster a sense of community. By recognizing the divine in everyday life, one can cultivate a more meaningful existence, grounded in purpose and collective harmony.
