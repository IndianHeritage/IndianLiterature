अन्व अस्य स्थूरं ददृशे पुरस्ताद अनस्थ ऊरुर अवरम्बमाणः |शाश्वती नारी अभिचक्ष्याह सुभद्रम आर्य भोजनं बिभर्षि ||
anv asya sthūraṁ dadṛśe purastād anastha ūrur avarambamāṇaḥ | śaśvatī nāry abhicakṣyāha subhadram arya bhojanam bibharṣi ||
  • anv asya sthūraṁ dadṛśe purastād: This phrase can be translated as "I see the great one in front of me." Here, "anv" means "towards," "asya" means "this," "sthūraṁ" means "great" or "strong," and "dadṛśe" means "I see." This suggests a recognition of strength or greatness that is present before us.
  • anastha ūrur avarambamāṇaḥ: This part translates to "the one who is unshaken and supports." "Anastha" means "unshaken," "ūrur" refers to "the one who supports," and "avarambamāṇaḥ" means "one who is holding." This indicates a sense of stability and support that is essential in our lives.
  • śaśvatī nāry abhicakṣyāha: This translates to "the eternal women who see." "Śaśvatī" means "eternal," "nāry" refers to "women," and "abhicakṣyāha" means "who see." This highlights the role of women as wise and perceptive beings, emphasizing their importance in society.
  • subhadram arya bhojanam bibharṣi: This can be interpreted as "you carry the auspicious food." "Subhadram" means "auspicious," "arya" refers to "noble," and "bhojanam bibharṣi" means "you carry food." This signifies the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the recognition of strength and support in our lives, the wisdom of women, and the significance of nourishment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as leadership, where strong and stable figures guide others, the role of women in nurturing and educating future generations, and the importance of providing for both physical and spiritual needs. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the strength around us, the wisdom of those who nurture, and the sustenance that supports our well-being.
