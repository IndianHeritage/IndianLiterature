इमे भोजा अङ्गिरसो विरूपा दिवस पुत्रासो असुरस्य वीराः |विश्वामित्राय ददतो मघानि सहस्रसावे प्र तिरन्त आयुः ||
ime bhojā aṅgiraso virūpā divas putrāso asurasya vīrāḥ | viśvāmitrāya dadato maghāni sahasrasāve pra tiranta āyuḥ ||
  • ime bhojā aṅgiraso: This phrase refers to the "food" or "nourishment" provided by the Aṅgirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be likened to the nourishment we receive from education and guidance from mentors.
  • virūpā divas putrāso: "Virūpā" means "diverse" or "varied," and "divas putrāso" translates to "sons of the day." This suggests that there are many paths and forms of knowledge that can lead to enlightenment. In modern terms, this can be seen in the diversity of educational systems and the various ways individuals can learn and grow.
  • asurasya vīrāḥ: "Asurasya" refers to the "asuras," often seen as powerful beings, and "vīrāḥ" means "heroes." This can symbolize the inner strength and courage we must cultivate to face challenges in life. For example, individuals who stand up against injustice embody this heroism.
  • viśvāmitrāya: This refers to the sage Viśvāmitra, known for his spiritual prowess and dedication. In real life, this can represent the importance of perseverance and commitment to one's goals, much like how scientists and researchers dedicate their lives to discovery and innovation.
  • dadato maghāni: "Dadato" means "giving" and "maghāni" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." This emphasizes the value of generosity and sharing resources with others. In practice, this can be seen in charitable acts and community service, where individuals contribute to the well-being of society.
  • sahasrasāve pra tiranta āyuḥ: "Sahasrasāve" means "with a thousand streams" and "pra tiranta āyuḥ" translates to "long life." This suggests that a life enriched with diverse experiences and knowledge leads to longevity and fulfillment. In real life, this can be reflected in the importance of lifelong learning and the pursuit of varied interests for a well-rounded life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment through knowledge, the diversity of paths to enlightenment, the courage to face challenges, the dedication to one's goals, the importance of generosity, and the value of a rich, varied life. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to seek wisdom, embrace diversity, act heroically, commit to their aspirations, share their resources, and pursue a fulfilling life. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, strength, and generosity in achieving a meaningful existence.
