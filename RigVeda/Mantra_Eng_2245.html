सनात ता क चिद भुवना भवित्वा माद्भिः शरद्भिर दुरो वरन्त वः |अयतन्ता चरतो अन्यद्-अन्यद् इद या चकार वयुनā ब्रह्मणस पतिḥ ||
sanā tā kā cid bhuvanā bhavītvā mādbhiḥ śaradbhir duro varanta vaḥ | ayatantā carato anyad-anyad id yā cakāra vayunā brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanā: eternal, everlasting
    • tā: that, which
    • kā: who, what
    • cid: indeed, certainly
    • bhuvanā: worlds, realms
    • bhavītvā: becoming, manifesting
    • mādbhiḥ: with us, through us
    • śaradbhir: with the seasons, with the years
    • duro: difficult, hard
    • varanta: to attain, to achieve
    • vaḥ: you, your
    • ayatantā: unyielding, persistent
    • carato: moving, wandering
    • anyad-anyad: different, various
    • id: this, here
    • yā: who, which
    • cakāra: created, made
    • vayunā: through the power, through the energy
    • brahmaṇas: of the divine, of the ultimate reality
    • patiḥ: lord, master
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal nature of existence and the various realms that manifest through divine will.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving difficult goals.
    • The reference to "seasons" suggests the cyclical nature of life and the importance of timing in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "different, various" indicates the diversity of paths and experiences that one may encounter in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, the success often depends on the collective effort of all members, highlighting the mantra's emphasis on collaboration.
    • In nature, the changing seasons remind us of the cycles of life, encouraging us to adapt and grow with each phase.
    • In personal development, individuals may pursue different paths to achieve their goals, reflecting the diversity mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that while the journey may be difficult, persistence and collaboration can lead to success.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork, personal growth, and understanding the cyclical nature of existence.
