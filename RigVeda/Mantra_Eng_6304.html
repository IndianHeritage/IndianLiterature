यदा वृतं नदीवृतं शवसा वज्रिन्न अवधीः |आद इति ते हर्यतां हरि ववक्षतुः ||
yadā vṛtraṁ nadīvṛtaṁ śavasā vajrinn avadhīḥ | ād it te haryatā harī vavakṣatuḥ ||
  • yadā - This word translates to "when" or "at that time." It sets the context for the mantra, indicating a specific moment or event.
  • vṛtraṁ - Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon-like figure in Vedic mythology, symbolizing obstacles or challenges that obstruct the flow of life and prosperity.
  • nadīvṛtaṁ - This can be broken down into "nadī," meaning "river," and "vṛta," meaning "enclosed" or "surrounded." Together, it suggests the idea of being surrounded by obstacles, akin to a river being blocked.
  • śavasā - This word means "with strength" or "powerful." It emphasizes the force or energy needed to overcome challenges.
  • vajrinn - Derived from "vajra," meaning "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing indestructibility and power. It represents the strength required to confront and defeat obstacles.
  • avadhīḥ - This translates to "to overcome" or "to destroy." It indicates the action of conquering the challenges represented by Vritra.
  • ād - Meaning "from" or "out of," it signifies the source or origin of the action.
  • it - This word means "that" or "there," pointing to the outcome or result of the actions taken.
  • te - Translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to the subject being addressed.
  • haryatā - This word can be interpreted as "with joy" or "happiness," suggesting that overcoming obstacles leads to a state of joy.
  • harī - Refers to "the one who removes" or "the remover," often associated with divine figures who help in overcoming difficulties.
  • vavakṣatuḥ - This means "may it be revealed" or "may it manifest," indicating a desire for the outcome to become apparent or realized.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the struggle against obstacles and the power needed to overcome them. It signifies that when one confronts challenges (like Vritra), with strength and determination (symbolized by the thunderbolt), they can achieve joy and success. In real life, this can be applied to various situations such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. By invoking the spirit of resilience and the support of divine forces, individuals can find the strength to navigate through difficulties, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and joyous life.
