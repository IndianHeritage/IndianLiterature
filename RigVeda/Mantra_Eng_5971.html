उलूकयातुं शुशुलूकयातुं जहि श्वयातुम उता कोकयातुम |सुपर्णयातुम उता गृध्रयातुं दृष्टदेव प्र मृण रक्ष इन्द्र ||
ulūkayātuṁ śuśulūkayātuṁ jahi śvayātum uta kokayātum | suparṇayātum uta gṛdhrayātuṁ dṛṣadeva pra mṛṇa rakṣa indra ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • ulūkayātuṁ: Refers to the owl, often symbolizing wisdom and knowledge.
    • śuśulūkayātuṁ: Another reference to the owl, emphasizing its qualities.
    • jahi: Means "to conquer" or "to overcome."
    • śvayātum: Refers to the "swan," symbolizing purity and beauty.
    • uta: Means "and" or "also," connecting thoughts.
    • kokayātum: Refers to the "crow," often associated with communication and intelligence.
    • suparṇayātum: Refers to the "eagle," symbolizing strength and vision.
    • gṛdhrayātuṁ: Refers to the "vulture," which can symbolize transformation and renewal.
    • dṛṣadeva: Means "to see the divine" or "to perceive the divine presence."
    • pra mṛṇa: Means "to protect" or "to safeguard."
    • rakṣa: Means "to guard" or "to defend."
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods, symbolizing power and authority.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes various birds, each representing different qualities and attributes.
    • The owl symbolizes wisdom, suggesting the importance of knowledge in overcoming challenges.
    • The swan represents purity, indicating the need for clarity and beauty in our actions.
    • The crow signifies intelligence and communication, reminding us of the power of words and connections.
    • The eagle embodies strength and vision, encouraging us to rise above difficulties and see the bigger picture.
    • The vulture symbolizes transformation, teaching us that endings can lead to new beginnings.
    • Overall, the mantra emphasizes the need for protection and guidance from divine forces, particularly from Indra, who represents strength and authority.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In times of uncertainty or challenge, invoking wisdom (like the owl) can help us make informed decisions.
    • Striving for purity in our intentions (like the swan) can lead to more meaningful relationships and actions.
    • Effective communication (like the crow) is essential in both personal and professional settings to foster understanding.
    • Having a vision (like the eagle) allows us to set goals and work towards them with determination.
    • Embracing change (like the vulture) can help us grow and adapt in a constantly evolving world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the diverse qualities we can embody to navigate life's challenges.
    • By invoking the attributes of these birds, we can seek wisdom, purity, intelligence, strength, and transformation.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek divine protection and guidance, particularly in our endeavors, ensuring we remain resilient and focused.
