ग्रावाणः सविता नु वो देवः सुवतु धर्मणा |यजमानाय सुन्वते ||
grāvāṇaḥ savitā nu vo devaḥ suvatu dharmaṇā | yajamānāya sunvate ||
  • Mantra: "grāvāṇaḥ savitā nu vo devaḥ suvatu dharmaṇā | yajamānāya sunvate ||"
  • Translation: "May the divine Savita, the Sun God, bestow upon you the strength to perform righteous deeds. May He bless the one who offers sacrifices with prosperity."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • grāvāṇaḥ: This term refers to the act of invoking or calling upon. It signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance.
    • savitā: Savita is a name for the Sun God, representing light, energy, and life. The Sun is often seen as a source of vitality and inspiration.
    • nu: This word is an expression of emphasis, akin to saying "indeed" or "truly," highlighting the sincerity of the invocation.
    • vo: This means "to you," indicating that the blessings are directed towards the listeners or the community.
    • devaḥ: This translates to "divine" or "god," emphasizing the sacred nature of the request.
    • suvatu: This means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the action of giving blessings or support.
    • dharmaṇā: Dharma refers to righteousness, duty, or moral law. It signifies the importance of ethical conduct in life.
    • yajamānāya: This term refers to the one who performs the sacrifice or offering, highlighting the role of the individual in seeking divine favor.
    • sunvate: This means "to prosper" or "to flourish," indicating the desired outcome of the invocation.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke blessings for prosperity and harmony, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal endeavors, such as starting a new business or project, individuals may seek divine guidance and support, reflecting the call for blessings from Savita.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or births, families often perform rituals to invoke divine blessings for a prosperous and righteous life ahead.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for strength and righteousness in our actions. It serves as a reminder that ethical conduct and the pursuit of dharma are essential for personal and communal prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals or communities seek guidance, support, and blessings for their endeavors, reinforcing the connection between the divine and human actions.
