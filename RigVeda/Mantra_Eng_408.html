आ नासत्याः गच्छतं हूयते हविः मध्वः पिबतं मधुपेभिः आसभिः |युवोः हि पूर्वं सवितोषसो रथम ऋताय चित्रम घृतवंतम् इष्यति ||
ā nāsatyā gacchataṁ hūyate havir madhvaḥ pibatam madhupebhir āsabhiḥ | yuvor hi pūrvaṁ savitoṣaso ratham ṛtāya citraṁ ghṛtavantam iṣyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā nāsatyā: "O Aśvins" or "O twin deities"
    • gacchataṁ: "may you go" or "may you proceed"
    • hūyate: "is invoked" or "is called upon"
    • havir: "offering" or "sacrifice"
    • madhvaḥ: "sweet" or "pleasant"
    • pibatam: "may you drink"
    • madhupebhir: "with the bees" or "with sweetness"
    • āsabhiḥ: "with the offerings" or "with the sacrifices"
    • yuvor: "of the two" or "of the youthful"
    • hi: "indeed" or "certainly"
    • pūrvaṁ: "earlier" or "before"
    • savitōṣaso: "of the rising sun"
    • ratham: "chariot" or "vehicle"
    • ṛtāya: "for truth" or "for righteousness"
    • citraṁ: "varied" or "colorful"
    • ghṛtavantam: "filled with ghee" or "rich in nourishment"
    • iṣyati: "is desired" or "is sought"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • The invocation emphasizes the importance of offerings and the sweetness of life, symbolized by "madhu" (honey).
    • The reference to the "rising sun" signifies new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
    • The "chariot" represents the journey of life, which should be guided by truth and righteousness.
    • The mantra encourages the pursuit of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, as indicated by "ghṛtavantam."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek health and well-being, much like how people turn to holistic practices or natural remedies.
    • The idea of invoking the Aśvins can be likened to seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who help navigate life's challenges.
    • The emphasis on truth and righteousness can be applied in ethical decision-making in personal and professional contexts.
    • Celebrating new beginnings, such as starting a new job or a family, can be seen as a modern-day interpretation of the "rising sun."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine support in our endeavors, emphasizing health, truth, and nourishment.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach life with positivity and righteousness, seeking guidance and support from higher powers.
    • In a world filled with challenges, the teachings of this mantra can help individuals cultivate a balanced and fulfilling life.
