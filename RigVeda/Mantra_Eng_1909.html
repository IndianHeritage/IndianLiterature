उता स्यां वां रुशतो वप्ससो गीस त्रिभरिषि सदसि पिन्वते नृन |वृषा वां मेघो वृषणा पीपाय गोर्न सेके मनुषो दशस्यन् ||
uta syā vāṁ ruśato vapsaso gīs tribarhiṣi sadasi pinvate nṝn | vṛṣā vām megho vṛṣaṇā pīpāya gor na seke manuṣo daśasyan ||
  • uta syā vāṁ ruśato vapsaso gīs: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the divine to bestow blessings and prosperity. The word "uta" signifies a request or invocation, while "vāṁ" refers to "you" (the divine). "Ruśato" implies the act of being pleased or satisfied, and "vapsaso" relates to the essence or nature of abundance. "Gīs" refers to the hymns or praises offered to the divine.
  • tribarhiṣi sadasi pinvate nṝn: Here, "tribarhiṣi" can be understood as the three realms or dimensions of existence. "Sadasi" refers to the gathering or assembly, and "pinvate" means to nourish or sustain. "Nṝn" translates to "men" or "humankind." This line emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being, suggesting that the divine nourishes humanity through unity.
  • vṛṣā vām megho vṛṣaṇā pīpāya: "Vṛṣā" denotes strength or power, while "vām" again refers to "you." "Megho" means cloud, and "vṛṣaṇā" relates to the act of raining or showering blessings. "Pīpāya" signifies the act of providing sustenance. This line metaphorically connects the divine to the nurturing aspects of nature, such as rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance.
  • gor na seke manuṣo daśasyan: "Gor" refers to cattle, symbolizing wealth and prosperity. "Na seke" can be interpreted as "may not be withheld," indicating a desire for abundance. "Manuṣo" means "human," and "daśasyan" relates to the ten directions or realms. This line expresses a wish for prosperity and abundance to flow freely to all beings, transcending boundaries.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings for prosperity, community well-being, and the nurturing aspects of nature. It highlights the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, emphasizing the importance of unity and collective nourishment.
  • Real-life significance: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, agricultural practices, and environmental conservation efforts. By recognizing the importance of collective well-being and the nurturing aspects of nature, individuals and communities can work towards sustainable living and mutual support.
  • Application: This mantra can be recited during community events, agricultural festivals, or rituals aimed at seeking blessings for prosperity and harmony. It serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, gratitude, and the nurturing relationship between humanity and nature.
