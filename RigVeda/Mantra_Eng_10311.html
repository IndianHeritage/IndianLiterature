रायो बुध्नः संगमनो वसूनां विश्वा रूपाभि चष्टे शचीभिः |देव इव सविता सत्यधर्मेन्द्रो न तस्थौ समरे धनानाम् ||
rāyo budhnaḥ saṁgamano vasūnāṁ viśvā rūpābhi caṣṭe śacībhiḥ | deva iva savitā satyadharmendro na tasthau samare dhanānām ||
  • rāyo budhnaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the wealth of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in acquiring and managing wealth. In real life, this can be seen in successful entrepreneurs who use their knowledge and experience to build and sustain their businesses.
  • saṁgamano vasūnāṁ: This means "the gathering of riches." It suggests that wealth is not just about individual accumulation but also about collaboration and community. For example, cooperative businesses or community-supported agriculture (CSA) initiatives illustrate how people come together to share resources and benefits.
  • viśvā rūpābhi caṣṭe: This translates to "the universal forms are manifested." It indicates that wealth and prosperity can take many forms, not just material. In modern life, this can refer to the wealth of knowledge, relationships, and experiences that enrich our lives beyond financial means.
  • śacībhiḥ: This word refers to "with strength or power." It highlights the idea that true wealth is accompanied by strength and capability. For instance, individuals who invest in their education and personal development often find themselves in positions of power and influence.
  • deva iva savitā: This phrase means "like the divine sun." It symbolizes enlightenment and the nurturing aspect of wealth. Just as the sun provides life and energy, wealth should be used to uplift and support others. Charitable actions and community service are examples of how individuals can use their resources for the greater good.
  • satyadharmendro: This translates to "the lord of truth and righteousness." It emphasizes the ethical dimensions of wealth. In practice, this can be seen in businesses that prioritize corporate social responsibility, ensuring that their operations benefit society and the environment.
  • na tasthau samare dhanānām: This means "he did not stand in the battle of wealth." It suggests that one should not be complacent or passive in the pursuit of wealth. In real life, this can be interpreted as the need for continuous effort and adaptability in a competitive environment, such as in career advancement or investment strategies.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the idea that true wealth is multifaceted, encompassing wisdom, community, strength, and ethical considerations. It teaches us that wealth should not only be pursued for personal gain but should also be used to uplift others and contribute positively to society. In today's world, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as in business practices that prioritize sustainability, in personal development through education, and in community initiatives that foster collaboration and support. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more equitable and prosperous society.
