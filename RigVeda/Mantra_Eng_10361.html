ऐषु चाकन्धि पुरुहूत सूरिषु वृद्धासो ये मगवन्न आनशुर मगहम् |अर्चन्ति टोके तनये परिष्टिषु मेधसातां वाजिनं अह्रये धनें ||
aiṣu cākandhi puruhūta sūriṣu vṛdhāso ye maghavann ānaśur magham | arcanti toke tanaye pariṣṭiṣu medhasātā vājinam ahraye dhane ||
  • aiṣu cākandhi: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine or the higher powers. It signifies the importance of seeking guidance and support from the universe or the divine forces in our endeavors.
  • puruhūta: This term refers to the many forms or manifestations of the divine. It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine in various aspects of life, suggesting that we can find support and strength in different forms.
  • sūriṣu vṛdhāso: Here, "sūriṣu" relates to the sun or light, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment. "Vṛdhāso" indicates growth or prosperity. Together, they suggest that through enlightenment and knowledge, one can achieve growth and success.
  • ye maghavann ānaśur magham: This part speaks of those who are blessed with abundance and prosperity. "Maghavann" refers to the powerful or wealthy, while "ānaśur" implies the attainment of wealth or resources. It highlights the importance of striving for prosperity in life.
  • arcanti toke tanaye: This phrase can be understood as a call to worship or honor the divine. "Toke" refers to offerings or sacrifices, while "tanaye" means offspring or future generations. It emphasizes the significance of honoring the divine for the well-being of future generations.
  • pariṣṭiṣu medhasātā: "Pariṣṭiṣu" refers to the surroundings or environment, while "medhasātā" relates to intelligence or wisdom. This suggests that wisdom should guide our actions in our environment, promoting harmony and balance.
  • vājinam ahraye dhane: This final part speaks of the importance of strength and resources. "Vājinam" refers to a strong or powerful entity, while "ahraye dhane" indicates the acquisition of wealth or resources. It emphasizes the need for strength and resources to achieve our goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, striving for prosperity, and honoring the divine for the benefit of future generations. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can seek knowledge and enlightenment to foster growth and success.
  • In family and community settings, honoring traditions and values can ensure the well-being of future generations.
  • In professional life, leveraging wisdom and resources can lead to achieving goals and prosperity.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine support, personal effort, and the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
