अग्नेमन्युम् प्रतिनुदन् परेषां अदब्धो गोपाः परि पाही नस् त्वम् |प्रत्यान्चो यन्तु निगुतः पुनस्तेऽमैषां चित्तं प्रबुधां विनेशत् ||
agne manyum pratinudan pareṣām adabdho gopāḥ pari pāhi nas tvam | pratyañco yantu nigutaḥ punas te3 'maiṣāṁ cittam prabudhāṁ vi neśat ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Manyum: This translates to 'anger' or 'passion,' indicating the emotional state that can be directed positively or negatively.
  • Pratinudan: This means 'to counter' or 'to oppose,' suggesting a need to manage or control emotions.
  • Pareṣām: This word means 'of others,' indicating a focus on the external world and relationships with others.
  • Adabdho: This translates to 'unharmed' or 'unobstructed,' suggesting a state of being free from negativity.
  • Gopāḥ: This means 'protectors' or 'guardians,' referring to those who safeguard and nurture.
  • Pari pāhi: This phrase means 'protect us all around,' emphasizing the need for comprehensive protection.
  • Nas: This means 'us,' indicating a collective plea for safety and guidance.
  • Tvam: This translates to 'you,' referring to Agni or the divine force being addressed.
  • Pratyañco: This means 'from all sides,' indicating a holistic approach to protection.
  • Yantu: This translates to 'may they go,' suggesting a desire for positive energies to flow towards us.
  • Nigutaḥ: This means 'hidden' or 'concealed,' referring to the unseen forces that influence our lives.
  • Punas: This translates to 'again,' indicating a recurring need for divine intervention.
  • Te: This means 'your,' referring to the divine presence being invoked.
  • Maiṣāṁ: This translates to 'of our minds,' indicating a focus on mental clarity and awareness.
  • Cittam: This means 'consciousness' or 'thought,' emphasizing the importance of mental state.
  • Prabudhāṁ: This translates to 'awakened' or 'enlightened,' suggesting a state of higher awareness.
  • Vi neśat: This means 'may it lead,' indicating a desire for guidance towards the right path.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in our lives. It acknowledges the emotional struggles we face and the need for external support from the divine and from those around us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in moments of stress, conflict, or uncertainty, where individuals can invoke this mantra to find peace, clarity, and direction. It serves as a reminder to cultivate a positive mindset and to seek the support of guardians—be they friends, family, or spiritual entities—who can help navigate life's challenges. The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to well-being, encouraging individuals to seek balance and protection in all aspects of life.
