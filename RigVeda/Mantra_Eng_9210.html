रथं यान्तं कुहा को ह वां नराः प्रति द्युमन्तं सुविताया भूषति |प्रातर्यावाणं विभवं विशे-विशे वस्तोर-वस्तोर वहमानं धियाऽशमि ||
rathaṁ yāntaṁ kuha ko ha vāṁ narā prati dyumantaṁ suvitāya bhūṣati | prātaryāvāṇaṁ vibhvaṁ viśe-viśe vastor-vastor vahamānaṁ dhiyā śami ||
  • Mantra: "rathaṁ yāntaṁ kuha ko ha vāṁ narā prati dyumantaṁ suvitāya bhūṣati | prātaryāvāṇaṁ vibhvaṁ viśe-viśe vastor-vastor vahamānaṁ dhiyā śami ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • yāntaṁ: going or moving
    • kuha: where
    • ko: who
    • ha: indeed
    • vāṁ: you
    • narā: men or people
    • prati: towards
    • dyumantaṁ: shining or radiant
    • suvitāya: well adorned or well equipped
    • bhūṣati: adorns or embellishes
    • prātaryāvāṇaṁ: in the morning, moving forward
    • vibhvaṁ: prosperous or wealthy
    • viśe-viśe: in every aspect or detail
    • vastor-vastor: of the things or objects
    • vahāmānaṁ: carrying or bearing
    • dhiyā: with wisdom or intellect
    • śami: to be understood or realized
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of life, symbolized by a chariot (rathaṁ) that is moving forward.
    • The questions posed—"where" (kuha) and "who" (ko)—reflect the search for purpose and direction in life.
    • The radiant (dyumantaṁ) and well-adorned (suvitāya) aspects signify the importance of being prepared and equipped for the journey ahead.
    • The mention of morning (prātaryāvāṇaṁ) symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities that arise each day.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom (dhiyā) in navigating through life's complexities and challenges.
    • In real life, this can be likened to embarking on a new project or phase in life, where one must be well-prepared and wise in decision-making.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and preparedness as we navigate through life's journey.
    • It encourages individuals to seek clarity on their goals and to approach challenges with wisdom and readiness.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as career changes, personal development, or any new venture where direction and preparation are crucial.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that life is a journey that requires both introspection and action, adorned with wisdom and purpose.
