त्वं अग्नि सुभृत उत्तमं वयस् तव स्पार्थे वर्ण आ संदृशि श्रियः |त्वं वाजः प्रतरणो बृहन्न असि त्वं रयिः बहुलो विश्वतस् पृथुः ||
tvam agne subhṛta uttamaṁ vayas tava spārhe varṇa ā saṁdṛśi śriyaḥ | tvaṁ vājaḥ prataraṇo bṛhann asi tvaṁ rayir bahulo viśvatas pṛthuḥ ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and the divine presence.
  • subhṛta - "well-nourished" - This term signifies abundance and prosperity, suggesting that Agni is a source of nourishment and sustenance.
  • uttamaṁ vayas - "the best of ages" - This phrase indicates the highest or most excellent state of existence, emphasizing the importance of Agni in achieving this state.
  • tava spārhe - "your desire" - This refers to the aspirations and intentions associated with Agni, highlighting the connection between divine will and human desires.
  • varṇa - "color" or "form" - This can symbolize the various aspects of life and existence, suggesting that Agni encompasses all forms of life.
  • ā saṁdṛśi - "in your sight" - This phrase indicates the presence of Agni in our lives, suggesting that he is always watching over us.
  • śriyaḥ - "prosperity" - This term signifies wealth and success, indicating that Agni is a source of blessings and abundance.
  • tvaṁ vājaḥ - "you are the strength" - This emphasizes Agni's role as a source of power and energy in our lives.
  • prataraṇo - "the one who helps" - This indicates that Agni is a supportive force, aiding individuals in their endeavors.
  • bṛhann asi - "you are vast" - This highlights the infinite nature of Agni, suggesting that his influence extends beyond the physical realm.
  • tvaṁ rayir - "you are wealth" - This reinforces the idea that Agni is synonymous with prosperity and abundance.
  • bahulo - "many" - This term suggests that Agni's blessings are plentiful and available to all.
  • viśvatas - "from all sides" - This indicates that Agni's influence is universal, affecting all aspects of life.
  • pṛthuḥ - "broad" or "expansive" - This signifies the vastness of Agni's presence and blessings in the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that nourishes, supports, and blesses individuals with prosperity and strength. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine, seeking guidance in our aspirations, and recognizing the abundance that surrounds us. By invoking Agni, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and awareness of the blessings in their lives, fostering a mindset of abundance and positivity. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the divine support available to us in our journeys.
