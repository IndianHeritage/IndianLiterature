अभी षु णः सखीनाम अविता जरीत्रीणाम् |शतम भवास्यूतिभिः ||
abhī ṣu ṇaḥ sakhīnām avitā jaritṝṇām | śatam bhavāsy ūtibhiḥ ||
  • abhī - This word can be translated as "towards" or "in the direction of." It signifies a movement or an intention towards something positive.
  • ṣu - This term means "good" or "auspicious." It emphasizes the quality of what is being sought or desired.
  • ṇaḥ - This is a possessive form, meaning "our." It indicates a sense of belonging or connection to the subject being discussed.
  • sakhīnām - This word translates to "friends" or "companions." It highlights the importance of relationships and community in our lives.
  • avitā - This means "to protect" or "to guard." It conveys a sense of safety and security that one seeks from their companions.
  • jaritṝṇām - This term refers to "those who are old" or "the elders." It acknowledges the wisdom and experience that comes with age.
  • śatam - This word means "hundred." It often symbolizes abundance or a large quantity of something.
  • bhavāsy - This translates to "may you become" or "may you be." It expresses a wish or a blessing for the future.
  • ūtibhiḥ - This term means "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of energy and vitality in achieving one's goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or a wish for the protection and support of our friends and elders, encouraging them to be strong and abundant in their lives. It reflects the interconnectedness of relationships and the importance of community in fostering a safe and prosperous environment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In a workplace setting, fostering teamwork and collaboration among colleagues can lead to a more productive and supportive environment.
  • In family dynamics, valuing the wisdom of elders and seeking their guidance can help in making informed decisions.
  • In friendships, being there for one another during challenging times can strengthen bonds and create a sense of security.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of relationships, protection, and strength within a community. It serves as a reminder to cherish our connections with friends and elders, and to support one another in our journeys. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous life for ourselves and those around us.
