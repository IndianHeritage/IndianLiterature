मात्सि वायुम् इष्टये राधसे च मात्सि मित्रावरुणा पूयमानः |मात्सि शर्धो मारुतम् मात्सि देवान मात्सि dyāvāpṛthivī देव सोम ||
matsi vāyum iṣṭaye rādhase ca matsi mitrāvaruṇā pūyamānaḥ | matsi śardho mārutam matsi devān matsi dyāvāpṛthivī deva soma ||
  • matsi vāyum: This phrase can be translated as "May I have the wind." Here, "vāyum" refers to the wind, which is often associated with life, movement, and energy. In real life, wind is essential for various natural processes, such as pollination and weather patterns.
  • iṣṭaye: This word means "for the purpose of worship" or "for the sake of offering." It signifies the intention behind the invocation, emphasizing the importance of devotion and purpose in our actions.
  • rādhase ca: This translates to "and for the sake of prosperity." "Rādhase" refers to abundance or prosperity, indicating a desire for growth and well-being in life.
  • matsi mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase means "May I have the blessings of Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. Their blessings are sought for maintaining balance in relationships and society.
  • pūyamānaḥ: This term means "being purified" or "being made pure." It suggests a desire for spiritual and moral purity, which is essential for personal growth and societal harmony.
  • matsi śardho: This translates to "May I have the strength." "Śardho" refers to strength or vigor, highlighting the importance of resilience and fortitude in overcoming life's challenges.
  • mārutam: This word refers to the "storm" or "wind," symbolizing the dynamic forces of nature. It reminds us of the power of nature and the need to respect and coexist with it.
  • matsi devān: This means "May I have the blessings of the gods." It signifies the importance of divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • matsi dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "May I have the blessings of the heavens and the earth." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of existence and the need for harmony between the spiritual and material worlds.
  • deva soma: "Deva" means "god," and "soma" refers to the divine nectar or essence. This part of the mantra invokes the divine energy that sustains life and promotes well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of seeking blessings from nature, deities, and the universe. It highlights the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence—spiritual, physical, and social. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or when seeking guidance in challenging situations. By invoking these energies, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, strength, and harmony in their lives, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling existence.
