पुरु त्वा दाश्वान वोचे 'रिर अग्ने तव सविद आ |तदस्येव शरण आ महस्य ||
puru tvā dāśvān voce 'rir agne tava svid ā | todasyeva śaraṇa ā mahasya ||
  • Mantra: "puru tvā dāśvān voce 'rir agne tava svid ā | todasyeva śaraṇa ā mahasya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, we invoke you, the one who is the source of all strength and energy. You are the refuge of all beings, and we seek your protection and guidance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Puru: Meaning "many" or "abundant," indicating the vastness of Agni's presence.
    • Tvā: Meaning "you," referring directly to Agni.
    • Dāśvān: Meaning "the one who is powerful" or "the strong one," emphasizing Agni's strength.
    • Voce: Meaning "we call" or "we invoke," indicating the act of seeking Agni's presence.
    • 'Rir: Meaning "to shine" or "to illuminate," referring to Agni's illuminating qualities.
    • Agne: A direct address to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • Tava: Meaning "your," indicating possession or connection to Agni.
    • Svid: Meaning "where," suggesting a quest for knowledge or understanding.
    • Ā: A particle that can denote direction or purpose.
    • Todasyeva: Meaning "as a refuge," indicating Agni as a protector.
    • Śaraṇa: Meaning "shelter" or "refuge," emphasizing safety and security.
    • Ā: Again, a particle indicating direction or purpose.
    • Mahasya: Meaning "of the great one," referring to the vastness and importance of Agni.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, people often seek guidance and strength during challenging times. Just as this mantra invokes Agni for support, individuals may turn to mentors, spiritual leaders, or community for strength and direction.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, fire is often used as a symbol of purification and transformation. This mantra can be recited during such rituals to invoke the protective and guiding qualities of fire.
    • In modern contexts, the concept of seeking refuge can be seen in mental health practices, where individuals seek therapy or support groups to find safety and guidance in their struggles.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Agni as a source of strength, guidance, and protection. It teaches us the importance of seeking support in our lives, whether through spiritual means, community, or personal relationships. In a world filled with challenges, this mantra serves as a reminder that we can always seek refuge in the strength of others and the divine, allowing us to navigate life's complexities with courage and clarity.
