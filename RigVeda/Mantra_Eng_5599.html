प्र बुध्न्याऽ व ईरते महांसी प्र नामानि प्रयज्यवस तिरध्वम् |सहस्रियं दाम्यं भागम् एतं गृहमेधीयं मरुतो जुषध्वम् ||
pra budhnyā va īrate mahāṁsi pra nāmāni prayajyavas tiradhvam | sahasriyaṁ damyam bhāgam etaṁ gṛhamedhīyam maruto juṣadhvam ||
  • pra budhnyā: This phrase can be translated as "awakened" or "enlightened." It signifies the importance of awareness and consciousness in our lives. For example, in a real-life scenario, being "awakened" can refer to someone who has gained insight into their purpose or the nature of reality, much like a person who has undergone a transformative experience that leads them to a more meaningful life.
  • va īrate: This means "and they are engaged" or "they are active." It emphasizes the need for action and participation in life. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who actively contribute to their communities, such as volunteers who dedicate their time to help others, thereby creating a positive impact.
  • mahāṁsi: Translated as "great" or "mighty," this word refers to the power and strength inherent in the universe. An example of this can be seen in natural phenomena, such as the force of a hurricane or the vastness of the ocean, which remind us of the greater forces at play in our lives.
  • pra nāmāni: This phrase means "the names" or "the identities." It suggests the importance of recognizing and honoring the various identities and roles we play in life. For instance, a person may identify as a parent, a professional, and a community member, each role contributing to their overall identity.
  • prayajyavas: This can be interpreted as "those who are devoted" or "those who are committed." It highlights the significance of dedication in achieving goals. A real-life example could be an athlete who trains rigorously for a competition, demonstrating commitment and perseverance.
  • tiradhvam: This word means "to be uplifted" or "to rise." It signifies the aspiration to elevate oneself and others. In everyday life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire their teams to achieve greater heights, fostering an environment of growth and development.
  • sahasriyaṁ: Translated as "thousandfold," this term suggests abundance and multiplicity. It can be related to the idea of opportunities in life, where one can find numerous paths to success, much like a business that diversifies its offerings to reach a wider audience.
  • damyam: This means "to be controlled" or "to be disciplined." It emphasizes the importance of self-control and discipline in achieving success. For example, a student who manages their time effectively to balance studies and extracurricular activities exemplifies this principle.
  • bhāgam etaṁ: This phrase translates to "this portion" or "this share." It signifies the idea of sharing and community. In real life, this can be seen in communal meals or shared resources, where individuals come together to support one another.
  • gṛhamedhīyam: This term refers to "the household" or "the home." It highlights the significance of family and community in our lives. For instance, a family gathering during festivals showcases the importance of togetherness and shared traditions.
  • maruto juṣadhvam: This phrase means "O Maruts, may you enjoy" or "O winds, may you be pleased." It invokes the blessings of the Maruts, the storm deities, symbolizing the connection between nature and humanity. In a practical sense, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where people work to protect nature for future generations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awareness, action, strength, identity, commitment, upliftment, abundance, discipline, sharing, community, and the connection with nature. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to be conscious of their actions, dedicated to their goals, and supportive of their communities. By embodying these values, one can lead a more fulfilling and impactful life, contributing positively to society and the environment.
