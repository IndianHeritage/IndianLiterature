भूरिदा ह्यसि श्रुतः पुरुत्रा शूर वृत्रहन् |आ नो भजस्व राधसि ||
bhūridā hy asi śrutaḥ purutrā śūra vṛtrahan | ā no bhajasva rādhasi ||
  • bhūridā hy asi śrutaḥ: This phrase translates to "You are the one who gives abundantly and is well-known." Here, "bhūridā" means one who gives a lot, and "śrutaḥ" refers to being renowned or famous. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or organizations that contribute significantly to society, such as philanthropists or charitable foundations that provide resources to those in need.
  • purutrā śūra vṛtrahan: This part means "You are the brave one who conquers everywhere." "Purutrā" implies being victorious in all directions, while "vṛtrahan" refers to the slayer of obstacles or enemies. This can be seen in the context of leaders or innovators who overcome challenges and adversities to achieve success, such as entrepreneurs who navigate through market difficulties to establish thriving businesses.
  • ā no bhajasva rādhasi: This translates to "May you bestow upon us your blessings." "Ā no" means "to us," "bhajasva" refers to bestowing or granting, and "rādhasi" signifies prosperity or abundance. In practical terms, this can be related to seeking guidance and support from mentors or spiritual leaders who inspire and uplift others, helping them to achieve their goals and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of abundance, bravery, and the granting of blessings. It signifies the value of generosity and the impact of courageous actions in overcoming challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in community service, leadership roles, and personal development. By embodying the qualities of giving, bravery, and seeking blessings, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and the potential for collective growth and prosperity.
