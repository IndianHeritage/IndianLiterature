विष्वे यजत्रा अधि वोकतोतये त्रायध्वं नो दुरेवायाः अभिहृतः |सत्यया वो देवहूत्या हुवेम श्रिण्वतो देवाः अवसे स्वस्तये ||
viśve yajatrā adhi vocatotaye trāyadhvaṁ no durevāyā abhihrutaḥ | satyayā vo devahūtyā huvema śṛṇvato devā avase svastaye ||
  • viśve yajatrā: This phrase can be translated as "all the worshippers" or "all those who perform sacrifices." It signifies the collective effort of individuals coming together for a common purpose, much like a community gathering for a festival or a religious ceremony.
  • adhi vocatotaye: This means "to call upon" or "to invoke." It emphasizes the importance of intention and focus in any spiritual or communal activity. For example, when people gather for a wedding, they invoke blessings for the couple, highlighting the significance of their intentions.
  • trāyadhvaṁ: This translates to "may you protect us." It is a plea for safety and guidance, akin to how individuals seek protection from their families or communities during challenging times, such as during natural disasters or personal crises.
  • no durevāyā abhihrutaḥ: This phrase means "may we not be far from the divine." It reflects the desire for closeness to the divine or spiritual guidance, similar to how people often seek solace in prayer or meditation to feel connected to a higher power.
  • satyayā vo devahūtyā: This translates to "with truth, we call upon the divine." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals strive to live authentically, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • huvema: This means "we invoke" or "we offer." It signifies the act of offering oneself or one's intentions to the divine, much like how people dedicate their efforts to a cause, such as volunteering for community service.
  • śṛṇvato devā avase svastaye: This translates to "may the gods hear us and grant us well-being." It is a prayer for health and prosperity, reflecting the universal human desire for well-being, which can be seen in various cultures through rituals and prayers for health and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, intention, and the pursuit of truth in our spiritual and everyday lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, urging us to seek protection, truth, and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in community gatherings, personal prayers, and moments of reflection, where individuals come together to invoke blessings and support one another in their journeys. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of unity and purpose, encouraging individuals to live with integrity and seek a deeper connection with the divine.
