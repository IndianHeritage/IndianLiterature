त्रयः पवयो मधुवाहने रथे सोमस्य वेनाम अनु विश्व इद विदुः |त्रयः स्कम्भासः स्कभितास आरभे त्रिर नक्तं याथस त्रिर व अश्विनाः दिवा ||
trayaḥ pavayo madhuvāhane rathe somasya venām anu viśva id viduḥ | trayaḥ skambhāsaḥ skabhitāsa ārabhe trir naktaṁ yāthas trir v aśvinā divā ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • trayaḥ pavayo madhuvāhane: The term "trayaḥ" refers to the three, while "pavayo" means the purifiers or the divine forces. "Madhuvāhane" can be interpreted as the one who carries sweetness or nectar. This suggests a connection to divine energies that bring joy and positivity.
    • rathe somasya venām anu viśva id viduḥ: "Rathe" means in the chariot, "somasya" refers to the moon or soma, which is often associated with nourishment and immortality. "Venām" means the flow or stream, and "anu viśva id viduḥ" translates to "all who know this." This part emphasizes the journey of life and the knowledge that guides us.
    • trayaḥ skambhāsaḥ skabhitāsa ārabhe: Here, "skambhāsaḥ" refers to the three pillars or supports, while "skabhitāsa" suggests stability or grounding. "Ārabhe" means to initiate or begin. This indicates the importance of foundational principles in our lives.
    • trir naktaṁ yāthas trir v aśvinā divā: "Trir" means three times, "naktaṁ" refers to night, and "yāthas" means to go or travel. "Aśvinā" refers to the twin deities associated with health and medicine, while "divā" means day. This part highlights the cyclical nature of life, encompassing both day and night, and the divine guidance we receive.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "trayaḥ pavayo" can be seen in teamwork, where three individuals or forces come together to create something beautiful, much like a successful project team that combines diverse skills to achieve a common goal.
    • The journey of life, as depicted in "rathe somasya," can be compared to personal growth. Just as the moon waxes and wanes, individuals experience ups and downs, learning and evolving through their experiences.
    • The "trayaḥ skambhāsaḥ" can be related to the three pillars of a strong foundation in life: health, relationships, and purpose. These pillars support our well-being and stability.
    • The cyclical nature of "trir naktaṁ" reflects the daily routines we follow, balancing work and rest, and the importance of seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to the aśvinā who provide healing and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance, the importance of foundational principles, and the cyclical nature of life. It teaches us to seek balance and harmony in our lives, drawing on the support of others and the wisdom of the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate teamwork, embrace personal growth, establish strong foundations, and maintain a balance between work and rest. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys and that divine forces are always at play, guiding us towards fulfillment and joy.
