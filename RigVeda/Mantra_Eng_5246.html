यः पञ्च चर्षणीर अभि निषसाद दमे-दमे |कविर गृहपतिर युवा ||
yaḥ pañca carṣaṇīr abhi niṣasāda dame-dame | kavir gṛhapatir yuvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • pañca: five
    • carṣaṇīr: (related to) the earth or the ground
    • abhi: towards
    • niṣasāda: sat down or settled
    • dame-dame: in each and every place or abode
    • kavir: the wise or the poet
    • gṛhapatir: the lord of the house or the master
    • yuvā: young or youthful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise individual who has settled in various places on earth.
    • The term "five" can symbolize the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that are foundational to life.
    • The phrase "kavir gṛhapatir yuvā" suggests that this wise person is also a youthful master of their domain, indicating a blend of wisdom and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, a leader who embodies wisdom and energy can effectively guide people through challenges, much like a mentor who helps students navigate their educational journey.
    • In environmental conservation, individuals who understand the importance of the five elements can work towards sustainable practices, ensuring that each aspect of nature is respected and preserved.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and youthful energy in leadership and stewardship of the earth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community leadership, environmental activism, and personal development.
    • By embodying the qualities of the "wise young master," individuals can inspire others and create positive change in their surroundings.
