न ता मिनन्ति मायिनो न धीरा व्रता देवानाम् प्रथमाः ध्रुवाणि |न रोडसी अद्रुहा वेद्याभिर न पर्वताः निनमे तस्थिवांसः ||
na tā minanti māyino na dhīrā vratā devānām prathamā dhruvāṇi | na rodasī adruhā vedyābhir na parvatā niname tasthivāṁsaḥ ||
  • na tā minanti māyino: This phrase can be translated as "those who are deluded do not understand." It emphasizes the idea that individuals who are caught in illusions or distractions fail to grasp the deeper truths of existence. For example, in modern life, people often get lost in material pursuits, neglecting their spiritual or emotional well-being.
  • na dhīrā vratā devānām prathamā dhruvāṇi: This part means "the wise, who are steadfast in their vows, are the first to attain certainty." It highlights the importance of wisdom and commitment. In real life, individuals who remain dedicated to their goals, whether in education, career, or personal development, often achieve success. For instance, a student who diligently studies and adheres to their academic commitments is more likely to excel.
  • na rodasī adruhā vedyābhir: This translates to "the heavens do not deceive those who are knowledgeable." It suggests that true knowledge leads to clarity and understanding. In practical terms, this can be seen in how informed individuals make better decisions. For example, a person who researches thoroughly before making a financial investment is less likely to fall prey to scams.
  • na parvatā niname tasthivāṁsaḥ: This means "the mountains do not shake for those who are steadfast." It symbolizes stability and resilience. In everyday life, this can be related to individuals who remain strong in the face of adversity. For instance, a community that comes together to support each other during a natural disaster exemplifies this steadfastness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, commitment, and resilience in navigating life's challenges. It teaches that those who are knowledgeable and steadfast in their pursuits will find clarity and stability, while those who are caught in illusions may struggle to understand their path. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, education, and community support, reminding us to seek knowledge, remain dedicated to our goals, and support one another in times of need.
