मन्त्र: उता नो हिर बुध्न्योऽ मयस् कः शिशुं न पिप्युषीव वेति सिन्धुः |येन नपातम अपां जुनाम मनोजुवो वृषणो यं वहन्ति ||
uta no 'hir budhnyo3 mayas kaḥ śiśuṁ na pipyuṣīva veti sindhuḥ | yena napātam apāṁ junāma manojuvo vṛṣaṇo yaṁ vahanti ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the wise ones, who are like the river, not deprive us of our sustenance. By whom we call upon the waters, the strong ones who carry the essence of life."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: May
    • no: us
    • hir: wise
    • budhnyo: those who know
    • mayas: sustenance or nourishment
    • kaḥ: who
    • śiśuṁ: child or offspring
    • na: not
    • pipyuṣīva: like the river
    • veti: knows or leads
    • sindhuḥ: the ocean or waters
    • yena: by whom
    • napātam: waters
    • apāṁ: of the waters
    • junāma: we call upon
    • manojuvo: strong or powerful
    • vṛṣaṇo: those who carry
    • yaṁ: whom
    • vahanti: they carry
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding us towards sustenance and nourishment.
    • The river symbolizes a source of life, much like how knowledge and wisdom can nourish our minds and spirits.
    • It reflects a plea for guidance from wise beings, akin to seeking advice from mentors or elders in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, elders often provide wisdom that helps younger generations make informed decisions, similar to how the wise ones in the mantra guide us.
    • In education, teachers act as the rivers of knowledge, nourishing students with information and critical thinking skills.
    • In personal life, seeking advice from trusted friends or family members can lead to better choices, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and guidance in our pursuit of sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
    • It teaches us to value the knowledge of others and to seek support from those who are wise.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, mentorship, and community support, reminding us that we are not alone in our journey and that wisdom is a vital resource.
