मन्त्र: उता त्वा भृगुवच्छुचेमनुष्वद अग्न आहुत |अङ्गिरस्वद धवामहे ||
uta tvā bhṛguvac chuce manuṣvad agna āhuta | aṅgirasvad dhavāmahe ||
  • uta tvā - This phrase can be translated as "we invoke you" or "we call upon you." It signifies a plea or a request for divine attention and support.
  • bhṛguvac - Referring to the "Bhrigus," a clan of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. This term emphasizes the importance of wisdom in our lives.
  • shuce - Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this word highlights the need for purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • manuṣvad - This translates to "human" or "mankind," indicating the connection between the divine and human beings, and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • agna - Referring to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. Fire is often seen as a purifying force.
  • āhuta - This means "offered" or "invoked," suggesting the act of making offerings to the divine, which is a common practice in many cultures.
  • aṅgirasvad - This term relates to the "Angirasas," another group of sages known for their spiritual insights and contributions to Vedic literature.
  • dhavāmahe - Translated as "we praise" or "we honor," this phrase encapsulates the act of reverence and gratitude towards the divine and the wisdom of the sages.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine wisdom and purity in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the connection between humanity and the divine, urging us to seek knowledge and maintain purity in our thoughts and actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their intentions and strive for purity in their actions, seeking wisdom from mentors or spiritual guides.
  • In community rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and guidance from the divine, fostering a sense of unity and purpose among participants.
  • In educational settings, it can inspire students to value knowledge and wisdom, encouraging them to pursue learning with a pure heart and mind.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance, the pursuit of wisdom, and the importance of maintaining purity in our lives, which are essential for personal and collective growth.
