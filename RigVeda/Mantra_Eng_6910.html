मन्त्र: उता त्वं मगवञ्च्छृणु यस्ते वष्टि ववक्षि तत् |यद्वीळयासि वीळु तत् ||
uta tvam maghavañ chṛṇu yas te vaṣṭi vavakṣi tat | yad vīḻayāsi vīḻu tat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tvam: you
    • maghavañ: O Mighty One
    • chṛṇu: listen
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • vaṣṭi: offering
    • vavakṣi: you will accept
    • tat: that
    • yad: which
    • vīḻayāsi: you will bestow
    • vīḻu: wealth
    • tat: that
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically addressing a powerful deity, often interpreted as Indra or a similar figure.
    • The speaker is inviting the deity to listen to their offerings and to accept them.
    • It emphasizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine, highlighting the importance of communication and reciprocity.
    • The mantra also expresses a desire for blessings, particularly in the form of wealth and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to a person seeking guidance or support from a mentor or leader. For instance, an entrepreneur may seek advice from a successful businessperson, hoping that their insights will lead to prosperity.
    • In a community setting, individuals may come together to offer prayers or rituals to invoke blessings for their collective well-being, similar to how the mantra seeks divine acceptance of offerings.
    • In personal life, someone may recite this mantra during times of need, such as when facing financial difficulties, as a way to express their hopes for divine intervention and support.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of faith and the act of reaching out to the divine for support and blessings.
    • It teaches us that communication with higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or rituals, is a vital aspect of spiritual practice.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help and guidance, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles and that support is available if we ask for it.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment in our relationships with others and the divine.
