वनेमा पूर्वीर आर्यो मनीषा अग्निः सुशोको विश्वाण्य अश्याः ||
vanema pūrvīr aryo manīṣā agniḥ suśoko viśvāny aśyāḥ ||
  • vanema: This word can be interpreted as "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking connection or guidance from a higher power.
  • pūrvīr: This term means "of the ancients" or "from the past." It emphasizes the wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations.
  • aryo: This word refers to "noble" or "worthy." It suggests a sense of honor and integrity in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • manīṣā: This translates to "intellect" or "thought." It highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in our actions and decisions.
  • agniḥ: This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence. It is often associated with sacrifice and offerings.
  • suśoko: This term can be interpreted as "well-being" or "happiness." It reflects the desire for a prosperous and fulfilling life.
  • viśvāny: This word means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • aśyāḥ: This term translates to "nourishing" or "sustaining." It emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, in our lives.

The mantra "vanema pūrvīr aryo manīṣā agniḥ suśoko viśvāny aśyāḥ" encapsulates a profound invocation for wisdom, nobility, and well-being. It calls upon the ancient knowledge and intellect to guide us in our pursuit of a fulfilling life, symbolized by the divine fire (Agni) that purifies and transforms our intentions into reality.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a meditative focus to seek wisdom from their ancestors and cultivate noble thoughts and actions in their daily lives.
  • Community Building: In a collective setting, such as a family or community gathering, reciting this mantra can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, encouraging members to support one another in their pursuits.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The invocation of Agni in rituals signifies the importance of offerings and sacrifices for the greater good, reminding participants of their responsibilities towards society and the environment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, nobility, and interconnectedness in our lives. By invoking the ancient knowledge and the divine fire, we can aspire to lead lives that are not only fulfilling for ourselves but also beneficial for the wider community. It encourages us to seek a balance between personal aspirations and collective well-being, making it highly applicable in today's interconnected world.
