त्रिश्चिन नो अद्या भवतं नवेदसां विभुर वां याम उता रातिर अश्विनाः |युवोर हि यन्त्रं हिम्येव वाससोऽभ्यायंसेन्याभवतं मनिषिभिः ||
triś cin no adyā bhavataṁ navedasā vibhur vāṁ yāma uta rātir aśvinā | yuvor hi yantraṁ himyeva vāsaso 'bhyāyaṁsenyā bhavatam manīṣibhiḥ ||
  • triś cin no adyā bhavataṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May we know you today." The word "triś" signifies a threefold aspect, which can refer to the three realms of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual. In real life, this can be seen as a call for holistic understanding and awareness in our daily lives.
  • navedasā vibhur vāṁ: This translates to "the one who is vast and knows the nine Vedas." The term "naveda" refers to the comprehensive knowledge that encompasses various fields of wisdom. In modern terms, this can relate to the importance of education and the pursuit of knowledge across different disciplines, emphasizing the value of being well-rounded.
  • yāma uta rātir aśvinā: This means "may we attain the night and the day, O Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the transition between day and night. This can be interpreted as a desire for balance in life, where one seeks harmony between work (day) and rest (night), akin to the modern concept of work-life balance.
  • yuvor hi yantraṁ: This translates to "the instrument of the two." Here, "yuvor" refers to the duality of existence, such as body and mind, or action and thought. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we navigate our relationships and responsibilities, ensuring that both aspects are in sync for a fulfilling life.
  • himyeva vāsaso 'bhyāyaṁ: This phrase means "like the warmth of the garments." It suggests the comfort and protection that comes from knowledge and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be likened to the support we receive from our community and loved ones, which provides us with the strength to face challenges.
  • senyā bhavatam manīṣibhiḥ: This translates to "may we be guided by the wise." The term "manīṣibhiḥ" refers to those who possess deep understanding and insight. In a real-world context, this emphasizes the importance of mentorship and guidance from experienced individuals in our personal and professional journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for knowledge, balance, guidance, and holistic understanding in life. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of various aspects of existence—physical, mental, and spiritual. In today's fast-paced world, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, maintain balance, and value the support of those around us. By applying these principles, we can navigate our lives with greater awareness and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
