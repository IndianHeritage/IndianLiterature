इच्छन्ति त्वा सोम्याशः सखायः सुन्वन्ति सोमं दधति प्रयांसी |तितिक्षन्ते अभिशस्तिं जनानाम इन्द्र त्वद आ कश्चन हि प्रकेतः ||
icchanti tvā somyāsaḥ sakhāyaḥ sunvanti somaṁ dadhati prayāṁsi | titikṣante abhiśastiṁ janānām indra tvad ā kaś cana hi praketaḥ ||
  • icchanti tvā somyāsaḥ sakhāyaḥ: This phrase translates to "the friendly companions desire you." Here, "sakhāyaḥ" refers to friends or companions, indicating a sense of camaraderie and support among individuals.
  • sunvanti somaṁ dadhati prayāṁsi: This means "they offer the soma and prepare the offerings." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and divine inspiration. The act of offering signifies gratitude and the importance of sharing resources within a community.
  • titikṣante abhiśastiṁ janānām: This translates to "the people endure the hardships." It emphasizes resilience and the ability to withstand challenges, highlighting the strength of the community in facing adversities together.
  • indra tvad ā kaś cana hi praketaḥ: This means "O Indra, no one can truly comprehend you." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, representing power and authority. This line reflects the idea that the divine or the ultimate truth is beyond human understanding, suggesting humility in the face of the vastness of existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, colleagues who support each other during challenging projects embody the essence of "icchanti tvā somyāsaḥ sakhāyaḥ." Their collaboration leads to success and a positive work environment.
    • Communities that come together to share resources during a crisis, such as food drives during natural disasters, reflect the spirit of "sunvanti somaṁ dadhati prayāṁsi," showcasing the importance of collective effort and generosity.
    • Individuals who face personal struggles, such as illness or loss, often find strength in their communities, illustrating "titikṣante abhiśastiṁ janānām." Support groups and friendships can provide the resilience needed to overcome hardships.
    • The acknowledgment of the mysteries of life and the universe, as expressed in "indra tvad ā kaś cana hi praketaḥ," can be seen in philosophical discussions or spiritual practices where individuals seek understanding but recognize the limits of human comprehension.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of friendship, community support, resilience in the face of challenges, and the humility required when confronting the divine or the unknown. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and communities in fostering strong relationships, sharing resources, enduring hardships, and embracing the mysteries of existence. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human experiences and the strength found in unity.
