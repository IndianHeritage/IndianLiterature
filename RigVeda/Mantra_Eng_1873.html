असुन्बन्तं समं जहि दूनाशं यो न ते मयः |अस्मभ्यम अस्य वेदनं दद्धि सूरीश चिद ओहते ||
asunvantaṁ samaṁ jahi dūṇāśaṁ yo na te mayaḥ | asmabhyam asya vedanaṁ daddhi sūriś cid ohate ||
  • asunvantaṁ: This term can be broken down into "asu" meaning "life" or "breath" and "nvantaṁ" meaning "possessing" or "having." Thus, it refers to those who possess life or vitality.
  • samaṁ: This word translates to "equal" or "balanced." It signifies harmony and equilibrium in life.
  • jahi: This means "to conquer" or "to overcome." It implies the act of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • dūṇāśaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is troubled" or "the one who is in distress." It refers to those who are facing difficulties or adversities.
  • yo: This is a pronoun meaning "who." It is used to refer to a person or entity.
  • na: This means "not" or "no," indicating negation.
  • te: This translates to "your" or "to you," indicating possession or relation.
  • mayaḥ: This term means "illusion" or "deception." It refers to the false perceptions or misunderstandings that can cloud judgment.
  • asmabhyam: This means "to us" or "for us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • asya: This translates to "of this" or "of that," referring to something specific.
  • vedanaṁ: This term means "pain" or "suffering." It refers to the experiences of distress or hardship.
  • daddhi: This means "to give" or "to bestow." It implies the act of providing or offering something.
  • sūriś: This term can be interpreted as "the wise" or "the enlightened." It refers to those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • cid: This means "consciousness" or "awareness." It signifies the state of being aware or enlightened.
  • ohate: This translates to "to be protected" or "to be shielded." It implies a sense of safety or security.

The mantra essentially conveys a message of overcoming distress and achieving balance in life through awareness and wisdom. It encourages individuals to conquer their troubles and not be deceived by illusions that may cloud their judgment. The call to action is directed towards those who are suffering, urging them to seek support and wisdom from the enlightened.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as health issues or emotional distress, one can draw strength from this mantra to seek balance and overcome challenges.
  • Community Support: In times of collective hardship, such as during natural disasters or economic downturns, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity and support among individuals.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, promoting personal development and spiritual enlightenment.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra emphasizes the significance of overcoming adversity through wisdom and community support. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals to maintain balance in their lives and to seek enlightenment, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
