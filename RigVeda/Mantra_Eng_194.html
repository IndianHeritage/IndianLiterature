अभि त्वां पूर्वपीतये सृजामि सोम्यम् मधु |मरुद्भिर अग्न आ गहि ||
abhi tvā pūrvapītaye sṛjāmi somyam madhu | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tvā pūrvapītaye sṛjāmi somyam madhu | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • pūrvapītaye: for the ancient drink (soma)
    • sṛjāmi: I create or I offer
    • somyam: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • madhu: sweet, honey
    • marudbhir: by the winds or the Maruts (storm deities)
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ā gahi: come, approach
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, invoking him to come forth and accept the offerings made to him.
    • The reference to "pūrvapītaye" indicates a connection to the ancient tradition of consuming soma, which is often associated with divine inspiration and immortality.
    • The use of "madhu" signifies sweetness and abundance, suggesting that the offerings are not just material but also imbued with positive qualities.
    • The mention of "marudbhir" highlights the role of the Maruts, who are considered the forces of nature, emphasizing the interconnectedness of natural elements and divine forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, offerings to Agni can be seen in various Hindu ceremonies, where fire is central to the process, symbolizing purification and the divine connection.
    • The concept of "soma" can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in contemporary life, where individuals seek experiences that elevate their consciousness.
    • The sweetness of "madhu" can be likened to the positive outcomes of our actions, where good deeds and intentions lead to fruitful results in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energies in our lives, particularly through the element of fire, which represents transformation and purification.
    • It teaches us to approach our endeavors with a sense of reverence and gratitude, recognizing the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to create positive intentions and actions, leading to a harmonious existence and a deeper connection with the divine.
