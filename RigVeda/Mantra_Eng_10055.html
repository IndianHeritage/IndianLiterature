दूरम इता पाणयो वरिया उद गावो यन्तु मिनतीर ऋतेन | बृहस्पतिर या अविन्दन निगूढाः सोमो ग्रावाण ऋषयश्च विप्राः ||
dūram ita paṇayo varīya ud gāvo yantu minatīr ṛtena | bṛhaspatir yā avindan nigūḻhāḥ somo grāvāṇa ṛṣayaś ca viprāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūram: far away
    • ita: from here
    • paṇayo: the merchants or traders
    • varīya: the best or the most excellent
    • ud: up or above
    • gāvo: cows (symbolizing wealth and prosperity)
    • yantu: may go or proceed
    • minatīr: with the help of
    • ṛtena: truth or righteousness
    • bṛhaspatir: the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge
    • yā: who
    • avindan: found or discovered
    • nigūḻhāḥ: hidden or concealed
    • somo: the moon or soma (a divine drink)
    • grāvāṇa: the one who brings or leads
    • ṛṣayaś: the sages or seers
    • ca: and
    • viprāḥ: the learned or wise ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of traders or merchants who, guided by truth and righteousness, seek prosperity and abundance.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth and nourishment, indicating that true prosperity comes from ethical means.
    • The reference to "Bṛhaspati" and "soma" suggests that wisdom and divine blessings are essential in achieving success.
    • The "sages" and "viprāḥ" represent the importance of knowledge and learning in guiding one's actions towards fruitful outcomes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern business, ethical practices and integrity are crucial for long-term success. Companies that prioritize honesty and transparency often build stronger relationships with customers and stakeholders.
    • Entrepreneurs who seek guidance from mentors or knowledgeable individuals can navigate challenges more effectively, much like the sages in the mantra.
    • The agricultural sector, where farmers rely on ethical practices to ensure sustainable growth, mirrors the symbolism of cows representing wealth and nourishment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of righteousness, wisdom, and ethical practices in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that true success is not just about material wealth but also about the means through which it is attained.
    • In today's world, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more sustainable and fulfilling life, both personally and professionally.
