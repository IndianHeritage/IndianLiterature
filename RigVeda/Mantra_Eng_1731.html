स्त्रियः सतीं तां उ मे पुंस आहुः पश्यद अक्षण्वान न विघेतद अन्धः |कविर यः पुत्रः स इमां आ चिकेत यस्तां विजानात स पितुः पितासत ||
striyaḥ satīs tām̐ u me puṁsa āhuḥ paśyad akṣaṇvān na vi cetad andhaḥ | kavir yaḥ putraḥ sa īm ā ciketa yas tā vijānāt sa pituṣ pitāsat ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks about the qualities of a wise person and the importance of knowledge and perception. It suggests that a person who is aware and observant is not blind to the truth, unlike those who lack insight.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • striyaḥ: Refers to women or feminine qualities, often symbolizing wisdom and nurturing.
    • satīs: Represents truthfulness or righteousness.
    • puṁsa: Refers to a man or masculine qualities, often associated with strength and action.
    • akṣaṇvān: One who has eyes, symbolizing perception and awareness.
    • andhaḥ: Means blind, representing ignorance or lack of insight.
    • kavir: A wise person or poet, someone who possesses deep knowledge.
    • putraḥ: Son, symbolizing lineage and continuity of wisdom.
    • ciketa: To understand or realize.
    • vijānāt: To know or comprehend.
    • pituṣ: Of the father, indicating the source of wisdom.
    • pitāsat: The essence of the father, representing inherited qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A wise leader in a community who listens to the concerns of others and makes informed decisions based on understanding rather than assumptions.
    • A teacher who encourages students to think critically and develop their own insights, rather than simply memorizing information.
    • A parent who imparts values of honesty and integrity to their children, ensuring that they grow up to be aware and responsible individuals.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, perception, and the transmission of knowledge across generations. It highlights that true understanding comes from being observant and aware, rather than being blind to the realities of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and parenting, where the cultivation of insight and awareness is essential for personal and communal growth.
