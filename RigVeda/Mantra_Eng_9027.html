विष्वे देवाः अकृपन्त समीयोर निष्पतन्त्योः |नासत्याव अब्रुवन् देवाः पुनर आवहताद इति ||
viśve devā akṛpanta samīcyor niṣpatantyoḥ | nāsatyāv abruvan devāḥ punar ā vahatād iti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśve: all, universal
    • devā: gods, divine beings
    • akṛpanta: they did not hold back, they did not restrain
    • samīcyor: in unity, together
    • niṣpatantyoḥ: they emerged, they came forth
    • nāsatyāv: in the context of the Asvins, the twin gods
    • abruvan: they spoke, they declared
    • devāḥ: the gods
    • punar: again, once more
    • ā vahatād: they brought forth, they carried
    • iti: thus, so
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and collective action of the gods.
    • It emphasizes that the divine beings did not hold back in their efforts and emerged together.
    • The reference to the Asvins, who are known for their healing and assistance, suggests a theme of support and collaboration.
    • The gods' declaration to "bring forth again" indicates a cycle of renewal and the importance of divine intervention in human affairs.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community project, when individuals come together with a shared purpose, they can achieve remarkable results, much like the gods in the mantra.
    • In healthcare, the collaboration of various specialists (akin to the Asvins) can lead to better patient outcomes, demonstrating the power of unity in action.
    • In nature, the changing seasons represent the cyclical renewal that the mantra speaks of, where life emerges anew after periods of dormancy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, collaboration, and the divine support that can be invoked in times of need.
    • It serves as a reminder that collective efforts can lead to greater achievements and that divine forces are always present to assist those who seek help.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together harmoniously, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
