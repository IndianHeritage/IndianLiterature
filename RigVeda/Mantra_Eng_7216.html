तुविशुष्म तुविक्रतो शचिवो विश्वयामते |आ पप्राथ महित्वना ||
tuviśuṣma tuvikrato śacīvo viśvayā mate | ā paprātha mahitvanā ||
  • tuviśuṣma: This term can be broken down into two parts: "tu" meaning "you" and "viśuṣma" which refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has great strength." In real life, this can be likened to individuals who possess immense inner strength, such as leaders or activists who inspire others through their actions.
  • tuvikrato: Here, "tu" again means "you," and "vikrato" refers to "the one who is wise" or "the one who acts with great thought." This can be compared to wise decision-makers in society, such as judges or mentors, who carefully consider their actions and their impact on others.
  • śacīvo: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is capable" or "the one who has the ability." In a real-world context, this could refer to skilled professionals, such as doctors or engineers, who utilize their capabilities to improve the lives of others.
  • viśvayā mate: "viśvayā" means "in all aspects" or "universally," and "mate" refers to "thought" or "intellect." This phrase emphasizes the importance of universal wisdom and understanding in all areas of life, akin to how educators or philosophers strive to impart knowledge that is applicable to various situations.
  • ā paprātha: This term can be understood as "to have achieved" or "to have attained." It signifies the culmination of efforts and the realization of goals, similar to how individuals achieve personal milestones through hard work and perseverance.
  • mahitvanā: This word translates to "greatness" or "majesty." It reflects the idea of achieving greatness through wisdom, strength, and capability, much like historical figures who have left a lasting impact on society through their contributions.

Conclusion: The mantra "tuviśuṣma tuvikrato śacīvo viśvayā mate | ā paprātha mahitvanā ||" encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, capability, and the pursuit of greatness. It serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in physical strength but in the ability to think wisely and act with purpose. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, education, and personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness through their actions and decisions. It highlights the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and capability in achieving significant goals and making a positive impact on the world.
