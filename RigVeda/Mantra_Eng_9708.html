इहैव स्तं मा वियौष्टं विश्वमायुर व्यश्नुतम् |कीर्णन्तौ पुत्रैर नप्तृभिर मोदमानौ स्वे गृहे ||
ihaiva stam mā vi yauṣṭaṁ viśvam āyur vy aśnutam | krīḻantau putrair naptṛbhir modamānau sve gṛhe ||
  • Translation: "Here, may we attain the strength of life, may we enjoy the fullness of life, may we be joyful with our children and grandchildren, rejoicing in our own home."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ihaiva: Here, in this life.
    • stam: Strength or vitality.
    • mā: May we.
    • vi yauṣṭaṁ: Attain or achieve.
    • viśvam: The entirety or fullness of life.
    • āyur: Life or longevity.
    • vy aśnutam: May we enjoy or experience.
    • krīḻantau: Rejoicing or celebrating.
    • putrair: With children.
    • naptṛbhir: With grandchildren.
    • modamānau: Being joyful or delighted.
    • sve gṛhe: In our own home.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of family and community. For instance, during family gatherings or festivals, the joy of being surrounded by loved ones reflects the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern life, achieving a work-life balance can be seen as a way to attain the "strength of life" mentioned in the mantra. Prioritizing time with family can lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • Celebrating milestones such as birthdays or anniversaries with family can be a practical application of this mantra, where joy and togetherness are experienced.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a fulfilling and joyful life, rooted in the strength of family bonds. It encourages individuals to cherish their relationships and find happiness in their homes. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by fostering strong family ties, celebrating togetherness, and ensuring that one's life is enriched by the love and support of family members. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true fulfillment comes from the connections we nurture with those we hold dear.
