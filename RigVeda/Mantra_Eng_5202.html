यो अपाचीनं तमसि मदन्तीः प्राचिश्चकार नृतमः शचीभिः |तम ईशानं वस्वो अग्निं गृणीषे 'नानतं दमयन्तं पृथन्यून् ||
yo apācīne tamasi madantīḥ prācīś cakāra nṛtamaḥ śacībhiḥ | tam īśānaṁ vasvo agniṁ gṛṇīṣe 'nānataṁ damayantam pṛtanyūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • apācīne: in the dark
    • tamasi: in ignorance
    • madantīḥ: intoxicated
    • prācīś: the eastern direction
    • cakāra: created
    • nṛtamaḥ: the best of men
    • śacībhiḥ: with the powerful
    • tam: that
    • īśānaṁ: lord
    • vasvo: of wealth
    • agniṁ: fire
    • gṛṇīṣe: I sing
    • 'nānataṁ: unyielding
    • damayantam: subduing
    • pṛtanyūn: the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force that brings light and knowledge to dispel ignorance.
    • The phrase "who created in the dark" suggests the emergence of wisdom from confusion.
    • The "best of men" refers to enlightened individuals who guide others towards knowledge.
    • The mention of "fire" symbolizes transformation and purification, essential for spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be related to moments of enlightenment when one overcomes ignorance, such as learning a new skill or gaining insight into a complex problem.
    • In a societal context, it can be seen in movements that aim to educate and uplift communities, such as literacy programs or awareness campaigns about health and wellness.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of knowledge and enlightenment in overcoming ignorance.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to be like the "best of men" who guide others.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, personal development, and community service, reminding us of our responsibility to share knowledge and foster growth.
