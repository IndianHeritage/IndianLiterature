स वृत्रहेन्द्रः कृष्णयोनीः पुरंदरो दासीर एयरयद वि |अजनयन मनवे क्षाम अपश च सत्रा शंसं यजमानस्य तूतोत ||
sa vṛtrahendraḥ kṛṣṇayonīḥ puraṁdaro dāsīr airayad vi | ajanayan manave kṣām apaś ca satrā śaṁsaṁ yajamānasya tūtot ||
  • sa vṛtrahendraḥ: This phrase refers to "He who is the Indra of Vritra." In Vedic mythology, Indra is the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, while Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents drought and obstacles. This part signifies the overcoming of challenges and the triumph of good over evil.
  • kṛṣṇayonīḥ: Translated as "the one born of the black (or dark) womb," this term can symbolize the source of creation and the nurturing aspect of nature. In real life, this can be related to the idea of the earth or the universe giving birth to life, emphasizing the importance of nature in our existence.
  • puraṁdaro: This term means "the destroyer of cities," often associated with Indra's role in vanquishing demons and protecting the heavens. It can be interpreted as the force that removes obstacles and paves the way for progress, akin to how innovation and creativity can dismantle outdated systems in modern society.
  • dāsīr airayad vi: This phrase can be understood as "the servants who have been called." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration. In contemporary life, this can be seen in teamwork and collective efforts in various fields, such as business, education, and social movements.
  • ajanayan manave: This translates to "giving birth to Manu," where Manu is considered the progenitor of humanity in Vedic texts. This signifies the importance of lineage, heritage, and the responsibility of nurturing future generations, which is relevant in family and societal contexts today.
  • kṣām apaś ca: This means "the one who is capable and has the ability." It emphasizes the qualities of resilience and capability, which are essential in overcoming life's challenges. In real life, this can be related to personal development and the pursuit of skills that empower individuals to succeed.
  • satrā: This term refers to "sacrifices" or "offerings." It signifies the importance of giving back to the community and the universe. In modern life, this can be seen in charitable acts, volunteering, and the concept of social responsibility.
  • śaṁsaṁ yajamānasya tūtot: This phrase can be interpreted as "the blessings of the one who is performing the sacrifice." It highlights the significance of intention and devotion in actions. In real life, this can be related to the idea that sincere efforts and good intentions lead to positive outcomes, whether in personal endeavors or community projects.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of overcoming obstacles, the nurturing aspect of nature, the importance of community, and the responsibility towards future generations. Its significance lies in the reminder that through collaboration, resilience, and intention, individuals can navigate challenges and contribute positively to society. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, teamwork, and social responsibility, encouraging a holistic approach to living harmoniously with oneself and the world.
