पाहि गायांधसो मद इन्द्राय मेध्यातिथे |यः सम्मिश्लो हर्योर यः सुते सचा वज्री रथो हिरण्ययः ||
pāhi gāyāndhaso mada indrāya medhyātithe | yaḥ sammiślo haryor yaḥ sute sacā vajrī ratho hiraṇyayaḥ ||
  • pāhi: This word translates to "protect" or "guard." It signifies the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • gāyāndhaso: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is full of joy" or "the one who brings happiness." It emphasizes the importance of joy and positivity.
  • mada: This means "intoxication" or "ecstasy." It refers to a state of bliss or heightened awareness, often associated with spiritual experiences.
  • indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • medhyātithe: This can be understood as "the one who is worthy of offerings" or "the noble guest." It highlights the importance of hospitality and respect for guests.
  • yaḥ: This means "who" and introduces a clause that describes a person or entity.
  • sammiślo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is united" or "the one who brings together." It signifies harmony and unity among people.
  • haryor: This refers to "the two horses," which can symbolize speed, strength, and the journey of life.
  • sute: This means "in the drink" or "in the offering," often referring to the sacred offerings made to the deities.
  • sacā: This translates to "and also" or "together with," indicating the inclusion of additional qualities or elements.
  • vajrī: This means "the one who wields the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • ratho: This translates to "the chariot," which can symbolize the journey of life and the means of achieving one's goals.
  • hiraṇyayaḥ: This means "golden" or "wealthy," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.

In summary, this mantra invokes the divine for protection, joy, and strength while emphasizing the importance of unity, hospitality, and prosperity. It reflects the interconnectedness of life and the need for divine guidance in our endeavors.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be recited during times of uncertainty or when seeking protection and guidance in personal or professional matters.
  • It serves as a reminder to cultivate joy and positivity in our lives, encouraging us to focus on the good and share that joy with others.
  • The emphasis on hospitality and respect for others can be applied in everyday interactions, fostering a sense of community and belonging.
  • In a world that often feels divided, the call for unity and harmony is particularly relevant, encouraging collaboration and understanding among individuals.
  • Finally, the aspiration for prosperity and abundance can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination and resilience.
