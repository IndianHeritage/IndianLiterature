इन्द्रं मित्रं वरुणं अग्निम् ऊतये मारुतं शर्धो अदितिं हवामहे |रथं न दुर्गाद् वसवः सुदानवो विश्वस्मान् नो अंहसो निष पिपर्तन ||
indram mitraṁ varuṇam agnim ūtaye mārutaṁ śardho aditiṁ havāmahe | rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, Indra symbolizes leadership and strength, akin to a leader in a community who brings prosperity.
  • Mitraṁ: Represents Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony. This can be related to the importance of building relationships and trust in personal and professional life.
  • Varuṇam: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna signifies the importance of maintaining balance and order in our lives, similar to how laws and ethics govern society.
  • Agnim: Means fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve goals, much like the motivation one feels when pursuing a dream.
  • Ūtaye: This term means to invoke or call upon. It emphasizes the act of seeking blessings or support, which is relevant in various rituals and personal prayers.
  • Mārutaṁ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, representing strength and power. This can be likened to the challenges we face in life that require resilience and courage to overcome.
  • Śardho: Means to gather or unite. This highlights the importance of community and collaboration, similar to how teams work together to achieve common goals.
  • Aditiṁ: Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, symbolizing infinity and the nurturing aspect of nature. This can be related to the unconditional love and support we receive from our families.
  • Havāmahe: Means we invoke or we call upon. This reflects the act of seeking divine assistance, which is a common practice in various cultures during times of need.
  • Rathaṁ: Refers to a chariot, symbolizing the journey of life. Just as a chariot carries its passengers, our choices and actions guide us through life.
  • Na durgād: Means not from difficulties. This emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles, akin to how individuals strive to rise above challenges in their careers or personal lives.
  • Vasavaḥ: Refers to the Vasus, deities of abundance and prosperity. This can be related to the pursuit of wealth and success in various endeavors.
  • Sudānavo: Means good nourishment or sustenance. This highlights the importance of health and well-being, similar to how a balanced diet supports a healthy lifestyle.
  • Viśvasmān: Refers to the all-pervading nature of the divine. This can be related to the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, emphasizing the importance of empathy and compassion.
  • No aṁhaso: Means our strength or power. This reflects the inner strength we possess, which is crucial for personal growth and resilience.
  • Niṣ pipartana: Means to protect or safeguard. This signifies the importance of safeguarding our values and principles in life, much like how communities protect their cultural heritage.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that emphasizes the importance of divine support, community, and personal strength. It teaches us to seek harmony, balance, and resilience in our lives. In real-life scenarios, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to build strong relationships, overcome challenges, and strive for prosperity while remaining connected to the greater universe. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to invoke divine blessings and work collaboratively towards a harmonious and fulfilling life.
