त्वां विष्णुर बृहन् क्षयो मित्रो गृणाति वरुणः |त्वां शर्धो मदत्य अनु मारुतम् ||
tvāṁ viṣṇur bṛhan kṣayo mitro gṛṇāti varuṇaḥ | tvāṁ śardho madaty anu mārutam ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and signifies the direct address to a divine entity, in this case, Lord Vishnu.
  • viṣṇur - Refers to "Vishnu," the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, representing the aspect of divinity that maintains cosmic order.
  • bṛhan - Means "great" or "vast," indicating the immense nature of Vishnu's presence and power.
  • kṣayo - Translates to "destruction" or "diminution," suggesting the aspect of Vishnu that can also bring about the end of negativity or chaos.
  • mitro - Means "friend," highlighting the benevolent and supportive nature of Vishnu towards his devotees.
  • gṛṇāti - This means "sings" or "praises," indicating the act of worship and reverence towards the divine.
  • varuṇaḥ - Refers to "Varuna," the god of water and cosmic order, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of the universe.
  • śardho - Means "with strength" or "with vigor," emphasizing the power and energy associated with the divine.
  • madaty - Translates to "is intoxicated" or "is filled with joy," suggesting a state of bliss that comes from divine connection.
  • anu - Means "following" or "after," indicating a sequence or continuity in the divine experience.
  • mārutam - Refers to "the winds" or "the storm," symbolizing the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life and the universe.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Lord Vishnu, acknowledging his greatness, his role as a protector, and his friendship towards humanity. It also recognizes the balance of creation and destruction, the joy of divine connection, and the influence of natural forces.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Protection: Just as Vishnu protects the universe, individuals can invoke this mantra for protection in times of distress or uncertainty.
  • Fostering Friendship: The mention of "mitro" encourages the cultivation of friendships and supportive relationships in our lives.
  • Embracing Change: The reference to "mārutam" reminds us to accept the changes and challenges in life, understanding that they are part of a larger cosmic order.
  • Finding Joy: The idea of being "madaty" encourages individuals to seek joy and fulfillment through spiritual practices and connections with the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, encouraging us to seek protection, foster relationships, embrace change, and find joy. It highlights the significance of maintaining a balance between creation and destruction, and the importance of connecting with the divine for guidance and support in our daily lives.
