त्वष्टुर जामातरं वयम् ईशानं राया इमहे |सुतावन्तो वायुः dyumnā जनासः ||
tvaṣṭur jāmātaraṁ vayam īśānaṁ rāya īmahe | sutāvanto vāyuṁ dyumnā janāsaḥ ||
  • tvaṣṭur: This word refers to the divine craftsman or the creator, often associated with the deity Tvashtar in Vedic literature. Tvashtar is known for his ability to create and shape the universe.
  • jāmātaraṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is the source of all beings" or "the one who brings forth life." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of creation.
  • vayam: This means "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community that is invoking the divine.
  • īśānaṁ: This word translates to "the lord" or "the ruler," referring to a higher power or divine authority that governs the universe.
  • rāya: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a desire for abundance and well-being in life.
  • īmahe: This means "we meditate upon" or "we invoke," indicating a call to the divine for guidance and blessings.
  • sutāvanto: This word refers to "those who are endowed with good qualities" or "virtuous beings," highlighting the importance of moral character.
  • vāyuṁ: This translates to "the wind" or "the vital breath," symbolizing life force and energy that sustains all living beings.
  • dyumnā: This term means "with glory" or "with brilliance," suggesting a pursuit of excellence and honor in life.
  • janāsaḥ: This refers to "the people" or "the community," emphasizing the collective nature of human existence and the importance of unity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a collective invocation to the divine craftsman, Tvashtar, asking for prosperity, guidance, and the nurturing of good qualities within the community. It reflects a deep understanding of the interconnectedness of life, where the divine is seen as the source of all creation and sustenance.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Building: The emphasis on collective identity and virtues can inspire communities to work together towards common goals, fostering unity and cooperation.
  • Personal Development: The call for good qualities encourages individuals to cultivate virtues such as honesty, kindness, and integrity in their daily lives.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the wind and life force can remind us of the importance of nature and the need to protect our environment for future generations.
  • Spiritual Practice: The act of meditation and invocation can be a part of personal spiritual practices, helping individuals connect with the divine and seek guidance in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of community, virtue, and the divine in our lives. It encourages us to seek prosperity not just for ourselves but for the collective, fostering a sense of responsibility towards each other and the world around us.
