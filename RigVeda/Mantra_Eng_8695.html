आ नः सुतास इन्दवः पुनानां धावतā रयिम् |वृष्टिद्यावो रीत्यापः स्वरविदः ||
ā naḥ sutāsa indavaḥ punānā dhāvatā rayim | vṛṣṭidyāvo rītyāpaḥ svarvidaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "May our" - a plea for blessings and support.
    • sutāsa: "sons" or "offspring" - referring to the progeny or the results of one's efforts.
    • indavaḥ: "the Indus" or "the divine" - often associated with divine blessings or prosperity.
    • punānā: "purifying" - a call for cleansing and sanctification.
    • dhāvatā: "running" or "flowing" - indicating movement towards a goal or aspiration.
    • rayim: "wealth" or "prosperity" - the desired outcome of efforts.
    • vṛṣṭi: "rain" - symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • dyāvo: "heavens" - representing the divine or higher realms.
    • rītyāpaḥ: "the waters" - often associated with life and sustenance.
    • svarvidaḥ: "knowledge of the heavens" - implying wisdom and understanding.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine blessings, particularly for prosperity and purification.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offspring and the continuity of life, seeking divine support in achieving wealth and abundance.
    • The reference to rain and waters signifies the essential elements for life, highlighting the interconnectedness of nature and human existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the well-being and success of their children, reflecting the desire for their offspring to thrive and prosper.
    • In agricultural communities, farmers pray for rain to ensure a good harvest, recognizing the vital role of nature in their livelihood.
    • In personal development, individuals seek knowledge and wisdom to navigate life's challenges, akin to the "knowledge of the heavens" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for prosperity, purity, and wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine, urging individuals to strive for a harmonious existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek balance in personal aspirations, familial responsibilities, and respect for nature's resources.
