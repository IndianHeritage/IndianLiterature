किमङ्ग त्वा मघवन भोजम् आहुः शिशीहि मा शिशयं त्वा श्रिणोमि |अप्नस्वती मम धीः अस्तु शक्र वसुविदम् भागम् इन्द्रा भर नः ||
kim aṅga tvā maghavan bhojam āhuḥ śiśīhi mā śiśayaṁ tvā śṛṇomi | apnasvatī mama dhīr astu śakra vasuvidam bhagam indrā bharā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim aṅga: What, indeed
    • tvā: to you
    • maghavan: O bountiful one
    • bhojam: food or nourishment
    • āhuḥ: they say
    • śiśīhi: may you protect
    • mā: do not
    • śiśayaṁ: let me be deprived
    • tvā: of you
    • śṛṇomi: I hear
    • apnasvatī: may there be abundance
    • mama: my
    • dhīr: intellect or understanding
    • astu: let it be
    • śakra: O powerful one
    • vasuvidam: who gives wealth
    • bhagam: fortune or blessings
    • indrā: O Indra
    • bharā: may you bestow
    • naḥ: upon us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the deity associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The speaker is seeking protection and nourishment from Indra, acknowledging his power and benevolence.
    • The phrase "may you protect" indicates a plea for safeguarding one's resources and well-being.
    • The request for "abundance" reflects a desire for prosperity and wisdom, essential for making sound decisions in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of drought, farmers may recite this mantra to invoke Indra's blessings for rain, which is crucial for their crops.
    • Individuals facing challenges in their careers might use this mantra to seek guidance and protection, hoping for clarity and wisdom in their decisions.
    • During community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to foster a sense of unity and collective strength, invoking blessings for the entire group.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors, whether they are personal, professional, or communal.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of nourishment, protection, and wisdom, suggesting that true prosperity comes from a holistic approach to life.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra serves as a reminder to remain humble and grateful for the resources we have, while actively seeking growth and understanding.
