विष्णुर इत्त्था परमम् अस्य विद्वाँज जातो बृहन् अभि पाति तृतीयम् |आसा यद अस्य पयो अक्रत स्वं सचेतसो अभ्य आर्चन्त्य अत्र ||
viṣṇur itthā paramam asya vidvāñ jāto bṛhann abhi pāti tṛtīyam | āsā yad asya payo akrata svaṁ sacetaso abhy arcanty atra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viṣṇur: Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hinduism.
    • itthā: thus, in this manner.
    • paramam: the supreme, the highest.
    • asya: of this, of him.
    • vidvāñ: the wise, the knowledgeable.
    • jāto: born, arisen.
    • bṛhann: great, vast.
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of.
    • pāti: protects, guards.
    • tṛtīyam: the third, the third aspect.
    • āsā: hope, desire.
    • yad: that which.
    • asya: of this, of him.
    • payo: milk, nourishment.
    • akrata: has made, has created.
    • svaṁ: his own, self.
    • sacetaso: with consciousness, with awareness.
    • abhy: towards, in the direction of.
    • arcanty: worship, honor.
    • atra: here, in this place.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of Vishnu as the supreme protector and preserver of the universe.
    • The wise individuals who understand the essence of this mantra recognize the greatness of Vishnu.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worship and reverence towards the divine, particularly in the form of nourishment and sustenance.
    • The mention of "milk" symbolizes purity and the nurturing aspect of life, suggesting that divine blessings are essential for growth and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and protection from higher powers during challenging times, much like how Vishnu is invoked for support.
    • In many cultures, rituals involving offerings of milk or other nourishing substances are performed to honor deities, reflecting the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • People often turn to wisdom and knowledge, akin to the "vidvāñ," to navigate life's complexities, highlighting the importance of understanding and reverence in personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives.
    • It teaches us that wisdom and reverence towards the higher powers can lead to protection and sustenance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge, nurture their spiritual connections, and engage in practices that honor the divine, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and protected existence.
