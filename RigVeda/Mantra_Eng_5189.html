एता नो अग्नि सौभागा दिदीहि अपि क्रतुं सुचेतसं वतेमा |विश्वा स्तोत्रिभ्यो गृणते च सन्तु युयम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
etā no agne saubhagā didīhy api kratuṁ sucetasaṁ vatema | viśvā stotṛbhyo gṛṇate ca santu yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • etā no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni (the divine fire), this is for us." Agni represents the divine energy and is often associated with purity and transformation.
  • saubhagā didīhy api: This means "may you bestow auspiciousness upon us." It reflects a desire for blessings and good fortune in life.
  • kratuṁ sucetasaṁ vatema: This translates to "we invoke the intellect and wisdom." Here, 'kratu' refers to mental power or will, and 'sucetasaṁ' implies clarity of thought.
  • viśvā stotṛbhyo gṛṇate: This means "may all beings sing praises." It emphasizes the importance of collective gratitude and recognition of the divine.
  • ca santu yūyam pāta: This translates to "may you all protect us." It is a plea for safety and guidance from the divine forces.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you always grant us well-being." It signifies a continuous wish for health, prosperity, and peace in life.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a deep yearning for divine blessings, wisdom, and protection. It highlights the significance of invoking higher powers for guidance and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection when individuals seek clarity, safety, and auspiciousness in their endeavors. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of collective gratitude and support in our lives.
