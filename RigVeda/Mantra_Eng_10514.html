प्र ससाहिषे पुरुहूत शत्रूञ्ज ज्येष्ठस ते शुष्म इह रातिर अस्तु |इन्द्रा भर दक्षिणेन वसूनि पतिः सिंधूनाम असि रेवतीनाम ||
pra sasāhiṣe puruhūta śatrūñ jyeṣṭhas te śuṣma iha rātir astu | indrā bhara dakṣiṇenā vasūni patiḥ sindhūnām asi revatīnām ||
  • pra sasāhiṣe: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be powerful" or "may you be strong." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in overcoming challenges.
  • puruhūta: This term refers to "the one who is invoked frequently" or "the one who is called upon." It signifies the importance of calling upon divine forces or energies in times of need.
  • śatrūñ: This word means "enemies" or "obstacles." It highlights the presence of challenges or adversaries in life that one must confront.
  • jyeṣṭhas te: This translates to "your elder" or "the elder one." It suggests the guidance and wisdom that comes from those who are more experienced.
  • śuṣma: This word means "subtle" or "fine." It indicates the need to be aware of the finer details in life and the subtleties of situations.
  • iha rātir astu: This phrase means "may there be night here." It can symbolize the need for rest, reflection, and rejuvenation in the midst of life's challenges.
  • indrā bhara: This means "O Indra, bestow" or "O Indra, grant." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing strength and power.
  • dakṣiṇenā: This translates to "with the right hand" or "with skill." It emphasizes the importance of using one's abilities wisely and effectively.
  • vasūni: This word refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance and resources that one seeks in life.
  • patiḥ: This means "lord" or "master." It indicates the need for leadership and guidance in one's endeavors.
  • sindhūnām: This translates to "of the rivers." It symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • asi revatīnām: This means "you are the one who brings prosperity." It highlights the role of divine forces in providing abundance and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, guidance, and the invocation of divine energies in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom from experienced individuals, to be aware of the subtleties in our surroundings, and to recognize the need for rest and rejuvenation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various situations such as facing personal challenges, seeking guidance in difficult decisions, or striving for prosperity and abundance in one's endeavors. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with resilience and purpose.
