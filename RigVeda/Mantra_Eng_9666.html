संश्रिष्टं धनम् उभयं समाकृतम् अस्मभ्यं दत्तां वरुणश्च मन्युः |भियं दधानाः हृदयेषु शत्रवः पराजितासो अप निलयन्ताम् ||
saṁsṛṣṭaṁ dhanam ubhayaṁ samākṛtam asmabhyaṁ dattāṁ varuṇaś ca manyuḥ | bhiyaṁ dadhānā hṛdayeṣu śatravaḥ parājitāso apa ni layantām ||
  • saṁsṛṣṭaṁ dhanam: This phrase refers to the wealth or resources that are gathered or accumulated. In real life, this can be likened to the collective wealth of a community or family, which is built through hard work and cooperation.
  • ubhayaṁ samākṛtam: This means that the wealth is created or formed in both ways, indicating a balance or harmony in the accumulation of resources. For example, a business that thrives through ethical practices and community support exemplifies this balance.
  • asmabhyaṁ dattāṁ: This translates to "given to us," suggesting that the wealth or resources are bestowed upon us, possibly by divine grace or through the efforts of ancestors. This can be seen in how families inherit properties or businesses, which are often seen as blessings.
  • varuṇaś ca manyuḥ: Varuna is a deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority, while "manyuḥ" refers to anger or wrath. This line implies that the wealth is protected by divine forces, and that one should be mindful of their actions to avoid incurring wrath. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of ethical behavior in maintaining prosperity.
  • bhiyaṁ dadhānā hṛdayeṣu: This means "instilling fear in the hearts," suggesting that there are adversaries or challenges that can threaten our wealth or peace. For instance, in business, competition can create fear, prompting companies to innovate and improve.
  • śatravaḥ parājitāso: This translates to "enemies who are defeated," indicating that overcoming challenges is possible. In a real-world context, this can refer to a team that successfully navigates market competition through strategic planning and teamwork.
  • apa ni layantām: This means "may they not take away," suggesting a plea for protection against loss. In life, this can be seen in the context of safeguarding one's assets, whether through insurance, legal means, or community support.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of collective wealth, ethical behavior, and the protection of resources. It highlights the balance between accumulation and moral responsibility, reminding us that while we may gather wealth, we must also be vigilant against challenges and adversaries. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in business ethics, community cooperation, and personal finance management. The significance lies in understanding that true prosperity is not just about material wealth, but also about maintaining harmony and integrity in our actions.
