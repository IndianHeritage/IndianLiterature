क्व स्य वीरः को अपश्यद इन्द्रं सुखरथम् ईयमानं हरिभ्यां |यो रायाऽवज्री सुतसोमम् इच्छन् तद ओको गन्ता पुरूहूत ऊती ||
kva1 sya vīraḥ ko apaśyad indraṁ sukharatham īyamānaṁ haribhyām | yo rāyā vajrī sutasomam icchan tad oko gantā puruhūta ūtī ||
  • kva1 sya vīraḥ: This phrase can be translated as "Where is the hero?" It signifies the quest for strength and valor in challenging situations. In real life, this can relate to moments when individuals seek courage to face their fears or challenges, such as a student preparing for an important exam or an athlete training for a competition.
  • ko apaśyad indraṁ: This translates to "Who has not seen Indra?" Indra is often regarded as the king of gods and a symbol of power and authority. This line suggests that everyone should strive to recognize and acknowledge the forces of strength and leadership in their lives. For example, in a workplace, recognizing a leader's guidance can inspire teamwork and collaboration.
  • sukharatham īyamānaṁ haribhyām: This means "moving towards happiness with the help of the horses." Here, 'horses' can symbolize the means or resources that aid in achieving happiness. In practical terms, this could refer to the support of friends and family that helps individuals pursue their goals, such as a supportive community for someone starting a new business.
  • yo rāyā vajrī sutasomam icchan: This translates to "He who desires wealth, like the one wielding the thunderbolt." The 'thunderbolt' symbolizes strength and determination. This line emphasizes the importance of ambition and the drive to achieve one's desires. For instance, a person working hard to achieve financial stability or a student aiming for academic excellence embodies this pursuit of wealth and success.
  • tad oko gantā puruhūta ūtī: This can be interpreted as "That which leads to the great abundance." It suggests that the path to prosperity is accessible to those who seek it. In real life, this could relate to the idea that hard work and perseverance lead to success, such as a farmer who diligently tends to their crops and reaps a bountiful harvest.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, recognizing leadership, pursuing happiness, and striving for wealth. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to harness their inner strength and resources to achieve their goals. In real life, it serves as a reminder that with determination and support, one can overcome challenges and attain prosperity, whether in personal endeavors, professional aspirations, or community contributions.
