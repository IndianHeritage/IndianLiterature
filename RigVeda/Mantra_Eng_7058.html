आजितुर्म् सत्पतिं विश्वचर्षणिं कृधि प्रजास्व आभगम् |प्र सू तिरा शचीभिर् ये त उक्तिनः क्रतुम् पुनत आanusक् ||
ājituraṁ satpatiṁ viśvacarṣaṇiṁ kṛdhi prajāsv ābhagam | pra sū tirā śacībhir ye ta ukthinaḥ kratum punata ānuṣak ||
  • ājituraṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is ever-present" or "the eternal." It signifies a divine presence that is constant and unchanging.
  • satpatiṁ: This translates to "the true lord" or "the righteous ruler." It refers to a figure of authority who embodies truth and righteousness.
  • viśvacarṣaṇiṁ: This term means "the one who encompasses all" or "the universal protector." It suggests a force that safeguards and nurtures all beings in the universe.
  • kṛdhi: This word means "to create" or "to make." It emphasizes the act of creation and the importance of bringing forth new life or ideas.
  • prajāsv: This translates to "the progeny" or "the offspring." It highlights the significance of life and the continuation of generations.
  • ābhagam: This means "to come forth" or "to manifest." It indicates the emergence of something significant or divine into the world.
  • pra sū: This can be interpreted as "to give birth" or "to produce." It emphasizes the act of creation and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • tirā: This word means "to cross over" or "to transcend." It suggests overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • śacībhir: This translates to "with the powerful" or "with the strong." It refers to the support and strength provided by those who are capable and influential.
  • ye ta ukthinaḥ: This phrase means "those who are devoted" or "those who are committed." It emphasizes the importance of dedication and loyalty to a cause or belief.
  • kratum: This word signifies "intention" or "resolve." It highlights the importance of having a clear purpose and determination in one's actions.
  • punata: This translates to "to purify" or "to sanctify." It indicates the process of cleansing oneself or one's intentions for a higher purpose.
  • ānuṣak: This means "to follow" or "to adhere." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path or adhering to moral principles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, protection, and the pursuit of righteousness. It signifies the importance of divine presence in our lives, the role of true leadership, and the nurturing of future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as parenting, leadership, and community building. For instance, a responsible leader in a community embodies the qualities of satpatiṁ by making decisions that benefit all, while parents can reflect prajāsv by nurturing their children with love and guidance. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for purity of intention and to follow a path of righteousness in all our endeavors.
