अधां nv अस्य जेन्यस्य पुष्टौ वृथा रेबन्त इमहे तद ū नु |सरण्युर अस्य सूनुर अश्वो विप्रश चासि श्रवसश च सातौ ||
adhā nv asya jenyasya puṣṭau vṛthā rebhanta īmahe tad ū nu | saraṇyur asya sūnur aśvo vipraś cāsi śravasaś ca sātau ||
  • adhā nv asya jenyasya: This phrase can be interpreted as "Let us invoke the strength of this being." Here, "adhā" means "down" or "below," and "asya" refers to "this." "Jenyasya" relates to "the one who is born" or "the progeny." This suggests a call to recognize and harness the inherent strength within ourselves or a community.
  • puṣṭau vṛthā rebhanta īmahe: This translates to "We meditate on the nourishment that is not in vain." "Puṣṭau" means "nourishment" or "prosperity," while "vṛthā" means "in vain" or "without purpose." "Rebhanta" refers to "to be filled" or "to be satisfied." This line emphasizes the importance of seeking meaningful sustenance, both physically and spiritually, that leads to fulfillment.
  • tad ū nu: This can be understood as "that indeed." It serves as a reinforcement of the previous thoughts, affirming the significance of the nourishment and strength being invoked.
  • saraṇyur asya sūnur: This phrase translates to "the protector of this being is its offspring." "Saraṇyur" means "protector" or "shelter," while "sūnur" means "son" or "offspring." This highlights the idea that protection and strength are often passed down through generations, emphasizing the role of family and community in providing support.
  • aśvo vipraś cāsi: This translates to "you are the horse and the wise one." "Aśvo" refers to "horse," symbolizing strength, speed, and vitality, while "vipraś" means "wise" or "intelligent." This line suggests that true strength is not just physical but also intellectual, and that wisdom is a vital component of power.
  • śravasaś ca sātau: This means "and you are the one who is renowned." "Śravasaś" refers to "renown" or "fame," while "sātau" means "in truth" or "in reality." This emphasizes the importance of being recognized for one's true qualities and contributions, suggesting that genuine merit leads to lasting respect and honor.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking strength, nourishment, and wisdom while recognizing the importance of community and legacy. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and family dynamics. For instance, in a workplace, a leader can draw upon the strengths of their team, ensuring that their contributions are meaningful and recognized. In family settings, the mantra serves as a reminder of the protective roles that family members play, fostering a supportive environment where wisdom and strength are shared and celebrated. Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to seek not just physical sustenance but also intellectual and emotional nourishment, leading to a fulfilling and impactful life.
