त्वं तं देव जिह्वया परि बाधस्व दुश्कृतम् |मार्तो यो नो जिघांसति ||
tvaṁ taṁ deva jihvayā pari bādhasva duṣkṛtam | marto yo no jighāṁsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • taṁ: That
    • deva: O God/Divine Being
    • jihvayā: By the tongue
    • pari: All around
    • bādhasva: Restrain/Control
    • duṣkṛtam: Evil deeds/Bad actions
    • marto: The mortal/human
    • yo: Who
    • no: Us
    • jighāṁsati: Wishes to harm
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a plea to the divine to restrain the evil actions that may arise from the words of others. It emphasizes the power of speech and the potential harm that can come from it. The speaker is asking the divine to protect them from those who wish to do harm through their words.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, gossip and negative speech can lead to a toxic environment. This mantra can be a reminder to seek divine protection from harmful words and to promote a culture of positivity.
    • In personal relationships, misunderstandings can escalate due to harsh words. This mantra serves as a reminder to communicate with kindness and to seek divine guidance in resolving conflicts.
    • In social media interactions, where words can be misinterpreted or used to harm, this mantra encourages individuals to be mindful of their speech and to seek protection from negativity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the power of words and the potential for harm they can cause. It serves as a reminder to be vigilant about the speech we engage in and to seek divine assistance in navigating the complexities of human interaction. In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios where communication plays a crucial role, urging individuals to foster a more compassionate and understanding environment.
