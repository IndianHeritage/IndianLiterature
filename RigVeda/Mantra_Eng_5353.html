आ नो विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः सजोषा ब्रह्म जुषाणो हर्यश्व याहि |वरिवृजत स्थविरेभिः सुशिप्रास्मे दधद वृषणं शुष्मं इन्द्र ||
ā no viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ sajoṣā brahma juṣāṇo haryaśva yāhi | varīvṛjat sthavirebhiḥ suśiprāsme dadhad vṛṣaṇaṁ śuṣmam indra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: "Come to us" - a call for divine presence.
    • viśvābhir: "with all" - signifies inclusiveness and universality.
    • ūtibhiḥ: "with energies" - refers to the various forms of energy or blessings.
    • sajoṣā: "with joy" - emphasizes the importance of joy in the invocation.
    • brahma: "the ultimate reality" - signifies the supreme consciousness or truth.
    • juṣāṇo: "enjoying" - indicates the act of embracing or cherishing.
    • haryaśva: "the swift horse" - symbolizes speed, strength, and vitality.
    • yāhi: "come" - a direct invitation to the divine or energies.
    • varīvṛjat: "to the vast" - refers to the expansive nature of the universe.
    • sthavirebhiḥ: "with the strong" - indicates the presence of powerful forces.
    • suśiprāsme: "with good rays" - symbolizes positivity and auspiciousness.
    • dadhad: "holding" - signifies possession or control.
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: "the strong" - refers to strength and power.
    • śuṣmam: "the dry" - can symbolize barrenness or lack of vitality.
    • indra: "O Indra" - a direct address to the king of gods, representing power and authority.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine blessings and energies to come forth and support the seeker.
    • It emphasizes the importance of joy and positivity in the pursuit of spiritual and material goals.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a call for strength and protection in overcoming challenges.
    • The imagery of the swift horse represents the need for speed and efficiency in achieving one's objectives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success in a new venture, inviting positive energies and support from colleagues and stakeholders.
    • In personal life, it can be used during challenging times, such as health issues or emotional struggles, to invoke strength and resilience.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to work together joyfully and inclusively for common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the importance of joy and strength in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us to embrace positivity and inclusiveness, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • In real life, this mantra can be a source of inspiration and motivation, encouraging us to seek help and work together towards our aspirations.
