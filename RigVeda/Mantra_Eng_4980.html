य इन्द्राग्नि सुतेषु वां स्तवत तेष्व ऋतावृद्धा |जोषवाकं वदतः पज्रहोषिणा न देवाः भसथश्चन ||
ya indrāgnī suteṣu vāṁ stavat teṣv ṛtāvṛdhā | joṣavākaṁ vadataḥ pajrahoṣiṇā na devā bhasathaś cana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing rain and fire)
    • suteṣu: in the offerings
    • vāṁ: your
    • stavat: praise
    • teṣv: in those
    • ṛtāvṛdhā: who increases the truth
    • joṣavākaṁ: who speaks with joy
    • vadataḥ: of those who speak
    • pajrahoṣiṇā: with the one who is powerful
    • na: not
    • devā: the gods
    • bhasathaś: should be harmed
    • cana: at all
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Agni, emphasizing the importance of their blessings in offerings.
    • It highlights the significance of truth and joy in communication, suggesting that those who speak with joy and truth are powerful.
    • The mantra also conveys a protective sentiment, indicating that the gods should not be harmed in any way.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate, they often invoke blessings from higher powers, similar to how this mantra seeks blessings from Indra and Agni.
    • In personal relationships, speaking with joy and truth can strengthen bonds, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In conflict resolution, ensuring that no harm comes to others, akin to the protective aspect of the mantra, can lead to peaceful outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in our lives, particularly through the elements of truth and joy.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community events, personal relationships, and conflict resolution, where the principles of joy, truth, and protection are essential.
    • By embodying these values, individuals can foster a harmonious environment that honors both the divine and the human experience.
