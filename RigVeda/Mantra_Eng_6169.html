इन्द्रं उक्तानि वावृधुः समुद्रम इव सिन्धवः |अनुत्तमन्युम अजरम् ||
indram ukthāni vāvṛdhuḥ samudram iva sindhavaḥ | anuttamanyum ajaram ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Ukthāni: Means 'utterances' or 'sacrificial hymns,' indicating the importance of words and prayers in invoking divine blessings.
  • Vāvṛdhuḥ: Translates to 'they have increased' or 'they have grown,' suggesting the amplification of blessings or prosperity.
  • Samudram: Means 'ocean,' representing vastness, depth, and abundance.
  • iva: Means 'like' or 'as,' drawing a comparison.
  • Sindhavaḥ: Refers to 'rivers' or 'water bodies,' symbolizing the flow of life, nourishment, and sustenance.
  • Anuttamanyum: Means 'the unparalleled energy' or 'the supreme spirit,' indicating a force that is beyond comparison.
  • Ajara: Translates to 'immortal' or 'everlasting,' signifying the eternal nature of the divine or the spirit.

  • This mantra emphasizes the power of divine invocation through words and hymns, as they have the ability to attract blessings and prosperity, much like the vastness of the ocean and the flow of rivers.
  • In real life, this can be seen in the practice of prayer and meditation, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power to enhance their lives and the lives of others.
  • For example, during significant life events such as weddings or festivals, people often recite mantras or prayers to invoke blessings for happiness and abundance.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of words and the divine. It teaches us that through sincere invocation and positive intentions, we can attract prosperity and strength into our lives.
  • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through practices such as affirmations, gratitude, and community prayers, fostering a sense of connection and abundance.
