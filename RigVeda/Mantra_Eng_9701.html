आशासनं विशासनम् अथो अधिकर्तनम् |सूर्यायाः पश्य रूपाणि तानि ब्रह्मा तु शुन्धति ||
āśasanaṁ viśasanam atho adhivikartanam | sūryāyāḥ paśya rūpāṇi tāni brahmā tu śundhati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āśasanaṁ: nourishment or sustenance
    • viśasanam: consumption or utilization
    • atho: and then
    • adhivikartanam: the act of transcending or going beyond
    • sūryāyāḥ: of the Sun
    • paśya: see or observe
    • rūpāṇi: forms or manifestations
    • tāni: those
    • brahmā: the creator or the ultimate reality
    • tu: but
    • śundhati: purifies or refines
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the processes of nourishment and consumption, suggesting that there is a cycle of receiving sustenance and then utilizing it.
    • The mention of the Sun indicates a source of energy and life, emphasizing the importance of observing the various forms that this energy takes.
    • It suggests that while we engage in these processes, there is a higher reality (Brahma) that purifies and refines our experiences and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we consume food (āśasanaṁ) for nourishment and then utilize that energy (viśasanam) to perform various activities.
    • When we engage in mindfulness practices, we can observe the forms of energy around us, such as sunlight, which can enhance our well-being.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra can be applied to personal growth; as we learn and experience life, we transcend our limitations (adhivikartanam) and strive for a higher understanding of ourselves and the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nourishment, energy, and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It serves as a reminder to observe the world around us and recognize the divine presence in all forms of life.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to engage in sustainable practices, be mindful of our consumption, and seek purification through knowledge and experience.
