प्र प्यायस्व प्र स्यन्दस्व सोम विश्वेभिर अंशुभिः |देवेभ्य उत्तमं हविः ||
pra pyāyasva pra syandasva soma viśvebhir aṁśubhiḥ | devebhya uttamaṁ haviḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pra pyāyasva pra syandasva soma viśvebhir aṁśubhiḥ | devebhya uttamaṁ haviḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Soma, come forth with your strength, flow forth with your essence, and grant us the best offerings to the gods."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," indicating a call to action.
    • pyāyasva: Derived from "pyāya," meaning "to flow" or "to come forth," often associated with the flow of Soma, a sacred drink.
    • syandasva: Meaning "to flow" or "to move," emphasizing the dynamic nature of the offering.
    • soma: A divine drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • viśvebhir: Meaning "with all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity in the offerings.
    • aṁśubhiḥ: Meaning "with rays" or "with light," symbolizing purity and divine energy.
    • devebhya: Meaning "to the gods," indicating the recipients of the offerings.
    • uttamaṁ: Meaning "the best" or "supreme," signifying the quality of the offerings.
    • haviḥ: Referring to "offerings" or "sacrifices," essential in Vedic rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, people often invoke divine blessings through offerings, such as flowers, fruits, or food, similar to how the mantra calls for the best offerings to the gods.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek to "flow forth" their strengths and talents, akin to the invocation of Soma, to achieve their goals and contribute positively to society.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals, offering their best to the divine, reflecting the essence of this mantra in collective worship.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine connection and the act of offering in spiritual practices.
    • It highlights the need for purity and quality in our intentions and actions, whether in rituals or daily life.
    • The call for Soma symbolizes the quest for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment, encouraging individuals to seek higher truths.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and quality in our offerings, both to the divine and in our daily interactions.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and to share them generously with the world, fostering a sense of community and connection.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that the pursuit of spiritual and personal growth is a collective journey, enriched by the contributions of all.
