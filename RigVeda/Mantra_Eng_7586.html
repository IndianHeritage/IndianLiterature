त्वं ह त्यत सप्तभ्यो जातमाणो 'शत्रुभ्यो अभवः शत्रुर इन्द्र |गूḻहे द्यावापृथिवी अन्व अविन्दो विभुमद्भ्यो भुवनेभ्यो रणं धाह् ||
tvaṁ ha tyat saptabhyo jāyamāno 'śatrubhyo abhavaḥ śatrur indra | gūḻhe dyāvāpṛthivī anv avindo vibhumadbhyo bhuvanebhyo raṇaṁ dhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: indeed
    • tyat: from that
    • saptabhyo: from the seven
    • jāyamāno: being born
    • śatrubhyo: from enemies
    • abhavaḥ: became
    • śatrur: an enemy
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • gūḻhe: hidden
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • anv: following
    • avindo: found
    • vibhumadbhyo: to the powerful
    • bhuvanebhyo: to the worlds
    • raṇaṁ: battle
    • dhāḥ: give
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine power of Indra, who is invoked as a protector and a source of strength.
    • The phrase "from the seven" may refer to the seven realms or aspects of existence, indicating a comprehensive approach to life.
    • The mantra acknowledges the challenges posed by enemies and the need for divine assistance in overcoming them.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of heaven and earth, suggesting that spiritual and material realms are linked.
    • The call to "give battle" signifies the importance of courage and action in the face of adversity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face challenges that can be likened to "enemies." This mantra can inspire one to seek strength and support from a higher power or community.
    • During times of personal struggle, such as illness or loss, invoking the essence of this mantra can provide comfort and a sense of connection to something greater.
    • In sports, athletes often call upon their inner strength and the support of their team, paralleling the invocation of divine assistance in overcoming challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking strength and support in times of difficulty.
    • It highlights the need for courage and action, encouraging individuals to confront their challenges head-on.
    • In a broader sense, it signifies the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, urging us to recognize the divine presence in our struggles and victories.
    • Ultimately, the mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to collective struggles, reinforcing the idea that we are never alone in our battles.
