गावा इव ग्रामं यूयुधिर इवाश्वान वाश्रेवं वत्सं सुमना दूहाना |पतिर इव जायाṁ अभि नो न्य एतु धार्ता दिवः सविता विश्ववाराḥ ||
gāva iva grāmaṁ yūyudhir ivāśvān vāśreva vatsaṁ sumanā duhānā | patir iva jāyām abhi no ny etu dhartā divaḥ savitā viśvavāraḥ ||
  • gāva iva grāmaṁ: This phrase translates to "like cows in a village." Cows are often seen as symbols of prosperity and nourishment in many cultures, particularly in India. In a real-life context, this can be related to the importance of community and the sustenance that comes from collective effort.
  • yūyudhir ivāśvān: This means "like horses in battle." Horses symbolize strength, speed, and valor. In modern life, this can be likened to teamwork and collaboration in achieving goals, much like a sports team working together to win a game.
  • vāśreva vatsaṁ sumanā duhānā: This translates to "like a calf that is well-nourished." A well-nourished calf represents growth and potential. In real life, this can be seen in the nurturing of young talent in various fields, such as education or mentorship programs that help individuals reach their full potential.
  • patir iva jāyām: This means "like a husband to his wife." This phrase emphasizes the importance of relationships and mutual respect. In contemporary society, it can be related to partnerships in both personal and professional realms, highlighting the need for support and understanding.
  • abhi no ny etu: This translates to "may he come to us." It expresses a desire for blessings and guidance. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as seeking help or wisdom from mentors, leaders, or even spiritual figures during challenging times.
  • dhartā divaḥ savitā viśvavāraḥ: This means "the sustainer of the heavens, the sun, the all-protecting." This phrase acknowledges a higher power that provides sustenance and protection. In real life, this can be seen in the appreciation of nature and the universe, as well as the reliance on spiritual beliefs for guidance and support.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of community, strength, nurturing, relationships, and the divine. It signifies the importance of collaboration and support in achieving common goals, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual pursuits. In real life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to value teamwork, nurture potential, and seek guidance from higher powers or mentors, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
