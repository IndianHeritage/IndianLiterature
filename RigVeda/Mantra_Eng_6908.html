आ बुण्डं वृत्रहा ददेजातः पृच्छद्वि मातरम् |का उग्राः के ह श्रिण्विरे ||
ā bundaṁ vṛtrahā dade jātaḥ pṛcchad vi mātaram | ka ugrāḥ ke ha śṛṇvire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • bundaṁ - the bond or connection
    • vṛtrahā - the slayer of Vritra (a demon representing chaos)
    • dade - gives
    • jātaḥ - born or created
    • pṛcchad - asking or inquiring
    • vi - indeed
    • mātaram - mother
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that overcomes chaos and ignorance.
    • The phrase "vṛtrahā" refers to the force that conquers obstacles, akin to how one might overcome personal challenges in life.
    • The act of asking or inquiring ("pṛcchad") signifies the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from our origins or 'mother'—which can be interpreted as nature, the universe, or our own roots.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, when facing difficulties, one might seek guidance from mentors or elders, akin to asking one's 'mother' for wisdom.
    • In a broader societal context, communities often come together to address chaos or challenges, much like how the mantra invokes the slaying of Vritra to restore order.
    • Environmental movements can be seen as a modern interpretation of this mantra, where humanity seeks to reconnect with nature (the 'mother') and overcome the chaos of pollution and climate change.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of overcoming chaos through inquiry and connection to our roots.
    • It serves as a reminder that in times of trouble, seeking knowledge and support from our origins can lead to clarity and resolution.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to confront challenges with wisdom and unity, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
