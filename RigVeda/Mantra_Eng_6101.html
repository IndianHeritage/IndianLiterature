तां सुदेवाय दाशुषे सुमेधाम अवितारिणीम् |घृतैर गव्यूतिम उक्षतम ||
tā sudevāya dāśuṣe sumedhām avitāriṇīm | ghṛtair gavyūtim ukṣatam ||
  • tā sudevāya: This phrase can be translated as "to those who are good and divine." It refers to the qualities of benevolence and divinity that one should aspire to embody or seek in others.
  • dāśuṣe: This word means "to the giver" or "to the one who bestows." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and the act of giving in human relationships.
  • sumedhām: Translated as "of good intellect" or "of wise understanding," this term highlights the value of wisdom and intelligence in decision-making and interactions.
  • avitāriṇīm: This word means "that which protects" or "that which leads to safety." It signifies the protective and guiding forces in life, whether they be divine or human.
  • ghṛtair: This term refers to "with ghee" or "with clarified butter," which is often used in Vedic rituals as a symbol of purity and nourishment.
  • gavyūtim: This word means "the wealth of cows" or "prosperity." In ancient times, cows were considered a symbol of wealth and abundance, representing sustenance and livelihood.
  • ukṣatam: This term translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It indicates the highest quality or the utmost excellence in whatever context it is applied.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine qualities, wisdom, and protection in our lives. It encourages individuals to be generous and to cultivate a wise understanding of the world around them.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In personal relationships, embodying the qualities of benevolence and generosity can strengthen bonds and create a supportive community.
    • In professional settings, making decisions based on wisdom and understanding can lead to better outcomes and foster a positive work environment.
    • In spiritual practices, invoking divine protection and guidance can provide comfort and assurance during challenging times.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the values of wisdom, generosity, and divine qualities in our lives. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous existence, both individually and collectively.
