जनिता दिवो जनिता पृथिव्याः पिबा सोमं मदाया कं शतक्रतो |यं ते भागम अधारयन विश्वाः सेहानः पृथना उरु ज्रयः सम अप्सुजिन मरुत्वां इन्द्र सत्पते ||
janitā divo janitā pṛthivyāḥ pibā somam madāya kaṁ śatakrato | yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin marutvām̐ indra satpate ||
  • janitā divo: This phrase translates to "the one who gives birth to the heavens." It signifies the divine source of creation and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • janitā pṛthivyāḥ: This means "the one who gives birth to the earth." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, highlighting the importance of both in sustaining life.
  • pibā somam madāya: This translates to "drink the soma for joy." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and bliss. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for seeking joy and fulfillment in life.
  • kaṁ śatakrato: This means "O hundred-fold powerful one." It refers to Indra, the king of gods, known for his strength and ability to overcome challenges. This can be related to individuals in real life who exhibit resilience and strength in the face of adversity.
  • yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ: This translates to "the world supports that portion of you." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and how the universe provides for those who are in harmony with it.
  • sehānaḥ pṛtanā: This means "the strong ones who support." It refers to the allies and forces that help individuals in their endeavors, akin to the support systems we have in our lives, such as family, friends, and community.
  • uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin: This translates to "the great strength that flows together." It symbolizes the collective power and unity that can be harnessed when individuals come together for a common purpose.
  • marutvām̐ indra satpate: This means "O Indra, lord of the Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature. This can be seen as a call to harness the natural energies around us for growth and prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, strength, and interconnectedness. It emphasizes the importance of seeking joy, drawing strength from the universe, and recognizing the support systems in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, community building, and the pursuit of happiness. By acknowledging the divine sources of our existence and the strength we derive from our connections, we can navigate life's challenges with resilience and joy.
