महः स राया एषते पतिर्दन्न इना इनस्य वसुनः पद आ |उप ध्रजन्तम अद्रयो विधन्न इति ||
mahaḥ sa rāya eṣate patir dann ina inasya vasunaḥ pada ā | upa dhrajantam adrayo vidhann it ||
  • mahaḥ - This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies the vastness and power of the subject being referred to.
  • sa - This means "he" or "that," indicating a specific entity or being.
  • rāya - This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of abundance in life.
  • eṣate - This translates to "is present" or "exists," indicating the presence of the subject in the context of wealth.
  • patir - Meaning "lord" or "master," this word denotes authority and leadership.
  • dann - This means "gift" or "offering," suggesting the act of giving or sharing wealth.
  • ina - This word can be interpreted as "of" or "from," linking the previous terms to the source of wealth.
  • inasya - This translates to "of the one who gives," emphasizing the giver's role in the context of wealth.
  • vasunaḥ - This means "of the wealth" or "of the riches," reinforcing the theme of prosperity.
  • pada - This translates to "foot" or "path," symbolizing the journey or way to achieve wealth.
  • ā - This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
  • upa - Meaning "near" or "close," this word suggests proximity to wealth or prosperity.
  • dhrajantam - This term refers to "those who support" or "uphold," indicating the importance of community and support in achieving wealth.
  • adrayo - This translates to "mountains" or "hills," symbolizing stability and strength in the pursuit of wealth.
  • vidhann - Meaning "to create" or "to establish," this word emphasizes the active role in generating wealth.
  • it - This can be interpreted as "thus" or "therefore," linking the previous ideas together.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of wealth, prosperity, and the role of community and support in achieving these goals. It highlights the importance of being a giver and the active pursuit of wealth, suggesting that true prosperity comes from both individual effort and collective support. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community development projects, charitable giving, and fostering a culture of sharing and support among individuals. By recognizing the interconnectedness of wealth and community, we can create a more prosperous and harmonious society.
