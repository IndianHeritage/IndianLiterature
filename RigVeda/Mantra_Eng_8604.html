एष प्रात्नेन वयसा पुनानस तिरो वर्पांसि दूहितुर दधानाḥ |वसानाḥ शर्मा त्रिवरूथम आप्सु होतेव याति समनेषु रेब्हन ||
eṣa pratnena vayasā punānas tiro varpāṁsi duhitur dadhānaḥ | vasānaḥ śarma trivarūtham apsu hoteva yāti samaneṣu rebhan ||
  • eṣa pratnena: This phrase can be translated as "this (mantra) through the effort." It emphasizes the importance of effort and intention in achieving one's goals.
  • vayasā: This word means "with age" or "through maturity." It suggests that wisdom and understanding come with experience and time.
  • punānas: This term refers to "renewal" or "rejuvenation." It indicates a process of revitalization, akin to the cycles of nature where life is constantly renewed.
  • tiro varpāṁsi: This phrase translates to "the hidden or concealed things." It implies that there are deeper truths or insights that may not be immediately visible.
  • duhitur dadhānaḥ: This means "the one who nourishes." It signifies the role of nurturing and supporting growth, whether in oneself or in others.
  • vasānaḥ: This word means "dwelling" or "residing." It suggests a sense of belonging and stability in one's environment.
  • śarma: This translates to "comfort" or "shelter." It highlights the importance of safety and security in life.
  • trivarūtham: This term can be understood as "threefold" or "triple." It may refer to the interconnectedness of various aspects of life, such as body, mind, and spirit.
  • apsu: This word means "in the waters." It symbolizes the source of life and the flow of energy that sustains all beings.
  • hoteva: This translates to "like a priest" or "as a performer of rituals." It indicates the importance of intention and reverence in our actions.
  • yāti: This means "goes" or "moves." It signifies progress and the journey of life.
  • samaneṣu: This term refers to "in harmony" or "among equals." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration.
  • rebhan: This word can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be supported." It suggests the idea of mutual support and care within relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, renewal, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that through effort and maturity, we can uncover hidden truths and nourish ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and fostering relationships. By recognizing the importance of effort, safety, and harmony, we can create a supportive environment that encourages growth and renewal, both individually and collectively.
