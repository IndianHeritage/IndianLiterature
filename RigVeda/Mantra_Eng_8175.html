पवमानā दिवस पर्य अन्तरिक्षाद असृक्षत |पृथिव्यā अधि सानवि ||
pavamānā divas pary antarikṣād asṛkṣata | pṛthivyā adhi sānavi ||
  • Mantra: "pavamānā divas pary antarikṣād asṛkṣata | pṛthivyā adhi sānavi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pavamānā: Purifying or cleansing
    • divas: Sky or heaven
    • pary: All around or surrounding
    • antarikṣād: From the space or atmosphere
    • asṛkṣata: It has been protected or preserved
    • pṛthivyā: Earth
    • adhi: Above or upon
    • sānavi: With strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purifying forces that come from the heavens and the atmosphere, which protect and preserve the Earth.
    • The term "pavamānā" suggests a cleansing or purifying energy, indicating that the divine forces are at work to maintain the sanctity of the environment.
    • The phrase "divas pary antarikṣād" emphasizes the connection between the sky and the atmosphere, suggesting that these realms are interconnected and influence the Earth.
    • "asṛkṣata" indicates a protective quality, implying that these divine energies safeguard the Earth from negative influences.
    • The mention of "pṛthivyā adhi sānavi" highlights the strength and power that these celestial forces impart to the Earth, ensuring its vitality and resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of purifying forces can be seen in efforts to clean rivers and restore ecosystems, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on protection and preservation.
    • In personal well-being, individuals often seek spiritual practices such as meditation or yoga, which can be viewed as ways to connect with the purifying energies of the universe, akin to the cleansing aspect of "pavamānā."
    • In agriculture, sustainable farming practices that respect the Earth and its resources can be seen as a manifestation of the protective qualities described in the mantra, ensuring the land remains fertile and productive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine protection and purification in maintaining the balance of nature and the Earth.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment and to seek harmony with the natural world.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards sustainable practices that honor the interconnectedness of all life.
