आप्रा राजांसी दिव्यानी पार्थिवा श्लोकं देवः कृतुते स्वाय धर्मणे |प्र बाहू अस्राक् सविता सवीमनि निवेशयन प्रसुन्नं अक्तुभिर जगत् ||
āprā rajāṁsi divyāni pārthivā ślokaṁ devaḥ kṛṇute svāya dharmaṇe | pra bāhū asrāk savitā savīmani niveśayan prasuvann aktubhir jagat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āprā: Unbounded or limitless
    • rajāṁsi: Kingdoms or realms
    • divyāni: Divine or celestial
    • pārthivā: Earthly or worldly
    • ślokaṁ: Verses or hymns
    • devaḥ: God or divine being
    • kṛṇute: Creates or establishes
    • svāya: For oneself or one's own
    • dharmaṇe: Righteousness or duty
    • pra: Forth or forward
    • bāhū: Arms or strength
    • asrāk: Flowing or abundant
    • savitā: The Sun or solar deity
    • saviṁani: With brilliance or radiance
    • niveśayan: Establishing or placing
    • prasuvann: Giving birth or producing
    • aktubhir: With energies or powers
    • jagat: The world or universe
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine creation and the establishment of both celestial and earthly realms.
    • It emphasizes the role of the divine in creating a harmonious balance between the spiritual and material worlds.
    • The mention of "Savitā" refers to the Sun, symbolizing life, energy, and the source of all creation.
    • The phrase "niveśayan prasuvann" suggests the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to how the Sun nurtures life on Earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the Sun's energy is crucial for the growth of crops, representing the divine nurturing aspect in our daily lives.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to balance their spiritual and material pursuits, reflecting the harmony described in the mantra.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals that honor the Sun, recognizing its role in sustaining life, akin to the divine establishment mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and the earthly realms, highlighting the importance of balance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor both our spiritual duties and our responsibilities in the material world.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek harmony in their lives, fostering a sense of duty towards both personal growth and community well-being.
