प्रास्मै हिनोता मधुमन्तं ऊर्मिं गर्भो यो वः सिन्धवो मध्वा उत्सः |घृतपृष्ठम् ईड्यम अध्वरेषु आपो रेवतीः श्रिणुत हवं मे ||
prāsmai hinota madhumantam ūrmiṁ garbho yo vaḥ sindhavo madhva utsaḥ | ghṛtapṛṣṭham īḍyam adhvareṣv āpo revatīḥ śṛṇutā havam me ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāsmai: To the one who is worthy or deserving.
    • hinota: May he bring or lead.
    • madhumantam: Sweet or full of sweetness.
    • ūrmiṁ: Wave or surge.
    • garbho: The womb or the source of creation.
    • yo: Who.
    • vaḥ: You (plural).
    • sindhavo: Of the rivers or waters.
    • madhva: Sweetness or nectar.
    • utsaḥ: Energy or enthusiasm.
    • ghṛtapṛṣṭham: With ghee on the surface.
    • īḍyam: Worthy of worship or praise.
    • adhvareṣv: In the sacrifices or rituals.
    • āpo: Waters.
    • revatīḥ: Prosperous or nourishing.
    • śṛṇutā: Listen or hear.
    • havam: My invocation or offering.
    • me: My.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the divine forces of nature, particularly the waters, to bless and nourish the offerings made during rituals.
    • The invocation emphasizes the importance of sweetness and nourishment, both physically and spiritually, suggesting that the divine presence is essential for growth and prosperity.
    • It highlights the connection between the natural elements (like rivers and waters) and the spiritual practices of humanity, indicating that rituals are a means to connect with and honor these forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and good harvests, recognizing the vital role of water in their livelihood. This mantra can be seen as a reflection of that prayer, seeking the blessings of nature.
    • During festivals, offerings of sweets and ghee are made to deities, symbolizing the sweetness of life and the nourishment provided by the divine. This aligns with the essence of the mantra, which calls for the sweetness of life to be present in rituals.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and blessings during significant life events (like weddings or new ventures), invoking the energies of prosperity and nourishment, akin to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of connecting with the natural elements and recognizing their role in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the sweetness and nourishment that life offers, both materially and spiritually.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek blessings for prosperity and well-being, reminding us of our interconnectedness with nature and the divine.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to honor and respect the forces that sustain us, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives.
