नावेव नः पारयतं युगेव नभ्येव न उपधीव प्रधीव |श्वानेव नो अरिषण्यां तनूनां खृगलेव विस्रसः पातम् अस्मान् ||
nāveva naḥ pārayataṁ yugeva nabhyeva na upadhīva pradhīva | śvāneva no ariṣaṇyā tanūnāṁ khṛgaleva visrasaḥ pātam asmān ||
  • nāveva naḥ pārayataṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be harmed" or "may we not be obstructed." In real life, this can relate to the idea of seeking protection from negative influences or harmful situations. For example, when embarking on a new venture, such as starting a business, individuals often seek blessings or support to ensure their path is clear of obstacles.
  • yugeva nabhyeva: This translates to "like the sky in the age." The sky is often seen as a symbol of vastness and freedom. In a practical sense, this can represent the limitless possibilities that exist in life. For instance, when pursuing education or personal growth, one might feel inspired by the idea that the sky is the limit, encouraging them to reach for their dreams.
  • na upadhīva pradhīva: This can be understood as "may we not be confined or limited." In everyday life, this speaks to the importance of breaking free from societal constraints or personal limitations. For example, someone might feel confined by traditional career paths and choose to pursue a passion project instead, embodying the spirit of this mantra.
  • śvāneva no ariṣaṇyā: This phrase suggests "like the dogs, may we not be troubled." Dogs are often seen as loyal companions, and this part of the mantra can signify the desire for steadfastness and loyalty in relationships. In real life, this could relate to the importance of surrounding oneself with supportive friends and family who provide comfort during challenging times.
  • tanūnāṁ khṛgaleva: This translates to "like the swift ones." This can refer to the idea of agility and adaptability. In a practical context, this could apply to individuals who need to be quick on their feet in a fast-paced work environment, adapting to changes and challenges as they arise.
  • visrasaḥ pātam asmān: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be free from fall." It emphasizes the desire for safety and security. In real life, this can relate to the importance of financial stability and emotional well-being, where individuals strive to create a secure environment for themselves and their loved ones.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for protection, freedom, loyalty, agility, and security. Its significance lies in the universal human experience of seeking safety and support in various aspects of life. Whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or individual growth, the principles expressed in this mantra can guide individuals toward a more fulfilling and secure existence. By embodying these values, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
