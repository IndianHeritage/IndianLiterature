प्रेह्य अभिःहि धृष्णुहि न ते वज्रो न यंसेते |इन्द्र नृम्णं हि ते शवो हनो वृत्रं जयाऽपोऽर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
prehy abhīhi dhṛṣṇuhi na te vajro ni yaṁsate | indra nṛmṇaṁ hi te śavo hano vṛtraṁ jayā apo 'rcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prehy: O Indra,
    • abhīhi: do not be afraid,
    • dhṛṣṇuhi: be courageous,
    • na: not,
    • te: your,
    • vajro: thunderbolt (symbol of strength),
    • ni: that which,
    • yaṁsate: does not strike,
    • indra: O Indra,
    • nṛmṇaṁ: the man,
    • hi: indeed,
    • te: your,
    • śavo: dead,
    • hano: kill,
    • vṛtraṁ: the obstacle,
    • jayā: victory,
    • apo: waters,
    • 'rcann: having been invoked,
    • anu: after,
    • svarājyam: sovereignty.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength, courage, and the power to overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "do not be afraid" encourages individuals to face challenges with bravery, suggesting that fear should not hinder one's actions.
    • The reference to "vajro" symbolizes the strength and power that one possesses, which can be harnessed to overcome difficulties.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of courage in the face of adversity, as indicated by the call to "be courageous."
    • In a real-life scenario, this can be applied to situations such as facing personal challenges, overcoming fears in public speaking, or tackling difficult tasks at work.
    • The invocation of "waters" and "sovereignty" suggests that overcoming obstacles leads to a state of abundance and control over one's life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of courage and strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that with determination and bravery, one can conquer obstacles and achieve success.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited during times of stress or uncertainty to instill confidence and a sense of empowerment.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true sovereignty comes from within, and by invoking our inner strength, we can navigate through life's trials effectively.
