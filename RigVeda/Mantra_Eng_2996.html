त्रिर उत्तमा दूनशा रोचनानि त्रयो राजन्त्य असुरस्य वीराः |ऋतावान इषिरा दूलभासस त्रिर आ दिवो विदथे सन्तु देवाः ||
trir uttamā dūṇaśā rocanāni trayo rājanty asurasya vīrāḥ | ṛtāvāna iṣirā dūḻabhāsas trir ā divo vidathe santu devāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "trir uttamā dūṇaśā rocanāni trayo rājanty asurasya vīrāḥ | ṛtāvāna iṣirā dūḻabhāsas trir ā divo vidathe santu devāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trir: three
    • uttamā: the best or highest
    • dūṇaśā: shining or radiant
    • rocanāni: those that illuminate
    • trayo: three
    • rājanty: rule or govern
    • asurasya: of the demons or non-divine beings
    • vīrāḥ: heroes or warriors
    • ṛtāvāna: one who is truthful or righteous
    • iṣirā: with arrows or weapons
    • dūḻabhāsas: shining or brilliant
    • trir ā: three times
    • divo: of the heavens
    • vidathe: in the assembly or gathering
    • santu: may they be
    • devāḥ: the gods
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of three radiant beings or forces that govern the realm of the non-divine, represented by the term "asurasya." These beings are depicted as heroes or warriors, suggesting a struggle between divine and non-divine forces.
    • The phrase "ṛtāvāna iṣirā" emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in the face of challenges, symbolized by the arrows or weapons. This can be likened to real-life situations where individuals must stand firm in their values and principles, even when faced with adversity.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for the gods to be present in the assembly, indicating the need for divine guidance and support in human endeavors. This can be seen in various cultural practices where people seek blessings or divine intervention in important decisions or gatherings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interplay between divine and non-divine forces, the importance of truth and righteousness, and the need for divine support in human affairs.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in situations where individuals or communities face moral dilemmas or conflicts. It serves as a reminder to uphold truth and righteousness while seeking guidance from higher powers.
    • Moreover, it highlights the collective strength that can be harnessed when individuals come together in unity, much like an assembly, to address challenges and seek solutions.
