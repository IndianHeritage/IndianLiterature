त्वं dyāṁ च महिव्रत पृथिवीं चाति जभृषे |प्रति द्रापिम अमुञ्चथाḥ पवमान महित्वना ||
tvaṁ dyāṁ ca mahivrata pṛthivīṁ cāti jabhriṣe | prati drāpim amuñcathāḥ pavamāna mahitvanā ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "You," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • dyāṁ - Meaning "heaven" or "sky," it represents the celestial realm, often associated with purity and divinity.
  • ca - This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the concepts that follow.
  • mahivrata - Translated as "great vow" or "great resolve," it signifies a commitment to a higher purpose or duty.
  • pṛthivīṁ - This means "earth," symbolizing the physical world and our earthly existence.
  • cāti - This word means "beyond" or "exceeding," suggesting a transcendence over the ordinary.
  • jabhriṣe - This can be interpreted as "you have taken" or "you have grasped," indicating an action of receiving or accepting.
  • prati - Meaning "towards" or "in the direction of," it implies movement or intention.
  • drāpim - This word translates to "the flow" or "the current," often referring to the flow of life or energy.
  • amuñcathāḥ - Meaning "release" or "let go," it suggests the act of freeing oneself from constraints.
  • pavamāna - This term refers to "the purifying one," often associated with the wind or breath, symbolizing cleansing and renewal.
  • mahitvanā - Translated as "with greatness" or "in majesty," it emphasizes the importance and power of the divine presence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, acknowledging the connection between the heavens and the earth, and expressing a desire for purification and release from worldly constraints. It emphasizes the importance of great vows and resolves in our lives, urging us to transcend our limitations and seek a higher purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of great vows, individuals can set personal goals or resolutions that align with their values and aspirations, striving for self-improvement.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to earth (pṛthivīṁ) reminds us of our responsibility towards nature. We can take actions to protect the environment, recognizing our connection to the earth.
  • Spiritual Practices: The call for purification (pavamāna) can be reflected in meditation or mindfulness practices, where individuals seek to cleanse their minds and spirits from negativity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the universe, the importance of our commitments, and the need for purification in our lives. It encourages us to strive for greatness and to let go of what holds us back, ultimately guiding us towards a more fulfilling existence.
