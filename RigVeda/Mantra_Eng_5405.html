भवā वरूथम मघवन मघोनां यत समजासि शर्धतः |वि त्वाहतस्य वेदनम् भजेमह्य ā दूनाशो भरā गयम् ||
bhavā varūtham maghavan maghonāṁ yat samajāsi śardhataḥ | vi tvāhatasya vedanam bhajemahy ā dūṇāśo bharā gayam ||
  • bhavā varūtham: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be prosperous and protected." The term "bhavā" refers to existence or being, while "varūtham" signifies protection or shelter. In real life, this can relate to the importance of having a safe and secure environment, whether it be in our homes, communities, or workplaces.
  • maghavan maghonāṁ: "Maghavan" refers to the one who is rich or bountiful, often associated with the deity of wealth and prosperity. "Maghonāṁ" means "of the rich." This part of the mantra emphasizes the value of abundance and generosity. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to share their wealth and resources with others, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • yat samajāsi śardhataḥ: This translates to "whatever you gather together." "Samajāsi" implies coming together or uniting, while "śardhataḥ" suggests a sense of harmony or collective effort. This can be seen in real-life scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace or community service projects where individuals unite for a common cause, highlighting the power of collaboration.
  • vi tvāhatasya vedanam: "Vi" means "to" or "for," while "tvāhatasya" refers to "your protection." "Vedanam" can be interpreted as "knowledge" or "wisdom." This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom for one's protection and growth. In everyday life, this can relate to the pursuit of education and self-improvement as a means to navigate challenges and make informed decisions.
  • bhajemahy ā dūṇāśo bharā gayam: "Bhajemahy" means "we worship" or "we honor," while "ā dūṇāśo" refers to "the one who brings abundance." "Bharā gayam" can be interpreted as "may we be filled with joy." This closing part of the mantra expresses gratitude and reverence for the forces that bring prosperity and happiness into our lives. In real life, this can be reflected in practices of gratitude, such as keeping a gratitude journal or participating in community celebrations that honor shared blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, protection, unity, wisdom, and gratitude. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek abundance not just for oneself but to share it with others, to come together in harmony, and to pursue knowledge for personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in professional settings, engaging in community service, and practicing gratitude in daily life. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more prosperous and harmonious society.
