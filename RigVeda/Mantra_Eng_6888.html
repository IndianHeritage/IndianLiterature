स नो मित्रमहस् त्वम् अग्ने शुक्रम् शोषिषा |देवैर् आ सत्सि बर्हिषि ||
sa no mitramahas tvam agne śukreṇa śociṣā | devair ā satsi barhiṣi ||
  • sa no mitramahas: This phrase can be translated as "May you, O Agni, be our great friend." Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. The term "mitramahas" emphasizes the importance of friendship and support.
  • tvam agne: This means "You, O Agni." It directly addresses Agni, acknowledging his presence and significance in rituals and daily life.
  • śukreṇa śociṣā: This translates to "with your pure and bright light." "Śukreṇa" refers to purity and brightness, while "śociṣā" relates to illumination. This highlights the idea that Agni brings clarity, wisdom, and purity into our lives.
  • devair ā satsi: This means "may we be united with the gods." It expresses a desire for connection and harmony with the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • barhiṣi: This term refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine. It signifies the importance of rituals in establishing a bond with the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa no mitramahas tvam agne śukreṇa śociṣā | devair ā satsi barhiṣi ||" emphasizes the significance of Agni as a friend and guide in our lives. It calls for purity, wisdom, and a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection. It serves as a reminder to seek clarity and support from the divine, fostering a sense of community and connection with others. By invoking Agni's qualities, individuals can strive for a life filled with light, friendship, and divine guidance, ultimately leading to personal and communal harmony.
