धेनुं न त्वा सूयवसे दुदुक्षन्न उप ब्रह्माणि ससृजे वसिष्ठः |त्वाम इन मे गोपतिं विश्व आहा न इन्द्रः सुमतिं गन्त्व अच् ||
dhenuṁ na tvā sūyavase dudukṣann upa brahmāṇi sasṛje vasiṣṭhaḥ | tvām in me gopatiṁ viśva āhā na indraḥ sumatiṁ gantv accha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Cow, I do not envy you; you are the one who gives birth to the divine. Vasistha created you, and you are the protector of all beings. May you, O Gopati (Lord of the Cows), grant me wisdom and lead me to prosperity, just as Indra, the king of gods, does."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dhenuṁ: Cow, symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • na: Not, indicating a lack of envy or jealousy.
    • sūyavase: One who gives birth, referring to the cow as a nurturing figure.
    • dudukṣann: To create or produce, emphasizing the cow's role in providing sustenance.
    • upa: Near or towards, indicating a relationship or connection.
    • brahmāṇi: Divine or sacred, highlighting the cow's spiritual significance.
    • sasṛje: Created, referring to the act of creation by Vasistha.
    • vasiṣṭhaḥ: A sage known for his wisdom and connection to the divine.
    • tvām: You, addressing the cow directly.
    • in: In, indicating a state of being or existence.
    • me: My, indicating possession or personal connection.
    • gopatiṁ: Lord of the cows, a title of reverence.
    • viśva: All or universal, indicating inclusivity.
    • āhā: Nourishment or sustenance.
    • na: Not, again indicating a negation.
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • sumatiṁ: Good thought or wisdom, indicating the desire for intelligence.
    • gantv: To go or lead, indicating a journey or direction.
    • accha: Good or auspicious, indicating a positive outcome.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The cow is often seen as a symbol of wealth and prosperity in many cultures, especially in India. For example, in rural areas, owning cows is a sign of economic stability.
    • In agriculture, cows provide milk, which is a vital source of nutrition for many families. This aligns with the mantra's emphasis on the cow as a nurturer.
    • The concept of 'Gopati' can be related to leaders or guardians in society who protect and provide for their communities, similar to how a cow provides for its owner.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reverence for the cow as a symbol of nourishment, prosperity, and divine creation. It teaches us to appreciate the resources we have and to seek wisdom and guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to foster a sense of gratitude towards nature and its offerings, promoting sustainable living and respect for all forms of life. By embodying the qualities of the cow, such as nurturing and providing, we can contribute positively to our communities and the environment.
