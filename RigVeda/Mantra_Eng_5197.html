ताम अग्नि अस्मे इषम् एरयस्व वैश्वानर द्युमतीं जातवेदः |यया राधः पिन्वसि विश्ववारा पृथु श्रवो दाशुषे मार्त्याय ||
tām agne asme iṣam erayasva vaiśvānara dyumatīṁ jātavedaḥ | yayā rādhaḥ pinvasi viśvavāra pṛthu śravo dāśuṣe martyāya ||
  • tām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, who symbolizes energy, light, and purity. In real life, Agni can be seen as a metaphor for the inner fire or passion that drives individuals to achieve their goals.
  • asme iṣam: This means "may we attain strength." It emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and resilience in our lives. For example, athletes train rigorously to build physical strength, which can be paralleled with mental and emotional strength in facing life's challenges.
  • erayasva: This word means "may you inspire." It suggests a call for divine inspiration. In a real-life context, this can relate to how mentors or leaders inspire others to reach their potential, much like a teacher igniting a student's passion for learning.
  • vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal aspect of Agni, representing the fire that connects all beings. It signifies unity and the shared experiences of humanity. In modern life, this can be seen in community gatherings where people come together to celebrate shared values and traditions.
  • dyumatīṁ jātavedaḥ: This translates to "the one who knows all." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom. In today's world, this can be related to the pursuit of education and the quest for understanding in various fields, from science to philosophy.
  • yayā rādhaḥ pinvasi: This means "by which you nourish." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of knowledge and strength. In real life, this can be seen in how parents nurture their children, providing them with the tools they need to thrive in society.
  • viśvavāra: This term means "the protector of all." It signifies the role of guardianship and protection in society. For instance, law enforcement and community leaders work to protect the rights and safety of individuals, embodying this principle.
  • pṛthu śravo: This translates to "with great fame." It suggests that the pursuit of knowledge and strength can lead to recognition and respect. In real life, individuals who excel in their fields, such as scientists or artists, often gain fame for their contributions to society.
  • dāśuṣe martyāya: This means "for the mortal." It highlights the human condition and the need for support and guidance in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the importance of community support systems, such as mental health services and social networks.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, knowledge, and inspiration from the divine while emphasizing the importance of community and support in our mortal lives. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected, and by nurturing our inner fire and sharing our strengths, we can uplift ourselves and those around us. This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in educational settings, community service, and personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while supporting one another in their journeys.
