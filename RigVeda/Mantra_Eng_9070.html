वृक्षे-वृक्षे नियता मīmयद गौस ततो वयः प्र पतान पूरुषादः |अथेदं विश्वं भुवनं भयात इन्द्राय सुन्वद ऋषये च शिक्षत ||
vṛkṣe-vṛkṣe niyatā mīmayad gaus tato vayaḥ pra patān pūruṣādaḥ | athedaṁ viśvam bhuvanam bhayāta indrāya sunvad ṛṣaye ca śikṣat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛkṣe-vṛkṣe: In the context of trees or plants, indicating a connection or relationship.
    • niyatā: Fixed, determined, or regulated.
    • mīmayad: To be made or created.
    • gaus: Referring to the cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • tato: Therefore or from that.
    • vayaḥ: Our age or lifespan.
    • pra patān: To fall or descend.
    • pūruṣādaḥ: From the essence of man or humanity.
    • athedaṁ: This or thus.
    • viśvam: The universe or all that exists.
    • bhuvanam: The world or realm.
    • bhayāta: From fear or danger.
    • indrāya: To Indra, the king of gods.
    • sunvad: To speak or utter well.
    • ṛṣaye: To the sages or seers.
    • ca: And.
    • śikṣat: To teach or instruct.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living beings, symbolized by the trees and the cow, which are vital for sustenance.
    • The phrase "vṛkṣe-vṛkṣe" suggests a deep relationship with nature, highlighting the importance of preserving the environment.
    • The mention of "niyatā" indicates a need for discipline and regulation in our actions towards nature and each other.
    • "Gaus" symbolizes not just physical nourishment but also spiritual wealth, suggesting that our actions should lead to holistic well-being.
    • The mantra calls for a collective understanding of our existence ("viśvam bhuvanam") and the need to protect it from fear and danger.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra seeks divine guidance and strength to overcome challenges and to speak wisely, as indicated by "sunvad ṛṣaye ca." This reflects the importance of wisdom in leadership and community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Environmental conservation efforts, such as tree planting initiatives, resonate with the idea of "vṛkṣe-vṛkṣe," emphasizing our duty to protect nature.
    • Community programs that promote sustainable farming practices reflect the essence of "gaus," ensuring that we nurture both the land and its people.
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, the call to protect "viśvam bhuvanam" becomes crucial, as communities come together to support one another.
    • Leadership that prioritizes wisdom and ethical decision-making aligns with the teachings of the mantra, fostering a society that values knowledge and compassion.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature, society, and ourselves.
    • Its significance lies in promoting harmony, sustainability, and wisdom in our actions.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that protect our environment, support our communities, and seek knowledge for better decision-making.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a collective effort to create a world that is safe, nurturing, and prosperous for all beings.
