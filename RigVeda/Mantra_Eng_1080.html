प्र यत ते अग्नि सूरयो जायत महि प्र ते वयम् |अप नः शोशुचद अगम् ||
pra yat te agne sūrayo jāyemahi pra te vayam | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • Mantra: "pra yat te agne sūrayo jāyemahi pra te vayam | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, may we become strong through your grace; may we be free from sin and may our actions be pure."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," indicating a sense of movement or progression.
    • yat: Meaning "that" or "which," referring to the qualities or attributes of Agni.
    • te: Meaning "your," indicating possession or connection to Agni.
    • agne: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purity.
    • sūrayo: Meaning "strong" or "powerful," indicating the strength that comes from Agni.
    • jāyemahi: Meaning "may we become" or "may we grow," expressing a desire for growth and strength.
    • vayam: Meaning "we," referring to the collective group seeking Agni's blessings.
    • apa: Meaning "from," indicating a separation or removal from something undesirable.
    • naḥ: Meaning "our," indicating possession or connection to the group.
    • śośucad: Meaning "to be purified" or "to cleanse," indicating a desire for purity.
    • agham: Meaning "sin" or "impurity," referring to negative actions or thoughts.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power or source of inspiration, much like the invocation of Agni in this mantra. For instance, someone preparing for a challenging exam may recite this mantra to seek mental clarity and strength.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during rituals or gatherings where people come together to seek collective strength and purity of purpose, such as during a wedding ceremony or a community service event.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on their actions and seek to cleanse themselves of negative thoughts or behaviors, aligning with the desire expressed in the mantra to be free from sin and to act with purity.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, purity, and the removal of negativity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance in our endeavors and the collective aspiration for growth and moral integrity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal reflection, community gatherings, and moments of challenge, serving as a reminder to strive for strength and purity in our actions and thoughts.
