स नो देवैभिः पवमान रदेंदो रयिम अश्विनं वावशानः |रथिरायताम उषती पुरंधिर अस्मद्र्यग् आदावने वसूनाम् ||
sa no devebhiḥ pavamāna radendo rayim aśvinaṁ vāvaśānaḥ | rathirāyatām uśatī puraṁdhir asmadrya1g ā dāvane vasūnām ||
  • sa no devebhiḥ: This phrase translates to "May the divine beings" or "May the gods." It signifies a call for blessings and support from the divine forces in the universe.
  • pavamāna: This term means "the purifying one" or "the one who brings purity." It refers to the sacred and cleansing aspects of the divine, often associated with the wind or breath of life.
  • radendo: This word can be interpreted as "of the sun" or "radiant." It emphasizes the importance of light, energy, and vitality in our lives.
  • rayim: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance and resources that one seeks in life.
  • aśvinaṁ: Referring to the "Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being. They symbolize the dual aspects of life and the balance between them.
  • vāvaśānaḥ: This term means "may we attain" or "may we achieve." It expresses a desire for success and fulfillment in our endeavors.
  • rathirāyatām: This translates to "may we have a good chariot" or "may we have a good journey." It symbolizes the journey of life and the importance of having the right tools and support to navigate it.
  • uśatī: This word means "prosperity" or "wealth." It emphasizes the importance of abundance in various forms, including material, emotional, and spiritual wealth.
  • puraṁdhir: This term refers to "the one who builds" or "the creator." It signifies the creative forces in the universe that help manifest our desires and goals.
  • asmadrya1g: This can be interpreted as "our strength" or "our power." It emphasizes the importance of self-empowerment and confidence in achieving our goals.
  • dāvane: This means "in the fire" or "in the heat." It symbolizes the challenges and trials we face in life, which can lead to growth and transformation.
  • vasūnām: This translates to "of the treasures" or "of the riches." It signifies the abundance of blessings and resources available to us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for divine blessings, prosperity, and guidance in our life's journey. It emphasizes the importance of seeking support from higher powers, recognizing the wealth of resources available to us, and the need for purity and strength in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during significant life transitions, when embarking on new ventures, or when seeking health and well-being. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance, resilience, and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
