वृषा त्वा वृषणं हुवे वज्रीं चित्राभिर ऊतिभिः |वावन्थ हि प्रतिष्ठुतिं वृषा हवः ||
vṛṣā tvā vṛṣaṇaṁ huve vajriñ citrābhir ūtibhiḥ | vāvantha hi pratiṣṭutiṁ vṛṣā havaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: The strong or powerful one.
    • tvā: You (addressing the deity).
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: The one who is worthy of praise or the one who brings strength.
    • huve: I invoke or I call upon.
    • vajriñ: The one who wields the thunderbolt, often associated with Indra.
    • citrābhir: With various or colorful offerings.
    • ūtibhiḥ: With praises or hymns.
    • vāvantha: May you be pleased or may you grant.
    • hi: Indeed.
    • pratiṣṭutiṁ: Praise or worship.
    • vṛṣā: The strong one (repeated for emphasis).
    • havaḥ: We offer or we invoke.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to a powerful deity, likely Indra, invoking strength and blessings through colorful offerings and praises. The repetition of "vṛṣā" emphasizes the strength and power of the deity being addressed. The mantra reflects a deep respect and a desire for divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to a person seeking strength and support during challenging times, such as a student preparing for exams or an athlete training for a competition. They may invoke their inner strength and seek motivation through positive affirmations.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, individuals may offer their prayers and praises to a higher power, similar to how this mantra is structured, seeking blessings for prosperity and success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength and divine support. It teaches us the importance of recognizing and calling upon our inner strength and the strength of the community or divine forces in times of need. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal challenges or collective endeavors, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek support, offer gratitude, and celebrate the strength that comes from unity and divine blessings.
