अप स्वसुर उषसो नग जिहीते रिणक्ति कृष्णीर अरुषाय पन्थाम् | अश्वामघा गोमघा वां हुवेम दिवा नक्तं शरुम अस्मद युयोतम् ||
apa svasur uṣaso nag jihīte riṇakti kṛṣṇīr aruṣāya panthām | aśvāmaghā gomaghā vāṁ huvema divā naktaṁ śarum asmad yuyotam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apa: away, from here
    • svasur: father-in-law
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • nag: mountain
    • jihīte: may he conquer
    • riṇakti: he who calls
    • kṛṣṇīr: black, dark
    • aruṣāya: to the red, bright
    • panthām: path, way
    • aśvāmaghā: the one who has horses
    • gomaghā: the one who has cows
    • vāṁ: we
    • huvema: we invoke
    • divā: by day
    • naktaṁ: by night
    • śarum: protection
    • asmad: our
    • yuyotam: may he fight
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the divine forces associated with the dawn and the natural elements.
    • The mention of "father-in-law" and "mountain" suggests a connection to familial and natural strength.
    • The terms "black" and "red" may symbolize the duality of existence, representing challenges and opportunities.
    • By calling upon the "path" and the "one who has horses and cows," it emphasizes the importance of prosperity and abundance in life.
    • The invocation of protection during both day and night signifies the need for constant vigilance and support in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for guidance and protection as one navigates the challenges of entrepreneurship.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the blessings of family and nature can help strengthen bonds and foster harmony.
    • During times of uncertainty, such as a job change or relocation, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek support and remain resilient.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of family, nature, and personal growth.
    • It encourages individuals to seek protection and guidance in their daily lives, reminding them of the importance of community and abundance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, including personal development, relationships, and professional endeavors, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
