सोमं राजा नम अवसे 'ग्निं गीरभिर हवामहे |आदित्यांन विष्णुं सूर्यं ब्रह्माणं च बृहस्पतिम् ||
somaṁ rājānam avase 'gniṁ gīrbhir havāmahe | ādityān viṣṇuṁ sūryam brahmāṇaṁ ca bṛhaspatim ||
  • Translation: "We invoke the King Soma with our hymns; we call upon Agni, the divine fire. We invoke the Adityas, Vishnu, Surya, Brahman, and Brihaspati."

  • Explanation of Terms:
    • Soma: In Vedic literature, Soma is both a deity and a sacred drink. It symbolizes immortality and is often associated with the moon. In real life, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Rājānam: This term means "king" or "ruler." It signifies authority and leadership. In modern contexts, it can relate to the qualities of a good leader in various fields, such as business or community service.
    • Agni: The fire god, Agni represents transformation and purification. In everyday life, fire can symbolize passion and creativity, as well as the warmth of human relationships.
    • Ādityān: Referring to the Adityas, a group of solar deities. They embody various aspects of the sun, which is essential for life. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of sunlight for agriculture and health.
    • Vishnu: The preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, Vishnu represents stability and balance. In real life, this can be seen in the role of individuals or organizations that work towards maintaining harmony in society.
    • Surya: The sun god, Surya symbolizes energy and vitality. In a contemporary context, this can relate to renewable energy sources and the importance of sustainability.
    • Brahman: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the interconnectedness of all existence. In real life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of unity and understanding among diverse cultures.
    • Brihaspati: The guru of the gods and the deity of wisdom and knowledge. This can be related to the importance of education and mentorship in personal and professional growth.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the invocation of divine forces that govern various aspects of life. It highlights the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Application: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom, leadership, and transformation in their personal and professional journeys. It encourages the pursuit of knowledge, the importance of community, and the need for balance in life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all elements in life and the importance of invoking divine guidance. It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth, community well-being, and a harmonious existence.
