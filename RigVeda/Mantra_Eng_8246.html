समु त्वा धीभिर अस्वरन् हिन्वतीः सप्त जामयः |विप्रम आजा विवस्वतः ||
sam u tvā dhībhir asvaran hinvatīḥ sapta jāmayaḥ | vipram ājā vivasvataḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sam u tvā dhībhir asvaran hinvatīḥ sapta jāmayaḥ | vipram ājā vivasvataḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "With our intellects, we invoke you, O divine one, who brings forth the seven offspring, the wise one who is born of the sun."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: Together, in harmony.
    • u: Indeed, or certainly.
    • tvā: You, referring to a divine entity.
    • dhībhir: With our intellects or understanding.
    • asvaran: To invoke or call upon.
    • hinvatīḥ: Bringing forth or producing.
    • sapta: Seven, often symbolizing completeness or fullness.
    • jāmayaḥ: Offspring or progeny.
    • vipram: The wise or learned one.
    • ājā: Born or produced.
    • vivasvataḥ: Of the sun, often associated with enlightenment and knowledge.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective intellect and wisdom in invoking divine energies.
    • The "seven offspring" can symbolize various aspects of life, such as the seven chakras in the human body, representing a holistic approach to well-being.
    • The invocation of the divine through intellect suggests that knowledge and understanding are essential in spiritual practices.
    • In real life, this can be seen in collaborative efforts in communities where collective wisdom leads to better decision-making and problem-solving.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and collective effort in achieving goals.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, which can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in team settings, educational environments, and community initiatives where collaboration is key to success.
