स पर्वतो न धरुणेष्व अच्युतः सहस्रमूतिस तविषीषु वावृधे |इन्द्रो यद वृत्रम अवधीं नदिवृतम् उभ्जन्न अर्णांसी जर्हृषाणो अन्धसा ||
sa parvato na dharuṇeṣv acyutaḥ sahasramūtis taviṣīṣu vāvṛdhe | indro yad vṛtram avadhīn nadīvṛtam ubjann arṇāṁsi jarhṛṣāṇo andhasā ||
  • Mantra: "sa parvato na dharuṇeṣv acyutaḥ sahasramūtis taviṣīṣu vāvṛdhe | indro yad vṛtram avadhīn nadīvṛtam ubjann arṇāṁsi jarhṛṣāṇo andhasā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sa: He (referring to a divine entity)
    • parvato: mountain or elevated
    • na: not
    • dharuṇeṣv: in the strong or firm
    • acyutaḥ: infallible or imperishable
    • sahasramūtis: having a thousand powers or forms
    • taviṣīṣu: in the powerful
    • vāvṛdhe: he has grown or expanded
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • yad: who
    • vṛtram: the demon Vritra (symbolizing obstacles)
    • avadhīn: killed or defeated
    • nadīvṛtam: surrounded by rivers
    • ubjann: having emerged or arisen
    • arṇāṁsi: the waters or oceans
    • jarhṛṣāṇo: rejoicing or celebrating
    • andhasā: in darkness or ignorance
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, who is not confined by the strong or firm.
    • It highlights the power of this entity, who has a thousand forms and has grown in strength.
    • Indra is depicted as the one who defeated Vritra, a demon representing obstacles and ignorance, thus liberating the waters and allowing them to flow freely.
    • The imagery of rivers and oceans symbolizes the flow of knowledge and abundance, which is celebrated after overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, this mantra can be related to overcoming obstacles such as fear, doubt, or ignorance. For instance, a student facing difficulties in studies can draw inspiration from this mantra to persevere and succeed.
    • In a broader context, communities facing natural disasters can find strength in this mantra, as it symbolizes resilience and the ability to rise above challenges.
    • In leadership, the qualities of Indra can be emulated by those in positions of power, encouraging them to lead with strength and wisdom, overcoming challenges for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of resilience, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in the face of challenges, one can emerge victorious and celebrate the abundance of life.
    • Applicable in various real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to harness their inner strength and wisdom to navigate through difficulties.
