या उक्ताः केवलā दधे यः सोमं धृतिपातिबत् |यस्मै विष्णुः त्रिणि पदā विचक्रम उप मित्रस्य धर्मभिः ||
ya ukthā kevalā dadhe yaḥ somaṁ dhṛṣitāpibat | yasmai viṣṇus trīṇi padā vicakrama upa mitrasya dharmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ukthā: uttered or spoken
    • kevalā: alone or solely
    • dadhe: gives or bestows
    • yaḥ: who
    • somaṁ: soma (a sacred drink)
    • dhṛṣitāpibat: drinks with courage
    • yasmai: to whom
    • viṣṇus: Vishnu (the preserver)
    • trīṇi: three
    • padā: steps or feet
    • vicakrama: traversed or moved
    • upa: near or towards
    • mitrasya: of the friend
    • dharmabhiḥ: with righteousness or duties
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a person who, through their utterances, bestows the sacred soma drink, which symbolizes divine inspiration and strength.
    • The reference to drinking soma with courage suggests the importance of bravery in seeking knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The mention of Vishnu and the three steps signifies the divine support and guidance in one's journey towards righteousness and truth.
    • The term "mitrasya" indicates the importance of friendship and alliances in achieving one's goals, especially in the pursuit of dharma (righteousness).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who inspires their team through motivational speeches can be seen as bestowing 'soma' by encouraging courage and innovation.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra emphasizes the value of supportive friendships that help individuals navigate challenges with righteousness.
    • In community service, working together with others towards a common goal reflects the essence of 'dharmabhiḥ', where collective efforts lead to positive outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of communication, courage, and the pursuit of righteousness in our lives.
    • It teaches us that through our words and actions, we can inspire others and create a supportive environment that fosters growth and enlightenment.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and to act with integrity in our relationships and endeavors.
