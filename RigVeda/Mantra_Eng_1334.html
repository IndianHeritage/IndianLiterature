आ वां रथं पुरुमायं मनोजुवं जीराश्वं यज्ञियं जीवसे हुवे |सहस्रकेतुं वनिनं शतद्वसुं श्रुष्टिवानं वरिवोधाम अभि प्रयः ||
ā vāṁ ratham purumāyam manojuvaṁ jīrāśvaṁ yajñiyaṁ jīvase huve | sahasraketuṁ vaninaṁ śatadvasuṁ śruṣṭīvānaṁ varivodhām abhi prayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: "We invoke you" or "We call upon you."
    • ratham: "Chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, or journey.
    • purumāyam: "The ancient one," referring to a timeless or eternal aspect.
    • manojuvaṁ: "Swift of mind," indicating intelligence and quick thinking.
    • jīrāśvaṁ: "The aged horse," representing experience and wisdom.
    • yajñiyaṁ: "Worthy of sacrifice," suggesting something that is revered or honored.
    • jīvase: "For life," indicating vitality and existence.
    • huve: "I call," a declaration of intent or invocation.
    • sahasraketuṁ: "With a thousand banners," symbolizing victory and glory.
    • vaninaṁ: "Of the forest," representing nature and its abundance.
    • śatadvasuṁ: "With a hundred riches," indicating prosperity and wealth.
    • śruṣṭīvānaṁ: "The creator," referring to the divine or the source of all creation.
    • varivodhām: "The one who knows the paths," symbolizing guidance and wisdom.
    • abhi prayaḥ: "Towards the goal," indicating direction and purpose.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine for guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • The chariot symbolizes our journey through life, and invoking the ancient one suggests seeking wisdom from the past.
    • Swift thinking and experience are essential for navigating challenges, much like a seasoned traveler who knows the best routes.
    • The mention of sacrifice highlights the importance of dedication and reverence in our pursuits.
    • Prosperity and abundance are sought, reflecting a desire for a fulfilling and rich life.
    • Ultimately, the mantra emphasizes the need for divine guidance as we strive towards our goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new project. Leaders may invoke the wisdom of past experiences and seek guidance to navigate challenges.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra when facing significant life decisions, such as marriage or career changes, to seek clarity and direction.
    • In environmental efforts, the reference to nature can inspire actions that honor and protect the environment, recognizing the abundance it provides.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, guidance, and reverence in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek knowledge from the past while moving forward with purpose and clarity.
    • By invoking the divine, we align ourselves with a greater force, enhancing our journey towards our goals and aspirations.
