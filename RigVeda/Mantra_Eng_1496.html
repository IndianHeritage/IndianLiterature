तेषां पिबताम अस्मयु आ नो गन्तम इहोत्याः | इन्द्रवायू सुतानां अद्रिभिर युवं मदाय वाजदाय युवं ||
teṣām pibatam asmayū ā no gantam ihotyā | indravāyū sutānām adribhir yuvam madāya vājadā yuvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • teṣām: of them
    • pibatam: may you drink
    • asmayū: of the stone (or rock)
    • ā: may you come
    • no: to us
    • gantam: to go
    • ihotyā: here (in this place)
    • indravāyū: O Indra, the wind
    • sutānām: of the well-prepared (or well-made) offerings
    • adribhir: with the mountains
    • yuvam: you two (Indra and Vayu)
    • madāya: for the purpose of intoxication (or joy)
    • vājadā: the one who gives strength
    • yuvam: you two (again)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces of Indra and Vayu, asking them to partake in the offerings made by the worshippers.
    • The phrase "may you drink of the stone" suggests a connection to the earth and its resources, symbolizing the sustenance and strength derived from nature.
    • The call for Indra and Vayu to come "here" emphasizes the importance of presence and participation in rituals, highlighting the need for divine blessings in human endeavors.
    • The mention of "well-prepared offerings" indicates the significance of quality and intention in the acts of devotion, paralleling how in real life, the effort and sincerity put into any task can lead to better outcomes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural practices, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather, akin to invoking Indra (the rain god) and Vayu (the wind god) for their blessings to ensure a good harvest.
    • In personal endeavors, individuals may seek divine guidance and support when embarking on significant life changes, such as starting a new job or moving to a new place, reflecting the desire for strength and clarity.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, the collective offerings made to deities can be seen as a way to foster unity and shared purpose, similar to the invocation of Indra and Vayu in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine presence and support in human activities, reminding us that our efforts are often complemented by higher powers.
    • It teaches us to value the quality of our intentions and actions, as well as the interconnectedness of nature and spirituality.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to seek harmony with the environment and to approach their endeavors with sincerity and dedication, recognizing the role of divine forces in their success.
