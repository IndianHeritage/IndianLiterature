तव च्युतानानि वज्रहस्त तानि नव यत पुरो नवतिं च सध्यः |निवेशने शततमाविवेषीर अहं च वृतं नमुचिं उताहन ||
tava cyautnāni vajrahasta tāni nava yat puro navatiṁ ca sadyaḥ | niveśane śatatamāviveṣīr ahañ ca vṛtraṁ namucim utāhan ||
  • tava cyautnāni vajrahasta: This phrase can be translated as "Your powerful (vajra) hands (hasta) are capable of overcoming obstacles (cyautnāni)." The term "vajra" symbolizes strength and indestructibility, often associated with divine power.
  • tāni nava yat puro navatiṁ ca sadyaḥ: This part means "Those new (nava) things that are established (puro) and the ninety (navatiṁ) that come suddenly (sadyaḥ)." It suggests the emergence of new opportunities or challenges that arise unexpectedly.
  • niveśane śatatamāviveṣīr: This translates to "You have entered (viveṣīr) into the abode (niveśane) of a hundred (śatatama)." It implies a sense of belonging or integration into a community or a larger purpose.
  • ahañ ca vṛtraṁ namucim utāhan: This means "I (ahañ) also (ca) invoke (utāhan) the obstacles (vṛtraṁ) and the demon (namucim)." Here, "vṛtra" and "namuci" represent challenges or adversities that one must confront.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the power of divine strength in overcoming obstacles and embracing new opportunities. It reflects the journey of life where one must face challenges and integrate into a larger community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, professional challenges, or community involvement. For instance, when starting a new job, one may encounter unexpected challenges (the "new things") but can rely on their inner strength (the "vajra hands") to navigate through them. The mantra serves as a reminder of resilience and the importance of community support in overcoming life's adversities.
