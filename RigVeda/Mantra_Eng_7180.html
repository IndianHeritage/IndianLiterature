न यं दुध्रā वरन्ते न स्थिरा मुरो मदे सुशिप्रम अन्धसः |य आदृत्या शशमानाय सुन्वते दाता जरीत्र उक्त्यम् ||
na yaṁ dudhrā varante na sthirā muro made suśipram andhasaḥ | ya ādṛtyā śaśamānāya sunvate dātā jaritra ukthyam ||
  • na yaṁ dudhrā varante: This phrase can be translated to "that which is not disturbed by the forces of nature." It signifies stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • na sthirā muro made suśipram andhasaḥ: This part means "not shaken by the intoxication of the mind." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining clarity and focus, even when faced with distractions or temptations.
  • ya ādṛtyā: This translates to "those who are steadfast." It refers to individuals who remain committed and dedicated to their goals and principles.
  • śaśamānāya: This means "to those who are wise." It highlights the value of wisdom and understanding in navigating life's complexities.
  • sunvate dātā: This phrase translates to "the giver of good things." It suggests that those who are generous and kind will receive blessings in return.
  • jaritra ukthyam: This can be interpreted as "the praise of the wise." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of stability, wisdom, and generosity in life. It teaches us to remain steadfast and focused, even amidst distractions, and to value the contributions of wise individuals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and supportive society, where individuals uplift one another through wisdom and generosity.
