यज्ञ इन्द्रं अवर्धयद यद भूमिं व्य अवर्तयत् |चक्राणो उपशं दिवि ||
yajña indram avardhayad yad bhūmiṁ vy avartayat | cakrāṇa opaśaṁ divi ||
  • Mantra: "yajña indram avardhayad yad bhūmiṁ vy avartayat | cakrāṇa opaśaṁ divi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yajña: Sacrifice or offering, often associated with rituals that invoke divine blessings.
    • indram: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and strength.
    • avardhayad: To increase or elevate, suggesting the act of enhancing or uplifting.
    • yad: That which or whatever.
    • bhūmiṁ: The earth or the ground, representing the physical realm.
    • vy avartayat: To turn or rotate, indicating a transformative action.
    • cakrāṇa: Referring to the wheel or cycle, symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the universe.
    • opaśaṁ: Peace or tranquility, suggesting a state of calmness.
    • divi: In the heavens or celestial realm, indicating a higher state of existence.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of yajña (sacrifice) that elevates Indra, the divine force, and transforms the earth.
    • It suggests that through our actions and offerings, we can invoke divine energies that not only uplift us but also bring about a transformation in our environment.
    • The reference to the wheel (cakrāṇa) indicates the cyclical nature of existence, where every action has a reaction, and peace can be achieved through harmony with these cycles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of yajña can be seen in community service or environmental initiatives where individuals come together to contribute positively to society and nature.
    • For instance, a community organizing a tree-planting event is a form of yajña, where the collective effort aims to enhance the earth (bhūmiṁ) and create a better environment.
    • Similarly, in personal development, when individuals engage in selfless acts or mindfulness practices, they are essentially performing a yajña that elevates their inner strength (indram) and brings peace (opaśaṁ) to their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of selfless actions and their transformative power in both personal and communal contexts.
    • It teaches us that by engaging in acts of service and sacrifice, we can invoke higher energies that lead to personal growth and societal harmony.
    • In essence, it reminds us of our interconnectedness with the universe and the importance of contributing positively to the world around us.
