एकस्त्वष्टुर अश्वस्यā विशस्तā द्वा यन्तारā भवतस् तथा ऋतुः |या ते गात्राणाम ऋतुथा कृतोमि ता-ता पिण्डानाम प्र जुहोम्य अग्नौ ||
ekas tvaṣṭur aśvasyā viśastā dvā yantārā bhavatas tatha ṛtuḥ | yā te gātrāṇām ṛtuthā kṛṇomi tā-tā piṇḍānām pra juhomy agnau ||
  • Mantra: "ekas tvaṣṭur aśvasyā viśastā dvā yantārā bhavatas tatha ṛtuḥ | yā te gātrāṇām ṛtuthā kṛṇomi tā-tā piṇḍānām pra juhomy agnau ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ekas: one
    • tvaṣṭur: of the divine craftsman (Tvashtar, the creator)
    • aśvasyā: of the horses (symbolizing strength and vitality)
    • viśastā: who is well-established or well-formed
    • dvā: two
    • yantārā: who are the movers or carriers
    • bhavatas: of you (the divine)
    • tatha: thus
    • ṛtuḥ: the seasons or cycles
    • yā: which
    • te: your
    • gātrāṇām: of the limbs or bodies
    • ṛtuthā: in accordance with the seasons
    • kṛṇomi: I create or I make
    • tā-tā: those
    • piṇḍānām: of the offerings or oblations
    • pra: in front of
    • juhomy: I offer
    • agnau: into the fire
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Tvashtar, the divine craftsman, emphasizing the importance of creation and the establishment of strength and vitality.
    • The reference to "horses" symbolizes power and energy, which are essential in various aspects of life, such as work, relationships, and personal growth.
    • The mention of "two movers" can be interpreted as the dual forces in life, such as action and reaction, or the balance between opposing elements.
    • The phrase "in accordance with the seasons" highlights the cyclical nature of life, reminding us that everything has its time and place.
    • The act of offering into the fire signifies the importance of sacrifice and dedication in achieving one's goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, one might reflect on their strengths (the "horses") and how they can harness these to overcome challenges.
    • In relationships, understanding the "two movers" can help individuals navigate conflicts by recognizing the balance of give and take.
    • In agriculture, farmers often work in harmony with the seasons, planting and harvesting at the right times to ensure a fruitful yield.
    • In professional settings, the mantra can inspire individuals to make sacrifices, such as time and effort, to achieve success in their careers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of creation, strength, and the cyclical nature of life.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to recognize their own power and the importance of timing and sacrifice in their endeavors.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their life journey and the forces that shape it.
