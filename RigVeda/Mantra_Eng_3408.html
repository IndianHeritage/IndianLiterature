भृमिश्चिद्घासीतूतुजिराचित्रचित्रीणिष्वा। चित्रं कृतोष्यूतये॥
bhṛmiś cid ghāsi tūtujir ā citra citriṇīṣv ā | citraṁ kṛṇoṣy ūtaye ||
  • bhṛmiś: This word can be interpreted as "earth" or "ground." It signifies stability, foundation, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness." It represents the essence of being and the understanding of existence.
  • ghāsi: This can be understood as "to consume" or "to absorb." It indicates the act of taking in or integrating experiences.
  • tūtujir: This word suggests "to be filled" or "to be satisfied." It conveys a sense of fulfillment and completeness.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • citra: This term translates to "varied" or "colorful." It symbolizes diversity and the richness of life.
  • citriṇīṣv: This word refers to "in the varied forms" or "in the colorful manifestations." It emphasizes the multiplicity of existence.
  • kṛṇoṣy: This means "you create" or "you make." It signifies the act of bringing forth or manifesting something.
  • ūtaye: This term can be interpreted as "for the purpose" or "for the benefit." It indicates intention and direction.

The mantra can be understood as a call to recognize the interconnectedness of the earth, consciousness, and the diverse experiences of life. It emphasizes the importance of grounding oneself in reality while being aware of the richness and variety that life offers. The act of consuming experiences and being filled with them leads to a deeper understanding of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the earth nurtures life, individuals can cultivate their consciousness by absorbing diverse experiences, leading to personal development.
  • Community Building: Recognizing the varied forms of life within a community can foster inclusivity and understanding, creating a more harmonious society.
  • Environmental Awareness: The connection to the earth reminds us of our responsibility to care for the environment, acknowledging its role in sustaining life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the relationship between the earth, consciousness, and the diversity of life. It encourages individuals to embrace their experiences, recognize their interconnectedness, and contribute positively to the world around them. By doing so, one can achieve a sense of fulfillment and purpose in life.
