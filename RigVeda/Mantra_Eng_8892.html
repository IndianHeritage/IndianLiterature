अर्चामि वां वर्धायापो घृतस्नू dyावाभूमी श्रिणुतं रोधसी मे |अहा यद dyावो 'सुनीतिम अयन मध्वा नो अत्र पितरा शिशीताम् ||
arcāmi vāṁ vardhāyāpo ghṛtasnū dyāvābhūmī śṛṇutaṁ rodasī me | ahā yad dyāvo 'sunītim ayan madhvā no atra pitarā śiśītām ||
  • arcāmi vāṁ: This phrase translates to "I invoke you" or "I worship you." It signifies the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine or the forces of nature.
  • vardhāyāpo: This means "may the waters increase" or "may the waters be abundant." It reflects the importance of water in sustaining life and prosperity.
  • ghṛtasnū: This term refers to "the one who is rich in ghee" or "the one who is nourishing." Ghee is a symbol of purity and nourishment in Vedic culture.
  • dyāvābhūmī: This translates to "heaven and earth." It represents the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of the cosmos.
  • śṛṇutaṁ rodasī me: This means "listen to my call, O space." It is a plea for attention from the universe, indicating a desire for connection and communication with the divine.
  • ahā yad dyāvo 'sunītim: This translates to "as the heavens are full of light." It signifies the presence of positivity and enlightenment in the universe.
  • ayan madhvā: This means "may we have sweetness." It symbolizes the desire for joy, happiness, and pleasant experiences in life.
  • no atra pitarā: This translates to "may our ancestors be pleased." It reflects the importance of honoring one's lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • śiśītām: This means "may they shower blessings." It is a wish for divine blessings and protection in one's life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, the importance of nature, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the need for abundance, nourishment, and blessings from both the divine and our ancestors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, and moments of reflection, reminding us to honor our roots, seek abundance, and cultivate a harmonious relationship with the universe. It serves as a reminder to be grateful for the resources we have and to strive for a life filled with joy and positivity.
