तमानā वहन्तो दुरो व्य ऋण्वन् नवन्त विश्वे स्वार दृष्टिके ||
tmanā vahanto duro vy ṛṇvan navanta viśve sva1r dṛśīke ||
  • Mantra: "tmanā vahanto duro vy ṛṇvan navanta viśve sva1r dṛśīke"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tmanā: with the self or soul
    • vahanto: those who carry or bear
    • duro: far or distant
    • vy ṛṇvan: by overcoming or conquering
    • navanta: new or fresh
    • viśve: all or universal
    • sva1r: in the realm of the self or in the world of the self
    • dṛśīke: with vision or perception
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of self-awareness and the journey of the soul.
    • It emphasizes the importance of carrying one's own self through challenges and overcoming obstacles.
    • The term "duro" suggests that the path may be long or difficult, but with determination, one can navigate through it.
    • "Navanta" indicates the potential for renewal and new beginnings, suggesting that every challenge can lead to growth.
    • The phrase "viśve sva1r dṛśīke" highlights the importance of perception and understanding in the journey of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that require them to dig deep within themselves ("tmanā") to find strength and resilience.
    • In a professional setting, overcoming obstacles in a project can lead to innovative solutions ("navanta") that benefit the entire team or organization.
    • In relationships, understanding and empathy ("dṛśīke") can help navigate conflicts and lead to stronger bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of self-awareness and the importance of overcoming challenges in life.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace their journey, recognizing that every obstacle can lead to personal growth and renewal.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to improved relationships, professional success, and a deeper understanding of oneself.
