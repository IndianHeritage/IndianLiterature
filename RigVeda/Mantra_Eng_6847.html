मन्त्र: कृष्णा राजांसि पत्सुतः प्रयाणे जातवेदसः | अग्निर यद रोधति क्षमि ||
kṛṣṇā rajāṁsi patsutaḥ prayāṇe jātavedasaḥ | agnir yad rodhati kṣami ||
  • kṛṣṇā rajāṁsi patsutaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "the black (or dark) kings (or rulers) who are powerful." Here, "kṛṣṇā" refers to the color black or dark, often symbolizing the unknown or the hidden aspects of life. "rajāṁsi" means kings or rulers, indicating authority and power. "patsutaḥ" suggests those who are strong or mighty.
  • prayāṇe jātavedasaḥ: This translates to "in the journey, O Jātavedas." "prayāṇe" refers to a journey or movement, while "jātavedasaḥ" is a name for Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between the divine and human realms. This phrase emphasizes the importance of guidance and protection during transitions or journeys.
  • agnir yad rodhati kṣami: This can be interpreted as "the fire that protects and shields." "agnir" refers to fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. "yad" means "that which," and "rodhati" implies protection or shielding. "kṣami" suggests the ability to endure or tolerate. Together, this line highlights the protective and transformative power of fire in our lives.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength and protection during life's journeys. It acknowledges the hidden powers (the dark kings) that influence our paths and the importance of divine guidance (Agni) in navigating challenges.
  • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals face transitions, such as starting a new job, moving to a new place, or embarking on a new relationship. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and protection from higher powers or inner resources during these times.
  • Moreover, it encourages individuals to recognize the unseen forces at play in their lives and to trust in the transformative power of challenges, much like fire that purifies and strengthens.
