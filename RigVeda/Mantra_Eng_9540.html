देवाश्चित्ते अमृताः जातवेदो महिमानं वाध्र्यश्व प्र वाचन |यत्सम्पृच्छं मानवीर विष आयन त्वं नृभिर अजयस त्वावृधेबिः ||
devāś cit te amṛtā jātavedo mahimānaṁ vādhryaśva pra vocan | yat sampṛccham mānuṣīr viśa āyan tvaṁ nṛbhir ajayas tvāvṛdhebhiḥ ||
  • devāś cit te amṛtā: This phrase translates to "O Gods, indeed you are immortal." It emphasizes the divine nature of the gods, suggesting that they transcend human limitations and possess eternal qualities. In real life, this can be related to the idea of inspiration and ideals that people strive for, such as wisdom, love, and justice, which are often seen as immortal values.
  • jātavedo mahimānaṁ: "O Jātavedas, with great glory." Jātavedas refers to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine. The mention of glory signifies the importance of fire in rituals and daily life, symbolizing purification and transformation. For example, in many cultures, fire is used in ceremonies to signify new beginnings or to honor ancestors.
  • vādhryaśva pra vocan: This can be interpreted as "speak to us, O Vādhryaśva." Vādhryaśva is a divine horse associated with speed and strength. This line suggests a call for guidance and support from divine forces. In everyday life, this can be seen as seeking help from mentors or leaders who can guide us through challenges.
  • yat sampṛccham mānuṣīr viśa: "Whatever is asked by the human beings." This line highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, indicating that humans have the ability to seek knowledge and assistance from higher powers. In practical terms, this can be related to the act of asking questions and seeking advice in various aspects of life, such as education, career, and personal growth.
  • āyan tvaṁ nṛbhir ajayas: "You are approached by humans, victorious." This suggests that the divine is accessible to humans and that through devotion and inquiry, one can achieve success. In real life, this can be seen in the context of perseverance and determination in achieving one's goals, where individuals often turn to spiritual practices for strength and guidance.
  • tvāvṛdhebhiḥ: "By your grace." This phrase emphasizes the importance of divine grace in human endeavors. It suggests that while humans strive for success, it is ultimately through the blessings of the divine that they can achieve their aspirations. In practical terms, this can be reflected in moments of gratitude and acknowledgment of the support one receives from others, whether it be family, friends, or a higher power.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance, acknowledging the immortal qualities of the divine, and recognizing the role of grace in achieving success. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, spiritual practices, and community support, where individuals strive for higher ideals and seek assistance in their journeys. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of humility and gratitude in our pursuits.
