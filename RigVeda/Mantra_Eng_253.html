सम् माग्ने वर्चसा सृजा सम् प्रजया सम् आयुषा |विद्युर मे अस्य देवाः इन्द्रो विद्याद् सह ऋषिभिः ||
sam māgne varcasā sṛja sam prajayā sam āyuṣā | vidyur me asya devā indro vidyāt saha ṛṣibhiḥ ||
  • Translation: "With the power of the divine, may I create strength; with progeny, may I create longevity. May the gods, especially Indra, grant me knowledge along with the sages."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: Together, in harmony.
    • māgne: With power or strength.
    • varcasā: Radiance or brilliance.
    • sṛja: To create or generate.
    • prajayā: Progeny or offspring.
    • āyuṣā: Longevity or long life.
    • vidyur: Knowledge or wisdom.
    • me: My.
    • asya: This.
    • devā: Gods or divine beings.
    • Indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • vidyāt: May he know or grant knowledge.
    • saha: Together with.
    • ṛṣibhiḥ: Sages or wise ones.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the health and success of their children, embodying the desire for progeny and longevity.
    • In educational pursuits, students seek knowledge and wisdom, akin to the request for divine guidance in learning.
    • In professional life, individuals strive to create strength and brilliance in their work, reflecting the mantra's call for power and creativity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of harmony in creation, the value of knowledge, and the blessings of strength and longevity. It is applicable in various aspects of life, including family, education, and career. By invoking the divine and seeking wisdom from sages, individuals can aspire to lead fulfilling lives enriched with purpose and strength. This mantra serves as a reminder to align one's actions with higher ideals and to seek support from the divine in all endeavors.
