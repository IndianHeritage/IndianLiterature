एता अग्न आशुषाणास इष्टीर युवोः सचाभ्य अश्याम वाजान् |मेंद्रो नो विष्णुर मरुतः परि ख्यन यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
etā agna āśuṣāṇāsa iṣṭīr yuvoḥ sacābhy aśyāma vājān | mendro no viṣṇur marutaḥ pari khyan yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • etā - This word can be translated as "these" or "this," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current offerings.
  • agna - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • āśuṣāṇāsa - This term can be interpreted as "quickly" or "swiftly," suggesting the urgency and immediacy of the offerings made to the divine.
  • iṣṭīr - This word means "offerings" or "sacrifices," highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • yuvoḥ - Translates to "of the young" or "youth," which can symbolize vitality, potential, and the future.
  • sacābhy - This can be understood as "with truth" or "with sincerity," emphasizing the need for genuine intentions in offerings.
  • aśyāma - Meaning "to be strong" or "to be powerful," this word reflects the desire for strength and support from the divine.
  • vājān - Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the blessings sought from the divine for abundance in life.
  • mendro - This term can be interpreted as "the lord" or "the master," often referring to a higher power or deity.
  • no - Meaning "our," indicating a collective approach to the divine, seeking blessings for the community.
  • viṣṇur - Refers to "Vishnu," the preserver in Hindu mythology, symbolizing protection and sustenance.
  • marutaḥ - This word refers to the "winds" or "storm gods," representing the forces of nature and their influence on life.
  • pari - Meaning "around" or "surrounding," indicating a protective presence or encompassing support.
  • khyan - This can be interpreted as "to be aware" or "to perceive," suggesting the importance of awareness in spiritual practices.
  • yūyam - Translates to "you all," indicating a collective address to the divine forces.
  • pāta - Meaning "to protect" or "to guard," emphasizing the desire for safety and security.
  • svastibhiḥ - Refers to "well-being" or "auspiciousness," highlighting the importance of health and prosperity.
  • sadā - Meaning "always," indicating a continuous or eternal aspect of the blessings sought.
  • naḥ - Translates to "us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the prayer.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of offerings made with sincerity and urgency to the divine, particularly to Agni and Vishnu, for strength, prosperity, and protection. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, rituals, or personal prayers where individuals seek blessings for well-being and abundance. The collective nature of the mantra encourages unity and shared intentions, making it relevant in both personal and communal spiritual practices. The significance lies in recognizing the divine presence in everyday life and the importance of maintaining a sincere connection through rituals and offerings.
