यद इन्द्राग्नि उditā सूर्यस्य मध्ये दिवः स्वधया मादयेतः |अतः परि वृषाणाव् आ हि यातम् अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī uditā sūryasya madhye divaḥ svadhayā mādayethe | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing strength and fire)
    • uditā: having risen or arisen
    • sūryasya: of the Sun
    • madhye: in the midst
    • divaḥ: of the sky or heaven
    • svadhayā: by one's own will or desire
    • mādayethe: may they be pleased or intoxicated
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: may they go or proceed
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • pibataṁ: may they drink
    • sutasya: of the brewed or prepared (nectar)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Agni, asking them to rise in the midst of the Sun and the heavens.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine will and the joy that comes from their presence.
    • The phrase "may they drink of the Soma" signifies the pursuit of spiritual and physical nourishment, akin to seeking enlightenment or fulfillment in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and strength. For instance, when students study hard for exams, they invoke the blessings of knowledge (similar to Indra and Agni) to succeed.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may seek inspiration and strength from their teams (Indra and Agni) to achieve collective goals, much like the divine forces working together.
    • During celebrations or rituals, people often seek blessings for joy and prosperity, akin to the intoxication of divine presence mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors, whether they are academic, professional, or personal.
    • It teaches us to seek strength and joy from higher powers, reminding us that our efforts are often supported by unseen forces.
    • In real life, it encourages us to pursue our goals with the understanding that we are part of a larger cosmic order, where our actions resonate with the divine.
