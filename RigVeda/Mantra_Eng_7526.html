यस्य ते नू चिदादिशं न मिनन्ति स्वराज्यम् |न देवो नाध्रिगुर जनः ||
yasya te nū cid ādiśaṁ na minanti svarājyam | na devo nādhrigur janaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • nū: indeed
    • cid: anything
    • ādiśaṁ: command or order
    • na: not
    • minanti: diminish or decrease
    • svarājyam: sovereignty or rule
    • na: not
    • devo: gods
    • nā: nor
    • adhri: support or uphold
    • guru: teacher or guide
    • janaḥ: people or beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the sovereignty and authority of a higher power, suggesting that those who are under this power do not diminish or lose their command.
    • The phrase "yasya te nū cid ādiśaṁ" implies that the commands or orders of this higher power are absolute and unyielding.
    • The latter part, "na devo nādhrigur janaḥ," indicates that neither the gods nor the wise teachers can undermine or diminish this sovereignty.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a strong leader who commands respect and loyalty can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra. For instance, a CEO who inspires their team and maintains authority without being undermined by external pressures exemplifies this principle.
    • In spiritual contexts, individuals who follow a particular faith may feel that their connection to the divine provides them with unwavering strength and guidance, reflecting the idea that their spiritual sovereignty is not diminished by worldly challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unwavering authority and the strength that comes from a higher power. It serves as a reminder that true sovereignty is not easily shaken by external forces.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership, personal resilience, and spiritual faith, where individuals draw strength from their beliefs or positions of authority.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize and uphold their own sovereignty while respecting the higher powers that guide them.
