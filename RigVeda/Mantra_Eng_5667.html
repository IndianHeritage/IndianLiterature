दिवि क्षयन्ता रजसः पृथिव्यां प्र वां घृतस्य निर्णिजो ददीरन् |हव्यं नो मित्रो आर्यमा सुजातो राजा सुख्षत्रो वरुणो जुषन्त ||
divi kṣayantā rajasaḥ pṛthivyām pra vāṁ ghṛtasya nirṇijo dadīran | havyaṁ no mitro aryamā sujāto rājā sukṣatro varuṇo juṣanta ||
  • Divi kṣayantā rajasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the forces of the heavens that are diminishing." It suggests a transition or a change in the celestial realm, possibly indicating a shift in energy or influence.
  • Pṛthivyām pra vāṁ ghṛtasya nirṇijo dadīran: This translates to "on Earth, they have established the unyielding essence of ghee." Ghee, or clarified butter, is often associated with purity and nourishment in Vedic traditions. This line emphasizes the importance of sustaining life and the earth through pure and nourishing means.
  • Havyaṁ no mitro aryamā sujāto: Here, "Havyaṁ" refers to offerings or sacrifices. "Mitro" means friend, and "Aryamā" is a deity associated with friendship and harmony. This line suggests that the offerings made to the divine should be done with the intention of fostering friendship and goodwill.
  • Rājā sukṣatro varuṇo juṣanta: This can be interpreted as "the king, the protector, Varuna, delights in the offerings." Varuna is a deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. This line highlights the significance of divine approval and the role of leadership in maintaining harmony and order in society.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of harmony between the celestial and terrestrial realms. It emphasizes the importance of pure offerings, the role of friendship and goodwill, and the significance of divine approval in leadership. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as community building, environmental sustainability, and ethical leadership. By fostering relationships based on trust and making conscious efforts to nourish our surroundings, we can create a more harmonious and balanced society. The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards each other and the world we inhabit.
