पयस्वतीरोषधयः पयस्वन मामकं वचः |अपाम पयस्वदित पयस् तेन मां सह शुन्धत ||
payasvatīr oṣadhayaḥ payasvan māmakaṁ vacaḥ | apām payasvad it payas tena mā saha śundhata ||
  • Payasvatīr oṣadhayaḥ: This phrase refers to the "nourishing herbs" or "medicinal plants." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the healing and nurturing aspects of nature.
  • Payasvan māmakaṁ vacaḥ: This translates to "let my words be nourishing." It emphasizes the importance of speech and communication that is beneficial and uplifting.
  • Apām payasvad: This means "from the waters that are nourishing." Water is often seen as a source of life and sustenance, essential for growth and health.
  • It payas: This can be interpreted as "that which is nourishing." It reinforces the idea of seeking sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • Tena mā saha śundhata: This translates to "may I be united with that." It expresses a desire for connection with the nourishing elements of life, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment in various forms—physical, verbal, and spiritual. In real life, this can be applied in numerous ways, such as:

  • Promoting healthy eating habits by consuming nourishing foods, akin to the "nourishing herbs."
  • Encouraging positive and uplifting communication, ensuring that our words contribute to the well-being of others.
  • Recognizing the importance of water and hydration in maintaining health and vitality.
  • Striving for a deeper connection with nature and the elements that sustain us, fostering a sense of unity with the environment.

This mantra serves as a reminder to seek nourishment in all aspects of life, promoting holistic well-being and interconnectedness with the world around us.
