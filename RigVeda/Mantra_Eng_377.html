नीचावयाऽभवद् वृतपुत्रेन्द्रोऽस्याऽव वधार जभारा। उत्तराऽसूर अधरः पुत्र आसीद् दानुः शये सहवत्सा न धेनुः॥
nīcāvayā abhavad vṛtraputrendro asyā ava vadhar jabhāra | uttarā sūr adharaḥ putra āsīd dānuḥ śaye sahavatsā na dhenuḥ ||
  • nīcāvayā abhavad: This phrase can be translated as "he became lowly" or "he was brought low." It signifies a fall from a higher state to a lower one, which can be interpreted in various contexts, such as moral degradation or loss of status.
  • vṛtraputrendro: This term refers to "the son of Vritra," where Vritra is a demon in Vedic mythology representing chaos and obstruction. Indra, the king of the gods, is often depicted as battling Vritra to restore order. This highlights the struggle between good and evil, or order and chaos, in life.
  • asyā ava vadhar jabhāra: This can be understood as "he who has been struck down." It suggests a moment of defeat or a significant challenge that one faces. In real life, this could relate to personal setbacks, such as losing a job or facing a health crisis.
  • uttarā sūr adharaḥ: Translated, this means "the upper hero is the lower one." This phrase can imply that true strength often lies in humility or that those who appear weak may possess hidden strengths. For example, a quiet person may be underestimated but can show great resilience in difficult situations.
  • putra āsīd dānuḥ: This translates to "the son was Danu." Danu is often associated with the primordial waters and is considered a mother figure in Vedic texts. This can symbolize the nurturing aspect of life, where challenges can lead to growth and new beginnings.
  • śaye sahavatsā na dhenuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the cow does not give milk when it is not with its calf." It emphasizes the importance of connection and support in nurturing relationships. In real life, this can relate to the idea that individuals thrive when they have a supportive community or family around them.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the cyclical nature of life, where one experiences highs and lows. It teaches us about the importance of resilience in the face of adversity and the value of humility and support from others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal challenges, reminding us that setbacks can lead to growth and that true strength often comes from our connections with others. It encourages us to embrace our struggles and recognize the potential for transformation that lies within them.
