विप्रं होतारं अद्रुहं धूमकेतुं विभावसुम् |यज्ञानां केतुम इमहे ||
vipraṁ hotāram adruhaṁ dhūmaketuṁ vibhāvasum | yajñānāṁ ketum īmahe ||
  • vipraṁ: This term refers to a learned or wise person, often a priest or sage who possesses knowledge and understanding.
  • hotāram: This word means the one who invokes or calls upon the deities during a ritual, typically the priest who performs the yajna (sacred fire ritual).
  • adruhaṁ: This signifies someone who is non-hostile or benevolent, emphasizing the importance of goodwill and harmony in relationships.
  • dhūmaketuṁ: This term translates to "the one with the smoke banner," often symbolizing the sacred fire that rises in smoke during rituals, representing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • vibhāvasum: This refers to the radiant or luminous one, often associated with the divine light or energy that is invoked during the yajna.
  • yajñānāṁ: This means "of the sacrifices" or "of the rituals," highlighting the significance of performing yajnas in Vedic tradition.
  • ketum: This word means "the goal" or "the essence," indicating the purpose or the desired outcome of the rituals.
  • īmahe: This translates to "we meditate upon" or "we invoke," suggesting an active engagement in the spiritual practice.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to invoke the wise and benevolent priest who performs the sacred rituals, emphasizing the importance of goodwill and harmony. It highlights the significance of the yajna as a means to connect with the divine and achieve spiritual goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Harmony: Just as the mantra emphasizes non-hostility and goodwill, it can inspire individuals to foster harmony within their communities, promoting peace and understanding.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of the priest and the sacred fire can be seen in modern spiritual practices, where rituals are performed to seek blessings and guidance from higher powers.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on wisdom and knowledge encourages individuals to seek learning and understanding in their personal and professional lives, leading to growth and development.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking wisdom, goodwill, and the divine light through rituals. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging practices that promote harmony, learning, and spiritual growth in everyday life.
