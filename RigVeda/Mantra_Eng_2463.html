अग्निं सुम्नाय दधिरे पुरो जनाः वाजश्रवसम इह वृक्तबरहिषः |यतस्रुचः सुरुचं विश्वदेव्यं रुद्रं यज्ञानां साधदिष्टिम अपसाम् ||
agniṁ sumnāya dadhire puro janā vājaśravasam iha vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | yatasrucaḥ surucaṁ viśvadevyaṁ rudraṁ yajñānāṁ sādhadiṣṭim apasām ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Sumnāya: Means for auspiciousness or well-being, indicating the desire for positive outcomes.
  • Dadhi: Translates to 'to hold' or 'to establish', suggesting the act of invoking or establishing a connection.
  • Puro: Means 'in front' or 'before', indicating the presence of Agni in rituals.
  • Janā: Refers to people or community, emphasizing the collective aspect of worship.
  • Vājaśravasam: Means 'one who brings prosperity', highlighting the role of Agni in providing abundance.
  • Iha: Means 'here', indicating the immediacy of the invocation.
  • Vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: Refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection to the divine.
  • Yatasrucaḥ: Means 'from where the offerings come', indicating the source of blessings.
  • Surucaṁ: Translates to 'beautiful' or 'well-formed', suggesting the aesthetic aspect of offerings.
  • Viśvadevyaṁ: Refers to the universal deities, indicating the inclusiveness of the worship.
  • Rudraṁ: Refers to Rudra, a form of Shiva, symbolizing destruction and regeneration.
  • Yajñānāṁ: Means 'of sacrifices', emphasizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Sādhadiṣṭim: Refers to the attainment of goals or objectives, indicating the purpose of the rituals.
  • Apasām: Means 'to remove' or 'to free', suggesting the liberation from obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a central figure in rituals, representing transformation, prosperity, and the collective effort of the community in seeking blessings from the divine. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, celebrations, and rituals where the intention is to invoke positive energy and abundance. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging collective efforts towards achieving common goals and overcoming challenges.
