अग्निर ईशे बृहतः क्षत्रियस्याग्निर वाजस्य परमस्य रायः |दधाति रत्नं विधते यविष्टो व्य अनुशङ मार्त्याय स्वधावान ||
agnir īśe bṛhataḥ kṣatriyasyāgnir vājasya paramasya rāyaḥ | dadhāti ratnaṁ vidhate yaviṣṭho vy ānuṣaṅ martyāya svadhāvān ||
  • Agnir: The term "Agnir" refers to fire, which is often seen as a symbol of purity, energy, and transformation. In many cultures, fire is a vital element that represents life and sustenance.
  • īśe: This word means "to the lord" or "to the ruler." It signifies respect and reverence towards a higher power or authority.
  • bṛhataḥ: This translates to "great" or "vast." It emphasizes the magnitude and importance of the subject being referred to.
  • kṣatriyasyāgnir: This phrase can be understood as "the fire of the warrior." Kṣatriyas are the warrior class in ancient Indian society, symbolizing strength, valor, and protection.
  • vājasya: Meaning "of the horse," this term is often associated with speed, power, and nobility. Horses were crucial in ancient warfare and transportation.
  • paramasya: This means "of the supreme" or "of the ultimate." It indicates something that is of the highest order or quality.
  • rāyaḥ: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the blessings that come from the divine.
  • dadhāti: This means "gives" or "bestows." It indicates the act of providing or granting something valuable.
  • ratnaṁ: This translates to "jewel" or "precious thing." It symbolizes something of great value, whether material or spiritual.
  • vidhate: This means "creates" or "establishes." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence.
  • yaviṣṭho: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is most excellent" or "the best." It denotes superiority and excellence.
  • vy ānuṣaṅ: This phrase means "to the mortal" or "to the human." It connects the divine with the earthly realm.
  • martyāya: This translates to "for the mortal" or "for humanity." It emphasizes the importance of human existence and the divine's relationship with it.
  • svadhāvān: This means "self-moving" or "self-acting." It indicates autonomy and the ability to act independently.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of fire as a divine force that bestows wealth, prosperity, and excellence upon humanity. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the mortal, suggesting that through reverence and respect for higher powers, individuals can receive blessings that lead to abundance and success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals and prayers for prosperity, as well as in moments of seeking guidance and strength in challenging situations. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence and to honor the forces that sustain life.
