मन्त्र: वैश्वानर तव तानि व्रतानि महान्य अग्ने नकिरे दधर्ष || यज जायतमानः पित्रोः उपस्थेऽविन्दः केतुं वयुनेषु अह्नाम् ||
vaiśvānara tava tāni vratāni mahāny agne nakir ā dadharṣa | yaj jāyamānaḥ pitror upasthe 'vindaḥ ketuṁ vayuneṣv ahnām ||
  • Vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal aspect of fire, representing the divine energy that sustains life. In real life, we can see this in the warmth of the sun or the fire we use for cooking, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
  • Tava tāni vratāni: This phrase translates to "your vows" or "your commitments." It emphasizes the importance of dedication and discipline in our lives. For example, a student committing to their studies or an athlete training rigorously for a competition embodies this principle.
  • Mahāny agne: "Great fire" or "O Agni," signifies the powerful and purifying nature of fire. In daily life, we can relate this to the transformative power of challenges that purify our character, much like how fire refines gold.
  • Nakir ā dadharṣa: This can be interpreted as "do not let anything obstruct." It encourages us to overcome obstacles in our path. For instance, a person facing adversity in their career but persevering to achieve their goals exemplifies this idea.
  • Yaj jāyamānaḥ: This means "while the sacrifice is being performed." It highlights the importance of rituals and community in our lives. For example, family gatherings during festivals can be seen as a form of collective sacrifice and celebration.
  • Pitror upasthe: This translates to "in the presence of the ancestors." It signifies the respect and remembrance of our forebears. In real life, this can be seen during rituals like ancestral worship or family reunions where stories of ancestors are shared.
  • 'Vindaḥ ketuṁ: This means "to find the banner." It symbolizes the pursuit of goals and aspirations. For instance, a person striving to achieve their dreams, like starting a business or pursuing higher education, is in search of their "banner."
  • Vayuneṣv ahnām: This translates to "in the energies of the days." It reflects the dynamic nature of life and the importance of harnessing daily energies for growth. For example, maintaining a daily routine that includes exercise, meditation, and work can help in personal development.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of dedication, respect for tradition, and the pursuit of goals. Its significance lies in reminding us to honor our commitments, overcome obstacles, and recognize the energies around us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, community involvement, and maintaining a connection with our roots. By embodying these principles, we can lead a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
