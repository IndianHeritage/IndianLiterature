अग्नि सहस्व पृतना अभिमातीर अपास्य |दुष्टरस तरन्न अरातीर वर्चो धा यज्ञवाहसे ||
agne sahasva pṛtanā abhimātīr apāsya | duṣṭaras tarann arātīr varco dhā yajñavāhase ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Sahasva: This term means "powerful" or "strong," indicating the might of Agni.
  • Pṛtanā: This can be interpreted as "praise" or "offering," suggesting the act of worship or reverence towards Agni.
  • Abhimātīr: This word means "to be favorable" or "to be pleased," indicating the desire for Agni's blessings.
  • Apāsya: This term means "to remove" or "to dispel," suggesting the act of eliminating negativity or obstacles.
  • Duṣṭaras: This word translates to "evil" or "malicious," referring to negative forces or adversities.
  • Tarann: This means "to cross over" or "to transcend," indicating the overcoming of challenges.
  • Arātīr: This term refers to "enemies" or "obstacles," emphasizing the presence of challenges in life.
  • Varco: This word means "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • Dhā: This means "to hold" or "to maintain," indicating the importance of sustaining positive qualities.
  • Yajñavāhase: This term refers to "the one who carries the sacrifice," emphasizing the significance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, seeking his powerful presence to dispel negativity and obstacles, while also requesting his blessings for strength and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with divine energies.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance to overcome adversities.
  • Spiritual Practices: Incorporating this mantra into daily prayers or meditation can help individuals focus on dispelling negativity and inviting positive energy into their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: During rituals or community events, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective strength and unity against common challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant importance in Vedic tradition as it encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support to overcome life's challenges. It serves as a reminder of the power of faith, rituals, and the pursuit of enlightenment in our daily lives.
