अमूरः कविर अदितिः विवस्वान् सुसंसन मित्रो अतिथिः शिवो नः |चित्रभानुर उषसां भाति अग्रे अपां गर्भः प्रस्व1 आ विवेश ||
amūraḥ kavir aditir vivasvān susaṁsan mitro atithiḥ śivo naḥ | citrabhānur uṣasām bhāty agre 'pāṁ garbhaḥ prasva1 ā viveśa ||
  • amūraḥ: This term can be interpreted as "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of certain truths or principles that govern existence.
  • kavir: This word means "the wise one" or "the poet." It refers to a figure of great knowledge and insight, often associated with divine inspiration.
  • aditir: Aditi is the mother of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing boundlessness and the infinite. This term emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • vivasvān: This refers to the sun or the radiant one, representing light, knowledge, and enlightenment. The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy.
  • susaṁsan: This term means "well-spoken" or "well-ordered." It suggests the importance of communication and the sharing of wisdom in a harmonious manner.
  • mitro: Meaning "friend," this word highlights the significance of friendship and alliances in human relationships and the support they provide.
  • atithiḥ: This translates to "guest" or "host." It underscores the value of hospitality and the respect given to visitors, which is a vital aspect of many cultures.
  • śivo: This term means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It indicates the importance of positive energy and good fortune in our lives.
  • naḥ: This means "to us," indicating a personal connection and the desire for these qualities to manifest in our lives.
  • citrabhānur: This term refers to "the colorful sun" or "the bright sun," symbolizing diversity and the beauty of creation.
  • uṣasām: This means "of the dawn," representing new beginnings, hope, and the promise of a new day.
  • bhāty: This translates to "shining" or "illuminating," emphasizing the enlightening aspect of knowledge and wisdom.
  • agre: This means "in front" or "ahead," suggesting progress and moving forward in life.
  • 'pāṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way," indicating the journey one takes in life.
  • garbhaḥ: This means "womb" or "source," symbolizing creation and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • prasva1: This term suggests "to manifest" or "to bring forth," indicating the act of creation and realization of potential.
  • ā viveśa: This means "to enter" or "to come into being," signifying the process of becoming and the unfolding of existence.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates profound themes of immortality, wisdom, nurturing, enlightenment, and the importance of relationships. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the significance of positive energies and auspiciousness in our daily experiences. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering friendships, practicing hospitality, and seeking knowledge. By embodying these qualities, individuals can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, contributing to the well-being of themselves and their communities.
