यो म इमं चिदुत्मनामन्दचित्रं दावने |अरट्वे अक्षे नहुषे सुखृत्वनि सुखृत्तराय सुख्रतुः ||
yo ma imaṁ cid u tmanāmandac citraṁ dāvane | araṭve akṣe nahuṣe sukṛtvani sukṛttarāya sukratuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • ma: me
    • imaṁ: this
    • cid: indeed
    • u: or
    • tmanā: by the self
    • mandac: with a good mind
    • citraṁ: colorful or diverse
    • dāvane: in the fire
    • araṭve: in the unshakable
    • akṣe: in the eye
    • nahuṣe: of the powerful
    • sukṛtvani: having done good deeds
    • sukṛttarāya: for the one who is more virtuous
    • sukratuḥ: of the one with good intentions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the quest for knowledge and understanding through the self.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good intentions and virtuous actions in achieving one's goals.
    • The imagery of fire suggests transformation and purification, indicating that through challenges, one can emerge stronger and wiser.
    • The reference to the "powerful" and "virtuous" highlights the significance of moral integrity and strength in character.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who acts with integrity and good intentions can inspire their team to achieve greater success.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who communicate openly and with good intentions foster trust and deeper connections.
    • During challenging times, such as a crisis, those who maintain a positive mindset and act virtuously can lead others through adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-awareness, good intentions, and virtuous actions in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding while remaining steadfast in their moral values.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a positive impact on society.
