बृहद्वयो हि भानवेऽर्चा देवायाग्नये |यम् मित्रं न प्रशस्तिभिर् मर्तासो दधिरे पुरः ||
bṛhad vayo hi bhānave 'rcā devāyāgnaye | yam mitraṁ na praśastibhir martāso dadhire puraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • vayo: age, life
    • hi: indeed
    • bhānave: to the shining one, the sun
    • 'rcā: hymns, praises
    • devāyāgnaye: to the divine fire, Agni
    • yam: whom
    • mitraṁ: friend, ally
    • na: not
    • praśastibhir: by praises, commendations
    • martāso: of mortals, human beings
    • dadhire: they have held, they have established
    • puraḥ: in front, before
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness of the divine, particularly Agni, the fire god, who is often associated with light and purity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of hymns and praises directed towards Agni, suggesting that such devotion is essential for human beings.
    • The phrase "whom friends do not commend" indicates that even without human praise, the divine remains powerful and significant.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked during ceremonies, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'Agni' can be related to the inner fire or passion that drives individuals to achieve their goals, regardless of external validation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of devotion and the recognition of the divine in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true strength and greatness come from within and that external praise is not necessary for the divine to hold its power.
    • In real life, this can be applied by fostering inner strength, pursuing our passions, and recognizing the divine in everyday experiences, regardless of external acknowledgment.
