ते अस्य सन्तु केतवो 'मृत्यवो 'दाब्यसो जनुशी उभे अनु |येभिर नृम्णा च देव्या च पुनत ād इद राजानम् मनना अगृभ्णत ||
te asya santu ketavo 'mṛtyavo 'dābhyāso januṣī ubhe anu | yebhir nṛmṇā ca devyā ca punata ād id rājānam mananā agṛbhṇata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you (plural)
    • asya: of this
    • santu: may they be
    • ketavo: wise or intelligent
    • 'mṛtyavo: of death
    • 'dābhyāso: of the two (or both)
    • januṣī: in the womb
    • ubhe: both
    • anu: following
    • yebhir: by whom
    • nṛmṇā: by men
    • ca: and
    • devyā: by the divine
    • ca: and
    • punata: purify
    • ād: from the beginning
    • id: this
    • rājānam: king
    • mananā: by thought
    • agṛbhṇata: may they grasp
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the collective wisdom and intelligence of individuals, urging them to be aware of the duality of life and death.
    • The phrase "may they be wise" suggests a call for enlightenment and understanding in the face of mortality.
    • The reference to "both in the womb" indicates the potential of life and the inevitability of death, emphasizing the cycle of existence.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of purification and the role of both humans and divine forces in this process.
    • It suggests that through thoughtful contemplation, one can grasp the essence of leadership and governance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, understanding the balance between life and death can lead to more compassionate decision-making, as seen in leaders who prioritize the well-being of their people.
    • In personal life, individuals facing loss may find solace in the wisdom of this mantra, encouraging them to reflect on the cycle of life and the importance of cherishing moments.
    • In spiritual practices, the mantra can be used as a meditative focus, helping individuals to purify their thoughts and intentions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and death, urging individuals to seek wisdom and understanding.
    • Its significance lies in its application to leadership, personal growth, and spiritual development, encouraging a thoughtful approach to existence.
    • By contemplating the themes presented in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper appreciation for life and the responsibilities that come with it.
