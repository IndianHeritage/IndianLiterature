स्वदस्व हव्यā सम इषो दिदीहि अस्मद्र्य1क सम मिमीहि श्रवांसी |विश्वां अग्नि पृत्सु तां जेषि शत्रून अहा विश्वा सुमना दिदिही नः ||
svadasva havyā sam iṣo didīhy asmadrya1k sam mimīhi śravāṁsi | viśvām̐ agne pṛtsu tāñ jeṣi śatrūn ahā viśvā sumanā dīdihī naḥ ||
  • svadasva havyā sam iṣo didīhy asmadrya1k sam mimīhi śravāṁsi
    • svadasva: This term can be interpreted as "may you be sweet" or "may you be pleasing." It signifies the desire for positive and harmonious experiences.
    • havyā: Refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving.
    • sam iṣo: Translates to "together with the divine." It suggests unity and collaboration with higher powers.
    • didīhy asmadrya1k: This phrase can be understood as "may our desires be fulfilled." It reflects the aspiration for personal and collective goals to be achieved.
    • sam mimīhi: Means "may you listen to us." It indicates a plea for attention and understanding from the divine.
    • śravāṁsi: Refers to "glories" or "fame." It highlights the importance of reputation and the impact of one's actions on society.
  • viśvām̐ agne pṛtsu tāñ jeṣi śatrūn
    • viśvām̐: Means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • agne: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • pṛtsu: Translates to "in the world" or "in the universe." It emphasizes the presence of divine energy in all aspects of life.
    • tāñ jeṣi: Means "may you conquer." It reflects the aspiration for overcoming challenges and adversities.
    • śatrūn: Refers to "enemies" or "obstacles." It signifies the struggles one faces in life.
  • ahā viśvā sumanā dīdihī naḥ
    • ahā: Means "indeed" or "truly." It emphasizes certainty and affirmation.
    • viśvā: Again refers to "all" or "universal," reinforcing the idea of inclusivity.
    • sumanā: Translates to "good thoughts" or "auspicious intentions." It highlights the importance of positive thinking and intentions.
    • dīdihī: Means "may you shine" or "may you illuminate." It signifies the desire for enlightenment and clarity.
    • naḥ: Refers to "us" or "our." It emphasizes the collective aspect of the prayer.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for harmony, fulfillment, and the overcoming of obstacles through divine assistance. It emphasizes the importance of positive intentions and collective aspirations. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can recite this mantra to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions, helping them to achieve their goals.
  • In community gatherings or rituals, it can be used to foster unity and collective strength against challenges.
  • In times of conflict or adversity, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on overcoming obstacles with a positive mindset.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to seek divine guidance, maintain positive intentions, and strive for collective success.
