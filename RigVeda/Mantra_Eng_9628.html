तद वाम ऋतं रोदसी प्र ब्रवीमि जायमानो मातरागर्भो अत्ति |नाहं देवस्य मर्त्यश चिकेताग्निर अङ्ग विचेताः स प्रचेताः ||
tad vām ṛtaṁ rodasī pra bravīmi jāyamāno mātarā garbho atti | nāhaṁ devasya martyaś ciketāgnir aṅga vicetāḥ sa pracetāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vām: your
    • ṛtaṁ: truth, order
    • rodasī: in the two worlds (heavens and earth)
    • pra: forth
    • bravīmi: I speak
    • jāyamāno: being born
    • mātarā: by the mother
    • garbho: the embryo
    • atti: attains
    • nāhaṁ: I do not
    • devasya: of the divine
    • martyaś: mortal
    • ciketā: think
    • agnir: fire
    • aṅga: body
    • vicetāḥ: aware
    • sa: he
    • pracetāḥ: wise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the relationship between the divine and the mortal, emphasizing the importance of truth and wisdom.
    • The phrase "tad vām ṛtaṁ" suggests a connection to a higher truth that transcends the physical realm.
    • The reference to "jāyamāno mātarā garbho atti" highlights the process of birth and the nurturing aspect of the mother, symbolizing the beginning of life and the potential it holds.
    • The line "nāhaṁ devasya martyaś ciketā" indicates a distinction between the divine and the mortal, suggesting that while we are part of the divine creation, we must also recognize our human limitations.
    • The mention of "agnir aṅga vicetāḥ" implies that wisdom and awareness are essential for navigating life, akin to how fire provides light and warmth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in the journey of personal growth, where individuals seek truth and wisdom in their experiences.
    • For instance, a student pursuing education embodies the essence of "jāyamāno mātarā garbho atti," as they are nurtured by their environment and education, growing into knowledgeable individuals.
    • In relationships, recognizing the divine aspect in others while understanding our mortal limitations can lead to deeper connections and empathy.
    • Professionals in any field can relate to "agnir aṅga vicetāḥ" by seeking knowledge and awareness to illuminate their paths and make informed decisions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking truth, and the wisdom that comes from understanding our place in the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace their journey of growth while recognizing the divine within themselves and others.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in daily life through self-reflection, education, and fostering meaningful relationships, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
