किमयं त्वां वृषाकपिश चकार हरितो मृगः |यस्मा इरस्यसीद उ न्व आर्यो वा पुष्टिमद वसु विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
kim ayaṁ tvāṁ vṛṣākapiś cakāra harito mṛgaḥ | yasmā irasyasīd u nv a1ryo vā puṣṭimad vasu viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • ayaṁ: this
    • tvāṁ: you
    • vṛṣākapiś: the one who is strong and powerful
    • cakāra: has done
    • harito: of the green or flourishing
    • mṛgaḥ: the deer or animal
    • yasmā: from whom
    • irasyasīd: the one who is full of strength
    • u: or
    • nv: indeed
    • āryo: noble
    • vā: or
    • puṣṭimad: one who is prosperous
    • vasu: wealth
    • viśvasmād: from all
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • uttaraḥ: the best or superior
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of strength and prosperity.
    • It reflects on the relationship between the divine (Indra) and the natural world (the flourishing deer).
    • The mantra suggests that true strength and prosperity come from a higher source, which is Indra.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea that success is often a result of collaboration and support from others, much like how Indra supports the flourishing of life.
    • In personal life, it can remind individuals that their strength and prosperity are often derived from their relationships and the community around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of strength, prosperity, and divine support.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek strength not just within oneself but also from the community and higher powers.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to foster relationships and seek support, recognizing that true prosperity is often a collective achievement.
