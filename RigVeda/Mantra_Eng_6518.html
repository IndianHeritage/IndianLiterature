मनोजवसा वृषणा मदाच्युता मक्षुंगमाभिर ऊतिभिः |आरात्ताच चिद्भूतम अस्मे अवसे पूर्वीभिः पुरुभोजसा ||
manojavasā vṛṣaṇā madacyutā makṣuṁgamābhir ūtibhiḥ | ārāttāc cid bhūtam asme avase pūrvībhiḥ purubhojasā ||
  • manojavasā: This term can be broken down into two parts: "manas" meaning mind and "javasa" meaning swift or quick. It refers to the swiftness of the mind, suggesting that thoughts can travel faster than physical actions.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and vigor, often associated with the qualities of a bull, which is a symbol of power in many cultures.
  • madacyutā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is intoxicated with joy." It reflects a state of bliss or ecstasy, often linked to spiritual experiences or deep emotional states.
  • makṣuṁgamābhir: This word can be understood as "with the help of the swift." It suggests the idea of assistance or support from those who are quick and agile, possibly referring to divine forces or energies.
  • ūtibhiḥ: This term means "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of energy and power in achieving one's goals.
  • ārāttāc: This can be translated as "from the source" or "from the origin." It indicates a connection to the fundamental aspects of existence or the universe.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness." It points to the idea of being aware of one's surroundings and the deeper truths of life.
  • bhūtam: This term translates to "the essence" or "the being." It refers to the fundamental nature of existence and the reality of life.
  • asme: This word means "to us" or "for us." It indicates a personal connection to the essence of being and existence.
  • avase: This can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to exist." It emphasizes the importance of being in the moment and recognizing one's existence.
  • pūrvībhiḥ: This term means "from the past" or "ancient." It suggests a connection to traditions, wisdom, and knowledge that have been passed down through generations.
  • purubhojasā: This word translates to "with abundant nourishment." It signifies the idea of being well-fed or supported, both physically and spiritually.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of swift thought, strength, joy, and consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of one's existence and the support one receives from the universe. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in meditation practices where one seeks to connect with their inner self and the universe. It encourages individuals to harness their mental agility and strength to navigate life's challenges while remaining grounded in their essence. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek joy, awareness, and nourishment from both their past and present experiences, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and conscious life.
