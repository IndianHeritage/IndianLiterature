विभिद्या पुरं शयतhem अपाचीं निस त्रिणि साकं उदधेर अकृत्नत् |बृहस्पतिर उषसं सूर्यं गाम अर्कं विवेद त्नयन्न इव द्यौः ||
vibhidyā puraṁ śayathem apācīṁ nis trīṇi sākam udadher akṛntat | bṛhaspatir uṣasaṁ sūryaṁ gām arkaṁ viveda stanayann iva dyauḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vibhidyā: by division or separation
    • puraṁ: the city or the abode
    • śayathem: as if it were sleeping
    • apācīṁ: the western direction
    • nis: out of
    • trīṇi: three
    • sākam: together
    • udadher: of the ocean
    • akṛntat: it was cut or divided
    • bṛhaspatir: the great lord or teacher (Brihaspati)
    • uṣasaṁ: the dawn
    • sūryaṁ: the sun
    • gām: the earth or the world
    • arkaṁ: the light or radiance
    • viveda: he has entered or penetrated
    • stanayann: as if nurturing or supporting
    • iva: like or as
    • dyauḥ: the sky or heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the cosmic processes of creation and division.
    • The imagery of a city that is asleep suggests a dormant state of potential, waiting to be awakened.
    • The reference to the ocean and the cutting of three parts may symbolize the division of the universe into different realms or aspects.
    • Brihaspati, as the teacher, represents wisdom and guidance, illuminating the path of knowledge.
    • The dawn and the sun signify new beginnings, enlightenment, and the dispelling of darkness.
    • The nurturing aspect of the sky indicates the protective and supportive nature of the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, the idea of awakening potential can be seen in individuals who strive for self-improvement, breaking free from their dormant states.
    • In education, the role of a teacher (like Brihaspati) is crucial in guiding students through their learning journey, illuminating their minds with knowledge.
    • The cycle of day and night, represented by the dawn and the sun, reflects the natural rhythms of life, emphasizing the importance of renewal and growth.
    • In environmental contexts, the division of the ocean into different ecosystems can be seen as a metaphor for the interconnectedness of life and the need for balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the themes of creation, knowledge, and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that wisdom and guidance are essential for awakening our potential and navigating the complexities of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, embrace new beginnings, and recognize the interconnectedness of all things.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a call to action for personal growth and collective harmony in the universe.
