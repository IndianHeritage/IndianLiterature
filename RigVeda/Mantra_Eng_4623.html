पातां सुतं इन्द्रो अस्तु सोमं प्रणेनीर उग्रो जारितारं ऊती |कर्तां वीराय सुष्वय उ लोकं दाता वसु स्तुवते कीरये चित ||
pātā sutam indro astu somam praṇenīr ugro jaritāram ūtī | kartā vīrāya suṣvaya u lokaṁ dātā vasu stuvate kīraye cit ||
  • pātā sutam: This phrase can be translated as "the son of Pātā," where Pātā refers to the Earth or the foundation. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the importance of grounding oneself in reality and being connected to one's roots.
  • indro astu somam: "May Indra be present with the soma." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. Soma is a sacred drink that symbolizes vitality and immortality. This can be related to the idea of seeking strength and nourishment in our lives, whether through physical health, mental well-being, or spiritual practices.
  • praṇenīr ugro jaritāram ūtī: This translates to "with the powerful energy of the breath." The word "ugro" signifies strength, while "jaritāram" refers to the act of moving or energizing. In real life, this can be connected to practices like yoga or meditation, where breath control is essential for harnessing energy and achieving mental clarity.
  • kartā vīrāya suṣvaya: "The doer of great deeds." This emphasizes the importance of action and courage in achieving one's goals. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who take initiative, whether in their careers, community service, or personal development.
  • u lokaṁ dātā vasu: "The giver of wealth to the world." This phrase highlights the significance of generosity and sharing resources. In a practical sense, this can be applied to philanthropy, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of society, enhancing the quality of life for others.
  • stuvate kīraye cit: "They praise the glory of the mind." This suggests the importance of recognizing and celebrating intellectual achievements and creativity. In real life, this can be reflected in the arts, sciences, and any field where innovation and thought are valued.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of strength, action, generosity, and intellectual pursuit. It serves as a reminder to ground ourselves in our roots while seeking nourishment and vitality. By embodying the qualities of courage, generosity, and creativity, we can contribute positively to our communities and the world at large. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to social responsibility, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while uplifting others.
