अग्निः सनोति वीर्याणि विद्वान् सनोति वाजम् अमृताय भूषण |स नो देवाम् एहा वहा पुरुष्को ||
agniḥ sanoti vīryāṇi vidvān sanoti vājam amṛtāya bhūṣan | sa no devām̐ eha vahā purukṣo ||
  • Agniḥ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Sanoti: Means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the act of giving or providing.
  • Vīryāṇi: Translates to "strengths" or "heroic qualities," representing valor and courage.
  • Vidvān: Means "the wise one" or "the knowledgeable," referring to someone who possesses wisdom and understanding.
  • Vājam: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with material and spiritual abundance.
  • Amṛtāya: Translates to "immortality" or "eternal," symbolizing the quest for everlasting life or spiritual enlightenment.
  • Bhūṣan: Means "ornament" or "adornment," indicating something that enhances beauty or value.
  • Sa no devām̐: Means "may the gods grant us," expressing a prayer or invocation for divine blessings.
  • Eha: Translates to "here" or "in this world," emphasizing the importance of the present life.
  • Vahā: Means "to carry" or "to bring," indicating the act of delivering or manifesting something.
  • Purukṣo: Refers to "the great being" or "the divine person," often associated with the universal spirit or consciousness.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for the bestowal of strength, wisdom, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving both material and spiritual goals. The invocation of Agni signifies the transformative power of fire, which can purify and energize one's life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as fire transforms raw materials into something valuable, individuals can seek wisdom and strength to overcome challenges in their lives.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Agni by inspiring others through their wisdom and courage, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be chanted during rituals or meditation to invoke divine blessings for health, prosperity, and spiritual growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving strength, wisdom, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment and abundance in their lives. By invoking the blessings of Agni, one can aspire to lead a life filled with purpose, courage, and spiritual fulfillment.
