त्री रोचनā दिव्या धारण्त हिरण्ययाः शुचयो धारपूताः |अस्वप्नजो अनिमिषा अदब्धा उरुशंसā ऋजवे मार्त्याय ||
trī rocanā divyā dhārayanta hiraṇyayāḥ śucayo dhārapūtāḥ | asvapnajo animiṣā adabdhā uruśaṁsā ṛjave martyāya ||
  • trī rocanā: This phrase translates to "three radiant ones" or "three luminous beings." In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as the three primary sources of light in our lives: the sun, the moon, and fire. These elements are essential for life and symbolize knowledge, purity, and energy.
  • divyā: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this word suggests a connection to the spiritual or the higher realms. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of inspiration or profound understanding that elevate our consciousness.
  • dhārayanta: This means "holding" or "sustaining." It signifies the act of maintaining balance and stability in life. For example, a family that supports each other through challenges embodies this principle.
  • hiraṇyayāḥ: Translated as "golden," this word represents wealth, prosperity, and abundance. In a practical sense, it can refer to the pursuit of financial stability and the importance of resources in achieving one's goals.
  • śucayo: This means "pure" or "clean." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. For instance, practicing honesty and integrity in business dealings reflects this purity.
  • dhārapūtāḥ: This translates to "those who are purified by holding." It suggests that maintaining purity in one's life leads to spiritual elevation. An example could be individuals who engage in regular meditation or mindfulness practices to cleanse their minds and spirits.
  • asvapnajo: Meaning "not born of sleep," this term can be interpreted as being awake or aware. In real life, it signifies the importance of being conscious and present in our actions and decisions, akin to mindfulness practices.
  • animiṣā: This translates to "without blinking," symbolizing vigilance and alertness. In practical terms, it can refer to being attentive and aware of one's surroundings, such as a parent watching over their child.
  • adabdhā: Meaning "unharmed" or "untouched," this word signifies resilience and strength. For example, a community that comes together to support each other during a crisis exemplifies this quality.
  • uruśaṁsā: This translates to "greatly praised" or "highly esteemed." It reflects the value of recognition and appreciation in our lives, such as receiving accolades for hard work or contributions to society.
  • ṛjave: Meaning "straight" or "righteous," this term emphasizes the importance of integrity and moral uprightness. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice and fairness.
  • martyāya: This translates to "for the mortal" or "for humanity." It signifies the connection between the divine and human realms, highlighting the importance of spiritual guidance in our earthly lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, purity, and the importance of being awake and aware in our lives. It emphasizes the need for balance, integrity, and resilience in the face of challenges. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community support, and ethical practices in various fields. By embodying the qualities described in this mantra, individuals can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives, contributing positively to society and fostering a sense of interconnectedness with the divine.
