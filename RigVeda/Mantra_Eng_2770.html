इन्द्रो वृत्मन अवृणोच छर्द्धनीतिः प्र मांयिनाम अमिनाद वर्पणीतिः |अहन व्यंसम उषधग वनेष्व आविर धेनाऽकृणोद रम्याणाम् ||
indro vṛtram avṛṇoc chardhanītiḥ pra māyinām aminād varpaṇītiḥ | ahan vyaṁsam uśadhag vaneṣv āvir dhenā akṛṇod rāmyāṇām ||
  • Indro vṛtram avṛṇoc: This phrase can be translated as "Indra, the king of gods, has vanquished Vritra." In this context, Indra represents strength and power, while Vritra symbolizes obstacles or challenges. This can be likened to overcoming personal challenges in life, such as facing fears or adversities.
  • Chardhanītiḥ pra māyinām: This translates to "the one who brings forth abundance for those who are wise." Here, the emphasis is on wisdom and the ability to attract prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who, through education and smart decision-making, create wealth and opportunities for themselves and others.
  • Aminād varpaṇītiḥ: This means "the one who grants the desires of the devoted." It highlights the importance of devotion and sincerity in one's pursuits. For example, a person who dedicates themselves to their work or a cause often finds fulfillment and success, as their commitment attracts positive outcomes.
  • Ahan vyaṁsam uśadhag vaneṣv: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth the healing herbs in the forests." This signifies the importance of nature and its resources in providing sustenance and healing. In modern life, this can relate to the growing interest in herbal medicine and sustainable living, where people seek natural remedies for health issues.
  • Āvir dhenā akṛṇod rāmyāṇām: This translates to "the one who manifests the beautiful and nourishing cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in many cultures. This can be related to the idea of nurturing relationships and communities that provide support and sustenance, much like how a family or community can be a source of strength and nourishment for its members.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, wisdom, devotion, nature, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder that overcoming challenges (like Indra overcoming Vritra) requires wisdom and devotion. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to face their obstacles with courage, seek knowledge, and nurture their relationships and environment. By embodying these principles, one can create a fulfilling and prosperous life, much like the abundance and beauty described in the mantra.
