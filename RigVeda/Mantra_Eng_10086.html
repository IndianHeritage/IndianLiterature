हरित्वता वर्चसा सूर्यस्य श्रेष्टै रूपैस् तन्वं स्पर्शयस्व |अस्माभिर इन्द्र सखिभिर हुवानः सदृचीनो माधयस्व निषद्य ||
haritvatā varcasā sūryasya śreṣṭhai rūpais tanvaṁ sparśayasva | asmābhir indra sakhibhir huvānaḥ sadhrīcīno mādayasvā niṣadya ||
  • Mantra: "haritvatā varcasā sūryasya śreṣṭhai rūpais tanvaṁ sparśayasva | asmābhir indra sakhibhir huvānaḥ sadhrīcīno mādayasvā niṣadya ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, may your body, adorned with the best forms of the sun's radiance, be touched by us. Together with our friends, we invoke you to be present and to delight us with your presence."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Haritvatā: Refers to the quality of being green or flourishing, symbolizing growth and vitality.
    • Varcasā: Denotes brilliance or radiance, often associated with the sun and its life-giving energy.
    • Sūryasya: Pertaining to the sun, the source of light and energy in our lives.
    • Śreṣṭhai: Meaning the best or the most excellent, indicating a desire for the highest quality.
    • Rūpais: Refers to forms or shapes, emphasizing the physical manifestation of energy and beauty.
    • Tanvaṁ: Refers to the body or form, highlighting the importance of physical presence.
    • Sparśayasva: Means to touch or to feel, indicating a desire for connection and interaction.
    • Asmābhir: Translates to "by us," indicating a collective effort in the invocation.
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and protection.
    • Sakhibhir: Meaning "with friends," emphasizing the importance of community and companionship.
    • Huvānaḥ: Refers to the act of invoking or calling upon, indicating a ritualistic aspect.
    • Sadhrīcīno: Meaning "together," reinforcing unity in the invocation.
    • Mādayasvā: Means "delight us," expressing a desire for joy and fulfillment.
    • Niṣadya: Refers to being present or seated, indicating a call for the divine to be among us.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often invoke blessings for prosperity and happiness, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for delight and presence.
    • In personal growth, individuals seek to embody qualities of vitality and brilliance, akin to the qualities of the sun, which can be seen in practices like yoga or meditation.
    • During celebrations, friends come together to invoke joy and blessings, reflecting the communal aspect of this mantra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine presence and the qualities of growth, brilliance, and community. It emphasizes the importance of connection—both with the divine and among individuals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as community rituals, personal development practices, and celebrations where the collective spirit is invoked for joy and prosperity. By recognizing the divine in our lives and seeking its presence, we can foster a sense of unity and fulfillment in our daily experiences.
