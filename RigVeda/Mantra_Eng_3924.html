चक्रं न वृत्तं पुरुहूत वेपते मनो भियā मे अमतेर इद अद्रिवः | रथाद अधि त्वा जरिता सदावृद्ध कुविन नु स्तोषन् मगवन पुरूवसुः ||
cakraṁ na vṛttam puruhūta vepate mano bhiyā me amater id adrivaḥ | rathād adhi tvā jaritā sadāvṛdha kuvin nu stoṣan maghavan purūvasuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakraṁ: wheel or cycle
    • na: not
    • vṛttam: revolving or turning
    • puruhūta: one who is invoked frequently
    • vepate: trembles or shakes
    • mano: mind
    • bhiyā: with fear
    • me: my
    • amater: of the immortal
    • id: this
    • adrivaḥ: of the non-moving
    • rathād: from the chariot
    • adhi: upon
    • tvā: you
    • jaritā: the one who is old or ancient
    • sadāvṛdha: always growing or increasing
    • kuvin: the wise or knowledgeable
    • nu: indeed
    • stoṣan: praising or worshipping
    • maghavan: the bountiful or generous
    • purūvasuḥ: the one who is rich in resources
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the cyclical nature of existence and the divine forces that govern it.
    • The "cakraṁ" symbolizes the cycles of life, death, and rebirth, which are central to many philosophical and spiritual traditions.
    • The invocation of "puruhūta" suggests a call to the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher powers.
    • The trembling of the mind ("mano bhiyā") indicates the fear and uncertainty that often accompany life's challenges.
    • The reference to "amater" and "adrivaḥ" highlights the contrast between the eternal and the transient, urging us to seek stability amidst chaos.
    • The "rathād" signifies the journey of life, where we must navigate through various experiences and challenges.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to praise the divine ("stoṣan maghavan purūvasuḥ"), recognizing the abundance and blessings that come from a higher source.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, such as during a personal crisis or a global pandemic, individuals often seek spiritual guidance, reflecting the invocation of divine support.
    • The cyclical nature of life can be observed in the changing seasons, where each phase brings its own challenges and opportunities for growth.
    • In professional settings, the journey of career advancement can be likened to the "rathād," where one must navigate through various roles and responsibilities to achieve success.
    • People often turn to meditation or mindfulness practices to calm their "trembling" minds, seeking stability and clarity in their thoughts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of seeking divine guidance in our journeys.
    • It encourages us to embrace the cycles of life, acknowledging both the challenges and the blessings that come our way.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience, seek wisdom, and express gratitude for the abundance in their lives.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes the significance of faith and the pursuit of higher knowledge in navigating the complexities of existence.
