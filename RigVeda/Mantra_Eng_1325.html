प्रवद्यामना सुवृता रथेन दश्राव इमं श्रिणुतं श्लोकम अद्रेः |किम अङ्ग वाम प्रत्य अवर्तिं गमिष्टाहुर विप्रासो अश्विनाः पुराजाः ||
pravadyāmanā suvṛtā rathena dasrāv imaṁ śṛṇutaṁ ślokam adreḥ | kim aṅga vām praty avartiṁ gamiṣṭhāhur viprāso aśvinā purājāḥ ||
  • pravadyāmanā: This term can be broken down into "pravadyā" meaning "speaking" or "proclaiming" and "manā" which refers to "mind" or "thought". Together, it suggests the act of expressing thoughts or ideas.
  • suvṛtā: This word means "well-formed" or "well-structured". It implies that the thoughts or expressions being shared are coherent and meaningful.
  • rathena: Translated as "by the chariot\ this symbolizes movement, progress, or a journey. In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the journey of life or the pursuit of knowledge.
  • dasrāv: This term can be interpreted as "to hear" or "to listen". It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • imaṁ: This means "this" and is used to refer to the subject matter at hand, which in this case is the mantra itself.
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This is a form of the verb "to hear" or "to listen\ reinforcing the call to pay attention to the teachings being shared.
  • ślokam: This refers to a "verse" or "hymn\ indicating that what follows is a structured expression of thought or wisdom.
  • adreḥ: This term can be understood as "of the mountain" or "of the firm ground\ symbolizing stability and strength.
  • kim aṅga: This phrase translates to "what indeed" or "what is it that\ prompting inquiry and reflection.
  • vām: Meaning "your" or "of you\ it personalizes the inquiry, making it relevant to the listener.
  • praty avartiṁ: This can be interpreted as "to turn back" or "to return\ suggesting a need to reflect on past actions or thoughts.
  • gamiṣṭhāhur: This term means "they who are going" or "those who are moving forward\ indicating progress or advancement.
  • viprāso: This refers to "the wise" or "the learned\ highlighting the importance of wisdom in the journey of life.
  • aśvinā: This term refers to the "Ashvins\ the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and healing.
  • purājāḥ: This means "the ancient ones" or "the elders\ suggesting a connection to tradition and the wisdom of those who came before.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the importance of expressing well-formed thoughts and being receptive to wisdom. It encourages individuals to listen carefully to the teachings of the wise, reflecting on their own journeys and the knowledge passed down through generations. The imagery of the chariot signifies the movement towards understanding and enlightenment, while the references to the Ashvins and the ancient ones highlight the significance of health, healing, and tradition in our lives.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of listening to the wisdom of others. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in educational settings, personal development, and even in community discussions where the sharing of ideas and experiences is vital. By embracing the teachings of the wise and reflecting on our own paths, we can foster growth and understanding in our lives and the lives of those around us.
