उताभये पुरुहूत श्रवोभिर एको दृढम अवदो वृत्रहा सन |इमे चिद इन्द्र रोदसी अपारे यत संगृभ्णा मगवन काशिर इत ते ||
utābhaye puruhūta śravobhir eko dṛḻham avado vṛtrahā san | ime cid indra rodasī apāre yat saṁgṛbhṇā maghavan kāśir it te ||
  • Mantra: "utābhaye puruhūta śravobhir eko dṛḻham avado vṛtrahā san | ime cid indra rodasī apāre yat saṁgṛbhṇā maghavan kāśir it te ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • utābhaye: In both (the realms)
    • puruhūta: O many-voiced one (Indra)
    • śravobhir: With fame or glory
    • eko: One
    • dṛḻham: Firm or strong
    • avado: Speak or declare
    • vṛtrahā: Slayer of Vritra (the demon)
    • san: Being or existing
    • ime: These
    • cid: Indeed
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms
    • apāre: Beyond
    • yat: That which
    • saṁgṛbhṇā: Is gathered or collected
    • maghavan: O bounteous one (Indra)
    • kāśir: Of the Kashi (a place of great significance)
    • it: Thus
    • te: To you
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, known for his strength and valor.
    • It emphasizes the unity of Indra as a singular force amidst the many voices of glory and fame.
    • The phrase "vṛtrahā san" refers to Indra as the slayer of the demon Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
    • The mantra also speaks of the realms beyond, indicating a connection to the divine and the vastness of existence.
    • It suggests that all that is gathered in the two worlds is under the purview of Indra, highlighting his omnipresence and power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of struggle or adversity, individuals often seek strength and guidance, much like how the mantra invokes Indra for support.
    • The concept of overcoming challenges can be seen in personal stories of resilience, where people face their 'Vritra'—be it fear, doubt, or external obstacles.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate victories, echoing the collective glory mentioned in the mantra.
    • In leadership, the idea of being a 'many-voiced' leader who listens to diverse opinions while maintaining a strong vision is crucial.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, unity, and the triumph of good over evil.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals or communities face challenges and seek empowerment.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can find inspiration to overcome obstacles and achieve greatness.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support available in times of need.
