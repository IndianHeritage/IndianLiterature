जज्ञानो नु शतक्रतुर विपृच्छद इति मातरम् |का उग्राः के ह श्रिण्विरे ||
jajñāno nu śatakratur vi pṛcchad iti mātaram | ka ugrāḥ ke ha śṛṇvire ||
  • Mantra: "jajñāno nu śatakratur vi pṛcchad iti mātaram | ka ugrāḥ ke ha śṛṇvire ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jajñāno: born, created, or generated
    • nu: indeed, certainly
    • śatakratur: the one who has a hundred powers or capabilities
    • vi: in various ways, or in different forms
    • pṛcchad: asked, inquired, or questioned
    • iti: thus, or so
    • mātaram: mother, or the nurturing aspect
    • ka: who
    • ugrāḥ: fierce, powerful, or intense
    • ke: who
    • ha: indeed, or certainly
    • śṛṇvire: have heard, or listened
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of creation and the inquiry into the nature of existence.
    • The phrase "jajñāno nu" emphasizes the importance of being born or created, suggesting that existence itself is a profound mystery.
    • "śatakratur" refers to the divine or cosmic power that governs creation, indicating that there are multiple forms and manifestations of this power.
    • The inquiry "ka ugrāḥ ke ha śṛṇvire" poses a question about who among the powerful has truly listened or understood the essence of creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of scientific inquiry, this mantra can be related to the quest for knowledge about the universe. Scientists ask profound questions about the origins of life and the universe, much like the inquiry posed in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to understand their purpose and the forces that shape their lives. This mantra encourages introspection and the pursuit of deeper understanding.
    • In a community setting, leaders may embody the qualities of "śatakratur" by empowering others and fostering a nurturing environment, akin to a mother figure.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inquiry and understanding in the journey of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and listen to the wisdom of the universe, recognizing the power of creation and the nurturing aspects of existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire personal growth, scientific exploration, and community leadership, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of understanding our origins.
