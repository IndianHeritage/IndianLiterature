यद वाजिनो दाम संदानम् अरवतॊ या शीरषण्याऽ रशनाऽ रज्जुर अस्य |यद वा घास्य प्रभरतम् आस्ये तृणं सर्वा ता ते अपि देवेष्व अस्तु ||
yad vājino dāma saṁdānam arvato yā śīrṣaṇyā raśanā rajjur asya | yad vā ghāsya prabhṛtam āsye3 tṛṇaṁ sarvā tā te api deveṣv astu ||
  • Mantra: "yad vājino dāma saṁdānam arvato yā śīrṣaṇyā raśanā rajjur asya | yad vā ghāsya prabhṛtam āsye3 tṛṇaṁ sarvā tā te api deveṣv astu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vājino: of the swift (or powerful)
    • dāma: restraint or control
    • saṁdānam: binding or connection
    • arvato: of the strong or mighty
    • yā: which
    • śīrṣaṇyā: with the head or top
    • raśanā: rope or cord
    • rajjur: of the rope
    • asya: of this
    • ghāsya: of the food or nourishment
    • prabhṛtam: that which is brought forth
    • āsye: in the mouth
    • tṛṇaṁ: grass or sustenance
    • sarvā: all
    • tā: those
    • te: to you
    • api: also
    • deveṣv: among the gods
    • astu: may it be
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection and control over the powerful forces of nature and life.
    • The phrase "yad vājino dāma" suggests the importance of restraint and control over one's desires and actions, akin to how a skilled horseman controls a powerful horse.
    • The mention of "arvato" and "raśanā" indicates the strength and binding nature of relationships, much like the bonds of family or friendship that hold us together.
    • In real life, this can be seen in how we manage our resources and relationships, ensuring that we do not let our desires overpower our responsibilities.
    • The reference to nourishment ("ghāsya prabhṛtam āsye tṛṇaṁ") emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that we should seek balance in what we consume and how we nurture ourselves.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of control, connection, and nourishment in our lives.
    • It teaches us to balance our desires with our responsibilities, ensuring that we remain grounded and connected to our roots.
    • In a practical sense, this can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal relationships, professional endeavors, and our approach to health and well-being.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek harmony with ourselves and the world around us, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
