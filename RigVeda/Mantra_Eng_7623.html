त्वं इन्द्र प्रतूर्तिष्व अभि विश्वा असि स्पृधः |अशस्तिहा जनिता विश्वतूर असि त्वं तूर्य तरुष्यतः ||
tvam indra pratūrtiṣv abhi viśvā asi spṛdhaḥ | aśastihā janitā viśvatūr asi tvaṁ tūrya taruṣyataḥ ||
  • tvam - You
  • indra - Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power
  • pratūrtiṣv - In the realms of protection and support
  • abhi - Towards, or in the direction of
  • viśvā - All, universal
  • asi - You are
  • spṛdhaḥ - Desiring, aspiring
  • aśastihā - The one who brings forth goodness
  • janitā - Creator, originator
  • viśvatūr - All-encompassing, universal
  • asi - You are
  • tvaṁ - You
  • tūrya - The one who is beyond
  • taruṣyataḥ - The one who is powerful and strong

This mantra can be interpreted as a profound acknowledgment of the divine presence and power that exists in all aspects of life. It emphasizes the idea that the divine (Indra) is not only a protector but also a creator of goodness and strength in the universe. The mantra calls upon this divine force to manifest in various realms, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings and the aspiration for universal well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Indra represents strength, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to cultivate their inner strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Community Support: The idea of universal protection can be reflected in community initiatives where individuals come together to support one another, fostering a sense of belonging and safety.
  • Environmental Awareness: The concept of being all-encompassing can extend to our relationship with nature, encouraging us to protect and nurture the environment for future generations.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for recognizing and harnessing the divine strength within ourselves and in the universe. It encourages individuals to aspire for goodness, support one another, and acknowledge the interconnectedness of all life. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive world, reflecting the essence of the mantra in our daily lives.
