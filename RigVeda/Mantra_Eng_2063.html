यथा विद्वां अरणं करद विश्वेभ्यो यजतेभ्यः |अयम् अग्नि त्वे अपि यं यज्ञं चक्रमा वयम् ||
yathā vidvām̐ araṁ karad viśvebhyo yajatebhyaḥ | ayam agne tve api yaṁ yajñaṁ cakṛmā vayam ||
  • yathā vidvām̐: This phrase translates to "as we know" or "as we understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our actions and rituals.
  • araṁ karad: This means "to perform" or "to do." It signifies the act of performing rituals or actions with intention and purpose.
  • viśvebhyo: This word refers to "all beings" or "the universe." It highlights the interconnectedness of all entities in existence.
  • yajatebhyaḥ: This translates to "to those who perform sacrifices" or "to those who worship." It indicates the act of offering and devotion to the divine.
  • ayam agne: This means "this is Agni" or "O Agni." Agni is the fire god, representing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • tve api: This translates to "to you also," indicating a direct address to Agni, acknowledging his importance in the ritual.
  • yaṁ yajñaṁ: This means "the sacrifice" or "the offering." It refers to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities, particularly to Agni.
  • cakṛmā: This translates to "we have made" or "we have performed." It signifies the completion of the ritual or offering.
  • vayam: This means "we." It emphasizes the collective participation of the community in the ritual.

The mantra essentially conveys the idea of performing rituals with knowledge and intention, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings, and honoring the divine presence of Agni through offerings. It reflects the importance of community participation in spiritual practices.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the role of knowledge, intention, and community in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for festivals or rituals, they can reflect on the importance of collective intention and knowledge in their practices.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can apply the principles of knowledge and intention in their daily actions, ensuring that their efforts are purposeful and connected to a greater whole.
  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings can inspire actions that promote sustainability and respect for nature.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of conscious action, community involvement, and reverence for the divine in our lives.
