दिवा नो अद्य पृथिवी अनागसो मही त्रायेतां सुविताया मातरा |उषा उचन्त्य अप बाधताम अगं स्वस्त्य अग्निं समिधानम् ईमहे ||
dyāvā no adya pṛthivī anāgaso mahī trāyetāṁ suvitāya mātarā | uṣā ucchanty apa bādhatām aghaṁ svasty a1gniṁ samidhānam īmahe ||
  • dyāvā no adya: This phrase translates to "May the heavens protect us today." Here, "dyāvā" refers to the sky or heavens, symbolizing a higher power or divine protection.
  • pṛthivī anāgaso mahī: This means "the earth, free from sin." "Pṛthivī" refers to the earth, while "anāgasa" signifies purity or being free from wrongdoing. This highlights the importance of a pure and untainted environment.
  • trāyetāṁ suvitāya mātarā: This translates to "May the nurturing mother protect us well." "Mātarā" refers to the mother, symbolizing care and protection, while "suvitāya" means well-nurtured or well-cared-for.
  • uṣā ucchanty: This means "the dawn rises." "Uṣā" symbolizes new beginnings and hope, indicating the arrival of light after darkness.
  • apa bādhatām aghaṁ: This translates to "May all obstacles and sins be removed." "Aghaṁ" refers to sin or negativity, emphasizing the desire for a life free from hindrances.
  • svasty a1gniṁ samidhānam īmahe: This means "We invoke the auspicious fire." "Agni" symbolizes fire, which is often associated with purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound prayer for protection, purity, and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the heavens, earth, and the nurturing aspect of nature. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or even in daily life when seeking protection and positivity. It serves as a reminder to maintain purity in our actions and thoughts, to seek divine guidance, and to foster a nurturing environment for ourselves and others. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for peace, protection, and the pursuit of a harmonious existence.
