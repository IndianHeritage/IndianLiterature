त्वं नः सोम विश्वतो वयोधास त्वं स्वर्विदा विशा नृचक्षाः |त्वं न इन्द ऊतिभिः सजोषाः पाहि पश्चाताद उत वा पुरस्तात ||
tvaṁ naḥ soma viśvato vayodhās tvaṁ svarvid ā viśā nṛcakṣāḥ | tvaṁ na inda ūtibhiḥ sajoṣāḥ pāhi paścātād uta vā purastāt ||
  • tvaṁ naḥ soma viśvato vayodhās: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Soma, are our strength from all directions." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and is a symbol of vitality and energy. In real life, this can be likened to the sources of inspiration and motivation we draw from various aspects of life, such as nature, relationships, and experiences.
  • tvaṁ svarvid ā viśā nṛcakṣāḥ: This translates to "You are the one who knows the essence of all beings." This line emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life. In practical terms, it suggests that knowledge and insight are crucial for making informed decisions, much like how a teacher or mentor guides students through their learning journey.
  • tvaṁ na inda ūtibhiḥ sajoṣāḥ: This means "You, O Indra, are the one who brings forth abundance." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods and a provider of prosperity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another, fostering a sense of abundance through collaboration and shared resources.
  • pāhi paścātād uta vā purastāt: This translates to "Protect us from behind and in front." This line signifies the need for protection and guidance in all aspects of life. In a real-world context, this can relate to the support systems we establish, such as family, friends, and community, which help us navigate challenges and uncertainties.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, wisdom, abundance, and protection in life. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of these elements, which are essential for personal and communal well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during times of decision-making, when seeking guidance from mentors, or when fostering community support. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
