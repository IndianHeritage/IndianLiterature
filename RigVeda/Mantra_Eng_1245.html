मन्त्र: कियात्य आ यत समया भवाति या व्यूषुर याश च नूनं व्युच्छान् |अनु पूर्वाः कृपते वावशानां प्रदीध्याना जोषम् अन्याभिर एति ||
kiyāty ā yat samayā bhavāti yā vyūṣur yāś ca nūnaṁ vyucchān | anu pūrvāḥ kṛpate vāvaśānā pradīdhyānā joṣam anyābhir eti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiyāty: What is it?
    • ā: This (or that)
    • yat: Which
    • samayā: Time
    • bhavāti: Happens or occurs
    • yā: Who
    • vyūṣur: Dawn or light
    • yāś: Fame or glory
    • ca: And
    • nūnaṁ: Certainly
    • vyucchān: Dispersed or separated
    • anu: After
    • pūrvāḥ: Previous or earlier
    • kṛpate: Bestows or grants
    • vāvaśānā: Those who are powerful
    • pradīdhyānā: Those who are enlightened
    • joṣam: Joy or delight
    • anyābhir: By others
    • eti: Comes or approaches
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of time and existence, asking what happens at the moment of dawn or enlightenment.
    • It reflects on the idea that certain moments are significant and transformative, much like the dawn that brings light after darkness.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are enlightened and powerful can bestow joy and delight upon others, indicating a connection between wisdom and happiness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider the moment of sunrise; it symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. Just as the dawn dispels darkness, moments of insight can illuminate our paths in life.
    • In a workplace, a leader who inspires and enlightens their team can create an environment of joy and productivity, reflecting the idea of bestowing happiness through wisdom.
    • In personal relationships, moments of understanding and connection can lead to deeper bonds, akin to the joy that comes from enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of time, enlightenment, and the joy that comes from wisdom.
    • It teaches us to recognize and cherish transformative moments in our lives, encouraging us to seek knowledge and share joy with others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling existence.
