तं अग्ने पाश्य उता तं पिपर्षि यस् त आनाṭ कवये शूर धीतिम् |यज्ञस्य वा निशितिं वोदितिं वा तं इत पृणक्षि शवसोता राया ||
tam agne pāsy uta tam piparṣi yas ta ānaṭ kavaye śūra dhītim | yajñasya vā niśitiṁ voditiṁ vā tam it pṛṇakṣi śavasota rāyā ||
  • tam agne pāsy uta: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, may we see you." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in our lives. In real life, this can be likened to seeking inspiration or guidance in our endeavors, much like how we look for a mentor or a source of motivation.
  • tam piparṣi: This means "may you nourish us." It emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual. In everyday life, this can relate to the need for nourishment through healthy food, education, and emotional support from family and friends.
  • yas ta ānaṭ kavaye: This translates to "who is the wise one." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, this can be seen in the respect given to teachers, scholars, and experienced individuals in various fields, as they guide us through complex decisions and challenges.
  • śūra dhītim: This means "the courage of the brave." It signifies the importance of bravery and determination in facing life's challenges. For instance, individuals who stand up for justice or pursue their dreams despite obstacles embody this courage in real life.
  • yajñasya vā niśitiṁ voditiṁ vā: This phrase refers to the "sacrifice or offering." It underscores the significance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good. In modern contexts, this can be seen in charitable acts, community service, or even in the workplace where teamwork and collaboration are essential.
  • tam it pṛṇakṣi: This translates to "may you grant us." It expresses a plea for blessings and support. In real life, this can be related to seeking help from others, whether it be through prayer, community support, or professional assistance during tough times.
  • śavasota rāyā: This means "the wealth of life." It signifies the abundance and richness that life can offer. In practical terms, this can refer to the pursuit of happiness, fulfillment, and the importance of relationships and experiences over material wealth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, nourishment, wisdom, courage, and the importance of selfless contributions in life. Its significance lies in its applicability to our daily lives, reminding us to seek inspiration, support one another, and strive for a life filled with purpose and abundance. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both individually and collectively.
