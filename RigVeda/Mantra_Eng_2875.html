मन्त्र: स्वयुर इन्द्र स्वराल् असि स्मद्दिष्टिः स्वयशस्तरः |स वावृधाना ओजसा पुरुष्टुत भवाऽनः सुश्रवस्तमः ||
svayur indra svarāḻ asi smaddiṣṭiḥ svayaśastaraḥ | sa vāvṛdhāna ojasā puruṣṭuta bhavā naḥ suśravastamaḥ ||
  • svayur: This word translates to "self-born" or "self-existent." It signifies the idea of being independent and self-sustaining.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • svarāḻ: This term can be interpreted as "radiant" or "brilliant," suggesting a state of enlightenment or clarity.
  • asi: This means "you are," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • smaddiṣṭiḥ: This can be understood as "by your command" or "by your will," emphasizing the power of intention and direction.
  • svayaśastaraḥ: This translates to "one who is renowned by oneself," highlighting the importance of self-recognition and personal achievement.
  • sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to a divine presence or force.
  • vāvṛdhāna: This term means "increasing" or "growing," suggesting a process of development or enhancement.
  • ojasā: This word refers to "vitality" or "energy," indicating the life force that drives growth and strength.
  • puruṣṭuta: This can be interpreted as "praise of the person," emphasizing the importance of recognizing and honoring individuals.
  • bhavā: This means "become" or "to be," indicating a transformation or evolution.
  • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • suśravastamaḥ: This means "the most renowned" or "the best of fame," suggesting the pursuit of excellence and recognition.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of self-realization, strength, and the pursuit of excellence. It emphasizes the importance of self-sufficiency and the power of intention in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and community building. For instance, an individual striving for success in their career can draw inspiration from this mantra by recognizing their own potential and working diligently towards their aspirations. Similarly, leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by empowering their teams and fostering an environment of growth and recognition. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine potential within each individual and the collective strength that can be harnessed for greater achievements.
