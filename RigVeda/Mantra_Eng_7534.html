कयां त्वं न ऊत्याभि प्र मन्दसे वृषन् |कयां स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
kayā tvaṁ na ūtyābhi pra mandase vṛṣan | kayā stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Mantra: "kayā tvaṁ na ūtyābhi pra mandase vṛṣan | kayā stotṛbhya ā bhara ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kayā: By what means or with what
    • tvaṁ: You
    • na: Not
    • ūtyābhi: By the offerings or praises
    • pra: For the sake of
    • mandase: The one who is gentle or mild
    • vṛṣan: The strong or powerful
    • stotṛbhya: To the worshippers or those who praise
    • ā: Come or bring
    • bhara: Fill or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to a divine entity, asking how one can approach or connect with the divine through offerings and praises.
    • The phrase "kayā tvaṁ" suggests a quest for understanding the nature of the divine and how to engage with it effectively.
    • The term "mandase vṛṣan" highlights the duality of the divine as both gentle and powerful, indicating that the divine encompasses all aspects of existence.
    • The invocation to "stotṛbhya ā bhara" emphasizes the importance of devotion and the need for the divine to respond to the worshippers' calls.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance or support from a higher power during challenging times, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • People engage in rituals, prayers, or meditative practices to connect with the divine, similar to the offerings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, the collective praise and offerings symbolize the unity of worshippers seeking blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of understanding the nature of the divine and the relationship between the worshipper and the divine.
    • It highlights the need for sincere devotion and the acknowledgment of the divine's multifaceted nature—gentle yet powerful.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek connection through devotion, fostering a sense of community and shared spirituality.
