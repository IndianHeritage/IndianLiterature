ताविद्दोषा ता उषसि शुभसपति ता यामन रुद्रवर्तनी |मा नो मर्ताय रिपवे वाजिनिवासू परो रुद्रावातिख्यातम् ||
tāv id doṣā tā uṣasi śubhas patī tā yāman rudravartanī | mā no martāya ripave vājinīvasū paro rudrāv ati khyatam ||
  • tāv id doṣā: This phrase can be translated as "these faults" or "these imperfections." It suggests an acknowledgment of the flaws or challenges that exist in life.
  • tā uṣasi śubhas patī: This translates to "that auspicious lord of dawn." Here, "uṣasi" refers to the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope, while "śubhas patī" indicates a benevolent or auspicious ruler.
  • tā yāman rudravartanī: This can be interpreted as "that which follows the path of Rudra." Rudra is often associated with storms and transformation, indicating a journey that may involve challenges but ultimately leads to growth.
  • mā no martāya ripave vājinīvasū: This phrase means "may we not be harmed by the enemy." It reflects a desire for protection and safety from adversities or negative influences in life.
  • paro rudrāv ati khyatam: This translates to "the supreme Rudra is well-known." It emphasizes the power and recognition of Rudra, suggesting that invoking this energy can lead to overcoming obstacles.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of recognizing imperfections in life while seeking the blessings of auspicious beginnings and protection from adversities. It serves as a reminder that while challenges may arise, there is always the potential for renewal and transformation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during times of difficulty, such as facing personal challenges, conflicts, or transitions. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope, resilience, and the strength to navigate through life's storms, ultimately leading to personal growth and a deeper connection with the divine forces that guide us.
