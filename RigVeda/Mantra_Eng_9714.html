विहि सोतोर असृक्षत नेंद्रं देवम् अमंसत |यत्रामदद वृषाकपिर आर्यः पुष्टेषु मत्सखā विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
vi hi sotor asṛkṣata nendraṁ devam amaṁsata | yatrāmadad vṛṣākapir aryaḥ puṣṭeṣu matsakhā viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The one who is the protector of the world, who does not let the blood of the innocent be shed, who is the divine Indra, the one who is the source of strength and nourishment, where the powerful and noble ones gather, may Indra, the supreme one, be exalted above all."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi hi: Indeed, truly.
    • sotor: Protector or guardian.
    • asṛkṣata: One who does not allow bloodshed.
    • nendraṁ: Indra, the king of gods.
    • devam: Divine or godly.
    • amaṁsata: Source of strength or nourishment.
    • yatrāmadad: Where the powerful gather.
    • vṛṣākapir: The noble or strong one.
    • aryaḥ: Noble or honorable.
    • puṣṭeṣu: In nourishment or abundance.
    • matsakhā: My friend or ally.
    • viśvasmād: From all or universal.
    • indra uttaraḥ: Indra, the supreme one.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of protecting life and ensuring that no innocent blood is shed. In modern society, this can be related to the role of leaders and governments in upholding justice and peace.
    • The concept of gathering powerful and noble individuals can be seen in community organizations or movements that aim to bring about social change, where collaboration and strength are essential for success.
    • The idea of nourishment can be applied to various aspects of life, such as mental health, where supportive relationships and community play a crucial role in personal growth and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection, justice, and the gathering of noble individuals for the greater good. It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with power and the importance of community support. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to act as protectors in their communities, to seek justice, and to foster environments where everyone can thrive. This mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as leadership, community service, and personal relationships, emphasizing the need for strength, unity, and compassion in our actions.
