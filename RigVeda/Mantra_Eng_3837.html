समिद्धस्य प्रमहसोऽग्ने वन्दे तव श्रीयम् |वृषभो द्युम्नवाम्ऽसि सम अध्वरिषु इध्यसे ||
samiddhasya pramahaso 'gne vande tava śriyam | vṛṣabho dyumnavām̐ asi sam adhvareṣv idhyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddhasya: One who is kindled or ignited.
    • pramahaso: Of the great or supreme light.
    • agne: O Fire, or O Agni (the deity of fire).
    • vande: I bow or I worship.
    • tava: Your.
    • śriyam: Glory, wealth, or prosperity.
    • vṛṣabho: The bull, symbolizing strength and power.
    • dyumnavām̐: One who is endowed with brilliance or glory.
    • asi: You are.
    • sam: Together or in harmony.
    • adhvareṣv: In the sacrifices or rituals.
    • idhyase: You shine or you are invoked.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a hymn of praise to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "samiddhasya pramahaso" refers to the ignited fire that symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge.
    • By bowing to Agni, the speaker acknowledges the divine presence and seeks blessings for prosperity and strength.
    • The mention of "vṛṣabho dyumnavām̐" highlights the qualities of strength and brilliance, suggesting that Agni embodies these attributes.
    • The invocation of Agni in rituals signifies the importance of fire in spiritual practices, representing purification and transformation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the "fire" can represent passion and determination in one's career or personal goals. Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can transform their lives through hard work and dedication.
    • In environmental contexts, controlled burns are used to manage forests, showcasing the dual nature of fire as both a destructive and a regenerative force.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of light, strength, and prosperity.
    • It teaches us to respect and honor the forces of nature, recognizing their role in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and strength in their endeavors, whether in personal growth, relationships, or professional pursuits.
    • By invoking Agni, we are reminded of the importance of rituals and the sacredness of our intentions, guiding us towards a path of harmony and fulfillment.
