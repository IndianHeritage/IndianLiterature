यā धारणन्त देवाः सुदक्षā दाक्षपितरā | असुर्याय प्रमहसā ||
yā dhārayanta devāḥ sudakṣā dakṣapitarā | asuryāya pramahasā ||
  • yā dhārayanta devāḥ: This phrase translates to "those who are upheld by the gods." It signifies the divine support and guidance that individuals receive in their lives. In real life, this can be seen in moments when people feel a sense of protection or inspiration, such as during challenging times when they rely on their faith or spiritual beliefs.
  • sudakṣā: This word means "skilled" or "capable." It emphasizes the importance of competence and expertise in various fields. For example, a skilled doctor who saves lives or a talented artist who creates beautiful works can be seen as embodying this quality. It reminds us to cultivate our skills and strive for excellence in our endeavors.
  • dakṣapitarā: This term refers to "the capable father" or "the skilled creator." It can symbolize the nurturing and guiding role of parental figures in our lives. In a real-life context, this could relate to a mentor or a parent who provides wisdom and support, helping individuals navigate their paths successfully.
  • asuryāya: This word translates to "for the non-divine" or "for those who are not enlightened." It serves as a reminder that not everyone is on the same spiritual or moral path. In practical terms, this can be observed in societal issues where individuals may act out of ignorance or malice, highlighting the need for compassion and understanding towards others.
  • pramahasā: This term means "with great strength" or "with great power." It signifies the importance of inner strength and resilience. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome adversity, such as a person battling illness or someone who stands up for justice despite facing opposition. It encourages us to harness our inner power to face challenges.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "yā dhārayanta devāḥ sudakṣā dakṣapitarā | asuryāya pramahasā ||" encapsulates the essence of divine support, skill, guidance, and inner strength. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; we have the support of the divine and the wisdom of those who guide us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to develop our skills, seek guidance from mentors, and cultivate resilience in the face of challenges. It also calls for compassion towards those who may not yet understand their paths, urging us to be a source of strength and support for one another.
