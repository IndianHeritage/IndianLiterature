स वावृधे नार्यो योषणासु वृषा शिशुर वृषभो यज्ञियासु |स वाजिनं मघवद्भ्यो दधाति वि सातये तन्मं मामृजीत ||
sa vāvṛdhe naryo yoṣaṇāsu vṛṣā śiśur vṛṣabho yajñiyāsu | sa vājinam maghavadbhyo dadhāti vi sātaye tanvam māmṛjīta ||
  • sa vāvṛdhe: This phrase can be translated as "he who has grown" or "he who has expanded." It signifies growth and development, which can be related to personal or spiritual growth in real life.
  • naryo yoṣaṇāsu: This refers to "men and women" or "the people." It emphasizes the importance of community and relationships in our lives.
  • vṛṣā: This word means "strength" or "power." It can be associated with the inner strength that individuals possess, which helps them overcome challenges.
  • śiśur vṛṣabho: "śiśur" means "child" and "vṛṣabho" refers to "the bull" or "the strong one." This can symbolize the potential and strength of the younger generation, representing hope for the future.
  • yajñiyāsu: This term relates to "sacrifices" or "offerings." It highlights the importance of selflessness and contributing to the greater good, akin to community service or charitable acts in modern society.
  • sa vājinam maghavadbhyo dadhāti: This phrase translates to "he who gives strength to the powerful." It signifies the role of leaders or mentors who empower others, similar to how a coach inspires athletes to perform better.
  • vi sātaye: This means "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving." It emphasizes the importance of merit and recognition of those who truly deserve it in society.
  • tanvam māmṛjīta: This can be interpreted as "may he cleanse my body" or "may he purify me." It signifies the desire for spiritual and physical purification, akin to practices like meditation or yoga that promote well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of growth, community, strength, and empowerment. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of contributing to the welfare of society. In real life, this mantra can inspire us to support one another, recognize the potential in the younger generation, and strive for personal and collective growth. It encourages us to engage in selfless acts and to seek purification of body and mind, which is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, community service, and spiritual practices.
