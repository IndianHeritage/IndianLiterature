अस्मिन समुद्रे अध्य उत्तरस्मिन्न आपो देवैभिर निवृता अतीष्टन् |तā अद्रवन्न आर्ष्टिषेणेन सृष्टा देवापिनā प्रेषिता मृक्षिणीषु ||
asmin samudre adhy uttarasminn āpo devebhir nivṛtā atiṣṭhan | tā adravann ārṣṭiṣeṇena sṛṣṭā devāpinā preṣitā mṛkṣiṇīṣu ||
  • Mantra: "asmin samudre adhy uttarasminn āpo devebhir nivṛtā atiṣṭhan | tā adravann ārṣṭiṣeṇena sṛṣṭā devāpinā preṣitā mṛkṣiṇīṣu ||"
  • Translation: "In this ocean, the waters have been established by the gods, having been sent forth by the divine. They have been created with the power of the seer, and they flow into the fields of the mortals."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • asmin: in this
    • samudre: ocean
    • adhy: above or in the midst
    • uttarasminn: in the northern part
    • āpo: waters
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • nivṛtā: established or set
    • atiṣṭhan: having been sent forth
    • tā: they
    • adravann: created or made
    • ārṣṭiṣeṇena: with the power of the seer
    • sṛṣṭā: created
    • devāpinā: by the divine
    • preṣitā: sent forth
    • mṛkṣiṇīṣu: into the fields of mortals

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The mantra speaks of the divine origin of water, which is essential for life. In agriculture, the importance of water is paramount, as it sustains crops and ensures food security.
    • In modern times, the concept of sustainable water management reflects the idea of divine waters being sent forth to nourish the earth. Initiatives like rainwater harvesting and watershed management can be seen as practical applications of this principle.
    • The mantra can also be related to the spiritual significance of water in rituals and ceremonies, where water is often seen as a purifying element, connecting the physical and spiritual realms.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the sacredness of water and its divine origins, reminding us of our responsibility to respect and protect this vital resource.
    • In real life, it encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, urging us to engage in practices that honor the divine gifts of the earth.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a call to action for environmental stewardship, highlighting the significance of water in sustaining life and the importance of maintaining harmony with nature.
