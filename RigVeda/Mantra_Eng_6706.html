पण्य आ दर्धिरच छता सहस्रā वाज्य अवृतः |इन्द्रो यो यजनो वृद्धः ||
panya ā dardirac chatā sahasrā vājy avṛtaḥ | indro yo yajvano vṛdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • panya: Wealth or riches
    • ā: To or towards
    • dardirac: To be protected or safeguarded
    • chatā: A thousand or multitude
    • sahasrā: Thousand
    • vājy: To be invoked or called upon
    • avṛtaḥ: Covered or enveloped
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • yo: Who
    • yajvano: The one who performs sacrifices or offerings
    • vṛdhaḥ: Great or powerful
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks about the invocation of wealth and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of protection and the role of Indra, the king of gods, in bestowing blessings upon those who perform sacrifices or offerings. It suggests that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and communal well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Wealth and Prosperity: In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of financial stability. For instance, individuals who engage in ethical business practices and contribute to their communities often find that their efforts lead to greater success and prosperity.
    • Protection of Wealth: Just as the mantra speaks of safeguarding wealth, individuals often invest in insurance and savings to protect their financial assets. This reflects the idea of being proactive in securing one's resources.
    • Community Offerings: Many cultures have traditions of giving back to the community, such as charity events or community service. These acts can be seen as modern-day sacrifices that invoke blessings for both the giver and the receiver.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth, emphasizing that true richness comes from a combination of material success, spiritual fulfillment, and community well-being. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek prosperity not just for themselves but also for the greater good, reminding us that our actions can invoke blessings that benefit all. By performing good deeds and making offerings, we align ourselves with the divine, inviting abundance and protection into our lives.
