पुरीयाशो अग्नयः प्रावणैभिः सजोषसः |जुषन्तां यज्ञं अद्रुहोऽनमीवाऽइषो महीः ||
purīṣyāso agnayaḥ prāvaṇebhiḥ sajoṣasaḥ | juṣantāṁ yajñam adruho 'namīvā iṣo mahīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • purīṣyāso: Refers to the "sacred offerings" or "sacrificial materials."
    • agnayaḥ: Means "to the fires" or "to the deities associated with fire."
    • prāvaṇebhiḥ: Translates to "with the offerings" or "with the oblations."
    • sajoṣasaḥ: Indicates "with joy" or "with enthusiasm."
    • juṣantāṁ: Means "may they enjoy" or "may they partake."
    • yajñam: Refers to the "sacrifice" or "ritual."
    • adruho: Means "without enmity" or "without malice."
    • 'namīvā: Translates to "may they be nourished" or "may they be sustained."
    • iṣo: Refers to "the powerful" or "the strong."
    • mahīḥ: Means "the great" or "the vast."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings made to the divine, particularly to the fire deities, which are central to Vedic rituals.
    • The phrase "with joy" suggests that the offerings should be made with a positive and enthusiastic spirit, reflecting the belief that the attitude of the giver influences the efficacy of the ritual.
    • The mention of "without enmity" highlights the need for purity of intention and harmony among participants in the ritual, promoting a sense of community and goodwill.
    • The concluding part, "may they be nourished," indicates a desire for the divine to bless the offerings and, in turn, bless the community with prosperity and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to share food and resources, fostering a spirit of unity and joy.
    • During festivals, such as Diwali, families perform rituals with enthusiasm and joy, offering sweets and prayers to deities, embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In corporate settings, team-building activities that promote collaboration and goodwill among employees can be seen as a modern interpretation of the mantra's call for harmony and positive intentions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a reminder of the significance of intention and community in rituals and offerings.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to approach their actions with joy, unity, and a spirit of goodwill.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can foster a harmonious environment that promotes collective well-being and prosperity.
