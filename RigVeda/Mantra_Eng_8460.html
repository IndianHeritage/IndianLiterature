स सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः परि व्यत तन्तुं तन्मानस त्रिवृतं यथा विदे |नयन्न ऋतस्य प्रशिषो नवीयसीः पतिर जनीनाम उप याती निष्कृतम् ||
sa sūryasya raśmibhiḥ pari vyata tantuṁ tanvānas trivṛtaṁ yathā vide | nayann ṛtasya praśiṣo navīyasīḥ patir janīnām upa yāti niṣkṛtam ||
  • sa sūryasya raśmibhiḥ: This phrase translates to "with the rays of the sun." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, symbolizing illumination and knowledge.
  • pari vyata tantuṁ tanvānas: This can be interpreted as "surrounding the body with its rays." It suggests a protective and nurturing aspect, where the sun's rays envelop and safeguard.
  • trivṛtaṁ yathā vide: This means "as if it were threefold." The number three often represents completeness and balance in various cultures, indicating a holistic approach to life.
  • nayann ṛtasya praśiṣo: This translates to "leading to the truth of the righteous." It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in guiding one's actions and decisions.
  • navīyasīḥ patir janīnām: This means "the new lord of beings." It signifies a fresh beginning or a new leader, which can be interpreted as the emergence of new ideas or leaders in society.
  • upa yāti niṣkṛtam: This translates to "approaching the liberation." It suggests a journey towards freedom or enlightenment, highlighting the importance of striving for personal and spiritual growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking truth, protection, and enlightenment through the metaphor of the sun's rays. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and the pursuit of knowledge. For instance, in a workplace, a leader who embodies the qualities of truth and righteousness can inspire their team, much like the sun nurtures life. Additionally, individuals can reflect on their own journeys towards enlightenment, striving to surround themselves with positivity and truth, ultimately leading to personal liberation and growth.
