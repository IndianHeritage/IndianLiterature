मां त्वा श्येन उद वधीन मां सुपर्णो मां त्वा विदद इषुमान वीरो अस्तां |पित्रीयाम् अनु प्रदिशं कनिक्रदत सुमंगलो भद्रवादि वदेषा ||
mā tvā śyena ud vadhīn mā suparṇo mā tvā vidad iṣumān vīro astā | pitryām anu pradiśaṁ kanikradat sumaṅgalo bhadravādī vadeha ||
  • mā tvā śyena ud vadhīn: This phrase translates to "May the fierce eagle not harm you." The eagle, often seen as a symbol of strength and power, represents challenges or adversities in life. In real life, this can be interpreted as a plea for protection against overwhelming forces or negative influences.
  • mā suparṇo: "May the well-flying bird not harm you." The term "suparṇa" refers to a bird that soars high, symbolizing freedom and transcendence. This can relate to the idea of avoiding distractions or temptations that may lead one astray from their path.
  • mā tvā vidad iṣumān vīro astā: This means "May the strong hero not strike you with arrows." Here, the "hero" can symbolize any formidable challenge or adversary in life. The arrows represent the difficulties or obstacles one may face. This line emphasizes the desire for resilience and protection against life's trials.
  • pitryām anu pradiśaṁ: This translates to "Following the path of the ancestors." This phrase highlights the importance of tradition, heritage, and the wisdom passed down through generations. In real life, it can be seen in the way families uphold their values and customs, guiding their actions and decisions.
  • kanikradat sumaṅgalo: "The auspicious one who brings good fortune." This part of the mantra invokes the presence of positivity and blessings in one's life. In practical terms, it can refer to the people or circumstances that bring joy and success, encouraging individuals to surround themselves with positivity.
  • bhadravādī vadeha: "Speak words of auspiciousness." This emphasizes the power of words and communication. In real life, it can be applied to the importance of positive affirmations and constructive dialogue, which can uplift and inspire others.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a powerful invocation for protection, guidance, and positivity in life. It emphasizes the significance of staying true to one's roots while navigating challenges. In a real-life scenario, individuals can apply this mantra by seeking protection from negativity, embracing their heritage, and fostering positive communication. By doing so, they can cultivate a life filled with strength, resilience, and auspiciousness, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
