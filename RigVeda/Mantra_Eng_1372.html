पत्नीव पूर्वहूतिं वावृद्धध्या उषासनक्ता पुरुधा विदाने |स्तरीर नात्कं व्युतं वसाना सूर्यस्य श्रिया सुदृशी हिरण्यैः ||
patnīva pūrvahūtiṁ vāvṛdhadhyā uṣāsānaktā purudhā vidāne | starīr nātkaṁ vyutaṁ vasānā sūryasya śriyā sudṛśī hiraṇyaiḥ ||
  • patnīva: This word translates to "like a wife" or "as a partner." It signifies companionship and the bond of love and support in relationships.
  • pūrvahūtiṁ: This means "the previous offering" or "the earlier invocation." It suggests the importance of tradition and the continuity of rituals and prayers.
  • vāvṛdhadhyā: This can be interpreted as "having grown" or "having been nourished." It indicates the idea of growth, both in a spiritual and material sense.
  • uṣāsānaktā: This refers to "the dawn" or "the early morning." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • purudhā: This word means "in many ways" or "variously." It reflects the diversity of experiences and the multifaceted nature of life.
  • vidāne: This translates to "in the act of knowing" or "in understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our lives.
  • starīr: This means "the stars" or "the celestial bodies." It signifies the vastness of the universe and our connection to it.
  • nātkaṁ: This can be interpreted as "not to be neglected" or "not to be overlooked." It highlights the importance of paying attention to the significant aspects of life.
  • vyutaṁ: This means "having been separated" or "having been distinguished." It suggests the idea of discernment and the ability to differentiate between various aspects of life.
  • vasānā: This translates to "dwelling" or "residing." It indicates the importance of a stable and secure environment.
  • sūryasya: This means "of the sun." The sun is often associated with energy, vitality, and life-giving force.
  • śriyā: This translates to "with prosperity" or "with wealth." It signifies abundance and the blessings that come from a fruitful life.
  • sudṛśī: This means "beautiful" or "well-formed." It reflects the aesthetic aspects of life and the appreciation of beauty.
  • hiraṇyaiḥ: This translates to "with gold" or "with riches." It symbolizes material wealth and the prosperity that comes from hard work and dedication.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of relationships, growth, knowledge, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder to cherish our connections, honor traditions, and seek wisdom in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal relationships, professional growth, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages us to embrace new beginnings, appreciate the beauty around us, and strive for a prosperous and fulfilling life. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious existence that reflects the teachings of the RigVeda.
