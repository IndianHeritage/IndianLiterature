सुवितस्य मनामहे 'ति सेतुं दुराव्यम् |साह्वांसो दस्युम अव्रतम् ||
suvitasya manāmahe 'ti setuṁ durāvyam | sāhvāṁso dasyum avratam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suvitasya: Of the good or auspicious.
    • manāmahe: We meditate or contemplate.
    • 'ti: Thus or so.
    • setuṁ: Bridge or connection.
    • durāvyam: Difficult to cross or insurmountable.
    • sāhvāṁso: With strength or power.
    • dasyum: The enemy or adversary.
    • avratam: Without transgression or violation.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for guidance and strength in overcoming obstacles.
    • The term "suvitasya" signifies the pursuit of goodness and auspiciousness in life.
    • By meditating on this goodness, one seeks to create a "setuṁ" or bridge that helps in navigating through life's challenges.
    • The phrase "durāvyam" highlights the recognition of difficulties that may seem insurmountable.
    • However, with "sāhvāṁso," or the strength derived from goodness, one can confront and overcome these adversities.
    • The mention of "dasyum avratam" suggests that this strength is to be used without transgressing moral or ethical boundaries.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges such as project deadlines or team conflicts. By focusing on positive values and maintaining ethical standards, they can build bridges with colleagues to resolve issues.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with health issues may find it difficult to maintain a positive outlook. By meditating on good health and seeking support from loved ones, they can create a pathway to recovery.
    • In community service, volunteers often encounter resistance or apathy. By embodying the principles of goodness and strength, they can inspire others to join their cause and overcome societal challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of cultivating goodness and strength in the face of adversity.
    • It encourages individuals to seek ethical paths while overcoming challenges, reinforcing the idea that true strength lies in moral integrity.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a positive impact on society.
