आ राजाना दिविष्पृशास्मत्रा गन्तम उप नः |इमे वाम मित्रावरुणा गवाशिरः सोमाः शुक्रा गवाशिरः ||
ā rājānā divispṛśāsmatrā gantam upa naḥ | ime vām mitrāvaruṇā gavāśiraḥ somāḥ śukrā gavāśiraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rājānā: O kings
    • divispṛśā: who touch the heavens
    • asmatrā: from this place
    • gantam: may you come
    • upa naḥ: near us
    • ime: these
    • vām: your
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Mitra and Varuna
    • gavāśiraḥ: the wealth of cows
    • somāḥ: the soma (sacred drink)
    • śukrā: the pure
    • gavāśiraḥ: the wealth of cows (repeated)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the kings and divine beings, asking them to come near and bless the speaker.
    • The mention of "kings who touch the heavens" signifies the importance of leadership that is not only earthly but also divine in nature.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna represents the duality of cosmic order and moral law, emphasizing the need for balance and righteousness.
    • The reference to "wealth of cows" symbolizes prosperity and abundance, which were highly valued in ancient societies.
    • The "soma" represents spiritual nourishment and the quest for enlightenment, indicating the importance of both material and spiritual wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern leadership, the qualities of integrity and moral responsibility are essential. Leaders who embody these traits can inspire their followers, much like the divine kings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community gatherings, invoking the spirit of cooperation and mutual respect, akin to the principles of Mitra and Varuna, can lead to harmonious relationships.
    • The pursuit of prosperity can be seen in businesses that prioritize ethical practices while striving for success, reflecting the balance of material and spiritual wealth.
    • In personal life, individuals can seek spiritual growth through practices like meditation, paralleling the quest for "soma" or enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance in leadership and the importance of moral values in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth encompasses both material abundance and spiritual fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in leadership, community building, and personal development, fostering a holistic approach to life.
