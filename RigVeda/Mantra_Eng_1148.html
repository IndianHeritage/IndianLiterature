अर्थमिद्वा उ अर्थिना आ जाया युवते पतिम् |तुञ्जाते वृष्ण्यम् पयः परिदाय रसं दुहे वित्तम् मे अस्य रोदसी ||
artham id vā u arthina ā jāyā yuvate patim | tuñjāte vṛṣṇyam payaḥ paridāya rasaṁ duhe vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • artham: for the purpose, meaning
    • id: this
    • vā: or
    • u: and
    • arthina: for the seeker, the one who desires
    • ā jāyā: may she be born, may she arise
    • yuvate: in youth, in the prime of life
    • patim: a husband, a protector
    • tuñjāte: may she attain, may she achieve
    • vṛṣṇyam: strength, vigor
    • payaḥ: milk, nourishment
    • paridāya: surrounding, enveloping
    • rasaṁ: essence, flavor
    • duhe: may she milk, may she extract
    • vittam: wealth, prosperity
    • me: my
    • asya: of this
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the heavens and the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the aspirations of a seeker, particularly in the context of relationships and prosperity.
    • The phrase "may she be born in youth" suggests a desire for vitality and strength in a partner, emphasizing the importance of youthfulness in relationships.
    • The mention of "may she attain strength" reflects the importance of personal growth and empowerment in a partnership.
    • The reference to "milk" symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, indicating the nurturing aspect of relationships.
    • Overall, the mantra encapsulates the desire for a harmonious and prosperous union, where both partners support and uplift each other.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern relationships, this mantra can be seen in the way partners encourage each other to pursue their goals and aspirations, fostering a supportive environment.
    • In a professional context, it reflects the importance of collaboration and mutual support among colleagues to achieve common goals.
    • In family dynamics, it highlights the nurturing role of parents in providing for their children’s growth and development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of mutual support, growth, and prosperity in relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing connections, whether in personal, professional, or familial contexts.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate meaningful relationships that contribute to their overall well-being and success.
