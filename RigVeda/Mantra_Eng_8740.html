यस्य न इन्द्रः पिबाद यस्य मरुतो यस्य वार्यमणā भगः |आ येन मित्रावरुणा करामह एन्द्रम अवसे महे ||
yasya na indraḥ pibād yasya maruto yasya vāryamaṇā bhagaḥ | ā yena mitrāvaruṇā karāmaha endram avase mahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • na: not
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • pibād: drinks
    • yasya: of whom
    • maruto: the Maruts (storm deities)
    • yasya: of whom
    • vāryamaṇā: the powerful
    • bhagaḥ: fortune or prosperity
    • ā: by
    • yena: by whom
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Mitra and Varuna (deities of friendship and cosmic order)
    • karāmaha: we perform
    • endram: Indra
    • avase: we invoke
    • mahe: great
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine powers and the importance of invoking the blessings of the gods.
    • It emphasizes that without the favor of Indra, the Maruts, and the blessings of Mitra and Varuna, one cannot achieve true prosperity and success.
    • The mantra is a call to the divine for assistance and recognition of their roles in the cosmic order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder that success is not solely dependent on individual effort but also on the support and goodwill of others, akin to how businesses often rely on partnerships and collaborations.
    • In personal life, it reflects the importance of community and relationships, suggesting that one’s fortune is intertwined with the well-being of others.
    • In environmental contexts, it can be interpreted as a call to respect and honor nature (represented by the Maruts) for sustainable living and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support.
    • It teaches us to acknowledge the forces beyond our control that contribute to our success and well-being.
    • In daily life, it encourages humility, gratitude, and the recognition of the importance of community and divine blessings in achieving our goals.
