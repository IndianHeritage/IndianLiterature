एतायामोप गव्यन्त इन्द्रं अस्माकं सु प्रमतिं वावृद्धाति |अनामृतः कुविदाद अस्य रायो गवां केतम परम आवर्जते नः ||
etāyāmopa gavyanta indram asmākaṁ su pramatiṁ vāvṛdhāti | anāmṛṇaḥ kuvid ād asya rāyo gavāṁ ketam param āvarjate naḥ ||
  • etāyāmopa gavyanta: This phrase can be translated as "In this world, we seek the divine." It emphasizes the importance of seeking higher truths and divine guidance in our lives.
  • indram asmākaṁ: This means "Indra, our lord." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens. This line signifies the reverence for divine power and the need for its blessings.
  • su pramatiṁ vāvṛdhāti: Translated as "may he increase our wisdom." This part of the mantra highlights the aspiration for enhanced understanding and intelligence, which is crucial for making informed decisions in life.
  • anāmṛṇaḥ kuvid: This can be interpreted as "the one who is immortal and wise." It suggests the pursuit of eternal knowledge and wisdom, which transcends the physical realm.
  • ād asya rāyo: This translates to "of this wealth." It refers to the abundance and prosperity that one seeks in life, both materially and spiritually.
  • gavāṁ ketam: Meaning "the essence of cows," this phrase symbolizes nourishment and sustenance. In Vedic culture, cows are revered as symbols of wealth and fertility, representing the importance of nurturing and sustaining life.
  • param āvarjate naḥ: This translates to "may it protect us." It expresses a desire for divine protection and guidance in our endeavors.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual aspiration for wisdom, divine guidance, and protection. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can recite this mantra to seek clarity and wisdom in their decisions.
  • In community gatherings or rituals, it can be used to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and well-being.
  • In times of uncertainty, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek higher guidance and remain grounded in one's values.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious relationship with the divine, the pursuit of wisdom, and the nurturing of life, which are essential for a fulfilling existence.
