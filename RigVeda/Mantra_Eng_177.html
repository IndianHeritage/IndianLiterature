सोमानं स्वराणं कृतुहि ब्रह्मणस पते |कक्षीवन्तं य औशिजः ||
somānaṁ svaraṇaṁ kṛṇuhi brahmaṇas pate | kakṣīvantaṁ ya auśijaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "somānaṁ svaraṇaṁ kṛṇuhi brahmaṇas pate | kakṣīvantaṁ ya auśijaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somānaṁ: Refers to the "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • svaraṇaṁ: Means "to shine" or "to illuminate," indicating clarity, brilliance, or enlightenment.
    • kṛṇuhi: A verb meaning "to make" or "to create," suggesting an action of bringing something into existence.
    • brahmaṇas: Refers to "Brahman," the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy.
    • pate: Means "lord" or "master," indicating a position of authority or divinity.
    • kakṣīvantaṁ: Refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," suggesting strength and resilience.
    • ya: Means "who," indicating a subject or a person being referred to.
    • auśijaḥ: Refers to "the wise" or "the learned," indicating knowledge and wisdom.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the creation of divine inspiration and enlightenment.
    • It invokes the essence of the sacred soma, which is often associated with spiritual awakening and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and wisdom in achieving one's goals and understanding the deeper truths of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking inspiration for creative projects or innovative solutions. For instance, a team brainstorming session can benefit from invoking the qualities of clarity and strength to generate impactful ideas.
    • In personal development, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation to cultivate inner strength and wisdom, helping them navigate life's challenges with resilience.
    • In educational contexts, students can use the essence of this mantra to inspire their pursuit of knowledge, encouraging them to seek enlightenment and understanding in their studies.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine inspiration and the cultivation of strength and wisdom.
    • It serves as a reminder that enlightenment and resilience are essential in both personal and professional realms.
    • By integrating the principles of this mantra into daily life, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their inner selves and the universe, leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
