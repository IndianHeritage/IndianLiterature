उग्रा इव प्रवहन्तः समायमुः साकं युक्ता वृषणो बिभ्रतो धुरः |यच छ्वसन्तो जाग्रसाना अराविषुः श्रिण्व एषाम् प्रोथतो अरवताम् इव ||
ugrā iva pravahantaḥ samāyamuḥ sākaṁ yuktā vṛṣaṇo bibhrato dhuraḥ | yac chvasanto jagrasānā arāviṣuḥ śṛṇva eṣām prothatho arvatām iva ||
  • ugrā iva pravahantaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "like powerful forces flowing." The term "ugrā" signifies strength or intensity, while "pravahantaḥ" refers to something that is flowing or moving forward. In real life, this can be likened to the unstoppable force of a river or a strong current that shapes the landscape around it.
  • samāyamuḥ sākaṁ yuktā: This means "together they are united." The word "samāyamuḥ" indicates a coming together or gathering, and "sākaṁ yuktā" suggests being joined or connected. This can be seen in teamwork or community efforts where individuals unite for a common goal, such as a group of volunteers working together to clean a park.
  • vṛṣaṇo bibhrato dhuraḥ: This translates to "the strong bear the burden." "Vṛṣaṇo" refers to the strong or powerful, while "bibhrato dhuraḥ" means to carry or bear a load. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by leaders or caregivers who take on responsibilities to support others, such as a parent working hard to provide for their family.
  • yac chvasanto jagrasānā: This phrase means "those who are awake and breathing." "Chvasanto" refers to breathing or living beings, and "jagrasānā" indicates being alert or aware. This can be related to individuals who are conscious of their surroundings and actively engaged in life, such as a student who is attentive in class and eager to learn.
  • arāviṣuḥ śṛṇva: This translates to "listening to the call." "Arāviṣuḥ" refers to those who are responsive or attentive, while "śṛṇva" means to hear or listen. This can be illustrated by someone who is open to feedback and willing to learn from others, like a mentor who listens to their mentee's concerns and provides guidance.
  • eṣām prothatho arvatām iva: This means "like the strong ones among them." "Eṣām" refers to "of them," "prothatho" means "among," and "arvatām" signifies strength or power. This can be seen in a community where certain individuals rise to leadership roles, guiding others with their strength and wisdom, such as a coach leading a sports team to victory.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, unity, and awareness in life. It highlights how powerful forces can come together to bear burdens and respond to the needs of the community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to teamwork, leadership, and the importance of being attentive and responsive to the world around us. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to recognize their strength and the strength of unity, encouraging them to work together for a common purpose and to remain aware and engaged in their lives and communities.
