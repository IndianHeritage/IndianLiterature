यān rāye martān सुषूदो अग्ने ते स्याम मघवानो वयं च |छायेव विश्वं भुवनं सिसक्ष्य आपप्रिवान रोदसी अन्तरिक्षम् ||
yān rāye martān suṣūdo agne te syāma maghavāno vayaṁ ca | chāyeva viśvam bhuvanaṁ sisakṣy āpaprivān rodasī antarikṣam ||
  • yān rāye martān suṣūdo agne te syāma maghavāno vayaṁ ca
    • yān rāye: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who seek wealth" or "those who desire prosperity." It emphasizes the human pursuit of material and spiritual wealth.
    • martān: Refers to "mortals" or "human beings," highlighting the transient nature of life and the importance of our actions during our lifetime.
    • suṣūdo: This term can mean "well-being" or "prosperity," suggesting that the mantra is invoking blessings for a fulfilling life.
    • agne: This word means "O Agni," referring to the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine spark within us.
    • te syāma: Translates to "may we be," indicating a desire for connection or alignment with the divine or higher powers.
    • maghavāno: This term refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty," often associated with divine forces that can bestow blessings and strength.
    • vayaṁ ca: Meaning "and we," it emphasizes the collective aspect of humanity in seeking blessings and prosperity.
  • chāyeva viśvam bhuvanaṁ sisakṣy
    • chāyeva: This means "like a shadow," suggesting that the blessings and prosperity we seek are closely linked to our actions and intentions.
    • viśvam bhuvanaṁ: Translates to "the entire world" or "all realms," indicating that the mantra encompasses all aspects of existence.
    • sisakṣy: This term can be interpreted as "we shall attain" or "we shall achieve," reinforcing the idea of striving for success and fulfillment.
  • āpaprivān rodasī antarikṣam
    • āpaprivān: This word means "having obtained" or "having achieved," suggesting a sense of accomplishment and fulfillment.
    • rodasī: Refers to "the earth" or "the world," emphasizing our connection to the physical realm and the importance of grounding our aspirations.
    • antarikṣam: This term means "the sky" or "the space between," symbolizing the spiritual realm and the connection between the earthly and the divine.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of human aspiration for prosperity, well-being, and connection with the divine. It serves as a reminder that our pursuits in life should be aligned with higher principles and that our actions have a direct impact on our reality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek not just material wealth but also spiritual growth and well-being.
  • In community settings, it emphasizes the importance of collective efforts in achieving prosperity and harmony.
  • In environmental contexts, it reminds us of our connection to the earth and the sky, urging us to act responsibly towards nature.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a balanced and fulfilling life, harmonizing our desires with the greater good of the world around us.
