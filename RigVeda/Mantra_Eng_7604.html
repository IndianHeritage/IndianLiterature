तं इन्द्रं जोहवीमि मगवाणं उग्रं सत्रा दधानं अप्रतिष्कुतं शवांसि |मंहिष्ठो गीरभिर आ च यज्ञियो ववर्तद राय नो विश्वा सुपथा कृणोतु वज्री ||
tam indraṁ johavīmi maghavānam ugraṁ satrā dadhānam apratiṣkutaṁ śavāṁsi | maṁhiṣṭho gīrbhir ā ca yajñiyo vavartad rāye no viśvā supathā kṛṇotu vajrī ||
  • tam indraṁ johavīmi: This phrase translates to "I invoke that Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and support in challenging situations, much like calling upon a leader or mentor for guidance.
  • maghavānam ugraṁ: This means "the powerful Maghavan." Maghavan is another name for Indra, emphasizing his might and ability to bestow wealth and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking abundance in one's life, whether it be in terms of financial stability or personal growth.
  • satrā dadhānam apratiṣkutaṁ: This translates to "who establishes the sacrifice without hindrance." This can be interpreted as the importance of dedication and commitment in one's endeavors. In everyday life, this could refer to the need for perseverance in achieving goals, such as completing a project at work or maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
  • śavāṁsi: This word means "the dead" or "the lifeless." It can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges that may seem insurmountable. In real life, this could relate to facing fears or past traumas and finding ways to move forward.
  • maṁhiṣṭho gīrbhir ā ca: This translates to "the best among the songs." This phrase highlights the power of words and communication. In practical terms, it can signify the importance of effective communication in relationships, whether personal or professional, and how it can lead to better understanding and collaboration.
  • yajñiyo vavartad: This means "may the sacrifices be fruitful." It emphasizes the idea that efforts and sacrifices made in life should yield positive results. In real life, this can be seen in the context of hard work leading to success, such as studying diligently for exams or investing time in a relationship.
  • rāye no viśvā supathā kṛṇotu vajrī: This translates to "may the all-powerful one create for us a path of prosperity." This phrase encapsulates the desire for guidance and support in achieving a prosperous life. In practical terms, it can relate to seeking opportunities for growth, whether in career, education, or personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support, perseverance, effective communication, and the pursuit of prosperity in our lives. It serves as a reminder that through dedication and the right intentions, we can overcome challenges and achieve our goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance in difficult times, work diligently towards their aspirations, and foster positive relationships that lead to mutual growth and success.
