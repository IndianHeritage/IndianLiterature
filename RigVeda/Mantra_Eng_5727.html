शुश्रुवांसा चिद अश्विनां पुरूण्य अभि ब्रह्माणि चक्षाथे ऋषीणाम् |प्रति प्र यातं वरम् आ जनायास्मे वाम अस्तु सुमतिश्च निष्ठा ||
śuśruvāṁsā cid aśvinā purūṇy abhi brahmāṇi cakṣāthe ṛṣīṇām | prati pra yātaṁ varam ā janāyāsme vām astu sumatiś caniṣṭhā ||
  • śuśruvāṁsā: This term refers to those who are attentive or who listen well. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who are open to learning and understanding, such as students or professionals who actively seek knowledge.
  • cid: This word means consciousness or awareness. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of one's surroundings and the knowledge one possesses. For example, a person who practices mindfulness is often more aware of their thoughts and feelings, leading to better decision-making.
  • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the importance of good health and well-being in our lives. For instance, modern medicine and holistic health practices aim to embody the qualities of the Aśvins by promoting physical and mental wellness.
  • purūṇy: This term means 'ancient' or 'of the past.' It suggests the value of tradition and the wisdom that comes from history. In contemporary society, many cultures emphasize the importance of learning from past experiences to avoid repeating mistakes.
  • abhi: This word can mean 'towards' or 'in the direction of.' It indicates a movement or intention towards something. In a practical sense, this can be seen in goal-setting, where individuals strive towards their aspirations.
  • brahmāṇi: This refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe. In real life, this can be reflected in the idea of community and the importance of working together for a common good.
  • cakṣāthe: This term means 'with vision' or 'with insight.' It highlights the importance of having clarity and understanding in our actions. For example, leaders who possess vision can guide their teams effectively towards achieving their goals.
  • ṛṣīṇām: This refers to sages or seers, individuals who possess deep knowledge and insight. In modern contexts, this can be compared to mentors or thought leaders who guide others through their wisdom.
  • prati pra yātaṁ: This phrase means 'may it come to us' or 'may it be directed towards us.' It expresses a desire for blessings or positive outcomes. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of setting intentions or prayers for success and well-being.
  • varam: This means 'boon' or 'blessing.' It signifies the importance of seeking and valuing the good things in life. For instance, people often express gratitude for the blessings they receive, which can enhance their overall happiness.
  • ā janāyāsme: This phrase translates to 'may it be for us' or 'may it be granted to us.' It reflects a collective aspiration for well-being and prosperity. In community settings, this can be seen in group prayers or collective efforts to achieve common goals.
  • vām: This means 'your' or 'of you.' It emphasizes the connection between individuals and the divine or higher powers. In real life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the influence of external forces in our lives.
  • astu: This word means 'let it be' or 'may it be so.' It conveys a sense of acceptance and surrender to the universe. In practice, this can be related to the concept of letting go and trusting the process in life.
  • sumatiś: This term means 'good thought' or 'wisdom.' It highlights the importance of cultivating positive thoughts and intentions. In real life, this can be seen in practices like positive affirmations and mindfulness, which promote mental well-being.
  • caniṣṭhā: This means 'the best' or 'the highest.' It signifies the pursuit of excellence and the highest ideals in life. In contemporary contexts, this can be reflected in striving for personal and professional growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awareness, health, tradition, and the pursuit of wisdom. It encourages individuals to seek blessings and strive for excellence while being mindful of their thoughts and actions. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and community building, fostering a holistic approach to well-being and success.
