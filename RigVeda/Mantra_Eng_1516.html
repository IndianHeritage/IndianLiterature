युवोर इत्ताधि सद्मस्व अपश्याम हिरण्ययम् |धीभिः चन मनसा स्वेभिर अक्षभिः सोमस्य स्वेभिर अक्षभिः ||
yuvor itthādhi sadmasv apaśyāma hiraṇyayam | dhībhiś cana manasā svebhir akṣabhiḥ somasya svebhir akṣabhiḥ ||
  • yuvor itthādhi sadmasv apaśyāma hiraṇyayam: This phrase can be translated to "May we see the golden abode of the young ones." Here, "yuvor" refers to the young or youthful, "itthādhi" means to see or perceive, "sadmasv" refers to a dwelling or abode, and "hiraṇyayam" signifies something golden or precious. This line emphasizes the importance of recognizing and valuing the potential and vitality of youth.
  • dhībhiś cana manasā: This translates to "with our intellect and mind." "Dhībhiś" refers to wisdom or intellect, while "manasā" means with the mind. This part of the mantra suggests that we should approach our understanding and perception with wisdom and clarity of thought.
  • svebhir akṣabhiḥ somasya svebhir akṣabhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with our own eyes, we perceive the essence of the moon." "Akṣabhiḥ" refers to the eyes or vision, "svebhir" means our own, and "somasya" relates to the moon or the essence of life. This line highlights the importance of personal experience and perception in understanding the deeper truths of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, wisdom, and personal perception. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, mentorship, and personal growth. Recognizing the potential in the younger generation and guiding them with wisdom can lead to a brighter future. Additionally, it encourages individuals to seek their own understanding of life’s truths rather than relying solely on external sources. This mantra serves as a reminder to value youth, think critically, and perceive the world through our own experiences, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
