आ सवं सवितुर यथा भागस्येव भुजिं हुवे |अग्निं समुद्रवाससम् ||
ā savaṁ savitur yathā bhagasyeva bhujiṁ huve | agniṁ samudravāsasam ||
  • Mantra: "ā savaṁ savitur yathā bhagasyeva bhujiṁ huve | agniṁ samudravāsasam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "I invoke the divine essence of all that is, just as the sun's rays illuminate the world. I call upon Agni, the fire that resides in the ocean."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā: A prefix meaning "from" or "out of."
    • savaṁ: Refers to "all" or "everything."
    • savitur: Pertaining to "Savitar," the sun god, symbolizing light and energy.
    • yathā: Means "as" or "like."
    • bhagasyeva: "Bhaga" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "eva" means "indeed" or "certainly."
    • bhujiṁ: Refers to "enjoyment" or "experience."
    • huve: Means "I invoke" or "I call upon."
    • agniṁ: Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing transformation and energy.
    • samudravāsasam: "Samudra" means "ocean," and "vāsasam" means "dwelling" or "residing."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The invocation of the sun's energy can be likened to how we harness solar power in modern technology, emphasizing the importance of light and energy in our daily lives.
    • Just as the sun nourishes all living beings, we can draw parallels to how education and knowledge illuminate our paths, guiding us toward prosperity and fulfillment.
    • Agni, representing transformation, can be seen in the process of cooking food, where raw ingredients are transformed into nourishing meals, symbolizing the importance of change and growth in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine energy that sustains life.
    • It serves as a reminder to invoke and appreciate the forces of nature, such as the sun and fire, which play crucial roles in our existence.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek enlightenment, embrace transformation, and recognize the abundance around us, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the environment and each other.
