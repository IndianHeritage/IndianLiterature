मन्त्र: विश्वाः पृथना अभिभूतारं नरं सजूस तातक्षुर इन्द्रं जजनुश च राजसे | कृत्वा वरिष्टं वर आमुरिम उतोग्रम ओजिष्टं तवसं तरस्विनम् ||
viśvāḥ pṛtanā abhibhūtaraṁ naraṁ sajūs tatakṣur indraṁ jajanuś ca rājase | kratvā variṣṭhaṁ vara āmurim utogram ojiṣṭhaṁ tavasaṁ tarasvinam ||
  • viśvāḥ: This word translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • pṛtanā: This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength in various forms, whether physical, mental, or spiritual.
  • abhibhūtaraṁ: This term can be understood as "overcoming" or "conquering." It suggests the idea of triumphing over challenges and adversities.
  • naraṁ: This translates to "man" or "human." It highlights the significance of humanity in the context of strength and overcoming obstacles.
  • sajūs: This word means "with vigor" or "with energy." It indicates the necessity of enthusiasm and vitality in our endeavors.
  • tatakṣur: This can be interpreted as "to be wise" or "to have insight." It points to the importance of wisdom in decision-making and actions.
  • indraṁ: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, it symbolizes leadership, power, and authority.
  • jajanuś: This means "born" or "created." It signifies the process of emergence and the potential for growth and development.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking ideas together.
  • rājase: This translates to "royal" or "of the king." It emphasizes nobility and the qualities associated with leadership.
  • kratvā: This means "with effort" or "with determination." It highlights the importance of hard work and perseverance.
  • variṣṭhaṁ: This translates to "the best" or "the highest." It signifies the pursuit of excellence in all endeavors.
  • vara: This means "boon" or "blessing." It indicates the importance of seeking and receiving blessings in life.
  • āmurim: This can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • utogram: This means "the highest" or "the supreme." It signifies the ultimate goal or aspiration.
  • ojiṣṭhaṁ: This translates to "the most energetic" or "the most vigorous." It emphasizes the need for vitality in our pursuits.
  • tavasaṁ: This means "through your power" or "by your strength." It indicates reliance on divine or inner strength.
  • tarasvinam: This translates to "swift" or "quick." It signifies the importance of agility and responsiveness in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and the pursuit of excellence. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of overcoming challenges with vigor and determination. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, leadership, and teamwork. For instance, in a corporate setting, a leader can embody the qualities of Indra by inspiring their team to overcome obstacles and strive for excellence. The mantra serves as a reminder that with strength, wisdom, and a collective effort, individuals can achieve great heights and contribute positively to society.
