शिशुर न जातोऽव चक्रदद वने स्वर्ण यद वाज्य अरुषः सिषासति |दिवो रेतसां सचते पयोवृद्धा तं ईमहे सुमती शर्मा सप्रथः ||
śiśur na jāto 'va cakradad vane sva1r yad vājy aruṣaḥ siṣāsati | divo retasā sacate payovṛdhā tam īmahe sumatī śarma saprathaḥ ||
  • śiśur na jāto 'va cakradad vane sva1r: This phrase can be translated to "the child is not born like a wheel in the forest." Here, "śiśur" refers to a child, "na jāto" means "not born," and "cakradad" suggests a wheel, symbolizing something that is cyclic or mechanical. The imagery of a forest indicates a natural setting, contrasting with the mechanical nature of a wheel.
  • yad vājy aruṣaḥ siṣāsati: This part translates to "whatever is born, the red one (or the one with energy) is nourished." "Yad" means "whatever," "vājy" can refer to something that is born or produced, and "aruṣaḥ" signifies a vibrant or energetic quality. This suggests that life is vibrant and full of energy, and it is essential for growth and nourishment.
  • divo retasā sacate payovṛdhā: This translates to "the essence of the heavens is nourished by the waters." "Divo" refers to the heavens, "retasā" means essence or energy, and "payovṛdhā" signifies the nourishing quality of water. This highlights the interconnectedness of celestial and earthly elements, emphasizing the importance of water in sustaining life.
  • tam īmahe sumatī śarma saprathaḥ: This can be interpreted as "we seek that wisdom which brings us peace and prosperity." "Tam" means "that," "īmahe" translates to "we seek," "sumatī" refers to wisdom or good thoughts, and "śarma saprathaḥ" signifies peace and prosperity. This emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom as a means to achieve a harmonious and prosperous life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, growth, and the pursuit of wisdom. It reflects the natural cycle of birth and nourishment, the importance of energy and vitality, and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
  • Real-life Application: In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:
    • Parenting: The idea that a child is not born mechanically but is a vibrant being emphasizes the importance of nurturing and supporting a child's natural growth and development.
    • Environmental Awareness: The connection between the heavens and water highlights the need for environmental conservation. Just as water nourishes life, we must protect our natural resources to sustain future generations.
    • Pursuit of Knowledge: The call to seek wisdom can be applied in education and personal development. By valuing knowledge and good thoughts, individuals can create a more peaceful and prosperous society.
