प्र वः शुक्राय भानवे भरध्वं हव्यं मतीं चाग्नये सुपूतम् |यो दैव्याणि मानवश जनूंष्य अन्तर विश्वानि विद्मना जिगाति ||
pra vaḥ śukrāya bhānave bharadhvaṁ havyam matiṁ cāgnaye supūtam | yo daivyāni mānuṣā janūṁṣy antar viśvāni vidmanā jigāti ||
  • pra vaḥ - This phrase can be interpreted as "let us invoke" or "let us call forth." It signifies the act of inviting divine energies or blessings into our lives.
  • śukrāya - This word means "to the bright" or "to the pure." It represents clarity, wisdom, and enlightenment, suggesting a pursuit of knowledge and purity in thought and action.
  • bhānave - This term translates to "to the shining one" or "to the radiant." It can refer to the sun or a divine entity that brings light and guidance.
  • bharadhvaṁ - This means "may you carry" or "may you bear." It implies a sense of responsibility and the act of taking on duties or burdens with grace.
  • havyam - This word refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving.
  • matiṁ - This translates to "intellect" or "thought." It highlights the significance of wisdom and understanding in our actions.
  • cāgnaye - Meaning "to Agni," this refers to the fire god, symbolizing transformation, purification, and the divine spark within us.
  • supūtam - This means "well-prepared" or "well-cooked," indicating the importance of preparation and quality in our offerings and actions.
  • yo - This word means "who." It introduces a subject, referring to those who are worthy or deserving.
  • daivyāni - This translates to "divine" or "heavenly," referring to the qualities or beings that are celestial in nature.
  • mānuṣā - Meaning "human," it emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, suggesting that we are part of a larger cosmic order.
  • janūṁṣy - This word means "bodies" or "forms," indicating the physical existence of beings.
  • antar - This translates to "within" or "inside," suggesting introspection and the exploration of inner truths.
  • viśvāni - Meaning "all" or "universal," it refers to the entirety of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vidmanā - This means "knowing" or "with knowledge," emphasizing the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.
  • jigāti - This translates to "moves" or "travels," indicating the dynamic nature of existence and the journey of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings, emphasizing the importance of purity, wisdom, and responsibility in our actions. It serves as a reminder that our offerings, both material and spiritual, should be well-prepared and made with intention. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or even in daily decision-making. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a harmonious existence, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the universe around them.
