दूराद इन्द्रं अनयन् आ सुतेन तिरो वैशन्तम् अति पान्तम् उग्रम् |पाशद्युम्नस्य वायतस्य सोमात सुताद इन्द्रो 'वृणीतā वसिष्ठान् ||
dūrād indram anayann ā sutena tiro vaiśantam ati pāntam ugram | pāśadyumnasya vāyatasya somāt sutād indro 'vṛṇītā vasiṣṭhān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūrād: From afar
    • indram: Indra (the king of gods)
    • anayann: Bringing
    • ā: With
    • sutena: The son (or offspring)
    • tiro: Beyond
    • vaiśantam: The one who is powerful
    • ati: Extremely
    • pāntam: The one who is protected
    • ugram: Fierce
    • pāśadyumnasya: Of the one who is strong
    • vāyatasya: Of the wind
    • somāt: From the moon (or nectar)
    • sutād: From the son
    • indro: Indra
    • 'vṛṇītā: May choose
    • vasiṣṭhān: The best or the most excellent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing his power and protective nature.
    • The phrase "from afar" suggests that divine assistance can be sought even from a distance, indicating the omnipresence of divine energy.
    • Indra is depicted as a fierce protector, capable of overcoming challenges and providing strength to his devotees.
    • The mention of "the son" and "the moon" symbolizes the connection between the divine and the earthly, highlighting the importance of lineage and nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek strength and guidance from a higher power, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for protection and support.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to invoke blessings from deities, reflecting the belief in divine intervention in human affairs.
    • The concept of lineage and heritage is significant in many societies, where individuals honor their ancestors and seek to embody their virtues, akin to the reference to "the son" in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine protection and strength in challenging times.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly, reminding us of the support we can receive from higher powers.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in moments of crisis, encouraging individuals to seek help and guidance from their faith or spiritual beliefs.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the strength that can be drawn from both divine sources and one's own heritage.
