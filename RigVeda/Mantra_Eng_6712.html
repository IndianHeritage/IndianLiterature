अध्वर्यव आ तु हि षिञ्च सोमं वीराय शिप्रिणे |भरā सुतस्य पीतये ||
adhvaryav ā tu hi ṣiñca somaṁ vīrāya śipriṇe | bharā sutasya pītaye ||
  • adhvaryav: This term refers to the priest or the officiant in a Vedic ritual, particularly in the context of the Soma sacrifice. The adhvaryu is responsible for the physical aspects of the ritual, ensuring that everything is performed correctly.
  • ā tu hi: This phrase can be translated as "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the importance of the action that follows.
  • ṣiñca: This verb means "to pour" or "to sprinkle." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the act of pouring the Soma, a sacred drink, which is believed to confer immortality and divine qualities.
  • somaṁ: Soma is both a deity and a sacred drink in Vedic tradition. It is often associated with the moon and is considered a source of inspiration, strength, and immortality.
  • vīrāya: This term means "for the hero" or "to the brave one." It signifies the qualities of valor and strength, suggesting that the offerings made are intended to empower the recipient.
  • śipriṇe: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving." It indicates that the offerings are made to someone who has earned them through their deeds or qualities.
  • bharā: This verb means "to fill" or "to bear." It implies the act of providing or bestowing something valuable.
  • sutasya: This term refers to "the son" or "the offspring." In this context, it may symbolize the continuation of lineage or the next generation.
  • pītaye: This word means "for drinking" or "to be consumed." It indicates that the offerings are meant to be enjoyed or utilized by the recipient.

In summary, this mantra is a call to the adhvaryu to pour the sacred Soma for the hero who is deserving, emphasizing the importance of strength, valor, and the continuation of lineage. The act of pouring Soma is not just a ritualistic action; it symbolizes the bestowing of divine qualities and blessings upon those who are worthy.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring those who exhibit bravery and virtue in society. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as:
    • Leadership: Leaders who demonstrate courage and integrity can inspire others and create a positive impact in their communities.
    • Mentorship: Mentors who guide and support the next generation embody the qualities of the "hero" and help in the growth of future leaders.
    • Celebrating Achievements: Recognizing and celebrating the accomplishments of individuals who contribute positively to society reinforces the values of bravery and virtue.
  • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, virtue, and the responsibility to uplift and honor those who embody these qualities in our lives.
