इमां ऊ नु कवितमस्य मायाम महीं देवस्य नकीर आ दधर्षा |एकं यद उद्ना न पृणन्त्य एनीर आसिञ्चन्तीर अवनयः समुद्रम ||
imām ū nu kavitamasya māyām mahīṁ devasya nakir ā dadharṣa | ekaṁ yad udnā na pṛṇanty enīr āsiñcantīr avanayaḥ samudram ||
  • imām ū nu kavitamasya māyām: This phrase can be translated as "this is the illusion of the greatest poet." Here, "kavitama" refers to the supreme or greatest poet, which can symbolize the divine or the ultimate creator. In real life, we often encounter situations where creativity and inspiration seem to come from a higher source, such as in art, music, or literature.
  • mahīṁ devasya nakir ā dadharṣa: This translates to "the great earth, which the divine does not forsake." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earth. In our daily lives, we can see this in the way nature sustains us and how we are interconnected with the environment. For example, the importance of environmental conservation reflects this divine connection.
  • ekaṁ yad udnā na pṛṇanty: This means "the one that does not fill the void." It suggests that there is a singular essence or truth that remains unfulfilled or incomplete. In real life, this can relate to the human experience of seeking purpose or fulfillment, often through relationships, careers, or spiritual journeys.
  • enīr āsiñcantīr avanayaḥ samudram: This translates to "the waters that flow towards the ocean." It symbolizes the journey of life and the collective movement towards a greater goal or unity. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals and communities work together towards common objectives, such as social justice or environmental sustainability.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between the divine, nature, and human experience. It signifies the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all things and the pursuit of a higher purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental activism, artistic expression, and personal growth. By understanding and embracing these concepts, we can foster a deeper connection with the world around us and strive for a more harmonious existence.
