विश्वश्रुष्टिः सखीयते रयिर इव श्रवस्यते | अदब्धो होतā नि षदद इḻस पदे परिवीत इḻस पदे ||
viśvaśruṣṭiḥ sakhīyate rayir iva śravasyate | adabdho hotā ni ṣadad iḻas pade parivīta iḻas pade ||
  • viśvaśruṣṭiḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "viśva" meaning "universal" or "all-encompassing," and "śruṣṭiḥ" meaning "creation" or "manifestation." Together, it refers to the universal creation or the act of bringing forth all that exists.
  • sakhīyate: This word translates to "friendship" or "companionship." It implies a sense of unity and support among beings, suggesting that creation is not just an isolated act but one that fosters relationships.
  • rayir: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the positive outcomes that arise from the universal creation and companionship.
  • iva: This word translates to "like" or "as," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • śravasyate: This term means "to be praised" or "to be renowned." It suggests that the wealth and prosperity resulting from universal creation and friendship are worthy of recognition.
  • adabdho: This word means "unhindered" or "unobstructed." It implies that the process of creation and the resulting relationships are free from obstacles.
  • hotā: This term refers to a "priest" or "one who invokes." It signifies the role of a mediator or facilitator in the act of creation and the establishment of relationships.
  • ni: This is a prefix that can denote negation or emphasis, often used to strengthen the meaning of the word it precedes.
  • ṣadad: This term means "to sit" or "to dwell." It indicates a state of being or existence.
  • iḻas pade: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the place of the sacred" or "in the realm of the divine." It suggests a connection to higher principles or spiritual dimensions.
  • parivīta: This word means "surrounded" or "enveloped." It indicates a protective or nurturing environment.
  • iḻas pade: Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the idea of being in a sacred or divine space.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all creation, the importance of friendship and support in fostering prosperity, and the unhindered nature of this process. In real life, this can be applied to the significance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals. Just as the universe thrives on relationships and mutual support, individuals can find strength and success through cooperation and unity. This mantra serves as a reminder that our actions and relationships contribute to a greater whole, encouraging us to cultivate harmony and abundance in our lives and communities.
