दण्डा इवेद गोजनास आसन परिच्छिन्ना भारताऽर्भकासः |अभवच च पुरेतā वसिष्ठ ād इति तृत्त्सूनां विश्व अप्रथन्त ||
daṇḍā ived goajanāsa āsan paricchinnā bharatā arbhakāsaḥ | abhavac ca puraetā vasiṣṭha ād it tṛtsūnāṁ viśo aprathanta ||
  • daṇḍā: This word translates to "staff" or "rod," symbolizing authority, discipline, and guidance. In real life, a teacher or a leader often uses their authority to guide others, much like a staff that provides support.
  • ived: This can be interpreted as "to know" or "to understand." Knowledge is crucial in our daily lives, whether in education, professional settings, or personal growth.
  • goajanāsa: This term refers to "the people" or "the community." It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being and harmony within society, akin to how communities work together for common goals.
  • āsan: Meaning "to sit" or "to reside," this word signifies stability and presence. In a real-life context, it can relate to finding a place of comfort and security, such as a home or a community gathering.
  • paricchinnā: This translates to "limited" or "restricted." It can refer to the boundaries we set in our lives, whether they are personal, social, or professional, highlighting the importance of knowing our limits.
  • bharatā: This word can be associated with "Bharata," which is often linked to the Indian subcontinent. It signifies cultural identity and heritage, reminding us of our roots and the values we carry forward.
  • arbhaḳāsaḥ: This term refers to "young ones" or "children." It emphasizes the significance of nurturing the younger generation, as they are the future of society.
  • abhavac: Meaning "to become" or "to manifest," this word signifies transformation and growth. In life, we often undergo changes that shape our identities and paths.
  • ca: This simply means "and," connecting ideas and concepts together.
  • puraetā: This can be interpreted as "the ancient" or "the past." It reminds us to learn from history and the wisdom of our ancestors, which can guide our present and future decisions.
  • vasiṣṭha: This term refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." It encourages us to strive for excellence in our endeavors, whether in personal achievements or contributions to society.
  • ād: Meaning "from" or "out of," it indicates a source or origin, reminding us of the roots of our actions and beliefs.
  • it: This word means "this" or "that," pointing to specific ideas or concepts being discussed.
  • tṛtsūnāṁ: This can be interpreted as "of the three," which may refer to various triads in life, such as mind, body, and spirit, or past, present, and future.
  • viśo: This term means "of the community" or "of the people," reinforcing the idea of collective identity and shared experiences.
  • aprathanta: This translates to "not spread" or "not disseminated," which can signify the importance of preserving knowledge and traditions within a community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of authority, knowledge, community, and the nurturing of future generations. It highlights the importance of learning from the past while striving for excellence in the present. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings where teachers guide students, in communities where collective efforts lead to progress, and in families where the values of discipline and nurturing are passed down to children. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the responsibilities we hold towards each other and the future.
