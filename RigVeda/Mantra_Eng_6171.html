त्वामिद्वृत्रहन्तमजनासो वृत्कतबरिषः |हवन्ते वाजसातये ||
tvām id vṛtrahantama janāso vṛktabarhiṣaḥ | havante vājasātaye ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" or "to you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • id - This means "now," suggesting immediacy and relevance in the present moment.
  • vṛtrahantama - This compound word can be broken down into "vṛtra," which refers to a demon or obstacle, and "hantama," meaning "the one who kills" or "the slayer." Thus, it signifies the divine force that overcomes obstacles and challenges.
  • janāso - This word means "of the people" or "of the community," indicating a collective aspect of the mantra, emphasizing the importance of community in overcoming challenges.
  • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "with the well-prepared offerings" or "with the sacred grass," symbolizing the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • havante - This means "they who offer" or "those who invoke," referring to the practitioners or devotees who perform rituals.
  • vājasātaye - This translates to "for the purpose of strength" or "for the attainment of power," indicating the goal of the offerings and rituals is to gain strength and support from the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine force that helps overcome obstacles and challenges faced by the community. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and the role of rituals in seeking divine assistance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Challenges: When a community faces difficulties, such as natural disasters or social issues, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and collective action in overcoming these challenges.
  • Personal Struggles: Individuals can recite this mantra during times of personal hardship, invoking the strength to overcome obstacles in their lives, whether they be emotional, financial, or health-related.
  • Rituals and Offerings: The significance of rituals in this mantra highlights the importance of spiritual practices in daily life, encouraging individuals to engage in acts of devotion and gratitude, which can foster a sense of community and connection to the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in overcoming obstacles, emphasizing the importance of community, rituals, and the pursuit of strength. It serves as a powerful reminder that through collective effort and spiritual practice, individuals and communities can navigate the challenges of life with resilience and faith.
