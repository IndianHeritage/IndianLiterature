प्र सोमासो मदाच्युतः श्रवसे नो मघोनः |सुतā विदथे अक्रमुः ||
pra somāso madacyutaḥ śravase no maghonaḥ | sutā vidathe akramuḥ ||
  • pra somāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "the ones who are filled with soma." In Vedic tradition, soma is a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine qualities. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • madacyutaḥ: This term translates to "the one who is intoxicated with joy." It signifies a state of bliss and spiritual ecstasy, often associated with divine experiences or enlightenment.
  • śravase: This word means "to fame" or "to glory." It indicates the pursuit of recognition and honor, often in the context of spiritual or moral achievements.
  • no maghonaḥ: This phrase translates to "may we not be deprived of wealth." It reflects a desire for abundance and prosperity, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and emotional wealth.
  • sutā: This term refers to "the one who is well-prepared" or "the one who is skilled." It emphasizes the importance of preparation and skill in achieving one's goals.
  • vidathe: This word means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It signifies the importance of community and collective effort in achieving success.
  • akramuḥ: This term translates to "they who do not falter" or "they who are steadfast." It highlights the value of perseverance and resilience in the face of challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of joy, prosperity, and community. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual intoxication, preparation, and steadfastness in achieving one's goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to cultivate joy and resilience, ensuring they remain steadfast in their pursuits.
  • In professional settings, teamwork and community efforts can lead to greater achievements, reflecting the importance of collective success.
  • In spiritual practices, the pursuit of bliss and divine experiences can lead to a more fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to seek out their own 'soma' through meditation, rituals, or other spiritual practices.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of joy, community, and perseverance in the journey of life.
