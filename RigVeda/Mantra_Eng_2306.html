प्र हि क्रतुं वृहतो यं वनुथो राध्रस्य स्थो यजमानस्य चोदौ | इन्द्रासोमा युवं अस्मांं अविष्टम अस्मिन भयस्थे कृणुतम उ लोकम् ||
pra hi kratuṁ vṛhatho yaṁ vanutho radhrasya stho yajamānasya codau | indrāsomā yuvam asmām̐ aviṣṭam asmin bhayasthe kṛṇutam u lokam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Indra and Soma, you who are powerful and who inspire us, grant us strength and courage. May you protect us from fear and lead us to the world of prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra hi: A call for assistance or support.
    • kratuṁ: Refers to willpower or determination.
    • vṛhatho: Powerful or strong.
    • yaṁ: Who.
    • vanutho: To inspire or motivate.
    • radhrasya: Of the prosperous or wealthy.
    • stho: To stand or be established.
    • yajamānasya: Of the one who is performing the sacrifice or worship.
    • codau: To encourage or incite.
    • indrāsomā: Referring to the deities Indra and Soma.
    • yuvam: You both.
    • asmām̐: Us.
    • aviṣṭam: Unharmed or protected.
    • asmin: In this.
    • bhayasthe: From fear.
    • kṛṇutam: Make or create.
    • u lokam: The world or realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek strength and courage to overcome challenges, similar to the invocation of Indra and Soma for support.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may call upon their collective willpower and determination to achieve goals, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, people may chant this mantra to invoke protection and prosperity for their community, emphasizing the importance of unity and strength.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in overcoming fear and achieving prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of willpower, collective strength, and the need for protection in various aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in personal challenges, team dynamics, and community efforts, serving as a reminder to seek strength from both within and from higher powers.
