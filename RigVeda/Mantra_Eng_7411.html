तīv्राः सोमास आ गहि सुतासो मदयिष्णवः | पिबा दधृग यथोचिषे ||
tīvrāḥ somāsa ā gahi sutāso mādayiṣṇavaḥ | pibā dadhṛg yathociṣe ||
  • tīvrāḥ somāsa ā gahi: This phrase can be translated to "O potent Soma, come quickly." Here, "tīvrāḥ" means intense or powerful, and "somāsa" refers to the divine drink Soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration in Vedic literature. The call for Soma to come quickly signifies a yearning for divine energy and inspiration.
  • sutāso mādayiṣṇavaḥ: This translates to "the sons of the Soma, who are intoxicated with joy." "Sutāso" refers to those who partake of Soma, and "mādayiṣṇavaḥ" indicates a state of bliss or intoxication. This reflects the idea that consuming Soma leads to a heightened state of consciousness and joy, akin to the effects of certain experiences in life that elevate our spirits.
  • pibā dadhṛg yathociṣe: This means "drink, holding firmly, as you wish." "Pibā" is the imperative form of "to drink," suggesting an active engagement with the divine. "Dadhṛg" implies holding or grasping, and "yathociṣe" means "as you desire." This encourages individuals to embrace their desires and aspirations with determination and confidence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine inspiration and joy through the metaphor of Soma. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek out experiences that elevate our consciousness, whether through art, nature, or spiritual practices. It emphasizes the importance of actively pursuing our desires and holding onto them with conviction. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to embrace the divine energy around us and to find joy in our pursuits, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and inspired life.
