आ तु न इन्द्र कौशिक मन्दसानः सुतं पिब |नव्यं आयुः प्र सू तिर कृत्धी सहस्रसाम ऋषिम ||
ā tū na indra kauśika mandasānaḥ sutam piba | navyam āyuḥ pra sū tira kṛdhī sahasrasām ṛṣim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • tū: you
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • kauśika: of the Kausika lineage
    • mandasānaḥ: who is seated comfortably
    • sutam: the son
    • piba: drink
    • navyam: new
    • āyuḥ: life
    • pra: forward
    • sū: bring forth
    • tira: crossing
    • kṛdhī: make
    • sahasrasām: of the thousand
    • ṛṣim: sage
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to drink the new life that is being offered.
    • It emphasizes the importance of vitality and the blessings of longevity.
    • The reference to "sahasrasām ṛṣim" suggests the wisdom and guidance of a thousand sages, indicating the collective knowledge and support in seeking a prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the importance of seeking guidance from experienced individuals in our lives, such as mentors or elders, to navigate challenges.
    • It can also be seen in health practices where individuals seek new ways to enhance their vitality, such as through nutrition, exercise, and mindfulness.
    • In community settings, this mantra reflects the collective effort to support one another in achieving a prosperous and healthy life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in enhancing life and vitality.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, health, and community in achieving a fulfilling life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek guidance, embrace new beginnings, and foster a supportive environment for growth and longevity.
