सुतेसुतं न्योकसे बृहद् बृहतः एद् अरिः |इन्द्राय शूषं अर्चति ||
sute-sute nyokase bṛhad bṛhata ed ariḥ | indrāya śūṣam arcati ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sute-sute: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who have earned respect and admiration.
    • nyokase: This word suggests the act of offering or presenting something. It indicates a gesture of giving or dedicating something valuable.
    • bṛhad bṛhata: "Bṛhad" means "great" or "vast," and "bṛhata" can be understood as "the greater one." Together, they signify the concept of greatness or the supreme being, often associated with divine qualities.
    • ed: This term can be interpreted as "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
    • ariḥ: This word translates to "enemy" or "adversary." In a broader sense, it can refer to obstacles or challenges that one faces in life.
    • indrāya: This term refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
    • śūṣam: This word means "dry" or "thirsty," often used metaphorically to represent a state of need or lack.
    • arcati: This term means "to worship" or "to honor," indicating an act of reverence or devotion.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra can be interpreted as a call to honor and offer to the great divine (Indra) while recognizing the challenges (enemies) that one faces in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of devotion and respect towards those who embody greatness and strength, while also acknowledging the adversities that may arise.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance and strength from higher powers or leaders when facing difficulties.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when a team faces challenges, they may look up to their leaders (akin to Indra) for guidance and support. Recognizing their efforts and honoring their leadership can foster a positive work environment.
    • In personal life, individuals may face adversities such as health issues or financial struggles. Turning to spiritual practices or community leaders for support and guidance can provide strength and direction.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or festivals, people often honor their ancestors and deities, acknowledging their contributions and seeking blessings for overcoming future challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of honoring greatness and seeking strength in the face of adversity.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the value of leadership and support systems in their lives, whether in personal or professional contexts.
    • By practicing reverence and seeking guidance, one can navigate challenges more effectively and cultivate a sense of community and strength.
