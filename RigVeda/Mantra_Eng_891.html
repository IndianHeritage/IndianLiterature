अभिषṭाने ते अद्रिवो यत् स्था जगच् च रेजते |त्वष्टा चित तव मन्यव इन्द्र वेविज्यते भियाऽर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
abhiṣṭane te adrivo yat sthā jagac ca rejate | tvaṣṭā cit tava manyava indra vevijyate bhiyārcann anu svarājyam ||
  • abhiṣṭane: This term can be understood as "in the act of invocation" or "in the process of calling upon." It signifies the importance of intention and focus when seeking divine assistance or blessings.
  • te: This means "your," indicating a direct connection to the divine or the deity being addressed.
  • adrivo: Translated as "mountains" or "hills," this word symbolizes stability, strength, and the natural world. Mountains often represent obstacles or challenges in life.
  • yat: This means "that which" or "whatever," introducing a clause that elaborates on the subject being discussed.
  • sthā: This translates to "place" or "position," indicating a sense of grounding or stability in one's life or environment.
  • jagac: Meaning "the world" or "universe," it encompasses all of creation and existence, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking ideas together.
  • rejate: This term means "shines" or "radiates," suggesting a sense of vitality, energy, and presence in the world.
  • tvaṣṭā: Referring to the divine craftsman or creator, this term emphasizes the role of creativity and craftsmanship in the universe.
  • cit: This means "consciousness" or "awareness," indicating the importance of mindfulness and understanding in our actions.
  • tava: Meaning "your," it again emphasizes the personal connection to the divine.
  • manyava: This translates to "thoughts" or "intentions," highlighting the significance of our mental state and desires in shaping our reality.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes power, strength, and leadership, representing the qualities we aspire to embody.
  • vevijyate: This means "is invoked" or "is called upon," indicating the act of seeking divine intervention or support.
  • bhiyārcann: This term can be understood as "with reverence" or "with fear," suggesting a respectful approach when addressing the divine.
  • anu: Meaning "following" or "after," it indicates a sequence or progression in thought or action.
  • svarājyam: This translates to "the realm of sound" or "the kingdom of the heavens," symbolizing a higher state of existence or consciousness.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of intention, creativity, and reverence in our interactions with the divine and the world around us. It teaches us that by focusing our thoughts and actions, we can invoke the strength and support of higher powers to navigate the challenges of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, creative endeavors, or when seeking guidance in decision-making. By cultivating mindfulness and a respectful attitude towards the divine, we can enhance our experiences and foster a deeper connection with the universe.
