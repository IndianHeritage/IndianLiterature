तं त्वा मदाय घृष्वय उ लोककृत्नुम ईमहे |तव प्रशस्तयो महीः ||
taṁ tvā madāya ghṛṣvaya u lokakṛtnum īmahe | tava praśastayo mahīḥ ||
  • taṁ tvā madāya ghṛṣvaya: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke you, O divine one, with our offerings." Here, "taṁ" refers to the divine entity being addressed, "tvā" means "you," "madāya" signifies "with our offerings," and "ghṛṣvaya" implies a sense of sweetness or delight in the offerings.
  • u lokakṛtnum īmahe: This part translates to "We meditate upon the one who is the creator of the worlds." "U" can be interpreted as "and," "lokakṛtnum" refers to the creator of the worlds or the universe, and "īmahe" means "we meditate" or "we contemplate."
  • tava praśastayo mahīḥ: This translates to "Your glorious greatness is vast." "Tava" means "your," "praśastayo" refers to "glorious" or "praiseworthy," and "mahīḥ" signifies "greatness" or "vastness."

  • Invocation and Offerings: The mantra begins with an invocation to a divine entity, emphasizing the importance of offerings in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be likened to how people express gratitude or seek blessings through rituals, prayers, or acts of kindness.
  • Contemplation of Creation: The second part encourages meditation on the creator of the universe. This can be related to the practice of mindfulness or meditation in modern life, where individuals reflect on their existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Recognition of Greatness: The final part acknowledges the vastness and glory of the divine. This can be seen in how people admire nature, art, or achievements, recognizing a greater force or inspiration behind them.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of invoking the divine through offerings, meditating on the creator, and recognizing the greatness of the universe. In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate gratitude, mindfulness, and appreciation for the world around them. By integrating these principles into daily life, one can foster a deeper connection with the divine and a greater understanding of their place in the universe.
