त्वां अग्नि हविष्मन्तो देवम् मर्तास इळते |मन्ये त्वा जातवेदसं स हव्याः वक्ष्य आनुषक ||
tvām agne haviṣmanto devam martāsa īḻate | manye tvā jātavedasaṁ sa havyā vakṣy ānuṣak ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature.
  • agne - This is the vocative form of Agni, emphasizing the direct address to the deity.
  • haviṣmanto - This term means "those who offer oblations," indicating the act of sacrifice or offerings made to the gods.
  • devam - This means "divine" or "god," reinforcing Agni's status as a deity.
  • martāsa - This translates to "mortal beings," referring to humans who are making the offerings.
  • īḻate - This means "invoke" or "worship," indicating the action of calling upon Agni.
  • manye - This translates to "I think" or "I believe," expressing a personal conviction or faith.
  • tvā - Again, this means "you," continuing the address to Agni.
  • jātavedasaṁ - This term refers to "the one who knows all births," a title for Agni that signifies his omniscience and connection to creation.
  • sa - This means "he" or "that," referring back to Agni.
  • havyā - This translates to "the offerings," specifically those that are suitable for the gods.
  • vakṣy - This means "will speak" or "will declare," indicating a future action.
  • ānuṣak - This term means "in accordance with," suggesting that the offerings should be made in a proper manner.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Agni, where the worshippers (mortals) are calling upon the divine fire to accept their offerings. It emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, highlighting the importance of rituals and sacrifices in Vedic culture.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a reflection of the importance of gratitude and respect towards the forces of nature and the divine. For example, in modern times, people may not perform Vedic rituals, but they often express gratitude through various means, such as:

  • Participating in community service or charitable acts, acknowledging the blessings they have received.
  • Practicing mindfulness and meditation, which can be seen as a way to connect with the divine or the universe.
  • Engaging in environmental conservation efforts, recognizing the importance of nature and its resources.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of acknowledging and honoring the divine forces that govern our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for respect and gratitude towards the universe. By applying the principles of this mantra in our daily lives, we can foster a sense of community, responsibility, and reverence for the world around us.
