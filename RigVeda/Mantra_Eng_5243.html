वयं ते अग्नि समिधा विधेम वयं दाशेम सुष्टुती यजत्र |वयं घृतेनाध्वरस्य होतर वयं देव हविषा भद्रशोचे ||
vayaṁ te agne samidhā vidhema vayaṁ dāśema suṣṭutī yajatra | vayaṁ ghṛtenādhvarasya hotar vayaṁ deva haviṣā bhadraśoce ||
  • vayaṁ te agne samidhā vidhema: This phrase translates to "We kindle you, O Agni." Here, Agni represents the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the act of lighting a candle or a fire during a significant event, such as a wedding or a religious ceremony, where the fire serves as a witness and a purifying element.
  • vayaṁ dāśema suṣṭutī yajatra: This means "We offer you our praises in the sacrifice." It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine forces in our lives. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how people express gratitude through various means, such as community service or charitable acts, acknowledging the blessings they have received.
  • vayaṁ ghṛtenādhvarasya hotar: This translates to "We are the priests of the sacrifice with ghee." Ghee, or clarified butter, is a sacred offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and purity. In everyday life, this can be related to the idea of nurturing relationships and providing support to others, akin to how ghee enhances the quality of food.
  • vayaṁ deva haviṣā bhadraśoce: This means "We invoke the gods with our offerings for auspiciousness." It highlights the significance of seeking blessings for well-being and prosperity. In contemporary life, this can be reflected in the practice of setting intentions or prayers for success in various endeavors, such as starting a new job or embarking on a new project.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, gratitude, and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of higher powers and the role of offerings in fostering a sense of community and purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a spirit of gratitude, engage in acts of service, and seek blessings for their endeavors, thereby enhancing their spiritual and social well-being.
