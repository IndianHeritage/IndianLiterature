स्मत्पुरंन्धिर न आगहि विश्वतोधीर्न नूतये |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
smatpuraṁdhir na ā gahi viśvatodhīr na ūtaye | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • smatpuraṁdhir: One who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • na: Not.
    • ā gahi: To grasp or hold.
    • viśvatodhīr: All-knowing or omniscient.
    • na ūtaye: Not for the purpose of elevation or upliftment.
    • divo: Of the heavens or divine.
    • amuṣya: Of this or that.
    • śāsato: Commanding or ruling.
    • divaṁ: Heaven or the divine realm.
    • yaya: By which.
    • divāvaso: Living in the day or in the light.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the nature of wisdom and knowledge.
    • It suggests that true wisdom is not something that can be grasped or held onto in a superficial manner.
    • It emphasizes that knowledge should not be used merely for personal elevation or gain.
    • The reference to the divine realm indicates that true understanding comes from a higher source.
    • Living in the light symbolizes clarity, truth, and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who possesses knowledge but uses it solely for personal gain may not be respected. In contrast, a leader who shares knowledge for the benefit of the team embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In education, a teacher who imparts knowledge selflessly, aiming to enlighten students rather than seeking accolades, exemplifies the wisdom described in this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who seek to understand and support each other rather than compete for superiority reflect the deeper meaning of wisdom and knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom that transcends personal ambition.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge for the greater good and to live in a way that promotes enlightenment and understanding.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective leadership, and a more compassionate society.
