मन्त्र: प्रेतां यज्ञस्य शम्भुवाऽ युवाम् इद् आवृणीमहे | अग्निं च हव्यवाहनम् ||
pretāṁ yajñasya śambhuvā yuvām id ā vṛṇīmahe | agniṁ ca havyavāhanam ||
  • Mantra: "pretāṁ yajñasya śambhuvā yuvām id ā vṛṇīmahe | agniṁ ca havyavāhanam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pretāṁ: the departed souls or ancestors
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice or offering
    • śambhuvā: by the grace of the auspicious or divine
    • yuvām: you (plural), referring to the divine or the deities
    • id: now
    • ā: here, in this place
    • vṛṇīmahe: we choose or invoke
    • agniṁ: Agni, the fire god
    • ca: and
    • havyavāhanam: the one who carries offerings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the blessings of the ancestors and the divine forces.
    • The term "pretāṁ" signifies the respect and remembrance of those who have passed away, emphasizing the importance of honoring our ancestors.
    • "Yajñasya" refers to the sacred rituals or sacrifices that are performed to connect with the divine and seek blessings.
    • "Śambhuvā" indicates that these blessings come from a divine source, highlighting the auspicious nature of the offerings.
    • The phrase "yuvām id ā vṛṇīmahe" expresses the intention to invoke the divine presence in the current moment, suggesting a call for guidance and support.
    • "Agniṁ ca havyavāhanam" refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine, carrying the offerings to the heavens.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, offerings are made to Agni during ceremonies, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • Family gatherings during festivals often include remembering ancestors, showcasing the cultural practice of honoring those who came before us.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be reflected in memorial services where families come together to celebrate the lives of their departed loved ones.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of honoring our ancestors and seeking divine blessings through rituals.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, death, and the spiritual realm, encouraging individuals to maintain a respectful relationship with their heritage.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as family traditions, memorials, and spiritual practices, fostering a sense of community and continuity.
