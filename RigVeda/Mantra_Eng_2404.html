इमौ देवौ जायमानौ जुषन्तेमौ तमांसि गूहताम अजुष्टा |आभ्यां इन्द्रः पक्वम आमास्व अन्तः सोमापूषभ्यां जनद उष्रियासु ||
imau devau jāyamānau juṣantemau tamāṁsi gūhatām ajuṣṭā | ābhyām indraḥ pakvam āmāsv antaḥ somāpūṣabhyāṁ janad usriyāsu ||
  • Mantra: "imau devau jāyamānau juṣantemau tamāṁsi gūhatām ajuṣṭā | ābhyām indraḥ pakvam āmāsv antaḥ somāpūṣabhyāṁ janad usriyāsu ||"
  • Translation: "These two deities, born together, enjoy the hidden darkness; Indra, with these two, may you be nourished with the essence of the soma, and may you bring forth prosperity to the people."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • imau: These two
    • devau: deities
    • jāyamānau: born together
    • juṣantemau: enjoy
    • tamāṁsi: darkness
    • gūhatām: hidden
    • ajuṣṭā: not enjoyed
    • ābhyām: with these two
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • pakvam: nourished
    • āmāsv: may you bring forth
    • antaḥ: essence
    • somāpūṣabhyāṁ: of the soma (a sacred drink)
    • janad: people
    • usriyāsu: prosperity
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the concept of teamwork in organizations, where two or more individuals (deities) work together to achieve a common goal, enjoying the fruits of their labor while navigating challenges (darkness).
    • In agriculture, the reference to Indra and soma can symbolize the importance of rain (Indra) and the nourishment of crops (soma) for the prosperity of farmers and communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on collaboration and the divine support that comes from working together towards a common purpose.
    • In real life, it teaches us the value of unity, the importance of nurturing our resources, and the need for divine or external support in our endeavors.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as community building, teamwork in professional settings, and even in personal relationships where mutual support leads to shared success.
