रश्मींर इव यच्छतम अध्वराम् उप श्यावाश्वस्य सुन्वतो मदाच्युताः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चाश्विनां तिरोहन्यं ||
raśmīm̐r iva yacchatam adhvarām̐ upa śyāvāśvasya sunvato madacyutā | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cāśvinā tiroahnyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • raśmīm̐r: rays or beams
    • iva: like or as
    • yacchatam: you give or bestow
    • adhvarām̐: of the sacrifice or ritual
    • upa: towards or near
    • śyāvāśvasya: of the dark horse (often associated with the dawn)
    • sunvato: of the one who is rich or prosperous
    • madacyutā: who is not intoxicated or who is steady
    • sajoṣasā: with joy or happiness
    • uṣasā: of the dawn
    • sūryeṇa: with the sun
    • cāśvinā: with the Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • tiroahnyam: who dispels darkness or night
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the rays of the sun, likening them to the gifts bestowed upon the rituals and sacrifices.
    • The mention of the "dark horse" symbolizes the transition from night to dawn, representing hope and new beginnings.
    • The phrase "who is not intoxicated" suggests a state of clarity and focus, essential for spiritual practices.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins emphasizes the importance of health, vitality, and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the rays of the sun can be seen as a source of energy and positivity, much like how we seek inspiration and motivation in our endeavors.
    • The transition from night to dawn can be compared to overcoming challenges and emerging stronger, akin to personal growth after hardships.
    • Practicing mindfulness and clarity in decision-making can be related to the idea of being "not intoxicated," leading to better outcomes in both personal and professional life.
    • The Ashvins' association with health can be reflected in the importance of maintaining physical and mental well-being through balanced living.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of clarity, positivity, and the transition from darkness to light in our lives.
    • It encourages us to embrace new beginnings with joy and to seek health and vitality in our daily routines.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life, where every ending leads to a new beginning, and the importance of rituals and practices that connect us to the divine.
